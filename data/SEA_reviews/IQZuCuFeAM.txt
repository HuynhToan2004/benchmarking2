 **Summary:**
The paper introduces SSFL, a novel approach for sparse decentralized federated learning that identifies a sparse network based on parameter saliency scores. This method aims to address the challenges of communication efficiency and computational cost in federated learning by focusing on the non-IID setting. The authors propose a method that leverages saliency scores to determine sparse network connections, which are then used to train models across clients. The paper claims that this method can improve communication efficiency and reduce computational costs. However, the experimental results are not convincing, and the paper suffers from several presentation and clarity issues.
