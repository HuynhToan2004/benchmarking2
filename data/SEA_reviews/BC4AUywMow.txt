 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel encoder-decoder architecture for neural shape representations, focusing on the zero-level-set encoder that operates on mesh-based inputs. The architecture is designed to efficiently handle zero-level sets of 3D shapes, utilizing a hybrid graph-grid convolution block for feature extraction. The authors claim that this approach can handle deformable objects and provides a more efficient alternative to traditional auto-decoder-based methods. The paper also discusses the integration of graph and grid convolutions, which is highlighted as a significant contribution. However, concerns are raised regarding the clarity of the motivation for using the zero-level-set encoder and the incremental nature of the contributions compared to existing methods.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, with clear presentation of the method and results.
- The proposed method shows good performance compared to baselines, demonstrating its effectiveness.
- The introduction of training encoder-decoder networks for 3D shapes on the Eikonal equation is a significant contribution, allowing for the avoidance of computing ground truth signed distance samples for training.
- The hybrid graph-grid convolution block is innovative, combining the strengths of graph-based and voxel-based methods, which is highlighted as a novel approach.
- The paper provides a comprehensive evaluation across multiple datasets, demonstrating the generalizability and scalability of the proposed method.
- The method is designed to be computationally efficient, which is beneficial for practical applications.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a clear motivation for using the zero-level-set encoder, particularly in terms of its advantages over existing methods like auto-decoder-based approaches.
- The paper does not adequately discuss the limitations of the proposed method, which could include its applicability to different types of 3D shapes or its performance on more complex datasets.
- The paper does not provide a detailed discussion on the computational complexity of the method, which is crucial for understanding its efficiency.
- The novelty of the work is questioned, as the method appears to be an incremental improvement over existing methods like Occupancy Networks, with similar performance metrics.
- The paper does not sufficiently discuss the impact of different hyperparameters on the performance of the method, which could be crucial for reproducibility and optimization.
- The paper lacks a discussion on the generalizability of the method to different types of 3D shapes, particularly those with complex topologies or non-manifold structures.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors provide a more detailed motivation for using the zero-level-set encoder and discuss its advantages over existing methods?
- How does the proposed method handle deformable objects, and what are the specific challenges in this application?
- Can the authors discuss the computational complexity of the method and provide insights into its efficiency compared to other methods?
- How does the method perform on more complex datasets, and what are the limitations of its application?
- Could the authors provide a more detailed discussion on the impact of different hyperparameters on the performance of the method, and suggest optimal values for practical applications?
- The paper mentions using the proposed decoder of Mehta et al. (2021). Can the authors clarify the specific contributions of their work beyond this decoder?
