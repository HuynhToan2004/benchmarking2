 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel attribution method called AttEXplore, which leverages model parameter exploration and frequency-based input feature alterations to enhance the interpretability of deep neural networks (DNNs). The methodology is inspired by the consistency between decision boundary exploration approaches of attribution and transferable attacks. Extensive experiments are conducted on ImageNet using models like Inception-v3, ResNet-50, and VGG-16, demonstrating the method's effectiveness in improving insertion and deletion scores. The paper also discusses the method's robustness and its ability to outperform existing methods. However, concerns were raised regarding the novelty of the approach, the clarity of the paper, and the justification of certain methodological choices.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel attribution method by performing Attribution for Explanation with Model Parameter Exploration based on transferable attacks, named AttEXplore.
- The methodology is well-explained, and the paper is easy to follow, with clear figures and descriptions that aid in understanding the proposed method.
- The paper includes a comprehensive set of experiments, demonstrating the effectiveness of the proposed method in improving insertion and deletion scores.
- The paper is well-written, making it accessible and understandable, which is crucial for a paper in the field of Explainable AI (XAI).
- The paper introduces a new method for attribution using model parameter exploration, which is novel and contributes to the field of XAI.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed discussion on the choice of evaluation metrics, particularly the use of insertion and deletion scores, which might not fully capture the effectiveness of the proposed method.
- The paper's claim of novelty is questionable as it appears to combine existing methods (AttEXplore = SSA + AGI) without sufficient differentiation or justification.
- The paper does not adequately discuss the limitations of the proposed method, which is crucial for understanding its applicability and effectiveness.
- The paper's contribution is limited as it primarily builds on existing methods without significant novelty or technical depth.
- The paper could benefit from a more thorough literature review and a clearer explanation of how the proposed method differs from existing approaches.
- The paper's methodology and some of its claims, such as the consistency between decision boundary exploration approaches of attribution and transferable attacks, are not convincingly supported by the evidence provided.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors clarify how the proposed method differs from existing approaches, particularly in terms of its novelty and technical contributions?
- How does the proposed method handle different types of noise and perturbations in real-world scenarios?
- Can the authors provide a more detailed explanation of the decision to use insertion and deletion scores as the primary evaluation metrics?
- How does the proposed method ensure that the attribution results are robust and not influenced by the specifics of the training data or the model architecture?
- Could the authors provide more details on the choice of hyperparameters and their impact on the method's performance?
- How does the method handle the trade-off between insertion and deletion scores, and what are the implications of this trade-off for the method's effectiveness?
