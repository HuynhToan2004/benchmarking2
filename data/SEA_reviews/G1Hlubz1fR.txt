 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel method for parameter-efficient fine-tuning (PEFT) in multi-task learning, specifically focusing on the decomposition of adapters into task-common and task-specific components. This methodology, termed C-Poly, leverages a Gumbel-softmax mechanism to optimize the allocation of adapters to tasks. The paper claims that C-Poly can outperform existing methods such as Poly and MHR, as demonstrated through experiments on benchmarks like SuperGLUE and Super NI. However, the paper has been criticized for its limited novelty and unclear differentiation from existing methods like Poly.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written and easy to follow, with clear presentation of the proposed method.
- The experiments are extensive, including various models and datasets, and the results are presented clearly.
- The proposed method, C-Poly, is intuitive and shows improvements over baseline methods, especially in multi-task learning.
- The paper provides a detailed analysis of the proposed method, including parameter efficiency and the effect of different numbers of tasks.
- The introduction of task-specific and task-common adapters is an interesting approach to parameter-efficient fine-tuning.

**Weaknesses:**
- The novelty of the paper is limited, as it primarily combines existing techniques such as LoRA and task-specific and task-common adapters, which have been previously explored.
- The paper lacks a thorough analysis of the learned weights and their impact on task performance, which could provide deeper insights into the effectiveness of the proposed method.
- There is a lack of comparison with other parameter-efficient methods like AdaLoRA, which could help in validating the proposed method's effectiveness.
- The paper does not adequately address the computational complexity introduced by the additional trainable parameters, which could be a significant drawback in practical applications.
- The paper does not provide a detailed comparison of the training time and memory usage of the proposed method, which is crucial for evaluating its practical applicability.
- The paper's experimental setup and results are not sufficiently robust, with insufficient details on the experimental setup and a lack of reproducibility due to missing code and detailed experimental setups.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors provide more details on the training time and memory usage of the proposed method, especially in comparison to other parameter-efficient methods?
- How do the authors justify the use of additional trainable parameters, and what are the computational implications of these parameters?
- Can the authors clarify the differences between C-Poly and Poly, particularly in terms of the weight allocation mechanism and its impact on task performance?
- How does the proposed method perform when applied to different types of models, such as encoder-only or decoder-only models?
- Could the authors provide more details on the experimental setup, including the specific models used and the training parameters?
- How does the proposed method handle the trade-off between task-specific and task-common adapters, and what are the implications of this trade-off on the overall performance?
