 **Summary:**
The paper introduces GraphGPT, a novel transformer-based model for graph learning that transforms graphs into sequences of tokens using an Eulerian path, which is then pre-trained using a next-token-prediction (NTP) task. This model is capable of handling large-scale graphs and has been tested on various graph-level, edge-level, and node-level tasks, showing state-of-the-art results on some datasets. The authors also discuss the challenges of scaling up transformer models for graph tasks and the potential of GraphGPT in advancing graph foundation models. Despite its strengths, the paper has been critiqued for its limited novelty, lack of comprehensive ablation studies, and insufficient discussion on scalability and computational efficiency.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces GraphGPT, a novel transformer-based model for graph learning that transforms graphs into sequences of tokens using an Eulerian path, which is a significant advancement in the field.
- GraphGPT demonstrates state-of-the-art results on various graph-level, edge-level, and node-level tasks, showing its effectiveness in handling different types of graph data.
- The model is capable of handling large-scale graphs, which is a critical challenge in graph learning.
- The paper provides a comprehensive evaluation of GraphGPT across different types of graph tasks, including graph classification, regression, link prediction, and node classification, which helps in understanding the model's versatility and applicability.
- The use of a semi-Eulerian path to transform graphs into sequences of tokens is an innovative approach that has not been explored in depth in previous works.
- The paper is well-written, easy to follow, and includes detailed experimental results, which are supported by an ablation study that demonstrates the importance of pre-training and node identity encoding.

**Weaknesses:**
- The novelty of the paper is somewhat limited as it primarily builds upon existing transformer architectures and does not introduce significant new theoretical insights or methodologies.
- There is a lack of comprehensive ablation studies, particularly regarding the impact of different components such as node re-indexing and the use of a semi-Eulerian path.
- The paper does not sufficiently discuss the scalability and computational efficiency of GraphGPT, which is crucial for understanding its practical applicability in large-scale graph learning scenarios.
- The paper could benefit from a more detailed discussion on the limitations of GraphGPT and potential directions for future research.
- There is a need for more comparative analysis with other graph transformer models to better understand the unique contributions and advantages of GraphGPT.
- The paper lacks a detailed discussion on the scalability of GraphGPT, which is crucial for understanding its practical applicability in large-scale graph learning scenarios.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors clarify the specific contributions of GraphGPT beyond the existing transformer architectures?
- How does the scalability of GraphGPT compare to other large-scale graph learning models, and what are the potential bottlenecks in scaling up GraphGPT?
- Could the authors provide more detailed comparative analysis with other graph transformer models to better understand the unique contributions and advantages of GraphGPT?
- How does GraphGPT handle the issue of over-smoothing and over-squashing, which are common challenges in graph neural networks?
- Can the authors discuss the potential of integrating GraphGPT with other large language models to enhance its capabilities?
- In light of the concerns regarding scalability and computational efficiency, how does GraphGPT compare to other models in terms of training time, memory usage, and other relevant metrics?
- Could the authors provide more details on the limitations of GraphGPT and potential directions for future research?
