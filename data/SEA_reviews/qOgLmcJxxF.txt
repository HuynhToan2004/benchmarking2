 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel sample complexity analysis for training diffusion models, focusing on the sample efficiency of the score-matching objective in the context of diffusion models. It proposes a new notion of error measure, $D_p^\delta(f,g)$, which is more robust to outliers compared to traditional $L^2$ error. This measure allows for a more efficient training process, particularly in scenarios where the score function is poorly approximated by the finite hypothesis class. The paper also discusses the implications of this new error measure on the training and sampling processes, showing that it can lead to significant improvements in sample complexity. The theoretical results are supported by a discussion on the limitations of existing $L^2$ error measures and the practical implications of the proposed error measure in real-world scenarios.
