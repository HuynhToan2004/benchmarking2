 **Summary:**
The paper introduces CANDLE, a method for training binary neural networks on long-tailed datasets using a pretrained full-precision model as a teacher. This method employs adversarial balancing and multi-resolution learning to optimize the model's performance. The approach involves a calibration step where a classifier is trained on the target dataset, followed by a distillation phase where the binary network is trained using the calibrated teacher. The method's effectiveness is demonstrated through experiments on 15 datasets, showcasing improvements over existing methods. Despite the innovative approach, concerns about the novelty and the clarity of the experimental setup are raised, particularly regarding the choice of datasets and the fairness of comparisons.

**Strengths:**
- The paper addresses an important and under-explored problem of long-tailed recognition using binary networks, which is both interesting and relevant to the field.
- The methodology is well-presented, with clear descriptions of the proposed method and its components, including adversarial balancing and multi-resolution learning.
- The experimental setup is extensive, involving 15 datasets, which is a significant contribution to the field.
- The paper is well-written, making it easy to follow and understand, with detailed explanations of the proposed method and its implementation.
- The results showcase the effectiveness of the proposed method, with improvements over baseline methods.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks clarity in several areas, including the definition and usage of terms like "calibration" and "distillation," which could be better explained or rephrased for clarity.
- The experimental setup and results are not clearly presented, with issues such as missing legends in figures and unclear explanations of the results.
- The novelty of the approach is questioned, as it appears to be a combination of existing methods without significant innovation.
- The choice of datasets and their imbalance ratios raises concerns about the fairness and relevance of the comparisons made.
- The paper lacks a detailed discussion on the limitations and potential negative societal impacts of the proposed method.

**Questions:**
- Could you clarify the terms "calibration" and "distillation" and their roles in the proposed method?
- How does the proposed method compare to other binary networks in terms of performance and computational efficiency?
- What is the rationale behind using binary networks for long-tailed recognition, and how does this approach compare to using full-precision networks?
- Can you provide more details on the choice of datasets and the imbalance ratios used in the experiments?
- How does the proposed method address the issue of classifier weight norms becoming larger for tail classes, as discussed in the paper?
- Could you elaborate on the computational efficiency of the proposed method, especially in terms of training time and memory usage?
- How does the adversarial balancing network function, and what specific role does it play in the overall methodology?
