 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel method for training Binary Neural Networks (BNNs) in binary weight space, aiming to reduce memory usage by eliminating the need for real-valued weights. The authors propose a method that generates binary weights by sampling a hypermask, which determines the probability of flipping the sign of weights based on the real-valued gradients. This approach is supported by theoretical analysis and empirical results showing comparable performance to baseline methods on CIFAR-10 and CIFAR-100 datasets. However, the paper faces criticism for its lack of comprehensive evaluation on larger datasets and more complex architectures, as well as its limited comparison to existing methods.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel method for training BNNs in binary weight space, which is memory-efficient and does not require the retention of real weights.
- The method is supported by theoretical analysis and empirical results that show comparable performance to baseline methods on CIFAR-10 and CIFAR-100 datasets.
- The paper is well-written, making it easy to follow and understand.
- The method's novelty lies in its ability to train BNNs without real-valued weights, which is a significant advancement in the field.
- The paper provides a comprehensive evaluation of the memory footprint required by BNN training methods, demonstrating a reduction in memory usage by a factor of 33 compared to conventional methods.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks comprehensive evaluation on larger datasets and more complex architectures, such as ResNet-50 and ResNet-101.
- The paper's comparison is limited to baseline methods, and it does not compare the proposed method with more recent and advanced BNN training methods.
- The paper does not discuss the limitations of the proposed method, such as the potential for increased training time due to the use of hypermasks.
- The paper does not provide a detailed discussion on the potential negative societal impacts of the proposed method.
- The paper's experimental results are not convincing, particularly in the comparison of the proposed method with existing methods, and the results on CIFAR-100 are not promising.
- The paper's writing and organization need improvement, particularly in the clarity of the algorithm descriptions and the presentation of experimental results.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors provide more details on the training time of the proposed method compared to baseline methods?
- How does the proposed method compare to other recent and advanced BNN training methods?
- Can the authors provide more detailed experimental results, particularly on larger and more complex datasets?
- How does the proposed method handle the potential for increased training time due to the use of hypermasks?
- Could the authors discuss the potential negative societal impacts of the proposed method and how these might be mitigated?
- In the context of the experimental results, how do the authors explain the discrepancies in performance between the proposed method and baseline methods?
