 **Summary:**
The paper explores a contextual multi-armed bandit problem where each arm can yield both rewards and harms. It introduces a novel generalized linear model to handle the contextual variables and formulates a new regret function that includes a penalty for harms. The authors propose a new algorithm based on the ε-greedy method and establish a regret bound for the proposed algorithm. The paper is well-written, clearly articulating the problem and its solution, although it lacks a comprehensive review of related work and has some issues with the clarity and depth of the experimental results.

**Strengths:**
- The paper addresses an important problem in bandit literature, focusing on the contextual multi-armed bandit problem where actions can have both rewards and harms.
- The authors propose a novel generalized linear model for handling the contextual variables and formulate a new regret function that includes a penalty for harms.
- The paper is well-written, making it easy to follow and understand the problem formulation and the proposed solution.
- The authors provide a regret bound for their proposed algorithm, which is a significant contribution to the field.
- The paper discusses the limitations of the proposed algorithm, including the need for more exploration rounds and the computational complexity of the MLE of β.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a comprehensive review of related work, particularly in the context of multi-objective bandits and safety-constrained bandits.
- The experimental results are limited and do not convincingly demonstrate the superiority of the proposed algorithm over existing methods.
- There are several typographical and mathematical errors throughout the paper that need correction.
- The paper does not adequately discuss the computational complexity of the MLE of β, which could be a significant limitation in practical applications.
- The paper does not provide a detailed comparison with existing methods, which makes it difficult to assess the novelty and effectiveness of the proposed approach.
- The paper does not discuss the implications of the assumptions made, such as the boundedness of the context, harm, and reward, which could limit the applicability of the results.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors clarify the definitions and roles of the parameters θ and λ in the problem formulation?
- How does the proposed algorithm compare with existing methods, particularly in terms of computational complexity and practical applicability?
- Could the authors provide more detailed experimental results, including comparisons with other algorithms and a discussion of the statistical significance of the results?
- How do the authors address the computational complexity of the MLE of β, especially in large-scale applications?
- Can the authors provide a more detailed discussion on the assumptions made in the paper, particularly the boundedness of the context, harm, and reward?
- How does the proposed algorithm handle the exploration-exploitation trade-off, and what are the implications of this trade-off on the practical applicability of the results?
