 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel method for debiasing machine learning models in the presence of multiple biases, using multi-task learning and multi-objective optimization. It proposes a new dataset, MultiCelebA, to evaluate the effectiveness of the proposed method. The method optimizes for a Pareto-stationary solution by adjusting the weights of different tasks, aiming to mitigate the negative effects of biases. The paper claims that the method outperforms existing baselines in various settings, including multi-bias and single-bias scenarios. However, the paper is critiqued for its clarity in explaining the grouping strategy and the necessity of the multi-objective approach, as well as the lack of detailed comparisons with existing methods.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, easy to follow, and provides a clear and concise explanation of the proposed method and its application to the MultiCelebA dataset.
- The proposed method is simple, effective, and can be applied to both single and multi-bias datasets, demonstrating strong performance in experiments.
- The introduction of a new dataset, MultiCelebA, is beneficial for future research in the field of debiasing.
- The paper provides a comprehensive analysis of the proposed method, including ablation studies on different components and a comparison with existing methods.
- The paper is the first to consider multi-bias cases in debiasing, which is a significant contribution to the field.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks clarity in explaining the grouping strategy, particularly how samples are assigned to different groups and the rationale behind the grouping method.
- The necessity and effectiveness of the multi-objective approach are not convincingly argued, especially in the absence of comparisons with other multi-objective methods.
- The paper does not adequately address the potential limitations of the proposed method, such as its scalability and generalizability to other datasets or tasks.
- The paper could benefit from a more detailed discussion on the choice of hyperparameters and their impact on the performance of the method.
- The paper could be improved by including more detailed comparisons with existing methods, especially in terms of the effectiveness of the proposed method in handling multiple biases.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors provide more details on how samples are assigned to different groups in the proposed grouping strategy?
- How does the proposed method compare to other multi-objective methods in terms of effectiveness and efficiency?
- Why was the MultiCelebA dataset chosen for the study, and how does it differ from other datasets in terms of bias and attribute distribution?
- Can the authors clarify the role and impact of the hyperparameters, particularly the update frequency U, on the performance of the method?
- How does the proposed method perform when applied to other datasets or tasks beyond those tested in the paper?
- Could the authors provide a more detailed comparison with existing methods, especially in terms of the handling of multiple biases and the scalability of the proposed method?
