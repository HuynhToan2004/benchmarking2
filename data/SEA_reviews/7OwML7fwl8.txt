 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel framework named Reckoner, designed to enhance fairness in machine learning models by leveraging a dual-model system. This system is based on a hierarchical structure of Variational Autoencoder (VAE) architectures, where one model learns from a high-confidence data subset and another from a low-confidence subset. The framework incorporates learnable noise to maintain predictiveness while avoiding biased predictions. The authors conducted exploratory data analyses on the COMPAS dataset, highlighting disparities in model fairness across different levels of confidence. The paper's main contributions include the observation of fairness disparities based on confidence levels and the development of a dual-model system to mitigate these disparities. The experimental results demonstrate the framework's effectiveness in achieving fairness while maintaining predictive performance.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel approach to fairness learning by leveraging the relationship between confidence and fairness, which is a significant departure from existing methods.
- The use of a dual-model system and learnable noise is innovative and well-explained, providing a clear methodological approach.
- The paper is well-written and easy to follow, making it accessible to a broad audience.
- The empirical results demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed method, showing improvements in fairness metrics and predictive performance.
- The authors have conducted thorough exploratory data analyses, which support the motivation behind the proposed framework.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed discussion on the limitations and potential negative societal impacts of the proposed method, which is crucial for understanding the broader implications of the work.
- The novelty of the approach is questionable, as similar ideas have been previously explored in other works.
- The paper does not sufficiently compare its method with other state-of-the-art methods, which could have provided a clearer picture of its advantages or disadvantages.
- The use of the COMPAS dataset, which has been previously used extensively, may not be sufficiently novel or groundbreaking.
- The experimental results are not convincingly superior to existing methods, and the paper does not adequately address the accuracy-fairness trade-off.
- The paper lacks a detailed discussion on the ethical implications of the proposed method, particularly in terms of data privacy and fairness in machine learning models.

**Questions:**
- Could you elaborate on the potential negative societal impacts of the proposed method, particularly in terms of data privacy and fairness?
- How does the proposed method address the accuracy-fairness trade-off, especially given the marginal improvements in fairness metrics?
- Can you provide more details on the feature engineering process, particularly how categorical features are handled?
- How does the proposed method perform with different types of data, such as tabular, image, or text data?
- What are the specific advantages of using the Reckoner method over other existing methods, particularly in terms of fairness and predictive performance?
- Could you clarify the role and impact of the learnable noise in the model's performance, especially in terms of fairness and accuracy?
