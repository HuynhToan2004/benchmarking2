 **Summary:**
The paper introduces AdaRec, a novel reinforcement learning-based method for adaptive sequential recommendation that addresses the challenges of distribution shifts in online recommendation systems. AdaRec leverages a context encoder to capture evolving user behavior patterns and optimizes exploration through a zero-order action optimization approach. The method is evaluated on both simulated and real-world datasets, demonstrating superior performance compared to existing baselines. The paper also explores the integration of optimism under uncertainty and zero-order action optimization to enhance the recommendation quality and adaptability in complex environments.

**Strengths:**
- The paper addresses a significant and relevant problem in the field of online recommendation systems, focusing on the challenges posed by distribution shifts.
- The proposed method, AdaRec, is innovative, integrating a context encoder to capture evolving user behavior patterns and optimizing exploration through zero-order action optimization.
- The paper is well-written, clear, and easy to follow, with extensive experiments conducted on both simulated and real-world datasets.
- The results show that AdaRec outperforms existing baselines, demonstrating its effectiveness in real-world applications.
- The paper provides a comprehensive analysis of the data-driven observations, which are crucial for understanding the problem and the proposed solution.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed comparison with other relevant methods, particularly those that address similar issues in evolving user interests.
- The evaluation of the method is limited to a few datasets and does not include a broader range of real-world scenarios, which could provide a more robust validation of the proposed approach.
- The paper could benefit from a more thorough discussion on the computational costs and scalability of AdaRec, especially in large-scale real-world applications.
- The novelty of the method is questionable as it resembles existing works, particularly in the use of context encoders and the exploration method.
- The paper does not adequately address the potential negative societal impacts of the proposed method, which could be significant in the context of recommendation systems.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors provide a detailed comparison with other relevant methods, particularly those that address similar issues in evolving user interests?
- How does AdaRec handle the computational costs and scalability issues in large-scale real-world applications?
- Could the authors provide a more detailed discussion on the method's novelty and how it differentiates from existing works?
- How does AdaRec ensure that it does not exacerbate existing biases in recommendation systems, particularly in terms of fairness and inclusivity?
- Can the authors provide additional results or experiments to support the claims made in the paper, particularly regarding the effectiveness of the exploration method and the context encoder?
