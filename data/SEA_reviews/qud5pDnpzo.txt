 **Summary:**
The paper explores the training of a vision transformer, termed ViP, using differential privacy (DP) with a masked autoencoder (MAE) on the LAION400M dataset. The authors propose a methodology that includes a warm-start with synthetic data and a two-step approach: first, training on synthetic data using standard optimizers, and then training on the LAION400M dataset with DP-SGD. The paper demonstrates that this method achieves high utility under a strict privacy budget, showing that it can scale to internet-scale data and potentially improve the performance of DP-trained models. However, the paper lacks comprehensive comparisons with other DP-trained models, particularly those using different architectures and datasets, which limits the understanding of its relative performance.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, clear, and easy to follow, with a clear motivation and a well-structured methodology.
- The proposed method achieves high utility under a strict privacy budget, which is significant for privacy-preserving training.
- The method is simple, practical, and can be applied to other large-scale datasets, making it a promising approach for future research.
- The paper includes a thorough evaluation of the proposed method, showing that it outperforms other DP-trained models in terms of linear probing accuracy and fine-tuning accuracy.
- The use of synthetic data for initialization and scaling up the training dataset size are innovative approaches that could enhance the performance of DP-trained models.
- The paper provides a comprehensive evaluation of the proposed method, including comparisons with existing models and a detailed analysis of the training process.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed discussion on the limitations of the proposed method, particularly in terms of its applicability to other datasets and architectures.
- There is a lack of comparison with other DP-trained models that use different architectures, such as ResNets, which could provide a more comprehensive understanding of the performance of the proposed method.
- The paper does not discuss the potential negative societal impacts of the proposed method, which is a critical aspect of any privacy-preserving technology.
- The paper could benefit from a more detailed comparison with other self-supervised learning methods, such as SimCLR, to demonstrate the advantages of the proposed method.
- The experimental results are not always convincing, with some results showing only marginal improvements over existing methods.
- The paper could provide more detailed comparisons with other DP-SGD methods to better understand the performance of the proposed method.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors provide a more detailed discussion on the limitations of the proposed method and its applicability to other datasets and architectures?
- How does the proposed method compare with other DP-trained models that use different architectures, such as ResNets?
- Can the authors discuss the potential negative societal impacts of the proposed method and how these might be mitigated?
- How does the proposed method compare with other self-supervised learning methods, such as SimCLR, in terms of performance and efficiency?
- Could the authors provide more detailed comparisons with other DP-SGD methods to better understand the performance of the proposed method?
- How does the proposed method handle the issue of privacy leakage, particularly in terms of membership inference attacks?
