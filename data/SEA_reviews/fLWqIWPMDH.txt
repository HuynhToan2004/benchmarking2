 **Summary:**
The paper introduces Expected-Expected Improvement Per Unit Cost (EEIPU), a novel acquisition function designed for Bayesian optimization (BO) in multi-stage pipelines with unknown costs. EEIPU integrates cost-awareness, memoization, and cost-cooling mechanisms to optimize the exploration-exploitation trade-off and reduce computational costs by reusing previous results. The methodology is evaluated on both synthetic and real-world datasets, demonstrating its effectiveness in achieving high objective values with lower total costs compared to existing methods. Despite its innovative approach, concerns are raised about the clarity of the paper, the novelty of the approach, and the limited evaluation on real-world datasets.

**Strengths:**
- The paper addresses the critical issue of cost-aware Bayesian optimization (BO) in multi-stage pipelines, which is a significant and relevant problem in the field of machine learning.
- The proposed method, EEIPU, is well-motivated and effectively integrates cost-awareness, memoization, and cost-cooling mechanisms to optimize the exploration-exploitation trade-off and reduce computational costs.
- The paper provides a detailed explanation of the methodology and the experiments are well-designed, using both synthetic and real-world datasets, which enhances the credibility of the results.
- The paper is well-written and provides a clear explanation of the methodology, which is beneficial for understanding the proposed approach.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed discussion on the limitations of the proposed method, which is crucial for understanding its applicability and potential drawbacks.
- The novelty of the approach is questionable as it appears to be a combination of existing techniques without significant innovation.
- The paper could benefit from a more rigorous evaluation, including a broader range of real-world datasets and more comprehensive comparisons with state-of-the-art methods.
- The presentation of the paper needs improvement, particularly in the clarity of figures and the overall organization of the content.
- There are concerns about the scalability of the proposed method, especially in scenarios with a large number of stages or parameters.
- The paper could benefit from a more thorough discussion on the implications of the assumptions made, such as the independence of stage costs.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors clarify the novelty of the approach and how it differs from existing methods like EI-COOL and CArBO?
- How does the proposed method perform in scenarios with a large number of stages or parameters?
- Can the authors provide more details on the limitations of the proposed method and scenarios where it may not be effective?
- In the experiments, how are the values of δ and r determined, and what impact do they have on the results?
- Could the authors provide a more detailed comparison with other state-of-the-art methods, including a discussion on how the proposed method outperforms or underperforms in different scenarios?
- How does the proposed method handle the assumption of independent stage costs, and what are the implications of this assumption on the results?
- Could the authors provide a more detailed explanation of the cost cooling mechanism and its impact on the optimization process?
