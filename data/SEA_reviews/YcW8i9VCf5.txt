 **Summary:**
The paper introduces Causal Bayesian Optimization with Multiplicative Weights (CBO-MW), a novel approach to causal Bayesian optimization that integrates adversarial elements. This algorithm aims to tackle non-stationary environments by modeling the impact of external events and other agents' interventions on the system. It extends the CBO-MW algorithm to handle adversarial settings, providing theoretical regret bounds and empirical evaluations across synthetic and real-world datasets. The methodology is based on a multiplicative weight update framework, which allows for handling combinatorial interventions and submodular rewards. The paper also discusses a distributed version of the algorithm, D-CBO-MW, which is designed for scalability in large action spaces.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, making it easy to follow, and the methodology is clearly explained.
- The authors provide theoretical guarantees for the proposed algorithm, which is backed by empirical evaluations showing the algorithm's effectiveness.
- The paper introduces a novel approach by combining CBO with adversarial settings, which is a significant contribution to the field.
- The authors demonstrate the potential of the proposed algorithm to outperform existing methods in both synthetic and real-world datasets.
- The paper provides a distributed version of the algorithm, which is scalable and can handle large action spaces.
- The theoretical analysis is comprehensive, and the paper includes a detailed appendix for further reading.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper could benefit from more detailed explanations in certain sections, particularly in the discussion of the causal Bayesian optimization setting and the adversarial elements.
- The paper does not sufficiently differentiate its methodology from existing works, such as the GP-MW algorithm, which might lead to confusion about the novelty of the contributions.
- The computational complexity of the proposed algorithm is not thoroughly discussed, which could be a concern for practical implementation.
- The paper could provide more examples and experimental results to better demonstrate the algorithm's performance in real-world scenarios.
- The paper could benefit from a more detailed discussion on the limitations and assumptions of the proposed method, particularly concerning the assumptions made about the adversarial actions and the impact of these assumptions on the algorithm's performance.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors provide more details on the computational complexity of the proposed algorithm?
- How does the algorithm handle scenarios where the adversarial actions are not fully known or predictable?
- Could the authors provide more examples or experimental results to demonstrate the algorithm's performance in real-world scenarios?
- How does the proposed algorithm compare to other existing methods in terms of computational efficiency and scalability?
- Can the authors clarify the assumptions made about the adversarial actions and how these assumptions affect the algorithm's performance?
- How does the proposed algorithm handle the assumption of a calibrated model, particularly in scenarios where this assumption may not hold?
