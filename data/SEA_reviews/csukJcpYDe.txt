 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel method called Generalized Policy Iteration using Tensor Train (TTPI) for handling hybrid action spaces in optimal control problems. TTPI leverages Tensor Train (TT) decomposition to approximate the state-value and advantage functions, which are crucial for handling hybrid action spaces. The method is demonstrated through simulations and real-world experiments, showing superior performance compared to existing baselines. The approach aims to address the challenges of handling hybrid action spaces in robotics, which are typically more complex than those found in traditional continuous action spaces. The paper also discusses the limitations and potential improvements of the TTPI method, suggesting future research directions.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, clear, and easy to follow, with a comprehensive literature review.
- The approach is novel, particularly the use of Tensor Train (TT) decomposition for handling hybrid action spaces, which is a significant contribution to the field.
- The paper demonstrates the effectiveness of the proposed method through extensive simulations and real-world experiments, showing superior performance compared to existing baselines.
- The method is scalable, which is crucial for handling complex robotic systems with large state and action spaces.
- The paper provides a clear explanation of the methodology and its applications, which is beneficial for readers unfamiliar with the TT-based approach.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed discussion on the limitations of the TTPI method, which could provide a more balanced view of the approach.
- There is a need for more extensive comparisons with existing methods, particularly in terms of computational efficiency and scalability.
- The paper could benefit from a more detailed discussion on the computational complexity and memory requirements of the TTPI method.
- The paper does not discuss the limitations of using TT for function approximation, which could be a significant drawback in certain scenarios.
- The paper does not include a discussion on the scalability of the method, which is crucial for real-world applications.
- There is a lack of discussion on the generalization of the method to other types of hybrid systems, which could limit its applicability.

**Questions:**
