 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel method for detecting AI-generated text by rewriting it and comparing the output to the original text. This method leverages the observation that AI-generated text is less likely to be modified during rewriting, a phenomenon attributed to the consistency in the generated text's structure. The approach is tested across various datasets and shown to outperform existing methods, including Ghostbuster and DetectGPT. The method's effectiveness is demonstrated by its ability to generalize across different LLMs and domains, and it does not require access to the LLM's internal workings or numerical outputs, making it compatible with black-box models. However, the method's reliance on a single rewriting prompt and its susceptibility to adversarial rephrasing are noted as limitations.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written and easy to follow, with clear explanations and detailed experiments that support the claims.
- The proposed method is novel, simple, and effective, demonstrating significant improvements over existing methods such as Ghostbuster and DetectGPT.
- The approach is generalizable across different LLMs and domains, showing robustness and adaptability.
- The method does not require access to the LLM's internal workings or numerical outputs, making it compatible with black-box models.
- The paper provides a comprehensive evaluation, including both in-distribution and out-of-distribution settings, and shows that the method can detect text generated from different LLMs, even if the LLM used for rewriting is different from the one used to generate the text.
- The method's effectiveness is demonstrated through extensive experiments, including robustness tests against adversarial rephrasing and different prompts.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper's reliance on a single rewriting prompt raises concerns about its robustness against adversarial rephrasing, as shown in Table 3.
- The method's effectiveness might be influenced by the quality of the rewriting prompt, which is manually designed and not optimized.
- The paper does not discuss the potential impact of the method on the detection of human-written text, which could be a significant limitation in practical applications.
