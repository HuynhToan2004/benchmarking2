 **Summary:**
The paper introduces LLaMA-Adapter, a method for fine-tuning the LLaMA model using a novel zero-initialized attention mechanism. This approach, which involves appending learnable adaption prompts to the model's transformer layers and initializing them with zeros, allows for efficient and effective instruction tuning of the LLaMA model. The method is shown to achieve comparable performance to fully fine-tuned models like Alpaca, using significantly fewer parameters and training time. The zero-initialized attention mechanism is also evaluated on other pre-trained models, demonstrating its general applicability. However, the paper's experimental results are not consistently superior to existing methods, and there are concerns about the novelty and clarity of the method's presentation.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, making it easy to follow, and introduces an efficient and effective method for instruction tuning of the LLaMA model.
- The proposed zero-initialized attention mechanism is innovative and shows good performance in various tasks, including visual question answering and instruction tuning.
- The methodology of LLaMA-Adapter is simple and effective, requiring only 1.2M learnable parameters, which is a significant reduction compared to full fine-tuning.
- The paper includes comprehensive evaluations and comparisons with existing methods, demonstrating the effectiveness of the proposed method.
- The zero-initialized attention mechanism is shown to be effective for fine-tuning other pre-trained models, such as ViT, RoBERTa, and CLIP, on traditional vision and language tasks.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed comparison with existing methods like LoRA, Alpaca, and Alpaca-LoRA, which could have provided a clearer understanding of the method's advantages and disadvantages.
- The results presented in the paper are not consistently superior to existing methods, and there are concerns about the method's novelty, particularly in terms of the zero-initialized attention mechanism.
- The paper does not sufficiently discuss the limitations of the proposed method, which could help in understanding its scope and applicability.
- The paper's evaluation is limited to a few benchmarks, and there is a need for more comprehensive evaluations across a broader range of tasks to fully demonstrate the method's effectiveness.
- The method's reliance on a specific set of hyperparameters (e.g., L=30, K=10) could limit its applicability in different scenarios, and the paper does not adequately address how these parameters were chosen.
- The paper does not provide sufficient details on the training process, such as the specifics of the gating mechanism and its impact on training stability.

**Questions:**
- Could you clarify the choice of hyperparameters, particularly L=30 and K=10, and discuss how these values were determined?
- How does the proposed method compare in terms of training stability and performance with other parameter-efficient fine-tuning methods, such as LoRA?
- Can you provide more details on the training process, particularly the role and impact of the gating mechanism on training stability?
- How does the zero-initialized attention mechanism compare to other methods in terms of its ability to preserve pre-trained knowledge and adapt to new tasks?
- In the multi-modal evaluation, why was only the ScienceQA dataset used, and could you discuss the potential impact of using other datasets like MME, MMBench, and LVLM-eHub?
- Could you provide more details on the method's application to other models beyond LLaMA, and discuss any potential limitations or challenges in doing so?
