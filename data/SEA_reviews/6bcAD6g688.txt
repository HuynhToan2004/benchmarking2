 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel method for detecting and correcting label errors in toxicity datasets, which is crucial for training safe language models. The methodology leverages a clustering approach to estimate the transition matrix from noisy labels to true labels, and it proposes a thresholding strategy to identify and correct mislabeled data points. The approach is evaluated across several datasets, demonstrating significant improvements in dataset credibility and model performance. The paper also discusses the societal implications of using these datasets, emphasizing the importance of dataset quality for model safety.

**Strengths:**
- The paper addresses a significant and timely issue in the field of training safe language models, focusing on the detection and correction of label errors in toxicity datasets.
- The methodology is clearly explained, with a focus on the estimation of the transition matrix from noisy labels to true labels, which is a novel approach.
- The paper provides a comprehensive evaluation of the proposed method across multiple datasets, demonstrating its effectiveness in improving dataset credibility and model performance.
- The paper is well-written, making it accessible and understandable to a broad audience, and includes a detailed analysis of the proposed method's effectiveness.
- The authors have made their code publicly available, which enhances the reproducibility and transparency of the research.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper's methodology is primarily based on existing techniques such as the k-NN label clusterability and thresholding, with limited novelty in its approach.
- The paper lacks a detailed discussion on the limitations and potential negative societal impacts of using the proposed method, particularly in terms of dataset quality and model safety.
- The paper's evaluation is limited to a few datasets, which may not fully demonstrate the generalizability of the proposed method across different types of datasets or tasks.
- There is a lack of comparison with other state-of-the-art methods for detecting and correcting label errors in toxicity datasets, which could provide a more robust validation of the proposed method.
- The paper does not adequately address the issue of noisy labels in the datasets, which could affect the reliability and validity of the results.
- The paper's claims about the effectiveness of the proposed method in improving dataset credibility and model performance are not sufficiently supported by empirical evidence.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors provide more details on how the proposed method could be adapted to handle noisy labels in datasets?
- How does the proposed method compare to other state-of-the-art methods for detecting and correcting label errors in toxicity datasets?
- Could the authors provide a more detailed discussion on the potential negative societal impacts of using the proposed method, particularly in terms of dataset quality and model safety?
- How does the proposed method perform when applied to datasets with different types of noise or different levels of noise?
- Could the authors provide more empirical evidence to support the claims made about the effectiveness of the proposed method in improving dataset credibility and model performance?
