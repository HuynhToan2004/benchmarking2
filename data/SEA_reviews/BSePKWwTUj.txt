 **Summary:**
The paper explores the multiobjective stochastic linear bandits (MOSLB) under lexicographic ordering, focusing on minimizing the regret for multiple objectives simultaneously. The authors propose two algorithms, STE2LO and MTE2LO, which aim to improve upon the regret bounds of existing methods. The first algorithm, STE2LO, uses a warm-up strategy to achieve a sub-optimal regret bound, while the second, MTE2LO, employs a more refined approach to achieve near-optimal regret bounds. The paper also discusses the theoretical aspects and experimental validations of these algorithms, comparing their performance against existing methods.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, clear, and easy to follow, with a clear motivation and good presentation of the results.
- The authors propose a new algorithm for the multiobjective stochastic linear bandits under lexicographic ordering, which is the first of its kind to the best of the authors' knowledge.
- The paper includes both theoretical analysis and numerical experiments, demonstrating the effectiveness of the proposed algorithms.
- The authors provide a thorough analysis of the regret bound for their algorithm, showing that it is near-optimal and achieving a regret bound of O(d√T), which is almost optimal for the problem.
- The paper is well-positioned within the literature, discussing the existing works and how the proposed algorithm improves upon them.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper could benefit from a more detailed explanation of the algorithm, particularly how it differs from existing methods like PF-LEX.
- The experimental setup could be improved to include more diverse scenarios, such as varying the number of objectives, the number of arms, and the difficulty of the problem.
- The paper could provide more insights into the parameter λ, which is crucial for the performance of the algorithm.
- There is a lack of discussion on the limitations of the proposed algorithms, which could help in understanding their practical applicability.
- The paper could benefit from a more comprehensive literature review, especially in the context of multi-objective bandits and stochastic linear bandits.
- The paper could be more explicit in explaining the technical contributions of the proposed algorithms, particularly how they differ from existing methods.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors elaborate on the technical contributions of their algorithm, particularly how it differs from existing methods like PF-LEX?
- How does the parameter λ affect the performance of the algorithm, and can the authors provide more insights into its practical implications?
- Can the authors discuss the limitations of their proposed algorithms and how these might affect their practical applicability?
- How does the proposed algorithm perform in scenarios where the number of objectives or the number of arms varies?
- Can the authors provide more details on the experimental setup, such as the choice of parameters and the rationale behind them?
- Could the authors clarify the use of certain symbols and equations, particularly those related to the confidence intervals and the filtering mechanism?
