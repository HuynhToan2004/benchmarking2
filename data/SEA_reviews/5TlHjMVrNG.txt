 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a new benchmark, ImageNet-UA, designed to evaluate the robustness of models against unforeseen adversarial attacks. It presents a suite of eighteen differentiable adversarial attacks, which are used to evaluate the robustness of models against a variety of perturbations beyond the standard Lp attacks. The benchmark includes eighteen novel attacks, each with different characteristics and optimization objectives, aimed at improving the evaluation of model robustness against realistic and diverse adversarial threats. The paper also proposes a new metric, Unforeseen Adversarial Accuracy (UA2), to quantify the robustness of models against these attacks. The benchmark and the proposed attacks are expected to provide a more comprehensive and realistic evaluation of model robustness, potentially guiding future research in adversarial robustness.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a new benchmark, ImageNet-UA, which is a significant contribution to the field, providing a more comprehensive and realistic evaluation of model robustness against a variety of adversarial attacks.
- The paper presents a suite of eighteen differentiable adversarial attacks, which are novel and diverse, and could be used to evaluate the robustness of models against realistic and diverse adversarial threats.
- The proposed attacks are differentiable, which is beneficial for optimization and model training, and are not limited by auxiliary generative models, making them applicable across different datasets.
- The paper is well-written, clear, and easy to follow, with a clear and concise presentation of the attacks and their characteristics.
- The proposed attacks are not limited by auxiliary generative models, which makes them applicable across different datasets, and are differentiable, which is beneficial for optimization and model training.
- The paper provides a comprehensive evaluation of various models using the new benchmark, which could help guide future research in adversarial robustness.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed discussion on the novelty of the proposed attacks, particularly in comparison to existing attacks such as those described in the paper "Real-World Adversarial Attacks: A Systematic Survey" by Qiu et al.
- There is a lack of clarity in the definition and role of the adversary distribution in the threat model, which could affect the interpretation and applicability of the results.
- The paper does not discuss the potential negative societal impacts of the work, which could be a significant concern given the implications of adversarial robustness in real-world applications.
