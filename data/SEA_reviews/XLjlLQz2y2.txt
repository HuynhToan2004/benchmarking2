 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel method for constructing graph coresets for Graph Neural Networks (GNNs) by selecting ego-graphs based on their spectral embeddings. The proposed method, Spectral Greedy Graph Coreset (SGGC), decomposes the coreset selection problem into two phases: a coarse selection of widely spread ego graphs and a refined selection to diversify their topologies. This method is evaluated on ten datasets, showing improvements in node classification tasks compared to other coreset methods. The theoretical analysis supports the method's effectiveness, and the paper includes extensive experiments and ablation studies to validate its performance. However, the paper's assumptions and the generalizability of the results to other types of GNNs are questioned.

**Strengths:**
- The paper addresses a significant and practical problem in the field of graph neural networks (GNNs) by proposing a novel method for constructing graph coresets, which is both theoretically sound and empirically validated.
- The proposed method, Spectral Greedy Graph Coreset (SGGC), is novel and theoretically justified, providing a new approach to selecting ego-graphs based on their spectral embeddings, which is a departure from traditional methods.
- The paper is well-written, clear, and easy to follow, with a logical structure that facilitates understanding of the proposed method and its implications.
- The experiments are comprehensive and convincing, demonstrating the effectiveness of the proposed method across various datasets and scenarios.
- The paper includes a detailed theoretical analysis and proofs, which support the claims made and contribute to the understanding of the proposed method.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper's assumptions, particularly Assumption 1, are not well justified, and their applicability in real-world scenarios is questionable.
- The paper's focus on message-passing GNNs may limit its applicability to other types of GNNs, such as non-message-passing GNNs, which are increasingly popular.
- The paper lacks a thorough discussion on the limitations of the proposed method, particularly in terms of its generalizability and scalability.
- There are several typographical and grammatical errors throughout the paper, which detract from its professionalism and clarity.
- The paper's experimental results, while convincing, are limited to a specific set of GNN architectures, which may not generalize well to other types of GNNs.
- The paper does not adequately address the computational complexity of the proposed method, which could be a significant limitation in practical applications.
