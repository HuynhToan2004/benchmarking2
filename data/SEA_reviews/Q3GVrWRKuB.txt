 **Summary:**
The paper introduces VulBench, a comprehensive vulnerability benchmark that aggregates data from CTF challenges, real-world applications, and existing datasets. It evaluates the performance of various Large Language Models (LLMs) and deep learning-based models against state-of-the-art static analyzers, revealing that several LLMs outperform traditional deep learning approaches in vulnerability detection. The dataset, which includes detailed annotations for each vulnerable function, is used to evaluate the effectiveness of LLMs in vulnerability detection, highlighting the potential of these models in enhancing software security. However, the paper's findings are somewhat limited by the complexity of the datasets used and the generalization of the LLM's performance to more complex, real-world scenarios.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces VulBench, a comprehensive vulnerability benchmark that aggregates high-quality data from a wide range of CTF challenges and real-world applications, with annotations for each vulnerable function detailing the vulnerability type and its root cause.
- The paper is well-organized, with a clear introduction and methodology, and includes a variety of LLMs and deep learning-based models, as well as static analyzers, for evaluation.
- The dataset is useful for researchers and practitioners in the field of software security, providing detailed annotations for each vulnerable function that can aid in understanding the vulnerabilities and their root causes.
- The paper provides a comprehensive evaluation of LLMs in vulnerability detection, setting a benchmark against state-of-the-art deep learning models and static analyzers.
- The introduction of VulBench addresses the quality issues prevalent in existing datasets, offering a comprehensive dataset for more accurate evaluations, along with new natural language description for the vulnerabilities.
- The paper is well-written and easy to follow, with clear and understandable figures and tables.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper's findings might not generalize well to more complex, real-world scenarios due to the simplicity of the datasets used.
- The paper does not adequately address the limitations of using LLMs for vulnerability detection, such as the need for more complex datasets and the potential for overfitting to specific vulnerability types.
- The paper does not provide sufficient details on the CTF dataset, such as the number of vulnerabilities and their types, which could affect the reliability of the results.
- The paper does not discuss the potential negative societal impact of using LLMs for vulnerability detection, such as the risk of misuse or the ethical implications of using these models to exploit vulnerabilities.
- The paper lacks a detailed discussion on the limitations of the LLM approach to vulnerability detection, including the potential for false positives and the need for more complex datasets to accurately evaluate the models.
- The paper does not adequately compare the LLM approach with other state-of-the-art methods for vulnerability detection, such as fuzzing or symbolic execution.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors provide more details on the CTF dataset, such as the number of vulnerabilities and their types, to enhance the reliability of the results?
- How do the authors address the potential for overfitting in the LLMs, especially given the simplicity of the datasets used?
- What are the potential negative societal impacts of using LLMs for vulnerability detection, and how do the authors address these concerns?
- How do the authors plan to ensure the accuracy and reliability of the LLM-based vulnerability detection systems, especially in real-world scenarios where the complexity of the vulnerabilities might be higher?
- Could the authors discuss the limitations of using LLMs for vulnerability detection and suggest potential areas for future research to address these limitations?
- How do the authors plan to ensure the generalizability of the findings to more complex, real-world scenarios, and what steps are being taken to address the potential for false positives in the LLM-based vulnerability detection systems?
