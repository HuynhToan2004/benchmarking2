 **Summary:**
The paper explores the implicit bias of stochastic gradient descent (SGD) in deep linear networks with L2 regularization, focusing on matrix completion tasks. It demonstrates that SGD has a non-zero probability of jumping from a higher rank critical point to a lower rank one, but the probability of jumping back is zero. This behavior is attributed to the low-rank bias of SGD, which is particularly useful in matrix completion. The study also shows that SGD tends to avoid rank-overestimating minima by continuing training for a sufficient amount of time. The paper presents theoretical results and numerical experiments to support these findings, albeit with some concerns regarding the generalizability of the results to non-linear networks and the practical implications of the findings.
