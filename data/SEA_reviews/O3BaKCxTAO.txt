 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel method for predicting stabilization parameters in the SUPG method using a convolutional neural network (ConvStabNet). The network takes inputs such as the diffusion coefficient, convection velocity, mesh size, and the norm of the gradient of the numerical solution, and predicts the stabilization parameters for each cell. The method is evaluated on several examples and is shown to improve upon existing methods like VarNet and AIStab-FEM. However, the paper suffers from several issues including a lack of clear motivation for the method, inadequate justification for the choice of inputs, and a lack of comparison with more relevant and recent works. The paper also lacks clarity in its presentation and the methodology, which makes it difficult to understand the technical details and the contributions of the work.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written and easy to follow, making it accessible to a broad audience.
- The proposed method of using a convolutional neural network (CNN) to predict stabilization parameters for the SUPG method is innovative and has the potential to improve the accuracy and efficiency of the SUPG method.
- The paper presents several examples and demonstrates that the proposed method outperforms existing methods such as VarNet and AIStab-FEM.
- The use of a CNN to predict stabilization parameters is a novel approach that could be useful for other applications in the field.
- The paper provides a clear description of the proposed method, including a schematic representation in Figure 1, which aids in understanding the methodology.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a clear motivation for the method, particularly in explaining why predicting stabilization parameters is necessary and how it improves the SUPG method.
- The choice of inputs for the CNN is not well justified, and it is unclear why certain inputs were chosen over others.
- The paper does not compare the proposed method with the most relevant and recent works, such as those using a similar approach to predict stabilization parameters for the SUPG method.
- The paper does not discuss the limitations of the proposed method, which is crucial for understanding its applicability and effectiveness in different scenarios.
- The paper suffers from several typographical and formatting errors, which detract from its professional presentation.
- The paper lacks clarity in its presentation, particularly in the technical details and the methodology, which makes it difficult to understand the contributions of the work.
- The paper does not provide sufficient details on the training and implementation of the CNN, which are crucial for reproducibility and understanding the method's effectiveness.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors provide a more detailed explanation of why predicting stabilization parameters is necessary and how it improves the SUPG method?
- How were the inputs for the CNN chosen, and what justification can be provided for these choices?
- Why was the comparison limited to VarNet and AIStab-FEM, and what are the reasons for not comparing with more relevant and recent works?
- Could the authors clarify the role and impact of the crosswind derivative term in the loss function, and how it contributes to the method's effectiveness?
- How does the proposed method perform in scenarios where the diffusion coefficient is not small, and what are the implications of this for the method's applicability?
- Could the authors provide more details on the training and implementation of the CNN, including the training data, the training process, and the evaluation metrics used?
