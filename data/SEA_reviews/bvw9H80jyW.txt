 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel unsupervised skill discovery method named Controversial multi-objectives Skill Discovery (ComSD), which addresses the challenge of balancing behavioral quality and diversity in robot behavior discovery. ComSD employs a contrastive learning approach to enhance the estimation of skill-conditioned entropy and proposes a Skill-based Multi-objective Weighting (SMW) mechanism to dynamically balance exploration and exploitation. The method is evaluated on various domains, demonstrating state-of-the-art performance in skill combination and skill finetuning tasks. However, concerns are raised about the clarity of the paper, the lack of novelty in the use of contrastive learning and the SMW mechanism, and the need for a more robust experimental validation.

**Strengths:**
- The paper addresses an important and relevant problem in the field of unsupervised skill discovery, focusing on balancing behavioral quality and diversity.
- The proposed method, ComSD, is well-motivated and provides a reasonable solution to the challenge of balancing exploration and exploitation in skill discovery.
- The paper is well-written, making it easy to follow, and includes comprehensive evaluations that demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed method.
- The use of contrastive learning for skill-conditioned entropy estimation and the Skill-based Multi-objective Weighting (SMW) mechanism for balancing exploration and exploitation are innovative approaches.
- The method achieves state-of-the-art performance on skill combination and skill finetuning tasks, demonstrating its effectiveness.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks clarity in some sections, particularly in the explanation of the contrastive learning approach and the role of the SMW mechanism.
- There is a lack of novelty in the use of contrastive learning and the SMW mechanism, as similar methods have been previously explored in other works.
- The experimental results do not convincingly demonstrate the superiority of the proposed method over existing baselines, especially in the skill finetuning task.
- The paper suffers from inconsistencies in the presentation of results and figures, which could confuse the reader.
- The paper does not sufficiently discuss the limitations of the proposed method, which could provide valuable insights for future research.
- The paper's reliance on specific baselines and its comparison with other methods like CIC and APS could be seen as unfair or incomplete.

**Questions:**
- Could you clarify the role and operation of the contrastive learning approach and the SMW mechanism in the context of the proposed method?
- How does the paper address the issue of balancing exploration and exploitation in skill discovery, and what are the specific advantages of the proposed method over existing approaches?
- Can you provide more details on the experimental setup, such as the specifics of the state and skill vectors used, and the hyperparameters tuned for each method?
- How does the paper ensure the fairness and completeness of the comparisons made with other methods, especially in terms of the baselines used and the evaluation metrics employed?
- In the context of the skill finetuning task, how does the proposed method perform in comparison to other methods that do not explicitly optimize for skill diversity?
- Could you address the concerns regarding the clarity and consistency of the paper, particularly in the presentation of results and figures?
