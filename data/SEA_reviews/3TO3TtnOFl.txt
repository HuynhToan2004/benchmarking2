 **Summary:**
The paper introduces BTR (Binary Token Representations), a novel approach to accelerate retrieval-augmented language models by using binary token representations for retrieved passages. BTR precomputes token representations for passages, significantly reducing computation during inference. This is achieved through a combination of calibration techniques and training objectives that restore performance. The method also includes offline and runtime compression techniques to reduce storage requirements. The approach has been evaluated on five knowledge-intensive NLP tasks, demonstrating a 4x acceleration in state-of-the-art inference while reducing storage by over 100x. Despite the performance improvements, the paper is criticized for its limited evaluation scope and the potential loss of accuracy due to binary token representations.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel approach to accelerating retrieval-augmented language models (LMs) by using binary token representations (BTR) for retrieved passages, which significantly reduces computation during inference.
- The BTR method includes calibration techniques and training objectives that restore performance, and it also includes offline and runtime compression techniques to reduce storage requirements.
- The paper demonstrates a 4x acceleration in state-of-the-art inference while reducing storage by over 100x, and the method is evaluated on five knowledge-intensive NLP tasks.
- The paper is well-written, easy to follow, and includes a detailed description of the method and its implementation, making it accessible to a broad audience.
- The experiments show that the proposed method accelerates state-of-the-art inference by up to 4x and reduces storage by over 100x while maintaining over 95% task performance.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper only evaluates the proposed method on five knowledge-intensive NLP tasks, which limits the generalizability of the findings.
- The use of binary token representations for retrieved passages may lead to a loss of accuracy, and the paper does not adequately address this issue.
- The paper does not discuss the potential loss of accuracy due to the use of binary token representations, which could be a significant drawback for some applications.
- The paper does not include a comparison with other retrieval-augmented LM methods, which could provide a more comprehensive evaluation of the proposed method.
- The paper's evaluation metrics are limited to EM and SP, and it does not include other metrics such as F1 scores which are commonly used in NLP tasks.
- The paper does not provide a detailed discussion on the potential negative societal impacts of the proposed method, which is a significant omission.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors provide more details on the loss of accuracy due to the use of binary token representations and how this affects the performance of the model?
- How does the proposed method compare with other retrieval-augmented LM methods, particularly in terms of accuracy and efficiency?
- What are the potential negative societal impacts of the proposed method, and how does the paper address these concerns?
- Could the authors provide a more detailed discussion on the limitations of the proposed method and potential areas for future research?
- How does the proposed method handle the retrieval of passages, and what are the implications for the performance and accuracy of the model?
