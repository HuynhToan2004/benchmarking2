 **Summary:**
The paper investigates the application of bandit algorithms to optimize the relaxation parameter in the Successive Over-Relaxation (SOR) method for solving linear systems. It explores both deterministic and semi-stochastic settings, providing theoretical guarantees on the regret of the bandit algorithms. The authors propose using a bandit algorithm to sequentially choose parameters for a sequence of linear systems, aiming to minimize the total number of iterations. The paper also discusses the practical implications of these theoretical findings, suggesting potential improvements in computational efficiency in scientific computing applications. However, the paper is critiqued for its limited scope, as it only addresses a specific type of linear system and does not discuss broader implications or potential applications beyond its specific setting.
