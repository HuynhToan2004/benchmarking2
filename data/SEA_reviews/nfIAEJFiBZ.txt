 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel exploration strategy in reinforcement learning (RL) by integrating Langevin Monte Carlo (LMC) with Least Squares Value Iteration (LSVI), termed LMC-LSVI. This method directly samples the Q function from its posterior distribution, aiming to improve exploration in deep RL settings. Theoretical analysis is provided for the linear MDP setting, demonstrating a regret bound of O(d3/2H3/2T). Empirical evaluations are conducted in the RiverSwim environment and Atari games, showing comparable or better performance than state-of-the-art methods. The paper also discusses the practical implementation of LMC-LSVI in deep RL, specifically using the Adam optimizer, and provides a theoretical analysis of the proposed method.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel exploration strategy using LMC-LSVI, which is a significant contribution to the field of reinforcement learning.
- Theoretical analysis is provided for the linear MDP setting, which is a strong theoretical foundation for the proposed method.
- The paper is well-written and easy to follow, making it accessible to a broad audience.
- Empirical results show that the proposed method achieves comparable or better performance than state-of-the-art methods, demonstrating its effectiveness.
- The method is simple to implement and can be used in high-dimensional RL tasks, which is a practical advantage.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed comparison with other exploration strategies, particularly those that use LMC, such as Langevin DQN, which might affect the perceived novelty and effectiveness of the proposed method.
- The theoretical analysis is limited to the linear MDP setting, which might not generalize well to more complex or practical scenarios.
- The paper does not provide a clear comparison of the computational efficiency or sample complexity of the proposed method with other existing methods.
- The empirical results are not convincingly superior to state-of-the-art methods, and the improvements are sometimes marginal or not statistically significant.
- There is a lack of discussion on the limitations of the proposed method and its applicability in different scenarios, which could affect the understanding and adoption of the method.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors clarify the novelty of the proposed method compared to existing methods that use LMC in similar settings?
- How does the proposed method perform in non-linear MDPs or other more complex environments, and what are the potential challenges or limitations in these scenarios?
- Could the authors provide a more detailed comparison of the computational efficiency and sample complexity of the proposed method with other existing methods?
- How does the proposed method handle the exploration-exploitation trade-off in practical scenarios, and what are the empirical results that demonstrate its effectiveness in this regard?
- Can the authors discuss the limitations of the proposed method and suggest areas for future research to address these limitations?
