 **Summary:**
The paper introduces Density-Softmax, a novel method for uncertainty estimation and robustness in deep learning models, particularly focusing on the test-time efficiency of deep ensembles. It employs a Lipschitz-constrained feature extractor and a density function built on normalizing flows to achieve robust generalization and uncertainty estimation. The method aims to balance computational efficiency and performance by using a sampling-free approach that does not require data augmentation or post-hoc calibration. The paper provides theoretical guarantees and empirical evaluations demonstrating competitive performance against state-of-the-art methods. However, concerns are raised regarding the incremental nature of the work, the lack of clarity in theoretical proofs, and the need for more comprehensive comparisons with existing methods.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel method for uncertainty estimation and robustness in deep learning models, focusing on test-time efficiency.
- The method is designed to be computationally efficient, which is crucial for real-time applications.
- The paper provides theoretical guarantees for the proposed method, enhancing its credibility.
- The empirical evaluation is comprehensive, including comparisons with state-of-the-art methods and a thorough ablation study.
- The approach is simple and can be easily integrated into existing models, making it practical for real-world applications.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a clear distinction from existing methods, particularly in terms of its theoretical contributions and practical advantages over existing methods.
- There is a need for more detailed comparisons with other methods, especially in terms of computational efficiency and robustness under different conditions.
- The paper does not adequately address the training costs associated with the proposed method, which could be a significant drawback for practical implementation.
- The theoretical proofs and the clarity of the paper need improvement, particularly in sections where the proofs and theorems are presented.
- The paper does not sufficiently discuss the limitations of the proposed method, which is essential for understanding its applicability and potential drawbacks.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors provide a more detailed comparison with existing methods, especially in terms of computational efficiency and robustness under different conditions?
- How does the proposed method perform in terms of training costs compared to other methods?
- Can the authors clarify the theoretical proofs and theorems presented in the paper to improve understanding and reproducibility?
- What are the specific advantages of the proposed method over existing methods in terms of practical applications and real-world scenarios?
- How does the method handle different types of distributional shifts, and what are the implications for the model's performance in such scenarios?
- Can the authors provide more detailed results and comparisons in the appendix to supplement the main paper?
