 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel method for learning diffusion models on non-Euclidean manifolds using a diffusion process defined on the manifold. This approach leverages the eigenfunctions of the Laplace-Beltrami operator to define a coordinate system on the manifold, allowing for the representation of functions on manifolds. The method is demonstrated to be effective in various settings, including fixed manifolds, manifolds with different parametrizations, and manifolds with different shapes. The paper includes extensive empirical evaluations, showing that the proposed method outperforms existing approaches in terms of performance metrics such as coverage and minimum matching distance. However, the paper has been critiqued for its lack of novelty, as it closely follows the diffusion model of Zhuang et al., 2023, and for not sufficiently addressing the computational challenges associated with learning on manifolds.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written and easy to follow, with clear explanations and detailed descriptions of the methodology.
- The methodology is innovative, extending the diffusion model to non-Euclidean manifolds, which is a significant advancement in the field.
- Extensive empirical evaluations are conducted, demonstrating the effectiveness of the proposed method across various datasets and settings.
- The paper includes a thorough discussion on related work, positioning the research within the existing literature.
- The methodology is flexible and can be applied to different types of manifolds, including those with different parametrizations and shapes.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a clear discussion on the limitations of the proposed method, which could help in understanding its applicability and potential drawbacks.
- The novelty of the method is questioned as it closely follows the diffusion model of Zhuang et al., 2023, with only minor modifications.
- The computational complexity of learning on manifolds is not adequately addressed, and the paper does not provide a detailed analysis of the computational efficiency of the proposed method.
- The paper does not sufficiently distinguish its method from existing approaches, particularly those that use similar techniques such as spectral methods and Laplacian eigenfunctions.
- The paper lacks a comprehensive discussion on the theoretical underpinnings of the method, particularly in terms of how the manifold's intrinsic geometry affects the learning process.
- The paper does not adequately address the generalization of the method to more complex manifolds, such as those with higher dimensions or more complex shapes.

**Questions:**
- How does the proposed method handle the computational complexity of learning on manifolds, particularly in terms of the computational cost and memory requirements?
- Can the method be applied to more complex manifolds, such as those with higher dimensions or more complex shapes?
- What are the specific challenges of extending the method to more complex manifolds, and how might these be addressed?
- How does the method compare to existing approaches that use similar techniques, such as spectral methods and Laplacian eigenfunctions?
- Can the method be adapted to handle non-smooth or noisy data, and how might this affect the learning process?
- How does the method perform in terms of generalization to unseen manifolds, and what steps are taken to ensure the model's robustness in such settings?
- Could the authors provide more detailed insights into the theoretical foundations of the method, particularly in terms of how the manifold's intrinsic geometry influences the learning process?
