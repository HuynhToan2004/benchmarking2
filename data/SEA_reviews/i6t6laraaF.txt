 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel POI-based traffic generation model, named TG-SCR, which integrates POI data and road network data to generate traffic flow patterns. The model comprises a graph reconstruction module and a POI supervised contrastive module, aiming to address the challenges of bias and anomalous structures in real-world road networks. The proposed methodology, including a k-NN graph builder and a k-NN graph aggregator, is designed to reconstruct the original roadnet graph into a k-NN graph and revise POI features. Extensive experiments on real-world datasets demonstrate the model's superiority over existing baselines. However, concerns were raised regarding the clarity of the motivation behind the proposed method, the complexity of the graph reconstruction module, and the lack of detailed comparative analysis with state-of-the-art methods.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written and easy to follow, making it accessible to readers.
- The motivation behind the proposed method is clear, and the problem is well-defined.
- The paper introduces a novel method for traffic generation using POI data, which is a significant contribution to the field.
- The methodology includes a graph reconstruction module and a POI supervised contrastive module, which are innovative and contribute to the understanding of traffic generation.
- The paper provides a detailed explanation of the proposed method, including a clear description of the loss function and the model's components.
- The experimental results show that the proposed method outperforms existing baselines, demonstrating the effectiveness of the approach.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed comparison with state-of-the-art methods, which is crucial for establishing the novelty and effectiveness of the proposed method.
- The complexity of the graph reconstruction module is high, and its impact on the overall performance and efficiency of the model needs further analysis.
- The motivation behind the proposed method is not clear, and the paper does not adequately address why the graph reconstruction module is necessary.
- The paper does not provide sufficient details on the experimental setup, such as the size and characteristics of the datasets used, which could affect the reproducibility and generalizability of the results.
- The paper does not discuss the limitations of the proposed method, which could help in understanding the scope and applicability of the findings.
- The paper could benefit from more extensive comparisons with other methods, including those that use different types of data or different approaches to traffic generation.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors provide a more detailed comparison with state-of-the-art methods to establish the novelty and effectiveness of the proposed method?
- How does the graph reconstruction module impact the overall performance and efficiency of the model?
- Can the authors clarify the motivation behind the proposed method, especially the necessity of the graph reconstruction module?
- How are the hyperparameters tuned, and what are the specific values used for each dataset?
- What are the characteristics of the datasets used in the experiments, and how do they affect the results?
- Could the authors discuss the limitations of the proposed method and suggest areas for future research?
