 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel method for unsupervised domain adaptation (UDA) under label shift conditions, focusing on aligning conditional feature distributions to address the challenges posed by label shifts. The proposed method, Conditional Adversarial Support Alignment (CASA), utilizes a conditional symmetric support divergence (CSSD) to minimize the divergence between source and target domain feature representations. Theoretical justification is provided for this approach, which is supported by empirical results demonstrating the method's effectiveness on various UDA benchmark tasks. The paper also discusses the limitations of existing methods that rely on marginal support alignment and argues that conditional support alignment is more robust to feature misalignment.

**Strengths:**

- The methodology is supported by a theoretical analysis, which provides a solid foundation for the proposed approach.
