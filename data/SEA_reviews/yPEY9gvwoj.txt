 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel framework for generating amicable perturbations to modify data points in a manner that optimizes both the effort required and the desirability of the modified classifications. The approach aims to change the true class probabilities, differentiating it from adversarial attacks and counterfactuals. The methodology involves a two-step process: first, generating perturbations that optimize a weighted sum of statistical distance to the target set and effort/cost measure, subject to the constraint that the perturbations are actionable. Second, verifying that the perturbations change the true class probabilities, which is crucial for ensuring the perturbations are not merely changing the classifier's prediction without affecting the underlying data. The paper provides theoretical and empirical evidence to support the effectiveness of this approach, comparing it to existing methods such as counterfactuals and adversarial attacks.

- The paper introduces a novel approach to generating amicable perturbations, which is a significant contribution to the field of machine learning.
- The methodology is well-articulated, with a clear distinction from adversarial attacks and counterfactuals, and the paper provides a detailed explanation of the verification procedure.
- The paper is well-written, with clear explanations of the methodology and the results, making it accessible to a broad audience.
- The empirical results demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed method, showing that amicable perturbations outperform counterfactuals in all test scenarios.
- The paper addresses a practical and important problem, which is the generation of amicable perturbations to modify data points in a manner that optimizes both the effort required and the desirability of the modified classifications.

**Weaknesses:**
