 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel method for multilingual visual speech recognition (VSR) by leveraging a self-supervised model called mAV-HuBERT to extract visual speech units. These units are then used to train a multilingual VSR system, which shows improved performance over existing methods. The authors propose a curriculum learning strategy to enhance training efficiency and reduce computational costs. The methodology includes a pre-training stage where the model is trained on a multilingual dataset and a fine-tuning stage where it is adapted to specific languages. Despite its innovative approach, the paper has been criticized for its limited evaluation on a single dataset and the lack of comprehensive comparisons with state-of-the-art methods.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, clear, and easy to follow, with a clear motivation and a well-designed methodology.
- The introduction of visual speech units as a new approach to multilingual visual speech recognition (VSR) is innovative and potentially impactful.
- The use of curriculum learning to enhance training efficiency and reduce computational costs is a notable contribution to the field.
- The paper includes a comprehensive evaluation of the proposed method, with results showing improvements over existing methods.
- The paper is significant in its application to low-resource languages, which could have a substantial impact on practical applications.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed comparison with state-of-the-art methods, particularly in terms of the number of training hours and the size of the training dataset.
- The evaluation is limited to a single dataset, which raises concerns about the generalizability of the findings.
- There is a lack of discussion on the limitations of the proposed method and potential negative societal impacts, such as privacy concerns.
- The paper does not provide sufficient details on the training process, such as the number of epochs and the training time, which are crucial for reproducibility and validation of the results.
- The paper does not adequately address the potential issues with the use of multilingual HuBERT for obtaining visual speech units, such as the potential loss of language-specific information.
- The paper's reliance on a single model for multiple languages raises concerns about the effectiveness of this approach, especially in high-resource languages.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors provide more details on the training process, such as the number of epochs and the training time, to enhance reproducibility and validation of the results?
- How does the proposed method perform on other datasets, particularly those with different characteristics or in different languages?
- Could the authors discuss the potential limitations of their approach, such as the potential loss of language-specific information in the visual speech units?
- How does the proposed method compare in terms of computational efficiency and training time to other methods, especially those that do not use visual speech units?
- Could the authors provide more details on the pre-training and fine-tuning stages, including the specific datasets and models used?
- How does the proposed method address the issue of the curse of multilinguality, especially in high-resource languages?
