 **Summary:**
The paper introduces TrojFair, a model-agnostic Trojan fairness attack designed to manipulate deep learning models by altering their behavior towards specific groups based on tainted inputs. TrojFair operates by injecting triggers into the training data, affecting the model's fairness metrics by skewing predictions towards the target group. The attack is designed to be stealthy and resilient against existing fairness auditing detectors, as the model's performance on clean inputs remains unaffected. The paper presents TrojFair as a significant advancement in understanding the vulnerabilities of fair deep learning models to malicious attacks, especially in high-stakes sectors like healthcare and finance. However, concerns were raised about the novelty of the approach and the clarity of the threat model, particularly regarding the accessibility of the attacker to the training data and the model's architecture.

**Strengths:**
- The paper addresses an important and relevant problem in the field of fairness attacks, which is crucial for understanding the vulnerabilities of deep learning models.
- The proposed method is model-agnostic, making it applicable across different model architectures, enhancing its practicality and relevance.
- The paper is well-written, making it easy to follow, and provides a clear and detailed explanation of the proposed method.
- The experimental setup is robust, with a variety of datasets and models used, and the results are thoroughly analyzed and discussed, contributing to the paper's credibility.
- The paper introduces a novel approach by integrating fairness-attack transferable optimization, which enhances the stealthiness and effectiveness of the attack.
- The potential application of the proposed attack in high-stakes sectors like healthcare and finance is highlighted, emphasizing the relevance and impact of the research.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed comparison with existing fairness attacks, which could have provided a clearer picture of the novelty and advantages of the proposed method.
- The threat model is not clearly defined, particularly in terms of the level of access the attacker has to the training data and the model's architecture, which could affect the practicality and effectiveness of the attack.
- The paper does not sufficiently address the potential defenses against the proposed attack, which could limit the comprehensiveness of the research.
- The motivation behind the attack and the potential real-world applications are not convincingly argued, which could affect the relevance and impact of the research.
- The paper does not provide a comprehensive evaluation of the attack's effectiveness against different types of models, which could limit the generalizability of the findings.
- The paper does not sufficiently discuss the limitations of the proposed method, which could help in understanding the scope and applicability of the research.

**Questions:**
- Could you clarify the threat model, specifically the level of access the attacker has to the training data and the model's architecture?
- How does the proposed attack compare in terms of effectiveness and stealthiness with existing fairness attacks and backdoor attacks?
- What are the potential defenses against the proposed attack, and how does the attack perform under these conditions?
- Could you provide a more detailed explanation of the motivation behind the attack and the potential real-world applications?
- How does the proposed attack perform against different types of models, and what are the specific challenges and opportunities in each case?
- Can you discuss the limitations of the proposed method and how these might affect the practical applicability and generalizability of the findings?
