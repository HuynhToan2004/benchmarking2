 **Summary:**
The paper introduces DiLu, a framework for autonomous driving that integrates a memory module, a reasoning module, and a reflection module to facilitate decision-making based on common-sense knowledge and experience accumulation. The system uses a large language model (LLM) to generate prompts from scenario descriptions and past experiences, which are then used to make driving decisions. The memory module stores experiences from past scenarios, and the reflection module corrects unsafe decisions, enhancing the system's learning capabilities. The framework is tested in the Highway-env simulation environment, showing improved performance compared to reinforcement learning-based methods. However, concerns were raised about the complexity of the system, the lack of detailed experimental results, and the generalizability of the approach to real-world scenarios.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel framework for autonomous driving that integrates a memory module, a reasoning module, and a reflection module, which allows the system to learn from past experiences and improve decision-making.
- The framework's approach to decision-making is novel and innovative, using a large language model (LLM) to generate prompts from scenario descriptions and past experiences, which is a significant advancement in the field.
- The paper is well-written, clear, and easy to follow, with a comprehensive experimental setup and evaluation that demonstrates the effectiveness of the proposed methods.
- The experiments show that the proposed framework can achieve good performance in closed-loop driving tasks and can generalize to new environments, indicating the potential for practical applications in autonomous driving systems.
- The paper is the first to leverage knowledge-driven capability in decision-making for autonomous vehicles, which is a significant contribution to the field.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed description of the memory module, particularly how it is initialized and how it stores and retrieves experiences, which could affect the reproducibility and understanding of the system.
- The paper does not provide sufficient details on the implementation of the LLM, such as the specific model used and the prompts given to the LLM, which could impact the reproducibility and generalizability of the results.
- The paper does not adequately address the generalizability of the approach to real-world scenarios, which is crucial for the practical application of the system.
- There is a lack of detailed experimental results, such as the number of training and test scenarios used, the specific driving tasks, and the performance metrics used, which could limit the evaluation of the system's effectiveness.
- The paper does not discuss the limitations of the proposed approach, such as the complexity of the system, the potential for hallucinations in the LLM's responses, and the scalability of the system to larger and more complex scenarios.
- The paper does not compare the proposed framework with other state-of-the-art methods, which could help in understanding the advantages and disadvantages of the proposed approach.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors provide more details on the initialization and operation of the memory module, including how it stores and retrieves experiences?
- How does the system handle scenarios where the LLM generates unsafe decisions, and how is this corrected in the reflection module?
- What specific LLM is used in the system, and how are the prompts given to the LLM determined?
- How does the system handle scenarios with a large number of vehicles, such as city driving scenarios, and what modifications are necessary for such scenarios?
- Could the authors provide more detailed experimental results, including the number of training and test scenarios used, the specific driving tasks, and the performance metrics used?
- How does the system handle scenarios where the LLM generates hallucinations, and what steps are taken to mitigate this issue?
- Could the authors discuss the limitations of the proposed approach and how these might affect the practical application of the system?
