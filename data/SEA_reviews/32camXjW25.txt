 **Summary:**
The paper investigates the issue of feature correlation in neural networks, particularly in imbalanced classification scenarios, where the class distribution is skewed. The authors propose a novel framework called Whitening-Net, which incorporates ZCA whitening before the linear classifier to address this problem. They introduce two new modules, GRBS and BET, to enhance the stability of batch covariance and improve the convergence of the whitening operation. The paper provides empirical evidence of the framework's effectiveness through experiments on various datasets, including CIFAR-LT-10/100, ImageNet-LT, and iNaturalist-LT. The methodology aims to normalize and decorrelate features to mitigate degenerate solutions caused by high linear dependence among features, thereby improving the model's performance in imbalanced classification scenarios.

**Strengths:**
- The paper addresses an important and relevant problem in machine learning, specifically the issue of feature correlation in imbalanced classification, which is crucial for the development of robust and effective machine learning models.
- The proposed methodology is simple, intuitive, and well-motivated, with clear explanations of the underlying principles and their implications.
- The paper is well-organized, with a clear structure and comprehensive experimental validation, including detailed results and analysis that support the claims made.
- The proposed method is novel and innovative, providing a new approach to handling feature correlation in imbalanced classification, which is a significant contribution to the field.
- The paper is well-written, with a clear presentation of the problem, methodology, and results, making it accessible and understandable to a broad audience.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a thorough comparison with existing methods, particularly those that address similar issues in imbalanced classification, such as DBN and other whitening methods.
- The paper does not sufficiently address the computational complexity introduced by the whitening operation, which could impact the scalability and efficiency of the proposed method.
- There are concerns about the novelty of the proposed method, as it seems to overlap with existing methods like DBN, and the differences are not clearly articulated.
- The paper lacks a comprehensive discussion on the limitations of the proposed method, including its applicability to different types of datasets and its potential impact on model performance.
- The experimental results presented in the paper are not sufficiently robust, with a lack of statistical significance testing and a limited number of datasets used for validation.
- The paper could benefit from a more detailed analysis of the results, particularly in terms of the impact of different parameters and settings on the performance of the model.
