 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a unified framework for reinforcement learning (RL) and imitation learning (IL) through dual RL, which casts various state-of-the-art offline RL and IL algorithms as instances of dual RL with shared structures. This approach allows for the identification of the root causes of performance limitations in prior methods and the development of new methods like ReCOIL for offline IL and f-DVL for offline RL. The framework is evaluated across multiple benchmarks, showing improvements over existing methods. The paper also addresses the limitations of previous methods by relaxing the coverage assumption and introducing a discriminator-free approach.

**Strengths:**
- The paper provides a unified framework for RL and IL, which is a significant contribution to the field.
- The authors have effectively cast several state-of-the-art offline RL and offline IL algorithms as instances of dual RL, highlighting the shared structures and identifying the root causes of performance limitations in prior methods.
- The proposed new methods, such as ReCOIL and f-DVL, show improvements over existing methods in both IL and RL, as demonstrated by extensive evaluations on a suite of simulated robot locomotion and manipulation tasks.
- The paper is well-written and easy to follow, making complex concepts accessible to readers.
- The dual RL approach is a novel perspective that can potentially lead to new insights and methodologies in both RL and IL.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a clear comparison with other methods that also cast RL as dual problems, which could have highlighted the novelty and advantages of the proposed dual RL approach.
- The paper does not provide a detailed analysis of the limitations of the dual RL approach, which could be a significant drawback for understanding the full implications of the proposed framework.
- There is a lack of discussion on the computational complexity and scalability of the dual RL approach, which is crucial for understanding its applicability in more complex or larger-scale scenarios.
- The paper does not sufficiently address the issue of training instability in offline RL methods, which is a known problem in the field.
- The experimental results, while promising, are not convincingly superior to existing methods, particularly in the online RL setting.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors provide a more detailed comparison with other methods that also cast RL as dual problems to highlight the novelty and advantages of the proposed dual RL approach?
- How does the dual RL approach handle the computational complexity and scalability issues, especially in more complex or larger-scale scenarios?
- Can the authors provide a more detailed analysis of the limitations of the dual RL approach, particularly in terms of its applicability and practical implications?
- How does the dual RL approach address the issue of training instability in offline RL methods, and what are the specific benefits of using this approach over other methods?
- Could the authors clarify the experimental results, particularly the discrepancies between the reported results and the actual performance of the algorithms?
- Is there a plan to release the code for the proposed methods to facilitate further research and reproducibility?
