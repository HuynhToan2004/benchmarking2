 **Summary:**
The paper introduces LEGO-Prover, a novel approach to theorem proving that employs a growing library of verified lemmas, referred to as skills, to enhance the capabilities of Large Language Models (LLMs) in theorem proving. The method involves a prover that retrieves and applies skills from the library, and an evolver that generates new skills from problem statements, enhancing the library's scope and applicability. The approach is evaluated on the miniF2F dataset, showing significant improvements over existing methods. The paper also includes an ablation study that demonstrates the effectiveness of the skill library and the evolver in improving the pass rate of LLMs.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, with clear and easy-to-follow explanations, and includes detailed experimental results and ablation studies.
- The approach of using a growing library of verified lemmas to enhance LLMs in theorem proving is novel and innovative, potentially having a significant impact on the field.
- The paper demonstrates the effectiveness of the method through rigorous evaluations on the miniF2F dataset, showing that the proposed method outperforms existing methods.
- The method is well-motivated and the design choices are well-explained, with a focus on improving the performance of LLMs in theorem proving.
- The use of a skill library and an evolver to generate new skills is a novel approach that could potentially enhance the capabilities of LLMs in other applications beyond theorem proving.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed discussion on the limitations and potential negative societal impacts of the proposed method.
- The experimental setup and results could be improved by including more detailed analysis, such as the number of successful skills used in proofs and the time taken for each step in the process.
- The paper does not adequately address the generalizability of the method to other theorem proving datasets or the scalability of the method to more complex problems.
- The paper does not include a comparison with the most recent and relevant methods, such as those using GPT-4, which could affect the perceived effectiveness of the proposed method.
- The paper does not sufficiently discuss the potential biases in the skill library and how they might affect the performance of the LLM.

**Questions:**
- How does the proposed method perform on other theorem proving datasets, and can it be scaled to more complex problems?
- How are the skills selected for inclusion in the skill library, and how does this affect the performance of the LLM?
- Could you provide more details on the experimental setup, such as the number of successful skills used in proofs and the time taken for each step in the process?
- How does the proposed method compare with other recent and relevant methods, such as those using GPT-4?
- How are the skills generated by the evolver verified, and what measures are taken to ensure their accuracy and applicability?
- How does the method handle the generation of skills that are not verified, and what are the implications of this for the performance of the LLM?
