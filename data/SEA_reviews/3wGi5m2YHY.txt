 **Summary:**
The paper introduces FlowHash, a method for audio fingerprinting using a novel approach that integrates normalizing flow within a self-supervised learning framework to generate balanced hash codes. This method aims to distribute vectors in the latent space uniformly, which enhances the retrieval process by accelerating audio search. The paper also addresses the challenges of high dimensionality, scalability, and noise in audio fingerprinting, showing improvements over existing methods like LSH and OT-based methods. Despite these strengths, the paper faces criticism for its lack of comprehensive comparisons with state-of-the-art methods and its reliance on a pre-trained encoder which could limit its applicability in real-world scenarios.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written and easy to follow, with a clear explanation of the proposed method.
- The use of normalizing flow within a self-supervised learning framework is innovative and effectively addresses the challenges of audio fingerprinting.
- The method is empirically demonstrated to be scalable, effective, and efficient in identifying short audio queries, especially in high noise and reverberation levels.
- The authors have conducted extensive experiments to evaluate the robustness and scalability of their approach, providing a detailed analysis of the experimental results.
- The paper introduces a novel application of NF in hash-based indexing, which is significant in the field of audio fingerprinting.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a comprehensive comparison with state-of-the-art methods, particularly in terms of computational efficiency and the ability to handle various audio distortions.
- The reliance on a pre-trained encoder might limit the generalizability of the proposed method in real-world scenarios, especially when dealing with diverse audio content.
- The paper does not sufficiently discuss the limitations of the proposed method, which could affect its practical application.
- There is a lack of detailed discussion on the computational complexity and memory requirements of the proposed method, which could be crucial for practical implementation.
- The paper could benefit from a more comprehensive literature review and a clearer explanation of the novelty of the proposed method compared to existing works.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors provide a more detailed comparison with state-of-the-art methods, particularly in terms of computational efficiency and the ability to handle various audio distortions?
- How does the proposed method perform in scenarios where the audio content is not pre-processed or pre-trained, such as in real-world applications?
- Can the authors discuss the limitations of the proposed method and suggest potential solutions or areas for future research?
- What are the computational complexity and memory requirements of the proposed method, and how do these compare to existing methods?
- Could the authors clarify the novelty of their method compared to existing works, particularly in terms of the application of normalizing flow in audio fingerprinting?
