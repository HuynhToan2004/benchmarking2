 **Summary:**
The paper introduces BindNet, a self-supervised learning approach for protein-ligand binding representation, which employs a Transformer-based interaction module to learn intricate binding patterns from fine-grained interactions. The methodology involves two pre-training tasks: Atomic pairwise Distance map Prediction and Mask Ligand Reconstruction, aimed at comprehensively modeling binding interactions. Extensive experiments demonstrate the superiority of BindNet across various binding tasks, including protein-ligand affinity prediction, virtual screening, and protein-ligand docking. The paper is well-written, clearly articulating the methodology and its application, and includes a comprehensive set of experiments. However, the novelty of the method is questioned, as it appears to be an extension of existing methods with minor modifications.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written and easy to follow, providing a clear and detailed explanation of the proposed method.
- The methodology is innovative, focusing on learning intricate binding patterns from fine-grained interactions, which is a novel approach in the field.
- The paper includes a comprehensive set of experiments that demonstrate the superiority of the proposed method across various binding tasks, including protein-ligand affinity prediction, virtual screening, and protein-ligand docking.
- The methodology is versatile and can be applied to different pre-existing encoders for pockets and ligands, enhancing its applicability.
- The paper is well-structured, with a clear introduction, methodology, and results sections, making it easy for readers to understand and replicate the experiments.

**Weaknesses:**
- The novelty of the method is questioned as it appears to be an extension of existing methods with minor modifications, such as the use of a Transformer-based interaction module and the inclusion of two pre-training tasks.
- The paper lacks a detailed discussion on the limitations and potential negative societal impacts of the proposed method.
- There is a lack of detailed experimental setup and methodological details, such as the specifics of the Transformer-based interaction module and the training details of the pre-trained encoders.
- The paper does not sufficiently compare the proposed method with other self-supervised learning methods, which could have provided a more robust validation of the proposed method.
- The paper could benefit from a more detailed discussion on the computational complexity and the scalability of the proposed method, particularly in terms of the size of the dataset used.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors provide more detailed information on the computational complexity and scalability of the proposed method, especially in terms of the size of the dataset used?
- How does the method perform when applied to different types of proteins and ligands, such as those with different sizes or conformations?
- Could the authors clarify the role and impact of the Transformer-based interaction module on the overall performance of the method?
- What are the specific training details for the pre-trained encoders, and how does this affect the performance of the method?
- How does the proposed method compare with other self-supervised learning methods, particularly in terms of computational efficiency and performance metrics?
- Can the authors provide a more detailed discussion on the limitations and potential negative societal impacts of the proposed method?
