 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel grammar-based semantic parsing method for knowledge-based question-answering (KBQA), utilizing a sequence-to-sequence pre-trained language model (PLM) fine-tuned on a knowledge base query answering benchmark. The approach employs a grammar augmented with candidate expressions to guide the parser in constructing compositional structures and generating KB components. This method achieves state-of-the-art performance on the KqaPro benchmark. The paper also discusses the integration of grammar with PLM and the use of candidate expressions to enhance the parser's performance.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel grammar-based semantic parsing method for KBQA, which is a significant advancement in the field.
- The proposed grammar is well-designed, integrating various types and candidate expressions, which are shown to be effective in reducing the search space and enhancing performance.
- The paper is well-written, with clear explanations of the proposed method and its components, including the grammar rules and candidate expressions.
- The experimental results demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed method, achieving state-of-the-art performance on the KqaPro benchmark.
- The paper provides a detailed explanation of the proposed grammar and its components, which is helpful for understanding the methodology and its implementation.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper does not sufficiently differentiate its method from existing grammar-based semantic parsing methods, particularly in terms of novelty and technical contributions.
- The experimental setup and comparisons with other semantic parsing methods are not comprehensive, lacking comparisons with recent state-of-the-art methods and not including a discussion on the computational cost of the proposed method.
- The paper does not adequately address the scalability of the proposed method, especially in terms of its applicability to larger or more complex knowledge bases.
- The paper lacks a detailed discussion on the limitations of the proposed method, which is crucial for understanding its applicability and potential challenges.
- The paper does not provide code for reproducibility, which is essential for validating the results and allowing others to build upon the work.

**Questions:**
- Can the proposed method be extended to other question answering datasets beyond KBQA, such as those based on encyclopedic knowledge or commonsense reasoning?
- How does the proposed method handle the scalability issue, particularly in terms of its applicability to larger or more complex knowledge bases?
- Could you provide more details on the computational cost of the proposed method, including the time required for decoding and the inference speed?
- How does the proposed method compare with other recent state-of-the-art semantic parsing methods in terms of performance and efficiency?
- Could you clarify the technical contributions of the proposed method compared to existing grammar-based semantic parsing methods?
- How does the proposed method handle the issue of handling unseen expressions during testing, especially if the test set includes expressions that are not covered by the candidate expressions?
