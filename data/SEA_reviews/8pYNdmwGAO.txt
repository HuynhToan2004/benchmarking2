 **Summary:**
The paper introduces EvolMPNN, a novel message-passing neural network designed to learn evolution-aware protein embeddings. It leverages a differentiable evolution-aware aggregation scheme to capture mutation effects on proteins relative to anchor proteins. The model integrates both protein sequence and evolutionary information, which is crucial for predicting mutational effects on homologous protein families. The methodology is supported by theoretical analysis and empirical evaluations across multiple datasets, demonstrating significant improvements in performance and inference speed compared to state-of-the-art methods. However, concerns were raised about the novelty of the approach, the clarity of the writing, and the generalization of the model to different datasets and mutation scenarios.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel method, EvolMPNN, which integrates both protein sequence and evolutionary information, effectively capturing mutation effects on homologous protein families.
- The methodology is supported by a theoretical analysis, which is crucial for understanding the underlying mechanisms of the model.
- Empirical evaluations are conducted across multiple datasets, demonstrating significant improvements in performance and inference speed compared to state-of-the-art methods.
- The paper is well-written, clear, and easy to follow, making it accessible to a broad audience.
- The authors provide code for their method, enhancing reproducibility and facilitating further research.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a clear discussion on the novelty of the proposed method compared to existing methods, particularly in terms of the aggregation scheme and evolution encoding.
- The paper does not adequately address the generalization of the model to different datasets and mutation scenarios, which is crucial for assessing the robustness and applicability of the model.
- The writing and organization of the paper could be improved, particularly in terms of clarity and the logical flow of sections.
- The paper does not sufficiently discuss the limitations of the model, which is essential for understanding the scope and applicability of the findings.
- The experimental setup and baseline comparisons could be strengthened by including more diverse and recent baselines, and by providing more detailed experimental results and ablation studies.

**Questions:**
- How does the proposed method compare to existing methods in terms of computational efficiency and scalability, particularly when dealing with large and complex datasets?
- Can the authors clarify the role and impact of the anchor proteins in the model's performance, and discuss the sensitivity of the model to the choice of anchor proteins?
- How does the model perform when applied to datasets with different mutation scenarios, and what are the implications for the model's generalization and applicability?
- Could the authors provide more details on the experimental setup, including the specifics of the baseline models used and the experimental conditions under which the model was evaluated?
- How does the model handle the integration of different types of data, such as 3D protein structures, and what are the implications for the model's performance and accuracy?
