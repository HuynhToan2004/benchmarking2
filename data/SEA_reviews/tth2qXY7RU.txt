 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel Super Floating Point (SuFP) data type for neural network quantization, designed to address the challenges of large and complex Deep Neural Networks (DNNs). SuFP employs multi-region piecewise quantization and tensor-wise scalable bias to optimize precision for different data regions, effectively capturing dense near-zero data and outliers. The SuFP Processing Element (PE) is designed to use only integer units and shifters, enhancing computational efficiency and energy efficiency. The paper presents extensive experiments demonstrating that SuFP matches or surpasses the accuracy of full-precision FP32 while reducing computational complexity and energy consumption by up to 9.00x and 17.04x, respectively. However, the paper lacks a comprehensive discussion on the limitations of SuFP and its applicability to various neural network architectures, and the hardware implementation details are not thoroughly discussed.
