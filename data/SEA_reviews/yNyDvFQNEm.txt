 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel method for unsupervised clustering of node attributes in a graph, using a combination of Graph Autoencoder (GAE), t-Distributed Stochastic Neighbor Embedding (tSNE), and DBSCAN. The approach aims to enhance clustering by integrating graph structure information into the clustering process, which is a departure from traditional methods that focus on clustering nodes within a graph. The authors demonstrate the method's effectiveness through experiments on both synthetic and real-world datasets, showing that it outperforms baselines in certain scenarios. However, the paper is criticized for its lack of clarity in presentation and the need for more rigorous comparisons with existing methods.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel approach to clustering node attributes using graph structure information, which is a significant departure from traditional methods that focus on clustering nodes within a graph.
- The methodology is well-motivated and the paper is well-written, making it easy to follow and understand.
- The proposed method is shown to be effective in clustering node attributes in both synthetic and real-world datasets, demonstrating its practical applicability.
- The paper provides a detailed description of the methodology, including the use of a graph autoencoder (GAE) for noise reduction, t-SNE for dimensionality reduction, and DBSCAN for clustering.
- The experiments are well-designed and the results are clearly presented, showing the effectiveness of the proposed method compared to baselines.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a thorough comparison with existing methods, particularly in the area of graph clustering, which limits the evaluation of the proposed method's effectiveness.
- The presentation of the paper is not clear and could benefit from better organization and clarity.
- The paper does not provide a detailed analysis of the computational complexity of the proposed method, which is crucial for understanding its scalability and efficiency.
- The paper does not adequately address the scalability of the proposed method, which is a significant concern given the potential complexity of the problem.
- The paper does not include a detailed discussion on the limitations of the proposed method, which could help in understanding its applicability and potential drawbacks.
- The paper could benefit from more detailed descriptions of the datasets used in the experiments, including their characteristics and the rationale behind their selection.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors provide a more detailed comparison with existing methods in the area of graph clustering to better evaluate the effectiveness of the proposed method?
- How does the proposed method handle the computational complexity of clustering node attributes, particularly in large and complex graphs?
- Could the authors provide a more detailed analysis of the scalability of the proposed method, including the time and space complexity of each step in the clustering pipeline?
- How does the proposed method perform in scenarios where the graph structure is not informative, or where the graph structure is noisy or incomplete?
- Could the authors provide more details on the datasets used in the experiments, including their characteristics and the rationale behind their selection?
- How does the proposed method handle the issue of clustering node attributes in directed graphs, and what modifications, if any, are necessary for this?
