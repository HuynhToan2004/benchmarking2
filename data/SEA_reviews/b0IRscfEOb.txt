 **Summary:**
The paper introduces ReLiK, a novel Retriever-Reader architecture designed for Entity Linking (EL) and Relation Extraction (RE). ReLiK utilizes a retrieval-based approach to identify potential entities or relations within text, followed by a reader module that discerns their alignment with textual spans. This approach allows for the simultaneous processing of EL and RE in a single forward pass, improving efficiency and potentially reducing computational costs. The model is evaluated on various benchmarks, demonstrating state-of-the-art performance in both in-domain and out-of-domain settings. Despite its strengths in efficiency and performance, concerns are raised about the novelty and the depth of experimental validation.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel and effective Retriever-Reader architecture that achieves state-of-the-art performance in Entity Linking and Relation Extraction, demonstrating a significant improvement over existing methods.
- The approach is well-motivated, with a clear focus on improving efficiency and reducing computational costs, making it accessible to academic groups.
- The paper is well-written, clear, and easy to follow, with a good balance between readability and technical detail.
- The experiments are well-designed, with a thorough analysis of the results and a clear comparison to existing methods.
- The authors provide a detailed description of their approach, which is useful for reproducibility and understanding the methodology.

**Weaknesses:**
- The novelty of the proposed method is limited as it mainly combines existing techniques such as the Retriever-Reader model and the use of a knowledge base for EL and RE.
- The paper lacks a comprehensive comparison with recent state-of-the-art methods, particularly those that use large-scale pre-trained language models (LLMs) and other advanced techniques.
- There is a lack of detailed experimental validation, such as ablation studies and hyperparameter tuning, which could provide deeper insights into the model's performance and robustness.
- The paper does not discuss the limitations of the proposed method, which could help in understanding the scope and applicability of the findings.
- The paper does not adequately address the scalability of the method, especially in terms of its applicability to larger or more complex datasets.
- The paper does not sufficiently discuss the potential societal impacts of the proposed method, which is a critical aspect of responsible AI development.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors clarify how the knowledge base is utilized in the proposed method and discuss the potential benefits of using a more comprehensive knowledge base?
- How does the proposed method perform on more complex datasets or in more challenging scenarios, such as those involving multiple entities or relations per document?
- Could the authors provide more details on the experimental setup, particularly the hyperparameters used and their impact on the model's performance?
- How does the proposed method compare to other state-of-the-art methods that use large-scale pre-trained language models?
- Could the authors discuss the potential limitations of the proposed method and suggest areas for future research to address these limitations?
