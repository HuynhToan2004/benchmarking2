 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel data augmentation approach for visual reinforcement learning (RL) in the DeepMind Control Suite (DMC) environment, focusing on segmenting primary pixels from the background to enhance generalization capabilities. The approach, termed Diverse Data Augmentation (DDA) and Differential Diverse Data Augmentation (D3A), utilizes a pre-trained encoder-decoder model to segment primary pixels, allowing for different data augmentation strategies for primary and background pixels. This method aims to improve generalization performance by preserving semantic invariance and enhancing sample efficiency. The paper is well-structured and presents clear experimental results, although it lacks a detailed discussion on the generalizability of the proposed methods and the impact of the segmentation model on performance.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-structured and easy to follow, with a clear introduction and background that effectively motivates the research.
- The proposed method is simple yet effective, demonstrating significant improvements in generalization performance over existing methods.
- The use of a pre-trained encoder-decoder model for segmenting primary pixels is innovative and enhances the understanding of image segmentation in the context of reinforcement learning.
- The paper presents a novel approach to data augmentation that preserves semantic invariance, which is crucial for maintaining the integrity of the original observation.
- The method is compatible with any model-free off-policy RL algorithm, making it adaptable to various reinforcement learning frameworks.
- The experimental results are convincing, demonstrating the effectiveness of the proposed methods in improving generalization performance.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed discussion on the generalizability of the proposed methods, especially in different environments beyond the DMC suite.
- The impact of the segmentation model on performance is not thoroughly analyzed, which is crucial for understanding the robustness and reliability of the method.
- The paper could benefit from a more comprehensive ablation study to better understand the individual contributions of the DDA and D3A components.
- The novelty of the paper is limited, as the use of a segmentation model and differential data augmentation are not entirely new concepts.
- The paper does not provide a detailed comparison with the latest state-of-the-art methods, which could undermine the perceived effectiveness of the proposed methods.
- The methodology is somewhat complex, with multiple components (DDA, D3A, and the segmentation model) that could be streamlined to enhance clarity and simplicity.

**Questions:**
- How does the proposed method perform in environments where the primary pixels are not clearly distinguishable from the background?
- Could the authors provide a more detailed comparison with the latest state-of-the-art methods to better establish the effectiveness of their approach?
- How does the performance of the segmentation model vary across different tasks and environments, and what are the implications for the overall performance of the method?
- Can the authors clarify the role and impact of the segmentation model in the generalization performance, possibly through additional ablation studies?
- How does the proposed method compare to other data augmentation methods that also consider semantic-invariant state transformations?
- Could the authors elaborate on the choice of the first quartile as the threshold for determining semantic-invariant state transformations?
