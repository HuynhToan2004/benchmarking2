 **Summary:**
The paper investigates the critical yet understudied issue of threshold optimization in neural network monitoring, focusing on the construction of datasets for threshold optimization. It introduces four distinct strategies for building threshold optimization datasets, each representing different assumptions about the data available for threshold tuning. The authors explore the impact of these strategies on threshold optimization, employing a variety of datasets and evaluation metrics. The paper also discusses the practical implications of these findings, emphasizing the importance of having prior knowledge of runtime threats for effective threshold optimization. Despite its relevance, the paper faces criticism for its lack of novelty, unclear presentation, and limited scope, which could affect its impact and acceptance.

- The paper addresses a significant and relevant problem in the field of neural network monitoring, specifically focusing on the selection of thresholds for monitoring, which is crucial for ensuring the safety and reliability of neural network predictions.
- The experimental design is well-structured and thorough, involving various datasets and evaluation metrics, which enhances the credibility and applicability of the findings.
- The paper provides clear and detailed explanations of the experimental setups and results, which are beneficial for understanding the effects of different threshold optimization strategies.
- The paper is well-written and easy to follow, making it accessible to a broad audience.

**Weaknesses:**
