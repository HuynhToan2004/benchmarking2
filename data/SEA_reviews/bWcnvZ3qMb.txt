 **Summary:**
The paper introduces FITS, a novel time series analysis model that operates in the complex frequency domain, utilizing a complex-valued linear layer for amplitude scaling and phase shifting. This model is designed to be lightweight, with a minimal parameter count, making it suitable for edge devices. The FITS model is evaluated against several state-of-the-art models across various benchmark datasets, showing competitive performance. It is also noted for its efficiency in terms of computational resources and memory usage, which is crucial for deployment on resource-constrained devices. The paper also discusses the application of FITS in anomaly detection tasks, demonstrating its effectiveness in this area as well.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel approach to time series analysis by utilizing a complex-valued linear layer, which effectively captures both amplitude and phase information, enhancing the representation of time series data.
- The FITS model is highly efficient, with a minimal parameter count (5k-10k), making it suitable for deployment on edge devices with limited computational resources.
- The paper is well-written, clearly explaining the proposed methodology and its application, with a focus on the frequency domain, which is a promising area for time series analysis.
- The model's performance is competitive with state-of-the-art models, demonstrating its effectiveness in real-world applications.
- The paper includes comprehensive experiments and ablation studies, providing a robust evaluation of the model's capabilities.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a thorough discussion on the limitations of the proposed method, particularly in scenarios where the model may not perform as well, such as with binary event data or in specific types of anomalies.
- The novelty of the approach is questioned, as similar methods using frequency domain representations have been explored in previous works.
- There is a lack of clarity in the paper regarding the specific advantages of using a complex-valued neural network over other types of networks, and how this choice impacts the model's performance.
- The paper does not adequately discuss the computational complexity and memory requirements of the model, which are critical for understanding its deployment on edge devices.
- There are inconsistencies in the experimental results, particularly in the anomaly detection task, where the model's performance is not consistently superior to existing methods.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors provide more details on the specific scenarios where the FITS model may not perform as well, and discuss the potential reasons for these limitations?
- How does the model handle binary event data, and can the authors provide insights into the performance of the model in such scenarios?
- Can the authors elaborate on the advantages of using a complex-valued neural network over other types of networks, and how this choice impacts the model's performance?
- How does the model handle scenarios where the frequency domain representation may not be effective, such as in the detection of certain types of anomalies?
- Could the authors clarify the computational complexity and memory requirements of the model, and discuss the implications of these requirements for deployment on edge devices?
- The paper mentions a lack of novelty in the approach; could the authors provide a more detailed discussion on how their method differs from existing approaches, particularly in terms of the frequency domain representation?
