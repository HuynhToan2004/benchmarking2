 **Summary:**
The paper explores the issue of uncertainty estimation in Large Language Models (LLMs) by introducing a novel method, SAR (Shifting Attention to Relevance), which aims to address the unequal importance of tokens and sentences in LLM outputs. The method reweights the uncertainty of tokens and sentences based on their relevance, thereby improving the accuracy of uncertainty estimation. The paper is supported by extensive experiments on various LLMs and datasets, demonstrating the effectiveness of the proposed method. However, concerns are raised about the novelty of the approach, as it resembles existing methods such as semantic entropy, and the clarity of the presentation, particularly in the methodology and experimental details.

**Strengths:**
- The paper addresses a significant and relevant issue in the field of uncertainty estimation in Large Language Models (LLMs), which is crucial for improving the reliability and trustworthiness of LLM outputs.
- The proposed method, SAR, is well-motivated and provides a novel approach to address the issue of unequal importance of tokens and sentences in LLM outputs.
- The paper is well-written, easy to follow, and includes extensive experiments across various LLMs and datasets, demonstrating the effectiveness of the proposed method.
- The method is simple, practical, and does not require extensive computational resources, making it accessible and implementable in real-world applications.
- The paper provides a comprehensive analysis of the impact of token and sentence-level relevance on uncertainty estimation, which is crucial for understanding the behavior of LLMs in generating uncertain outputs.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed explanation of the relevance score calculation, particularly the role of the cross-encoder RoBERTa in this process.
- The paper could benefit from a more thorough comparison with existing methods, such as semantic entropy, to clarify the advantages and differences between SAR and these methods.
- The methodology and experimental setup are not clearly described, making it difficult to understand the specifics of how the experiments were conducted and how the results were analyzed.
- The paper does not provide a detailed discussion on the limitations of the proposed method, which is essential for understanding its applicability and potential drawbacks.
- There are several typographical and formatting errors throughout the paper, which detract from its professional quality.

**Questions:**
- Could you clarify the role and calculation of the relevance score, particularly how the cross-encoder RoBERTa is used in this process?
- How does the proposed method compare to semantic entropy and other existing methods in terms of effectiveness and computational efficiency?
- Could you provide more details on the experimental setup, such as how the number of generations was determined and how the results were analyzed?
- How does the proposed method perform when compared to other methods in scenarios where the number of generations is varied?
- Can you provide a more detailed discussion on the limitations of the proposed method and how these might affect its practical application?
