 **Summary:**
The paper introduces VLAP, a novel approach for bridging vision and language models by aligning their embeddings through a linear layer and an optimal transport-based assignment prediction objective. This method aims to enhance the compatibility of visual and textual representations by mapping visual data to the LLM's word embedding space, which is then used to train a linear layer for tasks like image captioning and visual question answering. The method's effectiveness is demonstrated through experiments on various vision-language tasks, showcasing improvements over previous linear transformation-based methods. However, concerns are raised about the method's reliance on specific datasets and its generalization to other tasks or models.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel approach, VLAP, for aligning vision and language models, which is a significant contribution to the field of multimodal learning.
- The methodology is clearly explained, making it accessible and understandable, which is crucial for reproducibility and further research.
- The paper is well-structured, with comprehensive experiments and detailed analyses, which support the claims made about the effectiveness of the proposed method.
- The use of optimal transport for cross-modal alignment is innovative and shows potential for improving the alignment of visual and textual data.
- The paper demonstrates significant performance improvements over previous linear transformation-based methods, indicating the practical utility of the proposed approach.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a thorough analysis of the limitations of the proposed method, which is crucial for understanding its applicability and potential drawbacks.
- The evaluation is limited to specific datasets and tasks, which may not fully demonstrate the generalization capabilities of the method.
- The method's reliance on a specific set of word embeddings (from the CC3M dataset) might limit its applicability to other datasets or models that use different embeddings.
- There is a lack of discussion on the computational efficiency of the method compared to other approaches, which could be a significant concern for practical deployment.
- The paper does not provide sufficient ablation studies to validate the effectiveness of the proposed method, particularly in terms of the contribution of different components such as the assignment prediction loss and the image captioning loss.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors provide a more detailed analysis of the limitations of the proposed method, including scenarios where it may not perform well?
- How does the method perform when applied to different datasets or models that use different word embeddings?
- Could the authors provide more detailed computational efficiency analyses to help understand the practical feasibility of the method?
- How does the method compare to other recent approaches in terms of computational efficiency and performance?
- Can the authors provide more detailed results on the generalization capabilities of the method, particularly in terms of its applicability to different tasks or models?
- Why was the decision made to use the CC3M dataset for the word embeddings, and how does this choice affect the method's performance and applicability?
