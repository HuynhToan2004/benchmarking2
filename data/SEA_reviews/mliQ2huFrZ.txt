 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel class probability matching framework for adapting models to new, label-shifted domains, where the label distribution significantly differs from the source domain. This approach is computationally more efficient than traditional methods, which typically involve matching feature distributions. The proposed method, termed CPMCN, uses a calibrated neural network to estimate the class probability ratio, which is then utilized to predict the classifier for the target domain. Theoretical justification and empirical results are provided to support the efficacy of this method, demonstrating its superiority over existing methods in terms of computational efficiency and performance.

**Strengths:**
- The paper presents a novel class probability matching framework that directly matches on the probabilities of label Y, which is a departure from traditional matching methods that focus on feature distributions.
- The proposed method, CPMCN, is computationally more efficient than existing methods due to its direct matching of probabilities of label Y.
- The paper provides a rigorous theoretical guarantee for the proposed matching framework and establishes a generalization bound for the CPMCN algorithm.
- The empirical results show that CPMCN outperforms existing matching methods and EM-based methods in probability ratio estimation and target domain classification.
- The paper is well-written, clear, and easy to follow, making the complex theoretical concepts accessible and understandable.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed comparison of the computational efficiency of CPMCN with other methods, particularly in terms of time efficiency.
- The experimental setup is somewhat limited, with the use of only three benchmark datasets and a relatively small number of experiments.
- The novelty of the proposed method is questioned, as it appears to be a simple application of the Expectation formula without significant innovation.
- The paper does not sufficiently address the convergence behavior of the optimization process involved in the method, which is crucial for understanding its practical applicability.
- There is a lack of discussion on the scalability of the proposed method to larger datasets, which could affect its practical applicability in real-world scenarios.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors provide a detailed comparison of the computational efficiency of CPMCN with other methods, particularly in terms of time efficiency?
- How does the proposed method perform when scaled up to larger datasets, such as those used in large-scale domain adaptation scenarios?
- Could the authors provide a more detailed discussion on the convergence behavior of the optimization process involved in the method?
- What is the rationale behind the choice of specific label shift scenarios in the experiments, and how do these scenarios reflect real-world applications?
- How does the proposed method compare to other recent state-of-the-art methods in terms of computational efficiency and performance?
- Can the authors clarify the role and impact of the calibration network in the performance of the proposed method?
