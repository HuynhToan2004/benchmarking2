 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel method for unsupervised semantic segmentation using a causal inference approach, specifically focusing on the problem of unsupervised prediction. The method, named CAUSE, leverages insights from causal inference to define two-step tasks for unsupervised prediction, including the construction of a concept clusterbook and concept-wise self-supervised learning. The paper claims that CAUSE achieves state-of-the-art performance in unsupervised semantic segmentation across various datasets. However, the paper suffers from clarity issues, particularly in explaining the causal model and the role of the clusterbook in the process. It also lacks comprehensive comparisons with existing methods and detailed discussions on the limitations of the proposed method.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel approach to unsupervised semantic segmentation by leveraging insights from causal inference, which is a significant departure from traditional methods.
- The proposed method achieves state-of-the-art performance on several datasets, demonstrating the effectiveness of the proposed approach.
- The paper is well-organized and clearly written, making it easy to follow and understand the methodology and results.
- The use of causal inference in unsupervised semantic segmentation is a novel and interesting approach, potentially opening new avenues for research in the field.
- The paper includes an ablation study which helps in understanding the impact of different components of the proposed method.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper suffers from clarity issues, particularly in explaining the causal model and the role of the clusterbook in the process. More detailed explanations and visualizations would be beneficial.
- The paper lacks comprehensive comparisons with existing methods, which limits the understanding of how the proposed method performs against state-of-the-art methods.
- The paper does not provide sufficient details on the limitations of the proposed method, which is crucial for understanding the scope and applicability of the findings.
- The paper does not discuss the failure cases in detail, which could provide valuable insights into the practical applicability and robustness of the proposed method.
- The paper could benefit from a more thorough discussion on the potential negative societal impacts of the proposed method.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors provide more detailed explanations and visualizations of the causal model and the role of the clusterbook in the process?
- How does the proposed method compare with existing methods in terms of performance and efficiency?
- What are the potential negative societal impacts of the proposed method, and how does the paper address these concerns?
- Could the authors provide more examples of failure cases and discuss these in detail to understand the practical applicability and robustness of the proposed method?
- How does the proposed method perform when applied to different datasets or scenarios, and what are the limitations of the method in these contexts?
- Could the authors discuss the potential of extending the proposed method to other areas of unsupervised learning or computer vision?
