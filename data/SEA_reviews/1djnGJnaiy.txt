 **Summary:**
The paper introduces BrainMixer, a novel unsupervised learning framework that integrates both functional connectivity and voxel-level time series to learn voxel-level representations. This framework utilizes a dynamic self-attention mechanism for voxel-level signal dynamics and a temporal graph patching for functional connectivity learning. BrainMixer has been evaluated on six datasets and shown to outperform 14 baselines in various downstream tasks. The methodology is designed to address the limitations of existing methods by integrating voxel-level activity and functional connectivity, which is crucial for understanding brain dynamics and disease detection.

**Strengths:**
- The paper addresses a significant and relevant problem in neuroscience by integrating voxel-level activity and functional connectivity, which is crucial for understanding brain dynamics and disease detection.
- The methodology is well-motivated, well-designed, and effectively addresses the limitations of existing methods.
- The paper is well-written, clear, and easy to follow, making it accessible to a broad audience.
- The experimental results are robust, demonstrating the effectiveness of the proposed method in various downstream tasks.
- The paper provides a detailed description of the dataset and the experimental setup, which enhances reproducibility and transparency.
- The authors have provided a thorough evaluation of the proposed method, including an ablation study that validates the effectiveness of each component.
- The paper introduces a novel approach that bridges the gap between voxel-level activity and functional connectivity, which is a significant advancement in the field.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper could benefit from a more comprehensive review of related works, particularly those that have addressed similar issues in neuroscience and machine learning.
- The experimental setup and results could be improved by including more diverse datasets and a broader range of baselines for comparison.
- The paper lacks a detailed discussion on the limitations of the proposed method and potential avenues for future research.
- The computational cost of the proposed method is high, which might limit its applicability in real-world scenarios.
- The paper does not adequately address the scalability of the method to larger datasets, which is a critical issue in neuroscience research.
