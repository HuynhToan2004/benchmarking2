 **Summary:**
The paper introduces an Active Retrosynthetic Planning (ARP) framework aimed at enhancing the quality of retrosynthetic planning by integrating an actor-critic approach to manage the trade-off between query costs and route quality. The framework uses a surrogate model to predict reaction qualities, which are then used to guide the planning process. The authors demonstrate that ARP outperforms existing methods in terms of route quality and query cost reduction, with the ability to adapt to both abundant and sparse annotation environments. The paper is well-structured and provides a comprehensive evaluation, although it lacks a detailed discussion on the limitations of the proposed method and the potential impact of the surrogate model's performance on the overall system's effectiveness.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-structured and easy to follow, with clear explanations of the proposed method and its components.
- The authors provide a comprehensive evaluation of the proposed method, demonstrating its effectiveness through multiple datasets and baseline comparisons.
- The integration of an actor-critic approach into retrosynthetic planning is novel and addresses a significant challenge in the field by balancing query costs and route quality.
- The paper is well-written and includes a thorough analysis of the proposed method, making it accessible and understandable to a broad audience.
- The use of an actor-critic approach is innovative and shows promise in reducing the number of queries needed for high-quality retrosynthetic planning, which is a significant improvement over traditional methods.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper does not adequately discuss the limitations of the proposed method, which is crucial for understanding its scope and applicability.
- The paper lacks a detailed discussion on the potential impact of the surrogate model's performance on the overall system's effectiveness, which is a critical component of the proposed method.
- The paper does not include a discussion on the time complexity of the proposed method compared to existing methods, which could be a significant concern for practical applications.
- The experimental results are not comprehensive enough, with some results showing only marginal improvements over existing methods.
- The paper does not provide a detailed discussion on the scalability of the proposed method, which is essential for understanding its applicability in large-scale or complex retrosynthetic planning scenarios.
- The paper could benefit from a more detailed analysis of the performance of the surrogate model and its impact on the overall system's performance.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors provide more details on the training and performance of the surrogate model used in the proposed method?
- How does the proposed method perform in scenarios with a large number of reactions, and what are the implications for the scalability of the method?
- What are the specific challenges and limitations of the proposed method, and how do these impact its practical application in real-world scenarios?
- How does the proposed method compare in terms of time complexity and computational efficiency with existing methods?
- Can the authors provide more detailed experimental results, including a comparison of the proposed method with other active learning methods in the context of retrosynthetic planning?
