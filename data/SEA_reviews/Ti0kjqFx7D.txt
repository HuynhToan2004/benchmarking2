 **Summary:**
The paper introduces Editable Graph Neural Networks (EGNN), a novel approach to correcting misclassifications in graph neural networks (GNNs) without affecting other nodes. EGNN employs a neighbor propagation-free method by integrating a Multilayer Perceptron (MLP) with a GNN, allowing for efficient and effective model editing. The methodology is evaluated across various datasets, demonstrating its superiority over existing methods in terms of efficiency, generalizability, and effectiveness. Despite its strengths, the paper is criticized for its limited experimental scope, lack of theoretical analysis, and unclear motivation for the specific GNN model used.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel approach to model editing in graph neural networks (GNNs), which is a significant advancement in the field.
- The authors provide a comprehensive empirical evaluation, demonstrating the effectiveness of the proposed method across various datasets and models.
- The manuscript is well-written, making it accessible and easy to follow, with clear explanations of the proposed method and its experimental results.
- The proposed method is simple yet effective, with a straightforward approach that is easy to implement and understand.
- The paper presents a significant contribution to the GNN community by addressing the model editing problem, which is a crucial aspect of model deployment in real-world scenarios.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a thorough theoretical analysis, particularly concerning the locality loss and its relationship with node feature similarity, which could enhance the understanding of the proposed method.
- The experimental setup is limited to small-scale datasets, which may not adequately represent the performance of the proposed method on larger, more complex datasets.
- There is a lack of discussion on the computational complexity of the proposed method, which is crucial for understanding its scalability and efficiency in large-scale applications.
- The motivation behind the use of a specific GNN model (GCN) is not clearly articulated, leaving the reader uncertain about its relevance to the broader GNN landscape.
- The paper does not compare the proposed method with other state-of-the-art GNN editors, which could provide a more robust evaluation of its performance.
- The paper does not adequately address the limitations of the proposed method, which is essential for a complete understanding of its applicability and potential drawbacks.

**Questions:**
- Can you clarify the motivation behind the use of GCN in your experiments and how it contributes to the broader understanding or applicability of your method?
- How does the proposed method compare with other state-of-the-art GNN editors, and what are the specific advantages of your approach?
- Could you provide a more detailed theoretical analysis of the locality loss and its relationship with node feature similarity?
- How does the computational complexity of your method scale with the size of the graph, and what are the practical implications of this complexity in large-scale applications?
- Could you elaborate on the limitations of your method and discuss potential scenarios where it may not be effective or practical?
- In the context of the results presented in the paper, could you clarify the discrepancies noted in the accuracy drops and the generalizability of the edits to other similar nodes?
