 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel optimization algorithm named SaLSa, which enhances the Armijo line search method by incorporating a momentum term and a frequency limitation. This modification aims to address the challenges of mini-batch noise and computational precision issues, making the method more robust and efficient. SaLSa is evaluated across various architectures and datasets, including transformers, CNNs, and MLPs, and is shown to outperform the original Armijo line search method and other optimizers like Adam and SGD. The method is also supported by a theoretical analysis and extensive experiments.

**Strengths:**
- The paper addresses a significant issue in optimization algorithms by proposing a novel method to handle mini-batch noise, which is a common problem in stochastic optimization.
- The proposed method is supported by a theoretical analysis, enhancing the credibility of the approach.
- The experimental results are extensive and demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed method across various architectures and datasets.
- The paper is well-written and easy to follow, making it accessible to a broad audience.
- The introduction of a momentum term into the Armijo criterion is innovative and addresses the issue of mini-batch noise effectively.
- The method's simplicity and the absence of the need for a learning rate schedule make it practical and efficient.
- The code provided is clean and well-documented, which is crucial for reproducibility and further research.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a clear comparison with state-of-the-art methods, which could have provided a more robust evaluation of the proposed method.
- The motivation behind the proposed method is not clearly articulated, particularly in relation to the Armijo line search method.
- The paper does not adequately address the computational overhead introduced by the line search algorithm, which could affect the practical applicability of the method.
- The theoretical analysis provided is limited, and the paper could benefit from a more comprehensive discussion on the convergence properties of the proposed method.
- The paper does not sufficiently discuss the impact of different hyperparameters on the performance of the method, which could be crucial for practical implementation.
- There are inconsistencies and potential errors in the mathematical notations and equations, which could affect the understanding and reproducibility of the results.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors clarify the motivation behind the proposed method and how it addresses the specific issues with the Armijo line search method?
- How does the proposed method compare in terms of computational overhead and practical applicability to other state-of-the-art optimization methods?
- Could the authors provide more details on the theoretical analysis, particularly concerning the convergence properties of the proposed method?
- How does the choice of hyperparameters, such as beta values, affect the performance of the method?
- Can the authors address the inconsistencies and potential errors in the mathematical notations and equations to ensure the accuracy and reproducibility of the results?
- How does the proposed method perform in scenarios with large learning rates, and what are the implications of using different learning rate schedules?
