 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel neuron model, the Continual Memory Neuron (CMN), designed to enhance neural networks' capabilities in continual learning. The CMN integrates learnable memory units and attention mechanisms, which enable the neuron to adaptively select and blend weights based on the input, thereby improving the network's ability to learn from non-stationary data distributions. This model is evaluated on various continual learning benchmarks, showing promising results in mitigating forgetting and maintaining competitive performance. The authors also provide theoretical and empirical comparisons with existing methods, highlighting the model's effectiveness and novelty.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel neuron model, the Continual Memory Neuron (CMN), which is a significant contribution to the field of continual learning.
- The CMN model is designed to mitigate catastrophic forgetting, a critical issue in neural networks, and is evaluated on various continual learning benchmarks, demonstrating its effectiveness.
- The paper provides theoretical and empirical comparisons with existing methods, highlighting the strengths and weaknesses of the CMN model in the context of continual learning.
- The authors have conducted ablation studies to understand the impact of different components of the CMN model, providing a deeper insight into its functionality.
- The paper is well-written, making it accessible and easy to understand, with clear explanations of the methodology and results.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed analysis of the computational and memory burden of the CMN model, which is crucial for understanding its practical applicability.
- The paper does not sufficiently discuss the limitations of the CMN model, particularly in terms of its scalability and applicability to different network architectures.
- The paper does not provide a thorough comparison with state-of-the-art methods, which could help in understanding the relative performance of the CMN model.
- The paper does not provide a detailed discussion on the impact of different hyperparameters on the performance of the CMN model, which is essential for optimizing its performance.
- The paper's presentation could be improved, particularly in terms of clarity and organization, especially in the experimental section.
- The paper does not adequately address the generalization ability of the CMN model to different types of data distributions, which is crucial for its practical applicability.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors provide more details on the computational and memory burden of the CMN model, including the impact of different hyperparameters on these metrics?
- How does the CMN model perform when scaled up to larger datasets, and what are the potential limitations in terms of performance and computational efficiency?
- Could the authors provide a more detailed discussion on the limitations of the CMN model, particularly in terms of its applicability to different network architectures and data distributions?
- How does the CMN model compare to other state-of-the-art methods in terms of performance, and what are the specific advantages of the CMN model over these methods?
- Can the authors provide a more detailed analysis of the impact of different hyperparameters on the performance of the CMN model, and how can these parameters be optimally set to achieve the best performance?
- Could the authors discuss the potential of the CMN model to be integrated into existing neural network architectures, and what are the challenges and opportunities associated with such integration?
