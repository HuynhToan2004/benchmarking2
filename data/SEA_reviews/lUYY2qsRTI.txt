 **Summary:**
The paper investigates the challenge of hidden confounding in offline reinforcement learning (RL), proposing a novel approach to quantify uncertainty due to unobserved confounders. It introduces a method for measuring delphic uncertainty, which is distinct from epistemic and aleatoric uncertainties, and demonstrates its effectiveness through experiments on both synthetic and real-world medical datasets. The proposed method leverages variational inference to model compatible worlds and penalizes value functions to mitigate confounding bias. The paper also discusses the limitations and potential societal impacts of the proposed method, providing a comprehensive evaluation and comparisons with existing methods.

**Strengths:**
- The paper addresses a significant and important problem of hidden confounding in offline RL, which is often overlooked in current literature.
- The proposed method is novel, leveraging variational inference to model compatible worlds and quantifying uncertainty through delphic uncertainty, which is distinct from traditional epistemic and aleatoric uncertainties.
- The paper is well-written, with clear explanations and intuitive examples that aid in understanding complex concepts.
- Extensive experiments are conducted, including both synthetic and real-world medical datasets, demonstrating the effectiveness of the proposed method.
- The paper provides a comprehensive evaluation and comparisons with existing methods, highlighting the novelty and effectiveness of the proposed approach.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper could benefit from a more detailed discussion on the limitations of the proposed method, including its applicability to other types of data and potential negative societal impacts.
- The methodology for estimating delphic uncertainty is not thoroughly explained, and the paper lacks a detailed discussion on the assumptions and limitations of the method.
- The paper does not sufficiently discuss the computational cost and complexity of the proposed method, which could be a significant concern for large-scale applications.
- There is a need for more comparisons with existing methods, particularly in terms of computational efficiency and the robustness of the results.
- The paper could benefit from more detailed explanations and examples in sections where the methodology and results are presented, such as the algorithm and the experimental setup.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors provide more details on the limitations of the proposed method, including its applicability to other types of data and potential negative societal impacts?
- How does the proposed method compare in terms of computational cost and complexity with existing methods?
- Can the authors elaborate on the assumptions and limitations of the method, particularly in the context of different types of data and confounding variables?
- How does the proposed method perform when applied to different types of datasets, such as those with different confounding variables or different levels of complexity?
- Could the authors provide more detailed explanations and examples of the algorithm and the experimental setup to enhance the understanding of the proposed method?
