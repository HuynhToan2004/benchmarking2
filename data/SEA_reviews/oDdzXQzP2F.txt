 **Summary:**
The paper introduces Transformer-VQ, a decoder-only transformer that computes softmax-based dense self-attention in linear time through vector-quantized keys and a lossless compressive cache. This approach aims to address the computational efficiency challenges in handling long sequences by reducing the complexity of self-attention operations. The model is evaluated on various benchmarks including Enwik8, PG-19, and ImageNet64, showing competitive results. The paper also discusses the theoretical foundations of the attention mechanism and its implementation using vector quantization. However, concerns were raised about the novelty of the approach, the clarity of the paper, and the need for a more thorough evaluation across different tasks and settings.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel approach to computing softmax-based dense self-attention in linear time through vector-quantized keys and a lossless compressive cache, which is a significant contribution to the field.
- The paper is well-written, clear, and easy to follow, with a clear presentation of the proposed methodology and its implementation.
- The experimental results demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed method, showing that it can achieve state-of-the-art results on certain benchmarks.
- The paper includes a detailed theoretical analysis of the attention mechanism, which is a valuable contribution to the understanding of attention mechanisms in transformers.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a thorough evaluation across different tasks and settings, which limits the generalizability of the results.
- There is a lack of clarity in the presentation of the paper, particularly in the explanation of the attention mechanism and the role of the codebook in the model.
- The paper does not sufficiently address the scalability of the model with larger codebook sizes and the computational cost associated with vector quantization.
- The paper does not adequately compare its results with other models of similar size, which could lead to misleading conclusions about the effectiveness of the proposed method.
- There are several minor issues with the paper, such as typos and inconsistencies in the use of notation, which detract from the overall quality of the manuscript.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors clarify the role and impact of the codebook size on the model's performance, particularly in terms of computational cost and model size?
- How does the proposed method compare to other methods that also use vector quantization, such as those mentioned in the paper?
- Can the authors provide more details on the experimental setup and the specifics of the codebook learning process, including the size and complexity of the codebooks used?
- How does the proposed method handle the issue of overfitting, particularly in long-context autoregressive modeling tasks?
- Could the authors elaborate on the scalability of the model with larger codebook sizes and the potential impact on model performance and computational efficiency?
