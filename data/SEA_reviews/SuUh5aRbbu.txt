 **Summary:**
The paper introduces three models for generating story plots using a combination of supervised fine-tuning and reinforcement learning from human feedback. The models aim to address the challenges of generating high-quality story plots efficiently, with a focus on reducing the number of LLM calls and improving the training dataset's quality. The proposed methods include OpenPlot, which generates story plots using LLaMA2, and E2EPlot, which is fine-tuned on these plots using supervised learning. The third model, RLPlot, is further fine-tuned using reinforcement learning, showing improved performance over E2EPlot. The paper also discusses the use of GPT-4 for evaluation and compares the generated plots with those from existing models.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, easy to follow, and presents a clear motivation for the study of end-to-end story plot generation.
- It introduces a novel approach by integrating a chat model into the DOC pipeline, which is a significant advancement over previous methods that heavily relied on completion models.
- The use of a chat model for simulating a completion model is innovative and shows potential for improving the efficiency and effectiveness of story plot generation.
- The paper includes a detailed analysis of the challenges in using a chat model for generating story plots and provides a comprehensive evaluation of the generated plots using GPT-4.
- The inclusion of a reward model for fine-tuning the story plot generation model using RLHF is a notable contribution, enhancing the model's ability to adapt to human preferences.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a thorough evaluation of the generated plots, particularly in terms of their coherence and logical consistency. The evaluation primarily focuses on the quality of the generated plots without adequately addressing how well the plots hold together as a narrative.
- The comparison with existing models like DOC is not fair due to the different models used (Llama2 vs. GPT-3.5), which could affect the perceived effectiveness of the proposed methods.
- The paper does not adequately discuss the limitations of the proposed methods, such as the potential for overfitting to the training data or the generalizability of the models to different genres or styles of stories.
- There is a lack of discussion on the societal impacts of the proposed methods, which could be significant given the potential for automated story generation to influence cultural narratives.
- The paper could benefit from a more detailed analysis of the training data and the prompting strategies used, particularly in how these affect the quality and diversity of the generated plots.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors provide more details on the evaluation of the generated plots, particularly how coherence and logical consistency were assessed?
- How do the authors ensure that the models do not overfit to the training data, especially given the reliance on a specific dataset for training?
- What steps were taken to ensure the generalizability of the models to different genres or styles of stories?
- How does the use of a chat model for generating story plots compare to using a completion model, particularly in terms of efficiency and effectiveness?
- Can the authors discuss the potential societal impacts of the proposed methods, particularly in terms of how automated story generation might influence cultural narratives?
- Could the authors provide more examples or case studies to illustrate the effectiveness of the reward model in improving the quality of the generated plots?
