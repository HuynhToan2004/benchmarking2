 **Summary:**
The paper introduces the Continuous-Time Graph Anti-Symmetric Network (CTAN), a novel deep graph network designed to handle long-range information propagation in continuous-time dynamic graphs (C-TDGs). CTAN leverages anti-symmetric weight matrices and stable ODEs to ensure non-dissipative behavior, which is crucial for preserving long-term dependencies in information flow. The authors conduct extensive experiments to demonstrate the effectiveness of CTAN, comparing it with existing methods on both synthetic and real-world datasets. Despite the innovative approach and promising results, concerns were raised about the clarity of the paper, the novelty of the theoretical contributions, and the robustness of the experimental results.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel framework for learning long-range information in continuous-time dynamic graphs (C-TDGs), which is a significant advancement in the field of graph learning.
- The proposed method, CTAN, is simple, easy to understand, and shows better performance than existing methods, as demonstrated through rigorous experiments.
- The paper is well-written, making the complex concepts accessible and understandable, and the experiments are well-designed, showing that the proposed method outperforms existing baselines.
- The theoretical contributions, such as the use of anti-symmetric weight matrices and non-dissipative ODEs, are innovative and well-explained, providing a strong foundation for the proposed method.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks clarity in some sections, particularly in the definitions and explanations of key concepts and equations, which could confuse readers unfamiliar with the subject matter.
- There is a lack of novelty in some of the theoretical contributions, as similar ideas have been previously explored in other works.
- The paper does not adequately address the limitations of the proposed method, which could help in understanding its scope and applicability.
- The experimental results are not sufficiently robust, with some results showing only marginal improvements over existing methods, and the paper does not include a detailed analysis of the computational complexity of the proposed method.
- The paper does not sufficiently compare the proposed method with state-of-the-art methods, such as those that use self-attention mechanisms, which could provide a more comprehensive evaluation of the proposed method's effectiveness.

**Questions:**
- Can you clarify the definition and role of the aggregation function φ in the context of the proposed method?
- How does the proposed method handle the over-smoothing problem, particularly in the context of continuous-time dynamic graphs?
- Is there a specific reason why the paper does not include a detailed discussion on the computational complexity of the proposed method?
- Could you provide more details on how the proposed method compares with state-of-the-art methods that use self-attention mechanisms, particularly in terms of performance and computational efficiency?
- How does the proposed method ensure the stability and non-dissipative behavior of the system, especially in the context of long-term dependency modeling?
- Is there a possibility of extending the proposed method to handle more complex graph structures, such as directed graphs or graphs with heterophily?
