 **Summary:**
The paper introduces AdaSR, a method for adapting super-resolution models to various hardware platforms and dynamic runtime environments. AdaSR utilizes a progressive knowledge distillation approach to train adaptive models that maintain performance while reducing computational and memory requirements. The methodology involves adjusting the depth and channel sizes of models, optimizing loss functions, and using Bayesian tuning to adapt to different hardware constraints. The paper claims that AdaSR can achieve Pareto optimal performance and reduce memory footprint significantly compared to existing methods. However, concerns are raised about the novelty of the approach, the clarity of the presentation, and the lack of comprehensive experimental validation against state-of-the-art models.

**Strengths:**
- The paper addresses an important and relevant problem of adapting super-resolution models to various hardware platforms and dynamic runtime environments.
- The proposed method, AdaSR, is technically sound and innovative, particularly in its use of progressive knowledge distillation and Bayesian tuning for loss function optimization.
- The paper is well-written and easy to follow, with clear explanations of the methodology and experimental results.
- The experimental results demonstrate the effectiveness of AdaSR in terms of Pareto optimal performance and reduced memory footprint.
- The paper provides a detailed description of the AdaSR method and its components, which is crucial for understanding and replicating the method.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a clear explanation of the specific hardware constraints and dynamic runtime environments it targets, which could limit its applicability.
- There is a lack of novelty in the proposed method, as it resembles existing techniques such as pruning and knowledge distillation, which have been widely used in the field.
- The experimental validation is insufficient, with limited comparisons to state-of-the-art models and a lack of comprehensive results across different hardware platforms and datasets.
- The paper suffers from several presentation issues, including unclear figures, inconsistent notation, and a lack of detailed experimental setups and hyperparameter tuning.
- There are concerns about the scalability of the method, particularly in terms of its ability to adapt to very large models and diverse hardware platforms.
- The paper does not adequately address the potential negative societal impacts of its work.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors clarify the specific hardware constraints and dynamic runtime environments that AdaSR is designed to address?
- How does AdaSR compare to other state-of-the-art models in terms of performance and memory footprint?
- Could the authors provide more detailed experimental results, including comparisons with other models like RCAN and FMEN, and across different hardware platforms and datasets?
- How does the proposed method perform on larger models, and what are the implications for its scalability?
- Can the authors provide more details on the experimental setup, including the specific hardware used and the hyperparameter tuning methods employed?
- How does the proposed method handle the trade-off between performance and latency in dynamic runtime environments?
- Could the authors provide more qualitative results, such as visual comparisons of super-resolved images, to better illustrate the effectiveness of AdaSR?
