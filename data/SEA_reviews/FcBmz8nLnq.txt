 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel framework named ARGS (Adversarially Robust Graph Sparsification) aimed at enhancing the robustness of Graph Lottery Tickets (GLTs) against adversarial attacks. ARGS employs a loss function that considers graph homophily and information associated with both true labels of train nodes and pseudo labels of test nodes. The framework is designed to prune both perturbed graph adjacency matrices and GNN model weights iteratively, generating adversarially robust GLTs. The effectiveness of ARGS is demonstrated through extensive experiments, showing that it outperforms existing methods in terms of robustness and efficiency. Despite its strengths, the paper has been critiqued for its limited evaluation scope, lack of clarity in some experimental results, and potential over-reliance on specific datasets and attack methods.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel framework, ARGS, which addresses the robustness of Graph Lottery Tickets (GLTs) against adversarial attacks, specifically through sparsification techniques.
- The proposed loss function, which considers graph homophily and information associated with both true labels of train nodes and pseudo labels of test nodes, is innovative and well-explained.
- The paper is well-written, making it accessible and easy to follow, with clear illustrations and detailed explanations of the methodology.
- Extensive experiments are conducted, demonstrating the effectiveness of the proposed framework and providing a thorough analysis of the results.
- The paper addresses a significant and relevant problem in the field of graph neural networks, focusing on the robustness of GLTs against adversarial attacks, which is a critical area of research.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper's evaluation is limited to a specific set of attack methods and datasets, which may not fully demonstrate the generalizability of the proposed method.
- The clarity of experimental results could be improved, particularly in the presentation of results and the explanation of the methodology used.
- The paper lacks a detailed discussion on the limitations of the proposed method and potential negative societal impacts, which are crucial for understanding the broader implications of the research.
- The paper does not include a detailed comparison with other defense methods, which could provide a more comprehensive understanding of the proposed method's effectiveness.
- There are several minor typographical and formatting errors that need correction for better readability and professional presentation.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors clarify the methodology used in the evaluation of the robustness of GLTs against adversarial attacks, particularly the choice of attack methods and datasets?
- How does the proposed method perform against other types of attacks, such as evasion attacks, which were not included in the current evaluation?
- Can the authors provide more detailed explanations and examples of how the proposed method improves the robustness of GLTs in practical scenarios?
- How does the proposed method compare with other defense methods in terms of efficiency and effectiveness?
- Could the authors expand on the potential negative societal impacts of the proposed method and discuss strategies for mitigating these risks?
- Are there any plans to include more diverse datasets and attack methods in future evaluations to enhance the generalizability of the findings?
