 **Summary:**
The paper explores active learning in neural networks using a novel approach that integrates neural networks for both exploitation and exploration, which is a departure from traditional methods that transform instances into multiple arms for bandit-based approaches. The authors propose two algorithms, one for stream-based and another for pool-based active learning, and provide theoretical performance guarantees in a non-parametric setting. The algorithms are designed to improve computational efficiency and reduce the dependence on the number of classes, thereby potentially improving the performance in scenarios with a large number of classes. Extensive experiments are conducted to evaluate the proposed methods, demonstrating their effectiveness and efficiency compared to existing methods. However, the paper is criticized for its limited novelty, as it primarily builds on existing methods, and the theoretical analysis is seen as a straightforward extension rather than a significant advancement.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel approach to active learning by employing neural networks for both exploitation and exploration, which is a departure from traditional methods that transform instances into multiple arms for bandit-based approaches.
- The proposed algorithms, NeurOnAL-S and NeurOnAL-P, are designed to improve computational efficiency and reduce the dependence on the number of classes, potentially improving performance in scenarios with a large number of classes.
- Theoretical performance guarantees are provided for both algorithms, demonstrating a slower error-growth rate concerning the number of classes, which is a significant contribution to the field.
- Extensive experiments are conducted to evaluate the proposed algorithms, demonstrating their effectiveness and efficiency compared to existing methods.
- The paper is well-written, with clear and concise presentation of the proposed algorithms and theoretical analysis, making it accessible and understandable to readers.

**Weaknesses:**
- The novelty of the paper is limited as it primarily builds on existing methods and extends them to handle the number of classes, rather than introducing a fundamentally new approach.
- The theoretical analysis is seen as a straightforward extension rather than a significant advancement, and the paper lacks a detailed discussion on the technical challenges and insights involved in the theoretical analysis.
- The paper does not adequately address the issue of scalability with the number of classes, particularly in terms of the computational complexity and the impact on the performance of the algorithms.
- There is a lack of comparison with other active learning methods, such as ALPS, which could provide a more comprehensive evaluation of the proposed algorithms.
- The paper does not sufficiently discuss the limitations of the proposed methods, which is crucial for understanding the scope and applicability of the research.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors provide a more detailed discussion on the technical challenges and insights involved in the theoretical analysis of the proposed algorithms?
- How does the proposed approach compare in terms of computational complexity and performance with existing methods, especially in scenarios with a large number of classes?
- Could the authors provide a more detailed discussion on the limitations of the proposed methods and how these limitations affect the practical applicability and scalability of the research?
- In the context of the theoretical analysis, could the authors elaborate on the relationship between the NTK matrix and the performance guarantees provided by the proposed algorithms?
- How does the proposed approach perform in scenarios where the number of classes is very large, and what are the potential challenges or limitations in such scenarios?
