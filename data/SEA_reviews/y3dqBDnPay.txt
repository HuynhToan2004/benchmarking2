 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel self-supervised multimodal representation learning method named HyperRep, which leverages hypergraph-based modeling and the Multimodal Fusion Information Bottleneck principle to capture complex high-order inter- and intra-modality correlations in multimodal data. This method effectively fuses multimodal data, enhancing the representation of multimodal data by integrating information from different modalities. The paper includes extensive experiments across various datasets and tasks, demonstrating the method's effectiveness and outperforming existing state-of-the-art methods. The proposed approach is particularly notable for its ability to capture high-order correlations using hypergraphs and for its application in real-world scenarios.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel approach to multimodal representation learning using hypergraphs, which is a first in this field.
- The proposed method effectively fuses multimodal data by capturing high-order correlations using hypergraphs, which is a significant advancement in the field.
- The paper is well-written, making it easy to follow and understand the proposed methodology.
- Extensive experiments are conducted on four public datasets for three downstream tasks, demonstrating the superiority of the proposed method.
- The paper provides a detailed explanation of the proposed method and its components, including the hypergraph attention module and the self-supervised multimodal fusion information bottleneck principle.
- The ablation studies confirm the effectiveness of each part of the proposed method, providing a thorough evaluation of the proposed method.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed comparison with existing hypergraph-based multimodal representation learning methods, which could have provided a clearer understanding of the advantages and limitations of the proposed method.
- The paper does not provide a detailed analysis of the computational complexity of the proposed method, which is crucial for understanding its practical applicability.
- The paper does not discuss the limitations of the proposed method, which could help in understanding its applicability and potential drawbacks.
- The paper does not provide a detailed discussion on how the proposed method could be adapted for different types of multimodal data, such as images, which could limit its applicability.
- The paper does not provide a detailed discussion on how the proposed method could be adapted for different types of multimodal learning tasks, such as multimodal retrieval, which could limit its applicability.
