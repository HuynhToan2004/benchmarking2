 **Summary:**
The paper introduces UniAP, a novel framework that optimizes both inter- and intra-layer parallelism in deep learning model training, aiming to enhance training efficiency and reduce the time required for strategy searching. UniAP employs mixed integer quadratic programming (MIQP) to jointly optimize these parallelism dimensions, achieving better training throughput and strategy searching time compared to existing methods. The paper is well-structured, providing clear motivation and detailed experimental results, although it lacks a broader evaluation across different models and hardware configurations. The methodology is innovative, integrating MIQP into the automatic parallelism problem, and the experimental results showcase significant improvements. However, the paper could benefit from a more comprehensive evaluation and a deeper discussion on the limitations and societal impacts of the proposed approach.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-organized, easy to follow, and clearly written, making it accessible to a broad audience.
- The proposed method, UniAP, is innovative, integrating mixed integer quadratic programming (MIQP) into the automatic parallelism problem, which is a novel approach.
- UniAP shows significant improvements in training throughput and strategy searching time compared to existing methods, as demonstrated by extensive experimental results.
- The paper provides a detailed description of the proposed method and includes a comprehensive evaluation across various models and hardware configurations, showcasing the scalability and effectiveness of UniAP.
- The use of MIQP to jointly optimize inter- and intra-layer parallelism is a significant contribution to the field, and the paper's approach is the first of its kind to tackle this problem in a unified manner.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper could benefit from a more comprehensive evaluation, including a broader range of models and hardware configurations to validate the generalizability of UniAP.
- The methodology section could be improved by providing more detailed explanations and examples, particularly for equations and constraints, to enhance understanding.
- The paper lacks a discussion on the limitations of the proposed method, which is crucial for understanding its applicability and potential drawbacks.
- There is a need for a more detailed comparison with other state-of-the-art methods, including recent works that have achieved significant improvements in training efficiency.
- The paper could benefit from a more detailed discussion on the societal impacts of the proposed approach, particularly in terms of energy efficiency and computational resource utilization.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors provide a more detailed explanation of the methodology, particularly the MIQP formulation and the constraints used in the model?
- How does UniAP compare to other state-of-the-art methods, especially in terms of training throughput and strategy searching time?
- What are the potential limitations of the proposed method, and how do these affect the scalability and applicability of UniAP?
- How does UniAP handle different hardware configurations, and what are the implications of using different hardware in terms of performance and efficiency?
- Could the authors discuss the potential societal impacts of their approach, particularly in terms of energy efficiency and computational resource utilization?
- How does UniAP perform in scenarios where the model architecture or hardware configuration differs significantly from those used in the experiments?
