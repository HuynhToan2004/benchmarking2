 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel method for estimating the Independent Component Analysis (ICA) model using a genetic algorithm (GA) and a neural network (NN) to optimize the negentropy objective function. The proposed method aims to improve the convergence rate of the FastICA algorithm, although it does not demonstrate significant performance improvements over existing methods. The paper lacks clarity in its presentation, with several grammatical and structural errors, and the experimental results are not convincingly superior to existing methods. Additionally, the paper does not adequately compare its method with other state-of-the-art approaches, which limits the understanding of its contributions.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a new approach using a genetic algorithm (GA) and a neural network (NN) to optimize the negentropy objective function in ICA, which is a novel application of these techniques in this context.
- The paper provides experimental results that demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed method, although the results are not significantly superior to existing methods.
- The authors have attempted to compare their method with existing approaches, which is a commendable effort in validating the proposed method.
- The paper is well-written, and the authors have made an attempt to discuss related works, which helps in positioning the research within the existing literature.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks clarity in its presentation, with several grammatical and structural errors that make it difficult to follow.
- The paper does not adequately compare its method with other state-of-the-art approaches, which limits the understanding of its contributions and the relevance of the proposed method.
- The experimental results do not convincingly demonstrate the superiority of the proposed method over existing methods, such as FastICA.
- The paper does not provide a comprehensive theoretical analysis of the proposed method, which could help in understanding its advantages and limitations.
- The paper does not discuss the computational complexity of the proposed method, which is crucial for understanding its scalability and efficiency.
- The paper lacks a detailed discussion on the convergence properties of the proposed method, which is essential for assessing its reliability and effectiveness.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors provide a clearer explanation of the genetic algorithm and neural network components used in the proposed method, particularly how they contribute to the optimization of the negentropy objective function?
- How does the proposed method compare with other state-of-the-art approaches in terms of performance, computational complexity, and convergence properties?
- Can the authors provide a detailed theoretical analysis of the proposed method, including its advantages, limitations, and convergence properties?
- How does the proposed method handle different types of data, such as image data, which are not typically handled by standard FastICA?
- Could the authors provide more detailed experimental results, including comparisons with other methods on larger and more complex datasets?
- How does the proposed method handle the issue of local minima in the optimization process, and what steps have been taken to ensure that the method converges to the global optimum?
