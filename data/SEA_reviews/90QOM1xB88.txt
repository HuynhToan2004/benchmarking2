 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel exponential integrator for diffusion models, aimed at improving sampling efficiency and accuracy. It critiques the use of degenerate exponential integrators in existing methods and proposes a new approach that fulfills all order conditions, thereby reducing numerical defects and improving FID scores. The methodology involves a refined exponential solver (RES) that unifies the noise prediction and data prediction models into a canonical semilinear ODE, which is then used to derive an exponential integrator. The paper is well-written and provides a comprehensive theoretical analysis and empirical validation, showing that the proposed method outperforms existing techniques in terms of sampling quality and stability.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, with clear explanations of the proposed method and its implementation details.
- The proposed method, RES, is simple yet effective, providing a theoretical analysis of the error bound and demonstrating improved performance over existing methods.
- The experiments are comprehensive, comparing the proposed method with several state-of-the-art methods and showing significant improvements in performance.
- The paper provides a detailed analysis of the numerical defects of existing methods and proposes a new method to address these issues, which is both theoretically sound and empirically validated.
- The proposed method is orthogonal to other sampling improvements, making it a valuable contribution to the field.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper could benefit from a more thorough comparison with other methods, including those that use different parametrizations and time-scheduling methods.
- There is a lack of discussion on the limitations of the proposed method, particularly in terms of its applicability to other diffusion models and its computational efficiency.
- The paper does not provide a detailed comparison with other ODE solvers, which could be beneficial for understanding the effectiveness of the proposed method.
- The theoretical analysis and proofs, while sound, are not particularly novel or groundbreaking, and the paper could benefit from a more rigorous discussion of the theoretical contributions.
- The paper could be improved by including more detailed explanations of the equations and terms used, especially those that are less standard or require more explanation.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors clarify the definitions and roles of different terms and equations, such as Eq. (1), λD, and gD, in the context of the proposed method?
- How does the proposed method compare with other ODE solvers, particularly in terms of computational efficiency and accuracy?
- Could the authors provide more detailed comparisons with other methods, including those that use different parametrizations and time-scheduling methods?
- How does the proposed method perform when applied to other diffusion models, and what are the implications of its performance on different types of data or models?
- Can the authors discuss the limitations of the proposed method and suggest potential directions for future research to address these limitations?
