 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel Federated Hardware-Prompt learning (FedHP) framework aimed at addressing data privacy and heterogeneity issues in spectral snapshot compressive imaging (SCI) systems. The FedHP framework is designed to optimize spectral SCI systems by coordinating pre-trained models across multiple hardware configurations, thereby improving performance under challenging heterogeneous conditions. The paper also introduces a new Snapshot Spectral Heterogeneous Dataset (SSHD) to facilitate further exploration of this practical computational imaging problem. The proposed method, FedHP, leverages a hardware-conditioned prompt network to align inconsistent data distributions across clients, demonstrating superior performance over existing federated learning frameworks. The paper is well-received for its innovative approach, clear presentation, and the introduction of a new dataset, although concerns were raised about the practicality of the federated learning setup and the clarity of some methodological descriptions.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel Federated Hardware-Prompt learning (FedHP) framework for spectral snapshot compressive imaging (SCI), which effectively addresses data privacy and heterogeneity issues.
- The FedHP framework coordinates pre-trained models across multiple hardware configurations, outperforming existing federated learning frameworks.
- A new Snapshot Spectral Heterogeneous Dataset (SSHD) is introduced, which will be a valuable resource for further research in this field.
- The paper is well-written, clearly explaining the challenges in existing SCI methods and the motivation behind the proposed solution.
- The introduction of a hardware-conditioned prompt network to address the data inconsistency problem is innovative and contributes to the field of computational imaging.
- The paper includes extensive experiments that demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed method, showing improvements over existing methods.
- The authors plan to release the dataset and code, which will facilitate further research and reproducibility.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper could benefit from a more detailed description of the hardware-conditioned prompt network and the adaptors used in the network architecture.
- The practicality of the federated learning setup is questionable, particularly in the context of SCI, where the hardware is typically owned by a single entity.
- The paper does not adequately address the issue of data privacy, which is a significant concern in federated learning.
- The experimental setup and the choice of parameters (e.g., the number of clients and the number of iterations) are not clearly justified, which could affect the reproducibility and generalizability of the results.
- The paper could benefit from a more comprehensive discussion on the limitations of the proposed method and how these might affect its practical application.
- The paper could be improved by including more detailed results and comparisons, such as the performance of the prompt network and adaptors when trained on the same dataset.

**Questions:**
- Could you provide more details on the hardware-conditioned prompt network and the adaptors used in the network architecture?
- How does the proposed method address the issue of data privacy in the context of federated learning?
- Can you clarify the practicality of the federated learning setup in the context of SCI?
- How do the results compare when the prompt network and adaptors are trained on the same dataset?
- What are the specific challenges in applying the proposed method to real-world SCI systems, and how might these affect the performance?
- Could you provide more details on the experimental setup, such as the choice of parameters and the rationale behind the experimental design?
- How does the proposed method compare to other state-of-the-art methods in terms of performance and computational efficiency?
