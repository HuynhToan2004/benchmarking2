 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a domain-generalized time series forecasting model based on the N-BEATS architecture, which incorporates a stack-wise feature alignment using the Sinkhorn divergence to address domain shift issues. This method aims to align marginal feature measures across multiple source data sequences, enhancing the model's ability to learn invariant features and improve forecasting performance. The paper is structured around a theoretical framework that aligns features via Sinkhorn divergence, which is empirically validated through extensive experiments. The results demonstrate the model's effectiveness in various domain shift scenarios, although concerns are raised about the clarity of the paper's structure and the need for more comprehensive ablation studies.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, with a clear structure and logical flow, making it easy to follow.
- The proposed method is innovative, combining the strengths of the N-BEATS architecture with feature alignment, which is particularly useful for time series forecasting.
- The use of the Sinkhorn divergence for feature alignment is a novel approach that enhances the model's ability to learn invariant features across different domains.
- The paper includes comprehensive experimental evaluations and ablation studies that demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed method in various domain shift scenarios.
- Theoretical analysis is provided to justify the feasibility of the proposed method, enhancing the paper's credibility.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a clear explanation of how the Sinkhorn divergence is used to align marginal feature measures, which could confuse readers unfamiliar with this concept.
- There is a need for more detailed ablation studies, particularly concerning the impact of different hyperparameters and the choice of the Sinkhorn divergence.
- The paper could benefit from more intuitive visualizations and examples to better illustrate the benefits of the proposed method.
- Some parts of the paper, such as the definition of marginal feature measures and the training objective, are not clearly explained, which could hinder understanding.
- The paper does not sufficiently address the issue of domain shift in the target domain, which is crucial for the effectiveness of the proposed method.
- The paper could be improved by providing more detailed explanations of the theoretical results and their implications, as well as by including a more comprehensive literature review.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors clarify how the Sinkhorn divergence is used to align marginal feature measures and provide more intuitive examples to illustrate its benefits?
- How does the proposed method handle the issue of domain shift in the target domain, and what measures are taken to ensure that the model remains effective in such scenarios?
- Can the authors provide more details on the choice of the Sinkhorn divergence and its impact on the model's performance?
- How do the authors address the issue of gradient vanishing/exploding in the model, and what steps are taken to mitigate these issues?
- Could the authors elaborate on the theoretical results and their implications, particularly in the context of the proposed method?
- What are the specific advantages of using the Sinkhorn divergence over other divergence measures, and how does it contribute to the model's effectiveness?
