 **Summary:**
The paper investigates the impact of batch size on the performance of Byzantine-robust distributed learning (BRDL) under various attack scenarios, particularly focusing on the i.i.d. case. It introduces a novel method called ByzSGDnm that utilizes a normalized momentum to address the variance introduced by Byzantine workers. The theoretical analysis supports the claim that a larger batch size can enhance model accuracy and reduce communication costs, as shown through empirical results on the CIFAR-10 dataset. The paper also discusses the convergence of ByzSGDnm under non-convex cases and provides a theoretical analysis of the optimal batch size in BRDL.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-organized, clearly written, and easy to follow, making complex concepts accessible to a broad audience.
- The theoretical analysis is solid, with a novel proof of the convergence of ByzSGDnm, which is a significant contribution to the field.
- The empirical results are compelling, showing that ByzSGDnm outperforms existing methods like ByzSGDm and Byz-VR-MARINA in certain scenarios, demonstrating its practical utility.
- The paper addresses an important and relevant problem in distributed learning, focusing on the impact of batch size on model accuracy and communication costs, which is a critical aspect of Byzantine-robust distributed learning.
- The convergence analysis of ByzSGDnm under non-convex cases provides valuable insights into the behavior of the algorithm in more realistic scenarios.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed comparison with other existing methods, particularly those that also employ large batch sizes, which could have strengthened the argument for the superiority of ByzSGDnm.
- The experimental setup is limited to the CIFAR-10 dataset and a single GPU, which may not fully demonstrate the scalability or robustness of the proposed method across different datasets and hardware configurations.
- There is a lack of discussion on the potential limitations or negative societal impacts of the proposed method, which could be crucial for understanding its broader implications.
- The theoretical analysis and empirical results do not fully support the claim that a larger batch size always leads to better performance, as shown in the experimental results.
- The paper could benefit from a more detailed discussion on the scalability of the proposed method with the number of workers and the robustness against different types of attacks.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors provide more detailed comparisons with other methods that also employ large batch sizes to strengthen the argument for the superiority of ByzSGDnm?
- How does the proposed method perform when scaled to a larger number of workers, and what are the implications for communication costs and model accuracy?
- Can the authors discuss the potential limitations or negative societal impacts of the proposed method, and how these might be mitigated or addressed?
- How does the proposed method perform against different types of attacks, particularly those that are not specifically addressed in the current study?
- Could the authors provide more detailed experimental results, including the wall-clock running time for different batch sizes and the impact of varying the learning rate on the performance of ByzSGDnm?
