 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel benchmark, Performance-Improving Edits (PIE), for evaluating code optimization using large language models (LLMs). The benchmark, which includes a dataset of 77,000 C++ program pairs, is used to train and evaluate various LLMs, including CodeLlama and GPT-3.5, using techniques like prompting and fine-tuning. The authors demonstrate that these models can effectively optimize code, achieving significant speedups over baseline methods. The paper also discusses the challenges in benchmarking code performance and the potential for improving LLMs' ability to generate high-performance code.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel benchmark, PIE, which is a significant contribution to the field of LLM-based code optimization.
- The authors have conducted extensive experiments, including prompting and fine-tuning, to demonstrate the effectiveness of their approach.
- The paper is well-written, clear, and easy to follow, with a detailed appendix that provides additional insights and results.
- The proposed dataset, PIE, is comprehensive and includes a variety of performance-improving edits, which can be used to train and evaluate different LLMs.
- The paper presents a novel approach to adapting LLMs for code optimization, which is a significant challenge in the field.
- The dataset and code are expected to be released, which will benefit the community and allow for further research and validation of the proposed methods.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed discussion on the limitations of the proposed benchmark, which is crucial for understanding its applicability and scope.
- The paper does not compare its results with other benchmarks or baselines, which could provide a more robust evaluation of the proposed methods.
- There is a lack of discussion on the potential negative societal impacts of the work, which is an important consideration for any technological advancement.
- The paper does not provide sufficient details on the selection criteria for the problems in the dataset, which could affect the generalizability of the results.
- The paper does not discuss the scalability of the proposed methods, especially in terms of the size and complexity of the codebase, which is a significant challenge in practical applications.
- The paper does not provide a detailed analysis of the generated code, which could provide insights into the quality and effectiveness of the optimizations.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors clarify the selection criteria for the problems in the dataset and discuss how they ensure the diversity and representativeness of the problems?
- How do the authors address the scalability of their proposed methods to larger and more complex codebases?
- Could the authors provide more details on the quality and effectiveness of the optimizations generated by the LLMs, especially in terms of correctness and performance?
- How does the proposed benchmark compare with other existing benchmarks or datasets in terms of quality and effectiveness?
- Can the authors discuss the potential negative societal impacts of their work and propose mitigation strategies if necessary?
- How does the proposed benchmark ensure the reliability and reproducibility of the performance measurements, especially in practical applications?
- Can the authors provide more details on the generation process of synthetic data and discuss its impact on the performance of the LLMs?
