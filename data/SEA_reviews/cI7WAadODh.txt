 **Summary:**
The paper investigates the minimization of polarization in social networks, focusing on the Friedkin-Johnsen model of opinion dynamics. It introduces a non-convex relaxation method for minimizing polarization under budget constraints, showing that the optimization problem is NP-hard. The paper also discusses the global optimality of the proposed method and its effectiveness in reducing polarization in various network scenarios. It extends the analysis to include scenarios where initial opinions are only partially known, which is a novel contribution. The paper is structured around theoretical results, including the characterization of the polarization function as invex, and empirical results showing the effectiveness of the proposed method in reducing polarization in real-world and synthetic networks.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, clear, and structured, making it easy to follow.
- The theoretical results are strong, including the global optimality of the proposed method and the characterization of the polarization function as invex.
- The paper introduces a novel non-convex relaxation method for minimizing polarization, which is supported by empirical results showing its effectiveness.
- The paper extends the analysis to scenarios where initial opinions are only partially known, which is a novel contribution.
- The authors provide a comprehensive review of related work, which helps in understanding the context and significance of their research.

**Weaknesses:**

- The paper does not adequately discuss the limitations of the proposed method, which could help in understanding its applicability and potential drawbacks.
