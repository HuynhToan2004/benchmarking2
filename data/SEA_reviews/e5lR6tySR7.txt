 **Summary:**
The paper explores the expressive power of Transformer-based large language models (LLMs) from a theoretical perspective using universal circuits. It argues that a constant-depth-polynomial-size log-precision Transformer-based LLM cannot perform all basic operators of input length O(poly(log n)) or faithfully execute prompts of complexity n. The authors also demonstrate that these models can memorize O(poly(n)) instances, which might explain their successes in complex tasks despite theoretical limitations. The paper employs circuit complexity theory to analyze the computational power of LLMs, suggesting that they are not general learners due to their inability to simulate all polynomial-size circuits. The theoretical findings are supported by empirical evidence, highlighting the limitations of current LLM architectures and suggesting potential avenues for future research in architectures that could surpass these limitations.

**Strengths:**
- The paper provides a rigorous analysis of the expressive power of Transformer-based LLMs from a theoretical perspective, which is a significant contribution to the field.
- The authors effectively use circuit complexity theory to analyze the computational power of LLMs, demonstrating a deep understanding of the theoretical underpinnings of these models.
- The paper is well-organized and clearly written, making complex theoretical concepts accessible and understandable.
- The findings of the paper are significant and contribute to the ongoing discussion about the limitations and potential of current LLM architectures.
- The paper provides a novel perspective on the capabilities of LLMs by considering them in the context of universal circuits, which is a valuable approach for understanding the theoretical limitations of these models.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper's theoretical analysis might not be fully applicable to real-world LLMs due to the simplifying assumptions made, such as constant depth and fixed precision, which might not reflect the complexity of real-world LLMs.
- The paper does not sufficiently address the impact of data and learning on the computational power of LLMs, which could be a significant factor in their observed capabilities.
- The paper's conclusions about the expressive power of LLMs are somewhat expected based on existing literature, which might limit the perceived novelty of the findings.
- The paper could benefit from a more detailed discussion on the limitations of the theoretical framework and how these limitations might affect the practical applicability of the results.
- There is a lack of empirical evidence to support the claims made about the memorization capabilities of LLMs, which could be crucial for validating the theoretical predictions.

**Questions:**
- How does the paper's theoretical analysis compare to the actual performance of LLMs on tasks that require general problem-solving abilities?
- Could the authors elaborate on the practical implications of the theoretical results, especially in terms of how they might guide the design of future LLM architectures?
- How does the paper's analysis address the potential impact of data and learning on the computational power of LLMs?
- Could the authors provide more concrete examples or empirical evidence to support the claims about the memorization capabilities of LLMs?
- How do the authors respond to the concerns about the practical applicability of the theoretical results, especially in light of the simplifying assumptions made in the analysis?
