 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel function-space prior for Bayesian Neural Networks (BNNs) that aims to improve uncertainty estimation and out-of-distribution (OOD) detection. The authors propose a Bayesian model averaging (BMA) approach to construct the prior, utilizing the empirical mean and covariance of hidden features and the last layer's weights to define the function-space prior. This method is designed to provide a more informative prior compared to traditional uniform priors, which can lead to better calibration of predictive probabilities. The paper also includes a variational distribution and a refined function-space variational inference method, which are evaluated against existing methods on benchmark datasets. However, the paper suffers from issues such as lack of clarity in some sections, unclear presentation of results, and insufficient comparison with state-of-the-art methods.

**Strengths:**
- The paper addresses an important problem of uncertainty estimation in Bayesian Neural Networks (BNNs) and introduces a novel approach using a Bayesian model averaging (BMA) inspired prior.
- The method is well-motivated, and the paper is generally well-written, making it easy to follow.
- The proposed method achieves good results in terms of uncertainty estimation and out-of-distribution (OOD) detection, showing improvements over existing methods in some cases.
- The idea of using a function-space prior to improve uncertainty estimation is novel and innovative, providing a new perspective on the use of BNNs.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks clarity in some sections, particularly in the introduction and the explanation of the methodology. There are several grammatical and typographical errors that need correction.
- The presentation of results is not clear, with some tables and figures being hard to read or understand. The experimental results section could benefit from better visual aids and clearer explanations.
- The paper does not sufficiently compare its method with state-of-the-art methods, which could limit the understanding of its relative performance.
- There is a lack of detailed discussion on the limitations of the proposed method and its applicability in different scenarios.
- The paper does not adequately address the computational complexity of the method, which could be a significant concern for practical applications.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors clarify the role and definition of the categorical latent variable z in the context of the proposed method?
- How does the proposed method perform in terms of computational efficiency, especially in comparison to other methods?
- Can the authors provide more detailed explanations and examples of how the method handles different types of data, such as images or text, to better illustrate its applicability?
- How does the proposed method compare to other uncertainty quantification methods that do not rely on Bayesian Neural Networks?
- Could the authors elaborate on the choice of using adversarial hidden features in the context of the proposed method?
- In the experimental results section, could the authors provide more detailed explanations of the results, especially those that show significant variations or uncertainties?
