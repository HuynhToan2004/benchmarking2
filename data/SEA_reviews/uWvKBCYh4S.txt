 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel method for combining multiple LoRA experts using a gating function, aiming to preserve the individual characteristics of each LoRA. This method, termed Mixture of LoRA Experts (MoLE), is evaluated across various domains including NLP and VL tasks, demonstrating improvements over existing LoRA fusion methods. The approach is validated through extensive experiments, showcasing the method's effectiveness in handling different types of data and its ability to adapt to various scenarios. The paper also discusses the challenges and potential limitations of the proposed method, suggesting areas for further research and refinement.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel method for combining multiple LoRA experts, which is a significant contribution to the field.
- The proposed method is well-motivated and the experiments are well-designed, showing the effectiveness of the proposed method.
- The paper is well-written and easy to follow, making it accessible to a broad audience.
- The methodology is well-explained, and the paper provides a clear and detailed description of the experimental setup and results.
- The paper addresses an important and relevant problem in the field of LoRA adaptation, which is crucial for practical applications in various domains.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed comparison with other LoRA fusion methods, such as LoRAhub, which could have provided a more robust evaluation of the proposed method.
- The paper does not adequately discuss the limitations of the proposed method, which is crucial for understanding its applicability and potential drawbacks.
- The paper could benefit from a more comprehensive evaluation on different types of tasks and domains to validate the generalizability of the proposed method.
- The paper could provide more detailed insights into the training process of the gating function and the selection of LoRA experts, which are critical components of the proposed method.
- The paper does not sufficiently address the scalability of the proposed method, especially in terms of the number of LoRA experts and the complexity of the tasks.
- The paper could benefit from a more thorough discussion on the potential negative societal impacts of the proposed method.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors provide a more detailed comparison with other LoRA fusion methods, especially LoRAhub, to better understand the relative strengths and weaknesses of the proposed method?
- How does the proposed method perform when applied to different types of tasks and domains beyond those tested in the paper?
- Can the authors provide more insights into the training process of the gating function and the selection of LoRA experts to enhance the reproducibility and applicability of the proposed method?
- How does the proposed method scale with an increasing number of LoRA experts, and what are the potential challenges or limitations in such scenarios?
- Could the authors discuss the potential negative societal impacts of the proposed method, especially in terms of data privacy and security?
- How does the proposed method compare to other state-of-the-art methods in terms of computational efficiency and resource utilization?
