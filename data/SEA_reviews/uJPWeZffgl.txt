 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel approach to address the challenge of learning in neuro-symbolic systems by formulating the learning problem as a bilevel optimization problem. This approach involves a smooth reformulation of the NeuPSL energy-based model, which is then used to derive a gradient-based optimization method for end-to-end learning of both neural and symbolic parameters. The method leverages a dual block coordinate descent algorithm to improve learning efficiency, achieving significant runtime improvements over existing methods. The paper also provides empirical evidence of these improvements through experiments on various datasets, demonstrating the effectiveness of the proposed method.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel approach to solving neuro-symbolic learning problems by formulating them as bilevel optimization problems, which is a significant contribution to the field.
- The proposed method for optimizing NeuPSL, a state-of-the-art neuro-symbolic system, is innovative and provides a new perspective on how to tackle these complex problems.
- The paper is well-written, clearly articulating the methodology and results, which is crucial for understanding the complex concepts presented.
- The experimental results are robust, demonstrating the effectiveness of the proposed method across various datasets, which validates the theoretical claims.
- The paper provides a comprehensive evaluation of the proposed method, including a thorough discussion of the limitations, which enhances the credibility of the findings.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper does not adequately discuss the limitations of the proposed method, which is crucial for understanding the scope and applicability of the findings.
- There is a lack of comparison with other state-of-the-art methods, which makes it difficult to assess the relative performance of the proposed method.
- The paper does not provide a detailed explanation of the computational complexity of the proposed method, which is essential for understanding its practical implications.
- The paper does not discuss the scalability of the proposed method, which is crucial for assessing its applicability to larger, more complex problems.
- There is a lack of clarity in the presentation of the methodology, particularly in the explanation of the NeuPSL model and the bilevel optimization approach, which could confuse readers unfamiliar with these concepts.
- The paper does not adequately address the issue of scalability, which is a significant concern for practical applications of the proposed method.

**Questions:**
