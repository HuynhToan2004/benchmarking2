 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a unified framework for Bayesian optimization under contextual uncertainty (BOCU), which extends the existing distributionally robust Bayesian optimization (DRBO) framework to include various uncertainty objectives. It proposes a general Thompson sampling algorithm capable of optimizing any objective within the BOCU framework, providing theoretical analysis and experimental validation. The framework integrates several existing and new uncertainty objectives, such as worst-case sensitivity and robust satisficing, and demonstrates the utility of the proposed approach through numerical experiments. Despite the theoretical and empirical contributions, the paper has been critiqued for its limited novelty and the lack of a clear distinction from existing works.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a unified framework for Bayesian optimization under contextual uncertainty (BOCU), which subsumes various existing uncertainty objectives and extends the distributionally robust Bayesian optimization (DRBO) framework.
- The authors propose a general Thompson sampling algorithm for BOCU, which optimizes any objective covered within the framework and provides theoretical analysis and experimental validation.
- The paper is well-written, clear, and easy to follow, with a clear introduction and related work section that effectively positions the paper within the existing literature.
- The theoretical results are robust, and the numerical experiments demonstrate the utility of the proposed approach.
- The paper is innovative in its approach to extending the DRBO framework to a general framework that includes many other uncertainty objectives studied in the BOCU literature.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper does not provide a clear distinction from existing works, particularly in terms of its novelty and contributions.
- The theoretical analysis and proofs are not thoroughly explained, which could hinder understanding for readers not familiar with the subject matter.
- The experimental section lacks sufficient detail, particularly regarding the choice of parameters and the justification for the experimental settings.
- The paper's assumptions and limitations are not discussed in detail, which could limit the understanding of the practical applicability and robustness of the proposed framework.
- The paper's experimental evaluation is limited, and the choice of benchmarks and the comparison with other methods could be improved.
- The paper's assumptions and the proof of Theorem 4.1 are not convincingly justified, which could affect the credibility of the theoretical results.

**Questions:**
- How does the proposed approach compare with existing methods in terms of computational efficiency and practical applicability?
- Could you clarify the novelty of the proposed approach and its contributions to the existing literature?
- What are the specific advantages of using the proposed framework over existing methods in terms of performance and efficiency?
- How do the authors address the practical challenges of choosing the parameters for the Thompson sampling algorithm in real-world applications?
- Could you provide more details on the experimental settings and the choice of benchmarks to better understand the performance of the proposed algorithm?
- In light of the assumptions made in Theorem 4.1, how do the authors justify the practical applicability of the theoretical results?
