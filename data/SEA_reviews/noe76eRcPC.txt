 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel method for jointly estimating camera poses and reconstructing 3D shapes from unposed images, utilizing a transformer-based architecture. The approach employs a differentiable PnP solver to predict camera poses from image patches, which are then used to reconstruct a triplane NeRF. The methodology involves tokenizing input images and triplane NeRFs, using a single-stream transformer for processing, and a differentiable PnP solver for pose prediction. The paper demonstrates the method's effectiveness through extensive experiments and comparisons with state-of-the-art methods, showing improvements in both pose estimation and NeRF reconstruction quality. However, concerns are raised about the scalability of the method, the accuracy of the pose estimation, and the lack of comparisons with other baseline methods.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel method for jointly estimating camera poses and reconstructing 3D shapes from unposed images, which is a significant contribution to the field of 3D reconstruction.
- The methodology is well-organized, making it easy to follow, and the paper is well-written, enhancing its readability and comprehensibility.
- Extensive experiments are conducted, demonstrating the effectiveness of the proposed method, and the paper provides a comprehensive evaluation of the method's performance.
- The paper presents a clear and detailed description of the proposed method, which is supported by thorough experiments and comparisons with state-of-the-art methods.
- The use of a transformer-based architecture for jointly estimating camera poses and reconstructing 3D shapes is innovative and shows potential for significant impact in the field.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed explanation of how the method scales with the number of images, which is crucial for understanding its practical applicability.
- There is a significant concern about the accuracy of the pose estimation provided by the method, which is critical for applications such as pose-based 3D reconstruction.
- The paper does not adequately compare the proposed method with other baseline methods, which limits the ability to assess its relative performance.
- The method's complexity and the computational demands are not adequately discussed, which could affect its practical implementation.
- The paper does not sufficiently address the potential negative societal impacts of the proposed method, which is a significant omission in the ethical considerations section.
- The paper lacks a discussion on the limitations of the proposed method, which is essential for a complete understanding of its applicability and potential shortcomings.
