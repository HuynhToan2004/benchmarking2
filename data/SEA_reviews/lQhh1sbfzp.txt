 **Summary:**
The paper introduces Differential Model Scaling (DMS), a novel method for Neural Architecture Search (NAS) that focuses on optimizing the width and depth of neural network architectures. DMS utilizes a differentiable top-k operator to model structural hyperparameters, making the process more efficient and cost-effective compared to traditional NAS methods. This approach is evaluated across various tasks and architectures, including vision and language models, and shows promising results in improving model performance and reducing search costs. However, concerns were raised about the novelty of the method, the clarity of its advantages over existing methods, and the depth of its experimental validation.
