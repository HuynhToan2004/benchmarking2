 **Summary:**
The paper introduces IDEA, a novel method for enhancing the adversarial robustness of Graph Neural Networks (GNNs) by focusing on the causal features of graphs. IDEA leverages a causal model to learn node and structural features that are invariant to adversarial attacks, using a combination of node-based and structure-based invariance objectives. The method is validated through extensive experiments, demonstrating superior performance over existing baselines across various attack scenarios. The paper also provides theoretical insights into the causal invariance of the proposed method.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel perspective on graph adversarial robustness by focusing on causal features, which are shown to be beneficial for defending against adversarial attacks.
- The methodology is supported by a theoretical analysis, specifically Proposition 2, which provides a solid foundation for the proposed approach.
- The experimental results are robust, showing that IDEA significantly outperforms existing baselines across various attack scenarios, indicating its practical utility in real-world applications.
- The paper is well-written and easy to follow, making the complex concepts accessible to a broader audience.
- The proposed method is technically sound, with a clear motivation and a novel approach to learning causal features.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a clear definition and discussion of the term "causal feature," which is crucial for understanding the core contributions of the study.
- The theoretical analysis, particularly the assumptions and conclusions, is not sufficiently clear or well-explained, making it difficult for readers to fully grasp the implications and contributions of the proposed method.
- The paper does not provide sufficient ablation studies to demonstrate the effectiveness of each component of the proposed method, which could strengthen the argument for its practical utility.
- The experimental setup and the choice of hyperparameters are not adequately justified, which could limit the generalizability and applicability of the findings.
- There are some minor issues with the writing, such as unclear sentences and grammatical errors, which could detract from the overall quality of the paper.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors clarify the definition and role of "causal features" in the context of the proposed method?
- How does the proposed method perform under adaptive attacks, where the attacker is aware of the defense mechanism?
- Could the authors provide more details on the computational complexity of the proposed method compared to existing baselines?
- What are the specific advantages of using IDEA over other existing defense methods, especially in terms of generalization and efficiency?
- How does the method handle different types of adversarial attacks, and what are the implications for its performance under varied attack conditions?
- Could the authors elaborate on the choice of hyperparameters and their impact on the performance of the proposed method?
