 **Summary:**
The paper introduces JoinGym, a novel simulator designed to accelerate research in the intersection of reinforcement learning (RL) and database query optimization. It addresses the query order selection (QOS) problem, which is known to be NP-hard, by providing a lightweight and easy-to-use environment that enables rapid prototyping of RL algorithms on realistic database problems. The simulator is based on a POCMDP formulation and includes a dataset of intermediate result cardinalities for 3300 queries from the IMDb dataset. The paper benchmarks various RL algorithms and identifies challenges such as long-tailed returns, generalization in combinatorial optimization, and partial observability. Despite its strengths, the paper faces criticism for its limited technical contribution, insufficient experimental validation, and potential misalignment between the simulator's design and the complexity of the real-world QOS problem.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, clear, and easy to follow, making it accessible to a broad audience.
- The proposed simulator, JoinGym, is a significant contribution that can accelerate research in the intersection of RL and systems, particularly in the area of database query optimization.
- The simulator's design is innovative, focusing on simulating query executions offline, which is a novel approach that could potentially reduce costs associated with live system setups and query executions.
- The paper provides a new dataset of intermediate result cardinalities, which can be beneficial for improving cardinality estimation algorithms and representation learning for query and table embeddings.
- The authors have conducted extensive experiments to benchmark various RL algorithms, which could serve as a valuable resource for future research in this area.

**Weaknesses:**
- The technical contribution of the paper is limited, as it primarily involves using existing techniques without significant innovation.
- The paper lacks a detailed discussion on the limitations of the proposed simulator and the challenges it might pose for future research.
- The experimental validation is insufficient, with a lack of comparison with other existing simulators and a limited set of queries used in experiments.
- The paper does not adequately address the complexity of the real-world QOS problem, which might affect the applicability and effectiveness of the proposed solutions.
- The paper could benefit from more extensive comparisons with other existing simulators and a broader set of experiments to validate the effectiveness of the proposed methods.

**Questions:**
- How does the proposed simulator compare to other existing simulators in terms of performance, efficiency, and ease of use?
- Can the authors provide more details on the limitations of the proposed simulator and the challenges it might pose for future research?
- How do the authors plan to address the complexity of the real-world QOS problem, especially in terms of ensuring that the simulator's solutions are effective in practical scenarios?
- Could the authors clarify the discrepancies and potential errors in the formulation of the problem in Section 4.1?
- How does the proposed simulator handle the computational complexity of simulating query executions offline, and what are the implications for the scalability and applicability of the approach?
