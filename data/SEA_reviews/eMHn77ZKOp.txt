 **Summary:**
The paper explores the k-max combinatorial bandit problem where the agent selects k arms and the reward is the maximum value of the selected arms. The feedback is limited to the maximum value and the index of the arm achieving it, which is a novel feedback structure that lies between semi-bandit and full-bandit feedback. The authors propose an algorithm and provide a regret bound for problem instances with stochastic arm outcomes according to arbitrary distributions with finite supports. The algorithm is shown to achieve a regret bound comparable to the semi-bandit feedback setting, demonstrating its effectiveness through experimental results. The paper also discusses the application of the algorithm to general discrete distributions with finite supports, showing how it can be adapted to handle more complex feedback structures.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a new feedback structure for combinatorial bandits that lies between semi-bandit and full-bandit feedback, which is a novel contribution to the field.
- The proposed algorithm achieves a regret bound that is comparable to the semi-bandit feedback setting, demonstrating its effectiveness and efficiency.
- The paper is well-written, clear, and easy to follow, making it accessible to readers.
- The authors provide a regret analysis for the proposed algorithm and demonstrate its effectiveness through experimental results, showing that it achieves a regret bound comparable to the semi-bandit feedback setting.
- The paper discusses the application of the algorithm to general discrete distributions with finite supports, extending its applicability beyond specific scenarios.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a discussion on the practical implications and applications of the feedback structure, which could help in understanding its relevance and impact in real-world scenarios.
- There is a lack of comparison with other existing methods, which could help in evaluating the performance and effectiveness of the proposed algorithm more comprehensively.
- The paper does not provide a detailed analysis of the regret bounds and their implications, which could be beneficial for understanding the performance of the algorithm in different scenarios.
- The paper could benefit from a more detailed discussion on the assumptions made and their implications, as well as a more thorough comparison with related works.
- The paper lacks a discussion on the computational complexity and time complexity of the proposed algorithm, which are crucial for understanding its practical feasibility.
