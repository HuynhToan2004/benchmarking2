 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel variant of predictive coding called incremental predictive coding (iPC), which updates both activations and weights at every time step, thereby improving the training efficiency and stability of predictive coding networks. This method is compared against traditional backpropagation and other predictive coding methods across various tasks, including image classification and language modeling, showing promising results. Theoretical underpinnings and empirical evidence support the claims of faster convergence and improved performance, although some concerns are raised about the novelty and the clarity of the presentation.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, with clear and concise language that effectively communicates the content.
- The empirical results demonstrate that iPC outperforms other methods, showing a significant improvement in performance and stability.
- Theoretical guarantees and empirical evidence support the claims of faster convergence and improved performance.
- The algorithm is simple, intuitive, and easy to implement, which could make it appealing to a broader audience.
- The paper is structured in a logical and coherent manner, with a clear and well-organized presentation.
- The paper provides a thorough analysis of the limitations of predictive coding and proposes a novel algorithm to address these issues.

**Weaknesses:**
- The novelty of the algorithm is questionable as it closely resembles existing methods like Z-IL and incremental EM, which have been previously proposed.
- The paper lacks a detailed comparison with other predictive coding algorithms, such as forward-forward, which might perform better or differently.
- The paper does not provide a detailed analysis of the computational complexity or memory requirements of iPC, which could be crucial for practical implementation.
- The paper does not adequately discuss the biological plausibility of the algorithm, which could be a significant concern for some readers.
- There are some presentation issues, such as unclear figures and a lack of clarity in the experimental setup and results.
- The paper does not sufficiently address the limitations of the proposed method, such as its scalability and the potential impact of hyperparameter choices on performance.

**Questions:**

- How does iPC compare to other predictive coding algorithms like forward-forward, especially in terms of computational efficiency and performance?
- Can you provide more details on the computational complexity and memory requirements of iPC, and how these compare to other methods?
- How does the biological plausibility of iPC compare to other predictive coding algorithms, and what are the implications of this for practical implementation?
- Could you elaborate on the choice of hyperparameters and their impact on the performance of iPC?
- How does iPC perform in more complex or larger-scale tasks, and what are the potential limitations of the algorithm in such scenarios?
