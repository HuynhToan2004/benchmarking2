 **Summary:**
The paper explores the use of graph neural networks (GNNs) to learn approximate dynamics models for intuitive physics tasks, focusing on guiding exploration in reinforcement learning (RL) through action priors. The approach involves learning dynamics models from a set of tasks and using these models to guide action selection towards rewarding regions of the action space. The paper proposes a method where the model's predictions are used to identify high-reward action clusters, which are then used to guide exploration. The experiments are conducted in the Chain Reaction Tool Environment (CREATE), comparing the proposed method against standard DDPG and random exploration. The paper claims that the learned dynamics models can improve sample efficiency in RL by directing exploration towards promising regions of the action space.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces an innovative approach to using approximate models to guide exploration in reinforcement learning, which is a novel application of intuitive physics to RL.
- The use of graph neural networks (GNNs) to learn dynamics models is a significant contribution, particularly in the context of intuitive physics tasks.
- The paper is well-written, with clear explanations of the methodology and experiments, making it accessible to readers.
- The methodology is well-motivated, with a clear focus on improving sample efficiency in RL through the use of learned dynamics models.
- The paper includes a comprehensive literature review, which provides a solid foundation for the research.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a thorough comparison with existing methods, particularly in terms of sample efficiency and the generalization of the learned dynamics models.
- The experiments are limited to a single environment and do not include a broad range of tasks, which limits the generalizability of the findings.
- The methodology is complex and requires careful tuning of parameters, which might limit its applicability in more complex or varied environments.
- The paper does not sufficiently address the scalability of the method to more complex environments or the impact of different model architectures on the performance.
- There is a lack of theoretical analysis or empirical evidence to support the claims about the generalization of the learned dynamics models across different tasks.
- The paper does not include a detailed discussion on the limitations of the proposed method, which is crucial for understanding its applicability and potential drawbacks.

**Questions:**
- How does the method perform when the model's predictions are inaccurate, especially in scenarios where the model's confidence is low?
- Can the method be adapted to environments with more complex or varied dynamics, such as those involving multiple objects with different physical properties?
- How does the method handle scenarios where the model's predictions are not reliable, such as in environments with stochastic or non-deterministic dynamics?
- Could the authors provide more details on the scalability of the method to more complex environments and the impact of different model architectures on its performance?
- What are the specific advantages of using GNNs over other types of models for learning dynamics in intuitive physics tasks?
- How does the method compare in terms of sample efficiency and generalization to other methods that use learned dynamics models for exploration in RL?
