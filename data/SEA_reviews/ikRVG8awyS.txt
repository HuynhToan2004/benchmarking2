 **Summary:**
The paper introduces RFold, a novel method for RNA secondary structure prediction using a decoupled optimization process that decomposes the constraint satisfaction problem into row-wise and column-wise optimization. This approach simplifies the solving process and ensures the validity of the output. RFold utilizes attention maps as informative representations and achieves competitive performance with faster inference efficiency compared to state-of-the-art methods. The methodology includes a Seq2map attention module for sequence-to-map transformation, which simplifies pre-processing steps and enhances the model's generalization ability. Extensive experiments demonstrate RFold's superiority over existing methods, showing improvements in both accuracy and efficiency.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, clear, and easy to follow, with a clear introduction of the problem and a detailed explanation of the proposed method.
- The proposed method is simple yet effective, achieving state-of-the-art performance and faster inference efficiency compared to existing methods.
- The use of attention maps as informative representations and the decoupled optimization process are innovative and contribute to the simplification of the solving process.
- Extensive experiments demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed method, showing improvements in both accuracy and efficiency compared to existing methods.
- The paper provides a comprehensive evaluation of the proposed method, including a thorough comparison with state-of-the-art methods and an ablation study.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed explanation of the Seq2map attention module, particularly how it simplifies pre-processing steps and what specific advantages it offers over other methods.
- There is a lack of discussion on the limitations of the proposed method, particularly how the strict constraints might impact the model's ability to capture all possible interactions.
- The paper could benefit from more detailed explanations and examples in certain sections, such as the introduction of the Seq2map attention module and the optimization process.
- The paper does not compare the proposed method with the latest state-of-the-art methods, which could limit the assessment of its current relevance and effectiveness.
- The paper does not discuss the limitations of the proposed method, such as its ability to handle complex structures like pseudoknots, which could impact its practical applicability.

**Questions:**
- Could you provide more details on how the Seq2map attention module simplifies pre-processing steps and what specific advantages it offers over other methods?
- How does the proposed method handle the strict constraints, and what are the implications of these constraints on the model's ability to capture all possible interactions?
- Could you clarify the optimization process and the role of the Seq2map attention module in this process?
- How does the proposed method compare with the latest state-of-the-art methods, and what are the implications of not including these comparisons in your evaluation?
- Could you provide more examples and detailed explanations in the paper, particularly in the introduction of the Seq2map attention module and the optimization process?
