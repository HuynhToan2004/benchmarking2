 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel unlearning algorithm for diffusion models aimed at mitigating privacy concerns by erasing data influence. The algorithm formulates the unlearning problem as a bi-level optimization problem, with the outer objective preserving model utility and the inner objective deviating from the ground-truth denoising procedure to scrub information. The method was evaluated across various datasets, showing effectiveness in preserving model utility while removing data influence. The paper also discusses the algorithm's efficiency and its potential to prevent data memorization, which is crucial for data protection regulations.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel unlearning algorithm for diffusion models, which formulates the unlearning problem as a bi-level optimization problem, effectively addressing privacy concerns and the right to be forgotten.
- The proposed method is well-motivated and provides a clear and logical presentation, making it accessible and understandable for readers.
- The paper is well-written, with a clear and concise introduction and background sections that effectively set the stage for the proposed method.
- The methodology is well-explained, and the experiments are comprehensive, demonstrating the effectiveness of the proposed method in preserving model utility while removing data influence.
- The paper is original in its approach, offering a new perspective on unlearning in diffusion models, and provides a valuable contribution to the field.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed analysis of the algorithm's efficiency, particularly in terms of computational cost and scalability, which is crucial for practical applications.
- The evaluation of the proposed method is limited, focusing primarily on specific datasets and not providing a comprehensive comparison with other unlearning methods or different types of data.
- The paper does not discuss the potential negative societal impacts of the proposed method, which could be significant in terms of data privacy and security.
- The paper could benefit from a more detailed discussion on the limitations of the proposed method and potential areas for future research.
- The paper's experimental results are not convincingly superior to existing methods, and the method's effectiveness in real-world scenarios is not adequately demonstrated.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors provide more details on the computational cost and scalability of the proposed method?
- How does the proposed method perform when applied to different types of data, such as text or audio data?
- Can the authors provide more visualizations or qualitative results to support the effectiveness of the method in preventing data memorization?
- How does the proposed method compare with other unlearning methods in terms of effectiveness and efficiency?
- Could the authors discuss the potential negative societal impacts of the proposed method and propose mitigation strategies to address these concerns?
- How does the proposed method ensure that the model's utility is not compromised while removing data influence?
