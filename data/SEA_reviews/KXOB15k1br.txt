 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel method for time-series data augmentation named Time-Series AutoAugment (TSAA), which aims to address the challenges of overfitting in time-series forecasting by employing a Bayesian optimization approach to find the optimal augmentation policy. The methodology involves a two-step process: first, a partial training of the non-augmented model for a few epochs to generate a set of shared weights, and second, an iterative split process that alternates between finding a good augmentation policy and fine-tuning the model. The paper claims that TSAA outperforms several strong baselines in most cases, although the novelty and the effectiveness of the approach are questioned due to its similarity to existing methods and the lack of a thorough ablation study.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-organized and easy to follow, with a clear and concise presentation of the methodology and results.
- The problem addressed, the application of data augmentation to time-series data, is both relevant and interesting, with potential for significant impact in forecasting applications.
- The proposed method, TSAA, is novel and well-explained, with a thorough evaluation that shows improvements over several strong baselines in most cases.
- The paper includes a comprehensive evaluation on various datasets and a detailed ablation study, which helps in understanding the effectiveness of different components of the proposed method.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a thorough ablation study, which is crucial for understanding the impact of individual components of the proposed method.
- The novelty of the approach is questionable as it appears to be a combination of existing methods without significant innovation.
- The paper does not provide sufficient details on the implementation of the method, particularly the hyperparameters used, which could affect the reproducibility and comparability of the results.
- The paper does not adequately discuss the limitations of the proposed method, which could include the generalizability of the findings to other datasets or architectures.
- The experimental results do not convincingly demonstrate the superiority of the proposed method over existing methods, particularly in terms of computational efficiency and the effectiveness of the augmentation policies.

**Questions:**
- Could you provide more details on the hyperparameters used in the experiments, particularly the number of augmentation operations and the specific hyperparameters of the Bayesian optimization algorithm?
- How does the proposed method compare to other data augmentation methods for time-series data, especially in terms of computational efficiency and the effectiveness of the augmentation policies?
- Can you clarify the role and impact of the hyperparameter β in the methodology, particularly in the context of the ablation study?
- What are the main takeaways from the experiments, and how do the results support the claims made about the effectiveness of the proposed method?
- Could you discuss the limitations of the proposed method and how they might affect the generalizability of the findings to other datasets or architectures?
