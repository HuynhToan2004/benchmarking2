 **Summary:**
The paper explores the integration of sentiment analysis into stock price prediction models using a dataset sourced from Alpha Vantage and Yahoo Finance. The authors conducted a comparative analysis of various machine learning models, including RNN, LSTM, GAN, and WGAN-GP, and introduced a novel FB-GAN model to leverage sentiment data. The study demonstrates the effectiveness of sentiment data in enhancing prediction accuracy, although the methodology and the novelty of the approach are questioned. The paper's contributions are viewed as incremental and the methodology's rigor is questioned, particularly in the experimental setup and the handling of data.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-structured, clear, and easy to follow, with a comprehensive literature review that effectively situates the study within the existing body of knowledge.
- The inclusion of sentiment analysis in stock price prediction models is a novel and interesting approach, which is well-explained and supported by the study.
- The paper demonstrates a clear hypothesis and a logical flow, making it accessible to readers.
- The use of sentiment data in stock price prediction models is a significant and relevant contribution to the field, particularly in the context of financial forecasting.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks originality as it primarily builds on existing models without significantly advancing the methodology or theoretical framework.
- The dataset used is limited in scope, covering only a short time frame and a small set of stocks, which may not adequately represent the complexity and diversity of real-world financial markets.
- The experimental setup is not robust, with issues such as inconsistencies in data processing and the absence of detailed parameter settings.
- The paper suffers from a lack of rigorous methodology, including insufficient literature review, unclear data processing, and a lack of detail in experimental settings.
- The paper's claims of novelty are overstated, and the contribution of the proposed FB-GAN model is not convincingly differentiated from existing models.
- The paper's presentation could be improved, with issues such as poor figure quality and unclear writing in some sections.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors clarify the methodology used for data processing, particularly how the sentiment scores were assigned and how the relevance scores were calculated?
- How were the parameters for each model determined, and what was the rationale behind the choices made?
- Could the authors provide more details on the dataset used, including its size, diversity, and the criteria for selection?
- How does the FB-GAN model differ significantly from existing models, and what are its unique contributions to the field?
- Given the shortcomings in the experimental setup and methodology, how do the authors plan to address these issues in future research?
