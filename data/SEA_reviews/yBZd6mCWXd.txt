 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel task, Weakly Incremental 3D Object Detection (WI3D), which leverages 2D visual prompts to incrementally learn novel classes in 3D object detection. The approach includes a class-agnostic pseudo label refinement module, cross-modal knowledge transfer, and reweighting knowledge distillation to refine initial pseudo labels and preserve existing class information. The methodology is tested on two benchmark datasets, SUN RGB-D and ScanNet, showing improvements over baseline methods. However, the novelty of the task and the specific contributions of the proposed methods are questioned, particularly in comparison to existing weakly supervised and class-incremental methods.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a new task, Weakly Incremental 3D Object Detection (WI3D), which is both novel and well-defined.
- The proposed method is comprehensive, including a class-agnostic pseudo label refinement module, cross-modal knowledge transfer, and reweighting knowledge distillation, which are all well-motivated and technically sound.
- The paper is well-written, clear, and easy to follow, making it accessible to readers.
- The methodology effectively addresses the challenges of generating accurate 3D pseudo labels from 2D predictions, and the experimental results demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed method.
- The paper includes extensive ablation studies and visualization results, which help to validate the effectiveness of the proposed components.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed discussion on the novelty and specific contributions of the proposed methods compared to existing weakly supervised and class-incremental methods.
- The paper does not adequately compare the proposed method with state-of-the-art methods, which could undermine the claimed improvements.
- There are concerns about the fairness of the comparisons, particularly in the baseline settings, which could affect the validity of the results.
- The paper does not sufficiently address the limitations of the proposed method, such as the impact of different 2D teachers and the scalability of the method with an increasing number of novel classes.
- The paper could benefit from a more thorough discussion on the challenges and potential solutions in the field of weakly supervised 3D object detection.

**Questions:**
- How does the proposed method compare to state-of-the-art methods in terms of performance and efficiency?
- Can the authors provide a detailed comparison of the proposed method with existing weakly supervised and class-incremental methods, highlighting the specific contributions and improvements?
- How does the proposed method handle the scalability issues when the number of novel classes increases significantly?
- Could the authors discuss the potential impact of different 2D teachers on the performance of the proposed method?
- How does the proposed method address the challenges of generating accurate 3D pseudo labels from 2D predictions, particularly in terms of addressing the issues of projection migration, scale ambiguity, and overlapped boxes?
- Can the authors provide more details on the experimental setup, such as the specifics of the baseline methods used and the training settings?
