 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel post-training quantization method for large language models (LLMs) called Low-Rank Quantization (LRQ). LRQ leverages low-rank weight-scaling matrices to minimize the number of learnable parameters, thereby enhancing the generalization performance of quantized LLMs. The methodology is supported by extensive experiments demonstrating the superiority of LRQ over existing PTQ techniques, including 4-bit weight and 8-bit activation quantization with minimal accuracy loss. The paper also provides empirical insights into the significance of reducing the number of learnable parameters and the impact of low-rank matrices on the generalization ability of quantized LLMs.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, clear, and easy to follow, with comprehensive experiments that demonstrate the superiority of the proposed Low-Rank Quantization (LRQ) over existing PTQ techniques.
- The introduction of LRQ, which uses low-rank weight-scaling matrices to reduce the number of learnable parameters, is innovative and provides a significant advancement in the field of LLM quantization.
- The paper presents a novel approach by combining low-rank weight-scaling matrices with block-wise reconstruction, which is effective in reducing the number of learnable parameters and improving the generalization performance of quantized LLMs.
- The empirical insights provided in the paper, particularly the impact of low-rank matrices on the generalization ability of quantized LLMs, are valuable and contribute to a better understanding of the quantization process.
- The paper is the first to perform experiments on 4-bit weight and 8-bit activation quantization for LLMs, which is a significant contribution to the field.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed comparison with other weight quantization methods, such as AQuANT, which could provide a more comprehensive evaluation of the proposed LRQ.
- The paper does not provide a detailed comparison of the inference speed and hardware efficiency of the proposed LRQ with other methods like SmoothQuant and FlexRound, which could be crucial for practical deployment.
- The paper could benefit from a more thorough analysis of the impact of different calibration sample sizes on the performance of LRQ.
- The paper does not discuss the potential negative societal impact of the proposed method, which is a significant omission in the ethical considerations of the work.
- The paper could be improved by including more detailed explanations and examples of how the low-rank matrices are used in the training process, particularly in the context of block-wise reconstruction.

**Questions:**
- Could you provide a detailed comparison of the inference speed and hardware efficiency of LRQ with other methods like SmoothQuant and FlexRound?
- How does the choice of the rank r affect the performance of LRQ, and what are the optimal values for different models?
- Can you explain the rationale behind using a low-rank weight-scaling matrix in the training process, and how does it compare to using a full weight-scaling matrix?
- How does LRQ perform in terms of generalization ability when applied to different types of LLMs, such as those trained on different datasets or with different architectures?
- Could you provide more details on the training process, particularly how the low-rank matrices are initialized and how they are updated during training?
- How does the paper address the potential negative societal impacts of the proposed method, and what steps are being taken to mitigate these impacts?
