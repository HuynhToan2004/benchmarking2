 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel approach to uncertainty quantification using a method termed Uncertainty Aware Information Bottleneck (UA-IB). This method leverages a codebook of encoders to estimate uncertainty based on the distance of new examples from this codebook. The approach is designed to be computationally efficient, requiring only a single forward pass to provide uncertainty estimates, and is tested across various tasks and datasets. The method's effectiveness is demonstrated through experiments that show it outperforms existing methods in Out-of-Distribution (OOD) detection and calibration. However, concerns were raised about the clarity of the paper, particularly in the mathematical formulations and the presentation of experimental results.

**Strengths:**
- The paper presents a novel approach to uncertainty quantification, which is a significant challenge in machine learning.
- The methodology is well-explained and the paper is well-written, making it accessible to readers.
- The experimental results are robust, showing that the proposed method outperforms existing methods in Out-of-Distribution (OOD) detection and calibration.
- The use of a codebook of encoders to estimate uncertainty is innovative and provides a new perspective on uncertainty quantification.
- The paper is generally well-structured, with clear explanations of the mathematical formulations and the experimental setup.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks clarity in some mathematical formulations, particularly in the description of the codebook and the role of different variables in the equations.
- There is a need for more detailed explanations of the experimental results, especially in terms of how the method performs in different scenarios and how the results compare to other methods.
- The paper could benefit from a more thorough discussion of the limitations and potential applications of the proposed method.
- Some sections of the paper, such as the introduction and the discussion on the Information Bottleneck, are not well-explained, making it difficult for readers to fully understand the methodology and its implications.
- The paper could be improved by providing more detailed explanations of the methodology and the experimental setup, including the use of pseudo code and clearer visual aids.

**Questions:**
- Could you clarify the role and definition of the codebook in your methodology? How are the centroids in the codebook determined, and what is the significance of the variable 'k' in the equations?
- How does the method perform in scenarios where the training data is noisy or where the test data significantly differs from the training data?
- What are the potential applications of your method beyond Out-of-Distribution (OOD) detection and calibration?
- Could you provide more details on how the uncertainty estimates provided by your method compare to those from other methods, especially in terms of accuracy and computational efficiency?
- How does the method handle the trade-off between accuracy and computational efficiency, particularly in scenarios where the model may be overconfident in its predictions?
- Can you discuss the relationship between your method and existing methods, such as DUQ and VIB, and how your approach differs or builds upon these prior works?
