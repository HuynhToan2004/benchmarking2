 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel approach to learning dynamics on manifolds using neural ordinary differential equations (Neural ODEs). It proposes a method where the original data is projected onto a low-dimensional manifold, and the dynamics on this manifold are learned using a neural ODE. The authors leverage the Jacobian matrix of the encoder to map from the manifold space to the original space. The methodology is tested on various datasets, including image classification tasks, and the results show improved accuracy and computational efficiency compared to traditional Neural ODEs. However, the paper suffers from several issues, including a lack of clarity in the motivation and theoretical justification, insufficient comparison with existing methods, and a limited discussion on the implications of the results.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, with clear and concise explanations of the methodology, making it accessible to readers.
- The proposed method is novel and innovative, utilizing a manifold learning approach to enhance the understanding of dynamics in high-dimensional systems.
- The experimental results demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed method, showing improved accuracy and computational efficiency compared to traditional Neural ODEs.
- The paper introduces a new approach to learning dynamics on manifolds, which is an interesting and promising direction for future research.
- The theoretical analysis and mathematical proofs provided in the paper are solid, enhancing the credibility of the proposed method.

**Weaknesses:**
