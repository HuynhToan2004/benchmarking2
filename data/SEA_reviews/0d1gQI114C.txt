 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel post-training quantization (PTQ) method, LiDAR-PTQ, specifically designed for 3D lidar-based object detection. It addresses the challenges of sparsity and irregular distribution in point clouds by employing a sparsity-based calibration method and a task-guided global positive loss. The method aims to reduce the disparity between final predictions before and after quantization, ensuring minimal performance degradation while achieving a 3x speedup. Extensive experiments on the Waymo Open Dataset demonstrate the effectiveness of LiDAR-PTQ, achieving state-of-the-art quantization performance. The paper is well-structured and provides a comprehensive evaluation of the proposed method, although it lacks comparisons with some recent methods and lacks a detailed discussion on the limitations and potential negative societal impacts.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel PTQ method, LiDAR-PTQ, specifically designed for 3D lidar-based object detection, which is a significant advancement in the field.
- The methodology is well-structured, with clear explanations of the components and their roles, making the paper easy to follow.
- The paper provides extensive experimental results, demonstrating the effectiveness of the proposed method and showcasing its superiority over existing methods.
- The paper addresses the challenges of sparsity and irregular distribution in point clouds, which are crucial for 3D lidar-based object detection.
- The authors have conducted a thorough analysis of the numerical distribution of feature maps and visualized the main diversity, which helps in understanding the challenges and the effectiveness of the proposed solutions.
- The paper is well-written, with clear figures and tables that aid in understanding the content.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a comparison with some recent methods such as QD-BEV and QD-BEV++, which are also designed for 3D object detection.
- The paper does not discuss the limitations of the proposed method, which could provide insights into its applicability and potential drawbacks.
- There is a lack of discussion on the negative societal impacts of the proposed method, which is crucial for ethical considerations.
- The paper could benefit from a more detailed explanation of the TGPL loss and the rounding-to-nearest operation, which are crucial components of the proposed method.
- The paper lacks a detailed discussion on the computational complexity and memory requirements of the proposed method, which are important for practical deployment.
- The paper does not include a discussion on the potential limitations of the proposed method, which could affect its generalizability and applicability.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors provide a more detailed comparison with QD-BEV and QD-BEV++ to highlight the strengths and advantages of LiDAR-PTQ?
- Can the authors discuss the limitations of the proposed method and how these might affect its practical deployment?
- Could the authors provide a detailed discussion on the computational complexity and memory requirements of the proposed method?
- How does the proposed method perform on other datasets such as nuScenes and KITTI, which are commonly used in 3D object detection?
- Can the authors provide a more detailed explanation of the TGPL loss and the rounding-to-nearest operation, including the specific values used and their impact on the model's performance?
