 **Summary:**
The paper introduces FEATHER, a novel method for lifelong test-time adaptation (TTA) in neural networks, which employs a lightweight adapter module to adapt the model at test time without updating the entire network. FEATHER is designed to be parameter-efficient, using a small number of trainable parameters, and is orthogonal to existing TTA methods, allowing it to be integrated with them. The method is evaluated on various benchmarks, demonstrating comparable or superior performance with fewer parameters, and is shown to be effective in reducing computational and memory costs. However, the paper has been criticized for its limited novelty and incremental contribution, as it primarily builds on existing techniques like CoTTA and does not provide a significant advancement in performance or methodological innovation.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, clear, and easy to follow, making it accessible to a broad audience.
- The proposed method, FEATHER, is simple, intuitive, and effective, reducing computational and memory costs by only updating a small number of trainable parameters.
- FEATHER is orthogonal to existing TTA methods, allowing it to be integrated with them, potentially enhancing their performance.
- The method is evaluated on several benchmarks, demonstrating comparable or superior performance with fewer parameters, and shows significant improvements in computational efficiency.
- The paper is well-structured, providing a clear problem setup and formulation, and includes a detailed description of the proposed method.
- The initialization scheme of the adapter parameters is innovative, making the proposed adapters compatible with full test-time adaptation.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed comparison with existing methods in terms of computational complexity, training time, and memory usage, which is crucial for understanding the practical efficiency of the proposed method.
- The novelty of the paper is limited, as the use of adapters in TTA has been previously explored, and the method primarily builds on existing techniques like CoTTA.
- The paper does not provide a detailed analysis of the performance of the proposed method across different datasets or in more challenging scenarios, such as with different model architectures or larger datasets.
- The paper does not include a detailed discussion on the limitations of the proposed method, which is essential for understanding its applicability and potential drawbacks.
- The paper lacks a detailed comparison with the most recent and relevant works, such as EcoTTA and MECTA, which have shown superior performance in certain scenarios.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors provide a more detailed comparison of the computational complexity, training time, and memory usage of the proposed method compared to existing TTA methods?
- How does the performance of the proposed method vary across different datasets, and what are the specific advantages or disadvantages of using FEATHER with different model architectures?
- Is there a specific reason why the proposed method was not evaluated on larger datasets like ImageNet-1k, and how does the performance compare to existing methods on such datasets?
- Could the authors provide a detailed discussion on the limitations of the proposed method, and how these limitations affect its applicability and performance in real-world scenarios?
- How does the proposed method compare to the most recent and relevant works, such as EcoTTA and MECTA, which have shown superior performance in certain scenarios?
- Could the authors provide more details on the initialization scheme of the adapter parameters and how it affects the performance of the method?
