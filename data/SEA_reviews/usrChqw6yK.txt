 **Summary:**
The paper introduces DVDet, a novel method for open-vocabulary object detection that leverages hierarchical textual descriptors and conditional context prompts to enhance region-text alignment. DVDet utilizes a hierarchical update mechanism to iteratively generate and refine fine-grained descriptors, which are then used to align visual and textual embeddings, thereby improving the performance of open-vocabulary detectors. The method is evaluated on multiple benchmarks and shows consistent improvements over existing methods. However, the paper's novelty and the effectiveness of the proposed components are questioned, with some reviewers expressing concerns about the clarity of the method's components and the significance of the improvements.

**Strengths:**
- The paper addresses the problem of open-vocabulary object detection, a significant and under-explored area in the field.
- The proposed method is well-motivated and the design of the conditional context visual prompt is novel and effective.
- The use of LLMs as an interactive and implicit knowledge repository for iterative mining and refining visually oriented textual descriptors is a novel approach.
- The paper is well-written and easy to follow, with clear figures and a clear method description.
- The experiments are comprehensive, and the ablation studies are informative, demonstrating the effectiveness of the proposed method.
- The paper shows consistent improvements over existing methods, indicating the effectiveness of the proposed approach.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a clear explanation of the prompt design and the semantic selection strategy, making it difficult to understand the method's operation.
- The novelty of the proposed method is limited, as similar approaches have been explored in previous works, such as DetPro and PromptDet.
- The paper does not sufficiently compare the proposed method with these prior works, which could have provided a clearer picture of its advantages and disadvantages.
- The paper does not provide a detailed analysis of the limitations of the proposed method, which could help in understanding its applicability and potential drawbacks.
- The paper does not include a comparison with the latest open-vocabulary detectors, such as BARON, which could have provided a more comprehensive evaluation of the proposed method.
- The paper's claims about the effectiveness of the proposed method are not sufficiently supported by the experimental results, which do not convincingly demonstrate significant improvements over existing methods.

**Questions:**
- Could you clarify the prompt design and the semantic selection strategy to make the method more understandable?
- How does the proposed method compare with existing open-vocabulary detectors, particularly in terms of performance and efficiency?
- What are the potential limitations of the proposed method, and how might these affect its practical application?
- How does the proposed method handle the generation of fine-grained descriptors for rare or unseen categories?
- Could you provide more details on the computational cost of the proposed method, particularly in terms of memory and time requirements?
- How does the proposed method handle the potential for overfitting due to the iterative refinement of descriptors?
- Could you provide more details on the experimental setup, such as the specifics of the LLMs used and the training parameters?
