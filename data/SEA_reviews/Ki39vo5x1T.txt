 **Summary:**
The paper investigates the federated learning of offline policies using observational data across multiple heterogeneous data sources. It introduces a novel algorithm that aggregates local policies trained with doubly robust offline policy evaluation and learning strategies, providing regret bounds for both the central server and individual clients. The algorithm is designed to handle data heterogeneity and distribution shifts, which are common in federated learning settings. The paper also discusses the theoretical aspects of the algorithm, including regret analysis and its application in federated offline policy learning. The experiments conducted in both homogeneous and heterogeneous settings demonstrate the algorithm's effectiveness in handling data heterogeneity and distribution shifts.

**Strengths:**
- The paper addresses an important problem in federated learning of offline policies using observational data, which is a significant and timely research topic.
- The algorithm proposed is novel and well-suited for federated learning, with a clear and easy-to-follow presentation of the methodology and theoretical analysis.
- The paper provides a comprehensive regret analysis for both the central server and individual clients, which is a key contribution to the field.
- The empirical results show that the proposed algorithm can effectively handle data heterogeneity and distribution shifts, demonstrating its practical applicability.
- The paper is well-organized, with clear definitions and a clear presentation of the methodology, making it accessible to readers.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed discussion on the limitations of the proposed algorithm, particularly in terms of its assumptions and the conditions under which it may not perform optimally.
- The empirical results are based on a small number of clients, which might not adequately represent real-world scenarios with a larger number of clients.
- The paper does not provide a comparison with other baseline methods, which could help in evaluating the performance of the proposed algorithm more robustly.
- There is a lack of discussion on the scalability of the algorithm, especially in terms of computational complexity and memory requirements, which could be critical in large-scale applications.
- The paper could benefit from a more detailed discussion on the assumptions and their implications, particularly how they affect the practical applicability of the algorithm.
