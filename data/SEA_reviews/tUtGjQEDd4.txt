 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel generative modeling framework, Acceleration Generative Modeling (AGM), which leverages insights from Stochastic Optimal Control (SOC) to construct a path measure in the phase space, enabling efficient sampling. The AGM framework is designed to generate realistic data points at an early stage of dynamics propagation, which is a significant advancement over traditional diffusion models. The authors demonstrate that AGM outperforms existing models in small Number of Function Evaluations (NFEs) scenarios, showcasing its potential as a new tool for generative modeling. However, the paper suffers from a lack of clarity in presentation, which makes it challenging for readers to follow. The methodology, while innovative, is not clearly distinguished from existing models like CLD, and the experimental results, while promising, do not convincingly surpass current state-of-the-art methods in terms of performance metrics.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel generative modeling framework, AGM, which leverages insights from Stochastic Optimal Control (SOC) to construct a path measure in the phase space, enabling efficient sampling.
- AGM demonstrates the capability to generate realistic data points at an early stage of dynamics propagation, a significant advancement over traditional diffusion models.
- The authors have achieved competitive results compared to DM approaches equipped with specifically designed fast sampling techniques on image datasets, particularly in small NFE settings.
- The paper is well-written and provides a clear motivation for the research, with a strong theoretical foundation.
- The AGM framework is original and offers a new perspective on accelerating sampling in generative modeling by leveraging additional information from the dynamics.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper suffers from a lack of clarity in presentation, making it challenging for readers to follow.
- The methodology is not clearly distinguished from existing models like CLD, and the paper does not adequately address how AGM improves upon or differs from these existing models.
- The experimental results do not convincingly surpass current state-of-the-art methods in terms of performance metrics such as FID scores.
- The paper lacks a thorough discussion on the limitations and potential negative societal impacts of the proposed method.
- The paper does not provide sufficient empirical evidence to support the claims made about the superiority of the AGM framework over existing models.
- The paper does not adequately discuss the computational complexity of the proposed method compared to existing models.
