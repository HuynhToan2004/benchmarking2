 **Summary:**
The paper introduces the Cloud-Edge Model Adaptation (CEMA) framework aimed at enhancing model adaptation in cloud-edge environments, focusing on reducing communication costs and improving adaptation performance. CEMA employs a dynamic entropy thresholding scheme to selectively upload test samples to the cloud, thus reducing the number of samples transmitted. It also utilizes a replay buffer to store samples, which are reused for knowledge distillation to enhance the performance of the edge model. The framework is evaluated on ImageNet-C and ImageNet-R datasets, demonstrating improved performance and reduced communication costs compared to existing methods. However, the novelty of the approach is questioned due to its reliance on existing techniques like entropy minimization and knowledge distillation, and the paper's experimental setup is criticized for not adequately addressing the computational and communication costs.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written and easy to follow, with clear figures and a well-organized structure.
- The proposed Cloud-Edge Model Adaptation (CEMA) framework is well-motivated and addresses the significant problem of communication costs in cloud-edge model adaptation.
- The experimental results show that CEMA outperforms existing methods on the ImageNet-C dataset, demonstrating its effectiveness.
- The methodology is clear and concise, making it easy to understand and reproduce.
- The paper provides extensive ablation studies, which help in understanding the impact of different components of the proposed method.
- The proposed entropy-based criteria for sample selection and replay-based knowledge distillation are innovative and potentially beneficial for future research in the field.

**Weaknesses:**
- The novelty of the paper is limited as it primarily combines existing techniques such as entropy minimization and knowledge distillation without significant innovation.
- The experimental setup lacks comprehensive comparisons with other methods that also reduce communication costs, such as Tent, which could potentially upload fewer samples.
- The paper does not adequately address the computational costs of the proposed method, particularly in terms of the computational overhead introduced by the cloud-edge collaboration.
- There is a lack of discussion on the potential negative societal impacts of the proposed method.
- The paper does not provide a detailed comparison of communication costs with other methods, which could undermine the claimed reduction in communication costs.
- The paper lacks a detailed discussion on the limitations of the proposed method, particularly concerning its applicability to different types of models and datasets.
- The paper does not provide a detailed analysis of the computational and communication costs of the proposed method, which is crucial for understanding its practical applicability.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors provide more detailed comparisons with other methods that also reduce communication costs, such as Tent, to better understand the relative advantages of CEMA?
- How does the proposed method handle the computational overhead introduced by the cloud-edge collaboration? Is there a way to minimize this overhead without compromising the effectiveness of the method?
- What are the specific computational and communication costs of the proposed method, and how do these costs compare to other existing methods?
- How does the proposed method handle the issue of out-of-distribution samples in the edge devices, particularly in scenarios where the foundation model may not cover all possible test samples?
- Can the authors provide a detailed discussion on the limitations of the proposed method, including its applicability to different types of models and datasets?
- How does the proposed method handle the issue of data privacy, particularly in scenarios where sensitive data is being transmitted between the cloud and edge devices?
- Can the authors provide more details on the experimental setup, particularly concerning the data transmission costs and the computational overhead introduced by the proposed method?
