 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel method for multi-modal image completion, utilizing a pre-trained stable diffusion model enhanced by additional conditional networks that inject modality-specific information. The approach, named MaGIC, employs a modality-specific conditional U-Net (MCU-Net) and a consistent modality blending (CMB) method to integrate multiple modalities into the diffusion model without requiring retraining. This method allows for the flexible addition or removal of guidance modalities during inference, enabling real-time adaptation to various image completion tasks. The paper presents extensive experiments demonstrating the effectiveness of the proposed method, showcasing its ability to handle multiple modalities and its potential for real-time applications.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel method for multi-modal image completion, utilizing a pre-trained stable diffusion model enhanced by additional conditional networks that inject modality-specific information. This approach is innovative and well-explained, making it easy to follow.
- The method is flexible and can be used with different modalities, including text, canny edge, sketch, segmentation, depth, and pose, which is a significant advantage over previous methods that were limited to single-modality guidance.
- The proposed method achieves state-of-the-art (SOTA) results in image completion tasks, demonstrating its effectiveness and potential for real-time applications.
- The paper is well-written, making it easy to follow, and includes extensive experiments that demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed method.
- The method's flexibility allows for the addition or removal of guidance modalities during inference, enabling real-time adaptation to various image completion tasks.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a clear discussion on the limitations of the proposed method, particularly concerning its ability to generate high-frequency details and its efficiency compared to current single-step completion models.
- The paper could benefit from more detailed discussions on the potential negative societal impacts of the proposed method.
