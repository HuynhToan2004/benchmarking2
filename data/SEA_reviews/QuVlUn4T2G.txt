 **Summary:**
The paper explores the generalization of novel view synthesis from monocular video sequences, specifically focusing on dynamic scenes. It proposes a pseudo-generalized approach that integrates static and dynamic content rendering, leveraging existing techniques such as GNT for static content and depth and temporal priors for dynamic content. The paper also addresses the challenges of scene-specific optimization and demonstrates the feasibility of generalized methods in dynamic scenes, albeit with some limitations. The experimental results, although showing some improvements over existing methods, are not uniformly superior and highlight the need for further refinement and validation.

**Strengths:**
- The paper addresses an important and challenging problem of generalized novel view synthesis from monocular videos, specifically focusing on dynamic scenes which are less explored.
- The proposed pseudo-generalized approach, which integrates static and dynamic content rendering, is novel and offers a fresh perspective on the problem.
- The paper is well-written, making the methodology and experimental results clear and accessible.
- The results show that the proposed method outperforms several scene-specific approaches on LPIPS, indicating a significant improvement in rendering quality.
- The use of a pre-trained GNT for static content rendering and depth and temporal priors for dynamic content rendering is innovative and contributes to the field.
- The paper provides a thorough analysis of the limitations of the proposed method and suggests areas for future research, such as improving the accuracy of depth estimation and motion tracking.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a clear definition and distinction between "scene-specific" and "generalized" methods, which could lead to confusion and misinterpretation of the results.
- The experimental results are not consistently superior to existing methods, and the paper does not convincingly demonstrate a significant advantage of the proposed method over state-of-the-art approaches.
- The paper does not adequately address the challenges posed by dynamic scenes, particularly in terms of motion estimation and depth prediction, which are critical for the proposed method.
- The paper does not sufficiently compare the proposed method with the latest state-of-the-art methods, such as MonoNeRF, which could provide a more robust evaluation of the proposed approach.
- The paper's reliance on pre-trained models and depth estimation from existing methods might limit its applicability in real-world scenarios where these data are not available.
- The paper could benefit from a more detailed discussion on the limitations and potential negative societal impacts of the proposed method.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors clarify the definition and distinction between "scene-specific" and "generalized" methods, and how this impacts the evaluation of the proposed method?
- How does the proposed method handle dynamic scenes where the motion is not linear or predictable, and what are the implications of this on the quality of the rendered images?
- Could the authors provide more detailed comparisons with the latest state-of-the-art methods, such as MonoNeRF, to better understand the relative strengths and weaknesses of the proposed approach?
- How does the paper address the issue of depth prediction accuracy, particularly in dynamic scenes where the depth estimates are critical for the quality of the rendered images?
- Can the authors discuss the potential negative societal impacts of their work and how these might be mitigated or addressed in future research?
