 **Summary:**
The paper investigates online nonconvex optimization with single oracle feedback, focusing on the local regret of the original objective functions. It derives lower bounds on the local regret for both exact and stochastic gradient oracles, showing that classical online gradient descent algorithms are optimal for linear-span algorithms. For the more challenging setting with a single function value oracle feedback, the paper introduces an online algorithm using a one-point running difference gradient estimator, achieving optimal local regret. The paper also discusses the practical implications of these findings and provides a theoretical framework for understanding the performance of online learning algorithms in nonconvex optimization.

**Strengths:**
- The paper provides a lower bound on the local regret for online nonconvex optimization with a single oracle feedback, which is a novel and significant contribution to the field.
- The proofs are detailed and the paper is well-written, making the content accessible and understandable.
- The analysis of online nonconvex optimization with a single oracle feedback is both novel and non-trivial, providing a deeper understanding of the challenges and potential solutions in this area.
