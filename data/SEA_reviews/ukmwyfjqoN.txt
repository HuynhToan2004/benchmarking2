 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel method for real-time video enhancement, focusing on reducing computational complexity and latency while maintaining high-quality results. The proposed method, ReBotNet, utilizes a dual-branch framework with a ConvNext-based encoder and a bottleneck mixer to learn spatio-temporal features. The second branch processes tokens from individual frames, improving temporal consistency. ReBotNet is evaluated on two newly introduced datasets, demonstrating superior performance over existing methods in terms of computational efficiency and real-time capabilities. Despite its strengths, the paper is criticized for its limited novelty and the need for more extensive comparisons with state-of-the-art methods.

**Strengths:**
- The paper addresses a significant and practical problem by focusing on real-time video enhancement, which is crucial for applications like video conferencing and live streaming.
- The methodology is well-explained, with clear descriptions of the proposed framework and the datasets used, enhancing reproducibility and transparency.
- The proposed ReBotNet framework is innovative, combining a ConvNext-based encoder and a bottleneck mixer to learn spatio-temporal features, which is a novel approach.
- The paper introduces two new datasets specifically designed for video enhancement, which can be valuable resources for the community.
- The results show that the proposed method outperforms existing approaches in terms of computational efficiency, latency, and real-time capabilities, making it a significant contribution to the field.
- The paper is well-written, making it accessible and easy to follow, which is essential for disseminating research findings.

**Weaknesses:**
- The novelty of the proposed method is limited as it primarily combines existing techniques without introducing significant new insights or innovations.
- The comparison with state-of-the-art methods is insufficient, which could undermine the perceived effectiveness of the proposed method.
- The paper lacks detailed explanations for certain design choices, such as the use of ConvNext in the first branch and the necessity of the second branch.
- The paper does not discuss the limitations of the proposed method, which could help readers better understand its applicability and potential drawbacks.
- The datasets used in the study are relatively small, which might limit the generalizability of the findings.
- The paper does not include comparisons with recent state-of-the-art methods like VRCT, which could have strengthened the paper's claims.
- The paper does not discuss the potential negative societal impacts of the proposed technology, which is an essential consideration for ethical and responsible AI development.
