 **Summary:**
The paper introduces RLGF, a novel reinforcement learning framework for fine-tuning large language models (LLMs) by leveraging a guide policy. This framework, which includes algorithms such as PPO++ and D2LOLS, is designed to optimize the training of LLMs by using a guide policy to improve the learning process. The paper evaluates RLGF across various tasks, demonstrating its effectiveness in enhancing performance compared to traditional supervised learning and reinforcement learning baselines. The theoretical justification provided supports the framework's potential to outperform existing methods, although the empirical results show mixed outcomes and some concerns about the clarity and depth of the theoretical contributions.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel approach, RLGF, which integrates a guide policy into a policy gradient framework, offering a novel perspective on fine-tuning LLMs.
- Theoretical justification is provided, supporting the framework's potential to outperform existing methods.
- The proposed algorithms are evaluated on multiple tasks, demonstrating their effectiveness in improving performance over supervised learning and reinforcement learning baselines.
- The paper is well-written, making it easy to follow, and provides a comprehensive evaluation of the proposed algorithms.
- The integration of a guide policy into a policy gradient framework is a novel approach that could potentially improve the efficiency and effectiveness of LLM fine-tuning.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a clear explanation of how the guide policy is used to improve the learning process, and the role of the guide policy in the RLGF framework is not adequately described.
- The theoretical contributions, particularly the proofs and derivations, are not well-explained or understood, making it difficult for readers to grasp the full implications of the theoretical claims.
- The empirical results are mixed, with some algorithms showing significant improvements while others do not, and the paper does not provide a detailed analysis of why this might be the case.
- The paper does not sufficiently differentiate its contributions from existing methods like RLHF and does not adequately address the concerns raised in previous literature about the use of LLMs as guides in RL.
- The paper's experimental setup and results are not convincingly superior to existing methods, with some results showing only marginal improvements or no improvement at all.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors clarify how the guide policy is used to improve the learning process and why it is effective?
- What is the role of the guide policy in the RLGF framework, and how does it influence the training process?
- Can the authors provide more detailed explanations or proofs for the theoretical contributions, particularly the proofs and derivations mentioned in the paper?
- How does the RLGF framework compare to existing methods like RLHF, and what are the specific advantages of using RLGF over these methods?
- In the experimental results, why do some algorithms show significant improvements while others do not, and what factors contribute to these differences?
- Can the authors provide more detailed examples or case studies to illustrate the practical applications and benefits of the RLGF framework?
