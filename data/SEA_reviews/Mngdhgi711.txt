 **Summary:**
The paper introduces OKR-Agent, a novel framework designed to enhance the capabilities of Large Language Models (LLMs) in task-solving by utilizing a hierarchical agent system. This system decomposes tasks into Objectives and Key Results, assigns agents to these tasks based on their roles and the workflow's relative positions, and enables a more structured and coherent approach to task execution. The OKR-Agent also includes a multi-level self-evaluation mechanism, allowing each agent to offer evaluations from all related perspectives, refining the accuracy and quality of the outputs. The framework has been tested on tasks such as storyboard generation and trip planning, showing promising results. However, concerns were raised about the lack of comparative analysis with other hierarchical task decomposition methods, the clarity of the methodology, and the limited evaluation scope.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel framework called OKR-Agent, which utilizes a hierarchical agent system to enhance the capabilities of Large Language Models (LLMs) in task-solving.
- The framework includes a hierarchical Objectives and Key Results generation module and a multi-level evaluation module, which refine solutions by leveraging feedback from all associated agents.
- The methodology is well-articulated, and the paper is well-written, making it easy to follow.
- The paper includes a comprehensive literature review, and the experiments are well-designed, demonstrating the effectiveness of the proposed method.
- The proposed method has the potential to improve the performance of LLMs in complex tasks by enabling them to decompose tasks into manageable sub-tasks and assigning agents based on their roles and the workflow's relative positions.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a comparative analysis with other hierarchical task decomposition methods, which limits the ability to evaluate the effectiveness of the proposed method against existing approaches.
- The experiments are limited in scope, focusing on a few specific tasks, which may not fully demonstrate the generalizability of the method.
- The paper could benefit from a more detailed discussion on the limitations and potential negative societal impacts of the proposed method.
- The methodology is not clearly explained, and there is a lack of clarity in the presentation of the algorithm and the generation prompt.
- The evaluation is limited to a few tasks, and there is no comparison with other methods, which makes it difficult to assess the effectiveness of the proposed method.
- The paper could benefit from a more detailed analysis of the computational cost and scalability of the proposed method.

**Questions:**
- How does the OKR-Agent compare to other hierarchical task decomposition methods in terms of performance and efficiency?
- What are the specific advantages of the OKR-Agent over other hierarchical task decomposition methods?
- How does the OKR-Agent handle complex tasks that cannot be easily decomposed into manageable sub-tasks?
- What are the limitations of the OKR-Agent, and how do these limitations impact its performance and applicability?
- How does the OKR-Agent ensure that the solutions generated by the agents are accurate and practical, especially in complex tasks where multiple solutions may be possible?
- Can the authors provide more detailed examples or case studies to illustrate how the OKR-Agent is applied in different scenarios and how it compares to other methods in terms of performance and efficiency?
- How does the OKR-Agent handle the potential for hallucinations or inaccuracies in the solutions generated by the agents?
