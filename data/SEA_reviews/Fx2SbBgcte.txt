 **Summary:**
The paper introduces AnimateDiff, a method for generating personalized animations using a text-to-image diffusion model, without requiring extensive retraining. This is achieved by incorporating a motion module and a domain adapter, which are trained on video datasets to learn motion priors. The methodology involves three stages: training a domain adapter, learning motion priors, and fine-tuning with LoRA for specific motion patterns. The paper provides a detailed analysis of the training and inference stages and compares the results with existing methods, showing that AnimateDiff can generate high-quality animations with minimal additional data and training.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces AnimateDiff, a novel method for generating animations using text-to-image diffusion models, which does not require extensive retraining, thus reducing the need for large datasets and computational resources.
- The methodology is well-explained, with clear descriptions of the training and inference stages, making it accessible and understandable to a broad audience.
- The paper demonstrates the ability to generate high-quality animations with minimal additional data and training, which is a significant advancement in the field.
- The use of LoRA for fine-tuning the motion module is highlighted as a practical and efficient approach, especially when limited reference videos are available.
- The paper is well-written and easy to follow, with clear figures and a well-organized structure that facilitates understanding of the complex concepts presented.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed discussion on the limitations of the proposed method, which could provide valuable insights for future research and improvements.
- The evaluation of the method is somewhat limited, with only a few examples provided in the supplementary material, which could be expanded to provide a more comprehensive assessment.
- The paper does not discuss the potential negative societal impacts of the proposed method, which could be a significant concern for some readers.
- The paper's reliance on existing methods such as DreamBooth and LoRA for personalization and fine-tuning, respectively, might limit its perceived novelty.
- The paper could benefit from a more detailed comparison with existing methods, particularly in terms of performance metrics and visual quality.

**Questions:**
- Can you provide more details on the specific training data used for the domain adapter and the motion module?
- How does the performance of AnimateDiff compare to other methods when using a larger number of reference videos?
- What are the specific training parameters used for the domain adapter and the motion module, and how do these affect the final results?
- How does the method handle scenarios where the reference videos do not match the domain of the personalized T2I model?
- Can you clarify the role of the LoRA layers in the motion module and how they are trained?
- How does the method handle the trade-off between visual quality and motion diversity, particularly in scenarios where the motion module learns texture information from reference videos?
