 **Summary:**
The paper introduces "image hijacking" as a novel adversarial attack on vision-language models (VLMs), where an adversarial image is used to manipulate the model's behavior at inference time. The authors propose a method called "behavior matching" to train such adversarial images, demonstrating its effectiveness across various attack types, including specific string attacks, leak context attacks, and jailbreak attacks. The methodology involves perturbing images to match target behaviors, which can lead to undesirable outputs from the model. The paper also explores the challenges of defending against these attacks and suggests potential countermeasures. Despite its innovative approach, the paper is criticized for its limited evaluation scope and the lack of comprehensive defense strategies.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel adversarial attack on vision-language models (VLMs) using adversarial images, which is a significant advancement in the field.
- The authors demonstrate that their proposed method, "behavior matching," is effective across various attack types, showing a high success rate.
- The paper is well-written, clear, and easy to follow, making it accessible to a broad audience.
- The potential impact of the research is significant, as it highlights the vulnerabilities of VLMs to adversarial images, which could have far-reaching implications for the security and reliability of these models.
- The paper is original in its approach, combining existing techniques in a novel way to create a new method for adversarial attacks on VLMs.
- The experiments are well-designed, showing a clear and convincing demonstration of the effectiveness of the proposed method.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a comprehensive evaluation of the defense against the proposed attacks. It only evaluates the defense under one setting, which may not generalize to other scenarios.
- The methodology is limited to open-source models, which may not reflect the effectiveness on closed-source models.
- The paper does not provide sufficient analysis on the robustness of the proposed method against different types of attacks or the impact of different model configurations.
- The novelty of the paper is questioned as it appears to be a combination of existing techniques without significant new contributions.
- The paper does not adequately address the limitations of its method, such as its effectiveness against different types of adversarial attacks and its generalizability across different VLM architectures.
- The paper could benefit from a more thorough discussion on the implications of the findings and the potential countermeasures against the proposed attacks.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors provide more details on the defense evaluation, including the specifics of the defense mechanism used and the results of the evaluation?
- How does the proposed method perform against different types of adversarial attacks, such as those that use different perturbation methods or different attack models?
- Can the authors provide more examples of the adversarial images used in the experiments, and discuss the impact of different image qualities on the attack success rate?
- How does the proposed method compare to other existing methods for adversarial attacks on VLMs, such as those that use different perturbation strategies or different attack models?
- Can the authors discuss the potential implications of the findings for the security and reliability of VLMs, and suggest potential countermeasures against the proposed attacks?
