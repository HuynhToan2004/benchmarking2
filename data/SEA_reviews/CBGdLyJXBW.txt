 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel neural network architecture, CHNNet, which connects neurons within the same hidden layer, aiming to enhance convergence rates. This model, which is a variant of the feedforward neural network, is designed to allow information to flow between neurons in the same layer, which is not typically possible in traditional feedforward networks. The authors claim that this architecture can lead to faster convergence rates, although the paper lacks comprehensive comparisons with state-of-the-art methods and does not provide sufficient theoretical justification for its claims. The experiments, while showing some improvements, are not convincingly superior to existing methods, and the paper's presentation and writing quality need significant improvement.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, making it easy to follow.
- The idea of connecting neurons within the same hidden layer is innovative and could potentially enhance the learning process.
- The paper provides a detailed explanation of the proposed method, including its mathematical formulation and experimental setup.
- The experiments conducted demonstrate that the proposed method outperforms a simple baseline, showing some improvement in convergence rates.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a comprehensive comparison with state-of-the-art methods, making it difficult to assess the true effectiveness of the proposed method.
- The experimental results are not convincing, with some figures showing marginal improvements and others not showing any significant differences.
- The paper does not provide a thorough theoretical justification for why the proposed method should lead to faster convergence rates.
- The presentation quality of the paper is poor, with numerous typographical errors and unclear figures.
- The paper does not adequately address the computational complexity introduced by the additional connections within the network.
- The paper's claims about the method's ability to learn more robust and meaningful features are not substantiated by the experimental results.
- The proof of rapid convergence lacks rigor and clarity, and the mathematical formulation of the model is not convincingly different from existing models like RNNs.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors provide a more detailed comparison with state-of-the-art methods to better establish the effectiveness of their proposed method?
- How does the proposed method compare in terms of computational complexity and training time to other models like RNNs and skip-connections models?
- Can the authors provide a more rigorous theoretical justification for why the proposed method should lead to faster convergence rates?
- How does the proposed method address the issues of overfitting, especially considering the increased complexity introduced by the additional connections?
- Could the authors clarify the mathematical formulation of the model, particularly the differences between CHNNet and RNNs?
- Why were only simple baseline models used in the experiments, and how do the results compare with more advanced models like ResNet and DenseNet?
