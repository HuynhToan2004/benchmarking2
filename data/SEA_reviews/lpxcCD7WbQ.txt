 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel approach to multi-task learning through stochastic co-modulation of neuronal gains, inspired by biological neural systems. This method, applied to a ResNet-18 architecture, involves a controller network that generates task-specific context weights, which are then used to modulate the gains of neurons in the encoding layer. This approach is tested on the CelebA and CIFAR-100 datasets, demonstrating improvements over deterministic gain modulation and state-of-the-art methods. The method's effectiveness is attributed to its ability to adapt to different tasks and enhance the calibration of model confidence. However, the paper's novelty is questioned due to its similarity to existing methods, and the experimental results are not convincingly superior to existing methods.

**Strengths:**
- The paper presents a novel approach to multi-task learning, leveraging stochastic co-modulation of neuronal gains, which is a unique feature not extensively explored in previous research.
- The method is well-motivated by biological neural systems, providing a strong theoretical foundation for its application.
- The paper is well-written, clearly presenting the methodology and results, and is supported by a variety of experiments that demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed method.
- The approach is computationally inexpensive and does not require additional learnable parameters, making it a practical solution for real-world applications.
- The method shows promise in enhancing the calibration of model confidence, which is a significant issue in machine learning models.
- The paper is original in its application of stochastic gain modulation to multi-task learning, providing a novel approach that could potentially improve learning efficiency and performance.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper's novelty is somewhat limited as it builds upon existing methods such as Haimerl et al. (2022), which raises questions about the distinctiveness of the contributions.
- The experiments conducted are limited to specific datasets, which may not adequately demonstrate the generalizability of the proposed method across different domains.
- The paper lacks a detailed comparison with other methods, such as attention mechanisms and other gain modulation techniques, which could have provided a clearer picture of the method's advantages and disadvantages.
- The method's effectiveness is not convincingly superior to existing methods, as shown in the experimental results, which do not consistently outperform other methods.
- The paper's methodology and some experimental results are not clearly explained or justified, which could lead to confusion and misunderstanding of the results.
- The paper's scope is somewhat narrow, focusing on a specific dataset and not adequately exploring the method's applicability to other types of tasks or datasets.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors clarify the role and effectiveness of the controller network in generating task-specific context weights?
- How does the proposed method compare to other gain modulation techniques, particularly in terms of computational efficiency and effectiveness?
- Can the authors provide a more detailed explanation of the method's limitations and potential improvements?
- How does the method perform when applied to other types of tasks or datasets, and what are the implications of these results for the generalizability of the method?
- Could the authors provide more detailed comparisons with other methods, including a more comprehensive discussion of the results and their implications?
- What are the specific advantages of the stochastic co-modulation approach over other methods, and how does it address the limitations of deterministic gain modulation?
- How does the method perform when the number of tasks or the complexity of the tasks increases, and what are the implications of these results for the scalability of the method?
