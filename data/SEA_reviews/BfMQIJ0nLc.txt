 **Summary:**
The paper introduces MMBench, a novel benchmark designed to evaluate the multimodal capabilities of Vision-Language Models (VLMs). MMBench is composed of 2,948 multiple-choice questions, each covering 20 distinct ability dimensions, and employs a CircularEval strategy to ensure robustness and fairness in evaluations. The benchmark is evaluated using a variety of models, including MiniGPT-4, InstructBLIP, and others, and the results indicate that models like Shikra and Qwen-VL-Chat perform well due to their object localization capabilities. The paper also discusses the limitations of existing benchmarks and proposes a new evaluation strategy, CircularEval, to address these issues. However, concerns are raised about the clarity of the paper's contributions and the potential biases introduced by using ChatGPT for evaluation.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, with clear descriptions of the evaluation strategy and the benchmark's design, making it easy to understand.
- The benchmark, MMBench, is comprehensive, with a large number of questions covering various abilities, which is beneficial for evaluating different types of models.
- The paper presents a variety of models and includes a diverse set of questions, which is valuable for assessing the generalization capabilities of models.
- The introduction of CircularEval is innovative and addresses the limitations of traditional evaluation strategies.
- The use of ChatGPT as an evaluator is a novel approach that could potentially reduce bias in evaluations.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks clarity in explaining the contributions of the work, particularly how the benchmark and evaluation strategy address the limitations of existing benchmarks.
- The use of ChatGPT for evaluation raises concerns about the potential introduction of bias, which could affect the fairness and reliability of the evaluations.
- The paper does not sufficiently address the limitations of the benchmark, such as its applicability to models that do not support multiple-choice answers.
- There is a lack of detail on the data collection process, which could affect the reliability and generalizability of the results.
- The paper does not adequately compare MMBench with existing benchmarks, which could help establish its superiority or uniqueness.
- The paper does not provide sufficient details on the evaluation process, particularly the role of ChatGPT in the evaluation, which could lead to confusion about the benchmark's effectiveness.

**Questions:**
- Could you provide more details on the data collection process, including the number of volunteers involved and the quality control measures taken?
- How do you ensure that the questions in MMBench are diverse and representative of real-world scenarios?
- What steps are taken to prevent bias in the evaluations, especially when using ChatGPT as an evaluator?
- How does MMBench address the limitations of existing benchmarks, such as the need for exact matches between predictions and references?
- Can you clarify the role of ChatGPT in the evaluation process and how it affects the benchmark's reliability and fairness?
- How do you plan to address the potential biases introduced by using ChatGPT for evaluation, and what steps are being taken to ensure the benchmark's robustness and reliability?
