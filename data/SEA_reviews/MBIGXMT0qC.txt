 **Summary:**
The paper introduces ms-ESM, a novel pre-training method for multi-scale molecular modeling that integrates protein and small molecule information. This model, which extends ESM-2, is designed to handle both residue-level and atom-level information, employing a multi-scale position encoding to describe relationships among residues and atoms. The ms-ESM is trained on a combination of protein and molecule data, demonstrating its utility in various tasks such as protein-molecule tasks, protein-only tasks, and molecule-only tasks. Despite its innovative approach, the paper is criticized for its lack of novelty in the pre-training method, the absence of detailed ablation studies, and the unclear presentation of its methodology.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel pre-training method, ms-ESM, which effectively integrates protein and small molecule information, showcasing its utility in various tasks.
- The methodology is well-articulated, with a clear presentation of the proposed model and its components, including the multi-scale position encoding and the unification of residue and atom scales.
- The paper provides a detailed explanation of the training objectives, loss functions, and pre-training datasets, enhancing the reproducibility and understanding of the proposed method.
- The experimental results are robust, demonstrating the effectiveness of ms-ESM across multiple tasks, and the inclusion of ablation studies further supports the method's efficacy.
- The paper is well-written, making it accessible and easy to follow, which is crucial for readers to understand the complex concepts presented.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed comparison with existing methods, particularly in terms of the pre-training methodology and the use of different datasets.
- The novelty of the pre-training method is questioned, as the core components appear to be derived from existing models like ESM-2, which may limit the perceived innovation of the approach.
- The presentation of the paper could be improved, particularly in sections where the methodology and experimental results are detailed. More visual aids and clearer explanations could enhance understanding.
- The paper does not include a discussion on the limitations of the proposed method, which could provide valuable insights into its applicability and potential improvements.
- The paper's focus on protein-molecule tasks may limit its applicability in other areas, such as protein-only or molecule-only tasks, where it does not perform as well.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors clarify the differences between ms-ESM and ESM-2, particularly in terms of the pre-training methodology and the novel contributions of ms-ESM?
- How does the ms-ESM handle the unification of protein and small molecule information, especially in terms of the integration of atom-scale information into residue-scale models?
- Can the authors provide more details on the pre-training datasets used, including the size and diversity of the datasets, and how they were curated?
- How does the ms-ESM perform in other tasks beyond protein-molecule tasks, such as protein structure prediction or molecule property prediction?
- Could the authors discuss the potential limitations of the proposed method and suggest areas for future research or improvements?
