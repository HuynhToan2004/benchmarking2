 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel method, Time-dependent Importance reWeighting (TIW), to mitigate dataset bias in diffusion models. TIW leverages a time-dependent density ratio between perturbed biased and unbiased distributions, which is estimated using a time-dependent discriminator. This method aims to improve the accuracy of density ratio estimation, thereby reducing error propagation in generative learning. Theoretical and experimental evidence supports the efficacy of TIW, showing its superiority over existing methods such as time-independent importance reweighting. The approach is validated through experiments on various datasets, demonstrating its effectiveness in reducing bias in model outputs.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, making it easy to follow and understand the proposed method.
- The proposed method, TIW, is novel and innovative, leveraging a time-dependent density ratio for accurate density ratio estimation, which is crucial for mitigating bias in diffusion models.
- Theoretical analysis is provided to support the proposed method, which enhances the credibility of the approach.
- The paper is well-motivated, addressing the important problem of dataset bias in diffusion models, which is a significant concern in the field.
- Extensive experiments are conducted to demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed method, showing its superiority over existing methods in reducing bias and improving sample quality.
- The paper is clearly presented, with well-designed figures that aid in understanding the proposed method and its experimental results.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper could benefit from a more detailed explanation of the time-dependent discriminator and its training process, especially how it is trained on the perturbed distributions $p_{data}^t(x_t)$ and $p_{bias}^t(x_t)$.
- The experimental results, particularly in Table 1, are not convincing, and the paper lacks a discussion on why the proposed method performs better in certain cases and worse in others.
- The paper could include more comprehensive experiments, such as training on larger datasets like ImageNet and using different types of biases like age and ethnicity.
- The paper does not sufficiently discuss the limitations of the proposed method, such as its applicability to other types of biases or the potential impact of the time-dependent discriminator's training on the model's performance.
- The paper could benefit from a more detailed comparison with other methods, such as those that use different types of biases or different training strategies.
- The paper could include more detailed explanations of the experimental setups and results, particularly in the appendix, to enhance the reproducibility and transparency of the research.

**Questions:**

- How does the proposed method perform when applied to other types of biases, such as age and ethnicity?
