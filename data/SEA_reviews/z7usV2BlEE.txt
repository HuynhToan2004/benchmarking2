 **Summary:**
The paper introduces an alignment fine-tuning (AFT) paradigm to address the assessment misalignment problem in Large Language Models (LLMs) trained on chain-of-thought (COT) reasoning data. AFT employs a constrained alignment loss to ensure that the scores of high-quality COTs surpass those of low-quality ones, preventing model degradation. The approach is validated through experiments on four reasoning benchmarks, showing that AFT outperforms traditional fine-tuning methods. The paper also explores the integration of AFT with other ranking losses and its effectiveness in multi-task and out-of-distribution scenarios.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, easy to follow, and the experiments are well-designed, demonstrating the effectiveness of the proposed method.
- The problem of assessment misalignment is well-defined and the proposed constrained alignment loss is novel and effective.
- The paper is well-organized, and the motivation behind the study is clear, making it easy to understand the authors' intentions.
- The proposed method is simple and effective, and the experiments are comprehensive, including ablation studies on the hyperparameter β and the number of sampling COTs.
- The paper introduces a new constrained alignment loss that is novel and effective, and the experiments are comprehensive, including ablation studies on the hyperparameter β and the number of sampling COTs.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed analysis of the negative scores of low-quality COTs, which could provide valuable insights into the effectiveness of the proposed method.
- The paper does not adequately discuss the limitations of the proposed method, such as its applicability to other types of reasoning tasks or the potential impact of different training datasets.
- The paper does not compare the proposed method with other advanced ranking losses like LAMR and RM-LAMR, which could provide a more robust evaluation of the method's effectiveness.
- The paper does not explore the impact of different training datasets on the performance of the proposed method, which could affect the generalizability of the findings.
- The paper does not discuss the potential impact of the proposed method on the interpretability of LLMs, which is a critical aspect of AI safety and reliability.

**Questions:**
- Could you provide a detailed analysis of the negative scores of low-quality COTs and how they affect the model's performance?
- How does the proposed method perform when applied to other types of reasoning tasks beyond those tested in the experiments?
- Can you discuss the potential impact of different training datasets on the performance of the proposed method and how this might affect the generalizability of the findings?
- How does the proposed method compare with other advanced ranking losses like LAMR and RM-LAMR, and what are the implications of these comparisons for the effectiveness of the proposed method?
- Could you elaborate on the potential impact of the proposed method on the interpretability of LLMs and how this might affect the reliability and safety of the models?
