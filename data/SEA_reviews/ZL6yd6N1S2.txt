 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel approach to enhance the uncertainty estimation capabilities of Graph Neural Networks (GNNs) by integrating the Delta-UQ method with graph-specific anchoring strategies. This approach, termed G-Delta-UQ, aims to improve the calibration of GNNs under distribution shifts, particularly in node and graph classification tasks. The authors propose three anchoring strategies to induce stochasticity in GNNs, which are evaluated against various benchmarks and datasets. The paper highlights the utility of G-Delta-UQ in scenarios where data distribution shifts are prevalent, providing a practical solution for improving the reliability of GNN predictions.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, with clear and concise language that effectively communicates the proposed method and its benefits.
- The authors have conducted extensive experiments to evaluate the performance of the proposed method, providing a comprehensive analysis of the results.
- The integration of graph-specific anchoring strategies with the Delta-UQ method is innovative and offers a practical solution for enhancing the reliability of GNN predictions.
- The paper addresses the critical issue of calibration in GNNs, which is crucial for their practical deployment in real-world scenarios.
- The methodology is adaptable to different GNN architectures, making it a versatile solution for various applications.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a thorough discussion on the limitations of the proposed method, particularly in terms of its applicability to other types of GNNs and its performance on larger, more complex datasets.
- The motivation behind the proposed method is not convincingly articulated, especially in relation to the existing literature on uncertainty estimation in GNNs.
- The paper does not adequately address the computational complexity introduced by the proposed method, which could be a significant concern for large-scale applications.
- The paper does not provide a comprehensive comparison with other uncertainty estimation methods, which could help in understanding the relative advantages and disadvantages of the proposed method.
- The experimental setup and results are not consistently presented across different sections of the paper, which could lead to confusion and misinterpretation of the findings.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors clarify the rationale behind the choice of Delta-UQ as the baseline method for the proposed graph anchoring strategies?
- How does the proposed method perform when applied to other types of GNNs, such as those designed for graph generation or recommendation systems?
- Can the authors provide a more detailed explanation of the graph anchoring strategies and their impact on the performance of the GNNs?
- What are the specific advantages of the proposed method over other uncertainty estimation methods, particularly in terms of computational efficiency and practical applicability?
- How does the proposed method handle the issue of over-smoothing in GNNs, which is a common challenge in graph-level tasks?
- Could the authors discuss the potential limitations of the proposed method and suggest areas for future research to address these limitations?
