 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel graph transformation called CAT, designed to enhance the expressivity of message-passing graph neural networks (MPNNs) on outerplanar graphs. The transformation allows MPNNs to distinguish non-isomorphic outerplanar graphs, leveraging the fact that biconnected outerplanar graphs have a unique Hamiltonian cycle which can be computed in linear time. The CAT transformation is shown to be effective in reducing graph diameter and pairwise resistance, thus improving the predictive performance of MPNNs on molecular benchmark datasets. The paper also discusses the computational efficiency of CAT, which is linear, and provides empirical evidence of its effectiveness in molecular property prediction tasks. However, the paper is criticized for its limited scope to outerplanar graphs, potential over-reliance on the Weisfeiler-Lehman test, and lack of detailed theoretical analysis on the expressivity of the proposed method.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel graph transformation called CAT, which is shown to be effective in improving the expressivity of MPNNs on outerplanar graphs, a significant class of graphs in molecular property prediction.
- The CAT transformation is computationally efficient, with a time complexity of O(n), making it practical for real-world applications.
- The paper provides a clear and well-structured presentation of the proposed method, with detailed empirical results demonstrating the effectiveness of the CAT transformation in molecular property prediction tasks.
- The theoretical analysis of the CAT transformation's impact on graph diameter and pairwise resistance is well-articulated, providing a solid foundation for the claims made.
- The paper includes a detailed discussion on the limitations of the proposed method, acknowledging potential issues such as the inability to distinguish non-outerplanar graphs and the potential for over-reliance on the Weisfeiler-Lehman test.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper's scope is limited to outerplanar graphs, which may not be representative of all types of graphs, especially those that are not outerplanar.
- The theoretical analysis of the CAT transformation's expressivity is not detailed, which could limit the understanding of its limitations and potential applications.
- The paper does not provide a detailed comparison with other higher-order GNNs, which could have provided a more robust evaluation of the proposed method.
- The paper's claims about the expressivity of the proposed method are somewhat limited by the fact that the Weisfeiler-Lehman test is used as the benchmark, which may not be sufficient for distinguishing all types of graphs.
- There is a lack of discussion on the limitations of the proposed method, particularly in terms of its applicability to non-outerplanar graphs and its potential for over-reliance on the Weisfeiler-Lehman test.
- The paper could benefit from a more detailed discussion on the computational complexity and practical implications of the CAT transformation, especially in terms of its scalability and efficiency.

**Questions:**

- Is there a theoretical guarantee that the CAT transformation does not decrease the expressivity of MPNNs?

- Can you provide a more detailed analysis of the computational complexity of the CAT transformation, particularly in terms of its scalability and efficiency?
