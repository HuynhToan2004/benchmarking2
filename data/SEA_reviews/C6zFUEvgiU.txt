 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel framework for generating synthetic data to augment real datasets, focusing on improving the performance of classifiers in imbalanced datasets. The approach leverages feedback from a pre-trained classifier to guide the generation process, ensuring that the synthetic data is both useful and diverse. The framework is validated on datasets such as ImageNet-LT and NICO++, demonstrating significant performance improvements over existing methods. The paper also discusses the importance of generating data that is close to the support of real data and offers a comprehensive set of ablation studies to validate the effectiveness of the proposed method.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, clear, and easy to follow, with a clear motivation and a novel approach to generating synthetic data for imbalanced datasets.
- The proposed method is simple, intuitive, and effective, with a focus on generating useful synthetic data that is close to the support of real data, which is crucial for improving classification performance.
- The paper provides a comprehensive set of experiments and ablation studies, demonstrating the effectiveness of the proposed method across various datasets and scenarios.
- The use of feedback-guided synthesis and the incorporation of real images to guide the generation process are innovative and contribute to the diversity and relevance of the generated data.
- The paper addresses a significant challenge in the field of imbalanced classification, offering a valuable contribution to the community.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed discussion on the limitations of the proposed method, particularly in terms of its applicability to different types of datasets and its scalability.
- The experimental results, while promising, do not convincingly demonstrate the superiority of the proposed method over existing approaches in all scenarios, especially in terms of overall accuracy.
- The paper does not adequately address the computational efficiency of the method, which could be a significant concern for practical applications.
- The use of the term "synthetic data" is misleading, as the data generated is not entirely synthetic but rather conditioned on real images, which could confuse the reader.
- The paper does not sufficiently address the potential negative societal impacts of the proposed method, which could be a concern in certain applications.
