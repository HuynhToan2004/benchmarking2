 **Summary:**
The paper introduces CALICO, a novel pre-training framework designed for multimodal BEV perception, integrating both LiDAR and camera modalities. CALICO employs a two-stage approach: point-region contrast (PRC) and region-aware distillation (RAD), which optimizes the balance between region-level and scene-level representation learning. The framework is evaluated on the nuScenes dataset and demonstrates significant improvements over baseline methods, particularly in 3D object detection and BEV map segmentation tasks. Despite its innovative approach and empirical success, concerns about the novelty and clarity of the methodological contributions persist, as the paper heavily borrows from existing methods like PLRC and BEVDistill.

**Strengths:**
- The paper presents a novel and effective pre-training framework for multimodal BEV perception, integrating both LiDAR and camera modalities.
- The proposed two-stage approach, point-region contrast (PRC) and region-aware distillation (RAD), achieves significant performance improvements over baseline methods.
- The methodology is well-articulated, with clear and detailed descriptions of the proposed methods, including the use of semantic pooling and contrastive distillation.
- The paper is well-written, making it easy to follow, and includes comprehensive experimental results that demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed method.
- The authors have conducted thorough ablation studies to validate the effectiveness of each component of their method.

**Weaknesses:**
- The novelty of the proposed method is limited as it heavily borrows from existing methods like PLRC and BEVDistill, with minor modifications.
- The paper lacks a detailed comparison with other state-of-the-art methods, which could have strengthened the argument for the superiority of the proposed approach.
- There are inconsistencies and unclear explanations in the methodology, particularly regarding the motivation and effectiveness of the semantic pooling method and the contrastive distillation.
- The paper does not sufficiently address the computational and memory consumption introduced by the proposed method, which could be a significant drawback for real-world applications.
- The paper suffers from a lack of clarity in some sections, making it difficult to understand the full methodology and the rationale behind certain design choices.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors clarify the motivation and effectiveness of the semantic pooling method and the contrastive distillation in the context of the proposed method?
- How does the proposed method compare with other state-of-the-art methods in terms of computational efficiency and memory consumption?
- Can the authors provide more details on the design choices, such as the rationale behind the use of semantic-less points and the impact of these choices on the overall performance?
- How does the proposed method perform when applied to other datasets, such as Waymo, and what are the implications of these results for the generalizability of the method?
- Could the authors elaborate on the methodology and the experimental setup, particularly in sections where the clarity is lacking, to enhance the understanding of the proposed method?
