 **Summary:**
The paper introduces ClimODE, a novel physics-informed neural ODE model for climate forecasting, which integrates a neural ODE system with a physical constraint of advection and a Gaussian emission model to handle uncertainty. The model is designed to capture global and regional weather patterns, with a focus on value-preserving dynamics and uncertainty estimation. The authors compare ClimODE with existing models and demonstrate its superiority in global and regional forecasting. Despite its strengths in integrating physical constraints and uncertainty estimation, the paper faces criticism for its limited novelty, lack of detailed model descriptions, and unclear experimental setups.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel approach by integrating physics constraints with deep learning methods, specifically in the context of climate forecasting, which is a significant advancement in the field.
- The model's performance is impressive, particularly in global and regional forecasting, as demonstrated by the experimental results.
- The paper is well-written, clear, and easy to follow, which enhances its readability and accessibility.
- The inclusion of uncertainty estimation in the model is a notable feature that adds value to the forecasting capabilities.
- The model's design is innovative, particularly the use of a neural ODE to model the advection process, which is both novel and effective.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed description of the model architecture and its components, such as the neural ODE system and the attention mechanism, which could hinder understanding and reproducibility.
- There is a notable absence of comparisons with existing models, such as ClimaX and NODE, which limits the evaluation of the model's performance against state-of-the-art methods.
- The paper does not adequately address the computational efficiency of the model, which is crucial for real-time or large-scale applications.
- The model's assumptions, such as the value-preserving dynamics and the initial velocity inference, are not sufficiently justified or explained, which could affect the model's credibility.
- The paper's experimental setup and the choice of baseline models are not clearly justified, which raises questions about the fairness and validity of the comparisons.
- The paper could benefit from a more detailed discussion on the limitations of the model and the potential societal impacts of its use.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors provide a more detailed description of the model architecture, including the neural ODE system and the attention mechanism?
- How does the model handle the computational demands, especially considering the need for real-time or large-scale applications?
- Could the authors clarify the initial velocity inference process and its impact on the model's performance?
- Why was the decision made to exclude certain baseline models from the comparisons, and how does this affect the evaluation of the model's performance?
- How does the model handle the value-preserving dynamics, and what are the implications of this assumption on the model's forecasting capabilities?
- Could the authors discuss the potential societal impacts of using the model for climate forecasting, particularly in terms of policy-making and resource allocation?
