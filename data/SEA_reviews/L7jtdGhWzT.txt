 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel attribution method, FEI, aimed at enhancing the faithfulness of neural network explanations by optimizing attribution maps to align with perturbations in model outputs. This method, which includes gradient clipping and ensemble optimization, is designed to improve the faithfulness of explanations to model behavior. The paper also proposes a new metric for evaluating internal faithfulness and demonstrates the method's effectiveness through experiments on various models and datasets, including VGG16 and ResNet34. However, the paper is criticized for its lack of clarity in presentation and the limited scope of its experimental validation, which primarily focuses on CNNs and does not include a broader range of models or datasets.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel attribution method, FEI, which optimizes the faithfulness of explanations by considering the impact of perturbations on model outputs and internal representations.
- The methodology is well-articulated, with a clear description of the approach and its underlying principles, making it accessible and understandable.
- The paper proposes a new metric for evaluating internal faithfulness, which is a significant contribution to the field of neural network explanation.
- The authors demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed method through experiments on VGG16 and ResNet34, showing that FEI can produce more faithful explanations compared to existing methods.
- The paper addresses the critical issue of faithfulness in neural network explanations, which is a significant challenge in the field of explainable AI.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper suffers from unclear writing and presentation, with several sections and equations that are difficult to understand, particularly in the methods section.
- The experimental validation is limited in scope, focusing primarily on CNNs and not including a broader range of models or datasets, which could limit the generalizability of the findings.
- The paper lacks a detailed discussion on the limitations of the proposed method and does not include a comprehensive comparison with state-of-the-art methods, which could help establish the method's effectiveness more clearly.
- There is a lack of qualitative evaluation and visualization of the explanations produced by the method, which could help in understanding the method's impact on model behavior.
- The paper does not provide a clear explanation of the internal faithfulness metric and how it is evaluated, which could lead to confusion about the method's effectiveness.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors clarify the meaning and usage of the term "ensemble" in the context of the paper?
- How does the proposed method perform on other types of models, such as Transformers, and what are the implications of this for the generalizability of the findings?
- Can the authors provide more details on the hyperparameter tuning process and its impact on the results?
- How does the proposed method compare to other state-of-the-art methods in terms of faithfulness and interpretability?
- Could the authors provide a more detailed explanation of the internal faithfulness metric and how it is evaluated?
- In light of the limited experimental validation, how do the authors justify the effectiveness of the proposed method, especially given the lack of a broader range of models and datasets?
