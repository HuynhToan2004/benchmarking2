 **Summary:**
The paper investigates the problem of recovering a low-rank tensor signal from a random noisy tensor using the spiked tensor model. The authors introduce a novel algorithm called gamma-orthogonalized tensor deflation, which optimizes a parameter gamma to enhance the alignment of recovered signals with the original signal components. The theoretical analysis focuses on the rank-2 order-3 model, with a numerical study that supports the theoretical results. The paper also discusses the practical implications of the proposed method and its potential for real-world applications. However, the paper's scope is limited to specific tensor models and its theoretical assumptions, which may not generalize well to more complex or realistic scenarios.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-organized, clearly written, and provides a comprehensive literature review, making it accessible to readers.
- The proposed method, gamma-orthogonalized tensor deflation, is a novel approach that enhances the alignment of recovered signals with the original signal components, which is a significant contribution to the field.
- Theoretical results are presented in a clear and concise manner, with detailed proofs and explanations of the theoretical framework.
- The paper includes a detailed numerical study that supports the theoretical results, providing empirical evidence for the effectiveness of the proposed method.
- The paper discusses the limitations and potential future directions, which shows a thoughtful approach to the research.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper's scope is limited to the rank-2 order-3 model, which may not generalize well to more complex or realistic scenarios.
- The theoretical assumptions made in the paper are quite strong, and the paper does not discuss how these assumptions affect the results or the practical applicability of the proposed method.
- The paper lacks a broader experimental validation, particularly on real-world data, which could strengthen the claims made about the method's effectiveness.
- There is a lack of discussion on the computational complexity of the proposed method, which is crucial for practical implementation.
- The paper's theoretical results are primarily based on asymptotic analysis, which may not provide a clear picture of the method's performance in practical scenarios.
- The paper could benefit from a more detailed discussion on the limitations and potential applications of the proposed method, as well as a comparison with other existing methods.
