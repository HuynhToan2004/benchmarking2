 **Summary:**
The paper introduces DNABERT-2, a refined genome foundation model that utilizes Byte Pair Encoding (BPE) for tokenization, replacing the traditional k-mer approach. This model aims to address the limitations of k-mer tokenization by providing a more efficient and effective tokenization method. DNABERT-2 also incorporates several advanced techniques such as Attention with Linear Biases (ALBi) and Flash Attention to improve computational efficiency and performance. The paper presents a comprehensive benchmark, Genome Understanding Evaluation (GUE), which aggregates multiple datasets for genome understanding, and demonstrates that DNABERT-2 achieves comparable performance to state-of-the-art models with significantly fewer parameters and computational costs.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel tokenization method using Byte Pair Encoding (BPE), which is shown to be more efficient and effective compared to traditional k-mer tokenization.
- DNABERT-2, the proposed model, demonstrates comparable performance to state-of-the-art models with significantly fewer parameters and computational costs, making it a significant advancement in the field.
- The paper provides a comprehensive benchmark, Genome Understanding Evaluation (GUE), which aggregates multiple datasets for genome understanding, facilitating research in genome foundation models.
- The introduction of DNABERT-2 and the GUE benchmark marks a significant step forward in developing large genome foundational models, addressing the challenges of computation and sample inefficiencies introduced by k-mer tokenization.
- The paper is well-written, making it accessible and easy to follow, and includes a detailed appendix that provides further insights and results.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed comparison with other tokenization methods, particularly in terms of computational efficiency and performance.
- There is a lack of discussion on the limitations of the proposed model, which is crucial for understanding its applicability and potential future improvements.
- The paper does not provide a detailed comparison of the performance of DNABERT-2 with other models under different conditions, such as varying input lengths or different model architectures.
- The paper could benefit from a more comprehensive evaluation across a broader range of tasks and genome sequences to further validate the effectiveness of the proposed methods.
- The discussion on the advantages and limitations of BPE tokenization compared to k-mer tokenization is insufficient, which could help in understanding the choice of tokenization methods.
- The paper does not include a discussion on the impact of different tokenization methods on downstream tasks, which is crucial for understanding the practical implications of the proposed methods.

**Questions:**
- How does the proposed tokenization method compare with other methods in terms of computational efficiency and performance?
- Can the authors provide more details on the choice of the vocabulary size and its impact on the performance of the model?
- How does the proposed model perform under different conditions, such as varying input lengths or different model architectures?
- Could the authors discuss the limitations of the proposed model and potential areas for future research?
- How does the proposed model handle the issue of information leakage in masked language modeling, and what are the implications for downstream tasks?
- Can the authors provide more details on the pre-training data used for DNABERT-2, including its diversity and the impact on the model's performance?
- How does the proposed model perform on extra-long genome sequences, and what are the implications for practical applications in genome analysis?
