 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a new benchmark, MARCEL, designed to evaluate the performance of molecular representation learning models on conformer ensemble learning. The benchmark includes four datasets and covers a range of molecular properties, including molecular descriptors, reaction-based properties, and binding energy. The authors conduct a comprehensive empirical study, comparing various 1D, 2D, and 3D molecular representation learning models and strategies that incorporate conformer ensembles into 3D MRL models. The study reveals that learning from conformer ensembles can enhance the performance of MRL models, particularly in tasks involving molecular properties. However, the paper is criticized for its limited novelty and the lack of significant methodological advancements.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a new benchmark, MARCEL, which is the first to focus on conformer ensemble learning, providing a valuable resource for the community.
- The datasets used are diverse and include both molecule- and reaction-level properties, extending beyond the scope of common GNN benchmarks.
- The paper is well-written, clear, and easy to follow, with a comprehensive empirical study that benchmarks representative 1D, 2D, and 3D molecular representation learning models.
- The inclusion of conformer ensembles in MRL models is a promising direction for future research, and the paper provides insights into the potential of this approach.
- The paper evaluates various strategies for conformer ensemble learning, which is a significant contribution to the field.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks significant novelty as it primarily uses existing models and datasets without proposing new methods or models.
- The paper does not adequately address the computational cost of encoding all conformers in the ensemble, which is a critical issue for large datasets.
- The paper does not provide a detailed analysis of the limitations of the proposed benchmark, which is crucial for understanding its applicability and effectiveness.
- The paper does not include a detailed discussion on the limitations of the proposed method, which is essential for understanding the scope and applicability of the findings.
- The paper does not propose new methods or models, which limits its contribution to the field.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors provide a detailed analysis of the limitations of the proposed benchmark, including its applicability and effectiveness in various scenarios?
- How does the paper address the computational cost of encoding all conformers in the ensemble, especially for large datasets?
- Could the authors discuss the potential of incorporating more sophisticated conformer sampling strategies to improve performance?
- Could the authors provide a more detailed discussion on the limitations of the proposed method and its applicability in different scenarios?
- How does the paper justify the use of the Drugs-75K dataset, which is not an open-source dataset, and what steps were taken to ensure the reproducibility of the results?
