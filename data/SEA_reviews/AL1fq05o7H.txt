 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a new class of selective state space models (SSMs) aimed at addressing the computational inefficiencies of Transformers in long sequence modeling. The proposed model, named Mamba, incorporates a selective mechanism that allows the model to selectively propagate or forget information along the sequence length, enhancing its ability to perform content-based reasoning. Mamba utilizes a hardware-aware parallel algorithm in recurrent mode, which is integrated into a simplified end-to-end neural network architecture without attention or MLP blocks. This model achieves state-of-the-art performance across various modalities such as language, audio, and genomics, with a significant improvement in inference throughput. The model's design is hardware-aware, which allows for faster inference and linear scaling in sequence length, making it a promising alternative to Transformers.
