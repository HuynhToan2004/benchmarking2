 **Summary:**
The paper introduces CNNGen, a novel approach to Neural Architecture Search (NAS) that utilizes a Domain-Specific Language (DSL) to generate diverse CNN architectures without predefined cells or base skeletons. This method aims to address the limitations of cell-based NAS methods by focusing on energy consumption and carbon impact. The paper also proposes two performance predictors, leveraging both image and code representations, which are evaluated against state-of-the-art methods. Despite the innovative approach and the potential for generating diverse architectures, the paper is criticized for its limited evaluation scope, lack of comprehensive experimental validation, and unclear presentation of results.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel approach to NAS using a Domain-Specific Language (DSL), which is a significant departure from traditional methods.
- The proposed methodology is well-articulated, providing a clear and logical framework for understanding the proposed approach.
- The paper includes a comprehensive evaluation of the proposed method, demonstrating its effectiveness through rigorous testing and benchmarking against existing methods.
- The inclusion of a dataset of neural architectures and their detailed descriptions, Python code, PNG images, and critical metrics like accuracy and energy consumption is a valuable resource for the NAS community.
- The paper addresses the important and timely issue of sustainability in NAS, which is often overlooked in current research.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a thorough comparison with other methods, particularly in terms of energy consumption and carbon impact, which are critical aspects of the proposed approach.
- The evaluation is limited in scope, focusing only on CIFAR-10 and not extending to other datasets such as ImageNet or other modalities like NLP or reinforcement learning.
- The paper does not provide sufficient details on the experimental setup, particularly the hardware and software configurations used, which is essential for reproducibility.
- The presentation of results and methodology is confusing and lacks clarity, making it difficult for readers to follow and understand the results.
- The paper does not adequately address the scalability of the proposed method, particularly in terms of the computational resources required for generating and evaluating the large number of architectures.
- There is a noticeable absence of a comprehensive literature review, which could have provided a more robust context for the research.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors provide more details on how the energy consumption and carbon impact are calculated, particularly in comparison to other methods?
- How does the proposed method compare to other state-of-the-art NAS methods in terms of energy efficiency and sustainability?
- Can the authors clarify the presentation of results, particularly in figures and tables, to make the data more accessible and understandable?
- What are the specific hardware and software configurations used in the experiments, and how do they affect the results?
- How does the proposed method handle the scalability issues, particularly in terms of computational resources and time required for generating and evaluating the architectures?
- Could the authors expand on the methodology and include a more comprehensive literature review to better contextualize the research within the existing body of knowledge?
