 **Summary:**
The paper introduces Rayleigh Quotient Graph Neural Networks (RQGNN) for graph-level anomaly detection, focusing on the spectral properties of graphs. The proposed model leverages the Rayleigh Quotient to capture spectral energy, which is crucial for distinguishing anomalous from normal graphs. The methodology involves a Rayleigh Quotient learning component (RQL) and a Chebyshev Wavelet GNN with RQ-pooling (CWGNN-RQ), aiming to enhance the understanding and detection of graph anomalies. The paper presents a comprehensive theoretical analysis and experimental results demonstrating the superiority of RQGNN over existing methods on various datasets. However, concerns about the clarity of presentation and the novelty of the approach have been raised, suggesting a need for further refinement and justification of the theoretical contributions.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, easy to follow, and includes a comprehensive theoretical analysis, making it accessible to readers.
- The proposed method, RQGNN, is novel and demonstrates superior performance over existing methods, showing a significant improvement in graph-level anomaly detection.
- The paper provides a clear explanation of the Rayleigh Quotient and its application in graph-level anomaly detection, which is both intuitive and effective.
- The experimental results are robust and comprehensive, demonstrating the effectiveness of the proposed method across various datasets.
- The use of the Rayleigh Quotient for analyzing the spectral properties of graphs is innovative and provides a new perspective on graph anomaly detection.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed discussion on the computational complexity and efficiency of the proposed method, particularly in comparison to other spectral GNNs.
- There is a lack of clarity in some sections of the paper, particularly in the theoretical analysis and the explanation of certain components such as the class-balanced focal loss.
- The novelty of the approach is questionable as similar methods have been previously explored, such as the use of the Rayleigh Quotient in node anomaly detection.
- The paper could benefit from more detailed comparisons with existing methods, including a more comprehensive literature review and a discussion on the limitations of the proposed method.
- The presentation of the paper could be improved, particularly in terms of readability and the clarity of figures and tables.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors provide a detailed comparison of the computational complexity and efficiency of RQGNN with other spectral GNNs?
- How does the proposed method perform in scenarios where the Rayleigh Quotient is not applicable, such as in graphs with disconnected nodes?
- Can the authors clarify the role and effectiveness of the class-balanced focal loss in the proposed method?
- How does the proposed method compare to other state-of-the-art methods in terms of scalability and performance on larger datasets?
- Could the authors provide more detailed experimental results, including standard deviations and statistical significance tests, to support the claims of superior performance?
- How does the proposed method handle the issue of class imbalance in graph-level anomaly detection, and what are the implications of this on the model's performance?
