 **Summary:**
The paper introduces SEER, a novel preference-based reinforcement learning (PbRL) framework that leverages historical trajectories to construct a non-parametric model, enhancing the learning of a Q function and improving policy learning. SEER's key innovation lies in its ability to bootstrap an empirical Q function from replay memory, which aids in sampling informative trajectories and regularizing the neural Q function. This approach is supported by theoretical analysis and extensive experiments across various tasks, demonstrating SEER's superiority over existing methods. However, the paper's novelty is questioned due to similarities with existing methods, and the clarity of presentation could be improved.

- The paper is well-written, clear, and easy to follow, making it accessible to a broad audience.
- The proposed method, SEER, is novel and innovative, providing a new approach to preference-based reinforcement learning (PbRL) that outperforms existing methods.
- SEER's ability to construct a non-parametric model from historical trajectories and learn a Q function from this model is a significant advancement in the field.
- The theoretical analysis provided supports the effectiveness of SEER, and the experimental results are convincing, demonstrating the superiority of SEER over existing methods.
