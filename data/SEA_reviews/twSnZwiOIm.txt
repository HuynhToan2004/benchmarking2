 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel method for learning invariant representations of dynamical systems using a deep neural network-based approach. It addresses the challenges of learning invariant representations by formalizing the problem as an optimization over neural networks, which is then used to learn representations that are invariant under the action of the transfer operator. The method is applicable to both discrete and continuous dynamical systems and is shown to be effective in learning representations that capture the dynamics of the system. The paper also includes theoretical and experimental validations, demonstrating the method's superiority over existing methods in various applications.

**Strengths:**
- The paper addresses a significant problem in learning invariant representations for dynamical systems, which is crucial for understanding and predicting the behavior of complex systems.
- The proposed method is theoretically sound, with proofs provided to support the claims, and it is well-written, making it accessible to readers.
- The method is novel and innovative, providing a new approach to learning invariant representations using a deep neural network-based method.
- The paper includes a variety of experiments on both discrete and continuous dynamical systems, demonstrating the effectiveness of the proposed method in learning representations that capture the dynamics of the system.
- The theoretical results are supported by experimental results, showing that the proposed method outperforms existing methods in various applications.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed discussion on the limitations of the proposed method, particularly in scenarios where the method might not perform well or where it may not be applicable.
- There is a lack of clarity in the notation and definitions used in the paper, which could confuse readers and hinder understanding.
- The paper could benefit from more detailed explanations and examples of how the method can be applied in practical scenarios, especially in the context of learning dynamical systems.
- The method's performance is dependent on the choice of parameters, such as the number of dimensions of the representation, which is not thoroughly discussed.
- The paper does not include a discussion on the computational complexity of the method, which could be a significant concern for large-scale applications.
- The paper could benefit from a more comprehensive review of related work to position the proposed method within the existing literature.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors clarify the definition and use of the "transfer operator" in the context of the proposed method?
- How does the proposed method compare to other existing methods in terms of computational efficiency and scalability, particularly in large-scale applications?
- Can the authors provide more detailed explanations and examples of how the method can be applied in practical scenarios, especially in the context of learning dynamical systems?
- How does the choice of the number of dimensions of the representation affect the performance of the method, and what are the guidelines for choosing this parameter?
- Could the authors discuss the limitations of the proposed method and provide examples or scenarios where the method might not perform well or not be applicable?
- How does the method handle the stability and convergence issues that are inherent in the optimization of the score functional?
