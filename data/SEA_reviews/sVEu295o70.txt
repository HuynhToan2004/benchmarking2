 **Summary:**
The paper investigates the effects of data augmentation in reinforcement learning (RL) by focusing on three aspects: state-action coverage, reward density, and augmented replay ratio. The authors conduct experiments using a variety of data augmentation functions and RL algorithms to understand how these factors influence the performance of RL agents in sparse-reward tasks. They find that state-action coverage is crucial for data efficiency and that a low augmented replay ratio can significantly improve performance. The paper also discusses the impact of these factors on different RL algorithms and environments, providing insights into the effectiveness of data augmentation strategies in RL.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, making it easy to follow and understand the authors' motivations and the experiments they conduct.
- The experiments are comprehensive and cover a variety of data augmentation functions, RL algorithms, and environments, providing a thorough analysis of the impact of different factors on the performance of RL agents.
- The paper introduces a framework for studying data augmentation in RL, which is novel and contributes to the understanding of when and why data augmentation is beneficial.
- The findings are significant and have implications for future research in the field, particularly in terms of designing effective data augmentation strategies for RL tasks.
- The paper is well-organized and clearly presents the authors' contributions and the experimental setup, making it accessible to a broad audience.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper does not sufficiently differentiate its contributions from existing works, particularly in terms of the novelty of the framework and the insights provided.
- The experiments are limited to sparse-reward tasks, which may not generalize to other types of RL tasks, such as those with dense rewards or high-dimensional visual observations.
- The paper lacks a broader discussion on the implications of the findings and how they could be applied in practical settings.
- There is a lack of theoretical analysis or justification for the observed phenomena, which could strengthen the paper's contribution.
- The paper could benefit from a more detailed discussion on the limitations of the study and potential directions for future research.
