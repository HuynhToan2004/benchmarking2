 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel reward-based framework named SemiReward for semi-supervised learning (SSL) to address the challenge of pseudo-label selection in SSL. The framework utilizes a rewarder network to predict reward scores based on pseudo-labels and corresponding unlabeled data, which can be integrated into existing SSL methods to enhance performance and convergence speed. The methodology involves a two-stage training pipeline and a generator network to train the rewarder online, mitigating confirmation bias. Extensive experiments on 13 datasets across three modalities demonstrate significant performance gains and faster convergence speeds upon various SSL methods. Despite its innovative approach, the paper has been critiqued for its complexity, lack of detailed theoretical justification, and insufficient comparison with existing methods.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel reward score-based pseudo-label selection method, which is a fresh perspective in the field of SSL.
- The proposed method is simple, effective, and can be integrated with existing SSL methods, making it easy to apply to different scenarios.
- The methodology is well-presented, with clear figures and a clear writing style, making it accessible and easy to understand.
- Extensive experiments are conducted on 13 datasets, demonstrating significant performance gains and faster convergence speeds.
- The paper is well-organized, with a clear motivation and a logical structure that makes it easy to follow.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed theoretical analysis, which could strengthen the credibility of the proposed method.
- The complexity of the proposed method is a concern, with the need for additional training and inference costs, which might not be justified by the performance gains.
- The paper does not compare the proposed method with other existing SSL methods that also use reward-based strategies, such as SoftMatch and FreeMatch, which could provide a more comprehensive evaluation.
- The experiments are limited to specific SSL methods, and it would be beneficial to see the method applied to a wider range of SSL methods to validate its effectiveness across different approaches.
- There is a lack of detailed analysis on the impact of different pseudo-label quality metrics, such as confidence-based methods, which could affect the reliability of the results.
- The paper could benefit from a more detailed discussion on the limitations and potential negative societal impacts of the proposed method.

**Questions:**
- Could you clarify the definition of "S" in Definition 3.1 and its relation to the reward score?
- How does the proposed method compare in terms of training and inference costs with other SSL methods, particularly in scenarios with a large number of unlabeled data?
- Why was the cosine similarity chosen as the pseudo-label quality metric, and how does it compare with other metrics like confidence-based methods?
- How does the proposed method perform when applied to other SSL methods, and what are the implications for the convergence speed and performance gains?
- Could you provide more details on the training and inference costs associated with the proposed method, and how these costs compare with other SSL methods?
- How does the proposed method handle the potential issues of confirmation bias, especially in scenarios where the rewarder network might overfit to the pseudo-labels?
