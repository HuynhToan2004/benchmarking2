 **Summary:**
The paper introduces MetaDist, an infrastructure designed to facilitate automatic parallelism in deep learning frameworks such as PyTorch and JAX. MetaDist employs a novel approach by introducing two abstract data structures, MetaOp and MetaIR, which allow for the construction of the MetaSPMD space. This infrastructure eliminates the need for manual annotation of operator SPMD sharding rules, thereby reducing development and maintenance costs. The paper also presents the ShardCombine Algorithm, which obviates the need for manual annotation, and validates the design through experiments that demonstrate state-of-the-art performance. However, the paper faces criticism for its lack of novelty, as the approach resembles existing methods like Alpa and Rhino, and for not providing sufficient evidence of its effectiveness in real-world scenarios.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces MetaDist, an infrastructure for automatic parallelism, which includes two abstract data structures, MetaOp and MetaIR, that allow for the construction of the MetaSPMD space. This approach eliminates the need for manual annotation, reducing development and maintenance costs.
- The paper is well-organized and easy to follow, with clear explanations of the proposed ideas and methods.
- The proposed method is natively compatible with multiple ecologies, including PyTorch and JAX, and the experiments demonstrate that the approach achieves state-of-the-art performance compared with other distributed solutions.
- The paper provides a good overview of current automatic parallelism methods and their limitations, and presents a framework-agnostic approach for automatic parallelism.
- The implementation of the ShardCombine Algorithm is simple and effective, and the paper provides a good overview of current automatic parallelism methods and their limitations.

**Weaknesses:**

- There is a lack of discussion on the limitations of the proposed method, such as its applicability to different types of models and its scalability to larger models.
