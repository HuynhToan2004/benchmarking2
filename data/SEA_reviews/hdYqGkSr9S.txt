 **Summary:**
The paper introduces new benchmarks to evaluate the zero-shot recognition capabilities of Vision-Language Models (VLMs) using adapted ImageNet and MS-COCO datasets. The benchmarks focus on two critical aspects: granularity and specificity. The granularity benchmark evaluates the model's consistency in recognizing concepts across different levels of detail, while the specificity benchmark assesses the model's sensitivity to the specificity of language inputs. The study reveals that current VLMs struggle with granularity and are sensitive to text specificity, which could impact their effectiveness in open-world settings. The paper provides valuable insights into the limitations of VLMs and paves the way for future research to enhance their generalization in zero-shot recognition.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written and easy to follow, providing a clear and detailed description of the evaluation protocols and methodologies.
- The introduction of new benchmarks for evaluating Vision-Language Models (VLMs) in real-world zero-shot recognition tasks is innovative and fills a gap in the current literature.
- The paper conducts extensive evaluations on state-of-the-art VLMs, revealing insights into the limitations and challenges of using VLMs for zero-shot recognition.
- The findings are significant and contribute to the understanding of VLMs' performance in zero-shot recognition tasks, highlighting the limitations and paving the way for enhanced models with better generalization.
- The benchmark will be open-sourced upon acceptance, which is a valuable contribution to the community.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a clear definition and explanation of the term "granularity" in the context used, which could lead to confusion.
- The methodology used in the "granularity" experiments (Section 4) is not well-explained, particularly the rationale behind using a 0.5 threshold for classification and the impact of this choice on the results.
- The paper does not propose new approaches to address the identified issues, such as the inconsistent performance of VLMs across different levels of granularity and sensitivity to text specificity.
- The experiments are limited to specific datasets and models, which may not fully demonstrate the generalizability of the findings.
- The paper lacks a detailed discussion on the limitations of the proposed benchmarks and does not address how the benchmarks could be improved or extended in future work.

**Questions:**
- Could you clarify the definition and usage of the term "granularity" in your study?
- How does the choice of the 0.5 threshold for classification in your "granularity" experiments affect the results, and what would be the impact if a different threshold were used?
- How do the authors plan to address the identified issues in future work, and what new approaches or methodologies might be considered?
- Could you provide more details on the experimental setup and results, particularly in the "specificity" experiments, to better understand the impact of specificity on VLM performance?
- How does the proposed benchmark compare to other existing benchmarks, and what are the specific advantages of your benchmark in evaluating VLMs for zero-shot recognition?
