 **Summary:**
The paper introduces NAS-NGE, a training-free NAS method that utilizes the Neural Tangent Kernel (NTK) to predict generalization error. It employs a novel approach by normalizing the bias-variance decomposition of the generalization error to enhance the stability and accuracy of the search process. The method is evaluated against state-of-the-art training-free NAS methods on NAS-Bench-1Shot and NAS-Bench-201, demonstrating competitive performance with reduced computational costs. However, the paper's experimental results are not consistently superior to existing methods, and the novelty of the method is questioned due to its similarity to existing works.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, with clear definitions and a logical flow that facilitates understanding.
- The proposed method is novel, particularly in its application of the Neural Tangent Kernel (NTK) to predict generalization error, which is a significant advancement in the field of neural architecture search (NAS).
- The method's efficiency is highlighted, as it achieves similar or better performance with fewer search steps, which is crucial for practical applications.
- The paper provides a theoretical analysis of the proposed method, which is a notable strength in the field of NAS.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a comprehensive comparison with other training-free NAS methods, which is essential for establishing the method's competitive advantage.
- The experimental results presented are not consistently superior to existing methods, which undermines the claim of the method's effectiveness.
- The paper does not sufficiently discuss the limitations and potential negative societal impacts of the proposed method, which is a critical aspect for ethical considerations.
- There are issues with the clarity and detail in the experimental setup and results, which could lead to confusion and misunderstanding.
- The paper does not adequately address the scalability of the method, which is a significant concern for practical applications.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors provide a more detailed comparison with other training-free NAS methods, including the specifics of their experimental setups and results?
- How does the proposed method perform when compared to other methods in terms of computational efficiency and accuracy across different datasets and architectures?
- Could the authors clarify the role and impact of the hyperparameters introduced in the method, and discuss their sensitivity to different network architectures and datasets?
- How does the method handle the scalability issues, especially in larger and more complex search spaces?
- Could the authors elaborate on the method's potential negative societal impacts and discuss strategies for mitigating these risks?
