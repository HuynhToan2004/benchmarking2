 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel lossless data compression method using a finite-state autoregressive entropy coding and a Straight-Through Hardmax Quantization (STHQ) scheme. The method aims to enhance compression efficiency by utilizing a lookup table for autoregressive entropy coding and a quantization method that allows for gradient backpropagation. The approach is tested against various baseline models and shows significant improvements in compression ratio while maintaining similar compression and decompression speeds. The paper is well-written, providing clear explanations and comprehensive experimental results, although it lacks a detailed discussion on the computational efficiency and scalability of the proposed methods.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, with clear explanations and comprehensive experimental results.
- The proposed method is novel, combining a finite-state autoregressive entropy coding with a Straight-Through Hardmax Quantization (STHQ) scheme, which is both innovative and effective in enhancing compression efficiency.
- The method achieves significant improvements in compression ratio while maintaining similar compression and decompression speeds, which is a notable advancement in the field of learned lossless data compression.
- The paper provides a clear and comprehensive explanation of the proposed method, which is easy to understand and follow.
- The authors have demonstrated that the proposed method outperforms existing methods in terms of compression ratio, as shown in the experimental results.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed discussion on the computational efficiency and scalability of the proposed methods, particularly in terms of memory and computational requirements.
- There is a lack of comparison with more recent and relevant methods, such as the L3C and PILC, which limits the evaluation of the proposed method's effectiveness.
- The paper does not adequately address the scalability of the proposed methods to larger datasets or more complex models, which is crucial for practical applications.
- The method's performance is not compared across a wide range of datasets, which could help in understanding its generalizability and effectiveness.
- The paper does not provide sufficient details on the training process for the finite-state autoregressive model, which is crucial for reproducibility and understanding the method's implementation.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors provide a more detailed discussion on the computational efficiency and scalability of the proposed methods, particularly in terms of memory and computational requirements?
- How does the proposed method compare with more recent and relevant methods, such as the L3C and PILC, in terms of compression ratio and computational efficiency?
- Can the authors provide a comparison of the proposed method with a wider range of datasets to evaluate its generalizability and effectiveness?
- What are the specific details of the training process for the finite-state autoregressive model, and how does this affect the method's performance and reproducibility?
- Can the authors provide a detailed comparison of the computational complexity of the proposed method with other state-of-the-art methods, particularly in terms of FLOPs and memory usage?
