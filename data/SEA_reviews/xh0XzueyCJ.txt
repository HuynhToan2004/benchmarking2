 **Summary:**
The paper introduces PRODIGY, a novel method for generating graphs with specified constraints using diffusion models. It allows for the projection of graph samples onto a constrained space, which is beneficial for applications like drug discovery and network design. The approach involves integrating a projection operator to ensure that the generated graphs adhere to the constraints. The methodology is evaluated on various datasets, including generic and molecular graphs, and the results show that PRODIGY can effectively handle constraints while maintaining the data distribution. However, the paper's novelty is questioned due to its similarity to existing methods, and the experimental results, while promising, are not sufficiently compelling to outweigh these concerns.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, easy to follow, and provides a clear motivation for the proposed method.
- The proposed method is simple, effective, and easy to implement, with the ability to control graph generation by adding constraints.
- The method is applicable to a wide range of graph generation tasks, including both generic and molecular graphs, and can handle different types of constraints.
- The paper includes comprehensive experimental results and ablation studies, which demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed method.
- The authors have provided code and datasets, enhancing reproducibility and transparency.

**Weaknesses:**
- The novelty of the paper is limited, as the approach is similar to existing methods that use projected gradient descent for constrained optimization.
- The paper does not sufficiently discuss the computational complexity of the proposed method, which is crucial for understanding its practical applicability.
- The evaluation of the method is limited to simple constraints, which may not adequately demonstrate its effectiveness in more complex scenarios.
- The paper does not provide sufficient justification for the choice of constraints, which could affect the reliability of the results.
- The paper lacks a detailed discussion on the limitations of the method, which is essential for understanding its applicability and potential drawbacks.
- The paper does not compare its method with other existing methods that use diffusion models for graph generation, which could provide a more robust evaluation of the proposed method.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors provide more details on the computational complexity of the proposed method, including the time and space requirements?
- How does the proposed method perform when applied to more complex constraints, such as those related to graph properties like clustering coefficient or degree distribution?
- Can the authors provide a more detailed discussion on the limitations of the method, including scenarios where the method may not be effective or where it could lead to suboptimal results?
- How does the proposed method compare to other existing methods for constrained graph generation, such as those based on adversarial training or other optimization techniques?
- Could the authors provide a more detailed comparison with other methods, including the DruM model, to better understand the relative advantages and disadvantages of the proposed method?
- In light of the concerns about the novelty and the limited evaluation, how do the authors respond to the suggestions for a more comprehensive evaluation and a clearer discussion of the method's limitations?
