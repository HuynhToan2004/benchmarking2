 **Summary:**
The paper investigates reinforcement learning (RL) in the presence of perturbed rewards, introducing a novel approach using a distributional reward critic (DRC) to model the distribution of perturbed rewards. This method, which models the reward distribution using a neural network, is designed to recover true rewards under specific conditions. Theoretical analysis is provided under a generalized confusion matrix perturbation model, and empirical evaluations demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed method. The paper also discusses the challenges of handling reward perturbations and proposes a method that can handle a wider range of perturbations compared to existing methods.

**Strengths:**
- The paper addresses a significant problem in reinforcement learning (RL) by focusing on perturbations in the reward signal, which is crucial for real-world applications.
- The proposed method, DRC, is novel and innovative, particularly in its application of a distributional reward critic and a generalized confusion matrix perturbation model.
- The theoretical analysis provided is sound and solid, with rigorous proofs and a well-structured presentation that enhances understanding.
- The empirical evaluations are comprehensive, covering a wide range of reward perturbations and environments, and demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed method.
- The paper is well-written and clear, making it accessible to readers and contributing to its overall quality and impact.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a clear discussion on the limitations of the proposed method, particularly in terms of its assumptions and potential failure cases.
- There is a lack of comparison with other state-of-the-art methods, which could have provided a more robust evaluation of the proposed method.
- The paper's presentation could be improved, particularly in the organization and clarity of figures and the presentation of theoretical results.
- Some technical details and assumptions are not sufficiently explained or justified, which could lead to confusion or misinterpretation.
- The paper does not adequately address the computational complexity or scalability of the proposed method, which could be a concern for large-scale applications.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors clarify the conditions under which the DRC can recover true rewards and provide examples or scenarios where this might not be possible?
- How does the DRC compare to other state-of-the-art methods, particularly in terms of performance and computational efficiency?
- Could the authors provide more details on the computational complexity and scalability of the DRC, especially in large-scale applications?
- How does the DRC handle scenarios where the reward perturbations are not mode-preserving?
- Can the authors provide a clearer explanation of the theoretical results and assumptions, particularly in Section 4.2.1 and the proofs in Appendix B?
- How does the DRC ensure that the reward distribution is not biased towards certain classes, especially in highly skewed reward distributions?
