 **Summary:**
The paper investigates the query-policy misalignment issue in Preference-based Reinforcement Learning (PbRL), where the queries selected to improve the overall quality of the reward model may not align with the RL agent's interests, leading to ineffective policy learning. The authors propose a solution by aligning queries with the current policy's visitation distribution, which they term "policy-aligned query selection." Additionally, they introduce a "hybrid experience replay" mechanism to ensure bidirectional query-policy alignment. The proposed methodology is evaluated on benchmark environments from DeepMind Control Suite and MetaWorld, showing improvements in both human feedback and RL sample efficiency.

**Strengths:**
- The paper addresses a significant issue in Preference-based Reinforcement Learning (PbRL) by focusing on the alignment of queries with the current policy, which is crucial for effective policy learning.
- The proposed method is straightforward, effective, and can be easily integrated into existing PbRL approaches, making it accessible and practical.
- The paper is well-written, clear, and easy to follow, which facilitates understanding and reproducibility of the research.
- The experimental results demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed method, showing improvements in both human feedback and RL sample efficiency.
- The paper is original in its approach to addressing the query-policy misalignment problem, which is a critical issue in PbRL.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a thorough theoretical analysis of the query-policy misalignment issue, which could provide deeper insights into the problem and its solutions.
- The experimental results, while showing improvements, are not consistently superior across all tasks, and the paper does not adequately address the variability in results.
- The paper does not sufficiently compare the proposed method with other existing methods, such as on-policy PbRL methods, which could provide a more comprehensive understanding of the proposed method's advantages and limitations.
- The paper's reliance on a scripted oracle for feedback raises questions about the method's applicability in real-world scenarios where human feedback is less controlled.
- The paper does not discuss the potential societal impacts of the proposed method, which could be a significant concern in real-world applications.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors provide a more detailed theoretical analysis of the query-policy misalignment issue and its implications on policy learning?
- How does the proposed method compare to other existing methods, particularly on-policy PbRL methods, in terms of performance and efficiency?
- In scenarios where human feedback is less controlled, such as in real-world applications, how effective is the proposed method?
- How does the proposed method handle the potential for misaligned feedback from human overseers, and what measures are in place to mitigate this risk?
- Could the authors elaborate on the decision-making process for the size of the policy-aligned buffer and the sample ratio in the hybrid experience replay mechanism?
