 **Summary:**
The paper investigates the impact of adding safety examples during instruction tuning to enhance the safety of large language models (LLMs). It demonstrates that a small number of safety examples can significantly improve model safety without affecting its general performance. However, the paper also highlights the potential for exaggerated safety behaviors when too many safety examples are used, leading to models refusing even safe prompts. The study employs a variety of datasets and evaluation methods to assess the effectiveness of safety tuning, showing that while it can reduce harmful responses, it may also lead to a decrease in model helpfulness. The paper also discusses the challenges of finding the right balance between safety and helpfulness in LLMs.

**Strengths:**
- The paper addresses a significant and timely issue in the field of large language models (LLMs) by focusing on the safety of models, particularly through the use of safety examples during instruction tuning.
- The experiments conducted are well-designed, and the paper provides a comprehensive evaluation of the impact of safety tuning on model performance.
- The results are well-presented, and the paper is generally well-written, making it accessible and understandable.
- The paper introduces a novel approach by creating a dataset of safety examples, which is a significant contribution to the field.
- The findings on the impact of safety examples on model performance are valuable and could inform future research and development in the field.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper's claims regarding the effectiveness of safety tuning are somewhat contradictory, with some results suggesting that safety tuning improves safety but decreases helpfulness, while others suggest that safety tuning does not affect helpfulness.
- The evaluation methods used, such as the reward model and the content moderation API, are questionable and may not accurately reflect the model's behavior or safety.
- The paper does not sufficiently address the potential negative impacts of safety tuning, such as the loss of model helpfulness or the potential for models to become overly cautious.
- The paper's focus on instruction tuning with safety examples may not be generalizable to other types of safety tuning, such as reinforcement learning from human feedback (RLHF).
- The paper's presentation and clarity could be improved, particularly in explaining the experimental setup and results, and in avoiding ambiguous language that could confuse readers.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors clarify the discrepancies in the results regarding the impact of safety tuning on model performance?
- How does the paper address the potential for safety tuning to lead to models refusing even safe prompts?
- Could the authors provide more details on the experimental setup, particularly the number of safety examples used and the training parameters?
- How does the paper ensure the reliability and validity of the evaluation methods used, especially the reward model and the content moderation API?
- Could the authors provide more examples or detailed explanations of the experimental results, particularly those shown in figures like Figure 3 and Figure 7?
- How does the paper address the potential for safety tuning to impact the model's general performance or helpfulness in other ways?
