 **Summary:**
The paper explores the convergence properties of stochastic heavy-ball methods (SHB) in the context of quadratic objectives, focusing on the acceleration of convergence rates under large batch sizes and step decay learning rate schedules. The authors provide a theoretical analysis of the convergence rate, showing that SHB can achieve near-optimal acceleration compared to standard SGD. This is supported by empirical results on datasets such as ridge regression and CIFAR-10, demonstrating the practical implications of the theoretical findings. The paper's contributions include a novel analysis technique for SHB with multistage schedules, which is highlighted as a significant advancement in understanding the theoretical underpinnings of this optimization method.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel analysis technique for SHB with multistage schedules, which is a significant contribution to the field.
- The theoretical results are sound and provide a deeper understanding of the convergence properties of SHB, particularly under large batch sizes and step decay learning rate schedules.
- The empirical results support the theoretical findings, demonstrating the practical applicability of the theoretical results.
- The paper is well-written and accessible, making the complex theoretical concepts understandable to a broader audience.
- The paper fills a gap in the literature by focusing on the convergence properties of SHB in large batch settings, a topic that has been under-explored.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper primarily focuses on quadratic objectives, which may limit its applicability to more complex, non-convex objectives commonly found in machine learning applications.
