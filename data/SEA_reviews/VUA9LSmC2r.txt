 **Summary:**
The paper introduces Octopus, a novel vision-language programming agent designed to operate in the OctoVerse environment, which includes simulators such as OctoGibson and OctoGTA. Octopus leverages a vision-language model trained using a dataset generated by GPT-4, integrating reinforcement learning with environmental feedback to enhance its decision-making capabilities. The model's training involves supervised fine-tuning and reinforcement learning with environmental feedback, which allows it to generate executable code from visual inputs and natural language instructions. The paper presents a comprehensive evaluation of Octopus, comparing its performance against other models like CodeLLaMA and LLaMA, and demonstrates its capabilities in both seen and unseen environments.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel approach to vision-language programming by integrating GPT-4 for data collection, which is a significant advancement in the field.
- The design of the OctoVerse environment, including the OctoGibson and OctoGTA simulators, is well-executed and provides a robust platform for evaluating the proposed methods.
- The paper is well-written, making it easy to follow, and includes a comprehensive evaluation that demonstrates the effectiveness of the proposed methods.
- The use of a reinforcement learning with environmental feedback (RLEF) approach is innovative and enhances the decision-making capabilities of the vision-language programming agent.
- The paper presents a clear and detailed methodology, including the use of a vision-language model for planning and a reinforcement learning algorithm for fine-tuning, which is well-integrated into the OctoVerse environment.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks detailed information on the dataset used for training and testing, which could affect the reproducibility and comparability of the results.
- There is a need for more detailed explanations and justifications for certain design choices, such as the use of GPT-4 for data collection and the architecture of the vision-language model.
- The paper does not sufficiently address the limitations of the proposed methods, particularly in terms of scalability and generalization to more complex environments.
- The paper could benefit from a more thorough discussion on the ethical implications of the work, especially concerning the use of GPT-4 for data collection.
- There are some presentation issues, such as small font sizes in figures and a lack of clarity in some sections of the paper, which could make it difficult for readers to understand the content fully.

**Questions:**
- Could you provide more details on the dataset used for training and testing, including its size, diversity, and the process for creating the dataset?
- How does the proposed method compare to other approaches, such as those using different vision-language models or different reinforcement learning algorithms?
- Can you clarify the role and impact of the reinforcement learning with environmental feedback (RLEF) in the performance of the model?
- How does the model handle errors in the data generated by GPT-4, and what measures are in place to ensure the quality and accuracy of the training data?
- Could you elaborate on the design choices made in the vision-language model architecture, particularly the use of the Perceiver Resampler module and the Cross-Gated Attention modules?
- How does the model perform in more complex or realistic environments, and what steps are being taken to address the scalability and generalization issues?
