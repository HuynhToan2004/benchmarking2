 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel method for training ultra-sparse neural networks, focusing on a 1% connection density. It employs a four-step training methodology, including sparse topological initialization, sparse weight initialization, epitopological prediction, and early stop and weight refinement. The methodology is supported by empirical evidence showing that ultra-sparse networks can outperform fully connected networks in various tasks. The authors propose an epitopological sparse meta-deep learning (ESML) approach, which includes a Cannistraci-Hebb training (CHT) method. The paper also discusses the potential of this approach in reducing model complexity and enhancing training efficiency.

**Strengths:**
- The paper presents a novel method for training ultra-sparse neural networks, which is a significant advancement in the field of neural network research.
- The authors have conducted extensive experiments to validate their method, demonstrating its effectiveness in reducing model complexity and enhancing training efficiency.
- The paper is well-organized and easy to follow, making it accessible to a broad audience.
- The methodology introduces a new approach to training neural networks, which is both innovative and potentially impactful for the field.
- The paper provides a clear explanation of the proposed method and its components, which are supported by empirical evidence.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed discussion on the limitations and challenges of the proposed method, which could provide a more balanced perspective on its applicability and effectiveness.
- There is a need for more comprehensive comparisons with other existing methods, particularly in terms of training speed and model performance.
