 **Summary:**
The paper introduces Adaptive Sharpness-Aware Pruning (AdaSAP), a method aimed at enhancing the robustness and efficiency of deep neural networks. AdaSAP consists of three stages: a warmup stage for adaptive weight perturbations, a pruning stage, and a robustness encouragement stage. The methodology is designed to optimize the loss landscape, making the network robust to unseen inputs. The paper claims that AdaSAP outperforms existing pruning methods in terms of both clean and robust accuracy across various network architectures and pruning ratios. However, concerns are raised about the clarity of the methodology, the novelty of the approach, and the fairness of the comparisons made.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written and easy to follow, with a clear motivation for the research and a comprehensive evaluation that covers various aspects of the method.
- The proposed method is innovative, integrating the optimization of flatness-aware loss landscapes with pruning methods, which is a novel approach.
- The methodology is evaluated on both classification and object detection tasks, demonstrating its effectiveness across different applications.
- The paper provides a detailed analysis of the method's performance, including ablation studies and comparisons with state-of-the-art pruning methods, showing that the proposed method outperforms existing methods in terms of robustness and accuracy.
- The paper is well-organized, with a clear structure that makes it easy to understand the contributions and the experimental setup.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed explanation of the adaptive weight perturbation method, which is a crucial aspect of the proposed method. The absence of a detailed explanation makes it difficult to understand the method's effectiveness and its contribution to the overall performance.
- The methodology section is unclear, particularly the explanation of the perturbation radius and its relationship to the weight importance score. The paper also lacks a clear definition of the flatness of the loss landscape and its impact on the pruning process.
- The paper does not provide sufficient comparisons with other methods, particularly those that aim to improve robustness through pruning. This limits the ability to assess the true effectiveness of the proposed method.
- The paper does not adequately address the computational overhead of the proposed method, which could be a significant limitation in practical applications.
- The paper lacks a detailed discussion on the limitations of the proposed method, which could help in understanding the method's applicability and potential drawbacks.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors clarify the methodology behind the adaptive weight perturbation and its impact on the pruning process?
- How does the paper define the flatness of the loss landscape, and what is the relationship between the flatness and the pruning process?
- Could the authors provide more details on the computational overhead of the proposed method and discuss potential strategies to reduce this overhead?
- How does the proposed method compare with other robust pruning methods, particularly in terms of computational efficiency and effectiveness in improving robustness?
- The paper mentions that the method is applicable to different pruning methods. Can the authors provide more details on how the method can be adapted to different pruning strategies and what are the potential benefits and challenges of such adaptations?
