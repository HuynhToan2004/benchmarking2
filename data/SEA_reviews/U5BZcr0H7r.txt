 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel extension to the classical multi-armed bandit (MAB) problem by incorporating an abstention option, which allows agents to opt out of receiving rewards. This extension is significant as it allows for the exploration of strategic decision-making in scenarios where the agent can choose not to receive a reward, potentially mitigating risks or achieving other strategic objectives. The authors propose two settings: fixed-regret and fixed-reward, and develop algorithms for each that aim to minimize cumulative regret. They provide theoretical guarantees and empirical results to support their approach, comparing it to existing MAB algorithms. Despite the novelty and potential practical applications, the paper faces criticism for its limited scope, lack of real-world examples, and unclear presentation in some sections.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel extension to the classical multi-armed bandit (MAB) problem by incorporating an abstention option, which is both theoretically interesting and practically relevant.
- The authors provide a rigorous mathematical formulation of the multi-armed bandit model with abstention, focusing on cumulative regret minimization across two distinct settings: fixed-regret and fixed-reward.
- The paper is well-written, making it easy to follow, and the theoretical results are supported by empirical results.
- The paper addresses a significant and timely problem, as the multi-armed bandit model is a fundamental benchmark for capturing the delicate interplay between exploration and exploitation.
- The introduction of an additional strategic option (abstention) into the model enriches the strategic landscape, allowing for more nuanced decision-making.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks real-world examples or applications, making it difficult to assess the practical relevance of the proposed model.
- The novelty of the paper is limited as the addition of an abstention option to the MAB problem has been previously explored.
- The paper could benefit from a more detailed discussion on the differences between the proposed model and existing models, particularly in terms of the strategic implications of the abstention option.
- The theoretical results are not sufficiently compared to existing literature, particularly in terms of the novelty and the depth of the theoretical contributions.
- The paper could be clearer in its presentation, especially in sections where the results are not well-explained or the assumptions are not clearly stated.
- There is a lack of discussion on the computational complexity of the proposed algorithms, which is crucial for practical implementation.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors provide real-world examples or applications where the abstention option in the MAB model would be particularly useful?
- How does the abstention option affect the strategic landscape in the MAB model, particularly in terms of the balance between exploration and exploitation?
- Could the authors clarify the differences between the proposed model and existing models, particularly in terms of the strategic implications of the abstention option?
- How does the inclusion of the abstention option affect the computational complexity of the algorithms proposed in the paper?
- Could the authors discuss the limitations of their model and the potential directions for future research?
- Could the authors provide a more detailed comparison of their theoretical results with existing literature, particularly in terms of the novelty and the depth of the contributions?
