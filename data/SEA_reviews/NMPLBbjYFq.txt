 **Summary:**
The paper investigates the strategic reasoning capabilities of large language models (LLMs) by testing them in economic games such as beauty contests and second-price auctions. The authors examine how LLMs perform in different game settings, comparing their strategies against those of rational agents and against each other. The study reveals that while LLMs can adapt to changing game configurations and use historical information to improve their strategies, they often deviate from optimal strategies and fail to achieve the Nash equilibrium. The paper also highlights the need for more robust evaluation methods to assess the strategic reasoning abilities of LLMs in dynamic environments.

**Strengths:**
- The paper presents a novel approach to evaluating the strategic reasoning capabilities of large language models (LLMs) in economic games, which is a significant and timely area of research.
- The paper is well-written and structured, making it easy to follow and understand the methodology and results.
- The experimental results are robust and provide valuable insights into the strategic reasoning abilities of LLMs, particularly in how they adapt to changing game environments and use historical information.
- The paper introduces a new benchmark, EconArena, which could be a valuable resource for the research community to further explore the strategic reasoning abilities of LLMs.
- The authors have conducted a comprehensive evaluation of various LLMs, including GPT4, GPT3.5, Claude2, Claude-I, PaLM2, Llama2, Baichuan2, ChatGLM2, ChatGLM3, and Zeng-LLM, which adds depth to the analysis.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a clear definition and discussion of the strategic reasoning capabilities of LLMs, which is crucial for understanding the implications of the findings.
- The experiments are limited to only two types of games, which may not fully capture the strategic reasoning capabilities of LLMs across different game scenarios.
- The paper does not sufficiently address the impact of different game configurations and historical information on the strategic reasoning abilities of LLMs, which could influence the results.
- The paper does not include a detailed discussion on the limitations of the study, which could help in understanding the scope and applicability of the findings.
- The paper's experimental setup and methodology could benefit from more rigorous testing and validation to ensure the reliability and generalizability of the results.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors clarify the strategic reasoning capabilities of LLMs and how these capabilities are measured in the study?
- How do the authors ensure that the LLMs are not merely memorizing the game rules or using heuristics to win, but are genuinely reasoning strategically?
- What are the implications of the findings for the development and deployment of LLMs in strategic decision-making scenarios?
- How do the authors address the potential biases or limitations in the experimental setup, such as the choice of game configurations and the selection of LLMs?
- Could the authors provide more detailed explanations or examples to support the claims made about the strategic reasoning abilities of LLMs, particularly in the context of the experiments conducted?
