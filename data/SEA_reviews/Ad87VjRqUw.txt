 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel differentiable mesh representation, Ghost-on-the-Shell (G-Shell), which enables the modeling of both watertight and non-watertight meshes. G-Shell leverages a manifold signed distance field (mSDF) on watertight templates to facilitate efficient mesh extraction, differentiable rasterization-based reconstruction from multiview images, and generative modeling of non-watertight meshes. The method's effectiveness is demonstrated through applications such as mesh reconstruction from multiview images and mesh generation using diffusion models. The paper also explores the use of G-Shell in physics-based inverse rendering, showcasing its potential in various 3D modeling tasks.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel differentiable mesh representation, G-Shell, which enables the modeling of both watertight and non-watertight meshes, addressing a significant gap in current 3D modeling methods.
- G-Shell's efficient mesh extraction algorithm reduces the number of mesh faces created and the computational cost, making it suitable for applications like differentiable rasterization-based reconstruction from multiview images and generative modeling of non-watertight meshes.
- The paper demonstrates the versatility of G-Shell through various applications, including differentiable rasterization-based reconstruction from multiview images and generative modeling of non-watertight meshes using diffusion models.
- The methodology is well-explained, with clear figures and detailed explanations that aid in understanding the proposed methodologies and their applications.
- The paper is well-written, making it easy to follow, and includes a comprehensive evaluation of the proposed method against existing methods, demonstrating its effectiveness in different scenarios.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed discussion on the limitations of G-Shell, particularly concerning its ability to model shapes with self-intersections or non-orientable surfaces.
- The paper could benefit from a more detailed discussion on the inverse rendering process, including the handling of direct and indirect illumination, and the optimization process for complex geometries.
- There is a need for a more thorough analysis of the computational efficiency of G-Shell compared to other methods, especially in terms of training and inference times.
- The paper could be improved by including more qualitative results, such as visualizations of the mSDF and generated meshes, to better demonstrate the capabilities of the proposed method.
- The paper could benefit from a more detailed discussion on the training process for G-Shell, including the use of different loss functions and the impact of various hyperparameters on the results.
- The paper could be clearer in explaining the mathematical foundations of G-Shell, particularly the definition and role of the mSDF in the representation.

**Questions:**
- Can you provide more details on the limitations of G-Shell, particularly in terms of the types of shapes it can model and the computational resources required?
- How does the inverse rendering process handle direct and indirect illumination, and what optimization strategies are used to ensure accurate and efficient reconstruction?
- Could you elaborate on the computational efficiency of G-Shell compared to other methods, and provide more detailed analyses of the training and inference times?
- How does the G-Shell representation handle the generation of non-watertight meshes using diffusion models, and what are the specific advantages of using G-Shell over other mesh generation methods?
- Could you provide more visualizations of the mSDF and generated meshes to better demonstrate the capabilities of the proposed method?
- In terms of the training process, could you provide more details on the loss functions used and the impact of different hyperparameters on the results?
