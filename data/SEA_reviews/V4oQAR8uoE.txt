 **Summary:**
The paper introduces Rapid, a novel adversarial defense mechanism designed to repair semantics in adversarial examples by leveraging adversarial attacks to detect and correct malicious perturbations. Rapid utilizes an in-victim-model adversarial detector to pre-detect adversaries and an adversarial repair method called perturbation defocusing to minimize semantic shifts. The approach is evaluated across various datasets, demonstrating its effectiveness in detecting and repairing adversarial examples. Despite its innovative approach, the paper faces criticisms regarding its methodological soundness, particularly in the evaluation of the defense's effectiveness and the clarity of its methodological explanations.

**Strengths:**
- The paper addresses a significant and relevant problem in the field of adversarial attacks on natural language processing (NLP) models, focusing on the detection and repair of adversarial examples.
- The proposed method, Rapid, introduces a novel adversarial defense mechanism that utilizes an adversarial detector and a repair mechanism to address the semantic repair problem in adversarial examples.
- The paper is well-structured, easy to follow, and provides comprehensive experiments to validate the effectiveness of the proposed method.
- The evaluation results show that Rapid can achieve good performance on various datasets, demonstrating its effectiveness in detecting and repairing adversarial examples.
- The methodology is simple, intuitive, and effective, with a focus on repairing semantics in adversarial examples, which is a crucial aspect often overlooked in existing adversarial defense methods.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a clear motivation and detailed explanation of the proposed method, particularly in the sections discussing the adversarial detector and the repair mechanism.
- There is a lack of theoretical analysis and justification for the proposed method, which could enhance the understanding and credibility of the approach.
- The evaluation metrics used are not comprehensive, and the paper does not compare the proposed method with a sufficient number of baselines, limiting the ability to benchmark against existing methods.
- The paper does not adequately address the scalability and computational complexity of the proposed method, which could be a significant concern for practical applications.
- There are inconsistencies and potential errors in the experimental setup and results, such as missing data in tables and unclear explanations of experimental setups.
- The paper does not sufficiently discuss the limitations of the proposed method, which could include potential biases in the adversarial detection and repair mechanisms.

**Questions:**
- Could you clarify the motivation and the specific role of the adversarial detector in the Rapid method?
- How does the proposed method ensure that the repaired examples are not misclassified, especially when the semantics of the adversarial examples are repaired?
- Can you provide more details on the training process of the adversarial detector, particularly how it is trained with adversarial examples and how it generalizes to unknown attackers?
- How does the proposed method handle the problem of adversarial transferability, especially in cases where adversarial examples are transferred to different models?
- Could you discuss the potential biases in the adversarial detection and repair mechanisms, and how these could affect the performance and generalizability of the method?
- How does the proposed method compare to other state-of-the-art methods in terms of computational efficiency, scalability, and practical applicability?
