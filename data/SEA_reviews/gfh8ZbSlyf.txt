 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel deep learning architecture for unsupervised image registration, focusing on properties such as inverse consistency, symmetry, and topology preservation. It utilizes a multi-resolution approach and a deformation inversion layer to enhance registration accuracy and efficiency. The method is evaluated on two datasets, demonstrating state-of-the-art performance. However, the paper's technical novelty is questioned due to similarities with existing methods, and the computational overhead is noted as a limitation.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, clear, and easy to follow, with a comprehensive discussion on the proposed method and its theoretical properties.
- The proposed method is novel, particularly the use of a multi-resolution approach and a deformation inversion layer, which are both innovative contributions to the field.
- The method demonstrates state-of-the-art performance on two datasets, showing significant improvements over existing methods in terms of registration accuracy and deformation regularity.
- The paper is well-structured, with clear definitions of the problem and a detailed literature review, which helps in understanding the context and the contributions of the work.
- The proposed method is a general framework that can be applied to various registration tasks, making it a versatile tool for different applications.

**Weaknesses:**
- The technical novelty of the paper is questioned, as the methodology and theoretical properties closely resemble those of existing methods, such as those by Greer et al. and others.
- The computational cost of the method is higher than some existing methods, which could limit its practical application in real-world scenarios.
- The paper lacks a comprehensive comparison with state-of-the-art methods, particularly in terms of computational efficiency and the use of more challenging datasets like the MIDCAT dataset.
- The paper does not adequately address the issue of folding in the deformation field, which is a critical aspect of registration accuracy.
- The method's dependence on the initial alignment of images and the choice of hyperparameters is not thoroughly discussed, which could affect the reproducibility and generalizability of the results.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors clarify the technical novelty of their method compared to existing works, particularly those by Greer et al. and others?
- How does the proposed method perform on more challenging datasets, such as the MIDCAT dataset, which includes larger anatomical structures?
- Could the authors provide a more detailed comparison with state-of-the-art methods in terms of computational efficiency and memory usage?
- How does the method handle the issue of folding in the deformation field, and what steps are taken to minimize this effect?
- Could the authors discuss the choice of hyperparameters and their impact on the registration accuracy and deformation regularity?
- Is there a possibility to apply the method to other registration tasks beyond those described in the paper?
