 **Summary:**
The paper introduces ASRA, a novel algorithm designed to elicit toxic outputs from pre-trained language models (PLMs) by optimizing prompts. ASRA employs a determinantal point process (DPP) for prompt selection, which balances quality and diversity. The method is evaluated against existing adversarial attack baselines, showing superior performance across six different PLMs. The paper also explores the correlation between the perplexity of target outputs and the success rate of ASRA attacks, indicating a strong association. Despite its effectiveness, the paper has been critiqued for its limited novelty, lack of comprehensive ablation studies, and unclear presentation in some sections.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel approach to eliciting toxic outputs from pre-trained language models (PLMs) using a determinantal point process (DPP) for prompt selection, which balances quality and diversity.
- The proposed ASRA algorithm shows promising results, outperforming other adversarial attack baselines in terms of success rate in eliciting toxic content.
- The paper provides a comprehensive evaluation of the ASRA algorithm across six different PLMs, demonstrating its effectiveness and versatility.
- The correlation between the perplexity of target outputs and the success rate of ASRA attacks is highlighted, providing insights into the effectiveness of the proposed method.
- The paper is well-written, making it accessible and easy to follow, which is crucial for understanding the complexities of the proposed method.

**Weaknesses:**
- The novelty of the proposed method is limited as it builds upon existing adversarial attack methods by introducing the DPP for prompt selection.
- The paper lacks a comprehensive ablation study, particularly on the number of random tokens used in the approximation and the choice of hyperparameters.
- The presentation of the paper is not always clear, with some sections requiring additional clarity and explanation.
- The paper does not discuss the limitations of the proposed method in detail, which could help in understanding its applicability and potential drawbacks.
- The paper does not provide a detailed comparison with existing adversarial attack methods, which could help in understanding the advantages and disadvantages of the proposed method.
- The paper's reliance on the success rate of ASRA attacks as a measure of effectiveness might not be sufficient, as it does not consider other metrics such as the quality or coherence of the generated text.

**Questions:**
- Could you clarify the definition of "randomly initialized prompts" and explain how they are initialized?
- How does the proposed method compare to other adversarial attack methods, especially in terms of computational efficiency and effectiveness?
- What are the specific benefits of using the DPP for prompt selection over other methods?
- Can you provide more details on the choice of hyperparameters and their impact on the performance of the ASRA algorithm?
- How does the proposed method handle the potential issue of generating toxic outputs that are not representative of the model's toxicity?
- Could you provide more examples or case studies to illustrate the effectiveness of the ASRA algorithm in real-world scenarios?
- How does the proposed method address the potential negative societal impacts of generating toxic outputs from PLMs?
