 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel method for fine-tuning Vision Transformers (ViT) for deep metric learning (DML) tasks, specifically focusing on parameter-efficient methods. The approach utilizes visual prompts to enhance the performance of ViTs, which are optimized using a GRU to update semantic proxies. This method aims to improve the performance of ViTs in DML tasks while reducing the number of parameters and computational resources required. The paper also proposes a new parameter-efficient fine-tuning strategy based on the Proxy-Anchor loss, which initializes proxies with semantic information from input images and ViTs. Experiments conducted on several benchmarks demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed method, showing that it outperforms existing methods in terms of accuracy and computational efficiency.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-organized, with clear and easy-to-understand writing, and includes comprehensive experimental results and ablation studies.
- The proposed method is simple, intuitive, and effective, showing significant improvements over previous methods in deep metric learning tasks.
- The paper introduces a novel parameter-efficient fine-tuning strategy that is both simple and effective, enhancing the performance of ViTs in DML tasks.
- The integration of semantic information from input images and ViTs into the proxy-based DML paradigm is a novel approach that has not been explored in previous studies.
- The proposed method is compatible with other parameter-efficient fine-tuning methods, such as BitFit, and shows promising results in reducing the number of parameters and computational resources required.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed discussion on the limitations of the proposed method, particularly concerning the scalability and generalizability of the findings to other datasets or different model architectures.
- The experimental results are not comprehensive enough, with insufficient comparisons to other state-of-the-art methods and a lack of results on larger datasets.
- The paper does not provide sufficient evidence or theoretical analysis to support the claims made about the effectiveness of the proposed method, particularly in terms of its superiority over other parameter-efficient fine-tuning methods.
- The methodology section could be clearer, with more detailed explanations of the GRU mechanism and the semantic proxy initialization process.
- There are concerns about the fairness of the comparison due to the different pre-training datasets used by the authors compared to those in other studies.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors provide more detailed explanations of the GRU mechanism and the semantic proxy initialization process to enhance understanding of the proposed method?
- How does the proposed method perform on larger datasets, such as the iNaturalist dataset, and how does it compare to other state-of-the-art methods in terms of computational efficiency and accuracy?
- Could the authors clarify the differences between the proposed method and other parameter-efficient fine-tuning methods, particularly in terms of the initialization of proxies and the use of GRU for semantic proxy updates?
- How does the proposed method handle the scalability and generalizability issues, and what are the implications for its application to other datasets or model architectures?
- Can the authors provide more detailed comparisons with other state-of-the-art methods, including those that use different pre-training datasets, to ensure a fair comparison?
