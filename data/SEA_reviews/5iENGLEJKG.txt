 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a method for interpreting the latent tokens in transformer-based vision models, specifically focusing on CLIP models. By disabling the self-attention mechanism and utilizing a vocabulary set, the method aims to map latent tokens to text descriptions, providing interpretability and controllability over model predictions. This approach allows for the manipulation of tokens to alter model behavior, potentially improving robustness against biases and spurious correlations. The paper includes experimental results that demonstrate the method's effectiveness in various scenarios, such as fixing typographical attacks and reducing spurious correlations. However, concerns are raised about the novelty of the method, the clarity of the experimental setup, and the depth of the interpretations provided.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces an innovative method for interpreting and controlling the predictions of transformer-based models, focusing on CLIP models.
- The approach is simple, effective, and does not require additional training or data collection, making it accessible and practical.
- The paper is well-written, clearly presenting the methodology and results, with a clear and concise presentation of the main ideas.
- The proposed method is adaptable to open-world datasets, which is a significant advantage for its applicability in diverse scenarios.
- The experimental results demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed method in various scenarios, including fixing typographical attacks and reducing spurious correlations.
- The paper addresses an important problem in the field of AI transparency and interpretability, which is crucial for ensuring the reliability and trustworthiness of AI systems.

**Weaknesses:**
- The methodology section is not clearly explained, particularly the process of mapping latent tokens to text descriptions and the role of the vocabulary set in this process.
- The paper lacks a detailed comparison with existing methods, making it difficult to assess the novelty and effectiveness of the proposed approach.
- The paper does not provide sufficient experimental details, such as the specifics of the datasets used and the methodology for evaluating the interpretability and controllability of the model.
- There is a lack of clarity regarding the scalability of the method to larger models and more complex datasets, and the potential computational overhead of the proposed method.
- The paper does not sufficiently address the limitations of the proposed method, such as its applicability to other types of transformer-based models or the potential for overfitting to specific datasets.
- The paper does not provide a comprehensive evaluation of the method's performance on more complex or varied datasets, which could affect the generalizability of the results.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors provide a more detailed explanation of the methodology, particularly how latent tokens are mapped to text descriptions and the role of the vocabulary set in this process?
- How does the proposed method compare to existing methods in terms of interpretability, controllability, and computational efficiency?
- Can the authors provide more details on the experimental setup, such as the specific datasets used and the methodology for evaluating the interpretability and controllability of the model?
- How does the proposed method handle the potential for overfitting to specific datasets, and what steps have been taken to ensure the method's generalizability across different datasets?
- Can the authors clarify the scalability of the method to larger models and more complex datasets, and discuss any potential computational overheads associated with the proposed method?
- How does the proposed method perform on more complex or varied datasets, and what are the implications of these results for the practical applicability of the method?
