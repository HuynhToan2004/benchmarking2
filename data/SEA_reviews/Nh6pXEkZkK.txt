 **Summary:**
The paper addresses the challenges with AdaGrad, specifically the implicit learning rate scheduler and the ineffectiveness of decoupled weight decay, which hinder its performance in training deep neural networks. The authors propose a Learning Rate Re-scheduling (LRR) method to mitigate these issues, enhancing AdaGrad's performance on tasks such as image classification. This method involves a simple modification to AdaGrad by incorporating an explicit learning rate schedule, which is claimed to improve generalization performance. The paper also introduces a decoupled weight decay regularization to further optimize performance. The proposed method, AdaGradW, is tested on various datasets and architectures, showing promising results. However, the paper lacks a thorough theoretical analysis and a comprehensive comparison with other optimizers.

**Strengths:**
- The paper addresses the challenges with AdaGrad, particularly the implicit learning rate scheduler and the ineffectiveness of decoupled weight decay, which are significant issues in the training of deep neural networks.
- The proposed Learning Rate Re-scheduling (LRR) method is simple, effective, and can be applied to other AdaGrad-type algorithms, potentially improving their performance.
- The paper is well-written, with clear and concise presentation of the proposed method and its implications.
- The method is evaluated on various datasets and architectures, demonstrating its effectiveness in practical scenarios.
- The paper provides a clear explanation of the theoretical underpinnings of AdaGrad and the rationale behind the proposed modifications, making it accessible to a broad audience.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a thorough theoretical analysis, particularly in terms of the impact of the proposed modifications on the theoretical properties of AdaGrad, such as convergence and regret bounds.
- The experiments conducted are limited in scope, with only a few datasets and architectures tested, which may not fully demonstrate the generalizability of the proposed method.
- The paper does not provide a comprehensive comparison with other optimizers, which is crucial for understanding the relative performance of AdaGradW against other optimizers.
- The novelty of the proposed method is questioned, as it seems to be a straightforward combination of existing techniques without significant innovation.
- There are some inconsistencies and potential errors in the mathematical formulations and theoretical claims, which need clarification or correction.
- The paper could benefit from a more detailed discussion on the computational efficiency and memory requirements of the proposed method, especially in large-scale training scenarios.
