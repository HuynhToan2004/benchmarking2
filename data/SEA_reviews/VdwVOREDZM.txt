 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel approach to 3D-aware image synthesis using a latent diffusion model (LDM) that operates in view space, eliminating the need for a shared canonical space. The method leverages monocular depth prediction to ensure reasonable geometry and diversity in generated images. It trains an autoencoder to compress images into a latent space, which is then used by a diffusion model to generate high-quality images. The model is trained on datasets like SDIP and ImageNet, demonstrating superior performance compared to existing GAN-based methods. The paper's key innovation lies in its ability to handle in-the-wild datasets without requiring a shared camera system or explicit 3D geometry supervision.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel approach to 3D-aware image synthesis by modeling instances in view space, which eliminates the need for a shared canonical space and enables the generation of diverse images from unposed images without multiview supervision.
- The method is well-motivated and technically sound, with a clear methodology and thorough experimental validation that demonstrates superior performance over existing GAN-based methods.
- The use of a latent diffusion model (LDM) for 3D-aware image synthesis is innovative and allows for the generation of high-quality images with reasonable geometry and strong distribution coverage.
- The paper is well-written and clearly presents the methodology, making it accessible and understandable to readers.
- The results are impressive, with high-quality images generated and a significant improvement over existing methods in terms of image quality and diversity.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper could benefit from more detailed explanations and examples of the 3D-aware image synthesis process, particularly how it differs from existing methods like POF3D and 3DGP.
- There is a lack of discussion on the limitations and potential failure cases of the proposed method, which is crucial for understanding the practical applicability of the approach.
- The paper does not include a detailed comparison with the latest diffusion-based methods for 3D generation, which could provide a more comprehensive evaluation of the proposed method.
- The method's dependence on the quality of the input monocular depth estimation could affect the performance and diversity of the generated images.
- The paper does not provide a detailed discussion on how the method handles different types of images, such as those with large depth ranges or complex textures, which could affect the quality of the generated images.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors provide more detailed examples and explanations of the 3D-aware image synthesis process, particularly how it differs from existing methods like POF3D and 3DGP?
- How does the proposed method handle different types of images, such as those with large depth ranges or complex textures?
- What are the specific challenges in training a diffusion model in the latent space, and how does the method ensure the quality and diversity of the generated images?
- Could the authors provide more details on the limitations and potential failure cases of the proposed method?
- How does the method perform when trained on different datasets, and what are the implications for the performance and diversity of the generated images?
- Can the authors provide a more detailed discussion on how the method handles the dependence on input monocular depth estimation and how it could be improved to handle this limitation more effectively?
