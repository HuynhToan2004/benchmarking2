 **Summary:**
The paper investigates the phenomenon of "grokking" in neural networks, focusing on the transition from a lazy training regime to a rich feature learning phase. This transition is hypothesized to be influenced by parameters such as the magnitude of network outputs and the alignment between the initial neural tangent kernel and the test labels. The authors propose that these factors determine whether grokking occurs and suggest that the alignment between the initial neural tangent kernel and the test labels is a critical determinant of this phenomenon. The paper provides both theoretical and empirical evidence, including experiments on simple polynomial regression problems and more complex settings like MNIST and one-layer transformers. Despite the theoretical insights, the paper has been critiqued for its limited experimental scope, lack of detailed mathematical proofs, and unclear presentation in some sections.

**Strengths:**
- The paper addresses an important and intriguing phenomenon in neural network training, specifically the "grokking" phenomenon, which is both theoretically and empirically interesting.
- The authors provide a detailed analysis of the grokking phenomenon using a simple model of polynomial regression, which helps in understanding the theoretical underpinnings of the phenomenon.
- The paper is well-written, with clear and concise explanations of the experiments and theoretical results, which are supported by empirical evidence.
- The experiments are well-designed and show that the proposed factors can control the occurrence of grokking, providing a new perspective on the phenomenon.
- The paper is structured around a toy example, which helps in understanding the theoretical results and the experimental setups.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a clear definition and precise mathematical formulation of the grokking phenomenon, which makes it difficult to distinguish from other phenomena such as double descent or sudden improvement.
- The experiments are limited to simple settings, and it is unclear how the proposed factors affect grokking in more complex settings like deep neural networks or larger datasets.
- The theoretical results are not mathematically rigorous, and the proofs and mathematical derivations are not detailed enough, which could affect the reliability of the theoretical claims.
- The paper does not sufficiently discuss the role of initialization in the grokking phenomenon, which is a crucial aspect of neural network training.
- The paper's claims about the role of weight decay in grokking are not convincingly supported, and the experimental evidence provided is not compelling.
- The paper's presentation could be improved, particularly in sections where the text is dense and the figures are not clearly explained.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors clarify the mathematical definition of grokking and how it differs from other phenomena like double descent or sudden improvement?
- How do the proposed factors, such as the alignment between the initial neural tangent kernel and the test labels, affect the grokking phenomenon in more complex settings like deep neural networks or larger datasets?
- Could the authors provide more detailed mathematical proofs or derivations to support their theoretical claims, particularly in sections where the proofs are currently lacking?
- How does the paper address the role of initialization in the grokking phenomenon, and what implications does this have for the practical application of the findings?
- Can the authors provide more empirical evidence to support their claims about the role of weight decay in grokking, especially in settings where weight decay is used?
- How do the authors address the concerns regarding the clarity and presentation of the paper, particularly in sections where the text is dense and the figures are not well-explained?
