 **Summary:**
The paper introduces Musketeer, a sequence-to-sequence vision-language model designed to perform multiple tasks without requiring task-specific adapters or fine-tuning. The model leverages Task Explanation Prompts (TEPs) to manage task interference and ensure the model's focus on shared structures across tasks. The experiments demonstrate that Musketeer achieves competitive performance compared to specialist models trained on individual tasks and other multi-task models without task-specific fine-tuning. The paper also provides a detailed comparison of Musketeer with other multi-task models and discusses the benefits of using TEPs in managing task interference.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written and easy to follow, with clear explanations of the proposed methods and results.
- The introduction of Task Explanation Prompts (TEPs) is a novel approach that effectively manages task interference and ensures the model's focus on shared structures across tasks.
- The paper presents a comprehensive experimental evaluation that demonstrates the effectiveness of Musketeer in handling multiple tasks without task-specific adapters or fine-tuning, achieving competitive performance compared to specialist models.
- The use of TEPs is highlighted as a significant contribution, showing improvements over other prompt-based methods in managing task interference.
- The paper provides a detailed comparison with other multi-task models, showcasing the benefits of using TEPs and the model's ability to handle multiple tasks with a single model.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed discussion on the limitations of TEPs and their impact on performance when dealing with diverse tasks.
- The evaluation could be more comprehensive by including more diverse tasks and comparing the results with other multi-task models that also use TEPs or similar methods.
- The paper does not provide sufficient details on the training and inference processes, such as the training and inference time for each task, which could affect the model's efficiency.
- There is a need for more ablation studies to understand the impact of different components of the model, such as the TEPs and the size of the model, on performance.
- The paper could benefit from a more detailed comparison with other models in terms of model size, training time, and inference time to better understand the efficiency of Musketeer.
- The paper lacks a discussion on the potential negative societal impacts of the proposed model, which is a significant omission in the ethical considerations section.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors provide more details on the training and inference processes, such as the training and inference time for each task, to better understand the efficiency of Musketeer?
- How does the model handle tasks that are not directly related to the tasks it was trained on?
- Is there a possibility to extend the model to handle other tasks beyond the seven tasks evaluated in the paper?
- Could the authors provide more ablation studies to understand the impact of different components of the model on performance, particularly the size of the model and the TEPs?
- How does the model perform when trained on different datasets, and what are the implications for its generalizability across different domains?
- Could the authors discuss the potential negative societal impacts of the proposed model and how these impacts could be mitigated?
