 **Summary:**
The paper explores the relationship between diffusion models and information decomposition, proposing a novel method to decompose mutual information into pointwise mutual information and conditional mutual information. This approach is applied to various tasks such as image-text alignment, object localization, and image editing, showing potential in improving the interpretability of diffusion models. The method involves using pretrained diffusion models to compute mutual information and conditional mutual information between text and images, which are then used to measure the informative relationships between words and pixels. The paper also introduces a new metric for evaluating the compositional understanding of diffusion models, which is tested on the ARO benchmark.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel method for evaluating the compositional understanding of diffusion models, which is a significant advancement in the field.
- The proposed method provides a new perspective on understanding the relationships between text and images using diffusion models, which is a promising approach for improving interpretability in AI models.
- The paper is well-written, making it accessible and easy to understand, which is crucial for a broader audience.
- The experiments conducted demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed method, showing that it can be applied to various tasks such as image-text alignment, object localization, and image editing.
- The paper contributes to the field by highlighting the potential of diffusion models as a tool for evaluating the compositional understanding of text-image relationships, which is an important step towards more interpretable AI models.

**Weaknesses:**
