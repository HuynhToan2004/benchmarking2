 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel metric for measuring the complexity of neural network partitions, focusing on the dynamics of local complexity (LC) during training. This metric is derived from the concentration of linear regions inside neighborhoods around data points and is used to explore the training dynamics of neural networks, particularly the phases of memorization and generalization. The authors present experiments on various datasets such as MNIST and CIFAR10, demonstrating how the LC metric correlates with network performance and generalization. The paper also discusses the relationship between LC and the decision boundary, suggesting that the network's partition regions migrate towards this boundary during training. However, the paper lacks a comprehensive discussion on the theoretical foundations of the LC metric and the implications of its behavior.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel metric for measuring the complexity of neural network partitions, which is both task-agnostic and informative about training dynamics.
- The authors provide a detailed empirical analysis of the metric, including its dynamics during training and its relationship to network performance and generalization.
- The paper is well-written, with clear figures and detailed descriptions of the experiments, making the results easy to follow and understand.
- The proposed metric is fast to compute and can be applied to various deep neural networks, making it a practical tool for analyzing training dynamics.
- The paper offers a fresh perspective on understanding neural network training dynamics, which is of significant interest to the machine learning community.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a theoretical foundation for the LC metric, which could limit the understanding of its behavior and the insights it provides.
- The experiments are limited to small-scale datasets such as MNIST and CIFAR10, which may not adequately represent the behavior of the metric on larger or more complex datasets.
- There is a lack of discussion on the implications of the observed behavior of the LC metric, particularly in terms of its relationship to network performance and generalization.
- The paper does not compare the proposed metric with other existing metrics, which could help in understanding its advantages or disadvantages.
- The paper could benefit from a more thorough discussion of related work, particularly in the area of neural network complexity and partitioning.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors provide a theoretical foundation for the LC metric, explaining its behavior and the insights it provides?
- How does the LC metric behave on larger or more complex datasets, and what implications does this have for its practical application?
- What are the specific advantages or disadvantages of the LC metric compared to other existing metrics for measuring neural network complexity or partitioning?
- Can the authors elaborate on the implications of the observed behavior of the LC metric, particularly in terms of its relationship to network performance and generalization?
- Could the authors include more comprehensive comparisons with other metrics or related work to provide a more robust evaluation of the proposed metric?
