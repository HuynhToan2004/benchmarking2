 **Summary:**
The paper introduces Sign2GPT, a novel framework for gloss-free sign language translation that leverages large-scale pretrained vision and language models. It utilizes lightweight adapters to integrate these models into the sign language translation task, addressing the challenges of limited dataset sizes and computational requirements. The paper also proposes a pretraining strategy that automatically generates pseudo-glosses from spoken language sentences, which are used to pretrain the sign encoder. This approach eliminates the need for manual gloss annotations and enhances the model's ability to learn sign representations. The method is evaluated on two public benchmark sign language translation datasets, showing significant improvements over state-of-the-art methods.

**Strengths:**
- The paper addresses an important problem in the field of sign language translation, which is significant due to the limited availability of large-scale training data.
- The proposed method is innovative and effectively utilizes large-scale pretrained vision and language models, which are crucial for enhancing performance in this domain.
- The paper is well-written, making it easy to follow, and includes comprehensive experimental results that demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed method.
- The use of lightweight adapters to integrate large-scale pretrained models into the sign language translation task is highlighted as a practical solution to address the challenges of limited dataset sizes and computational requirements.
- The introduction of a novel pretraining strategy that automatically generates pseudo-glosses without requiring gloss order information or annotations is a significant contribution to the field.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed comparison of training times and computational costs between different methods, which is crucial for understanding the practical feasibility of the proposed approach.
- The paper does not include a comparison with other state-of-the-art methods that use pretrained models for sign language translation, which could provide a more comprehensive evaluation of the proposed method.
- The paper's reliance on automatically generated pseudo-glosses may limit its applicability to languages with complex grammatical structures or where the spoken language does not directly correspond to the sign language.
- The paper does not discuss the potential negative societal impacts of the work, which could be a significant concern for the application of such technology in real-world scenarios.
- The paper does not provide sufficient details on the training and evaluation of the model, particularly the use of data augmentation techniques and the specifics of the training process.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors provide more details on the training times and computational costs of their method compared to other methods, especially those that do not use pretrained models?
- How does the proposed method compare to other state-of-the-art methods that use pretrained models for sign language translation?
- Can the authors clarify the specifics of the training process, particularly how data augmentation techniques were applied and what impact they had on the model's performance?
- How does the proposed method perform in languages with complex grammatical structures or where the spoken language does not directly correspond to the sign language?
- Could the authors discuss the potential negative societal impacts of their work and how these might be mitigated?
