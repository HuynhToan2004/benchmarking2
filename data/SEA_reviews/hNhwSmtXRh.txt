 **Summary:**
The paper introduces Lemur and Lemur-Chat, two open-source models designed to bridge the gap between natural language processing and code generation. These models are pre-trained on a code-centric corpus and fine-tuned using instruction datasets, achieving state-of-the-art performance across diverse text and coding benchmarks. The models are evaluated on various agent tasks, demonstrating their versatility and effectiveness in handling both natural language and coding challenges. The paper also explores the integration of natural language and coding capabilities, which is crucial for developing advanced open-source agents capable of reasoning, planning, and operating seamlessly across environments.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces Lemur and Lemur-Chat, two open-source models that effectively bridge the gap between natural language and coding capabilities, providing a balanced approach to language agent development.
- The models are pre-trained on a code-centric corpus and fine-tuned using instruction datasets, achieving state-of-the-art performance across diverse text and coding benchmarks.
- Comprehensive experiments demonstrate Lemur's superiority over existing open-source models and its proficiency across various agent tasks involving human communication, tool usage, and interaction under fully- and partially- observable environments.
- The paper provides insights into developing advanced open-source agents adept at reasoning, planning, and operating seamlessly across environments.
- The model and code will be open-sourced, which can benefit the community in developing advanced open-source agents.
