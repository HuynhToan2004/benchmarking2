 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel framework named Diffusion Model-Based Predictor (DMBP) for enhancing the robustness of offline reinforcement learning (RL) algorithms against state observation perturbations. DMBP utilizes conditional diffusion models to predict actual states from noisy observations, mitigating error accumulation issues through a non-Markovian training objective. The framework is evaluated on various benchmarks, showing significant improvements in robustness against different scales of random noises and adversarial attacks. However, the paper has been critiqued for its limited novelty and insufficient empirical validation, particularly concerning the generalization of the proposed methods to diverse noise types and environments.

**Strengths:**
- The paper addresses a significant and relevant problem in the field of offline reinforcement learning (RL), focusing on the robustness of RL algorithms against state observation perturbations.
- The proposed Diffusion Model-Based Predictor (DMBP) is a novel approach that utilizes diffusion models to predict actual states from noisy observations, enhancing the robustness of existing offline RL algorithms.
- The paper is well-structured and easy to follow, providing a clear presentation of the proposed method and its applications.
- The empirical results demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed method, showing significant improvements in robustness against different scales of random noises and adversarial attacks.
- The paper is well-motivated and well-written, making it accessible to readers and providing a good foundation for further research in the area.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a thorough comparison with existing methods, particularly in terms of computational complexity and the variety of noise types that can be handled.
- The generalization of the proposed methods to diverse noise types and environments is not adequately discussed, which is a critical aspect for practical applications.
- The paper does not provide a detailed analysis of the proposed non-Markovian loss function, which is crucial for understanding the effectiveness of the proposed method.
- There are several typographical errors and inconsistencies in the paper, which could affect the credibility and professionalism of the work.
- The paper does not sufficiently discuss the limitations of the proposed methods, which is essential for understanding the scope and applicability of the findings.
