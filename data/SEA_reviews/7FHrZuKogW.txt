 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel Graph Neural Network (GNN) architecture, CSGNN, which addresses adversarial robustness by jointly learning the evolution of both node features and the adjacency matrix. This approach is inspired by contractive dynamical systems, aiming to enhance the robustness of GNNs against adversarial attacks. The theoretical framework supports the robustness of the proposed method, which is empirically validated on various benchmark datasets. The paper also discusses the practical implications of the model's robustness against different types of adversarial attacks, including targeted, non-targeted, random, and adaptive attacks. Despite the theoretical and empirical contributions, the paper faces criticisms regarding the clarity of its contributions, the completeness of the literature review, and the novelty of its approach compared to existing methods.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, clearly organized, and easy to follow, with a comprehensive literature review that effectively positions the proposed method within the existing research.
- The use of contractive dynamical systems to enhance the robustness of GNNs against adversarial attacks is a novel and interesting approach, supported by both theoretical and empirical evidence.
- The empirical results demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed method, showing improvements over existing baselines in various scenarios.
- The paper provides a detailed analysis of the robustness of the proposed method against different types of adversarial attacks, including targeted, non-targeted, random, and adaptive attacks.
- The theoretical framework supports the robustness of the proposed method, providing insights into its characteristics and expected behavior.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a clear explanation of the novel contributions of the proposed method, particularly in how it differs from existing methods like Pro-GNN and how it addresses the limitations of these methods.
- The literature review is incomplete, particularly in terms of not discussing the most recent and relevant works on adversarial robustness in GNNs.
- The paper does not adequately address the computational complexity and training time of the proposed method, which could be significant due to the need to learn multiple dynamical systems.
- The paper's experimental results do not consistently outperform existing baselines, and the performance improvements are not significant in some cases.
- The paper's focus on poisoning attacks limits its applicability to other types of adversarial attacks, which could be more prevalent in real-world scenarios.
- The paper lacks a detailed discussion on the limitations of the proposed method and does not include a comprehensive comparison with the latest state-of-the-art methods.

**Questions:**
- Could you clarify the novel contributions of the proposed method compared to existing methods like Pro-GNN? How does it address the limitations of these methods?
- How does the proposed method perform against other types of adversarial attacks beyond poisoning attacks?
- What are the computational complexities and training times of the proposed method compared to other methods?
- How does the proposed method handle the issue of over-smoothing, which is a known problem in GNNs?
- Can you provide more details on the experimental setup, particularly the hyperparameters used and the training conditions?
- How does the proposed method compare to the latest state-of-the-art methods in terms of performance and computational efficiency?
- Can you discuss the limitations of the proposed method and suggest potential directions for future research?
