 **Summary:**
The paper introduces NeuralFuse, a novel model-agnostic method aimed at enhancing the robustness of neural networks against bit errors induced by low voltage. NeuralFuse employs an input transformation module, trained using a custom loss function and optimizer, to mitigate the impact of bit errors on neural network performance. The approach is evaluated across various datasets and network architectures, demonstrating significant improvements in accuracy and energy savings. The methodology is designed to be model-agnostic, making it applicable to different types of neural networks without requiring model retraining. The paper also explores the transferability of NeuralFuse across different models and its effectiveness in scenarios with limited access to model details.

**Strengths:**
- The paper addresses an important and timely issue of reducing energy consumption in neural networks, which is crucial for practical applications.
- The proposed method, NeuralFuse, is model-agnostic, making it applicable to various neural network architectures without the need for model retraining.
- The methodology is straightforward and the paper is well-written, making it easy to understand and follow.
- Extensive experiments are conducted to evaluate the effectiveness of NeuralFuse across different datasets and network architectures, demonstrating significant improvements in accuracy and energy savings.
- The paper is original in its approach to mitigating bit errors in neural networks, offering a novel solution to a significant problem.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed discussion on the potential limitations of the proposed method, particularly in scenarios where the bit error rate is high or the input data distribution is not well-matched to the training data.
- The methodology section could benefit from more detailed explanations and justifications for certain design choices, such as the training scheme and the use of specific loss functions.
- The paper could benefit from a more comprehensive literature review, especially regarding related works on error-aware training and hardware-based energy saving strategies.
- There is a lack of discussion on the transferability of NeuralFuse across different datasets and models, which is crucial for understanding its applicability in real-world scenarios.
- The paper does not provide a clear comparison with existing methods, making it difficult to assess the relative effectiveness of NeuralFuse.
- The paper lacks a discussion on the potential negative societal impacts of the proposed method, which is a significant oversight.
