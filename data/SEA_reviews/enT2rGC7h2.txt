 **Summary:**
The paper explores the convergence properties of the Generalized Distributed SGD (GD-SGD) algorithm in federated learning (FL) settings, focusing on the impact of different sampling strategies and communication patterns among agents. It provides a comprehensive asymptotic analysis of GD-SGD, demonstrating that under suitable assumptions, all agents will asymptotically reach consensus and find the optimal model parameter. The analysis covers a wide range of communication patterns, including Distributed SGD (D-SGD) and its variants in FL, and examines the influence of agents' sampling strategies on the overall convergence speed. The paper also highlights the role of each agent's sampling strategy, extending previous findings from vanilla SGD to GD-SGD. The results are supported by both theoretical proofs and empirical validations, showing that different sampling strategies can lead to significant improvements in model performance.

**Strengths:**
- The paper provides a comprehensive asymptotic analysis of the Generalized Distributed SGD (GD-SGD) algorithm, covering various communication patterns and sampling strategies, which is a significant contribution to the field.
- The theoretical results are well-supported by empirical evidence, demonstrating the practical applicability of the findings.
- The paper is well-written, clear, and easy to follow, making it accessible to a broad audience.
- The authors have successfully extended previous findings from vanilla SGD to GD-SGD, providing a deeper understanding of the convergence speed of GD-SGD and emphasizing the role of each agent's sampling strategy.
- The paper is original in its approach, offering a novel perspective on the convergence properties of GD-SGD and the impact of sampling strategies on model performance.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed discussion on the practical implications of the theoretical results, particularly in terms of how the findings can be applied in real-world scenarios.
- The assumptions made in the paper, such as the boundedness of the gradient and the strong convexity of the objective function, are not clearly justified or explained in detail.
- The paper could benefit from a more thorough comparison with existing literature, particularly in terms of the novelty and depth of the theoretical contributions.
- The paper does not provide a detailed discussion on the limitations of the proposed methods, which could be crucial for understanding the practical applicability and scope of the findings.
- The experimental results are limited in scope, focusing only on logistic regression, and do not include a broader range of models or datasets to validate the claims.
- The paper does not adequately discuss the impact of non-i.i.d. data across agents and how this affects the convergence properties of GD-SGD.

- How do the findings of this paper compare with existing literature, particularly in terms of novelty and depth of the theoretical contributions?
- Could you clarify the assumptions made in the paper, particularly the boundedness of the gradient and the strong convexity of the objective function?

- In the context of non-i.i.d. data across agents, how does the GD-SGD algorithm perform, and what are the implications for the convergence properties?
