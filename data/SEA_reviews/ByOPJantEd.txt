 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel method for constructing body-ordered equivariant kernels using Wigner iterations, which represent an infinite-basis or infinite-width limit of many equivariant linear models and neural networks. These kernels are shown to exhibit excellent performance in various chemical applications, including the prediction of scalar and tensorial properties of molecules and clusters. The methodology involves iteratively computing kernels from lower-order kernels, which avoids the need for a basis expansion and is shown to be scalable and efficient. The paper provides a theoretical analysis of the scaling properties and practical applications of these kernels, comparing them against other state-of-the-art models.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel method for constructing body-ordered equivariant kernels using Wigner iterations, which are shown to be scalable and efficient in practical applications.
- The methodology is supported by a thorough theoretical analysis of the scaling properties and practical applications, demonstrating its effectiveness in various chemical applications.
- The paper is well-written, clearly presenting the methodology and results, and provides a comprehensive literature review, making it accessible and informative for readers.
- The proposed method achieves state-of-the-art performance on the QM9 benchmark dataset and is shown to be scalable, which is crucial for large-scale applications.
- The paper is significant as it presents a new approach to constructing body-ordered kernels, which is a novel contribution to the field of machine learning on point clouds.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper could benefit from a more detailed discussion on the computational complexity and the practical implementation of the Wigner kernels, particularly how they are computed in practice.
- The method's dependence on the choice of hyperparameters and the sensitivity to these choices is not adequately addressed, which could limit the generalizability of the results.
- The paper does not provide a clear comparison with other state-of-the-art models in terms of computational efficiency or the impact of different hyperparameter choices on performance.
- The method's reliance on the choice of hyperparameters and the lack of a detailed discussion on the computational complexity could limit the practical applicability of the proposed method.
- The paper does not adequately address the limitations of the method, such as its applicability to different types of point clouds or the potential impact of different hyperparameter choices on performance.
