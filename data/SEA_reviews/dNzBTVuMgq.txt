 **Summary:**
The paper introduces HiCS-FL, a client selection method for federated learning that utilizes hierarchical clustered sampling to enhance convergence and reduce variance in non-IID data scenarios. The method estimates the heterogeneity of client data using the client's update of the network's output layer, which is then used to cluster and sample clients. The paper provides theoretical analysis and extensive experimental results demonstrating the effectiveness of HiCS-FL in reducing communication costs and improving convergence speed and test accuracy. However, concerns were raised about the practicality of the method due to its reliance on the output layer of the model and the potential for overfitting.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, making it easy to follow and understand.
- The proposed method is well-motivated, theoretically sound, and includes a convergence analysis.
- Extensive experiments are conducted to demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed method.
- The paper is the first to use the output layer of the model to estimate data heterogeneity, which is a novel approach.
- The methodology is straightforward and easy to implement, and the paper provides a clear explanation of the method.
- The paper addresses the significant problem of non-IID data in federated learning, which is crucial for practical applications.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed discussion on the limitations of the proposed method, particularly concerning the potential for overfitting and the practicality of the method in real-world scenarios.
- The experimental setup could be improved to include more diverse datasets and a broader range of model architectures to validate the effectiveness of the proposed method.
- The paper does not discuss the impact of different model architectures on the method's effectiveness, which could limit its applicability.
- The paper could benefit from a more detailed discussion on the computational complexity of the proposed method and how it scales with the size of the neural network model.
- The paper lacks a discussion on the convergence analysis of the proposed method and how it relates to the practical implementation of the method.
- The method's reliance on the output layer of the model might limit its effectiveness in scenarios where the output layer is not informative enough to estimate data heterogeneity.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors provide a detailed discussion on the limitations of the proposed method, particularly concerning the potential for overfitting and the practicality of the method in real-world scenarios?
- How does the proposed method perform on more complex datasets such as ImageNet, and what are the implications for computational complexity and convergence speed?
- Could the authors provide a more detailed discussion on the convergence analysis of the proposed method and how it relates to the practical implementation of the method?
- How does the proposed method perform when the output layer of the model is not informative enough to estimate data heterogeneity?
- Could the authors provide more details on the experimental setup, particularly concerning the selection of datasets and model architectures?
- How does the proposed method handle scenarios where the data heterogeneity is not accurately estimated, and what are the implications for the convergence and effectiveness of the method?
