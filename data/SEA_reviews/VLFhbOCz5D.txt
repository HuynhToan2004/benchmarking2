 **Summary:**
The paper introduces Tangent Attention Fine-Tuning (TAFT), a method that linearizes pre-trained Transformer models by computing the Jacobian-Vector Product in a single forward pass. This technique allows for efficient parallel training and composition of models, which is beneficial for applications such as machine unlearning and differential privacy. The methodology involves linearizing the model around a pre-trained initialization and then fine-tuning the model using a rescaled square loss. The paper demonstrates that TAFT can achieve comparable performance to non-linear fine-tuning on various downstream tasks while reducing training and inference costs. The authors also discuss the challenges and limitations of the method, including its performance on datasets far from the pre-training objective and the potential for increased inference costs.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces TAFT, a method for fine-tuning linearized transformers, which is computationally efficient and can be used to train models in parallel, reducing training costs significantly.
- TAFT is applicable to various downstream tasks and shows comparable performance to non-linear fine-tuning, which is a significant advancement in the field of transformer models.
- The methodology is well-explained, making it easy to understand and replicate, and the paper provides extensive empirical results that demonstrate the effectiveness of TAFT.
