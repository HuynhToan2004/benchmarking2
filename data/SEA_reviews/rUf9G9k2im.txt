 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel method for large-hole image inpainting using a pixel spread model (PSM) that combines adversarial training with Gaussian regularization. The model iteratively predicts the mean and variance of pixel values, with the variance serving as a measure of uncertainty. The PSM is designed to optimize both the mean and variance terms, with the variance term serving as a regularizer. This approach allows for the model to progressively fill in larger holes in images, with the methodology being tested on datasets such as Places2 and CelebA-HQ. The paper claims that the PSM outperforms existing methods in terms of inference speed and FID score, although it has been noted that the method's effectiveness in handling small holes and its computational efficiency are areas of concern.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, with clear descriptions of the proposed method and its implementation, making it easy to follow and understand.
- The proposed pixel spread model (PSM) is novel, effectively combining adversarial training with Gaussian regularization to achieve both high-quality completion and efficient optimization.
- The method demonstrates good performance in handling large-hole image inpainting, with impressive results in terms of inference speed and FID score.
- The paper includes a comprehensive ablation study, which helps in understanding the impact of different components of the model.
- The use of Gaussian regularization to model uncertainty in image inpainting is a novel approach that has not been explored extensively in previous works.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks detailed comparisons with existing methods, particularly in terms of the computational efficiency and inference time of the proposed method.
- The effectiveness of the proposed method in handling small holes is not convincingly demonstrated, with some results showing artifacts and blurriness around the hole edges.
- There is a lack of discussion on the limitations of the proposed method, particularly in scenarios where the method may not perform optimally or may introduce artifacts.
- The paper could benefit from more detailed visual results, especially in the form of video demonstrations, to better showcase the method's capabilities.
- The paper does not adequately address the impact of hyperparameters on the performance of the model, which could be crucial for reproducibility and optimization.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors provide more detailed comparisons with existing methods in terms of computational efficiency and inference time?
- How does the proposed method perform in scenarios where the hole size is significantly smaller than what is typically tested?
- Can the authors provide more detailed visual results, especially in the form of video demonstrations, to better showcase the method's capabilities?
- How do the authors address the potential impact of hyperparameters on the performance of the model, and what strategies are used to optimize these parameters?
- Could the authors clarify the role and impact of the Gaussian regularization in the model, particularly in scenarios where the model may not perform optimally?
