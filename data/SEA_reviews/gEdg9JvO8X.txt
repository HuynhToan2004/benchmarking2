 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel offline reinforcement learning algorithm named Behavior Diffusion Q-Learning (BDQL), which utilizes a diffusion model to recover the behavior policy from offline datasets. This method updates the diffusion-based behavior policy using a behavior Q-function learned by SARSA, employing a two-stage training pattern. The first stage of training is characterized by the effective guidance of the behavior Q-function over the behavior policy, while the second stage exhibits training instability due to out-of-distribution (OOD) issues. The paper also introduces stochastic weight averaging (SWA) to mitigate training fluctuations. Theoretical analysis and empirical results on D4RL benchmarks are presented, showcasing the algorithm's performance and its comparison to other methods.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, clear, and easy to follow, with a clear structure and logical flow.
- The introduction of a diffusion-based behavior policy is novel and innovative, providing a new approach to offline reinforcement learning (RL).
- The paper provides a theoretical analysis of the algorithm, which is supported by empirical results on D4RL benchmarks, demonstrating the effectiveness of the proposed method.
- The use of stochastic weight averaging (SWA) to mitigate training instability is a useful technique, enhancing the robustness of the algorithm.
- The paper addresses the issue of out-of-distribution (OOD) actions, which is a significant challenge in offline RL, by proposing a method that does not require policy constraints.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper could benefit from a more comprehensive comparison with other offline RL algorithms, including those that use different training methodologies or different types of behavior policies.
- The theoretical analysis, while interesting, does not fully explain the empirical results, particularly the observed performance drops in certain environments.
- The paper's claims about the necessity of addressing the OOD issue during training are not convincingly supported by the empirical evidence, especially in light of the observed performance drops.
- There are inconsistencies and potential errors in the mathematical formulations and proofs, which could affect the credibility of the theoretical claims.
- The paper could benefit from a more detailed discussion on the limitations of the proposed method and potential avenues for future research.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors clarify the inconsistencies in the mathematical formulations and proofs, particularly regarding the use of Qb in the optimization process?
- How does the proposed method compare to other offline RL algorithms, especially in terms of performance and computational efficiency?
- Could the authors provide more detailed insights into the training instability observed in the second stage of training and how SWA mitigates this issue?
- How does the diffusion-based behavior policy compare to other types of behavior policies in terms of effectiveness and computational requirements?
- What are the specific advantages of the BDQL algorithm over other existing offline RL algorithms, and how does it address the challenges of out-of-distribution actions more effectively?
