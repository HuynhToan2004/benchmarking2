 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a new benchmark for open-ended visual question answering (VQA) by leveraging existing visual classification datasets. This approach allows for a granular evaluation of text-generative vision-language models, including those that do not generate text, by using a follow-up question mechanism based on a semantic hierarchy of labels. The benchmark includes datasets for objects, activities, and attributes, and uses metrics such as ExactMatch (EM) and Contains (Cont) to evaluate model responses. The paper also explores the use of GPT-4 as a gold standard metric and discusses the limitations of existing metrics. Despite its innovative approach, the paper has been critiqued for its limited evaluation scope, potential bias in the evaluation metrics, and lack of detailed analysis on the follow-up question generation process.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel approach to evaluating vision-language models (VLMs) using a follow-up question mechanism based on a semantic hierarchy of labels, which is a significant advancement over existing methods.
- It proposes a benchmark that leverages existing classification datasets to generate questions and answers, which is a cost-effective and scalable approach compared to traditional methods.
- The paper provides a comprehensive evaluation of various models using this benchmark, including models like BLIP-2, LLaVA, and InstructBlip, which are tested under different settings such as zero-shot and fine-tuned scenarios.
- The inclusion of human evaluation to validate the proposed metrics is a commendable effort to ensure the reliability and validity of the results.
- The paper is well-written, clear, and easy to follow, making it accessible to a broad audience.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper primarily focuses on evaluating models that generate text, which may not be representative of all VLMs. It would be beneficial to include models that do not generate text in the evaluation to provide a more comprehensive analysis.
- The use of GPT-4 as a gold standard metric raises concerns about the cost and accessibility of the evaluation process. It would be more practical to propose a metric that is not reliant on such high-cost tools.
- The evaluation scope is limited as it only considers a few datasets and models, which may not fully represent the diversity and complexity of VLMs.
- The paper does not adequately address the potential biases in the evaluation metrics, particularly the ExactMatch (EM) metric, which may not be suitable for evaluating models that generate multiple correct answers.
- The follow-up question generation process is not thoroughly analyzed, which is a critical aspect of the proposed benchmark. More detailed analysis on how these questions are generated and their impact on the evaluation results would be beneficial.
- The paper could benefit from more detailed comparisons with existing benchmarks and metrics to establish a clearer differentiation and demonstrate the advantages of the proposed benchmark.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors elaborate on the decision to use GPT-4 as a gold standard metric and discuss potential alternatives that could make the evaluation process more accessible and cost-effective?
- How does the proposed benchmark compare with existing benchmarks, particularly in terms of the diversity and complexity of the models and datasets evaluated?
- Can the authors provide more details on the follow-up question generation process, including how the semantic hierarchy is utilized and how it affects the evaluation results?
- How do the authors address the potential biases in the evaluation metrics, particularly the ExactMatch (EM) metric, and what steps are taken to ensure that the evaluation is fair and unbiased?
- Could the authors discuss the potential impact of the evaluation setup on the evaluation of models that generate multiple correct answers, and propose modifications to the evaluation process to better handle such scenarios?
