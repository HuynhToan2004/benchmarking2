 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel point process model for discrete event data over graphs, leveraging Graph Neural Networks (GNNs) to represent the influence kernel, which captures the underlying graph structure. The model aims to improve the estimation/learning efficiency and predictive performance by integrating statistical and deep models. It is evaluated against state-of-the-art methods on both synthetic and real-world data, showing superior predictive performance and the ability to uncover relational structures among data. The model's flexibility and scalability are highlighted, with the potential to capture complex event dynamics and adapt to various GNN architectures. However, concerns about the clarity of the paper's structure and the need for more detailed discussions on the limitations and potential applications of the model are noted.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel point process model for discrete event data over graphs, which effectively combines statistical and deep models to enhance model estimation/learning efficiency and predictive performance.
- The model's flexibility and scalability are highlighted, allowing it to adapt to various GNN architectures and capture complex event dynamics.
- Comprehensive experiments demonstrate the superior performance of the proposed approach against state-of-the-art methods in both simulated and real data settings, showcasing its ability to uncover relational structures among data.
- The paper is well-written and clearly articulates the model's objectives, methods, and results, making it accessible to a broad audience.
- The proposed method is flexible and can be adapted to various GNN architectures, enhancing its applicability in different scenarios.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed discussion on the limitations of the proposed model, which could hinder its practical application and understanding of its scope.
- There is a need for more clarity in the presentation of the model's technical details, particularly in the explanation of the influence kernel and its relation to the conditional intensity function.
- The paper does not adequately address the potential negative societal impacts of the proposed model, which could limit its acceptance in certain ethical frameworks.
- The paper's structure is not well-organized, making it difficult for readers to follow the logical flow of the content.
- The paper does not sufficiently compare its method with existing models, such as the Geometric Hawkes Process, which could provide a better understanding of the model's advantages and disadvantages.
- The paper's experimental setup and results could be more convincing with additional comparisons and a more detailed analysis of the results.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors provide a more detailed discussion on the limitations of the proposed model, including its assumptions and potential applications?
- How does the proposed method compare to existing models like the Geometric Hawkes Process, particularly in terms of model complexity and interpretability?
- Can the authors clarify the relationship between the influence kernel and the conditional intensity function, and provide a more detailed explanation of how these components contribute to the model's performance?
- How does the model handle the issue of negative event interactions, and what are the implications of this feature for the model's effectiveness and interpretability?
- Could the authors provide a more detailed analysis of the experimental results, including a comparison of the model's performance with different GNN architectures and a discussion of the model's scalability and computational efficiency?
