 **Summary:**
The paper introduces LMEye, a novel interactive perception network designed to enhance multimodal large language models (MLLMs) by enabling dynamic interaction between LLMs and external visual information. The system consists of a request-based visual information interaction module that allows LLMs to request specific visual information aligned with human instructions. This approach is differentiated from traditional methods that only project image features once, not considering the interaction between the image and human input queries. The methodology is evaluated on several multimodal benchmarks, demonstrating superior zero-shot performance compared to previous methods. However, the paper lacks clarity in some technical details and the novelty of the approach is questioned, as similar ideas have been previously explored.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written and easy to follow, making complex concepts accessible to a broader audience.
- The proposed interactive perception network is innovative and allows LLMs to incorporate desired visual information for various human queries, enhancing multimodal understanding and reasoning performances.
- The methodology is evaluated on several multimodal benchmarks, demonstrating superior zero-shot performance compared to previous methods.
- The approach is parameter-efficient and shows significant improvements in zero-shot multimodal performances for various scales and types of LLMs.
- The paper introduces a new dataset for multimodal instruction-following tuning, which is a significant contribution to the field.
- The proposed method is simple yet effective, and the experiments are conducted on multiple datasets and models, demonstrating the robustness of the approach.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks clarity in some technical details, particularly around the construction of the request-based visual information interaction module and the training of the model.
- The novelty of the approach is questioned, as similar ideas have been previously explored in other works.
- The performance improvements are marginal in some cases, and the paper does not adequately compare the proposed method with state-of-the-art models.
- The methodology section is not detailed enough, and the paper could benefit from more comprehensive explanations and examples to aid in understanding.
- The paper does not address the issue of hallucination in LLMs, which is a significant limitation in the field.
- The paper does not include a discussion on the limitations of the proposed method, which is crucial for a complete understanding of its applicability and potential drawbacks.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors provide more details on the construction of the request-based visual information interaction module and the training of the model?
- How does the proposed method compare with other state-of-the-art models in terms of performance and efficiency?
- What are the specific advantages of the proposed method over other interactive perception networks?
- Could the authors clarify the role and impact of the request-based visual information interaction module in the overall performance of the model?
- How does the proposed method address the issue of hallucination in LLMs?
- Could the authors provide more detailed examples and explanations to help understand the methodology and its implementation?
