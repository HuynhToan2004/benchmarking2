 **Summary:**
The paper introduces Tensor Trust, a web game designed to generate adversarial examples for instruction-following Large Language Models (LLMs). The game allows players to craft attacks and defenses, with the goal of tricking LLMs into granting access. The dataset generated by this game is significant, consisting of over 126,000 adversarial examples and 46,000 defenses. The paper evaluates various models using this dataset, demonstrating that even the most capable LLMs can be vulnerable to human-written attacks. The dataset is expected to be useful for evaluating prompt injection mitigations and understanding multi-step attack strategies.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written and easy to follow, with a clear presentation of the dataset and the game mechanics.
- The introduction of a web game, Tensor Trust, is innovative, allowing for the creation of a large dataset of human-generated adversarial examples for instruction-following LLMs.
- The paper provides insights into the weaknesses of LLMs, particularly through the analysis of attack strategies and their generalizability across different models.
- The dataset, which is the largest of its kind, is expected to be a valuable resource for future research on prompt injection attacks and defenses.
- The paper includes a variety of baselines, which helps in evaluating the robustness of different models against prompt injection attacks.
- The findings of the study are significant, showing that even the most capable LLMs can be vulnerable to basic human-written attacks, highlighting the importance of robustness in LLM applications.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed discussion on the limitations of the dataset, such as the potential biases introduced by the game's design or the selection process for attacks and defenses.
- There is a lack of discussion on the generalizability of the attacks beyond the specific models used in the study.
- The paper does not provide a detailed analysis of the defense strategies, which could be as important as the attack strategies in understanding the effectiveness of the dataset.
- The paper does not include a detailed discussion on the transferability of the attacks to real-world applications, which is crucial for evaluating the practical implications of the findings.
- The paper does not include a detailed discussion on the potential negative societal impacts of the findings, which could be significant given the potential misuse of the insights.
- The paper does not include a detailed discussion on the limitations of the dataset, which could affect the reliability and generalizability of the results.

**Questions:**
- How does the paper address the potential biases introduced by the game's design or the selection process for attacks and defenses?
- Can the authors provide more details on the transferability of the attacks to real-world applications?
- How do the authors ensure that the attacks and defenses in the dataset are not biased towards specific models or datasets?
- Can the authors discuss the potential negative societal impacts of the findings and how these could be mitigated?
- How do the authors plan to update the dataset to ensure that it remains relevant and effective in evaluating prompt injection attacks and defenses?
- Can the authors provide more details on the defense strategies and their effectiveness against different types of attacks?
- How do the authors plan to address the limitations of the dataset, such as the potential biases and the generalizability of the results?
