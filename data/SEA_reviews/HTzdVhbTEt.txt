 **Summary:**
The paper explores the problem of achieving long-term fairness in dynamical systems where decisions made at each time step influence the data distribution, leading to a shift in the underlying data-generating process. The authors propose a framework that leverages Markov chains to model these dynamics and optimize policies to converge to a fair stationary state, independent of the initial data distribution. The paper introduces a method for optimizing policies that ensure convergence to a fair stationary state, which is demonstrated through simulations on a semi-synthetic dataset. The approach aims to address the challenge of fairness in dynamic systems, where decisions made in one time step can affect future outcomes, and the system's dynamics are modeled as a time-homogeneous Markov chain.

**Strengths:**
- The paper addresses a significant and timely problem of fairness in dynamical systems, where decisions made at each time step can affect future outcomes.
- The proposed method is novel, leveraging Markov chains to model the system dynamics and optimize policies to converge to a fair stationary state, independent of the initial data distribution.
- The paper is well-written, with clear explanations and illustrative examples that aid in understanding the proposed methodology.
- The authors provide a comprehensive literature review and compare their approach with existing works, highlighting the novelty and relevance of their contributions.
- The simulations demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed method in achieving fairness in dynamical systems.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed discussion on the convergence properties of the proposed method, particularly how long it takes to converge and under what conditions it does not converge.
- There is a lack of discussion on the computational complexity of the proposed optimization problem and the scalability of the method to larger, more complex datasets.
- The paper does not provide sufficient theoretical guarantees for the convergence of the proposed method, which is crucial for ensuring the effectiveness of the approach.
- The assumptions made in the model, such as the time-independent kernel and access to the dynamics defining it, may not hold in real-world scenarios, limiting the practical applicability of the proposed method.
- The paper does not adequately address the limitations of the assumptions, particularly the assumption of a time-homogeneous Markov chain, which may not hold in all real-world scenarios.
- There is a lack of discussion on the sensitivity of the proposed method to different choices of long-term fairness objectives and constraints, which could affect the practical applicability of the approach.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors provide more details on the convergence properties of the proposed method, particularly how long it takes to converge and under what conditions it does not converge?
- How does the computational complexity of the proposed optimization problem scale with the size of the dataset, and what are the practical limitations of the method in terms of scalability?
- What are the assumptions made in the model, and how do they affect the practical applicability of the proposed method?
- How does the proposed method handle scenarios where the dynamics are not modeled as a time-homogeneous Markov chain?
- Can the authors provide a more detailed discussion on the limitations of the assumptions made in the model and how these limitations affect the practical applicability of the proposed method?
- How sensitive is the proposed method to different choices of long-term fairness objectives and constraints, and what are the implications of these choices for the effectiveness of the approach?
