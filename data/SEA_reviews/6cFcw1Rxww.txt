 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel algorithm, Local Search GFlowNets (LS-GFN), which integrates local search into the training process of Generative Flow Networks (GFlowNets) to enhance their performance in generating high-reward samples. The algorithm refines trajectories by backtracking and reconstructing them using forward and backward policies, which are then used to train GFlowNets. The experiments conducted across various biochemical tasks demonstrate the effectiveness of LS-GFN in improving the accuracy of GFlowNets and discovering a higher number of modes. The paper also discusses the limitations and potential improvements in the acceptance rate of the local search algorithm.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, with clear figures and detailed descriptions of the proposed algorithm.
- The experiments conducted are comprehensive and demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed algorithm.
- The algorithm is simple to implement and can be integrated into existing GFlowNet algorithms, making it accessible for further research.
- The paper addresses an important problem in GFlowNets, which is the exploration of high-reward regions, and proposes a novel algorithm to tackle this issue.
- The use of local search to refine trajectories and improve the performance of GFlowNets is a novel approach that has shown promising results.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed discussion on the computational complexity of the proposed algorithm, which could be a significant concern for practical applications.
- There is a need for more extensive experiments to validate the effectiveness of the algorithm across different types of tasks and to compare it with other state-of-the-art methods.
- The paper does not provide sufficient details on the acceptance rate of the local search algorithm, which is crucial for understanding the efficiency and effectiveness of the proposed method.
- The paper could benefit from a more thorough discussion on the limitations of the proposed algorithm and potential avenues for future research.
- The paper does not include a detailed comparison with other local search-based methods, which could provide a better understanding of the algorithm's effectiveness.

**Questions:**
- Could you provide more details on the computational complexity of the proposed algorithm, especially in comparison to other GFlowNet algorithms?
- How does the proposed algorithm perform when applied to other types of tasks beyond those considered in the experiments?
- Can you provide a more detailed analysis of the acceptance rate of the local search algorithm and how it affects the performance of the algorithm?
- What are the potential limitations of the proposed algorithm, and how might these be addressed in future research?
- Could you include a comparison with other local search-based methods to better understand the effectiveness of the proposed algorithm?
- How does the proposed algorithm compare to other methods in terms of computational efficiency and the number of modes discovered?
