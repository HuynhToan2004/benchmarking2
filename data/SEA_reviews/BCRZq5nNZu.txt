 **Summary:**
The paper investigates the performance of continual learning (CL) methods under different data chunking conditions, specifically focusing on the chunking problem where data is split into chunks and each chunk is learned independently without revisiting previous chunks. The authors argue that this setup, which is often overlooked in CL research, can significantly affect performance by causing forgetting, a problem typically associated with task shifts. They propose a simple method of per-chunk weight averaging to mitigate forgetting and enhance performance. The paper presents a detailed analysis of this problem across various datasets and settings, comparing it to standard CL methods and demonstrating the effectiveness of the proposed method.

**Strengths:**
- The paper provides a detailed analysis of the chunking problem in continual learning, which is an important but often overlooked aspect of CL research.
- The experimental results are well-supported by detailed analysis and visualizations, showing the impact of data chunking on model performance.
- The paper is well-written, making it easy to follow and understand, with clear explanations of the experimental setup and methodologies used.
- The proposed method of per-chunk weight averaging is simple yet effective, demonstrated to improve performance in various CL settings.
- The paper addresses an interesting and relevant problem in the field of CL, which is often neglected in current research.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper could benefit from a more comprehensive comparison with existing CL methods, especially those that handle forgetting effectively.
- The paper's claims about the chunking problem being a significant contributor to performance degradation in CL are not convincingly supported by the experimental results, which are limited to specific datasets and settings.
- The novelty of the proposed method is questionable as it is a well-known technique in online learning, and the paper does not adequately differentiate its approach from existing methods.
- The paper lacks a thorough discussion on the limitations of the proposed method, particularly in scenarios where it might not be effective, such as in large-scale or complex datasets.
- The paper's claims about the impact of chunking on CL performance are not convincingly demonstrated, and the results could be more convincing with a broader range of experiments and datasets.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors clarify the experimental setup and results regarding the impact of class imbalance on the chunking problem?
- How does the proposed method compare to other existing methods in terms of performance and computational efficiency?
- Could the authors provide more detailed insights into the choice of datasets and the experimental settings, particularly the decision to use specific datasets and settings?
- How does the proposed method address the forgetting problem in CL, and what are the specific mechanisms or techniques used to mitigate forgetting?
- Could the authors discuss the limitations of their method and provide suggestions for future research directions?
