 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel method for panoramic image-to-image translation, focusing on the conversion of panoramic images to narrow field-of-view images using a two-stage training approach. The first stage trains the model on panoramic images alone, while the second stage introduces a distortion-free discrimination loss and a sphere-based rotation augmentation to address geometric distortions and discontinuities at panorama edges. The method leverages deformable convolutions and positional embeddings to enhance the model's understanding of panoramic structures and maintain rotational equivariance. The paper presents qualitative and quantitative results, demonstrating the effectiveness of the proposed method over existing approaches.

**Strengths:**
- The paper addresses a novel and significant problem of converting panoramic images to narrow field-of-view images, which is crucial for various applications.
- The proposed method is technically sound and includes innovative components such as deformable convolutions, positional embeddings, and a two-stage training strategy, which are well-motivated and contribute to the effectiveness of the model.
- The paper is well-written, making it easy to follow, and the methodology is clearly presented, with a comprehensive experimental setup that includes comparisons with state-of-the-art methods.
- The results showcase the effectiveness of the proposed method, with improvements in both quantitative and qualitative metrics, demonstrating the model's ability to handle geometric distortions and maintain rotational equivariance.
- The paper includes a user study that supports the subjective quality of the results, further validating the effectiveness of the proposed method.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed discussion on the limitations of the proposed method, which could help in understanding the scope and applicability of the model.
- There is a need for more extensive comparisons with existing methods, particularly in terms of the impact of different losses and the effectiveness of the distortion-free discrimination loss.
- The paper does not include results on other datasets, which could help in validating the generalizability of the model.
- The paper could benefit from a more thorough analysis of the model's performance under different conditions, such as varying geometric distortions and rotation angles.
- The use of different datasets with different conditions (e.g., daytime and nighttime) in the experiments might not adequately represent real-world scenarios, which could affect the generalizability of the results.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors provide more details on the computational complexity and training time of the proposed method compared to existing methods?
- How does the model perform when the source and target domains have significant style differences, such as daytime to nighttime or rainy to sunny conditions?
- Can the authors provide more examples or qualitative results to demonstrate the effectiveness of the distortion-free discrimination loss and the sphere-based rotation augmentation?
- Is there a possibility to extend the method to handle other types of panoramic images, such as those captured by 360-degree cameras?
- How does the model handle different types of geometric distortions, such as those caused by varying camera angles or lens distortions?
- Could the authors clarify the role and impact of the different losses in the model, particularly the distortion-free discrimination loss and the style reconstruction loss?
