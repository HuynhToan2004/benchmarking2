 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel regularization technique for autoencoders, termed Least Volume (LV), which aims to reduce the dimensionality of the latent space by minimizing the volume of the latent space. This method is based on the intuition that a flat latent space can be enclosed by a cuboid of smaller volume, which aligns the latent set with the latent coordinate axes. The authors prove that PCA is a special case of this approach and demonstrate the effectiveness of LV through various experiments, including ablation studies. The paper also discusses the theoretical underpinnings of LV and its relationship to PCA, showing that it can induce a similar ordering effect.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, clear, and easy to follow, with a clear motivation and a novel approach to latent space compression.
- The proposed method, Least Volume (LV), is simple, intuitive, and effective, as demonstrated through several experiments.
- The method is well-motivated and supported by both theoretical analysis and experimental results, showing that it can be applied to non-linear autoencoders.
- The paper provides a comprehensive ablation study that supports the claims made about the effectiveness of the LV regularizer.
- The methodology is simple and easy to implement, making it accessible for further research and application.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed discussion on the limitations of the proposed method, particularly in terms of its applicability to more complex datasets or scenarios.
- The experiments are limited in scope, focusing primarily on image datasets and not exploring the application of LV in other domains or with different types of data.
- The paper does not provide a detailed comparison with other methods that aim to compress the latent space of autoencoders, such as PCA-AE or other sparsity-inducing regularizers.
- There is a lack of discussion on the potential negative societal impacts of the proposed method.
- The paper could benefit from a more detailed discussion on the theoretical underpinnings of LV, particularly the role of Lipschitz regularization and its relationship to PCA.
- The paper could be improved by including more detailed experimental results, such as visualizations of the latent space and a discussion on the computational efficiency of the method.
