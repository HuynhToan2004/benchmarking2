 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel Geometric Transform Attention (GTA) mechanism for transformers in the context of novel view synthesis (NVS). GTA encodes geometric structure using relative transformation determined by the geometric relationship between queries and key-value pairs, aiming to enhance the performance of transformer-based NVS models without additional parameters or significant computational overhead. The method is evaluated on multiple NVS datasets and shows promising results, though some reviewers express concerns about the clarity of the method's presentation and the lack of comprehensive comparisons with other state-of-the-art methods.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel method for encoding geometric structure into transformers using a relative transformation based on the geometric relationship between queries and key-value pairs, which is a significant contribution to the field.
- The proposed method, Geometric Transform Attention (GTA), is shown to improve the performance of transformer-based NVS models without requiring additional learned parameters or significant computational overhead.
- The paper is well-written, with clear and concise descriptions of the method and the experimental setup, making it easy to follow.
- The experimental results demonstrate that the proposed method outperforms other positional encoding schemes and shows significant improvements in performance and learning efficiency compared to existing methods.
- The paper includes a detailed analysis of the proposed method, including the design of the geometric representation and its impact on the model's performance.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a comprehensive comparison with other state-of-the-art methods, particularly those that use different types of positional encoding or transformer architectures.
- The presentation of the method is not clear enough, especially in the introduction and in the explanation of the relative transformation and the geometric attributes.
- There is a lack of detailed experimental setup and implementation details, which makes it difficult to reproduce the results and understand the full impact of the proposed method.
- The paper does not include a discussion on the limitations of the proposed method, which could provide valuable insights into its applicability and potential improvements.
- The paper could benefit from a more detailed discussion on the related work, particularly in the context of other transformer-based methods for NVS tasks.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors clarify the computational complexity of the proposed method, particularly in comparison to other positional encoding schemes?
- How does the proposed method compare to other transformer architectures, such as those that use different attention mechanisms or layer structures?
- Can the authors provide more details on the experimental setup, including the specifics of the training and testing environments, and the datasets used?
- How does the proposed method handle the issue of token permutation symmetry in transformers, and what are the implications of this for the model's performance?
- Could the authors elaborate on the design choices for the geometric representation and the impact of different design choices on the model's performance?
- How does the proposed method perform when applied to other types of vision tasks beyond novel view synthesis?
