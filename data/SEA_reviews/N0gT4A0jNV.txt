 **Summary:**
The paper addresses the low-rank matrix completion problem using an alternating minimization approach, focusing on the incoherence assumption and the near-linear time complexity of the algorithm. It extends the work of Jain et al. (2013) by analyzing the algorithm under conditions of approximate updates, which is a departure from the exact updates typically assumed in previous literature. The authors propose a method that can handle errors in the updates and show that the algorithm can recover the ground truth matrix with high probability, provided the errors are sufficiently small. The theoretical analysis is supported by a novel approach that allows for fast, high-accuracy solvers, which are crucial for practical implementation. The paper also discusses the implications of these findings for the broader field of matrix completion and optimization.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel framework for analyzing the alternating minimization algorithm for low-rank matrix completion, which is the first to tolerate errors in the updates.
- The algorithm achieves nearly linear time complexity, which is a significant improvement over previous methods.
- The paper is well-written, clear, and easy to follow, with a clear presentation of the theoretical results and their implications.
