 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel actor-critic algorithm for training stochastic differential equations (SDEs) using policy gradients, addressing the challenges of ill-defined policy gradients and uncontrolled behavior in data-scarce regions. The method is designed to ensure consistency between the forward and backward SDEs, which is crucial for accurate policy gradient estimation. The approach is evaluated on a drug design task, demonstrating superior performance over existing methods. The paper also includes a theoretical analysis and practical implementation details, such as regularization techniques to maintain consistency.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, clear, and easy to follow, making it accessible to a broad audience.
- The proposed method is simple yet effective, with a clear motivation and a novel approach to addressing the challenges of policy gradient training in SDEs.
- The theoretical analysis is sound, providing a strong foundation for the proposed method.
- The experiments conducted on the drug design task demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed method, showing superior performance over existing baselines.
- The paper addresses a significant problem in the field of SDEs and policy gradient training, which is crucial for advancing applications in areas like drug design and generative modeling.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks detailed experimental results and comparisons, particularly in terms of computational efficiency and convergence rates, which are crucial for assessing the practicality and scalability of the proposed method.
- The comparison with other policy gradient methods, such as REINFORCE and DDPG, is limited, which could provide a better understanding of the method's performance relative to existing techniques.
- The paper does not sufficiently discuss the limitations and potential negative societal impacts of the proposed method, which could be a concern for some readers.
- There are some minor typographical and formatting errors that need correction for better readability and professionalism.
- The paper could benefit from more extensive comparisons and evaluations in different settings to validate the generalizability of the proposed method.
