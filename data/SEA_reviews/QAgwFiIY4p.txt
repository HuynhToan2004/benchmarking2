 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel method for converting graphs into sets of points, which is then processed using a set encoder like the Transformer. This approach, utilizing Symmetric Rank Decomposition (SRD), allows for the encoding of graph structures without the need for traditional positional encodings. The method is demonstrated to perform well in both short-range and long-range tasks, outperforming several baseline models on various datasets. Theoretical analysis supports the method's expressivity, showing it to be at least as expressive as existing models. However, concerns were raised about the clarity of the presentation, particularly in explaining the theoretical underpinnings and the experimental setup, which could hinder the paper's accessibility and impact.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel approach for converting graphs into sets of points, which is a significant contribution to the field of graph representation learning.
- The method's theoretical analysis shows that it is at least as expressive as existing models, which is supported by empirical results demonstrating its effectiveness in both short-range and long-range tasks.
- The paper is well-written, making it easy to follow, and includes a thorough analysis of the proposed method, which is supported by both theoretical and empirical evidence.
- The paper addresses the issue of permutation invariance in graph neural networks (GNNs), which is a significant challenge in the field.
- The proposed method shows superior performance in experiments, particularly in molecular property prediction tasks, where it outperforms state-of-the-art methods.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a clear explanation of how the method addresses the problem of permutation invariance in GNNs, which is a critical aspect of the proposed solution.
- The theoretical analysis is complex and may be difficult for non-experts to understand, particularly in sections where the relationship between the SRD and graph isomorphism is discussed.
- The paper could benefit from more detailed explanations of the experimental setup, including the number of runs and the statistical significance of the results.
- The paper does not adequately address the scalability of the method, particularly in terms of its computational complexity and scalability with the size of the graph.
- There are concerns about the novelty of the method, as it appears to be a straightforward application of existing techniques from other fields, such as the SRD from matrix decomposition.
- The paper does not discuss the limitations of the proposed method, which could help readers understand its applicability and potential drawbacks.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors provide a clearer explanation of how the proposed method addresses the problem of permutation invariance in GNNs?
- How does the SRD ensure that the graph isomorphism is preserved when the graph is transformed into a set of points?
- Could the authors elaborate on the experimental setup, particularly the number of runs and the statistical significance of the results?
- How does the proposed method compare in terms of scalability and computational complexity with other existing methods?
- Can the authors provide more details on the limitations of the proposed method, including its applicability and potential drawbacks?
- How does the proposed method compare in terms of expressivity with other existing methods, particularly in terms of its theoretical and practical implications?
