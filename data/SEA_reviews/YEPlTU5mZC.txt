 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel extension of Gaussian processes (GPs) for learning vector signals over latent Riemannian manifolds, named Riemannian manifold vector field GP (RVGP). This method uses positional encoding with eigenfunctions of the connection Laplacian derived from a proximity graph, which approximates the manifold. The RVGP is demonstrated to possess global regularity over the manifold, allowing for the super-resolution and inpainting of vector fields while preserving singularities. The method's effectiveness is validated through applications in neuroscience, specifically in reconstructing high-density neural dynamics from low-density EEG recordings, showing comparable classification accuracy to high-density recordings. The paper also explores the utility of vector field singularities as disease markers and their potential in clinical settings.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, clear, and accessible, making it easy for readers to follow the methodology and results.
- The extension of Gaussian processes (GPs) to vector signals over latent Riemannian manifolds is a significant advancement in the field, particularly in the context of neuroscience applications.
- The methodology is well-motivated and well-supported by theoretical and experimental results, demonstrating the practical utility of the proposed approach.
- The paper provides a comprehensive literature review, highlighting the novelty of the proposed method compared to existing works.
- The application of the proposed method to EEG data and its potential for clinical applications, such as Alzheimer's disease diagnosis, adds a practical and impactful dimension to the research.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed discussion on the computational complexity and scalability of the proposed method, especially in terms of handling high-dimensional datasets or large datasets.
- The manuscript could benefit from a more detailed comparison with other existing methods for learning vector signals over latent Riemannian manifolds, particularly in terms of computational efficiency and scalability.
- The paper does not provide sufficient implementation details, making it difficult for readers to reproduce the results.
- The paper does not adequately address the limitations of the proposed method, such as the need for high sampling densities and the potential impact of noise on the results.
- The paper could benefit from a more thorough discussion on the assumptions and limitations of the method, particularly regarding the construction of the proximity graph and the approximation of the manifold.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors provide more details on the computational complexity and scalability of the proposed method, especially in terms of handling high-dimensional datasets or large datasets?
- How does the proposed method compare in terms of computational efficiency and scalability with other existing methods for learning vector signals over latent Riemannian manifolds?
- Could the authors elaborate on the choice of the number of eigenvectors used in the method and its impact on the results?
- How robust is the method to noise in the data, and what are the implications of using noisy data for training and testing?
- Could the authors discuss the potential impact of different similarity metrics and graph algorithms on the quality of the manifold approximation and the results of the method?
- How does the method perform when the manifold is not known a priori, and what are the implications of using different graph construction methods?
- Could the authors provide more details on the implementation of the method, including the specific algorithms and settings used?
