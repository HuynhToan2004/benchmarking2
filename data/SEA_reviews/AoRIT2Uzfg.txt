 **Summary:**
The paper introduces DRMguard, a novel defense mechanism designed to identify and mitigate backdoor attacks in deep regression models (DRMs) in image domains. The methodology involves a two-stage process: first, the identification of backdoor attacks using a generative model to model the trigger function, and second, the mitigation of these attacks through a benign dataset and a reversed poisoned dataset. The paper presents a theoretical analysis and empirical evidence to support the effectiveness of DRMguard against various backdoor attacks. However, concerns are raised about the limited scope of the experiments, which primarily focus on gaze and head pose estimation tasks, and the lack of comprehensive evaluation against a broader range of backdoor attacks.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel approach to defend against backdoor attacks in DRMs, which is a significant and timely research topic given the increasing use of DRMs in safety-critical applications.
- The theoretical analysis provided is clear, concise, and supported by empirical evidence, demonstrating the effectiveness of the proposed method.
- The paper is well-written, making it easy to follow and understand, with a clear presentation of the methodology and experimental results.
- The proposed method is technically sound, with a novel trigger function reverse engineering method that shows promising results in identifying and mitigating backdoor attacks in DRMs.
- The paper addresses a critical issue in the field of DRMs, which is the vulnerability to backdoor attacks, and provides a comprehensive evaluation of the proposed method against various state-of-the-art defenses.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed discussion on the limitations and potential negative societal impacts of the proposed method, which is crucial for understanding the broader implications of the research.
- The experimental evaluation is limited to only a few backdoor attacks and datasets, which may not adequately demonstrate the generalizability of the proposed method.
- The paper does not compare the proposed method with a broader range of backdoor attacks, which could provide a more comprehensive understanding of its effectiveness and robustness.
- The methodology section is not sufficiently detailed, particularly in the description of the two-stage process and the optimization problem, which could hinder reproducibility and understanding of the proposed method.
- The paper does not provide a detailed discussion on the computational complexity and scalability of the proposed method, which is important for practical deployment in real-world scenarios.
- The paper lacks a discussion on the potential adaptive attacks that could bypass the proposed method, which is a critical aspect of evaluating the robustness of the defense mechanism.

**Questions:**
- Could you provide a detailed discussion on the limitations and potential negative societal impacts of the proposed method?
- How does the proposed method perform against a broader range of backdoor attacks, and what are the implications of these results for the practical deployment of the method?
- Could you clarify the computational complexity and scalability of the proposed method, and how these factors might influence its practical deployment in real-world scenarios?
- How does the proposed method handle adaptive attacks that could potentially bypass the defense mechanism?
- Can you provide a more detailed description of the two-stage process and the optimization problem to enhance reproducibility and understanding of the proposed method?
- How does the proposed method compare to other existing defenses in terms of computational efficiency and practical deployment?
- Could you discuss the potential implications of the method's reliance on a benign dataset for mitigation, and how this might affect the practical deployment of the method in scenarios where such a dataset is not readily available?
