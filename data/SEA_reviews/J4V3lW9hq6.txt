 **Summary:**
The paper introduces NeuralMD, a machine learning (ML) surrogate for numerical molecular dynamics (MD) simulations, specifically designed for predicting protein-ligand binding dynamics. It leverages a multi-grained group symmetric framework to model interactions at different levels of granularity, including atoms in ligands, backbone structures in proteins, and residue-atom pairs in complexes. NeuralMD uses a neural ODE solver to predict trajectories and achieves a 2000x speedup over standard numerical MD simulations. The model is tested on the MISATO dataset, demonstrating superior performance compared to other ML approaches. Despite its strengths, the paper has been critiqued for its lack of novelty, insufficient comparison with other datasets, and limited discussion on the generalizability of its methodology.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, clear, and easy to follow, making it accessible to a broad audience.
- The proposed method, NeuralMD, is novel and innovative, particularly in its application of multi-grained group symmetric frameworks and the use of neural ODEs for trajectory prediction.
- The method achieves a significant speedup (2000x) over standard numerical MD simulations, which is crucial for practical applications in drug discovery.
- The paper provides a detailed description of the methodology, including the use of vector frames to ensure group symmetry and the implementation of a physics-informed neural ODE solver.
- The results show that NeuralMD outperforms other machine learning approaches on 12 tasks using recovery metric and on 3 tasks using stability metric.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a comprehensive discussion on the limitations of the proposed method, which could provide a more balanced view of its capabilities and potential improvements.
- There is a lack of comparison with other datasets, which could help in validating the method's performance and generalizability.
- The paper does not adequately address the issue of catastrophic failure in long-time simulations, which is a known challenge in the field.
- The paper could benefit from more detailed discussions on the scalability and applicability of the method to larger or more complex systems.
- The novelty of the method is questioned as it appears to be a straightforward combination of existing techniques, such as multi-grained MPNNs and neural ODEs.
- The paper could provide more clarity on the role of quantum physics in the methodology and its impact on the results.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors provide more details on the limitations of the proposed method and potential areas for future research?
- How does the method perform when applied to other datasets, and what are the implications for its generalizability?
- Can the authors discuss the potential impact of quantum physics on the methodology and its results?
- Could the authors clarify the role of quantum physics in the methodology and its impact on the results?
- What are the specific challenges and considerations involved in scaling the method to larger or more complex systems?
- How does the method handle the issue of catastrophic failure in long-time simulations, and what measures are in place to mitigate this issue?
