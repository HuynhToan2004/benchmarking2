 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel deep learning model for predicting 3D directions from 3D point clouds, leveraging a probabilistic approach using a heteroscedastic von Mises-Fisher distribution to model data uncertainty. The model is designed to predict the initial direction of low-energy electrons from their complex trajectories, which is crucial for directional dark matter detection. The approach involves a sparse 3D convolutional neural network architecture and approximations to the negative log-likelihood loss, which stabilizes training. The model is evaluated against a non-machine learning algorithm and shows improved performance, although the improvements are marginal. The paper also discusses the model's calibration and its potential application in other fields, such as robotics and computer vision.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel probabilistic deep learning model that predicts 3D directions using a heteroscedastic von Mises-Fisher distribution, which allows for modeling data uncertainty.
- The model is well-motivated and presents a clear, logical approach to the problem of predicting 3D directions from 3D point clouds.
- The paper provides a detailed explanation of the model's architecture and training, including the use of sparse convolutional layers and a novel approximation to the negative log-likelihood loss.
- The experiments conducted demonstrate the model's ability to outperform a non-machine learning algorithm and provide insights into the model's calibration.
- The paper is well-written, with clear figures and a comprehensive literature review that positions the work within the context of existing research.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a clear discussion on the limitations of the model, particularly in terms of its generalizability to other types of data or scenarios.
- The improvements in performance over the non-machine learning algorithm are marginal, and it is unclear whether these improvements are statistically significant.
- The paper does not adequately compare the proposed model with other state-of-the-art models, which could provide a more robust evaluation of the model's performance.
- The paper could benefit from more detailed explanations of the model's training and inference processes, including the specifics of how the model handles data uncertainty and how it integrates with existing machine learning frameworks.
- The paper does not discuss the ethical implications of the work, which could be a significant concern for applications in fields like medical imaging or autonomous systems.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors provide a more detailed discussion on the limitations of the model and how these might impact its generalizability or applicability in different scenarios?
- How does the model perform when applied to other types of data, such as 2D images or different types of 3D point clouds?
- Could the authors provide a more detailed analysis of the model's performance metrics, including the statistical significance of the observed improvements and the impact of different hyperparameters on the model's performance?
- How does the model handle data uncertainty in practice, and what are the implications of this for the model's accuracy and reliability?
- Could the authors discuss the potential ethical implications of the work and how these might be addressed in future applications of the model?
