 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel re-parameterization technique for Vision Transformers (ViTs) aimed at enhancing the training of lightweight models without increasing inference costs. The proposed Two-Dimensional Re-parameterized Linear module (TDRL) utilizes a pyramid structure to fuse hierarchical feature representations from linear-based branches of different depths, addressing the challenges of distribution rectification in transformer-specific architectures. The TDRL is tested on various vision tasks, including image classification, semantic segmentation, and object detection, showing state-of-the-art performance. However, concerns were raised about the novelty of the approach, as it resembles existing techniques like those in CNNs, and the clarity of some experimental results and methodological descriptions.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, with clear and detailed explanations that make the content accessible and understandable.
- The proposed method, TDRL, is innovative and simple, with a straightforward application to various vision tasks.
- The experimental results are robust and demonstrate significant improvements over existing methods, particularly in dense prediction tasks.
- The paper provides a comprehensive analysis of the challenges and solutions in adapting re-parameterization techniques from CNNs to ViTs, which is both interesting and valuable.
- The inclusion of a detailed ablation study and various experimental settings enhances the credibility of the results and the robustness of the method.
- The proposed TDRL is applicable to both self-supervised and supervised training, which broadens its applicability in different scenarios.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a clear explanation of how the proposed method, TDRL, differs from existing re-parameterization techniques in CNNs, which could lead to confusion about its novelty and effectiveness.
- The experimental results, particularly in Table 2, are not convincingly superior to existing methods, and the improvements are sometimes marginal or not statistically significant.
- The paper could benefit from more detailed comparisons with state-of-the-art methods, especially in terms of computational efficiency and model size.
- There are some typographical and formatting errors that detract from the professionalism of the paper.
- The paper does not adequately address the potential negative societal impacts of its work, which is a critical oversight in ethical considerations.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors clarify how TDRL differs from existing re-parameterization techniques in CNNs and why it is more effective for ViTs?
- How does the performance of TDRL compare when using a larger teacher model for distillation, and how does it perform without distillation?
- Is there a specific reason why the proposed method is not tested on larger models like ViT-Small, and what are the potential challenges in applying TDRL to larger models?
- Could the authors provide more detailed comparisons with state-of-the-art methods in terms of computational efficiency and model size?
- How does the proposed method handle the computational overhead introduced by the additional branches and layers, and what are the implications for training and inference times?
- Could the authors address the concerns about the clarity and presentation of some experimental results, particularly in Table 2, to enhance the credibility of their findings?
