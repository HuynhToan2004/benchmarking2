 **Summary:**
The paper introduces Dozerformer, a novel transformer-based architecture designed for multivariate time series forecasting. It employs a sparse attention mechanism, consisting of three components: Local, Stride, and Vary, to capture essential attributes of MTS data such as locality, seasonality, and global temporal dependencies. The method is evaluated against recent state-of-the-art methods on nine benchmark datasets, demonstrating superior performance in terms of both accuracy and efficiency. The proposed Dozer Attention mechanism, which includes these sparse components, is designed to capture locality, seasonality, and global temporal dependencies in MTS data. The paper includes ablation studies to analyze the impact of each component and discusses the computational complexity of the proposed method.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, making it easy to follow, and includes a comprehensive ablation study that helps understand the impact of each component.
- The proposed Dozer Attention mechanism is novel and demonstrates good performance on the benchmark datasets, showing superior performance compared to state-of-the-art methods.
- The method is well-motivated, and the paper provides a clear explanation of the motivation behind the design of the proposed method.
