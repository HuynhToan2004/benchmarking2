 **Summary:**
The paper explores the role of a specific attention head in the GPT-2 Small model, focusing on how it suppresses copying behavior to improve model calibration. This attention head, termed the copy suppression head, is analyzed through various experiments and ablations, revealing its impact on self-repair and other phenomena. The authors use the OpenWebText dataset to demonstrate the head's functionality and its role in broader language model behavior. The paper also discusses the implications of these findings for understanding language model behavior and improving model interpretability.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, making it easy to follow and understand the authors' motivations and hypotheses.
- The analysis of copy suppression in language models is novel and provides a detailed exploration of the mechanisms involved.
- The paper includes a comprehensive set of experiments and ablations, which support the claims made about the role of the copy suppression head.
- The use of the OpenWebText dataset for analysis is commendable, providing a robust foundation for the study.
- The paper provides a detailed analysis of the copy suppression head, which is a significant contribution to understanding the behavior of language models.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks clarity in some sections, particularly in the descriptions of experiments and the results, which could benefit from more detailed explanations and illustrations.
- The paper's claims about the generalizability of the findings to other models are not convincingly supported, and the implications for model interpretability are not adequately explored.
- There is a lack of discussion on the limitations of the study, such as the potential impact of dropout on the results and the generalizability of the findings to other models.
- The paper could benefit from a more rigorous comparison with existing literature, particularly in terms of how the copy suppression head relates to other mechanisms like dropout and the effects on model behavior.
- The paper's presentation could be improved by better organizing the content and providing clearer visualizations to aid in understanding the complex concepts and results.

**Questions:**
- How does the copy suppression head affect the model's behavior in terms of self-repair, and what are the implications for model interpretability?
- Can the authors clarify the role of the copy suppression head in different models, particularly in models that do not use dropout?
- How does the paper's analysis of copy suppression contribute to broader understandings of language model behavior, and what are the potential applications of these insights?
- Could the authors provide more detailed explanations or illustrations of the experiments and results, particularly in sections where the clarity could be improved?
- How do the findings of this study relate to other mechanisms like dropout, and what are the implications for future research on model interpretability?
