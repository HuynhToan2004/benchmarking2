 **Summary:**
The paper introduces LATEC, a large-scale benchmark designed to evaluate various Explainable AI (XAI) methods across different modalities (image, point cloud, and volume) using 20 distinct metrics. The benchmark employs 17 XAI methods, including attention-based and attribution-based approaches, and includes models trained on specific datasets to ensure state-of-the-art (SOTA) performance. The dataset and model weights are made publicly available to support further research. The study explores the impact of model architectures and input data types on XAI performance, aiming to provide a comprehensive understanding of XAI methods' effectiveness.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-structured and clearly written, making it easy to follow and understand.
- It introduces a large-scale benchmark, LATEC, which evaluates various XAI methods across multiple datasets, providing a comprehensive evaluation of different modalities.
- The benchmark includes a significant number of XAI methods and metrics, which can be useful for future research and comparative analysis.
- The paper is well-motivated, addressing the need for a comprehensive evaluation of XAI methods across different architectures and modalities.
- The dataset and model weights are made publicly available, which can facilitate further research and reproducibility.
- The paper includes a variety of metrics and evaluation methods, which are useful for assessing the effectiveness and robustness of XAI methods.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a thorough discussion on the limitations and potential biases of the benchmark, which could affect the generalizability and applicability of the results.
- There is a lack of detailed explanation on the methodology used for selecting the XAI methods and metrics, which could influence the perceived reliability and validity of the results.
- The paper does not adequately address the limitations of the benchmark, such as the absence of video data and the potential biases in the evaluation metrics.
- The paper does not provide sufficient details on the computational requirements and resources needed to run the benchmark, which could limit its accessibility and usability for researchers.
- The paper could benefit from a more comprehensive evaluation of the benchmark's performance and a more detailed discussion on the potential biases and limitations of the evaluation metrics.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors provide more details on the methodology used for selecting the XAI methods and metrics, including the criteria and rationale behind their choices?
- How were the parameters for the evaluation metrics tuned, and what impact does this have on the results?
- Could the authors discuss the potential biases and limitations of the benchmark, and how these might affect the generalizability and applicability of the results?
- How can the benchmark be extended to include more diverse modalities, such as video data, and what are the potential challenges and opportunities in doing so?
- What are the computational requirements and resources needed to run the benchmark, and how can these be made more accessible to researchers?
