 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel approach to NeRF rendering, focusing on the integration of coordinate-based networks to capture global contexts and multi-plane encoding for fine-grained details. This method aims to address the limitations of existing NeRFs in handling sparse input data and to enhance the representation of global contexts in dynamic scenes. The approach is evaluated against state-of-the-art methods, showing promising results in terms of capturing high-frequency details and reducing artifacts. The paper also includes a curriculum weighting strategy to manage the integration of different features effectively. Despite its strengths, the paper is criticized for its incremental nature and lack of significant novelty, as well as insufficient comparisons with existing methods.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written and easy to follow, making it accessible to a broad audience.
- The proposed method effectively captures high-frequency details in static scenes and reduces artifacts in dynamic scenes, as demonstrated through qualitative results.
- The combination of coordinate-based networks and multi-plane encoding is a novel approach that enhances the representation of global contexts in NeRFs.
- The method is evaluated against state-of-the-art methods, showing promising results in terms of capturing high-frequency details and reducing artifacts.
- The paper includes an ablation study that supports the effectiveness of the proposed method.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a clear justification for the need to capture global contexts in NeRFs, which is crucial for the proposed method's effectiveness.
- The method's reliance on coordinate-based networks for global context capture may lead to smoothing of details, as shown in the results.
- The paper does not sufficiently compare its method with existing works, particularly in terms of the frequency spectrum of coordinate-based networks and the use of skip connections.
- The paper does not provide sufficient visual results to support the claims made, such as the reduction in artifacts and the effectiveness of the curriculum weighting strategy.
- The paper's experimental setup and results are not convincingly superior to existing methods, and the improvements are marginal in some cases.
- The paper lacks a thorough discussion on the limitations and potential negative societal impacts of the proposed method.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors provide more detailed comparisons with existing works, particularly in terms of the frequency spectrum of coordinate-based networks and the use of skip connections?
- How does the proposed method perform in scenarios with severe occlusions or dynamic motions?
- Can the authors clarify the role of coordinate-based networks in capturing global contexts and the potential impact on detail preservation?
- How does the proposed method compare to other methods that use similar strategies, such as progressive weighting or curriculum weighting?
- Can the authors provide more visual results to support the claims made about the reduction in artifacts and the effectiveness of the curriculum weighting strategy?
- How does the method handle the trade-off between detail preservation and global context capture, particularly in scenarios with complex or dynamic scenes?
