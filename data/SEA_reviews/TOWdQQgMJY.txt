 **Summary:**
The paper introduces SAGE, a method designed to automatically discover failure modes in text-guided diffusion models (TDMs) by systematically exploring the discrete prompt space and high-dimensional latent space. SAGE uses image classifiers as surrogate loss functions to identify undesirable behaviors and failure cases in image generation. The method aims to overcome the gradient vanishing problem and systematically reveals four failure modes that have not been extensively studied before. These failure modes include natural text prompts that fail to capture semantics, distorted images independent of text prompts, latent samples generating unrelated images, and token embeddings that overwrite input prompts. The paper also includes human inspections to validate the identified failures and provides a comprehensive analysis of state-of-the-art generative models.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel method, SAGE, which is the first adversarial search method for discovering failure modes in text-guided diffusion models (TDMs), offering a new approach to understanding and addressing issues in these models.
- SAGE's ability to explore both the discrete prompt space and high-dimensional latent space is highlighted as a significant strength, providing a more comprehensive understanding of failure modes in TDMs.
- The paper presents a clear and well-organized structure, with detailed explanations of the methodology and experimental results, making it accessible and informative.
- The paper provides a thorough analysis of state-of-the-art generative models, offering insights into their strengths and weaknesses, and identifies four typical failure modes that have not been systematically studied before.
- The inclusion of human inspections to validate the identified failures adds a layer of rigor to the analysis, ensuring the reliability of the findings.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed discussion on the limitations of SAGE, which could provide a more balanced view of the method's capabilities and potential shortcomings.
- There is a lack of clarity on the specific role and impact of the adversarial search method used in SAGE, particularly in relation to the identification of failure modes.
- The paper does not adequately address the generalizability of the findings to other types of text-guided diffusion models or other generative models beyond TDMs.
- The paper's reliance on image classifiers as surrogate loss functions may not fully capture the nuances of text-guided diffusion models, potentially limiting the depth of the analysis.
- The paper could benefit from a more detailed discussion on the potential negative societal impacts of the findings, particularly in relation to the generation of safety-critical materials.

**Questions:**
- Can SAGE be applied to other types of text-guided diffusion models or other generative models beyond TDMs? If so, what are the specific challenges and limitations in doing so?
- How does the adversarial search method used in SAGE contribute to the identification of failure modes in TDMs? Could other optimization methods achieve similar results?
- In the context of the paper's findings, how can the identified failure modes be addressed to improve the reliability and effectiveness of TDMs?
- Could the authors provide more details on the generalizability of the findings, particularly in terms of whether the identified failure modes are specific to the models tested or more broadly applicable?
- How do the authors address the potential negative societal impacts of the findings, particularly in relation to the generation of safety-critical materials?
