 **Summary:**
The paper introduces CASPR, a novel adaptive regularization method for deep learning optimization that utilizes a Kronecker-sum based combination of axes preconditioners. This method aims to approximate the full-matrix statistic more accurately than existing Kronecker-product based methods like Shampoo. Theoretical analysis demonstrates that CASPR's regret bound in the online convex optimization framework is tighter than Shampoo's. Experimental results show that CASPR outperforms Shampoo and other adaptive regularization methods such as Adam in various tasks including graph neural networks, transformers, and auto-regressive language modeling. The paper also provides a theoretical analysis of CASPR's convergence guarantees, comparing it to Shampoo and full-matrix Adagrad.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, making it easy to follow, and the mathematical analysis is rigorous.
- The proposed method, CASPR, is novel and well-motivated, providing a tighter regret bound than Shampoo in the online convex optimization framework.
- The experiments are well-designed, showing that CASPR outperforms Shampoo and other adaptive regularization methods in various tasks.
- The theoretical analysis of CASPR's convergence guarantees is solid, and the proofs are correct.
- The paper provides a clear and concise explanation of the proposed method and its theoretical underpinnings, making it accessible to readers.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper could benefit from more detailed explanations and intuition for certain theoretical results, such as the choice of p in Lemma 3.2 and the implications of the Kronecker sum in Lemma 3.2.
- There is a lack of discussion on the limitations of the proposed method, particularly in terms of its scalability and applicability to different types of neural networks.
- The experimental results are not comprehensive enough, and the paper could benefit from more diverse and extensive experiments, especially in terms of comparing CASPR with other adaptive regularization methods and in different scenarios.
- The paper could be more self-contained, particularly in the introduction and related work sections, which currently assume prior knowledge of Shampoo.
- The paper could provide more details on the computational complexity and the practical implementation of CASPR, especially in terms of its scalability and efficiency.

**Questions:**
- Could you provide more intuition on the choice of p in Lemma 3.2 and the implications of the Kronecker sum in Lemma 3.2?
- How does the proposed method compare to other adaptive regularization methods, particularly in terms of computational complexity and scalability?
- Can you discuss the limitations of the proposed method and its potential applicability to different types of neural networks?
- In terms of practical implementation, how does CASPR perform in terms of computational efficiency and scalability compared to other adaptive regularization methods?
- Could you provide more details on the experimental setup, particularly in terms of the hyperparameters used and the training conditions for different methods?
