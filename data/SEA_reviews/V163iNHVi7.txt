 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel methodology for estimating mathematical expectations using a combination of diffusion bridge models and the Feynman-Kac operator, implemented through Physically Informed Neural Networks (PINNs). The approach aims to address the curse of dimensionality and enhance the efficiency of Markov chain Monte Carlo (MCMC) algorithms by estimating expectations more accurately with fewer samples. The methodology involves constructing a diffusion bridge over the target distribution or known data, using PINNs to solve the Feynman-Kac equation, and then solving the PDE to obtain the expectation. The paper also discusses the practical implications and theoretical foundations of this approach, although it lacks detailed experimental validation and a thorough discussion on the computational complexity and scalability of the proposed methods.

**Weaknesses:**
