 **Summary:**
The paper introduces P2RBox, a novel approach for oriented object detection using point annotations. The method involves a two-stage process where the first stage generates mask proposals from point annotations, and the second stage selects high-quality masks using an Inspector Module. These masks are then transformed into rotated bounding boxes using a Symmetry Axis Estimation Module. The paper claims that this method can outperform traditional methods using rotated bounding boxes and demonstrates its effectiveness on the DOTA dataset. However, concerns are raised about the clarity of the method's motivation, the novelty of the approach, and the generalization of the results to other datasets or scenarios.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, easy to follow, and presents a novel approach to oriented object detection using point annotations.
- The proposed method, P2RBox, is technically sound and shows promising results, particularly in the context of oriented object detection.
- The paper includes an ablation study that demonstrates the effectiveness of each component of the proposed method, providing a clear understanding of its contributions.
- The use of point annotations for oriented object detection is a significant advancement over traditional methods that rely on rotated bounding boxes, offering potential for cost savings and efficiency.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a clear motivation for using point annotations over traditional rotated bounding boxes, and it does not adequately address the challenges or limitations of using point annotations.
- The novelty of the proposed method is questionable as it appears to be a combination of existing techniques without significant innovation.
- The results presented are not convincing, with the paper relying heavily on a single dataset (DOTA) and not comparing the proposed method against state-of-the-art methods across multiple datasets.
- There is a lack of clarity in the paper regarding the method's applicability to other scenarios or datasets, and the generalization of the results is not adequately demonstrated.
- The paper does not provide sufficient details on the implementation and training of the proposed method, which could hinder reproducibility and further research.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors provide more detailed motivation for using point annotations over traditional rotated bounding boxes, and discuss the challenges or limitations associated with using point annotations?
- How does the proposed method compare to state-of-the-art methods across multiple datasets, and can the authors provide a more comprehensive evaluation of the method's performance?
- Can the authors clarify the method's applicability to other scenarios or datasets, and discuss the potential for extending the method to other domains or tasks?
- How does the proposed method handle scenarios where the point annotations are not accurate or reliable, and what measures are in place to ensure the quality of the point annotations?
- Can the authors provide more details on the implementation and training of the proposed method, including hyperparameters and training strategies, to facilitate reproducibility and further research?
