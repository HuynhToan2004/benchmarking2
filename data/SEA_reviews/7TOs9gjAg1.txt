 **Summary:**
The paper introduces InfoCORE, a novel method for learning multimodal molecular representations that addresses batch effects in high-throughput drug screening data. It leverages a conditional contrastive learning objective to optimize the representation of molecular structures and phenotypic data, aiming to mitigate the influence of batch effects on the learned representations. The method is evaluated on various datasets, including drug screening and fairness benchmarks, demonstrating its effectiveness in improving molecular representation and mitigating batch effects. Despite its theoretical contributions and empirical validation, concerns are raised about the clarity of the writing, the necessity of the proposed method over existing solutions, and the lack of detailed experimental results.

**Strengths:**
- The paper addresses an important and relevant problem in drug discovery, specifically focusing on the removal of batch effects in molecular representations, which is crucial for accurate drug screening.
- The proposed method, InfoCORE, is well-motivated and theoretically justified, with a solid foundation in information theory and contrastive learning.
- The paper is well-written, making it accessible and easy to follow, which is essential for a broad audience.
- The empirical evaluation is comprehensive, including various datasets and benchmarks, which demonstrates the effectiveness of the proposed method in practical scenarios.
- The authors have provided detailed derivations of the proposed method, which enhances the reproducibility and transparency of the research.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper suffers from several writing issues that make it difficult to follow, including unclear explanations and notations, and a lack of detail in certain sections.
- The experimental setup and results are not adequately described, making it hard to assess the robustness and generalizability of the findings.
- The comparison with existing methods is insufficient, and the paper could benefit from a more thorough discussion on the advantages and disadvantages of different approaches.
- The paper does not adequately address the limitations of the proposed method, which could include the potential for overfitting or the impact of different hyperparameters on the results.
- The paper lacks a detailed discussion on the fairness benchmarks used, which could confuse readers unfamiliar with these concepts.

**Questions:**
- Could you clarify the role and impact of the hyperparameter $\alpha$ in the proposed method, and how it affects the performance of InfoCORE?
- How does the proposed method compare to other existing methods like CCL and CLIP, particularly in terms of computational efficiency and scalability?
- What are the specific computational and memory requirements of the InfoCORE model, and how do these compare to other state-of-the-art methods?
- Can you provide more details on the experimental setup, including the specifics of the datasets used, the data preprocessing steps, and the evaluation metrics?
- How does the proposed method perform in scenarios with more complex or varied batch effects, and what are the potential limitations in such cases?
- Could you elaborate on the fairness benchmarks used in the experiments, and how these benchmarks contribute to the overall evaluation of the method?
