 **Summary:**
The paper investigates the impact of computational methods on the performance of integral reinforcement learning (IntRL) in continuous-time control problems. It focuses on the computational error introduced by quadrature rules used to approximate the integral of the utility function, which affects the convergence behavior of policy iteration. The paper provides theoretical analysis and numerical simulations to support its claims, showing that the choice of quadrature rule significantly influences the convergence rate of IntRL. It also explores the application of Bayesian quadrature to minimize worst-case errors and demonstrates how these errors propagate through iterative policy evaluation and improvement. The paper's contributions include a detailed analysis of computational errors in the context of continuous-time control and their impact on the performance of learned controllers.

**Strengths:**
- The paper provides a thorough theoretical analysis of the impact of computational methods on the performance of integral reinforcement learning (IntRL) in continuous-time control problems.
