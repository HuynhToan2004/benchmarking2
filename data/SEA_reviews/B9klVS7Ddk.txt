 **Summary:**
The paper introduces LLM-KICK, a benchmark designed to evaluate the performance of compressed Large Language Models (LLMs) using a variety of tasks. The benchmark includes settings such as factoid-based QA, multiple-choice reasoning-based QA, in-context retrieval augmented QA, in-context text summarization, and instruction-following. The authors argue that perplexity is an inadequate metric for evaluating the performance of compressed LLMs, and propose a more comprehensive evaluation framework. The paper also critiques the effectiveness of current pruning and quantization methods, suggesting that they may not adequately preserve knowledge in LLMs.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel benchmark, LLM-KICK, which comprehensively evaluates the performance of compressed Large Language Models (LLMs) across a variety of tasks.
- It critiques the use of perplexity as a metric for evaluating compressed LLMs and proposes a more comprehensive evaluation framework.
- The paper provides a detailed analysis of the performance of compressed LLMs in knowledge-intensive tasks, highlighting the challenges and opportunities for future research.
- It challenges the notion that pruning and quantization methods can effectively preserve knowledge in LLMs, and presents evidence of their limitations.
- The paper is well-written, easy to follow, and provides a detailed description of the experimental setup and results, making it accessible to a broad audience.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper primarily uses the Vicuna model for evaluation, which might limit the generalizability of the findings to other models.
- The paper does not propose new compression methods, but rather critiques the current methods, which could limit its impact on advancing the field.
- The paper's focus on a specific set of compression methods (pruning and quantization) might not fully capture the diversity of methods used in the field.
- The evaluation of LLM compression is limited to a few models and tasks, which could limit the robustness of the findings.
- The paper does not discuss the potential of other compression methods, such as LoRA, which could provide a more comprehensive view of the field.
- The paper could benefit from a more detailed discussion on the limitations of the proposed benchmark and the generalizability of the findings.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors elaborate on the choice of Vicuna as the primary model for evaluation and discuss the implications for the generalizability of the findings?
- How do the authors envision the development of new compression methods based on the insights provided by the benchmark?
- Could the authors discuss the potential of using other compression methods, such as LoRA, and how they might compare to the methods evaluated in the benchmark?
- How do the authors address the limitations of the benchmark, such as its focus on specific models and tasks, and discuss the potential for expanding the scope of the evaluation?
