 **Summary:**
The paper investigates the generalization performance of neural networks by proposing a modified natural gradient descent method, which aims to reduce generalization error by adjusting the training dynamics in the function space. The method is theoretically analyzed using the Neural Tangent Kernel theory, focusing on the NTK regime where the network width tends to infinity. The paper provides a theoretical analysis of the generalization error bound and demonstrates that the modified natural gradient descent can lead to a lower generalization error compared to conventional methods. The paper also includes numerical experiments to validate the theoretical findings, although the experiments are limited to specific datasets and network architectures. The paper's contributions are seen as significant, offering a new perspective on generalization in neural networks and providing a theoretical framework for improving generalization in deep learning.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel method for improving generalization in neural networks by modifying the natural gradient descent method, which is a significant contribution to the field.
- Theoretical results are well-supported by rigorous proofs, which enhances the credibility of the findings.
- The paper is well-written, clear, and easy to follow, making the complex theoretical concepts accessible to a broad audience.
- The connection between the proposed method and existing results on generalization-enhancing methods is well-explained, providing a clear link to previous research.
- The paper is well-organized, with clear explanations of the assumptions and methodologies used, which facilitates understanding and reproducibility of the results.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper is limited to the NTK regime, which might limit its applicability to more general cases of neural networks.
- The experiments are limited to specific datasets and network architectures, which might not generalize well to other settings.
- The paper does not provide a detailed discussion on the limitations of the proposed method, which could help in understanding its applicability and potential drawbacks.
- There is a lack of discussion on the practical implications of the proposed method, such as its computational complexity and scalability.
- The paper could benefit from more extensive numerical experiments to validate the theoretical claims more robustly.
- The paper does not discuss the limitations of the assumptions made, which could affect the generalizability and applicability of the results.
