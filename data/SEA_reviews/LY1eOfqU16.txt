 **Summary:**
The paper addresses the challenge of learning fair representations in the presence of partially annotated sensitive attributes, specifically focusing on the binary case. It introduces a regularization approach that leverages the output probability of an attribute classifier as "soft" pseudo labels to optimize fairness-aware learning. The authors propose two uncertainty-aware regularization methods to mitigate the impact of parameter uncertainty in the auxiliary models. Experiments are conducted on the Adult and CelebA datasets, demonstrating the effectiveness of the proposed methods in achieving both utility and fairness. The paper also explores the Bayesian viewpoint and employs Bayesian regularization to account for parameter uncertainty.

**Strengths:**
- The paper addresses an important and practical problem of fairness-aware learning with partially annotated sensitive attributes, which is relevant in real-world applications.
- The proposed method is novel, utilizing soft labels derived from the definition of fairness criteria, which is a different approach from traditional hard pseudo labels.
- The paper is well-organized, clearly written, and easy to follow, making it accessible to a broad audience.
- The experiments conducted on two different datasets demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed method in achieving both utility and fairness, validating the theoretical contributions.
- The paper includes code, enhancing reproducibility and transparency.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper's technical contribution is somewhat limited, as the proposed method is a simple modification of existing methods and lacks a detailed comparison with state-of-the-art methods.
- The experimental results, particularly on the CelebA dataset, are not convincing, with the proposed method showing poorer performance compared to the vanilla method in terms of accuracy.
- There is a lack of discussion on the limitations and potential negative societal impacts of the proposed method.
- The paper does not provide a clear explanation on how the Bayesian viewpoint and Bayesian regularization are utilized in the proposed method, and it's unclear how the uncertainty is accounted for in the model.
- The paper could benefit from a more detailed comparison with existing methods and a broader range of datasets to validate the effectiveness of the proposed approach.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors clarify the technical contribution of their method and provide a more detailed comparison with state-of-the-art methods?
- Why does the proposed method perform worse in terms of accuracy on the CelebA dataset compared to the vanilla method?
- How does the proposed method handle the uncertainty in the attribute classifier parameters, and what is the impact of this uncertainty on the model's performance?
- Can the authors provide a more detailed explanation on how the Bayesian viewpoint and Bayesian regularization are utilized in the proposed method?
- How does the proposed method perform when the proportion of labeled data is different across different datasets, and what are the implications for the model's performance?
