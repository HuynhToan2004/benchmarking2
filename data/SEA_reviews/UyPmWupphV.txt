 **Summary:**
The paper introduces Hyperion, a novel method that integrates multi-trial and one-shot NAS techniques to jointly optimize neural network hyperparameters and architectures. Hyperion leverages a smart splitter to distribute search parameters between optimizers, reducing GPU resource usage and improving optimization efficiency. The method is evaluated across various datasets, including open and industrial, demonstrating significant improvements in model performance and resource utilization. Despite its innovative approach, the paper is criticized for its limited experimental scope, primarily focusing on small datasets and a limited range of hyperparameters. The paper also lacks a comprehensive discussion on related works and a detailed comparison with state-of-the-art methods.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel method that integrates multi-trial and one-shot NAS techniques to jointly optimize neural network hyperparameters and architectures, which is a significant advancement in the field.
- The proposed Hyperion method is well-motivated, with a clear and easy-to-understand framework that effectively reduces GPU resource usage and enhances optimization efficiency.
- The paper is well-written, with a clear structure and effective use of figures and tables to support the findings.
- The methodology of Hyperion is novel and shows potential for improving neural architecture search, particularly in terms of computational efficiency.
- The paper is significant as it addresses the joint optimization of hyperparameters and neural architecture, which is crucial for improving the performance of neural networks.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a comprehensive discussion on related works, particularly in the area of joint hyperparameter and neural architecture optimization. This makes it difficult to assess the novelty and contribution of the proposed method.
- The experimental scope is limited, primarily focusing on small datasets and a limited range of hyperparameters, which may not fully demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed method in more complex scenarios.
- There is a lack of detailed comparative analysis with state-of-the-art methods, which is necessary to establish the superiority of the proposed method.
