 **Summary:**
The paper introduces Neuroformer, a transformer-based model designed for multimodal and multitask pre-training of neural data, including neuronal spiking data, visual stimuli, and behavior. The model uses a contrastive learning objective to align neural features with other modalities and a causal spike modeling objective for autoregressive decoding of neuron IDs and time intervals. Neuroformer is trained on simulated and real neural datasets and demonstrates its ability to accurately predict simulated neuronal circuit activity, infer neural circuit connectivity, and predict mouse behavior with few-shot fine-tuning. The model is evaluated on various datasets, showing its potential in neuroscience research and its ability to enhance the predictive capabilities of neural models.

**Strengths:**
- The paper presents a novel application of multimodal and multitask pre-training for neuroscience data, which is an innovative approach in the field.
- The methodology is well-explained, with clear figures and detailed descriptions of the model architecture and training procedures.
- The paper includes a comprehensive evaluation on both simulated and real neural datasets, demonstrating the model's ability to accurately predict simulated neuronal circuit activity and infer neural circuit connectivity.
- The results show that Neuroformer can accurately predict mouse behavior with few-shot fine-tuning, highlighting its potential for predicting behavior from neural representations.
- The paper provides an ablation study that shows joint training on neuronal responses and behavior boosts performance, indicating the model's ability to associate behavioral and neural representations in an unsupervised manner.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed discussion on the limitations of the proposed method, particularly the scalability of the model to larger datasets and the computational resources required for training and inference.
- There is a lack of comparative analysis with other models or methods, which could provide a better understanding of Neuroformer's advantages and disadvantages.
- The paper does not provide a comprehensive analysis of the model's performance on different types of neuronal data or across different brain regions, which could affect the generalizability of the results.
- The paper does not include a discussion on the potential societal implications of the work, which is a significant omission for a paper in this field.
- The model's architecture and training objectives are not sufficiently innovative, as they closely resemble existing models like Vision-Language Pre-training 2.0 (VL2), which may limit the perceived novelty of the work.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors provide more details on the computational resources required for training and inference with Neuroformer, particularly in relation to the scalability of the model to larger datasets?
- How does Neuroformer compare to other models or methods in terms of performance and efficiency on different types of neuronal data and across different brain regions?
- Could the authors elaborate on the potential societal implications of their work, particularly in the context of brain-computer interfaces and other applications in neuroscience research?
- What are the specific advantages of using Neuroformer over other models or methods for predicting behavior from neural data, and how does it compare in terms of performance and generalizability?
