 **Summary:**
The paper introduces Domain-Connecting Contrastive Learning (DCCL), a method aimed at enhancing domain generalization by improving intra-class connectivity in contrastive learning. DCCL leverages more aggressive data augmentation and cross-domain positive samples to bridge the gap between training and test domains. It also introduces model anchoring and a generative transformation loss to align pre-trained representations with unseen test domains. The method is evaluated on five standard domain generalization benchmarks, showing significant improvements over state-of-the-art baselines. The paper is praised for its clear presentation and extensive experimental validation, although some reviewers express concerns about the novelty and depth of the theoretical analysis.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, clear, and easy to follow, with a clear motivation and a logical structure that facilitates understanding.
- The proposed method is simple and effective, achieving significant improvements in domain generalization benchmarks.
- The authors provide a comprehensive analysis of the failure of self-contrastive learning in domain generalization and propose effective strategies to address this issue.
- The paper includes extensive experiments and ablation studies that demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed method.
- The use of pre-trained models as data augmentation is a novel approach that enhances the method's performance.
- The paper is well-organized and includes a detailed literature review, making it a valuable resource for understanding the current state of domain generalization research.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a thorough theoretical analysis, particularly in explaining why the proposed method improves intra-class connectivity and domain generalization.
- The novelty of the method is questioned as it seems to be a combination of existing techniques such as more aggressive data augmentation and cross-domain positive samples.
- The experimental results are not convincingly superior to state-of-the-art methods, and the paper does not adequately compare the proposed method with other domain generalization methods.
- The paper could benefit from more detailed explanations of the experimental settings and the methodology used, particularly in the application of data augmentation and the use of pre-trained models.
- There is a lack of discussion on the limitations of the proposed method, which could help in understanding its scope and applicability.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors provide a more detailed theoretical analysis of why the proposed method improves intra-class connectivity and domain generalization?
- How does the paper address the potential issue of overfitting to the training data due to the use of a pre-trained model as data augmentation?
- Could the authors clarify the role of data augmentation in the proposed method and how it contributes to the improvement in domain generalization?
- In the context of domain generalization, how does the proposed method compare with other methods that leverage pre-trained models, such as SWAD and MIRO?
- What are the specific contributions of each component of the proposed method, and how do they interact with each other to achieve the observed improvements?
- Can the authors provide more details on the experimental settings, particularly the application of data augmentation and the use of pre-trained models, to enhance the reproducibility and understanding of the results?
