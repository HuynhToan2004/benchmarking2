 **Summary:**
The paper introduces KW-Design, a novel method for protein structure design that leverages pretrained models to refine low-confidence residues, enhancing the accuracy and recovery rate of protein structures. The method utilizes a confidence-aware module to identify and refine residues based on predictive confidence, which is demonstrated to outperform existing methods such as PiFold. The approach also incorporates a memory-retrieval mechanism to save training time and is evaluated across multiple datasets, showing significant improvements. However, the paper has been critiqued for its limited novelty, lack of comprehensive experimental validation, and insufficient discussion on the computational efficiency and scalability of the method.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written and easy to follow, with clear figures that aid in understanding the proposed method.
- The use of pretrained models to enhance protein design is a novel approach, and the methodology is sound and innovative.
- The paper presents a robust experimental setup, demonstrating the effectiveness of the proposed method through a comprehensive evaluation.
- The introduction of a memory-retrieval mechanism and a multimodal fusion module are highlighted as significant contributions to the field.
- The paper provides a detailed ablation study, which helps in understanding the impact of each component of the method.
- The method achieves state-of-the-art (SOTA) performance on several benchmarks, demonstrating its effectiveness in real-world applications.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks comprehensive experimental validation, particularly in terms of wet experiments, which limits the practical applicability of the proposed method.
- The computational efficiency and scalability of the method are not thoroughly discussed, raising concerns about its feasibility for large-scale protein design tasks.
- The novelty of the approach is questioned, as the use of pretrained models and confidence-aware refinement are not entirely novel.
- The paper does not sufficiently discuss the limitations of the method, which could help in understanding its applicability and potential drawbacks.
- The method's performance improvements over existing methods are not sufficiently explained, and the reasons for its effectiveness are not adequately analyzed.
- The paper lacks a detailed comparison with other baseline methods, which could have provided a more comprehensive evaluation of the proposed method.

**Questions:**
- Can you provide more details on how the memory-retrieval mechanism is implemented and how it affects the computational efficiency of the method?
- How does the method perform when applied to different types of proteins, such as those with varying lengths or structural complexities?
- Could you elaborate on the choice of the PiGNN model for refinement and discuss its impact on the method's performance?
- What are the specific advantages of the proposed method over existing methods, particularly in terms of computational efficiency and scalability?
- How does the method handle the issue of predictive confidence, and what are the implications of using different confidence thresholds?
- Could you provide a more detailed comparison with other baseline methods, particularly in terms of computational resources and training time?
- How does the method perform when applied to proteins with unknown structures, and what are the implications for its practical applicability?
