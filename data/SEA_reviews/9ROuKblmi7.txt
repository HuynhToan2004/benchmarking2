 **Summary:**
The paper introduces NECO, a novel post-hoc method for out-of-distribution (OOD) detection that leverages the geometric properties of "neural collapse" and principal component spaces. NECO aims to identify OOD data by analyzing the relative norm of a sample within the subspace occupied by the Simplex ETF structure, preserving information from ID data exclusively and normalized by the norm of the full feature vector. The method's effectiveness is validated through extensive experiments on various benchmarks, showcasing superior performance compared to existing methods. Theoretical analysis supports the method's efficacy in OOD detection, highlighting its generalization capabilities across different network architectures.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel property of neural collapse in the presence of out-of-distribution (OOD) data, which is a significant contribution to the field.
- The proposed method, NECO, is straightforward yet highly efficient, making it easy to implement and understand.
- NECO demonstrates superior performance compared to state-of-the-art methods across different benchmarks and architectures, including ResNet-18 on CIFAR10/CIFAR100 and Vision Transformer networks on ImageNet-1K.
- The method's effectiveness is validated through extensive experiments, and theoretical analysis provides insights into the underlying mechanisms of NECO.
- NECO exhibits strong generalization capabilities across different network architectures, enhancing its applicability and relevance in various scenarios.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a clear definition and detailed explanation of the "neural collapse" phenomenon, which is crucial for understanding the method's foundational principles.
- The empirical analysis in Section 4.1 is not well-defined, and the relationship between the NC5 property and the NECO method is not clearly established.
- The paper does not discuss the limitations of the proposed method, which could affect its applicability in certain scenarios.
- There is a lack of detailed comparative analysis with existing methods, particularly in terms of computational efficiency and practical implementation.
- The paper does not provide a detailed discussion on the limitations of the proposed method, which could affect its applicability in various scenarios.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors provide a clearer definition and detailed explanation of the "neural collapse" phenomenon to enhance the understanding of the method's foundational principles?
- How does the NC5 property relate to the NECO method, and can the authors provide a more detailed explanation of this relationship?
- Can the authors discuss the limitations of the proposed method and provide examples or scenarios where the method might not be effective?
- How does the proposed method compare in terms of computational efficiency and practical implementation with existing methods?
- Can the authors provide a more detailed discussion on the limitations of the proposed method and potential future research directions?
