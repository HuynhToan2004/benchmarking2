 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel method for generating Chinese ancient building images using a pre-trained text-to-image diffusion model. It leverages a dataset of over 1200 images and incorporates Pinyin text prompts to enhance the model's understanding of Chinese architectural elements. The approach involves fine-tuning the model with LoRA and training it with specific views and prompts to improve the generation quality. Despite the innovative use of Pinyin and the comprehensive dataset, the paper suffers from several issues such as unclear methodological descriptions, insufficient experimental validation, and a lack of comparison with existing methods.

**Strengths:**
- The paper addresses a significant gap in the literature by focusing on the generation of Chinese ancient buildings, a topic under-explored in existing research.
- The use of Pinyin text prompts is innovative and effective, providing a unique approach to enhancing the model's understanding of Chinese architectural elements.
- The dataset of over 1200 images is comprehensive and well-curated, providing a rich source of data for training and testing the model.
- The paper is well-written and easy to follow, making the complex methodology accessible to a broader audience.
- The results show promising improvements in generating Chinese ancient buildings, demonstrating the potential of the proposed method.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a clear and detailed description of the methodology, particularly in the use of LoRA and the specifics of the training process.
- There is a notable absence of comparative analysis with existing methods, which makes it difficult to assess the relative performance of the proposed approach.
- The experimental validation is insufficient, with a limited number of generated images and a lack of quantitative metrics to support the claims of improved performance.
- The paper suffers from several grammatical errors and unclear figures, which detract from its professional presentation.
- The paper does not adequately address the limitations of the proposed method, such as the potential for overfitting or the generalizability of the findings to other architectural styles or cultures.

**Questions:**
- Could you provide a more detailed description of the training process, including the specifics of the LoRA training and the parameters used?
- How does the model handle variations in architectural styles or periods beyond those represented in the dataset?
- Can you clarify the role and impact of the Pinyin text prompts in the model's performance?
- How do the generated images compare in terms of quality and fidelity to existing images of Chinese ancient buildings?
- Could you include more comparative analysis with existing methods to better establish the relative performance of your approach?
- How does the model perform when generating images of modern or non-Chinese architectural styles?
