 **Summary:**
The paper introduces LLMCarbon, a tool designed to estimate the carbon footprint of large language models (LLMs) across their entire life cycle, including training, inference, experimentation, and storage. It integrates existing models and equations to predict the carbon emissions based on various parameters such as model architecture, hardware specifications, and data center efficiency. The tool is validated against real-world data from companies like Google, Meta, and OpenAI, demonstrating its accuracy and utility. The paper discusses the importance of considering both operational and embodied carbon emissions in the development and deployment of LLMs. Despite its strengths, the tool's reliance on assumptions about hardware efficiency and its potential to overestimate carbon emissions in certain scenarios are noted as limitations.

**Strengths:**
- The paper addresses the critical issue of estimating the carbon footprint of LLMs, which is crucial for the development and deployment of sustainable AI systems.
- The proposed tool, LLMCarbon, is a valuable addition to the field, providing a comprehensive approach to estimating the carbon footprint of LLMs.
- The paper is well-written, clear, and easy to follow, making it accessible to a broad audience.
- The integration of existing models and equations is innovative and practical, leveraging existing knowledge to create a new tool.
- The paper is well-referenced and provides a detailed description of the models used, enhancing the reproducibility and transparency of the research.
- The tool's accuracy is validated against real-world data from companies like Google, Meta, and OpenAI, demonstrating its practical applicability.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper relies on assumptions about hardware efficiency, which may not hold true in all scenarios, potentially leading to overestimation of carbon emissions.
- The tool's reliance on accurate parameter counts for LLMs could be challenging, especially for newer, untested models.
- The paper does not discuss the potential limitations or societal impacts of the tool, which could be crucial for understanding its practical applications and implications.
- There is a lack of discussion on the novelty of the approach, as much of the work appears to be a combination of existing models and equations.
- The paper does not provide a detailed comparison of the carbon footprint of different hardware types, which could be crucial for understanding the tool's effectiveness across different hardware configurations.
- The tool's applicability is limited to specific hardware types (V100 GPUs), which may not be representative of all possible hardware configurations used in LLM training.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors provide a detailed discussion on the potential limitations of the tool, particularly in scenarios where hardware efficiency is not optimal?
- How does the tool handle the estimation of parameter counts for untested LLMs?
- Could the authors discuss the societal implications of the tool, particularly in terms of its potential impact on the development and deployment of sustainable AI systems?
- How does the tool compare to other existing tools in terms of accuracy and practical applicability?
- Can the authors provide a more detailed comparison of the carbon footprint of different hardware types, including those beyond V100 GPUs?
- How does the tool handle the estimation of carbon emissions during the inference phase, which is critical for understanding the full carbon footprint of LLMs?
- Can the authors discuss the potential for the tool to be extended to include other types of hardware, such as TPUs and CPUs, which are also used in LLM training?
