 **Summary:**
The paper explores the computational complexity and robust memorization capabilities of neural networks. It demonstrates that robust memorization, which ensures a network memorizes training data points within a certain distance, is NP-hard for networks with small depth and width. The paper further constructs networks that can robustly memorize data with polynomial time complexity, providing necessary conditions for robust memorization and showing that depth 2 networks can be optimal for certain robust memorization scenarios. Additionally, it discusses the impact of network width and depth on robust memorization, and how controlling the Lipschitz constant can enhance robustness. The paper also includes proofs of theorems and propositions, which are supported by a detailed analysis and comparison with existing results.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces novel and significant results on the computational complexity of robust memorization, which is crucial for understanding the theoretical foundations of neural networks.
- The authors provide explicit constructions of robust memorization networks, which are practical and useful for real-world applications.
- The paper presents a comprehensive analysis of the computational complexity and necessary conditions for robust memorization, which are original contributions to the field.
- The proofs and theoretical results are rigorous and well-supported, enhancing the credibility of the claims made in the paper.
- The paper addresses an important and timely problem in the field of machine learning, specifically focusing on the robustness of neural networks, which is a significant concern in practical applications.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks clear motivation and explanation for the necessity and significance of robust memorization, which could be improved by including more real-world examples and applications.
- Some of the results and proofs are not clearly explained or require further clarification, which could hinder the understanding of the paper by less expert readers.
- The paper could benefit from a more detailed comparison with existing results and a more thorough discussion of the limitations and future work.
- The presentation of the paper could be improved to make it more accessible to a broader audience, including non-experts in the field.
- The paper could provide more practical insights and applications to make the theoretical results more relevant and applicable to real-world scenarios.

**Questions:**

- Could the authors discuss the potential applications and implications of their results in more detail, particularly in relation to practical scenarios where robust memorization might be beneficial?
