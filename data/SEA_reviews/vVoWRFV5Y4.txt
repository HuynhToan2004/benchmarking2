 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel approach to solving the Quadratic Assignment Problem (QAP) using a deep reinforcement learning (RL) method, specifically a double pointer network that alternates between selecting a location and a facility. The method was tested on synthetic data, showing results comparable to a local search heuristic and outperforming it in some cases. The paper's main contributions include the reformulation of the QAP as a sequential decision problem and the use of a double pointer network for training with A2C. Despite its innovative approach, the paper faces criticism for its limited empirical evaluation, lack of theoretical analysis, and unclear presentation.

**Strengths:**
- The paper addresses an important and difficult problem, the Quadratic Assignment Problem (QAP), which has significant practical applications.
- The approach of reformulating the QAP as a sequential decision problem and using a double pointer network is novel and could be of interest to the community.
- The paper is well-written and easy to follow, making it accessible to readers.
- The use of a double pointer network to solve the QAP is a significant contribution to the field.
- The results, while limited to synthetic data, are encouraging and show that the proposed method can perform well in certain scenarios.

**Weaknesses:**
- The empirical evaluation is limited, focusing on only 1000 test instances and not comparing the results to more established methods like Gurobi.
- The paper lacks theoretical analysis, which is crucial for understanding the performance and limitations of the proposed method.
- The presentation of the paper is not clear, with figures and tables not contributing much to the understanding of the results.
- The methodology is not clearly explained, particularly the interaction between the attention layer and the pointer network, and the role of the attention layer in the final output.
- The paper's contribution is questioned due to the limited scope of the experiments and the lack of comparison with more sophisticated baselines.
- There are several typographical and grammatical errors that need correction.

**Questions:**
- How does the proposed method compare to other state-of-the-art methods for solving the QAP, such as those mentioned in the literature review?
- Can you provide more details on the training procedure for the double pointer network, including the number of training instances and the parameters used?
- How does the method handle the symmetry in the QAP, and what are the implications of this symmetry for the model's performance?
- Could you clarify the role of the attention layer in the final output of the model, and how it interacts with the pointer network?
- What is the runtime of the swap heuristic compared to the RL model, and how does this affect the practical applicability of the proposed method?
- How do the authors address the challenges of training the double pointer network, particularly in terms of ensuring the model's ability to handle the complexity of the QAP?
