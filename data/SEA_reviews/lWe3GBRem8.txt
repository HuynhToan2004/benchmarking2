 **Summary:**
The paper introduces the Offline-to-Online-to-Offline (OOO) framework for reinforcement learning (RL), which separates the exploration and exploitation policies to mitigate exploration bias. This framework is designed to optimize the performance of RL by training an exploration policy on offline data and an exploitation policy on the combined offline and online data. The authors argue that this approach allows for more aggressive exploration and improved policy evaluation, especially in sparse reward tasks. The framework is evaluated on several benchmarks, showing significant improvements over existing methods. However, concerns are raised about the novelty and clarity of the method's contributions, as well as the empirical validation of the framework's effectiveness.

**Strengths:**
- The paper addresses the problem of exploration bias in reinforcement learning (RL) by proposing a novel framework that separates the exploration and exploitation policies, which is a significant contribution to the field.
- The experimental results are strong, demonstrating the effectiveness of the proposed method across various environments and tasks.
- The paper is well-written, making it easy to follow and understand the proposed methodology.
- The OOO framework is versatile, allowing for the integration of different exploration and exploitation methods, which enhances its applicability in various RL scenarios.
- The authors provide a clear motivation for their research, focusing on the importance of exploration in RL, and the potential for the OOO framework to address this challenge.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a clear definition and explanation of the OOO framework, making it difficult for readers to fully grasp the methodology.
- The novelty of the OOO framework is questionable, as it appears to be a simple combination of existing methods without significant new contributions.
- The paper's empirical validation is insufficient, with the experiments conducted primarily on small-scale environments, which may not adequately demonstrate the framework's effectiveness in more complex or realistic scenarios.
- The paper does not provide a comprehensive analysis of the computational cost and hyperparameter sensitivity of the proposed method, which are crucial for understanding its practical applicability and scalability.
- There is a lack of detailed discussion on the limitations of the OOO framework, which could help in understanding its scope and applicability more clearly.
- The paper does not sufficiently compare the OOO framework with other existing methods, such as those that also address the exploration-exploitation tradeoff in RL.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors clarify the definition and operation of the OOO framework, particularly how it separates the exploration and exploitation policies?
- How does the OOO framework compare with other methods that also address the exploration-exploitation tradeoff in RL?
- What are the specific advantages of the OOO framework over these other methods, and why should a researcher choose to use the OOO framework over others?
- How does the OOO framework handle the computational cost and hyperparameter sensitivity, especially in large-scale or complex environments?
- Can the authors provide more details on the limitations of the OOO framework, and how these limitations affect its practical applicability and scalability?
- How does the OOO framework handle the potential for data misuse, especially in scenarios where the offline dataset might contain biased or inaccurate data?
- Can the authors provide a more detailed analysis of the empirical results, particularly the improvements observed in the experiments, and how these improvements compare with existing methods?
