 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel method, IPDiff, for generating 3D molecules that bind to specific protein targets using diffusion models. IPDiff incorporates protein-ligand interaction information into both the forward and reverse processes, which is a departure from traditional methods that primarily focus on the reverse process. The model is evaluated using the CrossDocked2020 dataset and reportedly achieves state-of-the-art performance in terms of binding affinity and molecular properties. However, the paper has been critiqued for its clarity in explaining the model's components, such as the prior-shifting and prior-conditioning mechanisms, and the lack of detailed experimental validation. Additionally, the paper's reliance on pre-trained models and its generalizability across different datasets are noted as potential concerns.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel method for generating 3D molecules that bind to specific protein targets using diffusion models, which is a significant advancement in the field of structure-based drug design.
- The authors provide a comprehensive evaluation of the proposed method, demonstrating its effectiveness through a series of experiments and comparisons with existing methods.
- The paper is well-written, making it easy to follow and understand, and the figures and tables are clear and informative.
- The proposed method achieves state-of-the-art performance on the CrossDocked2020 dataset, demonstrating its effectiveness in generating molecules with high binding affinity and realistic 3D structures.
- The paper is the first to consider pocket-ligand interaction in both the forward and reverse processes, which is a novel approach in the field.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks clarity in explaining certain key components of the method, such as the prior-shifting and prior-conditioning mechanisms, which are critical for understanding the proposed methodology.
- The paper does not provide sufficient details on how the protein-ligand interaction prior is learned, which is a crucial aspect of the proposed method.
- There is a lack of detailed experimental validation, particularly in terms of the generalizability of the method across different datasets and the impact of different hyperparameters on the model's performance.
- The paper does not adequately address the potential negative societal impacts of the proposed method.
- The paper could benefit from a more thorough discussion of the limitations of the proposed method and the potential challenges in applying it to real-world drug discovery scenarios.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors provide more detailed explanations of the prior-shifting and prior-conditioning mechanisms to clarify how they work and their impact on the model's performance?
- How is the protein-ligand interaction prior learned, and what role does it play in the overall performance of the model?
- Could the authors provide more details on the experimental setup, such as the specific hyperparameters used and the impact of different hyperparameters on the model's performance?
- How does the model perform when applied to different datasets, and what are the potential challenges in applying the model to real-world drug discovery scenarios?
- Could the authors discuss the potential limitations of the proposed method and how these might affect its practical applicability in drug discovery?
