 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel method for link prediction in graphs using a learnable Lanczos algorithm with linear constraints, specifically focusing on the encoding of subgraphs as linear constraints. This approach, which utilizes Neumann eigenvalues, is shown to be effective in distinguishing between k-regular graphs and improving link prediction performance. The method is supported by theoretical analysis and empirical results on benchmark datasets, demonstrating its potential to enhance the expressiveness of Graph Neural Networks (GNNs) in node-level tasks. Despite its theoretical contributions, the paper has been critiqued for its limited empirical evaluation on a single dataset and the lack of comparison with other advanced baselines.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel approach using Neumann eigenvalues to encode subgraphs as linear constraints, which is a significant advancement in the field of link prediction.
- Theoretical analysis and empirical results demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed method, particularly in improving link prediction performance.
- The paper is well-written, clear, and easy to follow, making it accessible to a broad audience.
- The proposed method shows promise in enhancing the expressiveness of Graph Neural Networks (GNNs) in node-level tasks, addressing the limitations of Message Passing Neural Networks (MPNNs).
- The introduction of a learnable Lanczos layer with linear constraints (LLwLC) as a universal approximator is a significant theoretical contribution.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed comparison with other advanced baselines, particularly in terms of expressiveness and computational efficiency.
- The empirical evaluation is limited to a single dataset, which may not adequately demonstrate the generalizability of the proposed method.
- The paper could benefit from a more comprehensive discussion on the limitations of the proposed method and potential directions for future research.
- There are minor issues with the clarity and organization of the paper, such as the lack of detailed explanations for certain mathematical notations and the structure of the experimental results.
- The paper does not sufficiently address the scalability and computational complexity of the proposed method, especially in larger and more complex graph settings.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors provide more details on the computational complexity and scalability of the proposed method, particularly in larger and more complex graph settings?
- How does the proposed method compare in terms of expressiveness and computational efficiency with other advanced baselines, such as those mentioned in related works?
- Could the authors elaborate on the choice of the subgraph extraction policy and its impact on the performance of the model?
- In the context of the Neumann eigenvalue constraints, how do these constraints specifically contribute to the improved performance in link prediction tasks?
- How does the proposed method handle the issue of distinguishing between different graphs, especially in scenarios where the graph structure is not regular?
- Can the authors provide a more detailed explanation of the mathematical notations and theoretical concepts used in the paper, particularly those introduced in the preliminaries section?
