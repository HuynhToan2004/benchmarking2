 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel backdoor attack using visible, semantic, sample-specific, and compatible (VSSC) triggers to enhance the robustness of attacks against visual distortions. The authors leverage a large language model and text-guided image editing techniques to generate poisoned images with the trigger. The methodology is designed to be flexible and efficient, with the ability to adapt to generated images and the real world. The paper claims that VSSC triggers are effective, stealthy, and robust to visual distortions, as demonstrated through extensive experiments on natural image classification tasks. However, the paper's contribution is questioned due to the reliance on existing techniques and the lack of significant novelty in the methodology.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel approach to backdoor attacks using visible, semantic, sample-specific, and compatible (VSSC) triggers, which is a significant advancement in the field.
- The authors demonstrate the effectiveness of VSSC triggers through extensive experiments, showing that these triggers can be effectively used to generate poisoned images with the trigger.
- The methodology is flexible and efficient, allowing for the adaptation of the attack to generated images and the real world, which is a notable strength.
- The paper is well-written, making it easy to follow and understand, and the experiments are comprehensive, including evaluations of attack effectiveness, resistance to defenses, and robustness to visual distortions.
- The proposed method is innovative and shows promise in enhancing the robustness of backdoor attacks against visual distortions.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed discussion on the limitations of the proposed method, which could help in understanding its applicability and effectiveness in different scenarios.
- The methodology primarily relies on existing techniques, such as large language models and text-guided image editing, which may not be considered novel.
- The paper does not sufficiently address the practicality of the method in real-world applications, particularly in terms of the complexity and cost of generating VSSC triggers.
- The evaluation of the method's effectiveness is limited to specific datasets and models, which may not generalize well to other datasets or models.
- The paper does not sufficiently compare the proposed method with existing methods, particularly in terms of the robustness of the attacks against visual distortions.
- The paper lacks a thorough discussion on the limitations of the proposed method, which could help in understanding its applicability and effectiveness in different scenarios.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors clarify the process of generating VSSC triggers, particularly how the quality assessment module ensures the effectiveness of the generated triggers?
- How does the method perform in scenarios where the training and testing data are from different distributions, and how does it handle variations in image quality?
- Can the authors provide more details on the method's generalizability to other datasets and models, and discuss potential limitations in practical applications?
- Could the authors elaborate on the method's resistance to defenses and how it performs under different defense strategies?
- How does the method compare to other existing methods in terms of attack effectiveness, robustness to visual distortions, and other relevant metrics?
