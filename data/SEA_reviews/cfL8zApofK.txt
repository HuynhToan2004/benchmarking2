 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel evaluation framework using negotiation games to assess the reasoning and decision-making capabilities of Large Language Models (LLMs) in complex, multi-agent, multi-issue scenarios. It proposes a benchmark of diverse, text-based games designed to test LLMs' arithmetic, inference, exploration, and planning capabilities, and evaluates these models using GPT-4 and GPT-3.5. The framework includes a variety of game scenarios, such as "All in" and "One out," which simulate cooperative and adversarial settings, respectively. The paper provides a detailed analysis of LLMs' performance in these games, highlighting their strengths and limitations. Despite the comprehensive nature of the study, concerns were raised about the novelty and the clarity of the contribution, with some reviewers questioning the originality of the approach and the depth of the analysis.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel evaluation framework using negotiation games to assess the reasoning and decision-making capabilities of LLMs, which is a significant contribution to the field.
- The benchmark of diverse, text-based, multi-agent, multi-issue negotiation games is designed to test LLMs' arithmetic, inference, exploration, and planning capabilities, providing a comprehensive evaluation of LLMs.
- The paper is well-written, clear, and easy to follow, making it accessible to a broad audience.
- The framework allows for the evaluation of LLMs' negotiation capabilities and potential for manipulation, which is crucial for understanding the limitations and potential misuse of LLMs in real-world applications.
- The paper provides a detailed analysis of LLMs' performance in the negotiation games, including a thorough ablation study that reveals which skills are needed/lacking to reach success and which models lack these skills.
- The authors plan to make their toolkit of games and code publicly available, which will benefit the research community by facilitating further research and development in this area.

**Weaknesses:**
- The novelty of the approach is limited as similar frameworks have been previously proposed, such as using text-based games as a practical testbed.
- The contribution of the paper is not clearly defined, and the paper lacks a clear demonstration of how the proposed framework improves upon or differs significantly from existing methods.
- The paper does not sufficiently compare the proposed method with other relevant works, which limits the understanding of its advantages and disadvantages.
- The complexity of the games and the number of agents involved (only 6) might not adequately challenge LLMs' negotiation capabilities.
- The analysis of LLMs' performance in the negotiation games is not sufficiently detailed, particularly in terms of how the games' complexity and the number of agents affect LLMs' performance.
- The paper does not adequately address the issue of LLMs potentially using their superior reasoning and decision-making capabilities to manipulate or exploit the negotiation process, which is a significant concern in real-world applications.
- The paper does not provide a clear definition of the problem setting and the evaluation metric used, which could limit the understanding and applicability of the results.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors provide a more detailed comparison with other relevant works to better understand the advantages and disadvantages of their proposed method?
- How does the proposed framework address the issue of LLMs potentially using their superior reasoning and decision-making capabilities to manipulate or exploit the negotiation process?
- What are the key differences between the proposed method and other existing methods, and how does it improve upon or differ significantly from them?
- How do the authors ensure the fairness and reliability of the LLMs' performance in the negotiation games, especially when dealing with adversarial or manipulative behavior?
- Could the authors clarify the definitions and roles of the agents and issues in the negotiation games to enhance the understanding of the problem setting?
- How do the authors ensure that the evaluation metric used in the paper is appropriate and accurately reflects the performance of the LLMs in the negotiation games?
