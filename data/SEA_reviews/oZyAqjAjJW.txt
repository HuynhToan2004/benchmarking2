 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel regularization method, Local Dimensionality Regularization (LDReg), aimed at mitigating dimensional collapse in self-supervised learning (SSL) by optimizing local distance distributions. This method is based on the derivation of the Fisher-Rao metric to enhance local intrinsic dimensionality, which is crucial for improving representation quality. The authors demonstrate that LDReg can effectively regularize dimensionality at both local and global levels, showing its effectiveness through extensive experiments across various SSL methods and datasets. The approach is theoretically grounded and provides a new perspective on regularizing local intrinsic dimensionality, which is distinct from traditional global dimensionality approaches.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, clear, and easy to follow, with a clear introduction of the LDReg method and its application to SSL.
- The authors have provided extensive experimental results demonstrating the effectiveness of the LDReg method across various SSL methods and datasets.
- Theoretical analysis and mathematical proofs are presented to support the proposed method, enhancing the credibility of the work.
- The paper introduces a new perspective on regularizing local intrinsic dimensionality, which is distinct from traditional global dimensionality approaches.
- The proposed method is simple to implement and has the potential to be applied to various learning tasks.
- The paper presents a novel approach to measuring local intrinsic dimensionality and introduces a new regularization method that could potentially be used in other types of learning tasks.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a clear discussion on the limitations of the proposed method and does not provide a detailed discussion on the computational complexity of the method.
- The experiments are primarily conducted on ResNet-50 and ViT-B models, which may not adequately represent the performance of the method across a broader range of models.
- There is a lack of discussion on how the proposed method can be applied to other types of learning tasks, such as supervised learning or unsupervised learning.
- The paper does not provide a detailed comparison of the proposed method with existing methods, which could help in understanding the advantages and disadvantages of the proposed method.
- The paper does not discuss the potential negative societal impacts of the proposed method.
