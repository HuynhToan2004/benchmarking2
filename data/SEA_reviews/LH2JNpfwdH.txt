 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel framework for 4D video stylization that integrates style transfer, novel view synthesis, and human animation within a unified framework. It leverages a geometry-guided tri-plane representation to enhance feature representation robustness and achieves a balance between stylized textures and temporal coherence. The methodology involves a dual representation of both the human subject and the surrounding scene using two NeRFs, which facilitates animation of human subjects across various poses and novel viewpoints. The paper's experiments demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed method, showcasing superior performance compared to existing approaches. However, the novelty of the approach is questioned, as it builds upon existing methods like StyleRF and employs similar techniques such as U-Net for feature encoding and linear blend skinning for pose transformation.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, making it easy to follow, and the methodology is clearly explained.
- The proposed method achieves a good balance between stylized textures and temporal coherence, which is crucial for video stylization.
- The methodology is adaptable to novel poses and various backgrounds, enhancing the versatility of the stylization process.
- The paper demonstrates superior performance compared to existing approaches, as evidenced by extensive experiments and ablation studies.
- The use of a tri-plane-based representation and a geometry-guided tri-plane representation significantly improves feature representation robustness and reduces computational costs.
- The methodology is original in its approach to 4D video stylization, which is a significant advancement in the field.

**Weaknesses:**
- The novelty of the approach is limited as it builds upon existing methods like StyleRF and employs similar techniques such as U-Net for feature encoding and linear blend skinning for pose transformation.
- The methodology is complex, with multiple components that are not well-explained, making it difficult for readers to fully understand the methodology.
- The paper lacks detailed explanations for the design of the geometry-guided tri-plane representation and the optimization of the tri-plane feature.
- The paper does not provide sufficient details on the optimization process for the tri-plane feature, which is crucial for understanding the method's effectiveness.
- The paper's experiments do not include a comparison with state-of-the-art methods, which is necessary to validate the proposed method's effectiveness.
- The paper does not adequately address the limitations of the proposed method, such as the dependency on the quality of input videos and the complexity of the methodology.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors clarify the novelty of their approach compared to existing methods like StyleRF?
- How does the proposed method handle the complexity of the methodology, especially in terms of optimization and feature extraction?
- Could the authors provide more details on the optimization process for the tri-plane feature and the design of the geometry-guided tri-plane representation?
- How does the proposed method handle the limitations, such as the dependency on the quality of input videos and the complexity of the methodology?
- Can the authors provide a more detailed comparison with state-of-the-art methods to validate the effectiveness of their proposed method?
