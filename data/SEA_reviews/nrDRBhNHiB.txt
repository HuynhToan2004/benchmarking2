 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel method for computing the regularization path of deep neural networks using multi-objective optimization, specifically focusing on the trade-off between empirical loss and sparsity. The approach employs a continuation method, which iteratively adjusts weights to traverse the Pareto frontier, aiming to balance sparsity and loss. The method is evaluated on various datasets, including Iris, MNIST, and CIFAR-10, demonstrating its effectiveness in identifying optimal trade-offs. Despite its novelty and potential, the paper is criticized for its limited scope in terms of the number of objectives and datasets used, as well as the clarity and depth of the theoretical justification.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel method for computing the regularization path of deep neural networks using a continuation method, which is the first of its kind to handle non-convex problems with millions of degrees of freedom.
- The method is simple yet effective, with a clear presentation and detailed numerical experiments that demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed approach.
- The paper provides a new perspective on sparsity in deep neural networks, which is an important area of research.
- The authors have provided a detailed description of the method and its implementation, which is crucial for reproducibility.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a thorough theoretical justification for the proposed method, which is a significant drawback given the complexity of the problem addressed.
- The scope of the paper is limited in terms of the number of objectives and datasets used, which may not adequately demonstrate the generalizability of the findings.
- The paper does not adequately address the computational complexity of the method, which is a critical factor in practical applications.
- The presentation of the paper could be improved to make it more accessible to a broader audience, particularly in terms of defining key terms and providing more detailed explanations of the methodology.
- There are concerns about the novelty of the approach, as similar methods have been previously explored in the literature, and the paper does not sufficiently distinguish its contributions from existing works.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors provide more detailed theoretical justification for the proposed method, particularly in terms of its convergence and computational complexity?
- How does the proposed method perform when applied to more complex datasets or with more objectives?
- Can the authors clarify the computational complexity of the method and discuss potential strategies for reducing computational overhead?
- In the context of the experimental results, could the authors provide more detailed information on the training data used and the hyperparameters chosen for each dataset?
- How does the proposed method compare to other existing methods for computing the regularization path of deep neural networks, particularly in terms of efficiency and effectiveness?
- Could the authors elaborate on the choice of datasets and the rationale behind the specific experimental setups used in the paper?
