 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel bias-variance-covariance decomposition for kernel scores, which is applied to evaluate uncertainty in generative models across various tasks such as image, audio, and language generation. The authors propose unbiased and consistent estimators for variance and covariance, leveraging kernel entropy as a measure of uncertainty. Theoretical contributions are supported by empirical evaluations demonstrating the effectiveness of the proposed methods. The paper aims to provide a comprehensive understanding of model generalization and uncertainty quantification, which is crucial for assessing the performance of generative models in different scenarios.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel bias-variance-covariance decomposition for kernel scores, which is a significant theoretical contribution.
- The authors propose unbiased and consistent estimators for variance and covariance, which are practical and applicable to various generative models.
- The empirical evaluation demonstrates the effectiveness of the proposed methods, showing that kernel entropy is a robust measure of uncertainty across different tasks and model sizes.
- The paper is well-written, making it accessible and easy to follow, with clear explanations of the proposed methods and their applications.
- The theoretical analysis is solid, and the paper provides a clear and comprehensive explanation of the proposed methods and their applications.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed comparison with existing methods for uncertainty quantification in generative models, which could have strengthened the argument for the novelty and effectiveness of the proposed approach.
- The computational complexity of the proposed estimators is not discussed, which could limit their practical applicability in large-scale or real-time scenarios.
- The paper could benefit from a more thorough discussion on the limitations of the proposed methods and the conditions under which they might not be effective.
- The empirical evaluation is somewhat limited in scope, focusing primarily on a few datasets and models, which might not fully demonstrate the generalizability of the proposed methods.
- The paper does not sufficiently address the societal impacts of the proposed methods, which could be a significant concern for the application of the proposed methods in real-world scenarios.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors provide a more detailed comparison with existing methods for uncertainty quantification in generative models, discussing the advantages and disadvantages of the proposed approach?
- How do the authors plan to address the computational complexity of the proposed estimators, particularly in large-scale or real-time applications?
- What are the potential limitations of the proposed methods, and under what conditions might they not be effective?
- Can the authors provide more examples or case studies demonstrating the practical application of the proposed methods in real-world scenarios?
- How do the authors address the societal impacts of the proposed methods, particularly in terms of fairness and bias in the generated content?
