 **Summary:**
The paper introduces AgentVerse, a novel multi-agent framework designed to enhance collaboration among expert agents, inspired by human group dynamics. This framework facilitates expert recruitment, collaborative decision-making, action execution, and evaluation stages. It has been tested across various domains including text understanding, reasoning, coding, tool utilization, and embodied AI. The experiments show that AgentVerse outperforms single agents, highlighting the emergence of specific collaborative behaviors. The framework is noted for its ability to orchestrate a collaborative group of agents, effectively managing complex tasks and demonstrating emergent behaviors.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces AgentVerse, a novel multi-agent framework that effectively orchestrates a collaborative group of expert agents, enhancing task accomplishment.
- Extensive experiments across various domains, including text understanding, reasoning, coding, tool utilization, and embodied AI, demonstrate the effectiveness of AgentVerse.
- The paper provides a detailed analysis of agent interactions within AgentVerse, revealing the emergence of specific collaborative behaviors, contributing to heightened group efficiency.
- The authors plan to release the codebase, AgentVerse, to further facilitate multi-agent research.
- The paper is well-organized, making it easy to follow, and includes a thorough evaluation of the framework.
- The experiments are well-designed and demonstrate that the proposed framework is effective in enhancing the performance of LLM agents.
- The paper is well-written and easy to understand, with clear descriptions of the framework and its components.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed explanation of how the agents are recruited and how the evaluation stage is implemented, which could be crucial for understanding the framework's effectiveness.
- The paper does not provide sufficient details on the implementation of the feedback mechanism in the evaluation stage, which is crucial for the framework's performance.
- There is a lack of discussion on the limitations of the proposed framework and potential negative societal impacts.
- The paper could benefit from a more detailed comparison with existing multi-agent systems and a discussion on the framework's novelty and contributions compared to prior works.
- The paper does not include a discussion on the limitations of the proposed framework and potential negative societal impacts.
- The experiments are limited to GPT-3.5-Turbo and GPT-4, and it would be beneficial to see how the framework performs with other LLMs.
