 **Summary:**
The paper investigates the robustness of distributed deep neural networks (DNNs) against adversarial attacks by focusing on the latent representations, which are theoretically and experimentally shown to be more robust than input representations. The authors employ information theory to quantify the robustness of DNNs and propose new metrics for measuring distortion and robustness. They conduct extensive experiments using various DNN architectures and adversarial attacks to validate their theoretical findings. Despite the novelty of the approach and the theoretical insights, the paper has been critiqued for its lack of novelty in theoretical contributions, the clarity of its presentation, and the depth of its experimental validation.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, with a clear structure and a logical flow of ideas.
- The topic of adversarial attacks on distributed DNNs is timely and relevant, addressing the growing need for secure and efficient neural networks.
- The theoretical analysis and experimental results are comprehensive, covering a wide range of DNN architectures, distributed DNN approaches, and adversarial attacks.
- The paper provides a novel perspective on the robustness of DNNs by introducing new metrics based on information theory, which could inspire further research in this area.
- The authors have made their code publicly available, enhancing the reproducibility and transparency of their research.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks significant novelty in its theoretical contributions, as many of the ideas and results are already known or have been previously discussed in other works.
- The experimental validation is insufficient, with a lack of detailed results and comparisons, particularly in terms of the effectiveness of different DNN architectures and compression methods against various adversarial attacks.
- The paper's presentation could be improved, particularly in terms of clarity and organization, especially in the experimental sections where the results are presented.
- The paper's scope is somewhat limited, focusing mainly on the robustness of DNNs against adversarial attacks in the latent space, which might not be generalizable to other types of attacks or DNN architectures.
- There are some inaccuracies and inconsistencies in the paper, such as the description of the attack in the latent space and the comparison of different DNN architectures.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors clarify the differences between the latent space attack described in the paper and traditional adversarial attacks in the input space?
- How do the authors justify the choice of metrics used to measure robustness, and could they provide a more detailed explanation of the theoretical contributions beyond those already discussed in the literature?
- In terms of the experimental results, could the authors provide more detailed results, such as the success rates of different attacks across different DNN architectures and compression methods?
- Could the authors address the inconsistencies and inaccuracies noted in the paper, particularly in the descriptions of the attack in the latent space and the comparisons between different DNN architectures?
- How do the authors plan to address the concerns regarding the novelty and depth of their theoretical contributions, and could they expand on the practical implications of their findings for real-world applications?
