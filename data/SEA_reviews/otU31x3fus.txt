 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel accelerated second-order method for solving stochastic convex optimization problems, focusing on the inexactness of gradients and Hessians. It extends the Cubic Regularized Newton method to a stochastic setting and provides theoretical lower bounds, demonstrating the method's robustness to gradient and Hessian inexactness. The paper also proposes a tensor generalization for stochastic higher-order derivatives, matching the global convergence of the Nesterov Accelerated Tensor method when oracles are non-stochastic. Theoretical analysis and experimental results are presented, showing the method's effectiveness and efficiency in solving both deterministic and stochastic optimization problems.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel accelerated second-order method that is robust to both gradient and Hessian inexactness, which is crucial for machine learning applications.
- The theoretical lower bounds established by the authors are significant, providing a solid foundation for the proposed method's effectiveness.
- The method is well-motivated and the theoretical analysis is thorough, demonstrating the method's robustness to inexact gradients and Hessians.
- The paper includes a comprehensive literature review, highlighting the significance of the problem addressed.
- The proposed method achieves optimal convergence in both gradient and Hessian inexactness, which is a notable contribution to the field.
- The paper is well-written, making it accessible and easy to follow, with clear explanations of the proposed method and its theoretical underpinnings.

**Weaknesses:**

- There is a lack of discussion on the practical implementation of the method, particularly concerning the computational complexity and the selection of parameters.
