 **Summary:**
The paper investigates the selection of optimal kernels for score-based methods in causal discovery, particularly focusing on the RKHS framework. It introduces a method that automatically selects the best-fitting kernel by maximizing the marginal likelihood of the variables involved in each search step. The approach is validated through experiments on both synthetic and real-world datasets, showing improvements over heuristic kernel selection methods. However, the paper is critiqued for its lack of clarity in certain sections, insufficient comparisons with other methods, and potential overfitting issues due to the use of marginal likelihood as a score function.

**Strengths:**
- The paper addresses a significant problem in the field of causal discovery by focusing on the selection of optimal kernels, which is crucial for accurately characterizing causal relationships.
- The method proposed is novel, with a focus on selecting the optimal kernel that best fits the data, which is a significant advancement over traditional heuristic methods.
- The paper is well-organized, making it easy to follow, and the experimental results are compelling, demonstrating the effectiveness of the proposed method.
- The use of marginal likelihood as a score function for kernel selection is innovative and could potentially improve the accuracy of causal discovery.
- The paper provides a detailed explanation of the proposed method, including a clear motivation for the problem and a step-by-step explanation of the methodology.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks clarity in certain sections, particularly in Section 3.3, where the derivation of the score function is not clearly explained, making it difficult for readers to understand the method's theoretical underpinnings.
- The paper does not sufficiently compare its method with other existing methods, such as those mentioned in the review, which could provide a more robust validation of the proposed method.
- The paper does not address the issue of overfitting due to the use of marginal likelihood as a score function, which could potentially lead to inaccurate causal discovery.
- The experimental setup is limited, and the paper does not include a discussion on the scalability of the proposed method, which is crucial for practical applications.
- The paper does not sufficiently discuss the limitations of the proposed method, which could help in understanding its applicability and potential drawbacks.
