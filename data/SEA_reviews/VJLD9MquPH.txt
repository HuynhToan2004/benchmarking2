 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel quantization-based approach for stochastic gradient descent (SGD) and stochastic gradient Langevin dynamics (SGLD), aiming to enhance optimization performance by controlling the variance of noise and improving convergence properties. The methodology involves quantizing the optimization variables and the search direction, thereby treating the quantization error as additive white noise, which is crucial for theoretical analysis and practical implementation. The paper presents empirical results that demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed methods, showing improvements in convergence speed and error reduction compared to conventional SGD and ADAM-based methods. However, the theoretical analysis is limited to weak convergence, and the paper lacks a detailed comparison with existing methods and a comprehensive discussion on the practical implications and limitations of the proposed approach.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written and provides a clear, detailed explanation of the proposed quantization-based method, making it accessible to readers.
- The experimental results demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed method, showing improvements in convergence speed and error reduction compared to conventional SGD and ADAM-based methods.
- The theoretical analysis of the convergence properties of the proposed method is solid, with proofs provided in the supplementary material.
- The paper introduces a novel approach to controlling the variance of noise in SGD and SGLD by quantizing the optimization variables, which is a significant contribution to the field.
- The method's ability to treat quantization error as additive white noise is a novel and interesting idea that enhances the theoretical analysis and practical implementation of the algorithm.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed comparison with existing methods, particularly in terms of convergence properties and practical performance.
- The theoretical analysis is limited to weak convergence, which may not fully capture the practical implications and limitations of the proposed approach.
- The paper does not discuss the practical implications of the proposed method, such as computational cost, memory usage, and scalability, which are crucial for real-world applications.
- The proofs and theoretical results are not self-contained, with some results relying on external sources without adequate citation or explanation.
- The paper does not provide a detailed discussion on the limitations of the proposed method, which could help in understanding its applicability and scope.
- The experimental results are not comprehensive enough, particularly in terms of the number of experiments conducted and the variety of datasets used.

**Questions:**
- Could you provide more details on how the quantization error is treated as additive white noise in the theoretical analysis?
- How does the proposed method compare in terms of computational cost, memory usage, and scalability with other existing methods?
- Can you discuss the practical implications of the proposed method, such as its applicability to different types of neural networks and datasets?
- What are the limitations of the proposed method, and how do these limitations affect its performance and applicability?
- How does the proposed method perform in scenarios where the data distribution is non-IID, and what are the implications of this for the quantization process?
- Can you provide a more detailed discussion on the choice of hyperparameters and their impact on the performance of the algorithm?
- How does the proposed method compare with other quantization-based methods for deep neural networks, and what are the advantages of your approach over these methods?
