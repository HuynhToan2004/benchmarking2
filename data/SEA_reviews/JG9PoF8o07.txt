 **Summary:**
The paper explores the Generalized Gaussian Mechanism (GG) for Differential Privacy (DP), focusing on its applications in PATE and DP-SGD. The authors extend the existing privacy accountant to incorporate the GG mechanism, enabling the calculation of privacy loss under different conditions. They demonstrate that the choice of β, a parameter in the GG mechanism, has minimal impact on test accuracy, suggesting that Gaussian noise remains the preferred choice for many applications. The paper also investigates the use of GG mechanisms in PATE and DP-SGD, showing that the GG mechanism can be applied to these settings but may not significantly outperform existing methods.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, clear, and easy to follow, making it accessible to a broad audience.
- The introduction of the Generalized Gaussian Mechanism (GG) and its application to PATE and DP-SGD is innovative and provides a new perspective on differential privacy.
- The paper provides a comprehensive numerical analysis of the GG mechanism's impact on test accuracy, which is a significant contribution to the field.
- The authors demonstrate that the choice of β in the GG mechanism has minimal impact on test accuracy, which is an interesting and valuable finding.
- The paper's theoretical contributions, such as the proof of the GG mechanism's differential privacy and the application of the PRV accountant to the GG mechanism, are solid and contribute to the understanding of differential privacy.
- The paper is well-organized, with clear sections and a logical flow that facilitates understanding of the complex concepts presented.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper's novelty is somewhat limited, as the GG mechanism is a generalization of existing mechanisms like the Gaussian and Laplace mechanisms.
- The paper's experimental results do not convincingly demonstrate the superiority of the GG mechanism over existing methods, such as the Gaussian mechanism.
- The paper lacks a thorough discussion on the practical implications and limitations of the GG mechanism, particularly in terms of its real-world applicability and scalability.
- The paper's theoretical contributions, while solid, are not particularly novel or groundbreaking, and the proofs and theoretical results are not particularly surprising or insightful.
- The paper could benefit from a more detailed discussion on the computational and memory requirements of the GG mechanism, especially in large-scale applications.
- The paper's experimental results are somewhat limited in scope, and it would be beneficial to see more comprehensive testing across a wider range of datasets and parameters.

**Questions:**
- Could you elaborate on the practical implications and limitations of the GG mechanism, particularly in terms of its scalability and real-world applicability?
- How does the GG mechanism perform in terms of computational and memory requirements, especially in large-scale applications?
- Is there a specific reason why the GG mechanism was chosen over other mechanisms, such as the Gaussian mechanism, in the experiments described in the paper?
- Could you provide more details on the experimental setup and conditions under which the GG mechanism was tested?
- In the experiments involving the GG mechanism in DP-SGD, were there any significant differences in the results when using different values of β?
- How does the GG mechanism compare to other mechanisms in terms of its utility and privacy guarantees, especially in scenarios where the privacy budget is tight?
- Could you discuss the potential implications of the GG mechanism's performance in scenarios where the privacy budget is very low or very high?
