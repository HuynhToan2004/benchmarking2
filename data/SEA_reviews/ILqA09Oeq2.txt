 **Summary:**
The paper explores the performance of tensor-based methods in multi-view clustering, specifically focusing on the nested matrix-tensor model. It compares the performance of tensor-based methods with matrix-based methods, demonstrating that tensor-based methods can achieve better clustering accuracy, although they are computationally harder. The authors employ random matrix theory to derive theoretical bounds on the performance of tensor-based methods, showing that they can recover signals more effectively than matrix-based methods under certain conditions. The paper also discusses the computational challenges associated with tensor-based methods and proposes a hybrid approach to address these issues.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel approach to multi-view clustering using tensor-based methods, which are computationally hard but offer significant advantages in terms of performance.
- The authors provide a comprehensive theoretical analysis of the performance of tensor-based methods, which is a significant contribution to the field.
- The paper is well-written and easy to follow, making complex theoretical concepts accessible to readers.
- The results are supported by numerical simulations that demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed methods.
- The authors address an important question regarding the performance gap between tensor-based and matrix-based methods in multi-view clustering, which is a significant gap in the current literature.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper does not provide a clear comparison between the computational costs of tensor-based methods and matrix-based methods, which could help in understanding the practical implications of using tensor-based methods.
- The paper does not discuss the generalizability of the results to other types of multi-view clustering methods, which could limit the applicability of the findings.
- The assumptions made in the model, such as the specific form of the tensor-based method and the matrix-based method, may not be applicable in all practical scenarios, which could limit the relevance of the results.
- The paper does not discuss the limitations of the proposed methods, which could provide a more balanced view of the contributions and challenges of the research.
- The paper could benefit from a more detailed discussion on the implications of the results for practical applications, particularly in terms of computational efficiency and scalability.
