 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel method for inverse design using diffusion models, termed Compositional Inverse Design with Diffusion Models (CinDM). This method addresses the issue of adversarial modes in learned dynamics models by optimizing over the learned energy function captured by the diffusion model. It enables the design of more complex systems by combining multiple different diffusion models representing subcomponents of a desired system. The approach is tested across various domains including N-body interactions and multi-airfoil design, showing improved performance over existing methods. The paper also discusses the challenges of compositional inverse design and provides a set of benchmarks for inverse design in 1D and 2D.

**Strengths:**
- The paper addresses an important problem in the field of inverse design, focusing on the challenge of compositional inverse design, which is a significant and under-explored area.
- The proposed method, CinDM, is innovative and provides a novel approach to inverse design by leveraging the energy function of a diffusion model to optimize the design process.
- The paper is well-written, making it easy to follow and understand, and includes a set of benchmarks for inverse design in 1D and 2D to test the capability of the proposed method.
- The methodology is supported by thorough experiments that demonstrate the effectiveness of CinDM in solving the inverse design problem across various domains.
- The paper provides a novel perspective on inverse design by viewing it as an energy optimization problem over the learned generative manifold of design parameters, which is a significant contribution to the field.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed discussion on the limitations of the proposed method, which could provide a more balanced view of its strengths and weaknesses.
- The experimental setup and results are not convincingly presented, particularly in the 2D compositional design for multiple airfoils, where the results are not as convincing as in other domains.
- There is a lack of clarity and definition in some key concepts and equations, such as the role of the energy function in the optimization process and the definition of certain variables used in the equations.
- The paper could benefit from more comprehensive comparisons with existing methods, including both theoretical and practical comparisons, to better establish the novelty and effectiveness of the proposed method.
- The paper's claims regarding the compositional nature of the method are not well-supported, and the generalization capabilities of the method are not convincingly demonstrated.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors provide a more detailed discussion on the limitations of the proposed method and how these limitations might affect the practical application of CinDM?
- Could the authors clarify the role of the energy function in the optimization process and how it contributes to the overall effectiveness of the method?
- How does the proposed method compare to existing methods in terms of computational efficiency and resource requirements?
- In the experiments involving 2D compositional design for multiple airfoils, can the authors provide more detailed results or comparisons to demonstrate the effectiveness of their method?
- How does the proposed method handle the generalization from simpler to more complex systems, particularly in the context of compositional inverse design?
- Can the authors provide more details on the training and optimization processes, including the specifics of the diffusion model and the energy function used?
