 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel method for text-to-3D generation using a differentiable volume renderer and score distillation sampling (SDS) with a non-increasing timestep sampling strategy. This approach aims to improve the quality, diversity, and convergence speed of the 3D generation process by aligning the training process of 3D models with the sampling process of diffusion models. The proposed method, termed Time Prioritized SDS (TP-SDS), is evaluated against the vanilla SDS and shows significant improvements in terms of convergence speed, quality, and diversity. The paper also discusses the mathematical formulation and supervision misalignment issues in SDS and provides a detailed analysis of the proposed method's effectiveness.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, easy to follow, and includes comprehensive ablations, making it accessible to a broad audience.
- The proposed method is simple yet effective, with a clear rationale for the design choices, and is well-motivated by the analysis of the limitations of existing methods.
- The method is evaluated thoroughly, showing significant improvements in convergence speed, quality, and diversity compared to the vanilla SDS.
- The paper is well-structured, with a clear introduction of the problem, a detailed analysis of the limitations of existing methods, and a well-motivated design of the proposed method.
- The proposed method is not only effective for text-to-3D generation but also applicable to image-to-3D generation, which is a significant advantage.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed discussion on the limitations of the proposed method, such as potential drawbacks or failure cases.
- The paper could benefit from more diverse baseline comparisons, including other recent methods like DreamWaltz, ProlificDreamer, and others.
- The experimental results are not comprehensive enough, with some results, such as the diversity results, being presented in a qualitative manner without quantitative data.
- The paper does not provide sufficient details on the implementation of the method, such as the specifics of the differentiable volume renderer used and the optimization process.
- There is a lack of discussion on the impact of the proposed method on the convergence speed, quality, and diversity of other methods, which could strengthen the paper's claims.

**Questions:**
- How does the proposed method compare with other recent methods like DreamWaltz, ProlificDreamer, and others in terms of convergence speed, quality, and diversity?
- Can the authors provide more details on the implementation of the method, such as the specifics of the differentiable volume renderer used and the optimization process?
- How does the proposed method perform when applied to other types of 3D representations, such as mesh representations?
- Can the authors provide more quantitative results to support the claims of improved diversity and quality of the generated 3D assets?
- How does the proposed method handle the potential for mode collapse, especially in the early stages of the optimization process?
- Could the authors provide more details on the hyperparameters used in the experiments, such as the learning rate and the number of optimization steps?
