 **Summary:**
The paper explores the impact of decoding strategies on the performance of abstractive summarization, comparing various techniques across multiple datasets and models. The study includes a comprehensive evaluation of 2,656 runs using 30 million GPU days, covering a wide range of decoding strategies and hyperparameters. It presents a detailed analysis of the effects of these strategies on metrics such as fluency, factuality, and coverage, and introduces a new dataset, PRISM, to facilitate further research in this area. The paper also discusses the implications of different decoding strategies on the quality of summaries and provides recommendations based on the findings.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written and easy to follow, with a clear presentation of the problem and a comprehensive literature review.
- The authors have conducted extensive experiments, analyzing the performance of various decoding strategies across different datasets, models, and metrics, providing valuable insights into the effectiveness of these strategies.
- The paper introduces a new dataset, PRISM, which is a significant contribution to the field and will be beneficial for future research.
- The study is thorough, including a large number of runs and a detailed analysis of the results, which will be useful for practitioners in choosing decoding strategies.
- The paper is well-organized and provides a clear and concise presentation of the findings, making it accessible to a broad audience.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed discussion on the hyperparameters used in the experiments, which could affect the reproducibility and understanding of the results.
- There is a need for more clarity on the evaluation metrics used, particularly the definition and interpretation of the Kendall's tau coefficient.
- The paper could benefit from a more detailed discussion on the decoding strategies and their impact on different aspects of summarization quality, such as fluency and factuality.
- The paper could be improved by including more qualitative examples of summaries generated using different decoding strategies, which would enhance the reader's understanding of the results.
- The paper's claims about the superiority of certain decoding strategies over others are not convincingly supported by the data, and the results could be better presented in a more accessible format, such as a table.
- There is a lack of discussion on the limitations of the study, which could affect the generalizability and applicability of the findings.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors provide more details on the hyperparameters used in the experiments, particularly how they were chosen and their impact on the results?
- How were the hyperparameters for the human evaluation set, and were they chosen based on the same criteria as those used for the automatic metrics?
- Can the authors clarify the definition and interpretation of the Kendall's tau coefficient, and discuss its relevance and reliability in evaluating the quality of summaries?
- How do the decoding strategies affect the performance of different models, and are there any general trends or patterns in the results?
- Could the authors provide more detailed examples of summaries generated using different decoding strategies, and discuss how these summaries differ in terms of fluency, factuality, and other metrics?
- How do the authors address the limitations of the study, and what implications do these limitations have for the generalizability and applicability of the findings?
