 **Summary:**
The paper investigates the computational complexity of attention mechanisms, specifically focusing on higher-order generalizations of attention that capture correlations among tokens beyond pairs. It introduces a novel tensor-based attention mechanism, ATAttC, which generalizes the traditional attention mechanism by considering higher-order interactions among tokens. The authors provide a theoretical analysis of the computational complexity of this mechanism, showing that it can be computed efficiently under certain conditions and is computationally hard when the input matrices have large entries. They also discuss the implications of these results for the expressivity and efficiency of attention mechanisms in large language models.

**Strengths:**
- The paper addresses an interesting and important question about the computational complexity of attention mechanisms, which is crucial for understanding and improving the efficiency of large language models.
- The authors provide a clear and detailed explanation of the technical challenges involved in analyzing the computational complexity of attention mechanisms, which is essential for understanding the theoretical underpinnings of these models.
- The paper is well-written, with a clear presentation of the results, which makes it accessible to a broad audience, including both experts and non-experts in the field.
- The paper introduces a novel tensor-based attention mechanism, ATAttC, which is a significant contribution to the field, potentially opening new avenues for research in attention mechanisms.
- The theoretical results are robust, providing a solid foundation for further research and practical applications in the field of large language models.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper is quite dense and technical, which might make it challenging for non-experts to fully grasp the main ideas and contributions.
- The practical implications of the theoretical results are not clearly articulated, which could limit the paper's impact on the broader machine learning community.
- The paper primarily focuses on the computational complexity of attention mechanisms without discussing potential practical applications or empirical evaluations, which could enhance the paper's relevance and applicability.
- The paper's title and some of its content might be misleading, as it claims to generalize attention mechanisms but primarily focuses on a specific type of attention mechanism that might not be generally applicable.
- The paper lacks a comprehensive discussion on the limitations and potential negative societal impacts of the proposed methods, which is essential for a complete assessment of the proposed work.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors provide a more intuitive explanation of the computational complexity of attention mechanisms, possibly using simpler examples or visual aids to illustrate the main ideas?
- How does the proposed tensor-based attention mechanism compare to other types of attention mechanisms in terms of computational efficiency and expressivity?
- What are the potential practical applications of the proposed attention mechanism, and how might it be used to enhance the performance or capabilities of large language models?
- Could the authors discuss the limitations of their work and suggest potential directions for future research to address these limitations?
- How do the computational complexities of different attention mechanisms scale with the size of the input data, and what are the practical implications of these scalabilities?
- Could the authors provide a more detailed discussion on the potential negative societal impacts of their work, and how these impacts might be mitigated or addressed?
