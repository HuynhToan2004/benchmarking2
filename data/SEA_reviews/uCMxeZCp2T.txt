 **Summary:**
The paper explores the dynamics of recurrent neural networks (RNNs) from an optimal control perspective, specifically focusing on the learning process as a variational problem. It introduces a novel approach to solving the ODEs using a time reversal method, which is applied to the continuous-time RNNs, demonstrating that this method can approximate gradient descent. The authors also discuss the implications of this approach for the biological plausibility of backpropagation and propose a method for approximating the solution of the Hamilton's equations with boundary conditions using Cauchy's initial conditions. However, the paper is critiqued for its lack of clarity in presentation, insufficient discussion on the limitations of the proposed methods, and a need for more extensive experimental validation.

**Strengths:**
- The paper provides a novel approach to studying RNNs from an optimal control perspective, which is a relatively new and interesting direction in machine learning.
- The authors propose a time reversal method to approximate gradient descent, which is a significant contribution to the field.
- The paper is well-written and provides a comprehensive theoretical analysis, including the proofs of propositions and theorems.
- The authors discuss the limitations of their work, which is commendable for transparency.
- The paper explores the biological plausibility of backpropagation, which is a significant and relevant topic in the field.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks clarity in presentation, making it difficult to follow, especially in the mathematical formulations and the explanation of the proposed methods.
- There is a lack of discussion on the limitations of the proposed methods, which is crucial for understanding the scope and applicability of the findings.
- The experimental validation is insufficient, with only a few simple examples used to demonstrate the proposed methods.
- The paper does not adequately address the biological plausibility of the proposed methods, which is a significant claim made by the authors.
- There are several typographical and grammatical errors throughout the paper, which detract from its professional quality.
- The paper does not provide sufficient empirical evidence to support the claims made, particularly regarding the biological plausibility of the proposed methods.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors clarify the relationship between the proposed time reversal method and the backpropagation algorithm, especially in terms of how the backpropagation algorithm is derived from the time reversal method?
- How do the authors justify the assumption of the equivalence between the proposed time reversal method and the backpropagation algorithm, given the significant differences in their implementations?
- What are the implications of the time reversal method for the biological plausibility of learning in RNNs, especially considering the differences in the dynamics of biological neurons compared to those modeled in the paper?
- Can the authors provide more detailed examples or experimental setups to demonstrate the practical applicability of the proposed methods in real-world scenarios?
- How do the authors address the concerns regarding the biological plausibility of the proposed methods, particularly in light of the criticisms raised about the lack of empirical evidence and the potential oversimplification of the biological systems?
