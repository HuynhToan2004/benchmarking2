 **Summary:**
The paper introduces TaCA, a novel method for hot-plugging upgrades of visual foundation models without retraining downstream modules, which is a significant advancement in the field. TaCA utilizes a parameter-efficient adapter that aligns new and old representation spaces, ensuring compatibility and performance enhancement. The methodology is evaluated on various tasks such as video-text retrieval, video recognition, and visual question answering, demonstrating its effectiveness across different scales of models. Despite its innovative approach, concerns about the novelty and the depth of experimental validation persist, with reviewers suggesting a need for more comprehensive comparisons and deeper analysis.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel task, "Hot-Plugging Upgrades for visual foundation models," which is a significant advancement in the field.
- The proposed method, TaCA, is innovative and addresses a practical problem in the field of foundation models.
- The methodology is evaluated on various tasks such as video-text retrieval, video recognition, and visual question answering, demonstrating its effectiveness across different scales of models.
- The paper is well-written, making it easy to follow, and the authors have made the code and models available, enhancing reproducibility.
- The experiments are conducted on multiple downstream tasks, and the results are promising, showing the effectiveness of the TaCA method.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a comparison with other baseline methods, particularly in terms of computational efficiency and training time, which is crucial for evaluating the practicality of the proposed method.
- The experimental validation is not comprehensive, and the paper could benefit from a broader range of experiments to validate the effectiveness of TaCA across different tasks and models.
- The novelty of the paper is questioned, as the use of adapters and distillation loss is not new, and the methodology appears to be an incremental improvement rather than a significant breakthrough.
- The paper lacks a detailed analysis of the limitations and potential negative societal impacts of the proposed method.
- The paper could benefit from a more detailed comparison with existing methods, particularly in terms of computational efficiency and the effectiveness of TaCA in scenarios where the new foundation model is not as strong as the old one.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors provide a comparison with other baseline methods in terms of computational efficiency and training time?
- How does the TaCA method perform when the new foundation model is not as strong as the old one?
- Could the authors provide more detailed experimental results and analysis to validate the effectiveness of TaCA across different tasks and models?
- How does the TaCA method handle the potential issues of domain gaps between the pretrained datasets and target tasks?
- Could the authors provide more insights into the limitations and potential negative societal impacts of the proposed method?
