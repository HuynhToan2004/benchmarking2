 **Summary:**
The paper addresses the online advertising problem where the advertiser aims to maximize revenue while adhering to Return on Investment (ROI) and budget constraints. It formulates this problem as a combinatorial multi-armed bandit problem and proposes two algorithms: GCB and GCB_safe. The GCB algorithm is designed to maximize revenue but may violate constraints, while GCB_safe ensures constraint safety at the cost of lower revenue. The paper provides theoretical analysis, including a pseudo-polynomial time algorithm for the discretized version and discussions on the trade-offs between regret and constraint violations. However, the paper is criticized for its limited novelty and the lack of empirical validation against state-of-the-art methods.

**Strengths:**
- The paper addresses a significant problem in online advertising, focusing on the balance between revenue maximization and constraint adherence, which is crucial for real-world applications.
- The theoretical results are robust, particularly in demonstrating the inapproximability of the problem and the trade-offs between regret and constraint violations.
- The paper is well-structured, making it easy to follow, and includes a clear problem formulation and algorithm description.
- The authors provide a comprehensive analysis of the problem, including both theoretical and empirical results, which are supported by numerical experiments.
- The paper discusses the limitations of the proposed algorithms, such as the potential for sub-optimality in the GCB algorithm and the high regret in the GCB_safe algorithm.

**Weaknesses:**
- The novelty of the paper is limited as it primarily adapts existing methods like GCB and dynamic programming to the specific problem of online advertising.
- The empirical evaluation is insufficient, lacking comparisons with state-of-the-art methods and not including real-world data, which could validate the practical applicability of the proposed algorithms.
- The paper does not adequately discuss the practical implications of the theoretical results, particularly how the trade-offs between regret and constraint violations affect the performance of the algorithms in real-world scenarios.
- There is a lack of discussion on the computational complexity and scalability of the proposed algorithms, which is crucial for understanding their applicability in large-scale, real-world advertising systems.
- The paper could benefit from a more detailed discussion on the assumptions made, such as the assumption of known values per click, which might not hold in all practical scenarios.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors provide a detailed comparison of their algorithms with state-of-the-art methods in terms of performance and computational efficiency?
- How do the authors address the practical implications of the theoretical results, particularly the trade-offs between regret and constraint violations, in real-world advertising scenarios?
- Could the authors discuss the computational complexity and scalability of their algorithms, especially in the context of large-scale advertising systems?
- How do the assumptions made in the paper, such as the known value per click, affect the applicability of the proposed methods in real-world advertising platforms?
- Can the authors clarify the assumptions and limitations of their theoretical results, particularly the trade-offs between regret and constraint violations, and discuss how these might vary in different practical scenarios?
