 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel method for causal discovery in the presence of latent variables, leveraging rank information to identify latent variables and their relationships. This method, named Rank-based Latent Causal Discovery (RLCD), is designed to handle scenarios where latent variables are causally related to both observed and other latent variables. The RLCD algorithm is theoretically grounded and empirically validated using both synthetic and real-world data, demonstrating its effectiveness in identifying causal structures. The paper is structured around three phases: identifying the conditional independence skeleton, finding causal clusters, and refining these clusters. It also includes a discussion on the limitations and future work, suggesting potential improvements and extensions of the method.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, clear, and easy to follow, with a clear motivation and a solid theoretical foundation.
- The proposed method, RLCD, is novel and effective, particularly in its application to linear latent causal models, which is a significant contribution to the field.
- The paper provides a comprehensive review of related work and presents a detailed discussion of the method's theoretical underpinnings, including necessary and sufficient conditions for latent variable identification.
- The experimental results are convincing, demonstrating the effectiveness of the RLCD algorithm in both synthetic and real-world data sets.
- The paper is structured around three phases of the algorithm, which helps in understanding the method's implementation and effectiveness.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper could benefit from more detailed explanations and examples, particularly in sections where complex mathematical concepts are introduced, to enhance reader comprehension.
- There is a lack of discussion on the limitations of the proposed method, which could help in understanding its scope and applicability.
- The paper does not sufficiently compare its method with existing approaches, such as Huang et al. (2022), which could have strengthened the argument for the novelty and effectiveness of the RLCD algorithm.
- The paper could be improved by providing more detailed examples and explanations in the appendix, particularly in the sections discussing the method's theoretical underpinnings and experimental results.
- There is a need for a more thorough discussion on the computational complexity and scalability of the RLCD algorithm, especially in relation to other existing methods.
