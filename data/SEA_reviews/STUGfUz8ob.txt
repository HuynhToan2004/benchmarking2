 **Summary:**
The paper investigates the capability of Transformers in handling abstract symbols and symbolic labels, specifically in tasks where input symbols are variables and outputs are real numbers or symbols. It introduces a new framework termed "template tasks" to analyze how Transformers generalize to unseen symbols, focusing on both real and symbolic labels. The authors propose modifications to the Transformer architecture to enhance its data efficiency and generalization capabilities. Theoretical insights are provided alongside empirical validations, showing that these modifications can improve performance in tasks like GPT-2. However, concerns are raised about the practical applicability of the findings, as the experiments are limited to simplified settings and the paper's claims about the generalizability of Transformers are not convincingly supported.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel framework for analyzing the generalization of Transformers to unseen symbols, which is both original and interesting.
- The theoretical results are well-explained, providing a clear and detailed analysis that is supported by both theoretical and empirical evidence.
- The paper is well-written, making it accessible and easy to understand, which is crucial for a broad audience.
- The proposed modifications to the Transformer architecture are simple and effective, showing promise in improving data efficiency and generalization capabilities.
- The study of the generalization of Transformers to unseen symbols is a significant and relevant topic, given the widespread use of Transformers in various applications.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper's claims about the generalizability of Transformers are not convincingly supported, particularly in more complex or realistic settings.
- The experiments are limited to simplified settings, which may not accurately reflect the performance of Transformers in real-world scenarios.
- The paper does not sufficiently discuss the limitations of its work, such as the generalizability of the findings to more complex or varied tasks.
- The paper's focus on symbolic labels and their handling by Transformers is not well-motivated, and the relevance of these findings to practical applications is unclear.
- The paper could benefit from a more detailed discussion on the implications of the findings for practical applications, particularly in terms of the practical utility and applicability of the proposed modifications to the Transformer architecture.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors provide more details on the experiments, such as the specifics of the training data and the training methods used?
- How do the authors justify the choice of symbolic labels and their relevance to practical applications?
- Could the authors clarify the role of symbolic labels in the tasks studied and explain why these labels are relevant or interesting?
- Is there a possibility to extend the theoretical analysis to more complex or varied tasks, such as those involving more than two symbols?
- How do the proposed modifications to the Transformer architecture perform in more complex or varied tasks, and what are the implications of these modifications for practical applications?
