 **Summary:**
The paper introduces DFormer, a novel RGB-D pre-training framework designed to learn transferable representations for RGB-D scene understanding tasks, specifically semantic segmentation and salient object detection. DFormer pre-trains the backbone using image-depth pairs from ImageNet-1K, allowing for more efficient encoding of RGB-D representations. The framework is evaluated on several benchmarks, showing state-of-the-art performance with reduced computational costs compared to existing methods. The paper also introduces new building blocks for encoding both RGB and depth information, which are crucial for achieving these results.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-organized and easy to follow, with clear explanations of the proposed methods and their applications.
- The introduction of a novel RGB-D pre-training framework, DFormer, which effectively learns transferable representations for RGB-D scene understanding tasks.
- The use of image-depth pairs from ImageNet-1K for pre-training the backbone, which is a novel approach.
- The paper achieves state-of-the-art performance on several benchmarks with reduced computational costs compared to existing methods.
- The proposed building blocks for encoding both RGB and depth information are innovative and crucial for achieving the results.
- The paper is well-written, making it easy to understand, and includes comprehensive ablation studies that validate the proposed methods.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed comparison with existing RGB-D pre-training methods, which could help clarify the advantages of the proposed method.
- The paper does not provide a detailed analysis of the computational complexity of the proposed method, which is crucial for understanding its efficiency.
- There is a lack of discussion on the limitations of the proposed method, which could help in understanding its scope and applicability.
- The paper does not provide sufficient visual results to support the claims made, such as qualitative comparisons with existing methods.
