 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel dataset for Fair Graph Anomaly Detection (FairGAD), focusing on social media platforms like Reddit and Twitter. This dataset is significant as it incorporates real-world data with sensitive attributes and anomaly labels, providing a more realistic and practical evaluation framework for existing methods. The paper evaluates various Graph Anomaly Detection (GAD) and non-graph Anomaly Detection (AD) methods using five fairness metrics, highlighting the need for further research to address the identified gaps in fairness and performance trade-offs. The dataset is constructed using real-world user behaviors and is intended to serve as a benchmark for future research in this area.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel dataset that is specifically designed for the Fair Graph Anomaly Detection (FairGAD) problem, which is a significant advancement in the field.
- The dataset is based on real-world user behaviors and incorporates sensitive attributes and anomaly labels, which are critical for evaluating the fairness and performance of existing methods.
- The paper provides a comprehensive evaluation of various Graph Anomaly Detection (GAD) and non-graph Anomaly Detection (AD) methods using five fairness metrics, which helps in understanding the current state of research and identifying areas for improvement.
- The datasets are collected from real-world social media platforms, enhancing the relevance and applicability of the research.
- The paper is well-written and easy to follow, making it accessible to a broad audience.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed description of the data collection process, which is crucial for understanding the reliability and validity of the dataset.
- The datasets are limited in size, which may not be representative of larger or more diverse social media platforms.
- There is a lack of novelty in the methods used for anomaly detection, as the paper primarily uses existing methods without significant innovation.
- The paper does not adequately address the potential biases in the data, which could affect the fairness and reliability of the results.
- The paper does not provide a detailed comparison with other datasets or methods, which could help in understanding the unique contributions and advantages of the proposed dataset.
- The paper could benefit from a more comprehensive literature review and a more detailed discussion on the implications of the findings.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors provide more details on the data collection process, including the sources and methods used for data acquisition?
- How were the anomaly labels assigned, and what criteria were used for this assignment?
- What are the specific advantages of using these datasets over existing datasets, and how do they address the identified gaps in the field?
- Could the authors clarify the role and impact of the sensitive attributes in the anomaly detection process?
- How were the nodes in the graph assigned sensitive attributes, and what were the criteria for this assignment?
- What are the implications of the findings regarding the fairness and performance trade-offs, and how do these findings impact the design and implementation of future anomaly detection systems?
