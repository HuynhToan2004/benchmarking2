 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel approach to enhancing the robustness of deep neural networks (DNNs) against adversarial attacks by training data representations using an implicit neural representation (INR) method. The authors propose a min-max optimization problem to balance reconstruction quality and robustness, and develop a defense strategy termed "defense-in-creation" to address potential attacks. The paper presents a Double Projection Gradient Descent (DPGD) algorithm for attacking INRs and evaluates the robustness of the proposed method against various attacks. Despite the innovative approach, the paper is criticized for its limited evaluation on small datasets and lack of comparison with more robust defense methods.
