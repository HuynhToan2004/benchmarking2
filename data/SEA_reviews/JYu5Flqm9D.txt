 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel approach to text watermarking for large language models (LLMs) by encoding multi-bit customizable information into text, enhancing the capabilities of LLMs beyond traditional one-bit watermarking. The proposed method, named Balance-Marking, utilizes a proxy language model to balance the probability distribution of available and unavailable parts of the vocabulary, thereby preserving text quality. This method is evaluated against a vanilla baseline and shows improved performance in terms of watermark success rate, robustness, coding rate, encoding/decoding efficiency, and text quality. The paper also addresses the limitations of existing watermarking techniques, which are often encoding-inefficient and lack flexibility in encoding various types of information.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, with clear and easy-to-follow content that effectively communicates the authors' ideas and the proposed methodology.
- The proposed method, Balance-Marking, is innovative and effectively balances the probability distribution of available and unavailable parts of the vocabulary, enhancing the quality of the watermarked text.
- The paper includes a comprehensive evaluation system for codable text watermarking for LLMs, which covers multiple dimensions such as watermarking success rate, robustness, coding rate, encoding/decoding efficiency, and text quality.
- The authors have conducted extensive experiments to demonstrate the effectiveness of their method, comparing it against a vanilla baseline and showing that it outperforms the baseline in various metrics.
- The paper is well-structured, with a clear introduction of the problem, mathematical formulation, and methodology, making it easy for readers to follow and understand the proposed method.
- The authors have addressed the limitations of existing watermarking techniques, including encoding-inefficiency and lack of flexibility in encoding various types of information, and have proposed a method to address these limitations.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a discussion on the potential limitations of the proposed method, such as its applicability to different types of LLMs and the scalability of the method to larger or more complex models.
- The paper does not include a discussion on the potential negative social impacts of the proposed method, such as the potential misuse of watermarked text by malicious actors.
- The evaluation system for codable text watermarking for LLMs is not clearly defined, and the paper does not provide a detailed description of the evaluation metrics used.
- The paper does not adequately discuss the computational complexity of the proposed method, particularly the trade-off between computational complexity and watermark quality.
- The paper does not compare the proposed method with other existing watermarking techniques, which could help to establish the superiority of the proposed method.
- The paper does not provide a detailed discussion on the impact of the proposed method on the robustness of the watermark against various attacks, such as copy-paste and substitution attacks.

**Questions:**
- How does the proposed method perform when applied to different types of LLMs, and what are the potential limitations or challenges in scaling the method to larger or more complex models?
- Could the authors provide more details on the evaluation metrics used in the evaluation system for codable text watermarking for LLMs?
- How does the computational complexity of the proposed method compare to other existing watermarking techniques, and what are the trade-offs between computational complexity and watermark quality?
- Can the authors provide more details on the impact of the proposed method on the robustness of the watermark against various attacks, such as copy-paste and substitution attacks?
- How does the proposed method compare with other existing watermarking techniques in terms of watermarking success rate, robustness, coding rate, encoding/decoding efficiency, and text quality?
- Could the authors provide more details on the implementation of the proposed method, including the specifics of the proxy language model used and the computational resources required for encoding and decoding the watermark?
