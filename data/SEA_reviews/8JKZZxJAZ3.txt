 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel approach to nonnegative matrix factorization (NMF) by defining nonnegative canonical edges (NCEs), which are the intersections of the principal subspace containing the data to be factored with canonical faces of the nonnegative orthant. This method yields a closed-form solution for the special case where the factors are orthogonal and provides a deterministic optimal solution when the data lies within or near the cone formed by the NCEs. The paper also discusses the initialization of classical NMF methods using NCEs and their application in noisy and non-orthogonal cases. Despite its theoretical contributions, the paper lacks experimental validation and detailed comparisons with existing methods, which limits its practical applicability and the clarity of its advantages over existing techniques.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel approach to NMF using nonnegative canonical edges (NCEs), which is both theoretically sound and practically applicable.
- The method provides a closed-form solution for the special case where the factors are orthogonal, offering a deterministic optimal solution when the data resides within or in proximity to the cone formed by the NCEs.
- The paper provides a theoretical framework for NCEs, which can be used to initialize classical NMF methods, potentially improving their performance.
- The approach offers a new perspective on NMF, which could lead to further research and advancements in the field.
- The paper is well-written and easy to follow, with clear explanations and illustrations that aid in understanding the proposed method.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks experimental validation, which is crucial for demonstrating the effectiveness and practical applicability of the proposed method.
- There is a lack of detailed comparison with existing methods, which makes it difficult to assess the advantages and disadvantages of the proposed method over existing techniques.
- The paper does not provide sufficient theoretical analysis or proofs for some of the claims made, such as the global convergence of the proposed algorithm.
- The paper does not discuss the computational complexity of the proposed method, which is important for practical implementation.
- The paper does not address how the proposed method handles scenarios where the data does not satisfy the assumptions required for its application, such as the canonical inclusion property (CIP).
