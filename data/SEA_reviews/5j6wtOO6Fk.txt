 **Summary:**
The paper introduces Hieros, a hierarchical policy learning framework that utilizes S5 layers to predict next world states and imagine trajectories at multiple time scales in latent space. This method is designed to enhance sample efficiency in reinforcement learning by training an agent in imagination, eliminating the need for direct environment interaction during training. Hieros is evaluated on the Atari 100k benchmark, demonstrating superior performance compared to existing methods. The framework also includes a hierarchical design that allows for the prediction of world states at multiple levels, which is claimed to improve exploration capabilities and runtime efficiency. However, the paper has been criticized for its lack of clarity in explaining the method's advantages over existing methods and the necessity of its hierarchical design.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, clear, and easy to follow, with a good structure and clear figures that aid in understanding the proposed methods.
- The use of S5 layers in Hieros is novel and interesting, providing a new approach to predicting next world states and enhancing exploration capabilities.
- The method achieves state-of-the-art results on the Atari 100k benchmark, demonstrating its effectiveness in practical applications.
- The paper includes a thorough ablation study, which helps in understanding the influence of different components of Hieros.
- The method's efficiency in terms of runtime and resource demand is highlighted, which is crucial for practical deployment.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a clear explanation of the advantages of Hieros over existing methods, such as DreamerV3, and does not sufficiently demonstrate how Hieros improves upon or diverges from these methods.
- The paper could benefit from a more detailed explanation of the subgoal autoencoder and its role in the system.
- There is a need for more comprehensive comparisons with other hierarchical reinforcement learning (HRL) methods, especially in terms of runtime efficiency and exploration capabilities.
- The paper could be improved by providing more detailed explanations of the method's design choices, such as the use of S5 layers and the hierarchical design.
- The paper's claims about Hieros being more efficient than other methods like DreamerV3 are not convincingly supported with sufficient evidence.
- The paper could benefit from a more thorough discussion on the limitations of the proposed method and potential areas for future research.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors provide more details on the subgoal autoencoder and its role in the system?
- How does the proposed method compare to other hierarchical reinforcement learning methods, especially in terms of runtime efficiency and exploration capabilities?
- Can the authors clarify the advantages of Hieros over existing methods like DreamerV3, particularly in terms of performance and efficiency?
- How does the proposed method handle different levels of abstraction in the hierarchical design, and what are the implications for the performance and generalization of the model?
- Could the authors provide more detailed evidence to support the claims of Hieros being more efficient than other methods, especially in terms of runtime and resource usage?
- How does the proposed method handle the trade-off between sample efficiency and generalization ability, especially in complex environments?
