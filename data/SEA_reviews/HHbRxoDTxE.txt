 **Summary:**
The paper explores the use of looped transformers for in-context learning of mathematical tasks, specifically focusing on linear regression. The authors propose a novel training methodology for looped transformers, which involves a recurrent prompting technique that iteratively refines predictions, potentially reducing the number of parameters needed compared to traditional transformers. The experiments demonstrate that looped transformers can perform comparably to standard transformers while using significantly fewer parameters. The paper also discusses the potential of using these models for solving more complex mathematical problems, though the scope of the experiments is limited to linear regression.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel training methodology for looped transformers, which can be applied to a wide range of mathematical problems, potentially reducing the number of parameters needed.
- The empirical results are convincing, showing that the looped transformer can achieve comparable or better performance to standard transformers while using fewer parameters.
- The paper is well-written, with clear explanations of the training strategy and the model's performance, and includes a comprehensive analysis of the model's training and inference.
- The use of a looped transformer to solve linear regression tasks is innovative and provides a new perspective on how transformers can be used to solve mathematical problems.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper is limited in scope, focusing only on linear regression and not exploring other mathematical problems or more complex functions, which could limit the generalizability of the findings.
- The experimental results are not convincingly superior to existing methods, and the paper lacks a detailed comparison with other state-of-the-art models, particularly in terms of performance metrics and computational efficiency.
- The paper does not sufficiently address the computational complexity and memory requirements of the looped transformer, which could be a significant limitation in practical applications.
- The paper does not provide sufficient theoretical insights into the behavior of the looped transformer, which could help in understanding the model's performance and limitations.
- The paper does not adequately address the potential negative societal impacts of the work, which is a critical consideration for any research in the field.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors provide more details on the computational complexity and memory requirements of the looped transformer, and how these compare to other models?
- How does the model perform on more complex mathematical problems, such as higher-order polynomial regression or non-linear functions?
- Can the authors provide a more detailed comparison with other state-of-the-art models, including both qualitative and quantitative comparisons?
- How does the model handle the issue of convergence to a fixed point, and what are the implications for the model's performance and generalizability?
- Could the authors clarify the choice of parameters such as b and T, and how these were chosen to optimize the model's performance?
- How does the model perform in different types of mathematical problems, such as those involving non-linear functions or higher-order polynomials?
- Can the authors provide a more detailed explanation of the model's behavior and the insights it offers into the in-context learning capabilities of transformers?
