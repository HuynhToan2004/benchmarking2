 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel multi-modal Gaussian Process Variational Autoencoder (MM-GPVAE) designed to analyze neural and behavioral data concurrently. This model leverages a Fourier domain representation to enhance the identification of latent variables in high-dimensional time-series data. The MM-GPVAE is tested on both simulated and real-world datasets, demonstrating its ability to reconstruct neural and behavioral data effectively. The methodology involves a shared latent space for both modalities, which is crucial for understanding the relationship between neural activity and behavior. The paper also discusses the limitations and potential applications of the model, including its interpretability and scalability.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, with clear explanations of the methodology and its applications in real-world scenarios.
- The MM-GPVAE model is novel and innovative, combining the strengths of Gaussian Process Factor Analysis (GPFA) and Gaussian Process Variational Autoencoders (GP-VAE) to analyze both neural and behavioral data.
- The use of a Fourier domain representation for latent variables is a significant improvement over standard GP-VAEs, enhancing the model's ability to reconstruct neural and behavioral data effectively.
- The paper provides a detailed comparison with existing models, highlighting the advantages of the proposed method.
- The application of the model to real-world datasets, such as those from Drosophila and Manduca sexta, demonstrates its practical utility and potential impact on neuroscience research.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper could benefit from more detailed explanations and examples of the mathematical equations used, particularly in the Fourier domain representation section.
- There is a lack of discussion on the limitations and potential negative societal impacts of the model, which could be crucial for understanding its broader implications.
- The paper could be improved by including more detailed comparisons with other existing models, especially in terms of computational efficiency and scalability.
- The paper does not sufficiently address the scalability of the model to larger datasets or more complex behavioral paradigms, which could limit its applicability in certain neuroscience settings.
- The paper lacks a detailed discussion on the assumptions and limitations of the model, which could be crucial for understanding its applicability and potential biases.

**Questions:**
- Could you clarify the dimensions of the latent variables in the Fourier domain representation and their implications for the model's performance?
- How does the model handle the potential biases that could arise from the choice of prior and the assumptions made about the data?
- Can you provide more details on the computational efficiency and scalability of the model, particularly in relation to other existing models?
- How does the model perform when applied to datasets with more complex behavioral paradigms or larger datasets?
- Could you elaborate on the choice of the GP prior and how it affects the model's performance and interpretability?
- How does the model handle the potential for overfitting, especially in datasets with high-dimensional or complex data structures?
