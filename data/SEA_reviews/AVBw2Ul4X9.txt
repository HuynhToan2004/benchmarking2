 **Summary:**
The paper introduces Simi-Mailbox, a novel calibration method for Graph Neural Networks (GNNs) aimed at improving calibration by categorizing nodes based on neighborhood representational similarity and confidence, irrespective of proximity or connectivity. The method groups nodes with similar local topology and confidence, adjusting predictions with group-specific temperatures to address miscalibration. Extensive experiments across various datasets demonstrate the effectiveness of Simi-Mailbox, particularly in reducing calibration error. The paper also discusses the limitations of existing calibration methods and how Simi-Mailbox addresses these issues.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, easy to follow, and the experiments are extensive, covering a wide range of datasets and GNN architectures.
- The proposed method, Simi-Mailbox, is straightforward and effective, showing improvements over existing methods in terms of calibration error reduction.
- The paper provides a thorough analysis of the limitations of existing calibration methods and proposes a novel approach to address these issues.
- The methodology is clearly explained, with detailed experiments and ablation studies that support the claims made.
- The paper contributes to the ongoing research in the field of GNN calibration, which is crucial for improving the reliability and trustworthiness of GNN predictions.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper could benefit from more detailed explanations of the motivation behind the proposed method, particularly how it addresses the limitations of existing methods like CaGCN and GATS.
- There is a lack of clarity in the definition and computation of neighborhood similarity, which could affect the reproducibility and understanding of the results.
- The paper primarily focuses on homophilous graphs, which might limit the generalizability of the findings to other types of graphs.
- The paper could provide more detailed comparisons with other calibration methods, including those that use different metrics like class-wise ECE and KDE-ECE.
- The paper does not discuss the limitations of its method thoroughly, which could help in understanding its scope and applicability.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors clarify the motivation behind the proposed method and how it addresses the limitations of existing methods like CaGCN and GATS?
- How is the neighborhood similarity computed, and what is the rationale behind using the sigmoid function in equation 8?
- Could the authors provide more details on the selection of the number of clusters (N) in the KMeans clustering process?
- How does the method perform on heterophilous graphs, and what are the implications of this for the generalizability of the findings?
- Can the authors provide a more detailed comparison with other calibration methods, including those that use different metrics like class-wise ECE and KDE-ECE?
- How does the method perform in terms of execution time, and what are the implications of this for practical applications?
