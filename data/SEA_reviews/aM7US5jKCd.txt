 **Summary:**
The paper introduces novel adversarial attacks for semantic segmentation and proposes a robust initialization method using robust ImageNet backbones. It critiques the use of cross-entropy loss in adversarial training for segmentation and suggests alternative loss functions. The authors propose a new adversarial training method, PIR-AT, which leverages robust ImageNet models as initialization to enhance adversarial robustness in semantic segmentation. The paper also presents a new attack ensemble, SEA, which is evaluated against existing methods and shows promising results. However, the paper is criticized for its limited evaluation on only two datasets and the lack of comparisons with recent state-of-the-art methods.

**Strengths:**
- The paper addresses the important problem of adversarial robustness in semantic segmentation, a topic that has been understudied compared to image classification.
- The proposed methods, such as the new loss functions and the adversarial training approach, are well-motivated and supported by comprehensive experiments.
- The paper is well-written, making it easy to follow and understand, with a clear presentation of the proposed methods and their implications.
- The experimental results are promising, showing that the proposed methods can significantly improve adversarial robustness in semantic segmentation.
- The paper provides a detailed analysis of the challenges and limitations of adversarial training in semantic segmentation, which is crucial for advancing the field.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks comparisons with recent state-of-the-art methods, which could have strengthened the evaluation of the proposed methods.
- The experimental evaluation is limited to only two datasets, which may not fully demonstrate the generalizability of the proposed methods.
- The paper does not adequately address the limitations of the proposed methods, particularly in terms of their applicability to different datasets and architectures.
- The paper could benefit from a more detailed discussion on the computational costs and the scalability of the proposed methods to larger datasets.
- There are some inconsistencies and potential errors in the presentation of the paper, such as incorrect equations and unclear figures.

**Questions:**
- How do the proposed methods perform on other datasets beyond Pascal-Voc and Ade20K?
- Could the authors provide more details on the computational costs of the proposed methods and discuss their scalability to larger datasets?
- How do the proposed methods compare to other recent state-of-the-art methods, particularly in terms of adversarial robustness and computational efficiency?
