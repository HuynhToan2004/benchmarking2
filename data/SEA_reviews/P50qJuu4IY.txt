 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel self-supervised learning (SSL) method based on the matching gap loss, which contrasts with traditional methods like InfoNCE by focusing on the difference between the average cost of paired images and the optimal matching cost. This approach, which utilizes the Sinkhorn algorithm for optimal matching, aims to improve performance by reducing the need for extensive data augmentations. The method is tested on ImageNet-1k using ViT-B/16, showing competitive results. However, the paper's experimental evaluation is limited to this specific setup, and the method's generalizability and computational efficiency are not thoroughly explored.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel SSL loss function, the matching gap, which offers an alternative to traditional contrastive learning methods.
- The methodology is supported by a comprehensive theoretical analysis, and the paper provides a detailed explanation of the matching gap loss and its implementation.
- The experiments conducted on ImageNet-1k demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed method, showing competitive results against existing methods.
- The paper is well-written, with clear explanations and a logical structure, making it easy to follow and understand.
- The use of optimal transport for self-supervised learning is a significant contribution, providing a new perspective on how to optimize features for downstream tasks.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a broader experimental evaluation, focusing only on the ImageNet-1k dataset and not exploring other datasets or architectures such as ResNet-50.
- The computational efficiency of the method, particularly the use of the Sinkhorn algorithm, is not thoroughly addressed, raising concerns about the scalability of the approach.
- The paper does not provide a detailed comparison with other SSL methods, especially those that use similar approaches like optimal transport, which could have strengthened the argument for the superiority of the proposed method.
- The paper's experimental results are not convincingly superior to existing methods, and the improvements are marginal, which might not justify the added complexity of the proposed method.
- There is a lack of clarity in the presentation of the paper, particularly in the definitions and explanations of key terms and concepts, which could hinder understanding and reproducibility.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors provide more detailed explanations of the matching gap loss and its implementation, particularly how it differs from other SSL losses like InfoNCE?
- How does the proposed method compare to other SSL methods, especially those that also use optimal transport or similar approaches?
- What are the specific computational costs and efficiency of the proposed method compared to other SSL methods, especially in terms of training time and resource usage?
- Could the authors clarify the definitions and roles of certain terms and concepts used in the paper, such as the "optimal matching" and the "cost function" in the matching gap loss?
- How does the proposed method handle the scalability issues, particularly in large-scale datasets or with a large number of samples?
- Can the authors provide more detailed results on the transfer learning performance of the proposed method, especially on smaller datasets or with different architectures?
