 **Summary:**
The paper explores a new weight initialization method, G-Init, for Graph Neural Networks (GNNs) to address the issue of oversmoothing. The method is based on the variance of signals flowing forward and gradients flowing backward through the network, considering the effect of graph convolution. Theoretical analysis and experimental results on several benchmark datasets demonstrate the effectiveness of G-Init in reducing oversmoothing and improving performance. However, the paper's contributions are seen as limited as it primarily adapts existing methods without significant novelty or broader applicability. The experimental evaluation is also criticized for being insufficient and not including more recent GNN architectures.

**Strengths:**
- The paper addresses the significant issue of oversmoothing in Graph Neural Networks (GNNs), which is crucial for the advancement of GNN research.
- The theoretical analysis is robust, and the paper is well-written, making it accessible and understandable.
- The proposed G-Init method is simple and effective, showing promising results in experiments.
- The paper provides a clear explanation of the problem of oversmoothing and its impact on GNNs, which is a significant contribution to the field.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks novelty as it primarily adapts existing methods without significant modification or innovation.
- The experimental evaluation is insufficient and does not include more recent GNN architectures such as GIN, GraphSAGE, and Graphormer, which could provide a more comprehensive comparison.
- The paper does not sufficiently discuss the limitations of the proposed method, particularly its applicability to different GNN architectures and its scalability to larger datasets.
- The paper's claims about the impact of weight initialization on oversmoothing are not convincingly supported by the experimental results.
- The paper's theoretical analysis is seen as a direct application of existing methods without sufficient originality or depth in addressing the unique challenges of GNNs.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors clarify the theoretical contributions of the paper, especially how they differ from or build upon existing works like He et al. (2015)?
- How does the proposed G-Init method perform when applied to more recent and diverse GNN architectures, such as GIN, GraphSAGE, and Graphormer?
- Can the authors provide more detailed experimental results, including standard deviations and comparisons with other initialization methods like Xavier and Kaiming, to better demonstrate the effectiveness of G-Init?
- How does the paper address the scalability of the proposed method to larger datasets, and what are the potential limitations in terms of computational resources and training time?
- Could the authors provide more insight into the practical implications of the G-Init method, particularly in terms of its impact on training stability and convergence speed?
