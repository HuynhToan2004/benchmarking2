 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel approach to Bayesian Optimization (BO) by integrating density ratio estimation with semi-supervised learning (SSL). The proposed method, termed DRE-BO-SSL, utilizes a semi-supervised classifier to estimate the density ratio of points close and far from the global optimum, aiming to address the overconfidence issue in supervised classifiers. This method is tested against various benchmarks, including synthetic and real-world problems, demonstrating its effectiveness. The paper also discusses the potential of using unlabeled data to enhance BO performance and explores the use of different acquisition functions. However, concerns are raised about the clarity of the experimental setup, the necessity of the SSL component, and the fairness of the comparisons made.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel approach to Bayesian Optimization (BO) by integrating density ratio estimation with semi-supervised learning, which is a significant advancement in the field.
- The paper is well-written and easy to follow, making it accessible to a broad audience.
- The use of unlabeled data in BO is explored, which is a promising direction for future research.
- The experimental results are robust and demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed method across various benchmarks.
- The paper addresses an interesting and relevant problem in BO, namely the overconfidence of supervised classifiers, which is a significant issue in the field.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a clear motivation for using SSL in BO, and the necessity of SSL over traditional methods is not convincingly argued.
- The experimental setup is not clearly described, making it difficult to assess the fairness and validity of the comparisons made.
- The paper does not sufficiently discuss the limitations of the proposed method, which is crucial for understanding its applicability and potential drawbacks.
- The paper does not provide a detailed discussion on the computational complexity of the proposed method, which is essential for practical implementation.
- The paper does not adequately address the scalability of the proposed method, especially in high-dimensional or large-scale optimization problems.
- The paper lacks a thorough discussion on the impact of different hyperparameters on the performance of the proposed method, which is crucial for practical implementation.
- The paper does not sufficiently discuss the assumptions required for the proposed method to be effective, which could limit its applicability in real-world scenarios.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors clarify the motivation for using SSL in BO and how it addresses the specific challenges in BO that traditional methods cannot?
- How does the proposed method compare in terms of computational complexity and scalability with other existing methods?
- Can the authors provide a more detailed discussion on the impact of different hyperparameters on the performance of the proposed method?
- How does the proposed method handle the scalability issues, especially in high-dimensional or large-scale optimization problems?
- Can the authors provide more details on the experimental setup, particularly how the comparisons were made and the fairness of these comparisons?
- How does the proposed method address the overconfidence issue in supervised classifiers, and what are the specific advantages of using SSL over traditional methods?
- Can the authors provide a more detailed discussion on the assumptions required for the proposed method to be effective, and how these assumptions might limit its applicability in real-world scenarios?
