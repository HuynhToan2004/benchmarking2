 **Summary:**
The paper introduces SciRE-Solver, a novel sampling algorithm for diffusion models that utilizes a recursive difference method to approximate the derivative of the score function. This method is claimed to improve the convergence order of the sampling process, potentially reducing the number of steps required for generation. The authors demonstrate the effectiveness of their method through experiments on various datasets, showing that SciRE-Solver can generate high-quality samples with fewer steps compared to existing methods. However, the paper has been critiqued for its lack of clarity in presentation and the limited scope of its experimental validation.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel recursive difference method for approximating the derivative of the score function, which is a significant contribution to the field of diffusion models.
- The proposed SciRE-Solver is shown to generate high-quality samples with fewer steps compared to existing methods, demonstrating its effectiveness.
- The paper is well-written and easy to follow, making it accessible to a broad audience.
- Theoretical analysis is provided to support the proposed method, enhancing the paper's credibility.
- The proposed method shows promising results in terms of sample quality and computational efficiency, particularly in high-resolution datasets.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks clarity in its presentation, with some sections being difficult to understand, especially the algorithm descriptions and the derivation of the recursive difference method.
- The experimental validation is limited to specific datasets, such as CIFAR-10 and CelebA, and does not include a broader range of datasets like ImageNet or COCO, which could limit the generalizability of the findings.
- There is a lack of comparison with other state-of-the-art methods, such as those mentioned in the review, which could have provided a more comprehensive evaluation of the proposed method.
- The paper does not sufficiently discuss the limitations of the proposed method, which could help in understanding its applicability and potential drawbacks.
- The paper does not provide a detailed analysis of the computational complexity of the proposed method, which is crucial for assessing its practicality and efficiency.

**Questions:**
- Could you clarify the recursive difference method and its derivation, particularly the use of the finite difference method in its derivation?
- How does the proposed method compare to other state-of-the-art methods, such as those mentioned in the review?
- Can you provide a more detailed analysis of the computational complexity of the proposed method?
- How does the method perform on larger datasets like ImageNet or COCO, and what are the implications of its performance on such datasets?
- Can you discuss the limitations of the proposed method and how they might affect its applicability and performance in different scenarios?
