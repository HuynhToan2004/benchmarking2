 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel sampling framework called Diffusion Generative Flow Samplers (DGFS), which extends recent sampling-based approaches by leveraging controlled stochastic processes to model approximate samples from intractable high-dimensional density functions. DGFS is designed to address the sluggish credit assignment issues in previous methods by allowing the learning process to be tractably broken down into short partial trajectory segments, which enhances the accuracy of estimates of the normalization constant. The paper also discusses the method's soundness and presentation, highlighting its contributions to the field and its potential impact on machine learning and statistics. However, concerns are raised about the clarity of the paper, the lack of a detailed discussion on the theoretical underpinnings, and the need for more extensive empirical evaluations.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, with a clear and structured presentation that makes it easy to follow.
- The proposed method, DGFS, is novel and offers a fresh perspective on sampling from intractable high-dimensional density functions.
- The paper provides a thorough discussion of the method, including its theoretical underpinnings and practical applications, which are supported by extensive empirical evaluations.
- The method's effectiveness is demonstrated through a variety of challenging experiments, which showcase its ability to generate accurate samples from target distributions.
- The paper's originality lies in its integration of diffusion models with generative flow networks (GFlowNets), which allows for the use of intermediate learning signals, enhancing the accuracy of estimates of the normalization constant.
- The paper's contribution to the field of machine learning and statistics is significant, as it addresses a fundamental task that appears in these areas and offers a novel approach to solving sampling problems.

**Weaknesses:**

- There is a need for more extensive empirical evaluations to validate the method's effectiveness across a wider range of applications and to compare it with a broader set of baselines.
- The paper's presentation could be improved, particularly in terms of readability and clarity, especially in sections where the text is dense and complex.
