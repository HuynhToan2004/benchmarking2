 **Summary:**
The paper introduces Trust Region Aware Minimization (TRAM), a novel optimization algorithm that integrates sharpness-aware minimization with trust region-based fine-tuning methods. TRAM aims to improve out-of-distribution generalization by optimizing for flat minima and smooth representations, while retaining pre-trained structure. The method is evaluated on zero-shot adaptation tasks and cross-domain language modeling, showing improvements over baseline methods. The paper also discusses the limitations of the method and suggests future research directions.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel optimization algorithm, TRAM, which integrates sharpness-aware minimization with trust region-based fine-tuning methods, providing a new approach to optimizing for flat minima and smooth representations.
- The methodology is clearly explained, with detailed descriptions of the experimental setup and results, making it easy to follow and understand.
- The paper provides a comprehensive evaluation of TRAM on zero-shot adaptation tasks and cross-domain language modeling, demonstrating its effectiveness and robustness.
- The combination of SAM and R3F is novel and well-motivated, offering a new perspective on optimizing for smoothness in both parameter and representation spaces.
- The paper addresses an important and relevant problem in the field of machine learning, specifically focusing on improving generalization in fine-tuning scenarios.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a clear explanation of why the proposed method is superior to existing methods, particularly in terms of its ability to handle out-of-distribution generalization effectively.
- There is a lack of comparison with state-of-the-art methods, such as those mentioned in the review, which could help in validating the effectiveness of the proposed method.
- The paper does not provide sufficient analysis of the limitations of the proposed method, particularly in terms of its applicability to different types of models and datasets.
- The experimental results are not convincingly superior to existing methods, and the improvements are sometimes marginal or not statistically significant.
- The paper could benefit from a more detailed discussion on the theoretical underpinnings of the method, particularly how the combination of SAM and R3F optimizes for smoothness in both parameter and representation spaces.
- The presentation of the paper could be improved, particularly in terms of clarity and organization, especially in the introduction and related work sections.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors provide a more detailed explanation of why the proposed method is superior to existing methods, particularly in terms of its ability to handle out-of-distribution generalization effectively?
- How does the proposed method compare to other state-of-the-art methods, such as those mentioned in the review?
- Can the authors provide a more detailed analysis of the limitations of the proposed method, particularly in terms of its applicability to different types of models and datasets?
- How does the proposed method address the issue of overfitting in fine-tuning scenarios, especially in cases where the training data is limited?
- Could the authors provide a more detailed theoretical analysis of how the combination of SAM and R3F optimizes for smoothness in both parameter and representation spaces?
- How does the proposed method compare to other trust region-based optimization methods, such as those mentioned in the literature?
- Can the authors provide a more detailed explanation of the experimental results, particularly the marginal improvements observed in some cases?
