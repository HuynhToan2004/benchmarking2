 **Summary:**
The paper introduces DS-CLIP, a novel approach to training CLIP models that incorporates data deduplication and semantic enhancement. DS-CLIP employs a data deduplication module (D3) to cluster and sample image-text pairs, and a semantic enhancement module (SE) that utilizes large language models to refine and augment text captions. This method aims to improve training efficiency and performance on various downstream tasks, including image classification, retrieval, and segmentation. The authors claim that DS-CLIP outperforms the original CLIP model in terms of performance and training efficiency, although concerns about the novelty and clarity of the methodology have been raised.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel training strategy, DS-CLIP, which includes Data De-Duplication (D3) and Semantic Enhancement (SE) modules to enhance the training of CLIP models.
- DS-CLIP demonstrates significant performance improvements on various downstream tasks, such as image classification, retrieval, and segmentation, compared to the original CLIP model.
- The methodology is well-explained, with clear figures and detailed descriptions of the proposed modules and their interactions.
- The paper includes comprehensive ablation studies and comparisons with other CLIP variants, providing a thorough evaluation of the proposed method.
- The authors plan to release the code and generated data, which could benefit the community by enabling further research and reproducibility.

**Weaknesses:**
- The novelty of the proposed method is limited as it primarily involves clustering and sampling strategies, which have been previously explored in other works.
- The performance gains over the original CLIP model are marginal in some cases, raising questions about the effectiveness of the proposed methods.
- The paper lacks clarity in some sections, particularly in the description of the clustering process and the details of the semantic enhancement module.
- The paper does not adequately address the issue of misalignment in the dataset, which is crucial for the effectiveness of the semantic enhancement module.
- The method's dependency on large language models (LLMs) and visual large language models (VLLMs) for semantic enhancement could limit its applicability in scenarios without access to these models.
- The paper does not sufficiently compare its results with other state-of-the-art methods, which could help in understanding the relative performance of the proposed method.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors clarify the specifics of the clustering process, particularly how the clusters are formed and the rationale behind the chosen parameters?
- How does the proposed method handle misalignment in the dataset, and what steps are taken to ensure the effectiveness of the semantic enhancement module?
- Can the authors provide more detailed examples or case studies demonstrating the benefits of the semantic enhancement module in terms of reducing misalignment?
- Given the dependency on LLMs and VLLMs, how does the proposed method perform when these models are not available, and what are the implications for the generalizability of the method?
- Could the authors elaborate on the performance discrepancies between the proposed method and the original CLIP model, especially in scenarios where the improvements are marginal?
- How does the proposed method compare to other state-of-the-art methods in terms of performance and efficiency, and what are the key advantages of DS-CLIP over these methods?
