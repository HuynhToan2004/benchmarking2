 **Summary:**
The paper introduces QLLM, a post-training quantization method for large language models (LLMs) designed to address the challenges of activation outliers and their impact on quantization accuracy. QLLM utilizes a channel reassembly technique to redistribute the magnitudes of outlier activations across multiple channels, thus mitigating the effects of these outliers on the quantization process. Additionally, QLLM incorporates an efficient error correction method and a low-rank parameter tuning strategy to enhance performance. The methodology is evaluated on the LLaMA-1 and LLaMA-2 families, demonstrating significant improvements in accuracy and efficiency compared to existing methods. However, concerns were raised regarding the clarity of the paper, the novelty of the approach, and the comparison with existing methods, particularly in terms of computational efficiency and memory usage.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written and easy to follow, with clear explanations of the proposed methodology.
- The experiments are extensive, covering a wide range of model sizes and quantization levels, and the results are clearly presented with detailed ablation studies.
- The proposed channel reassembly technique is novel and effective, particularly in addressing the challenges posed by activation outliers in LLMs.
- The method is evaluated on various datasets and shows promising results, outperforming existing methods in terms of accuracy and efficiency.
- The paper is well-organized and provides detailed implementation details, making it accessible and reproducible.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks clarity in some sections, particularly in the presentation of the channel reassembly technique and the details of the error correction method.
- The novelty of the approach is questioned as similar techniques have been previously explored in other works, such as weight quantization and gradient-based error correction.
- The computational efficiency of the method is not adequately addressed, with concerns about the additional computational costs introduced by the channel reassembly technique.
- The paper does not provide a comprehensive comparison with existing methods, particularly in terms of memory usage and computational efficiency.
- The paper could benefit from a more detailed discussion on the limitations and potential negative societal impacts of the proposed method.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors clarify the computational efficiency of the channel reassembly technique, specifically the additional computational costs introduced?
- How does the proposed method compare in terms of memory usage and computational efficiency with existing methods, particularly those that use similar techniques?
- What are the specific advantages of the channel reassembly technique over other outlier handling methods, and how does it address the challenges posed by activation outliers more effectively?
- Could the authors provide more details on the training process and the computational resources required for training the models?
- How does the proposed method handle the potential negative societal impacts of LLM quantization, and what measures have been taken to mitigate these impacts?
