 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel approach to 3D graph diffusion by utilizing a latent space, aiming to enhance the performance and efficiency of diffusion models in generating 3D graphs. The methodology involves a cascaded 2D-3D graph autoencoder for learning a low-dimensional latent space, which is then used to train a diffusion model. This approach is supported by theoretical analysis and empirical evidence, demonstrating its effectiveness in various applications such as molecular drug discovery. The paper also discusses the challenges in modeling 3D graphs and the benefits of using a latent space for diffusion models.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, clear, and easy to follow, with a clear motivation for using a latent space for diffusion models in 3D graphs.
- The proposed method is novel and well-explained, with a clear motivation for its use in 3D graphs.
- The paper includes theoretical analysis and empirical evidence to support the use of a latent space for diffusion models in 3D graphs, which is a significant contribution to the field.
- The methodology is well-explained, and the paper provides a detailed explanation of the challenges in modeling 3D graphs and how the proposed method addresses these challenges.
- The paper is well-positioned in the literature, discussing the limitations and potential extensions of the proposed method.
- The proposed method shows promising results in molecular drug discovery, demonstrating its potential practical applications.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed discussion on the limitations of the proposed method and potential extensions or improvements.
- The experimental results are not convincing in some cases, particularly in the comparison with other models in terms of validness and distribution discrepancy.
- The paper could benefit from more comprehensive comparisons with other models, especially in terms of training time and computational efficiency.
