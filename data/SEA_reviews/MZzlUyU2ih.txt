 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel framework named DoReMi, designed to enhance the execution of high-level plans by integrating vision language models (VLMs) to detect and recover from misalignments between planned and executed actions. The framework leverages LLMs to generate constraints for low-level execution and VLMs to monitor and detect constraint violations. This approach allows for immediate re-planning and recovery, thereby improving task success rates and reducing execution times in complex tasks involving robot arms and humanoid robots. The paper presents theoretical analyses and empirical results demonstrating the effectiveness of the proposed method, although it has been noted that the novelty and methodological contributions are somewhat limited, and the paper could benefit from a clearer presentation and more detailed empirical evaluations.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel framework that effectively leverages both LLMs and VLMs to enhance the execution of high-level plans, addressing the challenge of misalignments between planned and executed actions.
- The integration of LLMs and VLMs is innovative, allowing for immediate detection and recovery from misalignments, which is a significant improvement over traditional methods that only detect misalignments after the completion of a step.
- The paper is well-written, with clear figures and a logical structure that aids in understanding the complex concepts presented.
- The empirical results demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed method, showing improvements in task success rates and execution times in complex tasks involving robot arms and humanoid robots.
- The paper includes theoretical analyses and a detailed discussion on the limitations of the proposed approach, which adds depth to the understanding of the methodology.

**Weaknesses:**
- The novelty of the approach is somewhat limited as it primarily combines existing technologies without introducing significant new methodologies or insights.
- The paper lacks a detailed discussion on the technical challenges involved in integrating LLMs and VLMs, which could have provided more insight into the complexities of the system.
- The empirical evaluation is limited, with only a few tasks and a small number of seeds used, which may not fully demonstrate the generalizability or robustness of the proposed method.
- The paper could benefit from a clearer presentation and more detailed descriptions of the experimental setup and methodology, particularly in the appendix where critical details are relegated.
- The paper does not adequately address the limitations of the proposed approach, such as the reliance on zero-shot VLM which may not perform well in complex scenarios, and the potential impact of mis-detection, occlusion, and perspective on the effectiveness of the VLM detector.
- There is a lack of comparison with more advanced VLMs and other relevant baselines, which could provide a more comprehensive evaluation of the proposed method.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors provide more details on the technical challenges involved in integrating LLMs and VLMs, particularly how these challenges were addressed in the development of the DoReMi framework?
- How does the proposed method compare to more advanced VLMs in terms of performance and effectiveness?
- Could the authors clarify the role and effectiveness of the VLM detector in scenarios where misalignments occur frequently, such as in the "Prepare-food" task?
- How does the method handle different types of misalignments, such as those that may not be easily detectable by the VLM detector?
- In the context of the empirical evaluation, how were the success rates and execution times calculated, and what do these metrics represent in terms of the practical performance of the system?
- Could the authors provide more details on the training and fine-tuning of the VLM, particularly in terms of the dataset and methodology used?
