 **Summary:**
The paper introduces CircuitNet 2.0, an expanded version of the CircuitNet dataset, which includes over 10,000 samples from various chip designs such as CPU, GPU, and AI chips, using a 14nm FinFET technology node. The dataset provides comprehensive data including routability, timing, and power, enabling versatile machine learning tasks in Electronic Design Automation (EDA). The paper explores various machine learning tasks such as routability, IR-drop, and timing prediction using this dataset. However, the paper is criticized for its limited novelty, lack of significant technical contributions, and inadequate evaluation of the proposed methods. The dataset's utility is questioned due to its limited size and the absence of detailed comparisons with existing datasets.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces CircuitNet 2.0, a large-scale dataset for chip design, which supports various machine learning tasks and is useful for researchers in the field.
- The dataset is comprehensive and covers a wide range of chip designs, including CPU, GPU, and AI chips, which is beneficial for advancing machine learning in EDA.
- The paper is well-organized and easy to follow, making it accessible to a broad audience.
- The dataset is generated using commercial EDA tools and PDK, ensuring high consistency with the realistic chip design environment.
- The dataset includes a variety of features and labels, which can be used for various prediction tasks, enhancing its utility.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks novelty as it primarily focuses on expanding the CircuitNet dataset without significant technical contributions.
- The dataset's size is relatively small, which might limit its utility for training large machine learning models.
- The paper does not provide sufficient details about the dataset's generation process, which could be crucial for understanding the dataset's quality and reliability.
- The evaluation of the proposed methods is insufficient, with only a few models tested, and the results are not convincing enough to demonstrate the effectiveness of the dataset.
- The paper does not adequately compare the proposed dataset with existing datasets, which is necessary to understand its advantages and limitations.
- The paper lacks a detailed description of the dataset's features and labels, which could hinder its utilization by the research community.

**Questions:**
- What are the specific differences between CircuitNet 2.0 and the original CircuitNet dataset?
- How does the dataset's size impact its utility for training large machine learning models?
- Can the authors provide more details about the dataset's generation process, including the tools and methodologies used?
- How does the dataset's quality compare to other existing datasets in terms of data consistency and reliability?
- What are the advantages of using CircuitNet 2.0 over other existing datasets, especially in terms of its features and labels?
- How does the dataset's size and composition affect its utility for different machine learning tasks?
- Could the authors provide more detailed comparisons with other datasets to highlight the unique strengths and contributions of CircuitNet 2.0?
