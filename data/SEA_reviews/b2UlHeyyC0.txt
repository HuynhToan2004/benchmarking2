 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel approach to enhancing the performance of vision-language models by integrating a retrieval module that leverages external image-text pairs to refine the model's representations. The method, termed RECO, utilizes a one-layer multi-head self-attention transformer to fuse retrieved knowledge with the model's outputs, improving zero-shot classification performance across various fine-grained tasks. The approach is validated through extensive experiments, demonstrating significant improvements over existing models like CLIP and LiT. However, the method's effectiveness relies heavily on the quality and diversity of the retrieved data, which could be a limitation in practical applications.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-organized and easy to follow, with clear and well-explained experiments and results.
- The proposed method is simple, effective, and can be applied to various vision-language models, including CLIP and LiT.
- The method demonstrates significant improvements in zero-shot performance on fine-grained tasks, as shown in extensive experiments.
- The paper provides a detailed analysis of the components of the method, including the choice of retrieval strategies and the fusion mechanism, which is supported by ablation studies.
- The method's novelty lies in its ability to use retrieved knowledge to enhance model performance, which is a significant advancement in the field of vision-language models.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed discussion on the limitations of the method, particularly in terms of the scalability and generalizability of the approach.
- The effectiveness of the method heavily depends on the quality and diversity of the retrieved data, which could be a significant limitation in practical applications.
- The paper does not discuss the potential negative societal impacts of the work, which is a critical consideration in the deployment of such technologies.
- The paper could benefit from a more thorough discussion on related work, particularly on knowledge-based vision-text models and retrieval-based methods, which are closely related to the proposed method.
- The method's reliance on a retrieval module that may not be perfect could lead to noisy or incorrect fused representations, which could affect the model's performance.
- The paper does not discuss the potential negative societal impacts of the work, which is a critical consideration in the deployment of such technologies.

**Questions:**
- How does the method perform when the retrieved data is noisy or incorrect?
- Could the authors provide more details on how the retrieval module is trained and how it is integrated with the vision-language model?
- How does the method handle the scalability issues, particularly in terms of the size and diversity of the retrieved data?
- Can the authors discuss the potential negative societal impacts of the work and how these might be mitigated?
- How does the method compare to other retrieval-based methods in terms of performance and efficiency?
- Could the authors provide more examples or case studies to illustrate the method's effectiveness in practical applications?
