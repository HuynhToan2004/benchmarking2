 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel method for test-time defense against backdoor attacks, utilizing a masked autoencoder (MAE) to detect and restore images from triggers. The methodology involves two trigger scores based on image similarity and label consistency, which are refined using a topology-aware approach to enhance accuracy and generalization. The approach is tested across various datasets and attack scenarios, demonstrating its effectiveness against different backdoor attacks. Despite its innovative use of MAE for backdoor defense, the paper has been critiqued for its limited novelty, potential inefficiency, and lack of comprehensive evaluation against a broader range of attacks.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel use of masked autoencoders for backdoor defense, which is both innovative and well-motivated.
- The methodology is well-explained, with clear descriptions of the trigger score generation, topology-aware score refinement, and adaptive image restoration.
- The experimental results showcase the effectiveness of the proposed method across various datasets and attack scenarios, demonstrating its potential for practical applications.
- The paper is well-written, making it easy to follow, and provides a comprehensive discussion on the limitations and potential improvements of the proposed method.
- The use of MAE for backdoor defense is a significant contribution to the field, particularly in its application to test-time defense.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a comprehensive evaluation against a broader range of attacks, which could have strengthened the results and the paper's contribution to the field.
- The novelty of the method is somewhat limited as it builds on existing works, such as MAE and other image purification methods.
- There is a potential inefficiency in the method due to the need for multiple forward passes of the model, which could be time-consuming.
- The paper could benefit from a more detailed discussion on the potential negative societal impacts of the proposed method.
- The method's effectiveness is not thoroughly evaluated against various backdoor attacks, which could have provided a more robust validation of the proposed method.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors provide more details on the potential negative societal impacts of the proposed method?
- How does the method perform against other types of backdoor attacks, such as those that use different trigger patterns or are designed to be more stealthy?
- Can the authors provide more detailed experimental results, including the standard deviation of the results, to better understand the robustness and reliability of the method?
- Is there a potential for the method to be adapted to work with other types of models, such as transformers, and if so, how might this impact the method's performance?
- Could the authors clarify the use of certain symbols and equations, such as the notation for the masked image and the definition of the topology-aware score refinement?
