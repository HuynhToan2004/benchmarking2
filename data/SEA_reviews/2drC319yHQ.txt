 **Summary:**
The paper introduces RepoFusion, a novel approach to enhance the performance of code models by integrating repository context during training. This method leverages the Fusion-in-Decoder architecture to combine multiple contexts from the repository, including surrounding context and prompt proposal context, to predict missing sections of code. The approach is evaluated on a newly released dataset, Stack-Repo, and shows promising results, particularly in surpassing the performance of larger models without repository context. However, the novelty of the approach is questioned, as it builds on existing methods like FiD and RLPG, and the dataset's diversity and relevance to real-world scenarios are concerns.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, clear, and easy to follow, making complex concepts accessible to a broader audience.
- The proposed method is straightforward and effective, with good results demonstrated on the new Stack-Repo dataset.
- The dataset, Stack-Repo, is significant and could be beneficial for the community if made publicly available.
- The paper is well-organized, with clear presentation of results and thorough ablation studies that provide valuable insights.
- The use of the Fusion-in-Decoder architecture to combine multiple contexts from the repository is innovative and shows potential for improving code model performance.

**Weaknesses:**
- The novelty of the approach is limited as it primarily adapts existing methods like FiD and RLPG to the code domain without significant innovation.
- The paper lacks a detailed discussion on the selection of hyperparameters, which could affect the reproducibility and generalizability of the results.
- The dataset used for evaluation, Stack-Repo, is not diverse enough, with all repositories being Java, which may not reflect real-world scenarios where different languages are used.
- The paper's experimental setup and results are not sufficiently robust, with concerns about the selection of baselines and the generalizability of the results across different contexts.
- The paper's claims about the effectiveness of RepoFusion are not convincingly supported, especially when compared to larger models without repository context.

**Questions:**
- How does the proposed method compare to other retrieval-augmented code models that use similar contexts from the repository?
- Can the authors clarify the rationale behind the selection of hyperparameters and provide more details on their impact on the results?
- How does the performance of RepoFusion vary across different types of code repositories, particularly those in different programming languages?
- In light of the concerns about the novelty and the effectiveness of the method, can the authors provide more evidence or arguments to support the paper's claims?
- How does the method handle the potential for overfitting to the specific dataset used, and what steps have been taken to ensure the method's generalizability?
