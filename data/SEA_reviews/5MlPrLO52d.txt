 **Summary:**
The paper investigates the Neural Tangent Kernel (NTK) behavior in axis-aligned soft decision tree ensembles, extending prior work on oblique trees to the axis-aligned case. It explores the implications of different training constraints, such as always axis-aligned (AAA) versus axis-aligned at initialization but not during training (AAI). The authors provide a closed-form solution for the NTK induced by these trees, demonstrating how the ensemble can be transformed into a set of axis-aligned oblivious trees. The paper also discusses the application of multiple kernel learning (MKL) for feature selection in axis-aligned tree ensembles. Despite its theoretical contributions, the paper is criticized for its limited empirical evaluation and lack of practical implications, as well as the unclear presentation of some results.

**Strengths:**
- The paper provides a closed-form solution for the Neural Tangent Kernel (NTK) induced by axis-aligned soft decision tree ensembles, which is a significant theoretical contribution.
- The authors show that the axis-aligned non-oblivious tree ensemble can be transformed into a set of axis-aligned oblivious tree ensembles that induce exactly the same NTK, which is a practical and interesting result.
- The paper is well-written, with clear and detailed proofs of the theorems and propositions, making it accessible and understandable.
- The analysis of axis-aligned trees is novel and provides new insights into the behavior of NTK in tree ensembles.
- The paper is a good extension of previous work on NTK for soft decision trees, addressing the axis-aligned constraint which is crucial in real-world applications.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks empirical evidence to support the claims made, particularly in terms of practical implications and the effectiveness of the proposed methods in real-world scenarios.
- The experimental evaluation is limited and does not adequately demonstrate the superiority or practical applicability of the proposed methods over existing ones.
- The paper does not provide a clear motivation for why axis-aligned trees are more beneficial than oblique trees, which could be a significant limitation for the practical application of the findings.
- The paper suffers from some presentation issues, such as unclear figures and a lack of detailed explanation for certain results, which could hinder understanding and reproducibility.
- The paper's focus on theoretical analysis without sufficient empirical validation may limit its appeal to a broader audience, particularly those interested in practical applications.

**Questions:**
- Could you clarify the practical implications of the theoretical results presented in the paper? How do these results translate into real-world applications, particularly in terms of improving the performance or efficiency of decision tree ensembles?
- What are the advantages of using axis-aligned trees over oblique trees, and how do these advantages manifest in practical scenarios?
- How does the paper address the practical limitations of the NTK analysis, such as the assumption of infinite trees and the idealized training conditions?
- Can you provide more details on the experimental setup and results, particularly the choice of datasets and hyperparameters used? How do these choices affect the validity and applicability of the results?
