 **Summary:**
The paper explores the ternary neural network (TNN) by proposing a novel quantization method that utilizes asymmetric thresholds and symmetric scaling factors to enhance the representation capacity of TNNs. It introduces a bi-STE optimization method to optimize the TNNs and employs a two-stage knowledge distillation scheme to enhance performance. The method aims to achieve a uniform distribution among quantized candidates and to balance hardware implementation with performance. The paper claims significant improvements over previous methods, achieving a top-1 accuracy of 74.5% for ResNet-50 on ImageNet, outperforming both ternary and 2-bit quantization methods. However, concerns are raised about the novelty of the proposed method and the clarity of its presentation.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, easy to follow, and provides a comprehensive review of existing works on TNN and ternary logic circuits.
- The proposed method achieves good performance on the CIFAR-10 and ImageNet datasets, demonstrating significant improvements over previous methods.
- The paper introduces innovative methods such as asymmetric thresholds and symmetric scaling factors, which are crucial for the development of TNNs.
- The experimental results are robust, showing that the proposed method outperforms previous ternary quantization methods and even surpasses 2-bit quantization methods.
- The paper is well-organized and presents the experimental results in a clear and understandable manner.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed comparison with other recent methods, such as LCQ and FATNN, which could have provided a more comprehensive evaluation of the proposed method.
- The novelty of the proposed method is questionable as it seems to be a combination of existing methods without significant new insights or contributions.
- The paper does not adequately address the issue of hardware implementation, which is crucial for TNNs. The discussion on hardware implementation is limited and does not provide a detailed comparison with other methods.
- There are inconsistencies and potential errors in the presentation of the paper, such as incorrect equations and unclear descriptions in figures and tables.
- The paper does not sufficiently discuss the limitations of the proposed method, which could help in understanding its applicability and potential challenges in real-world scenarios.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors provide a more detailed comparison with other recent methods, such as LCQ and FATNN, to better understand the relative performance of the proposed method?
- How does the proposed method compare in terms of hardware implementation with other methods, especially in scenarios where hardware efficiency is critical?
- Can the authors clarify the inconsistencies and potential errors in the presentation of the paper, particularly in the equations and figures?
- How does the proposed method handle the limitations discussed in the paper, and what are the potential challenges or drawbacks in practical applications?
- Can the authors provide more details on the hardware implementation aspects of the proposed method, including the specific hardware architectures and the potential benefits in terms of power consumption, area, and throughput?
