 **Summary:**
The paper introduces Bold Actor Conservative Critic (BACC), a novel exploration strategy for deterministic policy reinforcement learning in continuous action spaces. BACC leverages Q-value to guide out-of-distribution exploration, using a modified double Q function framework. It integrates the Dynamic Boltzmann softmax update theorem with Q-value to derive an exploration policy, which is constructed using modified weights and Q values. BACC excels in complex environments like the Humanoid environment in Mujoco and Roboschool, demonstrating its effectiveness in tackling challenging continuous action space exploration problems. The methodology involves constructing an exploration policy with a limited set of actions and training a parameterized policy by minimizing the expected KL-divergence between the target policy and a policy constructed based on the minimum Q value.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, making it easy to follow and understand the proposed methodology.
- The introduction of the Bold Actor Conservative Critic (BACC) algorithm is novel and provides a significant contribution to the field of reinforcement learning, particularly in continuous action spaces.
- The experimental results demonstrate the effectiveness of the BACC algorithm, showing superior performance compared to existing methods in complex environments like the Humanoid environment in Mujoco and Roboschool.
- The paper is well-organized, with clear explanations of the theoretical concepts and practical applications of the BACC algorithm.
- The proposed method is simple, intuitive, and effective, with theoretical guarantees provided by Theorem 1.
- The paper includes a comprehensive evaluation across multiple environments, showcasing the robustness and generalizability of the proposed method.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed comparison with state-of-the-art methods, particularly those that also employ exploration strategies.
- There is a lack of clarity regarding the specific contributions of the proposed method, particularly how it differs from existing methods like SAC and OAC.
- The paper does not provide sufficient empirical evidence to support the claims made about the exclusive policy issue in SAC and the inaccurate predictions of OAC in high-dimensional action spaces.
- The paper does not adequately address the computational complexity and training time associated with the BACC algorithm.
- The experimental setup and results could benefit from more detailed analysis and discussion, particularly in terms of the stability and reproducibility of the results.
- The paper could benefit from a more rigorous proofreading to correct grammatical and typographical errors.
