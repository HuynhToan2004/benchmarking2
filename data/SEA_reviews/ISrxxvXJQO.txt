 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel method for image reconstruction using one-way wave equations, extending the FINOLA model to a set of wave equations with learnable speeds. This approach allows for the reconstruction of images from a set of initial conditions, which are derived from the original image using a visual encoder. The methodology involves the decomposition of images into special solutions of the wave equations, which are then used to reconstruct the images. The paper claims that this method provides a new mathematical perspective on images, revealing their underlying mathematical properties. The experimental results demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed method, particularly in achieving high PSNR values and improving image reconstruction quality. However, the paper's novelty and the clarity of its mathematical formulations have been questioned, suggesting a need for more rigorous theoretical proofs and clearer exposition.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, with clear and easy-to-follow explanations, making it accessible to readers.
- The proposed method is simple, elegant, and achieves good reconstruction results, as demonstrated by the experimental results.
- The introduction of the hidden waves phenomenon provides a novel perspective on the mathematical properties of images, which is both intriguing and insightful.
- The paper provides a new mathematical perspective on image reconstruction, potentially inspiring further research in the field.
- The method achieves high PSNR values and demonstrates improvements in image reconstruction quality, showing its effectiveness in practical applications.

**Weaknesses:**
- The novelty of the paper is questioned as it appears to be a straightforward extension of FINOLA, with the addition of learnable speeds and the relaxation of constraints.
- The paper lacks a comprehensive theoretical proof for the mathematical properties it claims, which undermines its scientific rigor.
- The experimental comparisons are insufficient, particularly in terms of fairness and comprehensiveness. The paper does not adequately compare its method with other existing methods, such as DCT and DWT, which could have provided a more robust validation of the proposed approach.
- The paper suffers from clarity issues, particularly in the mathematical formulations and the explanations of certain terms and concepts, which could confuse readers.
- There is a lack of discussion on the limitations of the proposed method, which could provide a more balanced view of its capabilities and potential challenges.
- The paper could benefit from a more thorough discussion on the implications of the hidden waves phenomenon for image representation and compression, as well as its broader implications for the field of computer vision.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors provide a more detailed explanation of the mathematical formulations used in the paper, particularly those that are unclear or confusing?
- How does the proposed method compare to other image representation and compression methods, such as DCT and DWT, in terms of effectiveness and efficiency?
- Could the authors provide a more comprehensive discussion on the limitations of their method, including potential challenges and future research directions?
- How does the proposed method perform in terms of computational efficiency and training time compared to other methods like FINOLA?
- Can the authors clarify the role and significance of the learnable speeds in the context of the hidden waves phenomenon?
- How does the proposed method handle different types of images, such as natural images versus text images, and what are the implications of these differences for the reconstruction process?
