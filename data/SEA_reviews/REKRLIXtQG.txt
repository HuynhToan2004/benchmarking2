 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel concept of "supermodular rank" for set functions, which is a generalization of the submodular rank. This concept is used to decompose set functions into a sum of at most r supermodular functions, each corresponding to a supermodular cone. The authors propose algorithms for monotone set function optimization, including the r-split and r-split ratio algorithms, which provide theoretical guarantees and are supported by experiments on determinantal, Bayesian, column, and random set functions. The paper also explores the theoretical aspects of supermodular rank and its implications for the optimization of set functions.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel concept of supermodular rank for set functions, which is a significant generalization of the submodular rank.
- The authors provide a detailed analysis of the supermodular rank, including the maximum supermodular rank and the maximum elementary supermodular rank.
- The paper presents a decomposition of set functions using supermodular rank, which can be used to optimize set functions with theoretical guarantees.
- The proposed r-split and r-split ratio algorithms are well-supported by theoretical guarantees and are shown to be effective in experiments.
- The paper is well-written, making it easy to follow, and the theoretical results are supported by experiments on determinantal, Bayesian, column, and random set functions.
