 **Summary:**
The paper explores the capabilities of transformer models in learning arithmetic algorithms, specifically focusing on generalization to long input lengths. It introduces two methods: Attention Bias Scaffolding (ABS) and Attention Bias Calibration (ABC), aimed at enhancing transformer models' ability to generalize to longer inputs. The paper presents extensive experiments on various arithmetic tasks, demonstrating that the proposed methods can achieve perfect generalization to inputs with up to 50 digits. The authors also discuss the attention patterns learned by transformers and how these patterns can be leveraged to improve generalization. The paper is well-written, clear, and presents a novel approach to addressing the challenge of length generalization in transformers.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, clear, and easy to follow, with detailed explanations of the methods and experiments.
- The experiments are extensive, covering various tasks and showing the effectiveness of the proposed methods.
- The paper addresses an important and challenging problem in the field of transformers, specifically focusing on length generalization in arithmetic tasks.
- The proposed methods, such as Attention Bias Scaffolding (ABS) and Attention Bias Calibration (ABC), are novel and provide a new perspective on enhancing transformer models' capabilities.
- The paper provides a comprehensive analysis of attention patterns and their impact on model performance, which is a significant contribution to the field.
