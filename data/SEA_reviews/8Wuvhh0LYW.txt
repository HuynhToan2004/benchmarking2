 **Summary:**
The paper introduces OmniQuant, a novel quantization technique for large language models (LLMs) that optimizes weight and activation quantization through learnable weight clipping and equivalent transformation. OmniQuant aims to achieve high performance in diverse quantization settings while maintaining computational efficiency. The method is evaluated across various LLMs, including LLaMA, OPT, and Falcon, demonstrating improvements over existing methods. Despite its strengths, the paper has been criticized for lacking comprehensive comparisons with state-of-the-art methods and for not providing sufficient ablation studies to validate the effectiveness of its components. The presentation and clarity of the paper also need improvement, particularly in the explanation of certain equations and methodological choices.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel quantization technique, OmniQuant, which optimizes weight and activation quantization through learnable weight clipping and equivalent transformation.
- Extensive experiments validate OmniQuant's superior performance across diverse quantization configurations and various model families, demonstrating improvements in inference speed and memory reduction on real devices.
- The methodology is clearly presented, with a well-structured paper that makes it easy to follow.
- The paper is well-written, with a clear introduction of the problem and the motivation for the proposed method.
- The experiments are comprehensive, covering a wide range of quantization settings and various model families.
