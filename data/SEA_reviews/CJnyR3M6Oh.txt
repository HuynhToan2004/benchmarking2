 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel sparse learning scheme for hyperbolic space-based representation learning (HSBRL), focusing on minimizing the dimension for each representation to reduce space complexity. This is achieved by defining sparsity and regularization in hyperbolic space and proposing a new optimization algorithm, HISTA, which avoids oscillations and achieves sparse representations. The authors address challenges such as the lack of a canonical coordinate system and the inability of existing gradient descent algorithms to reach sparse solutions. The paper provides theoretical insights into the sparsity and regularization in hyperbolic space, alongside a new optimization algorithm. However, the paper lacks comprehensive experimental validation, particularly in practical applications and comparisons with existing methods.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, clear, and easy to follow, making complex concepts accessible to readers.
- It introduces novel geometric definitions of sparsity and regularization in hyperbolic space, which are significant contributions to the field.
- The authors provide a closed-form formula for their method, which is computationally efficient and can be calculated in linear time/space complexity for the space dimension.
- The paper addresses the challenges of defining sparsity and regularization in hyperbolic space, which are crucial for reducing space complexity in HSBRL.
- The proposed algorithm is a novel extension of the Iterative Shrinkage-Thresholding Algorithm (ISTA) to the hyperbolic space, which is a significant advancement in the field.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a comprehensive experimental validation, particularly in practical applications and comparisons with existing methods.
- There is a need for more detailed discussions on the computational complexity and the convergence analysis of the proposed algorithm.
- The paper does not provide sufficient theoretical support for the effectiveness of the proposed method in reducing space complexity.
- The definition of sparsity and the regularization term are not clearly defined or justified, which could limit the understanding and applicability of the proposed method.
- The paper does not sufficiently discuss the limitations of the proposed method, particularly in terms of its applicability to different types of Riemannian manifolds.
- The paper could benefit from more detailed comparisons with existing methods, such as the Riemannian proximal gradient descent (RPG) descent, to establish the superiority or novelty of the proposed method.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors provide more detailed discussions on the computational complexity of their proposed method and the convergence analysis?
- How does the proposed method compare with existing methods, particularly in terms of computational efficiency and practical applications?
- Could the authors clarify the definitions of sparsity and the regularization term, and provide examples or theoretical support for their effectiveness in reducing space complexity?
- Is there a specific reason why the paper does not include experiments in the main text, and could more detailed results be provided in the supplementary materials?
- How does the proposed method handle different types of Riemannian manifolds, particularly those not covered by the Cartan-Hadamard manifold?
- Could the authors provide a more detailed comparison with existing methods, such as the Riemannian proximal gradient descent (RPG) descent, to establish the superiority or novelty of their proposed method?
