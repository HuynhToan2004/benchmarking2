 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel adversarial attack method for Spiking Neural Networks (SNNs) that leverages both rate and temporal information. The authors propose a hybrid adversarial attack (HART) that dynamically adjusts the rate and temporal attributes of SNNs, aiming to enhance the robustness of SNNs against adversarial attacks. The method is supported by extensive theoretical analysis and experimental results that demonstrate the superiority of HART over existing methods. The paper also discusses the vulnerability of SNNs under adversarial attacks and the potential of combining rate and temporal information to improve the reliability of SNNs.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, easy to follow, and provides a detailed analysis of rate and temporal information in SNNs, which is crucial for understanding the vulnerabilities and strengths of SNNs.
- The proposed method, HART, is novel and effective, achieving state-of-the-art attack success rates (ASR) across various hyper-parameter settings for both static and neuromorphic datasets.
- The paper provides a theoretical proof that the proposed method is mathematically equivalent to the rate gradient, which adds credibility to the proposed method.
- The analysis of the retention degree of temporal information in SNNs and its correlation with factors such as the membrane decay constant and the number of time-steps is insightful and contributes to a better understanding of SNNs.
- The paper is well-organized, with clear explanations of the proposed method and its components, which makes it accessible and understandable to readers.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed discussion on the potential applications of the proposed gradient techniques to SNN training, which could be a significant contribution to the field.
- The experimental results are not sufficiently comprehensive, as they are limited to a few datasets and do not include results on more complex datasets such as MNIST, CIFAR-100, or ImageNet.
- The paper does not adequately address the computational complexity of the proposed method, which could be a significant limitation for practical applications.
- There is a lack of discussion on the potential limitations or negative societal impacts of the proposed method, which is essential for a complete evaluation of the proposed approach.
- The paper could benefit from a more detailed comparison with existing methods, especially in terms of the attack success rates and the robustness of the SNNs under different attack scenarios.

**Questions:**
- How does the proposed method compare with existing gradient techniques for SNN training, particularly in terms of computational complexity and effectiveness?
- Can the authors provide more detailed results on the robustness of SNNs under different attack scenarios, such as different perturbation degrees and time-steps?
- How does the proposed method perform on more complex datasets such as MNIST, CIFAR-100, or ImageNet?
- Could the authors discuss the potential limitations or negative societal impacts of the proposed method, and how these might be mitigated or addressed?
- In the context of the proposed method, how does the retention degree of temporal information affect the performance of SNNs under different attack scenarios?
- How does the proposed method compare with other methods that leverage rate and temporal information for adversarial attacks on SNNs, such as the method proposed by Bu et al. (2023)?
