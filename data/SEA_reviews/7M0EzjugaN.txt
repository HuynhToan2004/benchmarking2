 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel approach to continual learning for embodied agents, focusing on two distinct scenarios: Behavior-IL and Environment-IL. It proposes a Confidence-Aware Moving Average (CAMA) method to dynamically update logits, which is compared against several baseline methods. The paper presents a benchmark for evaluating continual learning in embodied agents and demonstrates that the proposed method outperforms existing approaches. However, concerns are raised about the novelty and depth of the method, as well as the limited scope of the evaluation.

**Strengths:**
- The paper addresses an important and understudied problem in the context of embodied AI, focusing on continual learning, which is crucial for real-world applications.
- The proposed benchmark is comprehensive and could be useful for future research in this area.
- The paper is well-written, with clear descriptions of the methodology and results, and includes a detailed analysis of the results.
- The proposed method, CAMA, is simple and effective, and the authors provide a detailed explanation of how it works.
- The paper presents a novel approach to continual learning for embodied agents, which is a significant advancement in the field.

**Weaknesses:**
- The novelty of the proposed method is limited, as it appears to be a simple combination of existing techniques without significant innovation.
- The paper lacks a detailed comparison with state-of-the-art methods, which could have provided a clearer picture of the method's effectiveness.
- The evaluation of the method is limited to a single dataset and does not consider more challenging or varied environments, which could affect the generalizability of the results.
- The method's reliance on confidence scores for logit update raises concerns about the method's robustness and the potential for overconfident models to lead to catastrophic forgetting.
- The paper does not adequately address the issue of catastrophic forgetting, which is a critical aspect of continual learning.
- There are some issues with the clarity and organization of the paper, including a lack of detailed descriptions in some sections and a need for better presentation of figures and tables.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors clarify how the confidence scores are calculated and how they are used to determine the weighting of logits?
- How does the proposed method compare to other state-of-the-art methods in terms of performance and computational efficiency?
- Could the authors provide more detailed comparisons with other methods, particularly those that use different datasets or more varied environments?
- How does the method handle the issue of catastrophic forgetting, and what measures have been taken to mitigate this risk?
- The paper mentions the use of confidence scores to update logits; could the authors provide more details on how this is done and how it affects the learning process?
- How does the method perform in more challenging or varied environments, and what steps have been taken to ensure that the method can generalize to new scenarios?
