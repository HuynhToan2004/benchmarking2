 **Summary:**
The paper introduces Group Preference Optimization (GPO), a novel framework aimed at aligning large language models (LLMs) with the preferences of specific groups, such as demographic groups or individual users. GPO operates by training an independent transformer module that predicts group preferences based on LLM generations, using a few-shot learning approach. The method is evaluated against various baselines and shown to outperform them, demonstrating its effectiveness and efficiency in adapting to different groups. The approach is validated on two datasets and is noted for its ability to align LLMs with minimal training and inference resources, making it suitable for real-world applications where group-specific preferences are crucial.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written and easy to follow, with clear explanations of the proposed method and its evaluation.
- The proposed method, Group Preference Optimization (GPO), is novel and effective, particularly in aligning language models with group preferences, which is a significant and relevant problem.
- GPO is evaluated on two datasets, demonstrating its applicability and robustness across different scenarios.
- The paper includes a comprehensive evaluation against various baselines, showing that GPO outperforms them, highlighting its effectiveness.
- The methodology is straightforward and does not require extensive training or inference resources, making it practical for real-world applications.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed discussion on how GPO performs with different types of data or preferences, such as short-answer questions or relative pairwise responses.
- There is a lack of clarity on the scalability of GPO with larger language models, which could affect its applicability in various scenarios.
- The paper does not provide sufficient details on how GPO handles different types of responses or preferences, which could impact its effectiveness.
- The evaluation is limited to specific datasets and does not include a broader range of scenarios or applications, which could enhance the understanding of GPO's capabilities.
- The paper could benefit from a more detailed discussion on the limitations and potential negative societal impacts of using GPO, particularly in terms of aligning LLMs with harmful or biased preferences.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors provide more details on how GPO performs with different types of data or preferences, such as short-answer questions or relative pairwise responses?
- How does GPO scale with larger language models, and what are the implications for its applicability in various scenarios?
- Could the authors elaborate on the choice of evaluation metrics and how they align with the overall objectives of the study?
- What are the potential negative societal impacts of using GPO to align LLMs with group preferences, and how might these be mitigated?
- How does the proposed method compare to other existing methods in terms of computational efficiency and effectiveness in real-world applications?
