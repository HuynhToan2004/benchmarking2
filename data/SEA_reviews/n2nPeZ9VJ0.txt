 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel prompting strategy called Structured Rationale Responses (SRR) aimed at enhancing the performance of Large Language Models (LLMs) in analyzing Electronic Health Records (EHRs). The SRR strategy guides LLMs to generate structured responses in JSON format and provides rationales from EHR notes to support their answers, which are crucial for accurate diagnosis in medical settings. The methodology was evaluated using a dataset of 499 EHR notes from patients with life-threatening heart rhythm disorders, showing improvements in accuracy and reliability compared to standard prompts and other baseline methods. The paper also discusses the potential of SRR in various structured formats and different LLMs, suggesting its applicability in broader medical contexts.

**Strengths:**
- The paper presents a novel prompting strategy, Structured Rationale Responses (SRR), which guides LLMs to generate structured responses in JSON format and provides rationales from EHR notes to support their answers, enhancing the accuracy and reliability of LLM responses to nuanced inquiries in EHRs.
- The methodology was evaluated using a dataset of 499 EHR notes from patients with life-threatening heart rhythm disorders, demonstrating improvements in accuracy and reliability compared to standard prompts and other baseline methods.
- The paper discusses the potential of SRR in various structured formats and different LLMs, suggesting its applicability in broader medical contexts.
- The paper is well-written, with clear presentation of the methodology, results, and analysis, making it accessible and easy to follow.
- The study is significant as it addresses the limitations of current prompting strategies in analyzing important datasets that are not publicly available, such as electronic health records, which are crucial for medical diagnosis and treatment.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks clarity on how the structured output format (JSON) specifically enhances the performance of LLMs in medical diagnosis tasks compared to other structured formats like YAML or INI.
- The paper does not provide sufficient detail on the process of annotating the dataset, which is crucial for understanding the reliability and validity of the results.
- The evaluation of the proposed method is limited to a single task and a small dataset, raising concerns about the generalizability of the findings.
- The paper does not discuss the limitations of the methodology or the potential societal impact of the work, which could be significant in the medical field.
- The paper does not compare the proposed method with other state-of-the-art methods, which could provide a more robust evaluation of the proposed method.

**Questions:**
- How does the structured output format (JSON) specifically enhance the performance of LLMs in medical diagnosis tasks compared to other structured formats like YAML or INI?
- Could you provide more details on the process of annotating the dataset, including the number of annotators, inter-annotator agreement, and any quality control measures taken to ensure the reliability and validity of the annotations?
- How does the proposed method perform on other tasks or datasets, and what are the potential limitations or challenges in applying the method to different medical contexts or languages?
- Could you elaborate on the rationale behind the choice of the JSON format and how it contributes to the overall effectiveness of the SRR strategy?
- How does the proposed method compare to other state-of-the-art methods in terms of accuracy, reliability, and generalizability?
- Could you discuss the potential societal impact of the work and any limitations or ethical considerations related to the use of LLMs in medical diagnosis and treatment?
