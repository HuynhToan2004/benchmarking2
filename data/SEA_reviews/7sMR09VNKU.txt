 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel approach to learning the dynamics of nonlinear systems from sensory inputs, focusing on linearizing the dynamics in a feature space to enhance controllability. The methodology involves encoding system states and control inputs into a higher-dimensional space, where the dynamics are assumed to be linear. This approach is supported by an autoencoder-based system that maps states and controls to their respective feature spaces, and a linear quadratic regulator (LQR) controller is used to optimize the control signals. The paper validates the proposed method through simulations on benchmark systems like pendulum and cartpole, demonstrating improved controllability over traditional methods. However, concerns were raised regarding the practical applicability of the method, especially in real-world scenarios, and the need for a more rigorous experimental validation.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, making it easy to follow and understand.
- The proposed method is novel and innovative, introducing a new approach to learning the dynamics of nonlinear systems.
- The methodology is supported by a theoretical framework, which enhances its credibility.
- The paper includes comprehensive experiments, including ablation studies, which help in validating the proposed method.
- The approach of learning a representation space where the dynamics are forced to be linear and controllable in that space is a significant contribution to the field.
- The paper effectively integrates elements of Koopman operator theory, linear dynamics, and control, which are crucial for understanding and manipulating complex dynamical systems.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed discussion on the limitations of the proposed method, which could be crucial for understanding its applicability and effectiveness in real-world scenarios.
- The experimental results are not convincing enough, particularly in demonstrating the superiority of the proposed method over existing approaches.
- The paper does not sufficiently compare its method with other state-of-the-art methods, which could help in understanding its relative performance.
- The paper does not provide sufficient details on the implementation and practical application of the method, which could limit its reproducibility and applicability.
- The method's reliance on a feature space where the dynamics are linear may not be practical in many real-world scenarios, where the dynamics are inherently nonlinear.
- The paper's focus on simulation experiments may not adequately demonstrate the effectiveness of the method in real-world applications.
