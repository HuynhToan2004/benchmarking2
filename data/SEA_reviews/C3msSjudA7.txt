 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel method, ViFu, for segmenting and recovering a static, clean background and 360-degree objects from multiple scene observations. This is achieved by fusing visible parts from each scene to create a background that is occlusion-free. The method involves a visibility field to quantify the visibility of radiance fields and a visibility-aware rendering technique for multiple scene fusion. The approach is evaluated on both synthetic and real datasets, demonstrating the effectiveness of the proposed method. However, the paper has been critiqued for its limited novelty, unclear presentation, and lack of comparison with existing methods.

**Strengths:**
- The paper addresses a novel and interesting problem of segmenting and recovering a static, clean background and 360-degree objects from multiple scene observations.
- The proposed method is innovative, combining ideas from visibility field and visibility-aware rendering, which are well-explained and easy to follow.
- The paper is well-written, with clear figures and a logical structure that facilitates understanding.
- The method is validated on both synthetic and real datasets, showing good results.
- The paper introduces a novel method for segmenting and recovering a static, clean background and 360-degree objects from multiple scene observations, which is a significant contribution to the field.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a thorough comparison with existing methods, such as NeRF-in and SPin-NeRF, which also address the issue of under-modeled parts in NeRF.
- The methodology and results are not clearly presented, with some sections, such as the description of the method and the results, being unclear or difficult to follow.
- The paper does not adequately discuss the limitations of the proposed method, which could affect the reliability and applicability of the findings.
- The paper does not include a discussion on the time complexity of the method, which is crucial for understanding its scalability and efficiency.
- The paper does not include a discussion on the generalizability of the method to different types of scenes and objects, which could limit its applicability.
- The paper does not include a discussion on the limitations of the proposed method, which could affect the reliability and applicability of the findings.
