 **Summary:**
The paper introduces Hybrid Granularity Distribution Estimation (HGDE), a method that estimates distributions at both coarse-grained category and fine-grained instance levels to enhance few-shot learning. HGDE integrates statistics from different granularity levels through linear interpolation, aiming to provide a more representative description of novel categories. The method is evaluated on four FSL benchmarks, demonstrating improvements in recognition accuracy. Despite its novel approach, the paper's methodology is seen as a straightforward combination of existing methods, and the experimental results do not convincingly surpass state-of-the-art methods.

**Strengths:**
- The paper addresses the issue of the discrepancy between base categories and novel categories in few-shot learning, proposing a method that considers both coarse-grained category-level and fine-grained instance-level distributions.
- The method is simple yet effective, as demonstrated by its ability to improve few-shot learning performance across multiple datasets.
- The paper is well-written, making it easy to follow and understand, and includes comprehensive ablation studies that verify the effectiveness of the proposed method.
- The method is flexible and can be applied to various few-shot learning methods, enhancing its applicability and versatility.

**Weaknesses:**
- The method's novelty is limited, as it appears to be a straightforward combination of existing methods, primarily combining category-level distribution estimation and instance-level distribution estimation.
- The paper lacks a clear justification for the use of both category-level and instance-level distribution estimations, and the experimental results do not convincingly surpass state-of-the-art methods.
- The assumption that the feature vectors follow a Gaussian distribution may not hold true in all scenarios, which could limit the applicability of the method.
- The paper does not sufficiently compare its method with existing data augmentation techniques, nor does it provide sufficient ablation studies to verify the effectiveness of each component of the method.
- The method's reliance on linear interpolation for fusing statistics from different levels may not be the most effective method, and the paper does not explore other potential methods such as weighted interpolation.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors provide more details on the choice of using both category-level and instance-level distribution estimations, and how this combination enhances the method's effectiveness?
- How does the proposed method perform when compared with other data augmentation techniques, and could the authors provide a more detailed comparison?
- In the ablation studies, could the authors include results for a wider range of values for the hyperparameters to better understand the sensitivity of the method to these parameters?
- How does the method perform when compared with other state-of-the-art methods, and could the authors provide a more detailed comparison to highlight the advantages of their method?
- Could the authors provide more details on the experimental setup, such as the specifics of the feature extractors used and the training and testing data splits?
