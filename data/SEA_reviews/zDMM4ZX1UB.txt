 **Summary:**
The paper introduces SymC, a novel LLM architecture that incorporates group-equivariant self-attention to address the challenge of program analysis in code LLMs. SymC is designed to preserve program symmetries, which are critical for understanding code behavior across different transformations. The authors demonstrate that SymC outperforms existing models like GPT-4 and WizardCoder in various code analysis tasks, showing a significant improvement in generalization to unseen code transformations. The approach is evaluated on four program analysis tasks, including function name prediction, function similarity detection, function signature prediction, and memory region prediction, and shows superior performance compared to other LLMs.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, clear, and easy to follow, with a logical structure that aids in understanding the complex concepts presented.
- The proposed approach is novel and innovative, utilizing group-equivariant self-attention to preserve program symmetries, which is a significant advancement in the field of code LLMs.
- The paper provides a comprehensive evaluation of the proposed model, SymC, across various code transformations, demonstrating its effectiveness in generalizing to unseen code transformations.
- The paper is well-motivated, with a clear definition of the problem and a well-articulated solution that addresses the limitations of existing approaches.
- The experimental results are robust and convincing, showing that SymC outperforms existing models in terms of performance and resource efficiency.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed discussion on the limitations of the proposed approach, which could help in understanding the scope and applicability of the findings.
- The paper could benefit from a more comprehensive comparison with other state-of-the-art models, particularly those that also incorporate code symmetries in their design.
- The paper does not provide a detailed analysis of the computational complexity of the proposed method, which could be crucial for understanding its practical applicability in different scenarios.
- There is a need for more detailed explanations and justifications for certain methodological choices, such as the use of different types of self-attention layers and the choice of code representation units.
- The paper could benefit from a more thorough discussion on the potential negative societal impacts of the proposed technology, especially in areas like security and privacy.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors provide a more detailed analysis of the limitations of the proposed approach, particularly in terms of its applicability to different types of code and transformations?
- How does the proposed method compare in terms of computational complexity and resource requirements with other state-of-the-art models?
- Could the authors elaborate on the choice of using different types of self-attention layers and the impact of this choice on the performance and efficiency of the model?
- In the context of the paper's claims about the generalization of SymC to unseen code transformations, how does the model perform when faced with a completely new type of transformation that was not part of the training data?
- How does the proposed method handle the potential negative societal impacts, particularly in areas like security and privacy?
