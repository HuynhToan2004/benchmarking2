 **Summary:**
The paper introduces DiffusionShield, a novel watermarking method designed to protect images from copyright infringement by Generative Diffusion Models (GDMs). The method leverages pattern uniformity to ensure watermarks are consistently reproduced across different images, enhancing the reliability of watermark detection. It also employs a joint optimization method to optimize the watermark and decoder simultaneously, improving the robustness and invisibility of the watermark. The effectiveness of DiffusionShield is demonstrated through extensive experiments, showcasing its superiority over traditional watermarking methods and its ability to embed long messages without significant distortion. However, concerns about the practicality of the method due to its reliance on specific image formats and the potential for image quality degradation remain.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel watermarking method, DiffusionShield, which is specifically designed to protect images from copyright infringement by Generative Diffusion Models (GDMs).
- The method is based on the principle of pattern uniformity, which makes the watermark easily learnable by GDMs and effectively detectable in generated images.
- The paper provides theoretical analysis to support the design of the watermark and presents a joint optimization method to enhance the performance of the watermark and decoder.
- Extensive experiments are conducted to demonstrate the effectiveness of DiffusionShield in defending against infringement by GDMs and its superiority over traditional watermarking methods.
- The paper is well-organized, clearly written, and includes a comprehensive literature review, making it accessible and informative for readers.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a clear explanation of the practical implementation of the watermarking method, particularly how it is applied to images in different formats such as JPEG or PNG.
- The method's effectiveness is limited by the image quality, as adding a watermark might lead to noticeable distortions, which could affect the usability of the images.
- The paper does not adequately address the issue of watermark invisibility, which is crucial for practical applications.
- The paper's contribution is seen as limited due to the simplicity of the watermarking method and the lack of significant technical innovation.
- There is a lack of discussion on the potential negative societal impacts of the proposed method.
- The paper does not adequately address the limitations of the proposed method, particularly in terms of its applicability to different image formats and its robustness against various image processing techniques.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors clarify how the watermarking method is applied to images in different formats such as JPEG or PNG?
- How does the paper address the issue of watermark invisibility, and what measures are taken to ensure that the watermark does not significantly degrade the image quality?
- Could the authors provide more details on the practical implementation of the watermarking method, including the specific steps and tools used?
- How does the proposed method compare to existing watermarking techniques in terms of effectiveness, efficiency, and practicality?
- Can the authors discuss the potential negative societal impacts of their proposed method and how these impacts might be mitigated?
- How does the proposed method handle cases where the watermarked images are subjected to various image processing techniques, such as JPEG compression or low-pass filtering?
