 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a method for zero-shot grounded text-to-image generation that utilizes region-aware and boundary-aware cross-attention guidance to align the diffusion model's attention maps with layout information. This approach aims to enhance the spatial accuracy and semantic fidelity of the generated images by refining the attention maps through a dynamic thresholding process. The methodology involves the use of a region-aware loss and a boundary-aware loss to guide the model's attention towards specific regions and boundaries, thereby improving the generated images' adherence to the given layout constraints. The paper also includes extensive experiments and ablation studies to demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed method.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel method for zero-shot grounded text-to-image generation that does not require additional training, utilizing region-aware and boundary-aware cross-attention guidance to align the model's attention maps with layout information.
- The method is well-motivated, with clear and detailed descriptions of the proposed method and the underlying principles, making it easy to follow and understand.
- Extensive experiments and ablation studies are conducted to demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed method, including comparisons with state-of-the-art methods and analysis of different loss components.
- The paper is well-written, with clear and concise descriptions of the methodology, and includes sufficient details and figures to aid in understanding the proposed approach.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a thorough analysis of the limitations of the proposed method, particularly in scenarios where the method may not perform optimally, such as with complex or ambiguous layout constraints.
- The novelty of the method is questionable, as similar approaches have been previously explored in other works, such as those mentioned in the paper.
- The paper does not provide a detailed comparison with other baseline methods, particularly in terms of computational efficiency and the impact of different hyperparameters on the performance.
- The paper could benefit from more detailed explanations and examples of the boundary-aware loss and the dynamic thresholding approach, as these are crucial components of the proposed method.
- The paper's presentation could be improved, particularly in the clarity of the figures and the organization of the text, to enhance readability and comprehension.

**Questions:**
- How does the proposed method handle complex or ambiguous layout constraints, and what are the potential limitations in such scenarios?
- Could the authors provide more detailed examples and explanations of the boundary-aware loss and the dynamic thresholding approach to better illustrate their effectiveness and implementation?
- How does the proposed method compare in terms of computational efficiency and other practical considerations, such as the impact of different hyperparameters on the performance?
- Could the authors clarify the role and impact of the "guidance ratio" in the proposed method, and how it influences the performance of the model?
- In the ablation study, what are the specific effects of the "guidance ratio" on the performance of the model, and how does it compare to other loss components?
