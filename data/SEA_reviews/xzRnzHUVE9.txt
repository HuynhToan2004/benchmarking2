 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel method called Symmetric Replay Training (SRT) aimed at enhancing the sample efficiency of Deep Reinforcement Learning (DRL) in combinatorial optimization (CO) problems. SRT leverages the symmetry of solutions in CO problems to generate high-reward trajectories without additional reward evaluations. The method is evaluated across various benchmarks, including traveling salesman problems and hardware design, demonstrating consistent improvements in sample efficiency. Despite its innovative approach, the paper is criticized for its lack of theoretical rigor, unclear methodological explanations, and insufficient experimental validation. The presentation and clarity of the paper are also noted as needing improvement.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, easy to follow, and provides a clear description of the proposed method.
- The motivation behind the research is well-articulated, focusing on the need for efficient exploration in combinatorial optimization problems with expensive reward functions.
- The paper is original in its approach, introducing the concept of symmetric replay training, which is a novel method for improving sample efficiency in reinforcement learning.
- The experimental results demonstrate that the proposed method outperforms baseline methods, particularly in hardware design and de novo molecular optimization, showing a significant improvement in sample efficiency.
- The paper includes a detailed appendix with additional experiments and supplementary materials, which provide a more comprehensive understanding of the method's effectiveness.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks theoretical rigor, particularly in the proofs and explanations of the proposed methods, which could undermine the credibility of the results.
- The experimental results are not sufficiently robust, with insufficient variance in the results, which makes it difficult to assess the significance of the improvements claimed.
- The paper does not adequately address the scalability of the proposed method, which is crucial for its practical application in large-scale combinatorial optimization problems.
- The paper does not provide sufficient details on the computational costs and the scalability of the method, which are essential for understanding its practical applicability.
- The paper lacks a thorough discussion of related works, particularly those that address similar issues in combinatorial optimization and reinforcement learning.
- The presentation of the paper could be improved, with more detailed explanations and clearer figures to enhance understanding and clarity.
