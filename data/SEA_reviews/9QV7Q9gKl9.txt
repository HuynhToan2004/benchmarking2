 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel neural LNS solver named DIFUSCO-LNS, which integrates a diffusion model to learn high-quality destroy policies for Large Neighborhood Search (LNS) in Integer Linear Programming (ILP). The method leverages the strengths of both traditional solvers in symbolic reasoning and the generative power of neural diffusion models to enhance the performance of LNS. The approach is tested on four representative MIP problems, showing improvements over prior neural LNS solvers. However, the paper is criticized for its limited evaluation scope and the lack of a comprehensive comparison with state-of-the-art methods.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel approach by combining traditional LNS with a diffusion model, which is a significant advancement in the field of ILP.
- The use of diffusion models to learn high-quality destroy policies is innovative and shows potential for enhancing the performance of LNS.
- The method is well-motivated and the paper is well-written, making it accessible and easy to follow.
- The experiments demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed method, showing improvements over prior neural LNS solvers.
- The paper is well-positioned within the existing literature and provides a clear explanation of the methodology and its application.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a comprehensive evaluation, particularly in terms of the number of instances used and the diversity of benchmarks.
- There is a lack of comparison with state-of-the-art methods, which limits the understanding of the method's performance relative to current best practices.
- The paper could benefit from a more detailed discussion on the limitations and potential negative societal impacts of the proposed method.
- The presentation of the paper could be improved, particularly in terms of clarity and the organization of the content.
- The paper does not adequately address the computational complexity and time efficiency of the proposed method, which is critical for practical applications.
- The paper does not discuss the limitations of the proposed method in detail, which is essential for a complete understanding of its applicability and effectiveness.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors provide more details on the experimental setup, including the specific instances used and the computational resources required?
- How does the proposed method compare to other state-of-the-art methods, particularly in terms of performance and computational efficiency?
- What are the specific advantages of using diffusion models over other machine learning techniques for learning destroy policies?
- Could the authors discuss the potential negative societal impacts of the proposed method and how these might be mitigated?
- How does the proposed method handle the multimodal nature of optimal policies during variable selection, and what are the implications for the quality of the solutions?
- Can the authors provide more details on the training data used to train the diffusion model, particularly how it was collected and the quality of the solutions it represents?
