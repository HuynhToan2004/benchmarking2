 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel method for knowledge distillation (KD) that addresses the issue of fairness in student models by selectively distilling teacher features that are not correlated with protected attributes. This method, termed "BIRD," utilizes a meta-learning approach to update the FairDistill operator, which selectively distills fair and accurate teacher features. The framework aims to mitigate the bias inherited from the teacher model by focusing on uncorrelated features, thereby enhancing fairness without compromising predictive performance. The methodology is evaluated across various datasets and shows improvements in fairness metrics compared to existing KD methods. However, the paper has been criticized for its clarity in explaining the methodology, the necessity of the meta-learning approach, and the empirical evidence supporting the claims.

**Strengths:**
- The paper addresses a significant and timely issue of fairness in knowledge distillation, which is an essential area of research given the prevalence of large-scale foundation models.
- The methodology is well-motivated, with a clear focus on mitigating bias in student models through a novel approach that selectively distills unbiased teacher features.
- The paper is well-written and easy to follow, with a clear structure and logical flow that aids in understanding the proposed method.
- The empirical results are convincing, demonstrating the effectiveness of the proposed method in improving fairness in knowledge distillation across various datasets.
- The paper provides a comprehensive evaluation of the proposed method, comparing it to several baseline methods and demonstrating its effectiveness in improving fairness metrics.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks clarity in explaining the meta-learning approach and the necessity of using meta-learning for the FairDistill operator. More detailed explanations and justifications are needed.
- The methodology, particularly the FairDistill operator and the use of meta-gradients, is not clearly explained, which could hinder understanding and reproducibility.
- The paper does not provide sufficient empirical evidence to support the claim that the proposed method selectively distills unbiased teacher features. More detailed analysis and experimental evidence are required.
- The paper does not compare the proposed method with other relevant fairness-aware distillation methods, which could provide a more robust evaluation of its effectiveness.
- The computational complexity and efficiency of the proposed method are not discussed, which is crucial for practical deployment.
- The paper does not adequately address the limitations of the proposed method, which could include the potential for overfitting due to the selective distillation of uncorrelated features.

**Questions:**
- Could you provide more detailed explanations and justifications for the use of meta-learning in the FairDistill operator?
- How does the proposed method ensure that it selectively distills unbiased teacher features, and what empirical evidence supports this claim?
- Could you compare the proposed method with other fairness-aware distillation methods to provide a more robust evaluation of its effectiveness?
- What are the specific computational complexities and efficiency of the proposed method, and how do these compare to existing methods?
- Can you discuss the potential limitations of the proposed method, such as the risk of overfitting due to selective distillation?
- How does the proposed method perform when the teacher model is trained on a biased dataset, and what are the implications for fairness in this scenario?
