 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel stochastic path following quantization (SPFQ) algorithm for post-training quantization of deep neural networks. This algorithm extends the Greedy Path Following Quantization (GPFQ) approach by incorporating a stochastic quantizer, which is analyzed theoretically to provide error bounds for the quantization process. The analysis is based on the stochastic quantizer's properties and is applied to both infinite and finite alphabet cases. The paper also explores the computational complexity of the SPFQ algorithm, which is linear with the number of weights, making it efficient for large networks. Despite its theoretical contributions, the paper lacks empirical validation, which is crucial for demonstrating the practical effectiveness of the proposed method.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel approach to quantization by using a stochastic quantizer, which is a significant departure from traditional methods.
- The theoretical analysis provided is robust and well-structured, with clear explanations and logical proofs that support the claims made.
- The paper is well-written, making it easy to follow, and the theoretical results are presented in a clear and concise manner.
- The proposed algorithm is simple, intuitive, and easy to implement, which could facilitate its adoption in practical applications.
- The introduction of stochastic quantization in the context of neural network quantization is a novel and interesting idea that could potentially enhance the efficiency and effectiveness of the quantization process.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks empirical validation, which is crucial for demonstrating the practical effectiveness of the proposed method.
- The assumptions made in the theoretical analysis, such as the randomness of weights and the boundedness of weights, are strong and may not hold in real-world scenarios.
- The paper does not discuss the computational complexity of the proposed algorithm, which is a significant omission given the importance of efficiency in practical applications.
- The paper does not provide a comprehensive comparison with existing methods, which could help in understanding the advantages and disadvantages of the proposed method.
- The paper is limited to neural networks with ReLU activations, which may not be applicable to other types of neural networks.
- The paper does not discuss the limitations of the proposed method, which could help in understanding the scope and applicability of the results.
