 **Summary:**
The paper explores the vulnerability of large language and vision-language models to adversarial attacks that involve permuting the order of answer choices in multiple-choice question answering tasks. The study demonstrates that these models are highly susceptible to such attacks, often leading to a significant decrease in accuracy below the random chance level. The authors also investigate the impact of answer set pruning and other post-hoc strategies to mitigate these vulnerabilities. The paper provides extensive empirical evidence across various models and datasets, highlighting the need for improved robustness in these models against such attacks.

**Strengths:**
- The paper presents a significant and timely study on the robustness of large language models (LLMs) and vision-language models (VLLMs) against adversarial attacks, focusing on the vulnerability of these models to simple permutations in answer sets.
- The paper provides a comprehensive evaluation of various LLMs and VLLMs across different datasets, demonstrating the vulnerability of these models to adversarial permutation attacks.
- The findings of the paper are well-supported by extensive empirical evidence, showing that even with reduced answer sets, models remain susceptible to adversarial attacks.
- The paper is well-written, making it easy to follow and understand, and the experiments are clearly explained.
- The paper addresses a critical issue that is often overlooked in the deployment of LLMs and VLLMs in real-world applications.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed analysis of the causes behind the observed vulnerabilities, which could include a deeper exploration of the model's decision-making process, the impact of positional bias, and the role of other factors such as distractor similarity.
- The paper does not propose any new defense mechanisms or strategies to mitigate the vulnerabilities, limiting its practical applicability.
- The paper's focus on permutation attacks might not fully capture the complexity of real-world adversarial scenarios, which could involve more sophisticated attacks.
- The paper could benefit from a more thorough discussion of related work and a clearer distinction from existing studies.
- The paper's methodology and results are somewhat limited by the simplicity of the adversarial attack, which might not reflect the complexity of real-world adversarial scenarios.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors provide a more detailed analysis of the causes behind the observed vulnerabilities, particularly the role of positional bias and the impact of distractor similarity?
- How do the findings of this study compare with other recent studies on robustness in LLMs and VLLMs?
- In light of the observed vulnerabilities, what new defense mechanisms or strategies could be proposed to mitigate these vulnerabilities?
- How does the paper's methodology and results compare with other studies that use more complex adversarial attacks, such as those involving multiple permutations or more sophisticated adversarial strategies?
- Could the authors clarify the terminology used in the paper, particularly the use of "adversarial" to describe a simple permutation attack?
