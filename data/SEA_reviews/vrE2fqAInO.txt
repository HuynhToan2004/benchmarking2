 **Summary:**
The paper investigates the problem of best arm identification (BAI) in the context of linear bandits under differential privacy (DP) constraints. It introduces a novel algorithm, DP-BAI, which maximizes the determinant of the subsets of arms to ensure DP compliance, and derives theoretical upper and lower bounds on the error probability. The algorithm is designed to minimize the error probability exponentially with respect to the budget T, and the bounds are shown to match the sub-optimality gaps of the arms, the privacy parameter, and the complexity of the problem. The paper also includes a numerical study to validate the algorithm's performance.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, with clear explanations of the algorithms and theoretical results, and includes a comprehensive literature review.
- The paper introduces a novel algorithm for BAI under DP constraints, which is the first of its kind to provide both upper and lower bounds on the error probability.
- The theoretical results are robust, with matching upper and lower bounds that depend on the sub-optimality gaps of the arms, the privacy parameter, and the complexity of the problem.
- The paper is technically sound, with detailed proofs and explanations of the algorithm's operation, including the use of linear algebra techniques.
- The experimental results demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed algorithm, showing that it outperforms existing algorithms in terms of error probability.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper assumes that the feature vectors are known beforehand, which may not be practical in many real-world applications.
- The experimental setup is limited to a single instance, which does not adequately demonstrate the algorithm's performance across different instances.
- The paper does not discuss the practical implications of the algorithm, such as the computational complexity and the scalability of the algorithm with increasing numbers of arms.
- The paper does not address the privacy implications of the algorithm, particularly how the noise added to the arms affects the overall privacy guarantee.
- The proofs in the appendix are not self-contained, which could make it difficult for readers to fully understand the theoretical contributions.
- The paper could benefit from a more thorough discussion of related works, particularly those that address similar problems in the context of DP.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors clarify how the algorithm performs when the feature vectors are not known beforehand?
- How does the algorithm ensure privacy when adding noise to the arms, and what are the implications for the overall privacy guarantee?
- Could the authors provide more details on the computational complexity of the algorithm and how it scales with the number of arms?
- Is there a way to extend the algorithm to handle non-linear arm means, and how might this affect the theoretical results?
- How does the algorithm compare to other state-of-the-art algorithms in terms of computational efficiency and practical performance?
- Could the authors elaborate on the proofs in the appendix to make them more self-contained and accessible to readers?
