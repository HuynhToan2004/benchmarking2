 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel method called Q-score matching (QSM) for learning diffusion model policies in reinforcement learning, which matches the score of the policy to the gradient of the state-action value function with respect to the action. This method, aimed at optimizing the score function of a policy, is theoretically justified and empirically tested in simulated environments from the DeepMind control suite. The method's effectiveness is demonstrated through experiments that show competitive performance compared to existing methods like TD3 and SAC. Despite its innovative approach, the paper has been critiqued for its clarity in presentation, lack of detailed empirical results, and insufficient comparison with related works.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, making it easy to follow, and the ideas are clearly presented.
- The proposed method, Q-score matching, is novel and theoretically justified, providing a new perspective on policy optimization.
- The experiments demonstrate that the proposed method performs competitively with existing methods like SAC and TD3, showing that it can be effective in practice.
- The paper is well-organized, with clear explanations of the theoretical framework and the experimental setup.
- The method's ability to optimize the score function of a policy directly is a significant advancement in reinforcement learning, potentially leading to more expressive and effective policies.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks clarity in some sections, particularly in the definitions and explanations of key concepts such as the action gradient of the Q-function and the stochastic optimal control.
- The empirical results are not sufficiently detailed, with limited environments and metrics used for evaluation.
- The paper does not adequately compare its method with related works, particularly those that use similar approaches to optimize the score function of a policy.
- The theoretical contributions, while interesting, are not clearly connected to the practical implementation of the method, which could limit their applicability.
- There are several minor typographical and formatting errors that need correction.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors clarify the relationship between the score of the policy and the action gradient of the Q-function, particularly how these are matched in practice?
- How does the proposed method handle exploration in the policy space, especially in environments where the optimal policy is multi-modal?
- Could the authors provide more detailed empirical results, including comparisons with a broader range of baselines and environments?
- How does the method perform when the action space is discrete, and what are the implications for the continuous-time formulation used in the theoretical analysis?
- Is there a specific reason why the method was not compared with other related works that also optimize the score function of a policy?
- Can the authors elaborate on the practical implementation of the method, particularly how the theoretical insights are translated into practical algorithms?
