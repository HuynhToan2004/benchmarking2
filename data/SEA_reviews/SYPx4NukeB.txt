 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel self-supervised learning (SSL) framework aimed at achieving causal consistency between causal structures and representations in non-statistical data, specifically referred to as "indefinite data." This framework is designed to address the inconsistencies that arise when learning causal structures and representations using deep learning models. The paper also introduces a new dataset, Causalogue, to evaluate the proposed methodologies. The SSL framework is evaluated on various datasets and tasks, demonstrating its effectiveness in improving causal consistency and accuracy. The paper also discusses the challenges of extending causal discovery methods to non-statistical data and proposes a new approach that leverages causal dialogue data to enhance the understanding of causal relationships.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, making it easy to follow and understand, with clear explanations of the proposed methods and their applications.
- The authors have provided a clear motivation for their work, highlighting the importance of causal consistency in non-statistical data, particularly in the context of causal dialogue.
- The paper introduces a novel self-supervised learning (SSL) framework that effectively addresses the challenge of achieving causal consistency between causal structures and representations in non-statistical data.
- The proposed Causalogue dataset is innovative and well-designed, providing a valuable resource for evaluating the proposed methods and advancing research in the field.
- The paper includes a comprehensive evaluation of the proposed methods on both simulated and real-world datasets, demonstrating the effectiveness of the SSL framework in improving causal consistency and accuracy.
- The paper provides a clear explanation of the causal consistency condition (CCC) and its implications for causal structure learning, which is a significant contribution to the field.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper could benefit from a more detailed discussion on the limitations of the proposed methods and the assumptions made, particularly in the context of non-statistical data.
- The paper does not adequately address the challenges of extending causal discovery methods to non-statistical data, such as the absence of clear causal structures and the need for new learning approaches.
- The paper could provide more examples or case studies to illustrate the practical applications of the proposed methods in real-world scenarios.
- The paper's use of terminology, such as "indefinite data," is not well-defined or explained, which could lead to confusion among readers.
- The paper could benefit from a more detailed discussion on the related work, particularly in the context of causal dialogue and causal discovery in non-statistical data.
- The paper could provide more insights into the implications of the CCC for causal structure learning and discuss potential extensions or modifications of the CCC to address the challenges of non-statistical data.
