 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel approach to pipeline parallelism in large-scale distributed training, specifically focusing on reducing pipeline bubbles through a novel scheduling strategy. The authors propose a method that splits the backward computation into two parts, one for computing gradients for the input and another for the parameters, which is then used to create optimized pipeline schedules. This strategy aims to achieve zero pipeline bubbles under synchronous training semantics, enhancing throughput by up to 15% compared to traditional methods. The paper also includes an algorithm that automatically determines the optimal schedule based on specific model configurations and memory limits. Experimental results show significant improvements over baseline methods, although concerns are raised about the practicality of the approach due to increased memory requirements and the complexity of implementation.
