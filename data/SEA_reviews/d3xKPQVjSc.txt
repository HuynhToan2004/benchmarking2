 **Summary:**
The paper addresses the challenge of representation-induced confounding bias (RICB) in the estimation of conditional average treatment effects (CATEs) using low-dimensional representations. It introduces a novel framework that provides bounds on RICB, allowing for the estimation of lower and upper bounds of CATEs. The framework is tested on several benchmarks, demonstrating its effectiveness in reducing decision errors. The paper is well-written and offers a clear exposition of the problem, although it could benefit from more detailed comparisons with existing methods and a broader range of real-world data experiments.

**Strengths:**
- The paper addresses an important and relevant problem in the field of causal inference, specifically the issue of representation-induced confounding bias (RICB) in estimating conditional average treatment effects (CATEs).
- The authors propose a novel representation-agnostic framework that provides bounds on RICB, which is a significant contribution to the field.
- The paper is well-written and easy to follow, making it accessible to a broad audience.
- The experimental results demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed framework in reducing decision errors, showing a clear improvement over baseline methods.
- The paper provides a comprehensive literature review and positions itself well within the existing body of work, highlighting its originality and relevance.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed comparison with existing methods, which could help in understanding the novelty and effectiveness of the proposed framework more clearly.
- The experiments are primarily conducted on synthetic data, which may not fully reflect the challenges and complexities of real-world data.
- The paper does not provide sufficient detail on the implementation of the framework, particularly in terms of how it integrates with different types of representation learning methods.
- There is a lack of discussion on the limitations and potential negative societal impacts of the proposed framework.
- The paper does not sufficiently address the computational complexity and scalability of the framework, which could be a significant concern for large-scale applications.

**Questions:**
- Could you provide a more detailed comparison with existing methods, including a discussion on how your framework differs from and potentially improves upon these methods?
- How does the proposed framework perform on real-world data, and what are the implications of using real-world data for the estimation of CATEs?
- Can you clarify the role and implementation of the conditional normalizing flow (CNF) in the framework, particularly how it contributes to the estimation of RICB?
- How does the framework handle the computational complexity and scalability issues, especially in large-scale applications?
- Could you discuss the potential negative societal impacts of the proposed framework, and how these might be mitigated or addressed?
