 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel framework called Global Alignment Learning (GAL) aimed at enhancing Federated Learning (FL) by addressing the challenge of continual novel class learning. GAL employs a bi-level clustering mechanism to estimate the number of novel classes and constructs global prototypes. It also incorporates a novel semantic weighted loss to align local training with global prototypes, thereby mitigating the impact of pseudo-label noise and data heterogeneity. The framework is evaluated across multiple datasets, demonstrating its superior performance compared to existing methods. However, the paper's novelty is questioned due to its reliance on existing techniques, and there are concerns about its privacy implications and the complexity of its implementation.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel framework called Global Alignment Learning (GAL) specifically designed for Federated Novel Class Learning (FedNovel), which addresses the challenge of continual model evolution in FL by enabling the discovery and incorporation of unseen novel classes.
- The paper is well-written, clear, and easy to follow, making it accessible to readers.
- The proposed method is evaluated on multiple datasets, demonstrating its superior performance compared to existing methods.
- The paper provides a detailed description of the proposed method, including a thorough explanation of the components and their rationale.
- The experimental results are strong, showing the effectiveness of the proposed method.
- The paper includes ablation studies to verify the effectiveness of the proposed method.
- The paper is well-organized, making it easy for readers to understand the proposed method and its application.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed discussion on the privacy implications of the proposed method, particularly concerning the potential for privacy breaches in FL systems.
- The complexity of the proposed method is a concern, with multiple components and hyper-parameters that could make implementation challenging.
- The novelty of the proposed method is questioned due to its reliance on existing techniques such as PCL, which have been previously used in other contexts.
- The paper does not adequately address the challenge of data heterogeneity, which is a significant issue in FL.
- The paper's evaluation is limited to specific datasets and does not include a broader range of experiments to validate the method's effectiveness in more varied settings.
- The paper does not provide sufficient details on the hyper-parameters used in the experiments, which could affect the reproducibility and comparability of the results.
- The paper does not sufficiently discuss the limitations of the proposed method, which could help readers better understand its applicability and potential drawbacks.

**Questions:**
- How does the proposed method ensure privacy in FL systems, especially in the context of continual novel class learning?
- Could the authors provide a more detailed discussion on the privacy implications of the proposed method?
- How does the proposed method handle data heterogeneity, a significant challenge in FL?
- Can the authors provide more details on the hyper-parameters used in the experiments, particularly the sensitivity of the method to these parameters?
- How does the proposed method compare to existing methods in terms of computational complexity and resource requirements?
- Could the authors provide more detailed results on the performance of the proposed method in different FL algorithms, such as FedAvg and SCAFFOLD?
- How does the proposed method handle the issue of class imbalance in FL, which is a common problem in practical applications?
