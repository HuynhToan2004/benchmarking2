 **Summary:**
The paper introduces QuKerNet, a method that optimizes quantum feature maps by jointly considering gate layout and variational parameters. This approach is designed to address the limitations of previous methods that either overlooked gate layout or required high computational overhead. QuKerNet leverages a feature selection technique and a neural predictor to handle high-dimensional data and enhance the performance of quantum kernels. The method's effectiveness is demonstrated through numerical simulations, showcasing improvements over existing quantum kernel methods and classical kernels. However, the paper has been criticized for its limited evaluation on small datasets and its reliance on a surrogate loss function, which may not accurately reflect the performance of quantum kernels.

**Strengths:**
- The paper addresses a significant challenge in quantum machine learning by proposing a novel method to design quantum feature maps, which is crucial for enhancing the performance of quantum kernels.
- The method is well-motivated and clearly explained, with a thorough literature review that positions the work within the broader context of quantum kernel research.
- The use of a feature selection technique and a neural predictor to handle high-dimensional data is innovative and potentially beneficial for future research in quantum machine learning.
- The paper is well-written, making it accessible and easy to follow, which is crucial for a complex technical topic.
- The experiments demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed method, showing improvements over existing quantum kernel methods and classical kernels.

**Weaknesses:**
- The evaluation of the method is limited to small datasets, which may not adequately demonstrate its performance on larger, more complex datasets.
- The paper's reliance on a surrogate loss function, KTA, which is not directly related to training accuracy, raises concerns about the accuracy and relevance of the performance metrics reported.
- The method's complexity and the computational resources required for training and inference are not adequately discussed, which could impact its practical applicability.
- The paper lacks a detailed discussion on the limitations of the method, including potential biases in the feature selection process and the generalization capabilities of the trained quantum kernels.
- The paper does not provide sufficient details on the hardware and software environments used for the experiments, which could affect the reproducibility and comparability of the results.
- The paper does not adequately address the potential quantum advantages of the proposed method, which is a significant aspect of quantum machine learning research.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors clarify the specific quantum machine learning problem that QuKerNet is intended to solve and how it compares to other methods like QAS and QNNs?
- How does the method ensure that the feature selection process does not introduce biases that could affect the performance or generalizability of the quantum kernels?
- Can the authors provide more details on the hardware and software environments used for the experiments, including the specific quantum hardware and the quantum simulators employed?
- How does the proposed method address the vanishing similarity issue, and what are the specific improvements in test accuracy observed compared to other methods?
- Could the authors discuss the potential quantum advantages of the proposed method and how these advantages are demonstrated in the experiments?
- What are the computational resources required for training and inference with QuKerNet, and how do these compare to other methods in terms of efficiency and scalability?
