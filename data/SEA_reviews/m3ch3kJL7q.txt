 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel method for Composed Image Retrieval (CIR) by leveraging pretrained V-L models to generate sentence-level prompts from both reference images and captions. This approach aims to enhance the expressiveness and precision of the retrieval process by integrating these prompts with existing text-based image retrieval models. The methodology involves a lightweight Querying Transformer, Q-Former, to generate sentence-level prompts and an image-text contrastive loss and text prompt alignment loss to optimize the model's performance. The paper demonstrates state-of-the-art results on the Fashion-IQ and CIRR datasets, showcasing improvements over existing methods. However, the novelty of the approach is questioned due to its similarity to existing methods, and the paper lacks comprehensive comparisons with state-of-the-art methods.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written and easy to follow, making it accessible to readers.
- The proposed method achieves state-of-the-art results on the Fashion-IQ and CIRR datasets, demonstrating its effectiveness.
- The introduction of a sentence-level prompt to the relative caption is a novel approach that enhances the expressiveness and precision of the retrieval process.
- The paper provides extensive experimental results, including ablation studies, which validate the effectiveness of the proposed method.
- The method is straightforward and easy to implement, making it practical for real-world applications.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a comprehensive comparison with state-of-the-art methods, particularly those that have been published after the submission of this paper.
- The novelty of the approach is questioned due to its similarity to existing methods that use pseudo-word embedding to transform reference images into textual inversion.
- The paper does not adequately discuss the limitations of the proposed method, which could affect its applicability in various scenarios.
- There is a lack of visualization results, which could help in understanding the effectiveness of the method in handling complex changes in reference images.
- The paper does not include a discussion on how the method performs with different types of reference images, such as those with varying levels of complexity or different object categories.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors provide a more detailed discussion on the limitations of the proposed method and how these might affect its applicability in various scenarios?
- How does the proposed method perform with different types of reference images, and what are the specific challenges it faces in handling complex changes in reference images?
- Can the authors provide more visualization results to demonstrate the effectiveness of the method in handling different types of reference images and captions?
- How does the proposed method compare with other state-of-the-art methods that have been published after the submission of this paper?
- Could the authors clarify the differences between the proposed method and existing methods that use pseudo-word embedding to transform reference images into textual inversion?
