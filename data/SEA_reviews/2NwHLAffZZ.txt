 **Summary:**
The paper explores the linearization of gradient descent-based learning systems, particularly focusing on the wide neural network (WNN) regime. It introduces a new notion of weak derivative correlations, which are claimed to be crucial for the linearization of learning systems. The authors establish a relationship between weak derivative correlations and linearization, demonstrating that the linearization of learning systems can be attributed to these correlations. The paper also introduces a new method for bounding the asymptotic behavior of random tensors, which is used to analyze the behavior of the hypothesis function. The results are supported by theoretical proofs and numerical experiments, which are discussed in the context of their implications for the neural tangent kernel (NTK) limit.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel approach to linearization of learning systems using the concept of weak derivative correlations, which is a significant contribution to the field.
- The authors provide a detailed proof of the equivalence between weak derivative correlations and linearization, supported by a rigorous mathematical analysis.
- The paper is well-structured and easy to follow, with clear definitions and explanations of key concepts such as random tensor asymptotic behavior and the definite asymptotic bound.
- The authors demonstrate the applicability of their results to various types of neural networks, including CNNs, RNNs, and attention networks, which enhances the paper's relevance and impact.
- The paper includes both theoretical results and numerical experiments, which support the claims made and provide a comprehensive view of the problem.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed discussion on the practical implications of the results, particularly in terms of how the findings can be applied to improve the performance of neural networks in practical scenarios.
- There is a need for more concrete examples and numerical experiments to illustrate the theoretical results, which would help in better understanding the implications of the findings.
- The paper could benefit from a more detailed comparison with existing results, especially those related to the neural tangent kernel (NTK) and other linearization results, to clarify the novelty and contribution of the proposed approach.
- The paper is somewhat dense and could benefit from clearer explanations and more intuitive examples to help readers grasp the complex concepts more easily.
- The paper could be improved by addressing some minor typographical errors and inconsistencies in the notation and definitions used.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors provide more concrete examples or numerical experiments to illustrate the practical implications of their findings, particularly in terms of improving the performance of neural networks?
- How does the proposed approach compare with existing results on linearization and the NTK limit? What are the main differences and advantages of the proposed approach?
- In the context of the NTK limit, how does the paper's findings relate to the "NTK inferiority paradox"? Could the authors elaborate on this connection?
- Could the authors clarify the definition and role of the "definite asymptotic bound" in the context of the paper's results?
- How does the paper's results apply to different types of neural networks, such as convolutional neural networks (CNNs) and recurrent neural networks (RNNs)? Could the authors provide more detailed examples or numerical experiments to support these claims?
