 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel re-parameterization technique, termed Re-parameterized Refocusing Convolution (RefConv), aimed at improving the performance of Convolutional Neural Networks (CNNs) without altering their original structure or introducing additional inference costs. RefConv involves a trainable Refocusing Transformation applied to the basis kernels of pre-trained models, enhancing the connectivity between channels and potentially reducing channel redundancy. The method is tested on various backbone models and tasks, including image classification, object detection, and semantic segmentation, showing consistent improvements. The paper also explores the potential of RefConv to smooth the loss landscape, which could aid in model generalization. Despite its simplicity and the potential for broad applicability, the paper's empirical evaluations are limited, and the theoretical underpinnings of the method are not thoroughly explored.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel re-parameterization technique, RefConv, which is simple, effective, and can be easily integrated into various backbone models, enhancing their performance without altering their original structure.
- RefConv is designed to improve the representational capacity of CNNs by reducing channel redundancy and smoothing the loss landscape, which are critical for improving model generalization.
- The methodology is well-explained, making it accessible and understandable, which is crucial for its adoption by the community.
- The paper is well-written, with clear and concise language that effectively communicates the main ideas and contributions of the research.
- The experimental results are robust, showing consistent improvements across various tasks, including image classification, object detection, and semantic segmentation.
- The paper provides a detailed ablation study that helps in understanding the impact of different components of the proposed method, which is essential for further research and development.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a comprehensive theoretical analysis of the proposed method, which could strengthen the paper's contribution by providing a deeper understanding of the underlying principles and mechanisms of RefConv.
- The experimental evaluations are limited to a few models and datasets, which may not fully demonstrate the generalizability and effectiveness of RefConv across different architectures and tasks.
- The paper does not discuss the potential limitations or negative societal impacts of the proposed method, which could be crucial for understanding the broader implications of its use.
- The paper could benefit from a more detailed discussion on the computational and memory costs of RefConv, especially in comparison to other methods.
- The paper does not provide a detailed comparison with other re-parameterization methods, which could help in understanding the relative advantages and disadvantages of RefConv.
- The paper lacks a discussion on the potential negative societal impacts of the proposed method, which is an important consideration for any technological innovation.

**Questions:**
- How does RefConv perform when applied to models with a large number of parameters, such as those used in large-scale vision tasks like detection and segmentation?
- Can RefConv be applied to models trained with different training strategies, such as those using different learning rates or data augmentation techniques?
- How does RefConv compare to other re-parameterization methods in terms of performance, computational efficiency, and memory requirements?
- Could the authors provide a more detailed comparison of the computational and memory costs of RefConv with other methods, especially in terms of training and inference?
- What are the potential negative societal impacts of the proposed method, and how can these be mitigated or addressed?
- How does RefConv affect the training and inference times, and what are the implications for practical deployment in real-world applications?
