 **Summary:**
The paper introduces DetoxiGen, a novel parameter-efficient approach to detoxify text generation by leveraging a contrastive decoding method. The DetoxiGen system comprises a generator and a detoxifier, both based on the same pre-trained model. The detoxifier is trained using prompt tuning to recognize and generate toxic content, while the generator is optimized to avoid generating such content. The methodology is evaluated against various baseline models and demonstrates significant improvements in toxicity reduction and generation quality. The approach is noted for its efficiency in terms of computational resources and the ability to adapt to different model sizes.

**Strengths:**
- The paper presents a novel approach to detoxifying text generation by using a contrastive decoding method, which is a significant advancement in the field.
- The methodology is well-explained, making it easy to follow and understand the proposed solution.
- The experimental setup is robust, with a variety of baselines and model sizes used, which supports the validity of the results.
- The paper is well-written and accessible, making it easy for readers to grasp the main ideas and contributions.
- The approach is parameter-efficient, which is crucial for practical applications, and the experiments demonstrate that the proposed method outperforms previous approaches in terms of toxicity reduction and generation quality.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a thorough comparison with existing methods such as Inference-Time Policy Adapters, which also address text detoxification.
- The evaluation is limited to a single dataset (RealToxicityPrompts), which might not fully demonstrate the generalizability of the proposed method.
- The paper does not provide sufficient details on the computational cost of the proposed method, which is crucial for practical implementation.
- The method's dependence on the detoxifier's training data and the potential for overfitting to these data is not adequately addressed.
- The paper does not discuss the limitations of the proposed method, which could provide valuable insights for future research.

**Questions:**
- How does the proposed method compare with Inference-Time Policy Adapters in terms of performance and computational efficiency?
- Can the authors provide more details on the computational cost of the proposed method, especially in terms of memory and computational time?
- How does the method handle different types of toxicity, such as hate speech or microaggressions, beyond the general toxicity reduction?
- Could the authors clarify the role of the hyperparameter α in the model's performance and provide more details on its tuning process?
- How does the method ensure that the detoxifier does not overfit to the training data, especially when the training data is limited?
