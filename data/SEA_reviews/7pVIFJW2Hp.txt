 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel framework, FigCaps-HF, for generating figure captions optimized for reader preferences. It leverages a reinforcement learning with human feedback (RLHF) method to train a generative model that predicts human feedback on figure-caption pairs. The framework includes a benchmark dataset with human feedback on figure-caption pairs, which is publicly released to aid further research. The proposed method is evaluated against standard fine-tuning and other baselines, demonstrating improvements in metrics such as ROUGE, BLEU, and Meteor. The paper also explores the effectiveness of different human feedback labels and metrics, providing insights into the impact of feedback on model performance.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel method for figure-to-caption generation that incorporates domain expert feedback, which is a significant advancement in the field.
- The proposed framework is simple, effective, and easy to implement, making it accessible to a wide range of researchers and practitioners.
- The paper is well-written, clear, and easy to follow, enhancing its readability and accessibility.
- The benchmark dataset with human feedback on figure-caption pairs is publicly released, which is a valuable resource for the research community and can aid in the development of more effective RLHF techniques for figure-to-caption generation.
- The paper provides a thorough evaluation of the proposed method, demonstrating its effectiveness through empirical results and showing improvements over standard fine-tuning across different types of models.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed comparison with existing methods, particularly in the context of figure-to-caption generation. This could undermine the perceived novelty and effectiveness of the proposed method.
- The selection of human feedback metrics and their impact on model performance are not thoroughly explored. The paper could benefit from a more comprehensive analysis of different metrics and their effects.
- The paper does not sufficiently discuss the generalizability of the proposed method to other datasets or scenarios, which could limit its applicability.
- The qualitative results presented in the paper are not convincing, with some generated captions appearing inadequate or irrelevant, which raises questions about the effectiveness of the proposed method.
- The paper lacks a detailed discussion on the limitations of the proposed method, which could help in understanding its practical applicability and potential drawbacks.
