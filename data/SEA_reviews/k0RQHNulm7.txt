 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel cross-modality distillation method aimed at transferring knowledge from a source modality to a target modality using both positive and negative relationships in paired data. This approach leverages contrastive learning to distill generalizable representations from a source modality to a target modality, which is validated through extensive experiments across various modalities such as image, sketch, depth map, and audio, demonstrating superior performance over existing methods. Theoretical analysis supports the convergence bounds of the proposed method, and the paper provides a comprehensive experimental setup to validate the effectiveness of the proposed method.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel framework for cross-modality distillation using both positive and negative relationships in paired data, which is a significant contribution to the field.
- The proposed method demonstrates superior performance across various modalities and tasks, outperforming existing methods by a margin of 2-3%.
- The paper provides a comprehensive theoretical analysis of the algorithm, which is well-supported by empirical results.
- The writing is clear and easy to follow, making the paper accessible to a broad audience.
- The methodology is well-motivated and the experimental results are convincing, showing the effectiveness of the proposed method.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed discussion on the limitations of the proposed method and how it might perform in scenarios with a large domain gap between the source and target modalities.
- There is a need for more comprehensive experimental validation, particularly in scenarios where the domain gap is significant, to fully understand the generalizability of the method.
- The theoretical analysis is based on several assumptions, and the paper does not discuss the implications of these assumptions on the validity of the theoretical results.
- The paper could benefit from a more detailed discussion on the practical implications of the theoretical results and how they relate to real-world applications.
- The paper could be improved by including more recent literature on contrastive learning and dataset distillation, which are closely related to the topic.

**Questions:**
- Could you provide more detailed examples or scenarios where the proposed method might not perform as well due to a large domain gap between the source and target modalities?
- How does the proposed method compare in terms of computational efficiency and training time with other methods, especially when dealing with large-scale datasets?
- Could you provide more insights into the practical implications of the theoretical results and how they can be applied in real-world scenarios?
- How does the proposed method perform when the source and target modalities have a significant domain gap?
- Could you discuss the implications of the assumptions made in the theoretical analysis and how they affect the validity of the results?
