 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel method for parameter-efficient fine-tuning (PEFT) called Random Orthogonal Subspace Adapters (ROSA), which addresses the limitations of existing methods like LoRA. ROSA decomposes the weight matrix into fixed and trainable components using Singular Value Decomposition (SVD), allowing for a more flexible and expressive adaptation of model weights. The method is demonstrated to outperform LoRA in several benchmarks, including natural language understanding and generation tasks. The theoretical analysis supports the method's expressiveness and its ability to avoid the low-rank bias of LoRA. Despite its strengths, the paper is criticized for its limited empirical evaluation, lack of comparison with some relevant methods, and potential issues with computational efficiency and scalability.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, clear, and easy to follow, with a clear motivation and detailed theoretical analysis.
- The proposed method, ROSA, is innovative and offers a new approach to parameter-efficient fine-tuning (PEFT) by decomposing the weight matrix into fixed and trainable components, addressing the low-rank bias of LoRA.
- The experimental results show that ROSA outperforms LoRA in several benchmarks, including natural language understanding and generation tasks.
- The method is simple and effective, with a clear presentation of the methodology and its benefits over existing methods.
- The theoretical analysis supports the method's expressiveness and its ability to avoid the low-rank bias of LoRA, which is crucial for improving the performance of large language models (LLMs).

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a comprehensive comparison with other relevant methods such as BitFit, FISH, and AdaLoRA, which also address the issue of low-rank bias in PEFT.
- The empirical evaluation is limited to a few benchmarks, which may not adequately demonstrate the method's effectiveness across a broader range of tasks.
- The computational efficiency of ROSA is a concern, particularly the computational cost associated with the SVD operation, which could be a barrier to practical implementation.
- The paper does not adequately address the scalability of the method, particularly in large-scale settings, which could limit its applicability in real-world scenarios.
- The method's effectiveness is not clearly demonstrated in large-scale models, which are crucial for practical applications in natural language processing (NLP).
- The paper lacks a detailed discussion on the limitations of the method, such as its applicability to different model architectures and its potential impact on training time and model size.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors provide a more detailed comparison with other methods that address the low-rank bias in PEFT, such as BitFit, FISH, and AdaLoRA?
- How does the proposed method perform in large-scale models, particularly in terms of computational efficiency and effectiveness?
- Could the authors elaborate on the scalability of the method and discuss potential strategies for addressing the computational cost associated with the SVD operation?
- How does the method perform in different model architectures, such as those based on convolution operations?
- Could the authors provide a more detailed discussion on the limitations of the method and suggest potential strategies for addressing these limitations?
- Can the authors provide more experimental results, including the training and inference time of ROSA, to better understand its practical implications?
