 **Summary:**
The paper explores adversarial attacks in multi-agent reinforcement learning (RL) settings, where an attacker manipulates another agent's policy, aiming to reduce the victim agent's performance. It introduces a generalized attack formulation that includes a "budget" parameter to control the extent of the attacker's control over the agent. The paper also proposes a defense mechanism using a timescale separation in adversarial training, which aims to optimize the victim's policy against various adversarial policies. Theoretical analyses and empirical results are provided, demonstrating the effectiveness of the proposed methods in reducing state distribution shifts and improving robustness. The paper also discusses the practical implications of these attacks and defenses, although some concerns are raised about the clarity of the theoretical claims and the practical applicability of the proposed methods.

**Strengths:**
- The paper addresses a significant and relevant problem in the field of multi-agent reinforcement learning, focusing on the robustness of policies against adversarial attacks.
- The introduction of a "budget" parameter to control the attacker's influence over the agent's policy is a novel and interesting approach.
- The paper provides both theoretical analysis and empirical results, which support the effectiveness of the proposed methods.
- The paper is well-written, making the content accessible and understandable.
- The generalized attack formulation and the timescale separation in adversarial training are innovative contributions to the field.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks clarity in defining the attacker's objective and the constraints on the attacker's policy, which could affect the validity of the theoretical claims.
- The theoretical analysis, particularly in sections 3 and 4, is not clearly explained, and the assumptions made are not well-justified or explained.
- The paper does not adequately address the practical implications of the proposed methods, such as the computational complexity and scalability of the algorithms.
- The paper's claims about the robustness of the policies are not convincingly supported by the experimental results, which do not show a significant improvement in robustness.
- The paper could benefit from a more detailed discussion on the limitations of the proposed methods and a comparison with other existing methods in the literature.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors clarify the definition and role of the "budget" parameter in the attacker's policy?
- How does the proposed method ensure that the attacker's policy remains within the constraints defined in the paper?
- Can the authors provide a more detailed explanation of the theoretical assumptions and their implications on the practical applicability of the proposed methods?
- How do the authors justify the choice of the attacker's objective and the constraints on the attacker's policy?
- In the experimental results, how do the authors ensure that the attacker's policy is not overly simplistic or biased towards certain outcomes?
- Can the authors provide more details on the computational complexity and scalability of the proposed methods, particularly in large-scale multi-agent RL environments?
