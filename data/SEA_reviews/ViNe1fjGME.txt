 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel framework for temporal graph clustering, named TGC, which leverages deep clustering techniques to process temporal graphs more effectively. It addresses the challenges of handling dynamic information and computational efficiency in temporal graph clustering, which is often treated as static in existing methods. The framework is evaluated against multiple baselines and shows promising results, particularly in large-scale datasets. The paper also discusses the differences between temporal and static graph clustering, providing a comprehensive evaluation across various datasets. Despite some concerns regarding the novelty and the depth of comparison with existing methods, the paper is well-received for its clarity, methodological soundness, and potential impact on the field.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-organized, easy to follow, and provides a comprehensive evaluation of the proposed method, TGC, against multiple baselines.
- The proposed framework is simple, effective, and can be easily integrated into existing temporal graph learning methods to enhance their clustering performance.
- The paper is clearly written, making it accessible and understandable, with a clear motivation and clear writing that aids in understanding the methodology and its application.
- The methodology is simple, and the experimental results are promising, showing that the proposed method can handle large-scale temporal graphs effectively.
- The paper introduces a new temporal graph clustering framework that can be applied to various temporal graph learning methods, potentially enhancing their performance.

**Weaknesses:**
- The novelty of the proposed method is questionable as it primarily uses a combination of existing methods (node2vec and K-means) without significant innovation.
- The paper lacks a detailed comparison with other state-of-the-art methods, particularly those that have addressed the temporal graph clustering problem, such as TREND and TGAT.
- The paper does not sufficiently discuss the advantages of using temporal graphs over static graphs, which could limit its applicability in scenarios where static graphs are more suitable.
- There is a lack of discussion on the limitations of the proposed method, which is crucial for understanding its applicability and potential drawbacks.
- The paper does not adequately address the scalability of the method to extremely large datasets, which is a significant concern in practical applications.
- The method's performance on datasets with low label confidence or incomplete labels is not discussed, which could affect its applicability in real-world scenarios.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors clarify the advantages of using temporal graphs over static graphs in terms of dynamic information capture and computational efficiency?
- How does the proposed method compare to other state-of-the-art methods in terms of performance and efficiency?
- What are the specific advantages of the proposed method over existing methods like TREND and TGAT, particularly in terms of handling dynamic information and computational efficiency?
- How does the method perform on extremely large datasets, and what are the potential limitations or challenges in scaling the method to such datasets?
- Can the authors discuss the potential applications of the method beyond temporal graph clustering, such as in other graph learning tasks?
- What are the potential limitations or challenges of the method, and how might these impact its practical applicability?
