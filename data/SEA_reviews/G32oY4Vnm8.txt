 **Summary:**
The paper introduces PTARL, a novel prototype-based tabular representation learning framework aimed at addressing the challenges of representation entanglement and localization in deep tabular machine learning models. PTARL leverages prototype learning to construct a prototype-based projection space, which facilitates disentangled representation learning by optimizing the distance between data distributions using optimal transport. The framework involves two stages: prototype generating and prototype projecting, employing constraints like coordinates diversifying and matrix orthogonalization to ensure disentangled and orthogonal representations. The paper is supported by extensive experiments on various tabular benchmarks, demonstrating PTARL's effectiveness in enhancing performance across different tasks.

**Strengths:**
- The paper addresses the critical issues of representation entanglement and localization in deep tabular machine learning models, which are significant challenges in the field.
- The proposed PTARL framework is innovative, utilizing prototype learning to construct a prototype-based projection space, which effectively addresses these challenges.
- The paper is well-written, making it accessible and easy to follow, with clear explanations and comprehensive experiments that demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed methods.
- The use of optimal transport for learning disentangled representations is a novel approach, enhancing the model's ability to capture global data structure information.
- The paper provides extensive experiments and analysis, demonstrating the effectiveness of the proposed methods across various tabular benchmarks.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed discussion on the limitations of the proposed methods, particularly in terms of their applicability to different types of data and the scalability of the model to larger datasets.
- The paper does not provide a detailed analysis of the computational complexity or training time for the proposed methods, which could be crucial for practical implementation.
- There is a lack of comparison with other recent methods that address similar issues in deep tabular learning, such as XTab, which might provide a more comprehensive evaluation of the proposed methods.
- The paper's experimental results are not consistently superior to existing methods, and the improvements are sometimes marginal, raising questions about the practical significance of the proposed methods.
- The paper could benefit from a more thorough literature review and a clearer explanation of how the proposed methods differ from or build upon existing techniques like prototype learning.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors provide a detailed discussion on the limitations of the proposed methods, particularly in terms of their applicability to different types of data and scalability?
- What are the specific computational complexities and training times associated with the proposed methods, and how do these compare to existing methods?
- How does the proposed method compare to other recent methods that address similar issues in deep tabular learning, such as XTab?
- Can the authors provide more details on the experimental setup, particularly the hyperparameter tuning and the choice of datasets?
- How do the authors address the potential issues of representation entanglement and localization in deep tabular learning models, and what are the implications of these issues in practical applications?
