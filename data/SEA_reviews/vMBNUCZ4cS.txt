 **Summary:**
The paper explores the infinite-width limits of various graph neural network (GNN) architectures, including standard GNNs, Skip-Concatenate GNNs, and Graph Attention Neural Networks (GATs). It derives closed-form expressions for Gaussian processes and Neural Tangent Kernels (NTKs) for these architectures, aiming to provide a theoretical foundation for understanding the behavior of GNNs in the limit of infinite width. The paper also evaluates the performance of these models against their neural network counterparts, showing that in some cases, the kernels and Gaussian processes perform better or comparably. The experiments were conducted on datasets such as Cora, Citeseer, Pubmed, Wiki, Chameleon, Squirrel, and Crocodile, using transductive node regression and classification tasks.

**Strengths:**
- The paper provides a clear and detailed derivation of the NTK for various GNN architectures, including standard GNNs, Skip-Concatenate GNNs, and Graph Attention Neural Networks (GATs).
- The authors have successfully shown that the performance of kernels and Gaussian processes can be competitive with neural networks, and in some cases, they even outperform neural networks.
- The paper is well-written, with clear explanations and detailed derivations, making it accessible to readers.
- The theoretical results are presented in a clear and concise manner, and the paper includes a comprehensive discussion on the implications of the NTK theory for GNNs.
- The experiments are well-designed, providing a thorough comparison of the performance of kernels and Gaussian processes against neural networks.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper does not include an inductive setting, which is crucial for evaluating the generalization capabilities of the models.
- The paper's evaluation is limited to a small number of datasets, which may not adequately represent the performance of the models across a broader range of scenarios.
- The paper does not discuss the limitations of its approach, which could provide a more balanced view of the contributions and potential drawbacks of the work.
- There are some issues with the clarity and organization of the paper, particularly in the introduction and related work sections, which could make it more difficult for readers to follow the arguments and contributions.
- The paper does not sufficiently compare its results with existing literature, particularly in terms of the novelty and depth of the theoretical contributions.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors clarify the definition and role of the parameter σb in the model, especially in the context of regression tasks?
- How do the authors address the potential limitations of their approach, particularly in terms of the generalizability and applicability of the NTK theory to different GNN architectures?
- Could the authors provide a more detailed discussion on the experimental settings and results, especially concerning the performance of the GAT* models and the choice of datasets?
- How does the paper compare its results with existing literature, particularly in terms of the novelty and depth of the theoretical contributions?
- Could the authors provide a more detailed discussion on the implications of the NTK theory for GNNs, particularly in terms of the generalization capabilities and practical applications of the models?
