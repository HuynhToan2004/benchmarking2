 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel method called PLOT (Pareto Long-Tailed Optimization) to address the optimization conflicts in deep long-tailed recognition (DLTR) by integrating multi-objective optimization (MOO) with DLTR. The authors address the challenges of generalization and convergence in MOO-based DLTR by proposing a variability collapse loss and anticipating worst-case optimization. Extensive experiments on multiple benchmarks demonstrate the effectiveness of PLOT, which can be applied to existing DLTR methods to enhance their performance. The paper also discusses the theoretical underpinnings of the proposed method, including a MOO-based MTL pipeline and the optimization of variability collapse loss.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, making it easy to follow and understand.
- The proposed method is novel and effectively addresses the optimization conflicts in deep long-tailed recognition (DLTR), which is a significant issue in the field.
- The paper provides a comprehensive analysis of the optimization conflicts in DLTR and empirically establishes the severity of these conflicts, which is crucial for understanding the challenges in this area.
- The integration of multi-objective optimization (MOO) with DLTR is innovative, and the paper demonstrates how this integration can enhance the performance of existing DLTR methods.
- The paper includes extensive experiments on multiple benchmarks, which validate the effectiveness of the proposed method.
- Theoretical insights are provided to support the proposed method, enhancing the paper's credibility and depth.

**Weaknesses:**
