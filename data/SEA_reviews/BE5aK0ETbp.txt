 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a unified framework for continual learning, integrating various existing methods into a single optimization objective using Bregman divergence. This framework, termed "refresh learning," is designed to address catastrophic forgetting by unlearning and re-learning weights. The approach is validated through experiments on standard continual learning benchmarks, showing improvements over baseline methods. However, the paper's novelty is questioned, as it primarily integrates existing methods without significant innovation. Theoretical analysis and empirical results suggest the method's effectiveness, although concerns are raised about the depth of theoretical support and the practical implications of the unlearning phase.

**Strengths:**
- The paper provides a comprehensive review of existing continual learning methods and proposes a unified framework that encompasses these methods, potentially simplifying the development of new continual learning algorithms.
- The proposed "refresh learning" method is a novel approach that integrates unlearning and re-learning weights to address catastrophic forgetting, which is a significant challenge in continual learning.
- The paper is well-written, making it easy to follow, and the experiments are conducted on standard continual learning benchmarks, demonstrating the method's effectiveness.
- The theoretical analysis of the proposed method is provided, which helps in understanding the underlying principles of the approach.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed discussion on how the proposed framework and "refresh learning" method differ from existing methods, particularly in terms of novelty and technical contribution.
- The empirical results, while showing improvements over baseline methods, do not significantly outperform state-of-the-art methods, which raises questions about the practical effectiveness of the proposed approach.
- The theoretical analysis of the "refresh learning" method is not well-supported, with some claims, such as the flatness of the loss landscape, being overstated or incorrect.
- The paper does not adequately address the computational cost and complexity of the unlearning phase, which could be a significant drawback in practical applications.
- There is a lack of clarity in some sections of the paper, particularly in the explanation of the "refresh learning" method and the experimental results, which could hinder understanding and reproducibility.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors clarify the technical contributions of the proposed framework and "refresh learning" method compared to existing methods?
- How does the proposed method address the computational cost and complexity of the unlearning phase, particularly in large-scale or real-time applications?
- Can the authors provide more detailed experimental results, including the performance of the proposed method on larger datasets and more challenging continual learning tasks?
- How does the proposed method compare to other recent advancements in continual learning, such as those mentioned in recent surveys and reviews?
- Could the authors elaborate on the theoretical underpinnings of the "refresh learning" method, particularly in terms of the flatness of the loss landscape and the practical implications of the unlearning phase?
