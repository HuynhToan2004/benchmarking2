 **Summary:**
The paper introduces GroupLane, a novel 3D lane detection method that employs a fully convolutional detector using row-wise classification in the bird's-eye view (BEV) plane. It groups feature channels to represent lane detection instances and utilizes a single-win one-to-one matching strategy to associate predictions with labels during training. The method, which is inspired by the DETR framework, achieves significant performance improvements over existing methods, demonstrating a high F1 score and fast inference speed. Despite these strengths, the paper has been critiqued for its limited novelty, as it primarily extends existing methods to 3D lane detection and lacks a comprehensive discussion on related works and methodological contributions.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel approach to 3D lane detection by utilizing a fully convolutional detector with row-wise classification in the BEV plane, which is a significant advancement over traditional methods.
- The methodology is well-articulated, with clear explanations of the proposed strategies such as the single-win one-to-one matching and channel grouping, which are supported by comprehensive ablation studies.
- The paper is well-written and easy to follow, making it accessible to readers and facilitating understanding of the proposed methods.
- The proposed method achieves state-of-the-art (SOTA) performance in terms of F1 score and inference speed, demonstrating its practical applicability and efficiency.
- The paper includes a thorough evaluation on multiple datasets, showcasing the robustness and effectiveness of the proposed method across different scenarios.

**Weaknesses:**
- The novelty of the paper is limited as it primarily extends existing methods, such as row-wise classification, to 3D lane detection without significant innovation.
- The paper lacks a comprehensive discussion on related works, particularly on the use of row-wise classification in 2D lane detection, which could have provided a better context for the proposed method.
- There is a noticeable absence of comparisons with more recent and relevant works, such as "L_ATR: Lane Detection and Trajectory Representation Learning for Autonomous Driving," which could have highlighted the strengths and limitations of the proposed method more effectively.
- The paper does not adequately address the limitations of the proposed method, which could include the handling of complex topology structures in 3D lane detection and the potential impact of different backbone architectures on performance.
- The paper could benefit from a more detailed discussion on the choice of the backbone network and its impact on performance, as well as the inclusion of more visual results to support the claims made.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors provide a more detailed discussion on the choice of the backbone network and its impact on performance?
- How does the proposed method compare with other recent works, such as "L_ATR: Lane Detection and Trajectory Representation Learning for Autonomous Driving"?
- Can the authors clarify the role and effectiveness of the horizontal head group in the proposed method?
- How does the proposed method handle the complex topology structures in 3D lane detection, particularly in scenarios where the lane topology is more complex?
- Could the authors provide more visual results to support the claims made about the performance of the proposed method?
