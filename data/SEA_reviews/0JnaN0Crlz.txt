 **Summary:**
The paper investigates the robustness of deep neural networks (DNNs) against adversarial attacks on categorical data, particularly focusing on the tabular categorical attributes. The authors establish an information-theoretic upper bound on the expected adversarial risk and propose a novel adversarially robust learning method named Integrated Gradient-Smoothed Gradient (IGSG) to enhance the robustness of classification over categorical attributes. This method aims to smooth the attributional sensitivity of each feature and the decision boundary of the classifier, thereby reducing adversarial risk. The paper presents an extensive empirical study on various categorical datasets, demonstrating the effectiveness of the proposed method. However, concerns were raised regarding the clarity and completeness of the presentation, the robustness of the method against various types of attacks, and the need for more comprehensive experimental evaluations.

**Strengths:**
- The paper addresses the issue of adversarial robustness on categorical data, which is a significant and relevant problem in the field.
- It introduces an information-theoretic upper bound on the expected adversarial risk, which provides a theoretical foundation for the proposed method.
- The proposed Integrated Gradient-Smoothed Gradient (IGSG) method is novel and well-motivated, with clear explanations of its components and their roles in improving adversarial robustness.
- The paper is well-structured, making it easy to follow, and provides a comprehensive experimental evaluation that demonstrates the effectiveness of the proposed method.
- The paper is significant in its contribution to the field of adversarial robustness on categorical data, which is an important area of research with practical implications.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks clarity and completeness in its presentation, with some sections being difficult to understand and some references missing.
- The robustness of the proposed method against various types of attacks, such as PGD, is not adequately addressed.
- The paper could benefit from a more detailed discussion on the choice of hyperparameters and their impact on the performance of the model.
- The experimental evaluation could be improved by including more comprehensive comparisons with other robust training methods and by evaluating the proposed method on a wider range of datasets.
- The paper could provide more insights into the theoretical foundations of the method, particularly in terms of the tightness and applicability of the information-theoretic bounds.
- The paper could discuss the potential limitations of the proposed method and suggest directions for future research to address these limitations.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors clarify the discrepancy between the theoretical upper bound and the empirical upper bound, particularly in relation to the tightness of the bound?
- How does the proposed method compare to other robust training methods in terms of computational complexity and training time?
- Could the authors provide more detailed explanations of the IGSG method and its components, particularly in terms of how they contribute to the overall effectiveness of the method?
- How does the proposed method perform against different types of adversarial attacks, such as PGD and FGSM, and what are the implications of these results for the practical applicability of the method?
- Could the authors discuss the potential limitations of the proposed method and suggest directions for future research to address these limitations?
