 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel method for material property prediction using a transformer architecture that incorporates Pointwise Distance Distribution (PDD) matrices as input. The PDD matrices are derived from periodic crystal structures and provide a robust representation that is invariant to rigid motions. The method is evaluated against existing models on the Materials Project dataset and shows promising results, particularly in predicting lattice energy and various material properties. The paper also discusses the computational efficiency and scalability of the approach, although it lacks detailed comparisons with other state-of-the-art methods and does not provide comprehensive ablation studies.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel method for material property prediction using a transformer architecture that incorporates Pointwise Distance Distribution (PDD) matrices as input, which is a robust representation for periodic crystal structures.
- The method is evaluated against existing models on the Materials Project dataset and shows promising results, particularly in predicting lattice energy and various material properties.
- The paper is well-written and easy to follow, with a clear exposition of the methodology and results.
- The proposed method is computationally efficient, which is crucial for handling large crystal structures.
- The paper provides a detailed discussion on the computational efficiency and scalability of the approach, which is essential for practical applications.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed comparison with other state-of-the-art methods in the field of material property prediction, which could have provided a more comprehensive evaluation of the proposed method.
- There is a lack of ablation studies, which could have helped in understanding the impact of different components of the model on the performance.
- The paper does not discuss the limitations of the proposed method, which is crucial for understanding its applicability and potential drawbacks.
- The evaluation of the method is limited to the Materials Project dataset, which might not be sufficient to demonstrate the generalizability of the method across different types of crystal structures.
- The paper could benefit from more detailed discussions on the computational efficiency and scalability of the method, especially in relation to other state-of-the-art methods.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors provide a more detailed comparison with other state-of-the-art methods in the field of material property prediction?
- Could the authors conduct ablation studies to understand the impact of different components of the model on the performance?
- Could the authors discuss the limitations of the proposed method and how they might affect its applicability and performance?
- Could the authors provide a more detailed discussion on the computational efficiency and scalability of the method, especially in relation to other state-of-the-art methods?
- Could the authors provide a more detailed discussion on how the method performs on crystal structures that are not part of the Materials Project dataset?
- Could the authors provide more details on the training and testing splits used in the experiments?
