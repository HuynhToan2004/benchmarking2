 **Summary:**
The paper introduces SEA, a novel method for approximating attention matrices using a kernel-based linear attention mechanism, followed by a sparse attention mechanism. The method aims to reduce computational complexity by approximating the full attention matrix with a sparse one, which is beneficial for long sequence processing in transformer models. The authors demonstrate that SEA can achieve competitive perplexity scores on language modeling tasks and outperform other linear attention methods in terms of computational efficiency. The approach is supported by extensive experiments and ablation studies, showing that SEA can be used to distill attention matrices from pretrained models, enhancing interpretability and reducing memory usage.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel method for approximating attention matrices, which is a significant contribution to the field.
- The method is supported by extensive experiments and ablation studies, providing a robust evaluation of the proposed approach.
- The paper is well-written, making it accessible and easy to understand, which is crucial for reproducibility and further research.
- The proposed method shows promising results in reducing computational complexity and improving memory efficiency, which is crucial for handling long sequences in transformer models.
- The paper provides a detailed description of the proposed method, including the architecture and training details, which aids in understanding the model's implementation and operation.
