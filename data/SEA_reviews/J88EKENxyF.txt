 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel method for multi-modal contextual image retrieval (MMCIR) using a large language model (LLM) trained on a new dataset, Multi-Modal Captioning (MMC). This dataset is generated by an off-the-shelf LLM from existing image-caption pairs, allowing for the generation of a diverse and coherent dataset. The LLM is trained using context-aware captioning and context-aware text matching objectives, which are designed to enhance the model's ability to handle multi-modal contexts. The proposed method achieves state-of-the-art results on several benchmarks, including zero-shot composed image retrieval and dense multi-modal contextual retrieval. The paper also discusses the limitations of the proposed method, including the reliance on a single LLM and the need for more comprehensive comparisons with other LLM-based methods.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, making it easy to follow, and the figures are well-designed, enhancing the understanding of the proposed method.
- The proposed method is innovative, utilizing a Large Language Model (LLM) for multi-modal contextual image retrieval, which is a novel approach compared to traditional methods that rely on image-text matching models.
- The authors construct a new Multi-Modal Captioning (MMC) dataset, which is a significant contribution to the field, providing a valuable resource for future research.
- The paper demonstrates state-of-the-art performance on several benchmarks, including zero-shot composed image retrieval and dense multi-modal contextual retrieval, showcasing the effectiveness of the proposed method.
- The ablation studies are comprehensive, providing insights into the contributions of different components of the proposed method.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed discussion on the limitations of the proposed method, which is crucial for understanding the scope and applicability of the findings.
- The comparison with other LLM-based methods is insufficient, which is necessary to establish the superiority of the proposed method.
- The paper does not provide sufficient visual results to support the effectiveness of the proposed method, which could enhance the credibility of the findings.
- The use of a single LLM for the experiments limits the generalizability of the findings, and it would be beneficial to explore the performance of the proposed method with different LLMs.
- The paper does not sufficiently address the potential negative societal impacts of the proposed method, which is an essential consideration for ethical AI research.

**Questions:**
- Could you provide more detailed discussions on the limitations of the proposed method, including potential biases in the dataset and the generalizability of the findings?
- How does the proposed method compare with other LLM-based methods, especially in terms of performance and computational efficiency?
- Can you provide more visual results to support the effectiveness of the proposed method, possibly including examples of retrieved images and their relevance to the query?
- How does the proposed method perform with different LLMs, and what are the implications of using different LLMs on the performance and applicability of the method?
- Could you elaborate on the potential negative societal impacts of the proposed method and discuss how these impacts could be mitigated?
