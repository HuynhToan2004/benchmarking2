 **Summary:**
The paper discusses an algorithm for sampling from a log-concave distribution supported on a polytope, which is a significant improvement over existing methods. The algorithm leverages efficient linear solvers to reduce the computational complexity of matrix inversion and determinant computation, thereby enhancing the efficiency of the sampling process. This approach is particularly advantageous when the number of non-zero entries in the constraint matrix is large, which is a common scenario in many practical applications. The paper also introduces a randomized Taylor series-based estimator to speed up the computation of the determinantal term, thereby improving the runtime of the algorithm. The theoretical analysis and experimental results demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed method, although some reviewers suggest further improvements in clarity and the inclusion of more comprehensive experimental results.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, making it easy to follow and understand the main ideas.
- The proposed algorithm is a significant improvement over previous work, reducing the number of arithmetic operations per step by a factor of d.
- The algorithm's application to the sampling from a log-concave distribution supported on a polytope is novel and relevant, particularly in the context of differentially private low-rank approximation.
- The paper provides a detailed explanation of the algorithm's implementation and the theoretical analysis behind it, including the use of efficient linear solvers and the application of the Dikin walk Markov chain.
- The paper is timely and relevant, addressing an important problem in the field of sampling from log-concave distributions over polytopes.

**Weaknesses:**

- The paper lacks comprehensive experimental results, which could strengthen the claims made about the algorithm's effectiveness.

- The paper does not sufficiently discuss the limitations of the proposed algorithm, which could affect its practical applicability in certain scenarios.
