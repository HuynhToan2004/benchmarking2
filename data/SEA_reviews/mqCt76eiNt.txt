 **Summary:**
The paper introduces Advantage-Aware Policy Optimization (A2PO), a novel method designed to address the constraint conflict issue in offline reinforcement learning (RL) by utilizing a Conditional Variational Auto-Encoder (CVAE) to disentangle action distributions derived from mixed-quality datasets. A2PO conditions the agent policy on both state and estimated advantage values, enabling effective utilization of all samples with varying quality. The method has been tested on the D4RL benchmark, demonstrating superior performance compared to state-of-the-art offline RL methods. The paper is well-structured, providing a clear introduction and detailed experimental results, although it suffers from some issues such as lack of clarity in figures and a need for more extensive empirical evaluations.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel approach, Advantage-Aware Policy Optimization (A2PO), which disentangles action distributions using a Conditional Variational Auto-Encoder (CVAE) and conditions the agent policy on both state and estimated advantage values.
- The method is well-motivated and provides a clear introduction, making it accessible and understandable.
- The paper includes extensive empirical evaluations and ablation studies, demonstrating the effectiveness of the proposed method.
- The methodology is innovative, combining CVAE with offline RL, which is not commonly seen in previous works.
- The paper is well-written, with clear figures and detailed explanations, making it easy to follow.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks clarity in some figures, particularly in Figure 1, where the distinction between different methods and the representation of advantage values is not clear.
- The motivation behind using the advantage value in the CVAE is not convincingly justified, especially considering the potential instability of the advantage function.
- The paper could benefit from a more comprehensive empirical evaluation, including additional tasks and metrics to validate the effectiveness of the method.
- There are several minor typographical and formatting errors that need correction.
- The paper does not adequately address the issue of overfitting, which is a significant concern in offline RL.
- The paper lacks a detailed discussion on the limitations of the proposed method and potential negative societal impacts.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors clarify the distinction between the methods represented in Figure 1 and provide a clearer representation of advantage values?
- How does the proposed method compare to other offline RL algorithms, particularly in terms of computational efficiency and scalability?
- Could the authors provide more details on the limitations of the proposed method and potential negative societal impacts?
- In the context of the mixed-quality dataset, how does the proposed method ensure that the agent policy is not biased towards high-return samples?
- How does the paper address the issue of overfitting, especially in the context of the CVAE model?
- Can the authors provide more detailed justification for the choice of using the advantage value in the CVAE and discuss the potential implications of this choice?
