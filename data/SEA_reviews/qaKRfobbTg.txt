 **Summary:**
The paper investigates the query complexity of learning an optimal threshold in a latent space where the reward function depends on the threshold and the latent value. It provides a lower bound of Ω(1/ε^3) for the query complexity of learning a threshold, assuming monotonicity and Lipschitzness of the reward function and the value distribution. The paper also introduces a new lower bound of Ω(1/ε^3 + 1/ε^2 log(1/ε)) under stronger assumptions. The authors demonstrate that their results are tight and provide matching upper bounds, which are significant contributions to the field. The paper also discusses the implications of these findings in an online learning setting, showing a regret of O(T^(2/3)).

**Strengths:**

- The authors provide a tight lower bound that matches their upper bounds, demonstrating the robustness of their results.
