 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel method, Exploratory AI (EAI), which leverages large language models (LLMs) to autonomously generate diverse training data for AI models. This approach uses an actor-critic framework, where the actor generates novel content and the critic evaluates its quality and novelty, providing feedback to guide the actor's exploration. The method is evaluated on mathematical reasoning tasks, showing improvements over baseline methods. The paper also explores the application of EAI in code generation, demonstrating its potential in diverse domains. However, concerns are raised about the novelty of the approach, as it resembles existing methods, and the method's scalability and generalizability to other tasks.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written and easy to follow, with clear explanations of the methodology and results.
- The use of LLMs as both the reward function and exploration policy in a reinforcement learning framework is a novel and interesting approach.
- The experiments are well-designed, showing that the proposed method can generate diverse and high-quality training data, improving model performance.
- The paper demonstrates the potential of using LLMs to generate diverse training data, which is crucial for advancing AI capabilities.
- The authors provide detailed experimental results and analyses, showing the effectiveness of their method in mathematical reasoning and code generation tasks.

**Weaknesses:**
- The novelty of the method is questioned, as it closely resembles existing approaches such as RFT and WizardMath, with minimal technical innovation.
- The method's scalability and applicability to other tasks beyond mathematical reasoning are not adequately demonstrated, raising concerns about its generalizability.
- The paper lacks a detailed discussion on the limitations of the method, particularly concerning its reliance on LLMs for data generation and evaluation, which may introduce biases and errors.
- There is a need for more comprehensive comparisons with other methods, including the latest and most relevant works, to establish the superiority of the proposed method.
- The paper does not sufficiently address the computational costs and the efficiency of the method, which could be a significant concern for practical applications.
- The method's effectiveness in other domains and tasks, such as language modeling or general reasoning, is not explored, which could limit the understanding of its full potential.

**Questions:**
- Could you clarify how the diversity of the generated data is evaluated, particularly in terms of whether it is truly novel or just different variations of existing data?
- How does the method ensure that the generated data is not biased or influenced by the LLM's inherent biases and errors?
- In the context of the actor-critic framework, how does the method handle the potential for the critic to be biased by the actor's generation process?
- Can you provide more details on the computational costs and the efficiency of the method, especially in terms of the number of samples required and the time taken for data generation and evaluation?
- How does the method perform in other tasks and domains, such as language modeling or general reasoning?
- Can you discuss the potential of extending the method to include multiple actor and critic models, or incorporating different strategies to enhance exploration?
- How does the method compare with other approaches that use LLMs for data generation and evaluation, particularly in terms of performance and efficiency?
