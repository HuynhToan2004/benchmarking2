 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel method called CLIP with Guided Cropping (GC-CLIP) to enhance zero-shot classification performance of CLIP by utilizing bounding boxes from an open-vocabulary object detection model (OWL-ViT). GC-CLIP crops images based on these bounding boxes and averages logits from multiple crops to improve robustness. The method is evaluated on two datasets, ImageNetS919 and CUB, showing improved performance on small object subsets. However, the method's effectiveness is questioned on larger object subsets, and the paper lacks comprehensive evaluations on other datasets and with different detection models. Additionally, the paper's presentation and clarity need improvement, with concerns about the novelty and the depth of the experimental validation.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel method, CLIP with Guided Cropping (GC-CLIP), which utilizes bounding boxes from an open-vocabulary object detection model (OWL-ViT) to guide cropping and enhance zero-shot classification performance of CLIP.
- The method is evaluated on two datasets, ImageNetS919 and CUB, showing improved performance on small object subsets.
- The paper is well-organized and easy to follow, making it accessible to readers.
- The proposed method is simple and effective, as demonstrated by the experimental results.
- The paper provides a clear explanation of the method and its implementation, which aids in understanding the approach.

**Weaknesses:**
- The method's effectiveness is not convincingly demonstrated across different object sizes and datasets. The experiments are limited to small object subsets, and the method's performance on larger object subsets is not explored.
- The paper lacks comprehensive evaluations on other datasets and with different detection models, which limits the generalizability of the findings.
- The paper's presentation and clarity need improvement. There are issues with the formatting and presentation of figures and tables, which could confuse readers.
- The novelty of the method is questionable as it primarily involves cropping images based on bounding boxes from an object detection model, which is a standard technique in object detection.
- The paper does not adequately address the limitations of the method, such as the potential for over-cropping which could lead to loss of important contextual information.
- The experimental setup and results are not convincingly presented, with some results (e.g., in Table 1) being misleading or unclear.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors clarify the discrepancy in the results between Table 1 and the results reported in the paper, particularly the performance of GC-CLIP on ImageNetS919 and CUB?
- How does the method perform when applied to different object detection models, such as ViLD and GLIPv2?
- Could the authors provide more details on the training and evaluation of the OWL-ViT model used in the experiments?
- How does the method handle different object sizes and shapes, particularly in scenarios where the object of interest may not be a square or a rectangle?
- Can the authors provide more visual examples of the method's performance, particularly on datasets with different object sizes and shapes?
- What are the specific improvements in zero-shot classification performance that can be attributed to the Guided Cropping method, and how do these improvements compare to other state-of-the-art methods?
