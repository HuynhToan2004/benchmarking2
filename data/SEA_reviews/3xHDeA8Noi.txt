 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a new second-order optimizer named Sophia, which utilizes a lightweight estimate of the diagonal Hessian as a preconditioner to enhance the training efficiency of large language models (LLMs). The optimizer, which includes a clipping mechanism to manage update sizes, is designed to converge faster and more efficiently than existing methods like Adam. Sophia is evaluated on various LLM sizes and tasks, showing a 2x speed-up in training steps and a reduction in wall-clock time and total compute. Despite its promising results, concerns are raised about the novelty of the diagonal Hessian preconditioner and the limited evaluation on different architectures and tasks.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, with clear and easy-to-follow content, including a clear motivation and a well-structured methodology.
- The proposed optimizer, Sophia, is shown to be efficient in training large language models (LLMs), with a focus on reducing training time and computational resources.
- The use of a diagonal Hessian preconditioner and a clipping mechanism is innovative and contributes to the optimization process by controlling the update sizes and managing the Hessian's inaccuracies.
- The paper includes a detailed experimental setup, including comparisons with other optimizers and a thorough analysis of the proposed methods.
- The results are convincing, showing significant improvements in training speed and computational efficiency, which is crucial for large-scale pretraining of LLMs.

**Weaknesses:**
- The novelty of the diagonal Hessian preconditioner is questioned, as similar approaches have been used in previous works.
- The evaluation of the proposed method is limited to LLM tasks, and it would be beneficial to see how it performs on other types of tasks such as encoder-decoder models or other architectures like BERT.
- There is a lack of discussion on the limitations of the proposed method, which could provide a more balanced view of its applicability and effectiveness.
- The paper could benefit from more detailed comparisons with other optimizers, such as Lion, to clarify the advantages of Sophia.
- The paper does not provide sufficient details on the hyperparameter tuning, which is crucial for reproducibility and understanding the robustness of the results.
- The paper could benefit from a broader evaluation across different model architectures and training datasets to validate the generalizability of the findings.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors provide more details on the hyperparameter tuning process, including the specific values used and the rationale behind their choice?
- How does the proposed method perform when applied to different model architectures, such as encoder-decoder models or other types of LLMs?
- Can the authors discuss the limitations of the proposed method and how these might affect its applicability in various scenarios?
- It would be interesting to see a comparison of the proposed method with other optimizers, such as Lion, on different tasks to clarify the advantages of Sophia.
- How does the proposed method handle the issue of non-convexity in the training process, and what are the implications for the model's generalization and performance?
