 **Summary:**
The paper addresses the angular synchronization problem, a critical task in sensor network localization and phase retrieval, by proposing a novel method named GNNSync. This method leverages directed graph neural networks (GNNs) to handle the angular synchronization problem, particularly the heterogeneous k-synchronization setting. The authors introduce a cycle consistency loss function to improve robustness against noise and outliers, and demonstrate the effectiveness of their approach through extensive experiments, showcasing superior performance compared to existing methods. The paper also discusses the limitations and potential extensions of the proposed method, including its applicability to other groups and its performance in real-world scenarios.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel approach to angular synchronization by leveraging graph neural networks, specifically GNNSync, which effectively handles the heterogeneous k-synchronization setting.
- The proposed method demonstrates superior performance in high-noise regimes, showcasing its robustness and effectiveness.
- The paper is well-written, clear, and easy to follow, making it accessible to a broad audience.
- Extensive experiments are conducted, demonstrating the effectiveness of the proposed method across various synthetic and real-world datasets.
- The paper includes a detailed ablation study, which helps in understanding the contributions of different components of the proposed method.
- The authors provide a comprehensive review of related literature, highlighting the novelty of their approach.
