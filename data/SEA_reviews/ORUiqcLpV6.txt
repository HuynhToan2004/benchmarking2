 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel approach to 3D visual grounding by formulating the task as a sequence-to-sequence (Seq2Seq) problem, where the prediction of a chain of anchors precedes the final target object. This approach, termed CoT3DRef, utilizes a pseudo-label generator to create training data without manual annotations, which is then used to train the model on a limited dataset. The framework is designed to be interpretable and data-efficient, achieving state-of-the-art results on benchmarks like Nr3D, Sr3D, and ScanRefer. The paper also addresses the interpretability and data efficiency challenges in 3D visual grounding, offering a potential solution by predicting a chain of anchors before the final target.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel approach to 3D visual grounding by formulating the task as a sequence-to-sequence (Seq2Seq) problem, which is a significant advancement in the field.
- The proposed method, CoT3DRef, is highly data-efficient and can achieve state-of-the-art results on benchmarks like Nr3D, Sr3D, and ScanRefer without requiring manual annotations.
- The use of a pseudo-label generator to create training data without manual annotations is a notable contribution, reducing the need for extensive human intervention.
- The paper is well-written, easy to follow, and provides a clear explanation of the methodology, including detailed experimental results and ablation studies.
- The approach is highly interpretable, which is crucial for understanding the model's decisions and improving transparency.
- The method can be easily integrated into existing 3D visual grounding frameworks, making it a versatile solution.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a thorough comparison with other state-of-the-art methods in terms of data efficiency and interpretability. More detailed comparisons and discussions on the advantages of the proposed method over existing approaches are needed.
- The paper could benefit from a more detailed explanation of the pseudo-label generation process, particularly how it handles the variability in natural language descriptions.
- There is a need for more clarity on the training data used, especially concerning the pseudo-label generation process and its accuracy.
- The paper does not sufficiently address the potential biases introduced by the pseudo-label generation process, which could affect the model's performance and generalization.
- The paper could benefit from a broader evaluation on different datasets and more complex scenarios to validate the generalizability of the proposed method.
- The paper does not discuss the limitations of the pseudo-label generation process, which is a critical component of the proposed method.
- The paper lacks a discussion on the limitations of the proposed method and potential negative societal impacts.
