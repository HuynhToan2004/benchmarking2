 **Summary:**
The paper introduces Over-Smoothing adversarial Injection (OSI), a novel approach to enhancing Graph Injection Attacks (GIA) by leveraging the over-smoothing issue in Graph Neural Networks (GNNs). OSI employs a Smooth Injection Loss (SIL) to fine-tune the adjacency matrix, aiming to amplify over-smoothing and improve the attack's power. The methodology is supported by two metrics, Feature Shift Rate (FSR) and Perturbation Shift Rate (PSR), which are highly correlated with singular values of the adjacency matrix. The paper demonstrates the effectiveness of OSI through experiments on various datasets, showing significant improvements in attack capabilities compared to existing methods. However, the paper lacks clarity in some sections, particularly in the methodology and experimental setup, and the scalability of the proposed method on large-scale graphs is a concern.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel approach to enhancing Graph Injection Attacks (GIA) by leveraging the over-smoothing issue in GNNs, which is a significant advancement in the field.
- The proposed Smooth Injection Loss (SIL) is innovative and well-motivated, effectively addressing the over-smoothing problem in GNNs.
- The paper is well-written, clear, and easy to follow, making complex concepts accessible to readers.
- The experimental results are comprehensive, demonstrating the effectiveness of the proposed method across various datasets and models.
- The paper provides a theoretical analysis of the proposed method, enhancing the credibility and depth of the research.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a clear explanation of how the proposed method, OSI, integrates with existing GIA methods and how it enhances their attack power.
- The methodology section is not well-explained, particularly the process of injecting nodes and the optimization of features, which could benefit from additional clarity.
- There are inconsistencies and unclear definitions in the paper, such as the definition of FSR and the relationship between different metrics like FSR and PSR.
- The scalability of the proposed method on large-scale graphs is a concern, as computing singular values for the entire adjacency matrix can be computationally intensive.
- The paper does not sufficiently address the computational complexity of the proposed method, which could limit its practical application on large datasets.
- The paper lacks a detailed comparison with existing methods, particularly in terms of attack performance and the impact of different hyperparameters on the attack's effectiveness.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors clarify how the proposed method, OSI, integrates with existing GIA methods and how it enhances their attack power?
- How does the paper ensure that the injected nodes are not easily detectable by defense models, particularly in the black-box setting?
- Can the authors provide more details on the optimization process of features for injected nodes and the selection of injected edges?
- How does the paper address the issue of over-smoothing in the context of GNNs, particularly in relation to the over-smoothing issue mentioned in the paper?
- Could the authors provide a more detailed explanation of the computational complexity of the proposed method and how it scales with the size of the graph?
- How does the paper ensure that the proposed method is not only effective but also transferable to different GNN architectures?
- Could the authors provide a more detailed comparison with existing methods in terms of attack performance and the impact of different hyperparameters on the attack's effectiveness?
