 **Summary:**
The paper introduces BIOLS, a method for learning latent causal models from low-level data, focusing on linear Gaussian models. The approach involves Bayesian inference over the latent causal variables, structure, and parameters, using a variational inference framework. The method is evaluated on synthetic and image datasets, showing improved performance over existing methods. However, the paper has been critiqued for its lack of novelty, limited scalability, and inadequate experimental validation against state-of-the-art methods. Concerns were also raised about the clarity of the methodological presentation and the need for more robust empirical evidence to support the claims.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, making it easy to follow, and the authors have provided a detailed discussion on related work, which aids in understanding the context of their research.
- The proposed method, BIOLS, is novel in its approach to learning latent causal models from low-level data, which is an important and under-explored problem in the field of causal inference.
- The paper provides a thorough evaluation of the method, including synthetic and image datasets, which demonstrates the effectiveness of the proposed method.
- The paper includes a clear presentation of the methodology, which aids in understanding the technical details and the rationale behind the chosen approach.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed discussion on the limitations of the proposed method, which is crucial for understanding its applicability and potential shortcomings.
- The novelty of the proposed method is questionable as it appears to be a combination of existing methods without significant innovation.
- The experimental evaluation is limited, and the results are not convincingly superior to existing methods, particularly in terms of scalability and performance on more complex datasets.
- There are several minor typographical and grammatical errors that need correction to enhance the professional quality of the paper.
- The paper could benefit from a more comprehensive discussion on the identifiability of the causal model, especially given the assumptions made about the structure and parameters of the latent causal model.

**Questions:**
- How does the proposed method compare with existing methods in terms of scalability and performance on more complex datasets?
- Can the authors provide more details on the identifiability of the causal model, especially under the assumptions made about the structure and parameters of the latent causal model?
- What are the specific advantages of the proposed method over existing methods, particularly in terms of computational efficiency and accuracy of causal structure recovery?
- How does the method perform when the number of latent variables is larger than the number of observable variables?
- Could the authors clarify the role and impact of the noise in the model, particularly in scenarios where the noise is not fixed?
- How does the method handle the issue of potential overfitting to the training data, especially in scenarios where the data is limited?
