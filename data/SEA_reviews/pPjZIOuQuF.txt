 **Summary:**
The paper introduces RepoBench, a benchmark designed to evaluate repository-level code auto-completion systems, focusing on Python and Java programming languages. RepoBench comprises three interconnected tasks: RepoBench-R, RepoBench-C, and RepoBench-P, each assessing different aspects of code retrieval, code completion, and end-to-end pipeline performance. The benchmark includes a variety of datasets and models, including UniXcoder, CodeBERT, and StarCoder, to evaluate the effectiveness of different retrieval strategies and code completion methods. The paper provides a comprehensive evaluation of these models and discusses their performance in real-world scenarios, highlighting the importance of managing extended contexts and maintaining generalizability.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, clear, and easy to follow, with a clear motivation and a comprehensive evaluation of various retrieval methods and code completion models.
- The proposed benchmark, RepoBench, is a significant contribution to the field, providing a robust evaluation framework for repository-level code auto-completion systems.
- The paper includes a variety of datasets and models, such as UniXcoder, CodeBERT, and StarCoder, which are essential for evaluating the effectiveness of different retrieval strategies and code completion methods.
- The experiments are well-designed, and the results are comprehensive, providing valuable insights into the performance of different models in real-world scenarios.
- The paper is timely and addresses a critical gap in the literature by focusing on the evaluation of repository-level code auto-completion systems, which is crucial for advancing the field.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a discussion on the limitations of the proposed benchmark, which is essential for understanding its applicability and scope.
- There is a lack of comparison with other existing benchmarks like CoCoMIC and RepoEval, which could provide a more comprehensive evaluation of the proposed benchmark.
- The paper does not adequately address the issue of data leakage, which could affect the validity of the results.
- The paper does not include a detailed discussion on the impact of different retrieval methods on the performance of the code completion models, which could provide valuable insights into the effectiveness of different retrieval strategies.
- The evaluation is limited to specific programming languages (Python and Java), which might not fully capture the diversity of programming styles and requirements across different languages.

**Questions:**
- Can you provide more details on how the datasets are constructed, particularly how the cross-file context is determined and how it impacts the performance of the models?
- How does the paper ensure that the evaluation is not biased towards specific models or retrieval methods?
- Could you elaborate on the decision to focus on Python and Java, and how this choice affects the generalizability of the findings?
- How do the authors address the issue of data leakage, and what steps are taken to ensure that the test data does not overlap with the training data?
- What are the specific benefits of using UniXcoder over other retrieval methods, and how does it enhance the performance of the code completion models?
- Can you discuss the potential impact of different retrieval methods on the performance of the code completion models, and how these methods could be optimized to improve overall system performance?
