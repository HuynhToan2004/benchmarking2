 **Summary:**
The paper investigates the effects of underrepresentation in contrastive learning, focusing on how it can lead to representation harms where underrepresented groups' representations collapse into those of similar groups. This phenomenon is studied using both theoretical and empirical methods, including a stochastic block model and causal mediation analysis. The authors demonstrate that these harms can lead to allocation harms in downstream tasks, highlighting the need for mitigation strategies. The paper is structured around these two aspects, with the first focusing on theoretical and empirical demonstrations and the second on causal mediation analysis. Despite its strengths in addressing an important issue, the paper has been critiqued for its limited scope in terms of datasets and models, and for not providing a clear path for mitigating the identified harms.

**Strengths:**
- The paper addresses an important and timely issue of underrepresentation in contrastive learning, which is relevant in the current context of large-scale, uncurated datasets.
- The authors provide a thorough theoretical analysis, including a stochastic block model, which is a novel approach in this context.
- The paper is well-written, clear, and easy to follow, with detailed and well-organized figures that effectively illustrate the main findings.
- The authors demonstrate the impact of underrepresentation on the performance of minority groups, a significant issue in supervised learning, in the context of self-supervised learning.
- The paper includes both theoretical and empirical analyses, which support the findings and provide a solid foundation for the conclusions drawn.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper could benefit from a more diverse set of datasets and models to validate the findings across different contexts and scenarios.
- The experimental setup and the metrics used are not clearly explained, which could limit the reproducibility and understanding of the results.
- The paper primarily focuses on image datasets, which might not fully capture the complexity of underrepresentation in other domains such as text or audio.
- The proposed solutions for mitigating the harms are not clearly defined, which could limit the practical applicability of the findings.
- The paper lacks a comprehensive discussion on how the findings relate to existing literature on algorithmic bias and representation learning, which could enrich the context and relevance of the study.
- There are some typographical and formatting errors that need to be corrected for clarity and professional presentation.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors clarify the experimental setup and the metrics used in the experiments to ensure reproducibility and better understanding of the results?
- How do the findings of this study compare or contrast with existing literature on algorithmic bias and representation learning, particularly in terms of the impact of underrepresentation on model performance?
- Could the authors provide more detailed examples or scenarios where the proposed solutions for mitigating the harms could be applied, and discuss the potential limitations or challenges associated with these solutions?
- How do the authors address the potential limitations or ethical considerations of their work, particularly in terms of the impact on underrepresented groups and the broader societal implications of their findings?
