 **Summary:**
The paper investigates the impact of personalization techniques on fairness in federated learning (FL) by clustering clients based on data distributions and employing personalized FL algorithms. The authors demonstrate that these techniques can enhance local fairness compared to standalone training, especially in scenarios with imbalanced groups. They provide both theoretical analysis and experimental evidence using the ACS datasets to support their claims. The paper explores how personalized FL algorithms like Clustered FL and MAML FL can improve fairness by addressing the unique needs of different client groups. However, the paper's theoretical analysis is limited to specific conditions, and the experimental scope is narrow, focusing on two datasets and two groups.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, clear, and easy to follow, with a good balance of theoretical analysis and experimental results.
- The authors provide a comprehensive literature review, covering both personalized FL and fair FL, and discuss the potential for improving fairness through personalization techniques.
- The paper is original in its approach, highlighting the potential of personalization techniques to improve fairness in FL, which is an underexplored area.
- The experiments conducted are robust and showcase the effectiveness of personalization techniques in enhancing fairness, particularly in scenarios with imbalanced groups.
- The theoretical analysis is sound, providing a solid foundation for the empirical results.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper's theoretical analysis is limited to specific conditions, such as Gaussian distributions and identical data distributions within clusters, which may not generalize well to other scenarios.
- The experimental setup is narrow, focusing on two datasets (ACSIncome and ACSEmployment) and two groups, which may not adequately demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed methods in more diverse or complex settings.
- The paper does not sufficiently discuss the limitations of the proposed methods, particularly the assumptions made in the theoretical analysis and their practical implications.
- The paper lacks a broader comparison with existing fair FL algorithms, which could have provided a more comprehensive understanding of the proposed methods' advantages or disadvantages.
- The paper could benefit from a more detailed discussion on the impact of different personalization techniques on fairness and accuracy, as well as the scalability of the proposed methods in larger, more complex FL settings.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors clarify the assumptions made in the theoretical analysis, particularly the assumption of identical data distributions within clusters?
- How do the proposed methods perform in more complex or diverse FL settings, such as those with multiple clusters or more groups?
- Could the authors provide more detailed comparisons with existing fair FL algorithms, particularly in terms of fairness and accuracy trade-offs?
- How do the authors address the scalability of their methods in larger, more complex FL settings?
- Can the authors elaborate on the impact of different personalization techniques on fairness and accuracy, and discuss potential trade-offs between these two aspects?
- Could the authors provide more details on the experimental setup, particularly the choice of datasets and the rationale behind the experimental design?
