 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel gradient-based attack, termed $\sigma$-zero, designed to optimize adversarial perturbations under the $\ell_0$ norm, which is typically challenging due to the non-differentiability and non-convex nature of the $\ell_0$ norm. The method leverages an approximation of the $\ell_0$ norm, allowing for gradient-based optimization and efficient adversarial example generation. The approach is evaluated across multiple datasets, including MNIST, CIFAR10, and ImageNet, and the results show that $\sigma$-zero can outperform existing methods in terms of success rate, perturbation size, and computational efficiency. The paper also discusses the limitations and potential ethical implications of the proposed attack, such as its reliance on a white-box model assumption and potential misuse.

**Strengths:**
- The paper addresses a significant and understudied problem in the field of adversarial robustness by proposing a novel gradient-based attack that effectively optimizes adversarial perturbations under the $\ell_0$ norm.
- The methodology is well-articulated, with clear descriptions of the proposed approach and its theoretical underpinnings, which enhances the paper's accessibility and reproducibility.
- Extensive experimental evaluations are conducted across multiple datasets, demonstrating the effectiveness and efficiency of the proposed method compared to existing state-of-the-art attacks.
- The paper is well-structured, with clear and concise writing that effectively communicates the key contributions and findings, making it a valuable resource for both academic and practical applications.
- The proposed $\sigma$-zero attack is shown to be effective in finding adversarial examples with smaller perturbation sizes, which is a significant advantage over existing methods.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed discussion on the limitations and ethical implications of the proposed attack, particularly in scenarios where the attack could be misused or where it might not be effective.
- The experimental results, while extensive, are not sufficiently detailed, particularly in terms of the statistical significance and reliability of the reported results.
- The paper could benefit from a more thorough discussion of related work and how the proposed method compares to existing approaches, especially in terms of novelty and practical applicability.
- The paper's presentation could be improved, particularly in terms of readability and the clarity of figures and tables, which could enhance the paper's accessibility and impact.
- The paper's focus on white-box attacks may limit its applicability in real-world scenarios where such assumptions are not practical.
