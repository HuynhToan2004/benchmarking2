 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel Tree Cross Attention (TCA) module aimed at reducing the computational complexity of Cross Attention in machine learning models. TCA organizes input tokens into a tree structure, allowing for logarithmic retrieval of tokens during inference, thereby improving efficiency. The authors propose a reinforcement learning method to optimize the TCA module and demonstrate its effectiveness through various tasks, showing that it can outperform existing methods like Perceiver IO with fewer tokens. The paper also discusses the potential of TCA in reducing memory usage and improving inference speed.

**Strengths:**
- The paper addresses a significant and relevant problem by focusing on the reduction of memory usage and improving inference speed, which is crucial for large-scale applications.
- The proposed Tree Cross Attention (TCA) module is a novel approach that leverages reinforcement learning to optimize the selection of tokens, which is a unique contribution to the field.
- The paper is well-written, making it easy to follow and understand, which is essential for academic publications.
- The methodology is supported by a comprehensive set of experiments that demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed approach, showing improvements over existing methods like Perceiver IO.
- The paper provides a clear and detailed explanation of the proposed methods, including the use of reinforcement learning and the construction of a tree structure for efficient attention mechanisms.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed discussion on the limitations and potential negative societal impacts of the proposed methods, which is crucial for a comprehensive understanding of the technology's implications.
- The experimental results presented in the paper are not convincing, particularly in the context of the tasks used, which are not well-suited to demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed methods.
- There is a lack of clarity in the presentation of the paper, especially in the explanation of the TCA module and its integration with the encoder and cross-attention components.
- The paper does not sufficiently compare the proposed methods with existing state-of-the-art models, which is necessary to establish the novelty and effectiveness of the proposed methods.
- The paper does not provide a detailed discussion on the computational complexity and memory usage of the proposed methods, which are critical factors in assessing their practical applicability.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors clarify the specifics of the TCA module's integration with the encoder and cross-attention components, and how the information from the encoder is passed to the TCA?
- How does the TCA module perform when applied to different types of tasks, such as language modeling or image classification?
- Could the authors provide a more detailed comparison of the proposed methods with existing state-of-the-art models, particularly in terms of computational efficiency and memory usage?
- How does the proposed method handle the scalability issues, especially in large-scale applications where the tree structure might become unwieldy?
- Can the authors elaborate on the choice of tasks for the experiments and how they demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed methods in real-world scenarios?
