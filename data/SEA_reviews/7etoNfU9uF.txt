 **Summary:**
The paper introduces SpikePoint, a novel SNN architecture for processing event camera data, leveraging a point-based approach to handle both global and local features. This architecture is designed to process event streams efficiently, with a focus on reducing computational complexity and energy consumption. The methodology involves a sliding window approach to segment event clouds and a grouping strategy to manage the data. The paper claims state-of-the-art performance on several event-based action recognition datasets, demonstrating significant improvements in accuracy and efficiency compared to existing methods. However, concerns about the novelty of the approach, the clarity of the methodology, and the fairness of the comparisons are raised.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel approach to processing event camera data using SNNs, which is a significant advancement in the field.
- The methodology is well-articulated, with clear explanations of the proposed techniques and their implementation.
- The paper demonstrates impressive results, outperforming existing methods in terms of accuracy and efficiency, particularly in action recognition tasks.
- The use of a sliding window and grouping strategy for managing event data is innovative and contributes to the efficiency of the proposed method.
- The paper is well-written and easy to follow, which facilitates understanding of the proposed methodologies and results.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed comparison with state-of-the-art methods, particularly in terms of computational complexity and energy consumption.
- There is a significant overlap in the methodology with existing works, such as the PointNet++, which might limit the perceived novelty of the approach.
- The paper does not adequately address the scalability of the proposed method, particularly in handling larger or more complex event datasets.
- The paper suffers from several presentation issues, including poor figure quality and unclear explanations of certain methodological aspects.
- The novelty of the proposed method is questioned due to its similarity to existing works, and the paper does not sufficiently differentiate its contributions.
- The paper does not adequately discuss the limitations of the proposed method, which is crucial for understanding its applicability and potential improvements.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors provide a more detailed comparison with state-of-the-art methods, particularly in terms of computational complexity and energy consumption?
- How does the proposed method perform in scenarios where the event data is more complex or varied?
- Can the authors clarify the methodology and the rationale behind the use of certain techniques, such as the grouping strategy and the use of the ResF block?
- How does the proposed method handle different types of event data, such as those with varying frame rates or resolutions?
- Could the authors provide more detailed results and comparisons, particularly in the ablation studies and the power consumption analysis?
- How does the proposed method compare to other recent works in terms of accuracy and efficiency, especially in the context of event-based action recognition?
