 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel diffusion model framework called DiffEnc, which incorporates a time-dependent encoder that modifies the diffusion process by predicting the denoised image at each time step. This encoder is only utilized during training and not during sampling, thereby enhancing the model's flexibility without affecting sampling time. The authors also explore the variance ratio between the generative and reverse processes, proposing that the optimal variance ratio is 1 in the continuous-time limit. Experimental results on CIFAR-10 and MNIST datasets demonstrate the effectiveness of DiffEnc in achieving state-of-the-art likelihood. However, the paper is criticized for its limited evaluation scope, focusing only on small-scale datasets, and for not adequately comparing with existing methods like latent diffusion models.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel diffusion model framework, DiffEnc, which effectively integrates a time-dependent encoder to modify the diffusion process, enhancing the model's flexibility without affecting sampling time.
- The authors provide a thorough analysis of the assumption of equal variances in the generative and reverse processes, demonstrating that this assumption is critical for maintaining the well-defined Evidence Lower Bound (ELBO) in the continuous-time limit.
- The paper is well-written, with clear explanations and detailed derivations that support the proposed methodologies, making it accessible and understandable to readers.
- The experimental results on CIFAR-10 and MNIST datasets showcase the effectiveness of DiffEnc in achieving state-of-the-art likelihood, highlighting its potential for further research and application.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper's evaluation is limited to small-scale datasets like CIFAR-10 and MNIST, which may not fully demonstrate the generalizability or effectiveness of the proposed method on larger, more complex datasets.
- The motivation behind the time-dependent encoder is not clearly articulated, making it difficult for readers to fully grasp its necessity and impact.
- The paper lacks a detailed discussion on how the time-dependent encoder affects the model's performance, particularly in terms of its impact on the Evidence Lower Bound (ELBO) and the overall model efficiency.
- There is a lack of comparison with existing methods such as latent diffusion models, which could provide a better understanding of the proposed method's advantages and limitations.
- The paper does not provide sufficient experimental details, such as the architecture of the neural network used for prediction, which could affect the reproducibility and understanding of the results.

**Questions:**
- How does the time-dependent encoder affect the model's performance in terms of ELBO and overall model efficiency?
- Can the authors clarify the motivation behind the time-dependent encoder and its impact on the model's flexibility and computational efficiency?
- How does the proposed method compare with existing methods, particularly latent diffusion models, in terms of performance and efficiency?
- Could the authors provide more detailed experimental setups, such as the architecture of the neural network used for prediction, to enhance the reproducibility and understanding of the results?
- How does the proposed method handle the variance ratio between the generative and reverse processes, and what are the implications of this ratio on the model's performance?
