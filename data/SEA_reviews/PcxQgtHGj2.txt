 **Summary:**
The paper investigates the use of synthetic data for pre-training in offline reinforcement learning (RL), specifically focusing on the Decision Transformer (DT) and Conservative Q-Learning (CQL) models. It demonstrates that synthetic data, even when generated from simple Markov Decision Processes (MDPs), can enhance the performance of these models, often surpassing the results achieved with pre-training on large language datasets. The study also explores the impact of different data generation strategies and hyperparameters, providing insights into the effectiveness of synthetic data in RL settings.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, with clear and concise descriptions of the methodology, results, and conclusions.
- The experiments are comprehensive, covering various aspects of the proposed methods, and the results are convincing, demonstrating the effectiveness of synthetic data pre-training.
- The paper introduces a novel approach to pre-training with synthetic data, which is simple yet effective, and shows that it can improve performance in offline RL tasks.
- Theoretical insights are provided, which help explain the observed performance improvements, contributing to a deeper understanding of the pre-training process.
- The paper is original in its focus on the use of synthetic data for pre-training, which is a significant contribution to the field of offline RL.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a broader discussion on the generalizability of the findings to other offline RL algorithms and different types of synthetic data.
- There is a limited exploration of the impact of different hyperparameters and their effects on the performance of the pre-trained models.
- The paper could benefit from more detailed experimental results and comparisons with other pre-training methods to provide a more comprehensive evaluation.
- Some related works and recent advancements in pre-training methods for offline RL are not discussed, which could enrich the context and relevance of the study.
- The paper's scope is somewhat limited as it primarily focuses on the Decision Transformer and Conservative Q-Learning, without exploring other popular algorithms like Trajectory Transformer or RORL.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors provide a more detailed discussion on the generalizability of the findings to other offline RL algorithms and different types of synthetic data?
- How do the authors justify the choice of synthetic data over other types of pre-training data, such as those derived from real-world environments or other synthetic methods?
- Could the authors elaborate on the theoretical insights provided and how they contribute to the understanding of the pre-training process?
- In terms of the experimental setup, could the authors provide more detailed results, such as performance metrics across different seeds and environments, to strengthen the conclusions drawn from the study?
- How do the authors address the potential limitations and ethical considerations of using synthetic data in RL, particularly in terms of fairness and generalization to real-world scenarios?
