 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel neural network-based multigrid solver for linear PDEs, integrating U-Net and the multigrid method. This approach aims to address the limitations of traditional numerical solvers by learning the multigrid operators and utilizing a residual loss metric for unsupervised training. The methodology is validated through experiments on Poisson's equations, demonstrating improved performance over existing methods. However, the paper is criticized for its limited scope to linear PDEs, potential overfitting issues due to the dataset's size, and lack of comparison with more recent methods.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel approach to solving PDEs using a neural network-based multigrid solver, which is a significant advancement in the field.
- The methodology is well-explained, making it accessible and understandable to a broad audience.
- The paper is well-structured and clearly written, with a clear motivation and a solid theoretical foundation.
- The proposed method is innovative, combining the U-Net architecture with the multigrid method, which is a promising approach for solving PDEs.
- The paper provides a comprehensive review of related work, demonstrating a thorough understanding of the existing literature.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper's focus on linear PDEs limits its applicability to a broader range of PDEs, which could be a significant drawback for practical applications.
- The dataset used in the experiments is relatively small (16,000 data points), which raises concerns about the generalization ability of the model, especially in more complex scenarios.
- The paper lacks a detailed discussion on the limitations of the proposed method, which is crucial for understanding its scope and applicability.
- There is a lack of comparison with more recent and relevant methods, such as those using Fourier neural operators, which could have provided a more comprehensive evaluation of the proposed method.
- The paper does not adequately address the computational cost and efficiency of the proposed method, which is crucial for practical applications.
- The paper's experimental setup is somewhat limited, focusing primarily on Poisson's equations, which may not adequately demonstrate the method's effectiveness in more complex or varied scenarios.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors clarify the specific advantages of their method over existing methods, particularly in terms of computational efficiency and generalization ability?
- How does the proposed method compare to other recent methods, such as those using Fourier neural operators?
- Can the authors provide more details on the training data used, including the number of iterations and the size of the dataset?
- How does the proposed method handle non-linear PDEs, and what are the potential limitations in this regard?
- Could the authors provide more detailed results on the computational cost and efficiency of their method, especially in comparison to traditional numerical methods?
- How does the proposed method ensure that the neural solver converges to the correct solution, and what are the implications if it does not?
