 **Summary:**
The paper introduces ALD-VAE, a method for automatically determining the optimal latent space dimensionality in Variational Autoencoders (VAEs) during training, without the need for manual tuning. This method, which involves gradually decreasing the latent space size and evaluating the reconstruction and clustering quality using metrics such as FID score, Reconstruction loss, and Silhouette score, is claimed to be faster and more effective than traditional methods. The method is tested on four image datasets, demonstrating its ability to achieve comparable results to models trained with fixed latent dimensions. However, the paper has been critiqued for its limited experimental scope, lack of comparison with state-of-the-art methods, and insufficient theoretical justification.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-organized, with clear and concise writing, and the figures are well-designed, enhancing the understanding of the content.
- The proposed method is straightforward, easy to implement, and the experimental results demonstrate its effectiveness in reducing the latent space dimensionality without compromising the quality of the reconstructed data.
- The paper provides a clear explanation of the methodology, including the algorithm and its implementation, which is helpful for reproducibility.
- The experiments show that the proposed method can achieve similar or better results compared to models trained with fixed latent dimensions, and it is faster than traditional methods like grid search.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a thorough comparison with state-of-the-art methods, which is crucial for establishing the effectiveness and novelty of the proposed method.
- The experimental evaluation is limited to a few datasets, which may not adequately demonstrate the generalizability of the method across different types of data.
- The paper does not provide a theoretical analysis of the proposed method, which could strengthen the argument for its effectiveness and applicability.
- The method's effectiveness is not convincingly demonstrated, especially in terms of its ability to converge to the optimal latent space dimensionality across different seeds, which raises concerns about its reliability and robustness.
- The paper lacks a discussion on the limitations of the proposed method, which is essential for understanding its applicability and potential drawbacks.

**Questions:**
- How does the proposed method compare with other methods that automatically determine the optimal latent space dimensionality in VAEs?
- Can the method be extended to other types of data, such as text or robotic actions?
- Is there a theoretical analysis that supports the effectiveness of the proposed method?
- How does the method handle the issue of different optima across different seeds, and what implications does this have for the reliability and robustness of the method?
- Could you provide more details on the experimental setup, such as the specifics of the datasets used and the training parameters?
- How does the method perform when applied to more complex or diverse datasets, and what are the implications of this for its practical applicability?
