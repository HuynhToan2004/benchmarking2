 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a method for propagating stable probability distributions through neural networks using local linearization, aiming to minimize the total variation distance. This approach is demonstrated to be effective in quantifying uncertainties in neural network predictions, particularly in out-of-distribution data. The methodology is compared against other uncertainty quantification methods and shown to be competitive in terms of accuracy and computational efficiency. The paper also discusses the practical implications of the proposed method in various applications, including calibrated confidence intervals and selective prediction. Despite its strengths, the paper has been critiqued for its limited novelty and the need for a more comprehensive comparison with other methods.

**Strengths:**
- The paper addresses an important problem of uncertainty quantification in neural networks, which is crucial for practical applications.
- The methodology is well-explained, and the paper is easy to follow, making it accessible to a broad audience.
- The proposed method is novel, particularly in its application to ReLU activations, and provides a simple yet effective approach to uncertainty quantification.
- The paper is well-organized, with clear figures and tables that aid in understanding the results and methodologies.
- The method is computationally efficient and shows good performance in both accuracy and runtime, which is a significant advantage over other methods.
- The paper provides a thorough evaluation of the method, comparing it against other uncertainty quantification methods and demonstrating its effectiveness in various applications.

**Weaknesses:**
- The novelty of the method is limited, as it primarily builds upon existing methods such as local linearization and stable distribution propagation.
- The paper lacks a comprehensive comparison with other methods, particularly in terms of computational efficiency and the robustness of the results across different datasets and architectures.
- There is a lack of discussion on the limitations of the method, such as its applicability to different types of neural networks and the potential for over-confidence in predictions.
- The paper could benefit from a more detailed discussion on the theoretical underpinnings of the method, particularly in relation to the choice of stable distributions and the implications of local linearization.
- The paper could be improved by including a more detailed discussion on the practical implications of the method, such as its application in real-world scenarios and the potential impact on model training and performance.

**Questions:**
- Can the method be extended to other types of neural networks, such as transformer-based models?
- How does the method perform in more complex datasets or scenarios, such as those involving image classification or natural language processing?
- Could the authors provide a more detailed comparison of the computational efficiency of their method against other methods, particularly in terms of the number of network evaluations required?
- How does the method handle the potential for over-confidence in predictions, and what measures can be taken to mitigate this?
- Can the authors discuss the potential implications of the method on model training and performance, particularly in terms of the impact on accuracy and training time?
- How does the method compare to other uncertainty quantification methods in terms of accuracy and practical applicability?
