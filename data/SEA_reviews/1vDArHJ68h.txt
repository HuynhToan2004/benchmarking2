 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a new model-based reinforcement learning (MBRL) agent named Recall to Imagine (R2I), which integrates a variant of the S4 model into the DreamerV3 framework. This integration aims to enhance long-term memory and credit assignment in challenging memory and credit assignment tasks, achieving state-of-the-art performance on several benchmarks including Memory Maze, BSuite, and POPGym. The methodology involves replacing the recurrent state-space model (RSSM) with a state space model (SSM), which allows for parallel computations during training and recurrent mode during inference, improving computational efficiency. Despite its strengths, the paper has been criticized for its lack of novelty, as the integration of S4 into DreamerV3 is seen as a straightforward application rather than a significant innovation.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, clear, and easy to follow, with a clear motivation and a well-structured presentation.
- The integration of the S4 model with DreamerV3 is a natural and effective approach, enhancing the model's ability to capture long-term dependencies and improving computational efficiency.
- The paper includes a thorough ablation study that demonstrates the impact of different design decisions, providing insights into the model's performance.
- The results on several benchmarks show that the proposed method outperforms previous state-of-the-art methods, indicating the effectiveness of the approach.
- The paper addresses the significant issue of long-term memory in reinforcement learning, which is crucial for solving complex tasks involving extended time gaps between actions and outcomes.

**Weaknesses:**
- The novelty of the paper is limited as the integration of S4 into DreamerV3 is seen as a straightforward application rather than a significant innovation.
- The paper lacks a detailed discussion on how the S4 model's ability to capture long-term dependencies translates into improved performance in model-based reinforcement learning (MBRL).
- The methodology section could be improved by including a more detailed explanation of the S4 model and its integration with DreamerV3.
- The paper does not adequately address how the method performs in different types of environments, particularly those with varying degrees of complexity or memory requirements.
- The paper does not include a discussion on the limitations of the proposed method, which could be a significant drawback for understanding the scope and applicability of the work.
