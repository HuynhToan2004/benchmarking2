 **Summary:**
The paper introduces NuwaDynamics, a novel causal spatio-temporal dynamics framework aimed at enhancing the predictive performance of deep learning models in spatiotemporal forecasting. The framework consists of two stages: "discovery" and "update." In the discovery stage, causal regions are identified using upstream self-supervision, and non-causal regions are updated to enhance the model's generalizability. The update stage involves transferring data to downstream tasks, refining the model's causal perception capabilities. The paper presents extensive experiments across six real-world benchmarks, demonstrating the effectiveness of the proposed framework in various scenarios. However, concerns were raised about the novelty of the approach, the clarity of presentation, and the depth of experimental validation, particularly in terms of the generalizability of the findings and the comparison with existing methods.

**Strengths:**
- The paper addresses the crucial issue of data scarcity in spatiotemporal prediction, which is a significant challenge in many real-world applications.
- The introduction of causal reasoning into spatiotemporal forecasting is a novel approach, providing a fresh perspective on the problem.
- The paper is well-organized and easy to follow, with a clear explanation of the proposed framework and its components.
- The framework is evaluated on six real-world benchmarks, demonstrating its effectiveness across various scenarios.
- The paper provides extensive experiments that showcase the framework's ability to enhance the generalizability of deep learning models in spatiotemporal forecasting.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a clear definition and explanation of the "causal" nature of the proposed framework, which is crucial for understanding its effectiveness and applicability.
- The novelty of the approach is questionable, as similar techniques have been previously explored in other works, such as those cited in the paper.
- The experimental results are not sufficiently convincing, with some datasets showing minimal performance improvements and others not being adequately validated.
- The paper lacks a detailed comparison with existing methods, particularly in terms of computational efficiency and generalizability across different datasets and scenarios.
- There is a lack of clarity in the presentation of the paper, with some figures and tables being hard to read or understand.
- The paper does not adequately address the limitations of the proposed framework, such as its applicability to different types of data and its scalability to larger datasets.

**Questions:**
- Could you provide a more detailed explanation of the "causal" nature of the proposed framework and how it enhances the predictive performance of deep learning models in spatiotemporal forecasting?
- How does the framework perform when applied to datasets with different characteristics, such as varying resolutions or data types?
- Can you clarify the experimental setup and results, particularly in scenarios where the performance improvements are minimal or not statistically significant?
- How does the proposed framework compare in terms of computational efficiency and generalizability to other existing methods in spatiotemporal forecasting?
- Could you elaborate on the methodology used in the "discovery" stage of the framework and how it contributes to the overall effectiveness of the model?
- How does the framework handle scenarios where the data is imbalanced or where the causal relationships are not clearly defined?
