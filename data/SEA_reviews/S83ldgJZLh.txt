 **Summary:**
The paper introduces SPADE, a structured pruning algorithm designed to reduce computational complexity in model-based deep learning (MBDL) networks. This method is applied to model-based deep learning (MBDL) networks to prune non-essential weights, improving test-time efficiency while maintaining competitive performance. The paper validates SPADE on two distinct inverse problems: compressed sensing MRI and image super-resolution, demonstrating significant speedups and performance degradation. The methodology includes three distinct fine-tuning strategies, each leveraging different resources, and the paper provides a comprehensive experimental evaluation, showcasing the effectiveness of SPADE in reducing computational complexity.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel application of structured pruning for model-based deep learning (MBDL) networks, which is the first of its kind.
- The proposed SPADE method demonstrates significant speedups in testing time while maintaining competitive performance, as shown through comprehensive experiments across various imaging problems, different MBDL networks, and different pruning ratios.
- The paper is well-organized, making it easy to follow, and provides a clear explanation of the methodology and experimental setup.
- The use of three distinct fine-tuning strategies tailored to different availability of resources such as pre-trained models and ground truth data enhances the practical applicability of SPADE.
- The experimental results are convincing, showing the effectiveness of SPADE in reducing computational complexity and maintaining performance.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper does not adequately compare SPADE with other pruning methods, such as unstructured pruning, which could provide a more comprehensive evaluation of the proposed method.
- The paper lacks a detailed analysis of the computational complexity of the proposed method, particularly the time complexity of the pruning process and the training of the pruned model.
- The paper does not discuss the limitations of SPADE, which could provide valuable insights into its applicability and potential challenges.
- There is a lack of discussion on the potential negative societal impact of the proposed method, which is an important consideration in the field of machine learning.
- The paper could benefit from a more detailed comparison with other pruning methods and a discussion on how SPADE performs in different scenarios, such as varying pruning ratios and network architectures.

**Questions:**
- Could you provide more details on the time complexity of the pruning process and the training of the pruned model?
- How does SPADE compare with other pruning methods, particularly unstructured pruning?
- Can you discuss the potential limitations of SPADE and scenarios where it might not be the most effective solution?
- How does SPADE perform when applied to different network architectures, such as those based on attention mechanisms?
- Could you provide more insights into the computational efficiency of SPADE in comparison to other pruning methods?
- Is there a possibility to extend the application of SPADE to other types of imaging inverse problems beyond those discussed in the paper?
