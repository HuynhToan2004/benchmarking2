 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel SO(3)-equivariant neural network architecture for point cloud processing, specifically designed for Scan-to-CAD tasks. It proposes a Spherical Patch Field (SPF) representation, which is based on spherical Gaussians and allows for adaptive patch sizes, enhancing the extraction of both local and global contextual information. The authors also introduce a Patch Gaussian Layer (PG-Layer) to improve the learnability of the SPF representation. The method is evaluated on various tasks such as point cloud registration, retrieval, and completion, demonstrating improvements over existing methods in some cases. However, the paper's presentation and the clarity of its contributions have been criticized, and the novelty of the method is questioned due to its similarities with previous works.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel SO(3)-equivariant neural network architecture for point cloud processing, which is a significant advancement in the field.
- The use of spherical Gaussians for point cloud processing is innovative and provides a new perspective on feature extraction and contextual information handling.
- The methodology is clearly presented, making it easy to follow, and the paper is well-organized, with clear visualizations that aid in understanding the proposed methods.
- The paper demonstrates improvements in various tasks such as point cloud registration, retrieval, and completion, showing the effectiveness of the proposed methods.
- The use of spherical harmonics for feature extraction is a promising approach that could potentially enhance the performance of point cloud processing tasks.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper's presentation could be improved, particularly in terms of clarity and organization. Some sections are hard to follow, and the paper lacks a clear and concise summary of its contributions.
- The novelty of the proposed methods is questionable as they bear strong resemblance to previous works, such as those by Salihu and Steinbach (2023).
- The paper does not sufficiently differentiate its contributions from those of previous works, and the method's advantages over existing methods are not clearly articulated.
- The evaluation of the method is limited to specific tasks and datasets, which may not fully demonstrate the generalizability or applicability of the proposed methods.
- The paper could benefit from a more rigorous comparison with state-of-the-art methods, particularly in terms of computational efficiency and scalability.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors clarify the contributions of their work compared to previous methods, particularly in terms of the novelty and advantages of the proposed methods?
- How does the proposed method compare in terms of computational efficiency and scalability to other state-of-the-art methods?
- Can the authors provide a more detailed explanation of the spherical patch fields and their role in the proposed method?
- How do the spherical Gaussians and the PG-Layer contribute to the performance improvements observed in the experiments?
- Could the authors provide a clearer summary of the paper's contributions and the specific advantages of their method over existing approaches?
- In the context of the experimental results, could the authors provide more detailed explanations or additional experiments to support the claimed improvements in performance?
