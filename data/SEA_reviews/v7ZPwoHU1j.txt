 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel algorithm for solving the K-means problem using a nonnegative low-rank SDP formulation, which is a nonconvex Burer-Monteiro factorization approach. This method is designed to be computationally efficient and scalable, making it suitable for large-scale datasets. The algorithm is shown to have local linear convergence within a primal-dual neighborhood of the SDP solution and achieves lower mis-clustering errors compared to existing state-of-the-art methods. The theoretical analysis and experimental results demonstrate the effectiveness and efficiency of the proposed method, which is particularly beneficial for high-dimensional datasets.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written and easy to follow, making it accessible to readers.
- The proposed algorithm is innovative, combining the advantages of spectral clustering, NMF, and SDP to solve the K-means problem.
- The algorithm demonstrates strong theoretical guarantees and achieves lower mis-clustering errors compared to existing state-of-the-art methods.
- The paper includes extensive experimental results that validate the effectiveness of the proposed algorithm, showing its superiority over existing methods.
- The theoretical analysis is rigorous and well-supported, providing a solid foundation for the proposed method.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper does not sufficiently discuss the limitations of the proposed method, which could include scalability issues when the number of clusters K is large, or the assumption of equal cluster sizes which may not hold in real-world datasets.
- There is a lack of clarity in the experimental setup, particularly in the choice of parameters and the comparison with other methods.
- The paper could benefit from a more comprehensive discussion on the related work, including comparisons with other existing methods and a more detailed literature review.
- The paper could be improved by providing more detailed proofs and explanations for the theoretical results, especially the assumptions and implications of the results.

**Questions:**
- Could you clarify the assumptions made in Theorem 1, particularly the assumptions on the parameter r and the initialization U0?
- How does the proposed method perform when the number of clusters K is large, and how does this affect the scalability of the algorithm?
- Can you provide more details on the choice of parameters, such as the step size and the penalty parameter β, and how these were chosen for the experiments?
- How does the proposed method compare with other existing methods, especially in terms of computational efficiency and clustering accuracy?
- Could you discuss the implications of the results in more detail, particularly how the assumptions and results apply in practical scenarios?
