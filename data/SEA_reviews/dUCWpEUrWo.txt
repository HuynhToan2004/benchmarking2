 **Summary:**
The paper introduces the Asynchronous Graph Generator (AGG), a novel graph neural network architecture for handling multi-channel time series data with missing values. AGG represents each observation as a node on a dynamic graph, leveraging attention mechanisms to learn relationships across variables of interest. The model can perform data imputation and predict new measurements based on the learnt graph. The paper compares AGG with existing methods and demonstrates its effectiveness on benchmark datasets, showcasing state-of-the-art results in time series data imputation, classification, and prediction. However, the paper lacks a detailed discussion on the limitations and potential negative societal impacts, and the experimental results are not comprehensive enough to fully support the claims made.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel graph neural network architecture, AGG, which represents each observation as a node on a dynamic graph, enabling the modeling of relationships across variables of interest.
- The paper is well-written, clear, and easy to follow, with detailed explanations of the AGG architecture and its components.
- AGG demonstrates state-of-the-art results on benchmark datasets, showing its effectiveness in handling missing values in multi-channel time series data.
- The paper provides a detailed discussion of the limitations and potential negative societal impacts of the proposed method, which is commendable for its transparency and ethical considerations.
- The experimental results are solid, demonstrating the superiority of AGG over existing methods, and the paper includes a detailed description of the experimental setup and results.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed discussion on the limitations and potential negative societal impacts of the proposed method, which is crucial for understanding the broader implications of the work.
- The paper could benefit from a more comprehensive comparison with existing methods, including a discussion on the advantages and disadvantages of different approaches and a more detailed analysis of the experimental results.
- The paper's claims about the ability of AGG to handle asynchronous data and its impact on performance are not convincingly supported by the experimental results, which are limited to datasets with regular sampling intervals.
- The paper does not adequately address the issue of missing data, which is a significant challenge in time series analysis, and the proposed solution of adding nodes to the graph may not be effective in scenarios where data is truly missing.
- The paper's focus on imputation of missing values in time series data is somewhat limited, as it does not explore other applications of the proposed method, such as anomaly detection or forecasting.
- The experimental results are not comprehensive enough to fully support the claims made in the paper, particularly concerning the impact of data augmentation on the performance of AGG.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors provide more details on the limitations and potential negative societal impacts of the proposed method?
- How does the proposed method compare with other existing methods in terms of handling asynchronous data? Can the authors provide more experimental results or theoretical analysis to support the claims made about AGG's ability to handle asynchronous data?
- Could the authors provide a more detailed discussion on the advantages and disadvantages of the proposed method compared to other existing methods?
- How does the proposed method handle the issue of missing data, particularly in scenarios where data is truly missing and not just unobserved?
- Can the authors provide more experimental results or theoretical analysis to support the claims made about the impact of data augmentation on the performance of AGG?
- Is there any potential for the proposed method to be applied in other areas beyond time series data, such as spatial data or other types of data with temporal or spatial components?
