 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel adversarial attack framework targeting camera-LiDAR fusion models for 3D object detection, leveraging adversarial patches on camera images. The framework is designed to identify vulnerable regions in images and classify models as object-sensitive or globally sensitive, enabling tailored attack strategies. The authors demonstrate the effectiveness of their approach through experiments on six state-of-the-art fusion-based and one camera-only model, showing a significant decrease in mean average precision (mAP) and detection scores. The methodology includes a two-stage approach, with the first stage identifying vulnerable regions in images and the second stage applying dedicated attack strategies. The paper also discusses the physical realizability of the attacks and provides code for reproducibility.

**Strengths:**
- The paper addresses a significant and timely problem by focusing on the vulnerabilities of camera-LiDAR fusion models, which are crucial for autonomous driving applications.
- The methodology is well-organized, with a clear structure that facilitates understanding of the proposed techniques and their implementation.
- The paper includes comprehensive evaluations using six state-of-the-art fusion-based and one camera-only model, demonstrating the effectiveness of the proposed attack framework.
- The use of adversarial patches for camera-only attacks is innovative and could be more effective than multi-modal attacks, as shown in the experiments.
- The paper provides a detailed analysis of the vulnerabilities in camera-LiDAR fusion models and proposes a novel sensitivity distribution recognition algorithm to identify vulnerable image areas under adversarial attacks.
- The framework is designed to generate deployable patches, which are physically realizable and can be applied to real-world scenarios.
- The paper is well-written, making it accessible and easy to follow, and includes a detailed description of the attack pipeline and the experimental setup.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper does not sufficiently discuss the limitations of the proposed method, particularly how it might be affected by the resolution of input images or the type of adversarial perturbations used.
- The sensitivity distribution recognition algorithm is complex and may not be practical for real-time applications, requiring significant computational resources.
- The paper lacks a detailed comparison with existing adversarial attacks, which could help in understanding the novelty and effectiveness of the proposed method.
- The physical realizability of the attacks is questionable, as the paper does not adequately address how the adversarial patches would be applied in real-world scenarios, such as on road surfaces.
- The paper does not discuss potential defenses against the proposed attacks, which could limit the practical applicability of the findings.
- The paper could benefit from a more detailed discussion on the practical implications of the attack, such as the cost and feasibility of implementing the attack in real-world scenarios.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors clarify the feasibility of applying the adversarial patch to the road surface in real-world scenarios?
- How does the proposed method perform against other types of adversarial perturbations, such as gradient-based attacks?
- Could the authors provide more details on the physical implementation of the attack, including the cost and practicality of applying the patches in real-world scenarios?
- How does the proposed method compare to existing adversarial attacks in terms of effectiveness and practicality?
- Could the authors discuss the potential defenses against the proposed attacks and how these might impact the effectiveness of the attack?
- How does the proposed method handle different types of adversarial attacks, such as those based on different types of perturbations?
- Could the authors provide more details on the experimental setup, particularly how the physical experiments were conducted and what safety measures were taken?
