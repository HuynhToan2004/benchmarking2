 **Summary:**
The paper introduces KernelWarehouse, a novel approach to dynamic convolution that addresses the limitations of existing methods by partitioning kernels into smaller cells and sharing these across layers. This approach aims to improve parameter efficiency and performance by reducing the number of parameters while maintaining or enhancing accuracy. The methodology involves a new attention mechanism called CAF, which is designed to optimize the selection of kernel cells based on their importance. The paper presents extensive experiments on various datasets, including ImageNet and MS-COCO, demonstrating the effectiveness of the proposed method. However, concerns are raised about the clarity of some experimental results and the fairness of comparisons with other dynamic convolution methods.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, clear, and easy to follow, making complex concepts accessible to readers.
- The motivation behind the research is clear and well-articulated, highlighting the potential of the proposed method to improve parameter efficiency and accuracy in dynamic convolution.
- Extensive experiments are conducted, including on ImageNet and MS-COCO datasets, demonstrating the effectiveness of the proposed method.
- The proposed method is simple and effective, with a focus on reducing parameters while maintaining or improving accuracy.
- The paper provides a detailed analysis of the proposed method, including ablation studies that show the effectiveness of the CAF attention mechanism.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed discussion on the computational cost and efficiency of the proposed method, particularly in terms of FLOPs and runtime.
- There is a lack of comparison with other dynamic convolution methods that also aim to reduce parameters, which could have provided a clearer picture of the method's performance relative to existing solutions.
- The paper does not sufficiently address the limitations of the proposed method, such as the potential for increased computational complexity and the impact on inference speed.
- The paper contains some errors and inconsistencies, such as typographical errors and missing tables, which detract from its professional quality.
- The paper does not adequately address the issue of fairness in comparisons, particularly in terms of the number of parameters used in different experiments.

**Questions:**
- Could you clarify the computational cost and efficiency of the proposed method, particularly in terms of FLOPs and runtime?
- How does the proposed method compare to other dynamic convolution methods in terms of parameter efficiency and accuracy?
- Can you provide more details on the experimental setup, such as the specific values of m and n used in different experiments?
- What is the rationale behind the specific formulation of the CAF attention mechanism, and how does it compare to other attention mechanisms in terms of effectiveness?
- How does the proposed method perform in terms of inference speed, and what are the potential impacts on practical applications?
- Can you provide a more detailed discussion on the limitations of the proposed method and potential avenues for future research?
