 **Summary:**
The paper investigates the effects of fine-tuning on the capabilities of pretrained models, focusing on how these models adapt to new tasks without fundamentally altering their underlying capabilities. The authors employ a combination of synthetic and realistic datasets, including Tracr and TinyStories, to empirically demonstrate that fine-tuning typically involves minimal transformations, often localized to specific layers or neurons. This research contributes to the understanding of how fine-tuning influences model capabilities and provides insights into the preservation of pretrained knowledge during this process. The findings suggest that fine-tuning primarily refines existing capabilities rather than introducing new ones, which could have implications for the practical deployment of models in various applications.

**Strengths:**
- The paper addresses a timely and important question regarding the effects of fine-tuning on pretrained models, which is crucial for understanding how models adapt to new tasks.
- The empirical investigation is thorough, with a focus on both synthetic and realistic datasets, providing a robust evaluation of the hypotheses presented.
- The paper is well-written, clear, and easy to follow, with a comprehensive discussion of related work that effectively positions the research within the current literature.
- The findings contribute significantly to the understanding of how fine-tuning influences model capabilities, which is a critical aspect of machine learning system development.
- The paper provides a novel perspective on the effects of fine-tuning by proposing a "wrapper" that is learned on top of existing capabilities, which is a valuable insight for practitioners.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper primarily focuses on text-based models, which might limit the generalizability of the findings to other types of models such as image or multimodal models.
- The experimental setup is somewhat limited, focusing on a single task (counting) and a single model (minGPT), which may not fully capture the complexity and variability of real-world applications.
- The paper does not sufficiently explore the implications of the findings for practical applications, such as the removal of safety wrappers in models fine-tuned on unrelated tasks.
- The theoretical contributions are not clearly articulated, and the paper could benefit from a more formalized theoretical discussion to better support the empirical findings.
- The paper's reliance on synthetic data and simplified models might limit the perceived relevance and applicability of the findings to more complex, real-world scenarios.
