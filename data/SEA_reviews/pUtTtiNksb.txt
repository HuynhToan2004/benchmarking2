 **Summary:**
The paper introduces FFCA-Net, a novel method for stereo image compression that leverages side information at the decoder to enhance compression efficiency. FFCA-Net employs a coarse-to-fine cascaded alignment approach, including a feature domain patch-matching module, an hourglass-based sparse stereo refinement network, and a fast feature fusion network. This method achieves significant improvements in compression efficiency, as evidenced by its ability to reduce decoding latency by several to tens of times compared to traditional and learning-based stereo image compression methods. The paper is well-organized, with clear figures and detailed method descriptions, and includes comprehensive experimental results that demonstrate the superiority of the proposed method over existing approaches.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel approach to stereo image compression that leverages side information at the decoder, significantly reducing decoding latency.
- The methodology is well-organized, with clear figures and detailed method descriptions, making it easy to follow and understand.
- The proposed method achieves significant improvements in compression efficiency, as demonstrated by extensive experimental results on three large-scale high-resolution stereo datasets.
- The paper is well-written, with clear presentation and a comprehensive evaluation that includes a thorough comparison with existing methods and an ablation study.
- The use of a feature-based fast cascade alignment network and a fast feature fusion network is innovative and contributes to the efficiency and effectiveness of the stereo image compression process.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper could benefit from a more detailed discussion on the limitations of the proposed method and potential future research directions.
- The novelty of the approach is questionable, as it appears to be a combination of existing techniques such as multi-scale patch matching and sparse refinement.
- The paper lacks a detailed analysis of the computational complexity of the proposed method, which is crucial for understanding its practical applicability.
- The experimental evaluation could be strengthened by including more recent and diverse baseline methods, and by evaluating the method on a wider range of datasets.
- The paper does not adequately address the potential societal impacts of the proposed method, which is a significant omission in the ethical considerations section.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors clarify the specific contributions of the proposed method beyond the existing techniques mentioned?
- How does the proposed method perform when compared to more recent and diverse baseline methods, such as those mentioned in the review?
- Could the authors provide a more detailed analysis of the computational complexity of the proposed method, including a comparison with other state-of-the-art methods?
- What are the potential societal impacts of the proposed method, and how are these addressed in the ethical considerations section of the paper?
