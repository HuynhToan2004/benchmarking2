 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel method for detecting micro-expressions (MEs) in video sequences, focusing on the use of a skip-k-frame block-wise main directional mean optical flow (MDMO) feature to identify MEs. This method, which includes a pixelmatch-based facial alignment algorithm and a dynamic update of reference frames, is designed to differentiate MEs from macro-expressions (MEs) and other movements. The paper claims that the proposed method outperforms existing state-of-the-art methods on benchmark datasets, such as CASME II, CAS(ME)2, and SAMM-LV. However, the paper has been critiqued for its lack of clarity in the presentation, unclear methodological descriptions, and insufficient experimental validation.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel method for detecting MEs using a skip-k-frame block-wise main directional mean optical flow (MDMO) feature, which is a significant contribution to the field.
- The proposed method outperforms existing state-of-the-art methods on benchmark datasets, demonstrating its effectiveness.
- The paper provides a clear and comprehensive explanation of the proposed method, including a detailed algorithm description in the appendix.
- The use of MDMO to extract features from MEs is highlighted as a crucial aspect of the method, and the paper includes an ablation study that shows the effectiveness of the MDMO feature.
- The paper is well-written and easy to follow, with a clear structure that facilitates understanding of the proposed method.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks clarity in the presentation, with important details relegated to the appendix which could make it difficult for readers to fully understand the method.
- The methodology and experimental setup are not sufficiently detailed, with some key components such as the pixelmatch-based facial alignment algorithm and the dynamic update of reference frames not adequately explained.
- The paper does not include a comparison with the latest state-of-the-art methods, which could limit the evaluation of the proposed method's effectiveness.
- The paper's experimental validation is insufficient, with a lack of detailed analysis of the results and comparisons with other methods.
- The paper does not address the issue of global movements effectively, which could affect the accuracy of the ME detection.
- The paper's claims about the uniqueness of the M-pattern and its effectiveness in ME detection are not convincingly supported by the evidence provided.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors provide more detailed explanations of the pixelmatch-based facial alignment algorithm and the dynamic update of reference frames in the main text?
- How does the proposed method handle the issue of global movements in ME detection?
- Can the authors clarify the definition and role of the RoI in the MDMO feature extraction process?
- Why is the MDMO feature extraction limited to specific regions of interest (ROIs), and how does this affect the detection of MEs?
- Could the authors provide more detailed comparisons with other state-of-the-art methods to better establish the effectiveness of their proposed method?
- How does the proposed method perform on longer video sequences, and what are the implications of using a skip-k-frame strategy in such scenarios?
- Can the authors provide more detailed results and analyses to support the claims made about the M-pattern and its effectiveness in ME detection?
