 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel method for parameter-efficient multi-task model fusion using a technique called L-LoRA, which partially linearizes the adapter modules in LoRA fine-tuning. This approach is aimed at improving the disentanglement of task vectors, thereby reducing interference between different tasks. The method is evaluated on various tasks, including image and language tasks, and shows promising results in terms of task fusion and weight disentanglement. The paper also explores the concept of weight disentanglement and its impact on multi-task learning, using visualizations and experiments to support the proposed method. However, concerns were raised about the clarity of the paper's presentation, the scope of experiments, and the novelty of the approach compared to existing methods.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, with clear and easy-to-follow content, and provides a comprehensive review of related works.
- The proposed method is novel and shows promise in improving the disentanglement of task vectors and reducing interference between tasks, which is critical for multi-task learning.
- The paper provides visualizations of the weight disentanglement error, which helps in understanding the effectiveness of the proposed method.
- The methodology is simple, effective, and has been evaluated on a wide range of tasks, including image and language tasks.
- The paper introduces a new benchmark for evaluating multi-task models, which is valuable for the research community.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks clarity in some parts, particularly in the presentation of key concepts and figures, which could benefit from additional explanations or revisions.
- The experimental results, while promising, are not consistently superior to existing methods, such as LoRA, and the paper could benefit from more detailed comparisons and discussions on the performance of L-LoRA.
- The paper could benefit from more extensive experiments on a wider range of tasks and models to validate the effectiveness of the proposed method more robustly.
- The paper's contribution to the field might be limited due to the incremental nature of the proposed method, which is a modification of existing techniques like LoRA.
- There are several typographical and formatting errors throughout the paper that need to be corrected.
