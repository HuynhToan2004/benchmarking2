 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel dynamic feature selection (DFS) method called Dynamic Data Selection (DDS), which is designed to operate without labeled data, focusing on unsupervised scenarios. The method aims to minimize memory consumption and is invariant to the maximum number of selected features. The DDS model is tested on various tasks such as data compression and clustering, demonstrating its effectiveness in unsupervised learning. However, the paper suffers from several critical issues including unclear writing, lack of novelty, and insufficient experimental validation. The paper's methodology and results are not convincingly superior to existing methods, and the writing needs significant improvement to enhance clarity and logical flow.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel dynamic feature selection method, which is a significant contribution to the field of unsupervised learning.
- The methodology is well-explained, and the paper is easy to follow, making it accessible to a broad audience.
- The proposed method shows promising results in both data compression and clustering tasks, indicating its effectiveness in practical applications.
- The paper is well-organized, with clear problem formulation and a logical structure that guides the reader through the content.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper suffers from poor writing quality, with grammatical errors, unclear sentences, and a lack of logical flow that makes it difficult to understand.
- The novelty of the proposed method is questionable, as it appears to be a combination of existing techniques without significant innovation.
- The experimental validation is insufficient, with limited datasets and a lack of comparison with a broader range of methods, including state-of-the-art approaches.
- The paper lacks a detailed discussion on the limitations of the proposed method, which is crucial for understanding its applicability and potential drawbacks.
- The methodology and results are not convincingly superior to existing methods, raising concerns about the practical utility and effectiveness of the proposed approach.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors clarify the specific contributions of the proposed method beyond the combination of existing techniques?
- How does the DDS method perform when compared to other state-of-the-art methods in terms of data compression and clustering accuracy?

- Why was the decision made to limit the experimental validation to only two datasets? Would expanding to include more diverse datasets enhance the credibility of the results?
