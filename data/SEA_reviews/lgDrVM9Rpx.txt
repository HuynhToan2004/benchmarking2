 **Summary:**
The paper introduces P-MapNet, a novel method for constructing high-definition (HD) maps using both standard definition (SD) maps and high-definition (HD) maps. The approach leverages SDMap prior to enhance initial HDMap segmentation and HDMap prior to refine the output. The methodology involves a transformer-based architecture that integrates SDMap and HDMap features, allowing for the handling of misalignments between SDMap and HDMap. The paper is evaluated on datasets such as nuScenes and Argoverse, showing improved performance over existing methods. However, concerns are raised about the novelty of the approach, as similar techniques have been previously explored, and the incremental nature of the improvements.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, making it easy to follow and understand the proposed methodology.
- The integration of both SDMap and HDMap prior in the proposed method is innovative and helps in enhancing the initial segmentation results.
- The use of attention-based architecture to fuse SDMap features with HDMap features is a novel approach that effectively handles misalignments between the two maps.
- The method is evaluated on public datasets such as nuScenes and Argoverse, demonstrating its effectiveness and applicability in real-world scenarios.
- The paper provides a comprehensive evaluation of the proposed method, including ablation studies and comparisons with existing methods, which validates the improvements brought by the proposed approach.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a clear explanation of the differences between the proposed method and existing methods, particularly in the use of SDMap and HDMap priors.
- The novelty of the approach is questioned as similar techniques have been previously explored, such as the use of SDMap as a prior in online HD map construction.
- The paper does not adequately address the challenges posed by the misalignment between SDMap and HDMap, which could affect the accuracy and reliability of the generated maps.
- There is a lack of discussion on the limitations of the proposed method, which could hinder the understanding of its applicability and effectiveness in various scenarios.
- The paper does not provide a detailed comparison with other state-of-the-art methods, which could help in better understanding the contributions and advantages of the proposed approach.

**Questions:**
- Could you clarify the differences between the proposed method and existing methods, particularly in the use of SDMap and HDMap priors?
- How does the proposed method handle the misalignment between SDMap and HDMap, and what measures are taken to ensure the accuracy and reliability of the generated maps?
- Can you provide more details on the training and inference times for the proposed method, and how these compare with other state-of-the-art methods?
- How does the method perform when there are significant differences in the quality or accuracy of the SDMap and HDMap datasets?
- Could you elaborate on the design choices of the attention-based architecture used in the paper, and how it contributes to the overall performance of the method?
