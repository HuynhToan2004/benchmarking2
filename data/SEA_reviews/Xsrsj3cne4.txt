 **Summary:**
The paper introduces AdaLip, a method for adversarial training of Graph Neural Networks (GNNs) that leverages an optimization framework based on the Lipschitz constant. AdaLip integrates graph denoising and network regularization to enhance the robustness of GNNs against adversarial attacks. The paper proposes a convergent iterative algorithm using block majorization-minimization, graph learning, and alternate minimization techniques to address the optimization problem. The method is evaluated on real datasets and shows improved performance against various classes of poisoning attacks, achieving up to 20% GCN performance improvement against modification attacks and approximately 10% against injection attacks. However, concerns were raised about the clarity of the paper, the robustness of the results, and the novelty of the method, which is seen as an incremental improvement over existing techniques.

**Strengths:**
- The paper addresses the critical issue of adversarial attacks on Graph Neural Networks (GNNs), focusing on enhancing the robustness and stability of GNNs in the face of input perturbations.
- The proposed AdaLip method integrates graph denoising and network regularization, offering a comprehensive and versatile solution that extends applicability and enables robust regularization for diverse neural network architectures.
- The method is evaluated on real datasets and shows improved performance against various classes of poisoning attacks, achieving up to 20% GCN performance improvement against modification attacks and approximately 10% against injection attacks.
- The paper provides a detailed mathematical analysis, which is crucial for understanding the theoretical underpinnings of the proposed method.
- The authors have demonstrated the effectiveness of AdaLip in mitigating various forms of adversarial attacks through comprehensive empirical evaluations conducted on a diverse set of real-world graph datasets.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper is not clearly written, with several grammatical errors and unclear explanations, particularly in the algorithm section.
- The motivation behind the proposed method is not clear, and the paper lacks a thorough analysis of the limitations and challenges of existing methods.
- The novelty of the method is questionable, as it appears to be an incremental improvement over existing techniques rather than a significant advancement.
- The robustness of the results is not convincingly demonstrated, as the paper does not provide sufficient evidence to support the claims of superior performance over existing methods.
- The paper does not adequately address the limitations of the proposed method, which is crucial for understanding its applicability and effectiveness in different scenarios.
- The paper lacks a detailed discussion on the computational complexity of the proposed method, which is important for understanding its practical applicability.
- The paper does not provide a clear comparison with state-of-the-art defense methods, which is necessary to establish the effectiveness and novelty of the proposed method.
