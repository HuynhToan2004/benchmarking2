 **Summary:**
The paper introduces DISTA, a spiking neural network (SNN) architecture designed for vision tasks, specifically image classification, utilizing spatiotemporal attention mechanisms. DISTA extends the capabilities of existing spiking transformers by integrating intrinsic plasticity and network-level spatiotemporal attention, aiming to improve performance on datasets like CIFAR-10 and CIFAR-100. The proposed architecture is evaluated against existing models and shows competitive results, although the improvements are incremental and the paper lacks a thorough comparison with non-spiking transformers. The paper also includes an attention denoising mechanism to mitigate noise in spatiotemporal attention maps, which is claimed to enhance the model's robustness.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, making the content clear and accessible.
- The proposed method, DISTA, introduces novel mechanisms such as network-level spatiotemporal attention and an attention denoising layer, which are not present in existing models like Spikformer.
- The results are promising, showing that DISTA can outperform Spikformer and other SNNs on various datasets.
- The use of spatiotemporal attention and denoising mechanisms is innovative, enhancing the model's ability to handle noise in spatiotemporal attention maps.
- The paper includes comprehensive experiments and ablation studies, which help in understanding the contributions of different components of the model.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a thorough comparison with non-spiking transformers, which could provide a better understanding of the model's performance relative to state-of-the-art methods.
- The improvement over Spikformer is relatively small, raising questions about the significance of the proposed improvements.
- The paper does not include comparisons with other SNN models that incorporate spatiotemporal attention mechanisms, which could have provided a more robust evaluation of the model's performance.
- The paper could benefit from more detailed explanations and examples of the network-level spatiotemporal attention and the denoising mechanism, as these are crucial aspects of the proposed model.
- The paper's claims about the superiority of SNNs over ANNs are not well-supported and could be seen as overstated.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors clarify the rationale behind the choice of Spikformer as the baseline model and discuss how the proposed improvements compare to other SNNs that incorporate spatiotemporal attention mechanisms?
- How does the denoising mechanism affect the model's performance, particularly in terms of noise reduction and computational efficiency?
- Can the authors provide more detailed examples or simulations to illustrate the benefits of network-level spatiotemporal attention and the denoising mechanism?
- How does the model handle the noise in spatiotemporal attention maps, and what are the implications of this noise for the model's performance?
- Could the authors elaborate on the differences between the proposed denoising mechanism and other methods like spike-and-threshold quantization, and discuss how these methods compare in terms of effectiveness and computational efficiency?
