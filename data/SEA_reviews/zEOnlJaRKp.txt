 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a collaborative neural framework (CNF) for adversarial training in vehicle routing problems (VRPs), focusing on enhancing both standard generalization and adversarial robustness. CNF utilizes a neural router to distribute instances to models, promoting load balancing and collaborative performance. The framework is evaluated against various attacks, demonstrating its effectiveness in improving robustness while maintaining generalization. However, concerns are raised about the practicality of the method, particularly its scalability and the clarity of its contributions to the field.

**Strengths:**
- The paper addresses an important problem of adversarial robustness in neural methods for vehicle routing problems (VRPs), which is crucial for enhancing the reliability and security of these systems.
- The proposed method, Collaborative Neural Framework (CNF), is well-articulated and effectively demonstrated through comprehensive experiments, showing its effectiveness in improving both standard generalization and adversarial robustness.
- The use of adversarial training to enhance robustness in neural methods for VRPs is a novel approach, and the paper provides a clear and logical presentation of the proposed method, making it accessible and understandable to readers.
- The paper includes extensive evaluations and ablation studies, which help in understanding the impact of different components of the proposed method and the trade-offs involved.
- The methodology is well-explained, and the paper provides a clear overview of the problem, the proposed method, and the results, which is beneficial for readers to grasp the key concepts and findings.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a clear motivation for the use of adversarial training in VRPs, and it is unclear how the proposed method addresses the specific challenges posed by VRPs.
- The paper does not adequately address the scalability of the method, particularly in terms of computational resources and the practicality of the approach in real-world applications.
- The paper does not provide sufficient details on the attacker's model, which is crucial for understanding the effectiveness of the proposed method against various types of adversarial attacks.
- The paper's contribution to the field is not clearly articulated, and it is unclear how the proposed method advances the state-of-the-art in adversarial robustness for VRPs.
- There are some typographical and formatting errors in the paper, which could detract from its professionalism and readability.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors clarify the specific challenges posed by VRPs that necessitate the use of adversarial training, and how the proposed method addresses these challenges?
- How does the proposed method compare in terms of computational resources and scalability to other existing methods for adversarial training in VRPs?
- Can the authors provide more details on the attacker's model used in the experiments, including the specifics of the perturbation model and the attack budget?
- How does the proposed method perform in terms of generalization to out-of-distribution (OOD) data, and what are the implications of this for the practical application of the method?
- Can the authors discuss the potential of using the proposed method in real-world applications, and what are the key challenges that need to be addressed for successful deployment?
