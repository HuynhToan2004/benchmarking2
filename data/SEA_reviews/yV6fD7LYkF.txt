 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a comprehensive evaluation framework named VALUES, designed to assess uncertainty estimation in semantic segmentation. VALUES employs a systematic approach to identify key components of uncertainty estimation and evaluates them across various scenarios. It investigates the separation of aleatoric and epistemic uncertainties, the effectiveness of different prediction models, and the impact of aggregation strategies. The framework is tested on both simulated and real-world datasets, providing insights into the performance of different methods under controlled conditions. The paper also discusses the limitations of current evaluation practices and proposes a more rigorous approach to validating uncertainty estimation in semantic segmentation.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, clear, and easy to follow, making it accessible to a broad audience.
- It identifies and addresses key pitfalls in the evaluation of uncertainty estimation in semantic segmentation, providing a systematic framework for evaluating different methods.
- The evaluation of uncertainty estimation methods is comprehensive, covering various aspects such as aleatoric and epistemic uncertainties, prediction models, and aggregation strategies.
- The use of both simulated and real-world datasets for testing the framework enhances the credibility of the results.
- The paper provides a detailed discussion on the limitations of current evaluation practices and proposes a more rigorous approach, which is crucial for advancing the field.
- The framework is designed to be flexible and adaptable, which allows for its application in various settings and scenarios.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed discussion on the choice of evaluation metrics and their impact on the results, which could be critical for understanding the robustness of the findings.
- The experimental setup, particularly the design of the toy dataset, is not thoroughly justified, which raises questions about the validity of the results.
- The paper could benefit from a more comprehensive literature review, especially regarding recent advancements in uncertainty estimation and its applications in semantic segmentation.
- The paper's scope is somewhat limited, focusing primarily on semantic segmentation and not addressing other potential applications of uncertainty estimation in computer vision.
- The paper does not provide a clear explanation of how the uncertainty estimation methods are applied to different datasets, which could hinder the reproducibility of the results.
- The discussion on the limitations of the framework and potential future work is insufficient, which could limit the paper's impact and applicability.

**Questions:**
- How does the choice of evaluation metrics affect the results, and could different metrics lead to different conclusions?
- Can you provide a more detailed justification for the design of the toy dataset, particularly how it simulates aleatoric and epistemic uncertainties?
- How does the framework handle the integration of different components, such as the aggregation strategy, with the prediction model and uncertainty measures?
- Could you elaborate on the potential applications of uncertainty estimation beyond semantic segmentation, and how the framework could be adapted for these applications?
- How does the framework ensure the reproducibility of the results, particularly in terms of the methodology and the datasets used?
- Are there any specific challenges or limitations that need to be addressed in the future development of the framework?
