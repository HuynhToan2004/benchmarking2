 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel approach to constructing graph neural networks (GNNs) by translating algebraic operations into a context-free grammar (CFG), which is then used to define a GNN layer model. This method, termed G2N2, is designed to be 3-WL compliant and is evaluated against other 3-WL GNNs on various downstream tasks. The paper presents a framework that translates algebraic operations into a CFG, which is then reduced to a provably 3-WL compliant GNN model. The experiments demonstrate the efficiency and effectiveness of G2N2 compared to other 3-WL GNNs. However, concerns were raised about the clarity of the paper, the necessity of the approach, and the novelty of the results.

**Strengths:**
- The paper presents a novel approach to designing GNNs by translating algebraic operations into a context-free grammar (CFG), which is a significant contribution to the field.
- The proposed G2N2 model is shown to be more efficient and effective than other 3-WL GNNs, as demonstrated through various experiments.
- The paper is well-written and easy to follow, with clear theoretical proofs and experimental results that support the claims.
- The translation of algebraic operations into a CFG is a novel and interesting approach, and the reduction scheme used to reduce the CFG to a provably 3-WL compliant GNN model is a significant contribution.
- The paper is well-organized and the methodology is clearly presented, making it easy for readers to understand and follow the proposed framework.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper suffers from a lack of clarity in the presentation, particularly in the definitions and explanations of the CFG and the rules used in the GNN layer model.
- The novelty of the approach is questioned, as similar ideas have been previously explored in other works, and the paper does not sufficiently distinguish itself from these existing methods.
- The paper does not adequately address the limitations of the proposed method, which could include scalability issues and the potential for over-reducing the CFG, leading to loss of expressive power.
- The experimental results are not convincing enough, with some results showing only marginal improvements over existing methods, and others showing no significant advantage.
- The paper could benefit from a more detailed discussion on the limitations of the proposed method and a comparison with other recent 3-WL GNNs to better establish its advantages.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors clarify the definitions and explanations of the CFG and the rules used in the GNN layer model to improve the clarity of the paper?
- How does the proposed method compare to other recent 3-WL GNNs in terms of expressive power and efficiency?
- Can the authors provide more detailed explanations or examples to illustrate the practical applications and advantages of the proposed method over existing methods?
- In light of the concerns about the novelty and the clarity of the paper, how do the authors respond to these criticisms and what steps have been taken to address them?
