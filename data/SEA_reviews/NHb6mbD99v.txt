 **Summary:**
The paper introduces UDAC, a novel model-free offline reinforcement learning (RL) algorithm that addresses both epistemic and environmental uncertainties. UDAC employs a diffusion model to model the behavior policy and incorporates a perturbation model to mitigate the risks associated with suboptimal trajectories. The algorithm is evaluated across various benchmarks, demonstrating its superior performance in risk-sensitive and risk-neutral scenarios. However, concerns are raised about the clarity of the paper, the lack of detailed theoretical justification, and the absence of a comprehensive comparison with existing methods.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written and easy to follow, with clear explanations of the methodology and results.
- The proposed method, UDAC, is novel and addresses a significant problem in the field of offline RL by handling both epistemic and environmental uncertainties.
- The experiments are comprehensive, covering a variety of tasks and demonstrating the effectiveness of UDAC.
- The paper provides a detailed discussion on the related works and the differences between UDAC and other existing methods.
- The use of diffusion models for behavior policy modeling is an interesting approach that could potentially improve the performance of offline RL algorithms.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a clear and detailed explanation of the methodology, particularly the role and integration of the diffusion model and the perturbation model.
- There is a lack of theoretical justification for the proposed method, which could undermine the credibility of the results.
- The comparison with other methods is not fair or comprehensive, with some methods not being evaluated in the same risk-sensitive environments as UDAC.
- The paper suffers from several typos and grammatical errors, which could affect the readability and comprehension.
- The experimental results, while promising, are not consistently superior across all environments, and the performance improvements are sometimes marginal.
- The paper does not sufficiently address the issue of overfitting, which is a significant concern in offline RL.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors clarify the role and integration of the diffusion model and the perturbation model in the methodology?
- How does UDAC handle the issue of overfitting, particularly in the risk-sensitive environments where the dataset may be limited?
- Could the authors provide a more detailed explanation of the experimental results, particularly where UDAC performs poorly compared to other methods?
- How does UDAC compare to other risk-sensitive offline RL algorithms, especially those that do not use diffusion models?
- Could the authors elaborate on the choice of hyperparameters and their impact on the performance of UDAC?
- How does UDAC perform in environments where the behavior policy is not well-defined or not available, such as in the case of sparse reward settings?
