 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel defense mechanism named COIN, designed to counteract multiplication-based unlearnable datasets (UDs) by employing a random matrix for pixel-level interpolation. This approach, based on two metrics, Theta_imi and Theta_imc, aims to increase the inconsistency within intra-class multiplicative noise and enhance the similarity within inter-class multiplicative noise. The proposed defense is tested against various datasets and models, showing significant improvements in test accuracy compared to existing defense mechanisms. However, the paper has been critiqued for its limited evaluation scope, lack of comprehensive analysis, and potential over-generalization of its results.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, clear, and easy to follow, with a clear motivation and hypothesis that is well-supported by empirical results.
- The proposed defense method, COIN, is novel and effective, as demonstrated by extensive experiments that show a significant improvement in test accuracy compared to existing defense mechanisms.
- The paper is original in its approach, introducing new metrics and a defense method specifically tailored for multiplication-based unlearnable datasets.
- The experiments are extensive, covering various datasets and models, and the results are convincing, showing that the proposed method outperforms existing defenses.
- The paper is well-organized, with a clear structure and logical flow, making it accessible and understandable to readers.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a comprehensive analysis of the proposed defense method, particularly in terms of its theoretical underpinnings and the specific reasons for its effectiveness.
- The evaluation scope is limited, focusing primarily on CUDA and not including other multiplication-based UDs, which could limit the generalizability of the results.
- The paper could benefit from a more detailed discussion on the limitations of the proposed method and potential countermeasures that attackers could use to bypass it.
- The paper does not adequately address the computational cost of the proposed method, which could be a significant drawback for practical applications.
- There is a lack of clarity in some sections of the paper, particularly in the definitions and explanations of certain terms and equations.
- The paper could benefit from a more thorough discussion of related work and a comparison with existing defenses to better contextualize the contributions of the proposed method.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors clarify the specific reasons why COIN is effective against multiplication-based UDs?
- How does the proposed method perform against other types of unlearnable datasets, such as those based on other types of perturbations?
- Could the authors provide a more detailed analysis of the computational cost of the proposed method and compare it with other defense mechanisms?
- Is there a risk of overfitting the defense mechanism to the specific type of unlearnable datasets used in the experiments?
- How does the proposed method perform when combined with other defense mechanisms, such as adversarial training or JPEG compression?
- Can the authors provide more detailed explanations or examples of the mathematical definitions and equations used in the paper, particularly those in Section 3.3 and 3.4?
