 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel method for predicting training loss in large language models (LLMs) using a benchmark named nanoLM. This benchmark utilizes a loss prediction method based on scaling laws and hyperparameter transferability to estimate the loss of models without direct training. The method is evaluated across different model architectures and training scales, showing promising results in predicting the loss of larger models based on the training of smaller models. The paper also provides a dataset for pre-training large models and discusses its potential for reducing computational costs and enabling comparisons between different model architectures.
