 **Summary:**
The paper introduces kNN-ECD and kNN-ECSm, two methods aimed at improving the translation accuracy of the TransCoder-ST model by utilizing a kNN search and a key-value error correction datastore. These methods aim to enhance the interpretability of the translation process by focusing on the logic and structure of code rather than rare identifiers. The kNN-ECD method retrieves correction information from the error correction datastore, while kNN-ECSm employs a distributed structure with multiple sub-datastores for multi-round error correction. The paper also proposes a unified name rule to standardize rare identifiers and improve the focus on code logic and structure. The experimental results show that these methods significantly improve the translation accuracy of TransCoder-ST from 68.9% to 89.9%.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel approach, kNN-ECD, which combines kNN search with a key-value error correction datastore to enhance the interpretability of code translations.
- It proposes kNN-ECSm, a methodology that utilizes a distributed structure with multiple sub-datastores for multi-round error correction, providing a more comprehensive repair information.
- The paper presents a unified name rule, which encourages the datastore to focus more on code logic and structure rather than rare identifiers, improving the focus on code logic and structure.
- The experimental results show that the proposed methods significantly improve the translation accuracy of TransCoder-ST from 68.9% to 89.9%, demonstrating a significant improvement in translation accuracy.
- The paper is well-written and easy to follow, with clear and informative figures and tables that aid in understanding the proposed methods.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper does not compare its methods with other error correction methods such as those proposed by Meng et al., which could provide a more comprehensive evaluation of the proposed methods.
- The evaluation of the paper is limited to a single programming language pair (Java to Python), which may not generalize well to other programming languages.
- The paper does not discuss the scalability of the proposed methods with respect to the size of the error correction datastore, which is a critical factor in real-world applications.
- The paper does not discuss the computational complexity of the proposed methods, which is crucial for understanding their practical applicability.
- The paper does not provide a detailed discussion on the limitations of the proposed methods, which could help in understanding their scope and applicability.
- The paper does not include a detailed comparison of the proposed methods with other state-of-the-art error correction models, which could provide a better understanding of the contributions of the proposed methods.
