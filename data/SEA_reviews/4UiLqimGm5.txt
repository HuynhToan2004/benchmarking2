 **Summary:**
The paper introduces Coordinate-Aware Modulation (CAM), a novel approach for enhancing neural fields by integrating grid representations into MLPs. CAM allows for the modulation of intermediate features using scale and shift parameters derived from grid representations, thereby improving the representation of high-frequency components. The method was tested across various tasks including image generalization, novel view synthesis, and video compression, showing state-of-the-art performance in some cases. The paper also explores the use of feature normalization to mitigate potential biases and improve learning stability. Despite its innovative approach, the paper has been critiqued for its limited novelty, insufficient comparison with state-of-the-art methods, and lack of detailed theoretical analysis.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel method, Coordinate-Aware Modulation (CAM), which effectively integrates grid representations into MLPs, improving the representation of high-frequency components.
- CAM is a versatile method that can be applied to various tasks such as image generalization, novel view synthesis, and video compression, demonstrating its broad applicability.
- The paper provides a comprehensive set of experiments and ablation studies, including a detailed analysis of the impact of CAM on spectral bias and feature normalization.
- The writing is clear and easy to follow, making the complex concepts accessible to readers.
- The method achieves state-of-the-art performance in some tasks, such as novel view synthesis, and shows significant improvements in video compression performance.

**Weaknesses:**
- The novelty of the method is questioned as it resembles existing methods such as MFN and FINN, with the primary difference being the use of grids instead of positional encodings.
- The paper lacks a detailed comparison with state-of-the-art methods, particularly in terms of computational efficiency and memory usage, which are crucial for neural field applications.
- There is a lack of theoretical analysis to support the claims made about the method, such as the mitigation of spectral bias and the effectiveness of feature normalization.
- The paper does not provide sufficient implementation details, which could hinder reproducibility and further research.
- The performance improvements over existing methods are not consistently significant, with some tasks showing only marginal improvements or even performance degradation.
- The paper does not adequately address the impact of CAM on the computational cost and training time, which are critical factors for practical applications.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors clarify the novelty of their method compared to existing works like MFN and FINN, especially in terms of the use of grids versus positional encodings?
- How does CAM compare in terms of computational efficiency and memory usage with other state-of-the-art methods?
- Can the authors provide more detailed implementation details, particularly regarding the architecture and training parameters used in their experiments?
- How does the method perform when applied to different types of neural field applications, such as 3D shape representation or novel view image generation?
- Could the authors elaborate on the theoretical underpinnings of CAM, particularly in relation to the mitigation of spectral bias and the effectiveness of feature normalization?
- Can the authors discuss the potential limitations of CAM and suggest areas for future research or improvements?
