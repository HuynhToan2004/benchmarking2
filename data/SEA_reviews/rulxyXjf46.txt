 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel method for conformal prediction in regression tasks by converting the problem into a classification task using a neural network to estimate the conditional probability of labels given inputs. This approach involves discretizing the output space into bins and employing a loss function that encourages mass allocation near the true label. The method is evaluated against several baselines and real-world datasets, showing improvements in prediction interval lengths. However, the paper faces criticism for its limited theoretical guarantees, the need for further empirical validation, and the lack of comparison with other state-of-the-art methods.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel approach to conformal prediction by treating regression as classification, which is a novel and potentially useful method for handling heteroscedasticity in regression tasks.
- The methodology is clearly explained, with a detailed description of the experimental setup and results, which are supported by visualizations and empirical evidence.
- The paper is well-written and easy to follow, with a clear presentation of the methodology and results, making it accessible to a broad audience.
- The experiments are thorough, demonstrating the method's effectiveness in various settings and providing insights into the impact of different loss functions and model parameters.
- The approach is simple and intuitive, making it potentially applicable in practical scenarios, and the paper provides a comprehensive discussion of related work, positioning the research within the existing literature.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks theoretical guarantees, which is a significant limitation given the probabilistic nature of conformal prediction.
- The empirical evaluations are limited, with only a few datasets used, and the results are not sufficiently robust due to the lack of tuning of hyperparameters.
- The paper does not sufficiently compare its method with other state-of-the-art methods in the field, which could provide a better understanding of its relative performance.
- The method's assumptions and limitations are not clearly stated, which could lead to misunderstandings or misapplications of the method.
- The paper's experimental design and presentation could be improved, particularly in terms of clarity and detail in the experimental setup and results.
- There are several typographical and formatting errors that need to be corrected.
