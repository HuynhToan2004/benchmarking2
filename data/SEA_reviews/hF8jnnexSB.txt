 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel depthwise convolution-based model, LTSF-Conv, for long-term time series forecasting, which outperforms both Transformer-based and MLP-based models across seven benchmarks. The model's simplicity and efficiency are highlighted, as it significantly reduces the number of trainable parameters, GPU memory usage, running time, and inference time compared to more complex models. The authors claim that their model can capture long-range dependencies effectively, although some concerns are raised about the generalizability and theoretical justification of the model's superiority.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, clear, and easy to follow, with a logical structure that effectively communicates the research findings.
- The proposed model, LTSF-Conv, is simple yet effective, demonstrating strong performance across multiple benchmarks.
- The paper provides a detailed comparison of LTSF-Conv with state-of-the-art models, showing significant improvements in performance metrics such as MSE and MAE.
- The model's efficiency is highlighted, with a significant reduction in computational resources needed compared to more complex models.
- The authors have conducted extensive experiments to demonstrate the model's effectiveness, including a thorough comparison with existing models and a detailed analysis of the model's performance under different conditions.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed discussion on the limitations of the proposed model, particularly in terms of its generalizability and applicability to different types of time series data.
- The theoretical underpinnings of the model's superiority are not well-articulated, which could limit the understanding of its effectiveness.
- The model's performance is not consistently superior across all benchmarks, with some benchmarks showing poorer performance compared to existing models.
- The paper could benefit from a more detailed comparison with other models, including a broader range of metrics beyond MSE and MAE.
- The experimental setup and the choice of baselines could be improved to better demonstrate the model's advantages.
- There is a lack of discussion on the societal impact of the model, which is an important consideration for any new technology.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors provide a more detailed discussion on the limitations of the proposed model and how these limitations might affect its applicability in different scenarios?
- How does the model perform under different conditions, such as varying input window sizes and different data types?
- Could the authors provide a more detailed comparison with other models, including a broader range of metrics beyond MSE and MAE?
- How does the model handle different types of time series data, such as those with varying periodicities or non-stationary trends?
- Could the authors discuss the potential societal impacts of the model and how these might be mitigated or addressed?
