 **Summary:**
The paper introduces Sliced Denoising (SliDe), a novel pre-training method for molecular representation learning that employs a new noise strategy and network architecture to enhance the physical consistency of molecular representations. SliDe utilizes a quadratic energy function based on bond stretching, angle bending, and bond torsion, and a noise distribution derived from the Boltzmann distribution to perturb bond lengths, angles, and torsion angles. The method is supported by a theoretical framework and empirical evidence, demonstrating improvements in force field estimation accuracy and downstream task performance compared to existing methods. SliDe also introduces a sliced denoising technique to avoid the computational expense of calculating the Jacobian matrix, and a modified network architecture to handle the new noise strategy.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written and easy to follow, with clear explanations of the proposed method.
- The proposed method, Sliced Denoising (SliDe), is novel and supported by a strong theoretical foundation.
- SliDe demonstrates significant improvements in force field estimation accuracy and downstream task performance compared to existing methods.
- The paper provides a comprehensive ablation study that supports the claims made about the effectiveness of the proposed method.
- The use of a quadratic energy function based on bond stretching, angle bending, and bond torsion is a novel approach that better captures the nuances of molecular energy.
- The paper includes a detailed appendix that provides additional insights and justifications for the proposed method.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed discussion on the limitations of the proposed method, which could help in understanding its scope and applicability.
- The paper could benefit from more comparisons with other existing methods, especially in terms of computational efficiency and the ability to capture long-range interactions.
- The paper does not provide a detailed discussion on the scalability of the proposed method to larger datasets or more complex molecular systems.
- The experimental evaluation could be expanded to include more diverse downstream tasks and datasets to better demonstrate the generalizability of the method.
- There are some minor typographical errors and formatting issues that need to be corrected for clarity and professionalism.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors provide more details on the scalability of the proposed method to larger datasets or more complex molecular systems?
- How does the proposed method compare in terms of computational efficiency with other existing methods?
- Could the authors discuss the potential limitations of the proposed method and how these might affect its practical application?
- Can the authors provide more examples or case studies to demonstrate the physical consistency of the learned representations?
- How does the proposed method perform when applied to more complex molecular systems, such as proteins or large organic molecules?
- Could the authors elaborate on the choice of parameters in the quadratic energy function and how they were determined?
