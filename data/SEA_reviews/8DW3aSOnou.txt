 **Summary:**
The paper introduces AHFNet, a novel method for video deblurring that integrates unsharp masking with a deep learning framework to enhance the recovery of high-frequency details. The approach utilizes a spatio-temporal 3D convolution process with an adaptive kernel to extract high-frequency components from the data, achieving state-of-the-art performance on GOPRO and DVD datasets. The methodology involves a set of predefined high-pass filtering kernels and a kernel prediction module to adaptively learn high-frequency extraction kernels, which are combined with a recurrent deblurring module and a warping module for feature alignment. Despite its innovative approach, the paper faces criticism for its limited novelty, lack of comparison with state-of-the-art methods, and unclear presentation.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel method for video deblurring that integrates unsharp masking with a deep learning framework, emphasizing the importance of high-frequency information in deblurring.
- The method achieves state-of-the-art performance on GOPRO and DVD datasets, demonstrating its effectiveness in practical applications.
- The paper is well-written, clear, and easy to follow, which enhances its readability and accessibility.
- The use of high-frequency extraction kernels and the combination of first-order and temporal gradients significantly enhance the performance of the proposed method.
- The paper includes a detailed analysis of the learned kernels and the impact of different frequency sub-bands, providing insights into the effectiveness of the proposed method.

**Weaknesses:**
- The novelty of the paper is limited as the idea of using unsharp masking for video deblurring has been previously explored.
- The paper lacks a comprehensive comparison with state-of-the-art methods, which could have validated the superiority of the proposed method more convincingly.
- The presentation of the paper needs improvement, particularly in terms of clarity and organization, especially in the introduction and methodology sections.
- The paper does not adequately address the computational complexity of the proposed method, which might limit its practical application in real-world scenarios.
- The paper's contribution to the field is questioned due to its reliance on existing techniques like unsharp masking and the lack of significant innovation.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors clarify the specific advantages of their proposed method over existing methods, particularly in terms of computational efficiency and performance?
- How does the proposed method compare with other state-of-the-art methods in terms of computational complexity and runtime?
- Can the authors provide more detailed explanations and examples to support the claims made in the paper, particularly regarding the effectiveness of the proposed method in different scenarios?
- Could the authors consider including more diverse datasets and real-world applications to validate the generalizability and practicality of their method?
- How does the proposed method handle different types of blur, and can it be adapted to handle dynamic or non-stationary blur?
