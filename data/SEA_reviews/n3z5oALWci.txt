 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel framework named ReX, aimed at enhancing the interpretability of black-box models by integrating temporal information into explanations for variable-length input data. This framework extends existing explanation techniques such as Anchors, LIME, and Kernel SHAP, by incorporating temporal predicates and perturbation models that handle varying input lengths. The authors demonstrate the effectiveness of ReX through experiments on sentiment analysis and anomaly detection tasks, showing improvements in the fidelity and understandability of explanations. However, concerns are raised about the novelty and the depth of the experimental validation, particularly the lack of comparisons with other temporal-aware explanation methods and a broader range of models and datasets.

**Strengths:**
- The paper addresses a significant and relevant problem by enhancing the interpretability of black-box models, which is crucial for understanding and trusting machine learning systems.
- The proposed framework, ReX, is a novel approach that extends existing explanation techniques to incorporate temporal information, which is particularly useful for models that process sequential data.
- The paper is well-written, clear, and easy to follow, making it accessible to a broad audience.
- The experiments conducted on various models and tasks demonstrate the effectiveness of ReX in improving the fidelity and understandability of explanations.
- The use of temporal predicates and perturbation models is innovative and effectively handles variable-length inputs, enhancing the applicability of local explanation techniques.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a thorough comparison with other temporal-aware explanation methods, which limits the understanding of ReX's relative advantages.
- The experiments are primarily focused on specific models and datasets, which might not fully demonstrate the generalizability of ReX across different domains and models.
- There is a lack of discussion on the limitations of ReX, particularly how it handles complex temporal relationships and the scalability of the approach.
- The paper could benefit from a more detailed discussion on the choice of models and datasets, as well as the potential impact of these choices on the results.
- The paper does not sufficiently address the potential societal impacts of the proposed method, which is a critical aspect of responsible AI development.

**Questions:**
- Could you provide a more detailed comparison of ReX with other temporal-aware explanation methods to highlight its unique advantages?
- How does ReX perform when applied to other models or datasets beyond those used in the experiments?
- Can you elaborate on how ReX handles complex temporal relationships and the scalability of the approach?
- What are the specific limitations of ReX, and how do these affect the interpretability of the models it is applied to?
- How does the choice of models and datasets in the experiments impact the results, and could you discuss the potential implications of using different models or datasets?
- Could you discuss the potential societal impacts of ReX and how these are addressed in your research?
