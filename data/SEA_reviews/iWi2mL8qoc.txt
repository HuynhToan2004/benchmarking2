 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a Multi-Scale Window-based Transformer model for image inpainting, utilizing a Multi-Scale Window-based Polarized Self-Attention mechanism and a Selective Mask Update method. The model is designed to capture contextual information effectively by processing features through self-attention and updating masks selectively. The proposed method is evaluated against several state-of-the-art methods, showing competitive results on standard benchmarks. However, the paper suffers from significant issues in clarity and organization, with numerous grammatical errors and unclear descriptions. The methodology and its contributions are not convincingly articulated, and the experimental results are not sufficiently robust to support the claims made.

**Strengths:**
- The paper presents a novel approach to image inpainting by utilizing multi-scale windows to capture contextual information, which is a significant advancement over traditional methods.
- The proposed MSWTN model is well-motivated and well-designed, with a clear and comprehensive description of the model architecture and its components.
- The paper is well-written, making it easy to follow, and includes detailed experimental results and ablation studies that demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed methods.
- The use of a Selective Mask Update method is innovative, allowing for the capture of vital information from features processed by self-attention, which enhances the quality of the results.
- The paper is original in its application of multi-scale windows and polarized self-attention to image inpainting, offering a fresh perspective on the problem.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper suffers from numerous grammatical errors and typos, which detract from its professionalism and readability.
- The organization and presentation of the paper are unclear and confusing, making it difficult to follow the methodology and understand the contributions of the work.
- The experimental setup and results are not convincing, with the paper relying on a limited set of metrics and datasets, and not adequately comparing the results against state-of-the-art methods.
- The paper lacks a detailed discussion on the limitations of the proposed method and does not provide sufficient qualitative results or visualizations to support the claims made.
- There is a notable absence of quantitative results, which could have provided a more robust evaluation of the proposed methods.
- The paper does not adequately address the computational complexity of the proposed methods, which is crucial for practical applications.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors clarify the specific contributions of their work compared to existing methods, particularly in terms of the novelty and effectiveness of the proposed Multi-Scale Window-based Polarized Self-Attention mechanism and the Selective Mask Update method?
- How does the proposed MSWTN model compare in terms of computational complexity and inference speed to other state-of-the-art methods?
- Can the authors provide more detailed results and visualizations to support the claims made in the paper, particularly regarding the effectiveness of the proposed methods in capturing contextual information and generating high-quality results?
- How do the authors address the issue of computational cost and efficiency in their proposed methods, especially in large-scale applications?
- Could the authors provide a more detailed discussion on the limitations of their proposed methods and how these might affect the practical applicability and generalizability of the findings?
