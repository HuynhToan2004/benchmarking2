 **Summary:**
The paper introduces AugLocal, a novel method for supervised local learning that constructs auxiliary networks to enhance the synergy between local layers and subsequent layers. This method uniformly selects a subset of layers from subsequent layers to create auxiliary networks, reducing the depth of these networks as layers deepen. AugLocal aims to improve the accuracy of local learning methods by enabling hidden layers to be aware of their downstream layers during training, thus potentially achieving performance comparable to backpropagation (BP) methods. The method is evaluated on various datasets, including CIFAR-10, SVHN, STL-10, and ImageNet, demonstrating its effectiveness and efficiency in reducing GPU memory usage by around 40%. However, the paper's novelty is questioned due to its similarity to existing methods, and the computational cost of the method is not thoroughly analyzed.

**Strengths:**
- The paper addresses the significant issue of local learning, which is crucial for training deep neural networks in resource-constrained environments.
- The proposed method, AugLocal, is a principled and effective solution for supervised local learning, achieving comparable accuracy to backpropagation (BP) while reducing GPU memory usage by around 40%.
- The method is evaluated on various datasets, including CIFAR-10, SVHN, STL-10, and ImageNet, demonstrating its effectiveness across different architectures.
- AugLocal is a scalable solution for large-scale networks with up to 55 independently trained layers, which is a significant advancement in the field.
- The paper provides a thorough analysis of the hidden representations learned by local learning and BP methods, offering insights into the efficacy of the proposed method.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed comparison with other methods, particularly in terms of computational cost and memory efficiency.
- The method's novelty is questioned due to its similarity to existing methods such as those by Lowe et al. (2019) and Xiong et al. (2020).
- The paper does not provide sufficient details on the computational cost and memory usage of the proposed method, which is crucial for understanding its practical applicability.
- The paper does not sufficiently address the scalability of the method to larger datasets like ImageNet, which could limit its practical utility.
- There is a lack of discussion on the limitations of the proposed method, which could provide valuable insights for future research.

**Questions:**

- How does the proposed method scale to larger datasets like ImageNet, and what are the potential limitations in terms of computational resources and training time?
- What are the limitations of the proposed method, and how do these impact its practical utility and applicability?
- Can you discuss the potential negative societal impacts of the proposed method, if any?
