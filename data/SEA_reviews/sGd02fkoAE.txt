 **Summary:**
The paper introduces FusionViT, a novel Vision Transformer (ViT)-based framework for 3D object detection, which integrates both 2D image and 3D point cloud data. The framework comprises three main components: CameraViT, LidarViT, and MixViT, each designed to process different modalities and fuse the outputs for enhanced object detection capabilities. The experiments conducted on benchmarks like KITTI and Waymo demonstrate the effectiveness of FusionViT, showing improvements over existing methods. However, the paper has been criticized for its limited novelty, as it heavily relies on existing methods without significant innovation. The lack of detailed implementation and comparison with other state-of-the-art methods also raises concerns about the fairness and completeness of the evaluation.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, with clear explanations of the proposed methodology, and the experiments are well-designed and convincing.
- The introduction of a pure-ViT-based framework for 3D object detection is innovative, and the paper effectively demonstrates the integration of 2D and 3D data for enhanced performance.
- The experimental results show improvements over existing methods, indicating the effectiveness of the proposed approach.
- The paper provides a thorough ablation study, which helps in understanding the contributions of different components of the model.
- The use of ViT for 3D object detection is a novel approach, and the paper demonstrates its potential through competitive results on benchmarks like KITTI and Waymo.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks significant novelty as it heavily relies on existing methods like CameraViT and LidarViT without substantial innovation.
- The fusion strategy used in the paper is not clearly explained, and the comparison with other state-of-the-art methods is not comprehensive.
- The paper does not provide a detailed implementation of the proposed methods, which is crucial for reproducibility and further research.
- The paper claims to be the first pure-ViT-based framework for 3D object detection, but it does not compare its performance with other existing ViT-based methods, which limits the evaluation of its effectiveness.
- The paper's contribution is limited as it mainly focuses on a single dataset (Waymo Open Dataset) and does not demonstrate the model's effectiveness on other datasets like NuScenes or ApolloScape.
- The paper's claims of being the first pure-ViT-based framework for 3D object detection are not entirely accurate, as there are existing methods that use similar approaches.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors clarify the fusion strategy used in the paper and explain how it contributes to the overall performance of the model?
- How does the proposed method compare with other state-of-the-art methods, particularly in terms of computational efficiency and performance on different datasets?
- Could the authors provide more details on the implementation of the proposed methods, including the specifics of the training and testing procedures?
- How does the proposed method handle the integration of different modalities, especially in scenarios where one modality might be less informative or noisy?
- Could the authors provide a more detailed comparison with other existing methods, including a head-to-head comparison on the same datasets to better understand the advantages of their approach?
