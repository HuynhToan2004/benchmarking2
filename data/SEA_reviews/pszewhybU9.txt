 **Summary:**
The paper introduces InsTag, a novel instruction tagging method designed to analyze the diversity and complexity of instruction datasets used for supervised fine-tuning of large language models (LLMs). InsTag utilizes a proprietary high-performing chatbot, ChatGPT, to assign tags to training sample queries, which are then normalized to ensure consistency and quality. These tags are used to measure the diversity and complexity of instruction datasets, providing insights into the performance of LLMs fine-tuned on these datasets. The paper demonstrates that fine-tuning on more diverse and complex datasets leads to better alignment performance, as evidenced by the creation of a dataset of 6.6K fine-grained tags from popular open-sourced SFT datasets. The methodology includes a data selector based on InsTag, which selects 6K diverse and complex samples from open-source datasets for SFT, leading to the development of TagLM, which outperforms open-source models based on considerably larger SFT data.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, with a clear and easy-to-follow structure, making it accessible to a broad audience.
- The use of ChatGPT for instruction tagging is a novel and innovative approach, leveraging its capabilities effectively.
- The dataset of 6.6K fine-grained tags created from popular open-sourced SFT datasets is a valuable resource for the community.
- The paper provides a comprehensive analysis of the diversity and complexity of instruction datasets, which is crucial for understanding the performance of LLMs fine-tuned on these datasets.
- The experimental results are robust, showing the effectiveness of the proposed method in improving the performance of LLMs.
- The paper addresses an important and understudied problem in the field of LLMs, focusing on the diversity and complexity of instruction datasets.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper's reliance on ChatGPT for instruction tagging raises concerns about the potential for biases in the tagging process, which could impact the results and conclusions drawn from the data.
- The methodology used to assign and normalize tags is not clearly described, which could limit the reproducibility and understanding of the results.
- The paper's claims about the effectiveness of the proposed method in improving the performance of LLMs are not convincingly supported by the experimental results, particularly when compared to larger datasets.
- The paper does not sufficiently discuss the potential limitations or negative societal impacts of the proposed method, which could be crucial for understanding its broader implications.
- The paper could benefit from a more detailed discussion on the tag normalization process, including the rationale behind the chosen methods and their effectiveness.
- The paper's focus on specific datasets and models (LLaMA-13b) might limit the generalizability of the findings to other datasets or models.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors provide more details on the methodology used for assigning and normalizing tags, particularly the rationale behind the chosen methods?
- How does the proposed method address potential biases in the tagging process, especially considering the use of ChatGPT?
- Can the authors clarify the relationship between the proposed method and the quality of the dataset, especially in terms of how the method affects the performance of LLMs on different datasets?
- How does the proposed method compare to other methods for data selection or dataset annotation, particularly in terms of efficiency and effectiveness?
- Could the authors discuss the potential limitations or negative societal impacts of the proposed method, and how these might be mitigated or addressed?
- How does the proposed method perform when applied to different datasets or models, and what are the implications of these differences in performance?
