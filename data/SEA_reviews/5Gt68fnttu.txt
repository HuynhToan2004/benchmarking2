 **Summary:**
The paper introduces the Dynamic Seizure Network (DSN), a unified framework for EEG representation learning aimed at both seizure detection and real-time seizure detection. DSN leverages a recurrent attention network to capture dynamic dependencies within streaming EEG signals, enhancing the detection of seizure onsets. The framework's effectiveness is validated through experiments on three real-world datasets, demonstrating superior performance over existing methods. The paper also addresses the limitations of previous models by integrating a dynamic brain state modeling module and a dynamic brain correlation learning module, which are crucial for capturing the dynamic nature of EEG signals.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, clear, and easy to follow, with a comprehensive literature review that effectively highlights the limitations of existing models.
- The proposed Dynamic Seizure Network (DSN) is a novel and well-motivated model that integrates dynamic brain state modeling and dynamic brain correlation learning to capture the dynamic nature of EEG signals, enhancing seizure onset detection.
- The paper provides a theoretical analysis of the time and space complexity of DSN, demonstrating its efficiency.
- The methodology is supported by a robust experimental setup, including comprehensive evaluations on three real-world datasets and a detailed ablation study that verifies the effectiveness of each component.
- The paper includes visualizations of the learned brain correlations and attention weights, which aid in the interpretability of the model's outputs.
- The paper is well-structured, making it easy for readers to understand the proposed methodologies and their implications.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed discussion on the limitations of the proposed method, particularly concerning the choice of window sizes and the generalizability of the model to other types of epileptic seizures.
- There is a need for more detailed descriptions of the datasets used, including their characteristics and preprocessing steps, to ensure reproducibility and transparency.
- The paper could benefit from a more rigorous validation by clinical experts to ensure the clinical relevance and applicability of the proposed method.
- Some figures and equations in the paper are not clearly explained or referenced, which could confuse readers or limit the understanding of the proposed methods.
- The paper could benefit from a more thorough discussion on the limitations of existing methods and how the proposed method addresses these limitations.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors provide more details on the limitations of the proposed method and how these limitations might affect the practical application of the DSN in real-world scenarios?
- How does the choice of window size influence the performance of the DSN, and is there a general guideline for selecting window sizes in different scenarios?
- Can the authors provide more details on the preprocessing steps for each dataset and clarify any ambiguities in the experimental setup?
- How does the DSN perform with different types of epileptic seizures, and what are the implications of this for the generalizability of the model?
- Could the authors elaborate on the rationale behind the choice of metrics used in the evaluation, and how these metrics compare to other commonly used metrics in the field?
- In the context of the ablation study, could the authors provide a more detailed analysis of the contributions of each component to the overall performance of the DSN?
