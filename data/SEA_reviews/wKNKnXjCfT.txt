 **Summary:**
The paper introduces two methods, MMSE-ICP and fastICP, for identifying direct causal parents in a target variable using invariant causal prediction. These methods aim to address the challenge of running an exponential number of tests in traditional methods by employing error inequality to identify causal parents. MMSE-ICP and fastICP are evaluated against baseline methods on both simulated and real-world data, showing promising results in terms of accuracy and scalability. The paper also discusses the theoretical underpinnings of these methods and provides a comparative analysis with existing techniques.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, with clear and concise explanations that make the content accessible and easy to understand.
- The authors provide a thorough evaluation of their methods, comparing them against baseline methods on both simulated and real-world data, demonstrating the effectiveness of the proposed approaches.
- The theoretical underpinnings of the methods are well-articulated, with clear and concise proofs of theorems and propositions.
- The paper presents a novel approach to identifying causal parents by exploiting the error inequality, which is a significant contribution to the field.
- The paper is well-structured, with a clear introduction, related work, and methodology sections, making it easy to follow and understand the contributions of the research.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper could benefit from a more detailed discussion on the limitations of the proposed methods, particularly in terms of their assumptions and potential biases in causal discovery.
- The complexity analysis provided in the paper is not detailed enough, and it would be beneficial to see a more comprehensive analysis of the computational resources required by the proposed methods.
- The paper could benefit from a more detailed comparison with other related methods, such as CORTH, which might provide a clearer picture of the advantages and disadvantages of the proposed methods.
