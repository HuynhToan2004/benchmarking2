 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a method for estimating the Lipschitz constant of Convolutional Neural Networks (CNNs) by partitioning the convolutional block into smaller sub-blocks. This partitioning allows for a more efficient computation of the Lipschitz constant, which is crucial for understanding the robustness of neural networks. The method involves a dynamic convolutional partitioning (DCP) that can be applied to both deep and wide CNNs, aiming to improve scalability and efficiency in estimating Lipschitz constants. The proposed method is evaluated on various networks, including those trained on datasets like MNIST and CIFAR-10, demonstrating its effectiveness in terms of accuracy and computational efficiency.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel method for estimating the Lipschitz constant of Convolutional Neural Networks (CNNs), which is both scalable and efficient, particularly for wide networks.
- The method is well-explained and the paper is well-written, making it accessible and easy to follow.
- The proposed partitioning method is both novel and effective, providing a practical solution for estimating the Lipschitz constant of CNNs.
- The paper is supported by theoretical results, such as Theorem 4.1 and Corollary 4.2, which provide theoretical guarantees for the proposed method.
- The method is shown to be effective in practice, with results demonstrating its accuracy and efficiency compared to existing methods.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed comparison with other state-of-the-art methods for estimating the Lipschitz constant of CNNs, which could have provided a clearer picture of the method's advantages and disadvantages.
- The experimental results are not extensive enough, and the paper could benefit from more comprehensive evaluations on larger and more diverse datasets.
- There is a lack of discussion on the limitations of the proposed method, including potential issues with the tightness of the bounds and the scalability to very deep networks.
- The paper could benefit from a more thorough discussion on the computational complexity and scalability of the proposed method, especially in relation to the size and depth of the networks.
- The paper could be improved by providing more detailed explanations and examples in the methodology section, particularly in the partitioning process and the use of different norms in the estimation.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors provide more details on the computational complexity of the proposed method, especially in relation to the size and depth of the networks?
- How does the proposed method compare in terms of accuracy and efficiency with other state-of-the-art methods for estimating the Lipschitz constant of CNNs?
- Could the authors provide more detailed experimental results, including comparisons with other methods on larger and more diverse datasets?
- How does the proposed method handle the tightness of the bounds, and what are the potential implications for the robustness of the estimated Lipschitz constants?
- Could the authors clarify the use of different norms in the estimation process and provide examples or illustrations to help understand the method better?
