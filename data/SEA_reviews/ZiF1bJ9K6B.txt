 **Summary:**
The paper explores the application of reinforcement learning (RL) for coverage path planning (CPP) in unknown environments. It introduces a method that integrates multi-scale maps, frontier maps, and a novel reward function based on total variation to address the challenges of scalability and efficiency in CPP. The approach is evaluated through extensive simulations, demonstrating improved performance over existing methods. However, the paper lacks real-world validation, and there are concerns about the generalizability and computational efficiency of the proposed method, particularly in large-scale environments.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, clear, and easy to follow, with a detailed description of the proposed method.
- It introduces a novel approach to CPP by using multi-scale maps, frontier maps, and a total variation reward term, which is a significant contribution to the field.
- The experimental results are comprehensive, showing that the proposed method outperforms existing methods in terms of coverage and efficiency.
- The paper addresses the limitations of prior works by using a continuous action space, which allows for more flexible path planning and avoids the constraints of discrete action spaces.
- The use of multi-scale maps and frontier maps is innovative, enabling the agent to plan long-term paths in large-scale environments with feasible computational and memory complexity.
- The paper includes an extensive ablation study that demonstrates the effectiveness of the proposed method and its components.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks real-world validation, which limits the generalizability of the proposed method to real-world applications.
- There is a significant computational cost associated with the use of multi-scale maps and frontier maps, which could limit the practical applicability of the method in large-scale environments.
- The method's reliance on a specific sensor setup (omnidirectional) and a known localization might limit its applicability in more general or dynamic environments.
- The paper does not compare the proposed method with state-of-the-art methods, which could provide a more robust evaluation of its effectiveness.
- The method's performance in complex, cluttered environments and its ability to handle dynamic obstacles or multiple agents are not discussed, which are critical aspects of CPP in real-world scenarios.
- The paper does not discuss the limitations of the proposed method, which could help in understanding its applicability and scope.

**Questions:**
- Could you provide more details on the real-world validation of the proposed method? How does it perform in practical scenarios, and what are the challenges or limitations encountered in such applications?
- How does the proposed method handle dynamic obstacles or multiple agents in the environment? What are the implications of these dynamics on the coverage path planning?
- Could you discuss the limitations of the proposed method and suggest potential directions for future research to address these limitations?
- How does the method perform in complex, cluttered environments, and what are the implications of these complexities on the coverage path planning?
- Could you provide a more detailed discussion on the computational cost of the multi-scale maps and frontier maps, and how this cost scales with the size of the environment?
- How does the proposed method compare to state-of-the-art methods in terms of performance and computational efficiency?
- Could you discuss the method's ability to handle noise in the observations and its impact on the coverage path planning?
