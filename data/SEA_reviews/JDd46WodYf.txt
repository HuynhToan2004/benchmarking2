 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel approach to active procedure planning in instructional videos, focusing on the generation of action plans from visual observations. The proposed method leverages uncertainty metrics to guide the selection of additional visual observations, aiming to enhance the accuracy of generated plans. The authors evaluate their method on two datasets, demonstrating improved performance over baseline models. The approach is highlighted for its potential in real-world applications, although it is noted that the method's novelty and its application in more complex scenarios are limited. The paper is commended for its clarity and the experimental results, which showcase the method's effectiveness. However, concerns are raised about the limited novelty and the method's generalizability to more complex scenarios.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, clear, and easy to follow, with detailed and clear experimental results.
- The proposed method is novel and effective, as demonstrated by empirical results that show improvements over baseline models.
- The paper introduces an active procedure planning method that enables selective visual observations during the inference stage of procedure planning in instructional videos, which is a significant contribution to the field.
- The uncertainty metrics proposed are well-designed and enhance the accuracy of generated plans while keeping observation costs low.
- The paper includes a comprehensive evaluation of the proposed method, demonstrating its effectiveness and potential for real-world applications.

**Weaknesses:**
- The novelty of the proposed method is somewhat limited as it mainly involves the application of existing methods to a new task, which might not be seen as a significant advancement.
- The paper lacks a thorough discussion on the limitations of the proposed method and does not provide sufficient details on the implementation, such as the specific model used for uncertainty estimation and the method for selecting additional visual observations.
- The paper does not compare the proposed method with other active learning methods, which could have provided a more robust evaluation of its effectiveness.
- The method's generalizability to more complex scenarios is not discussed, which raises concerns about its applicability in real-world settings.
- The paper does not address the potential for the model to misclassify procedure plans, which could lead to incorrect predictions and misguided actions.
- The paper could benefit from a more detailed explanation of the method's components, such as the uncertainty metrics and the method for selecting additional visual observations.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors provide more details on the implementation of the uncertainty estimation and the selection of additional visual observations?
- How does the proposed method compare to other active learning methods, and what are the specific advantages of the proposed method over these approaches?
- Can the authors discuss the limitations of the proposed method and provide insights into how these limitations might affect its practical application?
- How does the proposed method handle scenarios where the model misclassifies procedure plans, and what are the implications of such misclassifications?
- Could the authors provide a more detailed discussion on the generalizability of the proposed method to more complex scenarios and real-world applications?
