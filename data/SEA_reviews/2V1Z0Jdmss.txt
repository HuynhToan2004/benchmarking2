 **Summary:**
The paper explores the phenomenon of over-memorization in deep neural networks (DNNs) across various training paradigms, including natural, robust, and catastrophic overfitting. The authors propose a unified perspective focusing on natural patterns, termed over-memorization, which affects the generalization capacity of DNNs. They introduce a framework called Distraction Over-Memorization (DOM) to mitigate this issue by either removing or augmenting high-confidence natural patterns. Extensive experiments are conducted to validate the effectiveness of DOM, showing improvements in generalization and robustness. However, concerns were raised about the novelty of the method and the clarity of the experimental setup, particularly in the presentation of results and the choice of datasets.

**Strengths:**
- The paper addresses a significant problem in deep learning, focusing on the shared behavior of over-memorization across different types of overfitting.
- The proposed method, Distraction Over-Memorization (DOM), is novel and effectively mitigates overfitting by preventing over-memorization of natural patterns.
- The paper is well-written, easy to follow, and presents a clear structure, making it accessible to readers.
- Extensive experiments are conducted to validate the effectiveness of DOM, demonstrating its ability to improve generalization and robustness across various training paradigms.
- The findings of the paper are significant, as they provide a unified perspective on overfitting, which is crucial for advancing the field of deep learning.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a clear definition and explanation of over-memorization, which is crucial for understanding the problem and the proposed solution.
- The choice of datasets is limited, focusing only on CIFAR-10 and CIFAR-100, which may not adequately represent more complex scenarios.
- The paper does not include a comparison with the most recent state-of-the-art methods for mitigating overfitting, which could undermine the perceived effectiveness of the proposed method.
- The experimental setup and results are not clearly presented, making it difficult to assess the effectiveness of the proposed method.
- The paper does not adequately discuss the limitations of the proposed method, which is crucial for understanding its applicability and potential drawbacks.
- There are issues with the clarity and presentation of the paper, including unclear figures and a lack of detailed experimental settings.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors clarify the definition and mechanism of over-memorization, particularly how it affects the generalization ability of DNNs?
- How does the proposed method compare with the most recent state-of-the-art methods for mitigating overfitting, especially in terms of effectiveness and efficiency?
- In the context of the experiments, can the authors provide more detailed settings and results, particularly for the DOM_DA method?
- Could the authors discuss the potential limitations of the proposed method and how these might affect its applicability in real-world scenarios?
- How does the proposed method perform on more complex datasets, such as those from the ImageNet dataset?
- Can the authors provide a more detailed theoretical analysis of the underlying mechanisms of over-memorization and how it affects different types of overfitting?
