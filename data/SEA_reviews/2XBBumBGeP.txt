 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel noise-aware sampling (NAS) method for noise generation using normalizing flows, specifically the NAFlow framework. The method aims to generate diverse noise distributions based on camera settings, which are crucial for training denoising models. The NAS method allows for the generation of noise without requiring metadata, which is a significant advantage over existing methods. The paper presents several experiments demonstrating the effectiveness of the NAS method in noise modeling and denoising performance. However, concerns are raised about the novelty and the clarity of the method's advantages over existing methods like NeCA.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-organized, easy to follow, and presents a clear motivation for the research.
- The NAS method is novel and effectively addresses the need for metadata-free noise generation, which is a significant advantage over existing methods.
- The paper provides a thorough evaluation of the proposed method, including ablation studies that demonstrate the effectiveness of the multi-scale noise embedding technique.
- The method achieves state-of-the-art denoising performance on benchmark datasets, indicating its practical utility.
- The paper is well-written, with clear figures and tables that aid in understanding the results.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a clear comparison with existing methods, particularly with NeCA, which could have been included in the experiments to highlight the advantages of the proposed method.
- The paper does not adequately address the limitations of the method, such as the need for a large dataset of noisy images and the potential for overfitting to the training data.
- The paper could benefit from more detailed explanations of the NAS method and the multi-scale noise embedding technique, as well as the architecture of the flow network.
- The novelty of the method is questioned, as it seems to be a straightforward extension of existing methods like NeCA, with similar performance metrics.
- The paper does not sufficiently discuss the potential societal impacts of the work, which could be a significant concern for the application of the method in real-world scenarios.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors clarify the advantages of the NAS method over existing methods like NeCA, particularly in terms of performance and dataset requirements?
- How does the NAFlow model compare to other noise modeling methods, such as those based on GANs or other generative models?
- Could the authors provide more details on the architecture of the flow network and the multi-scale noise embedding technique?
- How does the method handle different noise distributions across different camera settings, and what are the implications for the generalization of the model?
- Can the authors discuss the potential societal impacts of their work and how they plan to address these concerns?
