 **Summary:**
The paper explores a method to enhance the performance of small language models (SLMs) in medical settings by utilizing large language models (LLMs) to generate context for SLMs. The approach involves extracting keywords from medical data and using these to generate contexts that are then integrated into the training data for the SLM. This method aims to improve the performance of SLMs while maintaining data privacy by avoiding the direct use of sensitive medical data. The paper presents a comprehensive set of experiments across various datasets and models, demonstrating the effectiveness of the proposed method in both few-shot and full training settings. However, concerns about the novelty of the approach, the complexity of the method, and the clarity of the presentation were raised.

**Strengths:**
- The paper addresses an important and relevant problem of improving the performance of small language models (SLMs) in medical settings while maintaining data privacy.
- The proposed method is straightforward, effective, and easy to follow, with clear and well-explained experimental results and ablation studies.
- The paper is well-written, making it accessible and understandable, and provides a comprehensive set of experiments across various datasets and models, demonstrating the effectiveness of the proposed method in both few-shot and full training settings.
- The paper introduces a privacy-conscious prompting strategy that mimics clinicians' thinking to generate medical knowledge-intensive contexts from LLMs in privacy-restricted scenarios.
- The method significantly enhances performance in both few-shot and full training settings, achieving up to a 22.57% increase in absolute accuracy compared to SLM fine-tuning without context.

**Weaknesses:**
- The novelty of the approach is limited as similar methods have been previously proposed, such as using keywords to generate context for SLMs.
- The complexity of the method is high, with many components and potential points of failure, which could make it difficult to implement and maintain in real-world applications.
- The paper lacks a detailed discussion on the limitations of the proposed method and potential negative societal impacts.
- The method's generalizability to other domains and its scalability to larger or more complex datasets are not adequately addressed.
- The paper does not sufficiently compare the proposed method with more recent or advanced LLMs, which could limit the understanding of its effectiveness in current state-of-the-art models.
- The paper's presentation could be improved to better highlight the novelty and contributions of the proposed method, and to address potential issues such as the clarity of the method's description and the organization of the paper.

**Questions:**
- How does the proposed method compare with more recent or advanced LLMs, such as GPT-4 or Llama 2?
- Can the proposed method be adapted to other domains beyond medical QA, such as legal or financial domains?
- How does the proposed method scale with larger or more complex datasets?
- How does the method ensure the quality and accuracy of the generated contexts, especially when the LLM generates incorrect or misleading information?
- Could the authors provide more details on the privacy analysis, including how the privacy budget is calculated and what specific measures are taken to ensure data privacy?
- How does the method handle the potential for bias in the generated contexts, especially when the LLM is trained on biased data or when the keywords used are not representative of the data?
- Could the authors clarify the method's description and organization to better highlight the novelty and contributions of the proposed method?
