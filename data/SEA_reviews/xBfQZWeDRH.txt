 **Summary:**
The paper introduces GeoDiffusion, a method that leverages text-to-image diffusion models to generate synthetic object detection data, which can be used to augment real training sets. This approach allows for the manipulation of various geometric conditions such as camera views and bounding boxes through text prompts, enhancing the realism and diversity of the generated data. The methodology involves a foreground prior re-weighting mechanism to address the imbalance between foreground and background regions, and the results show significant improvements in detection performance. However, concerns were raised regarding the limited evaluation on diverse datasets and the generalizability of the method to different scenarios.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, clear, and easy to follow, with a clear motivation and presentation of the problem.
- The proposed method is simple and effective, with a focus on integrating geometric controls into pre-trained diffusion models for detection data generation via text prompts.
- The method demonstrates strong generalizability, showing significant improvements in detection performance and the ability to generate realistic images.
- The use of text prompts to control the generation process is innovative and allows for precise control over the generated data.
- The paper is well-organized, with clear explanations of the methodology and experimental results, which are supported by thorough ablation studies.

**Weaknesses:**
- The evaluation is limited to a single dataset (NuImages), which may not fully demonstrate the generalizability of the method.
- The method's reliance on a pre-trained object detector raises questions about its applicability in scenarios where such a detector is not available.
- The paper lacks a detailed comparison with other methods that use similar text-to-image diffusion models, which could provide a clearer picture of the method's advantages.
- The paper does not sufficiently address the potential negative societal impacts of the proposed method.
- The method's performance on rare classes is not consistently better than real data only, which is counter-intuitive and needs further explanation.
- The paper could benefit from a more detailed discussion on the limitations of the method and potential negative societal impacts.

**Questions:**
- How does the proposed method perform on other datasets such as COCO and CityScapes, and what are the specific challenges or advantages in these scenarios?
- Can the method be adapted to generate data for other tasks such as semantic segmentation or image captioning?
- How does the method handle scenarios where the geometric layouts are not realistic or violate physical laws?
- What are the specific advantages of using a text-to-image diffusion model over other methods that use similar models?
- How does the method handle scenarios where the generated images do not perfectly align with the geometric layouts, and what are the implications for downstream tasks like object detection?
- Could you provide more details on the limitations of the method and potential negative societal impacts, and how these might be addressed?
