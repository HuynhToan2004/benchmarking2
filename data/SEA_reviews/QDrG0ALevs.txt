 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel approach to offline reinforcement learning (RL) by combining the Implicit Q-Learning (IQL) algorithm with a diffusion model to learn an optimal policy conditioned on state-action pairs. The method leverages the advantage function to optimize the policy without explicit maximization, which is a significant departure from traditional RL methods. The paper presents a theoretical framework and empirical results showing that this approach can outperform existing methods in certain tasks, although it underperforms in others. The methodology involves training a diffusion model that generates actions given state and advantage information, and using this model to sample actions at test time. Despite its innovative approach, the paper is criticized for its limited experimental validation and lack of clear differentiation from existing methods.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel approach to offline RL by combining IQL with diffusion models, which is a significant advancement in the field.
- The use of an advantage-conditioned diffusion model is a novel application of diffusion models in RL, which is both innovative and potentially impactful.
- The paper is well-written and easy to follow, making it accessible to a broad audience.
- The theoretical framework provided is solid, and the empirical results are convincing, demonstrating the effectiveness of the proposed method.
- The paper presents a new method of combining IQL with a diffusion model, which is a promising approach to improving performance in offline RL.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a thorough comparison with existing methods, particularly in terms of computational efficiency and resource requirements.
- The experimental results are limited to a few tasks, and the paper does not sufficiently demonstrate the generalizability of the proposed method across different environments.
- The paper does not adequately address the issue of out-of-distribution actions in offline RL, which is a critical concern in the field.
- The paper's experimental setup and results are not convincingly superior to existing methods, particularly in terms of computational efficiency and performance on more complex tasks.
- The paper's presentation and clarity could be improved, particularly in sections where the methodology and results are presented.
- The paper does not sufficiently address the limitations of the proposed method, such as the computational cost and the need for a large dataset.

**Questions:**
- How does the proposed method compare in terms of computational efficiency and resource requirements to existing methods?
- Can the authors provide a more detailed comparison with other state-of-the-art methods in terms of performance and computational efficiency?
- How does the proposed method perform on more complex tasks, such as those involving continuous action spaces or more complex environments?
- What are the specific advantages of using a diffusion model over other types of models in offline RL?
- How does the proposed method address the issue of out-of-distribution actions in offline RL?
- Can the authors clarify the role and impact of the "null" token in the diffusion model, and how it is used during training and testing?
- What are the specific computational costs and resource requirements of the proposed method, and how do they compare to other methods?
