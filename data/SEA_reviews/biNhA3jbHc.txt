 **Summary:**
The paper investigates a Hopfield-type recurrent neural network (RNN) with hidden neurons, aiming to learn sequence attractors that can represent temporal sequence information. The authors propose a novel learning algorithm and demonstrate its effectiveness through experiments on both synthetic and real-world datasets. The model is shown to be capable of storing and retrieving sequences robustly, even under noisy conditions. The theoretical analysis and empirical evidence support the model's ability to generate complex sequences, although the paper's scope is limited to binary neurons and threshold dynamics, which may not fully capture the biological plausibility.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, with clear and concise explanations, making it easy to follow.
- The authors provide both theoretical analysis and empirical evidence to support their claims, demonstrating the effectiveness of their proposed learning algorithm.
- The paper introduces a novel approach by integrating hidden neurons into Hopfield networks, which enhances the network's ability to generate complex sequences.
- The experimental results show that the proposed learning algorithm is effective in learning sequence attractors in Hopfield networks with hidden neurons, as evidenced by robust retrieval under noise.
- The paper provides a theoretical analysis of the algorithm's convergence and its ability to generate sequences with the Markov property, which is a significant contribution to the field.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper primarily focuses on binary neurons and threshold dynamics, which may not fully capture the biological plausibility of the model.
- The experiments are limited to small-scale datasets, which may not adequately demonstrate the scalability of the proposed model.
- The paper lacks a detailed discussion on the biological plausibility of the model, particularly the role and implementation of hidden neurons in biological systems.
- There is a lack of comparison with other models or baselines, which makes it difficult to assess the relative performance of the proposed model.
- The paper does not discuss the limitations of the model, such as the scalability of the hidden neuron approach and the biological plausibility of the learning algorithm.
- The paper could benefit from more detailed explanations and justifications for certain claims, such as the necessity of hidden neurons and the biological plausibility of the learning algorithm.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors provide more details on the biological plausibility of the model, particularly the role and implementation of hidden neurons in biological systems?
- How does the proposed learning algorithm compare to other models or baselines in terms of performance and efficiency?
- Can the authors discuss the limitations of the model, such as scalability and biological plausibility, and suggest potential avenues for future research?
- Is there a possibility to extend the model to include continuous neurons and more complex dynamics, such as spiking neurons?
- Could the authors clarify the role of the fixed random matrix P in the learning algorithm and explain its impact on the model's performance?
- How does the model handle the complexity of biological sequences, and what are the implications for its application in real-world scenarios?
