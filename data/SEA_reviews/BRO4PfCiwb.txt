 **Summary:**
The paper introduces OS-net, a novel neural network architecture designed for learning dynamics from periodic data. It leverages the stability of the first variational equation of the dynamical system to regularize the neural network weights, ensuring stability in the learned dynamics. The paper provides theoretical analysis and empirical results on the Rossler and Sprott systems, demonstrating the effectiveness of the proposed method. However, the paper's novelty and the scope of its applications are questioned, as it primarily focuses on learning dynamics from periodic data and does not extend to other types of dynamical systems. The methodology, while sound, is seen as a straightforward application of existing stability theory, and the empirical results are limited to a few systems, raising concerns about the generalizability of the findings.

**Strengths:**
- The paper addresses an interesting problem of learning dynamics from periodic data, which is relevant and has potential applications in various fields.
- The proposed OS-net architecture is novel and well-motivated, utilizing the stability of the first variational equation of the dynamical system to regularize the neural network weights.
- The paper is well-written, clear, and provides a good background on the stability of periodic orbits of dynamical systems, which is crucial for understanding the proposed method.
- The methodology is supported by theoretical analysis and empirical results, showing the effectiveness of the proposed method in learning dynamics from periodic data.
- The paper introduces a new regularization strategy that depends on the matrix J, which is a novel approach in the field.

**Weaknesses:**
- The novelty of the paper is questioned as the methodology primarily involves applying existing stability theory to a neural network, which may not be seen as a significant advancement.
- The scope of the paper is limited to learning dynamics from periodic data, which may not be of interest to the broader machine learning community.
- The paper lacks a comprehensive literature review, particularly in the area of neural networks for dynamical systems, which could have provided a richer context for the research.
- The empirical results are limited to a few systems, raising concerns about the generalizability of the findings.
- The paper could benefit from a more detailed discussion on the limitations of the proposed method and a broader range of empirical evaluations to validate its effectiveness across different types of dynamical systems.
- The paper could be improved by providing more detailed explanations of the methodology and the theoretical results, particularly in the appendices.

**Questions:**
- How does the proposed method compare to other existing methods for learning dynamics from periodic data?
- Can the method be applied to other types of dynamical systems, such as those with chaotic dynamics?
- How does the method perform when the dynamics are not periodic?
- Can the authors provide more detailed explanations of the methodology, particularly in the appendices, to enhance the paper's clarity and depth?
- How does the method ensure stability in the learned dynamics, and what are the implications if the dynamics are not stable?
- Could the authors provide more empirical evaluations to demonstrate the effectiveness of the method across different types of dynamical systems?
- How does the method handle the complexity of learning dynamics from high-dimensional systems, and what are the implications for computational efficiency and accuracy?
