 **Summary:**
The paper introduces GlycoNMR, a novel dataset specifically curated for machine learning (ML) research in the field of carbohydrate NMR. This dataset, comprising 2,609 carbohydrate structures and 211,543 annotated NMR shifts, is designed to address the lack of comprehensive, well-curated carbohydrate-specific datasets, a significant barrier in advancing research in glycoscience and glycomaterials. The dataset is tailored to enhance machine learning models' capabilities in predicting NMR shifts, thereby contributing to the development of ML tools for carbohydrate research. The paper also discusses the challenges in interpreting and annotating carbohydrate data, which are more complex than those for proteins, and the need for specialized ML techniques to meet the unique challenges posed by carbohydrate data.

**Strengths:**
- The paper addresses a significant gap in the field by introducing GlycoNMR, a large, high-quality, ML-friendly carbohydrate NMR dataset specifically curated for ML research. This dataset is crucial for advancing research in glycoscience and glycomaterials.
- The authors have designed a set of chemically-informed features tailored for carbohydrates, which are chemically intuitive and enhance the performance of graph-based MRL models.
- The paper includes a baseline 2D GNN model for predicting carbohydrate NMR chemical shifts and adapts and benchmarks multiple 3D-based MRL methods on GlycoNMR, demonstrating the potential usage of ML approaches in glycoscience research.
- The authors have provided a detailed description of the dataset construction process, including the creation of a new dataset (GlycoNMR.Sim) using the GODESS platform and the manual curation of NMR data from Glycosciences.DB.
- The paper is well-written, making it accessible and easy to follow, and includes a detailed description of the dataset construction process, which is crucial for reproducibility and transparency in research.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper could benefit from a more detailed description of the data annotation process, including the specific methods used to match monosaccharides in the PDB and NMR files and the validation of topological connections between monosaccharides using the PDB structure data.
- The paper lacks a comprehensive discussion on the potential limitations of the dataset, such as the quality and completeness of the NMR data and the potential biases in the dataset due to the manual curation process.
- The paper does not provide a detailed comparison of the GlycoNMR dataset with existing datasets, which could help in understanding the unique characteristics and contributions of the GlycoNMR dataset.
- The paper does not discuss the potential limitations of the ML models used for predicting NMR shifts, such as the need for a large dataset to train the models effectively and the potential for overfitting to the dataset.
- The paper could benefit from a more detailed discussion on the potential societal impacts of the research, particularly in the context of the use of ML models for predicting NMR shifts in the field of carbohydrate research.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors provide more details on the data annotation process, including the specific methods used to match monosaccharides in the PDB and NMR files and the validation of topological connections between monosaccharides using the PDB structure data?
- How does the GlycoNMR dataset compare to existing datasets in terms of size, quality, and completeness of NMR data?
- How does the GlycoNMR dataset address the challenges of interpreting and annotating carbohydrate data, which are more complex than those for proteins?
- How can the GlycoNMR dataset be used to advance research in glycoscience and glycomaterials?
- How can the GlycoNMR dataset be used to develop ML tools for carbohydrate research, and what are the potential applications of these tools?
- How do the authors plan to address the potential limitations of the dataset, such as the quality and completeness of the NMR data and the potential biases in the dataset due to the manual curation process?
- How do the authors plan to ensure the reproducibility and transparency of the research, particularly in the context of the manual curation process and the use of ML models for predicting NMR shifts?
