 **Summary:**
The paper investigates the robustness of neural networks, specifically focusing on the relationship between geometry and spectrum. It explores the impact of regularization techniques like L2 and Jacobian regularization on the smoothness of neural network representations and their robustness against adversarial attacks. The study also examines the spectral properties of these networks, particularly how they relate to the robustness of representations. Despite the interesting premise, the paper is criticized for its limited scope, focusing on a single model and dataset, which limits the generalizability of the findings. The presentation of results is also noted as difficult to follow, with some figures and equations not clearly explained.

**Strengths:**
- The paper addresses a relevant and interesting problem, specifically the relationship between geometry and spectrum in neural network robustness.
- It provides a comprehensive analysis of various regularization techniques and their effects on neural network robustness, which is valuable for understanding the robustness mechanisms in neural networks.
- The paper is well-written, with clear and concise sections that are easy to follow, making it accessible to a broad audience.
- The experimental setup is well-designed, with a thorough exploration of different models and regularization techniques, which supports the robustness claims made in the paper.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper's scope is limited, focusing on a single model (KH) and dataset (CIFAR-10), which restricts the generalizability of the findings to other models and datasets.
- The paper lacks a clear explanation of the results, particularly in figures and equations, which could benefit from more detailed explanations or additional captions.
- The paper does not adequately address the computational cost of the experiments, which could be a significant concern for reproducibility and scalability.
- The paper does not sufficiently discuss the limitations of its work, such as the applicability of the findings to more complex models or different datasets.
