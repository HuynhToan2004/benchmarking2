 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel bit-flip attack specifically designed for Graph Neural Networks (GNNs), termed the Injectivity Bit Flip Attack (IBFA). This attack targets the injectivity of GNNs, exploiting the mathematical properties of the Weisfeiler-Lehman (WL) test to degrade GNNs' ability to distinguish graph structures. The authors demonstrate that IBFA is more destructive than random bit flips and other bit-flip attacks, showing that it can effectively compromise the performance of GNNs trained on various datasets. The paper also provides theoretical insights and extensive empirical evidence to support the effectiveness of the proposed attack.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel bit-flip attack specifically designed for GNNs, which is a significant contribution to the field.
- The authors provide a thorough theoretical analysis and mathematical proofs, enhancing the credibility of their work.
- The paper is well-written, with clear explanations and extensive empirical evidence that supports the effectiveness of the proposed attack.
- The authors demonstrate the effectiveness of their attack on several benchmark datasets, showing a higher destructive power compared to other bit-flip attacks.
- The paper is well-motivated, with a clear focus on the unique properties of GNNs and the potential vulnerabilities of quantized GNNs to bit-flip attacks.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper primarily focuses on GIN-based GNNs, which may limit the generalizability of the findings to other GNN architectures.
- The paper lacks a detailed discussion on the potential defense mechanisms against the proposed attack, which is a significant omission.
- There is a lack of comparison with other recent GNN attacks, such as those that use input graph data manipulation.
- The paper could benefit from a more comprehensive discussion on the limitations of the proposed attack and its applicability to different types of GNNs and datasets.
- The experimental setup could be improved by including more diverse datasets and comparing the proposed attack with a broader range of baselines.
- The paper could provide more detailed explanations on the experimental setup, such as the choice of datasets and the hyperparameters used.

**Questions:**
- How does the proposed attack compare with other types of GNN attacks, such as those that use input graph data manipulation?
- Can the authors provide more details on the experimental setup, particularly the choice of datasets and the hyperparameters used?
- How generalizable is the proposed attack to other GNN architectures beyond GIN?
- What are the potential defense mechanisms against the proposed attack, and how could these be addressed?
- Could the authors elaborate on the potential impact of the proposed attack on the security of GNNs in practical applications?
