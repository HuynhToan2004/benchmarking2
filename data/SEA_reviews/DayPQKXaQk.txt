 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel approach called CODEC, which leverages constrained decoding for cross-lingual label projection to improve the performance of zero-shot cross-lingual transfer learning. This method translates training data without markers and then applies constrained decoding to insert markers, preserving translation quality and enhancing the versatility of the approach. The CODEC methodology is evaluated on two cross-lingual transfer tasks, Named Entity Recognition and Event Argument Extraction, across 20 languages, demonstrating its effectiveness in improving performance over existing marker-based methods. The approach is versatile, applicable to both translating training and translating test data strategies, and is shown to outperform state-of-the-art marker-based methods significantly.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel approach to cross-lingual label projection using constrained decoding, which addresses the degradation of translation quality caused by marker injection in previous methods.
- The proposed method, CODEC, is versatile and applicable to both translating training and translating test data strategies, enhancing its utility in various scenarios.
- The paper is well-written, making it easy to follow, and includes comprehensive evaluations across 20 languages, demonstrating the method's effectiveness and applicability across diverse linguistic contexts.
- The use of constrained decoding in label projection is a significant contribution, potentially inspiring future research in this area.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper could benefit from a more detailed explanation of the constrained decoding algorithm and the heuristic lower bound used, especially in the main text to aid reader comprehension.
- The choice of baseline methods for comparison is limited, and the paper could benefit from a broader range of comparisons, including with recent and relevant studies.
- The method's reliance on high-quality machine translation models and the potential impact of their limitations on the performance of the label projection process are not adequately discussed.
- The paper could benefit from a more detailed discussion on the computational complexity and time efficiency of the proposed method, especially in comparison to other methods.
- There is a lack of clarity on the motivation for using constrained decoding over other methods, and the paper could benefit from a more thorough comparison with existing methods in terms of performance and efficiency.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors provide a more detailed explanation of the constrained decoding algorithm and the heuristic lower bound used in the paper?
- How does the proposed method compare in terms of computational complexity and time efficiency with other methods, especially when considering the translation of test data?
- Could the authors provide more examples of the label projection process to better illustrate the method's functionality and effectiveness?
- How does the method perform when using different machine translation models, and what are the implications of using a high-quality machine translation model versus a lower-quality one?
- Could the authors discuss the potential impact of different hyperparameters on the performance of the method, and provide guidelines for selecting these parameters?
- How does the method handle cases where the source sentence does not include markers during the translation phase, and what are the implications of this approach on the translation quality and performance?
