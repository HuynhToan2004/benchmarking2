 **Summary:**
The paper introduces LLaVA-Plus, an extension of the LLaVA model that integrates a skill repository to enhance its multimodal capabilities. LLaVA-Plus is designed to use a variety of tools, including vision-language models, to perform tasks in response to multimodal inputs. The model's performance is evaluated against LLaVA and other state-of-the-art models, showing improvements in certain benchmarks. However, concerns are raised about the novelty of the approach, the quality of the generated data, and the overall contribution to the field, particularly in terms of significant performance improvements over existing models.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, making it easy to follow, and the results are clear and easy to interpret.
- The proposed method is simple and effective, with a focus on a unified skill-oriented dialogue that allows for the integration of a wide range of tools.
- The paper introduces a new skill repository that enhances the model's capabilities and provides a comprehensive evaluation of the model's performance across various benchmarks.
- The integration of a skill repository and the use of a unified prediction format from LMMs are innovative approaches that allow for a more robust and adaptable model.
- The paper provides a detailed description of the data collection process, which is crucial for understanding the model's capabilities and limitations.

**Weaknesses:**
- The novelty of the approach is limited, as it primarily involves the integration of a skill repository and a unified prediction format from LMMs, which are not entirely new concepts.
- The performance of LLaVA-Plus is not significantly better than LLaVA in most cases, and there is a lack of detailed analysis on why the model's performance is not significantly improved.
- The paper does not adequately address the potential for hallucination in the model's responses, which could lead to inaccurate or misleading outputs.
- The evaluation of the model's performance is limited to a few benchmarks, and there is a need for more comprehensive testing to fully assess the model's capabilities.
- The paper does not discuss the limitations of the approach in detail, which could help in understanding the model's weaknesses and potential areas for improvement.

**Questions:**
- How does the model handle scenarios where multiple tools could be used to solve a task? Is there a mechanism for prioritizing or selecting the most appropriate tool?
- Can the model be adapted to work with different base models, such as Llama2, and how might this affect its performance?
- How does the model handle errors or inaccuracies in the tool outputs? Is there a mechanism for correcting or mitigating these errors?
- Could the model be adapted to work with different types of tools, such as those not specifically designed for vision tasks?
- How does the model handle the generation of new tools or the integration of new tools into its skill repository?
- What are the specific advantages of using LLaVA-Plus over other models like LLaVA or GPT4Tools, particularly in terms of performance and capabilities?
