 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel approach to scene understanding using radar spectra, leveraging a vision-language model (VLM) to align radar spectra with images, thereby enhancing the semantic understanding of radar data. This method involves fine-tuning the VLM to better match radar spectra, which is then used to improve object detection and free space segmentation tasks. The authors demonstrate the effectiveness of this approach by integrating the radar encoder's output into a detection-segmentation network, showing improvements over baseline models. However, concerns were raised about the novelty of the approach, the lack of comparison with state-of-the-art models, and the limited evaluation on only one dataset.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel approach by aligning the embedding space of a vision-language model (VLM) with radar spectra, which is a significant advancement in the field of automotive scene understanding.
- The methodology is well-explained, with clear figures and detailed descriptions of the proposed radar spectra-language model (RSLM).
- The paper demonstrates the effectiveness of the RSLM in improving object detection and free space segmentation tasks by injecting the spectra embedding into a baseline model.
- The motivation behind the research is clear, and the paper is well-structured, making it easy to follow.
- The authors provide a comprehensive evaluation of the RSLM, demonstrating its effectiveness in scene retrieval tasks and its potential to enhance the performance of downstream tasks.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a thorough comparison with state-of-the-art models, which is crucial for establishing the effectiveness and novelty of the proposed method.
- The evaluation is limited to only one dataset, which does not adequately demonstrate the generalizability of the RSLM across different datasets.
- The novelty of the approach is questionable as it primarily involves fine-tuning a VLM to match radar spectra, which may not be considered a significant advancement over existing methods.
- The paper does not sufficiently address the scarcity of radar spectra data, which is a significant challenge in the field.
- The performance of the RSLM is not consistently superior to the baseline model, which raises concerns about the effectiveness of the proposed method.
- The paper does not adequately discuss the limitations of the RSLM, which is crucial for understanding the scope and applicability of the model.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors provide a more detailed comparison with state-of-the-art models to establish the effectiveness and novelty of the proposed method?
- How does the RSLM perform on other datasets beyond the one used in the evaluation?
- Can the authors discuss the limitations of the RSLM and how these might affect its performance in real-world scenarios?
- How does the RSLM handle the scarcity of labeled radar spectra data, and what strategies could be employed to mitigate this issue?
- Could the authors clarify the discrepancies in performance between the RSLM and the baseline model, particularly in the object detection and free space segmentation tasks?
