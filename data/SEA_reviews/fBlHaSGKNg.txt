 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel sample selection method for semi-supervised learning, specifically for active learning and semi-supervised active learning, aimed at enhancing the efficiency of model training with limited annotation budgets. The method, named Unleashing the Power of Annotation (UPA), utilizes a modified Frank-Wolfe algorithm to minimize a new criterion, alpha-Maximum Mean Discrepancy (alpha-MMD), which is designed to ensure a representative and diverse subset of data is selected for annotation. Theoretical analysis and extensive experiments on public datasets demonstrate that UPA outperforms existing methods in terms of performance and sample efficiency. The paper also discusses the potential of UPA to improve the generalization ability of low-budget learning.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, clear, and easy to follow, with a clear presentation of the problem, methodology, and experimental results.
- The proposed method is simple, intuitive, and easy to implement, making it accessible to a broad audience.
- Theoretical analysis and extensive experiments on public datasets demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed method in improving the performance of semi-supervised learning (SSL) methods.
- The method is model-agnostic and does not require labeled data, making it applicable across different SSL frameworks and reducing annotation budget.
- The paper provides a novel criterion, alpha-MMD, which evaluates the representativeness and diversity of selected samples, and develops a fast and efficient algorithm, GKHR, for this problem using the Frank-Wolfe algorithm.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a thorough comparison with state-of-the-art methods in semi-supervised active learning, which could have provided a more robust evaluation of the proposed method.
- The experiments are limited to small-scale datasets, which might not adequately demonstrate the scalability and effectiveness of the proposed method on larger, more complex datasets.
- The theoretical analysis and the proof of theorems are not clearly explained, making it difficult for readers to fully understand the assumptions and implications of the results.
- The paper does not discuss the limitations of the proposed method, such as its applicability to different types of data or its performance under varying conditions.
- The paper does not provide a detailed discussion on the computational complexity of the proposed method, which is crucial for understanding its scalability and efficiency.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors provide a more detailed comparison with state-of-the-art methods in semi-supervised active learning to better evaluate the proposed method's effectiveness?
- How does the proposed method perform on larger, more complex datasets, and what are the implications for its scalability and effectiveness?
- Can the authors clarify the assumptions and implications of the theorems presented in the paper, especially in terms of their applicability and the conditions under which they hold?
- How does the proposed method handle the selection of labeled data, and what are the implications for the final results, especially when the annotation budget is very limited?
- Could the authors discuss the limitations of the proposed method and provide insights into how these limitations might affect its practical application?
