 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel method for training transformer models using reinforcement learning (RL) to dynamically select and tie layers together, reducing the number of trainable parameters while maintaining performance. The approach involves using a Q-network to determine whether each layer should be tied to an earlier layer, with the weights of tied layers updated accordingly. This method is evaluated on various language tasks and shown to reduce memory consumption and parameter counts significantly, with minimal performance degradation. The paper also explores the use of RL for architecture search and proposes a new method for parameter-efficient transformers.

**Strengths:**
- The paper presents a novel method for training transformer models using reinforcement learning, which is a novel approach in the field of neural architecture search.
- The method is evaluated on various language tasks and shows significant reductions in memory consumption and parameter counts, which is a significant contribution to the field.
- The paper is well-written and easy to follow, with clear explanations of the experimental setup and results.
- The use of reinforcement learning for architecture search is a novel and interesting approach, which could lead to new methods in the field.
- The paper provides a detailed ablation study that shows the impact of different components of the method, which helps in understanding the contributions of each component.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed discussion on the computational cost of the method, which is crucial for understanding its practical implications.
- The experimental results are limited to the language domain, and it is unclear whether the method would be effective in other domains such as computer vision.
- The paper does not discuss the limitations of the method in detail, which could help in understanding the scope and applicability of the findings.
- The method's impact on inference speed and model size is not discussed, which are important metrics for evaluating the efficiency of the proposed method.
- The paper could benefit from a more detailed discussion on the hyperparameter settings used in the experiments, as these could significantly affect the results.
- The method's reliance on reinforcement learning for architecture search raises questions about the scalability and generalizability of the findings, particularly in terms of their applicability to larger models or different architectures.
