 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel method for macro motion analysis using a phase-based neural polynomial Gabor fields (Phase-PGF). This approach represents scene dynamics by learning low-dimensional time-varying phases, which can be used for various tasks such as motion loop detection, motion factorization, motion smoothing, and motion magnification. The methodology involves a multi-stage training process that includes a phase generator, a neural latent decoder, and an adversarial training module. The paper also includes theoretical analysis and experimental validations, showcasing the effectiveness of the proposed method. However, the paper has been critiqued for its limited evaluation scope, lack of detailed comparative analysis, and insufficient experimental validation against existing methods.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, clear, and easy to follow, with a clear motivation for the research.
- The proposed method is innovative and can be applied to various tasks related to macro motion analysis, such as motion loop detection, motion separation, motion smoothing, and motion magnification.
- The paper includes theoretical analysis and experimental validations, which demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed method.
- The use of Gabor basis for phase representation is a novel approach, and the paper provides a detailed explanation of the method's design and implementation.
- The paper includes a human preference study to evaluate the interpretability of generated phases, which is a valuable addition to the experimental validation.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed discussion on the limitations of the proposed method and does not include an ablation study to validate the effectiveness of the proposed method.
- The experimental evaluation is limited, with only a few examples of real-world applications, and no comparative analysis with existing methods or datasets.
- The paper does not provide a detailed discussion on the computational complexity of the proposed method, which is crucial for understanding its practical applicability.
- The paper lacks a detailed discussion on the generalizability of the proposed method to different types of dynamic scenes and its scalability to complex, large-scale 3D dynamic scenes.
- The paper does not include a discussion on the failure cases of the proposed method, which is essential for understanding its limitations.
- The paper lacks a detailed discussion on the training process, including the choice of parameters and the optimization of the model.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors provide a detailed discussion on the limitations of the proposed method and include an ablation study to validate the effectiveness of the proposed method?
- How does the proposed method compare to existing methods, and what are the advantages of using the proposed method over these methods?
- Can the authors provide a detailed discussion on the computational complexity of the proposed method and its scalability to complex, large-scale 3D dynamic scenes?
- How does the proposed method perform when applied to dynamic scenes with complex motions, and what are the failure cases of the proposed method?
- Can the authors provide more examples of real-world applications of the proposed method and include a comparative analysis with existing methods?
- Can the authors provide a detailed discussion on the training process, including the choice of parameters and the optimization of the model?
