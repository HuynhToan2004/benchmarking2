 **Summary:**
The paper introduces SSL-GM, a method that integrates rich structural information into MLPs using self-supervised learning (SSL) to enhance graph inference acceleration while improving model generalization capabilities. The approach leverages contrastive learning to align GNN and MLP representations, with non-parametric aggregation to approximate GNN representations and prevent potential model collapse. The paper also includes extensive experiments demonstrating the method's effectiveness across various graph classification tasks, showing significant improvements over existing baselines. However, concerns were raised regarding the novelty of the approach, the clarity of the method's theoretical underpinnings, and the depth of the experimental validation.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, clear, and easy to follow, with a clear motivation and a clear structure.
- The proposed method, SSL-GM, is simple yet effective, and the results are impressive, showing significant improvements over existing baselines.
- The paper provides extensive experiments on various graph classification tasks, demonstrating the effectiveness of the proposed method.
- The use of self-supervised learning (SSL) to integrate structural information into MLPs for graph inference is innovative and shows promising results.
- The paper is well-organized, with a clear introduction and motivation, and the writing is clear and easy to understand.
- The methodology is well-explained, and the experiments are thorough, covering different graph classification tasks and different settings.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed discussion on the limitations of the proposed method, which could help in understanding its applicability and potential drawbacks.
- There are some missing details in the method description, particularly regarding the aggregation method used in the non-parametric aggregator.
- The experimental setup could be improved by including more details on the datasets used, such as their size and characteristics, and the number of training, validation, and test sets.
- The paper does not provide sufficient ablation studies to clearly demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed method.
- The paper lacks a comprehensive discussion on the related work, particularly in the area of graph inference acceleration.
- The theoretical analysis of the method is limited, and the paper does not provide sufficient theoretical guarantees for its effectiveness.
- The paper does not discuss the limitations of the method, which could help in understanding its applicability and potential drawbacks.

**Questions:**
- Can you clarify how the non-parametric aggregator is used to aggregate features in the MLP, and how this method compares to other aggregation methods?
- How does the SSL-GM method perform when using different aggregation methods, such as GCN or GAT?
- Could you provide more details on the experimental setup, such as the size and characteristics of the datasets used, and the number of training, validation, and test sets?
- How does the SSL-GM method handle different types of graphs, especially those with varying densities or structures?
- Can you discuss the potential negative societal impacts of the SSL-GM method, if any?
- How does the SSL-GM method compare to other methods in terms of inference speed and accuracy, particularly in real-world applications?
- Could you provide more details on the theoretical underpinnings of the SSL-GM method, particularly how it integrates rich structural information into MLPs?
