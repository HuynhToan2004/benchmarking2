 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel method for generating images with 3D annotations using diffusion models, leveraging ControlNet to incorporate visual prompts alongside text prompts. The approach involves rendering 3D shapes from various viewpoints and computing edge maps as visual prompts, which are then used alongside text prompts to guide the diffusion process. The method demonstrates its effectiveness in improving classification and 3D pose estimation tasks, showing significant improvements over baseline methods. The paper also explores the use of large language models to generate diverse text prompts, enhancing the visual diversity of the generated images. However, concerns were raised about the novelty of the method and the potential for overfitting due to the use of CAD models, which may not adequately represent real-world variations.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, easy to follow, and the proposed method is clear and intuitive.
- The method is novel and effectively integrates 3D visual prompts and text prompts to enhance the diversity of generated images.
- The proposed method significantly improves the performance of both classification and 3D pose estimation tasks, demonstrating its effectiveness.
- The paper includes comprehensive experiments and comparisons with various baselines, showing the superiority of the proposed method.
- The use of edge maps as 3D visual prompts is a simple yet effective approach that enhances the method's performance.
- The paper is well-organized, making it easy for readers to understand the method and its implications.

**Weaknesses:**
- The novelty of the method is questionable as it primarily combines existing techniques without significant innovation.
- The paper lacks a detailed discussion on the limitations and potential negative societal impacts of the proposed method.
- The method's effectiveness is dependent on the quality of the CAD models used, which may not adequately represent real-world variations, leading to overfitting concerns.
- The paper does not sufficiently compare the proposed method with existing data augmentation methods, such as Mixup and CutMix, which could have provided a more robust evaluation.
- The paper does not include a user study to evaluate the visual quality of the generated images, which is crucial for assessing the practical applicability of the method.
- The paper does not discuss the potential negative societal impacts of the proposed method, which is a significant omission in ethical considerations.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors provide a more detailed discussion on the limitations and potential negative societal impacts of the proposed method?
- How does the proposed method compare with other data augmentation methods, such as Mixup and CutMix?
- Could the authors include a user study to evaluate the visual quality of the generated images?
- What is the rationale behind using CAD models as the primary data source, and how do the authors address the potential overfitting due to the limited variability in these models?
- Could the authors provide more details on the experimental setup, such as the specific datasets used and the training parameters for the baseline models?
- How do the authors address the issue of the generated images potentially being too similar to real-world images, which could lead to issues in downstream tasks?
