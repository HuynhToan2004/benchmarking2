 **Summary:**
The paper introduces BatchPrompt, a novel method for improving the token efficiency of large language models (LLMs) by batching multiple data points into a single prompt, thereby increasing data density. This approach, termed Batch Permutation and Ensembling (BPE), involves permuting the order of data points within a batch and utilizing majority voting for labeling. The authors also propose Self-reflection-guided EArly Stopping (SEAS) to optimize the voting process and reduce token usage. Experiments conducted on various NLP tasks, including QQP, RTE, and Boolq, demonstrate that BPE+SEAS can achieve performance comparable to single-data prompting with significantly fewer LLM calls and input tokens.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, clear, and easy to follow, making it accessible to a broad audience.
- The proposed method, BatchPrompt, is simple yet effective, demonstrating significant improvements in token utilization and performance on various NLP tasks.
- The experimental results are comprehensive and showcase the effectiveness of BatchPrompt, particularly in reducing the number of LLM calls and input tokens while maintaining performance.
- The paper addresses a significant issue in the field of LLMs by focusing on token efficiency and proposing a novel approach to prompting that could be beneficial for future research.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed comparison with other baseline methods, particularly in terms of token efficiency and performance metrics.
- The experimental setup could be expanded to include more diverse tasks and datasets, such as GSM8K, to validate the generalizability of the findings.
- The paper does not provide a clear rationale for the choice of permutation and the number of rounds used in the experiments, which could affect the reproducibility and applicability of the results.
- There is a need for more detailed analysis on the effects of different permutations and the impact of varying batch sizes on performance.
- The paper could benefit from a more thorough discussion on the limitations of the proposed methods and potential negative societal impacts.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors clarify the rationale behind the specific permutation strategy used in the BPE method and its impact on performance?
- How does the proposed method perform when applied to more complex or diverse datasets, such as those in the SuperGlue benchmark?
- What is the impact of varying the number of rounds used in the SEAS method on performance and token efficiency?
- How does the method handle scenarios where the data points are not independent, such as in a multiple-choice question setting?
- Could the authors provide a more detailed analysis on the effects of different permutations on the performance of the BPE method?
- How does the proposed method compare in terms of token efficiency and performance with other state-of-the-art methods, particularly in scenarios where token efficiency is critical?
