 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel spatio-temporal GNN, termed Cycle2Mixer, designed to capture complex dependencies in spatio-temporal graphs. This model leverages topological non-trivial invariants to enhance the predictive performance of GNNs. The Cycle2Mixer architecture is composed of three main blocks: a message-passing block for spatial information, a cycle message-passing block for topological information, and a temporal block for temporal properties. The model's effectiveness is validated through experiments on traffic forecasting datasets, showing improvements over existing models. However, the paper's mathematical analysis and the novelty of the cycle message-passing block are questioned, with concerns about the clarity of the experimental setup and the theoretical underpinnings.

**Strengths:**
- The paper provides a novel approach to traffic forecasting by integrating topological invariants into GNNs, enhancing the predictive performance.
- The proposed Cycle2Mixer model is simple yet effective, and the experimental results show improvements over existing models.
- The paper is well-written and easy to follow, with clear explanations of the proposed method and its application to spatio-temporal graphs.
- The theoretical foundation of the paper is robust, with a focus on the homotopy invariance of fundamental groups and homology groups to discern spatio-temporal relationships.
- The experiments are comprehensive, covering various datasets and different spatio-temporal learning tasks, demonstrating the model's effectiveness and versatility.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed discussion on the limitations and potential negative societal impacts of the proposed model.
- The mathematical analysis and its relevance to the proposed model are not clearly explained, particularly the connection between the topological space and the traffic network.
- The novelty of the cycle message-passing block is questionable, as similar concepts have been explored in previous works.
- The paper does not sufficiently compare the proposed model with state-of-the-art models, especially in terms of computational complexity and resource usage.
- The experimental setup and results presentation are confusing, with unclear metrics and a lack of detailed experimental descriptions.
- The paper's claims about the effectiveness of the cycle message-passing block are not convincingly supported by the experimental results.

**Questions:**
- How does the Cycle2Mixer model handle the over-smoothing problem in traffic forecasting, and what measures have been taken to address this issue?
- Can you provide a more detailed explanation of the mathematical analysis and its relevance to the proposed model?
- How does the Cycle2Mixer model compare in terms of computational complexity and resource usage with other state-of-the-art models?
- Could you clarify the experimental setup, particularly the choice of metrics and the presentation of results?
- Is there a possibility to include more diverse datasets to validate the model's effectiveness across different scenarios?
- How does the Cycle2Mixer model handle the issue of cyclic subgraphs in traffic networks, and what are the implications of this for the model's performance?
