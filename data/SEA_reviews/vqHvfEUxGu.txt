 **Summary:**
The paper explores a collaborative exploration approach in reinforcement learning (RL) by employing multiple agents, each with its own set of policies and value functions, to collectively learn from a shared replay buffer. The authors propose a collaborative reward generator that considers the behaviors of all other agents to encourage exploration and specialization among agents. This approach is evaluated against various baselines on the DeepMind Control Suite benchmark, showing improved performance in terms of sample efficiency and exploration diversity. The methodology involves sharing information between agents during training and evaluation, aiming to enhance the exploration process. However, the paper is criticized for its clarity, lack of novelty, and insufficient theoretical justification.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, clear, and easy to follow, with a clear explanation of the methodology.
- The proposed method is simple, intuitive, and effective, with strong experimental results demonstrated on the DeepMind Control Suite benchmark.
- The paper addresses the important problem of exploration in multi-agent reinforcement learning (MARL), which is crucial for improving sample efficiency and performance.
- The collaborative exploration approach is innovative, leveraging the interactions between agents to enhance the exploration process.
- The experiments are well-designed and comprehensive, with thorough ablation studies and comparisons to baselines.
- The paper is self-contained, with sufficient details provided for reproducibility.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks novelty as it primarily builds upon existing methods such as parallel agents, shared replay, and intrinsic reward.
- The paper does not adequately discuss the limitations of the proposed method, including its scalability and generalizability to more complex environments.
- The paper does not provide a comprehensive theoretical justification for the proposed method, which could strengthen the argument for its effectiveness.
- The presentation of the paper could be improved, with some sections being difficult to understand and figures lacking clarity.
- The paper does not sufficiently discuss the computational complexity introduced by the use of multiple agents and the need for additional hyperparameters.
- The paper does not provide sufficient details on the implementation of the collaborative reward generator and the collaborative evaluation method, which could affect the reproducibility and understanding of the results.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors provide more details on the implementation of the collaborative reward generator and the collaborative evaluation method?
- How does the proposed method compare to other MARL baselines, such as those mentioned in the related work section?
- Could the authors discuss the potential limitations and challenges of the proposed method, particularly in terms of scalability and generalizability?
- How does the proposed method handle the issue of different agents having different exploration goals, which could lead to conflicts in the exploration process?
- Can the authors provide more detailed explanations of the experimental setups, particularly the computational costs and the selection of baselines?
- How does the proposed method perform in environments with sparse rewards, where the intrinsic reward might not be as effective?
- Could the authors provide more details on the computational complexity introduced by the use of multiple agents and the need for additional hyperparameters?
