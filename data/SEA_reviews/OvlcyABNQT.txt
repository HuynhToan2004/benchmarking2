 **Summary:**
The paper introduces Augmented Bayesian Search (ABS), a novel method that integrates Bayesian Optimization (BO) with reinforcement learning (RL) to optimize deterministic policies in high-dimensional environments. ABS leverages a new mean function for the probabilistic model, which incorporates the action-value function, thereby enhancing the exploration capabilities of BO. This integration is aimed at bridging the gap between BO and policy gradient methods, allowing for more effective exploration in complex RL scenarios. The method is evaluated on MuJoCo locomotion tasks, showing promising results compared to existing direct policy search schemes. However, the paper is criticized for its limited experimental validation and the lack of a comprehensive comparison with other RL methods.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written and easy to follow, with clear explanations of the methodology and its theoretical underpinnings.
- The integration of Bayesian Optimization (BO) with reinforcement learning (RL) is novel and innovative, offering a new perspective on how BO can be used in RL scenarios.
- The proposed method is evaluated on high-dimensional MuJoCo locomotion tasks, demonstrating its effectiveness in complex environments.
- The use of the performance difference lemma to introduce a new mean function for the probabilistic model is a significant contribution, potentially enhancing the exploration capabilities of BO in RL applications.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a thorough comparison with other RL methods, which could have provided a better understanding of the method's relative performance and advantages.
- The experimental results are not comprehensive, with only three tasks being evaluated, and the results are not convincingly superior to existing methods like MPD.
- The paper does not adequately address the computational complexity and scalability of the proposed method, especially in high-dimensional environments.
- The method's reliance on a deterministic policy gradient and the use of a deterministic policy search algorithm might limit its applicability in more complex, stochastic environments.
- The paper does not sufficiently address the issue of the curse of dimensionality in high-dimensional environments, which could limit the practical applicability of the proposed method.
