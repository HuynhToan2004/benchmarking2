 **Summary:**
The paper introduces Graph-JEPA, a novel method for self-supervised learning (SSL) in graph-level representation, inspired by the joint-embedding predictive architecture (JEPA). Graph-JEPA leverages a hyperbolic space to learn graph-level representations by predicting the coordinates of encoded subgraphs on the unit hyperbola in the 2D plane. The method involves a graph partitioning approach and subgraph embedding, followed by context and target encoding, and latent target prediction. The paper demonstrates the effectiveness of Graph-JEPA through extensive experiments, showcasing its ability to learn expressive and competitive representations for both graph classification and regression problems. Despite its innovative approach, the paper has been critiqued for its limited novelty and the need for more comprehensive comparisons with existing methods.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, with clear and detailed explanations of the proposed methodology, making it easy to follow and understand.
- The use of hyperbolic space for graph-level representation learning is a novel and interesting approach, providing a unique perspective on learning graph representations.
- The paper presents a comprehensive set of experiments that demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed method, showing that it can learn expressive and competitive representations for both graph classification and regression problems.
- The authors have provided a detailed ablation study, which is crucial for understanding the impact of different components of the model.
- The paper is original in its application of JEPAs to graph-level representation learning, a domain that has not been extensively explored.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed discussion on the limitations of the proposed method, which is essential for a complete understanding of its applicability and potential drawbacks.
- There is a notable absence of comparisons with the latest graph SSL methods, which could have provided a more robust evaluation of the proposed method's effectiveness.
- The novelty of the paper is questioned, as it appears to be a straightforward extension of existing methods like I-JEPA to the graph domain.
- The paper could benefit from more comprehensive experiments, including a broader range of datasets and comparisons with more recent and relevant graph SSL methods.
- There are some minor issues with the clarity and presentation of the paper, such as the use of undefined terms and inconsistent notation.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors clarify the rationale behind the choice of hyperbolic space for graph-level representation learning and discuss any potential limitations or challenges associated with this choice?
- How does the proposed method compare to the latest graph SSL methods, especially in terms of performance and efficiency?
- Can the authors provide more details on the training process, particularly how the context and target encoders are updated and how the predictor network is trained?
- What are the specific advantages of using hyperbolic space over other types of embedding spaces, and how does this choice impact the performance and interpretability of the learned representations?
- Could the authors elaborate on the potential applications of the learned graph-level representations, particularly in real-world scenarios?
