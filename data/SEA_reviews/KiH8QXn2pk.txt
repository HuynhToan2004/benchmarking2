 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel method, PPTSER, for few-shot semantic entity recognition (SER) in visually-rich documents. PPTSER leverages off-the-shelf multimodal pre-trained models and semantics of tags to guide the SER task, redefining it as entity typing and span detection. This method, which can be applied to any existing SER model, has been tested on several benchmarks and shows superior performance compared to traditional fine-tuning and other few-shot methods. The paper also explores the use of different pre-trained models and datasets, demonstrating the versatility and effectiveness of the proposed method.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel approach for few-shot SER on visually-rich documents, leveraging semantics of tags to guide the SER task.
- The method is simple yet effective, as demonstrated by its ability to outperform fine-tuning baselines and other few-shot methods.
- The paper is well-written, with clear explanations of the method and its components, making it easy to understand.
- The proposed method is versatile and can be applied to various pre-trained models, enhancing its applicability and adaptability.
- The paper provides extensive experimental results and ablation studies, which validate the effectiveness of the proposed method.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a comprehensive analysis of the limitations of the proposed method, which could provide valuable insights for future research and development.
- There is a need for more detailed comparisons with other few-shot SER methods, particularly in terms of the number of parameters and computational efficiency.
- The paper could benefit from more visual aids, such as figures and diagrams, to better illustrate the concepts and results.
- The paper does not sufficiently address the limitations of the method, particularly in scenarios where the method may not perform as well.
- The method's reliance on pre-trained models may limit its applicability in scenarios where such models are not available or not practical.
- The paper does not sufficiently discuss the limitations of the method, which could be crucial for understanding its applicability and effectiveness in real-world scenarios.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors provide more details on the limitations of the proposed method and how these limitations affect its performance in different scenarios?
- How does the proposed method compare in terms of computational efficiency and parameter count with other few-shot SER methods?
- Could the authors provide more visual aids, such as figures and diagrams, to better illustrate the concepts and results of the proposed method?
- How does the proposed method handle scenarios where the pre-trained models are not available or not practical?
- Can the authors provide more details on the experimental setup, particularly the division of data into few-shot settings and the selection criteria for these divisions?
- What are the specific reasons for the observed performance differences between the proposed method and other methods, particularly in scenarios with limited data?
