 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a quantum algorithm for solving linear programming problems using a quantum multi-Gibbs sampler, aiming to improve upon existing quantum algorithms that rely on Gibbs sampling. The proposed algorithm utilizes a quantum singular value transformation and a quantum maximum finding algorithm to enhance the efficiency of the Gibbs sampler, thereby reducing the time complexity of the quantum algorithm for finding the Nash equilibrium of matrix games. The paper also explores the application of this algorithm to linear programming problems, showing potential for quantum speedups over classical methods. Despite its theoretical contributions, the paper has been critiqued for its lack of experimental validation and the clarity of its presentation, particularly in the explanation of the main algorithm and its implications for practical applications.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel quantum algorithm for solving linear programming problems, which is a significant contribution to the field.
- The paper is well-written, making it accessible and easy to follow, which is crucial for a broad audience.
- The proposed quantum algorithm is based on a novel multi-Gibbs sampler, which shows potential for further research and development.
- The paper provides a thorough review of related work and presents a clear comparison with existing quantum algorithms, highlighting the advantages of the proposed method.
- The authors have made significant efforts to simplify the proofs and the presentation of the algorithm, which enhances the paper's readability and accessibility.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks experimental validation, which is crucial for demonstrating the practical applicability and effectiveness of the proposed quantum algorithm.
- The main algorithm (Algorithm 2) is not clearly explained, making it difficult for readers to understand the implementation and the implications of the algorithm.
- The paper does not adequately address the assumptions and limitations of the proposed quantum algorithm, which could affect the reliability and generalizability of the results.
- The paper's contribution is viewed as incremental, as it primarily builds upon existing methods rather than introducing new techniques or approaches.
- There are several typographical and formatting errors throughout the paper, which detract from its professional presentation.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors provide more details on the implementation of Algorithm 2, particularly the role and significance of the variables and operations involved?
- How does the proposed quantum algorithm compare in terms of time complexity and practical performance with classical algorithms for linear programming?
- Could the authors clarify the assumptions and limitations of their quantum algorithm, and discuss how these might impact the applicability and effectiveness of the method?
- Is there a possibility to extend the quantum algorithm to handle linear programming problems with a larger number of variables or constraints?
- Could the authors provide a more detailed explanation of the reduction from linear programming to matrix games, and discuss the implications of this reduction for the practical applicability of the quantum algorithm?
