 **Summary:**
The paper introduces G4SATBench, a benchmark for evaluating Graph Neural Network (GNN) solvers for the Boolean Satisfiability Problem (SAT). It presents a comprehensive evaluation framework that includes various GNN models and tasks such as satisfiability prediction, satisfying assignment prediction, and unsat-core variable prediction. The study provides insights into the performance of GNN-based SAT solvers and compares them with traditional CDCL and LS heuristics. The paper also introduces a new dataset, G4SATBench, which includes 7 datasets from 3 benchmark families, covering a wide range of prior datasets and introducing 3 levels of difficulty for each dataset. The evaluation shows that GNNs tend to develop a solving heuristic similar to greedy local search but fail to effectively learn the CDCL heuristic in the latent space.

**Strengths:**
- The paper provides a comprehensive evaluation of Graph Neural Network (GNN) solvers for the Boolean Satisfiability Problem (SAT), including various GNN models and tasks such as satisfiability prediction, satisfying assignment prediction, and unsat-core variable prediction.
- The authors have introduced a new dataset, G4SATBench, which includes 7 datasets from 3 benchmark families, covering a wide range of prior datasets and introducing 3 levels of difficulty for each dataset.
- The paper offers valuable insights into the performance of GNN-based SAT solvers and provides a detailed reference of prior work, laying a solid foundation for future research.
- The evaluation results reveal that GNNs tend to develop a solving heuristic similar to greedy local search to find a satisfying assignment but fail to effectively learn the CDCL heuristic in the latent space.
- The paper is well-written, easy to follow, and provides a unified framework for evaluating different GNN models in SAT solving, which is crucial for understanding the capabilities and limitations of GNNs in this domain.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper does not sufficiently compare the performance of GNN-based SAT solvers with traditional CDCL and LS heuristics, which could provide a more comprehensive understanding of the relative strengths and weaknesses of different approaches.
- The dataset used in the study is limited in size, which may not be sufficient to draw generalizable conclusions about the performance of GNN-based SAT solvers across a broader range of instances.
- The paper lacks a detailed discussion on the limitations of G4SATBench and potential avenues for future research, which could provide a more comprehensive understanding of the benchmark's scope and applicability.
- The paper does not address the potential biases or limitations in the G4SATBench dataset, which could affect the validity of the conclusions drawn from the evaluation.
- The paper does not include a discussion on the generalization ability of GNN-based SAT solvers, which is crucial for understanding the practical applicability of these models in real-world scenarios.

**Questions:**
- Could you clarify the definition of "difficulty levels" used in the paper and how they were determined?
- Why were the datasets chosen for inclusion in G4SATBench, and how do they represent the diversity of SAT instances?
- How does the paper address the potential biases or limitations in the G4SATBench dataset, and what steps were taken to ensure the validity of the conclusions drawn from the evaluation?
- Could you provide more details on the generalization ability of GNN-based SAT solvers, and how this was evaluated?
- How does the paper address the potential for overfitting in the GNN models, and what steps were taken to mitigate this risk?
- Can you discuss the potential for extending the benchmark to include real-world SAT instances, and how this might affect the conclusions drawn from the evaluation?
