 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel Bi-level Optimization method for Pseudo-label Learning (BOPL) in semi-supervised learning (SSL), which treats pseudo-labels as latent variables and optimizes model parameters and pseudo-labels jointly within a bi-level optimization framework. The method aims to produce high-quality pseudo-labels that are less susceptible to error accumulation, enhancing the overall model performance. The paper presents extensive experiments on multiple SSL benchmarks, showing that BOPL outperforms state-of-the-art SSL techniques. However, the novelty of the method is questioned, as similar bi-level optimization methods have been previously explored in SSL. The paper is well-written, with clear presentation and thorough experimental validation, but lacks a detailed discussion on the limitations and potential negative societal impacts of the proposed method.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, with a clear presentation that makes it easy to follow.
- The proposed method is technically sound, and the experiments are thorough, demonstrating the effectiveness of the proposed method.
- The paper introduces a novel bi-level optimization method for SSL, which treats pseudo-labels as latent variables and directly optimizes them, aiming to produce high-quality pseudo-labels that are less susceptible to error accumulation.
- The experimental results show that the proposed BOPL outperforms the state-of-the-art SSL techniques, demonstrating its effectiveness in diverse scenarios.
- The paper includes comprehensive ablation studies and impact of pseudo-label update strategies, providing a detailed analysis of the method's components and their contributions to performance.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed discussion on the limitations and potential negative societal impacts of the proposed method, which is crucial for understanding the broader implications of the work.
- The novelty of the proposed method is questionable, as similar bi-level optimization methods have been previously explored in SSL.
- The paper does not provide sufficient theoretical analysis of the proposed method, which could strengthen the paper's contribution to the field.
- There is a lack of discussion on the computational complexity and the time cost of the proposed method, which is essential for practical implementation.
- The paper does not include a discussion on the scalability of the proposed method, particularly in terms of its applicability to larger, more complex datasets or different domains.
- The experimental setup and results are not sufficiently detailed, particularly in terms of the hyper-parameter settings and the statistical significance of the results.
