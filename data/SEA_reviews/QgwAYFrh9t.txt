 **Summary:**
The paper investigates the learning of hierarchical polynomials using three-layer neural networks. It extends previous work by showing that under certain conditions, these networks can learn hierarchical polynomial functions more efficiently than kernel methods, which typically require a higher number of samples. The paper introduces a new theoretical framework that allows for the efficient learning of hierarchical polynomials, demonstrating that the network can learn the target function up to vanishing test error with a sample complexity that scales as d^k, where d is the number of input features and k is the degree of the polynomial. The paper also discusses the implications of these findings for understanding the behavior of neural networks in learning hierarchical structures.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, with clear explanations of the results and a clear presentation of the proofs.
- The results are significant, showing that three-layer networks can learn hierarchical polynomial functions with improved sample complexity compared to kernel methods.
- The paper provides a clear explanation of the limitations and assumptions, making it easier for readers to understand the scope and applicability of the results.
- The theoretical results are novel and potentially impactful, particularly in understanding the behavior of neural networks in learning hierarchical structures.
- The paper is structured well, with a clear discussion of related work and a good balance between technical details and high-level insights.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper is quite technical, and some sections may be challenging for readers not familiar with the specific mathematical concepts used.
- The assumptions made in the paper, such as the Gaussianity of the input distribution and the specific form of the target function, may limit the generalizability of the results.
- The paper lacks empirical validation, which could strengthen the claims made about the practical applicability of the theoretical results.
- There are some minor typographical errors and unclear notations that could be confusing for readers.
- The paper could benefit from a more detailed discussion on the practical implications and potential applications of the theoretical results.
