 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel variant of the recurrent switching linear dynamical system (rSLDS) model, termed the infinite recurrent switching linear dynamical system (irSLDS), which utilizes a distance-dependent Chinese restaurant process (dist-CRP) to model the discrete state transitions. This model allows for a more flexible state cardinality and incorporates a geometric structure into the state space, which is crucial for capturing complex temporal dependencies in neural data. The irSLDS is demonstrated to outperform the rSLDS model in various tasks, including synthetic data and real neural data from mice. The paper also discusses the computational challenges and proposes a method for efficient inference using a variational Laplace-EM algorithm.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, with a clear and logical presentation of the model and its applications, particularly in the context of neural data analysis.
- The introduction of the infinite recurrent switching linear dynamical system (irSLDS) is innovative, offering a new approach to modeling neural data that is both flexible and interpretable.
- The irSLDS model is shown to be more effective than the rSLDS in certain scenarios, particularly in capturing complex temporal dependencies in neural data.
- The paper provides a comprehensive literature review and effectively positions its work within the existing body of research.
- The model's ability to handle a variable number of states per trajectory is a significant advancement over traditional models, which are limited to a fixed number of states.
- The model's implementation is efficient, using a variational Laplace-EM algorithm for inference, which is a significant improvement over the Polya-gamma augmentation used in previous models.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed comparison with other state-of-the-art models, particularly in terms of computational efficiency and model performance.
- The model's reliance on the heat-equation prior for spatial smoothness is not adequately justified, and the choice of this prior over others is not convincingly argued.
- The paper does not provide a thorough discussion on the computational complexity of the irSLDS model, which could be a significant limitation for its practical application.
- The paper does not adequately address the issue of permutation invariance in the dist-CRP, which is crucial for understanding the model's behavior and interpretability.
- The paper's experimental validation is limited to a few datasets, which may not be sufficient to fully demonstrate the model's capabilities and robustness.
- The paper does not provide a clear explanation of how the model handles non-stationary data, which is a significant limitation given the model's claims to handle such data.

**Questions:**
- How does the model perform when compared to other state-of-the-art models in terms of computational efficiency and model performance?
- Can the authors provide a detailed comparison of the model's performance on different datasets, including a discussion on how the model handles non-stationary data?
- How does the model's performance vary with different choices of parameters, such as the decay parameter in the heat equation?
- Can the authors provide a more detailed discussion on the computational complexity of the irSLDS model, including the time and space complexity of the algorithm?
- How does the model handle the issue of permutation invariance, and what are the implications of this for the interpretability of the model's results?
- Can the authors provide a more detailed explanation of how the model handles non-stationary data, and what are the implications of this for the model's applicability in different experimental settings?
