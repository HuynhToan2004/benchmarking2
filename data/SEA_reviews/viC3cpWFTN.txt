 **Summary:**
The paper introduces Clip21-GD, a novel error feedback mechanism for distributed gradient descent with gradient clipping. It aims to address the convergence issues caused by gradient clipping in the distributed setting by employing a feedback mechanism to correct the biases introduced by the clipping operator. The authors prove that Clip21-GD converges at an O(1/K) rate, improving upon the O(1/√K) rate of previous methods. The paper also provides theoretical and empirical evidence of its effectiveness, demonstrating that it can converge faster than traditional methods in practical scenarios. However, the paper is criticized for its limited novelty, as it primarily builds upon existing techniques like EF21, and for its lack of comprehensive experimental validation across different network architectures and hyperparameter settings.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, easy to follow, and the theoretical results are sound.
- The convergence rate of Clip21-GD is faster than previous methods, and the algorithm is simple and effective.
- The paper provides a detailed theoretical analysis of Clip21-GD, including a convergence rate analysis and a comparison with previous results.
- The experimental results demonstrate the effectiveness of Clip21-GD in practice, showing that it can converge faster than traditional methods.
- The paper is well-organized and clearly explains the limitations of the proposed method.

**Weaknesses:**
- The novelty of the paper is limited as it primarily builds upon existing techniques like EF21, with only minor modifications.
- The paper lacks comprehensive experimental validation. It would be beneficial to include more datasets, different network architectures, and different hyperparameter settings to demonstrate the robustness of the proposed method.
- The paper's theoretical contributions are seen as limited, with the main results being straightforward extensions of existing methods.
- The paper's assumptions and the implications of these assumptions are not clearly stated, which could limit the understanding and applicability of the results.
- The paper's convergence analysis and proof techniques are seen as not sufficiently novel or significant, with some reviewers questioning the novelty of the main theorem.
- The paper could benefit from a more comprehensive discussion of the limitations of the proposed method and its potential applications.
