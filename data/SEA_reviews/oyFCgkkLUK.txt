 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a new clustering evaluation metric, αMax-B3, designed to address the limitations of the B3 metric when dealing with imprecise or coarse labels. This new metric reorganizes clusters into super-sets based on the most prevalent label and evaluates them alongside the original clusters using a modified weighted B3 metric. The extent of uncertainty, represented by α, can be either specified or estimated. The authors provide theoretical justifications for the proposed metric and demonstrate its effectiveness through experiments on synthetic and real datasets. However, the paper lacks a comprehensive comparison with existing metrics and lacks real-world dataset experiments, which are crucial for validating the metric's practical utility.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, easy to follow, and provides a clear motivation for the study.
- The proposed αMax-B3 metric is simple and effective in addressing the limitations of the B3 metric, particularly in scenarios with imprecise or coarse labels.
- The paper provides a detailed theoretical justification for the proposed metric, including theorems and proofs, which support its effectiveness.
- The experimental results demonstrate the superiority of the αMax-B3 metric over the B3 metric, especially in scenarios with imbalanced data sets.
- The paper addresses a significant problem in clustering evaluation and proposes a novel solution that could potentially improve the evaluation of clustering results.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a comprehensive comparison with existing metrics for evaluating clustering results, which could have provided a better understanding of the advantages and disadvantages of the proposed metric.
- The experimental setup is limited to synthetic data, which may not fully demonstrate the practical utility of the metric in real-world scenarios.
- The paper does not provide a detailed discussion on the limitations of the proposed metric and how these limitations might affect its applicability in different scenarios.
- The paper could benefit from a more thorough discussion of related work, particularly in the area of clustering evaluation metrics.
- The paper's presentation could be improved to make it more accessible to a broader audience, particularly by avoiding the use of complex mathematical notations and providing more intuitive explanations of the proposed metric.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors provide more details on how the α parameter is estimated and how this estimation affects the evaluation results?
- How does the proposed metric perform in scenarios with very imbalanced data sets, where the number of instances in different classes varies significantly?
- Could the authors provide a comparison of the proposed metric with other existing metrics, such as the Fowlkes-Mallows index or the Adjusted Rand Index, to demonstrate its practical utility and advantages?
- How does the proposed metric handle scenarios where the ground truth labels are not available, which is a common problem in clustering evaluation?
- Could the authors clarify the definition and use of the α parameter in the context of the proposed metric, particularly how it affects the evaluation results?
- In the experimental setup, how does the proposed metric perform when compared to other metrics in terms of evaluating the quality of clustering results, especially in real-world scenarios?
