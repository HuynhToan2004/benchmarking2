 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel neural message passing scheme for graphs and hypergraphs, inspired by the French-DeGroot and Hegselmann-Krause models from sociology. The proposed method, named ODNet, incorporates a bounded confidence mechanism to influence node weights, aiming to address issues such as oversmoothing and improve prediction performance. ODNet is tested across various datasets, including homophilic and heterophilic graphs, and hypergraphs, demonstrating its effectiveness in node classification tasks. The paper also discusses the application of ODNet in simplifying complex social networks and its potential in biological domains. Despite its novel approach and promising results, the paper is criticized for its limited technical novelty, lack of comprehensive literature review, and insufficient experimental validation.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel message passing scheme, ODNet, which effectively addresses the oversmoothing problem in graph neural networks (GNNs).
- The proposed method is versatile and applicable across different types of graphs, including homophilic, heterophilic, and hypergraphs, demonstrating its general applicability.
- The integration of opinion dynamics from sociology with neural message passing offers a unique perspective, enhancing the understanding of information propagation and aggregation in social networks.
- ODNet's ability to simplify complex social networks by leveraging knowledge of interaction frequencies within the system is highlighted as a significant advantage.
- The paper provides a clear and intuitive mathematical framework for understanding information propagation and aggregation among connected nodes in graphs, contributing to the field of graph neural networks.
- The experimental results show that ODNet outperforms conventional baselines in graph representation learning, demonstrating its practical significance in analyzing real-world co-occurrence networks of metabolic genes.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a comprehensive literature review, particularly in the area of opinion dynamics and neural message passing, which could have enriched the discussion and contextualized the research more effectively.
- The technical novelty of the proposed method is limited, as the bounded confidence mechanism and the piecewise message propagation rule are not significantly different from existing methods.
- The experimental validation is insufficient, with the paper not providing a detailed comparison with the latest state-of-the-art methods, nor does it include a comprehensive ablation study to validate the effectiveness of the proposed components.
- The paper's writing style and structure need improvement, particularly in the introduction and related work sections, which could make the paper more accessible and understandable to a broader audience.
- The paper does not sufficiently address the potential societal impact of the proposed method, which could be a significant concern in applications involving social networks and opinion dynamics.
- The paper's presentation and clarity could be improved, particularly in the explanation of key concepts and methodologies, which are currently confusing or insufficiently detailed.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors provide a more detailed comparison with the latest state-of-the-art methods, including a comprehensive ablation study to validate the effectiveness of the proposed components?
- How does the proposed method perform on larger datasets, and what are the computational costs associated with its implementation?
- Could the authors clarify the technical novelty of the proposed method, particularly how it differs from existing methods in terms of message passing and the use of bounded confidence mechanisms?
- How does the proposed method handle the issue of oversmoothing in GNNs, and what are the specific mechanisms or components that address this issue?
- Can the authors provide more details on the experimental setup, including the specific datasets used, the evaluation metrics, and the hyperparameters tuned for each model?
- How does the proposed method perform in real-world applications, particularly in domains such as social media analysis or biological network analysis?
- Can the authors elaborate on the potential societal impacts of the proposed method, particularly in applications involving social networks and opinion dynamics?
