 **Summary:**
The paper investigates the impact of feedback mechanisms on emergent communication in signaling games, particularly focusing on the effects of noise on generalization performance and compositionality. It introduces a new setting where the receiver can provide feedback to the sender, allowing for conversational repair. The study uses GRU-based models and the Coco dataset to explore these phenomena, showing that feedback mechanisms can enhance generalization under noisy conditions but may decrease compositionality. The paper also discusses the implications of these findings for the design of more realistic language evolution models.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, clear, and easy to follow, making complex concepts accessible to a broad audience.
- The authors provide a comprehensive discussion of related work, which effectively contextualizes their research within the existing literature.
- The paper includes a variety of experiments and analyses, demonstrating the robustness of the findings and providing valuable insights into the role of feedback in emergent communication.
- The introduction of a feedback channel in the signaling game setup is innovative, allowing for the study of conversational repair mechanisms, which is a significant contribution to the field.
- The paper addresses an important and relevant question concerning the role of feedback in emergent communication, which is crucial for understanding the evolution of communication systems.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a clear explanation of the benefits of feedback mechanisms, especially in terms of how they enhance communication efficiency or facilitate the emergence of more complex communication systems.
- The experimental setup is limited to binary feedback, which may not fully capture the complexity of natural conversational repair mechanisms.
- The generalization performance and compositionality scores are not convincingly explained, particularly in relation to the noise introduced and the feedback mechanisms used.
- The paper does not adequately address the potential limitations or negative societal impacts of its findings, which could be crucial for guiding future research and policy decisions.
- The paper could benefit from a more detailed discussion on the implications of the findings for the design of more realistic language evolution models, particularly in terms of how feedback mechanisms might be adapted or improved to better mimic natural conversational repair.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors provide more insights into the benefits of feedback mechanisms, particularly how they facilitate the emergence of more complex communication systems or enhance communication efficiency?
- How might the findings of this study be applied to real-world conversational agents to improve their performance and effectiveness?
- Could the authors elaborate on the potential negative societal impacts of their findings, and how these might be mitigated or addressed in future research?
- How does the use of feedback mechanisms affect the emergence of more complex communication systems, and what implications might this have for the design of more realistic language evolution models?
- In light of the findings regarding the impact of feedback on generalization performance and compositionality, how might these insights be used to guide the development of more effective conversational repair mechanisms in future research?
