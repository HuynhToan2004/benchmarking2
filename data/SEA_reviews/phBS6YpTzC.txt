 **Summary:**
The paper investigates the generator-validator consistency (GV-consistency) in language models (LLMs), a significant issue where the generator and validator outputs do not align. It proposes a method to enhance GV-consistency by fine-tuning models on GV-consistent data, demonstrating improvements in GV-consistency and model performance across various tasks. The methodology involves a consistency filtering step followed by fine-tuning on the filtered data, showing improvements over baseline methods. However, the paper has been critiqued for its limited novelty, the lack of comprehensive baseline comparisons, and the need for more extensive evaluations to substantiate its claims.

**Strengths:**
- The paper addresses a significant and practical issue of GV-consistency in LLMs, which is crucial for ensuring the reliability and trustworthiness of LLM-generated content.
- The proposed method of filtering GV-consistent data and fine-tuning on this data is simple, effective, and does not require labeled data, making it accessible and applicable to various scenarios.
- The paper provides a comprehensive evaluation across six tasks, demonstrating the effectiveness of the proposed method in improving GV-consistency and model performance.
- The paper is well-written, clear, and easy to follow, making it accessible to a broad audience.
- The proposed method is innovative and could potentially be applied to a variety of LLM applications, enhancing the consistency and accuracy of LLM outputs.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed discussion on related work, particularly on prompt consistency and consistency in LLMs, which are closely related to the topic.
- The methodology is somewhat limited in its application, as it requires a separate validator for each task, which may not be practical or scalable in all scenarios.
- The paper does not provide sufficient evidence or theoretical support for the claim that consistency is a criterion for data filtering, which is a crucial aspect of the proposed method.
- The baseline comparisons are insufficient, particularly missing comparisons with state-of-the-art methods such as LLM-Chain-of-Thought (CoT) and other relevant works in the field.
- The paper's claims about the effectiveness of the proposed method are not convincingly supported by the results presented, which are sometimes contradictory or insufficiently explained.
- There is a lack of clarity and detail in some sections of the paper, particularly in the methodology and experimental setups, which could hinder understanding and replication of the results.

**Questions:**
- Can you provide more details on how the validator queries are generated and how they are designed to ensure the consistency of LLM outputs?
- How does the proposed method compare to other state-of-the-art methods in terms of improving GV-consistency and overall model performance?
- Could you elaborate on the theoretical underpinnings of the consistency criterion for data filtering, and provide empirical evidence to support this claim?
- How does the proposed method perform in scenarios where the validator is not 100% accurate, and what measures can be taken to mitigate the impact of this inaccuracy?
- Can you clarify the discrepancies and contradictions in the results presented, particularly the significant improvements in GV-consistency but inconsistent performance improvements across different tasks and models?
- How does the proposed method compare to other methods that use consistency labels for training, and what are the advantages or disadvantages of these methods compared to the approach presented in this paper?
