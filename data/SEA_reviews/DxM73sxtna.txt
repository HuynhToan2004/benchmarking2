 **Summary:**
The paper investigates the problem of differentially private linear regression in the over-parameterized regime, where the number of features exceeds the number of samples. It proposes a variant of the DP-FTRL algorithm with a general covariance matrix for differential privacy, aiming to achieve sharper excess risk bounds that depend on the eigenspectrum of the data covariance matrix and the ground truth model parameter. The authors also consider the use of unlabeled public data to design a better noise covariance matrix structure, which can potentially improve utility. The paper claims that this method can achieve dimension-independent excess risk, a significant advancement over previous methods that rely on large batch gradients or privacy amplification techniques.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel variant of the DP-FTRL algorithm for private linear regression, which is a significant advancement in the field.
- The proposed method achieves sharper excess risk bounds that depend on the eigenspectrum of the data covariance matrix and the ground truth model parameter, which is a notable improvement over previous methods.
- The paper is well-written and easy to follow, making it accessible to readers.
- The authors consider the use of unlabeled public data to design a better noise covariance matrix structure, which is an innovative approach.
- The paper provides a clear presentation of the problem setting and the proposed algorithm, along with a detailed discussion of the related work.

**Weaknesses:**

- The computational complexity of the proposed algorithm is not discussed, which is important for understanding its scalability and efficiency.
- The paper does not adequately discuss the limitations of its approach, particularly the assumptions made and the potential impact of these assumptions on the applicability of the results.
