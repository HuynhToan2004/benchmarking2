 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel transformer-based model, MGTST, designed for multivariate long-term time-series forecasting. The model incorporates a parallel multi-scale architecture and a cross-channel attention mechanism to capture both temporal and cross-channel dependencies effectively. It also includes a channel grouping strategy to manage interaction redundancy. The model is evaluated on multiple datasets and shows superior performance over existing methods, with significant improvements in forecasting accuracy and computational efficiency. The paper is well-written, easy to follow, and includes comprehensive ablation studies and sensitivity analyses.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, clear, and easy to follow, making it accessible to a broad audience.
- The proposed model, MGTST, introduces innovative techniques such as Parallel Multi-Scale Architecture (PMSA), Temporal Embedding with Representation Tokens (TERT), and Cross-Channel Attention and Gated Mechanism (CCAGM), which are novel and effective.
- The model demonstrates superior performance compared to existing methods, as shown in extensive experiments and ablation studies.
- The paper includes a comprehensive sensitivity analysis, which helps in understanding the model's behavior and performance under different conditions.
- The authors provide a detailed theoretical analysis and a comprehensive experimental evaluation, which supports the claims made about the model's effectiveness and efficiency.
- The code is provided, which enhances the reproducibility of the results.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed discussion on the limitations of the proposed method, which could help in understanding its scope and applicability.
- The novelty of the proposed method is questionable as it combines existing techniques such as multi-scale attention and cross-channel attention, which have been previously used in other models.
- The experimental setup and results are not convincingly superior to existing methods, particularly in terms of statistical significance and the size of the datasets used.
- The paper does not adequately address the issue of overfitting, which is a common challenge in time series forecasting.
- There is a lack of comparison with some state-of-the-art models, which could provide a more robust validation of the proposed method.
- The paper does not discuss the limitations of the proposed method, which could help in understanding its scope and applicability.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors provide a detailed comparison of the proposed method with existing models, particularly in terms of computational complexity, model size, and performance metrics?
- How does the proposed method perform on larger datasets, and what are the implications for its scalability and applicability in real-world scenarios?
- Could the authors clarify the role and impact of the "T" token in the model's operation?
- What are the specific advantages of the proposed method over existing models, particularly in terms of computational efficiency and forecasting accuracy?
- Can the authors provide more detailed explanations of the model's architecture and its components, particularly the "T" token and the "gated mechanism"?
- How does the model handle the issue of overfitting, and what measures have been taken to mitigate this risk?
