 **Summary:**
The paper introduces GRformer, a Transformer-based model for multivariate long-term time series forecasting, which integrates graph neural networks and recurrent neural networks to enhance the handling of cross-channel dependencies and temporal order information. The model utilizes a mix-hop propagation layer and RNN-based position encoding to improve the understanding of correlations between multivariate time series. Extensive experiments on eight real-world datasets demonstrate the model's effectiveness, showing improvements over existing models. However, the novelty of the approach is questioned due to its similarities with previous works, and the paper's clarity and organization need improvement.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, making it easy to follow, and the proposed model is clearly described.
- The motivation behind the research is well-articulated, focusing on the importance of understanding correlations between multivariate time series.
- The model effectively integrates graph neural networks with Transformers, which is a novel approach.
- Extensive experiments are conducted on eight real-world datasets, demonstrating the model's effectiveness and outperforming existing models in most cases.
- The paper provides a clear analysis of the computational complexity of the proposed method.

**Weaknesses:**
- The novelty of the proposed model is limited as similar approaches have been previously explored, such as the use of graph neural networks and recurrent neural networks for multivariate time series forecasting.
- The paper lacks a thorough literature review, particularly in the area of multivariate time series forecasting, which could provide a more robust context for the research.
- The paper does not adequately address the limitations of the proposed method, which could help in understanding the scope and applicability of the findings.
- The paper could benefit from a more detailed discussion on the selection of datasets and the rationale behind the choice of baselines.
- The clarity and organization of the paper could be improved, particularly in the presentation of figures and tables, and the structure of the content.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors clarify the specific advantages of the proposed model over existing methods, particularly in terms of the integration of graph neural networks with Transformers?
- How does the proposed model handle the issue of chaotic information interactions in multivariate time series forecasting, and what are the implications for the model's performance?
- Can the authors provide more details on the computational complexity of the proposed method, particularly in comparison to other models?
- How does the model handle the potential for overfitting, especially in scenarios with large look-back windows?
- Could the authors elaborate on the selection of datasets and the rationale behind the choice of baselines?
- What are the potential applications of the proposed model, and how does it compare to other state-of-the-art models in terms of performance and computational efficiency?
