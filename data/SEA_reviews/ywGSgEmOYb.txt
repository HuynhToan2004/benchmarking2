 **Summary:**
The paper investigates the effectiveness of fine-tuning in mitigating backdoor attacks across various machine learning paradigms, including encoder-based, transfer-based, and standalone scenarios. It introduces a super-fine-tuning method that adjusts the learning rate dynamically to enhance defense performance while maintaining model utility. Extensive experiments demonstrate that super-fine-tuning can effectively reduce attack success rates and maintain high clean accuracy, often outperforming other defense methods. However, the paper's novelty is questioned as fine-tuning has been previously explored, and the methodological advancements over existing techniques are not convincingly justified.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, with clear and concise descriptions of the proposed methods and results, making it accessible to readers.
- Extensive experiments are conducted to validate the effectiveness of the proposed super-fine-tuning method, showing that it can effectively mitigate backdoor attacks across different scenarios.
- The paper provides a comprehensive comparison with other defense methods, highlighting the superiority of the proposed method in terms of attack success rate, clean accuracy, and computational cost.
- The simplicity and ease of implementation of the proposed method make it attractive for practical applications.
- The paper includes a detailed analysis of the proposed method, which is supported by extensive experiments.

**Weaknesses:**
- The novelty of the proposed method is limited as fine-tuning has been previously explored, and the paper does not convincingly demonstrate significant advancements over existing techniques.
- The paper lacks a detailed discussion on the limitations of the proposed method and its potential failures.
- The comparison with other defense methods is not comprehensive, and the paper does not include a comparison with state-of-the-art defenses such as those mentioned in [1].
- The paper does not provide code for reproducibility, which is essential for the verification of the results.
- The experiments are conducted on smaller datasets, which may not adequately represent the performance of the proposed method on larger, more complex datasets.
- The paper does not discuss the potential negative societal impacts of the proposed method, which is a critical aspect of ethical AI research.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors provide a more detailed discussion on the limitations of the proposed method and its potential failures?
- How does the proposed method compare with state-of-the-art defenses, particularly those mentioned in [1]?
- Could the authors provide the code for reproducibility of the results?
- How does the proposed method perform on larger, more complex datasets, and what are the implications for its scalability and performance?
- What are the potential negative societal impacts of the proposed method, and how do the authors address these concerns?
