 **Summary:**
The paper introduces SkipAt, a novel method aimed at reducing the computational cost of Vision Transformers (ViTs) by approximating self-attention operations across layers using a parametric function. The SkipAt method leverages the observed redundancy in attention mechanisms across layers, particularly the high correlation between CLS tokens, to reuse computations from preceding layers. This approach is validated through extensive experiments across various vision tasks such as image classification, semantic segmentation, image denoising, and video denoising. The method is shown to improve throughput and maintain or enhance performance, demonstrating its effectiveness and efficiency.
