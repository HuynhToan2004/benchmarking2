 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel self-supervised method for high dynamic range (HDR) image reconstruction, named SelfHDR. This method leverages the internal properties of multi-exposure images to estimate HDR color and structure components without requiring ground-truth HDR images. The color component is estimated from aligned multi-exposure images using optical flow, while the structure component is generated by a structure-focused network supervised by the color component and an input reference image. The method achieves comparable performance to supervised methods and outperforms existing self-supervised methods. Extensive experiments on various datasets demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed method.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel self-supervised method for HDR reconstruction, which is a significant advancement in the field.
- The method is well-explained, making it easy to understand and follow.
- The paper is well-written and presents a clear and concise methodology.
- The experimental results demonstrate that the proposed method achieves comparable performance to supervised methods and outperforms existing self-supervised methods.
- The use of color and structure components to guide the learning of the reconstruction network is a novel approach that effectively reduces the need for ground-truth HDR images.
- The paper provides a comprehensive ablation study, which helps in understanding the contributions of different components of the method.
- The availability of codes and datasets used in the experiments is a commendable practice for reproducibility and transparency.

**Weaknesses:**
- The method's performance is limited to dynamic scenes with camera shake, which may not generalize well to other types of dynamic scenes.
- The paper lacks a detailed discussion on the limitations and potential failure cases of the proposed method.
- The use of the same network architecture for both the structure-focused and reconstruction networks might not be optimal and could be improved by using different architectures tailored to each component.
- The paper does not provide a detailed analysis of the computational complexity of the proposed method, which is crucial for practical deployment.
