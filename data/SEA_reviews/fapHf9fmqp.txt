 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel method for non-parametric density estimation using the Root Sobolev Regularized density estimator (RSR), which employs a Sobolev norm regularization on the density function. The RSR method is formulated as an optimization problem in a Reproducing Kernel Hilbert Space (RKHS), with a regularizer that encourages the norm of the derivative of the density function to be small. The method is evaluated using a Fisher divergence-based score matching approach, demonstrating its effectiveness in anomaly detection tasks. The paper also discusses the computational challenges and provides a theoretical analysis of the consistency of the estimator.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel method for non-parametric density estimation, which is a significant contribution to the field.
- The method is clearly explained, with a thorough theoretical analysis, and the paper is well-written, making it accessible and easy to follow.
- The proposed method is evaluated on a comprehensive benchmark, demonstrating its effectiveness in anomaly detection tasks.
- The paper provides a detailed analysis of the consistency of the estimator, which is crucial for the validity and reliability of the method.
- The use of Fisher divergence for hyperparameter tuning is innovative and could be a significant contribution to the field.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed discussion on the computational challenges and complexity of the proposed method, which is crucial for practical applications.
- The methodology is not clearly distinguished from existing methods like Kernel Density Estimation (KDE), and the paper does not sufficiently highlight the differences or advantages over KDE.
- The paper does not provide a clear comparison with other state-of-the-art methods, which could help in understanding the relative performance of the proposed method.
- There are several minor typographical and grammatical errors that need correction.
- The paper could benefit from more detailed experimental results and comparisons, especially in terms of computational efficiency and scalability.

**Questions:**
- How does the proposed method compare to other state-of-the-art methods in terms of computational efficiency and scalability?
- Could the authors provide more details on the computational challenges and complexity of the proposed method?
- How does the proposed method perform in high-dimensional data, and what are the implications for practical applications?
- Could the authors clarify the distinction between the proposed method and KDE, and discuss the advantages and disadvantages of each approach?
- How does the paper address the issue of non-convex optimization in the context of the proposed method?
- Can the authors provide a more detailed comparison of the proposed method with other methods in terms of performance on different types of data, such as image data?
