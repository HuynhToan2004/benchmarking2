 **Summary:**
The paper introduces Hierarchical Side-Tuning (HST), a novel approach for Parameter-Efficient Transfer Learning (PETL) in Vision Transformers (ViTs). HST employs a lightweight hierarchical side network (HSN) that leverages intermediate activations from the backbone to generate multi-scale features, thereby adapting to various downstream tasks. The method is validated through extensive experiments on diverse visual tasks, including image classification, object detection, instance segmentation, and semantic segmentation. Despite the strong performance shown, the paper faces criticism for its incremental novelty and lack of significant improvements over existing methods. Concerns are also raised about the clarity of presentation and the depth of experimental validation.

**Strengths:**
- The proposed method is intuitive and easy to understand, with a clear motivation for the design of each component.
- Extensive experiments demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed method across various tasks, including image classification, object detection, instance segmentation, and semantic segmentation.
- The paper is well-written, with clear explanations and illustrations that aid in understanding the methodology.
- The ablation study is thorough, providing insights into the effectiveness of each component of the proposed method.
- The method shows significant improvements in performance, particularly in object detection and instance segmentation tasks.
- The paper is well-organized, with a clear structure and a comprehensive review of related work.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed analysis of the limitations of the proposed method, which could help in understanding its applicability and potential improvements.
- The novelty of the proposed method is questioned, with some reviewers suggesting that the approach is similar to existing methods like Side-Tuning.
- The paper does not adequately compare the proposed method with other state-of-the-art methods, particularly in terms of computational complexity and memory usage.
- The presentation of the paper could be improved, with some figures and explanations being unclear or confusing.
- The paper does not discuss the potential negative societal impacts of the proposed method, which is a significant oversight.
- The paper does not include a discussion on the limitations of the proposed method, which is crucial for understanding its applicability and potential improvements.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors clarify the differences between the proposed method and existing methods like Side-Tuning?
- How does the proposed method compare in terms of computational complexity and memory usage with other state-of-the-art methods?
- Can the authors provide a more detailed analysis of the limitations of the proposed method and suggest potential areas for future research?
- How does the proposed method perform in terms of inference speed, and can this be compared with other methods?
- Can the authors provide a more detailed explanation of the "Meta Token" and its role in the proposed method?
- Could the authors clarify the presentation of some figures and explanations to make the paper more accessible and understandable?
