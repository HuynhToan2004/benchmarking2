 **Summary:**
The paper introduces OmniControl, a novel approach to incorporating spatial control signals into text-conditioned human motion generation models. OmniControl utilizes both spatial and realism guidance to ensure generated motions closely conform to input control signals while maintaining realism. The method can handle control signals for any joint at any time, enhancing motion realism and control accuracy. The paper presents extensive experiments on the HumanML3D and KIT-ML datasets, demonstrating significant improvements over existing methods. However, concerns were raised regarding the generalization of the method to more complex or realistic scenarios and the potential for overfitting due to the use of ground truth trajectories as control signals.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel approach, OmniControl, which allows for the incorporation of spatial control signals over any joint at any given time, enhancing motion realism and control accuracy.
- The methodology is clearly explained, making the paper easy to follow, and the results are robust, demonstrating significant improvements over existing methods.
- The paper is well-written, with a clear presentation of the methodology and results, and includes comprehensive ablation studies to validate the effectiveness of each component.
- The use of both spatial and realism guidance is innovative, balancing control accuracy and motion realism, and the paper's experimental results are convincing.
- The paper is well-positioned in the literature review, highlighting the differences between OmniControl and previous methods.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper primarily evaluates the method on the HumanML3D and KIT-ML datasets, which are relatively simple and lack complex or realistic scenarios, raising concerns about the generalization of the method.
- The method's dependency on ground truth trajectories as control signals may lead to overfitting, which could limit its applicability in real-world scenarios where control signals are not available.
- The method's performance in handling complex scenarios, such as multiple joints moving simultaneously or complex actions, is not thoroughly tested.
- The paper does not provide a detailed analysis of the computational cost of the spatial guidance module compared to previous methods, which could affect the practical applicability of the method.
- The paper lacks a discussion on how the method handles scenarios where control signals are missing or incomplete, which could be crucial in real-world applications.

**Questions:**
- How does OmniControl perform when applied to more complex or realistic scenarios, such as multiple joints moving simultaneously or complex actions?
- Can the method handle scenarios where control signals are missing or incomplete?
- What is the computational cost of the spatial guidance module compared to previous methods, and how does this affect the practical applicability of the method?
- How does OmniControl compare to other methods that do not use control signals, such as those that use relative pose representations?
- Can the authors provide more details on the experimental setup, particularly the number of training samples and the specifics of the model's architecture?
- How does OmniControl handle scenarios where the control signals are not provided in the input text, such as in actions that do not involve specific joint movements?
