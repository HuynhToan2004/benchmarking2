 **Summary:**
The paper introduces IPR-NeRF, a framework designed to protect NeRF models from unauthorized use through both black-box and white-box methods. In the black-box setting, a diffusion-based watermark is embedded to ensure ownership verification, while in the white-box setting, a digital signature is embedded into the NeRF model's weights. The framework's effectiveness is demonstrated through experiments showing that the embedded watermarks and signatures can be robustly extracted, even under various attacks. However, concerns were raised about the novelty of the approach, as similar methods have been previously explored, and the computational efficiency of the proposed methods.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, with a clear motivation and structure that is easy to follow.
- The proposed method is technically sound, with a reasonable and well-explained approach.
- The paper introduces a novel method of embedding watermarks and digital signatures into NeRF models, which is an important step in the field of NeRF protection.
- The paper presents a comprehensive framework that addresses both black-box and white-box scenarios, providing a robust solution for NeRF protection.
- The experiments are convincing, demonstrating the effectiveness of the proposed method in maintaining rendering quality while being robust against various attacks.

**Weaknesses:**
- The novelty of the paper is limited as similar methods have been previously explored, particularly in the embedding of digital signatures and watermarks in NeRF models.
- The computational efficiency of the proposed methods is not thoroughly discussed, raising concerns about the practicality of the approach in real-world applications.
- The paper lacks a discussion on the potential negative societal impacts of the proposed methods, which could be significant in the context of intellectual property protection.
- The paper does not adequately address the issue of ambiguity in black-box settings, where multiple models could potentially embed the same watermark, leading to false positives in ownership verification.
- The method's effectiveness in real-world scenarios, particularly in terms of robustness against various types of attacks, is not convincingly demonstrated.
- The paper does not sufficiently compare its method with existing methods, particularly in terms of computational efficiency and practical applicability.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors provide more details on the computational efficiency of their proposed methods, including the time and computational resources required for embedding and extracting the watermarks and signatures?
- How does the proposed method handle the issue of ambiguity in black-box settings, particularly in scenarios where multiple models could potentially embed the same watermark?
- Can the authors provide more examples or case studies demonstrating the effectiveness of their method in real-world scenarios, particularly in terms of robustness against various types of attacks?
- How does the proposed method compare with existing methods in terms of computational efficiency and practical applicability?
- Could the authors discuss the potential negative societal impacts of their proposed methods, particularly in the context of intellectual property protection?
