 **Summary:**
The paper explores the use of kernel surrogate models for interpreting the behavior of deep neural networks (DNNs) trained on various datasets, focusing on the trace neural tangent kernel (trNTK) and its projection variants. It introduces a novel approach to approximating the empirical neural tangent kernel (eNTK) using random projections, which aims to enhance the computational efficiency and faithfulness of the kernel-based surrogate models to the original DNNs. The authors evaluate the performance of these models against different kernel functions, demonstrating that the trNTK and its projections can effectively approximate the eNTK and provide faithful explanations for the DNN's decisions. The paper also discusses the computational complexity and memory requirements of these models, providing a comprehensive evaluation across various datasets and models.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel approach to approximating the empirical neural tangent kernel (eNTK) using random projections, which is both innovative and effective in reducing computational complexity.
- The authors provide a comprehensive evaluation of the proposed methods across various datasets and models, demonstrating the effectiveness of the trNTK and its projections in approximating the eNTK and providing faithful explanations for DNN decisions.
- The paper is well-written, clear, and accessible, making it easy for readers to understand the methodologies and results.
- The use of the Kendall-tau rank correlation as a measure of faithfulness is a significant improvement over traditional metrics like test accuracy, which can be misleading.
- The paper presents a thorough discussion of the limitations and potential future directions of the proposed methods, which is commendable for its transparency and commitment to ongoing research.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper could benefit from a more detailed discussion on the limitations of the proposed methods, particularly in terms of their applicability to different types of neural networks and the scalability of the computations.
- There is a lack of discussion on the impact of the choice of random projection matrices on the faithfulness and performance of the surrogate models.
- The paper does not provide sufficient experimental validation for the claim that the trNTK and its projections are more faithful to the original DNNs than other kernel functions.
- The paper lacks a comparison with other kernel functions that are specifically designed for explainability, which could provide a more robust evaluation of the proposed methods.
- The paper's reliance on the Kendall-tau rank correlation as a measure of faithfulness raises concerns about the robustness of this metric, especially in scenarios where the correlation might not accurately reflect the faithfulness of the surrogate models.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors provide more details on how the choice of random projection matrices affects the faithfulness and performance of the surrogate models?
- How does the proposed method compare in terms of computational complexity and faithfulness to other kernel functions specifically designed for explainability?
- Can the authors clarify the definition and usage of the "poi. TAD" metric in Table 3, and explain why it is used as a measure of faithfulness?
- How does the proposed method perform when applied to other types of neural networks, such as recurrent neural networks (RNNs) or transformers?
- Can the authors provide a more detailed discussion on the scalability of the computations and the practical implications of using the trNTK and its projections in large-scale applications?
