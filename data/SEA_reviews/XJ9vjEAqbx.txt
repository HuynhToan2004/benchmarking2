 **Summary:**
The paper addresses the limitations of adversarial training (AT) by critiquing the use of surrogate losses and proposing a non-zero-sum bilevel formulation to enhance robustness against adversarial attacks. The authors argue that surrogate losses, such as cross-entropy, do not adequately reflect the true adversarial loss, leading to overestimation of robustness. They introduce a new algorithm, BETA-AT, which optimizes different objectives for the adversary and defender, thereby avoiding robust overfitting and providing a more accurate evaluation of adversarial robustness. The paper is supported by theoretical insights and experimental results on CIFAR-10, showing that BETA-AT matches the performance of state-of-the-art methods like AutoAttack.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel bilevel formulation for adversarial training, which provides a fresh perspective on the adversarial training problem.
- The proposed algorithm, BETA-AT, is simple and heuristic-free, making it potentially easier to implement compared to other methods.
- The paper is well-written, with clear explanations of the limitations of current adversarial training methods and the proposed solutions.
- The experimental results show that BETA-AT matches the performance of state-of-the-art methods like AutoAttack, indicating its effectiveness.
- The paper addresses the issue of robust overfitting, which is a significant challenge in adversarial training.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed discussion on the computational complexity of the proposed method, which could be a significant concern for practical implementation.
- The comparison with existing methods like Fast-AT is insufficient, which could have strengthened the argument for the superiority of the proposed method.
- The experimental results are limited to CIFAR-10, which might not generalize well to other datasets or more complex models.
- The paper does not provide a theoretical analysis of the proposed method, which could help in understanding its limitations and potential improvements.
- The paper could benefit from a more detailed discussion on the limitations and potential negative societal impacts of the proposed method.

**Questions:**
- Could you provide a more detailed comparison of the computational complexity of BETA-AT with other methods, especially in terms of training and testing times?
- How does BETA-AT compare to other methods like Fast-AT in terms of performance and computational efficiency?
- Is there a theoretical analysis available for the proposed method, and if not, what are the potential challenges in developing such an analysis?
- Could you discuss the potential negative societal impacts of the proposed method, especially in terms of its application in real-world scenarios?
