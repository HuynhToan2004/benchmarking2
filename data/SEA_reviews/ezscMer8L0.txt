 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a parameter-efficient fine-tuning (PEFT) approach, Conv-LoRA, designed to enhance the Segment-Anything Model (SAM) for specialized domains such as medical imagery and remote sensing. This method integrates ultra-lightweight convolutional parameters into LoRA to inject image-related inductive biases into the plain ViT encoder, thereby improving SAM's performance in real-world semantic segmentation tasks. The approach, which includes the integration of convolutional layers within the MoE structure, is evaluated across diverse benchmarks and is shown to outperform other PEFT techniques. The paper also discusses the challenges of adapting SAM to different domains and the limitations of its current performance compared to domain-specific models.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel PEFT technique, Conv-LoRA, which is a simple yet effective method for adapting SAM to real-world semantic segmentation tasks.
- The methodology is well-explained, and the paper is well-organized, making it easy to follow.
- The proposed Conv-LoRA outperforms other PEFT techniques in various downstream tasks, demonstrating its effectiveness.
- The paper is well-written, and the experiments are thorough, showing the effectiveness of the proposed method.
- The integration of convolutional layers within the MoE structure is a novel approach that enhances the performance of SAM.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper does not sufficiently discuss the limitations of the proposed method, such as the need for further fine-tuning of the mask decoder and prompt encoder, which could affect the performance on certain datasets.
- The paper does not provide a detailed comparison with other PEFT methods, such as LoRA and ViT-Adapter, which could have provided a more comprehensive evaluation of the proposed method.
- The paper lacks a detailed discussion on the limitations of the proposed method and how these limitations affect the performance of SAM in different domains.
- There is a lack of clarity in the methodology, particularly in the integration of convolutional layers within the MoE structure and the dynamic selection of experts.
- The paper does not discuss the computational overhead of the proposed method, which could be a significant concern for practical applications.
- The paper does not adequately address the limitations of SAM's pretraining and its impact on the performance of the proposed method.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors provide a more detailed discussion on the limitations of the proposed method and how these limitations affect the performance of SAM in different domains?
- How does the proposed method compare with other PEFT methods, particularly in terms of computational overhead and performance on different datasets?
- Can the authors provide more details on the integration of convolutional layers within the MoE structure and the dynamic selection of experts?
- How does the proposed method address the limitations of SAM's pretraining, and what steps are being taken to mitigate these limitations?
- Could the authors clarify the computational overhead of the proposed method and discuss potential strategies for reducing this overhead in practical applications?
