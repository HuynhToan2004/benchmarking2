 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel post-training quantization method for large language models (LLMs) using affine transformations, termed AffineQuant. This approach optimizes the equivalence transformation matrix to minimize quantization errors and ensures the invertibility of the transformation during optimization. The methodology involves a gradual mask optimization technique to manage the inversion process, and the results showcase significant improvements in perplexity and accuracy on various datasets, including WikiText2, PTB, C4, and LLaMA-2/7B. Despite the innovative approach, concerns were raised regarding the novelty, the clarity of the method's differentiation from existing methods, and the lack of comprehensive experiments, including comparisons with other baseline methods and different LLM models.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written and easy to follow, making complex concepts accessible.
- The proposed affine transformation method is innovative and shows significant improvements in performance, particularly in low-bit quantization scenarios.
- The optimization algorithm introduced is novel and effectively reduces computational costs.
- The paper achieves state-of-the-art performance in LLM quantization, particularly for small models or low-bit configurations.
- The paper provides comprehensive experimental results and demonstrates the effectiveness of the proposed method through extensive experiments.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a thorough comparison with other baseline methods, particularly in terms of computational efficiency and accuracy.
- The novelty of the proposed method is questionable, as it appears to be an extension of existing methods such as OmniQuant and AWQ.
- The paper does not include sufficient experiments to demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed method on larger or more complex models like LLaMA-65B.
- There is a lack of discussion on the limitations of the proposed method, which could help in understanding its applicability and potential drawbacks.
- The paper does not provide sufficient details on the implementation of the gradual mask optimization technique, which is crucial for reproducibility and understanding the method's effectiveness.

**Questions:**
- Could you provide more details on the implementation of the gradual mask optimization technique, including the specifics of how it is applied and its impact on the training process?
- How does the proposed method compare in terms of computational efficiency and accuracy with other baseline methods, particularly under different quantization configurations?
- Could you provide a more detailed comparison with other methods like OmniQuant and AWQ to highlight the unique contributions and advantages of your proposed method?
- What are the specific challenges in extending the method to larger or more complex models, and how do you plan to address these challenges in future research?
- Could you provide more insights into the limitations of the proposed method and discuss potential future directions for research in this area?
