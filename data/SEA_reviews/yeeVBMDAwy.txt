 **Summary:**
The paper introduces Variance-Enlarged Poisson Learning (VPL), a novel approach to address the challenges of degenerate solutions in graph-based semi-supervised learning with extremely sparse labeled data. VPL incorporates a variance-enlarged regularization term to induce a Poisson equation specifically for unlabeled data, which enhances the dispersion of labels from their average mean. The paper proposes two algorithms, V-Laplace and V-Poisson, to improve Laplace and Poisson learning, respectively. Additionally, it introduces Variance-Enlarged Graph Poisson Networks to facilitate improved label propagation. Theoretical analysis and extensive experiments are conducted to validate the effectiveness of VPL, showing its superiority over existing methods.

**Strengths:**
- The paper addresses a significant problem in graph-based semi-supervised learning with extremely sparse labeled data, introducing a novel Variance-Enlarged Poisson Learning (VPL) framework that enlarges the variance of labels to avoid degenerate solutions.
- The authors propose two algorithms, V-Laplace and V-Poisson, to enhance Laplace and Poisson learning, respectively, and introduce Variance-Enlarged Graph Poisson Networks to facilitate improved label propagation.
- The paper is well-written, with clear problem formulations and a comprehensive theoretical analysis in both discrete and variational cases.
- The proposed method shows significant improvement over existing methods in experiments, demonstrating its effectiveness and superior performance.
- The authors provide a detailed theoretical analysis, which helps in understanding the behavior of VPL and its advantages over existing methods.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed discussion on the limitations and potential negative societal impacts of the proposed method.
- The theoretical analysis, while detailed, is somewhat limited as it only considers a squared loss, which might not fully capture the complexity of real-world scenarios.
- The paper does not discuss the computational complexity of the proposed methods, which is crucial for practical implementation.
- There is a lack of clarity in the experimental setup, particularly in the description of the datasets used and the experimental conditions.
- The paper does not compare the proposed methods with other state-of-the-art methods, which could provide a more comprehensive evaluation of their effectiveness.
- The paper's reliance on assumptions, such as the uniformity of edge weights and the smoothness of the underlying distribution, might limit its applicability in more complex or varied data scenarios.

- How does the proposed method compare with other state-of-the-art methods, especially in terms of performance and computational efficiency?
- Could you elaborate on the experimental setup, particularly the datasets used and the experimental conditions?
- How does the proposed method perform when the graph is not fully connected?

- How does the proposed method handle the issue of degenerate solutions in more complex or varied data scenarios?
