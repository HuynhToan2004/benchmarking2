 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel method, Bellman Optimal Step-size Straightening (BOSS), aimed at optimizing the step sizes in flow-matching generative models to enhance sampling efficiency and image quality. BOSS utilizes a dynamic programming algorithm to determine the optimal step sizes and then refines the velocity network to match these steps, thereby improving the generation process. The method is evaluated against various baselines, showing significant improvements in efficiency and image quality. However, the paper is criticized for its limited novelty, insufficient comparison with existing methods, and potential issues in the presentation and clarity of its contributions.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, making it easy to follow and understand the proposed method.
- The proposed method, Bellman Optimal Step-size Straightening (BOSS), is novel and innovative, providing a new approach to optimize the step sizes in flow-matching generative models.
- The methodology is supported by extensive experimental evaluations across various image generation tasks, demonstrating its effectiveness in terms of both resource utilization and image quality.
- The paper addresses a significant problem in the field of generative models by focusing on reducing computational costs and improving image quality, which is crucial for practical applications.
- The paper is well-organized and provides clear explanations of the methodology and experimental results, enhancing its readability and accessibility.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a thorough comparison with existing methods, particularly those that also optimize sampling step sizes, which limits the evaluation of the proposed method's effectiveness.
- The novelty of the method is questioned, as it appears to be a direct application of existing techniques without significant innovation.
- The paper does not adequately discuss the computational complexity of the proposed method, which is crucial for understanding its practical applicability.
- There is a lack of clarity in the presentation of the method's contributions and the role of different components such as the BOSS method and the reflow procedure.
- The paper could benefit from a more comprehensive literature review and a clearer differentiation from related works, particularly those that address similar issues in generative models.
- The experimental setup and results are not sufficiently detailed or comprehensive, with some results being inconsistent or not convincingly superior to existing methods.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors provide a more detailed comparison with existing methods that optimize sampling step sizes, particularly in terms of computational efficiency and image quality?
- How does the proposed method compare to other recent methods like those mentioned in the literature review, especially in terms of computational complexity and image quality?
- Can the authors clarify the role and contribution of the BOSS method and the reflow procedure in the overall methodology?
- How does the proposed method handle the computational complexity, especially in terms of the additional computational resources required for the dynamic programming algorithm?
- Can the authors provide more detailed results, such as a plot of FID vs. number of NFEs, to better illustrate the method's performance across different settings?
- How does the proposed method compare to other methods that address similar issues in generative models, particularly those that use different sampling strategies or architectures?
