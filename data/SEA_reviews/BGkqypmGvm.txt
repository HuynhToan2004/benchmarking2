 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a new 2D State Space Model (SSM) layer designed to enhance spatial inductive bias in vision transformers (ViTs) and convolutional neural networks (ConvNeXt). This layer, which extends the 1D SSM layer to 2D, integrates positional information effectively, improving performance across various backbones and datasets. The authors demonstrate the layer's effectiveness by integrating it into different model architectures and showing significant improvements in accuracy, particularly in scenarios where positional encoding is not used. The paper also discusses the theoretical underpinnings and practical implications of the SSM layer, including its ability to capture spatial dependencies and its impact on training and inference times.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel 2D-SSM layer that effectively integrates positional information into vision transformers (ViTs), improving the model's performance without the need for additional positional encoding.
- The proposed layer is theoretically grounded, with a clear explanation of its design and functionality, including the ability to capture spatial dependencies and its impact on training and inference times.
- The experimental results demonstrate the layer's effectiveness across various backbones and datasets, showing significant improvements in accuracy, especially when positional encoding is not used.
- The paper is well-written, clear, and easy to follow, making it accessible to a broad audience.
- The authors have provided code and detailed experimental setups, which will be useful for reproducibility and further research.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a thorough comparison with other state-of-the-art methods, such as Hyena, which also addresses the issue of spatial inductive bias in ViTs.
- The computational cost and efficiency of the 2D-SSM layer are not adequately discussed, particularly in terms of training and inference times.
- The paper does not include a comprehensive ablation study to understand the contribution of each component of the proposed layer.
- The paper's experimental results are limited to specific datasets and model architectures, which may not generalize well to other scenarios or models.
- The paper could benefit from more detailed visualizations and explanations of the layer's functionality, especially in the context of different model architectures.
- The paper's claims about the layer's ability to capture unrestricted context and its impact on training times are not convincingly supported by the data provided.

**Questions:**
- How does the 2D-SSM layer compare with other state-of-the-art methods, such as Hyena, in terms of performance and efficiency?
- Can the authors provide more details on the computational cost and efficiency of the 2D-SSM layer, including training and inference times?
- How does the 2D-SSM layer perform when combined with other spatial inductive bias methods, such as spatial CNNs?
- Could the authors clarify the notation and definitions used in the paper, especially in Section 3.2, to improve understanding and reproducibility?
- How does the 2D-SSM layer handle different types of positional information, such as 2D positional encoding used in other models like DeiT?
- What are the implications of using the 2D-SSM layer in different model architectures, such as Swin Transformer, and how does it affect the model's performance and training times?
