 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel approach to regularized reinforcement learning (RL) by proposing a method to decouple the regularization from the action space. This method aims to maintain consistent regularization across different action spaces, thereby avoiding over-regularization. The authors demonstrate that standard regularizers like entropy can lead to over-regularization when the action space varies, which can negatively impact learning outcomes. They propose a simple heuristic to scale the regularization temperature based on the number of actions in a state, which is shown to improve learning outcomes in benchmark tasks. The paper also discusses the theoretical implications of this approach, including its generalizability and potential for broader application in RL.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, with clear and concise explanations of the proposed method and its implications.
- The authors effectively demonstrate the problem of regularization in RL and propose a novel solution that is both simple and effective.
- The theoretical analysis provided supports the proposed method, and the paper presents a clear and coherent argument for its efficacy.
- The paper is original in its approach to regularization in RL, offering a new perspective on how to address the issue of over-regularization in different action spaces.
- The methodology is supported by empirical evidence, showing that the proposed method can lead to improved learning outcomes in benchmark tasks.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a comprehensive discussion on the generalizability of the proposed method beyond the specific regularizer used, particularly in continuous action spaces.
- The paper could benefit from a more detailed discussion on the implications of the proposed method for other regularizers and in different action spaces.
- There is a lack of clarity in some sections of the paper, particularly in the explanation of certain equations and the methodology used.
- The paper does not adequately address the limitations of the proposed method, such as its applicability in different types of RL algorithms and its effectiveness in more complex or varied action spaces.
- The empirical results presented are somewhat limited, and the paper could benefit from a more extensive set of experiments to validate the proposed method more robustly.

**Questions:**
- How does the proposed method perform when applied to regularizers other than entropy, such as Tsallis entropy?
- Can the proposed method be extended to continuous action spaces, and if so, how would it be adapted to handle these spaces?
- What are the specific implications of the proposed method for other types of RL algorithms, such as off-policy algorithms or those that use different types of regularizers?
- Could the authors clarify the definitions and uses of certain terms and equations throughout the paper, particularly in sections where there is confusion or ambiguity?
- How does the proposed method compare to other existing methods for addressing the issue of over-regularization in RL, and what are the specific advantages or disadvantages of the proposed method compared to these alternatives?
