 **Summary:**
The paper explores the learned convolutional kernels in depth-wise separable convolutional neural networks (DS-CNNs), specifically focusing on the depthwise convolution layers. It employs unsupervised clustering with autoencoders to categorize these filters into distinct clusters, which are then analyzed to reveal patterns that resemble difference of Gaussian (DoG) functions and their derivatives. The study is based on various models trained on ImageNet and ImageNet-21k datasets, showing that these patterns persist across different layers and models. The paper also discusses the implications of these findings for the interpretability and biological plausibility of DS-CNNs.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written and easy to follow, with a clear and compelling narrative.
- The findings on the learned convolutional kernels in DS-CNNs are novel and significant, providing new insights into the interpretability and biological plausibility of these networks.
- The analysis is thorough, covering a wide range of models and including both qualitative and quantitative results.
- The paper bridges the gap between deep learning, neuroscience, and classical image processing, offering a unique perspective on the relationship between artificial and biological visual processing systems.
- The visualizations and analysis of the clusters are intriguing, with a clear connection to the DoG-derivatives models, which could inform the development of novel bio-inspired network designs and training methodologies.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a comprehensive discussion on the limitations of the study, particularly the generalizability of the findings to other datasets or architectures.
- The clustering methodology and its robustness are not sufficiently discussed, which could affect the reliability of the results.
- The paper does not provide a detailed explanation for the observed patterns in the learned kernels, which could benefit from further analysis or theoretical frameworks.
- The paper could benefit from a more detailed comparison with other methods, such as regularization techniques, to better understand the role of clustering in the observed patterns.
- The paper could be improved by providing more detailed results, including the number of filters in each model and the performance metrics for different models.
- The paper could benefit from a more thorough discussion on the potential societal impacts of the findings.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors provide a more detailed explanation for the observed patterns in the learned kernels, particularly the cross-shaped filters?
- How does the clustering methodology perform in different scenarios, and what are the implications for the reliability of the results?
- Could the authors clarify the role of clustering in the observed patterns, and discuss whether different clustering methods might yield different results?
- How do the findings generalize to other datasets or architectures, and what are the implications for the broader applicability of the insights?
- Could the authors provide more detailed results, including the number of filters in each model and the performance metrics for different models?
- How does the paper address potential biases in the clustering process, and what steps were taken to ensure the robustness of the results?
