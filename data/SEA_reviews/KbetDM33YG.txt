 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel method for evaluating the performance of Graph Neural Networks (GNNs) in real-world, unlabeled graphs with test-time distribution shifts. The method involves retraining a GNN on the test graph using pseudo labels and a parameter-free optimality criterion to estimate generalization errors. The proposed LeBed score, which measures the discrepancy between the retrained GNN's parameters and the original model, is validated through extensive experiments on real-world datasets. The paper also discusses the practical implications of the method, particularly in scenarios where test-time distribution shifts are significant. However, the paper is criticized for its lack of comparison with existing methods, and the method's effectiveness is questioned in scenarios with more complex or varied distribution shifts.

**Strengths:**
- The paper addresses a significant and relevant problem in the field of GNN evaluation, specifically focusing on the evaluation of GNNs in real-world scenarios with test-time distribution shifts.
- The proposed method is well-articulated and the paper is well-written, making it easy to follow and understand.
- The method is evaluated on real-world datasets, demonstrating its effectiveness in practical applications.
- The paper is original in its approach, introducing a novel method for evaluating GNNs under test-time distribution shifts.
- The proposed LeBed score is a straightforward and effective metric for evaluating the performance of GNNs in online settings.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a thorough comparison with existing methods, particularly those that address similar issues in GNN evaluation.
- The paper does not sufficiently address the limitations of the proposed method, such as its applicability in scenarios with more complex or varied distribution shifts.
- The experimental results are not sufficiently robust, with some results showing limited correlation with ground-truth test errors.
- The paper does not adequately discuss the computational cost of the proposed method, which could be a significant concern in practical applications.
- The paper lacks a comprehensive discussion on the societal impact of the proposed method, which is crucial for understanding the broader implications of the research.
