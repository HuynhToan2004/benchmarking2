 **Summary:**
The paper introduces FedOD, a novel federated learning (FL) system designed for outlier detection (OD) algorithms, which are typically not compatible with FL due to data dependency and computational costs. FedOD decomposes OD algorithms into basic operators and approximates them using neural networks, allowing for privacy-preserving learning without data exchange. This approach supports over 20 classical OD algorithms and can be extended to other fields like classification and clustering. The paper presents extensive experiments that demonstrate FedOD's accuracy and efficiency over state-of-the-art baselines, with improvements in performance and a reduction in errors. However, concerns are raised about the novelty of the approach, the lack of privacy guarantees, and the absence of a comparison with state-of-the-art OD algorithms and baselines.

**Strengths:**
- The paper addresses a significant and relevant problem in federated learning (FL) by focusing on outlier detection (OD), which is crucial in various fields such as security, finance, and healthcare.
- The proposed FedOD system is novel and innovative, providing a new approach to OD algorithms by decomposing them into basic operators and approximating them with neural networks, which is compatible with FL.
- The paper is well-written, clearly presenting the challenges and proposed solutions, and is supported by extensive experiments that demonstrate the effectiveness of the FedOD system in terms of accuracy and efficiency.
- The approach is adaptable and can be extended to other machine learning tasks, making it a versatile tool for various applications.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed comparison with state-of-the-art OD algorithms and baselines, which is crucial for understanding the relative performance of the proposed system.
- There is a lack of privacy guarantees in the FedOD system, which is a significant concern given the sensitivity of data in OD applications.
- The paper does not sufficiently address the scalability of the FedOD system, particularly in large-scale applications.
- The novelty of the approach is questionable, as similar methods have been previously explored in the literature, and the paper does not sufficiently differentiate its approach from existing methods.
- The paper lacks a comprehensive discussion on the limitations and potential negative societal impacts of the proposed system.

**Questions:**
- Could you provide a more detailed comparison of the FedOD system with state-of-the-art OD algorithms and baselines to better understand its relative performance?
- How does the FedOD system ensure privacy and data security, especially in applications where data sensitivity is high?
- Can you elaborate on the scalability of the FedOD system, particularly in large-scale applications?
- How does the FedOD system perform in terms of computational efficiency and resource utilization, especially in edge devices with limited computational power?
- Could you clarify the novelty of the FedOD system and how it differentiates from existing methods in the literature?
- What are the potential negative societal impacts of the FedOD system, and how are these addressed or mitigated?
