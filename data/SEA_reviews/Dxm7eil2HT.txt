 **Summary:**
The paper introduces RoCA, a novel method for determining whether a dataset exhibits a causal or anticausal relationship. This method leverages noise injection to assess the correlation between noise levels and mismatch between pseudo labels and observed labels, which is used to infer the causal or anticausal nature of the data. The authors demonstrate the effectiveness of RoCA through experiments on both synthetic and real-world datasets, showing that it outperforms existing methods. The method is particularly useful in scenarios where label errors are present, which is a common issue in real-world datasets. Despite its innovative approach, the paper has been critiqued for its lack of clarity in explaining certain assumptions and methodologies, which could hinder its accessibility to a broader audience.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, clear, and easy to follow, with a clear motivation and a novel approach to inferring the causal or anticausal nature of a dataset.
- The methodology, including the use of noise injection and the correlation analysis between noise levels and mismatch, is both innovative and effective, as demonstrated by empirical results on both synthetic and real-world datasets.
- The paper provides a comprehensive review of existing methods and positions the proposed method well within the existing literature.
- The authors have conducted extensive experiments and provided theoretical proofs to support their claims, enhancing the credibility of their findings.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed discussion on related work, particularly in the area of causal discovery, which could have strengthened the context and impact of the research.
- There is a lack of clarity in the assumptions made, such as the assumption of instance-dependent noise rates and the absence of latent confounders, which are critical for the validity of the results.
- The methodology section could benefit from more detailed explanations, especially regarding the noise injection process and the role of pseudo labels in the analysis.
- The paper's scope is somewhat limited, focusing primarily on binary classification settings, which may not generalize well to more complex or varied datasets.
- The paper does not adequately address the issue of scalability, which is crucial for practical applications, particularly in high-dimensional datasets.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors clarify the assumptions made in the paper, particularly regarding the instance-dependent noise rates and the absence of latent confounders?
- How does the method perform when the data is not binary classified, and what modifications are necessary for such cases?
- Could the authors provide more details on the noise injection process, including the specifics of how the noise rates are determined and how they affect the results?
- How does the method handle different types of label errors, such as class-dependent errors?
- Could the authors elaborate on the scalability of their method, especially in terms of handling high-dimensional datasets and large-scale real-world datasets?
- How does the method perform when compared to other existing methods, particularly in terms of computational efficiency and accuracy?
