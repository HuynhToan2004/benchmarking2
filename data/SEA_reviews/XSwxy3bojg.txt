 **Summary:**
The paper introduces Molecular Conformer Fields (MCF), a novel method for generating molecular conformers using a diffusion model. The approach employs a denoising diffusion process to learn a distribution over conformer fields, which are parameterized by the eigenvectors of the graph Laplacian. The methodology, which involves learning a distribution over these fields, allows for the generation of conformers without making assumptions about torsional angles or other domain-specific variables. The paper demonstrates the effectiveness of MCF through experiments on standard benchmarks, showing state-of-the-art performance in molecular conformer generation. Despite its strengths, the paper has been critiqued for its lack of detailed methodological explanations, particularly in the choice of the PerceiverIO architecture and the absence of an ablation study.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel approach to molecular conformer generation using a diffusion model, which is a significant advancement in the field.
- The methodology is well-explained, making the paper accessible to a broad audience, and the experiments are well-designed, showing state-of-the-art performance on standard benchmarks.
- The paper provides an extensive ablation study, which is crucial for understanding the factors that affect the performance of the proposed MCF in molecular conformer generation.
- The authors have demonstrated that their approach can achieve state-of-the-art performance on standard benchmarks, showing the effectiveness of the proposed method.
- The paper is well-written and easy to follow, which is a significant advantage in making the content accessible to a diverse audience.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed explanation of the choice of the PerceiverIO architecture, particularly why it was chosen over other potential architectures like Graph Transformers.
- There is a lack of an ablation study, which is crucial for understanding the impact of different components of the model on the performance.
- The paper does not adequately discuss the limitations of the proposed method, which is essential for a complete understanding of the model's capabilities and potential drawbacks.
- The paper could benefit from more detailed explanations of the methodology, particularly in sections where the methodology is introduced, to enhance clarity and understanding.
- The paper does not sufficiently compare its method with other state-of-the-art models, which is necessary to establish the superiority or equivalence of the proposed method.
- The paper lacks a discussion on the scalability of the proposed method, which is crucial for understanding its applicability to larger and more complex datasets.

**Questions:**

- Could the authors conduct an ablation study to understand the impact of different components of the model on the performance?
- How does the proposed method compare with other state-of-the-art models in terms of performance and scalability?
- What are the potential limitations of the proposed method, and how do these limitations affect the model's performance and applicability?
- Could the authors discuss the scalability of the proposed method, particularly in terms of its ability to handle larger and more complex datasets?
- How does the proposed method handle the issue of generating diverse conformers, especially in cases where the model might tend to generate similar conformers?
