 **Summary:**
The paper investigates the phenomenon of "grokking" in linear neural networks using a teacher-student setup, focusing on how this behavior manifests in simple linear models. It explores the dynamics of training and generalization losses, providing analytical predictions on how these losses evolve over time. The authors demonstrate that grokking, defined as a sudden improvement in generalization performance after a prolonged phase of high training loss, can be explained by the differences in loss metrics used for training and generalization. The paper also discusses the effects of various factors such as network architecture, regularization, and initialization on grokking dynamics. Despite its focus on a simplified setup, the paper provides valuable insights into the theoretical underpinnings of grokking and offers a novel perspective on the dynamics of generalization in neural networks.
