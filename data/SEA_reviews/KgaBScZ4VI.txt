 **Summary:**
The paper explores the application of language model cascades in generative tasks, specifically focusing on the use of token-level uncertainty metrics to improve the deferral rule in cascading language models. The authors propose a novel approach using quantile measures to address the length bias problem in sequence uncertainty, which is a significant challenge in cascading models. They demonstrate the effectiveness of this approach through experiments on various NLP tasks, showing that their method can outperform existing methods. The paper also introduces a post-hoc deferral rule trained on quantile features, which is shown to be effective in various scenarios. Despite its strengths, the paper is criticized for its limited scope in terms of model families and datasets, and its incremental nature compared to existing works.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, clear, and easy to follow, with a clear motivation and a well-articulated approach.
- The study on the use of language model cascades is timely and relevant, addressing the need for more efficient and effective language models.
- The paper introduces a novel approach using quantile measures to address the length bias problem in sequence uncertainty, which is a significant contribution to the field.
- The experiments conducted are thorough and demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed approach, with results showing that the proposed method outperforms existing methods in most cases.
- The paper provides a comprehensive evaluation across various NLP tasks, including classification, question answering, and translation, which helps in validating the proposed approach.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper is somewhat incremental compared to existing works, such as those by Jitkrittum et al. (2023) and Kag et al. (2023), which have already addressed similar issues in the context of classification tasks.
- The scope of the study is limited in terms of model families, focusing primarily on the FLAN-T5 model, which may not generalize well to other models like Llama-2 or GPT-3.5.
- The paper does not include a comprehensive analysis of the computational cost of the proposed method, which is a significant concern given the practical implications of cascading models.
- The paper does not sufficiently address the issue of over-reliance on token-level uncertainty metrics, which could lead to a lack of consideration of contextual information in the model's outputs.
- The paper could benefit from a more detailed comparison with other related works, particularly those that have explored similar ideas in different contexts.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors clarify how the proposed method could be adapted to other model families, such as Llama-2 or GPT-3.5?
- How does the proposed method perform when applied to different types of tasks, such as text summarization or code generation?
- Could the authors provide a more detailed analysis of the computational cost of the proposed method, including both the inference cost and the cost of training the post-hoc deferral rule?
- How does the proposed method compare to other methods that use different types of uncertainty metrics, such as those based on the variance of token probabilities?
- Could the authors elaborate on the choice of the quantile value used in the experiments and how it was determined?
- How does the proposed method address the issue of over-reliance on token-level uncertainty metrics, and what steps were taken to ensure that contextual information was not overlooked?
