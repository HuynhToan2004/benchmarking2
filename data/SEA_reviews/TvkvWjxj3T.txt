 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel method for text-guided image editing called "negative-prompt inversion," which aims to achieve real-image inversion in diffusion models without the need for optimization. This method is claimed to be faster than existing techniques, such as null-text inversion, and is demonstrated to produce high-quality reconstruction results. The approach is based on replacing the null-text embedding with the prompt embedding, thereby simplifying the inversion process. The paper also discusses the potential for improving reconstruction quality by increasing the number of sampling steps, although this comes at the cost of increased computational time. The method's effectiveness is validated through experiments on a dataset of 100 images from the COCO dataset, showing a significant speedup and comparable quality to null-text inversion.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written and easy to follow, with clear explanations of the proposed method and its implementation.
- The method achieves a remarkable speedup of over 30 times compared to null-text inversion, which is significant for practical applications.
- The proposed method is simple yet effective, and it does not require optimization, which is beneficial for real-time applications.
- The paper provides theoretical justification and empirical validation for the proposed method, which adds credibility to the findings.
- The method can be easily combined with existing image editing methods, such as prompt-to-prompt, to achieve fast real image editing.
- The paper includes a thorough evaluation of the proposed method, including a detailed comparison with existing methods and an analysis of the number of sampling steps.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a comprehensive evaluation of the proposed method, particularly in terms of the quality of the generated images. It would be beneficial to include more qualitative results and a broader set of metrics to assess the image quality.
- The method's effectiveness in terms of editing quality is not convincingly demonstrated. It would be helpful to include comparisons with other methods like Imagic to better understand the advantages of the proposed method.
- The paper does not discuss the limitations of the proposed method, such as the potential for failure in reconstructing certain types of images, which could affect the practical applicability of the method.
- The paper could benefit from a more detailed discussion on the potential negative societal impacts of the method, especially in terms of its application to real-time image editing.
- The paper lacks a broader set of examples and visualizations to demonstrate the method's effectiveness across different types of images and editing scenarios.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors provide more detailed results on the quality of the generated images, including metrics like FID and visual examples?
- How does the proposed method compare to other methods in terms of editing quality, especially in scenarios where the editing is not trivial?
- Could the authors discuss the potential negative societal impacts of the method, particularly in terms of its application to real-time image editing?
- What are the potential failure cases of the proposed method, and how do these affect the practical applicability of the method?
- Can the authors provide more visualizations and examples to demonstrate the method's effectiveness across different types of images and editing scenarios?
- How does the proposed method perform when combined with different image editing methods, such as those mentioned in the related work section?
