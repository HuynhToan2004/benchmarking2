 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel meta-optimization framework for discovering synthetic environments that facilitate the training of reinforcement learning (RL) agents. This framework utilizes contextual bandits to train RL agents, thereby reducing the time and computational resources needed for training. The synthetic environments are designed to generalize well across different hyperparameters and algorithms, and their interpretability is demonstrated through the extraction of optimal policies from synthetic reward functions. The paper also explores the use of these synthetic environments in downstream meta-learning setups and shows that they can be used for evolutionary optimization of agent policies. Despite its innovative approach, the paper faces criticism for its lack of novelty in some aspects, insufficient comparison with existing methods, and potential limitations in generalization to more complex environments.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel approach to discovering synthetic environments using meta-optimization, which is a significant advancement in the field of reinforcement learning (RL).
- The methodology is well-explained, with clear writing that makes the content accessible and understandable.
- The proposed framework shows promise in reducing the time and computational resources needed for RL training, which is crucial for practical applications.
- The use of contextual bandits for training RL agents is a novel and interesting approach, which could lead to more efficient and effective RL training.
- The paper demonstrates the generalization capabilities of the synthetic environments across different hyperparameters and algorithms, indicating their broad applicability.
- The interpretability of the synthetic environments is a valuable feature, allowing for insights into the tasks encoded by the evaluation environment and the importance of individual state dimensions.
- The paper is well-organized, with a clear presentation of the methodology and results, which aids in understanding the contributions and implications of the research.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a comprehensive comparison with existing methods, particularly in terms of computational efficiency and generalization capabilities.
- The novelty of the method is questioned, as similar approaches have been previously explored, such as learning a reward function from a neural network and using it for RL training.
- The paper does not provide sufficient details on the hyperparameters used in the meta-evolution process, which could affect the reproducibility and comparability of the results.
- The generalization capabilities of the synthetic environments are not convincingly demonstrated, especially in more complex environments beyond the simple ones used in the study.
- The paper lacks a discussion on the limitations of the proposed method, such as its applicability to more complex environments and the potential for overfitting.
- There is a noticeable absence of empirical results comparing the synthetic environments with real environments, which is crucial for validating the claims of generalization and performance.
- The paper does not adequately address the ethical implications of the research, which is a significant omission in a field that is increasingly focusing on the societal impact of AI and machine learning.

**Questions:**
- How do the synthetic environments perform when trained on more complex environments, such as those found in the Atari or MuJoCo domains?
- Can the authors provide a detailed comparison of the computational efficiency and generalization capabilities of their method with existing approaches?
- How do the authors ensure the reproducibility of their results, especially given the potential for variability in the meta-evolution process?
- Could the authors provide more details on the hyperparameters used in the meta-evolution process and their impact on the results?
- How do the synthetic environments handle the complexity of real-world environments, especially those with high-dimensional observations or non-Markovian dynamics?
- What are the specific ethical considerations of the research, and how do the authors address these in their methodology and findings?
