 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel method, LLM-Codebook, for compressing large language models (LLMs) by mapping weights into codebooks based on Hessian information. This method uses a Hessian-aware K-means algorithm to cluster weights, aiming to preserve critical parameters that influence predictions. The technique is validated through experiments on LLaMA-7B and Vicuna-7B models, showing that it maintains performance even under extreme compression ratios, outperforming existing methods like GPTQ, QLoRA, and LLM-Pruner. The method is designed to be hardware-friendly, reducing memory requirements to a quarter of the original size, and is claimed to be the first to employ codebooks for LLM compression.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel approach, LLM-Codebook, for extreme compression of LLMs, which is the first to employ codebooks for LLM compression.
- The method effectively preserves the generation and multi-task solving abilities of LLMs, surpassing existing methods like GPTQ, QLoRA, LLM-Pruner, and SparseGPT.
- The proposed method is hardware-friendly, reducing memory requirements to a quarter of the original size, and is designed to be automated, making it efficient and practical for deployment.
- The paper is well-written, clearly explaining the methodology and experimental results, and includes a detailed analysis of the performance and compression ratio.
- The method's ability to maintain 99% of the baseline performance even at a compression ratio of 81% is impressive, demonstrating its effectiveness in extreme compression scenarios.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks detailed information on the hardware requirements for the experiments and does not include a comparison of inference speed or hardware compatibility with existing methods.
- The paper does not discuss the potential negative societal impacts of the proposed method, which could be a significant concern for the deployment of LLMs.
- The method's sensitivity to the choice of parameters such as the number of clusters and the codebook size is not thoroughly addressed, raising concerns about its robustness and generalizability.
- The paper's claims of being the first to employ codebooks for LLM compression are not well-supported, as similar methods have been previously proposed.
- The paper does not include a comparison of the computational cost of the proposed method with existing methods, which is crucial for evaluating its practicality and efficiency.
- The paper lacks a discussion on the limitations of the proposed method, such as its applicability to different types of LLMs and its compatibility with various hardware platforms.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors provide more details on the hardware requirements for the experiments, including the specific hardware used and the computational resources consumed?
- How does the proposed method compare in terms of inference speed and hardware compatibility with existing methods like GPTQ, QLoRA, and LLM-Pruner?
- Could the authors elaborate on the potential negative societal impacts of the proposed method and discuss how these impacts could be mitigated?
- How sensitive is the proposed method to the choice of parameters such as the number of clusters and the codebook size? What strategies can be employed to ensure robustness and generalizability?
- Could the authors provide a more detailed comparison of the computational cost of the proposed method with existing methods, including both time and resource requirements?
- How does the proposed method perform on other types of LLMs, such as those with different parameter sizes or architectures?
- Could the authors discuss the limitations of the proposed method and suggest potential areas for future research to address these limitations?
