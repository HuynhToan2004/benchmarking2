 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel self-supervised contrastive pretraining method for learning a single encoding from multiple unlabeled and diverse time series datasets. This method, named XD-MixUp, leverages a combination of temporal contrastive loss and soft interpolation contextual contrastive loss to achieve robust time series representation learning. The approach is evaluated across 75 datasets, demonstrating its effectiveness in improving classification performance on target datasets with limited labeled instances. The methodology is based on existing techniques such as MixUp and TS-TCC, but the paper claims significant improvements over these methods. However, concerns are raised about the novelty of the approach and the clarity of the experimental setup, particularly in the ablation studies and the choice of datasets for evaluation.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, easy to follow, and the motivation is clear.
- The proposed method is simple and effective, particularly in improving the performance of the TS-TCC method.
- The empirical evaluation is thorough, and the experiments are well-designed to show the effectiveness of the proposed method.
- The method is innovative, leveraging ideas from contrastive learning and data augmentation to enhance the performance of time series models.
- The paper is well-structured, with a clear presentation of the methodology and results.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a comprehensive literature review, particularly in the area of data augmentation and contrastive learning, which are crucial for the proposed method.
- The novelty of the method is questionable as it heavily relies on existing techniques such as MixUp and TS-TCC, with only minor modifications.
- The paper could benefit from a more detailed discussion on the computational complexity of the proposed method compared to existing methods.
- The experimental setup and the choice of datasets are not clearly justified, particularly in the ablation studies where the results are not convincingly superior to existing methods.
- There is a lack of clarity in the presentation of the results, especially in tables where the rankings are not clearly explained.
- The paper could benefit from a more detailed discussion on the limitations of the proposed method and the potential negative societal impacts.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors provide more detailed comparisons of the proposed method with existing methods in terms of computational complexity and training time?
- How does the proposed method perform when applied to datasets with longer time series, such as those found in the UCR dataset?
- Could the authors clarify the choice of datasets for the ablation studies and explain why the results do not show a clear superiority of the proposed method over existing methods?
- How does the proposed method handle the potential negative societal impacts, particularly in healthcare applications where the accuracy of the model could have significant consequences?
- Can the authors provide more details on the choice of hyperparameters, particularly in the MixUp method, and how these choices affect the performance of the model?
