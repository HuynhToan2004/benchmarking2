 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel self-supervised learning framework for decoding natural images from EEG signals, aiming to address the challenges of using EEG signals for object recognition. The approach involves the use of a contrastive learning method to align image and EEG modalities, which are processed by separate encoders. The methodology is supported by extensive experiments, including a zero-shot task on a large dataset, demonstrating the feasibility of EEG-based image decoding. The paper also discusses the biological plausibility of the results and provides insights into the temporal, spatial, spectral, and semantic aspects of EEG signals.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, making it easy to follow and understand.
- The proposed method is innovative, combining existing techniques such as contrastive learning and self-attention in a novel way.
- The experiments are comprehensive, including a zero-shot task on a large dataset and ablation studies, which demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed method.
- The paper provides valuable insights into the biological plausibility of EEG-based image decoding, contributing to the understanding of neural decoding and brain-computer interfaces.
- The use of self-supervised learning for EEG-based object recognition is highlighted as a significant contribution, addressing the challenge of using EEG signals for object recognition.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed discussion on the choice of using a contrastive learning approach for EEG-based image decoding, which could benefit from a more thorough exploration of its advantages and limitations.
- The novelty of the proposed method is questioned as it appears to be a simple combination of existing techniques without significant innovation.
- The paper does not sufficiently discuss the limitations of the proposed method, such as the generalization of the results to different datasets or the scalability of the approach to more complex or diverse image datasets.
- There is a lack of comparison with state-of-the-art methods, which could provide a better understanding of the performance of the proposed method against current research.
- The paper could benefit from a more detailed discussion on the biological plausibility of the results, particularly in relation to the temporal, spatial, spectral, and semantic aspects of EEG signals.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors provide a more detailed discussion on the choice of using a contrastive learning approach for EEG-based image decoding, including its advantages and limitations?
- How does the proposed method compare to other state-of-the-art methods in terms of performance and computational efficiency?
- Could the authors elaborate on the generalization of the results to different datasets or the scalability of the approach to more complex or diverse image datasets?
- How do the authors address the issue of low signal-to-noise ratio in EEG signals, and what strategies are employed to mitigate this issue in the proposed method?
- Can the authors provide a more detailed discussion on the biological plausibility of the results, particularly in relation to the temporal, spatial, spectral, and semantic aspects of EEG signals?
