 **Summary:**
The paper explores the security of large language models (LLMs) against adversarial attacks, focusing on the effectiveness of various defense mechanisms such as perplexity-based detection, paraphrasing, and adversarial training. It evaluates these defenses against the GCG attack and discusses their practicality and feasibility in real-world scenarios. The paper highlights the challenges of applying traditional adversarial defenses from computer vision to LLMs, particularly due to the discrete nature of text and the high computational costs of adversarial training. Despite the comprehensive evaluation and the paper's clarity, the reviewers noted several issues including the limited scope of the attacks and defenses evaluated, the lack of comprehensive evaluation on open-source models, and the need for more rigorous testing against a broader range of adversarial attacks.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, clear, and easy to follow, making it accessible to a broad audience.
- It provides a comprehensive evaluation of existing defenses against adversarial attacks on LLMs, which is crucial for understanding the current state of the field.
- The paper is well-organized, with a clear introduction and background sections that effectively set the stage for the research.
- The authors discuss the limitations of their work and provide insights into the challenges of applying adversarial defenses from computer vision to LLMs, which is an important area of research.
- The paper includes a thorough discussion of the threat model, which is critical for understanding the security of LLMs against adversarial attacks.
- The paper provides a detailed analysis of the effectiveness of various defense mechanisms such as perplexity-based detection, paraphrasing, and adversarial training, which are essential for the robustness of LLMs.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper's focus on a single attack (GCG) limits the generalizability of the findings to other types of adversarial attacks.
- The evaluation of defenses is limited to a single open-source model, which may not reflect the performance of these defenses across different models.
- The paper does not sufficiently address the potential for adversaries to adapt their attacks to bypass the defenses, which is a significant concern in the context of adversarial attacks.
- The paper lacks a comprehensive evaluation of the impact of adversarial attacks on the performance of LLMs, which is crucial for understanding the effectiveness of the defenses.
- There is a lack of discussion on the potential negative societal impacts of the research, which is an important consideration for ethical research.
- The paper could benefit from a more detailed discussion on the potential limitations of the defenses and how they might be bypassed by more sophisticated attacks.

**Questions:**
- How do the findings of this paper apply to other types of adversarial attacks beyond the GCG attack?
- Could the authors provide a more detailed evaluation of the impact of adversarial attacks on the performance of LLMs, particularly in terms of the quality of the outputs generated by the models?
- How do the defenses perform against other types of adversarial attacks, such as those that do not rely on a suffix-based approach?
- Could the authors discuss the potential for adversaries to adapt their attacks to bypass the defenses, and how this might impact the effectiveness of the defenses over time?
- How do the defenses perform against different types of adversarial attacks, such as those that use different optimization techniques or different types of perturbations?
- Could the authors provide more detailed results on the performance of the defenses against a broader range of adversarial attacks, including those that are not suffix-based?
