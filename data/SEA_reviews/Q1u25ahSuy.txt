 **Summary:**
The paper introduces SpQR, a novel compression method for large language models (LLMs) that employs sparse quantization to achieve near-lossless compression. The technique identifies and isolates outlier weights, which are then stored in higher precision while compressing other weights to 3-4 bits. This approach allows for efficient GPU inference and significant memory compression, achieving a 4x compression ratio with minimal accuracy loss. The methodology is supported by extensive experiments on various LLMs, demonstrating its effectiveness and efficiency. However, the paper's claims of novelty are questioned due to similarities with existing methods like GPTQ and SparseGPT.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, making it easy to follow, and the methodology is well-explained with clear figures and tables.
- The proposed method achieves significant compression ratios (4x) while maintaining performance, which is crucial for deploying LLMs on edge devices.
- The paper is well-motivated, with a clear introduction of the problem and a novel approach to outlier weight identification and compression.
- The experiments are comprehensive, covering various LLMs and different model sizes, and the results are convincing, demonstrating the effectiveness of the proposed method.
- The paper includes a thorough analysis of parameter sensitivity and the impact of different weight groups on quantization error, which is crucial for understanding the behavior of LLMs under compression.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed discussion on the limitations and potential negative societal impacts of the proposed method, which is essential for a complete evaluation.
- There are concerns about the novelty of the method, as it seems to build on existing techniques like GPTQ and SparseGPT, and the paper does not sufficiently differentiate its contributions from these prior works.
- The paper does not discuss the generative quality of the compressed models, which is crucial for assessing the practical applicability of the proposed method.
- There are issues with the presentation and clarity of the paper, particularly in the algorithm descriptions and the explanation of certain parameters and methods.
- The paper does not adequately address the scalability of the method to larger models or different model architectures, which could limit its applicability.
- The paper could benefit from a more comprehensive discussion on the limitations and potential negative societal impacts of the proposed method.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors provide more details on the outlier detection algorithm and its impact on the overall performance of the model?
- How does the method perform on other model architectures, such as those based on attention mechanisms?
- Can the authors clarify the role and impact of different parameters in the algorithm, such as the number of outliers and the group sizes?
- How does the method perform on larger models, and what are the potential limitations or challenges in scaling up the method?
- Could the authors discuss the potential negative societal impacts of the proposed method and how these might be mitigated or addressed?
- How does the method compare to other state-of-the-art quantization methods in terms of performance and efficiency?
- Can the authors provide more details on the hardware and software configurations used in their experiments, and how these might affect the results?
