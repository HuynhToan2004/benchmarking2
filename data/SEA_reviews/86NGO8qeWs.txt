 **Summary:**
The paper introduces the CompA benchmark, designed to evaluate compositional reasoning in audio-language models (ALMs). This benchmark is divided into CompA-order and CompA-attribute, each focusing on different aspects of compositional reasoning in audio. The authors propose a novel training method, CompA-CLAP, which utilizes a novel modular contrastive loss to enhance the model's ability to learn fine-grained compositional understanding. The paper also introduces a new dataset, AudioSet-CompA, which is used to generate hard negatives for the training process. The proposed methodology is evaluated on the CompA benchmark, showing improved performance over baseline models. However, the paper's reliance on synthetic data for training and the limited real-world applicability of the benchmark are noted as significant drawbacks.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel benchmark, CompA, specifically designed to evaluate compositional reasoning in audio-language models (ALMs), which is a significant contribution to the field.
- The authors propose a novel learning method, CompA-CLAP, which is a robust solution for improving compositional reasoning in ALMs, demonstrating significant improvements over baseline models.
- The paper is well-organized, clearly written, and provides a comprehensive evaluation of the proposed method, including detailed experimental results and a discussion on the limitations of the approach.
- The introduction of the AudioSet-CompA dataset and the use of GPT-4 to generate captions for the dataset are innovative approaches that enhance the training process.
- The paper addresses a critical issue in the field of audio-language models, specifically the lack of compositional reasoning, and proposes a novel solution to address this gap.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper's reliance on synthetic data for training raises concerns about the generalizability of the results to real-world scenarios.
- The benchmark, CompA, is limited in scope and does not adequately represent real-world audio scenarios, which could limit its applicability and relevance.
- The paper does not sufficiently address the limitations of the proposed approach, particularly the potential for overfitting and the generalizability of the results.
- The use of synthetic data and the lack of real-world applicability in the benchmark could limit the practical utility of the proposed method.
- The paper lacks a comprehensive discussion on the limitations of the proposed approach and does not adequately address the potential for overfitting or the generalizability of the results.
- There are concerns about the novelty of the proposed method, as similar approaches have been used in previous works, and the paper does not sufficiently differentiate its contributions from existing methods.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors clarify how the proposed method, CompA-CLAP, would perform on real-world datasets, especially in scenarios where the data is not synthetic?
- How does the proposed method handle the generalizability of the results to real-world scenarios, and what measures have been taken to address potential overfitting?
- Could the authors provide more details on the template-based synthetic creation of audio-caption pairs and how it is aligned with the audio in the batch?
- Is there a possibility to include a broader range of audio scenarios in the benchmark to better represent real-world audio scenarios?
- Can the authors elaborate on the specific advantages of the proposed method over existing approaches, particularly in terms of novelty and practical utility?
