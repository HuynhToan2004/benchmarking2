 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel deep learning-based method for detecting identity swaps in images, leveraging a VGG19 architecture enhanced by Layer-Integrated Channel Attention (LICA) and Scaled Spatial Attention (SSA) mechanisms. The methodology aims to enhance the detection of manipulated faces by focusing on the importance of channels and spatial locations. The proposed approach is evaluated on the DFFD dataset and shows promising results in terms of accuracy and AUC. However, the paper is criticized for its limited novelty, lack of comprehensive experimental validation, and unclear presentation, particularly in the description of the attention mechanisms and the experimental setup.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces two novel attention mechanisms, LICA and SSA, which are effective in enhancing the performance of the VGG19 network.
- The methodology is well-structured, with clear explanations of the proposed attention mechanisms and their integration into the VGG19 architecture.
- The paper is well-written, making it easy to follow, and provides a clear explanation of the proposed methodology.
- The paper is the first to use explainable AI techniques such as LIME to analyze the effectiveness of the proposed attention mechanisms, enhancing the transparency and trustworthiness of the model's decision-making process.
- The proposed method achieves state-of-the-art performance on the DFFD dataset, demonstrating its effectiveness in detecting identity swaps in images.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks comprehensive experimental validation, particularly on different datasets and under various conditions, which could have strengthened the claims of the proposed method's effectiveness.
- The novelty of the proposed attention mechanisms is questioned, as they appear to be minor modifications of existing attention mechanisms.
- The paper does not adequately address the generalizability of the proposed method to other types of fake faces, limiting its applicability in broader contexts.
- The presentation of the paper is not clear, particularly in the description of the attention mechanisms and the experimental setup, which could hinder understanding and reproducibility of the results.
- The paper does not provide sufficient details on the training and validation of the model, which is crucial for reproducibility and understanding the robustness of the results.
- The paper's focus on identity swap detection is narrow, and it does not explore broader applications or the detection of other types of manipulated faces, which could limit its impact and relevance in the field.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors clarify the novelty of the proposed attention mechanisms and how they differ from existing methods?
- How does the proposed method perform on other types of fake faces, such as those generated by GANs or other methods?
- Can the authors provide more detailed explanations of the attention mechanisms and the experimental setup to improve clarity and reproducibility?
- How does the proposed method handle variations in image quality and resolution, and what are the implications for its performance in real-world scenarios?
- Could the authors provide more comprehensive experimental results, including comparisons with other state-of-the-art methods and different datasets?
- How does the proposed method compare to other explainable AI techniques, and what are the advantages of using LIME specifically?
- Can the authors discuss the limitations of their method and potential directions for future research to address these limitations?
