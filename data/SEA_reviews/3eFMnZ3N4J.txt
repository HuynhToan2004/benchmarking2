 **Summary:**
The paper introduces Efficient-3DiM, a method aimed at accelerating the training process of single-image novel view synthesis using diffusion models. Key innovations include a modified timestep sampling strategy, integration of a self-supervised Vision Transformer, and enhanced training paradigms. These improvements significantly reduce training time from 10 days to under 1 day, making the method more accessible for real-time applications. The methodology is supported by comprehensive experiments, demonstrating the effectiveness of the proposed components. Despite its strengths, the paper lacks detailed comparative analysis and ablation studies, which could further solidify the claims of its efficiency and effectiveness.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel sampling strategy for diffusion timesteps, which optimizes training efficiency.
- The integration of a self-supervised Vision Transformer enhances the concatenation of high-level 3D features, improving the performance of the model.
- The methodology is well-supported by comprehensive experiments, demonstrating the efficiency and effectiveness of the proposed components.
- The paper is well-written, easy to follow, and provides a clear explanation of the proposed method.
- The method significantly reduces the training time from 10 days to under 1 day, making it more accessible for real-time applications.
- The paper addresses an important problem in the field of novel view synthesis from a single image, which is a significant challenge in computer vision.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed comparison with other methods, such as [1] and [2], which could provide a better understanding of the method's performance relative to existing solutions.
- There is a lack of ablation studies, which could help in understanding the individual contributions of the proposed components and their impact on the overall performance.
- The paper does not provide sufficient details on the training and inference times, which are crucial for evaluating the practical efficiency of the method.
- The paper could benefit from a more thorough discussion on the limitations and potential negative societal impacts of the proposed method.
- The paper does not provide a clear explanation of how the proposed method enhances the training efficiency specifically, and the technical contributions appear somewhat limited.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors provide a detailed comparison with other methods, such as [1] and [2], to better understand the method's performance relative to existing solutions?
- Could the authors conduct more ablation studies to better understand the individual contributions of the proposed components and their impact on the overall performance?
- Could the authors provide more details on the training and inference times to better evaluate the practical efficiency of the method?
- Could the authors clarify how the proposed method enhances the training efficiency specifically, and what are the specific technical contributions of the method?
- Could the authors provide more details on the dataset used for training and the specifics of the training process, such as the batch size and number of training epochs?
- Could the authors discuss the potential limitations and negative societal impacts of the proposed method, and how these might be addressed in future work?
