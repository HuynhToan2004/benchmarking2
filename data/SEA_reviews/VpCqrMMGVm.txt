 **Summary:**
The paper explores the internal mechanisms of large language models (LLMs) by focusing on their ability to perform mathematical addition and subtraction tasks. It introduces a novel approach using a directed acyclic graph (DAG) to represent the computational process of LLMs, specifically focusing on attention heads. The study identifies a small subset of attention heads that significantly influence the model's performance on addition and subtraction tasks, suggesting a mechanism for understanding the model's mathematical reasoning. The paper also discusses the generalizability of these findings across different datasets and models, though it is limited to smaller-scale models and a narrow set of tasks.

**Strengths:**
- The paper provides a novel approach to understanding the inner mechanisms of large language models (LLMs) by focusing on mathematical reasoning tasks, specifically mathematical addition and subtraction.
- The authors conduct a comprehensive analysis of attention heads in LLMs, revealing a small fraction of attention heads that significantly impact the model's performance on these tasks.
- The paper is well-written, clear, and easy to follow, making complex concepts accessible to readers.
- The findings are supported by robust experiments and demonstrate the generalizability of the identified key heads across different datasets and tasks.
- The study offers insights into the mathematical prowess of LLMs, which could inspire further research into the intricate capabilities of these models.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper is limited to smaller-scale models (7B, 6B, and 7B) and does not explore the application of the proposed method to larger models like Llama2-13B, which might exhibit different behaviors.
- The dataset used for the study is limited to addition tasks, which may not be representative of the model's performance on other mathematical operations such as multiplication and division.
- The paper's methodology is limited to attention heads and does not explore other mechanisms like MLPs, which could provide a more comprehensive understanding of the model's behavior.
- The paper's claims about the model's ability to perform mathematical operations based on attention heads' attention to number tokens are not convincingly supported by the evidence presented.
- The paper lacks a detailed discussion on the implications of the findings for the interpretability of LLMs and does not explore how the identified attention heads might affect the model's performance on other tasks.

**Questions:**
- How does the proposed method apply to larger models like Llama2-13B? Could the findings be generalized to these models?
- What are the implications of the findings for the interpretability of LLMs? How can this knowledge be used to make LLMs more transparent and understandable?
- Could the identified attention heads affect the model's performance on other tasks, such as language understanding or natural language generation?
- How do the attention heads' attention to number tokens translate into the model's ability to perform mathematical operations? Is there a direct correlation between the attention to number tokens and the model's performance on mathematical tasks?
- How does the paper's methodology address the limitations of the dataset, particularly the absence of tasks involving multiplication and division?
- What are the potential applications of the findings, and how can they be used to enhance the model's performance or to better understand its behavior?
