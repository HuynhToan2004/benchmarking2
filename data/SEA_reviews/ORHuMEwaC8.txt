 **Summary:**
The paper explores the use of counterfactual explanations to extract models from APIs, focusing on the relationship between counterfactuals and model extraction. The authors introduce two strategies for model extraction, leveraging either the closest counterfactuals or the Lipschitz continuity of models, to address the decision boundary shift issue. Theoretical analysis is provided to support these strategies, including the use of polytope theory and Lipschitz continuity. The paper also includes experimental validations on both synthetic and real-world datasets, demonstrating the effectiveness of the proposed methods. Despite the theoretical and experimental contributions, concerns are raised about the practicality of the assumptions and the generalizability of the results to more complex or realistic scenarios.

**Strengths:**
- The paper addresses an important and timely issue of model extraction from APIs, which is a significant concern in the current AI landscape.
- It introduces a novel approach using counterfactuals to extract models, which is both theoretically sound and practically applicable.
- The paper is well-written, clear, and easy to follow, making it accessible to a broad audience.
- The theoretical analysis provided is robust and supported by empirical evidence, demonstrating the practical utility of the proposed methods.
- The paper is original in its approach, providing a new perspective on model extraction that could lead to further research and development in the field.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper's assumptions, such as the convexity of the decision boundary and the availability of counterfactuals for queries from both sides of the decision boundary, may not hold in practical scenarios.
- The theoretical results are based on simplifying assumptions, which might not generalize well to more complex or realistic scenarios.
- The paper lacks a detailed discussion on the limitations of the proposed methods and potential negative societal impacts, which could be crucial for understanding the practical applicability and ethical implications of the work.
- The experimental setup is somewhat limited, focusing on specific datasets and not adequately addressing the generalizability of the findings.
- The paper could benefit from a more comprehensive literature review and a clearer differentiation from existing works, particularly those that discuss similar issues in model extraction.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors clarify the assumptions made in the theoretical analysis, particularly concerning the convexity of the decision boundary and the availability of counterfactuals?
- How do the authors address the potential for adversarial attacks on the counterfactual generating mechanism, which could undermine the effectiveness of the model extraction process?
- Could the authors provide more details on the computational complexity and scalability of the proposed methods, especially in relation to the size and complexity of the datasets used?
- How do the authors ensure the robustness of the model extraction process against different types of attacks, such as membership inference attacks or other forms of explanation-based attacks?
- Can the authors discuss the potential negative societal impacts of their work, particularly in the context of model extraction from APIs, and propose mitigation strategies to address these concerns?
