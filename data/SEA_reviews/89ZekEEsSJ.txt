 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel backdoor attack for image caption models, utilizing a stealthy trigger that is optimized to be imperceptible to humans and difficult to detect by existing defenses. The trigger is strategically placed at the center of the bounding box of the target object, allowing for precise manipulation of the model's output without affecting the overall image quality. The paper also explores the use of an object detection model to generate universal adversarial perturbations, which are then used to create the trigger. The effectiveness of the attack is demonstrated through extensive experiments on the Flickr8K and Flickr30K datasets, showing high attack success rates and minimal impact on clean performance.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel approach to backdoor attacks in image caption models by using a stealthy trigger that is strategically placed to minimize detection.
- The methodology is well-explained, making the paper easy to follow and understand.
- The paper conducts extensive experiments on two datasets, demonstrating the effectiveness of the proposed method.
- The authors consider the interactions and relationships among multiple data types, which is crucial for multimodal tasks.
- The paper proposes a new trigger design that increases the hardness for the backdoored samples to be detected, which is a significant contribution to the field.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a comprehensive evaluation of the trigger's effectiveness in different scenarios, particularly how it performs with various object detection models and different trigger sizes.
- The paper does not provide a detailed analysis of the computational overhead required to generate the trigger, which is crucial for practical implementation.
- The paper does not compare the proposed method with other existing backdoor attack methods, which could provide a better understanding of its advantages and limitations.
- The paper does not address the potential limitations of the proposed method, such as its effectiveness against different image caption models and the potential for trigger generalization across different models.
- The paper's presentation could be improved, particularly in terms of clarity and organization, especially in the experimental setup and results sections.

**Questions:**
- How does the proposed method perform against different object detection models, and how does it handle variations in trigger sizes?
- Can the authors provide a detailed analysis of the computational overhead required to generate the trigger, including the time and resource requirements?
- How does the proposed method compare to other existing backdoor attack methods in terms of effectiveness and efficiency?
- Could the authors provide more details on the experimental setup, particularly the data shuffling and the selection of backdoor and clean samples?
- How does the proposed method perform against different image caption models, and what are the potential limitations of the method?
- Can the authors clarify the definition and role of the activation clustering in the backdoor defense evaluation?
