 **Summary:**
The paper introduces COPRA, a novel approach to formal theorem proving that integrates a large language model (LLM) with a stateful backtracking search. COPRA leverages GPT-4 to manage proof tactics and retrieve relevant lemmas and definitions, enhancing the efficiency and accuracy of theorem proving. The methodology includes a system prompt that guides the LLM to interact with the proof environment, executing tactics and providing feedback for subsequent steps. The paper evaluates COPRA on benchmarks such as miniF2F and Compcert, demonstrating its superiority over one-shot invocations of GPT-4 and state-of-the-art models. However, the paper is criticized for its lack of novelty in using LLMs for theorem proving, the absence of detailed comparisons with existing methods, and the potential for overgeneralization in the proof search process.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, clearly presenting the problem and the proposed solution.
- The idea of using LLMs to manage proof tactics and retrieve relevant lemmas and definitions is novel and innovative.
- The integration of a LLM with a stateful backtracking search enhances the efficiency and accuracy of theorem proving.
- The evaluation of COPRA on benchmarks such as miniF2F and Compcert demonstrates its superiority over one-shot invocations of GPT-4 and state-of-the-art models.
- The use of a language-agent approach that integrates LLM policies with a search that minimizes LLM queries and hallucinations by tracking domain-specific information from the past is innovative.

**Weaknesses:**
- The novelty of the paper is questioned as using LLMs for theorem proving is not a new concept.
- The paper lacks detailed comparisons with existing methods, particularly in terms of the specifics of how COPRA outperforms these methods.
- The paper does not sufficiently address the potential for overgeneralization in the proof search process, which could lead to incorrect or incomplete proofs.
- The evaluation of COPRA is limited to a few benchmarks, and it would be beneficial to see how COPRA performs on other benchmarks such as TSTP and TAC.
- The paper does not provide sufficient details on the architecture of COPRA, which could limit the reproducibility and understanding of the results.
- The paper lacks a detailed discussion on the limitations and potential negative societal impacts of the proposed method.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors provide a detailed comparison of COPRA with other existing methods, specifically highlighting the advantages of COPRA over these methods?
- How does COPRA handle the potential for overgeneralization in the proof search process, and what measures are in place to prevent incorrect or incomplete proofs?
- Can the authors provide more details on the architecture of COPRA, including how it integrates with the proof environment and how it manages the interaction between the LLM and the proof environment?
- How does COPRA perform on other benchmarks such as TSTP and TAC, and what insights can be gained from these evaluations?
- Can the authors discuss the limitations of COPRA and the potential negative societal impacts of the proposed method?
