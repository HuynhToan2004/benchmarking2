 **Summary:**
The paper introduces CoInception, a novel framework designed for robust and efficient time series representation learning. It addresses the challenges of noise in time series data by employing a DWT low-pass filter and incorporating Inception blocks with dilation concepts. The framework is evaluated across various tasks including forecasting, classification, and anomaly detection, demonstrating state-of-the-art performance in most cases. The authors claim that CoInception outperforms existing methods due to its noise-resilient sampling strategy and efficient encoder architecture. However, the paper has been critiqued for its limited novelty, unclear presentation, and insufficient experimental validation.

**Strengths:**
- The paper addresses the important issue of noise in time series data, which is often overlooked in previous works.
- The proposed CoInception framework demonstrates state-of-the-art performance in various time series tasks such as forecasting, classification, and anomaly detection.
- The methodology is well-explained, and the results are promising, showing the effectiveness of the proposed approach.
- The paper is well-written and easy to follow, with a clear presentation of the proposed method.
- The experiments are comprehensive, covering multiple datasets and tasks, and the results are supported by ablation studies.

**Weaknesses:**
- The novelty of the proposed method is limited, as it primarily combines existing techniques without significant innovation.
- The paper lacks a clear definition and explanation of key terms and concepts, which can make it difficult for readers to understand the proposed method and its contributions.
- There is a lack of detailed experimental validation, particularly in terms of the impact of noise on the performance of the proposed method.
- The paper does not adequately compare its results with state-of-the-art methods, which could undermine the claimed improvements.
- The paper suffers from poor presentation quality, with several grammatical errors and unclear figures and tables.
- The paper does not sufficiently address the limitations of its method, particularly in terms of scalability and generalizability.

**Questions:**
- Could you clarify the definitions and roles of the terms "noise" and "perturbed view" in the context of your method?
- How does the proposed method perform when the noise level in the data is increased?
- Can you provide more details on the experimental setup, particularly how the noise was introduced and how it affects the results?
- Why was the decision made to use a DWT low-pass filter for noise reduction, and what are the specific advantages of this approach over other methods?
- How does the proposed method compare in terms of computational efficiency and scalability with other state-of-the-art methods?
- Could you provide more details on the design of the Inception block and the choice of dilation factors used in the experiments?
- How does the proposed method handle different types of noise, such as seasonal or random noise, and what are the implications for the performance of the model?
