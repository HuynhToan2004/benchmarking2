 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel adversarial attack method that operates within a predefined subspace of a transformed image representation while adhering to the L∞ pixel constraint. This approach leverages the barrier method from non-linear programming to optimize adversarial perturbations in the transformed space, aiming to enhance adversarial robustness against various image corruptions. The method is demonstrated to be effective in adversarial training, showing improvements in robustness against common image corruptions. The paper also discusses the application of this method across different image transformations such as DCT, DWT, and Glow, and presents experimental results that validate its effectiveness.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, making it easy to follow and understand the proposed methodologies.
- The idea of using a subspace of a transformed image representation for adversarial attacks is novel and interesting.
- The paper introduces a new adversarial attack method that efficiently computes adversarial perturbations in a predefined transformed representation subspace while adhering to the L∞ pixel constraint.
- The method is evaluated across different image transformations, including DCT, DWT, and Glow, and is shown to be effective in adversarial training.
- The paper demonstrates the effectiveness of the proposed method through experiments that show improvements in adversarial robustness against common image corruptions.
- The use of the barrier method to avoid projection in the optimization process is a significant contribution to the field.

**Weaknesses:**
