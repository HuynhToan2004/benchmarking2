 **Summary:**
The paper introduces Tiny-StyleWizard, a novel framework designed to address complex text style transfer by leveraging a large language model (LLM) to generate a specialized dataset, which is then used to fine-tune a small language model. The framework's novelty lies in its ability to generate high-quality data using LLMs, which is then used to train smaller models, thereby reducing the need for extensive training on large datasets. The paper proposes a new evaluation protocol to assess the quality of generated datasets and the performance of different models. Experiments conducted on two complex style transfer tasks demonstrate that small language models can produce stylized text on par with larger models, and even surpass them in some cases. The paper also introduces a new dataset and evaluation metrics, highlighting the importance of data quality and the potential of smaller models in complex style transfer tasks.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written and easy to follow, with a clear presentation of the proposed framework and its components.
- The introduction of a new evaluation protocol for ranking the quality of generated datasets and measuring the performance of different models is a significant contribution to the field.
- The paper demonstrates the effectiveness of small language models in complex style transfer tasks, showing that they can produce stylized text on par with larger models.
- The use of ChatGPT to generate a specialized dataset and the fine-tuning of a small language model on this dataset is an innovative approach that addresses the challenges of data scarcity and model size in complex style transfer tasks.
- The paper provides a comprehensive evaluation of the proposed framework, demonstrating its effectiveness through extensive experiments and comparisons with state-of-the-art models.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed analysis of the generated dataset and the process of filtering and ranking, which could provide valuable insights into the quality and diversity of the data.
- The experimental results are not sufficiently robust, with only two datasets used for evaluation, which limits the generalizability of the findings.
- The paper does not adequately address the limitations of the proposed framework, such as the potential for overfitting to the training data and the scalability of the approach to different styles and domains.
- The use of ChatGPT as the LLM for data generation and evaluation raises concerns about the potential for biased or inaccurate results, especially in cases where ChatGPT may not fully understand the nuances of the style being transferred.
- The paper does not provide sufficient details on the implementation of the proposed framework, such as the specifics of dataset generation and model training, which could hinder reproducibility and further research.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors provide more details on the filtering and ranking process used to generate the training data, including the criteria used and the impact of these choices on the quality and diversity of the data?
- How does the proposed framework perform on other complex style transfer tasks, such as those involving different styles or domains?
- Could the authors expand the evaluation to include more datasets and models to provide a more robust assessment of the framework's effectiveness?
- What steps are taken to ensure the accuracy and fairness of the evaluation, especially when using ChatGPT as the LLM for both data generation and evaluation?
- How does the proposed framework handle the potential for overfitting to the training data, and what measures are in place to prevent this from affecting the performance of the fine-tuned model?
- Could the authors provide more details on the implementation of the proposed framework, including the specifics of dataset generation, model training, and evaluation procedures, to enhance reproducibility and further research?
