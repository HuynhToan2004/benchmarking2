 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel approach to the Information Bottleneck (IB) framework for regression using Cauchy-Schwarz (CS) divergence, aiming to address the limitations of traditional methods like KL divergence and MSE loss. The authors propose a new objective function that integrates CS divergence to estimate mutual information terms, enhancing the robustness and generalization capabilities of the model. This approach is supported by theoretical analysis and empirical results, demonstrating improvements in generalization error and adversarial robustness over existing methods. However, concerns were raised regarding the clarity of the paper's presentation, the novelty of the CS divergence in the context of IB, and the practical implications of the computational complexity of the proposed method.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel approach to the Information Bottleneck (IB) framework for regression, using Cauchy-Schwarz (CS) divergence, which is both theoretically sound and practically applicable.
- The proposed method demonstrates improved generalization error and adversarial robustness compared to existing methods, as evidenced by empirical results.
- The paper provides a clear and concise presentation, effectively communicating the theoretical and practical aspects of the proposed method.
- The use of CS divergence in the IB framework is innovative and offers a fresh perspective on addressing the challenges of mutual information estimation.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper could benefit from a clearer explanation and justification of the choice of CS divergence over other divergences, particularly in the context of the IB framework.
- The computational complexity of the proposed method, which involves the calculation of Gram matrices, is not adequately addressed, raising concerns about its practical applicability.
- The novelty of the method is questioned as CS divergence and its properties have been previously explored, and the paper's contribution is seen as incremental rather than groundbreaking.
- There is a lack of detailed discussion on the assumptions and limitations of the proposed method, which could affect the understanding and applicability of the findings.
- The paper suffers from some clarity issues, particularly in the notation and explanation of certain equations and methods, which could confuse readers.

**Questions:**
- Could you provide a more detailed comparison of the computational complexity of the proposed method with existing methods, including a discussion on how this complexity affects the practical applicability of the method?
- How does the choice of CS divergence over other divergences, such as KL divergence, impact the performance and theoretical soundness of the method?
- Can you clarify the assumptions and limitations of the proposed method, particularly in relation to the applicability and generalizability of the results?
- How does the proposed method address the challenges of mutual information estimation, and what are the specific advantages of using CS divergence over other divergences in this context?
- Could you provide a clearer explanation of the notation and equations used in the paper, particularly in Section 2.1.1 and 2.1.2, to enhance the readability and comprehensibility of the content?
