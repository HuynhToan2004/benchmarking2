 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel operator, BEE, aimed at addressing the under-exploitation issue in reinforcement learning (RL) training by utilizing the best-performing actions from historical policies alongside the current policy. This approach is designed to enhance Q-value estimation, particularly in the latter stages of training where under-exploitation is prevalent. The BEE operator is integrated into two algorithms, BAC and MB-BAC, which are evaluated across various tasks including real-world robotics, showing improved performance over existing methods. The authors provide theoretical and empirical evidence to support the efficacy of the BEE operator in mitigating under-exploitation and improving sample efficiency.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel operator, BEE, which addresses the under-exploitation issue in RL training by utilizing the best-performing actions from historical policies, enhancing Q-value estimation.
- The proposed BAC and MB-BAC algorithms, which incorporate the BEE operator, demonstrate superior performance in various tasks including real-world robotics, showing improved sample efficiency.
- The paper provides theoretical and empirical evidence to support the efficacy of the BEE operator in mitigating under-exploitation and improving sample efficiency.
- The paper is well-written, clear, and easy to follow, with a comprehensive appendix that provides detailed algorithmic descriptions and proofs.
- The authors provide visualizations and videos to illustrate the method's performance, which helps in understanding the practical implications of the proposed method.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper could benefit from a more thorough discussion on the limitations and potential negative societal impacts of the proposed method.
- The paper does not sufficiently compare the BEE operator with other methods that aim to reduce overestimation, which could provide a more comprehensive evaluation of the BEE operator's effectiveness.
- The paper lacks detailed explanations on the computational complexity and memory requirements of the BEE operator, which could affect the practical applicability of the method.
- There is a lack of clarity in some figures and the presentation of results, which could make it difficult for readers to interpret the data and understand the method's effectiveness.
- The paper does not discuss the potential negative societal impacts of the proposed method, which is a significant omission in the ethical considerations of the work.

**Questions:**
- Could you elaborate on the potential negative societal impacts of the proposed method and how these might be mitigated or addressed?
- How does the BEE operator compare in terms of computational complexity and memory requirements to other methods that aim to reduce overestimation?
- Can you provide more detailed explanations on the computational efficiency and memory requirements of the BEE operator, especially in large-scale deployments?
- How does the BEE operator perform in scenarios where the historical policies are not optimal, and what are the implications of using suboptimal policies in the replay buffer?
- Could you clarify the definition and role of the term "under-exploitation" in the context of the BEE operator, and how it differs from other forms of exploitation in RL?
- How does the BEE operator handle the exploration-exploitation trade-off, especially in the context of real-world applications where optimal policies may not be readily available?
