 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel method for learning Laplacian representations of state spaces in reinforcement learning (RL) through a modified graph drawing objective. The proposed method aims to address the challenges of hyperparameter tuning and stability in existing methods by introducing a new objective that is theoretically sound and empirically effective. The paper presents a theoretical framework and empirical evidence demonstrating the method's ability to recover true eigenvectors and eigenvalues of the graph Laplacian, which is crucial for accurate representation learning in RL. The method's robustness is highlighted through experiments in various environments, showing superior performance over existing methods.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel method for learning Laplacian representations in reinforcement learning, which is both theoretically sound and empirically effective.
- The proposed method is able to recover true eigenvectors and eigenvalues of the graph Laplacian, which is a significant improvement over existing methods.
- The paper provides a detailed theoretical analysis and empirical evidence to support the method's effectiveness, including a comprehensive set of experiments across various environments.
- The paper is well-written, clear, and easy to follow, making it accessible to a broad audience.
- The introduction of the Augmented Lagrangian Laplacian Objective (ALLO) and its theoretical analysis are novel contributions to the field.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper does not sufficiently discuss the computational complexity of the proposed method, which could be a significant limitation in practical applications.
- The method's reliance on a high barrier coefficient and its sensitivity to the rate of increase of this coefficient are not adequately explored, which could limit its applicability in real-world scenarios.
- The paper lacks a thorough discussion on the limitations of the proposed method, particularly in environments with high-dimensional state spaces or where the graph Laplacian is not well-defined.
- The paper does not sufficiently address the practical implications of the method, such as its scalability and the computational resources required for its implementation.
- The paper could benefit from a more detailed comparison with existing methods, including a discussion on the advantages and disadvantages of each approach.

**Questions:**
- How does the proposed method compare in terms of computational complexity and resource requirements with existing methods?
- Can the authors provide more details on the computational efficiency of the method, particularly in large-scale applications?
- How does the method perform in environments where the graph Laplacian is not well-defined or where the state space is high-dimensional?
- What are the practical implications of the method's reliance on a high barrier coefficient and its sensitivity to the rate of increase of this coefficient?
- Could the authors elaborate on the method's scalability and its applicability in real-world scenarios, especially in environments with complex state spaces?
- How does the method compare with other recent approaches that use Laplacian representations in reinforcement learning?
