 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a new knowledge distillation loss termed PTLoss, which perturbs the vanilla KL divergence loss function using Maclaurin series. This perturbation aims to transform the teacher model into a proxy teacher, aligning its output distribution with the ground truth label distribution. The paper provides theoretical analysis and empirical evidence to support the effectiveness of PTLoss, demonstrating its superiority over standard KL loss across multiple natural language processing tasks. It also explores the connection between the distribution closeness and student model generalizability, proposing a method to determine perturbation coefficients. Despite its theoretical and empirical strengths, the paper faces criticism for its clarity in presentation, the novelty of its approach, and the fairness of its experimental comparisons.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel loss function for knowledge distillation, which is a significant contribution to the field.
- Theoretical analysis is provided to support the proposed loss function, enhancing the paper's credibility.
- Extensive experiments across multiple natural language processing tasks demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed loss function.
- The paper is well-written, making it easy to follow and understand, which is crucial for academic publications.
- The proposed method shows good performance in the experiments, indicating its practical applicability.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks clarity in some sections, particularly in the presentation of the main idea and the mathematical notation, which could confuse readers.
- The novelty of the proposed method is questionable as it seems to be a simple modification of existing methods like PolyLoss.
- The paper does not adequately compare its method with state-of-the-art techniques, which is crucial for evaluating its effectiveness.
- There are issues with the fairness of the experimental comparisons, particularly in the selection of hyperparameters for the proposed method versus baselines.
- The paper does not provide sufficient details on the experimental setup, such as the specifics of the models used and the datasets, which could affect the reproducibility of the results.
- The paper does not sufficiently address the computational complexity introduced by the proposed method, which could be a significant drawback in practical applications.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors clarify the main idea of the paper and ensure that the mathematical notation is clearer to avoid confusion?
- How does the proposed method differ from existing methods like PolyLoss, and what are the specific advantages of using the proposed method?
- Could the authors provide a more detailed comparison with state-of-the-art techniques to better establish the effectiveness of their method?
- How were the hyperparameters for the proposed method selected, and how does this compare with the selection of hyperparameters for the baselines?
- What are the specific models and datasets used in the experiments, and how were they chosen?
- Can the authors address the concerns about the computational complexity introduced by the proposed method and discuss potential strategies for mitigating these issues?
