 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel quantum graph neural network (QGNN) designed to solve mixed-integer linear programming (MILP) problems, specifically addressing the challenge of distinguishing between foldable and unfoldable MILP instances, which are indistinguishable using classical graph neural networks (GNNs). The proposed quantum neural network, termed equivariant quantum GNN (EQGNN), leverages the unique properties of quantum computing to represent MILP graphs and predict their feasibility, optimal objective values, and optimal solutions. The paper provides theoretical proofs and experimental results demonstrating the superiority of EQGNN over GNNs in handling foldable MILPs. The approach, while promising, is currently limited to small-scale MILP instances due to the computational constraints of quantum computing.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel quantum graph neural network (QGNN) for mixed-integer linear programming (MILP), which is a significant contribution to the field of quantum machine learning.
- The authors provide a detailed analysis of the expressivity of EQGNN compared to GNN, which is crucial for understanding the theoretical underpinnings of the proposed model.
- The paper is well-written and easy to follow, with clear explanations of the quantum neural network architecture and its implementation details.
- The experimental results demonstrate the effectiveness of EQGNN in handling foldable MILPs, showing superior performance over GNNs in terms of accuracy and parameter count.
- The paper includes a thorough analysis of the expressivity of EQGNN and GNN, which helps in understanding the theoretical advantages of the proposed model.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed discussion on the scalability of the proposed quantum neural network, which is crucial for understanding its applicability to larger, more complex MILP problems.
- The paper does not provide a clear explanation of how the quantum neural network is trained, which could hinder understanding and replication of the model.
- The experimental results are limited to small-scale MILP instances, which may not convincingly demonstrate the general applicability of the model to larger, more complex problems.
- The paper does not sufficiently discuss the limitations of the proposed model, which is essential for understanding its practical applicability and potential future research directions.
- The paper could benefit from a more detailed comparison with existing quantum graph neural networks and a broader range of experimental results to better establish the superiority of EQGNN over GNNs.

**Questions:**
- How does the number of qubits affect the expressivity of the quantum neural network?
- Can the authors provide a detailed explanation of how the quantum neural network is trained, including the optimization algorithm used?
- How does the quantum neural network handle the barren plateau problem, which is known to affect the training of quantum neural networks?
- Could the authors clarify the role and implementation of the auxiliary layer in the quantum neural network?
- How does the quantum neural network ensure permutation invariance, and what are the implications of this invariance for the model's performance and scalability?
- Can the authors provide more details on the experimental setup, particularly how the MILP instances were generated and how the quantum neural network was trained on these instances?
- How does the quantum neural network compare to other quantum graph neural networks in terms of performance and efficiency?
