 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel method for out-of-distribution (OOD) detection using a diffusion-based approach to generate synthetic outlier data from in-distribution (ID) data. The method involves training a diffusion model on ID data and generating pseudo-words from textual labels, which are then used to select candidate words from a large corpus. These candidate words are used to generate outlier images using a text-to-image diffusion model. The generated outliers are used to fine-tune the OOD detection model, aiming to enhance its ability to detect OOD data. The paper also explores the use of these outliers to improve the OOD detection performance by synthesizing surrogate outliers that approximate the distribution of the ID data. Extensive experiments are conducted to demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed method, comparing it with various baselines.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel method for generating outlier data using a diffusion model, which is a significant advancement in the field of OOD detection.
- The method is clearly explained and easy to follow, with a detailed description of the proposed algorithm.
- The paper is well-organized, making it easy to understand, and provides a comprehensive review of related work.
- Extensive experiments are conducted on various datasets, including CIFAR-10, CIFAR-100, and ImageNet, demonstrating the effectiveness of the proposed method.
- The use of a diffusion model for generating outlier data is innovative and provides a new perspective on OOD detection.
- The paper is well-written, with clear figures and tables that aid in understanding the proposed method and its results.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed explanation of how the textual inversion is performed, and the process of obtaining pseudo-words from textual labels is not clearly described.
- The methodology of generating outlier data from textual labels is not well-explained, and it is unclear how the generated data approximates the distribution of ID data.
- The paper does not provide a detailed analysis of the time and computational cost of generating synthetic outliers, which could be a significant limitation for practical applications.
- The paper lacks a detailed comparison with other outlier exposure methods, which could provide a better understanding of the proposed method's effectiveness.
- There are some grammatical errors and unclear statements in the paper that need to be corrected for clarity.
- The paper does not discuss the limitations of the proposed method, which could provide valuable insights into its applicability and potential drawbacks.

**Questions:**
- Could you provide more details on how the textual inversion is performed and how pseudo-words are obtained from textual labels?
- How does the proposed method ensure that the generated outliers approximate the distribution of ID data, and what are the implications of this approximation for OOD detection performance?
- What is the time and computational cost of generating synthetic outliers, and how do these costs compare to other outlier exposure methods?
- How does the proposed method compare to other OOD detection methods that do not use outlier exposure, such as post-hoc methods?
- Could you clarify the statement in line 196 regarding the outlier generation process, particularly how the diffusion model is trained on ID data and how this affects the generation of outliers?
- How do the authors ensure that the generated outliers are representative of the OOD distribution, and what steps are taken to prevent the model from overfitting to the generated outliers?
