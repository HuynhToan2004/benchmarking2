 **Summary:**
The paper introduces ProbTS, a unified toolkit designed to compare and contrast two distinct branches of time-series forecasting: those focusing on customized neural architectures and those employing advanced probabilistic estimation. ProbTS offers a comprehensive evaluation framework, allowing for the comparison of various models across different datasets, scenarios, and forecasting horizons. It provides insights into the strengths and weaknesses of each approach, highlighting the potential for future research in merging these branches. The toolkit's utility is demonstrated through its application to multiple datasets, showcasing its versatility and potential for further advancements in time-series forecasting.

**Strengths:**
- The paper provides a comprehensive overview of the state-of-the-art in time-series forecasting, comparing various models and their performance across different datasets and scenarios.
- It introduces ProbTS, a unified toolkit that allows for the evaluation of both neural architecture-based and probabilistic forecasting methods, providing a valuable resource for researchers in the field.
- The paper is well-written, clear, and easy to follow, making it accessible to a broad audience.
- The authors provide a detailed analysis of the strengths and weaknesses of different approaches, offering valuable insights for future research in this area.
- The inclusion of a comprehensive benchmark that covers various scenarios, datasets, and evaluation metrics enhances the paper's value as a reference for future research.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed discussion on the specific contributions of ProbTS compared to existing toolkits, which could have strengthened the paper's argument for its relevance and novelty.
- There is a need for more detailed implementation details, particularly concerning the training and evaluation of models using ProbTS, which could enhance the paper's reproducibility and practical utility.
- The paper's focus on a limited set of datasets and scenarios might not fully represent the diversity of real-world applications, which could limit the generalizability of the findings.
- The paper does not sufficiently explore the potential of combining different approaches, such as integrating probabilistic forecasting with customized neural architectures, which could provide more robust and accurate forecasts.
- There is a lack of discussion on the limitations of the current study, which could have provided a more balanced perspective on the challenges and opportunities in time-series forecasting research.

**Questions:**
- Could you provide more details on the specific contributions of ProbTS compared to existing toolkits, and how it addresses the gaps or limitations in current tools?
- How does ProbTS handle the training and evaluation of models across different datasets and scenarios, and what are the recommended practices for using the toolkit effectively?
- What are the potential areas for future research in merging the two branches of time-series forecasting, and how could this be achieved within the framework of ProbTS?
- Could you discuss the potential of combining different approaches, such as integrating probabilistic forecasting with customized neural architectures, and how this might impact the accuracy and robustness of forecasts?
- How does ProbTS handle the integration of different types of data, such as time-series data with additional covariates, and what are the implications for the model's performance and interpretability?
