 **Summary:**
The paper introduces DASFormer, a self-supervised learning framework designed for earthquake detection using Distributed Acoustic Sensing (DAS) data. DASFormer leverages a coarse-to-fine approach to learn spatial-temporal correlations in DAS data, treating earthquake detection as an anomaly detection task. The framework includes a coarse-to-fine generator, a noise-injected discriminator, and a high-level encoder-based generative loss. Experimental results demonstrate the effectiveness of DASFormer in earthquake detection and other downstream tasks, outperforming several state-of-the-art methods. The paper also discusses the application of DASFormer to other seismic data, such as submarine DAS data, and explores its potential in semi-supervised learning scenarios.

**Strengths:**
- The paper addresses a significant problem in the field of earthquake monitoring, which is crucial for public safety.
- The proposed method, DASFormer, is well-designed and effectively leverages the unique characteristics of DAS data to learn meaningful representations without labeled data.
- The paper is well-written, making it easy to follow, and the experiments are extensive, demonstrating the effectiveness of DASFormer in various tasks such as earthquake detection and other downstream tasks.
- The introduction of a self-supervised framework for DAS data is innovative, allowing for the application of DASFormer to downstream tasks for earthquake monitoring.
- The paper includes a comprehensive literature review, which provides a good overview of the current state of research in the field.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed discussion on the limitations of the proposed method, which is crucial for understanding its applicability and potential drawbacks.
- There is a lack of clarity in the description of the model, particularly the training objective and the specifics of the training process, which could hinder reproducibility.
- The paper does not provide sufficient details on the training process, such as the training time and the specifics of the training environment.
- The paper could benefit from a more detailed discussion on the effectiveness of the proposed method in scenarios with different data characteristics or in real-world applications.
- The paper does not adequately address the limitations of the dataset used, particularly in terms of its diversity and generalizability to other datasets.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors provide a more detailed discussion on the limitations of the proposed method and how these limitations might affect its applicability in different scenarios?
- How does the proposed method perform in scenarios with different data characteristics, such as different sampling rates or different noise levels?
- Could the authors provide more details on the training process, such as the training time and the specifics of the training environment?
- How does the proposed method compare to other state-of-the-art methods in terms of computational efficiency and scalability?
- Can the authors clarify the specifics of the training objective and the role of the high-level encoder-based generative loss in the training process?
- How does the proposed method handle the issue of annotated data scarcity in practical applications?
- Could the authors provide more details on the experimental setup, particularly in terms of the hardware used and the specifics of the data preprocessing?
