 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel method for image interpolation using diffusion models, addressing the challenges of noise and artifacts in images that are not generated by diffusion models. The approach integrates linear spherical interpolation with SDEdit, utilizing noise injection and boundary control to enhance the interpolation process. The method aims to maintain the integrity of original images by minimizing noise and artifacts, which is crucial for applications in advertising and other creative fields. The paper also includes theoretical insights and experimental validations to support the proposed method's effectiveness.

**Strengths:**
- The paper addresses a significant problem in image interpolation by proposing a method that enhances the quality of interpolated images, particularly in cases where the images are not generated by diffusion models.
- The method is well-motivated, with clear theoretical insights and a logical flow in the presentation, making it easy to follow.
- The proposed method is technically sound, combining linear spherical interpolation with SDEdit to create a unified framework that effectively reduces noise and preserves original image features.
- The paper provides a detailed analysis of the noise in the latent space and introduces a novel constraint on the noise component to generate less artifacts, which is a significant contribution to the field.
- The experimental results demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed method, showcasing improvements in image quality compared to existing methods.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a comprehensive discussion on the limitations of the proposed method, particularly in scenarios where the method may not perform optimally or may introduce artifacts.
- The theoretical contributions (Theorems 1 and 2) are not well-explained, making it difficult for readers to fully understand their implications and applications.
- The paper does not provide a thorough comparison with existing methods, which could help in understanding the advantages and disadvantages of the proposed method more clearly.
- The experimental results are not convincingly superior to existing methods, and the paper does not include a quantitative evaluation of the interpolation quality, which could strengthen the claims made.
- The paper lacks a discussion on the computational complexity of the proposed method, which could be a significant concern for practical applications.
- The paper does not sufficiently address the societal impact of the proposed method, which is an important consideration for many applications.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors clarify the theoretical contributions (Theorems 1 and 2) and provide examples or practical applications where these theorems can be effectively utilized?
- How does the proposed method compare in terms of computational complexity and practical application with existing methods?
- Can the authors provide a more detailed comparison with other state-of-the-art methods, including a quantitative evaluation of the interpolation quality?
- How does the method perform in scenarios where the noise levels are not well-matched with the denoising threshold, and what are the potential sources of artifacts in the interpolated images?
- Could the authors discuss the societal implications of the proposed method and provide examples of how it could be used in practical applications, particularly in advertising and creative fields?
