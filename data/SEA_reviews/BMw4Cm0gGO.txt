 **Summary:**
The paper introduces C-MCTS, a novel Monte Carlo Tree Search (MCTS) algorithm tailored for solving constrained Markov Decision Processes (CMDPs). C-MCTS employs a safety critic trained offline to predict costs, which aids in pruning unsafe trajectories during deployment. This methodology allows C-MCTS to operate closer to the constraint boundary, enhancing safety and efficiency compared to existing methods. The algorithm's effectiveness is demonstrated through experiments in Rocksample and Safe Gridworld environments, showing that it can outperform traditional MCTS and CC-MCP in terms of safety and cost-effectiveness. However, the paper's novelty is questioned due to similarities with previous works, and concerns are raised about the clarity of the method's contributions and the robustness of the experimental results.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written and easy to follow, with clear definitions and notations.
- The proposed method is well-motivated, with a clear explanation of the methodology and its advantages over existing methods.
- The algorithm is simple and effective, as shown by the experimental results which demonstrate its superiority over traditional MCTS and CC-MCP.
- The paper provides a comprehensive review of related works, highlighting the differences and contributions of the proposed method.
- The use of a safety critic to prune unsafe trajectories is a novel approach that could potentially improve safety in decision-making tasks.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a clear definition and explanation of the safety critic, which is a critical component of the proposed method.
- The novelty of the method is questioned, as similar approaches have been previously explored in the literature.
- The experimental results are not convincing, with the paper not comparing the proposed method against a sufficient number of baselines, and the results not being robustly supported by statistical tests.
- The paper's reliance on simulated environments for training raises concerns about the generalizability of the results to real-world scenarios.
- There is a lack of discussion on the computational complexity of the proposed method, which could be a significant limitation in practical applications.
- The paper does not adequately address the limitations of the proposed method, which could affect its applicability and effectiveness in different scenarios.

**Questions:**
- Can you clarify the role and function of the safety critic in the proposed method?
- How does the proposed method compare to other existing methods for solving CMDPs, such as those mentioned in the paper?
- What are the specific advantages of the proposed method over these existing methods, especially in terms of safety and efficiency?
- How does the proposed method handle scenarios where the cost functions are unknown or not directly observable?
- Can you provide more details on the experimental setup, particularly the conditions under which the experiments were conducted and the statistical tests used to validate the results?
- How does the computational complexity of the proposed method compare to other existing methods, and what are the practical implications of this complexity in terms of deployment and scalability?
- Can you provide additional examples or case studies to demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed method in real-world scenarios?
