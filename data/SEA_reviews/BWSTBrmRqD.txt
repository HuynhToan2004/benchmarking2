 **Summary:**
The paper introduces DOMINO, a dual-system for multi-step visual language reasoning, designed to handle complex visual question answering tasks. DOMINO consists of two primary components: System-1, responsible for extracting information from visual data, and System-2, which conducts multi-step reasoning based on the extracted information. The system is evaluated on various datasets, demonstrating improvements over existing methods, particularly in few-shot settings. Despite its innovative approach, concerns were raised regarding the novelty of the method, the clarity of the paper, and the fairness of the comparisons made.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, clear, and easy to follow, with a clear presentation of the methodology and results.
- The proposed method, DOMINO, demonstrates strong performance on various datasets, including outperforming strong baselines in few-shot settings.
- The use of a dual-system approach, where System-1 handles visual information extraction and System-2 conducts reasoning, is a novel and effective way to handle complex visual question answering tasks.
- The methodology is supported by comprehensive evaluations, including ablation studies and analysis of performance under different conditions.
- The paper provides a detailed analysis of the system's performance, including how it handles complex charts and the impact of different training strategies on the system's effectiveness.

**Weaknesses:**
- The novelty of the method is questioned, with concerns that the dual-system approach is not sufficiently different from existing methods like DePlot.
- The paper lacks clarity in some sections, particularly in the descriptions of the datasets and the methodology, which could confuse readers.
- There are concerns about the fairness of comparisons made in the paper, particularly in the evaluation of different systems on different datasets.
- The paper does not adequately discuss the limitations of the proposed method, which could be critical for understanding its applicability and potential drawbacks.
- The paper could benefit from a more detailed discussion on the societal impacts and ethical considerations of the proposed method.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors clarify the differences between the proposed method and DePlot, particularly in terms of the dual-system approach and the handling of visual information?
- How does the paper ensure the fairness of comparisons between different systems, especially when evaluating them on different datasets?
- Could the authors provide more detailed explanations of the training and evaluation processes, especially in sections where the methodology is unclear?
- How does the paper address the potential societal impacts and ethical considerations of the proposed method?
- Can the authors provide more details on the training data used for System-1 and the specifics of the training process for System-2?
- How does the paper ensure that the method can handle more complex or varied types of visual data beyond those presented in the experiments?
