 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel watermarking method for large language models (LLMs) that does not compromise the quality of the generated text. It proposes two unbiased reweighting methods, delta-reweight and gamma-reweight, which preserve the output probability distribution and do not affect the performance of downstream tasks. The paper also presents a new statistical hypothesis test for watermark detection, which is theoretically grounded and supported by empirical results. The methodology is evaluated on tasks such as text summarization and machine translation, demonstrating its effectiveness in maintaining output quality while embedding watermarks. However, concerns are raised about the practicality of the watermarking approach, the complexity of the algorithm, and the limited evaluation scope.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, with clear presentation and a logical flow that is easy to follow.
- The introduction of unbiased watermarking is a novel and significant contribution to the field, addressing the challenge of watermarking in LLMs without compromising output quality.
- The proposed methods, delta-reweight and gamma-reweight, are innovative and supported by theoretical guarantees, which are validated through experiments.
- The paper provides a comprehensive framework for watermarking LLMs, including the design and detection of unbiased watermarks, which is a valuable resource for the community.
- The theoretical results and empirical validation of the proposed methods demonstrate their effectiveness in maintaining output quality while embedding watermarks.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed explanation of the watermarking method's practical implementation, which could make it difficult for practitioners to apply the method in real-world scenarios.
- The complexity of the algorithm, particularly in the detection process, could pose challenges for practical implementation and scalability.
- The evaluation scope is limited, focusing only on two tasks (text summarization and machine translation), which may not fully demonstrate the generalizability of the proposed methods.
- There is a lack of discussion on the potential negative societal impacts of watermarking LLMs, which is an important consideration in the context of AI ethics.
- The paper does not adequately address the potential for watermarking to be detected by users, which could undermine the effectiveness of the watermarking approach.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors provide a more detailed explanation of how the watermarking method can be practically implemented, including the specifics of the algorithm and the resources required?
- How does the proposed method perform when applied to other types of tasks or models, and what are the implications for the scalability and applicability of the approach?
- What are the potential negative societal impacts of watermarking LLMs, and how does the paper address these concerns?
- How does the proposed method compare to other watermarking techniques, and what are the advantages or disadvantages of using this approach compared to others?
- In the context of watermarking, how does the proposed method ensure that the watermark is not easily detected by users, and what measures are in place to prevent the watermark from being removed or altered?
