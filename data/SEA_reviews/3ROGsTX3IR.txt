 **Summary:**
The paper explores the phenomenon of "Grokking" in neural networks through the lens of phase transitions, focusing on two models: a teacher-student model and a modular algebra model. The authors extend previous work by Seroussi et al. (2023) to include a Gaussian mixture approximation, aiming to understand how neural networks adapt to different learning regimes. The paper presents a theoretical framework that connects neural network training dynamics with phase transitions, proposing a Gaussian mixture feature learning (GMFL) model that describes the transition from Gaussian feature learning (GFL) to GMFL-I and GMFL-II. Despite the theoretical rigor and the extension of previous work, the paper is critiqued for its limited experimental validation and the lack of broader applicability of the proposed framework.

**Strengths:**
- The paper extends previous work on phase transitions in neural networks, specifically in the context of Grokking, and provides a rigorous theoretical framework for understanding feature learning in neural networks.
- The paper is well-written, with clear explanations of the theoretical results and their implications, making it accessible to a broad audience.
- The authors provide detailed derivations and explanations of the theoretical results, which are supported by numerical experiments that demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed framework.
- The paper introduces a novel approach to studying feature learning in neural networks, which could potentially lead to new insights and applications in the field.
- The authors have successfully extended previous work by Seroussi et al. (2023) to include a Gaussian mixture approximation, which is a significant contribution to the field.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper primarily focuses on two specific models, which limits the generalizability of the findings to other neural network architectures or learning scenarios.
- The experimental validation is limited to these two models, and it is unclear how the proposed framework would apply to more complex or varied neural network setups.
- The paper does not provide a clear explanation of how the proposed framework could be extended to other types of neural network architectures or learning scenarios.
- The paper does not adequately address the practical implications of the theoretical results, such as how they could be applied to improve neural network performance or training strategies.
- The paper could benefit from a more comprehensive literature review, particularly in the context of phase transitions in neural networks and the relationship between Grokking and phase transitions.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors provide a more detailed explanation of how the proposed framework could be extended to other types of neural network architectures or learning scenarios?
- How do the authors envision the practical applications of the theoretical results presented in the paper?
- Could the authors elaborate on the relationship between Grokking and phase transitions, and how their work builds upon or differs from previous research in this area?
- In the context of the experiments presented, how do the authors justify the choice of models and parameters, and what insights do these choices provide into the broader applicability of the proposed framework?
