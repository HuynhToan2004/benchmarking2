 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a hierarchical reinforcement learning (HRL) framework named Skipper, which utilizes spatial and temporal abstractions to enhance generalization capabilities in complex tasks. Skipper decomposes tasks into manageable sub-tasks, represented as a graph where nodes are states and edges are estimated transitions. It employs a checkpoint generator to split states into context and partial descriptions, enabling the generation of diverse checkpoints for planning. The framework is evaluated in the MiniGrid-BabyAI environment, demonstrating superior generalization performance compared to existing HRL methods. Despite its innovative approach, the paper suffers from presentation issues and lacks clarity in some sections, making it difficult to follow.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel hierarchical reinforcement learning (HRL) framework, Skipper, which decomposes tasks into manageable sub-tasks, enhancing generalization capabilities in complex tasks.
- Skipper's approach to spatial and temporal abstractions is innovative, providing a new perspective on hierarchical reinforcement learning.
- The framework is evaluated in the MiniGrid-BabyAI environment, demonstrating superior generalization performance compared to existing HRL methods.
- The paper is well-written and presents a clear motivation for the research, making it easy to follow and understand.
- The Skipper framework is compatible with stochasticity, which is a crucial aspect for practical applications.
- The framework's ability to generate diverse checkpoints and its compatibility with stochasticity are significant advantages over other HRL methods.

**Weaknesses:**

- The paper lacks a detailed discussion on the limitations of the Skipper framework, which is crucial for understanding its applicability and potential drawbacks.
- The evaluation of the Skipper framework is limited to the MiniGrid-BabyAI environment, which may not adequately demonstrate its effectiveness in more complex or diverse settings.
