 **Summary:**
The paper introduces IceFormer, a novel method designed to accelerate self-attention during inference without retraining, which is crucial for handling long sequences on CPUs. IceFormer leverages k-Nearest Neighbor Search (k-NNS) to optimize both time and space complexity, focusing on the sparsity of the attention matrix. The methodology involves embedding keys and queries into a higher dimensional space and using a ranking-based k-NNS algorithm to identify important keys, thereby reducing computational complexity. The paper evaluates IceFormer on various benchmarks, demonstrating its effectiveness in improving inference speed and reducing memory usage.

**Strengths:**
- The paper addresses an important problem by focusing on accelerating self-attention during inference, which is critical for handling long sequences on CPUs.
- The proposed method is simple, intuitive, and well-motivated, with a clear and easy-to-follow presentation that enhances understanding.
- The paper provides extensive experimental results, including comparisons with multiple baseline models and detailed performance analysis, which demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed method.
- The use of k-NNS for accelerating attention is innovative and offers a novel approach to reducing computational complexity.
- The paper is well-written, making it accessible and easy to follow, which is beneficial for both academic and practical applications.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed discussion on the memory usage and computational cost of the k-NNS algorithm, which is crucial for understanding the practical implications of the proposed method.
- The comparison with other efficient attention methods is insufficient, particularly in terms of the number of models tested and the depth of the comparative analysis.
- There is a lack of discussion on the limitations and potential negative societal impacts of the proposed method.
- The paper does not sufficiently explore the scalability of the method to larger models, which is a significant limitation given the current trends in model sizes.
- The paper does not provide a clear explanation of the differences between IceFormer and other efficient attention methods like Nystromformer, which could lead to confusion about the novelty and effectiveness of the proposed method.
- The paper lacks a thorough discussion on the generalizability of the method across different model architectures and sequences, which could limit its applicability.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors provide more details on the memory usage and computational cost of the k-NNS algorithm?
- How does the proposed method compare in terms of speed and accuracy with other efficient attention methods, particularly those that are also designed for inference acceleration?
- Could the authors elaborate on the scalability of the method to larger models and how it performs under different hardware conditions?
- What are the specific advantages of IceFormer over other efficient attention methods, and how does it address the limitations of existing methods?
- How does the proposed method handle causal masks, and what are the implications for the model's performance and accuracy?
- Can the authors clarify the notation and terminology used in the paper to improve readability and understanding?
