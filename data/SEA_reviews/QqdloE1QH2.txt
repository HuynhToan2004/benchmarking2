 **Summary:**
The paper introduces MMA, a novel dataset consisting of 332k informal-formal pairs across multiple formal languages (Isabelle and Lean4) and domains, generated using GPT-4. The dataset is significant as it represents the largest and most diverse of its kind, aiming to enhance the capabilities of autoformalization models. The paper demonstrates that fine-tuning a base model on MMA leads to substantial improvements in performance on benchmarks like miniF2F and ProofNet, highlighting the dataset's utility. However, concerns are raised about the dataset's quality, as it is largely derived from a single source (GPT-4), and the manual evaluation methods used, which may not fully capture the dataset's effectiveness.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, clear, and easy to follow, with a strong motivation to create a large-scale autoformalization dataset.
- The creation of the MMA dataset is a significant achievement, providing a valuable resource for the research community.
- The dataset is diverse, covering multiple formal languages and domains, and its size makes it a substantial contribution to the field.
- The paper provides a comprehensive evaluation of the dataset, showing that fine-tuning models on MMA significantly improves performance on downstream tasks.
- The dataset's creation process is detailed, and the paper includes a thorough analysis of the quality of the dataset, which is crucial for its utility.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed discussion on the quality of the dataset, particularly the reliability of the informalization process and the potential for errors.
- There is a significant reliance on GPT-4 for dataset creation, which may limit the diversity of the dataset and introduce potential biases.
- The evaluation methodology is not robust, with a small sample size (50 examples) and a manual evaluation process that may not be objective or reproducible.
- The paper does not adequately address the potential for contamination from the pre-training data of GPT-4, which could affect the validity of the results.
- The paper does not sufficiently discuss the potential societal impacts of the work, which could be significant given the dataset's potential applications.

**Questions:**
- How does the quality of the dataset compare to other existing datasets, and what steps were taken to ensure its reliability?
- Could the authors provide more details on the evaluation process, particularly how the manual evaluations were conducted and the criteria used?
- How does the dataset handle the potential for contamination from GPT-4's pre-training data, and what steps were taken to mitigate this risk?
- Given the reliance on GPT-4 for dataset creation, how does the paper address the potential for biases or limitations introduced by this tool?
- Could the authors discuss the potential societal impacts of their work, particularly in the context of automated theorem proving and formal verification?
