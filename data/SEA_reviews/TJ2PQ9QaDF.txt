 **Summary:**
The paper investigates the benign overfitting phenomenon in a two-layer ReLU CNN trained on XOR-type data, which is a more complicated setting compared to previous studies. The authors establish matching upper and lower bounds on the test error achieved by the network, demonstrating that under certain conditions, the network can achieve near-Bayes-optimal accuracy. The paper also introduces a novel proof technique called "virtual sequence comparison" to analyze the learning of highly correlated XOR-type data. Despite its theoretical contributions, the paper has been critiqued for its limited applicability beyond the specific model and data distribution used, and for not sufficiently comparing its results with existing literature.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written and the proofs are clear, making the content accessible and easy to follow.
- The authors have provided a novel proof technique called "virtual sequence comparison," which is a significant contribution to the field.
- The paper establishes matching upper and lower bounds on the test error achieved by the network, demonstrating that under certain conditions, the network can achieve near-Bayes-optimal accuracy.
- The results are well-motivated, and the paper provides a clear and detailed explanation of the experimental results, which support the theoretical findings.
- The paper is original in its approach, as it is the first to study benign overfitting in two-layer CNNs for XOR-type data, which is a more complicated setting compared to previous studies.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper's applicability is limited as it only focuses on a specific model (two-layer CNNs) and data distribution (XOR-type data), which may not generalize to other models or data distributions.
- The paper does not sufficiently compare its results with existing literature, particularly in terms of the sharp phase transition between benign and harmful overfitting, which has been previously observed in other studies.
- The paper's contribution is seen as incremental, as it builds on existing work by Kou et al. (2023) and does not significantly advance the understanding of benign overfitting in more general settings.
- The proofs rely on strong assumptions, such as the condition on the learning rate and the initialization scale, which may not hold in practical scenarios.
- The paper does not provide a clear explanation of the technical challenges and the advantages of the new proof technique compared to existing methods.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors clarify the technical challenges of analyzing the XOR-type data and how these challenges are addressed by the new proof technique?
- How do the results compare with existing literature, particularly in terms of the sharp phase transition between benign and harmful overfitting?
- Can the authors provide a more detailed comparison with Kou et al. (2023) to highlight the technical differences and contributions of their work?
- Is it possible to extend the analysis to more general settings, such as deep networks or other data distributions?
- How do the results depend on the specific initialization scheme used in the model, and could the authors provide more details on this aspect?
