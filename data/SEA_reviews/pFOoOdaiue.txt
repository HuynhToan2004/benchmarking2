 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel approach to adversarial reinforcement learning (RARL) by utilizing entropy regularization to enhance the robustness of the adversarial agent. The authors propose a curriculum-based training method where the adversary's rationality is gradually increased, starting from a completely random behavior. This method, termed QARL, aims to address the complexity of finding Nash equilibria in high-dimensional control settings by modulating the rationality of the adversary. The paper presents empirical results showing that QARL outperforms existing RARL baselines in terms of performance and robustness across various MuJoCo locomotion and navigation problems. However, concerns are raised about the theoretical justification of the curriculum learning approach and the lack of detailed empirical comparisons with existing methods.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel approach to adversarial reinforcement learning (RARL) by utilizing entropy regularization to enhance the robustness of the adversarial agent.
- The proposed curriculum-based training method, where the adversary's rationality is gradually increased, is a significant contribution to the field.
- The paper is well-written, making it accessible and easy to follow, with clear descriptions of the proposed method and its implementation.
- The empirical results are convincing, showing that the proposed method outperforms existing RARL baselines in terms of performance and robustness.
- The use of entropy regularization to address the complexity of saddle point optimization problems in adversarial RL is a promising direction for future research.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed theoretical justification for the curriculum learning approach, particularly how it ensures the convergence of the algorithm to the optimal solution.
- There is a lack of comparison with existing methods that also use entropy regularization or similar approaches, which could have provided a more robust evaluation of the proposed method.
- The empirical comparisons are limited to a few baselines, and the paper does not sufficiently discuss the results of these baselines, which could have provided a more comprehensive evaluation of the proposed method.
- The paper does not adequately address the scalability of the proposed method to more complex or realistic environments, such as those found in robotic manipulation or autonomous driving.
- There is a lack of discussion on the limitations of the proposed method, particularly how it might perform in more complex or varied environments.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors provide more details on how the curriculum learning approach ensures convergence to the optimal solution?
- How does the proposed method compare with existing methods that also use entropy regularization or similar approaches?
- Can the authors provide a more detailed discussion of the results of the baselines, particularly how they compare to the proposed method in terms of performance and robustness?
- How does the proposed method scale to more complex or realistic environments, such as those found in robotic manipulation or autonomous driving?
- Could the authors provide more details on the scalability of the proposed method to larger or more complex environments?
- In the experiments, how does the proposed method perform when the adversary's rationality is not gradually increased, but rather kept at a constant level?
- How does the proposed method handle different types of adversaries, such as those with different rationality levels or different types of behavior?
