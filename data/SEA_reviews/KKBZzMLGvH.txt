 **Summary:**
The paper explores the application of Bayesian Optimization (BO) for decision-making in multi-agent systems, focusing on the challenges of high-dimensional spaces and sparse reward signals. The authors introduce a novel approach by integrating a Hessian-aware BO method with a higher-order model (HOM) that includes role and role interaction abstractions. This method aims to improve performance in complex multi-agent tasks by reducing the dimensionality of the policy space. The paper also presents a theoretical analysis and empirical validation, showcasing the method's effectiveness in scenarios with limited feedback and resource constraints.

**Strengths:**
- The paper addresses an important and relevant problem in the field of multi-agent decision-making, focusing on the challenges of high-dimensional spaces and sparse reward signals.
- It introduces a novel approach by integrating a Hessian-aware BO method with a higher-order model (HOM) that includes role and role interaction abstractions, which is a significant contribution to the field.
- The paper is well-written, making it accessible and understandable, and includes a thorough theoretical analysis and empirical validation, demonstrating the method's effectiveness in scenarios with limited feedback and resource constraints.
- The proposed method is shown to be effective in complex multi-agent tasks under sparse or malformed reward, and outperforms existing methods in these settings.
- The paper provides a clear and well-structured presentation, making it easy to follow, and includes a detailed description of the experimental setup, which enhances reproducibility.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed discussion on the limitations of the proposed method, particularly in terms of its scalability and applicability in different scenarios.
- There are some unclear explanations and potential errors in the mathematical formulations, which could affect the understanding and reproducibility of the results.
- The paper could benefit from more detailed explanations and examples of the role abstraction and role interaction abstractions, as well as the specific applications of these concepts.
- The paper's focus on a specific type of multi-agent task (multi-agent POMDP) may limit its applicability to other types of multi-agent systems.
- There is a lack of comparison with other multi-agent Bayesian optimization (MABO) methods, which could provide a more comprehensive evaluation of the proposed method's effectiveness.
- The paper does not sufficiently address the issue of scalability, which is crucial for the practical application of the proposed method in larger and more complex systems.

**Questions:**
- Could you provide more details on the limitations of the proposed method and how they might affect its scalability and applicability in different scenarios?
- How does the proposed method handle the issue of scalability, particularly in terms of the number of agents and the complexity of the tasks?
- Can you clarify the role of the Hessian in the method's effectiveness and whether the observed improvements are solely due to the Hessian or other factors?
- How does the method perform when the number of agents is significantly increased, and what are the potential challenges or limitations in such scenarios?
- Could you provide more details on the role abstraction and role interaction abstractions, and how they are applied in specific scenarios?
- How does the proposed method compare to other MABO methods, and what are the key differences in terms of performance and applicability?
