 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel approach to community detection in graphs using a differentiable formulation of the map equation, which is integrated with graph neural networks (GNNs) to optimize clustering performance. This method, termed Neuromap, leverages a loss function based on the map equation to perform end-to-end clustering, potentially improving upon traditional methods like Infomap. The authors demonstrate the effectiveness of this approach through empirical evaluations on both synthetic and real-world datasets, showcasing its ability to handle overlapping communities and avoid over-partitioning. Despite its innovative approach, the paper is criticized for its limited novelty and the lack of a detailed comparison with state-of-the-art methods.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, making it easy to follow and understand, with clear explanations of the proposed methodology.
- The integration of the map equation with graph neural networks (GNNs) is a novel approach that shows potential for improving community detection in graphs.
- The experimental results, including those on synthetic and real-world datasets, demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed method in detecting overlapping communities and avoiding over-partitioning.
- The paper provides a comprehensive evaluation of the proposed method, comparing it with other community detection algorithms and demonstrating its potential for application in real-world scenarios.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed discussion on the limitations and potential negative societal impacts of the proposed method, which could be crucial for understanding its broader implications.
- The novelty of the approach is questioned, as the integration of the map equation with GNNs seems like a straightforward combination without significant innovation.
- The experimental evaluation could be improved by including more recent and relevant baselines, such as those from the deep learning community, and by conducting more comprehensive ablation studies.
- The paper does not adequately discuss the computational complexity of the proposed method, which could be a significant drawback in practical applications.
- The paper's focus on a specific GNN architecture might limit its applicability to other GNNs, which could affect the generalizability of the results.

**Questions:**
- How does the proposed method compare with other recent community detection algorithms, particularly those that leverage deep learning techniques?
- Can the authors provide more details on the computational complexity of the proposed method and discuss how it scales with the size of the graph?
- How does the method handle overlapping communities, and can it be adapted to handle more complex community structures?
- Could the authors clarify the choice of the GNN architecture used in the experiments and discuss the potential implications of using different architectures?
- In light of the concerns about novelty and the need for more recent and relevant baselines, how does the paper address these issues, and what steps are being taken to ensure the method's competitiveness in the current research landscape?
