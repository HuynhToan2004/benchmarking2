 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a model-based offline reinforcement learning method for traffic signal control (TSC), aiming to address the challenges of instability and low data utilization in traditional online RL methods. The proposed method, MOTSC, utilizes a Bayesian Neural Network (BNN) to model the transition dynamics and introduces a movement-independent transition model to reduce uncertainty. The methodology is tested against various baselines using datasets from CityFlow and shows promising results in terms of performance and data utilization. However, concerns are raised regarding the novelty of the approach, the clarity of the methodological presentation, and the robustness of the experimental validation.

**Strengths:**
- The paper addresses a significant problem in the field of traffic signal control, focusing on the application of offline reinforcement learning to improve data utilization and stability.
- The introduction of a movement-independent transition model is innovative and provides a novel approach to model traffic dynamics more effectively.
- The theoretical analysis provided supports the claims made about the effectiveness of the model in reducing uncertainty and improving performance.
- The paper is well-structured and clearly written, making it accessible and easy to follow.
- The proposed method, MOTSC, shows promising results in terms of performance and data utilization, outperforming traditional methods and other offline RL methods.
- The methodology is well-motivated and addresses the challenges of offline RL in TSC, such as the complexity of the transition dynamics and the tendency to overestimate uncertainty.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a thorough discussion on the limitations and potential negative societal impacts of the proposed method.
- The novelty of the approach is questionable as similar methods have been previously explored, and the theoretical contributions are not clearly distinguished from existing works.
- The experimental validation is insufficient, with concerns about the robustness of the results and the generalizability of the findings.
- The paper could benefit from a more detailed explanation of the experimental setup and the methodology used, particularly in sections where the methodology is introduced.
- There are issues with the clarity and presentation of the paper, including unclear figures and a lack of detailed explanation in some sections.
- The paper does not adequately address the scalability of the method to more complex scenarios or larger datasets, which could limit its applicability in real-world settings.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors clarify the novelty of the approach and how it differs from previous works in terms of methodology and theoretical contributions?
- How does the proposed method compare in terms of performance and data utilization to other offline RL methods when applied to different datasets or more complex scenarios?
- Could the authors provide a more detailed explanation of the experimental setup and the methodology used, particularly in sections where the methodology is introduced?
- How does the method handle the scalability issues, and what steps have been taken to ensure its applicability in more complex or larger-scale scenarios?
- Could the authors provide a more detailed discussion on the limitations and potential negative societal impacts of the proposed method?
