 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel method for estimating uncertainty in neural networks using a multi-headed network architecture that integrates function space particle optimization. This approach, termed MH-f-POVI, leverages a single multi-headed network to represent ensemble members, thereby reducing computational and memory requirements compared to traditional deep ensemble methods. The methodology is validated through experiments on both synthetic and real-world datasets, demonstrating its effectiveness in disentangling aleatoric and epistemic uncertainty, detecting out-of-distribution data, and providing calibrated uncertainty estimates under distribution shifts. However, the paper has been critiqued for its lack of clarity in explaining the method's computational efficiency and its reliance on pre-trained models, which may limit its applicability in practical scenarios.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel method for uncertainty estimation using a multi-headed network, which is a significant advancement over traditional deep ensemble methods.
- The method is well-motivated and effectively presented, with a clear exposition of the methodology and its applications.
- The paper demonstrates the effectiveness of the proposed method through comprehensive experiments, showing that it can provide calibrated uncertainty estimates under distribution shifts while significantly reducing computational requirements.
- The approach is scalable and potentially more efficient than previous methods, making it a valuable contribution to the field.
- The paper is well-written and easy to follow, with clear explanations of the methodology and its applications.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks clarity in explaining the computational efficiency of the proposed method, particularly how it reduces computational requirements compared to deep ensemble methods.
- The method's dependence on pre-trained models may limit its applicability in practical scenarios where such models are not available.
- The paper does not sufficiently compare its method with existing approaches, particularly in terms of computational efficiency and the effectiveness of uncertainty estimates.
- The paper's evaluation is limited to specific datasets, which may not adequately demonstrate the method's performance across a broader range of scenarios.
- There are inconsistencies in the presentation of results and methodological details, which could confuse readers and undermine the credibility of the findings.
- The paper does not sufficiently discuss the limitations of its method, which could help in understanding its practical applicability and potential drawbacks.
