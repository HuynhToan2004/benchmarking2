 **Summary:**
The paper explores the relationship between image probability and perceptual sensitivity, using advanced generative models like PixelCNN++ to estimate image probabilities. It proposes that log(px) and log(px~) are the most significant factors in predicting perceptual sensitivity, validated through experiments on classical psychophysics such as the contrast sensitivity function. The authors suggest that these factors could be used to develop a new model for predicting perceptual sensitivity, potentially improving upon existing models. The paper also discusses the implications of these findings for understanding the efficiency of human visual systems in processing information.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, clear, and easy to follow, with a clear problem statement and a clear motivation.
- The authors provide a detailed and thorough analysis, including experiments on classical psychophysics, which are relevant and interesting.
- The proposed model is simple, interpretable, and achieves a high correlation with perceptual sensitivity, which is validated through various experiments.
- The paper provides a comprehensive overview of previous works and sets the stage for the current study, which is crucial for understanding the context and the significance of the findings.
- The experiments are carefully designed and executed, with detailed results that support the claims made in the paper.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed discussion on the limitations of the work, which could help in understanding the scope and applicability of the findings.
- The experiments are primarily based on a single dataset (CIFAR10), which might limit the generalizability of the results.
- There is a lack of discussion on the relationship between the proposed model and existing models, which could help in understanding the novelty and the contribution of the work.
- The paper does not sufficiently discuss the potential impact of the findings on future research, particularly in terms of developing new models or improving existing ones.
- The use of the term "information" in the paper is not clearly defined, which could lead to confusion about the meaning and application of the term.
- The paper could benefit from a more detailed discussion on the choice of the generative model and its implications for the results.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors clarify the definition and role of "information" in the context of the paper?
- How do the authors justify the choice of the generative model, and what are the implications of using a different model?
- Could the authors provide more details on the experimental setup, particularly the choice of the generative model and the parameters used?
- How do the authors address the potential limitations of their work, and what are the implications of these limitations for the applicability and generalizability of the findings?
- Could the authors discuss the relationship between their proposed model and existing models, and how their work contributes to a better understanding of the relationship between image probability and perceptual sensitivity?
