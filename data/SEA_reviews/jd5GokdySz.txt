 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel robustness metric, Foundation Model-oriented Robustness (FMR), which measures the robustness of image classification models against perturbations by comparing their performance with a surrogate oracle, a zoo of foundation models. The FMR is calculated by normalizing the perturbed accuracy with the standard accuracy, providing a dynamic evaluation protocol that generates perturbed test images using a foundation model. This method aims to evaluate the model's robustness beyond the limitations of fixed benchmarks, offering a new way to assess model performance. The paper also discusses the potential biases introduced by this method and provides empirical evidence on the robustness of various models.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel robustness metric, FMR, which measures the robustness of image classification models against perturbations by comparing their performance with a surrogate oracle.
- The proposed dynamic evaluation protocol generates perturbed test images using a foundation model, providing a new way to evaluate model performance beyond the limitations of fixed benchmarks.
- The paper provides a comprehensive evaluation of the proposed method, comparing it with existing methods and discussing potential biases introduced by the proposed method.
- The paper is well-written and easy to follow, making it accessible to a broad audience.
- The authors provide a systematic study of robustness evaluation, which is a critical aspect of machine learning model evaluation.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a clear discussion on the limitations and potential biases introduced by the proposed method.
- The paper does not sufficiently discuss the practical implications of the FMR metric, particularly in terms of its generalizability and applicability to different types of models and datasets.
- The paper does not include a detailed comparison with other robustness metrics, which could help in understanding the strengths and weaknesses of the proposed method.
- The paper does not discuss the computational costs associated with the proposed method, which could be a significant limitation for practical deployment.
- The paper does not provide a detailed analysis of the generated perturbed images, which could help in understanding the effectiveness and quality of the perturbations.
- The paper does not address the potential negative societal impacts of the proposed method, which is a critical consideration for any machine learning application.
