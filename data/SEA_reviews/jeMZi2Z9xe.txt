 **Summary:**
The paper investigates the adversarial bandit problem with heavy-tailed losses, proposing a Follow-the-Perturbed-Leader (FTPL) algorithm to address the challenges of managing negative and unbounded losses. This algorithm aims to achieve near-optimal worst-case regret, improving upon existing FTRL-based approaches. The paper also explores two significant applications: robust settings involving adversarial bandits with heavy-tailed losses and Huber contamination, and private settings where losses are bounded and Local Differential Privacy (LDP) is guaranteed. The authors demonstrate that their algorithm achieves a polylog(T) improvement in the regret bound compared to the best-known results in these settings.

**Strengths:**
- The paper addresses an interesting problem of adversarial bandits with heavy-tailed losses, which is a challenging and significant area of research.
- The proposed FTPL algorithm is innovative and offers a novel approach to handling heavy-tailed losses in adversarial bandit settings.
- The paper is well-structured and clearly presents the problem, methodology, and results, making it accessible and understandable to readers.
- The algorithm design and analysis are technically sound, providing a theoretical guarantee that matches the lower bound up to a log factor.
- The paper explores two important applications of the FTPL algorithm, demonstrating its versatility and potential impact in different scenarios.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks empirical results, which could enhance its practical applicability and credibility.
- The comparison with state-of-the-art methods and existing algorithms is insufficient, which could undermine the perceived novelty and effectiveness of the proposed algorithm.
- The paper's technical contributions are not clearly articulated, particularly in the algorithm design and analysis sections, which could hinder understanding and reproducibility.
- There are some inconsistencies and potential errors in the proofs and lemmas, which need clarification or correction to ensure the validity of the results.
- The paper does not sufficiently discuss the limitations and potential negative societal impacts of the proposed algorithm, which is crucial for ethical considerations.
