 **Summary:**
The paper introduces Str2Str, a novel method for zero-shot protein conformation sampling, leveraging a diffusion model trained on crystal structures from the Protein Data Bank (PDB) to generate diverse conformations without relying on simulation data. The method is designed to be agnostic to energy landscapes, using a score-based annealing process that mimics simulated annealing. Str2Str is evaluated against several baselines and shown to outperform them in terms of validity, fidelity, and diversity. The method's zero-shot capability and the absence of reliance on simulation data during both training and inference are highlighted as significant advantages. However, concerns about the method's reliance on initial conformations and the potential for overfitting to the PDB dataset are noted.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel method, Str2Str, for zero-shot protein conformation sampling, which is a significant advancement in the field of protein modeling.
- Str2Str does not rely on simulation data during training or inference, which is a significant advantage over existing methods that heavily rely on target-specific simulation data.
- The method is trained on general crystal structures, making it applicable to various proteins without requiring specific data for each target.
- The paper is well-written, with clear presentation and detailed implementation, which enhances the reproducibility of the results.
- Str2Str demonstrates significant performance improvements over existing methods in terms of validity, fidelity, and diversity.
- The method's ability to generate a diverse ensemble of conformations from an initial conformation without relying on simulation data is a significant advantage.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper does not discuss the potential for overfitting to the PDB dataset, which could limit the generalizability of the method to other datasets or protein types.
- The method's reliance on an initial conformation could limit its applicability, especially in scenarios where the initial conformation is not accurate or not available.
- The paper does not provide a detailed analysis of the computational cost of Str2Str compared to other methods, which could be crucial for understanding its practical applicability.
- The paper does not include a comparison with other methods that also do not rely on simulation data during inference, which could provide a more comprehensive evaluation of the method's performance.
- The paper does not discuss the limitations of the method in detail, which could help in understanding its applicability and potential future improvements.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors provide a detailed analysis of the computational cost of Str2Str compared to other methods?
- How does the method perform when the initial conformation is not accurate or not available?
- Can the authors discuss the potential for overfitting to the PDB dataset and how this could affect the method's generalizability?
- How does the method compare with other methods that also do not rely on simulation data during inference?
- Can the authors provide more details on the limitations of the method and potential future improvements?
