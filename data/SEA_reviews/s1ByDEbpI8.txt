 **Summary:**
The paper introduces SKiC, a novel prompting strategy aimed at enhancing the compositional generalization capabilities of large language models (LLMs). SKiC prompts are structured into three parts: a set of basic skills, examples of how to compose these skills, and the problem to be solved. This approach is designed to facilitate the composition of skills for solving complex problems, which is a significant challenge in current LLMs. The paper demonstrates that SKiC prompts can effectively improve the performance of LLMs on various benchmarks, including symbolic manipulation, arithmetic operations, question answering, and math reasoning tasks. The methodology involves manually or automatically distilling skills from LLMs, which are then used to construct the SKiC prompt. Despite its promising results, the paper has been critiqued for its lack of clarity in defining "compositional generalization," potential issues with the novelty of the approach, and the need for more comprehensive evaluations and comparisons with existing methods.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel prompting strategy, SKiC, which effectively enhances the compositional generalization capabilities of large language models (LLMs).
- SKiC prompts are structured into three parts: a set of basic skills, examples of how to compose these skills, and the problem to be solved, which facilitates the composition of skills for solving complex problems.
- The methodology involves manually or automatically distilling skills from LLMs, which are then used to construct the SKiC prompt, demonstrating a practical application of the method.
- The paper provides a comprehensive evaluation of SKiC on various benchmarks, including symbolic manipulation, arithmetic operations, question answering, and math reasoning tasks, showing significant improvements over baseline methods.
- The authors provide detailed descriptions of the experimental setup, including the datasets used, the baseline methods compared, and the evaluation metrics employed, which enhances the transparency and reproducibility of the research.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a clear definition of "compositional generalization," which is crucial for understanding the scope and effectiveness of the proposed method.
- The novelty of the SKiC prompting strategy is questioned, as it appears to be a combination of existing methods like CoT and least-to-most prompting, without sufficient differentiation or justification.
- The evaluation of SKiC is limited to a few benchmarks, which may not fully demonstrate its generalization capabilities across different domains or tasks.
- The paper does not include a detailed comparison with other prompting methods or baseline models, which is necessary to establish the effectiveness and novelty of the SKiC prompting strategy.
- There is a lack of discussion on the limitations of the SKiC prompting strategy, which is essential for understanding its applicability and potential drawbacks.
- The paper could benefit from a more rigorous experimental design, including a broader range of tasks and a more detailed analysis of the results, particularly in terms of the generalization capabilities of the SKiC prompting strategy.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors provide a clearer definition of "compositional generalization" and explain how the SKiC prompting strategy specifically addresses this capability?
- How does the SKiC prompting strategy differ from existing methods like CoT and least-to-most prompting, and what specific advantages does it offer?
- Could the authors expand the evaluation of SKiC to include a broader range of tasks and domains, and provide more detailed results on the generalization capabilities of the method?
- How do the authors ensure the quality and relevance of the basic skills and examples used in the SKiC prompts, particularly in scenarios where these prompts are automatically generated?
- In the context of the SKiC prompting strategy, how do the authors address the potential issues of error propagation and the complexity of generating effective prompts?
- Could the authors discuss the limitations of the SKiC prompting strategy and provide suggestions for future research directions to address these limitations?
