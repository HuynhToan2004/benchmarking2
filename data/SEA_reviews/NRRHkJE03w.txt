 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel method for discovering conservation principles in dynamical systems using a combination of representation learning and topological analysis. The approach, termed MSAP, utilizes a neural network to approximate the shape space and learn the number of conserved quantities by minimizing the Wasserstein distance between trajectories. The method is tested on various dynamical systems, including coupled oscillators and a quantum harmonic oscillator, demonstrating its ability to identify the correct number of conserved quantities. However, the paper suffers from significant issues in clarity, presentation, and methodological detail, making it difficult to fully understand the proposed method and its contributions.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel approach to discovering conservation principles in dynamical systems, which is an important and challenging problem.
- The methodology is well-articulated, and the paper is easy to follow, with clear descriptions of the experiments and results.
- The approach is innovative, combining representation learning and topological analysis to identify conserved quantities in dynamical systems.
- The method's robustness to noise and its ability to handle complex experimental setups, such as quantum systems, are highlighted.
- The paper presents a variety of physical simulations to demonstrate the effectiveness of the method, which is a significant strength in validating the approach.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a clear explanation of the main idea and the motivation behind the proposed method, making it difficult to understand the rationale behind the approach.
- There are numerous typos and grammatical errors throughout the paper, which detract from its professionalism and clarity.
- The paper does not adequately compare its method to existing approaches, nor does it discuss the limitations and potential drawbacks of the proposed method.
- The paper is not self-contained, and important details are relegated to the supplementary material, which is not ideal for a conference submission.
- The presentation of the method and results is confusing, with unclear descriptions and figures that do not effectively illustrate the method's operation or the results.
- The paper does not discuss the computational complexity of the method, which is a significant omission for assessing its practical applicability.

**Questions:**
- Could you clarify the main idea and motivation behind the proposed method? What specific advantages does it offer over existing approaches?
- How does the method ensure the robustness to noise, and what are the implications of this for the accuracy of the results?
- Can you provide a clearer explanation of the MSAP method, particularly how it operates and what it contributes to the overall methodology?
- How does the method handle systems with non-conserved quantities, and what are the implications for the accuracy and applicability of the method?
- What are the computational requirements of the method, and how do these impact the practicality of its application in real-world scenarios?
- Could you elaborate on the choice of Wasserstein distance and its role in the methodology? How does this choice affect the method's effectiveness and applicability?
