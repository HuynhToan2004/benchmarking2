 **Summary:**
The paper introduces Consistent123, a novel method for synthesizing multiple novel views of 3D objects simultaneously. This approach leverages cross-view attention and shared self-attention mechanisms to align synthesized views with the input view, improving consistency and reducing artifacts. The method allows for the synthesis of an arbitrary number of views, enhancing its applicability across various downstream tasks such as 3D reconstruction and image-to-3D generation. Extensive experiments are conducted to demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed method, showing improvements in view consistency and synthesized image quality.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, clear, and easy to follow, making it accessible to a broad audience.
- The proposed method is technically sound, with innovative approaches such as the shared self-attention mechanism that enhances consistency and reduces artifacts.
- The method allows for the synthesis of an arbitrary number of views, which is a significant advantage over traditional methods that can only generate a fixed number of views.
- The paper provides extensive experiments and ablation studies, demonstrating the effectiveness of the proposed method and its components.
- The method is evaluated on multiple benchmarks, including Objaverse, GSO, and RTMV, and shows improvements in view consistency and synthesized image quality.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed comparison with previous works, particularly in terms of the number of views generated and the evaluation metrics used.
- The novelty of the method is questionable as it primarily builds upon existing works like Zero123 and incorporates shared self-attention mechanisms, which have been previously used in other contexts.
- The paper does not sufficiently address the limitations of the proposed method, such as the potential for overfitting due to the large number of views generated.
- There is a lack of discussion on how the method performs with different types of objects, particularly those with complex or simple shapes, which could affect the generalizability of the findings.
- The paper does not include qualitative results for the ablation studies, which could help in understanding the impact of different components of the method.
- The paper does not adequately address the computational complexity of the method, which could be a significant limitation for real-time applications.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors clarify how the method performs with different types of objects, particularly those with complex or simple shapes?
- How does the method handle objects with varying levels of detail or texture, and what are the implications for the quality of the synthesized views?
- What are the specific advantages of the shared self-attention mechanism over other types of attention mechanisms, and how does it contribute to the overall effectiveness of the method?
- Could the authors provide more details on the computational complexity of the method and discuss potential strategies for reducing computational overhead?
- How does the method handle objects with large variations in shape or pose, and what are the implications for the consistency and accuracy of the synthesized views?
- Can the authors provide qualitative results for the ablation studies to help in understanding the impact of different components of the method?
