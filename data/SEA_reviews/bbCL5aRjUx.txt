 **Summary:**
The paper introduces MONet, a novel neural network architecture that utilizes multilinear operators to replace traditional non-linearities like activation functions. MONet's core innovation is the Mu-Layer, which captures multiplicative interactions between elements of the input token through linear combinations. The architecture is tested on various benchmarks including image classification and scientific computing tasks, demonstrating competitive performance with existing models. MONet's design is inspired by polynomial networks, and it is shown to be effective in high-degree interactions and robustness against perturbations. However, concerns about the novelty of the approach and the depth of theoretical analysis are highlighted.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written and easy to follow, with a clear motivation and well-designed experiments.
- The proposed Mu-Layer is a simple yet effective design, and the overall architecture is well-designed, with a focus on multilinear operators.
- The experimental results show that the proposed method can achieve comparable performance to state-of-the-art models, and the ablation study provides insights into the effectiveness of each component.
- The model demonstrates robustness to various perturbations, which is a significant advantage over some existing models.
- The paper includes a comprehensive set of experiments, covering various tasks such as image classification, fine-grained and small-scale image classification, and scientific computing.

**Weaknesses:**
- The novelty of the proposed method is questioned as it appears to be a combination of existing ideas, with the core Mu-Layer design being similar to existing polynomial networks.
- The paper lacks a deep theoretical analysis of the MONet architecture, particularly in terms of the polynomial degree of interaction and the effectiveness of the Mu-Layer in capturing multiplicative interactions.
- The training time and computational complexity of MONet are not thoroughly discussed, which is crucial for understanding the practical implications of the model.
- The paper does not adequately address the scalability of the Mu-Layer, particularly in terms of the rank and size of the learnable matrices, which could affect the model's performance and complexity.
- The paper's experimental setup and results are sometimes inconsistent or not well-explained, which could lead to confusion or misinterpretation of the results.

**Questions:**
- Could you provide a more detailed comparison of the MONet architecture with existing models, particularly in terms of the polynomial degree of interaction and the effectiveness of the Mu-Layer in capturing multiplicative interactions?
- How does the MONet architecture compare in terms of training time and computational complexity with other models, especially when considering the high-degree interactions it captures?
- Can you clarify the role and impact of the rank and size of the learnable matrices in the Mu-Layer on the model's performance and complexity?
- How does the MONet architecture perform when applied to other tasks beyond image classification, such as object detection or semantic segmentation?
- In the ablation study, why were different datasets used for different components, and how do these variations affect the generalizability of the results?
- Can you provide more details on the experimental setup, particularly the training parameters and the impact of different hyperparameters on the model's performance?
