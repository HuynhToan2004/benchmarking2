 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel method called "AgentInstruct" that leverages a language agent to generate task-specific instructions for enhancing the zero-shot reasoning capabilities of large language models (LLMs). This method involves the agent providing detailed instructions based on web knowledge, which are then used to guide the reasoning process of the LLM. The paper claims that this approach outperforms traditional zero-shot prompting and chain-of-thought reasoning, as demonstrated through extensive experiments across various datasets. Despite its innovative approach, the paper has been criticized for its limited novelty, potential unfairness in comparisons, and lack of detailed analysis on the quality and generalizability of the generated instructions.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, clear, and easy to follow, with comprehensive experiments that demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed method across a wide range of datasets and LLMs.
- The proposed method of using an agent to generate task-specific instructions is innovative and has the potential to enhance the zero-shot reasoning capabilities of LLMs, particularly in scenarios where few-shot examples are not available.
- The paper provides extensive ablation studies and error analysis, which are useful for understanding the contributions of different components of the method and for identifying areas where the method might need improvement.
- The experimental results show that the proposed method significantly outperforms zero-shot prompting and chain-of-thought reasoning, suggesting that it could be a promising alternative for improving the reasoning abilities of LLMs.

**Weaknesses:**
- The novelty of the proposed method is limited as it primarily involves using an agent to generate task-specific instructions, which is a common approach in existing methods.
- The paper lacks a detailed analysis of the quality and generalizability of the generated instructions, which is crucial for understanding the effectiveness and reliability of the method.
- There is a potential unfairness in the comparisons made in the paper, as the proposed method might have an unfair advantage due to the use of GPT-4 as the agent, which could provide more accurate and comprehensive instructions than other models.
- The paper does not sufficiently discuss the limitations and potential negative societal impacts of the proposed method, which could be a significant concern given the potential applications of the method.
- The experimental setup and results presentation could be improved, particularly in terms of clarity and detail, especially in the presentation of results and comparisons.

**Questions:**
- Can you provide more details on how the quality of the generated instructions is ensured, especially in scenarios where the agent might provide incorrect or misleading information?
- How does the proposed method compare to other methods that use similar approaches, such as those that use agents to generate task-specific instructions?
- Could you elaborate on the potential negative societal impacts of the proposed method and how these might be mitigated?
- How does the proposed method handle the potential issue of GPT-4's superior performance in generating task-specific instructions, and what steps are taken to ensure a fair comparison with other models?
- Could you provide more details on the experimental setup, particularly how the agent's instructions are integrated into the reasoning process of the LLM, and how the results are presented to ensure clarity and transparency?
