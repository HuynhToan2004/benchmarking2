 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel generative diffusion-guided framework for semantic communication, aimed at reducing bandwidth usage by transmitting compressed semantic information. The framework leverages diffusion models to synthesize semantically consistent images from semantic information, demonstrating effectiveness in various scenarios including low-quality channel conditions. The methodology involves training the diffusion model with noisy semantic maps and incorporating a fast denoising semantic block to improve image quality. The paper claims that this approach outperforms existing solutions in terms of generating high-quality images with preserved semantic information. However, the paper's novelty is questioned due to its similarity to existing works, and the experimental results, while showing improvements, are not convincingly superior.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel generative diffusion-guided framework for semantic communication, which is a significant advancement in the field.
- The framework effectively utilizes semantic information to synthesize semantically consistent images, which is a promising approach for reducing bandwidth usage in communication systems.
- The paper is well-written, making it easy to follow and understand the proposed methodologies and their implementation.
- The experimental results demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed method in various scenarios, particularly in noisy channel conditions, which is crucial for real-world applications.
- The use of a diffusion model for semantic image synthesis is innovative and shows potential for improving the quality of generated images.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a comprehensive literature review, particularly in the area of semantic communication, which limits the understanding of the current state of the art and the novelty of the proposed framework.
- The experimental results do not convincingly outperform existing methods, especially in terms of metrics like FID and LPIPS, which are crucial for assessing the quality of generated images.
- The paper does not provide a detailed comparison with state-of-the-art methods, which is essential for establishing the superiority of the proposed framework.
- There is a lack of discussion on the computational efficiency of the proposed framework, which is crucial for practical applications in real-world scenarios.
- The paper does not adequately address the limitations of the proposed framework, which is necessary for a comprehensive understanding of its applicability and potential improvements.
- The paper lacks a detailed analysis of the proposed FDS block, which is a critical component of the framework.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors provide a more detailed comparison with state-of-the-art methods to establish the superiority of the proposed framework?
- How does the proposed framework perform in terms of computational efficiency, especially in terms of sampling time, energy consumption, and transmission latency?
- Can the authors provide a detailed analysis of the FDS block to understand its effectiveness and potential limitations?
- How does the proposed framework handle different types of noise in the communication channel, and what are the implications for the quality of the generated images?
- Could the authors clarify the role and effectiveness of the noisy training in the proposed framework, and how it impacts the performance of the diffusion model?
- Is there a potential for the proposed framework to be adapted for other types of data beyond images, such as audio or video?
