 **Summary:**
The paper investigates adaptive regret minimization in the context of multi-armed bandits and bandit convex optimization, focusing on the query complexity of the algorithms. It proposes a novel algorithm, StABL, which achieves adaptive regret with only two queries per round, improving upon the previous best of O(log log T) queries. The algorithm uses a meta-learning approach to aggregate the results of multiple base learners, each with a history length that corresponds to the length of the interval in which it is optimized. The paper also extends this approach to bandit convex optimization, achieving a regret bound of O(I), which is independent of the condition number. Theoretical results are supported by numerical experiments demonstrating the algorithm's effectiveness in practical scenarios.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel algorithm, StABL, which achieves adaptive regret with significantly fewer queries (O(2)) compared to previous methods (O(log log T).
- The algorithm's design is innovative, using a meta-learning approach to aggregate the results of multiple base learners, each with a history length that corresponds to the length of the interval in which it is optimized.
- The paper is well-written, with clear and detailed explanations of the algorithm's design and its theoretical underpinnings.
- The experimental results demonstrate the effectiveness of the algorithm in practical scenarios, showing that it can adapt to changing environments and recover after the best arm has changed.
- The paper provides a lower bound for the problem, which is a significant contribution to the field.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper could benefit from a more detailed discussion on the limitations and potential negative societal impacts of the proposed algorithms.
- The computational cost of the algorithm, especially the need to run multiple instances of the base learner in parallel, is not adequately addressed.
- The proofs and some of the assumptions made (e.g., the assumption that the rewards are bounded) are not clearly explained or justified, which could limit the generalizability of the results.
- There are some minor errors and inconsistencies in the proofs and the notation used, which need to be corrected.
- The paper does not discuss the impact of different query models on the results, which could be a significant limitation in practical applications.

**Questions:**
- How does the computational cost of the algorithm compare to other methods, especially in terms of the number of queries required?
- Can the algorithm be adapted to handle different query models, such as those that do not require the same number of queries per round?
- How does the algorithm perform when the rewards are not bounded, and what are the implications of this assumption on the results?
- Is there a way to modify the algorithm to reduce the dependence on the condition number in the regret bound?
- Can the authors clarify the assumptions and proofs, particularly in Lemma 2 and the proof of Theorem 1, to make the results more robust and generalizable?
