 **Summary:**
The paper introduces HuRef, a novel method for fingerprinting large language models (LLMs) without revealing their model weights. HuRef identifies a stable vector direction in the LLM's parameters that remains invariant across various training procedures, which is then transformed into a human-readable image using a convolutional encoder and StyleGAN2. The method is designed to be robust against common attacks, such as linear mapping and permutation, and has been tested across a range of LLMs. Despite its innovative approach, the paper has been critiqued for its lack of rigorous empirical validation, insufficient theoretical grounding, and potential misuse in unauthorized training.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel approach to fingerprinting LLMs, which is an important area for safeguarding intellectual property and preventing unauthorized use of LLMs.
- The methodology is innovative, utilizing invariant terms and a convolutional encoder to map these terms to a human-readable image, which is a unique approach to the problem.
- The paper is well-written, clear, and easy to follow, making it accessible to a broad audience.
- The proposed method is robust against common attacks and has been tested across a range of LLMs, demonstrating its effectiveness and versatility.
- The paper provides a comprehensive evaluation of the proposed method, showing its effectiveness in various scenarios.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks rigorous empirical validation, particularly in terms of the stability of the proposed vector direction across different training procedures and the robustness of the invariant terms against various attacks.
- The theoretical grounding of the method is insufficient, with a lack of detailed explanation on how the invariant terms are derived and how they relate to the overall methodology.
- The method's reliance on specific token embeddings for the invariant terms may limit its applicability to LLMs with different tokenization schemes.
- The paper does not sufficiently address the potential misuse of the method, such as using it for unauthorized training or for malicious purposes.
- There is a lack of comparison with existing watermarking methods for LLMs, which could provide a better understanding of the proposed method's advantages and limitations.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors provide more details on how the invariant terms are derived and how they relate to the overall methodology?
- How stable is the proposed vector direction across different training procedures, and how robust are the invariant terms against various attacks?
- Could the authors provide a more detailed explanation of the experimental setup, particularly the training steps and settings for each LLM used in the experiments?
- How does the method perform when applied to LLMs with different tokenization schemes, and what are the implications for the stability and effectiveness of the invariant terms?
- Could the authors clarify the potential misuse of the method and discuss any measures that could be taken to prevent unauthorized use?
- Would it be possible to include a comparison with existing watermarking methods for LLMs to better understand the advantages and limitations of the proposed method?
