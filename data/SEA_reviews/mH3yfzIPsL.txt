 **Summary:**
The paper introduces XTSFormer, a transformer-based model designed for irregularly timed event data prediction, addressing the challenges of cyclical nature and multi-scale interactions in event sequences. It incorporates a Feature-based Cycle-aware Positional Encoding (FCPE) and a hierarchical multi-scale temporal attention mechanism to capture complex temporal patterns. The model's effectiveness is demonstrated through experiments on various datasets, showing improvements over existing methods. However, concerns were raised about the novelty and depth of the experimental validation, particularly in terms of dataset diversity and comparison with more recent and relevant models.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, clear, and easy to follow, with a clear motivation and problem statement.
- The proposed model, XTSFormer, is novel and addresses the challenges of irregularly timed event data by incorporating a Feature-based Cycle-aware Positional Encoding (FCPE) and a hierarchical multi-scale temporal attention mechanism.
- The experiments conducted on various datasets demonstrate the effectiveness of XTSFormer, showing improvements over existing methods.
- The paper provides a detailed explanation of the model and its components, including the FCPE and multi-scale temporal attention mechanism, which are novel contributions.
- The time cost analysis is included, which helps in understanding the computational efficiency of the proposed model.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed discussion on the limitations and assumptions of the model, which could affect the generalizability and applicability of the findings.
- The novelty of the work is questioned as the concept of multi-scale attention and hierarchical clustering for event sequences has been previously explored.
- The experimental validation is not comprehensive enough, with only a limited number of datasets used, and the comparison with more recent and relevant models such as Pyraformer and LogTrans is missing.
- There is a lack of clarity in the presentation of the model, particularly in the explanation of the FCPE and the multi-scale temporal attention mechanism.
- The paper does not provide sufficient detail on the datasets used, such as the number of events, time intervals, and event types, which could affect the reproducibility and evaluation of the model.
- The paper does not adequately address the computational cost and scalability of the model, which could limit its practical application in real-world scenarios.

**Questions:**
- Could you provide more details on the datasets used, including the number of events, time intervals, and event types, to better understand the complexity and diversity of the data?
- How does the model handle noisy or incomplete data, and what are the implications for the model's performance in such scenarios?
- Can you clarify the computational cost and scalability of the model, particularly in terms of the time and memory requirements for training and inference?
- How does the model perform when compared to more recent and relevant models such as Pyraformer and LogTrans, and what are the key differences in terms of performance and efficiency?
- Could you provide more details on the limitations and assumptions of the model, and how these might affect the generalizability and applicability of the findings?
- Can you provide a more detailed explanation of the FCPE and the multi-scale temporal attention mechanism, including the mathematical formulations and intuitive explanations behind these components?
