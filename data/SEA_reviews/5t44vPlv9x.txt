 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel approach for generating human avatars using a NeRF-based method that integrates a Graph Neural Network (GNN) to model correlations among body parts and a frequency modulation network to enhance texture details. The method employs a learnable window function to aggregate features from all parts, which is then used to compute frequency coefficients for each bone. The approach aims to improve the synthesis quality and generalization capabilities by integrating pose-dependent frequency modulation, which is a first in this context. The paper provides extensive experimental results, including ablation studies, and claims to outperform state-of-the-art methods. However, concerns were raised about the clarity of the methodological contributions, the novelty of the approach, and the experimental comparisons, particularly in terms of computational efficiency and the generalization to out-of-distribution poses.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, clear, and easy to follow, with a clear presentation of the proposed method.
- The use of a frequency modulation network to enhance the texture details of the avatar is innovative and effectively demonstrated through visual results.
- The method outperforms state-of-the-art methods in terms of synthesis quality and generalization capabilities, as evidenced by extensive experimental results and ablation studies.
- The integration of pose-dependent frequency modulation is a novel approach, which is a significant contribution to the field.
- The paper provides a thorough evaluation and ablation studies, which delve into the importance of various components of the proposed method.
- The method is computationally efficient, which is crucial for real-time applications.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a clear explanation of the technical novelty and the specific contributions of the proposed method compared to existing works, particularly in terms of frequency modulation and its integration with pose context.
- The experimental comparisons are limited, and the paper does not adequately compare the proposed method with other recent works, such as MonoHuman and Vid2Avatar, which could provide a more robust evaluation.
- There is a lack of discussion on the limitations of the proposed method, which is crucial for understanding its applicability and potential improvements.
- The paper does not provide a detailed comparison in terms of computational efficiency, which is a significant factor in practical applications.
- The method's generalization to out-of-distribution poses is not thoroughly evaluated, which is a critical aspect for the practical applicability of the proposed method.
- The paper does not sufficiently discuss the integration of pose-dependent frequency modulation with the GNN, which is a key component of the method.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors provide a more detailed explanation of the technical novelty and the specific contributions of the proposed method compared to existing works?
- How does the proposed method handle the generalization to out-of-distribution poses, and what are the limitations in this regard?
- Could the authors provide a more detailed comparison with other recent works, such as MonoHuman and Vid2Avatar, to better understand the performance of the proposed method?
- What are the limitations of the proposed method, and how can these be addressed in future work?
- How does the proposed method compare in terms of computational efficiency with other state-of-the-art methods?
- Can the authors provide a more detailed discussion on the integration of pose-dependent frequency modulation with the GNN, particularly in terms of its impact on the method's performance and efficiency?
