 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel method for learning cross-modal representations by focusing on the alignment of abstract concepts in vision and language models. It proposes a data mining procedure to extract intrinsic information from datasets, framing model inputs as triplets to enhance the understanding of abstract semantics in images. Two pre-training objectives, Matched Concept Prediction (MCP) and Matched Concept Contrastive (MCC), are introduced to facilitate concept-level alignment. The method is evaluated on various benchmarks, showing improvements over existing models. However, the paper lacks detailed comparisons with state-of-the-art methods and fails to address the computational efficiency of the proposed method.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, clear, and easy to follow, with a comprehensive review of related work.
- The proposed method is intuitive and well-motivated, addressing the challenge of aligning abstract concepts in vision and language models.
- The methodology includes a data mining procedure to extract intrinsic information from datasets, which is a novel approach to learning cross-modal representations.
- The paper demonstrates the effectiveness of the proposed method through extensive experiments and analysis, showing improvements in various benchmarks.
- The concept-centric pre-training objectives are well-designed and effective, contributing to the advancement of vision-language pre-training.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed comparison with state-of-the-art methods, particularly in terms of computational efficiency and the effectiveness of the proposed method in handling abstract concepts.
- The paper does not provide sufficient details on the implementation of the method, such as the specifics of the data mining procedure and the computational resources required.
- There is a lack of discussion on the limitations of the proposed method and potential negative societal impacts.
- The experimental results are not sufficiently convincing, with marginal improvements over existing methods in some cases.
- The paper could benefit from more comprehensive evaluations on different datasets and a more thorough discussion on the computational efficiency and scalability of the proposed method.

**Questions:**
- How does the proposed method compare in terms of computational efficiency with existing methods, especially in handling abstract concepts?
- Can the authors provide more details on the implementation of the data mining procedure, including the specifics of the database construction and the computational resources required?
- How does the proposed method handle the issue of abstract concepts that are not easily discernible in visual data?
- Could the authors provide more examples or case studies to illustrate the effectiveness of the proposed method in handling abstract concepts?
- How does the proposed method perform in zero-shot scenarios, and what are the implications for its practical application?
- Could the authors elaborate on the potential negative societal impacts of their work and how they plan to mitigate these risks?
