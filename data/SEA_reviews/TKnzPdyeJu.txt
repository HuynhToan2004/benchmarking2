 **Summary:**
The paper introduces SIDEC, a novel method for structural inference in dynamical systems, leveraging a variational dynamics encoder (VDE) to encode node dynamics into latent variables. These latent variables are then used to compute partial correlation coefficients (PCOR), which are used to infer directed edges based on temporal information. SIDEC demonstrates scalability and robustness across various datasets, including those with Gaussian noise. The method's performance is evaluated against several baselines and shows superior results in terms of scalability and accuracy. However, concerns were raised about the novelty of the approach, as it appears to be a combination of existing methods without significant innovation.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written and easy to follow, making complex concepts accessible to a broader audience.
- The proposed method, SIDEC, is a novel approach that integrates a variational dynamics encoder (VDE) with partial correlation coefficients (PCOR) to infer directed graphs from trajectory data.
- The method is scalable and can handle both one-dimensional and multi-dimensional features, making it applicable to various real-world scenarios.
- The paper includes a comprehensive evaluation against several baselines and demonstrates robustness against Gaussian noise, showcasing the method's effectiveness in practical applications.
- The use of VDE to encode node dynamics into latent variables and extract essential information from node features is innovative and provides a new perspective on structural inference.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed discussion on the limitations of the proposed method, which could help in understanding its applicability and potential drawbacks.
- The novelty of the method is questioned as it appears to be a combination of existing methods without significant innovation.
- The paper does not provide sufficient experimental results or comparisons with other methods, which could undermine the claims of superior performance.
- The use of synthetic data for evaluation may not adequately reflect the method's performance in real-world scenarios.
- The paper does not include a detailed analysis of the computational complexity or the scalability of the method, which could be critical for practical applications.
- The paper lacks a discussion on how the method performs with different types of noise or in scenarios where the dynamics are not Markovian, which could limit its applicability.
