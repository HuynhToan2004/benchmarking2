 **Summary:**
The paper introduces EventRPG, a novel data augmentation method for spiking neural networks (SNNs) designed to enhance the performance of event-based object and action recognition. EventRPG leverages relevance propagation to generate saliency maps, which are then utilized for guided data augmentation strategies such as RPGDrop and RPGMix. These strategies aim to increase the diversity and relevance of augmented samples, thereby improving model performance. The methodology includes the development of two novel relevance propagation rules, SLRP and SLTRP, which are used to generate saliency maps and class activation maps. The paper presents extensive experimental results demonstrating the effectiveness of EventRPG across various SNN structures and datasets, showcasing improvements in object and action recognition tasks.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel data augmentation method for event-based datasets, which is crucial for addressing the challenges of overfitting in event-based classification tasks.
- The proposed method, EventRPG, is technically sound and presents a clear, well-structured approach to data augmentation using relevance propagation and saliency maps.
- Extensive experimental results are provided, demonstrating the effectiveness of EventRPG across various SNN structures and datasets, showcasing improvements in object and action recognition tasks.
- The paper is well-written, making it accessible and easy to follow, which is essential for a broad audience.
- The introduction of Spiking Layer-Time-wise Relevance Propagation and Spiking Layer-wise Relevance Propagation rules is innovative and potentially impactful for the field of SNNs.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed comparison with existing data augmentation methods specifically designed for event-based datasets, which could have provided a more robust validation of the proposed method.
- There is a notable absence of discussion on the limitations of the proposed method, which could have provided valuable insights into its applicability and potential drawbacks.
- The paper does not include a detailed analysis of the computational complexity or time consumption of the proposed method, which is crucial for understanding its practical implications.
- The experimental results are somewhat limited in scope, focusing primarily on object and action recognition tasks, which might not fully demonstrate the generalizability of the method.
- The paper could benefit from a more comprehensive discussion on the theoretical underpinnings of the proposed method, particularly in relation to the biological plausibility of the spiking relevance propagation rules.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors provide a more detailed comparison with existing data augmentation methods specifically designed for event-based datasets?
- How does the proposed method compare in terms of computational complexity and time consumption with other data augmentation methods?
- Could the authors discuss the limitations of the proposed method and provide insights into its applicability and potential drawbacks?
- In the context of the experimental results, could the authors provide a more detailed analysis of the improvements achieved by EventRPG compared to other methods?
- Could the authors elaborate on the biological plausibility of the spiking relevance propagation rules and their implications for the field of SNNs?
