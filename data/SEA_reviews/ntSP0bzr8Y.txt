 **Summary:**
The paper introduces PowerGPT, a large-scale foundation model for electricity time series (ETS) data, designed to capture long-term temporal dependencies and hierarchical correlations. PowerGPT is pre-trained on a large-scale ETS dataset and is capable of generalizing to various downstream tasks such as forecasting, missing value imputation, and anomaly detection. The model's performance is validated through extensive experiments and comparisons with state-of-the-art methods. However, concerns are raised about the novelty and originality of the model, as it primarily adapts existing techniques without significant innovation. The paper's presentation also suffers from clarity issues and lacks a thorough discussion on the limitations and potential societal impacts of the model.

**Strengths:**
- PowerGPT is the largest foundation model in the field of power systems, pre-trained on a large-scale electricity time series (ETS) dataset, providing a generic model for power systems.
- PowerGPT is the first to attend to long-term temporal dependency, heterogeneity patterns of different instances, and capture hierarchical correlation across different instances in ETS data.
- The model's design effectively addresses the challenges of ETS data, such as long-term temporal dependency, heterogeneity patterns of different instances, and hierarchical correlation across different instances.
- Extensive experiments demonstrate the generalization ability of PowerGPT, showing its potential in ETS data modeling.
- The paper is well-written, making it easy to follow, and the authors provide a comprehensive evaluation of the model's performance on various downstream tasks.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a thorough discussion on the limitations of the model, which could help in understanding the scope and applicability of PowerGPT.
- There is a significant lack of novelty in the model design, as it primarily adapts existing techniques without significant innovation.
- The paper does not adequately discuss the potential societal impacts of PowerGPT, which could be crucial for understanding the broader implications of the model.
- The presentation of the paper is not clear, making it difficult to follow, and the figures and tables are not well-designed, which could affect the readability and comprehension of the results.
- The paper does not provide sufficient details on the construction of the hierarchical graph, which is a crucial component of the model's functionality.
- The paper does not discuss the limitations of the proposed model, which could help in understanding the scope and applicability of PowerGPT.

**Questions:**
- Could you provide more details on the construction of the hierarchical graph, including the data sources and processing methods used?
- How does the model handle the complex and dynamic nature of electricity time series data, which can be influenced by various factors such as weather, energy policy, and consumer behavior?
- Could you clarify the novelty of the model design and discuss how it differs from existing methods in terms of functionality and performance?
- How does the model perform on ETS data from different regions or countries, and what are the implications of using data from different sources or domains?
- Could you discuss the potential societal impacts of PowerGPT, particularly in terms of privacy, security, and ethical considerations?
- How does the model handle the issue of missing data in electricity time series, and what are the implications of using incomplete data for model training and prediction?
- Could you provide more details on the data preprocessing steps, including data cleaning, normalization, and feature extraction, which are crucial for the model's performance?
