 **Summary:**
The paper investigates the advantages of distributional reinforcement learning (RL) over expectation-based RL, focusing on the Categorical Distributional RL (CDRL) algorithm. It introduces a risk-sensitive entropy regularization that enhances exploration and reduces uncertainty, comparing it to the entropy regularization in MaxEnt RL. The authors propose a new algorithm, DERAC, which interpolates between expectation-based and distributional RL, showing potential improvements in exploration and performance. The paper includes theoretical analysis and experimental results, demonstrating the benefits of risk-sensitive regularization in distributional RL. However, the paper has been critiqued for its clarity and organization, with some sections being difficult to follow, and the experimental results not convincingly outperforming existing methods.

**Strengths:**
- The paper provides a novel analysis of distributional RL, particularly focusing on CDRL, which is both theoretically sound and practically applicable.
- It introduces a new algorithm, DERAC, which interpolates between expectation-based and distributional RL, offering a new approach to combining these methods.
- The paper is well-written, making complex concepts accessible and understandable, with clear explanations and logical arguments.
- Theoretical results are well-presented, supported by rigorous proofs, and are highly relevant to the field of distributional RL.
- The paper includes a thorough comparison of distributional RL with expectation-based RL, providing valuable insights into the benefits of distributional RL.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper suffers from poor organization and lacks clarity in some sections, making it difficult to follow and understand the main contributions and arguments.
- The experimental results are not convincingly superior to existing methods, and the paper lacks a detailed discussion on the hyperparameters used in the experiments.
- There are numerous typos and grammatical errors throughout the paper, which detract from its professionalism and readability.
- The paper does not adequately discuss the limitations of its approach, particularly the potential negative impacts of risk-sensitive entropy regularization on exploration and performance.
- The paper could benefit from more comprehensive experimental validation, including a broader range of environments and more detailed comparisons with existing methods.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors clarify the role and impact of the hyperparameter λ in the DERAC algorithm? How does it affect the interpolation between expectation-based and distributional RL?
- How does the risk-sensitive entropy regularization in DERAC compare to other regularization methods, such as those used in SAC?
- Could the authors provide more detailed experimental results, including standard deviations and a more thorough discussion of the experimental setup and results?
- In light of the concerns about the clarity and organization of the paper, could the authors consider reorganizing the content to make the main contributions and arguments clearer and more accessible?
- How does the paper address the potential negative impacts of risk-sensitive entropy regularization on exploration and performance? Could more experiments be conducted to explore these trade-offs?
