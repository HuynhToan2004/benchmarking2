 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel approach for training physics-informed neural networks (PINNs) by incorporating functional a posteriori error estimates into the loss function. This approach, termed Astral loss, is designed to optimize the prediction of the error majorant, which is used as a surrogate for the error itself. The Astral loss is demonstrated to be more effective than traditional residual and variational losses in terms of stability and error estimation. The paper also explores the application of Astral loss in supervised and unsupervised settings, showing improvements in error estimation and network performance. The methodology is validated through experiments on elliptic and convection-diffusion problems, demonstrating the practical utility of the Astral loss in real-world applications.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, with clear explanations of the concepts and a clear structure that aids in understanding the proposed methods.
- The proposed Astral loss is innovative, combining error majorants with neural networks to provide a novel approach to loss functions.
- The methodology is supported by rigorous experiments that demonstrate the effectiveness of Astral loss over traditional residual and variational losses.
- The paper is significant in its contribution to the field of neural network training for physics-informed problems, offering a potentially more effective and robust method for training neural networks.
- The paper is well-organized, with clear figures and tables that aid in understanding the experimental results and methodologies.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed discussion on the computational complexity and efficiency of the Astral loss, which could be a significant concern for practical applications.
- The experimental results, while promising, are somewhat limited in scope, focusing primarily on elliptic and convection-diffusion problems. It would be beneficial to see how the Astral loss performs on other types of PDEs.
- The paper does not sufficiently address the issue of overfitting, which is a known challenge in neural network training, especially when using large datasets.
- There is a need for more comprehensive comparisons with other existing methods, such as the Deep Ritz method, to fully establish the superiority of Astral loss.
- The paper could benefit from a more detailed explanation of the theoretical underpinnings of the Astral loss, particularly in how it relates to the error majorant and the optimization of the neural network weights.

**Questions:**
- Can you provide more details on the computational complexity and efficiency of the Astral loss compared to traditional residual and variational losses?
- How does the Astral loss perform on other types of PDEs, such as those involving hyperbolic or parabolic equations?
- Could you elaborate on the choice of the error majorant as the loss function and how it impacts the training of the neural network?
- How does the Astral loss handle the issue of overfitting, especially when using large datasets?
- Could you provide more details on the theoretical justification for using the error majorant as the loss function, particularly in terms of its impact on the optimization of the neural network weights?
- How do the authors ensure that the Astral loss is not overly sensitive to the choice of parameters, such as the regularization parameter beta?
