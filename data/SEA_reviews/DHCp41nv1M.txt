 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel method for video recovery through scattering media using a spatio-temporal diffusion model. The proposed method leverages a closed-form forward model derived from the shower-curtain effect in a dynamic scattering medium, which is used to maximize the statistical correlation between a series of frames and a series of scattered signals. The method extends the diffusion posterior sampling scheme to include temporal attention mechanisms, allowing for more accurate and temporally consistent video reconstruction. The approach is evaluated on two datasets, showing improved performance over existing methods. However, the paper suffers from a lack of detailed experimental validation and comparison with other state-of-the-art methods, which limits the clarity of its contributions.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, with clear and concise descriptions of the proposed method and its implementation.
- The use of diffusion models for video recovery through scattering media is innovative and addresses a significant challenge in the field of imaging through scattering.
- The methodology is technically sound, with a novel application of diffusion models to a new problem, and the paper provides a closed-form forward model which is crucial for the proposed method.
- The experimental results show the effectiveness of the proposed method in reconstructing videos, particularly in scenarios with high scattering.
- The paper provides a detailed explanation of the proposed method, including the closed-form forward model and the use of temporal attention in the diffusion model.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks detailed experimental validation, particularly in terms of the influence of different parameters on the reconstruction quality and the robustness of the method to variations in scattering conditions.
- There is a lack of comparison with other state-of-the-art methods for video recovery through scattering media, which could provide a better understanding of the method's advantages and limitations.
- The paper does not discuss the limitations of the method, such as its applicability to different types of scattering media or its performance in scenarios with varying levels of scattering.
- The paper could benefit from a more thorough analysis of the physical implications of the forward model and the assumptions made in its derivation.
- The experimental setup and results are not sufficiently detailed, making it difficult to assess the practical applicability and effectiveness of the proposed method.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors provide more details on the experimental setup, such as the size of the input frames, the number of diffusion steps used, and the specific parameters of the scattering medium?
- How does the proposed method perform in scenarios with different types of scattering media, such as fog or biological tissues?
- Could the authors discuss the limitations of the method and potential avenues for future research to address these limitations?
- How does the method handle variations in the scattering medium, such as changes in thickness or density?
- Can the authors provide a more detailed comparison with other state-of-the-art methods for video recovery through scattering media, including both qualitative and quantitative results?
- How does the method perform in scenarios with different levels of scattering, and what are the practical implications of these findings?
