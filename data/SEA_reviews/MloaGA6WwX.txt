 **Summary:**
The paper introduces TADRED, a method for experimental design in imaging that optimizes the selection of image channels to reduce acquisition time and costs. TADRED utilizes a dual-network architecture with a scoring network and a task network to iteratively select informative channels and train a machine learning model to execute the task using the selected channels. The method is tested across various imaging modalities including MRI and hyperspectral imaging, demonstrating improvements over classical experimental design and recent application-specific methods. However, the paper lacks a thorough discussion on the limitations and societal impacts of the proposed method, and the presentation of results and methodology could be clearer.
