 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel approach for computing the barycenter of a set of probability distributions with respect to an arbitrary cost function, using a dual formulation based on weak optimal transport. This method leverages energy-based models (EBMs) to optimize the dual function, providing a closed-form expression for the gradient of the objective function with respect to the parameters of the EBM. Theoretical contributions include a dual formulation of the barycenter problem, generalization bounds, and universal approximation results using neural networks. The method is evaluated on both low-dimensional and high-dimensional datasets, demonstrating its applicability and potential in various settings.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, clearly presenting the methodology and results, with a clear motivation for the use of the proposed method.
- The theoretical results are sound, and the paper provides a dual formulation of the EOT barycenter problem, which is crucial for understanding the problem's structure and behavior.
- The use of energy-based models (EBMs) for computing the barycenter of probability distributions is innovative and provides a novel approach to the problem.
- The paper includes a detailed comparison with existing methods, highlighting the advantages and limitations of the proposed method.
- The experimental results are convincing, demonstrating the effectiveness of the proposed method in practical scenarios.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a thorough comparison with existing methods, particularly in terms of computational efficiency and the quality of the barycenters produced.
- There is a lack of discussion on the limitations and potential negative societal impacts of the proposed method.
- The paper does not sufficiently address the scalability of the method, especially in high-dimensional settings.
- The paper could benefit from a more detailed discussion on the practical implications of the assumptions made, such as the availability of samples from the distributions and the regularization parameter.
- There are some minor typographical and formatting errors that need correction.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors clarify the meaning of "unconditional barycenter" and its relevance to the methodology described?
- How does the proposed method compare in terms of computational efficiency and quality of barycenters produced with other existing methods, particularly in high-dimensional settings?
- Could the authors provide a more detailed discussion on the practical implications of the assumptions made in the method, especially regarding the availability of samples from the distributions and the choice of the regularization parameter?
- Is there a possibility to include a discussion on the limitations and potential negative societal impacts of the proposed method?
- Could the authors elaborate on the choice of the MCMC algorithm used and consider using more efficient algorithms to potentially improve the method's performance?
