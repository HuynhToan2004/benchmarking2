 **Summary:**
The paper introduces V-Former, an offline reinforcement learning method designed to handle narrow data distributions and different control frequencies, particularly suited for robotic applications. It leverages a Transformer-based policy to learn from temporally extended actions, which are considered more efficient and effective in robotic scenarios where data distribution can be challenging. The method is evaluated on various robotic manipulation tasks and demonstrates improved performance over existing baselines. However, concerns about the novelty and the practical applicability of the method, especially in more complex robotic environments, are raised.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, making it easy to follow, and the idea of using sequence models to model temporally extended actions is innovative and makes sense for offline RL.
- The method is well-motivated and well-designed, with a good ablation study showing the impact of different design choices.
- The paper is well-structured and easy to read, making it accessible to readers.
- The method's effectiveness is demonstrated through extensive experiments, and the paper provides a good discussion on the limitations of the method.
- The authors have made the code and datasets available, which enhances the reproducibility of the research.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed discussion on the differences between the proposed method and existing methods, such as those using action repetition or skill extraction, which also handle non-Markovian behaviors.
- There is a lack of experimental evidence to support the claim that the proposed method can handle narrow data distributions effectively.
- The paper does not provide a detailed discussion on the limitations of the method, which is crucial for understanding its applicability and scope.
- The method's practical applicability is questionable, as it assumes that the agent can take multiple actions at once, which might not be feasible in real-world robotic applications.
- The paper could benefit from more detailed explanations and discussions on the methodology and experimental results, particularly in the context of robotic applications.

**Questions:**
- How does the proposed method compare to other methods that use action repetition or skill extraction in terms of handling non-Markovian behaviors?
- Can the authors provide more detailed experimental results, especially in more complex robotic environments, to demonstrate the practical applicability of the method?
- How does the method perform when the data distribution is not narrow, and what are the implications of this on the method's effectiveness?
- Could the authors discuss the limitations of the method in more detail, particularly in terms of its practical applicability and scalability?
- How does the method handle the issue of action repetition, and what are the implications of this for the method's effectiveness in real-world applications?
