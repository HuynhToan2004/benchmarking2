 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel unsupervised fine-tuning method for vision-language models (VLMs) using CLIP, specifically focusing on enhancing generalization and out-of-distribution (OOD) detection capabilities. The proposed method, Universal Entropy Optimization (UEO), leverages sample-level confidence to optimize the entropy of confident and less confident instances. The approach is validated across multiple datasets and settings, showing improvements over existing methods in both ID generalization and OOD detection. However, the paper has been critiqued for its limited novelty and the need for more comprehensive comparisons with existing methods.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written and easy to follow, with a clear introduction and motivation.
- The proposed method is simple yet effective, and the results demonstrate improvements over existing methods.
- The paper addresses an important problem in the field of vision-language models (VLMs) and provides a novel approach to unsupervised fine-tuning.
- The methodology is well-motivated and the paper provides extensive evaluations across various datasets, demonstrating the effectiveness of the proposed method.
- The paper is well-organized, with clear figures and tables that aid in understanding the results.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a thorough comparison with existing methods, particularly in the area of OOD detection and generalization.
- The novelty of the proposed method is questioned as it builds upon existing works such as DANCE and InfoMax, with limited new insights or techniques.
- The paper does not adequately address the limitations of the proposed method, such as its applicability to other VLMs beyond CLIP and its scalability to larger datasets.
- There is a lack of discussion on the computational cost of the proposed method compared to existing methods.
- The paper could benefit from a more detailed analysis of the results, particularly in explaining the performance of the proposed method in different scenarios.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors provide a more detailed comparison with existing methods, particularly in terms of the proposed method's effectiveness in OOD detection and generalization?
- How does the proposed method perform when applied to other VLMs, such as BLIP, and what are the implications for its scalability and applicability?
- Could the authors provide a more detailed discussion on the limitations of the proposed method and potential directions for future research?
- How does the proposed method compare in terms of computational cost and training time with existing methods?
- The paper mentions the use of different weights for in-distribution and out-of-distribution samples. Could the authors provide more details on how these weights are determined and their impact on the results?
