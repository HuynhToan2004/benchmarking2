 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel method for integrating constraints into deep generative models (DGMs) for tabular data, specifically addressing the issue of DGMs not adhering to constraints. The proposed Constraint Layer (CL) is designed to ensure that generated samples comply with given constraints, improving model performance and utility. The paper demonstrates that the CL can be seamlessly integrated with DGMs, showing that it can enhance the model's ability to generate realistic data while maintaining the constraints. The approach is validated through extensive experiments on various datasets, showing that the method can outperform standard DGMs in terms of utility and detection.

**Strengths:**
- The paper addresses a significant problem in the field of deep generative models (DGMs) by focusing on the integration of constraints, which is crucial for ensuring the quality and realism of generated data.
- The proposed method is innovative, providing a novel approach to integrating constraints into DGMs, which is supported by extensive experiments and theoretical proofs.
- The paper is well-written, making it easy to follow and understand, and includes clear demonstrations of the method's effectiveness through experiments on various datasets.
- The method's ability to enforce constraints at inference time without requiring retraining is highlighted as a practical advantage.
- The paper provides a detailed theoretical analysis, including proofs that the proposed method ensures constraints are met, and shows that the generated samples are optimal with respect to the constraints.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper's experimental validation could be enhanced by including more diverse datasets and comparing the proposed method with a broader range of existing methods, including those that handle different types of constraints.
- The paper's focus on linear constraints might limit its applicability to more complex or non-linear constraints, which could be a significant limitation in real-world applications.
- The method's reliance on linear constraints might not be practical in scenarios where constraints are not linearly separable, which could limit its applicability.
- The paper could benefit from a more detailed discussion on the practical implications of the method, particularly in terms of its scalability and performance with larger or more complex datasets.
- The method's integration into existing DGMs might require significant modifications, which could affect the models' performance and training efficiency.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors clarify how the proposed method handles non-linear constraints, and discuss the implications of this limitation on the practical applicability of the method?
- How does the proposed method compare to other existing methods that address similar problems, such as those mentioned in related works?
- Could the authors provide more details on the computational complexity of the method, particularly in relation to the size and complexity of the datasets used?
- How does the method perform when integrated into different types of DGMs, and what are the potential impacts on the models' performance and training efficiency?
- Could the authors discuss potential real-world applications of the method, and provide examples or case studies to illustrate its practical utility?
