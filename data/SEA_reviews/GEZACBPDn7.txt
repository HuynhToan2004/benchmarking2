 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel semi-supervised graph classification method, KDGCN, which integrates graph kernels and graph neural networks (GNNs) to address the scarcity of labeled data in graph classification tasks. The method constructs a supergraph from a set of graphs using a graph kernel, then employs a GCN module to learn meta-node representations over this supergraph. The approach is evaluated on seven benchmark datasets and demonstrates competitive performance compared to existing methods. Despite its simplicity and ease of implementation, the paper's novelty is questioned, and the experimental results are considered marginal. The method's complexity and the lack of detailed comparative analysis with state-of-the-art methods are noted as significant drawbacks.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, making it easy to follow and understand.
- The method proposed is simple, intuitive, and easy to implement, with a clear motivation and a novel approach to graph classification.
- The experiments are well-designed, with multiple label rates and multiple kernel choices, providing a robust evaluation of the method's performance.
- The paper includes a complexity analysis, which is a valuable addition to the literature.
- The method demonstrates good performance on some datasets, particularly when the label rate is low.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed comparative analysis with state-of-the-art methods, which is crucial for establishing the method's effectiveness and novelty.
- The complexity of the method is high, which might limit its practical applicability, especially in large-scale datasets.
- The experimental results are marginal, with the method not consistently outperforming existing methods, especially when the label rate is high.
- The method's novelty is questioned as it appears to be a combination of existing methods without significant advancement.
- The paper does not provide a detailed discussion on the limitations of the proposed method, which is essential for understanding its applicability and scope.
- The method's performance is not consistently superior to existing methods, especially in datasets like DD and COLLAB, where it underperforms.
- The paper lacks a discussion on the choice of GCN as the backbone network and the impact of different kernel choices on the method's performance.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors provide more detailed comparisons with state-of-the-art methods to establish the method's effectiveness and novelty?
- How does the method perform when applied to other types of datasets, such as citation networks or social networks, which might have different characteristics?
- What are the specific advantages of using the WL subtree kernel over other graph kernels, and how does it enhance the method's performance?
- How does the method handle the scalability issues, particularly in large-scale datasets, where the complexity might become a significant barrier?
- Can the authors provide more insights into the choice of GCN as the backbone network and discuss the potential impact of using different kernel choices on the method's performance?
- How does the method perform when the label rate is not scarce, and what are the implications for its practical application in real-world scenarios?
