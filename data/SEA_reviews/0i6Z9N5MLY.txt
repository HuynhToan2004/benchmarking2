 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a variance-reduced Halpern iteration for solving finite-sum monotone inclusion problems, aiming to improve convergence rates over previous methods. It proposes two algorithms, each leveraging variance reduction techniques to enhance the oracle complexity of the methods. The first algorithm, based on the constrained Halpern iteration, uses a variance-reduced PAGE estimator to approximate the operator F. The second algorithm employs inexact Halpern iteration with a stochastic error criterion, utilizing a variance-reduced forward-reflected-backward method to approximate the resolvent. The paper provides theoretical guarantees for the last iterate and the operator norm residual, improving upon existing methods by a factor up to n. Numerical experiments are included to validate the theoretical claims.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a new algorithm for solving finite-sum monotone inclusion problems using variance reduction, which improves upon existing methods by a factor of n.
- The algorithm is theoretically well-supported, with proofs and analysis provided in the appendix.
- The paper is well-written, making it easy to follow and understand, and it includes a good discussion of related works.
- The main contribution of the paper is the first variance reduction-type result for general finite-sum monotone inclusions and for more specific problems such as convex-concave optimization when operator norm residual is the optimality measure.
- The paper is structured well, with a clear presentation of the algorithm and its theoretical analysis, which is supported by numerical experiments.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed discussion on the limitations of the proposed algorithms and potential future research directions.
- The assumption of monotonicity and Lipschitz continuity of the operator F is not clearly justified, especially in the context of finite-sum monotone inclusion problems.
- The paper could benefit from more detailed numerical experiments, including comparisons with other variance-reduced algorithms and more challenging problem instances.
- The dependence on the batch size in the algorithm might not be optimal, and there is a lack of discussion on the implications of this dependence.
- The paper could be improved by including a more detailed discussion on the assumptions and their implications, especially in the context of the finite-sum monotone inclusion problem.
- There are some typographical and grammatical errors that need correction.

**Questions:**

- Could the authors clarify the dependence on the batch size in the algorithm and discuss its implications?
- Is there a possibility to include more detailed numerical experiments, including comparisons with other variance-reduced algorithms and more challenging problem instances?
- Could the authors provide a more detailed discussion on the assumptions and their implications in the context of the finite-sum monotone inclusion problem?
