 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a decentralized Riemannian conjugate gradient descent (DRCGD) method designed for optimization over the Stiefel manifold. This method aims to minimize a global function represented as a finite sum of possibly non-convex local functions, each associated with a network agent. The DRCGD method is noted for being retract-free and vector transport-free, which reduces computational complexity. Theoretical analysis and numerical experiments demonstrate the efficiency and convergence properties of the DRCGD method compared to existing methods. The paper's contributions include the development of a novel projection operator that simplifies the algorithm's implementation and the establishment of global convergence under extended assumptions.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel decentralized Riemannian conjugate gradient descent (DRCGD) method, which is the first to achieve global convergence over the Stiefel manifold, a significant advancement in the field.
- The authors have developed a new projection operator that simplifies the algorithm's implementation and replaces expensive retraction and vector transport operations, making the method computationally efficient.
- Theoretical analysis and numerical experiments are conducted to demonstrate the efficiency and convergence properties of the proposed method, providing a robust validation of the proposed approach.
- The paper is well-organized and easy to follow, with clear explanations and a logical structure that aids in understanding the complex optimization problem and the proposed solution.
- The contribution of the paper is significant, as it addresses the computational challenges of Riemannian conjugate gradient methods on Stiefel manifolds, which are relevant to many large-scale systems such as machine learning, control, and signal processing.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a comprehensive discussion on the limitations of the proposed method, particularly in terms of its applicability to different types of optimization problems and the scalability of the method to larger datasets.
- There is a lack of comparison with state-of-the-art methods in terms of computational efficiency and convergence rate, which could help in better understanding the advantages of the proposed method.
- The paper could benefit from a more detailed explanation of the theoretical results and the assumptions used, especially in the context of the Stiefel manifold and the distributed optimization problem.
- There is a need for more rigorous validation of the numerical experiments, including a comparison with a broader range of datasets and optimization problems to ensure the generalizability of the findings.
- The paper could be improved by including a more detailed literature review and discussion on the related work, particularly in the context of distributed optimization on Riemannian manifolds.
