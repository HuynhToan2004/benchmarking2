 **Summary:**
The paper introduces Kosmos-2, a multimodal large language model (MLLM) designed to enhance visual grounding capabilities by associating textual descriptions with visual elements using a new dataset, GrIT. The model is capable of understanding text spans as hyperlinks to bounding boxes, thereby facilitating a more direct mapping between text and visual elements. The authors have constructed a large-scale dataset of grounded image-text pairs to train the model, which is evaluated on various tasks including phrase grounding, referring expression comprehension, and referring expression generation. Despite the innovative approach and the potential utility of the GrIT dataset, the paper faces criticism for its reliance on existing models and datasets, lack of novelty in its technical contributions, and insufficient evaluation against state-of-the-art models.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a new dataset, GrIT, which is a significant contribution to the field, and the methodology for constructing this dataset is detailed and reproducible.
- The paper is well-written, making it easy to follow, and the experimental results demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed model, Kosmos-2, in various tasks such as grounding, referring, and perception-language tasks.
- The authors have conducted extensive experiments, including comparisons with previous models, which validate the effectiveness of Kosmos-2.
- The paper provides a comprehensive evaluation of the model, including both zero-shot and few-shot settings, which showcases the model's versatility.
- The construction of the GrIT dataset is a significant contribution, as it is a large-scale dataset that could potentially be beneficial for future research in the field.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a thorough comparison with existing models like Flamingo and BLIP-2, which are crucial for establishing the novelty and effectiveness of the proposed model.
- The technical contribution of the paper is limited, as the use of hyperlinks to connect text and visual elements is a straightforward approach that does not significantly advance the state of the art.
- The paper does not adequately address the limitations of the model, such as the potential for inaccuracies in grounding due to the discretization of bounding boxes.
- There are concerns about the fairness of the comparisons made in the paper, particularly in the evaluation of the model against other models that may not have undergone similar training or evaluation procedures.
- The paper does not sufficiently discuss the potential societal impacts of the model, which is a significant omission in a field that is increasingly focusing on ethical considerations.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors provide a more detailed comparison with existing models like Flamingo and BLIP-2 to clarify the unique contributions of Kosmos-2?
- How does the model handle cases where the text span is linked to multiple bounding boxes, and what are the implications of this for the accuracy of the grounding?
- Could the authors discuss the potential societal impacts of the model and how they plan to address these concerns?
- How does the model handle cases where the text span is not explicitly linked to a bounding box, but is still associated with a visual element in the image?
- In the evaluation of the model, how does the use of different datasets, such as the Flickr30k test set for image captioning, affect the comparability of the results?
- Could the authors provide more details on the construction of the GrIT dataset, including the sources of the data and the methodology used to ensure its quality and reliability?
