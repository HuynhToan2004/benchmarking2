 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel decision-making framework for human-AI interactions, focusing on the adherence level of humans to AI advice and the provision of advice selectively. It proposes two algorithms: one for environments where the human adherence level is known and another for unknown environments. The algorithms aim to optimize advice policies and adapt to human behaviors, showing empirical performance and theoretical guarantees. The paper also discusses the sample complexity of the proposed algorithms and provides empirical evidence in the form of experiments on the Flappy Bird game. However, concerns are raised about the practicality of the assumptions made and the generalizability of the results beyond the specific environments used.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written and easy to follow, with clear explanations and a well-structured presentation.
- The problem addressed is timely and relevant, focusing on the interaction between humans and AI systems, which is a critical area of research.
- The paper introduces a novel framework for modeling human-AI interactions, including the concept of adherence level and the provision of advice selectively.
- Theoretical guarantees are provided for the proposed algorithms, which are supported by empirical evidence showing the effectiveness of the algorithms.
- The paper provides a comprehensive analysis of the sample complexity of the proposed algorithms, which is a significant contribution to the field.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper assumes that the human's adherence level is known, which might not be practical in real-world scenarios where such information is not available.
- The paper does not discuss the limitations of its work, which could include the generalizability of the results beyond the specific environments used in the experiments.
- The experimental setup is limited to simple environments like the Flappy Bird game, which might not adequately represent more complex real-world scenarios.
- The paper does not provide a comparison with existing methods for modeling human-AI interactions, which could help in evaluating the novelty and effectiveness of the proposed framework.
- There is a lack of discussion on how the results might vary with different adherence levels or in environments with different dynamics.
- The paper does not discuss potential negative societal impacts of the proposed methods, which could be a significant concern in practical applications.

**Questions:**
- How does the proposed framework perform in more complex and realistic environments, such as those found in real-world applications?
- Could you elaborate on the choice of the Flappy Bird game for the experiments and how it represents more complex scenarios?
- How does the proposed framework handle different adherence levels, and what are the implications of varying adherence levels on the effectiveness of the advice policies?
- Can you discuss the potential negative societal impacts of the proposed methods and how these might be mitigated?
- How do the results compare to existing methods for modeling human-AI interactions, and what are the advantages of the proposed framework over these methods?
- How might the proposed framework be adapted to handle different dynamics in the human-AI interaction, and what are the implications of such adaptations on the effectiveness of the advice policies?
