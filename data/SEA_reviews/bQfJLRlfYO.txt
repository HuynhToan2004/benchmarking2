 **Summary:**
The paper introduces KEQING, a novel framework for Knowledge-based Question Answering (KBQA) that integrates Large Language Models (LLMs) with a knowledge graph to enhance the performance and interpretability of LLMs in complex question-answering scenarios. KEQING operates through a four-stage process: question decomposition, knowledge retrieval, candidate reasoning, and response generation. It leverages a knowledge graph to retrieve relevant entities and logical chains, which guide LLMs to answer complex questions by decomposing them into simpler sub-questions. The framework is evaluated on several KBQA datasets, demonstrating competitive performance and improved interpretability compared to existing methods. However, concerns were raised regarding the novelty of the approach, the complexity of the method, and the need for more extensive comparisons with state-of-the-art methods.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel framework for Knowledge-based Question Answering (KBQA) that integrates LLMs with a knowledge graph, providing a systematic and interpretable approach to KBQA.
- The proposed method demonstrates improved performance on KBQA datasets, showcasing its effectiveness in handling complex queries.
- The paper is well-written, making the methodology and experimental setup clear and understandable, which aids in reproducibility.
- The use of a knowledge graph to guide LLMs in answering complex questions is a significant contribution to the field of KBQA, as it offers a more structured and interpretable approach compared to traditional LLM-only methods.
- The methodology is simple and effective, with a clear motivation and methodology that is easy to follow, making it accessible to a broad audience.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a thorough comparison with state-of-the-art methods, particularly in terms of performance metrics like accuracy and F1 score, which could undermine the claims of superiority.
- The methodology is complex and involves multiple stages, which could limit its scalability and applicability in real-world scenarios.
- The paper does not sufficiently address the novelty of the approach, as similar methods have been previously explored in the literature, which may question the originality of the proposed framework.
- There is a lack of clarity regarding the role and integration of different components within the framework, which could confuse the reader about the specific contributions of each stage.
- The experimental results are not convincingly superior to existing methods, and the paper does not provide a detailed analysis of the results, which could limit the understanding of the framework's effectiveness.

**Questions:**
- Could you provide a more detailed comparison with state-of-the-art methods, including performance metrics like accuracy and F1 score, to substantiate the claims of superiority?
- How does the proposed method compare in terms of scalability and applicability to other KBQA methods, especially in real-world scenarios?
- Can you clarify the role and integration of different components within the framework, particularly how they contribute to the overall performance and effectiveness of the KEQING system?
- How does the framework handle the complexity of multi-hop questions, and what are the specific challenges it encounters in such scenarios?
- What are the specific contributions of each stage of the framework to the overall performance and effectiveness of KEQING, and how do these contributions compare to existing methods?
