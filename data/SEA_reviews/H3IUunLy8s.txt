 **Summary:**
The paper introduces CapABoost, a novel method designed to enhance the capacity of parameter-efficient fine-tuning (PEFT) methods without increasing computational costs. CapABoost employs static random masks to construct diverse weight matrices, increasing the rank of incremental weights without adding parameters. This approach is demonstrated to outperform various PEFT methods such as LoRA, Prefix Tuning, and Adapters across multiple tasks including natural language understanding, question answering, and image classification. The methodology is simple yet effective, and the paper provides extensive experimental results to support its claims. However, concerns are raised about the theoretical rigor and the depth of experimental validation, particularly in terms of the generalizability of the results and the comparison with more recent and relevant baselines.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, clear, and easy to follow, with a clear motivation and well-structured content.
- The proposed CapABoost method is simple yet effective, requiring no additional computation or storage costs, making it a significant contribution to the field.
- Extensive experiments are conducted on various tasks, including natural language understanding, question answering, and image classification, demonstrating the effectiveness of CapABoost.
- The method is simple and can be applied to many parameter-efficient fine-tuning methods, making it a versatile tool for enhancing model capacity.
- The paper provides a clear and concise explanation of the method, making it accessible and understandable to readers.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a comprehensive review of existing parameter-efficient fine-tuning (PEFT) methods, which could limit its relevance and impact in the field.
- The experimental results are not convincing, particularly in the context of the GLUE benchmark, where the performance of CapABoost is not significantly better than existing methods like LoRA.
- The paper does not provide sufficient ablation studies to demonstrate the effectiveness of CapABoost, especially in terms of the impact of different hyperparameters and settings.
- The theoretical justification for the method is weak, with limited rigorous analysis and proof, which could undermine the credibility of the claims.
- The paper lacks a discussion on the limitations of the proposed method, such as its applicability to different types of models and tasks, and the potential negative societal impacts.
- The paper does not adequately compare CapABoost with more recent and relevant baselines, which could affect the assessment of its novelty and effectiveness.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors clarify the inconsistency in the performance of CapABoost compared to LoRA as depicted in Figure 1?
- How does the method perform when applied to other types of models, such as vision models, and what are the specific benefits and challenges in these applications?
- Could the authors provide more detailed ablation studies to demonstrate the impact of different hyperparameters and settings on the performance of CapABoost?
- How does CapABoost compare to other parameter-efficient fine-tuning methods that have been proposed recently, particularly those that address similar challenges in model capacity?
- Can the authors discuss the potential negative societal impacts of the proposed method and how these might be mitigated or addressed?
- How does the proposed method address the issues of overfitting, especially in scenarios where the inner dimension is set to a large value?
