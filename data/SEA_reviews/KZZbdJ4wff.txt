 **Summary:**
The paper introduces Pseudo-label Regularized Optimization (PRO), a method aimed at enhancing the performance of pre-trained models on unlabeled test data without the need for label peeking. PRO is designed to prevent model collapse during test-time adaptation by integrating ideas from semi-supervised learning and test-time training. The method employs a combination of entropy minimization, consistency regularization, and information maximization, which are applied to both the linear classifier and the entire textual encoder. The paper includes extensive experiments across various datasets, demonstrating the effectiveness of PRO in improving model performance. However, concerns are raised about the novelty of the approach, as it primarily combines existing techniques without significant innovation.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, making it easy to follow and understand.
- The proposed method is simple and effective, with a clear presentation of the methodology and its components.
- The paper includes a comprehensive set of experiments, covering a wide range of datasets, which helps to validate the proposed method.
- The proposed method, PRO, is innovative in its application of pseudo-label regularization to prevent model collapse during test-time adaptation, which is a significant issue in the field.
- The method's effectiveness is demonstrated through extensive experiments, which show that it can improve the performance of pre-trained models on unlabeled test data.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a thorough discussion on the novelty of the approach, as the proposed method primarily combines existing techniques without significant innovation.
- The method's performance is limited by the quality of pseudo-labels generated by the model, which could be improved by using more reliable labeling methods.
- There is a lack of comparison with other test-time training methods, which could provide a more robust evaluation of the proposed method.
- The paper does not adequately address the issue of meta-overfitting, which is a significant concern in the test-time training setting.
- The paper's focus on CLIP models limits the generalizability of the findings to other types of pre-trained models, such as those trained for language tasks.
- The paper's contribution to the field is seen as incremental, as it primarily applies existing techniques without significant innovation.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors clarify how the proposed method addresses the meta-overfitting issue, which is a significant concern in the test-time training setting?
- How does the proposed method compare to other test-time training methods, particularly in terms of performance and efficiency?
- Could the authors provide a more detailed discussion on the novelty of their approach and how it builds upon existing methods in the field?
- Given the limitations in the generalizability of the findings due to the focus on CLIP models, how do the authors plan to extend their research to other types of pre-trained models?
- How does the proposed method perform on datasets with long-tail distributions, where the performance gains might be more pronounced?
- Can the authors provide more details on the hyperparameter tuning process and the sensitivity of the method to different hyperparameters?
