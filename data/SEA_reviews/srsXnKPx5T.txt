 **Summary:**
The paper introduces Hexa, a self-improving method for knowledge-grounded dialogue generation that leverages a bootstrapping scheme with a guided prompt and a modified loss function to enhance diversity and appropriate self-generated responses. Hexa addresses the challenge of limited data availability for intermediate steps by using a self-improving mechanism that iteratively refines the model's performance. The method was tested across various dialogue tasks and shown to outperform existing self-improving methods and supervised learning methods. The paper provides a detailed experimental setup, including automatic and human evaluations, and discusses the limitations and potential improvements of the approach.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces Hexa, a novel self-improving method for knowledge-grounded dialogue generation that addresses the challenge of limited data availability for intermediate steps.
- The methodology is well-articulated, with a clear explanation of the bootstrapping scheme, guided prompt, and modified loss function used to enhance the diversity and appropriateness of self-generated responses.
- Hexa is shown to outperform existing self-improving methods and supervised learning methods on various dialogue tasks, demonstrating its effectiveness.
- The paper includes detailed experimental setup, ablation studies, and human evaluations, which provide a robust validation of the method.
- The paper is well-written, making it easy to follow and understand, and includes a detailed analysis of the method's components and their impact on performance.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper could benefit from more detailed explanations of the bootstrapping scheme, guided prompt, and modified loss function, particularly how these components contribute to the method's effectiveness.
- There is a lack of clarity on the specifics of the model's architecture and the training process, which could make it difficult for readers to fully understand and replicate the method.
- The paper does not include a comprehensive comparison with other self-improving methods or a discussion on the limitations of the method, which could help contextualize its contributions and improvements.
- The paper's reliance on a single baseline (BB3) and a limited set of datasets for testing raises concerns about the method's generalizability and applicability to different scenarios.
- The paper does not adequately address the potential negative societal impacts of the proposed method, which could be a significant concern for some readers.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors provide more detailed explanations of the bootstrapping scheme, guided prompt, and modified loss function, particularly how these components contribute to the method's effectiveness?
- How does Hexa compare with other self-improving methods, and what are the specific advantages or disadvantages compared to these approaches?
- What are the limitations of the Hexa method, and how might these limitations affect its performance or applicability in different scenarios?
- Could the authors clarify the model's architecture and training process to help readers better understand and replicate the method?
- How does the paper's use of automatic evaluation metrics like ROUGE-L compare to human evaluations, and what steps were taken to ensure the reliability and validity of these evaluations?
