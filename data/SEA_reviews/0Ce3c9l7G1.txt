 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel method for communication in Multi-Agent Reinforcement Learning (MARL) named Attentive Regularized Communication (ARCOMM). ARCOMM utilizes an attention module to evaluate the importance of messages generated by agents and a message regularizer to facilitate the learning of more meaningful messages. The method is designed to improve the performance of agents in complex domains, even when communication channels are limited or messages are compressed. The paper presents results from various StarCraft environments, demonstrating the effectiveness of ARCOMM compared to existing methods. However, the experimental results are criticized for their lack of robustness and the paper's methodological approach is questioned for its clarity and originality.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel method for communication in Multi-Agent Reinforcement Learning (MARL) using an attention module and a message regularizer, which is a significant contribution to the field.
- The proposed method, ARCOMM, is evaluated in various complex scenarios, showing its effectiveness in improving the performance of agents in these environments.
- The paper provides a clear and detailed explanation of the methodology, including the use of an attention module to evaluate the importance of messages and a message regularizer to facilitate the learning of more meaningful messages.
- The experiments conducted in the StarCraft environment demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed method, and the paper is well-written, making it easy to follow.
- The paper includes an ablation study on the message regularizer, which helps in understanding the impact of this component on the performance of the method.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a thorough literature review and comparison with existing methods, making it difficult to assess the novelty and effectiveness of the proposed method.
- The experimental results are not convincing, with some results showing a lack of significance and inconsistency across different environments.
- The paper does not adequately address the scalability of the proposed method, especially in large-scale MARL scenarios, where communication bandwidth and message sizes are critical.
- The paper lacks a detailed analysis of the message regularizer, which is a critical component of the proposed method.
- The paper's methodological approach and the use of the DCT compression method are questioned for their clarity and effectiveness in practical scenarios.
- The paper's writing style and presentation could be improved to enhance clarity and readability, particularly in sections where the methodology and results are presented.

**Questions:**
- Could you clarify how the attention module in ARCOMM is different from similar modules used in other MARL methods?
- How does the message regularizer affect the performance of the method, and what are the specific impacts observed in the ablation study?
- How does the proposed method perform in scenarios with different communication bandwidths, and how does it handle the compression of messages to fit within these bandwidths?
- Could you provide more details on the scalability of the proposed method in large-scale MARL scenarios, and how it handles the challenges of communication in such environments?
- How does the DCT compression method perform in practical scenarios, and what are the implications of using this method in different communication environments?
- Could you provide more details on the experimental setup, including the hyperparameters used and the specifics of the communication protocols employed?
- How does the proposed method compare to other existing methods in terms of performance and computational efficiency, especially in scenarios where communication bandwidth is limited?
