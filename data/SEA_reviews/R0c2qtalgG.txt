 **Summary:**
The paper introduces MetaTool, a benchmark designed to evaluate the tool usage awareness and selection capabilities of large language models (LLMs). It includes the ToolLE dataset, which encompasses 21,127 user queries, and proposes four tasks to assess LLMs' performance in tool selection. The dataset is diverse, including both single-tool and multi-tool scenarios, and is generated using various prompting methods to avoid overlapping tool functionality. The paper conducts experiments involving eight popular LLMs and provides insights for tool developers to enhance LLM tool selection performance. However, the paper's evaluation is criticized for not sufficiently distinguishing between LLMs' tool selection capabilities and their general problem-solving abilities, and for not adequately addressing the issue of tool overlapping.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel benchmark, MetaTool, which evaluates LLMs' tool usage awareness and selection capabilities, providing a comprehensive evaluation of LLMs' tool usage.
- The dataset, ToolLE, is diverse and encompasses a wide range of 21,127 user queries, which is a significant contribution to the field.
- The paper provides insights for tool developers, which can enhance LLM tool selection performance, making it a valuable resource for tool developers.
- The dataset generation process is detailed, and the evaluation results are insightful, highlighting the limitations of LLMs in tool selection.
- The paper is well-written, making it easy to follow, and the experiments are comprehensive, involving eight popular LLMs, which provides a robust evaluation of the models.

**Weaknesses:**
- The evaluation of tool selection is criticized for not sufficiently distinguishing between LLMs' tool selection capabilities and their general problem-solving abilities, which could lead to biased conclusions.
- The dataset's generation process, particularly the handling of tool overlapping, is not clearly described, raising concerns about the dataset's quality and the robustness of the evaluation.
- The paper's approach to tool selection does not adequately address the issue of tool overlapping, which could significantly impact the evaluation results.
- The paper does not include a baseline model, which could help in understanding the effectiveness of the proposed benchmark and evaluation method.
- The evaluation methodology and the selection of models for evaluation are not well justified, and the paper lacks a detailed discussion on the limitations of the evaluation.
- The paper does not provide a detailed discussion on the limitations of the evaluation, which is crucial for understanding the scope and applicability of the findings.

**Questions:**
- How does the evaluation of tool selection in this paper differentiate from the general problem-solving capabilities of LLMs?
- Can the authors clarify the process of handling tool overlapping in the dataset generation and ensure that the evaluation is not biased by this issue?
- How does the proposed benchmark compare to other existing benchmarks in terms of evaluating LLMs' tool usage awareness and selection capabilities?
- Could the authors provide more details on the selection of models for evaluation and justify the choice of these models?
- How does the paper address the issue of tool overlapping in the dataset, and what steps have been taken to ensure that this does not bias the evaluation results?
- Is there a plan to include a baseline model in future evaluations to better understand the effectiveness of the proposed benchmark and evaluation method?
