 **Summary:**
The paper introduces Disco-Bench, a new evaluation benchmark designed to assess models' understanding and generation of discourse-level phenomena in both Chinese and English. The benchmark consists of nine datasets, each focusing on different discourse-related tasks such as cohesion and coherence, and includes a diagnostic test suite to evaluate models' ability to learn discourse knowledge. The paper also proposes a new pre-training dataset for fine-grained pre-training, which is claimed to enhance models' discourse understanding. The benchmark is evaluated using various models, including state-of-the-art large language models (LLMs), and shows that fine-grained pre-training on Disco-Bench data can improve discourse modeling. However, the paper is criticized for its limited novelty, lack of clarity in certain sections, and insufficient comparison with existing benchmarks.

**Strengths:**
- The paper addresses an important and timely issue by focusing on the evaluation of discourse phenomena in natural language processing (NLP), which is often overlooked in existing benchmarks.
- The creation of the Disco-Bench benchmark and diagnostic test suite is a significant contribution, providing a new tool for assessing models' ability to understand and generate discourse-level phenomena.
- The paper is well-written and easy to follow, with clear explanations of the tasks and datasets used.
- The proposed benchmark is comprehensive, covering various NLP tasks such as language understanding, translation, and generation, and is designed to be useful for both research and practical applications.
- The paper includes extensive experiments and comparisons, demonstrating the effectiveness of fine-grained pre-training on Disco-Bench data for discourse modeling.

**Weaknesses:**
- The novelty of the work is limited, as the proposed benchmark and diagnostic test suite are not significantly different from existing benchmarks like CLUE and XGLUE.
- The paper lacks a detailed explanation of the methodology used for constructing the benchmark and diagnostic test suite, which could affect the reproducibility and reliability of the results.
- There is a lack of clarity in some sections of the paper, particularly in the definitions and explanations of the tasks and datasets used.
- The paper does not adequately compare its results with existing benchmarks, which could help in understanding the relative performance of the proposed benchmark.
- The paper does not provide enough details on the pre-training methodology, such as the specific training algorithms and hyperparameters used, which could affect the reproducibility and generalizability of the results.

**Questions:**
- How does the proposed benchmark differ from existing benchmarks like CLUE and XGLUE, and what specific advantages does it offer over these benchmarks?
- Can the authors provide more details on the methodology used for constructing the benchmark and diagnostic test suite, including the data sources, annotation guidelines, and evaluation metrics?
- How do the authors ensure the reliability and validity of the benchmark, particularly in the construction of the diagnostic test suite?
- Could the authors clarify the definitions and explanations of the tasks and datasets used, especially in sections where the clarity is lacking?
- How does the proposed benchmark compare to existing benchmarks in terms of the diversity and complexity of the discourse phenomena it covers?
- Could the authors provide more details on the pre-training methodology, including the specific training algorithms and hyperparameters used, and the data sources and sizes for the pre-training datasets?
