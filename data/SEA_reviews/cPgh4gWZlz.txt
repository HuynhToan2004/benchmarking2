 **Summary:**
The paper introduces the Chain-of-Knowledge (CoK) framework, a novel approach to enhancing the factual correctness of large language models (LLMs) by integrating knowledge from heterogeneous sources. CoK consists of three stages: reasoning preparation, dynamic knowledge adapting, and answer consolidation. The framework leverages an adaptive query generator (AQG) to retrieve knowledge from various sources, including structured and unstructured data, and corrects rationales progressively to minimize error propagation. Extensive experiments demonstrate that CoK significantly improves the performance of LLMs on knowledge-intensive tasks across different domains, showing a consistent improvement over the CoT baseline. However, concerns were raised about the novelty and the methodological soundness of the approach, with some reviewers questioning the fairness of the comparisons and the effectiveness of the proposed methods.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, clear, and easy to follow, with a clear motivation for the proposed method.
- The proposed method is novel, integrating heterogeneous knowledge sources and addressing the issue of error propagation in knowledge-intensive tasks.
- The methodology is simple, effective, and innovative, with a focus on reducing hallucination and improving factual accuracy in LLMs.
- The paper includes a comprehensive evaluation across various datasets and knowledge domains, demonstrating the effectiveness of the proposed method.
- The use of an adaptive query generator (AQG) is highlighted as a significant contribution, enhancing the retrieval of knowledge from different sources.

**Weaknesses:**
- The novelty of the method is questioned, as similar approaches have been previously explored in the literature.
- The methodology lacks clarity in some areas, particularly in the description of the AQG model and the details of the training data and experimental settings.
- The paper does not adequately address the potential limitations or societal impacts of the proposed method.
- The paper's claims regarding the effectiveness of the proposed method are not convincingly supported by the experimental results, which are sometimes inconsistent or not sufficiently detailed.
- The paper's reliance on existing LLMs for knowledge retrieval and the potential for error propagation in the knowledge correction process are not sufficiently addressed.
- The paper's comparisons with other methods, such as ReAct, are not fair or well-justified, which could mislead the reader about the true effectiveness of the proposed method.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors provide more details on the training data and settings for the AQG model, particularly how it is trained and how it interacts with the LLM?
- How does the proposed method compare to other state-of-the-art methods in terms of performance and efficiency?
- In the context of the experiments, how were the rationales generated, and how were the knowledge sources selected?
- How does the proposed method handle the potential for error propagation in the knowledge correction process, and what measures are in place to ensure the accuracy of the retrieved knowledge?
- Could the authors clarify the role and impact of the LoRA fine-tuning on the performance of the AQG model?
- Given the potential for error propagation, how does the method ensure that corrected rationales do not introduce new errors or inconsistencies?
