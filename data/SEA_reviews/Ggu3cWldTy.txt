 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a unified mirror descent (UMD) algorithm aimed at solving various decision-making problems across different scenarios, including single-agent, cooperative multi-agent, competitive multi-agent, and mixed cooperative-competitive cases. The UMD algorithm integrates multiple base policy update rules, weighted by a vector of hyperparameters, which are optimized using a zero-order method. The approach is evaluated on 24 benchmark environments, demonstrating its effectiveness in most cases. However, concerns are raised about the incremental nature of the contribution, the lack of theoretical guarantees, and the clarity of the experimental setup, particularly the evaluation metric used in hyperparameter optimization.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, making it easy to follow, and the introduction is clear and easy to understand.
- The proposed algorithm is simple and can be applied to various decision-making problems, including single-agent, cooperative, and competitive multi-agent settings.
- The experimental results are convincing, showing the algorithm's effectiveness in most scenarios, and the paper includes a comprehensive evaluation across multiple benchmark games.
- The idea of using a unified mirror descent algorithm to address different decision-making problems is innovative and interesting, particularly the approach of weighting different policy update rules to adapt to various scenarios.
- The paper provides a comprehensive empirical study of all types of decision-making problems under a single algorithmic framework, which is a significant contribution to the field.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a clear motivation for the unified approach, and the contribution of the paper is not clearly defined. The incremental nature of the contribution is not convincingly justified.
- The experimental setup is not clearly described, particularly the evaluation metric used in hyperparameter optimization, which is crucial for the validity of the results.
- The paper does not provide a theoretical guarantee for the proposed algorithm, which is a significant limitation given the complexity and diversity of the decision-making scenarios addressed.
- The paper's focus on the tabular setting limits its applicability to more complex environments, and the method's effectiveness in such settings is not explored.
- The paper could benefit from a more detailed discussion on the related work, particularly in the area of multi-agent decision-making, to better position its contribution within the existing literature.
- The paper's presentation could be improved, particularly in the clarity and organization of figures and the overall structure of the manuscript.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors clarify the motivation behind the unified approach and how it contributes to a better understanding or solution of decision-making problems?
- How does the proposed algorithm compare to other existing methods, particularly in terms of performance and computational efficiency?
- What is the rationale behind the specific choice of hyperparameters, and how were these parameters tuned across different environments?
- How does the proposed method perform in more complex environments, and what modifications, if any, are necessary for such settings?
- Could the authors provide a more detailed theoretical analysis or guarantee for the proposed algorithm, especially in light of its claimed effectiveness across various decision-making scenarios?
- In the context of the experimental results, how do the authors justify the choice of evaluation metric in hyperparameter optimization, and what are the implications of this choice for the validity of the results?
