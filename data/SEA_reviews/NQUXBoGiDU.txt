 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel spiking neural network (SNN) architecture for object detection, leveraging knowledge distillation from a non-spiking teacher network to enhance performance. This architecture, termed Spiking CenterNet, is designed to operate on event data, which is more energy-efficient compared to traditional neural networks. The paper presents a detailed analysis of the energy efficiency of the proposed model, comparing it with existing models, and demonstrates its effectiveness on the GEN1 dataset. However, concerns were raised about the novelty of the method, the lack of comparison with state-of-the-art models, and the limited scope of the experiments.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel method of training an SNN object detector using knowledge distillation from a non-spiking teacher network, which is a significant advancement in the field.
- The proposed method achieves better performance and energy efficiency compared to previous SNN-based object detectors, as demonstrated through a comprehensive evaluation on the GEN1 dataset.
- The paper is well-written, making it easy to follow and understand the proposed methodology and experimental results.
- The authors have provided a detailed analysis of the energy efficiency of their model, which is a critical aspect for applications in energy-constrained environments.
- The use of knowledge distillation for SNNs in object detection is a first, which adds a new dimension to the field.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed discussion on the limitations of the proposed method, which could hinder its practical application in real-world scenarios.
- There is a lack of comparison with state-of-the-art models, which could provide a better understanding of the model's performance relative to the current best practices.
- The paper primarily focuses on the GEN1 dataset, which limits the generalizability of the findings to other datasets or scenarios.
- The novelty of the method is questionable as it primarily involves replacing certain modules with spiking counterparts and using knowledge distillation, which may not be considered a significant advancement.
- The paper does not provide a detailed comparison of the proposed model with other SNN-based object detectors, which could help in understanding the relative performance of the proposed model.
- The energy efficiency claims are not substantiated with actual energy consumption measurements, which could weaken the argument.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors provide a more detailed discussion on the limitations of their proposed method and how these could be addressed in future work?
- How does the proposed method compare with state-of-the-art models in terms of performance and energy efficiency?
- Could the authors provide a detailed comparison of the proposed model with other SNN-based object detectors, including a discussion on the advantages and disadvantages of each approach?
- How does the model perform on other datasets beyond the GEN1 dataset, and what are the implications of these results for the generalizability of the findings?
- Can the authors provide a more detailed discussion on the energy efficiency claims, including actual energy consumption measurements and a comparison with other models in terms of energy efficiency?
