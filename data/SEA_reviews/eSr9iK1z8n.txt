 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel view synthesis method for NeRFs, focusing on efficient sampling strategies and regularization terms inspired by causal representation learning. The authors propose a new loss function to guide sampling and regularization, aiming to improve the quality of synthesized images with fewer training samples. This approach, inspired by causal representation learning, aims to achieve more stable and excellent novel view synthesis results under different view sampling strategies. The paper is supported by theoretical analysis and extensive experiments, demonstrating the effectiveness of the proposed method. However, concerns were raised about the clarity of the paper, particularly the presentation of the loss function and the experimental setup, which could be improved for better comprehension and reproducibility.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel loss function to guide sampling and regularization, which is a significant contribution to the field of NeRF.
- The proposed method is theoretically grounded, providing a strong foundation for the practical application of the proposed techniques.
- The paper is well-written, making it accessible and easy to follow, with a clear presentation of the problem and the proposed solution.
- The experiments are extensive, demonstrating the effectiveness of the proposed method across various datasets and settings.
- The use of causal representation learning to formulate the task loss is an innovative approach that could inspire future research in this area.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks clarity in the presentation of the loss function and the experimental setup, making it difficult for readers to fully understand the proposed method and its implementation.
- The paper does not sufficiently compare the proposed method with existing state-of-the-art methods, which could undermine the perceived novelty and effectiveness of the proposed approach.
- The paper's focus on the few-shot setting might limit its applicability in more general scenarios where a larger number of training samples are available.
- The paper does not adequately discuss the limitations of the proposed method, which could help in understanding the scope and applicability of the findings.
- The paper's reliance on the causal representation learning framework might not be fully justified, and the connection between the causal representation learning and the proposed method is not convincingly established.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors clarify the presentation of the loss function and the experimental setup to make the paper more accessible and reproducible?
- How does the proposed method compare with existing state-of-the-art methods, particularly in terms of performance and computational efficiency?
- Can the authors provide a more detailed explanation of the connection between the causal representation learning and the proposed method, and how this connection enhances the understanding or application of the proposed method?
- How does the proposed method handle the few-shot setting compared to more general scenarios where a larger number of training samples are available?
- Could the authors discuss the limitations of the proposed method and how these limitations affect the applicability and generalizability of the findings?
