 **Summary:**
The paper explores the equivalence between gated recurrent neural networks (RNNs) and linear self-attention mechanisms, demonstrating that certain RNN configurations can effectively implement self-attention. The authors show that this equivalence is not limited to theoretical constructs but also extends to practical scenarios, where RNNs can be trained to mimic self-attention in transformer architectures. This finding challenges the conventional view of RNNs as distinct from transformers and suggests a new perspective on the relationship between these architectures. The paper also discusses the implications of this equivalence in terms of model efficiency and computational complexity.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel perspective on the relationship between gated recurrent neural networks (RNNs) and linear self-attention mechanisms, demonstrating a theoretical and practical equivalence.
- The experiments are well-designed, and the results are convincingly presented, supporting the theoretical claims.
- The paper is well-written, with clear explanations of the experimental setups and results, making it accessible and understandable.
- The implications of the findings for the community are significant, as they challenge the common perception that RNNs and transformers are fundamentally different architectures.
- The paper provides a comprehensive theoretical analysis and empirical evidence, which is crucial for understanding the behavior of gated RNNs and their relationship to self-attention mechanisms.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper primarily focuses on linear self-attention, which might limit its applicability to more complex, non-linear attention mechanisms.
- The theoretical analysis is somewhat limited to linear self-attention, and it is unclear how the findings extend to non-linear attention mechanisms.
- The experiments are primarily conducted on simple tasks, which might not fully demonstrate the practical implications of the theoretical results.
- The paper could benefit from a more detailed discussion on the implications of the findings, particularly in terms of practical applications and the scalability of the proposed methods.
- The paper could be improved by including a more comprehensive literature review, especially regarding the relationship between RNNs and transformers.
- The paper's presentation could be clearer, particularly in sections where the theoretical results are presented, to better guide the reader through the complex mathematical formulations.
