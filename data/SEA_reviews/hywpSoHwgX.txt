 **Summary:**
The paper introduces two novel components, Camouflageator and ICEG, designed to enhance the performance of camouflaged object detection (COD) methods. Camouflageator utilizes an adversarial training approach to generate more challenging camouflaged objects, while ICEG focuses on refining the detection process by leveraging internal coherence and edge guidance. The methodology includes an adversarial training framework and a novel detector, ICEG, which integrates the Camouflageator framework to improve detection performance. The paper presents extensive experiments demonstrating the effectiveness of these components, although it faces criticism for its lack of clarity in presentation and insufficient comparison with existing methods.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces an innovative adversarial training framework, Camouflageator, which enhances the performance of COD methods by generating more challenging camouflaged objects.
- The proposed ICEG detector employs a novel camouflaged feature coherence module to excavate the internal coherence of camouflaged objects, leading to more complete segmentation results.
- The paper is well-written, easy to follow, and includes extensive experiments that demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed components.
- The methodology introduces a novel edge-guided separated calibration module to remove false predictions, enhancing the detection accuracy.
- The paper provides a clear and detailed explanation of the methodology, including the use of a novel adversarial training strategy and a novel detector.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks clarity in some sections, particularly in the explanation of the generator and the adversarial training strategy, which could benefit from more detailed explanations and visual aids.
- The comparison with existing methods is insufficient, with the paper only citing a limited number of related works and not providing a comprehensive comparison across different metrics and datasets.
- The paper does not adequately address the fairness issues related to the training data, which could affect the generalizability of the results.
- There is a lack of discussion on the limitations of the proposed method and potential negative societal impacts, which could be critical for the ethical application of the technology.
- The paper does not provide sufficient experimental validation of the effectiveness of the proposed components, particularly the ICEG detector, which is crucial for demonstrating the superiority of the proposed method.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors provide a clearer explanation of the generator and the adversarial training strategy, possibly with additional visual aids?
- How does the proposed method compare with other existing COD methods, especially in terms of detection accuracy and computational efficiency?
- Could the authors discuss the limitations of the proposed method and potential negative societal impacts, especially in the context of military or surveillance applications?
- How does the proposed method handle the issue of fairness in the training data, and what steps can be taken to mitigate potential biases?
- Can the authors provide more detailed experimental results, including the performance of the ICEG detector across different datasets and metrics?
