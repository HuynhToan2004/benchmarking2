 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel method for estimating 3D rotations by representing them as pseudo rotation matrices (PRoM), which are unorthogonalized matrices that are orthogonalized post-training. The authors argue that this approach avoids the ambiguity and instability issues associated with orthogonalization methods like Gram-Schmidt and SVD. Theoretical and empirical evidence supports the claim that PRoM converges faster and achieves better results in various rotation-related tasks. The method's effectiveness is demonstrated through extensive experiments on large-scale benchmarks, showing improvements over existing methods.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written and easy to follow, making complex concepts accessible to readers.
- The authors provide a clear and detailed explanation of the issues with existing methods, particularly focusing on the ambiguity and instability caused by orthogonalization.
- The proposed method of using pseudo rotation matrices (PRoM) is novel and innovative, offering a new perspective on handling 3D rotations.
- The paper provides both theoretical and empirical evidence to support the effectiveness of the PRoM method, which is backed by extensive experiments on large-scale benchmarks.
- The authors demonstrate the superiority of their method over existing methods, achieving state-of-the-art results in various rotation-related tasks.
- The paper addresses a significant problem in the field of 3D rotation estimation, which is crucial for applications such as 3D pose and shape estimation.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper does not provide a detailed explanation of how the PRoM method is applied in practical scenarios, which could be improved with additional examples or case studies.
- The comparison with existing methods is limited, which could be expanded to include a broader range of techniques to strengthen the paper's claims.
- The paper lacks a discussion on the potential limitations or challenges of the PRoM method, which could help in understanding its applicability and scope.
- There is a need for more detailed experimental results, particularly in terms of training time and memory usage, to provide a more comprehensive evaluation of the PRoM method.
- The paper does not sufficiently discuss the implications of using unorthogonalized matrices, which could lead to issues such as non-symmetric matrices, and the potential impact on the accuracy and stability of the estimated rotations.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors provide more detailed examples or case studies on how the PRoM method is applied in practical scenarios?
- How does the PRoM method compare to other methods, particularly in terms of computational efficiency and accuracy?
- Can the authors discuss the potential limitations or challenges of the PRoM method and how these might affect its applicability and effectiveness?
- How does the PRoM method perform in terms of training time and memory usage compared to other methods?
- Could the authors clarify how the gradient analysis in the paper is related to the gradient of the loss function and how it affects the convergence and stability of the estimated rotations?
- How does the paper address the issue of non-symmetric matrices that might be produced by using unorthogonalized matrices, and what are the implications of this for the accuracy and stability of the estimated rotations?
