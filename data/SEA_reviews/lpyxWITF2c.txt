 **Summary:**
The paper explores the bias enhancement in Graph Neural Networks (GNNs) through a theoretical analysis of bias enhancement during GCN-like aggregation within the context of the stochastic block model (SBM). It introduces a sufficient condition for bias enhancement and proposes a novel fair graph rewiring algorithm, FairGR, to mitigate prediction bias while preserving useful graph topology. The paper also includes experimental validations on both synthetic and real-world datasets, showing the effectiveness of FairGR in reducing bias. However, the paper's focus is primarily on GCN-like aggregation and the CSBM, which might limit its applicability to other GNN architectures and real-world datasets.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel theoretical analysis of bias enhancement in GNNs, specifically focusing on GCN-like aggregation within the context of the stochastic block model (CSBM).
- It provides a sufficient condition for bias enhancement and derives several data-centric insights that could be useful for understanding and mitigating bias in GNNs.
- The paper proposes a fair graph rewiring algorithm, FairGR, which is compatible with many fair training strategies and can be adapted to improve existing fair graph learning strategies.
- The paper is well-written, clear, and easy to follow, with clear definitions and sufficient background information provided.
- The experiments conducted are comprehensive and convincing, showing the effectiveness of the proposed method.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper's focus is primarily on GCN-like aggregation and the CSBM, which might limit its applicability to other GNN architectures and real-world datasets.
- The experiments are conducted on relatively small datasets, which might not adequately represent the performance of the proposed method on larger, more complex datasets.
- The paper does not provide a comprehensive comparison with existing methods, such as graph-level fairness methods and other fair GNN training methods.
- The theoretical analysis is limited to GCN-like aggregation and the CSBM, which might not fully capture the complexity and diversity of real-world graph data.
- The paper does not discuss the limitations of the proposed method, such as the potential loss of useful information during graph rewiring.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors provide more detailed explanations for Theorem 3.5 and Lemma D.1, especially the definitions and roles of variables such as pconn, qconn, and λ?
- How does the proposed method perform on larger, more complex datasets, and what are the potential limitations or challenges in scaling up the method?
- Can the authors provide a more detailed discussion on the potential limitations of the proposed method, such as the loss of useful information during graph rewiring?
- How does the proposed method compare with existing graph-level fairness methods and other fair GNN training methods?
- Can the authors provide a more detailed discussion on the potential societal impacts of the proposed method, especially in terms of fairness and bias mitigation?
