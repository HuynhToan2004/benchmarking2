 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel search space for neural optimizer search (NOS) that includes the weight update equation, decay functions, and learning rate schedules, optimized using a particle-based genetic algorithm. The methodology is evaluated on various image classification tasks, demonstrating the ability to outperform standard optimizers like Adam. However, the novelty of the approach is questioned as it largely builds upon existing methods, and the experimental results are considered incremental, with limited generalization across different datasets and model architectures.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, clear, and easy to follow, making it accessible to a broad audience.
- The proposed method is simple and effective, with a clear and intuitive algorithm.
- The search space is well-designed, providing a comprehensive exploration of possible optimizers.
- The paper includes a thorough evaluation of the proposed method, demonstrating its effectiveness and generalization to different tasks.
- The use of a particle-based genetic algorithm for optimization is highlighted as a significant contribution, particularly in terms of its parallelization capabilities.

**Weaknesses:**
- The novelty of the approach is limited as it builds upon existing methods, such as those by Bello et al. (2017), with similarities in the search space and optimization techniques.
- The experimental results are considered incremental and not sufficiently generalizable, with a lack of results on larger datasets like ImageNet.
- There is a lack of detailed discussion on the limitations of the proposed method, including potential issues with overfitting and the generalization of the results to different model architectures.
- The paper lacks a comprehensive discussion on the computational cost of the proposed method and the efficiency of the genetic algorithm.
- The paper could benefit from more detailed explanations of the methodology, particularly in sections where the algorithm is introduced.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors provide more detailed results on the generalization of the proposed method to larger datasets, such as ImageNet?
- How does the proposed method compare in terms of computational cost and efficiency with other optimization methods, particularly in terms of wall-clock time?
- Can the authors clarify the role and impact of the integrity check in the optimization process?
- How does the proposed method handle the optimization of different learning rate schedules, and what are the implications for the optimization of the weight update equation?
- Could the authors discuss the potential for overfitting in the optimization process and how this was addressed in the experiments?

**Paper Decision:**
- Decision: Reject
