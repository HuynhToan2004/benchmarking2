 **Summary:**
The paper explores the representation capacity of various vector symbolic architectures (VSAs), including MAP-I, MAP-B, and Hopfield networks, using a Johnson-Lindenstrauss (JL) inspired analysis. It investigates how these architectures perform tasks like set intersection and membership testing, providing theoretical bounds on the dimensions of VSA vectors needed for these tasks. The paper also introduces a novel variant of a Hopfield network and discusses the connection between VSAs and sketching algorithms, particularly focusing on the Johnson-Lindenstrauss Lemma. Despite its theoretical contributions, the paper lacks empirical validation, which is crucial for understanding the practical implications of its findings.

**Strengths:**
- The paper provides a comprehensive theoretical analysis of the representation capacity of various vector symbolic architectures (VSAs), including MAP-I, MAP-B, and Hopfield networks, which is a significant contribution to the field.
- It introduces a novel variant of a Hopfield network and establishes connections between VSAs, "sketching" algorithms, and Bloom filters, which are original and potentially impactful.
- The paper is well-written, with clear and concise explanations of the technical details, making it accessible to readers.
- The analysis is based on the Johnson-Lindenstrauss Lemma, which is a well-known result in algorithmic sketching, and extends to the case where the sign vectors are not independent, using standard concentration inequalities.
- The paper provides a thorough review of the related literature and offers a clear and detailed explanation of the techniques used in the analysis.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks empirical validation, which is crucial for understanding the practical implications of the theoretical findings.
- The scope of the analysis is limited to specific VSAs, and it does not address other types of VSAs, which could limit the generalizability of the results.
- The paper's writing style and organization could be improved, particularly in terms of readability and the logical flow of the content.
- There is a lack of discussion on the limitations of the analysis and the assumptions made, which could affect the applicability and reliability of the results.
- The paper does not provide a detailed comparison with existing theoretical results, which could help in evaluating the novelty and effectiveness of the proposed methods.
- The paper does not discuss the limitations of the analysis, such as the assumptions made and the potential impact of these assumptions on the applicability and reliability of the results.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors clarify the scope of the analysis and whether it can be extended to other types of VSAs?
- How do the results compare with existing theoretical results, and what are the implications of these comparisons for the practical applicability of the findings?
- Could the authors provide more details on the limitations of the analysis and how these might affect the applicability and reliability of the results?
- How do the authors envision the practical implications of their findings, and what steps are being taken to validate the theoretical results through empirical testing?
- Could the authors elaborate on the novelty of their contributions and how they compare to existing work in the field?
