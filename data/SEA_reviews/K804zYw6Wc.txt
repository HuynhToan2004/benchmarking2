 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel Selective Fusion Module (SFM) for enhancing image denoising in low-light environments by leveraging near-infrared (NIR) images. The SFM is designed to merge NIR and RGB features effectively, addressing the inconsistencies between these modalities. The paper also introduces a new dataset, NIR-Assisted Image Denoising (NAID), which includes paired NIR and RGB images with varying noise levels, aimed at advancing research in this field. The methodology involves a plug-and-play approach that integrates the SFM into existing denoising networks, demonstrating superior performance over state-of-the-art methods on both synthetic and real-world datasets. However, the paper's novelty is questioned due to the simplicity of the SFM and the lack of significant technical advancement over existing methods.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel Selective Fusion Module (SFM) for NIR-assisted image denoising, which is a significant advancement in the field.
- The SFM effectively handles content inconsistency issues between RGB-NIR images by assigning appropriate fusion weights to deep NIR and RGB features through global and local modulation modules.
- The paper constructs a paired NIR-assisted real-world image denoising dataset, which is diverse and covers various scenarios and noise levels, providing a valuable resource for future research.
- The proposed method achieves better results than state-of-the-art methods on both synthetic and real-world datasets, demonstrating the effectiveness of the SFM.
- The paper is well-written, making it easy to follow, and the figures and tables are clear, enhancing the readability and comprehensibility of the content.
- The proposed SFM is plug-and-play, making it easy to integrate into existing denoising networks, which is a practical advantage for real-world applications.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed discussion on the limitations of the proposed method, particularly in scenarios where the method may not perform optimally or where it may introduce artifacts.
- The novelty of the SFM is questioned due to its simplicity and the limited technical advancement over existing methods.
- The paper does not provide a detailed comparison with other methods that utilize NIR images for image denoising, which could have strengthened the argument for the effectiveness of the proposed method.
- The performance improvement of the proposed method over existing methods is marginal, which may not justify the added complexity and computational cost.
- The paper does not sufficiently discuss the generalizability of the proposed method to other imaging modalities or scenarios beyond the specific dataset used.
- The dataset used in the study is relatively small, which may limit the statistical significance of the results.

**Questions:**
- Could you clarify the specific technical advancements of the SFM over existing methods, particularly in terms of handling content inconsistency between RGB-NIR images?
- How does the proposed SFM compare in terms of performance and computational cost with other methods that utilize NIR images for image denoising?
- What are the specific scenarios or conditions where the proposed method may not perform optimally, and how does it handle these cases?
- How does the SFM handle the generalization to other imaging modalities or scenarios beyond the specific dataset used in the study?
- Could you provide more details on the limitations of the proposed method and how these might affect its practical applicability in real-world scenarios?
