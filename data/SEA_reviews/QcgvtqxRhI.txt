 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel method for core set selection in deep learning, focusing on the balanced selection of diverse and difficult data samples. It proposes a loss bound that considers both diversity and difficulty in the subset selection process, aiming to optimize model performance by selecting a subset that closely approximates the full set's behavior. The method is evaluated on several datasets, showing improvements over existing methods in terms of accuracy and generalization. The paper also provides theoretical justification for the proposed approach and conducts comprehensive experiments to validate its effectiveness.

- The experimental results are comprehensive and demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed method, showing improvements over existing methods.
- The paper includes a theoretical analysis of the importance of balancing diversity and difficulty in subset selection, which is a significant contribution to the field.
