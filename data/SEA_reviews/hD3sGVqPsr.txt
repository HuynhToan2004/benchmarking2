 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel progressive partial optimal transport (P2OT) algorithm for deep imbalanced clustering, which addresses the challenges posed by imbalanced data distributions in clustering tasks. By integrating imbalance class distribution modeling and confident sample selection within a single optimization problem, the P2OT algorithm enables the generation of imbalanced pseudo labels and learning from high-confident samples progressively. The methodology is validated on several datasets, demonstrating its superiority over existing methods. The paper also proposes a new benchmark for evaluating deep clustering under imbalanced conditions, highlighting its practical relevance and theoretical contributions.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel P2OT algorithm that effectively addresses the deep imbalanced clustering problem by integrating imbalance class distribution modeling and confident sample selection within a single optimization problem.
- The proposed methodology is validated on several datasets, demonstrating its superiority over existing methods and its potential to be a new benchmark for evaluating deep clustering under imbalanced conditions.
- The paper is well-organized, with a clear and concise presentation of the problem, methodology, and experimental results, making it accessible and easy to follow.
- The authors have proposed a novel P2OT algorithm that formulates pseudo label generation as a progressive partial optimal transport problem, enabling the consideration of class imbalance distribution and progressive learning concurrently.
- The method is validated on several datasets, demonstrating its superiority over existing methods on a newly proposed challenging and large-scale benchmark.
- The paper provides a theoretical guarantee for the proposed method and solves it with an efficient scaling algorithm.
