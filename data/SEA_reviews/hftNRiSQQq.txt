 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel approach to task-agnostic reinforcement learning by integrating causal exploration, which involves leveraging causal relationships between variables to enhance the efficiency of data collection and model training. This methodology is aimed at improving the sample efficiency and reliability of world model learning in reinforcement learning environments. The approach employs a causal exploration algorithm that identifies causal relationships among environment variables and utilizes these to optimize data collection and model training. Theoretical guarantees are provided for the convergence of the proposed method, and empirical results demonstrate its effectiveness across various tasks, including synthetic and real-world applications.

- The idea of using causal knowledge to improve data collection and model training is novel and potentially significant, especially in the context of task-agnostic reinforcement learning.
- The experimental results are strong, demonstrating the effectiveness of the proposed method across a variety of tasks.
- The paper provides theoretical guarantees for the convergence of the proposed method, which adds credibility to the proposed approach.
