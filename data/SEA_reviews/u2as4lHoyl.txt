 **Summary:**
The paper introduces ReFACT, a method designed to edit factual associations in text-to-image models without requiring extensive retraining. It focuses on updating the weights of a specific layer in the text encoder, which allows for efficient editing of model parameters. The approach is evaluated on two datasets, TIME and RoAD, demonstrating improvements in editing accuracy and generalization. The method is contrasted with existing techniques like TIME and DreamBooth, showcasing its effectiveness and efficiency. Despite its strengths, concerns are raised about the novelty of the approach and the depth of its comparison with existing methods.

**Strengths:**
- The paper addresses a relevant and timely problem, focusing on updating factual associations in text-to-image models without extensive retraining.
- The proposed method, ReFACT, is efficient and effective, with significant improvements in editing accuracy and generalization over existing methods.
- The method is novel, focusing on updating weights in a specific layer of the text encoder, which is a different approach from previous methods like TIME and DreamBooth.
- The paper is well-written, clear, and easy to follow, making it accessible to readers.
- The experimental results are promising, showing that the method outperforms existing techniques in terms of editing accuracy and generalization.
- The paper includes a new dataset, RoAD, which is a significant contribution to the field and enhances the evaluation of the proposed method.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed comparison with existing methods like TIME, particularly in terms of computational efficiency and the number of parameters updated.
- The novelty of the approach is questioned as it appears to be an extension of existing methods, such as those by Meng et al.
- The paper could benefit from a more comprehensive evaluation, including comparisons with a wider range of methods and datasets.
- The paper does not adequately address the limitations of the proposed method, such as the potential for negative societal impacts and the scalability of the approach.
- The method's effectiveness in handling multiple edits simultaneously and its ability to edit unseen facts are not thoroughly explored.
- There is a lack of discussion on the potential negative societal impacts of the proposed method, which is a significant concern given the potential misuse of such technology.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors clarify the computational efficiency of ReFACT compared to TIME, particularly in terms of the number of parameters updated?
- How does the proposed method compare with other methods that use similar approaches, such as those by Meng et al.?
- Can the authors provide more details on the method's ability to handle multiple edits simultaneously and its effectiveness in editing unseen facts?
- How does the proposed method perform when applied to different models, such as those using different text encoders or different diffusion models?
- Could the authors elaborate on the potential negative societal impacts of the proposed method and discuss how these might be mitigated?
- What are the implications of the method's inability to handle multiple edits simultaneously, and how might this limit its practical application?
