 **Summary:**
The paper investigates the undertraining of CLIP models, particularly those trained on smaller datasets like CC12M, by proposing a simple yet effective training strategy. This strategy involves resetting the learning rate and training for a few additional epochs, which significantly enhances the zero-shot performance on various downstream tasks. The authors demonstrate that this method can improve the CLIP model's performance on benchmarks like ImageNet, ImageNet-V2, ImageNet-Sketch, and ObjectNet. However, the paper's novelty is questioned as similar strategies have been previously explored, and the experimental results are not convincingly superior to existing methods.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, making it easy to follow and understand the proposed methodology.
- The experimental results are promising, showing that the proposed method can improve the performance of CLIP models on various downstream tasks.
- The idea of re-training CLIP models to improve their performance is novel and interesting, particularly for smaller datasets.
- The paper provides a clear and concise description of the proposed method, which is simple yet effective.
- The experimental results demonstrate that the proposed method can achieve state-of-the-art performance on some benchmarks, such as ImageNet and ImageNet-V2.

**Weaknesses:**
- The novelty of the paper is limited as similar strategies have been previously explored, such as the use of additional training epochs to improve CLIP models.
- The experimental results are not convincingly superior to existing methods, as shown in Table 6, where the proposed method performs worse than some existing methods like OpenCLIP.
- The paper lacks a thorough analysis of why the proposed method works, particularly in terms of the learning rate and its impact on model performance.
- There is a lack of comparison with other baseline methods, such as those mentioned in the paper, which could have provided a more robust evaluation of the proposed method.
- The paper does not sufficiently address the generalizability of the findings to other types of datasets or models, which could limit the practical applicability of the proposed method.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors provide a detailed analysis of why the proposed method works, particularly in terms of the learning rate and its impact on model performance?
- How does the proposed method perform on other types of datasets, such as those used in other CLIP models like LAION-400M?
- Could the authors include a comparison with other baseline methods, such as those mentioned in the paper, to provide a more robust evaluation of the proposed method?
- How does the proposed method compare to other recent works that have also explored similar training strategies, such as those mentioned in the related work section?
- Can the authors clarify the experimental setup, particularly in terms of the training data used and the learning rate scheduling, to ensure reproducibility and transparency?
