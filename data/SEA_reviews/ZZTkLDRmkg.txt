 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel neural operator architecture, BENO, designed to address the challenges of inhomogeneous boundary conditions and complex boundary geometries in solving elliptic PDEs. BENO integrates two Graph Neural Networks (GNNs) for interior source terms and boundary values, alongside a Transformer encoder that maps global boundary geometry into a latent vector, influencing each message passing layer of the GNNs. This architecture allows for the effective modeling of boundary conditions, which is critical in the solution of elliptic PDEs. The paper demonstrates that BENO outperforms existing state-of-the-art methods by a significant margin, showing potential in handling complex boundary conditions and geometries.

**Strengths:**
- The paper addresses a significant problem in the field of elliptic PDEs, focusing on the challenges posed by inhomogeneous boundary conditions and complex boundary geometries.
- The proposed method, BENO, is innovative and effective, integrating a dual-branch architecture and a Transformer encoder to handle boundary information, which is crucial for solving elliptic PDEs.
- The paper is well-written, with clear explanations of the methodology and experimental results that demonstrate the effectiveness of BENO in solving elliptic PDEs.
- The inclusion of an ablation study provides insights into the contributions of different components of the model, enhancing the understanding of its functionality.
- The paper is well-motivated, with a clear rationale behind the design choices, which is supported by a thorough literature review and comparisons with state-of-the-art methods.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a comprehensive discussion on the limitations and potential negative societal impacts of the proposed method.
- The experimental setup is somewhat limited, focusing on a specific type of elliptic PDE (Poisson equation) and a narrow range of boundary conditions, which might not generalize well to other types of PDEs or more complex boundary conditions.
- The paper does not adequately address the scalability of the method, particularly in terms of handling larger and more complex domains, which could limit its practical applicability.
- There is a lack of detailed discussion on the computational efficiency and training costs of the proposed method compared to other state-of-the-art methods, which could be critical for practical deployment.
- The paper could benefit from a more detailed explanation of the dual-branch architecture and its specific contributions to the model's performance.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors provide a more detailed discussion on the limitations and potential negative societal impacts of the proposed method?
- How does the proposed method perform when applied to other types of elliptic PDEs beyond the Poisson equation, and what are the implications for its generalizability?
- Can the authors discuss the scalability of the method in terms of handling larger and more complex domains, and what are the potential challenges or limitations in this regard?
- What are the computational costs and training times associated with the proposed method compared to other state-of-the-art methods, and how do these compare in terms of practical deployment?
- Could the authors provide a more detailed explanation of the dual-branch architecture and its specific contributions to the model's performance?
- How does the model handle the integration of the Transformer output for edge message passing, and what are the implications for the model's performance and generalizability?
