 **Summary:**
The paper explores transfer learning in contextual bandits, focusing on the identification of causal effects between actions and rewards through optimization problems. The authors propose a method for sampling compatible causal models to obtain causal bounds, which are then applied to improve classical bandit algorithms. This approach allows for faster convergence rates and improved regret bounds. The paper also includes theoretical and experimental validations, demonstrating the effectiveness of the proposed methods. However, concerns were raised about the clarity of the presentation, the practicality of the assumptions, and the novelty of the contributions compared to existing literature.

**Strengths:**
- The paper addresses an important and timely problem in transfer learning in contextual bandits, which is crucial for improving the performance of learning algorithms in various real-world applications.
- The proposed algorithm is simple, intuitive, and theoretically justified, providing a novel approach to handling function approximation in contextual bandits.
- The paper is well-written, making it accessible and easy to follow, which is essential for a broad audience.
- Theoretical results are supported by rigorous proofs, and the experimental results demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed algorithm.
- The paper provides a comprehensive comparison with previous works, highlighting the contributions and improvements over existing methods.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper suffers from a lack of clarity in some sections, particularly in the explanation of certain equations and the assumptions made.
- The novelty of the contributions is questionable, as similar approaches have been previously explored in other works, such as those by Li and Pearl (2022).
- The assumptions made, such as the realizability assumption and the accessibility of causal bounds, may not hold in practical scenarios, limiting the applicability of the proposed methods.
- There is a lack of discussion on the limitations of the proposed methods, which is crucial for understanding the scope and applicability of the findings.
- The paper could benefit from a more detailed comparison with existing works, particularly in terms of the assumptions and the contributions made.
