 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel approach to convolutional deep kernel machines (DKMs), extending the deep kernel machine framework to handle convolutional layers. This extension is crucial for handling image data effectively, as it allows for the efficient computation of kernels using Gram matrices, thereby reducing computational complexity. The authors propose a new method for inducing point selection, which is critical for the scalability of the model. The methodology is validated through experiments on standard datasets such as MNIST, CIFAR-10, and CIFAR-100, showing competitive results with state-of-the-art methods. Despite the technical advancements, the paper's contributions are seen as incremental, and the experimental results are not compelling enough to fully justify the claims made.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel approach to extending the deep kernel machine (DKM) framework to handle convolutional layers, which is a significant advancement in the field of kernel methods.
- The methodology for inducing point selection is innovative and crucial for the scalability of the proposed method.
- The paper is well-written, making it easy to follow, and the theoretical underpinnings are well-explained.
- The proposed method is technically sound and presents a clear extension of the DKM framework, which is relevant for handling image data effectively.
- The experimental results, while not perfect, are competitive with state-of-the-art methods, demonstrating the practical applicability of the proposed approach.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper's contributions are seen as incremental, as it primarily extends existing methods rather than introducing a fundamentally new approach.
- The experimental results are not convincing enough, with the proposed method not consistently outperforming existing methods on standard datasets.
- The paper lacks a thorough discussion on the limitations and potential negative societal impacts of the proposed method.
- The paper does not sufficiently address the scalability issues associated with the method, particularly in terms of computational complexity and memory requirements.
- The paper's presentation could be improved, with some sections being overly dense and difficult to follow, particularly the mathematical derivations.

**Questions:**

- Could the authors provide a more detailed discussion on the limitations and potential negative societal impacts of the proposed method?
