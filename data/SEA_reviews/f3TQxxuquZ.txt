 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel one-stage prompt-based continual learning (PCL) framework, aiming to reduce computational costs by using intermediate layer token embeddings as prompts, eliminating the need for a separate query ViT. This framework, OS-Prompt, incorporates a Query-Pool Regularization (QR) loss to maintain similarity between the prompt query and the prompt pool, thereby improving representation power. The method is evaluated on benchmarks such as CIFAR-100 and ImageNet-R, showing a significant reduction in computational cost (up to 50%) while maintaining performance. The paper also includes a detailed analysis of the impact of different prompt designs and regularization methods on performance.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-organized, clearly written, and easy to follow, making it accessible to a broad audience.
- The proposed method, OS-Prompt, is simple yet effective, with a novel approach to using intermediate layer token embeddings as prompts, which reduces computational costs.
- The inclusion of a Query-Pool Regularization (QR) loss enhances the representation power of the prompt pool, improving performance.
- The paper provides a thorough analysis of the impact of different prompt designs and regularization methods on performance, which is crucial for understanding the effectiveness of the proposed methods.
