 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel adaptive stochastic gradient algorithm, ASMG, designed for black-box multi-objective optimization (MOO) problems. This method leverages the stochastic gradient approximation to estimate the gradients of the Gaussian smoothed MOO problem, which is then used to optimize multiple objectives simultaneously. Theoretical analysis is provided to ensure the convergence of the proposed algorithm, and empirical results demonstrate its effectiveness across various benchmark problems. Despite its innovative approach, the paper is critiqued for its limited novelty and the lack of comparison with more contemporary baselines, such as those using deep neural networks.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel adaptive stochastic gradient algorithm for black-box multi-objective optimization, which is the first of its kind to provide a theoretical convergence guarantee.
- Theoretical analysis is provided for both convex and non-convex scenarios, enhancing the understanding of the algorithm's behavior in different optimization environments.
- The empirical results are robust, demonstrating the algorithm's effectiveness across various benchmark problems, including synthetic and real-world datasets.
- The paper is well-written, making it accessible and easy to follow, with a clear presentation of the problem and the proposed solution.
- The use of Gaussian smoothed MOO problem formulation is innovative and helps in the convergence analysis of the proposed algorithm.

**Weaknesses:**
- The novelty of the paper is questioned due to similarities with existing methods such as CMA-ES, particularly in the formulation of the black-box multi-task learning problem.
- The paper lacks a detailed comparison with more contemporary baselines, such as those using deep neural networks, which could provide a more comprehensive evaluation of the proposed method.
- The experimental setup is limited to relatively simple datasets, which might not adequately demonstrate the algorithm's performance on more complex, high-dimensional datasets.
- There is a lack of discussion on the convergence rate of the proposed algorithm, which is crucial for understanding its practical applicability.
- The paper does not adequately address the computational complexity and the practical implementation of the algorithm, particularly in high-dimensional scenarios.
- The paper lacks a comprehensive discussion on the limitations of the proposed method, which could help in understanding its applicability and potential shortcomings.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors clarify the novelty of their approach compared to existing methods like CMA-ES, particularly in the formulation of the black-box multi-task learning problem?
- How does the proposed algorithm compare in terms of computational complexity and practical implementation, especially in high-dimensional scenarios?
- Could the authors provide a detailed comparison with more contemporary baselines, such as those using deep neural networks, to better evaluate the proposed method's performance?
- How does the proposed algorithm handle non-convex problems, and what are the implications of this for its practical applicability?
- Can the authors discuss the convergence rate of the proposed algorithm and its practical implications?
- How does the proposed algorithm perform in more complex, high-dimensional datasets, and what are the potential challenges or limitations in such scenarios?
