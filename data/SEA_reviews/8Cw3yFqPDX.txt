 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel Local Differential Privacy (LDP) mechanism for asynchronous federated learning (FL), specifically targeting the FedBuff algorithm. The authors propose a method that allows clients to adjust the amount of noise added to their updates based on the staleness of their models, thus potentially improving the privacy-utility tradeoff. The approach is evaluated on various datasets and shows promising results, including a reduction in the number of communication rounds needed to reach certain accuracy levels compared to traditional Central DP methods. However, concerns are raised about the theoretical underpinnings of the staleness-based privacy enhancement and the robustness of the privacy guarantees under various attack scenarios.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, easy to follow, and includes a comprehensive background on asynchronous federated learning and differential privacy.
- The proposed method is novel, particularly in the use of staleness to enhance privacy in asynchronous FL, which is a significant advancement in the field.
- The paper includes a detailed experimental evaluation that demonstrates the effectiveness of the proposed method in improving privacy-utility tradeoffs.
- The paper is well-motivated and addresses a practical problem in federated learning by introducing a novel approach to enhance privacy while maintaining utility.
- The authors have conducted a thorough evaluation of the proposed method, comparing it to existing methods and demonstrating its effectiveness through experiments.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a theoretical analysis of the staleness-based privacy enhancement, which could strengthen the claims made about its effectiveness.
- The robustness of the proposed method against various attack scenarios, particularly membership inference attacks, is not adequately addressed.
- The paper does not sufficiently discuss the impact of the proposed method on the performance of the server, which is crucial in federated learning setups.
- The paper's claims about the superiority of the proposed method over traditional Central DP methods are not convincingly supported, especially in terms of privacy guarantees.
- The experimental setup and the choice of parameters, such as the number of communication rounds and the privacy budget, are not sufficiently justified or explained.
- The paper could benefit from a more detailed discussion on the limitations of the proposed method and the challenges in achieving the privacy-utility tradeoff in federated learning.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors provide a theoretical analysis of the staleness-based privacy enhancement to strengthen the claims made about its effectiveness?
- How does the proposed method perform against other types of attacks, particularly membership inference attacks?
- What are the implications of the proposed method for the performance of the server, and how does this impact the overall federated learning process?
- Can the authors clarify the choice of parameters, such as the number of communication rounds and the privacy budget, and justify why these values were chosen?
- How does the proposed method compare to other existing methods in terms of privacy guarantees and performance metrics, such as accuracy and communication rounds?
- Could the authors discuss the limitations of the proposed method and the challenges in achieving the privacy-utility tradeoff in federated learning?
