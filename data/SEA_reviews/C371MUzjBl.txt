 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel column generation algorithm for computing team-maxmin equilibrium in adversarial team games. It leverages the Team Belief Directed Acyclic Graph (TB-DAG) to represent coordinated strategies and proposes a more efficient BRO to reduce the transformation cost. The algorithm's convergence is theoretically analyzed, and the paper presents experimental results showing significant improvements over existing methods. Despite the theoretical advancements and practical benefits, the paper's novelty and the clarity of its contributions are questioned, with concerns about the incremental nature of the algorithmic improvements and the complexity of the presentation.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, making it easy to follow, and provides a clear explanation of the algorithm's design and its application to the problem of computing team-maxmin equilibrium in adversarial team games.
- The experimental results are convincing, demonstrating the superior performance of the proposed algorithm over existing methods.
- The paper introduces a novel approach by combining column generation with TB-DAG, which is a significant advancement in the field.
- The theoretical analysis of the algorithm's convergence and the reduction in transformation cost are well-articulated and provide a strong foundation for the proposed method.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper's novelty is questioned as the main algorithmic contributions appear to be a combination of existing methods, such as the use of TB-DAG for column generation and the pure BRO for best response oracle.
- The presentation of the paper is complex and could benefit from simplification, particularly in the introduction of new concepts and terminology.
- The experimental results are not convincingly superior to existing methods, as the paper does not compare the results with state-of-the-art methods under the same conditions.
- The paper's theoretical analysis and the proofs provided are not always clear or convincing, particularly in the proof of Theorem 1 and the explanation of the algorithm's convergence.
- The paper lacks a comprehensive discussion on the limitations of the proposed method and the conditions under which it may not perform optimally.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors clarify the novelty of the main algorithmic contributions and explain how they differ from existing methods?
- How does the proposed method compare to state-of-the-art methods in terms of computational efficiency and scalability, especially in large-scale games?
- What are the specific advantages of using the proposed method over other existing methods, such as those based on TB-DAG or other column generation algorithms?
- Can the authors provide more detailed explanations or examples to support the claims made about the algorithm's convergence and the reduction in transformation cost?
- How does the proposed method handle scenarios where the game tree is very deep or wide, and what are the implications for the algorithm's performance in such cases?
