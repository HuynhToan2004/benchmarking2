 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel post-training quantization method that adjusts channel-wise scaling and offset to minimize distributional mismatch between quantized and full-precision values, aiming to improve the accuracy of low-bit quantization. The method involves channel-wise scaling and offset parameters to address quantization errors and is compatible with existing hardware. The authors evaluate the proposed method on various vision models and demonstrate its effectiveness, showing improvements in accuracy with minimal computational overhead. However, the paper lacks a thorough discussion on the computational complexity and hardware compatibility, which are critical for practical implementation.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-organized, easy to follow, and clearly explains the proposed methodology, making it accessible to a broad audience.
- The proposed method is simple yet effective, addressing the challenges of low-bit quantization by adjusting channel-wise scaling and offset parameters.
- The paper includes comprehensive experimental evaluations, demonstrating the effectiveness of the proposed method across various models and quantization levels.
- The method is compatible with existing hardware and does not require additional memory, which makes it practical for real-world applications.
- The paper includes an ablation study that shows the effectiveness of each component of the proposed method, providing a deeper understanding of its contributions.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a thorough discussion on the computational complexity of the proposed method, which is crucial for understanding its efficiency in practical scenarios.
- The paper does not provide a detailed comparison of the proposed method with other state-of-the-art methods, which could help in evaluating its advantages and disadvantages more comprehensively.
- The paper does not discuss the hardware compatibility of the proposed method, which is essential for understanding its practical applicability across different hardware platforms.
- The paper does not address the issue of non-uniform quantization, which could limit its effectiveness in scenarios where non-uniform quantization is required.
- The paper does not provide a detailed comparison of the proposed method with other low-bit quantization methods, which could help in evaluating its effectiveness more comprehensively.
