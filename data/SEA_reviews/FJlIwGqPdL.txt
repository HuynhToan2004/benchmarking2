 **Summary:**
The paper investigates the application of conformal prediction (CP) in adversarial settings, focusing on the challenges posed by adversarial attacks. It explores the impact of adversarial training (AT) on the performance of CP, highlighting the importance of AT for reducing prediction set sizes and improving robustness. The study introduces two techniques: entropy minimization and beta importance weighting, which aim to enhance the efficiency of CP in adversarial scenarios. The empirical evaluation across various datasets demonstrates that these techniques can improve CP efficiency, although the paper is criticized for its limited theoretical analysis and the lack of a comprehensive comparison with state-of-the-art methods.

**Strengths:**
- The paper addresses an important and relevant problem by combining adversarial training with conformal prediction, which is crucial for improving the robustness of machine learning models.
- The authors provide a thorough empirical evaluation, demonstrating the effectiveness of their proposed methods through rigorous testing across various datasets.
- The paper is well-written, making it accessible and easy to follow, which is essential for a broad audience.
- The research is significant as it tackles the issue of uncertainty quantification in adversarially trained models, which is crucial for safety-critical applications.
- The paper presents an innovative approach by using beta importance weighting and entropy minimization to improve the efficiency of conformal prediction in adversarial settings.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a comprehensive theoretical analysis, which is crucial for understanding the underlying mechanisms and limitations of the proposed methods.
- The experimental setup and the choice of baselines are not clearly justified, which raises questions about the fairness and validity of the comparisons made.
- The paper does not adequately address the limitations of its approach, particularly concerning the generalization of the findings to different model architectures and the scalability of the proposed methods.
- The paper's structure and presentation could be improved to better highlight the main contributions and findings, as some sections seem to detract from the core message.
- The paper's contribution to the field is not clearly articulated, particularly in terms of its novelty and the extent to which it advances the current state of research.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors provide a more detailed explanation of the beta importance weighting and entropy minimization techniques, including the rationale behind their choice and the theoretical underpinnings?
- How do the authors justify the choice of baselines and the experimental setup, particularly in terms of fairness and relevance to the problem at hand?
- What are the specific advantages of the proposed methods over existing methods, such as those mentioned in the paper, and how do these advantages translate into practical improvements in performance?
- Could the authors clarify the experimental results, particularly the discrepancies noted in the prediction set sizes and the robustness of the models under different attack scenarios?
- How do the proposed methods scale with larger datasets or more complex model architectures, and what are the potential limitations or challenges in such scenarios?
- Can the authors elaborate on the theoretical guarantees provided by their methods, especially in terms of the generalization error and the robustness of the models under different attack conditions?
