 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel approach to learning image representations from noisy entity annotated images, focusing on the creation of a new dataset, Image-to-Entities (I2E), which contains 1 billion images and 2 million distinct entities. The dataset is constructed using a named entity recognition model and a CLIP model to extract entities from alt-text and filter them based on their similarity to image embeddings. The paper explores various training recipes including supervised pre-training, contrastive pre-training, and multi-task learning, demonstrating that supervised pre-training is effective for image retrieval tasks. The authors claim that the I2E dataset significantly outperforms existing datasets in terms of size and entity diversity, and the MOFI model, trained on this dataset, achieves state-of-the-art performance on image retrieval tasks.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a new approach for automatically assigning entity labels to images from noisy image-text pairs, which is a significant contribution to the field.
- The proposed Image-to-Entities (I2E) dataset is a valuable resource for the community, offering a large-scale dataset with 1 billion images and 2 million distinct entities, which is a substantial improvement over existing datasets.
- The paper demonstrates the effectiveness of supervised pre-training with large-scale fine-grained entity labels for image retrieval tasks, and the multi-task learning further enhances the performance.
- The methodology is straightforward and easy to follow, with a clear presentation that aids in understanding the complex processes involved.
- The paper provides a comprehensive analysis of the dataset and the proposed method, which is well-supported by empirical results that show the effectiveness of the approach.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed discussion on how the proposed method compares to existing approaches, particularly in terms of the novelty and effectiveness of the entity label extraction and filtering process.
- The dataset's entity distribution is heavily skewed, with most entities having very few images associated with them, which could limit the model's ability to generalize to less common entities.
- The paper does not adequately address the potential negative societal impacts of the work, such as the use of entities associated with hate speech or other harmful content.
- The methodology for extracting entities from text and using them as labels is not sufficiently novel or well-explained, and the paper does not provide a clear explanation of how the entities are used in the training process.
- There are concerns about the scalability of the method due to the potential for increased complexity and computational demands with the addition of entity labels.
- The paper does not provide sufficient details on the experimental setup, such as the number of entities used in classification for each batch and the specifics of the entity extraction process.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors provide a more detailed comparison of the proposed method to existing approaches, particularly in terms of the novelty and effectiveness of the entity label extraction and filtering process?
- How does the proposed method handle the skewed distribution of entities in the dataset, and what steps are being taken to address this issue?
- Can the authors provide more details on the experimental setup, such as the number of entities used in classification for each batch and the specifics of the entity extraction process?
- How does the proposed method perform on downstream tasks, particularly in comparison to other models like CLIP?
- Could the authors clarify the role of entities in the training process and how they are used to improve the model's performance?
- Is there a plan to release the I2E dataset to the public, and if so, what steps are being taken to ensure its quality and usability?
