 **Summary:**
The paper introduces PTGOOD, a novel algorithm for offline-to-online (OtO) reinforcement learning (RL), focusing on exploration strategies to enhance the performance of policies in the online phase. PTGOOD leverages a model-based RL approach, where the offline dataset is used to plan exploration actions, aiming to avoid the bias introduced by policy-constraint mechanisms. The algorithm is evaluated against several baselines and is shown to outperform them in terms of performance and policy convergence. The paper also explores different online exploration strategies and provides a comprehensive evaluation across various environments. However, it lacks a detailed theoretical analysis and comparison with recent related works, which could strengthen its contribution.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, clear, and easy to follow, with a detailed appendix that provides additional insights.
- The proposed algorithm, PTGOOD, is novel and effectively utilizes the offline dataset for exploration in the online phase, which is a significant contribution to the field.
- The evaluation of the algorithm is comprehensive, covering a wide range of environments and baselines, which supports the robustness of the results.
- The paper provides a thorough analysis of online exploration strategies, which is valuable for understanding the challenges and opportunities in the offline-to-online RL setting.
- The introduction of new offline datasets is a valuable contribution to the community, enhancing the availability of resources for research in this area.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed theoretical analysis, which could strengthen the understanding of the proposed algorithm's effectiveness and limitations.
- The comparison with recent related works is insufficient, which could limit the assessment of the algorithm's novelty and effectiveness.
- There is a lack of discussion on the computational complexity and time efficiency of the proposed algorithm, which is crucial for practical implementation.
- The paper could benefit from a more detailed discussion on the limitations of the proposed algorithm and potential directions for future research.
- The experimental setup and results presentation could be improved for clarity and comprehensibility, especially in the presentation of results and the discussion of statistical significance.
- The paper does not adequately address the limitations of the offline dataset, such as the quality and diversity of the data, which could affect the performance of the algorithm.

**Questions:**
- How does the proposed algorithm perform when the offline dataset is of low quality or lacks diversity?
- Can the authors provide a more detailed discussion on the computational complexity and time efficiency of the PTGOOD algorithm?
- How does the proposed algorithm compare with other recent works in the field of offline-to-online RL, particularly those that also leverage exploration strategies?
- Could the authors provide a more detailed discussion on the limitations of the proposed algorithm and potential directions for future research?
- In the context of the experimental results, how do the authors ensure the statistical significance of the reported results, especially in the comparisons between different algorithms?
- How does the algorithm handle the exploration of high-reward regions in the state-action space, especially when the offline dataset may not cover these regions?
