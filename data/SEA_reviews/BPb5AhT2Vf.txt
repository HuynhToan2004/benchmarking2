 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel method for image-to-point cloud registration, utilizing pre-trained diffusion models to enhance feature extraction from both RGB images and depth maps. The approach combines semantic and geometric features, leveraging the semantic consistency between RGB images and depth maps to establish robust correspondences. The method demonstrates significant improvements over existing methods on several benchmarks, including 3DMatch, ScanNet, and KITTI-DC, showing a 20.6% increase in inlier ratio and a 48.6% improvement in registration recall. Despite these strengths, concerns were raised about the method's reliance on specific pre-trained models and the computational efficiency of the feature extraction process.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel approach by leveraging pre-trained large-scale models to unify the modality between images and point clouds, which is a significant advancement in the field of image-to-point cloud registration.
- The method is well-organized and easy to follow, with a clear and logical structure that facilitates understanding.
- The paper presents a comprehensive evaluation, including various datasets and benchmarks, which provides a robust validation of the proposed method.
- The use of diffusion features extracted by depth-to-image diffusion models for image-to-point cloud registration is innovative and shows potential for significant advancements in the field.
- The method achieves state-of-the-art performance on several benchmarks, demonstrating its effectiveness and potential for practical applications.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper does not adequately discuss the limitations of the proposed method, which could provide a more balanced view of its strengths and weaknesses.
- The method's reliance on specific pre-trained models, such as ControlNet and Zoe-Depth, could limit its generalizability and applicability in different scenarios.
- The computational cost of the method is high, particularly due to the use of multiple pre-trained models and the computational intensity of the feature extraction process.
- The paper does not sufficiently compare the proposed method with other recent state-of-the-art methods, which could provide a more comprehensive evaluation of its performance.
- The method's effectiveness might be influenced by the quality of the input depth maps and the accuracy of the depth estimation algorithms used.
- The paper could benefit from more detailed explanations and visualizations of the feature extraction and matching processes, particularly the diffusion features and geometric features.

**Questions:**
- How does the proposed method perform when applied to point clouds that are not generated by depth maps, such as those obtained from LiDAR sensors?
- Can the authors provide more details on the computational efficiency of the method, including the time and memory costs associated with the feature extraction and matching processes?
- Could the authors provide more visualizations of the diffusion features and geometric features to better illustrate their characteristics and effectiveness?
- How does the method handle scenarios where the input depth maps are noisy or inaccurate?
- Could the authors discuss the potential of integrating the proposed method with other state-of-the-art methods to further enhance its performance and applicability?
