 **Summary:**
The paper investigates the VC dimensions of deep neural networks (DNNs) with bounded-rank weight matrices and piecewise polynomial activation functions. It derives upper and lower bounds for the VC dimensions of these networks, showing that they are close to the upper bounds, indicating the tightness of the bounds. The study considers networks with low-rank weight matrices, which are practical and relevant in light of recent findings that neural networks often learn with low-rank weight matrices. The paper also provides theoretical insights into the generalization capabilities of these networks, comparing them to other architectures like ReLU networks. Despite its theoretical contributions, the paper is critiqued for its lack of experimental validation and the potential irrelevance of its findings to practical, full-rank DNNs.

**Strengths:**
- The paper addresses an important and interesting problem in the field of neural networks, focusing on the VC dimensions of networks with bounded-rank weight matrices, which is a novel approach.
- The paper is well-written, with clear definitions and notations, making it accessible to readers.
- The authors provide both upper and lower bounds for the VC dimensions of these networks, which is a significant contribution to the field.
- The theoretical results are supported by proofs and examples, which strengthen the validity of the claims made.
- The paper discusses the implications of the results for generalization bounds and provides a lower bound of a set of ReLU BRFCNNs, which is a notable contribution.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks experimental validation, which is crucial for assessing the practical relevance and applicability of the theoretical findings.
- The motivation behind studying networks with bounded-rank weight matrices is not convincingly justified, especially in terms of their practical relevance and the applicability of the results to full-rank networks.
- The paper's results are based on idealized conditions, which may not reflect real-world scenarios, raising concerns about the generalizability of the findings.
- The paper could benefit from a more thorough discussion on the practical implications of the results and how they might influence the design or training of neural networks.
- The paper's contributions are seen as incremental, building on existing work rather than introducing significant new insights or methodologies.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors provide more practical applications or real-world scenarios where networks with bounded-rank weight matrices are relevant?
- How do the authors envision their results being applied to improve the design or training of neural networks in practical settings?
- Could the authors elaborate on the choice of studying networks with bounded-rank weight matrices and discuss the implications of this choice for the generalizability of the findings?
- In terms of the theoretical contributions, how do the authors see their work contributing to a better understanding or improvement of neural network architectures or training methods?
- Given the idealized nature of the results, how do the authors plan to address the gap between theoretical and practical applications of their findings?
