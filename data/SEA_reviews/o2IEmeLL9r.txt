 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel method called PTGM (Pre-Training Goal-based Models) for enhancing sample efficiency in reinforcement learning (RL) by pre-training goal-based models on large, task-agnostic datasets. The method involves clustering goals in the dataset to form a discrete high-level action space and training a high-level policy to generate goals for subsequent RL tasks. The PTGM framework includes a goal prior model to regularize the behavior of the high-level policy in RL, improving sample efficiency and learning stability. The paper presents experimental results demonstrating the superiority of PTGM over existing baselines in both robotic simulation environments and the open-world Minecraft environment.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel method of pre-training goal-based models for RL, which holds advantages in sample efficiency, learning stability, interpretability, and generalization of low-level skills compared to existing methods.
- The proposed method of clustering in the goal space and pre-training the goal prior model provides effective approaches for enhancing the sample efficiency of training high-level policies given a pre-trained goal-conditioned policy.
- The paper is well-written, easy to follow, and includes a comprehensive ablation study that demonstrates the necessity of each component in PTGM.
- The experimental results validate the effectiveness of the proposed method, demonstrating its capability to learn on diverse domains and solve challenging Minecraft tasks efficiently.
- The paper provides a novel approach to solving long-horizon tasks by pre-training on a large task-agnostic dataset and training a high-level policy to generate goals for subsequent RL tasks, which is a significant advancement in the field of RL.
