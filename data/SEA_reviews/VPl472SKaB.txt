 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a conversational bot named FAQbot, designed to address agricultural queries using a retrieval-based approach. The bot leverages pre-trained large language models (LLMs) to retrieve answers from a database, focusing on the agricultural domain. The authors compare this method with generative and intent classification approaches, showcasing the retrieval-based method's superior performance. However, the paper suffers from significant issues such as insufficient evaluation, lack of clarity in methodology, and inadequate dataset size. The presentation of the paper is also criticized for being unclear and difficult to follow.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel approach using retrieval-based methods for generating answers to agricultural queries, which is a significant contribution to the field.
- The use of pre-trained LLMs to retrieve answers from a database is highlighted as a promising method for handling complex queries in the agricultural domain.
- The paper is well-written and easy to follow, providing a clear explanation of the methodology and the results.
- The integration of retrieval-based QA methods with LLMs is highlighted as a potentially effective approach for handling a wide range of user inquiries.

**Weaknesses:**
- The dataset used in the study is very small, consisting of only 38 question-answer pairs, which raises concerns about the generalizability and robustness of the model.
- The evaluation of the model is insufficient, relying on manual interpretation of the quality of responses, which lacks rigor and objectivity.
- The presentation of the paper is poor, with figures and descriptions being difficult to understand, and the methodology being unclear and hard to follow.
- The paper lacks a comprehensive comparison with other existing models or baselines, making it difficult to assess the effectiveness and novelty of the proposed approach.
- The methodology section is not well-explained, and the paper lacks clarity on how the models were trained and tested, which is crucial for reproducibility and validation of the results.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors provide more details on the dataset used, including its source, size, and diversity? How were the question-answer pairs collected and curated?
- How does the retrieval-based QA model perform when handling out-of-domain queries? Have the authors tested the model on such queries?
- What are the specific steps involved in the training and testing of the retrieval-based QA model? How were the models trained and tested, and what were the evaluation metrics used?
- How does the proposed approach compare with other existing models or baselines in terms of performance and efficiency?
- Can the authors clarify the role and impact of the negative samples in the training process? How were these samples selected and labeled?
- In the context of the retrieval-based approach, how were the question-answer pairs retrieved, and what was the rationale behind the specific retrieval strategy used?
