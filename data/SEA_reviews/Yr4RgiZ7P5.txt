 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a new benchmark, the Distorted Shape Testbench (DiST), to evaluate shape bias in deep learning models. This benchmark assesses models' ability to distinguish between original and distorted images by focusing on shape rather than texture. The authors argue that models trained with style transfer augmentations may not effectively learn global shapes, which is a critical aspect for object recognition. The paper presents experiments on both small and large datasets, comparing human performance with various models, including ViT and CNNs. It also proposes a new training method, DiSTinguish, to enhance models' sensitivity to shape. The results show that DiST can differentiate between models trained with different augmentations, highlighting the need for more effective shape bias evaluations.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a new evaluation metric that challenges the existing shape bias evaluations by focusing on shape rather than texture, which is a significant advancement in the field.
- The proposed method, DiST, is simple, effective, and supported by a well-designed psychophysical experiment that validates its effectiveness.
- The paper is well-written, making it easy to follow, and includes a comprehensive set of experiments that support the proposed method's efficacy.
- The introduction of the DiST testbench and the DiSTinguish training method offers a novel approach to evaluating shape bias in models, which is a critical aspect of object recognition.
- The paper's findings suggest that models trained with style transfer augmentations may not effectively learn global shapes, which is a significant insight into the limitations of current model training methods.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper could benefit from a more detailed explanation of how the DiST testbench operates and how it is different from other shape bias evaluations.
- The results presented in the paper are somewhat limited, with only a few models tested, which may not be sufficient to draw broad conclusions.
- The paper does not adequately address the limitations of the proposed method, such as the scalability of the method to larger datasets and the potential impact of the method on different types of models.
- The paper could benefit from a more thorough analysis of the results, particularly in terms of how the method performs across different types of models and datasets.
- The paper could be improved by including more detailed comparisons with existing methods and by providing more insights into the implications of the findings for future research and applications.

**Questions:**
- How does the DiST testbench compare to other shape bias evaluations, particularly in terms of its effectiveness and the insights it provides?
- Can the authors provide more detailed explanations of how the DiST testbench operates and how it differs from other shape bias evaluations?
- How does the DiST testbench scale to larger datasets, and what are the potential limitations of the method in this context?
- What are the implications of the findings for the broader community, particularly in terms of how they might be applied to improve model training and evaluation?
- Can the authors provide more detailed insights into the methodology used in the psychophysical experiment and how it supports the claims made in the paper?
- How does the method perform across different types of models, and what are the potential implications of these findings for the broader community?
