 **Summary:**
The paper introduces PolyVoice, a novel language model-based framework for speech-to-speech translation (S2ST) that includes a translation language model, a duration language model, and a speech synthesis language model. This framework aims to address the challenges of using semantic units for translation and synthesis, particularly in scenarios where textual information is limited or unavailable. The authors demonstrate the effectiveness of their approach through experiments on Chinese-English and English-Spanish language pairs, showing that PolyVoice outperforms existing models in terms of translation quality and speech naturalness. The paper also explores the use of decoder-only models and the impact of different training methods on the performance of these models.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel approach to speech-to-speech translation using a language model-based framework, which includes a translation language model, a duration language model, and a speech synthesis language model.
- The methodology is clearly explained, making it accessible and understandable to readers.
- The paper presents a comprehensive evaluation of the proposed framework, demonstrating its effectiveness in handling semantic units for translation and synthesis.
- The framework is shown to be effective in scenarios where textual information is limited or unavailable, such as in the translation of unwritten languages.
- The paper is well-written, with clear descriptions of the experiments and results, and includes a detailed discussion on the challenges and potential solutions in the field of speech-to-speech translation.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed comparison with other state-of-the-art models, particularly in terms of the size and complexity of the models used, which could affect the fairness of the comparisons.
- There is a lack of discussion on the limitations and potential negative societal impacts of the proposed framework.
- The evaluation of the framework is limited to a few language pairs, which may not fully demonstrate its generalizability to other languages or scenarios.
- The paper does not provide a detailed analysis of the quality of the semantic units generated by the model, which could affect the overall performance of the translation and synthesis processes.
- The paper does not discuss the potential ethical considerations related to the use of the framework, such as privacy concerns and the potential for misuse.
- The paper does not include a detailed discussion on the scalability and computational efficiency of the framework, which could be critical for practical applications.
