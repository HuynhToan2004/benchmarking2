 **Summary:**
The paper introduces CIPHER, a novel inter-model communication protocol that enables Large Language Models (LLMs) to communicate without natural language, focusing on embedding-level interactions. This approach aims to enhance the performance of debates among LLMs by reducing information loss during natural language communication. The proposed method, which involves averaging token embeddings to generate responses, is evaluated across various reasoning tasks and datasets, showing improvements over existing methods. Despite its innovative approach, the paper has been critiqued for its limited novelty, the lack of extensive experiments, and the potential for improved results with different temperature settings.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written and easy to follow, with clear and logical content.
- The proposed method, CIPHER, is novel and innovative, addressing the issue of information loss in natural language communication by using embedding-level interactions.
- The method is supported by empirical results, showing improvements over existing methods in various reasoning tasks and datasets.
- The paper provides a comprehensive evaluation of the proposed method, including extensive experiments and ablation studies.
- The paper addresses the important topic of enhancing the performance of debates among LLMs, which is crucial for improving the capabilities of AI systems.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed comparison with other state-of-the-art methods, which could have provided a more robust validation of the proposed method.
- The novelty of the method is questioned, as similar approaches have been previously explored in the field of natural language processing.
- The experimental setup and results are not sufficiently detailed or convincing, particularly in terms of the temperature settings used and the generalizability of the results.
- The paper does not sufficiently address the limitations of the proposed method, such as its applicability to LLMs with different tokenizers and the potential for overfitting to specific models.
- The paper does not provide sufficient evidence or theoretical support for some of its claims, such as the superiority of CIPHER over natural language communication in terms of information retention.

**Questions:**
- Could you provide more details on the temperature settings used in the experiments and how they were chosen?
- How does the proposed method compare to other state-of-the-art methods in terms of performance and generalizability?
- Can you clarify the intuition behind the method and provide more evidence or theoretical support for its effectiveness?
- How does the proposed method handle the potential for overfitting to specific models, and what steps have been taken to mitigate this risk?
- Could you expand on the limitations of the proposed method and discuss potential future directions for research in this area?
