 **Summary:**
The paper explores the application of reinforcement learning (RL) in the context of the 3D bin packing problem, utilizing the Dantzig-Wolfe decomposition method to decompose the problem into a set partition and 3D knapsack problem. This approach allows for the RL agent to focus on solving the 3D knapsack problem, while a mixed integer linear programming solver (CPLEX) handles the set partition problem. The methodology involves using a RL agent to optimize the 3D knapsack problem and an exact solver for the set partition problem, aiming to improve the solution quality and reduce the computational complexity. The paper demonstrates that this approach can achieve better solutions compared to existing RL methods in 28/47 instances and improved lower bounds in 17/47 instances.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, making it easy to follow, and provides a comprehensive review of the relevant literature.
- The application of Dantzig-Wolfe decomposition to the 3D bin packing problem is innovative and shows potential for improving solutions and reducing computational complexity.
- The methodology is clearly presented, and the paper provides a detailed explanation of the algorithm and its implementation.
- The paper introduces a new lower bounding technique for the 3D bin packing problem, which is a significant contribution to the field.
- The empirical results demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed method, showing that it can achieve better solutions and lower bounds compared to existing methods.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a thorough discussion on the limitations of the proposed method, which is crucial for understanding its applicability and potential drawbacks.
- The comparison with other methods, particularly in terms of computational efficiency and solution quality, is not comprehensive. The paper does not discuss how the proposed method compares to other exact methods or how it performs under different conditions.
- The paper does not adequately address the scalability of the proposed method, which is a critical concern given the complexity of the 3D bin packing problem.
- There is a lack of discussion on the convergence of the algorithm and how it handles cases where the algorithm does not converge.
- The paper does not sufficiently explore the impact of different hyperparameters on the performance of the algorithm, which could provide valuable insights for practical applications.
- The paper could benefit from a more detailed analysis of the results, particularly in terms of how the proposed method performs under different conditions and how it compares to other methods.

**Questions:**
- How does the proposed method compare to other exact methods in terms of computational efficiency and solution quality?
- Can the authors provide a more detailed discussion on the limitations of the proposed method and how these limitations affect its practical applicability?
- How does the proposed method handle cases where the algorithm does not converge, and what are the implications of this for the quality of the solution?
- Could the authors provide a more detailed analysis of the impact of different hyperparameters on the performance of the algorithm?
- How does the proposed method perform under different conditions, such as varying numbers of items or different types of containers?
- Could the authors provide more details on the computational complexity of the proposed method and how it scales with the size of the problem?
- How does the proposed method handle the issue of sub-optimality when using an RL agent in the sub-problem instead of an exact solver?
