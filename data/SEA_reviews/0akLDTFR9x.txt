 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel algorithm for goal-conditioned reinforcement learning (RL) that utilizes a temporal difference (TD) version of the contrastive predictive coding loss, termed TD InfoNCE. This method is designed to learn representations that encode long-term dependencies, reducing the amount of data required for prediction of future events. The authors claim that their approach is more sample efficient and effective in stochastic environments compared to existing methods. The paper presents experiments on the Fetch benchmark and offline AntMaze tasks, demonstrating the algorithm's superiority over several baselines. However, concerns are raised about the novelty and significance of the contributions, particularly in relation to existing works.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, making it easy to follow and understand.
- The proposed method is simple, elegant, and effectively utilizes the InfoNCE loss for learning representations, which is well-explained.
- The experiments conducted on the Fetch benchmark and offline AntMaze tasks demonstrate the superiority of the proposed method over several baselines.
- The paper provides a detailed comparison with prior work, highlighting the differences and contributions of the proposed method.
- The use of temporal difference (TD) to estimate the occupancy measure is a novel approach that has been empirically validated.
- The paper is well-structured, with clear explanations of the method and its derivation, and includes a detailed appendix that provides additional insights and proofs.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a clear explanation of the advantages of using the InfoNCE loss over the NCE loss, particularly in the context of learning representations.
- The novelty of the work is questioned, as the use of TD-based losses for contrastive learning is not new and has been previously explored in similar contexts.
- The experimental results, while showing some improvements, are not consistently superior across all tasks and environments.
- There is a lack of comparison with more recent and relevant baselines, such as CQL, which might have provided a more robust evaluation of the proposed method.
- The paper does not adequately address the scalability and generalizability of the proposed method, particularly in more complex or varied environments.
- The paper's focus on goal-conditioned RL limits the applicability of the method to a narrower set of problems.

**Questions:**
- Could you clarify the advantages of using the InfoNCE loss over the NCE loss in the context of learning representations?
- How does the proposed method compare to the C-learning approach, particularly in terms of performance and computational efficiency?
- Can you provide more details on the computational complexity and memory requirements of the proposed method compared to other baselines?
- How does the proposed method perform in environments with more complex or varied dynamics, such as those found in robotic manipulation tasks?
- Could you elaborate on the choice of baselines and why more recent or relevant methods were not included in the comparisons?
- How does the proposed method handle different types of stochasticity in the environment, and what are the implications for its performance in such scenarios?
