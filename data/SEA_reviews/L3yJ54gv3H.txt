 **Summary:**
The paper investigates the theoretical underpinnings of ConvResNeXts, a variant of Convolutional Residual Networks, focusing on their ability to learn smooth functions supported on low-dimensional manifolds. The authors demonstrate that ConvResNeXts can adapt to the function smoothness and low-dimensional structures, effectively learning these functions without being affected by the curse of dimensionality. They prove that these networks can approximate functions in the Besov space with polynomial dependence on the intrinsic dimension and exponential dependence on the hidden dimension. The paper also discusses the advantages of overparameterization and the role of weight decay in enforcing sparsity in the network.

**Strengths:**
- The paper provides a theoretical analysis of ConvResNeXts, which are a variant of Convolutional Residual Networks, and offers a novel approach to understanding their performance on low-dimensional manifolds.
- It introduces new theoretical results that demonstrate the effectiveness of overparameterization in ConvResNeXts, showing that they can adapt to the function smoothness and low-dimensional structures.
- The paper is well-written and clearly explains the theoretical results, which are supported by rigorous proofs.
- The study of ConvResNeXts, a less-studied architecture, is an interesting and important direction in understanding the effectiveness of neural networks in different settings.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks empirical validation, which is crucial for verifying the theoretical claims and assessing the practical applicability of the proposed methods.
- The manuscript is somewhat difficult to read, with complex notations and proofs that might be challenging for readers unfamiliar with the specific mathematical concepts used.
- The paper's focus on theoretical results without empirical validation limits its immediate applicability and may not be accessible to a broader audience, including practitioners and those without a strong background in the mathematical concepts used.
- The manuscript could benefit from more detailed explanations and examples, especially in sections where complex mathematical concepts are introduced, to enhance understanding and accessibility.

**Questions:**
