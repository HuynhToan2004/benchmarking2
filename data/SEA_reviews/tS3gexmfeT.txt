 **Summary:**
The paper introduces Fusion Token, a novel tokenization method that enhances Byte Pair Encoding (BPE) by merging n-grams up to 10-grams. This approach aims to improve compression rates and inference speeds by reducing the number of tokens required to represent text. The method is evaluated using JavaScript code and demonstrates improved performance in terms of compression rate and latency. However, the experiments are limited to a single language and small model sizes, raising concerns about its generalizability and scalability. The paper also discusses the potential impact of Fusion Token on larger models and different languages, suggesting that further research is needed to fully understand its benefits and limitations.

- The paper is well-written, clear, and easy to follow, making complex concepts accessible to a broad audience.
- The proposed Fusion Token method is novel and demonstrates improvements in compression rate and latency, particularly in the context of code generation.
- The paper provides a theoretical analysis of the upper bound of entropy, which is relevant and informative.

**Weaknesses:**
- The experiments are limited to a single language and small model sizes, which may not adequately demonstrate the generalizability or scalability of the Fusion Token method.
- The paper lacks a comprehensive comparison with other tokenization methods, such as UnigramLM or other byte-level tokenizers, which could provide a more robust evaluation of the proposed method.
