 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel first-order gradient-based method, FORUM, for solving Multi-Objective Bi-Level Optimization (MOBLO) problems. It reformulates MOBLO as a constrained multi-objective optimization (MOO) problem using the value-function approach, which allows for the efficient calculation of gradients without requiring the Hessian matrix. The paper provides theoretical analysis, including a complexity analysis and non-asymptotic convergence results, and validates the method through extensive experiments on various datasets, demonstrating its effectiveness and efficiency compared to existing methods. However, concerns were raised about the clarity of the paper, the validity of assumptions, and the novelty of the approach compared to existing methods.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written and easy to follow, with a clear and intuitive presentation that makes the content accessible to readers.
- The proposed FORUM algorithm is novel, efficient, and provides a non-asymptotic convergence result, which is a significant contribution to the field of multi-objective bi-level optimization.
- The paper includes a comprehensive experimental study that demonstrates the effectiveness of the proposed method across various datasets, showing improvements over existing methods.
- The authors provide a detailed convergence analysis, which is crucial for understanding the theoretical underpinnings of the proposed algorithm.
- The paper addresses a significant and relevant problem in the field of machine learning, specifically the multi-objective bi-level optimization problem, which has applications in various real-world scenarios.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper contains several typos and grammatical errors that need to be corrected for clarity and professionalism.
- The novelty of the proposed method is questionable as it heavily relies on existing methods such as the value-function approach and the multi-gradient aggregation method, which have been previously used in similar contexts.
- The assumptions made in the paper, particularly in the convergence analysis, are not clearly justified or supported, which could undermine the credibility of the results.
- The paper lacks a detailed comparison with existing methods in terms of computational complexity and memory requirements, which are crucial for understanding the practical efficiency of the proposed method.
- There is a lack of discussion on the limitations of the proposed method, which is essential for a comprehensive understanding of its applicability and potential shortcomings.
- The paper does not sufficiently address the scalability of the proposed method, which is a significant concern for large-scale applications.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors clarify how the proposed method differs significantly from existing methods, particularly in terms of its novelty and practical contributions?
- How does the proposed method perform when compared to existing methods in terms of computational complexity and memory requirements?
- Could the authors provide a more detailed discussion on the limitations of their method and how these might affect its practical applicability?
- How does the proposed method handle scenarios where the assumptions made in the convergence analysis do not hold?
- Can the authors provide more details on the implementation of the FORUM algorithm, including the specifics of the hyperparameter tuning and the experimental setup?
- Is there any theoretical or empirical evidence that supports the claim that the proposed method is more efficient than existing methods, especially in terms of computational complexity and memory requirements?
