 **Summary:**
The paper introduces PACIA, a parameter-efficient adapter for few-shot molecular property prediction using graph neural networks (GNNs). PACIA leverages a hierarchical adaptation mechanism to modulate the message passing process of GNNs, generating adaptive parameters for both the encoder and predictor. This approach allows for efficient adaptation to specific molecular properties and structures, which is crucial in scenarios with limited labeled data. Extensive experiments on MoleculeNet and FS-Mol demonstrate the effectiveness of PACIA, showing improvements over existing methods in few-shot MPP tasks. The paper also explores the use of hypernetworks to generate adaptive parameters, which enhances the model's ability to adapt to different molecule types and properties.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel parameter-efficient adapter, PACIA, which effectively addresses the challenges of few-shot molecular property prediction by adapting to specific molecule types and properties.
- The hierarchical adaptation mechanism proposed in PACIA is both rational and effective, as demonstrated by extensive experiments on MoleculeNet and FS-Mol, which show improvements over existing methods in few-shot MPP tasks.
- The use of hypernetworks to generate adaptive parameters is a novel approach that enhances the model's ability to adapt to different molecule types and properties, which is crucial in scenarios with limited labeled data.
- The paper is well-written, clearly explaining the motivation and design of the proposed method, and includes comprehensive experiments and ablation studies that support the effectiveness of PACIA.
- PACIA's design of using hypernetworks to generate adaptive parameters for few-shot MPP is a novel approach that reduces the number of adaptive parameters, thereby alleviating the risk of overfitting.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper does not compare its method with the latest state-of-the-art methods, such as those introduced in [1] and [2], which could provide a more robust evaluation of PACIA's effectiveness.
- The computational cost of PACIA is higher compared to other methods, which could be a significant drawback in practical applications where computational resources are limited.
- The paper does not discuss the limitations of the proposed method, which could provide a more balanced view of its strengths and weaknesses.
- The design of the hypernetwork, particularly how it generates adaptive parameters for different molecule types and properties, is not thoroughly explored, which could affect the generalizability of the method.
- The paper does not compare PACIA with other parameter-efficient adapters, which could provide a more comprehensive evaluation of its effectiveness.
- The paper does not provide a detailed comparison of the computational cost of PACIA with other methods, which is crucial for understanding its practical applicability.

**Questions:**

- How does PACIA handle scenarios where the labeled data are imbalanced?
- Could you provide more insights into the design of the hypernetwork, particularly how it generates adaptive parameters for different molecule types and properties?
- How does PACIA compare with other parameter-efficient adapters in terms of effectiveness and computational cost?
- Could you provide a detailed explanation of how PACIA handles scenarios where the labeled data are imbalanced?
