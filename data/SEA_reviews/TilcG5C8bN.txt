 **Summary:**
The paper introduces SimWnW, a novel self-supervised learning (SSL) framework designed to reduce computational costs by removing similar blocks in augmented images and feature maps. The methodology is based on a block matching process that identifies and removes similar blocks, aiming to accelerate model convergence without compromising accuracy. The approach is validated through experiments on various datasets, including CIFAR-10, CIFAR-100, and ImageNet, showing up to 54% and 51% computation savings in training from scratch and transfer learning tasks, respectively. Despite its innovative approach, the paper has been critiqued for its limited novelty, potential efficiency issues, and unclear motivation for the method's effectiveness.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, easy to follow, and presents a novel approach to reducing computational costs in self-supervised learning (SSL) by identifying and removing similar blocks in augmented images.
- The proposed method, SimWnW, is simple, intuitive, and can be applied to various SSL methods, demonstrating significant computational savings without compromising accuracy.
- The paper includes a comprehensive experimental evaluation, covering both training from scratch and transfer learning tasks on multiple datasets, and provides a detailed analysis of the impact of different parameters such as block size and similarity threshold.
- The methodology is generic and can be easily applied to different SSL training methods, enhancing its practical utility.

**Weaknesses:**
- The novelty of the approach is limited as similar ideas have been explored in previous works, such as "Efficiently Training Deep Neural Networks Using Gradient Pruning" and "Efficiently Training Deep Neural Networks Using Gradient Pruning," which also address computational efficiency in SSL.
- The paper lacks a clear motivation for the effectiveness of the proposed method, particularly in explaining why removing similar blocks in augmented images and feature maps accelerates model convergence.
- There is a potential inefficiency in the method due to the block-matching process, which might introduce additional computational overhead.
- The method's effectiveness is not convincingly demonstrated across a broader range of datasets, and the performance improvement is marginal in some cases.
- The paper does not adequately address the scalability of the method to larger datasets like ImageNet, and the computational savings are not consistently high across different datasets.
- The method's reliance on block matching for identifying similar blocks might not be optimal, as it could potentially overlook image details that are not directly related to the block's similarity.

**Questions:**
- How does the proposed method compare to other SSL methods that aim to reduce computational costs, such as those mentioned in the weaknesses section?
- Can the authors provide a more detailed analysis of the computational overhead introduced by the block-matching process?
- How does the method perform when applied to larger datasets like ImageNet, and what are the potential challenges or limitations in scaling up the method?
- Could the authors elaborate on the method's motivation and effectiveness, particularly in explaining why removing similar blocks in augmented images and feature maps accelerates model convergence?
- How does the method handle the removal of blocks that are not directly related to the block's similarity but are crucial for maintaining the image's semantic information?
