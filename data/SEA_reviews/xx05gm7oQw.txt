 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel framework, CVLD, for mitigating bias in vision-language models (VLMs) by generating counterfactual image-text pairs. This framework leverages a causal intervention module to produce counterfactual examples that are used to augment training data, thereby reducing bias in model predictions. The paper demonstrates the effectiveness of this approach through experiments on image classification, retrieval, and captioning tasks, showing that the proposed method can improve fairness without significantly impacting performance. The approach is evaluated on various datasets, including COCO and CelebA, and the results indicate that the method can effectively reduce bias in VLMs.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel framework for mitigating bias in vision-language models (VLMs) using counterfactual image-text pairs, which is a novel approach that has not been explored in previous literature.
- The proposed method is simple, intuitive, and effective, with clear explanations and detailed methodology that makes it accessible to readers.
- The experiments conducted are comprehensive, covering various tasks such as image classification, retrieval, and captioning, and the results show that the method can effectively reduce bias without significantly affecting performance.
- The paper addresses an important and timely problem of fairness in VLMs, which is crucial for ensuring that these models do not perpetuate biases from their training data.
- The paper is well-written, with clear figures and tables that aid in understanding the methodology and results.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper does not provide sufficient details on the quality of the generated counterfactual image-text pairs, which is crucial for understanding the effectiveness of the proposed method.
- The evaluation of the method is limited to a few datasets, and it would be beneficial to see how the method performs on a broader range of datasets to validate its generalizability.
- The paper does not discuss the limitations of the proposed method, which could provide a more balanced view of its strengths and weaknesses.
- The method's reliance on high-quality image editing models and large language models (LLMs) may limit its applicability in resource-constrained environments.
- The paper does not adequately address the potential negative societal impacts of the proposed method, which is a critical aspect of evaluating its ethical implications.

**Questions:**
- Can you provide more details on how the quality of the generated counterfactual image-text pairs is ensured? What metrics or measures are used to evaluate their quality?
- How does the method perform on other datasets beyond those used in the experiments? Are there any specific datasets that pose unique challenges for the method?
- The paper mentions that the method can be applied to other tasks beyond the ones tested. Can you provide examples or discuss the potential applications of the method in other domains?
- How does the method perform when applied to different types of VLMs, such as those that use different architectures or training methodologies?
- Can you discuss the limitations of the proposed method and how these might affect its practical application?
- The paper mentions that the method uses GPT-3.5 for text intervention. How does the choice of LLM affect the quality and effectiveness of the generated text prompts?
- The paper does not address the potential negative societal impacts of the proposed method. Can you discuss how these impacts might be mitigated or addressed?
