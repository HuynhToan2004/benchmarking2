 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel algorithm, Robust Policy Improvement (RPI), which interleaves between imitation learning (IL) and reinforcement learning (RL) based on an online estimate of their performance. The RPI algorithm utilizes an ensemble of value function estimators to select the best oracle policy for a given state and employs a robust policy gradient to learn from the selected oracle. Theoretical analysis and empirical evaluations are provided to demonstrate the effectiveness of the RPI algorithm. The paper proposes a robust active policy selection strategy and a robust policy gradient, which reason over whether to perform state-wise imitation from the oracles or learn from its own value function when the learner's performance surpasses that of the oracles in a specific state.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel algorithm, Robust Policy Improvement (RPI), which interleaves between imitation learning (IL) and reinforcement learning (RL) based on an online estimate of their performance.
- The RPI algorithm utilizes an ensemble of value function estimators to select the best oracle policy for a given state and employs a robust policy gradient to learn from the selected oracle.
- The paper provides theoretical analysis and empirical evaluations to demonstrate the effectiveness of the RPI algorithm.
- The paper is well-written, with clear notations and easy-to-follow writing, and includes a comprehensive literature review.
- The proposed algorithm is reasonable and the experimental results are good, demonstrating the effectiveness of the RPI algorithm.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a clear motivation for the proposed algorithm, particularly in explaining why RPI is necessary and how it addresses the limitations of existing methods.
- There is a lack of clarity in the definitions and roles of oracles, which are critical to the functioning of the RPI algorithm.
- The paper does not adequately address the computational complexity of the RPI algorithm, which could be a significant issue in practical applications.
- The experimental results are limited to simple environments, and it is unclear how the RPI algorithm would perform in more complex or realistic settings.
- The paper does not provide a detailed comparison with existing methods, particularly in terms of computational efficiency and performance in different scenarios.
- The paper does not discuss the limitations of the proposed algorithm, which could help in understanding its applicability and potential drawbacks.

**Questions:**
- Could you clarify the role and definition of oracles in the RPI algorithm, especially in terms of their expertise and the criteria for selecting the best oracle for a given state?
- How does the RPI algorithm handle the computational complexity of maintaining an ensemble of value function estimators?
- Can you provide more details on the experimental setup, particularly how the RPI algorithm was implemented in more complex or realistic environments?
- How does the RPI algorithm compare in terms of computational efficiency and performance with existing methods, especially in scenarios where the oracle's performance is suboptimal?
- Could you elaborate on the theoretical guarantees of the RPI algorithm, particularly how they compare to existing methods?
- How does the RPI algorithm handle scenarios where the oracle's performance is poor, and what are the implications for the overall performance of the RPI algorithm in such cases?
