 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel range regularization technique to address the issue of outliers in weight distributions during model training, which can hinder the effectiveness of quantization and compression methods. The authors propose three different types of regularization losses: L-inf, margin loss, and soft-min-max range regularization. These regularizations aim to constrain the weight ranges to enhance the model's quantization performance. The paper evaluates the proposed methods on various models such as MobileNet-V1 and V2, ResNet-18, and MobileBERT, demonstrating improvements in quantization and compression performance. Despite its novel approach, the paper's experimental scope is limited, focusing mainly on specific models and not comparing with the latest quantization and compression methods.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel range regularization technique to address the issue of outliers in weight distributions, which is crucial for effective quantization and compression methods.
- The authors propose three different types of regularization losses: L-inf, margin loss, and soft-min-max range regularization, each with distinct advantages.
- The paper is well-written, clear, and easy to follow, with a clear presentation of the problem, methodology, and results.
- The experiments conducted on various models such as MobileNet-V1 and V2, ResNet-18, and MobileBERT demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed regularization in enhancing quantization and compression performance.
- The paper provides a comprehensive evaluation of the proposed regularization methods, showing improvements in accuracy and model size across different compression techniques.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a broader comparison with the latest quantization and compression methods, limiting the understanding of how the proposed regularization performs against state-of-the-art techniques.
- The experimental results are not comprehensive, with a focus on a limited set of models and a lack of results for different quantization and compression methods.
- The paper does not include a detailed analysis of the impact of regularization on the model's performance in terms of accuracy and latency, especially for models like ResNet-18.
- There is a lack of discussion on the potential negative societal impacts of the proposed methods.
- The paper could benefit from more detailed explanations of the regularization methods and their implications for model training and compression.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors provide a more detailed comparison of the proposed regularization methods with the latest quantization and compression techniques?
- How does the regularization affect the model's performance in terms of accuracy and latency, especially for models like ResNet-18?
- Can the authors discuss the potential negative societal impacts of the proposed methods and how these might be mitigated?
- How do the authors justify the choice of models for their experiments, particularly the focus on smaller models like MobileNet-V1 and V2?
- Could the authors provide more detailed explanations of the regularization methods and their implications for model training and compression?
