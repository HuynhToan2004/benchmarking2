 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel Parameter-Efficient Cross-Task Prompt (PECTP) framework aimed at addressing the challenges of rehearsal-free and memory-constrained Class-Incremental Learning (CIL). The framework is designed to learn a fixed number of cross-task prompts, which are crucial for instructing pre-trained models across multiple tasks. It utilizes a Prompt Retention Module (PRM) to restrict the evolution of cross-task prompts from both outer and inner prompt granularities, ensuring effective performance on incremental tasks. Extensive experiments demonstrate the framework's effectiveness in terms of both performance and memory cost, surpassing existing prompt-extending and prompt-fixed methods.

**Strengths:**
- The proposed method is simple and effective, with a focus on rehearsal-free and memory-constrained Class-Incremental Learning (CIL), which is an important and practical problem.
- The paper is well-written and easy to follow, with clear descriptions of the proposed method and its implementation.
- The experimental results are promising, showing that the proposed method can achieve better performance than existing methods in terms of both average performance and performance after the last task.
- The paper provides extensive ablation experiments to demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed method.
- The motivation behind the research is clear, and the paper effectively summarizes the challenges and limitations of existing prompt-extending and prompt-fixed methods.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed comparison with other methods, particularly in terms of memory cost and performance. More detailed comparisons and analyses are needed to fully understand the advantages of the proposed method.
- The paper does not provide sufficient theoretical analysis or insights into the proposed method, which could enhance the understanding of its effectiveness and efficiency.
- The presentation of the paper needs improvement, particularly in the figures and tables, which could be clearer and more informative.
- The paper lacks a detailed discussion on the limitations of the proposed method, which could help in understanding its applicability and potential challenges.
- The experimental results are not comprehensive enough, and the paper could benefit from including results on more datasets to validate the generalizability of the findings.

**Questions:**
- How does the proposed method perform in terms of memory cost and performance compared to other methods, especially in scenarios with limited memory?
- Can the authors provide more detailed comparisons and analyses to highlight the advantages of the proposed method over existing methods?
- How does the proposed method handle the challenge of rehearsal-free and memory-constrained CIL, and what are the specific contributions of the PRM in addressing these challenges?
- Could the authors clarify the definitions and roles of different parameters and losses mentioned in the paper, such as the inner prompt granularity loss and the outer prompt granularity loss?
- How does the proposed method compare to other methods in terms of computational efficiency and training time, particularly in scenarios with a large number of classes?
