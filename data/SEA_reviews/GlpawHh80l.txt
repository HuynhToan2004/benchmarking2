 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel algorithm, pp-SPA, designed to address the vertex hunting problem, which involves estimating the vertices of a simplex from noisy data. The algorithm is an extension of the successive projection algorithm (SPA) and incorporates a denoising step using a K-nearest neighbor (KNN) approach to improve performance. The paper also provides theoretical analysis, including non-asymptotic bounds, which are claimed to be tighter than existing bounds. The algorithm is tested on various synthetic data, demonstrating its effectiveness. However, the paper's contribution is seen as incremental, and the novelty of the algorithm is questioned, as it is viewed as a simple extension of existing methods.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel algorithm for vertex hunting, which is a significant problem in various fields such as machine learning and statistics.
- The algorithm is supported by theoretical analysis, including non-asymptotic bounds, which are claimed to be tighter than existing bounds.
- The paper provides a clear and concise explanation of the algorithm, making it accessible to a broad audience.
- The authors have provided a detailed theoretical analysis, which is crucial for understanding the algorithm's behavior and performance.
- The paper is well-written, making it easy to follow, and includes a good number of examples that help in understanding the algorithm's application.

**Weaknesses:**

- The paper lacks comprehensive experimental validation, particularly on real-world datasets, which could have strengthened the claims of the algorithm's effectiveness.
- The paper's theoretical analysis is not entirely novel, as it relies on known results from previous works, and the proofs provided are not significantly different from existing ones.
