 **Summary:**
The paper introduces AmortizedPeriod, a novel method for periodicity detection in time series data using a Bayesian framework that integrates deep learning techniques. AmortizedPeriod leverages a Bayesian generative process to capture dependencies of periods, trends, noise, and outliers, and employs an attention-based inference network to learn from previously observed time series. This approach allows for robust handling of missing data and irregular periods. The method is evaluated against state-of-the-art methods on four datasets and demonstrates significant improvements in micro F1-score and inference time. However, concerns about the clarity and completeness of the experimental setup, the novelty of the approach, and the fairness of the comparisons were raised.

**Strengths:**
- The paper addresses an important and challenging problem of periodicity detection in time series data, which is crucial for various applications.
- It proposes a novel method called AmortizedPeriod, which integrates Bayesian statistics and deep learning to capture dependencies of periods, trends, noise, and outliers in time series data.
- The method is robust to missing data and irregular periods, and utilizes an attention-based inference network to learn from previously observed time series, enhancing its ability to handle new, unseen data.
- Extensive experiments are conducted on four datasets, demonstrating significant improvements in micro F1-score and inference time compared to state-of-the-art methods.
- The paper is well-written and easy to follow, making it accessible to a broad audience.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a clear explanation of the intuition behind using Ramanujan subspaces for periodicity detection, and it is unclear how the proposed method differs from existing methods like RobustPeriod.
- The experimental setup is not detailed enough, particularly the selection of datasets and the absence of a comparison with other state-of-the-art methods like AutoPeriod and RobustPeriod.
- There is a lack of discussion on the limitations of the proposed method and how it might perform in real-world scenarios with more complex or noisy data.
- The paper does not provide sufficient details on the inference time of the method, which is crucial for understanding its practical applicability.
- The novelty of the method is questionable as it seems to be a straightforward application of existing techniques without significant innovation.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors clarify the intuition behind using Ramanujan subspaces for periodicity detection and how it differs from existing methods like RobustPeriod?
- How does the proposed method handle irregular periods and missing data, and what are the implications of these assumptions in real-world scenarios?
- Can the authors provide more details on the experimental setup, particularly the selection of datasets and the comparison with other state-of-the-art methods?
- What are the specific advantages of using the AmortizedPeriod method over existing methods, and how does it compare in terms of computational efficiency and effectiveness?
- Could the authors discuss the limitations of their method and how it might perform in more complex or noisy data scenarios?
