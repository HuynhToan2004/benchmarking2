 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel correlated attention mechanism for Transformer models, aimed at enhancing the capture of cross-correlation between different features of multivariate time series (MTS) data. This mechanism, named Correlated Attention Block (CAB), is designed to learn both instantaneous and lagged cross-correlations, and can be integrated into existing Transformer architectures to improve performance in various non-predictive tasks such as imputation, anomaly detection, and classification. The authors demonstrate that CAB can outperform existing models on benchmark datasets, showing its potential to advance the field of MTS analysis.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel correlated attention mechanism that effectively captures cross-correlation between different features of multivariate time series (MTS) data, which is a significant advancement in the field.
- The proposed method is flexible and efficient, able to be seamlessly integrated into existing Transformer architectures, enhancing their performance in non-predictive tasks.
- The paper is well-written, with clear and concise explanations, and includes extensive experiments that demonstrate the advantages of the proposed correlated attention mechanism over existing methods.
- The method is capable of learning both instantaneous and lagged cross-correlations between different variates of MTS, as well as auto-correlation of series, which is a significant improvement over existing models.
- The paper provides a clear motivation for the proposed method and includes a detailed explanation of the method's design and its potential impact on the field.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper does not sufficiently compare the proposed method with other attention-based models for MTS, such as those mentioned in related works, which could provide a more comprehensive understanding of its advantages and limitations.
- The paper lacks a detailed discussion on the limitations of the proposed method, which could help in understanding its scope and applicability.
- The experimental results presented in the paper are not convincing enough to demonstrate the superiority of the proposed method over existing models, particularly in terms of statistical significance and the choice of baselines.
- The paper does not provide a detailed analysis of the computational complexity of the proposed method, which could affect its practical deployment in real-world applications.
