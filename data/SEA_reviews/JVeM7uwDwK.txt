 **Summary:**
The paper introduces QUAG, a non-parametric probe designed to analyze multimodal representations in VideoQA models. By averaging attention weights across modalities, QUAG aims to evaluate the reliance of models on multimodal interactions. The authors also propose a diagnostic dataset, CLAVI, to assess coupled multimodal understanding, focusing on temporal questions and videos with balanced counterfactuals. The study reveals that models can maintain high performance even with impaired multimodal understanding, indicating potential shortcuts in learning. The paper also proposes a simplistic replacement of self-attention, QUAG-attention, which achieves similar performance with less computational cost.

**Strengths:**
- The paper addresses a significant and timely issue in the field of VideoQA, questioning the genuine multimodal understanding of models and proposing a novel probe (QUAG) to assess this.
- The introduction of a new benchmark, CLAVI, provides a robust tool for evaluating models' ability to understand coupled multimodal representations, particularly in the context of video and language.
- The paper is well-written, with clear explanations of the proposed methods and results, making it accessible and easy to follow.
- The experimental results are convincing, demonstrating the effectiveness of the proposed methods in identifying shortcuts and evaluating multimodal understanding.
- The paper provides a detailed analysis of the limitations of current VideoQA models, which could guide future research and development in the field.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed discussion on the limitations of the proposed methods, particularly the potential for over-generalization of findings from specific datasets to broader scenarios.
- There is a concern about the fairness of the evaluation, as the proposed methods might not adequately assess the models' ability to understand complex multimodal interactions beyond simple temporal ordering.
- The paper does not sufficiently explore the potential of the proposed methods in enhancing model performance or generalizability to other types of multimodal tasks beyond VideoQA.
- The experimental setup and results are not sufficiently robust, with a limited number of models and datasets used, which could affect the validity of the conclusions drawn.
- The paper could benefit from a more comprehensive literature review, particularly in the area of multimodal learning and representation, to better position its contributions within the existing body of knowledge.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors clarify the definition and implications of "shortcuts" in the context of the proposed methods?
- How does the proposed method compare to other existing methods for evaluating multimodal understanding in VideoQA models?
- What are the specific contributions of the proposed methods beyond existing approaches, and how do these contributions advance the field?
- Could the authors provide more detailed experimental results, including a broader range of models and datasets, to strengthen the validity of their conclusions?
- How do the authors address the potential limitations and biases in the datasets used, particularly in the context of the CLAVI dataset?
- Can the authors elaborate on the potential applications and implications of the proposed methods beyond the specific tasks and datasets studied?
