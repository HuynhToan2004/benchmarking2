 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a new benchmark for continual learning with vision-language models, specifically focusing on the CLIP model. It constructs three datasets, TIC-DataComp, TIC-YFCC, and TIC-RedCaps, with over 12.7B timestamped image-text pairs spanning nine years, to evaluate the temporal robustness of existing models. The benchmark includes both dynamic and static tasks, and the authors propose a method for continual training that reuses old data and initializes from the last checkpoint, showing a 2.5x reduction in compute cost compared to retraining from scratch. The paper also discusses the limitations of the CLIP model in handling temporal shifts and proposes a method for continual learning that could be beneficial for adapting models to evolving data distributions.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel benchmark for continual learning with vision-language models, specifically focusing on the CLIP model, which is a significant contribution to the field.
- The benchmark is comprehensive, including a large amount of timestamped image-text pairs and both dynamic and static tasks, which are well-designed to evaluate the temporal robustness of models.
- The proposed method for continual training, which reuses old data and initializes from the last checkpoint, is simple yet effective and shows a 2.5x reduction in compute cost compared to retraining from scratch.
- The paper is well-written, with clear figures and a clear presentation of the methodology and results, making it easy to follow and understand.
- The experiments are thorough, with detailed analyses and ablation studies that provide valuable insights into the performance of different methods.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper could benefit from a more detailed discussion on the limitations of the CLIP model in handling temporal shifts and the potential biases in the datasets used.
- There is a lack of discussion on the limitations of the proposed method, particularly in terms of its applicability to other types of models or datasets.
- The paper could be improved by providing more detailed information on the data filtering process and the construction of the dynamic tasks, as well as the evaluation metrics used.
- Some figures and tables are hard to read, and the paper could benefit from better formatting and presentation to enhance readability and comprehension.
- The paper could include more experiments and comparisons with other models or datasets to strengthen the claims made about the effectiveness of the proposed method.

**Questions:**
- Could you provide more details on the data filtering process and the construction of the dynamic tasks, particularly how the timestamps are assigned and how the data is aggregated?
- How do the authors address the potential biases in the datasets used, such as the filtering techniques and the selection of data for training?
- Could you clarify the evaluation metrics used, particularly how the performance is measured across different time steps and the impact of different training strategies on these metrics?
- How does the proposed method compare to other continual learning methods, particularly in terms of computational efficiency and effectiveness?
- Could you discuss the potential applications of the proposed method beyond the CLIP model and the datasets used in the study?
