 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel method for selecting and integrating simulator-generated data into Gaussian Processes (GPs) to improve model performance, particularly in scenarios where training data is limited or inaccurate. The method leverages the negative log marginal likelihood of GPs as a metric to evaluate the utility of simulator-generated data, which is computationally expensive to calculate directly. The authors propose an algorithm that reduces this computational complexity by exploiting the Cholesky factor and matrix element dependencies, thereby making the method more practical. The paper provides empirical evidence supporting the effectiveness of the proposed method, comparing it to existing methods such as the Subset of Data (SoD) approach.

**Strengths:**
- The paper addresses a significant problem in Gaussian Processes (GPs) by proposing a method for selectively incorporating simulator-generated data into GP training data, which is a novel approach.
- The methodology is well-explained, with clear derivations and detailed explanations of the computational efficiency improvements.
- The paper includes a comprehensive literature review and comparison with existing methods, such as the Subset of Data (SoD) approach, which provides a robust context for the proposed method.
- The experiments conducted are convincing, demonstrating the effectiveness of the proposed method in improving the log marginal likelihood and mean squared error (MSE) in various scenarios.
- The paper is well-written, with a clear structure and a logical flow that aids in understanding the complex mathematical concepts.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a clear definition and discussion of the simulator, which is crucial for understanding the context and applicability of the proposed method.
- The novelty of the proposed method is questionable, as it primarily involves the use of a Cholesky factor and matrix element dependencies, which are not entirely novel.
- The paper does not provide sufficient details on the experimental setup, such as the specifics of the simulator used and the choice of parameters, which could affect the reproducibility and generalizability of the results.
- The paper could benefit from a more detailed discussion on the limitations of the proposed method, especially in scenarios where the assumptions of the model do not hold true.
- The paper's writing quality needs improvement, particularly in terms of grammar and formatting, which could affect the readability and professionalism of the manuscript.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors clarify the definition and role of the simulator in the context of their proposed method?
- How does the proposed method compare in terms of computational efficiency and accuracy with other methods that use simulator-generated data, such as those mentioned in the literature review?
- Could the authors provide more details on the experimental setup, including the specifics of the simulator used and the choice of parameters?
- How does the proposed method perform in scenarios where the assumptions of the model do not hold true, and what are the potential negative consequences of this?
- Can the authors discuss the potential limitations and societal impacts of their proposed method, particularly in terms of its applicability and scalability?
