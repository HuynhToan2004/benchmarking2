 **Summary:**
The paper introduces ARIMEC, a novel iterative minimum-entropy coupling (IMEC) algorithm that addresses the challenge of handling large-support distributions in minimum-entropy coupling problems. ARIMEC extends existing IMEC algorithms by using a prefix tree partition set, which enables it to handle arbitrary discrete distributions. This approach is evaluated in two settings: Markov coding games and steganography, showing improvements in communication rates and decoding error rates. The paper also provides theoretical analysis and empirical evidence to support the algorithm's effectiveness and efficiency.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel approach to computing minimum-entropy couplings for large-support distributions, which is a significant challenge in the field.
- The introduction of the prefix tree partition set is a novel and interesting contribution that simplifies the computation of minimum-entropy couplings.
- The paper provides theoretical analysis and empirical evidence to support the effectiveness and efficiency of the proposed algorithm, which is a strong point for the paper.
- The paper is well-written, making it easy to follow, and includes clear explanations and examples that aid in understanding the concepts presented.
- The authors have demonstrated the utility of their algorithm in both Markov coding games and steganography, showing its practical applicability and potential impact in these areas.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper could benefit from a more detailed comparison with existing methods, particularly in terms of computational efficiency and the specific conditions under which ARIMEC outperforms other methods.
- There are some minor issues with the clarity and consistency of the notation used throughout the paper, which could confuse readers.
- The paper could be more accessible to a broader audience by including more intuitive explanations and examples that help illustrate the main ideas and contributions.
- The paper could benefit from a more thorough discussion of the limitations and potential negative societal impacts of the proposed algorithm, particularly in the context of steganography.
- The paper lacks a comprehensive literature review, which could help position the work within the existing body of knowledge and highlight its contributions more clearly.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors provide a more detailed comparison of the computational efficiency of ARIMEC with other existing methods, particularly in scenarios where the support of the distributions is large?
- How does the prefix tree partition set compare to other methods for computing minimum-entropy couplings in terms of computational complexity and practical applicability?
- Could the authors provide a more detailed explanation of how the prefix tree partition set is constructed and used in practice, especially in the context of steganography?
- How does the proposed algorithm handle the potential negative societal impacts of steganography, particularly in terms of privacy and security?
- Could the authors elaborate on the choice of the prefix tree partition set and its implications for the practical implementation of the algorithm?
- How does the proposed algorithm compare to other methods in terms of its theoretical guarantees and practical performance, particularly in scenarios where the support of the distributions is large?
