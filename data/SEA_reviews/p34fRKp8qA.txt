 **Summary:**
The paper presents a novel framework for constructing equivariant neural networks that address the challenges of non-compact and non-abelian Lie groups, specifically focusing on the affine group and its subgroups. The authors introduce a method for sampling from the Haar measure of these groups, which is crucial for approximating convolution integrals. This approach is demonstrated to be effective in affine-invariant image classification tasks, showing robustness and generalization capabilities. The paper is structured around a detailed theoretical discussion on Lie group decompositions and their application to equivariant networks, with a focus on practical implications and experimental validations.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, with a clear presentation of the problem and its solutions, making it accessible to readers.
- The proposed method for sampling from the Haar measure of affine groups is innovative and could be useful in other applications beyond the specific problem addressed.
- The experimental results demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed method, showing that it outperforms previous methods in certain tasks.
- The theoretical framework presented is comprehensive and detailed, providing a solid foundation for the proposed methodologies.
- The paper is significant in its application to affine groups, which are not typically addressed in existing literature, and could have broad implications for other areas of machine learning.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed discussion on the limitations of the proposed method, particularly in terms of its applicability to other types of Lie groups and its scalability to more complex datasets.
- The experimental results are not comprehensive enough, with only one dataset used to evaluate the method's performance.
- The paper could benefit from a more thorough discussion on the computational complexity of the proposed method and its scalability.
- The paper does not adequately compare its method with existing approaches, which could help in understanding the relative advantages and disadvantages of the proposed method.
- The paper's reliance on the affine group, which is not a Lie group, raises questions about the validity of the proposed method within the theoretical framework of Lie groups.

**Questions:**
- Could you clarify the definition of the affine group as a semi-direct product and explain why it is not a Lie group?
- How does the proposed method perform when applied to other types of Lie groups, and what are the specific challenges in extending it to these groups?
- Could you provide more details on the computational complexity of the proposed method and how it scales with the size of the dataset or the complexity of the model?
- How does the proposed method compare with other existing methods in terms of computational efficiency and accuracy?
- Is there a plan to release the code for the proposed method to facilitate reproducibility and further research?
