 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel sparse coding-based network architecture, termed Sparse-Guard, designed to enhance robustness against model inversion attacks. This architecture alternates between sparse coding layers and dense layers, aiming to remove redundant or irrelevant information, thereby preventing the reconstruction of private data from intermediate outputs. The authors demonstrate that Sparse-Guard can maintain comparable or higher classification accuracy while significantly degrading the reconstruction quality of state-of-the-art training data. The paper is well-organized and presents a clear motivation, although it lacks detailed discussions on the underlying mechanisms of sparse coding and its impact on model inversion attacks.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-organized, with a clear motivation and a straightforward methodology.
- The proposed Sparse-Guard architecture is simple yet effective, requiring minimal hyperparameter tuning.
- The paper demonstrates that Sparse-Guard can achieve good robustness to model inversion attacks while maintaining comparable or higher classification accuracy.
- The experimental results show that Sparse-Guard significantly degrades the reconstruction quality of state-of-the-art training data, highlighting its effectiveness.
- The paper provides a cluster-ready PyTorch codebase, which is beneficial for further research.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed discussion on the underlying mechanisms of sparse coding and how it contributes to the prevention of model inversion attacks.
- There is a lack of comparison with other sparse coding-based methods, which could provide a more comprehensive evaluation of the proposed method.
- The paper does not discuss the limitations of the proposed method, particularly in terms of its applicability to more complex datasets or models.
- The paper does not provide a detailed analysis of the training cost of Sparse-Guard, which could be a significant drawback for practical deployment.
- The paper primarily focuses on the effectiveness of Sparse-Guard in preventing model inversion attacks and does not explore its impact on other types of privacy attacks.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors provide more detailed explanations on why sparse coding can prevent model inversion attacks?
- How does the sparsity level of the Sparse-Guard architecture affect its performance in terms of both robustness to model inversion attacks and classification accuracy?
- Is there any other sparse coding-based method that could be compared to the proposed method to validate its effectiveness?
- How does the training cost of Sparse-Guard compare to other methods, and is it practical for deployment in real-world scenarios?
- Could the authors discuss the potential of extending the proposed method to other types of privacy attacks, such as membership inference attacks?
