 **Summary:**
The paper explores the role of exploration in policy gradient methods, focusing on how it impacts the learning objective and gradient estimates. It introduces two criteria, epsilon-coherence and pseudoconcavity, to evaluate the effectiveness of exploration strategies. The authors provide theoretical insights into how these strategies can help in smoothing the learning objective and increasing the likelihood of policy improvement. The paper also discusses the practical implications of these theoretical findings, suggesting that exploration can enhance the performance of policy-gradient algorithms by modifying the learning objective and gradient estimates. However, the paper is criticized for its lack of novelty, as it primarily rehashes existing results and does not provide significant new insights or experimental validations.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, clear, and easy to follow, with a clear and intuitive explanation of the concepts.
- It provides a novel perspective on the role of exploration in policy gradient methods, focusing on how it impacts the learning objective and gradient estimates.
- The paper introduces new criteria, such as epsilon-coherence and pseudoconcavity, which are useful for evaluating the effectiveness of exploration strategies.
- The theoretical insights are relevant and well-presented, contributing to a better understanding of the role of exploration in policy gradient methods.
- The paper is original in its approach, focusing on the effects of exploration on the learning objective and gradient estimates, which is an understudied area in reinforcement learning.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks significant novelty as it primarily rehashes existing results without providing new insights or experimental validations.
- The theoretical contributions are not sufficiently novel, as the concepts of epsilon-coherence and pseudoconcavity are well-known in the optimization community.
- The paper does not adequately address the limitations of the exploration strategies it discusses, such as the potential for exploration strategies to hinder convergence.
- The empirical evidence provided is limited and does not convincingly demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed exploration strategies.
- The paper could benefit from more rigorous experimental validation to support its claims.
