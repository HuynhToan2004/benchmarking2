 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel data augmentation strategy, RelAug, aimed at addressing the imbalance in relation distribution in scene graph generation tasks. RelAug employs both semantic and visual perturbations to enhance the diversity of rare relation classes and increase the sample size of triplets with these labels. This method is evaluated on the Visual Genome dataset and demonstrates state-of-the-art performance. The authors propose a simple yet effective approach to augment scene graph data, which is highlighted by its ability to adapt to existing methods and its potential to be applied to other visual reasoning tasks.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, making it easy to follow and understand the proposed method.
- The method is simple yet effective, with a clear motivation and detailed explanation of the proposed augmentation strategy.
- The results are impressive, showing that the proposed method can produce state-of-the-art performance on the Visual Genome dataset.
- The paper introduces a novel relation augmentation method that effectively balances the relation distribution in scene graph generation tasks, which is a significant contribution to the field.
- The method is easy to adapt to existing methods, making it a practical solution for enhancing the performance of existing models.
- The paper demonstrates the effectiveness of the proposed augmentation method through a comprehensive set of experiments and ablation studies, which validate the method's effectiveness in balancing the distribution of rare relation classes.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed comparison with the state-of-the-art methods, which could provide a clearer understanding of the method's relative performance.
- The method's applicability to other datasets, such as the VG150 dataset, is not thoroughly explored, which could limit the generalizability of the findings.
- The paper does not discuss the limitations of the proposed method, which could provide valuable insights into its potential drawbacks or areas for future research.
- The paper does not include a discussion on the potential negative societal impact of the proposed method, which is an important consideration for ethical and responsible AI development.
- The method's novelty is somewhat limited as it is a straightforward application of existing data augmentation techniques to the scene graph generation task, which may not be seen as a significant advancement.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors provide a detailed comparison with the state-of-the-art methods to better understand the method's relative performance?
- How does the proposed method perform on other datasets, such as the VG150 dataset?
- Can the authors discuss the limitations of the proposed method and provide insights into potential areas for future research?
- What are the potential negative societal impacts of the proposed method, and how do the authors address these concerns?
- How does the proposed method compare in terms of computational efficiency and resource requirements to other data augmentation methods?
- Could the authors provide more details on the implementation and experimental setups to facilitate reproducibility and further research?
