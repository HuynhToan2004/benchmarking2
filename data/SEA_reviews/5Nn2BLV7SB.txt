 **Summary:**
The paper introduces PandaLM, a large language model (LLM) designed to evaluate and optimize hyperparameters for instruction-tuned LLMs. PandaLM is trained on a dataset of human-annotated responses, enabling it to assess the performance of different models based on criteria such as conciseness, clarity, comprehensiveness, and adherence to instructions. The model is evaluated against GPT-3.5, GPT-4, and human annotators, showing comparable or superior performance. The paper also explores the use of PandaLM for hyperparameter optimization, demonstrating its effectiveness in improving model performance over Alpaca's default parameters. Despite its strengths, the paper is criticized for its limited novelty, lack of detailed technical contributions, and potential over-reliance on GPT-3.5 for data collection.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, clear, and easy to follow, with a clear motivation for the work.
- The proposed method, PandaLM, is a novel approach to evaluating and optimizing hyperparameters for instruction-tuned LLMs, which is an important and timely topic.
- The methodology includes a comprehensive evaluation of PandaLM against GPT-3.5, GPT-4, and human annotators, demonstrating its effectiveness and reliability.
- The paper presents a new evaluation benchmark for LLM instruction tuning, which is significant given the current challenges in evaluating LLMs.
- The authors have conducted extensive experiments, including hyperparameter optimization and evaluations across different domains, which provide valuable insights into the effectiveness of PandaLM.

**Weaknesses:**
- The novelty of the paper is limited, as it primarily involves fine-tuning a pre-trained LLM for a specific task without significant technical contributions.
- The dataset used to train PandaLM is primarily derived from GPT-3.5, raising concerns about the model's independence and potential bias from this source.
- The paper lacks detailed technical contributions, particularly in the training and evaluation of PandaLM, which could benefit from more detailed explanations and comparisons with other methods.
- The evaluation of PandaLM is limited to a small set of models (LLaMA-7B, Bloom-7B, Cerebras-GPT-6.7B, OPT-7B, and Pythia-6.9B), which might not sufficiently demonstrate its effectiveness across a broader range of models.
- The paper could benefit from a more detailed discussion on the limitations of the method and the potential biases introduced by using GPT-3.5 as a data source.

**Questions:**
- How does the performance of PandaLM compare when trained on a dataset that does not include GPT-3.5?
- Could the authors provide more details on the training and evaluation of PandaLM, including hyperparameter settings and a comparison with other methods?
- How does PandaLM handle different types of evaluation tasks beyond win/lose/tie?
- Can the authors clarify the process of filtering out noisy data from GPT-3.5 and discuss the potential impact of this process on the model's performance?
- How does PandaLM perform when evaluated on a broader range of models, including those not included in the current study?
