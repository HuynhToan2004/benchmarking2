 **Summary:**
The paper presents a novel method, the Inexact Bilevel Conditional Gradient (IBCG), designed for constrained bilevel optimization problems with a strongly convex lower-level problem and a smooth upper-level objective function. The method is a single-loop projection-free approach that aims to improve per-iteration complexity and achieve optimal convergence rate guarantees. Theoretical analysis and numerical experiments are provided to demonstrate the method's effectiveness. The paper also addresses the challenges of existing methods, such as slow convergence rates or high computational costs per iteration, by proposing a new algorithm that utilizes nested approximation techniques to estimate the hyper-gradient and avoids the need for projections.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel single-loop projection-free method for constrained bilevel optimization, which is a significant contribution to the field.
- The method is well-presented, with clear explanations and intuitive descriptions, making it accessible to readers.
- The paper provides a comprehensive theoretical analysis of the proposed method, including convergence rate guarantees and complexity analysis.
- The numerical experiments are convincing, demonstrating the superior performance of the proposed method compared to existing state-of-the-art methods.
- The paper is well-written and organized, with a clear presentation of the methodology and results.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper could benefit from a more detailed comparison with existing methods, particularly in terms of computational complexity and convergence rates.
- The theoretical analysis is limited to strongly convex lower-level problems, which may not generalize well to more complex scenarios.
- The paper lacks a discussion on the practical implications of the theoretical results, such as how they translate into real-world applications.
- There are some minor typographical errors and inconsistencies in the paper, which could be corrected for clarity.
- The paper does not adequately address the limitations of the proposed method, such as the need for a strongly convex lower-level problem and the potential for high computational costs in certain scenarios.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors provide more details on how the proposed method can be extended to handle non-convex lower-level problems?
- How does the computational complexity of the proposed method compare to existing methods, especially in terms of per-iteration complexity?
- Is there a possibility to include a numerical comparison of the proposed method with existing methods to better demonstrate its advantages?
- Can the authors clarify the practical implications of the theoretical results, particularly in terms of how they can be applied in real-world scenarios?
- How does the proposed method handle scenarios where the lower-level problem is not strongly convex?
- Could the authors elaborate on the choice of step sizes and their impact on the convergence rate of the algorithm?
