 **Summary:**
The paper investigates the compositional generalization of vision-language models (VLMs) using CLIP, specifically focusing on the impact of dataset size and diversity on this capability. The authors introduce a new dataset, ImageNet-AO, to assess CLIP's performance in unseen compositions of attributes and objects. They find that larger, more diverse datasets lead to better disentanglement in text and image representations, enhancing CLIP's compositional generalization. The study also explores the relationship between disentanglement and CLIP's performance, suggesting that the model's ability to disentangle text and image representations is crucial for compositional generalization. However, the paper has been criticized for its lack of novelty, insufficient comparisons with other models, and unclear presentation, particularly in the experimental setup and results.

**Strengths:**
- The paper addresses a significant and timely issue by investigating the compositional generalization of CLIP, which is crucial for understanding the model's capabilities in real-world applications.
- The creation of a new dataset, ImageNet-AO, provides a valuable resource for future research and enhances the study's credibility.
- The paper presents a comprehensive evaluation of CLIP models using various metrics, including Z-Diff scores, which provides insights into the model's performance and disentanglement capabilities.
- The authors provide a detailed analysis of the factors influencing CLIP's compositional generalization, such as dataset size and diversity, which is crucial for improving the model's performance.
- The paper is well-written, making it accessible and easy to follow, which facilitates understanding of the complex concepts and results.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks novelty as it primarily focuses on CLIP models without exploring other VLMs, which could provide a more comprehensive understanding of compositional generalization.
- The experimental setup and results are not clearly explained, which makes it difficult for readers to understand the methodology and the implications of the findings.
- There is a lack of comparison with other models, particularly self-supervised methods, which could provide insights into the effectiveness of different training strategies.
- The paper's claims about the necessity of compositional diversity for CLIP's disentanglement are not convincingly supported by the data, particularly in the absence of comparisons with other models.
- The paper's writing and presentation need improvement, particularly in the introduction and methodology sections, to enhance clarity and logical flow.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors clarify the experimental setup and results, particularly how the datasets were used and how the results were obtained?
- How do the authors justify the use of only CLIP models in their study, and what would be the implications of including other VLMs in the analysis?
- Can the authors provide more detailed comparisons with other models, particularly self-supervised methods, to better understand the effectiveness of different training strategies?
- How do the authors address the concerns regarding the novelty and contribution of the paper, especially in light of existing research on CLIP's compositional generalization?
- Could the authors provide a more detailed explanation of the dataset creation process and the selection criteria for the objects and attributes used in the study?
