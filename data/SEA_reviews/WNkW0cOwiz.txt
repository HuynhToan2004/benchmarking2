 **Summary:**
The paper explores the Lipschitz singularities in diffusion models, specifically focusing on the infinite Lipschitz property of the network with respect to the time variable near the zero point. It argues that this issue, characterized by large Lipschitz constants, can affect both training and inference processes, leading to instability and inaccuracy. The authors propose a novel method, E-TSDM, which uniformly divides the target interval near the zero point into multiple sub-intervals and shares the same condition values within each sub-interval. This approach aims to reduce Lipschitz constants near the zero point to zero, thereby improving the performance of diffusion models. The paper includes theoretical proofs and empirical results to support the existence and impact of these singularities, and demonstrates the effectiveness of E-TSDM through various experiments on diverse datasets.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, clear, and easy to follow, with a clear motivation and a well-articulated problem statement.
- The proposed method, E-TSDM, is simple yet effective, and is supported by both theoretical analysis and empirical results.
- The paper provides a comprehensive analysis of the Lipschitz constants in diffusion models, which is both novel and insightful.
- The proposed method achieves significant improvements in performance, as evidenced by substantial reductions in Frechet Inception Distance and improvements in other metrics.
- The paper is well-organized, with detailed experiments and ablation studies that support the claims made.

**Weaknesses:**

- The theoretical analysis and proofs provided are somewhat limited, and the paper could benefit from a more rigorous mathematical treatment.
- The paper does not discuss the limitations of the proposed method in depth, particularly in relation to its scalability and applicability to different types of diffusion models.
