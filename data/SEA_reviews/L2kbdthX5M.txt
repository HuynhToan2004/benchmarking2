 **Summary:**
The paper introduces SignAvatars, a large-scale 3D sign language dataset, which includes 70,000 videos from 153 signers, totaling 8.34 million frames. The dataset covers both isolated and continuous, co-articulated signs, with multiple prompts including HamNoSys, spoken language, and words. The authors propose an automated annotation pipeline to generate 3D holistic motion annotations, including meshes and biomechanically-valid poses of the body, hands, and face, as well as 2D and 3D keypoints. The paper also introduces a benchmark for evaluating 3D sign language production from diverse inputs, demonstrating the potential of the dataset for various applications in the field of sign language research.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces SignAvatars, a large-scale multi-prompt 3D sign language motion dataset, which is a significant contribution to the field of sign language research.
- The dataset is comprehensive, including 70,000 videos from 153 signers, totaling 8.34 million frames, which covers both isolated signs and continuous, co-articulated signs with multiple prompts.
- The authors have proposed an automated annotation pipeline to generate 3D holistic motion annotations, which includes meshes and biomechanically-valid poses of the body, hands, and face, as well as 2D and 3D keypoints.
- The paper provides a new benchmark for evaluating 3D sign language production from diverse inputs, which is a significant step forward in the field.
- The dataset is curated to facilitate various tasks such as 3D sign language recognition and 3D sign language production from diverse inputs like text scripts, individual words, and HamNoSys notation.
- The paper is well-written, making it easy to follow, and the proposed methodology is detailed and clear, enhancing the understanding of the research.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed explanation of how the automated annotation pipeline operates, particularly how it handles the complex interactions between hands and respects biomechanical hand constraints.
- There is a lack of clarity regarding the accuracy and quality of the automated annotation pipeline, which is crucial for the reliability and utility of the dataset.
- The paper does not provide a detailed comparison with existing datasets, which could help in understanding the novelty and advantages of the proposed dataset.
- The paper lacks a discussion on the limitations and potential negative societal impacts of the proposed dataset and methodology.
- The evaluation of the dataset and the proposed methodology is limited, with only a few metrics used to assess the performance, which might not fully capture the quality and effectiveness of the dataset and the methodology.
- The paper could benefit from a more comprehensive literature review and a clearer discussion on the potential applications and societal impacts of the proposed dataset.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors provide more details on how the automated annotation pipeline operates, particularly how it handles the complex interactions between hands and respects biomechanical hand constraints?
- How accurate and reliable is the automated annotation pipeline, and what are the implications for the quality and utility of the dataset?
- Could the authors clarify the process of collecting data from different sources and how the data was integrated into the dataset?
- What are the specific contributions of the proposed dataset compared to existing datasets, and how does it address the limitations of these datasets?
- How do the authors plan to address the potential negative societal impacts of the dataset, particularly in terms of privacy and data security?
- Could the authors provide more details on the evaluation metrics used and consider including additional metrics to more comprehensively assess the quality and effectiveness of the dataset and the methodology?
