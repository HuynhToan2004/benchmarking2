 **Summary:**
The paper introduces SymPoint, a novel method for panoptic symbol spotting in computer-aided design (CAD) drawings. SymPoint represents CAD drawings as point clouds, leveraging a Point Transformer to extract features and a Mask2Former-like head for prediction. The method utilizes an attention with connection module (ACM) and a contrastive connection learning scheme (CCL) to enhance local connection information and discriminability. Additionally, a KNN interpolation mechanism is employed to handle primitive mask downsampling. The proposed method demonstrates significant improvements over existing methods on the FloorPlanCAD dataset, achieving state-of-the-art performance. However, the paper is criticized for its limited evaluation on a single dataset and for not providing sufficient comparisons with state-of-the-art methods.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-organized, easy to follow, and presents a novel approach by treating CAD drawings as point clouds, which is a significant shift from traditional methods.
- The proposed method, SymPoint, achieves state-of-the-art performance on the FloorPlanCAD dataset, demonstrating its effectiveness and potential for further research.
- The use of a Point Transformer for feature extraction and a Mask2Former-like head for prediction is innovative and shows promise in handling local connections and primitive mask downsampling.
- The paper includes comprehensive ablation studies that demonstrate the effectiveness of each component of the proposed method, providing a solid foundation for further research and development.
- The methodology is straightforward and effective, with the use of KNN interpolation being particularly noteworthy for its simplicity and efficiency.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks sufficient comparisons with state-of-the-art methods, particularly in terms of computational efficiency and the ability to handle different types of CAD drawings beyond floorplans.
- The evaluation is limited to the FloorPlanCAD dataset, which may not fully demonstrate the generalizability of the proposed method to other types of CAD drawings or real-world scenarios.
- The paper does not provide a detailed comparison of computational efficiency between the proposed method and existing methods, which is crucial for understanding the practical applicability of the proposed method.
- The paper does not discuss potential limitations or societal impacts of the proposed method, which could be important for understanding the broader implications of the work.
- The paper's novelty is somewhat limited as it primarily replaces existing modules with point cloud-based modules, which may not be seen as a significant advancement by some reviewers.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors provide more detailed comparisons with state-of-the-art methods, including computational efficiency and the ability to handle different types of CAD drawings?
- How does the proposed method perform on other types of CAD drawings beyond floorplans?
- Can the authors provide more qualitative results to better illustrate the effectiveness of the proposed method?
- How does the proposed method compare in terms of training time and inference speed with other methods, especially those that use rasterization?
- Could the authors discuss the potential limitations or societal impacts of the proposed method, and how these might be addressed in future work?
