 **Summary:**
The paper introduces DINAR, a novel privacy-preserving method for Federated Learning (FL) that specifically targets the most sensitive layers of neural networks, where privacy information leakage is most significant. This methodology, which includes obfuscation of these layers and adaptive gradient descent to maintain model accuracy, is tested against various datasets and baseline methods. The results demonstrate that DINAR effectively reduces membership inference attack success rates without significant computational overhead or accuracy loss. However, the paper's methodology is limited to white-box settings and does not consider the impact of different privacy budgets, which could affect the generalizability and robustness of the findings.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, making it easy to follow and understand.
- The proposed method, DINAR, is simple, intuitive, and effective, reducing membership inference attack success rates without significant computational overhead or accuracy loss.
- The paper includes comprehensive experiments on six widely used datasets and four neural networks, comparing DINAR with five state-of-the-art FL privacy protection mechanisms.
- The experiments demonstrate that DINAR is more effective than existing methods in terms of privacy protection and model utility.
- The paper provides a detailed analysis of the privacy-utility trade-off and includes a comparison with existing methods, highlighting the advantages of DINAR.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper primarily focuses on white-box settings, which may limit the generalizability of the findings to other settings.
- The methodology does not consider different privacy budgets, which could affect the robustness and effectiveness of the proposed method.
- The paper lacks a detailed comparison with other fine-grained methods, which could provide a more comprehensive evaluation of DINAR's effectiveness.
- The paper does not discuss the potential limitations of the proposed method, such as its applicability to different types of attacks or its effectiveness in different settings.
- The paper does not provide a detailed discussion on the impact of different privacy budgets on the effectiveness of the proposed method.
- The paper does not compare the proposed method with other fine-grained methods, which could provide a more comprehensive evaluation of its effectiveness.
