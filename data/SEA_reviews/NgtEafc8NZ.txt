 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel off-policy trust region optimization approach, Vlearn, which eliminates the need for an explicit state-action-value function and instead utilizes a state-value function as the critic. This approach simplifies the learning process by reducing computational complexity and enhances performance in complex environments with high-dimensional action spaces. Vlearn is validated through experiments on various benchmark tasks, demonstrating its ability to outperform existing methods in some cases. The paper also addresses the challenges of pure state-value function learning in the off-policy setting, providing a more efficient approach to off-policy policy gradient algorithms.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, with clear and well-explained ideas, making it easy to understand.
- The introduction of the V-trace loss as an upper bound to the importance-weighted Bellman Loss and its application in off-policy policy gradient using the state-value-function is a novel and interesting approach.
- The paper provides a comprehensive analysis of the importance sampling for full Bellman error, which is a significant contribution to the field.
- The experiments conducted on various benchmark tasks demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed method, showing improved performance over existing methods in some cases.
- The paper is structured well, with clear explanations of the methodologies and their implications.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a thorough comparison with existing off-policy trust region optimization methods, which limits the evaluation of its novelty and effectiveness.
- The experimental results do not consistently show superior performance over existing methods like SAC and MPO, particularly in lower-dimensional tasks.
- There is a need for more detailed ablation studies to understand the contributions of individual components of the proposed method.
- The paper could benefit from a more comprehensive discussion on the limitations and potential negative societal impacts of the proposed method.
- The paper could be improved by including a more detailed discussion on the advantages and disadvantages of the proposed method compared to existing methods, and by providing more detailed experimental results and comparisons.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors clarify the differences between the proposed method and existing off-policy trust region optimization methods, and discuss how the proposed method improves upon or differs from these existing methods?
- Why was the decision made to compare the proposed method with on-policy TRPL instead of off-policy TRPL?
- How does the proposed method handle the potential issues of high variance in the early stages of training, as mentioned in the paper?
- Could the authors provide more detailed experimental results and comparisons, including the performance of the proposed method on different tasks and in comparison to other methods?
- What are the specific advantages and disadvantages of the proposed method compared to existing methods, and how do these impact the practical applicability and effectiveness of the method?
