 **Summary:**
The paper introduces CrossQ, a novel algorithm for model-free off-policy reinforcement learning in continuous control environments. CrossQ employs a low Update-to-Data (UTD) ratio of 1, which is significantly lower than the state-of-the-art methods like REDQ and DroQ, yet achieves comparable or better sample efficiency. The algorithm leverages techniques such as batch normalization and wider critic networks to achieve these results. CrossQ eliminates the need for target networks, which is a significant departure from traditional methods. The paper presents a comprehensive empirical study that demonstrates CrossQ's effectiveness across various MuJoCo environments, showing that it matches or surpasses the performance of existing methods while reducing computational costs.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, clear, and easy to follow, making it accessible to a broad audience.
- The proposed algorithm, CrossQ, is simple and effective, achieving comparable or better sample efficiency than existing methods like REDQ and DroQ.
- The empirical results are comprehensive, demonstrating that CrossQ can be applied to a wide range of continuous control environments and shows good sample efficiency.
- The paper provides a detailed ablation study that helps in understanding the contributions of each component of the algorithm.
- The use of batch normalization in the critic network is innovative and helps in reducing the need for target networks, which is a significant departure from traditional methods.
- The paper is well-motivated and the authors have provided a clear rationale for the choices made in their algorithm, which enhances the credibility of their findings.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a thorough theoretical analysis of the proposed algorithm, which could help in understanding the underlying mechanisms and limitations of CrossQ.
- The empirical results are limited to a relatively small number of environments, which might not be sufficient to establish the robustness and generalizability of the findings.
- There is a lack of comparison with other recent methods such as DropTarget, which could provide a more comprehensive evaluation of CrossQ's performance.
- The paper does not discuss the limitations of the proposed algorithm, which could help in understanding the scenarios where CrossQ might not perform optimally.
- The paper could benefit from a more detailed discussion on the computational efficiency of CrossQ, particularly in terms of memory usage and the scalability of the algorithm to larger, more complex environments.
- The paper could provide more detailed explanations for certain figures and results, such as the performance of CrossQ on Ant and the effects of different hyperparameters on the algorithm's performance.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors provide more details on the computational efficiency of CrossQ, particularly in terms of memory usage and scalability to larger, more complex environments?
- How does the performance of CrossQ compare when using different network architectures, such as convolutional networks for image-based RL environments?
- Could the authors elaborate on the performance of CrossQ on Ant and explain why it does not perform as well as other methods in this environment?
- What are the implications of using a low UTD ratio of 1 on the performance of CrossQ, especially in environments where high UTD ratios are typically used?
- How does the algorithm perform when using different hyperparameters, and what are the effects of these changes on the algorithm's performance?
- Could the authors provide more details on the experimental setup and the choice of environments for the ablation studies?
- What are the potential limitations of CrossQ, and in what scenarios might other methods be more effective?
