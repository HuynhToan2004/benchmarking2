 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a comprehensive benchmark dataset for long-term time series forecasting, featuring both synthetic and real-world datasets. It presents a variety of models for evaluation, including traditional and state-of-the-art methods like LSTM, DeepAR, and PatchTST. The dataset, which is large and diverse, is intended to facilitate fair and comprehensive evaluations of long-term time series forecasting methods. The paper also proposes new models such as a latent NLinear model and a curriculum learning phase for DeepAR, which have been tested on this benchmark and shown to outperform their vanilla counterparts.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a large, diverse benchmark dataset for long-term time series forecasting, which is crucial for evaluating and comparing different models effectively.
- The dataset includes both synthetic and real-world datasets, ensuring a comprehensive evaluation of model performance across various scenarios.
- The paper proposes new models such as a latent NLinear model and a curriculum learning phase for DeepAR, which are novel contributions to the field.
- The benchmarking analysis conducted is extensive, involving classical and state-of-the-art models, which provides a comprehensive view of model performance across different methods.
- The paper is well-written and easy to follow, making it accessible to a broad audience.
- The proposed dataset is significant in size, which is beneficial for training and testing machine learning models.

**Weaknesses:**

- The evaluation metrics used, such as Mean Squared Error (MSE) and Mean Absolute Error (MAE), may not fully capture the performance of the models, especially in terms of predictive accuracy and generalizability.
