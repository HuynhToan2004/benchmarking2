 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel fair feature importance score, FairFIS, designed to interpret the fairness of features in decision trees and other tree-based models. The metric is based on the bias reduction from parent to child nodes, using demographic parity (DP) and equality of opportunity (EQOP) as group fairness metrics. The authors demonstrate the effectiveness of FairFIS through simulations and real-world examples, comparing it to traditional feature importance scores. The methodology is straightforward, yet the paper's presentation and experimental validation are criticized for lacking depth and clarity.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written and easy to follow, with clear definitions and intuitive explanations of the proposed methods.
- The concept of using bias as a feature importance score is innovative and addresses a significant gap in the literature.
- The paper provides both synthetic and real-world examples to validate the proposed method, enhancing its credibility and applicability.
- The methodology is simple and straightforward, making it accessible and potentially useful for practitioners.
- The authors have attempted to provide a fairness perspective to feature importance, which is a novel and relevant approach in the context of machine learning model interpretability.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a thorough literature review, particularly in the area of fairness metrics and feature importance scores, which could have provided a richer context and deeper insights.
- The experimental setup and results are not convincingly presented, with limited comparisons to existing methods and insufficient detail on the experimental setup.
- The paper does not adequately address the scalability of the proposed method, especially in large-scale datasets, which could limit its practical applicability.
- The paper does not sufficiently address the computational complexity of the proposed method, which could be a significant drawback in terms of its applicability to large-scale datasets.
- The paper's contribution is seen as limited, as the proposed method appears to be a simple adaptation of existing feature importance scores, lacking in novelty and depth.
- The paper could benefit from a more rigorous evaluation and a deeper exploration of the method's impact on model performance and fairness.

**Questions:**
- Could you elaborate on the computational complexity of the proposed method and discuss its scalability, especially in large-scale datasets?
- How does the proposed method compare to other existing methods for fairness-aware feature importance, and what are the advantages of using FairFIS over these methods?
- In the context of the paper's claims about the importance of fairness in machine learning models, could you provide more concrete examples or case studies that demonstrate the practical implications of using FairFIS in real-world scenarios?
- How does the proposed method address the issue of bias in tree-based models, particularly in scenarios where the bias might not be immediately apparent or measurable?
- Could you provide more details on the experimental setup, including the specific datasets used, the methodology for calculating fairness metrics, and the statistical significance of the results?
- The paper mentions that FairFIS can be used to interpret other models besides decision trees. Could you provide examples or theoretical justifications for this claim?
