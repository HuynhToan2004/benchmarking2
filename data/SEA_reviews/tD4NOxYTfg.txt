 **Summary:**
The paper discusses the convergence properties of Variance Exploding (VE) diffusion models, focusing on a new forward Variance Exploding Stochastic Differential Equation (VE-SDE) with a small drift term that converts data into pure Gaussian noise as variance explodes. The authors introduce a new framework that allows for unbounded diffusion coefficients, which is a departure from previous theoretical works. This framework provides a polynomial sample complexity for VE-based models under the manifold hypothesis, offering a faster convergence rate. The paper also explores the balance of error sources in synthetic experiments to support the theoretical results. However, the manuscript suffers from issues such as unclear writing, missing references, and potential inaccuracies in the literature review.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel framework for analyzing Variance Exploding (VE) diffusion models, which is a significant contribution to the field.
- The authors provide a polynomial sample complexity for VE-based models under the manifold hypothesis, which is a notable advancement in the theoretical understanding of these models.
- The paper is well-structured, with clear and detailed explanations that make the content accessible and understandable.
- The authors have conducted synthetic experiments to support their theoretical results, which is a valuable validation of their theoretical claims.
- The paper addresses a practical problem in the field of diffusion models, focusing on the convergence properties of VE-based models, which is a topic of significant interest and relevance.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper suffers from unclear writing and lacks detailed explanations in some sections, which makes it difficult to follow.
- There are several missing references and inaccuracies in the literature review, which undermine the credibility of the paper.
- The paper does not adequately discuss the limitations of the proposed framework, which is crucial for understanding the scope and applicability of the results.
- The manuscript does not sufficiently compare the results with existing literature, which is necessary to establish the novelty and significance of the findings.
- The paper's theoretical contributions are not convincingly linked to practical applications, which could limit the impact of the research.
- The paper lacks a comprehensive discussion on the assumptions made and their implications, which is essential for understanding the robustness and generalizability of the results.

**Questions:**
- Could you clarify the role and significance of the drift term in the new forward VESDE process?
- How does the paper address the balance of error sources in synthetic experiments, particularly in the context of the variance exploding property of VESDE?
- Can you provide more details on the choice of parameters such as tau and beta, and how they affect the convergence rate and sample complexity?
- How does the paper's framework handle the manifold hypothesis, and what are the implications of this hypothesis on the results?
- Could you elaborate on the differences between the proposed framework and existing works, particularly in terms of the manifold hypothesis and the unified framework for VE-based methods?
- How do the authors plan to address the limitations of the framework, such as the exponential dependence on R and delta in Theorem 2?
