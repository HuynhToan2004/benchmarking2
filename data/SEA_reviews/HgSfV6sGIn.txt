 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel method called STExplainer for global-level explainability of Graph Neural Networks (GNNs) by identifying frequent subtree patterns across datasets. The approach involves enumerating and extracting L-hop rooted subtrees based on frequency, which are then used to generate induced explanation subgraphs. This method aims to provide more intuitive and visualizable interpretations of GNN behavior on each class of graph samples. The paper also discusses the optimization method for quantitatively computing the relative importance of extracted subtrees and subgraphs, and uses node embeddings to represent graphical patterns of subtrees for efficient assessment. However, the method's effectiveness is questioned due to its reliance on frequent subtree patterns which may not be representative of all data instances. The paper also lacks comprehensive comparisons with other global GNN explainers and does not provide sufficient quantitative evaluation metrics.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel approach to global-level GNN explanation by directly enumerating and extracting L-hop rooted subtrees based on frequency, which is a significant advancement over existing methods.
- The proposed method offers more intuitive and visualizable interpretations of GNN behavior on each class of graph samples, making it a valuable tool for understanding and explaining the behavior of GNNs.
- The paper is well-written, making it easy to follow and understand, with clear explanations of the proposed method and its implementation.
- The use of node embeddings to represent graphical patterns of subtrees is an innovative approach that allows for efficient assessment of knowledge gained from a training set and its applicability to new graph instances.
- The paper provides a comprehensive evaluation of the proposed method, including comparisons with existing methods and demonstrations of its effectiveness on both synthetic and real-world datasets.

**Weaknesses:**
- The method relies solely on the enumeration and extraction of frequent subtree patterns, which may not fully capture the complex decision-making processes of GNNs.
- The paper lacks a thorough comparison with other global GNN explainers, particularly in terms of the ability to identify and explain the reasoning behind the decision-making processes of GNNs.
- There is a notable absence of quantitative evaluation metrics to assess the effectiveness of the proposed method, which could strengthen the paper's claims.
- The paper does not adequately address the limitations of the proposed method, such as the potential for overfitting to the training set and the lack of generalizability to unseen data.
- The paper does not provide sufficient details on the experimental setup and hyperparameter tuning, which are crucial for reproducibility and validation of the results.

**Questions:**
- How does the proposed method compare with other global GNN explainers in terms of the ability to identify and explain the reasoning behind the decision-making processes of GNNs?
- Can the proposed method be adapted to other types of GNNs, such as those based on graph attention mechanisms?
- How does the method handle the potential for overfitting to the training set, and what steps are taken to ensure that the extracted subtree patterns are generalizable to unseen data?
- Could you provide more details on the experimental setup, including the specific GNN models used, the hyperparameter tuning process, and the statistical significance of the reported results?
- How does the method ensure that the extracted subtree patterns are representative of the entire dataset, and not just the frequent patterns?
- Could you clarify the definitions and roles of certain parameters and variables used in the method, such as the subtrees P and the frequency function φ(P,G)?
