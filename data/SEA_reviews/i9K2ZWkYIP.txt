 **Summary:**
The paper investigates the scaling laws of sparse Transformers, focusing on the impact of sparsity on model performance and its relationship with training data and model size. It proposes a novel scaling law that incorporates sparsity, extending the existing scaling laws for dense models. The authors empirically validate this law across various Transformer architectures and datasets, demonstrating the optimal sparsity levels and their impact on model performance. The study also explores the implications of sparsity on model efficiency and computational costs, providing insights into the practical deployment of sparse models in real-world applications.

**Strengths:**
- The paper addresses an important and relevant topic in the field of large language models (LLMs), specifically focusing on the scaling laws for sparse Transformers, which is a significant gap in existing research.
- The empirical validation of the proposed scaling law is robust, involving multiple Transformer architectures and datasets, providing a comprehensive understanding of the impact of sparsity on model performance.
- The paper is well-structured and clearly written, with a logical flow that makes it easy to follow. The authors provide a detailed explanation of the methodology, experimental setup, and results, which are supported by comprehensive visualizations and data analysis.
- The study is significant as it provides insights into the optimal sparsity levels for different model sizes and training budgets, which is crucial for practical deployment of sparse models in real-world applications.
- The findings of the study have implications for the practical deployment of sparse models, particularly in terms of their efficiency and computational costs, which are critical for the scalability and accessibility of large language models.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a thorough theoretical analysis of the scaling law, which could enhance the understanding of the underlying mechanisms and provide a deeper insight into the observed phenomena.
- There is a need for more comprehensive experiments to validate the proposed scaling law, particularly in terms of the generalizability of the findings across different architectures and datasets.
- The paper does not sufficiently discuss the limitations of the study, which could include the applicability of the findings to different model architectures, the impact of different sparsity patterns, and the practical deployment challenges of sparse models.
- The paper could benefit from a more detailed discussion on the practical implications of the findings, particularly in terms of the practical deployment of sparse models and the challenges associated with their implementation.
- The paper does not sufficiently address the potential societal impacts of the findings, which could include ethical considerations and the broader implications of sparse models in real-world applications.
