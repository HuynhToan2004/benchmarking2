 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel adversarial training method for enhancing the robustness of Graph Neural Networks (GNNs) against adversarial attacks. The proposed method leverages an out-of-distribution (OOD) detection mechanism to identify and eliminate adversarial edges, thereby improving the adaptive robustness of GNNs. The authors demonstrate that traditional adversarial training methods can lead to incorrect feature-structure-label mappings, which is addressed by their new approach. Extensive experiments on multiple datasets show that the proposed method outperforms existing defenses in terms of adaptive and non-adaptive robustness. However, concerns are raised about the theoretical underpinnings of the method, the clarity of the writing, and the novelty of the approach compared to existing methods.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written and easy to follow, with clear figures and a comprehensive experimental setup.
- The idea of using OOD detection for adversarial robustness is novel and interesting, with theoretical support provided.
- The proposed method is simple and easy to implement, making it accessible for practical applications.
- The paper includes a comprehensive set of experiments that demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed method in both evasion and poisoning attacks.
- The authors provide a detailed analysis of the trade-off between the effectiveness and defensibility of attacks in the context of graph adversarial attacks.
- The method is adaptable to any GNN model and does not require specific properties to function effectively.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a clear definition and discussion on the out-of-distribution (OOD) data, which is crucial for understanding the proposed method's effectiveness.
- There is a noticeable absence of theoretical guarantees or proofs to support the claims made about the robustness of the proposed method.
- The paper does not provide a detailed analysis of the computational complexity or time efficiency of the proposed method, which is essential for practical implementation.
- The novelty of the proposed method is questioned as similar approaches have been previously explored, such as using OOD detection for adversarial robustness in GNNs.
- The paper does not sufficiently differentiate its method from existing approaches, and the theoretical analysis provided is considered insufficient.
- The paper could benefit from a more detailed discussion on the limitations and potential negative societal impacts of the proposed method.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors clarify the definition and role of OOD data in their proposed method?
- How does the proposed method ensure the robustness of the GNN model against different types of adversarial attacks, such as feature perturbations?
- Can the authors provide a more detailed analysis of the computational complexity and time efficiency of their method?
- What are the specific advantages of the proposed method over existing approaches, particularly in terms of novelty and effectiveness?
- How does the proposed method perform under different hyperparameter settings, and what are the recommended settings for optimal performance?
- Can the authors elaborate on the theoretical underpinnings of their method and provide proof or theoretical guarantees to support their claims?
