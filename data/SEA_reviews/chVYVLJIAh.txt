 **Summary:**
The paper investigates decision-aware model learning in reinforcement learning (RL), focusing on the IterVAML and MuZero algorithms, which are crucial in model-based RL. The authors highlight the importance of latent models in achieving good performance and analyze the biases in MuZero's value function learning in stochastic environments. They also discuss the theoretical implications of these biases and empirically validate their findings across various environments. The paper contributes by providing a clear exposition of the theoretical underpinnings and experimental validations, aiming to enhance the understanding and application of decision-aware model learning in RL.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written and easy to follow, making complex concepts accessible to a broad audience.
- The theoretical analysis of MuZero and IterVAML is thorough, providing a deeper understanding of these algorithms.
- The paper presents a comprehensive empirical study, validating the theoretical findings across multiple environments.
- The discussion on the role of latent models in decision-aware model learning is insightful, offering a new perspective on the effectiveness of these models.
- The paper addresses an important and timely question about the role of decision-aware losses in model-based RL, which is crucial for advancing the field.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper primarily focuses on two algorithms, which might limit its applicability to other decision-aware model learning methods.
- The experimental setup and results, particularly in stochastic environments, are not convincingly superior to existing methods like BYOL.
- The theoretical analysis is not entirely novel, as similar results have been previously established, which might diminish the perceived contribution of the paper.
- The paper lacks a broader discussion on the implications of the findings for other model-based RL methods, which could enhance its relevance and impact.
- There are some presentation issues, such as minor typos and inconsistencies in notation, which could detract from the professional quality of the paper.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors clarify the discrepancy in the performance of MuZero and IterVAML in stochastic environments as depicted in Figure 2?
- How does the paper's theoretical analysis compare to existing works, particularly in terms of novelty and depth of insight?
- What are the potential implications of the findings for other model-based RL methods, and how might these insights be applied to enhance their performance?
- Could the authors provide more detailed comparisons between the model-based policy gradient and the deep deterministic policy gradient, especially in the context of stochastic environments?
- Can the authors elaborate on the differences in the model-based policy gradient between MuZero and IterVAML, and how these differences affect the performance in stochastic environments?
