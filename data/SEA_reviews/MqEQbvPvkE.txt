 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel method, TRESNET, for estimating the shift-response function (SRF) using neural networks, specifically designed to handle non-continuous outcomes from the exponential family. This method is applied to a dataset of 68 million individuals and 27 million deaths across the US, focusing on the impact of revising the US National Ambient Air Quality Standards (NAAQS) for PM2.5. The authors demonstrate that their method can estimate the causal effect of exposure shifts with robustness and efficiency guarantees, which is crucial for policy-relevant questions in public health. The paper also discusses the theoretical underpinnings of the method, including double robustness and asymptotic efficiency, and provides a comprehensive evaluation against baselines.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, clear, and easy to follow, with a clear presentation of the problem and a well-structured methodology.
- The paper introduces a novel neural network method for estimating SRFs with robustness and efficiency guarantees, which is a significant contribution to the field of causal inference.
- The methodology is sound, and the paper is well-positioned within the existing literature, with a clear discussion of the relationship to prior work.
- The application of the method to a dataset of 68 million individuals and 27 million deaths across the US is a significant contribution to the field of causal inference, demonstrating the scalability and applicability of the proposed method.
- The paper provides a comprehensive evaluation of the method, including simulations and real-world data analysis, which supports the claims made about the method's effectiveness and applicability.

**Weaknesses:**

- The paper could be improved by including more detailed comparisons with existing methods, particularly in terms of computational efficiency and the scalability of the method to larger datasets.
- There is a lack of discussion on the assumptions made in the paper, particularly the assumption that the counterfactual distribution is known, which is a significant limitation for the applicability of the method in real-world settings.
- The paper could benefit from a more detailed discussion of the theoretical underpinnings of the method, particularly the assumptions and implications of the double robustness and asymptotic efficiency properties.
