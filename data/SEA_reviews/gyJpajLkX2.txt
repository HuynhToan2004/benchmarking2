 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel method, InfoTime, for multivariate time series forecasting that leverages mutual information to optimize the learning process. It employs two components: CDAM, which maximizes the joint mutual information between latent representations, historical series, and target series, and TAM, which maximizes the mutual information between adjacent sub-sequences of forecasted and target series. The method is designed to improve forecasting accuracy by minimizing the mutual information between historical series and latent representations, thereby enhancing the model's ability to focus on relevant features. The approach is evaluated on several datasets, showing promising results compared to existing models. However, the paper lacks detailed theoretical justification for the mutual information-based loss function and the selection of hyperparameters, which could affect the reproducibility and robustness of the results.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel method for multivariate time series forecasting that utilizes mutual information to optimize the learning process, which is a significant advancement in the field.
- The methodology is clearly presented, making it accessible and understandable to readers.
- The paper is well-structured, with clear figures and tables that aid in understanding the proposed methods and results.
- The experiments conducted are extensive, covering a wide range of datasets and models, demonstrating the effectiveness of the proposed approach.
- The use of mutual information to guide the learning process is innovative and provides a new perspective on time series forecasting.
- The paper is well-written, making it easy to follow and comprehend the proposed methodologies and their implications.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed theoretical justification for the mutual information-based loss function, which is crucial for understanding the theoretical foundations of the proposed method.
- The selection of hyperparameters, particularly the Lagrange multiplier and the downsampling factor, is not adequately justified, which could affect the reproducibility and robustness of the results.
- The paper does not provide a comprehensive comparison with other state-of-the-art methods, which could help in evaluating the effectiveness and efficiency of the proposed approach.
- The paper does not discuss the limitations of the proposed method, which is essential for a complete understanding of its applicability and potential drawbacks.
- There is a lack of discussion on the computational complexity of the proposed method, which is crucial for understanding its scalability and efficiency.
- The paper could benefit from a more detailed discussion on the choice of datasets and the impact of different hyperparameters on the results.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors provide a detailed theoretical justification for the mutual information-based loss function?
- How were the hyperparameters, particularly the Lagrange multiplier and the downsampling factor, selected? What was the rationale behind these choices?
- How does the proposed method perform in scenarios where the time series data is noisy or contains outliers?
- Could the authors discuss the limitations of the proposed method and provide examples or scenarios where it might not be effective?
- How does the proposed method compare in terms of computational complexity with other state-of-the-art methods?
- What are the implications of the downsampling process on the forecasting accuracy, and how does this affect the model's ability to capture long-term dependencies?
- How does the choice of datasets affect the results, and what are the implications of using different datasets with varying characteristics?
- Could the authors provide a more detailed discussion on the choice of hyperparameters and their impact on the results?
