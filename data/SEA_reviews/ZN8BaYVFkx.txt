 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel approach to enhancing the adversarial robustness of Spiking Neural Networks (SNNs) by incorporating gradient sparsity regularization. The authors demonstrate that SNNs exhibit greater resilience to random perturbations compared to adversarial perturbations, which is theoretically justified by an upper bound on the performance gap between adversarial and random perturbations. This gap is attributed to the gradient sparsity of the output probability after the softmax layer with respect to the input image. The paper validates the effectiveness of this approach through extensive experiments on the CIFAR-10 and CIFAR-100 datasets, showing significant improvements in robustness against adversarial attacks. However, the paper's approach to gradient sparsity regularization is not entirely novel, and the paper's contribution to the field is questioned due to its reliance on existing techniques.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel approach to enhancing the robustness of Spiking Neural Networks (SNNs) through gradient sparsity regularization, which is supported by both theoretical analysis and extensive experiments.
- The authors provide a clear and comprehensive analysis of the robustness of SNNs and the disparity between adversarial vulnerability and random vulnerability, which is upper bounded by the sparsity of the gradient of the output with respect to the input image.
- The paper is well-written, easy to follow, and includes a comprehensive related work section, which provides a good overview of the current state of the field.
- The experiments conducted are extensive, covering a range of attack settings and comparing the proposed method with state-of-the-art approaches.
- The paper is significant in its contribution to the field of SNNs, which are gaining increasing attention for their potential in energy-efficient operations and biological inspiration.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper's approach to gradient sparsity regularization is not entirely novel, as similar techniques have been previously explored in the literature.
- The paper lacks a clear explanation of why gradient sparsity can lead to better robustness against adversarial attacks, and the theoretical analysis does not convincingly support this claim.
- The paper does not adequately discuss the limitations of its method, particularly the trade-off between robustness and clean accuracy, and the potential negative societal impacts of the research.
- The paper does not provide sufficient details on the implementation of the gradient sparsity regularization, which could affect the reproducibility of the results.
- The paper's experimental results show that the proposed method can only achieve a clean accuracy of around 70% on CIFAR-10 and CIFAR-100, which is significantly lower than state-of-the-art methods.
- The paper's claims about the superiority of SNNs in handling random perturbations are not convincingly supported by the experimental results, which do not show a significant difference in robustness between SNNs and ANNs.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors provide a clearer explanation of how gradient sparsity leads to better robustness against adversarial attacks, and how this relates to the theoretical analysis presented in the paper?
- How does the proposed method compare to other methods that also use gradient sparsity regularization, such as those mentioned in the literature?
- Why does the proposed method achieve a clean accuracy of around 70% on CIFAR-10 and CIFAR-100, while state-of-the-art methods can achieve significantly higher accuracies?
- How does the proposed method perform against other types of adversarial attacks beyond those tested in the paper, such as CW attacks?
- Can the authors clarify the experimental setup, particularly the choice of adversarial perturbations and the comparison with other state-of-the-art methods?
- How does the proposed method address the trade-off between robustness and clean accuracy, and what steps are being taken to improve this trade-off in future work?
