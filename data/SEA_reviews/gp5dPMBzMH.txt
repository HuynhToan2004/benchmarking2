 **Summary:**
The paper introduces BELT-2, a novel multi-task model designed to enhance both encoding and decoding performance from EEG signals. BELT-2 is the first to adopt byte-pair encoding (BPE)-level EEG-language alignment and integrates multi-task training and decoding in the EEG domain. It leverages a prefix-tuning method to connect the multi-task EEG encoder with large language models (LLMs), demonstrating significant advancements over prior techniques in both quantitative and qualitative measures. The model shows significant improvements in tasks such as translation, summarization, and sentiment classification, achieving a BLEU-1 score of 52.2% on the ZuCo dataset and a sentiment classification accuracy of 74.62%. The paper also introduces a novel discrete querying conformer (Q-Conformer) to improve encoding capacity and multitasking capabilities.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written and easy to follow, making it accessible to a broad audience.
- It introduces a novel discrete querying conformer (Q-Conformer) to improve encoding capacity and multitasking capabilities, which is a significant advancement in the field.
- The authors propose a cost-effective solution for connecting an EEG encoder with a large language model (LLM) by turning virtual prefixes, which is a practical and innovative approach.
- The paper demonstrates significant advancements over prior techniques in both quantitative and qualitative measures, including a BLEU-1 score of 52.2% on the ZuCo dataset and a sentiment classification accuracy of 74.62%.
- The introduction of byte-pair encoding (BPE)-level EEG-language alignment and the integration of multi-task training and decoding in the EEG domain are pioneering contributions to the field.
- The paper is the first to achieve EEG summarization with a SOTA BLEU-1 score, which is a significant achievement.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed explanation of the training process, particularly the training process for the Q-Conformer, which is crucial for understanding the model's effectiveness.
- The paper does not compare its results with the state-of-the-art (SOTA) on the same dataset, which limits the ability to assess the model's performance relative to the current best practices.
- The paper does not provide a detailed analysis of the limitations of the proposed method, which is essential for understanding the practical applicability and potential improvements of the model.
- There is a lack of discussion on the generalizability of the proposed method to other datasets or scenarios, which is crucial for assessing the model's applicability in real-world settings.
- The paper does not provide a detailed comparison of the training time and computational resources required for the proposed model, which is essential for assessing the practical feasibility of the model in real-world applications.

**Questions:**
- Could you provide more details on the training process for the Q-Conformer, particularly how the model is trained to optimize its performance?
- How does the proposed method perform on other datasets or scenarios, and what are the limitations of the method in these contexts?
- What are the specific computational resources and training times required for the proposed model, and how do these compare to other models in the field?
- Can you provide more details on how the model handles different types of EEG data, such as those from different subjects or different recording conditions?
- How does the model handle the variability in EEG data, and what steps are taken to ensure that the model remains effective across different EEG datasets?
- Could you provide more details on the generalizability of the proposed method to other tasks or scenarios, and what steps are being taken to expand the application of the model beyond the specific tasks studied in this paper?
