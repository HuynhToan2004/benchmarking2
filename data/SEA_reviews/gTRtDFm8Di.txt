 **Summary:**
The paper introduces R3, a novel test-time adaptation (TTA) algorithm designed to handle out-of-distribution (OOD) data in test data streams. The algorithm identifies OOD data using energy scores and cosine similarity, then filters and recycles these data into an auxiliary loss to facilitate selective feature transfer. The approach is evaluated on datasets like CIFAR-10-C and ImageNet-C, showing improvements over existing methods. However, the paper's methodology and its experimental setup have been critiqued for their novelty and depth of evaluation. The use of well-known techniques like energy scores and mixup raises questions about the true innovation of the proposed method.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, clear, and easy to follow, with a clear motivation for the problem and a well-designed methodology.
- The proposed method, R3, is innovative and shows promising results in improving TTA performance, particularly in the context of OOD-contaminated data streams.
- The paper includes comprehensive experiments and ablation studies that support the effectiveness of the proposed method, demonstrating its ability to handle different data distributions and batch sizes.
- The inclusion of a detailed hyperparameter sensitivity analysis provides valuable insights into the robustness of the method under varying conditions.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper's novelty is limited as it primarily combines well-known techniques like energy scores and mixup, which may not sufficiently differentiate it from existing methods.
- The experimental setup is somewhat limited, focusing on a few datasets and not including more diverse or realistic datasets like ImageNet-1k, which could better validate the method's performance across different scenarios.
- The method's reliance on pre-defined thresholds for OOD detection might limit its applicability in more complex or dynamic scenarios where OOD data is less predictable.
- There is a lack of discussion on the computational overhead introduced by the proposed method, which could be a significant concern for practical deployment.
- The paper's claims about the novelty and effectiveness of the method are not adequately supported by the experimental results, particularly in terms of the statistical significance and the generalizability of the findings.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors provide more detailed results on the performance of R3 in different OOD detection scenarios, particularly in cases where OOD data is less predictable or more diverse?
- How does the computational overhead of the proposed method compare to other TTA methods, and what are the implications for practical deployment?
- Can the authors clarify the method's performance in scenarios where the OOD data is not as predictable or similar to the training data, such as in the case of ImageNet-1k?
- What is the rationale behind the choice of energy score and cosine similarity thresholds, and how do these choices impact the method's effectiveness in different scenarios?
- Could the authors provide more detailed statistics on the performance improvements claimed in the paper, particularly in terms of the statistical significance and the generalizability of the findings?
