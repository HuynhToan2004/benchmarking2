 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel evaluation framework for reinforcement learning (RL) algorithms under distribution shifts, employing time-series analysis to measure agent performance. It proposes a methodology that includes counterfactual analysis and comparative evaluation using metrics like pointwise plots and cumulative plots. The framework is tested across various environments, including single-agent and multi-agent scenarios, and aims to provide a robust evaluation of RL algorithms in real-world conditions. Despite its innovative approach, the paper is criticized for its lack of rigorous mathematical formulation, insufficient empirical results, and unclear presentation.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel approach to evaluate the robustness of RL algorithms under distribution shifts, which is a significant problem in the field.
- It proposes a methodology using time series analysis and counterfactual analysis, which is a first step towards rigorous RL evaluation in the presence of distribution shifts.
- The paper is well-written, making it easy to follow, and includes a detailed literature review, which is beneficial for readers unfamiliar with the topic.
- The methodology is practical and can be applied in both single-agent and multi-agent environments, providing a comprehensive evaluation framework.
- The paper introduces a novel evaluation metric for RL algorithms, which is relevant for the community and could be a valuable contribution to the field.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a rigorous mathematical formulation, which could undermine the credibility of the proposed methodology.
- The empirical results are not convincing, with some results (e.g., Figure 3) showing inconsistencies that are not adequately explained.
- The paper does not sufficiently address the issue of non-stationarity in RL environments, which could affect the validity of the results.
- The paper's presentation is unclear and lacks depth in certain areas, such as the explanation of key concepts like counterfactual analysis and the methodology's practical implications.
- There is a lack of comparison with existing methods, which could have provided a better understanding of the proposed method's advantages and disadvantages.
- The paper does not adequately address the issue of reproducibility, which is crucial in machine learning research.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors clarify the mathematical formulation of their methodology to ensure its rigor?
- How do the authors address the issue of non-stationarity in RL environments, and what impact does this have on the results?
- Could the authors provide a more detailed explanation of counterfactual analysis and its role in the proposed methodology?
- How does the proposed methodology compare with existing methods in terms of effectiveness and efficiency?
- What are the implications of the proposed methodology for the practical deployment of RL algorithms in real-world scenarios?
- How does the methodology handle the issue of reproducibility, and what steps are taken to ensure that the results can be replicated by other researchers?
