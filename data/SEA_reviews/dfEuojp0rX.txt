 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel approach to implementing a quantum support vector machine (QSVM) using a variational quantum linear solver (VQLS) to address the computational challenges associated with classical support vector machines. The VQLS is used to solve the linear system of equations in the kernel matrix, which is then used to train a support vector classifier (SVC). The paper is evaluated on the Iris dataset, showing the potential of the method in high-dimensional feature spaces. However, the evaluation is limited to a single dataset, and the scalability of the method to larger, more complex datasets is not demonstrated. Additionally, the paper lacks a clear comparison with existing methods and does not provide sufficient details on the quantum hardware and noise models used.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written and easy to follow, with a clear motivation for using the VQLS to solve the linear system of equations in the kernel matrix.
- The method proposed is novel and interesting, particularly the application of the VQLS to the kernel matrix in the QSVM, which could potentially enhance the performance of the model.
- The paper includes numerical experiments on a real dataset, which demonstrates the potential of the method in practical applications.
- The paper is well-structured and provides a clear overview of the problem and proposed solution, which is beneficial for understanding the methodology and its implications.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a thorough comparison with existing methods, particularly in terms of computational complexity and resource requirements.
- The evaluation of the method is limited to a single dataset (Iris), which may not adequately demonstrate the scalability or generalizability of the method to larger, more complex datasets.
- The paper does not provide sufficient details on the quantum hardware used for the simulations, including the number of qubits, connectivity, and noise models.
- There is a lack of discussion on the scalability of the method with increasing problem size, which is crucial for assessing the practical applicability of the method.
- The paper does not sufficiently address the issue of barren plateaus, which could limit the effectiveness of the method in high-dimensional feature spaces.
- The paper could benefit from a more comprehensive discussion on the limitations and potential negative societal impacts of the proposed method.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors provide more details on the quantum hardware used for the simulations, including the number of qubits, connectivity, and noise models?
- How does the method perform when scaled to larger, more complex datasets? What are the resource requirements and computational complexity of the method in such scenarios?
- How does the method handle the issue of barren plateaus in high-dimensional feature spaces?
- Could the authors clarify the role and impact of the SVD in the method, particularly in terms of its scalability and resource requirements?
- How does the method compare to other variational quantum algorithms for solving systems of linear equations, such as those proposed by Bravo-Prieto et al. (2019)?
- Could the authors provide a more detailed comparison with classical methods, including a discussion on the advantages and disadvantages of using quantum resources versus classical methods?
