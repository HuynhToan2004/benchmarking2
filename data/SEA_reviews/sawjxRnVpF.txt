 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel approach to accelerate stochastic gradient descent (SGD) by utilizing curvature information obtained from Hessian-vector products or finite differences of parameters and gradients. The authors propose two preconditioners: a matrix-free preconditioner and a low-rank approximation preconditioner, which are updated online using a robust criterion that does not require line search or damping. The approach leverages the equivariance property of Lie groups to simplify the preconditioner fitting process, eliminating the need for damping and reducing the complexity of the learning rate adjustment. The method's effectiveness is demonstrated across various tasks, including image classification, language modeling, and reinforcement learning, showing that it can outperform state-of-the-art optimizers in terms of convergence speed and robustness to noise.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, clear, and easy to follow, with a comprehensive empirical evaluation that demonstrates the effectiveness of the proposed method across various tasks.
- The introduction of Lie group preconditioners is novel and offers a new approach to accelerate stochastic gradient descent, which is particularly relevant for large-scale machine learning models.
- The paper provides theoretical guarantees for the convergence of the proposed method, which is crucial for ensuring the reliability and robustness of the optimization process.
- The method is shown to be effective in practice, outperforming state-of-the-art optimizers in terms of convergence speed and robustness to noise.
- The paper is well-organized, with clear explanations of the theoretical underpinnings and practical applications of the proposed method.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a thorough comparison with existing methods such as AdaHessian, K-BFGS, and Shampoo, which also use curvature information for optimization.
- The theoretical analysis is not sufficiently detailed, particularly in explaining how the preconditioner updates and the convergence properties of the proposed method are derived.
- The paper could benefit from a more detailed discussion on the limitations of the proposed method, such as the computational complexity and the scalability of the method to larger models.
- There are some minor typographical errors and unclear explanations in the paper that could be confusing to readers.
- The paper could be more comprehensive in its evaluation by including a broader range of tasks and datasets, particularly those that are more challenging or complex.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors provide more details on the computational complexity of the proposed method, particularly how it scales with the size of the model and the dataset?
- How does the proposed method compare in terms of convergence speed and robustness to noise with other methods that use curvature information for optimization, such as AdaHessian, K-BFGS, and Shampoo?
- Can the authors clarify the role and impact of the preconditioner updates on the convergence properties of the proposed method?
- How does the proposed method handle the potential for increased computational complexity due to the additional preconditioner updates?
- Could the authors elaborate on the theoretical justification for the choice of preconditioners and the convergence properties of the proposed method?
