 **Summary:**
The paper investigates the use of test-time augmentation (TTA) to enhance conformal prediction, specifically focusing on reducing the size of prediction sets while maintaining coverage. The authors propose a method that aggregates classifier probabilities across multiple augmentations, aiming to improve the efficiency of conformal predictors. The approach is evaluated on various datasets and shows that it can reduce prediction set sizes by up to 30%, although the paper's novelty and the practical implications of the method are questioned. The methodology is seen as a straightforward application of TTA to conformal prediction without significant theoretical or methodological advancements.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, clear, and easy to follow, with a good balance of theoretical and practical insights.
- The idea of using test-time augmentation (TTA) to improve conformal prediction is novel and interesting, particularly the application of TTA to conformal prediction, which is not commonly explored.
- The paper presents a clear and concise summary of the related work, effectively highlighting the novelty of the proposed approach.
- The experimental results are well-presented, showing a reduction in prediction set size and demonstrating the effectiveness of the proposed method.
- The paper is well-motivated and addresses a significant issue in conformal prediction, namely the large size of prediction sets, which can be misleading or overwhelming.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper's contribution is limited as the proposed method is seen as a straightforward application of TTA to conformal prediction without significant theoretical or methodological advancements.
- The paper lacks a thorough discussion on the limitations of the proposed method, particularly concerning its applicability to different types of conformal prediction methods and its generalizability across different domains.
- The paper does not adequately compare the proposed method with other state-of-the-art methods, which could have provided a better understanding of its advantages and disadvantages.
- The paper does not sufficiently address the potential negative societal impacts of the proposed method, which could be significant given the potential misuse or misinterpretation of the prediction sets.
- The paper's experimental setup and results are not convincingly superior to existing methods, and the reduction in prediction set size does not always correlate with improved performance.
- The paper lacks a detailed discussion on the computational complexity and practical implications of the proposed method, which could be a significant barrier to its adoption in real-world applications.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors clarify the choice of using cross-entropy loss over other losses in the conformal prediction literature?
- How does the proposed method perform when combined with other conformal prediction methods, such as those using different conformal scores?
- Is there a theoretical guarantee that the proposed method maintains the coverage rate of the conformal prediction?
- How does the proposed method handle the computational complexity introduced by the need for multiple forward passes of the classifier?
- Can the authors provide more details on the experimental setup, such as the number of augmentations used and the computational resources required?
- Could the authors discuss the potential negative societal impacts of the proposed method and how these could be mitigated?
