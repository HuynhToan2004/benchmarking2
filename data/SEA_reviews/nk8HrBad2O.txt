 **Summary:**
The paper introduces the Task-Guided Biased Diffusion Model (TGBDM) for enhancing the performance of deep learning methods in point localization tasks, such as crowd localization, human pose estimation, and cell localization. The model addresses the challenges of slow inference and stochasticity in diffusion models by introducing task bias in the forward diffusion process and a task-guided loss to reduce prediction variance. The TGBDM is designed to improve inference efficiency and prediction quality, as demonstrated by state-of-the-art performance on various datasets. However, the paper is criticized for its lack of comprehensive experimental validation, particularly in terms of its generalizability across different datasets and its computational efficiency.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, making it easy to follow and understand.
- The proposed method is simple yet effective, with the introduction of task bias in the forward diffusion process and a task-guided loss to reduce prediction variance.
- The experiments are well-designed, with comprehensive ablation studies and comparisons to state-of-the-art methods.
- The paper is well-motivated, addressing the challenges of slow inference and stochasticity in diffusion models for point localization tasks.
- The methodology is innovative, using a biased DDPM and task-guided loss to improve inference efficiency and prediction quality.
- The paper achieves state-of-the-art performance on various datasets, demonstrating the effectiveness of the proposed method.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed discussion on the limitations of the proposed method, particularly its generalizability across different datasets and its computational efficiency.
- The experimental results are not convincing due to a lack of comprehensive comparisons with state-of-the-art methods and insufficient validation on different datasets.
- The paper does not provide a detailed discussion on the computational complexity of the proposed method, which could be a significant drawback for practical applications.
- The methodology section could be more detailed, particularly in explaining the biased DDPM and the task-guided loss, which are crucial components of the proposed method.
- The paper lacks a thorough discussion on the potential negative societal impacts of the proposed method.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors provide more details on the computational complexity of the proposed method, including the time and space complexity?
- How does the proposed method perform on other datasets, such as those used in the COCO detection benchmark?
- Can the authors provide more comparisons with state-of-the-art methods to demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed method more convincingly?
- How does the proposed method handle the trade-off between prediction quality and computational efficiency?
- Could the authors clarify the role and impact of the task-guided loss in reducing prediction variance and improving prediction quality?
- How does the method perform when applied to different types of localization tasks, such as bounding box localization?
- Can the authors provide more details on the methodology, particularly the biased DDPM and the task-guided loss, to help readers better understand the proposed method?
