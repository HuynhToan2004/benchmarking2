 **Summary:**
The paper introduces SAGO, a novel algorithm for training sparse generalized linear models (GLMs) in a dynamic online setting where observations can be added or removed. SAGO leverages a bilevel optimization framework, transforming each online round into a bilevel optimization problem, which is solved through a PDE-ODE procedure. The algorithm's theoretical analysis demonstrates its ability to converge to the stationarity point on new sets and attain convergence within a finite number of steps. Empirical results on real-world benchmarks show SAGO's efficiency and accuracy compared to existing methods. The paper also discusses the practical implications of SAGO in scenarios where data-varying algorithms are crucial.
