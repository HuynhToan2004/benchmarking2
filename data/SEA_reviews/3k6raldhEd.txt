 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel online learning algorithm for constrained Markov Decision Processes (CMDPs) where both rewards and constraints can be either stochastic or adversarially chosen. The algorithm, named PDGD-OPS, employs a primal-dual approach, with a primal player optimizing the Lagrangian function and a dual player minimizing it. The paper provides theoretical analysis for this algorithm, demonstrating sublinear regret and constraint violation bounds. The algorithm's novelty lies in its ability to handle adversarial constraints, a setting not commonly explored in previous works. However, the paper's presentation and clarity could be improved, with concerns about the novelty of the technical contributions and the lack of empirical validation.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel approach to constrained MDPs, specifically handling adversarial constraints, which is a significant advancement in the field.
- The theoretical analysis provided is comprehensive and sound, with well-structured proofs and clear definitions of key concepts.
- The paper is well-written, with clear exposition of the problem and the proposed algorithm, making it accessible to readers.
- The paper is the first to consider adversarial constraints in CMDPs, which is a significant contribution to the field.

**Weaknesses:**
- The novelty of the technical contributions is questionable, as the approach closely resembles existing methods, particularly those by Rosenberg & Mansour (2019).
- The paper lacks empirical validation, which is crucial for demonstrating the practical applicability and effectiveness of the proposed algorithm.
- The paper does not sufficiently discuss the limitations of its approach, particularly in terms of the assumptions made and the conditions under which the algorithm may not perform optimally.
- The presentation of the paper could be improved, particularly in terms of clarity and the organization of the content, which could make it more accessible and easier to understand.
- The paper does not sufficiently compare its results with existing literature, which could help in understanding the contributions more clearly.

**Questions:**
- Could you clarify how the primal-dual algorithm works in the context of CMDPs, especially how the dual player updates its parameters based on the primal player's actions?
- How does the algorithm handle scenarios where the constraints are adversarially chosen, and what are the implications of this for the overall performance and robustness of the algorithm?
- Can you provide more details on how the algorithm performs under different conditions, such as when the constraints are stochastic versus adversarial?
- Is there a possibility to extend the analysis to handle scenarios where the rewards and constraints are non-stationary, and if so, what would be the implications for the algorithm's performance?
- Could you provide more intuition on why the algorithm performs better in adversarial settings compared to stochastic ones, as mentioned in Remark 1?
