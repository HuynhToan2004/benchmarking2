 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel discrete non-Markov diffusion model (DNDM) that accelerates sampling in discrete diffusion models. It proposes a de-randomized reverse sampling process that leverages a set of latent variables called transition times to speed up the generation process. The model is evaluated on text generation tasks, demonstrating improved sample quality and generation speed. The DNDM model is also shown to be compatible with continuous-time sampling, enhancing its applicability. However, concerns about the model's scalability, the necessity of the de-randomization, and the clarity of the experimental results are raised.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel discrete non-Markov diffusion model (DNDM) that significantly accelerates the sampling process in discrete diffusion models, achieving both high speed and high quality.
- The authors provide a detailed theoretical analysis, including proofs of theorems, which supports the proposed method's effectiveness and novelty.
- The DNDM model is compatible with continuous-time sampling, enhancing its applicability in various scenarios.
- The paper is well-written, with clear explanations and intuitive ideas that make the method accessible and understandable.
- The proposed method shows superior performance in terms of both generation speed and sample quality compared to existing methods for discrete diffusion models.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a clear explanation of why the de-randomization is necessary and how it contributes to the acceleration of the sampling process.
- There is a lack of discussion on the scalability of the proposed method, particularly concerning the memory and time complexity.
- The experimental results are not convincing, with insufficient comparisons to other methods and a lack of clarity in the presentation of results, such as the absence of error bars and unclear figures.
- The paper does not provide a detailed comparison of the proposed method with other existing methods, such as those mentioned in related works, which could have strengthened the argument for its superiority.
- The paper does not adequately address the scalability of the method, particularly in terms of the number of tokens in the sequence and the computational resources required.
- The paper lacks a thorough analysis of the impact of the hyperparameter λ on the performance of the model.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors provide more details on the scalability of the proposed method, particularly concerning the memory and time complexity?
- How does the proposed method compare to other existing methods in terms of generation quality and speed? Could the authors provide a detailed comparison with these methods?
- Could the authors provide a more detailed explanation of the de-randomization process and its necessity in the context of the DNDM model?
- How does the proposed method handle the issue of potential mode collapse in the diffusion process?
- In terms of experimental results, could the authors provide a more detailed analysis of the performance of the DNDM model under different conditions, such as varying numbers of tokens in the sequence?
- Could the authors clarify the discrepancies and uncertainties in the experimental results, particularly in Figure 1 and Table 2?
- How does the proposed method perform in scenarios where the number of tokens in the sequence is large?
