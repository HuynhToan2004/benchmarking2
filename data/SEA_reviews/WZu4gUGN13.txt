 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel method for simulating fluid dynamics using a latent intuitive physics approach, which involves training a particle simulator with a probabilistic model to infer unobserved physical properties from 3D video observations. This method employs a GRU to model latent dynamics and a neural renderer to optimize the latent distribution of the observed particles. The approach is validated through experiments, including novel scene simulations, future prediction of fluid dynamics, and supervised particle simulations. Despite its innovative approach, the paper has been critiqued for its clarity in explaining the methodology and its reliance on existing techniques without sufficient novelty or significant performance improvements over existing methods.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel approach to simulate fluid dynamics using a probabilistic model, which allows for the inference of unobserved physical properties from 3D video observations.
- The methodology is well-presented, with clear explanations of the model architecture and training stages, and the paper includes a detailed description of the experimental setup and results.
- The use of a probabilistic model for simulating fluid dynamics is a significant contribution, as it allows for the modeling of stochastic components and the handling of inaccessible physical properties.
- The paper includes a comprehensive ablation study that demonstrates the effectiveness of each stage in the proposed pipeline.
- The experiments conducted on both synthetic and real-world data show promising results, indicating the practical applicability of the proposed method.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks clarity in explaining the methodology, particularly in the descriptions of the particle-based posterior estimator and the training of the prior learner.
- The novelty of the method is questionable as it heavily relies on existing techniques such as CConv and GRU, with only minor modifications.
- The paper does not provide a detailed comparison with prior works, particularly those that use similar techniques, which limits the evaluation of the proposed method's effectiveness.
- The results presented in the paper are not convincing, with some quantitative results showing only marginal improvements over existing methods.
- The paper lacks a discussion on the limitations of the proposed method, which is crucial for understanding its applicability and potential shortcomings.

**Questions:**
- Can you clarify the role and operation of the particle-based posterior estimator in your model?
- How does the training of the prior learner differ from that of the particle transition module, and why is it necessary to train them separately?
- How does the proposed method handle the stochastic components in fluid dynamics, and what are the implications of this in practical applications?
- Could you provide more details on the training data used in your experiments, particularly in the context of the particle simulator and the neural renderer?
- In the context of the experiments, how does the proposed method compare to prior works that use similar techniques, and what are the specific advantages of your approach?
- Can you discuss the limitations of your method and potential areas for future research?
