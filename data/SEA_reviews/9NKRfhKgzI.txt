 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel framework, ARPRL, aimed at learning adversarially robust and privacy-preserving representations using information theory. The framework employs a min-max optimization problem to balance the trade-offs between adversarial robustness, utility, and attribute privacy. The authors provide theoretical results and empirical evaluations on various datasets to demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed method. However, concerns were raised regarding the clarity and depth of the theoretical analysis, the practical implications of the trade-offs, and the fairness of the experimental comparisons.

**Strengths:**
- The paper addresses an important and timely problem of learning adversarially robust and privacy-preserving representations, which is crucial for secure machine learning applications.
- The proposed method is theoretically sound, with clear mathematical formulations and a solid theoretical foundation.
- The empirical results demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed method in achieving a balance between adversarial robustness, utility, and privacy.
- The paper is well-written, making it easy to follow, and includes detailed derivations of the proposed method.
- The problem setting is significant and the paper provides a novel perspective by combining adversarial robustness and privacy, which is a first in this context.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a clear discussion on the practical implications of the trade-offs between adversarial robustness, utility, and privacy. It is unclear how these trade-offs can be effectively managed in practical scenarios.
- There are several instances of unclear writing and potential errors in mathematical expressions, which could mislead readers or undermine the credibility of the results.
- The paper does not sufficiently discuss the limitations of the proposed method, such as its scalability and the impact of different hyperparameter settings on performance.
- The experimental comparisons may not be entirely fair, as the proposed method uses different hyperparameters and optimization methods compared to baseline methods, which could influence the results.
- The paper does not adequately address the potential negative societal impacts of the proposed method, particularly concerning privacy protection.

**Questions:**
- Can you provide more detailed explanations or examples of how the trade-offs between adversarial robustness, utility, and privacy can be effectively managed in practical scenarios?
- In the experimental section, could you clarify the differences in hyperparameters and optimization methods used for the proposed method compared to the baselines, and discuss the implications of these differences on the results?
- How does the proposed method compare to other state-of-the-art methods in terms of computational efficiency and scalability?
- Can you provide more insights into the choice of hyperparameters and their impact on the performance of the proposed method?
- How does the proposed method handle different types of privacy attacks, such as membership inference attacks or data reconstruction attacks?
- Could you discuss the potential negative societal impacts of the proposed method, particularly concerning privacy protection, and how these impacts can be mitigated?
