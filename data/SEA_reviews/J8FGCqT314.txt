 **Summary:**
The paper introduces D2T2, an extension of the Decision Transformer (DT) aimed at addressing the variance issues in stochastic environments. D2T2 utilizes a steering guidance function derived from the optimal value function to guide the transformer towards high-reward regions, thus improving performance. The methodology involves a TD-learning approach to approximate the optimal value function, which is used to guide the DT towards optimal actions. The paper provides theoretical analysis and empirical evidence supporting the efficacy of D2T2 in both deterministic and stochastic environments, showcasing improved performance over baseline methods. However, concerns were raised regarding the clarity of the methodology, the necessity of certain components, and the fairness of comparisons with other methods.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, clear, and easy to follow, with a clear motivation and a logical flow of content.
- The authors provide a detailed theoretical analysis of the Decision Transformer (DT) in deterministic environments, which is both novel and insightful.
- The proposed D2T2 model effectively addresses the variance issues in stochastic environments by utilizing a steering guidance function derived from the optimal value function.
- The paper is well-organized, with a clear and logical structure that facilitates understanding of the proposed methodology.
- The methodology is supported by extensive empirical results, demonstrating the effectiveness of D2T2 in both deterministic and stochastic environments.
- The paper includes a detailed discussion on the limitations of the proposed method, which is commendable for its transparency and honesty.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks clarity in the methodology, particularly in the explanation of how the steering guidance function is learned and how it influences the decision-making process.
- The necessity and impact of the variational auto-encoder (VAE) on the performance of D2T2 are not clearly explained, and its absence in some environments raises questions about its effectiveness.
- The paper does not adequately address the fairness of comparisons with other methods, particularly in terms of the computational resources and dataset sizes used.
- The theoretical analysis of DT in stochastic environments is missing, which could provide a more comprehensive understanding of the method's behavior in different scenarios.
- The paper could benefit from more detailed explanations and examples of the mathematical notations used, especially those introduced in the later sections, to aid in understanding.
- There are some typographical and formatting errors that need correction to improve the readability and professionalism of the paper.

**Questions:**
- Could you clarify the role and impact of the variational auto-encoder (VAE) in the performance of D2T2, especially in environments where it is not used?
- How does the steering guidance function influence the decision-making process in D2T2, and can you provide more details on how it is learned and applied?
- Can you provide more details on the experimental setup, particularly the computational resources and dataset sizes used, to ensure a fair comparison with other methods?
- In the theoretical analysis, could you elaborate on the assumptions made about the stochasticity of the environment and how they affect the behavior of DT in stochastic environments?
- Could you clarify the definitions and uses of certain mathematical notations, especially those introduced in later sections, to improve the clarity and accessibility of the paper?
