 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel method for Riemannian batch normalization (RBN) on Lie groups, termed LieBN. This method extends the existing RBN techniques from SPD manifolds to more general Lie groups, allowing for the control of both Riemannian mean and variance. The approach is validated through experiments on various datasets, including radar recognition, human action recognition, and EEG classification, demonstrating its effectiveness and generalizability. The paper is well-structured, providing theoretical guarantees and practical demonstrations, although it is criticized for its limited novelty and potential lack of comprehensive experimental validation.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-structured and clearly written, making it accessible and easy to follow.
- It introduces a novel method for Riemannian batch normalization (RBN) on Lie groups, which is a significant contribution to the field.
- The method is theoretically grounded, with proofs provided in the appendix, and is supported by experiments that demonstrate its effectiveness.
- The paper extends the application of RBN from specific manifolds to general Lie groups, which is a significant advancement in the field.
- The authors provide a detailed discussion on the differences between their method and existing methods, which helps in understanding the novelty and contributions of the proposed approach.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper's novelty is questioned as it builds upon existing methods, particularly those of Kobler et al. (2022), and the improvements are seen as incremental rather than groundbreaking.
- The paper lacks a comprehensive comparison with other state-of-the-art methods, which could have provided a clearer picture of the method's relative performance.
- The experimental results are not convincing, with some metrics showing no improvement or even a decrease in performance compared to baseline methods.
- There are some typographical and notation errors that need correction to improve the paper's clarity and professionalism.
- The paper could benefit from a more detailed discussion on the computational efficiency and practical implications of the proposed method, especially in terms of training time and resource requirements.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors clarify the differences between their method and that of Kobler et al. (2022), particularly in terms of the theoretical contributions and practical implications?
- How does the proposed method compare with other state-of-the-art methods in terms of performance and computational efficiency?
- What are the specific advantages of using the proposed method over existing methods, especially in terms of handling different types of manifolds and metrics?
- Can the authors provide more detailed experimental results, including standard deviations and comparisons with other methods on larger, more diverse datasets?
- How does the proposed method handle the computational challenges associated with the normalization of Lie groups, and what are the practical implications of these computations in terms of training time and resource requirements?
