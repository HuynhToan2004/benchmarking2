 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel method to enhance fairness in transformer models without requiring sensitive labels. It employs two strategies: normalizing and applying absolute value operations to queries and keys to minimize bias in attention weight allocation, and local alignment via contrastive learning to reduce bias in values. The approach is evaluated across multiple datasets and tasks, demonstrating its effectiveness in improving fairness without compromising utility. The method is particularly noteworthy for its ability to be integrated into pre-trained models, reducing the need for extensive training and memory requirements. Despite its strengths, the paper could benefit from a more thorough theoretical analysis, clearer presentation, and a broader range of datasets to further validate its claims.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written and easy to follow, with a clear presentation of the proposed method and its implementation.
- The proposed method is simple yet effective, with a straightforward approach that does not require sensitive labels, making it applicable in scenarios where such data is unavailable.
- The method is evaluated on a variety of datasets and tasks, demonstrating its robustness and applicability across different domains.
- The paper provides a detailed discussion of related work, positioning the proposed method within the context of existing literature.
- The method is evaluated for its efficiency in terms of energy consumption, showing that it can be integrated into pre-trained models without significant memory requirements.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a thorough theoretical analysis, particularly in terms of the impact of the proposed method on different fairness metrics such as equalized odds and demographic parity.
- The evaluation is limited to specific datasets and tasks, which may not fully demonstrate the generalizability of the method across different domains and datasets.
- The paper could benefit from a more detailed discussion on the fairness metrics used and the implications of the proposed method on these metrics.
- There are concerns about the clarity and presentation of the paper, particularly in the explanation of the methodology and the experimental setup.
- The paper could be improved by including a more comprehensive literature review and a discussion on the limitations and potential negative societal impacts of the proposed method.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors provide more detailed theoretical analysis on the impact of the proposed method on different fairness metrics?
- How does the proposed method perform on other datasets and tasks beyond those tested?
- Can the authors clarify the choice of fairness metrics and the implications of the proposed method on these metrics?
- How does the proposed method compare to other methods that do not require sensitive labels, particularly in terms of computational efficiency and memory requirements?
- Could the authors provide more details on the experimental setup, particularly in the description of the datasets and the methodology used?
- Is there a possibility to include a discussion on the limitations and potential negative societal impacts of the proposed method?
