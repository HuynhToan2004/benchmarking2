 **Summary:**
The paper introduces FedEBA+, a novel federated learning (FL) algorithm designed to enhance fairness while maintaining global model performance. This algorithm employs an entropy-based aggregation strategy to assign higher weights to underperforming clients, promoting fairness. It also incorporates a model alignment update method to improve fairness and global model performance. Theoretical convergence analysis and extensive experiments demonstrate that FedEBA+ outperforms existing fairness FL methods, showing significant improvements in both fairness and global model performance. However, the paper has been criticized for its complexity, lack of clarity in certain sections, and insufficient experimental validation on larger datasets.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel entropy-based aggregation strategy, which is a significant contribution to the field of federated learning.
- The proposed FedEBA+ algorithm is theoretically proven to converge to the nearby of the optimal solution, demonstrating its effectiveness.
- The paper is well-organized, clearly written, and provides a comprehensive theoretical analysis, which is supported by extensive experiments.
- The algorithm's design is simple and effective, and the experimental results show that it outperforms other fairness FL methods in terms of both fairness and global model performance.
- The paper provides a convergence analysis for FedEBA+, which is a valuable contribution to the field.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper is complex and difficult to follow, particularly in sections where multiple components of the algorithm are introduced simultaneously.
- The experimental setup is limited to smaller datasets, which may not fully demonstrate the algorithm's effectiveness on larger, more diverse datasets.
- There is a lack of clarity in some sections, particularly in the explanation of certain equations and the algorithm's components.
- The paper does not sufficiently address the potential negative societal impacts of the proposed algorithm.
- The convergence rate of the algorithm is not significantly different from existing methods like FedAvg, which may limit its practical applicability.
- The paper could benefit from more detailed explanations of the assumptions made and the limitations of the proposed algorithm.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors clarify the role and impact of the ideal loss function in the proposed algorithm?
- How does the algorithm perform when applied to larger, more diverse datasets, such as those used in the ImageNet-1K dataset?
- Can the authors provide more detailed explanations of the assumptions made in the convergence analysis and the limitations of the proposed algorithm?
- How does the algorithm handle clients with different data distributions, and what measures are taken to ensure fairness in such scenarios?
- Could the authors elaborate on the choice of the temperature parameter and its impact on the algorithm's performance?
- What are the potential negative societal impacts of the proposed algorithm, and how do the authors address these concerns?
