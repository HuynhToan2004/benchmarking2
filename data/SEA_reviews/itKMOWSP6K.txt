 **Summary:**
The paper introduces FusionFormer, a novel multi-modal fusion framework designed for 3D object detection in autonomous driving scenarios. It utilizes a transformer-based architecture with a uniform sampling strategy to fuse features from LiDAR point clouds and 2D images, eliminating the need for explicit BEV space transformation. The framework incorporates deformable attention and residual structures within the fusion encoding module, allowing for flexible adaptability across different modalities. Extensive experiments on the nuScenes dataset demonstrate that FusionFormer achieves state-of-the-art performance without test-time augmentation. The paper also includes a temporal fusion module for historical frame feature fusion and a depth prediction branch for camera-only scenarios.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel multi-modal fusion framework, FusionFormer, which fuses features from LiDAR point clouds and 2D images without explicit transformation into BEV space, enhancing the adaptability of the framework.
- FusionFormer utilizes deformable attention and residual structures within the fusion encoding module, which allows for the uniform sampling of features from different modalities.
- The framework achieves state-of-the-art performance on the nuScenes dataset without test-time augmentation, demonstrating its effectiveness.
- The paper includes a temporal fusion module for historical frame feature fusion and a depth prediction branch for camera-only scenarios, enhancing the robustness and versatility of the framework.
- The experiments are well-designed, with extensive ablation studies conducted to validate the effectiveness of each module, and the results are supported by visualization of detection results.
- The paper is well-written, making it easy to follow, and the main idea is clearly presented.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed analysis of the time complexity and computational efficiency of the proposed method, which could be crucial for real-time applications.
- There is a lack of comparison with other recent methods such as BEVFusion4D and CMT, which could provide a clearer picture of the method's performance relative to state-of-the-art techniques.
- The paper does not discuss the limitations of the proposed method, which could help in understanding the scope and applicability of the findings.
- The performance improvement of the proposed method over existing methods like CMT is not significant, and the paper does not provide a detailed analysis of the reasons behind this.
- The paper lacks a detailed discussion on the efficiency of the proposed method, particularly in terms of computational resources and training time.
- The experimental results are not convincingly superior to existing methods, and the paper does not adequately address the generalization of the proposed method to other datasets.

**Questions:**
- Could you provide a detailed analysis of the time complexity and computational efficiency of the proposed method?
- How does the proposed method compare in terms of performance and efficiency with other recent methods like BEVFusion4D and CMT?
- Can you provide more details on the limitations of the proposed method and how these might affect its applicability in different scenarios?
- How does the proposed method handle the generalization to other datasets, and what are the expected performance outcomes in such scenarios?
- Could you provide more details on the training time and resource requirements of the proposed method?
- Is there a specific reason why the performance improvement of the proposed method over existing methods like CMT is not significant?
