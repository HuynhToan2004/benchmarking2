 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel method, Domain-Adaptive lane detection via Contextual Contrast and Aggregation (DACCA), for unsupervised domain adaptation in lane detection. DACCA utilizes a cross-domain contrastive loss and domain-level feature aggregation to enhance feature representation and cross-domain context dependency. The method is evaluated on six datasets, demonstrating improved performance over existing unsupervised domain adaptive lane detection methods. However, the paper suffers from unclear methodological descriptions and lacks a comprehensive comparison with state-of-the-art methods, which could undermine its credibility and impact.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel approach to unsupervised domain adaptive lane detection, combining cross-domain contrastive loss and domain-level feature aggregation, which are innovative and effective.
- The proposed method shows significant performance improvements over existing unsupervised domain adaptive lane detection methods, as demonstrated by experimental results on six datasets.
- The paper is well-structured and easy to follow, with detailed explanations of the methodology and experimental results.
- The methodology is well-motivated and the paper is well-written, providing a clear explanation of the proposed method and its contributions.
- The methodology is general and can be applied to various lane detection methods, not limited to segmentation-based approaches.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed explanation of the methodology, particularly in sections 3.2 and 3.3, which makes it difficult for readers to fully understand the proposed method.
- There is a significant gap in performance between the proposed method and state-of-the-art methods, which raises questions about the effectiveness of the proposed method.
- The paper does not provide sufficient ablation studies to demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed method, especially in terms of its contributions to cross-domain contrastive learning and domain-level feature aggregation.
- The methodology section is not clearly written, and the definitions and roles of certain parameters and components are not clearly explained, which could lead to confusion.
- The paper lacks a detailed comparison with existing methods, particularly in terms of the number of parameters and computational complexity, which could affect the credibility of the results.
- The paper does not provide enough qualitative results or visualizations to support the claims made about the effectiveness of the proposed method.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors provide more detailed explanations of the methodology, particularly in sections 3.2 and 3.3, to improve the clarity and understanding of the proposed method?
- How does the proposed method compare in terms of computational complexity and number of parameters with existing methods, especially in scenarios where computational resources are limited?
- Can the authors provide more detailed ablation studies to demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed method, especially in terms of its contributions to cross-domain contrastive learning and domain-level feature aggregation?
- How does the proposed method handle the issue of false positive and false negative rates, and what measures have been taken to address these issues in the experimental setup?
- Could the authors provide more qualitative results or visualizations to support the claims made about the effectiveness of the proposed method?
