 **Summary:**
The paper introduces GraphSAM, a novel optimization algorithm designed to improve the computational efficiency of Sharpness-Aware Minimization (SAM) in molecular graph transformer models. By approximating perturbation gradients with updating gradients, GraphSAM aims to reduce the computational overhead of SAM, which typically requires two gradient computations per step. The paper also proposes a gradient ball's size scheduler to adapt the perturbation gradient size during training. Extensive experiments on six datasets demonstrate that GraphSAM can achieve comparable or even better performance than SAM, while reducing computational costs. However, concerns were raised about the clarity of the motivation behind the proposed method, the lack of detailed comparisons with existing methods, and the potential impact of the proposed method on the generalization performance of models.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel method, GraphSAM, which effectively reduces the computational overhead of SAM by approximating perturbation gradients with updating gradients.
- The proposed method is supported by both theoretical analysis and empirical results, demonstrating its effectiveness in reducing computational costs while maintaining performance.
- The paper is well-written, clear, and easy to follow, making it accessible to a broad audience.
- The authors provide extensive experimental results, including comparisons with other optimization methods and an ablation study, which validate the proposed method's effectiveness.
- The paper introduces a gradient ball's size scheduler, which is a novel approach for adapting the perturbation gradient size during training, enhancing the method's applicability and effectiveness.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed comparison with other SAM variants and other optimization methods, which could help in understanding the advantages of the proposed method more clearly.
- There is a lack of clarity regarding the motivation behind the proposed method, particularly why the perturbation gradient needs to be approximated, and how this affects the generalization performance of the model.
- The paper does not adequately address the impact of the proposed method on the generalization performance of the model, which is a critical concern given the nature of SAM.
- The paper could benefit from more detailed visualizations and explanations of the loss landscape, as these are crucial for understanding the effectiveness of the proposed method.
- The paper lacks a discussion on the limitations of the proposed method, which could help in understanding its applicability and potential drawbacks.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors provide a more detailed comparison with other SAM variants and other optimization methods to clarify the advantages of the proposed method?
- How does the proposed method affect the generalization performance of the model, and can the authors provide more insights or experiments to address this concern?
- Could the authors provide more detailed visualizations and explanations of the loss landscape to help in understanding the effectiveness of the proposed method?
- What are the specific limitations of the proposed method, and under what conditions might it not be effective?
- Can the authors clarify the motivation behind the proposed method, particularly why the perturbation gradient needs to be approximated, and how this affects the model's generalization performance?
