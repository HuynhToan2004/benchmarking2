 **Summary:**
The paper explores the application of multi-source knowledge distillation and reinforcement learning to enhance the performance of low-resource machine translation from Mongolian to Chinese. The authors propose a novel reward function to guide the knowledge distillation process, incorporating adversarial noise to improve model robustness. The experiments, conducted on the MN-CH dataset, demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed method, although the novelty and generalizability of the approach are questioned due to its specific application to this dataset and its limited comparison with other state-of-the-art methods.

**Strengths:**
- The paper addresses the challenging task of low-resource machine translation from Mongolian to Chinese, which is not a commonly explored area in NLP.
- The authors propose a novel reward function to guide the knowledge distillation process, which is a significant contribution to the field.
- The paper is well-written, making it easy to follow, and the experiments are well-designed and clearly presented.
- The paper provides a detailed analysis of the performance of the proposed method, including ablation studies that help understand the impact of different components.
- The use of adversarial noise in the distillation process is a novel approach that enhances the robustness of the student model.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a comprehensive comparison with other state-of-the-art methods, limiting the ability to assess the generalizability and effectiveness of the proposed method across different datasets and languages.
- The novelty of the method is questioned as similar approaches have been previously explored in other domains.
- The paper does not adequately address the computational cost and resource requirements of the proposed method, which could be a significant barrier for practical implementation.
- The paper's focus on a single dataset (MN-CH) limits the ability to assess the generalizability of the findings to other datasets and languages.
- The paper does not sufficiently discuss the limitations of the proposed method, which could include the lack of evaluation on other datasets or the potential impact of different types of noise on the distillation process.

**Questions:**
- How does the proposed method compare with other state-of-the-art methods in terms of performance and computational efficiency?
- Can the authors provide more details on the computational cost and resource requirements of the proposed method?
- How does the proposed method perform on other datasets and languages, particularly in low-resource settings?
- Could the authors provide more details on the selection of the reward function and the sensitivity of the results to different hyperparameters?
- How does the proposed method handle the transfer of knowledge from the teacher models to the student model, especially in low-resource settings?
- Could the authors discuss the potential impact of different types of noise on the distillation process and how the adversarial noise is specifically beneficial?
