 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel data augmentation technique called CutSharp for learned image compression (LIC) models, aimed at improving rate-distortion performance. The methodology is based on a block-wise RGB standard deviation metric, which is used to categorize images into different difficulty levels. The paper demonstrates that models trained on subsets of images based on this metric can achieve superior performance. The proposed CutSharp method involves sharpening a randomly selected region of an image during training to enhance image sharpness, which is shown to improve performance on the Kodak and CLIC validation datasets. The paper also discusses the potential of using this method to create a more robust and efficient LIC model by combining data-centric and model-centric approaches.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel approach to image compression using data augmentation, which is a first in the field.
- The proposed CutSharp method is simple yet effective, demonstrated to enhance rate-distortion performance on the Kodak and CLIC validation datasets.
- The paper provides a comprehensive analysis of the relationship between the difficulty of compression and the standard deviation of RGB values, which is a significant contribution to the field.
- The experiments conducted are well-designed, and the results are presented clearly, supporting the claims made in the paper.
- The paper is well-written, making it easy to follow, and the figures effectively illustrate the proposed method.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a comparison with other data augmentation methods, which could have provided a more robust evaluation of the proposed method.
- The novelty of the CutSharp method is limited, as it appears to be a combination of existing techniques without significant innovation.
- The paper does not sufficiently address the scalability of the proposed method to larger datasets or different image resolutions.
- The results presented in the paper do not convincingly demonstrate the superiority of the proposed method over existing methods, with improvements being marginal and not consistently across different datasets.
- The paper does not discuss the limitations of the proposed method, which could help in understanding its applicability and potential drawbacks.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors clarify the specifics of the data augmentation technique used, particularly the region size and the method of sharpening?
- How does the proposed method perform when applied to larger datasets or different image resolutions?
- Why is the data augmentation technique effective for improving the performance of the LIC model, and how does it affect the distribution of B-RGB-SD values?
- Can the authors provide more detailed comparisons with other data augmentation methods to better understand the effectiveness of CutSharp?
- How does the proposed method compare in terms of computational efficiency and training time with other data augmentation techniques?
