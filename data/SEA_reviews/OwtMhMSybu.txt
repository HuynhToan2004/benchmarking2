 **Summary:**
The paper introduces RECODE, a novel exploration bonus for deep reinforcement learning (RL) agents that leverages clustering-based online density estimation to estimate visitation counts in an embedding space. This approach allows for the effective tracking of state visitation counts over extended periods, which is crucial for efficient exploration in challenging environments. RECODE is designed to be agnostic to the specific representation technique used, which is demonstrated through experiments on benchmarks like Atari and DM-HARD-8. The methodology is supported by a robust theoretical framework and extensive empirical evaluations, showcasing its effectiveness in noisy environments and its ability to surpass state-of-the-art exploration methods.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, clear, and easy to follow, with a clear motivation for the proposed method.
- The proposed method, RECODE, is a novel approach that effectively combines clustering-based online density estimation with an embedding space to enhance exploration in RL, particularly in challenging environments.
- The methodology is supported by a robust theoretical framework and extensive empirical evaluations, demonstrating its effectiveness in noisy environments and its ability to surpass state-of-the-art exploration methods.
- The paper provides a detailed analysis of the method's strengths and limitations, and includes a comprehensive set of experiments that validate the approach across various domains.
- The use of clustering-based online density estimation to estimate visitation counts in an embedding space is a novel approach that significantly improves the exploration bonus by increasing the effective memory span.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed discussion on the limitations of the proposed method, particularly in terms of its applicability to more realistic, real-world domains and its potential societal impacts.
- There is a lack of discussion on the potential negative societal impacts of the proposed method.
- The method's reliance on the choice of representation could limit its effectiveness, and the paper does not sufficiently address how it handles the choice of representation in practical applications.
- The paper could benefit from a more detailed comparison with existing methods, particularly in terms of computational efficiency and scalability.
- There is a lack of clarity in some parts of the paper, particularly in the introduction and some sections of the methodology.
- The paper could benefit from a more detailed discussion on the theoretical underpinnings of the method and a more comprehensive experimental validation, including a broader range of environments and more rigorous statistical analysis.
