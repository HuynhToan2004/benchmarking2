 **Summary:**
The paper addresses the instability issues in Euclidean equivariant generative models (ENF and EDM) by proposing a regularization technique to stabilize the backpropagation process. This regularization is aimed at reducing the sensitivity of the gradient to the distance between node coordinates, thereby improving the stability and computational efficiency of these models. The paper includes theoretical analysis and empirical results demonstrating the effectiveness of the proposed method on various datasets. However, concerns are raised about the limited novelty and depth of the theoretical analysis, the lack of comparison with other regularization methods, and the potential computational overhead of the regularized models.

**Strengths:**
- The paper identifies the root cause of the instability in EGNN-based generative models and proposes a novel regularization method to stabilize the training process, which is simple yet effective.
- Theoretical analysis is provided to explain the instability issues, and the paper is well-written, making it easy to follow and understand.
- The proposed method is evaluated on several datasets, including DW4, LJ13, and QM9, and shows improvements in computational efficiency and model performance.
- The paper includes empirical evidence of the proposed method's effectiveness, demonstrating its practical utility in real-world applications.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a comprehensive comparison with other regularization methods, limiting the understanding of the proposed method's advantages and disadvantages in relation to existing approaches.
- The theoretical analysis is not sufficiently detailed, particularly in explaining why the proposed regularization effectively addresses the instability issues.
- The paper does not discuss the limitations of the proposed method, such as potential computational overhead or the need for hyperparameter tuning, which could affect the practical applicability of the method.
- The experimental results, while showing improvements, are not convincingly superior to existing methods, and the paper does not adequately address the potential computational costs associated with the regularized models.
- The novelty of the proposed method is questioned as it seems to be a straightforward application of existing regularization techniques without significant innovation.

**Questions:**
- How does the proposed regularization compare to other regularization methods in terms of effectiveness and computational efficiency?
- Can the authors provide a more detailed theoretical analysis explaining why the proposed regularization effectively addresses the instability issues?
- What are the limitations of the proposed method, and how do these affect its practical applicability?
- How does the proposed method perform in terms of computational overhead and hyperparameter tuning compared to other existing methods?
- Could the authors clarify the potential computational costs associated with the regularized models and discuss any strategies for mitigating these costs?
