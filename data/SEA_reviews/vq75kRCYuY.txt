 **Summary:**
The paper introduces Surrogate Online Learning at Once (SOLO), a novel online training method for Spiking Neural Networks (SNNs) aimed at addressing the credit assignment problem. SOLO utilizes surrogate strategies to simplify the training process and enhance computational efficiency. The method is tested on various datasets, demonstrating competitive performance with traditional methods like backpropagation through time and spatio-temporal backpropagation. The paper also discusses the hardware-friendliness of SOLO, which is crucial for its deployment on neuromorphic computing substrates. However, the paper suffers from clarity issues in its presentation and lacks a detailed comparison with other online training methods, which limits the understanding of its advantages and disadvantages.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel approach to training spiking neural networks, which is hardware-friendly and simplifies the training process.
- The authors have proposed four surrogate strategies to reduce computational complexity, which are well-explained and supported by experimental results.
- The paper is well-written and easy to follow, with clear explanations of the surrogate strategies and their implementation.
- The proposed method, SOLO, is compared with other training methods on various datasets, showing competitive performance.
- The paper is relevant to the field of neuromorphic computing, addressing the significant challenge of training SNNs on neuromorphic substrates.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks clarity in its presentation, making it difficult to understand the specifics of the surrogate strategies and their implementation.
- The paper does not adequately discuss the differences between SOLO and other online training methods, which could have provided a better understanding of its advantages and disadvantages.
- There is a lack of detailed comparison with other methods, particularly in terms of computational complexity and hardware implementation.
- The paper does not include code for reproducibility, which is crucial for validating the results and allowing others to build upon the work.
- The experimental results do not convincingly demonstrate the superiority of SOLO over other methods, especially in terms of computational efficiency and hardware compatibility.
- The paper lacks a thorough discussion on the limitations and potential negative societal impacts of the proposed method.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors provide more detailed explanations of the surrogate strategies and their implementation, possibly with examples or visual aids?
- How does SOLO compare to other online training methods in terms of computational complexity and hardware compatibility?
- Can the authors clarify the differences between SOLO and other methods like BPTT and STBP, particularly in terms of the surrogate strategies used?
- What are the specific advantages of using SOLO over other methods, and how does it address the challenges of training SNNs on neuromorphic substrates?
- Could the authors provide more details on the hardware implementation of SOLO, including the type of neuromorphic substrate used and the specifics of the hardware-friendly design?
- How does SOLO handle the credit assignment problem in SNNs, and what are the implications for the biological plausibility of the model?
