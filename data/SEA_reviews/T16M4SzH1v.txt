 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel approach to distributional reinforcement learning (RL) by representing the return distribution as a sketch, which is a linear transformation of the original distribution. This method allows for the computation of return distributions without the need for imputation strategies, reducing computational overhead. The proposed method is supported by theoretical analysis and empirical results in both tabular and deep RL settings, showing that it can outperform or match the performance of existing methods. The paper also discusses the limitations and potential extensions of the proposed approach, including its applicability to different feature functions and the need for further theoretical and empirical validation.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written and clear, making it accessible and easy to follow.
- The proposed method of using sketches for distributional RL is innovative and offers a new perspective on the problem.
- The theoretical analysis provided is solid and well-supported by empirical results.
- The paper is well-positioned within the literature, clearly articulating its contributions and limitations.
- The methodology is supported by extensive experiments and ablation studies, which demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed approach.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper could benefit from a more comprehensive discussion on the limitations of the proposed method, especially in terms of its applicability to different feature functions and the computational efficiency of the Bellman coefficients calculation.
- The experimental results, while showing promising results, are not convincingly superior to existing methods, and the paper lacks a direct comparison with state-of-the-art methods in deep RL.
- The paper could benefit from a more detailed discussion on the practical implications of the method, such as its scalability and computational efficiency in different scenarios.
- The presentation of the paper, while clear, could be improved with better organization and clearer figures and tables.
- The paper could benefit from a more thorough exploration of the implications of the method for deep RL, particularly in terms of its scalability and practical application.
