 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel method for continual learning in video representation, specifically focusing on the adaptation of NeRV models. It proposes a Progressive Fourier Neural Representation (PFNR) that selects and prunes the weights of NeRV to enhance its capability for continual learning. The PFNR method is evaluated against several baselines, demonstrating its effectiveness in preserving learned representations across multiple video sessions. Despite its innovative approach, the paper is criticized for its lack of clarity in presentation and insufficient comparison with recent relevant works.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel method for continual learning in video representation, specifically adapting NeRV models, which is a significant contribution to the field.
- The proposed method, PFNR, is well-motivated and supported by comprehensive experimental results, demonstrating its effectiveness in handling multiple video sessions.
- The paper is well-organized, easy to follow, and provides clear explanations of the methodology and results, making it accessible to readers.
- The method's ability to identify and utilize Lottery tickets in frequency space is innovative and contributes to the advancement of video representation learning.
- The paper addresses a practical scenario of neural implicit representation where the model encodes multiple videos continually in successive training sessions, which is a significant contribution to the field.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed comparison with the latest continual learning baselines, particularly those that are relevant to the video representation learning domain.
- There is a significant gap in the discussion of recent works, such as those related to continual learning and video representation, which could have strengthened the paper's position in the current research landscape.
- The presentation of the paper could be improved to make it more accessible to a broader audience, especially those not familiar with the specific terminologies and methodologies used.
- The paper's reliance on the NeRV architecture limits its applicability to other video representation models, which could be a significant drawback for broader adoption.
- The paper's evaluation primarily focuses on PSNR and MS-SSIM, which may not fully capture the quality of the video representation. Additional metrics, such as LPIPS, could provide a more comprehensive assessment.
- The paper's methodology, particularly the use of binary masks for weight selection, is not clearly explained, which could confuse readers about the practical implementation of the proposed method.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors provide a more detailed comparison with the latest continual learning baselines, particularly those that are relevant to video representation learning?
- How does the proposed method compare to other methods that use different video representation models, such as those based on FNO?
- Can the authors clarify the practical implementation of the binary mask selection process and how it contributes to the overall effectiveness of the proposed method?
- How does the proposed method perform when applied to other video representation models, such as those based on FNO?
- Could the authors provide more details on the training and evaluation of the model, particularly the use of different layers and the impact of these choices on the model's performance?
- How does the proposed method address the issue of catastrophic forgetting in continual learning, especially in the context of video representation?
