 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel approach to learning with noisy labels by combining several widely-used regularization techniques, including learning rate decay, model weights averaging, and data augmentation. This method, referred to as Regularized Cross-Entropy (RegCE), is tested on various datasets and is shown to outperform existing state-of-the-art methods in certain scenarios. The authors argue that the simplicity and effectiveness of these techniques, which have been under-explored in the context of noisy label learning, are key to their success. The paper also discusses the potential for further improvements by integrating semi-supervised learning techniques.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel approach by combining several widely-used regularization techniques to tackle the learning with noisy labels problem, demonstrating the effectiveness of these techniques in a simple yet powerful manner.
- The proposed method, Regularized Cross-Entropy (RegCE), achieves state-of-the-art results on both synthetic and real-world datasets, showcasing its effectiveness and broad applicability.
- The paper is well-written, making it easy to follow, and includes a thorough literature review, highlighting the importance of regularization in learning with noisy labels.
- Extensive experiments are conducted to validate the effectiveness of the proposed method, demonstrating its superiority over existing state-of-the-art methods in some scenarios.
- The paper includes a detailed ablation study, which helps in understanding the individual contributions of each regularization technique.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed analysis on why the proposed method outperforms existing methods, especially in terms of the specific advantages of the chosen regularization techniques.
- There is a lack of comparison with other regularization methods, which could have provided a clearer understanding of the advantages of the proposed method.
- The paper does not discuss the potential negative societal impacts of the work, which is a significant omission.
- The paper does not provide sufficient details on the implementation of the proposed method, such as the specific learning rate decay schedule and the choice of data augmentation strategies.
- The paper does not include experiments on large-scale datasets, which could have provided a more comprehensive evaluation of the proposed method.
- There are some minor typographical and formatting errors that need correction.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors provide a more detailed analysis on why the proposed method outperforms existing methods, particularly in terms of the specific advantages of the chosen regularization techniques?
- How does the proposed method perform on large-scale datasets, and what are the potential challenges or limitations in applying it to such datasets?
- Could the authors clarify the choice of data augmentation strategies and the specific learning rate decay schedule used in the experiments?
- How does the proposed method compare with other regularization methods, especially in terms of computational efficiency and memory requirements?
- Could the authors discuss the potential negative societal impacts of the work and how these might be mitigated or addressed?
