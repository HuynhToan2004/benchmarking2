 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel approach to solving the Traveling Salesman Problem (TSP) using a deep reinforcement learning (DRL) model that incorporates a Transformer architecture to process local and global information. The model leverages a k-nearest neighbor (k-NN) approach to reduce the action space, which is trained using a modified REINFORCE algorithm with data augmentation. The proposed method, named TS3, is tested on both random and TSPLIB instances, demonstrating its effectiveness in solving larger TSPs with less inference time compared to existing methods. The paper also includes a Monte-Carlo Tree Search (MCTS) component to further enhance the model's performance.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, clear, and easy to follow, with a comprehensive explanation of the proposed method.
- The idea of using a k-nearest neighbor (k-NN) approach to reduce the action space is novel and effective, particularly in reducing inference time.
- The experimental results show that the proposed method outperforms other methods, especially on larger TSP instances, and the ablation studies demonstrate the effectiveness of each component of the proposed method.
- The paper is well-motivated, with a clear explanation of the methodology and its application to the TSP, and it includes a comprehensive set of experiments that validate the proposed method.
- The paper is well-structured, with a clear presentation of the method and its components, making it easy for readers to understand and follow.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a thorough comparison with state-of-the-art methods, particularly in terms of inference time and solution quality, which could be improved by including more recent and relevant comparisons.
- The methodology section could be clearer and more detailed, especially in explaining the data augmentation process and the training procedure for the baseline model.
- The paper could benefit from a more detailed discussion on the limitations of the proposed method, particularly in terms of its applicability to other combinatorial optimization problems and its scalability to even larger TSP instances.
- There is a lack of discussion on the computational complexity of the proposed method compared to other methods, which could affect its practical applicability in real-world scenarios.
- The paper could benefit from a more detailed analysis of the results, particularly in terms of the statistical significance of the observed improvements and the impact of different hyperparameters on the performance.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors provide a more detailed comparison with other state-of-the-art methods, particularly in terms of inference time and solution quality?
- How does the proposed method compare to other data augmentation techniques, and what are the specific advantages of the augmentation used in this study?
- Can the authors clarify the training procedure for the baseline model and the impact of different hyperparameters on the performance of the proposed method?
- How does the proposed method perform on even larger TSP instances, and what are the potential challenges and limitations in scaling up the method?
- Could the authors provide more details on the computational complexity of the proposed method and how it compares to other methods in terms of practical applicability?
