 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel method to suppress unwanted content in text-to-image diffusion models by manipulating text embeddings. The proposed method, which includes soft-weighted regularization and inference-time text embedding optimization, aims to effectively suppress unwanted content without affecting the generation of desired content. The approach is evaluated across various datasets and shown to outperform existing methods in terms of both quantitative and qualitative metrics. The method's effectiveness is demonstrated through extensive experiments and user studies, showcasing its ability to generate images with fewer unwanted elements. However, the paper is criticized for its limited novelty, as it primarily builds upon existing methods like ESD and Inst-Inpaint, and for its inefficiency due to the need for test-time optimization.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, easy to follow, and clearly explains the proposed method and its implementation details.
- The analysis of [EOT] embeddings is insightful and provides a novel approach to suppressing unwanted content in text-to-image generation.
- The proposed method is evaluated on both generated and real images, demonstrating its effectiveness across different scenarios.
- The method does not require training or fine-tuning the image generator, which simplifies its application.
- The results show that the proposed method outperforms existing methods in terms of both quantitative and qualitative metrics, indicating its effectiveness in suppressing unwanted content.
- The paper includes extensive experiments and user studies, which validate the effectiveness of the proposed method.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper's contribution is limited as it primarily builds upon existing methods like ESD and Inst-Inpaint, with the main difference being the use of test-time optimization.
- The paper does not adequately discuss the limitations of the proposed method, such as its inefficiency due to the need for test-time optimization.
- The method's effectiveness in suppressing unwanted content is not consistently superior to existing methods, as shown in some comparisons like the removal of Tyler Edlin's style.
- The paper could benefit from a more detailed discussion on the failure cases and a broader range of qualitative results to better demonstrate the method's effectiveness.
- The paper's use of metrics like Clipscore and IFID is questionable, as these metrics are not commonly used in the community and may not accurately reflect the method's effectiveness.
- The paper could be improved by providing more detailed explanations of the method's components, such as the SVD operation and the use of singular values.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors provide more details on how the method performs in scenarios where the unwanted content is not explicitly mentioned in the text prompt?
- How does the proposed method compare to other recent methods like SEGA and P2P, which are not included in the comparisons?
- Can the authors clarify the role and impact of the SVD operation and the use of singular values in the method's effectiveness?
- How does the method handle scenarios where the unwanted content is not clearly defined or where multiple unwanted contents need to be suppressed simultaneously?
- Can the authors provide more detailed results and discussions on the failure cases and the method's performance in a wider range of scenarios?
