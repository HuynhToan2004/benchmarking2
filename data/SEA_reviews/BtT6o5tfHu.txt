 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel sampling method for diffusion-based super-resolution (SR) models, focusing on optimizing the boundary conditions (BCs) in diffusion ordinary differential equations (ODEs) to enhance the stability and quality of SR images. The authors propose a method for approximating optimal BCs using a reference set of HR-LR image pairs and a Monte Carlo method, which is demonstrated to outperform existing methods on various benchmarks. The approach is supported by theoretical analysis and empirical evidence, showing that the proposed method can significantly improve the PSNR and LPIPS scores in SR tasks. Despite its strengths, the paper has been critiqued for its limited novelty and the need for more comprehensive comparisons with other state-of-the-art methods.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel sampling method that optimizes the boundary conditions in diffusion ODEs, which is both innovative and effective in improving the quality of super-resolution (SR) images.
- The method is theoretically grounded and empirically validated, showing significant improvements in PSNR and LPIPS scores compared to existing methods.
- The paper is well-written, making it easy to follow and understand, with clear explanations of the theoretical underpinnings and practical applications of the proposed method.
- The proposed method is simple yet effective, requiring only a few steps of diffusion ODEs to generate high-quality SR images, making it a significant contribution to the field.
- The paper provides a comprehensive analysis of the characteristics of optimal boundary conditions and their impact on SR results, which is crucial for understanding the behavior of diffusion-based SR models.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed comparison with other state-of-the-art methods, which could have provided a more robust validation of the proposed method's effectiveness.
- The novelty of the proposed method is limited, as it primarily involves optimizing the boundary conditions in diffusion ODEs, which is a known approach in the literature.
- The paper could benefit from a more thorough analysis of the computational complexity and efficiency of the proposed method, especially in comparison to other SR methods.
- The paper does not adequately address the issue of randomness in diffusion models, which could affect the reproducibility and reliability of the results.
- The method's effectiveness is not demonstrated across a wide range of image types or resolutions, which could limit its applicability in real-world scenarios.
- The paper could benefit from a more detailed discussion on the limitations and potential negative societal impacts of the proposed method.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors clarify the specific contributions of their method compared to existing approaches, particularly in terms of novelty and practical benefits?
- How does the proposed method compare in terms of computational complexity and efficiency with other SR methods, especially in real-time applications?
- Can the authors provide more details on the experimental setup and results, especially concerning the computational resources and the scalability of the method across different image resolutions?
- How does the proposed method address the issue of randomness in diffusion models, and what measures have been taken to ensure reproducibility and reliability of the results?
- Could the authors elaborate on the method's effectiveness across a wider range of image types and resolutions, and discuss any potential limitations or challenges in applying the method in these scenarios?
