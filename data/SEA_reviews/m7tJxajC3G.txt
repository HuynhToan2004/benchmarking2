 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel federated causal discovery (FCD) method, FedCDH, which aims to handle arbitrary causal models and heterogeneous data by utilizing summary statistics as a proxy for raw data. This approach allows for the handling of heterogeneous data across different clients, which is a significant challenge in traditional centralized causal discovery methods. The methodology includes a federated conditional independence test (FCT) for causal skeleton discovery and a federated independent change principle (FICP) to determine causal directions, both of which are non-parametric and do not require specific functional forms. The paper also discusses the communication costs and secure computations to ensure data privacy. The method's effectiveness is demonstrated through experiments on synthetic and real datasets, showing improvements over existing methods.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel federated causal discovery method, FedCDH, which handles arbitrary causal models and heterogeneous data, a significant advancement over traditional centralized methods.
- The use of summary statistics as a proxy for raw data is a novel approach that addresses the challenges of handling heterogeneous data across different clients.
- The methodology is non-parametric, which means it does not require specific functional forms, making it adaptable to various causal models.
- The paper is well-written, clear, and easy to follow, with a good balance between theoretical discussions and practical applications.
- The experiments conducted on both synthetic and real datasets demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed method, showing improvements over existing methods.
- The paper addresses an important problem of causal discovery from heterogeneous data, which is crucial for real-world applications.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed discussion on the limitations of the proposed method, which is crucial for understanding its applicability and potential drawbacks.
- There is a lack of discussion on the scalability of the method with an increasing number of clients or variables, which is important for practical applications.
- The communication costs associated with the method are not thoroughly discussed, which is crucial for understanding the practical feasibility of the proposed method.
- The paper does not compare its method with other non-parametric methods for causal discovery, which could provide a more robust evaluation of the proposed method.
- The paper could benefit from a more detailed discussion on the assumptions made by the method, particularly in relation to the faithfulness assumption and the handling of heterogeneous data.
- The paper does not discuss the computational complexity of the proposed method, which is crucial for understanding its practical applicability in real-world scenarios.
