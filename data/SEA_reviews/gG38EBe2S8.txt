 **Summary:**
The paper introduces IMPUS, a novel image morphing method that utilizes a diffusion model to interpolate between two images, optimizing text embeddings and latent states. It employs a heuristic bottleneck constraint to balance directness and fidelity, and a perceptually-uniform sampling technique to ensure smooth transitions. The method's effectiveness is demonstrated through extensive experiments, including comparisons with existing methods and ablation studies that highlight the importance of various components. Despite some concerns regarding the method's generalization and the necessity of additional textual prompts, the paper is well-received for its technical innovation and the potential impact on the field of image morphing.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, making it easy to follow and understand the proposed method.
- The proposed method, IMPUS, is innovative, particularly in its use of a diffusion model for image morphing and the heuristic bottleneck constraint.
- The experimental results are compelling, demonstrating the effectiveness of the method in generating smooth, direct, and realistic image morphing.
- The paper includes thorough ablation studies that support the claims made about the effectiveness of different components of the method.
- The method's ability to handle large semantic differences between input images is highlighted, showing potential for broader applications in image generation and manipulation.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed discussion on the necessity and effectiveness of the additional textual prompts used in the method.
- There is a lack of quantitative results to support the claims of improved directness and fidelity, which could be addressed by including metrics like LPIPS or FID.
- The method's generalization to different image pairs, especially those with significant semantic differences, is questionable, and the paper could benefit from more diverse testing.
- The paper could benefit from a more detailed discussion on the limitations of the method, including potential issues with the heuristic bottleneck constraint and the perceptually-uniform sampling technique.
- The paper's presentation could be improved by including more visual examples and comparisons in the main text to better illustrate the method's capabilities.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors clarify the necessity and effectiveness of the additional textual prompts used in the method?
- How does the method handle image pairs with significant semantic differences, and what are the limitations in such cases?
- Could the authors provide more detailed quantitative results to support the claims of improved directness and fidelity?
- In terms of the heuristic bottleneck constraint, how is the rank determined, and what are the implications for the method's performance?
- How does the method compare to other state-of-the-art methods in terms of computational efficiency and resource requirements?
- Could the authors provide more visual examples and comparisons in the main text to better illustrate the method's capabilities and limitations?
