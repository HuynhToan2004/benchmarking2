 **Summary:**
The paper introduces RHGNet, a novel approach to object-centric learning (OCL) inspired by the Reverse Hierarchy Theory (RHT) in human visual perception. RHGNet integrates a top-down pathway to enhance the model's ability to identify small and occluded objects by optimizing the consistency between high-level object masks and low-level feature maps. This is achieved through a top-down attention module that computes a KL divergence loss between the two, which is used during both training and inference to guide the model's learning and initialization of slots. The method is evaluated across various datasets, including synthetic and real-world scenes, showing improvements in object discovery and reconstruction. However, the paper has been critiqued for its limited novelty, unclear presentation, and insufficient comparison with existing methods.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, clear, and easy to follow, with detailed supplementary materials that provide additional insights.
- The integration of a top-down pathway in object-centric learning (OCL) is novel and innovative, inspired by the Reverse Hierarchy Theory (RHT) in human visual perception.
- The method is simple, well-motivated, and has been validated on both synthetic and real-world datasets, showing improved performance in object discovery and reconstruction.
- The paper provides a comprehensive ablation study, which helps in understanding the effectiveness of the proposed method.
- The paper includes a variety of experiments and comparisons, including on both synthetic and real-world datasets, which demonstrate the robustness and generalizability of the proposed method.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed comparison with existing methods, particularly in terms of performance metrics and qualitative results.
- The novelty of the proposed method is questioned, as it primarily adds a top-down attention module without significant modifications to the existing object-centric learning pipeline.
- The paper suffers from unclear presentation in some sections, particularly in the description of the top-down attention module and the conflict function.
- The paper does not sufficiently address the computational cost and time complexity of the proposed method, which could be a concern for practical applications.
- The paper does not sufficiently discuss the limitations of the proposed method, which could include the scalability to larger datasets or more complex scenes.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors clarify the computational cost and time complexity of the proposed method, especially in comparison to existing methods?
- How does the proposed method perform when applied to more complex or cluttered scenes, such as those found in the COCO dataset?
- Can the authors provide more detailed qualitative results, especially in terms of the performance of the top-down attention module and its impact on object discovery and reconstruction?
- How does the proposed method compare in terms of performance and computational efficiency when applied to different backbone networks, such as ViT or DETR?
- Can the authors provide a more detailed explanation of the top-down attention module and the conflict function, particularly in terms of their implementation and impact on the model's performance?
