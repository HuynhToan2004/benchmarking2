 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel linear attention mechanism, termed Linear Attention via Orthogonal Memory (LAVO), aimed at addressing the inefficiencies in causal language modeling. LAVO employs orthogonal decomposition to compress context into a fixed-size orthogonal memory, thereby minimizing redundancy and improving extrapolation ability. This mechanism is evaluated across various tasks including language modeling, text-to-speech, and summarization, showing improvements over existing methods in terms of efficiency and performance. The method's ability to scale to unbounded contexts is highlighted, and the paper includes extensive experimental evaluations to support its claims.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, clear, and easy to follow, making complex concepts accessible to a broad audience.
- The proposed method, LAVO, is innovative, leveraging orthogonal decomposition to address the efficiency degradation problem in causal language modeling, which is a significant issue in the field.
- The method demonstrates strong empirical performance, as evidenced by its ability to outperform other efficient baselines in various tasks including language modeling, text-to-speech, and summarization.
- The method is evaluated on a variety of tasks, including unbounded language modeling, which is a challenging and important problem in the field.
- The paper provides extensive experimental results, including an ablation study, which helps in understanding the contributions of different components of the method.
- The method is well-motivated, addressing the efficiency degradation problem in causal linear attention, which is a significant issue in the field.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a thorough analysis of the computational complexity of LAVO, particularly in terms of the number of orthogonal bases used and the computational overhead introduced by the orthogonal decomposition.
- The comparison with other efficient attention mechanisms, such as FlashAttention, is insufficient, especially in terms of the computational efficiency and memory usage.
- The paper does not provide a detailed analysis of the memory usage and speedup of LAVO compared to other efficient attention mechanisms, which is crucial for understanding its practical advantages.
- The paper does not include a discussion on the limitations of the proposed method, which is essential for a comprehensive understanding of its applicability and potential drawbacks.
- The paper could benefit from a more detailed comparison with other methods, especially in terms of the number of parameters and the computational efficiency.

**Questions:**
- Could you clarify the computational complexity of LAVO, specifically the number of orthogonal bases used and the computational overhead introduced by the orthogonal decomposition?
- How does the performance of LAVO compare to FlashAttention, particularly in terms of computational efficiency and memory usage?
- In the context of unbounded language modeling, how does LAVO handle the challenge of maintaining a fixed-size orthogonal memory across different context lengths?
- Can you provide a more detailed analysis of the memory usage and speedup of LAVO compared to other efficient attention mechanisms?
- How does the proposed method compare in terms of performance and efficiency with other methods like EVA, especially when applied to unbounded language modeling?
- Can you provide more insights into the limitations of the proposed method and how these might impact its practical application?
