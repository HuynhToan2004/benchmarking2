 **Summary:**
The paper introduces the Activation-Sharing Multi-Resolution (ASMR) coordinate network, a novel method for encoding natural signals such as images and videos using implicit neural representations (INRs). ASMR employs multi-resolution coordinate decomposition and hierarchical modulations to share activations across grids of data, thereby reducing inference costs while maintaining or improving reconstruction quality. This approach allows for a near O(1) inference complexity, which is crucial for applications where hardware constraints limit inference throughput. The ASMR model shows significant reductions in multiply-accumulate (MAC) operations compared to existing models like the SIREN, achieving up to 350x fewer MACs while maintaining or even improving reconstruction quality.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel hierarchical modulation scheme that efficiently incorporates multi-resolution coordinates with minimal parameter increase, allowing for more efficient sharing of activations across data points.
- The ASMR network is the first INR to decouple MAC from its depth, leading to a near O(1) complexity regardless of the number of layers, which is crucial for inference efficiency.
- The ASMR achieves better quality reconstruction with 350x fewer MAC than a vanilla SIREN in high-resolution image fitting tasks, demonstrating its effectiveness in reducing inference costs.
- The paper provides a clear and detailed explanation of the methodology, including a comprehensive literature review and detailed comparisons with existing methods.
- The experiments conducted show that the ASMR model outperforms the SIREN model in terms of reconstruction quality and inference efficiency, which is a significant improvement over the state-of-the-art.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed explanation of how the proposed method, ASMR, compares to existing methods like KiloNeRF and LoE, particularly in terms of inference efficiency and reconstruction quality.
- The paper could benefit from a more detailed explanation of the experimental setup, including the specific hardware used for the experiments and the exact conditions under which the experiments were conducted.
- The paper does not provide a detailed comparison of the training times of the different models, which is crucial for understanding the practical applicability of the ASMR model.
- The paper could benefit from a more detailed discussion on the limitations of the proposed method and potential areas for future research.
- The paper does not provide a detailed analysis of the computational complexity of the ASMR model, which is crucial for understanding its practical applicability in real-world scenarios.
- The paper could benefit from a more detailed discussion on the potential societal impacts of the proposed method, particularly in terms of its application in generative tasks and its potential impact on data privacy and security.
