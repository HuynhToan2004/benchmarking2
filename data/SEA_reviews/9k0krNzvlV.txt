 **Summary:**
The paper investigates the learnability of watermarks in language models through the lens of watermark distillation, where a student model is trained to mimic a teacher model that incorporates a watermark. The study examines the impact of various hyperparameters on the learnability of watermarks and demonstrates that these watermarks are robust against different decoding algorithms and text edits. The paper also discusses potential spoofing attacks and their implications, highlighting the importance of robust watermarking in the context of open models. Despite its innovative approach and experimental validation, the paper is critiqued for its limited scope, lack of comprehensive comparisons, and potential overemphasis on the learnability aspect without sufficient exploration of its broader implications.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, clear, and easy to follow, with a clear motivation and good experimental design.
- The authors provide a comprehensive evaluation of watermark distillation, including robustness to different decoding algorithms and text edits.
- The paper is the first to investigate the learnability of watermarks in language models, a topic of significant importance for open models.
- The paper is well-organized, with clear problem definitions, methodologies, and experimental results.
- The authors have conducted a thorough investigation of the learnability of watermarks, providing valuable insights into the robustness of watermarking against different decoding algorithms and text edits.
- The paper discusses potential applications of the findings, such as watermark spoofing attacks, which are important for understanding the potential misuse of watermarking technology.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed comparison with existing watermarking methods, which could help in understanding the novelty and effectiveness of the proposed approach.
- The paper does not adequately address the limitations of the proposed approach, particularly in terms of its practical applicability and scalability.
- The paper's focus on learnability might overshadow the broader implications of watermarking, such as its impact on model performance and the potential for misuse.
- The experimental setup and results could be more comprehensive, especially in terms of the variety of models and watermarking strategies tested.
- The paper could benefit from a more detailed discussion on the potential negative societal impacts of watermarking, such as the potential for misuse in harmful applications.
- The paper's claims about the robustness of watermarking against fine-tuning are not convincingly supported by the experimental results.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors clarify the potential negative societal impacts of watermarking, especially in terms of its misuse for harmful applications?
- How does the proposed approach compare with existing watermarking methods in terms of effectiveness and robustness?
- Could the authors provide more details on the experimental setup, particularly how the student model is initialized and how the watermarked samples are generated?
- How does the proposed approach perform under different hyperparameter settings, and what are the implications of these settings for the learnability and robustness of the watermarking?
- Could the authors discuss the potential applications of the findings beyond watermarking, such as in the context of model stealing or other forms of model security?
