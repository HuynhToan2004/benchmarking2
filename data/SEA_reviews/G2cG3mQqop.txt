 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel method for image clustering conditioned on text criteria, termed IC|TC. This approach leverages modern vision-language models and large language models to align clustering results with user-specified criteria. The methodology involves extracting image features through a vision-language model, obtaining cluster names from a large language model, and then clustering based on these labels. The paper demonstrates that IC|TC outperforms baseline methods on various datasets and provides a user-friendly interface for refining clustering results. However, concerns about the novelty of the method, the clarity of its positioning within existing literature, and the potential for unfair comparisons were raised.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel method for image clustering conditioned on text criteria, which is a significant advancement in the field.
- The methodology is well-articulated, with a clear explanation of the three stages involved in the clustering process.
- The paper is well-written, making it accessible and easy to understand.
- The experiments conducted demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed method, showing its potential for practical applications.
- The use of modern foundation models like vision-language models (VLMs) and large language models (LLMs) enhances the method's performance.
- The paper provides a user-friendly interface that allows for iterative refinement of clustering results, which is beneficial for practical applications.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a comprehensive comparison with existing methods, particularly in terms of fairness and the use of similar datasets.
- The methodology might be considered a combination of existing models without sufficient novelty, and the contribution of the proposed method is not clearly articulated.
- The paper does not adequately address the issue of data contamination, which could affect the validity of the results.
- The paper's evaluation metrics are limited, and it does not include a broader set of metrics that could provide a more comprehensive assessment of the method's performance.
- The method's reliance on user-defined text criteria may not be practical in real-world scenarios, as users might not have the necessary domain knowledge to effectively define clustering criteria.
- The paper does not sufficiently discuss the potential biases that could be introduced by the foundation models used in the method.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors clarify the contribution of their method beyond the use of existing models like VLMs and LLMs?
- How does the method handle data contamination, and what steps have been taken to mitigate this issue?
- Could the authors provide a more detailed comparison with existing methods, particularly in terms of fairness and the use of similar datasets?
- How does the method perform when compared to other clustering methods that do not use text criteria, such as those that use ground truth labels or other types of criteria?
- Could the authors discuss the potential biases that could be introduced by the foundation models used in the method and how these biases were addressed?
- How does the method handle the potential for unfair comparisons with other clustering methods that do not use text criteria?
- Can the authors provide a more detailed explanation of how the method handles the iterative refinement of clustering results, especially in terms of user involvement and the efficiency of the process?
