 **Summary:**
The paper explores the classification capabilities of generative models, specifically focusing on text-to-image generative models like Imagen, Stable Diffusion, and Parti. It evaluates these models on out-of-distribution (OOD) datasets and compares their performance to human observers, highlighting their ability to mimic human object recognition. Key findings include the models' shape bias, OOD accuracy, and error consistency, which are shown to align with human perception in certain aspects. The paper also discusses the implications of these findings for understanding the workings of generative models and their potential applications in machine vision.

**Strengths:**
- The paper provides a comprehensive analysis of the classification capabilities of generative models, specifically text-to-image generative models, which is a novel and interesting area of research.
- The authors have conducted a thorough evaluation of the models, comparing their performance against human observers and other models, and have presented their findings in a clear and organized manner.
- The paper is well-written, with a clear and concise introduction that effectively sets the stage for the research.
- The findings, particularly the alignment of generative models with human classification and the understanding of perceptual illusions, are intriguing and could have significant implications for the field.
- The paper is well-organized, making it easy to follow, and includes a comprehensive set of experiments that support the claims made.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed discussion on the implications of the findings, particularly how the insights gained from studying generative models can be applied to improve machine vision systems.
- There is a significant discrepancy between the performance of generative models and human observers, which is not adequately addressed in the paper.
- The paper does not sufficiently discuss the limitations of the models, such as their inability to handle rotated images and the potential biases in their performance.
- The paper could benefit from a more detailed discussion on the technical aspects of the models, including how they are trained and the specifics of the training data used.
- There is a lack of comparison with other generative models, such as those based on auto-regressive or diffusion processes, which could provide a more comprehensive understanding of the capabilities and limitations of generative models.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors provide a more detailed discussion on the implications of the findings for machine vision systems?
- How do the authors address the discrepancies between the performance of generative models and human observers?
- Could the authors clarify the specifics of the training data used for the generative models and discuss any potential biases in the training data that could affect the results?
- How do the authors explain the differences in performance between different generative models, such as Imagen and Stable Diffusion?
- Could the authors provide more details on the training procedures for the generative models, particularly how the noise is added during training?
- How do the authors ensure that the models are not overfitting to the training data, which could affect their performance on out-of-distribution data?
