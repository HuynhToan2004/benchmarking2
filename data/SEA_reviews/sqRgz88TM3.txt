 **Summary:**
The paper introduces VFLAIR, a research library and benchmark for Vertical Federated Learning (VFL), which aims to address the challenges of data inference and backdoor attacks in VFL. VFLAIR supports various models, datasets, and protocols, providing a comprehensive evaluation of attacks and defense strategies. The framework is designed to be lightweight and extensible, facilitating research development in VFL. The paper benchmarks 11 attacks and 8 defenses across different settings and draws insights into the choice of defense strategies. However, the paper lacks a detailed discussion on the limitations of existing works and the novelty of its contributions.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a comprehensive research library and benchmark for Vertical Federated Learning (VFL), which supports a wide range of models, datasets, and protocols.
- It provides a unified evaluation metric and benchmark for VFL, which is crucial for researchers in the field.
- The paper is well-written, clear, and easy to follow, making it accessible to a broad audience.
- The experiments are well-designed and comprehensive, covering various aspects of VFL, including model performance, communication efficiency, and robustness to attacks.
- The authors propose new evaluation metrics and modules, which can be beneficial for future research in VFL.
- The paper provides a valuable resource for the research community, as it is the first to provide a comprehensive evaluation of attacks and defense strategies in VFL.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed discussion on the limitations of existing works and the novelty of its contributions, which could have strengthened the paper's argument for the need for VFLAIR.
- The paper does not provide a clear discussion on the potential negative societal impacts of VFL, which is an important consideration for the field.
- There are some typographical and formatting errors in the paper, which could detract from its professionalism.
- The paper's contribution might be seen as limited, as it primarily provides a framework without introducing new models or techniques.
- The paper could benefit from a more detailed discussion on the implementation and performance of the framework, including a comparison with existing VFL frameworks.
- The paper does not adequately discuss the limitations of the framework, such as the scalability and generalizability of the results.
