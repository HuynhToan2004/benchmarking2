 **Summary:**
The paper introduces the Excitation-Inhibition Mechanism-assisted Hybrid Learning (EIHL) algorithm, which aims to balance local and global learning in Spiking Neural Networks (SNNs) by leveraging the excitation-inhibition mechanism. This approach allows the network to dynamically switch between local and global learning modes, enhancing both accuracy and sparsity. The EIHL algorithm is tested on various datasets, including CIFAR10, CIFAR100, and DVS-CIFAR10, and shows promising results in terms of accuracy and sparsity compared to other methods. However, the paper suffers from issues in clarity and depth of explanation, particularly in the algorithm's details and the biological plausibility of the excitation-inhibition mechanism.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel approach to balancing local and global learning in Spiking Neural Networks (SNNs) by integrating the excitation-inhibition mechanism, which is not commonly explored in this context.
- The methodology is well-explained, with a clear motivation for using the excitation-inhibition mechanism to balance local and global learning.
- The experimental results demonstrate that the proposed EIHL algorithm achieves better accuracy and sparsity compared to other methods, indicating its effectiveness.
- The paper is well-written and easy to follow, making it accessible to a broad audience.
- The authors provide a detailed analysis of the experimental results, which helps in understanding the implications of their findings.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks clarity in explaining the excitation-inhibition mechanism and its integration into the hybrid learning method. Specifically, there is a need for more detailed explanations on how the mechanism is used to balance local and global learning.
- The algorithmic details, such as the update rules and the mathematical formulations, are not clearly articulated, making it difficult to replicate the results or understand the methodology fully.
- The paper does not provide a detailed comparison with other hybrid learning methods, which could have provided a more robust evaluation of the proposed method.
- There is a lack of biological plausibility in the excitation-inhibition mechanism, which is crucial for the paper's claims of neuroscience-inspired learning.
- The paper's experimental results are not convincingly superior to existing methods, with marginal improvements reported in some cases.
- The paper suffers from poor writing and lacks sufficient details, making it difficult to follow and understand the methodology and results.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors provide more details on how the excitation-inhibition mechanism is integrated into the hybrid learning method? Specifically, how does the mechanism ensure a balance between local and global learning?
- How does the proposed method compare with other hybrid learning methods, particularly in terms of accuracy and sparsity?
- Can the authors clarify the mathematical formulations and update rules used in the EIHL algorithm?
- How does the proposed method ensure biological plausibility, especially in the context of the excitation-inhibition mechanism?
- Can the authors provide more detailed experimental results, including the standard deviation of the results and a comparison with other methods using the same dataset and network architecture?
- What are the implications of the marginal improvements in accuracy and sparsity reported in the paper? Are these improvements statistically significant?
