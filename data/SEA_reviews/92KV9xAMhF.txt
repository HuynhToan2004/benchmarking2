 **Summary:**
The paper investigates the conservativity of the score function in diffusion models, questioning whether it is necessary for exact sampling and density estimation. It introduces the concept of gauge freedom, demonstrating that conservativity is not a prerequisite for these tasks. The authors explore the intrinsic dimensionality estimation using diffusion models and provide empirical evidence to support their claims. The paper also discusses the practical implications of these findings, suggesting that conservativity might be beneficial for estimating local information of data manifolds. However, the paper's theoretical contributions are seen as somewhat limited, and the empirical evidence provided is not convincing enough to support the claims fully.

**Strengths:**
- The paper addresses a significant and timely question regarding the conservativity of the score function in diffusion models, which is a topic of ongoing debate in the field.
- The paper is well-written, with clear presentation of results and a logical flow that makes it easy to follow.
- The authors provide a novel perspective on the gauge freedom of the score function in diffusion models, which is both interesting and relevant to current research.
- The empirical results, while limited, are convincing and support the theoretical claims made in the paper.
- The paper is original in its approach, offering a fresh perspective on the role of conservativity in diffusion models, which is a significant contribution to the field.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper's theoretical contributions are seen as somewhat limited, with some reviewers questioning the depth and novelty of the results.
- The empirical evidence provided is not convincing enough to support the claims fully, particularly concerning the necessity of conservativity for exact sampling and density estimation.
- The paper lacks a detailed discussion on the practical implications of the findings, such as how the gauge freedom condition could be enforced in practical applications.
- There are concerns about the clarity and depth of some sections, particularly Section 5, which is seen as inadequately explained.
- The paper could benefit from a more thorough comparison with existing literature, particularly in terms of the assumptions and results presented.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors clarify the assumptions made in Theorem 1 and how they impact the practical applicability of the results?
- How does the paper's approach differ from or build upon existing works that have also explored the conservativity of diffusion models?
- In light of the empirical evidence provided, can the authors elaborate on why conservativity is necessary for inferring local information of the data manifold?
- How does the paper address the practical challenges of enforcing the gauge freedom condition in diffusion models?
- Could the authors provide more detailed examples or case studies to illustrate the practical implications of their findings?
