 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel approach to generative modeling by leveraging data-dependent couplings between base and target densities, extending the stochastic interpolant framework. This method allows for the generation of dependent samples, which can be conditioned on various factors such as labels or continuous embeddings. The authors demonstrate this through experiments in super-resolution and in-painting, showing improvements over traditional methods. However, concerns are raised about the clarity of the theoretical framework, the practical implementation, and the comparative analysis with existing methods.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel approach to generative modeling, specifically focusing on the use of data-dependent couplings to enhance the dependence between base and target densities. This is a significant advancement in the field, particularly in terms of generating dependent samples.
- Theoretical analysis is provided, which is crucial for understanding the proposed method's foundations and potential applications.
- The experiments conducted, particularly in image super-resolution and in-painting, demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed method, showing improved performance over traditional methods.
- The paper is well-written, making it accessible and easy to follow, which is essential for a broad audience.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks clarity in several areas, particularly in the theoretical sections where the relationship between the proposed method and existing frameworks such as Schrödinger bridges is not clearly articulated.
- The experimental setup and results are not convincingly presented, with insufficient details on the implementation and a lack of comparative analysis with other methods.
- The paper does not adequately address the practical implications of the proposed method, such as its scalability and computational efficiency, which are critical for real-world applications.
- There is a lack of discussion on the limitations and potential negative societal impacts of the proposed method, which is essential for a comprehensive evaluation.
- The paper does not sufficiently compare its method with existing methods, which makes it difficult to assess the novelty and effectiveness of the proposed approach.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors clarify the relationship between their proposed method and existing frameworks such as Schrödinger bridges, particularly in terms of how the method extends or diverges from these existing approaches?
- How does the proposed method compare in terms of computational efficiency and scalability with other existing methods, particularly in large-scale applications?
- Could the authors provide more detailed experimental results, including comparisons with other methods and a more comprehensive analysis of the performance metrics used?
- How does the proposed method handle different types of data-dependent couplings, and what are the implications for the generation of dependent samples in various applications?
- What are the potential negative societal impacts of the proposed method, and how do the authors address these in their research?
