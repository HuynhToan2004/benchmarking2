 **Summary:**
The paper introduces AutoM3L, an automated multimodal machine learning framework that utilizes large language models (LLMs) to manage machine learning pipelines. AutoM3L integrates various modules including modality inference, automated feature engineering, model selection, pipeline assembly, and hyperparameter optimization. The framework is evaluated on four multimodal datasets, showing competitive or superior performance compared to traditional rule-based AutoML methods. The authors conducted a user study to assess the usability and user-friendliness of the framework. However, the paper has been critiqued for its limited novelty, as it primarily replaces rule-based methods with LLMs without significant technical innovation. The evaluation is also seen as insufficient, with only one baseline and a lack of comprehensive testing across various datasets.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces AutoM3L, an innovative framework that leverages large language models (LLMs) to automate machine learning pipelines, addressing the challenges of multimodal data handling.
- The framework is designed to be user-friendly, with directives that allow customization, enhancing user engagement and reducing the need for manual feature engineering and hyperparameter optimization.
- Extensive experiments and user studies are conducted to evaluate the framework's performance, demonstrating its effectiveness in multimodal data processing.
- The paper is well-organized and easy to follow, with clear explanations of the framework's components and their functions.
- AutoM3L is a significant advancement in the field of automated machine learning, offering a novel approach to handling multimodal data and providing a more interactive and user-friendly experience compared to traditional rule-based methods.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a comprehensive evaluation, with only one baseline (AutoGluon) and limited testing across different datasets, which undermines the robustness of the results.
- The technical novelty of the framework is questioned, as it primarily replaces rule-based methods with LLMs without significant technical innovation.
- There is a lack of clarity in the methodology, particularly in how the LLMs are trained and how the user study was conducted, which raises concerns about the reliability and reproducibility of the results.
- The paper does not adequately address the scalability and generalization of the framework, which is crucial for broader application in real-world scenarios.
- The paper's focus on structured datasets may limit its applicability to unstructured data, which is increasingly common in real-world scenarios.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors provide more details on the training of the LLMs used in the framework, including the data sources, model architectures, and training strategies?
- How does the framework handle unstructured data, such as images or audio files?
- Can the authors clarify the methodology used in the user study, particularly the selection criteria for participants and the tasks involved?
- How does the framework handle the scalability and generalization issues, especially when applied to larger or more complex datasets?
- Could the authors elaborate on the performance of AutoM3L on other datasets, especially those with different modalities or characteristics?
- How does the framework compare to other existing multimodal AutoML frameworks, and what are the advantages of using AutoM3L over these alternatives?
