 **Summary:**
The paper introduces SPLiCe, a method for creating training examples for long-context language models (LLMs) by using a retrieval method to collate the most mutually relevant documents into a single training context. This method aims to enhance the utilization of long contexts in LLMs, which is crucial for improving performance on downstream tasks. The authors demonstrate that SPLiCe can improve perplexity and long-context performance on various datasets, including the StarCoder dataset. They also show that SPLiCe can be applied to different types of textual data, including code and webtext, and that it can be integrated with other methods for preparing long-context data.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel method, SPLiCe, for creating training examples by using a retrieval method to collate the most mutually relevant documents into a single training context, which is a significant contribution to the field.
- The methodology is clearly presented, and the paper is well-written, making it easy to follow.
- The paper demonstrates the effectiveness of SPLiCe in improving the utilization of long contexts in LLMs, which is crucial for enhancing performance on downstream tasks.
- The paper provides a thorough analysis of the design choices of SPLiCe, including the number of retrieved documents and the order in which documents are merged into a training example.
- The results indicate that SPLiCe enhances model performance and can be used to train large models to utilize long contexts better.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed comparison of the retrieval method used in SPLiCe with other retrieval methods, which could provide a more comprehensive understanding of the effectiveness of the method.
- There is a lack of discussion on how SPLiCe compares with other methods for improving context utilization in LLMs, such as using unrelated data or other methods for preparing long-context data.
- The paper does not provide a detailed analysis of the computational cost of using SPLiCe, which is a significant limitation given the potential for high computational cost.
- The paper does not include a discussion on how SPLiCe integrates with other methods for preparing long-context data, which could limit its applicability in real-world scenarios.
- The paper lacks a discussion on the potential negative societal impacts of the proposed method, which is an important consideration for any new technology.
