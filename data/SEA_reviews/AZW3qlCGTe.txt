 **Summary:**
The paper introduces FACILE, a novel method for instance-level image classification using set-level coarse-grained labels. It bridges the gap between set-level and instance-level image classification, leveraging coarse-grained labels to enhance fine-grained image classification performance. Theoretical analysis and experimental results on various datasets, including natural and medical images, demonstrate the effectiveness of FACILE in improving classification accuracy. The methodology involves a two-stage process: first, learning set-level coarse-grained representations, followed by instance-level fine-grained classification. The paper also discusses the theoretical foundations and empirical evidence supporting the use of coarse-grained labels in improving the learning process of fine-grained label prediction tasks.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel method for instance-level image classification using set-level coarse-grained labels, which is a significant advancement in the field.
- The method is supported by both theoretical analysis and empirical results, demonstrating its effectiveness in improving classification performance.
- The paper is well-written, making it easy to follow, and the experimental results are robust, showing the method's effectiveness across various datasets.
- The use of coarse-grained labels in pretraining models for fine-grained classification is a promising approach, which is validated by the results.
- The theoretical analysis provides insights into the learning process of fine-grained label prediction tasks, enhancing the understanding of the proposed method.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper could benefit from a more detailed discussion on the limitations and potential negative societal impacts of the proposed method.
- The methodology section could be clearer, especially in explaining the definitions and roles of different variables and functions used in the algorithm.
- The paper could provide more detailed comparisons with other methods, especially in the medical domain, to better highlight the advantages of the proposed method.
- The assumptions made in the theoretical analysis, such as the availability of a "good" embedding, may not hold in real-world scenarios, which could affect the practical applicability of the findings.
- The paper lacks a detailed discussion on the computational costs and scalability of the proposed method, which is crucial for practical implementation.
- The paper could benefit from more diverse experimental setups, including different training strategies and datasets, to validate the robustness of the method.

**Questions:**
- Could you clarify the definitions and roles of different variables and functions used in the algorithm, particularly those mentioned in the methodology section?
- How does the proposed method perform when pretrained on a large-scale dataset like ImageNet, and how does it compare to other methods in terms of performance and computational efficiency?
- How does the method handle cases where the coarse-grained labels are not representative or accurate, and what are the potential impacts on the fine-grained classification performance?
- Could you provide more details on the computational costs and scalability of the proposed method, particularly in terms of memory and training time?
- How does the method perform when the coarse-grained labels are not provided during testing, and how does it compare to other methods in such scenarios?
- Could you discuss the potential negative societal impacts of the proposed method, especially in medical applications, and how these impacts could be mitigated?
