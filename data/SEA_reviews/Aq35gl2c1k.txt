 **Summary:**
The paper investigates the phenomenon of critical learning periods in deep linear networks, focusing on how these periods manifest in multi-pathway networks and matrix completion tasks. It employs a differential equation approach to model the learning dynamics, demonstrating that early deficits can significantly impact learning outcomes, particularly in deeper networks. The authors also explore the implications of these findings for generalization and transfer learning, suggesting that the depth of the network and the structure of the data distribution are crucial factors influencing these processes. The paper provides both analytical and empirical evidence to support its claims, including a detailed exploration of the dynamics of singular values and vector learning in matrix completion settings.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel analytical model for critical learning periods in deep linear networks, which is a significant contribution to the field.
- The authors provide a detailed mathematical derivation and empirical evidence to support their claims, enhancing the paper's credibility and reproducibility.
- The analysis of the dynamics of singular values and vector learning in matrix completion settings is insightful and adds depth to the understanding of critical learning periods.
- The paper is well-written, with clear explanations and detailed experimental results, making it accessible and easy to follow.
- The findings have implications for both artificial and biological networks, potentially enhancing our understanding of critical learning periods in both contexts.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper's reliance on linear networks for its analysis may limit the generalizability of the findings to more complex, non-linear networks, which are more prevalent in practical applications.
- The experimental setups used in the paper are somewhat simplistic, which might not fully capture the complexity of real-world scenarios, especially in terms of data distribution and network architecture.
- There is a lack of discussion on the implications of the findings for practical applications in deep learning, which could limit the paper's impact on the field.
- The paper could benefit from a more comprehensive literature review, particularly in the area of critical learning periods in deep learning, to situate its contributions more effectively within the existing body of knowledge.
- The paper's focus on linear networks might be seen as a limitation, as it does not fully address the non-linearity inherent in biological systems, which could affect the generalizability of the findings.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors elaborate on the potential implications of their findings for practical applications in deep learning, particularly in terms of improving generalization and transfer learning?
- How does the paper's analysis of critical learning periods in linear networks translate to more complex, non-linear networks, and what are the potential limitations or challenges in this translation?
- Could the authors discuss the potential impact of their findings on the development of more robust and adaptable neural networks, especially in light of the observed critical learning periods?
- What are the specific contributions of the paper that distinguish it from existing works on critical learning periods in deep learning, and how do these contributions advance the understanding of this phenomenon?
- In light of the simplified experimental setups used in the paper, how do the authors plan to address the limitations of these setups in future work, and what steps will be taken to ensure that the findings are more representative of real-world scenarios?
