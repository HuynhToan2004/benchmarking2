 **Summary:**
The paper introduces TextGenSHAP, a novel method for post-hoc explanations in text generation using Large Language Models (LLMs). It adapts the Shapley value approach to handle hierarchical inputs, leveraging speculative decoding, block sparsity, and in-place resampling to enhance computational efficiency. TextGenSHAP is designed to scale to long inputs, large models, and open-ended text generation, with a focus on improving the performance of LLMs in long-document question answering and document retrieval. The methodology is tested on various datasets, showing improvements in speed and performance compared to baseline models. However, the paper's presentation and clarity need significant improvement, and the novelty and the practical implications of the speedup are questioned.

**Strengths:**
- The paper addresses a significant and challenging problem in the field of post-hoc explanations for text generation, which is crucial for understanding and improving the performance of large language models.
- The methodology is well-articulated, with a clear presentation of the proposed TextGenSHAP method and its components, including speculative decoding, block sparsity, and in-place resampling.
- The paper provides a thorough discussion on related work, including a detailed background on Shapley values and their application to text generation.
- The experiments are robust, demonstrating significant improvements in speed and performance over baseline models, particularly in long-document question answering and document retrieval tasks.
- The paper is well-written, with clear and detailed explanations of the methodology and results, which are supported by extensive visualizations and interactive demonstrations.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a clear and coherent narrative, making it difficult to follow and understand the main contributions and their implications.
- The novelty of the proposed method is questionable, as it primarily combines existing techniques such as Shapley values, speculative decoding, and block sparsity, without significant innovation.
- The paper does not adequately address the practical implications of the speedup, particularly how it translates into real-world applications or improvements in performance.
- There is a lack of discussion on the limitations of the method, which could include the complexity of the method, the scalability to larger models, and the generalizability of the results.
- The paper suffers from presentation issues, including unclear figures and a lack of clarity in the text, which could hinder understanding and reproducibility.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors clarify the practical implications of the speedup and how it translates into real-world applications or improvements in performance?
- How does the proposed method compare to other existing methods in terms of computational efficiency and explanatory power?
- Could the authors provide more detailed examples or case studies to illustrate the practical applications and benefits of the TextGenSHAP method?
- How does the method handle the complexity of longer inputs, and what are the computational costs associated with this complexity?
- Can the authors discuss the potential limitations of the method and how these might impact its applicability or effectiveness in different scenarios?
