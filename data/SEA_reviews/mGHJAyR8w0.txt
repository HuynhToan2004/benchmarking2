 **Summary:**
The paper explores the theoretical and empirical aspects of steerable features in k-hop invariant and equivariant graph neural networks (GNNs). It introduces a lemma that shows the relationship between steerable and invariant features, and argues that steerable features do not provide significant performance improvements when the feature dimension remains constant. The authors also demonstrate that k-hop invariant GNNs may fail to capture the changing geometry between local structures, leading to inaccurate global invariant features. The paper supports its theoretical findings with empirical evidence, showing that the performance of equivariant GNNs using steerable features does not significantly improve with increasing L.

**Strengths:**
- The paper provides a clear and detailed theoretical analysis of steerable features, which is both interesting and valuable for the community.
- The empirical results are well-presented, supporting the theoretical findings, and the experiments are well-designed.
- The paper is well-written, making the content accessible and understandable, and includes a detailed appendix that provides additional insights and proofs.
- The theoretical contributions are significant and could be of independent interest to the community, particularly in understanding the relationship between equivariant and invariant GNNs.
- The paper is original in its approach to analyzing the information-carrying ability of steerable features, which is a novel contribution to the field.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed discussion on the computational complexity of steerable features, which is crucial for understanding the practical implications of using these features.
- The paper does not sufficiently compare its theoretical contributions with existing works, particularly those that discuss the relationship between equivariant and invariant GNNs.
- The empirical results are not convincingly presented, and the paper could benefit from more comprehensive experimental validation and a clearer presentation of the results.
- There are some typographical errors and inconsistencies in the notation and definitions used throughout the paper, which could confuse readers.
- The paper could benefit from a more detailed discussion on the limitations and potential negative societal impacts of the proposed methods.

**Questions:**
- Could you clarify the definition and role of the function c in Lemma 1, and explain why it is set-theoretical?
- How does the paper address the potential confounding factors such as over-squashing in the experiments, and what steps were taken to mitigate their effects?
- In Theorem 1, how does the paper ensure that the AGG function is a homomorphism, and what implications does this have for the results?
- Could you provide more details on the computational complexity of steerable features, particularly how it scales with the number of hops and the size of the graph?
- How does the paper ensure that the equivariance of the UPD and AGG functions is maintained across different iterations, and what are the implications if this equivariance is lost?
- Can you discuss the relationship between the findings in this paper and the results from other recent works that also investigate the relationship between equivariant and invariant GNNs?
