 **Summary:**
The paper discusses adaptive attacks on image watermarking, specifically focusing on Stable Diffusion models. It introduces a methodology that optimizes adversarial attacks by training a differentiable watermark extraction neural network to predict messages from images. This approach allows for the computation of gradients, enabling the optimization of attack parameters. The paper also explores two types of adversarial attacks: adversarial noising and adversarial compression. The former adds noise to images to evade detection, while the latter compresses images to minimize quality loss. The experiments demonstrate that these attacks can effectively evade watermark detection, albeit with some limitations in the generalizability and practicality of the attacks.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel approach to adaptive attacks on image watermarking, which is an important and timely topic given the widespread use of image generators.
- The proposed method is technically sound, well-explained, and the presentation is clear, making it accessible to a broad audience.
- The paper provides a comprehensive analysis of the robustness of various watermarking methods, which is crucial for understanding the security of digital content.
- The methodology is supported by a strong experimental validation, showing the effectiveness of the proposed attacks against different watermarking methods.
- The paper is well-written, with clear figures and a clear presentation of the methodology and results.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a discussion on the limitations of the proposed method, which is essential for understanding its applicability and potential drawbacks.
- The practicality of the attacks is questionable, as the need for a differentiable watermark extraction neural network might limit their effectiveness in real-world scenarios.
- The generalization of the attacks to different types of watermarking methods and models is not thoroughly explored, which could affect the reliability of the results.
- The paper does not adequately address the ethical implications of the attacks, particularly in terms of their potential misuse.
- There are some issues with the clarity and consistency of the writing, including typos and inconsistencies in the use of terms and notation.
- The paper could benefit from a more detailed discussion on the computational costs and the scalability of the proposed attacks.

**Questions:**
- Could you elaborate on the limitations of the proposed method and how these might affect its practical application?
- How do the authors ensure the generalizability of their attacks to different types of watermarking methods and models?
- Can the authors provide more details on the computational costs and scalability of the proposed attacks, especially in terms of the resources required for optimization?
- How do the authors address the ethical implications of their work, particularly in terms of the potential misuse of their findings?
- Could you clarify the notation and terminology used in the paper, particularly in the description of the watermarking methods and the attacks?
- How do the authors ensure the reliability and reproducibility of their results, especially given the complexity of the attacks and the potential for variability in watermarking methods?
