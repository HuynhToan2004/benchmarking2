 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel approach to offline reinforcement learning (RL) called COmpositional COnservatism with Anchor-seeking (COCOA), which leverages bilinear transduction to address the out-of-distribution (OOD) problem. COCOA decomposes the state into an anchor and a delta, using a learned dynamics model to predict the next state, thereby enhancing generalization capabilities. The method is evaluated on the D4RL benchmark and shows improvements over various baselines. However, concerns are raised about the novelty and the clarity of the paper's presentation, particularly in explaining the method's contributions and its theoretical underpinnings.

**Strengths:**
- The paper addresses the significant and relevant problem of out-of-distribution (OOD) generalization in offline reinforcement learning (RL), which is crucial for practical applications.
- The proposed COCOA method is novel and effectively integrates bilinear transduction to enhance the generalization capabilities of offline RL algorithms.
- The paper is well-written, with clear explanations of the methodology and the results, which are supported by empirical evidence.
- The method is evaluated on a standard benchmark, demonstrating its effectiveness in improving performance across various tasks.
- The paper provides a detailed analysis of the method's components, including an ablation study that shows the importance of the anchor-seeking policy.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks clarity in explaining the method's contributions and the role of bilinear transduction, which could be better introduced earlier to enhance understanding.
- There is a lack of theoretical justification for the method's effectiveness, particularly in terms of its assumptions and the conditions under which it can guarantee OOD generalization.
- The paper does not adequately compare its method with existing approaches, such as those that use factorized architectures or other methods that address OOD generalization.
- The paper's presentation could be improved, particularly in the clarity of figures and the organization of the content.
- The paper does not sufficiently discuss the limitations of the method, which could affect its applicability in various scenarios.
- There are inconsistencies and potential errors in the presentation of mathematical equations and the descriptions of algorithms, which could confuse readers.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors clarify the role and contributions of bilinear transduction in the proposed method?
- How does the method handle scenarios where the anchor is not in the dataset, and what are the implications of this for the OOD generalization?
- Can the authors provide more details on the selection of the anchor-seeking policy and its impact on the method's performance?
- How does the COCOA method compare with other methods that use factorized architectures or other approaches to address OOD generalization in offline RL?
- Could the authors discuss the limitations of their method and how these might affect its applicability in various scenarios?
- How does the method perform in environments with discrete action spaces, and what modifications would be necessary for such environments?
