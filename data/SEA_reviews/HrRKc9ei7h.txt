 **Summary:**
The paper addresses the problem of online prediction with groupwise regret minimization, where the goal is to ensure that the cumulative loss of the algorithm is comparable to the best model within each group, not just overall. It builds on the framework of Blum and Lykouris (2020) and introduces a simple modification that makes the algorithm oracle-efficient, reducing the computational complexity. The paper also provides theoretical guarantees and empirical evaluations using real and synthetic datasets, demonstrating improvements over existing methods. The approach is particularly notable for its ability to handle large groups and complex hypothesis classes, such as linear models in online linear regression.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel algorithm for groupwise regret minimization in the online learning setting, which is an extension of existing algorithms like those by Blum and Lykouris (2020).
- The algorithm is oracle-efficient, which is a significant improvement over previous methods that were not oracle-efficient.
- The paper is well-written, clear, and easy to follow, with a clear presentation of the algorithm and its applications.
- Theoretical results are sound, and the paper provides a solid theoretical foundation for the proposed algorithm.
- The experimental results demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed algorithm in real-world applications, showing improvements over existing methods.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper does not clearly explain why the proposed algorithm is oracle-efficient, which is a critical contribution of the work.
- The paper lacks a detailed comparison with other related works, particularly those that address similar problems in different settings.
- The experimental setup could be improved by including more diverse datasets and baseline methods to better evaluate the performance of the proposed algorithm.
- There are some minor typographical errors and unclear sections in the paper that need to be addressed for clarity and accuracy.
- The paper does not adequately address the limitations of the proposed algorithm, which could include scenarios where the algorithm might not perform optimally or where it could be outperformed by other methods.

- Could the authors discuss the limitations of the proposed algorithm and provide scenarios where it might not perform optimally or where other methods might be more suitable?
