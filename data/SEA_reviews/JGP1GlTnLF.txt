 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel training-time defense method called Learning from Distinction (LfD) aimed at mitigating backdoor attacks by training a low-capacity model as a teacher to guide the learning of a backdoor-free student model. The method leverages the observation that the loss on a low-capacity teacher model is higher for clean data than poisoned data, which helps in distinguishing between the two. The LfD method dynamically weights the training data based on their loss on the teacher model, facilitating the training of the student model. Extensive experiments across various datasets demonstrate the effectiveness of the LfD method in reducing the attack success rate while maintaining clean accuracy. However, concerns were raised about the generalizability of the method to more advanced and realistic backdoor attacks, the scalability of the method to larger datasets, and the potential impact on the performance of the student model.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, easy to follow, and the motivation is clear.
- The observation that the low-capacity teacher model is more sensitive to clean data is interesting and novel.
- The proposed method is simple and effective, demonstrated through extensive experiments across various datasets.
- The paper provides a clear explanation of the method and its theoretical analysis, which is supported by experimental results.
- The proposed method is efficient and does not require additional datasets or training data, making it practical for real-world applications.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a thorough review of related work, particularly in the area of backdoor defenses.
- The method's effectiveness is limited to specific types of backdoor attacks, such as dirty-label attacks, and may not generalize to other types of attacks like clean-label attacks.
- The method's scalability to larger datasets and more complex models is not addressed, raising concerns about its practical applicability.
- The paper does not discuss the potential negative societal impacts of the proposed method.
- The paper lacks a detailed discussion on the limitations of the method, including its potential impact on the performance of the student model and its generalizability to more advanced and realistic backdoor attacks.
- There is a lack of comparison with other state-of-the-art backdoor defenses, which could provide a more comprehensive evaluation of the proposed method.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors clarify the impact of the proposed method on the performance of the student model, especially in terms of clean accuracy?
- How does the proposed method perform against clean-label attacks, which are known to be more difficult to detect?
- Can the authors provide more details on the scalability of the method to larger datasets and more complex models?
- How does the proposed method compare to other state-of-the-art backdoor defenses, particularly in terms of effectiveness and computational efficiency?
- Could the authors discuss the potential negative societal impacts of the proposed method and how these could be mitigated?
- How does the method handle different types of backdoor attacks, such as those that do not rely on trigger patterns?
