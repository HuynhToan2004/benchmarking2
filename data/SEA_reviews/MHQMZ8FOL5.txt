 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel adaptive self-labeling framework for novel class discovery in point cloud segmentation. It addresses the challenges of imbalanced class distribution and the lack of spatial context information by proposing a dual-level representation that integrates regional consistency into point-level classifier learning. This framework utilizes a semi-relaxed optimal transport problem with adaptive regularization to generate pseudo-labels for imbalanced classes, which are then used to train the model. The methodology is supported by extensive experiments on two widely-used datasets, demonstrating significant performance improvements over state-of-the-art methods. However, concerns about the clarity of the writing and the novelty of the approach, particularly the similarity to existing methods, were raised.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, making it easy to follow and understand.
- The proposed dual-level representation is reasonable and effective, integrating spatial context information into point-level classifier learning.
- The adaptive self-labeling framework is innovative, addressing the challenge of imbalanced class distribution and generating high-quality pseudo-labels for imbalanced classes.
- The paper includes extensive experiments on two widely-used datasets, demonstrating significant performance improvements over state-of-the-art methods.
- The methodology is supported by an ablation study that evaluates the significance of different components of the proposed method.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed explanation of the methodology, particularly the adaptive self-labeling framework and the dual-level representation.
- There are concerns about the novelty of the approach, as it appears to be similar to existing methods such as those proposed by Zhang et al. and Long et al.
- The experimental results, while showing improvements, do not consistently outperform the state-of-the-art, particularly in terms of IoU metrics for known classes.
- The paper suffers from poor writing quality, with several grammatical errors and unclear sentences that make it difficult to understand.
- The paper lacks a detailed discussion on the limitations of the proposed method and does not include a discussion on the limitations of the experimental setup.

**Questions:**
- Could you clarify the methodology, particularly the adaptive self-labeling framework and the dual-level representation?
- How does the proposed method handle the issue of degenerate solutions mentioned in the paper?
- Can you provide a more detailed explanation of the experimental setup and the results, especially the discrepancies in IoU metrics for known classes?
- How does the proposed method compare to existing methods in terms of computational efficiency and resource requirements?
- Could you provide a more detailed discussion on the limitations of the proposed method and suggest potential areas for future research?
