 **Summary:**
The paper introduces the Object-Oriented Causal Dynamics Model (OOCDM), which extends causal dynamics models to object-oriented environments. OOCDM leverages causality sharing among objects of the same class, which is crucial for handling large-scale environments. It employs a method called Object-Oriented Causal Discovery (OOCG) to reduce the computational complexity of causal discovery, thereby improving sample efficiency. The model is evaluated in various environments, including the StarCraft II mini-game, demonstrating its effectiveness in causal discovery and prediction accuracy. However, the paper's assumptions and the generalizability of the model across different environments are questioned.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, clear, and easy to follow, with a clear motivation for the research.
- The problem of causal discovery in large-scale environments is well-addressed, and the proposed Object-Oriented Causal Dynamics Model (OOCDM) is innovative in its approach to causal dynamics models.
- The paper introduces the Object-Oriented Causal Graph (OOCG), which is a novel contribution to the field, and the methodology is well-supported by theoretical and empirical evidence.
- The paper provides a detailed description of the methodology, including the use of conditional mutual information (CMI) for causal discovery, which is a significant contribution to the field.
- The experiments are well-designed, and the results show that OOCDM outperforms existing causal discovery models in terms of accuracy and computational efficiency.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper's assumption that the causal structure of the environment is object-oriented and that the causal effects of objects in the same class are the same is not well-supported by empirical evidence.
- The paper lacks a comprehensive discussion on the limitations of the proposed method, particularly in terms of its generalizability to different types of environments and the potential impact of spurious correlations.
- The paper could benefit from a more detailed discussion on the computational complexity of the proposed method and a comparison with other causal discovery models in terms of efficiency.
- The experimental results are limited to specific environments, and it would be beneficial to see how the model performs in other domains to assess its broader applicability.
- There are some minor issues with the paper's presentation, such as the clarity of certain figures and the need for more detailed explanations of certain methodological aspects.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors provide more details on how the causal structure of the environment is determined and whether it is a priori known or learned from data?
- How does the proposed method handle the presence of spurious correlations, and what are the implications for the accuracy of causal discovery?
- Could the authors discuss the potential impact of the number of objects on the performance of the proposed method and provide more detailed experimental results to support their claims?
- How does the proposed method compare in terms of computational complexity and efficiency with other causal discovery models, particularly in large-scale environments?
- Are there any plans to extend the experimental validation to include more diverse environments, such as different types of reinforcement learning tasks?
- Can the authors clarify the definitions and notations used in the paper, particularly those related to the Object-Oriented Markov Decision Process (OOMDP) and the Object-Oriented Causal Graph (OOCG)?
