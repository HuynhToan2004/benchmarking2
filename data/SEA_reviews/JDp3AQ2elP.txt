 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel approach, STOP, aimed at addressing the challenges of deep reinforcement learning (RL) in environments where state and cost functions are unbounded, and the system is reset-free. The authors propose a Lyapunov-inspired reward shaping method to encourage stability in learning, which is crucial for preventing divergence in unseen states. The STOP approach is evaluated across various real-world-inspired domains, demonstrating its effectiveness in achieving stability and optimality in such settings. The paper also includes a theoretical analysis and empirical results that validate the proposed method's efficacy. However, concerns are raised regarding the clarity of the theoretical results and the empirical results' generalizability.

**Strengths:**
- The paper addresses a significant and relevant problem in the field of deep reinforcement learning (RL), specifically focusing on environments where state and cost functions are unbounded and the system is reset-free.
- The proposed STOP approach is innovative, combining reward shaping, reverse annealing, and state transformations to tackle the challenges of learning in such environments.
- The paper is well-written, with clear and concise explanations of the proposed method and its components, making it accessible and understandable.
- The empirical results demonstrate the effectiveness of the STOP approach, showing that it can achieve stability and optimality in real-world-inspired domains.
- The paper includes a thorough empirical study that covers various components of the STOP approach, providing a comprehensive evaluation of its effectiveness.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks clarity in some theoretical results, particularly in the proofs and assumptions used. This could lead to confusion and difficulty in understanding the theoretical underpinnings of the proposed method.
- The empirical results are limited to specific domains, which may not generalize well to other types of environments or tasks. The paper does not provide sufficient evidence of the method's effectiveness in more diverse or complex scenarios.
- There is a lack of discussion on the limitations and potential negative societal impacts of the proposed method, which is crucial for a complete assessment of its applicability and ethical implications.
- The paper could benefit from a more detailed discussion on the practical implementation and tuning of the STOP approach, as well as a more comprehensive comparison with existing methods to highlight its advantages.
- The paper does not sufficiently address the scalability and computational efficiency of the STOP approach, which are important considerations for its practical deployment in real-world applications.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors clarify the assumptions and proofs used in the theoretical results, particularly in Proposition 1 and 2, to ensure their validity and clarity?
- How does the STOP approach perform in more diverse or complex environments beyond those tested in the empirical study? Can the authors provide additional evidence or insights into its generalizability?
- What are the specific advantages of the STOP approach over existing methods, particularly in terms of computational efficiency and scalability?
- How does the STOP approach handle scenarios where the state and cost functions are not directly comparable or have different scales?
- Could the authors provide more details on the practical implementation and tuning of the STOP approach, including any challenges or limitations encountered during its deployment?
- How does the STOP approach compare with other methods that also aim to address the challenges of learning in unbounded state spaces? What are the unique contributions of the STOP approach compared to these existing methods?
