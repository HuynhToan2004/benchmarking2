 **Summary:**
The paper investigates the impact of signal delay in deep reinforcement learning (DRL) by proposing a new framework, Delayed observation Markov Decision Process (DOMDP), to address the challenges posed by delayed observations. The authors explore various methods to mitigate the effects of signal delay, including historical state reconstruction for the critic and actor state augmentation with historical actions. The paper presents a comprehensive empirical evaluation across MuJoCo environments, demonstrating the effectiveness of the proposed methods in scenarios with significant delays. However, concerns are raised regarding the clarity of the paper, the depth of theoretical analysis, and the generalizability of the findings beyond the tested environments.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written and easy to follow, with clear explanations and figures that aid in understanding the proposed methods.
- The problem of signal delay in deep reinforcement learning is identified as a significant issue, and the paper provides a novel approach to address it by considering a special case of POMDP, Delayed observation Markov Decision Process (DOMDP).
- Extensive experiments are conducted to demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed methods, showing that the algorithms can achieve good performance even with large delays.
- The paper is well-organized, with a clear structure that makes it easy to follow the authors' arguments and findings.
- The authors have provided code, which enhances the reproducibility of the results.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed theoretical analysis, particularly in the proofs and theorems presented, which could benefit from more rigorous mathematical formulation and clarity.
- The generalization of the proposed methods to other environments or tasks beyond those tested is not clearly demonstrated.
- The paper could benefit from a more comprehensive literature review, particularly in the area of POMDP and related works that address signal delay in reinforcement learning.
- The paper is somewhat hard to follow, with some sections and figures that could be improved for better clarity and readability.
- There is a lack of discussion on the limitations of the proposed methods and their potential negative societal impacts.

**Questions:**
- Could you clarify the proofs and theorems, particularly Theorem 2.1, to make them more rigorous and accessible?
- How do the proposed methods perform in environments with different characteristics, such as stochastic transitions or different observation spaces?
- Could you provide more details on the hyperparameters used in the experiments and how they were chosen?
- Is there a possibility to extend the proposed methods to other types of reinforcement learning problems, such as those involving stochastic transitions or different observation spaces?
- How do the proposed methods compare to existing methods in terms of computational efficiency and scalability?
- Could you discuss the limitations of the proposed methods and their potential negative societal impacts?
