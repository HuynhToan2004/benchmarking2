 **Summary:**
The paper introduces ReLoRA, a method that enhances parameter-efficient training by utilizing low-rank updates to train high-rank networks, thereby reducing computational costs and improving training speed. This method, which is a modification of the LoRA method, is evaluated on transformer language models with up to 1.3 billion parameters, demonstrating comparable performance to regular neural network training. ReLoRA employs a warm-start from a full-rank training checkpoint and performs low-rank updates periodically, which are then merged back into the original parameters. The methodology is supported by empirical evidence, including speedup and memory savings, and is tested across various model sizes. However, concerns are raised about the clarity of the method's theoretical underpinnings and the robustness of the results, particularly in larger model sizes and different hardware configurations.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, making it easy to follow and understand the proposed method.
- The proposed method is simple and effective, with a clear and well-organized presentation that enhances readability.
- The empirical results are robust, demonstrating the effectiveness of the proposed method across various model sizes and hardware configurations.
- The paper provides a comprehensive evaluation of the proposed method, including a detailed comparison with existing methods and thorough ablation studies.
- The methodology is innovative, addressing the computational costs associated with training large-scale neural networks, which is a significant challenge in the field.
- The paper includes a detailed discussion of the experimental results, including a comparison of ReLoRA with LoRA and a full-rank training baseline, which helps in understanding the method's effectiveness.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed discussion on the theoretical underpinnings of the proposed method, which could enhance the understanding of its effectiveness and limitations.
- The results are not robust across different hardware configurations, particularly in larger model sizes, which raises concerns about the generalizability of the findings.
- The paper does not include a detailed comparison with other parameter-efficient training methods, which could provide a more comprehensive evaluation of the proposed method.
- The method's complexity, particularly the need for warm-starts and periodic merging of low-rank updates, might limit its practical applicability in some scenarios.
- The paper does not provide a detailed analysis of the computational costs and memory savings, which are crucial for evaluating the method's efficiency.
- There is a lack of discussion on the potential negative societal impacts of the proposed method, which is an important consideration for any technological innovation.

**Questions:**
- Could you provide more details on the theoretical underpinnings of the proposed method? How does it compare to other low-rank methods in terms of theoretical guarantees and practical efficacy?
- How does the proposed method perform on larger model sizes, such as those beyond 1.3 billion parameters?
- Could you include more comparisons with other parameter-efficient training methods to provide a more comprehensive evaluation of the proposed method?
- What are the computational costs and memory savings associated with using ReLoRA? How do these compare to other parameter-efficient training methods?
- How does the proposed method handle the complexity introduced by the need for warm-starts and periodic merging of low-rank updates?
- Are there any potential negative societal impacts of using ReLoRA or similar parameter-efficient training methods? How do you address these concerns?
