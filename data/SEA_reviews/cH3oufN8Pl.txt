 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel training method called Label-focused Latent-object Biasing (LLB) aimed at enhancing the generalization of neural networks in visual classification tasks. The LLB method decouples visual dependencies and integrates structured features from labels, allowing the model to learn latent object features that are not solely influenced by visual similarities. This approach is validated through experiments on various image classification benchmarks, showing significant improvements over existing methods. The paper also provides a detailed analysis of the method's components and their impact on model performance.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, making it easy to follow, and the proposed method is clearly explained.
- The introduction of the concept of "Undescribed World Knowledge" (UWK) is novel and interesting, providing a new perspective on visual classification.
- The proposed method, Label-focused Latent-object Biasing (LLB), is simple yet effective, with significant improvements demonstrated in various image classification benchmarks.
- The paper provides a detailed analysis of the LLB method, including its components and their impact on model performance, which is supported by extensive experimental results.
- The visualization of latent object features and their impact on model performance is insightful and helps in understanding the effectiveness of the proposed method.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed discussion on how the proposed method differs from existing knowledge graph-based methods, which might lead to confusion about the uniqueness of the proposed approach.
- The paper does not include a detailed discussion on the limitations of the proposed method, which could provide a more balanced view of its applicability and effectiveness.
- The paper's experimental setup and results are not sufficiently robust, with some results, such as those on the Places365 dataset, being significantly lower than expected.
- The paper's claims about the generalization of the model are not convincingly supported by the experimental results, which could undermine the credibility of the proposed method.
- There is a lack of discussion on the computational complexity of the proposed method, which is crucial for understanding its practical applicability.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors provide a more detailed comparison between the proposed method and existing knowledge graph-based methods to clarify the unique contributions of the LLB approach?
- How does the proposed method perform on other downstream tasks such as object detection and segmentation?
- Can the authors provide more detailed experimental results, especially for the Places365 dataset, to clarify the discrepancies observed in the results?
- How does the computational complexity of the proposed method compare to other methods, and what are the practical implications of this complexity in terms of real-world applications?
- Could the authors provide more detailed ablation studies, including the impact of different hyperparameters and the effects of different pre-training methods on the performance of the LLB approach?
