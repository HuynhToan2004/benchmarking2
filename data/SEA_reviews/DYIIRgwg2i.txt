 **Summary:**
The paper introduces LLM Surgeon, a novel method for structured pruning of large language models (LLMs) by leveraging a block-diagonal approximation of the Fisher information matrix. This method scales up the KFAC approximation of the loss landscape to LLMs, allowing for the computation of both dynamic allocation of structures that can be removed and updates of remaining weights. The approach is evaluated on various LLMs, including OPT and Llama-v2, and shows promising results in structured pruning, achieving up to 30% compression with minimal performance loss. The methodology includes a structured pruning framework that considers weight correlations and employs a multi-shot pruning schedule to mitigate the effects of local optima. Despite its strengths, the paper has some limitations, such as limited experiments to only one dataset and the absence of a discussion on the computational cost and complexity of the proposed method.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, making the methodology and contributions clear.
- The proposed method is novel and well-explained, with a clear and comprehensive description of the approach.
- The experimental results are promising, showing that the proposed method can achieve up to 30% compression with minimal performance loss.
- The paper is original in its approach to structured pruning of large language models, which is a significant contribution to the field.
- The methodology is robust and well-explained, with a clear motivation for the use of structured pruning.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a discussion on the computational cost and complexity of the proposed method, which is crucial for understanding its practical applicability.
- There is a limited evaluation of the method, focusing only on one dataset (wikitext-2) and not comparing the results against other datasets or models.
- The paper does not provide a detailed discussion on the limitations of the proposed method, which could help in understanding its applicability and potential drawbacks.
