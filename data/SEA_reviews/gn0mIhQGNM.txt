 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel method for machine unlearning, specifically focusing on the concept of weight saliency to enhance the unlearning process in both image classification and generation tasks. The authors propose a method called SalUn, which identifies and updates salient weights in a model to achieve effective unlearning. This approach is contrasted with traditional methods that either forget all weights or none, offering a more targeted and efficient solution. The paper provides extensive experiments and comparisons with existing methods, demonstrating the effectiveness and efficiency of SalUn. However, concerns were raised about the novelty of the approach, the clarity of the presentation, and the depth of the experimental validation.

**Strengths:**
- The paper addresses an important and timely topic of machine unlearning, which is crucial for enhancing the trustworthiness and privacy of AI systems.
- The concept of weight saliency is well-explained and provides a novel approach to unlearning, which is both intuitive and practical.
- The paper is well-organized, with clear and concise writing that effectively communicates the proposed methodology and its applications.
- The proposed method, SalUn, is simple yet effective, as demonstrated by the extensive experiments and comparisons with existing methods.
- The paper provides a comprehensive evaluation of the proposed method, including both qualitative and quantitative results, which help to validate the effectiveness of SalUn.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a thorough discussion on the limitations of the proposed method, which could help in understanding its scope and applicability.
- There are concerns about the novelty of the approach, as the concept of weight saliency has been explored in previous works, and the paper does not sufficiently distinguish its contributions from existing literature.
- The experimental results, while showing improvements, do not convincingly outperform existing methods in all scenarios, raising questions about the robustness and generalizability of the proposed method.
- The paper suffers from some presentation issues, such as unclear figures and a lack of clarity in the methodological descriptions, which could hinder the understanding of the proposed method.
- The paper does not adequately address the computational overhead of the proposed method, which could be a significant concern in practical applications.
- The paper could benefit from a more detailed discussion on the potential negative societal impacts of the proposed method, especially in the context of machine unlearning.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors clarify the novelty of their approach and how it differs from existing works on weight saliency and model unlearning?
- How does the proposed method compare in terms of computational efficiency and practical applicability to existing methods, especially in large-scale applications?
- Could the authors provide more detailed experimental results, including the computational costs and the impact of different hyperparameters on the performance of the method?
- What are the potential negative societal impacts of the proposed method, and how do the authors address these concerns?
- How does the proposed method perform in scenarios where the data distribution is significantly different from the training data, and what are the implications of this for the effectiveness of the unlearning process?
