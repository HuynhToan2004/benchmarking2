 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel class of adversarial attacks on sequential decision-makers, specifically focusing on observation-space attacks in reinforcement learning. The proposed method, termed "illusory attacks," aims to minimize the KL divergence between the victim's policy and the adversary's policy, thereby making the attacks imperceptible to both automated detectors and human observers. The paper also proposes a dual ascent algorithm and scalable estimators to learn these attacks effectively. The empirical results demonstrate that illusory attacks are more difficult to detect than traditional attacks, both by automated detectors and human subjects. The paper also discusses the implications of these attacks in real-world cyber-physical systems and suggests potential defense mechanisms.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel class of adversarial attacks, "illusory attacks," which are designed to be imperceptible to both automated detectors and human observers.
- The methodology is well-articulated, with clear explanations of the theoretical framework and empirical results.
- The paper is well-written, making it easy to follow, and the experiments are well-designed, showing that illusory attacks are harder to detect than existing attacks.
- The authors propose a dual ascent algorithm and scalable estimators to learn illusory attacks in high-dimensional control environments.
- The paper discusses the implications of these attacks in real-world cyber-physical systems and suggests potential defense mechanisms.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed discussion on the limitations of the proposed method, particularly in terms of its applicability to different types of sequential decision-making problems and its effectiveness against various types of defenses.
- The paper does not provide a comprehensive theoretical analysis of the Nash equilibria within the two-player zero-sum game introduced by the illusory attack framework.
- The paper does not include a discussion on the limitations of the proposed method in terms of its scalability and applicability to more complex real-world scenarios.
- The paper does not compare the proposed method with other existing methods for robustifying agent policies, which could provide a more comprehensive evaluation of the proposed approach.
- The paper does not discuss the potential negative consequences of the proposed method, such as its impact on cybersecurity and privacy.
- The paper could benefit from a more detailed discussion on the practical implications of the proposed method in real-world scenarios and the challenges associated with implementing it in complex, real-world systems.
