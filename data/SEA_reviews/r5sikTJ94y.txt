 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel approach called RAOQ (Reshape and Adapt for Output Quantization) to address the quantization errors in In-Memory Computing (IMC) systems. RAOQ employs techniques such as weight reshaping, activation shifting, and bit augmentation to optimize the training of neural networks for IMC. The method aims to maximize the utilization of ADC quantization levels, thereby improving the accuracy of neural networks in the presence of quantization noise. The approach is evaluated on various datasets and models, demonstrating its effectiveness in mitigating the impact of quantization on neural network performance.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, with clear explanations of the proposed methods and their implementation.
- The motivation behind the research is well-articulated, and the authors provide a comprehensive analysis of the impact of ADC quantization on neural network performance.
- The proposed methods, including weight reshaping and activation shifting, are novel and well-supported by empirical results.
- The paper includes a detailed analysis of the impact of quantization on the performance of neural networks, which is crucial for understanding the challenges of quantization in In-Memory Computing (IMC) systems.
- The experiments conducted are comprehensive, covering various datasets and models, which demonstrates the robustness and generalizability of the proposed methods.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a clear discussion on the specific IMC system used in the experiments, which could limit the applicability of the findings.
- There is a noticeable absence of comparisons with other state-of-the-art methods for quantization in IMC, which could help in understanding the relative performance of the proposed methods.
- The paper does not provide sufficient details on the hardware and software configurations used in the experiments, which could affect the reproducibility and applicability of the results.
- The paper does not address the energy efficiency of the proposed methods, which is a significant concern in IMC systems.
- There is a lack of discussion on the potential negative societal impacts of the proposed methods, which is essential for ensuring the ethical implications of the research.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors provide more details on the specific IMC system used in the experiments, including the hardware and software configurations?
- How does the proposed method compare with other state-of-the-art methods for quantization in IMC systems?
- Can the authors clarify the impact of the proposed methods on the energy efficiency of IMC systems?
- How do the authors address the potential negative societal impacts of their research, particularly in the context of IMC systems?
- Can the authors provide more detailed explanations of the BitAug method and its implementation, including the rationale behind the selection of different ADC bit precisions?
- Could the authors provide more detailed experimental results, such as the performance of the proposed methods on different datasets and models, to support the claims made in the paper?
