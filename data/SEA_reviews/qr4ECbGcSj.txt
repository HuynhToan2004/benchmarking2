 **Summary:**
The paper investigates the expressivity of various objective-specification formalisms in reinforcement learning (RL), comparing 17 different methods to understand their relative strengths and limitations. The study employs a Hasse diagram to illustrate the expressive power of each formalism, demonstrating that no single method dominates in all aspects. The authors provide theoretical results on the expressivity of each formalism, highlighting the unique capabilities of each and the challenges they pose in terms of optimizability. Despite the theoretical focus, the paper also discusses practical implications and future research directions, emphasizing the need for more tractable methods to balance expressivity and computational feasibility.
