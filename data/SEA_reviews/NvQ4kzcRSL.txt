 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel graph clustering method using a masked autoencoder framework, which is designed to automatically determine the number of clusters without prior knowledge. This is achieved by employing a density-based clustering algorithm as a second decoder, which optimizes the model's generalization and interpretability. The method is tested on several datasets, showing superior performance compared to state-of-the-art baselines. However, the paper is criticized for its poor writing, lack of clarity in the methodology, and inadequate experimental validation. The method's complexity and the absence of a clear motivation for using a masked autoencoder for graph clustering are also highlighted as significant concerns.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel approach to graph clustering by using a masked autoencoder framework, which is a first in this field.
- The methodology is well-motivated, with a clear explanation of the challenges in existing methods and the advantages of the proposed approach.
- The paper provides extensive experimental results and detailed ablation studies, demonstrating the effectiveness of the proposed method.
- The paper is well-written, making it easy to follow, and includes a comprehensive literature review that covers the most relevant and recent studies in the field.
- The method is innovative, using a masked autoencoder for graph clustering, which is a novel application of this technology.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper suffers from poor writing, with many grammatical errors and unclear sentences, making it difficult to understand the main contributions and the methodology.
- The methodology lacks clarity and depth, with insufficient explanation of key components such as the masking mechanism and the fusion image masking autoencoder.
- The paper does not adequately address the motivation for using a masked autoencoder for graph clustering, and the rationale behind the choice of this approach is not convincingly justified.
- The paper does not compare the proposed method with other non-parametric methods, which limits the understanding of its advantages and disadvantages compared to existing methods.
- The experimental setup and validation are inadequate, with no detailed explanation of the experimental conditions, such as the number of clusters used in the experiments and the methodology for setting hyperparameters.
- The paper lacks a discussion on the limitations of the proposed method, which is crucial for understanding its applicability and potential drawbacks.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors clarify the motivation for using a masked autoencoder for graph clustering and how this approach addresses the challenges in existing methods?
- How does the proposed method compare to other non-parametric methods, particularly in terms of performance and computational efficiency?
- Can the authors provide more details on the experimental setup, such as the number of clusters used and the methodology for setting hyperparameters?
- How does the proposed method handle different types of graphs, such as directed and undirected graphs, and what are the implications of this for the clustering results?
- Could the authors provide more details on the clustering algorithm used in the experiments and how it affects the clustering results?
- How does the proposed method handle the issue of over-smoothing in graph neural networks, and what are the implications of this for the clustering results?
