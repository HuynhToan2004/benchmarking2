 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a variant of Tsetlin Machines named Probabilistic Concept Learning (PCL), which modifies the training mechanism by allowing each clause to have a specific inclusion probability. This modification simplifies the convergence analysis, showing that PCL converges to a conjunction of literals when the inclusion probability is between 0.5 and 1. Theoretical proofs are provided to support this claim, along with empirical evidence from experiments. However, the paper has been critiqued for its lack of clarity in presentation, limited novelty, and insufficient empirical validation, particularly concerning the convergence of the original Tsetlin Machine.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel approach to Tsetlin Machines by integrating probabilistic inclusion, which is a significant advancement in the field.
- The theoretical analysis provided is robust and supports the convergence of the proposed variant, enhancing the understanding of Tsetlin Machines.
- The paper is well-written, making it accessible and easy to follow, with clear and concise explanations of the proposed framework and the convergence analysis.
- The convergence analysis of the proposed variant is comprehensive, offering insights into the convergence properties of Tsetlin Machines.
- The paper provides empirical evidence to support the theoretical findings, which is crucial for validating the proposed method.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks clarity in some sections, particularly in the definition and use of certain terms and symbols, which could confuse readers.
- The novelty of the proposed framework is limited as it appears to be a minor modification of existing Tsetlin Machines, and the convergence analysis seems similar to previous works.
- The empirical evaluation is limited and does not convincingly demonstrate the practical effectiveness of the proposed variant.
- The paper does not sufficiently address the convergence of the original Tsetlin Machine, which is a significant gap in the theoretical analysis.
- There are several typographical and grammatical errors that need correction to improve the readability and professionalism of the paper.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors clarify how the proposed variant relates to existing Tsetlin Machines, particularly in terms of its theoretical contributions?
- How does the proposed variant perform in practical applications, especially in scenarios where the original Tsetlin Machine might struggle?
- Could the authors provide a more detailed comparison with existing Tsetlin Machine variants to highlight the unique advantages of their proposed method?
- Can the authors provide more empirical evidence to support the convergence of the original Tsetlin Machine, especially in scenarios where the inclusion probability is not specified?
- How does the proposed variant handle the convergence in scenarios where the inclusion probability is not between 0.5 and 1?
