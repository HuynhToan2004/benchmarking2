 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel method for UI instruction grounding using a pixel-to-sequence model that leverages reinforcement learning to optimize the spatial decoding capability of the model. The approach aims to address the limitations of traditional pixel-to-sequence models by incorporating a policy gradient-based loss function that enhances the model's ability to understand the combinational semantics of tokens, thereby improving its performance in UI task automation. The methodology involves training the model on a dataset of 37K UI images from Common Crawl, which is used to generate 0.5M image-instruction pairs and their corresponding ground-truth labels. The paper also includes extensive experiments that demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed method, although concerns are raised about the dataset's diversity and the generalizability of the results.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel approach to UI instruction grounding using a pixel-to-sequence model, which is a significant departure from traditional methods that rely on metadata or additional information.
- The use of reinforcement learning to optimize the model's spatial decoding capability is innovative and addresses a limitation of traditional pixel-to-sequence models.
- The methodology is well-explained, with clear and comprehensive explanations of the dataset and experimental setup, including detailed results and comparisons to state-of-the-art models.
- The paper is well-written, making it easy to follow and understand, and includes a detailed ablation study that provides insights into the effectiveness of different components of the model.
- The proposed method demonstrates improvements in performance over existing methods, as shown in the experimental results.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed discussion on the diversity of the dataset used for training, which could affect the generalizability of the results.
- The evaluation metrics used are not well-explained, particularly the use of the term "Acc" which is not commonly used in the field of UI instruction grounding.
- The paper does not include a discussion on the limitations of the proposed method, which could help in understanding its scope and applicability.
- The method's reliance on a pre-trained document understanding model raises concerns about the novelty and the extent to which the improvements are due to the pre-training or the proposed method.
- The paper does not provide sufficient visualization results to support the claims made about the model's performance, which could be improved by including more detailed visualizations of the model's outputs.
- There is a lack of discussion on the societal impact of the work, which is crucial for understanding the broader implications of the research.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors clarify the evaluation metrics used, particularly the term "Acc," and provide a more detailed explanation of how it was calculated?
- How does the proposed method perform on other datasets, especially those with different characteristics than the one used in the study?
- Can the authors provide more detailed visualizations of the model's outputs to support the claims made about its performance?
- How does the method handle scenarios where the instructions are ambiguous or unclear, and what are the failure cases of the model?
- Could the authors discuss the limitations of the proposed method and how these could be addressed in future work?
- How does the method compare to other state-of-the-art models in terms of computational efficiency and scalability?
