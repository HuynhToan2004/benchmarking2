 **Summary:**
The paper introduces FARS, a method that integrates finite state machines (FSMs) with large language models (LLMs) to enhance API call generation. FARS utilizes a trie-based constrained decoding algorithm to manage the output of LLMs, ensuring that generated API calls align with a catalog of available APIs. This method is demonstrated to improve performance on various datasets, including SNIPS, MultiWOZ, and a Smart Home Control dataset, showing significant improvements over unconstrained LLMs. However, the method's generalization to unseen APIs and its dependency on a well-defined catalog are noted as limitations.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel approach to constraining the generation of LLMs using a finite state machine (FSM), which is a simple yet effective method for ensuring valid API calls.
- The proposed method, FARS, does not require additional training or fine-tuning of the LLM, making it a straightforward and efficient solution for API call generation.
- The paper is well-written, clear, and easy to follow, with a detailed explanation of the method and its implementation.
- The experiments conducted demonstrate that FARS outperforms unconstrained LLMs on various datasets, showing significant improvements in performance metrics.
- The integration of FSM with LLMs is a promising direction for enhancing the capabilities of LLMs in generating valid API calls, which is a significant challenge in many AI applications.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed discussion on the limitations of the proposed method, particularly in terms of its generalization to unseen APIs and its dependency on a well-defined catalog of APIs.
- The method's dependency on a finite state machine (FSM) may limit its ability to handle complex scenarios where the API calls are not predefined or where the FSM does not cover all possible scenarios.
- There is a concern about the scalability of the FSM approach, as the number of states and transitions could become very large, potentially affecting the performance and efficiency of the system.
- The paper does not include a comparison with other methods that use constrained decoding or other approaches to ensure valid API calls, which could provide a more comprehensive evaluation of the proposed method.
- The paper's evaluation is limited to a few datasets, which might not adequately demonstrate the method's effectiveness across a broader range of scenarios or domains.

**Questions:**
- Could you provide more details on how the FSM is constructed and how it is used to ensure valid API calls? Specifically, how is the FSM adapted to handle new or unseen APIs?
- How does the proposed method handle scenarios where the API calls are not predefined or where the FSM does not cover all possible scenarios?
- Is there a way to evaluate the method's performance in terms of the time taken for API call generation, especially in scenarios where the FSM might introduce additional latency?
- How does the method handle the inclusion of free text arguments in the API calls? Is there a mechanism to ensure that these arguments are valid and meaningful within the context of the API call?
- Could you discuss the potential limitations of the method, such as its dependency on a well-defined catalog of APIs and its scalability, and how these limitations might affect its practical application?
