 **Summary:**
The paper introduces TimeDiff, a novel method for generating synthetic EHR time series data using a combination of multinomial and Gaussian diffusion processes. This approach aims to address the challenges of generating both discrete and continuous data types in EHR, which is crucial for medical data analysis. The method is evaluated against several existing methods, demonstrating its effectiveness in generating diverse and realistic EHR data. Despite its strengths, the paper is criticized for its lack of novelty in the use of diffusion models, potential issues with data privacy, and the need for more comprehensive comparisons with state-of-the-art methods.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, making it easy to follow, and the experimental results are robust, showing the effectiveness of the proposed method.
- The proposed method, TimeDiff, is a simple yet effective approach for generating realistic EHR time series data, which is crucial for downstream analysis in healthcare.
- The paper introduces a novel diffusion-based method for generating realistic EHR time series data, which is a significant contribution to the field of medical data analysis.
- The method's performance is evaluated against several existing methods, demonstrating its effectiveness in generating diverse and realistic EHR data.
- The paper is well-organized, with a clear structure that makes it easy to understand the proposed method and its implementation.
- The experiments are well-designed, and the results are convincing, showing the superiority of the proposed method over existing methods.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed comparison with state-of-the-art methods in the field of EHR data generation, which could undermine the perceived effectiveness of the proposed method.
- The paper does not adequately address data privacy concerns, which is a critical issue in the generation of synthetic EHR data.
- The novelty of the method is questionable as it primarily combines existing diffusion models without significant innovation.
- The paper does not sufficiently discuss the limitations of the proposed method, which could help in understanding its applicability and potential drawbacks.
- The paper could benefit from more detailed experimental results, including the inclusion of more metrics and a more thorough comparison with existing methods.
- The method's performance in handling missing values is not adequately addressed, which is a significant issue in real-world EHR data.
- The paper does not discuss the potential limitations of the method, which could affect its applicability and effectiveness in real-world scenarios.
