 **Summary:**
The paper introduces EigenGuard, a novel defense mechanism against backdoor attacks in neural networks. It leverages the observation that backdoor features are concentrated in low-dimensional spaces, while natural features are spread across high-dimensional spaces. EigenGuard manipulates the singular values of the feature matrix to reduce the impact of backdoor features while preserving natural feature performance. The method is evaluated against various backdoor attacks and is shown to outperform existing defenses in terms of natural accuracy and resistance to backdoor attacks. However, the paper is criticized for its limited scope in terms of the range of attacks and datasets tested, and for potential over-reliance on specific network architectures.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written and easy to follow, with clear explanations of the proposed method.
- The experiments are comprehensive, covering a range of backdoor attacks and defenses, and are supported by visualizations that aid in understanding the method's effects.
- The proposed method, EigenGuard, is simple, effective, and does not require additional datasets or training procedures, making it practical for real-world applications.
- The paper provides theoretical analysis and ablation studies that support the effectiveness of the method, particularly in defending against backdoor attacks.
- The method is robust to different poison rates, which is crucial for practical applications.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a thorough analysis of the impact of EigenGuard on the performance of natural images, particularly in terms of potential degradation in natural accuracy.
- The method's effectiveness is limited to specific network architectures, such as ResNet-18, and its applicability to other architectures like VGG-16 and MobileNetV2 is not explored.
- The paper does not compare the proposed method with the latest backdoor attacks and defenses, which limits the evaluation of its effectiveness against the most current threats.
- There is a lack of discussion on the limitations of the proposed method, including its potential impact on the performance of natural images and its generalizability to different network architectures.
- The paper does not address the possibility of adaptive attacks, which could potentially bypass the defense mechanism.

**Questions:**
- How does the proposed method affect the performance of natural images, particularly in terms of natural accuracy?
- Can the method be adapted to other network architectures, such as VGG-16 and MobileNetV2?
- How does the method perform against the latest backdoor attacks and defenses?
- What are the potential limitations of the proposed method, and how can these be addressed in future research?
- Can the authors provide more details on the computational cost of the method compared to other defense methods?
- How does the method perform against adaptive attacks, and what are the potential countermeasures against such attacks?
