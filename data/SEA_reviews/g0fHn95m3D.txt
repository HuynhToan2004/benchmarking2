 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel approach, Text-To-Energy (T2E), which combines text-to-vector encoding and a multilayer perceptron (MLP) to predict material properties. This method converts material attributes into a vector representation and utilizes an MLP block to predict total energy, aiming to reduce computational burden and enhance prediction accuracy. The approach is validated against state-of-the-art methods such as Density Functional Theory (DFT), FermiNet, and PsiFormer, demonstrating superior performance. However, concerns are raised about the novelty of the approach, the clarity of the methodological explanation, and the adequacy of the experimental validation.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, with a clear and comprehensive introduction that effectively motivates the research.
- The proposed method, T2E, is innovative, combining text-to-vector encoding and a multilayer perceptron (MLP) to predict total energy, which is a significant advancement in the field of quantum chemistry.
- The paper provides a detailed explanation of the methodology, including the use of a text-to-vector encoder and an orbital-aware MLP, which enhances the understanding of the proposed approach.
- The results section is well-presented, with a clear comparison to state-of-the-art methods, and the paper includes comprehensive experimental validation and ablation studies.
- The paper addresses a crucial problem in the field of quantum chemistry, focusing on the reduction of computational burden and the requirement for substantial domain knowledge.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a thorough discussion on the limitations and potential negative societal impacts of the proposed method.
- There is a need for more detailed explanations and justifications for the choice of hyperparameters and the optimization algorithm used.
- The paper could benefit from a more comprehensive literature review, particularly in the area of quantum chemistry and machine learning.
- The novelty of the approach is questionable, as the use of text-to-vector encoding and MLPs is not new, and the method's comparison to existing methods like PsiFormer is insufficiently detailed.
- The experimental validation is limited, with a focus on small molecules and a lack of validation on larger, more complex systems.
- The paper contains several grammatical and spelling errors, which detract from its professionalism and clarity.

**Questions:**
- Can you clarify the process of generating simulated data using PySCF and how the material details are altered?
- How does the method handle the inclusion of spin in the model, and what specific impact does it have on the results?
- Could you provide more details on the experimental setup, particularly the training data and the choice of hyperparameters?
- How does the method compare to other state-of-the-art methods, such as FermiNet and PsiFormer, when evaluated on a broader range of molecules or systems?
- Could you elaborate on the role of the text encoder in the model and its contribution to the overall performance?
- What are the specific advantages of T2E over existing methods, particularly in terms of computational efficiency and accuracy?
