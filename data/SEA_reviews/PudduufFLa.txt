 **Summary:**
The paper explores the use of spherical harmonics and sinusoidal representation networks (SirenNets) for encoding geographic locations, focusing on the challenges of rectangular data domains in global data. It proposes a novel approach by combining spherical harmonic basis functions with SirenNets, aiming to enhance the representation of geographical data, particularly in polar regions where traditional methods struggle. The paper presents a comprehensive evaluation across various benchmarks, comparing the proposed method to existing approaches and demonstrating its competitive performance. However, it faces criticism for its limited novelty, as the use of spherical harmonics is well-established, and the paper does not sufficiently distinguish its contributions from existing works.

**Strengths:**
- The paper addresses a significant and timely topic, focusing on the importance of location encoding in global data, which is crucial for various applications such as remote sensing, ecology, and epidemiology.
- The proposed method using spherical harmonics and SirenNets is innovative and well-explained, providing a clear and concise presentation that is accessible to readers.
- The experimental results are comprehensive and demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed method across various benchmarks, highlighting its practical utility.
- The paper is well-written, with clear explanations and detailed experimental descriptions, which enhance its readability and reproducibility.
- The use of spherical harmonics and SirenNets is novel in the context of location encoding, offering a fresh perspective on how to represent geographical data.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper's novelty is somewhat limited as the use of spherical harmonics for encoding geographical locations is well-established in the literature.
- The experimental setup and results lack sufficient detail, making it difficult to fully understand the contributions of the proposed method.
- The paper could benefit from a more thorough discussion on the computational efficiency of spherical harmonics and how it compares to other methods.
- The paper does not sufficiently distinguish its contributions from existing works, particularly those by Mai et al. (2023), which might lead to confusion about the originality of the proposed method.
- The paper's scope is somewhat narrow, focusing primarily on the use of spherical harmonics and SirenNets without exploring other potential methods or broader applications of location encoding.
- There are minor presentation issues, such as inconsistencies in the use of symbols and equations, which could be improved for clarity and accuracy.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors provide a more detailed explanation of the experimental setup and results, particularly in terms of the methodology and the contributions of the proposed method?
- How does the computational efficiency of spherical harmonics compare to other methods, and what are the practical implications of this efficiency in real-world applications?
- Is there a possibility to extend the use of spherical harmonics and SirenNets to other domains beyond geographical data, such as image or audio processing?
- How do the authors address the potential issues of overfitting or underfitting in their models, particularly in the context of the ERA5 dataset?
- Could the authors clarify the role and impact of the "L" parameter in their model, and how it affects the performance and generalization of the model?
- Is there a plan to release the code and datasets used in the study to facilitate reproducibility and further research in this area?
