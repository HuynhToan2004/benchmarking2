 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel neural network architecture, PAC-FNO, designed to operate in the frequency domain, addressing image quality degradation issues such as resolution changes and natural variations in image recognition models. The PAC-FNO architecture, which includes an All-Component Fourier Neural Operator (AC-FNO) block without a low-pass filter, allows for the processing of high-frequency components in images, enhancing performance in low-resolution and input variation tasks. The paper evaluates PAC-FNO using seven image recognition benchmarks, demonstrating its effectiveness in handling multiple input variations at once and minimizing changes in downstream models during fine-tuning. Despite its promising results, the paper has been critiqued for its limited novelty and the lack of detailed comparisons with state-of-the-art methods.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel architecture, PAC-FNO, which operates in the frequency domain, addressing the issue of image quality degradation in real-world settings.
- The proposed AC-FNO block without a low-pass filter allows for the processing of high-frequency components in images, which is crucial for image recognition tasks.
- The PAC-FNO architecture, with its parallel structure and increased capacity, effectively handles multiple input variations at once, minimizing changes in the downstream model during fine-tuning.
- The paper is well-written, making it easy to follow, and includes comprehensive experimental results that demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed method.
- The method's ability to handle multiple input variations at once and minimize changes in the downstream model during fine-tuning is a significant advantage over existing methods.

**Weaknesses:**
- The novelty of the proposed method is limited as similar approaches have been previously explored, such as the use of Fourier neural operators in image classification tasks.
- The paper lacks a detailed comparison with state-of-the-art methods, particularly in terms of computational efficiency and performance under different conditions like low-resolution images with various types of degradations.
- The paper does not sufficiently discuss the limitations of the proposed method, which is crucial for understanding its applicability and potential drawbacks.
- The experimental setup and results are not convincingly superior to existing methods, with some results showing marginal improvements or even inferior performance in certain scenarios.
- The paper lacks a thorough discussion on the computational complexity of the proposed method, which is crucial for understanding its practical applicability.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors provide more detailed comparisons with state-of-the-art methods, particularly in terms of computational efficiency and performance under different conditions?
- How does the proposed method perform when faced with low-resolution images with various types of degradations, such as motion blur, noise, and weather changes?
- What are the specific advantages of the PAC-FNO architecture over other methods in terms of computational efficiency and performance?
- Could the authors clarify the role and impact of the zero-padding operation in the AC-FNO block?
- How does the two-stage training algorithm affect the overall performance and computational efficiency of the proposed method?
- Can the authors provide more detailed ablation studies to better understand the contributions of each component of the proposed method?
