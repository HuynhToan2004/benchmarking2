 **Summary:**
The paper introduces Synergistic Patch Pruning (STAR) for Vision Transformers (ViTs), focusing on the pruning of patch tokens in ViTs. STAR combines online evaluation of intra-layer importance for the CLS token and offline evaluation of inter-layer importance for each patch, which are then fused using a weighted average of Kullback-Leibler Divergences. The methodology aims to reduce computational costs by pruning patches based on their importance, demonstrated to be effective through experiments on the ImageNet dataset. However, the paper has been critiqued for its limited novelty, incremental contributions, and lack of detailed experimental validation, particularly concerning the generalization of the method across different datasets and models.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, making it easy to follow, and the proposed method is simple and effective.
- The method is well-motivated and the experiments are thorough, demonstrating the effectiveness of the proposed method.
- The paper addresses an important problem of efficient Transformer backbones, which is crucial for practical applications.
- The proposed method is simple and easy to implement, and the paper provides extensive experimental results to validate its effectiveness.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed discussion on the limitations and potential negative societal impacts of the proposed method.
- The novelty of the paper is limited, and the contributions are incremental compared to existing works.
- The methodology of the paper, particularly the use of LRP and the calculation of inter-layer importance, is not clearly explained, making it difficult for readers to understand the methodology fully.
- The paper does not adequately discuss the computational cost of the proposed method, which is crucial for understanding its practical applicability.
- The paper lacks a detailed comparison with other state-of-the-art methods, particularly in terms of computational efficiency and accuracy.
- The paper does not provide sufficient evidence to support the claim that the proposed method can be applied to other datasets or models beyond those tested.

**Questions:**
- How does the proposed method compare with other state-of-the-art methods in terms of computational efficiency and accuracy?
- Can the authors provide more details on the methodology, particularly how LRP is used to calculate inter-layer importance and how the fusion of intra- and inter-layer importance is achieved?
- How does the proposed method handle the computational cost, especially in terms of memory and time efficiency?
- Can the authors provide a more detailed discussion on the limitations and potential negative societal impacts of the proposed method?
- How does the proposed method perform on other datasets or models, and can the authors provide evidence to support its generalizability?
