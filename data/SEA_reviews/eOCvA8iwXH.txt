 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel approach called Neural Fourier Transform (NFT) for learning linear equivariant representations of data without requiring explicit knowledge of how the group acts on the data. This methodology extends the concept of Fourier transform to nonlinear, analytically intractable group actions, offering a new framework for equivariant representation learning. The authors provide theoretical insights and experimental validations, demonstrating the method's effectiveness in various scenarios, including unsupervised, group-supervised, and group-equivariant settings. The paper also discusses the limitations and potential future directions of this approach, including the need for further algorithmic guarantees and broader experimental validations.
