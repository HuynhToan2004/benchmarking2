 **Summary:**
The paper introduces TEMPO, a generative transformer designed for time series forecasting. TEMPO leverages a prompt pool and seasonal trend decomposition to enhance its performance, demonstrating superior results across various datasets. The model's innovative approach involves using a prompt pool to adapt to distribution shifts in non-stationary time series data, and it integrates trend, seasonality, and residual information to model time series patterns effectively. Extensive experiments and comparisons with existing models show that TEMPO outperforms state-of-the-art methods in forecasting accuracy and data efficiency. However, concerns about the novelty of the prompt pool and the clarity of some methodological descriptions were raised.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, easy to follow, and provides a clear motivation for using LLMs for time series forecasting.
- The proposed method, TEMPO, demonstrates strong performance in time series forecasting, showing improvements over state-of-the-art methods in forecasting accuracy and data efficiency.
- TEMPO integrates seasonal trend decomposition and a prompt pool, which allows the model to adapt to distribution shifts in non-stationary time series data, enhancing its performance and adaptability.
- The paper includes extensive experiments and comparisons with existing models, demonstrating the effectiveness of TEMPO in handling various datasets and scenarios.
- The methodology of TEMPO is interpretable, allowing for a better understanding of its decision-making processes, which is crucial for building trust and confidence in the model's predictions.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a thorough analysis of the prompt pool, particularly its selection and impact on performance, which is crucial for understanding the model's behavior and effectiveness.
- The novelty of the prompt pool is questionable, as similar methods have been explored in other LLMs. More detailed comparisons and justifications are needed to establish the unique contributions of TEMPO.
- The paper could benefit from more detailed discussions on the limitations of the model, such as its inability to handle multivariate time series forecasting and its dependency on the quality of the prompt pool.
- The paper does not provide sufficient details on the training and inference times of TEMPO compared to other methods, which could help in understanding the computational efficiency of the model.
- The paper contains several typographical errors and could benefit from better proofreading to ensure clarity and accuracy.

**Questions:**
- How does the selection of prompts from the prompt pool affect the performance of TEMPO? Can you provide more details on the criteria used for prompt selection and its impact on the model's predictions?
- Can you elaborate on the differences between TEMPO and other LLMs that use similar prompting techniques, particularly in terms of the unique contributions of TEMPO to the field of time series forecasting?
- How does TEMPO handle multivariate time series forecasting, and what are the implications of this limitation on the model's applicability in real-world scenarios?
- What are the specific advantages of using TEMPO over other state-of-the-art methods, particularly in terms of computational efficiency and model performance?
- Can you provide more details on the training and inference times of TEMPO compared to other methods, and how do these compare in terms of computational resources and efficiency?
