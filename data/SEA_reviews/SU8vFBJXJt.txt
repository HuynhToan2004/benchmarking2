 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a new loss function for policy evaluation in reinforcement learning, termed the "double Bellman" loss, which combines the Bellman error and projected Bellman error to improve the stability and efficiency of the Fitted Q-Evaluation (FQE) algorithm. This loss function is shown to be useful in the linear case, where it can be optimized to minimize the distance to the true Q function, and it is integrated with the Soft Actor Critic (SAC) algorithm to demonstrate improvements in sample efficiency in Mujoco tasks. The paper also discusses the theoretical underpinnings of the loss function and its practical implications, comparing it to existing methods like Bellman residual and projected Bellman error.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, with a clear and concise presentation that makes it easy to follow.
- The proposed loss function is novel and well-motivated, with a strong theoretical foundation that supports its use in policy evaluation.
- The experimental results are robust and demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed method, particularly in the Mujoco tasks.
- The paper provides a clear explanation of the theoretical underpinnings of the loss function, including the relationship between different types of errors and the implications for policy evaluation.
- The paper is original in its approach, combining existing ideas in a novel way to address a significant issue in reinforcement learning.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper could benefit from more comprehensive experimental validation, including a broader range of environments and a more rigorous comparison with existing methods.
- The theoretical analysis of the proposed method is not sufficiently robust, particularly in terms of its applicability beyond the linear case.
- The paper could be improved by providing more detailed explanations of certain equations and the implications of the theoretical results.
- The paper's experimental results are not consistently better than existing methods, and there is a lack of discussion on the significance of the observed improvements.
- The paper does not adequately address the limitations of its proposed method, which could be a significant drawback for practical applications.
