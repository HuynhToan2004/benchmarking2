 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel benchmark, MTOB, designed to evaluate the translation capabilities of large language models (LLMs) using a low-resource language, Kalamang, which has minimal web presence. The benchmark involves translating between Kalamang and English, leveraging a single grammar book as a reference. The authors demonstrate that while LLMs can utilize the grammar book to translate, their performance falls short of human capabilities. The paper includes a comprehensive evaluation of various LLMs under different conditions, highlighting the potential for improving LLM performance with better reference material and context. The study also discusses the ethical implications of using endangered languages in research and the need for more inclusive and diverse data in NLP research.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel benchmark, MTOB, which is the first of its kind to evaluate LLM performance on a language with minimal web presence, providing a unique and valuable contribution to the field.
- The paper is well-written, clear, and easy to follow, with a strong emphasis on ethical considerations, including the use of endangered languages and the need for diverse data in NLP research.
- The experimental design is robust, with a comprehensive evaluation of different LLMs under various conditions, demonstrating a thorough and rigorous approach.
- The paper addresses an important and timely issue of LLM performance on low-resource languages, which is crucial for advancing the field and ensuring that LLMs are effective across a wider range of languages.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper could benefit from a more detailed discussion on the choice of Kalamang as the benchmark language and how it compares to other low-resource languages in terms of complexity and availability of reference materials.
- There is a lack of clarity on the specific contributions of the paper and how they advance the state-of-the-art in LLM performance on low-resource languages.
- The paper does not provide a detailed analysis of the performance of different LLMs on the benchmark, which could help in understanding the factors influencing performance and identifying areas for improvement.
- The ethical considerations, while addressed, could be more thoroughly discussed, especially in relation to the use of endangered languages and the potential impact on their communities.

**Questions:**
- Could you provide more details on the choice of Kalamang as the benchmark language and how it compares to other low-resource languages in terms of complexity and availability of reference materials?
- How do you envision the MTOB benchmark being used in future research, and what specific insights do you hope it will provide?
- In your experiments, did you consider using more advanced techniques like GPT-4's chat mode for generating translations? How might this affect the results?
- How do you address the ethical concerns raised about the use of endangered languages in research, particularly in relation to the potential impact on their communities?
- Could you provide more details on the specific contributions of the paper and how they advance the state-of-the-art in LLM performance on low-resource languages?
