 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel noise-aware reweighting framework (NARF) for cross-domain recommendation from implicit feedback (CDRIF), addressing the challenges of implicit feedback's noise and the absence of negative signals in implicit feedback datasets. The framework is designed to calibrate training data and reduce noise dynamically, aiming to improve recommendation performance. The experiments, conducted on both synthetic and real-world datasets, demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed method, showing significant improvements over baseline methods. The paper is well-organized, with extensive experiments and ablation studies that validate the proposed framework's effectiveness. However, concerns were raised regarding the clarity of the methodology, the limited novelty of the proposed methods, and the lack of comparison with state-of-the-art methods.

**Strengths:**
- The paper addresses an important and relevant problem of cross-domain recommendation from implicit feedback, which is crucial in practical scenarios.
- The proposed noise-aware reweighting framework (NARF) is simple yet effective, with clear motivation and a well-designed framework that is easy to follow.
- Extensive experiments and ablation studies are conducted on both synthetic and real-world datasets, demonstrating the effectiveness of the proposed method.
- The paper is well-written, with clear presentation of the problem, motivation, method, and results, making it accessible and understandable.
- The methodology is straightforward and easy to implement, with a focus on reducing noise in implicit feedback, a common issue in recommendation systems.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks clarity in explaining the methodology, particularly in the descriptions of the calibration function and the dynamic noise reduction module.
- The novelty of the proposed method is limited, as the techniques used, such as reweighting and denoising, are not new.
- The comparison with state-of-the-art methods is insufficient, which could undermine the perceived effectiveness of the proposed method.
- The paper does not adequately discuss the limitations of the proposed method, which could help in understanding its applicability and effectiveness in different scenarios.
- The experiments are conducted only on small datasets, which may not fully demonstrate the scalability or effectiveness of the proposed method in larger, more complex datasets.
- The paper could benefit from more detailed explanations of the dataset and experimental setup, including the preprocessing and feature extraction methods used.

**Questions:**
- Could you provide more detailed explanations of the calibration function and the dynamic noise reduction module to enhance the clarity of the methodology?
- How does the proposed method compare with state-of-the-art methods, particularly in terms of performance and computational efficiency?
- Is there a possibility to conduct experiments on larger, more complex datasets to validate the scalability and effectiveness of the proposed method?
- How does the proposed method handle the issue of missing negative signals in implicit feedback datasets, and what are the implications of this for the performance of the recommendation system?
- Can you discuss the limitations of the proposed method and provide insights into how these limitations might affect its applicability and effectiveness in different scenarios?
