 **Summary:**
The paper introduces SARI, a novel framework for noisy partial label learning (NPLL) that combines elements of average-based and identification-based methods. SARI employs a weighted k-nearest neighbor algorithm for pseudo-labeling, followed by a noise-robust classifier training using label smoothing, consistency regularization, and mix-up. The framework is evaluated on various datasets and shows promising results, outperforming existing methods in certain scenarios. The paper also includes ablation studies to validate the effectiveness of its components. Despite its strengths, concerns are raised about the novelty of the approach, the complexity of the methodology, and the clarity of presentation.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel framework called SARI, which combines the strengths of both average-based and identification-based methodologies.
- The methodology is well-organized and easy to follow, with a clear and intuitive presentation that enhances understanding.
- The paper conducts extensive experiments on multiple datasets, demonstrating the effectiveness of the proposed method.
- SARI achieves state-of-the-art results in all studied settings, showing significant improvements over existing methods in fine-grained classification and extreme noise settings.
- The paper provides a thorough ablation study to support the design choices and demonstrate the efficacy of the approach.
- The authors have committed to releasing the code and datasets upon acceptance, enhancing the reproducibility and transparency of the research.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed discussion on the limitations of the proposed method, which could provide a more balanced perspective.
- There is a noticeable absence of comparisons with some state-of-the-art methods, such as those mentioned in the related work section, which could affect the perceived novelty and effectiveness of the proposed method.
- The paper does not provide a detailed analysis of the computational complexity of the proposed method, which could be crucial for practical applications.
- The paper lacks a clear motivation for the specific approach chosen and does not adequately compare its methodology with existing approaches, which could undermine the perceived contribution of the research.
- The paper does not provide a detailed explanation of the experimental setup, including the dataset generation process and the choice of baselines, which could affect the validity and reliability of the results.
- There are several typographical and formatting errors that need to be corrected to improve the readability and professionalism of the paper.

**Questions:**
- How does the proposed method perform on datasets with more complex structures, such as those involving hierarchical or nested labels?
- Could the authors provide more detailed comparisons with state-of-the-art methods, including those mentioned in the related work section, to validate the effectiveness of the proposed method?
- How does the computational complexity of the proposed method compare with existing methods, and what are the practical implications of this complexity?
- Can the authors provide a more detailed explanation of the experimental setup, including the dataset generation process and the choice of baselines, to enhance the transparency and reproducibility of the research?
- How does the proposed method handle different types of noise, and what are the specific strategies employed to mitigate the effects of noise on the learning process?
