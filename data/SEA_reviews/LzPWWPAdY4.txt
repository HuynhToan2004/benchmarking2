 **Summary:**
The paper introduces LoftQ, a novel quantization framework for large language models (LLMs) that integrates quantization and low-rank approximation for LoRA fine-tuning. This method addresses the discrepancy between quantized and full-precision models by optimizing the initialization of low-rank adapters. The proposed method is evaluated across various natural language understanding, question answering, summarization, and natural language generation tasks, showing promising results compared to existing methods like QLoRA. The paper is well-written, easy to follow, and includes extensive experiments and ablation studies. However, the novelty of the approach is questioned, as it primarily combines existing techniques without significant innovation.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, easy to follow, and includes extensive experiments and ablation studies.
- The proposed method is straightforward and easy to implement, with a focus on the low-bit regime where QLoRA performs poorly.
- The method shows promising results compared to QLoRA, with a significant improvement in the 2-bit quantization regime.
- The paper is well-organized, with clear explanations of the method and its implementation.
- The authors provide a thorough analysis of the results, including detailed discussions on the effectiveness of the alternating optimization process.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a clear motivation for the proposed method, which is a combination of existing techniques without significant innovation.
- The experimental results are not convincing, with inconsistencies in performance improvements across different tasks and datasets.
- The paper does not sufficiently compare the proposed method with other quantization methods like QAT, PTQ, or LoRA-based methods, which limits the understanding of its effectiveness.
- The paper does not discuss the computational cost or the practical implications of using LoftQ in real-world applications.
- There is a lack of discussion on the limitations of the proposed method, which is crucial for understanding its applicability and effectiveness in different scenarios.

**Questions:**
- How does the proposed method compare with other quantization methods like QAT, PTQ, and LoRA-based methods?
- Can the authors provide more detailed comparisons with other methods, especially in terms of computational cost and practical implications?
- Why does LoftQ perform better than LoRA in some cases, and what are the specific conditions under which this occurs?
- How does the proposed method address the issue of overfitting, and what are the implications of this for the generalization of the models?
- The paper mentions that LoftQ is more stable than QLoRA in the low-bit regime. Can the authors provide more details on this, possibly including visual representations or additional data points?
- Could the authors clarify the computational cost of LoftQ and discuss its practical implications in real-world applications?
