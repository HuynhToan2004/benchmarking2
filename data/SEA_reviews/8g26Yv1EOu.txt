 **Summary:**
The paper introduces the Amortized Network Intervention (ANI) framework, aimed at managing networked temporal dynamics by intervening in the influence matrix between nodes. The ANI framework utilizes a neural ODE model to predict the evolution of the network and employs a gradient-descent based Model Predictive Control (MPC) algorithm to optimize policy decisions. This framework is designed to generalize across different network structures and is evaluated on both synthetic and real-world datasets, including COVID-19 and traffic data. The paper explores the use of permutation equivalent embeddings to enhance generalization and proposes a novel metric to aid in learning these embeddings. Despite its innovative approach, the paper has been critiqued for its lack of clarity in presentation and the absence of detailed experimental results, which hampers its ability to convincingly demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed methods.

**Strengths:**
- The paper addresses a significant and timely problem of managing networked temporal dynamics, which has real-world applications in areas like traffic management and epidemic control.
- The proposed method is innovative, utilizing a neural ODE model to capture the dynamics of the network, which is a novel approach in this context.
- The use of permutation equivalent embeddings to enhance generalization is a novel and interesting idea, which could potentially improve the applicability of the model across different network structures.
- The paper is well-written and easy to follow, with clear explanations of the methodology and its application.
- The experiments conducted are comprehensive, including both synthetic and real-world data, which helps to validate the effectiveness of the proposed method.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks clarity in some sections, particularly in the explanation of the neural ODE model and the role of the influence matrix in the model.
- There is a lack of detailed experimental results, such as the absence of error bars in figures and the omission of standard deviations in tables, which could affect the reliability of the results.
- The paper does not adequately compare its method with existing works, particularly those that use similar approaches or address similar problems.
- The paper does not discuss the limitations of its method, which could provide valuable insights into its applicability and potential drawbacks.
- The paper lacks a detailed discussion on the computational complexity and scalability of the proposed method, which is crucial for understanding its practical implementation in large-scale real-world applications.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors provide more details on the neural ODE model, particularly how it is used to capture the dynamics of the network and how it differs from traditional ODE models?
- How is the influence matrix constructed, and what role does it play in the model's predictions and policy decisions?
- Can the authors clarify the role of the permutation equivalent embedding in the model's performance and how it contributes to the model's generalization capabilities?
- How does the paper address the issue of partial observability in its model, and what steps are taken to ensure the model's effectiveness in real-world applications where data might be incomplete or noisy?
- Could the authors provide more detailed experimental results, including error bars and standard deviations, to support the claims made about the model's effectiveness?
- How does the paper address the issue of computational complexity and scalability in large-scale real-world applications, and what are the potential limitations of the proposed method in such scenarios?
