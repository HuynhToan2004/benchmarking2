 **Summary:**
The paper introduces CoELA, a cognitive-inspired modular framework integrating Large Language Models (LLMs) into embodied agents for cooperative tasks. The framework consists of five modules: Perception, Memory, Communication, Planning, and Execution. CoELA's performance is demonstrated in two embodied environments, showing that it surpasses strong planning-based methods and can communicate effectively with humans. The paper also discusses the challenges of integrating LLMs into embodied agents, including the limitations of LLMs in handling spatial information and low-level actions. Despite these challenges, CoELA's ability to plan, communicate, and cooperate efficiently with other agents and humans is highlighted.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-organized, clearly written, and easy to follow, making it accessible to a broad audience.
- The integration of LLMs into embodied agents is a promising approach, particularly in enhancing communication and cooperation between agents.
- The experimental results demonstrate the effectiveness of CoELA in surpassing strong planning-based methods, showing a significant improvement in cooperation and communication.
- The user study conducted on C-WAH provides valuable insights into how CoELA performs in real-world scenarios, highlighting its potential for practical applications.
- The paper addresses an important research question about the integration of LLMs into embodied agents, which is crucial for advancing the field of AI.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed discussion on the limitations of LLMs and how these limitations affect the performance of CoELA. This could include a deeper exploration of the challenges in handling spatial information and low-level actions.
- The paper does not sufficiently discuss the scalability of CoELA, especially in more complex or larger-scale environments.
- There is a lack of comparative analysis with other state-of-the-art methods, which could provide a better understanding of CoELA's performance relative to existing solutions.
- The paper does not adequately address the generalizability of CoELA to different environments or the robustness of its performance under varying conditions.
- The paper's reliance on LLMs for planning and communication raises concerns about the agent's ability to operate effectively without LLMs, particularly in scenarios where LLMs fail or are unavailable.

**Questions:**
- How does CoELA handle the limitations of LLMs, particularly in terms of spatial reasoning and low-level actions?
- Can you provide more details on how CoELA's performance scales with the complexity of the environment?
- How does CoELA compare to other state-of-the-art methods in terms of efficiency and effectiveness?
- What are the specific challenges encountered when integrating LLMs into embodied agents, and how do these challenges impact the performance of CoELA?
- How does CoELA adapt to new environments or scenarios, and what are the implications of this adaptability for its overall performance?
