 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel approach to contrastive learning by incorporating a zero-mean regularization term into the spectral contrastive loss. This modification aims to enhance the performance and robustness of the model by relaxing the orthogonality constraint between negative pairs, thereby reducing the impact of incorrect connections. The theoretical analysis provided supports the effectiveness of this approach in both unsupervised and supervised scenarios, including unsupervised domain adaptation and learning with noisy labels. The paper also includes experimental validations, demonstrating the method's superiority over the original spectral contrastive loss in various tasks. However, the paper is criticized for its limited novelty and the lack of comprehensive experimental evaluations, particularly in more complex and realistic settings.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written and easy to follow, with a clear and concise introduction to the proposed method.
- The authors provide a theoretical analysis that supports the effectiveness of the proposed zero-mean regularization term, which is intuitive and well-explained.
- The experimental results demonstrate the superiority of the proposed method over the original spectral contrastive loss, with improvements shown across various tasks.
- The paper is well-organized, making it easy for readers to understand the content and the proposed methodology.
- The theoretical analysis is solid, with proofs provided for the proposed method, enhancing the credibility of the research.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a thorough comparison with other state-of-the-art contrastive learning methods, which could have validated the effectiveness of the proposed method more robustly.
- The experiments are limited in scope, focusing mainly on small datasets like CIFAR-10/100 and SVHN, and do not include larger or more complex datasets like ImageNet.
- The paper does not include a comprehensive analysis of the impact of the regularization strength on the performance, which could provide valuable insights into the optimal settings for different scenarios.
- The novelty of the paper is questioned, as the proposed method is seen as a simple modification of the existing spectral contrastive loss without significant innovation.
- The paper does not adequately address the computational efficiency of the proposed method, which is crucial for practical applications.
- The paper lacks a detailed discussion on the limitations of the proposed method, which could help in understanding its applicability and potential drawbacks.

**Questions:**
- Could you provide a more detailed comparison with other state-of-the-art contrastive learning methods to validate the effectiveness of the proposed method more robustly?
- How does the proposed method perform on larger and more complex datasets like ImageNet?
- Can you include a more detailed analysis of the impact of the regularization strength on the performance, possibly including a set of ablation studies?
- How does the proposed method compare in terms of computational efficiency with other contrastive learning methods?
- Could you discuss the limitations of the proposed method in more detail, including potential scenarios where it may not be effective or may introduce new challenges?
- Could you clarify the motivation behind the choice of the regularization term and how it impacts the performance of the model?
