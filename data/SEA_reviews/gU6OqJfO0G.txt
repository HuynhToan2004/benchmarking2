 **Summary:**
The paper investigates the learnability of node-wise graph incremental learning (NGIL) under structural shifts, focusing on the impact of these shifts on model performance. It introduces a novel experience replay method, SEA-ER, which leverages topological awareness and importance reweighting to mitigate catastrophic forgetting and structural shifts. Theoretical insights are provided on the learnability of NGIL, showing that it may not be always learnable when structural shifts are uncontrolled. Empirical results demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed method, validating the theoretical findings. The paper is well-written, clearly presenting the problem and its theoretical contributions, although it lacks a detailed discussion on the limitations and broader societal impacts.

**Strengths:**
- The paper provides a novel theoretical analysis of node-wise graph incremental learning (NGIL), focusing on the impact of structural shifts and their implications for model performance.
- The introduction of the SEA-ER method, which combines topological awareness and importance reweighting, offers a novel approach to mitigate catastrophic forgetting and structural shifts in NGIL.
- The paper is well-written, clearly presenting the problem and its theoretical contributions, making it accessible and understandable.
- Empirical results demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed method, validating the theoretical findings and showing the practical applicability of the approach.
- The paper introduces an impossibility result regarding the learnability of the NGIL problem, providing a theoretical foundation for understanding the challenges and limitations of this approach.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed discussion on the limitations of the proposed approach, particularly in terms of its practical applicability and scalability.
- There is a lack of discussion on the broader societal impacts of the proposed method, which could be significant in real-world applications.
