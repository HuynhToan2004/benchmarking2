 **Summary:**
The paper introduces RTMPose, a novel real-time multi-person pose estimation pipeline designed to balance performance and efficiency. RTMPose utilizes a top-down approach with a CSPNeXt backbone, SimCC for keypoint prediction, and a self-attention module for global and local spatial information. The model is optimized for deployment on various devices, achieving competitive performance on benchmarks like COCO and AP-100, while maintaining high inference speeds. The paper also discusses the impact of various design choices, such as the use of a SimCC-based architecture and a self-attention module, on the model's performance and efficiency.

**Strengths:**
- The paper provides a comprehensive analysis of various factors affecting the performance and latency of 2D multi-person pose estimation pipelines, including paradigm, backbone network, localization method, training strategy, and deployment.
- The proposed RTMPose achieves good performance on benchmarks such as COCO and AP-100, while maintaining high inference speeds, making it suitable for real-time applications.
- The paper is well-written, making it easy to follow, and the experiments are thorough, comparing the proposed method against other state-of-the-art methods.
- The authors have optimized the entire top-down inference pipeline to improve speed and reliability.
- The paper is original in its approach to real-time multi-person pose estimation, which is a significant challenge in the field.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper does not clearly distinguish its main contributions from existing methods, such as SimCC, which may lead to confusion about the novelty of the proposed model.
- The paper lacks a detailed discussion on the limitations and failure cases of the proposed method, which could help in understanding its practical applicability and reliability.
- The paper could benefit from a more detailed discussion on the impact of different training strategies on the performance of the model.
- There are inconsistencies in the results reported, such as the performance of RTMPose-m being lower than RTMPose-s, which is not explained.
- The paper does not include a comparison with some of the latest state-of-the-art methods, which could affect the perceived novelty and effectiveness of the proposed model.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors clarify the main contributions of the paper and how they differ from existing methods like SimCC?
- How does the proposed method handle different training strategies, and what are the specific impacts on the model's performance?
- Can the authors provide a detailed comparison with the latest state-of-the-art methods to demonstrate the effectiveness and novelty of their approach?
- What are the specific failure cases and limitations of the proposed method, and how do these impact the practical applicability of the model?
- Could the authors explain the inconsistencies in the results reported, particularly the lower performance of RTMPose-m compared to RTMPose-s?
