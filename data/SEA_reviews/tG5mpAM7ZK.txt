 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel approach to domain adaptation by leveraging text descriptions of unseen domains to augment visual data in the CLIP embedding space, aiming to enhance generalization capabilities. The proposed method, TEAM, utilizes a training-free augmentation technique that aligns modality directions, which is contrasted with the traditional global direction alignment used in previous methods. The paper presents theoretical analysis and empirical evidence to support the effectiveness of the proposed method, showing improvements over existing methods in domain generalization tasks. However, the paper's scope is limited to specific domains, and the method's performance is not consistently superior across all datasets.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, easy to follow, and the figures are clear, enhancing the reader's understanding of the content.
- The proposed method is novel, well-motivated, and theoretically grounded, providing a solid theoretical foundation for the approach.
- The experimental results demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed method, showing improvements over existing methods in domain generalization tasks.
- The paper introduces a training-free augmentation method, which is both time-efficient and more accurate compared to existing methods.
- The authors provide a thorough analysis of the modality gap in multimodal embedding space, which is both interesting and informative.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper's methodology heavily relies on the quality of the CLIP model, which may limit its applicability if the model does not accurately embed complex semantics from intricate descriptions.
- The performance of the proposed method is not consistently superior across all datasets, particularly on the CUB-Paintings dataset, where it underperforms compared to the LADS method.
- The paper does not sufficiently discuss the limitations of the proposed method, such as its reliance on the accuracy of the CLIP model and the similarity between crafted unseen domains and test domains.
- The paper's experimental results are not consistently superior to existing methods, and it lacks a broader range of baseline comparisons, including recent domain generalization methods.
- The paper's evaluation is limited to specific datasets, which may not fully demonstrate the generalizability of the proposed method.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors provide a more detailed discussion on the limitations of the proposed method, particularly its reliance on the accuracy of the CLIP model and the similarity between crafted unseen domains and test domains?
- How does the proposed method perform when the text descriptions of the test domain are not available, as is often the case in real-world scenarios?
- Can the authors elaborate on the choice of using a mean pooling method for extracting domain-invariant text representations and discuss potential improvements or alternatives?
- Why does the proposed method underperform on the CUB-Paintings dataset compared to the LADS method?
- Could the authors provide a more comprehensive comparison with recent domain generalization methods, including a broader range of baselines?
- The paper mentions a time-efficiency advantage; could the authors provide a more detailed comparison of training times to substantiate this claim?
