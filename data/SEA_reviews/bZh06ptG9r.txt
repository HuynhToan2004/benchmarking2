 **Summary:**
The paper introduces FedLoRA, a novel approach to personalized federated learning (PFL) inspired by the LoRA method from large language models. FedLoRA decomposes model parameters into shared full-rank matrices and personalized low-rank matrices, aiming to capture both general and client-specific knowledge. It employs a training strategy that prioritizes the training of the low-rank matrix to mitigate the effects of data heterogeneity. The method's effectiveness is evaluated across multiple datasets, showing significant improvements over existing methods. However, concerns are raised about the novelty of the method, as it primarily applies LoRA to PFL without significant modifications, and the experiments are limited to small-scale datasets.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-organized, easy to follow, and clearly explains the proposed method.
- The experimental results demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed method.
- The paper addresses a significant problem in personalized federated learning (PFL) by decomposing parameters into shared and personalized parts, which is a novel approach.
- The paper provides a thorough analysis of the proposed method, including an ablation study and comparisons with other methods.
- The motivation behind the research is well-articulated, and the paper is well-written, making it accessible and understandable.

**Weaknesses:**
- The novelty of the paper is limited as it primarily applies the LoRA method from large language models to PFL without significant modifications.
- The experiments are limited to small-scale datasets, which may not adequately represent the performance of the method on larger, more complex datasets.
- The paper lacks a detailed discussion on the hyperparameters and their impact on the performance of the method.
- The paper does not provide sufficient theoretical analysis to support the proposed method, which could strengthen the paper's argument.
- The paper does not compare the proposed method with the latest state-of-the-art methods, which could provide a more comprehensive evaluation of the method's effectiveness.
- The paper does not discuss the potential negative societal impacts of the proposed method, which is a critical aspect of ethical considerations in AI research.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors provide more details on how the hyperparameters were chosen and their impact on the performance of the method?
- How does the proposed method perform on larger, more complex datasets, and what are the potential limitations or challenges in scaling the method to such datasets?
- Could the authors provide a more detailed comparison with the latest state-of-the-art methods to demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed method?
- How does the proposed method handle the problem of data heterogeneity in PFL, and what are the potential limitations or challenges in applying the method to different types of data?
- Can the authors provide a theoretical analysis to support the proposed method, and how does it compare to other existing methods in terms of performance and efficiency?
