 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel asynchronous federated learning algorithm, CA2FL, which addresses the convergence issues in federated learning by caching updates on the server and reusing them for global update calibration. The algorithm aims to mitigate the negative effects of asynchronous delays and data heterogeneity on convergence rates. Theoretical analysis and empirical results are provided to support the claims of improved convergence and robustness against data heterogeneity. The paper also discusses the implications of these findings in practical scenarios, though it faces criticism for its limited novelty and the need for more comprehensive empirical validation.

**Strengths:**
- The paper provides a comprehensive theoretical analysis of the convergence of the proposed algorithm, which is supported by empirical results showing the algorithm's superior performance.
- The proposed method is simple and easy to implement, making it accessible for practical applications.
- The paper addresses an important problem in federated learning, focusing on the convergence rate in the presence of asynchronous delays and data heterogeneity.
- The authors have made efforts to improve the convergence rate by removing the O(K τmax τavg σg2/T) term, which is a significant contribution to the field.
- The paper is well-written, with clear presentation of the problem and proposed solution, and the experimental setup is well-described, including a detailed comparison with existing methods.

**Weaknesses:**
- The novelty of the proposed method is limited as it mainly builds upon existing asynchronous federated learning algorithms like FedBuff, with minor modifications that do not significantly advance the state of the art.
- The paper's empirical results are not convincing due to the lack of comprehensive experiments on a variety of datasets and models, and the absence of comparisons with more recent and relevant baselines.
- There are several typos and grammatical errors throughout the paper, which detract from its professional quality.
- The theoretical analysis relies on strong assumptions that may not hold in practical scenarios, such as the bounded variance of stochastic gradients and the bounded gradient delay.
- The paper does not adequately discuss the limitations of the proposed method, particularly concerning its applicability in scenarios with large-scale datasets and its scalability to distributed settings.

**Questions:**
- How does the proposed method compare with other recent asynchronous federated learning algorithms, particularly those that have shown improved convergence rates?
- Can the authors provide more detailed experimental results, including comparisons with more recent and relevant baselines, to better demonstrate the effectiveness of their method?
- How does the proposed method handle data heterogeneity beyond the simple partitioning used in the experiments?
- Is there a theoretical guarantee that the proposed method can converge to the optimal solution in the case of data heterogeneity?
- Could the authors clarify the assumptions made in the theoretical analysis and discuss how these assumptions affect the practical applicability of the method?
- How does the proposed method scale to distributed settings, and what are the potential challenges in implementing it in such environments?
