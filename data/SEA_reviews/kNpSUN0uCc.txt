 **Summary:**
The paper introduces MaxEnt Model Correction (MaxEnt MoCo) and its variants, Model Correcting Value Iteration (MoCoVI) and Model Correcting Dyna (MoCoDyna), aimed at improving the performance of model-based reinforcement learning (MBRL) algorithms by reducing the impact of model errors. The authors employ maximum entropy density estimation to correct the model's next-state distribution, enhancing the accuracy of the estimated value function. Theoretical analysis is provided to demonstrate the convergence of these algorithms, and empirical results are presented to validate their effectiveness. The paper discusses the practical implications and potential extensions of these methods, suggesting their applicability to various reinforcement learning scenarios.

**Strengths:**
- The paper presents a novel approach using MaxEnt Model Correction (MaxEnt MoCo) to reduce the impact of model errors in model-based reinforcement learning (MBRL), which is a significant contribution to the field.
- The theoretical analysis provided is comprehensive, covering both finite and continuous MDPs and the approximate versions of the algorithms, enhancing the understanding of the proposed methods.
- The paper is well-structured and easy to follow, making it accessible to a broad audience, including both theoretical and practical aspects of MBRL.
- The empirical results, while not extensive, support the theoretical claims and demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed methods in practical scenarios.
- The paper includes a discussion on the practical implications of the proposed methods, suggesting their applicability to various reinforcement learning scenarios.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a thorough discussion on the limitations of the proposed methods, which could help in understanding the scenarios where these methods might not be effective.
- The empirical results presented are somewhat limited, and it would be beneficial to include more extensive experiments to validate the claims more robustly.
- The paper could benefit from a more detailed discussion on how the methods compare to other existing approaches in MBRL, particularly in terms of computational efficiency and practical applicability.
- There is a need for clearer definitions and explanations of certain key terms and concepts used in the paper, such as "model error" and the "basis functions" used in the MaxEnt MoCo algorithm.
- The paper could be improved by including more detailed examples or case studies that illustrate the practical implementation and effectiveness of the proposed methods in specific scenarios.

**Questions:**
- How do the proposed methods compare to other existing approaches in MBRL, particularly in terms of computational efficiency and practical applicability?
- Could the authors provide more detailed examples or case studies that illustrate the practical implementation and effectiveness of the proposed methods in specific scenarios?
- How does the choice of basis functions affect the performance of the MaxEnt MoCo algorithm, and are there any guidelines or best practices for selecting these functions?
- Can the authors clarify the definition and role of "model error" in the context of their proposed methods, and how it is quantified and controlled?
- How does the paper address the potential overfitting issues mentioned in Section 3.2, and what measures are in place to prevent this?
- Could the authors provide more details on the experimental setup, including the specific environments used and the parameters of the algorithms tested?
