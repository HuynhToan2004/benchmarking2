 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel algorithm called PROSPECT, designed to address distributionally robust optimization (DRO) problems, specifically those involving spectral risk measures (SRMs). PROSPECT is a stochastic gradient-based method that requires tuning only a single learning rate, offering linear convergence guarantees for smooth regularized losses. The algorithm is tested across various benchmarks, demonstrating superior performance compared to existing methods. The paper provides theoretical justifications for the algorithm's effectiveness and empirical evidence of its superiority, highlighting its potential in handling distribution shifts and fairness in machine learning applications.

**Strengths:**
- The paper presents a novel algorithm, PROSPECT, which is a stochastic gradient-based method for distributionally robust optimization (DRO) that only requires tuning a single learning rate, making it practical and efficient.
- The authors provide both theoretical justification and empirical evidence to support the effectiveness of the proposed algorithm, which is tested on various benchmarks and shown to outperform existing methods.
- The paper is well-written, clear, and easy to follow, with a detailed analysis of the algorithm's convergence and practical implications.
- The algorithm's design is innovative, integrating bias and variance reduction techniques, which are crucial for handling distribution shifts and fairness in machine learning applications.
- The paper addresses a significant problem in machine learning, specifically the handling of distribution shifts and fairness, which are crucial for real-world applications.
