 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel batch normalization method for Spiking Neural Networks (SNNs) named Temporal Accumulated Batch Normalization (TAB). This method aims to address the challenges of temporal covariate shifts in SNNs by accumulating batch statistics across time steps, thereby improving the performance of SNNs in various datasets like CIFAR-10, CIFAR-100, and DVS-CIFAR10. The TAB method is claimed to be more effective than existing batch normalization methods, particularly in handling the temporal dependencies in SNNs. The paper also provides a theoretical connection between the TAB method and neuron dynamics, which is supported by experimental results. However, concerns were raised about the novelty of the method, the clarity of the theoretical connections, and the fairness of the experimental comparisons.

**Strengths:**
- The paper addresses a significant issue in Spiking Neural Networks (SNNs) by focusing on the temporal covariate shift, which is a critical challenge in training SNNs.
- The proposed Temporal Accumulated Batch Normalization (TAB) method is novel and provides a natural connection between neuron dynamics and batch normalization, enhancing the performance of SNNs.
- The paper is well-structured, making it easy to follow, and provides a clear theoretical explanation of the TAB method and its relationship to neuron dynamics.
- Extensive experiments are conducted to validate the effectiveness of the TAB method, showing improvements over existing methods in various datasets.
- The paper is well-written, with clear figures and tables that aid in understanding the proposed method and its results.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper does not sufficiently differentiate its method from existing approaches like TEBN, particularly in the accumulation of batch statistics, which may not be novel.
- The theoretical connection between the TAB method and neuron dynamics is not clearly explained, and the practical implications of this connection are not convincingly demonstrated.
- The experimental comparisons may not be entirely fair due to the use of different time steps for training, which could favor the TAB method.
- The paper does not include a discussion on the limitations of the proposed method, which is crucial for understanding its applicability and potential drawbacks.
- The paper lacks a discussion on the computational complexity of the TAB method, which is essential for understanding its scalability and efficiency.
- The paper does not include comparisons with recent state-of-the-art methods, which could provide a more comprehensive evaluation of the TAB method's effectiveness.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors clarify how the TAB method differentiates from existing approaches like TEBN, especially in the accumulation of batch statistics?
- How does the TAB method improve the theoretical connection with neuron dynamics, and what are the practical implications of this connection?
- Could the authors provide more details on the computational complexity of the TAB method and its scalability?
- In the experimental comparisons, how were the time steps chosen for each method, and what were the specific training parameters used?
- Can the authors discuss the limitations of the proposed method and provide suggestions for future research?
- Could the authors include comparisons with recent state-of-the-art methods to provide a more comprehensive evaluation of the TAB method's effectiveness?
