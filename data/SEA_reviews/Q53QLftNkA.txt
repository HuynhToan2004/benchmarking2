 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel Multi-Window Multi-Head Attention (MW-MHA) module for Multi-Window Masked Autoencoders (MW-MAE), designed to enhance audio representation learning by facilitating local-global interactions in every decoder transformer block. This approach allows for the modeling of both local and global interactions in the audio domain, which is crucial for audio representation learning. The proposed methodology has been empirically validated on ten downstream audio tasks, demonstrating consistent performance improvements over standard MAEs. The paper also includes a detailed analysis of attention head feature representations, which provides insights into the effectiveness of the proposed method.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written and easy to follow, making it accessible to a broad audience.
- The proposed Multi-Window Multi-Head Attention (MW-MHA) is a novel approach that effectively models both local and global interactions in audio representation learning.
- The method is empirically validated on ten downstream audio tasks, demonstrating consistent performance improvements over standard MAEs.
- The paper includes a detailed analysis of attention head feature representations, providing insights into the effectiveness of the proposed method.
- The experiments conducted are extensive, and the results are convincing, showing the effectiveness of the proposed method.
- The paper includes an ablation study that shows the importance of the proposed module, and the analysis of attention head feature representations provides insights into the effectiveness of the proposed method.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed discussion on the design of the Multi-Window Multi-Head Attention (MW-MHA) module, which is a crucial aspect of the proposed method.
- There is no comparison with other self-supervised learning methods for audio representation learning, which could provide a more comprehensive evaluation of the proposed method.
- The paper does not include experiments on larger datasets, which could demonstrate the scalability of the proposed method.
- The paper does not discuss the limitations of the proposed method, which is essential for understanding its applicability and potential drawbacks.
- The paper lacks a comparison with other methods like BEATS-iter3, which could provide a more robust evaluation of the proposed method.
- The paper does not include experiments on the AudioSet-20k balanced subset, which could provide insights into the effectiveness of the proposed method in more challenging scenarios.
- The paper does not include an analysis of the training time for the proposed method, which is crucial for understanding its practical applicability.
