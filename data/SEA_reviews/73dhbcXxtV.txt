 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel framework called LoLaMeMe, designed to evaluate different aspects of natural language using a mechanistic approach. This framework incorporates elements such as logic, memory, and nuances of language, and is used to test the performance of various language models, including GPT-2 and Hyena, on tasks involving memory and reasoning. The framework is tested using two instantiations, LoLa and MeMe, and the results suggest that the hybrid architecture T Hex, which combines GPT-2 and Hyena blocks, outperforms both models on most tasks. However, the paper's experimental design and the clarity of its contributions are criticized, with concerns about the novelty of the framework and the lack of comprehensive comparisons with existing methods.
