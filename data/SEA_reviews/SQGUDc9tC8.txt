 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel approach to identifying and mitigating social biases in pre-trained language models (PLMs) by proposing Integrated Gap Gradients (IG2) and Bias Neuron Suppression (BNS). IG2 pinpoints neurons responsible for social biases, and BNS suppresses their activation to mitigate biases. The methodology involves a new dataset and a training-free method that can be applied to various PLMs like BERT and RoBERTa. The paper evaluates the effectiveness of the proposed methods using metrics such as Stereotype Score, Language Modeling Score, and ICAT, showing that BNS outperforms existing methods in fairness and language modeling abilities. Despite its innovative approach and empirical validation, the paper is criticized for its limited evaluation scope, lack of comparative analysis with other debiasing methods, and potential over-simplification of the mitigation approach.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel approach to debiasing language models by identifying and suppressing social bias neurons, which is a significant advancement in the field of fairness research.
- The proposed Integrated Gap Gradients (IG2) method and Bias Neuron Suppression (BNS) are well-motivated, technically sound, and have been validated through extensive experiments.
- The paper is well-written, clearly articulating the proposed methodologies and their implications, and includes a comprehensive set of experiments and analyses that support the proposed method's effectiveness.
- The dataset and code used in the research are publicly available, which facilitates reproducibility and further research by the community.
- The paper is the first to use Integrated Gradients to analyze social biases, providing a novel approach to understanding and mitigating biases in large language models.

**Weaknesses:**
- The evaluation of the proposed method is limited to a single benchmark (StereoSet), which may not fully demonstrate the generalizability of the method across different datasets and models.
- The paper does not include a comparative analysis with other debiasing methods such as DPCE, AutoDebias, and Union_IG, which could have provided a more robust evaluation of the proposed method.
- The paper does not address the potential transferability of biases from one model to another, which is a critical issue in fairness research.
- The paper lacks a detailed discussion on the limitations of the proposed method, particularly in terms of its applicability to different types of models and datasets.
- The paper could benefit from a more comprehensive evaluation of the proposed method's effectiveness across different demographic groups and a more detailed analysis of the impact of the proposed method on the performance of the models.

**Questions:**
- How does the proposed method perform when evaluated on other datasets beyond StereoSet?
- Could you provide more details on the computational cost and efficiency of the proposed method, particularly in comparison to other debiasing methods?
- How does the proposed method perform when evaluated on different types of models, such as sequence-to-sequence models?
- Could you provide more insights into the potential transferability of biases from one model to another, and how the proposed method addresses this issue?
- How does the proposed method perform when evaluated on different types of biases, such as gender or race biases?
- Could you clarify the rationale behind the choice of the threshold for suppressing social bias neurons?
- How does the proposed method impact the performance of the models in terms of language modeling capabilities?
