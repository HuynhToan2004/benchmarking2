 **Summary:**
The paper investigates the self-contradictory hallucinations in large language models (LLMs) and proposes a method to detect and mitigate these contradictions. The approach involves triggering contradictions by generating a second sentence with the same context, detecting these contradictions using a different LLM, and mitigating them by revising the original text. The method is tested on various instruction-tuned LLMs, demonstrating its effectiveness in reducing contradictions while maintaining text informativeness and fluency. The paper also discusses the limitations of the approach and suggests future directions for research.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, clear, and easy to follow, making complex concepts accessible.
- The methodology is innovative, focusing on a novel aspect of hallucinations in LLMs, specifically self-contradictions, and proposes a simple yet effective approach to address this issue.
- The paper provides comprehensive evaluation results, demonstrating the effectiveness of the proposed method in reducing self-contradictions and improving text informativeness and fluency.
- The method is practical and can be applied to black-box LLMs, making it accessible and useful for real-world applications.
- The paper includes a detailed analysis of self-contradictions in LLMs, which is crucial for understanding and improving the reliability of LLM-generated text.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a discussion on the limitations of the proposed method, which could help in understanding its applicability and potential drawbacks.
- There is a need for a more detailed comparison with other methods, such as SelfCheckGPT, to establish the superiority of the proposed approach.
- The method's reliance on the accuracy of the detection LLM and the effectiveness of the revision LLM raises concerns about the reliability of the method, especially when dealing with contradictions that are not factual.
- The paper could benefit from a broader evaluation across different domains and a more comprehensive analysis of the types of contradictions that the method can and cannot handle.
- The presentation of the paper could be improved, particularly in terms of clarity and consistency in the use of symbols and terminology.

**Questions:**
- How does the proposed method perform when dealing with contradictions that are not factual?
- Can the method be adapted to handle contradictions in different domains, such as legal or medical texts?
- How does the method handle contradictions that are not directly related to factual information, such as those based on personal opinions or beliefs?
- Could the authors provide more details on the limitations of the proposed method and discuss potential improvements or alternative approaches?
- How does the method compare in terms of computational efficiency and practical applicability to other existing methods for detecting and mitigating contradictions in LLM-generated text?
