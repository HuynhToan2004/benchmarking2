 **Summary:**
The paper addresses the challenges of fine-tuning offline Reinforcement Learning (RL) agents using online interactions, focusing on three key issues: inefficient exploration, distributional shifts in samples, and distorted value functions. It proposes a method called Guided Decoupled Exploration (GDE) to tackle these challenges by decoupling exploration and exploitation policies and using a dynamic teacher policy to guide exploration. The method is evaluated on the D4RL benchmark and shows promising results, outperforming existing baselines in some scenarios. However, the paper's novelty is questioned, as it primarily modifies existing algorithms like IQL with minimal theoretical analysis. The presentation and clarity of the paper also need improvement, with some figures and explanations being unclear or inadequately explained.

**Strengths:**
- The paper addresses the challenges of fine-tuning offline RL agents with online interactions, which is a significant and relevant problem in the field.
- It proposes a simple yet effective method, GDE, which decouples exploration and exploitation policies and uses a dynamic teacher policy to guide exploration, which is a novel approach.
- The paper is well-written, easy to follow, and includes comprehensive experiments and ablation studies that demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed method.
- The method is evaluated on the D4RL benchmark and shows promising results, outperforming existing baselines in some scenarios.
- The paper provides a detailed analysis of the challenges in fine-tuning offline RL agents and discusses the challenges of efficient exploration, distributional shifts, and distorted value functions.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper primarily modifies existing algorithms like IQL with minimal theoretical analysis, which limits the depth of the contribution.
- The paper does not compare the proposed method with a wide range of baselines, which could provide a more robust evaluation of the method's effectiveness.
- The presentation of the paper could be improved, with some figures and explanations being unclear or inadequately explained.
- The paper lacks a detailed discussion on the limitations of the proposed method and potential negative societal impacts.
- The novelty of the method is questioned, as it primarily modifies existing algorithms like IQL without significant innovation.
- The paper does not provide sufficient analysis on the computational complexity and scalability of the proposed method, which could limit its applicability in more complex scenarios.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors provide a more detailed theoretical analysis of the proposed method, particularly how it addresses the challenges of fine-tuning offline RL agents?
- How does the proposed method compare with a wider range of baselines, including the latest state-of-the-art methods?
- Could the authors clarify the presentation of some figures and explanations to improve the readability and understanding of the paper?
- How does the proposed method perform when fine-tuned with more online steps?
- Can the authors provide more details on the computational complexity and scalability of the proposed method, especially in more complex scenarios?
- How does the proposed method handle the issue of distributional shifts in samples, and what are the implications of this in practical applications?
