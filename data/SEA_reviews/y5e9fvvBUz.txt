 **Summary:**
The paper introduces PRISM, a novel federated learning framework designed for generative models that focuses on strong and stable performance, resource efficiency, and privacy preservation. PRISM employs a stochastic binary mask to minimize communication overhead and ensures differential privacy (DP) through the aggregation of binary masks. The framework utilizes the maximum mean discrepancy (MMD) loss to enhance the stability of generative models, particularly in data-heterogeneous scenarios. PRISM also introduces a hybrid aggregation strategy to control the trade-off between communication and model capability. The framework is tested across various datasets, including MNIST, CelebA, and CIFAR10, demonstrating its effectiveness and efficiency compared to existing methods.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written and easy to follow, with a clear and structured presentation.
- The proposed PRISM framework is novel and addresses significant challenges in federated learning (FL) for generative models, including communication efficiency, privacy, and performance instability.
- The methodology is innovative, particularly the use of stochastic binary masks for communication efficiency and the integration of MMD loss for stability.
- The paper includes comprehensive experiments across various datasets, demonstrating the effectiveness and efficiency of the proposed method.
- The paper is the first to generate images in CelebA and CIFAR10 with a distributed and privacy-considered setting, which is a significant contribution to the field.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a thorough discussion on the limitations and potential negative societal impacts of the proposed method.
- The paper does not provide sufficient details on the hyperparameters used in the experiments, which could affect the reproducibility and comparability of the results.
- The novelty of the proposed method is limited as it mainly combines existing techniques without significant innovation.
- The paper does not adequately address the privacy concerns related to the aggregation of masks, which could potentially leak information about individual clients.
- The paper lacks a detailed comparison with other methods in terms of performance, particularly in non-IID data distribution scenarios.
- There are inconsistencies and potential errors in the presentation of results, such as incorrect formatting and misleading figures.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors provide more details on the hyperparameters used in the experiments to ensure reproducibility and comparability?
- How does the PRISM framework perform in more complex and diverse datasets, such as those from the ImageNet dataset?
- Can the authors clarify the privacy implications of the mask aggregation process and discuss potential mitigation strategies?
- How does the PRISM framework compare in terms of privacy and communication efficiency with other methods that use secure aggregation techniques?
- Could the authors elaborate on the novelty of their approach and how it differentiates from existing methods in the field of federated learning for generative models?
- Can the authors provide more detailed experimental results, possibly including additional datasets and a more comprehensive comparison with existing methods?
