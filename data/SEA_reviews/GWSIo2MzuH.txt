 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel set of PAC bounds using loss entropy, aiming to simplify the computation of generalization bounds and potentially improve the accuracy of bounds in scenarios with long-tailed loss distributions. This methodology replaces mutual information with loss entropy, which is argued to provide tighter bounds. The paper also explores fast-rate bounds and data-dependent bounds, validating these through empirical studies. Despite the theoretical and empirical advancements, the paper has been criticized for its limited novelty and the lack of significant improvement over existing bounds in certain scenarios.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, making the content accessible and easy to follow.
- The introduction of loss entropy as a replacement for mutual information in generalization bounds is novel and provides a new perspective on analyzing generalization.
- The paper provides both theoretical and empirical evidence to support the proposed bounds, which are shown to be tighter than previous bounds in some scenarios.
- The use of leave-one-out sampling and the supersample setting is a significant improvement over the traditional method of sampling a dataset twice, as it reduces the complexity of the bounds.
- The paper demonstrates a thorough understanding of the related literature and effectively positions its contributions within this context.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a clear and convincing argument for why the proposed bounds are significantly better than existing bounds, particularly in scenarios where the improvement is minimal or not statistically significant.
- The paper does not adequately address the computational complexity of estimating the loss entropy, which could be a significant challenge in practical applications.
- There is a lack of discussion on the limitations of the proposed bounds, particularly in scenarios where the bounds may not be applicable or where they may not provide significant improvements over existing bounds.
- The paper does not provide sufficient details on the experimental setup and methodology, which could limit the reproducibility and understanding of the results.
- The paper's theoretical contributions are seen as incremental, as they primarily build upon existing works, and the novelty is questioned due to the similarities with previous studies.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors clarify the discrepancies noted in the paper, such as the incorrect statements about the bounds in Appendix G.2 and the typographical errors in Theorem 1?
- How do the authors justify the use of loss entropy as a replacement for mutual information, particularly in scenarios where mutual information might be more appropriate?
- In light of the incremental nature of the theoretical contributions, how do the authors position their work within the broader context of existing literature, especially in terms of novelty and significance?
- Can the authors provide more details on the experimental setup, including the specifics of the datasets used, the network architectures, and the training parameters?
- How do the authors plan to address the computational challenges associated with estimating the loss entropy, particularly in large-scale applications?
