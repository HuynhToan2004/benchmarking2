 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel self-supervised learning framework, BrainMAE, aimed at learning transferable and interpretable representations from functional MRI (fMRI) data. This framework employs an embedding-informed graph attention mechanism and a masked autoencoding technique to capture temporal dynamics and mitigate noise in fMRI data. The methodology is evaluated on various datasets, including HCP 3T, HCP 7T, HCP aging, and NSD, demonstrating its effectiveness in predicting demographic and behavioral measures. The paper also includes an ablation study to validate the contributions of each component and a comparison with state-of-the-art models. Despite its strengths in methodology and experimental validation, the paper has been critiqued for its limited evaluation scope and the need for more extensive comparisons with existing models.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel self-supervised learning framework, BrainMAE, which effectively captures temporal dynamics in fMRI data and mitigates noise, outperforming state-of-the-art methods in various downstream tasks.
- The methodology is well-articulated, with a clear presentation of the problem, methodology, and results, making the paper accessible and understandable.
- The paper provides a comprehensive evaluation of the proposed method, including a detailed ablation study that demonstrates the contributions of each component.
- The authors have made the code and data available, enhancing reproducibility and transparency.
- The paper is well-written, with a clear introduction and motivation, and includes a thorough literature review and related work.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed comparison with existing models, particularly in terms of the number of parameters and computational efficiency, which is crucial for evaluating the scalability and efficiency of the proposed method.
- There is a limited evaluation of the proposed method, with the paper primarily focusing on a few datasets and not comparing the results across different datasets and preprocessing methods.
- The paper does not sufficiently address the potential negative societal impacts of the research.
- Some methodological details, such as the selection of hyperparameters and the impact of masking on data quality, are not sufficiently explained or justified.
- The paper does not discuss the limitations of the proposed method, which could provide a more balanced view of its strengths and weaknesses.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors provide more details on the hyperparameter selection, particularly the choice of the mask ratio and its impact on the results?
- How does the proposed method compare in terms of computational efficiency and parameter count with other state-of-the-art models?
- Can the authors discuss the potential negative societal impacts of their research and how they plan to mitigate these impacts?
- How does the proposed method perform when applied to other datasets or different preprocessing methods?
- Could the authors elaborate on the rationale behind the masking strategy and its impact on the data quality?
- Are there plans to extend the evaluation to include more diverse datasets and preprocessing methods?
