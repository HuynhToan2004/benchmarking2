 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel approach to Grobner basis computation using transformers, a machine learning model trained on pairs of non-Grobner sets and Grobner bases. This method leverages the backward Grobner problem to generate non-Grobner sets from Grobner bases, thereby reducing the computational complexity of the forward problem. The authors propose a new algorithm that efficiently generates these pairs, focusing on zero-dimensional ideals, and demonstrate its effectiveness through experiments that show significant speedup over traditional methods. Despite its innovative approach, the paper is criticized for its limited scope, focusing solely on zero-dimensional ideals, and the lack of broader evaluation against existing methods.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel approach to Grobner basis computation using transformers, which is a significant advancement in the field of computational algebra.
- The authors effectively leverage the backward Grobner problem to generate non-Grobner sets from Grobner bases, which is a clever application of mathematical concepts.
- The paper is well-written, clear, and provides a good introduction to the problem of Grobner basis computation, making it accessible to a broad audience.
- The experimental results demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed method, showing a significant speedup over traditional methods in generating datasets.
- The paper addresses an important problem in computational algebra, which is often considered computationally expensive, and offers a novel approach to address this challenge.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper's focus is limited to zero-dimensional ideals, which may not be representative of the broader applications of Grobner basis computation.
- The evaluation of the proposed method is limited to a single dataset, which does not provide a robust validation of the method's effectiveness across different scenarios.
- The paper lacks a thorough comparison with existing methods, which is crucial for establishing the novelty and effectiveness of the proposed approach.
- The paper does not adequately discuss the limitations of the proposed method, such as its applicability to non-zero-dimensional ideals and the potential inefficiencies in generating Grobner bases for larger polynomial systems.
- The paper could benefit from a more comprehensive discussion on the limitations of the backward approach and a broader evaluation of the method's performance against other state-of-the-art methods.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors discuss the limitations of the proposed method and provide insights into how these limitations might be addressed in future work?
- How does the proposed method compare to other state-of-the-art methods in terms of performance and computational efficiency?
- Could the authors provide more details on the experimental setup, such as the specific algorithms used for generating Grobner bases and the conditions under which the transformer model was trained?
- How does the proposed method handle non-zero-dimensional ideals, and what are the implications of this limitation for the practical applicability of the method?
- Could the authors clarify the role of the term order in the backward Grobner problem and discuss how this affects the performance of the method?
- What are the potential applications of the proposed method beyond cryptography, and how might these applications be evaluated in future work?
