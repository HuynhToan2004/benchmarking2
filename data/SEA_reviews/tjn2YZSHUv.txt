 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a new dataset, Social Reward, derived from a large online visual creation and editing platform, aimed at evaluating AI-generated images based on social popularity. The dataset is used to train a model, Social Reward, which predicts the social popularity of images by leveraging implicit user feedback. The model's effectiveness is demonstrated through comparisons with existing metrics, showing that it aligns better with social popularity. The paper also discusses the challenges of using this dataset to fine-tune text-to-image models, proposing a novel approach to evaluating AI-generated images in a social context.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a new dataset, Social Reward, which is a significant contribution to the field of AI-generated image evaluation.
- The dataset is collected from a large online visual creation and editing platform, providing a robust and diverse set of data for training and evaluation.
- The paper presents a novel approach to evaluating AI-generated images using social popularity as a metric, which is a new and interesting direction in the field.
- The paper is well-written, making it easy to follow, and includes a comprehensive review of existing literature, providing a solid foundation for the research.
- The dataset and model are evaluated using both quantitative and qualitative methods, providing a robust validation of the proposed approach.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed discussion on the limitations of the dataset, particularly concerning the potential biases and noise introduced by the implicit nature of the user feedback.
- The methodology used to collect the dataset and the process of selecting positive and negative images are not clearly explained, which could affect the reliability and validity of the results.
- The paper does not provide sufficient details on the training and evaluation of the Social Reward model, including the specifics of the triplet loss function and the selection of hyperparameters.
- The paper does not adequately address the potential societal impacts of using social popularity as a metric for evaluating AI-generated images, which could lead to biased or inappropriate content being promoted.
- The paper does not compare the Social Reward model with a sufficient number of baselines, which limits the ability to assess the model's effectiveness against established methods.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors provide more details on the data collection process, particularly how positive and negative images were selected?
- How does the Social Reward model handle noise and bias in the dataset, and what steps were taken to mitigate these issues?
- Can the authors clarify the specifics of the triplet loss function and the selection of hyperparameters used in the training of the Social Reward model?
- How does the Social Reward model compare with other metrics such as CLIP score and FID score, and what are the implications of these comparisons for the model's effectiveness?
- What are the societal impacts of using social popularity as a metric for evaluating AI-generated images, and how does the paper address these potential issues?
- Could the authors provide more details on the training and evaluation of the Social Reward model, including the specifics of the training setup and the evaluation metrics used?
