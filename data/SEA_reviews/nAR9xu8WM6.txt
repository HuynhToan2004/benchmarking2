 **Summary:**
The paper introduces DP-CLIP, a method that integrates differential privacy into the CLIP model to address privacy concerns in multimodal tasks. It employs per-batch clipping to ensure privacy, which is analyzed under linear representation settings. The paper presents empirical results showing that DP-CLIP retains performance on par with standard CLIP models while maintaining privacy. However, the theoretical analysis is limited to linear representation settings, and the experimental scope is restricted to simple datasets, raising concerns about the generalizability and effectiveness of the proposed method.

**Strengths:**
- The paper addresses an important and timely issue of ensuring privacy in multimodal tasks, which is increasingly relevant given the widespread use of CLIP and similar models.
- The authors have provided a thorough theoretical analysis, particularly focusing on the linear representation settings, which is a significant contribution to the field.
- The empirical results demonstrate that the proposed method, DP-CLIP, can maintain performance on par with standard CLIP models while ensuring privacy.
- The paper is well-written and easy to follow, making it accessible to a broad audience.
- The application of differential privacy to multimodal models is a novel approach, which could have significant implications for the field.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks comprehensive experimental validation, particularly in more complex and realistic scenarios, which limits the generalizability of the findings.
- The theoretical analysis is restricted to linear representation settings, which may not fully capture the complexities of non-linear representations commonly used in multimodal models.
- The empirical results are primarily focused on simple datasets such as MNIST and SVHN, which may not fully demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed method in more complex or varied settings.
- The paper does not provide a clear comparison with existing methods, particularly in terms of the privacy guarantees and performance metrics, which could help in understanding the relative advantages of the proposed method.
- The paper does not address potential negative societal impacts of the proposed method, which is a significant oversight given the potential implications of privacy in AI systems.

**Questions:**
- How does the proposed method compare to other existing methods in terms of privacy guarantees and performance metrics?
- Can the authors provide more complex experimental setups to demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed method in more varied and challenging scenarios?
- How does the proposed method perform in terms of privacy auditing, particularly in the context of membership inference attacks?
- What are the specific implications of using per-batch clipping in the context of DP-CLIP, and how does this compare to other clipping strategies?
- How does the proposed method handle the trade-off between privacy and utility, especially in terms of the convergence rate and the effectiveness of the privacy mechanisms?
- Could the authors provide more detailed comparisons with other differential privacy methods, particularly in terms of the theoretical underpinnings and practical implications?
