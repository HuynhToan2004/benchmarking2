 **Summary:**
The paper addresses the generation of differentially private synthetic data, focusing on low-dimensional subspaces to mitigate the curse of dimensionality. It introduces an algorithm that leverages a private PCA procedure to project high-dimensional data onto a lower-dimensional subspace, ensuring differential privacy. The algorithm is designed to provide utility guarantees with respect to the Wasserstein distance and does not rely on the assumption of a spectral gap for the covariance matrix. The paper also discusses the computational efficiency and presents a theoretical analysis that improves upon previous results. However, the paper's presentation and clarity need improvement, particularly in the notation and the explanation of the algorithms.

**Strengths:**
- The paper addresses the important and relevant problem of generating differentially private synthetic data, focusing on low-dimensional subspaces to mitigate the curse of dimensionality.
- It introduces a novel algorithm that leverages a private PCA procedure to project high-dimensional data onto a lower-dimensional subspace, ensuring differential privacy.
- The paper provides a theoretical analysis that improves upon previous results, including the removal of the logarithmic factor in the utility bound.
- The algorithm is computationally efficient and does not rely on the assumption of a spectral gap for the covariance matrix, which is a significant departure from previous works.
- The paper is well-motivated and provides a clear comparison to previous works, highlighting the novelty and contribution of the proposed method.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper suffers from poor writing and lacks clarity in presentation. Notations are not clearly defined, and the explanation of algorithms is insufficient.
- The paper does not adequately discuss the assumptions and limitations of the proposed algorithm, which could affect its applicability and reliability.
- The paper's theoretical contributions are not well-integrated with the practical applications, and the utility guarantees are not clearly explained or justified.
- The paper does not provide a detailed comparison with existing works, especially those that also address the generation of differentially private synthetic data.
- There are several minor typographical errors and inconsistencies in the paper that need to be corrected.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors clarify the definitions and uses of various notations throughout the paper, especially those used in Algorithm 1?
- How does the proposed algorithm handle the case where the data is not naturally low-dimensional? What are the implications of this for the utility guarantees?
- What are the assumptions made by the algorithm, and how do these affect the utility guarantees?
- Can the authors provide a more detailed comparison with existing works, particularly those that also address the generation of differentially private synthetic data?
- How does the paper justify the utility guarantees provided by the algorithm, and what are the implications of these guarantees for the practical application of the synthetic data?
- Can the authors elaborate on the limitations of the proposed algorithm and discuss potential directions for future research?
