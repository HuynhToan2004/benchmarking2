 **Summary:**
The paper introduces Kinematic Phrases (KP) as a novel intermediate representation to bridge the gap between human motion and action semantics. KP categorizes human motion into six types based on objective kinematic facts, aiming to enhance interpretability and generality. The authors propose a motion understanding system that leverages KP to perform tasks such as motion interpolation, modification, and generation. Additionally, a new benchmark, Kinematic Prompt Generation (KPG), is introduced to evaluate the consistency between generated motions and specific kinematic facts. The paper presents extensive experiments and user studies to validate the effectiveness of the proposed methods. However, concerns were raised regarding the generalization of KP across different modalities and the clarity of the motivation behind the KP design.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel intermediate representation, Kinematic Phrases (KP), which effectively bridges the gap between human motion and action semantics.
- The design of KP is intuitive and well-explained, with a clear rationale for its categorization and application.
- The proposed motion understanding system and the KP-mediated motion generation benchmark are innovative and demonstrate practical utility in various applications.
- The paper is well-written, with clear explanations of the methodologies and results, which enhances the readability and comprehensibility of the content.
- The experiments conducted are extensive and convincing, showcasing the effectiveness of the proposed methods.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed discussion on the generalization of KP across different modalities, particularly in scenarios where the granularity of motion data varies.
- There is a lack of clarity in the motivation behind the design of KP, particularly why it is necessary to abstract motion perturbations from semantics changes.
- The paper does not adequately address the issue of generalization to more complex or diverse action semantics, which could limit the practical applicability of the proposed methods.
- The paper's reliance on a limited set of datasets for training and evaluation raises concerns about the robustness and generalizability of the results.
- The paper could benefit from a more thorough discussion on the limitations and potential improvements of the proposed methods, as well as a clearer explanation of the experimental setup and results.

**Questions:**
- How does the proposed method perform when applied to more complex or diverse action semantics, such as those involving multiple actions or fine-grained movements?
- Could the authors clarify the motivation behind the design of KP and how it addresses the specific challenges in mapping motion to action semantics?
- How does the proposed method handle variations in motion data granularity, especially when the data is less detailed or more abstract?
- Can the authors provide more details on the experimental setup, particularly the selection and justification of the datasets used for training and evaluation?
- How does the proposed method compare to other existing methods, especially in terms of interpretability, generalization, and computational efficiency?
- Could the authors discuss the potential of extending the KP framework to include more detailed kinematic information, such as movement amplitude and speed?
