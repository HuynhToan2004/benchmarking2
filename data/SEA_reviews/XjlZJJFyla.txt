 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel method, Bayesian prompt tuning, aimed at generating stochastic prompts for vision-language pre-trained models. This approach leverages a Bayesian framework to model each category as a distribution over the embedding space, which is then used to generate diverse and generalizable prompts. The method incorporates a conditional transport framework to align textual prompts with image patches, addressing issues like overfitting and mode collapse. The paper presents a hierarchical model that generates stochastic prompts and integrates these with a lightweight generative model to optimize the alignment of visual patches and linguistic prompts. Extensive experiments are conducted to evaluate the effectiveness of the proposed method, showing improvements over existing methods in various downstream tasks.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel Bayesian probabilistic resolution to prompt tuning, which generates label-specific stochastic prompts hierarchically. This method models each label as a distribution over the embedding space, enabling the handling of diverse visual concepts.
- The use of the conditional transport (CT) framework for semantic regularization is innovative and helps in aligning textual prompts with image patches, addressing issues like overfitting and mode collapse.
- The paper is well-written, clear, and easy to follow, making it accessible to readers.
- The proposed method is shown to be effective in various downstream tasks, demonstrating its practical applicability.
- The paper provides extensive experiments that show the effectiveness of the proposed method, including a detailed ablation study.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed explanation of the conditional transport (CT) framework, which is crucial for understanding the methodology.
- There is a lack of clarity in the definitions and roles of certain parameters and variables used in the equations, such as the context prior $p(t^c)$, the context prior $p(t_c)$, and the cost function $C$.
- The paper does not sufficiently discuss the computational cost of the proposed method, which is crucial for practical implementation.
- The paper does not compare the proposed method with the latest prompt tuning methods, which could have provided a more comprehensive evaluation.
- The paper does not sufficiently discuss the limitations of the proposed method, which could help in understanding its applicability and scope.
- There is a lack of detailed discussion on how the method handles different types of vision-language models, which could affect its generalizability.

**Questions:**
- Could you clarify the role and definition of the context prior $p(t^c)$ and $p(t_c)$ in the methodology?
- How does the proposed method compare in terms of computational cost with existing methods like CoOp and CoCoOp?
- Can you provide more details on how the proposed method handles different types of vision-language models, such as those that use different architectures or training methods?
- How does the proposed method ensure that the generated prompts are not overly similar or redundant, which could lead to performance degradation?
- Could you elaborate on the design choices for the stochastic vector $r_c$ and the deterministic generative model $\phi(v_c|r_c)$?
- What are the specific benefits of using a hierarchical model over a direct distribution over the sequence of tokens?
- How does the proposed method handle the issue of overfitting, especially in scenarios where the training data is limited?
