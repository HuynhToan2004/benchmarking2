 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel method for solving complex tasks in Minecraft using a hierarchical reinforcement learning (RL) approach, where skills are learned and then planned to accomplish tasks. The method involves training three types of skills (Finding, Manipulation, and Crafting) using RL and a large language model (LLM) to construct a skill graph. This graph is used to generate task-specific skill plans, which are then executed by the agent. The approach is evaluated against various baselines and shown to outperform them in terms of success rate and sample efficiency. The paper also discusses the limitations of the method, including the need for better goal-awareness in the Finding-skill and the potential impact of LLM inaccuracies.

**Strengths:**
- The paper presents a novel approach to skill learning and planning in Minecraft, which is both innovative and well-executed.
- The methodology is clearly explained, making it easy to understand and follow, which is crucial for replication and further research.
- The paper demonstrates a high success rate in Minecraft tasks, showing the effectiveness of the proposed method.
- The use of a skill graph constructed by a large language model (LLM) is a novel approach that integrates LLM with RL in an interesting and practical way.
- The paper is well-written, with clear explanations of the methodology and results, which enhances its readability and comprehensibility.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed comparison with existing methods, particularly those that use similar hierarchical RL or LLM-based planning approaches. This makes it difficult to assess the true novelty and effectiveness of the proposed method.
- The paper does not adequately address the limitations of the method, such as the potential for LLM inaccuracies and the impact of these inaccuracies on the performance of the agent.
- The evaluation of the method is somewhat limited, focusing mainly on Minecraft tasks without a broader evaluation in more diverse or complex environments.
- The paper could benefit from a more detailed discussion on the generalizability of the method beyond Minecraft and how it might be adapted to other domains.
- The paper could provide more detailed results and analysis, such as the average number of steps required to complete each task and the success rates for individual skills.

**Questions:**
- Can you provide more details on the comparison with existing methods, particularly those that use similar approaches to skill learning and planning?
- How does the performance of the agent vary when the LLM is incorrect, and what strategies are in place to handle such errors?
- Could you provide a more detailed discussion on the generalizability of the method beyond Minecraft and how it might be adapted to other domains?
- How does the method perform when the agent is given a new task that was not included in the training data?
- Can you provide a more detailed analysis of the results, such as the average number of steps required to complete each task and the success rates for individual skills?
