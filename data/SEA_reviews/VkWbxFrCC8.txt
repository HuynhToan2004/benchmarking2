 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel method for data compression using implicit neural representations (INRs) by extending the combiner model with several modifications. These enhancements include a linear reparameterization for network weights, positional encodings, a hierarchical Bayesian model, and a permutation strategy to optimize the rate-distortion trade-off. The method aims to address issues such as overfitting and inefficiency in previous INR-based approaches. Extensive experiments across various data types demonstrate that the proposed method outperforms existing INR-based codecs and is competitive with other state-of-the-art methods. However, the paper suffers from some clarity issues in the presentation and lacks a detailed comparison with other methods.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, clear, and easy to follow, making it accessible to a broad audience.
- The proposed method, RECOMBINER, is a significant improvement over the COMBINER model, with extensive experiments demonstrating its effectiveness.
- The method is applicable to different modalities of data, including images, audio, and 3D protein structures, which showcases its versatility.
- The use of hierarchical Bayesian models and positional encodings is innovative and addresses the challenges of overfitting and local deviations in data.
- The paper provides a detailed comparison with other methods, including VAE-based codecs and classical codecs, which helps in evaluating the performance of RECOMBINER.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed comparison with other INR-based codecs, which could have provided a more comprehensive evaluation of the method's performance.
- There is a significant gap in performance between RECOMBINER and other state-of-the-art methods, particularly in terms of runtime and compression efficiency.
- The paper does not sufficiently address the computational complexity and runtime issues, which are crucial for practical applications.
- The method's dependency on hyperparameters such as the number of hidden units and the number of patches raises concerns about its robustness and scalability.
- The paper lacks a detailed discussion on the limitations and potential negative societal impacts of the proposed method.
- The presentation of the methodology and experimental results could be improved to enhance clarity and understanding.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors provide a detailed comparison with other INR-based codecs to better understand the method's performance in this context?
- How does the proposed method compare in terms of runtime and computational complexity with other state-of-the-art methods?
- Can the authors discuss the potential negative societal impacts of their method and propose mitigation strategies if necessary?
- How do the authors justify the choice of using a different split for video encoding compared to the method used by Schwarz et al. (2023)?
- Could the authors provide a more detailed explanation of the hierarchical Bayesian model and its impact on the method's performance?
- Why was the choice made to split data into patches, and how does this affect the method's performance in terms of artifact reduction?
