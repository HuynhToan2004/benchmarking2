 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel method for conditional sampling in denoising diffusion probabilistic models (DDPM) that utilizes a noise-free guidance approach. This method incorporates momentum-driven gradient filtering and gradient suppression to enhance the consistency and robustness of clean guidance throughout the denoising process. The approach aims to eliminate the need for noise-finetuning and can be applied to a wide range of guidance functions operating on clean data. The method is demonstrated to be effective in tasks like clean guided conditional image generation and arbitrary style transfer, showing superior performance compared to previous methods. However, concerns are raised about the novelty of the method, as it seems to be an extension of existing methods without significant innovation.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, clear, and easy to follow, making it accessible to a broad audience.
- The methodology is straightforward and effective, with a clear motivation and a well-structured presentation of the problem and proposed solutions.
- The proposed method is simple yet effective, improving the performance of clean-estimation guided DDPMs, and it does not require additional training costs.
- The paper demonstrates the effectiveness of the proposed method through extensive experiments, showing improvements in both quantitative and qualitative results.
- The methodology is versatile and applicable to a wide array of guidance functions on clean data, making it a valuable tool for various applications.
- The paper provides a detailed analysis of the limitations of clean-estimation guidance, which is crucial for understanding the challenges and opportunities in this area.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a thorough comparison with state-of-the-art methods, particularly in terms of computational cost, training efficiency, and performance metrics.
- The novelty of the proposed method is questionable as it appears to be an extension of existing methods without significant innovation.
- The paper could benefit from more detailed experimental results, including more visual examples and comparisons with other recent works.
- There are some minor presentation issues, such as inconsistencies in the use of symbols and figures, which could be improved for clarity and professionalism.
- The paper does not sufficiently address the limitations of the proposed method, which could be a significant drawback for readers seeking a comprehensive understanding.
- The paper's claims about the generalization capability of the framework are not adequately supported by experimental evidence.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors clarify the role and impact of the hyperparameter λ in the proposed method?
- How does the proposed method compare in terms of computational cost and training efficiency with other state-of-the-art methods, particularly those that use noised classifiers?
- Could the authors provide more detailed experimental results, including more visual examples and comparisons with other recent works?
- In the context of the proposed method, how does the guidance function P(y,x0) operate, and what specific guidance functions have been tested?
- Can the authors explain the discrepancies observed in the performance metrics between different experiments, particularly the differences in FID scores?
- How does the proposed method perform when compared to other methods that use noise-free guidance, and what are the specific advantages of the proposed method over these?
