 **Summary:**
The paper explores the connection between contrastive learning, particularly SimCLR, and spectral clustering, showing that the InfoNCE loss is equivalent to spectral clustering under specific conditions. This equivalence is further extended to the CLIP model, demonstrating that it performs spectral clustering on the multi-modal similarity graph. The authors propose a new Kernel-InfoNCE loss that incorporates mixtures of kernel functions to enhance performance. Theoretical insights are provided, and empirical results are presented to support the claims. However, the paper is criticized for its limited experimental validation, lack of clarity in some sections, and potential overstatement of its contributions.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, with clear theoretical results and interesting connections between contrastive learning and spectral clustering.
- The authors have made significant efforts to connect contrastive learning with spectral clustering, providing a new perspective on the theoretical understanding of contrastive learning.
- The proposed Kernel-InfoNCE loss shows promising empirical results, demonstrating its effectiveness in improving performance.
- The paper is well-organized, with clear theoretical explanations and a comprehensive literature review, making it accessible and informative for readers.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper could benefit from more extensive experiments to validate the claims, particularly in terms of the proposed Kernel-InfoNCE loss and the theoretical connections.
- There are inconsistencies and potential errors in the definitions and notations used, which could confuse readers and undermine the credibility of the results.
- The paper lacks clarity in some sections, particularly in the discussion of the spectral clustering equivalence and the experimental setups.
- The paper does not sufficiently discuss or compare its contributions with existing literature, particularly with works that have already established similar connections between contrastive learning and spectral clustering.
- The practical implications and limitations of the proposed methods are not adequately addressed, which could limit the understanding and applicability of the findings.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors clarify the role and definition of the matrix π in the context of contrastive learning, especially in relation to the SimCLR paper?
- How does the proposed Kernel-InfoNCE loss compare to other kernel functions in terms of performance and computational efficiency?
- Could the authors provide more detailed explanations or examples of how the theoretical results translate into practical improvements in model performance?
- What are the specific advantages of using the Kernel-InfoNCE loss over other kernel functions, and how does it address the limitations of existing methods like SimCLR?
- In the context of the spectral clustering equivalence, can the authors provide a more detailed explanation or proof of the equivalence, particularly in the context of the SimCLR algorithm?
- How does the paper address the limitations of the spectral clustering approach, and what are the potential negative societal impacts of the proposed methods?
