 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel post-hoc GNN-based method for enhancing the performance of fine-grained image classification tasks. This approach leverages GNNs to refine feature vectors extracted by a pre-trained DNN, aiming to improve classification accuracy by leveraging the clustering capabilities of GNNs. The methodology involves constructing a complete graph from feature vectors within a batch, passing these through multiple GNN layers, and combining the outputs with those from the DNN. The paper demonstrates the effectiveness of this approach through experiments on various datasets, showing improvements over existing methods. However, concerns were raised about the novelty of the approach, as similar methods have been explored in prior works.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, making it easy to follow and understand.
- The proposed method is simple yet effective, demonstrating improvements in fine-grained image classification tasks.
- The method is general and can be applied to various existing models, enhancing their performance.
- The experimental results are strong, showing significant improvements over baseline methods on several datasets.
- The paper is well-organized, with clear figures and tables that aid in understanding the proposed method.

**Weaknesses:**
- The novelty of the proposed method is questionable as similar approaches have been explored in prior works, such as using GNNs for post-hoc feature refinement.
- The paper lacks a comprehensive comparison with state-of-the-art methods, particularly in terms of performance and computational efficiency.
- The paper does not sufficiently discuss the limitations of the proposed method, which could affect its practical application.
- The paper does not provide a detailed analysis of the computational complexity introduced by the proposed method, which could impact its scalability.
- There are several typographical errors and minor issues in the paper that need correction.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors provide more details on how the graph is constructed from feature vectors within a batch?
- How does the proposed method compare in terms of computational efficiency and resource requirements to other state-of-the-art methods?
- Can the authors provide more insights into how the proposed method performs on other datasets, particularly those not specifically designed for fine-grained image classification?
- Why was the choice made to use a fully connected graph rather than a more structured graph for the GNN encoder?
- Can the authors discuss the potential limitations of the proposed method and how these might affect its practical application?
