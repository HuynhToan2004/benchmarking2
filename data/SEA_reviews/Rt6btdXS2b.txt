 **Summary:**
The paper introduces the Continuous Indeterminate Probability Neural Network (CIPNN) and Continuous Indeterminate Probability Auto-Encoder (CIPAE), which extend the Indeterminate Probability Neural Network (IPNN) by integrating continuous latent variables. These networks aim to address the intractability of posterior calculations and enhance the understanding of neural networks through a probabilistic lens. The authors claim that CIPNN and CIPAE offer analytical solutions to complex posterior calculations, which are validated through experiments on MNIST and Fashion-MNIST datasets. However, the paper faces significant criticism for its lack of originality, insufficient theoretical depth, and unclear presentation.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written and easy to follow, with clear explanations and a comprehensive review of related work.
- The authors have derived the CIPNN model and CIPAE model based on the indeterminate probability theory, which is a novel approach to handling complex posterior calculations.
- The proposed method shows good results on MNIST and Fashion-MNIST datasets, demonstrating its practical applicability.
- The paper is structured to be accessible, with a clear introduction and logical flow, which aids in understanding the complex concepts presented.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks originality as it heavily relies on the IPNN framework without sufficient novel contributions.
- The experiments conducted are limited to simple datasets, which may not adequately demonstrate the generalizability or effectiveness of the proposed models.
- The paper suffers from poor writing and lacks clarity in the presentation of results and methodologies, making it difficult to follow and understand the implications of the findings.
- The theoretical depth of the paper is insufficient, with some sections, such as the explanation of the Observer 3 in Section 3, being unclear and potentially misleading.
- The paper does not adequately address the computational complexity and scalability of the proposed models, which is crucial for practical applications.
- There are several grammatical and typographical errors that need to be corrected for better professional presentation.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors clarify the role and function of Observer 3 in the context of the proposed model?
- How does the proposed method compare to existing methods in terms of computational complexity and scalability?
- Can the authors provide more detailed explanations of the experimental results, particularly the discrepancies noted in the figures and tables?
- How does the proposed method handle the overfitting problem mentioned in the paper, and what are the implications of this for the practical application of the model?
- Could the authors elaborate on the theoretical underpinnings of the proposed method, particularly in relation to the indeterminate probability theory?
- How does the paper address the limitations discussed, such as the lack of originality and the simplicity of the experimental setup?
