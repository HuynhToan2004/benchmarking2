 **Summary:**
The paper introduces the Implicit Stacked Autoregressive Model for Weather Forecasting (IAM4WF), a novel approach that combines autoregressive and lead time embedding methods to address the limitations of existing methods in weather forecasting. The model utilizes an error-free queue and an error-prone queue to maintain spatiotemporal correlations and mitigate error accumulation. The method is evaluated on six datasets, showing promising results in terms of performance and error reduction. However, concerns are raised about the novelty of the approach, as it seems to be a simple combination of existing methods without significant innovation. The paper's contribution is questioned due to its reliance on existing methodologies and lack of comprehensive comparative analysis with state-of-the-art models.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, making it easy to follow and understand.
- The proposed method is simple yet effective, achieving state-of-the-art performance on three benchmark datasets for weather and climate prediction.
- The authors provide a detailed analysis of the proposed method, including the use of an error-free queue and an error-prone queue to maintain spatiotemporal correlations.
- The paper includes a thorough ablation study, which helps in understanding the impact of different components of the model.
- The experimental results are promising, showing that the proposed model outperforms existing methods in terms of performance and error reduction.

**Weaknesses:**
- The novelty of the proposed method is limited as it appears to be a simple combination of existing methods without significant innovation.
- The paper lacks a comprehensive comparison with state-of-the-art models, which is crucial for establishing the effectiveness and superiority of the proposed method.
- The paper does not provide sufficient details on the model's architecture, such as the number of layers in the encoder, predictor, and decoder, which could affect the reproducibility and understanding of the results.
- The paper does not adequately address the issue of error accumulation, which is a significant challenge in long-term weather forecasting.
- The paper's contribution is not clearly defined, as it seems to be primarily a combination of existing methods without significant innovation or novelty.

**Questions:**
- How does the proposed method compare to state-of-the-art models in terms of performance and efficiency?
- Could the authors provide more details on the model's architecture, such as the number of layers in the encoder, predictor, and decoder, and the size of the input and output frames?
- How does the model handle the issue of error accumulation, and what measures are in place to mitigate this issue?
- The paper claims to address the error accumulation problem in autoregressive methods. Can the authors provide a more detailed explanation of how this is achieved?
- In the ablation study, how does the model perform when different components are removed, and what are the implications of these changes for the model's performance?
- Can the authors clarify the contribution of their work and how it differs from existing methods, particularly in terms of novelty and methodological advancements?
