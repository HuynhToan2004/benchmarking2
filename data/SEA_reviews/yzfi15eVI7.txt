 **Summary:**
The paper introduces iHyperTime, a novel method for generating time series using implicit neural representations (INRs). It leverages a hypernetwork architecture to learn the weights of TSNet, which performs trend-seasonality decomposition. The method is designed to handle both regular and irregularly sampled time series, and it demonstrates superior performance in terms of predictive and discriminative scores. The authors also claim that their model outperforms existing methods in terms of handling long sequences and irregular data. However, the paper has been critiqued for its limited novelty and lack of comprehensive experimental validation, particularly in terms of missing data and the impact of hyperparameters on performance.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, clear, and easy to follow, making it accessible to a broad audience.
- The methodology of using implicit neural representations (INRs) for time series generation is novel and innovative, particularly in the context of handling irregularly sampled data.
- The proposed method, iHyperTime, demonstrates superior performance in handling irregularly sampled data compared to existing methods, as evidenced by the predictive and discriminative scores.
- The paper includes a thorough experimental setup, including comparisons with various baselines and a detailed ablation study, which supports the claims made about the model's effectiveness.
- The authors have provided a comprehensive codebase for reproducibility, which is a significant contribution to the field.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed discussion on the limitations of the proposed method, particularly in terms of its applicability to datasets with different characteristics, such as different numbers of features or different types of data.
- The paper does not adequately address the impact of hyperparameters on the performance of the model, which could lead to inconsistent results across different datasets.
- There is a lack of novelty in the methodology, as the use of INRs and TSNet for time series generation is not a new concept.
- The paper does not provide sufficient details on the computational costs associated with training and inference, which could be a significant concern for practical applications.
- The paper could benefit from a more detailed discussion on the potential negative societal impacts of the proposed method.
- The experimental results presented in the paper are not convincing due to the lack of comparisons with state-of-the-art methods and the absence of qualitative results.

**Questions:**
- How does the proposed method handle irregularly sampled data, and what are the specific challenges in applying the method to such data?
- Can the authors provide more details on the computational costs associated with training and inference, and how these costs scale with the size of the dataset?
- How does the method perform when applied to datasets with different characteristics, such as different numbers of features or different types of data?
- What are the potential negative societal impacts of the proposed method, and how are these addressed in the paper?
- Could the authors provide more details on the experimental setup, particularly in terms of the datasets used and the experimental conditions?
- How does the method perform when compared to state-of-the-art methods, and what are the specific advantages of the proposed method over existing approaches?
