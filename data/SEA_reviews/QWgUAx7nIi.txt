 **Summary:**
The paper introduces the Contrastive Graph Autoencoder (CGAE), a novel graph representation autoencoder designed for retrieving polygon geometries with similar shapes from real-world building maps. CGAE leverages graph message-passing layers, graph feature augmentation, and contrastive learning to embed highly discriminative latent embeddings that effectively reconstruct graph features based on graph representations of input polygons. The methodology demonstrates robustness to geometric transformations and generalizes well to complex real-world building footprints. The paper presents experimental results showing that CGAE outperforms existing graph-based autoencoders in geometry retrieval of similar polygons and is robust to geometric transformations.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel approach using a contrastive graph autoencoder (CGAE) for retrieving polygon geometries with similar shapes from real-world building maps, which is a significant contribution to the field.
- The methodology is robust to geometric transformations and generalizes well to complex real-world building footprints, demonstrating its effectiveness in handling complex geometries.
- The paper is well-written, making it easy to follow and understand, with clear and concise descriptions of the proposed method and its implementation.
- The experimental results are strong, demonstrating the effectiveness of the proposed method in geometry retrieval tasks, showing that CGAE outperforms existing graph-based autoencoders in retrieving similar polygons.
- The paper provides a clear motivation for the problem and a comprehensive experimental setup, including a detailed comparison with baseline methods and an ablation study that shows the effectiveness of each component of the proposed method.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed discussion on the limitations of the proposed method, which could help in understanding its applicability and potential areas for improvement.
- There is a lack of clarity in the definition and usage of certain terms and equations, such as the role of edge weights in Equation 2 and the definition of node centralization in Equation 3.
- The paper does not provide a detailed analysis of the computational complexity or training time of the proposed method, which could be crucial for practical applications.
- The paper's scope is somewhat limited to polygon retrieval, and it is unclear how the method could be extended to other types of geospatial data or applications.
- The paper does not include a discussion on the potential negative societal impacts of the proposed method, which could be an important consideration for future research.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors provide more details on the limitations of the proposed method and how these might affect its applicability in real-world scenarios?
- How does the proposed method compare in terms of computational complexity and training time with other existing methods for polygon retrieval?
- Can the authors provide a detailed explanation of the role of edge weights in Equation 2 and the definition of node centralization in Equation 3?
- How can the proposed method be extended to handle other types of geospatial data or applications beyond polygon retrieval?
- Could the authors provide a more detailed discussion on the potential negative societal impacts of the proposed method and how these might be mitigated?
