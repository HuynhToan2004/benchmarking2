 **Summary:**
The paper introduces GatedMTL, a novel multi-task learning (MTL) framework designed to balance shared and specialized representations, addressing the challenge of task interference and computational constraints. It utilizes a learnable gating mechanism that allows individual tasks to select and combine channels from both specialized and shared feature sets, promoting an asymmetric flow of information and facilitating varied contributions from individual tasks to the shared branch's representations. The methodology is evaluated on three MTL benchmarks, demonstrating state-of-the-art results with convolutional and transformer-based backbones on CelebA, NYUD-v2, and PASCAL-Context. However, the paper's novelty is questioned due to its similarity to existing methods, and the experimental results do not convincingly demonstrate superiority over baseline methods.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, clear, and easy to follow, with a clear motivation and a well-designed method.
- The methodology is novel and innovative, with a focus on learning to share, specialize, and prune representations, which is crucial for multi-task learning (MTL).
- The proposed GatedMTL method demonstrates superior performance compared to existing methods, showing a significant improvement in accuracy and computational efficiency.
- The paper provides a comprehensive ablation study that helps in understanding the effects of different components of the proposed method.
- The experiments are thorough, with a focus on convolutional as well as transformer-based backbones, which enhances the robustness of the results.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed discussion on related works, particularly on methods that also use gating mechanisms in MTL.
- The novelty of the proposed method is questioned due to its similarity to existing methods, such as the Mixture of Experts (MoE) and dynamic gating in MTL.
- The experimental results do not convincingly demonstrate the superiority of the proposed method over baseline methods, with some results showing marginal improvements or even worse performance.
- The paper does not adequately address the issue of task interference, which is a significant challenge in MTL.
- The paper's methodology is somewhat complicated, with multiple components that require careful tuning and hyperparameter selection, which could limit its practical applicability.
- There is a lack of discussion on the limitations of the proposed method and its scalability, particularly in terms of the number of tasks it can handle.

**Questions:**
- Could you clarify the role and impact of the gating mechanism in your method, especially how it differs from existing methods like MoE and dynamic gating in MTL?
- How does the proposed method address the issue of task interference, and what are the specific mechanisms in place to mitigate this issue?
- In terms of practical application, how does the method perform when scaled up to handle a large number of tasks? Are there any performance degradations or scalability issues?
- Can you provide more details on the experimental setup, particularly how the hyperparameters were selected and how they affect the performance of the method?
- How does the proposed method compare to other methods that also use gating mechanisms in MTL, such as those mentioned in related works?
