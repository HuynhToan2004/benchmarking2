 **Summary:**
The paper introduces ViCo, a novel method for personalized text-to-image generation using a diffusion model. It employs an image cross-attention module to integrate visual conditions into the denoising process, which enables the extraction of accurate object masks. These masks effectively isolate the object of interest, eliminating distractions from the background in the latent space. The method does not require fine-tuning of the original diffusion model, making it scalable and flexible for deployment. The paper demonstrates that ViCo can generate photorealistic images based on textual embeddings, showcasing its effectiveness in various applications. However, concerns were raised about the novelty of the method, the clarity of the presentation, and the fairness of the comparison with existing methods.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written and easy to follow, with clear and concise explanations of the methodology and results.
- The proposed method is simple yet effective, with a focus on integrating visual conditions into the diffusion process, which enables the extraction of accurate object masks.
- The paper demonstrates the method's ability to generate photorealistic images based on textual embeddings, showcasing its effectiveness in various applications.
- The method does not require fine-tuning of the original diffusion model, making it scalable and flexible for deployment.
- The paper includes a comprehensive evaluation, including quantitative and qualitative comparisons with state-of-the-art methods, and demonstrates the efficiency of ViCo in multiple applications.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed discussion on the limitations and failure cases of the proposed method, which could provide a more comprehensive understanding of its capabilities and challenges.
- The novelty of the method is questionable, as it primarily modifies the existing Textual Inversion method by adding visual conditions and object masks, which might not be considered a significant advancement.
- The paper does not adequately compare the proposed method with other state-of-the-art methods, particularly in terms of training and inference times, which could affect the fairness and completeness of the evaluation.
- The paper could benefit from more detailed explanations of the methodology, particularly in sections where the methodology is introduced, to improve clarity and understanding.
- The paper's reliance on a frozen diffusion model could limit the performance compared to methods that fine-tune the original diffusion model.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors provide more details on how the method handles different types of images, especially those with complex backgrounds or multiple objects?
- How does the method perform when the reference image does not match the object's appearance in the text prompt?
- Could the authors clarify the role and impact of the regularization term in the method, particularly in terms of its effectiveness and computational cost?
- Can the authors provide more details on the training and inference times for the proposed method, and how these compare to other state-of-the-art methods?
- How does the method handle different types of objects, such as those with complex shapes or intricate details, and what are the limitations in generating such objects?
