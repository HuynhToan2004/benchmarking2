 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel privacy attack suite tailored for point-of-interest (POI) recommendation models, focusing on data extraction and membership inference attacks. It explores the vulnerability of POI recommendation models to these attacks and their effectiveness in extracting sensitive information from mobility data. The authors also discuss potential defenses against these attacks and their implications for future research. The study is supported by experiments on two real-world mobility datasets, highlighting the potential risks and challenges in this area. However, the paper has been critiqued for its limited scope, lack of comprehensive evaluation, and unclear presentation.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel privacy attack suite for POI recommendation models, which is the first of its kind to comprehensively evaluate the privacy risks in this area.
- The experiments conducted are extensive, demonstrating that POI recommendation models are vulnerable to data extraction and membership inference attacks.
- The paper provides an in-depth analysis of what unique factors in mobility data make them vulnerable to privacy attacks, testing defenses against the attacks, and identifying future directions and challenges in this area.
- The paper is well-written, with clear explanations of the proposed attack suite and its components, including data extraction and membership inference attacks.
- The paper is original in its approach, focusing on the privacy risks of POI recommendation models, which is a significant and relevant topic in the field of location-based services.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a comprehensive evaluation of the attack suite, particularly in terms of the scope of the evaluation and the depth of analysis.
- There is a significant overlap with existing works, particularly in the area of membership inference attacks, which undermines the novelty of the study.
- The paper's presentation could be improved, with some sections being difficult to understand and requiring more clarity and detail.
- The paper's assumptions about adversary knowledge are not well-justified, and the paper could benefit from a more detailed discussion on the practicality and realism of these assumptions.
- The paper's contributions are somewhat limited by the scope of the experiments and the depth of the analysis, which could be expanded to include more diverse datasets and model architectures.
- The paper's conclusions and recommendations are somewhat limited by the scope of the study, and the implications for real-world applications are not fully explored.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors clarify the assumptions made about adversary knowledge and their practical implications in real-world scenarios?
- How do the authors justify the choice of datasets and models used in the experiments, and what steps were taken to ensure the validity and reliability of the results?
- Could the authors provide more detailed examples or case studies to illustrate the effectiveness and practical implications of the attacks and defenses discussed in the paper?
- How do the authors plan to address the limitations of the study, particularly in terms of expanding the scope of the evaluation and deepening the analysis of the attack suite?
- Can the authors discuss the implications of their findings for real-world applications, and what steps can be taken to mitigate the privacy risks identified in the study?
