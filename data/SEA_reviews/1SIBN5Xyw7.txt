 **Summary:**
The paper introduces Meta-SpikeFormer, a novel Transformer-based Spiking Neural Network (SNN) architecture designed for neuromorphic computing. This architecture integrates a Conv-based and Transformer-based SNN block, aiming to reduce energy consumption and enhance performance. The authors propose a new spike-driven self-attention (SDSA) module, which is crucial for the network's functionality. Extensive experiments on various vision tasks such as image classification, event-based action recognition, object detection, and semantic segmentation demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed model. However, concerns were raised about the novelty of the SDSA module, the energy efficiency claims, and the clarity of the experimental results and methodology.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, easy to follow, and includes a comprehensive ablation study, which aids in understanding the impact of different components of the model.
- The proposed architecture, Meta-SpikeFormer, shows promising results across various vision tasks, achieving state-of-the-art (SOTA) performance on ImageNet-1K.
- The integration of a Conv-based and Transformer-based SNN block within Meta-SpikeFormer is innovative, potentially inspiring future neuromorphic chip designs.
- The paper includes a comprehensive evaluation on multiple vision tasks, demonstrating the versatility and effectiveness of the proposed model.

**Weaknesses:**
- The novelty of the SDSA module is questionable as it is based on existing self-attention mechanisms, and the energy efficiency claims are not convincingly supported.
- The paper lacks a detailed discussion on the energy efficiency of the proposed model, which is crucial for neuromorphic computing applications.
- The experimental results and methodology are not clearly presented, making it difficult to replicate or understand the results fully.
- There are inconsistencies in the presentation of results and methodologies across different sections of the paper, which could mislead readers.
- The paper does not sufficiently compare the proposed model with other state-of-the-art models, especially in terms of energy efficiency and computational complexity.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors clarify the novelty and energy efficiency of the SDSA module compared to existing self-attention mechanisms?
- How does the proposed model compare in terms of energy efficiency and computational complexity with other state-of-the-art models, especially in the context of neuromorphic computing?
- Can the authors provide a more detailed discussion on the energy efficiency of the proposed model, possibly including a comparison with other models in terms of energy consumption per operation?
- How does the proposed model handle the computational complexity of the SDSA module, and what are the implications for energy efficiency and computational speed?
- Could the authors provide a clearer explanation of the experimental results and methodologies, possibly including additional figures or diagrams to aid understanding?
