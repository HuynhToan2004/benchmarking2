 **Summary:**
The paper introduces MultiIoT, a benchmark dataset and framework designed for multimodal machine learning in IoT scenarios. MultiIoT encompasses 12 different modalities and 8 tasks, involving over 1.15 million samples, which are intended to provide a comprehensive testing ground for IoT applications. The dataset includes various IoT sensors such as IMU, thermal, GPS, and others, and the tasks range from gaze estimation to event detection and pose estimation. The paper presents an extensive evaluation of different machine learning approaches, including unimodal, multimodal, and multitask learning, and discusses the challenges and opportunities presented by the dataset. However, the paper is criticized for lacking novelty in its approach, insufficient comparative analysis with existing methods, and a lack of detailed experimental results and methodological clarity.

**Strengths:**
- The paper presents a comprehensive benchmark dataset, MultiIoT, which covers 12 different modalities and 8 tasks, offering a significant contribution to the research community.
- The dataset is diverse, including various IoT sensors such as IMU, thermal, GPS, and others, which can be used for various IoT applications.
- The paper provides a detailed analysis of different machine learning approaches, including unimodal, multimodal, and multitask learning, which can help researchers understand the performance and challenges of different methods.
- The dataset is well-documented, including detailed descriptions of each modality and task, which can aid in the reproducibility and understanding of the results.
- The paper discusses the challenges and opportunities presented by the dataset, which can inspire further research and development in the field of IoT.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks novelty in its approach, as it primarily focuses on evaluating existing machine learning methods without introducing significant new techniques or insights.
- There is a lack of detailed experimental results and comparative analysis with existing methods, which makes it difficult to assess the effectiveness and efficiency of the proposed methods.
- The paper does not provide sufficient details on the experimental setup, such as the hardware used, which could affect the reproducibility and reliability of the results.
- The paper does not adequately address the challenges of multimodal learning in IoT applications, such as the integration of data from different sensors and the handling of noisy and heterogeneous data.
- The paper does not discuss the limitations of the dataset, such as the potential biases in the data and the impact of these biases on the results.
- The paper could benefit from a more detailed discussion on the potential societal impacts of the research, including privacy concerns and ethical considerations.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors provide more detailed experimental results, including the specific hardware used and the computational resources required for the experiments?
- How does the paper address the challenges of multimodal learning in IoT applications, particularly the integration of data from different sensors and the handling of noisy and heterogeneous data?
- What are the specific challenges and limitations of using the MultiIoT dataset, and how do these affect the results and the applicability of the findings?
- How does the paper ensure the privacy and security of the data used in the experiments, especially given the potential for privacy violations in IoT applications?
- Could the authors discuss the potential societal impacts of their research, including privacy concerns and ethical considerations, and propose strategies for addressing these issues?
