 **Summary:**
The paper introduces DOMR, a novel post-training quantization method aimed at improving the accuracy of quantized models in low-bit settings. DOMR addresses two critical issues in PTQ: the setting of quantization steps for weights and the handling of outlier activations. The method decouples the quantization step into two parts, optimizing the dequantization step while keeping the quantization step fixed. Additionally, it introduces Outlier-Migrated Reconstruction to preserve outlier activations, which are typically clipped in standard PTQ methods. Extensive experiments demonstrate that DOMR outperforms existing methods, setting new state-of-the-art benchmarks in various settings.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel method, DOMR, which addresses the significant issue of outlier activation in PTQ, a problem that has not been thoroughly explored in previous literature.
- The proposed method is simple, effective, and well-explained, making it easy to understand and implement.
- Extensive experiments have been conducted, demonstrating the effectiveness of the proposed method across various models, bitwidths, and settings.
- The paper provides a clear and logical explanation of the methodology, with a focus on the practical implications of the proposed solutions.
- The results are impressive, showing significant improvements over existing methods in terms of accuracy and efficiency.
- The paper is well-written, making it accessible and easy to follow, which is crucial for the dissemination of the research.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a comprehensive comparison with other state-of-the-art methods, particularly in terms of accuracy and efficiency across different settings and models.
- There is a notable absence of discussion on the computational overhead and hardware requirements of the proposed method, which could limit its practical applicability.
- The method's effectiveness and applicability in different settings, such as with vision transformers and in high-bit settings, are not adequately explored.
- The paper could benefit from a more thorough analysis of the limitations and potential negative societal impacts of the proposed method.
- There are minor typographical and formatting errors that need to be corrected for clarity and professionalism.
