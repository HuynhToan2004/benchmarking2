 **Summary:**
The paper investigates the development of fair classifiers without utilizing fair training algorithms, which are typically dependent on sensitive attribute information. The authors propose an influence-guided sampling strategy to solicit labels from an unlabeled dataset, focusing on reducing fairness disparity and improving model accuracy. This strategy involves sampling data that significantly influences fairness and accuracy without requiring sensitive attribute information. Theoretical analysis and empirical results on datasets like CelebA, Adult, COMPAS, and Jigsaw demonstrate the effectiveness of this approach. However, concerns are raised about the novelty of the method, the clarity of the theoretical results, and the comparative analysis with existing methods.

**Strengths:**
- The paper addresses a significant and relevant problem in the field of machine learning, focusing on the development of fair classifiers without utilizing fair training algorithms, which is a novel approach.
- The theoretical analysis is sound, and the paper provides a clear and detailed description of the proposed method, which is well-suited for real-world scenarios.
- The empirical results demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed method, showing improvements in fairness metrics without compromising accuracy.
- The paper is well-written, making it easy to follow and understand the methodology and results.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a comprehensive literature review, particularly in the area of fairness in machine learning, which could have provided a more robust context for the research.
- The novelty of the method is questionable, as similar approaches have been previously explored in the literature, such as active learning and importance reweighting.
- The theoretical results are not clearly presented, making it difficult to understand the implications and contributions of the research.
- The paper does not adequately address the limitations of the proposed method, which could include potential biases introduced by the sampling strategy or the impact of labeling strategies on fairness outcomes.
- The paper could benefit from a more detailed discussion on the assumptions made in the theoretical analysis and how these assumptions affect the applicability and robustness of the results.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors provide a more detailed comparison of their method with existing works in the field of fairness in machine learning?
- How does the proposed method compare in terms of fairness and accuracy with other methods that do not require sensitive attribute information?
- Could the authors clarify the theoretical results and provide more intuitive explanations or examples to help understand the implications of the findings?
- How does the proposed method address the issue of distribution shift, and what are the potential biases or limitations introduced by the sampling strategy?
- In terms of the experimental setup, could the authors provide more details on the selection and validation of the auxiliary hold-out validation dataset and the impact of different labeling strategies on the fairness outcomes?
