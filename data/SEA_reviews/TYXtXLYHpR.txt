 **Summary:**
The paper introduces TIMEVIEW, a novel interpretable time series forecasting model that focuses on static features. TIMEVIEW employs a top-down approach to understand trajectory changes by defining motifs and compositions. It uses cubic splines to model time series trajectories and allows for visualization of how feature changes affect the trajectory. The model is compared against various baselines and demonstrates comparable performance with a slight increase in error. The paper also discusses the limitations of traditional bottom-up approaches and argues for the need for more interpretable models in high-stakes domains.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel approach to time series forecasting by focusing on static features, which is a less explored area in machine learning.
- The methodology is well-explained, with clear definitions and mathematical formulations, making it accessible to a broad audience.
- The paper is well-structured and easy to follow, with a clear motivation and a well-defined problem setup.
- The use of B-splines and the concept of motifs and compositions are innovative and provide a new perspective on time series forecasting.
- The paper includes a comprehensive set of experiments, demonstrating the effectiveness of the proposed method against various baselines.
- The visualization tool provided is a valuable addition, allowing for interactive exploration of the model's predictions and their impact on the trajectory.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper could benefit from a more detailed discussion on the choice of cubic splines and how they contribute to the model's effectiveness.
- There is a lack of clarity in the experimental setup, particularly in how the model is trained and the specifics of the experimental conditions.
- The paper could be improved by including more detailed explanations of the experimental results and comparisons with other models, especially in the context of real-world applications.
- The paper's claims regarding the superiority of the top-down approach over traditional methods are not convincingly supported by the experimental results.
- The paper could benefit from a more comprehensive discussion on the limitations and potential negative societal impacts of the proposed method.
- The paper's writing style is sometimes overly formal and could be improved by making it more accessible and reader-friendly.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors provide a more detailed explanation of the experimental setup, including the specifics of how the model is trained and the conditions under which it is tested?
- How does the model perform when using more complex time series models, such as those that use recurrent neural networks?
- Could the authors elaborate on the choice of cubic splines and how they contribute to the model's effectiveness?
- How does the model handle noise and other statistical fluctuations in the data?
- Could the authors provide more detailed comparisons with other models, particularly in terms of the model's performance and interpretability?
- How does the model's performance vary when using different sets of static features?
- Could the authors discuss the potential negative societal impacts of the proposed method, particularly in high-stakes domains like healthcare and finance?
- How does the model's interpretability translate into practical benefits, such as improved decision-making or increased user trust?
