 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel algorithm, LAIfO, for visual imitation learning from observations, focusing on the representation learning of latent states from video data. It builds on theoretical frameworks such as GAN-based adversarial imitation learning and off-policy adversarial imitation from observations. The algorithm's effectiveness is demonstrated through empirical results on the DeepMind Control Suite, showing comparable performance to state-of-the-art methods while reducing computational time. The paper also discusses the theoretical bounds of suboptimality and provides a detailed comparison with existing methods. However, concerns are raised about the clarity of the presentation, the need for a more comprehensive literature review, and the experimental setup, particularly the lack of detailed comparisons and the choice of environments.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, making it easy to follow, and the theoretical analysis is sound, providing a clear explanation of the problem and the proposed solution.
- The algorithm introduced, LAIfO, is simple and effective, and the results demonstrate that it can match state-of-the-art performance while reducing computational time.
- The paper provides a detailed comparison with existing methods, including LAIL, PatchAIL, and VMAIL, and shows that LAIfO can improve the efficiency of RL from pixels.
- The theoretical analysis is rigorous, and the experimental results are convincing, showing that the proposed method can handle the visual imitation learning from observations problem effectively.
- The paper is the first to study the visual imitation learning from observations problem, which is a significant contribution to the field.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a comprehensive literature review, missing several relevant works in the field of imitation learning from video.
- The experimental setup is not detailed enough, particularly the choice of environments and the lack of detailed comparisons with other methods.
- The paper could benefit from more detailed explanations of the methodology and the experimental setup, including a clearer description of the algorithm's components and the experimental conditions.
- The paper's claims about the novelty and effectiveness of the proposed method are somewhat overstated, and the theoretical analysis could be more rigorous, particularly in terms of the assumptions made and their justification.
- The paper suffers from a lack of clarity in some sections, making it difficult to understand the methodology and the experimental results fully.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors clarify the choice of environments and the rationale behind the specific experimental setup?
- How does the proposed method compare to other recent works in the field of imitation learning from video, particularly those that address similar problems?
- Can the authors provide more details on the computational efficiency of LAIfO compared to other methods, including the computational resources required?
- How does the feature extractor learn to map the latent state to the correct state, and what role does the discriminator play in this process?
- Could the authors elaborate on the assumptions made in the theoretical analysis, particularly the assumption that the expert and agent act on the same POMDP?
- How does the paper address the issue of partial observability, and what are the implications of this assumption on the practical applicability of the method?
