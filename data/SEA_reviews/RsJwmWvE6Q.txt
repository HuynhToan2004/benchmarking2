 **Summary:**
The paper investigates the problem of estimating residual errors in matrix and vector norms using linear sketches. It provides a theoretical analysis on the size of bilinear sketches required to estimate the residual error in Frobenius norm for matrices and in p-norms for vectors, improving upon existing bounds. The authors also provide a lower bound for the problem, demonstrating the optimality of their approach. The paper includes experimental validations on real-world datasets, showing the practical applicability of their theoretical findings. Despite the theoretical advancements, the paper is criticized for lacking detailed experimental results and a comprehensive comparison with existing methods.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written and easy to follow, with clear explanations and good presentation of the results.
- The authors provide a lower bound for the problem, which is a significant contribution to the field.
- The proposed algorithm is simple and effective, with a low sketching dimension that is optimal.
- The paper includes experimental results that validate the practical applicability of the proposed method.
- The theoretical results are sound, and the paper provides a significant improvement over existing bounds.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks detailed experimental results, particularly in terms of the quality of the sketches produced and the actual runtime of the proposed algorithm compared to existing methods.
- There is a lack of comparison with existing methods, which makes it difficult to assess the practical advantages of the proposed method.
- The paper does not sufficiently discuss the limitations of the proposed method, particularly in scenarios where the method may not be as effective.
- The paper could benefit from a more thorough discussion of the implications of the results, especially in terms of their practical applications and potential societal impacts.
- The paper does not adequately address the computational complexity of the proposed algorithm, which is a significant concern for large-scale applications.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors provide more detailed experimental results, including the actual runtime of the proposed algorithm compared to existing methods and the quality of the sketches produced?
- How does the proposed method compare in terms of computational complexity and practical applicability to existing methods, especially in large-scale applications?
- Could the authors elaborate on the potential societal impacts of their work and discuss any ethical considerations related to the use of the proposed method?
- How do the authors address the limitations of their method, particularly in scenarios where the method may not be as effective?
- Could the authors provide a more detailed discussion on the implications of their results, including potential applications and the broader impact of their work?
