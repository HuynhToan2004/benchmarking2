 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel method for generating high-resolution images from pre-trained diffusion models without requiring additional training. The method addresses the issue of repetition in generated images by dynamically adjusting the convolutional perception field during inference, using a re-dilation strategy. It also introduces dispersed convolution and noise-damped classifier-free guidance to enhance the quality of ultra-high-resolution images. The paper is well-written, with clear explanations and comprehensive experiments that demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed methods. However, concerns are raised about the novelty and clarity of the method's contributions, particularly in how it handles the re-dilation and dispersed convolution techniques.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, clear, and easy to follow, making it accessible to a broad audience.
- The proposed method is simple, effective, and does not require additional training, which is a significant advantage in terms of computational efficiency.
- The method achieves state-of-the-art performance on higher-resolution image synthesis, particularly in texture details.
- The paper provides extensive experiments and ablation studies that demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed components.
- The method can be applied to video generation models, which is a valuable extension of the research.

**Weaknesses:**
- The novelty of the method is questioned, with some reviewers suggesting that the components are not sufficiently distinct from existing techniques like fractional re-dilation and dispersed convolution.
- The paper lacks a detailed comparison with other methods, particularly those that address similar issues in high-resolution image generation.
- The method's application to text-to-image generation is limited, which could be expanded to include text-to-video applications.
- The paper could benefit from more visual results, especially in the supplementary material, to better demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed methods.
- The method's generalizability to different types of images and its performance on large-scale datasets like LAION-5B are not adequately addressed.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors clarify the novelty of the proposed components, particularly the re-dilation and dispersed convolution techniques, in relation to existing methods?
- How does the method perform when applied to different types of images, such as those with complex backgrounds or intricate details?
- Could the authors provide more detailed comparisons with other methods, including qualitative results and a more comprehensive discussion on the method's effectiveness?
- Is there a possibility to extend the method to handle text-to-video applications, and how might this affect the method's performance or applicability?
- How does the method perform on large-scale datasets like LAION-5B, and what are the implications of using such datasets for training and evaluation?
