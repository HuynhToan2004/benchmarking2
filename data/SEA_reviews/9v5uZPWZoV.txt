 **Summary:**
The paper explores the use of text-to-image (T2I) generators as a tool for interventional data augmentation (IDA) in the context of domain generalization. The study evaluates various T2I generators across diverse benchmarks, comparing their performance against traditional data augmentation methods. The authors investigate the impact of different T2I generation strategies, including interventional prompts, conditioning mechanisms, and post-hoc filtering. The paper concludes that T2I generators can effectively simulate arbitrary interventions over spurious variables, potentially improving the performance of neural image classifiers in covariate shift scenarios. However, the paper's methodology and results have been critiqued for their clarity, depth of analysis, and the strength of the conclusions drawn.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel approach by using text-to-image (T2I) generators for interventional data augmentation (IDA), which is a promising direction for improving the robustness of neural image classifiers.
- The study covers a wide range of benchmarks and demonstrates the effectiveness of T2I generators in domain generalization tasks, showing that they outperform traditional data augmentation methods.
- The paper provides a comprehensive evaluation of different T2I generation strategies, including various interventional prompts, conditioning mechanisms, and post-hoc filtering, which helps in understanding the impact of these strategies on IDA performance.
- The paper is well-written, making it easy to follow, and the results are clearly presented, enhancing the understanding of the experimental outcomes.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a clear motivation for why T2I generators are effective for IDA, and the methodology of applying T2I generators is not adequately explained.
- There is a significant gap in the analysis of the quality of the generated images, which is crucial for the effectiveness of IDA. The paper does not provide a detailed evaluation of the image quality, nor does it discuss how the image quality affects IDA performance.
- The paper does not sufficiently address the limitations of using T2I generators for IDA, such as the potential for generating low-quality images that could negatively impact performance.
- The paper's conclusions are considered too strong, and the results are not convincingly supported by the data presented. The experiments are not sufficiently robust, and the conclusions are not well-supported by the evidence provided.
- The paper does not adequately compare its findings with existing literature, particularly with works that have already demonstrated the effectiveness of T2I generators in IDA.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors clarify the motivation behind using T2I generators for IDA and how these generators are applied in the context of IDA?
- How does the paper ensure that the generated images meet the quality standards necessary for IDA? What metrics or criteria are used to evaluate image quality?
- Could the authors provide more detailed results on the impact of image quality on IDA performance? How does the quality of the generated images affect the performance of neural image classifiers?
- The paper claims that T2I generators can simulate arbitrary interventions over spurious variables. Can the authors provide a more detailed explanation of how this is achieved and what specific interventions are simulated?
- How does the paper address the potential limitations of using T2I generators for IDA, such as the generation of low-quality images or the inability to generate images with certain characteristics?
