 **Summary:**
The paper introduces Pixel-wise Gradient Clipping (PGC) to address the issue of uncontrolled pixel-wise gradients during the backpropagation of VAE encoder gradients in 3D generative models. This method aims to enhance the texture quality in high-resolution 3D object generation by regulating the gradients, thereby improving the synthesis quality. The proposed method has been tested across various pipelines, including LDM and SDS, and has shown consistent benefits in enhancing texture details and stabilizing optimization processes. However, the paper has been critiqued for its limited novelty and the lack of comprehensive comparisons with other methods, particularly in the context of high-resolution 3D object generation.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-organized, easy to read, and provides a clear explanation of the proposed method, which is supported by both theoretical analysis and experimental results.
- The proposed PGC is a simple yet effective method that can be applied to various pipelines, including LDM and SDS, and consistently benefits 3D generative models in high-resolution object rendering.
- The paper addresses the issue of uncontrolled pixel-wise gradients during the backpropagation of VAE encoder gradients, which is crucial for high-resolution 3D object generation.
- The authors have conducted experiments on multiple pipelines, including Stable-DreamFusion, Fantasia3D, and Zero123, which demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed method.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed comparison with other methods, particularly in the context of high-resolution 3D object generation, which limits the comprehensiveness of the evaluation.
- The novelty of the proposed method is limited as it primarily applies gradient clipping to pixel-wise gradients, which is a known technique.
- The paper does not provide a detailed analysis of the computational cost associated with the proposed method, which is crucial for understanding its practical applicability.
- The paper does not sufficiently address the limitations of the proposed method, which could include the potential loss of texture details due to the clipping operation.
- The paper could benefit from more diverse and representative examples in the results section to better demonstrate the generalizability of the proposed method.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors provide more details on the computational cost associated with the proposed method and how it compares to other methods in terms of efficiency?
- How does the proposed method compare with other gradient regularization techniques, such as those mentioned in the review?
- Could the authors provide more visual examples to demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed method across a wider range of scenarios?
- What is the specific impact of the PNGD method on the overall performance of the proposed method, and how does it compare to the PGC method?
- How does the proposed method perform when applied to different types of textures, such as natural textures or textures with complex patterns?
