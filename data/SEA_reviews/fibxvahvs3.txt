 **Summary:**
The paper introduces GAIA, a benchmark designed to evaluate the capabilities of AI systems in handling complex tasks requiring advanced reasoning, multi-modality, web browsing, and tool-use proficiency. GAIA consists of 466 questions with answers and associated metadata, and is structured into three levels of increasing difficulty. The benchmark is designed to challenge AI systems by requiring them to navigate the web and use tools to find answers, thereby avoiding the pitfalls of current benchmarks. The paper also discusses the challenges in creating and validating the benchmark, including the need for human annotation and the potential for data contamination. The results show that current AI systems perform poorly on GAIA, highlighting the need for further research and development in this area.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces GAIA, a novel benchmark for evaluating AI systems, which is designed to challenge AI systems by requiring complex reasoning, multi-modality handling, web browsing, and tool-use proficiency.
- The benchmark is structured into three levels of increasing difficulty, which provides a clear progression for AI systems to follow.
- The paper is well-written, clear, and easy to follow, making it accessible to a broad audience.
- The benchmark is designed to be robust against memorization and data contamination, which is a significant issue in current AI evaluations.
- The benchmark includes a diverse set of tasks, covering a wide range of real-world scenarios, which makes it a valuable tool for evaluating AI systems.
- The paper discusses the challenges in creating and validating the benchmark, including the need for human annotation and the potential for data contamination, which adds transparency and credibility to the benchmark.

**Weaknesses:**
- The benchmark is currently limited to English and may not be effective in evaluating AI systems in non-English languages or cultures.
- The benchmark requires human annotation, which can be time-consuming and expensive, and the paper does not discuss the potential impact of annotation errors on the benchmark's accuracy.
- The benchmark is designed to be challenging for AI systems, but it may not be comprehensive enough to evaluate all aspects of AI capabilities.
- The paper does not discuss the potential impact of annotation errors on the benchmark's accuracy, which could limit the reliability of the results.
- The paper does not include a discussion on the potential ethical implications of the benchmark, which could be a significant concern for some stakeholders.

- The paper does not discuss the potential for data contamination in the benchmark, which could affect the reliability of the results.
