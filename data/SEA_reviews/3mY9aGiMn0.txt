 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel sparse initialization scheme named Exact Orthogonal Initialization (EOI), which leverages random Givens rotations to ensure orthogonality in sparse networks. The method aims to enhance the performance and stability of sparse training by dynamically adjusting the sparsity of the network, allowing for arbitrary sparsity levels. The paper demonstrates that EOI can achieve dynamical isometry, which is crucial for training highly sparse networks, and compares its performance against various baselines and existing initialization methods. The experiments are conducted on both MLP and CNN architectures, showing that EOI can outperform other methods in terms of accuracy and stability.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel sparse initialization scheme, Exact Orthogonal Initialization (EOI), which leverages random Givens rotations to ensure orthogonality in sparse networks.
- The method provides exact orthogonality, which is a significant advancement over previous methods that only approximate isometry.
- The approach is compatible with both fully-connected and convolutional layers, and supports any density-per-layer, making it adaptable to contemporary networks and compatible with any density distribution.
- The paper demonstrates that EOI consistently outperforms other popular static sparse training approaches, as evidenced by the results presented in the paper.
- The paper is well-written, with clear explanations and comprehensive experimental results that support the effectiveness of the proposed method.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper does not include a discussion on the limitations of the proposed method, which could provide a more balanced view of its applicability and effectiveness.
- The paper could benefit from a more detailed comparison with other initialization methods, particularly those that have been recently proposed.
- The paper's claims about the stability of the learning process are not convincingly supported by the experimental results, which are not extensive enough to draw robust conclusions.
- The paper does not provide a clear explanation on how the method can be applied to large-scale NLP models, which are increasingly popular in the field.
- The paper's presentation could be improved to better highlight the novelty and contributions of the proposed method, particularly in the introduction and conclusion sections.
- The paper's experiments are limited to smaller networks, which might not adequately demonstrate the method's effectiveness on larger, more complex models.

**Questions:**
- How does the proposed method compare to other recently proposed initialization methods, such as those mentioned in the paper?
- Can the authors provide more details on the experimental setup, particularly how the sparsity level was chosen and how it affects the results?
- How does the method perform when applied to larger, more complex models, such as those used in large-scale NLP applications?
- Could the authors clarify the meaning of "dynamical isometry" and its relevance to the proposed method?
- How does the method handle the different sparsity levels across different layers, and what are the implications of this for the training process?
- Can the authors provide more detailed results on the computational efficiency of the method, particularly in comparison to other initialization methods?
