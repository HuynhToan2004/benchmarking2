 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel boundary-denoising training paradigm aimed at improving video activity localization by addressing the ambiguity in action boundaries. The proposed method, DenoiseLoc, uses an encoder-decoder model to refine learnable proposals and noisy ground truth spans, achieving state-of-the-art performance on several datasets including MAD and QV-Highlights. The approach involves adding noise to temporal spans during training to simulate uncertainty, which is then removed during inference. Extensive experiments and ablation studies are conducted to validate the effectiveness of the proposed method, demonstrating its superiority over existing methods in terms of performance and efficiency.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel boundary-denoising training approach for video activity localization, which is a significant advancement in the field.
- The proposed method, DenoiseLoc, is simple yet effective, achieving state-of-the-art performance on several datasets.
- The paper is well-written, clear, and easy to follow, with extensive ablation studies that validate the design choices and modeling properties of the approach.
- The method is efficient and effective, with a simple yet effective approach to address the challenge of uncertain action boundaries in video activity localization.
- The paper provides a comprehensive evaluation of the proposed method, including extensive experiments and ablation studies that validate the design choices and modeling properties of the approach.
- The method is generalizable to various video domains and can be easily integrated into existing video activity localization pipelines.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed explanation of the boundary-denoising training process and the specifics of how noise is added to the temporal spans.
- The paper does not include a comparison with the latest state-of-the-art methods, which could have provided a more robust evaluation of the proposed method.
- The paper does not discuss the limitations of the proposed method, which could provide valuable insights for future research.
- The paper does not include a discussion on the potential negative societal impacts of the proposed method, which is an important consideration for any technological advancement.
- The paper does not provide a detailed analysis of the computational complexity of the proposed method, which is crucial for understanding its practical applicability.
