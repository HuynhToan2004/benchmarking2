 **Summary:**
The paper introduces EDDPM, a novel diffusion model that employs probabilistic masking to accelerate the diffusion process by identifying and skipping redundant steps during training. This approach, which optimizes the number of steps required for diffusion, is demonstrated to be effective in both image synthesis and time series imputation tasks. The method involves assigning a mask to each diffusion step, which can be either 0 or 1, and optimizing these masks alongside the model parameters to minimize the loss. The paper also explores the use of sparse constraints to encourage convergence of the mask to a deterministic value, thus reducing the number of diffusion steps needed.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, with a clear presentation of the proposed method, and the experimental results are robust, demonstrating the effectiveness of the proposed method.
- The introduction of probabilistic masking to accelerate the diffusion process is a novel and interesting approach that has been validated through extensive experiments.
- The paper provides a comprehensive analysis of the proposed method, including the convergence of the mask to a deterministic value and the improved training efficiency.
- The method's ability to identify and skip redundant denoising steps during training is a significant advantage, and the experiments conducted on time series imputation and image synthesis tasks show promising results.
- The paper includes an ablation study that shows the effectiveness of the proposed method in model compression, demonstrating its potential as a practical tool for exploring large diffusion models with limited resources.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed discussion on the limitations of the proposed method, which could help in understanding its applicability and potential drawbacks.
- The comparison with other acceleration methods, such as PNDM, is insufficient, and the paper could benefit from a more comprehensive comparison to establish the superiority of the proposed method.
- The paper does not provide sufficient details on the implementation of the method, such as the specifics of the gradient computation and the handling of the discrete optimization problem.
- The paper could benefit from a more thorough discussion on the convergence of the probabilistic masking method and the potential impact of the masking process on the performance of the diffusion model.
- The paper does not adequately address the computational cost of the proposed method, particularly the training time and the number of epochs required, which could be a significant drawback for practical applications.
- There are minor typographical and formatting errors in the manuscript that need to be corrected.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors provide a more detailed comparison with other acceleration methods, such as PNDM, to establish the superiority of the proposed method?
- How does the proposed method compare in terms of computational cost, particularly in terms of training time and the number of epochs required, compared to other methods?
- Could the authors provide more details on the implementation of the method, particularly the gradient computation and the handling of the discrete optimization problem?
- Is there a possibility to extend the method to handle more complex tasks, such as image generation or other applications beyond time series imputation?
- How does the proposed method handle the potential for increased noise in the diffusion process due to the masking of steps?
- Can the authors clarify the convergence of the probabilistic masking method and its impact on the performance of the diffusion model?
