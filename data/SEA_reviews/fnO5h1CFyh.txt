 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel algorithm called Distributed Hebbian Temporal Memory (DHTM) for online hidden representation learning in decision-making under uncertainty in non-stationary, partially observable environments. DHTM utilizes a factor graph formalism and a multi-compartment neuron model to capture sequential data relationships and make cumulative predictions about future observations, forming successor representations. The algorithm is inspired by neurophysiological models of the neocortex and aims to address the instability and slow learning process of traditional temporal memory algorithms like RNN and HMM. The paper provides experimental results showing that DHTM outperforms classical LSTM and performs comparably to more advanced RNN-like algorithms, speeding up Temporal Difference learning for SR in changing environments.
