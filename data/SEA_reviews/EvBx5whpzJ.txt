 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel approach, Con4m, for time series classification (TSC) in blurred-segmented time series (BSTS) data. It addresses the challenges of label inconsistencies and contextual dependencies by integrating a label consistency training framework and contextual information from both data and class levels. The proposed method, Con4m, is evaluated on both public and private datasets, demonstrating superior performance over existing methods. The paper is structured around a comprehensive theoretical framework, including the use of information theory to justify the inclusion of contextual information, and extensive experiments to validate the proposed method's effectiveness.

**Strengths:**
- The paper addresses a significant and relevant problem in the field of time series classification (TSC), focusing on blurred-segmented time series (BSTS) which have been overlooked by existing TSC methods.
- The proposed method, Con4m, is innovative and well-explained, with a clear theoretical framework that supports its design and implementation.
- Extensive experiments are conducted on both public and private datasets, demonstrating the superior performance of Con4m compared to existing methods.
- The paper is well-written and easy to follow, with a clear motivation and detailed experimental setup.
- The inclusion of a label consistency training framework and the integration of contextual information at both data and class levels are highlighted as significant contributions to the field.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper could benefit from a more detailed discussion on the theoretical underpinnings of the proposed method, particularly the use of information theory and its practical implications.
- There are some issues with the clarity and explanation of certain technical aspects, such as the operation of the Con-Attention mechanism and the use of Gaussian prior weights.
- The experimental results are not always consistent across different datasets, and the paper lacks a comprehensive comparison with state-of-the-art methods, which could undermine the claimed superiority of Con4m.
- The paper could benefit from a more rigorous evaluation, including a broader range of datasets and metrics, and a more detailed analysis of the performance of the proposed method in different scenarios.
- The paper contains several typographical and formatting errors that need to be corrected for clarity and professionalism.

**Questions:**
- Can you provide a more detailed explanation of how the Con-Attention mechanism operates, particularly how it integrates the Gaussian prior weights and the contextual sample set?
- How does the proposed method handle the inconsistencies in labeling that are inherent in BSTS data?
- Could you clarify the operation of the discriminator used in the label consistency training framework, and how it contributes to the overall performance of the method?
- How does the paper address the issue of overfitting, particularly in the context of the curriculum learning approach used?
- Could you provide more details on the experimental setup, including the specifics of the datasets used and the metrics employed for evaluation?
- How does the proposed method compare to other state-of-the-art methods in terms of computational efficiency and scalability, particularly in large-scale applications?
