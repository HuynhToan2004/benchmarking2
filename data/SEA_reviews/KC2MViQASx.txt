 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel approach to estimating mutual information (MI) using a discriminative method based on f-divergence, which is a generalization of the KL divergence. This method, termed f-DIME, avoids the need to compute the partition function, which is a significant challenge in other MI estimators. The paper also explores the use of derangement strategies to enhance the estimator's performance by ensuring that the marginal distributions are correctly estimated. The authors demonstrate the effectiveness of their method through experiments on synthetic datasets and compare it with existing methods. The paper is well-structured and provides a clear theoretical analysis, although it could benefit from more extensive real-world dataset evaluations and a deeper discussion on the implications of the derangement strategy.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel approach to estimating mutual information (MI) using a discriminative method based on f-divergence, which avoids the need to compute the partition function.
- The authors provide a clear and concise explanation of the proposed method and its theoretical underpinnings, making the paper accessible and easy to follow.
- The paper includes a thorough analysis of the variance of the proposed estimator and its comparison with existing methods, demonstrating the effectiveness of the proposed method in terms of bias/variance trade-off.
- The paper is well-written, with clear and detailed explanations of the theoretical results and their implications, which are supported by extensive experiments on synthetic datasets.
- The authors have demonstrated the practical applicability of their method by showing that it can be used to estimate MI in real-world scenarios, such as in the context of generative adversarial networks (GANs).

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a discussion on the implications of the derangement strategy, particularly in terms of its impact on the computational efficiency and the robustness of the estimator.
- The experimental results are limited to synthetic datasets, which may not fully demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed method in real-world scenarios.
- The paper could benefit from a more comprehensive evaluation on real-world datasets to validate the generalizability and effectiveness of the proposed method in practical applications.
- The paper does not sufficiently address the issue of bias in the estimator, particularly when the true mutual information is large, which could limit the practical applicability of the method.
- The paper could be improved by including more detailed comparisons with other existing methods, such as those mentioned in related work, to better establish the novelty and effectiveness of the proposed method.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors provide more details on the computational efficiency and robustness of the derangement strategy, particularly in terms of its impact on the training and inference times of the estimator?
- How does the proposed method compare with other existing methods, such as those based on skew-divergence, in terms of bias and variance trade-offs?
- Can the authors provide more detailed experimental results on real-world datasets to validate the effectiveness of their method in practical applications?
- Could the authors clarify the role and impact of the derangement strategy on the performance and applicability of the estimator, especially in scenarios where the true mutual information is large?
- How does the proposed method handle the estimation of mutual information in high-dimensional data, which is a common challenge in real-world applications?
