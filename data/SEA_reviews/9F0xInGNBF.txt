 **Summary:**
The paper introduces VideoPrompter, a framework designed to enhance the zero-shot performance of existing vision-language models (VLMs) in video understanding tasks. It leverages a video-to-text model to generate descriptive video captions and a text-to-text model to provide class-specific language descriptions, which are then integrated into the VLM's text encoder for improved classification and retrieval. The framework demonstrates its effectiveness across various video tasks, including action recognition, video-to-text retrieval, and time-sensitive video tasks, showing improvements over existing methods. Despite its strengths, the paper is criticized for its incremental innovation and lack of extensive experimental validation, particularly in terms of its scalability and robustness against noisy data and diverse video styles.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, making it easy to understand and follow.
- The proposed VideoPrompter is a simple yet effective framework that enhances the zero-shot performance of existing VLMs for video understanding tasks.
- The framework is versatile and can be integrated into various existing VLMs, such as CLIP, ViFi-CLIP, Action-CLIP, and AIM.
- The methodology is straightforward and can be easily replicated, which is beneficial for practical applications.
- The paper demonstrates the effectiveness of the proposed VideoPrompter across multiple video tasks, including action recognition, video-to-text retrieval, and time-sensitive video tasks.
- The integration of LLMs into VLMs provides a novel approach to video understanding tasks, particularly in zero-shot scenarios.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a comprehensive ablation study on the weights of different components, which could provide deeper insights into the framework's performance.
- The experimental results are not convincing, with marginal improvements over existing methods and inconsistencies in performance across different datasets.
- The paper does not adequately address the scalability and robustness of the proposed method, especially in scenarios with noisy data or diverse video styles.
- The novelty of the proposed framework is limited, as it primarily combines existing models without significant innovation.
- The paper does not sufficiently compare the proposed method with state-of-the-art methods, which could undermine the perceived effectiveness of the framework.
- The method's reliance on LLMs for class descriptions may limit its applicability in scenarios where LLMs do not perform well or are not available.
- The paper does not provide a detailed analysis of the computational costs associated with the framework, which could be a significant concern for practical applications.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors provide more detailed experimental results, including a comparative analysis with state-of-the-art methods and a more comprehensive ablation study?
- How does the proposed framework handle noisy data or diverse video styles, and what are the potential impacts on its performance?
- Can the authors clarify the methodology used in the experimental setup, particularly the integration of LLMs and the handling of class descriptions?
- How does the proposed framework compare in terms of computational efficiency and cost-effectiveness with other existing methods?
- Can the authors provide more insights into the design choices made in the framework, particularly the integration of LLMs and the handling of class descriptions?
