 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel approach for hierarchical visual recognition, termed Hyperbolic Visual-Semantic Alignment (HVSA), which leverages hyperbolic space to model hierarchical relationships between images and semantic concepts. The method involves a joint optimization process that aligns visual and semantic features in a shared hyperbolic space, facilitating the capture of hierarchical relationships. Extensive experiments on various datasets demonstrate the effectiveness of HVSA, showing improvements over existing methods. However, the paper is criticized for its limited novelty and lack of clarity in the explanation of its methodology, particularly in the use of hyperbolic metric learning and the necessity of hyperbolic space for modeling hierarchical relationships.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, easy to follow, and provides clear explanations of the proposed method.
- The use of hyperbolic space for modeling hierarchical relationships is innovative and well-explained, particularly in the context of visual recognition.
- The methodology is supported by extensive experiments on various datasets, demonstrating its effectiveness in capturing hierarchical relationships.
- The paper introduces a novel approach to structural visual recognition by leveraging hyperbolic spaces to embed tree-like data structures, which enhances the capture of underlying hierarchical relationships.
- The proposed HVSA method achieves state-of-the-art performance on six datasets, demonstrating its effectiveness in both image-level and pixel-level visual recognition tasks.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a clear explanation of why hyperbolic space is necessary for modeling hierarchical relationships, and whether similar results could be achieved using other types of geometric spaces.
- The paper does not sufficiently compare its method with existing methods, particularly in terms of computational efficiency and the handling of hierarchical relationships in different types of data.
- There is a lack of discussion on the limitations of the proposed method, which could affect its practical application in real-world scenarios.
- The paper does not provide a detailed comparison with state-of-the-art methods, which could undermine the claim of its effectiveness.
- The method's reliance on hyperbolic metric learning for hierarchical feature space construction may not be necessary, and its impact on performance is not thoroughly analyzed.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors clarify the necessity of using hyperbolic space for modeling hierarchical relationships and discuss whether similar results could be achieved using other types of geometric spaces?
- How does the proposed method compare with other state-of-the-art methods in terms of computational efficiency and practical applicability?
- Could the authors provide more detailed comparisons with other methods, particularly in terms of the handling of hierarchical relationships in different types of data?
- In the ablation study, why was the performance of HVSA+ not reported?
- How does the proposed method perform in scenarios where the hierarchical structure is not well-defined or where there are multiple hierarchies?
- Could the authors provide more detailed insights into the computational efficiency of the proposed method and discuss potential limitations in practical applications?
