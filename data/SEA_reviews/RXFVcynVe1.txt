 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel approach to enhance representation learning on text-attributed graphs (TAGs) by leveraging large language models (LLMs) to generate explanations that are then transformed into informative features for Graph Neural Networks (GNNs). This method, termed "explanations as features," involves prompting an LLM to perform zero-shot classification and providing textual explanations, which are then translated into features using an LLM-to-LM interpreter. The approach demonstrates state-of-the-art performance on various TAG datasets and shows significant speedups over existing methods. Despite its innovative approach, the paper faces criticisms for its reliance on LLM's knowledge cutoff, lack of novelty in feature integration, and potential issues with label leakage and the complexity of the method.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, with clear and comprehensive explanations of the proposed method, making it easy to follow.
- The introduction of a novel approach using LLM explanations as features for TAG representation learning is a significant contribution to the field.
- The paper demonstrates state-of-the-art performance on several TAG datasets and shows significant speedups over existing methods.
- The methodology is sound, with thorough experiments that show the effectiveness of the proposed method.
- The paper provides open-source access to codes, pre-trained networks, and enriched features, which is beneficial for the research community.
- The integration of LLM with GNN for TAG representation learning is a novel and promising approach, showcasing the potential of large language models in enhancing graph neural networks.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed discussion on the novelty of the proposed approach, particularly in the context of existing methods such as GIANT and GLEM.
- The reliance on LLM's knowledge cutoff could limit the applicability of the method to certain datasets or scenarios.
- The method's complexity and the need for multiple components (LLM, LM, and GNN) might introduce additional computational costs and complexity.
- There is a potential for label leakage if the LLM has access to the dataset, which could affect the fairness and reliability of the results.
- The paper does not adequately address the scalability and efficiency of the proposed method, especially when compared to simpler methods like shallow features or GIANT.
- The method's performance on larger datasets or more complex tasks is not thoroughly evaluated, which could limit the generalizability of the results.

**Questions:**
- Could you provide more details on the scalability and efficiency of the proposed method, especially in comparison to simpler methods?
- How does the method perform on larger datasets or more complex tasks, and what are the potential limitations or challenges in these scenarios?
- Can you clarify the novelty of the proposed approach in comparison to existing methods, particularly in terms of feature integration and the use of LLMs for explanation generation?
- How does the method handle potential label leakage from the LLM, and what measures have been taken to ensure the fairness and reliability of the results?
- Could you elaborate on the design choices for the prompt, especially in terms of its effectiveness and the rationale behind its formulation?
- What are the potential applications of the proposed method beyond node classification tasks, and how does it compare to other methods in terms of computational resources and performance?
