 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel method for trajectory forecasting in autonomous driving by integrating kinematic models with deep learning techniques. The authors propose using kinematic models as priors to guide neural networks, focusing on the incorporation of kinematic dynamics into Gaussian Mixture Models (GMMs). This approach aims to improve the interpretability and physical feasibility of predicted trajectories, as well as enhance performance on benchmarks. The methodology is evaluated on the Waymo Motion Prediction Dataset and shows significant improvements over the baseline, particularly in scenarios with limited data availability. However, the paper's contributions are seen as incremental and the experimental setup and comparisons with state-of-the-art methods are criticized for their fairness and depth.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, easy to follow, and effectively communicates the motivation and key ideas.
- The methodology is simple, effective, and easy to implement, making it accessible for practical applications.
- The paper presents a novel approach by integrating kinematic models with deep learning techniques, which is a promising direction for future research.
- The empirical results show improvements over the baseline, demonstrating the effectiveness of the proposed method.
- The paper is well-structured, with clear explanations of the methodology and results, which are supported by comprehensive evaluations on benchmark datasets.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper's contributions are seen as incremental and not sufficiently novel, as it primarily applies existing kinematic models to GMMs without significant innovation.
- The experimental setup and comparisons are not fair or comprehensive, particularly in terms of model size and training parameters.
- The paper lacks a detailed discussion on the limitations of the proposed method, which could help in understanding its applicability and effectiveness in different scenarios.
- The paper does not sufficiently address the scalability and generalization of the method across different datasets and real-world scenarios.
- There is a lack of comparison with state-of-the-art methods, which could have provided a clearer picture of the method's performance relative to current research.
- The paper does not adequately discuss the potential negative societal impacts of the proposed method, which could be a significant concern in the context of autonomous driving systems.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors clarify the specific contributions of their work beyond the application of kinematic models to GMMs?
- How does the proposed method perform when compared to state-of-the-art models in terms of accuracy and computational efficiency?
- Could the authors provide more details on the experimental setup, particularly the training parameters and model sizes used in the comparisons?
- How does the method handle the scalability and generalization across different datasets and real-world scenarios?
- Could the authors discuss the potential limitations and negative societal impacts of the proposed method, and how these might be addressed or mitigated?
