 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel probabilistic rewiring method for message-passing graph neural networks (MPNNs) aimed at addressing the limitations of over-squashing and under-reaching. By utilizing a differentiable k-subset sampling method, the authors construct a distribution over adjacency matrices, allowing for the sampling of graphs with a specified number of edges. This approach is supported by theoretical analysis and extensive empirical evaluations, demonstrating the method's effectiveness in molecular property prediction tasks and its potential for alleviating over-smoothing in heterophilic datasets. The method's novelty lies in its ability to learn the probability of adding or removing edges, which is a departure from traditional methods that rely on heuristics or random sampling.
