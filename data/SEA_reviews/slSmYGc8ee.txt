 **Summary:**
The paper explores the impact of initial weight structures on learning dynamics in neural circuits, specifically focusing on the effective rank of weights. It examines how different weight initialization strategies, such as high-rank and low-rank, affect the network's learning regimes, with a focus on the Neural Tangent Kernel (NTK) alignment. The study employs both theoretical and empirical analyses, including experiments with recurrent neural networks (RNNs) and feedforward networks, to demonstrate how initial weight structures can influence learning outcomes. The paper also discusses the implications for biology, suggesting that the findings could be relevant to understanding neural network dynamics in biological systems.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, clear, and easy to follow, with a clear motivation and a well-defined problem.
- The theoretical framework is well-presented, and the results are supported by a range of experiments, including both theoretical and empirical results.
- The paper provides a thorough discussion on the implications of the findings, including potential applications to biological systems.
- The authors have effectively integrated a range of experiments to validate their theoretical findings, which are well-explained and easy to understand.
- The paper addresses an important and relevant question concerning the influence of initial weight structure on learning dynamics in neural circuits, which is of significant interest to the theoretical neuroscience community.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper's theoretical framework is limited to two-layer linear networks, which may not generalize well to more complex or non-linear networks.
- The experiments primarily focus on recurrent neural networks (RNNs) and do not include feedforward networks, which could provide a more comprehensive understanding of the effects of weight initialization.
- The paper's definitions and measurements of effective richness and laziness are not clearly defined or justified, making it difficult to understand the theoretical contributions fully.
- The paper lacks a detailed discussion on the implications of the findings for biological neural networks, which could have been more thoroughly explored.
- The paper does not adequately address the potential impact of different initialization methods, such as truncated SVD versus other methods, which could affect the conclusions drawn.
- The paper's presentation could be improved, particularly in terms of the clarity of the figures and the organization of the content.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors clarify the theoretical contributions of the paper, particularly how the findings extend beyond the two-layer linear network model used in the proofs?
- How do the authors justify the use of specific definitions and measurements of effective richness and laziness, and what are the implications of these definitions for the theoretical contributions?
- Could the authors elaborate on the potential implications of the findings for biological neural networks, and discuss how these findings might be applied in practice?
- How do the authors address the potential impact of different initialization methods on the conclusions drawn, and could they provide more details on the initialization methods used in their experiments?
- Can the authors provide more details on the computational requirements and limitations of their experiments, particularly in terms of the number of runs and the scalability of the results to larger or more complex networks?
