 **Summary:**
The paper explores the in-context learning (ICL) capabilities of large language models (LLMs) and introduces two algorithms, RICL and LARICL, to enhance the performance of LLMs in biased or imbalanced input prompts. These algorithms aim to approximate unbiased ICL by fine-tuning language models with an unbiased validation set, adjusting weights for each input-output example. The paper also discusses the convergence of these algorithms and provides empirical evidence of their effectiveness through experiments on numerical datasets. However, the paper's theoretical contributions are seen as limited, and the empirical evidence is not convincing enough to support the claims made.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, easy to follow, and provides a clear motivation for the research.
- The introduction of RICL and LARICL, which are simple yet effective algorithms, addresses the issue of biased or imbalanced input prompts in in-context learning.
- The paper includes a detailed discussion on the convergence of the proposed algorithms, which is crucial for understanding their reliability and effectiveness.
- The experiments conducted on numerical datasets demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed algorithms, showing improvements over other methods such as fine-tuning and prefix-tuning.
- The paper provides a theoretical analysis of the proposed algorithms, which is beneficial for understanding their underlying principles and potential applications.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a thorough discussion on related works, particularly on how the proposed methods differ from or build upon existing techniques in in-context learning and PEFT.
- The empirical evidence provided does not convincingly support the claims made about the superiority of the proposed algorithms over existing methods. The experiments are limited to a few datasets and do not include comparisons with more recent or diverse models.
- The paper does not adequately address the issue of noisy labels in in-context learning, which is a significant challenge in real-world applications.
- The theoretical contributions of the paper are limited, and the paper primarily relies on existing theoretical frameworks without significant novel insights or proofs.
- The paper's experimental setup and results are not sufficiently detailed or convincing, raising concerns about the validity and generalizability of the findings.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors provide more detailed explanations on how RICL and LARICL differ from or build upon existing techniques in in-context learning and PEFT?
- How do the proposed algorithms handle the issue of noisy labels in in-context learning?
- Could the authors provide more detailed results on the robustness of the algorithms to different types of noise and imbalances in the input prompts?
- How does the proposed method compare with other parameter-efficient fine-tuning methods, particularly in terms of computational efficiency and effectiveness?
- Can the authors clarify the experimental setup and results, particularly the choice of datasets and the methodology used to evaluate the algorithms?
