 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel approach to modeling dynamical systems using Mechanistic Neural Networks (MNNs), which generate explicit differential equations that represent the evolution of input data. The MNNs employ a differentiable ODE solver that leverages a quadratic programming formulation to solve batches of ODEs in parallel, which is claimed to be faster and more scalable than traditional methods. The approach is tested against existing methods such as SINDy and Neural ODEs, showing potential for handling complex systems. However, the paper is criticized for lacking a comprehensive comparison with existing methods, insufficient experimental validation, and unclear presentation in certain sections.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel approach to modeling dynamical systems using Mechanistic Neural Networks (MNNs), which represent the evolution of input data as explicit differential equations.
- The MNNs are designed to handle complex systems and can be scaled to solve large batches of ODEs in parallel, which is a significant advancement in the field.
- The proposed method shows promise in predicting the evolution of systems, potentially outperforming existing methods like Neural ODEs and SINDy in certain scenarios.
- The paper is well-written, clear, and structured, making it accessible and understandable to a broad audience.
- The introduction of a differentiable ODE solver using a quadratic programming formulation is a significant contribution to the field, enabling the efficient solution of batches of ODEs in parallel.
- The method is demonstrated to be effective in discovering governing equations, particularly in simple cases, which is a crucial aspect of understanding dynamical systems.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a comprehensive comparison with existing methods, particularly in terms of computational efficiency, scalability, and accuracy.
- The experimental validation is insufficient, with some experiments being too simple or not adequately demonstrating the advantages of the proposed method over existing approaches.
- The paper does not sufficiently discuss the limitations of the proposed method, which is crucial for understanding its applicability and potential drawbacks.
- The presentation of the paper could be improved, particularly in sections where the content is overly complex or the writing is unclear.
- The paper does not adequately address the issue of identifiability, which is a significant concern in learning dynamical systems from data.
- There is a lack of discussion on the limitations of the method, such as the inability to handle high-dimensional systems or the impact of noise on the learning process.
- The paper does not provide a detailed discussion on the computational complexity of the proposed method, which is crucial for understanding its practical applicability.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors provide a more detailed comparison of the computational efficiency and scalability of their method against existing approaches?
- How does the proposed method compare in terms of accuracy and robustness to other methods, particularly in more complex scenarios?
- Could the authors discuss the limitations of their method in more detail, particularly in terms of its applicability and potential drawbacks?
- How does the proposed method handle high-dimensional systems, and what are the implications of this limitation?
- Could the authors provide more details on the identifiability of the learned equations, and how this is ensured or verified in their method?
- Is there a discussion on the impact of noise on the learning process, and how this is addressed in the proposed method?
- Can the authors provide a more detailed discussion on the computational complexity of their method, and how this impacts its practical applicability?
