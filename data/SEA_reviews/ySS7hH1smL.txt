 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel approach to multilingual machine translation (MMT) using a Sparse Mixture-of-Experts (SMoE) framework, named Lingual-SMoE. This method leverages linguistic hierarchy and language complexity to enhance the performance of SMoE in MMT. It employs a hierarchical routing policy that considers language family, language, and token information to guide expert selection, aiming to improve the handling of diverse languages and their complexities. The paper also proposes a dynamic expert allocation mechanism to adjust the number of experts based on the difficulty level of translations. Extensive experiments on various MMT datasets demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed method.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel approach to multilingual machine translation (MMT) by integrating linguistic hierarchy and language complexity into the SMoE framework, which is a significant advancement in the field.
- The proposed method, Lingual-SMoE, demonstrates superior performance over existing methods, including a dense baseline, as shown in extensive experiments across various datasets.
- The paper is well-written, with clear explanations and visualizations that effectively illustrate the advantages of the proposed method.
- The use of linguistic information in routing decisions is a novel and promising approach that could be applied in other NLP tasks, showcasing the paper's potential impact on the field.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed comparison with state-of-the-art models, which could provide a clearer picture of the proposed method's performance relative to current leading methods.
- The paper does not sufficiently discuss the computational complexity or the scalability of the proposed method, which are crucial for practical implementation in real-world scenarios.
- There is a lack of clarity in some experimental results and methodological details, which could benefit from further explanation or clarification to enhance the paper's comprehensibility.
- The paper does not sufficiently address the potential negative societal impacts of the proposed method, which should be considered in future research.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors provide a detailed comparison with state-of-the-art models to better understand the proposed method's performance relative to current leading methods?
- How does the proposed method handle computational complexity and scalability, especially in large-scale MMT applications?
- Could the authors provide more detailed experimental results, possibly including a comparison with other routing methods like those mentioned in references [1-3]?
- How do the authors ensure the robustness of the linguistic routing in scenarios where linguistic information is not sufficient or misleading?
- Is there a plan to release the code and datasets used in the experiments to facilitate reproducibility and further research in the community?
