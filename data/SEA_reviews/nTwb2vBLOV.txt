 **Summary:**
The paper explores the integration of graph canonization techniques into Graph Neural Networks (GNNs) to enhance their expressivity and stability. It introduces a novel approach termed "universal graph canonization" to address the trade-off between expressivity and stability, providing a sufficient condition for its application across various graph datasets. The paper presents a theoretical analysis of the expressive power and stability of GNNs with graph canonization, alongside experimental results demonstrating the method's effectiveness in real-world applications. Despite its innovative approach, the paper faces criticism for its limited novelty and insufficient experimental validation on large-scale datasets.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel approach to enhancing the expressivity of GNNs through graph canonization, which is a significant contribution to the field.
- The theoretical analysis provided is robust and comprehensive, effectively demonstrating the trade-off between expressivity and stability in GNNs.
- The methodology is clearly articulated, making the paper accessible and understandable to readers.
- The paper includes a thorough discussion on the potential applications of the proposed method, which is well-received.
- The paper is well-organized, with clear definitions and well-written sections, which aids in understanding the complex concepts presented.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed discussion on the scalability of the proposed method, particularly in large-scale datasets, which is a significant concern.
- The novelty of the method is questioned as similar approaches have been previously explored, such as the use of graph canonization in GNNs.
- The experimental results are limited to smaller datasets, which may not adequately demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed method in real-world scenarios.
- The paper does not provide a detailed comparison with state-of-the-art methods, which could have strengthened the argument for the superiority of the proposed method.
- There is a lack of clarity in some sections of the paper, particularly in the definitions and explanations of certain terms and concepts, which could confuse readers.
- The paper does not address the potential negative societal impacts of the proposed method, which is a critical consideration in current research.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors provide more details on the scalability of the proposed method, particularly in terms of its applicability to large-scale datasets?
- How does the proposed method compare with existing state-of-the-art methods in terms of expressivity and stability?
- Could the authors elaborate on the experimental setup and results, particularly in the application scenarios where the method shows significant improvements?
- How does the proposed method address the issue of scalability, especially in large-scale datasets?
- Can the authors clarify the definitions and concepts used in the paper, particularly those that are not well-explained or that are confusing?
- What are the potential negative societal impacts of the proposed method, and how does the paper address these concerns?
