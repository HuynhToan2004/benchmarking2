 **Summary:**
The paper introduces MiniFold, a streamlined protein structure prediction model that aims to balance accuracy with computational efficiency. By simplifying the architecture of Evoformer, MiniFold reduces the number of parameters and computational complexity, thereby improving inference speed and memory efficiency. The model's performance is evaluated against existing methods such as AlphaFold2 and ESMFold, demonstrating a significant speedup and memory savings. However, the paper's novelty is questioned due to its reliance on known techniques and the limited improvement over existing models. Concerns are also raised about the fairness of the comparisons, particularly in the training data and methodological approaches used.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces MiniFold, a simplified architecture for protein structure prediction that offers a balance between accuracy and efficiency.
- The authors have conducted a thorough analysis of the computational bottlenecks in Evoformer, which led to the development of MiniFold.
- The paper presents a hardware-optimized implementation of MiniFold, which includes novel GPU kernels designed to enhance both throughput and memory efficiency.
- The methodology of MiniFold is clearly described, making it easy to understand and replicate.
- The paper demonstrates the effectiveness of the proposed approach through experiments, showing that MiniFold achieves a significant speedup and memory savings compared to existing methods like ESMFold.
- The paper is well-written and provides a clear motivation for the research, which is crucial for understanding the relevance and importance of the study.

**Weaknesses:**
- The novelty of the paper is limited as the proposed techniques, such as the simplified architecture and GPU kernels, are already well-known in the field.
- The performance of MiniFold is inferior to existing methods like ESMFold, raising concerns about its effectiveness.
- The paper does not adequately compare MiniFold with other state-of-the-art methods like RosettaFold and OmegaFold, which could have provided a more comprehensive evaluation.
- The paper's claims of novelty and performance improvement are not convincingly supported by the experimental results, which are sometimes inconsistent or misleading.
- The methodology used in the paper, such as the training data and the use of recycling in ESMFold, is not clearly justified or explained, which could affect the validity of the results.
- The paper lacks a detailed discussion on the limitations of the proposed method, which is crucial for understanding the scope and applicability of the findings.

**Questions:**
- Could you provide more details on the training data used for MiniFold, particularly how it compares to the data used for ESMFold?
- How does the performance of MiniFold compare when using the same training data as ESMFold?
- What are the specific advantages of MiniFold over ESMFold, especially in terms of accuracy and efficiency?
- How does MiniFold handle the absence of multiple sequence alignments (MSA) in its input, which is a significant difference from ESMFold?
- Could you clarify the role of recycling in ESMFold and its impact on the performance of MiniFold?
- In light of the concerns about the novelty and performance of MiniFold, how do you justify the claim that it represents a significant advancement over existing methods?
- Can you provide more details on the hardware used for the experiments and how it might affect the results?
- How does MiniFold perform on larger datasets or more complex protein structures?
