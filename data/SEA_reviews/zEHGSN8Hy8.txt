 **Summary:**
The paper introduces SetCSE, a novel framework for information retrieval that leverages sets to represent complex semantics and employs contrastive learning to enhance sentence embeddings. SetCSE includes operations such as intersection, difference, and series operations, which enable structured information querying within a given context. The framework is evaluated against existing methods like BM25, TFIDF, and DPR, demonstrating its effectiveness in complex semantic search tasks. The authors also discuss potential applications in areas like data annotation and new topic discovery. Despite the innovative approach, concerns were raised about the limited experimental evaluation and the lack of comparative analysis with other methods.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel approach using sets to represent complex semantics, which is a significant departure from traditional methods in the field of information retrieval.
- The use of contrastive learning to enhance sentence embeddings is a novel and effective method, as demonstrated by the results presented in the paper.
- The proposed SetCSE operations, such as intersection, difference, and series operations, are well-defined and could be useful in various applications.
- The paper is well-written, making it easy to follow and understand the proposed methodologies and results.
- The experimental results show that SetCSE can significantly improve the discriminatory capability of underlying language models, indicating its effectiveness in complex semantic search tasks.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a comprehensive evaluation of SetCSE operations, particularly in terms of their performance and effectiveness in real-world scenarios.
- There is a limited comparison of SetCSE with other methods, such as traditional retrieval models like BM25 and TFIDF, and other recent approaches in the field.
- The paper does not sufficiently discuss the limitations of the proposed method, which could help in understanding its applicability and potential drawbacks.
- The presentation of the paper could be improved, particularly in terms of clarity and organization, especially in the experimental results section.
- The paper does not sufficiently discuss the potential negative societal impacts of the proposed method, which is an important consideration for ethical AI applications.

**Questions:**
- Could you provide more details on the experimental setup and results for the SetCSE series operations, as these are not thoroughly discussed in the paper?
- How does the proposed method compare to other recent approaches in the field of information retrieval, such as those using transformer models?
- Can you provide a more detailed discussion on the limitations of the proposed method and how these might impact its practical application?
- How does the proposed method handle the potential negative societal impacts, particularly in terms of bias and fairness in information retrieval?
- Could you clarify the definitions and usage of certain terms and operations, such as the "preceq" operation and the SetCSE operations in general?
