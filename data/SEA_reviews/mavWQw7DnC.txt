 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel approach to explaining recommendations in recommender systems using contrapositive logic. The authors propose a method that leverages contrapositive explanations to identify why a user was recommended a particular item, focusing on the logical equivalence between the recommendation and the user's interaction history. The approach is evaluated against existing methods such as counterfactual explanations and influence functions, showing superior performance in terms of computational efficiency and explanation quality. The paper also introduces a new evaluation metric specifically tailored for contrapositive explanations. Despite its innovative approach, the paper faces criticism for its limited novelty and the lack of comprehensive experimental validation.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel approach to explaining recommendations using contrapositive logic, which is both theoretically sound and practically applicable.
- The methodology is well-explained, making it accessible to readers and demonstrating its applicability across different recommendation models, including SVD and MLP-based models.
- The paper is well-written, with clear explanations of the proposed method and its evaluation, which enhances understanding and reproducibility.
- The contrapositive approach offers a unique perspective on explaining recommendations, which could be of interest to the ICLR community.
- The paper includes a new evaluation metric tailored for contrapositive explanations, which provides a fresh perspective on assessing the effectiveness of explanation methods in recommender systems.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a comprehensive experimental validation, with only limited datasets used for evaluation, which might not adequately demonstrate the generalizability of the proposed method.
- The novelty of the approach is questionable, as it appears to be a simple adaptation of existing methods such as influence functions, without significant innovation.
- The paper does not sufficiently differentiate its method from other recent works on counterfactual backtracking, which could lead to confusion about the true novelty and contribution of the research.
- The experimental results are not convincingly superior to existing methods, and the paper does not adequately address the limitations of its method, particularly in terms of computational complexity and the need for retraining.
- The paper could benefit from a more comprehensive literature review and a clearer delineation of its contributions to the field of explainable AI.

**Questions:**

- How does the contrapositive approach compare in terms of computational complexity and efficiency to other methods like influence functions, especially in large-scale recommender systems?
- Is there a possibility to extend the contrapositive approach to other types of recommender systems beyond those based on user-item interactions?

- Can you provide more details on the experimental setup, such as the specifics of the datasets used and the methodology for evaluating the contrapositive explanations?
- How does the paper address the limitations discussed, particularly in terms of the generalizability of the findings and the need for retraining in the contrapositive approach?
