 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel approach to causal dynamics modeling in reinforcement learning (RL) by leveraging local independence relationships between variables. This method involves a vector quantization technique to learn a discrete latent variable representing pairs of events and causal relationships specific to those events. The approach is theoretically grounded and demonstrates robustness and generalization in RL, showing superior performance in downstream tasks compared to prior methods. The authors claim that their method captures event-specific causal relationships, which is crucial for enhancing the robustness and generalization of model-based RL. The paper is well-written and presents a clear structure, although it lacks a comprehensive discussion on the limitations and potential negative societal impacts.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel approach to causal dynamics modeling in reinforcement learning, focusing on local independence relationships between variables.
- The methodology is well-structured and clearly presented, with a logical flow that enhances understanding.
- The proposed method demonstrates robustness and generalization in RL, as evidenced by experimental results showing superior performance compared to prior approaches.
- The paper provides a theoretical foundation for the proposed method, which is crucial for establishing its credibility and applicability.
- The use of vector quantization to learn a discrete latent variable that represents pairs of events and event-specific causal relationships is a novel and interesting approach.
- The experiments conducted are comprehensive and well-designed, supporting the claims made in the paper.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed discussion on the limitations of the proposed method, which is crucial for a comprehensive understanding of its applicability and potential drawbacks.
- The method's reliance on a predefined number of events (K) could limit its adaptability in real-world scenarios where the number of events might not be known in advance.
- There is a lack of discussion on the potential negative societal impacts of the proposed method, which is an essential consideration for any technological innovation.
- The paper does not provide a detailed comparison with existing methods in terms of computational efficiency and scalability, which are crucial factors for practical implementation.
- The paper does not provide code for the proposed method, which could hinder reproducibility and further validation of the results.
- The paper does not discuss the generalizability of the method to more complex scenarios, which could limit its applicability in real-world scenarios.
