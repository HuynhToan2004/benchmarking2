 **Summary:**
The paper introduces UpFusion, a novel view synthesis method that leverages a pre-trained UpSRT model to generate novel views from unposed sparse views without the need for explicit pose information. UpFusion utilizes a conditional diffusion model to generate high-quality novel views and optimizes an instance-specific neural representation to ensure 3D consistency. The method is evaluated on the Co3Dv2 dataset, demonstrating improvements over traditional methods that rely on explicit poses. The paper also introduces new metrics to account for coordinate mismatch and discusses the limitations and potential future work.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, clear, and easy to follow, with a clear motivation and well-designed methodology.
- The proposed method shows good performance and generalization ability, especially in the unposed sparse view setting, and can be applied to any pre-trained UpSRT model.
- The use of a conditional diffusion model for novel view synthesis is novel and interesting, and the paper introduces new metrics for evaluating unposed sparse view synthesis.
- The results demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed method, particularly in generating high-quality novel views from unposed sparse views.
- The paper is original in its approach, using a conditional diffusion model to generate novel views from unposed sparse views, which is a significant advancement in the field.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed discussion on the limitations of the method, particularly concerning the accuracy of the generated 3D consistency and the potential failure cases.
- The method's reliance on the UpSRT model for generating view-aligned decoder features might limit its applicability to other datasets or scenarios.
- The use of a conditional diffusion model for novel view synthesis is not entirely novel, as similar approaches have been previously explored.
- The paper does not sufficiently discuss the novelty of the method, particularly in its use of a conditional diffusion model for novel view synthesis, which has been previously explored.
- The paper does not adequately compare its method with state-of-the-art methods, such as those mentioned in related works, which could have strengthened the paper's claims.
- The paper does not provide sufficient implementation details, which could hinder reproducibility and further research.

**Questions:**

- Could the authors discuss the potential applications of this method beyond novel view synthesis, such as in other areas of computer vision or machine learning?

- Can the authors provide more details on the training and evaluation of the UpSRT model, particularly in terms of the datasets used and the specifics of the training process?
