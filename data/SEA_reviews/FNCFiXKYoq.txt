 **Summary:**
The paper explores the integration of adversarial debiasing with differential privacy (DP) to address fairness and privacy in machine learning models. It extends the adversarial debiasing framework to handle multiple sensitive attributes, leveraging a gradient-based learning algorithm with DP constraints. The approach aims to maintain fairness and privacy by ensuring that the model does not rely on sensitive attributes. The paper presents empirical results across various datasets, comparing different model configurations including non-private, DP-only, and DP+debiasing. Despite the innovative approach, the paper suffers from several issues such as lack of theoretical analysis, unclear presentation, and limited novelty in its methodology.

**Strengths:**
- The paper addresses a significant and timely issue of balancing fairness and privacy in machine learning models, which is crucial for ethical AI development.
- The integration of adversarial debiasing with differential privacy (DP) is novel and innovative, offering a new approach to handling multiple sensitive attributes.
- The paper is well-structured and easy to follow, making it accessible to readers.
- The experiments are extensive, covering various datasets and model configurations, which helps in validating the proposed method.
- The paper is the first to combine adversarial debiasing with DP, which is a significant contribution to the field.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a clear explanation of the methodology, particularly the adversarial training and the role of the adversary in the system.
- There is a significant absence of theoretical analysis, which could strengthen the paper's claims and provide a deeper understanding of the proposed method.
- The presentation of the paper needs improvement, with several sections being unclear or hard to follow, such as the introduction and the methodology section.
- The novelty of the method is limited as it primarily extends existing methods to handle multiple sensitive attributes without significant innovation.
- The experimental results are not convincing, with some methods like DP+debiasing showing inferior performance compared to non-private or debiasing-only models.
- The paper does not adequately address the limitations of its method, which could undermine the credibility of the proposed solution.

**Questions:**
- Could you clarify the role and function of the adversary in the system, particularly how it affects the fairness and privacy outcomes?
- How does the proposed method handle different fairness definitions, such as demographic parity or equal opportunity?
- Can you provide more details on the theoretical foundations of your method, especially how it ensures privacy and fairness under different conditions?
- How does the method perform under varying privacy budgets, and what are the implications for fairness and performance?
- Could you elaborate on the choice of datasets and the specific model configurations used in your experiments?
- How does the method compare to other existing methods in terms of fairness, privacy, and performance?
- Can you provide a more detailed explanation of the experimental results, particularly the discrepancies observed between different model configurations?
