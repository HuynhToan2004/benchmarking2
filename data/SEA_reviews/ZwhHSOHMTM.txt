 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel approach for analyzing neural data from Caenorhabditis elegans (C. elegans) to infer dynamic functional connectomes. This methodology involves the use of non-negative tensor factorization (NTF) to identify neuronal communities and their interactions over time. The approach is validated through experimental results that demonstrate its ability to predict neuronal roles and behaviors, such as the involvement of the aversive olfactory neuron AWB in salt sensing. The paper also includes a comparative analysis with other community detection methods and benchmarks against synthetic datasets. Despite its innovative application to neural data analysis, the paper is critiqued for its limited scope to C. elegans, lack of detailed comparative analysis with other methods, and potential overclaiming of its applicability to other domains.
