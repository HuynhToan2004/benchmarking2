 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel method for learning invariant neural networks through a combination of network pruning and contrastive learning. The proposed framework, termed IUNet, aims to discover invariance-preserving subnetworks by pruning weights and applying proactive initialization to prevent important weights from being pruned. This method is demonstrated to outperform dense neural networks on both vision and tabular datasets, showcasing its effectiveness and efficiency. The paper also addresses the challenge of designing invariant neural networks, which is crucial for various applications where invariance is essential. However, the experimental results are limited to smaller datasets, raising concerns about the method's scalability and applicability to more complex scenarios.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written and easy to follow, with clear explanations of the proposed methodologies.
- The combination of network pruning and contrastive learning is novel and provides a practical approach to discovering invariance-preserving subnetworks.
- The experiments conducted on both vision and tabular datasets demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed method in improving compression performance and maintaining invariance.
- The paper addresses a significant challenge in the field of neural networks by proposing a method that automatically discovers invariance-preserving subnetworks, which is a critical aspect of neural network design.
- The methodology is supported by both theoretical analysis and empirical results, which validate its effectiveness and efficiency.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed discussion on how the proposed method compares with other existing methods in terms of invariance learning, particularly in the context of neural network architectures.
- The experimental results are limited to smaller datasets, which may not fully demonstrate the scalability and applicability of the proposed method to more complex scenarios.
- The paper does not sufficiently address the limitations of the proposed method, which could include the dependency on specific types of invariance and the potential for overfitting due to the use of contrastive learning.
- The paper does not provide sufficient details on the hyperparameters used in the experiments, which could affect the reproducibility and comparability of the results.
- The paper does not discuss the limitations of the proposed method in detail, which could help in understanding its applicability and potential drawbacks.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors provide a more detailed discussion on how the proposed method compares with other existing methods in terms of invariance learning, particularly in the context of neural network architectures?
- How does the proposed method perform on larger, more complex datasets? Are there any limitations in terms of scalability or applicability to different types of data?
- Can the authors provide a more detailed analysis of the invariances learned by the proposed method, possibly through visualizations or other means?
- How does the proposed method handle different types of invariances, and what are the implications for the learned representations?
- Could the authors clarify the role of proactive initialization in preventing important weights from being pruned? Is there a possibility that this could lead to overfitting, especially in scenarios where the training data is limited?
- How does the proposed method compare with other pruning methods that are not based on contrastive learning? What are the advantages and disadvantages of using contrastive learning in this context?
