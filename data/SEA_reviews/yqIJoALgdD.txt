 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel reversible spiking neuron designed to reduce memory consumption during training in Spiking Neural Networks (SNNs). This neuron model, which is based on a reversible architecture, allows for the efficient storage of intermediate states, thereby reducing memory usage and computational overhead. The proposed method is evaluated against various state-of-the-art SNN training techniques and demonstrates significant improvements in memory efficiency and computational efficiency. The paper also provides a detailed analysis of the memory and computational complexity, supported by experimental results on standard datasets.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel reversible spiking neuron that significantly reduces memory usage compared to existing spiking neurons, achieving a 58.65x reduction in memory usage.
- The proposed method is supported by a detailed analysis of memory complexity, which is crucial for understanding the scalability of the proposed method.
- The paper is well-written, making it easy to follow, and the proposed method is simple and effective.
- The authors provide a detailed analysis of the memory complexity and computational complexity of the proposed method, which is supported by experimental results on standard datasets.
- The paper is well-motivated and addresses the significant problem of memory usage in Spiking Neural Networks (SNNs), which is a critical issue for deploying SNNs in resource-limited environments.
- The paper is well-organized, with clear figures and detailed experimental results, including a detailed ablation study that supports the proposed method.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a thorough comparison with other existing methods that claim to reduce memory usage in SNN training, such as OTTT and ND.
- The paper does not provide a detailed comparison of the training time with other methods, which is crucial for understanding the practical implications of the proposed method.
- The paper does not discuss the scalability of the proposed method with respect to the number of layers and timesteps, which is a significant limitation given the scalability claims made by the authors.
- The paper does not adequately address the limitations of the proposed method, particularly the potential impact of the method on the performance of SNNs, especially when the number of layers and timesteps increases.
- The paper lacks a comprehensive comparison of the proposed method with other existing methods, which could provide a more robust evaluation of the proposed method's effectiveness and efficiency.
- The paper does not provide a detailed discussion on the computational complexity of the proposed method, which is crucial for understanding the practical implications of the method in real-world applications.
