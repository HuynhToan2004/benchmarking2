 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel confidence refinement scheme for semi-supervised semantic segmentation, leveraging spatial correlations in segmentation maps to improve pseudo-label quality. The approach, termed S4MC, groups neighboring pixels and considers their pseudo-labels collectively, refining confidence levels and enabling the use of more unlabeled data. The method, evaluated on multiple benchmarks including PASCAL VOC 12, Cityscapes, and MS COCO, shows significant improvements over state-of-the-art methods. Despite its strengths, the paper is critiqued for its lack of comparison with certain baseline methods and for not sufficiently addressing the generalization of its approach to other dense prediction tasks.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, making it easy to follow and understand.
- The proposed method is intuitive, simple, and effective, with a clear and detailed algorithm description.
- The method achieves state-of-the-art performance on several benchmarks, demonstrating its effectiveness.
- The authors provide extensive experiments and ablation studies, which support the effectiveness of the proposed method.
- The use of marginal contextual information to refine pseudo-labels is a novel approach that enhances the quality of pseudo-labels.
- The paper is well-organized, with clear figures and tables that aid in understanding the proposed method.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed comparison with some baseline methods, such as PCR and PS-MT, which could have provided a more comprehensive evaluation.
- The paper does not sufficiently address the generalization of its approach to other dense prediction tasks, such as object detection and depth estimation.
- The method's reliance on event-union relaxation and fixed-shape neighborhoods may limit its applicability in scenarios where spatial coherency is not a primary concern.
- The paper does not adequately discuss the computational overhead of the proposed method, which could be a significant concern for practical applications.
- The paper could benefit from a more thorough discussion on the limitations of the proposed method and its potential negative societal impacts.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors clarify the choice of using a 3x3 neighborhood for refining pseudo-labels? How was this size determined?
- In the context of the Dynamic Partition Adjustment (DPA) module, how is the α0 value tuned, and what is the rationale behind this tuning?
- How does the proposed method perform in scenarios where the spatial coherency is not a primary concern, such as in object detection or depth estimation?
- Could the authors provide a more detailed discussion on the potential negative societal impacts of their work?
- What is the computational overhead of the proposed method, and how does it compare to other methods in terms of computational efficiency?
- Could the authors elaborate on the motivation behind the use of event-union relaxation and the fixed-shape neighborhood, and discuss how these choices impact the generalization of the method?
