 **Summary:**
The paper introduces Acoustic Prompt Tuning (APT), a novel approach that extends large language models (LLMs) and visual language models (VLMs) to the audio domain without requiring fine-tuning. APT uses a sequence-to-sequence approach, where audio feature maps are transformed into acoustic embeddings, which are then interleaved with text embeddings to form input sequences for LLMs. This method enables audio comprehension tasks such as audio tagging, audio captioning, and few-shot audio classification. The paper also introduces a new task, Natural Language Audio Reasoning (NLAR), which evaluates the model's ability to analyze and compare two audio clips. Experimental results show that APT-enhanced models perform competitively against expert models and can handle zero-shot audio-visual tasks.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, clear, and easy to follow, with a logical structure that aids in understanding the content.
- The proposed Acoustic Prompt Tuning (APT) is a novel approach that extends LLMs and VLMs to the audio domain without requiring fine-tuning, which is a significant advantage in terms of computational efficiency and flexibility.
- The APT-LLM model is capable of analyzing multiple audio clips in a single feed-forward process, which enhances its performance in audio understanding tasks such as few-shot audio classification and audio reasoning.
- The paper introduces a new task called Natural Language Audio Reasoning (NLAR), which evaluates the model's ability to understand and analyze audio content, providing a new benchmark for audio-language models.
- The methodology of using soft prompts to adapt LLMs to the audio domain is innovative and shows promise in enabling audio comprehension tasks.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks detailed explanations and examples of the natural language audio reasoning task, which could be improved by including more examples and detailed explanations in the main text or appendix.
- There is a lack of clarity regarding the novelty of the proposed method, as it seems to be a straightforward extension of existing methods like AudioMAE, with minimal modifications.
- The paper does not discuss the limitations of the proposed method, which could provide a more comprehensive understanding of its applicability and potential challenges.
- The performance of the APT-LLM model is not consistently superior across all tasks, with some tasks showing marginal improvements over existing methods.
- The paper could benefit from a more detailed analysis of the model's performance on specific audio types, such as speech and music, to better understand its capabilities and limitations.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors provide more detailed explanations and examples of the natural language audio reasoning task to enhance the understanding of this new task?
- How does the proposed method differ from existing methods like AudioMAE, and what are the specific contributions of the proposed method?
- What are the limitations of the proposed method, and under what conditions might it not perform well?
- Can the authors clarify the role and impact of the <AUDIO> token in the model's performance?
- How does the model handle different types of audio, such as speech and music, and what are the implications of its performance on these types?
- Could the authors discuss the potential of extending the model to handle more complex audio tasks, such as audio-visual question answering or speech recognition?
