 **Summary:**
The paper introduces SPTNet, a novel method for Generalized Category Discovery (GCD), which integrates a two-stage iterative learning framework to optimize both model parameters and data parameters through prompt learning. The core innovation is the Spatial Prompt Tuning (SPT) method, which attaches prompts to image patches to enhance alignment with pretrained models and facilitate the transfer of knowledge between seen and unseen classes. SPTNet demonstrates superior performance on various benchmarks, showing a significant improvement over existing methods. Despite its strengths, the paper has been critiqued for its limited novelty in the use of prompt tuning and the lack of comprehensive experimental validation across different architectures and datasets.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel Spatial Prompt Tuning (SPT) method for Generalized Category Discovery (GCD), which effectively leverages spatial properties of image data to focus on object parts, enhancing the transfer of knowledge between seen and unseen classes.
- The proposed method achieves state-of-the-art (SOTA) performance on various benchmarks, demonstrating its effectiveness and efficiency in improving GCD performance.
- The paper is well-organized, with clear and easy-to-understand content, and includes comprehensive ablation studies and visualizations that support the proposed method's effectiveness.
- The authors have provided a detailed comparison of their method with state-of-the-art methods, demonstrating significant improvements in performance metrics.

**Weaknesses:**
- The novelty of the proposed method is limited, as it primarily builds on existing prompt tuning techniques, which have been extensively explored in recent years.
- The paper lacks comprehensive experimental validation, particularly on larger-scale datasets such as ImageNet-1K, and does not include comparisons with other architectures like ResNet, which could provide a more robust evaluation of the proposed method.
- The paper does not sufficiently discuss the computational efficiency of the proposed method, which is crucial for practical applications, especially in resource-constrained environments.
- There are inconsistencies and potential errors in the manuscript, such as typographical errors and unclear references, which could affect the paper's credibility and clarity.
- The paper's contribution to the field might be considered incremental due to its reliance on existing techniques like prompt tuning and the lack of significant innovation in the GCD methodology.

**Questions:**
- How does the proposed SPT method compare to other prompt tuning methods, particularly in terms of computational efficiency and performance on different datasets?
- Could you provide more details on the experimental setup, including the specifics of the datasets used and the hyperparameter settings?
- How does the proposed method perform on larger-scale datasets such as ImageNet-1K, and what are the potential challenges or limitations in scaling up the method?
- Can you clarify the role and impact of the two-stage iterative learning framework in the overall performance of the SPTNet?
- How does the SPTNet handle the integration of model and data parameters learning, and what are the implications for the stability and convergence of the training process?
