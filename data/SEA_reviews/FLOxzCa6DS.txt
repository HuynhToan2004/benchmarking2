 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel approach to training differentially private latent diffusion models (DP-LDMs) by pre-training an auto-encoder and diffusion model on public data and fine-tuning only the attention modules and conditioning embedder on private data using DP-SGD. This method aims to improve the training efficiency and reduce the privacy loss by fine-tuning a smaller number of parameters compared to traditional methods that fine-tune the entire model. The authors demonstrate that their approach can generate high-quality images with differential privacy, achieving state-of-the-art results on several benchmark datasets. However, concerns about the novelty and the lack of comparison with recent related works, such as those using histogram mechanisms, were raised.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel approach to training differentially private latent diffusion models (DP-LDMs) by pre-training on public data and fine-tuning on private data, which is a significant advancement in the field of differential privacy in machine learning.
- The method achieves state-of-the-art results on several benchmark datasets, demonstrating its effectiveness in generating high-quality images with differential privacy.
- The paper is well-organized and clearly written, making it easy for readers to understand the proposed methodology and results.
- The proposed method allows for the generation of high-dimensional images (256x256) with differential privacy, which is a significant achievement in the field.
- The paper addresses the issue of slow training times in diffusion models by proposing a method that reduces the number of trainable parameters, making the training process more efficient.

**Weaknesses:**
- The novelty of the paper is limited as similar methods have been previously explored, such as the transfer learning paradigm for LDMs in non-DP settings.
- The paper lacks a thorough comparison with recent related works, particularly those that use histogram mechanisms for differential privacy, which could provide a more comprehensive evaluation of the proposed method.
- The paper does not discuss the limitations of its approach, which is crucial for understanding the scope and applicability of the findings.
- The paper's experimental setup is not detailed enough, particularly the choice of public datasets and the impact of different batch sizes on the results.
- The paper does not sufficiently address the generalizability of the method to other types of data beyond images, such as tabular or textual data.
- The paper does not provide sufficient details on the privacy budgets used in the experiments, which are crucial for evaluating the effectiveness of the differential privacy mechanisms used.

**Questions:**
- How does the proposed method compare in terms of training efficiency and privacy preservation with other methods that use histogram mechanisms for differential privacy?
- Could you clarify the choice of public datasets and their impact on the results, particularly in terms of the FID scores?
- What are the privacy budgets used in the experiments, and how do they affect the results?
- How does the proposed method handle the trade-off between privacy and utility, especially in terms of the FID scores reported?
- Can the authors provide more details on the experimental setup, particularly the choice of batch sizes and their impact on the results?
- How does the proposed method ensure that the privacy loss is minimized while maintaining the utility of the generated data?
- Could you provide more details on the generalizability of the method to other types of data beyond images, such as tabular or textual data?
