 **Summary:**
The paper introduces FedMD-CG, a novel federated learning (FL) method designed to enhance privacy preservation and performance by decoupling local models into feature extractors and classifiers. This approach employs a conditional generator for server-side model aggregation, facilitated by knowledge distillation. The methodology is tested on various datasets and reportedly outperforms existing FL methods in terms of test performance and privacy protection. However, the paper is criticized for its limited experimental scope, focusing primarily on LeNet-5 and not including more challenging datasets like CIFAR-100 or ImageNet. The paper also lacks a thorough discussion on privacy implications and the computational overhead introduced by the conditional generator.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, with clear and concise descriptions of the proposed method and its components.
- The proposed method, FedMD-CG, is novel and innovative, utilizing a conditional generator and knowledge distillation to enhance privacy preservation and performance in federated learning.
- The paper provides a comprehensive set of experiments that demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed method, including detailed comparisons with existing methods and ablation studies.
- The use of a conditional generator to replace the feature extractor in federated learning is a promising approach that addresses privacy concerns by avoiding the sharing of raw data.
- The paper is well-organized, making it easy to follow, and includes a detailed discussion of the methodology, including the use of a conditional generator and knowledge distillation.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a thorough discussion on the privacy implications of the proposed method, particularly the potential for privacy leakage through the use of the conditional generator.
- The experimental results are not convincing, with limited datasets used (only LeNet-5) and no comparisons with more challenging datasets such as CIFAR-100 or ImageNet.
- The paper does not adequately address the computational overhead introduced by the conditional generator, which could be a significant concern in federated learning environments.
- The paper does not compare the proposed method with more recent or relevant works, which limits the understanding of its novelty and effectiveness.
- The paper's focus on LeNet-5 limits the generalizability of the results, and it does not discuss the scalability of the proposed method to larger or more complex datasets.
- The paper does not include a discussion on the limitations of the proposed method, which is crucial for understanding its applicability and potential challenges.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors provide a more detailed discussion on the privacy implications of the proposed method, particularly in terms of potential privacy leakage through the use of the conditional generator?
- How does the proposed method compare with more recent or relevant works in the field of federated learning?
- Could the authors include more challenging datasets in their experiments, such as CIFAR-100 or ImageNet, to validate the effectiveness of the proposed method on more complex scenarios?
- What are the specific computational overheads introduced by the use of the conditional generator, and how do these impact the practical applicability of the proposed method in federated learning environments?
- Can the authors provide more details on the training process of the conditional generator, particularly how it is trained and how it impacts the overall performance of the federated learning system?
- How does the proposed method handle the potential for privacy leakage through the use of the conditional generator, and what measures are in place to mitigate these risks?
