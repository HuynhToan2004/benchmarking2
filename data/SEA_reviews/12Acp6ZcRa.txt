 **Summary:**
The paper explores the robustness of text-to-image diffusion models (T2I DMs) against various types of perturbations, including typo, glyph, and phonetic errors. It introduces a novel method for evaluating the robustness of these models by optimizing distribution-based objectives using a black-box attack. The paper presents a comprehensive evaluation of the robustness of popular T2I DMs, showing that they are susceptible to realistic errors, even with minimal perturbations. The study also includes real-world attack experiments and human evaluations to validate the findings. However, the paper's novelty and the clarity of its contributions are questioned, with concerns about the method's scalability and the depth of the human evaluation.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, clear, and easy to follow, with a clear motivation for the research.
- It introduces a novel evaluation method for the robustness of text-to-image diffusion models (T2I DMs) against real-world perturbations, which is a significant contribution to the field.
- The paper includes both diagnostic experiments and real-world attack experiments, providing a comprehensive evaluation of the robustness of T2I DMs.
- The paper is well-structured, with a clear introduction, related work, and methodology sections, and includes both quantitative and qualitative results, which are informative and interesting.
- The research is timely and relevant, addressing the growing interest in text-to-image diffusion models and their vulnerabilities.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed description of the human evaluation process, which is crucial for understanding the effectiveness of the proposed attack method.
- There is a lack of clarity on the choice of the attack method and the criteria for selecting the best attack method.
- The paper does not provide sufficient details on the experimental setup, such as the specifics of the attack method used and the datasets used for evaluation.
- The paper's contribution is questioned due to its reliance on existing attack methods and the limited novelty of its approach.
- The paper's claims about the robustness of T2I DMs against real-world perturbations are not convincingly supported by the experimental results, which are limited to a few models and datasets.
- The paper does not adequately address the scalability of the proposed attack method to larger models or more diverse datasets, which is a significant limitation for its practical application.

**Questions:**
- Can you provide more details on the human evaluation process, including the number of participants, the evaluation criteria, and the inter-rater reliability?
- How does the proposed attack method compare to other existing methods in terms of effectiveness and efficiency?
- Could you clarify the choice of the attack method and the criteria used for selecting the best attack method?
- What are the specifics of the attack method used in the experiments, and how does it compare to other methods in terms of computational resources and effectiveness?
- How does the proposed attack method perform against different types of T2I DMs, and what are the implications for the robustness of these models against real-world perturbations?
- Can you provide more details on the experimental setup, including the datasets used for evaluation and the specifics of the attack method used?
