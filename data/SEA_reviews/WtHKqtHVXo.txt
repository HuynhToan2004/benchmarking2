 **Summary:**
The paper investigates the application of large language models (LLMs) in generating robot policy code for high-precision, contact-rich manipulation tasks. It introduces a novel approach by reparameterizing the action space to include robot compliance with constraints on interaction forces and stiffnesses, which enables the LLM to generate policies for tasks such as peg insertion and cable routing. The methodology involves prompting the LLM with various strategies to guide the generation of code, which is then tested against baseline methods and expert-authored scripts. The experiments demonstrate that the LLM-generated code outperforms baseline methods in some scenarios, although there are concerns about the generalizability and robustness of the approach.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, clear, and easy to follow, with clear explanations of the prompting strategies and action spaces used.
- The experiments are well-designed, and the results are convincing, demonstrating the effectiveness of the proposed method in generating robot policy code for contact-rich manipulation tasks.
- The paper introduces a novel approach by reparameterizing the action space to include robot compliance with constraints on interaction forces and stiffnesses, which is a significant advancement in the field.
- The methodology is straightforward and simple, making it potentially adaptable to a variety of tasks and domains.
- The paper includes a comprehensive set of experiments and ablation studies that demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed method.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a thorough discussion on the limitations of the proposed method, particularly in terms of its generalizability and applicability to more complex tasks.
- The experiments are limited in scope, focusing primarily on peg insertion and cable routing, which may not fully demonstrate the method's capabilities in more complex or varied scenarios.
- There is a lack of comparative analysis with other recent works that also use LLMs for generating robot policy code, which could provide a better context for evaluating the contributions of this paper.
- The paper does not sufficiently address the scalability and robustness of the LLM-generated code, particularly in terms of its ability to handle more complex tasks or variations in the environment.
- The methodology does not include a comprehensive evaluation of the generated code's performance under different conditions or scenarios, which could affect the reliability and practicality of the proposed approach.

**Questions:**

- Could the authors elaborate on the limitations of the proposed method and discuss potential avenues for future research to address these limitations?
- How do the authors ensure the scalability and robustness of the LLM-generated code, particularly in more complex or varied tasks?
- Can the authors provide more examples or case studies of the generated code's performance in real-world scenarios, particularly in tasks that are more complex or varied than those presented in the paper?
