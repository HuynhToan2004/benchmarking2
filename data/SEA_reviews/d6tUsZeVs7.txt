 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel method for solving the entropy-regularized optimal transport (EOT) problem using energy-based models (EBMs). It bridges the gap between EBMs and EOT by proposing a duality between the weak OT problem and the EOT problem, which is then leveraged to derive a gradient ascent algorithm for training EBMs to recover the optimal conditional EOT plan. The paper provides theoretical insights into the optimization process and presents empirical results on both toy and high-dimensional image translation tasks. Despite its innovative approach, the paper has been criticized for its lack of clarity in explaining the relationship between the EBM and the EOT problem, and for not sufficiently distinguishing its method from existing works.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel method for solving the EOT problem using energy-based models (EBMs), which is a significant contribution to the field.
- The authors provide a clear and detailed explanation of the theoretical underpinnings of their approach, which enhances the paper's credibility and reproducibility.
- The paper includes comprehensive experimental results on both toy and high-dimensional image translation tasks, demonstrating the practical applicability of the proposed method.
- The authors provide a thorough discussion of related works, which helps contextualize their research within the existing literature.
- The paper is well-written, making it accessible and understandable to readers, which is crucial for disseminating the findings effectively.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks clarity in explaining the relationship between the EBM and the EOT problem, particularly in how the EBM recovers the optimal conditional EOT plan.
- The paper does not sufficiently distinguish its method from existing works, such as the work by Daniels et al. (2021), which also uses EBMs for EOT.
- The paper's empirical results are not convincingly superior to existing methods, and the choice of parameters and baselines needs more justification.
- The paper could benefit from a more thorough discussion of the limitations and potential negative societal impacts of the proposed method.
- The paper's presentation could be improved, particularly in terms of readability and clarity, to make the content more accessible to a broader audience.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors clarify how the EBM recovers the optimal conditional EOT plan, specifically in terms of the optimization process and the role of the EBM in this context?
- How does the proposed method differ from existing works, such as the work by Daniels et al. (2021), and what are the specific advantages of the proposed method over these existing approaches?
- The paper mentions the use of a neural network to approximate the conditional plan, but it is unclear how this network is trained and what its role is in the overall optimization process. Could the authors provide more details on this aspect?
- The paper includes several parameters and hyperparameters that are not clearly justified or explained. Could the authors provide more details on the choice and role of these parameters in the optimization process?
- The paper mentions several limitations, such as the time-consuming nature of MCMC methods and the potential for negative societal impacts. Could the authors expand on these limitations and discuss potential mitigation strategies or future directions for research?
