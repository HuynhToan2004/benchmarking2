 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel Multi-Granularity Time Series Diffusion (MG-TSD) model for time series forecasting, leveraging multiple granularity levels to guide the learning process of diffusion models. The model utilizes coarse-grained data as targets at intermediate diffusion steps to align with the process of smoothing fine-grained data into coarse-grained representations. The MG-TSD model outperforms existing methods across multiple datasets, demonstrating its effectiveness in handling time series forecasting tasks. The paper includes extensive experiments and ablation studies to validate the model's performance and robustness. However, the novelty of the approach is questioned due to its similarities with existing methods like TimeGrad, and the paper lacks a thorough discussion on the theoretical underpinnings and the choice of hyperparameters.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, easy to follow, and includes extensive experiments and ablation studies, which demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed MG-TSD model.
- The use of coarse-grained data as guidance to smooth the learning process is an innovative approach that has not been extensively explored in the literature.
- The MG-TSD model achieves state-of-the-art predictive performance on real-world datasets, outperforming existing methods.
- The paper provides a detailed description of the proposed model, including its components and the training and inference processes, which are supported by experiments and ablation studies.
- The authors have made their code available, which is a significant contribution to the reproducibility of the research.

**Weaknesses:**
- The novelty of the approach is limited as it builds upon existing methods like TimeGrad, and the main technical contribution (the guidance loss function) is not clearly differentiated from prior work.
- The paper lacks a comprehensive discussion on the theoretical underpinnings of the MG-TSD model, particularly the choice of hyperparameters and the guidance loss function.
- There are concerns about the stability and reliability of the model, as shown in the results where the CRPS sum of MG-TSD is consistently lower than that of TimeGrad, but the results are not convincingly better in terms of other metrics like NMAE and NRMSE.
- The paper does not adequately address the issue of instability in diffusion models, which is a significant challenge in time series forecasting.
- The selection of share ratio and the number of granularities is critical for the model's performance but is not thoroughly discussed, leaving it unclear how these parameters should be chosen in practical applications.

**Questions:**
- Could you provide more details on the choice of hyperparameters, particularly how they were chosen and how they affect the model's performance?
- How does the guidance loss function differ from that used in TimeGrad, and what are the specific contributions of the MG-TSD model over existing methods?
- In the experimental results, why does the MG-TSD model not consistently outperform TimeGrad across all metrics?
- How do the authors address the issue of instability in diffusion models, and what steps have been taken to ensure the reliability and stability of the MG-TSD model?
- Could you clarify the role of the share ratio in the model's performance and how it affects the model's ability to capture trends and fluctuations in the data?
- How do the authors select the number of granularities for their model, and what are the implications of using different numbers of granularities on the model's performance?
