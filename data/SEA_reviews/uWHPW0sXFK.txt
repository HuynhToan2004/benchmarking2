 **Summary:**
The paper introduces Physics-Informed Normalizing Flows (PINF), a novel extension of continuous normalizing flows that integrates diffusion through the method of characteristics to solve high-dimensional time-dependent and steady-state Fokker-Planck equations. PINF is designed to address the challenges posed by the normalization constraint in probability density, which is crucial for solving Fokker-Planck equations. The methodology, which is mesh-free and causality-free, leverages the change of variables formula to ensure probability density conservation and enables the learning of complex data distributions. The paper presents a detailed algorithm for training and testing PINF, along with a comprehensive discussion on the experimental results, demonstrating the method's effectiveness in solving Fokker-Planck equations.
