 **Summary:**
The paper introduces AMP, a dataset and challenge aimed at evaluating the handling of ambiguity in semantic parsing, specifically in natural language to formal representations like logic and code. It presents a framework for generating ambiguous natural language inputs and their corresponding formal representations, using a set of templates for five well-documented linguistic ambiguities. The dataset is used to investigate how large pre-trained models handle ambiguity, and new metrics are proposed to measure the models' performance. The paper explores the zero-shot and few-shot settings, demonstrating that models struggle to capture ambiguity in zero-shot settings but can do so when ambiguity is present in their inputs. However, the paper is criticized for its limited scope, lack of novelty, and insufficient evaluation against existing datasets.

**Strengths:**
- The paper addresses the issue of ambiguity in natural language processing, which is a significant challenge in the field.
- The dataset and challenge introduced are novel and contribute to the ongoing research in this area.
- The paper is well-written, clear, and easy to follow, making it accessible to a broad audience.
- The proposed metrics are new and reasonable, providing a useful tool for evaluating models' performance in handling ambiguity.
- The experiments conducted are thorough, and the results are insightful, contributing to the understanding of how models handle ambiguity.
- The paper is timely and relevant, addressing a critical issue in the field of semantic parsing.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks novelty as it primarily evaluates existing models without proposing new models or techniques.
- The evaluation is limited to a small number of models, which may not be sufficient to draw robust conclusions.
- The scope of the study is narrow, focusing only on a few types of linguistic ambiguities and not exploring broader aspects of ambiguity in natural language.
- The paper does not adequately compare its findings with existing datasets, which could have provided a more robust evaluation.
- The paper does not sufficiently address how the findings might be applied in practical scenarios, such as in text-to-code systems.
- There is a lack of diversity in the models used for evaluation, which could limit the generalizability of the results.
- The paper does not adequately discuss the limitations of its approach, which could have provided a more balanced view of the research.

**Questions:**
- How does the proposed approach compare with existing methods for handling ambiguity in natural language processing?
- Could the authors provide more detailed examples or case studies to illustrate the practical applications of the findings in text-to-code systems?
- How does the proposed dataset and challenge contribute to the broader research community beyond the specific models and datasets used in the study?
- Are there plans to extend the evaluation to include a more diverse set of models or to explore different types of ambiguity?
- How does the proposed dataset and challenge address the broader issue of ambiguity in natural language processing, and what are the implications for future research in this area?
