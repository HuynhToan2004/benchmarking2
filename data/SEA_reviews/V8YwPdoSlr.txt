 **Summary:**
The paper introduces CHRONOS, a novel self-supervised learning (SSL) method designed to capture static, short-term, and long-term dynamics in time series data, specifically focusing on ECG signals. The methodology employs a contrastive learning approach to distinguish between different temporal attributes, utilizing a static loss, short-term dynamic loss, and a new long-term dynamic loss. The paper evaluates CHRONOS across four downstream tasks, showing promising results, although the novelty and the clarity of the methodological contributions are questioned. The paper is criticized for its limited novelty, lack of comprehensive experimental validation, and insufficient comparison with existing methods.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, making it easy to follow and understand.
- The concept of capturing static, short-term, and long-term dynamics in time series data is innovative and relevant, especially in the context of ECG signals.
- The proposed method, CHRONOS, is novel and demonstrates some effectiveness in the experiments conducted.
- The use of static and short-term dynamic losses is innovative, as is the use of a long-term dynamic loss.
- The paper includes a comprehensive set of experiments and ablation studies, which support the proposed method's effectiveness.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a clear and detailed explanation of the methodology, particularly the role of different components and the loss functions used.
- The paper's motivation and the rationale behind the proposed method are not convincingly argued, which could affect the credibility of the results.
- The paper's contribution is seen as limited, as many of the methodological aspects are derived from existing works, such as DEBS.
- The experimental setup and results are not sufficiently robust or convincing, with concerns about the validity of the results and the fairness of the comparisons made.
- The paper's scope is limited to ECG data, which might not generalize well to other types of time series data.
- The paper could benefit from a more detailed discussion on the limitations and potential applications of the proposed method.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors clarify the specific role and function of each component in the proposed method, especially in relation to the static and short-term dynamic losses?
- How does the proposed method perform when applied to other types of time series data beyond ECG signals?
- Could the authors provide more details on the experimental setup, particularly how the results were validated and how the comparisons were made with existing methods?
- How does the proposed method handle the potential overlap or similarity between different temporal dynamics, such as static and short-term dynamics?
- Could the authors elaborate on the motivation behind the proposed method and how it addresses the specific challenges in capturing different temporal dynamics in ECG signals?
