 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel vector quantization (VQ) approach to enhance the computational efficiency of transformer models in probabilistic time series forecasting. The VQ-TR model, a variant of the VQ-AR model, employs a cross-attention mechanism to map long sequences to a discrete set of latent representations, thereby reducing computational complexity. The model's performance is evaluated against various state-of-the-art methods, demonstrating competitive results. The paper also explores the impact of different codebook sizes on model performance and provides an extensive comparison with other transformer-based models.

**Strengths:**
- The paper addresses an important and relevant problem in time series forecasting, focusing on computational efficiency and scalability.
- The introduction of the VQ-TR model, which leverages vector quantization to reduce computational complexity, is a novel and innovative approach.
- The paper is well-written, clear, and easy to follow, with comprehensive experimental results and a thorough analysis of model performance.
- The paper provides a systematic comparison of modern Transformer-based time series forecasting methods for probabilistic forecasting, offering a benchmark for future research.
- The model's performance is evaluated across various datasets, demonstrating its effectiveness and competitiveness compared to other state-of-the-art methods.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed discussion on the limitations of the VQ-TR model, particularly concerning its performance on datasets with complex dynamics and its comparison with other models in terms of computational efficiency.
- The use of vector quantization for time series forecasting is not entirely novel, as similar approaches have been explored in previous works.
- The paper does not provide a clear explanation on how the VQ-TR model performs on datasets with complex dynamics, and the impact of codebook size on model performance is not thoroughly explored.
- The paper does not include experiments on datasets with higher frequencies or longer sequences, which might affect the generalizability of the findings.
- The paper does not discuss the limitations of the VQ-TR model, such as its potential impact on model expressiveness and its performance under different data conditions.

**Questions:**
- Could you clarify the impact of codebook size on model performance and whether the VQ-TR model can effectively handle datasets with complex dynamics?
- How does the VQ-TR model compare in terms of computational efficiency with other models, particularly in scenarios where memory usage is a critical factor?
- Can you provide more details on the experimental setup, such as the specific parameters used for training and the selection of hyperparameters?
- How does the VQ-TR model perform on datasets with higher frequencies or longer sequences, and what are the implications of these conditions on model performance?
- Could you elaborate on the regularization effect of the vector quantization module and how it contributes to the model's performance?
