 **Summary:**
The paper introduces DataFreeShield, a method designed to enhance adversarial robustness in machine learning models without access to training data. It employs a novel approach by generating synthetic data and training a model on this synthetic dataset, which is then evaluated against various adversarial attacks. The methodology includes diversified sample synthesis and adversarial training using a soft label loss and a gradient refinement method. The paper demonstrates the effectiveness of DataFreeShield through experiments on datasets such as MedMNIST, SVHN, and CIFAR-10, showing that it can achieve robustness comparable to models trained on real data. However, concerns are raised about the practical applicability of the method due to the high computational costs and the lack of comprehensive comparison with existing data-free methods.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel approach to adversarial robustness by addressing the problem of data-free adversarial robustness, which is an innovative direction in the field.
- The methodology is well-explained, and the paper is easy to follow, making it accessible to readers.
- The proposed method, DataFreeShield, is evaluated on various datasets and shows promising results, outperforming existing baselines.
- The use of a synthetic dataset for adversarial training is a novel approach, which could be beneficial in scenarios where real data is not available.
- The paper includes a comprehensive evaluation and extensive experiments, demonstrating the effectiveness of the proposed method.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a clear definition and explanation of the "data-free" setting, which is crucial for understanding the scope and applicability of the proposed method.
- The paper does not compare the proposed method with existing data-free methods, which is essential for understanding its relative effectiveness and positioning in the field.
- The paper does not address the practicality of the method, particularly in terms of computational costs and the feasibility of generating high-quality synthetic data for complex models.
- The paper lacks a detailed discussion on the limitations of the proposed method, including its applicability to different model architectures and the potential impact of synthetic data on model robustness.
- The paper does not provide sufficient details on the hyperparameters used in the experiments, which could affect the reproducibility and comparability of the results.
- The paper does not include a discussion on the ethical implications of using synthetic data for adversarial training, which is an important consideration in the field.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors clarify the "data-free" setting and provide a clearer definition of what constitutes a "data-free" scenario in this context?
- How does the proposed method compare with existing data-free methods in terms of performance, computational efficiency, and practical applicability?
- What are the specific hyperparameters used in the experiments, and how do they affect the performance and reproducibility of the results?
- How does the proposed method perform on more complex datasets, such as those with higher resolution or more classes?
- Could the authors provide a detailed comparison of the proposed method with existing adversarial training methods, particularly in terms of computational efficiency and model robustness?
- What are the potential ethical implications of using synthetic data for adversarial training, and how does the paper address these concerns?
