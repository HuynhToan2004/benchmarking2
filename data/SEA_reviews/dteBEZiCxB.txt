 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a new watermarking method, Necessary and Sufficient Watermark (NS-Watermark), for Large Language Models (LLMs) to distinguish LLM-generated texts from human-written texts without compromising text quality. The method leverages a constrained optimization problem and proposes an efficient algorithm to solve it, enhancing both text quality and detection accuracy. The paper evaluates the NS-Watermark against existing methods like Hard/Soft-Watermark and demonstrates its effectiveness in machine translation tasks. However, concerns about the clarity of the method's presentation, the fairness of experimental comparisons, and the robustness of the watermark against post-editing are highlighted.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, with clear and easy-to-follow content, making it accessible to a broad audience.
- The proposed watermarking method, NS-Watermark, is novel and shows promise in enhancing the quality of generated texts while maintaining detection accuracy.
- The paper provides a robust theoretical foundation for the NS-Watermark, demonstrating its effectiveness through rigorous experiments and comparisons with existing methods.
- The methodology is innovative, addressing the challenge of generating high-quality texts with watermarks, a significant issue in the field of Large Language Models (LLMs).
- The paper's experimental results are convincing, showing that the NS-Watermark can outperform existing methods in terms of both text quality and detection accuracy.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed comparison with other watermarking methods, which could provide a more comprehensive understanding of the NS-Watermark's effectiveness and advantages.
- There is a lack of clarity in the presentation of the method, particularly in the explanation of the algorithm used and the assumptions made, which could hinder understanding and reproducibility.
- The paper does not adequately address the issue of robustness against post-editing, a critical concern in watermarking applications.
- The experimental setup and results are not clearly presented, making it difficult to assess the fairness and robustness of the comparisons made.
- The paper does not sufficiently discuss the limitations of the proposed method, which could provide valuable insights into its applicability and potential improvements.

**Questions:**
- Could you clarify the method's robustness against post-editing? How does the NS-Watermark perform when green words are replaced with red words, and what are the implications for the watermark's effectiveness?
- How does the NS-Watermark compare to other watermarking methods in terms of computational efficiency and resource requirements?
- Can you provide more details on the experimental setup, particularly how the hyperparameters were tuned and how the results were validated?
- How does the NS-Watermark perform in different language models, and what are the implications for its generalizability across different models and applications?
- Could you elaborate on the assumptions made in the NS-Watermark and how they affect the method's effectiveness and applicability?
