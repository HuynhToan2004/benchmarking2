 **Summary:**
The paper introduces Tree-Planner, a novel method for task planning that leverages Large Language Models (LLMs) to generate plans more efficiently by reducing token consumption and error corrections. The approach involves sampling multiple plans, constructing an action tree, and iteratively deciding on actions based on real-time observations. This method is evaluated in the VirtualHome environment and shows significant improvements in success rates and token efficiency compared to existing methods. The paper also includes an ablation study and error analysis to understand the performance and limitations of the proposed method.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, making it easy to follow and understand, with clear figures and tables that aid in comprehension.
- The proposed method of using LLMs for task planning is innovative and shows significant improvements in success rates and token efficiency compared to existing methods.
- The use of an action tree to aggregate sampled plans is a novel approach that allows for the filtering of plans at the action level, reducing the need for multiple LLM calls.
- The method is evaluated in a challenging environment, VirtualHome, and shows superior performance over existing methods, including a reduction in token consumption by 92.2% and a decrease in error corrections by 40.5%.
- The paper includes a detailed ablation study and error analysis, which provide insights into the performance and limitations of the proposed method.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed discussion on the limitations and potential drawbacks of the proposed method, which could provide a more balanced view of its applicability and effectiveness.
- The evaluation is limited to a single environment, VirtualHome, which may not fully demonstrate the generalizability of the method across different domains or environments.
- The method's dependence on LLMs for plan generation and correction may limit its applicability in scenarios where LLMs are not available or not effective.
- The method's performance relies heavily on the quality of the initial plan, which may not be feasible in complex or dynamic environments where generating a high-quality plan from scratch is challenging.
- The paper does not sufficiently discuss the potential negative societal impacts of the proposed method, which could be a concern for some readers.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors provide a more detailed discussion on the limitations and potential drawbacks of the proposed method?
- How does the proposed method perform in other environments or domains beyond VirtualHome?
- What are the specific challenges or limitations of the proposed method when applied to different types of tasks or environments?
- Could the authors provide more details on how the action tree is constructed and how it impacts the performance of the method?
- How does the method handle scenarios where the initial plan is not of high quality, and how does it ensure that the final plan remains effective?
- What are the potential negative societal impacts of the proposed method, and how do the authors address these concerns?
