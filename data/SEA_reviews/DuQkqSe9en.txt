 **Summary:**
The paper introduces AILBoost, a novel adversarial imitation learning (AIL) algorithm that leverages the gradient boosting framework to optimize off-policy learning. This method, which maintains a mixture of policies and optimizes the discriminator using the entire data collected so far, is designed to handle stochastic environments. The paper presents an algorithm that alternates between learning a new policy and updating the discriminator, with a focus on minimizing the reverse KL divergence between the learned policy and the expert policy. Experiments conducted on the DeepMind Control Suite demonstrate the algorithm's effectiveness compared to existing methods like DAC and ValueDICE. However, the paper lacks a comprehensive comparison with state-of-the-art methods and does not include a detailed theoretical analysis, which limits its contribution to the field.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, clear, and easy to follow, making complex concepts accessible to a broad audience.
- The authors provide a detailed description of the algorithm, including a clear explanation of how it differs from existing methods like DAC and ValueDICE.
- The experimental results are comprehensive and convincing, demonstrating the effectiveness of the proposed method.
- The paper introduces a novel approach by using the boosting framework to optimize off-policy learning in adversarial imitation learning (AIL), which is a significant contribution to the field.
- The methodology is well-supported by theoretical underpinnings, and the paper effectively connects the boosting framework to adversarial imitation learning (AIL).

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a thorough comparison with state-of-the-art methods, which could have provided a more robust evaluation of the proposed method.
- The theoretical analysis is insufficient, particularly in terms of explaining why the proposed method outperforms existing methods.
- The paper does not include a discussion on the limitations of the proposed method, which could help in understanding its applicability and potential drawbacks.
- The presentation of the paper, particularly in the experimental section, could be improved to enhance readability and clarity.
- The paper does not include a detailed discussion on the computational complexity or memory requirements of the proposed method, which could be crucial for practical implementation.
- There is a lack of discussion on the scalability of the method to more complex or larger-scale environments, which could affect the generalizability of the findings.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors clarify the discrepancies in the experimental results, particularly the performance of AILBoost compared to DAC and ValueDICE?
- How does the proposed method handle the issue of mode collapse, which is common in boosting-based methods?
- What are the potential advantages of using the boosting framework over other methods like ValueDICE, especially in terms of handling stochastic environments?
- How does the proposed method compare to other offline RL algorithms, especially in terms of performance and computational efficiency?
- Could the authors provide a detailed discussion on the computational complexity and memory requirements of the proposed method, especially in comparison to other methods like DAC and ValueDICE?
- How does the proposed method perform in more complex or larger-scale environments, and what are the potential challenges or limitations in such settings?
