 **Summary:**
The paper introduces AugCal, a novel training-time patch designed to enhance the reliability of Sim2Real adapted models by reducing miscalibration and overconfidence in incorrect predictions. AugCal operates by replacing vanilla Sim images with strongly augmented views and optimizing for a calibration loss on augmented Sim predictions. This approach is supported by a theoretical framework and validated through experiments across multiple adaptation methods, backbones, and tasks. The methodology aims to improve the confidence score reliability and guide misclassification detection, while maintaining or enhancing performance on real data. Despite its strengths, the paper is criticized for its limited novelty, as it primarily combines existing methods without significant innovation.

**Strengths:**
- The paper addresses a significant and timely issue of improving the reliability of Sim2Real adapted models, which is crucial for real-world applications.
- The proposed method is straightforward, easy to implement, and can be integrated with various existing methods, making it practical and adaptable.
- The paper is well-written, clear, and easy to follow, with a comprehensive set of experiments conducted on different datasets, methods, and backbones.
- The inclusion of theoretical justification and the use of an upper bound in Eq.9 to demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed method adds a solid foundation to the research.
- The paper is well-organized, with clear descriptions of the methodology and its application, and the results are supported by detailed analysis and comparisons with baseline methods.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a direct comparison with other calibration methods such as temperature scaling, which could provide a more comprehensive understanding of the proposed method's effectiveness.
- The paper does not discuss the potential negative societal impacts of the proposed method, which could be a significant limitation in the context of real-world applications.
- The novelty of the paper is limited as it primarily combines existing methods without significant innovation, which might not be perceived as a significant contribution to the field.
- The paper could benefit from more diverse datasets and tasks to validate the generalizability of the proposed method.
- The theoretical justification, particularly the use of the Cauchy-Schwarz inequality and the AM-GM inequality, is not well-explained, which could confuse readers.
- The paper does not provide sufficient detail on how the calibration loss is optimized, which is crucial for understanding the practical implementation of the proposed method.

**Questions:**
- How does the proposed method compare with other calibration methods, such as temperature scaling? Could you provide a direct comparison in terms of performance and efficiency?
- What are the potential negative societal impacts of the proposed method? How can these be mitigated or addressed?
- Can you provide more details on how the calibration loss is optimized during training? Is it optimized together with the other losses, or separately?
- How does the paper address the potential issue of overconfidence in models that are too confident in their predictions?
- Could you clarify the role and impact of the importance sampling in the proposed method?
- How does the paper ensure that the calibration loss does not interfere with the optimization of other losses, such as the UDA loss?
- In the context of the theoretical justification, could you provide a clearer explanation of how the Cauchy-Schwarz inequality and the AM-GM inequality are applied, and what they contribute to the overall analysis?
