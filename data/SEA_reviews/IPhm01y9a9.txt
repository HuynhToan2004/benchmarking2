 **Summary:**
The paper explores the performance degradation caused by interpolating absolute position embeddings in vision transformers, particularly in models like Hiera and ViTDet. The authors identify a bug in these models where the position embeddings do not align properly with the windows during interpolation, leading to suboptimal performance. To address this, they propose a new method of fixing the embeddings by using a combination of window embeddings and global embeddings, which aligns the embeddings with the windows during interpolation. This approach is tested across various models and datasets, showing improvements in performance and efficiency. The paper also discusses the implications of this bug in other models and provides suggestions for future research.

**Strengths:**
- The paper addresses a significant issue in the field of computer vision, specifically the misalignment of position embeddings with windows during interpolation, which is a common practice in many models.
- The proposed solution, which involves using a combination of window embeddings and global embeddings, is simple yet effective, and is supported by comprehensive experimental results.
- The paper is well-written, clear, and easy to follow, making it accessible to a broad audience.
- The proposed method is simple and easy to implement, making it a practical solution for improving the performance of models that use absolute position embeddings.
- The paper provides a detailed analysis of the problem and its implications, which is useful for understanding the behavior of models that use absolute position embeddings.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a thorough discussion on the limitations of the proposed method, particularly in scenarios where the model size or the dataset used for pretraining is different from those typically used.
- The paper does not adequately address the scalability of the proposed method, especially in larger models or datasets where the performance gains might not be as significant.
- There is a lack of comparative analysis with other methods, such as using different types of position embeddings or varying the window sizes, which could provide a more comprehensive understanding of the problem and its solutions.
- The paper does not discuss the potential negative societal impacts of the proposed method, which could be a concern in certain applications.
- The paper could benefit from a more detailed discussion on the theoretical underpinnings of the bug and the proposed fix, as well as a more rigorous evaluation of the computational costs associated with the proposed method.

**Questions:**
- Can the proposed method be applied to other models that use absolute position embeddings, such as Swin Transformer or PVT?
- How does the performance of the model vary with different window sizes, and what is the optimal window size for achieving the best performance?
- Could the authors provide more details on the computational costs associated with the proposed method, particularly in terms of inference speed and memory requirements?
- Is there a possibility of extending the proposed method to include other types of position embeddings, such as relative position embeddings or learnable position embeddings?
- How does the proposed method compare to other methods that address the same issue, such as using different types of position embeddings or varying the window sizes?
- Can the authors provide a more detailed analysis of the bug and the proposed fix, particularly in terms of its theoretical implications and practical applications?
