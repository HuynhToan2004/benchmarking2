 **Summary:**
The paper introduces Fast-ELECTRA, a novel pre-training method for language models that significantly reduces the training costs associated with ELECTRA by utilizing an existing language model as an auxiliary model. This approach avoids the need for joint training with the main model, thereby reducing computational and memory requirements. The methodology involves temperature scaling to smooth the output distribution of the auxiliary model, creating a learning curriculum for the main model. Extensive experiments demonstrate that Fast-ELECTRA achieves comparable performance to state-of-the-art ELECTRA-style pre-training methods while reducing training costs by up to 25%.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, easy to follow, and provides clear and detailed explanations of the proposed method and its experimental results.
- The proposed method significantly reduces the training costs of ELECTRA, making it more accessible and efficient for practical applications.
- The paper includes a comprehensive set of experiments that demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed method, showing that it can achieve comparable performance to state-of-the-art ELECTRA-style pre-training methods while reducing training costs by up to 25%.
- The methodology is novel and innovative, leveraging existing language models as auxiliary models and using temperature scaling to create a learning curriculum for the main model, which is a significant improvement over traditional methods.
- The paper provides a detailed analysis of the training costs of ELECTRA, which is a critical issue in the field of language model pre-training.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper does not include a direct comparison of inference costs between Fast-ELECTRA and other methods, which could be crucial for understanding the practical implications of the proposed method.
- The novelty of the proposed method is somewhat limited, as it primarily involves using an existing language model as an auxiliary model and temperature scaling, which may not be seen as a significant advancement.
- The paper could benefit from a more thorough discussion on the potential limitations and challenges of the proposed method, particularly in terms of its scalability and applicability to different types of language models.
- The experiments are primarily conducted on BERT-based models, which may not fully demonstrate the generalizability of the proposed method to other types of language models.
- The paper could benefit from a more detailed comparison with other methods, such as METRO, to better establish the relative advantages and disadvantages of the proposed method.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors provide a direct comparison of inference costs between Fast-ELECTRA and other methods to help understand the practical implications of the proposed method?
- How does the proposed method perform when scaled up to larger models, and what are the potential challenges or limitations in such scenarios?
- Could the authors discuss the potential limitations or challenges of the proposed method, and how these might affect its practical applicability?
- How does the proposed method compare to other existing methods, such as METRO, in terms of performance and efficiency?
- Could the authors provide more details on the experimental setup, particularly the hyper-parameter settings, to help understand the reproducibility and robustness of the results?
