 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel automatic evaluation metric, BooookScore, designed to assess the coherence of book-length summaries generated by large language models (LLMs). This metric is based on a taxonomy of coherence errors derived from human annotations, which are used to train the LLM to identify these errors. The paper also presents a human evaluation protocol to validate the metric's effectiveness, comparing it to GPT-4's coherence scores. The evaluation covers various aspects such as the impact of chunk size and base LLM on the generated summaries. Despite the innovative approach and the development of a new metric, concerns were raised about the novelty of the methodology and the generalizability of the findings due to the limited evaluation to only one dataset.

**Strengths:**
- The paper addresses an important and timely problem of evaluating summarization of book-length documents, which is crucial for the field.
- It introduces a novel automatic metric, BooookScore, which is shown to correlate well with human annotations, reducing the need for extensive human evaluations.
- The methodology is well-structured, with a clear presentation of the human evaluation protocol and the development of the automatic metric.
- The paper is well-written and easy to follow, making the complex concepts accessible.
- The experiments conducted are comprehensive, providing insights into the impact of various factors such as chunk size and base LLM on the performance of the summarization models.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed discussion on the design choices of the BooookScore metric, particularly how the questions are generated and how they contribute to the evaluation.
- There is a concern about the generalizability of the findings due to the limited evaluation to only one dataset.
- The paper does not sufficiently discuss the novelty of the methodology, as it appears to be a straightforward application of existing techniques without significant innovation.
- The paper could benefit from a more thorough discussion of the limitations, particularly the potential biases introduced by the use of GPT-4 in the evaluation.
- The paper does not adequately address the potential for overfitting to the specific dataset used, which could limit the applicability of the findings to other datasets.

**Questions:**
- Could you elaborate on how the questions for the BooookScore metric are generated and how they contribute to the evaluation?
- How does the BooookScore metric perform when evaluated on other datasets, and what are the implications for its generalizability?
- Could you provide more details on the validation of the BooookScore metric, particularly how the inter-annotator agreement was measured?
- How do the authors plan to address the potential for overfitting to the specific dataset used in the study?
- In the context of the findings, how do the authors justify the choice of the 88K chunk size for Claude 2, and what are the implications for the applicability of these findings to other datasets or models?
