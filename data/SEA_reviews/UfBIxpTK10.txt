 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel benchmark, DockGen, designed to evaluate the generalizability of protein-ligand docking models across different protein domains. It critiques the limitations of existing benchmarks in representing the full proteome diversity and proposes a new training method called Confidence Bootstrapping to enhance the generalization of docking models. The method leverages diffusion models to generate poses and a confidence model to score them, using these scores to refine the diffusion model. The paper also introduces a new dataset, DockGen, which is divided into training, validation, and test sets based on protein domains. The experiments demonstrate that Confidence Bootstrapping significantly improves the performance of the DiffDock model on the DockGen benchmark.

**Strengths:**
- The paper addresses a significant problem in the field of molecular docking, focusing on the generalizability of models across different protein domains.
- It introduces a novel benchmark, DockGen, which is designed to test the generalization of docking models across the proteome, and proposes a new training method, Confidence Bootstrapping, to improve the generalization of docking models.
- The paper is well-written, clear, and easy to follow, with a logical structure that makes it accessible to readers.
- The experiments are thorough, demonstrating the effectiveness of the proposed method in improving the performance of the DiffDock model on the DockGen benchmark.
- The paper provides a comprehensive review of existing docking benchmarks and discusses the limitations of these benchmarks in representing the full proteome diversity.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed discussion on the limitations of the proposed benchmark, which could affect its applicability in different scenarios.
- The methodology, particularly the Confidence Bootstrapping, is not sufficiently detailed, making it difficult for readers to fully understand the implementation and its impact on the results.
- The paper does not provide a detailed comparison with other methods such as AlphaDock, which could have strengthened the argument for the superiority of the proposed method.
- The paper does not discuss the scalability of the proposed method, which is crucial for its practical application in real-world scenarios.
- The paper could benefit from a more detailed discussion on the limitations of the proposed method and a broader comparison with other existing methods.

**Questions:**
- How does the performance of the DiffDock model on the DockGen benchmark compare to other existing benchmarks, such as those used in AlphaDock?
- Could the authors provide more details on the limitations of the proposed benchmark and how these limitations affect the generalizability of the results?
- How does the Confidence Bootstrapping method compare in terms of computational efficiency and practical applicability to other existing methods?
- Could the authors provide more details on the implementation of the Confidence Bootstrapping method, including the specifics of the confidence model and the scoring function used?
- How does the proposed method handle the potential overfitting of the confidence model to the training data, and what steps have been taken to mitigate this issue?
