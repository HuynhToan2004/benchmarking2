 **Summary:**
The paper explores active continual learning (ACL), a novel approach that integrates active learning (AL) and continual learning (CL) to manage the trade-off between retaining old knowledge and learning new tasks. The authors propose a forgetting-learning profile to evaluate the performance of ACL methods and compare them with existing CL and AL techniques. The experiments conducted on various datasets, including text and image classification, demonstrate the effectiveness of ACL in scenarios such as domain-IL and class-IL. However, the paper is criticized for its limited scope, lack of novelty, and inadequate theoretical justification for the proposed methods.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-organized, clearly written, and easy to follow, making it accessible to readers.
- The authors have conducted extensive experiments, covering a variety of active learning and continual learning methods, and have demonstrated the effectiveness of active continual learning (ACL) in scenarios such as domain-IL and class-IL.
- The proposed forgetting-learning profile is a novel and effective method for evaluating the performance of ACL methods.
- The paper provides a comprehensive analysis of the trade-off between forgetting and quick-learnability when combining AL and CL, which is a significant contribution to the field.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed discussion on the novelty of the proposed methods and does not adequately compare its contributions to existing literature.
- The experiments are limited to simple datasets such as P-MNIST and S-MNIST, which may not adequately demonstrate the effectiveness of ACL in more complex scenarios.
- The paper does not provide sufficient theoretical justification for the proposed forgetting-learning profile, which could enhance the credibility of the findings.
- There is a lack of discussion on the limitations of the proposed methods, which is crucial for understanding the scope and applicability of the research.
- The paper could benefit from a more thorough discussion of related works, especially in the context of active continual learning, to position the research within the broader literature.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors provide more detailed theoretical justification for the proposed forgetting-learning profile?
- How do the proposed methods perform in more complex scenarios beyond the simple datasets used in the experiments?
- What are the limitations of the proposed methods, and how do these impact the practical applicability of ACL in real-world scenarios?
- How does the paper compare its contributions to existing literature, particularly in terms of novelty and methodological advancements?
- Could the authors discuss the potential impact of task order on the performance of ACL, and how this might affect the generalizability of the findings?
