 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel approach to enhance the performance of Graph Neural Networks (GNNs) on heterophilic graphs by injecting homophily through a technique called "Spectral Highways." This method involves creating a new layer of nodes, each representing a cluster of nodes, and connecting these nodes to nodes within the same cluster based on importance measures like PageRank and DivRank. These new nodes are then used to augment the original graph, improving the information flow and connectivity between nodes. The proposed method is tested on various datasets, showing significant improvements in performance over existing methods, particularly in heterophilic settings. However, the paper lacks detailed theoretical analysis and comprehensive experimental validation, which could strengthen its claims.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, easy to follow, and provides a clear motivation for the proposed method.
- The proposed method, Spectral Highways, is simple yet effective, and the experimental results demonstrate significant performance improvements, especially on heterophilic datasets.
- The authors have conducted extensive experiments and comparisons with various baselines, including homophilic and heterophilic GNNs, which shows the effectiveness of the proposed method across different models.
- The method is innovative in its approach to injecting homophily into heterophilic graphs, which is a significant challenge in graph neural network research.
- The paper provides a comprehensive analysis of the homophily score, which is crucial for understanding the impact of the proposed method on graph structures.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed theoretical analysis of the proposed method, which could enhance the understanding of its effectiveness and limitations.
- The experimental setup and validation could be improved, particularly in terms of using more recent and diverse datasets, and conducting more extensive ablation studies to understand the impact of different hyperparameters.
- The paper does not adequately address the scalability of the proposed method, especially in larger or more complex graphs, which could affect its practical applicability.
- There are some issues with the clarity and presentation of the paper, such as unclear descriptions and potential errors in the text.
- The paper does not sufficiently discuss the limitations of the proposed method, which could help in understanding its applicability and potential drawbacks.

**Questions:**
