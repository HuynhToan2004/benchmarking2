 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel framework called DECU (Diffusion Ensembles for Capturing Uncertainty) for estimating epistemic uncertainty in diffusion models, focusing on high-dimensional image generation tasks. DECU utilizes ensembles of conditional diffusion models, with a subset of the network trained from scratch and the rest initialized with pre-trained weights. This approach allows for efficient computation and reduced training time. The paper also employs Pairwise-Distance Estimators (PaiDEs) to gauge mutual information, effectively capturing epistemic uncertainty. The method's effectiveness is demonstrated through experiments on the ImageNet dataset, showcasing the ability to distinguish undersampled classes and generate diverse images. However, the paper is criticized for its limited novelty and insufficient comparative analysis with existing methods.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel approach to estimating epistemic uncertainty in high-dimensional image generation tasks using diffusion models, which is a significant and relevant problem in the field.
- The methodology is well-structured and clearly presented, making it easy to understand and replicate.
- The use of pairwise distance estimators (PaiDEs) is innovative and effectively captures epistemic uncertainty, which is crucial for understanding and improving the performance of generative models.
- The paper is well-written, with clear figures and a clear exposition of the methodology, making it accessible to readers.
- The proposed method shows potential in reducing computational load by using only a fraction of the network for training, which could be beneficial for practical applications.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed comparison with existing methods, such as those mentioned in the literature, which could have provided a clearer context for the proposed method's contributions.
- The novelty of the method is questioned as similar approaches have been previously explored in other works, particularly in the use of ensembles and PaiDEs for uncertainty estimation.
- The paper does not adequately address the issue of diversity in generated images, which is a critical aspect of uncertainty estimation.
- The methodology section could be improved by including more detailed explanations and justifications for the choices made, such as the use of certain distance metrics and the selection of hyperparameters.
- The paper's experimental results are not sufficiently robust, with insufficient evidence to support the claims made about the method's effectiveness.
- The paper does not discuss the limitations of the proposed method, which could provide valuable insights into its applicability and potential drawbacks.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors provide a more detailed comparison with existing methods to better contextualize the contributions of their work?
- How does the proposed method compare in terms of computational efficiency and effectiveness with other methods, especially those that also use ensembles and PaiDEs for uncertainty estimation?
- Can the authors clarify the selection of hyperparameters, particularly the number of components in the ensemble and the branching point, and discuss the impact of these choices on the results?
- How does the method ensure that the uncertainty estimates are not biased by the specific choices made in the experimental setup, such as the selection of classes for evaluation?
- Could the authors provide more evidence to support the claims made about the method's effectiveness, perhaps through additional experiments or comparisons with other methods?
- How does the proposed method handle the issue of diversity in generated images, especially in undersampled classes?
