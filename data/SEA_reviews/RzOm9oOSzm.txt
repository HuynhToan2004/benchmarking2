 **Summary:**
The paper explores the linear mode connectivity (LMC) in neural networks, focusing on the relationship between neuron distribution entropy and LMC. It proposes that a decrease in neuron distribution entropy, which is equivalent to an increase in non-uniformity of neural distribution, can enhance LMC. The authors provide theoretical insights and empirical evidence to support this claim, using examples such as re-basin and pruning to validate their findings. The paper also discusses the implications of these findings for model ensemble and federated learning, suggesting potential improvements in these areas. Despite its theoretical and empirical strengths, the paper suffers from some presentation issues, such as unclear definitions and potential misleading claims.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel approach to studying the linear mode connectivity (LMC) in neural networks, focusing on the relationship between neuron distribution entropy and LMC.
- The theoretical framework presented is solid, and the experiments are well-designed to validate the theoretical results.
- The paper provides a clear and detailed explanation of the theoretical findings, which could be of interest to researchers in the field.
- The paper is well-written, making it accessible and understandable, with a clear motivation and well-defined problem statement.
- The theoretical analysis is supported by empirical results, which are convincingly presented.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a clear definition of the term "neuron distribution," which is crucial for understanding the theoretical framework.
- The claim that "increasing non-uniformity in neuron parameter distribution leads to the enhancement in linear mode connectivity after applying re-basin" is not sufficiently supported by the theoretical results.
- The experimental results are not sufficiently convincing, with some results not showing significant improvements over existing methods like re-basin.
- The paper contains several presentation issues, such as unclear figures and undefined notations, which could hinder understanding.
- The theoretical analysis and experimental results are not well-integrated, which makes it difficult to draw clear conclusions from the empirical evidence.
- The paper does not sufficiently address the limitations of its work, which could help in understanding the scope and applicability of the findings.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors clarify the definition of "neuron distribution" and its implications for the theoretical framework?
- How does the paper define "re-basin" and "linear mode connectivity," and what are the specific metrics used to measure these concepts?
- What is the significance of the "non-uniformity" mentioned in the paper, and how does it relate to the theoretical findings?
- How does the paper justify the claims made about the relationship between neuron distribution entropy and LMC, especially in light of the empirical results?
- Could the authors provide a clearer explanation of the experimental results, particularly the improvements observed in the pruning experiments?
- How does the paper address the potential misleading claims regarding the relationship between non-uniformity and LMC, especially in light of the empirical evidence presented?
