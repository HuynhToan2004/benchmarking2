 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel system, RoboTool, which leverages large language models (LLMs) to enable robots to creatively use tools in complex tasks involving implicit physical constraints and long-term planning. The system consists of four main components: an Analyzer, a Planner, a Calculator, and a Coder. The Analyzer extracts key concepts from natural language instructions, the Planner generates comprehensive strategies based on these concepts, the Calculator computes parameters for each skill, and the Coder translates these plans into executable Python code. The paper demonstrates the effectiveness of RoboTool through extensive experiments, showcasing its ability to handle tasks that are otherwise infeasible without creative tool use. However, concerns were raised about the system's reliance on the accuracy of initial state descriptions and the potential for overfitting to the specific tasks used in the evaluation.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, easy to follow, and effectively demonstrates the capabilities of the proposed system, RoboTool, through extensive experiments.
- The use of LLMs for tool-using tasks in robots is a novel approach that has shown promise in complex, long-horizon planning tasks.
- The proposed benchmark is a valuable resource for evaluating the creative tool-use capability of robots, and the paper provides a clear and detailed description of the benchmark tasks and the evaluation metrics used.
- The paper is well-organized, with a clear and detailed description of the benchmark tasks and the evaluation metrics used, and includes supplementary materials that provide additional insights into the system's capabilities.
- The proposed method is a significant advancement in the field of robotics, demonstrating the potential of LLMs to enhance robot capabilities in complex tasks.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper does not adequately address the potential for overfitting of the LLM to the specific tasks used in the evaluation, which could limit the generalizability of the results.
- The system's reliance on accurate initial state descriptions and the absence of a feedback mechanism in the Coder module could limit its effectiveness in real-world scenarios.
- The paper does not provide sufficient details on the implementation of the Calculator module, which is crucial for understanding the system's functionality and performance.
- The experimental setup and results are somewhat limited, with only six tasks evaluated, which may not be sufficient to convincingly demonstrate the system's capabilities across a broader range of scenarios.
- There is a lack of clarity on how the system handles uncertainty in the initial state description and how it manages the trade-off between exploration and exploitation in its planning process.

**Questions:**
- How does the system handle uncertainty in the initial state description, and what mechanisms are in place to manage the trade-off between exploration and exploitation in its planning process?
- Can you provide more details on the implementation of the Calculator module, including its input and output formats and the specific algorithms or techniques used?
- How does the system ensure the accuracy of the initial state description, and what are the implications if this description is inaccurate?
- In the real-world experiments, how does the system handle unexpected events or changes in the environment, and what contingency plans are in place to adapt to these situations?
- Could you elaborate on the choice of tasks for the evaluation and how they were selected to represent a diverse set of scenarios?
- What are the specific challenges or limitations of the proposed method, and how do these compare to other existing methods in the field?
