 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel memory-augmented Adam optimizer, termed Adam+CM, which aims to escape sharp minima by storing critical momenta from previous iterations. This approach contrasts with the traditional Adam+CG method, which stores critical gradients. Theoretical and empirical evidence support the claim that Adam+CM can achieve better generalization performance by converging to flatter minima. The paper provides a convergence analysis in simplified settings and demonstrates the effectiveness of Adam+CM through experiments on various datasets, including a language modeling task and image classification. However, the paper is critiqued for its limited novelty, as it primarily modifies the existing Adam+CG method, and the empirical results are not convincingly superior to existing methods.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a new memory-augmented Adam optimizer, Adam+CM, which is a novel approach to escaping sharp minima by storing critical momenta, a departure from the traditional Adam+CG method.
- The theoretical convergence analysis of Adam+CM in simplified settings provides a solid foundation for understanding the behavior of the optimizer.
- Empirical results demonstrate the effectiveness of Adam+CM in improving generalization performance, as evidenced by lower sharpness and better accuracy in various settings.
- The paper is well-written, making it easy to follow, and the authors provide detailed explanations and intuitions about the proposed method, which enhances understanding.
- The methodology is simple to implement, making it accessible to practitioners and researchers.

**Weaknesses:**
- The novelty of the paper is limited, as it primarily modifies the existing Adam+CG method by storing critical momenta instead of gradients, which might not be seen as a significant advancement.
- The empirical results do not convincingly demonstrate the superiority of Adam+CM over existing methods like Adam+CG, with marginal improvements in some cases.
- The paper does not adequately address the issue of gradient cancellation, which is a critical aspect of the proposed method.
- The convergence analysis provided is limited to quadratic loss functions, and it is unclear how the method performs in more complex, non-convex settings.
- The paper lacks a thorough comparison with other memory-augmented optimizers, which could have provided a more robust evaluation of the proposed method.
- The motivation behind the use of critical momenta is not clearly explained, and the paper does not provide a detailed discussion on the potential drawbacks or limitations of the proposed method.

**Questions:**
- Could you elaborate on the specific advantages of using critical momenta over critical gradients, and how these advantages translate into practical improvements in model performance?
- How does the proposed method address the issue of gradient cancellation, and what are the implications of this approach in terms of model convergence and generalization?
- Can you provide more details on the convergence analysis in more complex, non-convex settings, and how the results compare to those obtained in simpler quadratic settings?
- How does the paper address the potential issue of high variance in the buffer of critical momenta, and what measures are in place to mitigate this?
- Could you provide a more detailed comparison with other memory-augmented optimizers, and discuss the implications of these comparisons for the proposed method?
- How do the authors justify the choice of Adam+CM as the preferred method over other optimizers, especially in light of the marginal improvements observed in some experiments?
