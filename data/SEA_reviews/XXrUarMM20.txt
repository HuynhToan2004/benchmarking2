 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel fast convolution algorithm using symbolic arithmetic in the Fourier domain, aiming to enhance the efficiency and reduce the computational complexity of convolutional neural networks (CNNs). This approach utilizes ternary matrices and a post-training quantization method to minimize quantization errors. The algorithm is designed to be compatible with low-precision quantization, which is crucial for deployment on edge devices. The paper presents theoretical and experimental results demonstrating the effectiveness of the proposed method, showing improvements in computational efficiency and accuracy. However, the paper suffers from unclear writing and lacks detailed comparisons with existing methods, making it difficult to fully grasp the advantages of the proposed approach.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel approach by utilizing symbolic arithmetic in the Fourier domain to accelerate the convolution process, which is both innovative and well-explained.
- The authors provide a detailed explanation of the symbolic Fourier convolution algorithm, which is clear and easy to follow, enhancing the paper's readability and comprehensibility.
- The paper includes a comprehensive analysis of the computational complexity reduction and quantization error, which are crucial for understanding the practical implications of the proposed method.
- The experimental results are robust, showing a significant reduction in computational complexity and a negligible drop in accuracy, which is impressive and supports the effectiveness of the proposed method.
- The paper is well-organized, with clear writing and a logical flow, which makes it accessible to readers and facilitates understanding of the complex concepts presented.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks detailed comparisons with existing methods, which makes it difficult to assess the relative advantages of the proposed approach. Specifically, there is a need for more comprehensive comparisons with Winograd algorithms and other existing fast convolution methods.
- The paper could benefit from a more detailed discussion on the advantages of the proposed method over existing methods, particularly in terms of computational efficiency and accuracy.
- The paper's writing quality needs improvement, particularly in the clarity of the introduction and the organization of the content. There are several instances of unclear or confusing statements that could be clarified to enhance understanding.
- The paper's claims regarding the reduction in multiplicative complexity and the accuracy of the proposed method are not convincingly supported by the data presented, which could undermine the credibility of the results.
- The paper does not adequately address the limitations of the proposed method, which could include the applicability to different types of neural networks or hardware platforms, and the potential impact of the post-training quantization method on the accuracy of the model.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors provide a more detailed comparison with existing methods, particularly Winograd algorithms and other fast convolution methods, to better understand the advantages of the proposed approach?
- How does the proposed method compare in terms of computational efficiency and accuracy with other existing methods, especially under different hardware and software configurations?
- Could the authors clarify the claims regarding the reduction in multiplicative complexity and the accuracy of the proposed method, perhaps by providing more detailed experimental data or a more rigorous analysis?
- How does the post-training quantization method affect the accuracy of the model, and what are the implications of this method for the overall performance of the model?
- Could the authors address the writing quality issues, particularly in the introduction and the organization of the content, to enhance the readability and comprehensibility of the paper?
