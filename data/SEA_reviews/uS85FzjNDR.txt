 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel framework named Uni-HSSL, designed to address the challenges of heterogeneous semi-supervised learning (HSSL) where labeled and unlabeled data come from different domains. This framework, which includes components such as pseudo-labeling, prototype alignment, and mixup, leverages a unified framework to handle domain shifts and class-level discrepancies. The methodology is evaluated on several datasets, demonstrating superior performance over existing methods. However, concerns were raised about the fairness of comparisons, the novelty of the approach, and the clarity of the experimental setup, particularly the division of datasets and the handling of pseudo-labels.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel framework, Uni-HSSL, that addresses the heterogeneous semi-supervised learning problem, which is a significant contribution to the field.
- The proposed methodology is well-motivated, and the experiments conducted are comprehensive, demonstrating the effectiveness of the proposed method.
- The paper is well-written, making it easy to follow, and the experimental results are convincing, showing that the proposed method outperforms existing methods in most cases.
- The use of a unified framework for heterogeneous semi-supervised learning (HSSL) is innovative, and the methodology is simple and effective.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a clear explanation of why the proposed method, which utilizes a unified framework for HSSL, performs better than existing methods. More detailed analysis is needed to substantiate the claims.
- The experimental setup could be improved by using more recent and fair comparisons, especially in terms of the datasets used and the comparisons made.
- The novelty of the proposed method is questionable as it combines existing techniques without sufficient innovation.
- The paper does not adequately address the issue of domain shifts and class-level discrepancies in the heterogeneous data, which are critical challenges in the HSSL setting.
- The paper lacks a thorough discussion on the limitations of the proposed method and does not include error bars in the results, which could affect the reliability of the findings.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors provide a more detailed explanation of why the proposed method performs better than existing methods?
- How does the proposed method handle the domain shift and class-level discrepancies in the heterogeneous data?
- Can the authors clarify the experimental setup, particularly the division of datasets and the handling of pseudo-labels?
- Is there a possibility to include more recent and fair comparisons in future experiments?
- How do the authors address the concerns regarding the novelty and the fairness of the comparisons made in the paper?
