 **Summary:**
The paper introduces AFDistill, a method that leverages knowledge distillation to create a faster and differentiable model for inverse protein folding, focusing on structure consistency regularization. By using confidence metrics like pTM and pLDDT scores from AlphaFold, AFDistill generates a distilled model that predicts these scores, enhancing the efficiency and accuracy of protein design. The model is evaluated against various baselines and shown to improve sequence recovery and protein diversity while maintaining structural consistency. Despite its innovative approach, concerns about the novelty and the comparative performance of AFDistill against other distillation-based models were raised.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel application of knowledge distillation to inverse protein folding, specifically using confidence metrics like pTM and pLDDT scores, which is a significant advancement in the field.
- The methodology is well-documented, making it reproducible, and the experiments are robust, demonstrating the effectiveness of the proposed method.
- The paper is well-written, clear, and easy to follow, making it accessible to a broad audience.
- The proposed method achieves better performance in terms of sequence recovery and protein diversity compared to non-regularized baselines.
- The paper provides a comprehensive evaluation of the proposed method, including various datasets and metrics, which supports the robustness of the results.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed discussion on the limitations of the method, which could help in understanding its applicability and potential drawbacks.
- There is a noticeable absence of comparisons with other distillation-based models, which could provide a clearer understanding of the advantages and disadvantages of AFDistill.
- The paper could benefit from a more thorough discussion on the novelty and significance of the proposed method, particularly in relation to existing methods in the field.
- The experimental results are not consistently strong across all datasets and models, which could undermine the generalizability of the findings.
- The paper could be improved by including more detailed explanations and justifications for the experimental setup and results, particularly in the appendix.
- There are minor issues with the presentation of the paper, such as inconsistent font sizes and missing references, which could detract from the professional quality of the work.

**Questions:**
- Could you clarify the role of the "structure consistency loss" in your method and how it compares to other regularization techniques used in protein design?
- How does the proposed method compare to other distillation-based models in terms of performance and efficiency?
- In light of the observed differences in performance between different datasets and models, can you provide more insights into the factors contributing to these variations?
- How does the proposed method handle the potential for overfitting, especially considering the use of a smaller dataset for training?
- Could you provide more details on the training process, particularly the hyperparameters used and their impact on the performance of the model?
- Can you explain the choice of using a different dataset for training the AFDistill model compared to the test set, and how this affects the generalizability of the results?
