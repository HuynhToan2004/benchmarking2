 **Summary:**
The paper explores the Error Feedback (EF) mechanism in distributed training, aiming to improve the convergence rate of EF21 by replacing the quadratic mean of smoothness constants with the arithmetic mean. This improvement is achieved through client cloning and weighted update schemes, which are theoretically analyzed and validated through experiments. The paper also introduces a new variant of EF21, EF21-W, and extends the analysis to other settings such as stochastic gradients and partial participation. The theoretical contributions are supported by experiments, although the paper lacks detailed comparisons with other methods and a broader range of experiments.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written and easy to follow, with clear explanations and motivations for the proposed methods.
- The paper introduces a novel approach to improving the convergence rate of EF21 by replacing the quadratic mean of smoothness constants with the arithmetic mean, which is a significant theoretical contribution.
- The experiments conducted support the theoretical claims, showing the effectiveness of the proposed methods.
- The paper provides a comprehensive theoretical analysis, including proofs for the main theorems, which strengthens the credibility of the findings.
- The paper is well-structured, with clear sections and subsections that guide the reader through the content, making it accessible and understandable.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a thorough comparison with other methods, particularly in terms of communication complexity and convergence rates, which could have provided a more robust evaluation of the proposed methods.
- The experiments are limited in scope, focusing primarily on logistic regression and linear regression models, and do not include more complex models such as neural networks.
- There is a lack of detailed discussion on the practical implications of the proposed methods, particularly in terms of computational complexity and memory requirements.
- The paper does not adequately address the computational efficiency and scalability of the proposed methods, especially in large-scale distributed training scenarios.
- The paper could benefit from a more comprehensive literature review and a clearer discussion on the relationship of the proposed methods to existing works.
- The paper lacks a detailed discussion on the limitations and potential negative societal impacts of the proposed methods.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors provide a more detailed comparison of the communication complexity and convergence rates of EF21-W with other methods, particularly in scenarios where EF21-W may not perform as well?
- How do the proposed methods perform in scenarios where the smoothness constants vary significantly across different clients?
- Could the authors provide more details on the computational complexity and memory requirements of the proposed methods, especially in large-scale distributed training scenarios?
- What are the practical implications of the proposed methods in terms of scalability and efficiency in real-world applications?
- How do the proposed methods compare in terms of convergence rates and communication complexity with other methods that use different compression operators or different feedback mechanisms?
- Could the authors discuss the potential negative societal impacts of the proposed methods, particularly in terms of privacy and security in distributed training scenarios?
