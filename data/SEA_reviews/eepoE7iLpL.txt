 **Summary:**
The paper introduces INSET, a novel method for learning permutation-invariant representations for neural subset selection tasks. INSET extends the probabilistic approach of EquiVSet by incorporating the superset V into the subset S, enhancing the model's ability to capture set-level information. The authors propose a theoretical framework based on sufficient statistics and symmetry groups, aiming to model the conditional distribution P(Y|S,V). The method is evaluated on various datasets, demonstrating improvements over existing methods. Despite its theoretical contributions and empirical validations, the paper is criticized for its lack of clarity in presentation and insufficient discussion on the limitations and broader impacts of the work.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, clear, and easy to follow, with a good balance between theoretical and empirical content.
- The introduction of the INSET model, which integrates the representation of the superset into the subset, is a novel and interesting approach to neural subset selection.
- The methodology is supported by rigorous theoretical analysis, which is well-explained and relevant to the problem at hand.
- The paper includes extensive experiments that demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed method, showing improvements over existing methods in various scenarios.
- The paper is well-structured, with clear definitions and explanations of the mathematical concepts used, making it accessible to a broad audience.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a comprehensive discussion on the limitations of the proposed method, particularly in terms of its applicability to different types of data and its computational efficiency.
- There is a lack of clarity in the presentation of the paper, with some sections being difficult to follow due to complex mathematical notation and insufficient explanation of key concepts.
- The paper does not sufficiently discuss the broader impacts of the work, such as its potential societal implications and ethical considerations.
- There are some inconsistencies and errors in the mathematical notation and definitions, which could confuse readers and undermine the credibility of the paper.
