 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel image generation evaluation metric named CIGE, which utilizes a consensus-based approach to measure the quality of generated images. This metric, which is strongly correlated with existing metrics like FID and IS, is designed to improve the evaluation process by directly optimizing non-differentiable metrics through reinforcement learning. The CIGE metric is tested on various datasets, including LSUN and ImageNet, and the results demonstrate its effectiveness in enhancing image generation models. However, the paper is criticized for its lack of novelty, unclear motivation, and insufficient experimental validation. The methodology, while innovative in its approach, is seen as incremental and not sufficiently distinct from existing methods.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel evaluation metric for image generation, named CIGE, which is strongly correlated with existing metrics like FID and IS but requires fewer generated images for evaluation.
- The use of reinforcement learning to optimize non-differentiable metrics is an innovative approach that bridges the gap between training and evaluation phases, potentially improving image generation performance.
- The methodology is clearly presented, making it easy to follow, and the experiments are comprehensive, demonstrating the effectiveness of the proposed approach.
- The paper addresses the problem of the gap between training and evaluation metrics in image generation, which is a significant issue in the field.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks novelty as it primarily replaces the FID metric with the CIGE metric without providing significant new insights or methodologies.
- The motivation behind the CIGE metric is unclear, and the paper does not adequately explain why this metric is superior to existing metrics.
- The paper does not sufficiently compare the CIGE metric with other metrics like FID, and the experimental results do not convincingly demonstrate the superiority of the CIGE metric.
- The paper suffers from poor writing quality, with several typos and grammatical errors that detract from its professionalism.
- The paper lacks a detailed comparison with other evaluation metrics, such as human preference-based evaluations, which are crucial for validating the effectiveness of the proposed metric.
- The paper does not discuss the limitations of the proposed method, which is essential for understanding its applicability and potential drawbacks.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors clarify the motivation behind the CIGE metric and how it addresses the limitations of existing metrics like FID and IS?
- How does the CIGE metric compare to other metrics in terms of computational efficiency and practical applicability in real-world scenarios?
- What are the specific advantages of using the CIGE metric over other metrics like FID and IS, especially in terms of evaluating image generation models?
- How does the proposed method perform when compared to other reinforcement learning-based methods for image generation, such as those mentioned in related works?
- Can the authors provide more detailed experimental results, including human preference-based evaluations, to validate the effectiveness of the CIGE metric?
- How does the paper address the issue of the CIGE metric being potentially biased towards certain types of images, such as those with similar token distributions?
