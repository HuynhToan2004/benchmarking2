 **Summary:**
The paper introduces STLLM, a novel approach that integrates Large Language Models (LLMs) with Graph Neural Networks (GNNs) to address the challenges of spatio-temporal prediction in urban systems. STLLM leverages LLMs to capture implicit spatio-temporal dependencies and preserves spatial semantics through a cross-view mutual information maximization paradigm. The methodology involves the use of LLMs to generate node embeddings from textual descriptions, which are then aligned with GNN-based structural embeddings to enhance spatio-temporal understanding and forecasting. The paper presents extensive experiments and comparisons with state-of-the-art baselines, demonstrating the effectiveness of the proposed method in various prediction tasks. However, concerns are raised about the clarity of the paper's presentation, the lack of detailed theoretical analysis, and the need for more comprehensive experimental validations.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces an innovative approach by integrating Large Language Models (LLMs) with Graph Neural Networks (GNNs) to tackle spatio-temporal prediction tasks, which is a novel and promising direction.
- The methodology is well-explained, and the paper is generally well-written, making it accessible and understandable to readers.
- Extensive experiments and comparisons with state-of-the-art baselines are conducted, demonstrating the effectiveness of the proposed method in various prediction tasks.
- The use of LLMs to capture implicit spatio-temporal dependencies and preserve spatial semantics in urban space is a novel and potentially impactful approach.
- The paper is original in its approach to integrating LLMs with GNNs for spatio-temporal prediction, which is a significant contribution to the field.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks clarity in its presentation, particularly in the introduction and methodology sections, which could benefit from more detailed explanations and examples.
- The theoretical analysis is not sufficiently detailed, and the paper could benefit from a more comprehensive theoretical discussion on the integration of LLMs with GNNs and the implications for spatio-temporal prediction.
- The novelty of the approach is questioned as similar methods have been explored in other domains, and the paper does not sufficiently differentiate its approach from existing literature.
- The paper does not provide sufficient details on the implementation of the LLM-based spatio-temporal knowledge learning, which could be crucial for reproducibility and understanding the method's effectiveness.
- The experimental setup and results lack clarity, particularly in the description of the datasets used and the results presented, which could affect the reader's ability to evaluate the method's effectiveness.
- The paper does not adequately address the computational complexity and scalability of the proposed method, which is a significant concern for practical applications.
