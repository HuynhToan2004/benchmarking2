 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel training-free method for text-to-image generation, specifically focusing on visual-text-to-image (TV2I) tasks. It leverages the diffusion process within diffusion models to achieve unidirectional diffusion from visual conditions, which is a departure from traditional bidirectional diffusion. The method involves seed initialization and cyclic one-way diffusion to maintain visual fidelity while conditioning on textual prompts. The approach is evaluated against several baselines, demonstrating superior performance in terms of visual and textual fidelity, and is shown to be faster than existing methods. However, concerns are raised about the novelty of the method, the clarity of the writing, and the lack of comprehensive comparisons with more recent and relevant baselines.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel approach to text-to-image generation by leveraging the diffusion process within diffusion models, which is a significant advancement in the field.
- The method is training-free, making it more accessible and easier to implement compared to other methods.
- The paper is well-organized, with clear figures and a logical structure that aids in understanding the proposed method.
- The experimental results demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed method in maintaining both visual and textual fidelity, showing a clear advantage over existing methods.
- The paper provides a detailed analysis of the diffusion process within diffusion models, which is both interesting and insightful.
- The method is evaluated on several benchmarks and shows promising results, indicating its potential for practical applications.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks clarity in certain sections, particularly in the description of the method and the results. More detailed explanations and examples are needed to enhance understanding.
- The novelty of the method is questionable, as similar approaches have been explored in previous works, such as those by Zhang et al. and Mokady et al.
- The paper does not adequately compare its method with more recent and relevant baselines, such as those by Li et al., Shi et al., and Chen et al., which could have strengthened the argument for the superiority of the proposed method.
- There are several typographical and formatting errors throughout the paper, which detract from its professional presentation.
- The paper's claims regarding the novelty and effectiveness of the proposed method are not convincingly supported by the experimental results, which are sometimes limited or not sufficiently detailed.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors provide a more detailed explanation of the method, possibly with a step-by-step example or a simplified diagram to aid understanding?
- How does the proposed method compare to more recent and relevant baselines, such as those by Li et al., Shi et al., and Chen et al.?
- Why were these specific baselines chosen for comparison, and what are the implications of not comparing with other potentially relevant methods?
- Could the authors clarify the choice of visual conditions and the role of seed initialization in the results?
- How does the proposed method handle different types of visual conditions, such as those not involving faces, and what are the implications for the effectiveness of the method in these cases?
