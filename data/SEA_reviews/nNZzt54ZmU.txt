 **Summary:**
The paper explores the expressivity of ReLU neural networks with intra-layer links, which can be viewed as a variant of ResNet architectures. The authors demonstrate that these networks can represent more complex functions than traditional fully-connected networks, even with fewer parameters. The paper provides theoretical proofs and empirical evidence to support these claims, including the construction of functions with specific properties and the analysis of their expressivity. The findings suggest that intra-layer links can significantly enhance the expressive power of shallow networks, potentially reducing the width needed to represent certain functions. However, the paper's contributions are seen as incremental, and the practical implications of these findings are not clearly articulated.

- The paper is well-written and clear, with a good introduction and background on related work.
- The authors provide a comprehensive proof of the construction of functions with specific properties and an analysis of their expressivity.
- The paper demonstrates that intra-layer links can significantly enhance the expressive power of shallow networks, potentially reducing the width needed to represent certain functions.

- The paper introduces a novel perspective on the relationship between depth and width in neural networks, which is a significant contribution to the field.
