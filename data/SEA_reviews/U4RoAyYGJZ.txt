 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel adaptive sampling method named Proximal Robust On-Policy Sampling (PROPS) to address the issue of sampling error in on-policy reinforcement learning (RL) algorithms. By maintaining a replay buffer of on-policy data, PROPS adjusts the sampling policy to maximize the probability of sampling under-sampled actions, thereby reducing the sampling error. The methodology is evaluated against existing baselines, demonstrating improved data efficiency and reduced sampling error in both continuous and discrete action spaces. The paper also discusses the limitations and potential improvements, such as the need for theoretical analysis and a broader empirical validation across different RL algorithms.

**Strengths:**
- The paper addresses a significant and relevant problem in the field of reinforcement learning (RL), specifically the issue of sampling error in on-policy algorithms.
- The proposed method, PROPS, is novel and provides a practical solution to improve data efficiency and reduce sampling error, which is demonstrated through empirical results.
- The paper is well-written, making it easy to follow, and the experiments are thorough, including both continuous and discrete action spaces.
- The use of a replay buffer to store on-policy data and the adaptive sampling method are innovative approaches that could potentially enhance the performance of on-policy RL algorithms.
- The paper provides a clear and intuitive explanation of the problem and the proposed solution, which is supported by both theoretical and empirical evidence.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed discussion on the theoretical underpinnings of the PROPS algorithm, which could strengthen the argument for its effectiveness.
- The empirical evaluation could be expanded to include more complex environments and a broader range of RL algorithms to validate the generalizability of the findings.
- The paper does not adequately address the computational cost of the proposed method, which could be a significant concern for practical applications.
- There is a lack of discussion on the limitations of the proposed method, particularly in scenarios where the method might not perform as well or could lead to unexpected results.
- The paper could benefit from a more detailed discussion on the hyperparameter tuning and its impact on the results, as well as a comparison with other state-of-the-art RL algorithms.
