 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel post-hoc zero-shot OOD detection method, NegLabel, which leverages a large set of negative labels from extensive corpus databases to enhance OOD detection. The methodology includes a NegMining algorithm for selecting high-quality negative labels and a new scheme for the OOD score that effectively utilizes the knowledge of vision-language models. The paper presents theoretical analysis and extensive experiments demonstrating state-of-the-art performance on various OOD detection benchmarks, showcasing robustness against domain shifts. However, concerns are raised about the scalability of the method, the impact of hyperparameters, and the lack of detailed comparisons with existing methods.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel post-hoc zero-shot OOD detection method, NegLabel, which leverages a large set of negative labels from extensive corpus databases, providing a novel perspective to distinguish OOD samples.
- The NegMining algorithm is proposed to select high-quality negative labels, enhancing the distinction between ID and OOD samples, and a new scheme for the post-hoc OOD score is designed that effectively utilizes the knowledge encoded in VLMs.
- The paper presents theoretical analysis and extensive experimental analyses, demonstrating state-of-the-art performance on multiple OOD detection benchmarks and generalizing well on various VLM architectures.
- The method shows remarkable robustness against diverse domain shifts, indicating its practical applicability in real-world scenarios.
- The paper is well-written, clear, and easy to follow, with a clear problem setting and detailed explanations of the methodology.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed comparison with existing methods, particularly in terms of computational cost and scalability.
- The selection of hyperparameters, such as the number of negative labels and the grouping strategy, is not thoroughly analyzed, which could affect the reproducibility and applicability of the method.
- The method's performance on small-scale datasets and its scalability to larger datasets are not adequately addressed.
- The paper does not provide a detailed discussion on the limitations of the proposed method, which is crucial for understanding its applicability and potential drawbacks.
- The method's reliance on a large set of negative labels raises concerns about the scalability and the potential for overfitting, especially in scenarios where the ID and OOD data are not well-separated.
- The paper does not include a detailed discussion on the limitations of the proposed method, which is crucial for understanding its applicability and potential drawbacks.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors provide a detailed comparison with existing methods in terms of computational cost, scalability, and performance?
- How does the method perform on small-scale datasets, and what are the implications for its scalability to larger datasets?
- What are the limitations of the proposed method, and how do these affect its applicability and performance in different scenarios?
- Can the authors provide a more detailed discussion on the limitations of the proposed method, including its scalability and potential for overfitting?
- How do the authors address the issue of overfitting, especially in scenarios where the ID and OOD data are not well-separated?
- Can the authors provide more details on the selection of hyperparameters, such as the number of negative labels and the grouping strategy, and how these affect the method's performance?
