 **Summary:**
The paper presents a differentially private (DP) algorithm for clustering well-clustered graphs, which are characterized by high inner conductance and low outer conductance. The algorithm extends the work of Chen et al. by incorporating a semi-definite program (SDP) and a noisy solution to achieve differential privacy. Theoretical analysis and experimental results are provided to support the algorithm's effectiveness, demonstrating that it can recover clusters with similar error rates as non-private algorithms. The paper also discusses the limitations of pure DP algorithms and provides a proof sketch for a lower bound on the error of DP algorithms for clustering well-clustered graphs.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, making the content accessible and understandable.
- The proof sketch for a lower bound on the error of DP algorithms for clustering well-clustered graphs is a significant contribution.
- The algorithm's output is shown to have similar error rates as non-private algorithms, demonstrating its effectiveness.
- The paper provides a clear and detailed explanation of the algorithm and its theoretical underpinnings, which are supported by experimental results.
- The paper addresses an important problem of clustering well-clustered graphs, which is relevant to various applications in computer science and machine learning.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed discussion on the limitations of the proposed algorithm, particularly in terms of its applicability to other types of graphs or in scenarios where the graph is not well-clustered.
- The paper's novelty is questioned, as it appears to be a straightforward extension of existing work by Chen et al.
- There is a lack of comparison with other DP algorithms for graph clustering, which could provide a better understanding of the algorithm's performance relative to existing methods.
- The paper's assumptions about the graph's structure (e.g., well-clustered, c-balanced) may limit its applicability to more general graphs.
- The paper does not adequately discuss the limitations of pure DP algorithms, which could be a significant drawback for some applications.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors clarify the differences between their work and the work by Chen et al., particularly in terms of novelty and methodological contributions?
- How does the algorithm perform in scenarios where the graph is not well-clustered, and what are the implications of this for the algorithm's utility?
- Could the authors provide more details on the limitations of pure DP algorithms and how their proposed algorithm addresses these limitations?
- Is there a possibility to extend the algorithm to handle more general graph structures, and if so, what would be the implications for its performance and applicability?
- Can the authors discuss the potential for using their algorithm in real-world applications, and what are the practical implications of its use in such scenarios?
