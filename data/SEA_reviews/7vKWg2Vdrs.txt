 **Summary:**
The paper introduces the LayerCAM-enabled backdoor detector (LeBD) for monitoring backdoor attacks in object detection networks, specifically focusing on YOLOv5. LeBD utilizes LayerCAM to locate triggers and provides real-time risk warnings, enhancing precision through counterfactual attribution LayerCAM (CA-LeBD). The paper evaluates the performance of these detectors on both digital and physical world scenarios, demonstrating their effectiveness in real-time detection and mitigation of backdoor effects. However, the paper's novelty is questioned due to its similarity to existing methods, and concerns are raised about the practicality of the proposed solutions in real-world scenarios.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, making it easy to follow and understand the proposed methods.
- The research addresses a significant and timely issue of backdoor attacks in object detection, which is crucial for the security of autonomous driving systems.
- The paper introduces a novel defense method that can detect backdoor attacks in real-time, which is particularly relevant for applications in the physical world.
- The experiments conducted on both digital and physical world scenarios demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed defense methods.
- The paper provides a detailed analysis of the proposed method and its performance, which aids in understanding the method's strengths and limitations.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks novelty as it primarily uses existing methods like LayerCAM and counterfactual attribution, which have been previously used in similar contexts.
- The practicality of the proposed solutions is questionable, especially the real-time performance of the defense mechanisms, which might not be practical in all scenarios.
- The paper does not sufficiently discuss the limitations of the proposed methods, which could lead to a misleading assessment of their effectiveness.
- The paper's claims about the real-time performance of the defense mechanisms are not convincingly supported by the experimental results.
- The paper does not adequately address the generalizability of the proposed methods across different object detection models or in scenarios with varying trigger sizes.
- The paper could benefit from a more thorough discussion on the ethical implications of the proposed methods, particularly in the context of autonomous driving systems.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors provide a more detailed comparison of their proposed methods with existing ones, highlighting the specific advantages and novel contributions of their approach?
- How do the authors ensure the practicality of their proposed solutions in real-world scenarios, particularly in terms of computational efficiency and real-time performance?
- Could the authors clarify the experimental setup, especially the conditions under which the defense mechanisms were tested, to better understand their effectiveness in practical scenarios?
- How do the authors address the generalizability of their methods across different object detection models and in scenarios with varying trigger sizes?
- What are the specific ethical considerations of the proposed methods, and how do the authors address these concerns in their research?
