 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel approach to supervised learning by integrating reinforcement learning concepts, particularly the temporal difference (TD) algorithm, into the training process. This integration allows for the handling of various types of labels, including binary and multi-class, by transforming the labels into a continuous space for TD updates. Theoretical analysis and empirical evaluations are provided to support the convergence of the proposed method under specific conditions. The paper also discusses the practical implications of this method, such as the selection of hyperparameters and the impact of the transition matrix on the model's performance.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, clear, and easy to follow, with a clear motivation for the research.
- The approach is novel, integrating reinforcement learning with supervised learning, which is not commonly seen.
- Theoretical analysis is provided, which supports the convergence of the proposed method under specific conditions.
- Empirical evaluations are conducted to validate the practical utility of the proposed method.
- The paper introduces a novel approach to generalized temporal difference learning for supervised learning, which is both theoretically sound and empirically validated.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed discussion on the advantages of the proposed method over existing methods, particularly in terms of generalization performance.
- The theoretical results are not surprising, and the novelty of the approach is questioned as similar ideas have been explored in previous works.
- The empirical evaluations are limited to small datasets, which might not adequately demonstrate the scalability or effectiveness of the proposed method on larger, more complex datasets.
- The paper does not discuss the limitations of the proposed method, which could provide a more comprehensive understanding of its applicability and potential challenges.
- There is a lack of clarity in some sections of the paper, particularly in the definitions and notations used, which could make it difficult for readers to follow the arguments and results.
