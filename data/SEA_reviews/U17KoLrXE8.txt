 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a new dataset and a novel metric for evaluating image captioning models, focusing on the gap between human-generated and model-generated captions. The dataset, ObjectNet Captions, is designed to challenge models by reducing spurious correlations and featuring longer, more detailed captions. The new metric, HUMANr, is based on human judgments and aims to provide a more realistic evaluation of model performance. The paper also discusses the limitations of current metrics and the need for a more robust evaluation framework.

**Strengths:**
- The paper addresses a critical issue in image captioning, focusing on the gap between model-generated and human-generated captions, which is often overlooked by existing metrics.
- It introduces a new dataset, ObjectNet Captions, which includes longer and more detailed captions, providing a more comprehensive evaluation of image captioning models.
- The paper proposes a new metric, HUMANr, which is based on human judgments and is a significant improvement over traditional metrics that often overstate model performance.
- The paper is well-written, clear, and easy to follow, making it accessible to a broad audience.
- The experiments conducted are thorough, providing valuable insights into the performance of current models and the limitations of existing metrics.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper could benefit from a more detailed discussion on the limitations of the new dataset, ObjectNet Captions, particularly concerning its diversity and the potential biases it might introduce.
- There is a lack of clarity on how the HUMANr metric is computed and whether it is a valid measure of model performance. The paper does not provide enough evidence to support its claims of HUMANr's effectiveness or the reliability of its computations.
- The paper does not sufficiently address the generalizability of the findings to other datasets or the applicability of the new metric to other tasks.
- The paper could benefit from a more rigorous validation of the new metric, HUMANr, and a clearer explanation of its computational process and the assumptions it makes.
- There is a lack of discussion on how the new dataset and metric might be applied in real-world scenarios, and the paper could benefit from more practical examples or case studies to illustrate their utility.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors clarify how the HUMANr metric is computed and provide more details on the computational process?
- How do the authors address the potential biases in the new dataset, ObjectNet Captions, and ensure its diversity and representativeness?
- Could the authors provide more evidence or experimental results to support the claims made about the limitations of current metrics and the effectiveness of HUMANr?
- How does the new dataset and metric compare to existing datasets and metrics in terms of their ability to evaluate model performance and the quality of generated captions?
- What are the implications of using HUMANr as a metric for evaluating model performance, and how does it compare to other metrics like CLIPScore in terms of reliability and validity?
- How can the findings of this study be applied in practical scenarios, and what are the potential limitations or challenges in implementing the new dataset and metric in real-world applications?
