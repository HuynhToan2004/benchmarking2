 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel approach to text-to-3D generation by injecting noise into the text embeddings, termed Noisy Text Fields (NTFs), which are then used to guide the generation of 3D shapes and textures. The proposed method leverages a combination of a text-3D discriminator and a text-2.5D discriminator to ensure geometry and texture consistency. The model is trained on the Objaverse dataset, achieving a large vocabulary and text consistency, and demonstrates potential for open-vocabulary 3D generation. The paper includes extensive experiments and ablation studies to validate the effectiveness of the proposed modules, although it suffers from some clarity issues and lacks detailed comparisons with existing methods.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, easy to follow, and includes comprehensive experiments and ablation studies that demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed modules.
- The introduction of Noisy Text Fields (NTFs) is a novel approach to expanding the vocabulary of 3D generation, which is a significant challenge in the field.
- The methodology is innovative, using text-3D and text-2.5D discriminators to ensure geometry and texture consistency, which is crucial for high-quality 3D generation.
- The paper addresses the issue of limited text-3D data by proposing a method that can learn a general latent code from limited 3D data, which is a significant challenge in the field.
- The paper provides a detailed explanation of the proposed method and the experiments are well-designed, demonstrating the potential of the proposed method in open-vocabulary 3D generation.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed comparison with existing methods, particularly in terms of generation quality, which is crucial for evaluating the effectiveness of the proposed method.
- There is a lack of clarity in some sections of the paper, particularly in the description of the methodology and the role of certain components such as the text-3D discriminator.
- The paper does not provide sufficient quantitative results to support the claims of high-quality generation, which could be improved by including metrics such as FID scores.
- The paper does not discuss the limitations of the proposed method, which could provide a more balanced view of the contributions and challenges of the research.
- The paper lacks a detailed discussion on the role of the text-3D discriminator and the use of different discriminators for different modalities, which could affect the fairness of the comparisons made.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors provide a detailed comparison with existing methods, particularly in terms of generation quality and training data scale?
- How does the proposed method handle the generation of complex objects or scenes, and what are the limitations in terms of the complexity of the generated content?
- Could the authors provide more details on the training process, such as the specifics of the training dataset and the role of different components in the training process?
- How does the proposed method handle the generation of objects with complex textures or intricate details, and what are the limitations in terms of the complexity of the generated textures?
- Could the authors discuss the limitations of the proposed method and how these limitations could be addressed in future research?
- How does the proposed method handle the generation of objects with different viewpoints, and what are the limitations in terms of the diversity of generated viewpoints?
