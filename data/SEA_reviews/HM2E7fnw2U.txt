 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel method for sequential disentanglement, focusing on the decomposition of input sequences into static and dynamic components. It proposes a new model that conditions the static component on a single sample from the sequence and subtracts the resulting code from the dynamic factors, thereby mitigating mode collapse. The approach is supported by extensive experiments across multiple datasets, demonstrating superior performance compared to existing methods. The paper also discusses the challenges and limitations of the proposed method, including its reliance on the choice of the first sample and its generalizability to more complex datasets.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, clear, and easy to follow, with a clear motivation and explanation of the proposed method.
- The proposed method is simple, intuitive, and effective, with extensive experiments on various datasets and comparisons with existing methods.
- The paper provides a novel approach to mitigating mode collapse in sequential disentanglement by conditioning the static component on a single sample from the sequence and subtracting the resulting code from the dynamic factors.
- The method is supported by a comprehensive evaluation, including visualizations and comparisons with state-of-the-art methods, showing promising results.
- The paper is original in its approach to addressing mode collapse in sequential disentanglement, which is a significant issue in the field.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed discussion on the limitations of the proposed method, particularly concerning the choice of the first sample and the generalizability of the method to more complex datasets.
- The experiments are limited to specific datasets, and it would be beneficial to see how the method performs on more diverse and complex datasets.
- The paper does not provide sufficient theoretical justification for the effectiveness of the proposed method, and the choice of the first sample as the anchor example lacks a strong theoretical basis.
- The paper could benefit from more extensive comparisons with other methods, especially in terms of computational efficiency and the handling of different types of data.
- The paper does not address the potential negative societal impacts of the proposed method, which is a significant omission.

**Questions:**
- How does the choice of the first sample affect the performance of the proposed method, and what would be the impact if a different sample were chosen as the anchor?
- Is there a theoretical justification for the effectiveness of the proposed method in mitigating mode collapse?
- How does the proposed method perform on more complex and diverse datasets, and what are the potential limitations in such scenarios?
- Can the authors provide more details on the computational efficiency of the proposed method compared to existing methods?
- How does the proposed method handle different types of data, such as audio and video, and what are the potential limitations in these domains?
- Could the authors elaborate on the potential negative societal impacts of the proposed method and how these might be mitigated?
