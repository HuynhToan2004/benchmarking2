 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel tensor decomposition method, Tensor Decomposition Based on Slices-Wise Low-Rank Priors (TDSL), and a new tensor norm, the Tensor U1 norm, to address the challenges of high-order tensor recovery, particularly in scenarios with non-smooth changes. The proposed methods aim to enhance the recovery of low-rank tensors by leveraging the slices-wise low-rankness, which is more robust to tensor permutation variability. The paper also presents a proximal algorithm combined with the Alternating Direction Method of Multipliers (PADMM) to solve the resulting tensor completion model, demonstrating theoretical guarantees for exact recovery. The method's effectiveness is validated through experiments on synthetic and real-world datasets, showcasing improvements over existing methods.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, with clear definitions, notations, and descriptions, making it easy to follow.
- The proposed method is novel, particularly the new tensor norm and the Tensor Decomposition Based on Slices-Wise Low-Rank Priors (TDSL), which is a significant contribution to the field.
- The paper provides theoretical guarantees for the exact recovery of the proposed models, which is a strong point.
- The experiments conducted are comprehensive, involving both synthetic and real-world datasets, and the results show improvements over existing methods.
- The paper is well-organized, with clear presentation of the proposed method and its applications.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed discussion on the computational complexity of the proposed method, which is crucial for understanding its practical applicability.
- The experiments are limited to specific datasets and do not include comparisons with a broader range of methods or datasets, which could strengthen the validation of the proposed method.
- The paper does not provide a detailed comparison with existing methods, such as KBR, which could have highlighted the advantages of the proposed method more clearly.
- The paper does not adequately discuss the limitations of the proposed method, which is essential for understanding its applicability and potential drawbacks.
- The paper does not provide a detailed discussion on the convergence properties of the proposed method, which is crucial for understanding its effectiveness in practical scenarios.
- The paper does not adequately address the scalability of the proposed method to higher-order tensors, which is a significant limitation given the increasing interest in handling high-order tensor data.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors provide a detailed discussion on the computational complexity of the proposed method?
- How does the proposed method perform when compared to other state-of-the-art methods on a broader range of datasets?
- Can the authors provide a detailed comparison with existing methods, such as KBR, to highlight the advantages of the proposed method?
- How does the proposed method handle the scalability issues when applied to higher-order tensors?
- Can the authors provide a detailed discussion on the convergence properties of the proposed method?
- How does the proposed method handle the issue of tensor permutation variability, particularly in scenarios where the sequence of slices is disordered?
- Can the authors provide a detailed discussion on the limitations of the proposed method and its potential drawbacks?
