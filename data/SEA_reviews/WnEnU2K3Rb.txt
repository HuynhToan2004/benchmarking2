 **Summary:**
The paper introduces two new datasets, HMDB-AD and HMDB-Violence, for video anomaly detection, focusing on action-based anomalies. It proposes a Multi-Frame Anomaly Detection (MFAD) method, which integrates deep video encoding features to capture long-term temporal dependencies and utilizes a density estimation algorithm to compute anomaly scores. The MFAD method is evaluated on both the proposed datasets and benchmark datasets, showing competitive results. However, the novelty of the method is questioned, as it heavily relies on existing frameworks and datasets, and the experimental results are not convincingly superior to state-of-the-art methods.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces two new datasets, HMDB-AD and HMDB-Violence, which are well-organized and designed, focusing on action-based anomalies, a category not covered by existing benchmarks.
- The proposed MFAD method is clearly explained and evaluated on both the proposed datasets and benchmark datasets, showing competitive results.
- The paper is well-written, with clear presentation and easy-to-understand figures, making it accessible and comprehensible.
- The introduction of a new approach to video anomaly detection, focusing on action-based anomalies, is a significant contribution to the field.
- The paper addresses the limitations of current video anomaly detection benchmarks and advocates for further research in general video anomaly detection.

**Weaknesses:**
- The novelty of the method is questioned as it heavily relies on existing frameworks and datasets, with limited technical contributions.
- The experimental results are not convincingly superior to state-of-the-art methods, and the performance improvements over existing methods are not significant.
- The paper lacks a detailed comparison with other state-of-the-art methods and does not include a discussion on the limitations of the proposed method.
- The dataset is relatively small, with only 16 classes, which may limit the generalizability of the results.
- The method's performance is not thoroughly analyzed, particularly in terms of how different feature types contribute to the final results.
- The paper does not include a detailed discussion on the limitations of the proposed method, which could provide valuable insights for future research.

**Questions:**
- How does the proposed MFAD method compare to other state-of-the-art methods in terms of performance and computational efficiency?
- Can the authors provide a detailed comparison of the proposed method with other existing methods, including a discussion on the limitations of their approach?
- How does the proposed method perform when the number of frames used for feature extraction varies?
- What are the potential applications of the proposed method in real-world scenarios?
- Could the authors provide more details on the feature extraction process, particularly how it contributes to the final results?
- How does the proposed method handle false positives and false negatives in video anomaly detection?
- Can the authors discuss the potential impact of their method on the broader field of video anomaly detection and provide suggestions for future research directions?
