 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel self-supervised learning approach for time series analysis, focusing on patch-independent pretraining tasks and model architectures. The authors argue that this methodology is superior to traditional patch-dependent methods, as it allows for better handling of distribution shifts and patch sizes. The paper includes extensive experiments and ablation studies to validate these claims, demonstrating the effectiveness of the proposed methods in both forecasting and classification tasks. Despite some concerns about the clarity of the writing and the experimental setup, the paper is generally well-received for its innovative approach and the robustness of its results.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel self-supervised learning approach for time series analysis, focusing on patch-independent pretraining tasks and model architectures, which is a significant departure from traditional methods.
- The proposed methodology shows robustness to distribution shifts and patch sizes, which is crucial for real-world applications.
- Extensive experiments and ablation studies are conducted to validate the proposed methods, demonstrating their effectiveness in both forecasting and classification tasks.
- The paper is well-written and easy to follow, with clear figures and tables that aid in understanding the proposed methods and their performance.
- The authors provide a strong baseline for future works in time series analysis, and the proposed methodology is expected to have a significant impact in the field.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper could benefit from improved writing and clarity, particularly in the introduction and related work sections.
- The experimental setup and results are not clearly explained, which could make it difficult for readers to fully understand the implications of the findings.
- The paper lacks a comprehensive discussion on the limitations and potential negative societal impacts of the proposed methods.
- The comparison with other methods and baseline models is not always fair or comprehensive, which could affect the perceived effectiveness of the proposed methods.
- The paper does not sufficiently address the computational efficiency of the proposed methods, which could be a concern for practical applications.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors clarify the differences between the proposed method and other existing methods, such as those mentioned in related works?
- How does the proposed method compare in terms of computational efficiency with other methods, especially in practical applications?
- Could the authors provide more detailed explanations of the experimental setups and results, particularly in the context of the ablation studies and the comparison with baseline models?
- What are the specific negative societal impacts of the proposed methods, and how do the authors address these concerns?
- How does the proposed method perform in different types of time series data, such as those with varying frequencies or characteristics?
