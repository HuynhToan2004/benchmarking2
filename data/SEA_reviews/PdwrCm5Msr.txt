 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel method for indoor mapping using audio beacons, employing a conditional GAN architecture to generate 2D floorplans from audio recordings. The approach leverages the reflections of audio signals to infer room structures and stitches them together to create a map. The methodology is tested both in simulated and real-world environments, showing promising results. However, the paper is criticized for its lack of clarity in explaining the GAN training and the contribution of the hint map, as well as the absence of a detailed discussion on related works and a comprehensive evaluation of the method's limitations and real-world applicability.

**Strengths:**
- The paper presents a novel approach using audio beacons for indoor mapping, which is a creative and innovative method.
- The methodology is well-structured, with a clear presentation of the problem formulation, methodology, and evaluation.
- The paper is well-written, making it accessible and easy to follow, which is crucial for understanding complex technical concepts.
- The use of a conditional GAN to generate 2D floorplans from audio signals is a significant contribution to the field, demonstrating a new application of this technology.
- The paper includes a detailed description of the experimental setup, including the generation of a synthetic dataset and real-world experiments, which enhances the credibility of the results.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed discussion on the training of the GAN, particularly how the hint map is integrated into the training process, which is crucial for understanding the method's effectiveness.
- The paper does not adequately compare its method with existing works, such as those using similar approaches like cGAN or other methods for generating 2D floorplans from audio signals.
- The evaluation of the method is limited, with only a few real-world examples and a lack of quantitative results, which makes it difficult to assess the method's performance in various scenarios.
- The paper does not discuss the limitations of its method, such as its applicability in cluttered environments or its sensitivity to user location errors, which could significantly affect the accuracy of the generated floorplans.
- There are some presentation issues, such as inconsistencies in figure labeling and a lack of clarity in the description of certain methodological aspects, which could confuse readers.

**Questions:**
- Could you provide more details on how the GAN is trained, specifically how the hint map is integrated into the training process?
- How does the method perform in environments with more complex geometries, such as rooms with multiple levels or irregular shapes?
- Can you elaborate on the evaluation metrics used in the paper, particularly how they were chosen and how they compare to other metrics that could be used?
- How does the method handle scenarios where the user's location is not accurately known, and what are the implications of this on the accuracy of the generated floorplans?
- Could you discuss the potential of extending the method to include the estimation of room sizes and the handling of cluttered environments?
- In light of the limitations discussed, what are the main challenges that need to be addressed to improve the robustness and applicability of the method in real-world scenarios?
