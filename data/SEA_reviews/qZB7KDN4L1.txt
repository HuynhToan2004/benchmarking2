 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel method for personalized image generation using a single reference image, which is a significant advancement over existing methods that typically require multiple images or test-time fine-tuning. The authors construct a large-scale dataset from the LAION-5B dataset, using Grounding DINO for object detection and BLIP-2 for caption generation. This dataset is then used to train a model that integrates textual and image-level information to generate images that adhere closely to the reference image. The method involves a combination of textual and image features, including location and bounding box information, to control the generation process. The paper demonstrates the effectiveness of the method through extensive experiments and comparisons with existing models, showing improvements in both image and text alignment. However, concerns were raised about the novelty of the method, the clarity of some experimental results, and the potential for overfitting due to the method's dependence on the reference image.

**Strengths:**
- The paper addresses an important and interesting problem by proposing a method that does not require test-time fine-tuning, which is a significant advantage over existing methods.
- The methodology is clearly described, making it easy to follow, and the paper includes a large-scale dataset construction, which is a significant contribution to the field.
- The paper includes extensive experiments and ablation studies that demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed method, particularly in achieving good image and text alignment.
- The paper is well-organized and the results are promising, showing that the method can handle multiple subjects and perform smooth interpolation between customized images and text descriptions.
- The proposed method is the first to achieve open-domain personalized image generation without test-time fine-tuning, which is a notable achievement.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks clarity in some sections, particularly in the description of the training data construction and the experimental results, which could benefit from more detailed explanations.
- The novelty of the method is questionable as it appears to be a combination of existing techniques without significant innovation.
- The paper does not adequately address the potential for overfitting due to the method's dependence on a single reference image, which could limit its applicability in real-world scenarios.
- The paper could benefit from a more thorough comparison with existing methods, particularly those that also use a single reference image but do not require test-time fine-tuning.
- The paper's claims about the method's ability to handle multiple subjects and perform smooth interpolation are not convincingly supported by the experimental results.
- The paper's methodology, particularly the use of a large-scale dataset and the training process, could be more clearly explained to enhance understanding and reproducibility.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors clarify how the training data is constructed from the LAION-5B dataset and how the Grounding DINO model is used for object detection?
- How does the proposed method compare to other methods that also use a single reference image but do not require test-time fine-tuning?
- In the ablation study, how does the method perform when the location control is removed, and what are the implications for the model's ability to handle multiple subjects?
- Can the authors provide more detailed examples or visualizations to support the claims of smooth interpolation between customized images and text descriptions?
- How does the method handle the potential for overfitting, especially given its dependence on a single reference image?
- Could the authors provide more detailed explanations or examples to clarify the method's handling of multiple subjects and the use of segmentation masks in the training process?
