 **Summary:**
The paper introduces CPPO, a novel method for continual learning in reinforcement learning from human feedback (RLHF). CPPO employs a weighting strategy to manage the trade-off between policy learning and knowledge retention, categorizing rollout samples into five types based on their rewards and generation probabilities. This strategy is aimed at enhancing the efficiency and stability of RLHF by reducing the need for complete retraining when new queries or feedback are introduced. The method was tested on the Reddit TL;DR dataset, showing improvements over various baselines, including PPO and other continual learning methods. However, the paper's experimental scope is limited, focusing on a single dataset, and the method's generalizability and applicability in more diverse settings remain unclear.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel approach to continual learning in RLHF, focusing on the trade-off between policy learning and knowledge retention, which is crucial for maintaining performance in dynamic environments.
- The methodology is clearly explained, with a well-structured presentation that aids in understanding the proposed methodologies and their implications.
- The experimental results demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed method, showing improvements over various baselines, including PPO and other continual learning methods.
- The paper is well-written, with a clear and logical flow of information, making it accessible and easy to follow.
- The paper addresses a significant problem in the field of continual learning, which is particularly relevant in the context of RLHF, where human feedback is crucial for model adaptation.

**Weaknesses:**
