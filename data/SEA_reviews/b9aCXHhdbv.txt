 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel pipeline parallelism framework, DRL-PP, which utilizes deep reinforcement learning (DRL) to optimize pipeline scheduling for training large deep neural network (DNN) models across multiple accelerators. DRL-PP consists of a graph encoder, a recurrent partitioner, and a pipeline scheduler, which learn to partition and place operations on various GPU devices, improving accelerator utilization and speeding up DNN training. The framework is evaluated on various benchmarks, demonstrating significant improvements over existing methods like data parallelism and PipeDream. However, concerns are raised about the novelty of the approach and the generalizability of the results due to limited hardware diversity and a lack of comprehensive comparisons with state-of-the-art methods.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel pipeline parallelism framework, DRL-PP, which optimizes the pipeline schedule for training large DNN models across multiple accelerators using deep reinforcement learning.
- The framework is evaluated on various benchmarks, demonstrating significant improvements over existing methods like data parallelism and PipeDream, with speedups ranging from 1.3x to 6.8x.
- The paper is well-written, making the methodology and results clear and understandable, which aids in reproducibility and application of the proposed methods.
- The use of deep reinforcement learning for pipeline parallelism is a novel approach that addresses the challenges of optimizing pipeline placement in deep learning workloads.
- The paper is significant as it addresses the challenges of optimizing pipeline placement in deep learning workloads, which is crucial for efficient and effective training of large models.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a comprehensive comparison with state-of-the-art methods, which could provide a clearer picture of the performance of the proposed framework against the best current techniques.
- The evaluation is limited to only one type of hardware (GPUs), which may not generalize well to other types of accelerators such as TPUs, FPGAs, or CPUs.
- The paper does not adequately address the limitations of the proposed framework, which could include the scalability of the approach, the impact of varying model sizes and architectures, and the generalizability of the results across different hardware platforms.
- The novelty of the approach is questionable as it heavily relies on existing frameworks and methods, and the improvements over existing methods are not substantial.
- The paper could benefit from a more detailed discussion on the challenges of optimizing pipeline placement in deep learning workloads and how the proposed framework addresses these challenges.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors provide a more detailed comparison with state-of-the-art methods to clarify the performance of the proposed framework against the best current techniques?
- How does the proposed framework perform on other types of accelerators, such as TPUs, FPGAs, or CPUs?
- Could the authors elaborate on the scalability of the approach and how it performs with larger models or different architectures?
- How does the proposed framework handle the generalizability of the results across different hardware platforms?
- Can the authors provide more detailed results and analysis, including the impact of varying model sizes and architectures on the performance of the framework?
