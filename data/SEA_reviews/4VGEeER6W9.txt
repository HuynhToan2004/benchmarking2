 **Summary:**
The paper explores non-asymptotic convergence guarantees for diffusion-based generative models, specifically focusing on deterministic and stochastic samplers. It establishes convergence rates for both types of samplers, with the deterministic sampler achieving a $1/\epsilon$ rate and the stochastic sampler a $1/\sqrt{\epsilon}$ rate. The paper also addresses the impact of score estimation errors on the quality of the data generation process, using minimal assumptions on the target data distribution. The analysis is based on an elementary non-asymptotic approach, which does not require knowledge of SDEs or ODEs, making it accessible and potentially applicable to a broader range of diffusion models.

**Strengths:**
- The paper provides non-asymptotic convergence guarantees for diffusion-based generative models, which is a significant contribution to the field.
- It establishes a convergence rate for the deterministic sampler that does not depend on the smoothness assumption, which is a novel approach.
- The analysis is based on an elementary non-asymptotic approach, which makes it accessible and potentially applicable to a broader range of diffusion models.
- The paper is well-written and easy to follow, with a clear presentation of the results and a detailed comparison with existing literature.
- The paper addresses the important question of how score estimation errors affect the quality of the data generation process, which is crucial for understanding the performance of diffusion models.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper does not provide a clear discussion on the technical difficulties or the main ideas behind the proofs, which could enhance understanding and reproducibility.
- The convergence rate for the deterministic sampler depends on the dimension $d$, which is sub-optimal.
- The paper lacks a discussion on the limitations of the approach and the assumptions made, which could help in understanding the scope and applicability of the results.
- The novelty of the approach is questioned, as similar results have been previously obtained by other researchers.
- The paper does not discuss the implications of the results for practical applications, such as training diffusion models, which could be a significant limitation for the broader impact of the work.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors provide a more detailed discussion on the technical difficulties and the main ideas behind the proofs?
- How does the proposed approach compare to other existing results, particularly in terms of the convergence rates and the assumptions made?
- What are the implications of the results for practical applications, such as training diffusion models?
- Could the authors clarify the assumptions made in the analysis and discuss their implications for the applicability of the results?
- How does the paper address the issue of the sub-optimal dependence on the dimension $d$ in the convergence rate for the deterministic sampler?
