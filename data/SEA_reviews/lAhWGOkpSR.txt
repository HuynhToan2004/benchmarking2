 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel approach to multi-scale representation learning for semantic segmentation, focusing on the issues of scale inadequacy and field inactivation in existing methods. It proposes a Varying Window Attention (VWA) mechanism, which allows for varying context window sizes and integrates it into a Multi-Scale Decoder (MSD) called VWFormer. This approach aims to address the inadequacies of existing methods by learning multi-scale representations efficiently and effectively. The paper provides extensive experiments and visualizations to demonstrate the effectiveness of VWA and VWFormer, showing improvements over existing methods in terms of performance and efficiency.

**Strengths:**
- The paper addresses the critical issue of multi-scale representation learning in semantic segmentation, which is crucial for improving segmentation performance.
- The proposed VWA and VWFormer are innovative and effective, showing significant improvements in efficiency and performance over existing methods.
- The paper is well-written, easy to follow, and provides a comprehensive analysis of the issues in current methods, along with a detailed comparison with previous works.
- The experiments are extensive, including a wide range of backbones and datasets, demonstrating the effectiveness of the proposed methods.
- The visualizations and ablation studies provided are convincing, supporting the claims made by the authors.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a thorough discussion on the limitations of the proposed methods, particularly in terms of their applicability to other tasks or datasets.
- The presentation of the paper could be improved, with figures and tables sometimes being too small or hard to read.
- The novelty of the VWA mechanism is questionable, as it appears similar to existing methods, and the improvements over these methods are not clearly articulated.
- The paper does not adequately address the scalability of the proposed methods, particularly in terms of the computational cost and memory requirements, which could limit their practical application.
- There is a lack of detailed analysis on the effectiveness of the VWA mechanism and the impact of different settings on the performance.
- The paper could benefit from a more comprehensive discussion on the related work, particularly on the differences and similarities with existing methods.

**Questions:**
- Could you clarify the specific differences between VWA and the mechanisms used in other methods like Yuan et al. (2018) and Zhu et al. (2019)?
- How does the proposed method perform on other datasets, such as the PascalVOC dataset?
- Can you provide more details on the computational cost and memory requirements of VWA and VWFormer, particularly in comparison to other methods?
- How does the proposed method handle the issue of inactivated areas in the receptive field, and what are the implications of this for the segmentation results?
- Could you elaborate on the scalability of the proposed methods and discuss potential strategies for reducing computational costs and memory requirements?
- How does the proposed method compare in terms of efficiency and performance with other methods that use similar techniques, such as those mentioned in the related work section?
