 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel application of the Burer-Monteiro (BM) factorization to convex neural networks, focusing on the training of two-layer neural networks with ReLU activation. The authors demonstrate that this factorization can lead to a computationally tractable non-convex alternative, which may have no spurious local minima. They also provide theoretical results and experiments to support their claims, including the application of BM factorization to multi-layer perceptrons (MLPs), convolutional neural networks (CNNs), and self-attention networks. The paper aims to bridge the gap between convex neural networks and non-convex training by offering a new perspective on the training of neural networks, though it faces criticism for its limited novelty and the practical applicability of the proposed methods.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, clear, and structured, with a clear presentation of the Burer-Monteiro (BM) factorization and its application to convex neural networks.
- Theoretical results are provided, which are supported by experiments, enhancing the credibility of the proposed method.
- The paper introduces a novel application of BM factorization to convex neural networks, which is a significant contribution to the field.
- The experimental results demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed method, showing that it can match the performance of non-convex networks in certain scenarios.
- The paper provides a new perspective on the training of neural networks by connecting it to convex optimization problems, which could inspire further research in this area.

**Weaknesses:**
- The novelty of the paper is questioned as the application of BM factorization to convex neural networks is seen as a natural extension of existing work.
- The paper lacks a detailed discussion on the limitations and challenges of the proposed method, particularly in terms of its practical applicability and scalability.
- There is a lack of comprehensive experimental validation, with only a limited set of experiments conducted, which may not fully demonstrate the effectiveness or generalizability of the proposed method.
- The paper could benefit from a more thorough literature review and a clearer differentiation from existing works, particularly those that have also explored the use of BM factorization in neural networks.
- The paper does not sufficiently address the computational complexity and memory requirements of the proposed method, which are critical for practical implementation.

**Questions:**

- How does the proposed method compare in terms of computational complexity and memory requirements with other training methods for neural networks?
- Can the authors provide more examples or case studies to demonstrate the practical applicability and effectiveness of the proposed method in real-world scenarios?
- What are the specific challenges or limitations of the proposed method that need to be addressed for practical implementation?
