 **Summary:**
The paper investigates the phase transition phenomenon in the shuffled regression problem, focusing on the recovery of permutation matrices and coefficient matrices from shuffled data. It proposes a message-passing algorithm to identify phase transition thresholds and presents a Gaussian approximation method to derive closed-form formulas for these thresholds. Theoretical predictions are validated through numerical experiments, demonstrating the method's effectiveness in predicting phase transition points. The paper also discusses the application of this method in scenarios where the coefficient matrix is known and unknown, providing insights into the behavior of the phase transition under different conditions.

**Strengths:**
- The paper addresses an interesting and relevant problem in machine learning, focusing on the shuffled regression problem, which is an important application in data analysis and privacy.
- The theoretical analysis is well-structured and provides a clear understanding of the phase transition phenomenon, with the derivation of the message-passing algorithm being a significant contribution.
- The paper is well-written, with clear presentation of the problem and the proposed method, making it accessible to readers.
- The theoretical results are supported by numerical experiments, which demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed method in predicting phase transition points.
- The paper extends the analysis to scenarios where the coefficient matrix is unknown, providing a more comprehensive understanding of the phase transition behavior.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed discussion on the limitations of the proposed method, particularly in scenarios where the coefficient matrix is unknown.
- The clarity of the paper could be improved, especially in sections where the content is dense and the notation is complex, making it difficult for readers to follow.
- The paper does not adequately compare its results with existing literature, which could help in understanding the novelty and effectiveness of the proposed method.
- The paper does not provide sufficient intuition for the results, which could be enhanced by including examples or simplified explanations to aid in understanding the theoretical contributions.
