 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel infinite-dimensional diffusion model, termed ∞-Diff, which utilizes a mollified diffusion process and a multi-scale function-space architecture to generate high-resolution images from sparsely sampled coordinates. This approach allows for training on low-resolution images and sampling at higher resolutions, enhancing the model's ability to handle arbitrary resolution sampling. The methodology involves a U-Net-inspired multi-scale architecture and a mollified diffusion process to smooth irregularities, which is evaluated against existing finite-dimensional and infinite-dimensional models. Despite its innovative approach, the paper is critiqued for its lack of clarity in presentation and insufficient comparison with relevant works.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel approach to modeling infinite resolution data using a diffusion model, which is a significant advancement in the field.
- The methodology is clearly explained, and the experiments are well-designed, showing the effectiveness of the proposed method.
- The paper provides a comprehensive discussion of related works, including recent advancements in the field, and includes a detailed mathematical formulation of the proposed method.
- The proposed method achieves state-of-the-art FID scores on multiple high-resolution image datasets, demonstrating its effectiveness.
- The paper is well-written and easy to follow, with a clear motivation for the proposed method.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a clear explanation of the mollification process and its necessity, which could confuse readers unfamiliar with the concept.
- There is a lack of detailed discussion on the computational complexity of the method, which is crucial for understanding its practical implications.
- The paper could benefit from a more thorough discussion on the limitations and potential societal impacts of the proposed method.
- The paper does not sufficiently compare its method with existing works, particularly those that also use neural operators and mollified diffusion processes.
- The presentation of the paper is not clear, with some sections requiring additional clarity and explanation to aid understanding.
- The paper lacks a discussion on the limitations of the proposed method, which is crucial for understanding its applicability and potential drawbacks.
