 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel framework, Long-range Meta-path Search through Progressive Sampling (LMSPS), designed to discover effective meta-paths in large-scale heterogeneous information networks (HINs). It utilizes a progressive sampling algorithm to dynamically shrink the search space and select top-k meta-paths based on their relevance to the current HIN and task. The framework's performance is demonstrated through extensive experiments on eight datasets, showing that LMSPS can effectively leverage long-range dependencies and outperform state-of-the-art models. The paper is well-written, clear, and provides a comprehensive evaluation, although it has been noted for its incremental novelty and the limited scope of its experiments.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-organized, well-written, and easy to follow, with clear and intuitive figures that aid in understanding the proposed method.
- The proposed method, LMSPS, is innovative and shows good performance, particularly in large-scale heterogeneous information networks (HINs), demonstrating its effectiveness through extensive experiments.
- The methodology is simple yet effective, utilizing a progressive sampling algorithm to dynamically shrink the search space, which is a novel approach to meta-path search.
- The paper provides a thorough evaluation, including ablation studies, and the results are convincing, showing the superiority of LMSPS over state-of-the-art models.
- The paper is significant as it is the first to utilize long-range dependency in large-scale heterogeneous information networks, which is a crucial aspect for many real-world applications.

**Weaknesses:**
- The novelty of the paper is somewhat limited, as it builds upon existing works such as SeHGNN and introduces incremental improvements.
- The paper could benefit from a more comprehensive discussion on related works, particularly in the context of meta-structure search on HINs.
- The experimental scope is limited to only eight datasets, which may not adequately demonstrate the generalizability of the proposed method across diverse datasets.
- The paper does not sufficiently discuss the limitations of the proposed method, which could provide a more balanced view of its capabilities and potential challenges.
- The paper could be improved by providing a more detailed explanation of the progressive sampling algorithm and its impact on the search space reduction.
- The paper lacks a broader comparison with other meta-path-based HGNNs and does not include experiments on larger datasets, which could demonstrate the scalability of the proposed method.

**Questions:**
- Could you provide a more detailed explanation of the progressive sampling algorithm and its impact on the search space reduction?
- How does the proposed method compare with other meta-path-based HGNNs, especially in terms of performance and efficiency?
- Could you include more datasets in the experiments to demonstrate the generalizability of the proposed method?
- How does the proposed method perform on larger datasets, such as those in the OGB challenge?
- Could you discuss the limitations of the proposed method and how they might affect its practical application?
- How does the method handle the issue of noisy or redundant information in long-range dependencies, and what strategies are used to mitigate these issues?
- Could you provide a more detailed comparison with other methods, such as those mentioned in the related works section, to highlight the unique contributions of your proposed method?
