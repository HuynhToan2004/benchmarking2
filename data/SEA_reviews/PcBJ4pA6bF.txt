 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel approach, Decentralized Federated Learning with Synthetic Anchors (DeSA), aimed at addressing data and model heterogeneity in decentralized federated learning (FL) settings. DeSA utilizes synthetic anchor data to align client models and enhance generalization capabilities across different client datasets. The methodology includes two regularization terms: an anchor loss that matches client embeddings with anchor data and a knowledge distillation loss that facilitates learning from other clients. Theoretical analysis supports the proposed approach, and extensive experiments demonstrate DeSA's effectiveness in enhancing both inter- and intra-client performance. However, concerns about the clarity of the theoretical analysis and the practical implementation of the synthetic anchor data generation process are highlighted.

**Strengths:**
- The paper addresses an important and challenging problem of decentralized federated learning (FL) with both data and model heterogeneity, proposing a novel method called DeSA to address these challenges.
- The DeSA methodology is well-articulated, including the use of synthetic anchor data, which is a novel approach in this context.
- The paper is well-written, making it easy to follow, and includes a theoretical analysis that supports the proposed method.
- The experiments are extensive and demonstrate the effectiveness of the DeSA method in enhancing both inter- and intra-client performance.
- The paper provides a clear description of the methodology and includes detailed experimental results, which are beneficial for understanding the proposed method's effectiveness.

**Weaknesses:**
- The theoretical analysis in Section 4 is not clear and lacks detailed explanation, particularly regarding the definitions and roles of various constants and parameters used.
- The practical implementation of the synthetic anchor data generation process is not clearly described, which could lead to confusion about the actual generation process.
- The paper lacks a comprehensive comparison with existing methods, particularly in terms of the experimental settings and the types of heterogeneity considered.
- There is a lack of discussion on the potential negative societal impacts of the proposed method, which could be a significant concern in real-world applications.
- The paper does not adequately address the privacy concerns associated with sharing synthetic anchor data, which could be a critical issue in federated learning scenarios.

**Questions:**
- Could you provide more detailed explanations on the theoretical analysis, particularly regarding the definitions and roles of constants and parameters used?
- How is the synthetic anchor data generated in practice, and what are the specific steps involved in this process?
- Can you clarify the experimental settings, especially how data heterogeneity and model heterogeneity are controlled in different scenarios?
- How does the DeSA method compare to existing methods, particularly in terms of computational efficiency and data privacy concerns?
- Given the potential privacy concerns associated with sharing synthetic anchor data, how do you address these issues in your proposed method?
