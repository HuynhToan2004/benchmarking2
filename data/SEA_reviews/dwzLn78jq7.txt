 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel approach to estimating the Lipschitz constant of neural networks using semidefinite programs (SDPs) transformed into an eigenvalue problem. This transformation allows for more efficient handling of large neural networks, potentially improving computational efficiency and reducing memory requirements. The method, termed LipDiff, is evaluated against standard SDP methods and shows significant improvements in scalability and efficiency. The paper also discusses the theoretical foundations and practical implications of this method, including its potential to enhance the understanding and application of neural network Lipschitz constants.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, clear, and easy to follow, with a clear presentation of the proposed method and its theoretical underpinnings.
- The proposed method is innovative, transforming SDPs into an eigenvalue problem, which is a novel approach in the field of neural network Lipschitz constant estimation.
- The paper provides a thorough discussion of the method's theoretical foundations, including proofs and discussions that support the validity and effectiveness of the proposed method.
- The experiments conducted demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed method, showing improvements over existing methods in terms of scalability and computational efficiency.
- The paper is well-structured, with a clear introduction of the problem, a detailed explanation of the proposed method, and a comprehensive evaluation of the method's performance.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed comparison with other existing methods, such as those mentioned in the literature, which could provide a more comprehensive understanding of the proposed method's advantages and disadvantages.
- The scalability of the method is not convincingly demonstrated, with the experiments conducted on relatively small neural networks, which limits the generalizability of the results.
- The paper could benefit from a more detailed discussion on the limitations of the proposed method, including potential issues with the approximation of the largest eigenvalue and the scalability of the method to larger, more complex neural networks.
- The paper does not sufficiently address the practical implications of the Lipschitz constant estimation, such as its impact on network training or generalization, which could be crucial for understanding the real-world applicability of the proposed method.
- There are some minor typographical errors and inconsistencies in the paper, which could detract from its professional presentation.
