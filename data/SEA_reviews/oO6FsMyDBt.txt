 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel approach to representing neural networks as computational graphs, which is then processed by graph neural networks and transformers to address the permutation symmetry inherent in neural networks. This approach allows for the processing of heterogeneous architectures and the handling of varying network structures. The methodology is demonstrated across various tasks including INR classification, predicting CNN generalization, and learning to optimize, showing consistent improvements over existing methods. Despite its innovative approach, the paper has been critiqued for its limited scope in terms of the types of neural networks and tasks it addresses, and the lack of a broader evaluation across different architectures and datasets.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, with clear and detailed explanations that are easy to follow.
- The concept of representing neural networks as computational graphs is novel and provides a unique perspective on neural network processing.
- The proposed method is versatile and can handle a wide range of tasks, including INR classification, predicting CNN generalization, and learning to optimize, with consistent improvements over existing methods.
- The method is applicable to heterogeneous architectures, which is a significant advantage in the field of neural networks.
- The paper includes comprehensive evaluations that demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed method across various tasks.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a broader evaluation across different types of neural networks, such as RNNs and transformers, which could strengthen the results.
- The paper's focus on MLPs and CNNs limits the applicability of the proposed method to a narrower range of neural network architectures.
- The experiments conducted are primarily on small-scale datasets, which might not adequately represent the method's performance on larger, more complex datasets.
- The paper could benefit from a more detailed discussion on the limitations of the proposed method and potential directions for future research.
- There is a lack of clarity in some sections of the paper, particularly in the definitions and explanations of certain terms and concepts, which could hinder understanding.
- The paper could be improved by including more detailed visualizations and explanations of the neural graph representation, especially for CNNs.

**Questions:**
- How does the method perform on larger datasets, such as those used in the ImageNet-1k dataset?
- Could the method be adapted to handle more complex neural network architectures, such as transformers?
- How does the method handle non-linear layers, such as ReLU and sigmoid functions, in the neural graph representation?
- Can the method be applied to other types of neural networks, such as recurrent neural networks (RNNs)?
- How does the method compare to other recent works in the field, such as those by Ashkenazi et al. and Erkoc et al.?
- Could the authors provide more detailed visualizations and explanations of the neural graph representation, especially for CNNs, to enhance the clarity and understanding of the proposed method?
