 **Summary:**
The paper explores the challenges of quantizing weights in Large Language Models (LLMs) to reduce memory consumption, focusing on the per-channel quantization method that distinguishes between input and output channels (IC vs. OC). The proposed method, AdaDim, aims to adaptively select the most effective quantization method for each layer based on sensitivity patterns. The authors evaluate this approach using a variety of models, including LLaMA and Wizard, and demonstrate improvements over existing methods like GPTQ and RTN. However, the paper lacks a thorough comparison with state-of-the-art methods and does not provide sufficient hardware efficiency metrics, which limits its applicability in real-world scenarios.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, with clear explanations of the problem, proposed solution, and evaluation, making it easy to understand.
- The proposed method of per-channel quantization and its application to LLMs is innovative and could be beneficial for future research in the field.
- The paper demonstrates significant improvements over previous methods like GPTQ and RTN, showing that the proposed method can enhance the performance of LLMs.
- The experimental results are robust, showing that the proposed method can effectively reduce reconstruction errors and improve performance in various tasks.
- The paper provides a novel approach to addressing the challenge of activation outliers in LLMs, which is a significant issue in the field.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a thorough comparison with state-of-the-art methods, particularly in terms of hardware efficiency and accuracy.
- The paper does not provide sufficient details on the hardware efficiency of the proposed method, which is crucial for real-world applications.
- There is a noticeable gap in the experimental results between the proposed method and other methods, which could affect the credibility of the findings.
- The paper does not adequately address the limitations of the proposed method, which could hinder its practical implementation.
- The paper does not sufficiently discuss the novelty of the proposed method compared to existing methods like GPTQ and RTN.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors provide more details on the hardware efficiency of the proposed method, particularly in terms of memory consumption and computational overhead?
- How does the proposed method compare to other state-of-the-art methods in terms of accuracy and efficiency?
- Could the authors clarify the novelty of the proposed method compared to existing methods, particularly in terms of the adaptive dimension selection and the per-channel quantization approach?
- What are the specific limitations of the proposed method, and how do these affect its practical implementation?
- Can the authors provide more details on the experimental setup, particularly the calibration data used and the training and inference processes?
