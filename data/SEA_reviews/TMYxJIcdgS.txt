 **Summary:**
The paper investigates the creation of a dataset, LAIONet, from the LAION dataset using text-based queries, aiming to understand the differences between this dataset and ImageNet. The authors demonstrate that LAIONet, derived from LAION, exhibits lower intra-class similarity compared to ImageNet, which affects the performance of models trained on ImageNet when evaluated on LAIONet. The study also explores the role of text-based queries in dataset creation, suggesting that they can influence the diversity and quality of the resulting dataset. The paper provides a comprehensive analysis of the dataset creation process, highlighting the impact of text-based filtering on dataset characteristics and model performance.

**Strengths:**
- The paper presents a well-motivated study on the differences between ImageNet and LAION datasets, focusing on the impact of dataset creation methods on model performance.
- The analysis is comprehensive and well-supported by evidence, including the use of multiple datasets and models to validate the findings.
- The paper is well-written, making it easy to follow and understand, with clear visualizations and explanations of the experimental results.
- The study provides insights into the role of dataset creation methods in shaping the performance of models trained on these datasets, which is crucial for understanding the generalizability of models across different datasets.
- The paper introduces the concept of LAIONet, a new dataset created from LAION using text-based queries, which can be used as a benchmark for evaluating the performance of models trained on different datasets.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper could benefit from a more detailed discussion on the practical implications of the findings, particularly how the insights can be applied to improve dataset creation processes.
- The experimental setup and the selection of datasets could be more comprehensive to better support the conclusions drawn. For instance, including more models and datasets in the analysis could strengthen the results.
- The paper could provide more details on the methodology used for creating LAIONet, especially how the text-based queries were designed and the impact of different query strategies on the dataset's characteristics.
- The paper's focus on the ImageNet dataset might limit its applicability to other datasets, as the findings might not generalize well to other types of datasets.
- The paper could benefit from a more thorough discussion on the limitations of the study and the potential biases introduced by the use of text-based queries in dataset creation.

**Questions:**
- How does the methodology used for creating LAIONet compare to other methods of dataset creation, and what are the advantages or disadvantages of using text-based queries compared to other methods?
- Can the findings of this study be applied to other datasets beyond ImageNet and LAION, and if so, how might the results differ?
- How does the selection of text-based queries impact the characteristics of the resulting dataset, and what steps were taken to mitigate potential biases in the query selection process?
- In the creation of LAIONet, what strategies were used to ensure that the dataset is not overly influenced by the specific text-based queries used?
- How might the findings of this study be used to improve dataset creation processes, particularly in terms of balancing diversity and quality in the resulting datasets?
