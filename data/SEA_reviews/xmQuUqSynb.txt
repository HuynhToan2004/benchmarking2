 **Summary:**
The paper investigates the vulnerability of machine unlearning models to adversarial attacks, proposing a novel adversarial unlearning attack (AdvUA) that strategically selects unlearning samples to compromise the robustness of models. The attack exploits the unlearning process to create a sparse local density environment around test samples, making them more susceptible to adversarial manipulation. The authors demonstrate that AdvUA can enhance the success rates of adversarial attacks and improve the transferability of adversarial examples. The paper also discusses the impact of unlearning on adversarial robustness and presents a theoretical framework for analyzing the vulnerability of unlearned models. Despite its novelty and the thoroughness of the experimental results, the paper has been critiqued for its lack of clarity in some sections, particularly in explaining the threat model and the specifics of the attack.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, making it easy to follow, and the authors provide a comprehensive analysis of the vulnerability of unlearned models to adversarial attacks.
- The proposed attack, AdvUA, is novel and effectively exploits the unlearning process to create a sparse local density environment around test samples, enhancing the success rates of adversarial attacks.
- The paper includes a thorough experimental evaluation, demonstrating the effectiveness of AdvUA across various datasets and model architectures, including both exact and approximate unlearning methods.
- The authors provide a theoretical framework for analyzing the vulnerability of unlearned models, which is a significant contribution to the field of machine unlearning.
- The paper is well-motivated, with clear definitions and theoretical foundations that support the proposed attack and its implications for adversarial robustness in machine learning models.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a clear threat model, particularly concerning the attacker's knowledge and capabilities. It is unclear whether the attacker is assumed to have full knowledge of the training data, which could significantly affect the feasibility and impact of the proposed attack.
- The paper does not adequately discuss the limitations of the proposed attack, which could include scenarios where the attack might not be feasible or effective.
- The experimental setup and results are not sufficiently detailed, particularly in terms of the specific unlearning methods used and the performance metrics reported.
- The paper could benefit from a more detailed discussion on the practical implications of the proposed attack, including potential countermeasures and the societal impact of the findings.
- The paper contains some grammatical errors and could benefit from a more rigorous proofreading and editing process.

**Questions:**
- Could you clarify the threat model and the specific knowledge and capabilities assumed for the attacker? How does this affect the feasibility and impact of the proposed attack?
- How does the proposed attack perform against different types of adversarial attacks, such as those based on different perturbation budgets or using different attack algorithms?
- In the context of the proposed attack, how does the number of unlearning samples impact the attack's effectiveness? Is there a threshold beyond which additional samples do not significantly enhance the attack's success rate?
- Could you provide more details on the experimental setup, including the specific unlearning methods used and the performance metrics reported? How do these results compare to other state-of-the-art methods?
- How does the proposed attack impact the robustness of unlearned models against different types of adversarial attacks, such as those based on different perturbation budgets or using different attack algorithms?
