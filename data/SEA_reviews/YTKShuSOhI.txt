 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel method for inferring parameters of a Hidden Markov Model (HMM) on binary trees, using a 'path-of-bifurcations' approach. The method is tested on both synthetic and real-world datasets, including medical images, to evaluate its performance and robustness. The approach involves modeling branching angles and termination constraints, which are optimized using variational inference. The method's effectiveness is demonstrated through experiments on synthetic data, showing its ability to recover HMM parameters accurately. However, concerns were raised about the generalizability of the method to real-world datasets and its practical applicability, especially in terms of its complexity and computational demands.

**Strengths:**
- The paper addresses a significant and interesting problem in modeling binary trees, which is a common structure in various fields such as biology and computer science.
- The proposed method is novel, combining HMMs with spatial constraints, which is a novel approach in this context.
- The paper is well-written, clear, and provides detailed experimental results, making it easy to follow and understand.
- The methodology is supported by a comprehensive set of experiments, including synthetic and real-world datasets, which demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed method.
- The paper includes a detailed description of the methodology, including the use of probabilistic procedural modeling and variational inference, which are well-explained.
- The application of the method to real-world datasets, such as medical images, demonstrates its practical utility and relevance to specific domains.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a clear justification for the choice of the 'path-of-bifurcations' approach over other methods, such as the Viterbi algorithm, which could be more straightforward and efficient.
- The method's complexity and computational demands are not adequately addressed, raising concerns about its practical applicability, especially in real-world scenarios with large or complex datasets.
- The paper does not sufficiently compare the proposed method with existing approaches, which could help establish its novelty and effectiveness.
- The method's reliance on prior knowledge and assumptions about the data, such as the Gaussian emission distribution and the assumption of a Hidden Markov Model, may limit its generalizability and applicability to other types of data or models.
- The paper does not discuss the limitations of the proposed method, which could provide a more balanced view of its strengths and weaknesses.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors clarify the rationale behind choosing the 'path-of-bifurcations' approach over simpler methods like the Viterbi algorithm?
- How does the proposed method handle the complexity and computational demands, especially in real-world scenarios with large or complex datasets?
- Could the authors provide more details on the comparison with existing approaches and discuss how the proposed method differs from or improves upon these?
- What are the specific advantages of using HMMs over other types of models for binary tree modeling, and how do these advantages translate into practical benefits in real-world applications?
