 **Summary:**
The paper explores the ambulance dispatching problem using a reinforcement learning (RL) approach, specifically the Proximal Policy Optimization (PPO) algorithm. The authors aim to minimize response times while considering incident priority and resource utilization, integrating an EMS simulation model with Open Street Map travel time estimation and synthetic incident data generation. The experiments were conducted using both synthetic and real incident data from Oslo University Hospital, demonstrating the PPO model's superiority over heuristic policies. However, the paper is criticized for its lack of clarity in the problem definition, insufficient theoretical justification, and limited scope of experiments.

**Strengths:**
- The paper addresses a significant and relevant problem in emergency services, focusing on the ambulance dispatching problem, which is crucial for improving emergency response times.
- The integration of real-world data, such as incident and population data, enhances the practical applicability of the proposed method.
- The use of the PPO algorithm, a modern RL method, is highlighted as a strength, showcasing the paper's up-to-date methodological approach.
- The paper is well-written and easy to follow, making it accessible to a broad audience.
- The authors have provided extensive data analysis and a reproducible OSM travel time estimation, which are valuable contributions to the field.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks clarity in the problem definition and the specifics of the ambulance dispatching problem, which could confuse readers unfamiliar with the domain.
- There is a significant gap in the literature review, with many relevant works not cited, which limits the paper's contribution to the existing body of knowledge.
- The experiments are limited in scope, focusing only on a small subset of the city and using a simplistic simulation environment that does not adequately reflect real-world conditions.
- The paper does not sufficiently address the scalability and generalizability of the proposed method, which is crucial for practical implementation in larger, more complex scenarios.
- The paper lacks a thorough theoretical justification for the choice of the PPO algorithm and does not compare the proposed method with other RL-based ambulance dispatching methods, which could undermine the claim of its superiority.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors clarify the specifics of the ambulance dispatching problem, particularly the decision-making process and the constraints involved?
- How does the proposed method handle scenarios where multiple incidents occur at the same location, and how does it prioritize dispatching in such cases?
- Could the authors provide more details on the training process, including the duration and the specifics of the training environment?
- How does the PPO algorithm perform in more complex scenarios, such as those involving multiple ambulances and larger incident queues?
- Can the authors elaborate on the choice of the PPO algorithm and discuss how it compares to other RL-based methods in terms of effectiveness and efficiency?
- How does the proposed method ensure scalability and generalizability to larger, more complex scenarios, such as those involving multiple ambulance stations and a larger city area?
