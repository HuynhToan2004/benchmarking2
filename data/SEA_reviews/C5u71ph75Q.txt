 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel method for modeling protein structures using a variational autoencoder (VAE) that operates on internal coordinates, such as dihedrals and bond angles, to generate a full covariance matrix for the model. This approach is aimed at addressing the complex covariance structures in protein structures, which are typically challenging to model directly. The method is evaluated using both unimodal and multimodal settings, demonstrating its ability to generate physically plausible structures. The paper also discusses the application of this method in protein structure prediction and density modeling, highlighting its potential to scale to full protein backbones. Despite its innovative approach, the paper is critiqued for its limited experimental validation and the need for more comprehensive comparisons with existing methods.

**Strengths:**
- The paper addresses a significant and challenging problem in protein structure prediction by focusing on modeling the complex covariance structures in protein structures.
- The method is innovative, using a VAE to model fluctuations in internal coordinates, which is a novel approach in the field.
- The paper is well-written, clear, and easy to follow, with a clear explanation of the methodology and its implementation.
- The experimental results are convincing, demonstrating the method's ability to generate physically plausible structures and its potential to scale to full protein backbones.
- The paper provides a good comparison with existing methods, highlighting the advantages of the proposed method over traditional approaches.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a comprehensive comparison with existing methods, particularly in terms of the computational efficiency and effectiveness of the proposed method.
- The experimental validation is limited, with only a few test proteins used, which may not be sufficient to fully demonstrate the method's capabilities.
- The paper does not discuss the limitations of the method in detail, which could provide valuable insights into its practical applications and potential drawbacks.
- The paper could benefit from more detailed explanations and justifications for certain methodological choices, such as the use of a VAE for modeling fluctuations in internal coordinates.
- The paper's scope is somewhat narrow, focusing primarily on modeling distributions of protein structure states in internal coordinates, which may limit its applicability to other areas of protein structure prediction.

**Questions:**
- Can the method be adapted to model dynamics of proteins, and if so, how might it be extended to do so?
- How does the method compare to other existing methods in terms of computational efficiency and effectiveness?
- Can the method be generalized to other types of molecular structures beyond proteins?
- How does the method handle the complexity of modeling large conformational changes in proteins, especially in high data regime settings?
- Could the authors provide more details on the experimental setup, particularly the choice of test proteins and the experimental conditions used?
- How does the method ensure that the generated structures remain physically plausible, especially in terms of maintaining bond lengths and angles?
- Could the authors discuss the limitations of the method in more detail, including potential practical applications and areas where the method may not be effective?
