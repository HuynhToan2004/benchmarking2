 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel SVD-based method for class unlearning, which aims to remove specific classes from a trained model without the need for retraining. This method involves the decomposition of feature spaces into retain and forget spaces, which are then used to project the model weights onto the orthogonal space of the retain space. The paper demonstrates this method's effectiveness through experiments on various datasets, including CIFAR10 and CIFAR100, and shows that it can achieve comparable or superior results to other unlearning methods. However, concerns were raised regarding the clarity of the writing, the scalability of the method to larger datasets, and the potential impact of the method on model performance.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel method for class unlearning that does not require retraining, which is a significant advancement over existing methods.
- The method is based on SVD decomposition, which is both theoretically sound and computationally efficient.
- The experiments conducted demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed method, showing that it can achieve comparable or superior results to other unlearning methods.
- The paper is well-written and easy to follow, making it accessible to readers.
- The method is applicable to both convolutional and transformer architectures, which increases its utility and applicability.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper suffers from unclear writing, particularly in the methodology section, which makes it difficult to understand the main contributions and the intuition behind the proposed method.
- The scalability of the method to larger datasets, such as ImageNet, is not convincingly demonstrated, raising concerns about its practical applicability.
- The paper lacks a thorough comparison with state-of-the-art methods, particularly in terms of computational efficiency and performance on larger datasets.
- There is a lack of detailed discussion on the limitations of the method, including potential negative societal impacts and practical challenges in implementing the method in real-world scenarios.
- The paper does not sufficiently address the potential negative societal impacts of unlearning, which is a significant concern given the potential misuse of such technology.

**Questions:**
- Could you clarify the role and impact of the scaling coefficient α in the method, particularly how it influences the importance of different basis vectors?
- How does the method perform when unlearning multiple classes at once, and what are the implications for model performance?
- Can you provide more details on the computational efficiency of the method, especially in comparison to other unlearning methods?
- How does the method handle scenarios where the forget and retain classes are not distinct, such as in the case of overlapping classes in the dataset?
- Could you elaborate on the practical challenges of implementing the method in real-world scenarios, particularly in terms of data access and model deployment?
- How does the method perform when applied to larger datasets, such as ImageNet, and what are the implications for model performance and computational efficiency?
