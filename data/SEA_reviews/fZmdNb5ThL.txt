 **Summary:**
The paper introduces ShiftAddAug, a method designed to enhance the performance of multiplication-free tiny neural networks by augmenting them with multiplicative operators during training. This approach is aimed at improving the accuracy of multiplication-free networks, which are typically less powerful than their multiplicative counterparts, without increasing inference overhead. The method involves a hybrid weight sharing strategy to address the inconsistency in weight distribution between multiplication-free and multiplicative operators. The paper also employs a neural architecture search strategy to optimize the network architecture for hardware efficiency. The method is validated through experiments on various datasets, including CIFAR10/100, ImageNet, and several other datasets, demonstrating improvements in accuracy and energy efficiency.

**Strengths:**
- The paper addresses an important problem in the field of tiny neural networks, focusing on improving the accuracy of multiplication-free networks without increasing inference overhead.
- The proposed method, ShiftAddAug, is well-motivated and provides a clear and intuitive approach to solving the problem of weight inconsistency between multiplication-free and multiplicative operators.
- The methodology is supported by extensive experiments and ablation studies, demonstrating the effectiveness of the proposed method in improving the performance of multiplication-free networks.
- The paper is well-written, making it accessible and easy to follow, with clear explanations and a logical flow of content.
- The experimental results show that ShiftAddAug can achieve significant improvements in accuracy and energy efficiency compared to multiplication-free networks.

**Weaknesses:**

- The methodology section could benefit from more detailed explanations and intuitive insights, particularly around the use of the Laplace distribution and the training process.
- The paper does not sufficiently differentiate its contributions from existing works such as ShiftAddNAS, and the novelty of the proposed method is not clearly articulated.

- The paper could benefit from a more thorough discussion on the limitations and potential negative societal impacts of the proposed method.
