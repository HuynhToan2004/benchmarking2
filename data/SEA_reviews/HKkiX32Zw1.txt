 **Summary:**
The paper introduces PromptBreeder, a novel method for self-referential self-improvement of prompting strategies in large language models (LLMs). This approach uses LLMs to generate mutation prompts, which are then used to evolve task prompts through a genetic algorithm. The method is evaluated on a variety of datasets, showing promising results in improving LLM performance. However, concerns are raised about the novelty and the clarity of the method's presentation, particularly the lack of detailed explanation on how the genetic algorithm is applied and the absence of a comprehensive comparison with existing methods.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, clear, and easy to follow, with a strong empirical foundation.
- The idea of using LLMs to generate mutation prompts for task prompts is innovative and potentially impactful.
- The proposed method is simple, effective, and has been tested on various datasets, showing promising results.
- The paper includes a diverse set of mutation operators, which helps in exploring a large space of cognitive methods and linguistic self-questioning.
- The methodology is simple, with a straightforward design that makes it easy to understand and implement.
- The paper presents a good set of baselines and provides a comprehensive evaluation of the proposed method, showing its effectiveness across various datasets.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed explanation of how the genetic algorithm is applied, making it difficult to understand the method's core components.
- There is a lack of comparison with existing methods, particularly in terms of the number of parameters and the training data used.
- The novelty of the paper is questioned, as similar approaches have been explored in previous works.
- The paper does not adequately address the scalability of the proposed method, especially in terms of computational resources and time required for training.
- The experimental setup could be improved by including more detailed descriptions of the datasets and the training parameters used.
- The paper could benefit from a more comprehensive discussion on the limitations and potential negative societal impacts of the proposed method.

**Questions:**
- How is the fitness of a task prompt evaluated? Is it based on the accuracy of the model's response or the similarity to the ground truth answer?
- How is the genetic algorithm applied to the task of prompt evolution? What specific operators are used, and how are they applied?
- Could you provide more details on the initialization process for the genetic algorithm, particularly how the initial population is generated?
- How do the authors ensure that the mutation prompts generated by the LLM are effective and diverse enough to improve the task prompts?
- What are the specific training parameters used for the LLM, and how do they impact the performance of the method?
- How does the proposed method compare to other recent works in the field of prompt optimization, such as OPRO and LLMatic?
- Could you provide more details on the computational resources and time required for training the model, particularly in comparison to other methods?
