 **Summary:**
The paper introduces BEYOND, a novel adversarial example (AE) detection framework leveraging self-supervised learning (SSL) to detect adversarial perturbations. BEYOND operates by comparing the representation and label consistency of an input with its augmented neighbors, thereby identifying anomalous samples. The framework is designed to be a plug-and-play model that can be integrated with existing classifiers without compromising accuracy or incurring additional training costs. The methodology is supported by theoretical analysis and extensive experiments, demonstrating its effectiveness against various attacks and its ability to enhance the robustness of adversarially trained classifiers.

**Strengths:**
- BEYOND is a novel approach that utilizes SSL to detect adversarial examples, offering a fresh perspective in the field.
- The method is easy to implement and can be integrated as a plug-and-play model with existing classifiers, making it practical for real-world applications.
- The theoretical analysis provided is comprehensive, and the experimental results are robust, demonstrating the effectiveness of BEYOND against various attacks.
- BEYOND is adaptable to adversarial training classifiers, enhancing their robustness against adversarial attacks.
- The method is computationally efficient, which is crucial for real-time applications.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a clear discussion on how BEYOND performs against gradient masking attacks, which are known to be effective against similar methods.
- The novelty of BEYOND is questionable as it appears to be a combination of existing techniques like DkNN and SSL, with the main innovation being the use of SSL for AE detection.
- The paper could benefit from a more detailed discussion on the limitations of BEYOND, such as its performance under different attack scenarios and its generalizability to other types of networks.
- There is a lack of comparison with state-of-the-art methods in terms of computational efficiency and performance under different conditions.
- The paper does not adequately address the potential negative societal impacts of BEYOND, which is a critical aspect of responsible AI research.

**Questions:**
- Could you provide more details on how BEYOND performs against gradient masking attacks? What specific measures have been taken to mitigate the impact of these attacks?
- How does BEYOND compare to other state-of-the-art methods in terms of computational efficiency and performance under different conditions?
- Can BEYOND be adapted to other types of networks, such as transformer-based models? What are the potential challenges and benefits of such an adaptation?
- How does BEYOND handle adversarial attacks that are specifically designed to bypass its detection mechanisms? What are the potential countermeasures against such attacks?
- Can you provide a more detailed discussion on the limitations of BEYOND and how these limitations might affect its practical deployment?
- How does BEYOND address the potential negative societal impacts of its use, particularly in areas where adversarial examples are prevalent?
