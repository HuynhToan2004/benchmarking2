 **Summary:**
The paper introduces the Neural Evolutionary Kernel Method (NEKM) for solving a class of evolutionary PDEs using deep neural networks (DNNs) based on kernels. The method leverages operator splitting and boundary integral techniques to approximate the evolutionary kernels of solutions, which are then integrated into a neural network architecture. The NEKM aims to reduce the number of parameters and enhance training efficiency by utilizing mathematical prior knowledge, such as the fundamental solution of differential operators and geometry information in boundary integrals. The method is demonstrated through experiments on heat and Allen-Cahn equations, showcasing its potential for solving PDEs in complex domains and on manifolds. However, the paper suffers from clarity issues, particularly in explaining the methodology and its assumptions, and lacks a thorough discussion on the limitations and broader impacts of the proposed method.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, easy to follow, and provides a clear description of the methodology and its application.
- The methodology is innovative, integrating operator splitting, boundary integral techniques, and DNNs to approximate solution operators, which is a novel approach in the field of PDEs.
- The paper demonstrates the potential of the proposed method through experiments on heat and Allen-Cahn equations, showing promising results in terms of accuracy and generalizability across different domains.
- The use of mathematical prior knowledge, such as fundamental solutions and geometry information in boundary integrals, enhances the approximation capability of the proposed networks, leading to a significant reduction in the number of training parameters.
- The methodology is adaptable to problems in complex domains and on manifolds, which is a significant advantage over traditional methods.
- The paper provides a clear explanation of the method's components and their integration, making it accessible to readers unfamiliar with the subject matter.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a thorough discussion on the limitations and broader impacts of the proposed method, which is crucial for understanding its applicability and potential societal implications.
- The paper could benefit from a more detailed explanation of the mathematical assumptions and their implications, particularly in the context of the experiments conducted.
- The clarity of the paper could be improved, particularly in sections where the methodology and assumptions are discussed. More detailed explanations and examples would help in understanding the method's application and effectiveness.
- The paper does not provide sufficient details on the computational efficiency of the proposed method, which is a significant concern for practical applications.
- The paper does not compare the proposed method with other state-of-the-art methods, which is essential for evaluating its performance and positioning in the current research landscape.
- The paper's experimental setup and results lack depth and rigor, with insufficient details on the experimental conditions and the absence of error bars, which undermines the credibility of the results.
- The paper suffers from several typographical and formatting errors, which detract from its professional presentation.
