 **Summary:**
The paper introduces HART, a hypernetwork training approach aimed at generating task-specific weights for pre-trained language models (LLMs) without backpropagation. HART leverages layerwise dependencies by autoregressively generating weights for individual layers, enhancing stability through regularization of consistency across different demonstrations. The approach is evaluated on multiple datasets, demonstrating improvements over existing methods like HyperTuning. Key innovations include autoregressive parameter generation and local consistency regularization, which are shown to improve sample efficiency and training stability. Despite its strengths, the paper is criticized for its limited novelty and lack of comprehensive evaluation across diverse datasets.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, clear, and easy to follow, with a logical structure that effectively conveys the authors' ideas.
- The introduction of autoregressive parameter generation and local consistency regularization in HART represents a significant advancement in hypernetwork training, improving sample efficiency and training stability.
- HART demonstrates promising performance improvements over existing methods like HyperTuning, as shown in the experimental results.
- The paper provides a thorough analysis of the proposed approach, including ablation studies that validate the effectiveness of each component.
- The method is general and applicable to various PEFT methods, which enhances its utility and relevance to the field.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks comprehensive evaluation across diverse datasets, particularly in terms of the evaluation on the S-NI dataset, which is limited to 1616 tasks.
- There is a lack of novelty in the approach, as the idea of autoregressive generation and local consistency regularization has been previously explored in other works.
- The paper does not adequately compare HART with other state-of-the-art hypernetwork training methods, which could have provided a more robust validation of its effectiveness.
- The paper does not discuss the limitations of the proposed method, which is crucial for understanding its applicability and potential drawbacks.
- The paper does not provide sufficient details on the training and inference of the hypernetwork, which could affect the reproducibility and understanding of the results.
- The paper does not include a discussion on the computational cost and efficiency of HART, which is crucial for understanding its practical applicability.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors provide more details on the training and inference of the hypernetwork, including the computational resources required and the training time?
- How does the performance of HART vary with different hyperparameters, such as the learning rate and batch size?
- Could the authors elaborate on the differences between HART and HyperTuning in terms of parameter generation and local consistency regularization?
- How does the autoregressive parameter generation in HART differ from the parameter generation in HyperTuning?
- Can the authors discuss the potential limitations of the proposed method and how these might affect its practical application?
- Could the authors provide more comprehensive evaluations on additional datasets to validate the generalizability of the proposed method?
