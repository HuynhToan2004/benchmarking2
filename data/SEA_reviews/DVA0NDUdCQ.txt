 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel end-to-end training method, LEADING, aimed at reducing the computational and memory overheads in fine-tuning large language models (LLMs) on text-attributed graphs (TAGs). The method addresses the encoding redundancy and propagation redundancy issues by separating the LLM computation for target nodes and neighbor nodes, and utilizing an implicit graph modeling to reduce memory costs. The approach is validated through experiments on various datasets, demonstrating improved data and computation efficiency compared to existing methods. The paper is praised for its clear writing and comprehensive analysis, though concerns are raised about the novelty and the breadth of experimental validation.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written and easy to follow, with a clear motivation and a well-structured analysis of the computational redundancy in existing methods.
- The proposed method, LEADING, effectively addresses the encoding and propagation redundancy in LLM-GNN training, which is a significant issue in the field.
- The paper includes comprehensive experiments that validate the effectiveness of the proposed method, showing improved data and computation efficiency.
- The method is simple yet effective, and the analysis of computation redundancy in LLM-GNN training is insightful and well-presented.
- The paper provides a good ablation study to demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed method, and the experimental results are solid.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a broader comparison with more recent and relevant works, such as Grad and E2EG, which could provide a more comprehensive evaluation of the proposed method.
- The experimental setup is limited to only three datasets, which might not sufficiently demonstrate the generalizability of the findings.
- The paper does not adequately address the scalability of the method, particularly in terms of how it performs with larger graphs or more complex models.
- The method's novelty is questioned as it appears to be a combination of existing methods without significant new insights or contributions.
- There are minor issues with the clarity of some figures and the presentation of results, which could be improved for better readability and understanding.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors provide a more detailed comparison with Grad and E2EG to clarify the advantages of LEADING over these methods?
- How does the method perform when applied to larger graphs or more complex models, and what are the potential limitations or challenges in such scenarios?
- Could the authors provide a more detailed analysis of the experimental results, particularly in terms of the impact of different hyperparameters on the performance?
- How does the method handle the scalability issues, and what are the potential solutions for addressing these challenges?
- Can the authors discuss the potential impact of different hyperparameters on the performance of the method, and provide a more detailed analysis of the experimental results?
