 **Summary:**
The paper introduces Gift4Code, a novel method for fine-tuning large language models (LLMs) in code generation tasks using synthetic data derived from LLMs themselves. This approach leverages execution-derived feedback to enhance the model's alignment with user intentions, focusing on data science applications. The method involves generating synthetic data by prompting LLMs with natural language intents and code solutions, which are then filtered based on executability. The data is used to fine-tune the LLM, improving its ability to generate accurate code solutions. The paper demonstrates the effectiveness of this method through experiments on two data science benchmarks, showing improvements in code quality and alignment with user intentions.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written and easy to follow, with clear explanations of the proposed method.
- The idea of using execution-derived feedback to enhance the alignment of LLM-generated code with user intentions is innovative and effective, as demonstrated by the experiments.
- The methodology is well-motivated and the experimental results are strong, showing significant improvements over existing methods.
- The paper is well-structured, making it easy to understand the methodology and the results.
- The use of synthetic data generated by LLMs for instruction fine-tuning is a novel approach that leverages the capabilities of LLMs to generate high-quality synthetic data.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed discussion on the limitations of the proposed method, such as the scalability of the approach to other programming languages or different types of code generation tasks.
- There is a lack of clarity in the paper regarding the generation of the "generalist" LLM and its role in generating synthetic data.
- The paper does not sufficiently compare the proposed method with other existing methods, which could help in understanding its advantages or disadvantages.
- The method's reliance on synthetic data generated by LLMs might lead to issues with the quality and diversity of the data, which could affect the model's performance.
- The paper does not discuss potential negative societal impacts of the proposed method.

**Questions:**
- Could you provide more details on the "generalist" LLM, particularly how it is generated and what role it plays in the data generation process?
- How does the proposed method compare with other existing methods, and what are the specific advantages or disadvantages of using synthetic data generated by LLMs?
- Can you provide examples or more detailed explanations of how the I/O specification is generated from the execution results?
- How does the method handle cases where the generated code does not satisfy the I/O specification but is still valid or useful?
- Could you elaborate on the limitations of the proposed method, particularly in terms of its scalability and applicability to other programming languages or different types of code generation tasks?
