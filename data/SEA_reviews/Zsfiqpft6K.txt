 **Summary:**
The paper introduces DiffMatch, a novel diffusion-based framework for dense matching that explicitly models both data and prior terms. It leverages a cascaded pipeline to address the limited resolution of diffusion models, with a focus on enhancing the handling of inherent ambiguities in matching, such as textureless regions, repetitive patterns, large displacements, or noises. The framework is evaluated on standard benchmarks and shows significant performance improvements over existing approaches. Despite its strengths, the paper is criticized for its limited novelty, as it heavily relies on existing techniques without significant innovation, and its computational efficiency, which is less than ideal.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel diffusion-based framework for dense matching that explicitly models both data and prior terms, which is a significant advancement in the field.
- The cascaded pipeline effectively addresses the limited resolution of diffusion models, enhancing the model's ability to handle complex matching scenarios.
- The proposed method demonstrates significant performance improvements over existing approaches, validated through rigorous evaluation on standard benchmarks.
- The paper is well-written, making it easy to follow, and provides comprehensive experimental results that support the effectiveness of the proposed method.
- The ablation studies are thorough, offering insights into the effectiveness of various components of the proposed method.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a clear differentiation from existing methods, such as DKM, which also utilizes a kernel regression global matcher to find accurate global matches and their certainty.
- The method's computational efficiency is a concern, with the inference process being slower than some existing methods.
- The novelty of the proposed method is questionable, as it heavily relies on existing techniques without significant innovation.
- The method's performance improvement over existing methods is marginal, and the paper does not sufficiently discuss the limitations or failure cases of the proposed method.
- The paper's presentation could be improved, particularly in the clarity of the methodology and the organization of the experimental results.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors clarify the differences between their method and DKM, especially in terms of the use of a kernel regression global matcher?
- How does the proposed method compare in terms of computational efficiency with existing methods, and what are the potential strategies for improving the inference speed?
- Can the authors provide more details on the limitations or failure cases of the proposed method, and discuss how these might be addressed in future work?
- How does the proposed method handle the computational overhead of constructing the local matching cost, and what are the implications for the overall efficiency of the method?
- Could the authors discuss the potential for extending the method to handle other types of matching tasks, such as optical flow estimation?
