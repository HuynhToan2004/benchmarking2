 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel approach to knowledge graph (KG) completion using reinforcement learning (RL) and multi-relation graph neural networks (GRNs). The proposed method, RGMG, integrates a Multi-relation Graph Attention Network (MGAT) with a Query-aware Action Embedding (QAE) module to enhance the quality of entity and relation embeddings. The RGMG agent navigates through the KG to complete missing connections, demonstrating competitive performance on various KG reasoning benchmarks. The paper provides a detailed experimental setup, including ablation studies and comparisons with state-of-the-art methods, showing the effectiveness of the proposed components. However, concerns were raised about the novelty of the approach, the clarity of the writing, and the complexity of the model, which might limit its practical application.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, easy to follow, and provides a clear motivation for the proposed method.
- The integration of multi-relation graph attention networks (MGAT) and query-aware action embedding (QAE) modules within the reinforcement learning (RL) framework is a novel approach.
- The experimental setup is comprehensive, including ablation studies, comparisons with state-of-the-art methods, and a detailed description of the experimental setup.
- The method is supported by a strong theoretical foundation, and the paper provides a detailed description of the experimental setup, including the datasets used and the evaluation methods.
- The paper demonstrates a strong performance of the proposed method on various KG reasoning benchmarks, showing the effectiveness of the proposed components.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed explanation of how the MGAT module improves the quality of entity and relation embeddings, which is crucial for understanding the proposed method.
- The complexity of the model and the training process is high, which might limit its practical application.
- The novelty of the approach is questionable, as similar methods have been previously proposed, such as the use of graph attention networks for knowledge graph completion.
- The paper does not adequately address the limitations of the proposed method, which is a significant oversight in the evaluation of its applicability and effectiveness.
- The paper could benefit from a more thorough literature review and comparison with other relevant works in the field.
- The paper suffers from clarity issues, particularly in the writing and presentation of the experimental results and methodology.

**Questions:**
- How does the MGAT module specifically improve the quality of entity and relation embeddings?
- Could the authors provide a more detailed explanation of how the QAE module enhances the performance of the RL agent?
- How does the proposed method compare to other state-of-the-art methods in terms of computational efficiency and scalability?
- What are the specific limitations of the proposed method, and how do these affect its applicability and effectiveness in real-world scenarios?
- How does the proposed method perform on more complex knowledge graphs, such as those with a larger number of entities and relations?
- Can the authors clarify the notations and definitions used in the paper, particularly in the equations and method descriptions?
- What are the implications of the method's performance on more complex knowledge graphs, and how does it compare to other methods in terms of computational efficiency and scalability?
