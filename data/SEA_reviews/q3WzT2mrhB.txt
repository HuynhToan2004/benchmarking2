 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel method for estimating multi-person 3D human mesh from WiFi signals using a transformer architecture. It proposes a four-stage pipeline that includes CSI feature extractors, CSI feature encoders, coarse decoders, and refine decoders. The method employs a novel approach by using WiFi signals to estimate human poses and shapes, which is a significant departure from traditional camera-based methods. The paper also introduces a new dataset, which is the first of its kind for multi-person mesh regression from CSI signals. The method is evaluated against various metrics and achieves competitive results, although it has been noted that the dataset's quality and the novelty of the contributions are questionable.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel method for estimating multi-person 3D human mesh from WiFi signals, which is a significant departure from traditional camera-based methods.
- It proposes a four-stage pipeline that includes CSI feature extractors, CSI feature encoders, coarse decoders, and refine decoders, which is a comprehensive approach to the problem.
- The paper includes a new dataset specifically designed for multi-person mesh regression from CSI signals, which is the first of its kind and a valuable resource for future research.
- The method achieves competitive results, as shown in the experimental results, which are promising.
- The paper is well-written and easy to follow, making it accessible to readers.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a clear motivation for using WiFi signals for mesh regression, which could be better explained to enhance the paper's impact.
- The dataset used in the experiments is of low quality and lacks diversity, which could affect the robustness and generalizability of the proposed method.
- The paper does not sufficiently compare its method with existing works, such as mmMesh and M4Mesh, which could have provided a more robust evaluation of the proposed method.
- The novelty of the contributions is questionable, as the method primarily uses a transformer architecture and does not introduce significant new techniques or insights.
- The paper lacks detailed implementation and training details, which could hinder reproducibility and further research.
- The paper does not address the issue of occlusion in the dataset, which is a significant limitation for the practical application of the proposed method.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors clarify the motivation behind using WiFi signals for mesh regression and how it compares to other modalities like radar?
- How does the proposed method handle occlusion in the dataset, and what are the implications for the generalizability of the results?
- Could the authors provide more details on the dataset, such as the number of samples, diversity, and quality control measures?
- How does the proposed method compare to existing works, particularly in terms of performance and novelty?
- Can the authors provide more implementation and training details to enhance reproducibility and transparency?
- Is there a possibility of extending the method to handle single-person mesh regression, and how might this affect the results?
