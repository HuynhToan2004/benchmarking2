 **Summary:**
The paper investigates the challenges of invariant learning in node-level out-of-distribution (OOD) generalization on graphs. It critiques the effectiveness of existing methods like IRM and VREx, highlighting their reliance on spurious features. The authors propose a novel method, LoRe-CIA, which leverages cross-environment intra-class alignment to improve OOD generalization without requiring environment labels or complex partitioning processes. The method aligns node representations across different environments, aiming to enhance the model's ability to generalize to unseen test environments. The paper presents theoretical and empirical evidence supporting the proposed method's effectiveness, demonstrating its superiority over existing methods on the GOOD benchmark.

**Strengths:**
- The paper addresses a critical and underexplored problem of node-level out-of-distribution (OOD) generalization on graphs, which is crucial for real-world applications.
- The authors provide a theoretical analysis of the failure cases of existing methods like IRMv1 and VREx, which is both insightful and well-supported by empirical evidence.
- The proposed method, LoRe-CIA, is innovative and effectively addresses the challenges of invariant learning in node-level OOD generalization. It does not require environment labels, making it more practical and applicable.
- The paper is well-written, with clear and insightful theoretical analysis and empirical results that demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed method.
- The authors have conducted a comprehensive comparison with state-of-the-art methods, showing that their method outperforms them in most cases.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed discussion on the assumptions made in the theoretical analysis, which could limit the generalizability of the findings.
- The experimental setup could be improved to include more diverse datasets and different GNN architectures to validate the robustness of the proposed method.
- There is a need for more detailed ablation studies to understand the impact of different components of the proposed method, such as the alignment of cross-environment intra-class features and the regularization of spurious features.
- The paper could benefit from a more comprehensive comparison with other methods that address node-level OOD generalization on graphs, especially those that use different GNN architectures.
- The theoretical analysis is limited to a simple case, which might not fully capture the complexity of real-world graph data.
- The paper does not discuss the limitations of the proposed method, which could provide valuable insights into its applicability and potential improvements.
