 **Summary:**
The paper explores the adversarial robustness of dataset condensation methods, specifically focusing on the curvature of the loss landscape in input space. It introduces a new method, GUARD, which incorporates curvature regularization to enhance robustness. The authors empirically demonstrate that existing dataset condensation methods lack robustness and propose a new method to address this. The paper provides theoretical analysis and empirical evidence, including experiments on MNIST and CIFAR10 datasets, to support the claims. Despite the novel approach and the theoretical backing, the paper has been critiqued for its limited empirical scope, the lack of clarity in certain theoretical assumptions, and the need for more comprehensive experimental validation on larger, more complex datasets.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel perspective on dataset condensation by focusing on its robustness characteristics, which is a significant and timely issue in the field.
- The proposed method, GUARD, is well-motivated and theoretically grounded, providing a clear explanation of its design and its advantages over existing methods.
- The paper includes comprehensive empirical evaluations that demonstrate the effectiveness of GUARD in enhancing the robustness of models against various adversarial attacks.
- The theoretical analysis is solid, providing a theoretical bound on the robustness of dataset condensation, which is an interesting contribution to the field.
- The paper is well-written, making it accessible and easy to follow, with a clear structure that guides the reader through the various aspects of the research.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper's empirical scope is limited to relatively simple datasets like MNIST and CIFAR10, which may not adequately represent the challenges of more complex, high-resolution datasets like ImageNet.
- There is a lack of clarity in some theoretical assumptions and the paper's main theoretical contribution, which could benefit from a more detailed discussion or proof.
- The paper does not sufficiently address the scalability of the proposed method, particularly in terms of its computational efficiency and its applicability to larger, more complex datasets.
- The experimental results, while promising, are somewhat limited in scope and do not include comparisons with more advanced adversarial attacks or other robustness benchmarks.
- The paper could benefit from a more detailed discussion on the limitations of the proposed method and its potential negative societal impacts.
