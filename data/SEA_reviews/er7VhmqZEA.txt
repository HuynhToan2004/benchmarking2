 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel framework, Noisy Multi-view Contrastive Learning Framework (NMCLK), for enhancing top-K recommendation. This framework integrates multiple views of user-item interactions, including global-level structural, item-item semantic, and local views, to optimize recommendations. The NMCLK model employs contrastive learning and alignment/uniformity constraints to refine node embeddings, aiming to enhance recommendation accuracy. The framework was tested on two benchmark datasets, showing improvements over state-of-the-art methods. However, concerns were raised about the novelty of the approach, the clarity of the methodology, and the lack of comprehensive experimental validation.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, making it easy to follow, and the problem addressed is significant and relevant.
- The authors have proposed a novel framework, NMCLK, which introduces multiple views to enhance the recommendation process, including global-level structural, item-item semantic, and local views.
- The methodology of using contrastive learning and alignment/uniformity constraints is innovative and effectively refines node embeddings, improving the accuracy of recommendations.
- The paper includes detailed experimental results, demonstrating the effectiveness of the proposed method in enhancing the performance of top-K recommendation.
- The paper is well-organized, with clear explanations of the methodology and the experimental setup, and provides code for reproducibility.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks clarity in certain sections, particularly in the explanation of the methodology and the experimental setup, which could benefit from more detailed explanations and figures.
- The novelty of the approach is questionable as it appears to be a combination of existing methods without significant innovation.
- The experimental evaluation is limited, with only two datasets used, and the results do not convincingly demonstrate the superiority of the proposed method over state-of-the-art methods.
- There are several typographical and formatting errors that need correction, which could affect the readability and professionalism of the paper.
- The paper does not sufficiently address the limitations of the proposed method, which could affect the reliability and generalizability of the results.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors provide more details on the construction of the three views, particularly the item-item semantic view, to clarify how it integrates with the overall methodology?
- How does the proposed method handle different types of knowledge graphs, and what are the implications of using different types of knowledge graphs?
- Can the authors elaborate on the motivation behind the use of noise addition and the alignment/uniformity constraints, and how these contribute to the overall effectiveness of the method?
- How does the proposed method compare to other state-of-the-art methods in terms of computational efficiency and scalability, particularly in larger datasets or more complex recommendation scenarios?
- The paper mentions using two datasets; could the authors provide more details on the selection criteria for these datasets and consider using more diverse datasets to validate the generalizability of the findings?
