 **Summary:**
The paper investigates the application of Graph Neural Processes (GNPs) in drug discovery, specifically focusing on the DOCKSTRING dataset, which involves docking scores for various ligands against different proteins. The authors propose a fine-tuning strategy to enhance meta-generalization and demonstrate the effectiveness of GNPs in few-shot learning tasks, comparing them to other models like Gaussian Processes and various meta-learning approaches. The paper explores the use of GNPs in drug discovery, highlighting their potential in meta-learning scenarios and their ability to provide uncertainty estimates. Despite the paper's strengths in methodology and experimental design, concerns were raised regarding the novelty of the approach and the clarity of the writing, particularly in explaining the experimental setups and the implications of the results.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, clear, and structured, making it easy to follow and understand the proposed methods and their applications.
- The experimental design is comprehensive, including various models and settings, which supports the robustness of the findings.
- The paper addresses a significant and relevant problem in drug discovery, focusing on the use of Graph Neural Processes (GNPs) and their application in few-shot learning tasks.
- The authors provide a detailed explanation of the methodology, including the use of neural processes and their application to molecular functions, which is both innovative and practical.
- The paper includes a thorough analysis of the results, including calibration of uncertainty estimates and comparisons with other models like Gaussian Processes, which enhances the understanding of the proposed methods.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a clear definition and explanation of the "meta-learning" problem, which could confuse readers unfamiliar with the concept.
- The novelty of the approach is questionable, as the use of neural processes and fine-tuning strategies in drug discovery is not a new concept.
- The paper could benefit from a more detailed discussion on the limitations of the proposed methods and potential avenues for future research.
- The experimental setup and results are not always clearly explained, particularly in terms of the data splits and the statistical significance of the results.
- There is a need for more detailed comparisons with other models, especially in terms of computational efficiency and scalability, which could strengthen the paper's contributions.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors clarify the specific "meta-learning" problem addressed in the paper and how it differs from other applications of neural processes in drug discovery?
- How does the proposed method compare in terms of computational efficiency and scalability with other models like Gaussian Processes, especially in large-scale applications?
- Can the authors provide more details on the experimental setup, particularly the data splits and the statistical significance of the results?
- How does the fine-tuning strategy affect the calibration of uncertainty estimates, and what are the implications of this approach for the practical application of the models?
- Could the authors elaborate on the potential limitations of the proposed methods and suggest areas for future research to address these limitations?
