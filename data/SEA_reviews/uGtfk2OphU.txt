 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel method for selective rationalization in text classification tasks, specifically addressing the issue of shortcuts in data. It proposes a two-phase approach where the first phase identifies shortcut tokens using a trained unsupervised rationalization model, and the second phase mitigates the impact of these shortcuts on the rationalization process. The paper also includes data augmentation techniques to enhance the method's effectiveness. The method is evaluated on several datasets, showing improvements over existing methods. However, the paper has been critiqued for its lack of clarity in presentation, insufficient comparisons with recent state-of-the-art methods, and potential over-reliance on assumptions that may not hold in practical scenarios.

**Strengths:**
- The paper addresses the important problem of selective rationalization in text classification tasks, which is crucial for understanding and improving the performance of deep neural networks.
- The proposed method is novel and well-explained, with clear motivation and detailed experimental results that demonstrate its effectiveness.
- The paper is well-written, making it easy to follow, and includes a thorough literature review and comparative analysis with state-of-the-art methods.
- The method's application of shortcut discovery and data augmentation is innovative and shows potential for enhancing the performance of selective rationalization methods.
- The paper includes a detailed description of the proposed method, including a clear presentation of the experimental setup and results, which supports the claims made.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks clarity in some sections, particularly in the definitions and explanations of key terms and concepts, which could confuse readers.
- The experimental results are not consistently strong across all datasets, and the paper does not sufficiently compare its method with the most recent state-of-the-art methods.
- The paper's reliance on assumptions, such as the existence of shortcuts in the data, may not hold in all practical scenarios, which could limit the generalizability of the findings.
- There is a lack of discussion on the limitations of the proposed method, which could help in understanding its scope and applicability.
- The paper does not adequately address the potential negative societal impacts of the proposed method, which is a significant oversight in ethical considerations.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors clarify the definition and use of terms such as "selector" and "predictor" in the context of selective rationalization?
- How does the proposed method compare with the most recent state-of-the-art methods in terms of performance and efficiency?
- Could the authors provide more details on the experimental setup, such as the specific datasets used and the conditions under which the experiments were conducted?
- How does the proposed method handle the potential for shortcuts in the data, and what are the implications of this assumption not holding in practical scenarios?
- Could the authors discuss the limitations of their method and suggest potential avenues for future research to address these limitations?
- How does the proposed method ensure that the rationales it generates are not biased or misleading, particularly in cases where shortcuts are present in the data?
