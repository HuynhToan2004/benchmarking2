 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel method for regularizing classifiers in classifier-guided score-based models, aiming to improve the performance of conditional generation tasks with limited labeled data. The approach leverages energy-based models to calibrate the classifier's internal scores, which are then used to guide the generation process. The method is evaluated on datasets like CIFAR-10 and CIFAR-100, showing improvements over existing methods in certain scenarios. However, the paper's contributions are seen as incremental and the empirical results are not convincingly superior to existing methods. Concerns are raised about the theoretical justification of the approach and the clarity of its presentation.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, clear, and easy to follow, with a logical flow that aids in understanding the proposed methodology.
- The motivation behind the research is clear, focusing on improving the performance of classifier-guided score-based models, particularly in scenarios with limited labeled data.
- The experiments are well-designed, providing a comprehensive evaluation of the proposed method and demonstrating its effectiveness through various metrics.
- The paper includes a detailed comparison with existing methods, highlighting the strengths and weaknesses of each approach, which aids in understanding the proposed method's advantages.
- The method is simple, effective, and does not require additional training of the unconditional SGM, making it a practical solution for improving classifier-guided models.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a clear and compelling justification for why the proposed method is effective, particularly in terms of its theoretical contributions.
- The experimental results are not consistently superior to existing methods, with some results showing marginal improvements or even worse performance in certain scenarios.
- The paper does not sufficiently address the issue of overfitting in classifier-guided models, which is a critical problem in the field.
- The paper does not sufficiently compare its method with state-of-the-art methods, such as CFG and CFG-labeled, which could have provided a clearer picture of the method's performance relative to current best practices.
- The paper lacks a thorough discussion on the limitations of the proposed method, which could help in understanding its applicability and potential drawbacks.
- The presentation of the paper could be improved, particularly in terms of clarity and depth in the experimental results and methodological descriptions.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors clarify the main differences between the proposed method and existing methods, such as CG-DLSM and JEM, particularly in terms of their theoretical contributions and practical implications?
- How does the proposed method address the issue of overfitting in classifier-guided models, and what empirical evidence supports this claim?
- In the context of semi-supervised learning, how does the proposed method compare to other methods that utilize unlabeled data, such as self-training and noisy student?
- Could the authors provide more detailed results on the performance of the proposed method across different datasets and conditions, especially in scenarios where the performance is marginal or inconsistent?
- How does the proposed method handle the integration of unlabeled data into the training process, and what are the implications of this integration on the model's performance and generalizability?
