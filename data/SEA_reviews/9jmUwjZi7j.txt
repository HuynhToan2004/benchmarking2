 **Summary:**
The paper introduces DreamFuser, a novel method for offline reinforcement learning that integrates a diffusion model into a trajectory-based policy learning framework. By viewing the diffusion process as a Markovian transition, the authors propose a new objective function that optimizes the denoised action sequence, rather than the original action sequence. This approach is supported by a Generalized Noisy Action Markov Decision Process (GNMDP) framework, which facilitates the integration of Q-learning and behavior cloning losses. The method is tested on various benchmarks, including D4RL and ManiSkill2, showing improvements over existing methods. However, the paper suffers from unclear presentation, particularly in the explanation of the GNMDP framework and the integration of the diffusion model, which makes it difficult for readers to fully grasp the contributions and implications of the proposed method.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel approach to offline reinforcement learning by integrating diffusion models with Q-learning and behavior cloning, which is a significant advancement in the field.
- The method is well-motivated and well-explained, with clear and detailed descriptions of the proposed method and its implementation.
- The empirical results demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed method, showing improvements over existing baselines on several benchmarks.
- The paper is well-written, making it accessible and easy to follow, with clear and concise explanations of the methodology and its implications.
- The integration of the diffusion model with Q-learning and behavior cloning is a novel approach that addresses the challenges of learning from sub-optimal trajectories, which is a significant issue in offline RL.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper suffers from a lack of clarity in the presentation, particularly in the explanation of the GNMDP framework and the integration of the diffusion model.
- There are numerous typos and grammatical errors throughout the paper, which detract from the professional quality of the manuscript.
- The paper lacks a detailed discussion on the limitations of the proposed method, which could help readers understand the scope and applicability of the findings.
- The empirical results are not convincingly superior to existing methods, with some results showing only marginal improvements or even inferior performance in certain scenarios.
- The paper does not adequately compare the proposed method with other state-of-the-art methods, which could have provided a more robust evaluation of the method's effectiveness.
- There are concerns about the novelty of the method, as it seems to be a simple combination of existing ideas without significant new insights or contributions.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors clarify the role and significance of the GNMDP framework in the proposed method? How does it contribute to the overall effectiveness of the method?
- How does the proposed method handle the integration of the diffusion model with Q-learning and behavior cloning? What are the specific challenges and solutions in this integration?
- Can the authors provide more details on the experimental setup, including the hyperparameters used and the specifics of the baseline methods compared?
- In the results section, could the authors provide more detailed explanations of the results, particularly where the proposed method underperforms or performs similarly to existing methods?
- How does the proposed method compare to other state-of-the-art methods in terms of computational efficiency and memory utilization?
- Could the authors provide a more detailed discussion on the limitations of the proposed method and how these might affect its practical applicability?
