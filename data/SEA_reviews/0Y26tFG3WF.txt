 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel method for enhancing the precision of Lagrangian Neural Networks (LNNs) by integrating a regularization term that prioritizes significant bits in the Lagrangian estimates. This method is designed to improve the accuracy of LNNs in modeling chaotic systems, such as the double pendulum and Henon-Heiles systems, by reducing trajectory deviations. The approach involves a custom neural network architecture that emphasizes the importance of each significant bit in the Lagrangian estimates, which is validated through experiments against Finite Element solvers. Despite its innovative approach, the paper suffers from several issues, including unclear writing, lack of theoretical backing, and inadequate experimental validation.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel approach to enhancing the precision of Lagrangian Neural Networks (LNNs) by integrating a regularization term that prioritizes significant bits in the Lagrangian estimates.
- The methodology is supported by experiments conducted on two dynamic systems that exhibit chaotic behavior, demonstrating the effectiveness of the proposed approach in reducing trajectory deviations.
- The paper is well-structured, with clear and concise sections that effectively communicate the main ideas and results.
- The approach addresses the challenge of improving the precision of LNNs, which is crucial for accurately modeling chaotic systems.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper suffers from poor writing and lacks clarity in some sections, making it difficult to follow the logic and understand the proposed methodologies.
- There is a significant absence of theoretical backing for the proposed method, which undermines the credibility of the results.
- The experimental validation is insufficient and lacks depth, particularly in terms of comparing the proposed method with other existing approaches and providing a comprehensive analysis of the results.
- The paper does not adequately address the limitations of the proposed method, such as the scalability and generalizability of the approach.
- The paper does not sufficiently discuss the limitations of the experiments, such as the small size of the dataset used and the potential impact of different initial conditions on the results.

**Questions:**
- Could you clarify the role and impact of the regularization term in the LNN model, particularly in terms of its contribution to the overall precision and accuracy of the model?
- How does the proposed method compare with other existing approaches for improving the precision of LNNs, such as those that use different loss functions or optimization techniques?
- What are the specific advantages of using the proposed method over other existing methods for modeling chaotic systems, especially in terms of computational efficiency and practical applicability?
- Could you provide more details on the experimental setup, such as the size and diversity of the dataset used, the initial conditions tested, and the computational resources required?
- How does the proposed method perform in more complex or higher-dimensional chaotic systems, and what are the potential challenges or limitations in scaling up the approach?
