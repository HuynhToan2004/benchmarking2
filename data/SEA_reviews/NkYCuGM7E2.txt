 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel approach to autonomous driving by integrating a large language model (LLM) with model predictive control (MPC) to enhance decision-making in complex scenarios. The LLM is used to analyze high-level information, reason using commonsense, and provide interpretable outputs that can be translated into actionable driving commands. This integration aims to improve the generalization capabilities of autonomous driving systems, particularly in handling rare events and enhancing interpretability. The paper presents experimental results showing that this approach outperforms baseline methods in various single-vehicle tasks and can handle multi-vehicle coordination. However, the paper lacks a detailed comparison with other LLM-based methods and does not address the computational efficiency or scalability of the proposed method in more complex real-world scenarios.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, clear, and easy to follow, with a clear motivation and a well-designed framework for integrating LLMs with MPC for autonomous driving.
- The method is innovative, using LLMs for high-level decision-making and MPC for low-level control, which is a novel approach in the field of autonomous driving.
- The paper includes a comprehensive evaluation of the proposed method, demonstrating its effectiveness in various single-vehicle decision-making tasks and multi-vehicle coordination scenarios.
- The use of LLMs for high-level decision-making in autonomous driving is a significant advancement, as it addresses the limitations of traditional methods in comprehending high-level information, generalizing to rare events, and providing interpretability.
- The paper includes a chain-of-thought framework for LLMs, which is a useful contribution to the field, and demonstrates the superiority of the proposed method over existing learning-based and optimization-based methods.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed comparison with other LLM-based methods, which could provide a better understanding of the advantages and limitations of the proposed method.
- The paper does not address the computational efficiency or scalability of the proposed method, particularly in more complex real-world scenarios.
- The paper does not discuss the limitations of the proposed method, which is crucial for understanding its applicability and potential drawbacks.
- The paper's experimental results are limited to simulations, which may not fully represent real-world scenarios and do not include a comparison with existing LLM-based autonomous driving methods.
- The paper does not provide a detailed discussion on how the LLM's decisions are translated into actionable driving commands, which is a critical aspect of the proposed method.
- The paper does not address the potential for the LLM to provide incorrect or misleading outputs, which could lead to safety issues in autonomous driving.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors provide a detailed comparison with other LLM-based methods to highlight the advantages and limitations of the proposed method?
- How does the proposed method address the limitations of existing learning-based and optimization-based methods in autonomous driving?
- Can the authors provide more details on the computational efficiency and scalability of the proposed method in more complex real-world scenarios?
- How does the proposed method ensure safety and reliability in autonomous driving, particularly in scenarios where the LLM's decisions may not be accurate or complete?
- Can the authors provide more details on the experimental setup, including the specific LLM used and the simulation environment, to better understand the conditions under which the experiments were conducted?
- Can the authors discuss the potential for the LLM to provide incorrect or misleading outputs and how this risk is mitigated in the proposed method?
