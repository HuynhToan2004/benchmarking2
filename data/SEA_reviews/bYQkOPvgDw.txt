 **Summary:**
The paper introduces PRGNN, a novel probabilistic graphical model-based framework designed to address the challenge of robust graph neural networks (GNNs) in the presence of noisy labels. The framework, which includes two variants (PRGNN-v1 and PRGNN-v2), leverages a Bayesian network to generate clean labels from noisy and clean label sets. The methodology involves maximizing the likelihood of clean labels given the graph and noisy labels, and incorporating the noisy label set into the Bayesian network to generate clean labels. Extensive experiments across various datasets demonstrate the robustness and effectiveness of PRGNN, particularly in high noise-rate scenarios. However, the paper suffers from a lack of clarity in its methodology, particularly in the introduction of key concepts and the complexity of the model, which might limit its accessibility and applicability in practical scenarios.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel probabilistic graphical model-based framework, PRGNN, which effectively handles noisy labels in graph neural networks (GNNs) without relying on the label smoothness assumption.
- The methodology is well-explained, with clear derivations and comprehensive experimental results that demonstrate the robustness and effectiveness of PRGNN across different datasets and noise rates.
- The paper is well-written, making it easy to follow, and includes a detailed analysis of the hyper-parameter sensitivity and ablation studies, which provide valuable insights into the model's behavior.
- The approach is innovative in utilizing a probabilistic graphical model for robust GNNs against noisy labels, which is a significant contribution to the field.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper suffers from a lack of clarity in the introduction of key concepts and methodologies, which could hinder understanding and reproducibility.
- The complexity of the proposed model, including multiple GNNs and variational distributions, makes it difficult to understand and implement, especially in practical scenarios.
- The experimental results are not sufficiently convincing, with marginal improvements over existing methods, and the results are not compared with state-of-the-art methods, such as PIGNN and RTGNN.
- The paper does not provide a detailed discussion on the limitations of the proposed method, which is crucial for understanding the model's applicability and potential drawbacks.
- There are several typographical and grammatical errors that need correction to enhance the readability and professionalism of the paper.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors provide more detailed explanations of the key concepts and methodologies introduced in the paper to enhance clarity and understanding?
- How does the proposed method perform in scenarios where the clean label set is not available?
- Could the authors elaborate on the advantages of PRGNN over other existing methods, such as PIGNN and RTGNN, especially in terms of performance and practical applicability?
- How does the size of the clean label set affect the performance of PRGNN, and what are the optimal conditions for using PRGNN in practical scenarios?
- Could the authors discuss the limitations of their method and suggest potential directions for future research to address these limitations?
