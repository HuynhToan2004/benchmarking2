 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel approach to reinforcement learning (RL) by incorporating elastic time steps, allowing the policy to determine the duration of each time step. This approach aims to reduce computational and energy consumption by adjusting the frequency of actions based on the task's requirements. The proposed method, Soft Elastic Actor-Critic (SEAC), extends the Soft Actor-Critic (SAC) algorithm to include the duration of action execution as a variable. The paper presents experiments in a simplified 2D environment, comparing SEAC to traditional RL methods like SAC and PPO, showing improvements in computational efficiency and energy consumption. However, the paper's experimental setup is criticized for its simplicity and lack of comparison with other state-of-the-art RL algorithms.

**Strengths:**
- The paper addresses an important and timely problem by exploring the use of elastic time steps in reinforcement learning, which could lead to significant reductions in computational and energy costs.
- The proposed method is novel and extends existing methods like SAC to include the duration of action execution as a variable, which is a unique contribution to the field.
- The paper is well-written, clear, and easy to follow, with a clear motivation and well-defined problem statement.
- The experiments are well-designed and show that the proposed method, SEAC, outperforms the baseline methods in terms of computational efficiency and energy consumption.
- The idea of incorporating the duration of action execution into the reinforcement learning process is novel and could lead to more efficient and effective RL algorithms.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a thorough discussion on the challenges and potential limitations of the proposed method, especially in more complex environments.
- The experimental setup is too simplistic and does not adequately test the proposed method in more complex or realistic scenarios.
- The paper does not sufficiently compare the proposed method with other state-of-the-art RL algorithms, which could have provided a more robust evaluation of the proposed method.
- The paper's claims regarding the energy consumption and computational efficiency of the proposed method are not convincingly supported by the experimental results.
- The reward policy used in the experiments is not clearly defined, which could lead to confusion about the method's effectiveness.
- The paper's writing and presentation need improvement, particularly in terms of clarity and coherence, especially in the introduction and methodology sections.

**Questions:**
- How does the proposed method perform in more complex environments, such as those with many obstacles or more complex dynamics?
- Can the authors clarify the definition and use of the reward policy in the experiments, particularly how it affects the performance of the proposed method?
- How does the proposed method compare to other state-of-the-art RL algorithms in terms of computational efficiency and energy consumption?
- Could the authors provide more details on the experimental setup, particularly how the energy consumption and computational efficiency were measured?
- How does the proposed method handle the issue of non-Markovian dynamics, especially in the context of the simplified environment used in the experiments?
