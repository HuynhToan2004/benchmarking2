 **Summary:**
The paper introduces CleanBA, a distributed reinforcement learning (DRL) platform designed to address reproducibility issues in DRL. It focuses on the actor-learner framework, particularly in the context of IMPALA, and proposes a new architecture to ensure deterministic rollout data composition. The platform is evaluated on Atari games, showing improvements in performance and training efficiency compared to existing methods like IMPALA and PPO. The authors claim that CleanBA's architecture is more reproducible and scalable, though the paper's scope and the breadth of its evaluation are limited.

**Strengths:**
- The paper addresses an important and relevant issue in the field of distributed reinforcement learning (DRL), focusing on reproducibility and performance.
- The proposed CleanBA platform, which includes CleanBA PPO and CleanBA IMPALA, is designed to improve reproducibility and scalability in DRL.
- The experiments conducted on Atari games demonstrate that CleanBA can achieve similar or better performance than existing methods like IMPALA and PPO, while also reducing training time.
- The paper is well-written, clear, and easy to follow, making it accessible to a broad audience.
- The authors have made significant efforts to ensure the reproducibility of their results by making the codebase and experimental setups publicly available.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a comprehensive evaluation of the proposed architecture on a broader set of environments beyond Atari, which limits the generalizability of the findings.
- There is a notable absence of a discussion on related works, particularly concerning distributed RL systems, which could have provided a richer context for the research.
- The paper's focus on reproducibility raises questions about the originality of the work, as it primarily builds upon existing frameworks like CleanRL and Sebulba.
- The paper's title and claims about the platform's novelty are overstated, as the contributions appear to be incremental rather than groundbreaking.
- There is a lack of discussion on the scalability of CleanBA, particularly in terms of its performance and resource requirements in larger, more complex environments.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors provide more details on how the proposed architecture addresses reproducibility issues beyond those specific to IMPALA?
- How does the proposed architecture compare to other existing distributed RL systems in terms of performance, scalability, and resource efficiency?
- Could the authors elaborate on the scalability of CleanBA, especially in terms of its performance and resource requirements in more complex environments?
- How do the authors address the concerns about the originality of their work, particularly in light of the significant reliance on existing frameworks?
- Would it be possible to see the results of CleanBA on a broader set of environments, such as those used in benchmarks like Mujoco, to assess its generalizability?
