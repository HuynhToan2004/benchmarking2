 **Summary:**
The paper introduces the Reflected Schrodinger Bridge algorithm, which extends the Schrodinger Bridge algorithm to bounded domains using reflected forward-backward stochastic differential equations with Neumann and Robin boundary conditions. This approach is aimed at improving the generation of data within constrained domains, such as image datasets like CIFAR-10 and ImageNet. The methodology is supported by theoretical analysis, including proofs of convergence and numerical simulations, which demonstrate the algorithm's effectiveness in generating high-quality images. However, the paper's presentation and the clarity of its contributions are criticized, with concerns about the novelty and the practical utility of the approach compared to existing methods.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written and easy to follow, with clear explanations of the main ideas and theoretical results.
- The proposed method is novel and technically sound, extending the Schrodinger Bridge algorithm to bounded domains, which is a significant contribution to the field.
- The numerical results are convincing, demonstrating the effectiveness of the proposed method in generating high-quality images.
- The paper provides a comprehensive theoretical analysis, including proofs of convergence, which supports the robustness and reliability of the proposed method.
- The use of reflected SDEs for constrained generative modeling is a promising approach that could potentially enhance the performance of generative models in various applications.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a clear motivation for the use of the Schrodinger Bridge algorithm in the context of constrained generative modeling, and it's unclear how the proposed method improves upon existing methods like reflected diffusion models.
- The paper's theoretical results, such as the convergence of the algorithm, are not clearly explained or justified, and the assumptions used in the proofs are not well-explained or justified.
- There is a lack of discussion on the limitations of the proposed method, which could help in understanding its practical applicability and potential drawbacks.
- The paper does not adequately compare the proposed method with existing methods in terms of computational efficiency, scalability, or other practical metrics, which could affect the perceived utility of the method.
- The paper could benefit from a more thorough discussion of related work and a clearer presentation of the algorithm's contributions and its implications for the broader field of generative modeling.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors clarify the motivation behind using the Schrodinger Bridge algorithm in constrained generative modeling and how it improves upon existing methods?
- How does the proposed method handle the boundary conditions in practice, and what are the implications of these conditions on the generated images?
- Could the authors provide more details on the computational efficiency and scalability of the proposed method compared to existing methods?
- What are the practical applications of the proposed method, and how does it compare to other methods in terms of performance and efficiency in these applications?
- Can the authors elaborate on the theoretical assumptions used in the proofs and how they impact the practical applicability of the method?
- How does the proposed method compare to other methods in terms of the quality and diversity of the generated images, particularly in complex or high-dimensional datasets?
