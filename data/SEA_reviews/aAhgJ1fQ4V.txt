 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel dataset of over one million multi-resolution cloth simulations, leveraging the Progressive Cloth Simulation (PCS) method to generate high-fidelity, self-consistent cloth drapes across various resolutions. The dataset is designed to address the lack of high-quality, multi-resolution cloth data for training physics-based upsampling methods. It includes a variety of cloth materials, shapes, and colliders, and is evaluated using a new set of geometric and physical metrics. The paper also presents a learning-based baseline method for cloth upsampling and conducts experiments to demonstrate the effectiveness of the dataset in advancing research in this area.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a large-scale dataset of over one million cloth simulations, which is significant for advancing research in cloth simulation and upsampling.
- The dataset is diverse, covering a wide range of cloth materials, shapes, and colliders, which can be used to train data-driven methods for cloth upsampling.
- The paper presents a new set of geometric and physical evaluation metrics, which are useful for assessing the quality of cloth simulations and upsampling methods.
- The dataset is designed to be self-consistent across multiple resolutions, which is crucial for training methods that require consistent cloth geometries at different resolutions.
- The paper includes a learning-based baseline method for cloth upsampling, which is evaluated on the dataset and shown to outperform existing methods.
- The dataset is well-documented, including detailed descriptions of the simulation parameters and material properties, which can aid in the development of new methods for cloth upsampling.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper primarily focuses on using the PCS method for cloth simulation, which may limit the diversity of cloth behaviors that can be captured.
- The evaluation metrics used in the paper are not clearly defined or explained, which could limit the understanding and reproducibility of the results.
- The paper lacks a comprehensive discussion on the limitations of the dataset, such as the absence of real-world data or the lack of support for dynamic cloth simulations.
- The paper does not provide a detailed comparison with other existing cloth datasets, which could help in understanding the advantages and limitations of the proposed dataset.
- The paper does not discuss the potential impact of the dataset on the development of data-driven methods for cloth upsampling, which could be a significant contribution of the work.
- The paper does not include a detailed discussion on the limitations of the dataset, which could help in understanding the scope and applicability of the work.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors clarify the definition and calculation of the evaluation metrics used in the paper, particularly how they relate to the physical properties of the cloth?
- How does the proposed learning-based baseline method compare to other data-driven methods for cloth upsampling, such as those using GANs or other generative models?
- Could the authors provide more details on the training and evaluation of the learning-based baseline method, including the network architecture and training parameters?
- How does the dataset handle variations in material properties and initial conditions, and what are the implications for the simulation results?
- Could the authors discuss the potential impact of the dataset on the development of data-driven methods for cloth upsampling, and how this could advance the field?
- How does the proposed dataset compare to other existing cloth datasets in terms of quality, diversity, and applicability for training data-driven methods?
