 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel non-negative matrix factorization (NMF) method, termed Self-Matrix Factorization (SMF), which integrates elements of Self-Expressive Models (SEM) to address the issue of infinite solutions in traditional NMF. SMF aims to preserve subspace information, enhancing the interpretability and stability of the factorization. The method is evaluated using two datasets, showing improved performance over existing methods in certain metrics. However, the paper suffers from several critical issues, including unclear motivations for the method, insufficient experimental validation, and a lack of comparative analysis with more recent and relevant methods.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel approach to NMF by integrating elements of Self-Expressive Models (SEM), which is a significant contribution to the field.
- The method is well-explained, with clear notations and a comprehensive description of the optimization process, making it accessible to readers.
- The paper includes a thorough empirical evaluation, demonstrating the method's effectiveness through experiments on two datasets, showing improvements over existing methods in certain metrics.
- The proposed method, SMF, is shown to have better stability compared to traditional NMF methods, which is a notable advantage.

**Weaknesses:**
- The motivation behind the SMF method is not clearly articulated, particularly why preserving subspace information is crucial for NMF.
- The paper lacks a detailed comparison with more recent and relevant methods, which could have provided a clearer context for the proposed method's contributions.
- The experimental setup is limited, with only two datasets used, which may not adequately demonstrate the method's generalizability or effectiveness across different types of data.
- The paper suffers from several typographical and notation errors, which could confuse readers and undermine the paper's credibility.
- The paper's claims regarding the method's ability to outperform existing methods are not convincingly supported by the experimental results, which show only marginal improvements in some cases.
- There is a lack of discussion on the limitations of the proposed method, which is crucial for understanding its applicability and potential drawbacks.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors clarify the specific benefits of preserving subspace information in the context of NMF?
- Why was the choice made to focus on the RMSE metric in the evaluation, and how does this align with the overall goals of the study?
- How does the SMF method perform when applied to other types of data beyond those used in the experiments?
- Can the authors provide more details on the experimental setup, particularly how the datasets were prepared and how the hyperparameters were chosen?
- Could the authors elaborate on the method's stability claims and how this translates into practical benefits for users of the method?
- Why were only two datasets used in the experiments, and how do the authors justify the generalizability of the findings to other types of data?
