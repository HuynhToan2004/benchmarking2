 **Summary:**
The paper introduces Instructive Decoding (ID), a novel method aimed at enhancing the performance of instruction-tuned language models by incorporating noisy instruction variants. ID contrasts the logits from the original instruction with those from a noisy instruction, which can be randomly generated or based on opposite instructions. The methodology is supported by experiments on the SUPNATINST dataset, demonstrating improvements in model performance, particularly in zero-shot scenarios. The paper also discusses the use of different types of noisy instructions, including trunc-shuf, null, rand words, and opposite, and their impact on model performance. The proposed ID method is simple, effective, and does not require additional parameter updates, making it a practical solution for improving the performance of instruction-tuned models.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel method, Instructive Decoding (ID), which enhances the performance of instruction-tuned language models by utilizing noisy instruction variants.
- ID does not require additional parameter updates, making it a practical and efficient solution for improving model performance.
- The paper provides a comprehensive analysis of the behavior of ID, demonstrating its efficacy from various perspectives.
- The proposed ID method is simple and effective, and the experiments conducted on the SUPNATINST dataset demonstrate its effectiveness in zero-shot scenarios.
- The paper is well-written, easy to follow, and provides a clear explanation of the methodology and results.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper primarily focuses on the SUPNATINST dataset, which might limit the generalizability of the findings to other datasets.
- The methodology section is somewhat unclear, particularly in the description of how the noisy instructions are generated and how they are used in the decoding process.
- The paper does not sufficiently discuss the limitations of the proposed method, particularly in terms of its applicability to different types of tasks and models.
- The paper could benefit from a more detailed discussion on the choice of hyperparameters and their impact on the performance of the model.
- The paper does not adequately compare the proposed method with other existing methods, such as Contrastive Decoding, which could have provided a more robust evaluation of the proposed approach.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors provide more detailed examples or case studies to illustrate how the noisy instructions are generated and how they are used in the decoding process?
- How does the proposed ID method perform in few-shot scenarios, and what are the implications for its generalizability to other types of tasks and models?
- What are the specific hyperparameters used in the ID method, and how were they chosen? How sensitive is the method to changes in these parameters?
- How does the ID method compare with other methods, such as Contrastive Decoding, and what are the key differences in terms of effectiveness and efficiency?
- Could the authors provide more detailed results on the performance of the ID method across different types of tasks and models, particularly those not included in the current study?
