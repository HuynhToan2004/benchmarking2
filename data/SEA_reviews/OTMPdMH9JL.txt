 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel method for representation learning using Neural Eigenmaps, which leverage NeuralEF to model eigenfunctions in a parametric manner. This approach allows for the efficient learning of eigenfunctions, which are then used to extract structured representations from data. The method is demonstrated to be effective in various tasks, including image retrieval and graph representation learning. The paper provides theoretical insights into the learning of eigenfunctions from indefinite kernels and empirical evidence showing the method's superiority over existing methods. However, the paper is critiqued for its lack of clarity in certain sections, particularly the connection between NeuralEF and Neural Eigenmaps, and the absence of a detailed discussion on the scalability and computational complexity of the proposed method.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel method for representation learning using Neural Eigenmaps, which is a significant advancement in the field.
- The method is well-motivated, with a clear theoretical framework and empirical evidence that supports its effectiveness.
- The paper is well-written, making it accessible and easy to follow, which is crucial for a broad audience.
- The experiments conducted are thorough, demonstrating the method's effectiveness across various tasks, including image retrieval and graph representation learning.
- The paper provides a detailed analysis of the learned eigenfunctions and their utility in different applications, which adds depth to the understanding of the method.
- The method is scalable, which is a critical aspect for applications in large-scale datasets.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks clarity in certain sections, particularly the connection between NeuralEF and Neural Eigenmaps, which could be improved with more detailed explanations or figures.
- The scalability of the method is not adequately addressed, with concerns about the computational complexity and scalability to larger datasets.
- The paper does not discuss the limitations of the method, which is essential for a comprehensive understanding of its applicability and potential drawbacks.
- The experiments could be more comprehensive, especially in terms of the number of baselines and the variety of datasets used.
- The paper could benefit from a more detailed discussion on the scalability and computational complexity of the proposed method, particularly in comparison to other methods.

**Questions:**
- Could you provide more details on how the Neural Eigenmaps are trained, especially the role of the encoder in the training process?
- How does the method perform when the number of positive eigenvalues is less than expected?
- What is the computational complexity of the proposed method, and how does it scale with the size of the dataset?
- Can you discuss the limitations of the method and provide examples or scenarios where it might not perform well?
- How does the method compare in terms of scalability and computational efficiency to other state-of-the-art methods?
- Could you provide more details on the scalability of the method to larger datasets, and what are the practical implications of this scalability?
