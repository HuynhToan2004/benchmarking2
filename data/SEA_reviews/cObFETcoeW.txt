 **Summary:**
The paper addresses the challenges in evaluating the performance of saliency-based representation visualization (SRV) methods, which are crucial for explainable AI (XAI). It critiques the existing backdoor-based evaluation methods, highlighting their limitations and unreliability due to trigger generalization. The authors propose a generalization-limited backdoor watermark (GLBW) to enhance the reliability of SRV evaluations. This method is designed to minimize the trigger generalization, thus improving the accuracy and robustness of SRV evaluations. Extensive experiments on benchmark datasets demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed method.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, making it easy to follow and understand.
- The motivation behind the study is clear, and the experiments are comprehensive, demonstrating the effectiveness of the proposed method.
- The paper introduces a novel approach to evaluating saliency-based representation visualization (SRV) methods, which is a significant contribution to the field of Explainable AI (XAI).
- The authors address the limitations of existing evaluation methods and provide a new method that enhances the reliability of SRV evaluations.
- The paper provides a clear explanation of the challenges in evaluating SRV methods and proposes a new method to address these challenges.
- The proposed method is simple and effective, and the experiments are well-designed to demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed method.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed discussion on the limitations of the proposed method, which could help readers understand the scope and applicability of the findings.
- The paper could benefit from a more thorough discussion on the implications of the results, particularly how they might be applied in real-world scenarios.
- The paper does not sufficiently differentiate its approach from existing methods, particularly in terms of the novelty of the proposed backdoor watermark.
- The paper's evaluation is limited to specific datasets and does not include a broader range of models or datasets, which could limit the generalizability of the findings.
- The paper does not discuss the potential negative societal impacts of the proposed method, which could be a significant concern for some readers.
- The paper could benefit from a more detailed discussion on the computational complexity and scalability of the proposed method.
