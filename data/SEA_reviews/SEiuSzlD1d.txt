 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel self-supervised pretraining method for NeRF models, termed Masked Ray and View Modeling (MRVM), which aims to enhance the generalization of NeRFs by predicting scene representations from masked features. This method leverages masking at both the ray and view levels to encourage information interactions, improving the model's ability to generalize across diverse scenes. Extensive experiments on both synthetic and real-world datasets demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed method, showing improvements in generalization and detail preservation. However, the paper lacks a detailed discussion on the limitations and potential negative societal impacts of the proposed method.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, clear, and easy to follow, making it accessible to a broad audience.
- The introduction of mask-based pretraining into NeRFs is novel and innovative, showing promising results in improving the generalization and detail preservation of NeRFs.
- The experiments are extensive, covering various datasets and showing consistent improvements over baseline models.
- The paper includes an ablation study that demonstrates the impact of different masking strategies and masking ratios on the performance of the proposed method.
- The method is simple yet effective, making it potentially applicable to a wide range of NeRF-based methods.
- The paper is well-organized, with clear figures and tables that aid in understanding the proposed method and its results.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed discussion on the limitations of the proposed method, which could help in understanding its scope and applicability.
- The paper does not include a discussion on the potential negative societal impacts of the proposed method, which is an important consideration for ethical AI research.
- The paper does not compare the proposed method with other generalizable NeRF methods, which could provide a more comprehensive evaluation of its effectiveness.
- The novelty of the method is questioned, as similar masking strategies have been used in other works, such as VisionNeRF.
- The paper could benefit from a more detailed analysis of the impact of different masking ratios on the performance of the method.
- The paper could include more qualitative results, such as video demonstrations, to better showcase the improvements in detail preservation and generalization.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors provide a detailed discussion on the limitations of the proposed method, including its applicability and potential negative impacts?
- How does the proposed method compare with other generalizable NeRF methods, especially in terms of generalization and detail preservation?
- What are the specific advantages of the proposed method over other generalizable NeRF methods, and how does it address the limitations of existing methods?
- Can the authors provide more qualitative results, such as video demonstrations, to better showcase the improvements in detail preservation and generalization?
- How does the proposed method perform in scenarios where the number of reference views is limited, and what are the potential implications for the generalization of the method?
- Could the authors clarify the impact of different masking ratios on the performance of the method, and provide more detailed results on this aspect?
