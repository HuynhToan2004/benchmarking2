 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel momentum-based diffusion process aimed at accelerating the training and sampling in diffusion models. By integrating momentum SGD into the diffusion process, the authors propose a new method that enhances the convergence speed and reduces training and sampling steps. Theoretical analysis and empirical results demonstrate the effectiveness of this approach, showing improvements in training efficiency and sampling quality across various diffusion models like VP, VE, and EDM. The paper also discusses the application of this method to different datasets, including CIFAR-10, AFHQ, and FFHQ, and provides code for reproducibility.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel momentum-based diffusion process that accelerates the training and sampling in diffusion models, which is a significant contribution to the field.
- The theoretical analysis provided in the paper is robust, offering insights into the convergence speed of the momentum-based diffusion process.
- The empirical results show that the proposed method outperforms existing methods like CLD, demonstrating its effectiveness in practical applications.
- The paper is well-written, making it easy to follow and understand, which is crucial for its accessibility to a broad audience.
- The methodology is straightforward and can be easily integrated into existing diffusion models, enhancing their performance and efficiency.
- The paper includes a comprehensive evaluation and ablation studies, providing valuable insights into the performance of the proposed method under different conditions.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed comparison with other momentum-based methods, which could provide a more comprehensive understanding of the proposed method's advantages and limitations.
- The theoretical analysis, particularly Theorem 2, is not clearly explained, and its practical implications are not well-defined, which could limit the understanding of the theoretical contributions.
- The experimental results, while showing improvements, do not consistently outperform other methods like CLD across all datasets, raising questions about the generalizability of the proposed method.
- The paper could benefit from a more detailed discussion on the computational complexity and memory requirements of the proposed method, especially in comparison to existing methods.
- There are some typographical errors and inconsistencies in the presentation of equations, which could detract from the paper's professional quality.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors clarify the practical implications of Theorem 2 and provide a more detailed explanation of how it contributes to the theoretical understanding of the proposed method?
- How does the proposed method compare to other momentum-based methods, particularly in terms of computational complexity and memory requirements?
- Can the authors provide a more detailed comparison of the training and inference times between the proposed method and other methods like CLD, especially under similar training conditions?
- How does the proposed method perform when integrated with different diffusion models, and what are the specific benefits of using it with each model?
- Can the authors explain the choice of parameters in the experiments, particularly the NFE used in different datasets, and how this affects the comparison with other methods?
