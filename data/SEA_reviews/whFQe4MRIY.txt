 **Summary:**
The paper introduces MI-NeRF, a novel method for learning a single dynamic NeRF from monocular talking face videos of multiple identities. MI-NeRF employs a multiplicative module to learn non-linear interactions between identity and non-identity specific information, which significantly reduces training time compared to traditional single-identity NeRFs. The method can personalize facial expressions and achieve state-of-the-art performance for facial expression transfer and talking face video synthesis. MI-NeRF's ability to model complex non-rigid facial motion for multiple identities using only monocular videos of arbitrary length makes it a significant contribution to the field. The paper also includes extensive experiments and ablation studies, demonstrating the method's effectiveness and robustness.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel method for learning a single dynamic NeRF from monocular talking face videos of multiple identities, which is a significant contribution to the field.
- The use of a multiplicative module to learn non-linear interactions between identity and non-identity specific information is innovative and effective, reducing training time and improving performance.
- The method can be further personalized for a target identity, achieving state-of-the-art performance for facial expression transfer and talking face video synthesis.
- The paper is well-written, with clear explanations and extensive experiments and ablation studies that demonstrate the effectiveness and robustness of the proposed method.
- The proposed method significantly reduces training time compared to traditional single-identity NeRFs, making it more efficient and practical for real-world applications.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper does not compare the proposed method with other state-of-the-art methods such as AD-NeRF, LipNeRF, and GeneFace, which limits the evaluation of its effectiveness and efficiency.
- The method requires a 3DMM to extract head pose and expression parameters, which might not be practical for real-time applications.
- The performance of the method heavily depends on the quality of the 3DMM, which could limit its generalizability and applicability.
- The method's ability to handle complex facial expressions and identities with large variations, such as ethnicity or gender, is not thoroughly evaluated.
- The paper lacks a detailed analysis of the limitations of the proposed method, which is crucial for understanding its applicability and scope.
- The method's reliance on a single identity code per video might not be sufficient to capture all variations in identity, which could affect the accuracy and realism of the synthesized videos.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors provide more details on the training time and the number of identities used in the experiments?
- How does the method handle the extraction of head pose and expression parameters, especially in real-time applications?
- Can the authors elaborate on the limitations of the proposed method and how these might affect its practical application?
- How does the method perform when handling identities with large variations, such as ethnicity or gender?
- Could the authors provide more insights into the role of the latent code in the method's performance and how it affects the disentanglement of identity and expression?
- How does the method compare with other state-of-the-art methods in terms of performance and efficiency, especially in scenarios where the identity of the target subject is not known in advance?
