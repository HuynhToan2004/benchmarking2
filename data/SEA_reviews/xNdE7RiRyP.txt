 **Summary:**
The paper introduces TinyTrain, a novel on-device training method designed for resource-constrained IoT devices. TinyTrain employs a task-adaptive sparse-update method that dynamically selects layers and channels to update based on a multi-objective criterion, aiming to reduce training time and memory usage. It also incorporates a few-shot learning pre-training scheme to adapt to new tasks with limited data. The method is evaluated against various baselines, demonstrating improved accuracy, reduced memory consumption, and faster training times. However, concerns are raised about the novelty of the dynamic layer/channel selection and the generalizability of the method to different model architectures and datasets.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, clear, and easy to follow, with a clear presentation of the methodology and results.
- The proposed TinyTrain method is innovative, combining few-shot learning pre-training with dynamic layer/channel selection, which is more efficient than static selection methods.
- The experiments are comprehensive, covering various baselines and datasets, and the results demonstrate the effectiveness of TinyTrain in terms of accuracy, memory usage, and training time.
- The method is well-motivated, addressing the challenges of on-device training in resource-constrained IoT devices, and the use of a multi-objective criterion for layer/channel selection is novel and effective.
- The paper includes a thorough analysis of the trade-offs between accuracy gain, compute, and memory cost, which is crucial for understanding the efficiency of the proposed method.

**Weaknesses:**
- The novelty of the dynamic layer/channel selection is questionable as similar approaches have been used in other works, such as TinyTL and SparseUpdate.
- The method's generalizability is limited, as it has only been tested on CNN architectures and specific datasets. It is unclear how it would perform with other architectures like Transformers or with different types of data.
- The paper lacks a detailed discussion on the limitations of the proposed method, which could help in understanding its applicability and potential drawbacks.
- The comparison with baselines is not always fair, as some baselines (e.g., LastLayer) do not use pre-training, which could affect the perceived performance of TinyTrain.
- The paper could benefit from a more detailed analysis of the computational and memory costs of meta-training, which is a significant component of the proposed method.

**Questions:**
- How does the proposed method perform with other model architectures, such as Transformers, and with different types of data?
- Can the authors provide more details on the computational and memory costs of meta-training, and how these costs compare to the benefits of using meta-training?
- How does the proposed method handle the trade-offs between accuracy gain and other costs, such as memory and compute?
- Could the authors clarify the novelty of the dynamic layer/channel selection and discuss how it compares to similar approaches in existing literature?
- How does the method perform when applied to different datasets, and what are the implications of these results for the generalizability of the findings?
