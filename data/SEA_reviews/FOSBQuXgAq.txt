 **Summary:**
The paper investigates the complexity of Bayesian neural network (BNN) posteriors by focusing on the effects of weight symmetries, particularly permutation and scaling symmetries, on the posterior distribution. It introduces a mathematical framework to analyze the impact of these symmetries on the posterior and proposes a method to assess the quality of the posterior distribution. The paper also presents a dataset of trained models to aid in further research and provides empirical evidence of the effects of these symmetries on the posterior distribution. The authors explore how these symmetries affect the diversity of the posterior distribution and the accuracy of uncertainty quantification. The paper is well-written and provides a comprehensive review of related literature, although it lacks detailed discussions on some theoretical aspects and experimental setups.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel mathematical framework for analyzing the effects of weight symmetries on the posterior distribution of Bayesian neural networks (BNNs), which is a significant contribution to the field.
- The authors provide a detailed discussion on the impact of scaling and permutation symmetries on the posterior distribution, which is both theoretically sound and empirically validated.
- The paper is well-written, with clear figures and a comprehensive literature review, making it accessible to a broad audience.
- The proposed dataset of trained models could be a valuable resource for the community, facilitating further research into the complexities of BNN posteriors.
- The paper addresses a crucial issue in the field of BNNs by focusing on the impact of weight symmetries, which is an area that has not been extensively explored before.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed discussion on the impact of the choice of prior on the results, which could be a significant limitation given the sensitivity of BNNs to the choice of prior.
- The experimental setup and the choice of metrics used in the paper are not adequately justified, which could limit the generalizability of the findings.
- The paper does not provide a detailed discussion on the theoretical aspects of the proposed framework, such as the proofs and the assumptions made, which could affect the credibility of the results.
- The paper's reliance on a large number of checkpoints for estimating the posterior distribution might be seen as a significant computational expense, which could limit the practical applicability of the findings.
- There are several minor typographical and formatting errors that need to be corrected to enhance the clarity and professionalism of the paper.
