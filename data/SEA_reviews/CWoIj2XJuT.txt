 **Summary:**
The paper addresses the Schrödinger Bridge problem in unbalanced settings, extending the existing diffusion Schrödinger bridge framework to accommodate non-probability measures with mass imbalances. It introduces time-reversal formulas for stochastic differential equations with killing and birth terms, which are crucial for modeling population dynamics in biological and medical applications. The paper proposes a novel algorithm for training unbalanced diffusion Schrödinger bridges and demonstrates its effectiveness through experiments involving cellular responses to cancer drugs and simulating the emergence and dissemination of novel viral variants. Despite its theoretical contributions and potential practical applications, the paper is criticized for its presentation clarity and the limited scope of its experiments.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written and provides a clear introduction to the topic, with a good balance between theory and practical applications.
- The authors address a significant problem in the field of stochastic processes, particularly in the context of unbalanced Schrödinger bridges, which is a novel approach to modeling population dynamics with mass imbalances.
- The paper includes rigorous theoretical results, such as Proposition 3.1, which is a novel contribution to the field.
- The experiments are well-designed and demonstrate the practical utility of the proposed methods, particularly in modeling cellular responses to cancer drugs and simulating virus spread.
- The paper is well-motivated and presents a novel approach to solving the Schrödinger bridge problem with unbalanced initial and final measures, which is a significant contribution to the field.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper suffers from some presentation issues, particularly in the clarity of the text and the notation used. This makes it difficult for readers to follow the theoretical contributions and the experimental results.
- The paper lacks a comprehensive literature review, which could have positioned the work more effectively within the existing body of knowledge.
- The experiments are limited in scope, focusing primarily on synthetic and small-scale datasets. This limits the generalizability of the results and the ability to evaluate the scalability of the proposed methods.
- The paper could benefit from a more detailed discussion of related works, especially those that address similar problems or use similar methodologies.
- The paper could be improved by including more detailed explanations of the theoretical results and the experimental setups, as well as more extensive numerical results to support the claims made.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors provide a more detailed explanation of the theoretical results, particularly Proposition 3.1 and the implications of the time-reversal formulas for stochastic differential equations with killing and birth terms?
- How do the authors address the computational complexity of the proposed methods, particularly in high-dimensional or large-scale applications?
- In the experiments involving cellular dynamics, how do the authors ensure that the results are not biased by the specific datasets used?
- Could the authors clarify the role of the coffin state in the model and how it affects the predictions of the model?
- How do the authors justify the choice of using time-reversal formulas for stochastic differential equations with killing and birth terms, and what are the implications of this choice for the practical applicability of the model?
