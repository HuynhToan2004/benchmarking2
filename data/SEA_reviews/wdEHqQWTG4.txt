 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel multi-agent reinforcement learning (RL) framework, CCRRL, designed to manage a portfolio of stocks using both cooperative and competitive strategies. The framework integrates DDPG with a modification function that adjusts reward values to enhance robustness. It employs a multi-agent system where agents collaborate and compete to achieve a common goal, using real financial data from the ChiNext market. The authors claim that their approach outperforms traditional RL methods, although the novelty of the methodology and the clarity of the experimental setup are questioned.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, easy to follow, and provides a clear explanation of the methodology, making it accessible to readers.
- The authors have conducted extensive experiments using real historical stock trading data, which demonstrates the practical applicability of the proposed method.
- The integration of both cooperation and competition strategies within a multi-agent system is innovative and could potentially enhance the performance of reinforcement learning algorithms.
- The paper is well-organized, with a clear introduction of the problem, the method, and the results, which makes it easy to understand the authors' intentions and the results of their experiments.
- The proposed method is tested on real financial data, which adds credibility to the results and shows the practical applicability of the method.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a thorough review of related literature, particularly in the area of robust RL and multi-agent RL, which could have provided a richer context for the research.
- The novelty of the methodology is questioned as it seems to be a simple modification of existing algorithms like DDPG, without significant advancements in the field.
- The paper does not adequately address the scalability of the proposed method, especially concerning the number of assets and the complexity of the environment.
- The experimental setup and results are not clearly described, which makes it difficult to understand the experimental conditions and the impact of the proposed method.
- The paper does not provide a detailed comparison with existing methods, such as the FIN-RL library, which could have highlighted the advantages of the proposed method more effectively.
- The paper lacks a discussion on the limitations of the proposed method, which is crucial for understanding the scope and applicability of the findings.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors clarify how the proposed method differs from existing multi-agent RL approaches, particularly in terms of the novelty and effectiveness of the cooperation and competition strategies?
- How does the proposed method handle the scalability issues, especially in terms of the number of assets and the complexity of the environment?
- What are the specific advantages of using the proposed method over existing methods like FIN-RL, and how does it compare in terms of performance and computational efficiency?
- Can the authors provide more details on the experimental setup, such as the number of assets considered and the specific conditions under which the experiments were conducted?
- How does the modification function affect the performance of the RL algorithm, and what are the implications of using different reward functions in the CCRRL framework?
- Can the authors elaborate on the choice of the ChiNext market for testing the proposed method, and how this choice aligns with the broader applicability of the findings?
