 **Summary:**
The paper explores the interaction between human-driven vehicles (HVs) and automated vehicles (AVs) in a mixed traffic environment, focusing on the heterogeneous decision-making processes of each vehicle type. It employs a stochastic game framework to model the interactions and introduces a regret analysis to evaluate the performance of the system. The paper investigates how the bounded rationality of HVs and the planning horizon of AVs influence the overall learning performance. It also discusses the impact of different decision-making strategies on the system's regret dynamics. Despite its theoretical contributions, the paper is criticized for its lack of novelty and practical applicability, as well as its limited discussion on the assumptions and their realism in real-world scenarios.

**Strengths:**
- The paper addresses an important and timely problem of AV-HV interaction, which is crucial for the development of mixed traffic systems.
- The analysis is technically sound and well-structured, providing a clear presentation of the problem and the proposed solutions.
- The introduction of regret analysis into the study of AV-HV interaction is novel and adds depth to the understanding of the system's performance.
- The paper is well-written, making it accessible and easy to follow, which is crucial for a broad audience.
- The inclusion of theoretical results and numerical simulations provides a solid foundation for the claims made in the paper.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks novelty in its approach, as it primarily builds on existing works without introducing significant new insights or methodologies.
- The assumptions made in the paper, such as the linear dynamics model and the bounded rationality of HVs, are not well-justified or realistic in real-world scenarios.
- The paper does not adequately address the practical implications of its findings, particularly how the results could be applied in real-world scenarios.
- There is a lack of discussion on the limitations of the proposed method and the assumptions made, which could affect the generalizability and applicability of the findings.
- The paper's reliance on a stochastic game framework, which is not commonly used in the field, may limit its accessibility to a broader audience.

**Questions:**
- How does the proposed method compare to existing methods in terms of performance and computational efficiency?
- Can the authors provide more details on the assumptions made in the paper, particularly the linear dynamics model and the bounded rationality of HVs? How do these assumptions hold up in real-world scenarios?
- How can the findings of this paper be applied in practical settings to improve the interaction between AVs and HVs in mixed traffic environments?
- What are the limitations of the proposed method, and how do these limitations affect the generalizability and applicability of the findings?
- The paper mentions a diffusion process for modeling prediction error in non-linear cases. Can the authors provide more details on this process and its assumptions?
- How does the paper address the issue of Goodhart's Law in the context of AV decision-making, and what are the implications of this for the overall system performance?
