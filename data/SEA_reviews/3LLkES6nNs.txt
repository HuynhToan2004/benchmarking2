 **Summary:**
This paper explores the convergence of Neural ODEs to Gaussian Processes (GPs) in the limit of infinite width and depth. It extends previous work on Neural Tangent Kernel (NTK) to Neural Ordinary Differential Equations (Neural ODEs), providing theoretical insights into the behavior of Neural ODEs in the overparameterized regime. The paper introduces a dynamic programming algorithm for computing the NNGP kernel matrix, which is crucial for understanding the generalization and training dynamics of Neural ODEs. Theoretical results are supported by numerical experiments, showing that Neural ODEs exhibit Gaussian behaviors and converge to GPs in specific scenarios. The paper also addresses the strict positive definiteness of the NNGP kernel, which is crucial for ensuring the convergence of training processes and estimating generalization performance.

**Strengths:**
- The paper provides a novel and comprehensive analysis of Neural ODEs, extending previous results on Neural Tangent Kernel (NTK) to Neural ODEs, which is a significant advancement in the field.
- The introduction of a dynamic programming algorithm for computing the NNGP kernel matrix is a valuable contribution, enhancing the practical applicability of the theoretical results.
- The paper is well-written and structured, making the complex theoretical concepts accessible and understandable, even to readers without a deep background in the subject.
- The results presented are novel and contribute to the understanding of the behavior of Neural ODEs, providing insights into the generalization and training dynamics of these models.
- The paper effectively bridges the gap between the asymptotic behavior of Neural ODEs and the behavior of Gaussian processes, offering new insights into the behavior of Neural ODEs in the overparameterized regime.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper primarily focuses on a specific version of Neural ODEs, which may limit the generalizability of the results to more complex or varied models.
- There is a lack of discussion on the practical implications of the theoretical results, particularly in terms of how they can be applied to improve the training or design of Neural ODEs.
- The paper could benefit from a more detailed discussion on the limitations of the results, especially in scenarios where the assumptions of infinite width and depth may not hold.
- Some of the proofs and assumptions in the paper, such as the assumption of Lipschitz continuity and the use of Euler's method, are not thoroughly discussed or justified, which could limit the robustness of the results.
- The paper could benefit from a more rigorous comparison with existing results, particularly in terms of the specific conditions under which the results hold and the implications of these conditions for practical applications.

**Questions:**
- How do the results presented in this paper compare with the results from the original paper of Neural ODEs, particularly in terms of the assumptions and implications?
- Is there a specific reason why the paper focuses on a specific version of Neural ODEs, and how do the results generalize to other versions or architectures?
- Can the authors provide more detailed insights into the practical implications of the theoretical results, particularly in terms of how they can be applied to improve the training or design of Neural ODEs?
- How do the results on the strict positive definiteness of the NNGP kernel relate to the practical training of Neural ODEs, and what are the implications for the convergence and generalization of these models?
- Could the authors discuss the assumptions of Lipschitz continuity and the use of Euler's method more thoroughly, and how these assumptions affect the robustness and applicability of the results?
