 **Summary:**
The paper addresses the challenge of domain generalization with fairness considerations, focusing on scenarios where multiple sensitive attributes are present. It introduces a novel method that learns domain-invariant representations to generalize model performance across domains and selective domain-invariant representations to transfer fairness across domains. The approach is evaluated on two datasets, showing promising results in terms of both fairness and performance. However, the paper suffers from a lack of clarity in some sections, particularly in the explanation of the methodology and the use of multiple encoders. Additionally, the experimental setup and the comparison with existing methods are questioned for their comprehensiveness and fairness.

**Strengths:**
- The paper addresses a significant and relevant problem in the field of fair domain generalization, which is increasingly important as AI systems are deployed in real-world applications.
- The proposed method is novel, providing a new approach to fair domain generalization that considers multiple sensitive attributes.
- The paper is well-written, with clear descriptions of the methodology and experimental results, making it accessible and understandable.
- The experimental results demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed method, showing improvements over existing methods in terms of fairness and performance.
- The use of two encoders for domain shift and fairness is a novel approach that is well-motivated and effectively demonstrated through the experimental results.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks clarity in certain sections, particularly in the description of the methodology and the use of multiple encoders. More detailed explanations and possibly a simplified illustration would be beneficial.
- The experimental setup and comparisons with existing methods are not comprehensive enough. The paper should include more baselines and a more detailed comparison with existing methods to strengthen its claims.
- The paper does not adequately address the generalization of the proposed method to unseen sensitive attributes, which is a critical aspect of the problem it aims to solve.
- The method's complexity and the need for multiple models (two encoders and a classifier) increase the computational burden and may limit its practical applicability.
- The paper does not sufficiently discuss the societal impact of the proposed method, which is crucial for understanding the broader implications of the research.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors clarify the use of multiple encoders and the role of each in the proposed method?
- How does the proposed method generalize to unseen sensitive attributes, especially in scenarios where these attributes are not present in the training data?
- Could the authors provide a more detailed explanation of the methodology, possibly with a simplified illustration to aid understanding?
- How does the proposed method compare with existing methods, particularly in terms of computational complexity and practical applicability?
- Can the authors discuss the societal impact of their proposed method and how it addresses potential biases or inequalities in real-world applications?
