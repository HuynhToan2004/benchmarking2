 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel approach to learn latent causal factors from noisy data, aiming to infer the underlying generative process of noisy labels. The methodology involves a generative model that models the causal factors influencing both the observed data and the noisy labels. The proposed method is tested on various datasets, demonstrating its effectiveness in inferring clean labels from noisy data. The approach is supported by theoretical analysis and extensive experiments, showing improvements over existing methods. However, concerns are raised about the clarity of the methodology, the necessity of certain assumptions, and the generalizability of the findings to real-world scenarios.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel approach to learn the latent causal factors of noisy data, which is a significant advancement in the field of label noise learning.
- The methodology is supported by extensive experiments on various datasets, demonstrating the effectiveness of the proposed method in inferring clean labels from noisy data.
- The paper is well-written, making it accessible and understandable, which is crucial for academic research.
- The theoretical analysis provided in the paper adds depth to the understanding of the proposed method and its implications.
- The paper is well-motivated, with a clear focus on addressing the challenges posed by label noise in training machine learning models.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks clarity in explaining the methodology, particularly in the implementation details of the generative process and the inference of latent causal factors.
- The assumption that the causal factors influencing the observed data and noisy labels are different is not convincingly justified, and its necessity is questionable.
- The paper does not adequately address the generalizability of the findings to real-world scenarios, which is crucial for the practical application of the proposed method.
- The related work section is inadequately reviewed, missing significant recent advancements in the field, which could have provided a richer context for the research.
- The paper does not sufficiently discuss the limitations of the proposed method, which is essential for a comprehensive understanding of its applicability and potential shortcomings.
- The paper could benefit from a more detailed discussion on the computational complexity and efficiency of the proposed method, which is crucial for practical deployment.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors clarify the implementation details of the generative process and the inference of latent causal factors?
- How do the authors justify the assumption that the causal factors influencing the observed data and noisy labels are different?
- Could the authors provide more details on the generalizability of the findings to real-world scenarios?
- How does the proposed method compare to recent advancements in the field, such as the works cited in the related work section?
- Could the authors elaborate on the limitations of the proposed method and discuss potential avenues for future research to address these limitations?
- What are the computational complexities and efficiency of the proposed method, and how do these compare to existing methods?
