 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel CNN architecture, named Simple Convolutional Neural Network (SCNN), which utilizes a thin and deep design to capture spatial information effectively. SCNN employs a sequence of stacked 3x3 convolutions, enhanced by a thin and deep architecture and a new block with two 3x3 depthwise convolutions to enlarge the receptive field. The network also incorporates a Global Sigmoid Linear Unit (GSiLU) to capture global visual cues and a Global Average Pooling operation to capture global information. SCNN achieves competitive results on various vision tasks, including ImageNet-1K image classification, COCO object detection, and ADE20K semantic segmentation, demonstrating its potential as a viable alternative to Vision Transformers (ViTs).

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, making it easy to follow, and the proposed SCNN architecture is simple yet effective, with a focus on capturing spatial information effectively.
- SCNN achieves competitive results on various vision tasks, including ImageNet-1K image classification, COCO object detection, and ADE20K semantic segmentation, demonstrating its potential as a viable alternative to ViTs.
- The paper includes comprehensive experimental results, including comparisons with state-of-the-art methods, and provides detailed ablation studies to demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed components.
- The introduction of a thin and deep architecture and a new block with two 3x3 depthwise convolutions to enlarge the receptive field is a significant contribution to the field.
- The paper's motivation is clear, and the proposed GSiLU activation function is a novel contribution that could potentially improve the performance of CNNs.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a thorough discussion on the computational complexity of the proposed method, particularly in terms of the number of parameters and FLOPs.
- The novelty of the proposed method is questionable, as similar ideas have been previously explored in other works, such as the use of depthwise convolutions and global average pooling.
- The paper could benefit from a more detailed analysis of the receptive field of the proposed architecture and a comparison with other CNN architectures.
- The paper does not adequately discuss the limitations of the proposed method, particularly in terms of its applicability to different vision tasks and its potential impact on training and inference times.
- The paper lacks a detailed discussion on the training and inference times of the proposed method, which is crucial for evaluating its practicality and efficiency.
