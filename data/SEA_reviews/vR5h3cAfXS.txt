 **Summary:**
The paper investigates the phenomenon of robust overfitting in adversarial training, focusing on the correlation between the generalization difficulty of perturbation-induced distributions and robust overfitting. The authors propose a novel upper bound for generalization error with respect to these distributions, emphasizing the role of the perturbation operator in determining generalization performance. Through empirical experiments and theoretical analysis, the paper provides insights into the dynamics of adversarial training, suggesting that the trajectory of adversarial training plays a crucial role in robust overfitting. However, the paper is criticized for its lack of novelty and insufficient theoretical support for its claims.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, clear, and easy to follow, with a good flow from the introduction to the conclusion.
- The authors provide a novel upper bound for generalization error with respect to perturbation-induced distributions, which is an important contribution to the field.
- The paper presents a clear and concise summary of the experiments, effectively communicating the results and their implications.
- The experimental results are well-presented, with clear figures and a logical organization that facilitates understanding.
- The paper includes a comprehensive review of related work, which is useful for readers seeking a broad understanding of the field.
- The authors explore the dynamics of adversarial training, providing a novel perspective on robust overfitting.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks novelty as it primarily reiterates existing theories about robust overfitting and does not introduce significant new insights or approaches.
- The theoretical analysis is not robust enough, with some claims, such as those regarding the local dispersion of perturbations, not being adequately supported.
- The paper does not sufficiently differentiate its work from prior studies, particularly those that have already explored similar topics.
- There are several typographical and grammatical errors that detract from the professional quality of the paper.
- The paper could benefit from a more comprehensive literature review, especially regarding recent advancements in adversarial training and robust generalization.
- The paper's claims about the dynamics of adversarial training and the role of perturbation in generalization performance are not convincingly supported by the evidence provided.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors clarify the relationship between the perturbation operator and the generalization performance, particularly how the operator's properties influence the generalization error?
- How does the paper's theoretical analysis support the claim that the trajectory of adversarial training plays a crucial role in robust overfitting?
- Can the authors provide more detailed experimental results, especially regarding the dynamics of adversarial training and the impact of perturbation on generalization performance?
- How does the paper's approach differ from existing methods for improving robust generalization, such as those mentioned in related works?
- Could the authors elaborate on the implications of their findings for the development of more effective adversarial training strategies?
