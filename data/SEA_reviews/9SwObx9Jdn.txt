 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel reinforcement learning approach for generating geodesics on manifolds using a midpoint tree framework. The method involves training an actor-critic model to predict midpoints between two points, which are then used to recursively generate waypoints. Theoretical proofs and empirical evaluations demonstrate the effectiveness of this method, showing that it outperforms existing methods in certain path planning tasks. The paper also discusses the limitations and potential extensions of the proposed method, suggesting its applicability in various real-world scenarios.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, clear, and easy to follow, with a clear motivation and well-structured presentation.
- The proposed method is novel and interesting, combining the advantages of sub-goal trees with the ability to predict midpoints, which is a new approach.
- The theoretical proofs provided are solid, and the empirical results demonstrate the effectiveness of the method in practical scenarios.
- The paper includes a detailed analysis of the convergence of the proposed method and its theoretical guarantees, which is a significant contribution to the field.
- The midpoint tree framework is a novel approach that addresses the issue of sparse rewards in reinforcement learning, and the method's ability to generate geodesics on manifolds is a valuable contribution to the field.
