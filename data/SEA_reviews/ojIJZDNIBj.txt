 **Summary:**
The paper introduces CopulaCPTS, a novel conformal prediction method for multivariate time series forecasting. This approach leverages copulas to model the dependence between time series forecasts, providing finite sample guarantees for coverage and efficiency. The method is evaluated on both synthetic and real-world datasets, demonstrating its ability to produce sharper and more calibrated uncertainty estimates compared to existing methods. Despite its theoretical contributions and empirical validations, concerns were raised about the practical applicability of the method, particularly in scenarios with limited calibration data, and the clarity of the experimental setup and results presentation.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, clear, and easy to follow, with a clear motivation and a clear problem definition.
- The methodology is novel, using copulas to model dependencies between time series forecasts, which is a significant contribution to the field of conformal prediction.
- The paper provides a comprehensive set of experiments, both on synthetic and real-world datasets, demonstrating the effectiveness of the proposed method.
- Theoretical contributions include a finite sample validity guarantee, which is a significant advancement in the field.
- The paper is well-structured, with clear explanations of the methodology and results, which enhances its readability and accessibility.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper does not adequately address the issue of limited calibration data, which could significantly impact the performance of the proposed method.
- The methodology relies on the assumption of i.i.d. data, which may not be applicable in many real-world scenarios.
- There is a lack of clarity in the experimental setup, particularly in the description of the datasets used and the details of the experiments.
- The paper does not sufficiently discuss the limitations of the proposed method, which could help in understanding its practical applicability and potential shortcomings.
- The presentation of results, particularly in figures and tables, could be improved for better clarity and understanding.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors clarify how the methodology handles the issue of limited calibration data, and what steps can be taken to mitigate its impact?
- How does the proposed method compare to other existing methods for time series forecasting, particularly in terms of computational efficiency and accuracy?
- Can the authors provide more details on the experimental setup, such as the specifics of the datasets used and the conditions under which the experiments were conducted?
- How does the methodology ensure that the assumptions of i.i.d. data and exchangeability are met in real-world applications?
- Could the authors discuss the potential limitations of the proposed method and suggest areas for future research to address these limitations?
