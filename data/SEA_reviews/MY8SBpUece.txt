 **Summary:**
The paper investigates the feature learning process in a two-layer neural network under a single step of gradient descent, focusing on the role of the learning rate that grows with the sample size. The authors demonstrate that this setup allows for the emergence of multiple rank-one components in the feature matrix, each corresponding to a specific polynomial feature. These components are shown to improve the learning process by reducing the loss, as evidenced by theoretical analysis and numerical simulations. The paper also discusses the implications of these findings for understanding the feature learning capabilities of neural networks and the impact of different learning rates on the network's performance.

**Strengths:**
- The paper addresses an important and interesting problem, specifically the feature learning in two-layer neural networks after one gradient update, which is a significant contribution to the field.
- The authors provide a rigorous mathematical analysis of the learning process, which is a strong point of the paper.
- The paper is well-written, clear, and easy to follow, with a clear presentation of the results and theoretical analysis.
- The results are novel and provide a detailed analysis of the feature learning process, which is a crucial aspect of deep learning.
- The paper includes numerical simulations that support the theoretical findings, enhancing the credibility of the results.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper could benefit from a more detailed discussion of the implications of the findings for practical applications, such as in training neural networks.
- The assumptions made in the paper, particularly regarding the activation function and the initial conditions of the weights, are not clearly justified or explained.
- The paper relies heavily on specific conditions for the learning rate and the number of steps, which might limit the general applicability of the results.
- The paper could be more accessible to a broader audience by including a more detailed introduction and explanation of the main results.
- The paper lacks a discussion on the limitations of the results, which could help in understanding the scope and applicability of the findings.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors elaborate on the practical implications of the findings, particularly how they might be applied in training neural networks?
- How do the results depend on the specific assumptions made in the paper, such as the Hermite expansion of the activation function?
- Can the authors clarify the role and impact of the regularization parameter λ in the learning process?
- In the numerical simulations, how do the authors ensure that the results are not influenced by the specific initialization conditions used?
- Could the authors discuss the relationship of their work to other studies, such as those mentioned in the related works section, to provide a more comprehensive view of the field?
