 **Summary:**
The paper introduces ChatKBQA, a novel generate-then-retrieve KBQA framework that leverages fine-tuned large language models (LLMs) such as Llama-2, ChatGLM2, and Baichuan2 to generate logical forms and retrieve entities and relations from knowledge graphs. This approach simplifies the KBQA process by first generating logical forms and then retrieving relevant entities and relations, which is claimed to improve efficiency and accuracy. The framework is evaluated on benchmarks like WebQSP and CWQ, demonstrating state-of-the-art performance. However, concerns were raised about the novelty of the approach, the fairness of comparisons with retrieval-then-generate methods, and the lack of detailed analysis on the retrieval efficiency and the impact of different LLMs and retrieval methods.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, making it easy to understand and follow.
- The proposed framework is flexible and can be integrated with various LLMs and semantic retrieval models, enhancing its applicability.
- The framework achieves state-of-the-art performance on standard KBQA datasets, demonstrating its effectiveness.
- The experiments are well-designed, and the results are convincing, showing that the framework outperforms previous methods.
- The paper provides a detailed analysis of the impact of different LLMs and retrieval methods on performance, which is valuable for understanding the framework's effectiveness.

**Weaknesses:**
- The novelty of the approach is limited as the idea of using LLMs for KBQA is not new, and the framework primarily combines existing methods without significant innovation.
- The paper does not sufficiently compare its method with retrieve-then-generate methods, which might be more efficient or effective in certain scenarios.
- There is a lack of detailed analysis on the efficiency of retrieval, particularly how it compares to traditional methods and how it impacts the overall performance of the framework.
- The paper does not adequately address the limitations of using LLMs for KBQA, such as the potential for incorrect retrieval results and the impact on semantic parsing.
- The paper lacks a comprehensive discussion on the limitations and potential negative societal impacts of the proposed framework.
- The experimental setup could be improved, particularly in terms of the fairness of comparisons and the choice of baselines.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors provide a detailed comparison of the efficiency of their method with traditional retrieve-then-generate methods?
- How does the framework handle scenarios where the retrieved entities and relations are incorrect, and what are the implications for semantic parsing?
- Can the authors clarify the role of LLMs in the retrieval phase and how this affects the overall performance of the framework?
- How does the framework perform when compared to other state-of-the-art methods, particularly those that do not use LLMs for KBQA?
- Could the authors provide more detailed experimental results, including error rates and the impact of different LLMs and retrieval methods on performance?
- How does the framework handle scenarios where the KB is not well-structured or incomplete, and what are the implications for the accuracy and completeness of the answers generated?
