 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a method for automatically generating high-quality content-grounded data for long-form question answering (LFQA) and summarization tasks. The method consists of three stages: content preparation, generation, and filtering. The authors claim that their approach can generate data of high quality, which is on par with or outperforms data generated by humans. The methodology is evaluated on several datasets, including ASQA and ELI5, and the results show that models trained on the synthetic data perform comparably or better than those trained on human-generated data. The paper also discusses the potential of this method in other content-grounded tasks and domains, such as medical QA.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, easy to follow, and provides a clear description of the proposed method.
- The method is evaluated on several datasets and tasks, demonstrating its effectiveness and versatility.
- The authors have conducted extensive experiments, including human evaluations, which validate the method's effectiveness and its ability to generate high-quality content-grounded data.
- The proposed method is flexible and can be applied to various domains and tasks, making it a valuable contribution to the field.
- The paper discusses the potential of the method in other content-grounded tasks and domains, such as medical QA, which could be of significant interest to the community.

**Weaknesses:**
- The methodology is not entirely novel, as similar approaches have been previously explored, and the paper does not sufficiently differentiate its approach from existing methods.
- The evaluation metrics used are limited to lexical similarity and semantic similarity, which may not fully capture the quality of the generated data.
- The paper lacks a detailed discussion on how the method compares to other existing methods, particularly in terms of the effectiveness of the filtering process and the overall quality of the generated data.
- There is a lack of diversity in the datasets used for evaluation, which limits the generalizability of the findings.
- The paper does not discuss the potential negative societal impacts of the proposed method, which is a significant omission in ethical considerations.

**Questions:**
- How does the proposed method compare to other existing methods in terms of the effectiveness of the filtering process and the overall quality of the generated data?
- Can the authors provide more details on the crawling process, particularly how they ensure the quality of the data extracted from web pages?
- How does the method handle the potential for overfitting, especially when training models on synthetic data?
- Could the authors discuss the potential negative societal impacts of the proposed method and how these might be mitigated?
- What are the specific details of the human evaluation, including the number of participants, their qualifications, and the evaluation protocols used?
- How does the method perform when applied to other content-grounded tasks or domains, such as medical QA, and what are the expected outcomes?
