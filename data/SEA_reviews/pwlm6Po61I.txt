 **Summary:**
The paper explores the potential of Large Language Models (LLMs) in processing and understanding visual content by converting images into Scalable Vector Graphics (SVG) format. The authors evaluate LLMs across various tasks such as visual reasoning, image classification, and image generation using SVG representations. The experiments demonstrate that LLMs can perform these tasks effectively, albeit with limitations such as the inability to handle complex visual tasks and the loss of fine details during SVG conversion. The paper also discusses the potential of LLMs in image generation and manipulation, showing that they can generate images based on textual prompts. However, the novelty of the approach is questioned as similar methods have been previously explored, and the paper lacks a detailed comparison with existing methods.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, easy to follow, and provides a comprehensive evaluation of the proposed method across various tasks, including visual reasoning, image classification, and image generation.
- The experiments conducted are thorough and demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed method in handling different types of visual content, showing that LLMs can perform well in tasks involving SVG representations.
- The paper introduces an innovative approach by using SVG to represent images, which is a novel method that has not been extensively explored in prior research.
- The paper provides a detailed analysis of the limitations of the proposed method, which is crucial for understanding the scope and applicability of the approach.
- The use of SVG as a representation format for images is highlighted as a potential solution to the problem of generating text descriptions from images, which is a significant challenge in computer vision.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed comparison with existing methods, particularly those that use similar SVG representations or other vector formats for image processing.
- The novelty of the approach is questioned as similar methods have been previously explored, such as the use of SVG for image processing and the application of LLMs to visual tasks.
- The paper does not adequately address the limitations of the proposed method, particularly the loss of fine details during SVG conversion and the inability of LLMs to handle complex visual tasks.
- The paper could benefit from more detailed explanations and examples of how the SVG representation is used to answer visual reasoning questions and how the model generates answers.
- The evaluation of the proposed method is limited to simple datasets, which may not fully demonstrate its effectiveness in more complex or realistic scenarios.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors clarify the specific contributions of their method compared to existing approaches, particularly those that use similar SVG representations or other vector formats for image processing?
- How does the proposed method compare in terms of performance and efficiency with other methods that use SVG or other vector formats for image processing?
- Could the authors provide more detailed examples and explanations of how the SVG representation is used to answer visual reasoning questions and how the model generates answers?
- How does the model handle the loss of fine details during SVG conversion, and what strategies could be used to mitigate this issue?
- Could the authors discuss the potential of extending the proposed method to handle more complex visual tasks, such as object detection or segmentation?
