 **Summary:**
The paper introduces UCAN, a method that extends the randomized smoothing (RS) framework to anisotropic noise, aiming to enhance certified robustness. The authors propose a novel theory that transforms isotropic noise into anisotropic noise, theoretically supporting the approach. UCAN utilizes a neural network generator to customize noise distributions, demonstrating improved performance over state-of-the-art methods across various datasets. The paper also discusses the potential for further research in refining the noise parameter generators and exploring different noise distributions. However, it lacks a detailed discussion on the limitations and potential negative societal impacts of the proposed methods.
