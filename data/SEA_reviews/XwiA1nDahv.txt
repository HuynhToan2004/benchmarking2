 **Summary:**
The paper introduces SmoothECE, a novel method for estimating calibration error in binary classification models. SmoothECE uses a kernel smoothing technique to estimate the calibration error, which is shown to be a consistent measure that aligns well with the Wasserstein distance to perfect calibration. The method is designed to improve upon the traditional ECE by providing a smoother and more reliable calibration measure. The paper includes theoretical proofs and empirical evaluations demonstrating the method's effectiveness and computational efficiency. The authors have developed a Python package to facilitate the application of SmoothECE in practical scenarios.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, clear, and easy to follow, with a strong theoretical foundation and intuitive explanations that make the concepts accessible to a broad audience.
- The introduction of a new calibration measure, SmoothECE, which is a consistent calibration measure and provides a smoothed reliability diagram, is a significant contribution to the field.
- The paper includes comprehensive theoretical proofs and empirical evaluations, demonstrating the effectiveness of SmoothECE across various datasets and scenarios.
- The development of a Python package to facilitate the application of SmoothECE in practical scenarios is a valuable resource for practitioners and researchers.
- The methodology is straightforward, making it easy to understand and implement, and the paper provides a clear comparison with previous methods.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a thorough discussion on the limitations and potential negative societal impacts of the proposed method.
- The empirical evaluations are limited to binary classification tasks, and it is unclear how the method would perform in multi-class scenarios.
- The paper could benefit from more detailed comparisons with other calibration measures, such as those mentioned in related works, to better understand the advantages and disadvantages of SmoothECE.
- The computational efficiency of SmoothECE compared to other methods is not discussed, which could be crucial for practical applications.
- The paper does not provide a detailed discussion on the choice of bandwidth for kernel smoothing, which is a critical parameter for the method's effectiveness.
- The paper's experimental setup and results could be improved by including more datasets and a broader range of model types to better demonstrate the generalizability of the method.

**Questions:**
- How does the proposed method compare to other calibration measures, such as those discussed in related works?
- Could you elaborate on the choice of bandwidth for kernel smoothing and its impact on the method's performance?
- How does the method perform in multi-class scenarios, and are there plans to extend the method to handle such cases?
- Can you provide more details on the computational efficiency of SmoothECE compared to other methods, particularly in terms of runtime and memory usage?
- How does the method handle different types of datasets, such as those with varying densities or different distributions?
- Is there a possibility to apply SmoothECE to models other than those used in the experiments, and what would be the expected outcomes?
