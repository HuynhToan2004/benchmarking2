 **Summary:**
The paper introduces PODNet, a novel approach that leverages neural networks to emulate the Proper Orthogonal Decomposition (POD) method for parametric partial differential equations. By integrating neural networks, PODNet extends POD's capabilities to handle solutions from different discretization schemes, thereby enhancing its applicability and flexibility. The paper presents two case studies, a 1D trigonometric problem and a 2D Graetz problem, to validate the effectiveness of PODNet. Despite the innovative approach, concerns are raised about the novelty and the method's effectiveness in real-world scenarios. The paper is criticized for its lack of comprehensive literature review, insufficient comparison with existing methods, and inadequate discussion on the practical implications of its findings.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel approach to emulate the POD method using neural networks, which is a significant advancement in the field.
- The methodology is well-explained, making it accessible and understandable to readers.
- The paper is well-written, with clear and concise language, and the figures effectively support the content.
- The use of neural networks to emulate POD is a promising direction, as it allows for the handling of different discretization schemes and could potentially improve the efficiency of POD.
- The paper provides a clear explanation of the PODNet architecture and its components, which aids in understanding the proposed methodology.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a comprehensive literature review, which makes it difficult to assess the novelty and position of the proposed method within the existing body of research.
- The comparison with existing methods is insufficient, and the paper does not convincingly demonstrate the superiority of PODNet over other methods.
- The paper does not sufficiently discuss the practical implications of its findings, which limits the understanding of the real-world applicability of the proposed method.
- The paper does not adequately address the issue of orthogonality in the PODNet approach, which is crucial for maintaining the effectiveness of the POD method.
- The experimental setup and results are not robust enough to convincingly demonstrate the effectiveness of PODNet, particularly in real-world scenarios.
- The paper lacks a thorough discussion on the limitations and potential drawbacks of the proposed method, which is essential for a balanced assessment of its contributions.

**Questions:**
- Could you provide a more detailed comparison of PODNet with existing methods, including a discussion on how it addresses the limitations of previous approaches?
- How does PODNet perform in scenarios where the number of snapshots is limited, and what are the implications for the accuracy and effectiveness of the method?
- Can you discuss the practical implications of using PODNet in real-world scenarios, particularly in terms of computational efficiency and the quality of the reduced basis?
- How does PODNet handle scenarios where the solution space is nonlinear or where the POD modes are not orthogonal?
- Could you provide a more detailed discussion on the orthogonality constraints and their impact on the effectiveness of PODNet?
- How does PODNet compare to other neural network-based methods for POD, and what are the advantages or disadvantages of using PODNet over these other methods?
- Can you provide a more detailed discussion on the limitations of PODNet and potential directions for future research to address these limitations?
