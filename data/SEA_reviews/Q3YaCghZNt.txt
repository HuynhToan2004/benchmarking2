 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel framework, Lemur, that integrates Large Language Models (LLMs) with automated reasoners for program verification. This framework uses LLMs to propose program invariants, which are then checked by automated reasoners, aiming to enhance the verification process. The authors provide a theoretical foundation for this approach by formalizing the interaction between LLMs and automated reasoners, demonstrating its soundness and effectiveness through experiments on both synthetic and competition benchmarks. The paper also discusses the practical implementation of the framework, including its limitations and potential extensions.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel approach by combining LLMs and automated reasoners for program verification, providing a theoretical foundation for this integration.
- The proposed framework is evaluated on both synthetic and competition benchmarks, demonstrating its effectiveness and efficiency.
- The authors provide a detailed explanation of the implementation and optimization of the proposed framework, enhancing its practical applicability.
- The paper is well-written and clearly presents the proposed methodology and its results, making it accessible and understandable to a broad audience.
- The integration of LLMs and automated reasoners in a formalized manner is a significant contribution to the field, potentially opening new avenues for automated program verification.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed discussion on the limitations of the proposed framework, which is crucial for understanding its applicability and potential drawbacks.
- There is a need for more detailed experimental results, including a comparison with other state-of-the-art methods and a broader range of benchmarks.
- The paper could benefit from a more thorough discussion on the computational complexity and scalability of the proposed framework, especially in relation to the size and complexity of the programs being verified.
- The integration of LLMs and automated reasoners is seen as a straightforward combination, lacking a deeper exploration into how these components interact and complement each other.
- The paper does not adequately address the potential negative societal impacts of the proposed framework, which could be a significant concern in practical applications.

**Questions:**
- Could you provide more details on the limitations of the proposed framework and how these might affect its practical applicability?
- How does the proposed framework compare in terms of computational complexity and scalability with other state-of-the-art methods for program verification?
- Can you discuss the potential negative societal impacts of the proposed framework and how these might be mitigated or addressed?
- How do the authors envision the integration of LLMs and automated reasoners evolving in the future, and what are the key challenges and opportunities in this area?
- Could you clarify the role and effectiveness of the LLM in the proposed framework, particularly in generating sub-goals and repairing assumptions?
- How do the authors address the potential biases or inaccuracies in the LLM's outputs, and what measures are in place to ensure the reliability and validity of the generated invariants?
