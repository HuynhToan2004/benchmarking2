 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel latent diffusion model for image super-resolution (SR), which incorporates a frequency-augmented decoder and a sampling-space mixture of experts (SS-MoE) to address the challenges of high-frequency detail loss and computational efficiency. The proposed method enhances the model's capacity to generate high-frequency details without significantly increasing computational costs. It is evaluated on both synthetic and real datasets, demonstrating superior performance over existing methods in terms of perceptual quality and computational efficiency. The paper also includes a detailed analysis of the proposed components, such as SS-MoE and the frequency-augmented decoder, and provides comprehensive ablation studies to validate their effectiveness.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, clear, and easy to follow, with detailed explanations of the proposed methods.
- The proposed frequency-augmented decoder is innovative and effectively addresses the issue of high-frequency detail loss in image super-resolution.
- The sampling-space mixture of experts (SS-MoE) is a novel approach that enhances the model's capacity to generate high-frequency details without significantly increasing computational costs.
- The paper provides a comprehensive analysis of the proposed components, including SS-MoE and the frequency-augmented decoder, which are supported by extensive ablation studies.
- The method is evaluated on both synthetic and real datasets, demonstrating superior performance over existing methods in terms of perceptual quality and computational efficiency.
- The paper is well-motivated, addressing the limitations of existing methods such as compression of latent space and computational costs, and proposes innovative solutions to these challenges.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed comparison of computational costs and parameter numbers with existing methods, which could better demonstrate the efficiency of the proposed method.
- The paper does not provide a detailed comparison of the proposed method with state-of-the-art methods on blind super-resolution tasks, which could strengthen the paper's claims of superiority.
- The paper could benefit from a more detailed discussion on the limitations of the proposed method and potential future research directions.
- There are some minor issues with the presentation, such as the clarity of figures and the placement of tables and figures.
- The paper could benefit from a more rigorous evaluation, including a broader range of metrics and comparisons with more recent and relevant methods.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors provide a more detailed comparison of the computational costs and parameter numbers with existing methods to better demonstrate the efficiency of the proposed method?
- Can the authors provide a more detailed discussion on the limitations of the proposed method and potential future research directions?
- Can the authors provide more results on blind super-resolution tasks to strengthen the paper's claims of superiority?
- Can the authors provide a more detailed comparison with other state-of-the-art methods to better position the proposed method within the field?
- Can the authors clarify the use of different datasets and settings in the experiments, especially in relation to the claims of generalizability and scalability of the proposed method?
