 **Summary:**
The paper introduces the Insert-Fill-Halt (IFH) framework, which integrates one-shot and autoregressive graph generation models by employing a node removal process and a node insertion process. This framework allows for the generation of graphs with varying degrees of sequentiality, from fully sequential to one-shot. The IFH model is evaluated on molecular datasets, showing improved performance over existing models in terms of sample quality and time-memory trade-offs. The framework also allows for the adaptation of one-shot models to act as filler models, potentially improving sample quality. Despite its innovative approach, the paper is criticized for its complexity, limited novelty, and lack of comprehensive experimental validation on a broader range of datasets.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel framework that unifies one-shot and sequential graph generation, offering a fresh perspective on graph generation.
- The proposed framework is technically sound, well-structured, and clearly presented, making it accessible to readers.
- The IFH model is shown to perform well in terms of sample quality and time-memory trade-offs, demonstrating its practical utility.
- The paper provides a comprehensive evaluation of the IFH model, demonstrating its effectiveness on molecular datasets, which is a significant application area.
- The concept of using a diffusion-based node removal process to unify one-shot and sequential models is innovative and could inspire further research in the field.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper's complexity, particularly in the framework's details, may make it difficult for readers to fully grasp the proposed methodology.
- The novelty of the IFH framework is limited as it largely builds upon existing methods like DiGress, with only minor modifications.
- The experimental validation is limited to molecular datasets, which may not fully demonstrate the generalizability of the IFH model to other types of graphs.
- The paper lacks a detailed discussion on the limitations and potential negative societal impacts of the proposed method.
- The paper could benefit from a more comprehensive experimental validation, including a broader range of datasets and a comparison with a wider variety of baselines.
- The paper could be improved by providing more detailed explanations of the IFH framework and its components, as well as more detailed results and discussions on the experimental findings.

**Questions:**
- Could you clarify the differences between the IFH framework and the diffusion-based one-shot model DiGress, especially in terms of the novel contributions of the IFH model?
- How does the IFH framework handle the generation of nodes with features, such as node features in molecular graphs?
- What is the rationale behind the choice of the halting model in the IFH framework, and how does it impact the performance and efficiency of the model?
- Could you provide more details on the training and inference times for the IFH model, particularly how these times scale with the size of the graph?
- How does the IFH model compare in terms of performance and efficiency with other state-of-the-art graph generative models, such as those mentioned in the paper?
- Could you discuss the potential limitations and negative societal impacts of the IFH model, particularly in the context of its application to other types of graphs?
