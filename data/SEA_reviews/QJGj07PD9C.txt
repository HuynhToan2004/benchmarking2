 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel mixed-precision training method for Fourier Neural Operators (FNO), addressing the computational and memory challenges associated with high-resolution training data and large-scale problems. The proposed method, which includes a pre-activation layer and a tensor contraction optimization technique, is designed to minimize numerical instability and optimize memory usage. Theoretical analysis is provided to support the use of mixed precision in FNO, showing that the precision error is comparable to the approximation error. Extensive experiments across various datasets and GPUs demonstrate that the proposed method significantly reduces GPU memory usage and improves throughput, achieving up to 50% memory reduction and a 58% increase in throughput, with minimal or no reduction in accuracy.

**Strengths:**
- The paper presents a novel mixed-precision training method for Fourier Neural Operators (FNO), which is a significant contribution to the field of neural operator learning.
- The theoretical analysis provided in the paper is robust and well-articulated, providing a solid foundation for the proposed method.
- The empirical results are compelling, showing that the proposed method significantly reduces GPU memory usage and improves throughput, with minimal or no reduction in accuracy.
- The paper is well-written, with clear explanations and extensive experiments that demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed method.
- The proposed method is simple yet effective, and the theoretical analysis is well-grounded, providing a solid foundation for the empirical results.
- The paper is original in its approach to using mixed precision in FNO, which is a novel and significant contribution to the field.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a thorough discussion on the limitations of the proposed method, particularly in terms of its applicability to other types of neural operators or datasets beyond those tested.
- The theoretical analysis, while robust, may not fully capture the complexities of real-world datasets, particularly in terms of the discretization error which is a significant factor in the performance of FNO.
- The paper does not adequately address the potential impact of the proposed method on the generalization ability of FNO, which is a critical aspect of neural operator learning.
- There is a lack of discussion on the computational overhead introduced by the proposed method, which could affect the practicality of the method in large-scale applications.
- The paper does not sufficiently discuss the potential societal impacts of the proposed method, which could be a significant concern for some applications of FNO.
- The paper does not provide sufficient details on the implementation of the proposed method, which could make it difficult for other researchers to replicate the results.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors provide more details on how the discretization error is handled in the proposed method, particularly in terms of its impact on the overall performance of FNO?
- How does the proposed method perform in scenarios where the generalization ability of FNO is critical, such as in super-resolution tasks or in domains where the data distribution is significantly different from the training set?
- Could the authors elaborate on the computational overhead introduced by the proposed method and discuss potential strategies for minimizing this overhead in practical applications?
- How does the proposed method compare to other methods that use mixed precision in FNO, particularly in terms of accuracy and computational efficiency?
- Can the authors provide more details on the implementation of the proposed method, including the specific algorithms and techniques used to optimize tensor contractions and manage numerical stability?
- How does the proposed method perform in scenarios where the data distribution is significantly different from the training set, such as in zero-shot super-resolution tasks?
- Could the authors discuss the potential societal impacts of the proposed method and how these impacts might be mitigated or addressed in practical applications?
