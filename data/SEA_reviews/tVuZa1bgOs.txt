 **Summary:**
The paper introduces BiAug, a bimodal data augmentation framework designed to mitigate reporting bias in vision-language datasets. BiAug operates by decoupling object-attribute associations, generating both new captions and corresponding images, and constructing hard negative pairs. The framework is evaluated across various datasets, demonstrating improvements in object-attribute understanding and retrieval tasks. However, concerns about the clarity of the paper, the method's scalability, and the generalizability of the results are raised. The method's effectiveness is questioned, particularly in its ability to handle complex scenarios and its reliance on specific datasets and models.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, with clear and concise descriptions of the proposed method and its components.
- The motivation behind the study is clear, and the proposed method is reasonable and easy to follow.
- The paper is well-organized, with a clear description of the proposed method and its components.
- The proposed method is novel and effective, as demonstrated through experiments.
- The paper provides a detailed description of the proposed method, including the steps involved in generating the augmented dataset and the evaluation of the dataset quality.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a clear explanation of the impact of reporting bias on vision-language models and how the proposed method addresses this issue.
- There is a lack of clarity in the presentation of the method, particularly in the description of how the method operates and the results it produces.
- The paper does not adequately discuss the limitations of the proposed method, such as its scalability and generalizability.
- The paper does not provide sufficient details on the method's implementation, such as the specific models and datasets used, and the methodology for generating the augmented dataset.
- The paper does not compare the proposed method with existing methods, which limits the evaluation of its effectiveness.
- The paper does not discuss the potential negative societal impacts of the work, which is a significant omission.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors clarify how the proposed method addresses the specific issue of reporting bias in vision-language datasets?
- How does the proposed method ensure that the generated images and captions are not only diverse but also of high quality?
- Could the authors provide more details on the method's implementation, such as the specific models and datasets used, and the methodology for generating the augmented dataset?
- How does the proposed method handle scenarios where the object-attribute associations are complex or ambiguous?
- Can the authors discuss the potential negative societal impacts of the work and how these have been addressed?
- How does the proposed method compare with existing methods in terms of effectiveness and efficiency?
- Can the authors provide more details on the method's scalability and generalizability, particularly in terms of its applicability to different types of datasets and models?
