 **Summary:**
The paper introduces SRL, a distributed reinforcement learning (RL) system designed to address the limitations of existing open-source libraries by providing a scalable, efficient, and extensible framework for training RL agents. SRL employs a novel abstraction of RL dataflows, which is used to develop a system capable of parallelizing data collection and training processes across multiple CPU cores, improving training throughput significantly. The system is evaluated against other open-source libraries and industrial systems, demonstrating superior performance in terms of both training efficiency and learning outcomes. SRL's architecture is highlighted for its flexibility and adaptability, allowing for the customization of environments, policies, and algorithms. The paper also includes extensive evaluations on various benchmarks, showcasing the system's effectiveness in large-scale RL applications.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, clear, and easy to follow, with a clear motivation and well-defined research questions.
- The proposed system, SRL, is innovative, scalable, and efficient, providing a novel abstraction of RL dataflows that enables the development of a system capable of parallelizing data collection and training processes.
- SRL is extensible and customizable, allowing for the customization of environments, policies, and algorithms, which is crucial for large-scale RL applications.
- The paper includes a comprehensive evaluation of SRL, demonstrating its superior performance compared to existing open-source libraries and industrial systems in terms of training throughput and learning outcomes.
- The inclusion of a detailed ablation study and comparisons with open-source libraries and industrial systems provides a robust validation of the proposed system.
- The paper's focus on scalability and efficiency in RL training is significant, as it addresses a critical need in the field, particularly in the context of large-scale RL applications.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed discussion on the limitations of the proposed system, which could provide a more comprehensive understanding of its applicability and potential challenges.
- The evaluation primarily focuses on the training throughput and learning outcomes, but does not address other performance metrics such as memory efficiency, which could be crucial for large-scale RL applications.
- The paper does not adequately compare SRL with other distributed RL systems like MSRL, which could provide a more comprehensive evaluation of the system's performance.
- The paper does not discuss the potential negative societal impacts of the proposed system, which could be a significant concern for some readers.
- There are minor issues with the presentation of results, such as missing error bars and inconsistencies in the reporting of results across different figures.

**Questions:**
- Could you provide more details on the limitations of the proposed system and how these might impact its applicability in different scenarios?
- How does SRL compare in terms of memory efficiency with other distributed RL systems?
- Can you provide a more detailed comparison of SRL with other distributed RL systems like MSRL, particularly in terms of performance and scalability?
- How does SRL handle the challenges of scaling RL algorithms, and what are the potential limitations or challenges in this regard?
- Could you clarify the discrepancies in the results reported in different figures, particularly the performance of PPO in different scenarios?
