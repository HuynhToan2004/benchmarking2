 **Summary:**
The paper investigates the impact of label smoothing on model inversion attacks (MIAs), revealing that positive label smoothing can increase privacy leakage while negative label smoothing can mitigate this risk. The authors provide a novel attack metric and empirical evidence demonstrating that negative label smoothing can be an effective defense against MIAs, offering a better utility-privacy trade-off than existing methods. The study is based on extensive experiments and theoretical analysis, highlighting the potential of negative label smoothing as a privacy-preserving regularization technique.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, easy to follow, and provides a comprehensive analysis of the effects of label smoothing on model inversion attacks (MIAs).
- The authors introduce a novel attack metric and provide a detailed theoretical analysis of label smoothing, demonstrating its potential as a privacy-preserving regularization technique.
- The paper is original and fills a gap in the literature by exploring the impact of label smoothing on model privacy, specifically in the context of MIAs.
- The paper is supported by extensive experiments and includes a detailed analysis of the effects of label smoothing on model inversion attacks, which could be beneficial for improving the understanding of model privacy.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper's experiments are limited to specific datasets and models, which may not generalize to other types of datasets or models.
- The paper does not discuss the impact of label smoothing on other types of privacy attacks, such as membership inference attacks or model stealing attacks.
- The paper does not include a comparison with other privacy-preserving regularization techniques, which could provide a more comprehensive evaluation of the effectiveness of label smoothing.
- The paper's focus on label smoothing as a defense against MIAs might be seen as limited, as it does not explore the potential of label smoothing as a general privacy-preserving technique.
- The paper does not provide a detailed analysis of the computational cost associated with negative label smoothing, which could be a significant drawback in practical applications.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors provide a more detailed explanation of the negative label smoothing technique and its implications for model performance and privacy?
- How does the paper's finding that negative label smoothing can reduce privacy leakage compare to other privacy-preserving regularization techniques?
- Is there a possibility that the findings of this paper could be applied to other types of privacy attacks, such as membership inference attacks or model stealing attacks?
- How does the paper's conclusion regarding the effectiveness of negative label smoothing as a defense against MIAs compare to other defense mechanisms?
- Could the authors elaborate on the computational cost associated with negative label smoothing and discuss potential strategies for mitigating this cost?
