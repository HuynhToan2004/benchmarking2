 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel approach to addressing the simulator-to-real transfer problem in reinforcement learning (RL) by proposing a regularization term that aligns the policy trained in simulation with real-world data. This regularization term is integrated into the trust region optimization objective, which is shown to improve performance in various simulator-to-real transfer settings. The method is evaluated against several baselines and is demonstrated to outperform them in most cases. However, concerns are raised about the novelty of the approach and the clarity of its presentation, particularly in terms of its theoretical underpinnings and the experimental setup.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, easy to follow, and provides a clear motivation for the research.
- The proposed method is novel and theoretically justified, with a focus on regularization terms that are compatible with imitation learning.
- The paper is well-structured, with a clear presentation of the experimental results and a comprehensive evaluation against several baselines.
- The use of trust region algorithms to address the simulator-to-real transfer problem is a promising approach, which has shown effectiveness in the few-shot setting.
- The paper provides a theoretical justification for the proposed method, which is supported by empirical results demonstrating the method's effectiveness.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed discussion on the limitations of the proposed method, particularly in terms of its applicability and scalability.
- The novelty of the approach is questioned, as it seems to be a straightforward extension of existing methods, such as those proposed by Touati et al. (2020).
- The paper does not provide sufficient details on the experimental setup, including the hyperparameters used and the specifics of the real-world data collection process.
- The paper's contribution is seen as limited, with the main innovation being the integration of regularization terms from imitation learning into trust region optimization.
- The presentation of the paper could be improved, particularly in terms of clarity and the organization of the experimental results.
- There is a lack of discussion on the societal impact of the proposed method, which is a significant omission in the paper.

**Questions:**
