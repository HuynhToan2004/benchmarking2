 **Summary:**
The paper introduces AVSAC, a novel approach to audio-visual segmentation that addresses the modality imbalance issue by integrating audio and visual modalities through a bidirectional audio-visual decoder. This decoder is designed to enhance audio cues by interacting bidirectionally with visual representations, thus improving audio-visual representation learning. The proposed Audio Feature Reconstruction (AFR) module helps prevent harmful data bias and maintain audio information. Extensive experiments on the AVS benchmark demonstrate that AVSAC achieves state-of-the-art performances, particularly in the MS3 subset, which involves multiple sound sources. The methodology is well-articulated, and the paper is praised for its clarity and logical presentation.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, easy to follow, and the methodology is well-explained, making it accessible to a broad audience.
- The proposed bidirectional audio-visual decoder effectively addresses the modality imbalance issue in audio-visual segmentation, which is a significant challenge in the field.
- The Audio Feature Reconstruction (AFR) module is innovative and helps prevent harmful data bias, ensuring the existence of audio cues during training.
- The paper demonstrates state-of-the-art performance on the AVS benchmark, particularly in the MS3 subset, which is challenging due to the presence of multiple sound sources.
- The methodology is simple, logical, and supported by extensive experiments and ablation studies, which validate the effectiveness of the proposed components.
- The paper is well-motivated and addresses a significant problem in audio-visual segmentation, which is crucial for enhancing the interaction between audio and visual modalities in multimodal learning.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper could benefit from a more detailed discussion on the limitations of the proposed method, particularly in terms of its applicability to other datasets or scenarios where the modality imbalance might not be as pronounced.
- There is a need for a more thorough comparison with other state-of-the-art methods, including those that use different backbones or architectures, to fully understand the effectiveness of the proposed method.
- The paper could benefit from a more detailed analysis of the computational complexity and memory requirements of the proposed method, especially in comparison to existing methods.
- Some figures and tables in the paper are not clearly explained or referenced, which could confuse the reader or limit the understanding of the results.
- The paper could benefit from a more comprehensive evaluation on other datasets or scenarios to validate the generalizability of the proposed method.
