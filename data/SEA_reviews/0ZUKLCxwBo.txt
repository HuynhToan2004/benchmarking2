 **Summary:**
The paper explores the phenomenon of "grokking" in neural networks trained on algorithmic datasets, specifically focusing on modular arithmetic tasks. It proposes a minimal model consisting of a two-layer MLP without biases, trained using gradient descent, and demonstrates that this model can achieve high test accuracy for certain modular functions. The paper presents a novel analytical solution for the weights of the network that generalize to other modular functions, supported by empirical evidence showing that gradient descent can find solutions close to the analytical ones. The study also discusses the role of regularization and data availability in the grokking phenomenon.

**Strengths:**
- The paper provides a clear and well-structured presentation, making the main ideas and results accessible and understandable.
- It introduces a simple yet insightful model that contributes to the understanding of grokking in neural networks, particularly focusing on modular arithmetic tasks.
- The empirical results are supported by rigorous experiments, and the paper includes a thorough discussion of related work, providing a comprehensive review of the literature.
- The analytical solutions provided are novel and offer a new perspective on the grokking phenomenon, which is not commonly explored in previous studies.
- The paper addresses an important and timely problem, contributing to the ongoing discourse on the interpretability and generalization capabilities of neural networks.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper's claims are somewhat limited to specific modular functions, and the generalizability of the findings to other types of functions or architectures is not clear.
- The analytical solutions are not applicable to general modular functions, which could limit the practical utility of the proposed model.
- The paper's empirical results, while supported by experiments, are somewhat limited in scope and do not convincingly demonstrate the robustness or applicability of the findings across different settings or architectures.
- The paper does not sufficiently address the computational complexity or scalability of the proposed model, which could be a significant concern for more complex tasks or larger datasets.
- The paper's title and some of its claims (e.g., about the generalizability of the findings) may be overstated, which could mislead readers about the actual contributions and scope of the study.

**Questions:**
- Could you clarify the role and impact of regularization in the grokking phenomenon, especially in relation to the findings in previous studies?
- How does the paper's model compare to other models that might exhibit similar behaviors, such as those discussed in related works?
- Could you provide more details on the computational complexity and scalability of the proposed model, especially in relation to more complex tasks or larger datasets?
- In the context of the analytical solutions provided, how do they generalize to other types of functions or architectures, and what are the limitations of their applicability?
- How do the authors address the potential issues of overfitting or lack of generalization in their model, especially in light of the empirical results presented?
