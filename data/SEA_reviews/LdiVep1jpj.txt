 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel quadrature rule for approximating integrals in the context of Gaussian variational inference (VI), focusing on the projective integral updates (PIU) framework. The proposed quadrature rule, termed RASP quadratures, is designed to reduce the number of function evaluations required for Gaussian mean-field updates, aiming to improve the efficiency and accuracy of variational inference. The paper provides theoretical guarantees and experimental evidence supporting the effectiveness of RASP quadratures, demonstrating their ability to achieve higher-order exactness in univariate and bivariate terms with fewer evaluations compared to traditional Monte Carlo methods. The methodology is supported by a detailed analysis of the expected errors and a comparison with existing quadrature rules, showcasing the potential of RASP quadratures to enhance the performance of variational inference in high-dimensional settings.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, clear, and easy to follow, with a detailed description of the proposed RASP quadrature and its implementation.
- The RASP quadrature is a novel and interesting method for approximating integrals in the context of Gaussian variational inference, with theoretical guarantees on exactness.
- The method is compatible with existing variational inference algorithms such as SGVB and QNVB, and the paper provides an open-source PyTorch implementation.
- The experimental results demonstrate that RASP quadratures can achieve higher-order exactness with fewer evaluations, outperforming traditional Monte Carlo methods in some cases.
- The paper includes a detailed analysis of the expected errors and a comparison with existing quadrature rules, providing a thorough evaluation of the proposed method.

**Weaknesses:**
