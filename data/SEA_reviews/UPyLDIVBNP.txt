 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel initialization method called IDInit, which sets all weights to an identity matrix for non-square matrices by padding zeros to the matrix. This approach aims to address the rank constraint problem and improve the performance of neural networks, particularly in residual networks. The method is tested across various datasets including Cifar10, ImageNet, and BERT, demonstrating improved performance and faster convergence. However, the paper lacks comprehensive theoretical analysis and comparative experiments, which are crucial for validating the effectiveness and generalizability of the proposed method.

**Strengths:**
- The paper addresses a significant problem in neural network initialization by proposing a novel method that maintains identity in both main and sub-stem layers, which is a crucial aspect of network training.
- The proposed method is straightforward and easy to implement, making it accessible for practical applications.
- The method is tested across various settings, including large-scale datasets and deep models, demonstrating its effectiveness and applicability.
- The paper is well-written and provides clear explanations of the proposed method and its theoretical underpinnings, making it easy to understand.
- The experiments conducted show that the proposed method can improve the performance of neural networks, which is a notable contribution to the field.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a comprehensive theoretical analysis of the proposed method, which could strengthen the understanding and validation of its effectiveness.
- There is a lack of comparative experiments with other initialization methods, which is crucial for establishing the superiority or uniqueness of the proposed method.
- The paper does not provide sufficient empirical evidence to support the claims made, particularly concerning the generalization ability of the proposed method.
- The methodology is not clearly explained in some sections, making it difficult to follow the paper's arguments and conclusions.
- The paper does not provide sufficient details on the experimental setups, such as the training parameters and dataset details, which are essential for reproducibility and validation of the results.
- The paper could benefit from a more comprehensive discussion on the limitations and potential negative impacts of the proposed method.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors provide a more detailed theoretical analysis of the proposed method, particularly addressing how it maintains identity in both main and sub-stem layers?
- How does the proposed method compare to other initialization methods, such as those mentioned in the literature review?
- Can the authors provide more empirical evidence to support the claims made about the generalization ability of the proposed method?
- Could the authors clarify the methodology and provide more detailed explanations in sections where the methodology is unclear?
- How do the authors address the limitations of the proposed method, and what are the potential negative impacts or drawbacks of using this method?
- Can the authors provide more details on the experimental setups, such as the training parameters and dataset details, to enhance the reproducibility and validation of the results?
