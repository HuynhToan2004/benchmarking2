 **Summary:**
The paper introduces SparklesChat, a multimodal instruction-following model designed for dialogues involving multiple images. It is trained on a novel dataset, SparklesDialogue, which is a machine-generated dialogue dataset specifically tailored for multimodal interactions. The model is evaluated using SparklesEval, a benchmark designed to assess its conversational competence across multiple images and dialogue turns. The experiments demonstrate that SparklesChat outperforms existing models like MiniGPT-4 and LLaVA on various vision-language tasks and the SparklesEval benchmark. However, the paper's novelty is questioned due to its reliance on existing models and datasets, and the evaluation primarily using GPT-4 as a judge model.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel dataset, SparklesDialogue, which is specifically designed for multimodal instruction-following models and is the first to address this need in the field.
- The paper is well-written, with clear descriptions of the datasets, methodologies, and experiments, making it easy to follow and understand.
- The evaluation methodology is robust, using a comprehensive benchmark (SparklesEval) to assess the conversational competence of models across multiple images and dialogue turns.
- The proposed model, SparklesChat, demonstrates superior performance over existing models like MiniGPT-4 and LLaVA on various vision-language tasks, highlighting its effectiveness.
- The dataset and model are open-sourced, which enhances reproducibility and allows for further research and development in the field.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper primarily uses GPT-4 as the judge model for evaluating the conversational competence of models, which may introduce bias and limit the generalizability of the results.
- The dataset construction is solely reliant on GPT-4, raising concerns about the quality and diversity of the dataset, especially since GPT-4 is known for its bias and hallucination issues.
- The novelty of the work is limited as it primarily builds upon existing models and datasets, with minor modifications that do not significantly advance the state-of-the-art.
- The paper lacks detailed comparisons with existing multimodal dialogue datasets and does not discuss the potential societal impacts of the work, which are crucial for evaluating the broader implications of the research.
- The evaluation metrics used in the paper are not sufficiently robust or diverse, relying heavily on accuracy metrics which may not adequately capture the nuances of conversational competence.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors provide more detailed comparisons with existing multimodal dialogue datasets, discussing the similarities and differences between these datasets and SparklesDialogue?
- How does the paper address the potential biases and limitations introduced by the exclusive use of GPT-4 for dataset construction and evaluation?
- Can the authors discuss the potential societal impacts of their work, particularly in terms of privacy concerns and the use of AI in assisting with visual reasoning tasks?
- How does the proposed model handle the potential biases and hallucinations in GPT-4, especially in scenarios where the model's responses may not align with the ground truth?
- Could the authors provide more detailed results on how the model performs when trained on different combinations of datasets, such as SparklesDialogueCC and SparklesDialogueVG?
- How does the model handle scenarios where the user's questions are not well-formed or where the images provided are not sufficient for understanding the user's intent?
