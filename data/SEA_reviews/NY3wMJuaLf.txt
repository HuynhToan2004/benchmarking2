 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel approach to federated learning (FL) by generating complementary data to mitigate data heterogeneity, which is a significant challenge in FL. The approach involves each client generating data that is accurately predicted by the global model but incorrectly predicted by their local model, thereby reducing the discrepancy between the models. The generated data is used to train local models using knowledge distillation, which helps in reducing the heterogeneity further. The method, termed FedCOG, is compatible with standard FL protocols and is shown to outperform several state-of-the-art methods across various datasets. However, concerns are raised about the practicality of the method, particularly in scenarios where data generation might not be feasible, and the theoretical analysis is lacking.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel approach to addressing data heterogeneity in federated learning by generating complementary data, which is a creative and innovative solution.
- The method is compatible with standard federated learning protocols such as Secure Aggregation, making it practical and applicable in various scenarios.
- The paper is well-written, clear, and easy to follow, with a clear presentation of the methodology and experimental results.
- The empirical results demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed method, showing that it outperforms several state-of-the-art methods on various datasets.
- The paper includes a detailed discussion on the compatibility of the proposed method with standard federated learning communication protocols, which is a significant advantage for its practical application.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a theoretical analysis to support the effectiveness of the proposed method, which could strengthen the paper's claims.
- The practicality of the method is questionable, particularly in scenarios where data generation might not be feasible due to privacy concerns or practical limitations.
- The paper does not provide a detailed discussion on the computational overhead introduced by the proposed method, which is a significant concern in federated learning where computational efficiency is crucial.
- The paper's focus on generating data that is incorrectly predicted by the local model might not be practical, as it could lead to the generation of misleading or irrelevant data.
- The paper does not include a discussion on the limitations of the proposed method, which is essential for understanding its applicability and potential drawbacks.
- The paper does not include a detailed discussion on the scalability of the method, which is crucial for understanding its applicability in large-scale federated learning scenarios.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors provide a theoretical analysis to support the effectiveness of the proposed method?
- How does the proposed method handle privacy concerns in federated learning, particularly in the data generation phase?
- What is the computational overhead introduced by the proposed method, and how does it compare to other federated learning methods in terms of computational efficiency?
- How does the proposed method handle the scalability issues in federated learning, particularly in large-scale scenarios?
- Can the authors provide more details on the data generation process, particularly how it is ensured that the generated data is not misleading or irrelevant?
- How does the proposed method compare to other federated learning methods in terms of communication efficiency and privacy preservation?
- Can the authors provide a more detailed discussion on the limitations of the proposed method and potential scenarios where it might not be applicable?
