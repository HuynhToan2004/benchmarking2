 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel method for personalized federated learning (PFL) named pFedRLLA, which employs a reinforcement learning-based layer-wise aggregation strategy to address statistical heterogeneity in federated learning. This method involves a single DRL agent at the server-side that generates personalized aggregation weights for different layers of the neural network, with the body weights determined by data volume and the head weights by DRL. The approach is tested on four datasets and shows improvements over existing methods, although the experiments are limited to CNNs and the method's scalability and generalization to other models are not explored. The paper also discusses the use of multi-head autoencoders for dimensionality reduction and layer-wise aggregation strategies.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel approach to personalized federated learning (PFL) using reinforcement learning-based layer-wise aggregation, which is a novel application of reinforcement learning in federated learning.
- The methodology is well-explained, with clear explanations of the architecture and the use of reinforcement learning, which aids in understanding the proposed approach.
- The paper is well-written, making it easy to follow, and the experiments are conducted on four datasets, demonstrating the effectiveness of the proposed method.
- The use of multi-head autoencoders for dimensionality reduction is a novel approach that could be beneficial in reducing communication costs in federated learning.
- The paper provides a thorough analysis of the convergence speed of the proposed method, which is a significant contribution to the field.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a comprehensive comparison with existing methods, particularly in terms of computational efficiency and communication costs, which are critical in federated learning.
- The experiments are limited to CNNs and do not explore the application of the method to other models like Transformers, which could limit the generalizability of the findings.
- The paper does not adequately address the scalability of the method, particularly in terms of the number of clients and the complexity of the models, which are crucial factors in federated learning.
- The use of reinforcement learning for generating aggregation weights is not well-justified, and the paper does not provide a detailed comparison with other methods like FedAMP, which could have provided a clearer picture of the method's effectiveness.
- The paper does not discuss the limitations of the method, which could provide a more balanced view of the proposed approach.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors provide a more detailed comparison of the computational efficiency and communication costs of their method with existing methods?
- How does the method perform when applied to other models like Transformers, and what are the implications for the scalability and applicability of the method?
- Could the authors discuss the scalability of the method in terms of the number of clients and the complexity of the models, and provide insights into how the method could be adapted to handle larger and more complex datasets?
- How does the method handle the issue of client drift, which is a significant challenge in federated learning?
- Could the authors provide a more detailed explanation of the reinforcement learning approach and how it contributes to the effectiveness of the method?
- The paper mentions that the method outperforms existing methods in terms of convergence speed. Can the authors provide a more detailed analysis of the convergence behavior of the method?
