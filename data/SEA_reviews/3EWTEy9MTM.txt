 **Summary:**
The paper explores the expressive power of decoder-only transformers, focusing on the mechanism of Chain-of-Thought (CoT) reasoning. It provides a theoretical analysis of the expressiveness of transformers with CoT, comparing it to the expressiveness of standard transformers and other computational models like TC0 and AC0. The paper demonstrates that with a sufficient number of steps, CoT can simulate circuits of size equal to the number of steps, thereby enhancing the transformer's ability to solve complex problems. The theoretical insights are supported by empirical evaluations on synthetic data, showing that CoT can improve performance on specific tasks. However, the paper's scope is limited to decoder-only transformers and the CoT mechanism, and it does not address how these findings generalize to other types of transformers or tasks.
