 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a new evaluation metric, Importance-Weighted Orthogonality (IWO), to assess the orthogonality of latent subspaces in representation learning models. This metric is designed to measure the mutual orthogonality of subspaces encoding data-generating factors, regardless of their dimensionality or alignment with the canonical basis. The authors propose a methodology called Latent Orthogonal Analysis (LOA) to identify these subspaces and establish their importance, which is crucial for evaluating the disentanglement of latent factors in models. The paper provides empirical evidence through experiments on synthetic and real-world datasets, comparing IWO with existing metrics like MIG and DCI-D. However, the paper faces criticism for its limited scope in evaluating real-world datasets and the clarity of its methodological descriptions.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel metric, Importance-Weighted Orthogonality (IWO), which provides a new perspective on evaluating learned representations in a disentangled manner.
- The methodology, including the Latent Orthogonal Analysis (LOA) and the IWO metric, is well-explained and supported by empirical evidence.
- The paper is well-written and easy to follow, making it accessible to readers.
- The authors have conducted experiments on both synthetic and real-world datasets, demonstrating the practical applicability of their method.
- The paper addresses a significant gap in the current literature by focusing on the orthogonality of latent subspaces, which is a crucial aspect of disentangled representation learning.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a thorough discussion on the limitations of the proposed metric, particularly in real-world scenarios where the orthogonality of latent subspaces might not be as clear-cut as in synthetic datasets.
- The paper could benefit from a more detailed explanation of the methodology, particularly the training paradigm and the computational process involved in the Latent Orthogonal Analysis (LOA).
- There is a lack of clarity in the presentation of the methodology, which could hinder understanding for readers not familiar with the specific terms and concepts used.
- The paper does not sufficiently compare its method with existing metrics such as the Explicitness (E) metric from DCI-ES, which could provide a more robust validation of the proposed method.
- The experimental setup and results are not convincingly presented, with some results showing inconsistencies or insufficient explanation, such as the correlation between IWO and downstream task performance.
- The paper could benefit from more comprehensive evaluations on real-world datasets to demonstrate the practical applicability of the proposed method.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors provide more details on how the LOA method is trained and how the importance of each subspace is determined?
- How does the IWO metric compare with the Explicitness (E) metric from DCI-ES, particularly in scenarios where both metrics might yield different results?
- Could the authors clarify the computational process involved in the Latent Orthogonal Analysis (LOA) and the methodology for establishing the importance of latent subspaces?
- How does the proposed method perform in real-world datasets, and what are the implications of the observed correlations between IWO and downstream task performance?
- Could the authors provide a more detailed explanation of the experimental setup and results, particularly in sections where the results are not clearly explained or where there are inconsistencies?
