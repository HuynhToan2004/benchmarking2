 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel method for certifying robustness in visual graph matching (VGM) using a smoothed model that incorporates a joint Gaussian distribution to capture the correlation between keypoints. The method optimizes the smoothing range to achieve larger certified spaces and higher matching performance. It employs data augmentation and a regularization term to enhance the model's robustness against adversarial attacks. The method's effectiveness is demonstrated through experiments on various datasets, showing improvements over existing methods. However, the paper's novelty is questioned due to its similarity to previous works, and the computational cost and the effectiveness of the method under different perturbation conditions are not thoroughly addressed.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, making it easy to follow, and provides a comprehensive overview of the related works, particularly in the area of certified robustness.
- The authors address a significant problem in visual graph matching by proposing a method to certify robustness against adversarial attacks, which is a crucial aspect of machine learning models.
- The paper introduces a novel method that uses joint Gaussian distributions to capture the structural information between keypoints, which is a unique approach to enhancing the model's robustness.
- The experimental results demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed method, showing improvements in robustness certification and matching performance compared to existing methods.
- The paper provides a rigorous theoretical analysis, which is crucial for understanding the robustness of the proposed method.

**Weaknesses:**
- The novelty of the paper is limited as it closely resembles previous works, particularly in the use of joint Gaussian distributions for smoothing and the optimization of smoothing parameters.
- The computational cost of the proposed method is not discussed, which is a significant concern as it may not be practical for real-time applications.
- The paper lacks a detailed discussion on the effectiveness of the proposed method under different perturbation conditions, which is crucial for understanding its robustness.
- The method's reliance on a single type of perturbation (Gaussian noise) limits its applicability to other types of perturbations, which could be more realistic in practical scenarios.
- The paper does not adequately address the limitations of the proposed method, which could hinder its adoption in real-world applications.
- The experimental setup and results are not convincingly robust, as the method's effectiveness is not consistently demonstrated across different datasets and conditions.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors clarify the novelty of their method compared to previous works, particularly in the use of joint Gaussian distributions and the optimization of smoothing parameters?
- How does the proposed method perform under different perturbation conditions, such as non-Gaussian noise or other types of perturbations?
- Can the authors provide more details on the computational cost of their method and discuss potential strategies for reducing this cost?
- How does the proposed method compare in terms of robustness certification with other methods that use different types of perturbations or different robustness metrics?
- Could the authors provide a more detailed discussion on the limitations of their method and suggest potential directions for future research to address these limitations?
- How does the proposed method ensure robustness against various types of adversarial attacks, especially those that are not based on Gaussian noise?
