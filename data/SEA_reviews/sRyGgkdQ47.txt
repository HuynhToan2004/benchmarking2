 **Summary:**
The paper investigates the challenges of using Batch Normalization (BN) in Federated Learning (FL) and proposes a novel method, FixBN, to address these issues. FixBN is designed to improve the performance of BN in FL by freezing its statistics after a certain number of rounds, thereby reducing the sensitivity of gradients to batch statistics. The paper provides empirical evidence demonstrating the effectiveness of FixBN across various FL settings and non-IID data scenarios, showing that it outperforms other normalization methods like Group Normalization (GN). The authors also discuss the potential of BN in FL, challenging the conventional wisdom that GN is always superior. The paper is well-structured, providing detailed experimental results and theoretical insights, although it could benefit from more comprehensive theoretical analysis and broader experimental validations.

**Strengths:**
- The paper provides a thorough analysis of Batch Normalization (BN) in Federated Learning (FL), addressing the challenges and limitations of BN in FL, particularly in non-IID settings.
- It proposes a novel method, FixBN, which effectively addresses the issues of BN in FL by freezing the BN layer after a certain number of rounds, thereby improving performance.
- The paper is well-structured, providing a clear and detailed analysis of the challenges and limitations of BN in FL, supported by extensive experimental results.
- The proposed method, FixBN, is simple and easy to implement, making it accessible for practical applications in FL.
- The paper provides a comprehensive overview of the current state of research on BN in FL, highlighting the gaps in the current understanding and proposing a new perspective on the use of BN in FL.
- The paper is well-written, with clear explanations of the experimental results and the proposed method, making it accessible and understandable to a broad audience.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a comprehensive theoretical analysis of the proposed method, FixBN, which could strengthen the paper's contribution to the field.
- The experiments are limited to specific datasets and network architectures, which might not generalize well to other settings or architectures.
- The paper does not provide a detailed discussion on the potential negative societal impacts of the proposed method, which could be a significant limitation in the context of FL.
- The paper could benefit from a more detailed discussion on the limitations of the proposed method and potential future directions for research.
- There is a lack of comparison with other normalization methods, which could provide a more robust evaluation of the proposed method.
- The paper does not discuss the impact of the proposed method on the convergence rate of FL algorithms, which is a critical aspect of FL performance.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors provide a more detailed theoretical analysis of the proposed method, FixBN, to strengthen the paper's contribution to the field?
- How does the proposed method perform in other FL settings or with different network architectures, and can the authors provide more comprehensive experimental results to support the generalizability of the findings?
- Could the authors discuss the potential negative societal impacts of the proposed method in the context of FL, and propose strategies to mitigate these impacts?
- How does the proposed method compare with other normalization methods, and what are the advantages or disadvantages of using FixBN over other methods in FL?
- Can the authors provide more details on the impact of the proposed method on the convergence rate of FL algorithms, and discuss how this might affect the practical applicability of the method in FL settings?
