 **Summary:**
The paper introduces EduGym, a novel educational tool designed to facilitate the learning of reinforcement learning (RL) through a series of interactive notebooks and customizable environments. EduGym aims to simplify the learning process by focusing on specific challenges, such as exploration, credit assignment, and state/action spaces, using low-dimensional environments and tabular algorithms. The tool is evaluated among students and researchers, demonstrating its effectiveness in enhancing conceptual and practical understanding. However, the paper lacks a rigorous experimental setup and a comprehensive literature review, which could have strengthened its claims.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel and well-motivated approach to simplifying the learning of reinforcement learning (RL) through the creation of EduGym, a set of educational RL environments and interactive notebooks.
- The paper is well-written, easy to follow, and provides clear explanations of the challenges and solutions, making it accessible to a broad audience.
- EduGym addresses a significant gap in RL education by providing a simplified environment for learning RL, which is crucial for students entering the field.
- The paper includes a comprehensive evaluation of EduGym among students and researchers, demonstrating its effectiveness in enhancing both conceptual and practical understanding of RL.
- The interactive notebooks and environments are designed to be easily customizable, allowing for the exploration of different RL concepts and their implementation.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a rigorous experimental setup and quantitative evaluation, which could have strengthened its claims about the effectiveness of EduGym.
- The literature review is inadequate, missing out on significant related works that could have provided a richer context for the development of EduGym.
- The paper does not discuss the limitations of EduGym, which could help in understanding its scope and applicability.
- There is a lack of discussion on how EduGym compares to other educational tools or frameworks, which could provide a more comprehensive view of its contributions.
- The paper does not include a detailed algorithmic description for each environment, which could hinder the reproducibility and understanding of the results.
- The evaluation of EduGym is limited to a small sample size, which may not be representative of a broader audience.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors provide more details on the experimental setup and quantitative evaluation of EduGym to substantiate its claims?
- How does EduGym compare to other educational tools or frameworks in terms of effectiveness and accessibility?
- Can the authors discuss the limitations of EduGym and how they plan to address these in future developments?
- How does the choice of low-dimensional environments and tabular algorithms contribute to the learning process, and what are the potential drawbacks of this approach?
- Could the authors provide a more detailed algorithmic description for each environment to enhance reproducibility and understanding?
- How does the evaluation of EduGym among students and researchers translate into broader applicability and effectiveness in real-world educational settings?
