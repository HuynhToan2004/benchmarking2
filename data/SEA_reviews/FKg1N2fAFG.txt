 **Summary:**
The paper addresses the issue of architecture overfitting in dataset distillation, proposing a method that combines DropPath, knowledge distillation, and stronger data augmentation to improve generalization performance across different network architectures. The approach aims to enhance the performance of test networks trained on distilled data, mitigating the issue of architecture-specific performance degradation. Extensive experiments are conducted to demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed method across various scenarios, including different sizes of distilled data and different network architectures. However, the paper's contribution is seen as incremental, primarily applying existing techniques without significant innovation. The experimental results, while showing improvements, are not convincingly superior to existing methods, and the paper's writing quality needs improvement.

**Strengths:**
- The paper addresses an important and relevant problem in dataset distillation, focusing on the issue of architecture overfitting.
- It proposes a comprehensive approach that includes architecture design, training schemes, and data augmentation to mitigate architecture overfitting.
- The methodology is well-articulated, and the paper is easy to follow, with clear explanations and illustrations that aid in understanding the proposed methods.
- Extensive experiments are conducted to demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed method across various scenarios, including different sizes of distilled data, different network architectures, and different dataset distillation methods.
- The paper includes an ablation study that provides insights into the effectiveness of each component of the proposed method.

**Weaknesses:**
- The contribution of the paper is seen as incremental, primarily applying existing techniques without significant innovation.
- The experimental results do not convincingly demonstrate superior performance over existing methods, with only marginal improvements reported in some cases.
- The paper lacks clarity in explaining the main contributions and the rationale behind the proposed methods, particularly in the introduction and the related work section.
- The writing quality needs improvement, with some sections being difficult to follow and figures being poorly labeled.
- The paper does not adequately address the limitations of the proposed methods, nor does it compare the proposed method with the latest state-of-the-art methods in dataset distillation.
- The paper does not sufficiently discuss the computational cost and scalability of the proposed method, which are crucial for practical applications.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors clarify the main contributions of the paper and how they differ from existing methods?
- How does the proposed method compare with the latest state-of-the-art methods in dataset distillation?
- Can the authors provide more detailed explanations and illustrations to clarify the proposed methods, particularly in the related work and introduction sections?
- How does the proposed method address the computational cost and scalability issues in practical applications?
- Can the authors provide more insights into the limitations of the proposed method and potential areas for future research?
