 **Summary:**
The paper introduces the Heterogeneous-Agent Soft Actor-Critic (HASAC) algorithm, which aims to address challenges in multi-agent reinforcement learning (MARL) such as sample complexity, training instability, and the risk of converging to a suboptimal Nash Equilibrium. By embedding cooperative MARL problems into probabilistic graphical models, the authors derive the maximum entropy (MaxEnt) objective for MARL, which is used to develop the HASAC algorithm. This algorithm is shown to converge to quantal response equilibrium (QRE) properties and is evaluated against six benchmarks, demonstrating superior performance compared to strong baselines. The paper also proposes a unified template for MaxEnt algorithmic design, named Maximum Entropy Heterogeneous-Agent Mirror Learning (MEHAML), which provides theoretical guarantees for any induced method.

**Strengths:**
- The paper provides a novel approach to multi-agent reinforcement learning (MARL) by learning stochastic policies, which is a significant departure from traditional methods that often converge to suboptimal Nash Equilibrium (NE).
- The introduction of the MaxEnt objective and the derivation of the HASAC algorithm from this objective are innovative and theoretically sound.
- The experimental results demonstrate the superiority of the HASAC algorithm over strong baselines, showing improvements in sample efficiency, robustness, and exploration.
- The paper is well-written, with clear explanations of the theoretical concepts and practical applications.
- The authors have effectively demonstrated the limitations of existing MARL methods and provided a clear rationale for the proposed solution.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper does not sufficiently discuss the limitations of the HASAC algorithm, which could help in understanding its applicability and potential drawbacks.
- There is a lack of clarity on the computational complexity of the proposed method, which is crucial for understanding its scalability and practical implementation.
- The experimental results, while showing improvements, do not convincingly demonstrate the superiority of the HASAC algorithm across all benchmarks.
- The paper could benefit from a more detailed discussion on how the proposed method addresses the specific challenges in MARL, such as sample complexity and training instability.
- The theoretical analysis and proofs, while sound, could be made more accessible to a broader audience by including more intuitive explanations and simplified mathematical derivations.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors provide a more detailed discussion on the limitations of the HASAC algorithm and under what conditions it may not perform optimally?
- How does the computational complexity of the proposed method compare to existing methods, and what are the implications for scalability?
- In the experimental results, why does the HASAC algorithm not perform as well on all benchmarks? Is there a specific scenario where it underperforms?
- Could the authors clarify the role and impact of the temperature parameter α on the performance of the HASAC algorithm?
- How does the proposed method compare to other state-of-the-art MARL algorithms in terms of computational efficiency and resource requirements?
