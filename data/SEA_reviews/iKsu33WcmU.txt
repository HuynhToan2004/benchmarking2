 **Summary:**
The paper introduces ProtChatGPT, a system that integrates pre-trained protein encoders with a protein-language pre-training transformer (PLP-former) and a multi-level projection adapter to enable natural language processing for protein research. This system allows users to input protein structures and ask questions, which are answered by the model using a frozen LLM. The model's effectiveness is demonstrated through experiments, showing that it can generate accurate and relevant responses, although there are concerns about its novelty and the depth of its contributions to the field.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, clear, and easy to follow, with a clear motivation for the proposed method.
- The proposed method is innovative, integrating pre-trained protein encoders with a protein-language pre-training transformer (PLP-former) and a multi-level projection adapter to enhance the understanding of protein structures through natural language.
- The experiments demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed method, showing that it can generate accurate and relevant responses to user queries.
- The system is designed to be user-friendly, allowing users to input protein structures and ask questions, which are answered by the model using a frozen LLM.
- The paper provides a detailed explanation of the methodology and the experimental setup, which helps in understanding the contributions and the results.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed discussion on how the PLP-former aligns protein structures with text descriptions, and the role of the adapter in this alignment process is not clearly explained.
- The novelty of the approach is limited, as the integration of pre-trained protein encoders and the use of a protein-language pre-training transformer are common in similar systems.
- The paper does not adequately discuss the limitations of the proposed method, such as its applicability to other types of biological data or the potential for misinformation due to the use of large language models.
- The performance of the model is not convincingly superior to existing methods, and the comparison with other models is not sufficiently detailed.
- The paper does not provide a detailed analysis of the model's performance on specific types of proteins or in different scenarios, which could help in understanding the model's strengths and weaknesses.
- The paper lacks a discussion on the societal impact of the proposed method, particularly in terms of its potential misuse or negative consequences.

**Questions:**
- Can you provide more details on how the PLP-former aligns protein structures with text descriptions, and how the adapter contributes to this alignment?
- How does the model handle misinformation or incorrect data, especially since it uses a large language model that could potentially generate incorrect responses?
- Could you provide a more detailed comparison with other models, including a discussion on how your model performs in different scenarios or with different types of data?
- How does the model handle scenarios where the protein data is incomplete or of low quality?
- Can you provide more examples of user questions and the corresponding answers to better understand the model's capabilities and limitations?
- What are the specific types of proteins that the model is best suited for, and how does it perform in scenarios where the protein data is not well-characterized?
- How does the model ensure that the generated responses are scientifically valid and accurate, especially in the biomedical domain?
