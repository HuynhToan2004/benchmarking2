 **Summary:**
The paper explores the application of Markov chain Monte Carlo (MCMC) techniques in achieving pure differential privacy (pure DP) in the context of empirical risk minimization (ERM). It introduces a novel algorithm called ASAP, which perturbs MCMC samples with noise proportional to their Wasserstein-infinity distance from a reference distribution that satisfies pure DP. The authors demonstrate that this algorithm converges in Wasserstein-infinity distance and achieves optimal rates in the DP-ERM problem with strongly convex and smooth losses. The paper also provides a systematic method for converting total variation distance into Wasserstein-infinity distance, which is crucial for maintaining pure DP guarantees. Despite its technical contributions, the paper faces criticism for its clarity, particularly in the presentation of algorithms and theoretical results.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, making the content accessible and understandable, even for those not deeply familiar with the field.
- The problem addressed is of significant interest, focusing on the application of MCMC techniques to achieve pure differential privacy (pure DP) in the context of empirical risk minimization (ERM), which is a challenging and open problem.
- The authors provide a novel algorithm, ASAP, which achieves optimal rates in the DP-ERM problem with strongly convex and smooth losses, and the algorithm is shown to converge in Wasserstein-infinity distance.
- The paper introduces a generic lemma that converts total variation distance into Wasserstein-infinity distance, which is a significant contribution to the field.
- The paper provides a nearly linear-time algorithm for DP-ERM under pure DP and pure Gaussian DP, which is a significant improvement over previous works.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks clarity in the presentation of algorithms and theoretical results, making it difficult to follow the main results and the significance of the contributions.
- There is a lack of discussion on the limitations of the proposed algorithm, particularly in terms of its applicability to other settings such as DP-SCO.
- The paper could benefit from a clearer explanation of the main results and the significance of the contributions, especially in the abstract and introduction sections.
- There are numerous typographical and formatting errors throughout the paper, which detract from its professionalism and readability.
- The paper does not sufficiently distinguish its contributions from existing works, particularly in terms of the algorithmic contributions and the novelty of the results.
