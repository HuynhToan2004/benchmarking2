 **Summary:**
The paper introduces AutoAgents, a novel framework for generating multiple specialized agents to tackle complex tasks. It leverages large language models (LLMs) to dynamically generate agents based on task content, planning solutions, and refining them through self-refinement and collaborative refinement. The framework is evaluated on two benchmarks, demonstrating improvements over existing methods. Despite its innovative approach, the paper is criticized for its clarity, lack of novelty, and limited comparative analysis with existing methods. The paper also faces criticism for its presentation, particularly in the organization and clarity of its content.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written and easy to follow, with clear explanations of the proposed methods and their applications.
- The problem addressed is significant, focusing on the generation of diverse agents with different roles, which is a novel approach in the field of multi-agent systems.
- The paper includes comprehensive experiments that demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed methods, supported by detailed ablation studies and comparisons with existing methods.
- The proposed framework is generalizable and applicable to various tasks, showing potential for broader applications in the field.
- The paper includes a detailed appendix that provides additional insights and examples, enhancing the understanding of the proposed methods.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks clarity in its presentation, particularly in the organization and explanation of its content. Key concepts and methodologies are introduced without adequate explanation, making it difficult for readers to fully grasp the contributions and implications of the work.
- The novelty of the paper is questioned as it appears to be an extension of existing works such as SSP and AgentVerse, without sufficient differentiation or innovation.
- The experimental setup and results are not convincing, with some results showing marginal improvements or inconsistencies with previous studies.
- The paper suffers from a lack of clarity in the definition and role of the agents generated, particularly in how they are integrated into the system and how they contribute to the overall performance.
- The paper does not adequately address the limitations of its approach, such as the potential for over-specialization or the scalability of the framework in more complex or diverse scenarios.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors provide a more detailed explanation of the "Plan Observer" and its role in the system? How does it contribute to the overall performance of the framework?
- How are the agents generated by the framework evaluated for their quality and effectiveness? Are there specific metrics or criteria used for this evaluation?
- Can the authors clarify the role and integration of the Action Observer in the system? How does it coordinate and manage the interactions between different agents?
- How does the framework handle scenarios where the generated agents do not perform optimally or where the plan execution fails?
- Could the authors provide more detailed examples or case studies to illustrate the practical applications and benefits of the proposed framework in real-world scenarios?
- How does the framework ensure the generalizability and adaptability of the generated agents to different tasks or domains?
