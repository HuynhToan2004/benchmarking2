 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel diffusion-based model for end-to-end path planning, which incorporates constraints from road networks and learns path patterns via a diffusion process. This model, termed Graph-constrained Diffusion for Planning (GDP), aims to bypass traditional search-based frameworks and instead uses a diffusion process to plan paths based on origin and destination as prior evidence. The model's efficacy is demonstrated through experiments on real-world datasets, showing superior performance compared to strong baselines. However, the paper is critiqued for its lack of detailed experimental setup and comparative analysis, particularly in terms of computational efficiency and the handling of different road network constraints.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel diffusion-based model for end-to-end path planning, which effectively learns path patterns via a diffusion process and incorporates constraints from road networks.
- The model is capable of unconditional path generation, which could be useful in various applications.
- The paper is well-written and easy to follow, with clear explanations of the methodology and the experimental setup.
- The proposed diffusion-based path planning model is novel and demonstrates strong performance, as shown through experiments on real-world datasets.
- The paper provides a detailed description of the diffusion process and its application to path planning, which is both novel and effective.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks detailed information about the experimental setup, including the number of paths sampled and the computational efficiency of the proposed method.
- The paper does not sufficiently compare its method with existing diffusion models, which could have provided a clearer picture of its advantages and disadvantages.
- There is a lack of discussion on the handling of different types of constraints in road networks, such as one-way streets and turn restrictions.
- The paper does not adequately address the scalability of the proposed method, particularly in terms of the size and complexity of the road network graphs.
- The paper does not provide a detailed comparison with existing methods in terms of computational efficiency and scalability, which are crucial for practical applications.
- The paper does not provide a detailed analysis of the limitations of the proposed method, which could help in understanding its scope and applicability.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors provide more details about the experimental setup, such as the number of paths sampled and the computational efficiency of the proposed method?
- How does the proposed method handle different types of constraints in road networks, such as one-way streets and turn restrictions?
- Can the authors provide a detailed comparison with existing diffusion models in terms of performance and computational efficiency?
- How does the proposed method scale with the size and complexity of the road network graphs?
- Can the authors provide a detailed analysis of the limitations of the proposed method, including its applicability to different types of road networks and its scalability?
- How does the proposed method compare with existing search-based algorithms in terms of performance and computational efficiency?
