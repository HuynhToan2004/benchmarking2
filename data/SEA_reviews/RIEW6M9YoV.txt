 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel graph generation method using K2-trees, which represent graphs in a hierarchical, compact manner. The method involves a transformer-based architecture that generates a sequence from the K2-tree representation, leveraging positional encodings for effective graph generation. The approach is evaluated against various benchmarks and datasets, demonstrating competitive performance and efficiency. The method's effectiveness is highlighted by its ability to generate high-quality graphs with fewer computational resources compared to existing methods. However, concerns were raised regarding the method's scalability and the clarity of its novelty, particularly in comparison to existing graph compression techniques.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel approach to graph generation using K2-trees, which is both innovative and effective.
- The method is well-motivated and the experiments are well-designed, showing good results and efficiency.
- The paper is well-written, making it easy to follow, and the methodology is clearly explained.
- The approach is flexible and can be applied to various types of graphs, including attributed graphs, which enhances its applicability.
- The paper provides a thorough ablation study, which helps in understanding the impact of different components of the proposed method.
- The proposed method shows good performance and efficiency compared to existing methods, demonstrating its effectiveness in generating high-quality graphs.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed comparison with existing graph compression methods, which could have provided a clearer context for the proposed method's novelty and effectiveness.
- There is a lack of discussion on the limitations of the method, which is crucial for understanding its applicability and potential drawbacks.
- The paper could benefit from more comprehensive comparisons with other state-of-the-art methods, particularly in terms of performance metrics and computational efficiency.
- The scalability of the method is a concern, as the performance of the method on larger graphs is not addressed.
- The paper does not sufficiently address the potential societal impact of the proposed method, which could be a significant consideration for many applications.
- The novelty of the method is questionable as it appears to be a combination of existing techniques without significant innovation.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors provide a more detailed comparison with existing graph compression methods, particularly in terms of performance and efficiency?
- How does the proposed method compare to other state-of-the-art methods in terms of computational efficiency and performance metrics?
- Can the authors discuss the limitations of their method and potential applications where it might not be suitable?
- How does the method handle the scalability issue, particularly in generating graphs with a large number of nodes?
- Could the authors clarify the novelty of their method and how it differs from existing graph generation techniques?
- Is there a potential for the proposed method to be used in other domains beyond graph generation, such as image or language modeling?
