 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel type of adversarial attack, termed calibration attacks, which aim to manipulate the confidence scores of classifiers without affecting their accuracy. The attacks are designed to mislead models by either under-confusing or over-confusing them, and are evaluated against various models and datasets. The paper proposes two defense mechanisms to mitigate these attacks, and provides a comprehensive experimental evaluation demonstrating the effectiveness of the attacks and the proposed defenses. However, the novelty of the attacks is questioned, as similar methods have been previously explored, and the paper lacks theoretical justification for the proposed methods.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a new type of adversarial attack, calibration attacks, which are both theoretically and experimentally sound.
- The authors provide a comprehensive experimental evaluation of the proposed attacks, demonstrating their effectiveness across various models and datasets.
- The paper is well-written, making it easy to follow, and the experiments are extensive, including a variety of attack methods and defenses.
- The proposed defenses are evaluated against a range of existing defenses, providing a robust comparison.
- The paper addresses a significant problem in the field of adversarial attacks, specifically focusing on calibration attacks, which have been understudied.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks novelty in its attacks, as similar methods have been previously explored, and the theoretical justification for the proposed methods is insufficient.
- There is a lack of clarity in the paper, particularly in the definitions and explanations of key terms and methods, such as the "miscalibration function" and the "Square Attack."
- The paper does not adequately address the computational costs associated with the proposed attacks and defenses, which could limit their practical applicability.
- The experimental results are not convincingly superior to existing methods, and the paper does not sufficiently discuss the limitations of its proposed methods.
- The paper does not provide sufficient theoretical justification for the proposed defenses, and the experimental results do not convincingly demonstrate their superiority over existing methods.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors clarify the definitions and explanations of key terms and methods, particularly the "miscalibration function" and the "Square Attack"?
- How do the authors justify the choice of the "miscalibration function" in their attacks, and what are the theoretical underpinnings of this choice?
- Can the authors provide a more detailed comparison of the computational costs associated with their attacks and defenses compared to existing methods?
- In the context of the proposed defenses, how do the authors ensure that the defenses are not only effective against calibration attacks but also against other types of attacks?
- Could the authors discuss the limitations of their proposed methods and potential avenues for future research to address these limitations?
