 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a learning effort framework aimed at optimizing meta-learning strategies by controlling various aspects of the learning process, such as learning rates and task engagement, to maximize cumulative rewards. The framework is tested on a range of tasks, including linear networks and meta-learning algorithms, to investigate the impact of different control signals on learning dynamics. The paper proposes a normative approach that guides the learning process by adjusting parameters like learning rates and task engagement, aiming to improve overall performance. Despite its theoretical rigor and the exploration of a novel framework, the paper has been criticized for its limited empirical validation, particularly its reliance on simple linear models and a lack of comparison with more complex or non-linear models.

**Strengths:**
- The paper presents a novel framework for studying optimal meta-learning strategies, which is a significant contribution to the field.
- The framework is well-articulated and the paper is generally clear, with clear illustrations and a clear presentation of the results.
- The theoretical analysis is solid, and the empirical results are convincing, showing a good match between theory and practice.
- The paper addresses an important problem of understanding the optimal meta-learning strategies, which is crucial for both biological and artificial learning agents.
- The approach is flexible and can be applied to various meta-learning scenarios, providing a unified framework for studying different aspects of meta-learning.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper primarily focuses on linear models, which may limit the generalizability of the findings to more complex or non-linear models.
- There is a lack of comparison with other meta-learning algorithms, which could provide a more comprehensive understanding of the proposed framework's effectiveness.
- The paper does not sufficiently discuss the limitations of the linear models used, which could affect the validity of the results.
- The empirical validation is limited, with only a few datasets used, which might not be sufficient to establish the robustness of the findings.
- The paper does not adequately discuss the limitations of the work, which could provide a more balanced view of the contributions and challenges of the research.
- There is a lack of discussion on how the findings might be extended to other types of neural networks or learning scenarios, which could limit the applicability of the research.

**Questions:**
- How does the proposed framework compare to other meta-learning algorithms, particularly in terms of performance and efficiency?
- Could the authors provide more detailed examples or case studies to illustrate the practical applications of the learning effort framework in real-world scenarios?
- How does the learning effort framework handle the trade-off between exploration and exploitation in meta-learning, especially in scenarios where the cost of exploration might outweigh the potential rewards?
- What are the implications of using different discount factors in the framework, and how do these choices affect the learning dynamics and outcomes?
- How does the framework handle scenarios where the learning dynamics are not fully tractable, and what are the potential solutions or approximations in such cases?
- Can the authors clarify the relationship between the control signal and the learning dynamics, particularly how the control signal influences the learning process and the learning dynamics?
- How does the framework handle the optimization of multiple control signals simultaneously, and what are the potential challenges or limitations in such scenarios?
