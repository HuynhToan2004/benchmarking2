 **Summary:**
The paper introduces Neural Spectral Methods (NSM), a novel approach to solve parametric Partial Differential Equations (PDEs) by representing the solution as a series of orthogonal basis functions, which are then parameterized by a neural network. This method leverages Parseval's identity to compute the residual norm in the spectral domain, avoiding the need for sampling a large number of points and numerical quadrature. The authors claim that NSM is faster and more accurate than traditional methods like Physics-Informed Neural Networks (PINNs) and other neural operators. The paper provides theoretical and experimental evidence to support these claims, demonstrating significant improvements in computational efficiency and accuracy.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel method for solving parametric PDEs using neural spectral methods, which is a significant advancement in the field of scientific computing.
- The methodology is well-explained, making the paper accessible to a broad audience, including those not specialized in the field.
- The paper is well-written, with clear and concise language that aids in understanding complex concepts.
- The proposed method is shown to be more efficient and accurate than existing methods like FNO, particularly in terms of computational speed and accuracy.
- The paper provides a comprehensive analysis of the method's advantages over traditional methods like PINNs and other neural operators, highlighting the method's potential for broader applications.
- The paper is well-structured, with clear sections that effectively guide the reader through the methodology and results.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed discussion on the computational complexity of the proposed method, which is crucial for understanding its practical applicability.
- There is a lack of clarity regarding the choice of basis functions and their impact on the method's performance. The paper could benefit from a more detailed explanation of how different basis functions affect the results.
- The paper does not provide sufficient experimental evidence to support the claims of efficiency and accuracy, particularly in more complex or realistic scenarios.
- The paper could benefit from a more detailed comparison with other state-of-the-art methods, such as the Spectral Neural Operator (SNO), to establish the superiority of the proposed method more convincingly.
- The paper does not discuss the potential limitations or drawbacks of the proposed method, which could help in better understanding its applicability and effectiveness.
- The paper could benefit from a more detailed discussion on the scalability of the method, particularly in terms of the complexity of the PDEs that can be effectively solved using NSM.

**Questions:**

- How does the choice of basis functions impact the performance of the method, and can different basis functions be used interchangeably?
- Could the authors provide a more detailed comparison with other state-of-the-art methods, such as SNO, to establish the superiority of the proposed method more convincingly?
- How does the proposed method perform in more complex or realistic scenarios, such as those involving high-order derivatives or non-linear PDEs?
- Could the authors discuss the potential limitations or drawbacks of the proposed method, and how these might affect its practical applicability?
- How does the method handle different types of boundary conditions, particularly those that are not periodic?
