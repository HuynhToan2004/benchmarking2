 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel post-training quantization method for large language models (LLMs), specifically focusing on a W4A8 quantization scheme that combines the advantages of W8A8 and W4A16. This methodology involves layer-wise activation quantization strategies and a logarithmic equalization technique to manage activation ranges effectively. The paper demonstrates that this approach achieves state-of-the-art W4A8 performance on benchmarks like BLOOM, LLaMA, and LLaMA-2, without requiring fine-tuning. The methodology is supported by extensive experiments and ablation studies, showing improvements over existing methods like SmoothQuant and GPTQ. However, the paper has been criticized for not providing detailed implementation details, such as the calibration dataset used and the hyperparameters for quantization.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel W4A8 quantization scheme for large language models (LLMs), which is a significant advancement in the field.
- The methodology is well-articulated, with clear explanations of the proposed techniques and their implementation.
- The paper demonstrates the effectiveness of the proposed method through extensive experiments and ablation studies, showing improvements over existing methods like SmoothQuant and GPTQ.
- The approach is innovative, particularly in its use of layer-wise activation quantization strategies and logarithmic equalization to manage activation ranges.
- The paper is well-written, making it accessible and understandable to a broad audience.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks detailed implementation details, such as the calibration dataset used and the hyperparameters for quantization, which could affect the reproducibility and comparability of the results.
- There is a significant performance drop in the W4A8 setting compared to W8A8, which might limit the practical application of the proposed method.
- The paper does not provide a detailed analysis of the computational efficiency or memory usage of the proposed method, which are crucial for deploying LLMs in real-world scenarios.
- The paper does not discuss the limitations of the proposed method, which could provide valuable insights for future research and improvements.
- There is a lack of discussion on the hardware and software requirements for implementing the proposed method, which could limit the accessibility of the research to practitioners.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors provide more details on the calibration dataset used and the hyperparameters for quantization to ensure reproducibility and comparability of the results?
- How does the proposed method perform in terms of computational efficiency and memory usage compared to other methods like SmoothQuant and GPTQ?
- Can the authors discuss the limitations of the proposed method and suggest potential areas for future research and improvements?
- What are the hardware and software requirements for implementing the proposed method, and how accessible is the research to practitioners?
