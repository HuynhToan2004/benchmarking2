 **Summary:**
The paper investigates sample-efficient learning in POMDPs by proposing a new feedback model, k-MOMDP, which allows for the collection of additional observations from the latent states after each episode. This model is used to define two new classes of POMDPs: k-MO revealing POMDPs and distinguishable POMDPs. The paper introduces algorithms such as k-OMLE and OST to learn these POMDPs efficiently, demonstrating theoretical guarantees for their sample complexity. The authors provide a comprehensive theoretical analysis, including proofs of the sample complexity of their algorithms and discuss the implications of their work in relation to existing models like HOMDPs.
