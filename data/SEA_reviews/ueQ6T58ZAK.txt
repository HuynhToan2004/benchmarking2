 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel approach to model optimal transport (OT) as an ensemble control system, leveraging moment representations to train the system's parameters. This method allows for the representation of OT as a dynamic process, which is particularly useful for learning control systems. The paper provides examples and simulations to demonstrate the application of this approach, showing that it can effectively model OT processes. However, concerns are raised about the practical applicability of the method, as it may not be feasible for real-world problems due to the complexity and computational demands of the moment representation. Additionally, the paper's presentation could be improved to better clarify its contributions and the challenges associated with its implementation.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel approach to model optimal transport (OT) as an ensemble control system, which is a significant contribution to the field.
- The use of moment representation to parameterize the model and ensemble system is a clever and innovative approach, enhancing the paper's methodological contribution.
- The paper is well-written and easy to follow, making it accessible to a broad audience.
- The application of OT to ensemble systems is a new and interesting direction, which could have significant implications for various fields.
- The paper provides a clear motivation for the work and presents a novel framework for modeling OT as an ensemble control system, which is a significant advancement in the field.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper does not sufficiently address the practical applicability of the proposed method, particularly in real-world scenarios. It is unclear how the method can be scaled to handle more complex or high-dimensional problems.
- The presentation of the paper could be improved to better highlight the contributions and challenges of the proposed method. Specific sections, such as the introduction and the explanation of the moment representation, could be clearer and more detailed.
- There is a lack of discussion on the computational complexity and scalability of the method, which is crucial for understanding its practical feasibility.
- The paper does not adequately compare its method with existing approaches, which limits the assessment of its novelty and effectiveness.
- The paper does not provide a clear discussion on the limitations of the method, which could help in understanding its scope and applicability.
