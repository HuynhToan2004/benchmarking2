 **Summary:**
The paper introduces the Universal Sleep Decoder (USD), a novel model designed to align neural representations between wakefulness and sleep across subjects, utilizing a well-annotated electroencephalography (EEG) dataset from 52 subjects during both wakefulness and sleep. The USD leverages a pretrain-finetune paradigm and contrastive loss to integrate awake and sleep neural signals, achieving significant improvements in sleep decoding performance. The model's zero-shot accuracy on unseen subjects is notable, demonstrating a substantial advancement in the field of sleep decoding. Despite the innovative approach and the potential impact on neuroscience research, concerns were raised regarding the limited size of the dataset, the lack of comparison with other datasets, and the generalizability of the findings.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel cognitive neuroscience experiment that yields a comprehensive, well-annotated EEG dataset from 52 subjects during both wakefulness and sleep, which is crucial for sleep decoding research.
- The Universal Sleep Decoder (USD) is a significant contribution, demonstrating subject-agnostic features that can be applied to other types of brain recordings with high temporal resolution, such as magnetoencephalography (MEG) or stereo-electroencephalography (sEEG).
- The paper is well-written, clear, and easy to follow, making it accessible to a broad audience, including both neuroscientists and machine learning researchers.
- The authors have collected a substantial amount of EEG data from 52 subjects, which is a significant contribution to the field of sleep decoding.
- The paper presents a novel approach to align neural representations between wakefulness and sleep, which is a significant advancement in the field of sleep decoding.
- The results are promising, with the USD achieving up to 16.6% top-1 zero-shot accuracy on unseen subjects, which is comparable to decoding performances using individual sleep data.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed description of the dataset, including the number of channels, sampling rate, and the number of trials for each condition.
- There is a lack of comparison with other datasets, which limits the generalizability of the findings.
- The paper does not discuss the limitations of the study, which is crucial for understanding the scope and applicability of the results.
- The paper does not provide a clear rationale for the choice of the pretrain-finetune paradigm and the contrastive loss, which are key components of the model.
- The paper does not discuss the ethical implications of the study, which is essential for ensuring the ethical conduct of research involving human subjects.
- The paper does not include a comparison of the USD with other sleep decoders, which is necessary to establish the superiority of the USD.
- The paper does not discuss the potential impact of the findings on real-world applications, which is crucial for understanding the practical implications of the research.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors provide more details about the dataset, such as the number of channels, sampling rate, and the number of trials for each condition?
- How do the authors ensure the quality and reliability of the EEG data collected during sleep, especially given the challenges of collecting high-quality data during this phase?
- Could the authors clarify the rationale behind the choice of the pretrain-finetune paradigm and the contrastive loss?
- How do the authors ensure the generalizability of the findings across different datasets and conditions?
- Could the authors discuss the limitations of the study and the potential implications of these limitations for the generalizability and applicability of the findings?
- How do the authors plan to address the ethical implications of the study, particularly in terms of ensuring the privacy and confidentiality of the data and the informed consent of the participants?
- Could the authors provide more details about the experimental setup, including the specifics of the TMR paradigm and the stimuli used?
- How do the authors plan to make the dataset and the code publicly available to facilitate reproducibility and further research in the field?
