 **Summary:**
The paper investigates the use of subspace activation patching in large language models (LLMs), focusing on the potential for interpreting model behavior through linear subspaces within the activation space. The authors demonstrate that naive approaches to subspace interventions can lead to interpretability illusions, where activating a dormant parallel pathway through a component disconnected from the model output can appear to affect model behavior. The paper presents a mathematical example and empirical evidence from two settings, including the Indirect Object Identification (IOI) task and factual recall, to support this observation. It also discusses how these findings could influence the understanding and application of mechanistic interpretability in LLMs.

**Strengths:**
- The paper provides a clear and intuitive explanation of the phenomenon, using a mathematical example to illustrate the concept of interpretability illusion.
- The authors demonstrate the phenomenon in two different settings, showing that the findings are not limited to a single context.
- The paper is well-written, making it accessible and easy to follow, with clear explanations of the experiments and results.
- The findings are relevant to the community, particularly in the context of mechanistic interpretability and the use of subspace activation patching in LLMs.
- The authors discuss the limitations of their work and provide suggestions for future research, which enhances the paper's credibility and potential impact.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper could benefit from a broader range of experimental settings to validate the generalizability of the findings.
- There is a lack of quantitative analysis or formal proof to support the claims made about the prevalence of the illusion in real-world language models.
- The paper does not provide a clear explanation of why the illusion occurs, which could limit the understanding and applicability of the findings.
- The paper could benefit from more detailed discussions on the implications of the findings for practical applications, such as in causal inference or model development.
- The paper's claims about the dormant pathway and its relationship to the model's behavior are not convincingly supported by the data presented.
- The paper could benefit from more robust experimental validation, particularly in terms of the statistical significance and reliability of the results.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors provide more details on how the experiments were conducted, particularly in terms of the statistical significance and reliability of the results?
- How do the authors address the potential for overfitting in their experiments, especially given the small dataset used in some cases?
- Could the authors clarify the relationship between the dormant pathway and the model's behavior, perhaps by providing additional experimental evidence or theoretical insights?
- What are the implications of the findings for practical applications, such as in causal inference or model development?
- Could the authors elaborate on the concept of a "dormant pathway" and its role in the model's behavior, particularly in relation to the causal disconnectedness and the null space of the downstream model components?
- How do the authors envision the practical use of their findings in real-world scenarios, and what steps might be necessary to apply these insights effectively?
