 **Summary:**
The paper introduces SafeClip, a method designed to enhance the robustness of CLIP models against targeted data poisoning and backdoor attacks during pre-training. SafeClip employs a two-step process: first, it performs unimodal contrastive learning to initialize the model, followed by a slow-paced CLIP warmup to fine-tune the model. The method is evaluated against targeted data poisoning and backdoor attacks, demonstrating its effectiveness in reducing the attack success rate to zero without compromising CLIP's performance. The paper also includes an ablation study to validate the effectiveness of different components of SafeClip.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-organized, easy to follow, and clearly explains the proposed method.
- The method is evaluated against two different types of attacks, demonstrating its effectiveness.
- The proposed method is simple, intuitive, and does not require additional training data or hyperparameters, making it practical for real-world applications.
- The paper includes an ablation study that provides insights into the effectiveness of different components of the proposed method.
- The method is shown to be effective in reducing the attack success rate to zero without compromising CLIP's performance on various datasets.
- The paper provides a clear motivation and a novel approach to defending against backdoor attacks during pre-training, which is a significant contribution to the field.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed comparison with other defense methods, such as those mentioned in related work.
- The experimental setup is limited to a single dataset and a single model architecture, which may not generalize well to other datasets or architectures.
- The paper does not provide a comprehensive analysis of the computational cost of the proposed method, which could be a significant concern for practical applications.
- The paper does not include a discussion on the potential negative societal impacts of the proposed method.
- The method's effectiveness is not thoroughly analyzed in terms of its ability to defend against different types of attacks or different attack rates.
- The paper does not discuss the potential limitations of the method, such as its effectiveness against more sophisticated or varied attacks.
- The method's reliance on a slow-paced CLIP warmup epoch and a small increment ratio for updating safe data may limit its practical applicability in real-world scenarios.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors provide a detailed comparison of their method with other defense methods mentioned in the related work?
- How does the proposed method perform against more sophisticated or varied attacks?
- Can the authors provide more details on the computational cost of the proposed method and discuss potential strategies for reducing this cost?
- How does the proposed method perform when the attack rate is higher than 0.05%?
- Can the authors discuss the potential negative societal impacts of the proposed method and propose strategies for mitigating these impacts?
- Can the authors provide more details on the method's performance against different types of attacks, such as untargeted attacks or those that use different types of triggers?
