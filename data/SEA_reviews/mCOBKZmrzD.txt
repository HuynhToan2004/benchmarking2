 **Summary:**
The paper introduces EquiformerV2, an improved version of the Equiformer model designed to handle higher-degree representations in 3D atomistic systems. EquiformerV2 incorporates eSCN convolutions, attention re-normalization, separable S2 activation, and separable layer normalization to enhance its performance and scalability. The model is tested on various datasets, including OC20 and OC22, demonstrating significant improvements in force and energy predictions. The authors claim that EquiformerV2 outperforms previous models in terms of accuracy and efficiency, although concerns are raised about the clarity of some technical details and the generalizability of the model to other datasets.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces EquiformerV2, an improved version of Equiformer that addresses the scalability issue in handling higher-degree representations, which is crucial for accurate predictions in 3D atomistic systems.
- The authors provide a detailed description of the proposed method, including a thorough explanation of the eSCN convolution, attention re-normalization, separable S2 activation, and separable layer normalization, which are critical for the model's performance.
- The experiments conducted on the OC20 and OC22 datasets demonstrate the effectiveness of EquiformerV2, showing significant improvements in force and energy predictions compared to previous models.
- The paper is well-written, making it easy to follow, and the authors provide detailed explanations of the technical aspects of the model, which enhances its clarity and reproducibility.
- The proposed modifications to the Equiformer model, such as the incorporation of eSCN convolutions and the introduction of new architectural components like attention re-normalization, separable S2 activation, and separable layer normalization, are innovative and contribute to the advancement of equivariant Transformers.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a comprehensive comparison of the computational complexity of EquiformerV2 with other models like eSCN, which could provide a clearer understanding of the model's efficiency.
- There is a lack of detailed analysis on the performance of EquiformerV2 on other datasets beyond OC20 and OC22, which limits the generalizability of the model.
- The paper does not include a detailed discussion on the limitations of EquiformerV2, which could provide a more balanced view of the model's capabilities and challenges.
- The explanation of some technical details, such as the radial function and the S2 activation, could be improved for better clarity and understanding.
- The paper could benefit from a more thorough comparison with other models, especially in terms of the number of parameters and computational efficiency, to establish the superiority of EquiformerV2 more convincingly.

**Questions:**
- Could you provide a detailed comparison of the computational complexity of EquiformerV2 with other models like eSCN?
- How does EquiformerV2 perform on other datasets beyond OC20 and OC22?
- Can you provide a more detailed analysis of the limitations of EquiformerV2?
- Could you clarify the technical details, such as the radial function and the S2 activation, to enhance the understanding of these components?
- How does the performance of EquiformerV2 scale with different degrees of representations, and what are the implications for its generalizability and applicability to other domains?
