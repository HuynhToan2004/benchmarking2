 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel approach to multi-label learning by optimizing a Lebesgue measure of a set of models, each trained on a different loss function. This approach aims to ensure consistency across various loss functions, providing a unified perspective that avoids the need for surrogate loss functions. The methodology is supported by theoretical analysis and empirical evidence, demonstrating its effectiveness on several datasets. The paper also addresses the challenge of optimizing non-convex, discontinuous loss functions and discusses the potential for inconsistencies in surrogate loss functions. However, the paper has been critiqued for its lack of clarity in presentation, insufficient discussion on related works, and the need for more rigorous experimental validation.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel approach to multi-label learning by optimizing a Lebesgue measure, which is a significant departure from traditional methods that rely on surrogate loss functions.
- Theoretical analysis is provided to support the consistency of the proposed method, which is a crucial aspect of the paper.
- The experimental results demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed method, showing improvements over state-of-the-art methods in various datasets.
- The paper is well-written and easy to follow, making it accessible to readers.
- The method's simplicity is highlighted, which could make it easier to implement in practical scenarios.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks clarity in its presentation, with several sections and figures that are not well-explained, making it difficult for readers to fully understand the proposed method and its implications.
- The discussion on related works is insufficient, particularly in the context of multi-label learning where similar approaches have been previously explored.
- The experimental validation is not robust enough, with concerns about the selection of datasets and the depth of analysis.
- The paper does not sufficiently address the computational complexity and scalability of the proposed method, which is crucial for practical implementation.
- The paper could benefit from a more detailed discussion on the limitations of the proposed method and potential future research directions.

**Questions:**
- Could you clarify the definition and role of the Lebesgue measure in the context of multi-label learning, especially in relation to the optimization of loss functions?
- How does the proposed method handle the optimization of non-convex, discontinuous loss functions, and what are the implications for the convergence and stability of the optimization process?
- In the experimental section, could you provide more details on the selection of datasets and the rationale behind the choice of metrics?
- How does the proposed method compare with existing state-of-the-art methods in terms of computational complexity and scalability?
- Can you provide a more detailed explanation of the figures and sections that are currently unclear or confusing?
- How does the proposed method address the issue of inconsistencies in surrogate loss functions, and what are the implications for the overall effectiveness of the learning process?
