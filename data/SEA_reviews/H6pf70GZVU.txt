 **Summary:**
The paper introduces YoooP and YoooP+, novel non-exemplar methods for class-incremental learning that avoid catastrophic forgetting by storing only one prototype per class. YoooP optimizes prototypes using an attentional mean-shift algorithm to maintain class compactness, while YoooP+ generates synthetic data to preserve angular distributions between class prototypes and real data. Both methods demonstrate superior performance over existing non-exemplar baselines, with extensive experiments validating their effectiveness. The paper also addresses the limitations of Gaussian noise-based prototype perturbation and proposes a new prototype augmentation technique. However, concerns about the novelty of the method and its comparative performance with other non-exemplar methods were raised.
