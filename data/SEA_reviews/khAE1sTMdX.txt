 **Summary:**
The paper introduces UniMP, a unified multi-modal personalization framework that integrates various modalities (text, image, and user ID) to enhance personalized recommendations. UniMP leverages a large language model (LLM) to process user histories and generates multi-modal outputs, including text, images, and user IDs. The framework is evaluated on multiple tasks such as recommendation, retrieval, explanation, and image generation, demonstrating superior performance over existing methods. The experiments are conducted on several datasets, including Amazon datasets, and the results show that UniMP outperforms other baselines. However, concerns were raised about the novelty of the method, the limited evaluation scope, and the lack of detailed implementation and training details.

**Strengths:**
- The paper presents a unified framework for multi-modal personalization, which effectively integrates various modalities such as text, images, and user IDs, providing a comprehensive solution for personalized recommendation systems.
- The methodology is clearly explained, and the paper is well-written, making it accessible and understandable to readers.
- The experiments are thorough, covering multiple datasets and tasks, and the results consistently outperform baselines, demonstrating the effectiveness of the proposed method.
- The paper introduces a novel approach to multi-modal personalization, leveraging large language models (LLMs) to handle heterogeneous data formats, which is a significant advancement in the field.
- The proposed methodology, which includes a unified data format and a fine-grained multi-modal user modeling architecture, is innovative and addresses the challenges of processing multi-modal data in personalization tasks.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks novelty as it primarily builds upon existing methods such as Vision-Language Pre-Training (VLPT) and Large Language Models (LLMs) without introducing significant new techniques or insights.
- The evaluation scope is limited, focusing on e-commerce datasets which may not fully demonstrate the generalizability of the proposed method across different domains.
- The paper lacks detailed implementation and training details, which makes it difficult for readers to replicate or understand the method fully.
- The comparison with baselines is not fair due to the different training datasets used, which could skew the results in favor of the proposed method.
- The paper does not sufficiently address the scalability of the method, especially in terms of computational resources and training time, which could limit its practical application.
- The paper's contribution is seen as limited, as it primarily focuses on integrating existing methods rather than introducing new techniques or insights.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors provide more details on the implementation and training of the proposed method, including the specifics of the LLM used and the training parameters?
- How does the method perform on other types of datasets beyond e-commerce, such as social media or news datasets?
- Could the authors clarify the differences between the proposed method and existing methods, particularly in terms of the technical contributions and insights provided?
- How does the method handle the scalability issues, especially in terms of computational resources and training time?
- Could the authors discuss the potential limitations of the proposed method and suggest areas for future research or improvements?
