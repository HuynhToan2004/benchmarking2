 **Summary:**
The paper investigates the negative transfer problem in continual reinforcement learning (CRL) by introducing a novel method called Reset & Distill (R&D). This method involves resetting the online actor and critic networks to learn a new task and distilling knowledge from the online actor and previous expert action probabilities. The R&D method is evaluated against several baselines and shown to outperform them, demonstrating its effectiveness in mitigating negative transfer and forgetting in CRL. The paper also provides a comprehensive analysis of the negative transfer phenomenon, highlighting its prevalence and potential causes, and proposes a simple yet effective baseline to address these issues.

**Strengths:**
- The paper addresses a significant and overlooked problem in continual reinforcement learning (CRL), specifically the issue of negative transfer, which is crucial for practical applications.
- The proposed method, Reset & Distill (R&D), is simple, intuitive, and effective, requiring only the re-initialization of the actor and critic networks and the distillation of knowledge from the online actor to the offline actor.
- The paper is well-written, clear, and easy to follow, with extensive experiments conducted on the Meta-World RL environment to validate the effectiveness of the proposed method.
- The authors provide a thorough analysis of the negative transfer phenomenon, highlighting its prevalence and unique characteristics, which are distinct from mere plasticity or capacity loss observed in conventional RL algorithms.
- The proposed R&D method is shown to outperform several baselines, demonstrating its effectiveness in mitigating both catastrophic negative transfer and forgetting in CRL.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed comparison of the R&D method with existing approaches, particularly those that address similar issues such as catastrophic forgetting and negative transfer.
- The paper does not sufficiently discuss the computational complexity or the scalability of the R&D method, which could be critical for practical deployment in real-world scenarios.
- The paper could benefit from a more comprehensive analysis of the negative transfer phenomenon, including the underlying causes and potential mitigation strategies beyond the proposed R&D method.
- There is a lack of clarity in the definition and measurement of negative transfer, which could affect the interpretation and comparison of the results.
- The paper does not sufficiently discuss the limitations of the proposed method, such as its applicability to different RL algorithms and its performance under varying conditions.

**Questions:**
- Could you clarify the definition and measurement of negative transfer, and how it is distinct from other forms of forgetting or capacity loss in RL?
- How does the R&D method compare to other approaches that address similar issues in CRL, such as those mentioned in the literature?
- What are the specific computational requirements and complexity of the R&D method, and how do these impact its scalability and practical deployment?
- How does the R&D method perform under different conditions, such as varying task sequences or different RL algorithms?
- Can you provide more details on the experimental setup, particularly the selection of tasks and the conditions under which the experiments were conducted?
- How does the R&D method handle the potential trade-offs between plasticity and stability in CRL, and what are the implications of these trade-offs for the practical deployment of the method?
