 **Summary:**
The paper introduces BIRB, a benchmark designed to evaluate the generalization capabilities of machine learning models in bioacoustics. BIRB employs a retrieval task to measure out-of-distribution generalization, few-shot learning, and robustness to class imbalance and label shift. It utilizes a dataset of over 750,000 bird recordings to assess model performance across various conditions, including distribution shifts and novel classes. The benchmark is structured around a baseline system that uses representation learning and nearest-centroid search, aiming to provide a realistic and complex benchmark for advancing robustness to distribution shifts and generalization of ML models in bioacoustics.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces BIRB, a benchmark designed to assess the generalization capabilities of machine learning models in the field of bioacoustics, which is a novel and significant contribution to the field.
- The benchmark is well-motivated, structured, and provides a thorough evaluation of various models, including a detailed analysis of results and their implications.
- The paper is well-written, clear, and provides a good overview of the relevant literature, making it accessible to readers.
- The use of a retrieval task to measure generalization is innovative and provides a novel approach to evaluating model performance in real-world scenarios.
- The benchmark is significant as it addresses the generalization of models in bioacoustics, which is a crucial aspect for advancing the field.

**Weaknesses:**

- There is a lack of discussion on the societal impact of the research, which is an important consideration for any scientific study.
