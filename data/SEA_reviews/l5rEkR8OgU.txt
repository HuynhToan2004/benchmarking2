 **Summary:**
The paper explores the concept of implicit intermediate supervision in neural networks, particularly in multi-task and multi-label learning settings. It presents theoretical and empirical evidence supporting the effectiveness of this approach, which allows neural networks to utilize solutions from simpler tasks to solve more complex ones, thereby reducing the need for explicit supervision. The authors demonstrate this through experiments on synthetic datasets, showing that implicit supervision can enhance learning in various tasks, including parity learning and code interpretation. The paper also discusses the potential of this approach in reducing the reliance on curated data and explicit supervision, thereby simplifying the training process for large language models.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces an interesting and novel concept of implicit intermediate supervision, which is crucial for learning complex tasks without the need for explicit step-by-step supervision.
- The theoretical and empirical evidence provided supports the effectiveness of implicit intermediate supervision in multi-task and multi-label learning settings.
- The paper is well-written, with clear and concise explanations that make it easy to follow, and the experiments are well-designed, demonstrating the benefits of implicit intermediate supervision across various tasks.
- The findings of the paper offer valuable insights into how large language models learn complex tasks, potentially leading to the development of new methods for solving intricate tasks in language modeling.
- The paper is well-organized, with clear contributions outlined in the introduction and a comprehensive related work section that effectively positions the paper within the existing literature.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper could benefit from a more diverse set of experiments, including real-world datasets and a broader range of tasks to validate the effectiveness of implicit intermediate supervision in more practical scenarios.
- The theoretical analysis in the paper is limited to specific cases, which might not generalize well to more complex or varied tasks.
- The paper could be clearer in its explanations, particularly in the definitions and descriptions of the tasks, to better guide readers unfamiliar with the specifics of the experiments.
- There is a lack of detailed discussion on the limitations of the proposed approach, which could help readers understand the potential drawbacks or challenges associated with using implicit intermediate supervision.
- The paper could benefit from a more detailed discussion on the implications of the findings for practical applications, especially in terms of how the insights from implicit intermediate supervision could be applied to improve existing models or develop new methods for solving complex tasks.

**Questions:**
- How does the implicit intermediate supervision compare to other forms of supervision, such as explicit step-by-step supervision or no supervision at all?
- Can the authors provide more detailed insights into how the implicit intermediate supervision might be applied in practical scenarios, such as in the development of new models or the improvement of existing ones?
- How does the implicit intermediate supervision affect the generalization ability of the model, especially in scenarios where the training and test data differ significantly?
- Could the authors elaborate on the limitations of the proposed approach and discuss potential solutions or workarounds for these limitations?
- How does the implicit intermediate supervision impact the computational complexity and efficiency of the training process?
- Can the authors provide more details on the experimental setup, particularly in terms of the parameters used in the training and the specifics of the models and datasets used?
