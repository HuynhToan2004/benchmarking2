 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel pruning framework for Spiking Neural Networks (SNNs) aimed at enhancing energy efficiency by combining unstructured weight pruning with unstructured neuron pruning. The authors propose a methodology that optimizes energy consumption by reducing the number of spikes and synaptic connections, which is crucial for neuromorphic computing. The paper provides a theoretical analysis and empirical results showing that the proposed method can achieve significant energy savings while maintaining competitive performance. The framework's effectiveness is demonstrated through experiments on various datasets, including CIFAR-10 and DVS-CIFAR10, and it is compared against existing methods. However, the paper lacks detailed discussions on the hardware-software co-design and the specific hardware platforms used for validation, which are critical for the practical implementation of the proposed method.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, clear, and easy to follow, with a clear motivation and a novel approach to pruning in SNNs.
- The proposed method achieves impressive energy efficiency gains and is the first to apply unstructured neuron pruning to deep SNNs.
- The paper provides a comprehensive analysis of the energy consumption of SNNs and evaluates the effectiveness of weight and neuron pruning in reducing energy consumption.
- The proposed framework is simple and effective, and the experimental results demonstrate its effectiveness in reducing energy consumption while maintaining comparable performance.
- The paper introduces a novel design for the energy penalty term, addressing the ill-posed problem that arises when jointly pruning neurons and weights under energy constraints.
- The method outperforms previous works in reducing energy consumption while maintaining comparable performance, with a sparsest network pruned by the proposed method requiring only 8.5M SOPs for inference.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed discussion on the hardware-software co-design and the specific hardware platforms used for validation, which are crucial for the practical implementation of the proposed method.
- The paper does not provide a detailed comparison with other energy-saving methods for SNNs, such as quantization, reduction of firing rates, and knowledge distillation.
- The paper does not provide a detailed analysis of the energy consumption of the proposed method under different hardware platforms and does not discuss the potential limitations of the energy consumption model.
- The paper does not compare the proposed method with other state-of-the-art methods for energy efficiency in SNNs, which could provide a more comprehensive evaluation of the proposed method's effectiveness.
- The paper does not provide a detailed discussion on the hardware-software co-design, which is critical for the practical implementation of the proposed method.
