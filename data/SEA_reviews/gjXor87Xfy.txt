 **Summary:**
The paper explores the challenges in training Memory-based Dynamic Graph Neural Networks (MDGNNs) by focusing on the impact of temporal batch size on model training. It introduces a novel iterative prediction-correction scheme and a memory coherence learning objective to mitigate the effects of temporal discontinuity, enhancing the scalability of MDGNNs by allowing for larger temporal batches. Theoretical analysis and experimental results demonstrate that these innovations improve convergence rates and reduce variance, allowing for more efficient training. The paper also discusses the practical implications of these findings, suggesting potential improvements in the training of MDGNNs for various dynamic graph tasks.

**Strengths:**
- The paper provides a thorough theoretical analysis on the impact of temporal batch size on the convergence of MDGNN training, which is crucial for understanding the behavior of these models.
- The authors have introduced a novel iterative prediction-correction scheme and a memory coherence learning objective to address the temporal discontinuity issue, which is a significant challenge in training MDGNNs.
- The proposed method is supported by extensive experimental results, demonstrating its effectiveness in improving the convergence rate of MDGNN training and reducing variance.
- The paper is well-written and easy to follow, making the complex concepts accessible to a broader audience.
- The problem addressed, namely, the handling of temporal discontinuity in training MDGNNs with large temporal batch sizes, is both relevant and significant, as it impacts the practical applicability of these models in real-world scenarios.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper could benefit from a more detailed explanation of the motivation behind using a larger temporal batch size, particularly in relation to the handling of temporal discontinuity.
- The experimental results are limited to link prediction tasks, and it would be beneficial to include results on node classification tasks to provide a more comprehensive evaluation of the proposed method.
- The paper lacks a detailed discussion on the limitations of the proposed method and potential negative societal impacts, which are crucial for assessing the broader implications of the work.
- There is a lack of clarity in the definition and usage of certain terms and symbols, such as "temporal batch size" and "temporal discontinuity," which could confuse readers and detract from the paper's clarity.
- The paper does not provide a detailed comparison of the proposed method with existing methods that address similar challenges in MDGNN training, which could help in positioning the work within the current research landscape.
- The paper could benefit from a more detailed discussion on the computational complexity and GPU memory utilization of the proposed method, as these are critical factors in practical deployments of MDGNNs.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors clarify the motivation behind using a larger temporal batch size, particularly in relation to the handling of temporal discontinuity?
- How does the proposed method compare to existing methods that address similar challenges in MDGNN training, such as those mentioned in related works?
- Could the authors provide a detailed comparison of the computational complexity and GPU memory utilization of the proposed method with existing methods to help in evaluating its practical efficiency?
- How does the proposed method perform on node classification tasks, and what insights can be gained from these results?
- Can the authors provide more details on the experimental setup, including the specifics of the datasets used, the hardware configurations, and the training parameters?
- How does the proposed method handle the issue of temporal discontinuity, especially in scenarios where events are not evenly distributed across batches?
- Can the authors elaborate on the practical implications of the theoretical findings, particularly in terms of how they can be applied to improve the training of MDGNNs in real-world applications?
