 **Summary:**
The paper introduces ToolChain*, a novel method for decision-making in large language models (LLMs) by formulating the action space as a decision tree. This approach, which utilizes A* search, aims to balance exploration and exploitation in expansive action spaces, offering a significant improvement over existing methods. The methodology is evaluated on the ToolBench dataset, demonstrating superior performance and efficiency compared to baseline methods. The paper also discusses the integration of LLMs with external tools, highlighting the potential of this approach in enhancing LLM capabilities for complex problem-solving tasks.

**Strengths:**
- The paper presents a novel approach to decision-making in large language models (LLMs) by formulating the action space as a decision tree, which is a significant advancement in the field.
- The use of A* search for pruning high-cost branches and identifying the most low-cost valid path as the solution is a well-thought-out and effective approach, enhancing the efficiency and effectiveness of the model.
- The methodology is well-evaluated on the ToolBench dataset, demonstrating significant improvements in performance and efficiency over baseline methods.
- The paper is well-written, clear, and easy to follow, with a clear presentation of the methodology and results.
- The integration of LLMs with external tools, such as APIs, is highlighted as a promising approach for enhancing LLM capabilities in complex problem-solving tasks.
- The paper provides a detailed discussion on the limitations and potential negative societal impacts of the proposed method, which is commendable for its transparency and ethical considerations.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks clarity in some areas, particularly in the definitions and explanations of terms such as "tool-use scenarios" and "open-loop systems."
- There is a potential over-reliance on LLMs for generating all possible actions, which could limit the exploration of new or less common actions.
- The methodology, while innovative, may not be entirely novel as similar approaches have been previously explored in other fields.
- The paper does not sufficiently compare the proposed method with other state-of-the-art methods, such as those using Monte Carlo Tree Search (MCTS), which could provide a more robust evaluation of the proposed method.
- The methodology's scalability and applicability to more complex or varied action spaces are not adequately addressed, which could limit its practical utility in real-world scenarios.
- The paper does not discuss potential negative societal impacts of the proposed method, which is a critical omission for a paper on AI/ML ethics.

**Questions:**
- Can you clarify the definitions and distinctions between "tool-use scenarios" and "open-loop systems" as used in your paper?
- How does the proposed method handle the potential over-reliance on LLMs for generating all possible actions, and what measures are in place to ensure a comprehensive exploration of the action space?
- Could you elaborate on the method's scalability and applicability to more complex or varied action spaces?
- How does the proposed method compare with other state-of-the-art methods, particularly those using MCTS?
- In terms of ethical considerations, what potential negative societal impacts do you foresee from the proposed method, and how do you address these in your research?
