 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel concept bottleneck layer for generative models, aimed at enhancing interpretability and steerability by constraining the model to encode human-understandable concepts. The layer is designed to partition the generative model into pre-concept, concept, and post-concept sections, and is evaluated across various datasets and generative models including VAEs, GANs, and diffusion models. The experiments demonstrate that the proposed model can effectively steer the generative model's output, enhancing interpretability and debugging capabilities. The paper also discusses the challenges of training such models and their potential limitations in large-scale, unstructured datasets.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel concept bottleneck layer for generative models, which is a significant advancement in the field.
- The model is evaluated across multiple datasets and generative models, demonstrating its versatility and applicability.
- The paper is well-structured, with clear explanations of the model's architecture and training procedures, making it accessible to readers.
- The proposed model shows promising results in terms of steerability and interpretability, which are crucial for understanding and controlling the outputs of generative models.
- The use of a concept loss and orthogonality loss in training the model is a novel approach that adds depth to the methodology.
- The paper is well-written and easy to follow, with clear figures and detailed explanations that aid in understanding the model's functionality.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed discussion on how the model handles the orthogonality loss, which is a critical component of the training process.
- There is a noticeable absence of comparisons with other models that incorporate similar concepts, such as the GlanceNet model, which could provide a better understanding of the proposed model's advantages or disadvantages.
- The paper does not adequately address how the model performs with larger datasets or in scenarios where the number of concepts is significantly larger, which could affect its practical applicability.
- The experimental results, particularly in terms of FID scores, are not convincing, and the paper lacks a detailed analysis of the model's performance under different conditions.
- The paper could benefit from more comprehensive ablation studies and a deeper exploration of the model's limitations, such as the potential for concept leakage.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors clarify how the orthogonality loss is handled during training and how it affects the model's performance?
- How does the proposed model compare to other models that incorporate similar concepts, such as the GlanceNet model?
- Could the authors provide more details on the training procedure for the CB layer, particularly how the hyperparameters are chosen and how they affect the model's performance?
- How does the model perform with larger datasets or when the number of concepts is significantly larger?
- Can the authors elaborate on the potential limitations of the model, such as the risk of concept leakage, and how these limitations could be addressed?
- Would it be possible to include more qualitative results, such as visual comparisons of generated images, to better demonstrate the model's capabilities?
