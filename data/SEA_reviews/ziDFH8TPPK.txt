 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel approach for typhoon trajectory prediction using a data-driven method that integrates real-time Unified Model (UM) data with ERA5 reanalysis data. The model, named LT3P, employs a two-stage training process: first on ERA5 and then on UM, with a bias correction component to enhance the model's performance. The paper claims that this approach outperforms existing state-of-the-art models in terms of accuracy and real-time applicability. The dataset, including ERA5, UM, and typhoon best-track data, is made publicly available to support further research. However, concerns were raised about the novelty of the method, the comparative analysis with existing models, and the clarity of the methodological presentation.

**Strengths:**
- The paper addresses a significant and timely issue of typhoon trajectory prediction, which is crucial for disaster management and risk reduction.
- The proposed method is innovative, integrating real-time Unified Model (UM) data with ERA5 reanalysis data to enhance the prediction accuracy.
- The dataset introduced in the study is comprehensive and valuable for further research in the field.
- The paper is well-organized and easy to follow, with clear explanations of the methodology and experimental results.
- The evaluation of the proposed method is thorough, demonstrating its effectiveness and potential impact.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed discussion on the differences between the proposed method and existing approaches, such as the ECMWF tracker, which could lead to confusion about the novelty and advantages of the proposed method.
- There is a significant gap in the literature review, particularly in not discussing related works on trajectory prediction in tropical cyclone prediction, which is a critical omission given the focus of the paper.
- The methodology section could benefit from clearer explanations and more detailed descriptions, especially regarding the role and impact of different components like the bias correction and the use of UM data.
- The paper's claims about the superiority of the proposed method over existing models are not adequately supported by empirical evidence, particularly in the comparative analysis section.
- The paper could benefit from more rigorous comparisons with state-of-the-art models, including a broader range of metrics and a more comprehensive discussion of the results.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors clarify the role and impact of the bias correction component in the model's performance?
- How does the use of UM data specifically contribute to the model's effectiveness, and what are the specific advantages over using ERA5 data alone?
- Could the authors provide more detailed explanations for the methodological choices, such as the selection of variables and the training data split?
- How does the proposed method compare to other existing models in terms of accuracy, computational efficiency, and real-time applicability?
- Can the authors elaborate on the potential applications of the model beyond typhoon trajectory prediction, and how the dataset could be used in other research areas?
- Would it be possible to include a more detailed discussion on the limitations of the proposed method and potential areas for future research?
