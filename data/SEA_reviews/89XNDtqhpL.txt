 **Summary:**
The paper introduces MatFormer, a novel nested Transformer architecture designed to provide elasticity in various deployment constraints. MatFormer is a versatile model that can be adapted for different use cases, including language models and vision transformers, by adjusting the size of the feed-forward network (FFN) blocks. The model is trained with a unique nested structure, allowing for the extraction of multiple submodels with varying sizes and performances. This approach aims to reduce the training costs associated with maintaining multiple models for different resource constraints. The paper demonstrates MatFormer's effectiveness through experiments on both language and vision tasks, showing that it can be scaled up to larger models and still maintain its effectiveness.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel nested Transformer architecture that allows for the extraction of multiple submodels with varying sizes and performances, which is a significant advancement in the field.
- The concept of MatFormer is well-articulated, and the paper is well-written, making it easy to follow.
- The experiments conducted are extensive, covering both language and vision tasks, and the results are compelling, demonstrating the effectiveness of MatFormer in various applications.
- The paper addresses a practical problem of deploying large models on different hardware with different resource constraints, proposing a method that reduces the training cost by avoiding the need to train multiple models from scratch.
- The paper is well-motivated and provides a clear rationale for the proposed method, which is backed by extensive experimental results.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed comparison with existing methods, particularly in terms of training efficiency and the performance of the extracted submodels.
- There is a lack of discussion on the scalability of the method, especially in terms of the number of layers and the complexity of the tasks.
- The paper could benefit from a more thorough analysis of the performance of the extracted submodels, including their consistency and the impact of the training process on their performance.
- The paper does not provide sufficient details on the training process, particularly how the model parameters are optimized across different layers and how this affects the performance of the extracted submodels.
- The paper does not discuss the potential negative societal impacts of the proposed method, which is a significant omission.

**Questions:**
- How does the training efficiency of MatFormer compare to other methods, especially in terms of the time taken to train a model of a certain size?
- Can the authors provide more details on the training process, particularly how the model parameters are optimized across different layers and how this affects the performance of the extracted submodels?
- How does the performance of the extracted submodels compare to those trained independently, and what are the implications of this for the practical deployment of MatFormer?
- Could the authors elaborate on the scalability of the method, especially in terms of the number of layers and the complexity of the tasks?
- How does the method perform when applied to different types of tasks or domains, and what are the implications of this for the generalizability of the results?
- Could the authors discuss the potential negative societal impacts of the proposed method and how these might be mitigated?
