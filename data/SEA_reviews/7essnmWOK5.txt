 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel Graph Neural Network (GNN) model, named HSDGNN, designed for multivariate time-series forecasting. This model leverages hierarchical spatiotemporal dependencies to capture both intra-dependencies among attributes and inter-dependencies among variables. It organizes variables as nodes in a graph, with each node representing a subgraph of attributes, and uses a two-level convolution to model spatial dependencies with different granularities. The model also considers changes in graph topologies to enhance dependency modeling across time and spatial dimensions. Experiments on real-world datasets demonstrate the superiority of HSDGNN over state-of-the-art baselines in terms of prediction accuracy. However, concerns about the novelty of the approach, the complexity of the model, and the lack of a comprehensive analysis of the limitations are highlighted.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, making it easy to follow and understand.
- The proposed model, HSDGNN, is innovative in its approach to modeling multivariate time-series data by considering hierarchical spatiotemporal dependencies.
- The paper includes comprehensive experiments that demonstrate the effectiveness of HSDGNN in capturing complex dependencies in multivariate time-series data.
- The experimental results show that HSDGNN outperforms state-of-the-art baselines, indicating its potential for practical applications.
- The paper provides a clear explanation of the proposed method, which is supported by detailed ablation studies and experiments.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a thorough analysis of the limitations of the proposed method, which is crucial for understanding its applicability and effectiveness in different scenarios.
- The novelty of the approach is questioned, as the idea of using graph neural networks to capture spatiotemporal dependencies is not new.
- The computational complexity of HSDGNN is not discussed, which is a significant concern given the potential for high computational demands.
- The paper does not compare HSDGNN with the most recent and relevant models in the field, which could provide a more robust evaluation of its performance.
- The paper does not discuss the potential negative societal impacts of the proposed method, which is a critical aspect of ethical considerations in technology development.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors clarify how the intra-dependencies among attributes are modeled using the proposed method?
- How does the proposed method handle the generation of dynamic graphs, particularly in terms of computational efficiency and the selection of hyperparameters?
- Could the authors provide a more detailed comparison with other recent and relevant models in the field to demonstrate the superiority of HSDGNN?
- What are the specific scenarios where HSDGNN is most effective, and where might it struggle?
- Can the authors provide a more detailed analysis of the computational complexity of HSDGNN, including a comparison with other models in terms of runtime and memory usage?
- How does HSDGNN handle missing data, and what are the implications for its performance in such scenarios?
