 **Summary:**
The paper introduces DNA-GPT, a novel training-free method for detecting GPT-generated text based on the observation that GPT models tend to generate similar text given the same preceding text, while human-generated text varies more. DNA-GPT utilizes n-gram analysis and relative entropy to measure the differences between machine-generated and human-generated text, providing an explainable detection mechanism. The method was tested across various datasets and models, demonstrating its effectiveness in detecting GPT-generated text in both black-box and white-box scenarios. The paper also explores the robustness of the method against revised text attacks and its ability to solve model sourcing.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, clear, and easy to follow, with a clear and intuitive methodology.
- The proposed method, DNA-GPT, is innovative and provides an explainable detection mechanism, which is a significant advantage over existing methods.
- The method is robust against revised text attacks and can solve model sourcing, demonstrating its practical utility.
- The paper provides a comprehensive evaluation across multiple datasets and models, showing the effectiveness of the method in both black-box and white-box settings.
- The method is general and can be applied to any language model, making it a versatile tool for detecting AI-generated text.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed discussion on the limitations of the method, which is crucial for understanding its applicability and effectiveness in real-world scenarios.
- The paper does not provide a detailed comparison with existing methods like DetectGPT, which could have highlighted the unique advantages of DNA-GPT.
- The paper could benefit from more comprehensive experiments, including the impact of different truncation ratios and the sensitivity of the method to the number of regenerations.
- The method's effectiveness in detecting non-GPT models or models with different generation strategies is not discussed, which could limit its practical utility.
- The paper could be improved by providing more detailed theoretical explanations and empirical evidence to support the method's effectiveness.

**Questions:**
- How does the method perform when applied to non-GPT models or models with different generation strategies?
- Can the authors provide more detailed theoretical explanations and empirical evidence to support the method's effectiveness?
- How does the method perform when the truncation ratio is varied, and what are the optimal settings for different scenarios?
- Could the authors elaborate on the method's sensitivity to the number of regenerations and provide more detailed experimental results?
- How does the method compare to other state-of-the-art methods in terms of computational efficiency and practical applicability?
