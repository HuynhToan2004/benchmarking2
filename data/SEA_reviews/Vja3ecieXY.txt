 **Summary:**
The paper introduces GreenTrainer, a novel method aimed at reducing the FLOPs in large language model (LLM) fine-tuning by selectively choosing the most important tensors for training. This method employs a dynamic programming algorithm to optimize tensor selection based on their importance and backpropagation cost, thus minimizing computational overhead without compromising model performance. The approach is validated through experiments on various LLMs and datasets, showing significant FLOPs reduction and comparable or improved accuracy. However, the method's scalability and applicability to larger models and different types of datasets are concerns.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written and easy to follow, providing a clear explanation of the proposed method, GreenTrainer.
- The motivation behind the research is compelling, focusing on the environmental impact of LLM fine-tuning and proposing a method to reduce FLOPs while maintaining accuracy.
- The proposed method is simple, effective, and well-explained, with a focus on minimizing FLOPs and achieving comparable or better accuracy compared to existing methods like LoRA and Prefix Tuning.
- The paper includes a comprehensive experimental evaluation that demonstrates the effectiveness of the proposed method across different LLM sizes and datasets.
- The methodology is novel and addresses a significant challenge in the field of LLM fine-tuning, which is often overlooked in existing literature.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed comparison with other methods that aim to reduce FLOPs, such as pruning and quantization, which could provide a more robust evaluation of the proposed method.
- The scalability of the proposed method is questionable, as it may not be effective for larger models due to the potential for insufficient training data and the complexity of the model.
- The paper does not include a detailed discussion on the limitations of the proposed method, particularly concerning the scalability and generalization to different types of datasets.
- The method's applicability to different types of LLMs, such as those using different architectures or training methods, is not explored, which could limit the practical utility of the findings.
- The paper does not provide a detailed discussion on the computational complexity and efficiency of the proposed method, especially in terms of memory usage and computational time.
- The experimental setup could be improved by including more diverse datasets and LLM sizes to better evaluate the generalizability of the findings.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors provide more details on the computational complexity and efficiency of the proposed method, particularly in terms of memory usage and computational time?
- How does the proposed method perform on larger models, and what are the potential limitations or challenges in scaling up the method?
- Could the authors discuss the potential of the proposed method in different types of LLMs, such as those using different architectures or training methods?
- How does the proposed method compare to other methods that aim to reduce FLOPs, such as pruning and quantization?
- Could the authors provide a more detailed discussion on the limitations of the proposed method, particularly concerning the scalability and generalization to different types of datasets?
