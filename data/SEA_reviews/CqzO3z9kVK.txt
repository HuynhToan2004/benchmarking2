 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel method for merging multiple pretrained language models (LMs) into a unified model using an evolutionary algorithm. This approach, termed "knowledge fusion," aims to enhance performance across various data domains and tasks, demonstrating superior generalization capabilities. The methodology involves initializing a population of models, each trained on different datasets, and evolving these models through a process that selects the best-performing models based on a development dataset. The paper presents a comprehensive experimental evaluation, comparing this method with existing model merging techniques and demonstrating its effectiveness in improving performance. However, concerns were raised regarding the clarity of the paper's presentation, the novelty of the approach, and the absence of a clear comparison with state-of-the-art models.

**Strengths:**
- The paper addresses an important and relevant problem in the field of NLP, specifically focusing on the fusion of knowledge across different models to enhance performance and generalization capabilities.
- The methodology is clearly explained, with a detailed description of the evolutionary algorithm used and its application to model merging.
- Extensive experimental evaluation is conducted, comparing the proposed method with existing model merging techniques and demonstrating its effectiveness in improving performance.
- The paper provides a comprehensive analysis of the advantages and limitations of the model evolution method, which is a significant contribution to the field.
- The use of an evolutionary algorithm to merge models is an innovative approach that has not been extensively explored in the literature.
- The paper is well-structured, with clear sections and tables that aid in understanding the experimental results and methodology.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper could benefit from a clearer presentation, particularly in the introduction and related works sections, to better position the proposed method within the existing literature.
- The novelty of the approach is somewhat limited, as the use of evolutionary algorithms for model merging has been previously explored.
- There is a lack of clarity in some sections of the paper, particularly in the description of the model merging process and the role of the evolutionary algorithm in this context.
- The paper does not sufficiently compare the proposed method with state-of-the-art models, which could undermine the claims of its effectiveness.
- The paper does not provide a detailed analysis of the computational complexity or the scalability of the proposed method, which is crucial for understanding its applicability in large-scale NLP tasks.
- The experimental setup and the choice of baseline models are not adequately justified, which could affect the validity of the results.

**Questions:**
- Could you clarify the role and impact of the evolutionary algorithm in the model merging process, particularly in terms of its contribution to the overall performance improvement?
- How does the proposed method compare with other state-of-the-art models in terms of performance and computational efficiency?
- Could you provide more details on the computational complexity and scalability of the proposed method, especially in the context of large-scale NLP tasks?
- How do the results of the proposed method compare with those of other model merging techniques, especially in terms of performance and generalization capabilities?
- Could you elaborate on the choice of baseline models and the experimental setup, particularly in the context of the GLUE benchmark?
- What are the specific advantages of using an evolutionary algorithm for model merging compared to other optimization methods?
