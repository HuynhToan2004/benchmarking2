 **Summary:**
The paper investigates the training instability of planar flows, specifically focusing on the singularity in the original parameterization which can lead to unstable training dynamics. The authors propose a novel reparameterization to address this issue, enhancing the stability and convergence in variational inference tasks. The proposed method, while simple, is shown to improve the performance of planar flows in various experiments, including toy distributions, Bayesian regressions, and variational autoencoders. The paper is commended for its clarity and the originality of the reparameterization technique, which could potentially be applied to other normalizing flows.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, clearly explaining the motivation and methodology, with a clear exposition of the mathematical concepts.
- The authors have identified a significant issue with the original parameterization of planar flows, which is not commonly discussed in the literature.
- The proposed reparameterization technique is novel and has been validated through various experiments, showing improvements over the original parameterization.
- The paper provides a clear and concise explanation of the reparameterization technique, which could be useful in other contexts beyond planar flows.
- The experiments are well-designed and demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed method, showing improvements in training stability and convergence.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed discussion on the limitations of the proposed method, particularly in high-dimensional settings where the singularity might not be a significant issue.
- The paper does not adequately compare the proposed method with other state-of-the-art methods, which could help establish its relative performance.
- The paper could benefit from more extensive experiments, including comparisons with other normalizing flows and different datasets to validate the generalizability of the findings.
- The paper does not provide a theoretical justification for the improved performance of the proposed method, which could strengthen the argument for its effectiveness.
- The paper could be improved by including more recent references and updating the literature review to reflect the current state of the field.

**Questions:**

- How does the proposed method compare with other state-of-the-art methods in terms of performance and computational efficiency?

- What are the potential applications of the reparameterization technique beyond planar flows, and have there been any attempts to apply it in these contexts?
