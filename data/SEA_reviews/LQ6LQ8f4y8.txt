 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel data augmentation technique, CCIL, aimed at enhancing the robustness of imitation learning methods by generating corrective data from expert demonstrations. The approach leverages local continuity in the environment dynamics to generate corrective labels, which can be used to train a dynamics model that exhibits local Lipschitz continuity. This method is evaluated across various robotic domains, including classic control, drone flying, high-dimensional navigation, locomotion, and tabletop manipulation, demonstrating its effectiveness in improving the performance of imitation learning agents. The paper provides theoretical guarantees and extensive empirical validation to support the method's effectiveness.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, clear, and easy to follow, with a clear motivation for the proposed method.
- The proposed method is simple yet effective, requiring minimal assumptions and leveraging local continuity in the environment dynamics to generate corrective labels.
- The method is evaluated across multiple robotic domains, demonstrating its effectiveness in improving the performance of imitation learning agents.
- The paper provides theoretical guarantees and extensive empirical validation to support the effectiveness of the method.
- The approach is novel and innovative, offering a new perspective on data augmentation in imitation learning.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed discussion on the limitations of the proposed method, particularly in terms of its applicability to complex dynamics and its reliance on the assumption of local continuity.
- There is a lack of clarity in the methodology, particularly in the explanation of how the dynamics model is learned and how the corrective labels are generated.
- The paper does not adequately address the computational efficiency and scalability of the method, especially in complex robotic systems.
- The experimental setup is somewhat limited, with only a few tasks evaluated, and the results do not convincingly demonstrate the superiority of the proposed method over existing methods like NoiseBC.
- The paper could benefit from a more thorough literature review and a more detailed discussion on the related work, particularly in the area of data augmentation in imitation learning.

**Questions:**
- How does the proposed method compare to other data augmentation methods, such as those that leverage domain-specific invariances or knowledge about the systems' invariances?
- Can the authors provide more details on how the dynamics model is learned and how the corrective labels are generated?
- How does the method perform when applied to more complex robotic systems, and what are the computational costs associated with its implementation?
- Could the authors clarify the role and impact of the slack variable in the method's performance?
- How does the method handle the generation of corrective labels for complex dynamics, particularly in the context of robotic manipulation tasks?
- Can the authors provide more detailed experimental results, including the number of demonstrations used for training and the computational resources required for the experiments?
