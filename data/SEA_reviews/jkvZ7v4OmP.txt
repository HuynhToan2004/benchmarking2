 **Summary:**
The paper introduces DiffCSP++, a diffusion-based approach for generating crystal structures under space group constraints. It builds upon the DiffCSP framework, integrating space group constraints into the model by decomposing lattice matrices into invariant representations and utilizing Wyckoff positions for fractional coordinates. The method is tested on various datasets and shows improved performance over existing methods, though the novelty of the approach is questioned due to its similarity to previous works. The paper is commended for its clarity and thorough evaluation, but criticized for its limited novelty and potential lack of technical contribution.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, clear, and easy to follow, with a logical structure that facilitates understanding.
- The proposed method is technically sound, with a thorough evaluation that demonstrates its effectiveness.
- The integration of space group constraints into the model is innovative and effectively addressed, showing potential for further research in material discovery.
- The paper provides a clear explanation of the methodology and its implications, which is beneficial for readers not familiar with the field.
- The method's performance is robust, with results indicating that it outperforms existing methods in certain scenarios.

**Weaknesses:**
- The novelty of the approach is limited as it primarily builds on existing methods like DiffCSP, with only minor modifications.
- The paper lacks a detailed discussion on the technical contributions, making it difficult to ascertain the specific advancements over prior works.
- There is a notable absence of discussion on the limitations and potential negative societal impacts of the proposed method.
- The paper's focus on specific datasets and tasks might limit its generalizability to other domains.
- The paper does not adequately compare its method with more recent or relevant works, such as those by Zhao et al. and Liu et al., which could provide a more comprehensive evaluation.
- The method's performance on complex structures and its scalability to larger datasets are not thoroughly addressed.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors clarify the technical contributions of their method over previous works, such as DiffCSP?
- How does the method perform on more complex structures, and what are the scalability implications for larger datasets?
- Can the authors provide more details on the training and evaluation processes, particularly how the ground truth space group was obtained and how it was used in the experiments?
- How does the method handle the generation of structures with specific space groups, and what are the implications for material design?
- Could the authors discuss the potential negative societal impacts of their method and how these might be mitigated?
- It would be beneficial to include more recent and relevant works in the comparative analysis to provide a more comprehensive evaluation.
