 **Summary:**
The paper explores the use of reinforcement learning (RL) to solve symbolic equations, specifically focusing on linear equations with real or complex coefficients. The RL agent is designed to manipulate equations by applying a set of predefined operations, aiming to transform them into simpler forms. The study includes experiments that demonstrate the agent's ability to learn to solve equations, showing improvements in performance over time. The approach is tested on equations with varying coefficients, including real, rational, and complex numbers, and the results indicate that the agent can effectively learn to solve these equations. The paper also discusses the potential of this method in broader applications of symbolic mathematics, although the scope of the experiments is limited to linear equations.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, with clear figures and a clear explanation of the proposed method.
- The use of reinforcement learning (RL) to solve symbolic equations is an interesting and novel approach.
- The paper demonstrates that the RL agent can learn to solve linear equations, showing improvements in performance over time.
- The idea of using RL to solve symbolic equations is a significant contribution to the field, as it allows for the discovery of new rules and strategies without human intervention.
- The experiments are well-designed, and the results are convincing, showing that the RL agent can effectively learn to solve equations.

**Weaknesses:**
- The scope of the experiments is limited to linear equations, which may not fully demonstrate the capabilities of the proposed method in more complex mathematical scenarios.
- The paper lacks a detailed discussion on the limitations of the proposed method and how it might perform in different scenarios.
- There is a lack of comparison with existing methods, such as symbolic regression, which could help contextualize the contributions of this paper.
- The paper does not address the generalizability of the learned rules to more complex equations or different types of mathematical problems.
- The presentation of the paper could be improved, particularly in terms of clarity and organization, especially in the appendices.
- The paper does not adequately address the scalability of the method to more complex equations or different types of mathematical problems.

**Questions:**
- Can the RL agent be adapted to solve more complex mathematical problems, such as non-linear equations or equations with more complex variables?
- How do the learned rules generalize to more complex equations or different types of mathematical problems?
- Could the authors provide more details on the training process, such as the specifics of the reward function and the training algorithm used?
- What are the potential applications of this method beyond solving symbolic equations, and how might it be adapted for other types of mathematical problems?
- How does the RL agent handle different types of variables, such as complex numbers, and what are the implications for the solver's performance?
- Could the authors clarify the differences between the two types of equations used in the experiments and discuss the implications of these differences for the solver's performance?
