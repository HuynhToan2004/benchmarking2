 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel watermarking scheme for text generated by large language models (LLMs), focusing on the robustness of the watermark against various attacks such as paraphrasing and editing. The proposed method, termed Unigram-Watermark, simplifies the existing watermarking scheme by Kirchenbauer et al. (2023) by using a consistent green-red split, which is claimed to enhance robustness. The paper provides theoretical guarantees and empirical evidence supporting the effectiveness of the proposed method, comparing it against the state-of-the-art KGW method. The authors also discuss the security and robustness of the watermark against different types of attacks, including paraphrasing and editing, and evaluate its performance under these conditions.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel watermarking scheme for text generated by LLMs, focusing on the robustness of the watermark against various attacks such as paraphrasing and editing.
- The authors provide a rigorous theoretical framework for quantifying the performance drop, correctness of detection, and security property against post-processing, which is a significant contribution to the field.
- The paper is well-written, clear, and easy to follow, making it accessible to a broad audience.
- The experimental results are convincing, demonstrating the effectiveness of the proposed method in detecting machine-generated text from a specific language model.
- The paper addresses a timely and important problem of LLM watermarking, which is crucial for the responsible use of LLMs.
- The authors have provided a comprehensive evaluation of their method, including comparisons with existing watermarking methods and evaluations under different conditions.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed discussion on the limitations and potential negative societal impacts of the proposed method.
- The paper does not discuss the possibility of using other watermarking methods such as steganography, which might offer different advantages or challenges.
- The experimental evaluation is somewhat limited, focusing primarily on paraphrasing attacks and not exploring other types of attacks such as cropping or insertion of tokens.
- The paper could benefit from a more detailed comparison with existing watermarking methods, including a discussion on the advantages and disadvantages of the proposed method compared to others.
- The robustness of the watermark against different types of attacks and the impact of different model sizes and configurations on the watermark's effectiveness are not thoroughly explored.
- The paper does not provide a detailed analysis of the computational complexity or the scalability of the proposed method, which could be crucial for practical implementation.

**Questions:**
- How does the proposed method compare to other watermarking methods, particularly in terms of robustness and effectiveness?
- Can the authors provide a more detailed analysis of the computational complexity and scalability of the proposed method?
- How does the robustness of the watermark vary with different model sizes and configurations?
- What are the potential negative societal impacts of the proposed method, and how can these be mitigated?
- Can the authors provide a more detailed discussion on the limitations of the proposed method and potential future directions for research?
