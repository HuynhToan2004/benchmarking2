 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel action space for robot manipulation, termed End-Effector-Elbow (E3), which integrates control over both the end-effector and the elbow joint. This approach aims to balance the precision of joint control with the data efficiency of end-effector control, offering a potential solution for overactuated robots where precise control over the elbow is crucial. The E3 action space is evaluated through reinforcement learning (RL) and imitation learning (IL) in both simulated and real-world environments, demonstrating its effectiveness in tasks requiring precise control over the robot's configuration. The paper also discusses the challenges and potential improvements in the implementation and evaluation of the E3 action space.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel action space, End-Effector-Elbow (E3), which addresses the limitations of existing action spaces by combining the advantages of joint and end-effector control.
- The E3 action space is evaluated in both simulated and real-world environments, demonstrating its effectiveness in tasks requiring precise control over the robot's configuration.
- The paper is well-written, clear, and easy to follow, with a clear motivation and a comprehensive review of related work.
- The authors provide code and detailed experimental setups, making the work reproducible.
- The paper presents a new action space that could be of interest to the community, as it offers a fresh perspective on controlling robot arms.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed discussion on the challenges and potential solutions for implementing the E3 action space, especially in complex environments.
- There is a need for more robustness in the implementation of the E3 action space, particularly in handling invalid actions and ensuring consistent performance across different environments.
- The paper does not sufficiently compare the E3 action space with other action spaces that control the elbow joint, such as the elbow-angle action space.
- The evaluation of the E3 action space could be improved by including more complex tasks and a broader range of environments to better demonstrate its effectiveness.
- The paper does not discuss the limitations of the E3 action space, such as its scalability to different types of robots and the potential complexity in implementing it for different robots.
- The paper could benefit from a more detailed discussion on the practical implications of the E3 action space, such as the computational cost and the complexity of implementation.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors provide more details on how the E3 action space is implemented in complex environments and how it handles invalid actions?
- How does the E3 action space compare with other action spaces that control the elbow joint, such as the elbow-angle action space?
- Could the authors discuss the potential limitations of the E3 action space, such as its scalability and complexity of implementation?
- How does the E3 action space perform in more complex tasks and environments beyond those presented in the paper?
- Could the authors provide more details on the computational cost and the complexity of implementing the E3 action space for different types of robots?
