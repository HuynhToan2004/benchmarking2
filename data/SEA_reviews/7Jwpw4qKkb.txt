 **Summary:**
The paper introduces AutoDAN, a novel approach for generating stealthy jailbreak prompts for large language models (LLMs) using a hierarchical genetic algorithm. AutoDAN aims to automate the process of generating jailbreak prompts that are both semantically meaningful and stealthy, addressing the limitations of existing methods that either rely on manual crafting or token-based algorithms. The methodology is evaluated against several LLMs, including GPT-3.5-turbo, and the results demonstrate that AutoDAN outperforms baseline methods in terms of attack success rate and perplexity. However, the paper is criticized for its limited evaluation scope, focusing only on GPT-3.5-turbo and not exploring other models like GPT-4.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, with clear and concise descriptions of the proposed method and experiments.
- The use of a hierarchical genetic algorithm to generate jailbreak prompts is innovative and addresses the limitations of existing methods.
- The method is shown to be effective in generating semantically meaningful jailbreak prompts, which is a significant improvement over previous methods that often rely on nonsensical prompts.
- The paper includes thorough evaluations and ablation studies that demonstrate the effectiveness and efficiency of the proposed method.
- The authors have provided a comprehensive set of experiments, including evaluations on GPT-3.5-turbo, and have included an ablation study to analyze the effectiveness of each component of the method.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed discussion on the limitations and potential negative societal impacts of the proposed method, which is crucial for understanding the broader implications of the research.
- The evaluation is limited to GPT-3.5-turbo, which does not provide a comprehensive view of the method's effectiveness across different models.
- There is a lack of clarity regarding the definition of "stealthy" jailbreak prompts and how they compare to existing methods in terms of perplexity and attack success rate.
- The paper does not include a comparison with other methods, such as token-level attacks, which could have provided a more robust evaluation of the proposed method.
- The computational cost of the method, while not a significant issue, is not discussed in detail, which could be important for practical applications.

**Questions:**
- Can you provide more details on the limitations of the proposed method and how they might impact the practical deployment of the attack?
- How does the proposed method compare to other methods, such as token-level attacks, in terms of effectiveness and efficiency?
- Could you clarify the definition of "stealthy" jailbreak prompts and how they are differentiated from other types of prompts?
- How does the method handle the issue of semantic meaning in the generated prompts, and what steps are taken to ensure that the prompts remain semantically meaningful during the optimization process?
- Can you discuss the computational cost of the method in more detail, including the time required for each component of the method and any potential strategies for reducing this cost?
