 **Summary:**
The paper investigates the effectiveness of pre-trained visual encoders in video game environments, specifically focusing on Minecraft, Minecraft Dungeons, and Counter-Strike: Global Offensive. It evaluates various models including end-to-end trained and pre-trained visual encoders, and explores how these models perform with different amounts of training data. The study reveals that pre-trained models like DINOv2 can outperform task-specific models, particularly in scenarios with limited data. However, the paper faces criticism for its lack of novelty, limited scope in terms of game environments, and insufficient discussion on the generalizability of the findings.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written and easy to follow, with clear descriptions of the experiments and results.
- It provides a comprehensive evaluation of different visual encoders in various video game environments, which is crucial for understanding their effectiveness.
- The authors have conducted a thorough set of experiments, covering a wide range of models and scenarios, which is commendable for its depth and breadth.
- The paper presents a systematic study on the use of pre-trained visual encoders in video games, which is a timely and important topic given the increasing availability of such models.
- The study includes a diverse set of visual encoders and games, which enhances the generalizability of the findings.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks novelty as it primarily compares pre-trained models with end-to-end trained models without proposing new models or methods.
- The experiments are limited to a few game environments, which may not generalize well to other game domains.
- The discussion on the results is insufficient, particularly in explaining the differences in performance between different models and how these differences impact the conclusions.
- The paper does not adequately address the generalizability of the findings beyond the specific games tested.
- There is a lack of clarity in the presentation of results, with some figures and tables being difficult to interpret, which could hinder the reader's understanding of the data.
- The paper could benefit from a more detailed discussion on the limitations of the study and the potential implications of the findings.

**Questions:**
- How do the authors ensure that the results are not cherry-picked or biased towards specific models?
- Can the authors provide more detailed explanations for the differences in performance between different models, particularly those that underperform or outperform the expected models?
- How do the authors justify the choice of game environments for the study, and what steps were taken to ensure the generalizability of the findings to other game domains?
- Could the authors clarify the presentation of results in figures and tables to improve readability and understanding?
- How do the authors address the potential limitations of the study, such as the scope of the experiments and the generalizability of the findings?
