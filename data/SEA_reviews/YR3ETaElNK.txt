 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel method for fine-tuning Multi-Modal Large Language Models (MLLMs) by focusing on adjusting the LayerNorm parameters within attention blocks. This approach is claimed to significantly reduce the number of trainable parameters and memory usage compared to traditional methods like full parameter fine-tuning or LoRA. The method's effectiveness is demonstrated through empirical results across various models, including MM-Vicuna and MM-LLaMA2, showing improvements in performance on tasks like VQA and captioning. The paper also discusses the implications of this method in terms of its simplicity, efficiency, and the reduction in computational resources required.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel approach to fine-tuning Multi-Modal Large Language Models (MLLMs) by focusing on adjusting the LayerNorm parameters, which is a significant departure from traditional methods like full parameter fine-tuning or LoRA.
- The proposed method shows promising results in terms of performance, reducing the number of trainable parameters and memory usage, which is crucial for large-scale models.
- The paper is well-written, making it easy to follow, and the experimental results are clearly presented, showing a significant reduction in GPU memory usage compared to other methods.
- The paper provides a comprehensive analysis of the role of LayerNorm in adapting LLMs to the multi-modal domain and improving the expressive power of the model.
- The method's simplicity and efficiency in terms of computational resources required make it an attractive alternative to traditional methods.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed analysis of the impact of different learning rates on the performance of the proposed method, which could provide valuable insights into its effectiveness.
- The experiments conducted are limited to specific models and datasets, which might not generalize well to other models or datasets.
- The paper does not provide sufficient theoretical justification for why tuning the LayerNorm parameters is effective in adapting LLMs to the multi-modal domain.
- The method's effectiveness in terms of performance improvement is not consistently shown across all tasks and models.
- The paper does not sufficiently discuss the limitations of the proposed method, such as its sensitivity to hyper-parameters and its applicability to different model architectures.
- The paper's presentation could be improved to make it more accessible to a broader audience, particularly those not familiar with the specific models and datasets used.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors provide a more detailed analysis of the impact of different learning rates on the performance of the proposed method?
- How does the proposed method perform when applied to other models or datasets, and what are the implications for its generalizability?
- Could the authors provide a theoretical justification for why tuning the LayerNorm parameters is effective in adapting LLMs to the multi-modal domain?
- Why is the vision-language connector more important than the LayerNorm in terms of performance improvement?
- How does the proposed method compare to other parameter-efficient fine-tuning methods like QLoRA and Adapters?
- Can the authors clarify the inconsistencies in the performance improvements reported across different tasks and models?
- How does the proposed method handle the sensitivity to hyper-parameters, and what are the implications for its practical application?
