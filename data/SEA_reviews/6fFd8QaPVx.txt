 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel method for binarizing convolutional neural networks (CNNs) by decomposing 2D convolutions into 1D convolutions, which are then binarized using the XNOR-Net method. This approach aims to reduce computational complexity and improve efficiency on edge devices. The proposed method, named OneBNet, is tested on various datasets including FashionMNIST, CIFAR-10, and ImageNet, showing improved accuracy and reduced latency. The methodology involves adjusting the activation distribution and non-linear activation functions to enhance performance, and the results are supported by extensive experiments and comparisons with existing methods. However, concerns were raised regarding the novelty of the approach, the clarity of the presentation, and the comparative analysis with other state-of-the-art methods.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, easy to follow, and includes detailed experimental results, which are crucial for understanding the proposed methodology.
- The proposed method is simple and effective, with a straightforward approach to binarizing 2D convolutions using 1D convolutions, which is well-explained and supported by extensive experiments.
- The paper provides a thorough comparison of the proposed method with other binarized convolutional networks (BCNNs) on various datasets, including FashionMNIST, CIFAR-10, and ImageNet, demonstrating its effectiveness.
- The method's simplicity and the reduced number of learnable parameters contribute to its efficiency and potential for real-time applications.
- The paper includes a detailed discussion on the computational complexity and memory usage of the proposed method, which is beneficial for understanding its practical implications.

**Weaknesses:**
- The novelty of the proposed method is questionable as it primarily involves binarizing 2D convolutions using 1D convolutions, which has been previously explored in other works.
- The paper lacks a detailed discussion on the computational complexity and memory usage of the baseline models, which could affect the comparative analysis.
- The paper does not provide a comprehensive comparison with other state-of-the-art methods on various datasets, which could limit the understanding of the method's effectiveness and applicability.
- The presentation of the paper needs improvement, particularly in the clarity of figures and the organization of the content.
- The paper does not adequately address the limitations of the proposed method, which could include its applicability to other tasks beyond image classification and its scalability to larger models or datasets.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors clarify the novelty of their method compared to existing works, especially those that have explored similar approaches to binarizing convolutional networks?
- How does the proposed method compare in terms of computational complexity and memory usage with other state-of-the-art methods, especially when scaled up to larger models or datasets?
- Can the authors provide a more detailed comparison with other BCNNs, particularly those that have been shown to perform well on image classification tasks?
- How does the proposed method perform on other tasks beyond image classification, such as object detection or segmentation?
- Could the authors elaborate on the training details, particularly the use of the Straight-Through Estimator and the learning rates during training?
- What are the specific advantages of using 1-D binarized convolutions over 2-D binarized convolutions in terms of computational efficiency and accuracy?
- How does the proposed method handle the challenges of binarizing activations and weights, and what are the implications for the model's performance and training dynamics?
