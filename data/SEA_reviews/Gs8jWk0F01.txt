 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel deep reinforcement learning (DRL) model designed to address the dynamic Capacitated Vehicle Routing Problem (CVRP), where customer information is gradually disclosed during the delivery process. The model employs a dynamic attention network to adapt to changes in customer disclosure status, allowing for real-time tracking of these changes. The authors compare their proposed model with traditional methods such as LKH and OR-Tools, demonstrating its computational efficiency and solution quality. However, the paper suffers from significant presentation issues, including a lack of clarity in problem formulation and methodology, and insufficient experimental validation.

**Strengths:**
- The paper addresses an important and realistic problem in the field of vehicle routing, specifically the dynamic Capacitated Vehicle Routing Problem (CVRP), which is of significant practical relevance.
- The introduction of a dynamic attention network within a deep reinforcement learning (DRL) model is innovative, allowing for real-time tracking of customer disclosure status and adaptability to dynamic environments.
- The paper presents a novel approach by integrating dynamic attention mechanisms into DRL, which is a significant advancement in the field of dynamic routing problems.
- The proposed methodology is clearly presented, making it accessible and understandable to a broader audience.
- The paper is well-organized and includes a detailed mathematical formulation of the dynamic CVRP, which aids in understanding the problem's complexities.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks clarity in the problem formulation, particularly in the mathematical model section, which could benefit from more detailed explanations and possibly a visual aid like a table.
- There is a noticeable absence of detailed methodological descriptions, making it difficult to fully grasp the proposed DRL model and its components.
- The experimental validation is insufficient, with a limited number of instances tested (1024) and no comparison with other deep learning methods, which could undermine the robustness of the results.
- The paper does not adequately address the computational efficiency of the proposed model, which is crucial for real-world applications.
- The paper lacks a comprehensive literature review, particularly in the area of dynamic routing problems, which could provide a richer context for the research.
- The presentation of the paper needs improvement, particularly in the clarity of the problem formulation and the methodology, which could enhance the paper's readability and comprehensibility.

**Questions:**
- Could you provide more details on the dynamic attention network, specifically how it adapts to changes in customer disclosure status?
- How does the proposed DRL model compare in terms of computational efficiency with other deep learning methods, and what are the implications of its computational requirements for real-world applications?
- Can you clarify the mathematical formulation of the dynamic CVRP, particularly the role and interpretation of variables like r_ijk in equation (5)?
- How does the proposed DRL model handle the dynamic nature of the CVRP, especially in terms of its ability to adapt to new customer disclosures in real-time?
- Could you elaborate on the methodology used in the experiments, particularly the role of the dummy depot and the impact of different numbers of customer points on the model's performance?
- What are the implications of the computational time differences observed between the proposed model and other methods, and how do these differences affect the practical applicability of the model?
