 **Summary:**
The paper introduces Parrot, a novel approach for generating high-quality instruction-tuning data for multi-turn conversations. It begins by training a Parrot-Ask model to mimic human questioning styles, which is then used to engage in multi-turn dialogues with ChatGPT. These dialogues, totaling 40K, are then used to train a Parrot-Chat model, which shows improved performance over existing models on various benchmarks. The paper also introduces a new evaluation benchmark, MT-Bench++, which includes additional questions to assess multi-turn capabilities more thoroughly. Despite its innovative approach and the quality of the dataset, concerns are raised about the incremental nature of the contributions and the limited novelty in the methodology.

**Strengths:**
- The paper addresses a significant and relevant problem in the field of multi-turn conversations in chat models, which is crucial for enhancing the performance of chat models.
- The authors have conducted a thorough analysis of existing multi-turn instruction-tuning data, identifying their limitations, and propose a novel approach to generate high-quality instruction-tuning data using the Parrot-Ask model.
- The paper is well-organized, easy to follow, and provides detailed explanations of the methodology, including the training of the Parrot-Ask model, the generation of instruction-tuning data, and the training of the Parrot-Chat model.
- The introduction of the Parrot-40K dataset, which is superior to existing datasets in terms of topic diversity, number of turns, and resemblance to human conversation, is a significant contribution to the field.
- The paper includes a new evaluation benchmark, MT-Bench++, which assesses the multi-turn capabilities of chat models more thoroughly, providing a valuable tool for evaluating the performance of different models.
- The authors have made the code and datasets publicly available, which is crucial for advancing research in this area.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper's main contribution, the Parrot-Ask model, is seen as an incremental improvement over existing methods, as it primarily involves training a model to ask questions based on answers, which is not significantly novel.
- The methodology of using GPT-3.5-turbo to generate answers and the Parrot-Ask model to generate questions is considered a simple application of instruction tuning, which might not be sufficiently novel for a top-tier conference like ICLR.
- The paper lacks detailed information on the training and evaluation of the Parrot-Ask model, which is crucial for understanding the method's effectiveness and reproducibility.
- The paper's claims about the Parrot-Ask model's ability to generate human-like instructions are not substantiated by empirical evidence, which could weaken the paper's argument.
- There is a lack of comparison with other open-source chat models, which could provide a more comprehensive evaluation of the proposed method.
- The paper's evaluation benchmark, MT-Bench++, while useful, is not sufficiently novel or comprehensive to fully demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed method.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors provide more details on the training and evaluation of the Parrot-Ask model, including the training data, model architecture, training details, and evaluation metrics?
- How does the Parrot-Ask model compare to other open-source chat models, particularly in terms of performance and efficiency?
- What are the specific advantages of the Parrot-Ask model over other instruction-tuning methods, and how does it address the limitations of existing methods?
- Can the authors provide more empirical evidence to support the claim that the Parrot-Ask model generates human-like instructions?
- How does the Parrot-Chat model perform when trained on different datasets, and what are the implications for the generalizability of the method?
- Could the authors clarify the role and effectiveness of the Parrot-Ask model in generating high-quality instruction-tuning data, especially in comparison to other methods?
