 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel unsupervised method for detecting recurrent patterns in neural recordings, utilizing a set of spatiotemporal filters optimized through backpropagation. This method is designed to handle large-scale recordings, demonstrating significant speed improvements over existing methods. It is tested on both synthetic and real neural data, showing promising results in terms of accuracy and efficiency. The method's approach is innovative, combining elements of convolutional non-negative matrix factorization (convNMF) with backpropagation, which allows for the estimation of uncertainty in pattern detection without requiring multiple optimization runs. Despite its strengths, the paper has some limitations, including the assumption of known sequence numbers and the need for further validation in more complex scenarios.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces an unsupervised method for detecting recurrent patterns in neural recordings, which is a significant contribution to the field.
- The proposed method is scalable and efficient, demonstrated through speed benchmarks that show significant improvements over existing methods.
- The method provides uncertainty estimates for the patterns detected, which is a valuable feature for understanding the reliability of the results.
- The paper is well-written and clearly presents the methodology, making it accessible and understandable.
- The experiments are comprehensive, including both synthetic and real neural data, which strengthens the validity of the results.
- The use of backpropagation for optimization is a novel approach that enhances the method's effectiveness and efficiency.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper assumes that the number of sequences is known, which may not be the case in real-world scenarios.
- The method's effectiveness is not thoroughly tested in scenarios with weak or overlapping patterns, which could limit its applicability.
- There is a lack of discussion on the limitations of the proposed method, which is crucial for understanding its scope and applicability.
- The paper could benefit from a more detailed comparison with other methods, especially in terms of computational efficiency and pattern detection accuracy.
- The paper does not provide a detailed discussion on the scalability of the method with larger datasets, which is crucial for its practical application.
- The method's reliance on the backpropagation of gradients raises concerns about its biological plausibility, as it is not a naturally occurring process in biological neural networks.

**Questions:**
- How does the method perform in scenarios where the number of sequences is not known?
- Can the method be adapted to detect patterns in scenarios with weak or overlapping patterns?
- How does the method compare in terms of computational efficiency and pattern detection accuracy with other existing methods?
- Is there a way to incorporate the backpropagation of gradients in a more biologically plausible manner?
- Can the method be scaled to handle larger datasets, and how does its performance compare in such scenarios?
- How does the method handle the complexity of real-world neural data, particularly in terms of noise and variability?
- Is there a possibility to extend the method to handle more complex patterns or to incorporate additional data types, such as calcium imaging data?
