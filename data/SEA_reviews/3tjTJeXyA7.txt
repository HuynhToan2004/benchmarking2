 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel Channel-dimension Fourier transform learning (CFTL) mechanism for image enhancement, leveraging Fourier transforms to enhance global representation in image processing. CFTL applies Fourier transforms to the channel dimension, allowing for more efficient adjustment of global information in image enhancement tasks. The method is compatible with existing architectures and has been tested across various image enhancement tasks, showing improvements in performance over traditional methods. Despite its innovative approach, the paper is criticized for its lack of novelty in applying Fourier transforms to image enhancement and the incremental nature of its contributions. The method's effectiveness is demonstrated through experiments, but the paper is criticized for its lack of clarity in explaining the mechanism's operation and its limited novelty.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel approach to image enhancement by applying Fourier transforms to the channel dimension, which is a significant departure from traditional methods that focus on spatial dimensions.
- The proposed method is compatible with existing image enhancement network architectures, making it easy to integrate and adapt to various applications.
- Extensive experiments demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed method across multiple image enhancement tasks, including low-light image enhancement, exposure correction, SDR2HDR translation, and underwater image enhancement.
- The paper is well-written and easy to follow, with clear explanations of the method and its implementation.
- The method's effectiveness is validated through quantitative evaluations and comparisons with existing methods, showing improvements in performance.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks clarity in explaining the mechanism of the proposed method, particularly how the Fourier transforms are applied and their impact on image enhancement.
- The novelty of the paper is limited as Fourier transforms have been extensively used in image processing, and the application of CFTL to image enhancement is seen as incremental.
- The paper could benefit from more detailed comparisons with existing methods, including a broader range of metrics and visual examples to demonstrate the superiority of the proposed method.
- The paper's contribution is seen as limited as the proposed CFTL is a simple extension of existing Fourier transform-based methods, and the improvements in performance are marginal.
- There are several typographical and formatting errors in the paper, which detract from its professional presentation.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors clarify the mechanism of the proposed method, particularly how the Fourier transforms are applied and their impact on image enhancement?
- How does the proposed method compare to existing methods in terms of computational efficiency and resource requirements?
- Could the authors provide more detailed comparisons with existing methods, including a broader range of metrics and visual examples to demonstrate the superiority of the proposed method?
- How does the proposed method perform in terms of generalization across different types of images, and what are the limitations of its applicability?
- Can the authors provide more detailed explanations of the experimental setups and results, including the specifics of the training and testing datasets and the methodology used?
