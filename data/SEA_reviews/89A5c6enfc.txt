 **Summary:**
The paper explores the local graph clustering problem using noisy node labels, proposing a novel method that constructs a weighted graph based on these labels to enhance clustering performance. Theoretical analysis is provided to demonstrate the effectiveness of this approach, particularly under the stochastic block model. The paper also presents empirical results showing that the proposed method outperforms existing baselines, though the improvement is modest and sometimes marginal. The methodology involves a diffusion process that utilizes noisy labels to weight edges, which can improve clustering accuracy. However, concerns are raised about the novelty of the method and the clarity of its advantages over existing methods.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, with clear and concise sections that effectively communicate the problem, methodology, and results.
- The authors have provided a theoretical analysis and empirical evidence to support the proposed method, which is backed by both theoretical and experimental results.
- The methodology is straightforward and easy to implement, with a simple yet effective approach to utilizing noisy labels for local graph clustering.
- The paper is well-organized, making it easy to follow and understand, and it provides a thorough review of related work.
- The theoretical analysis is sound, and the experimental results are convincing, demonstrating the effectiveness of the proposed method.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed comparison with existing methods, particularly in terms of computational efficiency and scalability.
- The novelty of the proposed method is questionable as it mainly involves weighting edges based on noisy labels, which is a common approach in graph clustering.
- The theoretical analysis is limited to specific models and conditions, which may not generalize well to other graph models or real-world scenarios.
- The empirical results show only marginal improvements over existing methods, which raises concerns about the practical effectiveness of the proposed method.
- The paper could benefit from a more detailed discussion on the limitations of the proposed method and a broader comparison with other state-of-the-art methods.
- There is a lack of clarity in the presentation of the theoretical results, particularly in the main text, which could be improved by moving some of the theoretical discussions to the appendix.

**Questions:**
- How does the proposed method compare in terms of computational efficiency and scalability with other existing methods?
- Could the authors provide a more detailed comparison with other state-of-the-art methods, including a discussion on the advantages and disadvantages of the proposed method?
- How does the proposed method handle different types of noise in the labels, and what are the implications for the clustering performance?
- Can the authors provide a more detailed discussion on the theoretical assumptions and their implications for the practical applicability of the method?
- How does the proposed method perform under different graph models, and what are the implications for the clustering performance in these scenarios?
