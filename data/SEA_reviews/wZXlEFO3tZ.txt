 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a method for estimating counterfactual distributions using kernel Stein discrepancies, focusing on the doubly robust estimation of counterfactual distributions. The authors propose a novel approach by integrating a kernel method with a counterfactual setting, aiming to address the limitations of existing methods that primarily focus on mean estimation. The paper provides theoretical guarantees for the proposed estimator and demonstrates its effectiveness through experiments. Despite the innovative approach, the paper has been critiqued for its limited empirical evaluation, lack of comparison with existing methods, and insufficient discussion on the computational complexity and scalability of the proposed method.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel approach to estimating counterfactual distributions using kernel Stein discrepancies, which is a significant advancement in the field of causal inference.
- The theoretical analysis provided is robust and well-supported, with clear explanations of the methodology and its implications.
- The paper is well-written and clearly presented, making it accessible to readers with varying backgrounds in the field.
- The proposed method is shown to be effective in both semi-synthetic and real data experiments, demonstrating its practical utility.
- The use of a kernel method in the counterfactual setting is innovative and addresses a gap in existing literature, which primarily focuses on mean estimation.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a comprehensive discussion on the computational complexity and scalability of the proposed method, which is crucial for understanding its applicability in large-scale or complex datasets.
- There is a notable absence of comparisons with existing methods, which makes it difficult to assess the relative performance and novelty of the proposed approach.
- The empirical evaluation is limited, focusing mainly on synthetic data, which may not adequately reflect the performance of the method in real-world scenarios.
- The paper does not provide sufficient details on the estimation of nuisance parameters, which is a critical component of the proposed method.
- The paper could benefit from more extensive simulations and real data examples to better demonstrate the effectiveness and applicability of the proposed method.
- The presentation of the paper could be improved to make it more accessible to a broader audience, especially those not familiar with the intricacies of kernel methods and counterfactual distributions.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors provide more details on the computational complexity and scalability of the proposed method, particularly in terms of the number of parameters and the size of the dataset?
- How does the proposed method compare to existing methods in terms of performance and computational efficiency?
- Could the authors provide more examples or simulations that demonstrate the effectiveness of the method in different scenarios, such as time-varying treatments or different types of counterfactual distributions?
- How are the nuisance parameters estimated, and what are the implications of using different estimators for the nuisance parameters?
- Could the authors clarify the assumptions made in the theoretical analysis, particularly regarding the regularity conditions and their practical implications?
- How does the method perform in real-world scenarios, and what are the limitations or challenges in applying the method in such settings?
