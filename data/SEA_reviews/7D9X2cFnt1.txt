 **Summary:**
The paper introduces Elastic Feature Consolidation (EFC), a novel method for class-incremental learning that addresses the challenges of cold start scenarios. EFC utilizes a regularization loss based on the Empirical Feature Matrix (EFM) to manage feature drift and maintain model plasticity. It also introduces an asymmetric prototype replay loss to balance prototype rehearsal with new task data. The method is evaluated on several benchmarks, demonstrating superior performance compared to existing methods. Despite its strengths, the paper is criticized for its limited novelty and the need for more comprehensive comparisons with recent state-of-the-art methods.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, clear, and easy to follow, with a clear presentation of the proposed method and its components.
- The methodology is technically sound, with a novel approach using an Empirical Feature Matrix (EFM) to manage feature drift, which is supported by theoretical analysis and empirical evidence.
- The paper demonstrates strong experimental results, showing significant improvements over existing methods in both cold start and warm start scenarios.
- The paper provides a comprehensive evaluation across multiple datasets and tasks, demonstrating the effectiveness of the proposed method in various settings.
- The use of asymmetric prototype replay loss is highlighted as a significant contribution, allowing for a balance between prototype rehearsal and new task data.
- The paper is well-organized, with a clear presentation of the problem, methodology, and results, making it accessible to readers.

**Weaknesses:**
- The novelty of the method is questioned, as it appears to be a combination of existing techniques such as EFCIL and prototype rehearsal, which have been previously explored.
- The paper lacks a detailed comparison with recent state-of-the-art methods, which could have provided a clearer understanding of the method's advantages and limitations.
- There are some inconsistencies and potential errors in the mathematical formulations and experimental results, which could affect the reliability and reproducibility of the findings.
- The paper could benefit from a more thorough discussion on the limitations of the proposed method, including the computational complexity and the practical implications of the empirical feature matrix.
- The paper's evaluation could be strengthened by including more diverse datasets and tasks to better demonstrate the generalizability of the method.

**Questions:**
- Could you clarify the role and significance of the damping term η in the regularization loss?
- How does the paper address the potential issue of over-regularization in the EFM, especially in scenarios where the feature matrix is not well-conditioned?
- Is there a risk of over-fitting in the model due to the use of the empirical feature matrix?
- How does the proposed method compare with recent state-of-the-art methods, particularly those that use different regularization techniques or different datasets?
- Could you provide more details on the computational complexity of the proposed method and discuss potential solutions for mitigating the storage costs associated with the empirical feature matrix?
- What are the specific advantages of the proposed method over other recent methods in terms of computational efficiency and practical applicability?
