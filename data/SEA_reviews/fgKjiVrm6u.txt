 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel methodology for extracting theorems from proofs using a neural network, focusing on Metamath's proof trees. This methodology, termed REFACTOR, converts proofs into a binary classification problem by treating nodes as potential theorems and edges as their dependencies. The model is trained to identify nodes that can be extracted as theorems, which are then used to refactor proofs and potentially improve theorem proving. The approach demonstrates the extraction of both known and new theorems, showing potential for refining existing theorem libraries and enhancing automated theorem proving. However, the paper's scope is limited to Metamath, and the generalizability to other theorem proving systems is unclear.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel approach to extracting theorems from proofs, which is a significant contribution to the field of automated theorem proving.
- The methodology is clearly presented, making it easy to understand and follow, and includes detailed explanations of the proof expansion process and the algorithm for theorem extraction.
- The experiments are well-designed, demonstrating the effectiveness of the proposed method in identifying both known and new theorems, and showing improvements in theorem proving performance.
- The paper provides a clear motivation and justification for the research, highlighting the potential impact of the findings on the field.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper primarily focuses on Metamath, limiting its generalizability to other theorem proving systems. It would be beneficial to explore how the method could be adapted to other formal systems.
- The paper lacks a comprehensive discussion on the limitations of the method, which could help in understanding its practical applicability and potential drawbacks.
- The paper could benefit from a more detailed comparison with existing methods, particularly in terms of performance and efficiency.
- The paper's presentation could be improved by including more formal definitions and clearer explanations of the proof expansion process and the theorem extraction algorithm.
- There is a need for a more rigorous discussion on the scalability of the proposed method, especially concerning the computational resources required for training and the potential impact on performance.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors provide more details on how the method could be adapted to other theorem proving systems, such as Lean or Coq?
- How does the method compare in terms of performance and efficiency with existing methods, particularly in terms of computational resources and training time?
- Could the authors elaborate on the potential limitations of the method and discuss scenarios where it might not be effective?
- How does the method handle the potential for overfitting, especially given the small size of the training set?
- Can the authors clarify the definition and role of the "target" nodes in the proof trees, and provide a more detailed explanation of the proof expansion process?
- How does the method ensure the correctness and validity of the extracted theorems, and what measures are in place to prevent the extraction of incorrect or redundant theorems?
