 **Summary:**
The paper introduces Quantile-Guided Diffusion Policy (qGDP), an offline reinforcement learning (RL) method that utilizes a quantile network to label the training dataset and guides the diffusion model training with these labels. The qGDP method allows for the adjustment of action generation preferences by changing the input condition and guidance scale without requiring model retraining. This approach aims to reduce computational costs and enhance generalization capabilities, as demonstrated through experiments on D4RL datasets. The methodology is compared to existing diffusion-based offline RL methods, showing promising results in terms of computational efficiency and sample generation. However, the paper's contributions are seen as incremental, and the experimental results do not convincingly outperform existing methods.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces an innovative approach to offline RL by integrating a quantile network to guide the diffusion model, which is a novel application of diffusion models in this context.
- The methodology is clearly presented, with well-written explanations that facilitate understanding, particularly in the experimental results section.
- The paper demonstrates the potential of using diffusion models in offline RL, showing that they can be effectively utilized for action generation without requiring extensive computational resources.
- The approach of using quantile networks to label the training dataset and guide the diffusion model is a promising direction for future research in offline RL.
- The paper provides a detailed analysis of the hyperparameters and their impact on the performance of the qGDP model, which is crucial for practical applications.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a comprehensive comparison with existing diffusion-based offline RL methods, such as SfBC, which could provide a more robust evaluation of the proposed method.
- The experimental results are not convincingly superior to existing methods, with some tasks showing marginal improvements or even inferior performance compared to baseline methods.
- The paper's contribution is seen as incremental, primarily extending existing diffusion-based methods to offline RL without significant novelty or substantial improvements in performance.
- The paper does not sufficiently address the scalability of the method to more complex environments or larger datasets, which is a significant limitation for broader applicability.
- The paper lacks a detailed discussion on the limitations of the proposed method, which is crucial for understanding its applicability and potential challenges in real-world scenarios.
- There are concerns about the method's robustness, particularly in environments with high-dimensional state spaces, where the accuracy of the quantile network's predictions might be compromised.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors provide a more detailed comparison with existing diffusion-based offline RL methods, such as SfBC, to better establish the novelty and effectiveness of their approach?
- How does the proposed method compare in terms of computational efficiency and sample generation diversity to other diffusion-based offline RL methods?
- What are the specific advantages of using the qGDP method over other diffusion-based methods in terms of computational efficiency and sample generation quality?
- Could the authors provide a more detailed discussion on the limitations of their proposed method, particularly in terms of scalability and robustness?
- How does the qGDP method handle high-dimensional state spaces, and what measures are in place to ensure the accuracy of the quantile network's predictions in such environments?
- Could the authors provide a more detailed discussion on the potential societal impacts of their work, particularly in terms of the ethical implications of using diffusion models in offline RL?
