 **Summary:**
The paper presents a novel approach using Physics Informed Neural Networks (PINNs) to solve the continuous-time Hamilton-Jacobi-Bellman (HJB) equation, aiming to approximate the value function of a continuous-time Markov Decision Process (CTMDP) by training a PINN through an epsilon-scheduling iterative process. The methodology is designed to converge towards the viscosity solution of the HJB equation, which is claimed to have unique and stable properties. The paper evaluates the proposed method on several control tasks, showing that it can achieve competitive results compared to traditional reinforcement learning algorithms. However, the experimental results are limited to simple environments, and the paper lacks a comprehensive comparison with existing methods and a detailed discussion on the convergence and stability of the proposed solution.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, with clear and concise descriptions of the proposed method and the HJB equation.
- The proposed method is innovative, using PINNs to approximate the value function of a CTMDP, which is a novel approach in the field of continuous-time reinforcement learning.
- The paper includes a comprehensive literature review, which is beneficial for readers seeking a deeper understanding of the subject matter.
- The experimental results demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed method in solving control tasks, showing that it can achieve competitive results compared to traditional reinforcement learning algorithms.
- The paper addresses an important problem in the field of reinforcement learning, focusing on the continuous-time setting which is often overlooked.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed discussion on the convergence and stability of the proposed solution, which is crucial for understanding the reliability and applicability of the method.
- The experimental results are limited to simple environments, which may not adequately demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed method in more complex scenarios.
- The paper does not include a detailed comparison with existing methods, which could help in understanding the strengths and weaknesses of the proposed approach in relation to other state-of-the-art techniques.
- The paper does not discuss the limitations of the proposed method, which is essential for understanding the scope and applicability of the findings.
- There are some typographical errors and inconsistencies in the notation and definitions throughout the paper, which could confuse the readers.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors provide a more detailed discussion on the convergence and stability of the proposed solution, possibly including theoretical or empirical evidence?
- How does the proposed method compare with other existing methods for solving the HJB equation, especially in terms of computational efficiency and accuracy?
- Could the authors provide a detailed comparison of the proposed method with other state-of-the-art techniques in the field of continuous-time reinforcement learning?
- How does the proposed method handle the non-smoothness of the value function, especially in areas where it may lead to suboptimal control?
- Could the authors clarify the definitions and notations used in the paper, particularly in the equations and theorems, to improve the clarity and readability of the manuscript?
