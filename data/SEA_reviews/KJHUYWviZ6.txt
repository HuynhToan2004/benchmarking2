 **Summary:**
The paper investigates the computational complexity of socially fair regression and low-rank approximation, focusing on the differences in complexity between these two problems. It demonstrates that while fair regression can be solved in polynomial time, fair low-rank approximation requires exponential time under certain conditions. The paper also introduces bicriteria approximation algorithms that run in polynomial time for fair low-rank approximation and fair column subset selection. Theoretical analyses are provided, along with experimental results showing the effectiveness of the proposed methods. However, the paper has been critiqued for its clarity in explaining the theoretical results, the lack of comparison with existing works, and the limited scope of its experimental evaluations.

**Strengths:**
- The paper addresses an important and relevant problem in machine learning, particularly the optimization of fairness in regression and low-rank approximation, which is crucial for ensuring fairness in various applications.
- The authors provide a clear and detailed explanation of the theoretical results, making the paper accessible to readers not specialized in the field.
- The paper introduces novel algorithms for solving socially fair regression and low-rank approximation problems, which could be useful in practical scenarios.
- The paper is well-structured, with a clear introduction and a logical flow of content, making it easy to follow.
- The experimental results demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed methods, showing a significant improvement over existing methods in certain scenarios.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a comprehensive comparison with existing works, particularly those that also address fairness in regression and low-rank approximation. This makes it difficult to assess the novelty and effectiveness of the proposed methods.
- The presentation of the theoretical results is not always clear, especially in sections where complex mathematical notations are used without adequate explanation.
- The experimental evaluations are limited in scope, focusing only on a few datasets and not including a broader range of scenarios or comparisons with other methods.
- The paper does not sufficiently address the limitations of the proposed methods, which could affect the practical applicability and scalability of the solutions.
- The paper could benefit from a more detailed discussion on the computational complexity and the scalability of the proposed algorithms, especially in large-scale applications.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors clarify the distinction between "socially fair" and "fair" in the context used in the paper?
- How does the proposed method compare with existing works in terms of computational efficiency and effectiveness in practical scenarios?
- Could the authors provide more details on the experimental setup, including the specific datasets used and the parameters of the algorithms tested?
- What are the practical implications of the theoretical results presented in the paper, particularly in terms of their applicability to real-world scenarios?
- How do the authors address the computational complexity and scalability issues in their proposed methods, especially in large-scale applications?
