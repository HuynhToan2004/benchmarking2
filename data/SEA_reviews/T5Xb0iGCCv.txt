 **Summary:**
The paper introduces Neur2RO, a novel method for solving two-stage robust optimization (2RO) problems by leveraging machine learning to estimate the value function of the second-stage problem. This method integrates neural networks into the column-and-constraint generation (CCG) algorithm, enhancing its efficiency and scalability. The approach is validated through experiments on benchmark problems, demonstrating improvements over existing methods. Despite its innovative approach, the paper faces criticism for its limited scope in terms of the types of 2RO problems it addresses and the lack of broader experimental validation.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel approach by integrating neural networks into the column-and-constraint generation (CCG) algorithm, which is a significant advancement in the field of two-stage robust optimization (2RO).
- The methodology is clearly explained, making the paper accessible and understandable, which is crucial for replication and further research.
- The proposed method is validated through experiments, showing improvements over existing methods, and the paper is well-written, making it easy to follow.
- The use of machine learning to solve 2RO problems, particularly in the context of robust optimization, is a novel and interesting approach.
- The paper provides a comprehensive discussion of related work, highlighting the novelty of the proposed method.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper primarily focuses on two specific types of 2RO problems (knapsack and capital budgeting), which limits the generalizability of the findings to other types of 2RO problems.
- The method's performance on larger or more complex instances is not adequately tested, which could limit its practical applicability.
- The paper lacks a broader experimental validation, which could have provided more robust evidence of the method's effectiveness.
- The method's scalability and performance on more complex instances, such as those with higher dimensions, are not thoroughly explored.
- The paper does not discuss the limitations of the proposed method, which is crucial for understanding its applicability and potential drawbacks.
- The paper could benefit from a more detailed discussion on the computational complexity of the proposed method compared to existing methods.

**Questions:**
- Can the proposed method be applied to other types of 2RO problems beyond the knapsack and capital budgeting problems?
- How does the proposed method perform on larger or more complex instances, and what are the computational and memory requirements?
- Could the authors provide more details on the training data used and how it affects the performance of the method?
- How does the method perform when the uncertainty set is large or complex, and what are the implications for the scalability and performance of the method?
- Is there a potential for the method to be adapted to handle more general types of 2RO problems, such as those with different types of uncertainty or different objective functions?
- How does the proposed method compare in terms of computational complexity and efficiency to existing methods, particularly in scenarios where the uncertainty set is large or complex?
