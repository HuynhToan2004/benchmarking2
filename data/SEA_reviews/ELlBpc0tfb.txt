 **Summary:**
The paper introduces MedJourney, a novel method for counterfactual medical image generation, leveraging GPT-4 to process medical reports and generate natural language descriptions of disease progression. These descriptions are then used to train a latent diffusion model, enabling the generation of medical images that reflect the prescribed changes. The method is evaluated using the MIMIC-CXR dataset, demonstrating superior performance over existing methods. However, the paper is critiqued for its limited scope to the MIMIC-CXR dataset, lack of detailed analysis on the quality of generated images, and potential for hallucinations in the generated images.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, clear, and easy to follow, with a clear motivation and a novel approach to medical image generation.
- The introduction of a curriculum learning scheme to train the diffusion model using more abundant single image-report pairs is innovative and addresses the scarcity of image time series data.
- The paper proposes a method for counterfactual medical image generation, which is the first of its kind, and includes a comprehensive evaluation that demonstrates the method's effectiveness.
- The methodology is sound, and the paper includes a detailed description of the experimental setup, including the dataset and evaluation metrics, which are critical for understanding the results.
- The paper provides a detailed analysis of the results, including an ablation study that shows the effectiveness of the proposed method.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper is limited to the MIMIC-CXR dataset, which may not generalize well to other medical imaging modalities or datasets, such as CT or MRI.
- The paper does not provide a detailed analysis of the quality of the generated images, which is crucial for medical applications where image quality is paramount.
- There is a lack of discussion on the potential societal impacts of the proposed method, which could include issues related to privacy and ethical considerations.
- The paper does not include a detailed comparison with other state-of-the-art methods, which could help in understanding the relative performance of the proposed method.
- The paper could benefit from more detailed visualizations and comparisons to better illustrate the effectiveness of the method.
- There are some technical issues, such as the use of incorrect terminology and unclear definitions, which could confuse readers.

**Questions:**
- How does the proposed method perform on other medical imaging modalities, such as CT or MRI?
- Can the authors provide more detailed visualizations of the generated images, including comparisons with real images and other generated images?
- How does the method handle the potential for hallucinations in the generated images, and what steps are taken to prevent this?
- Could the authors discuss the potential societal impacts of the proposed method, particularly in terms of privacy and ethical considerations?
- How does the method compare with other state-of-the-art methods in terms of performance and efficiency?
- What are the specific technical challenges involved in applying the method to other medical imaging modalities, and how are these addressed?
