 **Summary:**
The paper addresses the issue of annotation mismatches across different object detection datasets, proposing a novel method called Label-Guided Pseudo-Labeling (LGPL). This method leverages a two-stage detector to translate annotations from source datasets to target datasets, aiming to improve downstream detection performance. The paper presents a comprehensive evaluation across various datasets and architectures, demonstrating that LGPL outperforms existing baselines. Despite its innovative approach, the paper has been critiqued for its limited novelty, lack of clarity in presentation, and potential overclaiming of results.

**Strengths:**
- The paper addresses a significant and practical problem of annotation mismatches across different datasets, which is crucial for real-world applications.
- The proposed method, LGPL, is well-motivated and easy to follow, with clear mathematical formulations and illustrative figures that aid understanding.
- The paper provides a detailed analysis of annotation mismatches and conducts comprehensive experiments, demonstrating the effectiveness of the proposed method across various datasets and architectures.
- The authors have made a significant effort to ensure reproducibility by providing detailed implementation details and source code, which is a commendable practice.
- The methodology is novel and practical, with potential to address real-world challenges in object detection.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper's novelty is questioned as it appears to be a combination of existing techniques without significant innovation.
- The presentation of the paper is criticized for being unclear and confusing, with issues such as inconsistent notation and unclear descriptions of methodologies.
- The paper's evaluation is considered limited, with a lack of comparison to more recent and relevant methods, and insufficient discussion on the limitations and potential negative societal impacts of the proposed method.
- There is a noted overclaiming of results, particularly in the claims of significant improvements over existing methods, which are not substantiated by the data presented.
- The paper does not sufficiently address the issue of class mismatches, which is a significant limitation given the stated goal of addressing annotation mismatches.

**Questions:**
- Could you clarify the definitions and roles of the variables used in the paper, particularly in the methodology section?
- How does the proposed method handle scenarios where the class labels do not match between the source and target datasets?
- Can you provide more details on the training process for the label translator, including the loss function and the training data used?
- How does the method handle cases where the target dataset has classes that are not present in the source dataset?
- Could you explain the choice of using a two-stage detector for label translation and how this affects the overall performance of the method?
- How does the method handle scenarios where the source and target datasets have significant domain shifts, such as differences in image resolution or lighting conditions?
