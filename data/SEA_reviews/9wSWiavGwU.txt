 **Summary:**
The paper introduces the SwapTransformer model for lane change and overtaking decision-making in highway scenarios, leveraging a novel dataset named OSHA. The model integrates auxiliary tasks such as future point prediction and car network prediction to enhance performance. The SwapTransformer, a transformer-based architecture, is evaluated against baseline models in a simulated environment, demonstrating improved performance in various metrics like time to finish, number of overtakes, and speed difference. The paper also discusses the method's application in a rule-based driving simulator, SimPilot, and provides a detailed analysis of the model's performance across different traffic densities.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written and easy to follow, providing a clear explanation of the proposed method and its implementation.
- The introduction of the OSHA dataset is a significant contribution to the community, offering a valuable resource for future research in the field of autonomous driving.
- The SwapTransformer model demonstrates good performance in terms of lane change and overtaking decisions, showing improved results over baseline models in various metrics.
- The use of auxiliary tasks, such as future point prediction and car network prediction, is innovative and helps in understanding the dynamics of the environment, enhancing the model's performance.
- The paper provides a detailed evaluation of the model in a simulated environment, demonstrating its effectiveness in real-world scenarios.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed discussion on how the SwapTransformer specifically addresses the challenges of lane change and overtaking decisions, especially in comparison to existing methods like reinforcement learning or rule-based systems.
- The paper does not sufficiently discuss the limitations of the proposed method, which is crucial for understanding its applicability and potential shortcomings.
- The paper could benefit from a more comprehensive evaluation, including real-world testing to validate the model's performance in practical scenarios.
- The novelty of the SwapTransformer is questionable as it appears to be a straightforward application of existing transformer architectures without significant innovation.
- The paper could improve its clarity by providing more detailed explanations of the auxiliary tasks and their impact on the model's performance.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors provide a more detailed comparison of the SwapTransformer with existing methods, particularly in terms of how it addresses the specific challenges of lane change and overtaking decisions?
- How does the SwapTransformer handle edge cases or scenarios that are not commonly encountered in the training data?
- Could the authors elaborate on the design choices of the SwapTransformer, especially the use of auxiliary tasks and their impact on the model's performance?
- What are the specific challenges or limitations of the SwapTransformer that were not addressed in the current study?
- How does the SwapTransformer perform in real-world scenarios, and what are the potential implications of its deployment in such environments?
