 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel method for multi-label cluster discrimination in visual representation learning, focusing on the clustering and classification of large-scale datasets. The authors propose a new loss function that separates losses from positive and negative classes, aiming to enhance the representation power of models. This methodology is tested on various downstream tasks, including zero-shot classification and retrieval, and is shown to outperform existing methods such as OpenCLIP and UNICOM. The approach involves clustering data using off-the-shelf embedding features and selecting multiple closest centers as additional class labels, which are then used for multi-label classification. Despite its strengths, the paper is criticized for its limited novelty and the lack of a comprehensive discussion on the choice of hyperparameters.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel multi-label cluster discrimination method that significantly enhances the representation power of models compared to instance discrimination-based and cluster discrimination-based models.
- The proposed method is well-explained and easy to understand, with a clear motivation and a straightforward methodology.
- Extensive experiments are conducted to evaluate the effectiveness of the proposed method, including zero-shot classification and retrieval, and the results show that the proposed method outperforms existing methods such as OpenCLIP and UNICOM.
- The paper provides a detailed explanation of the multi-label classification loss and the clustering step, which is crucial for understanding the methodology.
- The methodology is simple and effective, as evidenced by the experimental results which demonstrate its superiority over existing methods.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed discussion on the choice of hyperparameters, particularly the number of classes (k) and the ratio of sampled negative class centers (r), which are crucial for the performance of the model.
- The novelty of the proposed method is limited as it primarily combines existing methods such as multi-label classification and clustering.
- The paper does not provide sufficient experimental results to support the effectiveness of the proposed method in image retrieval tasks, which is a significant downstream application of the method.
- The paper does not sufficiently compare the proposed method with other existing methods in terms of computational cost and memory usage, which are critical factors in practical applications.
- The methodology and its comparison with existing methods are not clearly explained in some sections of the paper, which could lead to confusion about the contributions and advantages of the proposed method.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors provide more details on the choice of hyperparameters, particularly how the number of classes and the ratio of sampled negative class centers were chosen?
- How does the proposed method compare in terms of computational cost and memory usage with other existing methods, especially in large-scale applications?
- Can the authors provide more experimental results to support the effectiveness of the proposed method in image retrieval tasks?
- How does the proposed method handle the multi-label classification loss in the clustering step, and what are the specific advantages of this approach over other existing methods?
- Could the authors clarify the methodology and the contributions of the proposed method in more detail, particularly in the introduction and methodology sections?
