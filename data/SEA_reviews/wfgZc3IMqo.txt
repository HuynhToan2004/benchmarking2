 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel method for handling label noise in classification tasks by combining loss reweighting and label correction, framed as a regression problem using the isometric log-ratio (ILR) transform. This approach aims to enhance the robustness of deep neural networks against label noise by transforming the classification problem into a regression problem, which is then solved using a robust regression algorithm. The method's effectiveness is demonstrated through experiments on various datasets, showing improvements over existing methods. However, concerns are raised about the method's novelty and its reliance on the ILR transform, which might be seen as overly complex and not sufficiently justified in terms of its benefits over simpler methods.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, clear, and easy to follow, with a clear structure that aids in understanding the proposed method.
- The idea of using the isometric log-ratio (ILR) transform to transform the classification problem into a regression problem is innovative and well-explained.
- The methodology is well-motivated, and the paper includes extensive experiments and ablation studies that support the proposed approach.
- The experimental results demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed method, showing improvements over existing methods in terms of robustness and performance.
- The paper is well-organized, with a clear overview of the method and a detailed description of each step involved in the approach.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a thorough comparison with existing methods, particularly in terms of the effectiveness of the ILR transform and the overall performance of the method.
- The novelty of the approach is questioned, as it appears to be a combination of existing techniques without significant new insights or contributions.
- The paper could benefit from more detailed explanations and justifications for certain methodological choices, such as the use of the ILR transform and the rationale behind specific experimental setups.
- The experimental results are not always convincingly superior to existing methods, and the paper could benefit from more rigorous comparisons and a broader range of datasets to validate the proposed method.
- The paper could be improved by addressing some minor issues, such as typos and unclear figures, to enhance its overall quality and professionalism.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors provide more details on the choice of the ILR transform and its impact on the performance of the method?
- How does the proposed method compare in terms of computational efficiency and resource requirements to other existing methods?
- Could the authors clarify the experimental setup and results, particularly the comparisons with other methods and the choice of datasets?
- In the context of the proposed method, how does the use of the ILR transform contribute to the effectiveness of the approach, and what are the specific benefits of this choice?
- The paper could benefit from a more detailed discussion on the limitations of the proposed method and potential directions for future research. Can the authors provide some insights into these areas?
