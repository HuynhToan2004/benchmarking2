 **Summary:**
The paper investigates the problem of partial observability in decision-making processes by introducing a novel metric called the lambda discrepancy. This metric measures the difference in value functions estimated using different temporal difference (TD) methods, specifically TD(lambda), where lambda controls the trade-off between short-term and long-term Bellman backups. The authors propose using this metric to optimize memory functions, which are memory-augmented representations of the environment, to minimize the lambda discrepancy and thereby reduce the impact of partial observability. The paper provides theoretical analysis and empirical evidence demonstrating that this approach can improve decision-making in various partially observable environments.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel measure of non-Markovianity, the lambda discrepancy, which is both observable and minimizable, and can be used to optimize memory functions to reduce partial observability.
- The proposed method shows significant performance improvements over memoryless policies in several classic POMDP problems, demonstrating its practical utility.
- The paper provides a theoretical analysis of the lambda discrepancy, showing that it is a reliable signal of partial observability and can be used to optimize memory functions to minimize it.
- The empirical results are convincing, showing that the proposed method outperforms existing methods in several benchmark problems.
- The paper is well-written, making the concepts accessible and easy to understand, and provides a thorough background on reinforcement learning and POMDPs.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a clear definition of the memory function and its role in the algorithm, which could be improved by including a clearer definition and a more detailed explanation of how it is learned.
- The experimental setup is somewhat limited, focusing primarily on simple POMDPs and not comparing the proposed method with more complex or realistic environments.
- There is a lack of discussion on the limitations of the proposed method, particularly in terms of scalability and generalizability to more complex or less structured environments.
- The paper does not provide a comprehensive discussion on the computational complexity of the proposed method, which could be a significant limitation in practical applications.
- The paper does not adequately discuss the implications of the lambda discrepancy being zero for all policies, which could lead to confusion about the practical utility of the measure.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors clarify the definition and role of the memory function in the algorithm?
- How does the proposed method compare with other existing methods for solving POMDPs, particularly in terms of computational complexity and scalability?
- Could the authors provide more details on the experimental setup, including the specific POMDPs used and the conditions under which the experiments were conducted?
- How does the proposed method perform in more complex or less structured environments, and what are the implications of these conditions on the effectiveness of the method?
- Could the authors discuss the limitations of the proposed method and potential directions for future research to address these limitations?
