 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel method called Laplace-LoRA, which integrates Bayesian inference with the LoRA fine-tuning method for large language models (LLMs). This approach allows for efficient Bayesian inference over the LoRA parameters, providing a practical method for handling uncertainty in LLM fine-tuning. The methodology involves using a Laplace approximation to compute the posterior over the LoRA parameters, which can be applied post-hoc, requiring minimal changes to the existing fine-tuning pipeline. The paper presents experimental results showing improvements in calibration and out-of-distribution generalization, although it also highlights the need for further comparisons with other Bayesian methods and a broader range of models.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel method for Bayesian fine-tuning of large language models (LLMs) using LoRA, which is a significant advancement in the field.
- The methodology is well-explained, with clear descriptions of the experiments and their results, making the paper accessible to a broad audience.
- The paper demonstrates improvements in calibration and out-of-distribution (OOD) generalization, showing that the proposed method is effective in handling uncertainty in LLM fine-tuning.
- The authors have provided a detailed discussion on the challenges associated with Bayesian inference in the context of LLMs and have proposed a method that addresses these challenges.
- The paper is well-written, with a clear presentation of the methodology, results, and discussions, which enhances its readability and comprehensibility.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a thorough comparison with other Bayesian methods, particularly those that use Bayesian fine-tuning or other Bayesian inference techniques, which could provide a more robust evaluation of the proposed method.
- The experimental evaluation is limited to a single model (Llama2-7B), which may not adequately demonstrate the generalizability of the proposed method across different models and settings.
- The paper does not discuss the limitations of the proposed method in detail, which could help in understanding its scope and applicability.
- There are concerns about the computational cost and memory efficiency of the proposed method, which could limit its practical application in real-world scenarios.
- The paper does not provide a comprehensive discussion on the impact of different hyperparameters on the performance of the method, which is crucial for practical implementation.
- The paper lacks a detailed discussion on the implications of the results, particularly the high calibration errors observed in some scenarios, which could affect the reliability of the model's predictions.

**Questions:**
- Could you provide more details on the computational cost and memory efficiency of the proposed method compared to other Bayesian fine-tuning methods?
- How does the choice of the Laplace approximation impact the results, and could other approximations provide better results in certain scenarios?
- Can you discuss the limitations of the proposed method and provide examples or scenarios where it may not be applicable or effective?
- How does the proposed method compare to other Bayesian inference methods in terms of computational efficiency and memory requirements?
- Could you provide a more detailed discussion on the impact of different hyperparameters on the performance of the method, and how these parameters were chosen for the experiments?
- How does the proposed method perform when applied to different models, particularly larger models like Llama2-13B and Llama2-65B, and what are the expected outcomes in terms of calibration and OOD generalization?
