 **Summary:**
The paper introduces ColaBO, a novel Bayesian optimization (BO) framework that integrates user-defined prior beliefs into the optimization process. This approach allows for the incorporation of prior knowledge about the optimal value or location, which can be expressed as a prior over the function rather than the function itself. The framework is flexible and can be applied to any Monte Carlo acquisition function, demonstrating improved performance in experiments where the prior is well-located. However, the paper is criticized for its lack of clarity in presentation and the need for a more rigorous theoretical analysis and empirical validation.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel approach to incorporating prior knowledge into Bayesian optimization (BO) by defining a prior over the function itself, rather than the function's parameters. This is a significant departure from traditional methods and offers a new perspective on the use of prior information in BO.
- The methodology is well-articulated, with clear explanations of the mathematical underpinnings and the experimental setup. The authors have provided code for reproducibility, which is commendable.
- The paper is well-written and easy to follow, with a clear motivation for the research and a well-structured presentation of the results.
- The empirical results show that the proposed framework outperforms existing methods in certain scenarios, demonstrating the practical utility of the approach.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed comparison with existing methods, particularly those that also use prior knowledge in BO. This makes it difficult to assess the novelty and effectiveness of the proposed method against existing approaches.
- The theoretical analysis is insufficient, with the paper relying heavily on empirical results without a strong theoretical foundation.
- The paper could benefit from a more rigorous presentation, particularly in the explanation of mathematical concepts and the derivation of equations. Some sections are unclear or poorly explained, which could hinder understanding of the methodology.
- The paper's experimental setup is limited, focusing primarily on synthetic data and small-scale problems. It would be beneficial to see how the method performs on larger, more complex datasets to better evaluate its scalability and applicability.
- The paper's claims about the flexibility and applicability of the proposed framework are not well-supported by the experimental results, which are limited to specific scenarios.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors clarify the theoretical underpinnings of the proposed method, particularly in terms of how the prior over the function is defined and how it affects the optimization process?
- How does the proposed method compare to existing methods that also use prior knowledge in BO? What are the key differences in terms of performance and applicability?
- The paper mentions that the method is applicable to any Monte Carlo acquisition function. Can the authors provide more details on how this flexibility is realized in practice, and what are the limitations of this approach?
- How does the method handle scenarios where the prior information is not well-located or inaccurate? What are the implications of misleading prior information on the optimization process?
- Can the authors provide more detailed experimental results, including comparisons with other methods on larger, more complex datasets? How does the method perform in real-world scenarios, and what are the practical implications of its use?
