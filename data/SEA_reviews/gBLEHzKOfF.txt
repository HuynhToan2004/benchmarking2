 **Summary:**
The paper introduces GENOT, a novel neural optimal transport (OT) framework designed to handle both balanced and unbalanced entropic OT problems. It leverages the conditional flow matching (CFM) technique to optimize the transport plan, allowing for the handling of different cost functions and the mapping of points across incomparable spaces. The framework is evaluated on various synthetic and real-world datasets, including single-cell biology problems, demonstrating its effectiveness in modeling cell development, predicting cellular responses to drugs, and translating between different data modalities of cells. The paper also provides theoretical insights into the well-posedness of the GENOT loss and the consistency of the re-weighting function estimation, enhancing its credibility and applicability.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel neural optimal transport framework, GENOT, which is capable of handling both balanced and unbalanced entropic OT problems, providing a flexible solution for various applications.
- The methodology is well-supported by a solid theoretical foundation, including the well-posedness of the GENOT loss and the consistency of the re-weighting function estimation.
- The paper is well-written, making it easy to follow and understand, with a clear presentation of the mathematical concepts and experimental results.
- The experimental results demonstrate the effectiveness of the GENOT framework in various applications, particularly in single-cell biology, where it shows promise in modeling cell development and predicting cellular responses to drugs.
- The paper provides a detailed discussion on the limitations of the method, which aids in understanding the scope and applicability of the proposed framework.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper could benefit from more detailed discussions on the computational complexity and time efficiency of the GENOT framework, especially in comparison to other methods like Sinkhorn or Gromov-Wasserstein.
- There is a lack of discussion on the sensitivity of the GENOT framework to the choice of cost functions and the potential impact of different cost functions on the performance and accuracy of the method.
- The paper could provide more examples and case studies to illustrate the practical applications of the GENOT framework in real-world scenarios, particularly in fields other than single-cell biology.
- The paper does not include a comprehensive comparison with other state-of-the-art methods, which could help in better understanding the advantages and limitations of the GENOT framework.
- The paper could benefit from a more detailed discussion on the limitations of the GENOT framework and how these might affect its practical applicability in various scenarios.

**Questions:**

- Could you provide more examples and case studies to illustrate the practical applications of the GENOT framework in real-world scenarios, particularly in fields other than single-cell biology?
- How does the GENOT framework compare with other state-of-the-art methods in terms of performance and accuracy, especially in scenarios where the source and target distributions have different dimensions?
