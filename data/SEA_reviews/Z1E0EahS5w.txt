 **Summary:**
The paper investigates the information processing capacity (IPC) of reservoir computers, specifically focusing on stochastic reservoirs. It demonstrates that the IPC of stochastic reservoirs is polynomial in the system size, contrasting with the exponential capacity of deterministic reservoirs. The authors use the fat-shattering dimension to show that these stochastic reservoirs are not small-sample agnostically learnable. The paper also explores the limitations of stochastic reservoirs in terms of their information processing capabilities and their learnability, suggesting that stochasticity significantly impacts their performance.
