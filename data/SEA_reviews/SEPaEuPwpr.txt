 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a method named Scattered Online Inference (SOI) aimed at reducing computational complexity in neural networks, specifically in the context of time-series data processing. SOI leverages the predictability of time-series data to extrapolate values, thereby reducing the need for full model recalculation. The method involves the use of strided convolutions and extrapolation to generate partial states of the model, which can be used to predict future values. The paper presents two variants of SOI: Partially Predictive (PP) and Fully Predictive (FP), each with different trade-offs between computational complexity and model performance. Experiments conducted on speech separation and acoustic scene classification tasks demonstrate the method's effectiveness in reducing computational complexity, although at the cost of some performance degradation.
