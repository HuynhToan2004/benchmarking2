 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel cross-platform video compression framework utilizing a codebook-based method that avoids autoregressive entropy modeling, thereby eliminating the need for platform-specific distribution estimations. This approach allows for the transmission of index sequences of codebooks, which simplifies computations across different platforms. The proposed framework leverages a conditional cross-attention module to obtain context between frames, reducing computational complexity. The method's effectiveness is demonstrated through experiments on standard datasets, showing competitive performance against traditional video codecs like H.265. However, the paper lacks comprehensive comparisons with other neural video compression methods and fails to address the practical implications of using a codebook-based method in real-world applications.

**Strengths:**
- The paper addresses the cross-platform problem in video compression, which is an important and relevant issue.
- The proposed method is simple and easy to understand, with a clear presentation of the main idea and components.
- The paper provides a thorough evaluation of the proposed method, comparing it to traditional video codecs and other neural video compression methods.
- The method is well-motivated and theoretically sound, with a clear description of the proposed components and their implementation.
- The paper is well-written, making it accessible and easy to follow.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a comparison with other neural video compression methods, which could have provided a more robust evaluation of the proposed method.
- The practical implications of using a codebook-based method in real-world applications are not adequately addressed, which could limit the applicability of the proposed method.
- The paper does not include a comparison with other state-of-the-art neural video compression methods, such as AlphaVC, which could have highlighted the strengths and limitations of the proposed method more clearly.
- The method's performance in terms of PSNR is not competitive with state-of-the-art neural video compression methods, and the reasons for this are not thoroughly explored.
- The paper lacks a discussion on the potential negative societal impacts of the proposed method.
- The experimental results are not convincing, particularly in terms of the method's performance against traditional video codecs like H.265, and the paper does not adequately address the limitations of the proposed method.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors provide a more detailed discussion on the practical implications of using a codebook-based method in real-world applications?
- How does the proposed method compare in terms of performance and complexity with other state-of-the-art neural video compression methods, such as AlphaVC?
- Can the authors elaborate on the reasons for the observed performance differences in terms of PSNR between the proposed method and other neural video compression methods?
- How does the proposed method handle the cross-platform problem in more detail, particularly in terms of the impact of different hardware platforms on the performance of the codebook-based method?
- Could the authors provide more detailed experimental results, including comparisons with other neural video compression methods and a broader range of video compression methods?
