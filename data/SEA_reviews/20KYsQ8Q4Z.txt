 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel method called Group Testing Bayesian Optimization (GTBO), which addresses the challenge of high-dimensional Bayesian optimization by employing a group testing strategy. GTBO systematically selects groups of variables and tests them to identify active variables that influence the objective function. This method leverages the axis-aligned subspace assumption to optimize the function by focusing on active dimensions. The paper provides a theoretical extension of group testing theory to continuous functions and demonstrates the method's effectiveness through extensive experiments on both synthetic and real-world high-dimensional optimization tasks. Despite its innovative approach, the paper has been critiqued for its heavy reliance on certain assumptions, such as the axis-aligned subspace and the Gaussian process prior, which may limit its applicability in more general settings.
