 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a hybrid online and offline reinforcement learning (RL) approach to enhance the branching policy in Mixed Integer Programming (MIP) problems. The online RL agent dynamically selects sample generation processes, while the offline RL agent filters high-quality samples based on cumulative returns. This method aims to improve the performance of learning-based branching methods by reducing training complexity and enhancing sample quality. Extensive experiments demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed approach, showing improvements over traditional rule-based and learning-based methods. However, concerns are raised about the novelty of the approach, the lack of a theoretical analysis, and the potential for overfitting due to the use of a single dataset for training and testing.

**Strengths:**
- The paper presents a novel hybrid approach that integrates online and offline RL techniques to enhance the training samples for branching policy learning, which is a significant advancement in the field of MIP.
- The proposed method is supported by extensive experiments, demonstrating its effectiveness in making superior branching decisions compared to state-of-the-art learning-based models and even outperforming commercial solvers like Gurobi in some cases.
- The paper is well-written, clear, and easy to follow, making it accessible to a broad audience.
- The use of RL for branching policy learning is innovative and shows promise in improving the performance of learning-based branching methods.
- The methodology is novel and well-explained, providing a clear framework for understanding the proposed approach.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a comprehensive theoretical analysis, which could strengthen the argument for the effectiveness of the proposed approach.
- The novelty of the proposed method is questionable, as it appears to be a combination of existing techniques without significant innovation.
- The methodology section could be clearer and more detailed, particularly in explaining the role and operation of the online and offline RL agents.
- There is a lack of comparison with other learning-based methods, which could provide a more robust evaluation of the proposed approach.
- The paper does not address the issue of overfitting, which is a significant concern given the limited dataset used for training and testing.
- The paper does not discuss the limitations of the proposed approach, which could provide valuable insights into its applicability and potential drawbacks.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors provide a more detailed explanation of the role and operation of the online and offline RL agents in the proposed method?
- How does the proposed method compare to other learning-based methods in terms of performance and efficiency?
- What are the specific advantages of the hybrid approach over other existing methods, and how does it address the limitations of previous approaches?
- How does the paper ensure that the training data does not lead to overfitting, and what measures are in place to prevent this?
- Could the authors provide a more detailed discussion on the limitations of the proposed approach and potential areas for future research?
