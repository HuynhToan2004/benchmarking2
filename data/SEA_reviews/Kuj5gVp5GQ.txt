 **Summary:**
The paper introduces the Sinkhorn-Newton-Sparse (SNS) algorithm, an extension of the Sinkhorn algorithm that utilizes early stopping for matrix scaling steps and a Newton-type subroutine to achieve potentially super-exponential convergence. The SNS algorithm approximates the Hessian matrix to be sparse, which allows for fast per-iteration complexity. The paper provides theoretical and empirical evidence supporting the sparsity of the Hessian and the acceleration of the Sinkhorn algorithm. However, concerns were raised regarding the clarity of the paper, particularly in the presentation of the algorithm and its theoretical underpinnings. The paper's contributions are viewed as significant, but the presentation and the novelty of the approach were questioned.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel approach to accelerating the Sinkhorn algorithm by sparsifying the Hessian matrix, which is a significant contribution to the field.
- The theoretical analysis provided is robust, particularly the sparsity of the Hessian matrix, which is a key aspect of the proposed method.
- The paper is well-written, making it accessible and easy to follow, which is crucial for a broad audience.
- The authors have demonstrated the effectiveness of their method through both theoretical analysis and practical experiments, showing the potential of their approach.
- The paper provides a comprehensive literature review, which is beneficial for readers seeking a deeper understanding of the topic.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks clarity in its presentation, particularly in the algorithmic descriptions and the theoretical underpinnings.
- The novelty of the approach is questioned, as similar methods have been previously explored, such as sparsification and Newton's method.
- There is a lack of rigorous proof for the claims made about the sparsity of the Hessian matrix, which is a critical aspect of the proposed method.
- The paper does not sufficiently compare the proposed method with existing approaches, which could help in understanding its advantages and limitations.
- The experimental setup and the choice of parameters (such as the number of iterations and the regularization parameter) are not well justified, which could affect the credibility of the results.
- The paper could benefit from a more detailed discussion on the limitations and potential negative societal impacts of the proposed method.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors clarify the definition and role of the variable z in Algorithm 1?
- How does the sparsification of the Hessian matrix affect the convergence rate and the overall performance of the algorithm?
- Can the authors provide a more detailed explanation of the theoretical results, especially the sparsity of the Hessian matrix and its implications on the algorithm's performance?
- Why was the choice of parameters (such as the number of iterations and the regularization parameter) not justified in the paper?
- How does the proposed method compare with existing approaches in terms of computational efficiency and practical applicability?
- Could the authors discuss the potential negative societal impacts of their method and how they plan to address these concerns?
