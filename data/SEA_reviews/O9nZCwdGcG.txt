 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel Biased Temporal Convolution Graph Network (BiaTCGNet) for time series forecasting with missing values. The proposed model integrates a Multi-Scale Instance PartialTCN and a Biased GCN to capture temporal dependencies and spatial structures, respectively. These modules are designed to handle missing patterns effectively, enhancing the model's ability to predict missing data points. The model is evaluated on five benchmark datasets, showing significant improvements over existing methods. Despite its innovative approach, the paper is criticized for its limited novelty, lack of comparisons with state-of-the-art methods, and insufficient discussion on related works.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, making it easy to follow and understand the proposed methodology.
- The motivation behind the research is clear, focusing on the significant issue of handling missing values in time series forecasting.
- The proposed method is technically sound, combining TCN and GCN to model temporal dependencies and spatial structures, respectively.
- The experimental results demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed method, showing improvements over existing methods.
- The paper is well-organized, with a clear structure that facilitates understanding of the proposed method and its applications.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a thorough review of related works, particularly recent advancements in time series forecasting with missing values.
- There is a significant gap in the discussion on the novelty of the proposed method, as similar approaches have been explored in previous works.
- The paper does not adequately address the limitations of the proposed method, such as its applicability to different data types and the potential for overfitting due to the use of multiple convolutional kernels.
- The paper does not provide sufficient comparisons with state-of-the-art methods, which is crucial for validating the effectiveness of the proposed method.
- The paper's experimental setup and results are not convincingly superior to existing methods, particularly in terms of the datasets used and the comparisons made.
- The paper's methodology section could benefit from more detailed explanations and examples to enhance understanding.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors clarify how the proposed method addresses the limitations of existing methods, particularly in terms of handling missing values effectively?
- How does the proposed method compare to state-of-the-art methods in terms of performance and efficiency?
- Can the authors provide more detailed explanations and examples of the proposed method's operation, particularly in the methodology section?
- How does the proposed method handle different types of missing data, such as irregularly missing data or data with varying frequencies of missing values?
- Could the authors discuss the potential implications of using different convolutional kernels in the Multi-Scale Instance PartialTCN module?
- In light of the concerns about novelty and related works, can the authors expand the discussion on these topics to better contextualize their contributions within the existing literature?
