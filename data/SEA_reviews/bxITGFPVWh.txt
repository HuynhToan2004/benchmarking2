 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel approach to data poisoning attacks, leveraging the concept of sharpness to optimize the poisoning effect across various attack scenarios. This method, termed Sharpness-Aware Data Poisoning Attack (SAPA), aims to enhance the attack's effectiveness by considering the worst-case scenario in the model landscape. The approach is applied to both targeted and backdoor attacks, showing improvements over existing methods in terms of success rate and computational efficiency. However, the paper is criticized for its limited evaluation scope, focusing primarily on CIFAR10 and CIFAR100 datasets and ResNet models, and for not sufficiently comparing with state-of-the-art methods.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel method for data poisoning attacks using sharpness, which is a new approach in the field.
- The proposed method is versatile and can be applied to different types of poisoning attacks, such as targeted, backdoor, and un-targeted attacks.
- The paper is well-organized, with clear and concise writing, and provides a comprehensive review of related work.
- The methodology is efficient, requiring only one extra training step, which is a significant advantage over other methods that might require more computational resources.
- The paper demonstrates the effectiveness of the proposed method through extensive experiments, showing improvements over existing methods in terms of success rate and computational efficiency.
- The approach is adaptable to various model architectures and training algorithms, which is a notable strength.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed comparison with state-of-the-art methods in terms of success rate and computational efficiency.
- The evaluation is limited to CIFAR10 and CIFAR100 datasets, and ResNet models, which may not generalize well to other datasets or model architectures.
- The paper does not provide sufficient theoretical analysis to support the claims made, particularly regarding the relationship between sharpness and the poisoning effect.
- There is a lack of discussion on the limitations of the proposed method, which could help in understanding its applicability and effectiveness in different scenarios.
- The paper does not sufficiently address the limitations of existing methods, which could undermine the credibility of the proposed approach.
- The paper does not include a detailed discussion on the defense against the proposed method, which is crucial for understanding the robustness of the approach.
