 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel method for text-guided 3D portrait editing, leveraging a diffusion-based model conditioned on both textual and image inputs. This approach allows for precise manipulation of facial attributes and styles, ensuring identity preservation. The method involves a token position randomization strategy and an identity consistency module to handle multiple editing requirements and maintain facial identity. The proposed method is evaluated against several baselines, demonstrating superior performance in terms of identity preservation, image quality, and consistency. The paper also discusses limitations such as the need for a large-scale dataset and the potential for limited editing capabilities due to the dataset's size.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, clear, and easy to follow, making it accessible to a broad audience.
- The proposed method is novel, integrating the strengths of the diffusion model and NeRF-based GANs to achieve effective cross-modal editing.
- The paper introduces a novel token position randomization (TPR) training strategy, which allows the model to handle multiple editing requirements simultaneously, enhancing its versatility.
- The identity consistency module is well-designed, ensuring that the edited face retains its original identity, which is crucial for maintaining facial recognition capabilities.
- The paper presents a robust experimental setup, including comprehensive comparisons with state-of-the-art methods and a thorough evaluation of the proposed method.
- The use of a large-scale dataset and the combination of existing models like PREIM3D and CLIP with the proposed method demonstrate a strong baseline for future works in the field of instructed 3D-aware face editing.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed comparison with other recent methods like Instruct-NeRF2NeRF, AvatarStudio, and HeadSculpt, which also address text-guided 3D face editing.
- The method's reliance on a large-scale dataset for training raises concerns about the generalizability of the model to out-of-domain data and its ability to handle diverse editing instructions.
- The paper does not provide sufficient details on the training and inference time of the proposed method, which could be crucial for practical applications.
- There are some unclear descriptions and potential inconsistencies in the methodology, such as the role and definition of certain components like the identity modulation and the token position randomization.
- The paper's evaluation primarily focuses on specific attributes and does not provide a comprehensive evaluation across a broader range of attributes, which could limit the understanding of the method's effectiveness.

**Questions:**
- How does the proposed method handle semantically identical instructions that may produce slight errors?
- Can the method handle a wide range of editing instructions, including those that require complex or subtle changes?
- How does the method ensure that the edited face retains its identity while also allowing for significant changes in attributes like age or hairstyle?
- Could you provide more details on the training and inference time of the proposed method, and how these compare to other methods like InstructPix2Pix?
- How does the method handle the token position randomization during inference, and what are the implications of this approach on the model's performance and interpretability?
- Are there any plans to extend the evaluation to include a broader range of attributes or to conduct user studies to assess the method's practical utility and user satisfaction?
