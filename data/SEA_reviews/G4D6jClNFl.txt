 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel deepfake detection method named CTru, which employs a self-supervised anomaly detection approach using both hyperbolic and hypersphere spaces. CTru leverages contrastive learning in these spaces to learn rich representations of facial geometry, enhancing the detection of deepfake features. The methodology integrates angle-based similarity in spherical space and model confidence in hyperbolic space with Busemann distance, achieving state-of-the-art results on various datasets. CTru's approach is unique in its use of self-supervised anomaly detection, which allows it to generalize well across different datasets and manipulation scenarios, even when trained solely on pristine faces.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, making it easy to follow and understand.
- The methodology introduces a novel approach to deepfake detection by using hyperbolic and hypersphere spaces, which is both innovative and effective.
- The proposed method achieves state-of-the-art results on various challenging datasets, demonstrating its effectiveness and robustness.
- The paper provides a comprehensive analysis of the proposed method, including detailed explanations and justifications for the design choices, such as the use of spherical and hyperbolic spaces.
- The experimental results are convincing, showing the method's ability to generalize well across different datasets and manipulation scenarios.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed discussion on the limitations and potential negative societal impacts of the proposed method, which could be crucial for understanding its broader implications.
- The comparison with other deepfake detection methods is not comprehensive, and some state-of-the-art methods are not included in the comparisons, which could affect the perceived novelty and effectiveness of the proposed method.
- The paper does not provide sufficient ablation studies to demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed method over existing methods, which could undermine the claims of its superiority.
- The paper does not adequately address the issue of overfitting, which is a significant concern in deepfake detection, especially when using synthetic data.
- There are some minor issues with the clarity and organization of the paper, such as unclear references and inconsistencies in the use of notation.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors provide more details on the limitations and potential negative societal impacts of the proposed method?
- How does the proposed method compare with other state-of-the-art methods in terms of computational efficiency and resource requirements?
- Could the authors provide more experimental results or comparisons to demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed method against other deepfake detection methods, especially those that use synthetic data?
- How does the proposed method handle the issue of overfitting, especially when using synthetic data for training?
- Can the authors clarify the role and impact of the hyperbolic space in the proposed method, particularly in relation to the spherical space?
- How does the proposed method perform in scenarios where the data distribution is significantly different from the training data, such as in cross-dataset scenarios?
