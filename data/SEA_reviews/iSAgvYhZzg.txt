 **Summary:**
The paper introduces Auto-UI, a multimodal autonomous agent designed to interact with user interfaces (UIs) by directly processing screenshots and user instructions, without requiring environment parsing or application-specific APIs. This agent leverages a chain-of-action technique that integrates previous action histories and future action plans to predict actions, aiming to improve efficiency and reduce error propagation. The model is evaluated on the AITW benchmark, showing state-of-the-art performance with an action type prediction accuracy of 90% and an action success rate of 74%. Despite its innovative approach, concerns are raised about the generalizability of the model to new environments and the potential limitations of its application in more complex or varied UI settings.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written and easy to follow, with clear explanations of the proposed methods.
- The introduction of a chain-of-action technique that integrates previous action histories and future action plans is innovative and potentially beneficial for enhancing the efficiency and accuracy of UI automation.
- The experimental results demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed methods, showing a state-of-the-art performance on the AITW benchmark.
- The paper provides a comprehensive evaluation of the proposed methods, including ablation studies and comparisons with existing models.
- The use of a multimodal approach that directly interacts with the UI without relying on environment parsing or application-specific APIs is a significant advancement in the field.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed discussion on the limitations of the proposed methods, particularly in terms of their generalizability to new environments and their applicability in more complex or varied UI settings.
- There is a lack of comparison with more recent state-of-the-art models, such as those using advanced vision encoders like DINO-v2 or BLIP-2, and with more efficient prompting techniques like chain-of-thought.
- The paper does not adequately address the issue of error propagation, which is a significant concern in UI automation.
- The use of a frozen vision encoder might limit the model's ability to adapt to new or varied environments.
- The paper does not provide a detailed analysis of the computational cost or the impact of the model's size on its performance.
- The paper's reliance on a single dataset (AITW) for evaluation limits the generalizability of the findings.

**Questions:**
- Could you provide more details on how the chain-of-action technique integrates previous action histories and future action plans to enhance the efficiency and accuracy of UI automation?
- How does the model handle the generalizability to new environments and the complexity of UI settings?
- Can you elaborate on the computational cost and the impact of the model's size on its performance?
- What are the specific advantages of using a multimodal approach over other existing techniques in UI automation?
- How does the model handle the issue of error propagation, and what measures are in place to mitigate this risk?
- Could you provide more details on the model's performance when using different vision encoders or when fine-tuning the vision encoder?
- What are the potential applications of the proposed method, and how does it compare to other existing methods in terms of practical utility and efficiency?
