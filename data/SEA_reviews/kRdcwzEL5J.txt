 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a new large-scale 3D semantic segmentation dataset, CUS3D, which includes point clouds, meshes, and 2D images annotated with semantic labels. The dataset covers an area of approximately 2.85 square kilometers and is designed to support various research pursuits in semantic segmentation methodologies. It is intended to be a comprehensive benchmark for testing 3D semantic segmentation algorithms, with a focus on improving the understanding of urban scenes by machines. The dataset is rich in semantic information, including labels for both urban and rural scenes, and is accompanied by extensive experiments using state-of-the-art deep learning models. However, the paper has been criticized for its limited novelty and lack of comparison with recent methods, which could undermine its contribution to the field.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a large-scale 3D semantic segmentation dataset, which is significant for advancing research in this area.
- The dataset covers a vast area of approximately 2.85 square kilometers, providing a comprehensive view of urban scenes.
- The dataset is annotated with 10 semantic labels, which are useful for various research applications in urban planning, transportation planning, and architectural decision-making.
- The paper provides extensive experiments using state-of-the-art deep learning models, which demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed dataset in advancing research in 3D semantic segmentation.
- The dataset includes 2D aerial images, which are annotated with semantic labels, enhancing its utility for research in areas such as 3D rendering, 3D reconstruction, and remote sensing image semantic segmentation.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a clear description of the 3D data acquisition process, particularly the steps involved in obtaining the point cloud and mesh data.
- The dataset's annotation process is not adequately explained, with no details provided on the annotation tools or the manual cross-checking process.
- The paper does not compare the proposed dataset with recent methods, which could have strengthened its contribution to the field.
- The novelty of the dataset is limited, as it primarily focuses on providing a large-scale dataset without significant advancements in annotation or data processing techniques.
- The paper does not discuss the limitations of the dataset, which could be crucial for understanding its applicability and potential biases.
- The dataset's semantic labels are limited to only 10 categories, which may not be sufficient for more detailed urban scene analysis.
- The paper lacks a detailed discussion on the potential biases in the dataset, which could affect its utility in real-world applications.

**Questions:**
- Could you provide more details on the 3D data acquisition process, specifically how the point cloud and mesh data are obtained?
- How were the semantic labels assigned to the 3D data, and what tools were used for this process?
- What is the rationale behind the choice of the 10 semantic categories, and how were they determined?
- How does the dataset handle variations in urban scenes, such as different types of buildings or vegetation?
- What are the potential biases in the dataset, and how might these affect the results of semantic segmentation algorithms?
- Could you provide more details on the manual cross-checking process and the accuracy of the semantic labels?
- How does the dataset compare to other existing datasets in terms of semantic richness and coverage of urban scenes?
- What are the plans for future updates or expansions of the dataset to address potential limitations or biases?
