 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel method for protein-protein interaction (PPI) prediction using a microenvironment-aware protein embedding (MAPE-PPI). This method leverages a masked codebook modeling strategy to capture dependencies between microenvironments and proposes a pre-training strategy called Masked Codebook Modeling (MCM) to enhance the model's effectiveness and efficiency. The approach encodes microenvironments into chemically meaningful discrete codes using a sufficiently large microenvironment "vocabulary" (i.e., codebook) and uses this to encode proteins of different sizes and functions for large-scale PPI prediction. The paper claims that MAPE-PPI can outperform existing methods in terms of both effectiveness and computational efficiency.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, easy to follow, and the method is clearly described, with a clear motivation for the proposed method.
- The proposed method is novel and innovative, particularly the use of masked codebook modeling to capture dependencies between microenvironments.
- The paper includes extensive experiments that demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed method, showing that it outperforms existing methods in terms of both effectiveness and computational efficiency.
- The method's approach to protein-protein interaction (PPI) prediction using a microenvironment-aware protein embedding (MAPE-PPI) is a significant contribution to the field, as it addresses the challenge of training inefficiency in existing structure-based methods.
- The paper includes an ablation study that shows the importance of each component of the proposed method, providing a robust validation of the proposed approach.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed analysis of the limitations of the proposed method, particularly in terms of scalability and applicability to different types of proteins or protein structures.
- There is a lack of comparison with the most recent methods in the field, which could have provided a more robust evaluation of the proposed method's effectiveness.
- The paper does not discuss the limitations of the pre-trained model, which is a critical component of the proposed method.
- The experimental results are not sufficiently detailed, particularly in terms of the statistical significance of the results and the reproducibility of the experiments.
- The paper does not provide a detailed analysis of the computational complexity of the proposed method, which is crucial for understanding its practical applicability.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors provide more details on the limitations of the proposed method, particularly in terms of scalability and applicability to different types of proteins or protein structures?
- How does the proposed method compare with the most recent methods in the field, and what are the specific advantages of the proposed method over these methods?
- Could the authors provide more details on the pre-trained model used in the proposed method, including its limitations and how these were addressed in the experiments?
- How were the hyperparameters chosen for the experiments, and what was the impact of these choices on the results?
- Can the authors provide more detailed results on the computational complexity of the proposed method, including a comparison with existing methods in terms of training and inference times?
- How does the proposed method perform in terms of predicting the interfaces or conformations among multiple proteins, and what are the implications of these predictions for practical applications?
