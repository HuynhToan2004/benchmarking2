 **Summary:**
The paper introduces ChatDrug, a novel framework utilizing large language models (LLMs) for drug editing tasks, including small molecules, peptides, and proteins. ChatDrug employs a prompt design module, a retrieval and domain feedback module, and a conversation module to facilitate drug editing. The framework is evaluated on 39 tasks, showing superior performance over existing baselines. The authors also provide a detailed analysis of the framework's components and their contributions to the overall performance. Despite its innovative approach and strong empirical results, the paper has been criticized for its limited novelty and lack of comprehensive comparisons with other state-of-the-art methods.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel approach to drug editing using LLMs, which is a significant step towards automating and enhancing the drug discovery process.
- The framework, ChatDrug, is well-designed and easy to follow, with a clear explanation of its components and their roles in drug editing.
- The empirical results are strong, demonstrating the effectiveness of the proposed method across various drug types and tasks.
- The paper provides detailed ablation studies that help in understanding the contributions of different components of the framework.
- The use of domain feedback and retrieval modules to enhance the performance of LLMs is an interesting and potentially impactful approach.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a comprehensive comparison with other state-of-the-art methods, which could have provided a better understanding of ChatDrug's relative performance and contributions.
- There is a notable absence of discussion on the limitations of the proposed framework, which could have provided a more balanced view of its capabilities and challenges.
- The paper does not adequately address the novelty of the approach, as similar methods using LLMs for drug discovery have been previously explored.
- The dataset used for evaluation is relatively small, which could limit the generalizability of the results.
- The paper does not provide sufficient details on the training process for the retrieval and domain feedback module, which is crucial for understanding its effectiveness and generalizability.
- There is a lack of clarity on the role and impact of the retrieval module, particularly in scenarios where it might not provide relevant or effective solutions.

**Questions:**
- Could you provide more details on the training process for the retrieval and domain feedback module, including the data sources and methodology used?
- How does the retrieval module handle scenarios where it fails to provide relevant or effective solutions?
- What are the specific challenges in applying ChatDrug to other drug discovery tasks beyond the ones evaluated in the study?
- How does ChatDrug compare to other state-of-the-art methods in terms of performance and efficiency?
- Can you provide more examples or case studies that demonstrate the practical application and impact of ChatDrug in real-world drug discovery scenarios?
- How does ChatDrug handle the complexity and variability of drug discovery tasks, particularly in scenarios where the desired outcomes are not clearly defined or the available data is limited?
