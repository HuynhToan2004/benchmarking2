 **Summary:**
The paper introduces SEIGNN, a scalable and efficient implicit Graph Neural Network (GNN) designed for large graphs. SEIGNN employs a mini-batch training method with coarse nodes to capture global information effectively, and a stochastic solver to solve the fixed-point equation with fewer iterations, thereby reducing training time. The model is evaluated on six datasets, demonstrating improvements in accuracy and efficiency compared to baseline methods. The proposed methodology includes a novel stochastic solver that approximates the equilibrium, which is crucial for reducing computational complexity. Despite its strengths, the paper has been criticized for its limited novelty and the need for a more comprehensive comparison with other scalable GNNs.

**Strengths:**
- The paper addresses a significant and relevant problem in the field of Graph Neural Networks (GNNs) by focusing on the scalability and efficiency of implicit GNNs.
- The proposed method, SEIGNN, is well-motivated, technically sound, and supported by thorough experimental analysis, demonstrating improvements in accuracy and efficiency compared to baseline methods.
- The paper is well-written, with clear presentation of the methodology, experiments, and results, making it easy to follow and understand.
- The introduction of a stochastic solver to approximate the equilibrium is a novel approach that enhances the training efficiency of implicit GNNs.
- The experiments are comprehensive, covering various datasets and providing a detailed ablation study, which supports the claims of the paper.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a comprehensive comparison with other scalable GNNs, which could provide a better understanding of the relative performance of the proposed method.
- The novelty of the proposed method is questioned as it appears to be an extension of existing methods, such as those by Zeng et al. (2021) and Hamilton et al. (2017), with similar graph partitioning and mini-batch training techniques.
- The paper could benefit from a more detailed discussion on the limitations of the proposed method, such as potential biases introduced by the stochastic solver and the scalability of the model on extremely large graphs.
- There is a lack of discussion on the potential negative societal impacts of the proposed method, which could be a significant concern for some applications.
- The paper does not sufficiently address the limitations of the proposed method, particularly in terms of its scalability and the potential biases introduced by the stochastic solver.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors clarify the specific advantages of SEIGNN over other scalable GNNs, particularly in terms of accuracy and efficiency?
- How does the stochastic solver impact the accuracy and training time compared to other methods, and what are the potential biases introduced by this approach?
- Can the authors provide more details on the scalability of the model, especially on extremely large graphs, and discuss any potential limitations in this regard?
- How does the proposed method perform on different types of graphs, such as directed or weighted graphs, and what are the implications of these differences on the model's performance?
- Could the authors discuss the potential negative societal impacts of the proposed method and how these might be mitigated or addressed?
