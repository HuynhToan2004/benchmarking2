 **Summary:**
The paper discusses the integration of moral values into artificial intelligence (AI) systems, focusing on the utilitarian ethics prevalent in tech-centric cultures. It explores the implications of AI systems making moral choices, arguing that these choices are not inherently moral but reflect the values of their creators. The paper critiques the automation of morality and its potential impact on individual freedom and responsibility. It also examines the limitations of AI in making moral decisions and the ethical implications of AI's influence on society. Despite the paper's ambitious scope, it suffers from a lack of originality, clarity in presentation, and a focus on general philosophical discussions without sufficient empirical evidence or practical recommendations.

- The paper addresses a timely and significant topic, the integration of morality into AI systems, which is crucial for understanding the ethical implications of AI.
- It critically examines the role of utilitarian ethics in AI development and its impact on moral decision-making, providing a valuable perspective on the ethical considerations in AI.
- The paper is well-structured and clear, making complex philosophical concepts accessible to a broad audience, including those not deeply familiar with AI or ethics.
