 **Summary:**
The paper explores a novel approach to domain adaptation by combining pretraining with targeted augmentations. It argues that standard fine-tuning after pretraining does not consistently improve out-of-distribution (OOD) error over supervised learning, and proposes a method that utilizes targeted augmentations to better connect source and target domains. The method is tested across various datasets, including astronomical time-series classification, redshift prediction, wildlife species identification, and tumor detection, demonstrating improvements over standard fine-tuning and supervised learning. The paper also introduces new datasets and provides a methodology for constructing targeted augmentations, which are designed to connect the source and target domains more effectively.

**Strengths:**
- The paper addresses a significant and timely issue in the field of domain adaptation, focusing on the effectiveness of pretraining and fine-tuning for domain adaptation.
- The methodology is well-articulated, with a clear explanation of the proposed method and its components, including the use of targeted augmentations and their impact on domain adaptation.
- The paper introduces new datasets and provides a methodology for constructing targeted augmentations, which are designed to connect the source and target domains more effectively.
- The results are robust, showing significant improvements over standard fine-tuning and supervised learning across various datasets, with detailed ablation studies providing insights into the robustness of the proposed method.
- The paper is well-written, making it easy to follow and understand, and the experiments are well-designed, with a clear motivation and thorough evaluation of the proposed method.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a comprehensive comparison with existing methods for domain adaptation, such as those using domain adversarial training or other domain adaptation techniques.
- The evaluation is limited to a few datasets, and it would be beneficial to see the method applied to a broader range of datasets to assess its generalizability.
- The paper could benefit from a more detailed discussion on the choice of augmentations and their impact on domain adaptation, as well as a comparison with other domain adaptation methods.
- There is a lack of clarity in the explanation of some figures and tables, which could hinder the reader's understanding of the results.
- The paper does not adequately address the computational cost of the proposed method, which could be a significant concern for practical applications.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors clarify the computational cost of the proposed method, especially in comparison to other methods?
- How does the proposed method perform when applied to other datasets, especially those that are not astronomical in nature?
- Can the authors provide more details on the choice of augmentations and their impact on domain adaptation?
- How does the proposed method compare to other domain adaptation methods, especially in terms of computational efficiency and practical applicability?
- Can the authors provide more details on the experimental setup, such as the specifics of the datasets used and the experimental conditions?
