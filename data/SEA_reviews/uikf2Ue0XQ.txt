 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel method for visual grounding, focusing on the attention mechanisms in transformer-based models to optimize behavior within language-relevant regions. The proposed Attention-Driven Constraint Balancing (AttBalance) framework aims to address the limitations of current loss functions by optimizing the behavior of visual features within language-relevant regions. This method is evaluated on four widely used datasets, demonstrating consistent improvements over five different models. The authors also discuss the challenges in balancing the attention mechanisms and introduce a Difficulty Adaptive Training strategy to address data imbalance issues. Despite the method's effectiveness, concerns were raised about the novelty and the depth of the analysis, particularly in how the attention mechanisms are optimized and the impact of the proposed method on transformer-based models.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, making it easy to follow, and the problem addressed is significant and interesting.
- The proposed method is intuitive, and the analysis of attention maps is insightful, providing a clear understanding of the attention behavior in visual grounding tasks.
- The method is effective in improving the performance of existing models, showing a consistent improvement across different models and datasets.
- The paper provides a comprehensive ablation study, which helps in understanding the impact of different components of the proposed method.
- The proposed Difficulty Adaptive Training strategy is novel and helps in addressing the data imbalance problem in visual grounding tasks.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a clear motivation for the proposed method, particularly how it optimizes the behavior of visual features within language-relevant regions.
- The novelty of the proposed method is questionable, as similar approaches have been previously used in other contexts, such as in the MomModal paper.
- The analysis of attention mechanisms is not convincing, especially in terms of its relevance to the performance of visual grounding tasks.
- The paper does not adequately discuss the limitations of the proposed method, which could help in understanding its applicability and potential drawbacks.
- The paper does not compare the proposed method with state-of-the-art methods, which could provide a better understanding of its effectiveness and novelty.
- The paper could benefit from a more thorough analysis of the impact of the proposed method on different models and datasets, especially in terms of computational efficiency and scalability.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors clarify the motivation behind the proposed method and how it specifically optimizes the behavior of visual features within language-relevant regions?
- How does the proposed method differ from similar approaches used in other contexts, such as in the MomModal paper?
- Could the authors provide a more detailed analysis of the impact of the proposed method on different models and datasets, including a comparison with state-of-the-art methods?
- How does the proposed method handle the computational complexity introduced by the additional attention regularization and difficulty adaptive training components?
- Could the authors discuss the limitations of their proposed method and provide suggestions for future research directions?
