 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a new concept called "over-dilution" in graph neural networks (GNNs), focusing on the diminishment of node information at both the attribute and node levels. It distinguishes between intra-node dilution, where attributes lose their influence, and inter-node dilution, where node representations are aggregated, leading to a diminished influence of the node itself on the final representation. The authors propose a transformer-based solution to address these issues, integrating attribute representations based on attention scores between node-level and attribute-level representations. The paper is well-written, providing a clear definition of the problem and a comprehensive theoretical framework. However, the empirical results are somewhat limited, with the proposed method not significantly outperforming existing methods across various datasets.

**Strengths:**
- The paper provides a comprehensive theoretical framework and empirical analysis of the over-dilution problem, which is a significant limitation in existing graph neural networks.
- The authors have conducted a thorough empirical analysis, including the effects of over-dilution in different scenarios and the performance of the proposed method under various conditions.
- The paper is well-written, making it easy to follow and understand, with clear figures and detailed explanations of the proposed method.
- The introduction of the over-dilution problem and the proposed solution, Node Attribute Transformer (NATR), is novel and offers a new perspective on addressing the limitations of Message Passing Neural Networks (MPNNs).
- The paper provides a detailed analysis of the over-dilution problem, which is a significant issue in GNNs, and proposes a solution that is theoretically grounded and empirically validated.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a comprehensive comparison with state-of-the-art methods, which could have strengthened the empirical validation of the proposed solution.
- The empirical results are somewhat limited, with the proposed method not significantly outperforming existing methods across various datasets.
- The paper could benefit from a more detailed discussion on the computational complexity of the proposed method compared to existing methods.
