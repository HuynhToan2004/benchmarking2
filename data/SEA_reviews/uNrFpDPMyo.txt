 **Summary:**
The paper introduces FastGen, a method designed to reduce memory footprint in large language models (LLMs) during inference by adaptively compressing the KV cache. This is achieved through a profiling process that identifies attention structures, allowing for targeted compression policies tailored to specific attention heads. FastGen is claimed to reduce memory consumption significantly while maintaining generation quality. The method is evaluated across various tasks, showing substantial reductions in GPU memory usage with minimal impact on performance. However, the paper has been critiqued for its lack of novelty and insufficient comparisons with existing methods.

**Strengths:**
- The paper addresses a critical issue in the field by focusing on reducing the memory footprint of generative inference for large language models (LLMs), which is a significant challenge for practical deployment.
- The methodology is well-explained, making it easy to understand and implement.
- The paper provides a thorough evaluation, including a diverse set of tasks and models, which demonstrates the effectiveness of the proposed method.
- The approach is simple yet effective, with potential for broad applicability across various models and tasks.
- The paper is well-written, clear, and easy to follow, which enhances its accessibility and utility for the community.
- The empirical results are convincing, showing that the method can achieve significant memory reduction without significant loss in performance.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks novelty as the concept of using attention scores to prune the KV cache is not new, with similar methods having been proposed in previous works.
- The comparison with existing methods is insufficient, which makes it difficult to assess the true effectiveness of the proposed method.
- The paper does not provide a detailed comparison with state-of-the-art methods in terms of memory footprint reduction and performance, which is crucial for understanding the practical advantages of the proposed method.
- The paper does not adequately address the scalability of the method with different model sizes or the impact of varying batch sizes and sequence lengths.
- The paper lacks a detailed discussion on the limitations of the method, which could help in understanding its applicability and potential drawbacks.
- The paper does not include a detailed analysis of the computational overhead introduced by the profiling process, which could affect the overall efficiency of the method.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors provide a detailed comparison of FastGen with other state-of-the-art methods in terms of memory footprint reduction and performance?
- How does the method perform when applied to different model sizes, and what are the implications for memory reduction and performance?
- Could the authors clarify the computational overhead introduced by the profiling process and how it affects the overall efficiency of the method?
- Is there a plan to release the code and the compatible CUDA kernel to facilitate reproducibility and further research?
- Could the authors provide more detailed insights into the limitations of the method and when it might not be applicable or effective?
