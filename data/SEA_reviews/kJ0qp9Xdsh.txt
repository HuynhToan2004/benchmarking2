 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel continuous diffusion model, LACE, for layout generation, aiming to address the limitations of existing discrete diffusion models in terms of layout aesthetics. By leveraging a continuous state-space design, LACE incorporates differentiable aesthetic constraint functions during training, enhancing the model's ability to handle various layout generation tasks. Extensive experiments demonstrate that LACE outperforms existing state-of-the-art methods across multiple datasets and metrics. The model's innovative approach includes a time-dependent weight to prevent constraints from converging to local minima and post-processing techniques to refine layouts. Despite its strengths, the paper could benefit from a more comprehensive comparison with other continuous diffusion models and a broader range of visual examples to better demonstrate its capabilities.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, with clear and concise presentation of the main ideas, making it easy to follow.
- The proposed method, LACE, is novel and effectively addresses the issue of layout aesthetics by incorporating aesthetic constraint functions during training.
- The paper provides extensive experiments and achieves state-of-the-art results on public benchmarks, demonstrating the effectiveness of the proposed method.
- The methodology is simple yet effective, with a novel post-processing step that refines the layout without altering the FID score significantly.
- The paper includes a time-dependent weight to prevent constraints from converging to local minima, which is a useful addition to the model.
- The inclusion of an ablation study provides insight into the effectiveness of the proposed method.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a comprehensive comparison with other continuous diffusion models, which could provide a better understanding of LACE's position in the field.
- The visual examples provided in the paper are limited, which could make it difficult for readers to assess the model's capabilities.
- The paper could benefit from more diverse datasets and tasks to better demonstrate the generalizability of the proposed method.
- The paper's evaluation metrics are limited to FID and alignment, which might not fully capture the aesthetic quality of the generated layouts.
- There is a lack of discussion on the limitations of the proposed method, which could help in understanding the scope of its applicability.
- The paper could benefit from a more detailed discussion on the post-processing step and its impact on the FID score.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors provide more visual examples to better demonstrate the capabilities of the proposed method?
- How does the post-processing step affect the FID score, and could the authors elaborate on this in the paper?
- How does the model handle different types of layouts, particularly those with complex or varied element arrangements?
- What are the specific advantages of using a continuous diffusion model over a discrete diffusion model in terms of layout aesthetics?
- Could the authors discuss the limitations of their proposed method and how these might impact its practical application?
- Is there a possibility to include more diverse datasets and tasks in future experiments to further validate the effectiveness of the proposed method?
