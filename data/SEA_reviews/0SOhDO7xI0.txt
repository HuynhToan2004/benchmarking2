 **Summary:**
The paper introduces DeepDRK, a novel deep learning-based method for generating knockoffs in feature selection tasks. It leverages a Vision Transformer (ViT) to ensure the swap property and introduces a multi-swapper adversarial training procedure to enhance the model's performance. The methodology includes a dependency regularization loss to reduce reconstructability and a post-training perturbation to further improve power. Extensive experiments are conducted across synthetic, semi-synthetic, and real-world datasets to demonstrate the effectiveness of DeepDRK in balancing false discovery rate (FDR) and power. Despite its innovative approach, the paper faces criticism for its lack of clarity in presentation and the need for more rigorous experimental validation, particularly in terms of computational efficiency and the robustness of the results against different model configurations.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, making it easy to follow and understand the proposed methodologies and their applications.
- The authors provide a comprehensive overview of the limitations of existing methods, which motivates the development of the proposed method.
- The proposed method is innovative, particularly in its use of a Vision Transformer (ViT) to ensure the swap property and the introduction of a multi-swapper adversarial training procedure.
- The method is evaluated on a wide range of datasets, including real-world datasets, which supports its practical applicability.
- The paper includes extensive empirical evaluations and an ablation study that demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed method in balancing FDR and power.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks clarity in certain sections, particularly in the explanation of the methodology and the justification for specific design choices.
- The computational efficiency of the proposed method is not discussed, which is crucial for practical applications.
- The experimental validation is limited, with only a few datasets used to evaluate the method's effectiveness.
- The paper does not adequately address the scalability of the method to large datasets or high-dimensional feature spaces.
- There is a lack of discussion on the sensitivity of the method to different model configurations and hyperparameters, which could affect its robustness and generalizability.
- The paper could benefit from a more detailed comparison with existing methods, especially in terms of the swap property and the performance metrics used.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors provide a more detailed explanation of the methodology, particularly the role and effectiveness of the multi-swapper adversarial training procedure?
- How does the proposed method perform in terms of computational efficiency, and what are the practical implications of using this method in real-world applications?
- Can the authors discuss the scalability of the method to large datasets or high-dimensional feature spaces, and provide insights into how the method could be adapted to handle such scenarios?
- How sensitive is the method to different model configurations and hyperparameters, and what are the implications of these sensitivities for the robustness and generalizability of the method?
- Could the authors provide a more detailed comparison with existing methods, especially in terms of the swap property and the performance metrics used?
- In light of the concerns regarding the experimental validation, could the authors consider including more datasets or different experimental setups to validate the effectiveness of the method more robustly?
