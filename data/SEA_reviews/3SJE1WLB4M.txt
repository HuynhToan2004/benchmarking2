 **Summary:**
The paper explores the generalization error of kernel methods using spectral algorithms, focusing on the training of kernels with a family of spectral algorithms specified by a profile h(λ). The authors derive the generalization error as a functional of this learning profile for two data models: high-dimensional Gaussian and low-dimensional translation-invariant models. They provide full loss asymptotics for both noisy and noiseless observations, demonstrating the universality of the loss with respect to non-spectral details of the problem in the case of noisy observations. The paper also introduces a new kernel learning framework and discusses the implications of these results for neural network training.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, clear, and structured, making it easy to follow.
- The authors introduce a novel kernel learning framework that specifies the learning algorithm with a spectral profile h(λ), which is a significant contribution to the field.
- The paper provides a comprehensive derivation of the generalization error as a functional of the learning profile h(λ) for two data models: high-dimensional Gaussian and low-dimensional translation-invariant models.
- The authors demonstrate the universality of the loss with respect to non-spectral details of the problem in the case of noisy observations, which is an interesting and significant finding.
- The paper provides full loss asymptotics for both noisy and noiseless observations, which is a valuable contribution to the understanding of kernel methods.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper is dense and may be challenging for readers unfamiliar with the field to fully grasp.
- The paper focuses primarily on the case of power-law spectrum, which might limit its applicability to other types of spectral distributions.
- The paper does not provide a clear discussion on the implications of the results for neural network training, which could be a missed opportunity to connect the findings to a broader audience.
- The paper lacks a conclusion section, which could help in summarizing the main contributions and implications of the work.
- There is a lack of comparison with existing works, particularly in terms of the novelty and depth of the theoretical contributions.
- The paper does not include experimental validation, which could strengthen the claims made about the generalization error and the effectiveness of the proposed spectral algorithms.

**Questions:**
- How does the paper's approach compare to existing works in terms of novelty and depth of theoretical contributions?
- Could the authors provide a more detailed discussion on the implications of their findings for neural network training?
- How does the paper's approach handle different types of spectral distributions beyond power-law spectra?
- What are the implications of the results for the practical application of spectral algorithms in neural network training?
- Could the authors elaborate on the choice of the learning algorithm h(λ) and its implications for the generalization error?
- How does the paper's approach handle the saturation effect mentioned in the abstract, and what are the practical implications of this phenomenon?
