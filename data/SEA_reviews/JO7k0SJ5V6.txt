 **Summary:**
The paper explores the extrapolation capabilities of RoPE-based LLMs by fine-tuning with either smaller or larger bases within the training context length, which significantly enhances extrapolation performance. The authors propose a unified theoretical framework, Scaling Laws of RoPE-based Extrapolation, to describe the relationship between extrapolation performance, base value, and tuning context length. They demonstrate that fine-tuning with a smaller base on the original training length can achieve extrapolation up to 1M context length with only 16K training length on LLaMA2 7B and 13B. The paper also provides insights into the critical dimension for RoPE-based extrapolation, which helps in understanding the extrapolation issues and improving the performance of LLMs in long-context tasks.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written and easy to follow, with clear illustrations and figures that aid in understanding the content.
- The observation of fine-tuning a RoPE-based LLM with either a smaller or larger base in the pre-training context length is novel and significant, as it extends the context length of LLMs.
- The proposed Scaling Laws of RoPE-based Extrapolation provide a unified framework from a periodic perspective, which is both intuitive and effective in describing the relationship between extrapolation performance and base value and tuning context length.
- The paper provides a theoretical explanation for the phenomenon of RoPE-based extrapolation and establishes a new baseline for RoPE-based LLMs, which is a significant contribution to the field.
- The experiments conducted are thorough and convincing, demonstrating the effectiveness of the proposed methods.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper could benefit from more comprehensive experiments to validate the proposed methods across different LLMs and datasets, especially those with varying training context lengths and different base values.
- The paper lacks a detailed analysis of the computational complexity and efficiency of the proposed methods, which is crucial for practical deployment in real-world scenarios.
- The theoretical analysis in the paper is limited to LLMs with RoPE and does not extend to other positional embeddings, which could limit the generalizability of the findings.
- The paper could benefit from a more rigorous proofreading to correct minor typographical errors and inconsistencies in notation and terminology.
- The paper does not adequately discuss the limitations of the proposed methods, which is essential for understanding the scope and applicability of the findings.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors clarify the definition and role of the critical dimension in the context of RoPE-based extrapolation?
- How do the authors explain the observation that fine-tuning with a smaller base on the original training context length can achieve better extrapolation performance?
- Could the authors provide more details on the computational complexity and efficiency of the proposed methods, especially in comparison to existing methods?
- How do the authors plan to extend the theoretical analysis to other positional embeddings, and what are the implications for the generalizability of the findings?
- Could the authors elaborate on the limitations of the proposed methods and discuss potential directions for future research to address these limitations?
