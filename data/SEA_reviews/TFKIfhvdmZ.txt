 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel algorithm, Proximal Policy Gradient Arborescence (PPGA), which integrates on-policy Reinforcement Learning (RL) with the Differentiable Quality Diversity (DQD) framework, specifically using PPO. The algorithm aims to enhance the efficiency and effectiveness of skill discovery in challenging locomotion tasks, such as humanoid walking, by adapting PPO to DQD. The authors propose modifications to the CMA-MEGA algorithm, including the use of a vectorized PPO and a new multi-objective reward function, to optimize the search policy more effectively. Experiments conducted in the Mujoco environment demonstrate that PPGA outperforms existing methods in terms of diversity and performance, particularly in the humanoid domain.

**Strengths:**
- The paper presents a novel approach by combining on-policy RL with the DQD framework, which is a significant contribution to the field.
- The proposed algorithm, PPGA, shows a 4x improvement in best reward over baselines on the challenging humanoid domain, demonstrating its effectiveness.
- The paper is well-written, making the methodology and experimental results clear and understandable.
- The experiments are well-designed and comprehensive, providing a thorough evaluation of the proposed method.
- The paper introduces several innovative techniques such as vectorized PPO and the use of xNES for training stability, which are novel contributions to the field.
- The results are impressive, particularly in the humanoid domain, where PPGA outperforms existing methods.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks clarity in some sections, particularly in the explanation of the vectorized PPO and the multi-objective reward function, which could benefit from additional explanation and visual aids.
- There is a lack of comparison with other off-policy RL methods like SAC, which could provide a more comprehensive evaluation of the proposed method.
- The paper does not adequately address the computational cost and efficiency of the proposed method, which is a significant concern given the computational demands of the experiments.
- The paper does not include a discussion on the limitations of the proposed method, which could help in understanding the scope and applicability of the findings.
- The experiments are limited to specific environments, and it is unclear how the method would perform in other domains or real-world applications.

**Questions:**
- Could you clarify the computational cost and efficiency of the proposed method, particularly in comparison to other QD-RL methods?
- How does the method handle the issue of sample efficiency, given the computational demands of the experiments?
- Can you provide a more detailed explanation of the vectorized PPO and the multi-objective reward function, possibly with additional visual aids?
- How does the method perform in other domains, and what are the implications for real-world applications?
- Could you discuss the limitations of the proposed method and how these might affect its applicability and effectiveness in different scenarios?
