 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel Hyperbolic Hypergraph GNN (H2GNN) for learning on knowledge hypergraphs, focusing on the representation of hyperedges in a hyperbolic space. This approach, termed "hyper-star message passing," aims to preserve the hierarchical structure of hyperedges, which is crucial for understanding complex relationships within knowledge hypergraphs. The model is evaluated against 15 baselines and demonstrates superior performance in node classification and link prediction tasks. Despite its innovative approach, the paper suffers from several issues including unclear methodological descriptions, inadequate literature review, and a lack of theoretical justification for the hyperbolic space representation.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel approach to modeling knowledge hypergraphs using hyperbolic space, which is a significant advancement in the field.
- The proposed method, H2GNN, shows promising results in node classification and link prediction tasks, demonstrating its effectiveness.
- The use of hyperbolic space for hyperedge representation is innovative and could potentially lead to more accurate and comprehensive knowledge graph embeddings.
- The paper is well-structured, with clear explanations of the methodology and the results, making it accessible to readers.
- The experiments conducted are extensive, involving a variety of datasets and baseline methods, which helps in validating the proposed approach.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a clear and detailed explanation of the hyper-star message passing process, which is central to the proposed method.
- The literature review is insufficient and does not adequately position the proposed method within the existing body of work, particularly in the area of hyperbolic graph neural networks.
- The theoretical justification for using hyperbolic space for hyperedge representation is not convincingly argued, which raises questions about the necessity and effectiveness of this approach.
- The paper suffers from several typos and unclear descriptions, which could hinder understanding and reproducibility of the results.
- The experimental setup and results are not sufficiently detailed or explained, particularly in terms of the hyperparameters used and the methodology for generating negative samples.
- The paper does not adequately address the computational complexity and efficiency of the proposed method, which is crucial for practical applications.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors clarify the role and operation of the hyper-star message passing process in the context of H2GNN?
- How does the proposed method handle the issue of over-smoothing in GNNs, which is a common challenge in hypergraph neural networks?
- What is the rationale behind the choice of hyperbolic space for hyperedge representation, and how does it contribute to the overall performance of the model?
- How does the model handle the issue of multiple hyperedges connecting the same pair of nodes, and how does this affect the embedding process?
- Can the authors provide more details on the experimental setup, particularly the generation of negative samples and the choice of hyperparameters?
- How does the model perform on larger and more complex knowledge hypergraphs, and what are the computational requirements for training and inference?
- Could the authors expand on the literature review to better situate their work within the existing body of knowledge on hyperbolic graph neural networks and knowledge hypergraphs?
