 **Summary:**
The paper investigates the use of Generalized Cross-Validation (GCV) in sketched ridge regression ensembles, focusing on the consistency of GCV in estimating prediction risks. It extends the theoretical understanding of GCV's consistency to a broader class of sketches, demonstrating that the risk can be optimized by adjusting the sketch size in infinite ensembles. The authors also discuss the practical implications of their findings, including the use of GCV for efficient tuning of regularization and sketching parameters. The paper provides both theoretical analysis and empirical validation, showing that the results hold for a variety of sketches and are applicable to both synthetic and real-world datasets.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel approach to GCV risk estimation in sketched ridge regression, which is a significant contribution to the field.
- The paper is well-written, with clear and precise mathematical statements and proofs, making it accessible to readers.
- The theoretical results are robust and hold for a broad class of asymptotically free sketches, demonstrating the generality of the findings.
- The empirical results are convincing, validating the theoretical results and showing the practical applicability of the proposed methods.
- The paper is original in its approach, providing a new perspective on GCV in sketched ridge regression, which is a significant advancement in the field.

**Weaknesses:**
