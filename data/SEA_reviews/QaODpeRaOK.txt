 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel approach to text generation by integrating Monte-Carlo Tree Search (MCTS) with the Proximal Policy Optimization (PPO) algorithm. The method leverages the value network from PPO to guide the search process in MCTS, aiming to improve the preferability of generated text by integrating value-guided decoding. This approach is evaluated across several text generation tasks, demonstrating improvements in various metrics such as sentiment steering, toxicity reduction, and knowledge introspection. The paper also discusses the computational overhead and potential limitations of the method, suggesting areas for further research and development.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written and easy to follow, with a clear motivation for using the value model from PPO to guide MCTS, which is a novel application.
- The methodology is simple and straightforward, with a focus on utilizing the value model from PPO effectively, which is a significant aspect of the research.
- The paper includes a comprehensive evaluation across several text generation tasks, demonstrating the method's effectiveness in various scenarios.
- The use of a value model to guide MCTS is an innovative approach, particularly in the context of text generation, where it has not been extensively explored.
- The paper provides a detailed analysis of the proposed method, including ablation studies and comparisons with existing methods, which strengthens the argument for its effectiveness.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper could benefit from a more detailed comparison with existing methods, particularly in terms of computational overhead and the specific advantages of using PPO+MCTS over other guided decoding methods.
- There is a lack of discussion on the limitations of the proposed method, which is crucial for understanding its applicability and potential drawbacks.
- The paper does not sufficiently explore the impact of the value model on the effectiveness of MCTS, especially in terms of the value model's contribution to the search process.
- The paper could benefit from a more comprehensive evaluation, including additional benchmarks and a broader range of text generation tasks to validate the method's effectiveness more thoroughly.
- There are some minor presentation issues, such as inconsistencies in the use of symbols and formatting in equations, which could be clarified to improve readability.

**Questions:**
- Could you elaborate on the specific advantages of using PPO+MCTS over other guided decoding methods, particularly in terms of computational efficiency and the effectiveness of the value model in guiding the search process?
- How does the method perform when applied to different types of text generation tasks, such as machine translation or summarization?
- Have you considered using the value model to guide MCTS in a different manner, such as using the value of the current state as the initial Q value for the next action?
- Can you provide more details on the computational overhead introduced by the PPO+MCTS method, especially in comparison to other methods like PPO or beam search?
- How does the paper address the potential issues of overfitting or bias in the value model, which could impact the fairness and effectiveness of the MCTS search?
- Could you clarify the role of the value model in the MCTS search process, particularly in terms of its influence on the search strategy and the overall effectiveness of the method?
