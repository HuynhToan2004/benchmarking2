 **Summary:**
The paper investigates the fundamental limit of network pruning by leveraging tools from high-dimensional convex geometry, specifically the Approximate Kinematic Formula and the statistical dimension. It proposes a method to determine the pruning ratio threshold for a well-trained neural network, which is validated through experiments on various datasets and models. The paper also discusses the implications of the Hessian matrix's spectrum and weight magnitudes on pruning ratios. However, it faces criticism for its theoretical assumptions, such as the positive definiteness of the Hessian matrix, which is not typically met in neural networks. The paper's empirical results show that the proposed method can accurately predict pruning thresholds, but it does not outperform other pruning methods in terms of final accuracy.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel approach to pruning by considering the fundamental limit of network pruning, which is a significant contribution to the field.
- The paper is well-written, making it easy to follow, and the theoretical results are clearly presented, which aids in understanding the proposed methods.
- The experimental results demonstrate that the proposed method can accurately predict pruning thresholds, providing a practical application of the theoretical framework.
- The paper provides a comprehensive literature review, which is beneficial for readers seeking a deeper understanding of the topic.
- The methodology is supported by a rigorous theoretical framework, which is well-explained and could be of interest to researchers in the field.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper's theoretical assumptions, such as the positive definiteness of the Hessian matrix, are not met in practical neural networks, which could limit the applicability of the proposed method.
- The paper's empirical results show that the proposed method does not outperform other pruning methods in terms of final accuracy, which is a significant drawback.
- The paper's experimental validation is limited to specific datasets and models, which may not generalize well to other architectures or datasets.
- The paper's contribution to the field is not clearly articulated, and it does not sufficiently differentiate its approach from existing methods, which could lead to confusion about its novelty and utility.
- The paper's assumptions and methodologies, such as the use of the Hessian matrix and the approximation of the sublevel set, are not sufficiently justified or explained, which could limit the understanding and acceptance of the proposed methods.
