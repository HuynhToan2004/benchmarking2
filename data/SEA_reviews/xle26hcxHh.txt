 **Summary:**
The paper introduces AudioFormer, a novel method for source-free domain adaptation, which leverages a transformer architecture with an auxiliary domain module to capture diverse representations. This module helps in distinguishing invariable features from the source domain and aligning them with the target domain using conditional guided multi-kernel MMD. The methodology includes multiple consistency strategies to identify and handle hard samples, enhancing the robustness of the model. Extensive experiments on benchmarks like Office-31, Office-Home, and VisDA-C demonstrate the effectiveness of AudioFormer, showing improvements over existing methods. However, concerns are raised regarding the clarity of the motivation behind the auxiliary domain module and the novelty of the approach, which seems to heavily borrow from existing works.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, easy to follow, and the motivation is clear.
- The proposed method shows significant improvements over previous state-of-the-art (SOTA) methods, as demonstrated in Table 1, 2, and 3.
- The use of the transformer architecture with an auxiliary domain module to capture diverse representations is novel and effective.
- The method includes multiple consistency strategies to handle hard samples, which is a notable contribution.
- The experimental results are robust and convincing, demonstrating the effectiveness of the proposed method.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a clear motivation for the auxiliary domain module, and the explanation for why it is effective is insufficient.
- The novelty of the method is questionable, as it heavily borrows from existing works, particularly from G-SFDA, and the improvements are not substantial.
- The paper could benefit from a more detailed analysis of the consistency strategies and their impact on performance.
- There is a lack of discussion on the computational cost of the proposed method compared to existing methods.
- The paper does not provide a detailed comparison with the latest works, which limits the evaluation of its current relevance.
- The experimental results, particularly those on the VisDA-C dataset, are not as impressive as those on other datasets like Office-31 and Office-Home.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors clarify the motivation behind the auxiliary domain module and provide a more detailed explanation of its effectiveness?
- How does the proposed method compare to the latest works in terms of performance and computational cost?
- Can the authors provide a more detailed analysis of the consistency strategies and their impact on performance?
- Why does the performance of AudioFormer on the VisDA-C dataset not meet expectations, and what can be done to improve this?
- How does the proposed method handle the computational cost, especially considering the additional computational demands of the auxiliary domain module?
- Could the authors provide more details on the experimental setup, particularly the training epoch and the learning rate used for the Office-31 and Office-Home datasets?
