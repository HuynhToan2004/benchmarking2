 **Summary:**
The paper introduces DisenBooth, a novel framework designed to address the challenges of identity-preserving text-to-image generation. It employs a disentangled tuning approach, utilizing separate embeddings for identity-relevant and identity-irrelevant information. This approach aims to improve the fidelity of generated images by preserving the subject's identity and enhancing the control over the generation process. The paper presents a methodology that includes weak denoising and contrastive embedding objectives to achieve disentanglement, supported by extensive experiments that demonstrate the superiority of DisenBooth over existing methods. However, concerns were raised regarding the generalizability of the method to more complex or diverse scenarios and the lack of detailed comparisons with state-of-the-art methods.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel approach to text-to-image generation by disentangling identity-relevant and identity-irrelevant information, which is a significant advancement in the field.
- The methodology is well-explained, and the paper is easy to follow, making it accessible to a broad audience.
- The experimental results are robust and demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed method, showing superior performance over existing methods.
- The use of auxiliary disentangled objectives and the combination of textual identity-preserved embedding and visual identity-irrelevant embedding provides a more flexible and controllable image generation process.
- The paper addresses an important problem in text-to-image generation, which is crucial for applications in various domains.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks detailed comparisons with state-of-the-art methods, which could undermine the claims of superior performance.
- There is a lack of discussion on the limitations of the proposed method, particularly in scenarios where the method may not perform optimally.
- The methodology section is somewhat unclear, particularly in the explanation of the weak denoising and contrastive embedding objectives.
- The paper does not provide a detailed analysis of the computational cost or memory requirements of the proposed method, which is crucial for practical deployment.
- The generalizability of the method to more complex or diverse scenarios is not thoroughly addressed, which could limit the applicability of the findings.
- The paper does not include a user study, which is a common practice in evaluating text-to-image generation methods.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors provide a more detailed comparison with state-of-the-art methods to substantiate the claims of superior performance?
- How does the proposed method perform in scenarios where the subject identity is not clearly defined or when multiple subjects are present in the dataset?
- Could the authors provide a more detailed analysis of the computational cost and memory requirements of the proposed method?
- How does the method perform in scenarios where the input images are of low quality or highly varied in style?
- Can the authors provide a more detailed explanation of the weak denoising and contrastive embedding objectives, particularly how they contribute to the disentanglement of identity-relevant and identity-irrelevant information?
- How does the proposed method handle scenarios where the subject identity is not clearly defined or when multiple subjects are present in the dataset?
