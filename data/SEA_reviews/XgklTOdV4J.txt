 **Summary:**
The paper introduces DualAug, a novel data augmentation method that addresses the issue of out-of-distribution (OOD) data generated by heavy augmentations. DualAug employs a two-branch approach, where one branch applies basic augmentations and the other applies heavy augmentations. An OOD detector is used to identify and filter out OOD data from the heavy augmentation branch, replacing it with data from the basic augmentation branch. The method is evaluated on various datasets, including CIFAR-10/100, SVHN-Core, and ImageNet, demonstrating improvements in performance over existing methods. Despite its effectiveness, the paper has been criticized for its lack of novelty and the need for more extensive comparisons with state-of-the-art methods.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written and easy to follow, with clear explanations of the proposed method and its components.
- The motivation behind the research is clear, and the proposed method is simple yet effective.
- The method is applicable to various datasets and tasks, including semi-supervised learning and contrastive self-supervised learning, demonstrating its versatility.
- The proposed method achieves good performance and is easy to implement, which could potentially lead to its widespread adoption.
- The paper provides a thorough evaluation of the proposed method, including ablation studies and hyperparameter analyses, which help in understanding the method's effectiveness and its impact on performance.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a clear comparison with state-of-the-art methods, particularly in terms of performance metrics and methodological approaches.
- The paper could benefit from a more comprehensive literature review, especially in the area of data augmentation and out-of-distribution data detection.
- The experimental setup could be improved, particularly in terms of the choice of datasets and the range of hyperparameters tested.
- There is a lack of discussion on the limitations of the proposed method and potential negative societal impacts.
- The paper does not provide sufficient details on the implementation of the OOD detection module, which is crucial for understanding the method's effectiveness.
- The paper could benefit from more extensive comparisons with other data augmentation methods and a broader range of datasets to validate the generalizability of the proposed method.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors provide more details on the implementation of the OOD detection module, including how the threshold for OOD detection is set and how it affects the performance of the method?
- How does the proposed method compare with other state-of-the-art data augmentation methods, particularly in terms of performance and computational efficiency?
- Could the authors elaborate on the choice of datasets and the experimental setup, particularly the range of hyperparameters tested and the rationale behind these choices?
- What are the potential limitations of the proposed method, and how might these limitations impact its practical application?
- Could the authors discuss the potential negative societal impacts of their method, if any, and how these impacts might be mitigated?
