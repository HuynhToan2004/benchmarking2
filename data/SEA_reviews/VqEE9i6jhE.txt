 **Summary:**
The paper introduces DecGreenNet-TT and DecGreenNet-TT-C, extensions of the DecGreenNet model, which employ tensor-train structured low-rank decomposition to enhance the learning of Green's functions in high-dimensional partial differential equations (PDEs). These models aim to address the computational challenges associated with Monte-Carlo sampling by using tensor product grids and low-rank decomposition. The authors demonstrate that these methods can effectively learn Green's functions with fewer neural networks and smaller computational resources, particularly in high-dimensional PDEs. The paper also explores the use of tensor-train decomposition to improve the efficiency of neural network training.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written and easy to follow, with a clear explanation of the proposed methods and their applications.
- The use of tensor-train structured low-rank decomposition to learn Green's functions in high-dimensional PDEs is innovative and addresses a significant computational challenge in the field.
- The paper provides a novel approach to parameterizing the solution to PDEs using a neural network, which is a significant contribution to the field.
- The experimental results demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed methods, showing improvements in computational efficiency and accuracy compared to existing methods.
- The paper is well-organized and includes a comprehensive discussion on the limitations and potential future directions of the research.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper could benefit from more detailed explanations of the tensor product grid construction and the tensor-train decomposition used, as these are crucial aspects of the proposed methods.
- The paper lacks a thorough discussion on the limitations of the proposed methods, particularly in terms of their applicability to non-separable functions and the potential challenges in high-dimensional settings.
- The paper could include more comprehensive comparisons with existing methods, particularly in terms of computational efficiency and accuracy across a broader range of PDE problems.
- The paper does not provide a detailed analysis of the computational complexity of the proposed methods, which is crucial for understanding their practical applicability in high-dimensional settings.
- The paper could benefit from a more detailed discussion on the scalability of the proposed methods, particularly in terms of how they perform as the dimensionality of the PDEs increases.
