 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel method called CoPrompt for fine-tuning large vision-language models, focusing on improving generalization and reducing overfitting in few-shot settings. CoPrompt utilizes a consistency constraint across text and image branches, enforcing consistency between the pre-trained and trainable models. This constraint is achieved through perturbations in input images and text prompts, and the integration of adapters and prompts. The method is evaluated across various benchmarks and shows promising results, particularly in base-to-novel generalization and cross-dataset evaluation. However, the paper's contributions are seen as incremental, and the method's novelty is questioned due to its similarity to existing techniques like CoOp and MaPLe.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written and easy to follow, with a clear presentation of the proposed method and its experimental results.
- The idea of consistency regularization is simple yet effective, as demonstrated by the experimental results.
- The proposed method achieves good performance on base-to-novel generalization and cross-dataset evaluation, surpassing previous state-of-the-art methods.
- The method is straightforward and can be applied to different models, such as CLIP, which is widely used in the community.
- The paper provides extensive ablation studies, which help in understanding the impact of different components of the proposed method.
- The paper is well-organized, making it easy to understand and reproducible.

**Weaknesses:**
- The novelty of the proposed method is limited, as it builds upon existing techniques like CoOp and MaPLe, with only minor modifications such as the consistency constraint.
- The paper lacks a detailed comparison with previous methods, particularly in terms of the number of learnable parameters and training epochs, which could affect the fairness of the comparisons.
- The paper does not sufficiently discuss the differences between the proposed method and similar existing methods like PromptSRC, which also uses a consistency constraint.
- The paper could benefit from more comprehensive evaluations, such as domain generalization and more datasets in the cross-dataset evaluation, to provide a more robust validation of the proposed method.
- The paper does not sufficiently discuss the limitations of the proposed method, which could help in understanding its applicability and potential drawbacks.
- The paper could benefit from more detailed explanations of the consistency constraint and its implementation, particularly in the context of different datasets and model configurations.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors clarify the differences between the proposed method and similar existing methods like PromptSRC, particularly in terms of the consistency constraint and its implementation?
- How does the proposed method perform when compared to other methods in terms of fairness, especially in terms of the number of learnable parameters and training epochs?
- Why was the decision made to use a consistency constraint on the image branch, and how does this affect the performance of the model?
- Could the authors provide more detailed explanations of the consistency constraint and its implementation, particularly in the context of different datasets and model configurations?
- How does the proposed method compare to other methods in terms of inference speed and computational complexity?
- Could the authors provide more details on the training time and FLOPs of the proposed method compared to other methods?
- How does the proposed method perform in terms of zero-shot learning, and what are the implications of this for the generalizability of the model?
