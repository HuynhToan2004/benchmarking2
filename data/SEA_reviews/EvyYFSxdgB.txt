 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel difficulty-aware task sampler (DATS) for meta-learning physics-informed neural networks (PINNs), aimed at optimizing the sampling of PINN tasks based on their difficulty levels. The DATS approach is designed to adaptively allocate residual points across tasks, minimizing validation loss and improving the accuracy of meta-learned PINN solutions. The method is evaluated against uniform and self-paced task-sampling baselines, demonstrating its effectiveness in reducing performance disparity across different PDE configurations. Despite its novelty and potential, the paper faces criticisms regarding the limited scope of PDEs tested, the lack of theoretical analysis, and the practical implications of its methodology.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel difficulty-aware task sampler (DATS) for meta-learning of physics-informed neural networks (PINNs), which is a significant contribution to the field.
- The methodology of DATS is well-explained, with clear definitions and mathematical derivations that are easy to follow.
- The paper provides a thorough evaluation of the proposed method, comparing it to uniform and self-paced task-sampling baselines across five benchmark PDEs, demonstrating its effectiveness in reducing performance disparity across different PDE configurations.
- The authors have provided a detailed discussion on the limitations and potential future work, which shows a commitment to the ongoing development and improvement of the proposed method.
- The paper is well-written, making it accessible and understandable to readers.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper only evaluates the method on a limited set of PDEs, which raises concerns about the generalizability of the findings.
- There is a lack of theoretical analysis, which could strengthen the paper's claims and provide a deeper understanding of the proposed method.
- The paper lacks a clear explanation of how the difficulty is defined and measured, which is crucial for understanding the method's effectiveness.
- The paper does not include a comprehensive comparison with existing methods, which could help in positioning the proposed method within the current literature.
- The paper does not discuss the limitations of the method comprehensively, which could help in understanding the challenges and potential pitfalls of the proposed approach.
- The paper does not include a discussion on the practical implications of the method, particularly in terms of computational cost and scalability.

**Questions:**
- How does the DATS method handle the definition and measurement of difficulty in different PDEs?
- Could you provide a more detailed explanation of the role of the KL regularization in the DATS method?
- How does the DATS method compare to existing meta-learning PINN methods, particularly in terms of computational efficiency and accuracy?
- Can you clarify the role of the KL regularization in the DATS method and how it affects the performance of the model?
- How does the DATS method perform when applied to more complex PDEs, such as those involving higher dimensions or more complex physical phenomena?
- Could you provide more details on the computational cost and scalability of the DATS method, especially in comparison to other meta-learning PINN methods?
