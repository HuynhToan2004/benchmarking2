 **Summary:**
The paper introduces the Tubal Tensor Train (TTT) decomposition, a novel tensor decomposition model that effectively mitigates the curse of dimensionality inherent in the Tensor Singular Value Decomposition (T-SVD). The TTT decomposition represents an N-order tensor as the Tubal product of a series of two third-order and (N-3) fourth-order core tensors, contracted. The paper proposes two efficient algorithms to compute the TTT of a higher-order tensor and demonstrates the efficiency and accuracy of these algorithms through numerical simulations on diverse tasks such as tensor completion and compression of RGB images, videos, and hyperspectral images. Despite its novelty, the paper has been critiqued for its limited novelty and lack of comprehensive comparisons with other existing tensor decomposition models.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel tensor decomposition model called "Tubal Tensor Train" (TTT), which successfully mitigates the curse of dimensionality exhibited in the T-SVD model.
- Two efficient algorithms are proposed to compute the TTT of a higher-order tensor, and extensive simulations are conducted to show the efficiency of the approach on diverse tasks.
- The paper is well-organized, and the proposed algorithms are clearly explained, making the content accessible and understandable.
- The TTT model is a natural extension of the T-SVD model, and the authors have provided a detailed discussion on the properties of the TTT model.
- The paper is well-written, with clear figures and tables that aid in understanding the content.

**Weaknesses:**
- The novelty of the TTT decomposition is limited as it is a direct extension of the T-SVD model, and the paper does not provide a clear motivation or justification for the use of TTT decomposition over existing methods.
- The paper lacks comprehensive comparisons with other tensor decomposition models such as T-SVD, T-CANDECOMP, and T-ALS, which could have provided a more robust evaluation of the TTT model's performance.
- The paper does not discuss the computational complexity of the proposed algorithms, which is crucial for understanding their practical applicability.
- The paper does not include a discussion on the limitations of the proposed model, which could help in understanding its applicability and potential drawbacks.
- The paper does not provide a clear explanation of the TTT-SVD algorithm and its implementation, which could be improved for better understanding.
- The paper does not discuss the scalability of the proposed model, which is crucial for understanding its applicability to large-scale problems.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors provide a clearer motivation and justification for the use of TTT decomposition over existing methods?
- How does the TTT model perform in comparison to other tensor decomposition models such as T-SVD, T-CANDECOMP, and T-ALS?
- What is the computational complexity of the proposed algorithms, and how does it compare to other methods?
- How does the TTT model handle the scalability issues that are common in high-order tensor decompositions?
- Can the authors provide a more detailed explanation of the TTT-SVD algorithm and its implementation?
- How does the TTT model handle the potential issues of overfitting or underfitting in high-dimensional data?
- Can the authors discuss the potential limitations of the proposed model and provide examples or scenarios where it may not be applicable?
