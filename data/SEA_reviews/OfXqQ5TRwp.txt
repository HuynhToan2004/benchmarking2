 **Summary:**
The paper introduces ALAM, a novel framework for Activation-Compressed Training (ACT) that utilizes average quantization and a lightweight sensitivity calculation scheme to minimize gradient variance and achieve significant memory savings in large language models (LLMs). ALAM represents activations as group averages, reducing memory usage by up to 12.5x while maintaining training performance. It also introduces a cost-effective sensitivity calculation algorithm based on the variance of parameter gradients, which further reduces memory overhead. The framework's effectiveness is demonstrated through extensive experiments on various transformer models, showcasing its ability to compress activations while maintaining accuracy.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel method for activations compression, which is a significant advancement in the field of Activation-Compressed Training (ACT).
- The proposed method achieves a 12.5x compression rate while maintaining training performance, which is a significant improvement over existing methods.
- The paper is well-written, making it easy to follow and understand, with a clear presentation of the proposed method and its implementation details.
- Extensive experiments are conducted to demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed method, including comparisons with state-of-the-art methods and thorough ablation studies.
- The methodology is supported by theoretical analysis, which adds credibility to the proposed approach.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper does not adequately address the impact of the proposed method on training time, which is a critical factor in practical applications.
- There is a lack of detailed discussion on the limitations of the proposed method, particularly in large-scale models.
- The paper does not include a comparison with other compression methods, such as quantization and pruning, which are also relevant in the context of ACT.
- The paper lacks a comprehensive discussion on the generalizability of the proposed method to other models beyond the transformer architecture.
- There is a potential issue with the fairness of comparisons, as the proposed method may have advantages due to its specialized design, which might not be applicable to other methods.
- The paper could benefit from a more thorough discussion on the potential societal impacts of the proposed method.

**Questions:**
- How does the proposed method compare to other compression methods, such as quantization and pruning, in terms of effectiveness and efficiency?
- Can the proposed method be applied to other models beyond the transformer architecture, and if so, what are the expected outcomes?
- How does the proposed method perform in larger models, and what are the potential limitations or challenges in scaling up the method?
- Can the authors provide more details on the training time impact of the proposed method, particularly how it affects the overall training process?
- Could the authors elaborate on the potential societal impacts of the proposed method, particularly in terms of data privacy and security?
