 **Summary:**
The paper introduces FragSel, a novel approach to handle noisy labels in regression tasks by fragmenting the label space into contrasting pairs. This method leverages a mixture of experts to select clean samples based on neighborhood agreements, aiming to enhance the performance of regression models. The paper also introduces a new metric, Error Residual Ratio (ERR), to evaluate sample selection and refurbishment techniques in noisy regression tasks. The proposed method is evaluated on four benchmark datasets across various domains, showing significant improvements over thirteen state-of-the-art baselines. Despite its innovative approach, the paper is criticized for its lack of clarity in writing and the need for more comprehensive comparisons with existing methods.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel approach to noisy regression learning by fragmenting the label space, which is a unique contribution to the field.
- The proposed method, FragSel, is evaluated on four benchmark datasets across diverse domains, demonstrating its effectiveness in noisy regression tasks.
- The paper presents a comprehensive evaluation of the proposed method, including the introduction of a new metric, Error Residual Ratio (ERR), which is specifically designed for evaluating sample selection and refurbishment techniques in noisy regression tasks.
- The paper is well-written, with clear figures and a clear motivation for the proposed method.
- The proposed method achieves better performance than thirteen state-of-the-art baselines on four benchmark datasets, demonstrating its effectiveness.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks clarity in some sections, particularly in the introduction and methodology, which could benefit from further refinement.
- The paper does not provide sufficient details on the training of feature extractors and the selection of hyperparameters, which could affect the reproducibility and generalizability of the results.
- The paper does not compare the proposed method with existing methods like MixUp and other related works, which could provide a more robust evaluation of the proposed method.
- The paper's experimental setup and results are not sufficiently detailed, with some figures and tables not being sufficiently informative.
- The paper does not discuss the limitations of the proposed method, which could provide valuable insights for future research.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors clarify the definitions and roles of "fragment" and "contrasting fragment pair" in the context of the proposed method?
- How does the proposed method compare with existing methods like MixUp, and what are the specific advantages of FragSel over these methods?
- What is the rationale behind the choice of using symmetric and Gaussian noise for label injection, and how does this affect the performance of the proposed method?
- Could the authors provide more details on the training of feature extractors and the selection of hyperparameters, including the sensitivity of these parameters to the performance of the proposed method?
- How does the proposed method perform when applied to different types of regression tasks, such as image regression, and what are the potential challenges or limitations in these applications?
- Could the authors discuss the potential limitations of the proposed method and suggest directions for future research to address these limitations?
