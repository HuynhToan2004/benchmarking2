 **Summary:**
The paper introduces DeNEVIL, a novel prompt generation algorithm designed to dynamically exploit Large Language Models' (LLMs) value vulnerabilities, revealing their underlying value inclinations. It constructs a high-quality dataset, MoralPrompt, consisting of 2,397 prompts covering 500+ value principles. The study benchmarks the intrinsic values across a spectrum of LLMs, revealing that most models are misaligned with human ethical values. The authors then propose VILMO, an in-context alignment method aimed at enhancing the value compliance of LLM outputs. The methodology is based on Moral Foundations Theory and involves a generative evaluation framework to assess the ethical values of LLMs. The paper presents a comprehensive evaluation of 27 LLMs and introduces VILMO to improve LLM outputs' ethical value alignment.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel prompt generation algorithm tailored to dynamically exploit LLMs' value vulnerabilities, which is a significant contribution to the field.
- The proposed methodology is based on Moral Foundations Theory, providing a solid theoretical foundation for the research.
- The paper is well-written, with clear and concise descriptions of the proposed framework and methodologies.
- The experimental results are comprehensive, covering a wide range of LLMs and demonstrating the effectiveness of the proposed method.
- The paper addresses a timely and important issue of LLM alignment with human ethical values, which is crucial for the safe deployment of LLMs.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper could benefit from a more detailed explanation of the Moral Prompt dataset and its construction process, including details on the number of scenarios and their diversity.
- There is a lack of clarity on how the Moral Prompt dataset is used in the experiments, particularly how it is applied to different LLMs and how it contributes to the results.
- The paper does not adequately address the potential limitations of the Moral Prompt dataset, such as its ability to fully capture the complexity of human moral values and its potential biases.
- The paper lacks a comprehensive comparison with existing methods, such as those based on other moral theories or those that use different evaluation metrics.
- There is a lack of discussion on the potential negative societal impacts of the proposed method, such as the generation of harmful content or the reinforcement of existing biases.
- The paper could benefit from a more detailed analysis of the results, including a discussion on the trade-offs between ethical alignment and performance or generation quality.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors provide more details on the Moral Prompt dataset, particularly its construction process and the diversity of scenarios?
- How is the Moral Prompt dataset used in the experiments, and how does it contribute to the results?
- Could the authors elaborate on the potential limitations of the Moral Prompt dataset and how these might affect the results?
- How does the proposed method compare with other moral theories, and what are the advantages of using Moral Foundations Theory?
- Could the authors discuss the potential negative societal impacts of the proposed method, such as the generation of harmful content or the reinforcement of existing biases?
- How does the proposed method address the trade-offs between ethical alignment and performance or generation quality?
- Could the authors provide more details on the ethical considerations of the proposed method, particularly in terms of ensuring that the generated content is safe and beneficial?
