 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel text augmentation method aimed at generating pseudo-labels for text classification tasks, focusing on adjusting the level of manipulation systematically. This method involves combining sentence-level and document-level embeddings with SHAP values to create a combined sentence embedding, which is then used to adjust the candidate sentence pool's range based on cosine similarity. The proposed method, tested on three datasets, shows some improvement over baseline methods but lacks a significant performance boost. The paper's contribution is viewed as incremental and limited by the use of outdated models like Bi-LSTM and a lack of comprehensive experimental validation across different datasets and model architectures.

**Strengths:**
- The paper addresses an important problem in text augmentation, focusing on the level of manipulation in text augmentation, which is crucial for generating high-quality pseudo-labels.
- The methodology proposed is simple and easy to understand, with clear descriptions of the algorithms and methodologies used.
- The paper is well-written, making it accessible and easy to follow, which is essential for academic papers.
- The problem addressed is significant, and the paper's approach is innovative, combining sentence-level and document-level embeddings with SHAP values to create a combined sentence embedding, which is then used to adjust the candidate sentence pool's range based on cosine similarity.
- The paper includes a good number of experiments, demonstrating the effectiveness of the proposed method across multiple datasets, showing improvements in performance compared to baseline methods.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks comprehensive experimental validation, particularly in terms of the number of datasets used (only three), which limits the generalizability of the results.
- The performance improvement over baseline methods is limited, and the paper does not adequately discuss the limitations of the proposed method, such as the potential for introducing noise or misleading labels in the augmented data.
- The paper primarily focuses on sentiment analysis tasks, which may not fully demonstrate the general applicability of the proposed method across different text classification tasks.
- The use of outdated models like Bi-LSTM and the lack of experiments with more recent models like BERT or RoBERTa could limit the relevance and applicability of the findings.
- The paper does not provide a detailed comparison with state-of-the-art methods, which could help in understanding the competitive advantage of the proposed method.
- The paper lacks a discussion on the hyperparameter selection and its impact on the performance of the model, which is crucial for reproducibility and generalizability of the results.

**Questions:**
- Can you provide more details on the experimental setup, particularly the number of experiments conducted and the statistical significance of the results?
- How does the proposed method perform when applied to other text classification tasks beyond sentiment analysis?
- Could you elaborate on the choice of using Bi-LSTM over other models like BERT or RoBERTa, and how this affects the performance and generalizability of the findings?
- How do the authors ensure that the augmented data does not introduce noise or misleading labels, and what measures are in place to mitigate these risks?
- What are the specific hyperparameters used in the experiments, and how were they selected? How does the choice of hyperparameters affect the performance of the model?
- Can you provide a detailed comparison with state-of-the-art methods, including a discussion on the competitive advantages and disadvantages of your method?
