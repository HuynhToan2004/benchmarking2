 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel multi-label image classification method named Hybrid Sharing Query (HSQ), which utilizes a mixture-of-experts (MoE) architecture to optimize the fusion of task-specific and shared experts. The model is designed to handle label heterogeneity by leveraging label correlations and mitigating negative transfer effects. Extensive experiments on benchmark datasets such as MS-COCO and PASCAL VOC demonstrate the method's effectiveness, showing improvements over state-of-the-art methods. The paper also includes an ablation study to evaluate the impact of various components and a discussion on the performance of the proposed method.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written and easy to follow, with a clear and concise presentation of the proposed method.
- The use of a mixture-of-experts (MoE) architecture to handle label heterogeneity and optimize the fusion of task-specific and shared experts is a novel approach.
- The paper includes a thorough ablation study to evaluate the impact of different components, such as the number of shared experts, on the performance of the model.
- Extensive experiments demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed method, showing improvements over state-of-the-art methods on benchmark datasets like MS-COCO and PASCAL VOC.
- The paper discusses the performance of the proposed method and provides a detailed analysis of the results, which is helpful for understanding the model's behavior and limitations.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed discussion on the limitations of the proposed method, which could provide insights into its potential shortcomings and areas for future research.
- There is a lack of comparison with other state-of-the-art methods in some experiments, which could affect the validity of the results.
- The paper does not provide a detailed comparison of the computational cost of the proposed method with other approaches, which is crucial for understanding its practical applicability.
- The motivation behind the use of MoE in multi-label image classification is not clearly articulated, and the paper does not provide sufficient evidence to support the claim that MoE is the best solution for handling label heterogeneity.
- The paper does not include a discussion on the potential negative societal impacts of the proposed method, which is a significant omission in the ethical considerations section.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors provide a more detailed discussion on the limitations of the proposed method and potential areas for future research?
- How does the proposed method compare in terms of computational cost with other state-of-the-art methods?
- Can the authors clarify the motivation behind the use of MoE in multi-label image classification and provide more evidence to support this choice?
- How does the proposed method perform when applied to other datasets, such as the MS-COCO dataset?
- Could the authors provide more details on the training and inference time of the proposed method compared to other approaches?
- How does the paper ensure the reproducibility of the results, and what steps have been taken to prevent data leakage during the training phase?
