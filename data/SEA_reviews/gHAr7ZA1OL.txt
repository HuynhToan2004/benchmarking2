 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel method for unsupervised out-of-distribution (OOD) object detection using a Modulated Phase Diffusion (MPD) approach. The MPD method leverages the phase information of input features to synthesize OOD features, enhancing the discrimination ability of object detectors. The proposed method involves a forward process that gradually performs Gaussian Average on the phase of extracted features, followed by a reverse process to synthesize expected OOD features. The methodology is supported by extensive experiments on multiple datasets, demonstrating superior performance compared to existing methods. Despite its innovative approach, the paper faces criticism for its limited novelty, unclear explanations of certain components, and the need for a broader range of experimental validations.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel method for unsupervised OOD object detection using a Modulated Phase Diffusion (MPD) approach, which effectively synthesizes OOD features based on the phase of input features.
- The proposed method demonstrates superior performance in detecting OOD objects compared to existing methods, as shown by extensive experimental results across multiple datasets.
- The paper is well-written, making it easy to follow and understand the proposed methodologies and their applications.
- The use of Fourier analysis to explore the content and style of features is innovative and provides a new perspective on feature synthesis for OOD detection.
- The paper addresses the significant challenge of unsupervised OOD detection, which is crucial for real-world applications.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a clear explanation of the phase of input features, particularly how it is extracted and what it represents in the context of the proposed method.
- The novelty of the method is questioned as it appears to be a simple extension of existing methods like VOS, with limited technical innovation.
- The paper does not provide sufficient ablation studies to validate the effectiveness of the proposed components, such as the OOD and augmented feature synthesis modules.
- The performance of the method is not consistently superior across all metrics, and there are concerns about the method's ability to maintain performance on ID objects while enhancing OOD detection.
- The paper does not sufficiently compare the proposed method with the latest state-of-the-art methods, limiting the evaluation of its effectiveness.
- The paper lacks a detailed explanation of some components, such as the uncertainty loss and the forward diffusion via averaging phase, which are crucial for understanding the method's operation.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors provide a clearer explanation of how the phase of input features is extracted and what it represents in the context of the proposed method?
- How does the proposed method compare with the latest state-of-the-art methods in terms of performance and effectiveness?
- Could the authors provide more detailed ablation studies to validate the effectiveness of the proposed components, such as the OOD and augmented feature synthesis modules?
- In the context of the proposed method, how does the uncertainty loss contribute to the overall performance, and could this be validated through additional experiments?
- How does the proposed method perform on different datasets, especially those with varying OOD object distributions?
- Could the authors clarify the operation of the forward diffusion via averaging phase and the use of Gaussian Average in the forward process?
