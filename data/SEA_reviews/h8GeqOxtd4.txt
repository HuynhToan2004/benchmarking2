 **Summary:**
The paper investigates the training of a two-layer fully connected neural network using gradient descent (GD) to learn the score function in diffusion models. It introduces a novel parametric form for the score estimator based on score decomposition, transforming the score-matching objective into a regression with noisy labels. The authors provide theoretical results on the convergence of GD, establishing sample complexity bounds for score estimation. They also analyze the excess risk bound for score estimation, which is influenced by the number of training samples, network size, and the number of iterations. The paper aims to fill a gap in understanding the theoretical performance of widely-used gradient-based algorithms in training neural networks for score matching.

**Strengths:**
- The paper provides a comprehensive theoretical analysis of score estimation using neural networks trained by gradient descent, which is a significant gap in the literature.
- It introduces a novel parametric form for the score estimator based on score decomposition, which transforms the score-matching objective into a regression with noisy labels, enhancing the understanding of score estimation.
- The paper establishes sample complexity bounds for score estimation, which is a significant contribution to the field.
- The theoretical results are presented in a clear and concise manner, making the paper accessible and understandable.
- The paper is well-written, with a clear structure and logical flow, which facilitates comprehension of the complex theoretical concepts.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper focuses on a specific architecture (two-layer ReLU neural network) which might limit the generalizability of the results to other neural network architectures.
- There is a lack of empirical validation to support the theoretical claims, which could undermine the practical applicability of the results.
- The paper could benefit from more detailed discussions on the limitations of the proposed methods and the assumptions made in the theoretical analysis.
- The paper's theoretical results are primarily based on the Neural Tangent Kernel (NTK) regime, which might not fully capture the behavior of neural networks in practical scenarios.
- The paper does not adequately discuss the practical implications of the theoretical results, which could limit the paper's impact on the broader research community.

**Questions:**
- How do the results extend to other neural network architectures beyond the two-layer ReLU network used in the analysis?
- Could the authors provide empirical evidence to support the theoretical claims made in the paper?
- What are the practical implications of the theoretical results, and how can they be applied to improve the training of diffusion models?
- Could the authors discuss the limitations of the proposed methods and the assumptions made in the theoretical analysis?
- Is there a possibility to extend the theoretical analysis to include more complex neural network architectures or different training algorithms?
