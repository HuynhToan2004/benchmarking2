 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel architecture called ReCAT, which integrates a contextual inside-outside (CIO) layer with a Transformer to model hierarchical syntactic structures in text. The CIO layer enables direct communication between constituents at different levels, enhancing the model's ability to learn contextualized multi-grained representations. The architecture is evaluated on various sentence-level and span-level tasks, showing improvements over vanilla Transformer models and other models like DIORA and R2D2. The experiments demonstrate that ReCAT can effectively capture hierarchical structures and outperform other models in tasks requiring such structures. However, concerns are raised about the clarity of the paper, particularly in the explanation of the CIO layer and the overall structure of the model.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, with clear explanations and a comprehensive evaluation that demonstrates the effectiveness of the proposed model in capturing hierarchical structures in text.
- The model's novel approach of enabling direct communication between constituents at different levels and its ability to learn contextualized multi-grained representations are significant contributions to the field.
- The paper includes a detailed analysis of the model's performance on various tasks, showing improvements over other models like DIORA and R2D2, and demonstrating the model's ability to capture hierarchical structures effectively.
- The experiments are well-designed, and the results are convincing, showing that the proposed model outperforms other models in tasks requiring hierarchical structure understanding.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks clarity in explaining the CIO layer and its integration with the Transformer, making it difficult for readers to fully understand the model's architecture and functionality.
- There is a need for more detailed explanations and examples of how the model handles hierarchical structures and the implications of these structures on the model's performance.
- The paper could benefit from a clearer presentation and better organization, particularly in the explanation of the model's components and the experimental setup.
- The model's performance on sentence-level tasks is not as strong as on span-level tasks, which raises questions about the generalizability of the model across different types of tasks.
- The paper could provide more detailed comparisons with other models, especially in terms of computational efficiency and the scalability of the model to larger datasets.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors clarify the integration of the CIO layer with the Transformer and provide more detailed explanations of how the model handles hierarchical structures?
- How does the model perform on longer sentences, and what are the implications of sentence length on the model's performance?
- Could the authors provide more detailed examples or case studies to illustrate the model's ability to capture hierarchical structures and the impact of these structures on the model's performance?
- What are the specific advantages of the CIO layer over other models like DIORA and R2D2, and how does the model handle the information leakage issue mentioned in the paper?
- How does the model handle the pruning process, and what are the implications of this process on the model's performance and the accuracy of the hierarchical structures it induces?
