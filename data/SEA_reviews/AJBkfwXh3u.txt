 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel method for explaining the predictions of Dynamic Graph Neural Networks (DyGNNs) by leveraging a causality-inspired approach. The authors propose a framework that disentangles causal relationships into static and dynamic components, using a VGAE-based encoder-decoder architecture to generate soft masks for these relationships. The methodology includes a contrastive learning module to differentiate between causal and trivial relationships and a dynamic correlating module for static and dynamic causal relationships. The paper presents experiments on both synthetic and real-world datasets, demonstrating the effectiveness of the proposed method. However, concerns are raised about the clarity of the methodology, the complexity of the experimental setup, and the generalizability of the results.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, with a clear motivation and structure that makes it easy to follow.
- The proposed method is novel and innovative, particularly in its application of causality-inspired methods to dynamic graphs, which is a significant advancement in the field.
- The paper provides a comprehensive evaluation of the proposed method, including an ablation study and a case study, which demonstrate the effectiveness of the approach.
- The authors have developed a dataset specifically for dynamic graph interpretability, which is a valuable contribution to the research community.
- The paper is well-organized, with clear explanations of the methodology and the results, which are supported by detailed experimental setups.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks clarity in some sections, particularly in the explanation of the causal soft masks and the methodology used in the experiments.
- The complexity of the experimental setup and the computational demands of the proposed method might limit its practical application.
- The generalizability of the method to real-world datasets is questionable, as the experiments were primarily conducted on synthetic datasets.
- There is a lack of discussion on the limitations of the proposed method and how it might perform under different conditions or with different types of data.
- The paper does not sufficiently differentiate its approach from existing methods, which could lead to confusion about the novelty and effectiveness of the proposed method.

**Questions:**
- Can you provide a more detailed explanation of how the causal soft masks are estimated and how they relate to the causal relationships in the graph?
- How does the proposed method handle the complexity of dynamic graphs, particularly in terms of the computational demands and the scalability of the approach?
- What are the specific advantages of using the proposed method over existing methods, particularly in terms of interpretability and explanation accuracy?
- How does the method perform on real-world datasets, and what steps have been taken to ensure the generalizability of the findings to more complex, real-world scenarios?
- Could you elaborate on the role of the pre-trained aggregation function in the methodology, and how it impacts the overall performance and effectiveness of the approach?
