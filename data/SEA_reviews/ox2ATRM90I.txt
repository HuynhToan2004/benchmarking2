 **Summary:**
The paper introduces Yet Another ICU Benchmark (YAIB), a modular framework designed to standardize and compare clinical machine learning experiments across different ICU datasets. The framework supports multiple open-source ICU datasets, enabling unified model development, transfer, and evaluation. It includes a predefined set of prediction tasks and a flexible preprocessing pipeline, which is crucial for reproducibility and comparability in ICU prediction models. The paper highlights the impact of dataset, cohort definition, and preprocessing on prediction performance, emphasizing the need for a benchmarking tool like YAIB.

**Strengths:**
- The paper addresses a significant problem in the ICU prediction literature by providing a framework for standardized model development, transfer, and evaluation, which is crucial for reproducibility and comparability.
- The framework is designed to be flexible and extensible, allowing for the integration of new datasets and tasks, which is a notable strength.
- The paper includes a comprehensive set of experiments and benchmarks, which help in understanding the performance of different models and datasets.
- The authors have provided detailed documentation and code for their work, which enhances the reproducibility and accessibility of their research.
- The paper is well-written, making it easy to follow and understand, which is essential for a technical document.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper could benefit from a more detailed description of the preprocessing steps and their impact on the results, particularly how these steps affect the performance of different models.
- There is a lack of discussion on the limitations of the framework, which could help in understanding the scope and applicability of the research.
- The paper does not sufficiently discuss the generalizability of the findings to other datasets or settings, which is a critical aspect of the research.
- The paper does not provide a comprehensive evaluation of the proposed framework, particularly in terms of its performance and scalability, which could limit its practical utility.
- The paper could benefit from a more detailed comparison with other benchmarking tools and frameworks, which would help in understanding the unique contributions and advantages of the proposed framework.

**Questions:**
- Could you provide more details on the preprocessing steps and their impact on the results? How do these steps affect the performance of different models, and what are the implications for the generalizability of the findings?
- How does the proposed framework compare to other benchmarking tools and frameworks in terms of performance, scalability, and ease of use?
- What are the main limitations of the framework, and how do these limitations affect the practical utility and applicability of the research?
- Could you discuss the potential of extending the framework to other medical settings or datasets, and what challenges or adaptations would be required for such extensions?
- How do the authors plan to address the issues of data leakage and causal leakage in their framework, particularly in the context of ICU prediction models?
