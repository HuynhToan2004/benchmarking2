 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel method for training language models (LLMs) to sample from intractable posterior distributions, leveraging generative flow networks (GFlowNets) to amortize the sampling process. This approach is applied to various tasks such as sequence continuation, infilling, and tool use, demonstrating improved sample diversity and out-of-distribution generalization. The method is evaluated against several baselines, including supervised fine-tuning and PPO, showing superior performance in terms of diversity and accuracy. The paper also explores the use of GFlowNets for chain-of-thought reasoning, offering a principled approach that avoids the mode collapse issues typically associated with reward-maximizing reinforcement learning.

**Strengths:**
- The paper presents a novel approach to sampling from intractable distributions using GFlowNets, which is a principled and flexible method that can be applied to various tasks involving intractable inference in LLMs.
- The method is evaluated against several baselines, including supervised fine-tuning and PPO, showing superior performance in terms of diversity and accuracy.
- The paper is well-written, clear, and easy to follow, with a clear motivation and a logical structure that aids in understanding the proposed method and its applications.
- The experiments are well-designed, with a variety of tasks and datasets used to demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed method.
- The paper addresses an important and timely issue in the field of language modeling, focusing on improving the sample diversity and out-of-distribution generalization of LLMs.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper could benefit from more detailed explanations and examples of how to apply GFlowNets to different tasks, particularly in the context of chain-of-thought reasoning.
- The experimental results are not always convincing, with some results showing only marginal improvements or no significant differences compared to baselines.
- The paper lacks a thorough comparison with other methods, such as MCMC and other reinforcement learning methods, which could provide a more comprehensive evaluation of the proposed method.
- The paper does not adequately address the computational cost and efficiency of the proposed method, which is critical for practical applications.
- The paper could benefit from a more detailed discussion on the limitations of the proposed method, particularly in terms of its applicability to different types of LLMs and its generalization across different tasks.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors provide more detailed examples or a step-by-step guide on how to apply GFlowNets to different tasks, especially in the context of chain-of-thought reasoning?
- How does the proposed method compare to other methods such as MCMC and other reinforcement learning methods in terms of computational cost, efficiency, and practical applicability?
- What are the specific computational costs and training times associated with the proposed method, and how do these compare to other methods?
- How does the proposed method perform when applied to different types of LLMs, such as those trained for different tasks or with different architectures?
- Could the authors provide more detailed results on the computational efficiency and generalization of the proposed method across different tasks and datasets?
- How does the proposed method handle the issue of mode collapse, especially in tasks where the reward model may not be well-specified?
