 **Summary:**
The paper introduces SPIDER, a novel perturbation-based data augmentation method that enhances model robustness by adjusting the confidence of labels based on the magnitude of perturbations. This approach aims to increase the boundary thickness and flatten the minima, thereby improving robustness against both common and adversarial corruptions. The method is supported by theoretical analysis and empirical results across various datasets, including CIFAR and ImageNet. SPIDER's effectiveness is demonstrated through extensive experiments, showing improvements in robustness without significant degradation in clean accuracy. However, concerns about the novelty of the approach, the clarity of some experimental results, and the lack of comparison with other methods limit the paper's impact.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, making it easy to follow and understand the proposed methodology.
- The theoretical analysis provided is solid, with clear explanations and empirical evidence supporting the claims.
- The experiments conducted are comprehensive, covering various datasets and showing significant improvements in robustness.
- The proposed method is simple and effective, enhancing both common and adversarial robustness, which is a significant contribution to the field.
- The paper includes detailed explanations of the proposed method and its effects on model robustness, providing a clear understanding of the proposed approach.

**Weaknesses:**
- The novelty of the approach is limited, as similar ideas have been previously explored, such as the use of perturbation-based data augmentation and label smoothing.
- The paper lacks a clear definition and explanation of the perturbation function used, which is crucial for understanding the method's effectiveness.
- There is a lack of detailed comparison with other methods like Mixup, which could have provided a more robust evaluation of the proposed method.
- The experimental results, particularly those showing the impact of SPIDER on clean accuracy, are not convincing and need further clarification.
- The paper could benefit from a more thorough discussion of related works, especially those that have explored similar ideas in label smoothing and data augmentation.
- The presentation of some experimental results and the clarity of the method's description could be improved to enhance understanding and reproducibility.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors clarify the role and impact of the perturbation function in the proposed method?
- How does the proposed method compare with other data augmentation methods, particularly in terms of robustness and clean accuracy?
- Could the authors provide more detailed explanations or examples of the perturbation function's role in enhancing robustness?
- In the experimental results, why does SPIDER show a decline in clean accuracy, and how does this impact the overall effectiveness of the method?
- How does the proposed method address the issue of adversarial robustness, and what specific advantages does it offer over other methods in this area?
