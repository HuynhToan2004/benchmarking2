 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel knowledge distillation method for flow-based generative models, specifically designed for privacy protection and fairness enhancement. The method involves a student model trained to reconstruct the latent space and image space, respectively, from the teacher model, thereby reducing the size and complexity of the student model. This distillation approach allows the student model to be deployed on embedded devices, enhancing privacy protection by anonymizing images. The methodology is evaluated through experiments on various datasets, demonstrating the effectiveness of the distillation approach in improving privacy and fairness. However, the paper faces criticism for its limited experimental scope, lack of comprehensive baseline comparisons, and unclear explanations of certain methodological choices.

**Strengths:**
- The paper addresses the significant issue of privacy protection in machine learning, which is crucial for building trust in AI systems.
- The proposed method is novel, leveraging a flow-based generative model for privacy protection, which is an innovative approach.
- The paper is well-written, clear, and easy to follow, making it accessible to a broad audience.
- The methodology is evaluated through experiments on various datasets, demonstrating the effectiveness of the distillation approach in improving privacy and fairness.
- The proposed distillation method is novel and effective, reducing the size of the student model while maintaining performance and privacy.
- The paper provides a comprehensive evaluation of the proposed method, including both quantitative and qualitative results, and discusses the limitations of the approach.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a thorough discussion on the limitations of the proposed method, particularly in terms of its scalability and generalizability to other types of generative models.
- The experimental results are not convincing, with insufficient comparisons to other methods and baselines, and the results are not sufficiently detailed or comprehensive.
- The paper does not adequately address the potential for privacy breaches, especially in scenarios where the attacker has access to the device's memory.
- The paper's claims regarding the effectiveness of the proposed method in privacy protection and fairness enhancement are not convincingly supported by the experimental results.
- The paper lacks a detailed discussion on the computational cost of the proposed method, which is crucial for practical deployment on embedded devices.
- The paper does not provide sufficient implementation details, making it difficult to replicate the results or understand the methodology fully.
- The paper could benefit from a more comprehensive review of related work and a clearer presentation of the methodology, particularly in figures and tables.

**Questions:**
- How does the proposed method ensure that the student model does not introduce significant privacy risks, especially in scenarios where the attacker has access to the device's memory?
- Can the authors provide more details on the computational cost of the proposed method, particularly in terms of inference time and energy consumption?
- How does the proposed method compare to other privacy-preserving methods that do not rely on generative models?
- Could the authors provide more details on the experimental setup, including the specifics of the datasets used and the training parameters?
- How does the proposed method handle different types of privacy-sensitive data beyond face images, such as license plates or medical images?
- Can the authors clarify the role and effectiveness of the "anonymization key" in the privacy protection system?
- How does the proposed method ensure that the generated images are not too similar to the original images, potentially compromising privacy?
