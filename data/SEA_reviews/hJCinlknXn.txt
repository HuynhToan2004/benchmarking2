 **Summary:**
The paper introduces UOEP, a novel approach to enhancing user-oriented exploration in recommender systems (RS) using a distributional critic and a population of actors. Each actor optimizes towards different target quantiles within the return distribution, promoting effective exploration among distinct user groups. The methodology involves a distributional critic that learns the return distribution and a population of actors that optimize towards different quantiles, ensuring diversity and stability. The approach is evaluated across multiple datasets, showing promising results in terms of long-term performance, user satisfaction, and fairness. Despite its strengths, the paper is criticized for its clarity in writing, the limited scope of datasets, and the lack of detailed comparisons with existing methods.

**Strengths:**
- The paper addresses an important and practical problem in the field of recommender systems, focusing on enhancing user experiences and fairness through personalized exploration strategies.
- The proposed method, UOEP, is innovative, combining a distributional critic with a population of actors, each optimizing towards different quantiles, which is a novel approach to addressing the exploration challenges in RS.
- The paper is well-structured, with clear writing and a logical flow that aids in understanding the complex methodology.
- The experimental results demonstrate the effectiveness of UOEP in improving long-term performance, user satisfaction, and fairness, supported by comprehensive ablation studies that validate the contributions of individual components.
- The paper provides a detailed discussion on the design choices and their impact on the performance of UOEP, which enhances the understanding of the proposed method.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper could benefit from clearer writing, particularly in the introduction and methodology sections, to make the content more accessible to a broader audience.
- The scope of datasets used in the experiments is limited, which may not fully validate the generalizability of the proposed method across different types of recommendation tasks.
- The paper lacks detailed comparisons with existing methods, such as those that also employ multiple actors or similar exploration strategies, which could provide a more robust evaluation of UOEP's effectiveness.
- There is a need for more extensive evaluations, including additional datasets and metrics, to further validate the claims made about UOEP's performance and its impact on user satisfaction and fairness.
- The paper does not adequately address the computational complexity and scalability of UOEP, which could be a significant concern for large-scale recommender systems.
- The paper does not discuss the limitations of UOEP, which could provide a more balanced view of the method's strengths and weaknesses.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors clarify how the number of actors in UOEP is determined and how this affects the performance of the system?
- How does the proposed method ensure that the exploration strategies remain effective over time, particularly in scenarios where user preferences or system dynamics change?
- Could the authors provide more detailed comparisons with existing methods, such as those that also employ multiple actors or similar exploration strategies, to better understand the relative advantages of UOEP?
- How does the computational complexity of UOEP scale with the size of the user base or the number of items in the recommendation system?
- In the context of the experiments, how were the baselines chosen, and what specific metrics were used to evaluate the performance of UOEP against these baselines?
- Could the authors discuss the potential societal impacts of UOEP, particularly in terms of privacy and user autonomy, and how these impacts were addressed in the development and testing of the method?
