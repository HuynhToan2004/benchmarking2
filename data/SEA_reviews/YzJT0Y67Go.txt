 **Summary:**
The paper introduces HIPODE, a novel data augmentation method designed for offline reinforcement learning (RL) that focuses on generating high-return synthetic data to improve the performance of downstream RL policies. HIPODE operates by selecting states with potentially high value from candidate states, ensuring authenticity and applicability across various RL algorithms. The method is tested on MuJoCo tasks and shows competitive results against state-of-the-art policy-dependent and model-based offline RL methods. The approach is policy-decoupled, which allows for broader applicability and is supported by extensive experiments and ablation studies. However, concerns are raised about the generalizability of the findings to more complex environments and the clarity of the method's technical contributions.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel data augmentation method for offline RL that generates high-return synthetic data, which is a significant contribution to the field.
- The method is policy-decoupled, allowing it to be used with various RL algorithms, enhancing its versatility.
- The paper is well-written, with clear explanations and illustrations that aid in understanding the proposed method.
- Extensive experiments and ablation studies are conducted, providing a robust evaluation of the proposed method.
- The method is supported by a theoretical framework, which adds credibility to the proposed approach.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper's experiments are limited to MuJoCo environments, which may not fully demonstrate the generalizability of the proposed method to more complex or realistic environments.
- The method's technical contribution is unclear, particularly the role and integration of the value maximization mechanism and the use of a state transition model.
- The paper lacks a clear definition and discussion on the term "authenticity," which is crucial for understanding the method's effectiveness.
- The paper's claims about the superiority of high-return data over high-diversity data are not convincingly supported by the results, which show only marginal improvements.
- There are inconsistencies and potential errors in the experimental results and methodological descriptions, which need clarification.
- The paper could benefit from a more detailed discussion on the limitations and potential negative societal impacts of the proposed method.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors clarify the role and integration of the value maximization mechanism in the proposed method?
- How does the method ensure the authenticity of the generated data, and what are the implications if the authenticity is not maintained?
- Could the authors provide a clearer definition and discussion on the term "authenticity" as used in the paper?
- How does the proposed method compare in terms of computational efficiency and resource requirements to other data augmentation methods?
- The paper mentions using a state transition model to generate candidate next states. Can the authors elaborate on this process and how it integrates with the overall methodology?
- Can the authors provide more detailed experimental results and clarify the discrepancies noted in the current results?
- How does the proposed method handle the potential for overfitting, especially in scenarios where the synthetic data is not sufficiently diverse?
