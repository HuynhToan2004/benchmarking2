 **Summary:**
The paper introduces Forgedit, a novel text-guided image editing framework that leverages a forgetting strategy to address overfitting issues in diffusion models. It proposes a joint fine-tuning framework that optimizes vision and language information, along with vector subtraction and projection mechanisms to enhance text embedding accuracy. The framework demonstrates state-of-the-art performance on the TEdBench benchmark, showcasing significant improvements in CLIP and LPIPS scores. However, concerns about the novelty and depth of the forgetting strategy, as well as the clarity of presentation, were highlighted.

**Strengths:**
- The proposed forgetting strategy is innovative and effectively addresses the overfitting issue in text-guided image editing, as demonstrated by state-of-the-art performance on the TEdBench benchmark.
- The paper is well-organized, with clear figures and tables that aid in understanding the proposed methods and their results.
- The joint fine-tuning framework for vision and language information is a significant contribution, enhancing the efficiency and accuracy of the editing process.
- The vector subtraction and projection mechanisms are novel and contribute to the preservation of the characteristics of the original image during editing.
- The paper provides a comprehensive evaluation of the proposed method, including a detailed ablation study that demonstrates the effectiveness of the forgetting strategy.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed discussion on the forgetting strategy, particularly the rationale behind the choice of parameters and their impact on the results.
- The novelty of the forgetting strategy is questionable as similar approaches have been explored in previous works, such as the BLIP+DreamBooth method.
- The paper could benefit from a more detailed comparison with existing methods, including qualitative results and a broader range of benchmarks.
- The paper's writing could be improved for better clarity and comprehension, with some figures and captions being unclear or misleading.
- The paper's contribution to the field is somewhat limited, as the forgetting strategy, while innovative, does not represent a significant leap beyond existing methods.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors provide more details on the rationale behind the choice of parameters for the forgetting strategy?
- How does the forgetting strategy compare to similar approaches in previous works, such as BLIP+DreamBooth?
- Could the authors clarify the role and impact of the forgetting strategy on the editing process, particularly in terms of its contribution to the overall performance?
- How does the forgetting strategy affect the reconstruction quality, and can this be quantitatively measured?
- Could the authors provide more qualitative results to support the claims made about the effectiveness of the forgetting strategy?
- How does the forgetting strategy perform on different types of images or editing tasks beyond those tested in the current study?
