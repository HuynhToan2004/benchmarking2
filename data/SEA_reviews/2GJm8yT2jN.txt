 **Summary:**
The paper introduces URLOST, a novel unsupervised representation learning method designed for high-dimensional data lacking stationarity and topology. This method employs a combination of a learnable self-organizing layer, density adjusted spectral clustering, and masked autoencoders to effectively learn meaningful representations across diverse modalities. The approach is evaluated on various datasets including simulated biological vision data, neural recordings from the primary visual cortex, and gene expression datasets. The paper claims that URLOST outperforms existing methods like SimCLR and MAE, setting new benchmarks in the field. However, concerns are raised about the lack of comprehensive comparisons with other state-of-the-art methods and the clarity in explaining the method's novelty and its application to diverse data modalities.

**Strengths:**
- The paper addresses an important and relevant problem in unsupervised representation learning, focusing on high-dimensional data without stationarity or topology, which is a significant challenge in the field.
- The proposed method is straightforward, effective, and well-explained, making it accessible and understandable.
- The paper provides a comprehensive evaluation of the proposed method across various datasets, including biological vision data and neural recordings, demonstrating its effectiveness and applicability.
- The paper is well-written, making the content easy to follow, and the results are well-presented, enhancing the reader's understanding of the method's capabilities.
- The authors have conducted a thorough ablation study, which helps in understanding the impact of different components of the model on its performance.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a clear explanation of how the method extends to other data modalities, which is crucial for demonstrating its versatility and applicability.
- The paper does not sufficiently compare its method with other state-of-the-art methods, such as contrastive learning and masked autoencoders, which could have provided a more robust validation of the proposed method's effectiveness.
- The paper's evaluation is limited to a few datasets, which may not sufficiently demonstrate the method's generalizability across diverse data types.
- The paper does not provide a detailed discussion on the limitations of the proposed method, which could help in understanding its scope and applicability more clearly.
- There is a lack of clarity in the presentation of the method, particularly in the explanation of how the self-organizing layer and the clustering process are integrated, which could confuse readers.
- The paper's title and some of its claims, such as being the first to address the problem of unsupervised representation learning for general high-dimensional data, are overstated and not adequately supported by the evidence provided.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors clarify how the method can be applied to other data modalities beyond those tested, and provide examples or demonstrations of its effectiveness in these contexts?
- How does the method compare to other state-of-the-art methods in terms of performance, especially in the context of diverse data types?
- Could the authors provide more detailed explanations of the clustering process and the integration of the self-organizing layer into the model?
- What are the specific limitations of the proposed method, and under what conditions might it not perform effectively?
- Could the authors elaborate on the method's novelty and its contribution to the field, particularly in light of existing methods that might also address similar challenges?
