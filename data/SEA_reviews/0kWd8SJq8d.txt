 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel method for estimating mutual information (MI) using diffusion models, leveraging the Girsanov theorem to derive a new estimator that approximates the KL divergence between distributions. The methodology involves training a diffusion model on two distributions, A and B, and then using the trained score network to estimate the MI. The paper presents a family of MI estimators based on different diffusion models and evaluates their performance on a variety of synthetic datasets. Despite the theoretical novelty, the paper's empirical results are not convincing, with significant discrepancies between the estimated and true MI values, and the method's performance is not consistently superior to existing estimators.

- The theoretical derivations are sound and well-explained, providing a clear understanding of the methodology.
