 **Summary:**
The paper investigates the effectiveness of affine mapping in long-term time series forecasting (LTSF) models, focusing on the use of reversible normalization and increasing input horizon to enhance model robustness. It highlights the challenges of affine mapping in predicting non-periodic signals or time series with varying periods across channels. The paper provides theoretical and experimental evidence to support its findings, including a closed-form solution for linear models in forecasting periodic signals. It also discusses the limitations and potential remedies for linear models when dealing with more complex datasets. The paper's contributions include a thorough investigation of the intrinsic effectiveness of recent approaches in LTSF, demonstrating that affine mapping is critical to their forecasting performance.

- The paper discusses the limitations and future works, which is commendable for its transparency and commitment to continuous improvement.
