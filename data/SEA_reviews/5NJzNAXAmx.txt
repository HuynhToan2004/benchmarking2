 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel framework for learning in POMDPs by integrating additional information at training time, termed "informed POMDPs." This framework generalizes the standard POMDP by allowing access to extra information, which is not available during execution. The authors propose an objective for learning sufficient statistics based on this information, which is adapted to Dreamer to create an informed Dreamer algorithm. The paper presents experiments across various environments, showing that the informed Dreamer can learn faster than the standard Dreamer in most cases. However, it also highlights that this approach can lead to suboptimal policies in some environments. The paper is well-written, and the theoretical contributions are robust, although the empirical results are somewhat mixed.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, clearly organized, and easy to follow, with a clear motivation for the study.
- The proposed framework is novel and interesting, addressing the problem of learning in POMDPs with additional information at training time.
- The paper provides a solid theoretical foundation for the proposed method, with proofs for theorems and corollaries in the appendix.
- The methodology is well-motivated, with a clear theoretical foundation and empirical evidence supporting its effectiveness.
- The paper includes a good empirical evaluation, showing that the proposed method outperforms the standard Dreamer in most cases.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a formal definition of the information i, which is crucial for understanding the framework.
- The paper does not provide a detailed analysis of the performance of the informed Dreamer in environments where it underperforms, which could be valuable for understanding the limitations of the proposed method.
- The experiments are limited to simple environments, which might not fully demonstrate the effectiveness of the method in more complex or realistic scenarios.
- The paper does not discuss the limitations of the method in detail, which could help in understanding its practical applicability and potential drawbacks.
- The paper could benefit from a more thorough discussion of related work, particularly in the context of CTDE and multi-agent RL.
- The paper could be clearer in explaining the differences between the informed Dreamer and the standard Dreamer, and how these differences impact the performance of the method.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors provide a formal definition of the information i and discuss how it is used in the model?
- How does the proposed method perform in more complex or realistic environments, and what are the potential limitations in such scenarios?
- What are the specific reasons for the underperformance of the informed Dreamer in certain environments, and can these be addressed through modifications to the method?
- Could the authors discuss the potential of extending the method to handle multiple agents in a multi-agent RL setting?
- How does the method compare to other approaches that leverage additional information at training time, such as those discussed in related works?
