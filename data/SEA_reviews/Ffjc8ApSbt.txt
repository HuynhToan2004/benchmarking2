 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a kernel-based method for balancing propensity scores in recommender systems, focusing on the selection bias problem in collaborative filtering. It proposes a universal kernel-based balancing method to balance functions within the reproducing kernel Hilbert space and an adaptive kernel balancing method during alternating updates. Theoretical analysis is provided to show the impact of balancing finite-dimensional function classes on the bias of IPS and DR methods. Experiments on three real-world datasets demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed approach. However, the paper suffers from clarity issues, particularly in the explanation of kernel-related terms and the optimization problem, and the presentation of the experimental results.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written and easy to follow, with a clear and concise presentation of the proposed method.
- The proposed method is simple, effective, and theoretically grounded, offering a novel approach to balancing kernel functions for propensity estimation in recommender systems.
- The paper includes theoretical results and extensive experiments, demonstrating the effectiveness of the proposed method.
- The method's novelty lies in its ability to balance functions within the reproducing kernel Hilbert space, which is a significant advancement in the field.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper suffers from clarity issues, particularly in the explanation of kernel-related terms and the optimization problem, which could hinder understanding for readers unfamiliar with kernel methods.
- The paper lacks a detailed discussion on the computational complexity and scalability of the proposed method, which is crucial for practical applications.
- There are several typographical and notation errors that need correction to improve the readability and professionalism of the paper.
- The experimental results are not convincingly presented, with insufficient detail on the setup and results, which makes it difficult to assess the effectiveness of the proposed method.
- The paper does not sufficiently discuss the limitations of the proposed method, which could help in understanding its applicability and potential drawbacks.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors clarify the computational complexity and scalability of the proposed method, especially in terms of the number of user-item pairs and the number of balancing functions?
- How does the proposed method perform in terms of runtime and memory usage compared to other methods?
- Could the authors provide more detailed experimental results, including standard deviations and a comparison with other methods on the same datasets?
- In the context of the proposed method, what is the role of the kernel function, and how does it influence the performance of the method?
- How are the hyperparameters chosen in the experiments, and what is the impact of different hyperparameter settings on the performance of the method?
- Could the authors explain the intuition behind the kernel balancing method and provide a clearer explanation of the optimization problem used in the method?
- Is there a possibility to include a comparison with other state-of-the-art methods in terms of performance and computational efficiency?
