 **Summary:**
The paper explores the convergence of gradient descent (GD) for overparameterized two-layer linear networks with general losses, focusing on finite width, finite step size, and general initialization. The authors introduce a local PL condition and a local Descent Lemma, which are critical for analyzing the convergence rate of GD. They demonstrate that these conditions hold locally with constants that depend on the weights and other problem parameters. The paper also proposes an adaptive step size scheduler to accelerate convergence, which is supported by numerical experiments. The study extends beyond the NTK regime, providing a more realistic analysis of GD convergence in practical settings.

**Strengths:**
- The paper provides a comprehensive analysis of the convergence rate of gradient descent (GD) for two-layer linear neural networks, extending beyond the NTK regime.
- It introduces a local PL condition and a local Descent Lemma, which are crucial for understanding the convergence behavior of GD in overparameterized neural networks.
- The paper demonstrates that these conditions hold locally with constants that depend on the weights, providing a novel approach to analyzing GD convergence.
- The authors propose an adaptive step size scheduler to accelerate convergence, which is supported by numerical experiments.
- The paper is well-written, with clear explanations of the results and a detailed comparison with existing literature.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper assumes that the loss function is twice differentiable, K-smooth, and μ-strongly convex, which may not hold in practical scenarios.
- The adaptive step size scheduler proposed in the paper is heuristic and may not be the optimal choice for all scenarios.
- The paper only considers two-layer linear networks, which limits the generalizability of the findings to more complex neural network architectures.
- The analysis is primarily based on the overparameterized setting, which may not be applicable to practical scenarios where the width of the neural network is not infinite.
- The paper does not sufficiently address the practical implications of the theoretical results, particularly how they can be applied to improve the performance of neural networks in practical settings.
