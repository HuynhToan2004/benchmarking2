 **Summary:**
The paper introduces ChemRLformer, a novel method for molecular design using reinforcement learning (RL) and transformers, specifically designed for text-based molecular generation. The authors evaluate the performance of ChemRLformer against other RL-based methods, showing that it can achieve state-of-the-art results in various molecular design tasks. The approach involves pretraining a language model on existing datasets and fine-tuning it for specific tasks, utilizing different architectures and training strategies. The paper is praised for its thorough evaluation and clear presentation, although it is criticized for its limited novelty and the lack of a detailed comparison with other state-of-the-art methods.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, clear, and easy to follow, with a comprehensive evaluation of different text-based RL methods for molecule discovery.
- It presents a novel approach by using text-based RL methods for molecule discovery, which is a significant advancement in the field.
- The paper provides a thorough evaluation of various text-based RL methods for molecule discovery, including different text representations, architectures, and training strategies.
- The authors demonstrate that their proposed method, ChemRLformer, achieves state-of-the-art performance across multiple molecular design tasks, outperforming previous text-based RL algorithms.
- The paper includes a detailed ablation study that provides insights into the performance of different components of the proposed method.
- The authors have made significant efforts to ensure the reproducibility of their results by providing detailed experimental setups and code.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed comparison with other state-of-the-art methods, particularly in terms of the specific training strategies and architectures used.
- The novelty of the proposed method is questioned, as it seems to be a combination of existing methods without significant new contributions.
- The paper does not sufficiently discuss the limitations of the proposed method, particularly in terms of scalability and generalizability.
- The paper's claims about the effectiveness of certain components, such as the pre-training and fine-tuning steps, are not convincingly supported by the data presented.
- The paper's focus on text-based RL methods for molecule discovery may limit its applicability to other types of molecular design tasks.
- The paper's evaluation is limited to a specific set of datasets and tasks, which may not fully demonstrate the generalizability of the proposed method.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors clarify the specific contributions of their proposed method compared to existing methods, particularly in terms of novelty and performance improvements?
- How does the proposed method compare in terms of computational efficiency and scalability with other state-of-the-art methods, especially in larger and more complex molecular design tasks?
- Could the authors provide more details on the training strategies and hyperparameters used in their experiments, particularly in comparison to other state-of-the-art methods?
- How does the proposed method handle the exploration-exploitation trade-off in molecular design tasks, and what are the implications of this for the practical application of the method?
- Can the authors discuss the potential of extending the proposed method to other types of molecular design tasks beyond those evaluated in the paper?
