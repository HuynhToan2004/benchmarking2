 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel group fairness measure called Matched Demographic Parity (MDP) and proposes a learning algorithm, Fairness Through Matching (FTM), to achieve fairness in group-level decision-making. MDP is defined as the infimum of the expected difference in model predictions for a given sensitive group, where the matching function T_s is a map from the sensitive group to itself that ensures equal probability distributions. The FTM algorithm aims to minimize the expected difference in model predictions between the original and matched data points. The paper provides theoretical justifications and empirical evaluations on several datasets, comparing the proposed method with existing fairness algorithms. Despite its innovative approach, the paper has been critiqued for its limited experimental scope, lack of detailed comparisons with other fairness notions, and potential inefficiencies in handling noisy data.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, with a clear and coherent structure, and the main ideas are presented effectively.
- The introduction of Matched Demographic Parity (MDP) as a new group fairness measure is a significant contribution to the field, and the proposed algorithm, Fairness Through Matching (FTM), is innovative and well-explained.
- The paper provides theoretical justifications for the proposed method and empirical evaluations on several datasets, which support the claims made.
- The paper is well-motivated, addressing the issue of group unfairness and providing a novel approach to addressing this problem.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks detailed comparisons with other fairness notions, such as individual fairness and counterfactual fairness, which are crucial for a comprehensive evaluation of the proposed method.
- The experimental setup is limited, focusing only on tabular datasets, which may not adequately demonstrate the generalizability of the proposed method to more complex data types.
- There is a lack of discussion on the limitations of the proposed method, which is essential for understanding its applicability and potential drawbacks.
- The paper could benefit from a more detailed discussion on the computational complexity and scalability of the proposed method, particularly in terms of its practical implementation in larger, more complex datasets.
- The paper does not sufficiently address the potential societal impacts of the proposed method, which is a significant oversight given the ethical implications of fairness in AI.
