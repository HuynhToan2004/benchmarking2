 **Summary:**
The paper explores the Gaussian Process (GP) limit of neural networks initialized with Pseudo-iid distributions, which generalize the traditional iid Gaussian initialization. This approach allows for the derivation of GP limits in scenarios where previous methods did not apply, such as low-rank and structured sparse weight initialization. The paper extends the proof of Gaussian Process behavior from Matthews et al. (2018) to a broader class of initial weight distributions, including pseudo-iid distributions that encompass both iid and orthogonal weights. The theoretical results are supported by numerical simulations, and the paper discusses the implications of these findings for Bayesian Neural Networks and the Edge of Chaos (EoC) analysis.

**Strengths:**
- The paper extends the proof of Gaussian Process from Matthews et al. (2018) to a broader class of initial weight distributions, including pseudo-iid distributions which encompass both iid and orthogonal weights.
- It shows that fully connected and convolutional networks initialized with Pseudo-iid distributions are equivalent up to their variance, which is a significant generalization of previous results.
- The paper provides a unified proof for various initialization schemes, including low-rank and structured sparse weights, which are of practical interest due to their computational speed-up benefits.
- The authors demonstrate that fully connected and convolutional networks initialized with Pseudo-iid distributions are all effectively equivalent up to their variance, which is a significant generalization of previous results.
- The paper is well-written and easy to follow, making it accessible to a broad audience.
- The results have implications for Bayesian Neural Networks and the Edge of Chaos (EoC) analysis, which could lead to new insights and applications.

**Weaknesses:**
