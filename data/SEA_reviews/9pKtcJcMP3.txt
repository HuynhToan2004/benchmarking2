 **Summary:**
The paper introduces Video Language Planning (VLP), a novel algorithm that integrates vision-language models and text-to-video models to generate long-horizon video plans. VLP leverages a tree search procedure where vision-language models serve as both policies and value functions, and text-to-video models function as dynamics models. This approach allows VLP to synthesize long-horizon video plans across different robotics domains, which can be translated into real robot actions via goal-conditioned policies. The method's effectiveness is demonstrated through experiments in both simulated and real robotic environments, showing improvements in task success rates compared to prior methods. However, concerns were raised about the novelty of the approach and the clarity of the method's integration, particularly in the presentation of the algorithm.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel algorithm, Video Language Planning (VLP), which leverages vision-language models and text-to-video models to generate long-horizon video plans, a significant advancement in the field.
- The methodology is well-articulated, with clear explanations of the proposed algorithm and its components, which aids in understanding the approach.
- The paper includes comprehensive experiments that demonstrate the effectiveness of VLP in both simulated and real robotic environments, showcasing its practical applicability.
- The integration of vision-language models and text-to-video models is innovative, providing a new approach to video planning that could inspire further research in the field.
- The paper is well-written, with clear figures and a logical structure that facilitates comprehension of the complex methodologies involved.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed discussion on the limitations of the proposed method, which is crucial for understanding its scope and applicability.
- The novelty of the algorithm is questionable, as it appears to be a straightforward combination of existing models without significant innovation.
- The presentation of the algorithm, particularly in Algorithm 1, is confusing and not well-explained, which could hinder understanding and replication of the method.
- The paper does not adequately address the computational efficiency of the VLP algorithm, which could be a significant concern for real-world applications.
- The paper does not include a thorough discussion on the limitations of the VLP algorithm, which could impact its practical deployment in various scenarios.
- The paper does not provide a detailed comparison with existing methods, particularly in terms of computational efficiency and the ability to handle complex, real-world scenarios.

**Questions:**
- Can you clarify how the video model is trained to predict the next frame given the current frame and the action, as opposed to predicting the entire sequence?
- How does the heuristic function work, and what does it mean to predict the number of steps left until the end of the trajectory snippet?
- In the context of the heuristic function, what is the difference between a video model and a dynamics model, and how does this affect the planning process?
- Could you provide more details on the training data used for the video model, particularly whether it was generated or real, and how this affects the model's performance?
- How does the VLP algorithm handle scenarios where the visual input does not provide sufficient information for planning, such as occluded objects or complex environments?
- Can you discuss the potential societal impacts of VLP, particularly in terms of safety and ethical considerations?
