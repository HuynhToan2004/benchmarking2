 **Summary:**
The paper introduces SPADE, a method for enhancing the interpretability of neural networks by pruning the model to highlight relevant features. SPADE operates by sparsifying the weights of a neural network based on a target sample, aiming to improve the accuracy of saliency maps and neuron visualizations. This method is demonstrated to be effective in various tasks, including image classification and neuron visualization, showing improvements over existing methods like Sparse FC. The approach is validated through experiments on datasets such as ImageNet and involves a user study to assess the effectiveness of the pruned networks in aiding human understanding. However, concerns were raised about the novelty of the approach, as it builds on existing methods like Sparse FC, and the practicality of its application due to the computational overhead and potential limitations in generalizability.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, making it easy to follow, and the experiments are extensive, covering a variety of interpretability methods and tasks.
- The proposed method, SPADE, is straightforward and effective, with a simple yet powerful approach that can be applied to arbitrary models and samples.
- The method demonstrates practicality by being computationally efficient, requiring only 1-41 minutes per instance on a single GPU.
- SPADE is robust to variations in tasks, architectures, and other parameters, and it can be combined with various interpretability methods to produce more accurate saliency maps and neuron visualizations.
- The paper includes a user study to validate the effectiveness of the pruned networks in aiding human understanding, which is a significant contribution to the field.

**Weaknesses:**
- The novelty of the approach is limited as it builds on existing methods like Sparse FC, and the improvements over these methods are not substantial.
- The paper lacks a clear motivation for the need to disentangle feature representations for the model itself, and the computational overhead of the proposed method is significant, potentially limiting its practical application.
- The method's generalizability is questionable, as it requires the availability of calibration samples, which might not be accessible in real-world scenarios.
- The paper could benefit from a more detailed discussion on the limitations and potential negative societal impacts of the proposed method.
- The paper does not provide a thorough discussion on the limitations of the proposed method, which could help in understanding its scope and applicability.

**Questions:**
- Could you clarify the motivation behind the need to disentangle feature representations for the model itself?
- How does the computational overhead of the proposed method compare to other methods, and what are the practical implications of this overhead in terms of real-world applications?
- In terms of generalizability, how do you address the requirement for calibration samples, and what are the implications if these samples are not available?
- Could you elaborate on the method's potential negative societal impacts, and how these might be mitigated or addressed?
- What are the limitations of the proposed method, and how do these impact the practical applicability and effectiveness of SPADE?
