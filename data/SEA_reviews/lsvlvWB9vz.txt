 **Summary:**
The paper introduces EControl, a novel distributed optimization algorithm designed to handle data heterogeneity in distributed training scenarios. EControl is an extension of error compensation (EC) algorithms, employing a contractive compression operator to manage communication overhead. It offers theoretical guarantees of convergence in both strongly convex and general convex settings, with the potential for linear speedup. The algorithm's effectiveness is demonstrated through extensive experiments, showing improvements over existing methods in terms of convergence speed and communication efficiency. However, the paper's contribution is seen as incremental, as it largely builds upon existing EC frameworks with additional hyperparameters.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, making it easy to follow and understand the proposed methodologies.
- The proposed algorithm, EControl, is simple and effective, demonstrating convergence guarantees in both strongly convex and general convex settings.
- EControl offers linear speedup and is practical, as it does not require large batch sizes or repeated communication rounds.
- The paper provides a thorough theoretical analysis, including proofs that match lower bounds for nonconvex functions.
- The algorithm is shown to be effective in experiments, demonstrating improvements over existing methods in terms of convergence speed and communication efficiency.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper primarily focuses on the convergence analysis of EControl, which is seen as incremental and largely based on existing EC frameworks.
- There is a lack of comparison with other recent works in the field, which could have strengthened the paper's position.
- The paper assumes knowledge of h₀i, which is unrealistic in most applications, and the algorithm's convergence is only guaranteed under the convexity condition, which may not be practical in many scenarios.
- The experimental evaluation is limited, with a focus on simple datasets and a lack of comparison with other distributed training algorithms.
- The paper does not adequately address the practical challenges of implementing EControl in federated learning settings, where data heterogeneity and client drift are significant issues.

**Questions:**
- Can you provide a comparison of EControl with other recent works in the field, particularly in terms of convergence guarantees and practical performance?
- How does EControl handle data heterogeneity and client drift in federated learning settings?
- Is there a potential for EControl to achieve linear convergence in strongly convex settings, and how does this compare to the theoretical results presented?
- How is the parameter η chosen in practice, and what are the implications of its choice on the algorithm's performance?
- Could you provide more details on the experimental setup, particularly the choice of hyperparameters and the impact of these choices on the results?
- Is there a possibility to extend the analysis to the nonconvex case, and if so, what are the challenges involved?
