 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel model-based reinforcement learning (MBRL) algorithm named Dynamics Learning and predictive control with Parameterized Actions (DLPA), designed for Parameterized Action Markov Decision Processes (PAMDPs). DLPA learns a parameterized-action-conditioned dynamics model and utilizes a modified Model Predictive Path Integral control for planning. Theoretical analysis and empirical results demonstrate that DLPA achieves superior sample efficiency and asymptotic performance compared to state-of-the-art PAMDP methods. However, the paper's theoretical analysis and empirical results are critiqued for being insufficiently detailed and the method's novelty is questioned due to its similarity to existing model-based RL algorithms.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, clear, and easy to follow, with detailed explanations of the proposed algorithm and its components.
- The empirical results are strong, showing that the proposed algorithm outperforms other PAMDP algorithms in terms of sample efficiency and asymptotic performance.
- The paper introduces a novel method for learning a parameterized-action-conditioned dynamics model, which is a significant contribution to the field of model-based reinforcement learning (MBRL).
- The paper includes a theoretical analysis that quantifies the difference between the generated trajectory and the optimal trajectory during planning, which is a valuable contribution to the understanding of the algorithm's performance.
- The paper's methodology includes a novel approach to planning with parameterized actions, which is a significant contribution to the field.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper's theoretical analysis is insufficiently detailed, particularly in explaining the proofs and assumptions used.
- The novelty of the algorithm is questionable as it appears to be a straightforward combination of existing model-based RL algorithms.
- The empirical results are limited to a small number of environments, which may not generalize well to other, more complex environments.
- The paper lacks a detailed discussion on the limitations of the proposed method and does not compare the proposed method with a broader range of baselines, including recent state-of-the-art methods.
- The paper does not sufficiently address the computational complexity of the proposed method, which could be a significant limitation in practical applications.
- The paper does not provide a detailed comparison with other model-based RL algorithms, which could have provided a more robust evaluation of the proposed method.

**Questions:**
- Could you clarify the proofs and assumptions used in your theoretical analysis, particularly in Theorem 5.2 and Lemma 5.3?
- How does the proposed method compare to other model-based RL algorithms in terms of computational efficiency and sample efficiency in more complex environments?
- Could you provide a detailed comparison of the proposed method with other state-of-the-art methods, including recent advances in model-based RL?
- What are the specific advantages of the proposed method over existing model-based RL algorithms, particularly in terms of sample efficiency and asymptotic performance?
- Could you discuss the limitations of the proposed method and suggest potential areas for future research?
