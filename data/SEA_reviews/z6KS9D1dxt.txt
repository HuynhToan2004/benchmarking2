 **Summary:**
The paper explores robustness in cooperative multi-agent reinforcement learning (c-MARL) against Byzantine failures, where agents may enact arbitrary, worst-case actions. The authors propose a Bayesian Adversarial Robust Dec-POMDP (BARDec-POMDP) framework, viewing Byzantine adversaries as nature-dictated types, to enable agents to learn policies based on their posterior beliefs about other agents. The paper introduces a two-timescale actor-critic algorithm that ensures almost sure convergence under specific conditions. The method is tested across three environments, demonstrating its effectiveness against various adversarial threats. Despite its theoretical contributions and experimental validations, the paper is critiqued for its lack of comprehensive comparisons with existing works, the need for clearer presentation of theoretical results, and the absence of a detailed discussion on the limitations and assumptions of the proposed method.
