 **Summary:**
The paper presents a novel method for reconstructing training data from neural networks, particularly focusing on the Neural Tangent Kernel (NTK) regime. The proposed attack leverages the KKT conditions to reconstruct the entire training set, providing a theoretical framework that extends beyond the binary classification setting of previous works. The paper also explores the impact of outlier data points and the role of dataset distillation in improving model performance. It offers a comprehensive analysis of the attack's effectiveness under various conditions, including finite and infinite width networks, and demonstrates its applicability across different datasets and architectures.

**Strengths:**
- The paper provides a thorough analysis of the attack's effectiveness under various conditions, including finite and infinite width networks, and demonstrates its applicability across different datasets and architectures.
- The proposed attack is more general and applicable to a wider range of settings, including binary and multi-classification tasks, and can be applied to fine-tuned models.
- The paper is well-written, clear, and provides a comprehensive analysis of the attack's effectiveness, including its connection to dataset distillation.
- The theoretical framework is robust, supported by both theoretical and empirical evidence, which strengthens the credibility of the proposed attack.
- The experimental results are compelling, showing the effectiveness of the proposed attack in reconstructing training data, which is a significant contribution to the field.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper primarily focuses on MLPs, which may not fully capture the behavior of convolutional networks, which are more commonly used in practice.
- The paper does not adequately address the limitations of the proposed attack, such as its applicability to other types of neural networks and its effectiveness in different training scenarios.
- The practical implications of the attack, particularly its impact on model robustness and privacy, are not thoroughly explored.
- The paper could benefit from a more detailed discussion on the limitations of the proposed attack, such as its applicability to other types of neural networks and its effectiveness in different training scenarios.
- The paper lacks a comprehensive comparison with existing attacks, which could help establish the novelty and effectiveness of the proposed method.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors clarify the definition of $\\Delta \\theta$ in Equation 3 and explain how it relates to the training dynamics of neural networks?
- How does the proposed attack perform when applied to different types of neural networks, such as convolutional networks, and what are the implications for its effectiveness?
- Could the authors provide more details on the practical implications of the attack, particularly in terms of model robustness and privacy?
- How does the proposed attack compare to existing attacks in terms of effectiveness and novelty?
- In light of the limitations discussed, how might the proposed attack be adapted or improved to address these challenges and enhance its applicability and effectiveness?
