 **Summary:**
The paper introduces Quality Diversity through Human Feedback (QDHF), a novel approach that integrates human feedback into the Quality Diversity (QD) framework to enhance the diversity of solutions in reinforcement learning and robotics. QDHF leverages human judgments to dynamically infer diversity metrics, thereby improving the applicability and effectiveness of QD algorithms. The paper provides empirical evidence that QDHF outperforms state-of-the-art methods in automatic diversity discovery and matches the efficacy of manually crafted metrics on standard QD benchmarks. The approach is demonstrated to be effective in tasks such as robotics, reinforcement learning, and image generation, although concerns about the scalability and generalizability of the method to more complex, open-ended tasks remain.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, with a clear and concise structure that effectively communicates the key concepts and methodologies.
- The introduction of QDHF as a novel approach that integrates human feedback into the QD framework is innovative and addresses a significant gap in the field.
- The paper provides a comprehensive empirical evaluation of QDHF, demonstrating its effectiveness in various tasks such as robotics, reinforcement learning, and image generation.
- The use of human feedback to derive diversity metrics is a significant departure from traditional methods, offering a more intuitive and practical approach to measuring diversity.
- The paper includes a thorough analysis of the scalability and quality of the derived diversity metrics, which is crucial for understanding the robustness and applicability of the QDHF approach.
- The inclusion of user studies to assess the diversity of generated images is a valuable addition, providing insights into the perceived quality and diversity of the solutions.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed discussion on the limitations and potential negative societal impacts of the proposed method, which could be a significant oversight.
- There is a concern about the scalability of QDHF to more complex, open-ended tasks, especially in terms of the amount of human feedback required and the generalizability of the approach.
- The paper does not adequately address how the QDHF approach might be adapted or extended to other domains or tasks, limiting its applicability and impact.
- The methodology section could benefit from more detailed explanations and examples, particularly in the implementation of the QDHF algorithm and the use of human feedback.
- The paper does not sufficiently discuss the ethical considerations related to the use of human feedback, which is a critical aspect of the proposed method.
- The paper does not provide a comprehensive comparison with other existing methods for integrating human feedback into QD algorithms, which could help to establish the novelty and effectiveness of the QDHF approach.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors provide more details on the limitations and potential negative societal impacts of the QDHF approach?
- How does the QDHF approach compare to other methods for integrating human feedback into QD algorithms?
- Can the authors discuss the potential ethical considerations related to the use of human feedback in the QDHF approach?
- How might the QDHF approach be adapted or extended to other domains or tasks?
- Could the authors provide more detailed examples or case studies to illustrate the implementation of the QDHF algorithm and the use of human feedback?
- How does the QDHF approach handle the issue of mode collapse in generative models, particularly in the context of image generation?
- Can the authors discuss the potential for integrating QDHF with other reinforcement learning techniques to enhance the diversity of solutions in more complex tasks?
