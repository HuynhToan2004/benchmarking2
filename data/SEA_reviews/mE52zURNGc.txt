 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel approach to feature metric image alignment by deriving a closed-form solution for the Gauss-Newton loss, which is typically used to enhance the convergence basin in direct image alignment. This solution allows for the use of self-supervised feature descriptors, which can be dynamically adjusted to control the convergence properties of the algorithm. The method is evaluated against state-of-the-art direct image alignment methods and shows improved robustness to pose initialization. The authors argue that their approach provides a more flexible and intuitive approach compared to traditional end-to-end learning methods.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel approach to feature metric image alignment by deriving a closed-form solution for the Gauss-Newton loss, which is a significant contribution to the field.
- The use of self-supervised feature descriptors in the Gauss-Newton loss allows for more flexible and intuitive control over the convergence properties of the algorithm, enhancing the robustness of the alignment to pose initialization.
- The paper is well-written, with clear explanations of the proposed method and its implementation, and provides a comprehensive evaluation of the proposed method against state-of-the-art direct image alignment methods.
- The closed-form solution for the Gauss-Newton loss is a significant contribution to the field, providing a more flexible and intuitive approach compared to traditional end-to-end learning methods.
- The paper's approach to using self-supervised feature descriptors in the Gauss-Newton loss is innovative and has the potential to improve the robustness of image alignment to pose initialization.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a thorough discussion on the limitations of the proposed method, particularly in terms of its applicability to different types of feature descriptors and its scalability to larger datasets.
- The experimental results presented in the paper are not convincing, with some results showing inferior performance compared to existing methods like PixLoc, and a lack of results on the 7Scenes dataset.
- The paper does not adequately address the potential negative societal impact of the proposed method, which could be significant given its potential applications in areas like autonomous vehicles or surveillance systems.
- The paper's reliance on self-supervised feature descriptors may limit its applicability to more complex scenarios where supervised feature descriptors are necessary.
- The paper's experimental setup and results are not clearly explained, making it difficult to assess the effectiveness and reliability of the proposed method.
- The paper does not provide sufficient details on the computational complexity of the proposed method, which is crucial for understanding its practical applicability.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors clarify the specific advantages of using the closed-form solution for the Gauss-Newton loss over traditional end-to-end learning methods?
- How does the proposed method compare to other feature-matching approaches, particularly in terms of computational efficiency and accuracy?
- What are the specific limitations of the proposed method, and under what conditions does it not perform well?
- How does the proposed method handle outliers in the feature matching process, and what steps are taken to ensure the accuracy of the matches?
- Could the authors provide more detailed results on the 7Scenes dataset to better evaluate the effectiveness of the proposed method in more complex scenarios?
- How does the computational complexity of the proposed method compare to existing methods, and what steps have been taken to optimize its performance?
- Can the authors provide a more detailed explanation of the experimental setup and results, including the specific conditions under which the experiments were conducted and the metrics used to evaluate the performance of the proposed method?
