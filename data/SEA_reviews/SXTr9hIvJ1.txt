 **Summary:**
The paper investigates the weighted low rank approximation (WLRA) problem, focusing on the case where the weight matrix has low rank. The authors propose a novel approach by reweighting a low rank solution to provide an approximate solution with a theoretical guarantee. This method is applied to both model compression and communication complexity, showing potential in these areas. The paper presents theoretical results alongside empirical evidence, demonstrating the practical applicability of the proposed methods. However, the paper has been critiqued for its limited novelty, lack of comparison with state-of-the-art methods, and insufficient theoretical backing for certain claims.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written and easy to follow, with clear explanations of the proposed methods and their theoretical underpinnings.
- The problem addressed is significant and relevant, with potential applications in various fields including machine learning and signal processing.
- The paper provides both theoretical and empirical evidence to support the proposed methods, enhancing the credibility of the results.
- The proposed algorithm is simple and straightforward, which could make it appealing for practical implementation.
- The paper addresses the weighted low rank approximation (WLRA) problem, which is a significant issue in machine learning and has been studied extensively, highlighting the importance of the problem.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a thorough comparison with existing methods, particularly in terms of computational complexity and theoretical guarantees.
- The assumption that the weight matrix is low rank is not convincingly justified, and the paper does not adequately discuss the practical implications of this assumption.
- The theoretical results, such as Theorem 1.2, are not well-explained or intuitive, which could hinder understanding and application of the results.
- The paper does not provide a detailed discussion on the practical implications of the low rank assumption, which is crucial for the applicability of the proposed methods.
- There are several typographical and formatting errors throughout the paper, which could detract from its professional presentation.
- The paper does not adequately discuss the limitations of its approach, which is essential for understanding the scope and applicability of the proposed methods.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors clarify the assumptions regarding the rank of the weight matrix and discuss the practical implications of these assumptions?
- How does the proposed method compare in terms of computational complexity and theoretical guarantees with existing methods, particularly those that also address the WLRA problem?
- Can the authors provide a more detailed explanation of the theoretical results, particularly Theorem 1.2, to enhance understanding and application of the results?
- Is there a possibility to extend the proposed method to handle cases where the weight matrix is not low rank?
- Could the authors address the typographical and formatting errors in the paper to improve its professional presentation?
