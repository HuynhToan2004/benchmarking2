 **Summary:**
The paper introduces FENDA-FL, a novel federated learning (FL) method inspired by the FENDA algorithm for domain adaptation. FENDA-FL extends the FENDA method to the FL setting by integrating local and global feature extractors, allowing for personalized training across clients. The method is evaluated on both FLamby and GEMINI datasets, demonstrating improved performance over existing FL techniques. The paper also proposes a federated checkpointing and evaluation framework to enhance the robustness and generalization of FL models in clinical settings. Despite its novel approach and comprehensive evaluation, the paper is criticized for its limited novelty and insufficient comparison with state-of-the-art methods.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel application of the FENDA method to federated learning (FL), which is a significant advancement in the field.
- The authors have conducted comprehensive experiments across various datasets, demonstrating the effectiveness of their proposed method, FENDA-FL.
- The paper is well-written, clear, and easy to follow, making it accessible to a broad audience.
- The proposed method, FENDA-FL, shows promising results in both FLamby and GEMINI datasets, indicating its potential for practical applications.
- The paper includes a detailed discussion on the methodology and experimental setups, providing valuable insights into the application of FL in clinical settings.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a thorough discussion on the limitations of the FENDA-FL method, which could help in understanding its applicability and effectiveness in different scenarios.
- There is a noticeable absence of comparison with state-of-the-art methods in the field, which could undermine the perceived novelty and effectiveness of the proposed method.
- The paper does not adequately address the scalability of the proposed method in larger, more complex datasets or scenarios, which could limit its practical applicability.
- The experimental results, while promising, are not convincingly superior to existing methods, especially in terms of statistical significance and generalizability across different datasets.
- The paper could benefit from a more rigorous discussion on the potential negative societal impacts of federated learning in healthcare applications.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors provide more details on the scalability of the FENDA-FL method in larger, more complex datasets or scenarios?
- How does the FENDA-FL method compare with other state-of-the-art methods in terms of performance and efficiency?
- What are the specific advantages of using FENDA-FL over other personalized FL methods, especially in clinical applications?
- Could the authors elaborate on the potential negative societal impacts of federated learning in healthcare applications and how these are addressed by the FENDA-FL method?
- How does the FENDA-FL method handle data heterogeneity across different clients, and what measures are taken to ensure data privacy and security?
- Can the authors provide more details on the experimental setup, particularly the hyperparameter tuning and validation splits used in the experiments?
