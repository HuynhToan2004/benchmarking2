 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel approach to addressing the Janus problem in text-to-3D diffusion models using a negative prompt algorithm named Perp-Neg. This method leverages the geometrical properties of score space to ensure that negative prompts do not affect the main concept of the image, thus enhancing the model's ability to generate diverse and accurate views. The approach is validated through experiments on both 2D and 3D datasets, demonstrating its effectiveness in improving the model's performance in handling negative prompts and view interpolation. Despite its innovative approach, the paper faces criticisms regarding its clarity in explaining the technical details, the lack of comprehensive experimental validation, and the need for more rigorous comparisons with existing methods.

**Strengths:**
- The paper addresses a significant and under-explored issue in text-to-3D diffusion models, specifically the Janus problem, which is a critical challenge in the field.
- The proposed method, Perp-Neg, is novel and well-motivated, providing a creative solution to the problem of negative prompts in text-to-image diffusion models.
- The paper is well-written, making it accessible and easy to follow, with clear explanations of the methodology and experimental results.
- The experimental results show that the proposed method outperforms existing methods like CEBM, indicating its effectiveness in handling negative prompts.
- The integration of Perp-Neg with DreamFusion demonstrates its potential to address the Janus problem, a significant limitation in current 3D diffusion models.
- The paper includes a comprehensive set of experiments, demonstrating the method's effectiveness in both 2D and 3D scenarios.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks clarity in explaining the technical details, particularly in Section 2.2.3, which could benefit from a clearer and more concise explanation.
- The experiments are not comprehensive enough, with limited comparisons to other methods and a lack of quantitative metrics to support the claims.
- The paper could benefit from a more thorough discussion on the limitations of the proposed method and its potential negative societal impacts.
- The integration of Perp-Neg with DreamFusion is not thoroughly explained, and it is unclear how it specifically addresses the Janus problem.
- The paper does not provide sufficient evidence to support the claim that the Janus problem is caused by the failure of 2D diffusion models to follow view instructions.
- The methodology and experimental setup could be more detailed, including a clearer explanation of the weighting of negative prompts and the selection criteria for successful generations.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors clarify how Perp-Neg specifically addresses the Janus problem in 3D diffusion models?
- How does the method perform when using more complex prompts, and what are the implications for the model's ability to handle negative prompts effectively?
- Can the authors provide more details on the selection criteria for successful generations and the weighting of negative prompts in the experiments?
- How does the proposed method compare to other existing methods for handling negative prompts in text-to-image diffusion models?
- Could the authors provide more evidence to support the claim that the Janus problem is caused by the failure of 2D diffusion models to follow view instructions?
- How does the method perform when applied to other 3D diffusion models, and what are the implications for the model's performance in different scenarios?
