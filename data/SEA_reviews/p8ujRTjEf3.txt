 **Summary:**
The paper investigates the multi-armed bandit problem with Gaussian rewards and unknown heterogeneous reward variances. It introduces a Thompson sampling algorithm, VarTS, designed to handle unknown variances by utilizing a Gaussian-Gamma posterior for mean and precision of rewards. The authors derive a Bayes regret bound for VarTS, showing that it scales with the sum of variances and the log of the number of rounds. The paper also discusses the impact of prior knowledge on reward variances and compares the algorithm with existing methods, showing its superiority in some scenarios. However, the paper is criticized for its lack of novelty and the unclear presentation of some mathematical proofs and algorithmic details.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, clear, and easy to follow, with a clear motivation and problem setup.
- The algorithm design and analysis are novel, particularly in handling unknown reward variances, which is a significant challenge in bandit problems.
- The paper provides a comprehensive comparison with existing works, highlighting the advantages of the proposed algorithm.
- The experimental results demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed algorithm in practical scenarios.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed discussion on the novelty of the algorithm and analysis compared to existing works, particularly in the context of variance-adaptive Thompson sampling.
- There are several typographical and notation errors throughout the paper, which could confuse readers.
- The paper does not adequately address the issue of misspecification of the Gaussian-Gamma prior, which is a critical assumption in the analysis.
- The presentation of the mathematical proofs and some algorithmic details is not clear, particularly in Section 5.2 and the proofs of Theorems 1 and 2.
- The paper does not sufficiently discuss the limitations and potential negative societal impacts of the proposed method.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors clarify the novelty of their algorithm and analysis compared to existing works, particularly in the context of variance-adaptive Thompson sampling?
- How does the algorithm handle the misspecification of the Gaussian-Gamma prior, and what are the implications of this misspecification on the performance of the algorithm?
- Could the authors provide more detailed explanations or examples for the mathematical proofs and algorithmic details that are currently unclear?
- Is there a plan to extend the analysis to more general classes of reward distributions beyond Gaussian?
- How does the proposed algorithm compare in terms of computational efficiency and practical implementation to existing methods?
