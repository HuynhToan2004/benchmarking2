 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel method, LDINet, for the deblurring and matting of fast-moving objects (FMOs) in a single image. It leverages a decomposition-interpolation module to disentangle latent feature maps into discrete parts corresponding to different blur durations. The method uses a bi-branch decoder to generate sharp appearances and masks for the object at different time indices. The approach is validated through extensive experiments, showing superior performance compared to existing methods on benchmark datasets. Despite its strengths in methodological innovation and empirical results, the paper is criticized for its lack of clarity in some sections and the absence of certain comparisons and ablation studies.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, with clear motivation and detailed explanations of the proposed method, making it easy to follow.
- The method is innovative, particularly in its approach to disentangling latent feature maps into discrete parts, which is a novel idea in the field of image deblurring.
- Extensive experiments are conducted, demonstrating the effectiveness of the proposed method, and the results show significant improvements over existing methods.
- The paper includes a thorough ablation study, which helps in understanding the impact of various components of the method.
- The use of a bi-branch decoder to generate sharp appearances and masks for the object at different time indices is a novel and effective approach.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks clarity in certain sections, particularly in the explanation of the AffNet module and the training loss section.
- The paper does not include comparisons with some relevant methods, such as SfB and Bit++, which could have strengthened the evaluation of the proposed method.
- There are missing ablation studies, particularly concerning the number of latent parts and the effect of the disentangled latent parts.
- The paper's reliance on the DeFMO dataset for training raises concerns about the generalizability of the method to other datasets.
- The paper does not adequately address the computational complexity of the method, which could be a significant limitation in practical applications.
- The paper does not discuss the limitations of the proposed method, which could help in understanding its scope and applicability.

**Questions:**
- Could you clarify the role and functionality of the AffNet module in the context of the proposed method?
- How does the method handle the computational complexity, particularly in terms of the number of latent parts and the disentangled latent parts?
- Could you provide more details on the training process, particularly how the number of latent parts is determined and how it affects the training process?
- How does the method perform when applied to other datasets beyond those used in the experiments?
- Could you elaborate on the decision to not include certain comparisons and ablation studies, and how these omissions affect the evaluation of the proposed method?
