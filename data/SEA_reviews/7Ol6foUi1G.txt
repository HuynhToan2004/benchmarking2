 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel pruning method for hierarchical Vision Transformers (ViTs), focusing on Swin Transformers. The proposed method, DIMAP, treats attention weights as modules and assigns importance based on Frobenius distortion, which is data-independent. This approach aims to address the challenges of pruning local attention weights and the limitations of magnitude-based pruning. Extensive experiments on ImageNet-1K demonstrate that DIMAP can achieve significant computational savings (up to 52.5%) while maintaining or even improving accuracy. The paper also provides a comprehensive analysis of the importance of different modules and the effectiveness of the pruning strategy.

**Strengths:**
- The paper addresses an important and timely problem of pruning Vision Transformers (ViTs) and provides a novel method for pruning local attention weights, which is crucial for hierarchical ViTs.
- The proposed method is data-independent and based on Frobenius distortion, which is a novel approach that effectively prunes local attention weights and avoids the limitations of magnitude-based pruning.
- The paper is well-written and easy to follow, with a clear and logical presentation of the methodology and results.
- Extensive experiments on ImageNet-1K demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed method, showing significant computational savings and improved accuracy.
- The paper provides a comprehensive analysis of the importance of different modules in Swin Transformers, which is valuable for understanding the impact of pruning on different components of the model.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks comparisons with other pruning methods for ViTs, which could provide a more robust evaluation of the proposed method's effectiveness.
- The paper does not discuss the limitations of the proposed method, which could help in understanding its applicability and potential drawbacks.
- The paper does not provide a detailed analysis of the computational complexity of the proposed method, which could be crucial for practical deployment in resource-constrained environments.
- The paper does not include results on downstream tasks such as object detection, which could demonstrate the generalizability of the proposed method across different vision tasks.
- The paper does not provide a detailed comparison with other compression methods for ViTs, which could help in understanding the trade-offs between different pruning strategies.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors provide a comparison with other pruning methods for ViTs to demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed method?
- How does the proposed method perform on other vision tasks such as object detection?
- Can the authors provide a detailed analysis of the computational complexity of the proposed method?
- How does the proposed method compare with other compression methods for ViTs in terms of computational savings and accuracy?
- Could the authors discuss the limitations of the proposed method and suggest potential directions for future research?
