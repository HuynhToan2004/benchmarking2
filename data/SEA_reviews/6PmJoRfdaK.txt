 **Summary:**
The paper introduces LongLoRA, a novel method for fine-tuning large language models (LLMs) with extended context lengths, aiming to reduce computational costs and improve efficiency. LongLoRA employs a shifted sparse attention mechanism, S2-Attn, which splits context lengths into groups and computes attention in each group individually. This approach allows for efficient training and inference without significant accuracy loss. The method is evaluated on various datasets, including LongChat, and shows competitive performance with existing methods. The paper also explores the integration of LoRA for context extension and demonstrates that it can be compatible with other techniques like Flash-Attention2.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel approach, LongLoRA, which extends the context length of pre-trained LLMs with limited computational costs, addressing the challenges of training long-context LLMs.
- The proposed S2-Attn is simple yet effective, requiring only two lines of code, and it can be implemented with minimal additional trainable parameters.
- The methodology is well-explained, making it easy to understand and replicate, and the experiments are comprehensive, demonstrating the effectiveness of the proposed method.
- The paper is well-written, with clear and concise descriptions, and the experimental results are promising, showing that LongLoRA can achieve comparable performance to full attention or full fine-tuning while reducing training costs.
- The integration of LoRA for context extension is a significant contribution, as it can improve the performance of LLMs without requiring extensive computational resources.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed comparison with other methods, particularly in terms of computational efficiency and memory usage, which could undermine the claims of superior efficiency.
- The evaluation is limited to specific datasets, which might not fully demonstrate the generalizability of the proposed method.
- The paper does not sufficiently address the issue of information leakage due to token shifting in the S2-Attn mechanism, which could affect the fairness of the evaluation.
- The methodology section is not well-organized, making it difficult to follow the logic behind the proposed methods.
- There is a lack of discussion on the limitations and potential negative societal impacts of the proposed method.
- The paper could benefit from a more thorough analysis of the training cost and the impact of different group sizes on the performance.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors provide a more detailed comparison with other methods in terms of computational efficiency and memory usage?
- How does the proposed method compare in terms of training time and memory requirements with other methods that extend context lengths in LLMs?
- Can the authors provide more details on the information leakage issue due to token shifting in the S2-Attn mechanism and discuss potential mitigation strategies?
- What are the specific reasons for the observed performance degradation in small context sizes for the extended models?
- Could the authors provide a more detailed explanation of the methodology behind the S2-Attn and the rationale behind the group size selection?
- How does the proposed method perform when applied to other LLMs, such as those based on different architectures or pre-trained on different datasets?
- Could the authors discuss the potential negative societal impacts of their method and propose strategies to mitigate these impacts?
