 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel approach to score distillation for NeRF optimization, specifically addressing the noise-induced artifacts in 3D generation. By decomposing the score into three components—condition, domain, and denoising—the authors propose a new Noise-Free Score Distillation (NFSD) method. This approach aims to improve the quality of 3D generation by eliminating noise distillation and optimizing the Classifier-Free Guidance (CFG) scale. The paper also discusses the theoretical underpinnings of the SDS process and presents experimental results demonstrating the effectiveness of NFSD. However, concerns were raised about the clarity of the method's novelty and the lack of comprehensive experimental validation, particularly in terms of comparative analysis with existing methods and the generalizability of the findings.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, easy to follow, and provides a clear explanation of the proposed method.
- The proposed method is simple yet effective, with a straightforward approach that is easy to implement.
- The idea of decomposing the score into three components (alignment with the condition, domain correction, and denoising) is intuitive and well-motivated.
- The paper provides a detailed theoretical explanation of the SDS process, which is both novel and insightful.
- The experimental results demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed method, particularly in improving the quality of 3D generation.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed discussion on the limitations of the proposed method, particularly concerning the generalizability of the findings and the potential negative societal impacts.
- The experimental validation is insufficient, with a lack of comparative analysis with existing methods and a limited number of examples in the main paper.
- The methodology section is not clearly presented, making it difficult to understand the novelty and contribution of the proposed method.
- The paper does not adequately address the computational efficiency of the proposed method, which is crucial for practical applications.
- The paper does not include a discussion on the potential limitations or challenges in applying the proposed method to other domains or models.

**Questions:**
- How does the proposed method perform when applied to other domains or models, and what are the potential challenges or limitations in such applications?
- Can the authors provide a more detailed comparison with existing methods, particularly in terms of computational efficiency and the effectiveness of the proposed method in different scenarios?
- How does the proposed method address the issues of diversity and Janus problem in 3D generation, which are known challenges in the field?
- Could the authors elaborate on the theoretical contributions of the paper, particularly in relation to the novel decomposition of the score function and its implications for the field?
- How does the proposed method compare to other state-of-the-art methods in terms of practical performance and computational efficiency?
