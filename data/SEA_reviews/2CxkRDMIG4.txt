 **Summary:**
The paper introduces new evaluation metrics for classifiers with a reject option, focusing on precision and recall reject curves (PRC and RRC) instead of the traditional accuracy reject curve (ARC). These new metrics are aimed at enhancing the evaluation of classifiers in scenarios where precision and recall are more relevant, such as imbalanced datasets. The authors demonstrate the utility of these new metrics through experiments on both synthetic and real-world datasets, showing that PRC and RRC can provide more insightful results compared to ARC. However, the paper is criticized for its limited scope in terms of the datasets used and the lack of broader theoretical justification for the proposed metrics.

**Strengths:**
- The paper addresses an important and relevant problem in machine learning by focusing on the evaluation of classifiers with a reject option, particularly in scenarios where precision and recall are more crucial than accuracy.
- The proposed precision and recall reject curves are novel and provide a fresh perspective on evaluating classifiers, which is particularly useful in imbalanced datasets.
- The paper is well-written, making it easy to follow, and the experimental results are convincing, demonstrating the practical utility of the proposed metrics.
- The introduction of precision and recall reject curves is a significant contribution to the field, offering a new tool for evaluating classifiers in specific scenarios.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a thorough theoretical justification for the proposed metrics, which could have strengthened the argument for their utility.
- The experimental evaluation is limited in scope, focusing primarily on prototype-based classifiers and a few datasets, which may not generalize well to other types of classifiers or more diverse datasets.
- The paper does not adequately address the limitations of the proposed metrics, such as their applicability to different types of classifiers or their effectiveness in scenarios where precision and recall are not the primary concerns.
- The paper does not provide a comprehensive comparison with other existing metrics, which could have helped in better positioning the proposed metrics within the broader landscape of classifier evaluation.
- The paper could benefit from a more rigorous evaluation, including a broader range of datasets and classifiers, to demonstrate the robustness and generalizability of the proposed metrics.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors elaborate on the theoretical underpinnings of the proposed precision and recall reject curves? What specific properties or advantages do these metrics offer over other metrics, such as accuracy?
- How do the proposed metrics perform in scenarios where precision and recall are not the primary concerns, such as in datasets with balanced classes or where other metrics like F1 score are more relevant?
- Can the authors provide more details on how the certainty measures (rConf and rRelSim) are calculated and how they relate to the overall performance of the classifiers?
- In the context of prototype-based classifiers, how do the proposed metrics compare to other evaluation metrics that are commonly used in this domain?
- The paper mentions the potential application of the proposed metrics to other types of classifiers. Could the authors provide examples or scenarios where these metrics could be particularly useful or challenging in different types of classifiers?
