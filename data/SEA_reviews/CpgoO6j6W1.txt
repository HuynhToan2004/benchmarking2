 **Summary:**
The paper introduces ReWOO, a novel framework for tool-augmented language models (TALMs) aimed at reducing token consumption by separating the reasoning process from external observations. This modular approach involves a Planner, Worker, and Solver, each responsible for different aspects of the reasoning process. The Planner generates a blueprint of interdependent plans, the Worker retrieves evidence from external tools, and the Solver combines these to generate the final answer. The framework is evaluated across six public NLP benchmarks and a curated dataset, demonstrating a 5x reduction in token usage and a 4% improvement in accuracy on HotpotQA. The paper also explores the offloading of reasoning to smaller models, showing that a 7B model can replace a 175B model, which is a significant contribution to the field.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, making it easy to follow and understand.
- The proposed method is simple and effective, with a clear motivation for reducing token consumption in tool-augmented language models (TALMs).
- The paper introduces a novel approach by separating the reasoning process from external observations, which is a significant advancement in the field.
- The experimental results are strong, demonstrating a 5x reduction in token usage and a 4% improvement in accuracy on HotpotQA.
- The paper provides a detailed analysis of the framework's performance and includes a comprehensive evaluation across six public NLP benchmarks and a curated dataset.
- The method's ability to offload reasoning to smaller models is highlighted, showcasing its potential for scalability and efficiency.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed discussion on the limitations of the proposed method, which could provide a more balanced view of its applicability and effectiveness.
- The evaluation could be more comprehensive, including a broader range of tasks and benchmarks to better assess the method's generalizability and effectiveness.
- The paper does not sufficiently compare the proposed method with other existing methods, such as CoT and ReAct, which could provide a clearer understanding of its advantages and disadvantages.
- The experimental setup could be improved, particularly in terms of the selection and number of exemplars used, which could affect the fairness and validity of the results.
- The paper could benefit from a more detailed analysis of the computational cost and efficiency of the proposed method, especially in comparison to existing methods.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors provide more details on the limitations of the proposed method and how these might affect its practical application?
- How does the proposed method compare with other existing methods, such as CoT and ReAct, in terms of efficiency and effectiveness?
- Can the authors clarify the selection and number of exemplars used in the experiments, and discuss how this might affect the results?
- How does the proposed method handle scenarios where the Planner generates incorrect or incomplete plans, and what are the implications for the overall performance of the system?
- Could the authors provide more detailed information on the computational cost and efficiency of the proposed method, and how does this compare to existing methods?
