 **Summary:**
The paper introduces Dual-Balancing Multi-Task Learning (DB-MTL), a method designed to address the task balancing problem in multi-task learning by employing logarithmic transformation and gradient normalization. The method aims to ensure that all task losses have the same scale and all task gradients have the same magnitude, thereby improving performance across various datasets. The paper presents extensive experiments on several benchmark datasets, showing that DB-MTL achieves state-of-the-art performance. Despite its straightforward approach, the method is not without its limitations, as highlighted by concerns regarding its novelty and the depth of its theoretical analysis.

**Strengths:**
- The proposed method is simple and easy to implement, making it accessible for practical applications.
- The paper presents a comprehensive empirical evaluation, demonstrating the effectiveness of the proposed method across multiple datasets and tasks.
- The method's design is straightforward, which aids in its understanding and implementation.
- The paper is well-written, making it easy to follow and understand.
- The authors have conducted extensive experiments, demonstrating the effectiveness of the proposed method.
- The paper introduces a novel approach to addressing the task balancing problem in multi-task learning, which is a significant challenge in the field.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a clear motivation for the use of logarithmic transformation in the loss function, which is a critical component of the proposed method.
- The novelty of the proposed method is limited as it primarily combines existing techniques without significant innovation.
- The paper does not provide a detailed theoretical analysis of the proposed method, which could enhance its credibility and applicability.
- The experimental results, while extensive, do not convincingly demonstrate the superiority of the proposed method over existing baselines, particularly in terms of statistical significance.
- The paper does not adequately address the issue of gradient conflicts, which is a significant challenge in multi-task learning.
- The paper's contribution is seen as incremental rather than groundbreaking, as it primarily combines existing techniques without significant innovation.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors provide a detailed explanation of why the logarithmic transformation is effective in addressing the task balancing problem?
- How does the proposed method handle gradient conflicts, which are known to be a significant challenge in multi-task learning?
- Could the authors provide more detailed theoretical analysis to support the proposed method?
- In the experiments, how does the proposed method perform when compared to other state-of-the-art methods, particularly in terms of statistical significance?
- The paper mentions the use of logarithmic transformation to ensure all task losses have the same scale. Could the authors clarify how this transformation affects the optimization process and the final performance?
- How does the proposed method compare to other existing methods that also use logarithmic transformation or similar techniques?
- Could the authors provide more detailed implementation details, such as the learning rates and regularization terms used in the experiments?
