 **Summary:**
The paper introduces Differentially-Private Offsite Prompt Tuning (DP-OPT), a novel approach for generating private prompts to enhance the privacy of large language models (LLMs) without compromising their performance. The method leverages a differentially-private ensemble of in-context learning with private demonstrations to ensure that the generated prompts do not reveal private information. This approach allows for private prompt tuning on the client side, which can then be applied to desired cloud models, offering a balance between data confidentiality, information privacy, and cloud model ownership. The paper presents empirical results demonstrating the effectiveness of the DP-OPT framework across various language tasks, showcasing its ability to transfer prompts across models with favorable performance.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, clear, and easy to follow, with a clear introduction of the problem and its motivation.
- The proposed method is novel, well-motivated, and addresses a significant issue in the field of large language models (LLMs) by introducing DP-OPT, a framework that ensures data confidentiality, information privacy, and cloud model ownership.
- The paper presents a comprehensive analysis of the proposed method, including its effectiveness in transferring prompts across models and its ability to mitigate privacy risks associated with prompt tuning.
- The experimental results are convincing, demonstrating the effectiveness of the proposed method across multiple language tasks.
- The paper addresses a timely and significant issue in the field of LLMs, focusing on privacy risks and providing a practical solution through DP-OPT.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed discussion on the privacy costs of the proposed method, which is crucial for understanding the trade-offs involved.
- There is a lack of clarity in the experimental setup, particularly the definition and role of the "target model" and the "source model," which could affect the reproducibility and understanding of the results.
- The paper does not adequately discuss the limitations of the proposed method, which is essential for a comprehensive understanding of its applicability and potential drawbacks.
- The paper could benefit from more comprehensive evaluations, including comparisons with existing methods and a broader range of datasets to validate the generalizability of the findings.
- The paper could be improved by providing more detailed explanations of the algorithms and methodologies used, particularly in the appendix to enhance the accessibility and understanding of the content.
