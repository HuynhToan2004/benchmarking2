 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel method for learning rotationally equivariant representations in self-supervised learning, focusing on contrastive learning. The approach aims to learn a mapping from the input space to the embedding space that respects rotational symmetry, which is a significant improvement over traditional methods that learn invariance to data augmentations. The method is supported by both theoretical analysis and empirical results, showing improved performance on various datasets such as CIFAR and ImageNet. The authors propose a contrastive learning objective that encourages the network to learn a linear mapping from the input space to the embedding space, which is equivariant to rotations. This method is compared against existing methods like SimCLR and EquiMod, showing significant improvements in certain scenarios.

- The paper is well-written, with clear explanations of the theoretical results and the empirical findings, making it accessible to a broad audience.
