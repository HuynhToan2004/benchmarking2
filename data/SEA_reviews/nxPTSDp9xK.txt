 **Summary:**
The paper introduces CrossGET, a novel acceleration framework for vision-language Transformers that adaptively combines tokens through real-time, cross-modal guidance to achieve substantial acceleration while maintaining high performance. CrossGET employs two key innovations: Cross-Guided Matching and Ensemble, which utilize cross-modal information effectively without introducing extra parameters. Additionally, it introduces Complete-Graph Soft Matching, a parallelizable token-matching method that ensures high efficiency. The framework has been tested on various vision-language tasks, including image-text retrieval, visual reasoning, image captioning, and visual question answering, demonstrating its effectiveness and versatility.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, clear, and easy to follow, making it accessible to a broad audience.
- The proposed CrossGET framework is a pioneering work in token ensemble for vision-language Transformers, achieving a better performance-efficiency trade-off with negligible extra parameters than previous methods.
- The framework is versatile and applicable to both modality-independent and modality-dependent vision-language Transformers, making it a valuable tool for researchers and practitioners in the field.
- The introduction of cross-guided matching and ensemble, along with complete-graph soft matching, significantly enhances the efficiency and effectiveness of vision-language models.
- Extensive experiments demonstrate the effectiveness and versatility of the proposed CrossGET framework, validating its applicability across various vision-language tasks, datasets, and model architectures.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a thorough discussion on related works, particularly those focusing on token reduction and model pruning in multimodal models, which could provide a more robust context for the research.
- The paper does not include a comprehensive ablation study on the number of cross-tokens used in the model, which is crucial for understanding the impact of this parameter on performance.
- The paper does not provide a detailed analysis of the computational overhead introduced by the additional cross-modal guidance tokens, which could affect the overall efficiency of the model.
- The paper does not compare the proposed method with other recent pruning methods, which could provide a more complete assessment of its effectiveness and efficiency.
- The paper could benefit from a more detailed discussion on the potential negative societal impacts of the proposed method, particularly in terms of computational efficiency and resource utilization.

**Questions:**
- Could you clarify the specifics of the cross-modal guidance tokens mentioned in the paper, particularly how they are derived and what their impact is on the model's performance and computational efficiency?
- How does the CrossGET framework compare to other pruning methods in terms of computational overhead, model efficiency, and performance?
- Can you provide a more detailed analysis of the computational and memory overhead introduced by the CrossGET framework, particularly in relation to the baseline models used in the experiments?
- How does the CrossGET framework handle the reduction in model parameters, and what are the implications of this reduction on the model's performance and efficiency?
- Could you elaborate on the potential negative societal impacts of the CrossGET framework, particularly in terms of computational efficiency and resource utilization?
