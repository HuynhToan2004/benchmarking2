 **Summary:**
The paper explores the phenomenon of neural collapse in deep neural networks, focusing on the mixup data augmentation strategy. It investigates how mixup affects the last-layer activations and classifiers, revealing that they converge to a simplex equiangular tight frame (ETF) configuration. The study includes both empirical and theoretical analyses, showing that mixup-induced neural collapse is distinct from traditional neural collapse. The authors demonstrate that mixup enhances model calibration by aligning last-layer activations and classifiers, which is crucial for improving model performance. The paper is well-structured, providing clear visualizations and theoretical insights, although it could benefit from a broader range of datasets and a deeper discussion on the implications of the findings.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, easy to follow, and provides clear visualizations of the neural collapse phenomenon, making it accessible to readers.
- The authors have conducted an extensive empirical study, including various datasets and models, which supports the claims made about the effects of mixup on neural collapse.
- The theoretical analysis is well-integrated with the empirical findings, providing a comprehensive understanding of the mixup-induced neural collapse.
- The paper introduces an unconstrained features model tailored for mixup training, which is a significant contribution to the field.
- The paper provides a novel perspective on the mixup method, offering insights into how it influences the neural collapse phenomenon.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper primarily focuses on two datasets (CIFAR-10 and CIFAR-100), which may not be sufficient to generalize the findings across different datasets.
- The theoretical analysis assumes that the classifier is a simplex ETF, which might not hold true in practical scenarios, limiting the applicability of the findings.
- The paper lacks a discussion on the implications of the findings, particularly how they could be applied to enhance the mixup method or other data augmentation strategies.
- The visualizations and explanations, such as the projection of CLS token and the explanation of figures, could be improved for clarity and accuracy.
- There is a lack of a broader literature review, which could contextualize the work within the existing research on neural collapse and mixup.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors clarify whether the assumptions made in the theoretical analysis, such as the classifier being a simplex ETF, hold true in practical scenarios?
- How do the findings of this study compare to other data augmentation methods like RandAugment and CutMix?
- Could the authors provide more details on the projections used in the visualizations, particularly the two-dimensional representation of a three-dimensional simplex ETF?
- How does the paper's theoretical analysis relate to the practical implications of mixup in improving model calibration and generalization?
- Would it be possible to include a broader range of datasets in future experiments to validate the findings across different domains?
