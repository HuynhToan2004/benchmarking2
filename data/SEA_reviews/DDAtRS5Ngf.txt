 **Summary:**
The paper explores the vulnerability of multi-modal embeddings, specifically focusing on ImageBind, to adversarial perturbations that can misalign inputs across different modalities. The authors demonstrate that these perturbations can create "adversarial illusions," where an image or sound is manipulated to align with an adversary-chosen input in another modality, potentially misleading downstream tasks. The research presents a novel attack method that can align any image or sound with any text, and discusses potential countermeasures and limitations. The paper is well-structured and provides a comprehensive evaluation, although it is limited to the ImageBind model and does not explore broader societal impacts.

**Strengths:**
- The paper presents a novel and interesting problem of adversarial illusions in multi-modal embeddings, which is an emerging area of research.
- The writing is clear and easy to follow, with a good balance of technical detail and intuitive explanations.
- The paper includes a comprehensive evaluation of the attack method, demonstrating its effectiveness across various downstream tasks.
- The authors provide a thorough analysis of the attack method and its implications, including discussions on potential countermeasures and limitations.
- The paper includes several examples of adversarial perturbations that mislead downstream tasks, which are both interesting and convincing.
- The inclusion of code with the submission enhances the reproducibility of the results.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper primarily focuses on the ImageBind model, which may limit the generalizability of the findings to other multi-modal embedding models.
- There is a lack of discussion on the potential societal impacts of adversarial illusions in multi-modal embeddings, which could be a significant concern.
- The paper does not sufficiently address how the adversarial perturbations could be used to manipulate or mislead in various applications, which could be a critical concern.
- The evaluation is limited to a single model and dataset, which may not sufficiently demonstrate the robustness or generalizability of the findings.
- The paper does not provide sufficient detail on how the adversarial perturbations are generated, which could be a critical aspect for understanding the attack method.
- The paper lacks a detailed discussion on the limitations of the proposed method, which could help in understanding its applicability and effectiveness in real-world scenarios.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors provide more details on how the adversarial perturbations are generated, including the specific parameters used and the methodology behind their creation?
- How does the paper address the potential societal impacts of adversarial illusions in multi-modal embeddings?
- Could the authors discuss the potential applications of the attack method, both positive and negative, to help understand the broader implications of the research?
- How does the proposed method compare to other adversarial attack methods for multi-modal embeddings, and what are the key differences in terms of effectiveness and applicability?
- Could the authors provide more examples or case studies to illustrate the practical implications of the adversarial illusions in real-world scenarios?
- How do the authors plan to address the limitations of the proposed method, particularly in terms of generalizability and applicability to other multi-modal embedding models?
