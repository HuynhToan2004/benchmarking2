 **Summary:**
The paper introduces FastDCFlow, a method for generating counterfactual explanations (CE) in tabular data using normalizing flows. This approach aims to address the challenges of generating CE for both categorical and continuous variables, which is a common issue in existing methods. The paper evaluates FastDCFlow against various baselines, showing that it can handle the trade-off between diversity and proximity effectively. It also proposes using target encoding to handle categorical variables, which is a novel approach. The method is evaluated using multiple datasets, and the results show that FastDCFlow outperforms existing methods in terms of diversity and efficiency. However, the paper is criticized for its limited evaluation on only three datasets, lack of qualitative analysis, and potential issues with the scalability and interpretability of the generated counterfactuals.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, easy to follow, and provides a comprehensive evaluation of the proposed method, FastDCFlow.
- The use of normalizing flows in generating counterfactual explanations (CE) is novel and addresses a significant challenge in the field.
- The paper provides a thorough evaluation of the method, comparing it with various baselines and demonstrating its effectiveness through experiments.
- The methodology is well-explained, and the paper is generally easy to understand, which is beneficial for readers.
- The paper is the first to utilize normalizing flows for generating counterfactuals, which is a significant contribution to the field.
- The proposed method, FastDCFlow, is efficient and can generate counterfactuals in parallel, which is a significant advantage over some existing methods.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed discussion on the scalability of the method, particularly in terms of handling large datasets or high-dimensional data.
- The evaluation of the method is limited to three datasets, which may not be sufficient to demonstrate its generalizability across different types of data.
- There is a lack of qualitative analysis of the generated counterfactuals, which is crucial for understanding the method's effectiveness and applicability.
- The paper does not provide a detailed discussion on the limitations of the method, which could help in understanding its practical applicability and potential drawbacks.
- The method's effectiveness in handling complex relationships between features, such as interactions between categorical variables, is not clearly demonstrated.
- The paper does not adequately address the issue of interpretability in the generated counterfactuals, which is a significant concern for practical applications.

**Questions:**
- How does the method handle the scalability issue, particularly in terms of handling large datasets or high-dimensional data?
- Can the method be applied to other types of data, such as image or text data? If so, how well does it perform in these domains?
- How does the method handle the issue of interpretability in the generated counterfactuals?
- Can the authors provide more details on the limitations of the method, particularly in terms of its practical applicability and potential drawbacks?
- How does the method compare with other methods that use normalizing flows for generating counterfactuals?
- Can the authors provide more details on the training process of FastDCFlow and the selection of hyperparameters?
- Can the authors provide more qualitative results or case studies to demonstrate the effectiveness of the method in practical scenarios?
