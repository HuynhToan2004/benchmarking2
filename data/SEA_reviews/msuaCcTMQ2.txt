 **Summary:**
The paper introduces AutoActiveSelf-Labeling (AutoASL), an augmented AutoML system that integrates principles from self-training and active learning to leverage unlabeled data in training. AutoASL uses a powerful ensemble of AutoML and traditional ML algorithms to iteratively self-label previously unlabeled data, expanding the labeled training data and improving overall accuracy. The system is evaluated on various datasets and shows promising results, outperforming existing tools. However, concerns about the novelty and depth of methodological contributions, the clarity of presentation, and the practicality of the approach in real-world scenarios with large, unlabeled datasets are highlighted.

**Strengths:**
- The paper addresses a relevant and significant problem by combining active learning and self-training in the context of AutoML, which is crucial for handling scenarios where labeled data is scarce.
- The methodology is well-explained, with clear descriptions of the proposed AutoASL algorithm and its components, making it easy to follow and understand.
- The paper is well-organized, with a clear problem definition and a detailed description of the methodology, which facilitates reproducibility.
- The experimental results are robust, demonstrating the effectiveness of the proposed method across various datasets and tasks.
- The paper provides a novel approach by integrating AutoML with self-training and active learning, which is a significant advancement in the field.

**Weaknesses:**
- The novelty of the proposed method is questioned as it primarily combines existing techniques without significant new contributions.
- The paper lacks a thorough analysis of the limitations and potential negative societal impacts of the proposed method.
- The evaluation setup is not clearly defined, raising concerns about the fairness and robustness of the comparisons made.
- The presentation of the paper needs improvement, with some sections being hard to follow and the overall clarity and coherence needing enhancement.
- The paper does not adequately address the practicality of the approach in real-world scenarios where labeled data is scarce but unlabeled data is abundant, such as in medical data.
- The paper does not sufficiently discuss the challenges and limitations of the proposed method, particularly in terms of its applicability to larger, more complex datasets.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors clarify the novelty of the proposed method and how it differs from existing approaches that combine active learning and self-training?
- How does the proposed method handle scenarios where labeled data is extremely scarce, and what are the implications for its effectiveness?
- Could the authors provide more details on the practical implementation of the proposed method, especially in terms of how it can be adapted to different data types and scenarios?
- How does the proposed method perform in terms of computational efficiency, particularly in comparison to other state-of-the-art methods?
- Can the authors discuss the potential negative societal impacts of their proposed method and how these might be mitigated or addressed?
- How does the proposed method compare to other state-of-the-art methods in terms of performance and resource utilization?
