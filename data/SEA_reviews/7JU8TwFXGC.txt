 **Summary:**
The paper explores the use of large language models (LLMs) for predicting the performance of neural network architectures, specifically for machine translation tasks. It introduces a novel approach using LLMs to predict the performance of architectures, which is then distilled into a smaller model for practical applications. The authors propose a hybrid search algorithm that integrates LLM-distilled performance predictors with traditional supernet-based predictors to enhance the efficiency of neural architecture search. The methodology involves using LLMs to predict performance metrics such as BLEU score, latency, and model size, and demonstrates that this approach can reduce the search time and improve the accuracy of predictions. However, the paper faces criticism for its limited scope to machine translation tasks and the lack of comprehensive comparisons with other methods.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel approach using LLMs to predict the performance of neural network architectures, which is a significant advancement in the field of neural architecture search (NAS).
- The proposed hybrid search algorithm effectively integrates LLM-distilled performance predictors with traditional supernet-based predictors, enhancing the efficiency and accuracy of NAS.
- The paper provides a comprehensive evaluation of the LLM-based performance predictor and the distilled model, demonstrating their effectiveness through rigorous testing and comparison with existing methods.
- The methodology is well-explained, making it accessible and reproducible, and the paper includes a detailed analysis of the results, which supports the claims made.
- The paper is well-written, clear, and easy to follow, with a logical structure that aids in understanding the complex concepts presented.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper's evaluation is limited to machine translation tasks, which may not generalize well to other types of tasks or domains. This limits the practical applicability of the findings.
- There is a lack of comprehensive comparison with other performance predictors and NAS methods, which could provide a more robust evaluation of the proposed method.
- The paper does not sufficiently discuss the limitations of the proposed method, which is crucial for understanding the scope and applicability of the findings.
- The paper could benefit from a more detailed discussion on the scalability of the LLM-based performance predictor, especially in terms of the computational resources required for training and inference.
- The paper could provide more insights into the role of different LLMs in the performance prediction task and how these models contribute to the overall accuracy of the predictions.
- The paper's focus on BLEU score as the primary evaluation metric may not fully capture the performance of the architectures, as other metrics such as perplexity or accuracy could be more relevant for certain tasks.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors provide more details on how the performance predictor was trained, including the training data, the number of examples used, and the specifics of the training process?
- How does the LLM-based performance predictor perform on other types of tasks or domains beyond machine translation?
- Could the authors discuss the potential of extending the hybrid search algorithm to other types of NAS tasks or domains?
- What are the specific computational resources required for training and inference of the LLM-based performance predictor, and how does this compare to the computational requirements of other methods?
- How does the choice of LLM affect the performance predictions, and what are the implications of using different LLMs for different tasks or domains?
- Could the authors provide more insights into the role of different hyperparameters in the performance prediction task, and how these parameters influence the accuracy of the predictions?
- How does the proposed method compare to other state-of-the-art methods in terms of computational efficiency, accuracy, and practical applicability?
