 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel method for detecting out-of-distribution (OOD) samples using a k-NN density estimate based on the classification model's intermediate activations on in-distribution samples. The approach leverages the label smoothing technique to improve the clustering of in-distribution samples and the separation of out-of-distribution samples in the embedding space. The paper provides theoretical insights and empirical evidence, demonstrating the method's effectiveness across several datasets. However, concerns are raised about the clarity of the theoretical results and the empirical results' generalizability, particularly in more complex datasets and larger models.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, clear, and easy to follow, with a clear motivation and a well-structured presentation of the methodology and results.
- The use of label smoothing to improve clustering in the embedding space is an innovative approach that could potentially enhance the performance of k-NN density estimation for OOD detection.
- The paper provides both theoretical insights and empirical evidence, which support the proposed method and its effectiveness.
- The method is simple and does not require additional training, which makes it practical and efficient.
- The paper includes a comprehensive discussion on the hyperparameters and their impact on the results, which is helpful for practitioners.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed discussion on the computational complexity and time efficiency of the proposed method, which is crucial for practical implementation.
- The theoretical results, particularly Theorem 1 and 2, are not well-explained or intuitive, and their practical implications are not clear.
- The empirical results are limited to simple datasets and small models, which may not generalize well to more complex or larger datasets.
- The paper does not adequately address the potential issues with label smoothing, such as the potential for increased confidence in OOD samples.
- The paper could benefit from more comprehensive experiments, including comparisons with more recent and complex baselines, and evaluations on larger and more diverse datasets.
- There is a lack of discussion on the limitations of the method, which is crucial for understanding its applicability and potential drawbacks.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors clarify the theoretical results, particularly Theorem 1 and 2, and provide more intuitive explanations or examples to illustrate their practical implications?
- How does the proposed method perform on more complex and larger datasets, such as those used in large-scale benchmarks like ImageNet?
- Could the authors provide more details on the computational complexity and time efficiency of the proposed method, especially in comparison to other methods?
- How does the method handle the potential issues with label smoothing, such as increased confidence in OOD samples?
- Can the authors discuss the potential limitations of the method and scenarios where it might not be effective?
- How does the method perform when compared to more recent and complex baselines, and what are the implications of these comparisons for the practical applicability of the method?
