 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel method for quantization-aware training (QAT) in large language models (LLMs) using a data-free distillation method. This method generates data from the pre-trained model for knowledge distillation, which is used to fine-tune the student model. The authors employ a quantization-aware training approach that quantizes weights, activations, and the KV cache, which is crucial for increasing throughput and supporting long sequence dependencies. The proposed method is evaluated on various LLaMA models with different bit precision levels and demonstrates significant improvements over training-free methods, particularly in low-bit settings. However, the paper's methodology and results are criticized for not being sufficiently novel or comprehensive, with concerns about the clarity of the presentation and the novelty of the contributions.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, making it easy to follow, and the methodology is clearly explained.
- The proposed method is novel and addresses the challenge of quantization-aware training for large language models (LLMs), which is an open problem.
- The paper presents a data-free distillation method that leverages generations produced by the pre-trained model, which better preserves the original output distribution and allows quantizing any generative model independent of its training data.
- The experiments conducted demonstrate that the proposed method achieves better results than training-free methods, especially in low-bit settings.
- The paper introduces a quantization-aware training approach that quantizes weights, activations, and the KV cache, which is crucial for increasing throughput and supporting long sequence dependencies at current model sizes.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks sufficient novelty as the data-free distillation method is a well-known technique, and the contributions of the paper are not clearly distinguished from existing methods.
- The paper does not adequately discuss the limitations of the proposed method, which could help readers understand the scope and applicability of the work.
- The experimental results are not convincing, with only minor improvements observed in most cases, and the paper does not compare the proposed method with other state-of-the-art quantization methods.
- The paper could benefit from a more thorough discussion of related works and a clearer presentation of the experimental results and methodology.
- There is a lack of clarity in the presentation of the paper, particularly in the description of the methodology and the experimental results.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors clarify the novelty of their contributions and distinguish them from existing methods?
- How does the proposed method compare with other state-of-the-art quantization methods in terms of accuracy, efficiency, and other relevant metrics?
- Can the authors provide a more detailed explanation of the experimental results, including the methodology used and the significance of the observed improvements?
- How does the proposed method address the challenges of quantization in large language models, particularly in terms of preserving the original output distribution and supporting long sequence dependencies?
- Can the authors discuss the limitations of their method and how these might affect its practical application and scalability?
