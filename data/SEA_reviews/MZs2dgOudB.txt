 **Summary:**
The paper explores active learning in meta-learning, specifically focusing on the selection of labeled data points in the context set at meta-testing time. It proposes a novel method using Gaussian mixture models (GMMs) to select data points that maximize the likelihood of being the closest to the mean of the Gaussian distribution. This method is evaluated against various baselines and meta-learning algorithms, showing improvements in performance across several datasets. The paper also discusses the theoretical underpinnings of the GMM approach and its potential to enhance the performance of meta-learning models.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, clear, and easy to follow, with a clear and concise presentation of the proposed method.
- The proposed method is simple, effective, and has been demonstrated to outperform various baselines across multiple datasets, including both classification and regression tasks.
- The paper provides a comprehensive review of active learning in meta-learning, which is crucial for understanding the current state of research in this area.
- The methodology is simple yet effective, and the paper includes a thorough evaluation of the proposed method against various baselines and meta-learning algorithms.
- The paper is well-organized, with clear explanations of the experimental setup and results, which are supported by a robust theoretical framework.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed comparison with other active learning methods specifically designed for meta-learning, such as those mentioned in related works.
- The novelty of the proposed method is questioned as it appears to be a straightforward application of existing active learning methods, such as GMM, without significant innovation.
- The paper does not sufficiently discuss the computational complexity or the time efficiency of the proposed method, which could be critical in practical applications.
- There is a lack of discussion on the limitations of the proposed method and its applicability in different scenarios, such as different meta-learning algorithms or datasets.
- The paper does not adequately address the potential negative societal impacts of the proposed method, which could be a significant concern in real-world applications.

**Questions:**
