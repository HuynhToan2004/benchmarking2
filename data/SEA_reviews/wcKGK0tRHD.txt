 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel approach to improve the Forward-Forward (FF) algorithm by combining three techniques: SymBa loss function, batch normalization, and overlapping local updates. The authors claim that these modifications enhance the algorithm's performance on deeper networks, as demonstrated by improved accuracy on CIFAR-10 and other datasets. However, the paper has been critiqued for its lack of novelty, as the techniques used are not entirely new, and the empirical results do not convincingly surpass existing methods like backpropagation. The clarity of the paper and the depth of its theoretical contributions have also been questioned.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written and easy to follow, with clear explanations and a logical structure.
- The proposed techniques, including the SymBa loss function, batch normalization, and overlapping local updates, are well-motivated and well-explained.
- The experiments are comprehensive, covering various datasets and different architectures, and include a detailed ablation study.
- The paper provides a thorough analysis of the limitations of the original FF algorithm and proposes improvements that are well-justified and supported by empirical evidence.
- The use of batch normalization is particularly interesting and contributes to the paper's novelty.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a clear explanation of why the proposed modifications to the FF algorithm are effective, particularly in terms of their impact on the learning process.
- The novelty of the paper is limited, as the techniques used are not entirely new and have been previously explored in other contexts.
- The empirical results do not convincingly demonstrate a significant advantage over existing methods like backpropagation, especially in terms of accuracy and training speed.
- The paper could benefit from a more detailed discussion on the limitations and potential negative societal impacts of the proposed methods.
- The clarity of the paper could be improved, particularly in the explanation of certain technical details and the organization of the content.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors clarify the role and impact of batch normalization in the FF algorithm, particularly in terms of its contribution to the overall performance improvements?
- How does the paper address the issue of overfitting, which is a known problem in the FF algorithm?
- Why was the decision made to use batch normalization instead of other normalization techniques, such as layer normalization?
- How does the paper ensure that the modifications to the FF algorithm do not introduce new limitations or negative societal impacts?
- Could the authors provide more detailed explanations or examples to support the claims made about the learning process and the effectiveness of the proposed modifications?
