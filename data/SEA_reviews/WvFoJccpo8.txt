 **Summary:**
The paper introduces QA-LoRA, a method that integrates quantization and low-rank adaptation for large language models (LLMs) to address the challenges of deploying LLMs on edge devices. QA-LoRA employs group-wise operators to adjust the number of parameters for quantization and adaptation, enabling efficient fine-tuning and inference. The method is evaluated using the LLaMA family of models and shows improvements in efficiency and accuracy compared to existing methods like QLoRA. The paper also discusses the potential of QA-LoRA in reducing computational costs and enhancing the deployment capabilities of LLMs.

**Strengths:**
- The paper addresses a significant and timely problem by focusing on the deployment of large language models (LLMs) on edge devices, which is crucial for practical applications.
- The proposed method, QA-LoRA, is simple, effective, and generalizable, offering a novel approach to quantization-aware low-rank adaptation.
- The paper is well-written, clear, and easy to follow, making it accessible to a broad audience.
- The experiments conducted are comprehensive, covering various model sizes and quantization bit widths, which demonstrates the robustness and applicability of the proposed method.
- The paper provides a detailed analysis of the proposed method, including the balance between quantization and adaptation, and the impact of different hyperparameters.
- The methodology is supported by strong experimental results that show improvements over existing methods like QLoRA, demonstrating the effectiveness of the proposed approach.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a thorough discussion on the limitations of the proposed method, particularly in terms of its applicability to different LLM families and its scalability to larger models.
- The paper does not adequately address the potential negative societal impacts of the proposed technology, which could be significant given the deployment of LLMs on edge devices.
- The paper's experimental evaluation is somewhat limited, focusing primarily on the LLaMA family of models and not comparing the proposed method with a broader range of models or other relevant baselines.
- The paper does not provide sufficient details on the implementation of the method, particularly the algorithmic details and the computational complexity involved.
- There is a lack of discussion on the potential for overfitting due to the use of low-rank matrices in the adaptation process.
- The paper does not include a comprehensive analysis of the computational costs and memory requirements of QA-LoRA compared to other methods, which could be crucial for practical deployment.

**Questions:**
- How does the proposed method perform when applied to other LLM families, such as those based on different architectures like BERT?
- Can the authors provide more details on the implementation of the method, particularly the algorithmic details and computational complexity?
- How does the method handle the potential for overfitting due to the use of low-rank matrices in the adaptation process?
- Could the authors discuss the potential societal impacts of deploying LLMs on edge devices, particularly in terms of privacy and security concerns?
- How does the proposed method compare in terms of computational costs and memory requirements to other methods like QLoRA, especially in practical deployment scenarios?
- Can the authors provide a more detailed analysis of the experimental results, particularly the impact of different hyperparameters on the performance of the method?
