 **Summary:**
The paper introduces ProtoReg, a method designed to address the "granularity gap" in fine-grained transfer learning by utilizing class-discriminative prototypes. ProtoReg employs two regularization terms: an aggregation loss to pull features towards the prototype and a separation loss to push features away from prototypes of other classes. The method is tested across several benchmarks and demonstrates significant performance improvements over existing methods, particularly in scenarios with limited data. However, concerns are raised about the novelty and depth of the experimental validation, with some reviewers questioning the generalizability of the approach and the necessity of the proposed method over simpler alternatives.

**Strengths:**
- The paper addresses an important and interesting problem in fine-grained transfer learning, focusing on the granularity gap issue which is often overlooked.
- The proposed method, ProtoReg, is simple yet effective, demonstrated through extensive experimental results that show significant performance improvements over existing methods.
- The paper is well-written, making it easy to follow, and includes a detailed analysis of the proposed method and its components.
- ProtoReg has been evaluated on several benchmarks and sampling rates, demonstrating its effectiveness across different scenarios.
- The method's ability to enhance the performance of fine-grained transfer learning tasks, particularly in limited data scenarios, is highlighted.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a comprehensive literature review, missing out on significant recent works in the field.
- The experimental evaluation is limited to a few datasets, which might not fully demonstrate the generalizability of the proposed method.
- The necessity of the proposed method is questionable, as simpler alternatives such as using class-wise feature mean or linear classifier weights could also be effective.
- The paper does not adequately address the scalability of the method to larger datasets or models, which could limit its practical application.
- There is a lack of detailed analysis and explanation for certain experimental results, such as the performance differences between ProtoReg and other methods.
- The paper could benefit from a more thorough discussion on the limitations of the proposed method and its potential negative societal impacts.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors provide a more detailed explanation for the performance differences between ProtoReg and other methods, particularly in scenarios with limited data?
- How does the proposed method perform on larger datasets or models, and what are the potential challenges or limitations in scaling up the approach?
- Could the authors elaborate on the choice of using linear probing for prototype initialization and how this compares to other methods?
- How sensitive is the method to the choice of hyperparameters, and what are the recommended settings for different scenarios?
- Could the authors discuss the potential negative societal impacts of the proposed method and how these might be mitigated or addressed?
