 **Summary:**
The paper introduces ReweightOOD, a novel method for Out-of-Distribution (OOD) detection in neural networks, specifically focusing on reducing the minimum enclosing sphere for each class and increasing inter-class dispersion. This is achieved through a reweighting scheme that prioritizes less-optimized contrasting pairs and de-emphasizes well-optimized ones. The approach is evaluated on various datasets, including CIFAR-100 and ImageNet-100, showing significant improvements in OOD detection metrics such as FPR@95 and AUROC. However, the paper is criticized for its lack of clarity in explaining the reweighting mechanism and the absence of detailed experimental setups, which makes it difficult to replicate the results.

**Strengths:**
- The paper addresses the critical issue of OOD detection, which is crucial for ensuring the safety and reliability of neural networks in various applications.
- The proposed method, ReweightOOD, is a novel approach that prioritizes the optimization of less-optimized contrasting pairs, which is a significant improvement over existing methods.
- The paper is well-written, easy to follow, and provides a detailed explanation of the methodology, including the use of hyperspherical embeddings and a reweighting mechanism.
- The experimental results demonstrate significant improvements over baseline methods, showing the effectiveness of the proposed approach.
- The paper includes a thorough analysis of the embedding space, showing how the method reduces the minimum enclosing sphere for each class and increases inter-class dispersion, enhancing the separation between ID and OOD samples.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks clarity in explaining the reweighting mechanism and the specifics of the reweighting function, making it difficult for readers to understand the methodology fully.
- The experimental setup is not detailed enough, and the paper does not provide enough information on the hyperparameters used, which is necessary for replicating the results.
- The paper primarily focuses on CIFAR-100 and ImageNet-100 datasets, and it would be beneficial to include results on other datasets like SVHN to provide a more comprehensive evaluation.
- The paper does not compare the proposed method with some of the most recent and effective OOD detection methods, which could undermine the perceived effectiveness of the proposed method.
- The paper does not provide sufficient ablation studies, which are necessary to understand the impact of different components of the method on its performance.
- The paper lacks a detailed discussion on the limitations of the method, which is crucial for understanding the scope and applicability of the proposed approach.

**Questions:**
- Could you provide a more detailed explanation of the reweighting mechanism and the specifics of the reweighting function?
- How were the hyperparameters chosen for the experiments, and what is the impact of these choices on the results?
- Could you include results on other datasets like SVHN to provide a more comprehensive evaluation?
- How does the proposed method compare with some of the most recent and effective OOD detection methods, such as those mentioned in the review?
- Could you provide more ablation studies to understand the impact of different components of the method on its performance?
- How does the proposed method handle different types of OOD samples, such as those from different domains or those with different class distributions?
- Could you discuss the limitations of the proposed method and how these could be addressed in future work?
