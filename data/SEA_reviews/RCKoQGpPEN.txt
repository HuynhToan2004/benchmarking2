 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel meta-architecture for video panoptic segmentation, specifically focusing on video instance segmentation. It builds upon existing clip-level segmenters and introduces a two-stage tracking module: within-clip and cross-clip tracking. The within-clip tracking utilizes axial-trajectory attention to enhance local consistency, while cross-clip tracking employs trajectory attention to maintain consistency across clips. The method demonstrates improvements over baseline models and achieves state-of-the-art performance on various benchmarks. However, concerns about the novelty and the direct application of existing techniques without significant modifications were raised.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel meta-architecture for video panoptic segmentation, which builds on existing clip-level segmenters and introduces a two-stage tracking module for within-clip and cross-clip tracking.
- The proposed within-clip tracking module effectively improves the temporal consistency of clip-level predictions, and the cross-clip tracking module maintains consistency across different video clips.
- The method demonstrates significant improvements over baseline models, achieving state-of-the-art performance on various benchmarks.
- The paper is well-written, with clear and comprehensive explanations of the proposed methods and their contributions to the field.
- The methodology is simple yet effective, and the results are promising, showing a clear improvement over the baseline models.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper primarily applies existing techniques, such as trajectory attention and multi-scale deformable attention, without significant modifications or novel contributions.
- The performance of the proposed method does not consistently outperform existing methods, as shown in some comparisons like Youtube-VIS-21 and Youtube-VIS-22.
- The computational complexity of the proposed methods, particularly in terms of memory and runtime, is not adequately addressed, raising concerns about its practical applicability.
- The paper lacks detailed ablation studies, which are crucial for understanding the individual contributions of each component of the proposed method.
- The methodology is limited to clip-level segmenters, which might not be the most effective approach for video segmentation tasks.
- The novelty of the proposed method is questionable as it primarily combines existing techniques without significant advancements.

**Questions:**
- Could you provide more details on the computational complexity of the proposed methods, particularly in terms of memory and runtime?
- Why does the proposed method not consistently outperform existing methods, as seen in the results on Youtube-VIS-21 and Youtube-VIS-22?
- Can you provide more insights into the effectiveness of the proposed method in scenarios where it underperforms compared to existing methods?
- How does the method handle scenarios with fast motion or occlusion, which are known to be challenging for video segmentation?
- Can you elaborate on the novelty of the proposed method, especially in comparison to existing methods that have similar components?
- What are the specific contributions of the proposed method beyond the existing techniques, and how do these contributions impact the performance of the model?
