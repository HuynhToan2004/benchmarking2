 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel mixed effects deep learning (MEDL) framework aimed at enhancing fairness in machine learning models, particularly in scenarios where data is clustered and sensitive features are involved. This framework, which builds upon the ARMED model, integrates adversarial debiasing to ensure equalized odds fairness across various datasets and tasks. The methodology involves the introduction of fixed and random effects to address cluster-specific biases, and the results demonstrate significant improvements in fairness across different datasets, including census and healthcare data. Despite its innovative approach, the paper has been critiqued for its lack of clarity in presentation, the choice of baselines, and the generalization of the proposed methods to other datasets.

**Strengths:**
- The paper addresses a significant and timely problem in machine learning by focusing on fairness and mixed effects in deep learning.
- The authors provide a comprehensive framework that integrates adversarial debiasing with ARMED to enhance fairness, which is a novel approach.
- The experimental results are robust, showing significant improvements in fairness across different datasets and tasks.
- The paper is well-written, making it accessible and understandable, which is crucial for a broad audience.
- The proposed methodology is sound and technically solid, with a focus on improving fairness in machine learning models, which is a critical issue in current AI applications.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed discussion on the choice of baselines, particularly in relation to other fairness-enhancing methods such as those by Beutel et al. and Yang et al.
- The presentation of the paper needs improvement, with some sections being unclear or overly complex, which could hinder understanding.
- The paper's contribution to the field is not clearly articulated, especially in relation to existing literature.
- The experimental setup and results are not sufficiently detailed or generalizable, with a focus on specific datasets that may not be representative or generalizable to other scenarios.
- The paper does not adequately address the potential limitations or ethical considerations of the proposed methodology, which could be a significant concern in real-world applications.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors provide a more detailed comparison with other fairness-enhancing methods, particularly those mentioned in the review?
- How does the proposed methodology handle different types of biases, especially those that are not related to sensitive features?
- Could the authors clarify the presentation of the paper, particularly in sections that are currently unclear or overly complex?
- How generalizable is the proposed methodology to other datasets and scenarios beyond those tested in the paper?
- What are the specific ethical considerations of the proposed methodology, and how do the authors address these in their research?
- Can the authors provide more detailed results on the computational complexity and scalability of their methodology, especially in large-scale applications?
