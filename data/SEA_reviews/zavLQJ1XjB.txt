 **Summary:**
The paper investigates the calibration performance of temperature scaling in models trained on data distributions with overlapping class supports, comparing it to models trained using Mixup data augmentation. Theoretical analysis shows that temperature scaling fails to calibrate models effectively when there is significant overlap, while Mixup can maintain good calibration. The paper presents both synthetic and real-world data experiments to support these findings, providing a theoretical framework for understanding the limitations of temperature scaling and the benefits of Mixup in specific data scenarios.

**Strengths:**
- The paper provides a theoretical analysis of the temperature scaling method in the context of overlapping class supports, which is a significant contribution to the understanding of calibration in machine learning models.
- The authors demonstrate that temperature scaling can lead to poor calibration in models trained on data with overlapping class supports, and that Mixup can help maintain good calibration.
- The paper is well-written, clear, and easy to follow, making it accessible to a broad audience.
- The theoretical results are supported by empirical evidence, enhancing the credibility of the findings.
- The paper identifies a specific bottleneck for the performance of temperature scaling, which is an interesting and valuable contribution to the field.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper could benefit from a more detailed discussion on the practical implications and limitations of the theoretical results, particularly in terms of their applicability to real-world datasets.
- The assumption of interpolation in the theoretical analysis might not hold for all datasets, which could affect the generalizability of the findings.
- The experimental setup and results, such as the use of ResNeXt-50 and the results on CIFAR-10, might not be directly comparable or applicable to other datasets or models.
- The paper does not provide a clear explanation for why Mixup is more effective in calibration compared to temperature scaling, which could be a significant limitation in understanding the theoretical contributions.
- The paper could benefit from a more thorough discussion on the practical applications and limitations of the Mixup technique, particularly in terms of its computational efficiency and scalability.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors provide more detailed explanations or examples of how the theoretical results apply to real-world datasets, especially those with overlapping class supports?
- How does the assumption of interpolation affect the applicability of the theoretical results to datasets that do not interpolate the training data?
- Could the authors clarify the practical implications and limitations of the Mixup technique, particularly in terms of its computational efficiency and scalability?
- How does the paper's theoretical framework compare to existing works on calibration in deep learning models, particularly in terms of novelty and depth of analysis?
- The paper could benefit from a more detailed discussion on the practical applications and limitations of the Mixup technique. Can the authors provide more insights into how Mixup can be effectively used in real-world scenarios?
