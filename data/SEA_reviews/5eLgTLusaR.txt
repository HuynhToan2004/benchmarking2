 **Summary:**
The paper introduces Loco3D, a novel dataset featuring 3D body pose and spatial information for multi-person interactions in over 100 indoor VR scenes. This dataset is designed to facilitate the development of AI agents and virtual avatars that mimic human movements and postures in complex indoor environments. The dataset is constructed using a virtual reality system, which allows for efficient data collection and high accuracy. The paper also proposes a UNet-based framework for synthesizing human-like trajectories in complex indoor scenes. The framework considers complex scene geometry and other people's movements, potentially enhancing the performance of trajectory synthesis models. The dataset and the proposed framework are evaluated on real-world data, showing improved performance compared to other indoor datasets.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel dataset, Loco3D, which focuses on multi-person trajectories in complex indoor environments, including over 7000 multi-person trajectories in 130 indoor environments with 3D pose and 3D scene geometry with semantics.
- The dataset is constructed using a virtual reality (VR) system, which allows for efficient data collection and high accuracy, making it a significant contribution to the field.
- The paper proposes a UNet-based framework that considers the complex scene geometry and other people's movements in indoor scenes to synthesize human-like trajectories, which is a novel approach to trajectory synthesis.
- The evaluation demonstrates that models trained with the dataset have improved multi-person trajectory synthesis performance on real-world data, showing the effectiveness of the proposed method.
- The paper is well-written, with clear figures and tables that aid in understanding the content.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed description of the VR setup used for data collection, which is crucial for reproducibility and understanding the system's capabilities.
- The dataset is currently limited to only two people, which may not fully represent real-world scenarios with more complex social dynamics.
- The paper does not provide a detailed analysis of the dataset's diversity, such as the variety of scenes, tasks, and social interactions, which could affect the generalizability of the results.
- The evaluation is limited to a single baseline model, which may not adequately demonstrate the robustness or superiority of the proposed method.
- The paper does not discuss the potential limitations of the dataset or the proposed method, which could affect the practical application of the findings.
- The paper could benefit from a more detailed discussion on how the dataset and the proposed method could be used in real-world applications, such as in home robots or virtual avatars.

**Questions:**

- How does the dataset ensure diversity in scenes, tasks, and social interactions to reflect real-world complexity?
- Can you discuss the limitations of the dataset and the proposed method, and how these might affect the practical application of the findings?

- Could you provide more details on the data collection process, including the number of participants and the duration of data collection per scene?
- How does the proposed method compare to other state-of-the-art models in terms of performance and efficiency?
