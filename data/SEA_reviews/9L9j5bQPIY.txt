 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel approach to interpret neural networks by using a meta-autoencoder to encode the weights of neural networks into a latent space, which can then be used to predict the modality and dataset ID of the network. This methodology is tested on a set of 41 vision and audio datasets, demonstrating the ability to predict network characteristics with high accuracy. However, the paper is criticized for its limited scope in terms of network architecture and dataset diversity, and the experimental validation is seen as insufficient. The method's generalizability and its practical utility in real-world applications are also questioned.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel approach to interpreting neural networks by using a meta-autoencoder to encode weights into a latent space, which can be used to predict network modality and dataset ID.
- The proposed method shows promise in understanding the capabilities of neural networks, providing a new perspective on neural network interpretability.
- The paper is well-written, making it easy to follow, and the proposed method is straightforward and clear.
- The experiments demonstrate the ability of the proposed method to predict the modality and dataset ID of neural networks with high accuracy, indicating its potential utility.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a thorough discussion on related works, particularly missing recent studies on set autoencoders and feature visualization.
- The experimental validation is insufficient, with only one model architecture tested and a limited number of datasets used.
- The paper does not adequately address the generalizability of the method across different network architectures and dataset types.
- The method's practical utility is questionable, as it does not clearly demonstrate how it can be applied to real-world problems.
- The paper's claims about the method's ability to infer the training dataset of a model are not well-supported, and the method's performance in predicting dataset ID is poor.
- There is a lack of clarity on how the method would perform with more complex network architectures or different types of datasets.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors provide a more detailed explanation of how the method performs when applied to more complex network architectures or different types of datasets?
- How does the method compare to other existing methods for interpreting neural networks?
- Could the authors clarify the practical applications of the proposed method and provide examples of how it could be used in real-world scenarios?
- What are the specific advantages of the proposed method over existing methods, and how does it address the limitations of these methods?
- How does the method handle the variability in performance across different network architectures and dataset types, and what steps are being taken to address these issues?
- Can the authors provide more details on the experimental setup, particularly the choice of datasets and the criteria used for their selection?
- How does the method perform when applied to different types of neural networks, such as recurrent neural networks or transformers?
