 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel method called cooperative diffusion recovery likelihood (CDRL) for training energy-based models (EBMs) by integrating a diffusion model to enhance the learning process. CDRL utilizes a sequence of EBMs defined on increasingly noisy versions of a dataset, each paired with an initializer model. The method optimizes the recovery likelihood of the EBMs by refining samples from the initializer through a few MCMC sampling steps. The paper claims that CDRL achieves better sample quality and reduces sampling time compared to existing methods. However, the paper has been criticized for its limited novelty, as it builds upon existing methods like DRL and CoopNets, and for not sufficiently demonstrating the practical benefits of the proposed techniques.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, making it easy to follow and understand.
- The proposed method is simple and effective, showing promising results in various tasks such as image generation, OOD detection, and compositionality.
- The authors have provided a comprehensive evaluation of the proposed method, including a comparison with other methods like DRL and CoopNets.
- The method is designed to be efficient in terms of training and sampling time, which is crucial for practical applications.
- The paper introduces several practical designs to improve the training of EBMs, such as noise scheduling, MCMC sampling, and variance reduction.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks sufficient novelty as it builds upon existing methods like DRL and CoopNets, and does not sufficiently differentiate its contributions from these prior works.
- The performance of the proposed method is not consistently better than existing methods across all datasets and tasks. For example, the method performs worse in terms of FID on ImageNet 32x32 compared to DRL.
- The paper does not provide sufficient evidence to support the claims of reduced sampling time, and the experimental results do not convincingly demonstrate the superiority of the proposed method.
- There is a lack of detailed analysis on the computational efficiency and memory requirements of the proposed method, which is crucial for understanding its practical applicability.
- The paper could benefit from a more thorough ablation study to better understand the impact of different components of the method.
- The paper lacks a clear discussion on the limitations and potential negative societal impacts of the proposed method.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors clarify the specific contributions of their method compared to existing works like DRL and CoopNets?
- How does the proposed method compare in terms of computational efficiency and memory requirements to other state-of-the-art methods?
- Could the authors provide more detailed evidence to support the claims of reduced sampling time and improved performance across different datasets and tasks?
- Is there a possibility to extend the method to handle more complex tasks or datasets, such as higher resolution images or more complex generative tasks?
- Could the authors provide more details on the training and inference time of the proposed method compared to other baselines?
- How does the method handle the potential negative societal impacts, such as the generation of deepfakes or misinformation?
