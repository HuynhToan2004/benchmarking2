 **Summary:**
The paper introduces Vocos, a novel GAN-based vocoder that operates in the frequency domain, addressing the challenges of phase wrapping and computational inefficiencies associated with time-domain neural vocoders. Vocos utilizes a novel approach by directly generating Fourier spectral coefficients, employing a multi-period discriminator and a multi-resolution discriminator for training. The model demonstrates significant improvements in computational efficiency, achieving an order of magnitude increase in speed compared to existing time-domain neural vocoding approaches. The paper also includes extensive evaluations, both objective and subjective, which showcase Vocos' competitive performance and naturalness in comparison to state-of-the-art methods.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel GAN-based vocoder that operates in the frequency domain, addressing the challenges of phase wrapping and computational inefficiencies associated with time-domain neural vocoders.
- The use of a multi-period discriminator and a multi-resolution discriminator for training is innovative and enhances the model's performance.
- Extensive evaluations are conducted, both objective and subjective, which showcase Vocos' competitive performance and naturalness in comparison to state-of-the-art methods.
- The paper is well-written, easy to follow, and includes a detailed ablation study that demonstrates the effectiveness of the proposed method.
- Vocos achieves significant computational efficiency, which is crucial for real-time applications.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper does not adequately address the phase-related issues in speech, which could affect the model's performance and naturalness.
- There is a lack of clarity on how the Vocos model manages the phase estimation and wrapping, which is crucial for the model's effectiveness.
- The paper could benefit from more comprehensive comparisons with other state-of-the-art methods, including more recent and relevant models like the EnCodec.
- The paper lacks a detailed discussion on the limitations of the proposed method and potential future directions for research.
- The paper does not provide sufficient details on the training data and the specifics of the training process, which could affect the reproducibility and transparency of the research.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors provide more details on how the Vocos model manages phase estimation and wrapping?
- How does the Vocos model compare to other state-of-the-art methods, particularly in terms of computational efficiency and naturalness?
- Could the authors elaborate on the potential limitations of the proposed method and suggest future directions for research?
- What are the specifics of the training data and the training process used for Vocos? How does this compare to the training methods used for other models like BigVGAN and HiFi-GAN?
- How does the Vocos model perform in scenarios where the input is not in the form of mel-spectrograms, such as raw waveform audio or other spectrogram representations?
- Can the authors provide more insights into the computational efficiency of Vocos, particularly how it compares to other methods in terms of real-time performance and hardware requirements?
