 **Summary:**
The paper investigates robust reinforcement learning (RL) in the context of action robust Markov Decision Processes (MDPs), focusing on the probabilistic policy execution uncertainty. It introduces the Action Robust Reinforcement Learning with Certificates (ARRLC) algorithm, which aims to minimize regret and achieve sample complexity bounds. The algorithm is designed to handle adversarial actions and is theoretically analyzed for its performance. The paper also includes numerical experiments to validate the algorithm's effectiveness. Despite its theoretical contributions, the paper is criticized for its lack of novelty, limited scope of comparison, and insufficient empirical evidence to support its claims.

**Strengths:**
- The paper addresses an important and relevant problem of action robust RL, focusing on the probabilistic policy execution uncertainty, which is crucial for robust RL.
- It introduces a novel algorithm, ARRLC, which is designed to handle adversarial actions and achieves minimax optimal regret and sample complexity.
- The paper provides theoretical guarantees for the algorithm, including regret and sample complexity bounds, which are crucial for robust RL.
- The paper is well-structured, making it easy to follow, and the algorithm is clearly explained.
- The paper includes numerical experiments that demonstrate the effectiveness of the ARRLC algorithm, showing that it outperforms non-robust RL algorithms and converges faster than robust TD algorithms in the presence of action perturbations.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks novelty as it primarily builds upon existing methods like ORLC and does not introduce significant new theoretical insights or practical improvements.
- The empirical evaluation is limited, with insufficient comparisons to other robust RL algorithms, which could have provided a more robust validation of the proposed method.
- The paper does not adequately address the practical implications of the probabilistic policy execution uncertainty, which might limit the generalizability of the findings.
- The theoretical analysis and proofs are not convincingly robust, with some claims and proofs appearing overly simplistic or not adequately justified.
- The paper's writing and organization could be improved, particularly in terms of clarity and coherence, especially in the introduction and related work sections.

**Questions:**
- How does the ARRLC algorithm compare to other robust RL algorithms in terms of regret and sample complexity?
- Can the authors provide more detailed comparisons with other robust RL algorithms, especially in terms of empirical performance?
- How does the ARRLC algorithm handle different types of action robust MDPs, such as those with different transition and reward functions at each step?
- Could the authors clarify the practical implications of the probabilistic policy execution uncertainty and provide real-world examples where this uncertainty might occur?
- How does the ARRLC algorithm ensure robustness against different types of adversarial actions, and what are the implications of using different adversarial policies?
- Can the authors provide a more detailed explanation of the theoretical results, particularly in the proofs and lemmas, to make the analysis more convincing and understandable?
