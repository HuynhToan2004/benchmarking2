 **Summary:**
The paper introduces an instance smoothing algorithm that leverages Total Variation (TV) regularization to disentangle structural information from detailed features in images, aiming to enhance robustness against noise and adversarial attacks. The proposed method employs a differential inclusion to generate a regularized image path from coarse to fine scales, facilitating the disentanglement of structural and detailed information. The algorithm is validated through extensive experiments on datasets such as CIFAR10, miniImageNet, and ImageNet, demonstrating improvements in robustness and explainability. Despite its innovative approach, the paper's technical novelty is questioned, and concerns are raised about the clarity of presentation and the depth of experimental validation.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces an instance smoothing algorithm that effectively disentangles structural information from detailed features in images, which is crucial for robustness against noise and adversarial attacks.
- The use of Total Variation (TV) regularization in the differential inclusion to generate a regularized image path from coarse to fine scales is a novel and effective approach for disentangling structural and detailed information.
- The paper is well-written, with clear and detailed explanations of the proposed method and its implementation, which aids in understanding the approach and its potential applications.
- Extensive experiments are conducted on various datasets, demonstrating the effectiveness of the proposed method in enhancing robustness and explainability, particularly in low-resolution and adversarial scenarios.
- The paper provides a detailed analysis of the frequency domain, which helps in understanding the impact of the proposed method on the learning of low-frequency features.

**Weaknesses:**
- The technical novelty of the paper is limited, as the core idea of using Total Variation (TV) regularization and differential inclusion is not new, and the paper lacks a clear differentiation from existing methods such as the Split Bregman ISS.
- The paper suffers from a lack of clarity in presentation, with some sections being difficult to follow, and a need for better organization and explanation of the methodology and results.
- The experimental validation is insufficient, particularly in terms of the scale and diversity of the datasets used. The paper could benefit from more comprehensive testing on larger, more varied datasets to demonstrate the generalizability of the proposed method.
- There is a lack of detailed analysis on the computational complexity and memory requirements of the proposed method, which is crucial for practical implementation in real-world scenarios.
- The paper lacks a thorough discussion on the limitations of the proposed method and does not provide sufficient comparisons with existing methods, which could help in better understanding the advantages and disadvantages of the proposed approach.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors clarify the technical novelty of their approach and how it differs from existing methods such as the Split Bregman ISS?
- How does the proposed method perform when applied to larger-scale datasets like ImageNet, and what are the computational and memory requirements for such applications?
- Could the authors provide more detailed comparisons with existing methods to better establish the advantages of their approach?
- Can the authors provide a more detailed analysis of the computational complexity and memory requirements of the proposed method?
- How does the proposed method perform in scenarios where the image quality is not initially high, such as in medical imaging or other specialized applications?
- Can the authors discuss the limitations of their approach and how these might affect the practical implementation and effectiveness of the method in real-world scenarios?
