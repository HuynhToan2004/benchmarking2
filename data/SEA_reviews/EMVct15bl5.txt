 **Summary:**
The paper investigates the stability of ResNet models through the lens of dynamical systems theory, proposing new metrics such as g-expansiveness and g-shadowing to assess the behavior of ResNets. The authors argue that these metrics can provide insights into the stability and robustness of ResNets, although the paper is criticized for its lack of novelty, unclear presentation, and limited applicability due to the assumptions made about the ResNet architecture. The experiments conducted are not convincing, and the theoretical contributions are seen as insufficiently grounded in existing literature.

**Strengths:**
- The paper provides a novel perspective on analyzing the stability of ResNet models through the lens of dynamical systems, which is a significant contribution to the field.
- The introduction of new metrics, such as g-expansiveness and g-shadowing, is a valuable addition to the literature, potentially aiding in the evaluation of ResNet models.
- The paper is well-written and presents its ideas clearly, making it accessible to readers.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks originality as it heavily relies on existing definitions and concepts from dynamical systems literature without sufficient novelty or significant advancement.
- The presentation of the paper is poor, with numerous typographical errors, unclear figures, and inconsistencies in the use of mathematical symbols and notation.
- The experiments conducted are not convincing, with insufficient details provided on the experimental setup and results, which undermines the credibility of the findings.
- The paper makes strong assumptions about the ResNet architecture that may not hold in practical scenarios, limiting the generalizability of the results.
- The theoretical contributions are insufficiently grounded in existing literature, and the paper fails to adequately cite relevant works, which weakens its scholarly rigor.
- The paper's contributions do not seem to be novel or significant enough to warrant acceptance, especially given the lack of clarity and depth in the presentation and the experimental results.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors clarify the definitions and notations used in the paper, particularly in the definitions of g-expansiveness and g-shadowing?
- How do the authors justify the strong assumptions made about the ResNet architecture, and what implications do these assumptions have for the generalizability of the findings?
- Could the authors provide more detailed experimental results, including the training and testing accuracies for different datasets and architectures?
- How do the authors address the concerns regarding the novelty and originality of their contributions, especially in light of the existing literature on dynamical systems and ResNets?
- Can the authors explain the rationale behind the choice of metrics and their relevance to the stability and robustness of ResNet models?
