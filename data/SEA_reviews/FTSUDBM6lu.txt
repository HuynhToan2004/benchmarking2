 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel method for explaining the decision-making process of Convolutional Neural Networks (CNNs) by utilizing a Patch Ranking Map (PRM) to highlight top-ranked image patches associated with specific decisions. This approach involves a feature selection layer to identify critical features, which are then used to construct the PRM. The method is demonstrated on a dataset of Alzheimer's MRI scans, showing that the top-ranked patches are consistent across different feature selection methods. The paper also explores the utility of this method in other domains, such as medical diagnosis, where the interpretability of CNNs is crucial. However, the paper suffers from clarity issues, lacks detailed comparative analysis, and does not adequately address the generalizability of the findings beyond the specific dataset used.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel method for explaining the decisions of Convolutional Neural Networks (CNNs) by using a Patch Ranking Map (PRM) to highlight top-ranked image patches associated with specific decisions.
- The method is demonstrated to be effective on a specific dataset of Alzheimer's MRI scans, showing that the top-ranked patches are consistent across different feature selection methods.
- The paper is well-organized and easy to follow, with a clear explanation of the proposed method and its application.
- The method's utility in medical diagnosis, where the interpretability of CNNs is crucial, is highlighted.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks clarity in several areas, including the definitions and roles of various matrices and maps used in the method.
- There is a lack of detailed comparative analysis with other state-of-the-art methods for explaining CNN decisions, such as Grad-CAM and other attention-based methods.
- The paper does not adequately address the generalizability of the findings beyond the specific dataset used, which limits the broader applicability of the method.
- The paper does not provide a clear explanation of how the method can be applied in other domains or how it compares to other methods in terms of computational efficiency and effectiveness.
- The paper lacks a thorough discussion on the limitations of the proposed method and potential negative societal impacts.
- The experimental validation is limited to a single dataset, and the method's effectiveness on other datasets or in other application domains is not demonstrated.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors clarify the definitions and roles of the various matrices and maps used in the method, particularly the patch ranking matrix P and the patch ranking numbers pij?
- How does the proposed method compare to other state-of-the-art methods for explaining CNN decisions, such as Grad-CAM and other attention-based methods?
- How can the method be applied in other domains beyond medical diagnosis, and what are the potential negative societal impacts of using this method in different contexts?
- Can the authors provide more details on the computational efficiency and effectiveness of the method compared to other methods, and discuss any potential limitations or challenges in applying the method in different contexts?
- How does the proposed method handle the issue of interpretability in CNNs, especially in terms of ensuring that the selected features are meaningful and not just random or spurious correlations?
- Can the authors provide more details on the experimental setup and results, including the performance metrics used and the statistical significance of the findings?
