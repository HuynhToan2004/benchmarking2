 **Summary:**
The paper introduces Batch Styles Standardization (BSS), a novel Fourier-based method aimed at enhancing domain-invariance in Self-Supervised Learning (SSL) methods for Unsupervised Domain Generalization (UDG). BSS standardizes the style of images within a batch, reducing spurious correlations and improving domain-invariance without requiring domain labels or domain-specific network components. The method is evaluated on various UDG datasets and shows promising results, outperforming or matching other UDG methods. Despite its strengths, concerns are raised about the clarity in explaining the method's contributions, the choice of baselines, and the generalizability of the results.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel approach, Batch Styles Standardization (BSS), which is a simple yet effective method for enhancing domain-invariance in Self-Supervised Learning (SSL) methods for Unsupervised Domain Generalization (UDG).
- BSS is a Fourier-based method that standardizes the style of images within a batch, eliminating the need for domain labels or domain-specific network components, making it a versatile and scalable solution.
- The paper is well-written, clear, and easy to follow, with extensive experiments conducted on various UDG datasets, demonstrating the effectiveness of BSS in improving domain-invariance and enhancing performance on unseen domains.
- The method is simple and can be easily integrated with existing SSL methods, offering a practical and flexible approach to UDG.
- The paper provides a detailed analysis of the underlying mechanisms contributing to BSS's effectiveness, which is crucial for understanding its impact and potential for further research.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks clarity in explaining the contributions of BSS, particularly how it reduces spurious correlations and improves domain-invariance. The method's novelty is not clearly distinguished from existing methods like Fourier-based Augmentations (FA).
- The choice of baselines in the experiments is not well-justified, and some important comparisons with relevant methods are missing, which could affect the validity of the results.
- The paper does not adequately address the generalizability of the results, as the experiments are limited to a few datasets, and the results may not be applicable to other datasets or domains.
- There are some minor presentation issues, such as unclear figures and typos, which could detract from the paper's professionalism and readability.
- The paper does not discuss potential negative societal impacts, which is a significant omission in ethical considerations.

**Questions:**

- How does BSS perform when combined with other SSL methods like BYOL, and what are the implications for the model's performance and generalizability?
- In the experiments, why was the choice of baselines limited to a few methods, and why were some potentially relevant comparisons not included?
