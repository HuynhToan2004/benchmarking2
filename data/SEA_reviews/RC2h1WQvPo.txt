 **Summary:**
The paper explores the use of space-filling vector quantization (SFVQ) to analyze the latent space of pretrained GANs, specifically StyleGAN2 and BigGAN, to discover interpretable directions in the latent space. SFVQ is a novel approach that does not require labeled data or hyperparameter tuning, and it is applied to various image datasets to demonstrate its effectiveness in finding meaningful directions in the latent space. The paper also compares the SFVQ method with the GANSpace method and shows that SFVQ can capture more meaningful directions, although its application is currently limited to GANs.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel application of the Space-filling vector quantizer (SFVQ) to interpret the latent space of pretrained GANs, which is a significant advancement in the field of generative models.
- The method does not require labeled data or hyperparameter tuning, making it a more straightforward and accessible approach compared to other methods.
- The paper provides a clear and concise presentation of the methodology and results, with a focus on the interpretability of latent space directions, which is a critical aspect of generative models.
- The experimental results demonstrate that the proposed method can discover meaningful directions in the latent space of GANs, which is a significant contribution to the field.
- The paper is well-written, with a clear structure and a good balance between methodology and results, making it accessible to a broad audience.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper primarily focuses on GANs and does not explore the application of SFVQ to other types of generative models, which could limit the generalizability of the findings.
- The paper lacks a detailed comparison with other methods, such as those using principal component analysis (PCA), which could provide a more comprehensive evaluation of the proposed method.
- The paper does not sufficiently discuss the limitations of the proposed method, particularly in terms of its applicability to different types of generative models and its sensitivity to the choice of hyperparameters.
- The paper could benefit from more extensive experiments, especially on a broader range of datasets, to validate the effectiveness of the proposed method across different scenarios.
- The paper could be improved by including more visualizations and results in the main text to enhance the clarity and impact of the findings.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors provide a more detailed comparison of SFVQ with other methods, such as PCA, to better understand the relative strengths and weaknesses of each approach?
- How does the proposed method compare in terms of computational efficiency and interpretability with other methods like GANSpace, especially in terms of the number of parameters used?
- Can the authors discuss the limitations of the proposed method in more detail, particularly in terms of its applicability to different types of generative models and its sensitivity to hyperparameter choices?
- Would it be possible to include more extensive experiments, such as those on a broader range of datasets, to validate the effectiveness of the proposed method in different scenarios?
- Could the authors provide more visualizations and results in the main text to enhance the clarity and impact of the findings?
