 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel method for simulating physical phenomena at arbitrary locations in space and time using a double dynamical system formulation. The first system predicts anchor states from sparse observations, while the second system uses these anchor states to estimate the dense solution at the query point. The approach is supported by theoretical guarantees and demonstrates superior performance in space and time continuity compared to existing methods. The methodology involves an auto-regressive process that learns the dynamics of a latent space, followed by a state observer that leverages these dynamics to predict the solution at any point in space and time. The paper includes extensive experiments and theoretical analysis, showcasing the effectiveness of the proposed method in various physical scenarios.
