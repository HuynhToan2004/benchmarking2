 **Summary:**
The paper introduces Mol-Instructions, a novel dataset specifically designed for instruction tuning of large language models (LLMs) in the biomolecular domain. Mol-Instructions comprises three main components: molecule-oriented instructions, protein-oriented instructions, and biomolecular text instructions, each with distinct challenges and applications. The dataset is derived from various sources, including human-generated descriptions, existing data, and automated text generation, and undergoes rigorous quality control measures. The paper demonstrates that Mol-Instructions enhances the performance of LLMs on biomolecular tasks, showing improvements over baseline models in tasks such as molecular property prediction and protein function identification. Despite some concerns about the dataset's generalizability and the specificity of its tasks, the paper provides a valuable resource for advancing research in biomolecular studies and drug discovery.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces Mol-Instructions, a novel dataset specifically designed for instruction tuning of LLMs in the biomolecular domain, which is a significant step forward in advancing research in this area.
- The dataset is comprehensive, covering a wide range of biomolecular tasks and providing a large amount of instruction data, which is crucial for training LLMs to perform complex biomolecular tasks.
- The dataset is well-structured and includes detailed explanations of the dataset construction process, making it accessible and usable for other researchers in the field.
- The paper provides a thorough evaluation of the Mol-Instructions dataset, demonstrating its effectiveness in enhancing the performance of LLMs on biomolecular tasks.
- The authors have made efforts to ensure the quality of the dataset, including rigorous quality control measures and the use of SELFIES for generating valid molecules.
- The paper is well-written, making it easy to follow, and the authors provide a detailed description of the dataset and its construction, which is beneficial for reproducibility and transparency.

**Weaknesses:**
- The dataset is highly specific to the biomolecular domain and may not generalize well to other domains, which could limit its broader applicability.
- The tasks included in the dataset are somewhat specific and may not be general enough to cover a wide range of biomolecular tasks, which could limit the dataset's utility in more diverse applications.
- The evaluation of the dataset is limited to a few models, which may not fully demonstrate the dataset's effectiveness across a broader range of models.
- The paper lacks a detailed discussion on the limitations of the dataset, including its generalizability and the potential for biases in the data.
- The paper does not provide a detailed comparison with other existing datasets in the field, which could help contextualize the contributions of Mol-Instructions.
- There is a lack of discussion on the potential negative societal impacts of the dataset, which is an important consideration for the ethical use of AI in scientific research.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors provide more details on the quality control measures used to ensure the accuracy and reliability of the data in the dataset?
- How do the authors ensure that the dataset is generalizable to a broader range of biomolecular tasks beyond those specifically included in the dataset?
- What are the potential negative societal impacts of the Mol-Instructions dataset, and how are the authors addressing these concerns?
- Could the authors discuss the potential for biases in the dataset and how these were addressed during the data collection and preprocessing stages?
- How do the authors plan to update and expand the Mol-Instructions dataset to keep pace with the latest developments in biomolecular research and drug discovery?
- Can the authors provide more details on the tasks included in the dataset and how these were selected to ensure a comprehensive coverage of the biomolecular domain?
