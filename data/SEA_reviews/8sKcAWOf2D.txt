 **Summary:**
The paper investigates the impact of fine-tuning on the internal mechanisms of language models, specifically focusing on entity tracking. It identifies that fine-tuning enhances the model's ability to handle positional information, which is crucial for entity tracking. The study employs methods like DCM and CMAP to analyze the mechanisms of models like Llama-7B and its fine-tuned versions, Vicuna-7B and Goat-7B. The findings suggest that despite minor changes in the circuit design, the models perform the same tasks with improved efficiency. The paper also introduces a new method, CMAP, to analyze the effects of fine-tuning on model components.

**Strengths:**
- The paper addresses a crucial and relevant problem in the field of machine learning, specifically focusing on how fine-tuning impacts the internal mechanisms of models.
- The paper is well-written, clear, and easy to follow, making complex concepts accessible to a broader audience.
- The authors have conducted a thorough analysis using both established and new methods like DCM and CMAP, providing a comprehensive understanding of the effects of fine-tuning on model components.
- The findings of the study are novel and significant, contributing to a better understanding of how fine-tuning affects model performance and functionality.
- The paper introduces a new method, CMAP, which could be beneficial for future research in the field.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper's scope is limited to a specific task, which might not generalize well to other tasks or models, raising concerns about the scalability and applicability of the findings.
- The paper's claims about the generalizability of the findings to other models or tasks are not convincingly supported, which could limit the impact of the research.
- The methodology used in the paper, particularly the identification of circuit components and their functionality, is not sufficiently novel or well-explained, relying heavily on existing methods without a clear differentiation.
- The paper's focus on a single task and model family (LLaMA-7B and its fine-tuned variants) might not adequately address the broader implications of fine-tuning across different models and tasks.
- There is a lack of clarity and detail in some sections of the paper, particularly in the methodology and results sections, which could hinder understanding and reproducibility of the findings.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors clarify whether the findings of this study are applicable to other models or tasks beyond those specifically tested?
- How do the authors justify the choice of the box tracking task for their study, and what implications does this choice have for the generalizability of the findings?
- Could the authors provide more details on the methodology used in identifying circuit components and their functionality, particularly how these components were selected and what criteria were used?
- The paper claims that the same circuit is present in both the original and fine-tuned models. Can the authors elaborate on how this circuit is defined and how it contributes to the observed performance improvements?
- How do the authors address the potential limitations of their study, such as the scalability of their findings to other models or tasks, and the generalizability of their methods to other types of data or tasks?
