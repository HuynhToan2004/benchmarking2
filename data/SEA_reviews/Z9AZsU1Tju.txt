 **Summary:**
The paper introduces the Information-Theoretic Hierarchical Perception (ITHP) model, a novel approach to multimodal learning that integrates information from different modalities using a hierarchical information processing framework. The model is designed to compress and extract relevant information from the primary modality while retaining as much information as possible from other modalities. The ITHP model is evaluated on various datasets including MUStARD, CMU-MOSI, and CMU-MOSEI, demonstrating improvements over existing models like DeBERTa. The paper also discusses the neuroscience-inspired aspects of the model, though these are somewhat disconnected from the core technical contributions. The experimental results showcase the model's effectiveness in handling multimodal data, particularly in sentiment analysis tasks.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written and easy to follow, with clear explanations of the proposed methods and their applications.
- The ITHP model is a novel approach to multimodal learning, integrating information from different modalities using a hierarchical information processing framework.
- The model shows good performance in multimodal sentiment analysis tasks, particularly in sentiment analysis tasks, outperforming existing models like DeBERTa.
- The paper provides a comprehensive evaluation of the model across various datasets, demonstrating its effectiveness in handling multimodal data.
- The authors have made significant efforts to ensure reproducibility by providing detailed information on experimental setups and model configurations.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a thorough discussion on the neuroscience aspects, which are only marginally related to the core technical contributions.
- There is a lack of novelty in the proposed method, as it closely resembles existing methods such as the Deep Variational Information Bottleneck (DIB).
- The paper does not adequately address the computational efficiency of the model, which might be a concern for practical applications.
- The experimental results are not sufficiently robust, with some results showing only marginal improvements over existing models.
- The paper does not sufficiently discuss the limitations of the model, such as its reliance on a preset order of modalities and the potential impact of this on performance.

**Questions:**
- Could you clarify the neuroscience aspects of the model and how they contribute to the core technical contributions?
- How does the ITHP model compare to other models in terms of computational efficiency and scalability, especially in handling larger and more complex datasets?
- Can you provide more details on the model's performance with different orders of modalities, particularly when the primary modality does not provide sufficient information?
- How does the ITHP model handle different modalities with varying levels of information richness?
- Can you discuss the potential applications of the ITHP model in other multimodal learning tasks beyond sentiment analysis?
