 **Summary:**
The paper explores the vulnerabilities in the encoder module of latent diffusion models (LDMs) and proposes a novel protection method using Score Distillation Sampling (SDS) to enhance the robustness of LDMs against diffusion-based mimicry attacks. The authors demonstrate that the encoder is more susceptible to attacks, leading to significant perturbations in the latent space, while the denoiser remains robust. The proposed SDS method aims to reduce computational overhead and memory usage by 50% without compromising the effectiveness of the protection. The paper also presents a new approach to minimizing semantic loss to generate more natural perturbations, which are less noticeable to humans. The findings are substantiated through extensive experiments, showcasing the practicality and effectiveness of the proposed methods.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, clear, and easy to follow, with a comprehensive analysis of the vulnerabilities in the encoder module of latent diffusion models (LDMs).
- The proposed Score Distillation Sampling (SDS) method is novel and effective, reducing computational overhead and memory usage by 50% without compromising the effectiveness of the protection.
- The paper provides a detailed comparison of the proposed methods with existing approaches, demonstrating the effectiveness and efficiency of the proposed methods.
- The findings on the bottleneck of attacks against LDMs and the role of the encoder module in vulnerability are significant and contribute to the understanding of LDMs' security.
- The paper is well-organized, with clear explanations and visualizations that aid in understanding the complex concepts and results.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a comprehensive discussion on the limitations of the proposed methods, particularly concerning the generalizability of the findings to other types of diffusion models or different attack scenarios.
- There is a lack of discussion on the potential negative societal impacts of the proposed methods, which could be significant given the potential misuse of LDMs.
- The paper does not sufficiently address the computational cost and efficiency of the proposed methods, especially in comparison to existing approaches.
- The paper does not include a discussion on how the proposed methods could be adapted or extended to protect against attacks in other domains or scenarios.
- The paper's claims about the effectiveness of the proposed methods are not sufficiently supported by quantitative evidence, particularly in terms of the robustness of the denoiser module and the generalizability of the findings.

**Questions:**
- How does the proposed SDS method perform against other types of diffusion models, such as pixel space diffusion models?
- Can the proposed SDS method be adapted to protect against other types of attacks, such as those based on textual inversion or other forms of mimicry?
- How does the proposed method compare in terms of computational cost and efficiency to existing approaches, particularly in practical scenarios?
- Could the authors provide more detailed quantitative evidence to support the claims about the robustness of the denoiser module and the effectiveness of the proposed methods?
- How do the authors envision the application of their findings in practical scenarios, particularly in terms of the feasibility and cost-effectiveness of implementing the proposed methods in real-world systems?
