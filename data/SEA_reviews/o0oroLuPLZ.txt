 **Summary:**
The paper explores decision-focused learning (DFL) for forecast-informed linear optimization problems, proposing a new methodology that integrates neural networks with interior-point methods to address overfitting issues in forecasting. The authors introduce a novel SPO+ loss function and extend the reformulation framework to include neural network forecasters. This approach is demonstrated to outperform existing decision-focused benchmarks in a real-life electricity market scenario, showing a reduction in out-of-sample regret. The methodology involves an interior-point optimization method to solve the forecaster training procedure and tracks the validation regret of intermediate results, aiming to avoid overfitting.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, clear, and easy to follow, making it accessible to readers.
- The authors address the important problem of overfitting in forecast-informed linear optimization problems, proposing a novel methodology that integrates neural networks with interior-point methods to mitigate this issue.
- The proposed methodology is demonstrated to outperform existing decision-focused benchmarks, showing a reduction in out-of-sample regret in a real-life electricity market scenario.
