 **Summary:**
The paper introduces CLA-RA, a novel active learning framework aimed at optimizing the labeling process in crowdsourcing scenarios by selecting the most suitable annotators for specific instances. The framework incorporates an instance-dependent annotator confidence model and an annotator inference model based on past annotation knowledge. It utilizes an annotator agreement-disagreement based instance selection approach and an instance-dependent annotator confidence model to enhance classifier accuracy and reduce annotation efforts. Extensive experiments on 13 datasets demonstrate significant improvements over state-of-the-art multi-annotator active learning methods, highlighting the effectiveness of the approach in obtaining high-quality labeled data for training classifiers with minimal labeling costs and errors.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, making it easy to understand and follow.
- The problem is well-motivated, focusing on the practical issue of active learning with noisy annotators.
- The proposed method is technically sound, using an instance-dependent annotator confidence model and an annotator inference model based on past annotation knowledge.
- Extensive experiments have been conducted, demonstrating the effectiveness of the proposed method on 13 datasets.
- The paper addresses a significant problem in active learning, particularly in scenarios with multiple annotators, which is a relevant and challenging area of research.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed analysis of the computational cost associated with the proposed method.
- There is a need for more discussion on the assumptions made in the experiments, such as the availability of multiple annotators with different expertise levels and the impact of varying annotator accuracies.
- The paper could benefit from a more detailed description of the annotator model and its training process.
- The paper could provide more insights into the impact of varying annotator accuracies and the selection of annotators with the highest confidence.
- The paper could be improved by including more recent and relevant baselines for comparison.
- The experimental results do not show a significant improvement over some baselines, and the paper lacks a detailed analysis of the results.

**Questions:**
- How does the computational cost of the proposed method compare to other active learning methods?
- What are the assumptions made in the experiments, and how do they impact the results?
- How is the annotator model trained, and what are the implications of using different models for training?
- Could the authors provide more insights into the impact of varying annotator accuracies on the performance of the proposed method?
- How does the proposed method perform when the number of annotators is varied, and what are the implications of this on the results?
- Could the authors discuss the potential impact of using different models for training the annotator model on the performance of the proposed method?
- How does the proposed method compare to other active learning methods that do not rely on annotator models or knowledge bases?
