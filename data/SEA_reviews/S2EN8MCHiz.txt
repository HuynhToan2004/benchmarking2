 **Summary:**
The paper investigates the intrinsic dimensions (ID) of visual and language representations in the BLIP model, exploring how these dimensions vary across different layers and their impact on model pruning. It employs the TwoNN algorithm to estimate IDs, revealing distinct characteristics for each modality. The study also examines the correlation between ID and layer-wise importance, suggesting that ID can be used to guide pruning strategies. The paper presents a comprehensive analysis of IDs in multimodal learning, highlighting the differences in prunability between vision and language modalities. However, concerns are raised about the generalizability of the findings to other models and the methodological approach, particularly the use of the TwoNN algorithm and its assumptions.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, clear, and easy to follow, with a comprehensive literature review and a clear explanation of the experimental setup.
- It introduces a novel application of the TwoNN algorithm to the BLIP model, providing new insights into the intrinsic dimensions (IDs) of multimodal models.
- The paper provides a detailed analysis of the IDs of different layers in the BLIP model, which could be useful for understanding the intrinsic properties of multimodal models.
- The paper is the first to study the ID of a large-scale multimodal pre-training model, which is a significant contribution to the field.
- The authors propose an importance metric based on ID, which shows superior performance for multimodal model pruning, demonstrating the practical applicability of the findings.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper's claims about the hunchback shape of IDs and their correlation with model performance are not convincingly supported by the data presented.
- The paper does not adequately address the discrepancies between the IDs of different layers and the overall model performance, which could undermine the validity of the conclusions drawn.
- The methodology used, particularly the TwoNN algorithm, is not well-justified, and its assumptions may not hold true for the high-dimensional data in the BLIP model.
- The paper's scope is limited to the BLIP model, raising questions about the generalizability of the findings to other multimodal models.
- There is a lack of discussion on the potential negative societal impacts of the research, which is a significant omission in the ethical considerations section.
- The paper does not adequately compare its results with other models or methods, which could limit the understanding of the uniqueness and significance of the findings.

**Questions:**
- Can you clarify the discrepancies between the IDs of different layers and the overall model performance, particularly why the IDs of the last layer do not consistently correlate with performance?
- How does the TwoNN algorithm estimate the ID of each layer, and what assumptions does it make about the data?
- Could you provide more details on the computational efficiency of the TwoNN algorithm in estimating IDs for high-dimensional data?
- How do the authors justify the use of the TwoNN algorithm for estimating IDs in the BLIP model, given its assumptions about data density?
- In light of the findings on the prunability of different modalities, how do you explain the observed differences in performance when pruning different parts of the model?
- How do the authors address the potential limitations of their methodology, particularly the assumptions made by the TwoNN algorithm about the data?
