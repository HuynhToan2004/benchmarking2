 **Summary:**
The paper introduces FairerCLIP, a method aimed at debiasing zero-shot predictions from CLIP models by addressing both spurious correlations and intrinsic dependencies. FairerCLIP employs a kernel method, specifically the Hilbert-Schmidt Independence Criterion (HSIC), to measure and mitigate statistical dependencies between image and text representations. This approach allows for the handling of both ground-truth and pseudo-label scenarios, which is beneficial in various settings. The method's effectiveness is demonstrated through experiments on several datasets, showing improvements over existing methods. However, concerns were raised regarding the clarity of the experimental setup, the lack of detailed comparisons with other methods, and the potential limitations of the method in real-world applications.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, with clear and understandable notation and a clear objective function.
- The proposed method is simple, efficient, and effective, as demonstrated by empirical results.
- The method can handle both spurious correlations and intrinsic dependencies, which is a significant advantage over some existing methods.
- The use of kernel methods, particularly the Hilbert-Schmidt Independence Criterion (HSIC), is novel and provides a closed-form solution for the debiasing problem.
- The method can be trained with or without ground-truth labels, which is useful in various settings.
- The experiments are extensive and demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed method across different datasets.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a clear and detailed description of the experimental setup, particularly the datasets used and the training details.
- There is a lack of detailed comparison with other methods, particularly in terms of fairness metrics and computational efficiency.
- The paper does not provide sufficient detail on the choice of parameters and their impact on performance.
- The paper could benefit from a more comprehensive discussion on the limitations of the method, especially in real-world applications.
- The method's reliance on pseudo-labels could limit its effectiveness in scenarios where ground-truth labels are not available.
- The paper does not adequately address the computational complexity and memory requirements of the proposed method.
- The paper does not discuss the limitations of the method in detail, which is crucial for understanding its applicability and effectiveness in various scenarios.

**Questions:**
- How does the proposed method compare with other methods in terms of computational efficiency and memory requirements?
- Can the method be adapted to other large pre-trained models beyond CLIP?
- How does the method handle scenarios where ground-truth labels are not available, and what are the implications for the performance and effectiveness of the method?
- Could you provide more details on the choice of parameters and their impact on performance?
- How does the method perform in real-world applications, and what are the limitations of its application in such scenarios?
- Can you clarify the experimental setup, particularly the datasets used and the training details, to enhance the reproducibility and transparency of the results?
