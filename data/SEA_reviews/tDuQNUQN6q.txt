 **Summary:**
The paper investigates the online dynamic attention maintenance problem in large language models (LLMs), focusing on the efficient update and query of attention matrices. It proposes a data structure that supports amortized update time O(n^(1+\u03bd)Tmat(n,n^(1+\u03bd),d)/n^(1+\u03bd)) and worst-case query time O(n^(\u03bd)), assuming the HMV conjecture. The authors demonstrate that their algorithm is conditionally optimal, providing a novel approach to managing the computational complexity of attention matrices in LLMs. Despite its theoretical contributions, the paper lacks empirical validation, which is crucial for understanding the practical implications of the proposed methods.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel approach to managing the computational complexity of attention matrices in large language models (LLMs), addressing the online dynamic attention maintenance problem.
- The proposed algorithm is shown to be conditionally optimal, providing a theoretical foundation for the proposed methods.
- The paper is well-written, with clear definitions and explanations that aid in understanding the complex theoretical concepts.
- The problem formulation is novel, focusing on the online dynamic attention maintenance problem, which is a significant contribution to the field.
- The authors provide a theoretical analysis of the time complexity of the proposed algorithm, which is a significant contribution to the understanding of computational efficiency in LLMs.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks empirical validation, which is crucial for understanding the practical implications of the proposed methods.
- There is a significant gap between the theoretical results and their practical applicability, particularly in terms of the space complexity of the proposed data structure.
- The paper's contribution is not clearly articulated, particularly in how it builds upon or differs from existing literature.
- The paper's claims about the practical relevance and applicability of the proposed methods are not convincingly supported.
- The paper's focus on theoretical results without empirical validation limits the understanding of the practical implications and potential limitations of the proposed methods.
- The paper's organization and clarity could be improved, particularly in the presentation of the problem formulation and the technical details of the proposed methods.

**Questions:**
