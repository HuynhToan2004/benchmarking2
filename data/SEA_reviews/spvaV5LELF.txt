 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a new multimodal dataset, STEM, designed to evaluate the performance of neural models in STEM-related tasks. The dataset is comprehensive, encompassing 448 skills and 1,073,146 questions across various STEM subjects. The authors conduct experiments using zero-shot and finetuned models, comparing their performance to human capabilities. The results show that current models struggle to match the performance of elementary school students, highlighting the dataset's potential for advancing research in this area. Despite its strengths in dataset size and breadth of questions, the paper is critiqued for its limited analysis and lack of detailed experimental methodology, particularly in the dataset curation and model training processes.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a large-scale, multimodal dataset that covers a wide range of STEM skills, which is crucial for evaluating the performance of neural models in practical applications.
- The dataset is comprehensive, with over 1 million questions, which provides a robust testbed for evaluating the capabilities of models across various STEM domains.
- The paper is well-written, clear, and easy to follow, making it accessible to a broad audience.
- The dataset's curation is detailed, with the inclusion of meta information for each question, which aids in understanding the dataset's structure and composition.
- The experiments conducted are thorough, comparing zero-shot and finetuned models against human performance, which provides valuable insights into the current state of model capabilities.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed description of the dataset curation process, which could affect the reproducibility and transparency of the research.
- The dataset's quality and the process of selecting questions are not adequately discussed, which raises concerns about the reliability and validity of the dataset.
- The experiments conducted are limited in scope, with only a few models tested, which may not provide a comprehensive view of the state-of-the-art in neural model performance.
- There is a lack of analysis on the dataset's construction and the reasons behind the performance gaps observed between models and human performance.
- The paper does not provide sufficient details on the model training and evaluation processes, such as the specifics of dataset splits and model configurations.
- The paper does not discuss the potential biases in the dataset or the impact of these biases on model performance, which could limit the generalizability of the findings.

**Questions:**
- How were the questions selected for inclusion in the dataset, and what criteria were used to ensure their quality and relevance to the STEM skills being evaluated?
- Can you provide more details on the dataset curation process, including the sources of data and the methods used to ensure dataset quality?
- How were the models trained and evaluated, and what specific configurations were used for each model?
- What are the potential biases in the dataset, and how might these biases affect model performance?
- How do the authors plan to address the performance gaps observed between models and human performance, and what steps are being taken to improve model capabilities in STEM-related tasks?
- Could you provide more details on the dataset's construction, particularly the process of integrating different data sources and ensuring dataset quality?
