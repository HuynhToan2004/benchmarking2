 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel resonator-gated RNN (RG-RNN) architecture that integrates resonator neurons with LSTM units to enhance the detection and processing of periodic data patterns. The resonator neurons are designed to respond favorably to input signals that match their resonating frequency, thereby improving the model's ability to capture periodic components in time series data. The proposed method was evaluated on several benchmark tasks, showing some improvements over vanilla LSTMs, although the experimental results are limited to small-scale datasets and lack comprehensive comparisons with state-of-the-art models. The paper also discusses the potential of integrating resonators into other RNN architectures and explores their impact on model performance.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel resonator-gated RNN (RG-RNN) architecture, which integrates resonator neurons with LSTM units to enhance the detection and processing of periodic data patterns, offering a new inductive bias for gated RNNs.
- The resonator neuron model is well-motivated and supported by extensive experimental results, demonstrating the effectiveness of the proposed method.
- The paper is well-organized, easy to follow, and provides a clear and detailed explanation of the resonator-gated RNN, making it accessible to readers.
- The proposed method is simple to implement and can be integrated into existing RNN architectures, potentially enhancing their performance.
- The paper includes a variety of experiments and ablation studies, demonstrating the robustness and effectiveness of the proposed method across different tasks and datasets.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks comprehensive comparisons with state-of-the-art models, which could provide a better understanding of the proposed method's performance relative to current leading techniques.
- The experimental results are limited to small-scale datasets, which may not fully demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed method on more complex or larger-scale datasets.
- The paper does not provide sufficient theoretical analysis or intuitive explanations for the behavior of the resonator neurons, which could help in understanding the proposed method's effectiveness.
- The paper could benefit from a more detailed discussion on the potential limitations and societal impacts of the proposed method.
- The paper could be improved by including more detailed experimental setups, such as the number of hidden units in the LSTM and the number of parameters in the RG-RNN, to facilitate reproducibility and comparison with other models.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors provide more details on the training and inference times of the proposed method compared to vanilla LSTMs?
- How does the proposed method compare with other state-of-the-art models, especially in terms of performance and computational efficiency?
- Can the authors provide more intuitive explanations for the behavior of the resonator neurons and how they contribute to the detection and processing of periodic data patterns?
- How does the resonator neuron's response to input signals that match its resonating frequency enhance the model's ability to capture periodic components in time series data?
- Could the authors provide more experimental results on larger-scale datasets to demonstrate the scalability and effectiveness of the proposed method?
