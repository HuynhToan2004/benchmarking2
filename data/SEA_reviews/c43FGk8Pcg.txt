 **Summary:**
The paper introduces Denoising Diffusion Step-aware Models (DDSM), a novel approach to enhance the efficiency of denoising diffusion probabilistic models (DDPMs) by dynamically adapting the network size to the importance of each generative step. This method is based on an evolutionary search strategy that optimizes the network size for different steps, reducing computational overhead by up to 76% across various datasets including CIFAR-10, CelebA-HQ, LSUN-bedroom, AFHQ, and ImageNet. The authors demonstrate that this approach maintains high-quality image generation while significantly reducing computational costs. However, concerns are raised about the generalizability of the method to different datasets and the need for a more comprehensive ablation study to validate the effectiveness of the evolutionary search strategy.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel method of using different networks for different steps in the diffusion process, which is a significant advancement in the field.
- The approach is simple yet effective, as demonstrated by the experimental results showing a significant reduction in computational costs while maintaining high-quality image generation.
- The paper is well-written, making it easy to follow and understand the proposed methodology.
- The idea of step-aware models is innovative and could be applied to other domains beyond image generation.
- The methodology is validated through extensive experiments on multiple datasets, showing the effectiveness of the proposed method.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a comprehensive ablation study to validate the effectiveness of the evolutionary search strategy used to determine the optimal network size for each step.
- There is a need for more detailed comparisons with existing methods such as DDIM and DPM solver to fully understand the advantages and limitations of the proposed method.
- The paper does not discuss the potential impact of the evolutionary search strategy on the training time and computational resources required.
- The generalizability of the method to different datasets and the robustness of the results to variations in the search process are not thoroughly addressed.
- The paper could benefit from more detailed explanations and visualizations of the evolutionary search process and its impact on the final results.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors provide more details on the training time and computational resources required for the evolutionary search strategy?
- How does the proposed method compare in terms of computational efficiency and image quality to other existing methods like DDIM and DPM solver?
- Is there a possibility to extend the method to adaptively adjust the network size during training rather than using a pre-trained network?
- How does the proposed method perform when applied to different datasets, and what are the implications for the generalizability of the results?
- Could the authors provide more visualizations or comparisons to better illustrate the effectiveness of the proposed method, particularly in terms of the impact of different network sizes on image quality?
