 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel parameter-efficient fine-tuning (PEFT) method, Aligner, which utilizes a set of global learnable tokens that can be shared across all layers of a large language model (LLM). These tokens, which are used to modify the attention of every layer, are designed to be more efficient than existing methods like LoRA and LLaMA-Adapter, requiring significantly fewer parameters. The Aligner method is evaluated on both form alignment and value alignment tasks, demonstrating comparable or superior performance to these methods. The paper also explores the orthogonal nature of form adaptation and knowledge/reasoning within LLMs, providing insights into the model's internal mechanisms.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, clear, and easy to follow, with a clear motivation and well-defined tasks.
- The proposed method is simple yet effective, requiring only one or a few tokens for alignment, which is a significant reduction in parameter usage compared to existing methods.
- The method is evaluated on both form alignment and value alignment tasks, demonstrating competitive or superior performance to existing methods like LoRA and LLaMA-Adapter.
- The paper provides valuable insights into the internal mechanisms of LLMs, showing that they separate the handling of "form" and "knowledge" in a somewhat orthogonal manner.
- The experimental results are strong, demonstrating the method's effectiveness in both form and value alignment tasks.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed analysis of the differences between Aligner and other methods like LoRA and LLaMA-Adapter, which could help in understanding the specific contributions of Aligner.
- The method's applicability to reasoning tasks is not thoroughly explored, and the paper does not provide a clear explanation for the observed performance differences.
- The paper's claims about the orthogonal nature of form adaptation and knowledge/reasoning within LLMs are not convincingly supported by the experimental results.
- The method's efficiency is not clearly demonstrated, as the paper does not provide a detailed comparison of training times or memory usage.
- There is a lack of discussion on the method's limitations, particularly concerning its scalability and the potential impact of its design choices on the model's performance.

**Questions:**
- How does the proposed method compare to other PEFT methods like LoRA and LLaMA-Adapter in terms of performance and parameter efficiency?
- Can the authors provide a detailed analysis of the differences between Aligner and other methods, particularly in terms of parameter usage and performance?
- How does the Aligner method perform on reasoning tasks, and what are the implications of its performance in these tasks?
- Could the authors clarify the claims about the orthogonal nature of form adaptation and knowledge/reasoning within LLMs, possibly through additional experiments or theoretical analysis?
- How does the Aligner method handle the orthogonal nature of form adaptation and knowledge/reasoning within LLMs, and what are the implications of this design choice for the model's performance?
