 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel approach to change point detection in time series data using a variant of the Hidden Markov Model (HMM) termed TV-HMM. This model integrates a time-varying location transition matrix, which is different from traditional HMMs that employ a static transition matrix. The authors propose a variational EM algorithm to simultaneously detect change points and infer posterior distributions of regime parameters. The model's statistical consistency is established under Gaussian likelihood assumptions, and the paper includes theoretical convergence rate analysis and empirical evaluations. The method is compared against several baselines and shows promising results, although the paper lacks detailed comparisons with state-of-the-art methods and a broader range of datasets.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel approach to change point detection using a time-varying location transition matrix, which is different from traditional HMMs that use a static transition matrix.
- The proposed method is theoretically grounded, with the statistical consistency of change point location estimation established under a Gaussian likelihood assumption.
- The paper includes a thorough theoretical convergence rate analysis and empirical evaluations, demonstrating the method's effectiveness and robustness against model misspecification and outliers.
- The model's flexibility in handling different types of data distributions, such as Gaussian, Poisson, chi-squared, and exponential, is highlighted.
- The paper is well-written and easy to follow, with clear explanations of the methodology and results.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed comparison with state-of-the-art methods in change point detection, which could have strengthened the paper's position in the field.
- The experimental evaluation is limited to a few datasets, which may not adequately demonstrate the method's generalizability and applicability across different types of data.
- The computational complexity of the proposed method is not thoroughly discussed, which is crucial for understanding its practical applicability in real-world scenarios.
- The paper does not provide a detailed discussion on the limitations of the proposed method, which could help in understanding its scope and applicability.
- The paper's empirical results are not sufficiently convincing, with some results (e.g., in Table 2) showing high MSE values, which could affect the reliability of the method's performance claims.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors provide a more detailed comparison with state-of-the-art methods in change point detection to better establish the novelty and effectiveness of their proposed method?
- How does the proposed method perform in terms of computational complexity, especially in comparison to other methods in the literature?
- Can the authors provide a more detailed discussion on the limitations of the proposed method, including scenarios where it may not perform optimally?
- How does the proposed method handle the detection of change points in non-Gaussian distributions, and what are the implications of this for the method's applicability in real-world scenarios?
- Could the authors provide a more detailed discussion on the initialization process for the method, particularly in scenarios where the number of change points is unknown?
- How does the proposed method compare in terms of performance and computational efficiency with other methods that use similar approaches, such as those that also leverage variational inference or EM algorithms?
