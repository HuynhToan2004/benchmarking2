 **Summary:**
The paper introduces TOP-T, a method for template optimization aimed at detecting and estimating low-dimensional signals in high-dimensional spaces, particularly in the context of gravitational wave detection. It employs a non-parametric approach using kernel interpolation and PCA for embedding, which is trained using unrolled optimization. The method aims to address the computational inefficiencies of traditional matched filtering by optimizing templates on a low-dimensional manifold. Theoretical analysis supports the method's efficiency and convergence properties, although the practical applicability and the empirical validation are questioned.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces an innovative approach to template optimization for low-dimensional signals, which is a significant contribution to the field.
- The theoretical analysis provided is rigorous and insightful, offering a deep understanding of the method's behavior and its advantages over traditional matched filtering.
- The method is well-motivated, with a clear and concise presentation that effectively communicates the problem and the proposed solution.
- The paper demonstrates the method's applicability in real-world scenarios, such as gravitational wave detection, which adds to its relevance and practicality.
- The non-parametric extension of the method is a notable strength, allowing for the application in scenarios where analytical data models are unavailable.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a thorough comparison with other state-of-the-art methods, particularly in terms of computational efficiency and performance.
- The empirical validation is limited, with only one application scenario tested, which might not adequately demonstrate the method's versatility or effectiveness in other scenarios.
- The paper does not adequately address the scalability of the method with increasing intrinsic dimensions, which is a critical concern for practical applications.
- There are several typographical and formatting errors that need correction to enhance the paper's professionalism and readability.
- The paper could benefit from a more detailed discussion on the method's limitations, such as the scalability issues and the assumptions made in the theoretical analysis.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors provide a more detailed comparison with other state-of-the-art methods, particularly in terms of computational efficiency and performance?
- How does the method perform when applied to other types of data, such as images or audio signals?
- Can the authors elaborate on the scalability of the method with increasing intrinsic dimensions?
- How does the method handle scenarios where the signal space is not a manifold or is highly complex?
- In the gravitational wave detection scenario, how does the method perform when the signal space is not a manifold or is highly complex?
- Could the authors clarify the role and impact of the smoothing technique in the method's performance?
- How does the method compare to other optimization-based methods for signal detection, such as those mentioned in related literature?
