 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a benchmark for medical anomaly detection (BMAD), integrating six datasets from different medical domains and evaluating fifteen state-of-the-art anomaly detection algorithms. It employs three evaluation metrics to assess both pixel-level and sample-level anomaly detection performances. The benchmark aims to provide a standardized platform for fair comparisons and advancements in medical anomaly detection. Despite its comprehensive approach, the paper is criticized for its limited novelty, as it primarily combines existing datasets and methods without significant technical innovation. The benchmark's utility is also questioned due to its lack of technical depth and the absence of a dedicated discussion on the challenges and limitations of medical anomaly detection.

**Strengths:**
- The paper provides a comprehensive and detailed description of the BMAD benchmark, which includes six datasets from five different medical domains and 15 state-of-the-art (SOTA) anomaly detection algorithms.
- The authors have made significant efforts to reorganize and adapt the datasets to the unsupervised anomaly detection setting in computational medical imaging.
- The paper is well-written, easy to follow, and provides a well-structured codebase that supports the evaluation of 15 SOTA anomaly detection algorithms.
- The authors have conducted a comprehensive analysis of the algorithms' pros and cons on BMAD datasets, which will encourage researchers to develop better anomaly detection models for medical data.
- The paper includes a detailed evaluation of 15 state-of-the-art anomaly detection algorithms on BMAD datasets, providing valuable insights for future research in this field.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a dedicated discussion on the challenges and limitations of medical anomaly detection, which is crucial for advancing the field.
- The technical novelty of the paper is limited, as it primarily combines existing datasets and methods without significant technical innovation.
- The paper does not provide a detailed discussion on the limitations of the BMAD benchmark, which could affect the reliability and applicability of the results.
- The evaluation metrics used in the paper are not comprehensive enough, and the paper could benefit from including more metrics such as Dice coefficient.
- The paper does not discuss the ethical and fairness concerns related to the data used in the benchmark, which is a significant oversight.
- The paper lacks a discussion on the limitations of the BMAD benchmark, which is crucial for understanding the reliability and applicability of the results.
