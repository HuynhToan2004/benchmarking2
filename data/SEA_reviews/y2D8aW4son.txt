 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel memory-based forecasting method for multivariate time series using a student-pattern memory network, which integrates knowledge memory and episodic memory to capture both latent and evident channel dependencies. The method is evaluated on eight real-world datasets, demonstrating its effectiveness in capturing channel dependencies and improving forecasting accuracy. The authors propose a student-like pattern memory network to learn and recall channel dependencies, which are crucial for forecasting multivariate time series. The paper provides extensive experiments and ablation studies to support the effectiveness of the proposed method, although it lacks comparisons with some state-of-the-art methods and does not include code for reproducibility.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel memory-based forecasting method that effectively captures channel dependencies in multivariate time series forecasting, which is a significant advancement in the field.
- The use of knowledge memory and episodic memory to capture latent and evident channel dependencies is a novel approach that enhances the method's ability to understand complex patterns in time series data.
- The proposed student-pattern memory network is a general framework that can be applied to various deep learning models, making it a versatile tool for capturing channel dependencies.
- The paper provides extensive experiments and ablation studies to support the effectiveness of the proposed method, demonstrating its ability to outperform state-of-the-art methods in most settings.
- The visualization of student-like memory and additional model analysis in Figure 2 and Figure 3 further validate the effectiveness of the proposed method.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed comparison with state-of-the-art methods, particularly in terms of computational efficiency and memory requirements, which are crucial for practical deployment in real-world scenarios.
- There is a lack of clarity in the methodology, particularly in the descriptions of the knowledge memory and episodic memory, which could benefit from more detailed explanations and potentially, visual aids.
- The paper does not include the code, which is essential for reproducibility and further research by the community.
- The experimental results and their interpretations are not always clear or convincing, particularly in the ablation studies and the comparisons with other methods.
- The paper could benefit from a more comprehensive discussion on the limitations of the proposed method and potential areas for future research.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors provide more details on the computational efficiency and memory requirements of the proposed method compared to other state-of-the-art methods?
- How does the proposed method handle the issue of overfitting, especially in scenarios where the memory size is increased?
- Can the authors clarify the role and operation of the hard pattern selection strategy in the context of the proposed method?
- How does the proposed method compare to other methods that use memory mechanisms in time series forecasting, such as those mentioned in the related work section?
- Could the authors provide more details on the experimental setup, particularly the data preprocessing and the hyperparameters used in the ablation studies?
- What are the specific challenges or limitations of the proposed method, and how do these impact its practical application in real-world scenarios?
