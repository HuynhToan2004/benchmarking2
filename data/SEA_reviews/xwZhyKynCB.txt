 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel framework for generating and evaluating complex queries on knowledge graphs, specifically focusing on existential first-order queries with multiple variables (EFOk). The framework includes a dataset, EFOk-CQA, which extends the existing EFO-1-QA benchmark by incorporating multiple variables, thereby enhancing the complexity of queries. The dataset is designed to challenge existing models by including a variety of query types and topologies, aiming to advance the field of query answering in knowledge graphs. The paper also proposes a new set of metrics for evaluating model performance and demonstrates that existing models struggle with the new dataset, highlighting the need for improved models.

**Strengths:**
- The paper addresses an important problem of extending the existing EFO-1-QA benchmark to include multiple variables, which is a significant advancement in the field of query answering on knowledge graphs.
- The proposed framework is well-structured, with a clear and detailed explanation of the methodology, including the generation process, grounding, and evaluation protocol.
- The paper provides a comprehensive dataset, EFOk-CQA, which includes a variety of query types and topologies, challenging existing models and potentially leading to the development of more robust and generalizable models.
- The authors have conducted thorough experiments and provide detailed analysis, which is useful for understanding the challenges and potential solutions in the field of query answering on knowledge graphs.
- The paper is well-written, with clear explanations of the theoretical foundations and practical applications of the proposed methods, making it accessible to a broad audience.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper does not sufficiently differentiate its contributions from existing works, particularly the EFO-1-QA benchmark, which may lead to confusion about the novelty of the proposed dataset and methodology.
- The methodology for generating the dataset and the selection of parameters (such as the number of constants, existential variables, and edges) are not clearly explained, which could limit the reproducibility and understanding of the results.
- The paper lacks a detailed discussion on the limitations and potential negative societal impacts of the proposed framework, which could be crucial for assessing the broader implications of the work.
- The paper could benefit from a more detailed explanation of the assumptions and constraints used in the dataset generation, as well as a clearer definition and explanation of the metrics used for evaluation.
- The paper does not adequately address the scalability and computational complexity of the proposed framework, which could be a significant concern for practical applications.

**Questions:**
- How does the proposed dataset differ from the existing EFO-1-QA benchmark in terms of complexity and representation of queries?
- Could you provide more details on the methodology for generating the dataset, including the specific parameters and algorithms used?
- How do the authors ensure the quality and diversity of the queries in the dataset, and what steps are taken to prevent bias in the dataset?
- Can you explain the rationale behind the choice of metrics for evaluating model performance, and how these metrics compare to other existing metrics in the field?
- What are the potential negative societal impacts of the proposed framework, and how do the authors plan to mitigate these risks?
- Could you provide more details on the computational complexity and scalability of the proposed framework, and discuss any potential solutions for addressing these challenges?
