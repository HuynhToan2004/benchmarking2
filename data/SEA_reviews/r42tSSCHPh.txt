 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel method called "generation exploitation attack" to assess the robustness of open-source large language models (LLMs) against adversarial prompts. This method manipulates different generation strategies, including varying decoding hyperparameters and sampling methods, to disrupt model alignment and increase the misalignment rate across multiple LLMs. The authors propose a new alignment strategy named "generation-aware alignment" to mitigate these attacks. The study covers 11 open-source LLMs and evaluates the effectiveness of the proposed attack and alignment strategies. The paper also highlights the need for more comprehensive red teaming and better alignment before releasing LLMs.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, clear, and easy to follow, with a clear motivation and a comprehensive evaluation of the proposed method.
- The paper introduces a novel method to evaluate the robustness of LLMs against adversarial prompts, focusing on the generation strategies rather than the content of prompts.
- The proposed generation exploitation attack is effective, simple, and can be easily reproduced, showing a significant increase in misalignment rates across various LLMs.
- The paper provides a new alignment strategy named "generation-aware alignment" to mitigate the generation exploitation attack, demonstrating a reduction in misalignment rates.
- The experiments are conducted on a large number of open-source LLMs, and the results show that the proposed method is effective in attacking open-source LLMs.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed explanation of the "scorer" used in the experiments, which is crucial for understanding the methodology.
- There is no detailed explanation of the "generation-aware alignment" method, particularly how it works and how it mitigates the generation exploitation attack.
- The paper does not provide a detailed analysis of the reasons behind the vulnerability of open-source LLMs to the generation exploitation attack.
- The paper does not discuss the transferability of the generation exploitation attack to other models, which is a significant limitation.
- The proposed method of "generation-aware alignment" is not clearly explained, and its effectiveness is not adequately demonstrated.
- The paper does not provide a detailed explanation of how the "scorer" is trained and what it is trained on, which is crucial for understanding the methodology.

**Questions:**
- Can you provide more details on the "scorer" used in the experiments, including how it is trained and what it is trained on?
- How does the "generation-aware alignment" method work, and how does it mitigate the generation exploitation attack?
- Why are open-source LLMs more vulnerable to the generation exploitation attack compared to proprietary LLMs?
- Is there a way to evaluate the transferability of the generation exploitation attack to other models?
- How does the proposed method compare to other existing methods, especially in terms of effectiveness and efficiency?
- Can you provide more details on the experimental setup, including the number of samples used for training and the specifics of the human evaluation process?
