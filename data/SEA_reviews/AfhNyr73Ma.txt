 **Summary:**
The paper investigates the generalization error bounds for various zeroth-order optimization algorithms, including ZO-SGD, ZO-GD, and ZO-SVRG, under different gradient estimation methods such as 1-point, 2-point, and coordinate-wise. It proposes a unified framework to analyze these algorithms, providing theoretical insights into the generalization performance of zeroth-order optimization methods. The study extends existing results by considering a broader range of gradient estimators and applying these to various optimization algorithms. The paper is praised for its clarity and the comprehensiveness of its analysis, although concerns are raised about the novelty of the results and the practical implications of the theoretical findings.
