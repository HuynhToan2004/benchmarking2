 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel Multi-stage Decoupled Relational (MDR) knowledge distillation framework aimed at enhancing the effectiveness of relational knowledge transfer. The MDR framework decouples angle-wise and distance-wise information to address conflicts between these attributes, thereby improving transferring efficiency and distillation quality. This framework is evaluated across multiple image benchmarks, including CIFAR100, ImageNet, and Pascal VOC, demonstrating superior performance in various tasks such as classification, few-shot learning, transfer learning, and object detection. The adaptive stage selection strategy within the MDR framework helps in identifying the most effective stages for transferring relational knowledge. The paper also presents a comprehensive experimental evaluation, showcasing the framework's effectiveness and outperforming existing state-of-the-art methods.

**Strengths:**
- The paper presents a novel and innovative approach to knowledge distillation by leveraging multi-stage information and decoupling angle-wise and length-wise relational representation, which is a significant advancement in the field.
- The proposed method is clearly presented and well-motivated, with a focus on improving the distillation process by utilizing both angle-wise and distance-wise information, which is a novel approach.
- The experimental results are robust and demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed method, showing improvements over existing state-of-the-art methods.
- The paper provides a comprehensive experimental evaluation, covering various tasks and benchmarks, which is a strong point in validating the proposed method.
- The proposed method is intuitive and easy to understand, which is crucial for its adoption and implementation by other researchers.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed comparison with other advanced knowledge distillation methods, such as DKD, which could provide a clearer picture of the proposed method's advantages and limitations.
- The paper's writing and organization could be improved to enhance clarity and readability, particularly in the presentation of figures and tables.
- The paper does not provide sufficient implementation details, such as the number of stages used in the experiments and the specifics of the training process, which could limit the reproducibility and validation of the results.
- There is a lack of theoretical analysis or intuitive explanation for why the proposed method works, which could strengthen the paper's contribution to the field.
- The paper could benefit from a more thorough discussion on the limitations and potential negative societal impacts of the proposed method.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors provide more detailed comparisons with other advanced knowledge distillation methods, such as DKD, to highlight the advantages and limitations of the proposed method?
- How does the proposed method perform when combined with other distillation methods, such as ReviewKD, and what are the potential benefits or drawbacks of such combinations?
- Can the authors clarify the specifics of the training process, such as the number of stages used and the training hyperparameters, to enhance reproducibility and validation of the results?
- How does the proposed method perform in different network architectures, such as Transformer-based models, and what are the potential challenges or opportunities in applying the method to these models?
- Could the authors provide more insights into the theoretical underpinnings of the proposed method and its intuitive workings, perhaps through additional visualizations or experimental results?
