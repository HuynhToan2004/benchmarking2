 **Summary:**
The paper introduces GenCO, a novel framework that integrates deep generative models with combinatorial solvers to address combinatorial optimization problems in design. GenCO's approach involves training a generator to output a problem description, which is then optimized by a combinatorial solver to meet specific constraints. This method allows for the generation of diverse, feasible solutions that conform to user-defined constraints. The framework's application is demonstrated through experiments on Zelda game level generation and Warcraft map generation for path planning, showing improvements over existing methods. However, concerns are raised about the novelty of the approach, as similar methods have been previously explored, and the paper's experimental setup and the clarity of its presentation.

**Strengths:**
- The paper addresses a significant and timely problem of generating diverse solutions to combinatorial optimization problems while ensuring feasibility, which is crucial for applications in various domains such as game level generation and path planning.
- The proposed method, GenCO, integrates deep generative models with combinatorial solvers, offering a novel approach to handle discrete/combinatorial constraints and generate diverse, high-quality solutions that adhere to user-specified combinatorial properties.
- The paper is well-written, clearly articulating the motivation and the methodology, making it accessible to readers.
- The application of the proposed method to two different tasks demonstrates its versatility and adaptability, highlighting its potential in various domains.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a clear explanation of how the proposed method differs from existing approaches, particularly in terms of its novelty and its advantages over existing methods.
- The experimental setup is limited, with only two applications used to demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed method, which may not sufficiently validate its general applicability.
- There is a lack of clarity in the presentation, particularly in the explanation of the mathematical formulation and the role of different components such as the combinatorial loss and the projection layer.
- The paper does not adequately address the computational complexity and scalability of the proposed method, which is crucial for practical applications in large-scale problems.
- The paper does not provide sufficient detail on how the method is applied to specific problems, such as Zelda game level generation and Warcraft map generation, which could hinder understanding and replication of the results.

**Questions:**
- Can you clarify how the proposed method differs from existing approaches, particularly in terms of its novelty and advantages?
- How does the proposed method handle different types of combinatorial constraints, and what are the specific constraints addressed in the experiments?
- Could you provide more details on the computational complexity and scalability of the proposed method, especially in large-scale applications?
- How does the method ensure diversity in the generated solutions, and what measures are taken to prevent the generation of similar or identical solutions?
- Can you explain the role and effectiveness of the combinatorial loss in the training process, and how it influences the quality and diversity of the generated solutions?
- How does the method handle the integration of the combinatorial solver into the deep generative learning process, and what are the implications for the training efficiency and the quality of the generated solutions?
