 **Summary:**
The paper introduces VPG-C, a lightweight module designed to enhance the performance of multimodal large language models (MLLMs) in understanding demonstrative instructions. VPG-C utilizes a synthetic discriminative training strategy to fine-tune the model, focusing on the inference of missing visual details from interleaved images and text. This approach aims to improve the model's ability to follow complex instructions, demonstrated through a new benchmark, DEMON, which includes 31 tasks across various scenarios. The paper also discusses the limitations of existing MLLMs in handling demonstrative instructions and proposes a new training method to address these issues.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel lightweight module, VPG-C, which effectively enhances the performance of multimodal large language models (MLLMs) in understanding demonstrative instructions.
- The synthetic discriminative training strategy proposed in the paper is a significant advancement in fine-tuning the VPG-C module, eliminating the need for supervised demonstrative instruction data.
- The DEMON benchmark, a comprehensive benchmark for multimodal large language models, covers a wide range of scenarios and is a valuable resource for the community.
- The paper provides a detailed analysis of the VPG-C module, including its impact on performance when inserted at different layers of the LLM and the effects of synthetic training data.
- The writing is clear and easy to follow, making the paper accessible to a broad audience.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed explanation of the synthetic discriminative training strategy, which is crucial for understanding the effectiveness and efficiency of the proposed method.
- There is a noticeable absence of comparative analysis with other methods, such as the VPG-C-LinearVPG, which could provide a more robust evaluation of the proposed method.
- The paper does not adequately address the limitations of the VPG-C module, particularly concerning the scalability and applicability of the synthetic training strategy across different model architectures and training datasets.
- The paper's claims about the VPG-C module's ability to infer and complete missing visual details are not substantiated with sufficient evidence, and the experimental results are not convincingly superior to existing methods.
- The paper does not sufficiently discuss the limitations of the VPG-C module, particularly concerning the scalability and applicability of the synthetic training strategy across different model architectures and training datasets.

**Questions:**
- Could you provide more details on the synthetic discriminative training strategy, including the specifics of how the synthetic images are generated and how they are used in the training process?
- How does the VPG-C module perform when integrated with different language backbones, such as LLaMA2 and Vicuna-13B?
- Is there a possibility to apply the synthetic training strategy to other vision-language models, such as those that use different visual prompt generators (VPGs)?
- Can you clarify the role and impact of the synthetic training data on the performance of the VPG-C module? How does the performance change with varying amounts of synthetic training data?
- How does the VPG-C module handle different model architectures and training datasets? Is the synthetic training strategy scalable and applicable across different models and datasets?
- Could you provide more examples or case studies that demonstrate the effectiveness of the VPG-C module in inferring and completing missing visual details?
