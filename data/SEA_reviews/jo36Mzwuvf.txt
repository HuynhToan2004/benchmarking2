 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel method for enhancing time series forecasting by integrating a corruption model that generates correlated noise using Gaussian processes. This approach aims to improve the accuracy of forecasts by integrating a denoising model within the forecasting model, allowing for the correction of both coarse-grained and fine-grained behaviors. The methodology is evaluated on several datasets, showing significant improvements over traditional forecasting models and other denoising approaches. The paper also provides a theoretical framework and empirical evidence to support the use of Gaussian processes for generating smooth, correlated noise, which is more effective than isotropic Gaussian noise in capturing the temporal dynamics of time series data.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written and easy to follow, with clear illustrations and detailed methodology that enhances understanding of the proposed method.
- The proposed method of integrating Gaussian processes for generating correlated noise in time series forecasting is novel and innovative, showing potential for significant improvements in forecasting accuracy.
- The paper includes a comprehensive set of experiments and ablation studies that demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed method across various datasets and forecasting models.
- The use of Gaussian processes to model the corruption process is a significant contribution, providing a theoretical framework for understanding the dynamics of time series data and improving forecasting performance.
- The paper is well-structured, with clear explanations of the methodology, experimental setup, and results, making it accessible to readers with varying levels of expertise in the field.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed comparison with existing denoising models and other state-of-the-art forecasting models, which could provide a better understanding of the advantages and limitations of the proposed method.
- There is a need for more detailed discussions and comparisons with other denoising methods, such as diffusion models, to establish the superiority of the proposed method.
- The paper could benefit from a more rigorous evaluation of the computational efficiency and scalability of the proposed method, especially in terms of training and inference times.
- The paper could provide more insights into the specific types of noise that the proposed method is effective against and how it handles different types of noise in real-world scenarios.
- The experimental results are somewhat limited to univariate time series, and it would be interesting to see how the method performs on multivariate time series or time series with different noise types.
- The paper could benefit from a more detailed discussion on the theoretical foundations of using Gaussian processes for generating correlated noise and how this relates to the dynamics of time series data.

**Questions:**
- How does the proposed method compare with other denoising models, such as diffusion models, in terms of computational efficiency and forecasting accuracy?
- Can the proposed method be adapted to handle different types of noise, such as seasonal or trend-related noise, and how does it perform in such scenarios?
- How does the proposed method handle the trade-off between computational efficiency and forecasting accuracy, especially in large-scale applications?
- Can the authors provide more insights into the specific types of noise that the proposed method is effective against and how it handles different types of noise in real-world scenarios?
- How does the proposed method compare with other state-of-the-art forecasting models, such as transformer-based models, in terms of forecasting accuracy and computational efficiency?
