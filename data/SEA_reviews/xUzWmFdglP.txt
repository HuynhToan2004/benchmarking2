 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel privacy amplification mechanism, MMCC, designed to enhance the privacy guarantees of matrix mechanisms in differential privacy (DP). It extends the analysis of privacy amplification by shuffling to matrix mechanisms, which are crucial in various applications such as DP-FTRL. The authors demonstrate that MMCC can improve privacy amplification, particularly in scenarios where noise is correlated, and show that it can match the privacy guarantees of DP-SGD in certain conditions. The paper also includes empirical evaluations that validate the theoretical claims, showing that MMCC can provide tighter privacy guarantees than existing methods. However, the paper is criticized for its limited applicability to specific matrix mechanisms and its reliance on certain assumptions that may not hold in all practical scenarios.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel privacy amplification mechanism, MMCC, which is a significant advancement in the field of differential privacy.
- The authors have provided both theoretical and empirical evidence to support the effectiveness of MMCC, demonstrating its ability to enhance privacy guarantees in matrix mechanisms.
- The paper is well-written, clear, and easy to follow, with a comprehensive set of experiments that validate the theoretical claims.
- MMCC is shown to be effective in scenarios where noise is correlated, which is a common problem in practical applications of differential privacy.
- The paper contributes to the understanding of privacy amplification in matrix mechanisms, which is a significant and under-explored area in differential privacy.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper is limited in its applicability to specific types of matrix mechanisms, which may not include the most commonly used mechanisms in practice, such as those with a full support matrix.
- The proofs and assumptions made in the paper are not always clear or well-explained, which could limit the understanding and reproducibility of the results.
- The paper does not sufficiently address the practical implications of the assumptions made, such as the independence of xi across rounds, which may not hold in real-world scenarios.
- The paper could benefit from a more thorough discussion of the limitations and potential negative societal impacts of the proposed method.
- The paper's claims about the general applicability of MMCC to all matrix mechanisms are not convincingly supported, as the proofs and assumptions are limited to specific types of mechanisms.

**Questions:**
- Could you clarify how MMCC can be applied to matrix mechanisms that are not lower triangular?
- How does the privacy amplification provided by MMCC compare to other existing methods, such as those mentioned in related works?
- In the proofs and assumptions made, particularly in Theorem 3.1 and the independence of xi across rounds, could you provide more details or clarifications?
- Can you explain the choice of parameters and the implications of these choices on the privacy and utility guarantees provided by MMCC?
- How does the paper address the practical implications of the assumptions made, such as the independence of xi across rounds, which may not hold in real-world scenarios?
- Could you provide more details on the implementation and practical use cases of MMCC, particularly in scenarios where it could be applied to enhance privacy in existing systems?
