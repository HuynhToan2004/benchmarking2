 **Summary:**
The paper explores the problem of faithful rule extraction from the Drum model, a knowledge graph completion system that extends the Neural-LP model with additional inequalities and disjunctions. The authors aim to ensure soundness and completeness in the extracted rules, which are crucial for applications in safety-critical contexts. They propose two algorithms for rule extraction, focusing on practical constraints such as computational efficiency and expressive power. The paper also discusses the limitations of the Drum model in terms of its faithfulness to the underlying data and proposes modifications to address these issues. Despite the theoretical and practical contributions, the paper is criticized for its limited novelty and the complexity of its presentation, which could hinder broader understanding and adoption.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written and the motivation is clear, addressing the significant issue of ensuring soundness and completeness in rule extraction from knowledge graph completion systems.
- The authors provide a comprehensive theoretical analysis, including proofs of the soundness and completeness of the extracted rules, which are crucial for practical applications.
- The paper introduces a novel approach to rule extraction, which is a significant contribution to the field, particularly in the context of Drum, a model that has been shown to outperform other methods in certain scenarios.
- The paper is technically solid, with rigorous proofs and well-designed experiments that demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed methods.
- The inclusion of a detailed discussion on the limitations and future work of the proposed algorithms provides a comprehensive view of the challenges and potential improvements in the field.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper's contribution is seen as limited, as the modifications to the Drum model are not sufficiently novel, and the theoretical contributions are not significantly distinct from existing works.
- The paper's presentation is complex and could benefit from simplification, particularly in the introduction and background sections, to make it more accessible to a broader audience.
- There is a lack of detailed discussion on the computational complexity and practical implications of the proposed algorithms, which could hinder their adoption in real-world applications.
- The paper does not adequately address the issue of overfitting, which is a significant concern in the application of the Drum model to knowledge graphs.
- The paper does not sufficiently compare its results with state-of-the-art methods, which could undermine the claims of its effectiveness and novelty.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors clarify the specific contributions of their work in terms of novelty and distinctiveness from existing methods, particularly in relation to the modifications to the Drum model?
- How does the proposed approach handle overfitting in practical applications, and what measures can be taken to mitigate this issue?
- Could the authors provide more detailed comparisons with state-of-the-art methods, particularly in terms of computational efficiency and effectiveness in practical scenarios?
- What are the main challenges in applying the proposed methods to larger and more complex knowledge graphs, and how do the authors plan to address these challenges in future work?
- Can the authors elaborate on the practical implications of the computational complexity of their algorithms, and how these can be mitigated in practical applications?
