 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel method for solving the Kohn-Sham equations in Kohn-Sham density functional theory (DFT) using a differentiable optimization approach, aimed at enhancing the efficiency and accuracy of band structure predictions and geometry optimization in solid-state physics. The method involves a direct total energy minimization approach and a differentiable geometry optimization method, which are evaluated against existing SCF-based packages using both synthetic and real crystal structures. The paper claims that these methods can potentially reduce computational time and enhance the convergence properties, although the experimental validation and comparison with existing methods are limited.

**Strengths:**
- The paper presents a novel approach to solving the Kohn-Sham equations using a differentiable optimization method, which is a significant advancement in the field of density functional theory (DFT).
- The authors have conducted experiments on both synthetic and real crystal structures, demonstrating the practical applicability of their method.
- The paper is well-written, clear, and easy to follow, with a clear presentation of the problem and the proposed solution.
- The methodology is described in detail, including the computational graph, which aids in understanding the method's implementation.
- The paper introduces a differentiable geometry optimization method, which is a valuable contribution to the field.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed comparison with existing methods, particularly in terms of computational time and convergence properties.
- The experimental validation is limited, with only a few crystal structures used, which may not be sufficient to convincingly demonstrate the method's effectiveness across a broader range of materials.
- The paper does not provide a detailed discussion on the convergence properties of the method, which is crucial for assessing its practical applicability.
- The paper does not discuss the limitations of the method, which could be a significant drawback for readers seeking a comprehensive understanding of the proposed approach.
- The paper does not include a discussion on the potential societal impact of the proposed method, which is a standard requirement for many academic publications.
- There are some presentation issues, such as small font sizes in figures and a lack of clarity in certain sections of the paper.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors provide a more detailed comparison with existing methods, including computational time and convergence properties?
- How does the proposed method compare with other differentiable methods for geometry optimization?
- Can the authors discuss the limitations of their method and provide a detailed discussion on the convergence properties?
- Is the method limited to specific types of materials or crystal structures?
- How does the method perform when applied to larger or more complex systems, such as those involving multiple atoms or different functional forms?
- Could the authors clarify the role of the nuclear repulsion term in their calculations and provide a more detailed explanation of the computational graph?
- Is there a possibility to include a discussion on the potential societal impact of the proposed method?
