 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a toolkit for analyzing path-norms in deep neural networks (DNNs) with complex architectures, including ReLU networks with skip connections, biases, and k-max-pooling. The authors extend existing path-norm definitions to handle these complexities and provide a generalization bound based on the path-norm, which is applicable to modern neural networks. The paper also includes numerical experiments on ResNets trained on ImageNet, demonstrating the practical applicability of the proposed methods. Despite the theoretical and practical contributions, the paper faces criticism for its clarity, presentation, and the practical relevance of the results, particularly the large path-norms observed in trained networks.

**Strengths:**
- The paper provides a generalization bound for modern neural networks based on the path-norm, which is a significant theoretical contribution.
- The authors have extended the definition of path-norms to handle more complex architectures, including ReLU networks with skip connections, biases, and k-max-pooling.
- The paper is well-written, with clear explanations of the theoretical contributions and the extension of existing definitions to handle modern network architectures.
- The inclusion of numerical experiments on ResNets trained on ImageNet demonstrates the practical applicability of the proposed methods.
- The paper discusses the implications of the results and suggests possible leads to close the gap between theory and practice, showing a deep understanding of the implications of the findings.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper suffers from clarity issues, particularly in the definitions and explanations of key concepts such as the path-norm and its components.
- The practical relevance of the results is questioned, especially concerning the large path-norms observed in trained networks, which are several orders of magnitude larger than the theoretical bounds.
- The paper lacks a comprehensive discussion on the limitations of the proposed methods, including the potential for overestimation of generalization bounds due to the use of worst-case scenarios.
- The presentation of the paper is criticized for its complexity and the use of excessive notation, which could hinder understanding.
- The paper does not sufficiently address the computational complexity of computing the path-norm for large-scale networks, which could limit its practical applicability.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors clarify the definitions and computations of the path-norm, particularly in the context of k-max-pooling and the computational complexity?
- How do the authors justify the use of worst-case scenarios in their theoretical bounds, and what are the implications of this approach in practical settings?
- Could the authors provide more detailed explanations of the theoretical results, particularly the proofs and the assumptions made in the peeling lemma and the contraction lemma?
- How do the authors address the practical relevance of their findings, especially in light of the large path-norms observed in trained networks?
- Could the paper benefit from a simplification of the presentation and the use of more intuitive examples to illustrate the theoretical contributions?
