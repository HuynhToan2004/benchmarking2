 **Summary:**
The paper introduces Spectron, a novel approach that adapts pre-trained large language models (LLMs) to perform question answering (QA) and speech continuation by integrating a pre-trained speech encoder. The model operates directly on spectrograms, enabling it to process speech inputs and generate speech outputs. Spectron's training objective supervises speech recognition, text continuation, and speech synthesis using only paired speech-text pairs, which allows for a 'cross-modal' chain-of-thought within a single decoding pass. The model is evaluated on various benchmarks and shows improvements in speaker preservation and semantic coherence compared to existing spoken language models.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel approach by adapting pre-trained LLMs to perform question answering (QA) and speech continuation using a pre-trained speech encoder.
- The method is simple, efficient, and effective, as demonstrated by the experimental results.
- The paper is well-written, easy to follow, and includes a comprehensive ablation study.
- The use of a pre-trained speech encoder and a pre-trained language model as initialization helps in retaining the knowledge of the original LLM, enhancing the model's performance.
- The proposed model can perform both speech generation and text generation, making it versatile in its applications.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed discussion on the limitations of the proposed method, such as high time and space complexity in generating spectrogram frames, and the inability to handle long speech utterances.
- The evaluation of the model's performance is limited to a single dataset (LibriSpeech), which might not adequately demonstrate the model's generalizability to other datasets.
- The paper does not provide sufficient details on the training process, such as the duration and computational resources required.
- The paper could benefit from a more detailed analysis of the model's performance on different datasets and a comparison with other baseline models to establish its superiority more convincingly.
- The methodology and results section could be improved by including more comprehensive tables and a clearer presentation of the experimental setup and results.

**Questions:**
- How does the model handle long-form speech generation, and what are the potential solutions to the high time and space complexity in generating spectrogram frames?
- Could the authors provide more details on the training process, such as the duration and computational resources required?
- How does the model perform on other datasets, and what are the specific contributions of the proposed model compared to existing models like SpeechGPT?
- Is there a possibility of using other LLMs like Llama-2 for training, and how might this affect the model's performance?
- Could the authors clarify the discrepancies in the results reported in the paper, such as the performance on different datasets and the comparison with baseline models?
