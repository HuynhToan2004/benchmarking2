 **Summary:**
The paper introduces Gaussian EMD (GEMD) as a metric for few-shot learning, focusing on the efficient computation of Earth Mover's Distance (EMD) using Gaussian distributions. GEMD is proposed as an alternative to DeepEMD, offering a computationally efficient method for computing EMD in closed form. The paper also introduces a transfer learning-based method (GEMD-T) that learns a parametric EMD, which is faster and more efficient than GEMD-M. The proposed methods are evaluated on various benchmarks, showing improvements over existing methods. However, concerns are raised about the novelty of the approach, as the closed-form EMD computation for Gaussian distributions is not new, and the method's scalability and generalizability are questioned.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, clear, and easy to follow, with a clear motivation and a well-organized structure.
- The proposed GEMD-M and GEMD-T are novel and effective, with GEMD-T being particularly efficient and easy to implement.
- The experiments are thorough and convincing, showing that GEMD-T outperforms GEMD-M and other methods like DeepEMD.
- The paper introduces a new method for computing EMD in closed form, which is both theoretically sound and practically useful.
- The methodology is well-explained, with a clear motivation and a well-organized structure that facilitates understanding.

**Weaknesses:**
- The novelty of the approach is questionable, as the closed-form computation for EMD between Gaussian distributions has been previously explored.
- The paper lacks a detailed comparison with other methods, particularly in terms of scalability and generalizability, especially on larger datasets like ImageNet.
- The paper does not provide a detailed analysis of the computational complexity of GEMD-T, which could be crucial for understanding its practical efficiency.
- The method's reliance on the pre-training of a backbone network raises concerns about its applicability in scenarios where pre-training is not feasible.
- The paper does not adequately address the scalability and generalizability of the method, particularly in scenarios where the pre-trained model might not be as effective.
- The paper could benefit from a more detailed discussion on the limitations and potential negative societal impacts of the proposed method.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors provide a detailed comparison of GEMD-T with other methods, particularly in terms of scalability and generalizability?
- How does the computational complexity of GEMD-T compare to other methods, and what are the practical implications of this complexity?
- What are the limitations of the proposed method, and how do these limitations impact its applicability in different scenarios?
- Could the authors elaborate on the potential negative societal impacts of the proposed method, and how these impacts could be mitigated?
- Can the authors provide more details on the experimental setup, particularly the specifics of the pre-training phase and the computational resources used?
