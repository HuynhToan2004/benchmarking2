 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel approach to modeling time series data using Fourier Ordinary Differential Equations (FODEs), which represent the dynamics in the Fourier domain. This approach allows for a more comprehensive data representation and simplifies the modeling of dynamics by converting time differentiation into a multiplication operation in the Fourier domain. FODEs are shown to outperform existing methods on various time series datasets, demonstrating superior accuracy, efficiency, and generalization capabilities. The methodology involves a neural network that learns a mapping from Fourier space to another Fourier space, which is then inverse-transformed back to the time domain. This method is validated through extensive experiments and is compared favorably to existing methods. However, the paper is criticized for its limited novelty, lack of clarity in presentation, and insufficient discussion on the limitations of the proposed method.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel approach using Fourier Ordinary Differential Equations (FODEs) to model time series data, which is a significant advancement in the field.
- The methodology is well-explained, making it accessible to readers, and the paper is well-organized, enhancing its readability.
- The experiments conducted demonstrate the superiority of FODEs over existing methods, showing improved accuracy, efficiency, and generalization capabilities.
- The paper is original in its approach to modeling time series data using Fourier domain dynamics, which is a novel application of the Fourier transform in the context of neural ODEs.
- The proposed method is simple and straightforward, making it potentially easy to implement in practical scenarios.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks clarity in its presentation, particularly in the methodology section, which could benefit from more detailed explanations and examples.
- The novelty of the proposed method is questioned, as it seems to be a combination of existing techniques without significant innovation.
- The paper does not sufficiently discuss the limitations of the proposed method, which is crucial for understanding its applicability and effectiveness.
- The experiments are limited in scope, and the paper could benefit from a broader range of datasets and comparisons with more recent and relevant methods.
- There is a lack of discussion on the computational complexity and training time of the proposed method compared to other models, which is crucial for practical implementation.
- The paper could benefit from a more detailed discussion on the Fourier domain dynamics and how they contribute to the overall model's effectiveness.
