 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel approach to training vision-language models using differential privacy (DP) techniques, specifically focusing on image captioning. It demonstrates that using a DP-SGD algorithm for pretraining a vision-language model on a subset of the LAION-2B dataset can achieve comparable performance to non-private models under a privacy budget of 8. The authors also explore the scalability of their method by varying the batch size and model size, showing that larger batch sizes and model sizes can lead to better performance. The paper also discusses the challenges of DP training, such as the need for sufficient training steps and low effective noise, and proposes solutions to address these challenges.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel approach to training vision-language models using differential privacy (DP), which is a significant contribution to the field.
- The experiments conducted are comprehensive, covering various aspects such as the impact of different parameters on performance, the effect of varying batch sizes and model sizes, and the comparison with non-private models.
- The paper is well-written, clear, and easy to follow, making it accessible to a broad audience.
- The results demonstrate that the proposed method achieves performance comparable to non-private models under a privacy budget of 8, which is a notable achievement.
- The paper provides a detailed analysis of the challenges of DP training, such as the need for sufficient training steps and low effective noise, and proposes solutions to address these challenges.
- The proposed method shows good scalability with respect to dataset size and model size, which is crucial for practical applications.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed comparison with other DP training methods, particularly in terms of the privacy guarantees provided by the proposed method.
- The paper does not provide sufficient theoretical analysis or insights into why the proposed method works well, which could enhance the understanding of the underlying mechanisms.
- The results are not consistently superior to existing methods, such as CLIP, and the paper does not adequately address the limitations of its methodology.
- The paper does not discuss the computational costs of the proposed method, which is crucial for practical applications.
- The paper does not include a detailed discussion on the limitations of its methodology, which could provide valuable insights for future research.
- The paper could benefit from a more detailed discussion on the privacy implications of the proposed method, particularly in terms of the privacy guarantees provided and the potential for privacy leaks.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors provide a detailed comparison of their method with other DP training methods in terms of privacy guarantees and computational costs?
- How does the proposed method perform under different privacy budgets, and what are the trade-offs between privacy and performance?
- What are the specific challenges in scaling up the batch size to extreme levels, and how does the method address these challenges?
- How does the proposed method compare with other vision-language pre-training methods, such as BLIP, in terms of performance and privacy guarantees?
- What are the privacy implications of the proposed method, and how do these compare with other methods in terms of privacy guarantees and potential for privacy leaks?
- Could the authors provide a more detailed discussion on the limitations of their methodology and potential future research directions?
