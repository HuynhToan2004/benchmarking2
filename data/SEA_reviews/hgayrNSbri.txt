 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a method for lightweight image captioning using a linear mapping to bridge the modality gap between images and text in the CLIP embedding space. This method optimizes the linear mapping through a least-squares solution, which can be computed efficiently on a CPU, and retrieves captions from the training set based on the similarity to the image embeddings. The authors propose a self-improvement loop to refine the mapping by adding synthetic captions generated by a language model to the training set. The method achieves competitive performance on standard benchmarks like MS-COCO and Flickr30k, and the authors claim that it can be deployed on a CPU, which is a significant advantage in terms of computational efficiency.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written and easy to follow, with clear explanations and intuitive methodologies.
- The proposed method is simple, efficient, and effective, demonstrating good performance on standard benchmarks.
- The methodology includes a self-improvement loop, which iteratively refines the mapping by adding synthetic captions to the training set, enhancing the model's performance.
- The paper provides a detailed analysis of the CLIP-score, highlighting its vulnerability to hallucinated content and bag of words, which is a significant contribution to the field.
- The method's simplicity and efficiency in terms of computational resources and training time are highlighted, making it a practical solution for lightweight image captioning.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a comprehensive comparison with other lightweight image captioning methods, which could have provided a clearer understanding of the method's advantages and disadvantages.
- The method's reliance on a retrieval-augmented approach may limit its generalization ability to other datasets or domains, as it heavily depends on the quality and diversity of the retrieved captions.
- The paper does not sufficiently address the scalability of the method to larger datasets or more complex scenarios, which could limit its applicability in real-world scenarios.
- There is a lack of discussion on the method's limitations, such as its dependence on the quality of the retrieved captions and the potential for overfitting due to the use of synthetic captions.
- The paper's evaluation primarily focuses on n-gram-based metrics, which may not fully capture the quality of the generated captions, and could benefit from the inclusion of more qualitative metrics.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors clarify the method's performance on other datasets or domains, and discuss any potential challenges or limitations in applying the method to new or diverse datasets?
- How does the method handle the potential for overfitting due to the use of synthetic captions? Are there any strategies or modifications that could be implemented to mitigate this risk?
- Could the authors provide more details on the method's scalability and its ability to handle larger or more complex datasets?
- How does the method compare in terms of computational resources and training time with other lightweight image captioning methods?
- Could the authors discuss the potential impact of the linear mapping on the model's ability to generalize to new or diverse datasets, and what steps could be taken to address any potential limitations?
