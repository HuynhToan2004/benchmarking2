 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel multi-objective evolutionary algorithm (MOESR) for image super-resolution, aiming to optimize both objective and perceptual metrics. The algorithm is designed to balance PSNR and LPIPS, using a differential evolution approach to optimize multiple loss functions simultaneously. It employs a pre-trained model to initialize the optimization process and uses a weighted sum of losses to guide the training. The paper includes extensive experiments across various datasets, demonstrating the effectiveness of the proposed method. However, concerns are raised about the novelty of the approach, as it primarily involves the weighted sum of losses, and the computational complexity of the method. The paper also lacks a detailed discussion on the convergence of the evolutionary algorithm and the choice of hyperparameters.

**Strengths:**
- The paper addresses an important problem in image super-resolution by optimizing multiple objectives, which is crucial for practical applications.
- The proposed method is straightforward and easy to implement, with a focus on optimizing both objective and perceptual metrics, which is a significant advancement in the field.
- The paper is well-written, making it easy to follow, and includes extensive experiments that demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed method.
- The use of a pre-trained model to initialize the optimization process is innovative and helps in improving the performance of the model.
- The paper provides a comprehensive evaluation of the proposed method, comparing it with state-of-the-art methods and demonstrating its effectiveness in handling multiple objectives.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed discussion on the convergence of the evolutionary algorithm used, which is a critical aspect of the proposed method.
- The choice of hyperparameters, such as the number of generations and the weighting factors, is not adequately justified, which could affect the reproducibility and generalizability of the results.
- The novelty of the approach is limited as it primarily involves the weighted sum of losses, which is a common technique in multi-objective optimization.
- The computational complexity of the proposed method is not discussed, which is crucial for understanding its scalability and applicability in practical scenarios.
- The paper lacks a thorough comparison with existing methods that optimize both PSNR and LPIPS, which could provide a clearer picture of the advantages of the proposed method.
- The paper could benefit from a more detailed discussion on the choice of loss functions and their impact on the performance of the model.

**Questions:**
- How does the proposed method compare with other state-of-the-art methods that optimize both PSNR and LPIPS?
- Can the authors provide a more detailed discussion on the convergence of the evolutionary algorithm and the choice of hyperparameters?
- How does the proposed method handle the trade-off between different objectives, particularly in scenarios where one objective might be prioritized over another?
- Could the authors provide a more detailed comparison with other evolutionary algorithms for image super-resolution, such as those mentioned in the literature?
- How does the proposed method handle the computational complexity of the evolutionary algorithm, particularly in large-scale applications?
- Could the authors provide a more detailed discussion on the impact of different loss functions on the performance of the model, and how the weighting factors are chosen?
