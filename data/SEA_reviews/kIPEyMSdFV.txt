 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel Monte Carlo sampling algorithm based on reverse diffusion processes, which transforms score estimation into a mean estimation problem. This approach leverages the Ornstein-Uhlenbeck process to derive a reverse diffusion Monte Carlo (rdMC) algorithm that samples from a posterior distribution by estimating the mean of the posterior. The algorithm's effectiveness is demonstrated through theoretical analysis and empirical results, showing improvements over traditional Langevin-style MCMC sampling methods in certain scenarios. The methodology is particularly useful for sampling from distributions with heavy tails and multiple modes, and it is shown to converge faster than standard MCMC methods.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, clearly presenting the main ideas and results, with a clear motivation for the proposed algorithm.
- The use of the reverse diffusion process to solve the score estimation problem is innovative and theoretically sound, with a novel approach to sampling from complex distributions.
- The paper provides a detailed theoretical analysis of the convergence of the algorithm, including the dependence of convergence on the log-Sobolev constant, which is a significant contribution to the field.
- The empirical results demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed method in sampling from challenging distributions, such as those with heavy tails or multiple modes.
- The paper is original in its application of reverse diffusion to Monte Carlo sampling, offering a fresh perspective on sampling from complex distributions.

**Weaknesses:**

- There is a lack of comparison with other diffusion-based sampling methods, which could help to position the proposed method within the existing literature.
