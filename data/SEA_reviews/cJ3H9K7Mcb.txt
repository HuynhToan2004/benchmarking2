 **Summary:**
The paper explores the robustness of machine learning models under varying degrees of distribution shifts, using datasets such as NoisyMNIST and RotatedMNIST. It demonstrates that models trained on mild distribution shifts may not perform well under more severe shifts, and that models trained on strongly shifted data may not be robust to milder shifts. The study also investigates the performance of pre-trained models like CLIP, showing that they can be brittle to distribution shifts. The paper emphasizes the importance of considering a broad range of distribution shifts in model evaluation and suggests that models trained on mild shifts may not be robust to more severe shifts.

**Strengths:**
- The paper addresses an important problem of evaluating the robustness of machine learning models under different degrees of distribution shifts, which is crucial for understanding the generalization capabilities of models.
- The experiments conducted on various datasets and models demonstrate the brittleness of models, highlighting the need for a more nuanced evaluation of model robustness.
- The paper is well-written, clear, and easy to follow, making it accessible to a broad audience.
- The findings are supported by a comprehensive set of experiments, which are well-documented and provide a detailed analysis of model behavior under different conditions.
- The paper raises interesting questions about the generalization capabilities of models and the robustness of pre-trained models, which are relevant to the current state of machine learning research.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper primarily focuses on image classification tasks, which limits the generalizability of the findings to other types of tasks such as text or audio classification.
- The experiments are somewhat limited in scope, focusing on a few datasets and not adequately addressing the impact of different types of distribution shifts.
- The paper does not adequately address the limitations of its findings, particularly the generalizability of the results beyond the specific datasets used.
- The conclusions drawn from the experiments are not always clear or well-supported, and some of the findings are not surprising or novel.
- The paper lacks a thorough discussion on the implications of the findings for practical applications, and does not propose new methods or approaches to address the issues highlighted.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors clarify the discrepancies in the results between different datasets, particularly the inconsistencies in the robustness of models trained on different types of shifts?
- How do the findings of this study relate to existing literature on model robustness and generalization, particularly in terms of the theoretical contributions and practical implications?
- Could the authors provide more detailed explanations for the observed brittleness of models under certain conditions, and discuss potential strategies for improving model robustness?
- How do the findings of this study contribute to the broader understanding of out-of-distribution (OOD) generalization, and what are the practical implications for real-world applications?
