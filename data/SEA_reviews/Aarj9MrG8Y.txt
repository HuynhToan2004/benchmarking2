 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel approach to designing graph filters for graph neural networks (GNNs) by employing a power series with exponential decaying coefficients. This method aims to address the over-smoothing issue in GNNs by controlling the influence of high-order neighbors. Theoretical analysis is provided to ensure the convergence and stability of the proposed filters, and empirical results demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed method. The paper also presents a generalization bound for the proposed learning principle, which is claimed to be superior to existing methods. The proposed method is applied to node classification tasks, showing promising results.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel approach to designing graph filters for graph neural networks (GNNs) using a power series with exponential decaying coefficients, which is a novel and innovative contribution to the field.
- Theoretical analysis is provided to ensure the convergence and stability of the proposed filters, which is a significant strength as it enhances the credibility and applicability of the proposed method.
- Empirical results demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed method, showing improvements over existing methods in node classification tasks.
- The paper is well-written, making it easy to follow, and the authors have made a substantial effort to ensure the clarity and accessibility of their work.
- The proposed method is general and can be applied to a wide range of GNN architectures, which increases its practical utility and potential impact.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed comparison with other methods that address the over-smoothing issue in GNNs, which could have strengthened the argument for the superiority of the proposed method.
- The generalization bound provided in the paper is not clearly explained or compared with existing methods, which could limit the understanding of the theoretical contributions.
- The paper does not discuss the computational complexity of the proposed method, which is a significant concern for practical applications.
- The experimental results are not sufficiently detailed or comprehensive, particularly in terms of the number of datasets used and the diversity of tasks tested.
- The paper does not address the limitations of the proposed method, which could help in understanding its scope and applicability.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors clarify the choice of using a power series with exponential decaying coefficients for designing graph filters, and how this choice addresses the over-smoothing issue in GNNs?
- How does the proposed method compare with other existing methods that address the over-smoothing issue in GNNs, such as GraphSAINT and Graph ViT?
- Can the authors provide a detailed comparison of the generalization bound provided in the paper with existing methods, and explain the implications of this bound for practical applications?
- How does the proposed method perform on large-scale datasets, and what are the computational costs associated with its application?
- Could the authors discuss the limitations of the proposed method and suggest areas for future research to address these limitations?
