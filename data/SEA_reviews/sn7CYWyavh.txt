 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel hierarchical music generation framework that leverages a cascaded diffusion model to generate whole-song structures, focusing on pop music. The framework is designed to handle different levels of musical structures, from high-level form and counterpoint to low-level lead sheets and accompaniments. Each level is represented by a continuous image-like tensor, which is conditioned on the previous level, ensuring a structured and coherent generation process. The model is evaluated using a combination of objective and subjective metrics, showing improvements over existing methods. Despite some concerns about the novelty and the depth of evaluation, the paper presents a significant advancement in the generation of structured and coherent music, with a focus on pop music structures.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel hierarchical music generation framework that effectively models the compositional hierarchy of music, enhancing the generation process by conditioning each level on the previous one.
- The use of a cascaded diffusion model to generate whole-song structures is innovative and shows promise in improving the quality of generated music.
- The framework is well-explained, with clear and concise writing that effectively conveys the complexities of the proposed method.
- The evaluation of the generated music is thorough, using both objective and subjective measures, which demonstrates the effectiveness of the proposed method.
- The paper provides a detailed analysis of the generated music, including a breakdown of the generation process and a comparison with baseline models.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a comprehensive comparison with existing methods, particularly in the area of hierarchical music generation, which could undermine the perceived novelty and effectiveness of the proposed framework.
- There is a significant absence of musical examples and detailed analysis, which could enhance the understanding and evaluation of the generated music.
- The paper's focus on pop music structures might limit its applicability to other genres or styles of music, which could affect its broader relevance and impact.
- The evaluation metrics used are somewhat limited, and the paper could benefit from a more robust set of metrics that better reflect the quality and coherence of the generated music.
- The paper's claims about the novelty and effectiveness of the proposed framework are somewhat overstated, and there is a need for more rigorous comparisons and evaluations to substantiate these claims.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors provide more details on how the hierarchical music generation framework performs in other genres or styles of music, and what adjustments might be necessary for effective application in these contexts?
- How does the proposed framework compare with other existing methods in terms of computational efficiency and resource requirements?
- Can the authors elaborate on the choice of evaluation metrics and consider including additional metrics that might better reflect the quality and coherence of the generated music?
- In terms of the musical examples provided, could the authors include more detailed analysis and comparisons with ground truth data to enhance the understanding and evaluation of the generated music?
- How does the proposed framework handle the generation of complex musical structures, such as polyphonic music or music with multiple sections or movements?
- Could the authors clarify the meaning and application of the term "counterpoint" as used in the paper, and consider using a more descriptive term to avoid potential confusion?
