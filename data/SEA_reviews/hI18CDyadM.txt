 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel method for local motion deblurring using a Transformer architecture, specifically a U-Net structure with adaptive window pruning. The methodology includes a confidence predictor that determines which windows to prune based on blurriness, thereby reducing computational overhead and enhancing efficiency. The approach is evaluated on the ReLoBlur dataset, showing significant improvements in inference time and FLOPs without compromising performance. The paper also includes a comprehensive ablation study to demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed method.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, easy to follow, and includes a comprehensive ablation study, which supports the claims of the proposed method.
- The proposed method achieves the best performance on the ReLoBlur dataset, demonstrating significant improvements in inference time and FLOPs compared to existing methods.
- The paper introduces a novel approach to local motion deblurring using a Transformer architecture, which is both efficient and effective.
- The use of an adaptive window pruning strategy for local motion deblurring is innovative and has been shown to be effective in reducing computational costs without compromising performance.
- The paper includes a comprehensive set of experiments, including a user study, which validates the effectiveness of the proposed method.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed comparison with existing methods such as U-Former and Restormer, which also use Transformer architectures for local deblurring. This omission makes it difficult to assess the novelty and effectiveness of the proposed method.
- The paper does not provide a detailed analysis of the limitations of the proposed method, which could help in understanding its applicability and potential drawbacks.
- There is a lack of clarity in the description of the confidence predictor and its training process, which could benefit from more detailed explanations.
- The paper does not include a discussion on the limitations of the proposed method, which could help in understanding its applicability and potential drawbacks.
- The paper does not provide a detailed comparison with other methods in terms of computational resources and memory usage, which could affect the practical applicability of the proposed method.

**Questions:**
- How does the proposed method compare with existing methods such as U-Former and Restormer, especially in terms of performance and computational efficiency?
- Can the proposed method be adapted to handle other types of image deblurring, such as global motion blur or non-motion blur?
- How does the pruning strategy affect the performance of the model, especially in terms of the number of pruning blocks and the pruning threshold?
- What are the limitations of the proposed method, and how do these impact its practical applicability and effectiveness?
- Can the authors provide more details on the training process of the confidence predictor and the specifics of the Gumbel-Softmax re-parameterization used?
- How does the proposed method perform on other datasets, and can the authors provide results on these datasets to validate its effectiveness across different scenarios?
