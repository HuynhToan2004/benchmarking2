 **Summary:**
The paper introduces AdT-HyGCL, a novel hypergraph contrastive learning framework that addresses the limitations of existing methods by integrating a dual-level contrast strategy and an adaptive temperature-enhanced contrast optimization. This framework aims to capture both individual and group-wise behaviors within hypergraphs, improving the representation learning of hypergraph neural networks (HyGNNs) over unlabeled data. The methodology includes a noise-enhanced hypergraph augmentation and a dual-level hypergraph contrastive strategy to enhance the discriminative capacity between positive and negative contrastive pairs. The paper provides theoretical justifications and empirical experiments on eight benchmark hypergraphs, demonstrating the effectiveness and robustness of AdT-HyGCL.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel hypergraph contrastive learning framework, integrating a dual-level contrast strategy and an adaptive temperature-enhanced contrast optimization, which is a significant contribution to the field.
- The authors provide theoretical justifications and empirical experiments to demonstrate the effectiveness and robustness of AdT-HyGCL, which is a comprehensive approach to hypergraph representation learning.
- The methodology is well-organized, making it easy to follow, and the paper is well-written, with clear and concise descriptions of the proposed methods.
- The paper addresses the limitations of existing hypergraph contrastive learning methods by focusing on the importance of temperature in discriminating contrastive pairs and modeling group-wise collective behaviors of nodes within hyperedges.
- The empirical experiments conducted on eight benchmark hypergraphs demonstrate the rationality, effectiveness, and robustness of AdT-HyGCL, providing a strong foundation for its application in various domains.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed complexity analysis of AdT-HyGCL, which is crucial for understanding the computational efficiency and scalability of the proposed method.
- The selection of hypergraph augmentation methods and their impact on the results is not thoroughly discussed, which could affect the reproducibility and generalizability of the findings.
- There is a lack of discussion on the limitations and potential negative societal impacts of the proposed method, which is essential for a comprehensive understanding of its applicability and ethical considerations.
- The paper does not include a discussion on the limitations of the proposed method, which could provide a more balanced perspective and help in future research directions.
- The paper could benefit from a more detailed comparison with existing methods, especially in terms of computational efficiency and scalability, to better position AdT-HyGCL within the current research landscape.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors provide a detailed complexity analysis of AdT-HyGCL, including both time and space complexity, to better understand the computational efficiency and scalability of the proposed method?
- How does the selection of hypergraph augmentation methods affect the results, and could the authors provide insights into the optimal combination of augmentation methods for different datasets?
- Could the authors discuss the limitations of the proposed method, including potential negative societal impacts and ethical considerations, to provide a more balanced perspective on the applicability and implications of AdT-HyGCL?
- How does the proposed method perform in terms of computational efficiency and scalability compared to existing methods, and what are the implications of these findings for the practical application of AdT-HyGCL?
