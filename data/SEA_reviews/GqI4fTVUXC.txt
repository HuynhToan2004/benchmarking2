 **Summary:**
The paper explores the practical relevance of the infinite-width limit of neural networks, focusing on the disconnect between theoretical predictions and real-world applications. It investigates three key areas: optimization, uncertainty quantification, and continual learning, using both synthetic and real-world datasets. The authors find that the theoretical predictions, such as the fast convergence of second-order optimization methods and the reduction in catastrophic forgetting, do not hold in practical settings. They argue that the assumptions underlying these predictions are too stringent and not practical, which limits the applicability of the NTK framework in current neural network architectures.

**Strengths:**
- The paper addresses a significant and timely issue in the field, questioning the practical relevance of theoretical predictions about neural networks, particularly in the context of the infinite-width limit.
- The authors provide a comprehensive review of the literature and a clear exposition of the theoretical frameworks, making the paper accessible and informative.
- The experiments are well-designed, covering a range of neural network architectures and training scenarios, and the results are clearly presented with detailed explanations.
- The paper raises important questions about the practical applicability of theoretical results, particularly in the context of deep learning, and challenges the assumptions underlying these results.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a clear and compelling narrative that ties the findings together, making it difficult to discern the main takeaways and the implications of the results.
- The experiments are limited to specific architectures and datasets, which may not generalize to other settings, and the results do not convincingly support the conclusions drawn.
- The paper does not sufficiently address the limitations of its approach, particularly the potential impact of different initialization methods and the generalizability of the findings to other neural network architectures.
- The paper's reliance on theoretical predictions without a clear demonstration of their practical utility or applicability in real-world scenarios weakens the argument for their relevance.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors clarify the main takeaways from the experiments and how they support the conclusions drawn?
- How do the findings relate to the practical applicability of the NTK framework in current neural network architectures?
- Could the authors elaborate on the potential impact of different initialization methods on the results, and discuss how the findings might generalize to other neural network architectures?
- How do the authors address the limitations of their approach, particularly the assumptions made about the network architectures and the initialization methods?
