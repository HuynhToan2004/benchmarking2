 **Summary:**
The paper introduces PROSPECT, a novel online graph distillation multi-layer perceptron (MLP) framework designed to enhance the robustness of Graph Neural Networks (GNNs) against adversarial structure attacks on both homophilous and heterophilous graphs. PROSPECT leverages a dual-component architecture, integrating both a Graph Neural Network (GNN) and a Multi-Layer Perceptron (MLP), to achieve robustness against adversarial attacks and high inference scalability. The framework's effectiveness is demonstrated through extensive experiments on various datasets, showcasing its superiority over existing methods in terms of adversarial robustness and clean accuracy. The paper also includes a theoretical analysis and empirical evidence to support its claims, highlighting the benefits of the QACA learning rate scheduler and the MLP-to-GNN distillation mechanism.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel online graph distillation MLP framework, PROSPECT, which effectively addresses the challenges of GNNs in terms of adaptability to graph heterophily, generalizability to early GNNs, and inference scalability.
- The framework is supported by a robust theoretical analysis, which is substantiated by empirical evidence, demonstrating its effectiveness in enhancing the robustness and clean accuracy of GNNs.
- The paper provides a detailed theoretical analysis of the convergence conditions of the MLP in PROSPECT and proposes a QACA learning rate scheduler, inspired by the convergence analysis and alternating iterative turbo decoding from information theory.
- The experiments conducted on various datasets show that PROSPECT outperforms existing defense methods and offline GD-MLPs in adversarial robustness and clean accuracy, demonstrating its effectiveness in real-world scenarios.
- The paper is well-written, making it easy to follow, and includes a comprehensive discussion of related work, which helps in understanding the context and the contributions of the research.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed discussion on the convergence conditions of the GNN in PROSPECT, which could provide a more comprehensive understanding of the framework's behavior.
- The experiments do not include a discussion on the convergence speed of the proposed learning rate scheduler, which is crucial for understanding the practical implications of the framework.
- The paper could benefit from a more detailed comparison with other state-of-the-art defense methods, particularly in terms of adversarial robustness and clean accuracy, to better position the contributions of PROSPECT.
- The paper does not provide a detailed discussion on the computational complexity of the framework, which could be a significant concern for practical implementation in resource-constrained environments.
- There is a lack of discussion on the potential negative societal impacts of the proposed framework, which should be addressed to ensure the ethical implications of the research are considered.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors provide more details on the convergence conditions of the GNN in PROSPECT and how they contribute to the overall robustness of the framework?
- How does the proposed learning rate scheduler compare in terms of convergence speed with other schedulers, and what are the practical implications of this comparison?
- Could the authors elaborate on the computational complexity of the framework and discuss potential strategies for reducing this complexity in practical applications?
- How does the PROSPECT framework perform against other state-of-the-art defense methods in terms of adversarial robustness and clean accuracy, and what are the specific advantages of PROSPECT over these methods?
- Can the authors provide more details on the potential negative societal impacts of the proposed framework and discuss strategies for mitigating these impacts?
