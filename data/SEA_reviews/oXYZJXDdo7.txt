 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel approach to language modeling that shifts from token-level generation to phrase-level retrieval, aiming to enhance the interpretability and factuality of language models. This methodology, which involves the use of linguistic heuristics and iterative self-reinforcement, aims to improve the accuracy of retrieval-augmented language models. The paper presents experiments across various tasks, including open-domain question answering and open-ended text generation, showing that this approach outperforms standard language models and retrieval-augmented baselines. However, concerns were raised about the novelty of the approach, the clarity of the presentation, and the depth of the experimental validation.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel approach to language modeling that shifts from token-level generation to phrase-level retrieval, which is a significant advancement in the field.
- The proposed method is well-articulated, with clear explanations of the challenges addressed and the methodology employed.
- The experiments conducted are comprehensive, covering a variety of tasks including open-domain question answering and open-ended text generation, demonstrating the effectiveness of the proposed method.
- The paper is well-written, making it accessible and easy to understand, with clear figures that aid in understanding the proposed method.
- The results show that the proposed method outperforms standard language models and retrieval-augmented baselines, indicating its practical utility.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a clear differentiation from existing retrieval-augmented language models, particularly in terms of its novelty and the specific contributions it makes to the field.
- The experimental validation is limited, with only one dataset used for evaluation, which may not sufficiently demonstrate the generalizability of the proposed method.
- There are several presentation issues, such as unclear descriptions and typos, which detract from the professionalism and clarity of the paper.
- The paper does not sufficiently address the scalability of the proposed method, particularly in terms of the complexity introduced by the need to store and retrieve phrases from a large corpus.
- The paper does not adequately discuss the limitations of the proposed method, which could help in understanding its scope and applicability.

**Questions:**

- How does the proposed method compare to other retrieval-augmented language models, particularly in terms of performance and scalability?
- How are the negative samples selected in the model, and what impact does this have on the performance of the model?
- Could you provide more details on the training data and the pre-trained models used, particularly in terms of their impact on the results reported?
- How does the proposed method handle the scalability issues associated with storing and retrieving phrases from a large corpus?
- Can you provide a more detailed analysis of the results, particularly in terms of the performance metrics reported and their implications for the practical utility of the model?
