 **Summary:**
The paper investigates the vulnerability of diffusion models to backdoor attacks, specifically focusing on the "BadNets" methodology where the training data is contaminated with backdoor triggers. The study explores the generation of "Trojan Horses" in backdoored diffusion models, highlighting the potential for these models to produce incorrect images that deviate from the intended text conditions. The authors also examine the "Castle Walls" effect, where backdoored diffusion models can be leveraged for backdoor detection and the enhancement of anti-backdoor image classifiers. Furthermore, the paper delves into the data replication problem in diffusion models, proposing a novel approach to mitigate backdoor attacks by filtering out the top denoising loss of the backdoored model.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, with clear organization and presentation of the research.
- It introduces a novel perspective on backdoor attacks in diffusion models, focusing on the "BadNets" methodology which is less explored in this context.
- The study provides insights into the dual nature of backdoor attacks in diffusion models, highlighting both their vulnerabilities and potential defensive applications.
- The paper offers valuable insights into the data replication problem in diffusion models, which is an emerging issue in the field.
- The findings are supported by a comprehensive set of experiments and visualizations, which aid in understanding the complex interactions between backdoor attacks and data replication.
- The paper is original in its approach, challenging existing assumptions about backdoor attacks in diffusion models and proposing a new method for defending against these attacks.

**Weaknesses:**

- The experimental setup and methodology are not clearly explained, making it difficult to replicate the results or understand the methodology fully.
