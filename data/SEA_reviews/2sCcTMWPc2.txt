 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel architecture, TimelyGPT, designed to address the challenges of modeling long-term time series data, particularly in healthcare applications. TimelyGPT integrates a convolution-subsampling tokenizer, extrapolatable position embedding (xPOS), recurrent attention, and convolution modules to effectively capture both global and local temporal dependencies. The architecture is evaluated on various datasets, demonstrating superior performance in forecasting and classification tasks compared to existing methods. The authors also address the limitations of transformers in handling long sequences and the need for pre-training on large-scale data to capture temporal dependencies effectively.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, with clear and concise sections that effectively communicate the research findings.
- The introduction of the convolution-subsampling tokenizer and the temporal convolution module is innovative, enhancing the model's ability to capture local temporal patterns and multi-scale features.
- The integration of xPOS and the retention mechanism is well-explained and demonstrated to be effective in handling irregularly sampled time series.
- The paper includes a comprehensive ablation study that supports the effectiveness of each component in the model.
- The experimental results are convincing, showing the superior performance of TimelyGPT over existing methods in various tasks.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed discussion on the limitations and potential negative societal impacts of the proposed architecture.
- The motivation behind the choice of specific components, such as the convolution-subsampling tokenizer and the retention mechanism, is not adequately justified.
- The paper could benefit from a more thorough analysis of the computational complexity and efficiency of the model, particularly in the context of ultra-long-term forecasting.
- The experimental results are sometimes hard to interpret, and the paper could benefit from clearer visualizations and explanations of the results.
- The novelty of the model is limited as it primarily combines existing techniques without significant innovation.
- The paper does not adequately address the scalability and applicability of the model in real-world scenarios, such as in the healthcare domain.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors provide more details on the computational complexity and efficiency of the model, especially in the context of ultra-long-term forecasting?
- How does the model handle irregularly sampled time series, and what are the specific advantages of using xPOS over other position embedding methods?
- Can the authors clarify the motivation behind the choice of specific components in the model, such as the convolution-subsampling tokenizer and the retention mechanism?
- How does the model perform in real-world applications, and what are the potential challenges in deploying the model in healthcare settings?
- Could the authors provide more detailed visualizations and explanations of the results to enhance the understanding of the experimental findings?
