 **Summary:**
The paper explores the representation of sentiment in Large Language Models (LLMs) by identifying a linear representation of sentiment across various tasks and datasets. This representation, which is causally relevant and summarized at intermediate positions without inherent sentiment, is crucial for sentiment analysis and is shown to be effective in both toy tasks and real-world datasets. The study also reveals the mechanisms involved, highlighting the roles of specific attention heads and neurons. The paper provides a comprehensive analysis of the sentiment direction's impact on model performance and its correlation with sentiment information in the wild, demonstrating that it matters causally in a crowdsourced dataset.
