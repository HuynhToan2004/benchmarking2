 **Summary:**
The paper presents non-asymptotic convergence bounds for single-loop Actor-Critic (AC) and Natural Actor-Critic (NAC) algorithms with compatible function approximation. It addresses the challenges of analyzing the single-loop setting with a single Markovian sample trajectory, which is known for its complexity. The authors claim to have achieved the best known sample complexity by eliminating the term εcritic from the error bounds, thus providing a significant contribution to the field. The paper also discusses the challenges of analyzing the stochastic bias due to policy-dependent and time-varying compatible function approximation in the critic, and the non-ergodicity of the MDP due to the single Markovian sample trajectory.

**Strengths:**
- The paper provides a comprehensive review of the literature on non-asymptotic analysis for single-loop Actor-Critic (AC) and Natural Actor-Critic (NAC) algorithms, highlighting the challenges and contributions of the proposed analysis.
- The authors demonstrate a deep understanding of the challenges in analyzing the single-loop setting with a single Markovian sample trajectory, and have developed novel approaches to bound the tracking error as a function of the policy gradient norm and the optimality gap.
- The paper is well-written, with clear explanations of the challenges and contributions of the analysis, and provides detailed proofs and discussions of the main technical novelty.
- The results are significant, with the authors achieving the best known sample complexity for both AC and NAC algorithms, and the first single-loop NAC analysis for the challenging case of single Markovian sample trajectories.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks clarity in some sections, particularly in the discussion of the main technical novelty and the proof of the main results. The notation and definitions used are sometimes confusing, which could hinder understanding.
- The novelty of the analysis is not clearly articulated, and it is unclear how the paper's contributions differ significantly from existing works.
- The paper does not provide a clear comparison with other recent works, such as [1] and [2], which could have strengthened the argument for the paper's contributions.
- There are several minor errors and typos throughout the paper, which could detract from its professionalism and clarity.
- The paper does not adequately discuss the limitations of the analysis, particularly in terms of the assumptions made and their implications.

**Questions:**
