 **Summary:**
The paper investigates the stability of stochastic gradient descent (SGD) near minima, focusing on the mean-square stability threshold and its dependence on the batch size. It provides a closed-form expression for the stability threshold, showing that it is a monotonically non-decreasing function of the batch size. The authors also derive necessary conditions for stability that are computationally simpler. Theoretical results are supported by experiments on the MNIST dataset, demonstrating that SGD's stability threshold is close to that of gradient descent (GD) when the batch size is moderate. The paper also discusses the role of the batch size in influencing the stability threshold and provides a novel perspective on the stability of SGD compared to GD.

**Strengths:**
- The paper provides a closed-form expression for the exact threshold of mean-square stability for SGD, which is a significant contribution to the field.
- The authors derive necessary conditions for stability that are computationally simpler, which is useful for practical applications.
- The paper is well-written, with clear proofs and a comprehensive literature review, making it accessible to readers.
- The theoretical results are supported by experiments on the MNIST dataset, demonstrating the practical relevance and applicability of the theoretical findings.
- The paper provides a novel perspective on the stability of SGD compared to GD, which is an interesting and less explored area in the literature.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper does not sufficiently discuss the limitations of its results, particularly the assumptions made and the generalizability of the findings beyond the specific conditions studied.
- The paper does not provide a detailed comparison with existing works, which could help in understanding the novelty and contribution of the study.
