 **Summary:**
The paper introduces WizardCoder, a novel approach to fine-tuning Code LLMs using a method called Code Evol-Instruct. This method involves generating more complex code instruction data by evolving existing datasets, which is then used to fine-tune models like StarCoder and CodeLlama. The paper claims that WizardCoder outperforms other open-source models on various benchmarks, including HumanEval, HumanEval+, MBPP, DS-1000, and MultiPL-E. The methodology is inspired by the Evol-Instruct approach and is evaluated on multiple benchmarks, showing significant improvements over existing models. However, the paper's novelty and the clarity of its contributions are questioned, as the approach is seen as a simple application of existing techniques rather than a significant innovation.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel approach to fine-tune Code LLMs, specifically through the Evol-Instruct method, which is a significant contribution to the field.
- The proposed method achieves state-of-the-art (SOTA) performance on various benchmarks, demonstrating its effectiveness.
- The paper is well-written, easy to follow, and provides comprehensive evaluations, making it accessible and informative.
- The inclusion of extensive experimental results and analyses, including the evaluation of the role of instruction complexity, adds depth to the research.
- The paper's results are impressive, showing significant improvements over other open-source Code LLMs.

**Weaknesses:**
- The novelty of the approach is limited as it primarily applies existing techniques to the code domain without significant innovation.
- The paper lacks a clear explanation of the specific contributions of the proposed method, especially how it differs from or improves upon existing methods.
- The evaluation is limited to a few benchmarks, which may not fully demonstrate the generalizability or robustness of the proposed method.
- The paper does not discuss the limitations of the proposed method, which is crucial for understanding its applicability and potential drawbacks.
- The clarity of the paper could be improved, particularly in explaining the methodology and the experimental setups.

**Questions:**
- How does the proposed method compare to other fine-tuning methods, such as those using synthetic data or different training strategies?
- Can the authors provide more details on the evolution process, including the specific heuristics used and their impact on the model's performance?
- How does the proposed method perform on other code-related tasks, such as debugging or code refactoring?
- Can the authors provide more comprehensive evaluations, including comparisons with closed-source models and a broader range of benchmarks?
- How does the paper address the potential limitations of the proposed method, and what are the future directions for research in this area?
