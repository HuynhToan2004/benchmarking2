 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel method called Residual Bit Vector (ResBit) for representing categorical data, which aims to address the "out-of-index" problem in TabDDPM by encoding categorical values as a sequence of binary bits. This approach is inspired by Residual Vector Quantization and aims to reduce the dimensionality required for representing categorical data, thereby enhancing the performance and efficiency of diffusion models. The proposed method is evaluated on several datasets and shown to outperform TabDDPM in some aspects, such as reducing the number of dimensions and improving training and sampling times. However, the paper lacks detailed experimental validation, particularly in terms of the impact on model performance and the practical applicability of the method in scenarios with a large number of categories.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel method called Residual Bit Vector (ResBit) for representing categorical data, which is inspired by Residual Vector Quantization and aims to address the "out-of-index" problem in TabDDPM.
- The method is technically sound and well-written, with clear explanations of the proposed method and its implementation.
- The paper presents several experiments that demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed method in reducing the number of dimensions required for representing categorical data and in improving training and sampling times.
- The paper provides a comprehensive evaluation of the proposed method, including a detailed comparison with existing methods and a thorough analysis of the experimental results.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks detailed experimental validation, particularly in terms of the impact of the proposed method on the performance of the model and the practical applicability of the method in scenarios with a large number of categories.
- The paper does not sufficiently discuss the limitations of the proposed method, such as the potential for increased model complexity and the impact on model performance when handling a large number of categories.
- The paper could benefit from more detailed discussions on the choice of parameters and their impact on the performance of the method.

- The paper could benefit from a more comprehensive discussion on the practical implications of the proposed method, particularly in terms of its scalability and applicability in real-world scenarios.
