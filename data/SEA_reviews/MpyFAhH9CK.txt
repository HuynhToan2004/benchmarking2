 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel reinforcement learning (RL) framework for controlling reconfigurable soft robots, which can adapt their morphology to solve complex tasks. The authors propose a coarse-to-fine policy learning method that enables the robots to undergo fine-grained morphology changes, which is validated through experiments on a new benchmark, Morphological Maze. The framework integrates morphology change, locomotion, and environment interaction into a unified action space, using a coarse-to-fine curriculum to train policies that effectively adapt to different morphologies. The paper also presents a comprehensive RL benchmark for reconfigurable soft robots, which is expected to advance research in this field.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel RL framework for controlling reconfigurable soft robots, which is a significant advancement in the field of robotics.
- The proposed method, which includes a coarse-to-fine policy learning method, is well-explained and supported by empirical results that demonstrate its effectiveness.
- The paper is well-written, making it accessible and easy to understand, with clear figures that aid in comprehension.
- The introduction of a new benchmark, Morphological Maze, provides a valuable tool for evaluating the performance of reconfigurable soft robots, which is crucial for advancing research in this area.
- The paper's approach to integrating morphology change, locomotion, and environment interaction into a unified action space is innovative and potentially impactful for the field.
