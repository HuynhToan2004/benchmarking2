 **Summary:**
The paper introduces DSpodFL, a decentralized federated learning algorithm designed to address resource heterogeneity among clients by allowing them to perform SGD and aggregations sporadically. This approach aims to reduce computational and communication costs while maintaining convergence guarantees. Theoretical analysis is provided to justify the convergence of the algorithm, and experiments on the Fashion-MNIST dataset demonstrate the method's effectiveness in terms of delay and accuracy. However, the paper is critiqued for its limited novelty, as it builds on existing techniques, and the experimental evaluation is seen as insufficient to fully validate the claims.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, clear, and easy to follow, with a clear motivation for the proposed method.
- The method is simple and intuitive, making it easy to implement.
- The paper provides a comprehensive theoretical analysis, including convergence analysis, which is supported by a detailed proof.
- The proposed method is practical and can be applied to various scenarios, including resource-constrained devices.
- The paper provides a detailed comparison of the proposed method with existing methods, highlighting the advantages of the proposed approach.

**Weaknesses:**
- The novelty of the paper is limited as the proposed method is a combination of existing techniques such as sporadic SGD and aggregation, which have been studied in prior works.
- The convergence analysis assumes constant step sizes, which may not be practical in all scenarios.
- The experimental evaluation is insufficient and does not convincingly demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed method, especially in terms of accuracy and resource efficiency.
- The paper lacks a detailed discussion on the limitations of the proposed method, particularly in scenarios where the method may not perform optimally.
- The assumptions made in the theoretical analysis, such as the smoothness and strong convexity of the local objective functions, are not sufficiently justified or explained.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors provide more detailed experimental results, including the convergence rate and the impact of different hyperparameters on the performance of the proposed method?
- How does the proposed method perform in scenarios with a larger number of clients, and what are the implications for resource efficiency and accuracy?
- Can the authors clarify the assumptions made in the theoretical analysis, particularly the assumptions regarding the smoothness and strong convexity of the local objective functions?
- How does the proposed method compare to other recent works in the field of decentralized federated learning, such as those mentioned in the review?
- Can the authors provide a more detailed comparison of the computational and communication costs between the proposed method and other baselines, especially in terms of the number of iterations required to achieve convergence?
