 **Summary:**
The paper introduces the Evolving Graph Fourier Transform (EFT), a novel spectral transform designed for dynamic graphs, which captures evolving graph spectra and enhances computational efficiency by decomposing the transformation process. EFT is proposed as an invertible spectral transform that effectively captures the structural and positional properties of evolving graphs, which is crucial for downstream tasks. The paper provides a theoretical framework, demonstrating the theoretical bounds of the difference between EFT and the exact solution to the variational form, and analyzing its properties. Extensive experiments validate the effectiveness of the proposed method on large-scale and standard temporal graph benchmarks, showing superior performance compared to existing methods.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel transform, EFT, which captures the evolving representations on temporal graphs, enhancing computational efficiency and effectively capturing the evolving graph's structural and positional properties.
- Theoretical bounds are provided for the difference between EFT and the exact solution to the variational form, and the properties of EFT are analyzed.
- The paper is well-written, with clear explanations and detailed derivations of the proposed method, making it easy to follow.
