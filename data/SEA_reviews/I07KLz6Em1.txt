 **Summary:**
The paper introduces QuantEase, a novel post-training quantization method for large language models (LLMs) that utilizes a layer-wise quantization framework and a cyclic coordinate descent algorithm to minimize quantization error. This approach avoids memory-expensive matrix inversions and decompositions, making it scalable and efficient. QuantEase is compared against existing methods such as GPTQ and AWQ, showing improvements in accuracy and efficiency, particularly in low-bit regimes. The method is also evaluated on various LLMs, including BLOOM and OPT, demonstrating its effectiveness across different models and datasets. The paper also discusses the outlier-aware variant of QuantEase, which can handle extreme weights and improve accuracy.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces QuantEase, a novel post-training quantization method for large language models (LLMs) that leverages a layer-wise quantization framework and a cyclic coordinate descent algorithm to minimize quantization error.
- QuantEase does not require matrix inversion or decomposition, making it more efficient and scalable compared to other methods.
- The method achieves state-of-the-art performance in terms of perplexity and zero-shot accuracy across various LLMs and datasets, showing relative improvements of up to 15% over methods such as GPTQ.
- The outlier-aware variant of QuantEase can handle extreme weights and improve accuracy, particularly in the near-3 or sub-3 bit quantization regimes.
- The paper is well-written, clear, and easy to follow, with detailed explanations of the methodology and experimental results.
- The proposed QuantEase is an efficient and effective method for LLM quantization, as demonstrated by its ability to quantize models with up to 66B parameters on a single V100 GPU.
- The paper includes comprehensive experiments that compare QuantEase with other state-of-the-art methods, providing a thorough evaluation of its performance.
