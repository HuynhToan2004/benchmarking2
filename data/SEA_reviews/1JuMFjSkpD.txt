 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel approach to fair machine learning by utilizing distance covariance as a measure of fairness, aiming to promote independence between sensitive attributes and model predictions. The method integrates this measure into a machine learning process using a Lagrangian dual method for optimization, showing convergence guarantees for batch computations. Experiments are conducted on various datasets, demonstrating the method's effectiveness in improving fairness and accuracy. However, concerns are raised about the clarity of the paper, the lack of comprehensive comparisons with existing methods, and the potential over-reliance on the distance covariance measure, which may not fully address fairness issues.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel approach using distance covariance as a measure of fairness, which is both theoretically sound and practically applicable.
- The method is shown to be effective in improving fairness and accuracy through empirical results on various datasets.
- The theoretical analysis and proof of convergence guarantees for batch computations are well-received, providing a solid foundation for the proposed method.
- The paper is well-written, making it accessible and easy to follow, which is crucial for academic papers.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a clear definition and justification for the fairness metric used, particularly in relation to demographic parity and equalized odds.
- There is a notable absence of comparisons with other fairness metrics and existing methods, which limits the understanding of the method's relative effectiveness.
- The paper suffers from issues with clarity and readability, with some sections being difficult to understand and others containing typographical errors.
- The method's reliance on the distance covariance measure, while innovative, may not fully address fairness issues as it does not necessarily ensure independence between sensitive attributes and model predictions.
- The paper does not sufficiently address the potential societal impacts of the proposed method, which is a critical aspect of ethical machine learning research.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors clarify how the fairness metric used in the paper relates to demographic parity and equalized odds, and provide a clear definition of what constitutes a "fair" model according to this metric?
- How does the proposed method compare to other existing methods in terms of effectiveness and efficiency? Could the authors provide a more detailed comparison in future work?
- Given the potential limitations of the distance covariance measure in fully addressing fairness issues, how does the method handle scenarios where sensitive attributes are highly correlated with model predictions?
- Could the authors expand on the societal impacts of their method, particularly in terms of potential biases or ethical concerns that might arise from its use?
- How does the method handle different types of sensitive attributes, such as continuous variables like age, which might not be easily represented by the distance covariance measure?
- Could the authors provide a more detailed explanation of the theoretical results, particularly the proof of Theorem 3, to enhance the paper's clarity and accessibility?
