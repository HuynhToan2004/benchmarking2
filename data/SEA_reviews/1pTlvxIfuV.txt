 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel approach to discrete diffusion models for text generation, focusing on reparameterizing the backward process to enhance training and sampling efficiency. This approach, termed Reparameterized Discrete Diffusion Models (RDMs), aims to simplify the training process and improve the generation quality by reweighting the cross-entropy loss. The model's effectiveness is demonstrated through experiments on various benchmarks, showing improvements over existing diffusion models and autoregressive baselines. The paper also proposes a new training objective that simplifies the training process and a sampling strategy that routes tokens based on their confidence scores.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel approach to discrete diffusion models, focusing on reparameterizing the backward process which significantly simplifies the training process and improves the generation quality.
- The authors have provided a comprehensive analysis of the proposed method, including a detailed derivation of the reparameterization trick and its implications for training and sampling.
- The paper is well-written, clear, and easy to follow, making it accessible to a broad audience.
- The experimental results demonstrate that the proposed method outperforms existing discrete diffusion models and autoregressive baselines, showing significant improvements in text generation capabilities.
- The paper includes a new training objective and sampling strategy, which are innovative and contribute to the field of diffusion models for text generation.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper's focus on simplifying the training process for discrete diffusion models might not be as significant as claimed, as the main differences from existing models are in the training scheme and the reweighting strategy.
- The novelty of the proposed method is questioned, as it closely resembles existing models such as the CMLM and the D3PM, which also use a routing mechanism.
- The paper lacks a detailed discussion on the limitations of the proposed method, which is crucial for understanding its applicability and scope.
- There is a lack of discussion on the potential negative societal impacts of the proposed method, which is essential for ethical considerations.
- The paper does not include a detailed comparison with other non-autoregressive models like CMLM, which could provide a more comprehensive evaluation of the proposed method.
- The paper could benefit from a more detailed analysis of the computational costs associated with the proposed method, especially in comparison to other models.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors clarify how the proposed method differs from existing models like the CMLM and the D3PM, particularly in terms of the routing mechanism and the training scheme?
- How does the proposed method compare in terms of computational efficiency and training time with other models like GPT-2, especially when considering the number of parameters and the training steps?
- Could the authors provide more details on the training and sampling procedures, especially in the appendix, to enhance the clarity and completeness of the paper?
- What are the specific advantages of the proposed method over other non-autoregressive models like CMLM, especially in terms of generation quality and efficiency?
- How does the proposed method handle variable-length sequences, and what are the potential challenges or limitations in this regard?
- Could the authors discuss the potential negative societal impacts of the proposed method and suggest ways to mitigate these risks?
