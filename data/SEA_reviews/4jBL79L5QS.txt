 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel framework for traffic engineering in computer networks using reinforcement learning (RL) and a simulator based on ns-3. The framework models the routing problem as a Swarm Markov Decision Process (SwarMDP), where agents collaborate to optimize routing based on network state observations. The authors present a training and evaluation framework that allows for scalability and real-time responsiveness, and they compare their approach to traditional routing protocols such as OSPF and EIGRP. The paper also discusses the challenges of real-time traffic engineering and the limitations of existing heuristics, proposing a new approach that could potentially outperform traditional methods. However, the paper has been critiqued for its limited evaluation scope, lack of comprehensive comparison with state-of-the-art methods, and potential scalability issues due to the complexity of the routing problem.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, clearly structured, and easy to follow, making it accessible to a broad audience.
- The authors provide a detailed description of their framework, including its implementation in ns-3, which enhances its credibility and reproducibility.
- The use of reinforcement learning (RL) for traffic engineering is a novel approach that has the potential to outperform traditional methods in dynamic network environments.
- The paper addresses a significant and timely problem in network engineering, focusing on the challenges of routing in dynamic and complex network scenarios.
- The framework is designed to be scalable and flexible, allowing for the evaluation of different network topologies and traffic conditions, which is crucial for real-time traffic engineering.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a comprehensive evaluation of the framework's performance against state-of-the-art methods, which could undermine the credibility of the results.
- The comparison with traditional routing protocols such as OSPF and EIGRP is not detailed enough, and the advantages of the proposed method over these protocols are not clearly articulated.
- The paper does not adequately address the scalability of the routing problem, which could limit the applicability of the framework to larger and more complex network scenarios.
- The evaluation is limited to small-scale scenarios, which may not adequately demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed method in real-world, large-scale network environments.
- The paper does not provide a detailed discussion on the limitations of the framework, which could help in understanding the scope and applicability of the proposed method.

**Questions:**
- Could you provide more details on how the proposed method compares with OSPF and EIGRP in terms of performance metrics such as packet delay and drop ratio?
- How does the proposed method handle the scalability issues associated with the routing problem, particularly in larger and more complex network scenarios?
- Can you discuss the potential advantages of using RL for traffic engineering compared to traditional methods, particularly in terms of adaptability and real-time responsiveness?
- How does the framework handle the complexity of routing decisions in dynamic and unpredictable network environments?
