 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel reinforcement learning (RL) algorithm for continuous-time control of affine nonlinear systems, leveraging Kleinman's algorithm to address nonlinear control problems. This algorithm, named RCI, is designed to handle model-based learning and provides theoretical guarantees such as convergence, optimality, and closed-loop stability. The methodology is compared against existing deep RL and ADP approaches, demonstrating competitive performance in terms of data efficiency and convergence speed. The paper also includes extensive experimental evaluations on various systems, showcasing the algorithm's effectiveness and efficiency.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel RL algorithm for continuous-time control of affine nonlinear systems, which is a significant contribution to the field.
- The methodology is well-explained and supported by extensive experimental evaluations, demonstrating the algorithm's effectiveness and efficiency.
- The paper provides a comprehensive literature review, highlighting the differences between the proposed approach and existing methods.
- Theoretical guarantees such as convergence, optimality, and closed-loop stability are provided, which are crucial for the application of the proposed algorithm.
- The paper is well-written, with clear explanations of the algorithm and its theoretical underpinnings, making it accessible to a broad audience.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper primarily focuses on linear systems, which may limit its applicability to more complex, non-linear systems.
- The theoretical analysis and proofs, while solid, are not particularly novel or groundbreaking, relying heavily on existing methods like the Kleinman algorithm.
- The paper's experimental setup and results are not thoroughly validated, with some results, such as those in Figure 1, being inconclusive or misleading.
- There is a lack of discussion on the limitations and societal impacts of the proposed method, which is crucial for understanding its broader implications.
- The paper does not sufficiently compare its method with other existing methods, particularly those that might provide similar or better performance guarantees.
- The paper's presentation and organization could be improved, particularly in terms of clarity and the logical flow of the content.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors provide more details on how the proposed method can be extended to handle non-linear systems?
- What are the specific advantages of the proposed method over existing methods like the Kleinman algorithm?
- How does the method handle the challenge of integrating reinforcement learning with physics-based control systems?
- Could the authors clarify the role and impact of the reference command input in the algorithm's performance?
- In terms of practical implementation, what are the key challenges or limitations that need to be addressed?
- How does the method perform in scenarios where the system dynamics are non-linear or the initial conditions are unknown?
- Could the authors discuss the potential societal impacts of their method and how these might be mitigated?
