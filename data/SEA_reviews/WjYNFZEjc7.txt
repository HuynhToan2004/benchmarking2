 **Summary:**
The paper introduces the Head Information Bottleneck (HIB) method to evaluate the contribution of attention heads in transformer-based models, specifically focusing on speech processing tasks. The HIB method utilizes the information bottleneck theory to estimate the mutual information between output and input in each attention head, which helps in pruning less critical attention heads. The authors conducted experiments on various tasks such as keyword spotting, speaker identification, and intent classification, showing that the HIB method can effectively prune attention heads while maintaining high accuracy. However, the paper has been critiqued for its lack of novelty, insufficient experimental validation, and unclear methodological details.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel approach to evaluating the contribution of attention heads in transformer-based models, which is a significant advancement in the field of speech processing.
- The authors have conducted extensive experiments to demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed Head Information Bottleneck (HIB) method in pruning attention heads while maintaining high accuracy.
- The paper is well-written, making it easy to follow, and the proposed method is straightforward and effective.
- The methodology of the paper is well-explained, and the experiments are extensive, covering various tasks such as keyword spotting, speaker identification, and intent classification.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks sufficient experimental validation to support the claims made, particularly concerning the effectiveness of the HIB method in speech tasks.
- The methodology section of the paper is not clearly explained, especially the application of the information bottleneck and the derivation of equations, which could confuse readers.
- The paper does not provide a comprehensive comparison with existing methods, such as attention-based methods, which could have strengthened the argument for the effectiveness of the proposed method.
- The paper does not discuss the limitations of the proposed method, which could help in understanding its applicability and potential drawbacks.
- The paper lacks a detailed discussion on the hyperparameters used in the experiments, which could affect the reproducibility and reliability of the results.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors provide more detailed experimental validation to support the claims made in the paper, particularly concerning the effectiveness of the HIB method in speech tasks?
- How does the proposed method compare with existing methods, such as attention-based methods, in terms of performance and efficiency?
- Could the authors clarify the methodology section of the paper, especially the application of the information bottleneck and the derivation of equations, to make it more accessible to readers?
- How do the authors ensure the reproducibility of the results, particularly concerning the hyperparameters used in the experiments?
- Could the authors discuss the limitations of the proposed method and how these might affect its applicability and effectiveness in different scenarios?
