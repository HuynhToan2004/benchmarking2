 **Summary:**
The paper introduces NEFTune, a novel training method for language models that adds noise to embedding vectors during instruction fine-tuning. This approach aims to improve model performance by reducing overfitting to specific instruction datasets. The method is tested across various models and datasets, showing significant improvements in conversational ability and answer quality, as measured by AlpacaEval and other benchmarks. However, concerns are raised about the novelty of the method, as similar techniques have been previously used in the adversarial ML literature. The paper also lacks a comprehensive discussion on the limitations and broader impacts of the method.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, clear, and easy to follow, making it accessible to a broad audience.
- The proposed method is simple, effective, and can be easily integrated into existing instruction fine-tuning pipelines, making it practical for real-world applications.
- The paper presents significant improvements in conversational ability and answer quality, as measured by AlpacaEval and other benchmarks, demonstrating the effectiveness of the method.
- The method is supported by a thorough analysis, including an ablation study that shows the method's effectiveness in reducing overfitting to instruction datasets.
- The paper provides a detailed explanation of the method's operation, including the choice of parameters and the rationale behind their selection.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a comprehensive discussion on the limitations and broader impacts of the method, which is crucial for understanding its practical applicability and potential drawbacks.
- The novelty of the method is questionable as similar techniques have been previously used in the adversarial ML literature, particularly in the context of adversarial training.
- The paper does not provide a detailed comparison with other regularization techniques or baseline methods, which could help in understanding the method's relative effectiveness.
- The evaluation of the method is limited to specific datasets and models, and it would be beneficial to see how the method performs across a broader range of models and datasets.
- The paper does not discuss the potential negative societal impacts of the method, which is a critical aspect of ethical AI research.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors provide a more detailed discussion on the limitations and broader impacts of the method?
- How does the proposed method compare with other regularization techniques or baseline methods in terms of effectiveness and efficiency?
- Can the authors provide more insights into why the method works, particularly in terms of its impact on model generalization and overfitting?
- How does the method perform across a broader range of models and datasets, and what are the implications of these findings for the practical applicability of the method?
- Could the authors provide a more detailed discussion on the potential negative societal impacts of the method and how these risks could be mitigated?
