 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel method for modeling dynamic 3D scenes using a set of 4D Gaussians, where each Gaussian is parameterized by a 4D ellipsoid and a 4D rotation matrix. This approach allows for real-time rendering of dynamic scenes by optimizing a collection of 4D primitives, which are used to synthesize novel views at any desired time. The method is evaluated on both synthetic and real-world datasets, showing improvements over existing methods in terms of rendering quality and efficiency. The paper also includes ablation studies to validate the effectiveness of the proposed method, although it faces criticism for its limited novelty and the lack of comparison with recent state-of-the-art methods.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, easy to follow, and presents a clear and logical structure, making it accessible to a broad audience.
- The proposed method of using 4D Gaussian splatting for dynamic scene modeling is novel and innovative, providing a new perspective on how to represent dynamic scenes.
- The method achieves real-time rendering of dynamic scenes, which is a significant advantage over many existing methods.
- The paper includes extensive ablation studies that demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed method, particularly in handling complex dynamic scenes.
- The results presented are impressive, showing a clear improvement over existing methods in both synthetic and real-world datasets.
- The paper is well-motivated, addressing the challenge of modeling dynamic scenes from 2D images and generating diverse views over time, which is a critical issue in computer vision and graphics.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed comparison with recent state-of-the-art methods, such as those mentioned in the review, which could undermine the perceived novelty and effectiveness of the proposed method.
- The paper does not sufficiently address the limitations of its approach, such as the inability to handle very complex scenes or scenes with large motion, which could affect the generalizability of the findings.
- The paper does not provide sufficient implementation details, which could hinder reproducibility and further research by other researchers.
- There are concerns about the novelty of the approach, as it seems to build upon existing methods like 3D Gaussian splatting, without sufficient differentiation or justification for its superiority.
- The paper does not adequately address the scalability of the method, particularly in handling very complex scenes or scenes with large motion, which could affect the practical applicability of the proposed method.

**Questions:**
- How does the proposed method compare to recent state-of-the-art methods, particularly in terms of rendering quality and efficiency?
- Can the authors provide more details on the experimental setup, such as the specific hardware used and the training parameters, to ensure reproducibility and transparency?
- How does the proposed method handle very complex scenes or scenes with large motion, and what are the potential limitations or challenges in such scenarios?
- Could the authors elaborate on the novel contributions of their approach beyond the existing methods, particularly in terms of technical innovations or theoretical advancements?
- How does the method handle the optimization of the 4D Gaussian parameters, and what are the computational requirements for this process?
- Can the authors provide more visual results or comparisons to better demonstrate the effectiveness of the method in different scenarios, such as monocular vs. multi-view settings?
