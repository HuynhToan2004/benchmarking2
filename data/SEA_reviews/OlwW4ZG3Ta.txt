 **Summary:**
The paper introduces Reflective Policy Optimization (RPO), a novel on-policy reinforcement learning algorithm that integrates the effects of subsequent state-action pairs into the policy optimization process. The method is based on a modified lower bound that includes the current state-action pair and the next state-action pair, aiming to enhance sample efficiency. Theoretical analysis and empirical results are provided to support the effectiveness of the proposed method, which is compared against existing algorithms like TRPO and PPO. The paper claims that RPO offers a tighter lower bound for policy performance, which could potentially lead to more efficient policy optimization. However, the paper has been critiqued for its unclear writing, lack of detailed algorithmic descriptions, and insufficient experimental validation.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel on-policy reinforcement learning algorithm, Reflective Policy Optimization (RPO), which integrates the effects of subsequent state-action pairs into the policy optimization process, potentially improving sample efficiency.
- The authors provide a theoretical analysis of the proposed method, demonstrating a tighter lower bound for policy performance compared to existing algorithms like TRPO and PPO.
- The empirical results show that RPO outperforms other algorithms in terms of average return and convergence speed in continuous action space environments, indicating its effectiveness.
- The paper is well-organized and clearly written, making it accessible to readers.
- The methodology of the paper is novel and innovative, with a focus on utilizing the effects of subsequent state-action pairs, which is a significant departure from traditional methods.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a clear and detailed description of the RPO algorithm, making it difficult for readers to fully understand the methodology.
- The experimental results are limited, and the paper does not include sufficient ablation studies or comparisons with a broader range of algorithms, which could undermine the robustness of the findings.
- The paper suffers from unclear writing and grammatical errors, which can hinder comprehension and detract from the professional quality of the manuscript.
- The theoretical analysis and empirical results are not sufficiently linked, which could weaken the overall impact of the paper.
- The paper lacks a comprehensive discussion on the limitations and potential negative societal impacts of the proposed method.
- The paper does not provide sufficient details on the experimental setup, such as the number of seeds used and the statistical significance of the results.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors provide a more detailed and clearer description of the RPO algorithm, including a step-by-step explanation of how it operates?
- How does the RPO algorithm compare to other existing algorithms in terms of computational efficiency and memory requirements?
- What are the specific advantages of the RPO algorithm over other methods, particularly in terms of sample efficiency and policy optimization?
- How does the RPO algorithm handle the potential issues of high variance, especially in environments with long-horizon rewards?
- Can the authors provide more detailed experimental results, including additional ablation studies and comparisons with a broader range of algorithms?
- How does the RPO algorithm ensure that the policy remains monotonically improving, and what measures are in place to prevent policy collapse or instability?
