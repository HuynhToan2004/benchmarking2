 **Summary:**
The paper investigates the calibration of language models (LMs) across different slices of a distribution, focusing on the miscalibration of models in narrower slices despite being well-calibrated on broader distributions. It proposes a novel method for calibrating LMs using a few-shot recalibration technique that predicts precision curves for specific slices of a distribution. The method is tested on various datasets, including MMLU and XNLI, and demonstrates improvements in calibration accuracy compared to existing methods. The paper also discusses the practical implications of these findings, suggesting that the proposed method could be beneficial in real-world applications where models need to be calibrated for specific user query distributions.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, easy to follow, and the proposed method is clear and straightforward.
- The paper addresses an important problem in the field of calibrating large language models (LLMs) for specific slices of a distribution, which is crucial for real-world applications.
- The experiments are well-designed, and the results show improvements over existing methods, demonstrating the effectiveness of the proposed method.
- The paper introduces a novel approach to predicting precision curves, which is more flexible and adaptable than traditional calibration curves.
- The paper provides a thorough evaluation of the proposed method across multiple datasets and baseline models, showing its robustness and applicability.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper could benefit from a more comprehensive discussion of related work, particularly in the area of calibration and uncertainty estimation in LLMs.
- The choice of evaluation metrics, such as the Expected Calibration Error (ECE), is questioned due to its potential to hide calibration issues in specific slices of the distribution.
- The paper's focus on multiclass classification tasks limits its applicability to other types of tasks, such as natural language generation.
- The paper does not sufficiently address the scalability of the proposed method to larger models or different types of tasks.
- The paper's approach to calibration, particularly the use of precision curves, is not well-explained or justified, which could limit the understanding of its effectiveness.
- The paper's evaluation could be strengthened by including more diverse datasets and benchmarks, such as those from the OpenAI Uncertainty Benchmark.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors provide more detailed explanations or examples of how the precision curve is used to achieve the desired level of precision, reduce calibration error, or maximize utility?
- How does the proposed method perform on different types of tasks, such as natural language generation?
- How does the method handle the scalability to larger models or different types of tasks?
- Can the authors clarify the choice of evaluation metrics and discuss the potential limitations of ECE in evaluating calibration in specific slices of a distribution?
- How does the method perform on different types of datasets, particularly those that are not multiclass classification tasks?
- Could the authors provide more details on the training procedure, such as the learning rate and training steps, to better understand the impact of these hyperparameters on the results?
