 **Summary:**
The paper introduces Push, a library designed for concurrent execution of Bayesian deep learning (BDL) inference algorithms on multi-GPU hardware. It represents an input neural network (NN) as a particle and enables the creation of particles, allowing for asynchronous communication among them. This approach simplifies the scaling of particles across GPUs, enhancing the efficiency of BDL inference. The library has been tested on various tasks and shown to scale effectively across multiple GPUs. However, the paper lacks a detailed comparison with existing libraries and lacks a discussion on the limitations and societal impacts of the proposed approach.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, easy to follow, and clearly explains the methodology and implementation of the proposed library, Push.
- The use of a particle abstraction for neural networks (NNs) is innovative and facilitates the implementation of Bayesian deep learning (BDL) algorithms.
- The library is evaluated on several tasks, demonstrating its effectiveness and scalability.
- The paper provides a detailed explanation of the design choices and implementation details, which are crucial for understanding the system's operation and potential impact.
- The use of the actor model for concurrency and the integration of the node event loop (NEL) are novel and well-explained, enhancing the understanding of the system's architecture.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a thorough discussion on the limitations of the proposed approach, including potential issues with scalability, computational efficiency, and the generalizability of the results.
- There is no comparison with existing libraries, which makes it difficult to assess the relative advantages of Push.
- The paper does not discuss the societal impacts of the proposed system, which is a critical aspect of any technological innovation.
- The paper lacks a detailed discussion on the computational efficiency of the system, especially concerning the use of the reparameterization trick and the handling of stochastic nodes in the neural network.
- The experiments presented in the paper are limited in scope, focusing primarily on vision and scientific machine learning tasks, and do not include a broader range of tasks that could demonstrate the generalizability of the system.
- The paper does not include a discussion on the limitations of the system, which could help in understanding the scope and applicability of the proposed approach.
