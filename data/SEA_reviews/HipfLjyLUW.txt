 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a hierarchical generative model, CHGFlowNet, for crystal structure generation, leveraging Generative Flow Networks (GFlowNet) to decompose the material space into hierarchical subspaces. This approach facilitates efficient exploration and generation of diverse crystal structures with optimized properties. The model integrates physics-informed constraints and a hierarchical state space, allowing for multi-level policy networks to operate on action abstraction. The paper compares CHGFlowNet with existing methods, showing improvements in validity, diversity, and stability, and includes an ablation study on the reward function's components. Despite these strengths, the paper faces criticism for its clarity in presentation, lack of comprehensive experimental validation, and the need for more detailed discussions on related works.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel hierarchical generative model, CHGFlowNet, which effectively explores the material space by decomposing it into a hierarchy of subspaces, enhancing the generation of crystal structures with optimized properties and diverse configurations.
- The model integrates physics-informed constraints, which are crucial for generating stable structures and improving material properties.
- The use of a hierarchical state space and a multi-level policy network for crystal structure generation is innovative and allows for the embedding of physics and chemistry knowledge to guide the generation process.
- The paper is well-structured, with a clear presentation of the problem and a logical flow of ideas. It includes detailed experimental setups, which are crucial for reproducibility.
- The proposed method demonstrates improvements over existing methods in terms of validity, diversity, and stability, as evidenced by the results presented.

**Weaknesses:**
