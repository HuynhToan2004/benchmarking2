 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel approach to time series forecasting by utilizing Koopman theory to model dynamics under distribution shifts. It proposes a meta-Koopman operator that can be decomposed into linear combinations of Koopman operators, which are approximated by finite matrix multiplications. The authors develop an auto-encoder framework to implement the meta-Koopman decomposition, which can handle distribution shifts effectively. Extensive experiments on real-world datasets demonstrate the superiority of the proposed model over existing baselines. However, the paper lacks detailed theoretical proofs and clear definitions of key terms such as "temporal distribution shifts."

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, clear, and easy to follow, with a clear motivation and problem definition.
- The proposed method is novel, leveraging Koopman theory to model dynamics under distribution shifts, which is an innovative approach.
- The experiments are extensive, covering multiple real-world datasets, and the results show the superiority of the proposed method over existing baselines.
- The paper is well-organized, with a clear structure that makes it easy to understand the proposed method and its implementation.
- The theoretical analysis and mathematical derivations are well-explained, providing a solid foundation for the proposed method.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks detailed theoretical proofs and definitions for key terms, such as "temporal distribution shifts" and "meta-Koopman operators."
- The experimental settings and baselines are not clearly described, and the choice of datasets and their characteristics (e.g., long-term forecasting, distribution shifts) is not adequately justified.
- The paper does not sufficiently discuss the limitations of the proposed method, such as its applicability to different types of time series data and the computational complexity of the Koopman operator.
- There are inconsistencies in the experimental results and a lack of detailed analysis on the performance of the proposed method compared to other methods, particularly in handling distribution shifts.
- The paper could benefit from a more comprehensive literature review and a clearer discussion on the relationship between the proposed method and existing works in the field.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors provide a detailed definition and explanation of "temporal distribution shifts" and "meta-Koopman operators"?
- How does the proposed method handle different types of time series data, such as those with long-term forecasting or significant distribution shifts?
- Could the authors clarify the experimental settings and baseline comparisons, particularly in terms of the datasets used and the conditions under which the experiments were conducted?
- What are the specific advantages of using the Koopman operator over other methods for time series forecasting, and how does it compare in terms of computational complexity and accuracy?
- How does the proposed method ensure that the Koopman operator remains linear and effective under different conditions, such as distribution shifts?
- Could the authors provide a more detailed analysis of the experimental results, particularly in terms of the performance of the proposed method compared to other methods in handling distribution shifts?
