 **Summary:**
The paper introduces SpecAR-Net, a novel model for analyzing time series data in both time and frequency domains. SpecAR-Net utilizes a short-time Fourier transform (STFT) to transform the time series into spectrograms, which are then processed using a multi-scale convolution module. This model is designed to capture various patterns in time series data, including trends, seasonality, and sudden changes, by analyzing both time and frequency domains. The paper presents several experiments demonstrating the model's effectiveness across various tasks such as classification, anomaly detection, imputation, and forecasting. However, concerns were raised about the clarity of the experimental setup, the lack of detailed comparisons with existing methods, and the generalizability of the results due to the limited diversity in datasets.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written and easy to follow, with a clear structure that makes it accessible to readers.
- The proposed method, SpecAR-Net, shows strong performance across multiple tasks, including classification, anomaly detection, imputation, and forecasting, as demonstrated by empirical results.
- The paper provides a detailed description of the proposed method, which is supported by a thorough set of experiments.
- The methodology is well-explained, and the paper is generally easy to understand, with clear figures and a clear explanation of the method's components.
- The paper addresses an important and relevant problem in the field of time series analysis, which is crucial for various applications.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed comparison with existing methods, particularly in terms of computational complexity and memory requirements.
- There is a lack of clarity in the experimental setup, particularly in the descriptions of the datasets used and the evaluation metrics.
- The paper does not sufficiently differentiate its method from existing works, such as TimesNet, which also uses spectrograms for time series analysis.
- The paper does not provide sufficient detail on the choice of hyperparameters and their impact on the results.
- There are several typographical and formatting errors that need correction.
- The paper could benefit from a more detailed discussion on the limitations of the proposed method and potential avenues for future research.
- The paper could be improved by including more diverse datasets and benchmarks to better evaluate the generalizability of the results.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors provide more details on the choice of hyperparameters and their impact on the results?
- How does SpecAR-Net perform when compared to other methods in terms of computational complexity and memory requirements?
- Could the authors clarify the experimental setup, particularly in terms of the datasets used and the evaluation metrics?
- How does SpecAR-Net compare to other methods in terms of handling different types of time series data, such as those with different sampling rates or different types of noise?
- Could the authors provide more details on the limitations of the proposed method and potential avenues for future research?
