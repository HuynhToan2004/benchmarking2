 **Summary:**
The paper introduces MPXGAT, a novel attention-based deep learning model designed for embedding multiplex graphs. MPXGAT leverages both intra-layer and inter-layer connections to perform link prediction tasks across different graph layers, demonstrating superior performance over existing state-of-the-art methods. The model's effectiveness is validated through extensive experiments on three benchmark datasets, showcasing its potential in various applications where multiple types of relationships exist. Despite its innovative approach, concerns were raised about the clarity of the presentation and the limited comparison with existing methods.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel method for embedding multiplex graphs, which is a significant contribution to the field.
- The methodology is supported by extensive experiments on three benchmark datasets, demonstrating the effectiveness of the proposed model.
- The paper is well-written, making it easy to follow, and the authors provide a clear and detailed explanation of the proposed method.
- The proposed method addresses a practical and important problem in graph representation learning, which is applicable to various real-world scenarios.
- The paper provides a detailed description of the method and its implementation, which is crucial for reproducibility and further research.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed description of the datasets used, which is essential for understanding the context and applicability of the proposed method.
- There is a lack of comparison with existing methods, particularly those that are specifically designed for multiplex graphs or similar problems.
- The presentation of the paper could be improved, particularly in the clarity of the equations and the overall structure of the content.
- The paper does not provide a detailed analysis of the limitations of the proposed method, which is crucial for understanding its applicability and scope.
- The paper does not include a discussion on the societal impact of the proposed method, which is a significant omission given the potential applications in various domains.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors provide a more detailed description of the datasets used, including their characteristics and the specific challenges they pose for the proposed method?
- How does the proposed method compare with existing methods, particularly those that are specifically designed for multiplex graphs or similar problems?
- What are the main limitations of the proposed method, and how do these limitations affect its applicability and performance in different scenarios?
- Could the authors provide a more detailed analysis of the results, particularly the performance of the proposed method on different types of graph layers and the impact of different hyperparameters on the results?
- How does the proposed method handle the problem of missing links in multiplex graphs, and what are the implications of this approach for the overall performance and applicability of the method?
- Can the authors discuss the potential societal impact of the proposed method, particularly in terms of its applications in areas such as social media analysis, healthcare, and other domains where graph representation learning is critical?
