 **Summary:**
The paper introduces Federated Freeze-A Low Rank Adapters (FFA-LoRA), a novel approach to enhance LoRA's performance in privacy-preserving federated learning (FL) settings. FFA-LoRA addresses the challenges of data heterogeneity, differential privacy, and hyperparameter sensitivity by freezing the non-zero initialized low-rank matrices and only fine-tuning the zero-initialized matrices. This methodology, supported by theoretical insights and extensive empirical evaluations, demonstrates improvements in performance and computational efficiency over vanilla LoRA. However, the paper lacks a comprehensive theoretical justification for the effectiveness of FFA-LoRA and does not compare it with other PEFT methods, which are critical for a complete validation of its contributions.

**Strengths:**
- The paper addresses a significant and timely issue of improving LoRA in privacy-preserving federated learning, which is crucial for advancing the field.
- The writing is clear, well-organized, and easy to follow, making it accessible to a broad audience.
- The paper provides a comprehensive analysis of the challenges in applying LoRA in privacy-preserving FL settings, which is crucial for understanding the limitations and potential improvements of LoRA in these scenarios.
- The proposed FFA-LoRA is a simple yet effective solution that addresses the identified challenges, demonstrating improvements over vanilla LoRA in terms of performance and computational efficiency.
- The paper includes a thorough empirical evaluation, which supports the claims made about the effectiveness of FFA-LoRA.
- The theoretical insights provided in the paper are well-articulated and contribute to the understanding of the proposed method.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a comprehensive theoretical justification for the effectiveness of FFA-LoRA, which could strengthen the argument for its superiority over other methods.
- There is a lack of comparison with other PEFT methods, which is essential for validating the claims made about the improvements of FFA-LoRA over existing methods.
- The paper does not provide a detailed comparison of the performance of FFA-LoRA with different hyperparameters, which could help in understanding the sensitivity of the method to different settings.
- The paper does not discuss the potential negative societal impacts of the proposed method, which is a critical consideration for any technological innovation.
- The paper's experimental results do not consistently outperform vanilla LoRA in all settings, raising questions about the generalizability of the proposed method.
- The paper does not adequately address the potential limitations of the proposed method, which could hinder its broader adoption.
