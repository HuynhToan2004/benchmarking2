 **Summary:**
The paper presents a novel multi-agent reinforcement learning (MARL) algorithm, RLD3, designed to address the challenges of designated driver dispatch in a fast-growing market. The algorithm incorporates group-sharing structures, frequent rewards, and heterogeneous costs to manage heterogeneity, sparsity, and scalability. It also employs window-lasting policy ensembles to manage long-term agent cross-effects. The paper includes a simulator based on real-world data to evaluate the performance of the algorithm, which is shown to outperform traditional optimization-based policies and existing DRL algorithms. The methodology includes the use of a Decentralized Partially Observed Markov Decision Process (Dec-POMDP) formulation and a designated driving simulator to model agent interactions.

**Strengths:**
- The paper addresses the critical challenge of designated driver dispatch, a fast-growing market with unique challenges such as slow driver movement and sparse orders.
- It introduces a novel MARL algorithm, RLD3, which effectively balances scalability, heterogeneity, and sparsity in the designated driver dispatch problem.
- The methodology includes the use of a Decentralized Partially Observed Markov Decision Process (Dec-POMDP) formulation and a designated driving simulator to model agent interactions.
- The paper demonstrates the effectiveness of the proposed method through extensive experiments that show RLD3 outperforms existing DRL and optimization methods.
- The algorithm's design includes a group-sharing structure, a specially designed reward, and a window-lasting agent interaction technique, which address the challenges of sparsity, scalability, and heterogeneity in the designated driver dispatch problem.
- The paper is well-written, with clear and concise presentation, and the methodology is well-explained, making it accessible to a broad audience.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a comprehensive discussion on the limitations of the proposed method, which could help in understanding the scope and applicability of the findings.
- The assumption that drivers fully comply with movement instructions may not be realistic in practical scenarios.
- The scalability of the proposed method is not thoroughly discussed, particularly in terms of the number of agents and the complexity of the environment.
- The paper does not adequately address the challenges of non-compliance in the designated driver dispatch problem, which could significantly impact the performance of the algorithm.
- The methodology section could benefit from more detailed explanations and examples to clarify the implementation and operation of the RLD3 algorithm.
- The paper lacks a detailed analysis of the computational complexity and resource requirements of the RLD3 algorithm, which could be crucial for practical implementation.
- There is a need for a more comprehensive comparison with state-of-the-art methods in the field of multi-agent reinforcement learning to establish the superiority of the proposed algorithm.

**Questions:**
- How does the proposed method perform in scenarios with a larger number of agents and more complex environments?
- Could you provide a more detailed explanation of the window-lasting agent interaction technique and its impact on the performance of the algorithm?
- How does the group-sharing structure affect the performance of the algorithm, and could you provide more insights into its implementation and operation?
- Could you elaborate on the design choices for the reward function and how it affects the learning process of the algorithm?
- How does the RLD3 algorithm handle non-compliance in the designated driver dispatch problem, and what are the potential impacts on the performance of the algorithm?
- Could you provide a detailed analysis of the computational complexity and resource requirements of the RLD3 algorithm?
