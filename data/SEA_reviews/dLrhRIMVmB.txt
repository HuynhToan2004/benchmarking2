 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel quantum algorithm for estimating Betti numbers in Topological Data Analysis (TDA), which is a key problem in TDA known to be intractable classically. The algorithm, named NISQ-TDA, is designed to operate on Noisy Intermediate-Scale Quantum (NISQ) devices, making it accessible with current technology. It utilizes a quantum rejection sampling technique and stochastic rank estimation to project onto the data-defined simplicial complex and estimate the rank of the Laplacian. The algorithm is analyzed theoretically and experimentally, showing potential improvements over classical methods in terms of depth and noise resilience. However, the paper lacks a detailed discussion on the experimental setup and does not provide a clear comparison with existing methods, particularly in terms of performance and practical applicability.

**Strengths:**
- The paper addresses a significant and relevant problem in the field of quantum computing, focusing on the estimation of Betti numbers which is known to be intractable classically.
- The proposed quantum algorithm is innovative, utilizing a novel approach to estimating the rank of the Laplacian, which is crucial for the computation of Betti numbers.
- The paper provides a detailed theoretical analysis and experimental results, demonstrating the potential of the proposed algorithm to outperform classical methods in terms of depth and noise resilience.
- The paper is well-written and clearly presents the theoretical and experimental aspects of the work, making it accessible and understandable to a broad audience.
- The authors have successfully implemented their algorithm on real quantum hardware, which is a significant achievement and shows the practical applicability of their work.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed discussion on the experimental setup, including the specific quantum devices used and the experimental conditions under which the measurements were taken.
- There is a lack of comparison with existing methods, particularly in terms of performance and practical applicability, which makes it difficult to assess the true novelty and effectiveness of the proposed algorithm.
- The paper does not adequately discuss the assumptions made in the theoretical analysis, which could limit the generalizability and applicability of the results.
- The practical application of the proposed algorithm is not clearly demonstrated, particularly in terms of its utility in real-world scenarios.
- The paper could benefit from a more comprehensive discussion on the limitations and potential challenges in implementing the algorithm on current NISQ devices.
