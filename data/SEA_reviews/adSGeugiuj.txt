 **Summary:**
The paper explores the relationship between higher-order central moments of the posterior distribution and the higher-order derivatives of the posterior mean in the context of Gaussian denoising. It provides a recursive formula to compute these moments, which are crucial for understanding the uncertainty quantification in denoising tasks. The authors demonstrate how these moments can be used to extract principal components of the posterior distribution, aiding in the estimation of full marginal distributions along one-dimensional directions. The theoretical insights are supported by experiments on various datasets, including MNIST, natural images, human faces, and microscopy images, showing that the proposed method can effectively capture uncertainty in different localized regions of images. The paper also discusses the practical implications of these findings, suggesting that they could potentially enhance the performance of denoisers by providing a deeper understanding of the noise and its effects on the denoised output.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel method for computing the principal components of the posterior distribution in Gaussian denoising, which is a significant contribution to the field.
- The authors provide a clear and detailed explanation of the theoretical results, which are supported by a variety of experimental results across different datasets, including MNIST, natural images, human faces, and microscopy images.
- The paper is well-written, making it easy to follow, and the visualizations provided effectively demonstrate the practical applications of the proposed method.
- The paper addresses an important problem in the field of uncertainty quantification, which is crucial for understanding the reliability and robustness of denoising models.
- The methodology is simple and can be applied in practical scenarios, showing potential for improving the performance of denoisers by providing a deeper understanding of the noise and its effects on the denoised output.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a comprehensive comparison with existing methods for computing principal components of the posterior distribution, which could have helped in better understanding the advantages and disadvantages of the proposed method.
- There is a concern regarding the practicality of the method, particularly in terms of computational efficiency and the stability of high-order numerical differentiation.
- The paper does not sufficiently discuss the limitations of the proposed method, which could be crucial for understanding its applicability and effectiveness in different scenarios.
- The method's effectiveness in high-resolution images and its scalability to larger models are not adequately addressed, raising questions about its generalizability and applicability in more complex or real-world scenarios.
- The paper could benefit from a more detailed discussion on the theoretical underpinnings of the recursive formula for the central moments and its implications for the shape of the posterior distribution.
