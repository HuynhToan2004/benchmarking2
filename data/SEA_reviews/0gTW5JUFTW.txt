 **Summary:**
The paper introduces TopoMLP, a method for driving topology reasoning, focusing on lane detection and traffic element detection. It utilizes a Bezier curve to represent 3D lane detection and a YOLOv8 object detector for 2D traffic detection. The methodology includes a two-stage detection process, followed by topology reasoning, with the aim of improving the detection of lane markings and traffic elements. The paper claims that TopoMLP achieves state-of-the-art performance on the OpenLane-V2 benchmark. However, concerns were raised regarding the novelty of the method, the clarity of the writing, and the effectiveness of the topology reasoning component.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-organized and easy to follow, with clear explanations of the methodology.
- The proposed method achieves state-of-the-art performance on the OpenLane-V2 benchmark.
- The paper provides a comprehensive ablation study, which helps in understanding the impact of different components of the method.
- The use of Bezier curve for 3D lane detection is a novel approach, which is highlighted in the paper.
- The paper introduces a new metric, TOP-*, which is more reasonable and could be beneficial for future research in the field.

**Weaknesses:**
- The novelty of the method is questioned as it primarily combines existing techniques without significant innovation.
- The writing of the paper needs improvement, with some sections appearing rushed or incomplete.
- The paper lacks a detailed comparison with state-of-the-art methods, particularly in terms of computational efficiency and performance on different backbones.
- There is a lack of clarity in the explanation of some methodological details, such as the role of YOLOv8 in the traffic detection and the use of Bezier curves in lane detection.
- The paper does not adequately address the limitations of the proposed method, which could affect the reliability and generalizability of the results.
- The paper does not provide sufficient qualitative results to support the claims made about the effectiveness of the method.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors provide a detailed comparison with state-of-the-art methods in terms of computational efficiency and performance on different backbones?
- How does the proposed method perform when applied to other datasets or in different scenarios, such as urban or rural environments?
- Can the authors clarify the role of YOLOv8 in the traffic detection and explain how it enhances the overall performance of the method?
- What are the specific advantages of using Bezier curves for 3D lane detection over other representation methods?
- How does the proposed method handle scenarios where lane markings are not clearly visible or where there are multiple lanes with similar characteristics?
- Could the authors provide more details on the training and inference times for the proposed method, and how these compare to other methods in the field?
