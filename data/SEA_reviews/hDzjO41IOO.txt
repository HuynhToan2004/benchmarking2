 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel method for solving inverse problems using diffusion models, specifically focusing on the approximation of the posterior density using Tweedie's formula. This method, termed Tweedie Moment Projected Diffusions (TMPD), aims to improve upon existing methods by approximating the posterior density more accurately, which is crucial for generating samples from the posterior. The paper also presents a theoretical analysis and experimental results that demonstrate the method's effectiveness, although concerns are raised about the clarity of the presentation and the depth of the experimental validation.

**Strengths:**
- The paper addresses an important and relevant problem in the field of inverse problems, which is crucial for various applications in imaging and signal processing.
- The authors provide a clear theoretical analysis of the proposed method, which is supported by rigorous mathematical proofs and theoretical guarantees.
- The paper is well-written and easy to follow, with a clear structure and well-defined problem statement.
- The experimental results are convincing, showing that the proposed method outperforms existing methods in some scenarios.
- The paper introduces a novel approach to approximating the posterior density using Tweedie's formula, which is a significant contribution to the field.
- The theoretical analysis provided is robust, including a detailed discussion on the limitations of the method and suggestions for future work.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper suffers from a lack of clarity in some sections, particularly in the explanation of the experimental setup and the results.
- The paper does not adequately compare its method with existing works, such as those by Chung et al. (2022a) and Song et al. (2023), which are crucial for establishing the novelty and effectiveness of the proposed method.
- The paper could benefit from a more comprehensive discussion on the limitations of the method and a more detailed analysis of the experimental results, including the inclusion of standard deviations and a more thorough comparison with existing methods.
- There are some typographical errors and minor issues with the presentation of the paper, which could be addressed for clarity and professionalism.
- The paper's approach is based on a Gaussian approximation, which may not be applicable in all scenarios, and the method's effectiveness in non-Gaussian cases is not thoroughly explored.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors clarify the experimental setup and results, particularly in Section 6.1, to make the results more understandable and interpretable?
- How does the proposed method compare with existing works, such as those by Chung et al. (2022a) and Song et al. (2023), in terms of theoretical guarantees and practical performance?
- Could the authors provide a more detailed analysis of the experimental results, including standard deviations and a more thorough comparison with existing methods?
- What are the implications of using the proposed method in non-Gaussian cases, and how does it perform in such scenarios?
- Can the authors discuss the potential applications of the method in other inverse problems, such as those in MRI imaging, and provide examples or case studies to illustrate its effectiveness?
