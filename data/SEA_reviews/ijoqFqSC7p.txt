 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a tuning-free and time-efficient method for video diffusion models, focusing on enhancing the generation of longer videos by rescheduling noise and utilizing window-based attention fusion. The proposed method, termed FreeNoise, aims to extend the generative capabilities of pre-trained models without additional training, thereby preserving content consistency. It also introduces a motion injection method for generating videos conditioned on multiple text prompts, which is evaluated against existing methods such as Gen-L-Video. The paper is well-structured, providing both qualitative and quantitative results, and includes an ablation study to validate the effectiveness of the proposed components. However, concerns were raised regarding the clarity of some experimental results and the novelty of the approach compared to existing methods.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, easy to follow, and includes a detailed analysis of the limitations of existing methods.
- The proposed method is intuitive, straightforward, and achieves good results in terms of video quality and content consistency.
- The paper includes a comprehensive ablation study, demonstrating the effectiveness of the proposed components.
- The method is practical and does not require additional training, making it easier to implement in real-world scenarios.
- The method achieves better video quality and content consistency than previous methods, as shown in Figure 4 and Table 1.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks clarity in some experimental results, particularly in the comparisons with other methods like Gen-L-Video, which might mislead readers about the effectiveness of the proposed method.
- The novelty of the method is questionable as it seems to build upon existing methods like Gen-L-Video without sufficient differentiation.
- The paper could benefit from a more detailed explanation of the motion injection method and its effectiveness in handling multiple text prompts.
- The method's performance in handling longer videos (e.g., 128 frames) is not convincingly demonstrated, raising concerns about its scalability.
- The paper could benefit from a broader range of evaluation metrics, including user studies and comparisons with more recent or diverse methods.

**Questions:**
- Could you clarify the experimental results, especially the comparisons with Gen-L-Video, to ensure that the advantages of your method are clearly demonstrated?
- How does the proposed method handle longer videos, and what are the implications for its scalability and effectiveness?
- Can you provide more details on the motion injection method, particularly how it handles multiple text prompts and ensures smooth transitions between different actions or scenarios?
- How does the proposed method compare to other recent or diverse methods, and what are the implications of these comparisons for the perceived novelty and effectiveness of your approach?
