 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel distribution family, the Exponential Dissimilarity-Dispersion Family (EDDF), aimed at enhancing Variational Autoencoders (VAEs) by integrating domain-specific dissimilarity functions. The EDDF allows for the derivation of a new ELBO loss function, which is optimized using an approximated algorithm based on log-expected dissimilarity. This approach is validated through experiments on several image datasets, demonstrating improved generative performance compared to traditional VAEs. The authors claim that this methodology effectively leverages domain knowledge to improve VAEs' handling of high-dimensional natural data.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel distribution family, the Exponential Dissimilarity-Dispersion Family (EDDF), which can be used to derive a new ELBO loss function, enhancing the expressiveness of VAEs.
- The proposed methodology effectively leverages domain knowledge to improve VAEs' handling of high-dimensional natural data, which is a significant contribution to the field.
- The paper is well-written, clear, and easy to follow, making it accessible to a broad audience.
- The experiments are well-designed, demonstrating the effectiveness of the proposed method through improved performance on various image datasets.
- The paper provides a theoretical framework for approximating the normalizing constant, which is a significant theoretical contribution.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a comprehensive comparison with state-of-the-art methods, which could better position the proposed method within the current research landscape.
- The novelty of the proposed method is limited as it primarily replaces the traditional Gaussian decoder with a dissimilarity-based decoder, which might not be sufficiently distinct from existing methods.
- The experimental setup could be improved by including more challenging datasets and using a wider range of metrics to evaluate the performance of the proposed method.
- The paper could benefit from a more detailed discussion on the limitations and potential negative societal impacts of the proposed method.
- The paper does not sufficiently address the computational efficiency of the proposed method, which is a significant concern given the computational demands of VAEs.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors provide more details on the computational efficiency of the proposed method, particularly how it compares to other VAE-based methods in terms of training and inference times?
- How does the proposed method perform when using more complex or challenging datasets, such as those with higher resolution or more diverse image content?
- Can the authors provide a more detailed comparison with other state-of-the-art methods, including a direct comparison of performance metrics and a discussion of the methodological differences?
- How does the proposed method handle the computational challenges associated with the dissimilarity function, particularly in terms of ensuring differentiability and computational efficiency?
- Can the authors provide a more detailed discussion on the limitations and potential negative societal impacts of the proposed method?
