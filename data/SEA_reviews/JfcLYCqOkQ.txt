 **Summary:**
The paper investigates the impact of multiple rounds of masking on the performance of masked autoencoders, specifically focusing on the Conditional MAE framework. It explores the effects of masking at different stages of the encoder, with a focus on how this affects model performance in terms of transfer learning, robustness, and scalability. The authors conduct a series of experiments, including one-shot, two-shot, and three-shot masking, to understand the optimal conditions for masking and the resulting changes in model behavior. The paper also discusses the implications of these findings for practical applications in downstream tasks, such as image classification and object detection.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel approach to masked autoencoders by allowing for multiple rounds of masking, which is a significant advancement over traditional fixed masking strategies.
- It provides a comprehensive empirical study on multiple masking, revealing insights into where to mask, how much to mask, and the impact of masking on model performance.
- The authors have conducted extensive experiments, including one-shot, two-shot, and three-shot masking, which are well-documented and presented with clear visualizations and detailed explanations.
- The paper offers valuable insights into the behavior of masked autoencoders, such as the introduction of locality bias and the effects of different masking strategies on model performance.
- The findings have practical implications, suggesting that the proposed methods can enhance the performance of downstream tasks like image classification and object detection.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a broader exploration of different masking strategies beyond the Conditional MAE framework. This could include comparing the proposed method with other existing approaches like AMOM, AdaMAE, and others.
- The experiments are limited to smaller models, and it would be beneficial to see how the proposed methods perform on larger models like Huge ViT.
- The paper does not sufficiently address the potential negative effects of multiple masking on the model's performance, such as increased computational complexity or potential overfitting.
- There is a lack of discussion on the limitations of the study, which could include the scalability of the findings to larger models or different types of data.
- The paper could benefit from a more detailed discussion on the potential negative impacts of the proposed methods and how these might be mitigated.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors provide more details on how the proposed methods compare with other existing approaches, such as AMOM, AdaMAE, and others?
- How do the findings of this study apply to larger models like Huge ViT?
- Could the authors elaborate on the potential negative impacts of multiple masking, such as increased computational complexity or potential overfitting?
- How does the proposed method handle different types of data, such as noisy or low-quality images?
- What are the potential applications of the proposed methods beyond image classification and object detection?
