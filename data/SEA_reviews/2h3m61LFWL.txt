 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel approach to solving linear mixture Markov Decision Processes (MDPs) using Value-Biased Maximum Likelihood Estimation (VBMLE). This method aims to address the computational inefficiencies of existing regression-based approaches by requiring only one optimization problem per time step. The paper provides a theoretical analysis of VBMLE's regret bound and demonstrates its computational efficiency through simulations. It also discusses the connection between VBMLE and the Follow-the-Leader algorithm in online learning, potentially enhancing our understanding of model-based reinforcement learning (MBRL) in large state and action spaces.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, clear, and easy to follow, making it accessible to a broad audience.
- It introduces a novel approach to solving linear mixture MDPs using Value-Biased Maximum Likelihood Estimation (VBMLE), which is computationally efficient and theoretically sound.
- The paper provides a theoretical analysis of the regret bound of VBMLE and demonstrates its effectiveness through simulations, showing that it outperforms existing methods in terms of both regret and computation time.
- The authors discuss the connection between VBMLE and the Follow-the-Leader algorithm in online learning, which could be of independent interest and enhance our understanding of MBRL in large state and action spaces.
- The paper is well-positioned within the literature, highlighting the computational inefficiencies of existing regression-based approaches and providing a compelling argument for the need for a more efficient method.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed discussion on the computational complexity of the proposed method, particularly in large-scale environments, which could affect its practical applicability.
- The experimental setup and the choice of hyperparameters are not adequately justified, which could limit the generalizability of the results.
- The paper does not provide a comprehensive comparison with other existing methods, such as those mentioned in the literature, which could help in evaluating the novelty and effectiveness of the proposed approach.
- The paper's reliance on specific assumptions, such as the knowledge of pmin, might limit its applicability in real-world scenarios where such information is not readily available.
- The paper's contribution is seen as incremental due to its similarity to existing works, particularly those by Mete et al., which might not sufficiently differentiate it from prior research.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors provide a detailed analysis of the computational complexity of VBMLE in large-scale environments and discuss how it compares to other existing methods?
- How does the proposed method handle the computational challenges associated with the VBMLE algorithm, particularly in terms of the optimization problem and the choice of hyperparameters?
- Could the authors clarify the choice of hyperparameters in their experiments and discuss how these choices affect the results?
- How does the proposed method compare to other existing methods in terms of computational efficiency and regret performance, especially in more complex environments?
- Can the authors discuss the practical implications of their method, particularly in scenarios where the assumptions used in the analysis might not hold?
- Could the authors provide a more detailed comparison with related works, especially those that have also explored similar methodologies, to highlight the novel contributions of their research?
