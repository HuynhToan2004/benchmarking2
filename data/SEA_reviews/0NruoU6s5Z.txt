 **Summary:**
The paper introduces CompoDiff, a novel diffusion-based method for solving complex Composed Image Retrieval (CIR) tasks. It utilizes a massive dataset of 18.8M synthetic triplets, SynthTriplets18M, to train the model, which is designed to handle various conditions such as negative text and image masks. CompoDiff is capable of adjusting the importance of conditions and offers a balance between inference speed and performance. The model achieves state-of-the-art performance on four CIR benchmarks and is noted for its versatility and controllability. However, concerns were raised about the dataset's diversity and potential bias, the generalizability of the model, and the clarity of the presentation.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel diffusion-based model, CompoDiff, which is capable of handling various conditions such as negative text and image masks, offering a balance between inference speed and performance.
- The creation of a large-scale dataset, SynthTriplets18M, with 18.8M triplets, is a significant contribution to the field.
- The method achieves state-of-the-art performance on four CIR benchmarks, demonstrating the effectiveness of the proposed approach.
- The paper is well-written, clear, and easy to follow, with a comprehensive set of experiments that validate the method's effectiveness.
- The method is versatile and controllable, allowing for adjustments in the importance of conditions and enabling a more personalized user experience.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper does not adequately address the diversity and quality of the SynthTriplets18M dataset, which is crucial for the generalizability and robustness of the model.
- There is a lack of clarity on the method's ability to handle complex conditions and the extent of its controllability.
- The paper does not discuss potential societal impacts, which is a significant omission in light of the potential applications of the proposed method.
- The paper could benefit from a more detailed discussion on the method's limitations and potential failure modes, as well as a broader comparison with existing methods.
- There are several typos and grammatical errors that need correction to enhance the paper's professionalism.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors provide more details on the diversity and quality of the SynthTriplets18M dataset, including the sources of the data and the steps taken to ensure its quality and representativeness?
- How does the method handle complex conditions, and what are the limitations in terms of controllability and versatility?
- Could the authors discuss the potential societal impacts of the proposed method, especially in terms of image editing applications?
- How does the method compare to existing methods in terms of computational efficiency and resource requirements?
- Could the authors clarify the role and impact of different conditions (e.g., text, mask, image) on the retrieval performance, and provide more detailed results on the sensitivity of the model to these conditions?
