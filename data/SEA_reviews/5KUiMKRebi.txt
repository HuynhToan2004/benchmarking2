 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a Bayesian Neural Network (BNN) that integrates deterministic and probabilistic components to enhance scalability and uncertainty quantification. The probabilistic component is represented by an Implicit Neural Representation (INR) hypernetwork, which encodes the nuisance factor associated with each weight as a scalar value. The deterministic component is represented by the weights and biases of the network, while the probabilistic component is conditioned on the INR hypernetwork. The proposed method is evaluated on various benchmarks, including regression and classification tasks, and is shown to provide competitive results compared to other BNN methods. However, the paper's novelty is questioned due to its reliance on existing components, and the experimental results are not convincingly superior to existing methods.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written and easy to follow, making it accessible to readers.
- The proposed method is innovative, leveraging the strengths of implicit neural representations (INRs) to enhance Bayesian Neural Networks (BNNs).
- The integration of INRs into BNNs allows for the representation of uncertainty in a low-dimensional space, which is a novel approach.
- The method is evaluated on various benchmarks, including regression and classification tasks, demonstrating its applicability and effectiveness.
- The paper provides a thorough discussion of related work and presents a clear and well-structured literature review.

**Weaknesses:**
- The novelty of the paper is limited as it primarily combines existing components (BNNs and INRs) without significantly advancing the state of the art.
- The paper lacks a detailed discussion on the computational complexity and scalability of the proposed method, which is crucial for understanding its practical applicability.
- The experimental results do not convincingly demonstrate the superiority of the proposed method over existing approaches. The paper could benefit from more comprehensive comparisons and evaluations.
- The paper does not sufficiently discuss the limitations and potential negative societal impacts of the proposed method.
- The paper could benefit from more detailed explanations of the INR hypernetwork and the inference process, particularly how the INRs are trained and their impact on the overall performance.
