 **Summary:**
The paper explores calibration properties in neural architecture search (NAS) using the NATS-Bench search space, focusing on various aspects such as generalization across datasets, robustness, calibration metrics reliability, and the impact of post-hoc calibration methods. It employs extensive experiments to analyze these properties, addressing questions like the generalization of calibration across datasets, the use of robustness as a calibration measurement, and the reliability of calibration metrics. The study also investigates how calibration interacts with accuracy and the effect of bin size on calibration measurements. Despite its comprehensive approach, the paper is criticized for its lack of novelty and theoretical depth, with reviewers suggesting that it primarily presents empirical results without significant theoretical insights.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, with clear and concise sections that make it easy to follow.
- The use of the NAS search space to study calibration properties is innovative and provides a fresh perspective on the field.
- The dataset generated for this study is comprehensive, covering a wide range of model architectures and training configurations, which is beneficial for further research in the field.
- The paper addresses several important questions related to calibration in neural networks, which are crucial for understanding and improving the performance of deep learning models.
- The experimental setup is robust, with a large number of experiments conducted to validate the findings, which adds credibility to the results.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks novelty and theoretical depth, primarily presenting empirical results without significant theoretical insights.
- The experimental setup and results are not sufficiently novel or insightful, with many findings being expected or already known in the field.
- The paper's claims regarding the generalization of calibration across different datasets and the reliability of calibration metrics are not convincingly supported by the data presented.
- The paper's contribution to the field is limited, as it primarily rehashes existing knowledge without providing new insights or methodologies.
- There is a lack of clarity and detail in some sections of the paper, particularly in the methodology and results sections, which could be improved for better understanding.
- The paper's conclusions are somewhat weak, with a lack of robustness in the findings, particularly in the correlation between calibration and robustness metrics.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors clarify the experimental setup and results, particularly in sections where the findings are not convincingly supported by the data presented?
- How do the authors address the concerns regarding the generalization of calibration across different datasets and the reliability of calibration metrics?
- Could the authors provide more detailed theoretical insights or explanations for the observed phenomena, particularly in the areas of calibration and robustness?
- How do the findings of this study contribute to the existing body of knowledge in the field, and what new insights or methodologies does it offer?
- Could the authors consider expanding the scope of their study to include a broader range of datasets or architectures to enhance the generalizability of their findings?
