 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel Plug-and-Play Dialogue Policy Planner (PPDPP) to enhance the proactive dialogue capabilities of Large Language Models (LLMs). This approach integrates supervised fine-tuning with human-annotated data and reinforcement learning from AI feedback to improve the strategic decision-making in LLMs. The PPDPP is designed to be transferable across different applications, enabling LLMs to be generalized to various cases without requiring extensive retraining. The framework is evaluated across three different proactive dialogue applications, demonstrating significant improvements in performance compared to existing methods. However, concerns were raised about the novelty of the approach, the lack of comprehensive human evaluation, and the clarity in the presentation of the experimental results.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel plug-and-play dialogue policy planner, PPDPP, which enables LLMs to be generalized to different cases without extensive retraining, making it a significant contribution to the field.
- The methodology is well-articulated, with a clear and comprehensive description of the proposed framework, including detailed training procedures and evaluation metrics.
- The paper provides a detailed analysis of the performance of different LLMs, highlighting the strengths of each model in specific dialogue problems, which is crucial for understanding the capabilities and limitations of different LLMs.
- The experimental results are robust, demonstrating the effectiveness of the proposed approach across three different proactive dialogue applications, showcasing significant improvements over existing methods.
- The paper is well-written, with clear and concise descriptions of the proposed method and experimental results, making it accessible and understandable to readers.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a comprehensive human evaluation, which is crucial for assessing the quality of the generated responses and the overall effectiveness of the system.
- The novelty of the approach is questionable, as similar methods have been previously explored, such as using LLMs for goal-oriented feedback and RL-based dialogue systems.
- The paper's evaluation primarily focuses on automatic metrics, which may not fully capture the quality of the generated responses, especially in proactive dialogue applications where subjective factors like empathy and persuasiveness are crucial.
- The presentation of experimental results could be improved, particularly in terms of clarity and depth, especially in figures and tables where the results are not clearly presented or explained.
- The paper does not adequately address the limitations of the proposed approach, such as the potential negative impacts on the naturalness and coherence of the generated responses due to the use of LLMs for feedback and reward generation.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors clarify the novelty of their approach compared to existing methods, particularly in terms of the use of LLMs for goal-oriented feedback and RL-based dialogue systems?
- How does the proposed method handle the potential negative impacts on the naturalness and coherence of the generated responses due to the use of LLMs for feedback and reward generation?
- Can the authors provide more details on the human evaluation, such as the number of annotators, the criteria used for evaluation, and the inter-annotator agreement?
- How does the proposed method compare to other methods in terms of the quality of the generated responses, particularly in proactive dialogue applications where subjective factors are crucial?
- Could the authors provide more details on the experimental setup, such as the specifics of the LLMs used and the training parameters for the RL component?
- How does the proposed method handle the potential for overfitting, especially given the limited size of the datasets used for supervised fine-tuning?
