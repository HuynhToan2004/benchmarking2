 **Summary:**
The paper introduces Youku-mPLUG, a large-scale Chinese video-language dataset consisting of 10 million video-text pairs sourced from Youku, a popular Chinese video-sharing platform. The dataset is meticulously curated to ensure safety, diversity, and quality, and is used to train and evaluate various video-language models, including the mPLUG-video model. This model demonstrates state-of-the-art performance in tasks such as video captioning and category classification. The paper also presents a human-annotated benchmark for evaluating video-language models, which includes datasets for video-text retrieval, video captioning, and video category classification. The dataset and benchmarks are designed to address the scarcity of large-scale Chinese video-language datasets and to advance research in this area.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a large-scale Chinese video-language dataset, which is crucial for advancing research in this area.
- The dataset is well-curated, ensuring safety, diversity, and quality, and is annotated by humans, which enhances its utility for training and evaluating video-language models.
- The paper presents a comprehensive benchmark that includes datasets for video-text retrieval, video captioning, and video category classification, which are crucial for evaluating the performance of video-language models.
- The authors propose a novel modularized decoder-only model, mPLUG-video, which is pre-trained on the Youku-mPLUG dataset and demonstrates state-of-the-art performance on various video-language tasks.
- The paper is well-written, making it easy to follow, and the experiments are thorough, demonstrating the effectiveness of the proposed models.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks detailed information about the data collection process, which raises concerns about the dataset's quality and reliability.
- There is a lack of comparative analysis with existing datasets and benchmarks, which makes it difficult to assess the novelty and utility of the proposed dataset and benchmarks.
- The paper does not adequately address the challenges of building a large-scale Chinese video-language dataset, such as the scarcity of high-quality video-text pairs and the need for extensive data cleaning and annotation.
- The paper does not provide sufficient details about the data preprocessing and cleaning steps, which are crucial for ensuring the dataset's quality and reliability.
- The paper does not discuss the potential limitations or ethical considerations associated with the dataset and benchmarks, which are essential for ensuring their responsible use.
