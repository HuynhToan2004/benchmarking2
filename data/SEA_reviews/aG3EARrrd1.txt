 **Summary:**
The paper introduces the Online Sparse Residual Tree (OSRT) model, a novel predictive statistical model designed for handling streaming multivariate scattered data. OSRT is based on online tree decomposition and online adaptive radial basis function (RBF) exploration, dynamically expanding its network depth and incorporating sparse and appropriate RBF refinement at each child node to minimize residual error. The model's effectiveness is demonstrated through comparisons with existing online RBF methods, showing improved efficiency and accuracy. However, the paper has been critiqued for its clarity, with concerns about the presentation and the depth of experimental validation.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-organized and easy to follow, with a clear and straightforward presentation of the proposed method.
- The OSRT model effectively handles streaming multivariate scattered data, demonstrating good performance in terms of both accuracy and efficiency.
- The paper introduces a novel approach by integrating online tree decomposition and online adaptive radial basis function (RBF) exploration, which is innovative and potentially impactful.
- The experimental results show that the OSRT model outperforms existing online RBF methods, indicating its effectiveness and potential utility in real-world applications.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks clarity in its presentation, particularly in the introduction and methodology sections, which could benefit from further refinement to enhance readability and comprehension.
- The novelty of the OSRT model is somewhat limited, as it appears to be a combination of existing techniques without significant new insights or methodological advancements.
- The experimental evaluation is not comprehensive enough, with only a few datasets used and limited comparisons with other online learning algorithms.
- There are several grammatical errors and typographical mistakes throughout the paper, which detract from its professional quality.
- The paper does not adequately address the computational complexity of the OSRT model, which is crucial for understanding its practical applicability in real-world scenarios.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors clarify the specific contributions of the OSRT model beyond the integration of existing techniques?
- How does the OSRT model perform in scenarios where the data is not scattered, and what are the implications for its effectiveness?
- Can the authors provide more details on the computational complexity of the OSRT model and how it scales with increasing data complexity?
- How does the OSRT model compare in terms of computational efficiency and accuracy with other state-of-the-art online learning algorithms, particularly those that are not RBF-based?
- Could the authors consider expanding the experimental evaluation to include a broader range of datasets and scenarios to better validate the generalizability of the OSRT model?
