 **Summary:**
The paper investigates the unimodal bias in multimodal learning, specifically focusing on how different modalities are learned in multimodal neural networks. It introduces a theoretical framework that explains the bias in terms of the depth of modality fusion within the network and the correlation between modalities. The authors demonstrate that the modality learned first is not necessarily the one that contributes most to the output. They also explore the impact of dataset statistics and initialization on the duration of the unimodal phase. The paper is supported by empirical evidence from numerical simulations of deep linear networks and ReLU networks, providing insights into how unimodal bias arises and persists in multimodal learning systems.

**Strengths:**
- The paper provides a comprehensive analysis of the unimodal bias in multimodal learning, which is an important and relevant problem in the field.
- It introduces a novel theoretical framework that explains the unimodal bias in terms of the depth of modality fusion within the network and the correlation between modalities.
- The paper is well-written, with clear and detailed explanations of the theoretical results and empirical evidence.
- The authors demonstrate that the results derived for multimodal linear networks can be extended to ReLU networks in certain settings, showing the general applicability of the findings.
- The paper contributes to the understanding of how unimodal bias arises and is influenced by network configuration, dataset statistics, and initialization, which is crucial for designing effective multimodal learning systems.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper's theoretical framework is limited to linear networks, which may not fully capture the complexities of non-linear networks commonly used in practice.
- The empirical evidence provided is based on numerical simulations of simple networks, which may not fully represent the behavior of more complex or realistic multimodal neural networks.
- The paper lacks a discussion on how the findings can be applied to real-world multimodal learning problems, such as visual question answering or audio-visual scene understanding.
- The paper's theoretical results are limited to specific network configurations, which may not generalize to more complex or varied network architectures.
- The paper does not provide a clear explanation on how the findings can be used to alleviate unimodal bias in practical multimodal learning systems.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors clarify how the findings of this study can be applied to real-world multimodal learning problems, such as visual question answering or audio-visual scene understanding?
- How can the theoretical results be extended to more complex or varied network architectures, such as those with multiple fusion layers or different non-linearities?
- Can the authors provide more detailed explanations on how the unimodal bias observed in the simulations relates to the behavior of multimodal neural networks in real-world applications?
- How does the unimodal bias affect the performance of multimodal learning systems, and what are the potential implications for the development of more effective multimodal learning algorithms?
- Could the authors provide more empirical evidence or case studies to support the practical relevance and applicability of their findings?
