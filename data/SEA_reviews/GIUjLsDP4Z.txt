 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel structural encoding method for Graph Neural Networks (GNNs) called Local Curvature Profiles (LCP), which utilizes discrete Ricci curvature to enhance the expressivity and performance of GNNs. The LCP method is designed to capture complementary geometric information alongside global positional encodings, improving performance in node and graph classification tasks. The paper presents a theoretical analysis of LCP's computational efficiency and impact on expressivity, along with extensive empirical evaluations demonstrating its effectiveness. The LCP method is compared with other encoding techniques, including curvature-based rewiring, and shows superior performance in many cases. The paper also explores the integration of LCP with existing encoding methods and discusses its potential for improving GNN performance.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel structural encoding method, Local Curvature Profiles (LCP), which utilizes discrete Ricci curvature to enhance the expressivity and performance of Graph Neural Networks (GNNs).
- The LCP method is theoretically analyzed for its computational efficiency and impact on expressivity, providing a solid foundation for its application.
- The empirical evaluations conducted on various datasets demonstrate the effectiveness of the LCP method in improving the performance of GNNs, particularly in node and graph classification tasks.
- The paper is well-organized and clearly written, making it accessible and easy to understand.
- The proposed method is simple, efficient, and can be combined with other encoding methods to enhance performance.
- The paper includes a thorough literature review and comparisons with other encoding techniques, providing a comprehensive view of the current state of the field.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed discussion on the limitations of the proposed method, which could provide a more balanced perspective and guide future research.
- The computational complexity of the LCP method is not discussed in detail, which could be a significant concern for large-scale applications.
- The paper does not sufficiently discuss the potential negative societal impacts of the proposed method, which is a critical aspect for ethical considerations.
- The novelty of the method is questioned as similar approaches using discrete Ricci curvature for graph rewiring have been previously explored.
- The paper does not adequately compare the LCP method with other curvature-based rewiring methods, which could have provided a more robust validation of its effectiveness.
- The experimental setup and results are not sufficiently detailed, particularly in the ablation studies and the comparison with other methods.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors provide a more detailed discussion on the limitations of the proposed method and how these limitations might affect its practical application?
- How does the computational complexity of the LCP method compare to other encoding methods, and what are the implications for large-scale applications?
- Can the authors elaborate on the potential negative societal impacts of the proposed method and how these impacts might be mitigated or addressed?
- In light of the similarities to existing methods using discrete Ricci curvature for graph rewiring, how does the LCP method differentiate itself and provide a clear advantage?
- Could the authors clarify the discrepancies in the experimental results, particularly the significant improvements observed in the LCP method compared to other methods?
- How does the LCP method handle the computational complexity associated with the calculation of discrete Ricci curvature, and what are the implications for practical applications?
