 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a method for detecting adversarial examples in Automatic Speech Recognition (ASR) systems by analyzing the probability distribution of output tokens generated by the ASR model at each time step. The method computes various statistical characteristics such as entropy, KL divergence, and Jensen-Shannon divergence from the output distributions, which are used as features for binary classifiers. The proposed method is evaluated against several ASR models and adversarial attacks, demonstrating high detection rates and robustness against adaptive attacks. However, the paper has been critiqued for its limited evaluation scope, lack of comparison with existing methods, and insufficient detail on the adversarial attack generation process.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, easy to follow, and provides a comprehensive evaluation of the proposed method across multiple ASR systems and adversarial attacks.
- The proposed method is simple yet effective, and the experimental results demonstrate its robustness against adaptive attacks.
- The paper is the first to study the characteristics of output distributions in ASR systems, which is an interesting and potentially valuable approach for detecting adversarial examples.
- The authors have conducted extensive experiments to evaluate the performance of the proposed method, including against adaptive attacks and under noisy conditions.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a thorough comparison with existing methods for adversarial example detection in ASR systems, such as those based on temporal dependency or noise flooding.
- The evaluation is limited to a small number of test examples (100) and a single attacker, which might not sufficiently demonstrate the generalizability or robustness of the proposed method.
- The paper does not provide sufficient details on how adversarial examples are generated, particularly the choice of hyperparameters and the optimization process, which is crucial for understanding the effectiveness of the proposed method.
- The method's reliance on statistical characteristics of the output distribution might not adequately capture the complexity of adversarial examples, especially those that are not easily distinguishable by statistical measures alone.
- The paper's claims about the robustness of the method against adaptive attacks are not convincingly supported by the experimental results, which show only marginal improvements over baseline methods.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors provide a more detailed comparison with existing methods for adversarial example detection in ASR systems, including those based on temporal dependency or noise flooding?
- How does the proposed method perform against a broader range of adversarial attacks, including untargeted attacks and those that are not specifically designed to evade the proposed method?
- Could the authors clarify the adversarial attack generation process, including the choice of hyperparameters and the optimization process used to generate adversarial examples?
- How does the proposed method perform under different noise conditions, and could the authors provide a more detailed analysis of the false positive and false negative rates under various conditions?
- In the context of the proposed method's reliance on statistical characteristics of the output distribution, how does the method handle adversarial examples that are not easily distinguishable by these measures?
