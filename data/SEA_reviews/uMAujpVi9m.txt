 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel self-supervised pre-training approach, ProFSA, aimed at enhancing the representation of protein pockets by leveraging fragment-surrounding alignment. This methodology generates over 5 million pseudo-ligand-pocket pairs from protein-only data, which are used to train a pocket encoder in a contrastive manner. The methodology involves extracting pseudo-ligands from protein structures and defining the corresponding pockets based on their proximity to these ligands. The pocket encoder is trained to differentiate between positive ligands and other negative samples in the same batch, using a contrastive framework. The proposed approach demonstrates improved performance in various downstream tasks such as pocket druggability prediction, pocket matching, and ligand binding affinity prediction. The paper also discusses the generation of pseudo-ligand-pocket pairs from protein-only data, which could potentially be applied to much larger predicted structure data generated by AlphaFold or ESMFold.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel scalable pairwise data synthesis pipeline by extracting pseudo-ligand-pocket pairs from protein-only data, which could be applied to much larger predicted structure data generated by AlphaFold or ESMFold.
- The proposed molecular-guided fragment-surroundings contrastive learning method for pocket representations is innovative and effectively leverages pretrained small molecule encoders to distill comprehensive structural and chemical knowledge.
- The method achieves significant performance boosts in various downstream applications, including tasks that solely require pocket data and those involving pocket-ligand pair data.
- The paper is well-written, easy to follow, and includes a comprehensive evaluation on three different downstream tasks, demonstrating the versatility of the method.
- The idea of using protein-ligand interactions to generate pseudo-ligand-pocket pairs is novel and could potentially be applied to much larger predicted structure data generated by AlphaFold or ESMFold.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed discussion on the potential negative societal impact of the proposed method.
- There is a lack of clarity in the presentation of the methodology, particularly in the description of the pseudo-ligand construction and pocket construction phases.
- The paper does not adequately discuss the limitations of the proposed method, including potential biases introduced by the segmentation process and the potential impact of these biases on the performance of the model.
- The paper does not include a detailed comparison with other state-of-the-art methods, which could have provided a clearer picture of the method's relative performance.
- The paper does not address the potential negative societal impact of the proposed method, which could be significant given the potential applications in drug discovery.

**Questions:**
- Could you provide more details on how the pseudo-ligand is constructed, particularly how the fragment is extracted from the protein structure and how the N-terminal and C-terminal are treated?
- How does the proposed method handle the potential biases introduced by the segmentation process, and what steps are taken to mitigate these biases?
- Could you clarify the role of the fixed encoder in the performance of the model, and how the model would perform if the encoder were not fixed?
- How does the proposed method compare to other state-of-the-art methods in terms of computational efficiency and scalability?
- Could you discuss the potential negative societal impact of the proposed method, particularly in the context of drug discovery and the potential for misuse or overuse of the technology?
