 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a curriculum-enhanced attention distillation method to improve the training of Graph Transformers (GTs) in semi-supervised node classification tasks. This method employs a Local Graph Transformer (LGT) as a teacher model and a Global Graph Transformer (GGT) as a student model, leveraging the attention coefficients of the LGT to guide the training of the GGT. The authors propose two improvements: out-of-class entropy and top-k pruning, to facilitate out-of-class exploration under in-class guidance. The method is evaluated on seven public graph benchmark datasets, showing improvements over state-of-the-art methods. Despite the innovative approach and the demonstrated effectiveness, the paper is criticized for its limited novelty, the lack of comparison with recent and relevant methods, and the need for more comprehensive ablation studies and experimental validations.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel curriculum-enhanced attention distillation method to improve the training of Graph Transformers (GTs) in semi-supervised node classification tasks, which is a significant contribution to the field.
- The proposed method demonstrates improvements over state-of-the-art methods on seven public graph benchmark datasets, indicating its effectiveness.
- The paper is well-written, easy to follow, and provides a comprehensive review of related work, making it accessible to a broad audience.
- The introduction of out-of-class entropy and top-k pruning is innovative and helps in the training process of GTs.
- The motivation behind the proposed method is clearly articulated, and the paper provides a thorough evaluation of the proposed method.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed comparison with other knowledge distillation methods, which could provide a more robust evaluation of the proposed method.
- The novelty of the proposed method is limited as it primarily involves attention distillation and curriculum learning, which have been previously explored.
- The paper does not adequately address the scalability of the proposed method to larger datasets, which could limit its practical application.
- The paper lacks a detailed discussion on the limitations of the proposed method and the potential negative societal impacts of the proposed work.
- There is a need for more comprehensive ablation studies and experimental validations to fully understand the impact of different components of the proposed method.
- The paper could benefit from a more thorough discussion on the limitations of the proposed method and potential negative societal impacts.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors provide more details on the scalability of the proposed method to larger datasets?
- How does the proposed method compare with other knowledge distillation methods in terms of performance and efficiency?
- Could the authors provide more insights into the limitations of the proposed method and potential negative societal impacts?
- How does the proposed method perform in terms of computational efficiency and memory usage, especially in large-scale applications?
- Could the authors provide more detailed experimental results, including the standard deviation of the results, to better understand the robustness and reliability of the proposed method?
- How does the proposed method perform when applied to other graph neural networks, such as GCN?
