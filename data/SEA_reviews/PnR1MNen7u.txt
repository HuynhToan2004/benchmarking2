 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel method for learning representations from paired covariance-based data using a new measure called geodesic correlation, which is a generalization of canonical correlation analysis (CCA) on the symmetric positive definite (SPD) manifold. This method, named DeepGeoCCA, utilizes a deep learning framework to maximize the correlation between latent representations across different modalities, such as EEG and fMRI. The authors demonstrate that this method can effectively learn representations that maintain the geometric structure of SPD matrices, which is crucial for neuroimaging applications. The method is evaluated on both synthetic and real-world data, showing improvements over existing methods like CCA and RieCCA.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written and easy to follow, with a clear presentation of the methodology and results.
- The introduction of geodesic correlation as a measure of correlation for SPD manifold-valued data is novel and interesting, providing a new approach to learning latent representations from paired data.
- The proposed method is evaluated on both synthetic and real-world data, demonstrating its effectiveness in learning representations that maintain the geometric structure of SPD matrices.
- The method is validated against several baselines, including CCA, RieCCA, and DeepCCA, showing improvements in performance in both simulations and real data applications.
- The paper provides a comprehensive review of related literature and compares the proposed method with existing approaches, highlighting its contributions and advantages.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a thorough discussion on the advantages of the proposed method over existing methods like RieCCA, particularly in terms of computational efficiency and practical applications.
- There is a lack of clarity in the description of the experimental setup, particularly in the simultaneous EEG-fMRI experiment, which could benefit from more detailed explanations.
- The paper does not adequately address the choice of hyperparameters, such as the weighting factors in the loss function, which could affect the performance of the model.
- The paper does not sufficiently discuss the limitations of the proposed method, particularly in terms of its applicability to other types of data or scenarios beyond the neuroimaging domain.
- The paper does not provide a detailed comparison of computational complexity with other methods, which could help in understanding the practical implications of using the proposed method.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors provide a more detailed explanation of how the geodesic correlation is calculated and how it differs from other measures of correlation?
- How does the proposed method compare in terms of computational complexity and efficiency with other methods, particularly in large-scale applications?
- Could the authors provide more details on the choice of hyperparameters and their impact on the performance of the model?
- How does the method perform when applied to other types of data, such as non-covariance-based data, and what are the implications for the geometric structure of the data?
- Could the authors provide a more detailed comparison of the results with existing methods, particularly in terms of statistical significance and the practical implications of the improvements observed?
