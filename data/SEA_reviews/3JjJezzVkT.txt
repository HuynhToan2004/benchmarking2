 **Summary:**
The paper explores the role of momentum in Stochastic Gradient Descent (SGD) under small learning rates, focusing on the convergence of SGDM to SGD over different time horizons. It theoretically demonstrates that SGDM closely tracks SGD when the learning rate is small, and empirically validates this through experiments on ImageNet and RoBERTa models. The authors also discuss the practical implications of these findings, suggesting that momentum does not significantly enhance performance in small learning rate regimes. The paper is well-received for its clear theoretical analysis and empirical validation, although some reviewers have noted the limited scope of the experiments and the lack of novelty in the results.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, with clear and concise presentation of the main results.
- The theoretical analysis is robust and supported by solid empirical evidence, particularly in the small learning rate regime.
- The paper provides a thorough analysis of the convergence of SGDM to SGD, which is both interesting and relevant to the community.
- The experiments are well-designed and show that momentum does not significantly enhance performance in small learning rate regimes, which is an important contribution to the understanding of momentum in SGD.
- The paper includes a comprehensive literature review, which helps in situating the current work within the broader context of existing research.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper primarily focuses on the small learning rate regime, which might limit its applicability to larger learning rates where momentum is known to be beneficial.
- The experiments are somewhat limited in scope, focusing on a single model (ResNet-50) and a single dataset (ImageNet), which might not generalize well to other models or datasets.
- The paper does not provide a detailed discussion on the practical implications of the findings, particularly in terms of how the results could be applied in real-world scenarios.
- The novelty of the results is questioned, as the findings on the role of momentum in small learning rate regimes are not new and have been discussed in previous works.
- The paper could benefit from a more detailed comparison with existing works, particularly in terms of the theoretical contributions and empirical validations.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors clarify the relationship between the learning rate and the momentum coefficient, particularly how they are scaled in the experiments?
- How do the authors justify the choice of the learning rate schedule in the experiments, and could they provide more details on the selection process?
- Can the authors provide a more detailed discussion on the practical implications of their findings, particularly in terms of how the results could be applied in real-world scenarios?
- Could the authors elaborate on the theoretical contributions of their work, particularly how it advances the understanding of momentum in SGD beyond previous works?
- How do the authors address the concerns regarding the novelty and depth of the empirical validations, especially in light of similar findings in previous studies?
