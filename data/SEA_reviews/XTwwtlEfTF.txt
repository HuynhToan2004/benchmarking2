 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel method for robust multimodal learning with missing modalities by adapting pre-trained multimodal networks using low-rank adaptation and modulation of intermediate features. The approach aims to enhance the performance of the model when one or more modalities are absent during testing, thus improving robustness. The methodology is evaluated across various tasks, including RGB-Thermal and RGB-Depth semantic segmentation, multimodal material segmentation, and multimodal sentiment analysis. The proposed method is shown to outperform independent, dedicated networks and demonstrate versatility across different tasks and datasets.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written and easy to follow, making it accessible to a broad audience.
- The proposed method is simple, effective, and applicable to various multimodal learning tasks.
- The experimental results are promising, showing that the proposed method can outperform independent, dedicated networks and demonstrate versatility across different tasks and datasets.
- The paper introduces a novel approach to parameter-efficient adaptation for multimodal learning, which is a significant contribution to the field.
- The methodology is evaluated on diverse datasets and tasks, demonstrating the robustness and adaptability of the proposed method.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a comprehensive review of existing methods for multimodal learning with missing modalities, which could help contextualize the proposed method within the existing literature.
- The experimental results are not consistently strong across all datasets and tasks. For example, the method performs poorly on some datasets compared to dedicated networks.
- The paper does not sufficiently address the computational cost of the proposed method, particularly the additional computational overhead due to the insertion of SSF layers.
- The paper does not provide a detailed analysis of the failure cases or the limitations of the proposed method, which could help in understanding the scenarios where the method may not perform optimally.
- The paper does not discuss the potential negative societal impacts of the proposed method, which could be a significant concern for some applications.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors provide a more detailed comparison with existing methods, especially in terms of computational cost and performance?
- How does the proposed method perform in scenarios where only a subset of modalities is available during training?
- Can the authors discuss the potential negative societal impacts of the proposed method and how these might be mitigated?
- How does the proposed method compare to other parameter-efficient adaptation methods, particularly in terms of computational efficiency and performance?
- Could the authors provide more insights into the failure cases and the limitations of the proposed method?
