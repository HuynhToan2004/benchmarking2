 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel method for integrating pre-trained Vision-Language Models (VLMs) into Reinforcement Learning (RL) to enhance task-relevant knowledge transfer. The authors propose a method where VLMs are queried with task-specific prompts, generating intermediate representations that are then used to train RL policies. These representations are shown to outperform traditional image encoder features and instruction-following methods in Minecraft environments, demonstrating the method's effectiveness. The paper also explores the use of different VLM layers and decoding methods, and discusses the potential for optimizing prompts to enhance task performance.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, clear, and easy to follow, with detailed explanations of the methodology and experimental setups.
- The introduction of promptable representations from VLMs into RL is a novel approach that has not been extensively explored, and the paper provides a comprehensive discussion on different VLM layers and decoding methods.
- The paper demonstrates the effectiveness of the proposed method through ablation studies and empirical results, showing that promptable representations outperform traditional image encoder features and instruction-following methods in Minecraft environments.
- The method is innovative, using VLMs to generate task-specific representations that can be used to train RL policies, which is a promising direction for integrating VLMs into RL.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a broader evaluation of the method across different VLMs and RL algorithms, which could strengthen the claims of its general applicability.
- The experiments are limited to Minecraft environments, which might not fully demonstrate the generalizability of the method to other, more complex RL tasks.
- The method's reliance on hand-crafted prompts might limit its scalability and applicability in real-world scenarios where task-specific prompts are not readily available.
- There is a lack of discussion on the limitations and potential negative societal impacts of the proposed method.
- The paper does not sufficiently discuss the computational overhead and the scalability of the method with larger VLMs and more complex RL tasks.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors provide more details on the prompt optimization process and its impact on the final performance of the RL policy?
- How does the method perform when using different RL algorithms, such as SAC or A3C?
- What are the specific computational requirements of the proposed method, and how does it scale with larger VLMs and more complex RL tasks?
- How does the method perform when using different VLMs, such as BLIP-2 or LLaVA?
- Can the authors discuss the potential limitations and negative societal impacts of the proposed method, especially in terms of data privacy and bias in VLMs?
- How does the method compare to other approaches that directly query VLMs for actions or use VLMs to generate rewards?
