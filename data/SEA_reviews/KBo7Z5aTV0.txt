 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel pixel learning framework named PiXL, aimed at addressing pixel-level variance in semantic segmentation. The framework employs a pixel partition module to categorize pixels into joint and drift pixels based on entropy. It further utilizes a prototype generation and selection module to align these pixels, enhancing pixel feature consistency both intra- and inter-image. The PiXL framework has been tested across various settings, including unsupervised domain adaptation, semi-supervised, and fully supervised scenarios, demonstrating its effectiveness and robustness. The paper also includes a detailed ablation study and visualization results to support the claims.
