 **Summary:**
The paper introduces Cooperative Minibatching, a novel approach to minibatch training in Graph Neural Networks (GNNs) aimed at reducing computational costs and enhancing performance by utilizing the concave relationship between batch size and computational workload. This method leverages the observation that the computational workload per epoch is a concave function of the batch size, enabling the processing of minibatches across multiple Processing Elements (PEs) in parallel, thereby reducing the need for duplicate work and improving communication efficiency. The paper presents theoretical insights and empirical evidence supporting the efficacy of this approach, demonstrating significant speedups in large-scale GNN training.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, clear, and easy to follow, making complex concepts accessible to a broad audience.
- It introduces a novel approach to minibatch training in GNNs, leveraging the concave relationship between batch size and computational workload, which is both innovative and relevant to the field.
- The paper presents a comprehensive theoretical analysis, including proofs of theorems and detailed experimental evaluations, which support the proposed methodologies.
- The empirical results are strong, showing significant speedups and improvements in runtime efficiency, particularly in large-scale GNN training scenarios.
- The paper effectively communicates the practical implications of its findings, with clear explanations of the theoretical insights and their implications for real-world applications.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper could benefit from a more detailed discussion on the practical implications and challenges of implementing the proposed methods in real-world scenarios, particularly in terms of system design and implementation.
- There is a lack of discussion on the scalability and applicability of the proposed methods across different types of GNN architectures and datasets, which could limit the generalizability of the findings.
- The paper does not adequately address the potential negative societal impacts of the proposed methods, which could be a significant concern for some readers.
- The experimental setup and methodology could be more clearly articulated, with potentially more comprehensive comparisons to existing methods and baseline models.
- The paper could benefit from a more thorough discussion on the limitations of the proposed methods and potential areas for future research.
