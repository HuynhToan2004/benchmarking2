 **Summary:**
The paper introduces Pinocchio, a benchmark designed to evaluate the factual knowledge and reasoning abilities of large language models (LLMs). It consists of 20,713 factual questions sourced from various datasets, which are transformed into multiple-choice questions to assess the models' performance in handling factual information. The benchmark is evaluated using a range of LLMs, including both open-source and proprietary models, to assess their factual knowledge and reasoning capabilities across different domains and languages. The paper provides a comprehensive evaluation of LLMs' performance in handling factual knowledge, including their ability to handle multi-hop reasoning, structured data, and adversarial examples. It also discusses the limitations and potential improvements of the benchmark, suggesting that it could serve as a valuable tool for future research in this area.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel benchmark, Pinocchio, which evaluates the factual knowledge and reasoning capabilities of large language models (LLMs) across various domains and languages.
- The dataset is well-curated, with clear descriptions of the sources and characteristics of the factual statements, and the evaluation of LLMs is conducted using a variety of prompting strategies.
- The paper is well-written, making it easy to follow, and includes detailed experimental setups and results, which are supported by a comprehensive analysis of the models' performance.
- The benchmark covers a broad range of tasks, including multi-hop reasoning, adversarial examples, and temporal reasoning, providing a comprehensive evaluation of LLMs' factual knowledge and reasoning abilities.
- The paper addresses an important and timely research question regarding the factual knowledge and reasoning capabilities of LLMs, which is crucial for their deployment in real-world applications.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a clear motivation for using multiple-choice questions as a proxy for evaluating the factual knowledge and reasoning abilities of LLMs, which might not accurately reflect the models' performance in real-world scenarios.
- The benchmark primarily focuses on factual knowledge and reasoning capabilities of LLMs, but it does not evaluate the models' ability to generate factual knowledge, which is a significant limitation.
- The paper does not discuss the limitations of the benchmark in detail, which could be crucial for understanding its applicability and potential biases.
- The evaluation of the benchmark is limited to a few models, which might not be representative of the broader LLM landscape.
- The paper does not provide detailed statistics on the inter-annotator agreement (IAA) of the factuality labels, which is crucial for ensuring the reliability and validity of the benchmark.
- The paper does not include a detailed analysis of the models' performance on different types of questions, such as multi-hop questions, which could provide insights into the models' ability to handle complex reasoning tasks.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors provide more details on the motivation behind using multiple-choice questions as a proxy for evaluating the factual knowledge and reasoning abilities of LLMs?
- How does the benchmark handle the potential bias in the factual statements, especially those sourced from Wikipedia?
- Could the authors clarify the process of creating the factuality labels for the questions and provide statistics on the inter-annotator agreement (IAA) of these labels?
- How does the benchmark ensure the diversity and representativeness of the factual statements across different domains and languages?
- Could the authors discuss the limitations of the benchmark in more detail and suggest potential improvements or extensions to address these limitations?
- How does the benchmark handle the potential bias in the factual statements, especially those sourced from Wikipedia?
- Could the authors provide more details on the evaluation setup, including the number of trials and the statistical significance of the results?
