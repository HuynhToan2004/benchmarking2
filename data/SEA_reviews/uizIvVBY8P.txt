 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel method for continual supervised anomaly detection using a hybrid model of a Variational AutoEncoder (VAE) and a binary classifier. The method generates anomalous instances to reduce catastrophic forgetting, which is a significant issue in continual learning. The approach is evaluated on several datasets and is shown to outperform existing methods. However, concerns were raised regarding the clarity of the experimental setup, the lack of detailed comparison with state-of-the-art methods, and the insufficient discussion on the theoretical underpinnings of the method.

**Strengths:**
- The paper addresses the problem of continual supervised anomaly detection, which is a novel and important application of supervised anomaly detection.
- The proposed method is well-motivated and the paper is well-written, making it easy to follow.
- The method is evaluated on several datasets and shown to outperform existing methods, indicating its effectiveness.
- The paper includes an ablation study that shows the effectiveness of each component of the proposed method.
- The use of a binary classifier to detect seen anomalies is an interesting approach that adds a layer of complexity to the model.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks clarity in some experimental setups, particularly the generation of anomalous instances and the role of the binary classifier in detecting seen anomalies.
- There is a significant omission of state-of-the-art methods in the comparison, which limits the evaluation of the proposed method against the most advanced techniques.
- The paper does not discuss the limitations of the proposed method, which is crucial for understanding its applicability and potential drawbacks.
- The method's performance is not evaluated on datasets with a larger number of anomalous instances, which could affect the generalizability of the results.
- The paper does not include a detailed comparison with other generative replay methods, which could provide a better understanding of the proposed method's effectiveness.
- The paper lacks a detailed discussion on the theoretical underpinnings of the method, particularly how it addresses the challenges of continual learning such as catastrophic forgetting.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors clarify how the binary classifier is trained and how it operates, especially in relation to the generation of anomalous instances?
- How does the proposed method handle the imbalance in the number of normal and anomalous instances, particularly in the training set?
- Could the authors provide a more detailed comparison with other generative replay methods, including a discussion on how their method differs and performs better?
- How does the proposed method ensure that the generated anomalous instances are not simply a random sampling from the prior distribution?
- Could the authors discuss the theoretical implications of their method, particularly how it addresses the challenges of continual learning?
- How does the method perform when the number of anomalous instances in the training set is larger than the number of normal instances?
- Can the authors provide a detailed discussion on the limitations of their method and how these might affect its practical application?
