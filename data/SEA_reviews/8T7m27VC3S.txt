 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel middleware for autonomous driving systems, named DESIGN, which integrates 3D dense captioning beyond nouns to enhance the interaction between perception and planning modules. This middleware is designed to provide comprehensive descriptions of 3D bounding boxes, including attributes, locations, and moving statuses, which are crucial for enabling language foundation models to understand and process real-world driving scenarios. The authors propose a scalable auto-labeling methodology to generate ground truth data and construct a large-scale dataset called nuDESIGN, which is used to benchmark a DESIGN-former model. The paper also explores the potential of this middleware in improving autonomous driving algorithms and systems by integrating language foundation models.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel middleware named DESIGN, which is a significant step towards enabling language foundation models to understand and process real-world driving scenarios.
- The authors propose a scalable auto-labeling methodology to generate ground truth data, which is crucial for the development and training of the DESIGN-former model.
- The construction of a large-scale dataset called nuDESIGN, consisting of 2300k sentences, provides a substantial resource for the research community to further advance the field of autonomous driving.
- The paper is well-written, with clear presentation and a comprehensive experimental setup that includes multiple baselines and comparisons.
- The potential impact of this work on the development of autonomous driving algorithms and systems is significant, as it addresses the challenge of integrating language foundation models with autonomous driving systems.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks clarity on the exact role and integration of the LLM within the DESIGN-former model, particularly whether it is used for caption generation or other tasks.
- The paper does not provide sufficient details on the training and testing data split, which could affect the reproducibility and generalizability of the results.
- The novelty of the proposed method is questioned as it appears to be a simple combination of existing technologies without significant innovation.
- The paper does not sufficiently explore the potential of the proposed middleware in enhancing the interpretability of autonomous driving systems, which is a critical aspect for improving public trust and acceptance.
- The paper does not discuss the limitations of the proposed method, which could provide valuable insights into the challenges and potential improvements for future research.

**Questions:**
- Could you clarify the specific role and integration of the LLM within the DESIGN-former model? Is it used for caption generation or other tasks?
- How does the DESIGN-former model handle the integration of different modalities (visual, LiDAR, etc.) in the context of 3D dense captioning?
- Can you provide more details on the training and testing data split, particularly how the data is divided across different scenes and how this affects the generalizability of the results?
- How does the proposed method compare to other existing methods in terms of computational efficiency and practical deployment in real-world autonomous driving systems?
- Could you discuss the potential limitations of the proposed method and how these might affect its practical application in real-world scenarios?
