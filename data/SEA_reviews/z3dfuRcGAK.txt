 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel generative embedding-based entity alignment (GEEA) framework using a mutual variational autoencoder (M-VAE) to enhance entity alignment and synthesis tasks. The GEEA framework is designed to address the limitations of existing GAN-based methods by incorporating a generative perspective, which includes prior and post reconstruction processes. The paper demonstrates that GEEA outperforms state-of-the-art methods on both entity alignment and synthesis tasks, showcasing its effectiveness and efficiency. Despite its innovative approach, the paper has been critiqued for its high computational complexity and the need for more comprehensive comparisons with existing methods.

**Strengths:**
- The paper presents a novel perspective of generative models for entity alignment (EA) tasks, which is a significant departure from traditional methods.
- The proposed method, GEEA, effectively addresses the limitations of existing GAN-based EA methods by introducing a generative perspective and optimizing the generative objective to minimize the EEA objective.
- The paper is well-written, with clear explanations of the theoretical foundations and practical applications of the proposed method.
- The experimental results demonstrate the superior performance of GEEA compared to existing state-of-the-art methods, highlighting its effectiveness and efficiency.
- The introduction of the concept of generative models to entity alignment tasks is innovative and could potentially lead to new insights and methodologies in this field.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed comparison with existing methods, particularly in terms of computational complexity and efficiency.
- The motivation behind the use of M-VAE for entity alignment is not clearly articulated, and the advantages of M-VAE over other generative models like GAN are not convincingly demonstrated.
- The paper does not sufficiently address the issue of mode collapse in GAN-based EA methods, which is a significant limitation of these models.
- The experimental setup and results could be improved by including more comprehensive comparisons with existing methods and by providing more detailed ablation studies.
- The paper could benefit from a more thorough discussion on the limitations of the proposed method and potential future research directions.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors provide a more detailed comparison of the computational complexity and efficiency of GEEA with existing methods?
- How does the proposed method address the issue of mode collapse in GAN-based EA methods?
- Could the authors clarify the motivation behind the choice of M-VAE for entity alignment and discuss the potential advantages of this model over other generative models?
- In the experimental section, could the authors include more detailed ablation studies and comparisons with other generative models like GAN to better establish the effectiveness of their approach?
- Could the authors discuss the limitations of the proposed method and suggest potential directions for future research?
