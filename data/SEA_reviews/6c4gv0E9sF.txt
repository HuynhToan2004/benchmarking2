 **Summary:**
The paper introduces SpikeBERT, a spiking neural network (SNN) architecture adapted from Spikformer, designed for language processing tasks. It employs a two-stage knowledge distillation method to train SpikeBERT, leveraging a large collection of unlabeled texts for pre-training and fine-tuning with task-specific instances. The model is evaluated on multiple benchmark datasets, demonstrating competitive performance compared to BERT, while significantly reducing energy consumption. The paper discusses the challenges of training SNNs and proposes a novel approach to address these challenges through knowledge distillation.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, clear, and easy to follow, with a logical structure that effectively presents the research.
- The proposed method is innovative and the experiments are comprehensive, including both English and Chinese datasets, demonstrating the effectiveness of the proposed method.
- The paper introduces a novel approach to training spiking neural networks (SNNs) by addressing the challenges of training SNNs, particularly in terms of energy efficiency and performance.
- The paper provides a detailed analysis of the energy consumption of SpikeBERT, which is significantly lower than that of fine-tuned BERT, indicating a significant improvement in energy efficiency.
- The methodology of the paper is robust, with a thorough analysis of the impact of various factors such as model depth and time steps on performance.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed discussion on the limitations and potential negative societal impacts of the proposed method.
- The motivation behind the research, particularly the claim that SNNs can achieve comparable results to BERT with less energy consumption, is not adequately justified.
- The paper does not provide a comprehensive comparison with other spiking neural networks, which could help in understanding the relative performance of SpikeBERT.
- The paper does not discuss the hardware-friendliness of SpikeBERT, which is a significant claim of the paper.
- There are concerns about the novelty of the proposed method, as it primarily involves a two-stage knowledge distillation process, which has been previously used in other works.
- The paper does not sufficiently address the challenges of training SNNs, particularly in terms of the "self-accumulating dynamics" issue mentioned in the abstract.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors provide a more detailed discussion on the limitations and potential negative societal impacts of the proposed method?
- How does SpikeBERT compare to other spiking neural networks in terms of performance and energy efficiency?
- Could the authors provide more details on the hardware-friendliness of SpikeBERT, particularly in terms of how it can be implemented on neuromorphic hardware?
- Why does SpikeBERT perform worse than BERT in some datasets?
- Can the authors clarify the claims made about the "self-accumulating dynamics" issue and how SpikeBERT addresses it?
- How does the proposed method compare to other knowledge distillation methods in terms of effectiveness and efficiency?
- Can the authors provide more details on the training process of SpikeBERT, particularly in terms of the training time and computational resources required?
