 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel method for learning compressed representations of Markov chains, focusing on the modeling of slower dynamics and discarding faster, irrelevant information. The approach leverages the information bottleneck method and the autoinformation gap to optimize the representation of dynamic systems. The method, termed Time-Lagged Information Bottleneck (T-IB), is evaluated on both synthetic and real-world datasets, demonstrating its effectiveness in reducing computational complexity and improving statistical fidelity in simulations. Despite its theoretical grounding and empirical validation, the paper has been critiqued for its lack of clarity in presentation, particularly in the explanation of key concepts and the structure of the manuscript.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel method for learning compressed representations of Markov chains, focusing on the modeling of slower dynamics and discarding irrelevant information, which is particularly relevant for simulating large-scale systems at long time scales.
- The methodology is supported by a strong theoretical foundation, including the use of information theory and the concept of the autoinformation gap, which are well-integrated into the proposed approach.
- The paper is well-written, with clear explanations and detailed experimental results that demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed method.
- The method is shown to be effective in reducing computational complexity and improving statistical fidelity in simulations, making it a valuable contribution to the field of data compression and dynamical system modeling.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper suffers from clarity issues, particularly in the introduction and the explanation of key concepts such as the autoinformation gap and the Information Bottleneck method.
- There are inconsistencies and potential errors in the presentation of equations and figures, which could mislead readers or detract from the understanding of the method.
- The paper could benefit from more comprehensive comparisons with existing methods, especially those that also use information theory for learning compressed representations of Markov chains.
- The paper lacks a detailed discussion on the computational complexity and training costs of the proposed method, which could be crucial for understanding its practical applicability.
- The experimental validation is limited to a few datasets, which might not sufficiently demonstrate the generalizability of the method across different types of dynamic systems.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors clarify the definition and role of the autoinformation gap in the context of the Information Bottleneck method?
- How does the method perform when applied to chaotic and unstable systems, which might have large autoinformation content?
- Can the authors provide more details on the computational complexity and training costs of the proposed method, especially in comparison to other methods?
- How does the method handle the potential loss of information when discarding faster dynamics, and what are the implications of this for the accuracy and fidelity of the simulations?
- Could the authors elaborate on the choice of datasets and the selection criteria for the datasets used in the experiments?
- How does the method compare to other methods that use information theory for learning compressed representations of Markov chains, especially in terms of computational efficiency and statistical fidelity?
