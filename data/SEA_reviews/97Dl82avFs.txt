 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel approach for generating alternative text (alt-text) for images shared on social media, specifically Twitter. The proposed method leverages both textual information from the associated social media post and visual signals from the image to enhance the accuracy and relevance of alt-text descriptions. A new dataset of 371k images with alt-text and tweets is presented, which is used to evaluate the effectiveness of the proposed method against existing models like ClipCap and BLIP-2. The methodology involves using a pretrained CLIP image encoder to generate embeddings, which are then fed into a GPT-2 decoder to generate alt-text. The paper also explores the impact of different decoding strategies and reranking methods on the quality of alt-text generation.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel approach to alt-text generation that considers both textual information from the social media post and visual signals from the image, which is a significant advancement over previous methods.
- A new dataset of 371k images with alt-text and tweets is collected, which is a substantial contribution to the field and can be used to evaluate the effectiveness of the proposed method.
- The paper is well-written, clear, and easy to follow, with a comprehensive experimental setup that includes both quantitative and qualitative evaluations.
- The methodology is straightforward and effective, with a focus on the integration of tweet text and image embeddings to enhance alt-text generation.
- The paper addresses an important and relevant problem of improving accessibility for images on social media, which has significant real-world applications.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a thorough discussion on the limitations and ethical considerations of the proposed method, particularly concerning the privacy implications of using tweets and images without consent.
- The dataset used in the study is not thoroughly analyzed, which could provide valuable insights into the characteristics of the images and tweets that affect alt-text generation.
- The paper does not include a detailed analysis of the impact of different decoding strategies and reranking methods on the quality of alt-text generation, which could provide valuable insights into the effectiveness of these methods.
- The methodology and dataset are somewhat similar to existing works, which may limit the novelty of the contributions.
- The paper does not discuss the limitations of the dataset and the potential biases that could affect the performance of the model.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors discuss the limitations and ethical considerations of the proposed method, particularly concerning the privacy implications of using tweets and images without consent?
- How does the proposed method compare to other existing methods in terms of performance and efficiency?
- Can the authors provide more detailed analysis on the impact of different decoding strategies and reranking methods on the quality of alt-text generation?
- What are the specific characteristics of the images and tweets in the dataset that affect alt-text generation, and how do these characteristics influence the performance of the model?
- Can the authors discuss the limitations of the dataset and the potential biases that could affect the performance of the model?
