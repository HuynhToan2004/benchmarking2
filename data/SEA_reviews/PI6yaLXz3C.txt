 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel approach for fair contrastive learning using a Fairness-Aware (FARE) attention mechanism. This mechanism employs an attention-based approach to model bias-causing interactions and discards extreme bias-causing samples using sparsification. The method is evaluated on datasets like ColorMNIST and CelebA, showing improved performance in bias removal without compromising downstream accuracy. The proposed FARE and SparseFARE attention mechanisms are designed to enhance the learning of fair representations by focusing on bias-reducing samples and avoiding bias-causing samples. However, the paper is critiqued for its limited evaluation on only two datasets and its lack of clarity in certain methodological details.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written and easy to follow, with a clear presentation of the methodology and results.
- The proposed FARE attention mechanism is novel and effectively models bias-causing interactions, improving fairness in representation learning.
- The paper is well-motivated, addressing the critical issue of fairness in representation learning, particularly in the context of continuous protected attributes.
- The use of sparsification via locality-sensitive hashing to discard extreme bias-causing samples is innovative and enhances the practical applicability of the model.
- The paper demonstrates significant improvements in bias removal without compromising downstream accuracy, as evidenced by experimental results on ColorMNIST and CelebA datasets.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper's evaluation is limited to only two datasets, which may not adequately demonstrate the generalizability of the proposed method.
- There is a lack of clarity in some methodological details, particularly concerning the selection of negative samples and the role of the sparsification technique.
- The paper does not sufficiently compare its method with existing fair contrastive learning methods, which could provide a more robust validation of the proposed approach.
- The paper does not adequately address the computational efficiency of the proposed method, which could be a significant concern in practical applications.
- The method's reliance on the sparsification technique may not be universally applicable across different datasets or model configurations.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors provide more details on how the sparsification technique is applied and how it affects the model's performance?
- How does the proposed method compare to other fair contrastive learning methods, particularly in terms of bias removal and computational efficiency?
- Is there a theoretical guarantee that the proposed method can effectively remove bias, and if so, can this be quantitatively demonstrated?
- Could the authors clarify the role of the sparsification technique in the model's performance and discuss potential alternative methods for achieving similar results?
- How does the model handle the selection of negative samples, and what impact does this have on the model's performance and fairness?
