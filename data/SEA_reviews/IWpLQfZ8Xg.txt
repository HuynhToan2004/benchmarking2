 **Summary:**
The paper investigates the local sensitivity of dot-product self-attention, a crucial component in transformer architectures, by developing theoretical bounds for the effect of input feature perturbations on attention output. The authors employ mathematical techniques from optimization and matrix theory to analyze the local sensitivity of dot-product self-attention to ℓ2 perturbations. They validate their theoretical findings through experiments on vision transformers, demonstrating that their bounds are practical and significantly improve sensitivity analysis compared to naive approaches. The study provides insights into achieving low sensitivity in dot-product self-attention against ℓ2 input perturbations, potentially influencing the design of robust transformer architectures.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, clear, and easy to follow, making complex mathematical concepts accessible to a broad audience.
- The authors provide a detailed theoretical analysis of local sensitivity bounds for dot-product self-attention, which is a novel contribution to the field.
- The paper includes both theoretical and empirical validation, demonstrating the practical applicability of the theoretical findings.
- The authors have made significant efforts to address conservatism in their analysis, providing a more nuanced understanding of the impact of perturbations on attention mechanisms.
- The study addresses a critical aspect of transformer architecture, which is of significant interest to the community, and provides new insights into achieving low sensitivity in dot-product self-attention against ℓ2 input perturbations.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a comprehensive discussion on related works, particularly in the area of robustness of transformer networks, which could have enriched the context and depth of the study.
- The scope of the study is somewhat limited as it primarily focuses on the local sensitivity of dot-product self-attention without a broader exploration of other types of attention mechanisms or their impact on robustness.
- The paper could benefit from a more detailed discussion on the computational efficiency of the proposed methods, especially in comparison to existing techniques.
- The empirical results, while validating the theoretical findings, do not convincingly demonstrate the practical utility of the proposed method over existing techniques in terms of robustness or computational efficiency.
- The paper does not adequately address the limitations of the study, such as the scalability of the proposed methods to larger datasets or more complex architectures.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors clarify the difference between the local sensitivity and local Lipschitzness of the self-attention layer?
- How do the authors justify the choice of the norm in their analysis, particularly the use of the Frobenius norm over other norms like the operator norm?
- What are the potential applications of the proposed method in practical scenarios, especially in terms of improving the robustness of transformer architectures?
- Could the authors elaborate on the computational efficiency of their methods compared to existing techniques, particularly in terms of runtime and memory requirements?
- How do the authors address the scalability of their methods to larger datasets or more complex architectures, such as those used in large-scale vision or natural language processing tasks?
