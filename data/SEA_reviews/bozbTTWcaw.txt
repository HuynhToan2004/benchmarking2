 **Summary:**
The paper investigates the optimization of backpropagation through time (BPTT) in the context of physics simulations, focusing on the exploding and vanishing gradient problems. It introduces a modified update rule that disables backpropagation through the neural network inputs, which is aimed at improving gradient balance and reducing the impact of rotation on optimization. The methodology is tested on three control tasks, demonstrating its effectiveness in handling complex physical systems. The paper also discusses the implications of this approach on the stability of optimization landscapes and provides a theoretical analysis of the modified gradient vector field. Despite its innovative approach, the paper is criticized for its limited scope in terms of the complexity of the tasks and the clarity of its presentation.

**Strengths:**
- The paper addresses a significant problem in the field of physics simulations, where the backpropagation through time (BPTT) can lead to exploding and vanishing gradients, which can hinder the optimization process.
- The proposed method is straightforward and simple, yet effectively addresses the problem by modifying the backpropagation through time to balance the gradient and prevent the rotation problem.
- The paper is well-written, with clear and detailed explanations of the problem, method, and results, making it accessible to readers.
- The experimental results are promising, showing that the proposed method can outperform other methods in certain scenarios, such as in the guidance-by-repulsion model and cart pole swing-up tasks.
- The paper provides a theoretical analysis of the modified gradient vector field, which is a novel approach in the field.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a comprehensive analysis of the impact of the proposed method on the optimization landscape, particularly concerning the balance of the gradient and the rotation problem.
- The scope of the tasks used in the experiments is limited, which may not fully demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed method in more complex or realistic scenarios.
- The paper does not sufficiently discuss the limitations of the proposed method, such as its applicability to other types of tasks or the potential for increased computational complexity.
- The presentation of the paper could be improved, particularly in terms of clarity and organization, especially in the figures and the discussion of the results.
- The paper does not sufficiently compare the proposed method with other existing methods, which could provide a more robust evaluation of its effectiveness.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors provide more details on the computational complexity introduced by the proposed method, especially in terms of memory and runtime?
- How does the proposed method compare to other methods that have been proposed to address the exploding and vanishing gradient problems in BPTT?
- In the experiments, how were the hyperparameters chosen, and what impact do these choices have on the performance of the proposed method?
- Can the authors clarify the definition and role of the "modified" update in the context of the experiments, especially in relation to the "combined" update?
- How does the proposed method perform in more complex or realistic scenarios, such as those involving contact dynamics or more complex physical systems?
- Could the authors provide a more detailed discussion on the optimization landscape and how the proposed method affects it, possibly including visualizations or additional experimental results?
