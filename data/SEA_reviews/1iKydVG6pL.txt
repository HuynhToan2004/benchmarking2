 **Summary:**
The paper introduces AlphaSymbol, a novel symbolic regression algorithm that integrates Long Short-Term Memory (LSTM) networks with Monte Carlo Tree Search (MCTS) to enhance search efficiency and accuracy. AlphaSymbol is designed to address the challenges of traditional symbolic regression algorithms by combining MCTS with a pre-trained LSTM, which guides the expansion process and refines the network's capabilities. The algorithm is evaluated across various datasets, showing improvements over existing methods. However, concerns about the novelty and the clarity of the methodology are raised, suggesting that the paper might benefit from a clearer explanation and a more robust comparison with existing works.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel approach to symbolic regression by combining LSTM networks with Monte Carlo Tree Search (MCTS), which is both innovative and effective.
- The experimental results demonstrate that AlphaSymbol outperforms existing state-of-the-art algorithms in terms of accuracy and efficiency, showing a significant improvement over traditional methods.
- The paper is well-written, making it accessible and understandable, which is crucial for a technical paper.
- The proposed method is evaluated on various datasets, demonstrating its versatility and robustness across different scenarios.
- The use of LSTM to guide MCTS is a promising direction for future research, potentially leading to more efficient and accurate symbolic regression algorithms.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a thorough comparison with existing works, particularly those that also use MCTS for symbolic regression, which could undermine the perceived novelty and effectiveness of AlphaSymbol.
- The methodology of AlphaSymbol is complex and not clearly explained, making it difficult for readers to fully understand the proposed method.
- The paper does not adequately address the issue of generalizability, particularly how the pre-trained policy network performs on out-of-distribution (OOD) data.
- There are several typographical and grammatical errors that need correction to enhance the professional quality of the paper.
- The paper does not sufficiently discuss the limitations and potential negative societal impacts of the proposed method, which is crucial for a comprehensive evaluation.

**Questions:**
- Can you provide a more detailed comparison with existing works that also use MCTS for symbolic regression to clarify the unique contributions and advantages of AlphaSymbol?
- How does the pre-trained policy network perform on out-of-distribution (OOD) data, and what measures are in place to ensure generalizability?
- Could you clarify the methodology of AlphaSymbol, particularly how the LSTM network guides the MCTS expansion process and how this improves search efficiency?
- How does the paper address the potential negative societal impacts of the proposed method, and what steps are being taken to mitigate these risks?
