 **Summary:**
The paper investigates the application of Lipschitz constants to Graph Neural Networks (GNNs) to enhance fairness in node classification and link prediction tasks. It proposes a novel method, JacoLip, which leverages Lipschitz constants to control the stability of GNNs, ensuring fairness by preventing significant changes in model predictions based on input biases. The method is theoretically grounded and empirically validated through extensive experiments on six real-world datasets. Despite the novelty and practical utility of the proposed method, concerns were raised about the clarity of the theoretical underpinnings, the scope of the experiments, and the practical applicability of the method in large-scale graph learning scenarios.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel approach using Lipschitz constants to enhance fairness in GNNs, which is a significant advancement in the field of fairness in machine learning.
- The methodology is well-explained, with clear and detailed explanations that make the paper accessible to a broad audience.
- The paper is well-structured, with a clear introduction of the problem, methodology, and experimental results, making it easy to follow and understand.
- The proposed method is innovative, combining Lipschitz constants with GNNs to address fairness issues, which is a significant contribution to the field.
- The paper is well-written, with a clear and concise presentation of the methodology and results, which enhances its readability and comprehensibility.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper could benefit from a more detailed discussion on the computational complexity of the proposed method, particularly in large-scale graph learning scenarios.
- The experiments are limited to smaller datasets, which may not adequately demonstrate the scalability of the proposed method in real-world applications.
- There is a lack of discussion on the limitations of the proposed method, which is crucial for understanding its applicability and potential drawbacks.
- The paper does not sufficiently discuss the relationship between Lipschitz constants and fairness, which is a critical aspect of the proposed method.
- The experimental results do not convincingly demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed method, particularly in terms of its impact on fairness metrics.
- The paper could benefit from a more rigorous comparison with existing methods, including a discussion on how the proposed method compares in terms of computational efficiency and fairness performance.

**Questions:**

- Can the authors provide more detailed results on the impact of the Lipschitz constant on fairness metrics, particularly in terms of how it affects the NDCG metric?
- What are the limitations of the proposed method, and under what conditions might it not be effective?
- How does the proposed method compare to existing methods in terms of fairness performance, particularly in scenarios where fairness is a critical concern?
- Could the authors provide more detailed experimental results, including a broader range of datasets and more comprehensive evaluations of the proposed method?
