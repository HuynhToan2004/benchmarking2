 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a zero-shot human-object interaction detection framework, named PID, which leverages a two-stage approach to enhance the generalization of human-object interaction detection. The first stage utilizes a pre-trained object detector to extract human-object pairs, while the second stage employs a vision-language model (VLM) to predict interactions. The methodology includes the use of conditional multi-modal prompts to improve the alignment between visual and language representations, aiming to reduce the performance gap between seen and unseen classes. The paper is evaluated on the HICO dataset and shows promising results, although it faces criticisms regarding its novelty and the clarity of its contributions.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, making it easy to follow and understand.
- The proposed method achieves good performance on the HICO dataset, demonstrating its effectiveness.
- The use of multi-modal prompts for enhancing the generalization of zero-shot human-object interaction detection is a novel and interesting approach.
- The paper provides a detailed ablation study, which helps in understanding the impact of different components of the proposed method.
- The authors have conducted extensive experiments to evaluate the performance of the proposed method, showing promising results.

**Weaknesses:**
- The novelty of the paper is limited, as the idea of using multi-modal prompts for zero-shot human-object interaction detection has been previously explored.
- The paper lacks clarity in distinguishing between the contributions of the proposed method and existing works, particularly in the use of multi-modal prompts.
- There are concerns about the fairness of the comparison due to the use of a stronger pre-trained object detector, which may not be available to other methods.
- The paper could benefit from a more detailed discussion on the limitations of the proposed method and potential negative societal impacts.
- The paper does not sufficiently discuss the potential negative societal impacts of the proposed method.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors provide more details on the specific contributions of the proposed method beyond the use of multi-modal prompts, particularly in the context of zero-shot human-object interaction detection?
- How does the proposed method compare to other methods that also use multi-modal prompts, especially in terms of the novelty and effectiveness of the prompts used?
- Could the authors clarify the role and impact of the multi-modal prompts in the zero-shot human-object interaction detection task, especially in comparison to other methods that use similar prompts?
- How does the proposed method address the issue of overfitting to seen concepts in the prompt learning process, and what specific measures were taken to prevent this?
- Can the authors provide more details on the training and inference speed of the proposed method, and how it compares to other state-of-the-art methods in terms of computational efficiency?
