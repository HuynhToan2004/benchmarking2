 **Summary:**
This paper investigates training instabilities in transformer models by scaling them down to smaller sizes, focusing on issues like attention logit growth and output logit divergence. It employs various techniques to mitigate these instabilities, such as QK-layernorm and z-loss, and explores how these instabilities manifest across different model scales. The authors also examine the effects of hyperparameters like learning rate, weight decay, and layer width on model stability, providing insights into how these factors influence training dynamics. The paper also discusses the predictability of instabilities based on scaling behaviors observed in smaller models, suggesting potential applications in large-scale model training.

**Strengths:**
- The paper addresses an important and timely problem of training instabilities in large models, which is a significant issue in the field of large language models (LLMs).
- The paper is well-written, clear, and easy to follow, with detailed experiments that are well-executed and provide valuable insights into the behavior of transformer training.
- The paper effectively demonstrates how the results from smaller models can be used to predict instabilities in larger models, which is a crucial aspect for scaling up model training.
- The authors have conducted a comprehensive set of experiments, including sensitivity analysis, scaling analysis, and hyperparameter tuning, which are all valuable for understanding the dynamics of training large models.
- The paper provides a detailed analysis of the relationship between learning rate and loss across scales, which is a novel approach to studying training instabilities.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed discussion on the causes of the observed instabilities, particularly in relation to the scaling of model parameters and the role of the AdamW epsilon parameter.
- There is a limited discussion on the implications of the findings for practical applications, such as the selection of hyperparameters for training large models.
- The paper could benefit from a more systematic approach to predicting instabilities based on scaling behaviors, as the current methodology is somewhat ad-hoc.
- The paper does not provide sufficient evidence to support the claim that instabilities can be predicted before they occur, which is a critical aspect of the study.
- There is a lack of novelty in some of the findings, as similar observations have been made in previous studies, which could limit the perceived impact of the paper.

**Questions:**
- Could you elaborate on the causes of the attention logit growth and output logit divergence, particularly how they relate to the scaling of model parameters?
- How does the AdamW epsilon parameter affect the training dynamics, and what are the implications of setting it to zero?
- In the context of the observed instabilities, what role do the parameters of the Adam optimizer play, and how do they influence the training dynamics?
- How does the paper's approach to predicting instabilities based on scaling behaviors compare to other methods, and what are the advantages or disadvantages of the chosen methodology?
- Can you provide more evidence or examples to support the claim that instabilities can be predicted before they occur, and how does this prediction affect the practical training of large models?
