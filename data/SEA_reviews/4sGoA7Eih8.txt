 **Summary:**
The paper investigates the potential for recovering input data from Transformer models by analyzing their attention weights and outputs. It proposes a theoretical framework and a methodology to recover data from these models, focusing on the inversion of attention weights to reconstruct original data. The paper delves into the mathematical aspects of the attention mechanism and explores the feasibility of inverting attention weights to recover input data. Despite its theoretical approach, the paper lacks empirical validation, making it difficult to assess the practical applicability of the proposed methods. The paper also discusses the implications of these findings for the security and privacy of data processed by transformer models.

- The paper is well-written and structured, with clear explanations of the mathematical concepts and notations used.
- The authors have provided a detailed theoretical analysis of the attention mechanism, focusing on the inversion of attention weights to recover input data.
- The paper addresses an important and timely issue regarding the potential privacy risks associated with the use of Transformer models.
