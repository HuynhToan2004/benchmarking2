 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel loss function aimed at enhancing the confidence of neural networks in classification tasks by predicting confidence levels alongside classifications. The approach involves adding a new node to the output layer of the network to compute confidence scores, which are then integrated into the training process using a margin-based loss function. The method is evaluated on the CIFAR-10 and CIFAR-100 datasets, showing modest improvements in accuracy and confidence scores. However, the paper suffers from significant issues in clarity, originality, and methodological soundness. The presentation is unclear, with numerous grammatical errors and unclear explanations, and the methodology lacks novelty and rigorous experimental validation.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel loss function that incorporates confidence prediction into the training process, which is a significant contribution to the field.
- The proposed method is straightforward and easy to implement, which could facilitate its adoption by practitioners.
- The idea of incorporating a confidence prediction node into the network architecture is innovative and could potentially enhance the reliability of neural network predictions.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper suffers from numerous grammatical errors and lacks clarity in its presentation, making it difficult to follow and understand the proposed methodology.
- The experimental results are limited to small datasets like CIFAR-10 and CIFAR-100, which may not adequately demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed method on larger, more complex datasets.
- The paper lacks a thorough comparison with existing methods, which is essential for establishing the novelty and effectiveness of the proposed approach.
- The paper does not adequately address the issue of overconfident mistakes in neural networks, which is a significant limitation given the focus on confidence prediction.
- There is a lack of discussion on the potential negative societal impacts of the proposed method, which is crucial for ethical considerations in AI research.
- The paper does not provide sufficient details on the experimental setup, such as the specifics of the training procedure and the hyperparameters used, which are crucial for reproducibility and validation of the results.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors clarify the role and significance of the confidence node in the network, especially in relation to the regular classification nodes?
- How does the proposed method address the issue of overconfident mistakes in neural networks?
- Could the authors provide more detailed experimental results, including the standard deviation of the results and a comparison with other confidence prediction methods?
- How does the proposed method compare with other existing methods in terms of computational efficiency and scalability?
- What are the specific hyperparameters used in the experiments, and how were they chosen?
- How does the proposed method perform on larger, more complex datasets, and what are the implications for its applicability in real-world scenarios?
