 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel multi-modal generative model, Multi-modal Latent Diffusion (MLD), which addresses the coherence-quality tradeoff in multi-modal Variational Autoencoders (VAEs) by employing a set of independently trained, uni-modal, deterministic autoencoders. The latent variables from these autoencoders are concatenated into a common latent space, which is fed to a masked diffusion model to enable generative modeling. The model's effectiveness is demonstrated through extensive experiments, showing improvements in both generation quality and coherence across various modalities. Despite its strengths, the paper is criticized for its limited novelty and the complexity of its training process, which involves multiple stages.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, with clear explanations of the proposed method and its limitations.
- The experiments are extensive and well-documented, showing that the proposed method outperforms previous methods in terms of both generation quality and coherence.
- The paper addresses an important and relevant problem in the field of multi-modal generative modeling, specifically focusing on the coherence-quality tradeoff in multi-modal VAEs.
- The method is novel, using a deterministic latent variable model with multi-modal diffusion, which is a departure from traditional VAEs.
- The paper provides a detailed analysis of the limitations of multi-modal VAEs, which is insightful and helps to contextualize the proposed method.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a clear explanation of the intuition behind the proposed method, particularly in how it addresses the coherence-quality tradeoff.
- The methodology is complex and involves multiple stages of training, which could make it less practical for real-world applications.
- The paper does not sufficiently discuss the limitations of the proposed method, such as its potential for generating multi-modal data and its reliance on the quality of the pre-trained encoders.
- There is a lack of novelty in the approach, as it appears to be a combination of existing techniques without significant innovation.
- The paper could benefit from a more detailed discussion on the computational cost and efficiency of the proposed method, particularly in terms of inference time.
- The paper could provide more detailed examples and explanations to clarify the mathematical notations and the role of different variables in the model.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors provide more detailed intuition on how the proposed method addresses the coherence-quality tradeoff?
- How does the proposed method compare to other recent works, such as those mentioned in the review, in terms of performance and novelty?
- Could the authors clarify the role of the multi-time vector in the training process and how it affects the quality of the generated data?
- How does the proposed method handle the potential for generating multi-modal data, especially in scenarios where the modalities might not be strongly correlated?
- Could the authors provide more details on the computational cost and efficiency of the proposed method, particularly in terms of inference time and the complexity of the training process?
