 **Summary:**
The paper introduces LAMP, a novel method that leverages Vision-Language Models (VLMs) to pretrain reinforcement learning (RL) agents, focusing on the use of VLM rewards as an exploration signal during pretraining. The method combines VLM rewards with novelty-seeking exploration rewards from Plan2Explore, aiming to enhance exploration efficiency and task performance. The experiments conducted on RLBench tasks demonstrate that LAMP outperforms other baselines, highlighting its effectiveness in pretraining RL agents. However, concerns about the clarity of the methodology, the need for more robust experimental validation, and the generalizability of the results were raised.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, making it easy to understand, and the method is clearly described.
- The use of VLMs for pretraining RL agents is innovative and shows potential in improving performance across different tasks.
- The integration of VLM rewards with novelty-seeking exploration rewards from Plan2Explore is a novel approach that could enhance the exploration efficiency and task performance.
- The experiments conducted on RLBench tasks demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed method, showing that it outperforms other baselines.
- The paper provides a comprehensive evaluation of the proposed method, including a thorough ablation study on the design of the pretraining reward.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks clarity in several sections, particularly in the methodology and the experiments, which could be improved with better organization and clearer explanations.
- The generalizability of the results is questionable due to the limited variety in the pretraining tasks and the lack of testing on more complex or diverse environments.
- The paper does not adequately address the scalability of the proposed method, particularly in terms of computational resources and the practicality of scaling up to more complex tasks or larger environments.
- The experimental setup and the choice of baselines could be improved, particularly by including more recent and relevant works in the field.
- The paper does not sufficiently address the potential issues with the reliability of VLM-based rewards, which could lead to misleading or inaccurate rewards.

**Questions:**
- Could you clarify how the exploration reward is combined with the VLM reward, especially in terms of the weighting and the impact on the final reward signal?
- How does the proposed method compare to other methods that use VLMs for RL, particularly in terms of computational efficiency and scalability?
- What are the specific advantages of using VLM rewards over other types of rewards, and how do these rewards ensure that they are not misleading or inaccurate?
- How does the method handle the potential for overfitting or misleading rewards in the pretraining phase, and what measures are in place to prevent these issues?
- Could you provide more details on the computational resources required for pretraining the RL agents using the proposed method, and discuss the scalability of the method to more complex or diverse environments?
- How does the method ensure that the pretrained RL agents are generalizable to unseen tasks, and what are the implications if the pretraining environment is significantly different from the test environment?
