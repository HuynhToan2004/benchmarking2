 **Summary:**
The paper introduces PE2, a novel method for automatic prompt engineering, which leverages a step-by-step reasoning template and context specification to guide LLMs in optimizing prompts. PE2 is designed to enhance the performance of LLMs by improving the quality of prompts, particularly in complex reasoning tasks. The method is tested across various benchmarks, demonstrating improvements over existing methods like Iterative APE and APO. Despite its strengths, the paper is criticized for its limited evaluation scope and potential over-reliance on the quality of initial prompts. The methodology is also seen as incremental, with concerns about the novelty and depth of its contributions to the field.

**Strengths:**
- The paper addresses an important and timely topic, with a focus on improving the performance of LLMs through prompt engineering.
- The proposed method, PE2, is simple, effective, and well-explained, with a clear motivation and intuitive design.
- PE2 demonstrates strong performance on various benchmarks, including MultiArith and GSM8K, showing significant improvements over existing methods like Iterative APE and APO.
- The paper provides a comprehensive evaluation, including ablation studies that help in understanding the effectiveness of different components of the proposed method.
- The methodology is well-structured and easy to follow, with a clear presentation of the results and a thorough analysis of the limitations and failure cases.
- The paper is well-written, making it accessible and understandable, with a good balance of technical detail and clarity.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a comprehensive evaluation, particularly in not including a broader range of datasets and baselines, which could have provided a more robust comparison.
- The method's dependence on the quality of the initial prompt is not adequately addressed, with the paper not discussing how PE2 performs when given a poor initial prompt.
- The paper does not sufficiently differentiate its approach from existing methods, such as those discussed in related works, which could lead to questions about the novelty and uniqueness of the proposed method.
- There is a lack of discussion on the potential societal impacts of the proposed method, which could be a significant concern for some readers.
- The paper could benefit from a more detailed discussion on the limitations of the proposed method, including potential negative societal impacts and ethical considerations.

**Questions:**
- How does PE2 perform when given a poor initial prompt?
- Could you clarify the differences between PE2 and existing methods, particularly in terms of novelty and effectiveness?
- How does the proposed method compare to other prompt engineering methods, especially in terms of performance and efficiency?
- Can you provide more details on the computational costs associated with using PE2, particularly in terms of time and resource requirements?
- How does the proposed method handle scenarios where the model proposes incorrect or misleading prompts?
- Could you discuss the potential societal impacts of the proposed method, particularly in terms of fairness, privacy, and other ethical considerations?
