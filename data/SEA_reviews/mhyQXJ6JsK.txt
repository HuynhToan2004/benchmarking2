 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel approach to accelerate computations in equivariant neural networks by utilizing Gaunt coefficients instead of the traditional Clebsch-Gordan coefficients. This method allows for a change in basis from spherical harmonics to a 2D Fourier basis, which can be efficiently computed using Fast Fourier Transforms. The proposed method aims to reduce computational complexity, particularly in the tensor product of irreducible representations, which is a significant bottleneck in equivariant neural networks. The approach is tested on various benchmarks, showing improvements in efficiency and performance over existing methods.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel approach using Gaunt coefficients to accelerate computations in equivariant neural networks, which is a significant advancement in the field.
- The methodology is well-articulated, with clear explanations of the theoretical underpinnings and practical implications.
- The paper demonstrates a significant reduction in computational complexity, which is crucial for improving the efficiency and scalability of equivariant neural networks.
- The experimental results show a substantial improvement in efficiency and performance compared to existing methods, highlighting the effectiveness of the proposed approach.
- The paper is well-written and easy to follow, making it accessible to a broad audience, including both experts and non-experts in the field.
