 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a method to improve the consistency of text-to-3D generation by fine-tuning a pre-trained 2D diffusion model to generate viewpoint-conditioned canonical coordinate maps. This alignment is achieved by conditioning the diffusion model on the extrinsic parameters of a camera, allowing for the generation of consistent 3D shapes that can be integrated into various text-to-3D pipelines. The method is evaluated against several baselines, showing improvements in multi-view consistency and a higher success rate in generating 3D consistent objects. The approach leverages a coarse alignment of geometric priors to avoid over-reliance on detailed 3D data, which is computationally efficient. Despite its effectiveness, the method's novelty is limited, as it primarily fine-tunes existing models without introducing significant new components.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, with clear and easy-to-follow descriptions of the proposed method.
- The method is evaluated against several baselines, showing improvements in multi-view consistency and a higher success rate in generating 3D consistent objects.
- The method is simple, computationally efficient, and can be easily integrated into existing text-to-3D pipelines.
- The paper is well-organized, with clear figures and a clear description of the method.
- The method is technically sound and achieves good results in text-to-3D generation, showing a significant improvement over previous methods.

**Weaknesses:**
- The novelty of the method is limited as it primarily fine-tunes existing models without introducing significant new components.
- The paper lacks a detailed discussion on the limitations of the method, such as the potential for overfitting to the 3D dataset and the impact of the quality of the 3D dataset on the results.
- The paper could benefit from a more detailed discussion on the limitations of the method, such as the potential for overfitting to the 3D dataset and the impact of the quality of the 3D dataset on the results.
- The paper does not adequately address the issue of multi-view inconsistency in the results, which is a significant issue in text-to-3D generation.
- The method's effectiveness in handling complex scenes with multiple objects or intricate details is not thoroughly evaluated.

**Questions:**
- How does the method handle the multi-view inconsistency problem, especially in complex scenes with multiple objects or intricate details?
- What are the specific challenges encountered when integrating the aligned geometric priors into existing text-to-3D pipelines, and how are these challenges addressed?
- How does the method ensure that the canonical coordinates map is generated accurately and consistently across different views, especially in complex scenes?
- How does the method handle the potential for overfitting to the 3D dataset, and what measures are taken to prevent this?
- Could the authors provide more details on the evaluation methodology, particularly the selection and diversity of text prompts used to generate the results?
- How does the method perform in terms of computational efficiency, and what are the potential impacts on the overall performance and accuracy of the text-to-3D generation process?
