 **Summary:**
The paper introduces the Relay Diffusion Model (RDM), a novel approach to image synthesis that addresses the challenges of high-resolution generation using diffusion models. RDM leverages the Discrete Cosine Transform (DCT) to analyze the noise schedule across different resolutions, identifying the main issue as the Signal-to-Noise Ratio (SNR) in the frequency domain. The model introduces a block noise mechanism to ensure equivalence in SNR between low and high resolution, which is crucial for maintaining image quality during diffusion. The authors demonstrate that RDM achieves state-of-the-art performance on datasets like CelebA-HQ and ImageNet, showing significant improvements over existing methods like ADM, LDM, and DiT. Despite its strengths, the paper has been criticized for its limited novelty and the lack of detailed theoretical analysis and comparative experiments.

**Strengths:**
- The paper presents a novel approach to improving the performance of high-resolution generation using diffusion models, which is a significant advancement in the field.
- The introduction of a block noise mechanism is innovative and effectively addresses the issue of Signal-to-Noise Ratio (SNR) in the frequency domain, enhancing the model's performance.
- The paper is well-written, making it accessible and easy to understand, with clear explanations of the proposed method and its components.
- The experimental results are robust, demonstrating the effectiveness of the proposed method, and the paper provides a comprehensive evaluation across various metrics.
- The use of Discrete Cosine Transform (DCT) to analyze the noise schedule across different resolutions is a novel and insightful approach that contributes to a better understanding of the problem.
- The paper is well-motivated, providing a clear and compelling argument for the need to address the issue of noise schedule in high-resolution image generation.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed theoretical analysis of the proposed method, which could strengthen the paper's technical contribution.
- The novelty of the paper is questioned, as the block noise mechanism and the use of DCT for noise analysis have been previously explored in other works.
- There is a lack of comparative experiments with existing methods, particularly those that also use cascaded diffusion models or other advanced techniques like attention mechanisms.
- The paper does not adequately address the computational complexity of the proposed method, which could be a significant concern for practical implementation.
- The paper could benefit from a more detailed discussion on the limitations of the proposed method and potential areas for future research.
- There are minor typographical and formatting errors that need correction for clarity and professionalism.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors provide a more detailed theoretical analysis of the proposed method, particularly focusing on the noise schedule and its impact on high-resolution image generation?
- How does the proposed method compare to other existing methods that use cascaded diffusion models or other advanced techniques like attention mechanisms?
- Can the authors clarify the computational complexity of the proposed method and discuss potential strategies for reducing computational costs?
- How does the proposed method handle different noise levels and noise distributions in practical scenarios?
- Could the authors elaborate on the decision to use a fixed number of diffusion steps for different resolutions and discuss the potential implications of this choice?
- Can the authors provide more details on the training and sampling efficiency of the proposed method, particularly in comparison to other state-of-the-art methods?
- How does the proposed method handle the issue of training-inference mismatch, and what are the potential solutions to address this issue?
