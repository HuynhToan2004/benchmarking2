 **Summary:**
The paper explores the computational power of transformer models by examining their ability to simulate automata and Turing machines with intermediate steps, termed "chain of thought" (CoT). The authors demonstrate that the computational power of transformers increases with the number of intermediate steps, with a logarithmic number of steps only marginally expanding their capabilities, while a linear number allows them to simulate automata and Turing machines, recognizing all regular languages. The study also introduces a novel technique called "layer-norm hash" to simulate recurrence, which is crucial for understanding the transformers' computational power. Despite the theoretical advancements, the paper lacks empirical validation and practical application examples, which could enhance its relevance and applicability.

**Strengths:**
- The paper provides a clear and concise explanation of the computational power of transformers with intermediate steps, supported by rigorous theoretical analysis.
- The introduction of the "layer-norm hash" technique is novel and innovative, enhancing the understanding of how transformers can simulate recurrence.
- The paper is well-written, with a logical flow that makes it accessible and easy to follow, even for readers not deeply familiar with transformer models.
- The results presented in the paper are surprising and interesting, offering a new perspective on the computational capabilities of transformers.
- The paper addresses a significant and relevant topic in the field of transformer models, providing insights into their computational power and potential limitations.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks empirical validation, which could have provided practical evidence of the theoretical claims made.
- There is a notable absence of practical applications or examples that could demonstrate the utility of the results in real-world scenarios.
- The paper primarily focuses on the computational power of transformers without intermediate steps, which might limit its relevance to current research interests.
- The paper's results are theoretical, which might not directly translate to practical improvements in transformer models without further research.
- The paper's focus on logarithmic and linear steps in the chain of thought might not be of immediate interest to the community, as it does not address more complex scenarios such as polynomial steps.
