 **Summary:**
The paper introduces LIFT, a novel method for fine-tuning large language models (LLMs) by updating only one layer at a time, aiming to reduce memory and computational requirements. This approach, termed "Layer-wIse Fine-Tuning," is contrasted with traditional methods that update all layers, which can be computationally intensive. The method is evaluated across various models and tasks, showing improvements in memory efficiency and computational throughput, with results indicating comparable or superior performance to full fine-tuning. The paper also discusses the potential for combining LIFT with other parameter-efficient fine-tuning techniques to further enhance efficiency.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, clear, and easy to follow, making complex concepts accessible to a broad audience.
- The proposed LIFT method is simple, effective, and orthogonal to existing parameter-efficient fine-tuning methods, allowing for efficient training of large language models (LLMs).
- The method shows a significant reduction in memory usage and computational requirements, making it a practical solution for training large models.
- The paper includes a thorough evaluation of the method across various models and tasks, demonstrating its effectiveness and efficiency.
- The authors provide a detailed analysis of the memory and computational savings achieved by LIFT, which is crucial for understanding its impact on training large models.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed comparison of the computational savings achieved by LIFT, which could help in understanding its practical benefits more clearly.
- There is a lack of discussion on the limitations of the proposed method, which is crucial for understanding its applicability and potential drawbacks.
- The paper does not include a comprehensive comparison of LIFT with other parameter-efficient fine-tuning methods, which could help in positioning LIFT within the existing landscape of PEFT techniques.
- The paper does not include a detailed analysis of the impact of different layer selection policies on the performance of LIFT, which could provide insights into how the method can be optimally applied.
- There is a lack of discussion on the potential negative societal impacts of the proposed method, which is crucial for ensuring that the technology is used ethically and responsibly.

**Questions:**
