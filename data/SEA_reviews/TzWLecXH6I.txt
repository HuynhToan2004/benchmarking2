 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel metric, CS-Fluctuation, designed to detect overfitting during the fine-tuning of pre-trained foundation models using the LoRA method. This metric is based on the cosine similarity between the weights of the pre-trained model and those of the LoRA branch, which is used to determine the optimal stopping point for fine-tuning. The proposed method is evaluated on both vision and language models, showing that it can effectively identify the turning point where overfitting begins. However, the paper is critiqued for its limited scope in terms of model sizes and datasets, as well as the lack of a comprehensive comparison with other methods.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel metric, CS-Fluctuation, which is a significant contribution to the field of fine-tuning pre-trained models, particularly in the context of LoRA.
- The proposed method is well-motivated and supported by clear, well-written sections, making it easy to understand and follow.
- The paper presents a practical solution to a common problem in fine-tuning, namely overfitting, which is a significant issue in the field.
- The experiments are thorough, demonstrating the effectiveness of the proposed metric in detecting overfitting and providing insights into the behavior of LoRA fine-tuning.
- The paper is well-organized, with clear figures and tables that effectively illustrate the main points and results.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a comprehensive comparison with other methods, particularly in terms of computational efficiency and effectiveness across different model sizes and datasets.
- The method's applicability is limited to LoRA-based fine-tuning, which may not be the most common or effective method for all applications.
- The paper does not adequately address the issue of overfitting in other fine-tuning methods, such as those using full model fine-tuning.
- There is a lack of discussion on the potential negative societal impacts of the proposed method, which could be a significant concern for some applications.
- The paper could benefit from a more detailed discussion on the limitations of the proposed method and potential future directions for research.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors provide a more detailed comparison with other methods, particularly in terms of computational efficiency and effectiveness across different model sizes and datasets?
- How does the proposed method compare to other methods for detecting overfitting, such as those based on other metrics or methods?
- Can the authors discuss the potential negative societal impacts of the proposed method and how these might be mitigated or addressed?
- In terms of the method's applicability, how does it perform in different settings, such as those with larger or smaller datasets, or different types of models?
- Could the authors provide more details on the computational resources required for the proposed method and how this might impact its practical use?
