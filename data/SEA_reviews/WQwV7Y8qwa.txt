 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel model called multi-region switching dynamical systems (MR-SDS), designed to analyze multiregional neural dynamics by decomposing them into local and cross-region components. MR-SDS utilizes a switching nonlinear state space model that incorporates directed interactions between brain regions and accounts for sensory inputs, history effects, and heterogeneity across days and animals. The model is evaluated on both simulated and real neural data, showing that it outperforms existing models in recovering latent trajectories and cross-region communication profiles. The authors demonstrate the model's ability to capture non-linear interactions between brain regions and provide insights into the dynamics of neural systems.

**Strengths:**
- The paper addresses an important and relevant problem in the field of neural data analysis, focusing on the dynamics of neural systems across multiple brain regions.
- The methodology is well-articulated, with a clear presentation of the proposed model and its application to both simulated and real neural data.
- The paper provides a comprehensive evaluation of the model's performance, comparing it against various existing models and demonstrating its effectiveness in recovering latent trajectories and cross-region communication profiles.
- The inclusion of both simulated and real neural data, along with a thorough evaluation against existing models, strengthens the paper's claims and validates the proposed approach.
- The paper is well-written, making it accessible and easy to follow, which is crucial for a broad audience in neuroscience.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a clear and detailed description of the data used in the experiments, particularly the mesoscopic and widefield datasets, which are crucial for understanding the results and their applicability.
- There is a significant absence of theoretical analysis and justification for the model's design choices, which could strengthen the paper's credibility and provide a deeper understanding of the model's effectiveness.
- The paper could benefit from more comprehensive comparisons with other models, particularly in terms of the model's expressiveness and its ability to capture complex dynamics.
- There is a need for more detailed discussion on the model's assumptions and limitations, as well as a more thorough exploration of its potential societal impacts.
- The paper's presentation could be improved by including a clearer explanation of the model's components, such as the multiregion switching Lotka-Volterra simulation and the hierarchical evidence accumulation and reset with double-well dynamics.

**Questions:**
- Could you provide a more detailed description of the mesoscopic and widefield datasets used in the experiments, including the number of neurons, recording regions, and the experimental setup?
- How does the model handle the learning of the number of regions and the connectivity between them?
- Is there a possibility to extend the model to include unobserved regions or neural activity that influences the dynamics of the observed regions?
- Can you clarify the role and significance of the messages in the model, particularly in terms of their impact on the dynamics and the decoding of neural activity?
- How does the model compare in terms of expressiveness with other models, such as those that use RNNs or other types of neural networks?
- Could you provide more details on the model's assumptions and limitations, and discuss how these might affect the model's applicability and effectiveness in real-world scenarios?
