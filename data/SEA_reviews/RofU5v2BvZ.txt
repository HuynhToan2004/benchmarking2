 **Summary:**
The paper introduces HAPLAN, a framework for human-AI coordination that utilizes Large Language Models (LLMs) to generate action plans, or conventions, which guide both human and AI agents in task execution. The approach decomposes complex problems into simpler sub-problems, allowing for efficient coordination and human feedback to refine the plans. The framework is tested in the Overcooked-AI environment, showing improved performance over existing methods. However, the paper's experimental scope is limited to this environment, and the generalizability of the approach to other domains is unclear.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces an innovative approach using LLMs to facilitate human-AI coordination, which is a significant advancement in the field.
- The methodology of decomposing complex problems into simpler sub-problems and utilizing multiple sessions for LLM reasoning is a novel and effective approach.
- The paper is well-structured, clear, and easy to follow, making it accessible to a broad audience.
- The experimental results demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed method, showing improvements over existing methods in human-AI coordination.
- The paper provides a detailed analysis of the LLM's role in human-AI coordination, highlighting its potential for enhancing value alignment and domain knowledge incorporation.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper's experimental scope is limited to the Overcooked-AI environment, raising concerns about the generalizability of the findings to other domains.
- There is a lack of discussion on the limitations of the proposed method, which is crucial for understanding its applicability and potential challenges.
- The paper does not sufficiently compare its method with other LLM-based approaches for multi-agent coordination, which could provide a more comprehensive understanding of the method's advantages and disadvantages.
- The paper's evaluation metrics are not clearly defined, making it difficult to assess the effectiveness of the proposed method objectively.
- The method's dependency on LLMs for generating action plans could limit its scalability and applicability in more complex or diverse environments.
- The paper lacks a detailed discussion on the limitations and potential negative societal impacts of the proposed method, which is essential for a comprehensive understanding of its implications.

**Questions:**
- Can the proposed method be generalized to other domains beyond the Overcooked-AI environment? If so, how easily can it be adapted to new domains?
- What are the limitations of the proposed method, and how do these impact its performance and applicability?
- How does the proposed method compare with other LLM-based approaches for multi-agent coordination in terms of effectiveness and efficiency?
- Could you provide more details on the training process of the low-level policies mentioned in Section 4.2?
- How does the method handle scenarios where the LLM generates incorrect or inefficient conventions, and what measures are in place to prevent or correct such errors?
- Can you clarify the evaluation metrics used in the paper and how they were chosen to ensure the validity and reliability of the results?
