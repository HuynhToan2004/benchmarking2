 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a general framework for designing intrinsic Riemannian classifiers on SPD manifolds, which is a significant extension of existing methods that rely on specific geometric properties. The framework is based on the Riemannian logarithm and allows for the definition of deformed Riemannian metrics, which are then used to construct diverse SPD classifiers. The methodology is validated through experiments on three datasets, demonstrating improved performance over existing methods. The paper also discusses the theoretical underpinnings and practical implications of the proposed approach, which could potentially broaden the applicability of Riemannian classifiers in various machine learning applications.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written and easy to follow, with clear explanations of the proposed methodology and its application to various Riemannian metrics.
- The introduction of a general framework for designing intrinsic classifiers on SPD manifolds is a significant contribution to the field, as it allows for the application of Riemannian classifiers beyond specific metrics.
- The paper provides a comprehensive discussion on the existence of Riemannian logarithms and their implications for the design of classifiers, which is a novel approach compared to existing methods.
- The experimental results demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed method, showing improvements over existing methods in some scenarios.
- The paper is well-organized, with a clear structure that makes it easy to understand the contributions and the theoretical underpinnings of the proposed method.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a clear motivation for the use of Riemannian classifiers in general, which could limit its appeal to a broader audience in the machine learning community.
- The experimental evaluation is limited to a few datasets, which might not fully demonstrate the generalizability of the proposed method across different types of data and applications.
- The paper does not adequately discuss the limitations of the proposed method, which could include computational complexity, scalability, and the applicability to different types of data and metrics.
- There are some minor issues with the clarity and presentation of the paper, such as unclear definitions and notations, which could confuse readers unfamiliar with the subject matter.
- The paper does not sufficiently compare its method with existing approaches, which could undermine the claims of novelty and effectiveness.
