 **Summary:**
The paper introduces ModernTCN, a novel convolution-based model designed for time series analysis, aiming to address the limitations of traditional convolutional neural networks (CNNs) in capturing long-term temporal dependencies. ModernTCN integrates ideas from recent advancements in computer vision, particularly the use of large kernel sizes to expand the effective receptive field (ERF), and introduces new mechanisms like DWConvDW and ConvFFN1/2 to handle variable dimensions and cross-variable dependencies. The model is evaluated across various time series tasks, demonstrating competitive performance and efficiency improvements over existing methods. However, concerns are raised about the novelty of the model, its theoretical contributions, and the clarity of its advantages over existing models.

**Strengths:**
- The paper addresses a significant and timely issue in time series analysis, focusing on the limitations of traditional convolutional neural networks (CNNs) in capturing long-term temporal dependencies.
- The authors have made a notable effort to modernize the traditional TCN and incorporate time series-related modifications to make it more suitable for time series tasks, which is a novel approach.
- The paper is well-structured, with clear explanations of the proposed methodologies and the motivation behind them, making it accessible to readers.
- The experimental results are compelling, demonstrating the effectiveness of the proposed methods in various time series tasks, including forecasting, imputation, classification, and anomaly detection.
- The paper provides a detailed comparison with existing models, highlighting the advantages of the proposed model in terms of efficiency and performance.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed comparison with state-of-the-art methods in terms of performance and efficiency, which could undermine the claims of superiority.
- The novelty of the proposed method is questionable, as it appears to be a combination of existing techniques from computer vision and time series analysis without significant new insights or theoretical contributions.
- The paper could benefit from a more thorough discussion on the limitations of the proposed method and potential avenues for future research.
- The paper's writing style and organization could be improved to enhance readability and clarity, particularly in the presentation of figures and tables.
- The experimental setup and results could be more convincing if the paper included more comprehensive ablation studies and comparisons with a broader range of models.
- The paper's claims about the superiority of convolution-based models over transformer-based models are not well-supported, and the advantages of ModernTCN over existing models are not clearly articulated.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors provide a more detailed comparison with state-of-the-art methods in terms of performance and efficiency?
- How does the proposed method compare to other modernization efforts in CNNs, particularly in terms of efficiency and effectiveness?
- Could the authors elaborate on the theoretical contributions of their work, particularly how it advances the understanding or application of convolution in time series analysis?
- How does the proposed method handle variable dimensions and cross-variable dependencies in multivariate time series?
- Can the authors clarify the role and effectiveness of the ConvFFN1/2 in handling cross-variable dependencies?
- How does ModernTCN handle the limitations of convolution-based models in capturing long-term temporal dependencies, and what are the specific advantages of using convolution in time series analysis?
- Could the authors provide more detailed experimental results, including ablation studies and comparisons with a broader range of models, to support their claims?
