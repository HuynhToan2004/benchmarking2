 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel framework for cascading reinforcement learning (RL) to address the challenges of cascading bandits, which include the influence of user states and the transition of states. The authors propose a generalized cascading RL framework and develop algorithms such as CascadingVI and CascadingBPI to efficiently tackle these challenges. The framework is designed to handle the computational complexity of finding the optimal item list, which is crucial for maximizing expected cumulative rewards. The paper provides theoretical guarantees and empirical evidence, showcasing the superiority of the proposed algorithms over existing methods. However, the paper lacks a detailed discussion on the novelty of the algorithmic ideas and the practical applicability of the model in real-world scenarios.

**Strengths:**
- The paper addresses the significant problem of cascading bandits, which is of high importance in the field of online learning, particularly in recommendation systems.
- The authors have provided a thorough analysis of the problem and proposed a novel approach to address the computational challenges associated with the cascading bandit problem.
- The paper is well-structured, with clear problem formulation, detailed algorithm descriptions, and rigorous theoretical analysis.
- The proposed algorithms, such as CascadingVI and CascadingBPI, are computationally and sample efficient, offering near-optimal regret and sample complexity guarantees.
- The paper is the first to introduce reinforcement learning (RL) into the cascading bandit framework, enhancing the understanding and potential solutions for this problem.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed discussion on the novelty of the algorithmic ideas, particularly in relation to existing literature.
- The paper could benefit from a more detailed discussion on the practical implications and limitations of the model, especially in real-world scenarios where the assumptions might not hold.
- There is a need for more clarity in the presentation of the paper, particularly in the introduction and related work sections, to make the content more accessible to a broader audience.
- The paper could provide more detailed comparisons with existing methods, such as CascadingVI-Oracle and CascadingVI-Bonus, to better illustrate the advantages of the proposed algorithms.
- The paper could benefit from a more comprehensive literature review and a clearer delineation of its contributions compared to existing works.
