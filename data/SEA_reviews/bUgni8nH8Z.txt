 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel geometric parameterization (GmP) for ReLU networks, aiming to enhance feature learning by decoupling radial and angular parameters in the hyperspherical coordinate system. This approach is supported by theoretical analysis and empirical results demonstrating improvements in optimization stability, convergence speed, and generalization performance across various models and datasets, including ResNet-50 on ImageNet. The authors also explore the stability of the GmP under perturbations, showing it to be more robust than standard parameterizations. Despite its innovative approach, the paper is critiqued for its limited empirical evaluation, focusing primarily on ResNet-50, and for not sufficiently addressing the scalability and generalization of the GmP across different network architectures and datasets.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, clear, and easy to follow, with a logical flow that facilitates understanding.
- The introduction of the GmP parameterization is innovative and provides a novel approach to neural network training, particularly in its application to ReLU networks.
- The paper includes both theoretical and empirical evaluations, which support the claims made and demonstrate the effectiveness of the GmP.
- The experimental results are robust and show that the GmP outperforms other methods in terms of accuracy, convergence speed, and generalization performance.
- The paper provides a clear and detailed explanation of the GmP and its theoretical underpinnings, which could be useful for further research and development in the field.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks comprehensive empirical evaluations, focusing primarily on ResNet-50 and not adequately comparing the GmP with other state-of-the-art methods across a broader range of datasets and architectures.
- The paper does not sufficiently address the scalability and generalization of the GmP to different network architectures and datasets, which could limit its applicability and impact.
- The novelty of the GmP is questioned, as it seems to be a combination of existing techniques like weight normalization and batch normalization, without significant new contributions.
- The paper does not adequately discuss the limitations of the GmP, such as its computational cost and potential issues with layer-wise initialization.
- The experimental setup and results are not sufficiently detailed or compared with other methods, which could undermine the credibility of the results.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors clarify how the GmP can be applied to other network architectures beyond ReLU networks, and discuss any potential limitations or challenges in doing so?
- How does the GmP compare in terms of computational cost and training time with other methods, particularly in large-scale applications?
- Can the authors provide more detailed comparisons of the GmP with other methods, including both theoretical and empirical results, to better establish its advantages and disadvantages?
- How does the GmP perform on other datasets and models beyond those tested in the paper, and what are the implications of these results for the broader applicability of the method?
- Can the authors discuss the potential impact of the GmP on the training of transformer models, given the increasing popularity of these models in deep learning?
