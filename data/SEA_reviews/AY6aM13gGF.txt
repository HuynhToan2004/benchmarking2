 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel framework, LaMo, which integrates pre-trained language models with offline reinforcement learning (RL) to enhance decision-making capabilities in low-data regimes. LaMo utilizes a decision transformer architecture, initialized with pre-trained language models, and employs techniques like LoRA fine-tuning and auxiliary language prediction loss to adapt to RL tasks. The framework is tested across various sparse and dense reward tasks, demonstrating significant improvements over existing methods like CQL and DT, particularly in low-data scenarios. The paper provides extensive ablation studies to validate the contributions of its components and showcases the potential of using pre-trained language models in offline RL.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel framework, LaMo, which effectively leverages pre-trained language models to enhance the performance of offline reinforcement learning (RL) in scenarios with limited data.
- The methodology is well-articulated, with a clear motivation and a logical structure that makes it easy to follow.
- Extensive empirical results are presented, demonstrating the effectiveness of the proposed method across various tasks and data ratios.
- The paper provides a comprehensive set of ablation studies, which are valuable for understanding the impact of different components of the proposed method.
- The paper is well-written, with a clear presentation of the problem, methodology, and results, which enhances its readability and accessibility.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed comparison with other state-of-the-art methods, particularly those that use similar approaches like LoRA fine-tuning and auxiliary language prediction loss.
- The novelty of the proposed method is limited, as it builds upon existing techniques like LoRA and auxiliary language prediction loss, which have been used in similar contexts.
- The paper could benefit from a more thorough discussion on the limitations and potential negative societal impacts of the proposed method.
- There is a need for more detailed analysis and discussion on the results, particularly the performance of LaMo in dense-reward tasks and the role of the auxiliary language prediction loss in different scenarios.
- The paper could be improved by providing more detailed explanations and justifications for the design choices made in the LaMo framework.

**Questions:**
- Could you provide a more detailed comparison with other state-of-the-art methods, particularly those that use similar approaches like LoRA and auxiliary language prediction loss?
- How does the proposed method perform in scenarios with different types of rewards, such as dense vs. sparse rewards?
- Can you explain the rationale behind the specific design choices in the LaMo framework, particularly the use of LoRA and the auxiliary language prediction loss?
- What are the potential negative societal impacts of the proposed method, and how are these addressed or mitigated?
- How does the performance of LaMo compare when using different pre-trained language models, and what are the implications of using different models for different tasks?
