 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel approach to training neural networks by viewing the training process as a nonlinear dynamical system, leveraging Koopman operator theory to accelerate convergence. It proposes two methods: Koopman-based Gradient Acceleration (KGA) and Koopman-based Weight-level Masking (KWM), aimed at enhancing training efficiency by manipulating the decay rates of Koopman modes. The methods aim to reduce computational complexity and improve training efficiency, particularly in large-scale models. However, the paper faces criticism for its limited experimental scope, lack of comprehensive theoretical justification, and unclear presentation of results.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel approach to training neural networks by interpreting the training process as a nonlinear dynamical system, which is both original and theoretically interesting.
- The proposed Koopman-based Gradient Acceleration (KGA) and Koopman-based Weight-level Masking (KWM) are innovative strategies that aim to accelerate model convergence and reduce computational complexity, respectively.
- The paper is well-written and easy to follow, making the complex concepts accessible to a broader audience.
- The motivation behind the work is well-articulated, highlighting the potential of Koopman operator theory to improve the training process of deep neural networks.
- The paper provides a thorough evaluation of the proposed methods, comparing them with other baseline methods and demonstrating their effectiveness in reducing training time and improving convergence.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a comprehensive theoretical justification for the proposed methods, particularly in explaining why the Koopman operator, which is typically non-linear, would behave linearly in the context of neural network training.
- The experimental results are not convincing, with the proposed methods showing marginal improvements over baseline methods in some cases, and no significant improvements in others.
- The paper does not adequately address the scalability of the proposed methods to larger and more complex neural network architectures, which could limit their applicability.
- There are several presentation issues, including unclear figures and a lack of clarity in the methodology and results sections.
- The paper does not sufficiently discuss the limitations of the proposed methods, such as the potential for increased computational complexity and the need for additional hyperparameters.
- The paper does not include a comprehensive discussion on the ethical considerations or potential societal impacts of the proposed methods.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors clarify the assumptions made in applying Koopman operator theory to the training process of deep neural networks?
- How does the proposed method compare in terms of computational efficiency and convergence speed to other state-of-the-art training methods?
- What are the specific conditions under which the proposed method is expected to show significant improvements in training efficiency?
- Can the authors provide more detailed theoretical justification for the use of Koopman operator theory in the training process of neural networks?
- How does the proposed method handle the issue of overfitting, particularly in scenarios where the training data is limited or noisy?
- Could the authors elaborate on the choice of hyperparameters, such as the window size and the acceleration rate, and discuss how these choices impact the performance of the proposed methods?
- What are the implications of the proposed methods for the interpretability and explainability of neural network models?
