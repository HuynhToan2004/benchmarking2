 **Summary:**
The paper investigates the calibration error of teacher models in knowledge distillation (KD) and proposes a temperature-based calibration method to improve student model performance. The authors argue that a well-calibrated teacher model is crucial for effective KD, and the calibration error is a significant factor in determining the teacher's effectiveness. The paper presents empirical evidence showing that lower calibration errors in teacher models lead to better student model performance. The proposed method involves calibrating the teacher model using temperature scaling to reduce overconfidence, which in turn enhances the student model's performance. The experiments are conducted on various datasets, including CIFAR-100 and ImageNet, and the results show that the proposed method outperforms existing KD methods.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, easy to follow, and provides a clear motivation for the research.
- The proposed method is simple yet effective, and the results are promising, showing improvements over previous methods.
- The paper introduces a novel approach by using calibration error as a criterion for choosing a good teacher in KD, which is a significant contribution to the field.
- The experiments are comprehensive, covering various datasets and models, and demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed method.
- The paper provides a detailed analysis of the calibration error in KD, which is a less explored area, and offers insights into the relationship between teacher calibration and student model performance.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed explanation of why temperature scaling is effective in reducing calibration error and improving student model performance.
- There is a lack of comparison with other calibration methods such as isotonic regression, which could provide a more robust evaluation of the proposed method.
- The paper does not discuss the limitations of the proposed method, which could help in understanding its applicability and potential drawbacks.
- The paper does not provide sufficient justification for the choice of the temperature parameter (T) and its impact on the performance of the student model.
- The experiments are limited to specific datasets and models, which may not generalize well to other architectures or datasets.
- The paper could benefit from a more thorough discussion of related works, especially those that have explored similar aspects of KD such as temperature scaling and calibration error.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors provide more insights into why temperature scaling is effective in reducing calibration error and improving student model performance?
- How does the proposed method compare with other calibration methods like isotonic regression?
- Could the authors elaborate on the choice of the temperature parameter (T) and its impact on the performance of the student model?
- What are the limitations of the proposed method, and under what conditions might it not be effective?
- How does the proposed method perform when applied to other datasets or models beyond those tested in the paper?
- Could the authors discuss the potential societal impact of their work and any ethical considerations related to the use of KD in model deployment?
