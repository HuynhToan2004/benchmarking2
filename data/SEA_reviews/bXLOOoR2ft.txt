 **Summary:**
The paper introduces DoraemonGPT, a system designed to handle dynamic video tasks by utilizing a combination of large language models (LLMs) and a Monte Carlo Tree Search (MCTS) planner. The system is designed to process videos by first extracting task-related attributes into a symbolic memory, which is then queried by sub-task tools to provide intermediate results. The MCTS planner is used to explore the planning space and find feasible solutions, which are summarized into a final answer. The system's effectiveness is demonstrated through experiments on the NExT-QA benchmark, showing improvements over existing LLM-driven visual agents. However, the paper is criticized for its lack of clarity in describing the MCTS planner and its experimental setup, and for its limited novelty in integrating existing components.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, making it easy to follow and understand the methodology.
- The proposed method integrates several existing components, including LLM, MCTS, and symbolic memory, to solve dynamic video tasks.
- The method is evaluated on the NExT-QA benchmark, demonstrating improvements over existing LLM-driven visual agents.
- The paper includes ablation studies that help in understanding the contributions of different components of the proposed method.
- The method is innovative in its approach to querying symbolic memory using sub-task tools, which is a novel application in the context of dynamic video tasks.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks clarity in describing the MCTS planner, particularly in how it is used to solve the task and the specifics of its implementation.
- The novelty of the work is limited as it primarily integrates existing components without significant modification or innovation.
- The paper does not provide sufficient details on the experimental setup, such as the number of API calls and the specifics of the symbolic memory construction.
- The paper does not adequately address the scalability of the proposed method, particularly in terms of the complexity and computational cost of the MCTS planner.
- The paper could benefit from a more detailed discussion on the limitations of the proposed method and how these might affect its practical application.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors provide more details on the MCTS planner, specifically how it is used to solve the task and what the input and output formats are?
- How does the proposed method handle the scalability issues, particularly in terms of the complexity and computational cost of the MCTS planner?
- Could the authors clarify the specifics of the symbolic memory construction, such as how it is initialized and how the task-related attributes are extracted?
- How does the proposed method handle the integration of external knowledge, and what are the specifics of this integration process?
- Could the authors provide more details on the experimental setup, such as the number of API calls and the specifics of the symbolic memory construction?
- How does the proposed method compare to other state-of-the-art methods in terms of performance and computational efficiency?
