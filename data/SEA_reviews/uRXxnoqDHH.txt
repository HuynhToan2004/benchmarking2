 **Summary:**
The paper introduces MoAT, a multi-modal augmented time series forecasting framework that integrates both sample-wise and feature-wise augmentation methods to enrich multi-modal representation learning. MoAT leverages a joint trend-seasonal decomposition module to capture trend and seasonal patterns from both time series and text information, enhancing forecasting accuracy. The framework demonstrates its effectiveness through extensive experiments, showing improvements over state-of-the-art methods. However, concerns were raised regarding the novelty of the approach, as it appears to be a combination of existing techniques without significant innovation. The paper's contribution is seen as incremental, focusing on the integration of text data with time series forecasting, which has been explored in prior works.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, with clear descriptions of the proposed framework and the experiments conducted.
- The methodology is technically sound, with a clear motivation for the study.
- The paper introduces a novel multi-modal augmented framework that effectively integrates information from various sources, including textual data, to enhance time series forecasting accuracy.
- The joint trend-seasonal decomposition module is innovative, effectively capturing trend and seasonal patterns from both time series and text information.
- The paper is well-structured, with clear figures and tables that aid in understanding the proposed framework and the results.
- The authors have released the datasets and code, which is beneficial for reproducibility and further research in the field.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper's contribution is seen as incremental, primarily combining existing techniques without significant innovation.
- The paper's focus on text data as the only additional modality might limit its applicability to other types of data, such as images or audio.
- The paper lacks a detailed discussion on the challenges and limitations of the proposed framework, which could enhance its credibility.
- The experimental setup is limited, and the paper does not include comparisons with state-of-the-art models in the field of time series forecasting, which could provide a more robust evaluation.
- The paper does not discuss the potential negative societal impacts of the work, which is a critical aspect of ethical considerations in AI research.
- The paper's claims of novelty are not convincingly supported by the experimental results, which could be improved by including more comprehensive evaluations.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors clarify the novelty of their approach in comparison to existing methods, particularly in terms of the integration of text data with time series forecasting?
- How does the proposed framework handle the integration of other modalities beyond text data, such as images or audio?
- Could the authors provide more details on the experimental setup, including the specifics of the datasets used and the comparisons made with other models?
- What are the potential negative societal impacts of the proposed framework, and how do the authors address these concerns?
- Can the authors elaborate on the challenges and limitations of the proposed framework, and discuss how these might affect its practical implementation and effectiveness?
- How does the proposed framework perform when compared to state-of-the-art models in terms of computational efficiency and scalability?
