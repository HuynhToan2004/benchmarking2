 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel Thompson sampling algorithm, termed DML-TS-NNR, for contextual bandits in the mobile health (mHealth) domain. This algorithm leverages a Double Machine Learning (DML) framework to model baseline rewards and pool information across users and time using nearest-neighbor regularization. Theoretical analysis and empirical results demonstrate that DML-TS-NNR achieves smaller confidence sets and improved regret bounds compared to existing methods. The algorithm is designed to handle non-linear baseline rewards and pool information across individuals and time, which is crucial for mHealth applications. The paper also discusses the algorithm's robustness to misspecifications in the baseline reward model.

**Strengths:**
- The paper addresses the mHealth problem, which is a significant and relevant application area in the field of contextual bandits.
- The proposed algorithm, DML-TS-NNR, is novel and theoretically sound, offering a new approach to handling non-linear baseline rewards and pooling information across individuals and time.
- The paper is well-structured and clearly written, with a comprehensive literature review and a clear presentation of the problem and solution.
- The empirical results demonstrate the superior performance of DML-TS-NNR compared to existing methods, showing a reduction in confidence set sizes and an improved regret bound.
- The theoretical analysis is robust, providing a strong foundation for the algorithm's effectiveness.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed discussion on the computational complexity of the algorithm, particularly the time and space complexity of the matrix inverse and determinant computations.
- The experimental setup could be improved by including more realistic scenarios with varying numbers of users and stages, and by comparing the performance of the proposed method with other recent contextual bandit algorithms.
- The paper could benefit from a more thorough comparison with existing methods, particularly those that handle similar problems in the mHealth domain.
- The assumptions made in the theoretical analysis, such as the boundedness of the estimator and the boundedness of the weights, are not clearly justified or explained.
- The paper could be clearer in its presentation, particularly in the definitions and explanations of key terms and concepts, such as the differential reward and the pseudo-outcome.

**Questions:**
- Could you clarify the computational complexity of the algorithm, particularly the time and space complexity of the matrix inverse and determinant computations?
- How does the proposed method handle scenarios where the number of users and stages varies?
- Could you provide more details on how the network cohesion penalty is calculated and how it affects the performance of the algorithm?
- How does the proposed method compare with other recent contextual bandit algorithms, particularly those that handle similar problems in the mHealth domain?
- Can you elaborate on the assumptions made in the theoretical analysis, particularly the boundedness of the estimator and the weights?
- How does the proposed method handle scenarios where the differential reward is not linear?
- Could you explain the choice of using Thompson sampling over other algorithms and how it impacts the performance of the algorithm?
