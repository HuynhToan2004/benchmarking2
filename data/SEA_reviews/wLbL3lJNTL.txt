 **Summary:**
The paper explores the integration of multiple sensor modalities, specifically proprioceptive and visual information, into reinforcement learning (RL) through a novel combination of reconstruction-based and contrastive losses. This approach aims to enhance the learning of joint representations that can effectively handle different sensor modalities, improving performance in various tasks such as locomotion and mobile manipulation. The proposed methodology, which includes a recurrent state space model (RSSM), is evaluated across multiple benchmarks, demonstrating its effectiveness in handling complex tasks and distractions in visual inputs. Despite the innovative approach and the robust evaluation, concerns were raised regarding the clarity of the methodological details, the necessity of the proposed method over existing approaches, and the fairness of the comparisons made.

**Strengths:**
- The paper addresses an important problem in robotics, focusing on the integration of multiple sensor modalities in reinforcement learning (RL), which is crucial for improving performance in complex tasks.
- The authors provide a thorough evaluation across multiple tasks and environments, demonstrating the effectiveness of their approach.
- The paper is well-written, clear, and easy to follow, with a detailed appendix that provides additional information on the experimental setup and methodology.
- The combination of reconstruction-based and contrastive losses is novel and innovative, offering a unique approach to learning joint representations that can handle different sensor modalities effectively.
- The results are robust, showing that the proposed method outperforms existing methods in a variety of tasks, demonstrating its practical utility.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a clear and detailed explanation of the methodology, particularly the specifics of how the joint representations are learned and how they are used in different tasks.
- There is a lack of comparison with state-of-the-art methods, such as DreamerPro, which could have provided a more robust evaluation of the proposed approach.
- The paper does not adequately address the limitations of the proposed method, such as the potential for overfitting and the scalability of the approach to more complex or varied environments.
- The paper's claims about the superiority of the proposed method over existing approaches are not convincingly supported by the experimental results, which do not consistently show a significant improvement over the baselines.
- The paper could benefit from a more comprehensive discussion on the related work and a clearer explanation of the experimental results and their implications.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors clarify the specifics of how the joint representations are learned and how they are used in different tasks?
- How does the proposed method handle the potential for overfitting, especially in more complex or varied environments?
- Could the authors provide a more detailed comparison with state-of-the-art methods, such as DreamerPro, to better establish the effectiveness of their approach?
- What are the specific advantages of the proposed method over existing approaches, particularly in terms of performance and scalability?
- How does the method handle different types of sensor modalities, such as depth images versus RGB images, and what are the implications of these differences for the performance of the model?
- Can the authors provide more detailed results and comparisons, particularly in the appendix, to support the claims made in the paper?
