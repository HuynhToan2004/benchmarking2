 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel approach using Large Language Models (LLMs) to predict the feasibility of state-object pairs in Open-World Compositional Zero-Shot Learning (OW-CZSL). The method leverages in-context learning to guide LLMs on the task, improving performance across various benchmarks. The authors conducted extensive experiments, comparing their approach to existing methods and demonstrating significant improvements in accuracy and feasibility prediction. The paper also explores the impact of different LLMs and prompt variations, highlighting the effectiveness of the proposed method.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel approach using Large Language Models (LLMs) for feasibility prediction in Open-World Compositional Zero-Shot Learning (OW-CZSL), which is a significant advancement in the field.
- The methodology is well-explained, with a clear motivation and a detailed description of the experimental setup, including the use of different LLMs and their impact on performance.
- The paper demonstrates the effectiveness of the proposed method through extensive experiments, showing significant improvements over existing methods on three benchmark datasets.
- The use of in-context learning and the guidance provided by seen pairs enhances the LLM's understanding of the task, leading to improved performance.
- The paper is well-written, with clear explanations of the experimental results and the methodology, making it accessible and understandable to readers.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed analysis of the failure cases, particularly the misclassifications of state-object pairs, which could provide valuable insights into the limitations of the proposed method.
- There is a need for more diverse datasets to validate the generalizability of the findings, as the current study primarily uses datasets from the same domain.
- The paper could benefit from a more comprehensive discussion on the societal impact of using LLMs for feasibility prediction, especially considering the potential biases and limitations of these models.
- The paper does not sufficiently address the computational cost of using LLMs, which could be a significant limitation for practical applications.
- The paper could provide more details on the selection of the threshold for feasibility prediction and the impact of this selection on the performance of the model.
- The paper lacks a detailed comparison with other state-of-the-art methods, such as those using GCNs, which could provide a more robust evaluation of the proposed method.

**Questions:**
- Could you provide more details on the selection of the threshold for feasibility prediction and the impact of this selection on the performance of the model?
- How does the proposed method perform when applied to datasets from different domains, such as those involving natural scenes or medical images?
- Could you discuss the societal impact of using LLMs for feasibility prediction, particularly in terms of potential biases and limitations of these models?
- How does the computational cost of using LLMs compare to other methods, and what are the practical implications of this cost for real-world applications?
- Could you provide a more detailed comparison with other state-of-the-art methods, including those using GCNs, to provide a more robust evaluation of the proposed method?
- How does the proposed method perform when applied to different LLMs, particularly those with different architectures or training methods?
