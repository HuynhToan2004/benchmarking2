 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel algorithm, β-VMBPO, which extends the Variational Model-Based Policy Optimization (VMBPO) algorithm by incorporating a risk parameter, β. This modification aims to address the risk-seeking behavior of VMBPO by adjusting the dynamics of the environment to be more realistic. The algorithm is theoretically justified with proofs of convergence and optimality, and empirical results are provided to demonstrate its effectiveness in both tabular and continuous control environments. The authors have also made the code available, enhancing the reproducibility of the results.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, clear, and easy to follow, with a comprehensive theoretical analysis that includes proofs of convergence and optimality.
- The proposed algorithm, β-VMBPO, is a novel extension of VMBPO that addresses the risk-sensitive nature of variational inference in reinforcement learning (RL).
- The algorithm is supported by experimental results that demonstrate its effectiveness in both tabular and continuous control environments, showing improvements over baseline methods.
- The paper provides a clear motivation for the problem addressed and the proposed solution, with a logical structure that makes it easy to understand.
- The authors have made the code available, enhancing the reproducibility of the results and facilitating further research.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed comparison with existing methods such as SAC and MnM, which could provide a more robust evaluation of the proposed algorithm.
- There is an absence of a discussion on the limitations of the proposed algorithm, which could help in understanding its applicability and potential drawbacks.
- The experimental results are limited to a few environments, and it would be beneficial to see how the algorithm performs in a more diverse set of environments.
- The paper could benefit from a more detailed discussion on the practical implications of the theoretical results, particularly how they guide the design of the algorithm.
- There are some minor issues with the presentation, such as typographical errors and unclear figures, which could be improved for better clarity and professionalism.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors provide a more detailed comparison with SAC and MnM to clarify the relative performance of β-VMBPO in different scenarios?
- How does the performance of β-VMBPO depend on the choice of the hyperparameter β, and what are the implications of different values of β on the learning process?
- Can the authors discuss the practical implications of the theoretical results, particularly how they guide the design of the algorithm in different environments?
- How does the proposed algorithm handle stochastic environments, and what are the implications of using different variational families in the model?
- Could the authors provide a more detailed discussion on the limitations of the proposed algorithm, and how these limitations might affect its applicability in different scenarios?
