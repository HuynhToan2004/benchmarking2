 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel hypergraph dynamic system (HDS) that integrates hypergraphs with dynamic systems, aiming to improve the controllability and stability of hypergraph neural networks (HGNNs). HDS utilizes an ordinary differential equation (ODE) to model the continuous dynamics of hypergraph representations, proposing a multi-layer framework HDS^ode for effective vertex representation. The paper presents extensive experiments across seven datasets, demonstrating the superiority of HDS^ode over existing methods. Despite its innovative approach, the paper faces criticism for its limited novelty, lack of theoretical depth, and inadequate empirical validation, particularly in terms of its scalability and generalization capabilities.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-organized and clearly written, with a comprehensive review of related work.
- The proposed HDS^ode achieves superior performance over state-of-the-art methods in semi-supervised vertex classification tasks, demonstrating its effectiveness.
- The integration of hypergraphs with dynamic systems is a novel approach, which enhances the understanding and modeling of high-order correlations.
- The paper includes extensive experiments on seven datasets, which validate the proposed method's effectiveness and showcase its ability to capture long-range correlations among vertices.
- The theoretical analysis of the proposed method is comprehensive, including stability analysis and a discussion on the relationship between HDS^ode and hypergraph neural networks.
- The paper is original in its application of ODE-based hypergraph dynamic systems to improve the controllability and stability of HGNNs.

**Weaknesses:**
- The novelty of the proposed method is limited as it primarily builds on existing models like GraphCON and HGNN, with minor modifications.
- The theoretical depth of the paper is insufficient, lacking a detailed discussion on the expressive power of hypergraphs and the generalization capability of HDS^ode.
- The paper does not adequately address the scalability of HDS^ode, especially in terms of computational complexity and memory requirements.
- There is a lack of empirical evidence to support the claim that HDS^ode can capture long-range correlations among vertices, and the experimental results are not sufficiently convincing.
- The paper could benefit from a more rigorous experimental validation, including a broader range of datasets and more detailed comparisons with state-of-the-art methods.
- The paper lacks a thorough discussion on the limitations of the proposed method and does not provide sufficient insights into how HDS^ode can be applied in various real-world scenarios.

**Questions:**
- Can you provide more detailed insights into how the hypergraph dynamic system (HDS) can be applied in various real-world scenarios, especially in areas where high-order correlations are critical?
- How does HDS^ode handle the issue of scalability, particularly in terms of computational complexity and memory requirements?
- Could you elaborate on the stability analysis of HDS^ode and provide more empirical evidence to support the claim that it can capture long-range correlations among vertices?
- How does the proposed method compare with other state-of-the-art methods in terms of expressive power and generalization capability?
- Can you provide a detailed comparison of the computational complexity and memory requirements of HDS^ode with other methods like GNNs?
- How does the proposed method perform in scenarios with varying hyperedge sizes and different types of hypergraph structures?
