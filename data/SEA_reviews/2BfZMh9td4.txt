 **Summary:**
The paper introduces Multi-Objective Direct Preference Optimization (MODPO), a novel method that extends Direct Preference Optimization (DPO) to handle multiple alignment objectives in language model (LM) training. MODPO integrates linear scalarization early in the reward modeling process, allowing for the optimization of multiple objectives without the need for extensive computational resources. This method is compared to Multi-Objective RLHF (MORLHF) and other baselines, showing that it matches or surpasses their performance while requiring fewer computational resources. The paper also discusses the method's theoretical guarantees and empirical results across various tasks, including safety alignment and long-form question answering.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written and easy to follow, making it accessible to a broad audience.
- The proposed method, MODPO, is innovative and addresses a significant challenge in the field of language model training by extending DPO to handle multiple objectives.
- MODPO demonstrates improved computational efficiency and stability compared to Multi-Objective RLHF (MORLHF), as shown through empirical results that outperform or match existing methods.
- The methodology is theoretically grounded, with theoretical guarantees that it can produce the same optimal solutions as MORLHF.
- The paper includes comprehensive experimental evaluations, demonstrating the effectiveness of the proposed method across various tasks and scenarios.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper's claims of computational efficiency and stability are not substantiated with a detailed comparison of wall-clock times or with a broader range of models, which could affect the generalizability of the results.
- The paper lacks a detailed discussion on the limitations of the proposed method, particularly concerning its applicability in scenarios where the true ground-truth reward is unknown.
- The experimental setup could be improved by including more diverse baselines and a broader range of models to evaluate the robustness of the proposed method.
- The paper could benefit from a more detailed discussion on the potential negative societal impacts of the proposed method, especially in terms of its application in real-world scenarios.
- There are some minor typographical and formatting errors that need correction.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors provide a more detailed comparison of wall-clock times for MODPO versus MORLHF to better understand the computational efficiency claims?
- How does MODPO handle scenarios where the true ground-truth reward is unknown, and what are the implications of this on the method's performance and applicability?
- Could the authors elaborate on the potential negative societal impacts of the proposed method, particularly in terms of its application in real-world scenarios?
- How does the proposed method compare to other recent RL-free LM alignment methods, such as those mentioned in the related work section?
- What are the specific advantages of MODPO over other RL-free LM alignment methods, and how does it compare in terms of computational efficiency and stability?
