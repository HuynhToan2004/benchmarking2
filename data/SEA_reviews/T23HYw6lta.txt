 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel training mechanism for backdoor attacks, termed FMN (Forget-Me-Not), which enhances the durability of backdoors against various fine-tuning techniques. The mechanism incorporates a cyclical learning rate and a clean-backdoor interleaved training process, which are shown to improve the robustness of backdoors against fine-tuning-based defenses. Extensive empirical experiments are conducted to validate the effectiveness of the proposed method, demonstrating its ability to maintain high attack success rates and stealthiness against a variety of backdoor defenses. However, the paper's scope is limited to specific backdoor types and datasets, and it lacks a comprehensive analysis of the mechanisms behind the proposed method's effectiveness.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, making it easy to follow and understand, with clear presentation of the proposed methods and their implementation.
- The motivation behind the research is well-articulated, highlighting the importance of understanding backdoor attacks and their mitigation strategies.
- The proposed FMN method is effective against a wide range of backdoor attacks and can be applied to various backdoor types, demonstrating its versatility and practical utility.
- The paper provides a comprehensive evaluation of the proposed method, including ablation studies and transfer learning experiments, which help in understanding the method's strengths and limitations.
- The experimental results are convincing, showing that the FMN method can maintain high attack success rates and stealthiness against a variety of backdoor defenses.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed analysis of the mechanisms behind the proposed method's effectiveness, particularly in terms of how the cyclical learning rate and clean-backdoor interleaved training contribute to the robustness of the backdoor.
- The scope of the study is limited to specific types of backdoor attacks and datasets, which may not generalize well to other types of backdoors or datasets.
- There is a lack of discussion on the limitations of the proposed method, including its potential impact on the performance of the model in terms of clean accuracy and its applicability in real-world scenarios.
- The paper does not provide a detailed comparison with other backdoor training methods, which could help in understanding the advantages and disadvantages of the proposed method.
- The paper lacks a discussion on the potential negative societal impacts of the proposed method, which could be a significant concern given the potential malicious use of backdoor attacks.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors provide a more detailed analysis of the mechanisms behind the proposed method's effectiveness, particularly how the cyclical learning rate and clean-backdoor interleaved training contribute to the robustness of the backdoor?
- How does the proposed method perform against different types of backdoor attacks, such as those based on data injection or architecture manipulation?
- Is there a possibility of extending the method to be applicable in real-world scenarios where the attacker does not have full access to the backdoor model training?
- How does the proposed method compare to other backdoor training methods in terms of effectiveness and efficiency?
- Could the authors discuss the potential negative societal impacts of the proposed method and propose mitigation strategies to address these concerns?
