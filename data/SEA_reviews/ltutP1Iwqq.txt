 **Summary:**
The paper explores the interaction of categorical and perceptual learning signals in a two-domain learning problem, using a dataset inspired by infant visual experiences. It proposes a new set of cluster-based metrics to quantify feature alignment across categories and domains. The authors systematically vary these learning signals to observe their impacts on feature alignment and overall learning outcomes. They demonstrate that the network learns to apply inconsistent labelings to unseen images and that CNNs learn relationships between corresponding classes from different datasets. However, the paper has been critiqued for its experimental design, which lacks a clear motivation and comparison with other datasets, and for not adequately addressing the influence of perceptual signals on performance.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, making it easy to follow, and the motivation behind the research is clear.
- The authors propose a new set of cluster-based metrics to quantify feature alignment across category and domains in a two-domain learning problem.
- The paper provides a detailed analysis of the impact of different learning signals on feature alignment and overall learning outcomes.
- The experiments are well-designed, and the results are clearly presented, supporting the claims made by the authors.
- The paper discusses the influence of perceptual signals on performance, which is an interesting and novel contribution to the field.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a clear motivation for the experimental design, and it is unclear why the specific datasets were chosen for the experiments.
- The paper does not adequately address the influence of perceptual signals on performance, and the results do not convincingly demonstrate that these signals have a significant impact.
- The experimental setup is not well-explained, and there is a lack of clarity regarding how the results were obtained and what they represent.
- The paper could benefit from a more detailed discussion of the limitations and potential negative societal impacts of the research.
- The paper does not sufficiently compare its findings with existing works in the field, which could help to establish the novelty and significance of the contributions.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors clarify the motivation behind the experimental design and why the specific datasets were chosen?
- How do the authors explain the discrepancy in performance between different settings, such as TB+IN-12+Class-Diverged and TB+IN-12+Class-aligned?
- In the context of the IN-12 dataset, how do the authors address the potential bias towards certain classes due to the dataset's construction?
- Could the authors provide more details on the methodology used for outlier removal and the selection of core points?
- How do the authors justify the use of the IN-12 dataset, especially given its potential biases and the lack of detailed analysis of these biases in the paper?
- Could the authors elaborate on the potential negative societal impacts of their research and how these were addressed or mitigated?
