 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel approach to likelihood training in diffusion models using hierarchical volume-preserving maps, which facilitate the computation of likelihood functions across multiple scales. This methodology, applied to both Laplacian pyramids and wavelet transforms, allows for the efficient evaluation of likelihoods in a multi-scale context, enhancing the model's capacity for out-of-distribution detection and lossless compression. Theoretical insights are provided on the relationship between the proposed method and the Earth Mover's Distance, and empirical results demonstrate improvements over state-of-the-art methods.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel approach to likelihood training in diffusion models using hierarchical volume-preserving maps, which is a significant contribution to the field.
- Theoretical insights provided on the relationship between the proposed method and the Earth Mover's Distance are both interesting and valuable.
- The paper is well-written, with clear and precise definitions, and the authors effectively illustrate the theoretical aspects with practical examples.
- The proposed method is evaluated on various tasks, demonstrating its effectiveness and potential for future research.
- The paper is original in its approach to likelihood training in diffusion models and provides a clear and concise explanation of the methodology.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed comparison with existing methods, particularly in terms of computational efficiency and the quality of generated samples.
- There is a lack of clarity in the motivation behind the use of hierarchical volume-preserving maps, and the theoretical connection between the proposed method and the Earth Mover's Distance is not sufficiently explained.
- The paper does not adequately address the scalability of the proposed method, particularly in terms of the number of scales and the computational complexity.
- The experimental results do not convincingly demonstrate the superiority of the proposed method over existing methods, and there is a lack of comprehensive evaluation across different resolutions and datasets.
- The paper could benefit from a more detailed discussion on the limitations of the proposed method and a more thorough comparison with other likelihood-based models.

**Questions:**
- Could you clarify the definition of the optimal transport cost in the context of the proposed method?
- How does the proposed method compare to other likelihood-based models, particularly in terms of computational efficiency and sample quality?
- Is there a possibility to extend the method to other multiscale models, such as those based on wavelet transforms or other types of hierarchical volume-preserving maps?
- Could you provide more details on the computational complexity and the scalability of the proposed method with respect to the number of scales?
- How does the proposed method handle the computational challenges associated with likelihood training in diffusion models, particularly in high-dimensional settings?
- Could you elaborate on the choice of the Earth Mover's Distance as a metric for evaluating the performance of the model?
- How does the proposed method compare to other existing methods in terms of out-of-distribution detection and lossless compression?
