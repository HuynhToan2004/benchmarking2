 **Summary:**
The paper introduces FALCUN, a novel active learning method that utilizes a low-dimensional probability space to enhance efficiency in label selection. This approach contrasts traditional methods that rely on high-dimensional latent spaces, which can be computationally intensive. FALCUN aims to balance uncertainty and diversity in query selection, which is crucial for efficient label acquisition. The method's effectiveness is demonstrated through experiments on various datasets, showing improvements in efficiency and label selection quality. However, concerns about the novelty and the method's reliance on specific dataset characteristics remain.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written and easy to follow, making it accessible to a broad audience.
- The proposed method is simple yet effective, as shown by its performance in the experiments.
- The method's design is straightforward and easy to understand, which is beneficial for practical implementation.
- The paper provides a detailed analysis of the proposed method, including its scalability and performance across various datasets and settings.
- The method's focus on low-dimensional representations for active learning is innovative and has the potential to reduce computational costs.
- The experimental results demonstrate the method's effectiveness in terms of both efficiency and label selection quality, which is crucial for practical applications.

**Weaknesses:**
- The novelty of the method is questioned as it appears to be a combination of existing ideas without significant innovation.
- The method's reliance on the specific characteristics of datasets, such as the use of margin uncertainty for diversity estimation, may limit its applicability to other datasets.
- The paper lacks a detailed discussion on the limitations of the proposed method, which could help in understanding its scope and applicability.
- The experimental results do not convincingly demonstrate the superiority of the proposed method over existing methods, as shown in Figure 1 and Figure 4.
- The paper does not provide a thorough analysis of the computational costs of the proposed method, which is crucial for understanding its practical applicability.
- The method's focus on low-dimensional representations may not be effective for datasets with high-dimensional features, which could limit its applicability.
- The paper could benefit from a more comprehensive literature review and a clearer explanation of how the method addresses the challenges of active learning, particularly in terms of efficiency and label selection quality.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors clarify how the proposed method addresses the challenges of active learning, particularly in terms of efficiency and label selection quality?
- How does the method perform on datasets with high-dimensional features, and what are the implications for its applicability in such scenarios?
- Could the authors provide a more detailed analysis of the computational costs of the proposed method, including both time and space complexity?
- How does the method perform when compared to state-of-the-art methods in terms of efficiency and label selection quality?
- Can the authors discuss the limitations of the proposed method and how these might affect its practical applicability?
- How does the method handle scenarios where the dataset does not naturally exhibit the characteristics required for effective diversity estimation using margin uncertainty?
