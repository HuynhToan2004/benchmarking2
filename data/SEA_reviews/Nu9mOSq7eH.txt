 **Summary:**
The paper introduces InstructCV, a novel framework that leverages text-to-image diffusion models to perform various computer vision tasks guided by natural language instructions. It utilizes a large language model (LLM) to generate paraphrased prompts for vision tasks, which are then used to train the model on a diverse set of datasets. The approach is evaluated on tasks such as segmentation, object detection, depth estimation, and classification, demonstrating competitive performance against both task-specific and generalist models. The framework's ability to handle diverse prompts and its potential for generalization across different datasets and tasks are highlighted. However, concerns about the model's performance on complex tasks and its reliance on specific prompts for effectiveness are noted.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written and easy to follow, with clear presentation of the methodology and results.
- The proposed framework, InstructCV, is innovative in its application of text-to-image diffusion models for computer vision tasks, which is a novel approach compared to traditional methods.
- The method demonstrates competitive performance against both task-specific and generalist models, showcasing its effectiveness in handling diverse tasks.
- The use of a large language model (LLM) to generate paraphrased prompts for vision tasks is a significant contribution, enhancing the model's ability to handle diverse and complex instructions.
- The paper provides a comprehensive evaluation across multiple datasets and tasks, including comparisons with state-of-the-art models, which validates the effectiveness of the proposed method.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks detailed analysis on how the model handles complex tasks, especially in scenarios where the instructions are ambiguous or conflicting.
- The performance of the model on classification tasks is relatively weak compared to other models, which might limit its applicability in certain scenarios.
- The model's performance on external datasets, particularly those with different categories, is not thoroughly discussed, which could affect the generalizability of the results.
- The paper does not provide a detailed comparison with other generalist models like Unified-IO and Pix2SeqV2, which could have helped in better positioning the proposed method.
- There is a lack of discussion on the limitations of the model, particularly in terms of its reliance on specific prompts for effectiveness and its inability to handle complex tasks.

**Questions:**
- How does the model handle complex tasks, especially when the instructions are ambiguous or conflicting?
- Can you provide more details on the performance of the model on external datasets, particularly those with different categories?
- How does the model compare with other generalist models like Unified-IO and Pix2SeqV2 in terms of performance and generalization capabilities?
- Could you elaborate on the choice of using a diffusion model for this task and how it compares with other models like transformers in terms of performance and efficiency?
- How does the model handle the potential for hallucination in the generated images, especially in tasks like segmentation and object detection?
- Could you provide more details on the training process, particularly the use of different prompts and their impact on the model's performance?
