 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel approach to generalization error bounds using sliced mutual information, focusing on the generalization performance of neural networks trained on random subspaces. It provides theoretical bounds on the generalization error using the mutual information between the learned weights and the training data, assuming the loss function is sub-Gaussian. The paper also discusses the practical implications of these bounds, including the trade-offs between generalization error and training error, and the use of rate-distortion bounds to improve generalization performance. Experimental results are presented to validate the theoretical findings, demonstrating the effectiveness of the proposed method in real-world scenarios. The paper is well-written and offers a clear exposition of the theoretical results, although it lacks a thorough discussion on the practical implications and potential limitations of the proposed method.
