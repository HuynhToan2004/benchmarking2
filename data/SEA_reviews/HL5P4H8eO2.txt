 **Summary:**
The paper introduces DiffTOP, a novel policy class that leverages differentiable trajectory optimization for both model-based reinforcement learning and imitation learning. It utilizes the Theseus library for differentiable nonlinear least squares optimization to optimize the policy parameters, enabling backpropagation through the trajectory optimization process. This allows for the learning of dynamics and cost functions that directly optimize task performance. The method was evaluated across 15 model-based RL tasks and 13 imitation learning tasks, demonstrating significant improvements over state-of-the-art methods in both domains. The paper also includes extensive ablation studies and analysis to understand the method's effectiveness.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel approach to learning the policy using differentiable trajectory optimization, which is a significant advancement in the field of reinforcement learning and imitation learning.
- The method is well-motivated and well-written, with clear explanations of the experimental results and a clear structure that makes it easy to follow.
- The paper presents a new policy class, DiffTOP, which is well-motivated and shows significant improvements over state-of-the-art methods in both model-based reinforcement learning and imitation learning.
- The method is well-evaluated, with extensive experiments conducted on 15 model-based RL tasks and 13 imitation learning tasks, demonstrating its effectiveness and potential for broader applications.
- The paper includes ablation studies and analysis, providing insights into the method's effectiveness and addressing potential concerns about the method's contributions.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper's claim of being the first to apply differentiable trajectory optimization to model-based reinforcement learning is not entirely accurate, as similar approaches have been previously explored.
- The method's reliance on a specific trajectory optimization algorithm (Theseus) might limit its applicability to other optimization methods.
- The paper could benefit from a more detailed discussion on the limitations of the proposed method, including potential issues with scalability and generalization to more complex tasks.
- The paper's presentation could be improved by providing more intuitive explanations of the experimental results and a clearer explanation of how the method addresses the objective mismatch issue in model-based RL.
- There are some minor issues with the paper's presentation, such as inconsistencies in citation styles and unclear descriptions in figures.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors clarify the method's contribution in terms of novelty, particularly regarding the application of differentiable trajectory optimization to model-based RL?
- How does the method perform when using different trajectory optimization algorithms?
- Can the authors provide more details on how the method addresses the objective mismatch issue in model-based RL?
- Is there a possibility to include a comparison with other recent model-based RL algorithms, such as those using differentiable planning?
- How does the method handle the scalability and generalization issues, particularly in more complex tasks?
- Can the authors provide more intuitive explanations of the experimental results, particularly in terms of how the method performs in different scenarios?
