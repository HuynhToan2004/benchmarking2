 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel Transformer-Modulated Diffusion Model (TMDM) that integrates transformer-based forecasting models with diffusion models to enhance probabilistic multivariate time series forecasting. The TMDM leverages transformer-based models to extract insights from historical data, which are then used to guide the diffusion process. This integration allows for more accurate and precise distribution forecasting, addressing the limitations of traditional models that focus solely on point estimation. The paper also introduces new metrics for evaluating uncertainty estimation performance, which are crucial for decision-making in real-world applications. Extensive experiments demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed model, showcasing its potential to outperform existing methods.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel framework, TMDM, which integrates transformer-based forecasting models with diffusion models, providing a new perspective on probabilistic multivariate time series forecasting.
- The methodology is clearly presented, making it easy to follow and understand, and the paper is well-written, enhancing its readability and comprehensibility.
- The paper proposes new metrics for evaluating uncertainty estimation performance, which are crucial for decision-making in real-world applications.
- The experimental results demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed method, showing improvements in various metrics over existing methods.
- The paper is original in its approach, combining transformer-based forecasting models with diffusion models, which is a novel application in the field of time series forecasting.
- The authors have conducted extensive experiments to evaluate the performance of the proposed method, demonstrating its effectiveness and robustness.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed discussion on the advantages and disadvantages of using the TMDM over existing methods, particularly in terms of computational efficiency and scalability.
- There is a need for more clarity in the experimental setup and results, particularly in the presentation of results and the explanation of the metrics used.
- The paper does not sufficiently address the limitations of the proposed method, which could include potential biases in the forecasting results and the impact of different transformer-based models on the performance.
- The paper does not provide a clear explanation of the computational complexity of the TMDM, which is crucial for understanding its scalability and applicability in different scenarios.
- The paper could benefit from a more detailed discussion on the theoretical underpinnings of the TMDM, particularly in how it integrates transformer-based forecasting models with diffusion models.
- The paper does not sufficiently compare the proposed method with other state-of-the-art methods, which could help in better understanding its position in the current research landscape.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors provide a detailed comparison of the computational efficiency and scalability of the TMDM with existing methods?
- How does the choice of transformer-based models affect the performance of the TMDM, and what are the implications of using different models?
- Could the authors provide more detailed explanations of the experimental setup and results, particularly in how the metrics are computed and interpreted?
- What are the potential biases or limitations in the forecasting results produced by the TMDM, and how do these affect the reliability of the predictions?
- How does the TMDM handle different types of time series data, and what are the implications of using different types of data for training and testing the model?
- Can the authors provide a more detailed theoretical discussion on how the TMDM integrates transformer-based forecasting models with diffusion models, and what are the implications of this integration for the model's performance and applicability?
