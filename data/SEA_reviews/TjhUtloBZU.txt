 **Summary:**
The paper investigates the impact of label noise on pre-trained models and proposes a method to mitigate the negative effects of noise on both in-domain and out-of-domain tasks. It introduces a lightweight black-box tuning method, NMTune, which aims to adjust the feature space to reduce the malignant effects of noise. The paper presents extensive experiments using synthetic noisy ImageNet-1K and YFCC15M datasets, demonstrating that slight noise in pre-training can benefit in-domain transfer performance but deteriorate out-of-domain performance. It also proposes a method to adjust the feature space to mitigate the malignant effects of noise, which can improve generalization on both ID and OOD tasks.

**Strengths:**
- The paper addresses a significant and timely issue in the field of deep learning, focusing on the impact of label noise on pre-trained models and proposing a method to mitigate these effects.
- The experimental setup is well-designed, with a focus on both in-domain and out-of-domain tasks, providing a comprehensive evaluation of the proposed method.
- The paper is well-written, clear, and easy to follow, making it accessible to a broad audience.
- The methodology is novel and innovative, proposing a lightweight black-box tuning method that is adaptable to various pre-trained models and can be applied in both vision and language domains.
- The paper is well-organized, with clear figures and tables that aid in understanding the results and methodologies.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper could benefit from a more thorough analysis of the noise in pre-training datasets, particularly in terms of its impact on model performance and the effectiveness of the proposed mitigation methods.
- The discussion on the limitations of the proposed method is insufficient, which is crucial for understanding the scope and applicability of the findings.
- The paper could benefit from a broader range of experiments, including different types and levels of noise, and different pre-trained models to validate the generalizability of the findings.
- The paper could provide more details on the implementation and hyperparameters of the proposed method to ensure reproducibility and to clarify the experimental settings.
- The paper lacks a comprehensive discussion on the related work, particularly in the area of noisy label learning, which could help contextualize the contributions of the proposed method.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors provide more details on the noise in the pre-training datasets, such as the types and levels of noise and their impact on model performance?
- How does the proposed method perform when applied to different types of noise, such as label switching, label missing, and label mis-annotating?
- Can the authors provide more insights into the selection of hyperparameters and their impact on the performance of the proposed method?
- How does the proposed method compare to other methods that address similar issues, such as data augmentation and other parameter-efficient fine-tuning methods?
- Could the authors provide more details on the implementation of the proposed method, including the training and inference costs, to better understand the practical implications of the findings?
- How does the proposed method perform when applied to different pre-trained models, and what are the implications for the generalizability of the findings?
