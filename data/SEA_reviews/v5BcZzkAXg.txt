 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel approach to multi-label learning by replacing the output layer of deep neural networks with a circular vector representation, termed CHRR. This approach aims to reduce the size of the output layer while maintaining performance, as demonstrated through experiments on both synthetic and real-world datasets. The methodology involves converting the output of the neural network into a circular vector, which is then used to predict labels. This approach is compared against traditional methods like HRR and FC, showing potential improvements in computational efficiency and label encoding capacity. However, concerns are raised about the novelty of the approach, the clarity of the presentation, and the lack of comprehensive experiments across a broader range of datasets.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel approach by replacing the output layer of a deep neural network with a circular vector, which is a significant departure from traditional methods.
- The methodology is supported by theoretical and experimental evidence, demonstrating the effectiveness of the circular vector representation in multi-label classification tasks.
- The paper is well-written, making it accessible and easy to follow, which is crucial for readers to understand the proposed methodology.
- The experiments conducted show that the proposed method, CHRR, outperforms other methods like HRR and FC, indicating its potential effectiveness in practical applications.
- The paper provides a clear and detailed explanation of the experimental setup and results, which is helpful for readers to understand the implications of the findings.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a clear explanation of why the circular vector representation is superior to other methods, such as real-valued vectors. This is crucial for understanding the theoretical underpinnings of the proposed method.
- The experimental results are not convincingly superior to existing methods, particularly on larger datasets like Wiki10-31K and Delicious-200K. The performance improvements are marginal, and the reasons for these improvements are not adequately explained.
- The paper does not compare the proposed method with other state-of-the-art methods in the field, which limits the understanding of its relative effectiveness.
- There are several typographical and formatting errors throughout the paper, which detract from its professional presentation.
- The paper does not discuss the limitations of the proposed method, which is essential for a complete understanding of its applicability and potential drawbacks.
- The experimental setup and results are not clearly described, which makes it difficult for readers to replicate the experiments or understand the methodology fully.

**Questions:**
- Can you clarify the advantages of using a circular vector representation over other types of vectors, such as real-valued vectors?
- How does the proposed method compare to other state-of-the-art methods in terms of computational efficiency and accuracy?
- Could you provide more details on the experimental setup, such as the specifics of the datasets used and the experimental conditions?
- What are the limitations of the proposed method, and under what conditions might it not be effective?
- How does the proposed method perform on other types of datasets, such as image or audio datasets, which are not typically used in multi-label classification tasks?
- Can you discuss the potential impact of the proposed method on the broader field of machine learning, particularly in terms of its applicability and scalability?
