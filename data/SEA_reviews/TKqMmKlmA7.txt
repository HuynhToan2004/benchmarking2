 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel framework called Spectral Transformation (ST) aimed at modulating the spectrum of embeddings in self-supervised learning (SSL) to prevent dimensional collapse. The framework includes a new method called IterNorm with trace loss (INTL), which theoretically ensures equal-eigenvalue distributions during optimization. The authors empirically demonstrate that INTL outperforms other SSL methods in various datasets, including ImageNet, CIFAR-10, and COCO, and shows potential in transfer learning. The paper also explores the use of power functions and Newton's iterations to modify the spectrum of embeddings, providing a theoretical analysis and experimental validation.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, with clear and well-organized sections, making it easy to follow.
- The authors introduce a novel framework called Spectral Transformation (ST), which provides a new perspective on the whitening loss in SSL.
- The paper provides a theoretical analysis of the proposed method, which is supported by comprehensive experiments on various datasets, including CIFAR-10, CIFAR-100, ImageNet, and COCO.
- The proposed method, IterNorm with trace loss (INTL), is empirically shown to be effective in improving the performance of SSL models, particularly in terms of dimensional collapse prevention.
- The paper includes a detailed appendix that provides additional proofs, algorithms, and experiments, enhancing the reproducibility and comprehensiveness of the study.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed discussion on the limitations of the proposed method, particularly in scenarios where the method might not perform as well or could lead to unexpected results.
- There is a lack of clarity in the presentation of experimental results, especially in tables and figures, which could benefit from better labeling and organization.
- The paper does not adequately compare the proposed method with existing SSL methods, particularly in terms of computational overhead and practical implementation details.
- The paper does not provide a detailed discussion on the hyperparameter selection and its impact on the performance of the proposed method.
- The paper does not include a discussion on the potential societal impacts of the proposed method.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors provide more details on the computational overhead and practical implementation of the proposed method compared to other SSL methods?
- How does the proposed method perform in scenarios where the batch size is larger or smaller than those used in the experiments?
- Could the authors elaborate on the choice of hyperparameters, particularly the value of beta in the trace loss, and its impact on the performance of the method?
- How does the proposed method compare to other SSL methods in terms of computational efficiency and practical implementation details?
- Can the authors provide a more detailed discussion on the limitations of the proposed method and potential scenarios where it might not perform as well?
- How does the proposed method perform in terms of transfer learning to downstream tasks, and what are the potential applications of this method in real-world scenarios?
