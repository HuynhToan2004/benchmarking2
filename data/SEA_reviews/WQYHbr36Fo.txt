 **Summary:**
The paper addresses the issue of feature coupling in dense self-supervised learning (SSL) by introducing a novel augmentation method called Region Collaborative Cutout (RCC). This method is designed to decouple features from contextual information, thereby improving the learning of local visual semantics. The authors propose a de-coupling strategy that includes a decoupling branch and a loss function to enhance the robustness of feature learning. Extensive experiments are conducted to demonstrate the effectiveness of RCC across various SSL frameworks, including both CNN and ViT-based methods. The paper is well-received for its clarity, methodological soundness, and significant impact on the field.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, with clear and concise descriptions of the proposed method.
- The proposed method is simple yet effective, and the experiments are convincing, demonstrating significant improvements in performance.
- The authors provide a detailed ablation study, which helps in understanding the impact of different components of the proposed method.
- The paper includes a comprehensive evaluation across various SSL frameworks, including CNN-based and ViT-based methods, which shows the robustness of the proposed method.
- The introduction of a decoupling branch and loss function is a novel approach to addressing the feature coupling problem in SSL.
- The paper provides a thorough evaluation of the proposed method, including comparisons with existing methods and extensive ablation studies.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed discussion on the limitations of the proposed method, which could help in understanding its applicability and potential drawbacks.
- There is a need for more comparative analysis with other recent works, such as those using similar techniques like masked image modeling (MIM) or other data augmentation methods.
- The paper does not sufficiently discuss the computational cost and training time associated with the proposed method, which could be a significant concern for practical applications.
- The paper could benefit from a more detailed discussion on how the proposed method performs with different backbone architectures and datasets.
- The paper's methodology and experimental setup could be more transparent, particularly in the generation of de-coupled views and the selection of background images.
- There is a lack of discussion on how the proposed method performs with different types of data, such as images with multiple objects or images with complex backgrounds.

**Questions:**
- Could you clarify the selection process for the background image X^B in the de-coupled view generation? Is it sampled from a pre-defined set or randomly selected from the training dataset?
- How does the proposed method perform with different backbone architectures, such as ViT-B/16 or ViT-B/32?
- Is the proposed method applicable to other types of data, such as images with multiple objects or images with complex backgrounds?
- Could you provide more details on the computational cost and training time associated with the proposed method?
- How does the proposed method compare with other recent works that use similar techniques, such as masked image modeling (MIM) or other data augmentation methods?
- Can you elaborate on the impact of the de-coupling branch and loss function on the performance of the SSL model?
- How does the proposed method handle the issue of feature coupling in SSL, particularly in cases where the feature coupling is more pronounced?
