 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel adversarial attack method using diffusion models to generate imperceptible and transferable adversarial perturbations in the latent space. This approach aims to enhance the transferability of adversarial examples across different models, including those with adversarial defenses. The methodology involves leveraging the diffusion model's inherent recognition capabilities to craft adversarial examples that are not only visually imperceptible but also effective in transferring to black-box models. The paper presents extensive experiments on various model architectures and datasets, demonstrating the effectiveness of the proposed method over existing techniques. However, concerns were raised regarding the clarity of the paper's presentation, the need for more detailed comparisons with existing methods, and the potential computational costs and limitations of the proposed method.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel approach using diffusion models to craft adversarial perturbations in the latent space, which enhances the transferability of adversarial examples to black-box models.
- The methodology is well-explained, and the paper is well-written, making it easy to follow and understand.
- Extensive experiments are conducted on various model architectures, datasets, and defense methods, demonstrating the effectiveness of the proposed method.
- The paper provides a comprehensive analysis of the strengths and limitations of the proposed method, including a detailed discussion on the potential future directions of diffusion-based adversarial attacks.
- The paper is well-organized, with clear figures and detailed descriptions of the experimental setup, which facilitates reproducibility and understanding of the results.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed discussion on how the diffusion model's latent space is utilized for adversarial attacks, which could be clarified with more detailed explanations or additional figures.
- There is a need for more comparative analysis with existing methods, particularly in terms of computational cost and transferability across different models.
- The paper could benefit from a more thorough discussion on the limitations of the proposed method, including its applicability to different types of adversarial defenses and its potential vulnerability to specific types of attacks.
- Some figures and tables in the paper are difficult to read, which could be improved by using clearer fonts and better organization.
- The paper could provide more detailed examples and visualizations to better illustrate the effectiveness of the proposed method.
