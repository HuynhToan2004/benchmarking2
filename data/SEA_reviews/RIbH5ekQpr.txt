 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a new dataset, IMP, designed to challenge vision-language models (VLMs) by presenting images with diverse captions, ranging from descriptive to conceptual. The dataset is intended to test the ability of VLMs to handle image polysemy, a phenomenon where a single image can convey multiple different meanings. The authors conducted experiments using existing models to evaluate their performance on this dataset, highlighting the challenges in recognizing the multiple semantic dimensions of images. Despite the novel approach and the dataset's potential utility, the paper faces criticism for its limited scope, lack of technical novelty, and unclear methodology, particularly in the data selection and annotation processes.

**Strengths:**
- The paper addresses an important and interesting problem, specifically the recognition of multiple semantic dimensions of images, which is crucial for advancing the understanding and application of vision-language models (VLMs).
- The authors introduce a novel dataset, IMP, which challenges existing models by presenting images with diverse captions that range from descriptive to conceptual. This dataset is well-designed and provides a new benchmark for evaluating VLMs, which is a significant contribution to the field.
- The paper is well-written, making it easy to follow, and includes comprehensive evaluations and analyses that are insightful and well-presented.
- The dataset and code are promised to be made publicly available, which is a valuable resource for the research community.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks clarity in its methodology, particularly in the data selection and annotation processes. It is unclear how the images were selected and how the captions were annotated, which raises concerns about the dataset's quality and the validity of the results.
- The technical novelty of the paper is limited, as the dataset and the proposed benchmark are not sufficiently differentiated from existing methods. The use of the Google Vision API for data collection is not sufficiently explained or justified.
- The experimental results are not convincing, with marginal performance improvements observed after fine-tuning and no significant differences in performance across different models.
- The paper does not adequately address the limitations of its approach, particularly the potential for the dataset to be biased towards certain types of images or captions, which could affect the generalizability of the findings.
- The paper suffers from several presentation issues, such as small font sizes in figures and tables, which make the content difficult to read.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors provide more details on the data selection and annotation processes, including how the images were selected and how the captions were annotated?
- How do the authors ensure the quality and diversity of the captions in the dataset?
- What are the specific challenges posed by the IMP dataset that are not addressed by other datasets like MSCOCO or Flickr30K?
- How do the authors address the potential biases in the dataset, such as the prevalence of certain types of captions or images?
- Can the authors clarify the technical contributions of their work, particularly in relation to existing methods and datasets?
- How do the authors plan to address the limitations of their approach, such as the potential for biased or noisy data, in future research?
