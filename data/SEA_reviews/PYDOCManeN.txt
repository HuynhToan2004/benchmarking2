 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel approach for generating periodic structures of materials using a denoising diffusion model in the representation space, specifically utilizing Embedded Atom Density (EAD) descriptors. This method aims to preserve periodicity and invariances of the structure space, enhancing the performance of generative models. The approach is evaluated on various datasets and shown to outperform existing methods in terms of reconstruction and generation metrics. However, concerns were raised about the clarity of the methodology, particularly in the presentation of the reconstruction algorithm and the choice of baselines. Additionally, the paper lacks a comprehensive discussion on the limitations and societal impacts of the proposed method.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel approach for generating periodic structures of materials, utilizing a denoising diffusion model in the representation space, which is a significant advancement in the field.
- The methodology is well-presented, and the paper is generally well-written, making it easy to follow.
- The authors have provided a detailed description of the proposed method, including a comprehensive evaluation of the proposed method on various datasets, demonstrating the effectiveness of the proposed method.
- The paper is the first to use periodic materials generation with diffusion models in the representation space, which is a novel application of this technology.
- The approach is shown to be effective in generating materials in the representation space, which is a significant contribution to the field.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a comprehensive discussion on the limitations and potential societal impacts of the proposed method.
- The presentation of the reconstruction algorithm is unclear, and the choice of baselines seems limited and outdated, which could affect the perceived effectiveness of the proposed method.
- The paper does not provide sufficient details on the computational cost and efficiency of the proposed method, which is crucial for practical applications.
- The paper does not discuss the limitations of the proposed method, such as the potential for overfitting or the scalability of the method to larger or more complex datasets.
- The paper does not compare the proposed method with the latest state-of-the-art methods, which could limit the understanding of its relative performance.
- The paper lacks a detailed discussion on the physical properties of the generated materials and how they relate to the material properties in real-world applications.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors provide a more detailed explanation of the reconstruction algorithm, especially how it is used to recover the Cartesian positions of the crystal structure?
- How does the proposed method compare to the latest state-of-the-art methods, and what are the specific advantages of using the denoising diffusion model in the representation space?
- Could the authors discuss the potential limitations of the proposed method, such as scalability and the risk of overfitting, and how these limitations could be addressed?
- What are the specific advantages of using the denoising diffusion model in the representation space over other methods, and how does it compare in terms of computational cost and efficiency?
- How do the generated materials compare in terms of their physical properties to real-world materials, and what are the implications of these differences for practical applications?
- Could the authors provide a more detailed discussion on the limitations of the proposed method, including potential negative societal impacts and ethical considerations?
