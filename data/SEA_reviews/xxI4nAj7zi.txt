 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel method termed Invariant-content Feature Reconstruction (IFR) for cross-domain few-shot classification (CFC), aiming to enhance the generalization performance on unseen domains by focusing on invariant-content features. The method leverages content-preserving augmentations to reconstruct invariant-content features, which are then combined with high-level semantic features for classification. Extensive experiments on the Meta-Dataset benchmark demonstrate the effectiveness of IFR in improving generalization performance on unseen domains. However, concerns about the clarity of presentation, the theoretical justification of the method, and its novelty compared to existing works like URL are highlighted.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, making it easy to follow, and the methodology is clearly explained.
- The proposed method, IFR, is simple yet effective, and the experimental results demonstrate its superiority over existing methods like URL.
- The paper provides a clear description of the method, including a detailed explanation of the IFR pipeline and the use of augmented data for feature reconstruction.
- The motivation behind the work is well-articulated, and the experimental results are convincing, showing the effectiveness of the method.
- The paper is well-organized, with a clear structure that facilitates understanding of the proposed method and its implementation.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a comprehensive discussion of related works, particularly the similarities and differences with existing methods like URL.
- There is a lack of clarity in the presentation of the method, with some figures and explanations being unclear or misleading.
- The paper does not adequately address the issue of high-level semantic features being too general to cover key content features, which is a critical aspect of the study.
- The novelty of the proposed method is questionable, as it bears strong similarities to existing methods like URL, and the improvements over these methods are not convincingly demonstrated.
- The paper lacks a thorough theoretical analysis and justification for the use of attention-based feature reconstruction, which is a crucial aspect of the method.
- The paper could benefit from a more detailed discussion of the limitations and potential negative societal impacts of the proposed method.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors clarify the differences between the proposed IFR and existing methods like URL, especially in terms of the use of attention-based feature reconstruction and the handling of invariant-content features?
- How does the proposed method address the issue of high-level semantic features being too general to cover key content features?
- Could the authors provide a more detailed explanation of the feature reconstruction process, particularly how it contributes to the overall performance of the method?
- What are the specific advantages of the proposed method over existing methods in terms of computational efficiency and practical application?
- How does the method handle the potential for overfitting to the augmented data, which could limit its generalization ability?
- Can the authors provide a more detailed theoretical analysis or justification for the use of attention-based feature reconstruction in the context of invariant-content feature reconstruction?
