 **Summary:**
The paper explores the challenges in offline reinforcement learning (RL) by focusing on the Distribution Correction Estimation (DICE) methods, which are known for their inability to match the performance of methods that solely use action-level behavior constraints. The authors propose a novel approach by projecting the backward gradient onto the normal plane of the forward gradient, which they term as orthogonal-gradient update. This method is aimed at improving the performance of DICE-based methods by addressing the conflicting directions of gradient terms. The paper provides theoretical analysis and empirical evidence, including extensive experiments and toy examples, to support the effectiveness of the proposed method.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, clear, and easy to follow, with a clear motivation and a well-defined problem.
- The authors provide a thorough analysis of the gradient term in the DICE-based method, revealing the mystery of its performance.
- The proposed method, O-DICE, is simple, intuitive, and supported by both theoretical analysis and empirical results.
- The paper includes a toy example that effectively demonstrates the differences between semi-gradient update and orthogonal-gradient update.
- Extensive experiments and theoretical analysis are conducted, showing the effectiveness of the proposed method.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed discussion on the limitations of the proposed method, particularly concerning its applicability in stochastic environments and its performance compared to other methods like EQL.
- The paper does not sufficiently discuss the computational cost of the proposed method, which could be a significant concern in practical applications.
- The paper's claims about the superiority of the proposed method over other DICE-based methods are not convincingly supported by the results presented.
- There is a lack of discussion on the impact of the backward gradient on the performance of DICE-based methods, which is crucial for understanding the effectiveness of the proposed method.
- The paper does not adequately address the computational complexity introduced by the orthogonal-gradient update, which could limit its practical applicability.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors clarify the discrepancy in performance between O-DICE and EQL, especially in the context of the backward gradient's impact on performance?
- How does the proposed method handle stochastic environments, and what are the implications of this for its applicability?
- Can the authors provide a more detailed comparison of the computational cost and complexity of O-DICE with other methods, particularly those that do not use orthogonal-gradient update?
- How does the proposed method perform in environments with varying levels of noise or stochasticity, and what are the implications of this for its practical application?
- Could the authors discuss the potential impact of the backward gradient on the performance of DICE-based methods, and how the proposed method addresses this issue?
