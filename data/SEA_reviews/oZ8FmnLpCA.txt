 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel knowledge distillation framework, Knowledge Distillation via Flow Matching (FM-KD), which leverages rectified flow to address the challenges of knowledge distillation. The methodology involves a meta-encoder, such as an MLP, that models the transport map between student and teacher feature distributions, and a metric-based distillation method. The paper proposes a new training objective that is theoretically equivalent to minimizing the upper bound of the negative log-likelihood of the teacher's output. The framework is evaluated on various datasets including CIFAR-100, ImageNet-1k, and MS-COCO, showing promising results. The authors also introduce a variant, FM-KD∏, which eliminates additional computational overhead during inference. Despite the innovative approach, the paper has been critiqued for its clarity in presentation, lack of detailed experimental setup, and potential over-reliance on existing techniques without sufficient novelty or significant performance improvements.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel approach to knowledge distillation using rectified flow, which is a significant departure from traditional methods.
- The proposed method is theoretically sound, with a clear demonstration of the equivalence of the training objective to minimizing the upper bound of the negative log-likelihood of the teacher's output.
- The paper is well-written, making it easy to follow, and includes a detailed theoretical analysis and a comprehensive experimental setup.
- The method shows promise in improving the accuracy of student models in various datasets, including CIFAR-100, ImageNet-1k, and MS-COCO.
- The paper is well-organized and includes a thorough discussion on related works, providing a comprehensive review of the field.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks clarity in explaining certain key concepts, such as the role and implementation of the meta-encoder in the distillation process.
- The paper does not sufficiently differentiate its contributions from existing methods, particularly in terms of novelty and performance improvements.
- There is a notable absence of detailed experimental setups, including specifics on the number of samples used and the computational resources required.
- The paper could benefit from more detailed comparisons with existing methods, especially in terms of computational efficiency and performance under different conditions.
- The paper's presentation could be improved by including more visual aids, such as graphs and diagrams, to better illustrate the results and methodologies.
- The paper's claims regarding the superiority of the proposed method over existing techniques are not convincingly supported by the experimental results.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors clarify the role and implementation details of the meta-encoder in the distillation process?
- How does the proposed method compare in terms of computational efficiency and performance with existing methods, particularly under different conditions?
- Could the authors provide more detailed experimental setups, including the number of samples used and the computational resources required?
- How does the proposed method perform in terms of generalization to different types of models and datasets?
- Can the authors elaborate on the theoretical underpinnings of the method, particularly how it achieves the claimed equivalence to minimizing the upper bound of the negative log-likelihood of the teacher's output?
- How does the proposed method handle the issue of ensuring that the student's features are derived from the same input as the teacher's features?
