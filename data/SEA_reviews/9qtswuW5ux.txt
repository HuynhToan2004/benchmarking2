 **Summary:**
The paper introduces QRF-GNN, a novel approach to solving combinatorial optimization problems using graph neural networks (GNNs) with a recurrent feature for unsupervised learning. The model leverages a QUBO relaxation to minimize the loss function, demonstrating superior performance on benchmark datasets for maximum cut and graph coloring problems. The authors claim that QRF-GNN outperforms existing GNN-based approaches and is competitive with state-of-the-art conventional heuristics. However, the paper lacks a detailed discussion on the computational complexity and scalability, and the experimental results are not convincingly superior to existing methods.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel architecture, QRF-GNN, which employs a recurrent feature that significantly enhances the performance of GNNs in solving combinatorial optimization problems.
- The method is well-explained, with clear pseudocode and a detailed description of the training process, making it easy to follow.
- The experiments are comprehensive and demonstrate the effectiveness of QRF-GNN in solving combinatorial optimization problems, showing superior performance compared to existing GNN-based approaches.
- The paper is well-written, with clear descriptions of the proposed method and experimental results, which are supported by a detailed discussion on the training process and the effectiveness of the proposed method.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed discussion on the computational complexity and scalability of QRF-GNN, which is crucial for understanding its practical applicability in large-scale problems.
- There is a notable absence of a detailed comparison with other state-of-the-art methods, particularly in terms of training time and solution quality, which could undermine the claims of superior performance.
- The experimental results are not convincingly superior to existing methods, and the paper does not adequately address the scalability of QRF-GNN to larger and more complex problems.
- The paper could benefit from a more thorough ablation study to better understand the individual contributions of each component of the proposed method.
- There are several minor typographical errors and inconsistencies in the paper, which could detract from its professional presentation.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors provide a detailed discussion on the computational complexity and scalability of QRF-GNN, particularly in comparison to other state-of-the-art methods?
- How does QRF-GNN compare in terms of training time and solution quality with other state-of-the-art methods, especially when applied to larger and more complex problems?
- Could the authors elaborate on the specific advantages of using QRF-GNN over other GNN-based approaches, especially in terms of performance and computational efficiency?
- Would it be possible to include a more detailed ablation study to better understand the individual contributions of each component of the proposed method?
- How does the proposed method handle the issue of over-smoothing, which is a common challenge in GNNs?
- Can the authors provide more details on the experimental setup, including the specific datasets used and the methodology for evaluating the results?
