 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel unsupervised multi-modal machine translation method that leverages images as pivots to align different languages, aiming to achieve source-to-target alignment in a shared semantic space. The proposed method uses cross-modal contrastive learning to achieve sentence-level and token-level alignment, which is claimed to effectively learn source-to-target alignment with monolingual data only. The approach is evaluated against state-of-the-art methods, showing significant improvements in BLEU and METEOR scores. However, the novelty of the method is questioned as it seems to be a combination of existing techniques without significant innovation. The paper is criticized for lacking clarity in the description of the method and the experimental setup, and for not providing sufficient comparisons with recent state-of-the-art methods.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, clear, and easy to follow, with a clear motivation and a well-designed method.
- The proposed method achieves significant improvements in BLEU and METEOR scores, outperforming previous state-of-the-art methods in unsupervised machine translation.
- The idea of using cross-modal contrastive learning to achieve sentence-level and token-level alignment is novel and interesting.
- The paper provides a comprehensive evaluation of the proposed method, including ablation studies, and shows good performance on the Multi30K dataset.
- The authors have made significant efforts to evaluate their method on multiple datasets and have conducted ablation studies to verify the effectiveness of each component.

**Weaknesses:**
- The novelty of the method is limited as it seems to be a combination of existing techniques without significant innovation.
- The paper lacks clarity in the description of the method, particularly in the initialization process and the use of images as pivots.
- The experimental setup is not clearly described, and the results are not compared with the most recent state-of-the-art methods.
- The paper does not provide sufficient details on the training and inference of the method, which makes it difficult to replicate the results.
- The paper could benefit from more comprehensive comparisons with other recent unsupervised machine translation methods, especially those that use similar approaches.
- The use of images as pivots is not clearly explained, and it is unclear how the proposed method differs from previous methods that also use images for alignment.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors provide more details on how the images are used as pivots in the proposed method?
- How does the proposed method differ from previous methods that also use images for alignment in unsupervised machine translation?
- Could the authors clarify the experimental setup and provide more details on the training and inference of the method?
- How does the proposed method perform on other datasets, such as those used in the Multi-30k dataset?
- Can the authors provide more comparisons with other recent unsupervised machine translation methods, especially those that use similar approaches?
- How does the proposed method handle the challenge of aligning languages with different textual representations using images as pivots?
