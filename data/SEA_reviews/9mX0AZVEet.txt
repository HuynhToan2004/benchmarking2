 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel framework for zero-shot methods in inverse problems using diffusion models, focusing on the approximation of conditional posterior mean for the reverse process. The authors propose a unified approach that optimizes the posterior covariance to enhance the performance of existing methods. They demonstrate the framework's effectiveness through experiments on various datasets, showing improvements over existing methods. However, concerns were raised regarding the novelty of the approach, the clarity of presentation, and the robustness of the results, particularly in noisy conditions.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, clear, and easy to follow, making complex concepts accessible.
- The authors propose a unified framework for zero-shot methods in inverse problems using diffusion models, which is a significant contribution to the field.
- The theoretical analysis provided is sound and solid, offering a deep understanding of the problem.
- The proposed method is shown to outperform existing zero-shot methods, demonstrating its effectiveness.
- The paper introduces a novel approach to optimizing the posterior covariance using maximum likelihood estimation, which is a novel contribution to the field.

**Weaknesses:**
- The novelty of the approach is limited as it primarily optimizes the posterior covariance, which is a common approach in Bayesian inference.
- The paper lacks a clear definition and explanation of certain key concepts, such as the role and definition of the conditional posterior mean.
- The experimental results are not convincing, with marginal improvements over existing methods and inconsistencies in performance across different datasets and noise levels.
- The paper's presentation could be improved, particularly in terms of clarity and the organization of content.
- The paper does not sufficiently address the limitations of the proposed method, which could lead to overestimation of its capabilities.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors clarify the role and definition of the conditional posterior mean in the context of their method?
- How does the proposed method compare to other Bayesian inference methods, particularly in terms of performance and computational efficiency?
- What are the specific advantages of the proposed method over existing zero-shot methods, and how does it address the limitations of these methods?
- Could the authors provide more detailed explanations and examples of how the optimal posterior covariance is derived and used in practical applications?
- How does the proposed method perform under different noise conditions, and what are the implications of these conditions on the performance of the method?
