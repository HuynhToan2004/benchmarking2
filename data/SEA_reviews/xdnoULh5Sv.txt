 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel adversarial defense mechanism, Carso, which integrates adversarial training and adversarial purification to enhance the robustness of image classification models. The method involves training a purifier using a variational autoencoder (VAE) to generate perturbation-free samples from the activations of an adversarially trained classifier. These samples are then aggregated to provide a robust prediction, which is shown to improve adversarial robustness against various attacks. The method is evaluated on CIFAR-10 and CIFAR-100 datasets, demonstrating significant improvements in robust accuracy at the cost of clean accuracy. The paper also discusses the potential scalability issues and the need for further research to address these limitations.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel approach by combining adversarial training and purification, which is a significant advancement in the field of adversarial robustness.
- The methodology is well-explained, making the paper easy to follow, and the experiments are comprehensive, demonstrating the effectiveness of the proposed method.
- The paper is well-written and structured, with a clear motivation and a logical flow of ideas.
- The experimental results show that the proposed method significantly enhances adversarial robustness, with improvements in robust accuracy compared to state-of-the-art (SOTA) methods.
- The paper provides a detailed explanation of the method and its implementation, which is helpful for reproducibility and further research.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper could benefit from a more detailed comparison with other adversarial defense methods, particularly in terms of computational efficiency and scalability.
- The method's reliance on adversarial training, which typically requires extensive computational resources and time, may limit its practical applicability.
- The paper does not adequately address the scalability issues associated with the use of VAEs, which could be a significant limitation in real-world applications.
- The paper's evaluation is limited to specific datasets and attack types, which may not generalize well to other datasets or attack scenarios.
- The paper does not sufficiently discuss the limitations of the proposed method, which could include its susceptibility to specific types of attacks or its applicability to different types of models.
- The paper lacks a thorough discussion on the potential negative societal impacts of the proposed method.

**Questions:**
- How does the proposed method compare in terms of computational efficiency and scalability with other adversarial defense methods?
- Could you provide more details on the training and inference times for the purifier, and how these times compare to those for the classifier?
- How does the proposed method perform against adaptive attacks, which are known to be effective against some adversarial defense methods?
- Can you clarify the role of the context encoder in the method and how it affects the overall performance of the model?
- How does the method perform when the adversarial perturbations are larger than those used in the experiments, and what are the potential impacts on the model's robustness?
- Could you discuss the potential negative societal impacts of the proposed method and how these might be mitigated?
