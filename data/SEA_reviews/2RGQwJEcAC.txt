 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel approach to channel selection in transformer architectures, focusing on visual transformers like MobileViT and EfficientViT. It employs a differentiable channel selection mechanism, leveraging the Information Bottleneck (IB) principle to optimize the channel selection process. This method aims to reduce the number of channels in attention outputs and the MLP layers, thereby reducing FLOPs while maintaining or even improving prediction accuracy. The approach is validated through extensive experiments on ImageNet-1k, demonstrating the efficiency and effectiveness of the proposed method. However, the paper's novelty and the clarity of its contributions have been questioned, with concerns about the complexity of the IB loss and the generalizability of the method to other datasets and models.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-organized, with clear figures and tables that aid in understanding the proposed method and its results.
- The proposed method is simple and effective, as evidenced by its application to MobileViT and EfficientViT, which shows improvements in model efficiency and performance.
- The use of the Information Bottleneck (IB) loss to guide channel selection is an innovative approach that has been validated through extensive experiments.
- The paper is well-written, making it accessible and easy to follow, which is crucial for understanding complex methodologies.
- The authors have provided code, which is a significant contribution to the reproducibility of the research.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed analysis of the channel selection process, particularly how it affects the model's performance and the generalizability of the results.
- The paper does not include experiments on other datasets, such as ImageNet-22k, which could have provided a more comprehensive evaluation of the proposed method.
- The paper does not sufficiently discuss the limitations of the proposed method, which could help in understanding its applicability and potential drawbacks.
- The paper's contribution to the field is questioned, with concerns about the novelty and the clarity of the proposed method's advantages over existing techniques.
- The complexity of the IB loss and the need for additional hyperparameters (e.g., λ and η) could make the method less practical for some applications.
- The paper's reliance on certain hyperparameters and the sensitivity of the results to these parameters are not adequately discussed, which could limit the reproducibility and generalizability of the findings.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors provide more details on how the channel selection process affects the model's performance and the generalizability of the results?
- How does the proposed method perform on other datasets, such as ImageNet-22k?
- Can the authors clarify the role and impact of the IB loss on the model's performance, particularly in terms of its complexity and the need for additional hyperparameters?
- How does the proposed method compare to other pruning techniques, such as token pruning or block pruning, in terms of efficiency and effectiveness?
- Could the authors discuss the limitations of their method and provide insights into how these limitations might affect the practical application of the proposed approach?
