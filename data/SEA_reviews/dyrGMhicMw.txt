 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel weight initialization method called "weight selection," which selects a subset of weights from a pretrained larger model to initialize a smaller model. This method is aimed at reducing training time and enhancing performance in smaller models, particularly in resource-constrained settings. The authors have conducted extensive experiments across various image classification datasets, comparing this method with traditional initialization methods and knowledge distillation. The paper also discusses the potential of this method in leveraging pretrained models in edge devices, though it lacks a broader evaluation across different architectures and datasets.

- The proposed method is simple yet effective, as demonstrated by the experiments conducted.

- The paper provides a comprehensive analysis of the method, including its impact on training time and performance, and compares it with traditional initialization methods and knowledge distillation.
