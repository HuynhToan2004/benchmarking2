 **Summary:**
The paper investigates the inverse optimal transport (iOT) problem, focusing on the recovery of ground costs from samples drawn from a coupling that solves an entropic regularized optimal transport problem. It introduces a novel approach by using $\ell_1$ regularization to model sparsity in the ground cost, which is derived from the graphical lasso and Lasso problems. The paper provides theoretical analysis, including a sufficient condition for the robust recovery of sparsity, and presents a large-scale solver for the proposed $\ell_1$-iOT method. The theoretical contributions are supported by numerical experiments, particularly in the context of Gaussian distributions, which demonstrate the method's effectiveness in recovering sparsity. However, the paper's presentation and clarity could be improved, and the scope of the experiments is limited.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel approach to the inverse optimal transport problem by using $\ell_1$ regularization, which is a significant advancement in the field.
- The theoretical analysis provided is robust and well-supported, with a detailed discussion on the irrepresentability condition and its implications.
- The paper is well-written, making it easy to follow, and includes a large-scale solver for the proposed $\ell_1$-iOT method, which is a valuable addition to the field.
- The numerical experiments, particularly those involving Gaussian distributions, are well-executed and support the theoretical claims effectively.
- The paper is the first to provide a theoretical analysis of the performance of regularized iOT estimation, which is a significant contribution to the field.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper suffers from issues with clarity and presentation, particularly in the definitions and explanations of key terms and concepts.
- The experimental setup is limited, focusing primarily on Gaussian distributions, which may not adequately represent real-world scenarios.
- The paper lacks a comprehensive comparison with existing methods, which could help establish the novelty and effectiveness of the proposed approach.
- The proofs and derivations in the paper are sometimes unclear or incomplete, which could hinder the understanding of the theoretical contributions.
- The paper could benefit from a more detailed discussion on the assumptions and limitations of the model, particularly in the context of practical applications.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors clarify the definitions and roles of key terms such as $I$ and $U_{I,I}$ in the context of the paper?
- How does the proposed method compare to other regularization techniques, such as nuclear norm regularization?
- What is the relationship between the regularization parameter $\lambda$ and the parameter $\epsilon$ in the context of the inverse optimal transport problem?
- Can the authors provide a more detailed explanation or proof for the irrepresentability condition and its implications for the performance of the $\ell_1$-iOT method?
- How does the proposed method handle the non-uniqueness of the solution in the inverse optimal transport problem?
- Could the authors elaborate on the practical implications and assumptions of their model, particularly in real-world applications?
