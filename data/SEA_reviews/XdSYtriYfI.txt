 **Summary:**
The paper introduces FEDORA, a federated offline reinforcement learning (FRL) algorithm designed to address the challenges of heterogeneity in offline datasets and the distribution shift problem. It employs a novel approach by utilizing an ensemble of critics to compute weights for the federated policy, which is updated using a weighted combination of local policies. The algorithm is evaluated on both simulated and real-world datasets, demonstrating its effectiveness in handling diverse data and improving performance over centralized and federated baselines. However, the paper faces criticisms regarding the clarity of its contributions, the novelty of its approach, and the lack of a comprehensive comparison with existing methods.

**Strengths:**
- The paper addresses a significant and challenging problem in federated learning (FL) by integrating offline RL with FL, which is a novel and under-explored area.
- The methodology is clearly described, making it easy to follow and understand, and the paper includes comprehensive experiments that demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed method.
- The paper provides a thorough evaluation of the proposed FEDORA algorithm, showing its superiority over existing methods in both simulated and real-world scenarios.
- The paper is well-written, with a clear structure and detailed explanations that aid in understanding the complexities of the proposed method.
- The inclusion of real-world experiments adds credibility to the claims made in the paper and demonstrates the practical applicability of the proposed method.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a clear explanation of the specific contributions of the FEDORA algorithm, making it difficult to discern the novelty and the advantages over existing methods.
- There is a noticeable absence of comparisons with other relevant offline RL methods, which is crucial for establishing the superiority of the proposed method.
- The paper could benefit from a more detailed discussion on the limitations of the proposed method and potential negative societal impacts, which are currently relegated to an appendix.
- The experimental setup and results could be improved by including more diverse datasets and a broader range of baseline methods, as well as more detailed performance metrics and error bars to better quantify the results.
- The paper suffers from some inconsistencies in notation and terminology, which could confuse readers and detract from the clarity of the presentation.

**Questions:**
- Can you clarify the specific contributions of the FEDORA algorithm and how it addresses the challenges of federated offline RL more effectively than existing methods?
- How does the FEDORA algorithm handle the distribution shift problem, particularly in scenarios where data heterogeneity is significant?
- Could you provide more details on the experimental setup, such as the number of seeds used and the variability in results, to better understand the robustness of the proposed method?
- How does the FEDORA algorithm perform when applied to other datasets or real-world scenarios beyond those tested in the paper?
- Could you elaborate on the choice of baselines and why certain methods were not included in the comparisons?
- Can you explain the rationale behind the specific design choices in the FEDORA algorithm, particularly the use of entropy regularization and the weighting of local policies?
