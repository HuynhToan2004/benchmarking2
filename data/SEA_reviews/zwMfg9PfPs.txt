 **Summary:**
The paper explores the out-of-variable (OOV) generalization problem, focusing on how an agent generalizes across environments with different sets of variables. The authors introduce a method that leverages marginal information to achieve OOV generalization, particularly in scenarios where the target environment contains variables not observed in the source environment. This method is supported by theoretical analysis and experimental results, demonstrating its effectiveness in predicting outcomes in new environments. The paper also discusses the limitations of existing methods and proposes a novel approach that utilizes residual distributions to infer causal relationships and generalize across different environments.

**Strengths:**
- The paper addresses an important and interesting problem of out-of-variable (OOV) generalization, which is a novel and relevant topic in the field of machine learning.
- The paper is well-written, clear, and easy to follow, with a clear motivation and a logical structure that makes it accessible to readers.
- The authors provide a thorough theoretical analysis and experimental results, which support the effectiveness of their proposed method.
- The paper includes a variety of experiments that demonstrate the method's performance across different function classes, showing its robustness and generalizability.
- The authors consider the OOV generalization problem from a causal perspective, which is a novel approach that could be beneficial for future research in this area.
