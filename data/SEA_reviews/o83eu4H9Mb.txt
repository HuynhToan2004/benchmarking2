 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel framework called InfoR-LSF, which aims to enhance feature learning by retaining as much relevant information from the input as possible, rather than compressing it through the traditional information bottleneck method. The framework consists of three stages: initial training, saliency erasing, and joint training. It employs a regularization term to prevent the learning of redundant features, thereby improving generalization capabilities in low-resource settings. The paper presents various experiments across different domains and datasets to demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed method. However, concerns were raised about the clarity of the method's operation and its comparison with existing methods, particularly in terms of computational overhead and the theoretical underpinnings of its effectiveness.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel approach to feature learning by retaining as much relevant information as possible, which is a significant departure from traditional methods that often compress or suppress irrelevant features.
- The proposed method is well-motivated and the experiments are comprehensive, including a variety of datasets and different training data sizes, which helps in validating the effectiveness of the method across different scenarios.
- The paper is well-written and easy to follow, making it accessible to a broad audience.
- The method's ability to improve performance in low-resource settings is highlighted, which is a significant contribution to the field.
- The paper includes a detailed analysis of the attention distribution of the learned features, which provides valuable insights into the method's operation.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks clarity in explaining the method's operation, particularly in the second stage where the selection and erasure of salient features are not clearly described.
- The comparison with existing methods is not robust, particularly in terms of the computational overhead and the effectiveness of the proposed method in more complex or diverse datasets.
- The paper does not provide a clear theoretical justification for why the proposed method should be effective, which could strengthen the argument for its utility.
- There are several typographical and formatting errors that need to be corrected for better readability and professional presentation.
- The paper does not adequately address the issue of feature suppression, which is a critical aspect of the proposed method.
- The paper could benefit from a more comprehensive discussion on the limitations of the proposed method and how these might affect its practical application.

**Questions:**
- Could you provide more details on how the salient features are selected and erased in the second stage of your method?
- How does the proposed method compare in terms of computational overhead and efficiency with other methods, particularly in large-scale or complex datasets?
- Can you clarify the role and effectiveness of the regularization term in preventing the learning of redundant features?
- How does the method perform when the training data size is large, and what are the implications for its generalization capabilities?
- Could you provide more theoretical justification for why the proposed method should be effective in improving generalization capabilities?
- How does the method handle the potential issue of feature suppression, and what measures are in place to prevent this from occurring?
