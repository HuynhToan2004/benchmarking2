 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel method for simulating discrete Langevin dynamics using stochastic gradient estimates, aimed at improving sampling efficiency in discrete spaces. This method, termed SGDLD, employs a caching scheme and Polyak step size control to mitigate biases and stabilize the algorithm. Theoretical contributions include the proof of asymptotic unbiasedness and the analysis of convergence rates. Empirical evaluations across various applications demonstrate the method's effectiveness, showcasing improvements over existing methods. However, the paper's theoretical contributions are seen as limited, and the experimental evaluation is considered insufficient to fully support the claims made.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, clear, and easy to follow, with a clear motivation and well-defined problem setting.
- The proposed algorithm is simple and intuitive, with a clear explanation of its components and their contributions.
- The empirical results demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed method, showing improvements over existing methods in various applications.
- The theoretical analysis of the convergence rate is comprehensive, providing a solid foundation for the proposed method.
- The paper provides a novel approach to sampling from a stochastic distribution using stochastic gradient estimates, which is an interesting and original contribution to the field.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a thorough theoretical analysis of the proposed method, particularly in terms of convergence guarantees and the impact of different hyperparameters on the convergence rate.
- The experimental evaluation is limited, with insufficient details and comparisons to state-of-the-art methods.
- There are concerns about the scalability of the method, particularly in high-dimensional or complex discrete spaces.
- The paper does not adequately discuss the computational cost of the proposed method, which could be a significant limitation in practical applications.
- The paper does not provide a detailed discussion on the limitations of the proposed method, which could be crucial for understanding its applicability and effectiveness in different scenarios.
