 **Summary:**
The paper introduces LiResNet++, an innovative approach to enhancing the certified robustness of neural networks by optimizing the design space of Lipschitz-based certification methods. It explores various aspects such as architecture, Lipschitz control, and data augmentation to improve the certified accuracy of neural networks. The authors propose a novel Cholesky-based orthogonalization method for the neck of LiResNet and a new data augmentation strategy using DDPM. Extensive experiments are conducted on CIFAR-10/100 and TinyImageNet datasets, demonstrating the effectiveness of these innovations. However, the paper is criticized for its limited scope, focusing primarily on CNN-based models and not addressing the generalization of the proposed methods to other model architectures or datasets.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, with clear presentation and easy-to-follow content.
- Extensive experiments are conducted, demonstrating the effectiveness of the proposed methods.
- The paper provides a comprehensive survey of the design space for Lipschitz-based certification methods, covering various aspects such as architecture choice, Lipschitz control, and data augmentation.
- The proposed Cholesky-based orthogonalization method for the neck of LiResNet and the new data augmentation strategy using DDPM are innovative and contribute to the field.
- The paper presents a significant improvement in certified accuracy on CIFAR-10/100 datasets, surpassing the state of the art by up to 8.5 percentage points.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed comparison with other robust models, which could provide a better understanding of the proposed methods' effectiveness.
- The experimental results are not comprehensive, as the paper does not include results on larger datasets such as ImageNet or more recent datasets like ImageNet-1K.
- The paper's focus on CNN-based models limits the generalizability of the findings, as the proposed methods might not be directly applicable to other model architectures.
- There is a lack of theoretical analysis on the proposed methods, which could strengthen the paper's contribution.
- The paper's reliance on existing techniques such as GloRo training and Layer-wise Orthogonal Training Layer might limit its novelty.
- The paper does not sufficiently discuss the limitations of the proposed methods, which could affect its practical application.

**Questions:**
- How does the proposed method compare to other robust models, and what are the specific advantages of the LiResNet++ model?
- Can the proposed method be generalized to other model architectures, such as transformers or attention-based models?
- How does the proposed method perform on larger datasets like ImageNet-1K, and what are the potential challenges in scaling up the method?
- Could the authors provide a more detailed comparison of the computational costs associated with different Lipschitz control mechanisms?
- How does the paper address the issue of overfitting in the context of robust training, especially when using generated data augmentation?
- Is there a possibility to extend the proposed methods to other types of adversarial attacks beyond those considered in the paper?
