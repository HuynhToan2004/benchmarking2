 **Summary:**
The paper investigates the stability of deep reinforcement learning (RL) policies in continuous control tasks, particularly focusing on the chaotic dynamics produced by RL controllers. It employs the Maximal Lyapunov Exponent (MLE) and Sum of Lyapunov Exponents (SLE) to analyze the stability of the trained policies, revealing that they are highly sensitive to initial conditions, which can lead to significantly different long-term outcomes. The study also proposes a method to improve the stability of RL policies by constraining the reward function. The paper claims that this instability is a critical issue that limits the application of RL in real-world problems, suggesting that the reward function needs to be modified to enhance stability.

**Strengths:**
- The paper addresses an important and relevant problem by focusing on the stability of RL policies in continuous control tasks, which is crucial for real-world applications.
- The use of MLE and SLE to quantify the stability of deep RL policies is innovative and provides a new perspective on the stability of RL policies.
- The paper is well-structured and easy to follow, with clear explanations of the methodology and results.
- The empirical findings are supported by a detailed analysis of the stability of RL policies, which is a significant contribution to the field.
- The paper provides a new control environment to address the instability of RL policies, which could be beneficial for further research.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks clarity in some sections, particularly in the description of the methodology and results. For instance, the details of how the MLE and SLE are computed are not adequately explained.
- The paper's claims about the instability of RL policies and the impact of initial conditions on long-term outcomes are not convincingly supported by the empirical evidence presented.
- The paper does not adequately address the potential causes of the observed instability, such as the complexity of the environment or the specifics of the RL algorithms used.
- The paper's methodology and results are not sufficiently novel or surprising, as the instability of RL policies is well-known and the proposed solution (constraining the reward function) is not new.
- The paper's presentation could be improved, with more detailed descriptions of the methodology and results, and better integration of figures and text.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors clarify how the MLE and SLE are computed and provide more details on the methodology used?
- How do the authors ensure that the perturbations used in their experiments do not significantly affect the dynamics of the system?
- Is there a specific reason why the paper focuses on the Walker environment, and how does this choice affect the generalizability of the findings?
- Could the authors provide more detailed explanations of the reward function and how it affects the stability of the RL policies?
- How do the authors address the potential issues of overfitting in their experiments, and what steps have been taken to ensure the robustness of the results?
- Can the authors provide a more detailed comparison of their methodology and results with existing literature to better contextualize their contributions?
