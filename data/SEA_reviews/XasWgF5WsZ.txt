 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a unified framework for diffusion models, focusing on the extended reverse-time stochastic differential equation (ER-SDE) and its applications in solving variance-exploding (VE) and variance-preserving (VP) stochastic differential equations (SDEs). The authors propose a novel solution space for ER-SDE, highlighting the equivalence of VP and VE SDEs when the correct noise scale function is used. The paper also presents a fast and training-free sampler, ER-SDE-Solver, which achieves state-of-the-art performance in terms of FID score with fewer non-linear forward evaluations (NFE). The theoretical contributions include the exact solution for VE SDE and VP SDE, and the paper demonstrates the practical implications of these solutions through extensive experiments.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a unified framework for diffusion models, integrating various existing methods and proposing a new solution space for ER-SDE, which is a significant contribution to the field.
- The authors provide a clear and comprehensive review of the literature, effectively situating their work within the existing body of knowledge.
- The paper is well-written, with a clear structure and logical flow, making it accessible and easy to follow.
- The theoretical analysis is rigorous and well-explained, with a focus on the equivalence of VP and VE SDEs under specific conditions, which is an important insight for the community.
- The proposed ER-SDE-Solver is a fast and training-free sampler that achieves state-of-the-art performance in terms of FID score with fewer non-linear forward evaluations (NFE), demonstrating practical applicability.
- The paper includes extensive experiments that validate the theoretical claims and show the effectiveness of the proposed methods.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed discussion on the practical implications and limitations of the new solution space for ER-SDE, particularly in terms of its applicability and the conditions under which it can be used effectively.
- There is a lack of clarity on the specific advantages of the proposed methods over existing ones, particularly in terms of computational efficiency and sample quality.
- The paper could benefit from a more thorough comparison with existing methods, including a discussion on how the proposed methods compare in terms of computational cost, sample quality, and other relevant metrics.
- The paper could be improved by providing more detailed explanations and examples for the mathematical formulas and notations used, particularly those introduced in sections 3.2 and 3.3.
- There is a need for more extensive experimental validation, including comparisons on larger and more diverse datasets to fully demonstrate the effectiveness and generalizability of the proposed methods.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors provide more detailed explanations and examples for the mathematical formulas and notations introduced in sections 3.2 and 3.3?
- How does the proposed ER-SDE-Solver compare in terms of computational cost and sample quality with other training-free samplers, particularly when the number of non-linear forward evaluations (NFE) is limited?
- Could the authors discuss the practical implications and limitations of the new solution space for ER-SDE, and provide more detailed examples or case studies to illustrate its effectiveness and applicability?
- How does the proposed method handle the issue of non-analytical and nonlinear neural network integration in the nonlinear term of the ER-SDE solution, and what are the potential solutions or improvements for this challenge?
- In the experimental results, why does the ER-SDE-Solver-3 achieve the best FID score at NFE=20 but not at NFE=50?
- Can the authors provide more insights into the selection of the noise scale function in ER-SDE, and discuss how this choice affects the performance and applicability of the method?
