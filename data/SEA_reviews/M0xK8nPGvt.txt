 **Summary:**
The paper introduces C-PSRL, a novel method for posterior sampling in reinforcement learning (RL) that utilizes a causal graph to factorize the Markov Decision Process (MDP) into independent components. This approach allows for the efficient learning of a causal graph and the estimation of model parameters conditioned on the sampled graph. Theoretical analysis is provided to demonstrate the regret bound of C-PSRL, showing a dependence on the degree of prior knowledge and the number of episodes. Empirical evaluations are conducted on simple domains, demonstrating that C-PSRL outperforms existing methods like PSRL and F-PSRL. However, concerns were raised about the novelty and the clarity of the paper, particularly in the presentation of the algorithm and the theoretical results.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces an innovative approach to posterior sampling in reinforcement learning by utilizing a causal graph as a prior, which is a novel idea not explored in prior works.
- The theoretical analysis provided is robust, with a detailed regret analysis and an interesting connection to causal discovery.
- The empirical results demonstrate that the proposed algorithm, C-PSRL, outperforms existing methods such as PSRL and F-PSRL in terms of regret and model error.
- The paper is well-written and the presentation is clear, making it accessible to readers familiar with the concepts discussed.
- The paper offers a novel perspective on the use of causal graphs in reinforcement learning, which is both theoretically intriguing and practically relevant.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a clear and concise presentation of the algorithm, particularly in the description of Algorithm 1 and the derivation of the regret bound.
- There is a lack of novelty in the algorithm design, which appears to be a straightforward extension of existing methods.
- The empirical evaluations are limited to simple domains, which may not adequately demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed method in more complex scenarios.
- The paper does not sufficiently address the computational complexity of the algorithm, which is a significant concern for practical applications.
- There are several typographical and formatting errors throughout the paper that detract from its professional presentation.
- The paper does not adequately discuss the limitations of its methodology, particularly in terms of scalability and generalizability to more complex scenarios.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors clarify the relationship between the causal graph and the factorization of the MDP, especially in terms of the derivation of the regret bound?
- How does the degree of prior knowledge affect the regret bound in the proposed algorithm?
- Could the authors provide more detailed examples or experimental results that demonstrate the effectiveness of the algorithm in more complex scenarios?
- What are the specific computational challenges associated with the implementation of C-PSRL, and how do these impact the practical applicability of the method?
- How does the algorithm handle the discovery of new edges in the causal graph, especially when these may not be initially known or assumed by the algorithm?
- Can the authors elaborate on the connection between the regret bound and the causal structure, particularly in terms of how the causal graph influences the regret rate?
