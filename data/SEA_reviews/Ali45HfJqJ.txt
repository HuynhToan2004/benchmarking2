 **Summary:**
This paper investigates the uncertainty evolution in learning dynamics of zero-sum games, specifically focusing on the observer uncertainty and its relationship to the evolution of covariances. The authors explore the use of covariance matrices as a measure of uncertainty, contrasting with the traditional use of differential entropy. They analyze the dynamics of cumulative strategies and payoffs through the lens of symplectic geometry and Hamiltonian systems, providing theoretical results on the growth rates of covariance information for different discretization methods. The paper also presents experimental results to support these theoretical findings. However, the paper has been critiqued for its lack of clarity in defining key terms and its assumptions, which may limit the generalizability of its results.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel perspective on measuring observer uncertainty in learning dynamics of zero-sum games by focusing on the evolution of covariance matrices, which contrasts with the traditional use of differential entropy.
- The theoretical results presented are well-supported by experimental evidence, demonstrating the practical applicability of the proposed methods.
- The paper is well-written and easy to follow, making it accessible to a broad audience.
- The use of symplectic geometry to analyze the dynamics of cumulative strategies and payoffs in zero-sum games is innovative and provides a new angle for understanding these complex systems.
- The paper addresses an important problem in the field of game theory, specifically the study of uncertainty in learning dynamics, which is crucial for understanding the behavior of agents in strategic interactions.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks clarity in defining key terms such as "observer uncertainty" and "day-to-day behavior," which could confuse readers and limit the comprehensibility of the paper.
- The assumptions made in the paper, such as the boundedness of higher order differentials and the smallness of standard deviations, may not hold in practical scenarios, which could limit the applicability of the results.
- The paper does not adequately discuss the limitations of its approach, which could help in understanding the scope and applicability of the findings.
- The experimental setup is not well-explained, which could hinder the reproducibility and validation of the results.
- The paper does not sufficiently distinguish its contributions from existing works in the field, which could lead to confusion about the novelty and significance of the research.
