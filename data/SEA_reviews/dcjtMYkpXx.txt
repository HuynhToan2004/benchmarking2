 **Summary:**
The paper explores the use of ensembles of reward models to mitigate overoptimization in the RLHF setting, employing methods like worst-case optimization (WCO), uncertainty-weighted optimization (UWO), and mean optimization. The authors introduce a synthetic human feedback setup to study the efficacy of these methods, comparing them to a single reward model. The experiments are conducted using different ensemble sizes and optimization methods, showing that ensembles can reduce overoptimization and improve performance. However, the paper is criticized for its lack of novelty, insufficient comparison to existing methods, and unclear presentation of results, particularly in the figures.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel approach to the overoptimization problem in RLHF by utilizing ensembles of reward models, which is a significant advancement in the field.
- The experiments are well-designed and conducted, providing a robust evaluation of the proposed methods.
- The paper is well-written, making it easy to follow and understand the experimental setup and results.
- The inclusion of a synthetic human feedback setup is a valuable contribution, allowing for a more controlled and systematic study of the overoptimization problem.
- The paper addresses an important problem in RLHF, which is crucial for the development and deployment of large language models.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a thorough discussion on the relationship between the proposed method and existing methods, such as those mentioned in the review.
- There is a lack of clarity in the presentation of results, particularly in the figures, which could benefit from better labeling and organization.
- The novelty of the approach is questionable as similar methods have been previously explored in other contexts, such as in model-based reinforcement learning.
- The paper does not adequately address the issue of overoptimization in the context of online RLHF, which is a significant limitation given the prevalence of this setup in practical applications.
- The experimental setup and results are not sufficiently detailed, making it difficult to fully understand the implications and effectiveness of the proposed methods.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors clarify the relationship between their proposed method and existing methods, particularly those mentioned in the review?
- How does the proposed method perform in the context of online RLHF, where reward models are updated frequently?
- Could the authors provide more detailed experimental results, including the specifics of the ensembles used and the impact of different ensemble sizes on performance?
- How do the authors address the issue of overoptimization in the context of online RLHF, and what are the implications of this for the practical deployment of their methods?
