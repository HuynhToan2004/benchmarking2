 **Summary:**
The paper investigates the application of unsupervised pretraining techniques, specifically SimCLR and SimCLR-Rank, to enhance the performance of deep learning models in the Learning-to-Rank (LTR) task. It compares these methods against Gradient Boosted Decision Trees (GBDTs) and other non-pretrained models, showing that pretrained models can outperform GBDTs when there is more unlabeled data than labeled data. The research is supported by experiments on public benchmark tabular LTR datasets and a large industry-scale proprietary ranking dataset. The paper also introduces a new pretraining loss, SimCLR-Rank, to address the specific challenges in LTR tasks. Despite the novelty of applying pretraining to LTR, the paper's contributions are seen as limited due to the similarity of SimCLR-Rank to existing methods and the lack of significant performance improvements over GBDTs.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written and easy to follow, making it accessible to a broad audience.
- The authors have provided code for reproducibility, enhancing the transparency and reliability of the research.
- The study explores the potential of unsupervised pretraining in Learning-to-Rank (LTR), which is an interesting and relevant research direction.
- The paper includes experiments on both public benchmark tabular LTR datasets and a large industry-scale proprietary ranking dataset, providing a comprehensive evaluation of the proposed methods.
- The authors have proposed a new pretraining loss, SimCLR-Rank, which is specifically designed for LTR tasks, demonstrating a novel approach to addressing the challenges in this field.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper's main contribution, SimCLR-Rank, is seen as limited as it closely resembles existing methods like SimCLR, with the main difference being the contrastive loss applied to items in the same query group rather than across the entire batch.
- The performance improvement of the proposed methods over GBDTs is not significant, and in some cases, GBDTs outperform the pretrained models.
- The paper lacks a thorough analysis of the reasons behind the observed performance differences between pretrained models and GBDTs, especially in scenarios where GBDTs perform better.
- The paper does not sufficiently discuss the challenges and complexities of applying pretraining to LTR tasks, which could limit the understanding of the practical implications and challenges of the proposed methods.
- The paper does not provide a detailed comparison with other state-of-the-art methods, which could help in understanding the relative performance of the proposed methods.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors provide a more detailed analysis of the reasons behind the observed performance differences between pretrained models and GBDTs?
- How do the authors address the challenges and complexities of applying pretraining to LTR tasks, particularly in terms of data availability and diversity?
- Could the authors discuss the potential of using different types of pretraining methods, such as contrastive learning with hard negatives, to improve the performance of LTR models?
- In light of the similarities between SimCLR-Rank and existing methods, how does the proposed method differ significantly in terms of performance or approach?
- The paper mentions that the performance of GBDTs is sometimes better than that of pretrained models. Can the authors provide more insights into these scenarios and discuss the factors contributing to these results?
