 **Summary:**
The paper introduces MoleBlend, a novel self-supervised learning method designed to integrate 2D and 3D molecular representations. It leverages a blend-then-predict training paradigm to align atom relations across different modalities, enhancing the model's ability to predict various molecular properties. The approach is supported by extensive experiments on benchmarks such as MoleculeNet and QM9, demonstrating state-of-the-art performance in both 2D and 3D tasks. The methodology involves a multimodal pretraining approach that utilizes both 2D and 3D data, with the model trained on the PCQM4Mv2 dataset and evaluated on various tasks including molecular property prediction, molecular generation, and molecular property classification.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel multimodal pretraining method, MoleBlend, which effectively integrates 2D and 3D molecular representations by aligning atom relations.
- The proposed method achieves state-of-the-art performance on both 2D and 3D molecular tasks, demonstrating its effectiveness and versatility.
- The paper provides a detailed theoretical analysis of the mutual information maximization objective, which helps in understanding the method's underlying principles.
- Extensive experiments are conducted to evaluate the method's performance across various molecular tasks, including molecular property prediction, molecular generation, and molecular property classification.
- The methodology is well-motivated, clearly presented, and supported by a thorough literature review, making the paper accessible and informative.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a clear comparison with existing multimodal pretraining methods, such as those mentioned in references [1] and [2], which could provide a better context for evaluating the proposed method.
- The theoretical analysis in Section 3.4 is not well-explained, and the mutual information maximization objective is not clearly defined or explained.
- The paper does not sufficiently discuss the limitations of the proposed method, which could help in understanding its applicability and potential drawbacks.
- The experimental results, particularly the performance of MoleBlend in 3D tasks, are not convincingly superior to other methods, and the paper could benefit from more detailed comparisons and ablation studies.
- The paper could benefit from a more detailed discussion on the scalability of the proposed method, especially in terms of computational resources and data requirements.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors clarify the comparison with existing multimodal pretraining methods, particularly those mentioned in references [1] and [2]?
- How does the proposed method compare in terms of performance and computational efficiency with other multimodal pretraining methods, such as those mentioned in the review?
- Can the authors provide more detailed explanations or examples to clarify the theoretical analysis in Section 3.4, particularly the mutual information maximization objective?
- How does the method perform when applied to larger or more complex datasets, and what are the scalability limitations?
- Can the authors provide more detailed comparisons and ablation studies to better understand the impact of different components of the method, such as the blending ratio and the choice of 2D and 3D features?
