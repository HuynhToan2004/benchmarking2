 **Summary:**
The paper investigates the consistency of answers provided by large language models (LLMs) when subjected to follow-up questioning, which can lead to significant shifts in responses, particularly when the initial answers were correct. The authors propose a Follow-up Questioning Mechanism inspired by questioning strategies in education and evaluate its impact on models like ChatGPT, PalM2-Bison, and Vicuna-13B across eight reasoning benchmarks. They also explore different prompting methods to mitigate these inconsistencies. The study reveals that even when initial answers are correct, judgement consistency decreases when LLMs face disturbances such as questioning, negation, or misleading prompts. The paper provides empirical evidence and proposes potential solutions, but concerns remain about the generalizability of the findings and the method's applicability to other types of LLMs.

**Strengths:**
- The paper addresses a critical and timely issue regarding the inconsistency in LLM responses when subjected to follow-up questions, which is a significant concern for practical applications.
- The proposed Follow-up Questioning Mechanism is well-designed, intuitive, and easy to understand, providing a clear methodology for evaluating LLMs under different conditions.
- The paper includes a thorough evaluation using multiple benchmarks, which demonstrates the reliability and robustness of the proposed method.
- The authors have conducted extensive experiments and error analysis, which provide valuable insights into the behavior of LLMs under different conditions.
- The paper is well-written, making it accessible and easy to follow, which is crucial for a broad audience.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper primarily focuses on a single model (ChatGPT) and does not evaluate other models like GPT-4 or Bard, which could limit the generalizability of the findings.
- The evaluation methodology, particularly the use of prompt templates, might not adequately represent real-world scenarios, which could affect the reliability of the results.
- The paper does not provide sufficient details on the selection of the temperature for the models, which could impact the consistency and accuracy of the results.
- The paper lacks a comprehensive discussion on the limitations of the proposed method, which is crucial for understanding its applicability and effectiveness in different scenarios.
- The paper could benefit from a more detailed analysis of the impact of different types of questions and prompts on the consistency of LLM responses.

**Questions:**
- How does the Follow-up Questioning Mechanism compare to other methods of evaluating LLM consistency, such as those using different types of prompts or questioning strategies?
- Can the authors provide more details on the selection of the temperature for the models and how this affects the consistency and accuracy of the results?
- How does the proposed method perform when evaluated against other models like GPT-4 or Bard, and what are the implications of these results for the generalizability of the findings?
- Could the authors elaborate on the potential limitations of the proposed method and how these might affect its applicability in different scenarios?
- How does the paper address the potential bias introduced by the use of prompt templates, and what steps were taken to ensure the validity of the results?
