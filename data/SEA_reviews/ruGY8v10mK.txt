 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel data-driven measure of uncertainty for misclassification detection, which is inspired by Rao's diversity measure. This measure, termed Rel-U, is designed to identify misclassified instances based on the predicted class probabilities, using a closed-form solution for training. The authors claim that their method outperforms existing methods in various image classification tasks, particularly in scenarios involving mismatched data distributions. However, the paper has been criticized for its lack of clarity in explaining the method's motivation and its theoretical underpinnings. Additionally, the experimental setup and the choice of baselines have been questioned, suggesting potential biases in the evaluation.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel data-driven measure of uncertainty for misclassification detection, which is a significant contribution to the field.
- The methodology is supported by a closed-form solution for training, which is a practical advantage.
- The experimental results show that the proposed method outperforms existing methods in various image classification tasks, demonstrating its effectiveness.
- The paper is well-organized, making it easy to follow, and the methodology is clearly explained.
- The proposed method does not require retraining the model, which is a practical advantage.
- The paper addresses an important problem in machine learning, which is the identification of instances where the model's predictions are unreliable.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks clarity in explaining the motivation behind the proposed method and the intuition behind the Rel-U measure.
- The theoretical underpinnings of the method are not adequately explained, making it difficult to understand the method's effectiveness and reliability.
- The experimental setup and the choice of baselines have been questioned, suggesting potential biases in the evaluation.
- The paper could benefit from a more detailed discussion on the limitations and potential negative societal impacts of the proposed method.
- The paper's presentation could be improved by providing a more detailed discussion on the limitations and potential negative societal impacts of the proposed method.
- The paper's claims about the superiority of the proposed method over existing methods are not convincingly supported by the experimental results, which are sometimes inconsistent or incomplete.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors clarify the motivation behind the proposed method and the intuition behind the Rel-U measure?
- How does the proposed method compare to other uncertainty estimation methods like MCDropout, Deep Ensembles, and Bayesian neural networks?
- Why was the Gini coefficient chosen as the baseline, and how does it compare to other measures like Shannon entropy?
- How does the proposed method handle the issue of overconfident predictions, especially in cases where the model is not well-calibrated?
- Can the authors provide more details on the experimental setup, particularly the choice of baselines and the evaluation metrics used?
- How does the proposed method perform in scenarios where the training and test distributions do not match, and what are the implications of this mismatch on the method's effectiveness?
