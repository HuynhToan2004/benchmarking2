 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel Branch-and-Bound (BaB) framework for neural network verification, focusing on general nonlinearities beyond the ReLU activation. The authors extend the BaB framework by introducing a new branching heuristic and linear relaxation to support a wider range of nonlinearities. The proposed method, BBPS, optimizes the selection of neurons to branch and enhances the linear relaxation to tighten bounds. The paper demonstrates the effectiveness of the proposed method through experiments on various neural networks and benchmarks, showcasing improvements over existing methods. However, concerns were raised about the novelty and the depth of the theoretical contributions, as the method largely builds upon existing techniques.

**Strengths:**
- The paper addresses a significant and relevant problem in neural network verification, focusing on the verification of neural networks with general nonlinearities, which is a crucial area of research.
- The proposed method is technically sound and extends the BaB framework to support a wider range of nonlinearities, which is a significant contribution to the field.
- The paper is well-written, making it easy to follow, and includes a comprehensive evaluation of the proposed method against existing baselines, showing promising results.
- The methodology is general and can be applied to various neural network architectures, enhancing its applicability and potential impact.
- The paper provides a thorough evaluation, including comparisons with existing baselines and ablation studies, which help in understanding the effectiveness of the proposed method.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper's novelty is somewhat limited as it primarily extends existing methods (e.g., α,β-CROWN) to support general nonlinearities, which might not be perceived as a significant advancement by some reviewers.
- The theoretical contributions are not as strong as the empirical results, with the paper not providing sufficient theoretical analysis to support the proposed methods.
- There is a lack of comparison with some relevant baselines, such as the VeriNet and PRIMA, which could have provided a more comprehensive evaluation of the proposed method.
- The paper could benefit from a more detailed discussion on the limitations of the proposed method and the potential directions for future research.
- The paper's focus on general nonlinearities might limit its applicability to specific types of networks, as it does not address the verification of networks with other types of nonlinearities like ReLU networks.
- The paper could be improved by providing a more detailed discussion on the limitations of the proposed method and the potential directions for future research.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors clarify the choice of K in the branching process and how it affects the performance of the method?
- How does the proposed method compare in terms of runtime with other baselines, especially those that might be slower due to the use of commercial solvers?
- Could the authors provide more details on the experimental setup, particularly the choice of models and the conditions under which the method was evaluated?
- In light of the limitations discussed, what are the potential directions for future research to address these limitations and enhance the applicability of the proposed method?
- How does the proposed method handle different types of nonlinearities, especially those that are not typically considered in the verification literature?
