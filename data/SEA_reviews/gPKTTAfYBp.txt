 **Summary:**
The paper introduces FlashFFTConv, a novel algorithm for efficient convolution operations using FFT, aimed at enhancing the performance of long-sequence tasks. By employing a Monarch decomposition, the algorithm optimizes the use of tensor cores and reduces I/O operations, thereby improving computational efficiency. The approach is validated through experiments on various benchmarks, including language modeling and DNA modeling, demonstrating significant improvements in model quality and efficiency. The paper also proposes two sparse convolution methods, partial and frequency-sparse convolutions, which further enhance the memory savings and computational efficiency.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel approach to optimize the FFT convolution algorithm, which is a significant bottleneck in long-sequence tasks, by using a Monarch decomposition to map operations to tensor cores effectively.
- The proposed FlashFFTConv algorithm is shown to be more efficient than the standard PyTorch FFT convolution algorithm, as demonstrated by extensive experiments.
- The paper is well-written, clear, and provides a detailed analysis of the computational and memory costs of different FFT convolution algorithms, contributing to a better understanding of the underlying mechanisms.
- The algorithm's efficiency is validated through experiments on various benchmarks, demonstrating its effectiveness in improving model quality and efficiency.
- The paper provides a detailed cost model analysis and proposes a simple heuristic for selecting the decomposition order, which is crucial for achieving the best performance.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed discussion on the limitations of the proposed approach, particularly in terms of its applicability to different hardware architectures and its generalizability to other types of convolutional models.
- There is a lack of comparison with other state-of-the-art methods, which could have provided a more comprehensive evaluation of the proposed algorithm's effectiveness.
- The paper does not discuss the potential negative societal impacts of the proposed work.
- The experimental evaluation could be improved by including more diverse benchmarks and a more detailed analysis of the impact of different hyperparameters on the performance of the algorithm.
- The paper does not include a discussion on the potential limitations or failure cases of the proposed approach, which could help in understanding the practical applicability and robustness of the algorithm.

**Questions:**
- How does the proposed algorithm perform on different hardware architectures, particularly those that do not support tensor cores?
- Can the authors provide a more detailed comparison with other state-of-the-art methods, including a discussion on the computational and memory costs of these methods?
- How does the proposed algorithm scale with different sequence lengths, and what are the practical limitations in terms of the maximum sequence length that can be effectively handled?
- Can the authors provide more details on the computational and memory costs of the proposed algorithm compared to other methods, particularly in terms of the FLOPs and memory usage?
- Could the authors discuss the potential negative societal impacts of the proposed work and provide a more detailed discussion on the limitations and failure cases of the proposed approach?
