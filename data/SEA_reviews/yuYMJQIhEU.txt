 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel communication-efficient random-walk optimizer for decentralized learning, focusing on reducing communication costs by eliminating the need to transmit auxiliary parameters like momentum and preconditioners. The proposed method, Localized SAM, is designed to perform multiple local updates before sending the model to the next client, thereby reducing communication costs significantly. Theoretical analysis and empirical results demonstrate that Localized SAM achieves faster convergence rates compared to existing methods with similar communication costs. The paper also integrates sharpness-aware minimization (SAM) to prevent overfitting on local data. However, concerns are raised about the novelty of the approach, as it appears to be a combination of existing techniques, and the paper's clarity in presenting its contributions and theoretical results.

**Strengths:**
- The paper addresses a significant problem in decentralized learning by focusing on reducing communication costs, which is a crucial aspect in practical applications.
- The proposed method, Localized SAM, is simple yet effective, and the integration of sharpness-aware minimization (SAM) to prevent overfitting is an interesting approach.
- The paper provides theoretical analysis on the convergence of the proposed method, which is supported by empirical results showing that Localized SAM can achieve faster convergence rates compared to existing methods with similar communication costs.
- The experimental results are promising, demonstrating the effectiveness of the proposed method in reducing communication costs while maintaining similar loss performance to various stochastic optimization baselines.
- The paper is well-written, making it easy to follow, and the problem is clearly defined, which enhances the readability and comprehension of the content.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks novelty as it primarily combines existing techniques such as removing momentum and preconditioners from adaptive optimizers, and performing multiple local updates before sending the model to the next client, without introducing new methodologies.
- The theoretical analysis and proofs are not clearly explained, particularly in the proof of Theorem 3.7, which is crucial for understanding the theoretical contributions of the paper.
- The paper does not adequately address the issue of overfitting, especially when performing multiple local updates, which could lead to overfitting on local data.
- The paper does not compare the proposed method with the latest decentralized learning methods, which could provide a more comprehensive evaluation of its effectiveness and efficiency.
- The paper's presentation needs improvement, particularly in the clarity of the contributions and the explanation of the theoretical results.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors clarify how the proposed method addresses the issue of overfitting, especially in the context of multiple local updates?
- How does the proposed method compare with the latest decentralized learning methods, and what are the specific advantages or disadvantages compared to these methods?
- In the proof of Theorem 3.7, can the authors provide more detailed explanations or simplify the proof to make it more accessible to readers?
- How does the proposed method perform when compared to other decentralized optimization methods, such as those based on gossip algorithms or other random-walk methods?
- Can the authors provide more details on the experimental setup, particularly how the hyperparameters were chosen and how the results were validated across different datasets and network configurations?
