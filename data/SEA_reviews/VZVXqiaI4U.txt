 **Summary:**
The paper introduces two new metrics, Single-attribute Divergence (SaD) and Paired-attribute Divergence (PaD), to evaluate the attribute strength and relationships in generated images. These metrics are designed to improve upon existing metrics like CLIPScore by using heterogeneous initial points for image and text embedding vectors, which enhances the interpretability of the metrics. The metrics are evaluated across various generative models and datasets, including FFHQ and CelebA, to demonstrate their effectiveness in revealing attribute strengths and relationships. The paper also discusses the limitations of the metrics, such as their dependence on the quality of attribute detection and the potential for misleading results if attributes are not chosen carefully.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel approach to measuring attribute strength in generated images, which is a significant advancement in the field of generative models.
- The proposed metrics, SaD and PaD, are designed to be interpretable and customizable, allowing for a more nuanced evaluation of generative models.
- The paper provides a detailed analysis of the proposed metrics, including experiments that demonstrate the metrics' effectiveness in revealing attribute strengths and relationships in generated images.
- The paper is well-written, clear, and easy to follow, making it accessible to a broad audience.
- The introduction of Heterogeneous CLIPScore, which addresses the limitations of CLIPScore, is a valuable contribution to the field.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a comprehensive evaluation of the proposed metrics, particularly in terms of how well they correlate with human judgments or other established metrics.
- The paper does not adequately address the potential for misleading results if the attributes used in the metrics are not chosen carefully.
- The paper does not discuss the limitations of the proposed metrics, such as their dependence on the quality of attribute detection and the potential for misleading results.
- The paper does not provide a detailed discussion on how the metrics perform in different scenarios, such as when attributes are rare or when the generated images deviate significantly from the training data.
- The paper does not include a discussion on the computational efficiency of the proposed metrics compared to existing metrics.

**Questions:**
- How do the proposed metrics perform when attributes are rare in the training data, and what implications does this have for the evaluation of generative models?
- Can the proposed metrics be used to evaluate text-to-image models, and if so, how do they compare to existing metrics like FID?
- How do the proposed metrics compare to other metrics like Rarity score, particularly in terms of their ability to evaluate the rarity of generated images?
- Can the authors provide more detailed examples of how the metrics can be used to evaluate specific aspects of generative models, such as the diversity of generated images or the fidelity of text-to-image models?
- How do the proposed metrics perform in scenarios where the generated images deviate significantly from the training data, and what implications does this have for the evaluation of generative models?
