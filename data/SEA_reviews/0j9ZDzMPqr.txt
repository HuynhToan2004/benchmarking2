 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel method for generating counterfactual explanations in unsupervised node representation learning using a Monte Carlo Tree Search (MCTS)-based explainability method called UNR-Explainer. The method aims to identify the most important subgraphs that significantly change the k-nearest neighbors of a node in the learned embedding space upon perturbation. This is achieved by optimizing the importance of the subgraph while minimizing its size. The approach is evaluated on six datasets, demonstrating improved performance over baselines in both unsupervised GraphSAGE and DGI. However, concerns are raised regarding the clarity of the method's motivation, the choice of evaluation metrics, and the generalizability of the results to other unsupervised models.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel method for generating counterfactual explanations in unsupervised node representation learning, which is a significant advancement in the field.
- The method is well-motivated and clearly explained, making it accessible and understandable to readers.
- The experimental results are robust and demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed method, showing improvements over baseline methods.
- The paper is well-written, with clear figures and tables that aid in understanding the content.
- The introduction of a new selection policy in MCTS and the use of an LSH hashing to obtain the nearest neighbors efficiently are innovative and contribute to the field.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks clarity in its motivation, particularly in how the proposed method addresses the issue of unsupervised node representation learning, and why it focuses on counterfactual explanations.
- There is a lack of comparison with other baseline methods that could potentially provide more insight into the effectiveness of the proposed method.
- The choice of evaluation metrics and their relevance to the research question is not clearly justified, raising concerns about the validity of the results.
- The paper could benefit from a more detailed discussion on the limitations of the proposed method, including its generalizability to other unsupervised models and the potential biases introduced by the selection policy.
- The paper could be improved by providing a more detailed explanation of the theoretical underpinnings of the importance measure and the Monte Carlo Tree Search, and by including more detailed experimental results and ablation studies.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors clarify the motivation behind the proposed method and how it specifically addresses the challenges in unsupervised node representation learning?
- How does the proposed method compare to other baseline methods, particularly in terms of its effectiveness and efficiency?
- Could the authors provide more detailed explanations of the theoretical underpinnings of the importance measure and the Monte Carlo Tree Search, and how these contribute to the overall effectiveness of the method?
- In light of the concerns about the evaluation metrics, how do the authors justify their choice and what steps have been taken to ensure the validity of the results?
- How does the proposed method perform when applied to other unsupervised models, and what are the potential biases introduced by the selection policy?
- Could the authors provide more detailed experimental results and ablation studies to support the claims made in the paper?
