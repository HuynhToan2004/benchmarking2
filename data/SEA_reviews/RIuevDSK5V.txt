 **Summary:**
The paper introduces ConR, a novel contrastive regularizer designed to address the challenges of deep imbalanced regression. By modeling global and local label similarities in feature space, ConR aims to prevent the features of minority samples from collapsing into those of majority samples, thereby improving the performance of regression models on imbalanced datasets. The methodology is supported by extensive experiments across various datasets, demonstrating significant performance enhancements. The paper also provides a theoretical analysis and a comprehensive review of related works, highlighting the novelty and effectiveness of the proposed approach.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, clear, and easy to follow, with a clear motivation for the study and a comprehensive review of related work.
- The proposed method, ConR, is novel and well-explained, with a clear rationale for its design and implementation.
- The experiments are well-designed, comprehensive, and convincingly demonstrate the effectiveness of ConR.
- The paper provides a theoretical analysis of the proposed method, which adds depth to the understanding of its operation.
- The results are strong, showing significant improvements over existing methods, and the method is well-motivated and well-explained.
- The paper includes a good amount of supplementary material and the code is provided, which enhances reproducibility.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed discussion on the limitations of the proposed method and its applicability to other types of regression tasks.
- The paper does not sufficiently discuss the computational complexity of the proposed method, which could be a concern for large-scale applications.
- The paper could benefit from a more thorough discussion on the selection of hyperparameters and their impact on the performance of the model.
- The paper could provide more visualizations of the learned representations to better understand the impact of the proposed method.
- The paper could include a broader range of benchmark datasets to better demonstrate the generalizability of the proposed method.
- The paper could discuss the potential impact of the proposed method on the fairness of predictions, especially concerning the handling of imbalanced data.

**Questions:**
- How does the proposed method perform when applied to other types of regression tasks, and what are the expected outcomes?
- Can the authors provide more details on the computational complexity of the proposed method, particularly in terms of time and memory requirements?
- How are the hyperparameters selected, and what is the impact of different hyperparameter settings on the performance of the model?
- Can the authors provide more visualizations of the learned representations to better understand the impact of the proposed method?
- How does the proposed method handle the issue of imbalanced data, and what steps are taken to ensure fairness in predictions?
- Could the authors clarify the methodology used in the selection of positive and negative pairs, and how this affects the performance of the model?
