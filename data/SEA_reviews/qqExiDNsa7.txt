 **Summary:**
The paper explores the effectiveness of pre-trained models in speech separation, particularly focusing on the impact of training on noisy and mixed speech. It introduces a novel deep modularization technique for unsupervised speech separation, which is evaluated on several datasets. The authors investigate how pre-trained models perform when trained on contaminated speeches versus clean ones, and the results indicate that models trained on contaminated speeches produce better embeddings for speech separation. The paper also discusses the use of STFT features over time-domain features, suggesting that STFT features are more effective. However, the paper suffers from several issues including unclear writing, lack of detailed experimental setup, and insufficient comparisons with existing methods.

**Strengths:**
- The paper investigates the effectiveness of pre-trained models in speech separation, which is an interesting and relevant topic.
- The authors propose a novel deep modularization technique for unsupervised speech separation, which is a significant contribution to the field.
- The experiments conducted are thorough, providing a comprehensive evaluation of the proposed methods.
- The paper is well-organized and easy to follow, which facilitates understanding of the proposed methods and results.
- The use of contaminated speeches for training pre-trained models is a novel approach that could potentially enhance the performance of speech separation systems.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper suffers from unclear writing and lacks detailed explanations of the experimental setup and methodologies used, making it difficult to fully understand the results and their implications.
- The novelty of the deep modularization technique is questionable, as it appears to be a simple application of existing methods without significant innovation.
- The paper lacks a detailed comparison with existing methods, which is crucial for establishing the effectiveness and novelty of the proposed approach.
- The results presented in the paper are not convincing, with some metrics showing no significant improvement or even a decrease in performance compared to baseline methods.
- The paper could benefit from more comprehensive evaluations, including the use of more diverse datasets and different evaluation metrics to better demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed methods.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors clarify the experimental setup, particularly the details of the deep modularization technique and the pre-training process?
- How does the proposed deep modularization technique compare to other existing methods for speech separation, and what are the specific advantages of this approach?
- Can the authors provide more detailed results and comparisons, including the use of different evaluation metrics and more diverse datasets to better demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed methods?
- How does the paper address the permutation ambiguity problem in speech separation, and what are the specific contributions of the proposed methods in this regard?
- Can the authors provide more detailed explanations of the experimental results, particularly those that show no improvement or even a decrease in performance compared to baseline methods?
