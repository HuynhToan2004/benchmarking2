 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a variance reduction technique for the stochastic zeroth-order hard-thresholding (SZOHT) algorithm, addressing the limitations of the number of random directions and improving convergence rates. The proposed methodology, which includes the pM-SZHT and VR-SZHT algorithms, leverages historical gradients to mitigate the conflict between the expansivity of the hard-thresholding operator and the deviation of zeroth-order gradients. The paper provides theoretical analysis and empirical evidence to support the efficacy of these algorithms, demonstrating their ability to improve convergence rates and reduce the number of random directions required. The methodology is validated through experiments on ridge regression and black-box adversarial attacks, showing competitive performance against state-of-the-art methods.
