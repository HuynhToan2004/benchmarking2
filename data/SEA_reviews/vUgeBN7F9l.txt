 **Summary:**
The paper introduces PolyFormer, a novel graph Transformer model that utilizes polynomial attention mechanisms to enhance the scalability and expressiveness of node-level tasks. PolyFormer employs a polynomial tokenization method that integrates spectral information, which is then processed by a tailored polynomial attention mechanism (PolyAttn). This approach allows for node-wise filtering, improving the model's expressiveness and computational efficiency. Extensive experiments across various datasets demonstrate the model's superior performance compared to existing methods, showing its potential in handling large-scale graphs.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, clear, and easy to follow, with a clear motivation for the proposed method.
- The proposed method is simple, intuitive, and easy to implement, making it accessible for practical applications.
- The use of polynomial tokens for node-wise filtering is novel and innovative, providing a new perspective on graph filtering.
- The experimental results are strong, demonstrating the effectiveness of the proposed method across various datasets, including large-scale graphs.
- The paper provides theoretical analysis to support the proposed method, enhancing its credibility and applicability.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed discussion on the advantages and limitations of using polynomial attention mechanisms compared to other attention mechanisms like vanilla multi-head self-attention.
- There is a need for more detailed comparisons with other state-of-the-art methods, particularly in terms of computational complexity and scalability.
- The novelty of the polynomial attention mechanism is questioned as it appears to be a minor extension of existing methods like NAGphormer.
- The paper could benefit from more comprehensive experimental results, including comparisons with a broader range of baselines and different datasets.
- The scalability of the proposed method is not convincingly demonstrated, as the experiments are limited to small datasets.
- The paper could provide more insights into the choice of polynomial bases and the design of the attention mechanism, particularly the use of the hyperbolic tangent function.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors provide a detailed comparison of the computational complexity and scalability of the proposed method with other state-of-the-art methods?
- How does the choice of polynomial basis affect the performance and scalability of the model?
- What are the specific advantages of using polynomial attention mechanisms over other attention mechanisms, and how does it compare in terms of expressiveness and computational efficiency?
- Could the authors elaborate on the design choices of the attention mechanism, particularly the use of the hyperbolic tangent function?
- How does the proposed method perform on other node-level tasks such as link prediction and graph classification?
- Can the authors provide more detailed insights into the scalability of the model on larger datasets, possibly through ablation studies or additional experiments?
