 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel ensemble learning method named First-order Repulsive Deep Ensemble (FoRDE), which promotes diversity in input gradients to enhance the performance of neural networks. The methodology involves a kernel comparison in the space of first-order input gradients, which is shown to be effective in improving robustness against input perturbations. The paper also addresses the computational complexity and efficiency of the method, comparing it to existing ensemble methods like Deep Ensembles and Bayesian Neural Networks. Despite its innovative approach, the paper is criticized for its high computational complexity and limited experimental evaluation, particularly on larger datasets and with more comprehensive baselines.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, with clear and concise language, and includes a comprehensive literature review.
- The introduction of the First-order Repulsive Deep Ensemble (FoRDE) method, which promotes diversity in input gradients, is innovative and well-explained.
- The method is supported by a robust theoretical framework, including a detailed description of the WGD method and the kernel used for gradient comparisons.
- The paper presents a novel approach to improving the robustness of neural networks by focusing on the input gradients, which is a significant contribution to the field.
- The method's ability to enhance robustness against input perturbations is demonstrated through experiments, which are described in detail.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a comprehensive comparison with existing ensemble methods, particularly in terms of computational complexity and performance on larger datasets.
- The experimental results are limited to small datasets (CIFAR-10/100), and the performance of the proposed method on larger datasets like ImageNet is not reported.
- The method's computational complexity is high, which might limit its practical applicability.
- The paper could benefit from a more detailed discussion on the limitations of the proposed method, including its assumptions and potential negative societal impacts.
- The paper does not include a detailed discussion on the limitations of the proposed method, which could affect its practical applicability and the robustness of the results.
