 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel framework named Latent Embedding Alignment (LEA) aimed at addressing the challenges of fMRI decoding and encoding. It leverages a unified latent space to facilitate bidirectional transformations between fMRI signals and visual images, thereby enabling the prediction of brain activity from visual stimuli and vice versa. The framework is evaluated on two benchmark datasets, demonstrating superior performance over existing methods. However, the paper is critiqued for its limited novelty, as it primarily combines existing techniques without significant innovation. Additionally, concerns about the clarity of methodological descriptions and the fairness of comparisons are raised.

**Strengths:**
- The paper addresses a significant problem in the field of neuroscience, focusing on the prediction of brain activity from visual stimuli and vice versa, which is crucial for understanding brain functions.
- The proposed framework, LEA, is innovative in its approach to fMRI decoding and encoding, using a unified latent space for both modalities, which is a novel approach.
- The paper is well-written, making it easy to follow, and the experimental results demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed method.
- The paper includes comprehensive evaluations on two benchmark datasets, showing the superiority of the proposed method over existing approaches.
- The introduction of the ROI-induced embedding layer and the use of global representations for fMRI signal reconstruction are innovative contributions to the field.

**Weaknesses:**
- The novelty of the paper is limited as it primarily combines existing techniques without significant innovation.
- The paper lacks a detailed explanation of how the alignment between the two latent spaces is achieved, which is crucial for understanding the methodology.
- The comparison with existing methods is not fair as it does not consider the use of pre-trained models like CLIP, which could significantly affect the results.
- The paper does not adequately address the potential limitations or ethical considerations of the proposed method, which could be crucial for its practical application.
- There are several grammatical errors and unclear descriptions in the paper, which could confuse readers and detract from the credibility of the research.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors provide more details on how the alignment between the two latent spaces is achieved, particularly in the alignment module mentioned in the paper?
- How does the proposed method compare with other methods that use pre-trained models like CLIP, especially in terms of fairness and effectiveness?
- Can the authors clarify the role and impact of the ROI-induced embedding layer and the use of global representations in the fMRI signal reconstruction?
- How does the paper address the potential limitations or ethical considerations of the proposed method, especially in terms of privacy and data security?
- Could the authors provide more detailed explanations for the claims made in the paper, particularly in sections where the methodology or results are unclear or misleading?
