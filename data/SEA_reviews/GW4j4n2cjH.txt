 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel dataset, DD100, specifically designed for dance accompaniment tasks, featuring 100 dance clips from 10 different dance genres. It proposes a GPT-based method, Duolando, for generating dance sequences, leveraging off-policy reinforcement learning for more stable and adaptable results. The methodology involves a VQ-VAE for motion quantization, followed by a GPT-based model that generates motion sequences based on music and leader's movements. The paper also presents a benchmark for evaluating dance accompaniment, with metrics such as FID, Div, CF, BED, and BAS. Despite the innovative approach and the creation of a valuable dataset, concerns were raised about the novelty of the methodology, the clarity of the paper, and the limited comparisons with existing methods.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel dataset, DD100, which is significant for dance generation tasks and could potentially support more multi-modal human-human interaction tasks.
- The methodology, particularly the use of a GPT-based model for dance generation, is innovative and demonstrates a new application of GPT in dance accompaniment.
- The paper is well-written, with clear and detailed descriptions of the methodology and the dataset, making it accessible and understandable.
- The inclusion of a benchmark and the development of metrics for evaluating dance quality, interaction, and alignment with music is a valuable contribution to the field.
- The paper is original in its approach to dance accompaniment, with a clear and well-structured presentation that aids in understanding the complex methodology.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a thorough comparison with existing methods, particularly in terms of dance accompaniment, which could have provided a clearer context for the contributions of the proposed method.
- The novelty of the methodology is questionable, as it appears to be a combination of existing techniques without significant innovation.
- The paper suffers from clarity issues, particularly in the description of the dataset and the methodology, which could make it difficult for readers to fully understand the contributions and implications of the research.
- The evaluation metrics used are not well-explained, and the paper could benefit from a more detailed discussion on how these metrics were chosen and what they represent in the context of dance accompaniment.
- There is a lack of discussion on the limitations of the proposed method, which could help in understanding the scope and applicability of the findings.

**Questions:**
- Could you clarify the role of the VQ-VAE in the overall methodology, particularly in terms of its contribution to the dance generation process?
- How does the GPT model handle the complexity of dance accompaniment, and what specific adaptations were necessary to apply GPT to this domain?
- Can you provide more details on the dataset, including the number of sequences, the variety of dance styles, and the criteria for selecting the sequences?
- How does the proposed method compare to existing methods in terms of performance and efficiency? Could you include more baselines in your comparisons to strengthen the evaluation?
- Could you elaborate on the design of the evaluation metrics, particularly how they were chosen and what they represent in the context of dance accompaniment?
- How do you address the concerns regarding the novelty of the methodology, and what specific contributions does your work make beyond existing techniques?
