 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel method for fast retrieval in hyperbolic space by binarizing hyperbolic embeddings, thereby reducing storage requirements and improving search efficiency. The method leverages a linear approximation of hyperbolic distance and binary quantization to transform hyperbolic vectors into binary codes, which are then used to compute Hamming distances for retrieval. The approach is evaluated on several datasets, demonstrating significant speed-ups and competitive performance compared to full-precision embeddings. However, the paper is criticized for its lack of clarity in the experimental setup and the absence of a comprehensive comparison with other state-of-the-art methods.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel method for fast retrieval in hyperbolic space using binarized hyperbolic embeddings, which is a significant advancement in the field.
- The paper is well-written, clear, and easy to follow, making it accessible to readers.
- The methodology is sound, with a clear explanation of the proposed method and its theoretical foundations.
- The experiments are well-designed, showing the effectiveness of the proposed method in terms of both speed and performance.
- The paper provides a good survey of related work, helping to situate the research within the broader context of the field.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed comparison with state-of-the-art methods for fast retrieval in hyperbolic space, which could have strengthened the argument for the superiority of the proposed method.
- The experimental setup is not clearly described, particularly the details of the datasets used and the hyperparameters of the models.
- The paper could benefit from more comprehensive experiments, including larger datasets and more complex retrieval tasks.
- There is a lack of discussion on the limitations of the proposed method, which could help in understanding its applicability and potential drawbacks.
- The paper could be improved by providing more detailed visualizations and comparisons, particularly in figures and tables, to better illustrate the results and enhance the reader's understanding.
