 **Summary:**
The paper investigates the generalization capabilities of deep ResNets in the mean-field regime, where the gradient flow is described by a partial differential equation. The authors establish a lower bound on the minimum eigenvalue of the Gram matrix under these conditions, which is crucial for understanding the generalization performance of ResNets beyond the lazy training regime. The analysis includes the linear convergence of the empirical error and an upper bound on the KL divergence over the parameters distribution. The paper also provides insights into the generalization ability of deep ResNets beyond the lazy training regime, contributing to a better understanding of the fundamental properties of deep neural networks. However, the paper's results are limited to the mean-field regime and the two-layer MLP predictor, which may not fully capture the complexity of more realistic neural network architectures.

**Strengths:**
- The paper provides a lower bound on the minimum eigenvalue of the Gram matrix under the mean-field regime, which is crucial for understanding the generalization performance of ResNets.
- The authors establish the linear convergence of the empirical error and estimate the upper bound of the KL divergence over the parameters distribution, which are essential for analyzing the generalization of ResNets.
- The paper builds a connection between the Rademacher complexity result and the KL divergence, providing insights into the generalization ability of deep ResNets beyond the lazy training regime.
- The paper is well-written, making it easy to follow, and the results are presented in a clear and logical manner.
- The theoretical analysis provides an in-depth understanding of the global convergence under minimal assumptions, shedding light on the KL divergence of network measures before and after training, and building generalization guarantees under the mean field regime.

**Weaknesses:**

- The paper relies heavily on assumptions about the activation function and predictor, which may not hold for more complex or non-standard activation functions and predictors.
- The proofs and derivations in the paper are complex and require a deep understanding of the underlying mathematical concepts, making it challenging for readers without a strong background in the field.
