 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel graph coarsening method called Universal Graph Coarsening (UGC), which utilizes a combination of node features and graph structure to reduce the dimensionality of graphs. The method is based on locality-sensitive hashing (LSH) and is designed to be efficient in terms of time complexity and scalability. The paper claims that UGC can handle both homophilic and heterophilic graphs, and it has been tested against various benchmarks, showing promising results in terms of accuracy and efficiency. However, concerns were raised about the clarity of the paper, the lack of detailed theoretical analysis, and the need for more comprehensive comparisons with other graph coarsening methods.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel approach to graph coarsening by combining node features with graph structure, which is a significant advancement over traditional methods that focus solely on graph structure.
- The proposed method, UGC, is efficient in terms of time complexity and is scalable, making it suitable for handling large graphs.
- The paper provides a thorough evaluation of the proposed method, comparing it against various baselines and demonstrating its effectiveness in terms of accuracy and efficiency.
- The use of locality-sensitive hashing (LSH) to coarsen graphs is a novel and interesting application of this technique.
- The paper is well-written and easy to follow, making it accessible to a broad audience.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed theoretical analysis of the proposed method, which could enhance the understanding of its effectiveness and limitations.
- There is a lack of comparison with other graph coarsening methods, which could provide a more comprehensive evaluation of the proposed method.
- The paper is not well-organized, with some sections being difficult to follow due to poor writing and a lack of clarity in the presentation of results and methodology.
- The paper does not adequately address the issue of heterophily in graphs, which is a significant challenge in graph coarsening.
- The paper does not provide a detailed analysis of the time complexity of the proposed method, which is crucial for understanding its scalability.
- The paper does not adequately address the issue of node features, which are crucial for graph coarsening, and the integration of these features into the proposed method is not well-explained.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors provide a detailed comparison of UGC with other graph coarsening methods, including a discussion of the advantages and disadvantages of each approach?
- How does UGC handle heterophily in graphs, and what are the implications of this for the effectiveness of the method?
- Could the authors provide a more detailed theoretical analysis of the proposed method, including a discussion of its limitations and potential applications?
- How does UGC handle node features in the graph coarsening process, and what are the implications of this for the accuracy and efficiency of the method?
- Can the authors clarify the role of node features in the proposed method and explain how these features are integrated into the graph coarsening process?
- How does UGC compare to other graph coarsening methods in terms of accuracy, efficiency, and scalability, especially on large and complex graphs?
