 **Summary:**
The paper introduces SyncDreamer, a novel diffusion model designed to generate multiview-consistent images from a single-view image. This model leverages a 3D-aware feature attention mechanism to ensure consistency across different views, which is crucial for applications such as novel-view synthesis and text-to-3D generation. SyncDreamer extends the Zero123 model by incorporating a 3D-aware attention module that processes features from all target views simultaneously, enhancing the model's ability to maintain consistency across different views. The paper provides extensive evaluations demonstrating the model's effectiveness in generating consistent images and improving the reconstruction quality compared to existing methods. However, concerns are raised about the novelty of the approach, as it appears to be a direct extension of existing models like Zero123, and the generalization ability of the model is questioned due to its limited evaluation on a single dataset.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, with clear and detailed explanations of the methodology, making it easy to understand.
- The proposed method, SyncDreamer, demonstrates superior performance over existing methods, showing consistent and high-quality results.
- The use of 3D-aware attention to improve the consistency of generated images is novel and effective, as evidenced by the qualitative results.
- The paper provides a comprehensive evaluation, comparing the proposed method with several baseline methods and demonstrating its effectiveness through quantitative and qualitative results.
- The methodology is sound, and the paper is well-structured, making it accessible to readers.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed discussion on the limitations and potential negative societal impacts of the proposed method.
- The novelty of the approach is questioned, as it appears to be a direct extension of existing models like Zero123, without significant architectural or algorithmic innovations.
- The evaluation is limited to a single dataset, which raises concerns about the generalization ability of the method.
- The paper does not adequately address how the proposed method handles objects with complex shapes or large variations in appearance.
- The method's performance in scenarios with large object-viewpoint angles or significant object-scale variations is not explored, which could limit its applicability.
- The paper lacks a detailed analysis of the computational cost and memory requirements of the proposed method compared to existing methods.

**Questions:**
- How does the proposed method handle objects with complex shapes or large variations in appearance?
- Can the method be extended to handle objects with significant object-viewpoint angles or large object-scale variations?
- How does the method perform when the object is not located at the origin and normalized inside a cube?
- How does the method compare in terms of computational cost and memory requirements to existing methods?
- Could you provide more details on the training and inference times of the proposed method compared to Zero123?
- In the context of the experimental setup, how were the images selected for evaluation, and how does this affect the fairness of the comparison with other methods?
- What are the potential negative societal impacts of the proposed method, and how are these addressed in the paper?
