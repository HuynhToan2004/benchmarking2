 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel deep learning architecture, Multi-resolution Periodic Pattern Network (MPPN), designed for long-term time series forecasting. It employs a multi-resolution periodic pattern mining mechanism to capture inherent multi-resolution and multi-periodic patterns of time series data. The model also includes a channel-adaptive module to model each variate's perception of different extracted patterns for multivariate series prediction. The paper presents extensive experiments on nine real-world datasets, demonstrating the superiority of the proposed method compared to state-of-the-art methods. Despite its innovative approach, the paper has been critiqued for its limited novelty, inadequate comparison with existing methods, and insufficient discussion on the predictability of time series.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel deep learning architecture, MPPN, which effectively captures the inherent multi-resolution and multi-periodic patterns of time series data, enhancing the accuracy of long-term series forecasting.
- The channel-adaptive module is a significant contribution, allowing for adaptive modeling of different perceptions of multivariate series towards various temporal patterns, improving prediction performance.
- Extensive experiments on nine real-world benchmarks demonstrate that MPPN significantly outperforms state-of-the-art methods, maintaining linear computational complexity.
- The paper is well-organized, easy to follow, and includes comprehensive ablation studies to assess the effect of each module in MPPN.
- The authors provide a theoretical guarantee for conducting long-term time series forecasting by adopting an entropy-based method for evaluating the predictability of time series and providing an upper bound on how predictable the time series is before carrying out predictions.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a clear discussion on the novelty of the proposed method, especially in relation to existing methods like the inception mechanism and dilated convolution, which have been previously used in other works.
- The comparison with other state-of-the-art methods is insufficient, particularly in terms of training time and computational complexity.
- The paper does not adequately discuss the predictability of time series, which is crucial for understanding the limitations and applicability of the proposed method.
- The experimental setup and the choice of datasets need more detail and justification. For instance, the choice of benchmark datasets and the rationale behind the selection of specific datasets are not explained.
- The paper does not provide sufficient insights into the performance of the proposed method on datasets with different characteristics, such as those with varying degrees of periodicity or complexity.
- There is a lack of discussion on the limitations of the proposed method and its applicability in real-world scenarios.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors provide more detailed insights into the performance of the proposed method on datasets with different characteristics, such as those with varying degrees of periodicity or complexity?
- How does the proposed method perform in scenarios where the time series data exhibits complex patterns that are not easily modeled using periodic patterns?
- What are the specific advantages of the proposed method over existing methods, particularly in terms of computational complexity and training time?
- Could the authors discuss the predictability of time series in more detail, and how this affects the performance and applicability of the proposed method?
- How does the proposed method handle noisy or incomplete time series data?
- Can the authors provide a more detailed comparison with other state-of-the-art methods, including a head-to-head comparison in terms of computational complexity and training time?
