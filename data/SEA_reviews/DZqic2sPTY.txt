 **Summary:**
The paper introduces GraphPulse, a framework designed to predict graph properties in dynamic graphs using a novel approach that combines topological data analysis with recurrent neural networks. GraphPulse employs a Mapper algorithm to transform graphs into compact topological representations, which are then used to predict future graph properties. The framework is evaluated on various datasets, showing improvements over existing methods. However, concerns are raised about the clarity of the methodology, the choice of datasets, and the generalization of the results. The paper is criticized for its lack of clarity in explaining the methodology and the choice of experiments, which limits its reproducibility and applicability to broader scenarios.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel approach using Topological Data Analysis (TDA) and Mapper networks to summarize graph properties, which is a significant contribution to the field.
- The experimental results demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed method, showing improvements over existing methods in some datasets.
- The paper is well-written, making it accessible and easy to follow, which is crucial for understanding complex machine learning methodologies.
- The use of Mapper networks to represent graph properties in a compact form is innovative and could be a valuable tool for future research in this area.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks clarity in explaining the methodology, particularly the role and integration of the Mapper algorithm and the choice of datasets.
- The choice of datasets and their suitability for the task of predicting graph properties is questionable, with some datasets being too small or not representative of the broader graph property prediction task.
- The paper does not adequately address the scalability and computational complexity of the proposed method, which is crucial for its practical application in real-world scenarios.
- The experimental setup is limited, and the results are not convincingly generalizable across different datasets and scenarios, raising concerns about the robustness and applicability of the proposed method.
- The paper does not sufficiently discuss the limitations of the proposed method, which is essential for understanding its applicability and potential shortcomings.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors clarify the role and integration of the Mapper algorithm in the proposed method, especially in terms of how it contributes to the prediction of graph properties?
- How do the authors justify the choice of datasets, particularly those that are not representative of the broader graph property prediction task?
- Can the authors provide more details on the scalability and computational complexity of the proposed method, especially in terms of its practical applicability to larger and more complex graphs?
- How does the proposed method handle different types of graph properties, and what are the implications for its performance and generalizability across different datasets and scenarios?
- Could the authors discuss the potential limitations of their method and how these might affect its practical application and future research directions?
