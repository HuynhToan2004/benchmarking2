 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel Multi-Modality Adversarial Attack (MMA) algorithm designed to perturb both text and image inputs of latent diffusion models (LDMs) to mislead the models' outputs. MMA employs a query ranking framework to iteratively update perturbations on the input text and images, using a unified framework that ranks candidates based on their distortion on the cross-attention module. The algorithm is evaluated against several baseline methods, demonstrating its effectiveness and stealthiness in both white-box and black-box settings. However, concerns about the novelty of the method and the clarity of its contributions persist, as the paper does not sufficiently distinguish itself from existing adversarial attack methods.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-organized and easy to follow, with clear writing that aids in understanding the proposed methodology and results.
- The problem of multi-modality adversarial attacks on latent diffusion models is well-motivated, and the paper effectively identifies the weaknesses of existing single-modality adversarial attacks.
- The proposed method, Multi-Modality Adversarial Attack (MMA), is innovative in combining text and image perturbations, which is a significant advancement in the field of adversarial attacks on latent diffusion models.
- Extensive experiments are conducted to validate the effectiveness and stealthiness of the proposed MMA algorithm, demonstrating its superiority over existing single-modality adversarial attacks.
- The paper provides a detailed analysis of the multi-modality adversarial attacks on latent diffusion models, which is crucial for understanding the robustness of these models.

**Weaknesses:**
- The novelty of the proposed method is questionable, as it primarily combines existing adversarial attack methods without introducing significant new techniques or insights.
- The paper lacks a detailed discussion on the limitations and potential negative societal impacts of the proposed method, which is essential for a comprehensive understanding of its implications.
- The methodology section is not clearly explained, particularly the candidate generation process, which is crucial for understanding the effectiveness and efficiency of the proposed method.
- The paper does not sufficiently distinguish its contributions from existing works, particularly those that have explored similar aspects of adversarial attacks on diffusion models.
- The paper's focus on adversarial attacks on latent diffusion models is not entirely novel, as similar approaches have been explored in previous works, which limits the perceived novelty of the proposed method.
- The experimental results are not sufficiently robust, with some results showing marginal improvements or even degradation in performance compared to baseline methods.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors clarify how the proposed method is novel and different from existing adversarial attack methods, particularly in terms of the combination of text and image perturbations?
- How does the proposed method compare in terms of effectiveness and stealthiness to other multi-modality adversarial attacks on latent diffusion models, such as those mentioned in related works?
- Could the authors provide a more detailed explanation of the candidate generation process and the role of the attribution method in this process?
- How does the proposed method perform in terms of transferability to different models, and what are the implications of this for the practical application of the method?
- The paper mentions the use of different metrics for evaluating the effectiveness and stealthiness of the attacks. Could the authors explain why these specific metrics were chosen and how they contribute to the overall evaluation of the method?
