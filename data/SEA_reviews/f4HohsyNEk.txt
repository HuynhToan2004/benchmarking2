 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel method for generating watertight manifold meshes from multi-view images, leveraging a combination of neural field rendering and differentiable rasterization. The proposed method, termed NeuManifold, utilizes a differentiable marching cubes algorithm to optimize mesh geometry and appearance, aiming to produce high-quality, watertight meshes suitable for various downstream applications such as physical simulations and geometry processing. The methodology involves a two-stage approach, where the first stage uses a neural field representation like TensoRF for initialization, followed by a differentiable rasterization stage for mesh optimization. The paper also presents a new Differentiable Marching Cubes (DiffMC) algorithm for mesh extraction, which reportedly enhances rendering quality and efficiency. Despite its strengths, the paper is criticized for its incremental nature, lack of novelty in methodology, and limited evaluation on real-world datasets.

**Strengths:**
- The paper presents a novel method for generating watertight manifold meshes from multi-view images, which is a significant advancement in the field of 3D reconstruction.
- The proposed method combines the advantages of neural field rendering and differentiable rasterization, effectively addressing the limitations of existing methods.
- The introduction of a new Differentiable Marching Cubes (DiffMC) algorithm for mesh extraction is a notable contribution, enhancing the rendering quality and efficiency.
- The paper is well-written, with clear figures and detailed explanations, making it easy to follow and understand.
- The methodology is supported by a comprehensive set of experiments, demonstrating the effectiveness of the proposed approach.
- The paper addresses the issue of mesh reconstruction from multi-view images, which is crucial for various downstream applications such as physical simulations and geometry processing.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks novelty in its methodology, as it primarily builds upon existing techniques such as TensoRF and differentiable rasterization.
- The evaluation is limited to synthetic datasets, which may not adequately represent real-world scenarios.
- The paper does not compare its method with other state-of-the-art methods like MobileNeRF, BakedSDF, and others, which could have provided a clearer picture of its performance.
- There are concerns about the method's ability to handle specular areas, which could affect the accuracy of the reconstructed meshes.
- The paper could benefit from a more detailed discussion on the limitations of the proposed method, such as its performance in handling specular areas and its applicability to real-world datasets.
- The paper does not provide sufficient implementation details, such as the specifics of the neural field representation used and the training parameters, which could hinder reproducibility.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors clarify the specifics of the neural field representation used in the proposed method, including the neural network architecture and training parameters?
- How does the proposed method compare to other state-of-the-art methods in terms of performance, particularly in real-world scenarios?
- Can the authors provide more details on the limitations of the proposed method, especially in handling specular areas and real-world datasets?
- What are the potential applications of the proposed method beyond the ones discussed in the paper?
- How does the proposed method handle the initialization from neural fields, particularly in terms of ensuring manifoldness and avoiding self-intersections?
- Can the authors provide more visual results to demonstrate the performance of the method in real-world scenarios, such as the LLFF dataset?
