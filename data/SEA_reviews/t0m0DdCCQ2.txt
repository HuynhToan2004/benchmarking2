 **Summary:**
The paper introduces Liteformer, a variant of Evoformer aimed at reducing computational complexity in protein structure prediction, specifically addressing the high memory consumption of the original Evoformer in AlphaFold2. Liteformer employs a novel Bias-aware Flow Attention mechanism to transform MSA and pair representations with linear complexity, significantly reducing memory usage and training time. The methodology is supported by a theoretical analysis of computational complexity and extensive experiments demonstrating its efficiency and competitive performance compared to Evoformer. Despite its technical merits, the paper faces criticism for its limited novelty and the lack of detailed experimental validation, particularly concerning the effectiveness of the proposed modifications in practical scenarios.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, clear, and easy to follow, making it accessible to a broad audience.
- The authors provide a comprehensive theoretical analysis of the computational complexity of the attention mechanism in Evoformer, highlighting the challenges and proposing a novel solution.
- The proposed method significantly reduces computational complexity by up to 44%, which is a significant improvement in memory efficiency.
- The experimental results are robust, demonstrating the effectiveness of the proposed method in reducing computational costs while maintaining competitive performance.
- The paper is well-organized, with clear figures and tables that aid in understanding the methodology and results.
- The novelty of the proposed method is evident, particularly in the innovative use of flow network theory to linearize biased attention mechanisms.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed comparison with existing efficient transformer models, which could have provided a more robust validation of the proposed method.
- There is a noticeable absence of results from the AlphaFold2 baseline, which is crucial for evaluating the effectiveness of the proposed modifications.
- The experimental validation is limited to specific datasets and models, which might not generalize well to other scenarios or models.
- The novelty of the proposed method is questioned due to its similarity to existing models like Flowformer, with the primary distinction being the handling of bias matrices.
- The paper does not provide a comprehensive analysis of the computational complexity of the proposed method, which could be crucial for understanding its practical implications.
- There is a lack of discussion on the limitations of the proposed method, particularly concerning its applicability to different sequence lengths and the potential for overfitting.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors provide more details on the experimental setup, such as the number of layers in the Evoformer and the specifics of the computational environment?
- How does the proposed method compare in terms of computational complexity and efficiency with other existing efficient transformer models like Flash Attention?
- Can the authors clarify the role and impact of the bias flow weight and flow capacity in the performance of the proposed method?
- How does the proposed method perform in scenarios where the sequence length is not as long as 256 or 350, and what are the implications for its effectiveness?
- Could the authors provide a more detailed analysis of the computational complexity of the proposed method, including a comparison with the original Evoformer in terms of memory and computational requirements?
- How does the proposed method handle different sequence lengths, and what are the implications for its performance in such scenarios?
