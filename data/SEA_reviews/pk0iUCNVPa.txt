 **Summary:**
The paper introduces CheAtt, a novel self-attention mechanism for tabular data representation learning, inspired by graph signal processing and the PageRank algorithm. CheAtt leverages Chebyshev polynomials to address the oversmoothing issue in transformers, aiming to enhance representation performance. The methodology is validated through experiments on various datasets, showing improvements over existing methods. However, concerns are raised about the novelty of the approach, the clarity of experimental results, and the lack of a comprehensive comparison with existing methods.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel approach by integrating Chebyshev polynomial-based self-attention (CheAtt) into transformer models for tabular data representation learning, addressing the oversmoothing problem effectively.
- The methodology is well-explained, with clear and detailed writing, and the paper is easy to follow, making it accessible to readers.
- The proposed method is supported by experimental results showing improvements over existing methods, demonstrating its effectiveness.
- The use of Chebyshev polynomials and their orthogonal properties in the attention mechanism is a novel and interesting approach.
- The paper is well-structured, making it easy to follow, and the motivation behind the research is clear.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a thorough comparison with existing methods, particularly in terms of computational efficiency and the handling of different data types.
- The experimental results are not convincingly superior to existing methods, and the improvements are marginal in some cases.
- The novelty of the approach is questionable as it seems to be a combination of existing techniques without significant innovation.
- The paper does not adequately address the scalability of the proposed method for larger datasets, which could affect its practical applicability.
- There is a lack of clarity in the experimental setup and results, particularly in the tables and figures, which could benefit from better organization and labeling.
- The paper does not sufficiently discuss the limitations of the proposed method, which could help in understanding its applicability and potential drawbacks.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors clarify the experimental results and provide more detailed comparisons with existing methods?
- How does the proposed method perform with different data types, especially those not typically considered tabular?
- What are the specific advantages of using Chebyshev polynomials over other polynomial bases in the attention mechanism?
- Can the authors provide more detailed insights into the scalability of the proposed method for larger datasets?
- How does the proposed method address the oversmoothing problem in transformers, and what are the implications for the model's performance and generalization?
- Could the authors elaborate on the choice of parameters and their impact on the model's performance?
