 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel method named ReBaR for human pose and shape estimation from monocular images. It leverages reference-based reasoning to enhance the handling of occlusions and depth ambiguity, which are critical challenges in this domain. The methodology involves an attention-guided encoder that extracts features from both body and part regions, followed by a body-aware regression module that encodes these features for part representation. This approach allows for the inference of spatial relationships of occluded parts with the body, utilizing visible parts and body reference information. The paper presents a comprehensive set of experiments, including ablation studies, that demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed method. Despite its strengths, the paper has been critiqued for its lack of novelty and insufficient comparison with existing methods.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel method, ReBaR, which effectively handles occlusions and depth ambiguity in human pose and shape estimation from single-view images.
- The methodology is well-explained, with clear illustrations and a detailed explanation of the attention-guided mechanism, which is a significant contribution to the field.
- The paper presents a comprehensive set of experiments, including ablation studies, that demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed method.
- The paper is well-written, making it easy to follow, and the results are promising, showing significant improvements over previous methods.
- The authors have provided sufficient implementation details and conducted comprehensive experiments, which support the effectiveness of the proposed method.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks novelty as it primarily builds upon existing methods like PARE, incorporating an attention-guided encoder and a body-aware regression module.
- There is a lack of comparison with recent state-of-the-art methods such as ProPose and MotionBERT, which could have provided a more robust evaluation of the proposed method.
- The paper does not adequately discuss the limitations of the proposed method, which is crucial for understanding the scope and applicability of the research.
- The paper could benefit from a more detailed discussion on the method's generalization ability, particularly in scenarios where the method may not perform optimally.
- The experimental setup and results could be improved by including more detailed comparisons and evaluations, such as comparisons with different backbones and more challenging datasets.
- There are some typographical and formatting errors in the manuscript, which could be addressed to enhance the paper's professionalism.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors provide more details on how the proposed method generalizes to different scenarios, especially those where it may not perform optimally?
- How does the proposed method compare with the latest state-of-the-art methods like ProPose and MotionBERT, especially in terms of handling occlusions and depth ambiguity?
- Could the authors clarify the use of certain terms and notations, such as "ReBaR" and "Fbapf," and ensure that all figures and tables are properly formatted and labeled?
- Is there a possibility to include more challenging datasets in the experiments to evaluate the method's performance under more diverse conditions?
- Could the authors provide a more detailed discussion on the limitations of the proposed method and suggest potential directions for future research?
