 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel zero-shot NAS method for lightweight language models, utilizing a gradient-free proxy metric based on Principal Component Analysis (PCA) and the number of parameters. This method aims to improve the efficiency and effectiveness of NAS in lightweight language models by reducing the training time and enhancing the model's performance on benchmarks like GLUE and SQuAD. The proposed W-PCA method is evaluated against existing NAS techniques and shows promising results in terms of model size, latency, and performance. However, the paper is critiqued for its limited novelty, as the method largely builds on existing zero-shot NAS techniques, and for the lack of comprehensive empirical validation, particularly concerning the generalizability of the findings.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel zero-shot NAS method specifically tailored for lightweight language models, utilizing a gradient-free proxy metric based on Principal Component Analysis (PCA) and the number of parameters.
- The proposed method significantly reduces training time compared to one-shot NAS methods and achieves higher scores in the testing phase compared to previous state-of-the-art training-based methods.
- The methodology is clear, well-written, and easy to follow, making it accessible to a broad audience.
- The paper is original in its application of zero-shot NAS to NLU tasks, which is a first in the field.
- The method demonstrates a significant reduction in training time, which is crucial for practical applications.
- The paper includes a detailed comparison of the proposed method with previous methods, showing improvements in performance and efficiency.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed comparison of the proposed method with existing zero-shot NAS methods, particularly in terms of the number of parameters and latency.
- The novelty of the method is questioned as it appears to be a straightforward combination of existing techniques without significant innovation.
- The paper does not provide sufficient empirical validation of the proposed method, particularly concerning its generalizability and applicability to different types of models and tasks.
- The paper does not include a comprehensive analysis of the computational resources required for the proposed method, which could be critical for practical implementation.
- The paper does not address the potential limitations or societal impacts of the proposed method, which could be important considerations for its adoption.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors provide a more detailed comparison of the proposed method with existing zero-shot NAS methods, including a comparison of the number of parameters and latency?
- How does the proposed method perform when applied to different types of models and tasks beyond lightweight language models?
- Can the authors provide a more comprehensive analysis of the computational resources required for the proposed method, including both time and space complexity?
- How does the proposed method address the limitations and potential societal impacts of zero-shot NAS, particularly concerning the generalizability and applicability of the findings?
- Could the authors clarify the role and impact of the PCA threshold in the proposed method, and how it is set in different contexts?
- How does the proposed method compare to other zero-shot NAS methods in terms of the number of parameters and latency?
