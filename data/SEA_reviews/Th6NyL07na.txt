 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel decoding strategy called Decoding by Contrasting Layers (DoLa) aimed at reducing hallucinations in pretrained large language models (LLMs) without requiring external knowledge or fine-tuning. DoLa contrasts the differences in logits obtained from projecting later layers versus earlier layers to the vocabulary space, utilizing the fact that factual knowledge in LLMs is typically localized to specific transformer layers. This method is tested across various datasets and models, showing improvements in truthfulness across multiple choice and open-ended generation tasks. The approach is simple, effective, and does not require additional training or access to external knowledge bases, making it a practical solution for enhancing the factuality of LLM outputs.
