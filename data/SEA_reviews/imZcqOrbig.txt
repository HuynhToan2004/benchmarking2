 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel pre-training approach for 3D point clouds by leveraging pre-trained 2D networks to enhance the feature extraction process. The method projects 3D features onto 2D planes and uses a transformer-based decoder to predict pixel-wise correspondences, which is then used to supervise the learning of 3D features. The approach aims to address the data scarcity problem in 3D point cloud learning by utilizing the vast amounts of 2D data. The paper demonstrates the effectiveness of this method through extensive experiments, showcasing improvements in various downstream tasks such as 3D shape classification, part segmentation, 3D object detection, and semantic segmentation. However, concerns were raised about the novelty of the approach, the clarity of the methodological descriptions, and the fairness of the experimental comparisons.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel approach to point-cloud pre-training by leveraging pre-trained 2D networks, which is a significant departure from traditional methods that predict 2D features first and then obtain 3D features through dimensionality lifting.
- The method is well-motivated, and the paper is well-written, making it easy to follow and understand.
- The experimental results demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed method, showing improvements over existing methods in various downstream tasks such as 3D shape classification, part segmentation, 3D object detection, and semantic segmentation.
- The paper includes an ablation study that justifies the design choices, providing a deeper understanding of the method's components and their impact on performance.
- The methodology is straightforward and technically sound, making it a valuable contribution to the field of 3D point cloud learning.

**Weaknesses:**
- The novelty of the paper is limited, as similar methods have been proposed in previous works, such as the use of 2D pre-trained models to supervise 3D pre-training and the prediction of 2D multi-view pixel-wise correspondences from projected 2D feature maps.
- The methodological description is not clear, particularly the loss function and the training details, which could lead to confusion and misunderstanding of the proposed method.
- The paper does not sufficiently discuss the limitations of the proposed method, which could provide valuable insights into its applicability and potential improvements.
- The experimental comparisons may not be fair, as the proposed method uses pre-trained 2D networks and larger datasets, which could give it an unfair advantage over other methods.
- The paper does not adequately address the generalization of the method to other datasets or scenarios, which could limit its applicability and impact.
- The paper lacks a detailed discussion on the computational complexity and training time of the proposed method, which are crucial for practical deployment.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors provide more details on the novelty of their approach compared to previous works, particularly in terms of the methodology and experimental setup?
- Could the authors clarify the methodological descriptions, especially the loss function and the training details, to enhance the understanding of their proposed method?
- How do the authors address the limitations of their method, and what are the potential areas for improvement?
- Is there a plan to extend the method to other datasets or scenarios, and if so, what are the expected outcomes and challenges?
- Could the authors provide more details on the computational complexity and training time of the proposed method, and how these compare to other state-of-the-art methods?
