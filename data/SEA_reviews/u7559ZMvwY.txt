 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel framework, Adversarial Training on Purification (AToP), which integrates adversarial training and adversarial purification to enhance the robustness and generalization of deep neural networks against adversarial attacks. AToP comprises two components: perturbation destruction by random transforms and purifier model fine-tuning by adversarial loss. The framework is evaluated on CIFAR-10, CIFAR-100, and ImageNette datasets, demonstrating state-of-the-art results and robust generalization against unseen attacks. Despite its innovative approach, the paper faces criticism for its limited novelty, lack of comprehensive theoretical analysis, and potential computational complexity.

**Strengths:**
- The paper addresses a significant problem in the field of adversarial attacks by combining adversarial training and adversarial purification, proposing a novel defense technique that enhances robustness and generalization.
- The proposed method, AToP, is evaluated on various datasets including CIFAR-10, CIFAR-100, and ImageNette, demonstrating state-of-the-art results and robust generalization against unseen attacks.
- The paper is well-written, with clear presentation and detailed experimental results, including an ablation study that provides insights into the impact of different components of the proposed method.
- The method achieves state-of-the-art results in terms of both standard accuracy and robust accuracy, demonstrating its effectiveness in enhancing the performance of the purifier model in robust classification.

**Weaknesses:**
- The novelty of the proposed method is limited as it primarily combines existing techniques without significant innovation.
- The paper lacks a comprehensive theoretical analysis, which could strengthen the understanding of the proposed method's effectiveness and limitations.
- The computational complexity of the method is high due to the need for training on the purifier model, which could be a significant drawback for practical applications.
- The method's generalization ability is not adequately demonstrated, particularly against unseen attacks, and the paper does not discuss the potential for overfitting to specific attacks.
- The paper does not include experiments on more challenging datasets such as ImageNet, which could limit the understanding of the method's effectiveness in real-world scenarios.
- There are several minor typographical and formatting errors that need correction.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors provide a more detailed comparison of the proposed method with existing methods, particularly in terms of computational complexity and generalization ability?
- What are the specific advantages of the proposed method over existing methods like PAT, and how does it address the limitations of these methods?
- How does the proposed method perform against more complex and realistic adversarial attacks, such as those using transfer learning or gradient masking?
- Could the authors discuss the potential for overfitting to specific attacks and how the method could be adapted to prevent this?
- Can the authors clarify the experimental settings and the selection of hyperparameters, particularly in the context of different datasets and attack types?
