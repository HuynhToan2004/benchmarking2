 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel measure called T-Measure for assessing model transferability in zero-shot transfer learning scenarios, focusing on the dataset's influence without requiring labeled data in the target domain. This measure, based on the alignment of representation spaces between source and target datasets, is evaluated across various NLP tasks and datasets, showing its effectiveness in ranking models for model selection. The paper also discusses the methodology and its application in different scenarios, providing insights into the transferability of models across datasets. Despite its innovative approach, the paper has been critiqued for its limited experimental scope, lack of comparison with state-of-the-art methods, and unclear presentation in some sections.

**Strengths:**
- The paper addresses a significant and relevant problem in the field of transfer learning by focusing on model selection without requiring labeled data in the target domain.
- The introduction of the T-Measure is novel and innovative, providing a new approach to assessing model transferability.
- The paper is well-written, making it easy to follow, and the methodology is clearly explained.
- The evaluation of the T-Measure across various NLP tasks and datasets demonstrates its effectiveness in ranking models for model selection.
- The paper provides a new perspective on the problem of model selection in transfer learning, focusing on the dataset confounder and its effect on transfer.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed comparison with state-of-the-art methods in the field, which could have strengthened its claims.
- The experimental setup is limited to specific datasets and tasks, which may not generalize well to other scenarios or domains.
- The paper could benefit from a more comprehensive evaluation that includes a broader range of tasks and datasets to validate the robustness and generalizability of the T-Measure.
- The presentation of the paper needs improvement, particularly in the clarity and detail of the experimental setup and results.
- The paper does not sufficiently address the limitations of the T-Measure, which could affect its applicability in real-world scenarios.
- The paper's focus on zero-shot transfer learning may limit its relevance to other types of transfer learning, such as few-shot or full-shot transfer.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors clarify the relationship between the T-Measure and the V-Usability measure, particularly how they differ in their approach to model selection?
- How does the T-Measure perform when applied to datasets with different characteristics, such as varying sizes or label distributions?
- Could the authors provide a more detailed explanation of the experimental setup and results, including the specific datasets used and the methodology for evaluating the T-Measure?
- How does the T-Measure compare to other transferability measures that do not require labeled data in the target domain?
- The paper mentions that the T-Measure is based on the alignment of representation spaces between source and target datasets. Could the authors elaborate on how this alignment is achieved and how it affects the transferability of the models?
- How does the T-Measure perform when applied to datasets from different domains, such as computer vision or graph modeling?
