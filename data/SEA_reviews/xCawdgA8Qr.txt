 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a method for transferring learned policies across different domains by aligning latent state representations using unaligned trajectories of proxy tasks. The proposed approach leverages multi-domain behavioral cloning and regularization with maximum mean discrepancy to achieve this alignment. The method is evaluated across various domain shifts, including cross-robot and cross-viewpoint settings, demonstrating its effectiveness compared to existing methods. The paper also includes ablation studies to investigate the effectiveness of each loss component and the impact of different regularization terms. However, concerns were raised about the clarity and detail in the methodology, the selection of environments, and the generalizability of the results.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel method for aligning latent state representations across different domains, which is a significant contribution to the field of cross-domain policy transfer.
- The methodology is well-articulated, with clear explanations of the proposed approach and the use of visualizations to illustrate the alignment process.
- The paper includes a comprehensive set of experiments and ablation studies, which demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed method and provide insights into the impact of different components of the model.
- The paper is well-written, making it accessible and easy to follow, which is crucial for readers to understand the complexities of the proposed method.
- The approach shows potential for transferring learned policies to unseen domains, which is a critical aspect of policy transfer in real-world applications.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed discussion on the limitations of the proposed method, which is crucial for understanding its applicability and potential shortcomings.
- The selection of environments for evaluation is somewhat limited, which might not fully demonstrate the generalizability of the proposed method across diverse domains.
- There is a lack of clarity in the methodology, particularly in the definitions and roles of different components such as the state encoder, common policy, and action decoder.
- The paper could benefit from a more detailed explanation of the MMD loss and its impact on the alignment process, as well as a clearer explanation of how the action decoder operates.
- The paper does not discuss the potential negative societal impacts of the proposed method, which is a critical consideration for any technological innovation.
- There are minor typographical errors and inconsistencies in the paper that need to be corrected for clarity and professionalism.
