 **Summary:**
The paper explores the use of diverse auxiliary outliers to enhance the performance of out-of-distribution (OOD) detection. It introduces a method called Provable Mixup Outlier (ProMix) which leverages mixup to generate diverse auxiliary outliers, theoretically demonstrating that this diversity can lead to better OOD detection. The paper presents a theoretical framework for generalization error bounds and conducts extensive experiments to validate the proposed method, showing significant improvements over existing methods. However, concerns are raised about the clarity of the experimental setup, the generalizability of the results, and the novelty of the approach, as similar methods have been previously explored.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, with clear and well-organized content that effectively communicates the research findings.
- The authors provide a theoretical analysis that connects auxiliary outlier diversity to the upper bound of OOD detection error, emphasizing the importance of leveraging diverse auxiliary outliers to enhance the generalization capacity of the OOD detector.
- The paper introduces ProMix, a simple yet effective data augmentation method that increases the diversity of auxiliary outliers, which is theoretically guaranteed to improve OOD detection performance.
- The proposed method achieves state-of-the-art OOD detection performance, outperforming all compared methods training with auxiliary outliers.
- The paper provides extensive experiments to evaluate the effectiveness of the proposed method, demonstrating significant improvements in OOD detection performance.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks clarity in the experimental setup, particularly in the description of the auxiliary dataset and the training process.
- The generalizability of the results is questionable, as the experiments are limited to specific datasets and do not adequately address the applicability of the proposed method across different domains or datasets.
- The paper does not sufficiently discuss the limitations of the proposed method, particularly in scenarios where the method may not be effective or where it may introduce biases.
- There is a lack of discussion on the novelty of the method, as similar approaches have been previously explored in the literature.
- The paper could benefit from a more detailed comparison with existing methods, including a discussion on the advantages and disadvantages of the proposed method over these approaches.

**Questions:**
- Could you clarify the experimental setup, particularly the description of the auxiliary dataset and the training process?
- How does the proposed method perform when applied to datasets with different characteristics or distributions?
- Can you discuss the limitations of the proposed method and provide examples or scenarios where the method may not be effective or where it may introduce biases?
- How does the proposed method compare with existing methods in terms of computational complexity and resource requirements?
- In the context of the theoretical analysis, how does the paper address the practical implications of the assumptions made, particularly in real-world applications?
- Can you provide more details on the computational complexity and resource requirements of the proposed method, especially in comparison to existing methods?
