 **Summary:**
The paper introduces DuMBO, a decentralized Bayesian optimization (BO) algorithm that relaxes the assumption of low Maximum Factor Size (MFS) in the additive decomposition of the objective function. DuMBO leverages an augmented Lagrangian and the Alternating Direction Method of Multipliers (ADMM) to solve the optimization problem, which allows for the handling of large MFS without compromising the no-regret property. The algorithm is evaluated on both synthetic and real-world datasets, demonstrating its effectiveness in scenarios where the objective function exhibits a complex additive decomposition. Despite its innovative approach, the paper is critiqued for its lack of clarity in certain sections, particularly in the explanation of the algorithm and its theoretical guarantees.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, making it easy to follow and understand the proposed methodologies.
- The introduction of the DuMBO algorithm, which does not require the assumption of low Maximum Factor Size (MFS) in the additive decomposition of the objective function, is a significant advancement in Bayesian Optimization (BO).
- The paper provides a thorough analysis of the algorithm, including its theoretical guarantees and empirical evaluations that demonstrate the effectiveness of DuMBO in various scenarios.
- The use of the Alternating Direction Method of Multipliers (ADMM) to solve the optimization problem is highlighted as a novel approach in BO.
- The paper includes both synthetic and real-world datasets in its experiments, providing a robust evaluation of the proposed method.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks clarity in certain sections, particularly in the explanation of the algorithm and the theoretical guarantees, which could benefit from more detailed explanations.
- The paper could benefit from a more comprehensive discussion on the scalability of the proposed method, especially in terms of computational complexity and scalability with increasing dimensions.
- The comparison with other baseline methods, such as TuRBO, is not adequately discussed, which could affect the evaluation of the proposed method's effectiveness.
- There are some typographical and formatting errors that need correction to enhance the professionalism and readability of the paper.
- The paper could benefit from a more detailed discussion on the assumptions made, particularly Assumption 3.3, which is crucial for the theoretical results.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors provide more details on how the algorithm operates, especially in terms of message passing and the computational complexity involved?
- How does the proposed method compare to other recent works in the field of BO, such as those that use Variational Auto-Encoders (VAEs) or other advanced methods?
- Could the authors elaborate on the scalability of the proposed method and discuss potential strategies for handling large-scale BO problems?
- In the context of the experiments, can the authors clarify the discrepancies in the results between different algorithms, especially those involving the use of known additive decompositions?
- How does the paper address the scalability issues, particularly in terms of computational complexity and scalability with increasing dimensions?
