 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel simulation framework for neural organoids using artificial neural networks, aiming to replicate real-world experiments. The framework is designed to stimulate, record, and learn from neural organoids, employing a spiking neural network (SNN) for intelligent expansion. The paper also proposes a benchmark for evaluating the framework's performance, comparing it to real-world organoid experiments. Despite the innovative approach, the paper faces criticism for its lack of clarity in defining the problem it aims to solve, insufficient comparison with existing methods, and potential overclaiming of its contributions. The methodology and the results presented are not convincingly novel or superior to existing techniques, leading to questions about the paper's originality and practical utility.

**Strengths:**
- The paper addresses the significant issue of the high cost and time consumption associated with real-world neural organoid experiments, proposing a simulation framework that could potentially reduce these expenses and time.
- The authors have developed an intelligent expansion platform based on SNN, which could facilitate organoid-machine collaborative intelligence research.
- The paper introduces a benchmark for evaluating the simulation framework, including evaluation metrics and real-world organoid experiment data, which is crucial for assessing the performance of the proposed method.
- The proposed simulation framework is innovative, offering a novel approach to simulate neural organoids using artificial neural networks, which is not commonly explored in the field.
- The paper is well-organized, making it easy to follow, and includes detailed descriptions of the methodology and results, which are helpful for understanding the proposed approach.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks clarity in defining the problem it aims to solve, making it difficult to understand the motivation behind the research and the specific contributions of the proposed method.
- The methodology is not sufficiently novel or superior to existing methods, as it primarily employs standard neural network models and learning rules without significant innovation.
- The paper's claims of realism and accuracy in simulating neural organoids are overstated, as the model does not adequately represent the complexity and variability of real organoids.
- There is a significant gap between the simulation results and real-world neural organoid experiments, which raises concerns about the practical utility of the proposed framework.
- The paper lacks a thorough discussion of related work and fails to compare its methodology with existing approaches, which could have provided a better context for the research.
- The paper's presentation and clarity need improvement, with numerous typographical errors and unclear descriptions that make it difficult to understand the proposed methodology and results.

**Questions:**
- Could you clarify the problem the paper aims to solve and the specific contributions of the proposed method?
- How does the proposed simulation framework compare to existing methods in terms of accuracy, realism, and practical utility?
- Can you provide more details on the methodology used for simulating neural organoids, particularly how it differs from standard neural network models?
- How does the paper address the ethical concerns related to using real-world neural organoid data for simulation?
- Could you elaborate on the training process for the SNN and the role of the linear layer in the SNN model?
- How does the paper ensure that the simulated neural organoids behave realistically, given the significant differences from real organoids?
