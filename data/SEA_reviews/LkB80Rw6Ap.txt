 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel method for addressing over-squashing in Graph Neural Networks (GNNs) by leveraging curvature-constrained message passing. This approach aims to enhance the performance of GNNs by modifying the propagation of information based on the curvature of edges, which is claimed to mitigate over-squashing and improve the efficiency of GNNs. The method is tested on several datasets, demonstrating its effectiveness in node classification tasks. However, the paper has been critiqued for its lack of clarity in technical details, insufficient experimental validation, and unclear novelty.

**Strengths:**
- The paper addresses an important issue in GNNs, specifically the problem of over-squashing, which is a significant bottleneck in deep GNNs.
- The proposed method is simple, intuitive, and easy to understand, making it accessible to a broad audience.
- The paper includes a thorough analysis of the proposed method, including a discussion on the limitations and potential improvements.
- The experiments conducted are extensive, covering various datasets and GNN models, which helps in validating the proposed method's effectiveness.
- The paper is well-written and easy to follow, making it accessible to readers with varying levels of expertise in the field.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks clarity in technical details, particularly in the definition and application of curvature-constrained message passing.
- The novelty of the proposed method is questionable as it appears to be a combination of existing methods without significant innovation.
- The experimental validation is insufficient, with a limited number of datasets and a lack of comparisons with other state-of-the-art methods.
- The paper does not adequately address the computational complexity of the proposed method, which could be a significant limitation in practical applications.
- The paper does not provide sufficient theoretical analysis or empirical evidence to support the claims made about the method's ability to mitigate over-squashing.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors clarify the technical details, particularly the definition and application of curvature-constrained message passing?
- How does the proposed method differ from existing methods, and what are the specific advantages of using curvature-constrained message passing over other methods?
- Can the authors provide more detailed experimental results, including comparisons with other state-of-the-art methods and a broader range of datasets?
- How does the computational complexity of the proposed method compare to other methods, and what are the implications for practical applications?
- Can the authors provide a theoretical analysis or additional empirical evidence to support the claims made about the method's ability to mitigate over-squashing?
