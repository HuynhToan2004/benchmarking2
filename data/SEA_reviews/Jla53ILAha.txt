 **Summary:**
The paper explores the implicit regularization effects in multi-task learning (MTL) and pre-training followed by fine-tuning (PT+FT) using diagonal linear networks. It demonstrates how these methods bias the learning process towards feature reuse and sparsity in task-specific features. The authors extend these findings to ReLU networks, showing that the PT+FT method can learn a subset of features learned during pretraining, which is a novel "nested feature selection" behavior. The paper also proposes practical tricks to enhance the performance of PT+FT by adjusting the weight scaling. However, the study is limited to linear networks and does not extend to more complex architectures like deep convolutional networks.

**Strengths:**
- The paper provides a detailed analysis of the implicit regularization effects in multi-task learning (MTL) and pre-training followed by fine-tuning (PT+FT) using diagonal linear networks.
- The authors extend the findings to ReLU networks, showing that the PT+FT method can learn a subset of features learned during pretraining, which is a novel "nested feature selection" behavior.
- The paper is well-written, making it accessible and easy to follow, with clear and insightful figures that enhance understanding.
- The theoretical framework and empirical results are well-connected, providing a comprehensive understanding of the learning dynamics in neural networks.
- The paper includes a practical trick to improve the performance of PT+FT, which is a valuable contribution for practitioners.

**Weaknesses:**
- The study is limited to linear networks, which may not generalize well to more complex architectures like deep convolutional networks.
- The paper does not provide a direct comparison of the proposed methods with other multi-task learning strategies, which could help in understanding the relative effectiveness of the proposed methods.
- The theoretical results are not directly applicable to non-linear networks, which could limit the practical applicability of the findings.
- The paper does not discuss the limitations of its study, which could help in understanding the scope and applicability of the findings.
- The paper does not include a discussion on the potential societal impacts of the work, which is an important consideration for any research in the field of artificial intelligence.
