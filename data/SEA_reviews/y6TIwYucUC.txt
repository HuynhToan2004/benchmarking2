 **Summary:**
The paper introduces SPI-GAN, a novel GAN-based method that leverages a mapping network and a time-dependent discriminator to learn the straight-path interpolation from noisy to clean images. This approach aims to reduce training and sampling time compared to score-based generative models (SGMs) by directly generating images from a latent vector without recursive denoising steps. SPI-GAN's methodology includes a NODE-based mapping network and a time-dependent discriminator, which are claimed to improve the balance between sampling quality, diversity, and time. The paper provides both quantitative and qualitative results, demonstrating the method's effectiveness in reducing sampling time and improving image quality, although some reviewers noted issues with the clarity of the presentation and the novelty of the approach.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel method of learning the straight-path interpolation from noisy to clean images, which is a significant advancement in the field of generative models.
- The use of a mapping network and a time-dependent discriminator is innovative and contributes to the reduction of training and sampling time, making the model more efficient compared to traditional score-based generative models.
- The method is well-explained, with clear and detailed explanations of the model's architecture and the training process, which facilitates reproducibility and understanding.
- The paper presents both quantitative and qualitative results, demonstrating the effectiveness of the proposed method in generating high-quality images with improved diversity and faster sampling times.
- The authors have provided detailed experimental settings and have made the code and trained checkpoints publicly available, enhancing the reproducibility and accessibility of the research.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a thorough comparison with other generative models, particularly in terms of sampling quality and diversity, which are crucial aspects of generative models.
- The novelty of the approach is questioned as it appears to be a combination of existing methods such as diffusion-GAN and straight-path interpolation, which might not represent a significant advancement over existing models.
- The paper suffers from unclear presentation and organization, with some sections and figures being confusing or misleading, which could hinder the understanding and evaluation of the proposed method.
- There is a lack of detailed discussion on the limitations and potential negative societal impacts of the proposed method, which is essential for a comprehensive assessment of the model's applicability and ethical considerations.
- The paper does not adequately address the generalizability of the method across different datasets or the scalability of the model to higher resolutions, which could limit the practical utility of the proposed method.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors clarify the differences between SPI-GAN and other existing methods, particularly in terms of novelty and effectiveness?
- How does the proposed method compare in terms of sampling quality and diversity with other state-of-the-art generative models, especially in high-resolution datasets like CelebA-HQ-256 and LSUN-Church-256?
- Can the authors provide more details on the training process, particularly how the mapping network and discriminator are trained, and how the time-dependent discriminator is conditioned on time?
- How does the proposed method handle the generation of diverse samples, especially in scenarios where the model might tend to converge on a single mode?
- Could the authors discuss the potential negative societal impacts of their proposed method and outline strategies to mitigate these risks?
- What are the specific advantages of the proposed method over other models in terms of training and sampling efficiency, and how do these advantages translate into practical applications?
