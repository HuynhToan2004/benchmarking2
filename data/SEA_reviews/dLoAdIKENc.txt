 **Summary:**
The paper investigates the robustness of AI-image detectors, focusing on watermarking and classifier-based deepfake detectors. It identifies a fundamental trade-off between evasion and spoofing errors in watermarking, demonstrating that diffusion purification attacks can effectively remove low perturbation budget watermarks. The paper also introduces a spoofing attack that can misclassify real images as watermarked, highlighting potential risks. Theoretical insights are provided on the robustness-reliability trade-off of deepfake detectors, suggesting that they cannot achieve both robustness and reliability simultaneously. The paper's contributions include the theoretical analysis of the trade-off between evasion and spoofing errors, empirical validation of these theories, and the introduction of practical attacks on watermarking and deepfake detection methods.

**Strengths:**
- The paper provides a comprehensive review of various AI-image detectors, including watermarking and classifier-based deepfake detectors, and offers a detailed analysis of their robustness.
- It introduces a diffusion purification attack that effectively removes low perturbation budget watermarks by applying minimal changes to images, demonstrating a fundamental trade-off between evasion and spoofing errors in watermarking.
- The paper introduces a spoofing attack against watermarking by adding a watermarked noise image to clean images, which can deceive detectors into flagging them as watermarked.
- Theoretical insights are provided on the robustness-reliability trade-off of deepfake detectors, demonstrating that they cannot achieve both robustness and reliability simultaneously.
- The paper is well-written, easy to follow, and provides a good balance between theoretical analysis and practical experiments, which are useful for understanding the limitations of current watermarking and deepfake detection methods.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed discussion on the potential limitations of the proposed methods, particularly the diffusion purification attack, which might not be effective against high perturbation watermarking methods.
- The evaluation of the proposed methods is limited, with only a few datasets used for testing. More comprehensive testing on a variety of datasets could strengthen the paper's claims.
- The paper could benefit from a more detailed comparison with existing methods, especially in terms of the robustness and reliability of deepfake detectors.
- There is a need for more clarity in the presentation of theoretical results, particularly in the definitions and notations used, to make the content more accessible to readers.
- The paper could provide more practical recommendations or suggestions for improving the robustness of watermarking and deepfake detection methods based on the findings.

**Questions:**
- Could you elaborate on the potential limitations of the proposed diffusion purification attack, particularly in terms of its effectiveness against high perturbation watermarking methods?
- How does the proposed spoofing attack affect the performance of watermarking and deepfake detection methods, and what are the implications of this attack in practical scenarios?
- Could you provide more details on the experimental setup and the methodology used in the empirical evaluations, especially in the context of the robustness-reliability trade-off of deepfake detectors?
- How do the findings of this study contribute to the development of more robust and reliable watermarking and deepfake detection methods?
- Could you clarify the definitions and notations used in the theoretical results to make the content more accessible to readers?
