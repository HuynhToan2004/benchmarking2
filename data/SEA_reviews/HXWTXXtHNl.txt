 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel approach to train conditional diffusion models using noisy labels by proposing a Transition-aware weighted Denoising Score Matching (TDSM) objective. This objective leverages instance-wise and time-dependent label transition information to adjust the gradient signal from the score matching, enabling the score network to align with clean-label conditional scores. The paper provides theoretical insights into the relationship between clean-label and noisy-label conditional scores and empirical evidence demonstrating the effectiveness of the proposed method. It also discusses the practical implementation and the estimation of transition-aware weights, which are crucial for the method's operation. The paper is well-written, with clear explanations and extensive experimental validation, although it could benefit from more detailed comparisons with other methods and a broader range of datasets.
