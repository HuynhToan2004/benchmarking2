 **Summary:**
The paper introduces AGILE3D, a novel interactive 3D segmentation model that enables the simultaneous segmentation of multiple objects in a scene, improving efficiency and reducing user clicks compared to traditional methods that process objects sequentially. AGILE3D leverages a click-as-query module to encode user clicks as spatial-temporal queries, enabling explicit interactions between click queries and the 3D scene through a click attention module. This approach allows for a more holistic segmentation of objects in a scene, which is a significant advancement over existing methods. The model has been tested on multiple datasets and shown to outperform state-of-the-art methods in both interactive multi- and single-object segmentation benchmarks. However, the paper has been critiqued for its limited comparison with existing methods, particularly in terms of dataset and model complexity, and the lack of comprehensive evaluation across various scenarios and datasets.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel approach to interactive 3D segmentation, specifically enabling the simultaneous segmentation of multiple objects in a scene, which is a significant advancement over traditional methods that process objects sequentially.
- The AGILE3D model is well-designed, with a clear motivation and a logical structure that effectively communicates the problem and the proposed solution.
- The paper includes comprehensive ablation studies and user studies, providing a robust validation of the model's performance and effectiveness.
- The model's architecture is innovative, utilizing a click-as-query module to encode user clicks as spatial-temporal queries, which allows for more effective interactions between the user and the segmentation process.
- The paper is well-written, with clear figures and a clear presentation of the methodology and results, making it accessible and easy to understand.
- The experimental results are strong, showing that the AGILE3D model outperforms state-of-the-art methods in both interactive multi- and single-object segmentation benchmarks.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a comprehensive comparison with existing methods, particularly in terms of dataset and model complexity. It only evaluates the proposed model on a limited set of datasets and does not compare it with a variety of other models, which could provide a more complete understanding of its performance.
- The paper does not discuss the limitations of the proposed method, which is crucial for understanding its applicability and potential drawbacks.
