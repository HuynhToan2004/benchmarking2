 **Summary:**
The paper introduces EquiAV, a novel audio-visual representation learning framework that integrates single-modal equivariant contrastive learning with audio-visual contrastive learning. It proposes a method to learn audio-visual correspondence and rich modality-specific representations in separate latent spaces, ensuring the full benefit of diverse augmentations applied to both audio and visual modalities. The framework demonstrates robustness to strong augmentations and outperforms existing audio-visual self-supervised learning methods in audio-visual event classification and zero-shot audio-visual retrieval tasks. Extensive ablation studies verify the effectiveness of the proposed framework, highlighting its potential in maximizing the benefits of data augmentation.

**Strengths:**
