 **Summary:**
The paper introduces PEL, a novel parameter-efficient fine-tuning (PEFT) method for long-tailed recognition, which leverages semantic-aware classifier initialization and test-time ensembling to adapt pre-trained models to long-tailed recognition tasks in fewer than 20 epochs without additional data. The method addresses the overfitting issue in traditional fine-tuning methods by introducing a small number of task-specific parameters and employs a semantic-aware classifier initialization technique derived from the CLIP textual encoder. The paper claims that PEL outperforms state-of-the-art methods on four benchmark datasets, demonstrating its effectiveness and efficiency.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel parameter-efficient long-tailed recognition method, PEL, which effectively adapts pre-trained models to long-tailed recognition tasks in fewer than 20 epochs without the need for additional data.
- The proposed method demonstrates its effectiveness and efficiency by achieving state-of-the-art (SOTA) performance on four benchmark datasets.
- The method is versatile, allowing for the integration of various parameter-efficient fine-tuning methods, which enhances its applicability and adaptability.
- The paper is well-organized, easy to follow, and includes comprehensive experimental results and detailed ablation studies, which support the claims made.
- The semantic-aware classifier initialization technique is novel and simple, yet effective, and the test-time ensembling method is a useful technique that can be applied in other areas.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a clear justification for the choice of the test-time ensembling method, particularly the use of different image cropping positions as perturbations, which could be better motivated.
- The paper does not provide a detailed comparison of the computational cost or training time of the proposed method against other parameter-efficient fine-tuning methods, which could be crucial for practical applications.
- The paper primarily focuses on vision transformers and does not explore how the method might perform with other types of models, such as convolutional neural networks (CNNs), which could limit its applicability.
- The paper's contribution is limited by the existing PEFT methods, and the novelty of the method is not clearly distinguished from these existing techniques.
- The paper's methodology section lacks clarity in explaining the classifier initialization and test-time ensembling techniques, which could confuse readers.
- The paper's experimental results are not sufficiently robust, with some results showing marginal improvements over existing methods, and the method's performance on certain datasets like CIFAR-100-LT is not as strong as on others.

**Questions:**
- How does the proposed method compare in terms of computational cost and training time with other parameter-efficient fine-tuning methods?
- Can the method be applied to other types of models beyond vision transformers, such as convolutional neural networks (CNNs)?
- Could the authors provide more detailed explanations or theoretical justifications for the choice of the test-time ensembling method and the semantic-aware classifier initialization technique?
- How does the proposed method perform when using different types of learnable parameters, such as those introduced by LoRA or AdaptFormer?
- Can the authors clarify the methodology and provide more detailed explanations for the classifier initialization and test-time ensembling techniques?
- Could the authors include more comparisons with other methods that also use test-time ensembling to better understand the relative effectiveness of their approach?
