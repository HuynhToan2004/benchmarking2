 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel approach to audio-text matching through a mini-batch learning-to-match (m-LTM) framework, which utilizes optimal transport metrics and a Mahalanobis distance to bridge the gap between audio and text embeddings. This method aims to enhance the expressiveness of joint embeddings and improve noise tolerance in training data. The framework is tested on various datasets, including AudioCaps, Clotho, and ESC-50, demonstrating its effectiveness in audio-text retrieval and sound event detection. The authors also address the noisy data correspondence problem by introducing a variant using partial optimal transport to mitigate pairing uncertainty in training data.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, clear, and easy to follow, with a comprehensive review of related work.
- The proposed method is innovative, particularly in its application of optimal transport metrics and Mahalanobis distance to enhance the expressiveness of joint embeddings and improve noise tolerance.
- The experiments conducted are extensive, involving multiple datasets and comparisons with state-of-the-art models, demonstrating the effectiveness of the proposed method.
- The paper provides a novel approach to the audio-text retrieval problem, which is a significant and understudied area in the field of multimodal learning.

**Weaknesses:**
- The novelty of the approach is somewhat limited, as it builds upon existing methods like LTM and m-LTM, which have been previously used in similar contexts.
- The paper lacks detailed explanations and justifications for certain components of the method, such as the parameterization of the ground metric and the choice of hyperparameters.
- There is a lack of comparative analysis with more recent and relevant works, which could have strengthened the paper's position in the current literature.
- The paper could benefit from more comprehensive evaluations, including zero-shot learning and transfer learning experiments, to demonstrate the generalizability of the proposed method.
- The method's reliance on optimal transport metrics and Mahalanobis distance, which are not specific to audio-text matching, may limit its applicability to other domains.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors provide a more detailed explanation of the parameterization of the ground metric and the choice of hyperparameters?
- How does the proposed method perform in zero-shot learning and transfer learning scenarios?
- Can the authors clarify the distinction between the proposed method and existing methods, particularly in terms of novelty and practical application?
- Could the authors expand on the experimental setup and results to include a broader range of datasets and scenarios, such as different noise ratios and different types of audio-text matching tasks?
- How does the proposed method compare to other state-of-the-art methods in terms of computational efficiency and scalability, especially when dealing with large-scale datasets?
