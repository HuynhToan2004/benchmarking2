 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel diffusion model for generating crystal structures, utilizing a periodic table-based representation termed UniMat. This model is designed to handle large and complex crystal structures by effectively capturing inter-atomic relationships. The model is evaluated against existing benchmarks and compared with a diffusion-based model, demonstrating improved performance. It also proposes new metrics for evaluating generative models of materials, including per-composition formation energy and stability with respect to convex hulls from Density Function Theory (DFT). The model's scalability is highlighted, as it can handle up to millions of crystal structures, outperforming random structure search in discovering new stable materials.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel diffusion model for generating crystal structures, which is a significant advancement in the field of material science.
- The model is based on a periodic table representation, which is a novel approach that effectively captures inter-atomic relationships, enhancing the model's ability to generate realistic and diverse crystal structures.
- The paper proposes new metrics for evaluating generative models of materials, including per-composition formation energy and stability with respect to convex hulls from Density Function Theory (DFT), which are crucial for assessing the quality and novelty of generated materials.
- The scalability of the model is highlighted, with the ability to handle up to millions of crystal structures, which is a significant improvement over existing methods.
- The paper is well-written, clearly presenting the motivation, methodology, and results, making it accessible to a broad audience.
- The proposed method shows superior performance compared to existing methods, demonstrating its effectiveness and utility in the field.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed discussion on the scalability of the model, particularly in terms of computational resources and training time, which could limit its practical application in real-world scenarios.
- The evaluation of the model is limited to a few datasets, which might not adequately demonstrate its performance across a broader range of materials.
- There is a need for more detailed comparisons with existing methods, including a discussion on the advantages and disadvantages of the proposed model compared to these methods.
- The paper does not provide a detailed analysis of the generated materials, particularly in terms of their novelty and practical applicability, which could limit the understanding of the model's impact.
- The paper does not discuss the limitations of the proposed model, which could provide valuable insights into its applicability and potential areas for improvement.

**Questions:**
- Could you provide more details on the scalability of the model, particularly in terms of computational resources and training time?
- How does the model perform on a broader range of materials, especially those that are not well-represented in the datasets used in the study?
- Can you discuss the advantages and disadvantages of the proposed model compared to existing methods, particularly in terms of performance, scalability, and practical applicability?
- How does the model handle the generation of materials with complex structures or those that deviate significantly from known materials?
- Could you provide more details on the novelty and practical applicability of the generated materials, particularly in terms of their potential for real-world applications?
- How does the model compare to other generative models for material discovery, particularly in terms of performance and computational efficiency?
