 **Summary:**
The paper investigates optimization on the generalized Stiefel manifold, a smooth manifold defined by the constraint X^T B X = I, where B is a random matrix. It proposes a novel landing algorithm that iteratively approximates this constraint without exact enforcement, using a smooth merit function. Theoretical analysis demonstrates the convergence of this algorithm to critical points, and empirical evaluations are provided to support the claims. The method is compared against other optimization techniques, showing comparable performance in certain scenarios. However, concerns are raised about the practical relevance of the stochastic case, the clarity of the theoretical results, and the overall contribution of the paper.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, making the content accessible and easy to follow.
- The proposed algorithm is innovative, offering a novel approach to optimization on the generalized Stiefel manifold.
- Theoretical analysis is robust, providing a solid foundation for the proposed method.
- Empirical evaluations are comprehensive, supporting the theoretical claims with practical evidence.
- The paper addresses an important problem in optimization, which has significant applications in various fields.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a clear justification for the practical relevance of the stochastic case, which is a significant focus of the study.
- Theoretical results are not clearly presented, making it difficult to understand the main contributions and the implications of the findings.
- The paper could benefit from a more detailed discussion on the limitations and potential negative societal impacts of the proposed method.
- The paper does not sufficiently differentiate its contributions from existing works, particularly in the area of landing methods on the Stiefel manifold.
- The experimental results do not convincingly demonstrate the superiority of the proposed method over existing approaches.
- There is a lack of clarity in the presentation of the main results and the assumptions used, which could lead to confusion about the applicability and effectiveness of the proposed method.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors provide more details on the practical relevance of the stochastic case, possibly with examples or scenarios where this type of optimization is particularly relevant?
- How does the proposed method compare to existing works, particularly in terms of computational efficiency and theoretical contributions?
- Can the authors clarify the theoretical results, particularly the assumptions and the implications of these assumptions on the practical applicability of the method?
- How does the proposed method perform in scenarios where the constraint matrix B is not random but rather deterministic?
- Could the authors elaborate on the potential negative societal impacts of the proposed method, and how these might be mitigated or addressed?
