 **Summary:**
The paper introduces SAVO (Successive Actors for Value Optimization), a novel actor-critic algorithm designed to address the challenges of local optima and non-stationary landscapes in actor-critic methods. SAVO trains a sequence of actor-critic pairs, each successively improving upon the previous pair by conditioning on the actions selected by the previous actors. This approach is aimed at optimizing the action-value landscape more effectively, which is particularly beneficial in complex discrete and continuous action space tasks. The paper provides empirical evidence of SAVO's effectiveness through experiments in various environments, demonstrating its ability to outperform baseline methods. However, the paper is criticized for its limited novelty and lack of a strong theoretical foundation.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel approach by training a sequence of actor-critic pairs, which is a significant departure from traditional methods.
- The empirical results are strong, demonstrating the effectiveness of the proposed method in various environments, including continuous and discrete action spaces.
- The paper is well-written, making it easy to follow, and provides a clear motivation for the research.
- The authors have conducted extensive ablation studies, which help in understanding the impact of different components of the proposed method.
- The paper addresses a significant problem in actor-critic methods, which is the non-stationary and non-convex nature of the action-value landscape.
- The experiments are well-designed, covering a range of environments and tasks, which helps in validating the effectiveness of the proposed method.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a strong theoretical foundation, which could strengthen the argument for the proposed method.
- The novelty of the approach is limited, as it is a simple modification of existing methods like TD3, which may not be perceived as a significant advancement.
- The paper does not adequately discuss the limitations of the proposed method, which could help in understanding its applicability and effectiveness in different scenarios.
- The experimental setup and results presentation could be improved, particularly in terms of clarity and detail.
- There is a lack of comparison with more recent and relevant methods, which could provide a better context for the evaluation of the proposed method.
- The paper could benefit from a more detailed discussion on the computational efficiency and memory requirements of the proposed method, especially in large-scale applications.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors clarify the role and impact of the k-nearest neighbors in the proposed method?
- How does the proposed method compare to more recent and relevant methods, such as those mentioned in the review?
- What are the specific advantages of using SAVO over other ensemble-based methods, such as those mentioned in the review?
- How does the proposed method perform in environments with sparse rewards, and what modifications, if any, are necessary for such environments?
- Can the authors provide more details on the computational efficiency and memory requirements of the proposed method, especially in large-scale applications?
- How does the proposed method handle the non-stationary and non-convex nature of the action-value landscape in practical scenarios?
