 **Summary:**
The paper introduces the Hierarchical Contact Mesh Transformer (HCMT), a novel method for simulating flexible body dynamics in mesh-based models. HCMT utilizes a hierarchical structure to efficiently handle long-range interactions, which are crucial for modeling complex physical systems. It extends the capabilities of existing models like MGN by integrating contact edges and a hierarchical mesh transformer. The paper also proposes a new dataset, the Impact Plate, which is specifically designed for evaluating the performance of HCMT. The method's effectiveness is demonstrated through experiments, showing improvements over existing baselines. However, concerns are raised regarding the novelty of the approach, the complexity of the method, and the clarity of the presentation.

**Strengths:**
- The paper addresses an important and challenging problem of modeling flexible body dynamics in mesh-based models, which is crucial for various applications such as product design and manufacturing.
- The proposed method, Hierarchical Contact Mesh Transformer (HCMT), is innovative in its use of hierarchical structures and contact mesh transformers to handle long-range dependencies and collision effects in flexible body dynamics.
- The paper introduces a new dataset, Impact Plate, which is specifically designed for evaluating the performance of HCMT, providing a valuable resource for future research in this area.
- The experimental results demonstrate the effectiveness of HCMT in outperforming other baseline models in static, rigid dynamics, and flexible dynamics systems.
- The paper is well-written, clear, and easy to follow, making it accessible to a broad audience.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a thorough discussion on the limitations of the proposed method, particularly in terms of its applicability to different types of flexible body dynamics and its generalizability to more complex or realistic scenarios.
- The novelty of the method is questionable, as it appears to be a simple extension of existing models like MGN with additional contact edge features and a hierarchical mesh transformer.
- The paper does not sufficiently discuss the computational complexity of HCMT, which is crucial for understanding its practical applicability and efficiency.
- There are concerns about the clarity and organization of the paper, particularly in the presentation of the methodology and the experimental results.
- The paper does not adequately compare HCMT with state-of-the-art methods, which could have provided a better understanding of its advantages and limitations.
- The paper does not sufficiently address the potential societal impacts of the proposed method, which could be significant in applications such as product design and manufacturing.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors clarify the novelty of the proposed method and how it differs from existing models like MGN?
- How does HCMT handle different types of flexible body dynamics, particularly those that are not as simple as the ones used in the experiments?
- What are the specific advantages of using HCMT over other methods, particularly in terms of computational efficiency and accuracy?
- Can the authors provide more details on the computational complexity of HCMT and how it scales with the size and complexity of the system being modeled?
- How does HCMT handle the simulation of complex or realistic scenarios, such as those involving multiple objects or more complex physical interactions?
- Could the authors discuss the potential societal impacts of the proposed method, particularly in applications such as product design and manufacturing?
- How does HCMT compare to other state-of-the-art methods in terms of accuracy, efficiency, and generalizability?
