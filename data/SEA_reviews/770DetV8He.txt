 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel approach to retrosynthesis prediction using a Markov Bridge Model, which approximates the dependency between two intractable discrete distributions. This model, termed RetroBridge, operates by learning the dependency between two discrete distributions using a finite sample of coupled data points. The authors claim that this method outperforms diffusion models in the context of learning the dependency between two intractable discrete distributions. The model is evaluated on the USPTO-50k dataset, showing promising results compared to existing methods. Despite its innovative approach, the paper has been criticized for its limited evaluation, lack of comprehensive baseline comparisons, and potential issues with the model's interpretability and scalability.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel approach to retrosynthesis prediction using a Markov Bridge Model, which is a significant advancement in the field.
- The model is trained using a novel variational lower bound loss, which is not commonly used in this context.
- The authors have conducted extensive experiments to demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed method, showing improvements over existing models in terms of both exact match accuracy and round-trip accuracy.
- The paper is well-written, making it easy to follow and understand the proposed methodology and its implementation.
- The use of a novel model to address the retrosynthesis problem is highlighted as a significant contribution to the field.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a comprehensive evaluation of the model's performance across different datasets and scenarios, which limits the generalizability of the results.
- There is a lack of detailed explanation regarding the model's interpretability and the reasoning behind its predictions, which is crucial for understanding the model's effectiveness in practical applications.
- The paper does not include a discussion on the limitations of the proposed method, which could provide valuable insights into its applicability and potential improvements.
- The scalability of the proposed method is not adequately addressed, especially in terms of its applicability to larger or more complex datasets.
- The paper lacks a detailed comparison with existing diffusion models, which could provide a clearer understanding of the model's advantages and disadvantages.
- There are concerns about the model's efficiency in terms of computational resources and training time, which could limit its practical implementation.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors provide more details on how the model's interpretability is ensured and how it can be improved?
- How does the model perform when applied to different datasets or scenarios, and what are the implications of these variations on its performance?
- Can the authors discuss the limitations of the proposed method and suggest potential avenues for future research to address these limitations?
- How does the proposed method compare in terms of efficiency and scalability with existing diffusion models, and what are the specific advantages of using the Markov Bridge Model over these models?
- Can the authors provide more examples of the model's predictions and the reasoning behind these predictions to enhance the understanding of the model's capabilities?
