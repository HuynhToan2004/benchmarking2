 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel method, SingleInsert, for inverting single images to textual embeddings, addressing the challenges of foreground-background entanglement and language drift in existing image-to-text (I2T) inversion models. The approach involves a two-stage training process, where the first stage focuses on the foreground and the second on the background, utilizing a semantic loss to prevent language drift. The method is evaluated against various baselines and shows promising results in terms of both qualitative and quantitative assessments. However, concerns were raised about the novelty of the approach, as similar methods have been previously explored, and the method's generalization to more complex or diverse scenarios remains unclear.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, making it easy to follow and understand the methodology and results.
- The proposed method, SingleInsert, is simple yet effective, demonstrating a good balance between visual fidelity and editing flexibility.
- The paper introduces a novel approach to inverting a single image into a textual embedding, which is a significant contribution to the field of image-to-text inversion.
- The evaluation of the proposed method is comprehensive, covering various aspects such as image-alignment, text-alignment, and editing flexibility.
- The method's ability to generate results that align well with the condition prompts and maintain the fidelity of the target concept is commendable.
- The paper includes both qualitative and quantitative results, which provide a robust evaluation of the method's performance.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed discussion on the limitations of the proposed method, which is crucial for understanding its scope and applicability.
- The novelty of the approach is questionable as similar methods have been previously explored, such as using a foreground loss to prevent the learned embedding from reproducing the irrelevant background.
- The method's generalization to more complex or diverse scenarios is not thoroughly discussed, which raises concerns about its applicability in real-world settings.
- The paper could benefit from a more extensive comparison with state-of-the-art methods to better establish its position within the current research landscape.
- The paper does not sufficiently address how the method handles different types of images or the impact of varying image qualities on the results.
- There are some issues with the clarity and presentation of the paper, particularly in the figures and the descriptions of the methodology.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors clarify the differences between the proposed method and previous methods that use a foreground loss?
- How does the proposed method handle different types of images, especially those that are not as clear or detailed as those used in the experiments?
- What are the potential applications of the SingleInsert method beyond the examples provided in the paper?
- Can the authors discuss the limitations of their method and how these might affect its practical implementation?
- How does the method handle the generalization to more complex or diverse scenarios, and what steps could be taken to address these limitations?
- Could the authors provide more detailed examples or case studies to illustrate the method's performance in real-world settings?
- How does the method handle the potential for the learned embedding to influence the background, especially in scenarios where the background is not explicitly masked?
