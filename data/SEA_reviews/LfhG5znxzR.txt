 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel method for enhancing the interpretability of neural networks by quantizing continuous features into discrete codes, termed codebook features. This approach, which involves training a network with a vector quantization bottleneck at each layer, allows for the identification of discrete features that can be used to guide the network's behavior. The method is validated through experiments on various datasets, including a finite state machine dataset and two natural language datasets, demonstrating its effectiveness in improving interpretability. The authors also discuss the application of this method to language models, showing that it can be integrated into existing models without significant performance degradation.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, with clear and intuitive explanations of the methodology and results, making it accessible and easy to follow.
- The proposed method of using codebooks to enhance interpretability is innovative and practical, offering a method for gaining insights into the internal workings of neural networks.
- The paper presents a robust evaluation of the proposed method, demonstrating its effectiveness through experiments on different datasets and network architectures.
- The methodology is well-articulated, with a clear description of the training procedure and the application of the method to various settings, including language models.
- The paper provides a comprehensive set of experiments, including ablation studies and comparisons to other interpretability methods, which support the claims made about the effectiveness of the proposed method.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a direct comparison with existing methods for improving interpretability in neural networks, such as those using attention weights or saliency maps.
- There is a lack of discussion on the computational complexity of the proposed method, particularly in terms of the number of additional parameters introduced by the codebook.
- The paper does not adequately address the issue of interpretability in the context of larger, more complex models, such as those with more than 410M parameters.
- The paper does not provide a detailed discussion on the limitations of the proposed method, particularly in terms of its applicability to different types of neural networks and datasets.
- There is a lack of discussion on the generalizability of the findings, particularly in terms of how the method might perform on different types of tasks or in different domains.
- The paper does not include a detailed discussion on the ethical implications of the proposed method, which could be a significant concern for some applications.
