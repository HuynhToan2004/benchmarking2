 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel approach to fine-tuning large language models (LLMs) using a mixture of experts (MoE) architecture, specifically combining it with parameter-efficient fine-tuning (PEFT) methods such as IA3 and LoRA. The MoE architecture is designed to be lightweight, with each expert consisting of a single PEFT layer, which allows for efficient parameter updates while maintaining comparable performance to full fine-tuning. The methodology is tested on the T5 model across various parameter sizes, showing competitive results against state-of-the-art models like T0. The paper also explores different routing strategies and hyperparameters to optimize performance, although it lacks a comprehensive comparison with other PEFT methods and does not include experiments on decoder-only models.

**Strengths:**
- The paper presents a novel approach by combining MoE with PEFT, which is a significant advancement in the field of large language models (LLMs) fine-tuning.
- The methodology is well-explained, making it easy to understand and replicate, and the experimental results are robust, showing consistent performance improvements over PEFT methods.
- The use of lightweight experts in MoE architecture significantly reduces the number of parameters that need to be updated, making it a practical solution for parameter-efficient fine-tuning.
- The paper includes comprehensive ablation studies that systematically evaluate various MoE architectures and PEFT strategies, providing a detailed analysis of their impact on performance.
- The proposed method achieves comparable results to full fine-tuning while updating a tiny fraction of the model parameters, demonstrating its efficiency and effectiveness.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a thorough comparison with other PEFT methods, particularly those that have been shown to perform well in similar settings, such as adaptors and LoRA.
- The experimental setup is limited to encoder-decoder models, and it would be beneficial to include experiments with decoder-only models to validate the generalizability of the findings.
- The paper does not provide a detailed comparison of training and inference costs between MoE and other PEFT methods, which could help in understanding the practical implications of the proposed approach.
- The paper does not include a discussion on the limitations of the proposed method, which is crucial for understanding its applicability and potential drawbacks.
- There are minor issues with the presentation, such as the use of abbreviations before they are introduced, and some figures and tables are not clearly explained or labeled.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors provide a detailed comparison of training and inference costs between MoE and other PEFT methods, particularly in terms of parameter updates and computational efficiency?
- How does the proposed method compare with other PEFT methods, especially those that have been shown to perform well in similar settings?
- Could the authors include experiments with decoder-only models to validate the generalizability of the findings?
- How does the proposed method perform when compared to full fine-tuning, especially in terms of parameter updates and computational efficiency?
- What are the limitations of the proposed method, and how do these impact its practical applicability and performance?
- Could the authors clarify the use of abbreviations and ensure that all figures and tables are clearly labeled and explained?
