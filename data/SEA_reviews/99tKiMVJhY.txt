 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel method for handling decentralized, partially observable multi-agent reinforcement learning (MARL) problems using mean field control (MFC). The authors propose a method for approximating mean field control dynamics in a decentralized, partially observable setting, and provide theoretical guarantees for the convergence of the mean field limit. The paper also introduces a policy gradient-based algorithm for training policies in this setting, supported by empirical results demonstrating the method's effectiveness in various swarming tasks. The theoretical contributions include the derivation of the dynamic programming principle for the proposed Dec-POMFC model and the development of an algorithm for training policies in this setting. The paper's empirical results, although promising, are somewhat limited in scope, and the paper could benefit from a broader range of experiments to better validate the proposed method's applicability and effectiveness.

**Strengths:**
- The paper addresses a significant and relevant problem in the field of multi-agent reinforcement learning (MARL), focusing on the decentralized, partially observable setting which is challenging and less explored.
- The authors provide a clear and well-structured presentation of their ideas, supported by theoretical results that are mathematically rigorous.
- The paper introduces a novel approach to mean field control in a decentralized, partially observable setting, which is an interesting and significant contribution to the field.
- The methodology is supported by empirical results that demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed algorithm in various swarming tasks, showing that it can achieve similar performance to existing state-of-the-art methods like IPPO and MAPPO.
- The paper provides a clear and detailed explanation of the theoretical results, which are supported by empirical results showing the convergence of the mean field limit to the solution of the MFC problem.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper could benefit from a more detailed discussion of related works, particularly in the area of mean field games (MFG) and mean field control (MFC), to better position the current work within the existing literature.
- The empirical results presented in the paper are somewhat limited in scope, focusing mainly on the Kuramoto and Vicsek swarming models, which might not fully demonstrate the general applicability of the proposed method.
- The paper could benefit from a more thorough discussion of the assumptions and limitations of the proposed method, particularly in terms of its applicability to different types of swarming models and its scalability to larger, more complex systems.
- The paper could be improved by providing more detailed explanations and justifications for certain theoretical results and assumptions, particularly in the areas of policy gradient approximation and the convergence of the mean field limit.
- The paper could benefit from a broader range of experiments to validate the proposed method's effectiveness in different scenarios and to demonstrate its general applicability.
