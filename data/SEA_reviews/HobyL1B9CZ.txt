 **Summary:**
The paper introduces Chain-of-Experts (CoE), a novel multi-agent cooperative framework that leverages large language models (LLMs) to solve complex operations research (OR) problems. CoE employs a conductor agent that orchestrates a chain of experts, each specialized in different roles such as terminology interpretation, modeling, programming, and code review. The framework is evaluated on two datasets, demonstrating superior performance over existing LLM-based methods. The authors also introduce a new benchmark dataset, ComplexOR, to facilitate further research in this area. Despite its innovative approach and promising results, the paper is criticized for its limited novelty, unclear explanations, and insufficient comparisons with other state-of-the-art methods.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel approach, Chain-of-Experts (CoE), which effectively utilizes a conductor agent to manage a chain of experts, each with distinct roles and expertise, to solve complex operations research problems.
- The CoE framework is evaluated on two datasets, demonstrating superior performance over existing LLM-based methods, showcasing its effectiveness in handling complex OR problems.
- The introduction of a new benchmark dataset, ComplexOR, will be beneficial for future research in this area.
- The paper is well-written and provides a detailed description of the proposed method, making it easy to follow and understand.
- The use of a multi-agent approach with a conductor agent and a chain of experts is a promising direction for solving complex problems, particularly in operations research.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a clear explanation of the reflection mechanism and its integration with the CoE framework, which is crucial for understanding the system's operation.
- The novelty of the CoE framework is limited as it primarily extends existing methods like Solo Performance Prompting by adding additional agents.
- The paper does not adequately compare its method with other state-of-the-art methods, such as those mentioned in references [1][2][3], which could provide a more robust evaluation of the CoE framework.
- The evaluation metrics used in the paper, such as accuracy, CE rate, and RE rate, are not well-explained, which could confuse readers about the effectiveness of the proposed method.
- The paper does not sufficiently address the limitations of the CoE framework, which is crucial for understanding its applicability and scope.
- The paper's methodology and experimental setup are not thoroughly described, making it difficult to replicate the results and understand the method's effectiveness.

**Questions:**
- Can you provide a more detailed explanation of the reflection mechanism and how it integrates with the CoE framework?
- How does the CoE framework compare with other state-of-the-art methods, such as those mentioned in references [1][2][3]?
- Could you clarify the evaluation metrics used in the paper and explain their relevance and effectiveness in evaluating the CoE framework?
- How does the CoE framework handle scenarios where the problem description is not well-defined or contains ambiguities?
- Can you provide more details on the training process of the experts and the conductor, including the types of examples used and the training data sources?
- How does the CoE framework ensure the reliability and accuracy of the answers generated by the experts, especially in scenarios where the problem description is complex or ambiguous?
- Could you explain the rationale behind the choice of GPT-3.5-turbo as the base reasoning model and how this affects the performance of the CoE framework?
