 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a new problem setting for continual learning (CL) termed "Adaptive CL," which focuses on scenarios where tasks may recur over time, necessitating rapid adaptation and knowledge refinement. The authors propose the LEARN algorithm, which integrates a fast learner for quick adaptation to new tasks and a slow learner for refining knowledge on recurring tasks. Theoretical guarantees are provided for this algorithm, including regret bounds and knowledge consistency. Experiments on various datasets demonstrate the effectiveness of the LEARN algorithm, showing superior performance compared to existing methods. The paper also discusses the practical implementation of the algorithm using a Gaussian mixture model (GMM) and provides a scalable implementation for deep learning models.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel problem setting for continual learning (CL) that focuses on the recurrence of tasks, which is a significant and relevant aspect of human learning.
- The proposed algorithm, LEARN, is simple, intuitive, and supported by theoretical guarantees, making it easy to implement and understand.
- The paper is well-written, with clear and concise descriptions of the algorithm, theoretical analysis, and experimental results.
- The experiments conducted demonstrate the effectiveness of the LEARN algorithm, showing improvements over existing methods in various datasets.
- The theoretical analysis provided is solid and convincing, with clear explanations of the algorithm's behavior and its relationship to existing methods.
- The scalable implementation of the LEARN algorithm using a Gaussian mixture model (GMM) allows for efficient training of deep learning models, enhancing its applicability.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed discussion on the relationship between the proposed setting and the task-free CL setting, which could provide a clearer context for the research.
- The experimental results, while promising, are not consistently superior across all datasets and metrics, with some results showing only marginal improvements or even lower performance compared to existing methods.
- The paper could benefit from a more comprehensive evaluation on larger, more complex datasets like ImageNet to validate the effectiveness of the proposed method more robustly.
- There is a need for a more thorough discussion on the limitations and potential negative societal impacts of the proposed method.
- The paper could be improved by including more detailed discussions on the technical challenges and the rationale behind the design choices of the LEARN algorithm.
- The theoretical analysis is limited to the proposed algorithm and does not include a comparison with other existing continual learning algorithms, which could provide a more comprehensive understanding of the algorithm's performance.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors clarify the relationship between the proposed setting and the task-free CL setting, possibly by including a comparison with task-free CL methods?
- How does the LEARN algorithm perform on larger, more complex datasets like ImageNet, and what are the implications of its performance on such datasets?
- Can the authors provide a detailed explanation of the experimental results, particularly the discrepancies between the proposed method and existing methods in certain datasets?
- What are the limitations of the proposed method, and how do these limitations affect its practical applicability?
- How does the proposed method compare to other existing continual learning algorithms in terms of performance and theoretical guarantees?
- Could the authors discuss the potential negative societal impacts of their method and how these impacts could be mitigated?
