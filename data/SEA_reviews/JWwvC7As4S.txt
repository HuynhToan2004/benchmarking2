 **Summary:**
The paper investigates the emergence of Neural Collapse (NC) in deep neural networks (DNNs) under the influence of batch normalization (BN) and weight decay. It provides a theoretical analysis of the conditions necessary for NC to occur, focusing on the near-optimal regime where the loss function is not exactly optimal. The authors introduce a cosine similarity measure to quantify the proximity to NC and demonstrate its effectiveness through experiments on both synthetic and real-world datasets. The paper also discusses the impact of these conditions on the emergence of NC and proposes that batch normalization and weight decay can significantly influence its occurrence.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, with clear and concise sections that effectively convey the main ideas and results.
- The theoretical results are novel and provide a new perspective on the emergence of Neural Collapse (NC) in deep neural networks, particularly in the near-optimal regime.
- The paper is structured around the cosine similarity measure, which is both simple and intuitive, making it a useful tool for understanding the proximity of different networks to the NC structure.
- The theoretical results are supported by experiments on both synthetic and real-world datasets, demonstrating the effectiveness of the proposed measures in achieving NC.
- The paper provides a comprehensive review of related work, effectively situating the current study within the existing literature.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper primarily focuses on the near-optimal regime, which may not be reflective of real-world neural network training scenarios where the optimal loss is often unattainable.
- The theoretical results are based on the layer-peeled model, which may not fully capture the behavior of NC in practical settings where batch normalization and weight decay are applied across multiple layers.
- The experiments are limited to specific datasets and network architectures, which may not fully represent the generalizability of the findings.
- The paper does not discuss the limitations of the theoretical results, particularly how they apply to more realistic settings where batch normalization and weight decay are applied across multiple layers.
- The paper could benefit from a more detailed discussion on the limitations of the theoretical results and how they might be addressed in future work.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors elaborate on how the results would be affected if the network is trained to the optimal loss?
- Is it possible to extend the theoretical results to settings where batch normalization and weight decay are applied across multiple layers?
- How do the results compare to those obtained using other similarity measures, such as the Frobenius norm?
- Can the authors provide a more detailed discussion on the limitations of the theoretical results and how they might be addressed in future work?
- Could the authors clarify the use of the cosine similarity measure in quantifying the proximity to NC, particularly in relation to the results presented in the paper?
