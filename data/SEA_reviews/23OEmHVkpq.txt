 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel approach to unsupervised learning of disentangled representations in Variational Autoencoders (VAEs) by incorporating a topological regularization term, the TopDis loss. This term, based on Representation Topology Divergence (RTD), is designed to ensure topological similarity across different traversals of the latent space. The methodology is evaluated on various datasets such as dSprites, 3D Shapes, 3D Faces, and MPI 3D, showing improvements in disentanglement metrics while maintaining reconstruction quality. The paper also discusses the application of this method to StyleGAN, demonstrating its effectiveness in discovering disentangled directions. However, the paper has been critiqued for its clarity in explaining the topological regularization term and the lack of comprehensive comparisons with existing methods.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel approach to learning disentangled representations using a topological regularization term, which is a significant advancement in the field.
- The proposed method is evaluated on multiple datasets, including dSprites, 3D Shapes, 3D Faces, and MPI 3D, demonstrating its effectiveness across different scenarios.
- The paper is well-written, making it easy to follow and understand, with a clear presentation of the proposed methodology and its application.
- The introduction of the topological regularization term is innovative and has the potential to significantly impact the field of disentangled representation learning.
- The methodology is demonstrated to be effective in discovering disentangled directions in pretrained StyleGAN, which is a notable achievement.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a comprehensive comparison with existing methods, which is crucial for establishing the novelty and effectiveness of the proposed approach.
- The explanation of the topological regularization term and its implications for disentanglement is not clear, which could hinder understanding and replication of the results.
- The paper does not provide a detailed discussion on the limitations of the proposed method, which is essential for a complete assessment of its applicability and effectiveness.
- There is a lack of quantitative evaluation on the disentanglement metrics, which could strengthen the claims made about the method's effectiveness.
- The paper does not adequately address the scalability of the method to high-dimensional datasets or complex data distributions, which are crucial for real-world applications.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors provide a more detailed explanation of the topological regularization term and its implications for disentanglement?
- How does the proposed method compare to other existing methods in terms of performance and efficiency?
- What are the specific limitations of the proposed method, and how do these affect its applicability and effectiveness?
- How does the method perform in terms of quantitative metrics such as MIG, FactorVAE score, SAP score, and DCI disentanglement score?
- Can the authors clarify the role of the gradient orthogonalization in the method and its impact on the reconstruction quality?
- How does the method handle the scalability issues when applied to high-dimensional datasets or complex data distributions?
- What are the potential applications of the method beyond the domains explored in the paper, and how might these applications benefit from the disentangled representation learning?
