 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel method for 3D scene reconstruction using a generalizable Neural Fields Prior (NFP) that leverages depth-map inputs to reconstruct both geometry and texture. The method involves a two-stage training process where the first stage learns a depth-to-SDF model and a color-to-texture model, while the second stage fine-tunes these models with additional RGB-D images. The NFPs are shown to outperform existing methods in terms of reconstruction accuracy and efficiency, with the capability to handle large-scale scenes and novel view synthesis. The method's novelty lies in its ability to generalize across different scenes and provide a fast, efficient alternative to traditional neural fields.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel approach to 3D scene reconstruction using a generalizable Neural Fields Prior (NFP) that leverages depth-map inputs to reconstruct both geometry and texture, which is a significant advancement in the field.
- The method is designed to be fast and efficient, capable of handling large-scale scenes and achieving state-of-the-art (SOTA) performance in terms of reconstruction accuracy and efficiency.
- The paper is well-written, with clear figures and a logical structure that makes the methodology and results easy to follow.
- The paper provides comprehensive evaluations and comparisons with existing methods, demonstrating the superiority of the proposed method in various aspects such as reconstruction accuracy, optimization time, and novel view synthesis.
- The method's ability to generalize across different scenes and its capability to handle large-scale scenes make it a promising tool for real-world applications.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed discussion on the limitations of the proposed method, which could help in understanding its applicability and potential drawbacks.
- The paper does not provide sufficient details on the training and inference times of the proposed method, which are crucial for practical deployment.
- The paper does not include a comprehensive comparison with the latest state-of-the-art methods, which could affect the perceived novelty and effectiveness of the proposed method.
- The paper could benefit from a more detailed discussion on the technical contributions and the novelty of the method, particularly in comparison to existing works such as Go-Surf and Neural-RGBD.
- The paper lacks quantitative results for novel view synthesis, which could provide a more comprehensive evaluation of the method's capabilities.
