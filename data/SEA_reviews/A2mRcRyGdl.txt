 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel approach for learning semantic fields from monocular videos, leveraging flow features to capture motion information. This method is designed to enhance the understanding of dynamic scenes by integrating semantic information with flow features, which is then used to predict semantics of each flow. The authors propose a flow attention module to extract motion information from flow features and a semantic network to output semantic logits of each flow. These logits are integrated with volume densities in the viewing direction to supervise the flow features with semantic labels on video frames. The paper also introduces a new dataset, the Semantic Dynamic Scene Dataset, which is manually annotated with pixel-wise semantic labels for each video frame. The method's effectiveness is demonstrated through various tasks such as semantic completion, tracking, and instance-level scene editing.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel approach for learning semantic fields from monocular videos, which is a significant advancement in the field.
- The method is well-motivated, clearly written, and easy to follow, with a clear explanation of the motivation and the proposed method.
- The paper provides a detailed explanation of the dataset and implementation details, which are crucial for reproducibility and understanding the method's effectiveness.
- The experimental results demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed method, showing improvements over existing methods like DynNeRF and MonoNeRF.
- The paper is well-organized, with a clear structure and a detailed discussion of related work, which helps in positioning the research within the current state of the art.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper does not compare its method with the most recent dynamic NeRF methods, which could provide a more robust evaluation of the proposed approach.
- The novelty of the method is limited, as it primarily builds on existing works like DynNeRF and MonoNeRF, with the main difference being the use of flow features instead of point features.
- The paper lacks a thorough discussion on the limitations of the proposed method, which is crucial for understanding its applicability and potential shortcomings.
- The experimental results are not convincing, with some results showing significant overlap between semantic labels, which could indicate the method's inability to distinguish between different classes effectively.
- The paper does not sufficiently address the computational complexity of the proposed method, which could be a significant concern for real-time applications.
- The paper does not adequately discuss the generalizability of the method across different types of dynamic scenes, which could limit its applicability.

**Questions:**
- How does the proposed method compare with the most recent dynamic NeRF methods, such as those mentioned in the review?
- Could the authors clarify the role and impact of the pretrained model in the proposed method?
- How does the proposed method handle the generalization to novel scenes, especially in terms of the accuracy of semantic labels?
- What are the computational requirements of the proposed method, and how do these compare with existing methods in terms of computational efficiency?
- How does the proposed method handle the computational complexity of the flow attention module, and what are the implications for real-time applications?
- Could the authors provide more details on the training and testing datasets, including the number of frames and the specific scenes used?
- How does the proposed method handle the semantic consistency constraint, and what are the implications for the accuracy and reliability of the semantic labels?
