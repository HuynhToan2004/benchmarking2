 **Summary:**
The paper introduces BandVAE, a method designed to align interest factors from mutually disentangled modalities, particularly focusing on text and rating data in recommendation systems. BandVAE formulates the interest factor alignment problem as an optimal transport problem, employing the Sinkhorn algorithm to find optimal mappings between probability masses. The method integrates alignment probability-guided regularization and barycentric mapping to transfer knowledge between discovered factors. Experiments conducted on real-world datasets demonstrate that BandVAE outperforms existing models, showcasing its effectiveness in aligning interest factors and enhancing recommendation accuracy.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, clear, and easy to follow, making complex concepts accessible to readers.
- The motivation behind the work is compelling, focusing on the alignment of interest factors from mutually disentangled modalities, which is a significant and relevant problem in recommendation systems.
- The proposed method, BandVAE, introduces innovative approaches such as optimal transport-based alignment and barycentric mapping, which are novel and well-explained.
- The paper includes extensive experiments that demonstrate the effectiveness of BandVAE in outperforming existing models, providing valuable insights into the performance of the proposed method.
- The use of the Sinkhorn algorithm for optimal transport and the barycentric mapping strategy for transferring knowledge between factors are highlighted as key contributions.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a clear explanation of why the Sinkhorn algorithm was chosen over other potential methods for optimal transport.
- The novelty of the method is questioned as some components, such as the use of optimal transport for alignment and barycentric mapping for transferring knowledge, have been previously explored.
- The paper does not adequately address the limitations of the proposed method, which could include issues related to the scalability, generalizability, or interpretability of the findings.
- The method's reliance on textual content for disentanglement might limit its applicability in scenarios where textual data is not available or is of low quality.
- The paper could benefit from a more detailed discussion on the alignment of interest factors and how it impacts the interpretability of user preferences.

**Questions:**
- Could you clarify the rationale behind choosing the Sinkhorn algorithm over other potential methods for optimal transport?
- How does the proposed method handle the potential for overfitting, especially in scenarios where the training data is limited?
- Could you provide more details on the computational complexity of the proposed method and how it scales with the size of the datasets used?
- How does the method handle the alignment of interest factors across different modalities, and what are the implications for the interpretability of user preferences?
- Can you discuss the potential of extending the method to handle more than two modalities, such as image data in addition to text and ratings?
- In the context of the experimental results, could you elaborate on the observed differences in performance across different datasets and metrics?
