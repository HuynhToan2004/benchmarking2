 **Summary:**
The paper introduces PROOF, a novel method for Class-Incremental Learning (CIL) with Vision-Language Models (VLMs). PROOF addresses the challenges of catastrophic forgetting and the integration of multimodal information by employing task-specific projections and a fusion module. The method leverages the strong zero-shot performance of VLMs by freezing the image and text encoders and introducing learnable projections to adapt to new tasks. The paper includes extensive experiments across multiple datasets and continual learning scenarios, demonstrating the effectiveness of PROOF in enhancing the performance of VLMs in CIL.

**Strengths:**
- The paper addresses an important and relevant problem in the field of Class-Incremental Learning (CIL) with Vision-Language Models (VLMs), focusing on the challenges of catastrophic forgetting and the integration of multimodal information.
- The proposed method, PROOF, is innovative and well-motivated, providing a clear explanation of its components and their effects on performance.
- The paper is well-written, making it easy to follow, and includes a comprehensive set of experiments that demonstrate the effectiveness of PROOF in various continual learning scenarios.
- The methodology is supported by a thorough analysis of its components, providing insights into the importance of different aspects of the model.
- The paper includes extensive experiments across multiple datasets and continual learning scenarios, demonstrating the effectiveness of PROOF in enhancing the performance of VLMs in CIL.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed comparison with other state-of-the-art methods in terms of computational efficiency and resource requirements, which could affect the practical deployment of the proposed method.
- The paper does not provide a clear comparison with other VLM-based methods in CIL, which could help in understanding the unique advantages of PROOF over other approaches.
- The paper could benefit from a more detailed discussion on the limitations of the proposed method, particularly in terms of its scalability and applicability to larger, more complex datasets.
- The paper does not sufficiently address the issue of forgetting in the context of continual learning, which is a critical aspect of the problem it aims to solve.
- The paper does not include a detailed discussion on the potential negative societal impacts of the proposed method, which is a significant omission.

**Questions:**

- How does the proposed method compare with other VLM-based methods in CIL, particularly in terms of performance and efficiency?
- Could the authors provide a more detailed analysis of the limitations of the proposed method, particularly in terms of its scalability and applicability to larger, more complex datasets?

- Could the authors include a discussion on the potential negative societal impacts of the proposed method and how these impacts might be mitigated or addressed?
