 **Summary:**
The paper introduces DeTrans, a novel pre-trained model protection framework designed to prevent both unauthorized inference and cross-domain transfer of deep neural networks (DNNs). It employs bi-level optimization to protect critical weights and generates auxiliary domains to optimize model performance. The framework aims to reduce accuracy on the source domain to random guessing levels and minimize transferability to target domains. The paper includes experiments that demonstrate DeTrans's effectiveness in reducing transferability and accuracy, though it faces criticism for its limited scope and lack of comprehensive evaluation across various domains and model sizes.

**Strengths:**
- The paper addresses the critical issue of protecting pre-trained models against unauthorized inference and cross-domain transfer, which is highly relevant given the increasing use of machine learning as a service.
- It introduces a novel approach using bi-level optimization to protect critical weights, which is a significant advancement in the field.
- The paper is well-organized, easy to follow, and provides detailed descriptions of the proposed method, making it accessible and understandable.
- The use of a trusted execution environment (TEE) for secure storage of critical model weights is a practical and innovative solution to prevent unauthorized access.
- The paper includes a comprehensive evaluation that demonstrates the effectiveness of the proposed method in reducing transferability and accuracy, which is crucial for protecting intellectual property.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed comparison with existing methods, particularly in terms of the impact on model accuracy and transferability, which could have strengthened the argument for the effectiveness of the proposed method.
- The assumption that the target domain is similar to the source domain might not hold true in practical scenarios, which could limit the applicability of the proposed method.
- The paper does not adequately address the limitations of the proposed method, such as the potential for attackers to reverse-engineer the model using the protected weights stored in the TEE.
- The experimental results are limited to specific datasets and model sizes, which might not generalize well to other scenarios.
- The paper does not provide a clear explanation of how the method would perform with larger models or different types of datasets, which could affect the practical applicability of the proposed solution.
- The paper lacks a detailed discussion on the computational cost and the scalability of the proposed method, which are critical factors for real-world deployment.

**Questions:**
- How does the proposed method perform when the target domain is significantly different from the source domain?
- Can you provide a detailed comparison of the computational cost and scalability of the proposed method with existing solutions?
- How does the proposed method handle scenarios where the attacker has access to both the protected model and the auxiliary domains?
- Can you clarify the role and impact of the auxiliary domains in the proposed method?
- How does the proposed method ensure that the protected model remains effective against different types of attacks, such as black-box attacks?
- Could you provide more details on how the method would perform with larger models or different types of datasets, and discuss the potential challenges and limitations in these scenarios?
