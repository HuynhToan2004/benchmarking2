 **Summary:**
The paper introduces Targeted Negative Training (TNT), a method aimed at updating language models to avoid undesirable outputs while minimally altering their behavior. TNT uses negative examples generated by the model to adjust the model's behavior, optimizing for minimal changes in distribution. The method is evaluated against baselines on two tasks, showing that TNT can reduce unwanted behavior while maintaining model performance. However, the paper has been critiqued for its limited evaluation, lack of comparison to state-of-the-art methods, and insufficient clarity in explaining the method's technical details.
