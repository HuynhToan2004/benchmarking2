 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel approach to network pruning, termed Bi-level Fair Pruning (BiFP), which addresses fairness concerns by integrating fairness constraints into the pruning process. BiFP is formulated as a bi-level optimization problem, with the lower level optimizing weights and the upper level optimizing the pruning mask, ensuring fairness across different demographic groups. The paper demonstrates that BiFP maintains performance and fairness, showing superiority over baseline methods in certain scenarios. However, concerns are raised about the practical applicability of the method due to its high computational cost and limited scalability to larger datasets or more complex models. The method's effectiveness is also questioned when fairness constraints are not as stringent, and the paper's contribution is seen as incremental rather than groundbreaking.

**Strengths:**
- The paper addresses a significant and relevant problem of fairness in network pruning, which is crucial for ensuring fairness in machine learning models.
- The proposed method, Bi-level Fair Pruning (BiFP), is well-articulated and clearly explained, making it accessible to readers.
- The paper is well-structured, with clear explanations of the methodology and experiments, which support the claims made.
- The use of a bi-level optimization framework to tackle fairness in model pruning is innovative and adds a new dimension to the field.
- The paper provides extensive experiments and ablation studies that demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed method, showing improvements in fairness and performance.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a thorough comparison with existing methods, particularly those that address fairness in model pruning. This makes it difficult to assess the novelty and effectiveness of the proposed method.
- The experimental setup is limited to small datasets and simple models, which may not adequately demonstrate the practical applicability of the method in real-world scenarios.
- The paper does not address the computational complexity of the proposed method, which could be a significant barrier to its implementation in larger or more complex models.
- The fairness constraints used in the study are very stringent, which might not reflect real-world scenarios where fairness constraints are less stringent.
- The paper does not sufficiently discuss the limitations of the proposed method, such as its scalability and computational efficiency, which could impact its practical implementation.
- The paper's contribution is seen as incremental rather than groundbreaking, as it primarily extends existing methods by adding fairness constraints.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors provide a more detailed comparison with existing methods, particularly those that address fairness in model pruning?
- How does the proposed method perform on larger and more complex datasets, such as those used in large-scale applications?
- Can the authors provide a more detailed discussion on the computational complexity and efficiency of the proposed method, especially in comparison to existing methods?
- How does the method perform when fairness constraints are less stringent, and what are the implications for performance and fairness?
- Could the authors clarify the practical implications of the method in real-world scenarios, particularly in terms of its scalability and computational efficiency?
