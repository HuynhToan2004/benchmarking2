 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel image-based representation framework named Time2Image for time series classification (TSC). It employs the Adaptive Time Series Gaussian Mapping (ATSGM) module to encode time series data into 2D images, which are then processed using a Vision Transformer (ViT) for classification. The methodology is tested on 158 public datasets from UCR/UEA, demonstrating superior performance compared to existing state-of-the-art methods. However, the paper's presentation lacks clarity in explaining the technical details, particularly the ATSGM module and the choice of datasets. The paper also suffers from a lack of detailed methodological description, which could hinder understanding and reproducibility.

**Strengths:**
- The proposed method achieves state-of-the-art (SOTA) performance on most datasets, demonstrating its effectiveness.
- The use of ViT for time series classification (TSC) is innovative and has shown promising results.
- The paper is well-organized, making it easy to follow, and the methodology is clearly described.
- The image-based representation of time series data is a novel approach that could potentially enhance the performance of TSC methods.
- The paper includes a comprehensive evaluation using 158 datasets from UCR/UEA, which helps in validating the proposed method's performance across a wide range of datasets.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed explanation of the ATSGM module, particularly how it extracts features from time series data and its connection to the image representation.
- The choice of datasets for evaluation is questionable, with many datasets being too short or too long for the proposed method, which uses cubic interpolation for data resizing.
- The methodology section is not detailed enough, with some key steps and equations not adequately explained, which could hinder understanding and reproducibility.
- The paper does not compare the proposed method with other state-of-the-art methods like ROCKET, CIF, and HIVE-COTE, which could have provided a more robust evaluation of the proposed method.
- The paper does not discuss the limitations of the proposed method, which is crucial for understanding its applicability and potential drawbacks.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors clarify how the ATSGM module extracts features from time series data and how it is connected to the image representation?
- How does the proposed method perform on datasets with different lengths, particularly those that are too short or too long for the method's capabilities?
- Could the authors provide more details on the methodology, particularly the steps and equations mentioned in the review?
- What are the specific advantages of using ViT over other methods for TSC, and how does it enhance the performance of the proposed method?
- How does the proposed method handle different types of time series data, such as those with varying frequencies or data types?
- Can the authors discuss the limitations of the proposed method and suggest potential improvements or modifications to address these limitations?
