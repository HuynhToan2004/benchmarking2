 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel SE(3)-equivariant model, PPiformer, which leverages a large and non-redundant dataset of 3D protein-protein interactions, PPIRef, to predict the effects of mutations on protein-protein interactions. The model is trained using a self-supervised approach and fine-tuned for predicting binding affinity changes upon mutations. The authors claim that their method outperforms other state-of-the-art methods in terms of generalization and practical relevance. The paper also discusses the challenges in training machine learning methods for protein-protein interactions, including the scarcity of labeled data and the need for generalization beyond training data.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, clear, and easy to follow, making it accessible to a broad audience.
- It introduces a large and non-redundant dataset of 3D protein-protein interactions, which is a significant contribution to the field.
- The authors have developed an SE(3)-equivariant transformer for modeling coarse-grain structures of protein-protein interactions, which is a novel approach.
- The paper provides a thorough evaluation of the proposed method, demonstrating its effectiveness in predicting binding affinity changes upon mutations.
- The methodology is well-supported by detailed ablation studies and extensive evaluations, which provide a robust validation of the proposed model.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper could benefit from a more detailed discussion on the limitations of the proposed method, particularly in terms of its applicability to different protein-protein interaction problems.
- The generalization of the method to different protein-protein interaction problems is not adequately discussed, which could limit the practical utility of the model.
- The paper could provide more insights into the potential societal impacts of the proposed method, particularly in the context of protein engineering and biotechnology.
- The paper does not sufficiently compare the proposed method with existing methods, such as the ESM-IF method, which could have provided a more robust validation of the proposed model.
- The paper lacks a detailed discussion on the potential negative societal impacts of the proposed method, which could be a significant concern for some readers.
