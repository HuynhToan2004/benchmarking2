 **Summary:**
The paper explores the performance of Graph Neural Networks (GNNs) under the contextual Stochastic Block Model (cSBM) and its variants, focusing on how different GNN architectures handle various graph structures and feature distributions. It provides theoretical insights into the limitations of GCNs compared to linear models and empirically evaluates the recoverability thresholds of several GNN architectures. The paper also investigates the impact of higher-order structures on GNN performance and demonstrates that the performance of GNNs on real-world datasets can be affected by the presence of higher-order structures. However, the paper's theoretical contributions are seen as limited, and the experimental setup is considered too simplistic, which might limit its practical applicability.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, making it easy to follow, and the theoretical results are sound.
- It provides a comprehensive experimental analysis on the performance of GNNs on synthetic data, which is a significant contribution to the field.
- The paper offers a novel approach by integrating the analysis of graph structure and node features, which is a critical aspect of GNN performance.
- The paper includes both theoretical and empirical results, which complement each other effectively.
- The authors provide code for reproducibility, which is a valuable resource for other researchers.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a thorough discussion on related works, particularly those that have already addressed similar issues such as the performance of GNNs on stochastic block models.
- The theoretical contributions are seen as limited, with many results being previously established in the literature.
- The paper's experimental setup is too simplistic, focusing only on binary classification and not extending to multi-class settings.
- The paper does not adequately address the limitations of its work, such as the focus on binary classification and the lack of discussion on the practical implications of the findings.
- There are several typographical errors and inconsistencies in the paper, which could detract from its professional presentation.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors clarify the relationship between the results presented in this paper and those from previous works that have also studied the performance of GNNs on stochastic block models?
- How do the authors justify the choice of focusing only on binary classification, and what are the implications of this choice for the generalizability of the findings?
- Could the authors elaborate on the experimental design, particularly the choice of parameters and the rationale behind the simplified settings?
- What are the practical implications of the findings, and how can they be applied to real-world scenarios?
- Could the authors address the typographical errors and inconsistencies noted in the paper to improve its clarity and professionalism?
