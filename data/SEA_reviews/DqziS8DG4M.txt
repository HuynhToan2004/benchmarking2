 **Summary:**
The paper introduces Point2SSM, a novel unsupervised learning method that constructs statistical shape models (SSMs) directly from point clouds, addressing challenges such as noise, sparsity, and incompleteness. The method leverages an attention-based module to infer correspondence mappings from learned point features, showing superior performance over existing methods in terms of surface sampling and correspondence accuracy. The paper also includes robustness evaluations against noise, partiality, and sparsity, demonstrating the model's effectiveness in clinical scenarios. Despite its strengths, the paper faces criticism for its limited evaluation on only three datasets and the lack of comparison with more recent and relevant methods, such as those using mesh-to-point conversion or unsupervised methods.

**Strengths:**
- The paper addresses a significant and relevant problem by focusing on the construction of statistical shape models (SSMs) from point clouds, a topic that has not been extensively explored in the literature.
- The proposed method, Point2SSM, is innovative and demonstrates superior performance over existing methods in terms of surface sampling and correspondence accuracy.
- The paper is well-written, clear, and easy to follow, with detailed and convincing evaluations that support the claims made.
- The robustness of the method is demonstrated through various experiments, including robustness against input noise, partial input, and sparse input, showing that the model can handle real-world clinical scenarios effectively.
- The methodology is straightforward and well-explained, making it accessible and understandable to a broad audience.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a comprehensive comparison with other recent and relevant methods, particularly those that use mesh-to-point conversion or unsupervised methods, which could provide a more robust validation of the proposed method.
- The evaluation is limited to only three datasets, which might not be sufficient to convincingly demonstrate the generalizability of the method across different anatomies and pathologies.
- There is a lack of discussion on the limitations of the method, particularly in terms of its applicability to different anatomies and its robustness against various types of noise and incomplete data.
- The paper does not adequately address the scalability of the method to larger or more complex datasets, which could limit its practical utility in real-world applications.
- The method's reliance on point clouds as input could limit its applicability in scenarios where meshes or other types of data are more readily available or more informative.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors provide more details on the specific types and amounts of noise added to the input point clouds in the robustness experiments?
- How does the method perform when applied to different anatomies or pathologies beyond those tested in the current study?
- Have the authors considered using mesh-to-point conversion techniques to potentially enhance the method's performance or applicability?
- Could the authors provide more details on the training and inference times for the Point2SSM model, particularly in comparison to other methods?
- How does the method handle cases where the input point clouds are not aligned or where the alignment is not perfect?
- Are there any plans to release the code for the method to facilitate reproducibility and further research by the community?
