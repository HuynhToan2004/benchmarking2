 **Summary:**
The paper explores the impact of frequency-domain perturbations on the generalization of Convolutional Neural Networks (CNNs) in the context of backdoor attacks. It identifies that high-frequency components are more susceptible to perturbations, leading to the development of a frequency-domain backdoor attack method that achieves high attack accuracy across multiple models and datasets. The methodology includes a novel strategy for rendering visible triggers invisible, which maintains raw attack performance while enhancing stealthiness. The paper also proposes a backdoor attack algorithm based on low-frequency semantic information, which can bypass multiple defenses. However, the paper is criticized for its limited scope in terms of datasets and models, and for not sufficiently comparing its methodology with existing frequency-domain backdoor attacks.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel frequency-domain backdoor attack method, which is the first of its kind to be proposed.
- The methodology is well-structured, providing a clear explanation of the proposed method and its implementation.
- The paper is well-written, making it accessible and easy to understand, with a comprehensive literature review and detailed analysis.
- The proposed method achieves high attack accuracy on multiple models and datasets, demonstrating its effectiveness.
- The paper provides a new perspective on backdoor attacks by focusing on frequency-domain perturbations, which is an innovative approach.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a comprehensive comparison with existing frequency-domain backdoor attacks, which limits the understanding of the novelty and effectiveness of the proposed method.
- The experiments are limited to specific datasets and models, which may not generalize well to other scenarios or models.
- The paper does not sufficiently address the limitations of the proposed method, which could help in understanding its applicability and potential drawbacks.
- The paper does not provide a detailed comparison with other frequency-domain backdoor attacks, which could help in understanding the advantages and disadvantages of the proposed method.
- The paper's claims about the superiority of high-frequency components over low-frequency components are not convincingly supported by the experimental results.
- The paper does not sufficiently discuss the limitations of the proposed method, which could help in understanding its applicability and potential drawbacks.
