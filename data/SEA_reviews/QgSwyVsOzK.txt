 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel approach to knowledge representation using functional representations, termed KASF, which simulates semantic flow among tokens to facilitate knowledge reasoning. This method is evaluated on various benchmarks including WikiQA, SQuAD2.0, and CommonsenseQA, showing significant improvements in performance compared to baseline models. The method is designed to capture semantic features effectively and eliminate ambiguities in representing entities and relations. The paper's empirical results demonstrate the superiority of KASF in knowledge reasoning tasks, although the novelty and clarity of the method's description have been questioned.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel approach to knowledge representation using functional representations, which is a significant advancement in the field.
- The methodology is well-explained and easy to follow, with a clear presentation of the proposed approach.
- The empirical results are strong, demonstrating the effectiveness of the proposed method across various benchmarks, including WikiQA, SQuAD2.0, and CommonsenseQA.
- The paper provides a comprehensive evaluation of the proposed method, including a comparison with existing models and baseline methods, which validates the superiority of the proposed approach.
- The paper is well-written and easy to read, making it accessible to a broad audience.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed description of the method, which makes it difficult to understand the specifics of how the functional representations are implemented and trained.
- There is a lack of clarity regarding the specifics of the functional representations, such as the dimensions and the nature of the dynamic states used.
- The novelty of the proposed method is questionable, as it appears to be a combination of existing ideas without significant innovation.
- The paper does not adequately address the scalability of the proposed method, particularly in terms of its applicability to larger and more complex datasets.
- The paper could benefit from a more detailed discussion on the limitations of the proposed method, including potential challenges in scaling up and its applicability to different types of knowledge-intensive tasks.
- The paper's claims about the superiority of functional representations over traditional methods are not well-supported, and the empirical results could be strengthened by including more rigorous comparisons and ablation studies.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors provide a more detailed description of the functional representations, including the dimensions and the nature of the dynamic states used?
- How does the proposed method compare to other existing methods in terms of computational efficiency and scalability?
- What are the specific advantages of using functional representations over traditional methods, and how do these advantages translate into practical benefits in real-world applications?
- How does the proposed method handle the scalability issues that are inherent in knowledge-intensive tasks, particularly in terms of handling large and complex datasets?
- Could the authors provide more detailed empirical results, including ablation studies and comparisons with more rigorous baseline models, to better support the claims made in the paper?
- How does the proposed method perform when applied to different types of knowledge-intensive tasks, and what are the potential limitations or challenges in applying the method in these contexts?
