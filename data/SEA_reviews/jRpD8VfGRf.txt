 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel Multi-interest Disentangled Representation Learning (MIDR) method for multimodal recommendation systems, aimed at addressing the challenges of utilizing multimodal data effectively and avoiding multimodal noise. It proposes a framework that leverages disentangled representation learning and a multi-interest contrast module to enhance the recommendation system's performance. The methodology is validated through experiments on three real-world datasets, demonstrating the effectiveness of the proposed approach. Despite the innovative approach, the paper faces criticism regarding the clarity of the writing, the theoretical grounding, and the experimental design.

**Strengths:**
- The paper addresses an important and challenging problem in the field of multimodal recommendation systems, focusing on the effective utilization of multimodal data and the avoidance of multimodal noise.
- The authors propose a novel method for multimodal recommendation, utilizing disentangled representation learning to extract shared and specific interest representations from multimodal data.
- The paper is well-organized and easy to follow, with a clear introduction of the methodology and a comprehensive ablation study that demonstrates the effectiveness of the proposed method.
- The experimental results show that the proposed method outperforms other state-of-the-art models, highlighting its practical utility and potential impact.
- The paper provides a theoretical foundation for the methodology, which adds depth to the understanding of the proposed approach.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper suffers from unclear writing and lacks detailed explanations in certain sections, particularly in the introduction and methodology.
- The motivation behind the use of disentangled representation learning and the theoretical grounding of the methodology are not clearly articulated, which could limit the understanding of the proposed approach.
- The experimental design lacks depth and does not adequately compare the proposed method with existing state-of-the-art models, which could undermine the credibility of the results.
- The paper does not discuss the limitations of the proposed method, which is crucial for understanding the scope and applicability of the findings.
- The paper could benefit from a more comprehensive literature review and a clearer explanation of the experimental setup, including the datasets used and the methodology for hyperparameter selection.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors clarify the motivation behind the use of disentangled representation learning and how it specifically addresses the challenges of multimodal recommendation?
- How does the proposed method compare with existing state-of-the-art models in terms of performance and efficiency?
- Can the authors provide a more detailed explanation of the experimental setup, including the datasets used and the methodology for hyperparameter selection?
- How does the proposed method perform under different conditions, such as varying levels of noise in the multimodal data?
- Could the authors discuss the limitations of the proposed method and suggest potential areas for future research?
