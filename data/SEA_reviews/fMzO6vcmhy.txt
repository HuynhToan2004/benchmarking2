 **Summary:**
The paper introduces QORA, a novel algorithm designed for learning object-oriented transition models in reinforcement learning (RL) without requiring domain-specific knowledge. QORA operates by extracting relational predicates from observations and using statistical methods to assemble these predicates into informative hypotheses. It is evaluated across various benchmark environments, demonstrating significant improvements in sample complexity and generalization compared to neural network baselines. The paper also introduces a new benchmark suite for evaluating learners' generalization capabilities, showcasing QORA's ability to adapt to new object interactions and achieve zero-shot transfer to modified environments. Despite its strengths, the paper is critiqued for its lack of comprehensive comparisons with existing methods and for not adequately addressing the computational complexity of its algorithm.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces QORA, a novel algorithm for learning object-oriented transition models without domain-specific knowledge, which is a significant advancement in the field of reinforcement learning.
- QORA demonstrates impressive sample complexity, achieving zero-shot transfer to more complex scenarios and continual learning of new interactions, outperforming neural network baselines.
- The paper provides a comprehensive evaluation of QORA across various benchmark environments, showcasing its ability to generalize to unseen tasks and its efficiency in terms of sample complexity.
- The methodology of QORA is well-explained, making the paper accessible and understandable, which is crucial for reproducibility and further research.
- The paper introduces a new benchmark suite for evaluating learners' generalization capabilities, which is a valuable contribution to the field.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a comprehensive comparison with existing methods, particularly those that also address the GIE problem. This limits the understanding of QORA's unique contributions and advantages over existing methods.
- There is a lack of detailed discussion on the limitations of QORA, which is crucial for understanding the scope and applicability of the method.
- The computational complexity of the algorithm is not adequately addressed, raising concerns about its scalability and practical applicability in more complex or larger-scale environments.
- The paper does not provide sufficient details on how QORA performs in more complex or large-scale environments, which is necessary to fully assess its capabilities and limitations.
- There is a lack of discussion on the societal impact of the work, which is an important consideration for any technological advancement.
