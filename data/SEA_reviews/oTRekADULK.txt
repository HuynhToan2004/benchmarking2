 **Summary:**
The paper introduces SparseDiff, a novel denoising diffusion model for graph generation that leverages sparsity during training to enhance scalability. It utilizes a message-passing neural network to predict a subset of edges during each forward pass, which is then iteratively refined to generate the full graph. This approach allows SparseDiff to handle larger graphs more efficiently, with a focus on maintaining sparsity throughout the diffusion process. The model is evaluated on various datasets and shows competitive performance compared to existing methods, demonstrating its effectiveness in large-scale graph generation.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, clear, and easy to follow, with a clear explanation of the proposed method.
- The method is innovative, particularly in its approach to sparsity-preserving noise models and loss functions, which are crucial for maintaining the sparsity of generated graphs.
- The empirical results are promising, showing that the proposed method outperforms baselines on various datasets, demonstrating its effectiveness in large-scale graph generation.
- The paper includes a thorough analysis of the proposed method, including theoretical analysis and detailed ablation studies, which support the claims made.
- The method's scalability is highlighted, with the ability to handle graphs with up to 1000 nodes, which is a significant improvement over previous methods.
- The paper is well-organized, making it easy to read and understand, with clear figures and tables that aid in understanding the methodology and results.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed discussion on the limitations of the proposed method, particularly in terms of scalability and the choice of hyperparameters such as the edge fraction.
- The experimental results, while promising, are not comprehensive enough, with some datasets (like QM9) showing similar or worse results compared to baselines, which could be due to the choice of hyperparameters.
- The paper could benefit from a more detailed comparison with other state-of-the-art methods, especially those that also address scalability in graph generation.
- There is a need for more detailed explanations of the sparsity-preserving loss function and the iterative sampling process, which are critical components of the proposed method.
- The paper could be improved by including more diverse datasets and benchmarks to better demonstrate the generalizability of the proposed method.

**Questions:**
- Could you clarify the choice of hyperparameters, particularly the edge fraction, and how it affects the performance of the method?
- How does the proposed method compare to other state-of-the-art methods in terms of scalability and performance on large-scale graphs?
- Could you provide more details on the computational complexity of the proposed method and how it scales with the size of the graphs?
- How does the proposed method handle the choice of hyperparameters, particularly in scenarios where the optimal values might vary across different datasets?
- Can you provide more detailed results on the training time and memory usage of the proposed method compared to baselines?
- Could you include more diverse datasets and benchmarks to better demonstrate the generalizability of the proposed method?
