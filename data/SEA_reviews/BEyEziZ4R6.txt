 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel approach to training Differentially Private (DP) Deep Neural Networks (DNNs) by employing Lipschitz constrained networks, which avoid the need for gradient clipping and provide a more efficient method for computing sensitivity bounds. This method leverages Lipschitz constraints on the neural network layers to ensure that the gradient norms remain within bounds, thereby enabling the application of the Gaussian mechanism to ensure privacy. The approach is supported by a Python package, Lip-DP, which facilitates the practical application of these methods. The paper provides theoretical analysis and empirical results demonstrating the effectiveness of this method, particularly in terms of computational efficiency and privacy guarantees.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel approach to training Differentially Private (DP) Deep Neural Networks (DNNs) by leveraging Lipschitz constrained networks, which avoids the need for per-sample gradient clipping and provides a more efficient method for computing sensitivity bounds.
- The authors provide a Python package, Lip-DP, which implements the building blocks necessary for constructing and training such networks, enhancing the practical applicability of the proposed method.
- The paper is well-written, clear, and easy to follow, with a comprehensive theoretical analysis and empirical results that demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed method.
- The methodology introduced in the paper could potentially lead to faster training of DP-SGD, as it avoids the need for per-sample gradient clipping which is known to be computationally expensive.
- The paper is significant as it addresses the critical issue of training DP-SGD, which is a fundamental challenge in the field of machine learning.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed discussion on the limitations of the proposed approach, such as its compatibility with other DP techniques and its impact on the performance of DP-SGD.
- The experimental results presented in the paper are somewhat limited, with the evaluation primarily focused on simple datasets like MNIST and CIFAR-10. More comprehensive evaluations on larger and more complex datasets are needed to validate the effectiveness of the proposed method.
- There is a lack of comparison with other recent works in the field, which could help to contextualize the contributions of the paper and highlight its advantages over existing methods.
- The paper could benefit from a more detailed discussion on the practical implications of the Lipschitz constraint on the performance of DP-SGD, particularly in terms of the trade-off between privacy and utility.
- The paper could be improved by including a more detailed discussion on the computational complexity and memory requirements of the proposed method, especially in comparison to other existing methods.
