 **Summary:**
The paper introduces MG-NeRF, a novel framework that integrates multimodal information, including text captions, to enhance the generalization capabilities of NeRF. This approach leverages a pre-trained vision-language model, IMAGEBIND, to generate text captions and align them with image features. The method includes a semantic loss and frequency regularization to improve rendering quality and prevent overfitting. Experiments demonstrate that MG-NeRF can synthesize novel views from unseen scenes with less computational resources than prior works. However, the paper lacks a detailed analysis of the effectiveness of each component and does not provide code or comparisons with other multimodal NeRF methods.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, making it easy to follow and understand.
- The proposed method is simple yet effective, with a straightforward design that is easy to implement.
- The use of multimodal information, such as text captions, is innovative and can potentially enhance the generalization capabilities of NeRF.
- The paper includes comprehensive experiments that demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed method, showing improvements in rendering quality and generalization to unseen scenes.
- The integration of multimodal information and the use of pre-trained models like IMAGEBIND and LoRA are highlighted as significant contributions to the field.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed analysis of the effectiveness of each component, particularly the semantic loss and frequency regularization.
- There is a significant performance gap between MG-NeRF and other methods, raising concerns about its competitiveness.
- The paper does not provide code, which limits the reproducibility of the results.
- The novelty of the method is questioned, as it primarily relies on existing pre-trained models and techniques without significant innovation.
- The paper does not compare with other multimodal NeRF methods, which could provide a better understanding of the method's position within the current research landscape.
- The paper lacks a thorough discussion on the limitations and potential negative societal impacts of the proposed method.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors provide a detailed analysis of the effectiveness of each component, particularly the semantic loss and frequency regularization?
- How does the proposed method handle different types of multimodal information, such as audio or IMU data?
- Could the authors clarify the training and inference details, especially the computational requirements and the impact of different training strategies on the results?
- Why was the decision made to use only one image as input, and how does this affect the performance of the method?
- How does the method perform when using different pre-trained models, and what are the implications of this choice on the results?
- Can the authors provide more details on the frequency regularization mechanism, particularly how it is applied and its impact on the training process?
- Why was the decision made to use only one scene for testing, and how does this affect the generalizability of the results?
