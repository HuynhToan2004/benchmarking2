 **Summary:**
The paper introduces FairPATE, a novel framework designed to enhance fairness in machine learning models while maintaining privacy through differential privacy. FairPATE extends the traditional PATE framework by incorporating a rejection mechanism based on fairness constraints, which allows for the rejection of queries that violate demographic parity. This mechanism aims to balance fairness with privacy and accuracy, offering a trade-off curve between fairness, privacy, and accuracy. The paper provides theoretical and empirical evidence to support its claims, comparing FairPATE with existing state-of-the-art methods. Despite its innovative approach, concerns were raised about the clarity of the experimental results and the theoretical contributions, particularly in the areas of differential privacy and fairness guarantees.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, clear, and easy to follow, making it accessible to a broad audience.
- The problem addressed is highly relevant, focusing on the integration of fairness and privacy in machine learning models, which is a critical issue in current data-driven systems.
- The paper introduces a novel approach by combining fairness and privacy in a single framework, which is innovative and addresses a significant gap in the literature.
- The experimental results are robust and support the claims made in the paper, providing evidence for the effectiveness of the proposed method.
- The paper is well-organized, with clear explanations of the methodology and the results, which aid in understanding the complex interactions between fairness, privacy, and accuracy.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a thorough comparison with existing methods, particularly in terms of differential privacy and fairness guarantees. This is crucial for establishing the novelty and effectiveness of the proposed method.
- The experimental results, particularly those shown in figures, are not convincingly presented, with some figures (e.g., Figure 2) being misleading or not well-explained.
- The paper does not adequately address the issue of differential privacy, which is a significant aspect of the work. The theoretical contributions related to differential privacy are not clearly articulated, and the paper does not convincingly demonstrate how the proposed method improves upon existing techniques.
- The paper could benefit from a more detailed discussion on the limitations and potential negative societal impacts of the proposed method.
- There are several typographical and formatting errors throughout the paper, which detract from its professional presentation.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors clarify the privacy accounting in the context of differential privacy, particularly how it impacts the privacy guarantees provided by the system?
- How does the proposed method compare to other state-of-the-art methods in terms of fairness-privacy-accuracy trade-offs?
- In the context of the experimental results, can the authors provide more detailed explanations or additional results to clarify the findings, particularly those related to the effectiveness of the rejection mechanism?
- Could the authors provide a more detailed discussion on the theoretical contributions related to differential privacy and fairness, and how these contributions advance the field beyond existing methods?
- What are the potential negative societal impacts of the proposed method, and how do the authors address these concerns in their work?
