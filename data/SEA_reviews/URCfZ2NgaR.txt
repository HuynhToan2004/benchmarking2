 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a Structural Knowledge Informed Continual Learning (SKI-CL) framework for multivariate time series forecasting, focusing on the continual learning setting where different regimes are sequentially encountered. The SKI-CL framework leverages structural knowledge to characterize dynamic variable dependencies within each regime, using a dynamic graph structure learning module to capture temporal dependencies and infer dependency structures. It employs an adaptive regularization scheme to incorporate structural knowledge and a novel representation-matching memory replay scheme to tackle the catastrophic forgetting problem. The paper presents a comprehensive experimental evaluation on both synthetic and real-world benchmark datasets, demonstrating the effectiveness of the SKI-CL framework in continual MTS forecasting tasks.

**Strengths:**
- The paper addresses an important and relevant problem in the field of multivariate time series forecasting, specifically focusing on the continual learning setting which is less explored.
- The authors have developed a novel Structural Knowledge Informed Continual Learning (SKI-CL) framework, which includes a dynamic graph structure learning module and an adaptive regularization scheme to incorporate structural knowledge.
- The SKI-CL framework has been evaluated on both synthetic and real-world benchmark datasets, demonstrating its effectiveness in continual MTS forecasting tasks.
- The paper is well-written and easy to follow, with a clear motivation and problem definition.
- The proposed method is technically sound, with a novel representation-matching memory replay scheme that maximizes the temporal coverage of MTS data to preserve the underlying temporal dynamics and dependency structures of each regime.
- The paper provides a comprehensive experimental evaluation, including a thorough analysis of the impact of hyperparameters on the model's performance.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed discussion on the differences between continual learning and online learning, particularly in the context of multivariate time series forecasting.
- There is a lack of clarity in the definition and role of structural knowledge in the proposed framework. It is unclear how this knowledge is integrated into the model and how it influences the forecasting outcomes.
- The paper does not sufficiently compare the proposed method with existing methods in the field, particularly in terms of computational complexity and memory requirements.
- The paper does not discuss the limitations of the proposed method, which could help in understanding its applicability and potential drawbacks.
- The paper lacks a detailed discussion on the experimental results, particularly the results on the synthetic dataset and the comparison with other methods on the Traffic-CL dataset.
- The paper does not provide a detailed discussion on the scalability of the proposed method with the number of variables and time steps in multivariate time series.

**Questions:**
- Could you provide more details on the differences between continual learning and online learning in the context of multivariate time series forecasting?
- How is structural knowledge defined and integrated into the model, and what role does it play in the forecasting outcomes?
- How does the proposed method compare with existing methods in terms of computational complexity and memory requirements?
- Can you discuss the limitations of the proposed method and provide examples or scenarios where it might not be applicable?
- How does the model handle the scalability with the number of variables and time steps in multivariate time series?
- Could you clarify the role and impact of the memory replay scheme in the proposed method, particularly in terms of its contribution to the overall performance?
- How does the model handle the different types of structural knowledge, such as discrete/continuous edge descriptions and fully/partially observed structural knowledge?
- In the experimental evaluation, could you provide more details on the results, particularly the results on the synthetic dataset and the comparison with other methods on the Traffic-CL dataset?
