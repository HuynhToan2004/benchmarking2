 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a method for real-time rendering of neural Signed Distance Functions (SDFs) using a multiscale sphere tracing algorithm, GEMM-based normal computation, and neural attribute mapping. This approach allows for the efficient handling of dynamic SDFs and supports real-time rendering of attributes like normals and textures. The method leverages a nested neighborhood concept to accelerate rendering and avoids the use of spatial data structures, which is crucial for real-time applications. The paper also proposes a GEMM-based analytical computation of normals, which enhances rendering quality by avoiding auto-differentiation.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel approach to real-time rendering of neural SDFs, which is a significant advancement in the field.
- The methodology is well-explained, with clear and concise writing that effectively conveys the technical details.
- The paper is well-organized, with a clear structure that makes it easy to follow and understand.
- The proposed method achieves good performance and is compared with state-of-the-art methods, demonstrating its effectiveness.
- The paper provides a detailed description of the method, including the use of GEMM-based analytical normal computation, which is innovative and enhances the rendering quality.
- The method's ability to handle dynamic SDFs and support real-time rendering of attributes like normals and textures is a significant advantage, particularly in the context of real-time applications.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a comprehensive evaluation of the method's performance, particularly in terms of memory usage and the scalability of the method to larger, more complex models.
- The paper does not discuss the limitations of the proposed method, which could help in understanding its applicability and potential drawbacks.
- The comparison with other methods, such as IDF, is not convincingly superior, and the paper could benefit from a more detailed comparison or a discussion on why IDF was not chosen as the baseline.
- The paper's evaluation is limited to a few examples, which might not be sufficient to convincingly demonstrate the method's effectiveness across a broader range of models or scenarios.
- The paper's focus on neural SDFs might limit its applicability to other types of SDFs, which could be a significant drawback if the method is intended for more general use.

**Questions:**
- How does the proposed method compare in terms of memory usage and computational efficiency with other methods, particularly IDF?
- Can the method be adapted to handle signed distance functions (SDFs) instead of neural SDFs?
- Is there a plan to release the code for the proposed method to facilitate further research and development in the community?
- How does the proposed method handle the nesting condition in practical scenarios, especially when dealing with complex or dynamic SDFs?
- Can the authors provide more detailed comparisons or discussions on why IDF was not chosen as the baseline, and how the proposed method outperforms it in terms of rendering quality and efficiency?
