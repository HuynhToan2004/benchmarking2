 **Summary:**
The paper introduces the Improved MLP (IMLP) module for Vision Transformers, focusing on enhancing the non-linearity of the MLP module through the Arbitrary GeLU (AGeLU) function and spatial enhancement. The IMLP module is designed to replace the standard MLP module in Vision Transformers, aiming to reduce computational costs while maintaining or even improving classification accuracy. The authors provide a theoretical analysis of the Lipschitz constant and conduct extensive experiments to validate the effectiveness of the proposed module. The experiments demonstrate that the IMLP module can reduce FLOPs and parameters without compromising performance on the ImageNet dataset. However, the paper has been criticized for its limited novelty, as the AGeLU function and spatial enhancement are not entirely new, and the theoretical analysis lacks depth and clarity.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, clear, and easy to follow, with a clear presentation of the proposed method.
- The authors have conducted extensive experiments to demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed method, showing promising results in terms of FLOPs reduction and classification accuracy on the ImageNet dataset.
- The use of the Arbitrary GeLU (AGeLU) function and the spatial enhancement part in the IMLP module is novel and contributes to the reduction of computational costs in Vision Transformers.
- The paper includes a thorough analysis of the MLP module in Vision Transformers, demonstrating that it primarily acts as a non-linearity generator, which is a novel perspective.
- The authors have provided a theoretical analysis of the Lipschitz constant of the proposed module, enhancing the reliability and stability of the network.

**Weaknesses:**
- The novelty of the AGeLU function and the spatial enhancement part is limited as these concepts have been previously introduced in other works.
- The paper lacks a detailed comparison with existing methods, particularly in terms of computational efficiency and model performance.
- The theoretical analysis provided in the paper is not comprehensive, with a lack of detailed proofs and explanations, especially in the proof of Theorem 1.
- The paper does not discuss the impact of the proposed method on other tasks such as object detection and semantic segmentation, nor does it explore the application of the IMLP module to other vision transformer architectures.
- The paper could benefit from a more rigorous experimental validation, including a broader range of datasets and a more comprehensive evaluation of the computational efficiency and model performance.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors provide a detailed comparison with existing methods in terms of computational efficiency and model performance?
- How does the proposed method perform on other tasks such as object detection and semantic segmentation?
- Can the IMLP module be applied to other vision transformer architectures, and what are the expected outcomes?
- In the proof of Theorem 1, how does the matrix inequality M(ρi,T) hold, and what are the implications of this inequality on the model's performance and computational efficiency?
- Can the authors clarify the role and impact of the AGeLU function and the spatial enhancement part in the overall performance of the Vision Transformer?
