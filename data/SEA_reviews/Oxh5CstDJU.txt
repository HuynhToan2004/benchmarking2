 **Summary:**
The paper introduces TD-MPC2, a model-based reinforcement learning algorithm that leverages a learned implicit world model for trajectory optimization in the latent space. TD-MPC2 builds upon the TD-MPC algorithm, integrating several improvements such as joint-embedding prediction, reward prediction, value prediction, and a policy prior. It is tested across 104 continuous control tasks and demonstrates scalability, achieving strong results with a single set of hyperparameters. The authors also discuss the scalability of the model with increasing model and data sizes, and the potential for training large-scale models. Despite its strengths, the paper is critiqued for its limited novelty and the lack of comparison with other model-based RL algorithms like DreamerV3.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, clear, and easy to follow, with a clear presentation of the methodology and results.
- The TD-MPC2 algorithm is described as simple, effective, and efficient, with a focus on scalability and robustness.
- The experiments conducted are comprehensive and cover a variety of tasks, demonstrating the effectiveness of the proposed method across different environments.
- The paper provides a thorough discussion on the scalability of the model with increasing model and data sizes, and the potential for training large-scale models.
- The use of an implicit world model, which does not require reconstruction of future observations, is highlighted as an innovative approach.
- The paper includes a detailed appendix with a variety of experiments, providing a comprehensive evaluation of the proposed method.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed discussion on the novelty of the proposed method, particularly in comparison to existing model-based RL algorithms like DreamerV3.
- The paper does not provide sufficient details on the design choices for the world model, such as the number of latent variables and the choice of activation functions.
- There is a lack of clarity regarding the scalability of the model with different task domains and action spaces, and how the model's performance varies across different environments.
- The paper does not include a detailed comparison of training times and resource usage, which could be crucial for understanding the practical implications of using the proposed method.
- The paper does not adequately address the limitations of the method, such as its applicability to more complex tasks or its scalability to larger, more complex environments.
- The paper lacks a detailed discussion on the limitations of the model, such as its inability to handle discrete actions or its dependence on specific types of rewards.

**Questions:**
- How does the TD-MPC2 algorithm compare to other model-based RL algorithms like DreamerV3 in terms of performance and efficiency?
- Can you provide more details on the design choices for the world model, such as the number of latent variables and the choice of activation functions?
- How does the model perform when scaled up to handle more complex tasks or larger, more complex environments?
- What are the specific advantages of using an implicit world model over a generative model, and how does this affect the model's performance and scalability?
- Can you clarify the scalability of the model with different task domains and action spaces, and how the model's performance varies across different environments?
- How do the authors address the limitations of the model, such as its inability to handle discrete actions or its dependence on specific types of rewards?
