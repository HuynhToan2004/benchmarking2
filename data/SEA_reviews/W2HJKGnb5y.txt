 **Summary:**
The paper introduces PopDescent, a novel memetic algorithm designed for hyperparameter tuning in neural network training. PopDescent employs an m-elitist selection strategy and a normalized fitness-based randomization scheme to optimize gradient descent hyperparameters, showing improvements over existing methods in terms of performance and insensitivity to its own hyperparameters. The algorithm's effectiveness is demonstrated through experiments on benchmark datasets, showcasing its ability to surpass existing hyperparameter tuning methods. However, concerns are raised about the algorithm's novelty and the breadth of its experimental validation, as it is currently limited to specific datasets and does not include comparisons with state-of-the-art methods in the field.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written and easy to follow, making complex concepts accessible to a broader audience.
- The proposed method, PopDescent, is innovative and demonstrates significant performance improvements over existing methods, including a 13% boost in performance.
- PopDescent is insensitive to its own hyperparameters, which is a crucial requirement for hyperparameter tuning methods.
- The method is backed by extensive experimental results, which are clearly presented and convincing.
- The paper provides a clear explanation of the PopDescent algorithm and its components, which are crucial for understanding its effectiveness.
- The simplicity of the method makes it easy to implement and understand, which is a significant advantage for practical applications.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed comparison with state-of-the-art methods in the field of hyperparameter tuning, which could undermine the perceived effectiveness of the proposed method.
- The novelty of the PopDescent algorithm is questionable as it appears to be a combination of existing techniques without significant innovation.
- The paper does not provide sufficient justification for the choice of benchmark datasets, which could limit the generalizability of the results.
- The paper does not adequately address the scalability of the proposed method, particularly in terms of its applicability to larger datasets or more complex models.
- There are several minor issues with the presentation of the paper, including inconsistencies in the use of notation and grammatical errors.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors provide a more detailed comparison with state-of-the-art methods in the field of hyperparameter tuning to better establish the effectiveness of PopDescent?
- How does the PopDescent algorithm compare in terms of computational efficiency and resource requirements to other hyperparameter tuning methods?
- Can the authors clarify the specific contributions of the PopDescent algorithm beyond the existing techniques it combines?
- Is there a possibility to extend the PopDescent algorithm to other types of neural network architectures or training methods beyond gradient descent?
- Could the authors address the concerns regarding the novelty and generalizability of the PopDescent algorithm, perhaps by including additional experimental results or theoretical analysis?
