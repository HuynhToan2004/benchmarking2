 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel approach to variance reduction in text-to-3D generation using Stein's identity. It proposes a method called Stein Score Distillation (SSD) which integrates control variates to enhance the stability and convergence of score distillation. The SSD method is shown to outperform existing techniques like SDS and VSD, demonstrating improvements in visual quality and convergence speed. The paper also presents a new method for training a diffusion model on a 3D representation, which could potentially be beneficial for the community. The experiments conducted show that SSD can effectively reduce distillation variance and improve visual quality, leading to faster convergence.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, easy to follow, and clearly explains the methodology and the motivation behind the research.
- The proposed method, Stein Score Distillation (SSD), is novel and effectively reduces distillation variance, leading to improved visual quality and faster convergence.
- The paper includes a comprehensive comparison with previous methods, demonstrating the effectiveness of SSD in reducing variance and improving performance.
- The theoretical insights provided are solid and well-explained, contributing to a deeper understanding of the underlying mechanisms.
- The results are impressive, showing significant improvements over existing methods in both object-level and scene-level text-to-3D generation.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed comparison with the most recent methods, such as those mentioned in the references, which could have provided a more robust evaluation of the proposed method.
- The experimental results are not convincingly superior to state-of-the-art methods, particularly in terms of visual quality and convergence speed.
- The paper does not adequately address the computational complexity of the proposed method, which might be a significant concern for practical applications.
- There are some unclear aspects in the methodology, such as the choice of parameters and the optimization process, which could benefit from further clarification.
- The paper could benefit from a more thorough discussion on the limitations and potential negative societal impacts of the proposed method.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors provide more details on the computational complexity of the proposed method compared to existing methods?
- How does the proposed method compare in terms of visual quality and convergence speed with the most recent state-of-the-art methods?
- Could the authors clarify the optimization process, particularly the role of the parameter μ and the choice of baseline functions?
- How does the proposed method address the societal impacts of text-to-3D generation, especially concerning the potential misuse or ethical concerns?
- Can the authors provide additional results or comparisons to further validate the effectiveness of the proposed method?
