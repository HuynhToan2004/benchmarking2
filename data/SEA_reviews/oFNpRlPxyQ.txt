 **Summary:**
The paper introduces MSPipe, a novel framework designed to accelerate the training of Temporal Graph Neural Networks (TGNNs) by addressing the significant memory and temporal dependencies that hinder their scalability. MSPipe employs a minimal staleness pipeline to maximize training throughput, which is crucial for large-scale applications. The framework includes a staleness mitigation mechanism that effectively reduces staleness errors, ensuring the convergence rate remains the same as vanilla sampling-based GNN training. The paper is supported by comprehensive experiments demonstrating significant speed-ups and improved model accuracy. However, concerns are raised about the incremental nature of the contributions, the clarity of the presentation, and the novelty of the proposed methods compared to existing works.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-organized, easy to follow, and provides a comprehensive analysis of the TGNN training pipeline, profiling the bottlenecks and addressing the temporal dependencies effectively.
- The proposed MSPipe framework is a novel approach that decomposes TGNN training into different stages, enabling a thorough analysis of training bottlenecks and optimizing the training process.
- MSPipe introduces a minimal staleness algorithm and a lightweight staleness mitigation method, which are innovative and effective in reducing staleness errors and maintaining model accuracy.
- The paper includes theoretical convergence analysis and empirical results that demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed method, showing significant speed-ups and improved model accuracy.
- The implementation is provided, which is a valuable resource for the community.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a clear definition and detailed explanation of the TGNN model, which is crucial for understanding the proposed methodologies.
- The similarity-based staleness mitigation method is not thoroughly analyzed, and the results of this analysis are not presented, which could undermine the effectiveness of the proposed method.
- The paper does not adequately discuss the limitations of the proposed method, particularly concerning the scalability of the proposed method to larger datasets.
- The presentation of the paper is not clear in some sections, and there are grammatical errors that need correction.
- The novelty of the proposed method is questioned, as similar ideas have been explored in previous works.
- The paper lacks a detailed discussion on the convergence rate and the impact of the staleness mitigation mechanism on model accuracy.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors provide a clearer definition and explanation of the TGNN model and its components, especially in the early sections of the paper?
- How does the similarity-based staleness mitigation method perform in different scenarios, and what are the results of this analysis?
- Is there a theoretical or empirical analysis that supports the claim that MSPipe maintains the same convergence rate as vanilla sampling-based GNN training?
- How does the proposed method handle the scalability issues, especially when applied to larger datasets?
- Could the authors clarify the definition and role of the "memory module" in the context of TGNNs, and provide a clearer explanation of the staleness mitigation mechanism?
- How does the proposed method compare to other existing methods in terms of speed and accuracy, especially in scenarios where the batch size is increased?
