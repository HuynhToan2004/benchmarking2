 **Summary:**
The paper introduces DV-3Dlane, a novel end-to-end multi-modal 3D lane detection framework that leverages dual-view representation to enhance 3D lane detection. This framework integrates multi-modal features from both images and LiDAR points, utilizing a bidirectional feature fusion strategy and a unified query generator to facilitate feature aggregation across different view spaces. The proposed methodology includes a 3D dual-view deformable attention mechanism that aggregates discriminative features from both perspective and bird's-eye views. Extensive experiments on the OpenLane dataset demonstrate the effectiveness and efficiency of DV-3Dlane, showing significant improvements in F1 score and a substantial reduction in errors compared to existing methods.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel end-to-end multi-modal 3D lane detection framework, DV-3DLane, which effectively integrates multi-modal features from both images and LiDAR points.
- The bidirectional feature fusion strategy, unified query generator, and 3D dual-view deformable attention mechanism are innovative approaches that enhance the performance of 3D lane detection.
- Extensive experiments on the OpenLane benchmark demonstrate the effectiveness and efficiency of DV-3Dlane, showing significant improvements in F1 score and a substantial reduction in errors.
- The paper is well-written, clear, and easy to follow, with a comprehensive explanation of the proposed methodologies and experimental results.
- The use of dual-view representation and multi-modal features effectively harnesses the strengths of both images and LiDAR points, which is a significant advancement in the field of 3D lane detection.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper does not compare its method with a large number of state-of-the-art methods, which limits the understanding of its relative performance.
- The novelty of the paper is limited as the bidirectional feature fusion strategy, unified query generator, and 3D dual-view deformable attention mechanism are similar to existing methods.
- The paper lacks a detailed explanation of the 3D dual-view deformable attention mechanism, which is crucial for understanding the method's effectiveness.
- The computational complexity of the proposed method is not analyzed, which is essential for practical deployment in real-time systems.
- The paper does not provide a detailed comparison with other multi-modal methods, which could have provided a better understanding of the method's advantages over existing methods.
- The paper lacks a discussion on the limitations of the proposed method and does not provide a detailed analysis of the results, particularly in terms of the impact of different modalities on performance.
