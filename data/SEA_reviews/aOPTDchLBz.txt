 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a substantial Hebrew speech dataset, "ivrit.ai," which includes over 10,000 hours of speech from 1,000 speakers, transcribed using the Whisper ASR tool. The dataset is open-sourced under an augmented CC-BY 4.0 license, allowing for commercial use. The dataset's diversity in age, gender, and language backgrounds is highlighted, aiming to enhance Automated Speech Recognition (ASR) technology in Hebrew. However, the paper lacks detailed methodological descriptions and experimental evaluations, which are crucial for assessing the dataset's quality and utility. The absence of a detailed description of the dataset's characteristics, such as acoustic and linguistic diversity, and its potential biases, limits the understanding of its utility and impact.

**Strengths:**
- The dataset is substantial, consisting of 10,000 hours of speech, which is significant for advancing research in Hebrew Automated Speech Recognition (ASR).
- The dataset is open-sourced, available for both academic and commercial use, which enhances accessibility and utility.
- The dataset is diverse, featuring a wide range of speakers with different ages, genders, and language backgrounds, which is beneficial for developing robust ASR models.
- The dataset is fully transcribed using the Whisper ASR tool, providing a valuable resource for researchers and developers.
- The dataset is released under an augmented CC-BY 4.0 license, which allows for commercial use, potentially increasing its impact and utility.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks detailed methodological descriptions, particularly in the data acquisition and processing stages, which are crucial for understanding the dataset's quality and reliability.
- There is a lack of experimental evaluations or comparisons with existing datasets, which is necessary to assess the dataset's utility and impact.
- The dataset's characteristics, such as acoustic and linguistic diversity, and potential biases, are not thoroughly described, limiting the understanding of its utility and impact.
- The paper does not provide details on the data preprocessing techniques used, such as Voice Activity Detection (VAD), which could affect the dataset's utility and reliability.
- The dataset's transcription quality is not thoroughly evaluated, which is crucial for assessing its utility in ASR research.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors provide more detailed methodological descriptions, particularly in the data acquisition and processing stages, to enhance understanding of the dataset's quality and reliability?
- How does the dataset's transcription quality compare to other datasets, and what measures have been taken to ensure its accuracy?
- Could the authors provide more detailed information on the dataset's characteristics, such as acoustic and linguistic diversity, and potential biases, to better understand its utility and impact?
- How does the dataset's release under an augmented CC-BY 4.0 license affect its accessibility and utility for researchers and developers?
- What are the plans for future updates or expansions of the dataset, and how will these be communicated to the research community?
