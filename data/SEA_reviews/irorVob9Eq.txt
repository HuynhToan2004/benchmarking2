 **Summary:**
This paper investigates the interpretability of Capsule Networks (CapsNets) by assessing their ability to encode part-whole relationships. The authors conducted experiments on various datasets, including MNIST, SVHN, and PASCAL, using a perturbation-based analysis and a layer-wise unit selection method. They found that the part-whole relationships are not as distinct as claimed in previous literature. The paper also explores the impact of perturbing different dimensions of the output vector on the network's performance. Despite the interesting findings, the paper is criticized for its limited scope, focusing only on a subset of CapsNets and not addressing the broader interpretability issues in the field.

**Strengths:**
- The paper addresses an important and relevant question regarding the interpretability of Capsule Networks (CapsNets), which is a critical aspect for understanding and improving the transparency and explainability of machine learning models.
- The experimental setup is well-designed and thorough, involving a variety of datasets and different CapsNets, which enhances the robustness and generalizability of the findings.
- The paper is well-written, making it easy to follow and understand, which is crucial for disseminating complex research to a broader audience.
- The authors have conducted a systematic study on the interpretability of CapsNets, which is a timely and relevant topic given the increasing interest in explainable AI.
- The paper includes a novel methodology for evaluating the interpretability of CapsNets, which is a significant contribution to the field.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper's focus is limited to a specific subset of CapsNets, which may not be representative of the broader family of CapsNets, thus limiting the generalizability of the findings.
- The interpretability of CapsNets is a complex and multi-faceted issue that goes beyond the scope of part-whole relationships. The paper could benefit from a more comprehensive exploration of interpretability in CapsNets.
- The paper's methodology and results are not sufficiently novel or insightful, as many of the observations and findings have been previously reported in the literature.
- The paper lacks a thorough comparison with other models, particularly CNNs, which could provide a more robust evaluation of the proposed methodologies.
- The paper does not adequately address the broader implications of its findings for the field of Capsule Networks, which could limit its impact on advancing the understanding and application of these models.
- The paper's writing could be improved for clarity and coherence, particularly in the abstract and the introduction, which could make it more accessible to a broader audience.

**Questions:**
- How does the proposed methodology compare to other interpretability methods for CapsNets, such as those mentioned in related works?
- Could the authors provide a more detailed comparison with other models, particularly CNNs, to better contextualize the findings and contributions of this study?
- What are the broader implications of the findings for the field of Capsule Networks, and how might these findings influence future research and applications of these models?
- How does the proposed methodology address the broader issue of interpretability in Capsule Networks, and what are the specific contributions of this paper to advancing our understanding of this topic?
- Could the authors clarify the relevance and applicability of the proposed methodologies to other types of Capsule Networks beyond those specifically studied in this paper?
