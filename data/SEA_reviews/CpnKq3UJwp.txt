 **Summary:**
The paper introduces MAZero, a model-based multi-agent reinforcement learning (MARL) algorithm that extends the MuZero framework to multi-agent settings. It utilizes a centralized model with Monte Carlo Tree Search (MCTS) for policy search, incorporating a novel network structure to facilitate distributed execution and parameter sharing. The paper presents two new techniques: Optimistic Search Lambda (OS(\u03bb)) and Advantage-Weighted Policy Optimization (AWPO), aimed at enhancing search efficiency in deterministic environments with large action spaces. The experiments conducted on the SMAC benchmark demonstrate that MAZero outperforms model-free approaches in terms of sample efficiency and provides comparable or better performance than existing model-based methods.

**Strengths:**
- The paper effectively extends the MuZero framework to multi-agent settings, incorporating a novel network structure that facilitates distributed execution and parameter sharing.
- The introduction of two new techniques, Optimistic Search Lambda (OS(\u03bb)) and Advantage-Weighted Policy Optimization (AWPO), enhances search efficiency in deterministic environments with large action spaces.
- The experiments conducted on the SMAC benchmark demonstrate that MAZero outperforms model-free approaches in terms of sample efficiency and provides comparable or better performance than existing model-based methods.
- The paper is well-written, making the content accessible and understandable, and includes a comprehensive literature review and theoretical analysis.
- The proposed method is innovative, combining planning and search methods into multi-agent systems, which is a significant advancement in the field of multi-agent reinforcement learning.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed discussion on the challenges and limitations of extending the MuZero framework to multi-agent settings, which could provide a deeper understanding of the complexities involved.
- The paper does not adequately compare the proposed MAZero algorithm with other state-of-the-art model-based MARL algorithms, such as Dreamer-v2, which could have provided a more robust validation of the MAZero's performance.
- The paper does not include experiments in stochastic environments, which is a significant limitation given that most real-world multi-agent systems operate in stochastic environments.
- There are issues with the clarity and completeness of the paper, including missing references, unclear notations, and a lack of detailed explanations in some sections.
- The paper does not discuss the limitations of the proposed method, which is crucial for understanding the scope and applicability of the findings.

**Questions:**
- Could you provide a detailed discussion on the challenges and limitations of extending the MuZero framework to multi-agent settings?
- How does the proposed MAZero algorithm compare with other state-of-the-art model-based MARL algorithms, such as Dreamer-v2?
- Why was the decision made to not include experiments in stochastic environments, and what are the implications of this decision on the generalizability of the findings?
- Can you clarify the notations used in the paper, particularly the meaning of \u03bb in the context of the proposed methods?
- How does the proposed method handle the issue of non-stationarity in multi-agent environments, and what are the implications of this for the stability and generalizability of the results?
