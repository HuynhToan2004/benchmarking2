 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a method for analyzing robustness proofs of deep neural networks (DNNs) by dissecting them into smaller, interpretable parts called "proof features." These features are used to identify the importance of individual neurons in the proof and to prune less important neurons, potentially improving the efficiency and interpretability of robustness proofs. The method, termed ProFit, employs a priority function to evaluate the importance of each proof feature and uses this to guide the pruning process. The paper provides experimental results demonstrating the effectiveness of the proposed method in reducing the number of proof features required and in identifying spurious features in robustness proofs. However, concerns are raised about the scalability of the method to larger, more complex networks and the practicality of the visualization tools.
