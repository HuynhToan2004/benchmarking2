 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel visual object navigation system named SkillTron, which employs a two-level semantic map representation for indoor navigation. The system integrates a PONI-based exploration skill with a goal-reaching skill, which utilizes a RL-based policy for navigation. The interactive segmentation module, SegmATRon, is utilized to predict semantic segmentation masks from image sequences, enhancing the navigation accuracy. The system's performance is evaluated against state-of-the-art methods on the Habitat Challenge 2023, demonstrating significant improvements in navigation metrics. The paper also discusses the use of a filtering module to refine segmentation predictions and a skill fusion decider to select between exploration and goal-reaching strategies.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, making it easy to follow and understand the proposed methods and results.
- The integration of a two-level semantic map representation with a skill fusion decider and a filtering module is a novel approach that enhances the navigation system's effectiveness.
- The interactive segmentation module, SegmATRon, is a significant contribution to the field, providing a more accurate semantic map for navigation.
- The paper demonstrates a significant improvement in navigation performance on the Habitat Challenge 2023, showcasing the effectiveness of the proposed methods.
- The use of a RL-based policy for goal-reaching and a classic path planner for path planning are innovative approaches that could potentially improve navigation efficiency.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed comparison of the interactive segmentation module, SegmATRon, with other state-of-the-art methods, which could provide a clearer understanding of its advantages and limitations.
- The integration of the RL-based policy and classic path planner seems disconnected, and the paper does not adequately explain how these components are integrated.
- The paper's experimental setup and results are not convincingly superior to existing methods, particularly in the success rate and path length metrics.
- The paper could benefit from a more comprehensive discussion on the limitations of the proposed methods and potential future directions for research.
- The use of a single dataset (Habitat Challenge 2023) for evaluation limits the generalizability of the results.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors provide a more detailed comparison of SegmATRon with other state-of-the-art methods, including a discussion on its advantages and limitations?
- How is the RL-based policy and classic path planner integrated within the navigation system? Could the authors provide a clearer explanation of this integration?
- How does the proposed system handle noisy or inaccurate segmentation predictions from the interactive segmentation module?
- Could the authors discuss the potential impacts of using a different dataset for evaluation, such as the Habitat Challenge 2023 with 150 classes, and how this might affect the results?
- How does the skill fusion decider function, and what are the specific criteria used for switching between exploration and goal-reaching strategies?
- Could the authors provide a more detailed discussion on the limitations of the proposed methods and potential future directions for research?
