 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel method for classifier-guided diffusion generation using off-the-shelf classifiers, aiming to improve the calibration of classifiers to enhance diffusion model performance. This method, which includes pre-conditioning techniques such as smoothing the classifier and adjusting the guidance schedule, demonstrates significant performance improvements over existing methods, including training-free and training-based classifier guidance. The approach is validated on ImageNet with various diffusion models and shown to outperform state-of-the-art methods, suggesting its potential applicability in other domains like text-to-image generation. The paper also explores the use of off-the-shelf classifiers, which are typically not robust to Gaussian noise, and proposes techniques to improve their calibration and effectiveness.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, clear, and easy to follow, with a comprehensive experimental setup that includes various diffusion models and off-the-shelf classifiers.
- The proposed method significantly outperforms existing training-free classifier guidance methods and achieves comparable or better performance than training-based methods.
- The methodology is simple and effective, with clear explanations of the intuition behind the proposed techniques.
- The paper introduces novel pre-conditioning techniques that are useful for leveraging off-the-shelf classifiers to guide diffusion generation, potentially applicable to other areas like text-to-image generation.
- The theoretical analysis and experimental results demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed method, showing improvements in performance and calibration of classifiers.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed comparison with other training-free methods, which could help in understanding the unique contributions of the proposed method.
- The paper does not include a discussion on the limitations of the proposed method, such as the applicability to different types of classifiers and the scalability to higher resolution images.
- There is a need for more detailed experimental results, such as visual examples and comparisons with other methods like GLIDE, to better demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed method.
- The paper could benefit from more comprehensive ablation studies and a broader range of experiments to validate the effectiveness of the proposed techniques across different models and datasets.
- The paper's reliance on off-the-shelf classifiers, which may not be robust to Gaussian noise, could limit the generalizability of the findings.

**Questions:**
- Could the proposed method be applied to other types of classifiers, such as those used in text-to-image generation?
- How does the method perform on higher resolution images, and what are the implications for performance and computational efficiency?
- Can the authors provide more visual examples to demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed method, particularly in comparison to other methods like GLIDE?
- What are the potential limitations of the proposed method, and how might these affect its applicability in different scenarios?
- How does the method perform when applied to different diffusion models, and what are the implications for performance and computational efficiency?
