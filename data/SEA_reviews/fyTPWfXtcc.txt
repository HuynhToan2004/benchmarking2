 **Summary:**
The paper explores the application of invex functions in constrained optimization problems, specifically focusing on non-convex constrained inverse problems in signal processing. It introduces a family of invex and quasi-invex functions that can be used to ensure global optima in optimization problems, extending the capabilities of existing methods like the alternating direction method of multipliers and accelerated proximal gradient methods. The paper also provides theoretical guarantees for global optimality and presents numerical tests to demonstrate the effectiveness of these functions in achieving better reconstruction quality compared to convex mappings. Despite its innovative approach, the paper is critiqued for its limited practical applicability and lack of clear comparative analysis with existing methods.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a new family of invex and quasi-invex functions that can be used to ensure global optima in optimization problems, which is a significant contribution to the field of non-convex optimization.
- It provides theoretical guarantees for global optimality, which is a key advantage over existing methods that can only provide local optima.
- The paper is well-written and structured, making it easy to follow and understand the proposed methods and results.
- The application of invex functions to constrained inverse problems is novel and provides a new perspective on solving non-convex problems.
- The paper demonstrates the effectiveness of the proposed functions through numerical tests, showing that they can achieve better reconstruction quality compared to convex mappings.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a clear comparison with existing methods, making it difficult to assess the advantages of the proposed functions over existing methods.
- The practical applicability of the proposed functions is limited, as they are primarily applied to specific signal processing tasks and do not demonstrate broader applicability.
- The paper does not provide sufficient details on the computational efficiency and complexity of the proposed methods, which is crucial for practical implementation.
- The theoretical results and proofs are not always clear or convincing, and some statements and proofs are either incorrect or unclear.
- The paper could benefit from more comprehensive experimental evaluations and comparisons with other non-convex optimization methods to strengthen its claims.

**Questions:**
