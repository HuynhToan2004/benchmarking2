 **Summary:**
The paper investigates the solvability of the Non-negative Inverse Eigenvalue Problem (NIEP) under certain conditions, focusing on the construction of orthogonal matrices that make the inverse eigenvalue problem solvable. The authors propose various methods including Haar basis, Discrete Fourier Transformation (DFT), and a combined approach to address different cases of K and m. They also provide a new algorithm for Non-negative Matrix Factorization (NMF) and discuss its implications for network modeling. The paper presents a comprehensive theoretical analysis and experimental results, showing that the proposed methods can handle a significant portion of the cases and provide insights into the solvability of the NIEP.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, with clear explanations of the problem and its implications, making it accessible to readers.
- The authors provide a detailed analysis of the conditions under which the Non-negative Inverse Eigenvalue Problem (NIEP) is solvable, offering a comprehensive theoretical framework.
- The paper includes a novel algorithm for Non-negative Matrix Factorization (NMF), which is supported by experimental results and theoretical analysis.
- The paper is innovative in its approach, using tools like Haar basis, Discrete Fourier Transformation (DFT), and a combined Haar-DFT approach to tackle the NIEP, which is a challenging problem in the field.
- The paper is well-structured, with clear definitions and a logical flow that makes it easy to follow, even for readers not familiar with the specific sub-problems of the NIEP.
- The authors provide a Python code for the proposed algorithm, which is a valuable resource for practitioners and researchers in the field.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed discussion on the computational complexity of the proposed algorithms, which could affect their practical applicability.
- There is a need for more clarity in the presentation of the algorithms and their theoretical underpinnings, particularly in the explanation of the Haar and DFT approaches.
- The paper could benefit from more experimental results to validate the theoretical claims, especially in scenarios where the results are not as clear-cut.
- The paper's focus on a specific type of NIEP might limit its applicability to other types of non-negative matrices, which could be a significant drawback for broader applicability.
- The paper could benefit from a more thorough comparison with existing works, particularly in terms of the novelty and depth of the theoretical contributions.
