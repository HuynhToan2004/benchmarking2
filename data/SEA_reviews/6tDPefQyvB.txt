 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel method for keypoint detection and description, focusing on rotation equivariance in feature extraction. It leverages a multi-scale rotation-equivariant feature fusion with positional information to enhance the robustness of keypoint descriptors. The proposed method integrates a multi-scale feature pyramid with rotation equivariance and global positional information, along with a directional uncertainty weighted descriptor loss. Experiments on various datasets, including rotated-HPatches, rotated-MegaDepth, and rotated-YFCC100M, demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed method. Despite its innovative approach, the paper has been critiqued for its lack of clarity in methodological details, insufficient experimental validation, and potential over-reliance on existing techniques without significant novel contributions.

**Strengths:**
- The paper addresses a significant and relevant problem in the field of robot vision, focusing on rotation equivariance in feature extraction, which is crucial for enhancing the robustness of keypoint descriptors.
- The proposed method integrates a multi-scale feature pyramid with rotation equivariance and global positional information, which is a novel approach that could potentially enhance the performance of keypoint extraction and description.
- The paper provides a comprehensive evaluation of the proposed method on various datasets, including rotated-HPatches, rotated-MegaDepth, and rotated-YFCC100M, demonstrating the effectiveness of the method in handling rotational variations.
- The paper is well-written, making it accessible and easy to follow, which is essential for a technical paper.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed explanation of the multi-scale rotation-equivariant feature fusion module and the Directional Uncertainty Weighted Descriptor Loss, which are crucial components of the proposed method.
- There is an absence of experimental validation on real-world datasets, which could provide a more robust assessment of the proposed method's effectiveness in practical scenarios.
- The paper does not sufficiently differentiate its approach from existing methods, such as the LoFTR, and does not provide a clear explanation of how it addresses the limitations of these methods.
- The paper does not include a detailed runtime analysis or a comparison of the number of parameters and FLOPs with other methods, which could help in understanding the computational efficiency of the proposed method.
- The paper lacks a thorough discussion on the limitations of the proposed method and potential negative societal impacts, which are essential for a complete evaluation of the technology.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors provide a detailed explanation of the multi-scale rotation-equivariant feature fusion module and the Directional Uncertainty Weighted Descriptor Loss, including the mathematical formulations and their roles in the overall method?
- How does the proposed method perform in scenarios where the rotation angle is not known, such as in real-world applications where the rotation angle may not be available?
- Could the authors clarify the differences between the proposed method and existing methods, such as the LoFTR, and explain how the proposed method addresses the limitations of these methods?
- How does the proposed method compare in terms of runtime, number of parameters, and FLOPs with other methods, especially in scenarios where computational efficiency is critical?
- Can the authors provide more detailed results on the effectiveness of the proposed method in real-world scenarios, such as in applications like visual odometry, visual position recognition, and object tracking?
