 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel method for transfer learning in Bayesian Neural Networks (BNNs) using a nonparametric approach. This method, termed Nonparametric Transfer Learning (NPTL), employs a Dirichlet Process (DP) prior to sample weights from the posterior distribution, which is then used for Bayesian model averaging. The method is designed to handle large datasets and large parameter spaces, utilizing a block Dirichlet distribution for efficient sampling. The paper includes empirical validations across various datasets, demonstrating the method's effectiveness in improving performance over traditional methods. However, concerns are raised about the clarity of the method's theoretical underpinnings, particularly in how the Dirichlet Process prior is used, and the practical implications of the method's computational complexity and scalability.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, easy to follow, and provides clear explanations of the methodology, making it accessible to readers.
- The empirical results are strong, showing the method's effectiveness across multiple datasets and tasks, including vision and language models.
- The paper addresses the significant challenge of transfer learning in Bayesian Neural Networks (BNNs) by proposing a novel method that leverages nonparametric learning to improve performance.
- The use of a Dirichlet process prior for Bayesian neural networks is innovative and provides a practical solution for handling large datasets and parameter spaces.
- The method is shown to be efficient, reducing the computational cost of Bayesian model averaging, and is scalable to large datasets.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed theoretical analysis of the method's performance, particularly in terms of how the Dirichlet Process prior is used and its impact on the results.
- The method's scalability is limited by the number of data points, which could affect its practical applicability in real-world scenarios.
- The paper does not adequately address the computational complexity of the method, which could be a significant barrier for practical implementation.
- There are several minor errors and inconsistencies in the paper, such as incorrect citations and formatting issues.
- The method's reliance on a fixed base measure from the source task could limit its effectiveness in scenarios where the source and target tasks have significant differences.
- The paper does not sufficiently discuss the limitations of the method, such as its inability to handle complex data distributions or its reliance on a fixed base measure.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors provide a more detailed theoretical analysis of how the Dirichlet Process prior is used in the method and its impact on the results?
- How does the method perform when the number of data points is large, and what are the computational implications of this setup?
- Can the authors clarify the role of the hyperparameter $\alpha$ in the method and discuss its sensitivity in different scenarios?
- How does the method compare to other nonparametric transfer learning methods, and what are the advantages of using the Dirichlet Process prior specifically?
- Could the authors discuss the limitations of the method, particularly in scenarios where the source and target tasks have significant differences?
- How does the method handle the computational complexity, and are there any optimizations that could be made to improve its efficiency?
