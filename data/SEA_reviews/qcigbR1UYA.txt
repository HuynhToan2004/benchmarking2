 **Summary:**
The paper investigates the performance of the InfoMax algorithm in binary testing scenarios, focusing on the case where tests are either oracle (deterministic) or noisy. It introduces the concept of a δ-unpredictable set of tests, which is crucial for the InfoMax algorithm to perform effectively. The paper derives upper bounds on the expected number of tests needed for the InfoMax algorithm to accurately predict a binary random variable Y, considering both oracle and noisy tests. The analysis assumes that tests are δ-unpredictable, meaning that they can approximate bisecting tests, and that test outcomes are corrupted by a binary symmetric channel. The paper also discusses the limitations of these assumptions and the potential impact of more complex noise models.

**Strengths:**
- The paper provides a novel analysis of the InfoMax algorithm in binary testing scenarios, introducing the concept of a δ-unpredictable set of tests, which is a significant contribution to the field.
- The results are supported by a thorough theoretical analysis and practical examples, demonstrating the applicability of the theoretical findings.
- The paper is well-written, clear, and structured, making it accessible to a broad audience and enhancing its readability.
- The authors have derived new upper bounds for the InfoMax algorithm in both oracle and noisy test cases, which are potentially useful for real-world applications.
- The paper discusses the limitations of the assumptions made, such as the i.i.d. noise assumption and the δ-unpredictability assumption, which are critical for understanding the scope and applicability of the results.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper heavily relies on the assumption that tests are δ-unpredictable, which may not hold in practical scenarios where tests are not bisecting. This could limit the generalizability of the results.
- The results presented are based on theoretical analysis and do not include experimental validation, which could strengthen the paper by demonstrating the practical applicability of the theoretical findings.
- The paper does not discuss the impact of different noise models, such as dependent noise, which could affect the performance of the InfoMax algorithm in real-world scenarios.
- The paper's assumptions, such as the i.i.d. noise assumption and the δ-unpredictability assumption, are not thoroughly justified or discussed, which could lead to confusion or misinterpretation of the results.
- The paper could benefit from a more detailed discussion on the limitations of the results and the assumptions made, as well as potential directions for future research.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors elaborate on the practical implications of the δ-unpredictability assumption and discuss how it might be verified in real-world scenarios?
- How does the paper's analysis extend to more complex noise models, such as dependent noise, and what are the implications for the performance of the InfoMax algorithm in such scenarios?
- Can the authors provide examples or scenarios where the InfoMax algorithm with a δ-unpredictable set of tests outperforms the optimal algorithm, or where it fails to perform as expected?
- How does the paper's analysis compare to existing results, particularly in terms of the assumptions made and the bounds derived?
- Could the authors discuss the potential impact of different noise models on the performance of the InfoMax algorithm and how this might affect the practical applicability of the results?
