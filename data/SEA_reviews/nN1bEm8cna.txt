 **Summary:**
The paper investigates the expressive power of spiking neural networks (SNNs) by analyzing the Spike Response Model (SRM) and the linear response function. It demonstrates that SRMs generate continuous piecewise linear mappings and can emulate ReLU neural networks with similar complexity. The authors prove that the number of linear regions generated by a spiking neuron scales exponentially with respect to the input dimension, distinguishing it significantly from a ReLU neuron. The study also explores the approximation properties of SNNs and suggests new applications where SNNs can be deployed instead of ANNs without performance loss. However, the paper's main contributions are seen as extensions of existing theoretical results, with the practical implications of the findings not fully explored.

**Strengths:**
- The paper provides a comprehensive analysis of the expressive power of spiking neural networks (SNNs) using the Spike Response Model (SRM) and the linear response function, demonstrating that SRMs generate continuous piecewise linear mappings and can emulate any multi-layer ReLU neural networks with similar complexity.
- It shows that the number of linear regions generated by a spiking neuron scales exponentially with respect to the input dimension, a characteristic that distinguishes SNNs significantly from ANNs.
- The paper is well-organized, with clear definitions and theorems, and includes a good literature review, making it accessible and informative.
- The authors have successfully demonstrated that SNNs can emulate the expressiveness of ReLU networks using a two-layer LSRM, which is a significant theoretical contribution.
- The paper's findings open up new avenues for deploying SNNs instead of ANNs without performance loss, which is crucial for energy-efficient AI applications.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper's main contributions are seen as extensions of existing theoretical results, such as the universality theorem for SRMs and the exponential number of linear regions for SNNs, which were previously known.
- The practical implications of the findings are not fully explored, particularly in terms of how the results can be applied in real-world scenarios.
- The paper's analysis is limited to a specific model of SNNs, which might not generalize well to other models or practical implementations.
- The paper does not sufficiently address the practical challenges in training SNNs, which is a significant limitation given the current state of the field.
- The paper's assumptions, such as the linear response function and the single-spike condition, are not well-justified or explained, which could limit the applicability of the results.
- The paper does not sufficiently compare the expressive power of SNNs with other neural network architectures, such as convolutional neural networks (CNNs), which could provide a more comprehensive understanding of SNNs' capabilities.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors provide more details on the practical implications of their findings? How can these results be applied in real-world scenarios, especially in energy-efficient AI applications?
- How does the paper's analysis compare to other models of SNNs, such as the leaky integrate-and-fire model?
- What are the practical challenges in training SNNs, and how do these compare to the challenges in training ANNs?
- How does the paper's analysis of SNNs' expressive power compare to other neural network architectures, such as CNNs?
- Can the authors clarify the assumptions made in their analysis, such as the linear response function and the single-spike condition, and discuss the implications of these assumptions on the results?
