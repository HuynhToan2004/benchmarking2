 **Summary:**
The paper explores the use of analytic functions to formulate Directed Acyclic Graph (DAG) constraints in continuous DAG learning. It introduces a set of functions, F, which includes all analytic functions and is closed under various operators like differentiation, summation, and multiplication. This property allows for the creation of novel DAG constraints based on existing ones. The authors propose a framework for DAG learning, focusing on the convergence radius of analytic functions as a performance indicator. They argue that the convergence radius can be used to mitigate gradient vanishing and non-convexity issues in DAG learning. The paper also discusses the computational efficiency of these constraints and provides empirical evidence to support their effectiveness.

**Strengths:**
- The paper provides a unified framework for DAG constraints, which is significant for the DAG learning community.
- The paper is well-written, making it easy to follow and understand.
- The authors propose a novel set of DAG constraints based on analytic functions, which is a new approach in DAG learning.
- The paper includes a detailed discussion on the convergence radius of analytic functions and its implications for DAG learning.
- The proposed analytic function-based DAG constraints are shown to be closed under various functional operators, enhancing their utility.
- The paper provides empirical evidence to support the effectiveness of the proposed constraints, which is crucial for validating the theoretical claims.
- The paper addresses the issue of gradient vanishing in DAG learning, which is a significant challenge in the field.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper primarily focuses on linear SEM with known data scale, which may limit its applicability to more complex or varied scenarios.
- The paper does not sufficiently address the issue of gradient vanishing, which is a significant limitation given the claims made about mitigating this issue.
- The empirical results presented are not convincing, particularly in the comparison between different types of DAG constraints.
- The paper lacks a detailed discussion on the computational complexity and efficiency of the proposed constraints, which is crucial for practical applications.
- The paper does not sufficiently compare the proposed constraints with existing methods, such as those by Bello et al. (2022) and Zhang et al. (2022), which could provide a more robust validation of the proposed approach.
- The paper does not sufficiently address the scalability of the proposed constraints, particularly in large-scale applications.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors clarify how the convergence radius of analytic functions impacts the gradient vanishing issue in DAG learning?
- How do the proposed analytic function-based DAG constraints compare in terms of computational complexity and efficiency with existing methods?
- Can the authors provide more detailed empirical evidence to support the claims made about the effectiveness of the proposed constraints?
- How do the authors plan to address the scalability issues with larger datasets or more complex DAGs?
- Can the authors provide a more detailed comparison with existing methods, particularly those by Bello et al. (2022) and Zhang et al. (2022), to validate the claims made about the novelty and effectiveness of the proposed approach?
- How do the authors address the non-convexity of the optimization problem in DAG learning, particularly in scenarios where the ground truth scale is unknown?
