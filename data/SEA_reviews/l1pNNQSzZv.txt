 **Summary:**
The paper introduces RaDAgent, a novel decision-making agent that integrates an Elo-based utility construction system to assess the rationality of its decisions. This system assigns Elo scores to each decision step, allowing the agent to judge the utility of these steps through pairwise comparisons. The methodology is evaluated on various datasets, including Game of 24, WebShop, and ToolBench, demonstrating significant improvements over existing baselines. The paper highlights the method's efficiency in reducing the number of API calls, showcasing its practical utility. However, concerns about the novelty of the approach, the fairness of the comparisons, and the lack of detailed error analysis were raised.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written and easy to follow, making complex concepts accessible to a broader audience.
- The proposed method is innovative, particularly in its application of Elo-based utility construction to LLMs, which enhances the decision-making process.
- The method's efficiency is demonstrated through a significant reduction in the number of API calls, which is crucial for practical applications.
- The paper provides a comprehensive evaluation across multiple datasets and benchmarks, demonstrating the method's effectiveness and robustness.
- The authors have conducted a thorough error analysis, which adds depth to the understanding of the method's capabilities and limitations.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed discussion on the limitations of the proposed method, which is crucial for understanding its applicability and scope.
- The novelty of the approach is questioned as similar methods using Elo scores for decision-making have been previously explored.
- The fairness of the comparison is not adequately addressed, particularly in the use of different models (ChatGPT vs. GPT-4) for different baselines.
- The paper does not adequately address how the method handles exceptions, which could significantly impact the decision-making process.
- The paper could benefit from a more detailed discussion on the rationality construction process and the specifics of how Elo scores are calculated and used.
- The paper's evaluation metrics and their relevance to the decision-making process are not clearly explained, which could confuse the reader about the method's effectiveness.

**Questions:**
- How does the method handle exceptions, particularly in scenarios where decisions need to be reversed or corrected?
- Can the authors provide a more detailed discussion on the rationality construction process and the specifics of how Elo scores are calculated and used?
- How does the method compare in terms of performance when using different models for the baseline, particularly in scenarios where different models are used for different baselines?
- Could the authors clarify the evaluation metrics used and their relevance to the decision-making process?
- How does the method perform when compared to other decision-making methods that do not rely on external performance measures?
- Can the authors provide more details on the implementation of the method, particularly how it is integrated with the LLM and how the decision steps are selected?
