 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel framework for program induction using evolution strategies, focusing on sketch-based program induction. The approach involves parameterizing programs and optimizing them using gradient-based search algorithms, which are applied to both discrete and continuous program parameters. The method is evaluated on simple programs, demonstrating the ability to optimize programs from a sketch, although the evaluation is limited to toy examples. The paper discusses the challenges of program induction, such as search space complexity and valid program generation, and proposes a method that reduces the search space by using a sketch as a starting point. However, the paper lacks a comprehensive experimental evaluation, particularly against state-of-the-art methods, and the presentation of the methodology and results is considered insufficiently clear.

**Strengths:**
- The paper addresses an important and relevant problem in the field of program induction, which is a significant challenge in the development of automated programming systems.
- The approach of using evolution strategies to optimize program parameters is novel and shows promise in reducing the search space and potentially improving the efficiency of program induction.
- The paper is well-written and provides a clear explanation of the challenges in program induction, making it accessible and understandable for readers.
- The proposed method of using sketching to guide program induction is innovative and could potentially be a useful technique for reducing the complexity of program induction tasks.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a comprehensive experimental evaluation, with only two toy examples used to demonstrate the method, which does not convincingly show the effectiveness or scalability of the proposed approach.
- The presentation of the methodology and results is unclear and insufficiently detailed, making it difficult for readers to fully understand the experimental setup, parameters, and results.
- The paper does not compare the proposed method against existing state-of-the-art methods, which limits the ability to assess the relative performance and contributions of the proposed approach.
- The paper does not provide a clear explanation of the main contributions or the technical details of the method, which could hinder understanding and replication of the results.
- The paper suffers from several typographical and formatting errors, which detract from the professionalism and readability of the manuscript.
