 **Summary:**
The paper investigates generalization bounds in minimax problems using uniform localized convergence, which relaxes the Lipschitz continuity assumption. It introduces sharper high-probability bounds for nonconvex-strongly-concave stochastic minimax problems, providing dimension-independent results under the Polyak-Lojasiewicz condition for the outer layer. The authors analyze popular algorithms such as the empirical saddle point, gradient descent ascent, and stochastic gradient descent ascent, improving generalization bounds for primal functions. The paper aims to provide a tighter analysis of generalization bounds compared to previous works, though it faces criticism for its lack of novelty and clarity in presentation.

**Strengths:**
- The paper provides a tighter analysis of generalization bounds compared to previous works, particularly in the context of minimax problems.
- The authors have introduced a new framework for analyzing generalization bounds in minimax problems, which is a significant contribution to the field.
- The paper is well-structured, making it easy to follow, and the results are clearly presented with detailed proofs.
- The paper provides a sharper high-probability generalization bound for nonconvex-strongly-concave stochastic minimax problems, which is a notable improvement over previous works.
- The authors have analyzed the generalization bound for popular algorithms such as the empirical saddle point, gradient descent ascent, and stochastic gradient descent ascent, which are widely used in minimax problems.
- The paper is well-written, with clear notation and definitions, and the results are well-motivated and clearly presented.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks novelty as it builds on existing works such as Li and Liu (2021), which also analyzed generalization bounds using uniform localized convergence.
- The paper requires strong assumptions such as the Polyak-Lojasiewicz condition for the outer layer, which might limit its applicability in practical scenarios.
- The paper's presentation could be improved, particularly in the clarity of the proofs and the explanation of the assumptions and results.
- The paper does not provide a clear comparison with previous works, particularly in terms of the novelty and the contributions of the new framework.
- The paper suffers from some typographical errors and inconsistencies in the proofs and assumptions, which could affect the credibility of the results.
- The paper's scope is somewhat limited as it focuses primarily on minimax problems, which may not be of broad interest to the machine learning community.
