 **Summary:**
The paper introduces Syntheseus, a benchmarking library for retrosynthesis algorithms designed to promote best practices in evaluating both single-step and multi-step retrosynthesis models. The library aims to address inconsistencies in previous evaluations by providing a standardized evaluation framework, which has led to re-evaluations of existing models, revealing discrepancies in their reported performance. The authors highlight the importance of evaluating precision and diversity in addition to recall and success rate, respectively. The paper also discusses the challenges in evaluating retrosynthesis algorithms and proposes new metrics and best practices to enhance the evaluation process.

**Strengths:**
- The paper provides a comprehensive evaluation of existing retrosynthesis algorithms, including both single-step and multi-step models, which is crucial for understanding the current state of the field.
- The introduction of the Syntheseus library is a significant contribution, as it allows for a consistent and standardized evaluation of retrosynthesis algorithms, which is essential for advancing the field.
- The paper is well-written, clear, and easy to follow, making it accessible to a broad audience, including both experts and newcomers to the field.
- The authors have highlighted important issues in the evaluation of retrosynthesis algorithms and proposed new metrics and best practices to address these issues, which could lead to more robust and reliable evaluations in the future.
- The re-evaluation of existing models using the Syntheseus library reveals discrepancies in their reported performance, which could lead to new insights and improvements in the field.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a clear explanation of the new metrics and best practices, particularly in Section 2, which could benefit from more detailed examples and explanations.
- There is a lack of discussion on the limitations and potential biases of the Syntheseus library, which could affect the reliability and generalizability of the results.
- The paper does not adequately address the issue of data leakage in retrosynthesis models, which could significantly impact the validity of the results.
- The evaluation of multi-step search algorithms is limited, and the paper does not include a comprehensive evaluation of all relevant models, which could limit the generalizability of the findings.
- The paper does not provide sufficient details on the implementation and operation of the Syntheseus library, which could make it difficult for users to adopt and use effectively.
- The paper does not discuss the ethical considerations of retrosynthesis algorithms, which could be a significant concern for the field.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors clarify how the Syntheseus library handles data leakage, particularly in models that use atom mapping information?
- How does the Syntheseus library ensure that the evaluation metrics are not biased towards certain types of models or algorithms?
- Could the authors provide more detailed examples and explanations of the new metrics and best practices proposed in the paper?
- How does the Syntheseus library compare to other benchmarking tools, and what are its unique strengths and weaknesses?
- Could the authors discuss the ethical considerations of retrosynthesis algorithms, particularly in terms of their potential impact on chemical manufacturing and the environment?
- How can the community ensure that the Syntheseus library remains up-to-date and relevant as new models and algorithms are developed?
