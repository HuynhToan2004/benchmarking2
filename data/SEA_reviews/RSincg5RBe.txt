 **Summary:**
The paper introduces the Graph Latent Diffusion Model (GLDM) and the Latent Diffusion Model for Hierarchical Graph (HGLDM) for molecule generation, focusing on addressing the mismatch between continuous diffusion space and discrete data space. GLDM encodes molecules into graph-level embeddings, while HGLDM incorporates node-level and subgraph-level information to enhance the model's understanding of molecular structures. Both models leverage a hierarchical latent diffusion process to generate molecules, aiming to improve computational efficiency and performance over existing methods. The paper also includes experiments on four benchmark datasets, demonstrating the superiority of HGLDM over GLDM and other baseline methods.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel approach to molecule generation using diffusion models, leveraging hierarchical latent embeddings to improve performance over existing methods.
- The methodology is well-articulated, with clear explanations of the hierarchical latent diffusion process and its application to molecule generation.
- The paper is well-organized, making it easy to follow and understand, and includes extensive experiments on four benchmark datasets, demonstrating the superiority of the proposed methods over GLDM and other baseline methods.
- The paper addresses the mismatch problem of continuous diffusion space and discrete data space, which is crucial for generating molecules effectively.
- The experimental results show that the proposed method outperforms existing methods in terms of performance and computational efficiency.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed discussion on the limitations and potential negative societal impacts of the proposed methods, which is crucial for ensuring ethical and responsible use of the technology.
- The novelty of the method is questioned, as similar approaches using hierarchical latent diffusion have been previously explored in other domains.
- The paper does not provide sufficient details on the experimental setup, such as the hyperparameters used and the specifics of the conditional generation process, which could affect the reproducibility and comparability of the results.
- There are concerns about the scalability of the method, particularly in terms of the number of latent variables and the complexity of the diffusion process, which could impact the model's performance and computational efficiency.
- The paper does not sufficiently address the limitations of the proposed methods, such as the potential for overfitting and the need for more diverse training data to ensure generalizability.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors provide more details on the limitations and potential negative societal impacts of the proposed methods?
- How does the proposed method compare to other existing methods in terms of scalability and computational efficiency?
- What are the specific hyperparameters used in the experiments, and how were they chosen?
- Can the authors provide more details on the conditional generation process, including how the conditioning information is incorporated into the model?
- How does the model handle the potential for overfitting, and what steps have been taken to mitigate this risk?
- Could the authors clarify the role of the autoencoder in the hierarchical diffusion process, particularly in terms of its impact on the model's performance and the generation of diverse and novel molecular structures?
