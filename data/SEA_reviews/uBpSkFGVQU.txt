 **Summary:**
The paper explores the integration of depth signals and 3D augmentations into self-supervised learning (SSL) frameworks, aiming to enhance the robustness and generalization of representations learned. The authors evaluate their methods on various datasets including ImageNet, ImageNet-C, and ImageNet-3DCC, comparing the performance of SSL methods with and without depth signals and 3D augmentations. The proposed methods, including the use of depth maps and synthesized views, are shown to improve the performance of SSL methods like BYOL, SimSiam, and SwAV. However, the paper is criticized for its limited novelty and lack of comprehensive experimental validation across different datasets and SSL methods.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, easy to follow, and the methodology is clearly described.
- The integration of depth signals and 3D augmentations into SSL frameworks is a novel approach that enhances the robustness and generalization of learned representations.
- The paper provides a comprehensive evaluation of the proposed methods on various datasets, including ImageNet, ImageNet-C, and ImageNet-3DCC, and across different SSL methods such as BYOL, SimSiam, and SwAV.
- The use of depth maps and synthesized views in SSL frameworks is a promising direction for improving the performance of these methods.
- The paper includes ablation studies and additional results in the appendix, providing a more detailed analysis of the proposed methods.

**Weaknesses:**
- The novelty of the paper is limited as similar ideas have been explored in previous works.
- The paper lacks a comprehensive experimental validation across different datasets and SSL methods. The experiments are primarily focused on ImageNet and its variants, and do not include other commonly used datasets such as Kinetics, COCO, or JFT-300.
- The paper does not sufficiently compare the proposed methods with state-of-the-art SSL methods like DINO, MOViE, and MAE, which could provide a more robust evaluation of the proposed methods.
- The paper's claims regarding the superiority of the proposed methods over existing SSL methods are not convincingly supported by the experimental results.
- The paper lacks a detailed analysis of the computational cost and training time associated with the proposed methods, which could be a significant limitation for practical implementation.
- The paper's evaluation is limited to a single backbone (ResNet-18), which may not generalize well to other backbones such as ViT or Swin.

**Questions:**
- How does the proposed method perform on other datasets such as Kinetics, COCO, or JFT-300?
- Can the authors provide a more detailed comparison of the proposed methods with state-of-the-art SSL methods like DINO, MOViE, and MAE?
- How does the proposed method perform on different backbones such as ViT or Swin?
- Can the authors provide a more detailed analysis of the computational cost and training time associated with the proposed methods?
- How does the proposed method perform on out-of-distribution datasets, and what are the implications for practical applications?
- Can the authors clarify the discrepancies in the results reported in different tables and figures, particularly the performance of the baseline methods and the proposed methods?
