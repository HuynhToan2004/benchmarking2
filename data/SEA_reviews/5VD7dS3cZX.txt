 **Summary:**
The paper investigates the "curse of dimensionality" in certified robustness for neural networks, focusing on the Randomized Smoothing (RS) method. It proposes using Exponential General Gaussian (EGG) distributions as smoothing distributions, which are theoretically shown to provide tighter lower bounds for certified robustness radii than the traditional General Gaussian distribution. The authors also provide numerical simulations to support their claims, showing that EGG distributions can improve robustness certificates in high-dimensional settings. However, the paper faces criticisms regarding the practicality of the theoretical results, the necessity of the proposed distributions, and the clarity of the experimental results.

**Strengths:**
- The paper addresses the significant issue of the "curse of dimensionality" in certified robustness, which is a critical area of research in adversarial machine learning.
- The authors provide a theoretical analysis of the lower bounds of certified robustness radius for EGG distributions, which is novel and contributes to the understanding of robustness in high-dimensional spaces.
- The paper is well-written and easy to follow, making it accessible to a broad audience.
- The proposed EGG distribution is novel and theoretically sound, offering potential improvements over existing methods like DSRS.
- The paper presents numerical simulations that support the theoretical claims, demonstrating the practical applicability of the EGG distribution.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed explanation of why EGG distributions are particularly effective in improving robustness certificates, which could enhance the understanding of the theoretical contributions.
- The experimental results, particularly those shown in Table 3, are not convincing and do not clearly demonstrate the superiority of EGG over other distributions.
- There is a significant gap between the theoretical results and their practical application, which is not adequately addressed in the paper.
- The paper does not sufficiently compare the proposed methods with existing approaches, such as those using anisotropic or sample-specific smoothing noise, which could provide a more robust evaluation of the proposed methods.
- The paper's claims about the "curse of dimensionality" being solved are overstated, as the proposed methods do not fully address the fundamental limitations of certified robustness in high-dimensional spaces.
- The paper's presentation could be improved to better clarify the contributions and the implications of the results, particularly in the experimental sections.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors provide a more detailed explanation of why EGG distributions are particularly effective in improving robustness certificates?
- How does the proposed method compare with other existing methods that use anisotropic or sample-specific smoothing noise?
- In light of the concerns about the practical applicability of the theoretical results, can the authors provide more evidence or justification for the effectiveness of EGG in real-world scenarios?
- How does the paper address the limitations of the certified robustness in high-dimensional spaces, especially in terms of the fundamental limitations of the RS method?
- Can the authors clarify the experimental results, particularly the discrepancies between the theoretical predictions and the empirical results?
