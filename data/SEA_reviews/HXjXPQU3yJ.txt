 **Summary:**
The paper investigates the performance of the Plug-and-Play (PnP) algorithm under mismatched conditions, specifically when the distribution of the data used for training the denoiser differs from the distribution of the data used for inference. The authors extend the PnP-ADMM algorithm to handle such mismatches and provide a theoretical analysis of its convergence, which is applicable to nonconvex data-fidelity terms and expansive denoisers. They also propose a domain adaptation strategy to mitigate the effects of distribution mismatch. The paper is supported by numerical results demonstrating the effectiveness of the proposed methods in image super-resolution tasks. However, the paper's novelty is questioned, as similar theoretical analyses and domain adaptation strategies have been previously explored.

**Strengths:**
- The paper addresses the practical problem of distribution mismatch in the Plug-and-Play (PnP) algorithm, which is a significant and relevant issue in the field.
- The theoretical analysis of the PnP-ADMM algorithm with mismatched priors is a valuable contribution to the literature, providing insights into the behavior of the algorithm under different conditions.
- The numerical results presented in the paper demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed methods in mitigating the effects of distribution mismatch, which is crucial for practical applications.
- The paper is well-written, clear, and easy to follow, making it accessible to a broad audience.
- The problem addressed is important and relevant, as it pertains to the practical deployment of deep learning-based denoisers in real-world scenarios.

**Weaknesses:**
- The novelty of the theoretical analysis is limited, as similar analyses have been previously conducted for the PnP-ADMM algorithm.
- The paper lacks a detailed discussion on the domain adaptation strategy, which is a significant aspect of the proposed method.
- The assumptions made in the theoretical analysis, such as the boundedness of the gradient of the regularizer, may not hold in practical scenarios, which could limit the applicability of the results.
- The paper does not sufficiently differentiate its contributions from existing works, particularly those by Chan and Teodoro, which also address similar issues in the PnP framework.
- The numerical experiments are limited to a single task (super-resolution), and it would be beneficial to see how the proposed methods perform in other tasks such as image denoising or deblurring.
- The paper could benefit from a more detailed comparison with existing methods, especially those that address similar issues in the PnP framework.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors clarify the novelty of their theoretical analysis compared to existing works, particularly those by Chan and Teodoro?
- How does the proposed domain adaptation strategy compare to other existing methods in terms of effectiveness and practical applicability?
- Can the authors provide more details on the assumptions made in the theoretical analysis, particularly the boundedness of the gradient of the regularizer?
- How do the authors justify the choice of the ADMM algorithm over other optimization methods in their experiments?
- Could the authors discuss the practical implications of the theoretical results, especially in terms of the assumptions made and their applicability in real-world scenarios?
