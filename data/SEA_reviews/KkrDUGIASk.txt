 **Summary:**
The paper introduces HETerogeneous ALliance (HEAL), a novel framework for collaborative perception in heterogeneous environments. HEAL addresses the challenge of accommodating new heterogeneous agent types by establishing a unified feature space through a Pyramid Fusion network. This framework enables new agents to align their features to the established space, reducing training costs and ensuring privacy. The paper also introduces a new large-scale dataset, OPV2V-H, which supports diverse agent types and modalities. Experiments demonstrate that HEAL outperforms existing methods in terms of performance and cost-efficiency.

**Strengths:**
- The paper addresses an important and relevant problem in the field of multi-agent collaborative perception, focusing on the integration of heterogeneous agent types.
- The proposed Pyramid Fusion network is innovative and effective in establishing a unified feature space, which is crucial for accommodating new agents.
- The introduction of a new dataset, OPV2V-H, is a significant contribution to the research community, supporting diverse agent types and modalities.
- The paper is well-written, with clear and concise presentation of the problem, methodology, and results.
- The proposed HEAL framework shows promising results in terms of performance and cost-efficiency, outperforming existing methods in the same scenario.
- The paper includes ablation studies that demonstrate the effectiveness of individual components of the HEAL framework.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a comprehensive comparison with other state-of-the-art methods, particularly in terms of training costs and performance.
- The description of the Pyramid Fusion network and the training process is not detailed enough, making it difficult to understand the specifics of how the network operates and how new agents are integrated.
- The paper does not adequately address the issue of occlusion, which is a significant challenge in multi-agent collaborative perception.
- The experimental results are not convincingly superior to existing methods, and the paper does not sufficiently discuss the limitations of the proposed method.
- There is a lack of discussion on the generalizability of the proposed method to other types of heterogeneous agents and the scalability of the framework with an increasing number of agent types.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors clarify how the Pyramid Fusion network is trained and how it integrates new agents?
- How does the HEAL framework handle occlusion in multi-agent collaborative perception?
- Could the authors provide more details on the training costs and performance comparisons with other state-of-the-art methods?
- How does the HEAL framework perform in scenarios with a larger number of agent types, and what are the potential limitations in such scenarios?
- Can the authors discuss the potential impact of the proposed method on the privacy and security of the data used in the collaborative perception process?
