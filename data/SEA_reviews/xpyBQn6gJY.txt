 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel method, TAROT, for analyzing temporal trajectories in single-cell RNA-seq data using optimal transport and machine learning techniques. It leverages a large-scale dataset of mouse retinal ganglion cells to extract high-quality cell representations and optimize temporal trajectories. The method integrates biology priors and regularization to improve the accuracy of trajectory inference, and it is evaluated against several existing methods on two public datasets. The results show that TAROT outperforms existing methods in terms of several metrics, suggesting its effectiveness in predicting cell differentiation and understanding cell lineages.

**Strengths:**
- The paper presents a novel approach by integrating optimal transport with biology priors to analyze single-cell RNA-seq data, which is a significant advancement in the field.
- The introduction of a large-scale dataset of mouse retinal ganglion cells, along with the development of a new algorithm, TAROT, provides a robust framework for understanding cell differentiation and lineages.
- The paper is well-written, clearly presenting the methodology and results, with detailed ablation studies that demonstrate the effectiveness of each component of the algorithm.
- The use of B-spline for modeling trajectory is a novel and interesting approach, which could be applied to other biological datasets.
- The paper includes a comprehensive evaluation of TAROT against several existing methods, showing that it outperforms them in terms of several metrics, such as correlation, gene pattern, and time order consistency.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed comparison with existing methods in terms of computational efficiency and scalability, which is crucial for evaluating the practical applicability of TAROT.
- The paper does not sufficiently discuss the limitations of the method, particularly the assumptions made and the scenarios where TAROT may not perform optimally.
- There is a need for more rigorous validation of the results, including comparisons with ground truth data and more extensive ablation studies to fully understand the contributions of different components of the algorithm.
- The paper could benefit from a more comprehensive discussion of related work, particularly in the area of optimal transport and its applications in single-cell RNA-seq analysis.
- The presentation of the paper could be improved, particularly in terms of clarity and organization, especially in sections where the methodology and results are discussed.
