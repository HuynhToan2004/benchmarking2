 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel active learning algorithm that utilizes the least disagree metric (LDM) to estimate the sample's closeness to the decision boundary. This metric is used to select samples that are most likely to change their predictions when the model's parameters are perturbed slightly. The authors propose an estimator for LDM that is asymptotically consistent and provide theoretical justifications for its use. The algorithm is tested on various datasets and shows state-of-the-art performance. The paper also discusses the computational efficiency and scalability of the proposed method, highlighting its potential to improve active learning strategies.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel least disagree metric (LDM) for active learning, which is a significant contribution to the field.
- The proposed LDM-based active learning algorithm demonstrates state-of-the-art performance across multiple datasets and deep architectures, validating its effectiveness.
- The theoretical analysis provided is robust, with rigorous proofs and a clear mathematical framework.
- The paper is well-written, making it easy to follow and understand, with a comprehensive set of experiments that validate the proposed method's effectiveness.
- The computational efficiency of the proposed method is highlighted, which is crucial for practical applications.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed discussion on the limitations of the proposed method, particularly in scenarios where it might not perform optimally or where it may be computationally expensive.
- There is a noticeable absence of experiments on large-scale datasets such as ImageNet, which could have validated the scalability of the proposed method further.
- The paper does not sufficiently discuss the computational complexity of the proposed method, especially in comparison to existing methods.
- The paper could benefit from a more detailed explanation of the experimental setup, particularly how the results were validated and the statistical significance of the results.
- The paper does not provide a detailed comparison with other active learning strategies, such as Bayesian active learning or self-paced learning, which could have provided a more comprehensive evaluation of the proposed method.
