 **Summary:**
The paper investigates the offline-to-online reinforcement learning problem, proposing a Bayesian approach to balance exploration and exploitation effectively. It introduces a novel algorithm that leverages Thompson Sampling to sample from the posterior of model parameters, aiming to avoid the pitfalls of overly optimistic or pessimistic policies. Theoretical analysis and empirical results demonstrate that the proposed method can outperform existing approaches, showing practical applicability in various settings. Despite the theoretical and empirical strengths, the paper is criticized for its clarity in explaining the algorithm's implementation and its theoretical assumptions, which may limit its broader applicability.

**Strengths:**
- The paper addresses a significant and challenging problem in the field of reinforcement learning, focusing on the offline-to-online setting which is crucial for real-world applications.
- The proposed Bayesian approach is well-motivated and theoretically justified, providing a novel perspective on balancing exploration and exploitation in offline-to-online RL.
- The paper is well-written, with clear and concise language that effectively communicates the key ideas and results.
- The empirical results are strong, demonstrating the practical effectiveness of the proposed method across various tasks.
- The theoretical analysis is solid, providing a clear explanation of the information-theoretic approach and its application to Thompson Sampling.
- The paper includes a thorough discussion of related works, positioning the research within the current literature.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper could benefit from a more detailed explanation of the algorithm's implementation, particularly the sampling process and the role of hyperparameters.
- There are some unclear theoretical assumptions, such as the relationship between the offline and online phases, which could be better clarified.
- The paper's focus on linear MDPs might limit its applicability to more complex scenarios, and it would be beneficial to discuss how the findings could be extended to other types of MDPs.
- The paper's theoretical results and their implications are not always clearly explained, which could hinder understanding of the practical implications of the research.
- The experimental setup and results could be improved by including more detailed comparisons with existing methods and by providing more comprehensive experimental results.
- There are some minor issues with the presentation and formatting of the paper, such as unclear figures and typos, which could be addressed for clarity and professionalism.

**Questions:**

- How does the proposed method handle the transition from offline to online phases, and what are the implications for the overall learning process?
