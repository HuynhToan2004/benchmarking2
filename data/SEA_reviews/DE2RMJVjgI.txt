 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel point-level supervised framework, FS-PTAL, aimed at addressing the action-background confusion in weakly-supervised temporal action localization. The framework utilizes a label extension module and a pseudo label mining strategy to enhance the accuracy of action localization. It generates pseudo segment-level labels from video sequences, which are then used to amplify the differences in scores and features between segment labels. The proposed score contrast module and feature separation module are designed to enhance the separation of action and background, thereby improving the performance of the model. The framework is evaluated on four benchmarks, demonstrating its effectiveness and outperforming existing state-of-the-art methods.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel point-level supervised framework, FS-PTAL, which addresses the action-background confusion in weakly-supervised temporal action localization.
- The framework is well-designed, with a label extension module and a pseudo label mining strategy that effectively enhance the accuracy of action localization.
- The proposed score contrast module and feature separation module are innovative and contribute to the separation of action and background, improving the performance of the model.
- The paper is well-written and easy to follow, with clear descriptions of the proposed framework and its components.
- The experimental results demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed framework, showing significant improvements over existing state-of-the-art methods.
- The paper provides a comprehensive analysis of the experimental results, including ablation studies and comparisons with existing methods, which support the effectiveness of the proposed framework.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks clarity in some sections, particularly in the descriptions of the label extension module and the pseudo label mining strategy.
- There are inconsistencies in the notation used in the paper, which could lead to confusion.
- The paper does not discuss the limitations of the proposed framework, which could help in understanding its scope and applicability.
- The experimental results are not convincingly superior to existing methods, as shown in Table 1, and the paper could benefit from a more comprehensive comparison with state-of-the-art methods.
- The paper does not provide a detailed analysis of the computational complexity or the training time of the proposed framework, which could be crucial for practical deployment.
- The paper could benefit from a more detailed discussion of the related work, particularly in the context of point-level supervised temporal action localization.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors provide a more detailed explanation of the label extension module and the pseudo label mining strategy, perhaps with additional figures or examples?
- How does the proposed framework handle the issue of action-background confusion, and what specific improvements does it offer over existing methods?
- Can the authors provide more detailed results on the training time and computational complexity of the proposed framework?
- How does the framework perform under different conditions, such as with varying numbers of point-level labels or different types of labels?
- Could the authors discuss the limitations of the proposed framework and suggest areas for future research?
