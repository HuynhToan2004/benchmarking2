 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel bi-directional cross-attention transformer architecture (BiXT) that addresses the computational efficiency challenges in processing longer sequences, such as point clouds or high-resolution images. BiXT leverages a bi-directional cross-attention mechanism to enable simultaneous refinement of input tokens and latent variables, thereby reducing computational and memory requirements while maintaining performance. The architecture is evaluated across various tasks, including image classification and semantic segmentation, showing competitive results against existing methods. However, concerns were raised about the novelty of the approach, the clarity of the paper's presentation, and the depth of the experimental validation.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, with clear and easy-to-follow descriptions of the proposed architecture and its components.
- The bi-directional cross-attention mechanism is novel and innovative, allowing for the efficient processing of longer sequences with fewer computational resources.
- The experiments conducted demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed method across various tasks, showing competitive results against existing methods.
- The paper provides a comprehensive discussion on the limitations and potential improvements of the proposed method, which is commendable for its transparency and honesty.
- The methodology is simple and intuitive, making it accessible and understandable to readers.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed discussion on the limitations and potential negative societal impacts of the proposed method.
- The novelty of the approach is questioned as it builds upon existing methods like Perceiver and Perceiver-IO, with the primary innovation being the bi-directional cross-attention mechanism.
- The paper does not sufficiently compare the proposed method with other efficient transformer-based architectures, which could have provided a clearer picture of its advantages and disadvantages.
- The experimental validation is not comprehensive enough, with some results being based on a limited number of seeds, which may not adequately reflect the reliability and reproducibility of the results.
- The paper could benefit from a more detailed explanation and justification of the bi-directional cross-attention mechanism, particularly in how it differs from existing methods and how it contributes to the efficiency and effectiveness of the proposed architecture.
- The presentation of the paper could be improved, with some figures and sections being unclear or confusing, which could hinder the reader's understanding of the content.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors provide a more detailed discussion on the limitations and potential negative societal impacts of the proposed method?
- How does the proposed method compare with other efficient transformer-based architectures in terms of computational efficiency, memory requirements, and performance?
- Could the authors clarify the differences between the proposed bi-directional cross-attention mechanism and existing methods, particularly in terms of computational complexity and efficiency?
- How does the proposed method handle the issue of the number of latents scaling quadratically with the number of layers, as mentioned in the paper?
- Could the authors provide more detailed experimental results, such as the number of seeds used and the statistical significance of the improvements claimed?
- How does the proposed method perform when scaled up to larger models, and what are the implications of this scaling in terms of computational efficiency and performance?
