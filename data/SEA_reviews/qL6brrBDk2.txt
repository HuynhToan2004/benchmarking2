 **Summary:**
The paper introduces SAFLEX, a novel data augmentation technique aimed at enhancing model performance by refining existing augmentation pipelines. It utilizes a bilevel optimization approach to learn sample weights and soft labels, aiming to improve the generalization capabilities of models. The method is designed to be compatible with various upstream augmentation techniques and has been tested across diverse datasets, showing improvements in performance. However, the paper has been critiqued for its limited novelty and lack of comprehensive experimental validation, particularly in terms of computational efficiency and the generalizability of the results.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, with clear and concise sections that effectively communicate the research and results.
- The introduction of a bilevel optimization approach to learn sample weights and soft labels is innovative and addresses the challenges of existing augmentation pipelines.
- The methodology is well-explained, with detailed derivations that support the proposed approach.
- The experimental results showcase the effectiveness of the proposed method across various datasets, demonstrating its potential to improve model performance.
- The paper addresses an important problem in machine learning, specifically the need for more effective data augmentation techniques to enhance model generalization.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a thorough discussion on the computational efficiency of the proposed method, which is crucial for practical applications.
- The novelty of the approach is limited as similar methods using bilevel optimization for data augmentation have been previously explored.
- The experimental validation is insufficient, with a limited number of datasets used and a lack of comprehensive comparisons with other state-of-the-art methods.
- The paper does not adequately address the generalizability of the results, particularly in terms of the applicability of the method to different datasets and model architectures.
- There is a lack of clarity in the presentation of some experimental results, making it difficult to draw definitive conclusions about the effectiveness of the proposed method.
- The paper does not sufficiently discuss the limitations of the proposed method, which could affect its practical implementation in real-world scenarios.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors provide more details on the computational efficiency of the proposed method, including the computational overhead introduced by the bilevel optimization approach?
- How does the proposed method perform when compared to other state-of-the-art methods in terms of computational efficiency and model performance?
- Can the authors clarify the generalizability of the results, particularly in terms of the applicability of the method to different datasets and model architectures?
- How does the proposed method handle the problem of over-augmentation, and what are the implications of this for model performance?
- Can the authors provide a more detailed comparison of the proposed method with other existing methods, particularly in terms of the effectiveness of the sample weighting and soft labeling techniques?
