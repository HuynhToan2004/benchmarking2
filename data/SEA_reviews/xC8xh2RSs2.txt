 **Summary:**
This paper presents an empirical analysis of dataset documentation on Hugging Face, focusing on the adherence of dataset creators to community-endorsed guidelines, the content of documentation, and its correlation with dataset popularity. The study reveals that popular datasets are more likely to follow community guidelines, and the content of documentation is often inadequate, particularly in the section discussing the limitations of datasets. The authors employ various statistical and qualitative methods to evaluate the quality of documentation and its impact on dataset usage. Despite the comprehensive nature of the study, concerns were raised about the limited scope of the dataset analysis and the potential for selection bias due to the focus on a single platform.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, with clear presentation of the research and findings, making it easy to follow and understand.
- The empirical analysis conducted on a large scale, involving 7,433 dataset cards, provides a comprehensive view of the current state of dataset documentation on Hugging Face.
- The study offers valuable insights into the quality of dataset documentation, highlighting the importance of comprehensive and detailed documentation for enhancing the usability and reliability of datasets.
- The paper includes a detailed analysis of the content of dataset cards, revealing trends and patterns in the documentation, which can inform future research and practices.
- The authors' approach to evaluating dataset card quality using human annotations provides a nuanced perspective that complements quantitative analysis, offering a more holistic view of dataset documentation quality.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper primarily focuses on datasets from Hugging Face, which might limit the generalizability of the findings to other platforms or types of datasets.
- The evaluation of dataset card quality is based on a small sample size of 150 cards, which could introduce bias and uncertainty into the results.
- The paper does not sufficiently address the potential biases introduced by the focus on popular datasets, which might skew the results towards datasets that are already well-documented and widely used.
- The methodology used in the study, particularly in the evaluation of dataset card quality, is not clearly described, which could affect the reproducibility and transparency of the research.
- The paper does not adequately discuss the potential limitations of the dataset cards, such as biases in dataset creation or usage, which could significantly impact the reliability and applicability of the findings.

**Questions:**
- Could you clarify the methodology used in the evaluation of dataset card quality, particularly how the five human annotators were selected and trained?
- How do the findings of this study compare with similar studies conducted on other platforms or datasets, and what are the implications of these differences for the generalizability of the findings?
- Given the focus on popular datasets, how do you address the potential for selection bias in your analysis, and what steps were taken to ensure that the results are not skewed by the popularity of the datasets studied?
- Can you provide more details on the human annotation process, including the selection criteria for the annotators and the inter-rater reliability calculations?
- How do you address the potential for biases in the dataset cards, particularly in terms of the representation of underrepresented groups or the inclusion of diverse perspectives?
