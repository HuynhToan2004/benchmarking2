 **Summary:**
The paper examines the capabilities of pre-trained code models (PCMs) in understanding code syntax and data flow, focusing on the encoding of relations between tokens and the representation of hidden states. It critiques previous assumptions about how PCMs encode syntactic and semantic structures in code, showing that these models do not fully capture all relations, particularly those between different types of tokens. The study employs various probing techniques and attention analysis to demonstrate these limitations, suggesting that PCMs may not generalize well beyond their training datasets. Despite its thorough examination, the paper's conclusions are seen as somewhat expected and do not significantly advance the understanding of PCMs.

**Strengths:**
- The paper provides a comprehensive analysis of the attention maps and hidden representations of pre-trained code models (PCMs), highlighting their limitations in encoding syntactic-identifier relations.
- It introduces novel probing techniques such as DirectProbe and t-SNE for analyzing the capabilities of PCMs, which are not commonly used in previous studies.
- The study is well-organized and clearly presents the research questions and methodology, making it easy to follow.
- The paper includes a detailed analysis of the performance of different models across various datasets and tasks, providing insights into the limitations of current PCMs.
- The authors critically examine the assumptions made in previous works and propose new methods to address the identified limitations.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper's conclusions are seen as expected and not surprising, as the limitations of PCMs have been previously discussed in the literature.
- The novelty of the paper is questioned as the probing techniques used are not significantly different from those in existing works.
- The paper lacks a clear motivation for the probing tasks and does not provide sufficient evidence to support the claims made about the encoding of data flow and syntactic-syntactic relations.
- There are issues with the clarity and explanation of certain figures and tables, making it difficult for readers to understand the results and conclusions.
- The paper does not sufficiently address the generalization capabilities of PCMs, which is a critical aspect of their practical application.

**Questions:**
- Could you clarify the differences between the attention analysis and probing techniques used in this study compared to those in previous works?
- How do the conclusions drawn from this study contribute to the understanding of PCMs beyond what has already been established in previous research?
- Can you provide more detailed explanations and examples to support the claims made about the encoding of data flow and syntactic-syntactic relations in PCMs?
- How do the findings of this study relate to the generalization capabilities of PCMs, and what implications do these findings have for the practical application of PCMs in real-world scenarios?
