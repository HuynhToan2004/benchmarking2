 **Summary:**
The paper investigates the adversarial robustness of vision-language models (VLMs) by proposing a non-targeted white-box attack method that maximizes information entropy (MIE) to induce misleading image descriptions. The method is evaluated on the ImageNet dataset, demonstrating a high success rate in adversarial attacks. The authors argue that this method can serve as a benchmark for evaluating the robustness of diverse VLMs. However, the paper is critiqued for its lack of novelty, limited scope of evaluation, and insufficient comparison with existing methods. The method's effectiveness and the clarity of its presentation are also questioned.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-organized, making it easy to follow.
- The proposed method is simple and effective, with a high attack success rate on large VLMs.
- The experiments are well-designed, and the results are promising.
- The paper addresses a relevant and interesting topic, focusing on the adversarial robustness of vision-language models.
- The proposed method is simple and easy to understand, which is beneficial for replication by other researchers.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks novelty as the method of maximizing information entropy is not new.
- The evaluation is limited, focusing only on the ImageNet dataset, which may not generalize to other datasets.
- The comparison with existing methods is insufficient, particularly with recent works that use similar methodologies.
- The paper's contribution is not clearly articulated, and the method's effectiveness is not convincingly demonstrated.
- The paper could benefit from a more thorough evaluation on larger datasets and a broader range of VLMs.
- The presentation of the paper could be improved, with some sections appearing redundant or not contributing significantly to the understanding of the method.
- There are several typographical errors and unclear figures that need correction.

**Questions:**
- How does the proposed method compare with the recent work by Carlini et al. (2023) that also uses a similar approach to maximize information entropy?
- Can the proposed method be applied to other tasks beyond image captioning, such as visual question answering or visual reasoning?
- How does the method perform on other datasets beyond ImageNet, and what are the implications for its generalizability?
- Can the authors provide a more detailed comparison with existing methods, including a discussion on how their method differs and performs better?
- How are the hyperparameters, such as lambda and epsilon, selected, and what is the impact of these choices on the method's performance?
- Could the authors clarify the method's application to other architectures beyond the universal Transformer structure mentioned in the paper?
