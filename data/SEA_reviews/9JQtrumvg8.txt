 **Summary:**
The paper introduces WebAgent, a novel LLM-driven agent designed for web automation, integrating two modular LLMs: HTML-T5 for planning and HTML summarization, and Flan-U-PaLM for code generation. WebAgent processes real websites using natural language instructions, decomposing them into sub-instructions and summarizing long HTML documents into task-relevant snippets. The system is evaluated on various datasets, showing improved performance over existing methods. However, concerns about the novelty of the approach, the clarity of methodology, and the generalization of the results across different websites and datasets are raised.

**Strengths:**
- The paper addresses the significant challenge of real-world web automation by integrating two modular LLMs, HTML-T5 for planning and summarization, and Flan-U-PaLM for code generation, which is a novel approach.
- The proposed method demonstrates improved success rates on real websites, showcasing the effectiveness of the approach.
- The evaluation is comprehensive, covering both real-world and simulated environments, and includes a variety of datasets, providing a robust assessment of the system's capabilities.
- The paper is well-written, making it easy to follow, and includes a detailed discussion of the limitations and future directions, which adds to the paper's credibility.

**Weaknesses:**
- The novelty of the approach is questioned as the integration of LLMs for web automation is not entirely novel, and the paper does not sufficiently differentiate its methodology from existing works.
- The methodology section is unclear, particularly the role and integration of the two LLMs (HTML-T5 and Flan-U-PaLM), which could benefit from more detailed explanations and examples.
- The evaluation is limited to a few websites, raising concerns about the generalizability of the results across different websites and the robustness of the system.
- The paper lacks a thorough discussion on the limitations of the proposed system, particularly in terms of scalability and the potential negative societal impacts.
- There are issues with the presentation of results and the clarity of some figures and tables, which could be improved for better comprehension.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors clarify the role and integration of the two LLMs (HTML-T5 and Flan-U-PaLM) in the methodology, possibly with examples?
- How does the system handle the integration of HTML-T5 and Flan-U-PaLM, particularly in terms of data flow and model interactions?
- Could the authors provide more details on the pre-training dataset for HTML-T5 and the training data for Flan-U-PaLM?
- How does the system ensure the generalizability of the results across different websites, especially those not included in the evaluation?
- Can the authors elaborate on the scalability of the proposed system and discuss potential negative societal impacts?
- Could the authors consider including more detailed examples and figures to illustrate the system's capabilities and enhance the clarity of the paper?
