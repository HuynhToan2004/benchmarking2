 **Summary:**
The paper introduces MOTIF, a method that leverages a Large Language Model (LLM) to provide intrinsic rewards for training agents in complex, open-ended environments like NetHack. This approach uses LLM's preferences over event captions to construct an intrinsic reward, which is then used to train agents via reinforcement learning. The method's effectiveness is demonstrated through extensive experiments, showing that MOTIF can outperform direct training on the score and achieve better results in sparse reward tasks. The paper also discusses the potential of MOTIF to scale with the size of the LLM and the amount of information in the prompt, and explores the alignment of the LLM's knowledge with human intentions.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, with clear and concise sections, and the main ideas are well-motivated.
- The proposed method is innovative and has the potential to be applied in various domains, showing significant performance improvements in the NetHack environment.
- The experiments are extensive, including a variety of tasks, and the paper provides a detailed analysis of the behavior and alignment of the trained agent with human intuition.
- The use of LLM to provide intrinsic rewards for training agents is a novel approach, and the paper demonstrates that MOTIF can outperform direct training on the score and achieve better results in sparse reward tasks.
- The paper discusses the scalability of MOTIF with the size of the LLM and the amount of information in the prompt, and explores the potential of MOTIF to steer agent behaviors by prompt modifications.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed discussion on the limitations of the proposed method, such as its applicability in different environments and the potential for overfitting to specific datasets.
- The experiments are primarily focused on the NetHack environment, and it would be beneficial to test the method in other environments to validate its generalizability.
- There is a need for more clarity in the paper, particularly in the introduction and the explanation of the method, which could be improved with the addition of figures and more detailed descriptions.
- The paper does not sufficiently discuss the scalability of the method with the size of the LLM and the amount of information in the prompt, and the effects of prompt rewording on the performance of the trained agent.
- The paper could benefit from a more detailed discussion on the potential negative societal impacts of the proposed method, such as misuse or bias in the LLM's knowledge.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors provide a more detailed discussion on the limitations of the proposed method and how these might affect its applicability in different environments?
- How does the MOTIF method compare to other methods that use LLMs to generate intrinsic rewards, and what are the advantages of using MOTIF over these methods?
- Could the authors clarify the methodology behind the MOTIF method, particularly how the LLM's knowledge is integrated into the reward function and how this affects the behavior of the trained agent?
- In the experiments, how does the MOTIF method perform in other environments beyond NetHack, and what are the implications of these results for the generalizability of the method?
- How does the MOTIF method handle the potential for overfitting to specific datasets, and what measures can be taken to mitigate this risk?
- Could the authors provide a more detailed discussion on the potential negative societal impacts of the proposed method, such as misuse or bias in the LLM's knowledge, and how these risks might be mitigated?
