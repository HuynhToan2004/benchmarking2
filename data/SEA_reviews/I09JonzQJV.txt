 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel concept of fairness called Lookahead Counterfactual Fairness (LCF), which extends the traditional Counterfactual Fairness (CF) by considering the impact of machine learning decisions on individuals' future status. This extension is crucial as it acknowledges that individuals may change their behaviors and features in response to ML predictions, which could lead to disparities in future outcomes. The paper presents a theoretical framework and an algorithm to address this issue, supported by experiments on both synthetic and real-world datasets. The approach aims to ensure fairness in scenarios where individuals can respond to ML models by adjusting their features, thus potentially mitigating disparities in future outcomes.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, with clear problem formulation and theoretical results.
- The authors have identified the limitations of existing fairness notions and proposed a new approach to address these limitations.
- The proposed approach is novel and has the potential to be more effective than previous methods in ensuring fairness in dynamic settings.
- The paper provides both theoretical and empirical evidence to support the proposed method, which is a strength in demonstrating the robustness and applicability of the approach.
- The experiments are well-designed and demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed approach, showing that it can improve fairness in both synthetic and real-world datasets.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper's assumptions about individuals' responses to ML models are strong and may not hold in real-world scenarios where individuals' behaviors are more complex and varied.
- The experimental setup is limited, with only two baselines compared, which may not adequately demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed method.
- The paper could benefit from a more detailed discussion on the limitations of the proposed approach and how these limitations might affect its practical application.
- The paper lacks a clear explanation of how the proposed approach ensures fairness in real-world applications, particularly in scenarios where individuals' responses to ML models are not directly observable or controllable.
- There is a lack of clarity in some sections of the paper, particularly in the definitions and assumptions used, which could make the paper more difficult for readers to understand.

**Questions:**
- How does the proposed approach ensure fairness in real-world applications where individuals' responses to ML models are not directly observable or controllable?
- Can the authors provide more details on the assumptions made about individuals' responses to ML models and how these assumptions align with real-world behavior?
- How does the proposed approach compare to other fairness notions that also consider the dynamic impact of ML decisions on individuals' future status?
- Could the authors clarify the definitions and assumptions used in the paper, particularly in the context of causal models and the impact of ML decisions on individuals' behaviors?
- How does the proposed approach handle scenarios where individuals' responses to ML models are not aligned with the model's predictions, and what are the implications of this misalignment for the fairness of the model's outcomes?
