 **Summary:**
The paper investigates the impact of different p-norms on the preservation of information in high-dimensional data, focusing on the use of L2-normalization and geodesic distance for data visualization. The authors propose a new method, HS-SNE, which utilizes these techniques to enhance the visualization of high-dimensional data, particularly in the context of single-cell RNA sequencing. The paper argues that L2-normalization and geodesic distance are more effective than Euclidean distance in preserving information and reducing computational complexity. However, the paper has been critiqued for its limited novelty, lack of theoretical justification, and insufficient experimental validation.

**Strengths:**
- The paper addresses a significant problem in the field of high-dimensional data visualization, particularly relevant for single-cell RNA sequencing data, which is often high-dimensional and complex.
- The authors provide a clear and concise explanation of the proposed method, which is supported by theoretical arguments and empirical evidence.
- The paper is well-organized, making it easy to follow and understand, with clear illustrations that aid in the comprehension of the proposed method.
- The proposed method, HS-SNE, shows promising results in preserving information in high-dimensional data, which is crucial for downstream tasks such as clustering.
- The paper is well-written and provides a detailed explanation of the methodology, including the use of p-norms and geodesic distance, which are not commonly explored in data visualization.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a thorough literature review, particularly in the area of data normalization and high-dimensional data visualization, which could have provided a richer context and justification for the proposed method.
- The paper's theoretical underpinnings are not sufficiently strong, with the arguments relying heavily on intuitive explanations rather than rigorous mathematical proofs.
- The experimental validation is limited and does not convincingly demonstrate the superiority of the proposed method over existing methods such as t-SNE or UMAP.
- The paper does not adequately address the computational complexity of the proposed method, particularly in high-dimensional data, which could limit its practical application.
- The paper's claims regarding the effectiveness of L2-normalization and geodesic distance are not convincingly supported by the experimental results, which do not clearly demonstrate the superiority of the proposed method.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors provide a more detailed explanation of how the proposed method, HS-SNE, differs from existing methods such as t-SNE or UMAP, particularly in terms of its theoretical foundations and practical applications?
- How does the proposed method handle the computational complexity associated with high-dimensional data, and what strategies are employed to mitigate this issue?
- Can the authors provide more robust experimental validation to support the claims made about the effectiveness of L2-normalization and geodesic distance in preserving information in high-dimensional data?
- How does the proposed method perform in terms of preserving local and global structures in high-dimensional data, and what are the implications of this for downstream tasks such as clustering?
- Given the limitations in the theoretical justification and experimental validation, how do the authors plan to address these concerns in future work, and what steps are being taken to enhance the novelty and impact of the proposed method?
