 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel non-autoregressive approach for generating long image captions using a semantic memory guided diffusion network. This approach integrates a semantic concept predictor (SCP) to enhance the representation of semantic concepts, a semantic conditional memory (SCM) to align images and texts, and a diffusion decoder (DD) to generate long text descriptions. The methodology is evaluated on four datasets, demonstrating its effectiveness in generating long and coherent captions. However, the paper suffers from clarity issues in its presentation and lacks detailed ablation studies and comparisons with more recent and relevant works.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel approach using a semantic memory guided diffusion network, which effectively addresses the limitations of previous works in image-to-text generation.
- The proposed method is well-motivated and the experiments are comprehensive, demonstrating the effectiveness of the approach through detailed ablation studies.
- The paper is well-written, making it easy to follow, and the results are impressive, showing significant improvements over previous methods.
- The introduction of a semantic concept predictor (SCP) and a semantic conditional memory (SCM) enhances the representation of semantic concepts and aligns images and texts, providing a more precise control over the generation process.
- The paper includes a new dataset specifically designed for image-to-long text generation, which is a significant contribution to the field.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks clarity in some sections, particularly in the descriptions of the semantic concept predictor and semantic conditional memory, which could benefit from more detailed explanations.
- There is a lack of comparisons with more recent and relevant works, such as the SCD-Net and other non-autoregressive methods, which could have provided a more robust validation of the proposed method.
- The paper does not include a user study, which is crucial for evaluating the quality and coherence of the generated captions from a human perspective.
- The evaluation metrics used are somewhat limited, and it would be beneficial to include more metrics such as CIDEr and MUSE to provide a more comprehensive assessment of the generated captions.
- The paper could benefit from more detailed descriptions of the training and implementation details, particularly the use of specific hyperparameters and the training data sources.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors provide more detailed explanations of the semantic concept predictor and semantic conditional memory, including how they are trained and how they interact with each other?
- How does the proposed method compare with other non-autoregressive methods, particularly in terms of generating long and coherent captions?
- Could the authors clarify the specific role and impact of the semantic concepts in the generated captions, and how they compare to the gold standard semantic concepts?
- How does the proposed method handle the ambiguities in conveying all essential semantics, especially in scenarios where the image descriptions are incomplete or ambiguous?
- Could the authors provide more details on the training data sources and the specific hyperparameters used in the training process?
- How does the proposed method handle the potential issue of overfitting, especially in scenarios where the semantic matrix size is too large?
