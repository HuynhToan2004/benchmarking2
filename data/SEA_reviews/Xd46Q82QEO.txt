 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel pointwise representation similarity measure, Pointwise Normalized Kernel Alignment (PNKA), which extends the Centered Kernel Alignment (CKA) to analyze the similarity of individual data points in learned representations. The authors demonstrate that not all points in a dataset are represented similarly, highlighting the importance of examining representations at the granularity of individual data points. They show that adversarially trained models represent a wider variety of out-of-distribution inputs similarly, indicating more stable representations. The PNKA measure is applied to understand the effects of interventions on model representations, such as fairness constraints, and is used to analyze the representations of adversarially trained models. The paper also investigates the impact of perturbations on representation similarity and provides insights into the behavior of models under different conditions.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel pointwise representation similarity measure, PNKA, which is a significant extension of the CKA measure.
- The paper is well-written, clear, and easy to follow, with a clear motivation and structure.
- The findings are significant and have the potential to impact the field of representation similarity, particularly in the analysis of adversarially trained models and their representation of out-of-distribution inputs.
- The paper provides empirical evidence that PNKA is a valid measure and shows its applicability in understanding model interventions and the effects of perturbations.
- The paper is original and provides a fresh perspective on the importance of analyzing representations at the granularity of individual data points.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed explanation of the PNKA measure, particularly how it is used and the intuition behind its calculation.
- There is a lack of comparison with other measures, which could have provided a more comprehensive evaluation of PNKA.
- The paper's claims about the impact of perturbations on representation similarity are not adequately supported with evidence, and the results presented are not convincing.
- The paper does not sufficiently discuss the limitations of its approach, such as the potential for different reference points affecting the results and the generalizability of the findings across different datasets and model architectures.
- The paper does not sufficiently address the potential negative societal impacts of the research, particularly in the context of fairness and representation in machine learning models.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors clarify how the PNKA measure is calculated and what it represents in terms of similarity between representations?
- How does the choice of reference points affect the results, and what would be the implications if different reference points were used?
- Could the authors provide more detailed evidence to support the claims about the impact of perturbations on representation similarity?
- How does the PNKA measure perform in different scenarios, such as when comparing representations from models with different architectures or when analyzing the effects of different interventions on model representations?
- Could the authors discuss the potential limitations of their approach and how these might affect the generalizability of their findings?
