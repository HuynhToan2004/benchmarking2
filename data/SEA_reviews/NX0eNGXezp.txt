 **Summary:**
The paper introduces the Semi-HyperGraph Benchmark (SHGB), a novel dataset that integrates both hypergraphs and simple edges, aiming to enhance the flexibility of machine learning systems in handling complex node relations. The SHGB dataset is designed to facilitate the training and evaluation of Graph Neural Networks (GNNs) on complex graphs, with 23 real-world hypergraph datasets and simple edges included. The paper also presents an evaluation framework and provides a codebase to support the training and evaluation of GNNs on SHGB. The empirical study of existing GNNs on SHGB reveals various research opportunities and gaps, including the evaluation of hypergraph GNNs versus simple graph GNNs, the impact of different sampling strategies on hypergraph learning methods, and the exploration of ways to integrate simple edge and hyperedge information.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel dataset, the Semi-HyperGraph Benchmark (SHGB), which combines hypergraphs and simple edges, enhancing the flexibility of machine learning systems in handling complex node relations.
- The SHGB dataset is designed to facilitate the training and evaluation of Graph Neural Networks (GNNs) on complex graphs, providing a comprehensive collection of 23 real-world hypergraph datasets and simple edges across various domains.
- The paper provides an extensible evaluation framework and a supporting codebase to facilitate the training and evaluation of GNNs on SHGB, which is crucial for advancing research in this area.
- The empirical study of existing GNNs on SHGB reveals various research opportunities and gaps, which can stimulate further research in this field.
- The paper is well-written and easy to follow, making it accessible to a broad audience.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed discussion on the advantages and disadvantages of using hypergraphs over simple graphs, which could provide a more robust argument for the necessity of the proposed dataset.
- There is a lack of clarity on the specific research questions or hypotheses that the paper aims to address, which could limit the understanding of the paper's contributions.
- The dataset construction process is not thoroughly explained, particularly the rationale behind the construction of hyperedges and their impact on the performance of GNNs.
- The paper does not adequately address the scalability of the proposed methods, especially in large-scale applications, which could limit the practical applicability of the findings.
- The paper could benefit from a more comprehensive literature review, especially in the area of hypergraph learning, to position the research within the existing body of knowledge.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors provide a more detailed discussion on the advantages and disadvantages of using hypergraphs over simple graphs, and how the proposed dataset addresses these issues?
- How do the authors plan to address the scalability concerns raised in the paper, especially in large-scale applications?
- Could the authors elaborate on the construction process of the hyperedges and how they impact the performance of GNNs?
- How do the authors justify the choice of specific hypergraph GNNs and simple graph GNNs for comparison in the empirical study?
- In light of the limited novelty of the proposed dataset and the lack of significant technical innovation, how do the authors plan to differentiate their work from existing datasets and methods?
