 **Summary:**
The paper introduces the Equivariant Graph Neural Operator (EGNO), a novel method for modeling 3D dynamics by treating dynamics as a function over time, rather than just predicting the next state. EGNO leverages equivariant temporal convolutions in the Fourier space to capture temporal correlations while maintaining SE(3) equivariance. The method is evaluated on various datasets, including N-body systems, motion capture, and molecular dynamics, demonstrating superior performance over existing methods. EGNO's approach allows for efficient parallel decoding of future states and maintains the 3D spatial equivariance, making it suitable for a wide range of physical dynamics scenarios.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel method, EGNO, for modeling 3D dynamics as a function over time, which directly models the entire trajectory dynamics instead of just the next time-step prediction.
- EGNO is a general framework that can be easily integrated with any specially designed EGNN layers, allowing for efficient parallel decoding of future states and maintaining 3D spatial equivariance.
- The paper is well-written, with clear explanations of the method and its implementation, and includes comprehensive experiments that demonstrate the superior performance of EGNO over existing methods.
- The proposed method is original and innovative, offering a new perspective on modeling dynamics, and the experimental results are convincing, showing the effectiveness of the proposed method.
- The paper is well-organized, with clear figures and tables that aid in understanding the method and its results.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper could benefit from more detailed discussions on the limitations of the proposed method, particularly in scenarios where it may not perform as well.
- The integration of EGNO with existing models like EGNN is not thoroughly explained, and the paper could benefit from a more detailed discussion on how EGNO can be integrated with other models.
- The paper lacks a detailed discussion on the computational efficiency of EGNO compared to other methods, which could be a significant concern for practical applications.
- The paper does not include a discussion on the potential societal impacts of the proposed method, which could be a concern for some readers.
- There are some minor issues with the writing and formatting of the paper, such as missing references and formatting errors in tables.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors provide more details on how EGNO can be integrated with other models, such as EGHN, to enhance its capabilities?
- How does the computational efficiency of EGNO compare to other methods, particularly in terms of training and inference times?
- In the experiments, why was EGNO-S not included in the comparisons for the N-body system simulation?
- Could the authors clarify the discrepancy in the performance of EGNO-U and EGNO-L in different scenarios, as shown in Table 3?
- How does the proposed method handle the challenges of maintaining equivariance while integrating it with other models?
- Could the authors provide more details on the computational efficiency and scalability of EGNO, particularly in large-scale applications?
