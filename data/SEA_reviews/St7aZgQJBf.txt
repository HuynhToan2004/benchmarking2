 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a curriculum learning approach to enhance the robustness of deep metric learning (DML) models against adversarial ranking attacks. It begins by training models to be robust against adversarial perturbations using adversarial pre-training on ImageNet, followed by metric learning for precise retrieval. The authors hypothesize that robust features acquired through adversarial training inherently exhibit resistance to ranking attacks, thereby fortifying defenses against adversarial ranking attacks. The approach is tested across three benchmark datasets and shows promising results, though it is noted that the methodology and contributions are somewhat limited, with the main novelty being the curriculum learning strategy.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written and easy to follow, with clear explanations of the methodology and results.
- The hypothesis that robust models trained with adversarial training are resistant to ranking attacks is both interesting and novel, contributing to the field of adversarial ranking defenses.
- The experimental results demonstrate that the proposed method significantly improves the recall of benign examples and enhances defenses against adversarial ranking attacks, achieving state-of-the-art results on three benchmark datasets.
- The use of adversarial pre-training to enhance the robustness of models is a significant contribution, as it is a common technique in adversarial training.
- The paper provides a comprehensive evaluation of the robustness of the proposed method against various adversarial ranking attacks, demonstrating its effectiveness in improving the robustness of deep metric learning models.

**Weaknesses:**
- The main contributions of the paper are limited, primarily focusing on the curriculum learning strategy and the use of adversarial pre-training, which are not entirely novel.
- The paper lacks a thorough discussion on the limitations of the proposed method and does not provide a detailed comparison with other state-of-the-art methods in terms of computational cost, training time, and memory requirements.
- The paper does not sufficiently explore the impact of different adversarial training methods on the robustness of the models, which could provide a more comprehensive understanding of the robustness enhancement.
- The evaluation of the proposed method is limited to only three datasets, which may not sufficiently demonstrate the generalizability of the method across different domains.
- The paper does not adequately address the potential negative societal impacts of the proposed method, such as its use in malicious applications like promoting harmful content.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors clarify the specifics of the curriculum learning strategy, particularly how it is applied and what makes it effective in this context?
- How does the proposed method compare to other state-of-the-art methods in terms of computational cost, training time, and memory requirements?
- What are the potential negative societal impacts of the proposed method, and how can these be mitigated?
- Can the authors provide more details on the adversarial training methods used and how they affect the robustness of the models?
- Is there a possibility of extending the evaluation to include more datasets to demonstrate the generalizability of the method?
