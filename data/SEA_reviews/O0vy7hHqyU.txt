 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel methodology, AFMO, for detecting fake news using both textual and visual features. It employs a simulated annealing algorithm to optimize feature selection and fusion, aiming to enhance the robustness of the detection system. The approach involves a multimodal feature extraction using BERT and VGG, followed by a KNN-based outlier detection and simulated annealing for feature selection. The methodology is tested on three datasets and shows promising results, although the paper suffers from issues such as poor writing, insufficient literature review, and lack of novelty. The reviewers highlighted the need for more detailed experimental setups, comparisons with state-of-the-art methods, and clearer presentation of the methodology.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel methodology, AFMO, for detecting fake news using both textual and visual features, which is a significant advancement in the field.
- The application of the simulated annealing algorithm for feature selection is innovative and contributes to the effectiveness of the methodology.
- The paper is well-organized and easy to follow, making it accessible to a broad audience.
- The authors have provided a comprehensive set of experiments and comparisons with state-of-the-art methods, which supports the validity of their approach.
- The paper is technically sound, with a clear methodology and a good presentation of the results.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed discussion on how the proposed methodology differs from existing methods, particularly in terms of feature selection and fusion.
- The literature review is insufficient, with many relevant works not cited or discussed, which limits the contextual understanding of the research.
- The paper suffers from poor writing and presentation, with many grammatical errors and unclear descriptions of the methodology.
- The experimental setup and results are not sufficiently detailed, making it difficult to assess the effectiveness and reliability of the proposed methodology.
- There is a lack of novelty in the approach, as the use of simulated annealing for feature selection is not a new concept.
- The paper does not adequately address the potential biases in the datasets used, which could affect the generalizability and applicability of the findings.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors provide more details on how the proposed methodology differs from existing methods, particularly in terms of feature selection and fusion?
- How does the proposed methodology handle the potential biases in the datasets used, and what steps have been taken to mitigate these biases?
- Could the authors clarify the experimental setup, including the specifics of the datasets used, the preprocessing techniques applied, and the evaluation metrics considered?
- How does the proposed methodology compare to other state-of-the-art methods, particularly in terms of computational efficiency and scalability?
- What are the potential applications and limitations of the proposed methodology, and how does it perform in real-world scenarios?
- Can the authors provide more details on the hyperparameter settings used in the simulations, and how these settings affect the performance and reliability of the methodology?
