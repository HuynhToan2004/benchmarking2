 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel method for text-guided editing of NeRF models, termed ED-NeRF. It leverages a latent space of a pre-trained stable diffusion model to optimize NeRF parameters, aiming to enhance the model's editing capabilities. Key components include a refinement layer to address issues in latent feature reconstruction and a masked delta denoising score (MDDS) for targeted editing. The method is evaluated against several baselines, showing improvements in both editing quality and computational efficiency. However, concerns are raised about the novelty of the approach, the lack of comprehensive ablation studies, and the method's generalizability across different datasets and 3D scenes.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel approach to 3D scene editing, specifically focusing on text-guided editing of NeRF models, which is an emerging and relevant area of research.
- The proposed method shows improvements over existing baselines in terms of both editing quality and computational efficiency, which is crucial for practical applications.
- The paper is well-written, making the methodology clear and accessible, which aids in understanding the complex technical aspects of the research.
- The use of a latent space of a pre-trained stable diffusion model for optimizing NeRF parameters is innovative and addresses the challenges of traditional NeRF editing methods.
- The inclusion of a refinement layer and masked delta denoising score (MDDS) adds a layer of complexity and effectiveness to the editing process, enhancing the method's ability to handle specific target regions.
- The paper provides extensive experimental results and comparisons, demonstrating the method's effectiveness and outperforming several existing methods.

**Weaknesses:**
- The novelty of the approach is questioned as it appears to be a combination of existing techniques without significant new insights or methodological advancements.
- The paper lacks comprehensive ablation studies, particularly concerning the effectiveness of the refinement layer and the masked reconstruction regularization.
- There is a concern about the generalizability of the method, as it was only tested on a limited set of datasets and 3D scenes, raising questions about its applicability to a broader range of scenarios.
- The paper does not provide a detailed comparison with some state-of-the-art methods, which could have strengthened the argument for the method's effectiveness.
- The method's dependency on the quality of the pre-trained diffusion model and the stability of the training process is not adequately addressed, which could affect the reliability and reproducibility of the results.
- The paper lacks a thorough analysis of the limitations and potential negative societal impacts of the proposed method, which is crucial for ethical considerations in AI research.

**Questions:**
- Could you provide more details on the pre-training process of the diffusion model and the specifics of the training data used?
- How does the method perform when applied to different datasets or 3D scenes beyond those tested?
- What are the specific challenges or limitations of the method that prevent it from being applied to a broader range of scenarios?
- Could you clarify the role and effectiveness of the refinement layer and masked reconstruction regularization in the editing process?
- How does the method handle the stability and consistency of the training process, especially when dealing with complex or noisy data?
- Could you elaborate on the potential negative societal impacts of the proposed method and how these are mitigated or addressed?
