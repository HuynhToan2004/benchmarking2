 **Summary:**
The paper introduces FARZI, a data distillation method tailored for autoregressive machine learning tasks, which summarizes large datasets into smaller, synthetic sequences. FARZI employs a latent space to distill data, enhancing the sample efficiency and reducing the computational complexity of model training. The methodology involves a meta-matching framework that optimizes the Adam optimizer using Hessian-Vector products and factorizes high-dimensional discrete event-space into a latent-space. The experiments conducted on various datasets demonstrate that FARZI can achieve similar or superior performance to full-data training with significantly less data, thereby reducing the computational burden and potentially enhancing the scalability of auto-regressive models.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel approach to data distillation, specifically tailored for autoregressive machine learning tasks, which is a significant advancement in the field.
- The methodology is well-articulated, with a clear and concise writing style that makes the paper accessible and easy to understand.
- The experiments are comprehensive, demonstrating the effectiveness of the proposed method across various datasets and tasks, showing that it can achieve similar or better performance than full-data training with significantly less data.
