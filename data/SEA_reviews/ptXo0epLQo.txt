 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel Variational Autoencoder (VAE) model, named Alpha-TCVAE, which aims to optimize the joint total correlation (TC) between the latent representation and input data. This model is designed to maximize disentanglement and latent variable informativeness by leveraging a convex combination of the variational information bottleneck (VIB) and conditional entropy bottleneck (CEB) terms. The authors demonstrate that Alpha-TCVAE outperforms existing models in terms of disentanglement and latent variable informativeness, as evidenced by various experiments across different datasets and tasks. The model is evaluated on both synthetic and real-world datasets, showing improvements in visual fidelity and generative diversity. Despite some concerns regarding the clarity of the derivation and the necessity of certain assumptions, the paper provides a significant theoretical contribution to the field of disentangled representation learning.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, with a clear presentation of the proposed method and its theoretical underpinnings.
- The motivation behind the research is well-articulated, and the authors provide a clear rationale for their approach.
- The paper introduces a novel method for optimizing the joint total correlation (TC) between the latent representation and input data, which is both innovative and theoretically grounded.
- Extensive experiments are conducted on both synthetic and real-world datasets, demonstrating the effectiveness of the proposed method in terms of disentanglement and latent variable informativeness.
- The paper provides a comprehensive evaluation of the proposed method, including visualizations of latent traversals and a comparison with various baselines.
- The authors discuss the limitations of their method and suggest future directions for research, which enhances the credibility of the paper.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper could benefit from a more detailed explanation of the assumptions made in the derivation of the TC bound, particularly in relation to the independence of the latent variables and the Gaussian assumption.
- There is a lack of clarity in the presentation of the TC bound and its derivation, which could confuse readers.
- The paper does not adequately address the issue of scalability in high-dimensional spaces, which is a significant challenge in disentangled representation learning.
- The paper does not sufficiently compare the proposed method with other state-of-the-art methods, such as those using normalizing flows or other advanced optimization techniques.
- The paper lacks a detailed discussion on the limitations of the method, particularly in terms of its practical applicability and the challenges it may pose in real-world scenarios.
- There are several typographical errors and inconsistencies in the paper, which could detract from its professionalism and readability.

**Questions:**
- Could you clarify the assumptions made in the derivation of the TC bound, particularly regarding the independence of the latent variables and the Gaussian assumption?
- How does the proposed method compare with other state-of-the-art methods in terms of scalability and performance in high-dimensional spaces?
- Can you provide more details on the practical implementation of the method, including the choice of hyperparameters and the computational resources required?
- How does the proposed method address the challenges of scalability and efficiency in disentangled representation learning?
- Could you elaborate on the role of the CEB term in encouraging the discovery of new generative factors, and how this relates to the overall effectiveness of the method?
- How do the authors address the issue of overfitting, which is a known problem in VAEs?
- Can you provide more details on the computational efficiency of the method, particularly in terms of training and inference times?
