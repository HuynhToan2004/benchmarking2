 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel framework, 3DFuse, aimed at enhancing the consistency and coherence of generated 3D scenes in text-to-3D generation tasks. It addresses the "Janus problem" by incorporating 3D awareness into pretrained 2D diffusion models, using a consistency injection module that constructs a 3D point cloud from text prompts and utilizes projected depth maps for conditioning. This approach is designed to improve the robustness and 3D consistency of score distillation-based methods. The paper also presents a new technique called semantic coding to reduce semantic ambiguity in text prompts, which is shown to improve results. Experiments demonstrate that 3DFuse outperforms previous baselines in terms of geometric robustness and fidelity. However, concerns are raised about the novelty of the approach and the clarity of the writing, with some reviewers pointing out the similarity to existing methods like Zero-123.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, easy to follow, and provides a clear explanation of the proposed methodology.
- The proposed method, 3DFuse, effectively addresses the "Janus problem" in text-to-3D generation by incorporating 3D awareness into pretrained 2D diffusion models, which enhances the robustness and 3D consistency of score distillation-based methods.
- The introduction of a consistency injection module that constructs a 3D point cloud from text prompts and utilizes projected depth maps as conditions for the diffusion model is innovative and technically sound.
- The paper includes a new technique called semantic coding, which reduces semantic ambiguity of the text prompt, leading to improved results.
- The proposed method is easy to implement and can be easily adapted to various text-to-3D baselines, making it accessible and practical for real-world applications.
- The paper provides a comprehensive evaluation, including both qualitative and quantitative results, demonstrating the effectiveness of the proposed method.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper does not sufficiently differentiate its approach from existing methods like Zero-123, raising concerns about the novelty of the proposed method.
- The paper's writing is sometimes confusing, with terms and concepts introduced without adequate explanation, making it difficult for readers to fully understand the methodology and its contributions.
- There is a lack of detailed information on the training process for the proposed sparse depth injector, which could be crucial for understanding the method's effectiveness and robustness.
- The paper does not adequately address the issue of camera pose, which is a significant limitation in the text-to-3D generation task.
- The paper's experimental setup and results lack clarity, with insufficient details provided on the training datasets and the training process, which could affect the reproducibility and comparability of the results.
- The paper does not sufficiently discuss the limitations of the proposed method, which could provide valuable insights for future research and improvements.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors provide more details on the training process for the proposed sparse depth injector, including the datasets used, training strategies, and performance metrics?
- How does the proposed method handle different camera poses in the text-to-3D generation task, and what are the implications of this limitation on the method's effectiveness?
- Could the authors clarify the discrepancies and potential errors in the figures and equations presented in the paper?
- How does the proposed method compare to Zero-123 in terms of novelty and technical contribution, especially considering the similarities in the use of sparse depth maps for conditioning diffusion models?
- Could the authors provide more details on the training data and the experimental setup to enhance the reproducibility and comparability of the results?
