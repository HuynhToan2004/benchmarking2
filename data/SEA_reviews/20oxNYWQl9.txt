 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel data selection algorithm aimed at reducing the computational and time costs associated with training machine learning models on large datasets. It utilizes k-means clustering and sensitivity sampling to select a representative subset of the data, providing theoretical guarantees for the accuracy of the loss function on the selected data. The approach is shown to be more efficient and effective than previous methods, particularly in scenarios with large datasets. The paper also explores the use of this algorithm in active learning and regression settings, comparing it favorably to existing methods. However, it faces criticism for its experimental setup and the clarity of its assumptions and results.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, with clear explanations of the problem and the proposed solutions, making it accessible to a broad audience.
- The proposed algorithm is simple, practical, and efficient, requiring only k inferences from the model, which is a significant improvement over previous methods.
- The paper provides theoretical guarantees for the accuracy of the loss function on the selected data, which is a valuable contribution to the field.
- The approach is versatile and can be applied to different loss functions, showing potential for broader application in various settings.
- The paper is well-organized, with a clear structure and logical flow, which facilitates understanding and application of the proposed methods.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed comparison with other active learning methods, which could have provided a more robust evaluation of the proposed approach.
- The experimental setup and results are not convincingly presented, with some results (e.g., in Figure 2) being unclear or inconsistent with theoretical predictions.
- There is a lack of discussion on the computational complexity of the proposed algorithm, particularly in terms of the time required to compute embeddings and the clustering process.
- The paper does not adequately address the limitations of the proposed method, such as its applicability to different types of data or the potential for overfitting due to the selection of representative samples.
- The paper could benefit from a more thorough discussion of related work and a clearer explanation of the assumptions and results, especially in the appendices.

**Questions:**
- Could you clarify the discrepancy between the theoretical predictions and the experimental results, particularly in Figure 2?
- How does the proposed method compare in terms of computational efficiency and accuracy with other active learning methods, especially in scenarios with large datasets?
- Can you provide more details on the computational complexity of the proposed algorithm, including the time required for different components such as embedding computation and clustering?
- How sensitive is the proposed method to the choice of hyperparameters, and what are the recommended values for practical applications?
- Could you elaborate on the choice of the Holder continuity assumption and its implications for the practical applicability of the method?
