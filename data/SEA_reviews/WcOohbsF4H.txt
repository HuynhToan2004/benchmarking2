 **Summary:**
The paper introduces ST-MEM, a self-supervised learning framework designed for ECG data analysis. ST-MEM leverages a spatio-temporal masked auto-encoder to learn general representations of ECG signals, focusing on the spatial and temporal relationships inherent in ECG data. The framework is evaluated against various benchmarks and demonstrates competitive performance, particularly in arrhythmia classification tasks. The methodology involves a lead-wise shared decoder, lead-wise shared encoder, and lead embedding to learn spatial and temporal relationships effectively. Extensive experiments are conducted to validate the approach, showing that ST-MEM outperforms other SSL baselines in various settings. However, the paper lacks detailed analysis of the learned representations and could benefit from more comprehensive comparisons with state-of-the-art methods.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written and easy to follow, with clear presentation of the methodology and experimental results.
- The proposed method is simple, effective, and has been validated through extensive experiments, demonstrating its effectiveness in learning general representations of ECG signals.
- The methodology is designed to consider both spatial and temporal relationships within ECG data, which is crucial for understanding the complex dynamics of heart electrical activity.
- The paper provides a comprehensive evaluation of the proposed method against multiple baseline methods, demonstrating its competitive performance in various settings.
- The use of a self-supervised learning approach to learn general representations of ECG signals is innovative and addresses the challenges of limited labeled ECG data.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks detailed analysis of the learned representations, particularly how the model captures spatial and temporal relationships in ECG signals.
- The performance improvement over existing methods is not significant, and the paper does not sufficiently compare its results with state-of-the-art methods in terms of AUROC scores.
- The paper could benefit from a more detailed discussion on the limitations of the proposed method, particularly in terms of its applicability to different ECG datasets and its scalability to larger, more diverse datasets.
- The experimental setup and dataset descriptions are not detailed enough, which could limit the reproducibility and understanding of the results.
- The paper could be improved by providing more detailed visualizations and comparisons to better illustrate the spatio-temporal relationships learned by the model.

**Questions:**
- Could you provide more detailed visualizations or examples to better illustrate the spatio-temporal relationships learned by the model?
- How does the model perform when applied to different ECG datasets, particularly those with different characteristics or acquisition methods?
- Can you elaborate on the limitations of the proposed method and discuss potential avenues for future research to address these limitations?
- How does the model handle the variability in ECG signals due to patient-specific factors, such as body size and age?
- Could you discuss the potential applications of the learned representations beyond ECG analysis, such as in other medical imaging or physiological signal processing tasks?
