 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel method for solving inverse problems using latent diffusion models (LDMs), focusing on the hard data consistency approach to address the non-linearity of the encoder and decoder. The proposed algorithm, ReSample, incorporates data consistency during the reverse sampling process, which is a new concept termed "hard data consistency." The method is validated through extensive experiments on various datasets, demonstrating state-of-the-art performance. The theoretical analysis supports the method's effectiveness, showing that the proposed resampling scheme can map measurement-consistent samples back onto the noisy data manifold, enhancing the model's ability to handle inverse problems.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, clear, and easy to follow, with a clear motivation for the research and a comprehensive literature review.
- The proposed method is simple yet effective, with a clear explanation of the methodology and its theoretical underpinnings.
- The experimental results are convincing, showing that the proposed method outperforms existing state-of-the-art methods in terms of both quantitative and qualitative results.
- The paper introduces a novel resampling scheme that effectively maps measurement-consistent samples back onto the correct noisy data manifold, enhancing the model's ability to handle inverse problems.
- The theoretical analysis provides a strong foundation for the proposed method, showing that it can achieve state-of-the-art performance on various inverse problem tasks.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed comparison with existing methods that use GANs for solving inverse problems, which could provide a more robust evaluation of the proposed method.
- The computational cost of the proposed method is higher than existing methods, which might limit its practical application in real-world scenarios.
- The experimental settings and results are not clearly described, making it difficult to fully understand the impact and effectiveness of the proposed method.
- The paper does not adequately address the potential limitations of the proposed method, such as the assumptions made about the forward operator and the potential impact of measurement noise.
- The paper could benefit from a more detailed discussion on the choice of hyperparameters and their impact on the performance of the model.

**Questions:**

- How does the proposed method perform when the measurement noise is high?

- Can the authors provide more details on the experimental settings, such as the choice of datasets and the specific conditions under which the experiments were conducted?
- How does the proposed method handle non-linear inverse problems, and what are the implications of using a linear operator in the optimization process?
