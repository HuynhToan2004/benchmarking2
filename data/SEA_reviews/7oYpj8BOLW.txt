 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel benchmark, BackBench, designed to evaluate the robustness of vision and multimodal models against changes in the background of images. This benchmark utilizes text-driven image generation to alter the backgrounds of real images while preserving the objects' appearance and semantics. The benchmark is validated through extensive experiments across various vision tasks such as classification, segmentation, detection, and captioning, using models like ResNet, ViT, and CLIP. The results demonstrate that models trained on natural or adversarial backgrounds can perform poorly when tested on images with different backgrounds. The paper also explores the impact of different background variations on model performance and provides insights into the robustness of models under different conditions.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, clear, and easy to follow, with a clear motivation and a well-designed framework that effectively generates a diverse set of object-to-background context variations.
- The proposed benchmark is novel and practical, focusing on the evaluation of object-to-background context changes, which is an important and under-explored aspect in vision models.
- The paper provides a comprehensive set of experiments across various tasks such as classification, segmentation, detection, and captioning, using a wide range of models including ResNet, ViT, and CLIP, which helps in validating the effectiveness of the proposed method.
- The analysis conducted on the performance of different models under various background variations is insightful and contributes to a better understanding of model robustness.
- The paper is well-structured, with clear presentation of the methodology, results, and discussions, which enhances the reader's comprehension of the research.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed discussion on the limitations of the proposed method, particularly in terms of the generalizability of the results across different datasets and the potential impact of varying image qualities on model performance.
- There is a need for more comprehensive evaluation metrics, including qualitative evaluations, to better understand the performance of models under different background conditions.
- The paper could benefit from more detailed explanations and examples of the adversarial background generation process, which is currently not well-explained.
- The paper does not sufficiently discuss the potential negative societal impacts of the proposed method, which could include the misuse of the benchmark for malicious purposes.
- There are some minor issues with the writing and formatting of the paper, such as inconsistencies in the captioning and referencing of figures and tables.

**Questions:**
- How do the models perform when evaluated on different datasets such as ImageNet-R, which includes more diverse backgrounds compared to ImageNet?
- Could you provide more details on how the adversarial backgrounds are generated and what specific conditions are used during this process?
- How do the models trained on adversarial backgrounds compare in terms of performance when tested on natural or adversarial backgrounds?
- What are the potential applications of the proposed benchmark, and how can it be used to improve the robustness of models in real-world scenarios?
- Can you provide more qualitative results, such as visualizations of the adversarial backgrounds and the performance of models under different conditions?
- How do the results compare when using different segmentation models like FastSAM, and what impact does this have on the overall performance of the benchmark?
