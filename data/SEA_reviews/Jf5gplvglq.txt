 **Summary:**
The paper introduces Skill-Mix, a novel evaluation method aimed at assessing the ability of language models (LLMs) to combine skills, specifically by randomly selecting a subset of skills and asking the LLM to generate text based on these skills. This approach is intended to test the models' generalization capabilities and their ability to handle complex, unseen combinations of skills. The authors conduct experiments using various models, including GPT-4 and LLaMA-2, to demonstrate the effectiveness and generalization of the models under different skill combinations. The paper also discusses the potential for scaling up the evaluation to more complex models and the challenges posed by the need for extensive human grading.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, with clear explanations and easy-to-understand figures and tables.
- The introduction of Skill-Mix as a novel evaluation method for assessing the ability of language models to combine skills is innovative and timely, addressing the current limitations of existing evaluations.
- The paper provides a detailed analysis of the saturation point of models with increasing skill complexity and discusses the potential for scaling up the evaluation to more complex models.
- The authors have conducted experiments using different models and compared their results to demonstrate the effectiveness of the Skill-Mix evaluation method.
- The paper includes a comprehensive discussion on the limitations of current evaluations and the potential for improving the evaluation of AI capabilities.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a clear definition and delineation of the skills used in the evaluation, which could lead to confusion about what constitutes a skill and how it is measured.
- There is a potential for bias in the evaluation due to the use of GPT-4 as the grader, which could affect the results and the interpretation of the findings.
- The paper does not adequately address the issue of training-set contamination, which could impact the validity of the results.
- The paper does not include a comprehensive discussion on the potential for overfitting in the evaluation, which could affect the reliability of the results.
- The paper does not provide sufficient details on how the models were trained or the specifics of the training data used, which could impact the generalizability of the findings.
- The paper does not include a discussion on the potential ethical implications of the evaluation, such as the impact on fairness and diversity in AI systems.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors clarify how the skills were defined and selected for the evaluation, and how they were verified to ensure their validity and relevance to the task at hand?
- How were the models trained, and what specific training data were used? Could the authors provide more details on the training process to ensure the reliability and generalizability of the results?
- Given the use of GPT-4 as the grader, how were steps taken to mitigate potential biases in the grading process? Could the authors discuss the potential impact of these biases on the results and their interpretation?
- The paper mentions the potential for overfitting in the evaluation. Could the authors provide more details on how they addressed this issue and what steps were taken to ensure the validity of the results?
- How does the Skill-Mix evaluation compare to other existing evaluations in terms of its ability to assess the generalization capabilities of models? Could the authors discuss the potential advantages and disadvantages of the Skill-Mix approach compared to other methods?
- Could the authors discuss the potential ethical implications of the Skill-Mix evaluation, particularly in terms of fairness and diversity in AI systems? How might the evaluation be adapted to address these issues and ensure that it promotes equitable and inclusive AI development?
