 **Summary:**
The paper explores the decomposition of returns into components attributable to agent actions (skill) and those influenced by environmental stochasticity (luck). It introduces a method called Direct Advantage Estimation (DAE) for off-policy settings, which extends the original DAE method to handle stochastic environments. The authors propose using a conditional variational auto-encoder (CVAE) to approximate the constraint on the B component, which is crucial for off-policy learning. The paper includes empirical evaluations in simple environments to demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed method, although concerns are raised about the clarity of the writing and the robustness of the experimental results.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written and clearly presents the problem, making it accessible to a broad audience.
- The proposed method is simple and easy to follow, with a clear connection to existing methods such as DAE and Monte Carlo methods.
- The paper provides a novel approach to off-policy learning by decomposing returns into skill and luck components, which is a significant contribution to the field.
- The use of a conditional variational auto-encoder (CVAE) to approximate the constraint on the B component is a creative and potentially effective solution to a complex problem.
- The paper includes empirical evaluations that demonstrate the method's effectiveness in simple environments, providing a solid foundation for further research.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a clear discussion on the limitations of the proposed method, particularly in high-dimensional domains.
- There is a notable absence of detailed discussion on the experimental setup, including the choice of environments and the hyperparameters used, which could affect the reproducibility and generalizability of the results.
- The paper does not provide sufficient evidence to support the claims made, particularly regarding the effectiveness of the method in more complex environments.
- The writing style is not clear in some sections, particularly in the introduction and the experimental results, which could confuse readers and undermine the paper's credibility.
- The paper does not adequately address the computational overhead and additional hyperparameters introduced by the use of CVAEs, which could limit the practical applicability of the method.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors provide more details on the experimental setup, including the choice of environments, the hyperparameters used, and the criteria for selecting these parameters?
- How does the proposed method perform in more complex environments, and what modifications, if any, are necessary for it to be effective in such settings?
- Could the authors clarify the role and impact of the CVAE in the proposed method, particularly in terms of computational overhead and additional hyperparameters?
- How does the paper address the limitations discussed, such as the lack of clarity in some sections and the need for more robust empirical evidence?
