 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel method for generating human-producible adversarial examples using simple line drawings, which can be easily reproduced by untrained individuals. This approach is aimed at producing adversarial examples that are effective in both digital and physical environments, demonstrating their impact on YOLO-based object detection systems. The methodology involves a generate-and-prune process to optimize the placement of lines, which can be drawn with minimal training or equipment. The paper includes a user study to evaluate the effectiveness of these adversarial examples, showing that they can be reliably replicated by humans. However, concerns were raised about the generalizability of the findings beyond the specific models tested and the limited evaluation of the robustness of the adversarial examples against different conditions.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel method for generating human-producible adversarial examples using simple line drawings, which can be easily reproduced by untrained individuals.
- The methodology is evaluated in both digital and physical environments, demonstrating its effectiveness in disrupting YOLO-based object detection systems.
- The paper includes a user study to evaluate the effectiveness of the adversarial examples, showing that they can be reliably replicated by humans.
- The approach is simple and easy to reproduce, making it accessible for a broader audience.
- The paper is well-written and the method is clearly explained, making it easy to follow.
- The paper demonstrates the potential of adversarial attacks that can be easily reproduced by humans, which is a significant contribution to the field.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a comprehensive evaluation of the robustness of the adversarial examples against different conditions, such as variations in drawing style, lighting conditions, and printing quality.
- The evaluation primarily focuses on YOLO-based models, and it is unclear whether the findings can be generalized to other object detection models or different types of machine learning models.
- The paper does not provide a detailed analysis of the transferability of the adversarial examples across different models or datasets.
- The user study involves a limited number of participants (only 4), which may not be sufficient to draw robust conclusions about the effectiveness of the adversarial examples.
- The paper does not discuss the ethical implications of the work, which could be a significant concern given the potential misuse of the technology.
- The paper does not compare the proposed method with other existing methods for generating adversarial examples, which could help in understanding the advantages and limitations of the proposed approach.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors provide more details on the evaluation setup, particularly how the adversarial examples were applied in the physical world and how the results were validated?
- How do the authors ensure the robustness of the adversarial examples against different conditions, such as variations in drawing style, lighting conditions, and printing quality?
- Can the proposed method be generalized to other types of machine learning models beyond object detection?
- How do the authors address the ethical concerns raised by the potential misuse of the technology?
- Could the authors provide more details on the user study, such as the demographics of the participants and the statistical significance of the results?
- How do the authors compare their method with other existing methods for generating adversarial examples, and what are the advantages and limitations of their approach compared to these methods?
