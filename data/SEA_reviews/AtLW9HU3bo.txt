 **Summary:**
The paper introduces E-STR, a novel method designed to enhance Multi-modal Large Language Models (MLLMs) in complex Video Question Answering (VideoQA) tasks. E-STR employs a differentiable question-critical keyframes retriever to selectively focus on question-relevant moments in videos, reducing the computational burden and improving performance on benchmarks such as NExT-QA, Causal-VidQA, and STAR. The methodology includes a general context encoder to encode unselected parts and lightweight adapters to adaptively retrieve question-critical frames. Despite its innovative approach, the paper has been critiqued for its reliance on existing methodologies, lack of detailed comparative analysis, and potential over-reliance on specific components like the question-critical keyframes retriever.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel methodology for Video Question Answering (VideoQA) using Multi-modal Large Language Models (MLLMs), specifically the E-STR method, which effectively identifies question-critical moments in videos.
- The E-STR method is supported by extensive experiments, demonstrating its effectiveness and efficiency in complex VideoQA tasks.
- The paper is well-written, clear, and easy to follow, making it accessible to a broad audience.
- The methodology is straightforward and cost-effective, with the introduction of lightweight adapters that facilitate the learning of spatial-temporal representations.
- The paper provides a detailed ablation study, highlighting the importance of each component of the proposed method.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper primarily focuses on a specific component of the E-STR method, the question-critical keyframes retriever, which may limit the perceived novelty and depth of the research.
- The methodology is not sufficiently differentiated from existing methods, particularly those that use similar components like the question-critical keyframes retriever and general context encoder.
- The paper lacks a detailed comparative analysis with other state-of-the-art methods, which could have provided a clearer picture of the method's advantages and disadvantages.
- The paper does not sufficiently address the issue of redundancy in question-irrelevant frames, which could affect the performance of the method.
- There is a lack of discussion on the limitations of the proposed method, which is crucial for understanding its applicability and potential improvements.
- The paper could benefit from a more thorough discussion on the computational efficiency and memory usage of the proposed method, especially in comparison to other state-of-the-art methods.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors provide a more detailed discussion on the computational efficiency and memory usage of the proposed method compared to other state-of-the-art methods?
- How does the E-STR method perform when applied to other types of video question answering tasks, such as those involving long videos or complex scenarios?
- Could the authors elaborate on the potential limitations of the proposed method and suggest areas for future research to address these limitations?
- Is there a possibility to include a discussion on the impact of the size of the event window on the performance of the E-STR method?
- Can the authors provide a more detailed comparison with other state-of-the-art methods, particularly in terms of the effectiveness and efficiency of the different components of the E-STR method?
