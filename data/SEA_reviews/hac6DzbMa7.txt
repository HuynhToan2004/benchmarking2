 **Summary:**
The paper introduces STIL, a novel task-incremental learning method that employs orthogonal weight subspaces to address catastrophic forgetting and knowledge transfer. The authors argue that orthogonal gradients alone are insufficient for preventing forgetting and propose a method that orthogonalizes the weight space of each task, allowing for efficient transfer of knowledge across tasks. Theoretical analysis and extensive experiments demonstrate the method's effectiveness in preventing forgetting and transferring knowledge, outperforming existing methods in various benchmarks. However, concerns were raised about the clarity of some theoretical arguments and the practical implications of the method's computational complexity.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, making it easy to follow and understand.
- The authors provide extensive theoretical analysis and experimental results, including detailed descriptions of the proposed method.
- The proposed method, STIL, is innovative in its approach to orthogonal weight subspaces for task-incremental learning, which is a novel concept in the field.
- The paper is supported by a thorough theoretical analysis and extensive experimental results, demonstrating the effectiveness of the proposed method in preventing catastrophic forgetting and transferring knowledge.
- The proposed method is computationally efficient, which is a significant advantage over some existing methods.
- The paper is well-organized, with clear figures and tables that aid in understanding the experimental results.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper's theoretical analysis is not always clear, particularly in the proofs and some assumptions made, which could benefit from further clarification.
- The experimental setup and results, such as the use of small datasets and the performance of the method on larger, more complex datasets, need more detailed explanation and justification.
- The paper could benefit from a more detailed discussion on the limitations of the proposed method and how these might impact its practical application.
- There are some minor typographical and formatting errors that need to be corrected.
- The paper could be improved by providing more detailed explanations and justifications for certain methodological choices and experimental setups.

**Questions:**
