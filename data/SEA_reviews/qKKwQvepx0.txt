 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel framework for optimizing generative models using natural language strings as parameters, aiming to enhance explainability and steerability in statistical models. The authors propose a method that iteratively optimizes continuous relaxations of string parameters and then discretizes them using a language model. This approach is demonstrated across various models such as clustering, topic modeling, and regression, showing potential in improving explainability and interpretability of machine learning models. The framework is evaluated on real-world datasets, showing that it can effectively learn natural language strings as model parameters and generate explainable image clusters. However, the paper is criticized for its lack of clarity in methodological descriptions, the need for more comprehensive experimental validation, and the absence of a detailed discussion on the limitations of the proposed methods.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel approach to optimizing generative models using natural language strings as parameters, which can enhance the explainability and steerability of these models.
- The methodology is well-presented, with clear explanations of the mathematical formulations and the optimization algorithm.
- The experiments are well-designed, demonstrating the effectiveness of the proposed method in improving the performance of various models.
- The paper is well-written, making it easy to follow and understand the proposed framework and its applications.
- The framework is flexible and can be applied to different types of models, including clustering, topic modeling, and regression.
- The application of the framework to image clustering is innovative and demonstrates the potential of the method in practical scenarios.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks clarity in some sections, particularly in the description of the optimization algorithm and the experimental setup.
- There is a need for more comprehensive experimental validation, including comparisons with existing methods and more detailed results.
- The paper does not discuss the limitations of the proposed method in detail, which could help in understanding its applicability and potential drawbacks.
- The paper could benefit from a more detailed discussion on the computational costs and scalability of the optimization algorithm.
- The paper does not sufficiently differentiate its contributions from existing works, particularly in the area of learning discrete latent variables using large language models.
- The experimental results are not convincingly superior to existing methods, and the paper does not sufficiently address the potential biases introduced by the use of large language models.

**Questions:**
- Can you clarify the specific contributions of your work compared to existing methods, particularly in the area of learning discrete latent variables using large language models?
- How do you address the potential biases introduced by the use of large language models in your methodology?
- Could you provide more details on the experimental setup, including the specific datasets used and the parameters of the models?
- How do you ensure that the natural language strings generated by your method are not biased or misleading?
- In the optimization algorithm, how do you handle the potential for the algorithm to converge to suboptimal solutions, especially in the case of discrete variables?
- Could you elaborate on the computational costs and scalability of the optimization algorithm, particularly in terms of the number of parameters and the size of the dataset?
- How do you ensure that the natural language strings generated by your method are interpretable and meaningful to humans?
