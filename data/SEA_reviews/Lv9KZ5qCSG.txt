 **Summary:**
The paper introduces the EyeFairness dataset, a large-scale medical dataset containing 3D imaging data (OCT scans) and 2D imaging data (SLO fundus images) alongside demographic attributes for fairness learning in medical imaging. It focuses on three major eye diseases: age-related macular degeneration, diabetic retinopathy, and glaucoma, which collectively affect over 380 million patients globally. The dataset is designed to address the lack of large-scale public medical datasets with comprehensive demographic identity attributes, which is crucial for fairness learning in medicine. The paper also presents a novel fair identity scaling (FIS) approach and a performance-scaled disparity metric to evaluate model performance. The FIS method combines group and individual scaling to improve fairness, while the disparity metric provides a comprehensive evaluation of model performance.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a large-scale public medical dataset with 3D imaging data, which is crucial for fairness learning in medicine.
- The dataset is well-structured, covering three major eye diseases affecting millions of patients, and includes comprehensive demographic identity attributes.
- The paper proposes a novel fair identity scaling (FIS) method and a performance-scaled disparity metric, which are innovative contributions to the field.
- The dataset and code are made publicly available, enhancing reproducibility and facilitating further research.
- The paper addresses the significant issue of fairness in medical imaging, which is an important and under-explored area in the field.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed discussion on how the dataset was collected and curated, which is essential for understanding the dataset's quality and reliability.
- There is a lack of clarity in the definitions and explanations of certain terms and metrics used in the paper, such as "performance-scaled disparity" and "fair identity scaling."
- The paper does not adequately address the potential biases that could exist in the dataset, which could affect the fairness of the models trained on it.
- The experimental results presented in the paper are not convincing, with some results showing inconsistencies and a lack of statistical significance.
- The paper does not sufficiently compare its contributions to existing literature, which could undermine the perceived novelty and significance of the work.
- The methodological details, such as the choice of hyperparameters and the impact of different model architectures, are not thoroughly discussed, which could limit the reproducibility and applicability of the findings.

**Questions:**

- Can you clarify the definitions and calculations of "performance-scaled disparity" and "fair identity scaling"? How do these metrics contribute to fairness in machine learning models?
- How do the authors address potential biases in the dataset, and what steps were taken to mitigate their impact on the fairness of the models trained on the dataset?
- Could you provide more details on the experimental setup, such as the choice of hyperparameters and the impact of different model architectures on the results?
- How do the proposed methods compare to existing fairness learning models, and what are the specific advantages of the FIS method over other approaches?
- Could you discuss the potential societal impacts of the proposed methods, particularly in terms of fairness and equity in medical diagnoses?
