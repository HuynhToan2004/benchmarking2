 **Summary:**
The paper addresses the solution of bilevel programming problems where the lower-level decision variables are binary, using a neural network to approximate the value function. The proposed method involves sampling, training, and solving, with the neural network's output supermodular to ensure the validity of the optimization problem. The approach is compared against a state-of-the-art solver, showing some advantages in computational efficiency and accuracy. However, the paper's experimental scope is limited to small-scale problems, and the theoretical analysis is insufficient to support the claims of the method's effectiveness and general applicability.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-organized, easy to follow, and provides a clear presentation of the proposed method.
- The methodology is novel, particularly the use of a neural network to approximate the value function in bilevel programming problems, which is a significant contribution to the field.
- The paper is well-written, with clear explanations of the sampling and training processes, and the use of supermodular neural networks to ensure the supermodularity of the neural network's output.
- The methodology is supported by theoretical analysis and numerical experiments, demonstrating its effectiveness and efficiency in solving bilevel programming problems.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a comprehensive theoretical analysis, particularly in the area of generalization bounds, which is crucial for understanding the method's applicability to more complex and larger-scale problems.
- The experimental evaluation is limited to small-scale problems, which does not adequately demonstrate the method's scalability and effectiveness in larger, more realistic scenarios.
- The paper does not sufficiently compare the proposed method with other existing methods, such as those that use neural networks for bilevel optimization, which could provide a better understanding of the method's advantages and limitations.
- The paper does not address the potential limitations or negative societal impacts of the proposed method, which is a significant omission in ethical considerations.
- The paper could benefit from a more detailed discussion on the practical implications of the method, including its computational complexity and scalability, which are crucial for its practical application.
