 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel graph embedding method called Adversarial Enhanced Representation (AER) for link prediction in multi-layer networks. AER is designed to learn inter-layer transferable representations and fuse them with intra-layer representations using a gated unit. The method involves a representation generator, a layer discriminator, and a link predictor, all working together to predict missing links in multi-layer networks. Extensive experiments on real-world datasets demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed method. However, the paper is criticized for its lack of clarity in presentation, particularly in explaining the mathematical notations and the role of different components in the model. The novelty of the method is questioned due to its similarity to existing works, and the experimental results are not convincingly superior to state-of-the-art methods.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written and easy to follow, with a clear description of the proposed method.
- The proposed method is novel and effectively utilizes multi-layer correlation for link prediction, achieving significant improvements over state-of-the-art methods.
- The paper is well-organized and includes extensive experiments on real-world datasets, demonstrating the effectiveness of the proposed method.
- The paper includes an ablation study and a case study, which provide insights into the performance of the proposed method.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks clarity in some parts, particularly in the mathematical notations and the explanation of the role and function of different components in the model.
- The novelty of the method is questionable, as similar approaches have been previously proposed.
- The experimental results do not convincingly demonstrate superiority over state-of-the-art methods, and the improvements are not significant.
- The paper could benefit from a more detailed discussion on the limitations of the proposed method and potential directions for future work.
- The paper does not include a detailed discussion on the computational complexity of the proposed method, which could be crucial for practical applications.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors clarify the mathematical notations and the role of different components in the model, particularly the representation generator, layer discriminator, and link predictor?
- How does the proposed method handle the transferability of representations across different layers, especially in multi-layer networks?
- Can the authors provide a detailed discussion on the computational complexity of the proposed method and compare it with existing methods?
- How does the proposed method perform on larger datasets or in scenarios with a larger number of layers?
- Could the authors provide more detailed explanations on the adversarial training process and its impact on the performance of the model?
- How does the proposed method compare with other state-of-the-art methods in terms of computational efficiency and scalability?
