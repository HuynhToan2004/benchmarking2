 **Summary:**
The paper investigates the Lion optimization algorithm, a method discovered through automated algorithm search, which is known for its efficiency and memory efficiency. The authors extend the Lion algorithm by replacing the sign function with a subgradient of a convex function, introducing a new family of Lion-K algorithms. This extension allows for a theoretical analysis of the Lion algorithm, including the derivation of a Lyapunov function and the demonstration of its convergence in both continuous and discrete time settings. The paper also discusses the implications of these theoretical findings for practical applications, suggesting potential improvements and extensions of the Lion algorithm.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, with a clear structure and logical flow that aids in understanding the complex theoretical concepts presented.
- The authors provide a comprehensive theoretical analysis of the Lion algorithm, including the derivation of a Lyapunov function and the demonstration of its convergence in both continuous and discrete time settings.
- The paper includes a detailed discussion on the implications of these theoretical findings for practical applications, suggesting potential improvements and extensions of the Lion algorithm.
- The introduction of a new family of Lion-K algorithms, which extends the Lion algorithm by replacing the sign function with a subgradient of a convex function, provides a broader theoretical framework for understanding the behavior of the Lion algorithm.
- The paper includes a thorough experimental section that demonstrates the behavior of the Lion-K algorithms on both toy examples and large-scale neural networks, showing that the Lion-K algorithms closely follow the theoretical predictions.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a clear discussion on the practical implications of the theoretical results, particularly how these results can be applied to improve the Lion algorithm in practical settings.
- The paper does not sufficiently compare the Lion algorithm with other existing optimization algorithms, which could help in understanding the unique advantages and limitations of the Lion algorithm.
- The experimental results presented are somewhat limited, focusing primarily on the behavior of the Lion-K algorithms on toy examples and large-scale neural networks, without sufficient exploration of the performance of the Lion-K algorithms on smaller, more diverse datasets.
- The paper could benefit from a more detailed discussion on the limitations of the Lion algorithm, particularly in terms of its applicability to different types of optimization problems and its performance in various settings.
- The paper could be improved by including more practical applications and a more detailed discussion on the limitations and potential extensions of the Lion algorithm.
