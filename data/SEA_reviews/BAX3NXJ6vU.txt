 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel algorithm named PRGDA for finding second-order stationary points in stochastic nonconvex minimax and bilevel optimization problems. This algorithm employs perturbed gradient descent ascent and variance reduction techniques to improve convergence rates and escape saddle points. Theoretical analysis and numerical experiments are provided to support the algorithm's efficacy, showing that it can achieve the same gradient complexity as finding first-order stationary points. The paper also discusses the algorithm's performance in escaping saddle points and its applicability to various optimization problems.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a new algorithm, PRGDA, which is the first to achieve finding second-order stationary points in stochastic nonconvex minimax optimization problems.
- The proposed algorithm is pure first-order and does not require the computation of second-order derivatives, making it more efficient in implementation compared to other methods.
- The paper provides a comprehensive theoretical analysis of the proposed algorithm, including the gradient complexity of the algorithm which is at the same level as that of finding first-order stationary points.
- The paper is well-written, making it easy to follow, and the experimental results are promising, showing the effectiveness of the algorithm in escaping saddle points.
- The convergence analysis of the algorithm is solid, and the paper provides a thorough comparison with related works, highlighting the novelty and contributions of the proposed method.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed comparison of the computational complexity and time complexity of the proposed algorithm with other existing methods, which is crucial for understanding the practical efficiency of the algorithm.
- The paper does not provide a detailed comparison of the proposed algorithm with other second-order methods in terms of the second-order stationary point, which is the main focus of the paper.
- There is a lack of clarity in the algorithm design and the proofs, particularly in the inner loop of the algorithm and the proofs of the main theorems.
- The paper does not discuss the limitations of the proposed algorithm, which could provide a more balanced view of its strengths and weaknesses.
- The experiments are limited to a specific dataset, and it would be beneficial to include more diverse datasets to validate the generalizability of the findings.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors provide more details on the computational complexity and time complexity of the proposed algorithm compared to other existing methods?
- How does the proposed algorithm compare with other second-order methods in terms of the second-order stationary point?
- Could the authors clarify the algorithm design, particularly in the inner loop of the algorithm and the proofs of the main theorems?
- What are the specific advantages of the proposed algorithm over other existing methods, especially in terms of practical efficiency and convergence rates?
- How does the proposed algorithm perform in different optimization scenarios, and what are the limitations of its applicability?
- Can the authors discuss the limitations of the proposed algorithm and provide insights into potential areas for future research?
