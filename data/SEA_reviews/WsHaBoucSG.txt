 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel multi-agent reinforcement learning framework for emergent language-based dialogue in a collaborative navigation task. This framework involves two agents: a Tourist and a Guide, who communicate through a sequence of messages to guide the Tourist to a target location. The Tourist's messages are used to infer its current location, and the Guide generates navigation instructions based on this inference. The model is evaluated on the R2R and CVDN datasets, showing promising results in terms of goal progress and adapted goal progress. The paper also includes an ablation study on the length of the dialogue and the vocabulary size, demonstrating the model's effectiveness in complex, dynamic environments. However, the paper's evaluation is limited to these datasets, and the generalizability of the approach to other navigation tasks or more complex environments remains unclear.

**Strengths:**
- The paper addresses an interesting and novel problem of emergent language-based dialogue in a multi-agent setting, which is relevant to real-world applications.
- The proposed framework is innovative, involving a Guide agent that infers the Tourist's location based on messages and generates navigation instructions, while the Tourist uses these instructions to navigate.
- The paper is well-written, clear, and easy to follow, with a comprehensive evaluation that includes an ablation study on the length of the dialogue and the vocabulary size.
- The model's performance is evaluated on two datasets, demonstrating significant improvements over baseline methods.
- The emergent language used by the agents is evaluated, showing that it is grounded in the environment and task-specific.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a clear explanation of how the Guide agent's environment encoding is used to infer the Tourist's location, which is crucial for understanding the model's functionality.
- The evaluation is limited to the R2R and CVDN datasets, which may not fully demonstrate the model's generalizability to other navigation tasks or more complex environments.
- The paper does not discuss the limitations of the approach, such as its applicability to other types of environments or the scalability of the model to larger, more complex scenarios.
- The comparison with other models, particularly those that do not use emergent language, is not adequately discussed, which could help in understanding the advantages and disadvantages of the proposed approach.
- The paper does not provide a detailed discussion on the limitations of the emergent language used by the agents, which could affect the practical applicability of the model.

**Questions:**
- How is the Guide agent's environment encoding used to infer the Tourist's location? Could you provide more details on this process?
- How does the model handle the issue of grounding the emergent language to the environment, especially in more complex or dynamic environments?
- Can you discuss the limitations of the approach and how they might affect the practical applicability of the model?
- How does the model perform in scenarios where the Guide's instructions are incorrect or misleading?
- Could you provide more details on the model's training process, particularly how the reinforcement learning algorithm is used to train the agents?
- How does the model handle the issue of scalability, especially in larger or more complex environments?
- Can you provide more examples of the emergent language used by the agents and discuss how it is grounded in the environment and task-specific?
