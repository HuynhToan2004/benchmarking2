 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel Bayesian Optimization (BO) framework named Bayesian Optimization with Abstract Properties (BOAP), which integrates expert feedback on abstract properties to enhance BO performance. BOAP models these abstract properties using a rank Gaussian process (GP) and incorporates them into the BO process by augmenting the input space. This approach allows BO to leverage expert knowledge effectively, potentially improving optimization efficiency. The framework is evaluated on both synthetic and real-world datasets, demonstrating its effectiveness compared to traditional BO methods. However, the paper is criticized for its limited empirical evaluation, lack of comprehensive theoretical analysis, and potential issues in the presentation and clarity of the methodology.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, clear, and easy to follow, with a novel approach that integrates expert feedback into Bayesian Optimization (BO), which is a significant contribution to the field.
- The methodology is straightforward and intuitive, with a clear explanation of the algorithm and its components, including the use of rank GPs and the augmentation of the input space with expert feedback.
- The paper provides a theoretical discussion on the convergence behavior of the proposed BOAP method, which is a valuable addition to the empirical results.
- The empirical results show that the proposed method outperforms existing methods, demonstrating its effectiveness in both synthetic and real-world datasets.
- The paper is original in its approach, combining existing techniques in a novel way to address the challenge of incorporating expert feedback into BO, which is a significant contribution to the field.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a comprehensive theoretical analysis of the proposed BOAP method, particularly in terms of its convergence behavior and the impact of expert feedback on the optimization process.
- The empirical evaluation is limited, with only a few datasets used, and the results do not convincingly demonstrate the superiority of the proposed method over existing methods.
- The paper does not adequately address the potential for expert bias in the feedback, which could lead to suboptimal outcomes in the optimization process.
- There is a lack of discussion on the computational complexity and scalability of the proposed method, which could be a significant limitation in practical applications.
- The paper could benefit from a more detailed explanation of the experimental setup, including the number of runs and the statistical significance of the results.
- The paper could be improved by including a more thorough discussion of related work and a more detailed comparison with existing methods.

**Questions:**
- Can you provide a more detailed discussion on the convergence behavior of the BOAP method, including the impact of expert feedback on the optimization process?
- How does the BOAP method handle the potential for expert bias in the feedback, and what measures are in place to mitigate this?
- Could you provide more details on the computational complexity and scalability of the proposed method, particularly in terms of the number of abstract properties and the size of the input space?
- How does the BOAP method compare to other existing methods that incorporate expert feedback into BO, and what are the advantages of your approach over these methods?
- Could you provide more details on the experimental setup, including the number of runs and the statistical significance of the results?
- Is there a plan to extend the empirical evaluation to include more datasets and to compare the results with a broader range of existing methods?
