 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel framework called Forward-Backward Multi-Objective Reinforcement Learning (FB-MORL) for handling multi-task control problems in environments with forward-backward dynamics. This framework is applied to a wireless caching system, where it demonstrates effectiveness through experiments and an ablation study. The FB-MORL algorithm, which includes the FB-MOAC algorithm, is designed to optimize rewards in systems with forward-backward dynamics. The paper also provides a convergence analysis for the FB-MOAC algorithm, which is a multi-objective actor-critic algorithm tailored for FB-MDPs. The experiments show that the FB-MOAC algorithm outperforms a single-objective actor-critic algorithm in a wireless caching scenario.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel framework called FB-MORL, which is applied to a wireless caching system, demonstrating its effectiveness through experiments and an ablation study.
- The proposed algorithm, FB-MOAC, is a multi-objective actor-critic algorithm tailored for FB-MDPs, and it provides a convergence analysis for FB-MOAC.
- The paper is well-written and easy to follow, with a clear presentation of the algorithm and its application.
- The paper includes a convergence analysis for the FB-MOAC algorithm, which is a significant contribution to the field of reinforcement learning.
- The experimental results are promising, showing that the FB-MOAC algorithm outperforms a single-objective actor-critic algorithm in a wireless caching scenario.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a discussion on the limitations of the proposed algorithm and a comparison with other multi-objective reinforcement learning algorithms.
- The paper does not discuss the computational complexity of the proposed algorithm, which is crucial for practical applications.
- The experiments are limited to a single application scenario, which might not fully demonstrate the generalizability of the proposed method.
- The paper does not provide a discussion on the scalability of the proposed method to larger-scale problems.
- The paper could benefit from a more detailed explanation of the experimental setup and the selection of hyperparameters.
- The paper does not adequately address the practical challenges of applying FB-MORL to real-world scenarios, such as the availability of data for training and the computational resources required.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors provide a more detailed discussion on the limitations of the proposed algorithm and how it compares to other multi-objective reinforcement learning algorithms?
- What is the computational complexity of the proposed algorithm, and how does it scale with the size of the problem?
- How does the proposed method handle scenarios where the forward and backward dynamics are not coupled?
- Can the authors provide a more detailed discussion on the experimental setup, including the selection of hyperparameters and the impact of these choices on the results?
- How does the proposed method handle scenarios where the forward and backward dynamics are not known in advance?
- Could the authors provide a more detailed discussion on the practical challenges of applying FB-MORL to real-world scenarios and how these challenges were addressed in the experiments?
