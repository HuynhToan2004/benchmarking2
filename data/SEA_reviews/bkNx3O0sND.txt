 **Summary:**
The paper introduces two novel fine-tuning methods, MBR fine-tuning and QE fine-tuning, aimed at enhancing the quality of neural machine translation (NMT) models by leveraging Minimum Bayes' Risk (MBR) and Quality Estimation (QE) metrics. These methods aim to address the inefficiency of MBR and QE during inference by distilling the knowledge from these methods into the training phase. The paper also explores the use of a large language model (LLM) as a teacher model, demonstrating significant improvements in model performance. The proposed methods are evaluated across various datasets and settings, showing that they outperform traditional beam search and greedy decoding methods. The paper also includes a comprehensive set of experiments and ablation studies, highlighting the effectiveness of the proposed methods.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written and easy to follow, with clear explanations of the proposed methods and their implications.
- The idea of using MBR and QE as training objectives is innovative and effective, particularly in leveraging the strengths of these methods without their computational overhead.
- The paper includes a comprehensive set of experiments, covering various settings and demonstrating the effectiveness of the proposed methods.
- The use of a large language model (LLM) as a teacher model is a novel approach that significantly enhances the performance of the models.
- The paper addresses an important problem in the field of natural language processing (NLP) by improving the quality of neural machine translation (NMT) models.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a direct comparison with other advanced decoding methods such as greedy decoding, which could provide a more comprehensive evaluation of the proposed methods.
- The use of beam search as the primary evaluation metric might not fully capture the strengths of the proposed methods, especially in terms of efficiency and computational cost.
- The paper does not sufficiently discuss the limitations of the proposed methods, such as the potential for overfitting or the impact of different decoding strategies on model performance.
- The paper could benefit from a more detailed analysis of the generated data and the impact of different decoding strategies on model performance.
- There is a lack of discussion on the applicability of the proposed methods to other natural language generation (NLG) tasks beyond neural machine translation (NMT).

**Questions:**
- Can the authors provide a more detailed comparison of the proposed methods with other advanced decoding methods, such as greedy decoding?
- How do the proposed methods perform when evaluated using other metrics beyond beam search, such as BLEURT or MetricX-XXL?
- Could the authors discuss the potential impact of different decoding strategies on the performance of the models?
- How does the paper address the potential for overfitting in the models, especially when using MBR and QE as training objectives?
- Is there a possibility to extend the proposed methods to other NLG tasks, such as text summarization or question answering?
