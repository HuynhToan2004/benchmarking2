 **Summary:**
The paper introduces SineNet, a novel architecture designed to address the misalignment issue in U-Net architectures when applied to time-dependent PDEs. SineNet employs a multi-stage architecture with multiple U-Net blocks, each responsible for advancing the solution by a small time step. This approach allows for better handling of temporal dynamics, particularly in scenarios involving both diffusion and advection. The paper demonstrates SineNet's effectiveness through experiments on various PDE datasets, showing improvements over existing methods like U-Net and FNO. Despite its innovative approach, concerns were raised about the novelty of the method, the clarity of its differentiation from existing methods, and the depth of its theoretical analysis.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, with clear explanations of the proposed method and its implementation, making it accessible and understandable.
- The authors provide a thorough analysis of the limitations of U-Net architectures in modeling temporal dynamics, particularly addressing the misalignment issue in skip connections.
- The proposed SineNet architecture is novel and shows promising results in experiments, demonstrating improvements over existing methods like U-Net and FNO.
- The paper includes a comprehensive ablation study that helps in understanding the impact of various components of the proposed architecture.
- The inclusion of an ablation study and a discussion on the number of waves versus test error provides valuable insights into the effectiveness of the proposed method.
- The paper addresses the misalignment issue in U-Nets, which is a significant problem in modeling time-evolving PDEs.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a clear differentiation from existing methods, particularly in terms of novelty and distinct contributions.
- There is a need for more detailed theoretical analysis and empirical evidence to support the claims made about the misalignment issue and the effectiveness of the proposed solution.
- The paper could benefit from more comprehensive comparisons with other existing methods, such as PDE-Refiner and other variants of U-Net.
- The paper's focus on specific PDEs, such as Navier-Stokes equations, may limit its applicability to other types of PDEs, which could affect its broader relevance.
- The paper's reliance on a multi-stage approach may limit its ability to handle complex, time-evolving PDEs effectively.
- There are some presentation issues, such as missing figures and unclear descriptions in some sections, which could confuse readers.
- The paper could benefit from a more detailed discussion on the computational efficiency and scalability of the proposed method, especially in terms of inference time and space requirements.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors clarify the distinction between SineNet and other existing methods, particularly in terms of novelty and distinct contributions?
- How does the proposed method handle different types of PDEs, especially those that do not involve both diffusion and advection?
