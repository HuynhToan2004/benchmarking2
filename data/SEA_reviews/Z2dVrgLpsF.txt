 **Summary:**
The paper investigates the issue of prototype collapse in clustering-based self-supervised learning, specifically focusing on the DINO family of methods. The authors propose a regularization technique to prevent prototype collapse, enhancing the diversity of prototypes and thereby improving the robustness of clustering. This approach is validated through experiments on ImageNet and other datasets, showing that it can improve few-shot learning performance and maintain transfer learning capabilities. The paper also discusses the implications of this regularization on the number of unique prototypes and the overall performance of the models.

**Strengths:**
- The paper addresses an important and relevant problem in the field of self-supervised learning, specifically the issue of prototype collapse, which is crucial for understanding and improving the performance of clustering-based SSL methods.
- The proposed regularization technique is novel and well-motivated, offering a new perspective on how to prevent prototype collapse and improve the diversity of prototypes, which is beneficial for learning more informative representations.
- The paper is well-written, clear, and easy to follow, making it accessible to a broad audience and contributing to the clarity of the research.
- The experiments conducted are comprehensive, providing a robust validation of the proposed method across various datasets and settings, demonstrating its effectiveness in improving few-shot learning performance and maintaining transfer learning capabilities.
- The paper contributes to the literature by proposing a new regularization method that addresses the issue of prototype collapse, which is a significant step forward in the field of self-supervised learning.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper could benefit from a more detailed discussion on the computational cost of the proposed method, especially in relation to the number of prototypes used and the impact on training times.
- There is a lack of discussion on the limitations of the proposed method, particularly in terms of its applicability to other datasets or settings where the prototype collapse issue might not be as pronounced.
- The paper does not provide a detailed analysis of the impact of the proposed method on transfer learning performance, which is a critical aspect of self-supervised learning.
- The paper could benefit from a more thorough comparison with existing methods, particularly those that also address the issue of prototype collapse, to better establish the novelty and effectiveness of the proposed approach.
- The paper could be improved by providing more visualizations or quantitative results to support the claims made about the effectiveness of the proposed method in preventing prototype collapse.

**Questions:**
- How does the proposed regularization method compare to other methods that address the issue of prototype collapse, particularly in terms of computational cost and effectiveness?
- Can the authors provide more details on the computational cost of the proposed method, especially in relation to the number of prototypes used and the impact on training times?
- How does the proposed method perform on other datasets or settings where the prototype collapse issue might not be as pronounced?
- Could the authors provide more detailed visualizations or quantitative results to support the claims made about the effectiveness of the proposed method in preventing prototype collapse?
- How does the proposed method affect the training time and the overall computational cost compared to other methods?
- Can the authors discuss the limitations of their method and potential directions for future research to address these limitations?
