 **Summary:**
The paper investigates the convergence rate of gradient descent in over-parametrized deep equilibrium models (DEQs) with general activation functions, extending the analysis beyond the ReLU activation function used in previous works. It introduces a new population Gram matrix and a dual activation with Hermite polynomial expansion to handle the non-linearity of the activation functions. The paper derives conditions for the initialization and learning rate that ensure linear convergence to a global minimum, supported by numerical experiments on MNIST and CIFAR-10 datasets. Theoretical contributions include a new formulation of dual activation and a lower bound on the least eigenvalue of the Gram matrix, which is crucial for proving convergence.

**Strengths:**
- The paper extends the analysis of over-parametrized DEQs with general activation functions, which is a significant advancement over previous works that primarily focused on ReLU activations.
- The introduction of a new formulation of dual activation and a novel population Gram matrix is a notable contribution, enhancing the understanding and applicability of DEQs.
- The paper is well-written, making it accessible and easy to follow, with clear explanations and examples that aid in understanding complex concepts.
- Theoretical results are supported by numerical experiments, which validate the theoretical findings and provide empirical evidence of the model's effectiveness.
- The paper addresses a timely and important topic, contributing to the ongoing research in deep learning and machine learning.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper primarily focuses on quadratic loss functions, which may limit its applicability to other types of loss functions commonly used in deep learning, such as cross-entropy.
- The assumptions and conditions for convergence are stringent, which might not be practical or applicable in real-world scenarios.
- The paper lacks a detailed discussion on the implications and limitations of the assumptions made, particularly the assumption of bounded second derivatives.
- The proofs and mathematical formulations are sometimes complex and difficult to follow, which could hinder comprehension for readers not familiar with the subject matter.
- The paper does not sufficiently discuss the novelty of the results compared to existing literature, particularly the work by Ling et al. (2022), which might lead to questions about the originality of the contributions.
- There is a lack of comparison with other models or methods, which could help in understanding the relative advantages and disadvantages of the DEQ model.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors clarify the assumptions and conditions for convergence, particularly how they relate to the practical applicability of the model?
- How does the paper address the stringent assumptions, such as the boundedness of second derivatives, in real-world scenarios?
- Can the authors provide more intuition or justification for the stringent assumptions and conditions, especially in comparison to similar models like the DNN with implicit weight-sharing?
- In terms of the proofs and mathematical formulations, could the authors provide additional clarification or examples to make the content more accessible?
- How does the paper compare to existing literature, particularly the work by Ling et al. (2022), and what are the main differences or advancements in the results presented?
- Could the authors elaborate on the choice of activation functions and their impact on the model's performance and convergence rates?
