 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel method for detecting machine-generated texts (MGTs) using a multi-population aware optimization method for Maximum Mean Discrepancy (MMD). The authors identify the high variance issue in MMD when applied to MGTs generated by different Large Language Models (LLMs), proposing a new method, MMD-MP, to address this. This method is evaluated against various LLMs and datasets, demonstrating superior performance in detecting MGTs compared to existing methods. The paper also includes theoretical analyses and empirical results, showing the effectiveness of MMD-MP in handling the variance problem and improving the stability of MMD for MGT detection.

**Strengths:**
- The paper addresses an important and timely issue of machine-generated text detection, which is crucial for the development of Large Language Models (LLMs).
- The proposed method, MMD-MP, is novel and well-explained, with a clear motivation and theoretical analysis that supports its effectiveness.
- The paper is well-written, making it easy to follow, and includes both theoretical and empirical results that validate the proposed method's effectiveness.
- The method is evaluated against various LLMs and datasets, demonstrating its robustness and generalizability.
- The authors provide a comprehensive evaluation that includes both synthetic and real-world data, which strengthens the credibility of the results.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper could benefit from a more detailed explanation of the high variance problem in MMD, especially in terms of its impact on the detection of MGTs.
- The method's effectiveness is limited to kernel-based MMD methods, which might not be as powerful as other methods like C2ST.
- The paper could be improved by including more recent and diverse baselines, particularly model-based methods that are not limited to kernel-based MMD.
- The paper could benefit from a more detailed discussion on the limitations of the proposed method, such as its applicability to different types of LLMs and the potential for overfitting due to the use of synthetic data.
- There is a need for a more comprehensive evaluation on different types of LLMs, including those that are not open-source, to ensure the method's generalizability.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors clarify the role of the parameter ε in the optimization process and its impact on the detection of MGTs?
- How does the proposed method perform when applied to different types of LLMs, especially those that are not open-source?
- Can the authors provide more details on the synthetic data used in the experiments, such as the specific LLMs used and the criteria for selecting the data?
- How does the proposed method compare to other model-based methods, particularly in terms of computational efficiency and scalability?
- Could the authors discuss the potential impact of the method on the development of LLMs, especially in terms of improving their transparency and trustworthiness?
