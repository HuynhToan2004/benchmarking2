 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel method for generating unlearnable examples by embedding high-level semantic features from natural images, aiming to enhance the robustness of deep learning models against various countermeasures such as data augmentations and preprocessing techniques. The proposed method employs a Deep Hiding (DH) scheme that integrates an Invertible Neural Network (INN) with a Latent Feature Concentration (LFC) module and a Semantic Images Generation (SIG) module. The LFC module regularizes the intra-class variance of perturbations, while the SIG module generates semantic images to maintain consistency within classes. The paper demonstrates the method's effectiveness through extensive experiments on datasets like CIFAR-10, CIFAR-100, and ImageNet-subset, showing that it can significantly reduce model performance. However, concerns were raised about the method's novelty, the clarity of its contributions, and the lack of comparative analysis with state-of-the-art methods.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, clear, and easy to follow, with comprehensive experimental results that demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed method across various datasets and models.
- The methodology is innovative, using high-level semantic features to generate unlearnable examples, which is a novel approach in the field.
- The paper includes extensive experiments that show the method's effectiveness in reducing model performance, and the results are convincing, with good visualizations and clear explanations of the method's components.
- The paper addresses an important and timely issue of data privacy and protection, which is crucial in the era of deep learning.

**Weaknesses:**
- The novelty of the paper is limited as it primarily combines existing techniques without significant innovation.
- The paper lacks a detailed discussion on the limitations and potential negative societal impacts of the proposed method.
- There is a lack of comparative analysis with state-of-the-art methods, which makes it difficult to assess the relative performance of the proposed method.
- The method's generalizability is questionable, as the experiments are limited to specific datasets and models, and there is no evidence of the method's effectiveness on more diverse or larger datasets.
- The paper does not provide sufficient details on the training process, such as the loss functions used and the specifics of the hyperparameter tuning, which could affect the reproducibility and understanding of the results.
- The paper's contributions are not clearly defined, and the roles of different modules (e.g., LFC and SIG) are not adequately explained, which could lead to confusion about the method's effectiveness and novelty.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors clarify the specific contributions of the proposed method beyond the combination of existing techniques?
- How does the proposed method compare to state-of-the-art methods in terms of robustness and generalizability?
- Can the authors provide more details on the training process, including the loss functions used and the hyperparameter tuning?
- How does the method perform on more diverse or larger datasets, such as the full ImageNet dataset?
- Could the authors elaborate on the method's potential negative societal impacts and discuss strategies for mitigating these risks?
- What are the specific roles and contributions of the LFC and SIG modules, and how do they impact the method's effectiveness?
