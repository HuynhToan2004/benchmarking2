 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a computational approach to visual ecology using a deep reinforcement learning framework, specifically focusing on a foraging task in VizDoom. The authors explore how different network architectures affect agent behavior and survival, comparing feedforward and recurrent models with varying complexity. They also investigate the impact of input satiety on agent behavior and the relationship between agent satiety and the estimated value function. The study reveals that recurrent models outperform feedforward models in more complex environments and that input satiety can improve agent behavior by reducing wasted nourishment. Despite the innovative approach, the paper is criticized for its lack of novelty, insufficient experimental validation, and unclear presentation.

**Strengths:**
- The paper presents a novel approach to modeling animal behavior using deep reinforcement learning, focusing on a foraging task in VizDoom.
- The authors conduct a thorough analysis of different network architectures and their impact on agent behavior and survival, providing insights into the effectiveness of different models in complex environments.
- The integration of input satiety as a signal to improve agent behavior is a significant contribution, showing that even simple signals can lead to improved performance.
- The paper is well-written, making it easy to follow, and the results are clearly presented, enhancing the paper's readability and comprehensibility.
- The experimental design is robust, with a comprehensive set of experiments conducted, and the results are well-supported by the data presented.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks novelty as the use of deep reinforcement learning for modeling animal behavior is not new, and the foraging task in VizDoom is a simplified version of similar tasks in other environments like Minecraft.
- The experimental validation is insufficient, with only three random seeds used for training, which may not adequately represent the variability in results.
- The paper suffers from poor presentation, with figures and descriptions not clearly explaining the results or the experimental setup, making it difficult to understand the contributions and conclusions of the study.
- There is a lack of discussion on the limitations of the study, which is crucial for understanding the scope and applicability of the findings.
- The paper does not adequately address the computational demands of the experiments, which are significant, and does not discuss the ethical implications of the research.

**Questions:**
- How does the paper address the ethical implications of the research, particularly in terms of animal welfare and the use of animal models in computational studies?
- Can the authors clarify the role and impact of the input satiety signal on agent behavior and survival, especially in comparison to other models without this feature?
- How does the paper ensure that the results are robust and generalizable, given the limited number of seeds used for training?
- Could the authors provide more detailed explanations of the figures and experimental results, especially in the abstract and introduction sections, to enhance the paper's clarity and accessibility?
- How does the paper address the limitations of the study, particularly in terms of the simplicity of the foraging task and the generalizability of the findings to more complex or varied environments?
