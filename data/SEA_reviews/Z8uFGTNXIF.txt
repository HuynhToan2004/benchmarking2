 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a new task named Referred Visual Search (RVS), where users can define their search interests by providing an image and additional conditioning information. The authors propose a method using a Vision Transformer (ViT) to learn conditionally embedded representations, which are then used for image retrieval. A new dataset, LAION-RVS-Fashion, is introduced, consisting of 272k fashion products and 842k images, designed to support this task. The paper compares its method with several baselines and demonstrates superior performance, although the novelty of the approach and the clarity of its contributions are questioned.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel task, Referred Visual Search (RVS), which is an interesting and potentially impactful application of visual similarity search.
- The authors provide a new dataset, LAION-RVS-Fashion, which is significant for the field and could be useful for further research.
- The proposed method is simple, effective, and well-explained, making it accessible and understandable.
- The paper is well-written, with clear explanations of the methodology and results, which are supported by extensive experiments.
- The introduction of a new dataset and the task of referred visual search could lead to new research directions and applications.

**Weaknesses:**
- The novelty of the method is limited as it primarily involves learning conditionally embedded representations using a ViT, which has been previously explored in similar contexts.
- The paper lacks a thorough discussion on the limitations of the proposed method, particularly in terms of its applicability to different types of images and the potential for overfitting due to the dataset's size.
- The comparison with existing methods, such as CIR, is not adequately discussed, which could lead to misunderstandings about the contributions of the proposed method.
- The paper does not adequately address the ethical implications of the work, particularly concerning the potential for bias in the dataset and the use of images from commercial fashion catalogs.
- There are several typographical and formatting errors throughout the paper, which detract from its professionalism and readability.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors clarify how the conditioning information is represented within the model, particularly in terms of its impact on the embedding space?
- How does the proposed method handle different types of conditioning information, such as different textual descriptions or images?
- Could the authors provide more details on the dataset construction process, including how the dataset is curated and how the synthetic labels are generated?
- How does the proposed method compare with state-of-the-art methods in terms of efficiency and scalability, especially when applied to larger datasets?
- What are the implications of the method's reliance on the dataset's size and diversity? How does the method perform when applied to datasets with different characteristics?
- Can the authors discuss the potential for overfitting and how they plan to address this in future work?
