 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel approach to reinforcement learning (RL) by integrating a Langevinized ensemble Kalman filter (LENKF) with the Kalman Temporal Difference (KTD) algorithm to handle uncertainty in RL environments. This integration is aimed at improving computational efficiency and uncertainty quantification in RL algorithms. The paper provides theoretical proofs and empirical evidence, including experiments in simple environments and classical control problems, to support the effectiveness of the proposed method. However, the paper is criticized for its limited novelty, lack of clarity in the experimental setup, and insufficient comparison with existing methods.

**Strengths:**
- The paper addresses an important and relevant topic in reinforcement learning (RL) by focusing on the handling of uncertainty in RL environments.
- The integration of LENKF with KTD is novel and provides a new approach to uncertainty quantification in RL.
- The paper is well-written, making it accessible and understandable, with clear theoretical explanations and empirical evidence.
- The experimental results, while limited to simple environments, demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed method in handling uncertainty and improving computational efficiency.
- The paper is the first to combine LENKF with KTD, which is a significant contribution to the field.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a clear definition and explanation of the key concept of "uncertainty" in the context of RL, which could lead to confusion about the actual contributions of the proposed method.
- The experiments are limited to simple environments, which may not adequately demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed method in more complex or realistic scenarios.
- The paper does not sufficiently compare the proposed method with existing methods such as EKF and SGLD, which could provide a better understanding of its advantages and disadvantages.
- The theoretical results, while interesting, are not particularly novel or surprising, and the proofs and assumptions used are not sufficiently explained or justified.
- The paper could benefit from a more detailed discussion on the computational efficiency of the proposed method, especially in relation to other state-of-the-art methods.
- There are several typographical and formatting errors that need correction to improve the readability and professionalism of the paper.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors clarify the definition and role of "uncertainty" in the context of RL, and how it is handled by the proposed method?
- How does the proposed method compare in terms of computational efficiency with existing methods, particularly in more complex or realistic environments?
- Could the authors provide more detailed experimental results, possibly including more complex environments or comparisons with other state-of-the-art methods?
- What are the specific advantages of using LENKF over other methods like SGLD or EKF, and how does it contribute to the overall effectiveness of the proposed method?
- How does the proposed method handle the stochastic nature of RL environments, and what are the implications of this for the practical application of the method?
