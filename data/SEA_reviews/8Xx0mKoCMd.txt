 **Summary:**
The paper introduces ExoViP, a novel method aimed at enhancing the performance of visual question answering (VQA) systems by integrating verification modules to correct errors during the planning and execution stages of VL programming. These verification modules are designed to refine predictions made by large language models (LLMs) and vision models, improving the overall accuracy and reliability of the VQA system. The methodology involves a three-step process: verification, exploration, and post-hoc self-correction, which is supported by experiments on various benchmarks, demonstrating the effectiveness of ExoViP in improving performance.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, clear, and easy to follow, making it accessible to a broad audience.
- The proposed method, ExoViP, is novel and effective, integrating verification modules to enhance the performance of VQA systems, particularly in correcting errors during the planning and execution stages.
- The experiments conducted demonstrate the effectiveness of ExoViP, showing improvements in performance on various benchmarks.
- The methodology is supported by a comprehensive analysis and ablation studies, which help in understanding the contributions of different components of the proposed method.
- The paper provides a clear motivation for the need to improve the accuracy of VL programming systems, which is essential for practical applications.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed discussion on the limitations of the proposed method, which could provide a more comprehensive understanding of its applicability and potential drawbacks.
- There is a need for more detailed comparisons with other VQA systems and baselines to fully demonstrate the superiority of ExoViP.
- The paper could benefit from a more thorough analysis of the computational resources required by ExoViP, especially in comparison to other methods.
- The paper does not adequately address the generalizability of ExoViP to different VL programming systems or datasets, which could limit its applicability.
- The paper could provide more detailed examples and visualizations to enhance the understanding of the method's operation and effectiveness.
- The paper does not include user studies or evaluations by human annotators, which could provide valuable insights into the practical utility and user-friendliness of the proposed system.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors provide a more detailed discussion on the limitations of ExoViP and how these might affect its practical application?
- How does ExoViP compare to other VQA systems and baselines in terms of performance and computational efficiency?
- Can the authors provide more detailed examples or visualizations to illustrate the operation and effectiveness of ExoViP in different scenarios?
- How does ExoViP perform when applied to different VL programming systems or datasets?
- Could the authors include user studies or evaluations by human annotators to assess the practical utility and user-friendliness of the proposed system?
