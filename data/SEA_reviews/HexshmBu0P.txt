 **Summary:**
The paper presents a novel watermarking method for diffusion models (DMs), focusing on both unconditional/class-conditional and text-to-image generation tasks. It introduces a watermark encoder and decoder to embed watermark information into the training data, which can be decoded from the generated images. The proposed method uses different strategies for watermarking based on the model's generation capabilities, such as embedding binary strings for unconditional generation and using text prompts for text-to-image generation. The paper provides a comprehensive evaluation of the method's effectiveness, robustness, and impact on model performance. However, concerns are raised about the novelty of the approach, as similar techniques have been previously explored in other generative models, and the practicality of the watermarking method given the stochastic nature of DMs.

**Strengths:**
- The paper addresses the important issue of watermarking diffusion models (DMs), which is a timely and relevant topic given the growing popularity and potential misuse of DMs.
- The proposed method is straightforward, intuitive, and well-motivated, with a clear and well-written presentation that effectively communicates the ideas and methodologies.
- The paper includes a comprehensive evaluation of the watermarking method, covering various aspects such as effectiveness, robustness, and impact on model performance.
- The authors have provided code and pre-trained models, enhancing the reproducibility and accessibility of their research.
- The paper addresses two different scenarios of watermarking, offering a flexible and adaptable solution that can be applied to different types of DMs.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed discussion on the novelty of the proposed method compared to existing works, particularly in the area of watermarking generative models.
- The practicality of the watermarking method is questioned, especially in the context of DMs which are known for their stochastic nature and the difficulty of accurately recovering watermarks from generated images.
- The paper's claims about the effectiveness and robustness of the watermarking method are not convincingly supported by the provided evidence, particularly in the context of unconditional/class-conditional generation.
- The paper does not adequately address the potential negative societal impacts of the proposed method, such as the possibility of misuse or the potential for copyright infringement.
- The methodology for embedding watermarks in training data and decoding from generated images is not sufficiently detailed, which could limit the reproducibility and understanding of the proposed method.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors provide a more detailed discussion on the novelty of their method compared to existing works, particularly in the context of watermarking generative models?
- How does the proposed method compare in terms of effectiveness and robustness to other watermarking methods for generative models, especially in the context of DMs?
- Given the stochastic nature of DMs, how accurate is the decoding process for the watermark information? What are the error rates associated with this process?
- How does the proposed method handle different types of watermarking, such as binary strings versus image-based watermarks? What are the advantages and disadvantages of each approach?
- Can the authors provide more detailed examples or case studies demonstrating the practical application and effectiveness of their method in real-world scenarios?
- How does the proposed method address potential negative societal impacts, such as misuse or copyright infringement?
