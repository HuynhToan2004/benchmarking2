 **Summary:**
The paper explores the stability of continuous attractors in recurrent neural networks (RNNs) using Fenichel's persistence theorem from dynamical systems theory. It distinguishes between bounded and unbounded attractors, demonstrating that bounded attractors are stable under perturbations, thus avoiding exploding gradients. The authors present a theoretical framework supported by numerical simulations, showing that bounded attractors maintain their stability even under parameter variations. This stability is crucial for applications in machine learning and neuroscience, where RNNs are used to model continuous-valued memory and neural computation.
