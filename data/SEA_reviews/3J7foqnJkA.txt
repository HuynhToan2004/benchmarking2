 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel approach to parameter saliency analysis using Extreme Value Theory (EVT) to rank convolutional filters in a CNN. It critiques the assumption of gradient norms following a normal distribution and proposes a new method, POT-saliency, to address this. The methodology is tested on various datasets, including MNIST and SVHN, and the results show that the POT-saliency method can outperform traditional methods in certain scenarios. However, concerns are raised about the clarity of the experimental setup, the fairness of comparisons, and the generalizability of the findings to larger, more complex datasets.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel approach to parameter saliency analysis using Extreme Value Theory (EVT), which is a significant departure from traditional methods.
- The authors have provided a clear and detailed explanation of the theoretical underpinnings of their method, making it accessible to readers.
- The experiments conducted demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed method, particularly in scenarios where domain shift occurs.
- The paper is well-written, with clear figures and a logical flow that aids in understanding the complex concepts presented.
- The methodology is innovative, providing a new perspective on parameter saliency that could potentially lead to more robust and interpretable models.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a comprehensive discussion on the practical implications and limitations of the proposed method, particularly in terms of computational efficiency and scalability.
- The experimental setup and comparisons are not clearly defined, making it difficult to assess the fairness and validity of the results.
- The paper does not adequately address the generalizability of the findings to larger, more complex datasets, such as those used in large-scale vision tasks.
- The paper's claims regarding the bias of existing methods and the superiority of the POT-saliency method are not convincingly supported by the evidence provided.
- The paper suffers from several typographical and formatting errors, which detract from its professional presentation.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors clarify the practical implications and limitations of their proposed method, particularly in terms of computational efficiency and scalability?
- How does the proposed method perform on larger, more complex datasets, such as those used in large-scale vision tasks?
- Could the authors provide more detailed comparisons and discussions on the performance of the POT-saliency method against other state-of-the-art methods?
- In light of the concerns regarding the experimental setup and comparisons, could the authors provide a more detailed explanation or justification for their experimental design and results?
- How does the proposed method compare in terms of computational complexity and resource requirements to other methods for parameter saliency analysis?
