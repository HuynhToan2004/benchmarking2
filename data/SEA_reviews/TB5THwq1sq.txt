 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel approach for solving partial differential equations (PDEs) using a physics-informed neural ODE (PINECONE). This method aims to address the challenges of traditional physics-informed neural networks (PINNs) by integrating neural ODEs with physics-informed constraints in the loss function. By leveraging the properties of neural ODEs, PINECONE is designed to handle irregularly sampled time-series data, which is beneficial for a wide range of PDE problems. The method is evaluated using two canonical PDEs, showing that it can achieve higher accuracy with fewer iterations compared to standard PINNs. However, the paper's scope is limited to simple PDEs, and the method's effectiveness on more complex, high-dimensional PDEs remains untested.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel method for solving PDEs using a neural ODE approach, which is a significant contribution to the field.
- The methodology is well-explained, with clear and concise writing, making the paper accessible to a broad audience.
- The paper demonstrates that PINECONE can achieve lower errors with fewer iterations compared to standard PINNs, which is a significant advantage.
- The method is straightforward to implement and can be integrated with other PINN strategies, making it a versatile tool for various applications.
- The paper provides a detailed description of the methodology and implementation, which enhances reproducibility and transparency.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper's scope is limited to simple PDEs, which may not fully demonstrate the method's capabilities on more complex, high-dimensional PDEs.
- The method's effectiveness is not thoroughly compared with other methods such as Hamiltonian neural ODEs, which could provide a more comprehensive evaluation.
- The paper lacks a broader range of experiments to validate the method's performance across different types of PDEs and real-world applications.
- There is a potential issue with the method's sensitivity to hyperparameters, which could affect the results and require careful tuning.
- The paper's presentation could be improved by including more detailed visualizations and a clearer explanation of the method's limitations and future directions.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors provide more details on how the method handles irregularly sampled time-series data and how this affects the solution's accuracy?
- How does the method compare in terms of computational efficiency and accuracy with other methods like Hamiltonian neural ODEs?
- Could the authors provide more examples or experiments to demonstrate the method's effectiveness on more complex, high-dimensional PDEs?
- How does the method handle the sensitivity to hyperparameters, and what strategies can be used to mitigate this issue?
- Could the authors clarify the method's limitations and potential future directions for improving its performance and applicability?
