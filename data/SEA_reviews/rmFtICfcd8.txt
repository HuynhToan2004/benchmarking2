 **Summary:**
The paper addresses the challenge of variational inference in Bayesian neural networks (BNNs) by introducing a regularized KL divergence to handle the infinite KL divergence problem in function-space variational inference. It proposes a method using generalized variational inference (GVI) with regularized KL divergence, which is shown to provide competitive uncertainty estimates and closely approximate true posterior distributions on synthetic and real-world datasets. The method is evaluated against several baselines, including weight-space and function-space priors, and shows promising results in terms of accuracy and mean rank. However, concerns were raised regarding the scalability of the method to larger datasets and the lack of comparisons with other methods such as those using weight-space priors.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, clearly structured, and easy to follow, with a good balance between theoretical contributions and practical applications.
- The proposed method addresses the critical issue of infinite KL divergence in function-space variational inference, which is a significant challenge in the field.
- The method is evaluated on both synthetic and real-world datasets, demonstrating its effectiveness in approximating true posterior distributions and providing competitive uncertainty estimates.
- The use of regularized KL divergence is innovative and well-explained, providing a clear motivation and theoretical justification for its application.
- The paper is well-positioned within the existing literature, providing a comprehensive review of related work and highlighting the novelty of the proposed approach.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks comparisons with other methods using weight-space priors, which could have provided a more comprehensive evaluation of the proposed method.
- The method's scalability to larger datasets and more complex models is not thoroughly addressed, raising concerns about its practical applicability in real-world scenarios.
- The experimental results are limited to small-scale datasets, which might not convincingly demonstrate the method's effectiveness on larger, more complex datasets.
- The paper does not sufficiently discuss the choice of hyperparameters, which is crucial for the performance of the method.
- The paper could benefit from more detailed discussions on the theoretical underpinnings of the method, particularly in terms of the regularized KL divergence and its implications for the inference process.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors provide more details on the scalability of the method to larger datasets and more complex models?
- How does the method perform when compared to other methods using weight-space priors?
- Could the authors clarify the choice of hyperparameters and their impact on the method's performance?
- In the context of the regularized KL divergence, could the authors provide a more detailed explanation of its theoretical implications and how it affects the inference process?
- How does the proposed method compare to other recent works that have also addressed the issue of infinite KL divergence in function-space variational inference?
