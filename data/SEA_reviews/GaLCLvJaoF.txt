 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel model-based reinforcement learning (MBRL) method, L1-MBRL, which integrates L1 adaptive control to enhance the robustness of MBRL algorithms against uncertainties. This approach involves approximating the learned transition function using an affine model, which is then perturbed by the L1 adaptive control to improve robustness. The paper provides a theoretical analysis of the estimation error and empirical evaluations on MuJoCo environments, demonstrating the effectiveness of the proposed method. Despite its innovative approach, the paper faces criticism for its limited novelty and the lack of significant improvement over existing methods like METRPO.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, easy to follow, and provides a clear explanation of the proposed method, making it accessible to a broad audience.
- The integration of L1 adaptive control with MBRL is a novel approach that enhances the robustness of the model against uncertainties.
- The theoretical analysis and empirical results demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed method in improving the robustness of MBRL algorithms.
- The paper includes a detailed explanation of the L1 adaptive control method, which is crucial for understanding the robustness of the model.
- The experimental results show that the proposed method can improve the performance of MBRL algorithms, especially in noisy environments.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a thorough discussion on the limitations of the proposed method, particularly in terms of its applicability to different types of models and its performance in complex environments.
- The novelty of the approach is questioned as similar methods have been previously explored, such as the use of adaptive control in model-based RL.
- The paper does not adequately address the computational efficiency of the L1 adaptive control method, which could be a significant concern in practical applications.
- The experimental results do not convincingly demonstrate significant improvements over existing methods like METRPO, which raises questions about the practical utility of the proposed method.
- The paper could benefit from a more detailed comparison with other robust RL methods and a broader range of environments to validate the effectiveness of the proposed method.

**Questions:**
- Could you provide a more detailed explanation of how the L1 adaptive control method is integrated into the MBRL framework, particularly in terms of the computational efficiency and the impact on the overall performance of the model?
- How does the proposed method perform in environments with more complex dynamics, and what are the potential challenges or limitations in such scenarios?
- Can you elaborate on the choice of the epsilon value in the switching law and how it affects the performance of the model?
- How does the proposed method compare with other robust RL methods, and what are the specific advantages or disadvantages of using L1 adaptive control over other robust control methods?
- Could you provide more details on the experimental setup, such as the specific model used for training and the conditions under which the experiments were conducted?
