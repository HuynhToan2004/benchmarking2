 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel method, Unpaired Neural Schrodinger Bridge (UNSB), for unpaired image-to-image translation tasks using a diffusion model. The method addresses the challenges posed by the curse of dimensionality by employing a multi-step generative process and advanced discriminators. The UNSB formulation enables the translation between arbitrary distributions, thereby generalizing previous methods. The paper provides theoretical insights into the self-similarity of Schrödinger bridges and empirical evidence of the method's effectiveness. However, the paper is criticized for its complex notation and lack of clarity in some sections, which could hinder understanding.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, clear, and easy to follow, with a clear motivation for addressing the curse of dimensionality in unpaired image-to-image translation tasks.
- The proposed method, Unpaired Neural Schrödinger Bridge (UNSB), is innovative and effective, demonstrated by its ability to translate images between arbitrary distributions and improve upon existing methods.
- The method's scalability and its ability to handle high-resolution images are highlighted, making it suitable for various applications.
- The paper provides a theoretical foundation for the method and includes detailed explanations and proofs, enhancing its credibility and reproducibility.
- The empirical results are convincing, showing that the method outperforms existing approaches in terms of FID and KID scores.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper contains some typographical errors and inconsistencies in notation, which could confuse readers and detract from the clarity of the presentation.
- The comparison with other methods is not always fair or comprehensive, and the paper lacks a detailed discussion on the limitations of the proposed method.
- The paper could benefit from more detailed explanations and examples of the notation used, especially in the early sections where the notation is introduced.
- The computational cost and training time of the method are not thoroughly discussed, which could be crucial for practitioners considering the implementation of the method.
- The paper does not adequately address how the method handles different types of images or domains, which could affect the generalizability of the results.
- The paper's claims about the curse of dimensionality and its solutions are not convincingly supported by empirical evidence, especially in high-dimensional cases.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors clarify the notation used in the paper, particularly in the early sections where it is first introduced?
- How does the proposed method compare to other diffusion models, such as those using VP-SDE or VE-SDE, in terms of computational efficiency and performance?
- Can the authors provide more details on the computational cost and training time of the UNSB method, especially in comparison to other state-of-the-art methods?
- How does the method perform on other types of images or domains, and what are the limitations of the method in terms of its generalizability?
- Could the authors elaborate on the method's ability to handle different types of image translations, such as style transfer or domain adaptation, and discuss any potential challenges or limitations in these scenarios?
- Can the authors provide more examples or case studies to support the method's effectiveness in high-dimensional cases, especially in scenarios where the curse of dimensionality is expected to be more pronounced?
