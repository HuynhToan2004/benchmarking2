 **Summary:**
The paper explores the theoretical foundations of offline reinforcement learning (RL) with general function approximation, focusing on the use of completeness-type and realizability-type assumptions. It introduces a novel lower bound for offline RL, demonstrating the necessity of these assumptions for achieving optimal policy learning. The paper also discusses the implications of these assumptions on the learnability of policies, providing a comprehensive theoretical framework for understanding the role of function approximation in offline RL. The authors classify assumptions into two types and analyze their practical usage, establishing lower bounds for model-realizability and extending these to other function classes. The paper is well-written, clear, and contributes to the understanding of the limitations of general function approximation in offline RL.
