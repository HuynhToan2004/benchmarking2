 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel method called RLCF, which integrates reinforcement learning with feedback from a compiler and a discriminator LLM to coarsely tune pre-trained LLMs for code generation. The approach leverages static analysis and a discriminator LLM to ensure the generated code's syntax and semantic correctness, aiming to improve the performance of LLMs in code generation tasks. The method is evaluated on the MBJP and MathQA datasets for Java, demonstrating improvements in the ability of LLMs to generate code free of errors. However, the paper lacks detailed discussion on the limitations and broader impacts of the proposed method, and the experimental setup could benefit from a more comprehensive comparison with existing methods.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written and easy to follow, with a clear and concise presentation of the proposed method.
- The idea of using a compiler as a grounding function and a discriminator LLM to balance the objectives of generating "natural" code and passing correctness checks is innovative and effective.
- The experimental results demonstrate that the proposed method can improve the performance of LLMs in code generation tasks, particularly in Java, by training them to generate code that is both syntactically and semantically correct.
- The paper provides a detailed algorithm for the proposed method, which could be beneficial for replication and further research.
- The use of a discriminator LLM to balance the objectives of generating "natural" code and passing correctness checks is a novel approach that could potentially be applied to other domains.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed discussion on the limitations and broader impacts of the proposed method, which could include the potential for overfitting to specific domains or the impact on the interpretability of generated code.
- The experimental setup could be improved by including more comprehensive comparisons with existing methods, such as CodeRL, and by evaluating the method on a broader range of programming languages and datasets.
- The paper does not provide sufficient details on the implementation and training of the discriminator LLM, which is a critical component of the proposed method.
- The paper could benefit from a more detailed analysis of the types of errors that are corrected by the proposed method and how these errors affect the performance of the LLMs.
- The paper does not sufficiently discuss the potential negative societal impacts of the proposed method, such as the generation of malicious code or the misuse of the method for unethical purposes.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors provide more details on the implementation and training of the discriminator LLM, including the specifics of the training data and the model architecture?
- How does the proposed method handle the trade-off between generating "natural" code and passing correctness checks? Is there a way to adjust the weighting of these objectives during training?
- Have the authors considered evaluating the proposed method on other programming languages or datasets to assess its generalizability?
- How does the proposed method compare to other existing methods, such as CodeRL, in terms of performance and efficiency?
- Can the authors provide more details on the types of errors that are corrected by the proposed method and how these errors affect the performance of the LLMs?
- How does the proposed method handle the potential for overfitting to specific domains, and what steps have been taken to mitigate this risk?
- Could the authors discuss the potential negative societal impacts of the proposed method and how these risks might be mitigated?
