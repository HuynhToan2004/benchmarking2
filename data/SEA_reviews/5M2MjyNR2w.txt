 **Summary:**
The paper introduces Adaptive Expansion (AdE), a novel method for converting hypergraphs into weighted graphs, which is designed to address the limitations of traditional hypergraph expansion methods. AdE employs a Global Simulation Network to select representative nodes and a distance-aware kernel function to adjust edge weights based on node similarity. The method aims to preserve high-order information within hypergraphs and enhance the performance of hypergraph neural networks (HyGNNs) by expanding hypergraphs adaptively. The paper presents theoretical justifications and empirical evaluations on several benchmark datasets, demonstrating the method's effectiveness and rationality. However, concerns were raised about the clarity of the paper, the novelty of the method, and the lack of comprehensive experimental validation.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel method, Adaptive Expansion (AdE), which is a significant advancement in the field of hypergraph neural networks (HyGNNs).
- The methodology is well-articulated, with clear explanations of the challenges in hypergraph expansion and the proposed solutions.
- The paper includes comprehensive theoretical justifications and empirical evaluations, demonstrating the effectiveness of AdE compared to classic expansion methods.
- The proposed method is innovative, as it learns to find the optimal graph structures adaptively during hypergraph expansion, which is a novel approach.
- The paper is well-written, with clear figures and a clear explanation of the proposed method.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a clear differentiation from existing methods, particularly in terms of the novelty and contribution of the proposed method.
- The theoretical justifications and empirical evaluations provided do not convincingly demonstrate the superiority or novelty of the AdE method over existing methods.
- The paper suffers from poor writing, with grammatical errors, incorrect terminology, and unclear explanations in some sections.
- The experimental validation is not comprehensive enough, with limited datasets and a lack of comparisons with more recent and relevant methods.
- The paper does not adequately address the limitations of the proposed method, such as the computational complexity and the scalability of the method to larger hypergraphs.

**Questions:**
- Could you clarify the main differences between the proposed method and existing methods like HyperGCN, particularly in terms of the selection of representative nodes and the adjustment of edge weights?
- How does the proposed method compare to other recent and relevant methods, such as UniGNN and AllSet, in terms of performance and computational efficiency?
- Can you provide more detailed explanations or examples of how the Global Simulation Network operates, particularly in the context of hypergraph expansion?
- How does the paper address the issue of information loss in hypergraph expansion, and what steps are taken to minimize this loss?
- Could you elaborate on the computational complexity of the proposed method and discuss any strategies for scaling the method to larger hypergraphs?
