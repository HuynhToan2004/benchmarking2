 **Summary:**
The paper explores the fundamental limits of knowledge transfer from a teacher to a student model, focusing on three progressive levels of privileged information: hard labels, partial soft labels, and soft labels. The authors provide theoretical bounds on the convergence rates of the student model to the teacher model under various loss functions and data acquisition protocols. They also discuss the implications of these findings for the practical application of knowledge distillation in machine learning. The paper is well-structured, with clear theoretical contributions and empirical validations, although it lacks a comprehensive discussion on the practical applicability and the generalizability of the results.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, with clear and concise explanations of the theoretical results and empirical validations.
- The results are original and interesting, providing a novel perspective on the convergence rates of student models under different data acquisition protocols and loss functions.
- The paper is structured effectively, making it easy to follow, and the authors have provided rigorous proofs and clear definitions of the theoretical results.
- The paper discusses the limitations of the results and the practical implications of the findings, which are relevant to the field of knowledge distillation.
- The paper is well-positioned within the literature, with a clear discussion of the differences from previous works and the relevance of the results to current research in machine learning.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a discussion on the practical implications of the theoretical results, particularly in terms of how the findings can be applied in real-world scenarios.
- The paper could benefit from more detailed examples and a clearer explanation of the experimental setups, especially in the context of knowledge distillation.
- The paper assumes a simplified setup where the teacher model is assumed to be the true data-generating distribution, which may not reflect practical scenarios where the teacher model is trained on a different dataset.
- There is a lack of discussion on the generalizability of the results to more complex scenarios, such as those involving continuous input and label spaces.
- The paper could benefit from a more detailed discussion on the practical relevance of the results, especially in terms of how the findings can be applied to improve knowledge distillation techniques in practice.
