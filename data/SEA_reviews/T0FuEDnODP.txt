 **Summary:**
The paper introduces Cooperative Graph Neural Networks (Co-GNNs), a novel framework where nodes are treated as players choosing to listen, broadcast, listen and broadcast, or isolate. This approach allows for dynamic and flexible message passing, enhancing the expressivity of GNNs beyond the 1-WL test. Co-GNNs are evaluated on both synthetic and real-world datasets, demonstrating improvements in performance and expressivity. The paper also discusses the theoretical aspects of Co-GNNs, including their ability to handle long-range dependencies and their expressive power.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel framework for training graph neural networks (GNNs) where nodes are treated as players, which is a unique and innovative approach.
- The authors provide a detailed discussion on the properties of Co-GNNs, demonstrating that they are more expressive than the 1-dimensional Weisfeiler-Leman algorithm and better suited for long-range tasks.
- The paper is well-written, with clear explanations and motivations, making it easy to follow.
- The experimental results show that Co-GNNs achieve state-of-the-art results on both synthetic and real-world datasets, indicating their effectiveness.
- The paper provides a theoretical analysis of the new message-passing scheme, which is supported by an extensive empirical analysis on both synthetic and real-world datasets.
- The proposed method is flexible and can be applied to any existing GNNs, which is a significant advantage.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a comprehensive discussion on the limitations of the proposed method, which is crucial for understanding its applicability and scope.
