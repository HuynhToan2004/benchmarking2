 **Summary:**
The paper introduces FusPlace, a hybrid action space reinforcement learning (RL) framework designed for chip placement in Electronic Design Automation (EDA). FusPlace integrates a feature fusion representation to address the limitations of existing methods, particularly focusing on the local and global information of the chip. The framework uses a Parameterized Deep Q-Network (P-DQN) to manage both discrete and continuous action spaces, aiming to improve precision and accuracy in chip placement. The methodology includes the extraction of local and global information from the chip, and the use of a parameterized action space to manage these actions. The paper claims to enhance precision and accuracy, but its technical novelty and the clarity of its presentation are questioned.

**Strengths:**
- The paper addresses the chip placement problem in Electronic Design Automation (EDA), which is a significant and complex issue in the field.
- The proposed method integrates local and global information, which is crucial for effective chip placement.
- The use of a hybrid action space in the Parameterized Deep Q-Network (P-DQN) is innovative and addresses the limitations of existing methods.
- The paper is well-structured, with clear explanations of the methodology and its components, including feature fusion and the use of a parameterized action space.
- The authors have provided a clear and detailed description of the feature fusion representation and the hybrid action space, which is beneficial for understanding the proposed method.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a comprehensive comparison with existing methods, particularly in terms of performance metrics and computational efficiency.
- The technical novelty of the proposed method is questioned, as it appears to be a combination of existing techniques without significant advancement.
- The clarity of the paper needs improvement, particularly in the explanation of the feature fusion representation and the hybrid action space.
- The paper does not adequately address the limitations of the proposed method, which is crucial for understanding its applicability and effectiveness.
- There is a lack of detailed experimental results and comparisons with state-of-the-art methods, which makes it difficult to assess the effectiveness and efficiency of the proposed method.
- The paper does not provide sufficient details on the implementation and training of the proposed method, which is necessary for reproducibility and further research.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors provide more detailed comparisons with existing methods, including performance metrics and computational efficiency?
- How does the proposed method perform under different conditions, such as varying chip sizes and complexities?
- Can the authors clarify the technical novelty of their method and how it advances beyond existing techniques?
- How is the feature fusion representation implemented, and what specific features are considered important?
- Can the authors provide more detailed experimental results and comparisons with state-of-the-art methods to better assess the effectiveness and efficiency of their proposed method?
- How does the proposed method handle different types of chips, and what are the implications for its applicability across various chip designs?
