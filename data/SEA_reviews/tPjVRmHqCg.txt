 **Summary:**
The paper presents a novel approach to protein sequence design using reinforcement learning (RL) in a latent space, specifically leveraging the ESM-2 model to map protein sequences to a continuous representation. This approach aims to improve the efficiency and effectiveness of protein sequence design by utilizing a general value function (GVF) to encourage exploration and a curiosity mechanism to enhance search efficiency. The method was evaluated against various baselines and shown to outperform in terms of performance and novelty. However, concerns were raised regarding the clarity of the methodology, the choice of baselines, and the scalability of the approach to larger datasets.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, with a clear and structured presentation that effectively communicates the proposed methodology and its contributions to the field.
- The use of reinforcement learning (RL) in a latent space for protein sequence design is a novel and promising approach, leveraging the ESM-2 model to map protein sequences to a continuous representation, which could potentially improve the efficiency and effectiveness of protein sequence design.
- The methodology includes the use of a general value function (GVF) to encourage exploration and a curiosity mechanism to enhance search efficiency, which could be particularly beneficial in scenarios where the search space is vast and sparsely populated.
- The paper provides a comprehensive evaluation of the proposed method against various baselines, demonstrating significant performance improvements over existing methods in terms of performance and novelty.
- The authors have conducted multiple ablation studies to validate the rationality of their design, which adds credibility to the findings and methodology.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks clarity in explaining the specifics of the methodology, particularly the details of how the GVF is trained and how the policy is trained using the intrinsic reward.
- The choice of baselines appears somewhat limited, with potentially stronger or more relevant methods not included in the comparisons.
- There is a lack of discussion on the scalability of the approach to larger datasets and the potential computational challenges that might arise.
- The paper does not adequately address the potential limitations or societal impacts of the proposed method, which could be critical for understanding the broader implications of the work.
- The paper's presentation could benefit from better organization and clarity, particularly in the introduction and related works sections, which currently contain redundant or irrelevant information.

**Questions:**
- Could you clarify how the GVF is trained and how the policy is trained using the intrinsic reward? What specific role does the ESM-2 model play in this process?
- How does the method handle the scalability issues, particularly in terms of computational efficiency and memory requirements when applied to larger datasets?
- In the context of the ablation studies, could you provide more detailed results on how the method performs when different components of the GVF are removed?
- How does the method ensure that the generated sequences are not overly similar to existing sequences, which could limit the diversity of the results?
- Could you discuss the potential societal impacts of the proposed method, particularly in terms of ethical considerations and broader implications for the field of protein engineering?
