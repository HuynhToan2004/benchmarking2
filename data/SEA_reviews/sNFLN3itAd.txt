 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel method for link prediction using graph neural networks (GNNs), specifically focusing on the MPNN-then-SF architecture. This approach leverages structural features (SF) to guide MPNN's representation pooling, aiming to enhance expressiveness and scalability. The paper also addresses the issue of graph incompleteness, proposing a method to complete the common neighbor structure, which is crucial for link prediction. The NCN and NCNC models are introduced, demonstrating superior performance over existing models in link prediction benchmarks. The paper also includes a comprehensive ablation study and visualization of results to support the findings.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, easy to follow, and provides a comprehensive literature review, effectively summarizing existing models in a tabular format.
- The proposed method is simple, effective, and well-motivated, with a novel architecture that combines MPNN-then-SF for link prediction, addressing the issue of graph incompleteness.
- The paper includes a thorough analysis of the impact of graph incompleteness and proposes a novel method to complete the common neighbor structure, which is crucial for link prediction.
- The experiments are comprehensive and convincing, demonstrating the effectiveness of the proposed method on several datasets, including a detailed ablation study.
- The paper provides code in the supplementary material, enhancing reproducibility.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed explanation of why the MPNN-then-SF architecture is more expressive than other architectures, particularly why it is more expressive than the SF-and-MPNN architecture.
- The paper does not include experiments on large-scale datasets or real-world applications, which could validate the scalability and practicality of the proposed method.
- There is a lack of discussion on the limitations of the proposed method, which is crucial for understanding the scope and applicability of the findings.
- The paper does not include a detailed analysis of the computational complexity or scalability of the proposed method, which is important for understanding its practicality in large-scale applications.
- The paper could benefit from a more detailed explanation of the NCNC method and its components, particularly the "soft completion of common neighbors" and the "reapplication of NCN on the completed graph."

**Questions:**
- Can the authors provide a detailed explanation of why the MPNN-then-SF architecture is more expressive than other architectures, particularly the SF-and-MPNN architecture?
- How does the proposed method perform on large-scale datasets or real-world applications?
- Can the authors provide a detailed analysis of the computational complexity or scalability of the proposed method?
- How does the proposed method handle the issue of graph incompleteness in real-world scenarios, especially when dealing with noisy or incomplete data?
- Can the authors provide more details on the NCNC method, particularly the "soft completion of common neighbors" and the "reapplication of NCN on the completed graph"?
- How does the proposed method compare to other state-of-the-art methods in terms of computational efficiency and memory requirements?
- Can the authors provide a more detailed explanation of the "soft completion of common neighbors" and the "reapplication of NCN on the completed graph"?
