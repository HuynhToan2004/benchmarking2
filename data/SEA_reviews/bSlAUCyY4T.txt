 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel regularization method for knowledge graph completion (KGC) based on tensor decomposition. It proposes a new regularization term, Intermediate Variables Regularization (IVR), which minimizes the norms of intermediate variables involved in the computation of the predicted tensor. The authors provide theoretical proofs and empirical evidence to support the efficacy of their regularization method, demonstrating its ability to reduce overfitting in TDB models. The method is applied to various models including CP, ComplEx, SimplE, Analogy, QuatE, and TuckER, showing improvements in performance. Despite the theoretical and empirical contributions, the paper has been critiqued for its clarity in presenting the regularization method and its theoretical foundations, as well as the lack of a comprehensive comparison with existing methods.

**Strengths:**
- The paper provides a detailed overview of existing tensor decomposition-based (TDB) models and derives a general form for them, which is beneficial for further exploration of TDB models.
- The proposed regularization method is simple, effective, and can be applied to most TDB models, offering a novel approach to address overfitting issues in KGC.
- Theoretical analysis is provided to support the regularization, and empirical analysis demonstrates the effectiveness of the regularization and validates the theoretical analysis.
- The method is computationally tractable and has the potential to be applied to other fields such as tensor completion.

**Weaknesses:**
- The presentation of the regularization method and its theoretical foundations is unclear, making it difficult to understand the specifics of how the regularization term is applied and its impact on model performance.
- The paper lacks a comprehensive comparison with existing regularization methods, particularly in terms of computational complexity and parameter count.
- The empirical results show only marginal improvements over existing methods, and the significance of these improvements is not clearly demonstrated.
- The paper does not sufficiently discuss the limitations of the proposed method, nor does it provide a detailed discussion on the computational complexity or the choice of hyperparameters.
- There is a lack of discussion on the scalability of the proposed method, especially in large-scale knowledge graph completion scenarios.
- The paper does not sufficiently address the computational complexity or the choice of hyperparameters, which could affect the reproducibility and applicability of the findings.

**Questions:**
- Could you clarify how the regularization term is applied in practice and what specific impact it has on model performance?
- How does the proposed method compare in terms of computational complexity and parameter count with existing regularization methods?
- Can you provide more detailed results on the scalability of the proposed method in large-scale knowledge graph completion scenarios?
- How do you choose the hyperparameters for the proposed method, and what are the implications of these choices on model performance?
- Could you elaborate on the limitations of the proposed method and discuss potential areas for future research?
