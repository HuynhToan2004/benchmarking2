 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel approach to curriculum learning in graph neural networks (GNNs) to address the challenges of noisy labels in graph data. The method leverages a new measure called Class-conditional Betweenness Centrality (CBC) to identify informative nodes and construct a curriculum that progressively introduces hard samples. This approach is designed to mitigate the over-smoothing problem in GNNs and is supported by theoretical analysis and empirical evaluations. The paper also discusses the potential of the CBC measure in improving the selection of informative nodes and the overall performance of GNNs in noisy environments.

**Strengths:**
- The paper addresses an important and timely problem of handling noisy labels in graph data, which is crucial for real-world applications.
- The proposed CBC measure is innovative and well-motivated, providing a novel approach to identifying informative nodes in graph data.
- The paper is well-written, clear, and easy to follow, with a clear presentation of the methodology and results.
- The experimental results demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed method in various settings, showing improvements over baseline methods.
- The theoretical analysis provides a solid foundation for the proposed method, enhancing the understanding of the proposed approach.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a comprehensive discussion on related works, particularly in the area of curriculum learning and graph-based methods for noisy labels.
- There is a need for more detailed explanations and justifications for the CBC measure and the assumptions made in the theoretical analysis.
- The experimental setup and results could be improved by including more datasets and baselines, particularly those that address similar problems in graph-based curriculum learning.
- The paper could benefit from a more thorough discussion on the limitations of the proposed method and potential negative societal impacts.
- The presentation of some figures and tables could be clearer and more informative, with better labeling and clarity in the data visualization.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors provide a more detailed discussion on how the CBC measure compares to traditional graph-based measures like betweenness centrality and how it addresses the challenges of noisy labels?
- How does the proposed method perform on larger datasets with a higher density of nodes and edges, and how does it compare to other methods in terms of scalability and computational efficiency?
- Can the authors clarify the assumptions made in the theoretical analysis and discuss how these assumptions affect the applicability and generalizability of the results?
- How does the proposed method handle different types of noisy labels, such as label shift and label skew, and what are the implications for the performance and generalizability of the model?
- Could the authors provide more details on the experimental setup, including the selection criteria for the datasets and the specifics of the baseline methods used?
