 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel algorithm for solving nonconvex-nonconcave min-max problems by extending the extragradient method to include multiple exploration steps. This algorithm aims to relax the weak Minty condition, which is a significant improvement over existing methods that typically require a smaller parameter range for convergence. The authors demonstrate that their approach can handle more general cases and provide a convergence analysis, although the experimental validation is limited. The paper's contributions include a new algorithm that exploits more local information and an adaptive exploration method to tackle cyclic behaviors effectively. However, the paper lacks detailed comparisons with existing methods and comprehensive numerical experiments.
