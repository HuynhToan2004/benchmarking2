 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel approach for simulating turbulent fluid dynamics using autoregressive conditional diffusion models. This method aims to improve the stability and accuracy of predictions over longer time horizons compared to traditional learned PDE solvers. The model is trained on simulated turbulence data and evaluated against various baselines, demonstrating its potential for generating physically plausible and statistically consistent predictions. The paper is well-written, clear, and includes detailed experimental setups and results, which support the claims of improved stability and accuracy. However, concerns were raised regarding the novelty of the approach, the clarity of the methodology, and the depth of the experimental validation.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel approach to simulating turbulent flows using autoregressive conditional diffusion models, which is a promising direction for improving the stability and accuracy of predictions over longer time horizons.
- The method is well-motivated, with a clear explanation of the benefits of the diffusion model in generating physically plausible and statistically consistent predictions.
- The paper is well-written, clear, and easy to follow, with a comprehensive experimental setup and detailed results that support the claims of improved stability and accuracy.
- The application of diffusion models to fluid dynamics is a novel and interesting approach, which could potentially lead to significant advancements in the field.
- The paper provides a detailed analysis of the performance of the proposed method, comparing it to various baselines and demonstrating its superiority in terms of stability and accuracy.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a clear and detailed explanation of the methodology, making it difficult to understand the specifics of how the diffusion model is applied to the fluid dynamics problem.
- There is a lack of novelty in the approach, as similar methods have been previously explored, and the paper does not adequately distinguish its contributions from existing works.
- The experimental validation is limited, and the paper does not sufficiently compare its method to state-of-the-art methods or provide a comprehensive analysis of the limitations and failure cases of the proposed approach.
- The paper does not discuss the computational cost of the proposed method, which could be a significant concern for practical applications.
- The paper could benefit from a more detailed discussion on the limitations of the proposed approach and how these might affect its applicability in real-world scenarios.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors provide a more detailed explanation of the methodology, particularly how the diffusion model is applied to the fluid dynamics problem?
- How does the proposed method compare to state-of-the-art methods in terms of accuracy, stability, and computational cost?
- What are the specific advantages of using the diffusion model over other methods for simulating turbulent flows?
- How does the method handle the computational cost, especially in longer simulations?
- Can the authors provide more detailed comparisons with other diffusion-based models and discuss how the methodology differs from these existing models?
- How does the method handle the physical constraints of the fluid dynamics problem, such as the conservation of mass and momentum?
- What are the limitations of the proposed approach, and how do these affect its applicability in real-world scenarios?
