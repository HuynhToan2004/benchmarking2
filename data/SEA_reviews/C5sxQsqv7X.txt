 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel method for private data selection using Transformer models, focusing on the efficient evaluation of nonlinear operations in Transformer models through Multi-Layer Perceptrons (MLPs) in a Multi-Party Computation (MPC) setting. The approach aims to reduce communication rounds and computational costs by approximating nonlinearities with MLPs, thereby enhancing the efficiency of data selection. The methodology is supported by a comprehensive evaluation across various datasets and models, demonstrating its practicality and effectiveness. However, the paper faces criticism for its limited scope, focusing only on Transformer models and not exploring broader applications or alternative methods.

**Strengths:**
- The paper addresses a significant problem of private data selection in the context of Transformer models, which is of high relevance and practical importance.
- The proposed solution is novel and technically sound, providing a new approach to private data selection that could be beneficial for both model owners and data owners.
- The paper is well-written, clear, and easy to follow, with a detailed explanation of the methodology and experimental setup.
- The proposed solution is supported by thorough experiments that demonstrate its effectiveness and efficiency compared to existing methods.
- The paper introduces innovative techniques such as multi-phase selection and parallel MPC executions, which could be valuable for other applications beyond the current focus.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a broader exploration of alternative methods for private data selection, limiting the scope of its impact and applicability.
- The paper focuses solely on Transformer models, which may not be the most common or relevant models for data selection, and does not consider other types of models such as CNNs or RNNs.
- The experimental setup and results are not convincingly presented, with some results (e.g., accuracy of the proposed method compared to oracle results) being inconsistent or not well-explained.
- The paper does not adequately address the privacy concerns related to the use of MPC and the potential leakage of information, which could be a significant issue in real-world applications.
- The paper could benefit from a more comprehensive discussion on the limitations and potential negative societal impacts of the proposed solution.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors elaborate on the choice of Transformer models for data selection and discuss the potential advantages or disadvantages compared to other types of models?
- How does the proposed method compare in terms of accuracy and efficiency when applied to different types of models or datasets beyond those used in the experiments?
- Can the authors provide a more detailed explanation of the experimental setup, particularly the results that show significant discrepancies between the proposed method and the oracle results?
- How does the proposed method ensure privacy in the MPC setting, and what measures are taken to prevent information leakage?
- Could the authors discuss the potential negative societal impacts of their proposed solution and how these might be mitigated or addressed?
