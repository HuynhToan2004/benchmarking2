 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel distortion measure for images, termed Wasserstein distortion, which aims to unify the concepts of pixel-level fidelity and perceptual realism. The measure is based on the Wasserstein distance between distributions of local features extracted from images, with a weighting function that varies from strict to loose locality, thereby interpolating between fidelity and realism. The paper also explores the theoretical properties of this measure, such as its metric properties under certain conditions, and demonstrates its application through various experiments, including texture synthesis and image reproduction. However, the paper has been criticized for its limited novelty, lack of comparison with existing methods, and insufficient experimental validation, particularly in terms of its applicability and effectiveness in real-world scenarios.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel approach to unifying the concepts of pixel-level fidelity and perceptual realism through the use of Wasserstein distortion, which is a significant advancement in the field.
- The theoretical underpinnings of the proposed method are well-articulated, providing a solid foundation for the proposed approach.
- The paper is well-written, clear, and easy to follow, making it accessible to a broad audience.
- The experiments conducted demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed method, particularly in texture synthesis and image reproduction, showing that it can interpolate between fidelity and realism.
- The use of a weighting function that varies from strict to loose locality is an innovative approach that allows for the interpolation between fidelity and realism, which is a significant contribution to the field.

**Weaknesses:**
- The novelty of the proposed method is limited as it builds upon existing works such as Heitz et al. (2021), which also use similar approaches to texture synthesis.
- The paper lacks a comprehensive comparison with existing methods, particularly in terms of computational efficiency and practical applications.
- The theoretical analysis is somewhat limited, particularly in terms of the practical implications of the conditions required for the metric properties of the distortion measure.
- The experimental results are not convincingly superior to existing methods, and the paper lacks a detailed discussion on the limitations of the proposed approach.
- The paper could benefit from a more thorough discussion on the practical implications of the assumptions made in the theoretical analysis, particularly the requirement for the pooling PMF to have no spectral nulls.
- The paper could be improved by including more diverse examples and comparisons, especially in terms of the robustness of the proposed method across different types of images and conditions.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors clarify the distinction between fidelity and realism as used in the paper, and provide a more detailed explanation of how the proposed method interpolates between these two concepts?
- How does the proposed method perform in scenarios where the reference image is not a texture, and what are the implications of this for the interpolation between fidelity and realism?
- Can the authors provide a more detailed comparison with existing methods, particularly in terms of computational efficiency and practical applications?
- How does the proposed method handle the issue of positivity, particularly in scenarios where the pooling PMF has spectral nulls?
- Could the authors elaborate on the practical implications of the assumptions made in the theoretical analysis, particularly the requirement for the pooling PMF to have no spectral nulls?
- How does the proposed method perform in terms of preserving fine details and structures in images, especially in high-resolution images?
- Can the authors provide more examples and comparisons to demonstrate the robustness and effectiveness of the proposed method across different types of images and conditions?
