 **Summary:**
The paper introduces UNREST, a novel method for offline reinforcement learning in stochastic driving environments. UNREST segments sequences into certain and uncertain parts based on estimated uncertainties, conditioning on truncated returns in certain parts and ignoring stochastic returns in uncertain parts. This approach allows for more effective learning from true outcomes of agent actions rather than environment transitions. The method is evaluated extensively in the CARLA simulator, demonstrating its effectiveness in various driving scenarios. The paper also includes an ablation study and visualization of uncertainty estimation results, which support the method's performance.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel method for offline RL in stochastic driving environments, which is a significant contribution to the field.
- The method is well-motivated, with a clear rationale for its design and a comprehensive evaluation across multiple metrics.
- The experiments are extensive, covering a variety of metrics and scenarios, which demonstrates the robustness and effectiveness of the proposed method.
- The paper is well-written, making it accessible and easy to understand, which is crucial for a broad audience.
- The method's novelty lies in its segmentation strategy, which distinguishes it from existing approaches and offers a new perspective on handling stochasticity in RL environments.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed discussion on the computational complexity and efficiency of the proposed method, which is crucial for real-world applications.
- There is a lack of clarity on how the uncertainty is computed and the methodology behind the segmentation strategy, which could benefit from more detailed explanations or additional visual aids.
- The paper's reliance on the CARLA simulator raises concerns about the generalizability of the results to real-world scenarios, especially in terms of safety and practicality.
- The method's complexity and the need for additional models during inference could limit its practical applicability, especially in resource-constrained environments.
- The paper could benefit from a more detailed comparison with existing methods, particularly those that address similar issues in offline RL, to better position its contributions.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors provide more details on the computational complexity and efficiency of the proposed method, especially in comparison to existing methods?
- How does the method handle the segmentation strategy in more complex scenarios, such as those involving multiple uncertain events in quick succession?
- Could the authors elaborate on the methodology behind the uncertainty estimation and its impact on the overall performance of the model?
- How does the method compare to existing methods in terms of safety and practicality, especially when applied in real-world scenarios?
- What are the implications of the segmentation strategy on the overall performance of the model, and how does it affect the model's ability to adapt to different scenarios?
