 **Summary:**
The paper introduces Jumanji, a new open-source reinforcement learning (RL) environment suite designed to be fast, flexible, and scalable. Jumanji leverages JAX and hardware accelerators like GPUs and TPUs to enhance its performance and adaptability. It offers a variety of combinatorial problems and challenging decision-making tasks, including routing, packing, and logic games, which are crucial in industry settings. The paper provides actor-critic baselines and initial experiments, demonstrating the scalability and flexibility of the environments. Despite its strengths, the paper is criticized for its limited novelty, lack of comprehensive benchmarks, and inadequate discussion on the scalability and complexity of the environments.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a new open-source RL environment suite, Jumanji, which is designed to be fast, flexible, and scalable, addressing a significant need in the field of RL research.
- Jumanji is built on JAX, a modern, flexible, and fast numerical computation library, which allows for efficient and scalable simulations.
- The paper provides actor-critic baselines and initial experiments, demonstrating the scalability and flexibility of the Jumanji environments.
- Jumanji supports a variety of combinatorial problems and challenging decision-making tasks, which are crucial in industry settings.
- The paper is well-written and easy to follow, making it accessible to a broad audience.
- The authors have committed to releasing the code, which is a valuable contribution to the community.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a comprehensive discussion on the scalability and complexity of the Jumanji environments, particularly how they can be used to study the scalability of agents.
- There is no discussion on the novelty of Jumanji compared to existing RL environment suites.
- The paper does not provide a comprehensive benchmark of the performance of the Jumanji environments against other existing RL environments.
- The paper does not adequately address the limitations of the Jumanji environments, such as the lack of multi-agent support and the focus on discrete actions.
- The paper could benefit from a more detailed discussion on the hardware requirements and the scalability of the Jumanji environments across different hardware platforms.
- The paper does not provide sufficient details on the experimental setup, such as the specifics of the hardware used and the training parameters for the A2C algorithm.

**Questions:**
- How does the scalability of the Jumanji environments compare to other existing RL environments, and what are the specific benefits of using Jumanji for studying the scalability of agents?
- Can you provide more details on the hardware requirements for running Jumanji and how it scales across different hardware platforms?
- How does the Jumanji environment handle multi-agent scenarios, and what are the plans for supporting continuous actions in future versions?
- Could you clarify the experimental setup, particularly the hardware used and the training parameters for the A2C algorithm?
- How does Jumanji compare to other RL environments in terms of performance and efficiency, and what are the specific advantages of using Jumanji over other existing environments?
- What are the plans for extending Jumanji to include more complex and diverse environments, and how will this be achieved?
