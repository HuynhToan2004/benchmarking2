 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel approach to video diffusion models by decomposing video into content frames and motion latents, leveraging a pre-trained image diffusion model for content frame generation and training a new lightweight diffusion model for motion generation. This method aims to improve memory and computational efficiency while maintaining high-quality video generation. The model is evaluated on benchmarks such as UCF-101 and WebVid-10M, showing competitive performance and efficiency compared to existing methods. Despite its strengths in efficiency and performance, the paper is criticized for its lack of clarity in methodological details, especially the design choices of the autoencoder and the motion latent representation.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-organized, easy to follow, and presents a novel approach to video diffusion models that effectively decomposes videos into content frames and motion latents, improving computational efficiency.
- The proposed method achieves state-of-the-art (SOTA) performance on various benchmarks, demonstrating significant improvements in memory and computational efficiency compared to existing methods.
- The paper is well-written, with clear figures and detailed experimental results, including comprehensive ablation studies that support the effectiveness of the proposed method.
- The methodology is innovative, particularly in the use of pre-trained image diffusion models for content frame generation and the training of a lightweight diffusion model for motion generation.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks clarity in some methodological details, particularly the design choices of the autoencoder and the motion latent representation, which are crucial for understanding the effectiveness of the proposed method.
- The performance of the method on long videos is not thoroughly evaluated, which is a significant limitation given the potential applications in generating longer video content.
- The paper does not provide a detailed analysis of the limitations of the proposed method, which could hinder the understanding of its applicability and effectiveness in various scenarios.
- There are concerns about the fairness of the comparison due to the use of different pre-trained models and the absence of certain baseline comparisons, which could affect the perceived performance of the proposed method.
- The paper does not adequately address the societal impact of the proposed method, which could be significant given the potential for misuse or unintended consequences.

**Questions:**
- Could you provide more details on the design choices of the autoencoder, particularly how it encodes video frames and the rationale behind the motion latent representation?
- How does the proposed method perform when generating longer videos, and what are the potential limitations or challenges in this scenario?
- Can you clarify the training details, such as the training data and the number of training epochs, to ensure a fair comparison with other methods?
- How does the proposed method compare with other video diffusion models that also use pre-trained image diffusion models? Could you include a comparison with these models to validate the effectiveness of your approach?
- What are the potential applications of the proposed method, and how do you address the concerns regarding the potential misuse or unintended consequences of the generated videos?
