 **Summary:**
The paper addresses the challenge of selection bias in recommender systems by proposing a novel probabilistic doubly robust learning approach. It identifies the bias in doubly robust learning methods and derives unbiasedness conditions for probabilistic models, introducing a balancing enhanced doubly robust joint learning approach. The approach aims to improve the accuracy of imputed errors and achieve unbiased learning under probabilistic error imputations and learned propensities. The paper provides theoretical analysis and empirical validation using real-world datasets, demonstrating the effectiveness of the proposed method. However, the paper lacks a detailed comparison with existing probabilistic methods and does not provide a comprehensive analysis of the computational complexity and scalability of the proposed method.

**Strengths:**
- The paper addresses a significant problem in recommender systems, specifically the selection bias, which is crucial for unbiased evaluation and learning in recommendation models.
- The paper is well-written, clear, and easy to follow, making complex concepts accessible to a broad audience.
- The authors provide a comprehensive theoretical analysis and empirical validation of their proposed method, which is supported by extensive experiments on real-world datasets, including a large-scale industrial dataset.
- The paper introduces a novel approach by combining probabilistic error imputation and propensity models, which extends the capabilities of doubly robust learning methods.
- The proposed method is shown to be effective in improving the accuracy of imputed errors and achieving unbiased learning under probabilistic error imputations and learned propensities.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed comparison with existing probabilistic methods for selection bias in recommender systems, which could provide a more comprehensive understanding of the proposed method's advantages and limitations.
- The computational complexity and scalability of the proposed method are not thoroughly analyzed, which could be crucial for understanding its applicability in different scenarios and its efficiency compared to other methods.
- The paper does not provide a comprehensive analysis of the limitations of the proposed method, which could help in understanding its scope and applicability.
- The experimental results are not sufficiently detailed, with missing standard deviations and comparisons with the best-performing method in each dataset.
- The paper could benefit from a more detailed discussion on the generalization bound and its implications for the proposed method.
- There are some typographical errors and unclear notations in the paper, which could confuse readers and detract from the paper's credibility.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors provide a detailed comparison with existing probabilistic methods for selection bias in recommender systems?

- Can the authors provide a more detailed analysis of the limitations of the proposed method?
