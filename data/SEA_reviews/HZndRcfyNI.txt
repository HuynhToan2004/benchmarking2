 **Summary:**
The paper investigates the hyperparameter tuning for neural networks with complex architectures, focusing on learning rates and initialization. It proposes a method that scales these parameters based on the depth and width of the network, which is crucial for the success of Neural Architecture Search (NAS). The methodology is tested across various architectures, including MLPs and CNNs, and is shown to outperform the NAS-Bench-201 benchmark by a significant margin. The paper also introduces a new initialization scheme that considers the in-degree of each layer, which is a departure from traditional methods. Despite its strengths, the paper is criticized for its limited experimental scope, focusing only on MLPs and CNNs, and for not sufficiently differentiating its contributions from existing literature.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, clear, and easy to follow, with a clear motivation and a strong focus on the problem of hyperparameter tuning for NAS.
- The proposed method is simple and effective, with a focus on a new initialization scheme that is intuitive and easy to apply.
- The paper includes a comprehensive set of experiments that demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed method, particularly in improving the performance of MLPs and CNNs.
- The authors provide a theoretical analysis of the impact of network architecture on hyperparameters, which is a novel contribution to the field.
- The paper includes detailed derivations in the appendix, which helps in understanding the theoretical underpinnings of the proposed method.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a thorough discussion on related work, particularly in the area of hyperparameter tuning for NAS, which could help position the paper within the existing literature.
- The experimental setup is limited to MLPs and CNNs, which may not generalize well to other types of networks such as Transformers.
- The paper does not sufficiently differentiate its contributions from existing literature, particularly from works that have already addressed similar issues.
- The paper's reliance on the ground truth maximal learning rate from a grid search may not be practical in real-world scenarios where computational resources are limited.
- The paper does not sufficiently address the limitations of its method, such as its applicability to other types of networks and its potential impact on the training of networks with normalization layers.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors elaborate on the differences between their initialization scheme and that of Defazio & Bottou (2022)?
- How does the proposed method perform when applied to other types of networks, such as Transformers?
- Could the authors discuss the potential impact of their method on the training of networks with normalization layers, such as batch normalization?
- How does the proposed method compare to other methods that have been proposed for hyperparameter tuning in NAS, such as those mentioned in related works?
- Could the authors provide more details on the computational resources required for the grid search used to determine the ground truth maximal learning rate?
- How does the proposed method handle the issue of hyperparameter tuning for NAS in scenarios where computational resources are limited?
