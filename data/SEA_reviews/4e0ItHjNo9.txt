 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel concept of principal counterfactual fairness, focusing on individuals whose protected attributes do not influence the outcome of interest. This approach aims to ensure fairness by adjusting decision-making processes based on the causal effects of protected attributes. The paper provides theoretical frameworks, including necessary conditions and post-processing approaches, to achieve this fairness. It also includes experimental validations on both synthetic and real-world datasets. The methodology is grounded in causal inference and counterfactual fairness, offering a novel perspective on fairness in machine learning. However, the paper's assumptions, such as the ignorability of protected attributes, are seen as strong and potentially limiting. The paper's clarity and organization are also criticized for making it difficult to follow, and the experimental results are not convincingly robust.

**Strengths:**
- The paper addresses a significant and relevant problem in the field of fairness, focusing on the causal effects of protected attributes on outcomes.
- The authors provide a thorough literature review, effectively summarizing the state of the art in fairness research.
- The paper is well-written, with clear definitions and a clear motivation for the problem studied.
- The proposed method is novel and interesting, offering a new approach to fairness through principal counterfactual fairness.
- The paper is technically sound, with a clear mathematical formulation and a well-articulated methodology.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper assumes strong conditions, such as ignorability and consistency, which may not hold in real-world scenarios, limiting the practical applicability of the proposed methods.
- The experimental results are not convincing, with the paper lacking a comparison to existing methods and a clear demonstration of the effectiveness of the proposed approach.
- The paper is difficult to follow due to its complex mathematical formulations and lack of clear explanations in some sections.
- The paper's claims about the necessity of the proposed method are not convincingly argued, and the theoretical results are not adequately justified.
- The paper does not sufficiently address the limitations of its method, such as the strong assumptions required for its applicability.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors clarify how the method handles scenarios where the protected attribute is not ignorable?
- How does the method ensure that the decision-making process remains fair when the protected attribute is not directly causally related to the outcome?
- Could the authors provide a clearer explanation of the experimental results, including a comparison to existing methods and a more detailed analysis of the data used?
- How does the proposed method compare to existing methods in terms of computational efficiency and scalability?
- Can the authors provide a more detailed discussion on the assumptions required for the method to be applicable, and how these assumptions are justified in practical scenarios?
