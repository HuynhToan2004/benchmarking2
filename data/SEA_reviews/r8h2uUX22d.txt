 **Summary:**
The paper investigates the MLP-Mixer architecture, proposing a new model called RP-Mixer that utilizes random permutation matrices to approximate sparse weights, thereby reducing memory requirements. The authors demonstrate that MLP-Mixer can be seen as a wide MLP with sparse weights, highlighting the effectiveness of this approach through experiments on CIFAR and ImageNet datasets. The paper also explores the relationship between MLP-Mixer and other models like SW-MLP and Monarch matrices, providing insights into the model's performance and the role of sparsity in neural network architectures.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, clear, and easy to follow, making complex concepts accessible to a broader audience.
- The experimental results are robust and convincing, demonstrating the effectiveness of the proposed RP-Mixer model.
- The paper provides a novel perspective on the MLP-Mixer architecture, highlighting its similarities to other models like SW-MLP and Monarch matrices, which adds depth to the understanding of this architecture.
- The use of random permutation matrices to approximate sparse weights is a creative and effective approach to reduce memory requirements in MLP-Mixer.
- The paper is timely and addresses a significant problem in the field of deep learning, contributing to the ongoing discourse about efficient neural network architectures.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper's claims about the memory efficiency of RP-Mixer are somewhat overstated, particularly in comparison to other models like SW-MLP, which may not be entirely fair.
- The paper lacks a thorough comparison with other models, such as ResMLP, which could provide a more comprehensive evaluation of the proposed model.
- The paper's reliance on the MLP-Mixer architecture, which has been criticized for its inefficiency compared to other architectures like ResMLP, limits the generalizability of the findings.
- The experimental setup and results could benefit from more detailed explanations and potentially more rigorous testing, particularly in terms of reproducibility and the statistical significance of the results.
- The paper could benefit from a more comprehensive discussion on the implications of the findings for broader applications in machine learning and artificial intelligence.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors clarify the memory efficiency claims regarding RP-Mixer, particularly in comparison to other models like SW-MLP?
- How does the RP-Mixer perform in terms of computational efficiency and training time compared to other models like SW-MLP?
- Could the authors provide more detailed experimental results, including error bars and a larger number of seeds, to enhance the statistical significance of the findings?
- What are the specific advantages of using RP-Mixer over other models like SW-MLP, particularly in terms of training stability and generalization performance?
- How does the proposed model compare in terms of accuracy and efficiency with other state-of-the-art models, such as those mentioned in related works?
- Could the authors elaborate on the implications of the findings for broader applications in machine learning and artificial intelligence, particularly in terms of practical implementation and scalability?
