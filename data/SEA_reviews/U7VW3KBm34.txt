 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel method for generating saliency maps, termed Sharing Ratio Decomposition (SRD), which calculates saliency maps by recursively decomposing pointwise feature vectors. This method aims to improve the robustness of saliency maps by considering the contributions of both active and inactive neurons, which is a significant departure from traditional methods that focus solely on active neurons. The SRD method is evaluated against several baselines on ImageNet-S50 and shown to outperform them in terms of localization, complexity, faithfulness, and robustness. The paper also discusses the method's ability to provide counterfactual explanations, which are crucial for understanding the decision-making processes of neural networks.
