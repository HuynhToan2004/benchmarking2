 **Summary:**
The paper investigates the problem of environmental generalization in reinforcement learning (RL) by introducing a novel non-parameterized randomization (NPR) method. This method randomizes task-agnostic components to enhance generalization capabilities across unseen environments without requiring retraining. The authors propose a structured mathematical framework to describe environmental generalization and demonstrate the effectiveness of their method through challenging environmental generalization tasks. The experiments are designed to verify the method's superiority in zero-shot environmental generalization tasks across diverse environments. The paper is praised for its innovative approach and the clarity of its presentation, although some concerns are raised about the practicality and the depth of the theoretical analysis.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, clear, and easy to follow, making complex concepts accessible to readers.
- The authors introduce a novel non-parameterized randomization (NPR) method to tackle environmental generalization, which is a significant contribution to the field.
- The paper provides a structured mathematical framework to describe environmental generalization, which is a first in this context.
- The experiments are designed to verify the superiority of the proposed method in zero-shot environmental generalization tasks, demonstrating the effectiveness of the method.
- The theoretical analysis substantiates that the proposed method can enhance generalization capabilities across unseen environments without requiring retraining.
- The paper is well-motivated, addressing the important problem of environmental generalization in reinforcement learning.
- The proposed method is simple, intuitive, and does not require parameterized models, which makes it applicable across a broad range of environments.
- The experiments are extensive, covering various environments, and the results showcase the effectiveness of the proposed method.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a comprehensive discussion on the limitations of the proposed method, particularly in terms of its practicality and applicability in real-world scenarios.
- The theoretical analysis is somewhat shallow and could benefit from a deeper exploration of the mathematical foundations and their implications.
- The paper does not sufficiently compare the proposed method with other existing methods, such as those using domain randomization, which could provide a more robust evaluation of the proposed method.
- The paper's experimental setup and results could be improved to better demonstrate the method's effectiveness in more complex and varied environments.
- The paper could benefit from a more detailed explanation of the experimental setup and the specifics of how the method was implemented and evaluated.
- The paper does not sufficiently address how the method handles different types of environments, particularly those with significant differences in state and action spaces.
- The paper's theoretical contributions are somewhat limited, and the paper could benefit from a more rigorous theoretical analysis and more detailed mathematical proofs.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors provide a more detailed explanation of how the proposed method handles different types of environments, particularly those with significant differences in state and action spaces?
- How does the proposed method compare to other existing methods in terms of generalization performance?
- Could the authors provide more details on the experimental setup, including the specifics of how the method was implemented and evaluated?
- How does the proposed method handle environments where the state and action spaces are significantly different from those in the training environment?
- Can the authors clarify the definition of the term "environment" used in the paper and its implications for the generalization capabilities of the method?
- Could the authors elaborate on the practical implications of the method, particularly in real-world scenarios where environmental generalization is crucial?
