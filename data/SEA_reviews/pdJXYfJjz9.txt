 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel method, CoIC, aimed at enhancing image deraining by leveraging a joint rain-/detail-aware contrastive learning strategy. This strategy involves the use of a context-based instance-specific modulation (Col-M) mechanism to modulate the parameters of CNN or Transformer models, guided by rain-/detail-aware representations. The proposed methodology integrates a rain-/detail-aware contrastive learning approach with Col-M to create a unified embedding space that effectively captures both rain and detail information. Extensive experiments are conducted to validate the effectiveness of the proposed method, demonstrating improvements in deraining performance on both synthetic and real-world datasets.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel context-based instance-specific modulation (Col-M) mechanism, which effectively modulates the parameters of CNN or Transformer models using rain-/detail-aware representations.
- The proposed joint rain-/detail-aware contrastive learning strategy facilitates the learning of high-quality representations for Col-M, enhancing the deraining performance for models trained on mixed datasets.
- The methodology is clearly presented, making it easy to follow and understand.
- The paper provides extensive experiments and analyses to demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed method, including a comparison with state-of-the-art methods and an ablation study on the effectiveness of different components of the proposed method.
- The method is flexible and can be applied to various types of image deraining models, enhancing its applicability and versatility.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed discussion on the computational cost and memory requirements of the proposed method, which could be crucial for practical applications.
- The paper does not sufficiently discuss the limitations of the proposed method, which could help in understanding its scope and applicability.
- The effectiveness of the proposed method is not adequately demonstrated on real-world datasets, which could limit the practical applicability of the findings.
- The paper does not include a comparison with some of the latest image deraining methods, which could have provided a more comprehensive evaluation of the proposed method.
- The paper does not discuss the potential negative societal impacts of the proposed method, which is an important consideration for any technological innovation.
