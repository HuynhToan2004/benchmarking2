 **Summary:**
The paper introduces three large-scale time series forecasting datasets from the cloud operations domain, each containing billions of observations. It explores the pre-training and scaling of time series models using these datasets, proposing a masked encoder Transformer architecture with a Student-T parametric distribution output head. The paper benchmarks this architecture against classical and deep learning baselines, demonstrating its effectiveness as a zero-shot baseline. The research also investigates the impact of model size and dataset size on performance, showing that the proposed model can outperform existing methods. Despite its contributions, the paper faces criticism for its limited novelty, lack of comprehensive baseline comparisons, and potential overfitting on the pre-training dataset.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces three large-scale time series datasets from the cloud operations domain, which are crucial for further research in pre-training and scaling of time series models.
- The proposed masked encoder Transformer architecture with a Student-T parametric distribution output head is a strong zero-shot baseline, demonstrating its effectiveness through benchmarking against classical and deep learning baselines.
- The research provides a detailed empirical study on the effectiveness of pre-training and scaling of time series models, which is a significant contribution to the field.
- The paper is well-written, making it easy to follow, and the methodology is clearly explained, enhancing its readability and comprehensibility.
- The authors have demonstrated that their proposed model outperforms existing methods on the proposed datasets, highlighting the practical applicability of their findings.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a comprehensive comparison with existing state-of-the-art methods, which could have strengthened its claims and provided a clearer picture of its contributions.
- There is a notable absence of discussion on the limitations and societal impacts of the proposed methods, which is crucial for understanding the broader implications of the research.
- The novelty of the proposed method is limited as it primarily focuses on using a masked encoder Transformer architecture with a Student-T parametric distribution output head, which is not significantly different from existing methods.
- The paper does not adequately address the issue of overfitting, which is a significant concern given the size of the datasets used.
- The paper's methodology and results lack clarity in certain sections, making it difficult for readers to fully understand the implications and contributions of the research.
- The paper could benefit from a more thorough analysis of the scalability of the proposed model and a more detailed comparison with other large-scale time series datasets to validate its claims.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors provide a more detailed comparison with existing state-of-the-art methods to clarify the novelty and effectiveness of their proposed method?
- How do the authors address the issue of overfitting, especially given the size of the datasets used?
- Could the authors discuss the limitations and societal impacts of their proposed methods in more detail to provide a more comprehensive understanding of the implications of their research?
- How does the proposed method perform when applied to other large-scale time series datasets, and what are the implications of these findings for the broader applicability of the model?
- Could the authors provide more detailed results on the scalability of the proposed model, particularly in terms of how performance scales with increasing dataset size and model size?
- How does the proposed model compare to other methods that leverage large-scale datasets for time series forecasting, such as those mentioned in the literature?
