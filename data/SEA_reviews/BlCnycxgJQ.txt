 **Summary:**
The paper introduces RAMDA, a novel adaptive algorithm for training structured neural networks, which leverages regularization to ensure that the iterates converge to the stationary point of the objective function. This method builds on previous work by Huang and Lee, integrating an adaptive preconditioner to enhance the model's predictive ability. The paper also presents a subproblem solver to address the computational challenges of regularized adaptive methods, which typically lack closed-form solutions. The proposed method is evaluated against several benchmarks, showing improvements in model predictive ability and structured sparsity. However, the paper is criticized for its limited novelty and the lack of comprehensive comparisons with state-of-the-art methods.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, clear, and easy to follow, with a clear motivation and a logical structure that effectively explains the methodology and its contributions.
- The proposed method, RAMDA, is a natural extension of previous work, integrating adaptiveness and momentum to enhance model predictive ability, which is a significant improvement over existing methods.
- The subproblem solver is a novel contribution that addresses the computational challenges of regularized adaptive methods, providing a practical solution that is both efficient and effective.
- The paper includes a thorough analysis of the convergence properties of the algorithm, which is supported by theoretical proofs and empirical evidence.
- The experimental results demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed method in achieving both high structured sparsity and good prediction performance, showing a clear advantage over other methods.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a comprehensive comparison with state-of-the-art methods, particularly in terms of computational efficiency and model performance. This limits the ability to convincingly demonstrate the superiority of the proposed method.
- The novelty of the paper is somewhat limited as it builds on previous work, and the technical contributions are not clearly distinguished from existing methods.
- The paper could benefit from a more detailed discussion on the convergence properties of the algorithm, particularly the conditions under which the algorithm converges and the implications of these conditions.
- There is a lack of clarity in some parts of the paper, particularly in the definitions and explanations of key terms and concepts, which could confuse readers.
- The paper could be improved by including more detailed experimental results, such as the convergence curves and the impact of different hyperparameters on the performance.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors provide a more detailed comparison with state-of-the-art methods, including both computational efficiency and model performance metrics?
- How does the proposed method compare to other adaptive methods in terms of computational efficiency and model performance, particularly in large-scale applications?
- Can the authors clarify the technical contributions of the paper and how they differ from existing methods, particularly in terms of the subproblem solver and the adaptive preconditioner?
- How does the proposed method perform in terms of convergence speed and stability, and what are the implications of these properties for practical applications?
- Could the authors provide more detailed experimental results, such as the convergence curves and the impact of different hyperparameters on the performance, to support the claims made in the paper?
