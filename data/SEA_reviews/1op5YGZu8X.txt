 **Summary:**
The paper investigates the robust overfitting phenomenon in adversarially trained (AT) wide neural networks using the Neural Tangent Kernel (NTK) theory. It introduces a novel method, Adv-NTK, which optimizes the linearized DNN dynamics to enhance robustness in infinite-width DNNs. The authors demonstrate that long-term AT can lead to a degeneration of the network's robustness, which is similar to that of a network without AT. The paper also proposes a new theoretical analysis of the AT dynamics and provides a closed-form solution for the linearized DNN dynamics. However, the paper's assumptions and the practical applicability of the proposed method are questioned, as the results are not consistently better than existing methods, and the theoretical contributions are seen as extensions rather than groundbreaking.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, making it easy to follow and understand.
- The proposed method, Adv-NTK, is innovative and offers a novel approach to enhancing the robustness of wide neural networks.
- The theoretical analysis is sound and provides a new perspective on the robust overfitting phenomenon in adversarially trained networks.
- The paper introduces a closed-form solution for the linearized DNN dynamics, which is a significant contribution to the field.
- The experimental results support the theoretical findings, showing that Adv-NTK can achieve comparable or higher performance than AT in some cases.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper heavily relies on the assumption that the adversarial perturbation scale is small enough, which may not hold in practical scenarios, limiting the generalizability of the results.
- The paper's methodology and assumptions are not clearly justified, particularly the assumptions made in Theorem 2 and the practical implementation of the proposed method.
- The paper's theoretical contributions are seen as extensions rather than groundbreaking, as similar results have been reported in previous works.
- The paper lacks a comprehensive comparison with existing methods, particularly in terms of computational efficiency and practical applicability.
- The paper's assumptions, such as the infinite-width limit and the linearization of DNNs, are not well-justified, and the practical implications of these assumptions are not discussed.
- The experimental results do not consistently show superior performance over existing methods, and the paper does not provide a detailed analysis of the robustness of the proposed method against different types of adversarial attacks.

**Questions:**
- Can you clarify the assumptions made in Theorem 2 and how they are justified in practical scenarios?
- How does the proposed method perform against different types of adversarial attacks, particularly those that are not based on the $\ell_\infty$ norm?
- Can you provide a detailed comparison of the computational efficiency and practical applicability of Adv-NTK with other existing methods?
- How does the paper address the concerns regarding the practicality of the assumptions made, particularly the infinite-width limit and the linearization of DNNs?
- Can you provide more details on the experimental setup, such as the choice of hyperparameters and the validation data used?
- How does the paper address the robustness of the proposed method against different types of adversarial attacks, and what are the implications of this robustness in practical scenarios?
