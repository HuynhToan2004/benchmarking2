 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel approach to attribute masking in molecular graph pre-training, focusing on motif-aware masking strategies to capture long-range inter-motif structures. This method leverages the BRICS algorithm to decompose molecules into motifs, masking nodes within these motifs and predicting masked features using a decoder. The authors argue that this approach enhances the learning of long-range dependencies and inter-motif knowledge transfer, as demonstrated through extensive experiments on eight molecular property prediction datasets. However, concerns were raised regarding the novelty of the method, as it appears to be a variant of existing methods, and the effectiveness of the proposed method in handling long-range dependencies was not convincingly demonstrated.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel motif-aware attribute masking strategy for attribute reconstruction during graph model pre-training, which outperforms existing methods that use random attribute masking.
- The proposed method achieves competitive results with state-of-the-art methods due to the explicit transfer of long-range inter-motif knowledge and intra-motif structural information.
- The paper is well-written, easy to follow, and provides a clear motivation for the proposed method.
- Extensive experiments have been conducted, including ablation studies and analysis of inter-motif knowledge transfer, which demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed method.
- The paper introduces a new method of measuring inter-motif knowledge transfer, which is a significant contribution to the field.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a clear demonstration of the proposed method's ability to handle long-range dependencies effectively, as shown in Figure 1.
- The novelty of the method is questioned as it appears to be a variant of existing methods, and the differences between the proposed method and previous methods are not clearly articulated.
- The experimental results are not convincing, with only marginal improvements over existing methods, and the paper does not adequately address the computational cost of the proposed method.
- The paper does not provide sufficient details on the experimental setup, such as the specifics of the BRICS algorithm used and the pre-training data used in the experiments.
- There is a lack of comparison with more recent and relevant baselines, such as Mole-BERT and other state-of-the-art methods.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors provide a more detailed explanation of how the proposed method effectively handles long-range dependencies compared to existing methods?
- How does the proposed method differ from previous methods, and what are the specific advantages of the proposed method over existing methods?
- Could the authors provide more details on the experimental setup, such as the specifics of the BRICS algorithm used and the pre-training data used in the experiments?
- How does the proposed method compare to more recent and relevant baselines, such as Mole-BERT and other state-of-the-art methods?
- What is the computational cost of the proposed method, and how does it compare to existing methods in terms of computational efficiency?
