 **Summary:**
The paper introduces InstructScene, a novel generative framework that integrates a semantic graph prior and a layout decoder to enhance controllability and fidelity in 3D scene synthesis. It leverages a diffusion-based approach to generate semantic graphs and 3D scenes conditioned on natural language instructions. The framework's effectiveness is demonstrated through extensive experiments and ablation studies, showing improvements over existing methods. The paper also introduces a dataset of scene-instruction pairs, which is expected to be made public, to facilitate further research in this area.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel generative framework that integrates a semantic graph prior and a layout decoder, enhancing the controllability and fidelity of 3D scene synthesis.
- It proposes a novel diffusion-based approach for generating semantic graphs, which is a significant contribution to the field.
- The paper presents a comprehensive dataset of scene-instruction pairs, which is expected to be made public, providing a valuable resource for the research community.
- The methodology is clearly explained, making the paper accessible and understandable.
- Extensive experiments and ablation studies are conducted, demonstrating the effectiveness of the proposed method.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed discussion on the limitations of the proposed method, which is crucial for understanding its applicability and potential drawbacks.
- The dataset used in the experiments is not publicly available, which limits the reproducibility of the results and the ability for other researchers to validate or build upon the findings.
- The paper does not compare the proposed method with other generative models for graph generation, which could provide a more comprehensive evaluation of its performance.
- The paper's claims about the method's ability to generate "holistic semantic graphs" are not well-supported by the experimental results, which primarily focus on specific types of scenes (bedroom, living room, and dining room).
- The method's reliance on large language models (LLMs) and multimodal-aligned models raises concerns about the generalizability of the results and the potential for overfitting to specific datasets.

**Questions:**
- Could you clarify how the semantic graph prior is trained, especially regarding the generation of the codebook and the training of the quantization VAE?
- How does the method handle the generation of scenes with different numbers of objects, and what are the implications of this on the quality of the generated scenes?
- Can you provide more details on the training and inference of the layout decoder, particularly how it handles the generation of different numbers of objects in the scene?
- How does the method ensure that the generated scenes are aesthetically pleasing and adhere to regular arrangements, as mentioned in the introduction?
- Could you elaborate on the choice of the diffusion model over other generative models, and how the method compares to other graph-based methods in terms of performance and complexity?
