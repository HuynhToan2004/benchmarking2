 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel approach to font generation using a diffusion model, aiming to address the challenges of training instability and mode collapse in traditional generative adversarial networks (GANs). The methodology involves a decoupling of content and style through a pre-trained encoder and the integration of a classifier-free guidance diffusion model. The approach is evaluated on a dataset of 400 fonts, demonstrating improved performance over existing methods in terms of both quantitative and qualitative metrics. Despite the promising results, the paper has been critiqued for its limited experimental scope, lack of comprehensive baseline comparisons, and potential over-reliance on specific datasets.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel application of diffusion models to the field of font generation, which is both innovative and relevant to current research trends.
- The methodology is well-explained, making the paper accessible and understandable to readers.
- The experimental results show a clear improvement over existing methods, indicating the effectiveness of the proposed approach.
- The paper is well-organized and easy to follow, enhancing its readability and accessibility.
- The use of a pre-trained encoder for content and style decoupling is a significant contribution to the field, potentially benefiting future research.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks comprehensive baseline comparisons, which could have strengthened the validation of the proposed method.
- The experimental setup is limited, using a small dataset (400 fonts) which might not adequately represent real-world font diversity.
- The paper could benefit from a more detailed discussion on the limitations of the diffusion model, particularly concerning the training instability and mode collapse issues.
- There is a need for more quantitative comparisons with state-of-the-art methods to better establish the effectiveness of the proposed approach.
- The paper could be improved by addressing the clarity of some figures and sections, particularly in the description of the content encoder and the results of the experiments.
- The generalization of the method to other writing systems and the scalability of the model to larger datasets are not adequately addressed.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors clarify the role and impact of the content encoder in the overall performance of the model?
- How does the proposed method compare with the latest state-of-the-art methods in terms of performance metrics?
- Can the authors provide more details on the training and inference time of their method compared to other baselines?
- Is there a plan to release the dataset used in the study to facilitate further research in the field?
- Could the authors elaborate on the selection of the Chinese Song font as the source font and discuss its implications for the model's performance?
- How does the proposed method handle the issue of mode collapse, and what steps have been taken to address this in the training process?
