 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel method for training Variational Autoencoders (VAEs) using samples generated by a pre-trained diffusion model, aimed at mitigating overfitting issues in VAEs. The approach, referred to as DMAAPx, utilizes a diffusion model trained on the same dataset as the VAE to generate synthetic data, which is then used to enhance the VAE's performance on various metrics such as generalization, amortization, and robustness gaps. The method is evaluated across multiple datasets including MNIST, FashionMNIST, and CIFAR-10, showing improvements over traditional VAE training and data augmentation techniques. Despite these improvements, the paper's novelty and the depth of its theoretical contributions are questioned, as the method primarily builds on existing techniques and does not significantly advance the field beyond current practices.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, clear, and easy to follow, with a clear motivation and well-explained methodology.
- The experiments are comprehensive, including various metrics such as generalization, amortization, and robustness gaps, which are convincingly demonstrated through figures.
- The proposed method is simple yet effective, showing improvements over traditional training and data augmentation methods in various scenarios.
- The method is novel in its application of diffusion models to address overfitting in VAEs, which is a significant problem in the field.
- The paper provides a clear distinction between the proposed method and existing data augmentation techniques, highlighting the advantages of the proposed method.

**Weaknesses:**
- The novelty of the method is limited as it primarily uses a diffusion model to generate synthetic data for VAE training, which has been previously explored in other works.
- The paper lacks a thorough comparison with other state-of-the-art methods, which could have provided a clearer picture of the method's advantages and limitations.
- The theoretical contribution of the paper is limited, with most of the content focused on experimental results rather than new theoretical insights.
- The method's effectiveness and the choice of hyperparameters, such as the number of epochs and the size of the training data, are not adequately discussed.
- The paper does not address the issue of overfitting in the decoder, which could limit the generalizability of the findings.
- The paper's claims about the method's ability to reduce overfitting and improve performance are not convincingly supported by the experimental results, which show only marginal improvements in some cases.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors clarify the novelty of their method compared to existing works that use diffusion models for data generation?
- How does the proposed method compare to other state-of-the-art methods in terms of performance and computational efficiency?
- What are the specific advantages of using a diffusion model over other data generation techniques, such as GANs or other generative models?
- Can the authors provide more detailed experimental results, such as the training and test ELBO curves for different datasets, to better support their claims?
- How does the method perform in terms of computational cost and training time, especially when compared to traditional VAE training and data augmentation methods?
- Could the authors discuss the potential impact of the method on the decoder's performance and generalizability, especially in light of the observed improvements in encoder performance?
