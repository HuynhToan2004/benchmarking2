 **Summary:**
The paper explores the representation of truth in large language models (LLMs) using datasets of true/false statements. It introduces a novel technique called mass-mean probing, which involves using the mean of true and false data to identify a direction that distinguishes between true and false statements. The study demonstrates that this method can generalize well across different datasets and layers of LLMs, and that it can be causally implicated in the model's output. The paper also critiques the limitations of existing probing techniques and provides empirical evidence for the effectiveness of mass-mean probing. However, it is noted that the study primarily focuses on simple, uncontroversial statements and does not address the representation of truth in more complex or ambiguous statements.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a new technique, mass-mean probing, which is a significant contribution to the field of probing the internal representations of large language models (LLMs).
- The paper is well-written, making it easy to follow and understand, and includes a comprehensive discussion on the limitations of existing probing techniques.
- The study is empirically grounded, providing a detailed analysis of the structure of LLM representations of truth, which is crucial for understanding the inner workings of these models.
- The authors have curated high-quality datasets of true/false statements, which are a valuable resource for future research.
- The paper includes a thorough discussion on the limitations of the study, which enhances its credibility and transparency.

**Weaknesses:**
- The study primarily focuses on simple, uncontroversial statements, which may not fully capture the complexity of truth representation in LLMs. It would be beneficial to include more diverse and ambiguous statements to test the robustness of the findings.
- The paper does not sufficiently address the representation of truth in LLMs beyond simple, uncontroversial statements, which limits the generalizability of the findings.
- The experiments are somewhat limited in scope, focusing on a single model architecture (LLaMA) and not comparing the results across different model families.
- The paper could benefit from a more detailed discussion on the limitations of the datasets used and the potential impact of these limitations on the results.
- The paper does not include a discussion on the implications of the findings for practical applications, such as improving the truthfulness of LLM outputs.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors clarify the definition of "truth" used in the study, particularly in relation to the datasets and the methodology employed?
- How does the mass-mean probing technique compare to other probing methods in terms of accuracy and generalization?
- What are the implications of the findings for the practical application of LLMs, such as improving their truthfulness?
- How do the authors address the potential limitations of the datasets used, particularly in terms of their representativeness and diversity?
- Could the authors provide more details on the choice of model architecture and the implications of this choice for the findings?
