 **Summary:**
The paper introduces Sparling, a novel method for learning extremely sparse representations in deep neural networks, specifically designed for tasks where intermediate states are sparse. The method, which includes a Spatial Sparsity Layer and an Adaptive Sparsity Algorithm, enforces sparsity by setting activations below a threshold to zero and adjusting this threshold over time. Sparling is evaluated on three tasks: DigitCircle, LaTeX-OCR, and AudioMnistSequence, demonstrating its effectiveness in achieving high levels of sparsity and maintaining performance. The paper also discusses the challenges of achieving extreme sparsity and the trade-offs between sparsity and accuracy.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, clearly presented, and easy to follow, with a clear motivation and novel approach to learning extremely sparse representations.
- The proposed method is innovative, simple, and effective, achieving high sparsity levels and good performance on various tasks.
- The experimental results are convincing, showing that the method can achieve extreme sparsity while maintaining performance, and the methodology is clearly explained.
- The paper addresses a significant problem in learning interpretable representations from deep neural networks, which is crucial for understanding and interpreting the network's predictions.
- The proposed method is well-motivated and the experiments are well-designed, providing a thorough analysis of the method's performance.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper could benefit from a more comprehensive discussion on the limitations of the proposed method, particularly in terms of its applicability to different types of data and the potential for overfitting.
- The experiments are somewhat limited to specific domains (e.g., digit recognition and LaTeX-OCR), which might not generalize well to other types of data.
- The paper does not sufficiently compare its method with other existing methods for enforcing sparsity in deep neural networks, which could help establish the superiority or novelty of the proposed method.
- The paper's evaluation metrics are not clearly defined, which could lead to confusion about the actual performance of the method.
- The paper could benefit from a more detailed discussion on the computational cost of the proposed method, especially in terms of training and inference times.

**Questions:**
- Could you provide more details on the computational cost of the proposed method, particularly in terms of training and inference times?
- How does the method perform when applied to other types of data, such as natural images or audio signals?
- Could you clarify the definition and usage of the evaluation metrics, particularly the edit distance and its computation?
- How does the method compare to other existing methods for enforcing sparsity in deep neural networks, and what are the advantages of your method over these?
- In the context of the experiments, could you provide more details on the training and validation procedures, especially in terms of the data used and the hyperparameters employed?
- Could you elaborate on the limitations of the proposed method and discuss potential future directions for research in this area?
