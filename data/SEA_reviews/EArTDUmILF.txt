 **Summary:**
The paper introduces Variational Bayesian Heterogeneous Graph Neural Networks (VBH-GNN) for cross-subject emotion recognition, leveraging multi-modal physiological signals and domain adaptation (DA) to address the challenges of individual differences in EEG data. The proposed method, Relationship Distribution Adaptation (RDA), aligns the distribution of spatio-temporal relationships between features, enhancing the model's ability to recognize emotions across subjects. Extensive experiments on two public datasets demonstrate the effectiveness of VBH-GNN, showing improvements over existing methods. However, the paper lacks clarity in some technical details and experimental setups, and the novelty of the approach is questioned due to similarities with existing methods.

**Strengths:**
- The paper addresses a significant and challenging problem in emotion recognition using EEG signals, focusing on cross-subject emotion recognition.
- The proposed method, VBH-GNN, is well-motivated and presents a novel approach to handling multi-modal physiological signals and domain adaptation.
- The methodology is well-explained, with clear descriptions of the proposed method and its components, making the paper accessible and understandable.
- The paper is well-organized, with clear and concise writing that effectively communicates the research findings and methodology.
- Extensive experiments are conducted, demonstrating the effectiveness of the proposed method in improving emotion recognition accuracy.
- The use of variational Bayesian inference and graph neural networks (GNNs) is innovative, particularly in the context of emotion recognition.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks clarity in some technical details, such as the specifics of the heterogeneous graph (HetG) and the training details of the VBH-GNN.
- There is a lack of comparison with the latest state-of-the-art methods, which could provide a more robust evaluation of the proposed method.
- The paper does not adequately discuss the limitations of the proposed method, which could help in understanding its applicability and potential improvements.
- The paper does not address the issue of data leakage, which is crucial in emotion recognition tasks.
- The novelty of the approach is questioned due to similarities with existing methods, such as HetEmotionNet, which also uses graph neural networks for emotion recognition.
- The paper lacks a detailed discussion on the choice of hyperparameters and their impact on the model's performance.
- There are inconsistencies and potential errors in the experimental setup, such as the use of the same hyperparameters across different models and the absence of standard deviation in results.

**Questions:**
- Could you clarify the definition and role of the heterogeneous graph (HetG) in your model?
- How does the VBH-GNN handle the training details, particularly the loss function and the optimization process?
- Why was the choice made to use the same hyperparameters across different models, and how does this affect the model's performance?
- Can you provide more details on the data leakage issue and how it was addressed in your experiments?
- How does the proposed method compare with the latest state-of-the-art methods, and what are the specific advantages of your approach?
- Could you elaborate on the choice of hyperparameters and their impact on the model's performance?
- In the context of the modality-deficient experiments, how does the model perform when different modalities are removed, and what does this indicate about the model's reliance on specific modalities?
- Could you provide more details on the experimental setup, particularly the data preprocessing and the choice of evaluation metrics?
