 **Summary:**
The paper introduces TwinS, a transformer-based model designed for multivariate time series forecasting, which addresses the challenges of non-stationary periodic distributions in time series data. TwinS comprises three modules: Wavelet Convolution, Period-Aware Attention, and Channel-Temporal Mixed MLP. The Wavelet Convolution module models nested periods by scaling convolution kernel sizes, while Period-Aware Attention generates relevance scores for attention computation. The Channel-Temporal Mixed MLP captures relationships between time series. The model's effectiveness is demonstrated through experiments on eight datasets, showing significant improvements over existing models. However, concerns about the novelty of the approach, the clarity of the presentation, and the robustness of the results against hyperparameter settings were raised.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written and easy to follow, making complex concepts accessible to a broader audience.
- The proposed TwinS model effectively addresses the challenges of nested periodicity, missing states in non-stationary periodic distributions, and interrelationships with hysteresis among time series, which are crucial for improving the performance of transformer models in multivariate time series forecasting (MTSF).
- The model's performance is validated through rigorous experiments and ablation studies, demonstrating its superiority over existing models in handling non-stationary time series.
- The paper provides a detailed analysis of the model's performance across various datasets, showcasing its versatility and applicability in real-world scenarios.
- The authors have open-sourced their code, enhancing reproducibility and facilitating further research in the field.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a comprehensive review of existing literature on non-stationary time series forecasting, which could provide a clearer context for the proposed model's contributions.
- The novelty of the proposed method is questioned as it appears to be a combination of existing techniques without significant innovation.
- The paper does not adequately address the computational efficiency of the proposed model, which is crucial for practical applications.
- The experimental results are not robust, with significant variations in performance depending on the hyperparameters used, which could undermine the reliability of the findings.
- The paper does not sufficiently compare the proposed model with state-of-the-art models, which could help in validating its effectiveness more convincingly.
- The paper suffers from several typographical and formatting errors, which detract from its professional quality.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors clarify the novelty of the proposed method by discussing how it differs from existing models and what unique advantages it offers?
- How does the computational efficiency of the TwinS model compare to other state-of-the-art models, and what steps have been taken to optimize its performance in terms of computational resources?
- The paper mentions the importance of handling non-stationary time series. Can the authors provide more detailed examples or case studies to illustrate the practical implications of this challenge in real-world scenarios?
- The experimental results show significant variations in performance depending on the hyperparameters used. Can the authors provide more insights into how these hyperparameters were chosen and what impact they have on the model's performance?
- The paper's claims regarding the effectiveness of the proposed model are supported by experimental results. Can the authors provide a more detailed analysis of these results, possibly including visualizations or additional data to support their claims?
- The paper's presentation could benefit from better organization and clarity. Could the authors consider restructuring the paper to make it more accessible and easier to understand for readers?
