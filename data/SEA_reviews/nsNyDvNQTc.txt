 **Summary:**
The paper investigates the integration of model score and uncertainty to enhance binary classification performance, specifically focusing on the binary decision boundary construction. It introduces a novel approach by partitioning the score-uncertainty space into bins and proposing algorithms to optimize recall under precision constraints. The paper theoretically explores the relationship between model score, uncertainty, and test positivity rate, providing insights into how these factors influence decision-making. Empirical evaluations on three datasets demonstrate the efficacy of the proposed methods, showing improvements in recall at high precision bounds. However, the paper suffers from issues in clarity, presentation, and methodological soundness, with concerns about the generalizability of the results and the practical applicability of the proposed methods.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel approach to integrating model scores with uncertainty to improve binary classification performance, which is a significant contribution to the field.
- Theoretical analysis is provided to support the proposed method, enhancing the credibility of the approach.
- Empirical evaluations demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed method, showing improvements in recall at high precision bounds.
- The paper is well-written, making it easy to follow and understand.
- The study of the relationship between model score, uncertainty, and test positivity rate is insightful and contributes to the understanding of how these factors influence decision-making.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper suffers from clarity issues, particularly in the presentation of figures and the explanation of certain concepts, such as the binning strategy and the NP-hardness of the problem.
- The generalizability of the proposed method is limited due to its reliance on specific datasets and models, which may not apply universally.
- The paper lacks a comprehensive comparison with existing methods, particularly in terms of computational efficiency and practical applicability.
- The methodological soundness of the proposed algorithms, particularly the DP algorithm, is questionable due to its exponential computational complexity and potential inefficiency.
- The paper could benefit from a more rigorous evaluation, including a broader range of datasets and a more thorough comparison with state-of-the-art methods.
- The paper does not adequately address the issue of differential sampling, which could significantly impact the performance of the proposed methods.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors clarify the binning strategy and its impact on the results, particularly how it affects the decision-making process?
- How does the proposed method perform on datasets with different characteristics, such as those with high class imbalance or different levels of uncertainty?
- Could the authors provide a more detailed comparison with existing methods, including a discussion on the computational efficiency and practical applicability of their approach?
- How does the proposed method handle differential sampling, and what are the implications of this for the performance of the model?
- Can the authors provide more details on the experimental setup, including the specifics of the datasets used and the experimental conditions?
- Is there a possibility to extend the method to multi-class classification scenarios, and if so, how would this be implemented?
