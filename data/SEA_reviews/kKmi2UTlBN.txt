 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel approach to knowledge distillation (KD) using cosine similarity between teacher and student model logits, aiming to enhance the performance of the student model by dynamically adjusting the temperature scaling based on the similarity between the models. This approach, termed Cosine Similarity Weighted Temperature (CSWT), is proposed to address the class bias issue in KD by making the student model's logits more similar to the teacher's. The method is tested on datasets like CIFAR-100 and ImageNet, showing promising results in terms of performance and entropy analysis. However, the paper lacks a comprehensive comparison with state-of-the-art methods and does not sufficiently discuss the limitations and broader impacts of the proposed method.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, making it easy to follow, and the experiments are well-designed, with results showing the effectiveness of the proposed method.
- The use of cosine similarity for knowledge distillation is a novel approach that has been shown to be effective in various experiments, demonstrating its ability to improve the student model's performance.
- The paper provides a clear motivation for using cosine similarity in knowledge distillation, which is a significant departure from traditional methods that use other metrics like KL divergence.
- The methodology is straightforward and easy to implement, with good reproducibility, which is crucial for practical applications.
- The proposed method shows promising results, outperforming existing methods in some cases, and is scalable to larger datasets like ImageNet.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a thorough comparison with state-of-the-art methods, particularly in terms of the proposed CSWT, which is crucial for validating the effectiveness of the proposed approach.
- The analysis of the proposed method is not sufficiently detailed, especially in terms of its scalability and the impact of different temperature settings on performance.
- The paper does not adequately discuss the limitations of the proposed method, which is essential for understanding its applicability and potential drawbacks.
- There is a lack of discussion on the broader impacts of the proposed method, such as its potential negative societal implications or ethical considerations.
- The paper could benefit from a more detailed discussion on the computational costs and training time associated with the proposed method, which are crucial for practical deployment.

**Questions:**
- How does the proposed method perform when compared to other state-of-the-art methods in terms of computational efficiency and training time?
- Can the authors provide a more detailed analysis of the proposed method, including its scalability and the impact of different temperature settings on performance?
- What are the potential limitations of the proposed method, and how do these limitations affect its practical applicability?
- Could the authors provide more detailed results on the performance of the proposed method on larger datasets like ImageNet, and how does it compare to other methods in terms of accuracy and training time?
- How does the proposed method address the class bias issue in knowledge distillation, and what are the implications of this approach in terms of model generalization and robustness?
