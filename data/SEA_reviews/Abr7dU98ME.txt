 **Summary:**
The paper introduces ForwardGNN, a novel forward learning procedure for Graph Neural Networks (GNNs) that aims to address the limitations of traditional backpropagation (BP) algorithms. It extends the forward-forward algorithm to GNNs, allowing for efficient training without the need for negative inputs. The proposed methodology is evaluated across various benchmarks, demonstrating its effectiveness in node classification and link prediction tasks. The paper also discusses the biological plausibility of the forward-only training, which is a significant departure from the conventional BP methods.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel approach to training Graph Neural Networks (GNNs) using a forward-only learning procedure, which is a significant departure from traditional backpropagation methods.
- The proposed methodology is well-explained, making it accessible to readers and providing a clear structure that enhances understanding.
- The experiments conducted are comprehensive, demonstrating the effectiveness of the proposed method across various datasets and tasks, including node classification and link prediction.
- The paper addresses the scalability and efficiency issues associated with backpropagation, which are crucial for large-scale applications.
- The methodology is biologically plausible, aligning with how the brain processes information, which is an interesting aspect for those interested in neuroscience.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed discussion on the limitations of the proposed method, particularly concerning its applicability to different GNN architectures and its scalability to larger datasets.
- There is a lack of comparison with other advanced GNN methods, which could provide a more robust evaluation of the proposed method's effectiveness.
- The paper could benefit from a more thorough discussion on the biological plausibility of the forward-only training, especially in relation to how it aligns with the brain's learning processes.
- The experimental setup and results, particularly the comparison with baselines, are not clearly presented, which could lead to confusion about the method's effectiveness.
- The paper does not adequately address the scalability issues related to the generation of negative samples, which is a critical aspect of the forward-forward algorithm.

**Questions:**
- How does the proposed method perform on large-scale datasets, and what are the potential challenges in scaling it up?
- Can the authors provide a more detailed comparison with other advanced GNN methods to demonstrate the superiority of their approach?
- How does the proposed method compare in terms of computational efficiency and memory usage with other methods, particularly those that use negative samples?
- Could the authors elaborate on the biological plausibility of the forward-only training and how it aligns with the brain's learning processes?
- In the context of the experiments, how does the proposed method perform when compared to other methods in terms of accuracy and memory usage?
- Can the authors clarify the experimental setup, particularly how the results were obtained and what the implications are for the practical application of the method?
