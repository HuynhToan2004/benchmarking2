 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel method, Fair Mapping, aimed at addressing biases in text-to-image diffusion models by modifying the prompt to control image generation. The method involves a lightweight linear mapping network that projects language embeddings into an unbiased space, reducing the computational cost and enhancing fairness in image generation. The paper also proposes a new evaluation metric to assess fairness and conducts extensive experiments on face image generation, showing significant improvements in fairness metrics. However, the paper has been criticized for its limited evaluation scope, focusing primarily on face images, and the lack of comprehensive baseline comparisons. The method's effectiveness and generalizability to other types of images remain unclear.

**Strengths:**
- The paper addresses a significant and timely issue of bias in text-to-image diffusion models, which is crucial for fair and unbiased image generation.
- The proposed method is simple, effective, and lightweight, requiring minimal parameter updates during training, making it efficient and practical.
- The introduction of a new evaluation metric to assess fairness in diffusion models is a valuable contribution to the field.
- The paper is well-written, easy to follow, and provides comprehensive experiments and ablation studies that demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed method.
- The proposed method can be applied to any text-to-image diffusion model, making it versatile and adaptable.
- The paper introduces a new fairness evaluation metric, which is a significant contribution to the field.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a comprehensive baseline comparison, which could have provided a clearer understanding of the method's effectiveness and limitations.
- The evaluation is limited to face images, which might not generalize well to other types of images, such as landscapes or animals.
- The paper does not provide a detailed analysis of the impact of the hyperparameter λ on the results, which is crucial for understanding the method's performance.
- The paper does not address how the method performs with different types of text prompts, which could affect the fairness and effectiveness of the generated images.
- The paper does not provide a detailed comparison with existing methods that address fairness in text-to-image diffusion models, which could have provided a better context for the proposed method.
- The paper's focus on fairness in text-to-image diffusion models might be too narrow, as fairness issues could also arise in other applications of these models.
- The method's effectiveness in handling different types of biases, such as cultural or social biases, is not thoroughly discussed.

**Questions:**
- How does the method perform with different types of text prompts, and what are the implications for fairness and effectiveness?
- Can the method be applied to other types of images beyond faces, such as landscapes or animals?
- How does the method handle different types of biases, such as cultural or social biases?
- What is the impact of the hyperparameter λ on the results, and how should it be set for different types of text prompts?
- Can the method be applied to other text-to-image diffusion models beyond those tested in the paper?
- How does the method compare to existing methods that address fairness in text-to-image diffusion models?
- Could the authors provide more details on the training and inference stages of the proposed method, particularly how the "detector" works?
- How does the proposed method address the fairness issues in other applications of text-to-image diffusion models, such as in social media or recommender systems?
