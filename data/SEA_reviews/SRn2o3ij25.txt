 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel approach to long-tail recognition, focusing on two components: Implicit Uncertainty Regularization (IUR) and Implicit Correlation Labeling (ICL). IUR leverages the uncertainty in predictions across epochs to reduce uncertainty and improve performance, while ICL utilizes inter-class similarity information to reduce bias towards head classes. These components are designed to enhance the performance of long-tailed learning methods by addressing the challenges of prediction uncertainty and bias. The paper provides extensive experiments across various datasets, demonstrating improvements over existing methods. However, concerns about the novelty of the approach and the clarity of its presentation have been raised.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces two innovative components, IUR and ICL, which address significant challenges in long-tailed recognition, such as prediction uncertainty and bias towards head classes.
- The experimental results are extensive, demonstrating improvements over existing methods, and the proposed method is compatible with various backbone architectures.
- The paper is well-organized and easy to follow, with detailed experimental results and ablation studies that support the effectiveness of the proposed method.
- The method is straightforward, intuitive, and well-motivated, with a focus on reducing uncertainty in predictions and leveraging inter-class similarity information to improve performance.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper's novelty is questioned as the components, while effective, are seen as incremental improvements over existing methods.
- The writing and presentation of the paper could be improved for clarity and comprehension. The introduction of certain components and equations (e.g., ICL, Eq. 7) is not adequately explained, making it difficult for readers to fully understand their implications.
- The paper lacks a thorough discussion on the limitations of the proposed method, particularly in terms of scalability and computational efficiency.
- The paper's approach to handling uncertainty and bias is not sufficiently differentiated from existing methods, such as those using ensemble learning or knowledge distillation.
- The experimental results are not convincingly superior to existing methods, with some improvements being marginal or not statistically significant.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors clarify the novelty of the proposed method in relation to existing works, particularly those using ensemble learning or knowledge distillation?
- How does the proposed method compare in terms of computational efficiency and scalability, especially when applied to large-scale datasets?
- Could the authors provide more detailed explanations for the components and equations introduced in the paper, particularly ICL and Eq. 7, to enhance reader comprehension?
- How does the proposed method handle the potential for increased uncertainty in predictions when using soft labels derived from previous epochs?
- The paper mentions the use of different datasets (e.g., CIFAR-100-LT and ImageNet-LT) with varying levels of imbalance. Can the authors explain the rationale behind these choices and their impact on the results?
- Can the authors provide more detailed experimental results, such as standard deviations and statistical significance tests, to support the claimed improvements over existing methods?
