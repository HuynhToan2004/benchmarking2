 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel open-world semi-supervised learning framework named CONTROL, which integrates nearest neighbor contrastive learning, supervised contrastive learning, and unsupervised contrastive learning to address the challenges of insufficient classification of unseen classes and representation collapse in existing methods. The framework is evaluated on CIFAR-10, CIFAR-100, and ImageNet-100 datasets, demonstrating significant improvements in unseen class classification and overall classification accuracy compared to state-of-the-art methods. The theoretical analysis supports the benefits of contrastive learning at the feature level for unseen classification, and the uniformity mechanism is proposed to prevent representation collapse. The paper is well-organized, easy to follow, and provides extensive experimental results, though some concerns are raised regarding the novelty, clarity in presentation, and the lack of comparisons with recent relevant works.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel open-world semi-supervised learning framework named CONTROL, which integrates nearest neighbor contrastive learning, supervised contrastive learning, and unsupervised contrastive learning to address the challenges of insufficient classification of unseen classes and representation collapse in existing methods.
- Theoretical analysis supports the benefits of contrastive learning at the feature level for unseen classification, and the uniformity mechanism is proposed to prevent representation collapse.
- The paper is well-organized, easy to follow, and provides extensive experimental results, demonstrating significant improvements in unseen class classification and overall classification accuracy compared to state-of-the-art methods.

- The paper provides a theoretical analysis of the proposed method, which supports its effectiveness and addresses the problem of unseen class collapse.

**Weaknesses:**

- The novelty of the paper is limited as it combines existing methods (BCE-based methods and contrastive learning) without significant innovation.
- The presentation of the paper could be improved, particularly in the clarity of figures and the explanation of the methodology.
- The paper does not discuss the computational complexity of the proposed method, which is crucial for practical deployment.
- There are several typos and grammatical errors throughout the paper, which detract from its professionalism and readability.

- The paper does not discuss the limitations of the proposed method, which is essential for understanding its applicability and potential drawbacks.
