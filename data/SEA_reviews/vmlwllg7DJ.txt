 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel method, GrowLength, aimed at accelerating the pretraining of large language models (LLMs) by progressively increasing the training length. This method starts with a short sequence length and gradually extends it to a longer one, leveraging the RoPE positional embedding mechanism. The approach is evaluated across various LLM sizes and sequence lengths, showing potential for reducing training time without significant performance degradation. The paper is praised for its clarity and simplicity, but criticized for its limited novelty and the lack of extensive experimental validation.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, clear, and easy to follow, with a clear motivation and a novel approach to reducing training time for LLMs.
- The proposed method, GrowLength, is simple yet effective, and shows potential for reducing training time without significant performance degradation.
- The paper includes a comprehensive set of experiments, demonstrating the method's effectiveness across various LLM sizes and sequence lengths.
- The method is compatible with existing acceleration methods, making it potentially useful for practitioners in the field.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed explanation of how the RoPE positional embedding mechanism contributes to the observed performance improvements.
- The method's effectiveness is limited to specific sequence lengths and model sizes, and it's unclear whether the method can be extended to other positional embedding methods or different model architectures.
- The paper's experimental validation is not extensive enough, and the results are sometimes inconsistent or not convincingly superior to existing methods.
- The paper's novelty is questioned, as the approach of progressively increasing sequence length during pretraining is similar to existing methods like FlashAttention.
- The paper lacks a detailed analysis of the computational and memory overheads associated with the method, which could be significant due to the need to train multiple models with varying sequence lengths.
- The paper does not provide sufficient empirical evidence to support claims of faster convergence rates and superior performance metrics.

**Questions:**
- Could you provide more details on how the RoPE positional embedding mechanism specifically contributes to the observed performance improvements?
- How does the method perform when applied to different positional embedding methods or other model architectures?
- Can you clarify the discrepancies in the performance metrics and experimental results, particularly the differences in training time and model performance between different sequence lengths?
- How does the method handle the computational and memory overheads associated with training multiple models with varying sequence lengths?
- Could you provide more empirical evidence, such as detailed training logs or additional experiments, to support the claims of faster convergence rates and superior performance metrics?
