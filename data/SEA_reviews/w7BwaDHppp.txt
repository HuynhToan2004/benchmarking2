 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel method for rendering unbounded scenes using a new mapping function and a modified ray parameterization. The method is designed to address the challenges of representing distant objects in unbounded scenes, which is a critical issue in traditional NeRF models. The authors propose a p-norm based mapping function and an angular ray parameterization to optimize the representation of distant objects. These modifications are aimed at improving the quality of novel view synthesis in unbounded scenes. The method is evaluated on various datasets and shown to outperform existing methods in certain scenarios. However, the paper lacks comprehensive comparisons with state-of-the-art methods and does not provide sufficient visual evidence to support its claims.

**Strengths:**
- The paper presents a novel approach to addressing the problem of unbounded NeRF by proposing a p-norm based mapping function and an angular ray parameterization.
- The methodology is clearly explained, and the paper is well-written, making it accessible and easy to follow.
- The proposed method shows improvements in rendering quality, particularly in distant objects, compared to traditional methods like inverted sphere and contract mapping.
- The paper provides a thorough analysis of existing methods and proposes a new mapping function that is adaptive to the scene geometry, which is a significant advancement in the field.
- The experimental results demonstrate that the proposed method outperforms several baseline methods, indicating its effectiveness.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks comprehensive comparisons with state-of-the-art methods, particularly in terms of computational efficiency and visual quality.
- The novelty of the method is questioned, as similar approaches have been previously explored, such as using p-norm in mapping functions and angular ray parameterization.
- The paper does not provide sufficient visual evidence to support the claims made, particularly in the results section, which could benefit from more detailed visualizations and comparisons.
- The paper does not adequately address the limitations of the proposed method, such as its applicability to scenes with varying object distributions and the potential for misalignment of p-norm values with actual object distances.
- The paper could benefit from a more detailed discussion on the limitations of the proposed method and how these could be addressed in future work.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors provide more detailed visualizations and comparisons to better illustrate the improvements in rendering quality, particularly in distant objects?
- How does the proposed method compare in terms of computational efficiency and memory requirements with other state-of-the-art methods?
- Could the authors clarify the novelty of the proposed method, especially in relation to existing works that have used similar approaches?
- How does the proposed method handle scenes with varying object distributions, and what are the potential implications for the accuracy of the p-norm based mapping function?
- Could the authors discuss the limitations of the proposed method and suggest potential avenues for future research to address these limitations?
