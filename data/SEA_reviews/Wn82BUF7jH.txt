 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel acceleration scheme for diffusion-based molecular conformation generation in SE(3)-invariant space, aiming to enhance the efficiency of existing methods like GeoDiff and SDDiff. It proposes a modified score function that incorporates the degree of nodes to improve computational efficiency and reduces the number of sampling steps. Theoretical analysis and empirical evidence support the effectiveness of the proposed method, which can generate high-quality conformations with significant speedup compared to existing methods. The paper also addresses the computational challenges in molecular conformation generation, a critical task in drug discovery and material science, by focusing on the acceleration of diffusion-based methods.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, with clear presentation and a clear motivation for the work.
- The authors have introduced a novel acceleration scheme for generating molecular conformations in SE(3)-invariant space, which is a significant contribution to the field.
- The proposed method can generate high-quality conformations with 50x-100x speedup compared to existing methods, demonstrating its practical utility.
- The paper provides theoretical analysis and empirical proof, which supports the effectiveness of the proposed method.
- The methodology is well-explained, making it accessible and understandable to readers.
- The proposed method is a simple modification to existing methods, which makes it easy to implement and use.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed comparison with other methods like EC-Conf, which could provide a more comprehensive evaluation of the proposed method.
- The theoretical analysis is not thoroughly explained, and some equations are not clearly defined or referenced, which could hinder understanding.
- The paper does not discuss the limitations of the proposed method, which is crucial for understanding its applicability and potential drawbacks.
- The paper does not provide a detailed analysis of the computational cost of the proposed method, which is important for assessing its efficiency.
- The paper does not include a comprehensive evaluation of the proposed method on larger datasets or more complex molecules, which could provide a more robust validation of the method.
- The paper does not discuss the potential negative societal impacts of the proposed method, which is an important consideration for ethical applications.

**Questions:**
- How does the proposed method compare to other existing methods like EC-Conf in terms of performance and efficiency?
- Can the authors provide a detailed analysis of the computational cost of the proposed method, including the time and memory requirements?
- How does the proposed method perform on larger datasets or more complex molecules, and what are the limitations in terms of scalability?
- Could the authors clarify the theoretical analysis, especially the definitions and roles of various parameters and functions mentioned in the equations?
- How is the hyperparameter k_n chosen, and what are the implications of this choice on the performance of the method?
- Can the authors provide a more detailed comparison with other diffusion-based methods, such as those mentioned in the paper, to better position their work within the existing literature?
