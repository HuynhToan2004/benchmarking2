 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel physics-aware spatiotemporal causal graph network (P-stCGN) aimed at integrating physics equations into spatiotemporal models without requiring precise knowledge of underlying principles. The approach leverages causal relationships derived from prior physics knowledge to learn causal weights from spatially close and temporally past observations, thereby enhancing forecasting performance. The paper evaluates the proposed method on real-world time series data, demonstrating improved forecasting accuracy compared to existing methods. However, the paper's novelty is questioned due to its reliance on existing techniques such as GCN and the lack of significant advancement in the field of physics-informed machine learning.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel approach by integrating physics equations into spatiotemporal models, which is significant for applications in various domains.
- The methodology is well-articulated, with clear explanations of the proposed approach and the experimental setup.
- The paper is well-written, making it easy to follow, and the authors provide a clear motivation for their work.
- The use of physics-aware causality for soft integration is innovative and potentially useful for improving forecasting performance in spatiotemporal modeling.
- The paper is well-structured, with a clear presentation of the proposed method and its applications, which makes it accessible to a broad audience.
- The experiments are conducted on real-world data, demonstrating the effectiveness of the proposed method in practical scenarios.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper's novelty is limited as it primarily combines existing techniques such as GCN with a causal learning framework, which has been explored in previous works.
- The methodology does not significantly advance the field of physics-informed machine learning, as it does not introduce new insights or methodologies.
- The paper lacks a thorough comparison with existing methods, particularly in terms of forecasting performance and the integration of physics-aware causality.
- The paper's experimental evaluation is limited to a single dataset, which does not adequately support the generalizability of the proposed method.
- The paper does not provide a detailed discussion on the limitations of the proposed method, which could affect its applicability in different scenarios.
- The paper could benefit from a more comprehensive discussion on the related work, especially in the context of physics-informed machine learning and causal discovery.
- The paper's claims about the integration of physics-aware causality and its impact on forecasting performance are not convincingly supported by the experimental results.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors provide more details on how the causal relations are derived from physics knowledge and how these relations are used to guide the learning process in the model?
- How does the proposed method handle scenarios where the physics-aware causality labels are not available or are only partially available?
- Can the authors clarify the role of the "causal module" in the model and how it contributes to the learning of causal weights?
- How does the proposed method compare with existing methods in terms of forecasting performance and the integration of physics-aware causality?
- Can the authors provide more details on the experimental setup, particularly the data generation process and the selection of baselines?
- How does the proposed method perform when applied to datasets with different characteristics, such as those with more complex spatiotemporal dynamics or different types of physical systems?
- Can the authors provide more details on the computational complexity and the scalability of the proposed method, especially in terms of its applicability to large-scale spatiotemporal datasets?
