 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel Transformer-based framework named PINNsFormer, designed to enhance the capabilities of Physics-Informed Neural Networks (PINNs) in capturing temporal dependencies in the solution of Partial Differential Equations (PDEs). The framework, PINNsFormer, utilizes a pseudo sequence generator to transform point-wise inputs into pseudo sequences, replacing point-wise PINNs loss with a sequential loss. It also incorporates a new activation function, Wavelet, and a novel learning scheme to improve the generalization and accuracy of PINNs. The paper presents several experiments that demonstrate the superiority of PINNsFormer over traditional PINNs and other baseline methods in handling failure modes and high-dimensional PDEs.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, easy to follow, and the proposed method is simple and straightforward.
- The proposed Wavelet activation function is novel and holds promise for the broader machine learning community.
- The experimental results demonstrate the superiority of the proposed method over traditional PINNs and other baseline methods in handling failure modes and high-dimensional PDEs.
- The paper introduces a novel Transformer-based framework named PINNsFormer, which is the first to explicitly focus on and learn temporal dependencies within PDEs.
- The new activation function, Wavelet, is designed to anticipate the Fourier Transform for arbitrary target signals, making it a universal approximator for infinite-width neural networks.
- The paper conducts comprehensive evaluations of PINNsFormer for various scenarios, demonstrating its advantages in optimization and approximation accuracy.
- The framework diagram is presented in Figure 1, providing a clear overview of the PINNsFormer architecture.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed comparison with other state-of-the-art methods, particularly those that also focus on temporal dependencies.
- The paper does not provide a detailed analysis of the computational overhead of the proposed method compared to traditional PINNs.
- The paper does not discuss the limitations of the proposed method, which is crucial for understanding its applicability and potential drawbacks.
- The paper does not include experiments on high-dimensional PDEs, which are crucial for demonstrating the scalability and effectiveness of the proposed method.
- The paper does not compare the proposed method with other methods that use different activation functions or different learning schemes, which could provide a more comprehensive evaluation of the proposed method.
- The paper lacks a detailed discussion on the choice of parameters such as the number of heads in the multi-head attention mechanism and the number of layers in the transformer, which could affect the performance and efficiency of the model.
