 **Summary:**
The paper explores the phenomenon of posterior collapse in variational autoencoders (VAEs), focusing on conditional VAEs (CVAEs) and hierarchical VAEs (HVAEs). It provides a theoretical analysis of the conditions that lead to posterior collapse in these models, emphasizing the role of learnable encoder variance and input-output correlations. The authors also investigate the effects of different hyperparameters such as beta values on the collapse behavior. The paper is structured around a theoretical analysis of global solutions and empirical validation through experiments on MNIST data, demonstrating the predictive nature of the insights derived from linear cases to non-linear settings. However, the paper's experimental scope is limited to MNIST, and the applicability of the findings to more complex datasets remains unclear.

**Strengths:**
- The paper provides a clear theoretical analysis of the conditions that lead to posterior collapse in conditional VAEs (CVAEs) and hierarchical VAEs (HVAEs), contributing to a better understanding of these models.
- The authors demonstrate the predictive nature of the insights derived from linear cases to non-linear settings, which is a significant strength in validating the theoretical findings.
- The paper is well-written, making it accessible and easy to follow, which is crucial for readers to understand the complex theoretical concepts presented.
- The study addresses a significant problem in VAEs, which is the occurrence of posterior collapse, and provides a novel theoretical perspective that could guide future research in this area.
- The paper is structured around a theoretical analysis of global solutions and empirical validation through experiments on MNIST data, which is a good approach to validate the theoretical insights.

**Weaknesses:**

- The paper's main theoretical contributions are limited to linear models, which might not fully capture the complexity of non-linear models.
- The paper's reliance on linear models for theoretical analysis might limit the practical applicability of the findings to more complex, non-linear models.
