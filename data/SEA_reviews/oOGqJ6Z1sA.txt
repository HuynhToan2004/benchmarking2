 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a new weighting method for estimating treatment effects in observational studies, focusing on the ATT and ATE. The method, referred to as WUNT, transforms the covariate distribution in the control group to a uniform distribution using a uniform transformer. This transformation allows for the estimation of the density of covariates in the treated group, which is used to construct weights for estimating the mean of the outcome variable. Theoretical results demonstrate the consistency and rate-optimality of the proposed estimator under certain conditions, and the paper includes numerical examples to support these claims. The approach is novel in its application of functional estimation to weighting methods and its handling of the ATT and ATE, although it assumes strong regularity conditions for the underlying model.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, with clear explanations and easy-to-follow proofs, making it accessible to readers.
- The authors provide a novel perspective on weighting methods, focusing on the ATT and ATE, which is an interesting approach.
- The paper is original in its application of functional estimation to weighting methods, which is a significant contribution to the field.
- The theoretical results are sound, and the paper provides a detailed explanation of the theoretical aspects of the proposed method.
- The paper includes a comprehensive literature review, which helps contextualize the current work within existing research.

**Weaknesses:**

- The paper does not adequately address the computational complexity of the proposed method, which could be a significant limitation in real-world applications.
