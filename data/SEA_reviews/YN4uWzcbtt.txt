 **Summary:**
The paper addresses the positivity of the Neural Tangent Kernel (NTK) in deep neural networks, focusing on the strict positive definiteness of the NTK under certain conditions. Specifically, it investigates the NTK of feedforward networks with non-polynomial activation functions, showing that the NTK is strictly positive definite for any depth and width, provided the training data points are distinct. This result generalizes previous findings by removing assumptions on the activation function and the data distribution. The paper also provides a proof of a new theorem (Theorem 3) that characterizes polynomial functions, which is of independent interest.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a new theorem (Theorem 3) that characterizes polynomial functions, which is of independent interest and could be valuable to the broader machine learning community.
- The proof of the main theorem is novel and interesting, providing a significant contribution to the field.
- The paper is well-written, with clear explanations and intuitive examples, making it accessible and easy to follow.
- The paper addresses a fundamental problem in deep learning, namely the positivity of the Neural Tangent Kernel (NTK), which is crucial for understanding the behavior of neural networks in the infinite-width limit.
