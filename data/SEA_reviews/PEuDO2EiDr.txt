 **Summary:**
The paper introduces RTFS-Net, a novel audio-visual speech separation method operating in the time-frequency domain, which models and captures time and frequency dimensions of audio independently using a multi-layered RNN along each dimension. This approach integrates audio and visual information using an attention-based fusion technique, aiming to outperform existing time-domain methods with fewer parameters and computational complexity. The method is evaluated on standard datasets and demonstrates superior performance compared to contemporary methods. However, concerns are raised about the clarity of the paper, the necessity of the proposed techniques, and the fairness of the comparisons made.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, with a clear structure and detailed explanations, making it easy to follow.
- The proposed method is technically sound and innovative, introducing a novel approach to audio-visual speech separation in the time-frequency domain.
- The method achieves state-of-the-art performance with fewer parameters and computational complexity, demonstrating efficiency and effectiveness.
- The experimental results are convincing, showing that the method outperforms existing methods in terms of performance and efficiency.
- The paper provides a comprehensive analysis of the proposed method and its components, contributing to a better understanding of the method's strengths and limitations.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper suffers from clarity issues, particularly in the introduction and some sections of the methodology, which could be improved for better reader comprehension.
- The motivation behind the proposed method is not clearly articulated, and the paper lacks a detailed discussion on the necessity and advantages of the time-frequency domain approach over other domains.
- The comparison with existing methods is not fair, as the proposed method uses more advanced techniques (like SRU) and does not adequately compare against other state-of-the-art methods.
- The paper lacks a detailed discussion on the limitations of the proposed method and potential negative societal impacts.
- The experimental results are not sufficiently detailed, particularly concerning the impact of different parameters on the performance and the effectiveness of the proposed method in different scenarios.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors clarify the motivation behind the proposed method and how it addresses specific challenges in audio-visual speech separation?
- How does the proposed method compare against other state-of-the-art methods in terms of performance and efficiency? Could the authors provide a more detailed comparison?
- In the ablation study, how does the performance of the method vary with different parameters, such as the number of RTFS blocks?
- Could the authors provide more detailed results on the performance of the method in different scenarios, such as noisy environments or with different types of audio-visual mixtures?
- How does the proposed method handle the potential societal impacts, particularly in terms of privacy and data security?
