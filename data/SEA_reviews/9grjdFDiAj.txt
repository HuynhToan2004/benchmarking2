 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel concept of "probabilistic stability" to enhance the understanding of Stochastic Gradient Descent (SGD) dynamics, particularly around saddle points. The authors propose that the convergence in probability of SGD to saddle points is not only possible but also likely, depending on the noise level and learning rate. They argue that this probabilistic stability is a significant departure from the traditional norm-based stability definitions, providing a new lens through which to view the behavior of SGD. The paper supports its claims with both theoretical analysis and empirical evidence, showing that SGD can converge to saddle points and that this behavior is influenced by various factors such as noise and learning rate. However, the paper's theoretical contributions are critiqued for their lack of depth and the paper's reliance on strong assumptions that may limit its applicability to more general cases.

- The theoretical analysis and empirical evidence presented in the paper are robust, supporting the claims made about the behavior of SGD.

**Weaknesses:**
