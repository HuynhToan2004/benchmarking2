 **Summary:**
The paper introduces TOSS, a novel method for generating novel views from a single RGB image using text-based conditioning. It builds upon the Zero123 framework, integrating a dense cross-attention mechanism to enhance the conditioning of images and camera poses. TOSS also utilizes a frozen text encoder from CLIP and expert denoisers to improve the accuracy and plausibility of the generated views. The method's effectiveness is demonstrated through experiments on datasets like GSO and RTMV, showing improvements over Zero123. However, concerns are raised about the method's novelty and the clarity of its contributions, particularly in distinguishing its improvements over Zero123.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel dense cross-attention module to condition the novel view generative process on input view image and relative camera pose, which is a significant contribution to the field.
- The use of textual guidance to constrain the solution space of novel view synthesis is innovative and shows potential for improving the plausibility and controllability of generated views.
- The methodology is well-articulated, with clear explanations of the proposed methods and their applications.
- The paper includes extensive experiments and ablation studies that demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed methods, showing improvements over existing methods like Zero123.
- The integration of textual inversion with the input view image and the use of expert denoisers for pose correctness and preservation of fine details are novel and promising contributions.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper does not adequately distinguish its contributions from those of Zero123, particularly in terms of the dense cross-attention module and the use of textual guidance.
- The paper lacks a comprehensive evaluation of the method's performance on a broader range of datasets and in different scenarios, which could strengthen the claims of its effectiveness.
- There is a lack of clarity in the presentation of the methodology, particularly in the explanation of the dense cross-attention module and its impact on the model's performance.
- The paper does not adequately address the limitations of the method, such as the reliance on textual descriptions which may not always be available or accurate.
- The method's novelty is questionable, as it heavily relies on existing methods like Zero123 and the use of textual guidance, which may not be seen as a significant advancement.

**Questions:**
- Could you clarify the specific contributions of the dense cross-attention module over Zero123's existing mechanisms?
- How does the textual guidance affect the model's performance, and can you provide more details on the ablation studies conducted?
- Is the method capable of handling scenarios where the input view image is not a clear representation of the object, such as low-resolution or heavily occluded images?
- How does the method perform when the textual description is inaccurate or not specific enough to the object in the image?
- Can you provide more details on the training and inference costs of the proposed method compared to Zero123?
- How does the method handle the potential for textual inversion to introduce new artifacts or inaccuracies in the generated views?
