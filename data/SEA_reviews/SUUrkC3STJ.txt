 **Summary:**
The paper introduces VCR-Graphormer, a novel graph transformer designed for mini-batch training, which assigns each node a token list based on personalized PageRank. This approach allows for efficient handling of large-scale graphs by focusing on relevant nodes and their neighbors. The methodology includes the introduction of virtual connections to enhance the model's ability to capture local and global contexts, long-range interactions, and heterophilous information. The paper provides empirical results showing the method's effectiveness in node classification tasks, although it has been noted for its incremental novelty and lack of extensive experimental validation.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel approach to graph transformers by using personalized PageRank for tokenization, which is a significant departure from traditional methods.
- The proposed method is well-motivated and theoretically grounded, providing a clear explanation of the method's effectiveness and its relationship to graph convolutional networks.
- The paper is well-written, making it accessible and easy to follow, which is crucial for a complex technical topic.
- The experimental results demonstrate the method's effectiveness in node classification tasks, showing that it outperforms some baselines.
- The paper addresses an important and relevant problem in the field of graph learning, focusing on the efficient handling of large-scale graphs, which is a critical issue in real-world applications.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a thorough comparison with existing methods, particularly in terms of computational complexity and efficiency. This makes it difficult to assess the true advantages of the proposed method.
- The novelty of the method is questioned, as it appears to be an incremental improvement over existing methods, particularly NAGphormer.
- The paper does not adequately address the scalability of the method to larger graphs, which is crucial for practical applications.
- The experimental setup and results are not convincing, with some results showing marginal improvements over existing methods, and others showing no improvement or even a decline in performance.
- The paper lacks a detailed discussion on the limitations of the proposed method, which is essential for understanding its applicability and potential drawbacks.
- There are several presentation issues, such as unclear notations and a lack of clarity in the experimental setup and results, which could hinder understanding and reproducibility of the research.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors provide a more detailed comparison with existing methods, particularly in terms of computational complexity and efficiency?
- How does the proposed method handle the scalability issues, especially when applied to larger graphs?
- Could the authors elaborate on the experimental setup and results, particularly the choice of baselines and the rationale behind the observed performance variations?
- What are the specific advantages of using personalized PageRank over other tokenization methods, and how does it contribute to the overall performance of the model?
- How does the method perform in terms of scalability and efficiency when applied to larger, more complex graphs?
- Can the authors discuss the potential limitations of the proposed method and how these might affect its practical applicability?
