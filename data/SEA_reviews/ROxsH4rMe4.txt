 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel architecture for accelerating Spiking Neural Networks (SNNs) using a systolic array, which includes techniques such as Split-Time Temporal coding (STT) and Integration Through Time (ITT). STT limits the number of spikes per time window and encodes temporal information, while ITT processes integration across different time windows in parallel. This architecture aims to improve data movement and reduce computational overhead, achieving significant improvements in latency and energy efficiency. The paper also explores the trade-off between machine learning performance and hardware accelerator performance, demonstrating a 77x and 60x latency and energy efficiency improvement on average over a conventional SNN baseline.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel architecture for accelerating Spiking Neural Networks (SNNs) using a systolic array, which includes techniques such as Split-Time Temporal coding (STT) and Integration Through Time (ITT).
- The proposed architecture delivers 77x and 60x latency and energy efficiency improvements on average over a conventional SNN baseline on different benchmarks.
- The paper is well-written, with clear explanations of the proposed methods and their implementation in hardware.
- The proposed techniques, such as STT and ITT, are designed to be universally applicable for accelerating spiking models, with flexibility in choosing the TW size.
- The evaluation of the proposed techniques is comprehensive, including a trade-off analysis between machine learning performance and hardware accelerator performance.
- The paper provides a detailed explanation of the proposed methods, including the STT and ITT techniques, which are expected to be useful in the field of SNN acceleration.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a thorough analysis of the energy efficiency of the proposed architecture, particularly in terms of the energy cost of the systolic array.
- There is a lack of discussion on the impact of the proposed techniques on the accuracy of the model, especially in comparison to other models that do not use these techniques.
- The paper does not provide a detailed comparison of the proposed architecture with other existing SNN accelerators, which limits the understanding of its advantages and disadvantages.
- The paper does not provide a detailed discussion on the limitations of the proposed techniques, such as the impact on model accuracy and the scalability of the architecture to larger, more complex networks.
- The paper does not discuss the hardware cost implications of the proposed architecture, which is crucial for practical implementation.
