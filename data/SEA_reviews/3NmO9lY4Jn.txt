 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel method for generating minority samples from diffusion models, addressing the challenge of diffusion models' inherent bias towards majority samples. The authors propose a metric based on Tweedie's formula to measure the uniqueness of samples and introduce a sampling technique that conditions on this metric to guide the generation process towards minority samples. The method is evaluated on various datasets, demonstrating its effectiveness in generating minority samples. However, concerns are raised regarding the clarity of the motivation, the theoretical justification of the method, and the experimental setup. The paper's presentation and the depth of theoretical analysis are criticized, with suggestions for clearer writing and a more rigorous experimental validation.

**Strengths:**
- The paper addresses an interesting and relevant problem in the field of generating minority samples, which is crucial for various applications such as medical diagnosis and fairness in AI.
- The proposed method is simple, intuitive, and effective, as demonstrated by the results presented.
- The paper is well-written, making it easy to follow and understand, which is crucial for a broad audience.
- The experiments conducted on various datasets, including medical imaging, demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed method.
- The use of Tweedie's formula to measure the uniqueness of samples is innovative and provides a novel approach to the problem.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a clear motivation and explanation for the proposed method, particularly in how it addresses the bias towards majority samples and the rationale behind the use of Tweedie's formula.
- The theoretical justification of the method is insufficient, with the paper not adequately explaining the connection between the Tweedie formula and the generation of minority samples.
- The experimental setup is not comprehensive enough, with only a few datasets used, and the results are not convincingly superior to existing methods.
- There is a lack of discussion on the limitations of the proposed method, which is essential for understanding its applicability and potential drawbacks.
- The paper could benefit from more detailed explanations and examples, particularly in the methodology section, to aid in understanding the proposed approach.
- The paper's claims about the bias of Tweedie's formula towards majority samples are not convincingly supported by empirical evidence.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors clarify the motivation behind the proposed method and how it addresses the bias towards majority samples?
- How does the proposed method compare to existing methods in terms of computational efficiency and practical applicability, especially in medical imaging applications?
- Can the authors provide more detailed examples and explanations of how the method works, particularly in the methodology section?
- How does the proposed method perform in different datasets, especially those that are not used in the current experiments?
- Can the authors discuss the potential limitations of the proposed method and how these might affect its practical application?
- How is the minority score defined, and what does it represent in the context of the proposed method?
- Can the authors provide more details on the experimental setup, such as the specifics of the datasets used and the parameters of the diffusion models?
