 **Summary:**
The paper investigates the impact of data distribution shifts on cross-modal knowledge distillation (KD), proposing a novel method called the "perceptual solution space mask" (PSSM) to address these issues. The authors argue that the effectiveness of cross-modal KD is not solely determined by the accuracy of the teacher model but rather by the data distribution shifts between modalities. This hypothesis is supported by experimental results on four multimodal datasets, demonstrating the effectiveness of PSSM in reducing the negative effects of data distribution shifts. The paper also discusses the theoretical underpinnings of cross-modal KD and presents a comprehensive analysis of its effectiveness.

**Strengths:**
- The paper provides a comprehensive analysis of the effectiveness of cross-modal knowledge distillation (KD) and its dependence on data distribution shifts in multimodal data.
- The proposed method, the perceptual solution space mask (PSSM), is novel and effectively addresses the issue of data distribution shifts in cross-modal KD.
- The paper is well-written, with clear and concise language, making it accessible and easy to understand.
- The experimental results are convincing, showing that the proposed method outperforms existing methods in certain scenarios.
- The paper introduces a novel hypothesis, the Solution Space Divergence Hypothesis (SSDH), which helps explain the effectiveness of cross-modal KD and its dependence on data distribution shifts.
- The paper includes comprehensive experiments that validate the proposed method and demonstrate its effectiveness in improving cross-modal KD.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed comparison with other existing methods in the field, which could help in understanding the novelty and effectiveness of the proposed method.
- The theoretical analysis is not convincing, particularly in the proof of Assumption 1, which is critical for the validity of the proposed hypothesis.
- The experimental results, while convincing in some aspects, are not comprehensive enough, especially in terms of the datasets used and the scope of the experiments.
- The paper's claims about the impact of data distribution shifts on cross-modal KD are not convincingly supported by the experimental results.
- There is a lack of discussion on the limitations of the proposed method, which is crucial for understanding its applicability and effectiveness in various scenarios.
- The paper does not adequately address the issue of noisy or incorrect labels in the student model, which could affect the performance of the proposed method.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors clarify the theoretical proof of Assumption 1 and provide more detailed explanations or additional evidence to support its validity?
- How does the proposed method compare with other existing methods in terms of effectiveness and computational efficiency?
- Can the authors provide more detailed experimental results, including a broader range of datasets and scenarios, to validate the effectiveness of the proposed method more comprehensively?
- How does the proposed method address the issue of noisy or incorrect labels in the student model, and what are the implications of this for the overall performance of the distillation process?
- Could the authors discuss the limitations of their method and provide insights into potential future research directions to address these limitations?
