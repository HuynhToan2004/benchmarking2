 **Summary:**
The paper introduces HelmSim, a novel fluid simulator that leverages Helmholtz decomposition to decompose fluid dynamics into curl-free and divergence-free parts, enhancing the interpretability and predictability of fluid dynamics. This method, termed HelmDynamic, is integrated into a Multiscale Integration Network to predict future fluid states. The paper claims that HelmSim outperforms existing methods in both simulated and real-world data, showcasing its effectiveness and practical utility. However, the paper faces criticism for its lack of clarity in explaining the Helmholtz decomposition and its relevance to the specific fluid dynamics studied. The methodology and its practical implications, particularly in terms of computational efficiency and real-world applicability, are also questioned.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel approach to fluid simulation by using Helmholtz decomposition, which is both innovative and relevant to the field.
- The methodology is well-explained, with clear visualizations and detailed explanations of the HelmDynamic block and the Multiscale Integration Network.
- The paper demonstrates strong results across multiple datasets and benchmarks, showcasing the effectiveness of the proposed method.
- The integration of Helmholtz dynamics into the simulation process is a significant advancement in the field, offering a new perspective on fluid dynamics modeling.
- The paper is well-written, making it accessible and easy to follow, which is crucial for a technical paper.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks clarity in explaining the Helmholtz decomposition and its relevance to the specific fluid dynamics studied, which could confuse readers unfamiliar with the topic.
- The paper does not adequately address the computational efficiency of the proposed method compared to existing methods, which is a critical factor in practical applications.
- The methodology section is unclear in some areas, particularly the role and function of the HelmDynamic block and the Multiscale Integration Network.
- There is a lack of detailed discussion on the limitations of the proposed method and its potential negative societal impacts, which are crucial for a comprehensive evaluation.
- The paper does not sufficiently compare the proposed method with existing methods in terms of accuracy, computational efficiency, and real-world applicability, which could undermine the claims of its superiority.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors clarify the relevance and application of Helmholtz decomposition in the specific fluid dynamics studied in the paper?
- How does the proposed method compare in terms of computational efficiency and practical applicability to existing methods, especially in real-world scenarios?
- Could the authors provide more detailed explanations of the HelmDynamic block and the Multiscale Integration Network, possibly with additional visualizations or examples?
- Is there a plan to extend the method to 3D fluid simulation, and if so, what are the anticipated challenges and potential solutions?
- How does the proposed method handle complex boundary conditions, and what are the implications for its practical application in real-world scenarios?
- Can the authors discuss the potential limitations and negative societal impacts of their proposed method, and how these might be addressed or mitigated?
