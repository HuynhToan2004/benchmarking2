 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel method, Text2Data, for text-to-data generation, specifically designed for low-resource scenarios where text-data pairs are limited. The approach leverages an unsupervised diffusion model to understand the underlying data distribution from unlabeled data, followed by finetuning on text-labeled data using a constraint optimization-based learning objective to ensure controllability and mitigate catastrophic forgetting. The method is evaluated across various modalities including molecules, motions, and time series, demonstrating enhanced performance in terms of both generation quality and controllability compared to existing baselines. However, the paper has been critiqued for its unclear presentation, lack of detailed experimental setups, and insufficient comparison with other state-of-the-art methods.

**Strengths:**
- The paper addresses the problem of text-to-data generation in low-resource scenarios, a significant and relevant topic in the field.
- The proposed method is innovative, utilizing an unsupervised diffusion model to understand the underlying data distribution and a novel constraint optimization-based learning objective to ensure controllability.
- The experiments conducted are comprehensive, covering various modalities including molecules, motions, and time series, and demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed method.
- The theoretical validation provided adds depth to the paper, enhancing its credibility.
- The paper is well-written and easy to follow, making it accessible to a broad audience.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks clarity in its presentation, particularly in the methodology section, which could benefit from clearer explanations and potentially the use of figures to aid understanding.
- The experimental setup and details are not sufficiently provided, making it difficult to replicate or fully understand the results.
- The comparison with other state-of-the-art methods is insufficient, which limits the understanding of the method's relative performance.
- There is a lack of discussion on the limitations and potential negative societal impacts of the proposed method.
- The paper does not sufficiently address how the method handles complex data structures or the potential for overfitting, which are critical concerns in low-resource scenarios.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors clarify the specifics of the diffusion model used in the unsupervised stage and how it is integrated with the text-labeled data in the supervised stage?
- How does the method ensure that the parameters remain in close proximity to those established during the unsupervised stage, as mentioned in the text?
- Can the authors provide more details on the experimental setup, particularly how the data is split into training and testing sets and how the results are validated?
- How does the method perform when applied to more complex data structures, such as those found in image or video data?
- Could the authors discuss the potential negative societal impacts of the proposed method and how these might be mitigated?
- In light of the concerns about overfitting and the use of unlabeled data, how does the method address these issues, and what steps are taken to prevent overfitting?
