 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel recurrent-based reconstruction framework for spike cameras, focusing on low-light environments. It proposes a light-robust representation (LR-Rep) to aggregate temporal information in spike streams, which is then used to extract temporal features. The framework is evaluated on both synthetic and real datasets, demonstrating superior performance compared to existing methods. The paper also introduces a new dataset, RLLR, to train the model and evaluates it against existing benchmarks. Despite the promising results, concerns are raised about the novelty of the approach, the clarity of the methodology, and the generalizability of the findings to real-world scenarios.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, making it easy to follow, and the proposed method is well-motivated and well-explained.
- The experimental results demonstrate the superiority of the proposed method over existing methods, showing a significant improvement in performance.
- The proposed dataset, RLLR, is a valuable contribution to the field, and the methodology is solid, with a focus on the reconstruction problem for spike cameras.
- The paper includes a user study to assess the quality of reconstructed images, providing a comprehensive evaluation of the proposed method.
- The use of a recurrent-based reconstruction framework is a novel approach that effectively handles different light conditions.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed comparison with state-of-the-art methods, particularly in terms of computational efficiency and real-time performance.
- The novelty of the proposed method is questionable, as it appears to be a combination of existing techniques without significant innovation.
- The methodology is not clearly described, making it difficult to understand the technical contributions and the rationale behind certain design choices.
- The paper does not adequately address the generalizability of the findings to real-world scenarios, which could limit the practical applicability of the proposed method.
- There are issues with the clarity and presentation of the paper, including grammatical errors and unclear figures and captions.
- The paper lacks a detailed discussion on the limitations of the proposed method, which is crucial for understanding its applicability and potential drawbacks.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors provide a more detailed comparison with state-of-the-art methods, including computational efficiency and real-time performance?
- How does the proposed method perform in real-world scenarios, particularly in terms of generalizability and practical applicability?
- Can the authors clarify the technical contributions of the proposed method and provide a more detailed description of the methodology?
- How does the proposed method handle noise in spike cameras, and what are the implications of this for the accuracy and reliability of the reconstructed images?
- Could the authors provide more detailed results on the number of continuous spike streams used in the experiments and their impact on performance?
- How does the proposed method compare to other existing methods in terms of computational complexity and training requirements?
