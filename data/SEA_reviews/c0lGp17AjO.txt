 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel loss function based on the Wasserstein-2 distance for reconstructing stochastic differential equations (SDEs) from noisy data. It derives theoretical bounds for the Wasserstein distance between the distributions of solutions to different SDEs, providing a practical application in the reconstruction of SDEs. The proposed method is demonstrated through numerical experiments to outperform other loss functions in terms of efficiency and accuracy in SDE reconstruction. The paper also discusses the computational challenges and potential applications in various fields, such as computational finance and physics.

**Strengths:**
- The paper provides a theoretical analysis of the squared Wasserstein-2 distance between two probability distributions associated with two stochastic differential equations (SDEs), which is novel and interesting.
- The authors have derived an upper bound for the W2 distance between solutions to two SDEs, given the same initial condition, and have shown that this distance can be approximated by estimating the W2 distance between their finite-dimensional projections.
- The proposed method is demonstrated to be more efficient than other loss functions in terms of computational cost and accuracy in SDE reconstruction.
- The paper is well-written, with clear exposition and a logical structure that makes it easy to follow.
- The use of Wasserstein distance in SDE reconstruction is a promising approach that could be beneficial for uncertainty quantification.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper is limited to the univariate case, which might not generalize well to higher-dimensional SDEs.
- The theoretical analysis is not comprehensive, and the proofs are not detailed enough, especially in the proof of Theorem 1.
- The computational cost of the proposed method is higher than other methods, which might limit its practical application.
- The paper does not adequately address the limitations of the proposed method, such as its applicability to high-dimensional SDEs and the potential impact of computational costs on its practical use.
- The paper could benefit from a more detailed discussion on the assumptions made in the theoretical analysis and how they affect the applicability of the results.
- The paper lacks a thorough comparison with existing methods, especially those that use different loss functions or approaches for SDE reconstruction.
