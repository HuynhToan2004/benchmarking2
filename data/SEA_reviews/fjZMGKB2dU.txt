 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel neuro-symbolic framework for answering arbitrary conjunctive queries over incomplete knowledge graphs. It proposes a method that approximates cyclic queries using an infinite family of tree-like queries, thereby leveraging existing models for tree-like queries. The framework is complete and optimal, ensuring no false negatives and providing the best possible approximation using tree-like queries. It is evaluated against the GNN-QE baseline, showing competitive results. However, the paper is critiqued for its lack of theoretical analysis, limited experimental evaluation, and insufficient comparison with existing methods. The approach, while innovative, does not significantly outperform existing methods, and the experimental results are not convincing enough to support the claims made.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, with clear explanations of concepts and methodologies, making it accessible to readers.
- The proposed method is novel and innovative, addressing the challenges of answering cyclic queries over incomplete knowledge graphs.
- The paper includes a comprehensive theoretical analysis and proofs, which support the claims made about the completeness and optimality of the proposed method.
- The experimental results demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed method, showing improvements over the baseline GNN-QE in certain scenarios.
- The approach is flexible and adaptable, allowing for the handling of unanchored tree-like queries, which is a significant advancement in the field.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a thorough theoretical analysis, particularly in terms of the computational complexity and scalability of the proposed method.
- The experimental evaluation is limited, with only one baseline compared, and the results are not convincing enough to support the claims made about the effectiveness of the method.
- There is a lack of discussion on the limitations of the proposed method, which could help in understanding its practical applicability and potential drawbacks.
- The paper does not adequately compare the proposed method with existing methods, which could have provided a clearer picture of its advantages and disadvantages.
- The paper suffers from minor presentation issues, such as unclear figures and a lack of clarity in some sections, which could hinder understanding.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors provide more detailed theoretical analysis on the computational complexity and scalability of the proposed method?
- How does the proposed method compare with other existing methods for answering cyclic queries over incomplete knowledge graphs?
- Could the authors elaborate on the practical implications of the proposed method, particularly in terms of its scalability and applicability in real-world scenarios?
- Why were only two datasets used for evaluation, and could the authors consider including more diverse datasets to validate the method's effectiveness across different domains?
- How does the proposed method handle the integration of existing neuro-symbolic models, and what are the potential challenges and benefits of such integration?
