 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel computational framework for solving Wasserstein Lagrangian flows, which are formulated as a Lagrangian action minimization problem on the density manifold. This framework generalizes the optimal transport problem by allowing the definition of a Lagrangian with kinetic and potential energies, and it utilizes a dual objective involving cotangent vectors. The method is tested on various datasets, including single-cell RNA-seq data, and is shown to outperform existing methods in some cases. The framework is based on a deep learning approach that optimizes the action functional and employs a Hamiltonian flow to model the dynamics of the system.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel approach to solve the Wasserstein Lagrangian flow problem, which is a significant advancement in the field of optimal transport.
- The methodology is well-explained, making it accessible and understandable to readers, and the paper provides a detailed theoretical analysis of the proposed method.
- The computational efficiency of the method is highlighted, as it avoids the need for simulating or backpropagating through the trajectories of the learned dynamics, which is beneficial for practical applications.
- The paper demonstrates the versatility of the proposed framework by applying it to various problems, including single-cell trajectory inference, and shows that it can outperform existing methods in some scenarios.
- The paper is well-written, with clear explanations of the mathematical concepts and a comprehensive review of related work, which positions the research effectively within the current literature.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a clear demonstration of the advantages of the proposed method over existing methods, particularly in terms of computational efficiency and performance.
- The experimental results presented in the paper do not convincingly show that the proposed method is superior to existing methods, and the improvement over baselines is not significant in some cases.
- The paper does not adequately address the issue of discontinuous interpolation in the method, which could affect the accuracy of the results.
- There is a lack of discussion on the limitations of the proposed method, which is crucial for understanding the scope and applicability of the research.
- The paper could benefit from a more detailed discussion on the computational complexity and the scalability of the method, especially in high-dimensional datasets.
- The paper could be improved by including more detailed experimental results, such as plots of the learned vector fields and comparisons with more recent and relevant baselines.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors clarify how the proposed method handles discontinuous interpolation and discuss any potential impacts on the results?
- How does the method perform when applied to datasets with high-dimensional data, and what are the computational implications in such scenarios?
- Could the authors provide more detailed experimental results, such as plots of the learned vector fields and comparisons with more recent and relevant baselines?
- Is there a possibility to include a discussion on the limitations of the proposed method and its applicability in different scenarios?
- Can the authors provide a more detailed explanation of the computational complexity and the scalability of the method in high-dimensional datasets?
