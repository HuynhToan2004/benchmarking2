 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel brain-inspired convolution method, termed $L_p$-convolution, which employs $L_p$-masks derived from multivariate p-generalized normal distribution to mimic the receptive field shapes of biological neurons. The authors argue that this method bridges the gap between artificial and biological receptive fields, aiming to improve the performance of large kernel CNNs in various vision tasks. The methodology is supported by experiments on the Sudoku challenge and vision classification tasks, showing that $L_p$-convolution with biological RFs significantly enhances model performance. However, the paper's contributions are questioned due to the limited novelty of the proposed method and the lack of a thorough comparison with existing methods. The experiments, while showing some improvements, are not convincing enough to demonstrate significant benefits over traditional convolutions.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel method for generating structured sparsity based on the multivariate p-generalized normal distribution, which is a significant contribution to the field.
- The paper is well-written, with clear figures and a clear motivation, making it easy to follow and understand.
- The proposed $L_p$-convolution method shows promising results in improving the performance of large kernel CNNs in vision classification tasks.
- The experiments are conducted on both the Sudoku challenge and vision classification tasks, providing a comprehensive evaluation of the method's effectiveness.
- The paper is well-organized, with clear explanations of the methodology and its implementation, which enhances the reproducibility of the results.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a thorough comparison with existing methods, particularly in terms of computational complexity and performance on larger datasets.
- The experiments are not convincing enough to demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed method, especially in terms of its impact on performance and its comparison to traditional convolutions.
- The paper does not provide sufficient details on the optimization of the parameters $C$ and $p$ in the $L_p$-convolution, which is crucial for understanding the method's effectiveness.
- The paper does not adequately address the scalability of the proposed method, particularly in terms of its applicability to larger, more complex datasets.
- The paper does not sufficiently discuss the limitations of the proposed method, which is crucial for understanding its practical applicability and potential drawbacks.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors provide more details on the optimization of the parameters $C$ and $p$ in the $L_p$-convolution, including how these parameters are optimized and what impacts they have on the performance of the model?
- How does the proposed method compare to other methods that also use structured sparsity in convolutional neural networks?
- What are the specific advantages of using the multivariate p-generalized normal distribution over other distributions for modeling receptive fields?
- Can the authors provide more details on the computational complexity of the proposed method, particularly in comparison to traditional convolutions?
- How does the proposed method perform on larger, more complex datasets, and what are the implications of scaling up the method to larger datasets?
- Can the authors provide more details on the limitations of the proposed method, particularly in terms of its practical applicability and potential drawbacks?
