 **Summary:**
The paper introduces AdjointDPM, a novel method for gradient backpropagation in diffusion probabilistic models (DPMs) that utilizes the adjoint sensitivity method to compute gradients of the loss with respect to model parameters, including network weights, text prompts, and intermediate noisy states. This method allows for the efficient computation of gradients by solving backward ODEs, which significantly reduces memory consumption compared to naive gradient backpropagation. AdjointDPM is applied to various tasks such as guided sampling, security auditing, and stylization, demonstrating its effectiveness and versatility. The paper also explores the use of exponential integration to simplify the ODEs, enhancing computational efficiency.

**Strengths:**
- The paper presents a novel approach to gradient backpropagation in diffusion models, which is a significant contribution to the field.
- The method is well-motivated and the paper is well-written, making it accessible and easy to understand.
- The experiments demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed method, particularly in tasks such as vocabulary expansion and security auditing.
- The use of exponential integration to simplify the ODEs is a clever application that enhances the computational efficiency of the method.
- The paper is original in its application of the adjoint sensitivity method to diffusion models, which is a first in this context.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper could benefit from a more detailed discussion on the limitations of the method, particularly in terms of its applicability to different types of diffusion models and the scalability of the method to larger, more complex models.
- The presentation of the paper could be improved, particularly in the introduction and related works sections, which are currently lacking in detail and clarity.
- The method's reliance on the semi-linear structure of the diffusion ODEs may limit its applicability to other types of ODEs, which could be a significant drawback.
- The paper lacks a comprehensive comparison with existing methods, particularly in terms of computational efficiency and memory consumption.
- The paper could benefit from more detailed explanations and examples in the background sections, particularly in the application of the adjoint sensitivity method to neural ODEs.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors clarify the term "NFE" used in the paper and provide a brief explanation for readers unfamiliar with the term?
- How does the method perform when applied to different types of diffusion models, such as those that do not have a semi-linear structure?
- Could the authors provide more details on the computational efficiency of the method, particularly in terms of the number of steps required for solving the ODEs and the memory consumption?
- Is there a possibility to extend the method to other types of ODEs, and if so, how would this be achieved?
- How does the method compare to other gradient backpropagation methods for DPMs in terms of computational efficiency and memory consumption?
- Can the authors provide more details on the optimization process used in the experiments, particularly in the vocabulary expansion and security auditing tasks?
- Could the authors provide more examples and explanations in the background sections to better illustrate the application of the adjoint sensitivity method to neural ODEs?
