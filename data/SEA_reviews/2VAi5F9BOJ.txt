 **Summary:**
The paper introduces Prompt Learning with Perplexity (PLPP), a method aimed at enhancing the comprehensibility of prompts in vision-language models, particularly in the context of few-shot learning. PLPP utilizes a perplexity metric to guide the training of learnable prompts, aiming to improve the interpretability and effectiveness of the prompts. The methodology involves a simple linear layer to output word probability distributions, which are then used to calculate perplexity. Despite the novel approach, the paper has been critiqued for its limited novelty and the lack of significant improvements over existing methods like CoOp. The experimental results, while showing some improvements, are not convincingly superior to state-of-the-art methods.

**Strengths:**
- The paper addresses an important problem in the field of prompt learning for vision-language models, focusing on the comprehensibility of prompts which is a significant issue in current models.
- The introduction of perplexity as a metric for prompt learning is innovative and provides a novel approach to improving the interpretability of prompts.
- The methodology is straightforward and well-explained, making it accessible and understandable to readers.
- The paper includes comprehensive experiments across various settings, demonstrating the effectiveness of the proposed method.
- The use of perplexity as a metric to guide prompt learning is an interesting and potentially impactful contribution to the field.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a clear motivation for the use of perplexity as a metric for prompt learning, and the connection between perplexity and comprehensibility is not convincingly established.
- The improvements in performance over existing methods like CoOp are not substantial, and the paper does not adequately demonstrate the superiority of its method over state-of-the-art techniques.
- The paper's experimental design and results presentation are confusing and not well-organized, which makes it difficult for readers to understand the key findings and contributions.
- There is a lack of discussion on the limitations and potential negative societal impacts of the proposed method.
- The paper does not sufficiently address the computational efficiency and scalability of the proposed method, which could be a significant concern in practical applications.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors provide more detailed explanations or examples to clarify the connection between perplexity and comprehensibility?
- How does the proposed method compare to other state-of-the-art techniques in terms of computational efficiency and scalability?
- Can the authors provide more insights into the limitations and potential negative societal impacts of their method?
- Why was the decision made to use perplexity as the primary metric for prompt learning, and how does this metric translate into improved comprehensibility of the prompts?
- How does the proposed method handle the issue of incomprehensible prompts, especially in scenarios where the model might still output nonsensical prompts despite improved perplexity?
- Could the authors provide more detailed experimental results, possibly including a table summarizing the performance improvements across different datasets and settings?
