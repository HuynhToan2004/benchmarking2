 **Summary:**
The paper introduces OTMatch, a novel semi-supervised learning method that leverages optimal transport (OT) to enhance the alignment of pseudo-labels with true labels. This approach aims to improve upon existing methods like FixMatch and FlexMatch by integrating OT into the loss function, which is shown to outperform these methods on various datasets including CIFAR-10, CIFAR-100, and ImageNet. The methodology involves the use of a cost matrix derived from the classification head weights, which is used to optimize the alignment of pseudo-labels with true labels. The paper also includes a theoretical analysis and empirical results that demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed method.

**Strengths:**
- The paper presents a novel approach to semi-supervised learning by incorporating optimal transport (OT) into the loss function, which is a significant advancement in the field.
- The methodology is well-explained, making it easy to follow and understand, and the paper provides a clear theoretical analysis of the OT framework.
- The empirical results demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed method, showing improvements over existing state-of-the-art methods on various datasets.
- The paper is well-written and provides a comprehensive analysis of the OT framework, which is a promising direction for future research in semi-supervised learning.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a thorough discussion on the computational complexity of the OT loss compared to existing methods, which could be crucial for practical implementation.
- There is a need for more detailed comparisons with other methods that use OT in semi-supervised learning, such as those mentioned in references [1] and [2].
- The paper does not provide sufficient implementation details, particularly regarding the integration of the OT loss with existing methods like FreeMatch, which could hinder reproducibility.
- The paper does not include a discussion on the limitations of the proposed method, which is essential for a complete understanding of its applicability and effectiveness.
- The paper could benefit from a more detailed analysis of the cost matrix and its impact on the performance of the method.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors provide a more detailed comparison of the computational complexity of the OT loss with existing methods?
- How does the proposed method compare with other OT-based methods mentioned in references [1] and [2]?
- Could the authors provide more implementation details, particularly regarding the integration of the OT loss with FreeMatch?
- What are the limitations of the proposed method, and how do these impact its applicability and effectiveness?
- Could the authors provide more detailed results on the performance of the method with varying hyperparameters, particularly the momentum coefficient of the cost update?
