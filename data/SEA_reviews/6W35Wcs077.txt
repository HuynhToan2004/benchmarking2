 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel method for image restoration, leveraging Singular Value Decomposition (SVD) to optimize singular vectors and singular values separately. This approach is claimed to improve performance in various image restoration tasks such as deblurring, denoising, and deraining. The methodology involves the use of two operators, SVEO and SVAO, which are designed to optimize singular vectors and singular values, respectively. The paper also includes extensive experimental validations and ablation studies, demonstrating the effectiveness of the proposed method. However, the paper has been critiqued for its complex presentation, lack of clarity in methodological explanations, and insufficient comparative analysis with existing methods.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, clear, and easy to follow, making it accessible to a broad audience.
- The proposed method is novel and innovative, utilizing singular value decomposition (SVD) in a unique way for image restoration, which is not commonly explored.
- The paper includes extensive experiments and ablation studies that demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed method, showing superior performance over existing methods in some cases.
- The method is computationally efficient, which is crucial for real-world applications.
- The paper provides a thorough analysis of the problem and proposes a novel solution, which is supported by theoretical justification and empirical evidence.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a clear explanation of the methodology, particularly the roles and operations of the SVEO and SVAO operators, which are critical to understanding the proposed method.
- The presentation of the paper is complex and could be improved by simplifying the language and providing clearer visual aids.
- There is a lack of comparison with existing methods, particularly in terms of computational complexity and inference time, which is crucial for evaluating the practical efficiency of the proposed method.
- The paper does not adequately address the limitations of the proposed method, which could affect the reliability and generalizability of the results.
- The paper suffers from several grammatical and spelling errors, which detract from its professionalism and readability.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors clarify the roles and operations of the SVEO and SVAO operators, particularly how they optimize singular vectors and singular values, respectively?
- How does the proposed method compare in terms of computational complexity and inference time with existing methods, especially in large-scale applications?
- What are the specific advantages of using SVD for image restoration over other methods, such as Fourier transform or wavelet transform?
- How does the method handle different types of degradations, such as rain, noise, and blur, which may require different approaches?
- Can the authors provide more detailed comparisons with existing methods, including a head-to-head comparison of performance metrics and computational efficiency?
- How does the method perform when the training data is limited, and what are the implications for the generalizability of the results?
