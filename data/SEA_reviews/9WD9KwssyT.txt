 **Summary:**
The paper introduces Zipformer, a novel ASR encoder architecture that builds upon the Conformer model, aiming to enhance efficiency and performance. Key modifications include a U-Net-like structure for downsampling at different rates, a new block structure with more modules, a modified LayerNorm called BiasNorm, and a new optimizer called ScaledAdam. These changes aim to improve the model's speed, memory efficiency, and performance on benchmarks like LibriSpeech, Aishell-1, and WenetSpeech. Despite its promising results, the paper has been criticized for its lack of novelty, unclear explanations of certain components, and insufficient comparisons with other state-of-the-art models.

**Strengths:**
- The paper presents a comprehensive ablation study, effectively demonstrating the contributions of each component of the proposed Zipformer model.
- The Zipformer model, with its U-Net-like structure and modified block structure, shows improvements in both speed and performance, which is highlighted by the results on the LibriSpeech dataset.
- The paper provides a detailed explanation of the proposed modifications, including the ScaledAdam optimizer and the BiasNorm layer, which are expected to have broader applicability beyond the specific ASR task.
- The experimental results are robust, showing significant improvements over the baseline Conformer model, particularly in terms of speed and performance.
- The paper is well-written, making it easy to follow and understand, and the proposed modifications are well-motivated and justified.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a clear explanation of the new block structure and its components, such as the Bypass module and the Non-Linear Attention module, which are critical to understanding the model's operation.
- The novelty of the Zipformer model is questioned, as it appears to be a combination of existing techniques without significant new insights or methodologies.
- There is a lack of detailed comparative analysis with other state-of-the-art models, such as the ESPnet Transformer, which limits the evaluation of the model's effectiveness and efficiency.
- The paper does not discuss the limitations of the proposed model, which is crucial for understanding its applicability and potential drawbacks.
- The paper does not include a detailed discussion on the computational complexity of the Zipformer model, which is crucial for understanding its efficiency.
- The paper lacks a detailed comparison of the training and inference times of the Zipformer model, which is essential for assessing its practical applicability.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors provide a detailed explanation of the new block structure, including the Bypass module and the Non-Linear Attention module?
- How does the Zipformer model compare in terms of computational complexity and efficiency with other state-of-the-art models, such as the ESPnet Transformer?
- Could the authors clarify the role and impact of the Eden learning rate schedule on the model's performance?
- How does the Zipformer model handle the handling of the bypass connection in the Bypass module, and what are the implications of this design choice?
- Can the authors provide more details on the training and inference times of the Zipformer model, and how do these compare with other models like the Conformer and ESPnet Transformer?
- Why was the decision made to use the U-Net-like structure, and how does it contribute to the model's efficiency and performance?
- Could the authors discuss the limitations of the proposed model and suggest areas for future research or improvement?
