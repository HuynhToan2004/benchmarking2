 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel hierarchical reinforcement learning (HRL) method inspired by boosting algorithms from supervised learning, specifically designed to enhance stability and knowledge transfer in multi-task HRL. The approach involves incrementally adding new options while maintaining older ones, with a focus on the option library mechanism that facilitates knowledge sharing among agents. The method is tested in two pixel-based 2D grid-world environments, MiniGrid and CraftingWorld, demonstrating improvements over existing methods like PPOC. Despite its innovative approach, the paper suffers from a lack of comprehensive baseline comparisons, unclear definitions, and insufficient experimental validation, which limits its impact and applicability.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written and clearly structured, making it easy to follow.
- The introduction of the option library mechanism for knowledge sharing among agents is a novel and interesting approach.
- The paper effectively demonstrates the method's ability to improve stability and knowledge transfer in multi-task hierarchical reinforcement learning (HRL).
- The idea of using boosting to train options is innovative and shows potential for improving learning stability.
- The paper is well-motivated and provides a clear explanation of the proposed method.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks comprehensive baseline comparisons, which could have provided a more robust evaluation of the proposed method.
- There is a lack of clarity in some sections of the paper, particularly in the definitions and explanations of key concepts such as weak policies and the option library.
- The experimental results are not convincing, with the method's performance not consistently outperforming existing methods like PPOC.
- The paper does not adequately address the issue of instability in HRL, which is a significant concern in the field.
- The paper's reliance on a single baseline method limits the generalizability of the findings.
- The paper does not adequately address the issue of computational complexity, which is a significant concern in HRL.
- The paper does not discuss the limitations of its approach, which could have provided valuable insights for future research.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors clarify the definition and role of weak policies in the context of the proposed method?
- How does the option library mechanism facilitate knowledge sharing among agents, and what are the specific benefits of this approach?
- Can the authors provide more detailed explanations of the experimental results, particularly why the proposed method does not consistently outperform PPOC?
- How does the proposed method address the instability issues commonly found in HRL?
- Could the authors discuss the limitations of their approach and suggest potential directions for future research?
- What are the specific advantages of the proposed method over existing methods like PPOC, and how does it compare in terms of computational complexity and learning efficiency?
