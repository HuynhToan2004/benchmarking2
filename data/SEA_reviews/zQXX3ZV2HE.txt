 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel adversarial attack method targeting Human-Object Interaction (HOI) models, specifically focusing on altering the predicted interaction categories without affecting the detection of objects. This methodology involves a three-stage framework: Interaction Area Sampling (IAS), Object Category Search (OCS), and Adversarial Instance Generation (AIG). The IAS module identifies vulnerable areas, OCS selects the most confusing object categories, and AIG generates adversarial instances to perturb the image. The paper claims that this method achieves high attack success rates, although concerns are raised about the clarity of the method's implementation and the robustness of the experimental results.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel adversarial attack on Human-Object Interaction (HOI) models, focusing on altering interaction categories without affecting object detection.
- The methodology is well-explained, with a clear three-stage framework that includes Interaction Area Sampling (IAS), Object Category Search (OCS), and Adversarial Instance Generation (AIG).
- The paper is well-written, making it easy to follow, and includes comprehensive evaluations on two HOI models, demonstrating the effectiveness of the proposed method.
- The proposed method achieves a high attack success rate, demonstrating the effectiveness of the adversarial attack on HOI tasks.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks clarity in explaining the methodology, particularly the implementation details of the Interaction Area Sampling (IAS) module and the Object Category Search (OCS) module.
- The experimental setup and results are not convincingly robust, with limited diversity in the dataset used and potentially misleading success rates.
- The paper does not sufficiently address the transferability of the proposed attack, which is crucial for evaluating the robustness of the attack in real-world scenarios.
- There is a lack of discussion on the limitations and potential negative societal impacts of the proposed attack.
- The paper's contribution is not clearly defined, and it is unclear whether the proposed method is a white-box or black-box attack.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors clarify the implementation details of the Interaction Area Sampling (IAS) module and the Object Category Search (OCS) module?
- How does the proposed method perform when applied to different HOI models, and what are the success rates for different interaction categories?
- How does the method ensure that the perturbations are not easily detectable by humans, especially when they are added to the image?
- Can the authors provide more details on the experimental setup, particularly the diversity of the dataset and the evaluation metrics used?
- How does the proposed method compare to existing adversarial attacks in terms of effectiveness and transferability?
- Can the authors discuss the potential negative societal impacts of their proposed attack and how these might be mitigated?
