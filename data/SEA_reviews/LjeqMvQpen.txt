 **Summary:**
The paper explores the fusion of transformer models using optimal transport (OT) to align various components such as multi-head self-attention, layer normalization, and residual connections. It proposes a method that generalizes to different transformer architectures, including vision and natural language processing tasks. The paper presents extensive experiments demonstrating the effectiveness of the proposed method over traditional vanilla fusion and individual models. It also investigates the impact of soft alignment on the performance of fused models, showing that it can outperform vanilla fusion and individual models in some cases. The method's applicability across different model sizes and architectures is highlighted, although the paper could benefit from a clearer presentation and a more rigorous theoretical justification for the OT-based approach.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel method for fusing transformer-based models using optimal transport (OT), which is a significant advancement over traditional methods like vanilla fusion.
- The proposed method is versatile and can be applied to various transformer architectures, including vision and natural language processing tasks, demonstrating its broad applicability.
- The paper presents extensive experiments that demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed method in enhancing the performance of fused models, outperforming vanilla fusion and individual models in some cases.
- The methodology is well-explained, with clear descriptions of the fusion process and the use of the OT-based approach, making it accessible and understandable.
- The paper provides a comprehensive analysis of the method's effectiveness across different tasks and datasets, including both vision and natural language processing tasks, showcasing its robustness and generalizability.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a clear motivation for using OT-based fusion specifically for transformer models, especially when compared to other methods like weighted scalar or weighted matrix approaches.
- The presentation of the paper could be improved, particularly in the explanation of the OT-based fusion and the visualization of the transportation map flow graph, which could enhance the reader's understanding of the methodology.
- The paper does not include a detailed theoretical justification for why OT-based fusion should work for transformers, which could strengthen the paper's argument for its effectiveness.
- The experimental setup could be expanded to include more complex tasks and datasets, such as large-scale image classification and more challenging natural language processing tasks, to further validate the method's capabilities.
- The paper could benefit from a more rigorous comparison with other model fusion methods, such as those that use attention-based fusion or other types of fusion strategies, to establish a more robust baseline for evaluation.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors provide a more detailed comparison between OT-based fusion and other methods like weighted scalar or weighted matrix approaches, especially in terms of their effectiveness and computational efficiency?
- How does the proposed method perform in scenarios where the models being fused are trained on different datasets or different versions of the same dataset?
- Can the authors clarify the role and impact of the Sinkhorn regularizer in the OT-based fusion process, particularly in terms of its influence on the fusion outcomes?
- Could the paper benefit from a more detailed visualization or explanation of the transportation map flow graph, possibly including a step-by-step guide or additional diagrams to aid in understanding the methodology?
- How does the proposed method compare in terms of computational efficiency and resource requirements with other model fusion methods, such as those that use attention-based fusion or other types of fusion strategies?
