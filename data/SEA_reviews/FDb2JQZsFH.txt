 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel Attention-based Iterative Decomposition (AID) module aimed at enhancing the decomposition operations for structured representations in Tensor Product Representation (TPR)-based models. AID utilizes a competitive attention mechanism to bind sequential features to abstract structured representations, thereby improving the systematic generalization performance of TPR-based models. The AID module is evaluated on various tasks, including synthetic, text, and visual question-answering tasks, demonstrating its effectiveness in improving the performance of TPR-based models. However, the paper lacks a detailed discussion on the limitations and societal impact of the proposed method, and the novelty of the approach is questioned due to its similarity to existing methods.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, making it easy to follow and understand the proposed method.
- The authors have provided a detailed discussion on the limitations of prior TPR-based models, specifically focusing on the incomplete decomposition problem.
- The proposed AID module is simple, effective, and can be easily integrated into various TPR-based models, demonstrating its flexibility and applicability.
- The paper includes comprehensive experimental results, which show the effectiveness of the proposed method in improving the performance of TPR-based models across various tasks.
- The authors have conducted ablation studies to demonstrate the impact of different components of the proposed method, providing a deeper understanding of its effectiveness.

**Weaknesses:**
- The novelty of the proposed method is limited as it closely resembles existing methods such as Slot Attention, and the authors have not adequately discussed or compared their method with these existing approaches.
- The paper lacks a detailed discussion on the limitations of the proposed method, particularly in terms of scalability and computational efficiency.
- The paper does not include a discussion on the societal impact of the proposed method, which is a significant omission given the potential applications in various domains.
- The experimental results are not convincing, particularly in the text and visual question-answering tasks, where the performance improvements are marginal or even negative.
- The paper does not adequately address the limitations of the proposed method, which could affect the reliability and applicability of the results.
- The paper lacks a detailed comparison with other state-of-the-art methods, which could provide a clearer understanding of the advantages and disadvantages of the proposed method.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors provide a more detailed comparison with existing methods, particularly in terms of the novelty and effectiveness of their approach?
- How does the proposed method compare in terms of computational efficiency and scalability with other TPR-based models?
- Can the authors provide more detailed results on the computational efficiency and scalability of the AID module?
- How does the proposed method handle the potential issues related to the binding problem, particularly in terms of ensuring that the roles and fillers are effectively separated and bound?
- Could the authors clarify the role of the "goal" MLP in the AID module and its impact on the overall performance of the model?
- How does the proposed method address the limitations discussed in the review, particularly in terms of the clarity and depth of the experimental results and the discussion on the limitations and societal impact of the proposed method?
