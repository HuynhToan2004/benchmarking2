 **Summary:**
The paper introduces PPBench, a framework designed to evaluate the psychological characteristics of Large Language Models (LLMs) using thirteen scales derived from clinical psychology. The framework categorizes these scales into four domains: personality traits, interpersonal relationships, motivational tests, and emotional abilities. The paper employs a jailbreak approach to bypass safety alignment protocols and tests the intrinsic nature of LLMs. Five popular models, including Text-Davinci-003, ChatGPT, GPT-4, LLaMA-2-7b, and LLaMA-2-13b, are evaluated using this framework. The results indicate that LLMs exhibit distinct personality traits, interpersonal dynamics, and emotional abilities, with variations in model sizes and updates leading to diverse characteristics. However, the paper faces criticism for its limited novelty and depth in exploring the psychological aspects of LLMs.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, easy to follow, and provides a comprehensive evaluation of various models, including different model sizes and updates.
- It introduces a novel framework, PPBench, which evaluates the psychological characteristics of LLMs, contributing to the understanding of how these models mimic human behavior.
- The paper provides insights into the intrinsic nature of LLMs, which is crucial for understanding their capabilities and limitations.
- The framework is flexible and scalable, making it applicable to a wide range of LLMs and potentially adaptable to other domains.
- The paper is significant as it addresses the psychological aspects of LLMs, a topic of growing importance with the widespread use of these models.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a thorough exploration of the psychological aspects of LLMs, focusing more on the evaluation of LLMs rather than the psychological implications.
- The results are somewhat expected, with LLMs exhibiting higher emotional intelligence and empathy, which might not be surprising given their training.
- The paper does not adequately address the limitations of its methodology, such as the potential for LLMs to mimic human responses without truly understanding or experiencing emotions.
- The paper does not sufficiently compare its results with existing studies, which could help in validating the findings and positioning the work within the current body of research.
- The framework's scalability and flexibility are not convincingly demonstrated, and the paper could benefit from more detailed examples or case studies to illustrate these aspects.

**Questions:**
- Can you provide more detailed examples or case studies to illustrate the scalability and flexibility of the PPBench framework?
- How do you ensure the reliability of the results when comparing LLMs to human subjects, especially considering the potential for LLMs to mimic human responses without truly understanding or experiencing emotions?
- How does the paper address the potential for LLMs to exhibit biases or stereotypes, especially in the context of evaluating their psychological characteristics?
- Could you elaborate on the choice of the jailbreak approach and how it affects the results?
- How do the authors justify the use of the jailbreak approach in the context of evaluating the psychological characteristics of LLMs?
- Can you provide a more detailed comparison of your results with existing studies to help position your work within the current body of research?
