 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel method for localized text-to-image generation using cross-attention control (CAC) without requiring additional training or inference time. The method leverages cross-attention maps to guide the generation process, allowing for the integration of location information such as bounding boxes and semantic segmentation maps. This approach enhances the controllability of text-to-image models by enabling them to generate images with specific objects or features at desired locations. The paper also proposes a standardized suite of evaluations using large pretrained recognition models to assess the performance of the proposed method. Despite its simplicity and effectiveness, the method's novelty and the depth of its contributions are questioned due to its reliance on existing techniques and the simplicity of its approach.

**Strengths:**
- The proposed method is simple, intuitive, and effective, enhancing the controllability of text-to-image models without requiring additional training or inference time.
- The method is portable and can be easily integrated into existing models, making it a valuable plugin for various text-to-image models.
- The paper is well-written, clear, and easy to follow, with a clear motivation and a well-designed evaluation suite.
- The method's simplicity and effectiveness are highlighted by the experimental results, which show improvements in localized text-to-image generation performance.
- The paper provides a standardized suite of evaluations using large pretrained recognition models, which can be used to assess the performance of the proposed method in various scenarios.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper's novelty is limited as it mainly focuses on utilizing cross-attention control for localized generation, which is a straightforward application of existing techniques.
- The method's effectiveness is somewhat limited by the quality of the pretrained models used, which could affect the performance of the generated images.
- The method's ability to handle complex compositional prompts is questionable, as it relies on heuristics that may not be effective in all scenarios.
