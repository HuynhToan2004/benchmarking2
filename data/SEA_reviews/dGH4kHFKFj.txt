 **Summary:**
The paper introduces GenCorres, a novel method for joint shape matching that leverages a data-driven approach to learn a mesh generator from a collection of unorganized shapes. This generator is used to create correspondences between shapes, addressing the challenges of pairwise matching by enforcing deformation priors between adjacent synthetic shapes. The method is evaluated on multiple datasets, demonstrating superior performance over existing methods. It also includes an ablation study to validate the effectiveness of different components. The paper is well-written, with clear explanations and extensive experimental results, although it lacks a discussion on the limitations and societal impact of the proposed method.

**Strengths:**

- The method is well-explained, with clear descriptions of the approach, the datasets used, and the experimental setup.
