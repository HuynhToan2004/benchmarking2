 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel formulation of Sharpness-Aware Minimization (SAM) by replacing the cross-entropy loss with the 0-1 loss, aiming to directly optimize the misclassification error. This reformulation necessitates a bilevel optimization approach, which is addressed by proposing a new algorithm, BiSAM. The authors argue that this modification not only improves the theoretical guarantees but also enhances the practical performance of SAM. Extensive experiments across various datasets, including CIFAR-10, CIFAR-100, and ImageNet-1K, demonstrate that BiSAM outperforms SAM and other variants in terms of accuracy and robustness.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, clear, and easy to follow, making it accessible to a broad audience.
- The motivation behind the proposed method is strong, particularly the shift from cross-entropy to 0-1 loss, which is a significant change from previous SAM formulations.
- The theoretical foundation of the paper is robust, with a solid theoretical result (Theorem 1) that supports the proposed method.
- The paper is innovative in its approach to bilevel optimization, which is not commonly explored in SAM research.
- Extensive experiments are conducted across various datasets and models, demonstrating the effectiveness and robustness of the proposed method.
- The paper is well-organized, with clear explanations of the methodology and results, which are supported by comprehensive experiments.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper could benefit from a more detailed comparison with existing works, particularly those that also use bilevel optimization in SAM.
- The experimental results, while promising, are not consistently superior across all models and datasets. For instance, the performance of BiSAM (-log) is not consistently better than SAM in all cases.
- The paper lacks a detailed discussion on the computational efficiency of BiSAM compared to SAM, which is crucial for understanding the practical implications of the proposed method.
- There is a lack of discussion on the limitations of the proposed method, which could help in understanding its scope and applicability.
- The paper could benefit from a more comprehensive literature review, especially in the context of bilevel optimization and its application in machine learning.

**Questions:**
- Could you provide a detailed comparison of the computational efficiency of BiSAM with SAM?
- How does the choice of the lower bound function affect the performance of BiSAM?
- Is there a theoretical guarantee that BiSAM will always outperform SAM, or is there a scenario where SAM might be preferred?
- Can you clarify the differences in the results between BiSAM (-log) and BiSAM (tanh) as shown in the tables?
- How does the proposed method perform in terms of training time and memory requirements compared to SAM?
- Is there a possibility to incorporate the 0-1 loss directly into the SAM framework without the need for bilevel optimization?
