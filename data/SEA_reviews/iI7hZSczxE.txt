 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel method for learning disentangled representations in time series data using a weakly supervised contrastive approach. The method aims to address the challenges of disentanglement in correlated data by promoting pairwise factorized support through contrastive learning. It introduces a new regularization term, DIoSC, and a Time Disentanglement Score (TDS) to evaluate the disentanglement quality of the learned representations. The methodology is evaluated on three public datasets, demonstrating improvements over existing methods in terms of disentanglement and downstream tasks. The paper also discusses the challenges of evaluating disentanglement in time series data and proposes a new metric to address these challenges.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel method for disentangled representation learning in time series data, which is particularly relevant for applications such as NILM.
- The methodology is well-supported by theoretical frameworks and empirical results, demonstrating improvements over existing methods in terms of disentanglement and downstream tasks.
- The paper is well-written, clear, and easy to follow, making it accessible to readers.
- The introduction of a new regularization term, DIoSC, and a Time Disentanglement Score (TDS) to evaluate the disentanglement quality of the learned representations is a significant contribution to the field.
- The paper addresses the challenge of evaluating disentanglement in time series data, which is often overlooked in existing literature.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper could benefit from a more detailed discussion on the choice of metrics for evaluating disentanglement, particularly in the context of time series data.
- There is a lack of clarity regarding the definition and application of the IoS constraint, which is central to the methodology.
- The paper could provide more detailed explanations and examples of the data augmentation techniques used, especially in the context of time series data.
- The paper does not adequately address the limitations of the methodology, such as the impact of hyperparameters on the results and the generalizability of the findings to other datasets or applications.
- There is a lack of discussion on the computational complexity and scalability of the proposed method, which could limit its practical application in real-world scenarios.
- The paper does not include a discussion on the potential societal impacts of the work, which is an important consideration for many applications of machine learning.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors provide a more detailed explanation of the IoS constraint and its application in the methodology?
- How do the authors define and measure the disentanglement of the learned representations, and what are the implications of this for the practical application of the method?
- Can the authors clarify the choice of metrics for evaluating disentanglement and discuss the rationale behind their selection?
- How does the proposed method handle the issue of variable dimensions in time series data, and what are the implications of this for the disentanglement performance?
- Can the authors provide more details on the computational complexity and scalability of the proposed method, particularly in terms of the number of appliances and the length of the time series?
- How does the proposed method compare to other methods that use similar approaches, such as those discussed in related literature?
