 **Summary:**
The paper investigates the robustness of LLMs across different versions, focusing on GPT-3.5 and LLaMA models. It examines the impact of adversarial queries on these models and compares their robustness in zero-shot and few-shot learning scenarios. The study highlights that updated models do not consistently demonstrate enhanced robustness against adversarial attacks. The experiments are comprehensive, involving various adversarial attacks and models, and the results suggest that robustness may not improve with model updates. However, the paper is criticized for its limited scope, lack of novelty, and unclear presentation. The methodology is straightforward and the conclusions are not surprising, which raises concerns about the paper's originality and impact.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written and easy to follow, making it accessible to a broad audience.
- The study of the robustness of LLMs over time is an important and relevant topic, particularly given the continuous updates and improvements in these models.
- The paper conducts a comprehensive set of experiments, analyzing the impact of adversarial examples on LLMs and comparing the performance of different versions.
- The findings provide valuable insights into the robustness of LLMs and could be useful for developers and users in understanding the limitations and risks associated with model updates.
- The paper's approach to evaluating the robustness of LLMs using adversarial examples within the in-context learning framework is innovative and could inspire further research in this area.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper's scope is limited, focusing only on a few models (GPT-3.5 and LLaMA) and not considering a broader range of models, which could limit the generalizability of the findings.
- The methodology is straightforward and the conclusions are not surprising, which may not provide significant new insights or contributions to the field.
- The paper lacks novelty as it primarily uses existing attack methods and datasets, without introducing new techniques or datasets specifically designed for evaluating the robustness of LLMs.
- The presentation of the paper needs improvement, with some sections being unclear and difficult to understand.
- The paper does not adequately address the transferability of adversarial examples across different model versions, which is a critical aspect of robustness evaluation.
- The paper does not discuss the limitations of its work, which could provide a more balanced view of the findings.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors elaborate on the selection of GPT-3.5 v0613 as the latest version for comparison? What were the specific reasons for this choice?
- In Section 3.2, the paper mentions using the surrogate model to generate adversarial examples. How do the authors ensure that these examples are effective against the target models?
- Why were only the latest versions of GPT-3.5 and LLaMA compared? What would be the implications if older versions were also included in the study?
- The paper mentions that the updated versions of GPT-3.5 and LLaMA do not show enhanced robustness against adversarial attacks. Can the authors provide more detailed explanations or hypotheses for this observation?
- How do the authors address the potential limitations of their work, such as the transferability of adversarial examples across different model versions?
- In the context of the findings, what are the potential implications for the development and deployment of LLMs, particularly in terms of security and privacy?
