 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel method for deriving entity-specific embeddings within the transformer architecture, aimed at enhancing performance in tasks like emotion recognition in conversation and player performance projection in baseball. This method involves the introduction of a new [ENTITY] embedding and a boolean mask to modify the attention mechanism, allowing entities to interact with each other, termed entity-to-entity (E2E) attention. The authors also propose a distribution-aware clustering method to handle the long-tail partial-label learning problem. The method's effectiveness is demonstrated through experiments on datasets such as EmoryNLP and MELD, showing improvements over existing models like RoBERTa and InstructERC. However, the paper lacks clarity in its methodological descriptions and experimental setups, which could hinder understanding and replication of the results.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel method for deriving entity-specific embeddings within the transformer architecture, which is a significant advancement in the field.
- The proposed method is generalizable and can be applied to various tasks, demonstrating its versatility.
- The method's effectiveness is demonstrated through experiments on datasets such as EmoryNLP and MELD, showing improvements over existing models like RoBERTa and InstructERC.
- The paper provides a detailed description of the proposed method, including the use of entity embeddings and entity-to-entity attention, which could be useful for practitioners.
- The experiments are conducted on both NLP and sports analytics tasks, showcasing the method's applicability across different domains.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks clarity in its methodological descriptions, particularly in the pre-training phase and the entity-to-entity (E2E) attention mechanism.
- The experimental setup and results are not clearly described, making it difficult to understand the contributions of the proposed method.
- The paper does not adequately compare its method with existing works, such as the InstructERC model, which could have provided a better understanding of the method's advantages.
- The paper's focus on baseball as a use case might limit its appeal to a broader audience, as the problem of deriving entity-specific embeddings is not unique to this domain.
- The paper's writing style and organization need improvement, with some sections appearing redundant or unclear.

**Questions:**
- Could you clarify the methodology used in the pre-training phase, particularly how the entity embeddings are initialized and updated?
- How does the entity-to-entity (E2E) attention mechanism function, and how does it contribute to the overall performance of the model?
- Can you provide more details on the experimental setup, including the specific tasks and datasets used, and the metrics employed to evaluate the model's performance?
- How does the proposed method compare to other existing models, particularly in terms of computational efficiency and resource requirements?
- Can you elaborate on the choice of using baseball as a use case and discuss how the method might be adapted for other domains or tasks?
