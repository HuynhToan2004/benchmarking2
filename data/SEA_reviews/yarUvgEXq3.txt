 **Summary:**
The paper introduces SAFER2, a collaborative filtering method aimed at improving the tail performance of recommender systems by minimizing the conditional value at risk (CVaR). It extends the implicit alternating least squares (iALS) method to handle the computational challenges of CVaR minimization, employing a smoothing technique for quantile regression. The method is evaluated on real-world datasets, showing competitive computational efficiency and improved tail performance. However, the paper faces criticism for its limited novelty and incremental nature, as it primarily adapts existing techniques without significant innovation.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, making it easy to follow and understand, with a clear and logical structure.
- The proposed method, SAFER2, is innovative in its application of convolution-type smoothing to optimize the CVaR objective, which is a novel approach in the field of recommender systems.
- The paper demonstrates the effectiveness of SAFER2 through rigorous experimental evaluations, showing superior performance in handling challenging samples and maintaining computational efficiency.
- The methodology is supported by theoretical proofs and extensive experimental results, which validate the effectiveness of the proposed approach.
- The paper addresses a significant problem in the field of recommender systems, focusing on improving the tail performance, which is crucial for ensuring user satisfaction and reducing user churn.

**Weaknesses:**
- The novelty of the paper is limited as it primarily adapts existing techniques, such as the convolution-type smoothing for quantile regression, without significant innovation.
- The paper lacks a comprehensive review of related work, particularly in the area of risk-averse collaborative filtering, which could provide a better context for the research.
- The experimental evaluation is limited in scope, with only a few datasets used, and the results are not sufficiently robust to demonstrate the generalizability of the proposed method.
- The paper does not provide a detailed analysis of the computational complexity or the scalability of the proposed method, which is crucial for understanding its practical applicability in large-scale systems.
- The paper does not sufficiently discuss the limitations of the proposed method, which could help in understanding its applicability and potential drawbacks.
