 **Summary:**
The paper investigates the convergence properties and performance of various optimal transport (OT)-based adversarial networks, including WGAN, OTM, and UOTM. It proposes a novel method, UOTM with Scheduled Divergence (UOTM-SD), to address the \(\tau\)-sensitivity of UOTM, which is crucial for enhancing the generation quality of OT-based GANs. The methodology involves a scheduled increase in the importance of the divergence term, which is shown to improve the robustness and performance of UOTM. The paper also provides a theoretical analysis and empirical validation, demonstrating the effectiveness of UOTM-SD over existing methods.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, clear, and easy to follow, with a clear structure that effectively guides the reader through the content.
- It provides a comprehensive analysis of the convergence properties of various OT-based GANs, including WGAN, OTM, and UOTM, and proposes a novel method, UOTM with Scheduled Divergence (UOTM-SD), to address the \(\tau\)-sensitivity of UOTM.
- The paper is original in its approach to unifying different OT-based adversarial networks and in proposing a new method, UOTM-SD, which improves the performance of UOTM by addressing its \(\tau\)-sensitivity.
- The empirical results are convincing, demonstrating the effectiveness of UOTM-SD over existing methods, and the theoretical analysis is solid, providing a strong foundation for the proposed method.
- The paper is well-organized, with a clear presentation of the literature review and the motivation behind the proposed method, and the methodology is clearly described, making it easy to understand and implement.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed discussion on the limitations of the proposed method, particularly concerning its scalability and applicability to larger datasets.
- The experimental results are not comprehensive enough, with only CIFAR-10 being used for testing, which might not adequately demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed method across different datasets.
- There are some minor issues with notation and clarity in the presentation of the results, which could be improved for better readability and understanding.
- The paper does not sufficiently address the broader implications of the findings, particularly in terms of their practical applications and the potential societal impacts of the proposed method.
- The paper could benefit from a more detailed discussion on the related work and a broader comparison with other OT-based GANs to provide a more comprehensive view of the state of the art.

**Questions:**
- How does the proposed method perform on larger datasets such as ImageNet, and what are the scalability limitations of UOTM-SD?
- Could the authors provide more details on the experimental setup, such as the number of iterations and the specifics of the training process?
- How does the proposed method compare to other OT-based GANs in terms of performance and computational efficiency?
- What are the specific advantages of using the quadratic cost function in UOTM, and how does it compare to other cost functions in terms of performance and theoretical guarantees?
- How does the proposed method address the issue of mode collapse in OT-based GANs, and what are the implications of this for the generation of high-quality samples?
- Could the authors clarify the notation and terminology used in the paper to improve the clarity and accessibility of the content?
