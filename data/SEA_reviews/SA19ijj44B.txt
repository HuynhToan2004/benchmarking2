 **Summary:**
The paper investigates the use of Bayesian Neural Networks (BNNs) as surrogates for Bayesian Optimization (BO), comparing them to traditional Gaussian Processes (GPs). The study evaluates various BNN methods including fully stochastic, deep kernel learning, and infinite-width BNNs, employing different inference techniques such as Hamiltonian Monte Carlo, stochastic gradient Markov Chain Monte Carlo, and deep ensembles. The experiments are conducted on both synthetic and real-world datasets, demonstrating the competitive performance of BNNs in high-dimensional problems and their ability to handle non-stationary functions. However, the paper does not convincingly show that BNNs outperform GPs in all scenarios, and the choice of experimental settings and baseline comparisons has been critiqued for being potentially misleading.

**Strengths:**
- The paper provides a thorough evaluation of Bayesian Neural Networks (BNNs) as surrogates for Bayesian Optimization (BO), examining various BNN methods and inference techniques, which is a valuable contribution to the field.
- The paper is well-written, clear, and easy to follow, making it accessible to a broad audience.
- The authors explore the impact of different model hyperparameters on the performance of BNNs, which is crucial for understanding their effectiveness in BO.
- The study is comprehensive, evaluating BNNs across a wide range of problems, including synthetic and real-world datasets, which helps in understanding their generalizability.
- The paper includes a detailed analysis of the limitations of Gaussian Processes (GPs) and how BNNs can address these, which is an important contribution to the field.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper does not convincingly demonstrate that BNNs outperform GPs in all scenarios, which undermines the main claim of the paper.
- The choice of experimental settings and baselines is questionable, particularly the inclusion of Deep Ensembles (DEs) which are not typically used for BO, and the omission of other relevant baselines like mean-field BNNs and in-context learning BNNs.
- The paper lacks a clear motivation for why BNNs should be used in BO instead of GPs, especially considering the additional computational demands and potential issues with overfitting.
- The paper does not sufficiently address the high variance of BNNs, which is a significant issue in BO where uncertainty estimates are crucial.
- The paper could benefit from a more detailed discussion on the limitations of BNNs in BO and a comparison with other state-of-the-art methods to better establish the effectiveness of BNNs.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors clarify why Deep Ensembles (DEs) were chosen as a baseline and whether there are plans to include other relevant baselines such as mean-field BNNs and in-context learning BNNs?
- How does the choice of experimental settings affect the results, particularly the inclusion of high-dimensional problems which might not be representative of typical BO scenarios?
- Can the authors provide more details on the computational demands of BNNs in BO and how these compare to GPs in terms of runtime and memory requirements?
- How does the paper address the issue of overfitting in BNNs, especially in high-dimensional problems?
- Could the authors elaborate on the choice of hyperparameters for GPs and how these affect the performance of BNNs in BO?
- Why were certain baselines omitted from the study, and how do the results compare to these omitted methods?
