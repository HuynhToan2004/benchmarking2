 **Summary:**
The paper introduces FeatUp, a task- and model-agnostic framework designed to enhance the spatial resolution of deep features in computer vision tasks. It utilizes a novel multi-view consistency loss inspired by NeRFs to reconstruct features at any resolution. The framework is evaluated on various tasks such as semantic segmentation and depth prediction, showing improved performance over existing methods. The approach is flexible and can be integrated into existing applications without retraining, making it a valuable tool for improving the accuracy and resolution of deep features in vision models.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel framework, FeatUp, which is task- and model-agnostic, enhancing the spatial resolution of deep features without changing their semantics.
- The use of a novel multi-view consistency loss inspired by NeRFs is innovative and effective, as demonstrated by the results.
- The paper is well-written, clear, and easy to follow, with a comprehensive evaluation of the proposed method across various tasks such as semantic segmentation and depth prediction.
- The approach is flexible and can be integrated into existing applications without retraining, making it a valuable tool for improving the accuracy and resolution of deep features in vision models.
- The paper provides a fast CUDA implementation of Joint Bilateral Upsampling, which is orders of magnitude faster than standard PyTorch implementations.
- The proposed method can be used as a drop-in replacement for ordinary features to improve performance on dense prediction tasks and model explainability.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed discussion on the computational cost and efficiency of the proposed method compared to existing approaches, which could be critical for practical applications.
- There is a lack of quantitative and qualitative results for the implicit version of FeatUp, which limits the understanding of its effectiveness.
- The paper does not sufficiently discuss the differences and advantages of the proposed method compared to existing methods like FADE, which also uses a semi-shift operator for feature upsampling.
- The paper could benefit from more detailed discussions on the implementation and training of the multi-view consistency loss and the downsampler, which are crucial components of the proposed method.
- There are some typographical errors and missing references in the paper, which need to be corrected for clarity and accuracy.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors provide a detailed comparison of the computational cost and efficiency of the proposed method with existing approaches?
- How does the proposed method compare to other state-of-the-art methods in terms of accuracy and efficiency in practical applications?
- Could the authors provide more quantitative and qualitative results for the implicit version of FeatUp to better understand its effectiveness?
- How does the proposed method handle different types of deep features, such as those from CNNs and Transformers, and what are the implications for performance and computational cost?
- Could the authors discuss the potential of extending the proposed method to other vision tasks beyond semantic segmentation and depth prediction?
- How does the proposed method compare to other feature upsampling methods that do not require retraining, such as those based on bilinear interpolation or nearest neighbor sampling?
