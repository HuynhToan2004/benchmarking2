 **Summary:**
The paper explores the convergence properties of Differentially Private Stochastic Gradient Descent (DP-SGD) and its averaged variant (DP-ASGD) in both strongly convex and non-convex settings. It provides non-asymptotic bounds for these algorithms, focusing on the effects of various hyperparameters such as learning rates, privacy budgets, and problem dimensions. The theoretical analysis is supported by numerical simulations, although the novelty of the results is questioned due to similarities with existing works. The paper is criticized for its lack of originality and depth in the literature review, particularly in its handling of non-strongly convex functions and its reliance on known results without significant new contributions.

**Strengths:**
- The paper provides a comprehensive theoretical analysis of the DP-SGD algorithm, including the convergence properties of both DP-SGD and DP-ASGD in both strongly convex and non-convex settings.
- It offers non-asymptotic bounds for the algorithms, which are useful for understanding the performance of DP-SGD in practical scenarios.
- The paper is well-written, making it easy to follow, and the results are clearly presented, making it accessible to readers.
- The study of DP-SGD with a non-constant privacy budget is an important and interesting direction in differential privacy research.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks originality in its results, as the non-asymptotic bounds for DP-SGD with strongly convex losses and the convergence of averaged stochastic gradient descent (SGD) under local DP are well-known in the literature.
- The paper does not sufficiently differentiate its contributions from existing works, particularly from those by Gower et al. and Vaswani et al., which have similar results and proof techniques.
- The literature review is inadequate, particularly in discussing the convergence of DP-SGD with non-strongly convex losses, which is a significant gap in the current understanding of DP-SGD.
- The paper's experimental results are limited to strongly convex settings, which does not adequately address the performance of DP-SGD in non-convex scenarios.
- The proofs and theorems presented are either incorrect or not well-explained, which undermines the credibility of the results.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors clarify how their results differ from those of Gower et al. and Vaswani et al., particularly in terms of the non-asymptotic bounds and the convergence of averaged SGD under local DP?
- Why is the averaged SGD under local DP considered more difficult than the standard SGD with a constant privacy budget, as mentioned in the paper?
- How does the paper address the convergence of DP-SGD in non-strongly convex settings, which is a significant gap in the current understanding of DP-SGD?
- Can the authors provide a more detailed literature review, especially concerning the convergence of DP-SGD in non-convex scenarios?
- How do the authors justify the use of the parallel composition of GDP in their proofs, and what implications does this have for the privacy guarantees of their algorithms?
