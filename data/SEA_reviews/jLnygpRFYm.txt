 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel approach to masked image modeling by incorporating stochastic positional embeddings (StoP) to enhance the robustness of models to location uncertainties. This method involves learning a covariance matrix for each masked token, which is then used to sample random positions for masked tokens. The authors claim that this approach improves the performance of downstream tasks by reducing overfitting to location features. The method is tested across various datasets and shown to improve performance in image classification, counting, and depth ordering tasks. However, the paper's novelty is questioned due to its similarity to existing methods like I-JEPA, and its effectiveness is limited to specific types of self-supervised learning tasks.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, clear, and easy to follow, with a comprehensive experimental evaluation that demonstrates the effectiveness of the proposed StoP method.
- The proposed StoP method is simple yet effective, and its application to image-level pretraining is novel.
- The methodology is well-explained, including a detailed explanation of the reparameterization trick and the use of stochastic positional embeddings.
- The paper includes a thorough analysis of the proposed method, including the use of stochastic positional embeddings and the visualization of learned positional embeddings.
- The results show significant improvements over baseline methods, indicating the effectiveness of the proposed approach.

**Weaknesses:**
- The novelty of the method is questionable, as it bears strong similarities to existing methods like I-JEPA, with the main difference being the use of stochastic positional embeddings.
- The paper lacks a detailed explanation of how the method works in practice, particularly how the stochastic positional embeddings are applied and how they affect the model's performance.
- The paper does not provide sufficient comparisons with other methods, such as I-JEPA and data2vec, which could have helped in validating the proposed method's effectiveness.
- The method's effectiveness is limited to specific types of self-supervised learning tasks, and it is unclear how it would perform in other settings.
- The paper could benefit from a more detailed discussion on the limitations of the proposed method and how it might be improved in future work.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors provide more details on how the stochastic positional embeddings are applied in practice and how they affect the model's performance?
- How does the proposed method compare to other methods, such as I-JEPA and data2vec, in terms of performance and efficiency?
- Could the authors provide a more detailed explanation of how the method addresses the issue of location uncertainty in masked image modeling?
- How does the proposed method handle the trade-off between location uncertainty and other factors like object appearance and context?
- Could the authors clarify the role of the learned matrix A in the model's performance and how it affects the model's ability to balance location certainty and contextual information?
