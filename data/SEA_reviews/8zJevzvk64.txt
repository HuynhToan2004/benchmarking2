 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel method for off-policy evaluation (OPE) in reinforcement learning (RL) using a diffusion Schrödinger bridge generative model. This approach aims to address the challenges of high-dimensional state spaces and the curse of dimensionality by providing a polynomial rate of O(H2√d) for the estimation error of the value function. The method is tested against existing baselines in the DeepMind control suite and shows promising results. However, the paper is critiqued for its presentation, which is considered dense and challenging to follow, and for its assumptions, which are not thoroughly justified or explained. The paper also lacks a detailed discussion on the limitations of the proposed method and does not include a comprehensive comparison with state-of-the-art methods.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel approach using diffusion Schrödinger bridge for off-policy evaluation (OPE), which is a significant advancement in the field.
- The methodology is supported by a theoretical analysis that provides a polynomial rate of O(H2√d) for the estimation error of the value function, which is a notable contribution.
- The paper is well-written, and the experiments are conducted on a standard benchmark, which adds credibility to the results.
- The paper is the first to apply diffusion Schrödinger bridge to reinforcement learning, which is a significant theoretical and practical contribution.
- The authors have demonstrated the method's effectiveness by showing that it can outperform existing methods in some scenarios.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper suffers from dense and challenging presentation, which makes it difficult for readers to follow and understand the methodology and results.
- The paper lacks a detailed discussion on the limitations of the proposed method, which is crucial for understanding its applicability and scope.
- There are several assumptions made in the paper that are not justified or explained, which undermines the credibility of the theoretical analysis.
- The paper does not include a comprehensive comparison with state-of-the-art methods, which is necessary to establish the method's effectiveness and novelty.
- The paper does not discuss the computational complexity of the proposed method, which is a significant concern for practical applications.
- The paper lacks a discussion on the convergence rate of the proposed method, which is crucial for understanding its practical applicability.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors clarify the assumptions made in the paper and justify their necessity and relevance to the problem at hand?
- How does the proposed method compare to other state-of-the-art methods in terms of computational complexity and convergence rate?
- What are the limitations of the proposed method, and under what conditions would it not be effective?
- Could the authors provide more details on the experimental setup, such as the specifics of the datasets used and the training parameters?
- How does the proposed method handle the curse of horizon and dimensionality, and what are the implications of these assumptions in practical applications?
- Can the authors elaborate on the method's applicability in different types of reinforcement learning environments, such as those with different types of state spaces and reward functions?
