 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel approach to instruction tuning in natural language processing (NLP) by integrating a Structural Causal Model (SCM) to enhance zero-shot capabilities. The proposed method, Structural Instruction Tuning (SIT), leverages a meta-SCM to learn task-specific causal factors, thereby improving generalization across various tasks. Theoretical underpinnings are provided to ensure the identifiability of latent factors, and empirical results show improvements in zero-shot performance across several datasets. However, the paper has been critiqued for its limited experimental scope, lack of clarity in model design, and potential over-reliance on specific assumptions.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel approach by integrating a Structural Causal Model (SCM) into instruction tuning, which is a significant advancement in the field.
- Theoretical proofs are provided to ensure the identifiability of latent factors, which is crucial for the effectiveness of the model.
- The empirical results show improvements in zero-shot performance, demonstrating the practical utility of the proposed method.
- The paper is well-written, making it accessible and understandable, which is essential for a broad audience.
- The idea of integrating causal factors into instruction tuning is innovative and could potentially enhance the generalization capabilities of language models.

**Weaknesses:**
- The experimental design is limited, focusing on only a few tasks and datasets, which may not fully demonstrate the generalizability of the proposed method.
- The model design lacks clarity, particularly in the roles and interactions of different components such as the task-guided latent encoder and the source reconstruction decoder.
- There is a lack of detailed discussion on the identifiability conditions and assumptions, which are critical for understanding the theoretical underpinnings of the model.
- The paper could benefit from a more comprehensive comparison with existing methods to better establish the novelty and effectiveness of the proposed approach.
- The computational efficiency and scalability of the model are not discussed, which are important considerations for practical implementation.
- The paper does not adequately address the limitations of the proposed method, which could hinder the understanding of its applicability and potential drawbacks.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors clarify the roles and interactions of the task-guided latent encoder and the source reconstruction decoder in the model?
- How does the model handle the identifiability of latent factors under different conditions, especially when assumptions are not met?
- Could the authors provide more details on the computational efficiency and scalability of the model, particularly in terms of training and inference times?
- How does the model ensure the generalizability of the learned causal factors across different tasks and domains?
- Could the authors discuss the potential limitations of the proposed method and how these could be addressed in future research?
- How does the model handle the spurious correlations mentioned in the paper, and what steps are taken to mitigate their impact?
- Can the authors provide a more detailed discussion on the assumptions made in the model and how these affect the identifiability of latent factors?
