 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel Lyapunov Noise Pruning (LNP) algorithm for designing sparse recurrent spiking neural networks (RSNNs), focusing on task-agnostic pruning of a randomly initialized dense model. The LNP method leverages the Lyapunov spectrum and spectral graph sparsification to prune synapses and neurons while maintaining network stability, enhancing computational efficiency, and improving prediction performance. The algorithm's effectiveness is demonstrated through experiments on various datasets, including time-series prediction and image classification, showing that it outperforms traditional activity-based pruning methods. Despite its innovative approach, the paper has been critiqued for its unclear writing, lack of comprehensive comparisons with state-of-the-art methods, and insufficient discussion on the biological plausibility of the model.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel task-agnostic pruning method for sparse recurrent spiking neural networks (RSNNs), which is distinct from traditional methods that are task-dependent and adapted from deep neural network (DNN) pruning.
- The proposed Lyapunov Noise Pruning (LNP) algorithm is innovative, utilizing the Lyapunov spectrum and spectral graph sparsification to prune synapses and neurons while maintaining network stability, which is a significant advancement in the field.
- The paper is well-organized and clearly written, making it easy to follow and understand.
- The experiments are comprehensive, covering both time-series prediction and image classification tasks, demonstrating the versatility and applicability of the proposed method.
- The paper presents a novel approach to designing sparse RSNNs, which is expected to be beneficial for researchers in the field.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a thorough discussion on the biological plausibility of the proposed model, which is crucial for understanding its relevance and applicability in neuroscience.
- There is a lack of clarity in the writing, particularly in the introduction and methodology sections, which could confuse readers and hinder understanding of the paper's contributions.
- The paper does not sufficiently compare its method with other state-of-the-art methods, which is essential for establishing the effectiveness and novelty of the proposed approach.
- The paper does not discuss the limitations of the proposed method, which is crucial for understanding its applicability and potential drawbacks.
- The paper could benefit from a more detailed discussion on the role of different factors in the pruning process and their impact on the final performance.
- The paper does not provide a detailed comparison of the proposed method with other task-agnostic pruning methods, which could have provided a more comprehensive evaluation of its effectiveness.
