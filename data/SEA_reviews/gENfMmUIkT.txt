 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a pipeline-based approach for object detection on resource-constrained IoT devices, specifically designed for smart surveillance systems. The methodology employs a multi-step detection pipeline that considers object size and correlation, utilizing YOLOv3 and YOLOv4 Tiny models to detect objects such as people, firearms, vehicles, license plates, and license plate characters. The paper highlights the execution time and mean average precision (mAP) improvements on a mobile IoT device, with real-world testing conducted in partnership with the Sao Paulo Police Department. However, the paper is criticized for its lack of comprehensive evaluation, insufficient comparison with existing methods, and inadequate presentation of results.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a pipeline-based approach for object detection on resource-constrained IoT devices, which is a significant contribution to the field.
- The approach is validated in real-world scenarios, demonstrating its practical applicability.
- The paper is well-written, making it easy to follow and understand.
- The authors have conducted a pilot study with the Sao Paulo Police Department, providing a practical application of the proposed method.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a comprehensive evaluation of the proposed method. More experiments are needed to demonstrate the effectiveness of the pipeline-based approach.
- The paper does not sufficiently compare the proposed method with existing methods, such as MobileNet-SSD, which could have provided a better understanding of the method's advantages and disadvantages.
- The paper does not provide sufficient details on the dataset used for training the models, which is crucial for reproducibility and validation of the results.
- The paper does not report the number of false positives, which is a critical metric for evaluating the performance of object detection systems.
- The presentation of results is poor, with insufficient details on the experimental setup and results, making it difficult to assess the effectiveness of the proposed method.
- The paper does not discuss the limitations of the proposed method, which is essential for understanding the scope and applicability of the findings.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors provide more details on the dataset used for training the models, including its size, diversity, and annotation details?
- How does the proposed method compare with other existing methods, such as MobileNet-SSD, in terms of execution time and mAP?
- Could the authors clarify the experimental setup and results, including the number of false positives and the impact of different object sizes and correlations on detection performance?
- What are the limitations of the proposed method, and how do these impact the practical application of the system?
- Could the authors provide a more detailed comparison of the proposed method with other state-of-the-art methods in terms of execution time and mAP?
- How does the proposed method handle variations in object sizes and correlations, and what are the implications of these variations on detection performance?
