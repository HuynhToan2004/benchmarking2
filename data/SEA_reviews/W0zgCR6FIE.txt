 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a new benchmark dataset, Spawrious, designed to challenge deep learning models with spurious correlations in image classification tasks. This dataset includes both one-to-one and many-to-many spurious correlations, generated using a text-to-image model and an image captioning model to ensure high-quality images. The dataset is divided into three difficulty levels, with the hardest level showing a 27% worst-group accuracy, demonstrating the dataset's effectiveness in testing model robustness. The paper also discusses the limitations of existing benchmarks and provides a detailed analysis of the dataset's construction and evaluation, showing that state-of-the-art methods struggle with Spawrious.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a new benchmark dataset, Spawrious, which is well-designed and addresses the limitations of existing benchmarks by focusing on spurious correlations.
- The dataset includes both one-to-one and many-to-many spurious correlations, which are essential for understanding and mitigating spurious correlations in deep learning models.
- The paper is well-written, clear, and provides a comprehensive evaluation of the dataset, including a detailed analysis of model misclassifications.
- The dataset is generated using high-quality models, ensuring its utility and relevance to the field.
- The paper includes a detailed discussion on the limitations of existing benchmarks and provides a valuable contribution by addressing these limitations with the new dataset.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed discussion on how the dataset is constructed, particularly how the text-to-image model is used to generate images and how the image captioning model is employed to filter out unsuitable images.
- The dataset's utility is limited by its small size (only 152k images), which may not be sufficient to train large models or test robustness across different environments.
- The paper does not discuss the potential ethical implications of using a generative model to construct the dataset, which could be a significant concern for some applications.
- The dataset's diversity is limited, focusing only on dogs and their backgrounds, which may not adequately represent real-world scenarios where spurious correlations are more complex and varied.
- The paper does not provide sufficient detail on the methodology used to generate the dataset, which could affect the reproducibility and transparency of the research.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors provide more details on how the text-to-image model is used to generate images and how the image captioning model is employed to filter out unsuitable images?
- How does the dataset's size (152k images) impact its utility for training large models or testing robustness across different environments?
- Are there plans to expand the dataset to include more diverse scenarios or to increase its size to better represent real-world conditions?
- How do the authors address the potential ethical concerns related to using a generative model to construct the dataset?
- Could the authors provide more details on the methodology used to generate the dataset, particularly how the prompt-image alignment is measured and how the substring matching is performed?
