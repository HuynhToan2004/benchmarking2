 **Summary:**
The paper investigates the robustness of two-time-scale stochastic approximation (TTSA) algorithms against structured perturbations, employing an error-feedback mechanism to enhance communication efficiency. It presents a unified framework for analyzing the impact of different forms of perturbations, including compression, local updates, and delays. The paper demonstrates that TTSA algorithms can maintain the same convergence rate as their unperturbed counterparts, even with these perturbations. This is supported by theoretical guarantees and empirical evidence, showing that the effects of perturbations are negligible after a few iterations. The study is significant as it extends the error-feedback framework from SGD to TTSA, providing a robustness analysis that is crucial for distributed systems.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, with clear and concise proofs that are easy to follow, and the results are presented in a clear and organized manner.
- The problem addressed is relevant and important, focusing on the robustness of two-time-scale stochastic approximation (TTSA) algorithms, which are widely used in machine learning and reinforcement learning.
- The paper provides a unified framework for analyzing the impact of different forms of structured perturbations on TTSA algorithms, which is a significant contribution to the field.
- The theoretical guarantees provided for error-compensated TTSA with arbitrary compressors, local TTSA with periodic global averaging, and TTSA with delayed updates are robust and comprehensive.
- The paper is well-motivated, with clear application examples that demonstrate the relevance and practical implications of the research.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper could benefit from a more detailed comparison with existing works, particularly in terms of the error-feedback mechanism and the specific contributions of the proposed algorithms.
- The proofs and theoretical results are not always clear or intuitive, and some assumptions and lemmas are not well-explained or justified.
- The paper could be improved by including more numerical experiments to support the theoretical claims and to demonstrate the practical performance of the proposed algorithms.
- There are some minor issues with notation and formatting that could be clarified or corrected to enhance the readability and professionalism of the paper.

**Questions:**
