 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel method, Extending Multi-modal Contrastive Representations (Ex-MCR), aimed at extending pre-trained multi-modal contrastive representations to new modalities without requiring paired data. This methodology involves aligning existing spaces into a base-MCR, thereby preserving the semantic alignment of the base-MCR. The Ex-MCR approach integrates various techniques such as different types of data, a decoupled projector, and dense alignment loss to enhance the alignment learning pipeline. The paper demonstrates the effectiveness of Ex-MCR through extensive experiments, showing improved performance on multiple tasks and modalities, including audio-visual, 3D-image, audio-text, visual-text retrieval, and 3D object classification. However, the paper has been critiqued for its lack of clarity in explaining the method's advantages over existing methods like C-MCR and for not providing sufficient details on the experimental setup and results.

**Strengths:**
- The paper addresses a significant and relevant problem in the field of multi-modal learning by proposing a novel method for extending pre-trained multi-modal contrastive representations to new modalities without requiring paired data.
- The methodology is supported by a comprehensive set of experiments, demonstrating the effectiveness of the proposed method across various tasks and modalities, including audio-visual, 3D-image, audio-text, visual-text retrieval, and 3D object classification.
- The paper is well-written, making it accessible and easy to follow, which is crucial for understanding the complexities of the proposed method.
- The use of a decoupled projector and dense alignment loss is highlighted as a novel approach that enhances the alignment learning pipeline, potentially leading to improved performance in multi-modal learning tasks.
- The paper presents a new benchmark for multi-modal learning, showcasing the potential of the proposed method in advancing the state-of-the-art in this field.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks clarity in explaining the advantages of the proposed method over existing methods like C-MCR, particularly in terms of the method's scalability and performance.
- There is a noticeable absence of detailed experimental results and settings, which makes it difficult to fully evaluate the effectiveness and robustness of the proposed method.
- The paper does not adequately address the computational costs and training efficiency of the proposed method, which are crucial factors for practical implementation in real-world scenarios.
- The paper's claims about the method's ability to handle unified representation for more than three modalities are not convincingly supported by the experiments, which primarily focus on extending to two modalities.
- The novelty of the proposed method is questioned due to its similarities with existing methods, particularly in terms of the use of a decoupled projector and dense alignment loss, which have been previously explored in similar contexts.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors clarify the specific advantages of the proposed method over existing methods like C-MCR, particularly in terms of scalability and performance?
- How does the proposed method handle the computational costs and training efficiency, especially in scenarios where multiple modalities need to be aligned?
- Could the authors provide more detailed experimental results and settings to better evaluate the effectiveness and robustness of the proposed method?
- How does the proposed method perform when extended to more than three modalities, and what are the potential challenges or limitations in such scenarios?
- Can the authors elaborate on the method's ability to handle unified representation for more than three modalities, and provide examples or scenarios where this capability is particularly useful?
