 **Summary:**
The paper introduces COPL, a method that leverages MineCLIP to perform open-vocabulary tasks in Minecraft. It utilizes a focal reward system based on the confidence map generated by MineCLIP to guide the agent's behavior towards specific objects in the environment. The methodology involves training an agent to follow natural language instructions, such as "hunt a cow," by converting these instructions into a confidence map that indicates the presence of the target object. This approach is tested in both single-task and multi-task scenarios, demonstrating the method's ability to handle unseen objects and generalize to new tasks. The paper also explores the use of a focal reward system to enhance the agent's ability to navigate towards targets, improving upon the limitations of traditional intrinsic rewards in MineCLIP.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, making it easy to follow and understand the proposed methodologies and their applications.
- The concept of using a focal reward system based on confidence maps generated by MineCLIP is innovative and shows promise in enhancing the agent's ability to navigate towards targets.
- The experiments conducted demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed method, particularly in the single-task and multi-task scenarios, showcasing the method's ability to handle unseen objects and generalize to new tasks.
- The paper provides a clear and detailed explanation of the methodologies used, including the modifications to MineCLIP and the denoising process for confidence maps.
- The use of Minecraft as a testbed for open-vocabulary tasks is a novel and interesting approach, which could inspire further research in this area.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed comparison with other baseline methods, particularly those that use similar confidence maps for guiding behavior in Minecraft.
- The generalizability of the method to other environments or tasks beyond Minecraft is not thoroughly explored, which limits the practical applicability of the findings.
- The paper does not provide a detailed analysis of the limitations of the proposed method, which could help in understanding its scope and applicability.
- The use of ChatGPT for extracting objects from instructions might not be considered open-vocabulary, as it still relies on a pre-trained model's capabilities.
- The method's reliance on MineCLIP's object recognition capabilities raises concerns about its generalizability to other environments or tasks where MineCLIP may not perform as well.
- The paper does not adequately address how the method handles multiple objects with similar appearances, which could lead to incorrect or ineffective targeting.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors clarify how the method handles multiple objects with similar appearances, especially in scenarios where the confidence map might not accurately distinguish between them?
- How does the method ensure that the confidence map accurately reflects the presence of the target object, especially in cases where the object might be partially obscured or overlapping with other objects?
- Could the authors provide more details on the denoising process for confidence maps, particularly how the threshold for setting values to one is determined?
- What are the specific advantages of the focal reward system over other types of rewards, such as those based on distance or visual cues?
- How does the method perform in scenarios where the agent is required to interact with objects that are not visible in the current frame, such as those behind obstacles or in different locations?
- Could the authors discuss the potential limitations of their method and how these might affect its practical application in real-world scenarios?
