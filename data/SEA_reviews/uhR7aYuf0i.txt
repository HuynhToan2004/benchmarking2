 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a meta-learning approach to enhance Stochastic Gradient Markov Chain Monte Carlo (sgMCMC) methods for Bayesian Neural Networks (BNNs). The approach learns the gradient of the kinetic energy term using neural networks, aiming to improve sampling efficiency and reduce computational overhead. The method is evaluated on various image classification tasks and demonstrates better predictive accuracy and functional diversity compared to existing methods. The paper also discusses the meta-learning strategy's ability to transfer learning across different tasks and datasets, although it lacks a thorough theoretical analysis and comparison with more recent methods.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, clear, and easy to follow, with a clear motivation and structure.
- The proposed method is novel and innovative, combining meta-learning with MCMC methods, which is a relatively new area of research.
- The method demonstrates good performance in terms of predictive accuracy and functional diversity, as shown through various experiments.
- The paper includes a comprehensive set of experiments and ablation studies, which are well-documented and provide a thorough evaluation of the proposed method.
- The use of neural networks to approximate the gradient of the kinetic energy term is a novel approach that could potentially improve the efficiency of MCMC methods.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a thorough theoretical analysis, particularly in terms of the convergence and generalization properties of the learned gradient estimator.
- The comparison with existing methods is limited, particularly with more recent and relevant works such as those by Zhang et al. (2020) and Wang et al. (2022).
- The experimental setup and results could be improved by including more detailed comparisons, such as the number of iterations required for convergence and the computational overhead of the proposed method.
- There are several minor typographical and formatting errors throughout the paper that need to be corrected.
- The paper does not adequately address the computational cost and scalability of the proposed method, which are critical for practical applications.
- The method's generalization ability to different datasets and its performance in scenarios with different data distributions are not thoroughly evaluated.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors provide a more detailed theoretical analysis of the convergence and generalization properties of the learned gradient estimator?
- How does the proposed method compare with more recent and relevant works, such as those by Zhang et al. (2020) and Wang et al. (2022)?
- Could the authors clarify the role and impact of the burn-in period in the meta-training procedure, and discuss how the method's performance varies with different settings of the burn-in period?
- How does the proposed method handle the computational cost and scalability issues, particularly in large-scale applications?
- Can the authors provide more detailed experimental results, such as the computational overhead and the number of iterations required for convergence, to better compare the efficiency of the proposed method with existing approaches?
