 **Summary:**
The paper investigates the Borda regret minimization problem in generalized linear dueling bandits, where the contextual vectors associated with each pair of items are known. It introduces an explore-then-commit type algorithm for the stochastic setting and an EXP3-type algorithm for the adversarial linear setting. The paper provides a regret lower bound of order Ω(d2/3T2/3) and a matching upper bound, which are nearly optimal. The theoretical analysis is supported by empirical evaluations on both synthetic data and a simulated real-world environment. The paper also discusses the theoretical implications and practical applications of the proposed algorithms in various settings.

**Strengths:**
- The paper provides a theoretical analysis of the proposed algorithms, which is supported by empirical evaluations, enhancing the credibility of the results.
- The authors have shown a novel construction of a hard instance for the linear model, which is a significant contribution to the field.
- The paper is well-organized, clearly written, and easy to follow, making it accessible to a broad audience.
- The proposed algorithms are novel and achieve optimal regret bounds, which are a significant contribution to the field.
- The paper addresses a generalized linear dueling bandit model, which covers many existing models, providing a comprehensive framework for further research.
- The theoretical results are supported by empirical studies, which validate the theoretical claims and provide practical insights.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a discussion on the assumptions made, particularly the assumption of known contextual vectors, which may not hold in practical scenarios.
- The paper does not compare the proposed algorithms with other existing algorithms in terms of computational efficiency, which is crucial for practical implementation.
- The proof of the lower bound is not clearly explained, which could hinder the understanding of the theoretical contributions.
- The paper does not discuss the potential limitations or challenges of the proposed algorithms in practical settings, which could affect their applicability.
- The paper does not provide a detailed discussion on the computational complexity of the proposed algorithms, which is a significant factor in their practical implementation.
- The paper lacks a discussion on the implications of the results for future research, which could help guide further investigations in the field.
