 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel approach to motion planning using a neural network to learn potentials for trajectory optimization, leveraging diffusion models to refine trajectories and handle multiple motion constraints. The method is evaluated in both simulated and real-world environments, demonstrating improved performance over traditional sampling-based methods and other learned motion planners. It allows for the composition of multiple potentials to handle a variety of motion constraints, enhancing generalization capabilities. The approach also includes a refinement step to handle collisions, which is crucial for practical applications. However, the paper suffers from clarity issues in explaining the refinement step and the overall methodology, and the evaluation could benefit from more rigorous comparisons and a broader range of scenarios.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel approach to motion planning using a neural network to learn potentials for trajectory optimization, which is a significant departure from traditional methods.
- The use of diffusion models to refine trajectories and handle multiple motion constraints is innovative and well-explained.
- The paper demonstrates improved performance over traditional sampling-based methods and other learned motion planners, as evidenced by both qualitative and quantitative results.
- The method allows for the composition of multiple potentials to handle a variety of motion constraints, enhancing its generalization capabilities.
- The paper is well-written, with clear explanations of the methodology and results, making it accessible to a broad audience.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a clear explanation of the refinement step, which is crucial for understanding the method's effectiveness in handling collisions.
- The evaluation could benefit from more rigorous comparisons, particularly with a broader range of baselines and scenarios to better demonstrate the method's advantages.
- There is a lack of clarity in some sections of the paper, making it difficult to follow the methodology and results.
- The paper could benefit from a more detailed discussion on the limitations and potential negative societal impacts of the proposed method.
- The paper's reliance on a large amount of training data and the potential for overfitting to specific environments are not adequately addressed.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors clarify the refinement step and provide more details on how it handles collisions?
- How does the method compare to other learned motion planners that also use potential-based approaches?
- Could the authors provide more details on the training data used and how the method generalizes to new environments?
- How does the method handle different types of obstacles, such as moving or static obstacles?
- Could the authors discuss the limitations of their approach and how these might affect the method's applicability in real-world scenarios?
