 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel framework called PASS, which utilizes visual prompts to guide channel pruning in neural networks. The framework leverages a recurrent hypernetwork to learn sparse masks across layers, incorporating visual prompts and network weight statistics. This approach allows for the generation of high-quality structural sparsity, which is evaluated against several baseline methods on various datasets. The methodology demonstrates significant improvements in accuracy and computational efficiency, showcasing its potential in practical applications. However, concerns were raised regarding the novelty of the approach and the depth of experimental validation, particularly in terms of the generalizability across different network architectures and datasets.

**Strengths:**
- The paper presents a novel approach using visual prompts to guide channel pruning, which is a significant advancement in the field of model compression.
- The proposed method shows superior performance compared to several baseline methods, as demonstrated through rigorous evaluations across multiple datasets and network architectures.
- The paper is well-written, easy to follow, and provides comprehensive experimental results that validate the effectiveness of the proposed method.
- The idea of using visual prompts to guide channel pruning is innovative and could potentially be applied to other areas such as language models.
- The paper includes a detailed ablation study that provides insights into the effectiveness of different components of the proposed method.

**Weaknesses:**
- The novelty of the proposed method is limited as it primarily combines existing techniques without introducing significant new insights or methodologies.
- The paper lacks a thorough discussion on the transferability of the learned sparse structure across different datasets and network architectures, which is crucial for practical applications.
- The experimental results are not sufficiently robust, particularly in terms of the statistical significance of the improvements and the generalizability of the findings.
- The paper does not adequately address the limitations of the proposed method, such as its applicability to different network architectures and the potential for overfitting due to the use of visual prompts.
- The paper could benefit from a more detailed comparison with existing methods, including a discussion on how the proposed method compares in terms of computational efficiency and performance.

**Questions:**
- Could you clarify how the proposed method compares with other recent methods that also use visual prompts for channel pruning?
- How does the proposed method perform when applied to other network architectures, such as Vision Transformers?
- Can you provide more details on the transferability of the learned sparse structure across different datasets and network architectures?
- How does the proposed method handle the potential for overfitting due to the use of visual prompts?
- Could you elaborate on the computational efficiency of the proposed method, particularly in terms of the training and inference times compared to other methods?
- How does the proposed method handle the issue of sparsity pathways, especially in the context of recurrent mechanisms?
