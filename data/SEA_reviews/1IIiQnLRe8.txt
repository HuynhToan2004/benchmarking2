 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel method, Bi-directional Regularized Diversity Modulation (BiRDM), for semantic shift detection in deep neural networks. BiRDM leverages smoothness regularization and batch normalization simulation to manage feature diversity and prevent over-generalization to unseen semantic shifts. It also incorporates auxiliary data to enhance the discriminability between in-distribution (ID) and out-of-distribution (OOD) samples. The method is evaluated on various datasets, demonstrating its effectiveness in detecting semantic shifts. However, the paper lacks comprehensive experimental results, particularly on large-scale datasets like ImageNet, and the clarity of presentation needs improvement.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel approach to modeling feature diversity for semantic shift detection, which is a significant contribution to the field.
- The use of smoothness regularization and batch normalization simulation is innovative and helps in managing feature diversity effectively.
- The paper is well-written, making it easy to follow, and includes detailed explanations of the methodology and experimental setups.
- The proposed method shows effectiveness in experiments, demonstrating its practical utility.
- The paper is well-motivated, addressing the challenges of modeling feature diversity for semantic shift detection, which is a relevant and challenging problem.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks comprehensive experimental results, particularly on large-scale datasets such as ImageNet, which could strengthen the validity of the proposed method.
- There is a lack of clarity in the presentation of the paper, particularly in the introduction and the methodology sections, which could confuse readers.
- The paper does not adequately address the limitations of the proposed method, which is crucial for understanding the scope and applicability of the findings.
- The paper's dependence on auxiliary data might limit its applicability in scenarios where such data is not available.
- The paper could benefit from a more detailed discussion on the hyperparameters used and their impact on the results.
- The paper does not sufficiently compare the proposed method with other state-of-the-art methods, which could provide a more robust evaluation of its effectiveness.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors provide more details on how the proposed method performs on large-scale datasets like ImageNet?
- How does the proposed method compare with other state-of-the-art methods in terms of effectiveness and efficiency?
- Could the authors provide a more detailed discussion on the limitations of the proposed method and how these might affect its practical application?
- How do the hyperparameters used in the experiments affect the results, and could the authors provide more detailed insights into their selection and impact?
- Could the authors clarify the role and impact of the auxiliary data in the proposed method, particularly in scenarios where such data is not available?
- How does the proposed method handle different types of semantic shifts, and what are the implications of these variations on the method's effectiveness?
