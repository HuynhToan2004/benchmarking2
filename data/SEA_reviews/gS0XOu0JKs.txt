 **Summary:**
The paper introduces an uncertainty-aware in-context learning framework to enhance the reliability of Large Language Models (LLMs) by filtering out responses with high uncertainty. The approach involves fine-tuning the LLM using a calibration dataset to refine the model's responses based on uncertainty scores. The paper proposes using a calibration dataset to estimate uncertainty and correct responses accordingly. However, the method's effectiveness is questioned due to the lack of comprehensive experiments and comparisons with state-of-the-art models. The paper also suffers from poor presentation, including unclear figures, grammatical errors, and insufficiently detailed methodologies.

**Strengths:**
- The paper addresses a significant and timely problem in the field of Large Language Models (LLMs) by focusing on the reliability of LLMs, which is crucial for practical applications.
- The proposed method of using uncertainty-aware in-context learning to enhance LLMs is innovative and could potentially improve the reliability of LLMs.
- The paper provides a comprehensive review of existing uncertainty estimation methods and integrates them into the proposed method.
- The authors have conducted extensive experiments to demonstrate the effectiveness of their approach, showing that the logit output values of LLMs reflect inherent uncertainty and that the model can autonomously recognize uncertainty, leading to improved responses.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a thorough comparison with state-of-the-art models, making it difficult to assess the effectiveness of the proposed method against the current best practices.
- The methodology and implementation details are insufficiently described, making it difficult for readers to replicate or understand the proposed method fully.
- The paper suffers from poor presentation, including unclear figures, grammatical errors, and a lack of clarity in the writing, which makes it difficult to follow the argumentation and the experimental setup.
- The experiments are not sufficiently detailed or comprehensive, with insufficient baseline comparisons and a lack of clarity on the experimental setup and results.
- The paper does not adequately address the limitations of the proposed method, such as the potential for over-confidence in the model's responses and the impact of different uncertainty estimation methods on the results.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors clarify the experimental setup, including the specifics of the calibration dataset and the training process for the LLM?
- How does the proposed method compare to other state-of-the-art methods for improving the reliability of LLMs, such as those that use different uncertainty estimation methods or other types of in-context learning?
- What are the potential limitations of the proposed method, and how might these affect the reliability of the LLM's responses?
- Can the authors provide more detailed comparisons with other uncertainty estimation methods, such as those mentioned in the related work section?
- How does the proposed method handle different types of questions, such as those that are ambiguous or have multiple correct answers?
- Can the authors elaborate on the impact of the number of responses generated on the results and the computational cost of the proposed method?
