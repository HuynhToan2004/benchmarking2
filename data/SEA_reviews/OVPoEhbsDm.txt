 **Summary:**
The paper introduces Refine-PPI, a framework designed to predict the mutation effect on protein-protein interactions (PPI) by addressing the challenges of obtaining mutant protein structures and integrating dynamics into deep learning architectures. The framework incorporates a structure refinement module trained on mask mutation modeling and a new geometric network, PDC-Net, to capture dynamic 3D variations and encode uncertainty. The approach is evaluated on the SKEMPI benchmark dataset, showing superior performance over existing methods. However, the paper faces criticisms for its unclear motivation, insufficient ablation studies, and lack of comparison with state-of-the-art methods.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel framework, Refine-PPI, which addresses the challenge of predicting mutation effects on protein-protein interactions (PPI) by focusing on the absence of mutant protein structures and the dynamic nature of interactions.
- The proposed PDC-Net is innovative, integrating the dynamics of atomic resolution into geometric GNNs, which is crucial for accurately modeling protein-protein interactions.
- The paper is well-written, making it easy to follow, and the experimental results are convincing, showing the effectiveness of the proposed method.
- The introduction of a structure refinement module trained by a mask mutation modeling task and the use of a new geometric network to capture dynamic 3D variations are significant contributions to the field.
- The paper is comprehensive, providing a detailed introduction of the proposed method and extensive experimental results, including a case study of 16 seed complexes with different numbers of mutations.

**Weaknesses:**
- The motivation behind the proposed framework is not clearly articulated, making it difficult to understand the rationale behind the design choices.
- The paper lacks sufficient ablation studies to validate the effectiveness of each component of the framework.
- There is a lack of comparison with state-of-the-art methods, which could have provided a clearer picture of the framework's performance relative to current leading techniques.
- The paper does not adequately address the limitations of the proposed framework, such as the potential for overfitting due to the limited size of the SKEMPI dataset.
- The paper does not provide a detailed discussion on the computational complexity of the proposed framework, which is crucial for understanding its practical applicability.
- The paper does not include a detailed discussion on the limitations of the proposed framework, such as the potential for overfitting due to the limited size of the SKEMPI dataset.

**Questions:**

- How does the proposed PDC-Net integrate the dynamics of atomic resolution into geometric GNNs, and what are the specific advantages of this approach over traditional methods?
- Why was the SKEMPI dataset chosen for evaluation, and how does it ensure the generalizability of the findings to real-world applications?
- Could the authors provide more detailed comparisons with state-of-the-art methods to better understand the relative performance of the Refine-PPI framework?
- What are the specific challenges in obtaining the structures of mutant proteins, and how does the Refine-PPI framework address these challenges?
- How does the Refine-PPI framework integrate the dynamics of interactions into the deep learning architecture, and what are the implications of this approach for the accuracy and applicability of the predictions?
