 **Summary:**
The paper introduces HTFlows, a novel framework for flow-based graph generation that addresses heterophily in molecular contexts. HTFlows utilizes multiple interactive flows to learn graph structures and node features across varying degrees of homophily and heterophily, demonstrating its effectiveness through experiments on QM9 and ZINC-250k datasets. The model leverages heterophily to generate molecules, addressing the issue of oversmoothing in GNNs. It is evaluated against existing baselines and shows promising results in terms of molecule generation and property optimization. However, the paper lacks a detailed discussion on how it addresses the heterophily issue specifically, and the experimental results do not convincingly outperform existing methods.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, clear, and easy to follow, with a clear presentation of the methodology.
- The problem of heterophily in molecular graphs is highlighted, which is often overlooked in existing works.
- The proposed method, HTFlows, is novel and innovative, using multiple interactive flows to capture heterophily patterns in molecular space.
- The method is evaluated on two benchmark datasets, demonstrating its effectiveness in molecular generation and property optimization.
- The paper provides a detailed discussion on the limitations and potential negative societal impacts of the proposed method, which is commendable for transparency.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed discussion on how the proposed method specifically addresses the heterophily issue in molecular graphs, which is a critical aspect of the research.
- The experimental results do not convincingly outperform existing methods like MoFlow and GraphDF in terms of molecule generation, and the improvements are marginal.
- The paper does not include a comprehensive ablation study to demonstrate the effectiveness of each component of the proposed method, which is crucial for understanding the model's contributions.
- The paper could benefit from a more detailed comparison with existing methods, particularly in terms of the novelty and effectiveness of the heterophily-aware message passing scheme.
- The computational complexity and training time of the proposed method are not discussed, which is important for practical implementation.
- The paper could be improved by including more visualizations of the latent space, such as 2D embeddings, to better illustrate the model's capabilities.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors clarify how the proposed method specifically addresses the heterophily issue in molecular graphs?
- How does the proposed method compare in terms of computational complexity and training time with existing methods like MoFlow and GraphDF?
- Could the authors provide more detailed visualizations of the latent space, such as 2D embeddings, to better illustrate the model's capabilities?
- How does the proposed method handle the issue of oversmoothing in GNNs, and what specific advantages does it offer over existing methods?
- Can the authors provide more details on the experimental setup, such as the hyperparameters used and the training conditions, to ensure reproducibility and transparency?
- How does the proposed method perform in terms of generating valid molecules, especially in comparison to existing methods like MoFlow and GraphDF?
