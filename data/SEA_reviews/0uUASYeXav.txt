 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel object-centric reinforcement learning (RL) algorithm, termed ROCA, which combines actor-critic and model-based approaches using an object-centric world model in the critic module. This model-based world model is designed to handle both discrete and continuous action spaces, enhancing sample efficiency and performance in complex environments. The ROCA algorithm is evaluated in simulated 3D robotic and 2D environments, demonstrating competitive performance against state-of-the-art baselines. Despite its innovative approach, the paper is critiqued for its lack of significant experimental validation, particularly in more challenging environments, and the need for more thorough comparisons with other model-based RL algorithms.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel approach by combining actor-critic and model-based methods, which is a significant advancement in object-centric reinforcement learning.
- The use of a pre-trained SLATE model for object-centric feature extraction is a promising direction that enhances the performance of model-based RL algorithms.
- The paper is well-written and clearly presents the methodology and experimental results, making it accessible and understandable to readers.
- The experimental results demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed ROCA algorithm, showing improvements over model-free and model-based baselines in simulated environments.
- The integration of object-centric representations with model-based RL is a significant contribution to the field, potentially enhancing the sample efficiency and generalization capabilities of RL agents.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks significant experimental validation, particularly in more challenging environments such as those involving more complex dynamics or a higher number of objects.
- The comparison with other model-based RL algorithms like Dreamer and DreamerV3 is not comprehensive enough, which limits the understanding of the ROCA's relative performance.
- The performance of ROCA in more complex environments like Habitat or other realistic datasets remains unclear, which is crucial for evaluating its practical applicability.
- The paper does not adequately address the limitations of the SLATE model, which is crucial for the performance of the ROCA algorithm.
- The novelty of the method is questioned as it primarily builds upon existing works like CSWM, with the main contribution being the application of a GNN-based object-centric world model for policy learning in the RL setting.
- The paper does not sufficiently discuss the limitations of the proposed method, such as its sensitivity to the target entropy hyperparameter and its scalability to more complex environments.

**Questions:**
- Can you clarify how the ROCA algorithm handles stochastic environments, particularly in terms of predicting the dynamics of objects?
- How does the ROCA algorithm compare with other model-based RL algorithms like Dreamer and DreamerV3 in terms of performance and sample efficiency?
- Could you provide more details on the experimental setup, particularly the hyperparameters used and how they were tuned?
- How does the ROCA algorithm perform in more complex environments like Habitat, and what are the challenges in applying it to such settings?
- Can you discuss the potential of the ROCA algorithm in more general RL settings, beyond the specific environments tested in this study?
- How does the ROCA algorithm address the limitations of the SLATE model, and what are the implications of these limitations on the ROCA's performance?
