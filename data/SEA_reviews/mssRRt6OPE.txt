 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel approach to relevance retrieval by focusing on the use of relevance-based embeddings to enhance the efficiency and effectiveness of the retrieval process. The methodology involves embedding queries and items into a vector space based on their relevance to a set of support items, which are chosen strategically to optimize the retrieval process. The paper presents several strategies for selecting support items and compares the proposed method with existing dual encoder models, showing significant improvements in performance. Theoretical justifications and empirical validations are provided to support the proposed method's effectiveness.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel approach to relevance retrieval, focusing on the use of relevance-based embeddings, which is a significant departure from traditional methods.
- The methodology is well-articulated, with clear explanations of the theoretical underpinnings and empirical validations.
- The paper is well-organized, making it easy to follow and understand, with clear presentation of the proposed method and its evaluation.
- The results demonstrate significant improvements over existing methods, showing the effectiveness of the proposed approach.
- The paper provides a clear and intuitive explanation of the proposed method, which is crucial for understanding its application and potential impact.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed comparison with other relevant methods, such as those mentioned in related works, which could have strengthened the argument for the proposed method's effectiveness.
- The choice of support items and their impact on the retrieval process is not adequately justified, which could limit the generalizability of the findings.
- The paper's focus on the use of relevance-based embeddings could be seen as a limitation, as it might not apply to all types of retrieval tasks.
- There are some typographical and formatting errors that need to be corrected for clarity and professionalism.
- The paper does not sufficiently discuss the scalability of the proposed method, which is crucial for practical applications.

**Questions:**
- Could you clarify the choice of support items and how their selection impacts the retrieval process?
- How does the proposed method compare with other methods mentioned in related works, particularly those that use different embedding techniques?
- Can you provide more details on the scalability of the proposed method, especially in large-scale applications?
- How does the method perform when the support items are selected randomly?
- Could you elaborate on the differences between the proposed method and other relevance-based methods, particularly in terms of their theoretical underpinnings and practical applications?
