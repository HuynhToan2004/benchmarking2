 **Summary:**
The paper introduces ARTDiff, a novel method aimed at enhancing temporal consistency in diffusion models for sequential data generation, such as audio, motion, and video. ARTDiff modifies the noise distribution by incorporating autoregressive techniques directly into the diffusion process, which allows for the effective capture and preservation of temporal dependencies inherent in sequential data. This method maintains the efficient non-autoregressive sampling scheme of diffusion models while incurring minimal computational overhead, making it suitable for generating long sequences. The paper presents extensive experiments across various tasks, demonstrating that ARTDiff significantly improves temporal coherence, preserves fidelity, and captures long-range dependencies. However, the paper has been critiqued for its lack of novelty, as similar approaches have been explored in previous works, and for insufficient comparison with state-of-the-art methods.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel method, ARTDiff, for addressing the challenge of temporal inconsistency in diffusion models, which is particularly relevant for generating sequential data such as audio, motion, and video.
- The proposed method is simple yet effective, requiring minimal computational overhead and demonstrating strong performance across various tasks.
- The paper is well-written, making it easy to follow, and includes a comprehensive evaluation that demonstrates the effectiveness of the proposed method.
- The methodology is well-motivated and the paper is well-organized, with clear explanations of the methodology and results.
- The experiments are extensive, covering a wide range of applications, and the results showcase the method's ability to enhance temporal coherence, preserve fidelity, and capture long-range dependencies.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a clear comparison with state-of-the-art methods, particularly in terms of performance and computational efficiency.
- There is a lack of novelty in the proposed method, as similar approaches have been explored in previous works.
- The paper does not sufficiently address the computational cost of the proposed method, especially in terms of inference time.
- The experimental results are not convincing, with some results showing no improvement or even a decrease in performance compared to baseline models.
- The paper lacks a detailed discussion on the limitations of the proposed method, especially in terms of its applicability to different types of data and its scalability to larger datasets.
- There is a need for more comprehensive ablation studies to better understand the impact of different design choices and hyperparameters on the performance of the proposed method.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors provide a more detailed comparison with state-of-the-art methods in terms of performance and computational efficiency?
- How does the proposed method compare to other recent works that address similar issues in diffusion models, such as those mentioned in the paper?
- Can the authors clarify the discrepancies in the experimental results, particularly the lack of improvement or even a decrease in performance in some cases?
- How does the proposed method handle the trade-off between computational efficiency and performance, especially in terms of inference time?
- Could the authors provide more detailed insights into the design choices and hyperparameters used in the proposed method, and how these affect the performance across different tasks and datasets?
- How does the proposed method perform in scenarios where the temporal consistency is not the primary concern, such as in image generation?
