 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel method for constructing interpretable word representations by utilizing a pre-trained word embedding matrix and a transition matrix derived from WordNet. This method aims to enhance the interpretability of word vectors by reducing them to a smaller, more manageable size, which can be visualized and understood more easily. The paper also proposes a hierarchical vector approach to improve performance in downstream tasks. Despite its innovative approach, the paper has been critiqued for its lack of clarity in presentation, the absence of a comprehensive related work section, and insufficient experimental validation.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel approach to constructing interpretable word representations using a pre-trained word embedding matrix and a transition matrix derived from WordNet.
- The method proposed is innovative and has the potential to enhance the interpretability of word vectors, which is crucial for understanding the meaning of words in natural language processing.
- The paper is well-written, making it easy to follow and understand the proposed methodology and experimental results.
- The experimental results show that the proposed method can improve the interpretability of word vectors, which is a significant contribution to the field.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks clarity in its presentation, particularly in the definitions and explanations of key terms and concepts.
- The related work section is inadequate, missing out on several relevant studies that have explored similar topics.
- The experimental validation is insufficient, with only a few datasets used, and the results are not convincingly superior to existing methods.
- The paper's methodology and results are not sufficiently supported by a detailed discussion of the limitations and potential negative societal impacts of the proposed approach.
- The paper does not adequately address the issue of polysemy, which is a significant limitation in the interpretability of word vectors.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors clarify the definitions and roles of terms such as "syntactic representation," "hierarchical vectors," and "syntactic subspace"?
- How does the proposed method compare to existing methods in terms of interpretability and performance?
- Can the authors provide more detailed explanations of the methodology and results, particularly in sections where the paper is less clear?
- How does the proposed method address the issue of polysemy, and what are the implications of this limitation for the interpretability of word vectors?
- Could the authors expand the related work section to include more recent and relevant studies, and discuss how their methodology differs or builds upon these existing works?
