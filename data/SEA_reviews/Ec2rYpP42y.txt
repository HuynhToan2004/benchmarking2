 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel method, UFODM, for solving inverse problems with unspecified forward operators using diffusion models. The method leverages a Bayesian framework to estimate both the reconstructed image and the characteristics of the forward operator during the inference stage, which is a significant departure from traditional methods that require prior knowledge of the forward operator. The approach is demonstrated to be effective in various tasks such as blind deconvolution, JPEG restoration, and super-resolution, showing promise in real-world applications. However, the paper has been critiqued for its lack of novelty, as it builds upon existing methods like GibbsDDRM, and for its unclear theoretical underpinnings and insufficient experimental validation.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel method for solving inverse problems with unspecified forward operators, which is a significant advancement in the field.
- The methodology is well-articulated, with clear explanations of the mathematical formulation and the experimental setup.
- The paper is well-written, making it accessible and easy to follow, with a clear introduction and a well-structured experimental section.
- The proposed method demonstrates superior performance in various tasks such as blind deconvolution, JPEG restoration, and super-resolution, showing its effectiveness in practical applications.
- The use of a diffusion model to solve inverse problems is a promising approach that has shown success in recent years, and this paper extends this approach to more complex scenarios.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks novelty as it heavily relies on existing methods like GibbsDDRM and does not sufficiently differentiate itself from these prior works.
- The theoretical underpinnings of the method are not well-explained, particularly the convergence of the method and the theoretical justification for the MAP estimation used.
- The experimental validation is insufficient, with a limited number of baseline comparisons and a lack of quantitative results for the blind setting.
- There are inconsistencies and potential errors in the experimental setup and results, such as the use of different datasets and resolutions without clear justification.
- The paper lacks a thorough discussion on the limitations of the method and does not provide sufficient details on the computational complexity and scalability of the proposed approach.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors clarify the theoretical justification for the MAP estimation used in the method and provide a convergence analysis?
- How does the proposed method compare to other state-of-the-art methods in terms of computational complexity and scalability?
- In the experimental setup, why were different datasets and resolutions used, and how do these choices affect the results?
- Could the authors provide more details on the optimization of the forward operator and the selection of the optimal operator?
- How does the method handle different types of forward operators, particularly those that are non-differentiable or non-linear?
- Can the authors provide more quantitative results for the blind setting to better assess the performance of the method in practical scenarios?
