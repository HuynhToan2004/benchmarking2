 **Summary:**
The paper explores the generalization properties of stochastic gradient descent (SGD) through the lens of stochastic differential equations (SDEs), specifically focusing on the dynamics of SGD approximated by SDEs. It derives two generalization bounds using mutual information-based analysis, one based on the full trajectory and the other on the terminal state. The first bound is time-dependent and shows how the generalization error grows with the number of training steps, while the second bound is time-independent and provides a norm-based bound. The paper also empirically validates these bounds against SGD and SDE approximations, demonstrating their effectiveness in characterizing the generalization dynamics of SGD. However, the paper faces criticisms for its reliance on Gaussian noise assumptions, lack of novelty, and potential inaccuracies in the SDE approximation.

**Strengths:**
- The paper provides a comprehensive analysis of SGD using stochastic differential equations (SDEs), including the derivation of generalization bounds for SGD and SDE approximations.
- The paper is well-organized, with clear explanations and detailed derivations of the results, which are supported by empirical validations.
- The analysis of SGD dynamics using SDEs and mutual information-based analysis is novel and adds depth to the understanding of SGD behavior.
- The paper includes both trajectory-based and terminal-state-based bounds, which are significant contributions to the field of generalization error analysis.
- The paper is well-written, making it accessible and easy to follow, with clear explanations of the assumptions and limitations.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper relies heavily on the assumption that the gradient noise is Gaussian, which may not be true in practical scenarios, limiting the generalizability of the results.
- The derivation of the bounds is not entirely novel, as similar bounds have been derived in previous works, such as Wang and Mao (2022).
- The paper's analysis is limited to the approximation of SGD by SDEs, which may not accurately reflect the true behavior of SGD in practical settings.
- The paper lacks a discussion on the implications of the findings for practical applications, which could enhance its relevance and impact.
- There is a potential issue with the accuracy of the SDE approximation, as it may not capture the true behavior of SGD in certain scenarios.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors clarify the assumptions regarding the independence of the gradient noise and the data samples?
- How does the paper address the potential inaccuracies in the SDE approximation, and what implications does this have for the validity of the bounds derived?
- Could the authors elaborate on the choice of using a Gaussian prior in the analysis and how this affects the results?
- What are the implications of the findings for the practical application of SGD, and how can these insights be used to improve the generalization performance of SGD in real-world scenarios?
- Could the authors provide more details on the experimental setup and the specific conditions under which the bounds were derived and validated?
