 **Summary:**
The paper introduces Tactis-2, a novel model for multivariate time series prediction that builds upon the Tactis framework, addressing limitations in scalability and training dynamics. Tactis-2 introduces a two-stage training procedure that significantly reduces the number of parameters needed, thereby simplifying the training process and enhancing performance. The model's architecture includes a dual encoder and a decoder that processes marginal and copula distributions separately, enabling faster training and improved convergence. Tactis-2 is validated against Tactis and other state-of-the-art models on various datasets, demonstrating superior performance in forecasting and interpolation tasks.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel two-stage training procedure for Tactis, which significantly improves the training dynamics and performance over the original Tactis.
- The architecture of Tactis-2 is well-explained, making it easy to follow and understand the proposed methodologies.
- The paper provides a comprehensive theoretical analysis, including the proof of the validity of the proposed model, which adds to its credibility and depth.
- The results demonstrate that Tactis-2 outperforms the original Tactis in various benchmarks, highlighting its effectiveness and advancement over existing methods.
- The paper is well-written, with clear explanations and detailed theoretical proofs, which are supported by rigorous experiments and ablation studies.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed discussion on the limitations of the proposed method, which could provide a more balanced view of its strengths and weaknesses.
- The experimental results are not sufficiently robust, with insufficient statistical evidence to support the claims made, particularly concerning the superiority of Tactis-2 over Tactis in all datasets.
- The paper does not adequately discuss the computational efficiency of Tactis-2 compared to Tactis, which could be a significant concern for practical applications.
- The paper does not provide sufficient comparisons with other recent state-of-the-art methods, which could help in better positioning the contributions of Tactis-2.
- There are some minor typographical errors and inconsistencies in the paper, which need to be corrected for clarity and professionalism.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors provide more details on the computational efficiency of Tactis-2 compared to Tactis, especially in terms of training and inference times?
- How does Tactis-2 compare to other state-of-the-art methods in terms of performance and efficiency?
- Can the authors clarify the differences between Tactis-2 and Tactis in terms of their theoretical contributions and practical implications?
- Why was the decision made to use a dual encoder in Tactis-2, and how does it impact the model's performance and efficiency?
- Could the authors provide more details on the experimental setup and results, particularly in the areas where Tactis-2 underperforms compared to Tactis?
- How does the proposed method handle missing data, and what are the implications of this on the model's performance?
