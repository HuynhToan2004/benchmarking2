 **Summary:**
The paper introduces CommFormer, a novel method for learning multi-agent communication using a graph-based approach. CommFormer optimizes communication graphs and network parameters through a bi-level optimization process, enabling efficient communication among agents. It employs continuous relaxation of graph representations and attention mechanisms to optimize the communication graph and network parameters concurrently. The method was tested across various cooperative tasks, demonstrating superior performance compared to existing methods. However, concerns were raised about the novelty of the approach, as it resembles prior works such as MAGIC, and the clarity of the experimental results and methodological descriptions.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel approach to multi-agent communication, specifically focusing on the learning of communication graphs, which is a significant advancement in the field.
- The methodology is well-supported by extensive experiments across various cooperative tasks, demonstrating the robustness and effectiveness of the proposed approach.
- The paper is well-written, with clear and concise explanations of the methodology and results, making it accessible to readers.
- The use of a graph-based approach to model communication between agents is innovative and could potentially enhance the efficiency and effectiveness of multi-agent communication.
- The paper includes a comprehensive set of experiments, including ablation studies, which help in understanding the impact of different components of the proposed method.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a clear differentiation from existing works, particularly MAGIC, which also employs a similar approach to learning communication graphs.
- The experimental results are not sufficiently detailed, making it difficult to assess the effectiveness and reliability of the proposed method.
- The paper could benefit from a more rigorous evaluation, including comparisons with a broader range of baselines and a more detailed analysis of the results.
- The methodology, particularly the use of a bi-level optimization process and the handling of continuous relaxation of graph representations, is not well-explained, which could confuse readers.
- The paper's contribution to the field is somewhat limited due to the similarity of the proposed method to existing works, and the lack of a significant novel technical contribution.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors clarify the key differences between their proposed method and MAGIC, particularly in terms of the learning of communication graphs and the optimization process?
- How does the proposed method handle scenarios where agents have different communication requirements or capabilities?
- Could the authors provide more detailed results and analysis, including the performance of the proposed method across different sparsity levels and the impact of different hyperparameters on the results?
- How does the proposed method handle the bi-level optimization process, and what are the implications of this approach for the training and execution of the model?
- Can the authors explain the handling of continuous relaxation of graph representations in more detail, and how does this affect the performance and effectiveness of the model?
