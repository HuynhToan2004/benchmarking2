 **Summary:**
The paper investigates the duality gap in constrained cooperative multi-agent reinforcement learning (MARL), demonstrating that strong duality fails to hold in this setting. The authors propose a decentralized primal algorithm to address this issue and provide convergence results. They also discuss the limitations of existing primal-dual algorithms and introduce a new algorithm that does not rely on the duality gap. The paper includes theoretical analyses and numerical experiments to support its claims, highlighting the challenges and potential solutions in constrained cooperative MARL.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, clear, and easy to follow, with a logical structure that aids in understanding the content.
- It provides a novel analysis of the duality gap in constrained cooperative MARL, which is an important and relevant topic for the field.
- The paper includes detailed proofs and rigorous theoretical analysis, supported by numerical experiments that demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed algorithms.
- The authors have identified a significant gap in the literature, specifically the lack of proof for strong duality in constrained cooperative MARL, and have provided a clear explanation of this gap.
- The paper offers a fresh perspective by reanalyzing the convergence of primal-dual algorithms in constrained cooperative MARL, which could inspire future research in this area.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a clear explanation of the relevance and significance of the proposed algorithms, particularly how they address the identified gaps in existing methods.
- The experimental results are limited to simple examples, which may not adequately demonstrate the practical applicability of the proposed algorithms in more complex scenarios.
- The paper does not sufficiently discuss the limitations of its approach, such as the potential for suboptimal solutions or the scalability of the proposed algorithms in larger, more complex environments.
- There is a need for more detailed comparisons with existing methods, including a discussion on the computational complexity and scalability of the proposed algorithms.
- The paper could benefit from a more comprehensive discussion on the assumptions made in the proofs and the implications of these assumptions on the generalizability of the results.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors provide a more detailed explanation of how the proposed algorithms address the identified gaps in existing methods?
- How do the authors envision the practical application of the proposed algorithms in more complex, real-world scenarios?
- In light of the limited experimental results, could the authors consider expanding the scope of their experiments to include more diverse and complex scenarios?
- How do the authors address the potential for suboptimal solutions in their proposed algorithms, and what steps can be taken to mitigate this risk?
- Could the authors clarify the assumptions made in their proofs and discuss the implications of these assumptions on the generalizability of their results?
