 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel machine learning-based framework, ConPaS, designed to predict solutions for Mixed Integer Linear Programs (MILPs) using a contrastive learning approach. The method involves training a neural network to predict the probability of binary variables taking specific values based on positive and negative samples, which are generated through perturbations of optimal solutions. The framework is evaluated against existing methods like Neural Diving and Predict-and-Search, demonstrating improved performance in terms of solution quality and runtime. The paper also discusses the generalization capabilities of the model, showing that it can effectively handle larger instances. However, concerns are raised about the novelty of the method, the clarity of the presentation, and the robustness of the empirical results.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, making it easy to follow and understand.
- The proposed method is straightforward and innovative, particularly in the use of contrastive learning for negative sampling in MILP problems.
- The method shows promising results, outperforming existing methods like Neural Diving and Predict-and-Search in terms of solution quality and runtime.
- The paper provides a clear explanation of the methodology and includes comprehensive experiments that demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed method.
- The use of contrastive learning for negative sampling is a novel approach, which enhances the method's ability to learn from both positive and negative samples.

**Weaknesses:**
- The novelty of the method is limited as it primarily builds upon existing works like Neural Diving and Predict-and-Search, with only minor modifications.
- The paper lacks a detailed discussion on the limitations of the proposed method, particularly in terms of its applicability to other combinatorial optimization problems and its scalability to larger instances.
- The presentation of the paper could be improved, particularly in terms of clarity and organization, especially in the experimental setup and results sections.
- The paper does not adequately discuss the potential negative societal impacts of the proposed method.
- The empirical results, while promising, are not convincingly superior to existing methods, and the paper does not sufficiently compare the proposed method with state-of-the-art solvers like Gurobi.
- There is a lack of discussion on the computational complexity and scalability of the proposed method, which could limit its practical application in larger or more complex MILP problems.

**Questions:**
- How does the proposed method compare with other learning-based methods for MILP, especially those that use contrastive learning like CL-LNS?
- Could you clarify the experimental setup, particularly the generation of negative samples and the selection of hyperparameters?
- How does the proposed method handle instances with more than two binary variables? Is the method limited to binary MILP problems?
- What are the specific advantages of using contrastive learning over other methods for negative sampling in MILP problems?
- Can you provide more details on the computational complexity and scalability of the proposed method, especially in comparison to other state-of-the-art methods?
- How does the proposed method handle instances where the optimal solution is not unique?
- What are the potential negative societal impacts of the proposed method, and how are these addressed or mitigated?
