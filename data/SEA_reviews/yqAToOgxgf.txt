 **Summary:**
The paper presents an innovative approach by enhancing an old neural network architecture, the Pi-Sigma block, using modern techniques such as skip connections, normalization, and data augmentation. This revised architecture, termed RPS, is evaluated across various benchmarks including CIFAR, ImageNet, and medical imaging datasets. The authors conduct extensive ablation studies to determine the impact of each technique on performance, showing that RPS can achieve competitive results with contemporary models. The study also explores the role of different architectural components and their interactions, providing insights into the effectiveness of various techniques in neural network design.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, easy to follow, and clearly presents the motivation and the experimental results.
- The study provides a comprehensive evaluation of the impact of various architectural components and their interactions, offering valuable insights into the effectiveness of different techniques in neural network design.
- The paper includes a detailed ablation study on the effectiveness of each technique, which helps in understanding the contributions of individual components to the overall performance.
- The experimental results are robust, demonstrating that the revised architecture, RPS, can perform competitively with contemporary models, despite its older origins.
- The paper includes a thorough comparison with different neural network architectures, including both modern and classic models, which provides a comprehensive view of the performance of the RPS architecture.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a clear explanation of why certain techniques, such as skip connections and normalization, were chosen over others, which could affect the perceived novelty and the depth of the analysis.
- There is a significant reliance on existing techniques without sufficient discussion on how these techniques were selected or why they were chosen, which could undermine the perceived originality and significance of the study.
- The paper does not sufficiently address the limitations of the Pi-Sigma architecture, which could affect the generalizability and applicability of the findings.
- The experiments are limited to specific datasets, and it is unclear how the results would generalize to other datasets or different domains, which could limit the practical impact of the findings.
- The paper does not include a discussion on the potential negative societal impacts of the research, which is a crucial aspect of ethical research.

**Questions:**
- Could you clarify the rationale behind the choice of specific techniques, such as skip connections and normalization, and how these choices affect the overall performance and design of the RPS architecture?
- How do the findings of this study compare with other classic neural network architectures, such as MLP and RBFN, and what are the key differences in terms of performance and design?
- Can you provide more details on the data augmentation techniques used in the experiments, particularly the specific strategies and their impact on the performance of the RPS architecture?
- How does the RPS architecture perform in terms of computational efficiency, especially in comparison to other modern architectures like ViT and MLP-Mixer?
- Could you discuss the potential societal impacts of this research, particularly in terms of ethical considerations and the broader implications of the findings?
