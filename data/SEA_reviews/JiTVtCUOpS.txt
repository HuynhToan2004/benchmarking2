 **Summary:**
The paper introduces LIFT, a novel method designed to enhance multivariate time series (MTS) forecasting by leveraging lead-lag relationships between variates. This method involves estimating leading indicators and refining predictions using a lead-aware refiner, which operates in the frequency domain. The authors propose that this approach can improve forecasting accuracy by utilizing the information from leading indicators effectively. The paper presents an algorithm for estimating leading indicators and a refinement method using an adaptive frequency mixer. Extensive experiments on six datasets demonstrate that LIFT outperforms existing state-of-the-art methods, although the improvements are marginal in some cases. The paper also discusses the limitations of the method and suggests areas for future research.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written and clearly presents the proposed method, which is intuitive and easy to follow.
- The use of leading indicators to improve forecasting performance is a novel and interesting approach.
- The paper includes extensive experiments that demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed method across various datasets and forecasting horizons.
- The methodology is simple and effective, with a focus on using leading indicators to improve forecasting accuracy.
- The paper is well-motivated, with a clear focus on the use of leading indicators to enhance forecasting performance.
- The experiments are well-designed, and the results are comprehensive, showing that the proposed method outperforms existing state-of-the-art methods in most cases.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed discussion on the limitations of the proposed method, which could help in understanding its applicability and potential drawbacks.
- The improvements in forecasting performance are marginal in some cases, which raises questions about the practical significance of the proposed method.
- The paper does not sufficiently differentiate between cross-variate and cross-time dependencies, which are crucial for understanding the method's effectiveness.
- The experimental setup and results could benefit from more detailed explanations and comparisons with other state-of-the-art methods.
- There is a lack of clarity in the description of the method, particularly in how the leading indicators are selected and how they are used to refine predictions.
- The paper could benefit from more detailed discussions on the choice of datasets and the selection of leading indicators, as well as the impact of these choices on the results.

**Questions:**
- How does the proposed method perform when the number of leading indicators is increased?
- Can the method be extended to handle different types of leading indicators, such as those that are not stationary?
- How does the method handle cases where the leading indicators are not clearly defined or are dynamic?
- Is there a way to validate the effectiveness of the lead-aware refiner without using the refined predictions as targets?
- Could the method be adapted to handle different types of time series data, such as those with different frequencies or varying levels of noise?
- How does the method compare with other state-of-the-art methods in terms of computational efficiency and scalability?
