 **Summary:**
The paper introduces an adaptive algorithm for bilevel optimization problems, specifically addressing the challenge of not requiring the knowledge of Lipschitz constants. This algorithm aims to converge at a rate of 1/T, which is comparable to existing methods. The paper also explores the use of adaptive step-size policies for both the inner and outer loops, which are claimed to be the first of their kind in bilevel optimization. Theoretical contributions include a convergence analysis that is adaptive to the smoothness of the functions and does not rely on knowledge of Lipschitz constants. The paper is supported by numerical experiments, although the practical relevance and the novelty of the approach are questioned.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, easy to follow, and the algorithm is simple and intuitive.
- The adaptive algorithm is the first of its kind in bilevel optimization, which is a significant contribution.
- The convergence analysis is adaptive to the smoothness of the functions, which is a notable feature.
- The paper is well-organized, with clear explanations of the theoretical results and the experimental setup.
- The numerical experiments demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed algorithm, showing better performance compared to existing methods.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed discussion on the practical relevance and applications of bilevel optimization problems, which could make the contributions more compelling.
- The novelty of the approach is questioned, as the adaptive step-size policy for the inner loop is similar to existing methods, and the adaptive step-size policy for the outer loop is not novel.
- The convergence analysis is limited to the adaptive algorithm, and it does not compare the convergence rate of the adaptive algorithm with other existing algorithms.
- The paper does not provide sufficient theoretical guarantees on the convergence rate of the adaptive algorithm, which is crucial for practical applications.
- The paper does not adequately address the computational complexity of the proposed method, which is a significant concern for practical implementation.
- The assumptions made in the paper, such as the boundedness of the domain and the strong convexity of the inner problem, are not well-justified or explained.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors provide a more detailed discussion on the practical relevance and applications of bilevel optimization problems?
- How does the proposed adaptive algorithm compare to other existing algorithms in terms of convergence rate and practical performance?
- Can the authors provide more theoretical guarantees on the convergence rate of the adaptive algorithm, especially in non-convex settings?
- How does the proposed adaptive algorithm handle the computational complexity, especially in large-scale problems?
- Can the authors clarify the assumptions made in the paper, particularly the boundedness of the domain and the strong convexity of the inner problem?
- How does the proposed adaptive algorithm perform in scenarios where the inner problem is not strongly convex or the domain is not bounded?
