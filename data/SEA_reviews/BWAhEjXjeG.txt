 **Summary:**
The paper addresses the limitations of Randomized Smoothed Conformal Prediction (RSCP) by proposing a new method, RSCP+, which aims to provide provable robustness guarantees and improve efficiency. RSCP+ modifies the non-conformity score to incorporate Monte Carlo estimators, theoretically linking it to the robustness guarantee. Additionally, two methods, Post-Training Transformation (PTT) and Robust Conformal Training (RCT), are introduced to reduce the size of prediction sets. The paper also discusses the challenges in ensuring robustness guarantees and efficiency, with empirical results showing significant improvements over the baseline.

**Strengths:**
- The paper addresses a significant problem in the field of conformal prediction, specifically the robustness certification of RSCP, and proposes a novel approach to address this issue.
- The proposed method, RSCP+, is simple to implement and provides a provable robustness guarantee, which is a notable improvement over the previous method.
- The paper is well-written, clear, and easy to follow, with a logical structure that makes it accessible to readers.
- The experiments conducted demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed methods, showing significant improvements in prediction set size reduction and robustness.
- The paper provides a theoretical analysis of the proposed method, which is crucial for understanding the theoretical underpinnings of the approach.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed discussion on the efficiency of RSCP+ and the potential computational costs associated with the Monte Carlo estimator used in the method.
- The paper does not provide a clear discussion on the limitations of the proposed method, particularly in scenarios where the method might not be effective or where it may introduce new issues.
- There is a lack of comparative analysis with other robust conformal prediction methods, which could provide a better understanding of the relative advantages and disadvantages of the proposed approach.
- The paper could benefit from more comprehensive experimental results, including results for different levels of perturbation and different datasets to better demonstrate the generalizability of the findings.
- The paper does not sufficiently address the computational costs and practical implications of the proposed methods, particularly in terms of training and inference times.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors provide more detailed discussions on the computational costs associated with the Monte Carlo estimator used in RSCP+?
- How does the proposed method perform in scenarios where the perturbation magnitude is different from what was used in the experiments?
- Could the authors provide a more detailed discussion on the limitations of the proposed method and when it might not be effective?
- How does the proposed method compare to other robust conformal prediction methods, particularly in terms of efficiency and robustness?
- Could the authors provide additional experimental results, such as those for different levels of perturbation and different datasets, to better demonstrate the generalizability of the findings?
