 **Summary:**
The paper introduces LeRaC, a novel curriculum learning method that adjusts the learning rates of different layers in a neural network to simulate a curriculum. This approach is based on the hypothesis that the level of noise increases as layers get deeper, and it is designed to optimize weights of low-level feature layers first to reduce noise. The method was tested on various datasets and neural architectures, showing improved performance over conventional training and Curriculum by Smoothing. However, the paper lacks a comprehensive comparison with other curriculum learning methods and does not provide sufficient empirical evidence to support its claims.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, with clear and concise language, and the presentation is clear and easy to follow.
- The proposed method is novel, simple, and effective, with a strong theoretical foundation and empirical evidence that supports its effectiveness.
- The experiments are well-designed, covering a wide range of datasets and neural architectures, and the results demonstrate the method's superior performance compared to conventional training and Curriculum by Smoothing.
- The paper provides a comprehensive discussion on related work, highlighting the differences and advantages of the proposed method over existing approaches.
- The paper includes a detailed analysis of the experimental results, which helps in understanding the impact of different hyperparameters on the performance of the method.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a comprehensive comparison with other curriculum learning methods, which limits the understanding of the method's relative performance and advantages.
- The empirical evidence provided does not convincingly support the hypothesis that the level of noise increases as layers get deeper, which is a critical assumption underlying the proposed method.
- The paper does not discuss the limitations of the proposed method, which could help in understanding its applicability and potential drawbacks.
- The method's applicability to different types of neural networks and datasets is not thoroughly explored, which could limit the understanding of its generalizability.
- The paper does not provide sufficient details on how the learning rate schedule is set, which could affect the reproducibility and applicability of the method.
- The paper could benefit from a more detailed discussion on the impact of different hyperparameters on the performance of the method, and how these parameters were selected.

**Questions:**
- How does the proposed method compare to other curriculum learning methods, particularly in terms of performance and computational efficiency?
- Can the authors provide more empirical evidence to support the hypothesis that the level of noise increases as layers get deeper?
- How does the method perform when applied to different types of neural networks and datasets, and what are the observed performance improvements?
- How sensitive is the method to the choice of hyperparameters, and what are the recommended settings for different types of neural networks and datasets?
- Can the authors discuss the potential limitations of the proposed method and how these might affect its applicability and performance?
- How does the method handle the initialization of learning rates, and what impact does this have on the performance of the method?
- Can the authors provide more details on the experimental setup, particularly how the learning rate schedule was set and how this affects the performance of the method?
