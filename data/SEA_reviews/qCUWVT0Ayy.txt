 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel approach to layout generation by treating it as a code generation task, utilizing Large Language Models (LLMs) to enhance semantic information and harness their expertise. The proposed method, termed LayoutNUWA, consists of three modules: Code Initialization, Code Completion, and Code Rendering. The approach is evaluated on multiple datasets and shows significant improvements over existing methods, with the potential to revolutionize the field of layout generation. The paper's novelty lies in its innovative use of LLMs to generate layouts, which is a departure from traditional numerical optimization methods. However, concerns are raised about the clarity of the methodology, the lack of detailed experimental results, and the potential limitations of the approach in handling complex layouts and varying element sizes.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel approach to layout generation by treating it as a code generation task, which is a significant departure from traditional numerical optimization methods.
- The proposed method is innovative and shows promising results, demonstrating a new way to harness the hidden expertise of Large Language Models (LLMs) in the layout generation process.
- The paper is well-written, making it easy to follow, and the authors have provided a clear and detailed explanation of the proposed method.
- The experimental results show significant improvements over existing methods, indicating the effectiveness of the proposed approach.
- The paper addresses an important and challenging problem in the field of layout generation, which is crucial for enhancing user engagement and information perception in various applications.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed explanation of the methodology, particularly the Code Initialization and Code Completion modules, which are crucial for understanding the proposed approach.
- There is a significant lack of detail in the experimental section, including the absence of specifics on the datasets used, the training process, and the evaluation metrics.
- The paper does not adequately discuss the limitations of the proposed method, which is crucial for understanding its applicability and potential drawbacks.
- The paper's claims of superiority over existing methods are not convincingly supported by the experimental results, which are either insufficient or not convincingly superior.
- The paper does not adequately address the issue of handling complex layouts with varying element sizes, which is a significant limitation of the proposed approach.
- The paper does not sufficiently discuss the potential negative societal impacts of the proposed method, which is a critical aspect of any technological innovation.

**Questions:**
- Could you provide a more detailed explanation of the Code Initialization and Code Completion modules, including how they operate and the specifics of their implementation?
- How does the proposed method handle complex layouts with varying element sizes? What are the potential limitations or challenges in such scenarios?
- Can you clarify the role and impact of the LLMs in the proposed method, particularly how they enhance the semantic information of layouts?
- How does the method perform in scenarios where the layouts are not structured as regular squares? What are the potential limitations or challenges in such scenarios?
- Could you provide more details on the experimental setup, including the datasets used, the training process, and the evaluation metrics? How were these chosen, and what were the considerations behind their selection?
- How does the proposed method compare to other recent approaches in the field of layout generation, such as those mentioned in the related work section?
- Could you discuss the potential negative societal impacts of the proposed method and how these might be mitigated or addressed?
