 **Summary:**
The paper introduces ADoPD, a large-scale document page decomposition dataset, which utilizes a novel data-driven document taxonomy discovery method for data collection. The dataset covers four document image understanding tasks: Doc2Mask, Doc2Box, Doc2Tag, and Doc2Seq, with annotations including human-labeled entity masks, text bounding boxes, and automatically generated tags and captions. The dataset is designed to support future research on document image understanding, leveraging both large-scale pretrained models and human-in-the-loop processes to ensure diversity and balance in data collection. The paper provides a detailed description of the dataset's construction and its utility in document image understanding tasks, although it lacks a clear explanation of the taxonomy used and the methodology behind data selection.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a large-scale document page decomposition dataset, ADoPD, which covers four document image understanding tasks and is expected to support future research on document image understanding.
- The authors propose a data-driven approach for constructing document taxonomies during data collection, which is an innovative method for dataset creation.
- The dataset is diverse and includes a variety of document types, which is beneficial for research in document image understanding.
- The paper is well-written, making it easy to follow, and the experiments are comprehensive, providing a detailed analysis of the dataset.
- The dataset is annotated with labels for document images, covering four document image understanding tasks, which is a significant contribution to the field.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a clear explanation of the taxonomy used in the dataset and the methodology behind data selection, which is crucial for understanding the dataset's utility and effectiveness.
- The dataset's focus on document image understanding tasks does not clearly distinguish it from other datasets, such as those focusing on document classification or other types of document analysis.
- The paper does not adequately address the potential limitations or challenges in using the dataset, which could affect its applicability and impact.
- The dataset's size and diversity are not convincingly justified, and the paper does not sufficiently demonstrate the dataset's utility in advancing research in document image understanding.
- The paper's experimental results and methodologies are not convincingly superior to existing methods, and the dataset's generalization ability to other tasks is not adequately demonstrated.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors provide more details on the taxonomy used in the dataset and the methodology behind data selection?
- How does the dataset's focus on document image understanding tasks differentiate it from other datasets in the field?
- What are the potential limitations or challenges in using the dataset, and how do these affect its applicability and impact?
- How does the dataset's size and diversity contribute to its utility in advancing research in document image understanding?
- Could the authors provide more detailed examples or case studies demonstrating the dataset's utility in advancing research in document image understanding?
- How does the dataset's generalization ability to other tasks compare to existing methods, and what are the implications of this for the dataset's utility in broader research contexts?
