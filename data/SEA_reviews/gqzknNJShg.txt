 **Summary:**
The paper introduces FourierAugment, a novel data augmentation technique that enhances the learning of lightweight models by utilizing Fourier transforms to augment images with different frequency components. This method is designed to address the challenges of learning high-frequency components in resource-constrained environments. The authors demonstrate that their approach improves the performance of various models, including ResNet18, on datasets such as miniImageNet and ImageNet, and shows potential in few-shot learning scenarios. The method's effectiveness is validated through empirical studies and visualizations, highlighting its ability to improve model performance by focusing on different frequency bands.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel and innovative approach to data augmentation by leveraging frequency analysis, specifically focusing on high-frequency components which are often overlooked in resource-constrained environments.
- The methodology is clearly explained, and the paper is well-written, making it accessible and easy to understand.
- The empirical results are robust, showing that the proposed method significantly outperforms existing data augmentation techniques in resource-constrained settings.
- The use of visualizations such as occlusion sensitivity maps and feature similarity analysis provides valuable insights into the model's attention mechanisms and learning patterns.
- The paper is well-motivated, addressing the problem of resource-constrained environments in computer vision tasks, which is an important and relevant issue.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a thorough comparison with existing frequency-based data augmentation methods, which could have provided a better context for the proposed method's contributions.
- The evaluation of the method is limited to specific datasets and models, which might not fully demonstrate its generalizability or effectiveness across different scenarios.
- The paper does not provide a detailed discussion on the computational overhead or the impact of FourierAugment on training times, which could be critical for practical deployment in resource-constrained environments.
- The paper does not sufficiently discuss the limitations of the proposed method, such as its applicability to larger models or different types of datasets.
- There is a lack of detailed implementation details, such as the specifics of the filter bank used in FourierAugment, which could affect the reproducibility and comparability of the results.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors provide more details on the computational overhead of FourierAugment, including the additional time required for training and inference?
- How does the proposed method perform on larger models, such as ResNet50 or EfficientNet, and what are the expected outcomes?
- Could the authors discuss the potential impact of FourierAugment on the training stability, particularly in resource-constrained environments where computational resources are limited?
- How does the proposed method compare to other frequency-based data augmentation methods, and what are the key differences in terms of performance and computational efficiency?
- Could the authors provide more detailed implementation details, such as the specifics of the filter bank used in FourierAugment, to enhance the reproducibility and comparability of the results?
