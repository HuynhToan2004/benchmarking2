 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel method for debugging code generated by large language models (LLMs) through a self-debugging process that involves generating feedback messages and using them to refine the LLM's predictions. The method, inspired by human debugging practices like rubber duck debugging, is evaluated across various code generation tasks including SQL-to-text, C++-to-Python translation, and Python code generation. The results show that this approach improves the performance of LLMs, particularly in scenarios where the initial predictions are incorrect. The paper also discusses the potential of using this method to enhance the reliability and accuracy of LLM-generated code.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel approach to debugging code generated by LLMs, which is a significant advancement in the field of code generation.
- The methodology is well-explained, making it easy to understand and replicate, and the experiments are comprehensive, covering various code generation tasks.
- The paper is well-written, with clear explanations and visualizations that aid in understanding the complex processes involved in debugging LLM-generated code.
- The results demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed method, showing that it can improve the performance of LLMs in various scenarios.
- The paper provides a detailed analysis of the different types of errors fixed by the debugging process, which is crucial for understanding the impact of the method.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a thorough comparison with existing methods for debugging LLM-generated code, which could provide a better understanding of the novelty and effectiveness of the proposed method.
- The evaluation of the method is limited to specific benchmarks, which may not fully demonstrate its applicability across different domains or scenarios.
- The paper does not discuss the limitations of the proposed method, which could provide a more balanced view of its strengths and weaknesses.
- There is a lack of discussion on the potential negative societal impacts of the method, which is an important consideration for any new technology.
- The paper could benefit from a more detailed discussion on the practical implementation of the debugging process, including how it might be adapted for different types of LLMs or code generation tasks.

**Questions:**
- Could you provide more details on the comparisons made with existing methods for debugging LLM-generated code? How does your method differ and what are the advantages?
- How does the proposed method perform in scenarios where the LLM initially generates correct code, but the debugging process still identifies errors?
- Are there specific scenarios or types of code generation tasks where the debugging process is particularly effective or ineffective?
- How does the method handle cases where the LLM generates code that is not executable or where the execution environment is not fully supported?
- Could you discuss the potential negative societal impacts of the method, particularly in terms of the reliability and security of the code generated by LLMs?
- How does the method compare to other methods that use feedback from humans or other sources to improve LLM-generated code?
