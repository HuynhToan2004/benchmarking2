 **Summary:**
The paper introduces HyperDT, a novel decision tree algorithm adapted for hyperbolic spaces, which extends the traditional Euclidean decision tree algorithm by utilizing homogeneous hyperplanes to partition hyperbolic space. This method maintains constant-time decision complexity and avoids computationally intensive Riemannian optimization. The authors also propose HyperRF, an extension of random forests tailored for hyperbolic space, which enhances accuracy and reduces susceptibility to overfitting. Extensive benchmarking across various datasets demonstrates the superior performance of these models, providing a swift, precise, accurate, and user-friendly toolkit for hyperbolic data analysis. The paper also discusses the computational efficiency and scalability of the proposed methods, which are crucial for handling high-dimensional data in hyperbolic spaces.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, clear, and easy to follow, with detailed explanations of the proposed algorithms and their advantages.
- The introduction of HyperDT, a novel decision tree algorithm tailored for hyperbolic spaces, represents a significant contribution to the field of hyperbolic machine learning.
- The paper provides a Python implementation of HyperDT and HyperRF, which will be beneficial for the community.
- The proposed methods, HyperDT and HyperRF, demonstrate superior performance compared to existing counterparts on various datasets, showcasing their effectiveness in hyperbolic data analysis.
- The paper addresses the scalability issues inherent in high-dimensional Euclidean spaces by maintaining constant-time decision complexity.
- The methodology is well-explained, making it accessible to readers not familiar with the specifics of hyperbolic decision trees.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper does not adequately discuss the limitations of the proposed methods, which could help in understanding the scope and applicability of the research.
- The benchmarking results, while comprehensive, do not convincingly demonstrate the superiority of the proposed methods over existing ones, particularly in terms of accuracy.
- The paper lacks a detailed comparison with other hyperbolic classifiers, which could provide a more robust evaluation of the proposed methods.
- The novelty of the approach is questioned, as similar methods have been previously proposed, and the paper does not sufficiently differentiate its contributions from existing work.
- The paper does not sufficiently address the computational challenges associated with hyperbolic classifiers, which could limit its applicability in practical scenarios.
- The paper could benefit from a more detailed discussion on the computational complexity and efficiency of the proposed methods, especially in high-dimensional hyperbolic spaces.
