 **Summary:**
The paper introduces the Context-NER task, which involves generating contextual phrases for entities in a sentence, and presents the EDGAR10-Q dataset, a large-scale dataset derived from financial reports. The dataset is designed to aid in the development of sophisticated language models for financial text analysis. The paper also proposes a baseline method and evaluates its performance using various models, including T5 and BART, achieving a ROUGE-L score of 49%. The dataset and the proposed task aim to advance research in the generation of entities' context, which is crucial for understanding financial reports. However, the paper's experimental results are limited, and the evaluation metrics used are criticized for not being sufficient to assess the quality of the generated phrases.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel task called Context-NER, which involves generating contextual phrases for entities in a sentence, and proposes a baseline method to address this task.
- The EDGAR10-Q dataset is introduced, which is a large-scale dataset derived from financial reports, providing a valuable resource for research in the financial domain.
- The paper is well-written, clear, and easy to follow, making it accessible to a broad audience.
- The dataset and the proposed task have the potential to advance research in the generation of entities' context, which is crucial for understanding financial reports.
- The paper provides a baseline method for the Context-NER task, which could be a starting point for further research in this area.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper's evaluation is limited, with only a few models tested, and the results are not sufficiently robust to support the claims made about the dataset's utility.
- The evaluation metrics used, such as ROUGE-L, are not sufficient to assess the quality of the generated phrases, as they do not consider the semantic similarity between the generated phrases and the gold labels.
- The paper lacks a detailed comparison with other methods for extracting entities and their context, which could have provided a more comprehensive evaluation of the proposed approach.
- The paper does not provide a detailed explanation of the baseline method's design, which could limit the understanding of its effectiveness and applicability.
- The paper's focus on a specific domain (finance) may limit its generalizability to other domains, and it does not address how the method could be adapted to other types of text.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors provide more details on the baseline method's design, particularly how it generates questions and phrases from sentences?
- How does the proposed method compare to other methods for extracting entities and their context, especially in terms of performance and generalizability?
- Could the authors clarify the relationship between the context-NER task and the OpenIE task, and how the proposed method differs from existing approaches?
- How do the authors plan to address the limitations in the evaluation metrics, particularly the lack of consideration for semantic similarity between the generated phrases and the gold labels?
- Could the authors provide more detailed results on the performance of the baseline method across different models and datasets, especially in comparison to other methods?
