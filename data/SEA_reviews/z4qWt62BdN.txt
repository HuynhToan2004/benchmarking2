 **Summary:**
The paper introduces DSparsE, a knowledge graph completion model that utilizes sparse MLP layers and residual connections to address issues like overfitting and network depth limitations in current methods. The model employs a dynamic MLP layer and a relation-aware MLP layer, with residual connections between the encoding and decoding ends. The authors claim that DSparsE outperforms existing methods on standard benchmarks, demonstrating its effectiveness in feature interaction and interpretability. However, the novelty of the approach is questioned, as it heavily relies on existing techniques such as sparse MLP layers and residual connections. The paper also lacks a thorough discussion on the limitations of these techniques and does not sufficiently compare DSparsE with state-of-the-art models like KG-BERT.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, easy to follow, and provides a comprehensive evaluation of the proposed method, DSparsE, which outperforms previous state-of-the-art methods on standard benchmarks.
- The authors have conducted a comprehensive set of ablation studies to analyze the impact of each component of the proposed model, which is a valuable contribution to the field.
- The use of sparse MLP layers and residual connections in the model is highlighted as a significant advancement over previous methods, enhancing the model's performance and interpretability.
- The paper effectively addresses the limitations of existing methods like ComDensE and InteractE, offering a novel approach to knowledge graph completion tasks.
- The proposed model is simple yet effective, and the results are strong, demonstrating the effectiveness of the proposed method.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed discussion on the limitations of the proposed approach, particularly concerning the use of sparse MLP layers and residual connections, which have been previously explored in other models.
- The novelty of the proposed approach is questioned, as it heavily relies on existing techniques such as sparse MLP layers and residual connections, which have been extensively used in other models.
- The paper does not sufficiently compare DSparsE with state-of-the-art models like KG-BERT, which could provide a clearer understanding of the model's relative performance.
- The paper does not provide a detailed discussion on the limitations of the proposed approach, which is crucial for understanding the scope and applicability of the model.
- The paper does not discuss the limitations of the proposed approach in detail, which could affect the understanding of the model's practical applicability and potential future improvements.

**Questions:**
- How does the proposed method compare with other state-of-the-art models like KG-BERT, particularly in terms of performance and computational efficiency?
- Can the authors provide more details on the limitations of the proposed approach and how these might impact the model's performance in real-world scenarios?
- How does the model handle the scalability issue, especially when dealing with larger knowledge graphs?
- In the experimental results, what are the main factors contributing to the superior performance of DSparsE compared to other models?
- Could the authors provide more details on the limitations of the proposed approach and discuss potential future research directions to address these limitations?
