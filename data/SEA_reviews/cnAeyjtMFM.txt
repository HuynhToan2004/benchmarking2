 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel approach, Witness Graph Topological Layer (WGTL), to enhance the robustness of Graph Neural Networks (GNNs) against adversarial attacks. WGTL leverages witness complexes to focus on essential nodes, integrating both local and global topological graph features, and employs a robust regularized topological loss. This methodology, supported by theoretical stability guarantees, aims to improve GNN performance on clean graphs and defend against adversarial attacks. The paper is well-structured, with clear explanations and theoretical backing, although it could benefit from more detailed empirical evaluations and a broader range of datasets to substantiate its claims fully.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, easy to follow, and clearly explains the proposed methods, making it accessible to readers.
- The integration of witness complexes with GNNs is a novel approach, which is theoretically supported by stability guarantees.
- The paper provides a theoretical analysis of the proposed method, which is backed by experimental results showing improvements over existing methods.
- The use of topology-aware features for adversarial defense is innovative and has been shown to be effective in improving robustness against adversarial attacks.
- The methodology is supported by extensive experiments and ablation studies, demonstrating the effectiveness of the proposed method.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed discussion on the limitations of the proposed method, particularly in terms of its scalability and applicability to different types of graphs.
- There is a need for more detailed explanations of the experimental setup, such as the choice of hyperparameters and the rationale behind the selection of datasets.
- The paper does not include a comprehensive evaluation of the proposed method against a wide range of datasets, which could limit the generalizability of the results.
- The paper does not provide sufficient details on the computational complexity of the proposed method, which is crucial for understanding its practical implications.
- The paper could benefit from a more thorough comparison with existing methods, particularly those that use topology-aware features for adversarial defense.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors provide more details on the choice of hyperparameters and their impact on the performance of the proposed method?
- How does the proposed method perform when applied to different types of graphs, and what are the specific challenges encountered in each case?
- What is the computational complexity of the proposed method, and how does it compare to other methods in terms of efficiency?
- How does the proposed method perform against a broader range of adversarial attacks, and what are the specific advantages of using topology-aware features for adversarial defense?
- Could the authors provide a more detailed explanation of the experimental setup and the rationale behind the selection of datasets?
