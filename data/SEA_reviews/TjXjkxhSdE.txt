 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel sparse-dense-sparse (SDS) framework aimed at enhancing the performance of pruned pre-trained language models (PLMs). The SDS framework is designed to optimize the weight distribution of pruned models through a three-step process: initial pruning, re-dense weight reconstruction, and a second pruning round. The methodology is supported by extensive experiments, demonstrating that SDS outperforms existing techniques like SparseGPT and Wanda, showing improvements in perplexity and accuracy on various models including OPT, GPT, and LLaMA. Despite these strengths, the paper has been criticized for its lack of clarity in certain sections, insufficient theoretical justification, and limited experimental scope, which may affect its broader applicability and the depth of its contributions.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-organized and easy to follow, with clear explanations of the proposed method.
- The experimental results demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed method, showing improvements in perplexity and accuracy on various models.
- The paper introduces a novel approach by combining different techniques to optimize the weight distribution of pruned models, which is a significant contribution to the field.
- The proposed method is validated through comprehensive experiments, showing that it outperforms existing techniques in terms of perplexity and accuracy.
- The paper provides a detailed analysis of the weight distribution before and after pruning, which is a valuable insight into the behavior of pruned models.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed explanation of the re-dense weight reconstruction process, which is a critical component of the proposed method.
- There is a lack of theoretical analysis to support the design of the proposed method, which could strengthen the paper's contributions.
- The experimental scope is limited, focusing primarily on models from the OPT family, which may not generalize well to other models.
- The paper does not adequately address the computational overhead introduced by the proposed method, which could be a significant concern for practical applications.
- The paper could benefit from a more thorough discussion on the limitations of the proposed method and potential directions for future research.
- There are issues with clarity and precision in the presentation of the methodology and results, which could be improved to enhance the paper's readability and comprehensibility.

**Questions:**
- Could you provide more details on the re-dense weight reconstruction process, particularly how the weights are reconstructed and what inputs are used?
- How does the proposed method compare to other pruning techniques like Wanda, especially in terms of computational efficiency and model performance?
- What are the specific advantages of the SDS method over other pruning techniques, and how does it address the limitations of existing methods?
- Can you clarify the computational overhead introduced by the proposed method and discuss potential strategies for mitigating these costs?
- How does the proposed method perform on other models beyond those from the OPT family, and what are the implications of these results for the broader applicability of the method?
- Could you provide a more detailed analysis of the weight distribution before and after pruning, and how does this analysis inform the design of the proposed method?
