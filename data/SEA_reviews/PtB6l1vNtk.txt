 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel approach using deep learning to predict Lagrangian multipliers for Mixed Integer Linear Programs (MILPs), aiming to enhance the performance of Lagrangian relaxation methods. The method involves encoding MILP instances into a bipartite graph and passing this through a graph neural network (GNN) to extract features. These features are then used to predict Lagrangian multipliers, which can be used to improve the performance of Lagrangian relaxation methods. The method is evaluated on various datasets, showing that it can significantly reduce the gap between the continuous relaxation and the best Lagrangian bound, and provide a good warm-start for descent-based Lagrangian methods. However, the paper suffers from several issues such as lack of clarity in the presentation, insufficient empirical results, and a lack of comparison with state-of-the-art methods.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel approach using deep learning to predict Lagrangian multipliers, which is a significant contribution to the field of MILP solving.
- The methodology is well-described, and the paper is generally well-written, making it accessible to readers.
- The experiments are conducted on two different datasets, which helps in validating the proposed approach.
- The paper includes a comprehensive discussion on the related work, providing a good overview of existing methods in the field.
- The use of a graph neural network to encode MILP instances and predict Lagrangian multipliers is an innovative approach that could potentially enhance the performance of Lagrangian relaxation methods.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks clarity in its presentation, particularly in the description of the experimental setup and the results. More detailed explanations and examples are needed to enhance understanding.
- The empirical results are insufficient and do not convincingly demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed method. The paper could benefit from more comprehensive experiments on larger and more diverse datasets.
- The novelty of the approach is questionable as similar methods have been explored in previous works, and the paper does not adequately differentiate its approach from these existing methods.
- The paper suffers from a lack of comparison with state-of-the-art methods, which is crucial for evaluating the effectiveness and efficiency of the proposed method.
- The paper's reliance on a heuristic approach for predicting Lagrangian multipliers raises concerns about the method's accuracy and reliability, especially in scenarios where the continuous relaxation solution is not optimal.
- The paper does not discuss the computational complexity of the proposed method, which is crucial for understanding its practical applicability in real-world scenarios.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors provide more detailed explanations and examples to clarify the experimental setup and results?
- How does the proposed method compare with state-of-the-art methods in terms of effectiveness and efficiency?
- Can the authors discuss the potential limitations and challenges of the proposed method, and how these could be addressed in future research?
- How does the proposed method handle scenarios where the continuous relaxation solution is not optimal?
- Can the authors discuss the computational complexity of the proposed method and how it scales with the size of the MILP instances?
- How does the proposed method perform when applied to MILP instances with varying degrees of difficulty, and what are the implications for its practical applicability?
