 **Summary:**
The paper introduces SPOT, a method for large-scale pre-training on LiDAR point clouds, focusing on occupancy prediction as a pre-training task. SPOT employs beam re-sampling for point cloud augmentation and class-balancing strategies to mitigate domain gaps. The method demonstrates scalability and effectiveness across various datasets and tasks, including 3D object detection and LiDAR semantic segmentation. Extensive experiments on multiple benchmarks showcase the method's ability to improve downstream performance with increased pre-training data. However, concerns are raised about the novelty of the method, as it primarily relies on existing techniques, and the paper's writing quality, which is criticized for its clarity and organization.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel pre-training task, occupancy prediction, which is shown to be effective in improving the performance of downstream tasks.
- The methodology is straightforward, making it easy to follow, and the experiments are extensive, covering multiple datasets and tasks.
- The paper demonstrates scalability by showing that the performance of downstream tasks improves with the amount of pre-training data.
- The proposed strategies, such as beam re-sampling augmentation and class-balancing strategies, are effective in narrowing domain gaps and improving the performance of downstream tasks.
- The paper is well-written and easy to read, with clear explanations of the methodology and experimental results.

**Weaknesses:**
- The novelty of the method is limited, as it primarily relies on existing techniques such as BEV-MAE and AD-PT, with minor modifications.
- The paper lacks detailed implementation details, particularly the specifics of how the proposed strategies are implemented, such as the beam re-sampling and class-balancing strategies.
- The writing quality of the paper is subpar, with issues such as grammar errors, unclear figures, and inconsistent use of symbols and formatting.
- The paper does not adequately address the potential negative societal impacts of its work.
- The paper's claims about the method's ability to learn general representations are not convincingly supported by the experimental results.
- The paper does not compare its method with a sufficient number of baselines, particularly with other pre-training methods that use different tasks such as segmentation and detection.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors provide more detailed implementation details, particularly regarding the beam re-sampling and class-balancing strategies?
- How does the proposed method compare with other pre-training methods that use different tasks such as segmentation and detection?
- Can the authors clarify the differences between the proposed method and existing methods, such as AD-PT, in terms of their contributions to the field?
- How does the proposed method handle the issue of domain gaps between different datasets, especially when the pre-training dataset is not from the same domain as the downstream dataset?
- Can the authors provide a more detailed analysis of the experimental results, particularly in terms of how the method's performance varies with different amounts of pre-training data?
- How does the proposed method address the scalability issues, especially in terms of the computational resources required for pre-training on large datasets?
