 **Summary:**
The paper addresses the issue of community bias amplification in graph representation learning, proposing a novel method called Random Graph Coarsening Contrastive Learning (RGCCL). This method utilizes random graph coarsening as a data augmentation technique to mitigate bias amplification and improve fairness in node classification. The authors provide theoretical insights into the causes of community bias and empirical evidence demonstrating the effectiveness of their method. The proposed RGCCL model contrasts the coarsened graph with the original graph, aiming to enhance the learning of node embeddings. Extensive experiments on various datasets validate the effectiveness of RGCCL in reducing performance disparities between different classes and achieving better overall accuracy than baseline methods.

**Strengths:**
- The paper addresses an important and timely issue of community bias amplification in graph representation learning, which is a significant problem in real-world applications.
- The proposed method, RGCCL, is simple yet effective, and the paper provides a theoretical analysis and empirical evidence to support its effectiveness.
- The paper is well-written, making it easy to follow, and includes a comprehensive literature review, enhancing its readability and accessibility.
- The proposed method of random graph coarsening as a data augmentation technique is novel and can be applied to other graph learning methods, potentially enhancing their performance.
- The paper includes detailed theoretical analysis and empirical evidence, demonstrating the effectiveness of the proposed method in reducing community bias amplification.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed discussion on the limitations of the proposed method, which is crucial for understanding its applicability and potential drawbacks.
- The motivation behind the proposed method is not clearly articulated, particularly the rationale behind using contrastive learning in conjunction with random graph coarsening.
- The paper does not provide a detailed comparison of the proposed method with other existing methods for mitigating community bias, which could help in understanding its relative effectiveness.
- The paper does not include a discussion on the potential negative societal impacts of the proposed method, which is essential for ethical considerations.
- There is a lack of detailed analysis on the scalability of the proposed method, particularly in large-scale datasets, which could affect its practical applicability.
- The paper could benefit from a more detailed explanation of the theoretical analysis and empirical evidence, particularly in terms of their relevance and applicability to real-world scenarios.

**Questions:**

- How does the proposed method compare with other existing methods for mitigating community bias in graph representation learning?
- Could the authors provide more detailed experimental results, such as the impact of different coarsening rates on the performance of the proposed method?
- How does the proposed method perform in larger-scale datasets, and what are the potential challenges in scaling up the method?
- Can the authors clarify the motivation behind using contrastive learning in conjunction with random graph coarsening, and how this combination benefits the learning of node embeddings?
- How does the proposed method address the issue of community bias amplification in practical scenarios, and what are the potential negative societal impacts of using this method?
