 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel adversarial attack method for Latent Diffusion Models (LDMs) aimed at protecting copyrighted images from being used in few-shot generation. The authors propose a new objective function to improve the attack's effectiveness and introduce mechanisms to reduce GPU memory usage, making the attack more accessible to individual users. The paper also evaluates the attack's effectiveness against state-of-the-art LDMs and demonstrates its transferability across different models. The methodology includes a targeted attack on the UNet and a poisoning attack on LoRA, with a focus on reducing computational costs and improving memory efficiency.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel method for adversarial attacks on Latent Diffusion Models (LDMs), focusing on improving the effectiveness of adversarial attacks and reducing computational costs.
- The authors provide a detailed background on LDMs and adversarial attacks, which aids in understanding the context and the motivation behind the proposed method.
- The paper is well-structured and easy to follow, making it accessible to a broad audience.
- The proposed method achieves better performance than existing attacks in terms of both effectiveness and memory efficiency, which is crucial for practical applications.
- The paper is original in its approach to targeted attacks, which is a significant contribution to the field of adversarial attacks on LDMs.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a clear motivation for the proposed method and does not sufficiently justify why the new attack method is needed or how it addresses specific limitations of existing attacks.
- The novelty of the method is questionable as it appears to be an incremental improvement over existing methods, particularly in the use of the KL-divergence between the target distribution and the model's output.
- The experimental setup and results are not convincingly superior to existing methods, with some results showing marginal improvements or even degradation in performance.
- The paper suffers from a lack of clarity in certain sections, particularly in the explanation of the attack method and its components, which could confuse readers.
- The paper does not adequately address the transferability of the proposed method across different LDMs, which is a critical aspect for practical applications.
- There are several grammatical and typographical errors throughout the paper, which detract from its professionalism and readability.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors clarify the motivation behind the proposed method and how it addresses specific limitations of existing attacks?
- How does the proposed method perform when applied to different LDMs, and what are the implications of this transferability in practical applications?
- Could the authors provide more detailed comparisons with existing methods, particularly in terms of computational efficiency and memory usage?
- How does the proposed method compare to other state-of-the-art attacks in terms of effectiveness and practical applicability?
- Can the authors provide more detailed explanations of the attack method and its components, particularly the use of the KL-divergence and the impact of different target images on the attack's effectiveness?
- How does the proposed method handle different types of images, such as natural images versus images with artificial patterns, and what are the implications of these choices for the attack's effectiveness?
