 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel hierarchical model for recognizing activities of daily living (ADL) in smart home environments using a combination of pre-trained GPT and ELMO embeddings. The model's architecture includes a multi-timescale approach that integrates sensor event sequences, time-of-day information, and hierarchical activity representations. The paper evaluates the model on three datasets from the CASAS dataset and demonstrates that the proposed method outperforms existing models. However, the paper suffers from clarity issues in its writing and lacks a detailed comparison with state-of-the-art methods. It also does not provide sufficient analysis of the results, particularly in terms of the model's performance on specific activities and its generalizability across different datasets.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel hierarchical model for recognizing activities of daily living (ADL) in smart home environments, which is a significant contribution to the field.
- The model integrates pre-trained GPT and ELMO embeddings, which are innovative and enhance the model's ability to capture inter-activity nuances and temporal aspects.
- The paper is well-written, and the experiments are comprehensive, including multiple datasets and ablation studies that demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed method.
- The paper presents a clear motivation for the research and provides a good explanation of the proposed method, which is well-motivated and well-explained.
- The use of a hierarchical model for activity recognition is a significant contribution to the field, and the paper demonstrates that this approach can outperform state-of-the-art models in certain scenarios.

**Weaknesses:**

- The writing quality of the paper is poor, with numerous grammatical errors and unclear sentences, which makes it difficult to understand.
