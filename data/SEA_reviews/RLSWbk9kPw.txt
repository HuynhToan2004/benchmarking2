 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel differentiable sorting network that addresses the error accumulation issue in traditional sorting algorithms by proposing an error-free swap function and a permutation-equivariant Transformer network. This network is designed to handle diverse inputs, including multi-digit images and image fragments, by learning a mapping from high-dimensional inputs to ordinal variables. The approach is validated through experiments on various sorting benchmarks, demonstrating its effectiveness in improving sorting performance. The paper also discusses the theoretical and practical implications of the proposed methods, comparing them with existing techniques and providing a comprehensive analysis of their strengths and limitations.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, clear, and easy to follow, making complex concepts accessible to readers.
- It introduces a novel approach to sorting high-dimensional data using a differentiable swap function, which effectively addresses the error accumulation problem in traditional sorting algorithms.
- The proposed error-free DSF is a significant contribution to the field, offering a practical solution to the challenges posed by error accumulation in sorting networks.
- The paper is technically sound, with solid theoretical analysis and comprehensive experiments that demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed methods.
- The use of a permutation-equivariant Transformer network with multi-head attention enhances the model's capacity to capture dependencies between inputs, which is crucial for handling complex sorting tasks.
- The methodology is well-explained, with clear definitions and intuitive explanations that aid in understanding the complex concepts presented.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed discussion on the limitations of the proposed methods, which is crucial for a comprehensive understanding of their applicability and effectiveness.
- The experiments are limited to small-scale datasets, which may not adequately demonstrate the scalability or generalizability of the proposed methods to larger, more complex datasets.
- There is a need for more comparative analysis with existing methods, particularly in terms of computational efficiency and the handling of different types of data, such as image fragments.
- The paper could benefit from a more detailed analysis of the computational complexity and the scalability of the proposed methods, especially in relation to the size and complexity of the input data.
- The paper could be improved by including a more detailed discussion on the choice of hyperparameters and their impact on the performance of the model.
- The paper could benefit from a more comprehensive literature review, including a discussion on the limitations and potential improvements of the proposed methods compared to existing approaches.

**Questions:**
- Can the proposed methods be extended to handle sorting tasks with more complex data types, such as image fragments or other types of high-dimensional data?
- How do the hyperparameters affect the performance of the model, and what are the optimal settings for different types of data?
- Could the authors provide more details on the computational complexity and scalability of the proposed methods, particularly in relation to the size and complexity of the input data?
- How does the proposed method compare in terms of computational efficiency and effectiveness with other existing sorting algorithms, especially in scenarios where data is not as structured as in the experiments presented?
- Could the authors provide a more detailed analysis of the limitations of the proposed methods and discuss potential improvements or extensions that could address these limitations?
