 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel differential privacy (DP) mechanism for the Bag of Little Bootstraps (BLB) method, aiming to preserve privacy while conducting statistical inference on aggregated data. It proposes a method to adjust the privacy parameters based on the sensitivity of the data, ensuring that the privacy budget is not exceeded. The paper also discusses the construction of confidence intervals and hypothesis testing under DP, using a G-normal distribution. However, the paper is criticized for its poor writing, lack of clarity in presentation, and insufficient experimental validation. The theoretical contributions are seen as limited and not sufficiently novel, and the paper does not adequately address the computational complexity of the proposed methods.

**Strengths:**
- The paper addresses an important and relevant problem of privacy-preserving statistical inference, which is crucial for protecting data privacy in large-scale data analysis.
- The methodology presented is novel and innovative, particularly the use of a G-normal distribution for constructing confidence intervals and hypothesis testing under DP.
- The paper is well-structured, providing a clear overview of the methodology and results, with a good balance between theoretical and experimental sections.
- The paper is generally well-written, with clear explanations of the theoretical results and their implications.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper suffers from poor writing, with many notations and terms introduced without adequate explanation, making it difficult to follow.
- The paper lacks clarity in explaining the main contributions and the advantages of the proposed method over existing methods.
- The experimental validation is insufficient, with only one simulation study using a small dataset, which does not convincingly demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed method.
- The paper lacks a thorough discussion on the computational complexity of the proposed methods and the scalability of the approach to large datasets.
- The theoretical results and their implications are not adequately explained, particularly in terms of their practical applications and the novelty of the contributions.
- There are several typographical and formatting errors throughout the paper, which detract from its professional presentation.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors clarify the definitions and uses of the notations introduced in the paper, particularly those used in Algorithm 1 and the equations presented?
- How does the proposed method compare in terms of computational efficiency and scalability to existing methods, especially in handling large datasets?
- Can the authors provide more detailed examples or case studies to illustrate the practical applications of the theoretical results presented?
- How does the proposed method address the issue of data privacy, especially in terms of the sensitivity of the data and the privacy budget?
- Could the authors provide more robust experimental validation, including larger and more diverse datasets, to better demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed method?
- In light of the criticisms regarding the clarity and presentation of the paper, could the authors consider a significant revision to improve the readability and comprehensibility of the manuscript?
