 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel diffusion model for generating 3D molecules with controllable properties by leveraging a semantic embedding from an auxiliary encoder. This embedding, conditioned on the molecular graph, guides the diffusion process, enabling the model to generate molecules with specific properties while maintaining the original structure. The method is evaluated against various baselines and shows superior performance in terms of generating valid and diverse molecules. The paper also discusses the theoretical underpinnings of the model and provides a comprehensive set of experiments to demonstrate its effectiveness. However, the novelty of the approach is questioned, as similar methods have been previously explored, and the method's performance in complex scenarios is not thoroughly evaluated.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, with clear explanations of the proposed method and its implementation details, making it accessible to readers.
- The use of an auxiliary encoder to guide the diffusion process is innovative and effective, as demonstrated by the experimental results.
- The methodology is sound, with a thorough evaluation of the proposed method against various baselines, which shows the effectiveness of the approach.
- The paper is original in its application of diffusion models for molecular generation, which is a novel approach in the field.
- The theoretical justification provided for the proposed method is robust and helps in understanding the underlying principles of the model.

**Weaknesses:**
- The novelty of the approach is somewhat limited, as similar methods using diffusion models for molecular generation have been previously explored.
- The paper lacks a detailed comparison with state-of-the-art models in the field, which could have provided a clearer picture of the model's relative performance.
- The method's performance in more complex scenarios, such as generating molecules with large or complex structures, is not thoroughly evaluated.
- The paper does not adequately discuss the limitations of the proposed method, which could help in understanding its applicability and potential drawbacks.
- The use of deterministic noise in the model could limit the diversity of the generated molecules, which might not be as varied as those generated using stochastic processes.
- The paper does not provide sufficient details on how the semantic embedding is learned, which is a critical aspect of the model's operation.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors clarify the role of the semantic embedding in the generation process and how it influences the diversity of the generated molecules?
- How does the model perform when generating molecules with large or complex structures, and what are the implications of this for the method's applicability?
- Could the authors provide more details on the training process for the semantic embedding, particularly how it is initialized and how it evolves during training?
- What are the specific advantages of using the proposed method over other state-of-the-art models in terms of generating valid and diverse molecules?
- How does the method handle the generation of molecules with properties that are not directly related to the molecular structure, such as solubility or permeability?
- Could the authors discuss the potential applications of the method in drug discovery and design, particularly in generating molecules with specific properties for therapeutic applications?
