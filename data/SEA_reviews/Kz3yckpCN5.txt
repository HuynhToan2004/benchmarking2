 **Summary:**
The paper explores the effectiveness of fine-tuning open-source language models (LLMs) using imitation datasets derived from proprietary models like ChatGPT. It examines various aspects such as the impact of data size, model scale, and the quality of imitation datasets. The study reveals that while imitation datasets can enhance the style and persona of open-source LLMs, they do not significantly improve their factuality, coding, or problem-solving capabilities. The authors also highlight the limitations of human evaluations and suggest that the effectiveness of imitation datasets is questionable. The paper argues that the capabilities gap between open-source and proprietary models is substantial and cannot be bridged by imitation alone. Instead, the focus should be on improving the base models, which is a more promising approach for advancing open-source LLMs.

**Strengths:**
- The paper addresses an important and timely topic, which is the replication of proprietary models using open-source models.
- The study is well-structured and provides a clear narrative, making it easy to follow.
- The paper includes a comprehensive analysis of the effectiveness of imitation datasets in improving open-source LLMs, including both quantitative and qualitative evaluations.
- The paper is well-written, with clear figures and tables that aid in understanding the results.
- The authors have conducted a thorough evaluation using a variety of models and datasets, which strengthens the credibility of their findings.
- The paper provides valuable insights into the limitations of imitation datasets and the challenges in evaluating LLMs, which are crucial for the community.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a clear and compelling message, which makes it difficult to draw definitive conclusions from the results.
- The paper's conclusions are not surprising, as similar findings have been reported in previous studies.
- The paper does not sufficiently address the limitations of the work, particularly the potential for overfitting and the generalizability of the findings to other types of LLMs.
- There is a lack of detail in the methodology, such as the specifics of the data collection and preprocessing, which could affect the reproducibility and reliability of the results.
- The paper does not adequately address the ethical implications of using imitation datasets, which could potentially infringe on intellectual property rights.
- The paper does not provide sufficient justification for the choice of datasets and models used in the study, which could limit the generalizability of the findings.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors clarify the discrepancy between the results in Figure 1 and Figure 3 regarding the impact of imitation data on model performance?
- How do the authors ensure the quality and diversity of the imitation datasets used in the study?
- What steps were taken to mitigate the risk of overfitting during the fine-tuning process?
- Could the authors provide more details on the data collection and preprocessing methods used in the study?
- How do the authors address the ethical concerns related to the use of imitation datasets, particularly in terms of intellectual property rights?
- Could the authors provide more examples or evidence to support the claim that imitation datasets primarily enhance the style and persona of LLMs without improving their factuality or problem-solving capabilities?
