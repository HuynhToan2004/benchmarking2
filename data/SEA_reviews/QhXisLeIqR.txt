 **Summary:**
The paper introduces WinNet, a novel CNN-based method for time series forecasting, focusing on extracting and leveraging both long-term and short-term periodicity. It utilizes a combination of Inter-Intra Period Encoder (I2PE), Two-Dimensional Period Decomposition (TDPD), and Decomposition Correlation Block (DCB) to enhance the model's ability to capture periodic patterns. The model's architecture includes a Series Decoder that integrates features from different periods, aiming to improve prediction accuracy and computational efficiency. Extensive experiments on nine benchmark datasets demonstrate the model's effectiveness, although concerns are raised about its novelty and the depth of the experimental validation.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel CNN-based method, WinNet, for time series forecasting, which effectively captures both long-term and short-term periodicity, enhancing its ability to model complex time series patterns.
- The methodology is well-explained, with clear descriptions of the model architecture and the roles of each component, making it accessible and understandable.
- The paper presents comprehensive experimental results across nine benchmark datasets, demonstrating the model's effectiveness in multivariate and univariate forecasting tasks, and showcases its computational efficiency.
- The paper includes an ablation study that validates the effectiveness of the proposed modules, providing insights into the model's design choices.
- The manuscript is well-written, with clear explanations of the methodology, experiments, and results, which facilitates understanding and reproducibility of the research.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed discussion on the limitations and potential drawbacks of the proposed method, which could provide a more comprehensive view of its applicability and effectiveness.
- There is a need for a more thorough comparison with existing methods, particularly in terms of computational efficiency and model performance, to establish the superiority of the proposed method.
- The paper could benefit from a more detailed explanation of the proposed modules, particularly the Inter-Intra Period Encoder and Decomposition Correlation Block, to enhance understanding of their roles and contributions.
- The experimental setup could be improved by including more diverse datasets and varying input/output lengths to better assess the model's generalizability and adaptability.
- The paper could benefit from a more rigorous theoretical analysis to support the claims made about the model's ability to capture periodic patterns and its computational efficiency.

**Questions:**
- How does the proposed method compare to other existing methods in terms of computational efficiency and model performance, particularly in scenarios where computational resources are limited?
- Can the authors provide more details on the computational efficiency of the proposed method, including the training and inference times, and how these compare to other methods?
- What are the specific advantages of the proposed method over existing methods in terms of handling different types of time series data, such as those with varying degrees of periodicity or noise?
- Could the authors provide more insights into the design choices of the model, particularly the decision to use a CNN-based approach and the rationale behind the choice of each module?
- How does the model perform on datasets with irregular or non-periodic patterns, and what adjustments, if any, are necessary to adapt the model to such datasets?
