 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel semi-smooth, self-shifting, and singular Newton method (S5N) for solving sparse optimal transport (OT) problems. The S5N method is designed to handle non-smooth gradients and singular generalized Hessian matrices, which are common in sparse OT problems. The method is globally convergent and has a fast local quadratic convergence rate, as demonstrated through extensive numerical experiments on a large collection of test problems. The S5N algorithm is shown to outperform existing methods in terms of computational efficiency and robustness, particularly in scenarios where the transport plans are sparse. The paper also provides a theoretical analysis of the convergence properties of the S5N algorithm, which is crucial for its application in various optimization problems beyond sparse OT.
