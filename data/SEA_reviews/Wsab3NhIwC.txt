 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a non-contrastive self-supervised learning (SSL) approach for speech representation learning, focusing on reducing training time and resource requirements compared to existing methods like Wav2Vec2 and Data2Vec2. The proposed method leverages a non-contrastive Barlow-Twins methodology, which is adapted from image-based SSL methods. It employs a dual pair of Siamese networks, with augmentations performed in the latent space, and introduces new loss functions for time-merging and time-unrolling. The method is evaluated on the LibriSpeech dataset and shows competitive results, particularly in terms of training speed and resource efficiency. However, the novelty of the approach is questioned due to its similarity to existing SSL methods, and the paper lacks a thorough comparison with other state-of-the-art models.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, making it easy to follow, and the motivation behind the research is clearly articulated.
- The methodology of the paper is well-explained, and the authors have provided a clear description of the training process, including the use of Siamese networks and the computation of cross-correlation matrices.
- The paper includes a comprehensive evaluation of the proposed method, comparing it to existing SSL methods and showing improvements in training speed and resource efficiency.
- The application of non-contrastive learning to speech embeddings is innovative and has the potential to reduce training time and resource requirements.
- The paper is well-organized, with clear descriptions of the methodology and results, and includes a detailed evaluation of the proposed method across different scenarios.

**Weaknesses:**
- The novelty of the proposed method is limited as it primarily extends existing SSL methods from image-based to audio-based, without significant modifications.
- The paper lacks a thorough comparison with other state-of-the-art models, such as Hubert and Data2Vec2, which could have provided a more robust evaluation of the proposed method.
- The results presented in the paper are not significantly better than existing methods, with only marginal improvements reported.
- The paper does not adequately address the limitations of the proposed method, which could include the lack of a robustness analysis and the potential for overfitting due to the use of a small dataset like LibriSpeech.
- The paper could benefit from a more detailed discussion on the computational complexity and the potential for overfitting with the use of a small dataset.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors provide a more detailed comparison with other state-of-the-art models, particularly in terms of computational efficiency and resource requirements?
- How does the proposed method perform when applied to larger datasets, such as those used in other SSL methods?
- Can the authors elaborate on the potential for overfitting with the use of a small dataset like LibriSpeech?
- How does the proposed method compare to other SSL methods in terms of computational complexity and resource requirements?
- Could the authors provide a more detailed discussion on the potential limitations of the proposed method and how these might affect its practical application?
