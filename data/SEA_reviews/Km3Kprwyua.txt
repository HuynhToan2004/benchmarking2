 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel method called online speculative decoding, aimed at reducing the latency of large language models (LLMs) by continually updating smaller draft models based on user query data. This approach leverages spare computational power in LLM serving clusters to retrain draft models, thereby improving their predictive accuracy and adapting to the query distribution in real-time. The paper demonstrates that this method can significantly enhance token acceptance rates and reduce latency, showing substantial improvements over existing speculative decoding methods. The methodology is supported by empirical results across various datasets, highlighting its effectiveness and potential in real-world applications.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel method, online speculative decoding, which leverages spare computational power in LLM serving clusters to continually update draft models, thereby reducing latency and improving predictive accuracy.
- The method is supported by empirical results that demonstrate significant improvements in token acceptance rates and latency reduction, showing substantial improvements over existing speculative decoding methods.
- The paper is well-written, clearly presenting the methodology and empirical results, making it accessible and understandable to readers.
- The proposed method is technically sound and innovative, addressing a significant challenge in the field of LLM serving by reducing latency and improving the efficiency of model inference.
- The paper provides a comprehensive evaluation of the method across various datasets, demonstrating its effectiveness and applicability in real-world scenarios.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper could benefit from a more detailed comparison with existing methods, particularly in terms of computational efficiency and the practical implications of retraining draft models.
- The evaluation of the method could be expanded to include a broader range of datasets and real-world scenarios to better understand its generalizability and effectiveness in varied conditions.
- The paper lacks a detailed discussion on the limitations of the method, particularly concerning its scalability and applicability in different scenarios.
- The paper could benefit from a more thorough analysis of the computational costs and resource requirements associated with the online speculative decoding approach.
- The method's effectiveness in handling distribution shifts and its impact on the quality of the draft model's predictions need further exploration.

**Questions:**
- How does the method perform in scenarios with large distribution shifts, and what are the implications for the draft model's predictions?
- Can the authors provide a more detailed comparison of the computational efficiency and resource requirements of their method compared to existing speculative decoding methods?
- What are the practical implications of retraining draft models in real-world LLM serving systems, and how does this impact the overall performance and efficiency of the system?
- Could the authors elaborate on the scalability of the online speculative decoding approach, particularly in terms of its applicability to different LLM architectures and serving systems?
- How does the method handle the trade-off between the computational cost of retraining draft models and the benefits in terms of latency reduction and predictive accuracy?
