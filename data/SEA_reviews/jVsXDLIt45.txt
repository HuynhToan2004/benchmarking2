 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel approach for scaling decoder-only language models (LMs) by compressing contextual information into "nuggets," a subset of tokens selected by a scorer to capture the most contextually rich information. This method is inspired by the Nugget approach for encoder-decoder models, adapted for decoder-only models to reduce computational overhead. The method is evaluated across various tasks including auto-encoding, language modeling, and question answering, showing promising results in terms of efficiency and performance. However, the paper lacks a thorough comparison with other context compression methods and does not adequately discuss the limitations and societal impacts of its approach.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, clear, and easy to follow, making it accessible to a broad audience.
- The method of compressing contextual information into "nuggets" is novel and innovative, particularly the use of a scorer to select the most contextually rich tokens.
- The approach demonstrates significant efficiency gains over previous methods, as shown in the results section.
- The method can be applied to a variety of tasks including language modeling, question answering, and summarization, showing potential for broad applicability.
- The paper includes a detailed discussion on the training process and the use of scorers, which are critical for the method's effectiveness.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a thorough discussion on the limitations and societal impacts of the proposed method, which is crucial for understanding the broader implications of the work.
- The evaluation of the method is limited to a few datasets, and the results are not sufficiently robust to demonstrate the method's effectiveness across a broader range of tasks or datasets.
- There is a lack of discussion on the computational cost of the method, particularly in terms of training and inference times, which could impact the practicality of the approach.
- The paper does not adequately compare the proposed method with other context compression methods, which could help in understanding the relative advantages and disadvantages of the proposed approach.
- The paper lacks a detailed discussion on the hyperparameter choices and their impact on the results, which could affect the reproducibility and generalizability of the findings.

**Questions:**
- How does the proposed method perform in scenarios where the contextual information is not clausal, such as in the middle of a sentence?
- Could the authors provide a more detailed discussion on the computational cost of the method, including training and inference times?
- How does the method perform when compared to other context compression methods, particularly in terms of efficiency and effectiveness?
- What are the specific hyperparameters used in the experiments, and how do they affect the results?
- Could the authors provide more detailed results on the efficiency gains of the method, perhaps in terms of FLOPs or other computational metrics?
- How does the method handle the selection of nuggets in scenarios where the contextual information is not clausal?
- Could the authors provide more detailed results on the training and inference times for the proposed method, and how do these compare to other methods?
