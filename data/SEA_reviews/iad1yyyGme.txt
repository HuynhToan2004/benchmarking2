 **Summary:**
The paper introduces CausalTime, a novel benchmarking framework for time-series causal discovery (TSCD) that generates realistic time-series data along with their corresponding ground-truth causal graphs. The framework leverages deep neural networks, normalizing flows, and importance analysis to extract causal graphs and generate synthetic data that closely resembles real-world data. This allows for a more robust evaluation of TSCD algorithms, providing a significant advancement in the field. The paper is praised for its clarity, originality, and the potential impact it could have on the community.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, clear, and easy to follow, making it accessible to a broad audience.
- The proposed method is novel and innovative, introducing a new benchmarking framework for time-series causal discovery (TSCD) that is expected to be widely adopted in the community.
- The framework is comprehensive, covering the entire process from data generation to causal graph extraction, and is supported by a detailed ablation study.
- The paper provides a clear and detailed description of the methodology, including the use of deep neural networks and normalizing flows to generate realistic time-series data.
- The dataset generated by the framework is realistic and diverse, covering various fields such as weather, traffic, and healthcare.
- The paper includes a detailed evaluation of existing TSCD algorithms on the generated datasets, which could be useful for future research and development in the field.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a comprehensive evaluation of the dataset's realism and the causal graph's accuracy, which could undermine the credibility of the results.
- There is a significant overlap in content with previous works, particularly the CausalAssembly paper, which could lead to confusion about the novel contributions of this study.
- The paper does not adequately address the issue of missing data in real-world datasets, which is a significant challenge in many applications.
- The methodology and results sections are not well-integrated, with some results and methodological details relegated to the appendix, which could make it difficult for readers to fully understand the implications of the findings.
- The paper does not sufficiently discuss the limitations of the proposed method, which could help in understanding its applicability and potential pitfalls.
- There is a lack of discussion on the potential societal impacts of the work, which could be important for understanding the broader implications of the research.

**Questions:**
- Could you clarify the novel contributions of this study compared to previous works, particularly the CausalAssembly paper?
- How does the proposed method handle missing data in real-world datasets?
- Can you provide more details on the methodology used for extracting the causal graph, particularly how the importance matrix is calculated and how it relates to the ground truth causal graph?
- How does the method handle the issue of potential overfitting when using deep neural networks to fit the data?
- Can you discuss the potential societal impacts of the work and how these might be mitigated or addressed?
- How does the proposed method compare to other existing benchmarks, such as those proposed by Lawrence et al. (2021)?
