 **Summary:**
The paper examines the stitching generalization property in reinforcement learning (RL) algorithms, particularly focusing on off-the-shelf supervised learning (SL) algorithms. It argues that SL algorithms lack this property, which is crucial for dynamic programming-based RL algorithms. The paper proposes a data augmentation technique, temporal data augmentation, to enhance the generalization capabilities of SL algorithms. This technique is demonstrated to improve performance on tasks where the agent needs to navigate between states and goals that were not encountered together during training. The paper also introduces a new benchmark to evaluate stitching generalization in RL algorithms.

**Strengths:**
- The paper addresses an important and relevant question in the field of reinforcement learning (RL), specifically focusing on the stitching generalization property and its implications for SL algorithms.
- The paper is well-written, with clear and intuitive explanations that make complex concepts accessible to readers.
- The introduction of a new benchmark for evaluating stitching generalization is a significant contribution to the field, providing a valuable tool for future research.
- The proposed data augmentation technique is novel and effective, demonstrated to improve the performance of SL algorithms on tasks where stitching generalization is required.
- The paper provides a comprehensive theoretical analysis and empirical evidence to support its claims, enhancing the credibility of the findings.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper's theoretical analysis and empirical evidence do not convincingly demonstrate that the proposed data augmentation technique always results in stitching generalization, which is a critical claim.
- The paper's experimental setup and results are limited to specific environments, which may not generalize well to other types of tasks or environments.
- There is a lack of discussion on how the proposed method compares to existing methods, such as those using dynamic programming, which also aim to improve generalization in RL.
- The paper's claims about the limitations of SL algorithms in stitching generalization are not entirely novel, as similar observations have been made in previous works.
- The paper does not sufficiently address the scalability of the proposed method, particularly in high-dimensional state spaces, which could limit its applicability in more complex RL environments.

**Questions:**
- Could you clarify whether the proposed data augmentation technique always results in stitching generalization, and provide more detailed theoretical or empirical evidence to support this claim?
- How does the proposed method compare to existing methods that use dynamic programming to improve generalization in RL?
- Given the limitations in the experimental setup and the generalizability of the results, how do you plan to expand the scope of your research to include a broader range of environments and tasks?
- Could you discuss the potential impact of the proposed method on the scalability of RL algorithms, particularly in high-dimensional state spaces?
- How do you address the concerns about the novelty of your claims regarding the limitations of SL algorithms in stitching generalization, especially in light of previous works that have made similar observations?
