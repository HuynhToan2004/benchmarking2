 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel method, Alternating Denoising Diffusion Process (ADDP), which integrates pixel and token spaces to address both image generation and recognition tasks. AADP utilizes a diffusion process to transform raw pixels into VQ tokens and vice versa, aiming to enhance the generalization of image representations. This methodology is supported by extensive experiments that demonstrate competitive performance in unconditional image generation, image classification, object detection, and semantic segmentation. The paper also discusses the limitations of the approach, including the reliance on a pre-trained VQ Encoder-Decoder and the potential for increased computational complexity.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-organized, clearly written, and easy to follow, with a clear presentation of the main idea and motivation.
- The proposed method, Alternating Denoising Diffusion Process (ADDP), is innovative and effective, demonstrating superior performance in both image generation and recognition tasks.
- Extensive experiments and ablation studies are conducted to support the effectiveness of the proposed method, and the results show that AADP can achieve competitive performance in unconditional image generation, image classification, object detection, and semantic segmentation.
- The paper is the first to develop general representations applicable to both generation and dense recognition tasks, a significant advancement in the field.
- The proposed method is simple yet effective, and the experimental results are convincing, demonstrating the effectiveness of the method across various tasks.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed comparison with recent state-of-the-art methods in image recognition and generation, such as BEiT, MAE, and others, which could provide a clearer picture of the method's performance relative to the current state of the art.
- The paper does not discuss the computational complexity of the proposed method compared to other methods, which is crucial for understanding the practical applicability of the method.
- The paper relies heavily on a pre-trained VQ Encoder-Decoder, which may limit the diversity of generated images and could be a bottleneck for the method's performance.
- The paper does not provide sufficient qualitative results, such as visual examples of generated images, which could help in evaluating the quality of the generated images.
- The paper does not discuss the limitations of the proposed method, which could help in understanding the method's scope and potential areas for improvement.
- The paper could benefit from a more detailed discussion on the design choices and the impact of different design decisions on the method's performance.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors provide more details on the computational complexity of the proposed method compared to other methods?
- How does the method perform in terms of generalization to other datasets, especially those with different characteristics from ImageNet?
- Could the authors discuss the potential impact of the method on other downstream tasks, such as image captioning or image-text retrieval?
- What are the limitations of the proposed method, and how do these limitations affect the method's performance and applicability?
- Could the authors provide more qualitative results, such as visual examples of generated images, to help in evaluating the quality of the generated images?
- How does the method perform in terms of training and inference time, and how does this compare to other methods in terms of computational efficiency?
- Could the authors discuss the impact of different design choices on the method's performance, such as the number of VQ tokens used or the type of VQ tokenizer employed?
