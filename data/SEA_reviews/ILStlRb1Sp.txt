 **Summary:**
The paper investigates the memorization dynamics of large language models (LLMs) using random strings, specifically focusing on how these models memorize tokens within a string. It identifies two phases in the memorization process: a "guessing phase" where the model learns the distribution of tokens and a "memorization phase" where individual tokens are memorized. The study also explores the role of local and global contexts in token memorization, revealing that local contexts are sufficient for memorization, while global contexts are necessary to maintain token distribution. The paper provides a detailed analysis of how these dynamics influence the memorization process and offers insights into the mechanics of token memorization. However, the paper's scope is limited to random strings, which might not generalize well to more complex or realistic text scenarios.

**Strengths:**
- The paper provides a comprehensive analysis of the memorization process in LLMs, which is crucial for understanding the internal workings of these models.
- It introduces a novel approach by focusing on random strings, which is a simple yet effective method for studying the memorization dynamics.
- The paper is well-written, making the content accessible and easy to follow, with clear visualizations that aid in understanding the complex dynamics of memorization.
- The experiments are well-designed, providing a detailed analysis of the memorization phases and the role of local and global contexts in token memorization.
- The findings are significant and could have implications for privacy concerns and the reliability of generated outputs in LLMs.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper's scope is limited to random strings, which might not generalize well to more complex or realistic text scenarios.
- The paper's conclusions are somewhat limited by the simplicity of the test strings, which might not reflect real-world scenarios where LLMs are trained on complex and varied data.
- The paper's findings on the memorization dynamics might not be applicable to other types of models or data, limiting the broader impact of the research.
- The paper does not sufficiently discuss the implications of its findings for practical applications, such as privacy concerns or the reliability of generated outputs.
- There is a lack of discussion on the limitations of the study, which could include the generalizability of the findings to other types of data or models.

**Questions:**
- How do the findings on memorization dynamics in LLMs trained on random strings translate to more complex or realistic text scenarios?
- Can the authors provide examples or additional experiments to demonstrate the applicability of their findings to more varied and complex data?
- What are the implications of the findings for privacy concerns and the reliability of generated outputs in LLMs?
- How do the findings on the memorization dynamics in LLMs compare to other types of models or data?
- Could the authors elaborate on the limitations of their study and suggest areas for future research?
