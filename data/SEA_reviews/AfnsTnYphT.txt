 **Summary:**
The paper explores the sample complexity of different neural network architectures, specifically focusing on CNNs, LCNs, and FCNs, using a new task called Dynamic Signal Distribution (DSD). This task is designed to model locality and translation invariance in real-world images, which are crucial for vision-based tasks. The authors establish sample complexity bounds for these networks, showing that CNNs and LCNs outperform FCNs in terms of sample efficiency. The paper introduces a new variant of Fano's theorem to analyze the sample complexity and provides a theoretical framework for understanding the advantages of CNNs and LCNs over FCNs.

**Strengths:**
- The paper addresses a significant and interesting question regarding the sample complexity of different neural network architectures, specifically focusing on CNNs, LCNs, and FCNs.
- It introduces a novel task, Dynamic Signal Distribution (DSD), which is designed to capture properties of real-world images such as locality and translation invariance, providing a higher fidelity to real-world vision tasks.
- The paper provides a theoretical framework for understanding the advantages of CNNs and LCNs over FCNs, which is crucial for advancing the field of neural network design.
- The analysis is technically sound, and the paper is well-written, making it accessible and understandable to a broad audience.
- The results are surprising and interesting, showing that CNNs and LCNs outperform FCNs in terms of sample efficiency, which is a significant contribution to the field.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks empirical validation of the theoretical results, which could strengthen the claims made about the sample complexity of different architectures.
- There is a limited discussion on the practical implications of the theoretical results, which could be expanded to provide a more comprehensive understanding of the findings.
- The paper does not adequately address the limitations of its approach, such as the assumptions made about the input distribution and the generalizability of the results to other architectures or tasks.
- The notation used in the paper is dense and could be made more accessible to readers by providing a clearer definition and explanation of the symbols used.
- The paper does not sufficiently discuss the role of depth in a CNN in capturing dependency between different patches, which is a critical aspect of CNNs.
- The paper's presentation could be improved by including a summary of the main results and findings in the introduction, which would make the paper more accessible to readers.
