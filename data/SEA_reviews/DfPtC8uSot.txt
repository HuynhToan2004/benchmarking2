 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel method for enhancing the robustness of Graph Neural Networks (GNNs) against node feature attacks, focusing on the expected robustness of GCNs and GINs. It proposes a theoretical framework to define the expected robustness of GNNs, which is related to the orthogonality of weight matrices. The authors introduce the Graph Convolutional Orthogonal Robust Networks (GCORNs) to address this issue, which are shown to outperform existing defense methods in experimental evaluations. The paper also provides a probabilistic method for estimating expected robustness and conducts extensive experiments to demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed method.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, clear, and easy to follow, with a clear motivation and a clear problem definition.
- The authors propose a novel approach, GCORN, for enhancing the robustness of GCNs against node feature attacks, which is supported by theoretical analysis and extensive experiments.
- The theoretical analysis provides a deep insight into the robustness of GCNs and GINs, which is crucial for understanding the behavior of these models under adversarial attacks.
- The proposed probabilistic method for evaluating the expected robustness of GNNs is a significant contribution, as it allows for a more realistic and comprehensive evaluation of defense approaches.
- The paper presents an upper bound of the expected robustness of GCNs and GINs, which is a significant theoretical contribution.
- The experiments conducted are extensive and demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed method, showing improvements over existing defense methods.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed discussion on the limitations of the proposed method, particularly in terms of its scalability and applicability to other types of GNNs.
- The methodology used for estimating the expected robustness of GNNs is not clearly explained, and the paper does not provide a detailed explanation of how the expected robustness is estimated.
- The paper does not compare the proposed method with the most recent and advanced defense methods against node feature attacks, which could limit the understanding of its effectiveness.
- The theoretical analysis is limited to GCNs and GINs, and it is unclear whether the proposed method can be extended to other types of GNNs.
- The paper does not discuss the computational complexity of the proposed method, which could be a significant concern for large-scale applications.
- The paper does not provide a detailed discussion on the scalability of the proposed method, which is crucial for its practical application in real-world scenarios.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors provide more details on the limitations of the proposed method, particularly in terms of its scalability and applicability to other types of GNNs?
- How does the proposed method compare with the most recent and advanced defense methods against node feature attacks?
- Can the authors clarify the methodology used for estimating the expected robustness of GNNs, and provide a detailed explanation of this process?
- How does the proposed method handle structural perturbations, and what are the implications for the robustness of GNNs against these types of attacks?
- Can the authors provide a detailed discussion on the computational complexity of the proposed method, and how it scales with the size of the graph?
- How does the proposed method handle the trade-off between robustness and input sensitivity, and what are the implications for the performance of GNNs under adversarial attacks?
