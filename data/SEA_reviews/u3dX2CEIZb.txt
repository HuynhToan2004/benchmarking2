 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel method for enforcing hard physical constraints in neural network training for spatiotemporal problems, particularly in the context of solving partial differential equations (PDEs). The authors propose a mixture-of-experts (MoE) approach, where each expert solves a localized problem using a differentiable optimization method, which is then combined to form the final solution. This method is claimed to improve computational efficiency and accuracy compared to traditional methods that enforce soft or hard constraints directly. The approach is demonstrated through experiments on three challenging PDEs, showing significant improvements in runtime and error reduction. However, the paper's scope is limited to specific types of PDEs, and the generalization to other types of problems is not explored.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel method for enforcing hard physical constraints in neural network training, which is both innovative and potentially impactful for the field.
- The method is well-explained and supported by clear, detailed explanations of the theoretical underpinnings and practical implementation, making it accessible to a broad audience.
- The experimental results are robust and demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed method, showing significant improvements in runtime and error reduction compared to traditional methods.
- The paper is well-written and easy to follow, with clear figures and a logical structure that aids in understanding the complex concepts presented.
- The use of implicit differentiation and mixture of experts (MoE) is novel and well-integrated into the methodology, enhancing the paper's technical contribution.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper's scope is somewhat limited, focusing on a specific type of PDE (FNO) and not generalizing to other types of PDEs or problems, which could limit the practical applicability of the findings.
- The paper's claims about the scalability of the proposed method are not convincingly supported by the experimental results, particularly in the 2D Navier-Stokes experiments where the results are not as clear.
- The paper lacks a comprehensive discussion on the limitations and potential negative societal impacts of the proposed method, which could be crucial for understanding the broader implications of the research.
- There is a noticeable absence of a broader comparison with other existing methods for enforcing physical constraints in neural networks, which could have provided a more robust evaluation of the proposed method's effectiveness.
- The paper could benefit from a more detailed discussion on the potential negative societal impacts of the proposed method, as well as a more comprehensive comparison with other existing methods.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors clarify the computational efficiency of the proposed method compared to traditional methods, particularly in terms of the number of parameters and the computational complexity?
- How does the proposed method compare to other existing methods for enforcing physical constraints in neural networks, and what are the specific advantages of the proposed method?
- Could the authors provide more details on the experimental setup, particularly in terms of the initial conditions used and the training and testing data sets?
- In the context of the mixture of experts (MoE), how does the number of experts and the size of the local domains affect the performance of the method?
- How does the proposed method handle different types of PDEs, and what are the implications for its applicability to other types of problems?
- Can the authors discuss the potential negative societal impacts of the proposed method, and how these might be mitigated or addressed?
