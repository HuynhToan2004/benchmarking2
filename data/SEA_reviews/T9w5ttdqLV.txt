 **Summary:**
The paper investigates the representational limitations in linear mixing functions (LMF) used in centralized training with decentralized execution (CTDE) in multi-agent reinforcement learning (MARL). It identifies that LMF suffers from representational limitations in most MARL tasks, particularly in non-decomposable MMDPs. The authors propose a two-stage mixing framework, MUD, to address these limitations by mapping the differences into unbound variables, thus achieving complete representational capacity. The paper also introduces gradient shaping to address cross interference in value decomposition, enhancing the framework's effectiveness. The paper provides theoretical analysis and experimental validation, showcasing the framework's superiority over existing methods.

**Strengths:**
- The paper addresses a fundamental problem in value decomposition in MARL, focusing on the representational limitations of linear mixing functions (LMF) in most MARL tasks.
- It introduces a novel two-stage mixing framework, MUD, which maps differences into unbound variables, achieving complete representational capacity under the IGM constraint.
- The paper provides a theoretical analysis of the representational limitations of LMF and proposes a new mixing function that addresses these limitations, backed by empirical results.
- The paper is well-organized, with clear and comprehensive writing, and includes a thorough literature review and detailed analysis of the limitations of existing methods.
- The proposed framework is intuitive and easy to understand, with a focus on addressing the cross interference problem in value decomposition, which is crucial for achieving optimal performance in MARL tasks.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper could benefit from a more detailed explanation of the concepts of decomposable and indecomposable MMDPs, as they are crucial for understanding the theoretical contributions.
- The experimental results, particularly the comparisons with other methods, are not convincingly superior, and the paper lacks a broader range of comparisons with other state-of-the-art methods.
- There are several typographical and formatting errors throughout the paper, which could detract from its professional presentation.
- The paper does not adequately address the scalability of the proposed methods, particularly in more complex MARL tasks.
- The paper's theoretical contributions, while innovative, are somewhat limited in scope, as they primarily focus on LMF and do not explore the limitations of other mixing functions.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors clarify the definitions and implications of decomposable and indecomposable MMDPs, especially in relation to the linear factorizability of action-value functions?
- How does the proposed framework perform in more complex MARL tasks, and what are the expected outcomes?
- Could the authors provide more detailed experimental results, including comparisons with other state-of-the-art methods and a broader range of MARL tasks?
- How does the proposed framework handle the scalability issues, particularly in larger-scale MARL environments?
- Could the authors elaborate on the choice of mixing functions and the rationale behind their selection?
