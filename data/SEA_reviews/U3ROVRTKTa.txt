 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel prompting-based approach for temporal domain generalization, focusing on adapting a pre-trained model to future time periods without access to target domain data. The method involves learning global prompts, domain-specific prompts, and drift-aware prompts, which are parameter-efficient and time-efficient. The approach is evaluated on various datasets and tasks, including classification, regression, and time series forecasting, demonstrating its effectiveness and generalizability. However, the paper's reliance on synthetic datasets and its lack of comparison with recent prompting methods in domain generalization are noted as significant drawbacks.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel prompting-based approach for temporal domain generalization, which is both parameter-efficient and time-efficient.
- The proposed method is compatible across diverse tasks such as classification, regression, and time series forecasting, and sets a new state-of-the-art benchmark in temporal domain generalization.
- The paper is well-written, clear, and easy to follow, with a clear explanation of the proposed method and its application.
- The experiments conducted are comprehensive and demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed method across various datasets and tasks.
- The idea of generating time-sensitive prompts to capture temporal dynamics is innovative and adds a new perspective to the field of temporal domain generalization.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed comparison with recent prompting methods in domain generalization, which is a significant gap in the literature review.
- The experiments are primarily conducted on synthetic datasets, which may not fully represent real-world scenarios and could limit the generalizability of the findings.
- The paper does not adequately discuss the limitations of the proposed method, which could help in understanding its applicability and potential drawbacks.
- The paper does not provide sufficient details on the temporal prompt generator, such as the architecture and the training process, which could affect the reproducibility and understanding of the results.
- The paper lacks a thorough discussion on related works, particularly those that address similar issues in temporal domain generalization, which could provide a more robust context for the research.
- The paper's evaluation metrics and methodology could be improved to better reflect the performance of the proposed method in practical scenarios.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors provide a detailed comparison with recent prompting methods in domain generalization to clarify the advantages of their approach?
- How does the proposed method perform on real-world datasets, and what are the implications of using synthetic datasets for evaluation?
- Can the authors provide more details on the temporal prompt generator, such as its architecture and the training process, to enhance the reproducibility and understanding of the results?
- How does the proposed method handle scenarios where the distribution shift is abrupt rather than gradual, and what are the potential limitations in such scenarios?
- Can the authors discuss the potential limitations of the proposed method and how these might affect its practical applicability?
- Could the authors provide more details on the evaluation metrics and methodology used to ensure that the performance of the proposed method is accurately reflected in the results?
