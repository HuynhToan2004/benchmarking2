 **Summary:**
The paper introduces SparseFormer, a novel vision architecture designed for sparse visual recognition, which significantly reduces computational costs by processing only a limited number of tokens in the latent space. The methodology involves a focusing transformer that iteratively adjusts token regions of interest, enabling the model to focus on foregrounds and perform accurate recognition. The proposed approach is evaluated on ImageNet-1K classification, demonstrating competitive performance with less computational overhead compared to traditional dense architectures. The method is also applied to video classification, showing promising results. The paper is well-organized, clearly written, and includes extensive experiments and ablation studies.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, easy to follow, and provides a clear explanation of the methodology and experimental results.
- The proposed SparseFormer method is innovative, focusing on the sparse visual recognition process, which is a novel approach compared to existing dense visual recognition methods.
- The paper includes extensive experiments and ablation studies, demonstrating the effectiveness of the proposed method and providing a solid foundation for its application in various domains.
- The method is shown to be effective in reducing computational costs while maintaining performance, which is a significant contribution to the field of visual recognition.
- The paper is well-organized and provides a clear motivation for the proposed method, making it accessible and understandable to readers.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a direct comparison with other sparse visual recognition methods, which could provide a more robust evaluation of the proposed method.
- The experimental results are limited to ImageNet-1K classification and Kinetics-400 video classification. Additional experiments on other datasets such as COCO object detection, Pascal VOC semantic segmentation, and ADE20K segmentation could provide a more comprehensive evaluation.
- The paper does not discuss the scalability of the proposed method to larger input resolutions, which could be a limitation for its application in high-resolution tasks.
- The paper does not compare the proposed method with other efficient visual recognition methods, such as EfficientViT, which could provide insights into the relative performance and efficiency of the proposed method.
- The paper does not discuss the limitations of the proposed method, which could help in understanding its scope and applicability.

**Questions:**
- How does the proposed method compare with other sparse visual recognition methods, particularly in terms of computational efficiency and performance?
- Can the method be scaled up to larger input resolutions, and how would this affect its performance and efficiency?
- How does the method perform on other datasets such as COCO object detection, Pascal VOC semantic segmentation, and ADE20K segmentation?
- Can the method be adapted for other tasks beyond classification, such as detection and segmentation?
- How does the method handle the initialization of latent tokens, and what impact does this have on the performance of the model?
- Could the authors provide more details on the initialization of latent tokens and the impact of different initialization strategies on the performance of the model?
