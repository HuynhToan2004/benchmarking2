 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel model-agnostic explanation method named LINE, which integrates linear and non-linear perspectives to enhance the interpretability of machine learning models. It aims to address the limitations of existing methods by combining linear simplification and non-linear feature interaction analysis. The method is evaluated through experiments and a user study, demonstrating its effectiveness in explaining model predictions and highlighting potential biases. Despite its innovative approach, the paper suffers from significant issues in clarity, presentation, and the novelty of its contributions, which are not sufficiently distinct from existing methods like SHAP and LIME.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel approach by combining linear and non-linear perspectives to enhance the interpretability of machine learning models, which is a significant advancement in the field.
- The proposed method, LINE, is evaluated through experiments and a user study, demonstrating its effectiveness in explaining model predictions and highlighting potential biases.
- The paper is well-organized, making it easy to follow, and provides a clear and comprehensive explanation of the proposed method.
- The combination of linear and non-linear perspectives is a novel and promising approach that could potentially provide more nuanced and accurate explanations compared to existing methods.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper suffers from significant issues in clarity and presentation, with numerous grammatical and typographical errors that make it difficult to understand.
- The novelty of the proposed method is questionable, as it appears to be a combination of existing methods such as SHAP and LIME, without sufficient differentiation or innovation.
- The paper lacks a robust comparison with existing methods, particularly in terms of computational efficiency and the ability to handle complex feature interactions.
- The paper does not adequately address the limitations of its method, such as the assumptions of feature independence and the potential for over-simplification of complex models.
- The experiments and user studies presented are insufficiently detailed and do not convincingly demonstrate the superiority or practical utility of the proposed method over existing approaches.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors clarify the novelty and distinct contributions of their method compared to existing approaches like SHAP and LIME?
- How does the proposed method handle complex feature interactions that cannot be easily decomposed into pairwise interactions?
- What are the specific advantages of the proposed method over existing methods in terms of computational efficiency, scalability, and practical applicability?
- Can the authors provide more detailed examples or case studies to illustrate the effectiveness and practical utility of their method in real-world scenarios?
- How does the method handle scenarios where the feature independence assumption does not hold, and what are the implications of this assumption for the validity and reliability of the explanations provided?
- Could the authors elaborate on the methodology and results of the user studies, particularly in terms of the statistical significance and practical implications of the findings?
