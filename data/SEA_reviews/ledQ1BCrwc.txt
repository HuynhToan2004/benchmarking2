 **Summary:**
The paper introduces GraphMaker, a novel diffusion model tailored for generating large attributed graphs, which is a significant advancement in the field of graph machine learning. It addresses the challenges of generating complex patterns and scalability issues associated with traditional models. GraphMaker employs a minibatch strategy for scalability and node-level conditioning to enhance the quality of synthetic data. The paper also introduces a new evaluation pipeline to assess the quality of synthetic data, demonstrating GraphMaker's superiority in generating realistic and diverse large-attributed graphs. The authors provide extensive experiments and ablation studies to support their claims, although the paper could benefit from a more detailed discussion on the limitations and potential societal impacts of the proposed method.
