 **Summary:**
The paper investigates the impact of soft prompt normalization on the performance of vision-language models (VLMs), specifically focusing on the Low-Norm Effect. It introduces two corruption operations, REPLACE and RESCALE, to alter the norms of soft prompts, and proposes two normalization losses, PEN and PAN, to address the Low-Norm Effect. The authors conduct extensive experiments on various datasets, demonstrating that the proposed methods can enhance the performance of VLMs. The paper is well-organized and presents a novel approach to soft prompt tuning, although it lacks a thorough theoretical explanation for the observed phenomena and the computational cost of the proposed methods is not adequately discussed.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel approach to soft prompt tuning by proposing two corruption operations, REPLACE and RESCALE, which alter the norms of soft prompts, and two normalization losses, PEN and PAN, to address the Low-Norm Effect.
- The authors conduct extensive experiments to demonstrate the effectiveness of their proposed method, showing improvements in performance on various datasets.
- The paper is well-organized and easy to follow, with a clear presentation of the methodology and results.
- The phenomenon of the Low-Norm Effect is well-documented, and the paper provides a detailed analysis of its occurrence and impact on model performance.
- The proposed method is simple, effective, and easy to implement, making it accessible for further research and application.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a thorough theoretical explanation for the Low-Norm Effect and its relationship with the soft prompt.
- The computational cost of the proposed methods, particularly the PAN loss, is not discussed, which could be a significant concern for practical applications.
- The experiments are limited to the CoOp method, and it is unclear whether the proposed method can be applied to other soft prompt tuning methods.
- The paper could benefit from a more detailed discussion on the choice of parameters, such as the number of shots and the threshold for the Low-Norm Effect.
- The paper does not adequately discuss the potential negative societal impacts of the proposed methods.
- The paper's claims about being the first to study the role of soft prompt norms in VLMs are not entirely accurate, as similar studies have been previously conducted.

**Questions:**
- Could you clarify the relationship between the Low-Norm Effect and the soft prompt, particularly how the Low-Norm Effect is related to the soft prompt?
- How does the PEN loss degrade the performance, and why is it necessary to normalize soft prompts that are unaffected by the Low-Norm Effect?
- Is there a specific reason why the PEN loss was chosen over other normalization methods, such as L2 normalization?
- How does the proposed method perform when applied to other soft prompt tuning methods, such as PLOT?
- Can you provide more details on the computational cost of the PAN loss and how it impacts the practical application of the proposed method?
- How were the hyperparameters chosen for the experiments, and what is the rationale behind the choice of parameters such as the number of shots and the threshold for the Low-Norm Effect?
- Could you discuss the potential negative societal impacts of the proposed methods and how these might be mitigated?
