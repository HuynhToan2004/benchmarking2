 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel method for compressing NeRF representations using neural compression techniques, specifically targeting the TensoRF model. The approach involves a lightweight decoder, importance-weighted training loss, and a masked entropy model to optimize the compression of feature planes. The methodology includes an iterative inference framework for joint optimization of latent codes and a decoder, which is claimed to achieve significant compression ratios while maintaining high reconstruction quality. The paper provides both quantitative and qualitative results, demonstrating the effectiveness of the proposed method against existing baselines. However, concerns are raised about the novelty of the approach, the limited evaluation on different datasets, and the lack of detailed comparisons with other neural compression methods.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, easy to follow, and provides a clear explanation of the proposed method.
- The method achieves significant compression ratios, particularly for TensoRF-VM, with minimal impact on rendering quality.
- The paper includes a detailed ablation study that validates the effectiveness of the proposed method.
- The method is applicable to other grid-based NeRF methods, demonstrating versatility.
- The paper is well-organized, with clear figures and tables that aid in understanding the experimental results.
- The approach of removing the encoder and using importance-weighted training loss is innovative and could potentially improve the efficiency of NeRF compression.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper's novelty is questioned as it primarily combines existing techniques without significant innovation.
- The evaluation is limited to a few datasets, which may not adequately demonstrate the generalizability of the method.
- There is a lack of detailed comparison with other neural compression methods, which could help in understanding the advantages of the proposed method.
- The method's applicability to other NeRF models beyond TensoRF is not clearly demonstrated.
- The paper does not provide a detailed analysis of the training and rendering time, which is crucial for practical deployment.
- The paper lacks a detailed discussion on the limitations of the method, which could help in understanding its scope and applicability.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors provide a detailed comparison with other neural compression methods, especially those that have been proposed recently?
- How does the proposed method perform on different NeRF models beyond TensoRF?
- Could the authors provide a more detailed analysis of the training and rendering time, including a comparison with other compression methods?
- What are the specific advantages of the proposed method over other compression methods, particularly in terms of efficiency and effectiveness?
- Could the authors clarify the role of the masked entropy model in the overall compression process and its impact on the method's performance?
- How does the proposed method handle the storage and transmission constraints in real-world applications, especially in scenarios where data size is a critical concern?
