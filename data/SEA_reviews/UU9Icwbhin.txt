 **Summary:**
The paper introduces RetNet, a novel architecture for large language models that aims to balance training parallelism, low-cost inference, and good performance. RetNet leverages a multi-scale retention mechanism, which supports parallel, recurrent, and chunkwise recurrent representations. The authors claim that RetNet achieves favorable scaling results, parallel training, low-cost deployment, and efficient inference. The architecture is compared against Transformer-based models, demonstrating competitive performance and inference efficiency. The paper also discusses the theoretical underpinnings of RetNet and provides empirical evidence of its effectiveness through experiments.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel architecture, RetNet, which achieves training parallelism, low-cost inference, and good performance.
- RetNet supports three computation paradigms, including parallel, recurrent, and chunkwise recurrent representations, which enhance its flexibility and applicability.
- The paper is well-written, making it easy to follow, and includes a comprehensive literature review, effectively positioning the proposed architecture against existing models.
- RetNet shows significant improvements in inference efficiency and memory usage compared to Transformer-based models, which is crucial for deploying large language models in real-world scenarios.
- The theoretical derivation of the connection between recurrence and attention is well-explained, providing a solid foundation for the proposed architecture.
- The paper includes a detailed ablation study, which helps in understanding the impact of various components of the architecture.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed discussion on the scalability of RetNet, particularly in terms of its ability to handle very large models and long sequences.
- The evaluation of RetNet is limited to language modeling tasks, and it would be beneficial to see its performance on other tasks such as image classification, object detection, or NLP tasks.
- The paper does not provide a detailed analysis of the computational complexity of RetNet compared to Transformer-based models, which is crucial for understanding its efficiency.
- The paper does not discuss the limitations of RetNet, which could help in understanding its applicability and potential areas for improvement.
- The comparison with other efficient attention models like FlashAttention and RoPE is missing, which could provide a better understanding of RetNet's performance relative to existing models.
- The paper could benefit from a more detailed discussion on the implementation details of RetNet, particularly how it is implemented in practice and how it can be efficiently deployed on different hardware platforms.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors provide a more detailed analysis of the computational complexity of RetNet compared to Transformer-based models?
- How does RetNet perform on other tasks beyond language modeling, such as image classification or object detection?
- Can the authors discuss the limitations of RetNet and potential areas for future research?
- How does RetNet compare to other efficient attention models like FlashAttention and RoPE in terms of performance and efficiency?
- Can the authors provide a detailed comparison of RetNet's training and inference speed with other models, especially in scenarios where training and inference are critical, such as in real-time applications?
- How is RetNet implemented in practice, and what are the key implementation details that could impact its performance and efficiency on different hardware platforms?
