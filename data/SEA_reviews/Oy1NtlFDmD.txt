 **Summary:**
The paper introduces StructDrop, a method aimed at improving the training efficiency of graph neural networks (GNNs) by replacing the time-consuming message passing with fast and sparse matrix multiplication. StructDrop involves uniformly sampling column-row pairs from the graph adjacency matrix and node embedding matrix, which reduces computational complexity. The method also incorporates instance normalization to address the distribution shift caused by random sampling. Extensive experiments across various datasets demonstrate that StructDrop achieves significant speedups and maintains performance. However, concerns are raised regarding the novelty of the method, as it builds on existing techniques like top-k sampling and instance normalization. The paper also faces criticism for its lack of theoretical analysis and insufficient comparison with other state-of-the-art methods.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, making it easy to follow and understand the proposed method.
- The proposed method, StructDrop, is simple yet effective, achieving significant speedups without compromising performance.
- The paper provides comprehensive experimental results, demonstrating the efficiency and effectiveness of StructDrop.
- The method's application of instance normalization to address the distribution shift caused by random sampling is a novel approach.
- The paper includes an ablation study that shows the effectiveness of the proposed method.

**Weaknesses:**
- The methodology is not entirely novel, as it builds on existing techniques like top-k sampling and instance normalization.
- The paper lacks a thorough theoretical analysis, which could strengthen the claims made about the method's effectiveness.
- The paper does not adequately compare the proposed method with other state-of-the-art methods, which could provide a clearer picture of its advantages and disadvantages.
- The paper does not provide sufficient details on the hardware and software used in the experiments, which is crucial for reproducibility and understanding the impact of different setups on the results.
- The paper could benefit from a more detailed discussion on the limitations and potential negative societal impacts of the proposed method.

**Questions:**
- Could you provide more details on the hardware and software used in the experiments, including the specific GPU model and the software versions used?
- How does the proposed method perform when compared to other state-of-the-art methods in terms of speed and accuracy?
- What are the specific reasons for the observed performance improvements in the experiments, and how do these compare to the theoretical expectations?
- Can you clarify the role and impact of instance normalization in the proposed method, particularly in terms of its contribution to the overall performance and efficiency?
- How does the method handle the potential for underfitting due to the uniform sampling of column-row pairs, and what steps are taken to mitigate this risk?
- Could you provide more details on the experimental setup, including the specifics of the datasets used and the training parameters, to help understand the results more clearly?
