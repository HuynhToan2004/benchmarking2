 **Summary:**
The paper introduces BECLR, a novel unsupervised few-shot learning (U-FSL) method that addresses the challenges of positive sampling and sample bias in U-FSL. BECLR incorporates the DyCE module for positive sampling and the OpTA module for bias reduction. The DyCE module dynamically constructs memory clusters to enhance positive sampling, while OpTA aligns query and support set representations to mitigate sample bias. BECLR is evaluated across various datasets and shows significant performance improvements over existing methods. However, the paper has been critiqued for its complexity, lack of clarity in some sections, and potential over-reliance on certain components like OpTA.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces two novel modules, DyCE and OpTA, which are effective in addressing the challenges of positive sampling and sample bias in unsupervised few-shot learning.
- BECLR, the proposed method, sets a new state-of-the-art across various U-FSL benchmarks, demonstrating significant performance improvements over existing methods.
- The paper is well-organized, clearly written, and provides extensive quantitative and qualitative experiments to support the claims.
- The DyCE module is a significant contribution to the field, providing a more efficient way to enhance positive sampling compared to traditional methods.
- The paper is well-presented, with clear figures and tables that aid in understanding the proposed methods and their impacts.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper is overly complex, with multiple modules and hyperparameters, which may hinder its practical application and reproducibility.
- The explanation of some components, such as the DyCE module and the use of optimal transport in OpTA, is insufficient or unclear, which could confuse readers.
- There is a lack of comparison with some relevant works, such as Deep Metric Learning-based methods, which could provide a more comprehensive evaluation of the proposed methods.
- The paper does not discuss the limitations of the proposed methods, which is crucial for understanding the scope and applicability of the findings.
- The computational efficiency of BECLR, especially compared to existing methods, is not adequately addressed, which is important for practical deployment.
- The paper could benefit from a more detailed discussion on the computational cost and memory requirements of BECLR, as these are significant factors in real-world applications.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors clarify the role and impact of the DyCE module on positive sampling, particularly how it addresses the issue of potential positives being treated as negatives?
- How does the DyCE module perform in different scenarios, such as when the base and novel classes are drawn from different datasets?
- Can the authors provide more details on the computational efficiency of BECLR, including the training and inference times, and how these compare to existing methods?
- How does BECLR handle the computational and memory requirements, especially in large-scale applications?
- Could the authors discuss the limitations of the proposed methods and how these might affect their practical deployment?
- The paper mentions that BECLR sets a new state-of-the-art across various U-FSL benchmarks. Could the authors provide a more detailed comparison with the previous state-of-the-art, including the specific datasets and conditions under which BECLR outperforms other methods?
- How does BECLR perform when the number of support samples is significantly reduced, as in some real-world scenarios?
