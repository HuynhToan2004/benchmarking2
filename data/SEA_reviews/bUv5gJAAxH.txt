 **Summary:**
The paper investigates the relationship between the implicit biases of neural networks, specifically focusing on the essential Fourier frequencies required for accurate image classification and those necessary for successful adversarial attacks. It proposes a novel method using intrinsic dimension to quantify the correlation between these frequencies, which is empirically validated on datasets such as CIFAR-10 and ImageNet. The paper also explores the potential for using this correlation to understand adversarial attacks and their impact on model robustness. However, concerns are raised about the generalizability of the findings to more complex or diverse datasets, the lack of theoretical justification for the correlation method, and the potential limitations of the experimental setup.

**Strengths:**
- The paper addresses an important and timely research topic, focusing on the relationship between the implicit biases of neural networks and adversarial attacks, which is crucial for understanding the robustness of machine learning models.
- The experimental results are well-presented, providing a clear and detailed analysis of the correlation between essential frequencies and adversarial frequencies, which is supported by empirical evidence.
- The paper is well-written, making it easy to follow, and the methodology used is well-explained, contributing to its clarity and accessibility.
- The authors propose a novel approach to quantify the correlation between essential frequencies and adversarial frequencies using intrinsic dimension, which is a promising method for future research in the field.
- The paper provides a comprehensive literature review, effectively contextualizing the research within the existing body of knowledge.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper primarily focuses on a limited set of datasets (CIFAR-10 and ImageNet) and does not explore more diverse or complex datasets, which could limit the generalizability of the findings.
- The correlation between essential frequencies and adversarial frequencies is empirically observed but lacks a theoretical justification, which could weaken the credibility of the results.
- The paper does not sufficiently discuss the limitations of the proposed method, such as its applicability to different types of neural networks or the scalability of the experiments to larger datasets.
- The paper could benefit from a more detailed discussion on the potential societal implications of the research, particularly in relation to the ethical considerations of adversarial attacks.
- The experimental setup and methodology, particularly the use of specific adversarial attacks and the choice of models, are not sufficiently justified or explained, which could raise questions about the robustness and validity of the results.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors provide more details on the choice of adversarial attacks and the rationale behind using specific models in their experiments?
- How does the proposed method perform when applied to more complex or diverse datasets, and what are the implications of these findings for the broader applicability of the results?
- Could the authors elaborate on the potential societal implications of their research, particularly in relation to the ethical considerations of adversarial attacks?
- How does the proposed method compare to existing methods for quantifying the correlation between essential frequencies and adversarial frequencies, and what are the advantages or disadvantages of the proposed approach?
- Can the authors provide more theoretical justification for the observed correlation between essential frequencies and adversarial frequencies, and discuss the potential limitations or assumptions of their method?
