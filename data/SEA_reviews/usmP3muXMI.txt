 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel regularization method aimed at reducing overfitting in deep neural networks (DNNs) by minimizing the Chebyshev Prototype Risk (CPR). This method optimizes the feature extractor layer by incorporating a new loss function that encourages intra-class feature covariance reduction and inter-class feature vector separation. Theoretical analysis is provided, including proofs of the CPR's mathematical basis, and empirical results demonstrate the method's effectiveness in improving test performance. However, the paper is criticized for its limited experimental scope, lack of comparison with other regularization methods, and unclear presentation in some sections.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel regularization method that optimizes the feature extractor layer, which is a significant contribution to the field.
- The theoretical analysis provided is comprehensive and well-explained, including proofs of the mathematical basis of the Chebyshev Prototype Risk (CPR) and its implications for overfitting.
- The empirical results demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed method, showing improvements in test performance and reducing overfitting.
- The paper is well-written, making it accessible and easy to follow, which is crucial for understanding complex theoretical concepts.
- The method's approach to reducing intra-class feature covariance while maintaining inter-class feature vector separation is innovative and could potentially improve the generalization performance of deep neural networks.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a thorough comparison with other regularization methods, which limits the understanding of how the proposed method performs against existing techniques.
- The experimental results are limited to specific datasets and network architectures, which might not generalize well to other scenarios.
- The presentation of the paper could be improved, particularly in the introduction and some sections of the methodology, to better engage the reader and clarify the contributions.
- The paper does not discuss the limitations of its method, which is crucial for understanding its applicability and potential drawbacks.
- The empirical results are limited to small-scale datasets, which might not convincingly demonstrate the method's effectiveness on larger, more complex datasets.
- The paper does not provide a detailed discussion on the computational complexity of the proposed method, which is crucial for understanding its scalability and efficiency.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors provide a more detailed comparison with other regularization methods to better understand the relative advantages and disadvantages of their proposed method?
- How does the proposed method perform on larger, more complex datasets, and what are the implications for its scalability and efficiency?
- Can the authors clarify the computational complexity of the proposed method and discuss any potential challenges in scaling it to larger datasets or more complex network architectures?
- How does the proposed method compare in terms of computational complexity and efficiency with other regularization methods, and what are the implications for its practical application?
- Could the authors provide a more detailed discussion on the limitations of their method and how these might affect its applicability and effectiveness in different scenarios?
