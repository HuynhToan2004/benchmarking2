 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel approach to analyzing the behavior of neural networks trained with derivative loss, a variant of the traditional least-squares loss. This derivative loss, which includes the derivative of the network's output, is shown to affect the convergence properties of the Neural Tangent Kernel (NTK) equations. The authors extend the NTK framework to include this derivative loss and provide theoretical insights into its implications, such as slower convergence rates and changes in the eigenvalues of the NTK. The paper also includes numerical experiments to support these theoretical findings. However, the clarity and depth of the theoretical contributions are questioned, and the paper is criticized for its lack of novelty and the limited scope of its experiments.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel approach to analyzing the behavior of neural networks trained with derivative loss, which is a significant extension of the traditional NTK framework.
- The authors provide theoretical insights into the effects of derivative loss on the convergence properties of the NTK equations, which could be useful for understanding the behavior of neural networks in practical settings.
- The paper includes numerical experiments to support the theoretical findings, which helps in validating the theoretical claims.
- The topic of derivative loss and its implications for neural network training is of significant interest, and the paper provides a comprehensive analysis of this topic.
- The paper is well-written, making it accessible and understandable to a broad audience, and the inclusion of numerical experiments adds credibility to the theoretical claims.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks clarity in explaining the theoretical contributions, particularly the relationship between the derivative loss and the NTK equations, which is crucial for understanding the implications of the findings.
- The experimental setup is limited, focusing on simple toy examples which may not adequately represent real-world scenarios, and the experiments are not extensive enough to convincingly support the theoretical claims.
- The paper does not sufficiently discuss the practical implications of the findings, such as how the results might be applied to improve neural network training in practical scenarios.
- The novelty of the paper is limited, as the use of derivative loss is not a new concept, and the theoretical contributions do not significantly advance beyond existing literature.
- The paper suffers from poor writing quality, with numerous typos, grammatical errors, and inconsistencies in notation, which detracts from its professionalism and readability.
