 **Summary:**
The paper explores the use of untrained neural networks for MRI reconstruction, focusing on the impact of network architecture on the reconstruction quality. Specifically, it investigates how certain architectural components, such as unlearnt upsampling and kernel sizes, influence the performance of the reconstruction. The authors propose two methods: constraining the frequency bandwidth of the white-noise input and enforcing function smoothness via Lipschitz regularization, to mitigate overfitting and improve reconstruction quality. The study is supported by empirical evidence from both knee and brain datasets, demonstrating that these methods can enhance the performance of underperforming architectures without requiring significant architectural modifications.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, clear, and easy to follow, making it accessible to a broad audience.
- The authors provide a thorough analysis of the impact of different architectural components on reconstruction quality, which is a significant contribution to the field.
- The proposed methods are simple, effective, and do not require significant computational resources, making them practical for real-world applications.
- The paper includes a comprehensive experimental evaluation that demonstrates the effectiveness of the proposed methods in improving reconstruction quality.
- The authors' insights into the architectural biases in MRI reconstruction are valuable and could guide future research in this area.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper could benefit from a more detailed discussion on the limitations of the proposed methods, particularly in terms of their applicability to different types of MRI data and the potential impact on computational efficiency.
- There is a lack of comparison with other state-of-the-art methods, which could help to establish the relative effectiveness of the proposed methods.
- The paper does not adequately address the issue of overfitting in more complex architectures, which could limit the generalizability of the findings.
- The experimental setup and results are not convincingly presented, with some figures and tables being difficult to interpret or understand.
- The paper could benefit from a more comprehensive literature review and a clearer articulation of the novelty and contribution of the proposed methods.

**Questions:**
- Can the proposed methods be applied to other types of MRI data, such as dynamic or functional MRI?
- How do the proposed methods compare in terms of computational efficiency and practicality with other methods that require more complex architectures or additional training data?
- Could the authors provide more detailed examples or case studies to illustrate the effectiveness of the proposed methods in real-world scenarios?
- How do the authors address the issue of overfitting in more complex architectures, and what are the implications of this for the generalizability of the findings?
- Could the authors clarify the experimental setup and results, particularly in figures and tables where the data and conclusions are not clearly presented?
