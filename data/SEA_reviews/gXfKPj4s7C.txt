 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel approach to open-vocabulary 3D object detection, leveraging large-scale 3D object datasets to augment existing 3D scene datasets. The proposed method, named Object2Scene, inserts objects from various sources into 3D scenes, generating text descriptions for the newly inserted objects. A unified framework, L3Det, is introduced to achieve 3D object-text alignment, and a cross-domain category-level contrastive learning method is employed to mitigate domain gaps. The method is evaluated on the ScanNet and SUN RGB-D datasets, demonstrating superior performance compared to existing methods. However, concerns were raised about the novelty of the approach, the generalizability of the method to other datasets, and the clarity of some methodological descriptions.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel approach to open-vocabulary 3D object detection by leveraging large-scale 3D object datasets to augment existing 3D scene datasets, which is a significant advancement in the field.
- The proposed method, Object2Scene, is well-motivated and effectively addresses the inconsistent annotation issue, providing a clear and intuitive methodology.
- The paper is well-written, making it easy to follow, and includes a comprehensive review of related work, which aids in understanding the context and contributions of the research.
- The experiments conducted on the ScanNet and SUN RGB-D datasets demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed method, with significant improvements over existing methods.
- The paper includes a detailed ablation study that supports the effectiveness of the proposed method, providing a robust evaluation of the approach.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed explanation of the data augmentation techniques used, particularly in the context of the "Data augmentation" section.
- There is a noticeable absence of discussion on the limitations of the proposed method, which could provide a more balanced view of the approach's strengths and weaknesses.
- The novelty of the proposed method is questioned, as similar approaches using 3D object datasets for 3D scene augmentation have been previously explored.
- The paper does not include qualitative results, which could provide a more comprehensive understanding of the method's performance and limitations.
- There is a lack of clarity in some methodological descriptions, such as the "Object Normalization and Resampling" and "Object Placement" sections, which could benefit from additional details or examples.
- The paper's claims regarding the novelty and effectiveness of the proposed method are not adequately supported by comparisons with existing methods or other large-scale 3D datasets.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors provide more details on the data augmentation techniques used, particularly how they are applied and their impact on the performance of the model?
- How does the proposed method handle the inconsistency in annotation mentioned in the paper?
- Could the authors clarify the process of "Object Normalization and Resampling" and "Object Placement" to enhance the understanding of these critical steps?
- What is the role and impact of the "Cross-domain category-level contrastive learning" in the overall performance of the model?
- How does the method perform on other large-scale 3D datasets, such as Objaverse, and how does it compare to other methods in terms of open-vocabulary 3D object detection?
- Can the authors provide qualitative results or additional experimental evidence to support the effectiveness of the proposed method?
