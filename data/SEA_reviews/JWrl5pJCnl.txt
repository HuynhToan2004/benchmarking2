 **Summary:**
The paper introduces Instruct2Act, a framework that utilizes a large language model (LLM) to convert multi-modal instructions into sequential actions for robotic manipulation tasks. The system integrates several foundation models such as SAM and CLIP to identify objects and classify them, generating Python programs that form a perception, planning, and action loop. The framework is evaluated on various benchmarks and real-world scenarios, demonstrating its effectiveness and adaptability. It leverages LLMs to generate decision-making actions, which can handle different instruction modalities and input types, making it a promising approach for robotic applications.
