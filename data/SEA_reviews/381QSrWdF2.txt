 **Summary:**
The paper explores the stationary distribution of stochastic gradient descent (SGD) in the context of neural networks, focusing on the impact of symmetries in the loss function. It introduces the "Law of Balance" which suggests that the norms of two variables are balanced under certain conditions, and applies this law to a deep diagonal linear network to derive a stationary distribution. The authors argue that this distribution exhibits unique phenomena such as phase transitions and loss of ergodicity, which are specific to deep networks. The paper also discusses the implications of these findings for understanding the behavior of SGD in practical scenarios.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, with clear exposition of the problem and a logical structure that effectively guides the reader through the content.
- The introduction of the Law of Balance is innovative and provides a novel perspective on the dynamics of stochastic gradient descent (SGD).
- The paper presents a rigorous mathematical analysis, including detailed proofs and examples, which support the claims made about the stationary distribution of SGD.
- The implications of the results are discussed in depth, highlighting the potential impact of the findings on the understanding of SGD dynamics.
- The paper provides a unique analysis of the stationary distribution of SGD in the context of symmetries in the loss function, which is a significant contribution to the field.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks empirical validation of the theoretical results, which could enhance its credibility and practical applicability.
- The focus on diagonal linear networks may limit the generalizability of the findings to more complex neural network architectures.
- The paper could benefit from a more comprehensive literature review, particularly regarding the use of symmetries in the analysis of SGD dynamics.
- The paper's theoretical results are limited to specific types of loss functions and network architectures, which may not reflect real-world scenarios.
- The paper does not sufficiently discuss the limitations of its theoretical approach, which could lead to confusion about the scope and applicability of the findings.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors provide a more detailed discussion on the potential practical applications of the Law of Balance and the stationary distribution of SGD?
- How does the stationary distribution of SGD compare to other distributions commonly used in statistical physics, such as the Boltzmann distribution?
- Can the authors provide more examples or scenarios where the Law of Balance and the stationary distribution of SGD could be applied in practical machine learning problems?
- How does the paper's theoretical analysis hold up against more complex loss functions and neural network architectures, such as those with non-linearities or multiple hidden layers?
- Could the authors elaborate on the choice of focusing on diagonal linear networks and discuss the implications of this choice for the generalizability of the findings?
