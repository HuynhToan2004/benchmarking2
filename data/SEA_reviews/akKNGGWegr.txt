 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a knowledge distillation method for spatio-temporal graph forecasting, aiming to enhance the performance of models by distilling knowledge from complex Graph Neural Networks (GNNs) to simpler Multi-Layer Perceptrons (MLPs). The proposed method integrates the information bottleneck principle to filter out task-irrelevant noise and uses spatio-temporal prompts to improve generalization. The approach is tested on three large-scale datasets, showing improvements in efficiency and accuracy compared to state-of-the-art methods. However, the paper has been critiqued for its lack of novelty, unclear presentation, and insufficient comparison with relevant baselines.

**Strengths:**
- The paper addresses the scalability and generalization issues in large-scale spatio-temporal prediction, which is crucial for real-world applications.
- The proposed method of knowledge distillation from GNNs to MLPs is innovative and effectively transfers knowledge from complex models to simpler, more efficient ones.
- The paper is well-written and easy to follow, making it accessible to a broad audience.
- The methodology is supported by extensive experiments and ablation studies, demonstrating the effectiveness of the proposed approach.
- The integration of the information bottleneck principle and spatio-temporal prompts enhances the generalization ability of the student MLP and improves the robustness of the knowledge distillation process.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a clear motivation for using knowledge distillation in the spatio-temporal domain, and the advantages of the proposed method over existing methods are not convincingly demonstrated.
- The paper does not adequately address the scalability of the proposed method, particularly in terms of the complexity of the datasets used.
- There is a notable absence of novelty in the methodology, as the use of the information bottleneck and knowledge distillation is not new.
- The paper suffers from unclear presentation and lacks detailed explanations of key components such as the spatio-temporal IB and the teacher-bounded loss.
- The comparison with baselines is insufficient, particularly in terms of not including relevant works such as ST-SSL and not discussing the impact of different teacher models on the performance of the student model.
- The paper does not sufficiently address the generalization ability of the student MLP, which is critical for practical applications.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors provide a more detailed explanation of the motivation behind using knowledge distillation in the spatio-temporal domain and how it addresses the specific challenges in this area?
- How does the proposed method compare in terms of scalability and efficiency with other state-of-the-art methods, particularly in handling larger and more complex datasets?
- Can the authors clarify the role and impact of the teacher model on the performance of the student model, and discuss how the methodology could be adapted to different teacher models?
- How does the paper ensure the robustness and generalization ability of the student MLP, particularly in scenarios where the teacher model may not perform optimally?
- Could the authors provide more detailed comparisons with relevant baselines, including ST-SSL, and discuss the impact of different hyperparameters on the performance of the model?
- Can the authors provide more detailed explanations of the spatio-temporal IB and the teacher-bounded loss, and discuss how these components contribute to the overall effectiveness of the model?
