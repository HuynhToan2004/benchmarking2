 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel method called Variance Reduced Meta-Continual Learning (VR-MCL), which aims to address the variance issues in Meta-Continual Learning (Meta-CL) by leveraging variance reduction techniques. The method integrates momentum-based variance reduction into Meta-CL, enhancing the estimation of the Hessian matrix and reducing variance in the hypergradient. Theoretical analysis and extensive experiments across three datasets demonstrate the effectiveness of VR-MCL in improving performance and reducing variance. The paper also provides a unified framework for regularization-based methods in continual learning, highlighting the connection between regularization-based methods and Meta-CL.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel perspective on Meta-Continual Learning (Meta-CL) by characterizing it as a technique that approximates the Hessian matrix, thus establishing a connection between Meta-CL and regularized methods.
- The proposed Variance-Reduced Meta-CL (VR-MCL) method effectively reduces the variance in Meta-CL, which is a significant contribution to the field.
- The paper provides a unified framework for regularization-based methods in continual learning, enhancing the understanding of these methods and their applications.
- The theoretical analysis and empirical results are robust, demonstrating the effectiveness of VR-MCL in reducing variance and improving performance.
- The paper is well-written, making it accessible and easy to follow, with clear explanations of the theoretical concepts and empirical results.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a thorough discussion on the novelty and contributions of the proposed VR-MCL method, particularly in comparison to existing methods such as SAGA and SGD.
- There is a need for more detailed experimental results, including the performance of VR-MCL with different memory buffer sizes and different backbone networks.
- The paper could benefit from a more comprehensive literature review, especially in the area of variance reduction techniques in continual learning.
- The paper's focus on variance reduction might limit its applicability to other types of continual learning settings, such as incremental learning.
- The paper could provide more detailed explanations of the theoretical concepts, particularly the assumptions and implications of the regret bound and the variance reduction method.

**Questions:**
- Could you elaborate on the specific advantages of VR-MCL over existing methods like SAGA and SGD, particularly in terms of variance reduction and performance improvement?
- How does the performance of VR-MCL vary with different memory buffer sizes, and what are the optimal memory buffer sizes for different settings?
- Could you provide more details on the experimental setup, such as the number of runs and the standard deviation of the results?
- How does the proposed method perform with different backbone networks, and what are the implications of using different networks on the performance and variance reduction?
- Could you clarify the assumptions and implications of the regret bound in Theorem 1, and how these assumptions affect the practical applicability of the model?
