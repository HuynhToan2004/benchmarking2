 **Summary:**
The paper investigates the temporal information encoding in unconditional video generation models, specifically focusing on the MoCoGAN, MoCoGAN-HD, and StyleGAN-V architectures. The authors introduce a method using a Gradient Reversal Layer (GRL) with a lightweight CNN to prevent the implicit encoding of temporal information. The experiments conducted on datasets like FaceForensics, SkyTimelapse, and UCF-101 demonstrate that this method can effectively reduce the temporal accuracy of the generated videos, which is beneficial for improving the Fréchet Video Distance (FVD) score. However, the paper has been critiqued for its limited novelty, as the method is a straightforward application of GRL without significant technical innovation. Furthermore, the paper's focus on the FVD metric and its potential misalignment with human perception has been questioned.

**Strengths:**
- The paper addresses an interesting problem in video generation, specifically focusing on the temporal information encoded in unconditional video generation models.
- The authors have conducted extensive experiments on various datasets, including FaceForensics, SkyTimelapse, and UCF-101, to validate their claims.
- The paper is well-organized and easy to follow, making it accessible to readers.
- The proposed method using a Gradient Reversal Layer (GRL) with a lightweight CNN is a simple yet effective solution for preventing the implicit encoding of temporal information in video generation models.
- The paper introduces a new metric, temporal accuracy, which is a valuable addition to the evaluation of video generation models.
- The paper is well-written and the results are presented clearly, which enhances the understanding of the research findings.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a thorough analysis of the impact of the proposed method on other metrics such as human preference, which is crucial for evaluating the realism and quality of the generated videos.
- The novelty of the method is questioned as it is seen as a straightforward application of GRL without significant technical innovation.
- The paper's focus on the Fréchet Video Distance (FVD) metric raises concerns about its alignment with human perception, particularly in terms of its ability to accurately reflect the quality of the generated videos from a human perspective.
- The method's effectiveness is limited by its reliance on the FVD metric, which may not adequately capture the temporal coherence or other aspects of video quality that are important for human perception.
- The paper does not sufficiently discuss the limitations of the proposed method, which could include potential issues with the generalizability of the results across different datasets and architectures.
- The paper could benefit from a more detailed discussion on the potential negative societal impacts of the research, particularly in relation to the generation of fake news videos.

**Questions:**
- How does the proposed method affect other metrics such as human preference, and what are the implications of this for the realism and quality of the generated videos?
- Could the authors provide a more detailed analysis of the impact of the proposed method on the temporal accuracy metric, particularly in terms of its correlation with other metrics like human preference?
- Why was the decision made to focus on the FVD metric, and how does this choice align with the overall goals and contributions of the paper?
- How does the proposed method perform when applied to different video generation architectures, such as those based on diffusion models?
- Could the authors discuss the potential negative societal impacts of their research, particularly in relation to the generation of fake news videos, and how these impacts might be mitigated?
