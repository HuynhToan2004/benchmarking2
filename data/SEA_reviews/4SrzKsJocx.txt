 **Summary:**
The paper investigates the dimensionality reduction of multimodal datasets, comparing independent dimensionality reduction (IDR) and simultaneous dimensionality reduction (SDR) methods. The authors employ a generative linear model to analyze the performance of PCA, PLS, and CCA under various conditions, such as sample size, noise levels, and signal-to-noise ratios. They find that SDR methods, particularly rCCA, are more effective in reconstructing shared signals, especially in scenarios with low sample sizes. The study also explores the impact of data dimensionality and the number of samples on the performance of these methods. Despite the extensive numerical experiments, the paper's theoretical contributions are limited, and the discussion on non-linear models and the generalizability of the findings is insufficient.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written and easy to follow, with clear figures and tables that aid in understanding the results.
- The authors provide a comprehensive analysis of various dimensionality reduction (DR) methods, comparing their performance in different scenarios.
- The paper addresses a significant problem in the field by exploring the performance of different DR methods in multimodal data analysis.
- The experiments are well-designed, and the results are robust, providing insights into the strengths and weaknesses of different DR methods.
- The paper is original in its approach, providing a novel perspective on the use of SDR methods for dimensionality reduction in multimodal data.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a theoretical contribution, relying heavily on numerical experiments without a strong theoretical foundation.
- The discussion on non-linear models and the generalizability of the findings to more complex data structures is insufficient.
- The paper's scope is limited to linear models, which may not generalize well to more complex, non-linear datasets.
- There is a lack of clarity in the presentation of the methodology and results, particularly in the explanation of the generative linear model and the dimensionality reduction methods used.
- The paper does not adequately address the limitations of its approach, such as the assumption of linearity in the data and the potential impact of non-linear relationships between variables.
- The paper could benefit from a more comprehensive discussion on the practical implications of the findings and the potential societal impacts of the research.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors clarify the role and significance of the generative linear model in their study, particularly how it contributes to the understanding of dimensionality reduction in multimodal data?
- How do the findings of this study apply to non-linear models, and what are the implications for the performance of different DR methods in such scenarios?
- Could the authors provide a more detailed explanation of the methodology used in the experiments, especially the dimensionality reduction methods and the choice of parameters?
- How do the authors address the issue of spurious correlations in their experiments, and what steps are taken to ensure that the results are not influenced by these correlations?
- Could the authors discuss the potential societal impacts of their research and how these impacts might be mitigated or addressed?
- In the context of the findings, what are the practical implications for real-world applications of dimensionality reduction in multimodal data analysis?
