 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel online learning framework for non-stationary data, leveraging a Kalman filter to model the dynamics of parameter drift in neural networks. The methodology allows for efficient online inference and adaptation to non-stationary data streams, with a focus on continual learning applications. The framework is demonstrated through experiments on benchmarks such as CIFAR-100 and CLOC, showing improved performance over existing methods. Despite the promising approach, concerns were raised about the practicality of the method, particularly its computational complexity and the need for further validation on more diverse datasets.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, with clear and concise explanations of the methodology and the experiments.
- The proposed method is simple, elegant, and effective, with a focus on practical implementation in online learning scenarios.
- The method is theoretically grounded, with a strong theoretical foundation that supports its application in continual learning.
- The experiments are well-designed and demonstrate the method's effectiveness in both stationary and non-stationary settings, showing improvements over existing methods.
- The paper provides a detailed derivation of the Kalman filter for classification and online updates of the forgetting coefficient, which is a novel approach in the field.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed discussion on the computational complexity of the proposed method, particularly the number of parameters and the computational cost of each iteration.
- There is a lack of comparison with other relevant methods, such as those that use different types of neural networks or different approaches to handling non-stationary data.
- The paper does not provide sufficient theoretical justification for the choice of the Kalman filter over other methods, and the method's performance in more complex or diverse settings is not explored.
- The experiments are limited to a few datasets, which may not adequately represent the generalizability of the method.
- The method's sensitivity to hyperparameters and the choice of neural network architecture is not thoroughly investigated, which could affect its practical applicability.
- The paper does not address the issue of catastrophic forgetting, which is a critical challenge in continual learning.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors provide a detailed discussion on the computational complexity of the proposed method, including the number of parameters and the computational cost per iteration?
- How does the method perform in more complex or diverse settings, such as those with more classes or more significant non-stationarity?
- Could the authors provide a theoretical analysis of the method's performance in terms of convergence rates or generalization bounds?
- How does the method compare to other methods that use different neural network architectures or different approaches to handling non-stationary data?
- Can the authors provide more details on the choice of hyperparameters and their impact on the method's performance?
- How does the method handle catastrophic forgetting, and what steps have been taken to mitigate this issue?
