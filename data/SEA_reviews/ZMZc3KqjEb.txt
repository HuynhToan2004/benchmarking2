 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel variational bound for multi-modal data, aiming to improve upon existing bounds by integrating permutation-invariant neural networks for encoding different modalities. The proposed bound, which can be optimized using standard Monte Carlo techniques, is intended to enhance the expressiveness of multi-modal encoding distributions, potentially leading to better performance in various generative modeling tasks. The authors evaluate their approach using both synthetic and real-world datasets, comparing it to existing methods such as PoE and MoE. The paper also discusses the theoretical underpinnings of the bound and its implications for the generative modeling community.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, with a clear and structured presentation that makes it easy to follow.
- The proposed variational bound is novel and provides a tighter approximation of the log-likelihood compared to existing bounds.
- The paper introduces a novel multi-modal aggregation scheme that is more flexible than previous approaches, enhancing the expressiveness of multi-modal encoding distributions.
- The theoretical analysis is thorough, providing a solid foundation for the proposed methods.
- The paper is well-motivated, with clear explanations of the contributions and their implications for the generative modeling community.
- The experiments demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed methods, showing improvements over existing approaches in some scenarios.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a comprehensive comparison with state-of-the-art methods in the field, particularly in terms of performance and computational efficiency.
- The experimental results are not convincingly superior to existing methods, with some results showing only marginal improvements or even worse performance.
- The paper does not sufficiently discuss the limitations of the proposed methods, which could help in understanding the practical applicability and scope of the findings.
- The paper could benefit from a more detailed discussion on the computational complexity and scalability of the proposed methods, especially in terms of the number of modalities.
- The paper does not sufficiently address the practical implications of the theoretical results, particularly how they translate into practical improvements in generative modeling tasks.
- There are several typographical and formatting errors that need to be corrected for clarity and professional presentation.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors provide a more detailed comparison with state-of-the-art methods, including both quantitative and qualitative results?
- How does the proposed method perform in terms of computational efficiency and scalability, particularly as the number of modalities increases?
- Can the authors provide more detailed insights into the practical implications of the theoretical results, particularly how they translate into improvements in generative modeling tasks?
- How does the proposed method handle scenarios where the data modalities are not equally important or where the data distribution is imbalanced across modalities?
- Could the authors clarify the experimental setup and results, particularly in Table 1 and other sections where the results are not clearly explained?
- How does the proposed method compare to other recent approaches in multi-modal data analysis, such as those using contrastive objectives or other multi-modal variational bounds?
