 **Summary:**
The paper introduces the LogoRA framework for unsupervised domain adaptation (UDA) in time series data, leveraging a two-branch encoder to extract both local and global features. The framework incorporates a fusion module that aligns these features across different domains, utilizing strategies such as invariant feature learning and adversarial training. The methodology is evaluated on four datasets, showing improvements over baseline methods. However, concerns are raised regarding the novelty of the approach, as it closely resembles existing methods like LogoDA, and the clarity of the experimental setup, particularly the use of different evaluation metrics and the absence of error bars in results.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, easy to follow, and provides clear explanations of the proposed method.
- The proposed method, LogoRA, is effective in handling unsupervised domain adaptation (UDA) of time series data, with a novel network architecture that combines a multi-scale local encoder and a global encoder.
- The use of a fusion module to integrate local and global representations is innovative and helps in aligning features across different domains.
- The methodology is supported by extensive experiments and ablation studies, which demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed approach.
- The paper includes visualizations of the attention weights and T-SNE plots of learned representations, which aid in understanding the model's behavior and the effectiveness of the learned representations.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a clear differentiation from existing methods, particularly LogoDA, which also employs similar techniques. The novelty of the proposed method is not convincingly demonstrated.
- The experimental setup is not clearly explained, particularly the use of different evaluation metrics and the absence of error bars in results.
- There is a lack of discussion on the limitations of the proposed method, which could help in understanding its applicability and potential improvements.
- The paper does not provide sufficient details on the computational complexity or inference time of the proposed method, which could be crucial for practical deployment.
- The paper does not include comparisons with other relevant methods, such as CLUDA and RAINCOAT, which could have provided a more comprehensive evaluation of the proposed method.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors clarify the differences between LogoRA and LogoDA, particularly in terms of novelty and methodological contributions?
- How does the proposed method handle different types of time series data, such as those with varying data densities or different sampling rates?
- Can the authors provide more details on the computational complexity and inference time of the proposed method, especially in comparison to other state-of-the-art methods?
- How does the proposed method perform on other types of time series data, such as those with different modalities or from different domains?
- Could the authors provide a more detailed explanation of the experimental setup, particularly the use of different evaluation metrics and the absence of error bars in results?
- How does the proposed method compare to other relevant methods, such as CLUDA and RAINCOAT, in terms of performance and computational efficiency?
