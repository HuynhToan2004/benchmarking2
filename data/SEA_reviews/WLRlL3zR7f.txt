 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel benchmark dataset for predicting the frequency response of vibrating plates, focusing on a dataset of 12,000 plate geometries with associated numerical solutions. It proposes a new evaluation metric, the Earth Mover Distance (EMD), and evaluates various models including FQO-RN18, FQO-ViT, and FQO-UNet, which outperform traditional methods like DeepONets and Fourier Neural Operators. The paper also explores the use of a query-based operator network (FQ-Operator) to predict frequency response functions, showcasing its superiority over existing methods. Despite its innovative approach and the creation of a significant dataset, the paper's novelty and the generalizability of its findings are questioned, as the method's applicability to more complex structures and broader frequency ranges remains untested.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel benchmark dataset for frequency response prediction of vibrating plates, which is a significant contribution to the field.
- It proposes a new evaluation metric, Earth Mover Distance (EMD), which is a useful measure for evaluating the performance of models in this context.
- The paper is well-written, making it easy to follow and understand the methodology and results.
- The creation of a benchmark dataset for vibroacoustic frequency response prediction from variable geometries is a valuable resource for the community.
- The paper explores the use of a query-based operator network (FQ-Operator) to predict frequency response functions, which outperforms existing methods on the proposed dataset.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a clear explanation of the significance and novelty of the benchmark dataset, particularly in terms of its generalizability to more complex structures and broader frequency ranges.
- There is a lack of discussion on the generalizability of the proposed method to more complex structures beyond plates.
- The paper does not adequately address the limitations of the method, such as its applicability to more complex geometries and its generalization to different types of structures.
- The novelty of the proposed method is questioned, as it appears to be a straightforward application of existing techniques without significant innovation.
- The paper does not provide a comprehensive comparison with other state-of-the-art methods, which could help in validating the effectiveness of the proposed approach.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors provide more details on the generalizability of the proposed method to more complex structures and broader frequency ranges?
- How does the proposed method compare to other state-of-the-art methods in terms of performance and efficiency?
- Could the authors discuss the limitations of the proposed method and suggest potential directions for future research to address these limitations?
- How does the proposed method handle the variation in plate geometry and material properties, and what are the implications of these variations on the model's predictions?
- Could the authors provide more details on the training and testing data, including the number of samples and their distribution across different frequencies?
- What are the specific advantages of using the proposed FQ-Operator model over other existing models, such as DeepONet and Fourier Neural Operators?
