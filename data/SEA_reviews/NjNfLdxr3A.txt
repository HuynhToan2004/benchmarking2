 **Summary:**
The paper introduces ELoRA, a novel parameter-efficient fine-tuning method for large language models (LLMs), which significantly reduces the number of trainable parameters compared to existing methods like LoRA. ELoRA uses a pair of randomly initialized low-rank matrices shared across all layers and introduces trainable scaling vectors. This approach allows for efficient storage and reduces memory requirements, which is crucial for deploying numerous per-user or per-task adapted models. The method was evaluated across various benchmarks, including GLUE, E2E, instruction-tuning, and image classification tasks, showing competitive performance with fewer parameters. Despite its promising results, concerns were raised about the novelty and the clarity of the method's contributions, as it appears to be a simple combination of existing techniques without significant innovation.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel parameter-efficient fine-tuning method with no additional inference time cost, which is significant for reducing the computational burden in large language models.
- The methodology is well-explained, with clear and well-organized presentation, making it accessible and understandable to readers.
- The paper provides a comprehensive evaluation of the proposed method across various benchmarks, demonstrating its effectiveness and efficiency.
- The proposed ELoRA method significantly reduces the number of trainable parameters compared to LoRA, which is a significant improvement in parameter efficiency.
- The paper is well-written, making it easy to follow, and includes a detailed ablation study that helps in understanding the individual components of the method and their effects on performance.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a thorough analysis of the computational cost of the proposed method, particularly in terms of training time and memory usage.
- There is a lack of novelty in the method as it seems to be a simple combination of existing techniques like LoRA and random matrices, without significant innovation.
- The paper does not sufficiently discuss the limitations of the proposed method, which could affect its practical applicability and scalability.
- The paper does not compare the proposed method with the latest parameter-efficient fine-tuning methods like FlashLoRA, which might have better performance or efficiency.
- The paper does not provide a detailed discussion on how the method performs in different scenarios or with different types of models, which could affect its generalizability.
- The paper does not sufficiently discuss the implications of the method in terms of data efficiency and its scalability to larger models, which are critical for practical deployment.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors provide a detailed analysis of the computational cost of the proposed method, including training time and memory usage?
- How does the method perform in terms of data efficiency, and what are the implications for scalability to larger models?
- Can the authors discuss the limitations of the proposed method and how these might affect its practical applicability and scalability?
- Could the authors provide a comparison with the latest parameter-efficient fine-tuning methods like FlashLoRA, and discuss the implications of using different initialization strategies for the scaling vectors?
- How does the method perform in different scenarios, such as with different types of models or in different domains, and what are the implications of these variations on the method's performance?
