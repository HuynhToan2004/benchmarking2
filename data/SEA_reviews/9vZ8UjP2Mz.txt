 **Summary:**
The paper investigates the generalization ability of AID-based bi-level optimization algorithms, focusing on the stability and convergence properties of these methods. It presents a stability analysis under mild conditions and discusses the convergence of AID-based methods using a step size chosen to ensure stability. The paper also provides a theoretical framework for understanding the behavior of multi-level variables in bi-level optimization, which is crucial for understanding the generalization capabilities of these methods. The authors conduct an ablation study to assess the performance of these methods on real-world tasks, demonstrating the effectiveness of AID-based bi-level optimization in practical scenarios.

**Strengths:**
- The paper addresses the stability of AID-based bi-level optimization algorithms, which is an important and relevant topic for machine learning applications.
- The stability analysis of AID-based bi-level optimization algorithms under mild conditions is a significant contribution, providing insights into the behavior of multi-level variables in bi-level optimization.
- The paper is well-written and easy to follow, with clear explanations of the main results and theoretical contributions.
- The authors provide a convergence analysis for AID-based bi-level optimization algorithms, which helps in understanding the trade-offs between optimization error and generalization gaps.
- The study reveals the uniform stability of AID-based methods, which is analogous to those found in nonconvex single-level optimization and ITD-based bi-level methods, enhancing the reliability of these methods.
- The paper includes an ablation study of the parameters, which assesses the performance of these methods on real-world tasks, demonstrating the effectiveness of AID-based bi-level optimization in practical scenarios.
