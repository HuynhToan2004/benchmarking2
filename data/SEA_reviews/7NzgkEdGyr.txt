 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel parameter-efficient fine-tuning method called Orthogonal Butterfly (BOFT), which leverages a butterfly structure to achieve dense orthogonal parameterization in Orthogonal Finetuning (OFT). BOFT aims to reduce the number of trainable parameters while maintaining expressivity and training stability. It is evaluated across various tasks such as natural language understanding, mathematical question answering, and image segmentation, showing competitive results against existing methods. The approach is supported by theoretical insights and extensive experimental validation, demonstrating the method's effectiveness in parameter efficiency and generalization ability.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, making it easy to follow, and the presentation is clear and logical.
- The proposed method, BOFT, is a significant advancement in parameter-efficient fine-tuning (PEFT), showing promising results in various tasks such as natural language understanding, mathematical question answering, and image segmentation.
- The introduction of butterfly factorization into the orthogonal fine-tuning framework is innovative and provides a new perspective on parameter efficiency.
- The experimental results are strong, showing that BOFT outperforms existing methods in terms of parameter efficiency and generalization ability.
- The paper includes thorough theoretical insights and discussions, which are helpful in understanding the method's underlying principles and its advantages over existing methods like OFT.
- The paper is well-organized, with a clear motivation for the research and a logical structure that guides the reader through the methodology and results.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a comprehensive comparison with other PEFT methods, particularly in terms of parameter efficiency and generalization ability.
- The experimental setup could be improved by including more diverse datasets and different model architectures to better demonstrate the method's effectiveness across various scenarios.
- The paper could benefit from a more detailed discussion on the limitations of the proposed method and potential directions for future research.
- There is a need for a more rigorous comparison with other methods like LoRA, especially in terms of parameter efficiency and generalization ability.
- The paper could benefit from a clearer explanation of the mathematical underpinnings of the method, particularly the use of orthogonal matrices and the butterfly factorization.
