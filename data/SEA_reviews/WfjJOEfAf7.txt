 **Summary:**
The paper introduces Matrix Variational Masked Auto-Encoder (M-MAE), a novel self-supervised learning method that leverages matrix information theory to enhance existing techniques like masked image modeling. M-MAE integrates matrix variational auto-encoder with matrix mutual information and joint entropy to improve the effectiveness of existing methods. The authors provide a theoretical framework for analyzing the relationships between various self-supervised learning methods, including contrastive learning and feature decorrelation, and demonstrate the practical utility of these theoretical insights through empirical evaluations. The proposed method shows promising results in terms of performance improvements, although the paper is criticized for its limited novelty and the lack of comprehensive comparisons with other methods.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-organized and clearly written, making it accessible and easy to understand.
- The use of matrix information theory to enhance self-supervised learning (SSL) methods is innovative and provides a novel approach to understanding and improving SSL.
- The proposed method, Matrix Variational Masked Auto-Encoder (M-MAE), shows practical utility by improving the performance of existing methods in both linear probing and fine-tuning tasks.
- The theoretical framework presented in the paper offers a novel perspective on the relationships between various SSL methods, including contrastive learning and feature decorrelation, which could be beneficial for future research in this area.
- The paper includes empirical evaluations that demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed method, showing improvements over existing methods.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a thorough comparison with other methods, particularly in terms of performance metrics and computational efficiency.
- The novelty of the proposed method is questionable as it primarily extends existing methods by incorporating matrix information theory, which may not be seen as a significant advancement.
- The theoretical insights provided by the paper are not sufficiently novel or surprising, as they largely build upon existing works in the field.
- The paper's experimental setup and results are not convincingly superior to existing methods, with marginal improvements reported.
- The paper's claims about the superiority of matrix mutual information and joint entropy over traditional information-theoretic measures are not adequately supported by empirical evidence.
- There is a lack of discussion on the limitations of the proposed method and the potential societal impacts of the research.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors clarify the specific advantages of using matrix mutual information and joint entropy over traditional information-theoretic measures in their methodology?
- How does the proposed method compare in terms of computational efficiency and resource requirements with existing methods?
- Can the authors provide more detailed comparisons with other methods, including both qualitative and quantitative results?
- How does the proposed method perform on other datasets or tasks beyond image classification, and what are the implications of these findings?
- Could the authors elaborate on the theoretical insights provided by the paper and how they advance the understanding of SSL methods?
- In light of the criticisms regarding the novelty and practical utility of the proposed method, how do the authors justify the claimed improvements and the relevance of their work to the field?
