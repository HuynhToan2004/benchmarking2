 **Summary:**
The paper explores the use of random gradient guesses in neural network optimization, focusing on methods that estimate gradients without backpropagation. The authors propose several techniques, including using the transpose of the weight matrix to constrain the guess space and utilizing activation subspaces to enhance the accuracy of gradient estimations. They demonstrate that these methods can achieve high cosine similarity to true gradients and improve optimization performance, albeit with significant computational overhead. The paper also discusses the limitations of these methods, such as their inability to scale to larger models and the potential for bias in the gradient estimates.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, making it easy to follow and understand the proposed methods.
- The proposed methods are innovative, particularly in their approach to constraining the guess space using the transpose of the weight matrix and utilizing activation subspaces.
- The paper provides a comprehensive evaluation of the proposed methods, comparing them to existing approaches and demonstrating their effectiveness in terms of cosine similarity to true gradients and optimization performance.
- The paper includes a detailed analysis of the bias in the gradient estimates and the impact of this bias on the optimization process.
- The experimental results are convincing, showing that the proposed methods can achieve high cosine similarity to true gradients and improve optimization performance.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed discussion on the computational cost of the proposed methods, which is crucial for understanding their practical applicability.
- The method of using the transpose of the weight matrix to constrain the guess space is not novel and has been previously discussed in the literature.
- The paper does not adequately address the limitations of the proposed methods, particularly the bias in the gradient estimates and the inability to scale to larger models.
- There are some inconsistencies and potential errors in the mathematical formulations, which could affect the validity of the results.
- The paper could benefit from a more thorough comparison with existing methods, including those that use different types of activation functions and different architectures.
