 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel method for Active Speaker Detection (ASD) in egocentric videos using a multimodal transformer architecture. The proposed framework models both visible and invisible speakers, enhancing performance by integrating a speech decoder and incorporating face location information. The model is evaluated on the Ego4D dataset, showing state-of-the-art results. The paper also includes an ablation study to analyze the contributions of different components and settings. However, it lacks a detailed discussion on the limitations and societal impacts of the proposed method.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, making it easy to follow and understand the methodology and results.
- The proposed method for active speaker detection in egocentric videos is innovative, integrating a speech decoder and utilizing face location information effectively.
- The paper provides a detailed ablation study, which helps in understanding the impact of various components and settings on the performance of the model.
- The results, as shown in Table 1, are impressive, demonstrating the effectiveness of the proposed method.
- The use of a transformer encoder-decoder for modeling each individual prediction in egocentric ASD as a token is a novel approach.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed discussion on the limitations and potential negative societal impacts of the proposed method, which is crucial for a comprehensive understanding of the technology.
- There is a lack of comparison with state-of-the-art methods, which could help in validating the proposed method's effectiveness.
- The paper does not provide sufficient details on the training and inference time, which are essential for assessing the practicality of the proposed method.
- The paper does not adequately address the generalizability of the proposed method to other datasets or scenarios, which could limit its applicability.
- The paper does not include a discussion on the failure cases of the proposed method, which is necessary for understanding its limitations.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors provide more details on the training and inference time of the proposed method?
- How does the proposed method perform on other datasets or scenarios beyond those tested?
- Could the authors provide more details on the training data and how it was prepared, including the number of frames used and the pre-trained models employed?
- How does the proposed method handle different scenarios such as noisy or low-quality audio and video data?
- What are the failure cases of the proposed method, and how do these impact the overall performance and applicability of the method?
- How does the proposed method compare with other state-of-the-art methods, especially in terms of computational efficiency and accuracy?
