 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel method for continual learning using a single prompt, which accumulates knowledge over time by leveraging contrastive learning. The approach involves learning a prompt that guides the model's behavior towards optimal performance across all tasks, without requiring a prompt pool or task identification during inference. This method is evaluated on several datasets, showing superior performance compared to existing methods. The paper also includes a comprehensive analysis of the feature space and a comparison with upper bounds, demonstrating the method's effectiveness in preserving knowledge and mitigating catastrophic forgetting.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel approach to continual learning using a single prompt, which is both innovative and effective.
- The methodology is well-articulated, with clear explanations of the method's components and their roles in the overall process.
- The paper is well-written, making it easy to follow and understand, and includes comprehensive experimental results that demonstrate the method's effectiveness.
- The paper provides a thorough analysis of the feature space and its alignment, which is crucial for understanding the method's impact on knowledge preservation.
- The paper is original in its approach to continual learning, offering a new perspective on how to accumulate knowledge over time.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed discussion on the computational cost of the method, which is crucial for practical implementation.
- There is a lack of clarity regarding the method's applicability to other types of continual learning settings beyond class-incremental learning.
- The paper does not sufficiently discuss the limitations of the proposed method, which could include its reliance on specific model architectures or the potential for increased computational complexity.
- Some figures and explanations in the paper could be improved for clarity and consistency.
- The paper could benefit from a more detailed comparison with other state-of-the-art methods, particularly in terms of computational efficiency and performance on different datasets.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors clarify the computational cost of the proposed method, including the number of parameters and inference time?
- How does the proposed method perform in other types of continual learning settings, such as task-free or sequence-free continual learning?
- Can the authors provide more details on the training process, such as how the model is initialized and the specifics of the training schedule?
- How does the method handle different types of prompts, such as text or image prompts?
- Could the authors elaborate on the potential limitations of the proposed method and suggest areas for future research?
- Can the authors provide more details on the experimental setup, including the datasets used and the specifics of the training and evaluation procedures?
