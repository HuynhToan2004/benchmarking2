 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel approach to forward learning (FL) by integrating contrastive learning with adaptive label embeddings, aiming to improve the similarity between local features and label embeddings. This approach, termed Dictionary Contrastive Forward Learning (DC-FL), is designed to enhance performance and reduce computational complexity compared to traditional methods like backpropagation (BP). The methodology involves the dynamic update of label embeddings during training, which are used to compare with local features from intermediate layers, thus avoiding the need for backward error propagation. The paper provides a thorough experimental evaluation, demonstrating that DC-FL outperforms other FL techniques and approaches the performance of BP, while maintaining computational efficiency.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, clear, and easy to follow, with detailed explanations of the methodology and experiments.
- The motivation for the research is clear, focusing on the challenges of backpropagation (BP) and the need for more efficient and biologically plausible methods.
- The introduction of adaptive label embeddings and the use of contrastive learning objectives in the FL setting are innovative and well-explained.
- The paper provides a comprehensive experimental evaluation, demonstrating the effectiveness of the proposed method across various datasets and architectures.
- The analysis of the method's performance and the comparison with existing methods are thorough and convincing, showing the advantages of the proposed approach.
- The paper includes visualizations and additional experiments in the appendix, which help in understanding the method's behavior and impact.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper could benefit from more detailed explanations and justifications for certain technical aspects, such as the use of adaptive label embeddings and the choice of parameters like K in the experiments.
- There is a lack of discussion on the computational complexity of the proposed method compared to other FL methods, which might be crucial for practical applications.
- The paper primarily focuses on image classification tasks, which might limit the generalizability of the findings to other types of tasks or datasets.
- The paper does not adequately address the biological plausibility of the proposed method, which could be a significant concern for some readers.
- There is a need for more detailed comparisons with other FL methods, especially those that use similar approaches, to establish the superiority of the proposed method.
- The paper could benefit from more comprehensive theoretical analysis and discussion on the limitations and potential negative societal impacts of the proposed method.

**Questions:**

- How does the proposed method perform when applied to other types of tasks or datasets, such as natural language processing or generative models?
- Can you clarify the role and impact of adaptive label embeddings in the performance of the method?
- How does the proposed method compare in terms of biological plausibility with other FL methods?
- Could you provide more details on the choice of parameters, particularly K, in your experiments and how it affects the performance of the method?
- How does the method handle the update of label embeddings, and what are the implications for the training process?
