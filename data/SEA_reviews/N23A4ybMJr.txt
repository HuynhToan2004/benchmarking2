 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel training strategy for high-resolution vision transformers, focusing on masking most of the high-resolution inputs during training and using N random windows. This approach allows the model to learn local and global interactions between tokens, enabling efficient training and inference. The method is demonstrated to be effective on tasks like semantic segmentation and optical flow estimation, showing significant improvements in training speed and memory usage. The authors also discuss the importance of specific masking configurations and the use of relative positional embeddings for high-resolution generalization.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, making it easy to follow and understand the methodology.
- The proposed training strategy is simple yet effective, with a significant improvement in training speed and memory usage.
- The paper provides comprehensive experimental results and ablation studies, demonstrating the effectiveness of the proposed method.
- The methodology is straightforward and can be easily implemented, which is beneficial for practical applications.
- The paper addresses a significant issue in the field by focusing on the training of high-resolution vision transformers, which is a critical area for further research.
- The use of relative positional embeddings and the temperature factor in the softmax of the attention are highlighted as innovative contributions to the field.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed comparison with state-of-the-art methods, which could have strengthened the argument for the proposed method's effectiveness.
- The experimental setup and results are not convincingly superior to existing methods, especially when compared to hierarchical architectures like DPT.
- The paper does not sufficiently discuss the limitations of the proposed method, which could affect its practical applicability in real-world scenarios.
- The method's effectiveness in high-resolution image classification tasks is not explored, which could have provided a more comprehensive evaluation of the method's capabilities.
- The paper could benefit from more detailed explanations and justifications for certain methodological choices, such as the use of specific masking configurations and the temperature factor in the softmax of the attention.
- There is a lack of discussion on the societal impact of the proposed method, which is an essential consideration in the field of AI research.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors provide more detailed comparisons with state-of-the-art methods to strengthen the argument for the proposed method's effectiveness?
- How does the proposed method compare to other hierarchical architectures in terms of training speed and memory usage?
- Can the authors provide more insights into the limitations of the proposed method and discuss potential strategies for addressing these limitations?
- How does the proposed method perform in high-resolution image classification tasks? Could the authors consider including this task in future evaluations?
- Could the authors provide more detailed justifications for the methodological choices made in the paper, particularly the use of specific masking configurations and the temperature factor in the softmax of the attention?
- How does the proposed method handle the societal impact of AI, especially in terms of fairness and non-discrimination?
