 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel decentralized decoupled training mechanism for federated long-tailed learning, which leverages abundant local data to rebalance classifiers during local training. The methodology includes a two-stream classifier architecture to address the contradictory optimization goals and a mixed gradient re-balancing mechanism to enhance classifier re-balancing. The paper also proposes a Lagrange Multiplier method to handle the optimization difficulty caused by local classifier re-balancing. Extensive experiments demonstrate that the proposed method outperforms existing state-of-the-art methods in various settings, highlighting its effectiveness and practical applicability in real-world scenarios.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, with clear explanations of the proposed method and its components, making it easy to follow.
- The motivation behind the paper is clear, focusing on the re-balancing of classifiers in federated learning (FL) settings, which is a significant issue in FL.
- The proposed method is novel, utilizing a two-stream classifier architecture to address the contradictory optimization goals and a mixed gradient re-balancing mechanism to enhance classifier re-balancing.
- The paper includes extensive experiments that demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed method, showing significant improvements over existing state-of-the-art methods in various settings.
- The paper addresses a critical and challenging problem in federated learning, which is the long-tailed distribution, and provides a method that is effective in handling this issue.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a clear discussion on the computational cost of the proposed method, which is crucial for understanding its practical applicability.
- The paper does not provide a detailed analysis of the impact of different hyperparameters on the performance of the proposed method, which could help in optimizing its performance.
- The paper does not adequately compare the proposed method with other existing methods in terms of computational efficiency, which is necessary for understanding its practical advantages.
- The paper does not address the potential negative societal impacts of the proposed method, which is an important consideration in the context of federated learning.
- The paper could benefit from a more detailed discussion on the scalability of the proposed method, especially in large-scale federated learning settings.
- The paper does not provide a detailed discussion on the potential limitations of the proposed method, which could help in understanding its scope and applicability.
