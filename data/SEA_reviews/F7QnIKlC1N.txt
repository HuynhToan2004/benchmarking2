 **Summary:**
The paper introduces GTMGC, a novel method using a Graph Transformer to predict the ground-state conformation of molecules from their 2D topological architecture, aiming to outperform existing methods like RDKit and GPS. It employs a novel self-attention mechanism called Molecule Structural Residual Self-Attention (MSRSA) to enhance molecular structure modeling. The methodology is validated on the Molecule3D benchmark dataset and the QM9 dataset, showing promising results. However, the paper is criticized for lacking detailed comparisons with state-of-the-art methods and for insufficient experimental validation, particularly in terms of generalizability and robustness across different datasets and molecular structures.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, clear, and easy to follow, with a clear introduction to the problem and the proposed solution.
- The proposed method, GTMGC, is novel and innovative, particularly in its use of a graph transformer for predicting the ground-state conformation of molecules.
- The paper includes comprehensive experimental results that demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed method, outperforming existing methods such as RDKit and GPS.
- The ablation study is well-designed, providing a detailed analysis of the performance of the model and its components.
- The proposed MSRSA module is simple yet effective, contributing to the overall performance of the model.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks detailed comparisons with state-of-the-art methods, which could help in understanding the relative performance of the proposed method.
- The experimental setup and results are not sufficiently robust, with concerns about the generalizability and applicability of the method to different molecular structures and datasets.
- The paper could benefit from more comprehensive experimental validation, including more datasets and a broader range of molecular structures to ensure the robustness and applicability of the proposed method.
- The paper does not provide a detailed discussion on the limitations and potential negative societal impacts of the proposed method.
- There are several typographical and formatting errors that need to be corrected for clarity and professionalism.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors provide a more detailed comparison with state-of-the-art methods to better understand the relative performance of the proposed method?
- How does the proposed method perform on different types of molecules, such as those with aromatic rings or complex structures like proteins?
- Could the authors provide more details on the experimental setup, including the specifics of the datasets used and the conditions under which the experiments were conducted?
- How does the MSRSA module contribute to the overall performance of the model, and what are the specific improvements it provides over existing methods?
- Could the authors discuss the potential limitations and negative societal impacts of the proposed method, and how these might be addressed or mitigated?
- Could the authors clarify the role and impact of the learnable parameters in the MSRSA module, and how these parameters are trained and optimized?
