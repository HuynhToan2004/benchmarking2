 **Summary:**
The paper addresses the challenge of fairness in machine learning when sensitive attributes are missing, proposing a method to infer these attributes and optimize fairness-accuracy trade-offs. The authors introduce an uncertainty-aware attribute classifier to predict sensitive attributes and use these predictions to enforce fairness constraints on samples with the least uncertainty. The method is evaluated across multiple datasets, showing that it can achieve better fairness-accuracy trade-offs compared to existing methods. However, concerns were raised about the novelty of the approach, the clarity of the methodological description, and the generalizability of the findings.

**Strengths:**
- The paper addresses an important and relevant problem, focusing on fairness in machine learning models when sensitive attributes are missing, which is a common issue in real-world applications.
- The proposed method is well-motivated, theoretically grounded, and supported by empirical results, showing that it can achieve better fairness-accuracy trade-offs compared to existing methods.
- The paper is well-written, making it accessible and easy to follow, with clear explanations of the methodology and results.
- The use of uncertainty in sensitive attribute prediction to guide fairness constraints is a novel approach that adds depth to the discussion on fairness in machine learning.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks clarity in some sections, particularly in the methodology, which could benefit from more detailed explanations and examples to aid understanding.
- There are concerns about the novelty of the approach, as similar methods have been previously explored, and the paper does not sufficiently distinguish its contributions from existing literature.
- The empirical evaluation could be improved by including more datasets and fairness metrics, and by comparing the proposed method with a broader range of baselines to demonstrate its effectiveness more convincingly.
- The paper's assumptions and limitations are not adequately discussed, which could affect the generalizability and applicability of the findings.
- The paper could benefit from a more thorough discussion on the ethical implications of using proxy attributes for sensitive attributes, especially in terms of privacy and potential biases introduced by these proxies.

**Questions:**
- Could you provide more detailed examples and clarifications in the methodology section to help readers better understand the proposed method?
- How does the proposed method compare to other state-of-the-art methods in terms of fairness-accuracy trade-offs?
- Could you discuss the potential societal implications of using proxy attributes for sensitive attributes, particularly in terms of privacy and potential biases introduced by these proxies?
- How does the method perform when the sensitive attributes are binary, and how does it handle scenarios where the sensitive attributes are not binary?
- Could you elaborate on the decision-making process for selecting the threshold for uncertainty in the sensitive attribute prediction?
- How does the method perform when the sensitive attributes are missing for a significant portion of the data, and what are the implications for fairness and accuracy in such scenarios?
