 **Summary:**
The paper explores the application of deep reinforcement learning (RL) to the periodic review inventory control problem, focusing on the estimation of inventory arrival dynamics. It introduces a generative model, Gen-QOT, which predicts the joint probability distribution of inventory arrivals over time, allowing for the estimation of the full joint probability distribution of inventory arrivals. The authors demonstrate the effectiveness of the Gen-QOT model through simulations and real-world experiments, comparing it to a baseline lead-time model. The paper also discusses the practical implications of the model in real-world scenarios, showcasing its potential to enhance inventory management and supply chain optimization.

**Strengths:**
- The paper addresses an important and relevant problem in the field of inventory control, which is crucial for supply chain optimization.
- The proposed generative model, Gen-QOT, is innovative and could be beneficial for the inventory management community, potentially improving upon existing models.
- The paper is well-written, clear, and easy to follow, making it accessible to a broad audience.
- The authors demonstrate the effectiveness of the Gen-QOT model through simulations and real-world experiments, showing improvements over a baseline lead-time model.
- The paper includes a real-world A/B test, which adds credibility to the findings and shows the practical applicability of the model.
- The inclusion of a calibration metric to assess the quality of the generated samples is a novel approach that could be useful in other contexts.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a clear definition and explanation of the key concept "accurate forecast" (Assumption 3.1), which is crucial for the validity of the results.
- The paper does not sufficiently distinguish its contributions from existing literature, particularly in relation to the work by Madeka et al. (2022).
- The paper does not discuss the limitations of the Gen-QOT model, which could affect its generalizability or applicability in different scenarios.
- The paper does not provide sufficient details on the implementation and training of the Gen-QOT model, which could hinder reproducibility and further research.
- The paper does not discuss the computational complexity of the model, which could be a significant concern for practical applications.
- The paper does not include a detailed discussion on the sensitivity of the model to different parameters or hyperparameters, which could affect its performance and applicability.

**Questions:**
- Could you clarify the definition and implications of "accurate forecast" as stated in Assumption 3.1?
- How does the Gen-QOT model differ from the approach by Madeka et al. (2022), particularly in terms of model complexity and performance?
- What are the limitations of the Gen-QOT model, and under what conditions might it not be applicable or effective?
- How does the model handle different scenarios where the supply and fill rates might not be accurately forecasted?
- Can you provide more details on the implementation and training of the Gen-QOT model, including the architecture, training data, and hyperparameters used?
- How sensitive is the model to different parameters or hyperparameters, and what are the potential impacts on performance and applicability?
- Could you discuss the computational complexity of the model and how it scales with the size of the inventory system or the complexity of the arrival dynamics?
