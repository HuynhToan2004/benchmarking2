 **Summary:**
The paper introduces AutoFD, a JAX extension for automatic differentiation of higher-order functions, including functionals and operators. It proposes a set of primitive operators for functional differentiation and demonstrates their application in variational problems, density functional theory, and neural operator learning. The authors provide a theoretical framework for JVP and transpose rules, extending JAX's capabilities to handle functional derivatives. The paper also discusses the implementation of these rules and their practical implications, such as the computational efficiency of chain rules and the handling of non-analytical integrals. Despite the theoretical advancements, the paper is criticized for its limited experimental scope and lack of comprehensive comparative analysis with existing methods.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces AutoFD, an extension of JAX for automatic differentiation of higher-order functions, which is a significant advancement in the field.
- It provides a theoretical framework for JVP and transpose rules, which is crucial for the implementation of functional derivatives.
- The paper is well-written, making it accessible to a broad audience, and includes a clear explanation of the theoretical foundations and practical applications.
- The authors have demonstrated the application of AutoFD in variational problems, density functional theory, and neural operator learning, showcasing its versatility and potential impact.
- The paper includes a detailed discussion on the implementation of the AutoFD system, which is a valuable contribution for practitioners seeking to apply these methods in their work.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a comprehensive experimental validation, with only a few examples used to demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed method.
- There is a notable absence of comparative analysis with existing methods, which could help in understanding the advantages and limitations of AutoFD.
- The paper does not sufficiently address the computational efficiency of the chain rule, which is a critical aspect of functional differentiation.
- The application of AutoFD in real-world problems is not thoroughly explored, and the paper does not provide a detailed analysis of its practical implications, such as scalability and computational cost.
- There is a lack of discussion on the limitations of the AutoFD system, which could help in setting realistic expectations for its application.
