 **Summary:**
The paper introduces CoRe-GD, a novel framework for graph visualization that utilizes a hierarchical coarsening process to optimize stress in graph layouts. The approach leverages a positional rewiring technique and a hierarchical graph coarsening strategy to enhance communication between nodes, even when they are far apart in the original graph. The method is evaluated against several datasets and demonstrates competitive performance with state-of-the-art methods while maintaining scalability. The paper is well-written, with clear explanations and extensive experimental evaluations, although it lacks a detailed discussion on the limitations and potential negative societal impacts of the proposed method.

- The method is scalable, which is crucial for handling large graphs, and achieves state-of-the-art performance while remaining scalable.
