 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel framework for offline multi-task reinforcement learning (RL) called Contextual Goal-Oriented (CGO) problems. This framework leverages datasets consisting of dynamics and context-goal pairs to construct an augmented Markov Decision Process (MDP), aiming to solve CGO problems using a simple data sharing technique. Theoretical analysis and empirical evaluations demonstrate that the proposed method can learn near-optimal context-conditioned policies, outperforming offline RL baselines in simulated environments. The approach is designed to handle contexts that map to different goal sets, and the authors claim that it can solve CGO problems without the need for negative data, which is a common requirement in many offline RL methods.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, with clear and intuitive explanations, making it accessible to readers.
- The problem of contextual goal-oriented (CGO) learning is addressed, which is an important and relevant area in the field of reinforcement learning.
- The proposed method is simple yet effective, and the experimental results are promising, showing that it outperforms offline RL baselines in simulated environments.
- The paper provides a theoretical analysis that is both solid and intuitive, enhancing the understanding of the proposed method.
- The approach of using a fictitious action and constructing an augmented MDP to address the inconsistency between dynamics and context-goal datasets is innovative and well-explained.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed discussion on the limitations of the proposed method, particularly in terms of its applicability in real-world scenarios and its scalability to more complex environments.
- There is a concern about the generalizability of the results, as the experiments were conducted primarily in simulated environments, which may not fully represent real-world conditions.
- The paper could benefit from a more comprehensive evaluation across a broader range of tasks and environments to demonstrate the robustness and effectiveness of the proposed method.
- The notation used in the paper is sometimes confusing, and there are inconsistencies in the use of symbols, which could hinder understanding.
- The paper does not adequately address the issue of goal set coverage in the dynamics dataset, which is crucial for the method's effectiveness.

**Questions:**
- How does the proposed method handle scenarios where the contexts map to different goal sets, and how does this affect the performance and applicability of the method?
- Can the authors clarify the conditions under which the method can guarantee the success of learning, particularly in terms of the coverage of the dynamics dataset and the context-goal dataset?
- How does the method perform in more complex environments, such as those involving continuous state and action spaces, and what are the potential challenges and limitations in such settings?
- Could the authors provide more details on the experimental setup, particularly the construction of the context-goal dataset and the selection of states for goal examples?
- How does the proposed method compare to other goal-conditioned RL methods, especially in terms of performance and practical applicability?
