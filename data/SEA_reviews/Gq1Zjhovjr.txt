 **Summary:**
The paper explores domain generalization through a novel method called Logit Attribution Matching (LAM), which leverages targeted data augmentation to create semantic sharing pairs for regularization. This approach, which focuses on matching logit attributions between original and augmented images, is contrasted with traditional methods that match logits, probabilities, or features. The authors demonstrate that LAM can outperform existing methods on several benchmarks, particularly in scenarios with multiple classes. The theoretical underpinnings of LAM are grounded in causal invariance, and the paper includes extensive empirical evaluations to support its claims.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, with a clear structure and presentation that makes it easy to follow.
- The proposed method, LAM, is simple yet effective, and is supported by both theoretical and empirical evidence.
- The authors provide a thorough evaluation of existing methods, demonstrating the effectiveness of LAM in domain generalization.
- The paper is well-organized, with a clear introduction of the proposed method and its theoretical foundations.
- The empirical results are strong, showing that LAM outperforms existing methods in most cases.
- The use of semantic sharing pairs for consistency regularization is a novel approach that enhances the understanding of domain generalization.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper could benefit from a more detailed explanation of the data augmentation process used to create semantic sharing pairs, especially in the appendix.
- There is a lack of discussion on the limitations of the proposed method, which could provide a more balanced view of its applicability and effectiveness.
- The paper could benefit from additional experiments to further validate the effectiveness of LAM, particularly in different scenarios and with different datasets.
- The theoretical underpinnings of the paper, particularly the causal invariance aspect, are not sufficiently explained or justified, leaving some parts of the argumentation unclear.
- The paper does not sufficiently address the practical implementation challenges of LAM, such as the computational complexity of computing logit attribution matching.
- The paper could benefit from a more detailed discussion on the relationship between LAM and other existing methods, particularly those that also use logit matching.

**Questions:**
- How does the proposed method perform in scenarios where the data augmentation process is not as effective in creating semantic sharing pairs?
- Could the authors provide a more detailed explanation of the data augmentation process used to create semantic sharing pairs, especially in the appendix?
- How does the proposed method compare to other methods that also use logit matching, such as CoRE?
- Could the authors provide more details on the computational complexity of computing logit attribution matching, and discuss potential strategies for efficient implementation?
- How does the proposed method handle scenarios where the data augmentation process does not create semantic sharing pairs effectively?
- Could the authors provide a more detailed discussion on the relationship between LAM and other existing methods, particularly those that also use logit matching?
- How does the proposed method perform in scenarios where the data distribution is significantly different from the training data, such as in the ImageNet-1K dataset?
