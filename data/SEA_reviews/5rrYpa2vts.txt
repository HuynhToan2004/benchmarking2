 **Summary:**
The paper introduces EA2N, a novel model for fake news detection that leverages Abstract Meaning Representation (AMR) and Wikidata to capture complex semantic relations and external knowledge. EA2N integrates a language encoder with affective lexical features, a graph encoder using AMR and Wikidata, and a path-aware graph learning module to capture semantic relationships. The model is evaluated on two datasets and shows superior performance compared to existing state-of-the-art methods. The paper also includes an ablation study to demonstrate the effectiveness of each component of the model.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel approach by integrating AMR and Wikidata for fake news detection, which is a significant advancement in the field.
- The methodology is well-structured, with a clear explanation of the model components and their integration, including the use of AMR and Wikidata for knowledge integration.
- The paper provides extensive experiments and ablation studies that demonstrate the effectiveness of each component of the model, supporting the claims of superior performance.
- The use of affective features and the path-aware graph learning module adds depth to the model's capabilities, enhancing its ability to capture complex semantic relationships.
- The paper is well-written, making it accessible and understandable, which is crucial for a broad audience.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed discussion on the limitations of the proposed method, which could provide a more balanced view of the research.
- The experimental setup is limited to two datasets, which may not fully demonstrate the generalizability of the model across different domains or datasets.
- There is a lack of comparison with other state-of-the-art models that also use AMR or other knowledge graphs, which could provide a more robust evaluation of the model's performance.
- The paper does not include a discussion on the computational complexity or the scalability of the model, which is crucial for practical applications.
- The paper could benefit from a more detailed analysis of the model's performance on different types of fake news, such as those related to politics or health, to demonstrate the model's effectiveness across various domains.
- The paper lacks a discussion on the ethical implications of the model, which is important for a model that deals with fake news, a critical issue in the digital world.
