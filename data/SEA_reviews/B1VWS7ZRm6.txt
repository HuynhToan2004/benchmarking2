 **Summary:**
The paper introduces CHARMS, a method designed to transfer tabular knowledge to images by aligning image channels with tabular attributes using optimal transport. The approach aims to improve the performance of image classifiers by integrating knowledge from tabular data. Experiments were conducted on six datasets, showing that CHARMS outperforms other methods in transferring knowledge from tabular data to images. The paper also discusses the use of different network structures and their impact on the method's performance. Despite its innovative approach, the paper has been critiqued for its lack of clarity in methodological details and the need for more comprehensive comparisons with state-of-the-art methods.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, making it easy to follow, and the experiments are thorough, demonstrating the effectiveness of the proposed method.
- The approach is novel, leveraging optimal transport to align channels and attributes, which is a new direction for transferring tabular knowledge to images.
- The proposed method shows better performance compared to other methods in transferring knowledge from tabular data to images, and it is applicable to different network structures.
- The paper provides a good analysis of the method, including the use of mutual information to analyze the learning process of knowledge transfer models.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks clarity in some sections, particularly in the methodology and the experimental setup.
- There is a need for more detailed explanations of the experimental setup and the results, including the specifics of the datasets used and the methodology employed.
- The paper could benefit from a more comprehensive comparison with state-of-the-art methods, including a discussion on how the proposed method compares to existing techniques like CLIP.
- The paper's contribution to the field is not clearly articulated, and the novelty of the method is questioned due to its similarity to existing methods.
- The paper does not adequately address the limitations of the proposed method, such as its scalability and generalizability across different types of tabular data.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors clarify the methodology and the experimental setup, particularly in Section 4.3, to make it clearer and more accessible?
- How does the proposed method compare to other state-of-the-art methods, especially in terms of performance and efficiency?
- What are the specific advantages of using optimal transport for aligning channels and attributes, and how does this approach differ from other methods?
- Could the authors provide more detailed results and visualizations, including the alignment of channels and attributes, to better illustrate the method's effectiveness?
- How does the proposed method handle the transfer of knowledge from tabular data to images, especially in scenarios where the tabular data is incomplete or noisy?
- What are the potential limitations or challenges of the proposed method, and how might these affect its practical application?
