 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel approach to time series forecasting by integrating the Perceiver architecture with the copula-based decoder from TACTiS. This integration aims to address the computational inefficiencies of TACTiS, which heavily relies on self-attention mechanisms. The proposed model, named PrACTiS, employs a perceiver-based encoder and a copula-based decoder to handle both observed and missing data points effectively. It also introduces midpoint inference and local attention mechanisms to capture dependencies within imputed samples, thereby improving computational efficiency. The paper presents experimental results showing that PrACTiS outperforms TACTiS in terms of computational efficiency and prediction accuracy on various benchmark datasets. However, the paper has been critiqued for its lack of clarity in some sections, particularly in explaining the technical contributions and the experimental setup.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel approach by combining the Perceiver architecture with the copula-based decoder from TACTiS, which is a significant advancement in time series forecasting.
- The proposed model, PrACTiS, is shown to outperform TACTiS in terms of computational efficiency and prediction accuracy, as demonstrated through comprehensive experimental results.
- The paper is well-written, making it easy to follow, and provides a detailed description of the experimental setup, including the datasets used and the model training parameters.
- The integration of the perceiver-based encoder and the copula-based decoder is innovative and contributes to the reduction of computational demands in time series forecasting.
- The paper includes a thorough analysis of the model's performance across various datasets, highlighting the model's effectiveness in handling different types of time series data.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks clarity in explaining the technical contributions of the proposed model, particularly the role of the perceiver architecture and the copula-based decoder in addressing the computational inefficiencies of TACTiS.
- The paper does not provide a detailed comparison of the computational efficiency of PrACTiS with other state-of-the-art models, which could help in understanding the model's advantages more clearly.
- The experimental setup and results are not convincingly presented, with some results (e.g., Figure 3) being misleading or inadequately explained.
- The paper lacks a discussion on the limitations of the proposed model and does not address the scalability of the model to larger datasets or the handling of different types of time series data.
- The paper could benefit from a more comprehensive discussion on the theoretical underpinnings of the model, particularly how the use of copulas enhances the model's performance.

**Questions:**
- Could you provide a more detailed explanation of how the perceiver architecture and the copula-based decoder specifically address the computational inefficiencies of TACTiS?
- How does the computational efficiency of PrACTiS compare to other state-of-the-art models, and what are the main advantages of using PrACTiS over these models?
- In the experimental setup, how were the hyperparameters of PrACTiS tuned, and what was the impact of these hyperparameters on the model's performance?
- Can you provide more details on the training procedure for the copula-based decoder, particularly how the parameterized diffeomorphism fθ,c was trained?
- How does the model handle different types of time series data, such as those with varying sampling frequencies or different variable types?
- Could you clarify the results in Figure 3, particularly the discrepancies in the prediction length and the performance metrics between PrACTiS and TACTiS?
- What are the main limitations of the proposed model, and how do these limitations impact the model's performance in real-world applications?
