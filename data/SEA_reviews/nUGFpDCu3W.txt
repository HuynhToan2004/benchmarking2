 **Summary:**
The paper investigates the storage and retrieval mechanisms of linguistic information in the GPT-2 model by focusing on the identification of brackets. It utilizes activation patching to trace the effects of perturbations in different layers of the model, specifically the first MLP layer, which is found to be crucial for storing and retrieving information related to brackets. The paper also explores how this information is transferred to later layers, suggesting a hierarchical memory system within the model. The findings contribute to a better understanding of how transformer models process and retain information, which could be beneficial for improving model interpretability and performance.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, clear, and easy to follow, with a clear motivation and a novel approach to analyzing the storage and retrieval of linguistic information in transformer models.
- The use of activation patching to study the role of different layers in the model is innovative and provides a detailed analysis of the information flow within the model.
- The paper contributes to the understanding of how transformer models process and retain information, which is crucial for improving model interpretability and performance.
- The findings on the storage and retrieval mechanisms of linguistic information in transformer models are significant and could be beneficial for future research in this area.
- The paper is well-organized, with clear figures and tables that aid in understanding the results and the methodology used.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper's focus on a specific task (identification of brackets) limits the generalizability of the findings to other linguistic or factual knowledge stored in the model.
- The paper does not sufficiently differentiate its approach from previous works, particularly in terms of the methodology and the insights provided.
- The paper lacks a comprehensive evaluation of the model's performance on more complex linguistic structures, such as nested brackets, which could have provided a deeper understanding of the model's capabilities.
- There is a lack of discussion on the limitations of the model and the potential biases that could affect the findings, which could undermine the credibility of the results.
- The paper does not sufficiently explore the implications of the findings for improving model performance or interpretability, which could be a significant contribution to the field.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors clarify how the findings on the storage and retrieval of linguistic information in transformer models could be generalized to other tasks or types of knowledge?
- How does the paper's approach differ from previous works in terms of methodology and insights provided?
- Could the authors provide more details on how the model's performance on more complex linguistic structures, such as nested brackets, was evaluated?
- How does the paper address the potential biases that could affect the findings, and what steps were taken to mitigate these biases?
- What are the implications of the findings for improving model performance or interpretability, and how could these insights be applied in practical scenarios?
