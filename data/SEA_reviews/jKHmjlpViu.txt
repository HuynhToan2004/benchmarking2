 **Summary:**
The paper introduces OpenWebMath, a dataset of 14.7 billion tokens from mathematical webpages extracted from Common Crawl. The dataset undergoes rigorous filtering and deduplication to ensure high quality. It includes text and LaTeX content, which is crucial for training large language models in mathematical reasoning tasks. The paper provides a detailed description of the dataset's construction, including the challenges faced in extracting LaTeX formulas from HTML documents. It also presents a small-scale experiment using a 1.4 billion parameter language model, demonstrating the effectiveness of the dataset in enhancing mathematical reasoning abilities.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a large-scale dataset of mathematical webpages, which is a significant contribution to the field of natural language processing.
- The dataset is extensively documented, providing a clear and detailed description of its construction and the challenges faced, such as the extraction of LaTeX formulas from HTML documents.
- The paper includes a small-scale experiment demonstrating the effectiveness of the dataset in enhancing mathematical reasoning abilities, showing that models trained on OpenWebMath outperform models trained on general-domain tokens.
- The dataset is open-sourced, making it accessible for further research and development in the field.
- The paper is well-written, clear, and easy to follow, making it accessible to a broad audience.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed discussion on the limitations of the dataset, which could be crucial for understanding its applicability and potential biases.
- There is a need for more detailed analysis of the dataset, including the distribution of mathematical formulas and the diversity of mathematical content.
- The paper does not discuss the potential impact of the dataset on the research community, particularly in terms of its utility and potential biases.
- The paper does not provide a comprehensive comparison with other datasets, which could help in understanding the unique contributions and advantages of OpenWebMath.
- The paper lacks a detailed discussion on the methods used for filtering and deduplication, which are crucial for ensuring the quality and uniqueness of the dataset.
- The paper does not include a detailed discussion on the ethical considerations of data collection, which is crucial for ensuring the responsible use of the dataset.
