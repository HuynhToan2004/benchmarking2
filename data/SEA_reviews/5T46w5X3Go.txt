 **Summary:**
The paper explores the generalization performance of transfer learning in linear regression models, focusing on the impact of task similarity and the allocation of features between common and task-specific parts. It investigates two options for parameter transfer and characterizes the double descent of transfer learning, showing that allocating more redundant features to the task-specific part can enhance performance. The paper also introduces a theoretical framework that provides insights into the generalization performance of the target task and offers practical guidelines for designing effective transfer learning approaches. Despite its theoretical contributions, the paper is critiqued for its limited empirical validation and lack of discussion on the practical implications of its findings.
