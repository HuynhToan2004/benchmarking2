 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a hybrid model for time series forecasting, integrating multiple existing methods to enhance performance. It includes a series decomposition, patching, and instance normalization, along with global and local feature extraction. The model is evaluated on multiple datasets, demonstrating competitive performance against state-of-the-art methods. However, the novelty of the approach is questioned, as it primarily combines existing techniques without significant innovation. The paper also lacks clarity in its experimental setup and comparisons, and the presentation needs improvement.

**Strengths:**
- The paper provides a comprehensive overview of existing techniques for time series forecasting, including data patching, instance normalization, series decomposition, and local and global feature extraction.
- The proposed method integrates these techniques into a unified framework, which is demonstrated to perform well across various datasets.
- The experimental results are robust, showing the effectiveness of the proposed method in outperforming state-of-the-art methods in most cases.
- The paper is well-written, making it easy to follow, and the authors provide detailed explanations of the experimental results.
- The proposed method is simple and easy to implement, with a focus on improving the performance of time series forecasting.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks clear differentiation from existing methods, particularly in terms of the novelty and significance of the contributions.
- The experimental setup and comparisons are not clearly described, making it difficult to understand the advantages of the proposed method over existing baselines.
- The presentation of the paper needs improvement, particularly in the clarity of figures and tables, and the organization of the content.
- The paper does not adequately address the computational complexity of the proposed method, which could be a significant limitation in practical applications.
- The paper's claims about the effectiveness of the proposed method are not sufficiently supported by the experimental results, which are sometimes inconsistent or misleading.
- The paper lacks a detailed discussion on the limitations of the proposed method and potential negative societal impacts.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors clarify the specific contributions of the proposed method beyond the existing techniques it integrates?
- How does the proposed method compare in terms of computational efficiency and practical applicability to other state-of-the-art methods?
- Could the authors provide more detailed explanations of the experimental results, particularly in cases where the proposed method does not outperform the baselines?
- How does the proposed method handle different types of time series data, such as univariate vs. multivariate data?
- What are the specific advantages of the proposed method over existing baselines, and how do these advantages translate into practical applications?
- Could the authors provide a more detailed discussion on the limitations of the proposed method and potential negative societal impacts?
