 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel method for reconstructing labels from intermediate gradients in a federated learning setting, focusing on the use of label smoothing and mixup augmentations. The proposed algorithm aims to recover augmented labels from single-input gradients, which is a significant departure from previous methods that primarily focused on reconstructing one-hot labels. This approach is evaluated on various datasets and is shown to improve image reconstruction quality. However, the paper's contribution is seen as incremental, primarily building upon existing methods, and the experimental validation is considered insufficient.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel algorithm that can recover the augmented label of a single input image along with the input feature, which is a significant advancement in gradient inversion attacks.
- The methodology is well-explained, making it easy to understand and replicate, with a clear presentation of the proposed method.
- The paper provides a comprehensive evaluation of the proposed method, demonstrating its effectiveness through extensive experiments on various datasets and models.
- The analysis of the limitations of previous bias-based recovery methods is insightful, offering a deeper understanding of the challenges and opportunities in this area.
- The paper is well-written, making it accessible and easy to follow, which is crucial for advancing research in this field.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper's contribution is seen as incremental, primarily building upon existing methods without sufficiently differentiating its approach from prior works.
- The experimental validation is considered insufficient, with a lack of comparison to state-of-the-art methods and a limited evaluation on only a few datasets.
- The paper does not sufficiently address the limitations of the proposed method, such as its applicability to different types of neural network architectures and the potential impact of varying input data.
- The paper's focus on specific label augmentations (label smoothing and mixup) may limit its applicability to other types of label augmentations, which could affect the generalizability of the findings.
- There are some minor presentation issues, such as inconsistencies in the use of bold fonts and unclear descriptions in figures and tables.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors clarify the novelty of their approach compared to prior works, particularly in terms of the methodology and the results?
- How does the proposed method perform when applied to different types of neural network architectures, such as transformer-based models?
- Can the authors provide a more detailed comparison of their method with state-of-the-art methods, especially in terms of performance and efficiency?
- What are the potential implications of the proposed method for privacy and security in federated learning systems?
- Could the authors expand the evaluation to include a broader range of datasets and models to better demonstrate the generalizability of their findings?
- How does the proposed method handle different types of label augmentations beyond those specifically tested?
