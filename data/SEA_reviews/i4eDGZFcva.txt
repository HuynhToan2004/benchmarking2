 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a new variant of Q-learning, termed Centered Q-learning, which adjusts the reward signal by subtracting the average reward rate to improve learning efficiency in discounted reinforcement learning (RL) problems. This method is particularly beneficial when the discount factor is close to one, and it is robust to constant shifts in rewards. The authors provide theoretical justification and empirical evidence across various domains, including tabular, linear, and non-linear function approximations. The method is shown to enhance the robustness and efficiency of RL algorithms, although it is limited to discounted RL settings and does not address the challenges of convergence or the applicability to episodic tasks.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel method for adjusting rewards in RL algorithms, which is a significant contribution to the field.
- The theoretical analysis provided is robust and supports the empirical results, enhancing the credibility of the proposed method.
- The method is simple, easy to understand, and implement, which is crucial for practical applications.
- The paper is well-written and clearly explains the methodology and results, making it accessible to a broad audience.
- The empirical results demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed method across various domains and problem settings.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper is limited to discounted RL settings, which may not generalize well to episodic tasks, where the reward signal might not be discounted.
- There is a lack of discussion on the convergence of the Centered Q-learning algorithm, which is a critical aspect of the theoretical analysis.
- The paper does not address how the method performs with non-stationary rewards, which is a significant limitation given the practical relevance of such scenarios.
- The paper could benefit from a more detailed discussion on the limitations and potential negative societal impacts of the proposed method.
- There are some minor issues with clarity and presentation, such as unclear definitions and formatting errors.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors clarify the convergence of the Centered Q-learning algorithm and discuss the implications of this convergence in practical scenarios?
- How does the proposed method perform in non-stationary reward environments, and what are the potential solutions to address this issue?
- Could the authors provide a more detailed discussion on the limitations and potential negative societal impacts of the proposed method?
- Is there a possibility to extend the method to episodic tasks, and if so, what are the potential challenges and solutions?
- Can the authors elaborate on the choice of parameters, such as the step-size parameters in the experiments, and discuss how these choices affect the performance of the method?
