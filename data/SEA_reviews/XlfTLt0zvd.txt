 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel transformer-based architecture for 3D face alignment, focusing on two key modules: the Query-aware Memory (QAMem) and the Multi-layer Additive Residual Regression (MARR). The QAMem module allows for separate memory values per query, enhancing the model's ability to handle low-resolution feature maps effectively. The MARR module, on the other hand, utilizes an average face model to enhance the robustness of predicted landmarks. The paper also incorporates an Euler Angles Loss to improve the model's handling of atypical head poses. Extensive experiments on two public datasets demonstrate the effectiveness of these modules, with the proposed method showing superior performance over existing state-of-the-art methods.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written and easy to follow, with clear figures and tables that aid in understanding the proposed methods.
- The introduction of the Query-aware Memory (QAMem) and Multi-layer Additive Residual Regression (MARR) modules represents a significant contribution to the field of 3D face alignment, addressing the challenges of handling low-resolution feature maps and improving the robustness of predicted landmarks.
- The paper presents strong experimental results, demonstrating the effectiveness of the proposed methods over existing state-of-the-art methods.
- The motivation behind the research is clear, and the paper provides a comprehensive ablation study, which supports the effectiveness of the proposed modules.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed comparison with other state-of-the-art methods, particularly those that also use transformers for 3D face alignment. This makes it difficult to assess the true novelty and superiority of the proposed methods.
- The paper does not adequately address the computational cost of the proposed methods, which could be a significant concern for real-time applications.
- There is a lack of clarity in the description of the MARR module, particularly how the average face model is utilized during the inference stage.
- The paper does not sufficiently discuss the limitations of the proposed methods, which could help in understanding the scope of applicability and potential improvements.
- The novelty of the proposed methods is questionable as the use of transformers and the concept of query-aware memory are not entirely new.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors provide a more detailed comparison with other state-of-the-art methods, including a discussion on the computational efficiency and real-time performance of the proposed methods?
- How does the MARR module perform when the average face model is not available during the inference stage?
- What are the specific contributions of the proposed methods compared to existing works, particularly in terms of novelty and superior performance?
- Can the authors clarify the role of the MARR module in improving the robustness of the predicted landmarks and discuss its effectiveness in handling different head poses?
- How does the Euler Angles Loss specifically enhance the model's ability to handle atypical head poses, and what are the specific improvements observed in the experimental results?
