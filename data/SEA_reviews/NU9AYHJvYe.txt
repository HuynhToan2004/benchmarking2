 **Summary:**
The paper investigates the sample complexity of contrastive learning, focusing on the number of labeled tuples required for achieving high generalization accuracy. It provides theoretical bounds on sample complexity for various distance metrics, including ℓp-distances, tree metrics, and cosine similarity. The authors employ the VC/Natarajan dimension to derive these bounds, demonstrating that the theoretical results align well with empirical findings. The paper also discusses the implications of these bounds for practical applications in deep learning, suggesting that the theoretical insights can guide experimental results.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, clear, and easy to follow, with a comprehensive presentation that includes informal versions of theorems.
- The theoretical results are solid and well-supported by the proofs, which are detailed and clear.
- The paper addresses a significant and important problem in the field of contrastive learning, focusing on the sample complexity which is crucial for understanding the generalization performance of models.
- The paper provides tight bounds on the sample complexity of learning ℓp-distances for integer p, which is a significant contribution to the field.
- The experimental results demonstrate a good match between the theoretical predictions and empirical findings, which supports the practical applicability of the theoretical results.
