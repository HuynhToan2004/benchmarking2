 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel approach to training Bayesian last layer neural networks using variational inference. The method aims to improve predictive accuracy, calibration, and out-of-distribution detection by optimizing a lower bound on the marginal likelihood. The proposed method is computationally efficient, allowing for single-pass training and inference, which is a significant advantage over traditional methods. The paper provides a theoretical framework and empirical evidence, including experiments on datasets like CIFAR-10 and CIFAR-100, demonstrating the method's effectiveness. However, the method's novelty and computational efficiency claims have been questioned, with concerns about the theoretical underpinnings and the practical implications of the method's computational complexity.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel approach to training Bayesian last layer neural networks using variational inference, which is a significant contribution to the field.
- The method is computationally efficient, which is a major advantage over traditional methods, and the paper provides a clear theoretical framework for the proposed method.
- The empirical results are strong, demonstrating the effectiveness of the proposed method across various datasets and tasks.
- The paper is well-written, making it accessible and easy to understand, which is crucial for advancing research in this area.
- The proposed method is a significant step towards improving the computational efficiency of Bayesian neural networks, which is a critical issue in the field.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper does not sufficiently distinguish its contributions from existing methods, particularly in the area of variational inference for Bayesian neural networks.
- The theoretical underpinnings of the method are not adequately explained, and the paper lacks a detailed discussion on the assumptions and limitations of the variational posterior.
- The empirical results are not sufficiently robust, with some results showing no significant improvement over baseline methods, and the paper does not include a comprehensive comparison with state-of-the-art methods.
- The paper lacks a discussion on the computational complexity of the proposed method, which is crucial for understanding its practical implications.
- The paper's presentation could be improved, particularly in terms of clarity and organization, which could enhance its readability and accessibility.

**Questions:**
- Could you clarify the novelty of your approach compared to existing methods, particularly in the area of variational inference for Bayesian neural networks?
- How do you address the computational complexity of the proposed method, and what are the practical implications of this complexity in terms of training and inference times?
- How do you ensure the reliability and robustness of the variational posterior, particularly in the context of the assumptions and limitations you have discussed?
- Can you provide a more detailed comparison of your method with state-of-the-art methods, particularly in terms of computational efficiency and predictive accuracy?
- Could you elaborate on the choice of prior for the variational posterior and how it affects the results?
- How does the proposed method perform in scenarios where the data is highly noisy or where the model is overfitting?
