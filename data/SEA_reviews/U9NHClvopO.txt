 **Summary:**
The paper introduces SuperPos-Prompt, a novel reparameterization technique for soft prompt tuning in language models, which involves the superposition of multiple pretrained vocabulary embeddings to enhance learning. The approach is evaluated across several GLUE and SuperGLUE benchmarks, demonstrating improvements over residual prompt tuning and full fine-tuning in terms of average score increases and faster convergence. Additionally, the paper suggests that omitting dropout from the frozen network can lead to more efficient and expedited convergence in prompt tuning. However, the novelty of the approach is questioned, with some reviewers suggesting that the method is a simple combination of existing techniques.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written and easy to follow, with a clear and concise presentation of the proposed method.
- The proposed method, SuperPos-Prompt, shows significant improvements in average score increases and faster convergence compared to residual prompt tuning and full fine-tuning methods.
- The paper includes an ablation study on the number of sampled token embeddings, demonstrating the optimal plateau at 128 tokens.
- The experiments are conducted across a variety of GLUE and SuperGLUE benchmarks, providing a robust evaluation of the proposed method.
- The paper provides a detailed comparison of the proposed method with various other prompt tuning methods, which helps in understanding its effectiveness and advantages.
- The paper is well-structured, with a clear motivation for the proposed method and a thorough evaluation of its performance.

**Weaknesses:**
- The novelty of the approach is questioned, with some reviewers suggesting that the method is a simple combination of existing techniques, such as residual prompt tuning and superposition of multiple pretrained vocabulary embeddings.
- The paper lacks a detailed discussion on the limitations of the proposed method, which could help in understanding its scope and applicability.
- The experiments are conducted on smaller models (T5-Small and T5-Base), which might not adequately represent the performance of the method on larger models.
- The paper does not include a comparison with some existing methods, such as IPT, which could have provided a more comprehensive evaluation of the proposed method.
- The paper does not discuss the potential negative societal impacts of the proposed method, which is a significant omission.
- The paper lacks a detailed discussion on the choice of hyperparameters and their impact on the performance of the method.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors provide more details on the choice of hyperparameters and their impact on the performance of the method?
- How does the proposed method compare in terms of computational efficiency and resource requirements with other prompt tuning methods?
- Could the authors provide a more detailed discussion on the limitations of the proposed method and its applicability in different scenarios?
- How does the proposed method perform on larger models, and what are the implications of this performance on the practical applicability of the method?
- Could the authors discuss the potential negative societal impacts of the proposed method and how these might be mitigated?
- Could the authors provide a more detailed discussion on the choice of benchmarks and the rationale behind their selection?
