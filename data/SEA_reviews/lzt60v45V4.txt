 **Summary:**
The paper introduces Variational Federated Continual Learning (VFCL), a novel Bayesian neural network-based framework designed to address local overfitting and catastrophic forgetting in federated continual learning (FCL). VFCL integrates variational inference with a mixture prior to merge global and local historical knowledge, which is aggregated globally to minimize errors. Theoretical analysis is provided on the generalization error upper bound of VFCL, supported by empirical evaluations on CIFAR100 and TinyImageNet datasets. The framework outperforms other state-of-the-art methods, demonstrating its effectiveness. However, concerns are raised about the clarity of the methodology, the novelty of the approach, and the lack of comprehensive experimental validation, including a lack of ablation studies and insufficient comparisons with existing methods.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, clear, and easy to follow, with a clear motivation for the research and a clear presentation of the proposed method.
- The methodology is innovative, combining variational inference with a mixture prior to merge global and local historical knowledge, which is aggregated globally to minimize errors.
- Theoretical analysis is provided on the generalization error upper bound of VFCL, supported by empirical evaluations on CIFAR100 and TinyImageNet datasets, which demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed method.
- The paper includes a theoretical analysis on the upper bound of the generalization error of VFCL, which supports the design of VFCL and the selection of hyperparameters.
- Extensive experiments are conducted by comparing VFCL with other methods on CIFAR100 and TinyImageNet, and the results clearly demonstrate the superiority of VFCL over other methods.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed explanation of the proposed method, particularly how variational inference with a mixture prior and global posterior aggregation are integrated to address local overfitting and catastrophic forgetting.
- The novelty of the approach is questioned as similar methods have been proposed in previous works.
- The paper lacks ablation studies to demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed components, such as the mixture prior and global posterior aggregation.
- The experimental setup is not comprehensive enough, with insufficient comparisons to existing methods and lack of experiments on larger datasets like ImageNet.
- The paper lacks a detailed comparison of the computational and communication costs of the proposed method compared to existing methods.
- The paper suffers from several typographical errors and could benefit from improved clarity and logical flow in some sections.

**Questions:**
- Could you clarify how variational inference with a mixture prior and global posterior aggregation are integrated to address local overfitting and catastrophic forgetting?
- How does the proposed method differ from existing methods in terms of novelty and effectiveness?
- Can you provide more details on the experimental setup, including the number of clients, the number of tasks, and the communication and computational costs of the proposed method?
- How does the method handle the integration of global and local historical knowledge, and what are the implications of this integration on the performance of the model?
- Could you provide more details on the experimental results, particularly the performance of the model on different tasks and the impact of different hyperparameters on the performance?
- How does the method perform in scenarios where the data distribution across clients is not i.i.d., and what are the implications of this on the performance of the model?
