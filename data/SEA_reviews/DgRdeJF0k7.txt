 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel masked dual-temporal autoencoder (MDTA) for semi-supervised time-series classification, leveraging masked time-series modeling to capture intricate temporal structures. The MDTA framework incorporates a dual-temporal encoder that captures temporal dependencies across different time scales, a simple decoder, and a classification head. The model is evaluated on 15 univariate time-series datasets, demonstrating superior performance over existing methods. The methodology includes a relation-preserving loss function to maintain temporal structures and random masking ratios to avoid high-cost tuning processes. Despite the innovative approach, concerns were raised about the scalability of the method to multivariate datasets and the need for a broader range of baseline comparisons.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel approach using masked time-series modeling for semi-supervised time-series classification, which is a first in this domain.
- The methodology is well-articulated, with a clear explanation of the proposed dual-temporal encoder and its ability to capture temporal dependencies across different time scales.
- The paper is well-structured, making it easy to follow, and includes comprehensive experimental results and ablation studies that demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed method.
- The use of random masking ratios is innovative and helps avoid the high-cost tuning process for finding optimal masking ratios.
- The paper is well-written, with a clear presentation of the methodology and results, making it accessible and understandable.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks clarity in some sections, particularly in the explanation of the relation-preserving loss function and its impact on model performance.
- There is a notable absence of comparisons with a broader range of baseline methods, which could have provided a more comprehensive evaluation of the proposed method.
- The paper does not adequately address the scalability of the method to multivariate datasets, which could limit its applicability in real-world scenarios.
- The computational complexity of the method, particularly the use of two consecutive sub-encoders, is not discussed, raising concerns about the efficiency of the proposed approach.
- The paper could benefit from a more detailed discussion on the limitations of the proposed method and potential directions for future research.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors provide a clearer explanation of the relation-preserving loss function and its impact on model performance?
- How does the proposed method perform when applied to multivariate datasets?
- Is there a plan to include more baseline comparisons in future work to provide a more comprehensive evaluation of the proposed method?
- Could the authors discuss the computational complexity of the method and provide insights into how it could be made more efficient?
- How does the proposed method handle the challenge of masking ratios, especially in scenarios where different datasets might require different masking ratios?
