 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel method for handling label noise in machine learning models, focusing on the estimation of a global transition matrix rather than individual matrices for each sample. This method is inspired by implicit regularization techniques and is validated through theoretical analysis and extensive experiments on both synthetic and real-world datasets. The approach aims to address the limitations of previous methods by simplifying the estimation process and potentially reducing computational costs. Despite its innovative approach, the paper faces criticism for its clarity, lack of comprehensive literature review, and potential over-simplification of the theoretical analysis.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel approach to estimating a global transition matrix, which is a significant advancement over traditional methods that require individual matrix estimation for each sample.
- The use of implicit regularization in the method is innovative and well-explained, providing a clear theoretical framework for understanding the method's operation.
- The paper is well-structured and easy to follow, with clear explanations of the methodology and its theoretical underpinnings.
- The experimental results demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed method, showing improvements over previous methods in handling noisy labels.
- The paper is well-written, making it accessible and understandable, which is crucial for a broad audience.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a comprehensive literature review, particularly in the area of implicit regularization, which is crucial for positioning the work within the existing body of knowledge.
- The theoretical analysis is overly simplified and may not accurately reflect the complexity of real-world scenarios, which could undermine the credibility of the method's claims.
- The paper suffers from clarity issues, with some sections being difficult to understand, particularly in the explanation of the transition matrix and the method's assumptions.
- There are inconsistencies and potential errors in the paper, such as missing references and incorrect citation formats, which detract from its professional quality.
- The paper does not adequately address the computational complexity of the method, which is a significant concern given the potential for high computational costs.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors clarify the role and operation of the implicit regularization in the method, particularly how it affects the estimation of the transition matrix?
- How does the paper ensure the identifiability of the transition matrix, especially given the assumptions made about its structure?
- Could the authors provide more detailed explanations of the assumptions made in the theoretical analysis, particularly how these assumptions apply in real-world scenarios?
- How does the paper address the potential for over-smoothing in the transition matrix, which could lead to inaccurate label predictions?
- Can the authors explain the choice of the loss function in the theoretical analysis and its implications for the practical application of the method?
- How does the paper handle the computational complexity of the method, and what strategies are used to minimize these costs in practical applications?
