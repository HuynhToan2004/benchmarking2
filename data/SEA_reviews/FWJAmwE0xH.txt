 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel neural-symbolic model called the Neural-Symbolic Recursive Machine (NSR), designed to handle sequence-to-sequence tasks and demonstrate superior generalization capabilities. The model integrates neural perception, syntactic parsing, and semantic reasoning, leveraging a Grounded Symbol System (GSS) to represent data, symbols, and their meanings. NSR employs a deduction-abduction algorithm for training and has been evaluated on several benchmarks, showing superior performance compared to existing methods. The paper also discusses the challenges in training NSR and the potential limitations in real-world applications.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, making it easy to follow and understand the proposed methodologies and experimental results.
- The introduction of the Grounded Symbol System (GSS) as a novel internal representation for systematic generalization is a significant contribution to the field.
- The paper provides a thorough evaluation of the proposed method on several benchmarks, demonstrating its superior performance compared to existing methods.
- The methodology integrates neural perception, syntactic parsing, and semantic reasoning, which are crucial for achieving systematic generalization.
- The paper includes a detailed description of the methodology and experimental setup, making it accessible and reproducible.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed discussion on the limitations of the proposed method, which is crucial for understanding its applicability and potential drawbacks.
- There is a lack of clarity on the specific advantages of the proposed method over existing neural-symbolic methods, particularly in terms of scalability and efficiency.
- The paper does not adequately address the challenges in training the NSR model, which could limit its practical application.
- The paper's focus on specific datasets and tasks might limit its generalizability to other domains.
- There is a need for more detailed experimental results, including failure cases and a broader range of test cases, to better understand the model's behavior and limitations.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors provide a detailed discussion on the limitations of the proposed method and how these might impact its practical application?
- How does the proposed method compare in terms of scalability and efficiency with other neural-symbolic methods?
- Could the authors elaborate on the specific advantages of the Grounded Symbol System (GSS) over other symbolic representations?
- In the context of the NSR model, how is the training process optimized to address the challenges mentioned?
- How does the model handle noisy and abundant concepts in real-world applications?
- Could the authors provide more detailed experimental results, including failure cases and a broader range of test cases, to better understand the model's behavior and limitations?
