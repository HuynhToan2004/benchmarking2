 **Summary:**
The paper explores the training dynamics of infinitely wide neural networks in the context of reinforcement learning (RL), specifically focusing on the TD learning method. It extends the neural tangent kernel (NTK) approach from supervised learning to RL, providing a theoretical framework for understanding the behavior of ensembles of neural networks during training. The paper derives closed-form solutions for the training dynamics of TD policy evaluation methods and investigates the effects of uncertainty quantification in the infinite-width limit. Experiments on toy environments demonstrate the applicability of these theoretical insights, although the practical implications and generalizability to more complex environments remain unclear.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written and clearly presents the main ideas and results, making it accessible to readers.
- The theoretical results are novel and provide a deeper understanding of the training dynamics of infinitely wide neural networks in the context of RL.
- The experiments are well-designed and effectively demonstrate the applicability of the theoretical insights, particularly in simple environments.
- The paper is significant as it addresses the challenge of understanding the training dynamics of deep RL agents, which is crucial for improving the stability and performance of RL algorithms.
- The paper is well-organized, with a clear structure and logical flow, which aids in understanding the complex theoretical concepts.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed discussion on the practical implications of the theoretical results, particularly in terms of their applicability to real-world RL problems.
- The experiments are limited to simple environments, which may not fully demonstrate the generalizability of the findings to more complex or realistic scenarios.
- The paper does not sufficiently discuss the limitations of the NTK approach and its assumptions, which could affect the reliability and applicability of the results.
- There is a lack of comparison with existing works, which could help in positioning the paper within the current literature and highlighting its contributions.
- The paper could benefit from a more comprehensive discussion on the practical challenges and potential solutions in applying the theoretical insights to real-world RL problems.

**Questions:**
- How do the theoretical results translate to more complex or realistic RL environments, and what are the practical implications of these findings?
- Could the authors provide more details on the practical challenges and potential solutions in applying the theoretical insights to real-world RL problems?
