 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel approach to unsupervised covariance estimation in regression tasks, focusing on the limitations of traditional methods like the negative log-likelihood. It proposes a new metric, Conditional Mean Absolute Error (CMAE), to evaluate the accuracy of covariance estimation. The paper also introduces a new method, Spatial Variance, which estimates the covariance of the predicted output using a Taylor expansion around the input. The method's effectiveness is demonstrated through experiments on synthetic and real-world datasets, showing improvements over existing methods. However, concerns were raised about the practicality of the method due to its computational complexity and the need for additional data for training.

**Strengths:**
- The paper addresses a significant and under-explored problem in unsupervised covariance estimation, which is crucial for understanding the distribution of target variables.
- The proposed Spatial Variance method is novel and provides a more accurate estimation of covariance compared to existing methods.
- The paper is well-written, clear, and easy to follow, with a comprehensive experimental setup that demonstrates the effectiveness of the proposed method.
- The introduction of the CMAE metric provides a more direct and effective evaluation of covariance estimation compared to traditional metrics.
- The paper is significant in its contribution to the field, as it addresses a fundamental problem in regression tasks and provides a new approach to estimating covariance.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a clear explanation of why the proposed method, Spatial Variance, is more effective than existing methods, particularly in terms of its computational efficiency and practical applicability.
- There is a significant computational cost associated with the Spatial Variance method, which might limit its practical use in real-world applications.
- The paper does not adequately address the scalability of the method, particularly in high-dimensional datasets, which could limit its applicability.
- The experimental setup and results could be improved by including more detailed comparisons with other methods, such as the Faithful Heteroscedastic Regression method, and by providing a more detailed analysis of the results.
- The paper could benefit from a more rigorous theoretical analysis and a clearer explanation of the method's theoretical underpinnings.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors provide a more detailed explanation of why the Spatial Variance method is more effective than existing methods, particularly in terms of its computational efficiency and practical applicability?
- How does the proposed method handle the scalability issues in high-dimensional datasets, and what are the potential solutions to address these challenges?
- Could the authors clarify the role and impact of the additional data used in training the covariance estimator, and how this affects the fairness of the comparisons made in the experiments?
- In terms of practical applications, how does the Spatial Variance method compare to other methods in terms of computational complexity and runtime?
- Can the authors provide more detailed comparisons with other methods, such as the Faithful Heteroscedastic Regression method, and include a more comprehensive analysis of the results to better understand the method's effectiveness?
