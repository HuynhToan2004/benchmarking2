 **Summary:**
The paper explores the dynamics of the PEPITA learning rule, a forward-only algorithm that avoids backpropagation, through a theoretical analysis of its performance in deep neural networks. It introduces modifications to the PEPITA rule, such as the adaptive feedback alignment (AFA) and the Hebbian-like PEPITA-Hebbian, to enhance its effectiveness in training deeper networks. The authors also provide empirical evidence supporting these modifications, showing that they can lead to improved performance and alignment with forward weights and top-down connections. Theoretical insights are provided on the dynamics of PEPITA, comparing it with feedback alignment and other forward-only algorithms like FF. Despite these contributions, the paper faces criticisms for its limited experimental scope and lack of clarity in certain sections, which could affect its impact and readability.

**Strengths:**
- The paper presents a novel theoretical analysis of the PEPITA learning rule, providing valuable insights into the dynamics of forward-only algorithms in training deep neural networks.
- The authors have derived a set of ODEs for AFA with online learning for teacher-student regression, which helps in understanding the dynamics of PEPITA in a deeper context.
- The paper is well-written, with clear explanations of the algorithms and their modifications, making it accessible to a broad audience.
- The theoretical results are supported by empirical evidence, demonstrating the effectiveness of the proposed modifications in improving the performance of PEPITA.
- The paper discusses the limitations of the approach, including the limited experimental scope and the need for further research to address these limitations.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a comprehensive experimental evaluation, particularly in terms of the breadth of tasks and architectures considered. It primarily focuses on MNIST and CIFAR-10/100 datasets, which limits the generalizability of the findings.
- The paper's theoretical analysis is limited to two-layer networks, which may not adequately represent the dynamics in deeper networks.
- The presentation of the paper could be improved, particularly in the explanation of certain sections and figures, such as Figure 1 and the discussion on the "modulated" pass in the PEPITA algorithm.
- There is a lack of discussion on the biological plausibility of the modifications to the PEPITA rule, which is a significant aspect of the paper's claims.
- The paper does not adequately address the limitations of the approach, such as the scalability of the proposed modifications to larger networks and the potential biological plausibility of the modified rules.

**Questions:**
- Could you clarify the statement on page 2 regarding the PEPITA learning rule, particularly the use of the term "modulated" pass?
- How does the Hebbian-like PEPITA-Hebbian rule compare with the original PEPITA rule in terms of performance and biological plausibility?
- Can you provide more details on the biological plausibility of the modifications to the PEPITA rule, especially in light of the concerns about their bio-inspired nature?
- How do the modifications to the PEPITA rule affect the learning dynamics in deeper networks, and what are the implications for the scalability of the approach?
- Could you expand on the discussion of the limitations of the approach, particularly in terms of the experimental scope and the biological plausibility of the modifications?
