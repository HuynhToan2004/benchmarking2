 **Summary:**
The paper introduces Symb4CO, a novel framework that leverages genetic programming to learn symbolic branching policies for combinatorial optimization (CO) problems. Symb4CO utilizes a recurrent neural network (RNN) to generate symbolic expressions from a set of features, aiming to improve the efficiency and interpretability of CO solvers. The approach is evaluated against various baselines, including both traditional and machine learning-based methods, demonstrating comparable performance and faster inference times. The framework is also noted for its simplicity and potential for wider deployment in industrial settings. However, the paper is criticized for its limited novelty and the lack of significant improvements over existing methods, particularly in terms of performance and scalability.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel approach by combining genetic programming and recurrent neural networks (RNN) to generate symbolic branching policies for combinatorial optimization (CO) problems, which is a significant advancement in the field.
- The methodology is well-explained, making it easy to understand, and the experiments are comprehensive, demonstrating the effectiveness of the proposed method.
- The paper addresses the challenges of training and inference efficiency, as well as interpretability, which are critical for the deployment of machine learning methods in combinatorial optimization.
- The results show that the learned policies are compact and easy to interpret, which is a significant advantage over traditional methods.
- The paper is well-written, making it accessible and easy to follow, which is essential for a technical paper.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks significant novelty as it primarily combines existing techniques without significant innovation.
- The performance of the proposed method is not significantly better than existing methods, and in some cases, it is worse, which raises concerns about its practical utility.
- The scalability of the method is limited, as the number of training instances is small, which might limit its applicability to larger, more complex problems.
- The paper does not adequately address the generalization capabilities of the learned policies, which is a critical aspect of machine learning applications.
- The interpretability of the learned policies is questionable, as the paper does not provide a detailed analysis of how the policies are interpreted.
- The paper does not sufficiently compare the proposed method with other recent state-of-the-art methods, which could have provided a more robust evaluation of its effectiveness.
- The paper lacks a discussion on the limitations of the proposed method, which is essential for understanding its applicability and potential drawbacks.

**Questions:**
- Could you clarify how the symbolic policies are interpreted, especially in terms of their impact on the branching process?
- How does the proposed method compare with other recent state-of-the-art methods, particularly in terms of performance and interpretability?
- What are the specific advantages of using symbolic policies over traditional methods, and how do these advantages translate into practical benefits?
- How do the learned policies generalize to different types of combinatorial optimization problems, and what are the limitations of this generalization?
- Could you provide more details on the training process, particularly how the RNN is trained and the role of the hierarchical entropy regularizer in this process?
- How does the proposed method handle the scalability issues, especially when applied to larger, more complex problems?
- Could you discuss the potential limitations of the proposed method and how these might affect its practical deployment in industrial settings?
