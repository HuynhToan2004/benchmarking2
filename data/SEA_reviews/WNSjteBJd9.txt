 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel watermarking scheme for federated learning (FL) to prevent model leakage and identify infringing clients. The approach involves encoding a client-unique key into the model using a backdoor-based watermark injection, which is decodable from the model parameters. The method is designed to be robust against various removal attacks and maintains model utility. However, concerns are raised about the practicality of the method, particularly the assumption of access to a pre-trained encoder and the computational overhead of the decoder. The paper also faces criticism for not adequately addressing the scalability and robustness of the watermarking scheme in large-scale FL systems.

**Strengths:**
- The paper addresses a significant and relevant problem of preventing IP leakage in federated learning, which is crucial for maintaining model security and integrity.
- The proposed watermarking scheme is robust against various removal attacks, demonstrating its effectiveness in detecting IP infringers.
- The method is innovative in using a decodable watermark that can be embedded without requiring access to client training data, enhancing the practicality of the approach.
- The paper is well-written, making it easy to follow and understand the proposed methodology and experimental results.
- The experimental results show that the proposed method is effective in preventing IP leakage and can be applied to various datasets, demonstrating its versatility.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a clear motivation and detailed explanation of the necessity of the decoder in the watermarking process, which could confuse readers about its role and effectiveness.
- The computational overhead introduced by the decoder is not adequately addressed, raising concerns about its practicality in large-scale federated learning systems.
- The paper does not adequately address the scalability of the watermarking scheme in large-scale federated learning systems, where the number of clients can be very large.
- The robustness of the watermarking scheme is questioned, as the method relies on the assumption that the malicious client cannot access the decoder, which may not be a practical assumption in real-world scenarios.
- The paper lacks a discussion on potential collisions between watermarks, which could lead to false positives in identifying infringing clients.
- The paper does not sufficiently compare the proposed method with existing methods, such as FedTracker, which could have provided a clearer picture of its advantages and disadvantages.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors clarify the role and necessity of the decoder in the watermarking process, and provide a detailed explanation of how it enhances the robustness of the watermark?
- How does the proposed method handle the scalability issues in large-scale federated learning systems, particularly in terms of computational overhead and the number of clients?
- Can the authors provide a more detailed comparison with existing methods, such as FedTracker, to highlight the advantages of their proposed method?
- How does the proposed method ensure the robustness of the watermarking scheme against advanced attacks, such as parameter perturbations and model extraction attacks?
- Could the authors discuss the potential collisions between watermarks and their impact on the accuracy of identifying infringing clients?
