 **Summary:**
The paper introduces Large Content and Behavior Models (LCBMs) to address the effectiveness level in communication, focusing on the prediction and optimization of communication to achieve desired receiver behaviors. It leverages behavior tokens from YouTube to train models, showing improvements in behavior simulation, content simulation, and behavior understanding tasks. The LCBM framework is demonstrated to perform well in various tasks, with a focus on content and behavior prediction. The paper also introduces the Content Behavior Corpus (CBC) to facilitate further research in this area. Despite its innovative approach, the paper is criticized for its lack of clarity in methodology and presentation, and the absence of comparisons with state-of-the-art models.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, clear, and easy to follow, with a clear and concise presentation of the methodology and results.
- The problem of communication effectiveness is well-motivated and relevant, particularly in the context of current challenges in communication systems.
- The introduction of behavior tokens in training corpora is a novel and interesting approach, providing a new perspective on optimizing communication for desired receiver behaviors and intents.
- The paper demonstrates the effectiveness of the proposed method through a series of tasks, showing the model's ability to understand and predict behavior in different contexts.
- The Content Behavior Corpus (CBC) is a valuable resource for further research in this area, providing a comprehensive dataset for training and evaluating models.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a clear comparison with state-of-the-art models, particularly in terms of model architecture and performance.
- The methodology and training process are not sufficiently detailed, making it difficult to fully understand the experimental setup and results.
- The paper does not adequately address the limitations of the proposed method, such as its applicability to different types of data and the potential impact of data quality on model performance.
- There is a lack of clarity in the presentation, with some figures and tables being difficult to interpret, and some references and citations being missing or incorrect.
- The paper does not provide a comprehensive evaluation of the model's performance on different types of data, which could affect the generalizability of the results.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors provide a more detailed comparison with state-of-the-art models, including a discussion on the advantages and disadvantages of the proposed method compared to existing approaches?
- How does the model perform when trained on different types of data, such as social media interactions versus email communications?
- Can the authors clarify the training process and the specifics of the model architecture, including the number of layers and the types of layers used?
- How were the hyperparameters for the model determined, and what impact do these parameters have on the model's performance?
- Could the authors provide more detailed explanations for the results shown in the tables and figures, particularly in terms of the data and methods used?
- How does the model handle different types of behavior tokens, such as those from different platforms or domains, and what impact does this have on the model's performance?
