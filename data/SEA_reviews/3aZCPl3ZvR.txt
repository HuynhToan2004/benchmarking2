 **Summary:**
The paper investigates the robustness of Sharpness-Aware Minimization (SAM) under label noise, particularly focusing on the effects of SAM on the logit scale and Jacobian terms in both linear and non-linear models. The authors demonstrate that SAM's robustness is not solely due to the logit scale term, as previously believed, but also significantly influenced by the Jacobian term, which induces implicit regularization effects. This study provides theoretical insights and empirical evidence to support these findings, suggesting that SAM's robustness under label noise is not only due to its preference for flat minima but also its ability to up-weight gradients of low-loss points.

**Strengths:**
- The paper provides a detailed analysis of the logit and Jacobian terms in SAM, which are crucial for understanding the robustness of SAM under label noise.
- The authors demonstrate that SAM's robustness is not solely due to the logit scale term but also significantly influenced by the Jacobian term, which induces implicit regularization effects.
- The paper presents theoretical insights and empirical evidence to support the findings, enhancing the understanding of SAM's behavior under label noise.
- The experiments are well-designed and conducted, showing a clear preference for flat minima and robustness to label noise.
- The paper is well-written and easy to follow, making it accessible to a broad audience.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a clear explanation of why the Jacobian term is more important than the logit scale term in explaining SAM's robustness under label noise.
- The analysis primarily focuses on 1-SAM, which may not be the most effective variant of SAM in practice, and the results may not generalize to other variants like n-SAM.
- The paper does not adequately compare the performance of SAM with other state-of-the-art methods under label noise, which could help in validating the claims made.
- The experimental setup and results, particularly in the nonlinear setting, are not convincingly explained or supported, which could undermine the credibility of the findings.
- The paper does not address the limitations of its work, such as the potential impact of different hyperparameter settings or the generalizability of the findings to other types of neural networks.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors clarify why the Jacobian term is more important than the logit scale term in explaining SAM's robustness under label noise?
- How does the proposed regularization method compare to other state-of-the-art methods under label noise, and what are the implications of these comparisons for the practical utility of SAM?
- Could the authors provide more detailed experimental results, particularly in the nonlinear setting, to support the claims made about the importance of the Jacobian term?
- How do the findings of this study generalize to other types of neural networks, such as those with different architectures or training methods?
- Can the authors address the potential limitations of their work, such as the impact of different hyperparameter settings or the generalizability of the findings to other types of data or noise conditions?
