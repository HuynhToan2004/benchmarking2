 **Summary:**
The paper introduces Solis-seg, a deep neural network optimized for detecting and segmenting solar farms in satellite imagery. It leverages Neural Architecture Search (NAS) and semantic segmentation techniques to achieve high accuracy and efficiency. The model is evaluated against various datasets and shows promising results, outperforming existing solutions in terms of accuracy and computational efficiency. However, concerns about the novelty and depth of the NAS approach, as well as the limited scope of the evaluation, are highlighted.

**Strengths:**
- The paper addresses a significant and timely issue of accurately mapping and monitoring solar farms, which is crucial for achieving net-zero emissions by 2050.
- The use of Neural Architecture Search (NAS) to find the optimal network architecture for solar farm detection and segmentation is innovative and well-explained.
- The methodology is well-documented, making it easy to follow and replicate the research.
- The paper is well-written and easy to understand, which facilitates comprehension and application of the findings.
- The experimental results are promising, demonstrating the effectiveness of the proposed method in detecting and segmenting solar farms in satellite imagery.
- The application of advanced machine learning techniques for solar farm detection is highlighted, which could encourage further exploration in earth observation and sustainability.

**Weaknesses:**
- The novelty of the NAS approach is questionable, as it is not clear how much the results are influenced by the NAS technique itself or by the use of specific datasets.
- The paper lacks a comprehensive comparison with state-of-the-art models, which could provide a clearer picture of the model's performance relative to existing solutions.
- The evaluation is limited to a single dataset, which might not sufficiently demonstrate the model's generalizability and robustness across different datasets.
- The paper does not adequately discuss the limitations of the proposed method, which could help in understanding the potential drawbacks and areas for improvement.
- The computational cost and efficiency of the model are not thoroughly discussed, which is crucial for practical deployment and scalability.
- The paper could benefit from a more detailed discussion on the impact of different data sources and pre-processing techniques on the model's performance.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors provide a more detailed explanation of how the NAS approach was implemented and how it influenced the model's performance?
- How does the model perform on other datasets, and what are the implications of using different datasets for training and evaluation?
- What are the specific computational costs and efficiency metrics of the model, and how do these compare to other state-of-the-art models?
- How does the model perform under different conditions, such as varying image quality or different solar farm configurations?
- Can the authors provide more details on the dataset used, including its size, diversity, and representativeness of real-world solar farm configurations?
- How does the model handle false positives and false negatives in the detection and segmentation of solar farms, and what are the implications of these errors in practical applications?
