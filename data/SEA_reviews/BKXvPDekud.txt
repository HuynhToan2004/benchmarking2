 **Summary:**
The paper introduces CellPLM, a novel pre-trained model designed for single-cell data analysis, leveraging a transformer-based architecture to model cell-cell relationships. The model integrates both scRNA-seq and spatially-resolved transcriptomic data, utilizing a Gaussian mixture prior to enhance the model's performance. The authors claim that CellPLM outperforms existing methods on various downstream tasks, demonstrating its effectiveness in handling single-cell data. The paper also addresses the challenges posed by the limited quantity and quality of single-cell data, and the complex cell-cell interactions, which are often overlooked in traditional models.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel pre-trained model, CellPLM, which takes a unique approach by treating cells as tokens and tissues as sentences, utilizing spatially-resolved transcriptomic data to facilitate learning cell-cell relationships.
- CellPLM is the first single-cell pre-trained transformer that encodes cell-cell relations, leveraging spatially-resolved transcriptome data, and incorporates a Gaussian mixture prior distribution to address data limitations.
- The paper is well-written, clear, and easy to follow, with a comprehensive evaluation demonstrating the effectiveness of the proposed method.
- The model's performance is validated through various downstream tasks, showing that CellPLM outperforms existing pre-trained and non-pre-trained models, and achieves 500x times higher inference speed on generating cell embeddings compared to existing pre-trained models.
- The paper addresses the limitations of existing pre-trained models by highlighting the differences between single-cell data and natural language data, particularly focusing on the bag-of-genes approach and the intricate cell-cell relationships.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a thorough comparison with other large language models (LLMs) in terms of training data size, model size, and performance metrics, which could provide a more robust validation of the proposed model.
- The paper does not discuss the limitations of the model in detail, which could help in understanding the scope and applicability of the proposed method.
- The model's training time and computational requirements are not clearly stated, which could impact the practical implementation of the model.
- The paper does not provide a detailed analysis of the computational complexity or memory requirements of the proposed model, which could be crucial for understanding its scalability and efficiency.
- The paper lacks a detailed discussion on the impact of different hyperparameters on the model's performance, which could help in optimizing the model's settings for different datasets.

**Questions:**
- Can you provide a comparison of the training data size, model size, and performance metrics with other LLMs to validate the effectiveness of CellPLM?
- How does the model perform when trained on different types of single-cell data, such as different cell types or different tissues?
- What are the limitations of the proposed model, and how do these limitations impact the model's performance and applicability?
- How does the model handle the computational complexity and memory requirements, and what are the implications for scalability and efficiency?
- Could you provide more details on the training time and computational requirements of the model, and how these impact the practical implementation of the model?
- How do the different hyperparameters of the model affect the model's performance, and what are the optimal settings for different datasets?
