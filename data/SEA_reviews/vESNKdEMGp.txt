 **Summary:**
The paper explores the vulnerability of large language models (LLMs) to jailbreak attacks in multilingual settings, specifically focusing on low-resource languages. The authors demonstrate that LLMs, such as ChatGPT and GPT-4, are susceptible to jailbreaks when prompted with non-English queries, which can lead to unsafe outputs. To address this issue, a novel framework named Self-Defense is proposed, which generates multilingual safety training data for fine-tuning LLMs. This framework aims to enhance the safety of LLMs by automatically generating multilingual safety training data. The paper provides extensive experimental results and analysis, highlighting the need for more robust safety measures in LLMs, particularly in multilingual contexts.

**Strengths:**
- The paper addresses a timely and significant issue of multilingual jailbreak challenges in large language models (LLMs), which is crucial for ensuring the safety and reliability of AI systems.
- The study is well-structured, with a clear introduction and motivation, and the experiments are well-designed, providing a thorough analysis of the problem.
- The paper introduces a novel framework, Self-Defense, which is a valuable contribution to the field, aiming to enhance the multilingual safety capabilities of LLMs.
- The dataset created for this study is a valuable resource for further research on jailbreak challenges in LLMs.
- The experiments are extensive, covering various scenarios, and the results are convincing, demonstrating the effectiveness of the proposed framework.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed discussion on the limitations of the proposed framework, particularly how it addresses the trade-off between safety and usefulness.
- The paper's claims about the effectiveness of the Self-Defense framework in improving safety are not sufficiently supported by quantitative data, making it difficult to assess the framework's true impact.
- The paper does not adequately address the issue of noisy translations, which could affect the accuracy of the results and the overall effectiveness of the framework.
- There is a lack of discussion on how the proposed framework could be adapted or improved to address potential limitations or biases in the translation process.
- The paper's claims about the effectiveness of the Self-Defense framework in reducing unsafe responses are not convincingly supported by quantitative data, and the methodology for evaluating the framework's effectiveness is not clearly described.
- The paper does not discuss the potential impact of the proposed framework on the generalization capabilities of LLMs, which is a critical aspect of safety in AI systems.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors clarify the methodology used to evaluate the effectiveness of the Self-Defense framework, particularly how the trade-off between safety and usefulness was measured?
- How does the Self-Defense framework address the issue of noisy translations, and what steps were taken to ensure the accuracy and reliability of the translations used in the study?
- Given the potential for bias in machine translation, how does the Self-Defense framework ensure that the translated instructions do not introduce additional biases or errors?
- Could the authors provide more detailed quantitative data to support their claims about the effectiveness of the Self-Defense framework in reducing unsafe responses?
- How does the Self-Defense framework handle the issue of low-resource languages, which are known to be more susceptible to jailbreak attacks?
- Could the authors discuss the potential impact of the proposed framework on the generalization capabilities of LLMs, and how this might affect the safety and reliability of the AI systems that use these models?
