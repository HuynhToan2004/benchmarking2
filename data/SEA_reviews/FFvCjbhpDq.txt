 **Summary:**
The paper addresses the issue of forgetting pre-trained capabilities in reinforcement learning (RL) models during fine-tuning, focusing on two scenarios: state coverage gap and imperfect cloning gap. It proposes knowledge retention techniques to mitigate forgetting and demonstrates their effectiveness through experiments in various environments including robotic tasks and games like Montezuma's Revenge and NetHack. The paper also explores the forgetting phenomenon in detail, providing a conceptualization and analysis that could be beneficial for the community. However, the paper is criticized for its limited scope in terms of environments and the generalizability of its findings.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, clear, and easy to follow, with a comprehensive discussion of the forgetting phenomenon in RL, including detailed analysis and visualizations that enhance understanding.
- The authors have conducted extensive experiments to investigate the forgetting problem, including in a variety of environments, and have proposed several methods to mitigate this issue, demonstrating the effectiveness of these methods.
- The paper provides a thorough examination of the forgetting phenomenon, particularly in the context of RL, and offers a detailed analysis that could be beneficial for the community.
- The paper is original in its focus on the forgetting problem in RL, which is a significant issue in the field, and it presents a novel approach to addressing this problem through knowledge retention techniques.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper is limited in its scope, focusing only on a few environments (RoboticSequence, Montezuma's Revenge, and NetHack) and not exploring a broader range of tasks or environments, which could limit the generalizability of the findings.
- The paper lacks a comprehensive discussion on the limitations of the proposed methods and the potential negative societal impacts of forgetting in RL models.
- The experiments presented are somewhat limited in scope, with the same methods being tested across multiple environments without sufficient variation to demonstrate the robustness of the findings.
- There is a lack of discussion on how the proposed methods compare to existing approaches in the literature, which could help in positioning the work within the broader context of RL research.
- The paper could benefit from a more detailed discussion on the practical implications of the forgetting problem in RL, particularly in terms of real-world applications and the potential for negative societal impacts.
