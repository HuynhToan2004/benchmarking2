 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a new benchmark, CriticBench, aimed at evaluating the critique abilities of large language models (LLMs) across various tasks such as math problem-solving, code completion, and question answering. The benchmark consists of 3K high-quality natural language queries and corresponding model responses, annotated for correctness. The paper presents a unified evaluation framework and a new capacity, the self-check method, which leverages self-critique to improve task performance. The study explores the critique abilities of LLMs, revealing that even top-performing models struggle with self-critique and that critique accuracy tends to be lower where models are most uncertain. The paper also introduces a self-check baseline that utilizes self-critique to enhance model performance.

**Strengths:**
- The paper addresses an important and timely topic, focusing on the evaluation of large language models (LLMs) in terms of their critique abilities, which is a significant gap in current research.
- The authors introduce a new benchmark, CriticBench, which is comprehensive and covers a diverse set of tasks, providing a valuable resource for future research.
- The paper is well-written, clear, and easy to follow, with a logical structure that facilitates understanding.
- The proposed self-check method is simple yet effective, demonstrating a potential for enhancing model performance through self-critique.
- The study includes a detailed analysis of the critique abilities of LLMs, revealing insights such as the emergence of critique abilities only in sufficiently large models and the correlation between critique accuracy and model uncertainty.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a thorough discussion on the limitations of the self-check method and its potential impact on the evaluation of LLMs.
- The evaluation of the self-check method is limited to a single dataset (GSM8K), which may not generalize well to other datasets or tasks.
- The novelty of the self-check method is questioned, as it appears to be a simple application of existing techniques without significant innovation.
- The paper does not adequately address the potential for overfitting due to the use of the same model for both generation and evaluation, which could affect the reliability of the results.
- The paper does not provide sufficient evidence or examples to support some of the claims, such as the correlation between critique ability and model size or the effectiveness of the self-check method across different datasets.
- There is a lack of discussion on how the self-check method could be improved or adapted for different tasks or datasets.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors provide more details on the limitations of the self-check method and how these might affect the evaluation of LLMs?
- How does the self-check method perform when applied to different datasets or tasks beyond those evaluated in the paper?
- Could the authors elaborate on the potential for overfitting due to the use of the same model for both generation and evaluation, and how this was addressed in the study?
- How does the self-check method compare to other baseline methods, and what are the implications of using different models for generation and evaluation?
- Can the authors provide more examples or evidence to support the claims made about the correlation between critique ability and model size, and the effectiveness of the self-check method in improving model performance?
