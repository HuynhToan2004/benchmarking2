 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel approach to multimodal named entity recognition (MNER) using prompt-based question-answering (QA) tasks. It leverages modern large language models (LLMs) to recognize entities in text by integrating visual cues from images. The methodology involves constructing prompts that guide LLMs to identify entities within text, using visual context to enhance the model's performance. The paper presents experiments using zero-shot learning on two publicly available datasets, demonstrating some effectiveness but also highlighting limitations and the need for further dataset diversity. The approach, while innovative, faces challenges in ensuring the visual context is effectively utilized and in managing the complexity introduced by the need for unique prompts per entity type.

**Strengths:**
- The paper addresses a significant problem in the field of multimodal named entity recognition (MNER) by utilizing visual cues from images, which is a novel approach.
- The proposed method demonstrates promising results in zero-shot learning on publicly available datasets, showing some effectiveness in enhancing MNER performance.
- The paper provides a detailed analysis of the interplay between model design, prompt crafting, and training data characteristics, which is crucial for understanding the complexities involved in visual integration.
- The experimental results, while not perfect, are promising, indicating potential for further research and development in this area.
- The paper is well-written, making it easy to follow and understand, which is essential for academic publications.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a comprehensive evaluation, particularly in terms of dataset diversity and the robustness of the results across different datasets and model sizes.
- The effectiveness of the proposed method is not convincingly demonstrated, with some results showing marginal improvements or even decreased performance in certain scenarios.
- The paper does not adequately address the challenges associated with prompt engineering, particularly the complexity introduced by the need for unique prompts per entity type.
- The experimental setup is limited to zero-shot learning on two specific datasets, which may not fully represent the generalizability of the proposed method.
- There is a lack of discussion on how the visual context is effectively integrated into the model, and the paper does not provide sufficient evidence to support the claims made about the effectiveness of visual integration.
- The paper could benefit from a more detailed discussion on the limitations of the proposed method and a more comprehensive evaluation using additional datasets and models.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors provide more detailed results on how the proposed method performs across different datasets and model sizes?
- How does the proposed method compare to other existing methods in terms of performance and efficiency?
- What are the specific challenges associated with integrating visual context into MNER, and how does the proposed method address these challenges?
- Can the authors clarify the discrepancies in performance observed across different entity types and datasets?
- How does the paper ensure the robustness of the results against potential biases or confounding factors?
- Could the authors elaborate on the potential of the proposed method in real-world applications and discuss any potential societal impacts?
