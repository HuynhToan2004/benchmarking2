 **Summary:**
The paper introduces ResolvNet, a novel graph neural network architecture designed to address the issue of multi-scale consistency in graph learning. It focuses on the challenges posed by strongly connected subgraphs and the presence of bottlenecks in traditional GNNs, which can hinder information flow and propagation. The authors propose a theoretical framework and empirical evidence to demonstrate that ResolvNet can effectively handle these issues by ensuring consistent feature vectors across different graph resolutions. The paper also provides a comprehensive theoretical analysis and experimental results, showcasing the architecture's ability to maintain information consistency and improve performance in various graph-level tasks.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel concept of multi-scale consistency, which is crucial for handling multi-scale graphs effectively.
- The proposed ResolvNet architecture is innovative, providing a solid theoretical foundation and empirical evidence to support its effectiveness.
- The paper is well-written, clear, and easy to follow, with a comprehensive theoretical analysis and empirical results.
- The experiments conducted are extensive, covering various datasets and tasks, which demonstrates the robustness and applicability of the proposed method.
- The paper addresses a significant problem in graph learning by focusing on the impact of strongly connected subgraphs on information flow, which is a less explored area in the field.
- The proposed method shows good performance in real-world datasets, indicating its practical utility.

**Weaknesses:**
