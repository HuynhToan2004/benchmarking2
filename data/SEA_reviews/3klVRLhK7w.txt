 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel online continual learning method called Layer freezing and Similarity-Aware Retrieval (L-SAR), which optimizes the computational and memory budgets by selectively freezing layers and employing a frequency-based memory retrieval system. This approach aims to enhance the efficiency of online continual learning by balancing the computational and memory demands. The methodology is validated through extensive experiments on various datasets, including CIFAR-10/100, CLEAR-10, and ImageNet-1K, demonstrating significant improvements in accuracy and efficiency compared to existing methods. Despite its innovative approach, the paper is criticized for its limited novelty, insufficient comparisons with state-of-the-art methods, and the lack of comprehensive ablation studies.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel approach to online continual learning by addressing the computational and memory budgets, which is a significant contribution to the field.
- The proposed method of adaptive layer freezing and frequency-based memory retrieval is well-articulated and provides a clear explanation of its implementation.
- The paper is well-structured, making it easy to follow, and the presentation is clear, enhancing the understanding of the proposed methodology.
- Extensive empirical validations are conducted to demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed method, showcasing improvements in accuracy and efficiency compared to existing methods.
- The paper provides a comprehensive analysis of the computational and memory costs of various continual learning algorithms, which is crucial for understanding the practical implications of different methods.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed discussion on the novelty of the proposed method compared to existing literature, particularly in the areas of layer freezing and similarity-aware retrieval.
- There is an insufficient comparison with state-of-the-art methods, which could undermine the perceived effectiveness of the proposed method.
- The paper does not provide a detailed ablation study, which is necessary to understand the individual contributions of each component of the proposed method.
- The computational cost of the proposed method, particularly in terms of the number of forward and backward passes required, is not adequately addressed, which could impact the practical applicability of the method.
- The paper could benefit from a more thorough discussion on the limitations and potential negative societal impacts of the proposed method.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors provide a more detailed comparison with state-of-the-art methods, including a discussion on how the proposed method outperforms or complements these existing approaches?
- How does the proposed method perform when compared to other baselines that also employ layer freezing and similarity-aware retrieval?
- Can the authors provide a more detailed analysis of the computational cost of the proposed method, including the number of forward and backward passes required?
- Is there a possibility to include a detailed ablation study to understand the individual contributions of each component of the proposed method?
- How does the proposed method handle the trade-off between computational efficiency and model accuracy, particularly in scenarios where computational resources are limited?
