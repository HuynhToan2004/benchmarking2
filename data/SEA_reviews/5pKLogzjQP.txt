 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel pre-training purification method for perturbative availability poisoning attacks, leveraging a disentangled variational autoencoder (D-VAE) to separate clean data from poisoned data. The methodology involves a two-stage purification process, where the first stage suppresses poison patterns and the second stage refines the results to ensure effectiveness and robustness. The paper provides theoretical insights into the effectiveness of the proposed method and demonstrates its superior performance over existing defenses across various datasets such as CIFAR-10, CIFAR-100, and a subset of ImageNet. Despite its strengths in theoretical analysis and empirical validation, the paper faces criticism for its lack of clarity in presentation, potential inaccuracies in threat modeling, and insufficient comparison with state-of-the-art methods.

**Strengths:**
- The paper provides a novel disentanglement mechanism to build an efficient pre-training purification method for perturbative availability poisoning attacks.
- Theoretical analysis is conducted to explain the effectiveness of the proposed method, offering a deeper understanding of its workings.
- The paper is well-organized, making it easy to follow, and the presentation is clear and concise.
- Extensive experiments demonstrate the remarkable performance of the proposed method across various datasets, including CIFAR-10, CIFAR-100, and a subset of ImageNet.
- The method is simple and effective, with a two-stage purification approach that ensures effectiveness and robustness.
- The paper is well-written, with a clear structure and detailed explanations of the proposed method and its theoretical underpinnings.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks clarity in its presentation, particularly in explaining the threat model and the specifics of the proposed method.
- The theoretical analysis and proofs provided in the paper are not convincing, with some sections appearing incomplete or incorrect.
- The paper's contribution to the field is not clearly defined, and the methodology does not sufficiently differentiate itself from existing works.
- There is a lack of detailed comparison with state-of-the-art methods, which limits the understanding of the proposed method's effectiveness and novelty.
- The paper's threat model and the specifics of the poisoning attacks are not clearly defined, which could limit the generalizability of the results.
- The paper does not address the limitations of its approach, which could include the potential for overfitting or the need for large amounts of clean data for effective purification.

**Questions:**
- Can you clarify the threat model and the specifics of the poisoning attacks that the proposed method aims to counter?
- How does the proposed method differ from existing works in terms of its approach and effectiveness?
- Could you provide more detailed comparisons with state-of-the-art methods to better establish the novelty and effectiveness of your approach?
- What are the specifics of the poisoning process, and how does the proposed method handle different types of poisoning attacks?
- How does the method handle the potential for overfitting, especially in scenarios where the amount of clean data is limited?
- Can you provide more details on the experimental setup, particularly how the results were validated and how the effectiveness of the purification process was measured?
