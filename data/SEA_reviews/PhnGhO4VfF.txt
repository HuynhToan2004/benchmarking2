 **Summary:**
The paper investigates the impact of pretraining label granularity on the generalization of deep neural networks in image classification tasks. It empirically demonstrates that pretraining on leaf labels of ImageNet21k enhances transfer results on ImageNet1k compared to other coarser granularity levels. Theoretically, the paper explains the benefits of fine-grained pretraining by showing how it aids in learning "rarer" or "fine-grained" features, which improve accuracy on challenging test samples. The study also emphasizes the importance of a meaningful label hierarchy and label function alignment between the source and target tasks for effective fine-grained training. The paper's theoretical and empirical insights contribute to a better understanding of how pretraining label granularity influences the performance of deep neural networks in image classification tasks.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, making it easy to follow and understand the arguments presented.
- The problem of understanding the influence of pretraining label granularity on the generalization of deep neural networks in image classification tasks is both interesting and relevant.
- The paper provides a comprehensive theoretical analysis of the impact of pretraining label granularity on the generalization of deep neural networks, which is supported by empirical results.
- The paper is well-organized, with clear and detailed explanations of the theoretical analysis and empirical results.
- The experiments conducted are comprehensive, covering different datasets and architectures, which supports the validity of the conclusions drawn.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper's theoretical analysis and empirical results are not well-aligned, particularly in the definition of "rarer" or "fine-grained" features.
- The paper assumes that the label hierarchy in the target dataset is the root of the hierarchy, which may not be practical in real-world scenarios.
- The paper does not consider the transfer scenario where there is a nontrivial distribution shift between the source and target datasets, which could affect the generalizability of the findings.
- The paper's experimental results are not very convincing, with some results (e.g., in Figure 4) being counter-intuitive or not well-explained.
- The paper lacks a discussion on the limitations of the work, which is crucial for understanding the scope and applicability of the findings.
- The paper could benefit from a more detailed discussion on the scalability of fine-grained label acquisition and its impact on the practical implementation of the findings.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors clarify the definition of "rarer" or "fine-grained" features and how they relate to the theoretical analysis?
- How does the paper's theoretical analysis and empirical results align, particularly in the context of the label granularity's impact on the generalization of deep neural networks?
- Could the authors provide a more detailed explanation of the results in Figure 4, especially the counter-intuitive findings regarding the performance of models trained on different label hierarchies?
- How do the authors address the scalability issues in acquiring fine-grained labels, and what are the implications of this for the practical implementation of the findings?
- Can the authors provide more details on the experimental setup, particularly the choice of models and datasets, to ensure the reproducibility and generalizability of the results?
