 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel method for handling data that evolves over time, proposing a time-varying propensity score to address gradual shifts in data distributions. This score is estimated using a logistic regression model trained on a modified dataset, which includes time-varying labels. The method is tested across various applications, including supervised learning and reinforcement learning, and is shown to outperform baseline methods in some scenarios. However, concerns are raised about the clarity of the experimental setup, the robustness of the results, and the theoretical justification of the method's effectiveness.

**Strengths:**
- The paper addresses a significant and relevant problem in the field of machine learning, focusing on the handling of data that evolves over time.
- The introduction of a time-varying propensity score is innovative and provides a novel approach to handling data shifts, which is well-explained and supported by theoretical underpinnings.
- The paper is well-written, making it easy to follow, and includes a comprehensive literature review, enhancing its contribution to the field.
- The methodology is clearly described, and the paper provides a good theoretical justification for the proposed method.
- The paper is original in its approach to handling data shifts, which is a significant contribution to the field of machine learning.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks clarity in its experimental setup, particularly in the description of the data generation process and the training details, which could lead to confusion about the experimental results.
- The results presented in the paper are not consistently strong, and the performance of the proposed method is sometimes not superior to existing methods.
- There is a lack of rigorous theoretical justification for the proposed method, which could limit the understanding and acceptance of the proposed approach.
- The paper does not sufficiently address the limitations of its method, which could affect the reliability and applicability of the proposed approach.
- The paper does not adequately compare its method with existing approaches, particularly in scenarios where the method might not perform as well.
- The paper could benefit from a more detailed discussion on the choice of baseline methods and the rationale behind the experimental design.
