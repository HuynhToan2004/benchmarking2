 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel method for training energy-based models (EBMs) by proposing a self-normalized log-likelihood (SNL) objective, which includes an additional learnable parameter representing the normalization constant. This approach, which is based on the variational representation of the logarithm, allows for the estimation of the normalization constant, thereby avoiding the need for MCMC sampling. The SNL objective is shown to be a lower bound of the log-likelihood and is concave in the model parameters for exponential family distributions. The paper validates the effectiveness of the proposed method through experiments on various density estimation tasks and EBMs for regression, demonstrating its ability to outperform existing techniques.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel approach to training energy-based models (EBMs) by optimizing the logarithm of the normalization constant, which is a significant challenge in EBM training.
- The authors provide a detailed theoretical analysis of the proposed self-normalized log-likelihood (SNL) objective, demonstrating its concavity for exponential family distributions and its connection to information geometry.
- The paper is well-written, clear, and easy to follow, with a comprehensive experimental evaluation that demonstrates the effectiveness of the proposed method across various tasks and datasets.
- The introduction of a new objective for training EBMs, which is a lower bound of the log-likelihood, is a significant contribution to the field.
- The paper provides a solid theoretical foundation and practical implementation details, making it accessible and useful for both theoretical and practical applications.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper could benefit from a more thorough discussion on the limitations of the proposed method, particularly in scenarios where the normalization constant might not be well-behaved or where the method might not be applicable.
- The experimental results are somewhat limited in scope, focusing primarily on low-dimensional tasks and not including more complex or high-dimensional datasets.
- The paper lacks a detailed comparison with other methods for training EBMs, such as those using MCMC sampling or other optimization techniques.
- There is a need for more clarity in the presentation of certain mathematical formulations and proofs, particularly in the derivation of the SNL objective and its relationship to the true log-likelihood.
- The paper could benefit from a more comprehensive literature review, particularly in the context of related works on energy-based models and variational inference.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors elaborate on the choice of the proposal distribution and how it affects the performance of the proposed method?
- How does the proposed method compare to other recent works on training EBMs, such as those using MCMC sampling or other optimization techniques?
- In the context of the proposed method, how does the normalization constant behave in different scenarios, and what are the implications for the training and sampling processes?
- Can the authors provide more details on the computational efficiency and scalability of the proposed method, particularly in high-dimensional or more complex datasets?
- How does the proposed method relate to other approaches that aim to bypass the need for normalization constants in EBMs, such as those using score-matching or other optimization techniques?
