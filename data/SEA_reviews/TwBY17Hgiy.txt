 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel multi-task learning method that integrates 3D-aware regularization to enhance the performance of multiple computer vision tasks, including depth estimation, boundary detection, and surface normal estimation. This approach leverages a 3D-aware regularizer to project features from an image encoder into a shared 3D feature space, which is then decoded into task-specific outputs using differentiable rendering. The method is tested on datasets like NYUv2 and PASCAL-Context, showing improvements over existing methods. The approach is claimed to be architecture-agnostic and does not add inference costs, making it potentially broadly applicable.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, easy to follow, and the experiments are thorough, demonstrating the effectiveness of the proposed method across various tasks and datasets.
- The introduction of 3D-aware regularization is innovative and effectively enhances the performance of multi-task learning, particularly in scenarios where depth estimation, boundary detection, and surface normal estimation are critical.
- The method is described as architecture-agnostic and does not introduce additional inference costs, which makes it potentially broadly applicable.
- The experimental results show improvements over existing methods, and the paper includes a variety of ablation studies to validate the effectiveness of the proposed components.

**Weaknesses:**
- The novelty of the approach is somewhat limited, as similar ideas have been explored in previous works, such as those using 3D regularization for depth estimation.
- The paper lacks a comprehensive discussion on related works, particularly those that have explored similar ideas, which could have provided a better context for the current research.
- The paper could benefit from a more detailed explanation of the technical contributions, especially how the 3D-aware regularization works and its specific advantages over existing methods.
- The paper's experimental setup and results, such as the comparison with InvPT and the performance on different datasets, are not convincingly superior to existing methods.
- There are concerns about the generalizability of the proposed method, particularly in scenarios where the training data is limited or not diverse enough.

**Questions:**
- How does the proposed method compare to previous works that have explored similar ideas, particularly in terms of performance and technical contributions?
- Can the authors provide more detailed explanations of the 3D-aware regularization and its technical advantages over existing methods?
- How does the proposed method handle the potential issues of overfitting or noisy correlations in the feature space, especially given the complexity of the tasks involved?
- Could the authors clarify the experimental setup, particularly the training and testing data used, and provide more detailed results to support the claims of performance improvements?
- How does the method perform when applied to other datasets or tasks beyond those tested, and what are the implications for its generalizability and applicability?
