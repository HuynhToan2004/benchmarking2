 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel method for predicting reactive motions in close interactions between two people using a transformer-based model, TopoFormer. This model employs a topology-aware spatio-temporal embedding and spatial relation-aware multi-headed self-attention to enhance the learning of latent representations of close interactions. The model is evaluated on two datasets, ExPI and CHI3D, and shows significant improvements over existing methods, particularly in reducing interpenetration and improving motion quality. The approach is highlighted by its ability to effectively model spatial relations and prevent implausible motions, which is crucial for realistic motion prediction in close interactions.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, making it easy to follow and understand.
- The proposed method, TopoFormer, effectively models spatial relations and prevents implausible motions, which is crucial for realistic motion prediction in close interactions.
- The method achieves state-of-the-art (SOTA) performance in terms of alignment mean error (AME) and average interpenetration per frame (AIF) across multiple datasets and metrics.
- The paper introduces a novel method for predicting reactive motion in close interactions, which is a significant contribution to the field.
- The results are convincing, demonstrating the effectiveness of the proposed method.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed explanation of the topology-aware spatio-temporal embedding, which is a key component of the proposed method.
- There is a need for more clarity in the description of the topological structure used in the method, particularly in Figure 3.
- The paper does not provide sufficient details on the training and inference time of the proposed method, which could be crucial for practical applications.
- The methodology section could be improved by providing more details on the computational aspects of the method, such as the number of parameters and computational complexity.
- The paper could benefit from a more detailed discussion on the limitations of the proposed method.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors provide more details on the topology-aware spatio-temporal embedding and the topological structure used in the method?
- How does the method handle the different interaction classes in the datasets, and what are the implications for the model's performance?
- Could the authors provide a more detailed explanation of the SR-MSA block and its contributions to the overall performance of the model?
- How does the method handle the issue of implausible motions, and what are the specific mechanisms in place to prevent these?
- What are the specific challenges faced in modeling close interactions, and how does the method address these challenges?
