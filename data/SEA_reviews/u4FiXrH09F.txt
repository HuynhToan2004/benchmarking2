 **Summary:**
The paper introduces IDGNN, an implicit neural network for dynamic graphs that addresses the challenges of over-smoothing and capturing long-range dependencies in dynamic graphs. It leverages a bilevel optimization algorithm to train the model efficiently and demonstrates the method's effectiveness through extensive experiments on real-world datasets. The paper also discusses the theoretical underpinnings of the model, including the proof of its well-posedness and the derivation of an efficient training algorithm. Despite its theoretical contributions and experimental validations, the paper has been criticized for its presentation clarity and the limited novelty of its approach compared to existing implicit GNNs.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces IDGNN, a novel implicit neural network for dynamic graphs, which addresses the challenges of over-smoothing and capturing long-range dependencies in dynamic graphs.
- The methodology of IDGNN is supported by a theoretical proof of its well-posedness, enhancing the credibility of the proposed model.
- The paper conducts extensive experiments on real-world datasets, demonstrating the superiority of IDGNN over state-of-the-art baseline approaches.
- The paper provides a clear and comprehensive explanation of the IDGNN model, its training algorithm, and its performance, making it accessible and understandable to readers.
- The authors effectively compare IDGNN with existing models and demonstrate its advantages, contributing to the understanding and validation of the proposed method.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper suffers from presentation issues, particularly in the organization and clarity of the content, which could be improved for better readability and comprehension.
- The novelty of the IDGNN model is questioned as it appears to be a simple extension of existing implicit GNNs, with limited technical innovation.
- The paper's experimental results, while extensive, are not sufficiently convincing due to the lack of comparison with more advanced dynamic GNNs and the absence of results on larger, more complex datasets.
- There are several instances of typographical errors and unclear explanations in the paper, which detract from its professional quality.
- The paper does not adequately discuss the limitations of the IDGNN model, which could provide valuable insights for future research and improvements.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors provide a clearer explanation of the theoretical underpinnings of IDGNN, particularly the derivation of the implicit function theorem and the proof of Theorem 2?
- How does IDGNN handle the inductive setting, and can it be extended to handle multiple layers for each time stamp?
- Can the authors provide a more detailed comparison with other dynamic GNNs, including more recent and advanced models?
- How does IDGNN perform on larger and more complex datasets, and what are the implications of its performance on such datasets?
- Can the authors clarify the role and impact of the V parameter in the model, and discuss its significance in the context of the IDGNN model?
- Could the authors elaborate on the limitations of IDGNN and suggest potential directions for future research to address these limitations?
