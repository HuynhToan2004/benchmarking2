 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel approach to robust contextual stochastic optimization by discretizing the feasible region and approximating the objective function within each subset. This method aims to minimize the probability of incurring regret above a specified threshold, thereby balancing both average and worst-case costs. Theoretical analysis and empirical results are provided to support the effectiveness of this approach, demonstrating its competitiveness against other end-to-end learning methods and robust optimization methods. The paper also discusses the computational aspects and the practical implications of the proposed method.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, making it easy to follow, and the methodology is clearly explained, making it accessible to a broad audience.
- The approach of discretizing the feasible region and approximating the objective function within each subset is innovative and provides a novel perspective on robust optimization.
- The paper includes both theoretical analysis and empirical results, which support the effectiveness of the proposed method.
- The paper is well-organized, with a clear introduction, theoretical analysis, and experimental results that demonstrate the practical applicability of the method.
- The paper discusses the computational aspects of the proposed method, which is a significant contribution to the field.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a thorough discussion on the limitations of the proposed method, which could help in understanding its applicability and effectiveness in different scenarios.
- The experimental setup is limited to specific datasets and does not include a broader range of benchmarks, which could enhance the validity of the results.
- There is a lack of clarity regarding the definition and role of certain parameters, such as $\phi$ and $\epsilon$, in the methodology.
- The paper does not sufficiently differentiate its approach from existing methods, particularly in terms of the novelty and the theoretical contributions.
- The paper could benefit from a more detailed discussion on the computational complexity and the practical implications of the discretization process.
