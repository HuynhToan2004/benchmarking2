 **Summary:**
The paper introduces ZEST, a novel method for identifying important layers in a pre-trained model for fine-tuning, using a predictor trained on zero-shot metrics. The approach involves training a predictor on various zero-shot metrics, which are then used to rank layers for fine-tuning. This method is evaluated across different model architectures and datasets, showing potential for reducing the number of trainable parameters while maintaining or even improving performance. The paper also explores the use of ZEST in combination with other parameter-efficient fine-tuning methods like LoRA, demonstrating its effectiveness in reducing training latency and memory consumption.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, easy to follow, and the experiments are solid.
- The proposed method is simple yet effective, and the experiments demonstrate that it is effective across various models and datasets.
- The paper is original in its approach to identifying important layers for fine-tuning, which is a significant challenge in model adaptation.
- The methodology is simple and straightforward, which makes it easy to implement and understand.
- The paper includes a thorough evaluation of the proposed method, demonstrating its effectiveness in various scenarios.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed discussion on the differences between the proposed method and existing methods, such as surgical fine-tuning and sparse update.
- The paper could benefit from a more detailed explanation of the zero-shot metrics and their selection process, especially how they are used to train the ZEST predictor.
- There is a lack of discussion on the scalability of the method to larger models, such as those used in large-scale language models.
- The paper does not adequately address the potential negative societal impacts of the proposed method.
- The method's reliance on zero-shot metrics may not fully capture the complexity of layer importance, and the paper could benefit from a more nuanced approach to layer selection.
- The paper could be improved by including more detailed ablation studies and a broader range of models and datasets to validate the method's effectiveness.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors clarify the differences between the proposed method and existing methods like surgical fine-tuning and sparse update?
- How are the zero-shot metrics selected for training the ZEST predictor, and what criteria are used to determine their effectiveness?
- Is there a possibility to extend the method to include more fine-grained layer selection, such as individual weights or groups of weights?
- How does the method perform when applied to larger models, such as those used in large-scale language models?
- Could the authors provide more details on the scalability of the method and the potential impacts on training latency and memory consumption for larger models?
- How does the method perform when combined with other parameter-efficient fine-tuning methods, such as LoRA?
- Could the authors elaborate on the method's potential negative societal impacts and how these might be mitigated?
