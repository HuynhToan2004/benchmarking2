 **Summary:**
The paper explores the transferability of knowledge between Large Language Models (LLMs) through a novel parametric knowledge transfer method. It introduces a sensitivity-based technique for parameter extraction from larger models and their injection into smaller models using LoRA. The methodology is tested across four benchmarks, demonstrating improved performance in various downstream tasks. The paper provides a detailed analysis of the factors influencing the knowledge transfer process, including the number of seed samples, initialization strategies, and the structure of extracted parameters. Despite its innovative approach, the paper faces criticisms regarding the novelty and depth of analysis, particularly in terms of how the extracted knowledge is characterized and the generalizability of the findings.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, clear, and easy to follow, with a comprehensive evaluation across four benchmark categories.
- The proposed method is simple and effective, with clear explanations of the methodology and results.
- The sensitivity-based technique for parameter extraction and knowledge injection using LoRA is innovative and has been shown to be effective in enhancing the performance of smaller models.
- The paper provides a detailed analysis of the factors influencing the knowledge transfer process, including the number of seed samples, initialization strategies, and the structure of extracted parameters.
- The experiments are well-designed, and the results are convincing, showing improvements in performance on downstream tasks.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a thorough comparison with existing methods, particularly in terms of how it differs from and improves upon existing knowledge transfer techniques.
- The novelty of the paper is questioned, as similar methods for transferring knowledge between LLMs have been previously explored.
- The analysis of the extracted knowledge is limited, and the paper does not provide a detailed characterization of the knowledge being transferred, which could enhance understanding of the method's effectiveness.
- The method's generalizability to other types of LLMs or datasets is not explored, which could limit the applicability of the findings.
- The paper's approach to knowledge transfer might be limited to specific types of LLMs or datasets, and the method's effectiveness in more diverse settings is not demonstrated.
- There is a lack of discussion on the limitations of the proposed method and potential negative societal impacts.

**Questions:**
- Can you clarify the differences between your method and existing knowledge transfer techniques, particularly in terms of the novel contributions and improvements over prior work?
- How does the proposed method compare to other knowledge transfer methods in terms of effectiveness and efficiency?
- Could you provide more details on how the sensitivity-based technique for parameter extraction works and how it is applied in practice?
- How does the proposed method perform when applied to other types of LLMs or datasets, and what are the implications for its generalizability?
- Can you discuss the potential limitations of the proposed method and how they might affect its practical application?
- How does the proposed method address the potential negative societal impacts of knowledge transfer in LLMs?
