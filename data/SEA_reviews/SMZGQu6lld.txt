 **Summary:**
The paper introduces LLM-Prop, a method that leverages large language models (LLMs) to predict physical and electronic properties of crystals from their text descriptions. The authors propose a novel benchmark dataset, TextEdge, which contains text descriptions of crystal structures with their properties. This dataset is used to evaluate the performance of LLM-Prop against existing methods, demonstrating its superiority in predicting band gap, classifying band gap types, and predicting unit cell volume. The paper also explores the use of different pre-training methods and data sizes to fine-tune LLMs for this task, highlighting the potential of LLMs in predicting crystal properties from textual descriptions.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, with a clear and easy-to-follow structure, making it accessible to a broad audience.
- The proposed method, LLM-Prop, shows promising results in predicting crystal properties, outperforming existing methods such as GNN-based predictors and domain-specific pre-trained models like MatBERT.
- The introduction of the TextEdge dataset, which contains text descriptions of crystal structures with their properties, is a valuable contribution to the field, potentially accelerating research in this area.
- The paper provides a comprehensive evaluation of the proposed method, including ablation studies that demonstrate the effectiveness of different preprocessing strategies.
- The method's simplicity and the ease of implementing the proposed techniques are highlighted, making it potentially useful for other researchers in the field.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed discussion on the limitations of the proposed method, particularly concerning the use of textual data which might not fully capture the complex interactions between atoms and molecules within a crystal structure.
- There is a lack of comparison with other large language models (LLMs) beyond T5, which could provide insights into the effectiveness of different LLMs for this task.
- The paper could benefit from a more detailed analysis of the data used, including the quality and diversity of the text descriptions, which could affect the performance of the model.
- The paper does not sufficiently discuss the potential negative societal impacts of the work, which is a significant omission in ethical considerations.
- The paper's claims about the superiority of LLM-Prop over GNN-based methods are not convincingly supported by the experimental results, which are sometimes inconsistent or unclear.

**Questions:**
- How does the performance of LLM-Prop compare when using different LLMs, particularly those pre-trained on in-domain data?
- Could you elaborate on the data preprocessing techniques used for MatBERT, especially the replacement of bond distances and angles with special tokens, and how these affect the model's performance?
- How do the authors address the issue of data quality and diversity in the TextEdge dataset, and what measures are in place to ensure the reliability and robustness of the predictions made using this dataset?
- Given the potential limitations of using textual data for crystal property prediction, how do the authors plan to address these challenges in future work?
- Can you provide more details on the computational resources and time required for training and inference with LLM-Prop, particularly in comparison to other methods?
