 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel approach to learning rate scheduling for Stochastic Gradient Descent (SGD) by focusing on the last iterate, which is a departure from traditional methods that primarily analyze the average iterate. The authors propose a refinement method that uses gradient norm information to adjust the learning rate, aiming to improve performance. This method is supported by both theoretical analysis and extensive empirical evaluations across various deep learning tasks, demonstrating the practical effectiveness of the proposed schedules. The paper also discusses the theoretical underpinnings of these schedules, comparing them to existing methods and providing insights into their practical implications.

**Strengths:**
- The paper provides a thorough empirical evaluation of the proposed learning rate schedule, demonstrating its effectiveness across various deep learning problems and optimizers.
- The theoretical analysis is robust and well-supported by extensive experiments, which helps in validating the theoretical claims.
- The paper is well-written and clearly articulates the challenges in setting learning rates, offering a novel approach that addresses these challenges.
- The proposed learning rate schedule is simple to implement and shows superior performance compared to existing schedules, particularly in deep learning tasks.
- The paper offers a comprehensive evaluation of learning rate schedules, including a detailed comparison with state-of-the-art methods, which helps in understanding the effectiveness of the proposed approach.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed discussion on the limitations of the proposed method, which is crucial for understanding its applicability and potential drawbacks.
- The empirical evaluation is limited to specific datasets and optimizers, which might not generalize well to other scenarios or optimizers.
- The theoretical results are not clearly linked to practical applications, making it difficult to understand the implications of the theoretical findings for real-world scenarios.
- The paper could benefit from a more comprehensive discussion on the practical implications of the proposed method, particularly in scenarios where the gradient norm information might not be available.
- There is a need for more detailed comparisons with other state-of-the-art methods, such as those mentioned in the related work section, to establish the superiority of the proposed method more convincingly.

**Questions:**
- Could you clarify the role and significance of the parameter α in the learning rate schedule? How does it affect the performance of the model?
- How does the proposed method perform in scenarios where the gradient norm information is not available? Is there a fallback mechanism or alternative approach in such cases?
- Can you provide more details on how the proposed method handles different types of optimization algorithms, particularly those that do not use SGD?
- In the empirical evaluation, how were the hyperparameters tuned for each schedule, and what were the specific values used?
- Could you elaborate on the theoretical results and their practical implications? How do these results guide the practical application of the learning rate schedule?
- The paper mentions a need for further theoretical analysis. Could you provide more insights into the theoretical underpinnings of the learning rate schedule and its practical implications?
