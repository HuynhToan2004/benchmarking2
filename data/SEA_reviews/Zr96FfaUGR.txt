 **Summary:**
The paper introduces ARIES, a novel dataset designed to aid in training and evaluating large language models (LLMs) in scientific paper editing. The dataset consists of review comments and their corresponding paper edits, enabling the study of tasks like comment-edit alignment and edit generation. The paper conducts extensive evaluations using various baseline methods, including GPT-4, and provides insights into the performance of LLMs in handling scientific paper edits. Despite the dataset's utility and the thoroughness of the experiments, the paper's contributions are seen as somewhat limited due to the lack of significant novelty in the tasks and the methodologies used.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, with a clear and concise introduction that effectively sets the stage for the research.
- The introduction of the ARIES dataset, which includes 196 human-labeled review comments matched to their corresponding paper edits, is a significant contribution to the field.
- The paper provides a thorough analysis of the ARIES dataset, including detailed results on the performance of various models and baseline methods.
- The paper includes a comprehensive evaluation of the ARIES dataset using various models and baseline methods, which helps in understanding the capabilities and limitations of large language models (LLMs) in handling scientific paper edits.
- The analysis of the dataset and the experiments conducted are detailed and informative, providing valuable insights into the challenges and potential solutions for improving the performance of LLMs in scientific paper editing.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed discussion on the methodology used to construct the ARIES dataset, which could be crucial for understanding the dataset's quality and reliability.
- The tasks proposed in the paper, such as comment-edit alignment and edit generation, are not particularly novel or groundbreaking, which may limit the paper's impact.
- The paper's reliance on GPT-4 as a baseline model raises concerns about the generalizability of the findings to other LLMs, especially those that might perform differently or have unique capabilities.
- The paper does not adequately address the potential biases in the dataset, which could significantly affect the results and the conclusions drawn from the study.
- The paper's evaluation is limited to a single model (GPT-4), which might not fully represent the capabilities of other LLMs, and the dataset's size is relatively small, which could limit the statistical significance of the results.

**Questions:**
- Can you provide more details on the methodology used to construct the ARIES dataset, particularly how the human-labeled review comments were collected and annotated?
- How do the authors plan to address the potential biases in the dataset, and what steps have been taken to ensure the dataset's quality and reliability?
- Could you elaborate on the decision to use GPT-4 as the baseline model, and how the findings from this model can be generalized to other LLMs?
- How do the authors envision the ARIES dataset being used in practical applications, and what are the potential implications for the development and deployment of LLMs in scientific paper editing?
- Given the limitations of the current dataset and the methodologies used, what future directions do the authors see for expanding and improving the ARIES dataset, and how might this impact the field of scientific paper editing?
