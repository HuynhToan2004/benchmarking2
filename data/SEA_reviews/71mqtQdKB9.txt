 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel discrete score matching loss, termed "score entropy," for training discrete diffusion models, specifically focusing on language modeling. This loss function aims to address the challenges of training discrete diffusion models by providing a stable and scalable alternative to existing methods. The authors demonstrate that their approach can achieve competitive perplexities with autoregressive models like GPT-2, while also offering advantages in terms of computational efficiency and sample quality. The paper includes theoretical analysis and empirical validation, showing that the proposed method can outperform existing discrete diffusion models and match the performance of autoregressive models.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel discrete score matching loss, which is a significant contribution to the field of discrete diffusion models.
- The proposed method demonstrates a competitive performance in terms of perplexity and sample quality, showing a good balance between model performance and computational efficiency.
- The theoretical analysis provided is comprehensive, and the empirical results are convincing, demonstrating the practical utility of the proposed method.
- The paper is well-written, making the content accessible and understandable, and includes a detailed analysis of the method's properties and performance.
- The methodology is innovative, combining theoretical insights with practical applications, and offers a new approach to training discrete diffusion models, which is a growing area of interest in machine learning.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a thorough comparison with other diffusion models, particularly those that use different sampling strategies or loss functions, which could have provided a more comprehensive evaluation of the proposed method.
- There is a significant gap in performance between the proposed method and state-of-the-art models like GPT-3, which could limit the practical applicability of the proposed method in real-world scenarios.
- The paper does not sufficiently discuss the limitations of the proposed method, such as its applicability to other discrete data domains or the scalability of the method to larger models.
- The paper could benefit from more detailed experimental results and a broader range of datasets to better evaluate the generalizability of the proposed method.
- The paper does not provide sufficient justification for the choice of the Q matrix and the sampling strategy, which are crucial components of the method.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors provide a more detailed comparison with other diffusion models, particularly those that use different sampling strategies or loss functions?
- How does the proposed method compare to other state-of-the-art models in terms of computational efficiency and sample quality?
- What are the specific advantages of the proposed method over existing discrete diffusion models, and how does it compare to continuous diffusion models?
- How does the method perform on other discrete data domains, such as images or speech?
- Can the authors provide more details on the choice of the Q matrix and the sampling strategy, and discuss how these choices affect the performance of the method?
- Could the authors clarify the experimental setup, particularly the choice of datasets and the evaluation metrics used?
