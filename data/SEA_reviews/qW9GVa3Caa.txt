 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a method for generating prototypes from neural networks, which are intended to maximize the activation of a specific logit while maintaining similarity to natural images. This approach is aimed at improving the interpretability of deep neural networks, particularly in image classification models. The method is validated through experiments on ImageNet, demonstrating that the generated prototypes can approximate the mean activation of natural images, thus potentially enhancing the understanding of model decisions. The paper also discusses the limitations of the approach, including its applicability to out-of-distribution data and the potential for misleading interpretations.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel method for generating prototypes from neural networks, which is a significant advancement in the field of interpretability.
- The method is well-motivated and clearly explained, with a logical structure that aids in understanding the proposed approach.
- The paper includes comprehensive experiments that demonstrate the effectiveness of the method, showing that the generated prototypes can approximate the mean activation of natural images.
- The results are presented in a clear and concise manner, with figures that effectively illustrate the findings.
- The paper addresses an important problem in the field of interpretability, which is crucial for understanding the decisions made by neural networks.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed discussion on the limitations of the method, which is essential for a complete understanding of its applicability and effectiveness.
- The methodology for generating prototypes is not sufficiently detailed, particularly the process of generating prototypes from different classes and the choice of hyperparameters.
- The paper does not adequately address the issue of misleading interpretations that could arise from the use of prototypes, which is a significant concern in the field of interpretability.
- The paper could benefit from a more thorough discussion on the potential societal impacts of the proposed method, especially in terms of bias and fairness.
- The experimental validation is limited to a single dataset (ImageNet), and it would be beneficial to see the method applied to other datasets to confirm its generalizability.

**Questions:**
- How do the authors ensure that the generated prototypes do not mislead or provide misleading interpretations of the model's decisions?
- Can the authors provide more details on the process of generating prototypes from different classes and the choice of hyperparameters?
- How does the proposed method perform when applied to out-of-distribution data?
- What are the potential societal impacts of the proposed method, particularly in terms of bias and fairness?
- Could the authors discuss the potential for using the generated prototypes to identify spurious correlations in the model's learning?
- How does the proposed method compare to other existing methods for generating prototypes from neural networks?
