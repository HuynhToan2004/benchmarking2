 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel approach to routing in graphs using a transformer-based model that predicts the next vertex on a path given a text query and a prefix of the ground truth path. The model is trained using a next token prediction framework, which allows it to generalize well to unseen geographies. The methodology involves a multimodal architecture that jointly encodes text and graph data, with a focus on efficient training via next token prediction. The paper presents extensive experiments on both synthetic and real-world data, demonstrating the effectiveness of the approach. The methodology is scalable and can be trained on massive graphs without the need for distributed training, making it a promising solution for routing in complex environments.
