 **Summary:**
The paper introduces Latent Consistency Models (LCMs), a novel approach to accelerate sampling in latent diffusion models (LDMs) by leveraging the consistency models in the latent space. This method allows for few-step or even one-step sampling, thereby significantly reducing the generation time. The authors propose a guided distillation method and a skipping-step technique to optimize the distillation process, enhancing the model's efficiency. Extensive experiments are conducted on the LAION-5B-Aesthetics dataset, demonstrating the effectiveness of the proposed method in generating high-quality images. However, the paper is criticized for its limited evaluation on different datasets and potential issues with reproducibility due to the lack of detailed methodology and experimental setups.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel approach by applying consistency models to the latent space, which is a significant advancement in the field of accelerating sampling in diffusion models.
- The methodology is well-explained, making it easy to follow and understand, and the paper provides a clear description of the proposed methods.
- The proposed method achieves high-quality results with few steps, demonstrating its effectiveness in practical applications.
- The paper is well-written, with clear and concise language, and the presentation of the methodology and results is clear and easy to understand.
- The experimental results are impressive, showing significant improvements over baseline methods in terms of FID and CLIP scores, especially for lower step numbers.
- The paper includes a detailed analysis of the effects of different parameters such as the guidance scale and the skipping step, providing valuable insights for future research.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed explanation of the skipping-step technique and the choice of the number of skipping steps, which could affect the reproducibility and generalizability of the results.
- The evaluation is limited to the LAION-5B-Aesthetics dataset, and it would be beneficial to see how the method performs on other datasets such as LAION-400M and LAION-Open.
- There is a lack of discussion on the limitations of the proposed method, which is crucial for understanding its applicability and potential drawbacks.
- The paper does not include sufficient ablation studies to verify the effectiveness of the proposed method, particularly in scenarios where the model is trained on a specific dataset.
- The paper lacks a detailed comparison with existing methods such as Guided-Distill, which could help in understanding the advantages and disadvantages of the proposed method.
- The paper does not provide sufficient details on the implementation and training of the model, which could hinder the reproducibility of the results.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors provide more details on the skipping-step technique, including the choice of the number of skipping steps and the rationale behind this choice?
- How does the proposed method perform on other datasets such as LAION-400M and LAION-Open?
- Can the authors discuss the limitations of their method and provide examples or scenarios where it may not be effective?
- How does the proposed method compare with other state-of-the-art methods in terms of efficiency and effectiveness, particularly in scenarios where the model is trained on a specific dataset?
- Could the authors provide more details on the training and implementation of the model, including the specific parameters and hyperparameters used?
