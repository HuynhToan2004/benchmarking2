 **Summary:**
The paper investigates the nonconvex-nonconvex bi-level optimization (BLO) problem using a novel Moreau envelope-based reformulation, which allows for the development of a single-loop gradient-based algorithm with Hessian-free computations. The proposed method, named MEHA, is designed to handle nonconvex lower-level problems and is theoretically supported with non-asymptotic convergence analysis. The algorithm's practicality is demonstrated through numerical experiments on both synthetic and real-world datasets, including few-shot learning and neural architecture search. The paper also addresses the computational efficiency and theoretical guarantees in the absence of strong convexity assumptions.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel method for nonconvex-nonconvex bi-level optimization (BLO) using the Moreau envelope, which is a first in the literature.
- The proposed algorithm is a single-loop gradient-based method that avoids second-order derivative approximations related to the Hessian matrix, making it computationally efficient.
- The paper provides a non-asymptotic convergence analysis for the proposed algorithm, which is a significant contribution to the field.
- Theoretical analysis is provided under milder conditions, avoiding the need for convexity assumptions or the Polyak-Lojasiewicz condition on the lower-level problem.
- The algorithm is validated through numerical experiments on both synthetic and real-world datasets, demonstrating its effectiveness and efficiency.
- The paper is well-written, making it accessible and easy to follow, which is crucial for academic papers.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed comparison with existing methods, particularly in terms of computational efficiency and convergence rates.
- The experimental results, while showing the effectiveness of the proposed method, are not sufficiently robust or convincing, especially in large-scale problems.
- The paper does not adequately discuss the limitations of the proposed method, which could help in understanding its applicability and scope.
- The theoretical analysis assumes that the lower-level problem is weakly convex, which might not hold for all nonconvex problems, limiting the general applicability of the results.
- The paper does not discuss the implications of the assumptions made in the theoretical analysis, particularly Assumption 3.2(ii), which is crucial for the applicability of the results.

**Questions:**
- Could you provide a detailed comparison of the proposed algorithm with existing methods in terms of computational efficiency and convergence rates?
- How does the proposed algorithm perform in large-scale problems, and what are the computational costs associated with its implementation?
- Can you clarify the implications of Assumption 3.2(ii) and discuss its applicability in practical scenarios?
- How does the proposed algorithm handle the nonconvex-nonconvex BLO problem, and what are the theoretical guarantees for this scenario?
- In the experimental results, how were the hyperparameters selected, and what were the criteria used for their selection?
- Can you discuss the limitations of the proposed method and how they might affect its practical applicability?
