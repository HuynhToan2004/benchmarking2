 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel reinforcement learning (RL) approach for training diffusion models, focusing on optimizing the denoising process to align with user-defined objectives. This approach, termed Denoising Diffusion Policy Optimization (DDPO), frames denoising as a multi-step decision-making problem, enabling the application of policy gradient algorithms. The methodology is demonstrated through experiments involving text-to-image diffusion models, showcasing improvements in tasks such as image compressibility and aesthetic quality. The paper also explores the use of vision-language models (VLMs) to provide feedback on the quality of generated images, enhancing the model's alignment with user-specified rewards. Despite the innovative approach, concerns about the clarity of the paper's presentation and the novelty of the methodology were raised, along with the need for a more comprehensive experimental validation and a clearer discussion on the limitations and societal impacts of the proposed method.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel approach using reinforcement learning (RL) to optimize diffusion models for various downstream tasks, which is a significant departure from traditional methods that focus on maximizing the likelihood of data.
- The use of vision-language models (VLMs) to provide feedback on the quality of generated images is a promising direction for training diffusion models to align with user-specified rewards, enhancing the model's ability to adapt to user preferences.
- The paper is well-written, making it accessible and easy to follow, with clear explanations of the proposed methods and their implications.
- The experimental results demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed method, showing improvements over traditional methods such as Reward-Weighted Regression (RWR).
- The paper addresses an important problem in the field of diffusion models, focusing on optimizing the model for specific user-defined objectives, which is a significant step towards more targeted and effective use of generative models.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a clear discussion on the novelty of the proposed method, particularly in relation to existing works such as those by Fan and Lee (2023) and Lee et al. (2023), which also use similar RL approaches for training diffusion models.
- The experimental validation could be improved by including more diverse tasks and a broader range of prompts to better assess the generalizability and robustness of the proposed method.
- The paper could benefit from a more detailed discussion on the limitations of the proposed method, including potential issues such as over-optimization and the potential for the model to diverge too far from the original data distribution.
- The presentation of the paper could be clearer, particularly in the explanation of the RL framework and the experiments, which are currently not well-explained, making it difficult for readers to fully understand the methodology and results.
- There is a need for a more comprehensive comparison with existing methods, including a discussion on the computational efficiency and scalability of the proposed method.

**Questions:**
- Can the proposed method be adapted to optimize the diffusion model for other types of generative models, such as VAEs or GANs?
- How does the proposed method compare in terms of computational efficiency and scalability with other existing methods?
- Could the authors provide more details on the experimental setup, particularly how the reward functions were defined and the specifics of the VLM feedback integration?
- How does the proposed method handle the issue of over-optimization, where the model may diverge too far from the original data distribution in pursuit of the user-defined objective?
- In terms of societal impact, how does the paper address the potential misuse of the proposed method, particularly in areas such as disinformation or impersonation?
