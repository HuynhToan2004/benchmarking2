 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel approach to off-policy reinforcement learning (RL) by integrating policy representations into the value function, aiming to enhance function approximation and off-policy learning. The authors propose a new Bellman operator and a generalized off-policy evaluation method to leverage policy representations, which are then validated through empirical studies on OpenAI continuous control tasks. The approach shows improvements over existing methods, although the paper suffers from clarity issues, especially in the presentation of the methodology and the empirical results.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel approach by integrating policy representations into the value function, which is a significant departure from traditional methods.
- The empirical results demonstrate that the proposed method outperforms existing baselines in most cases, showcasing its effectiveness.
- The paper is well-written, making it easy to follow, and the methodology is clearly explained, which aids in understanding the proposed approach.
- The paper addresses a critical problem in reinforcement learning by focusing on the "Deadly Triad" of Bootstrapping, Function Approximation, and Off-Policy Learning, which are crucial aspects of the field.
- The paper provides a detailed empirical study, including ablation studies, which helps in understanding the contributions of different components of the proposed method.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a clear explanation of how the proposed method improves over existing approaches, particularly in terms of theoretical underpinnings and empirical evidence.
- The paper suffers from a lack of clarity in the presentation, making it difficult to follow the methodology and the empirical results.
- The paper does not adequately compare the proposed method with state-of-the-art methods, which limits the understanding of its relative performance.
- The paper does not discuss the computational complexity or the scalability of the proposed method, which are important considerations for practical applications.
- The paper does not sufficiently address the limitations of the proposed method, which could help in understanding its scope and applicability.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors clarify how the proposed method improves over existing approaches, particularly in terms of theoretical justification and empirical evidence?
- How does the proposed method handle the "Deadly Triad" of Bootstrapping, Function Approximation, and Off-Policy Learning, which is a critical issue in reinforcement learning?
- Can the authors provide more details on the computational complexity and scalability of the proposed method?
- How does the proposed method compare with other state-of-the-art methods in terms of performance and efficiency?
- Can the authors elaborate on the limitations of the proposed method and discuss potential directions for future research?
