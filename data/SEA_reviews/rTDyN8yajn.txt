 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel framework, Octavius, aimed at mitigating task interference in multimodal large language models (MLLMs) by integrating a MoE-based decoder with LoRA. This approach allows for task-specific learning, improving performance across various 2D and 3D downstream tasks. The framework, which includes an Object-As-Scene encoder for 3D point clouds, is evaluated extensively, demonstrating significant improvements over baseline models. However, concerns are raised about the clarity of the MoE integration and the need for more comprehensive ablation studies to validate the proposed methods.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, clear, and easy to follow, with a clear motivation for addressing the tug-of-war problem in multimodal large language models (MLLMs).
- The proposed method, LoRA-MoE, is novel and effective, as demonstrated by significant improvements in zero-shot performance on various downstream tasks.
- The paper provides extensive experimental results and ablation studies, which support the claims made and help in understanding the impact of different components.
- The Object-As-Scene encoder is a valuable contribution to the field, potentially useful in other applications beyond the current study.
- The paper is well-organized and includes a comprehensive literature review, which positions the work within the existing body of research.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a clear explanation of how the MoE is integrated into the model, particularly in terms of how it is trained and the role of different components like the gating network and expert networks.
- There is a need for more detailed analysis and ablation studies to better understand the impact of different components, such as the MoE architecture and the role of different experts.
- The paper could benefit from a more thorough comparison with existing methods, particularly in terms of model capacity and the number of parameters used.
- Some experimental results and methodological details are missing or inadequately explained, which could affect the reproducibility and understanding of the results.
- The paper could be improved by addressing the presentation quality, particularly in figures and tables, to enhance clarity and readability.

**Questions:**
- Could you provide more details on how the MoE is trained, including the training objectives and the role of the gating network and expert networks?
- How does the MoE architecture affect the performance of the model, and what are the specific contributions of each expert network?
- Can you clarify the discrepancies in the results reported in Tables 1 and 2, particularly the performance on the PASCAL VOC dataset?
- How does the Object-As-Scene encoder perform in scenarios with a large number of objects or complex scenes?
- Could you explain the decision to use a different test set for the Scan2Cap task compared to the original 3D-LLM paper?
- How does the proposed method compare to other parameter-efficient fine-tuning methods like Adapters, and what are the implications for model capacity and performance?
