 **Summary:**
The paper introduces DIG-MILP, a novel deep generative framework designed to generate Mixed Integer Linear Programming (MILP) instances using a Variational Autoencoder (VAE). The methodology leverages MILP duality to ensure the generated instances are feasible and bounded, which is crucial for practical applications in industries. The framework is validated through two tasks: data sharing for solver tuning and data augmentation for machine learning models. The paper also addresses the scarcity of real-world MILP data by generating synthetic data that closely resembles real data, which can be beneficial for both industrial and academic applications. However, the paper could benefit from more comprehensive comparisons with existing methods, including a broader range of MILP instances and a more detailed discussion on the limitations and potential applications of the proposed method.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel method for generating MILP instances using a Variational Autoencoder (VAE), which is both theoretically sound and practically applicable.
- The methodology is well-explained, with clear descriptions of the training and inference pipelines, and the neural network architecture used.
- The paper provides a comprehensive evaluation of the proposed method across various MILP instances, demonstrating its effectiveness in both data sharing and data augmentation scenarios.
- The authors have addressed the critical issue of data scarcity in MILP, which is a significant challenge in many industrial and academic applications.
- The paper is well-written, making it accessible and easy to follow, which is essential for a broad audience.
- The experiments conducted are thorough, demonstrating the effectiveness of the proposed method in practical applications.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper could benefit from a more detailed discussion on the limitations of the proposed method, particularly in scenarios where the method may not perform optimally.
- There is a need for a broader range of MILP instances to be tested, including more complex and varied scenarios to fully assess the robustness and applicability of the method.
- The paper could provide more insights into the potential applications of the proposed method beyond the two tasks currently discussed.
- The evaluation could be enhanced by including a more comprehensive comparison with existing methods, particularly in terms of the effectiveness and efficiency of the generated instances.
- The paper could benefit from a more detailed explanation of the MILP duality and its implications for the feasibility and boundedness of the generated instances.
- The paper could discuss the scalability of the proposed method to larger and more complex MILP instances, which are common in real-world applications.
