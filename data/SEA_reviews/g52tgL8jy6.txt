 **Summary:**
The paper introduces the Learnable Multi-hierarchical (LM-H) model, a novel spiking neural network (SNN) model that extends the Leaky Integrate-and-Fire (LIF) model by incorporating learnable parameters in the dendrite and soma layers. This model aims to address the limitations of the LIF model in deep networks, particularly in gradient calculation and the representation of historical information. The paper also proposes a hybrid training method that leverages both ANN-SNN conversion and STBP to optimize the LM-H model, demonstrating superior performance on various datasets like CIFAR-10, CIFAR-100, and ImageNet-200. Despite its innovative approach, the paper has been critiqued for its lack of novelty, insufficient theoretical analysis, and potential issues with gradient vanishing.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel approach by combining the strengths of ANN-SNN conversion and STBP, which is noteworthy and could be of interest to the community.
- The proposed model, LM-H, is well-explained and easy to understand, with a clear and concise writing style that aids in comprehension.
- The experimental results demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed model, particularly in terms of performance and energy efficiency, which is a significant advantage for the field of spiking neural networks.
- The paper addresses a significant issue in the field of spiking neural networks by proposing a new model that could potentially advance the state of the art.
- The LM-H model's ability to learn multiple parameters in the dendrite and soma layers is a unique feature that enhances the model's expressivity and adaptability.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a clear comparison with other existing models, particularly in terms of the number of learnable parameters, which is crucial for understanding the model's performance and efficiency.
- The theoretical analysis provided is insufficient, especially in explaining the model's behavior and the role of the learnable parameters.
- The paper does not adequately address the issue of gradient vanishing, which is a critical problem in deep networks.
- The paper's structure and presentation could be improved to better guide the reader through the content and enhance the clarity of the explanations.
- There are several minor typographical and formatting errors that need to be corrected for better professional presentation.
- The paper's experimental setup and results are not convincingly superior to existing methods, particularly in terms of energy efficiency and performance on large datasets.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors clarify the specific advantages of the LM-H model over other models in terms of performance and energy efficiency?
- How do the learnable parameters in the dendrite and soma layers affect the model's behavior and performance?
- Is there a theoretical basis for the choice of parameters in the LM-H model, and how do these parameters influence the model's expressivity and learning capabilities?
- Could the authors provide more detailed comparisons with other models, particularly in terms of the number of learnable parameters and the computational efficiency of training?
- How does the LM-H model handle the issue of gradient vanishing, and what measures have been taken to address this issue?
- Can the authors provide more detailed explanations or examples to illustrate the model's behavior and the role of the learnable parameters in practical applications?
