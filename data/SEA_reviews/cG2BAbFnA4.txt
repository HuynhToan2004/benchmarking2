 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel approach to complementary label learning by leveraging the SCAR assumption, which allows for the generation of complementary labels without requiring a uniform distribution or an ordinary-label training set. This approach is demonstrated to be risk-consistent and theoretically sound, with empirical results showing its superiority over existing methods on both synthetic and real-world datasets. The paper also provides a theoretical analysis of the risk consistency and convergence rate of the corrected risk estimator, enhancing the understanding of the proposed method. However, the paper is critiqued for its clarity in explaining the SCAR assumption and its assumptions, which may not be intuitive or easily understood by readers unfamiliar with the field.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel approach to complementary label learning by leveraging the SCAR assumption, which is a significant departure from traditional methods that rely on uniform distribution or ordinary-label training sets.
- The method is theoretically sound, with proofs of risk consistency and convergence rate of the corrected risk estimator, which are crucial for ensuring the reliability and effectiveness of the proposed approach.
- Extensive experimental results demonstrate the superiority of the proposed approach over existing methods, showcasing its practical applicability and effectiveness.
- The paper is well-organized, making it easy to follow, and provides a clear and concise explanation of the proposed method and its theoretical underpinnings.
- The introduction of the risk correction approach is a valuable contribution to the field, addressing the overfitting issues that can arise in complex models.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper assumes that the marginal density of the complementary label is known, which may not be a realistic assumption in practical scenarios.
- The explanation of the SCAR assumption and its implications is not clear, making it difficult for readers to understand the assumptions and their implications.
- The paper could benefit from a more detailed explanation of the risk correction approach and its theoretical underpinnings to better understand its effectiveness and limitations.
- The paper could be improved by providing more detailed experimental results and comparisons with other methods, especially on more challenging datasets like CIFAR-100.
- There is a lack of discussion on the limitations of the proposed method and its assumptions, which could affect the practical applicability and reliability of the results.
- The paper could benefit from a more comprehensive literature review and comparison with other related works to better position the proposed method within the existing body of research.

**Questions:**
- Could you clarify the assumptions made in the paper, particularly the SCAR assumption, and provide examples or intuitive explanations to help readers understand its implications?
- How does the risk correction approach address the overfitting issues in complex models, and what are the theoretical underpinnings of this approach?
- Can you provide more detailed experimental results, including comparisons with other methods on more challenging datasets, and discuss the limitations of your method and its assumptions?
- How does the proposed method perform when the class priors are not known, and what are the implications of this on the performance of the model?
- Could you discuss the relationship between the proposed method and other weakly supervised learning problems, and how this method could be applied to other types of weakly supervised learning tasks?
