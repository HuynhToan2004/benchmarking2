 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel method called RoBoT (Robustifying and Boosting training-free Neural Architecture Search) aimed at enhancing the robustness and performance of training-free NAS by combining multiple training-free metrics through Bayesian optimization. The methodology involves a weighted linear combination of existing metrics, optimized using Bayesian optimization to predict the optimal architecture, and a greedy search to select the best architecture from the top-performing candidates. Theoretical analysis and extensive experiments are conducted to demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed method. The paper also provides a theoretical analysis and extensive experiments to support the method's effectiveness.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, easy to follow, and includes comprehensive theoretical analysis and experiments.
- The method proposed, RoBoT, is a novel approach that combines existing training-free metrics to create a robust and consistently better-performing metric, which is optimized using Bayesian optimization.
- The paper includes extensive experiments that demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed method across various NAS benchmark tasks, showing improvements over existing methods.
- The method's theoretical analysis provides a theoretical guarantee for its effectiveness, which is supported by empirical evidence.
- The paper is original in its approach to addressing the challenges of training-free NAS by proposing a new method that can robustify existing training-free metrics and bridge the estimation gap.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed comparison with other methods like HNAS, which also combines multiple training-free metrics.
- The paper does not adequately address the computational cost of the Bayesian optimization process, which is a significant component of the proposed method.
- The method's reliance on Bayesian optimization and the weighted linear combination of metrics may limit its applicability to larger and more complex search spaces.
- The paper's claims of robustness and the effectiveness of the proposed method are not convincingly supported by the experimental results, which do not clearly demonstrate superior performance over existing methods.
- The paper's theoretical analysis and the assumptions made (such as the uniform distribution assumption) are not convincingly justified, which could limit the generalizability of the findings.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors provide more details on the computational cost of the Bayesian optimization process and how it compares to other methods?
- How does the proposed method perform when applied to larger and more complex search spaces, such as those found in DARTS?
- Could the authors clarify the assumptions made in the theoretical analysis and provide more evidence to support these assumptions?
- How does the proposed method compare in terms of computational cost and effectiveness with other methods that combine multiple training-free metrics, such as HNAS?
- Could the authors provide more detailed experimental results, including standard deviations, to better support the claims of robustness and effectiveness?
