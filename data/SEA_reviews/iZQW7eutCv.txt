 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel probabilistic approach to embedding-based retrieval, focusing on the use of a probabilistic cumulative distribution function (CDF) to dynamically set the cosine similarity threshold. This approach is aimed at addressing the challenges of insufficient retrieval for head queries and irrelevant retrieval for tail queries. The proposed method, termed pEBR, is evaluated against DSSM-topk and DSSM-score on a dataset of 87 million user click logs, showing improvements in recall and precision metrics. The paper also explores the use of betaNCE and expNCE loss functions to optimize the model, which is compatible with most two-tower retrieval models.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel probabilistic approach to embedding-based retrieval, which is the first of its kind in the field.
- The proposed method is compatible with most two-tower retrieval models, making it adaptable to various existing systems.
- The paper is well-written, clear, and easy to follow, making complex concepts accessible to a broad audience.
- The experimental results demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed method, showing improvements in recall and precision metrics.
- The use of betaNCE and expNCE loss functions is innovative and enhances the model's ability to handle different types of queries effectively.
- The paper addresses a significant problem in the field of retrieval systems, focusing on the challenges of insufficient retrieval for head queries and irrelevant retrieval for tail queries.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed comparison with other advanced embedding-based retrieval methods, which could provide a more comprehensive evaluation of the proposed method.
- The dataset used in the experiments is relatively small and specific, which might limit the generalizability of the results.
- The methodology section is not clearly explained, particularly the details of the probabilistic approach and the loss function used.
- The paper does not provide sufficient details on the experimental setup, such as the specifics of the dataset used and the experimental conditions.
- The paper does not adequately address the limitations of the proposed method, which could help in understanding its scope and applicability.
- The paper does not include a comprehensive literature review, which could help in positioning the work within the existing body of knowledge.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors clarify the specifics of the dataset used in the experiments, including its size and diversity?
- How does the proposed method perform when compared to other advanced embedding-based retrieval methods, and what are the key differences in performance?
- Can the authors provide more details on the methodology, particularly how the probabilistic approach is implemented and how the loss function is optimized?
- How does the proposed method handle different types of queries, such as head, torso, and tail queries, and what are the specific advantages in handling these types of queries?
- Could the authors elaborate on the choice of the probabilistic approach and how it contributes to the overall effectiveness of the model?
- How does the proposed method compare to other state-of-the-art methods in terms of computational efficiency and scalability?
