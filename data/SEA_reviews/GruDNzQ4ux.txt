 **Summary:**
The paper introduces DreamSmooth, a method designed to enhance reward prediction in Model-Based Reinforcement Learning (MBRL) by smoothing sparse rewards. This approach aims to address the challenges posed by sparse rewards, which are often difficult to predict, by averaging rewards over a certain time horizon. The method is tested across various environments including DMC and Atari, showing improvements in reward prediction and performance in sparse reward tasks. The paper also discusses the potential limitations of the method, such as its performance in dense reward tasks and its impact on exploration.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, clear, and easy to follow, with a clear motivation and a novel approach to addressing the issue of sparse reward prediction in Model-Based Reinforcement Learning (MBRL).
- The proposed method, DreamSmooth, is simple and effective, requiring minimal changes to existing algorithms and demonstrating improved performance in sparse reward tasks.
- The paper includes a thorough empirical evaluation, demonstrating the effectiveness of DreamSmooth across various environments and tasks, and showing that it can be integrated with different MBRL algorithms.
- The analysis provided is detailed, including a clear explanation of the method's limitations and potential negative effects, such as reduced exploration.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper could benefit from a more detailed discussion on the theoretical underpinnings of the method, particularly the connection between smoothing and reward prediction accuracy.
- The experimental results, while robust, are somewhat limited in scope, focusing primarily on sparse reward tasks and not sufficiently exploring the impact of the method in dense reward environments.
- The paper lacks a comprehensive comparison with other smoothing methods or a more detailed discussion on the choice of smoothing parameters.
- The paper does not sufficiently address the potential negative effects of the method, such as the reduction in exploration due to smoothing.
- The paper could benefit from a more thorough literature review, especially in relation to existing works on reward prediction and its impact on exploration.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors clarify the theoretical connection between smoothing and reward prediction accuracy, possibly including a proof or more detailed analysis?
- How does the choice of smoothing parameters (L, σ, α) affect the performance of the method, and have different parameter choices been tested?
- What is the impact of the method on exploration, and how does this trade-off between exploration and exploitation affect the overall performance of the MBRL algorithms?
- Can the authors provide more details on the environments used in the experiments, particularly the DMC tasks and the Atari tasks, to help understand the results better?
- Is there a possibility of extending the method to include a temperature parameter to adjust the smoothing effect, and how might this affect the performance and exploration dynamics?
