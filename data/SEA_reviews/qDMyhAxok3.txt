 **Summary:**
The paper introduces MorphGrower, a novel method for generating neuronal morphologies that mimics natural growth mechanisms, contrasting with traditional one-shot treatments. MorphGrower operates by generating morphologies layer by layer, conditioned on the morphological structure of previous layers, ensuring topological validity. This method is evaluated against MorphVAE and other baselines, showing improvements in performance and plausibility. The method is designed to enhance the generation of neuronal morphologies by incorporating domain knowledge and conditioning on previous layers, which is a significant advancement over existing methods. The paper also includes a detailed analysis of the model's performance and presents several visualizations to demonstrate its effectiveness.

**Strengths:**
- The paper addresses an important and relevant topic by proposing a novel method for generating neuronal morphologies, which is crucial for understanding brain functioning and neurodegenerative disorders.
- The methodology is well-explained, with clear and detailed descriptions of the proposed approach, including the use of a layer-by-layer conditional morphology generation scheme and a conditional variational autoencoder.
- The paper includes a comprehensive evaluation against MorphVAE and other baselines, demonstrating the effectiveness of the proposed method through both quantitative and qualitative results.
- The paper is well-written, making it accessible and understandable, with clear explanations of the methodology and results.
- The inclusion of supplementary material, such as code and detailed appendices, enhances the reproducibility and transparency of the research.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a direct comparison with other methods for generating neuronal morphologies, which could provide a more comprehensive evaluation of the proposed method's performance.
- The evaluation metrics used are not clearly defined or explained, which could limit the understanding and reproducibility of the results.
- The paper does not adequately address the computational complexity or scalability of the proposed method, particularly in terms of training and inference times.
- The paper could benefit from a more detailed discussion on the limitations and potential negative societal impacts of the proposed method.
- There is a lack of diversity in the datasets used for evaluation, which could limit the generalizability of the findings.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors provide more details on the computational complexity and scalability of the proposed method, including training and inference times?
- How does the proposed method compare in terms of performance and computational efficiency with other methods for generating neuronal morphologies?
- Could the authors clarify the definition and use of the evaluation metrics, particularly in the context of the results presented in the paper?
- How does the proposed method ensure that the generated morphologies are not only plausible but also diverse and representative of real neuronal morphologies?
- Could the authors provide more detailed examples or visualizations of the generated morphologies to better illustrate the method's capabilities?
