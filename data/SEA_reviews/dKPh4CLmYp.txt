 **Summary:**
The paper introduces Fishnets, a novel aggregation method for sets and graphs that maximizes the Fisher information matrix to achieve information saturation. This approach is applied to both set-based learning and graph neural networks (GNNs), demonstrating improvements in scalability, robustness, and Bayesian inference. The method is evaluated across various datasets, including linear regression and graph datasets, showing competitive performance with state-of-the-art models using fewer parameters. Despite its innovative approach, the paper faces criticism for its lack of clarity in the presentation of theoretical aspects, particularly the use of the Fisher information matrix, and the limited scope of its experiments.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written and easy to follow, with a clear introduction to the concept of information saturation.
- The proposed method, Fishnets, is novel and well-motivated, particularly in its application to graph neural networks (GNNs) and set-based learning.
- The paper demonstrates competitive performance of Fishnets on graph datasets, showing potential for application in various domains.
- The method is robust to changes in data distribution and can be used as a drop-in aggregation scheme within GNNs, potentially improving scalability and robustness.
- The paper includes a variety of experiments that showcase the effectiveness of the proposed method, particularly in the context of Bayesian inference and graph aggregation.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a clear explanation of the role and computation of the Fisher information matrix, which is critical to the method's effectiveness.
- The experiments are limited in scope, particularly in terms of the variety of datasets used and the depth of the comparisons made.
- There is a lack of detailed comparison with existing methods, such as DeepSets and Graph Attention Networks (GATs), which could help establish the superiority or novelty of the proposed method.
- The paper does not provide sufficient details on the experimental setup, such as the number of layers, learning rates, and other hyperparameters used, which could affect the reproducibility and comparability of the results.
- The paper does not adequately address the computational efficiency of the proposed method, which could be a significant concern for larger datasets or more complex models.
- The paper suffers from several typos and grammatical errors that could detract from its professionalism and readability.

**Questions:**
- Can you provide a clearer explanation of how the Fisher information matrix is computed and utilized within the Fishnets framework?
- How does the proposed method compare in terms of computational efficiency and scalability with other aggregation methods, such as DeepSets and GATs?
- Could you provide more detailed comparisons with existing methods, particularly in terms of the architecture and hyperparameters used?
- How does the proposed method perform on more diverse datasets, such as those from the OGB benchmark, and what are the implications of these results for the generalizability of the method?
- Can you clarify the role and impact of the "score" and "Fisher" networks in the proposed method, and how they contribute to the overall performance and scalability of the model?
- How does the method handle the heterogeneity of data within sets, particularly in terms of the impact on the aggregation process and the resulting embeddings?
