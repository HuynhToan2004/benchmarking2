 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel framework, Concept Influence for Fairness (CIF), which quantifies the influence of training samples on model unfairness by counterfactually changing samples based on predefined concepts. The CIF framework extends the use of influence functions to understand the impact of various training concepts, such as sensitive attributes, labels, and features, on model fairness. It proposes a method for generating counterfactual samples using W-GAN and applies this to a variety of datasets, including tabular and image data. The framework aims to help practitioners understand and mitigate unfairness in models by providing insights into the effects of different training concepts. However, the paper has been critiqued for its lack of clarity in explaining the framework and its assumptions, and for the limited scope of its experiments.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel framework, Concept Influence for Fairness (CIF), which extends the use of influence functions to understand the impact of various training concepts, such as sensitive attributes, labels, and features, on model fairness.
- The framework is general and can be applied to a wide range of scenarios, providing a flexible approach to addressing unfairness in models.
- The paper is well-written and easy to follow, with a clear introduction and presentation of the CIF framework.
- The proposed method is simple, effective, and easy to implement, and the experimental results demonstrate its effectiveness.
- The paper addresses an important and under-explored problem in the field of fairness, and the proposed method has potential applications in various areas of machine learning.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks clarity in explaining the framework, particularly the assumptions and the methodology used for generating counterfactual samples.
- The experiments are limited in scope, focusing on a small number of datasets and not sufficiently diverse in terms of data types and fairness metrics.
- The paper does not adequately discuss the limitations of its approach, such as the potential for inaccurate counterfactual samples and the computational complexity of generating counterfactuals.
- The paper does not provide a detailed discussion on the fairness metrics used, which could limit the understanding of the results.
- The paper does not compare its approach with existing methods for data repairing for fairness, which could provide a better context for evaluating the proposed method.
- The paper does not discuss the limitations of its approach, which could help in understanding the scope and applicability of the CIF framework.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors clarify the assumptions made in the framework and how they affect the results?
- How does the proposed method compare to existing methods for data repairing for fairness, and what are the advantages and disadvantages of each approach?
- How does the method perform when applied to different types of data, such as text data, and how does it handle different fairness metrics?
- Can the authors provide more details on the computational complexity of generating counterfactual samples and the accuracy of the generated samples?
- How does the method handle the issue of inaccurate counterfactual samples, and what are the implications of this for the fairness of the model?
- Can the authors provide a more detailed discussion on the limitations of their approach and how these limitations affect the applicability and effectiveness of the CIF framework?
