 **Summary:**
The paper investigates the computational complexity of generating abductive and counterfactual explanations for sequential models, specifically focusing on Recurrent Neural Networks (RNNs) and Transformers. It demonstrates that these models, due to their Turing completeness, cannot generate explanations in polynomial time, and proposes alternative models like deterministic finite automata (DFA) and deterministic register automata (DRA) to address this issue. The paper provides a polynomial-time algorithm for counterfactual explanations in these models, but notes that abductive explanations remain computationally intractable. The authors also explore the use of regular languages to represent features, which are used to define counterfactual queries. The paper is well-written and provides a solid theoretical foundation, although it lacks empirical validation and practical application examples.
