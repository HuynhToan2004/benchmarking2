 **Summary:**
The paper investigates the convergence of stochastic gradient descent (SGD) with various learning rate schedules, specifically focusing on the batch size and its impact on the number of iterations and stochastic first-order oracle (SFO) complexity. The authors provide theoretical bounds for the squared norm of the gradient and analyze the optimal batch size for minimizing SFO complexity. They compare their findings with existing optimizers and empirically demonstrate the effectiveness of their approach using ResNet-18 on CIFAR-10 and CIFAR-100 datasets. The paper also discusses the practical implications of these results, although the theoretical contributions are seen as somewhat limited and the paper lacks a broader empirical validation across different datasets and architectures.

**Strengths:**
- The paper provides a comprehensive analysis of the impact of batch size on the convergence of stochastic gradient descent (SGD) and the stochastic first-order oracle (SFO) complexity.
- It introduces novel theoretical insights into the convergence of SGD with different learning rate schedules, particularly focusing on the optimal batch size for minimizing SFO complexity.
- The authors provide a clear and detailed theoretical analysis, including proofs and mathematical derivations, which are well-organized and easy to follow.
- The paper includes empirical results that demonstrate the practical applicability of the theoretical findings, showing that SGD with a step-decay learning rate and a small batch size can outperform other optimizers in terms of SFO complexity.
- The paper is well-written, making it accessible and understandable, and includes code for reproducibility.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a broader empirical validation, focusing only on ResNet-18 on CIFAR-10 and CIFAR-100 datasets, which limits the generalizability of the findings.
- The theoretical contributions, while novel in some aspects, are seen as somewhat limited as they do not significantly advance the current understanding of SGD convergence.
- The paper does not discuss the practical implications of the theoretical results, such as how to choose the optimal batch size in real-world applications.
- The paper could benefit from a more comprehensive literature review, particularly regarding the convergence analysis of SGD with different learning rate schedules and batch sizes.
- The paper's experimental setup and results, such as the comparison with other optimizers and the selection of batch sizes, are not convincingly justified or explained.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors provide more detailed justification for the selection of the learning rate schedule and batch size in their experiments?
- How do the theoretical results compare with other existing works, particularly in terms of the novelty and depth of the analysis?
- Could the authors clarify the practical implications of their findings, especially in terms of how they can be applied to improve the training of deep neural networks?
- Is there a possibility to extend the analysis to other datasets and architectures to validate the generalizability of the findings?
- Could the authors discuss the computational efficiency and practical feasibility of the step-decay learning rate and small batch size settings, especially in terms of wall-clock time and computational resources?
