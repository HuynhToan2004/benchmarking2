 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel backdoor attack framework, Poisoned Forgery Face, designed to manipulate face forgery detection systems by embedding backdoors into models using specific trigger patterns. This approach, which involves generating translation-sensitive trigger patterns to maximize discrepancies between real and transformed triggers, aims to enhance the stealthiness and effectiveness of the attacks. The framework is tested against various baseline models and shows promising results, although it is noted that the experimental validation could be enhanced with more comprehensive testing across different datasets and attack scenarios.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel backdoor attack framework, Poisoned Forgery Face, which is the first of its kind to address the vulnerabilities in face forgery detection systems.
- The methodology is well-articulated, with a clear explanation of the challenges in applying backdoor attacks to face forgery detection and the proposed solutions to overcome these challenges.
- The paper is well-written, making it easy to follow and understand, with a comprehensive review of existing literature and a detailed description of the experimental setup.
- The experimental results demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed method, showing that it outperforms existing backdoor baselines in terms of attack success rate and reduction in visibility.
- The paper also introduces a scalable trigger generator and a novel convolving process to generate translation-sensitive trigger patterns, which are novel contributions to the field.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a thorough discussion on the limitations of the proposed method, which is crucial for understanding the scope and applicability of the findings.
- The experimental setup is not sufficiently robust, with the attack being tested on only one dataset and not evaluated against a variety of different face forgery detection methods.
- The paper does not adequately address the generalizability of the results, particularly in terms of how the findings might apply to different types of face forgery detection systems or under different conditions.
- There is a lack of discussion on the potential ethical implications of the research, which is crucial given the sensitivity of the topic.
- The paper could benefit from a more detailed analysis of the results, particularly in terms of how the proposed method performs under different conditions or against different types of defenses.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors provide more details on the scalability of the trigger generator and how it might perform under different conditions?
- How does the proposed method perform against different types of face forgery detection systems, such as those based on deepfake artifact detection or blending artifact detection?
- Can the authors clarify how the trigger patterns are generated and optimized, particularly in relation to the translation transformation used?
- How do the authors ensure the stealthiness of the trigger patterns, especially when applied to different face sizes or under varying conditions?
- Could the authors provide more details on the experimental setup, particularly in terms of how the attack was implemented and how the results were analyzed?
- How do the authors address the ethical implications of their research, particularly in terms of the potential misuse of the findings?
