 **Summary:**
The paper introduces the State Transformer (STR), a novel approach to motion prediction and planning in autonomous driving, framed as a sequence modeling task. STR leverages a large trajectory model (LTM) inspired by large language models, using a GPT-2 backbone to predict key points and future states. The model's scalability is demonstrated through experiments on the NuPlan and WOMD datasets, showcasing improved performance over smaller models. The paper also explores the scalability of the model with dataset size and model parameters, highlighting the benefits of using large trajectory models for motion prediction and planning.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written and clearly presents the motivation and idea behind the proposed method, making it accessible to readers.
- The proposed method, STR, demonstrates impressive performance improvements over existing baselines, particularly in motion planning and prediction tasks.
- The scalability of the proposed method is thoroughly analyzed, showing that larger models exhibit better performance and generalization capabilities.
- The use of diffusion models to predict key points in the future trajectory is innovative and enhances the model's ability to handle complex scenarios.
- The paper effectively integrates various components such as proposals, key points, and future states into a unified sequence modeling task, which is well-explained and easy to follow.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks detailed comparisons with other state-of-the-art methods, particularly those that also use large trajectory models, which could provide a more robust evaluation of the proposed method.
- The paper does not discuss the limitations of the proposed method, which is crucial for understanding its applicability and potential drawbacks.
- There is a lack of clarity in the definition and role of key points in the model, particularly how they are used and their impact on the model's performance.
- The paper does not adequately address the generalization capabilities of the model to unseen scenarios, which is a critical aspect of autonomous driving applications.
- The novelty of the method is questionable as it primarily reuses existing techniques without significant innovation.
- The paper could benefit from more detailed experimental setups and results, especially in terms of the impact of different model sizes and dataset sizes on performance.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors provide a more detailed comparison with other state-of-the-art methods, particularly those that also use large trajectory models?
- How does the model perform in scenarios with significant differences from the training data distribution?
- Could the authors clarify the role and definition of key points in the model, and discuss how they are generated and used in the model?
- What are the specific benefits of using a diffusion model for key point prediction, and how does it compare to other methods like MLPs in terms of performance and efficiency?
- How does the model generalize to unseen scenarios, and what measures have been taken to ensure robustness in such cases?
- Can the authors provide more details on the experimental setups, such as the impact of different model sizes and dataset sizes on performance, and the specifics of the training and testing environments?
