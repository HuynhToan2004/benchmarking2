 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel dual encoder-decoder model, DualGen, designed for AMR-to-text generation. It integrates a specially designed GNN into a pre-trained sequence-to-sequence model, utilizing pre-trained BART parameters to initialize the GNN encoder. The model is evaluated against state-of-the-art methods, demonstrating superior performance in AMR-to-text generation tasks. The authors also provide a detailed analysis of the model's performance and its comparison with large language models like GPT-4. Despite its strengths, the paper is criticized for its limited novelty and the lack of a thorough comparison with other graph encoders.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, clear, and easy to follow, making it accessible to readers.
- The proposed dual encoder-decoder model, DualGen, is innovative and integrates a specially designed GNN into a pre-trained sequence-to-sequence model, which is a novel approach.
- The model outperforms existing methods and achieves state-of-the-art performance in AMR-to-text generation tasks, demonstrating its effectiveness.
- The paper includes extensive experiments and a case study, providing a thorough evaluation of the model's performance.
- The model's design allows for the initialization of the GNN encoder with parameters from a pre-trained sequence-to-sequence model, which is a practical and efficient solution.

**Weaknesses:**
- The novelty of the paper is limited as the dual encoder-decoder architecture and the use of pre-trained BART parameters for initialization have been previously explored.
- The paper lacks a comprehensive comparison with other graph encoders, which could provide a more robust evaluation of the proposed model.
- The paper does not adequately address the limitations of the proposed model, which could help in understanding its scope and applicability.
- There is a lack of detailed analysis on the impact of silver data on the model's performance, which could be crucial for understanding the model's effectiveness.
- The paper could benefit from a more detailed discussion on the differences between the proposed model and existing models, particularly in terms of its technical contributions.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors provide a more detailed comparison with other graph encoders to better understand the model's performance relative to existing methods?
- How does the model perform when using different types or amounts of silver data? Could the authors provide more insights into the impact of silver data on the model's performance?
- The paper mentions that the model's performance is comparable to large language models like GPT-4. Could the authors elaborate on the model's performance relative to these models, especially in terms of handling complex or ambiguous AMR graphs?
- In terms of the model's design, how does the dual encoder-decoder model specifically enhance the model's ability to process graph structures? Could the authors provide a more detailed explanation or analysis of this aspect?
