 **Summary:**
The paper discusses the convergence of finite-width neural networks, focusing on the random sparse lift of perceptron modules. It introduces a theoretical framework that allows for the convergence analysis of these networks, particularly in the context of random sparse lifts. The paper explores the tangent approximation property and its implications for the convergence of gradient flows, providing a new perspective on the convergence of neural networks. The authors propose a method for constructing random sparse lifts, which are shown to satisfy the tangent approximation property, thereby ensuring convergence. The theoretical results are supported by a discussion on the practical implications of these findings, although the paper lacks empirical validation.
