 **Summary:**
The paper introduces ILPO-Net, a novel convolutional network designed to handle 3D volumetric data with rotational invariance. The authors propose a new convolution operator, ILPO, which operates on spherical harmonics and utilizes Wigner matrices to perform convolutions. This method aims to maintain invariance to local spatial pattern orientations, which is a significant challenge in traditional convolutional networks. The paper presents experiments on two datasets, MedMNIST and CATH, demonstrating that ILPO-Net outperforms existing methods in terms of accuracy and parameter count. However, the paper's novelty and the significance of its contributions are questioned due to the limited scope of datasets and the lack of comparison with more robust baseline methods.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel approach to convolutional networks, specifically addressing the challenge of rotational invariance in 3D data, which is a significant and under-explored area in deep learning.
- The proposed ILPO operator is well-motivated and theoretically sound, with clear explanations of the mathematical principles behind its operation.
- The paper is well-written, making the content accessible and understandable, which is crucial for a broad audience.
- The methodology is innovative, leveraging spherical harmonics and Wigner matrices to perform convolutions, which is a novel approach in the field.
- The paper presents a significant reduction in the number of parameters required for effective performance, which is a notable achievement.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed comparison with state-of-the-art methods, particularly those that claim similar properties such as rotational invariance. This oversight undermines the paper's claims of superiority.
- The experimental validation is limited to two datasets, which may not be sufficient to establish the generalizability of the proposed method.
- There is a notable absence of visualizations and detailed explanations of the learned filters, which are crucial for understanding the practical application of the proposed method.
- The paper could benefit from a more thorough discussion on the limitations of the proposed method, including its applicability to different types of data and its computational efficiency.
- The paper does not adequately address the scalability of the proposed method, which is crucial for practical applications in real-world scenarios.
