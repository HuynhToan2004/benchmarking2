 **Summary:**
The paper introduces CityGPT, a generative model designed for 2D city layout generation, employing a two-stage transformer-based architecture. The first stage predicts the probability of building presence at each location, while the second stage generates the polygon shape of the buildings. This methodology allows for the generation of arbitrary building shapes and city layouts without relying on prior conditions like satellite images or road networks. The model is evaluated against several baselines and shows superior performance in terms of metrics like FID and IPR. However, concerns were raised about the method's novelty, the clarity of experimental results, and the need for more detailed comparisons with existing models.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, with a clear and easy-to-understand structure, making it accessible to readers.
- The proposed method achieves better performance than several baselines, as demonstrated by the results in Table 2.
- The method can generate arbitrary shapes of buildings and city layouts, which is a significant advancement in the field.
- The paper includes a comprehensive evaluation of the proposed method, comparing it with several baselines and conducting ablation studies.
- The method is capable of generating diverse results, as shown in Figure 4, which demonstrates the model's ability to handle various building shapes and layouts.
- The paper provides a detailed description of the method and its implementation, which aids in reproducibility and understanding.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed comparison with existing models, particularly in terms of the methodology and results. This makes it difficult to assess the true contribution of the proposed method.
- There are several typographical errors and inconsistencies in the paper, which detract from its professionalism and clarity.
- The method's novelty is questionable, as it appears to be a combination of existing techniques without significant innovation.
- The paper does not sufficiently address the limitations of the proposed method, such as the lack of a latent space for complete generation sampling and the potential for misalignment between building and road widths.
- The paper does not provide sufficient details on the experimental setup, such as the dataset used and the specifics of the training process, which could affect the reproducibility and the interpretation of the results.
- The paper does not adequately address the computational cost and efficiency of the proposed method, which is crucial for practical applications.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors clarify the discrepancy in the IPR metric between their method and the baselines, as highlighted in Table 2?
- How does the proposed method compare with other models like SketchRNN, LayoutVAE, and LayoutTrans in terms of performance and novelty?
- Can the authors provide more details on the training and experimental setup, including the dataset used and the specifics of the training process?
- How does the proposed method handle the generation of building shapes that are not present in the training data?
- Could the authors provide more qualitative results to support the claims made about the model's ability to generate realistic and diverse city layouts?
- How does the proposed method handle the generation of city layouts with different styles or characteristics, such as those found in different geographical locations?
- Can the authors discuss the potential societal impacts of their work, particularly in terms of urban planning and design?
