 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel probabilistic forecasting model for irregularly sampled multivariate time series with missing values, leveraging conditional normalizing flows. The model, named ProFITi, incorporates a novel invertible triangular attention layer and a Shiesh activation function to address the challenges of handling irregular sampling and missing values. The model is evaluated on several datasets, demonstrating significant improvements over existing methods. The paper also discusses the theoretical underpinnings and practical applications of the model, highlighting its potential for handling complex time series data.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel model, ProFITi, which is the first to utilize normalizing flow models for conditional permutation invariant structured distributions, enhancing its applicability to irregularly sampled multivariate time series with missing values.
- The authors propose a novel invertible equivariant transformation, making the self-attention mechanism invertible, which is crucial for handling variable-length time series data.
- The paper introduces a new non-linear, invertible, differentiable activation function, Shiesh, which can be used in normalizing flows, providing a new tool for modeling complex data distributions.
- The methodology is well-articulated, with a clear and structured presentation that makes the paper accessible to readers.
- The paper includes extensive experimental results that demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed model, showcasing its ability to outperform existing methods in terms of normalized joint negative log-likelihood.
- The introduction of a sorted invertible triangular self-attention layer and a new activation function, Shiesh, provides a novel approach to modeling complex data distributions, potentially advancing the field of probabilistic forecasting.

**Weaknesses:**
