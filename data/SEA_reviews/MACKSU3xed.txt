 **Summary:**
The paper introduces PeriodNet, a lightweight model for multivariate time series forecasting, focusing on the extraction of periodic features using a Fourier module and the fusion of local and global features. The model is designed to be efficient and effective, particularly in resource-constrained environments. It employs a HALFP module for data decomposition and a LTWG module for feature fusion. The model is evaluated on seven benchmark datasets, showing promising results. However, the paper suffers from issues in clarity, presentation, and methodological depth. The experimental setup lacks detail, and the comparison with existing models is insufficient. Furthermore, the paper's claims about the novelty of the Fourier module and the LTWG module are questionable, as similar approaches have been previously explored.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel approach to time series forecasting by focusing on the extraction of periodic features and the fusion of local and global features, which is a promising direction for efficient and effective time series prediction.
- The model is lightweight, making it suitable for resource-constrained environments, which is a significant advantage in practical applications.
- The paper is well-written and easy to follow, which enhances its accessibility and readability.
- The model's design is straightforward and intuitive, which could make it easier for practitioners to understand and implement.
- The paper includes an ablation study to evaluate the effectiveness of the proposed modules, which provides valuable insights into the model's performance.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks clarity in several sections, particularly in the descriptions of the Fourier module and the LTWG module, which could hinder understanding and replication of the model.
- The experimental setup is not detailed enough, and the paper does not compare the proposed model with a sufficient number of state-of-the-art methods, which limits the evaluation of its effectiveness.
- The novelty of the Fourier module and the LTWG module is questionable, as similar approaches have been previously explored, which undermines the paper's claim of innovation.
- The paper does not adequately address the issue of periodicity in time series data, which is crucial for the effectiveness of the proposed model.
- There are several grammatical and typographical errors that need correction for better presentation.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors clarify the mathematical and operational details of the Fourier module and the LTWG module to enhance understanding and reproducibility?
- How does the model handle the issue of periodicity in time series data, and what are the implications of this for the model's performance?
- Can the authors provide more detailed information on the experimental setup, including the specific datasets used, the experimental conditions, and the metrics for evaluating performance?
- How does the model compare with other state-of-the-art methods in terms of computational efficiency and prediction accuracy?
- Could the authors address the grammatical and typographical errors to improve the paper's presentation quality?
