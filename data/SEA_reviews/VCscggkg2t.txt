 **Summary:**
The paper introduces Goal2FlowNets, a novel approach to goal-conditioned reinforcement learning (RL) that leverages Generative Flow Networks (GFlowNets) to learn diverse policies that cover a wide range of trajectories. The method aims to address the challenge of limited exploration in current RL approaches by learning multiple nearly optimal paths to reach goals, enhancing sample efficiency and generalization to novel environmental changes. The paper presents experimental results that demonstrate the effectiveness of Goal2FlowNets in various gridworld environments, showing improvements over existing methods like PPO and DEIR. Despite its innovative approach, the paper is critiqued for its limited scope in terms of the environments used and the lack of comprehensive comparisons with other exploration-based RL methods.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, with clear and easy-to-follow text, and it provides a comprehensive literature review.
- The proposed method, Goal2FlowNets, is novel and well-motivated, addressing the challenge of limited exploration in current RL approaches by learning multiple nearly optimal paths to reach goals.
- The experiments conducted on gridworld environments demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed method, showing improvements in sample efficiency and zero-shot generalization compared to other methods like PPO and DEIR.
- The paper is original in its application of GFlowNets to goal-conditioned RL, which is a novel approach that has not been explored before.
- The method's capability to generalize to new environments and its robustness to changes in the environment are highlighted, showing that the method can handle distribution shifts effectively.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a comprehensive comparison with other exploration-based RL methods, which could provide a better understanding of the advantages and disadvantages of the proposed method.
- The experiments are limited to a few environments, primarily gridworlds, which may not adequately demonstrate the generalizability of the method across more complex or varied environments.
- The paper does not provide a detailed analysis of the computational cost of training and inference, which is crucial for understanding the practical applicability of the method.
- The paper does not compare the proposed method with other policy-cover methods, which could provide insights into how the proposed method compares in terms of exploration efficiency and effectiveness.
- The paper lacks a detailed discussion on the limitations of the method and potential future research directions.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors provide more details on how the method can be applied to more complex environments beyond gridworlds?
- How does the proposed method compare in terms of computational cost with other exploration-based RL methods?
- Can the authors provide more insights into how the method can be adapted to handle different types of environments, such as those with continuous state and action spaces?
- How does the method handle the exploration-exploitation trade-off, especially in environments where there are multiple ways to reach a goal but some are more effective than others?
- Can the authors provide more details on the training and inference times for the proposed method compared to other methods like PPO and DEIR?
