 **Summary:**
The paper introduces Bayesian knowledge distillation (BKD) for online action detection, leveraging a teacher-student architecture with a Bayesian neural network for the teacher model. This model estimates mutual information and uncertainty, which are distilled to a student model based on an evidential neural network. The student model efficiently selects features and quantifies uncertainty, enhancing performance on benchmark datasets like THUMOS14, TVSeries, and HDD. Despite its innovative approach, the paper has been critiqued for its clarity, particularly in the methodology and experimental results, and for not sufficiently distinguishing its contributions from existing literature.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces an innovative Bayesian knowledge distillation (BKD) approach for online action detection, which effectively integrates Bayesian neural networks and evidential deep learning models.
- The proposed method is well-articulated and supported by comprehensive experiments that demonstrate its effectiveness in enhancing performance, computational efficiency, and model complexity.
- The use of Bayesian knowledge distillation to address the challenge of online action detection is novel and contributes significantly to the field.
- The paper provides a thorough analysis of the BKD method, including its ability to select important features, make fast inferences, and quantify prediction uncertainty.
- The experiments conducted on three benchmark datasets showcase the robustness and generalizability of the proposed method.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper suffers from unclear writing and lacks sufficient detail in certain sections, particularly in the methodology and experimental setup, which makes it difficult to fully understand the proposed method and its implications.
- The novelty of the paper is limited as it heavily relies on existing techniques such as Bayesian neural networks and evidential deep learning models, which have been previously used in similar contexts.
- The paper lacks a detailed comparison with state-of-the-art methods, which could have provided a clearer picture of the proposed method's advantages and disadvantages.
- The experimental results and their interpretations are not convincingly presented, with some results being misleading or not adequately explained.
- There is a lack of clarity in the definitions and roles of key components such as the Bayesian teacher model and the evidential probabilistic student model, which could confuse readers about the method's operation and effectiveness.
- The paper does not sufficiently address the computational efficiency of the proposed method, which is crucial for real-time applications.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors clarify the role and operation of the Bayesian teacher model and the evidential probabilistic student model in the context of online action detection?
- How does the proposed method compare to state-of-the-art methods in terms of computational efficiency and model complexity?
- Could the authors provide more detailed explanations of the experimental results and their implications, particularly in the context of uncertainty quantification and abnormal action detection?
- How does the proposed method handle the computational demands of Bayesian inference, and what strategies are used to ensure real-time performance?
- Could the authors elaborate on the choice of parameters and the training strategies used in the experiments, and how these affect the performance and generalizability of the model?
