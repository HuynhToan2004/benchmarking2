 **Summary:**
The paper introduces SMAAT, a novel adversarial training method designed to enhance the robustness of large language models (LLMs) by focusing on adversarial examples generated in the last layer. This approach is based on the observation that deeper layers of LLMs exhibit lower intrinsic dimensionality, which is used to identify the optimal layer for adversarial training. The methodology involves a layer-wise search for the layer with the lowest intrinsic dimension, which is then used to generate adversarial examples. The paper provides empirical evidence of SMAAT's effectiveness, showing that it outperforms other state-of-the-art methods in terms of robustness and generalization. However, the paper's scope is limited to encoder-based LLMs, and its applicability to vision models and other architectures remains unclear.

**Strengths:**
- The paper presents a novel approach to adversarial training (AT) by focusing on the last layer of encoder-based large language models (LLMs), which is both intuitive and effective in terms of runtime efficiency and robustness.
- The methodology is well-supported by theoretical insights and empirical evidence, demonstrating a significant improvement in robustness over existing state-of-the-art methods.
- The paper is well-written, making it easy to follow, and includes a comprehensive literature review and a clear explanation of the proposed method.
- The experimental results show that SMAAT achieves state-of-the-art performance on several tasks, excelling in both runtime efficiency and robustness, while maintaining comparable levels of generalization.
- The paper is the first to apply the manifold conjecture to the training process of adversarial training, which is a significant contribution to the field.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks clarity in some sections, particularly in the methodology and the experimental setup, which could benefit from more detailed explanations and examples.
- The paper's focus on encoder-based LLMs limits its applicability to other model architectures, such as vision models or decoder-only models, which might exhibit different intrinsic dimensionality behaviors.
- There is a lack of discussion on the limitations of the proposed method, particularly in terms of its applicability to other model architectures and its effectiveness against more powerful attacks.
- The paper does not provide sufficient details on the hyperparameters used in the experiments, which could affect the reproducibility and comparability of the results.
- The paper could benefit from a more thorough discussion on the scalability of the proposed method, particularly in terms of its computational efficiency and the scalability of the adversarial training process.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors clarify the methodology for identifying the optimal layer for adversarial training, particularly how the intrinsic dimension is computed and how it influences the choice of layer?
- How does the proposed method compare to other state-of-the-art methods in terms of computational efficiency and runtime?
- Can the authors provide more details on the experimental setup, such as the specific hyperparameters used and the datasets employed?
- How does the proposed method perform against more powerful adversarial attacks, such as those based on feature-level perturbations?
- Could the authors discuss the limitations of their method and provide suggestions for future research directions?
