 **Summary:**
The paper introduces FedRDN, a novel data augmentation technique aimed at mitigating feature distribution skew in federated learning (FL) environments. FedRDN operates by randomly selecting statistics of local datasets to augment data, which enhances the generalization of features and addresses the issue of feature shift. The method is designed to be a plug-and-play component that can be seamlessly integrated into the data augmentation flow with minimal code changes. Extensive experiments are conducted across various datasets to demonstrate the effectiveness of FedRDN in improving the performance of FL methods. However, concerns about the novelty of the approach, the clarity of the experimental setup, and the generalizability of the results to more diverse FL settings are raised.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel approach to addressing the feature distribution skew problem in federated learning, focusing on data augmentation at the input level, which is a less explored area.
- The proposed method, FedRDN, is simple yet effective, with minimal computational overhead, making it a practical solution for improving FL performance.
- The paper is well-written, with clear and concise explanations of the methodology and results, which are supported by extensive experimental results across multiple datasets and baseline models.
- The experimental setup is comprehensive, including various datasets and baseline models, which helps to validate the effectiveness of the proposed method.
- The paper addresses a significant problem in FL, namely feature distribution skew, which is often overlooked in existing literature.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed discussion on the limitations of the proposed method, particularly in terms of its applicability to different FL settings and the potential negative impacts on communication efficiency.
- There is a notable absence of privacy analysis, which is crucial given the sensitivity of data in FL environments.
- The experimental setup could be improved by including more diverse FL settings and comparing the proposed method with a broader range of existing methods.
- The paper does not adequately address the issue of feature shift, which is a critical aspect of FL, and the method's effectiveness in addressing this issue is not convincingly demonstrated.
- The paper's focus on image datasets may limit the generalizability of the findings to other types of data, such as text or audio.
- The paper could benefit from a more detailed discussion on the theoretical underpinnings of the proposed method and how it compares to existing data augmentation techniques.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors clarify how the proposed method, FedRDN, specifically addresses the feature distribution skew problem in FL, and provide more detailed experimental results to support this claim?
- How does the proposed method perform in different FL settings, particularly in scenarios where data distribution skew is more pronounced?
- Can the authors provide a more detailed analysis of the communication efficiency of the proposed method, especially in comparison to other existing methods?
- Given the focus on image datasets, how do the findings of this study generalize to other types of data, such as text or audio?
- How does the proposed method compare to existing data augmentation techniques in terms of effectiveness and computational efficiency?
- Can the authors provide a more detailed privacy analysis to address concerns about the security of data in FL environments?
