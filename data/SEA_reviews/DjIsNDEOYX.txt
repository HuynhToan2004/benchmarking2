 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel monotonic neural network architecture, termed Scalable Monotonic Neural Networks (SMNN), which addresses the challenge of learning monotonic neural networks by integrating exponentiated units, ReLU units, and confluence units in hidden layers. This architecture aims to ensure monotonicity while maintaining scalability and rapid training through conventional error-backpropagation. The paper provides theoretical proof and experimental validation on both synthetic and real-world datasets, showing comparable performance to state-of-the-art methods while addressing issues like computational efficiency and scalability. The proposed methodology, although innovative, is critiqued for its limited novelty and marginal performance improvements over existing methods.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, making it easy to follow and understand.
- The proposed SMNN architecture is simple, efficient, and scalable, allowing for the repetitive integration of scalable monotonic hidden layers without structural constraints.
- The paper provides a comprehensive comparison of the proposed method with existing approaches, highlighting its strengths and limitations.
- The authors have provided a theoretical proof and experimental validation on both synthetic and real-world datasets, demonstrating the effectiveness of the proposed method.
- The paper addresses the critical issue of monotonicity in neural networks, which is crucial for practical applications across various domains.

**Weaknesses:**
- The novelty of the proposed method is limited, as similar approaches have been previously explored in the literature.
- The paper lacks a thorough discussion on the limitations of the proposed method, which could provide a more balanced view of its strengths and weaknesses.
- The experimental results do not convincingly demonstrate the superiority of the proposed method over existing methods, with marginal performance improvements and inconsistent results across different datasets.
- The paper does not sufficiently address the computational efficiency of the proposed method, which is crucial for practical applications.
- The paper does not provide a detailed analysis of the computational complexity of the proposed method, which is essential for understanding its scalability.
- The paper lacks a detailed comparison with other state-of-the-art methods in terms of computational efficiency and scalability.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors provide a more detailed comparison with other state-of-the-art methods in terms of computational efficiency and scalability?
- How does the proposed method perform when applied to datasets with different characteristics, such as those with varying degrees of monotonicity?
- Could the authors clarify the computational efficiency of the proposed method, especially in terms of training and inference times, and provide a more detailed analysis of its scalability?
- What are the specific advantages of the proposed method over existing methods, particularly in terms of monotonicity and scalability?
- Could the authors provide a more detailed explanation of the confluence unit and its role in the proposed method?
- In the experimental results, why do the MSEs for SMNN differ significantly between training and testing phases, and how does this affect the generalization performance of the model?
