 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel method for generating hyper-realistic human images using a large-scale dataset named HumanVerse, which includes comprehensive annotations like human pose, depth, and surface-normal maps. The proposed Latent Structural Diffusion Model (LSDM) jointly denoises RGB, depth, and normal maps, leveraging a unified network that models image appearance, spatial relationship, and geometry. This model is designed to handle input/output of different domains and guarantees spatial alignment among denoised textures and structures. The paper also presents a Structure-Guided Refiner to enhance visual quality and resolution. Extensive experiments demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed method, showing superior performance compared to existing methods.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel approach to human image generation by leveraging a large-scale dataset with comprehensive annotations, which is a significant contribution to the field.
- The proposed method is technically sound, with a clear motivation and well-designed methodology that includes a unified framework for handling various structural information.
- The paper is well-written, with clear explanations of the methodology and results, making it accessible and understandable to readers.
- The experimental results are robust and demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed method, showing superior performance compared to existing methods.
- The use of a large-scale dataset and the development of a novel dataset annotation pipeline are commendable, as they provide valuable resources for future research.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed discussion on the limitations of the proposed method, which is crucial for understanding the scope and applicability of the findings.
- The novelty of the method is questioned, as it appears to be a combination of existing techniques without significant innovation.
- The paper does not adequately address the potential limitations of the dataset, particularly concerning the diversity and quality of annotations.
- The paper could benefit from a more thorough discussion on the challenges and potential solutions for generating hyper-realistic human images, especially in scenarios where the model may struggle.
- There is a lack of clarity on how the model handles different types of structural information and whether it can be generalized to other domains or tasks.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors provide more details on the limitations of the proposed method and how these might affect the practical application of the findings?
- How does the model handle different types of structural information, such as depth and normal maps, and what are the implications of these different types of information for the generation process?
- Can the authors clarify the role and impact of the Structure-Guided Refiner in the overall performance of the model?
- How does the model ensure the generation of hyper-realistic human images, especially in scenarios where the input conditions might not be sufficient or accurate?
- Could the authors discuss the potential for extending the model to other domains or tasks, and what challenges or opportunities might arise in such applications?
