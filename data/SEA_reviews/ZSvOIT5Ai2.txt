 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel method for discovering and learning interpretable visual concepts from pre-trained Vision-Language Models (VLMs) using a self-supervised approach. It proposes a framework that jointly discovers and learns class-agnostic visual concepts from VLMs, leveraging mutual information between images and concepts to evaluate their discriminability. The method involves a new self-supervised framework that fine-tunes the pre-trained VLM to recognize these concepts, achieving state-of-the-art performance on various benchmarks. The paper also discusses the limitations of previous methods and provides empirical evidence of the effectiveness of the proposed approach.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel approach to discover and learn interpretable visual concepts from pre-trained VLMs, which is a significant contribution to the field.
- The methodology is clearly presented, making it easy to understand and replicate.
- The paper includes comprehensive evaluations, including both automatic and human evaluations, which provide robust evidence of the effectiveness of the proposed approach.
- The proposed method achieves state-of-the-art performance on both recognition accuracy and interpretability, demonstrating the practical utility of the discovered concepts.
- The paper addresses the limitations of previous methods, such as class name bias and non-visual concepts, and provides a detailed analysis of these issues.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a thorough discussion on the limitations of the proposed approach, which could help in understanding the potential challenges and opportunities for future research.
- The paper could benefit from a more detailed explanation of the mutual information method used for concept selection, as it is a crucial aspect of the proposed method.
- The comparison with existing methods such as LaBo and LM4CV could be improved by including a more detailed analysis of the differences and similarities between these methods.
- The paper does not sufficiently address the potential biases in the generated concepts, which could limit the generalizability of the findings.
- The paper does not discuss the potential negative societal impacts of the proposed method, which is a significant oversight given the potential applications of the technology.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors clarify the differences between the proposed method and existing methods like LaBo and LM4CV?
- How does the proposed method handle the potential biases in the generated concepts, and what steps are taken to mitigate these biases?
- Can the authors provide more details on the mutual information method used for concept selection, particularly how this method was chosen and how it compares to other methods?
- How does the proposed method perform in few-shot settings, and what are the implications of using a smaller number of concepts in these settings?
- Can the authors discuss the potential negative societal impacts of the proposed method and how these impacts could be mitigated?
