 **Summary:**
The paper investigates the impact of data balancing on reducing biases in multimodal models, specifically focusing on the CLIP model. It introduces a novel data balancing algorithm designed to address both representation and association biases in multimodal datasets. The study explores the effectiveness of this approach through extensive experiments and evaluations, revealing that while fine-tuning can mitigate representation biases, it is less effective for association biases. The paper also discusses the mixed impact of data balancing on model quality, noting that it can enhance zero- and few-shot classification but hinder retrieval. The authors provide a theoretical analysis of the convergence rate of the proposed algorithm and present a comprehensive evaluation of data balancing in multimodal systems.

**Strengths:**

- The authors have conducted a comprehensive evaluation of the effectiveness of data balancing in multimodal systems, providing insights into the strengths and limitations of this approach.
- The study includes a novel data balancing algorithm that is designed to address both representation and association biases in multimodal datasets, which is a significant contribution to the field.
- The paper provides a theoretical analysis of the convergence rate of the proposed algorithm, which adds depth to the understanding of the methodology used.
