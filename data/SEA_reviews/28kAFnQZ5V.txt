 **Summary:**
The paper introduces the Tensorized Attention Model (TAM), an extension of the Tensorized Transformer that leverages Tucker decomposition to enhance the Transformer's ability to capture complex multi-object relationships. TAM is designed to handle various object types and is evaluated using datasets like Reddit and TweetQA, demonstrating superior performance in response selection tasks. The model's innovative approach includes the addition of a new semantic component to the attention mechanism, which is shown to improve performance over existing methods. However, the paper's scope is limited to specific datasets, and the novelty of the Tucker decomposition approach is questioned due to its similarity to existing methods.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, making it easy to follow and understand.
- The proposed method, TAM, is simple yet effective, and the use of Tucker decomposition is novel and interesting.
- The experiments conducted are sufficient, demonstrating the effectiveness of TAM over existing methods.
- The paper introduces a new semantic component to the attention mechanism, which is a novel approach.
- The integration of TAM into the Transformer encoder is seamless, enhancing the model's ability to capture multi-object relationships.
- The use of Tucker decomposition in TAM is innovative and potentially more efficient than previous methods.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a clear definition and role of the "semantic" component in the model, which could lead to confusion.
- The novelty of the paper is limited, as Tucker decomposition has been used in similar contexts before.
- The experimental scope is limited to specific datasets, and it would be beneficial to see how TAM performs on other datasets like TweetQA.
- The paper does not sufficiently differentiate TAM from existing methods, particularly in its use of Tucker decomposition.
- The paper does not provide sufficient implementation details, which could hinder reproducibility.
- The paper lacks a comprehensive discussion on the limitations of TAM and its potential negative societal impacts.

**Questions:**
- Could you clarify the role and definition of the "semantic" component in TAM?
- How does TAM perform on other datasets like TweetQA?
- What are the specific advantages of TAM over existing methods, particularly in terms of computational efficiency and performance?
- Can you provide more details on the implementation of TAM, including hyperparameters and training procedures?
- How does TAM handle different object types in real-world scenarios, and what are the implications for its generalizability?
- Could you discuss the limitations of TAM and its potential negative societal impacts?
