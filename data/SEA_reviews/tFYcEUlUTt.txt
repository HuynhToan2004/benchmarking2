 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel approach to mesh-based physical simulations using a two-stage model: a coarse long-term prediction model using a graph ODE and a refinement model using channel aggregation. The method leverages a multi-task learning framework to predict trajectories, which are refined through interpolation. This approach aims to address the challenges of long-term predictions by integrating future states into the prediction process. Extensive experiments on benchmark datasets demonstrate the model's effectiveness in reducing prediction errors compared to existing methods. However, the paper lacks a detailed comparison with more recent and relevant baselines, and the efficiency and applicability to more complex physical systems are not thoroughly explored.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, making it easy to follow and understand the proposed methodology.
- The introduction of a new perspective on long-term prediction in mesh-based physical simulations is innovative and contributes to the field.
- The proposed method achieves state-of-the-art results on four benchmark datasets, demonstrating its effectiveness.
- The paper includes a comprehensive ablation study that supports the effectiveness of the proposed method.
- The use of a graph ODE for long-term prediction and a channel aggregation strategy for refinement is novel and contributes to the advancement of the field.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed comparison with more recent and relevant baselines, which could have provided a clearer picture of the method's performance relative to current state-of-the-art methods.
- The efficiency of the proposed method is not thoroughly explored, particularly in terms of computational cost and the scalability of the method to more complex physical systems.
- The paper does not adequately discuss the limitations of the proposed method, which could help in understanding its applicability and potential drawbacks.
- The paper could benefit from a more detailed discussion on the choice of hyperparameters and their impact on the method's performance.
- The paper does not adequately address the potential limitations of the proposed method, such as its applicability to more complex physical systems and its efficiency in long-term simulations.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors clarify the choice of baselines and discuss how the proposed method compares to more recent and relevant methods in the field?
- How does the proposed method perform in terms of computational cost and scalability, particularly in long-term simulations?
- Could the authors provide a more detailed discussion on the limitations of the proposed method and potential areas for future research?
- In the ablation study, how does the refinement step contribute to the overall performance of the method?
- How does the method perform when applied to more complex physical systems, and what are the potential challenges or limitations in such applications?
- Can the authors discuss the potential impact of the choice of hyperparameters on the method's performance and provide guidelines for choosing these parameters?
