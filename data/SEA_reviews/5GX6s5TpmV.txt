 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel attack method, termed Certification Aware Attack, which leverages the certified radius information to construct smaller adversarial perturbations. This method exploits the information provided by certifications to guide the attack process, potentially leading to more effective evasion attacks. The paper provides empirical evidence that this attack can be more effective than traditional methods, demonstrating its potential to reduce the size of adversarial perturbations significantly. However, the paper is criticized for its lack of theoretical support and the limited scope of its experiments, which primarily focus on a single certification method. The paper also lacks a comprehensive discussion on the broader implications and potential mitigations of the proposed attack.
