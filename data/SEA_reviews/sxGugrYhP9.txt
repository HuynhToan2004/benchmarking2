 **Summary:**
The paper introduces BatteryML, an open-source platform designed to unify data preprocessing, feature extraction, and implementation of both traditional and state-of-the-art models for battery degradation modeling. BatteryML aims to address the challenges of heterogeneous battery data, domain knowledge for feature design, and model applicability by offering a standardized data representation method and comprehensive tools for battery research tasks. The platform integrates various models and covers essential battery research tasks, providing a holistic suite of tools that can be used by experts from diverse backgrounds. However, the paper lacks a detailed discussion on the methodological advancements of BatteryML and its contributions to the field, which is crucial for understanding its significance and impact.

**Strengths:**
- BatteryML is an open-source platform that simplifies every stage of the process, from data preprocessing and feature extraction to applying both traditional and avant-garde models.
- BatteryML introduces a standardized data representation method, which provides a uniform evaluation criterion for assessing battery degradation, promoting robust comparisons and insights across diverse battery contexts.
- BatteryML offers a comprehensive suite of tools that covers essential battery research tasks like State of Charge (SOC), State of Health (SOH), and Remaining Useful Life (RUL).
- BatteryML integrates a broad spectrum of tasks and models, both conventional and state-of-the-art, ensuring clear demarcation between models and data processing stages, facilitating effortless integration and refinement by machine learning experts.
- The platform's modular design ensures clear demarcation between models and data processing stages, facilitating effortless integration and refinement by machine learning experts.
- BatteryML provides a holistic suite of tools that can be used by experts from diverse backgrounds, including machine learning professionals and battery experts.
