 **Summary:**
The paper addresses the issue of generating diverse image restorations using diffusion models, focusing on the heavy-tailed nature of the posterior distribution which can limit the diversity of outputs. The authors propose a method that guides the generation process to maintain a minimum distance between outputs, thus increasing the diversity of the generated images. This is achieved by adding a gradient of a loss function that captures the dissimilarity between predictions to the loss function. The method is evaluated through user studies and quantitative measures, showing improvements in diversity without significant loss in quality. However, the paper has been critiqued for its limited scope in terms of the number of tasks evaluated and the lack of comprehensive quantitative results.

**Strengths:**
- The paper addresses a significant and interesting problem of generating diverse image restorations, which is a relevant issue in the field of image processing.
- The proposed method is well-motivated and easy to follow, with a clear presentation that makes the paper accessible to readers.
- The paper provides a thorough analysis of the diversity of the posterior distribution and its impact on the diversity of generated images, which is a novel approach.
- The results of the user studies are convincing, showing that the proposed method can improve the diversity of the generated images without significantly affecting their quality.
- The paper is well-written, making it easy to understand, and includes a comprehensive literature review that positions the work effectively within the current research landscape.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a comprehensive quantitative evaluation, with the results being limited to only a few tasks and not comparing the proposed method to a sufficient number of baselines.
- The methodology section is not clearly explained, particularly the application of the guidance mechanism and the selection of parameters such as $D$.
- The paper does not include a comparison with other methods that could potentially improve diversity, such as those using different sampling strategies or clustering techniques.
- The paper could benefit from more diverse examples in the figures to better illustrate the effectiveness of the proposed method across different types of images.
- The paper's claims about the diversity of the generated images are not well-supported by the experimental results, which could be enhanced by including more detailed quantitative metrics and comparisons.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors clarify the methodology section, particularly the application of the guidance mechanism and the selection of parameters such as $D$?
- How does the proposed method compare to other methods that use different sampling strategies or clustering techniques for improving diversity?
- Could the authors provide more detailed quantitative results, including a comparison of the diversity of the generated images across different tasks and a broader range of baselines?
- How does the proposed method handle the trade-off between diversity and quality, especially in terms of the user's perception of the diversity and the overall image quality?
- Could the authors include more diverse examples in the figures to better illustrate the effectiveness of the proposed method across different types of images?
