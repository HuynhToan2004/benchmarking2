 **Summary:**
The paper introduces CAST, a novel self-training method for tabular data that leverages cluster-awareness to enhance pseudo-labeling accuracy. By assigning high confidence to pseudo-labels in high-density regions and low confidence to those in low-density regions, CAST aims to improve the reliability of pseudo-labels, thus benefiting downstream supervised learning. The method's effectiveness is demonstrated through experiments on various datasets, showing that CAST outperforms existing self-training methods in terms of pseudo-label quality and downstream classification accuracy. However, concerns were raised about the novelty of the approach and its generalizability to other domains, particularly where density estimation is challenging.

**Strengths:**
- The paper addresses an important and timely problem of improving self-training on tabular data, which is crucial for data-scarce scenarios.
- The proposed method, CAST, is simple, intuitive, and easy to implement, requiring only a small number of hyperparameters.
- The experiments are well-designed and demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed method, showing improvements in pseudo-label quality and downstream classification accuracy.
- The paper is well-written, with clear explanations of the methodology and the experimental results, making it accessible and understandable.
- The method's compatibility with existing self-training algorithms and its adaptability to various models make it a versatile tool for improving self-training performance.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a thorough literature review, particularly in the area of pseudo-labeling in self-training, which could have provided a better context for the proposed method.
- The novelty of the approach is limited as the idea of assigning pseudo-labels based on density has been explored in previous works.
- The experiments are limited to tabular data, which might not generalize well to other domains such as images or text where density estimation is more challenging.
- The paper does not provide a clear comparison with existing methods in terms of performance metrics, such as the number of pseudo-labels used or the specific performance improvements.
- The paper could benefit from a more detailed discussion on the choice of density estimators and their impact on the method's performance.
- The paper does not sufficiently address the issue of high ECE values, which could indicate potential issues with the calibration of the classifier.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors provide more details on how the density estimator is chosen and its impact on the method's performance?
- How does the proposed method perform when applied to other types of data, such as images or text?
- Could the authors clarify the choice of threshold (0.6) for fixed-threshold pseudo-labeling and explain why this value was chosen?
- What are the specific performance improvements of the proposed method compared to existing methods, and how do these improvements compare in terms of pseudo-label quality and downstream classification accuracy?
- How does the proposed method handle the issue of high ECE values, and what steps are taken to ensure the calibration of the classifier?
- Can the authors provide more details on the experimental setup, particularly how the hyperparameters were chosen and how they affect the method's performance?
