 **Summary:**
The paper investigates the performance gap between attention-based and gated-convolution language models, particularly focusing on the associative recall (AR) task. It reveals that attention-based models outperform gated-convolution models in AR, which is attributed to the inability of the latter to efficiently handle variable-distance token-to-token interactions. The authors propose a new synthetic task, multi-query associative recall (Mqar), to better capture the differences between attention and gated-convolution models. They also explore how adding attention to gated-convolution models can enhance their performance in AR tasks. Theoretical and empirical analyses are provided to support these findings, along with recommendations for improving gated-convolution models.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, clear, and easy to follow, with a clear motivation and a well-defined problem that is relevant to the field.
- The authors provide a comprehensive theoretical analysis, which is supported by extensive empirical studies, and the results are well-presented and easy to interpret.
- The paper includes a novel definition of the multi-query associative recall (Mqar) task, which is a significant contribution to the field.
- The study on the associative recall gap between attention and gated convolution models is thorough and provides valuable insights into the limitations of gated convolution models.
- The authors propose a simple and effective solution to the associative recall problem by adding attention to gated convolution models, which shows promising results.
- The paper is well-structured, with a clear flow of ideas and logical progression, making it easy to understand the authors' arguments and conclusions.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed discussion on the limitations of the proposed solutions, particularly in terms of practical implementation and scalability.
- The theoretical analysis and empirical evidence provided do not fully explain the observed performance gaps, and the paper could benefit from a more in-depth analysis of the underlying causes.
- The synthetic Mqar task used in the study may not fully capture the complexities of real-world language modeling tasks, which could limit the generalizability of the findings.
- The paper could benefit from a more detailed discussion on the scalability and practical implications of the proposed solutions, especially in larger models.
- There is a lack of discussion on the limitations of the proposed solutions and their practical implementation, which could affect the adoption of the proposed methods.
- The paper could benefit from a more comprehensive comparison with existing methods, such as the SeqBoat architecture, to better understand the performance of the proposed solutions.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors clarify the relationship between the AR hits and the performance of the model? Specifically, how does the model's performance on AR hits correlate with its overall performance?
- How does the proposed solution compare to other methods, such as the SeqBoat architecture, in terms of performance and practical implementation?
- Could the authors provide more details on the scalability of the proposed solutions, especially in larger models?
- How does the proposed solution address the scalability issues observed in gated convolution models?
- Could the authors elaborate on the practical implications of the proposed solutions, particularly in terms of their implementation and adoption in real-world scenarios?
- What are the main challenges in scaling up the proposed solutions to larger models, and how do the authors address these challenges?
