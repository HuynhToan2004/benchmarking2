 **Summary:**
The paper introduces the Groupwise Diffusion Model (GDM), which divides data into multiple groups and processes each group independently, thereby influencing only specific elements of the data. This model generalizes autoregressive and cascaded diffusion models, providing a unified framework for various applications such as image editing and semantic disentanglement. GDM's novelty lies in its ability to interpret the latent space in a group-wise manner, enabling the manipulation of different aspects of data at various levels of abstraction. The model's effectiveness is demonstrated through experiments, including image generation and editing, showcasing its potential for further research and practical applications.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel approach to diffusion models by dividing data into multiple groups, which allows for more efficient and effective processing of data, particularly in scenarios where data is naturally structured.
- The proposed method is flexible and can be adapted to various applications, including image editing and generation, demonstrating its versatility and potential for broader use cases.
- The paper is well-written, with clear explanations of the proposed method and its applications, making it accessible to readers and facilitating understanding of the proposed ideas.
- The experiments conducted are comprehensive, providing evidence of the method's effectiveness and its potential for further research and development.
- The method's ability to disentangle data into different groups, each with distinct characteristics, offers a new perspective on how data can be manipulated and understood, which is particularly useful in applications like image editing and generation.
