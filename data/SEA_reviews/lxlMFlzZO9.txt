 **Summary:**
The paper introduces DS-Prover, a novel dynamic sampling method for theorem proving using large language models (LLMs) and interactive theorem provers (ITPs). DS-Prover adjusts the number of tactics applied based on the remaining time during proof search, aiming to balance exploration and exploitation. The method also includes an augmentation strategy to enhance the training dataset by decomposing tactics with multiple premises into tactics with single premises, thereby providing the model with more examples to learn from. The approach is evaluated on the Mathlib dataset of the Lean theorem prover and two standard datasets, MiniF2F and ProofNet, showing significant performance gains over state-of-the-art methods. Despite these advancements, the paper's novelty is questioned due to similarities with existing methods like DT-Solver, and the lack of comprehensive comparisons with other recent works in the field.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, making it easy to understand and follow, with clear explanations of the methodology and experiments.
- The proposed method, DS-Prover, is innovative and effective, dynamically adjusting the number of tactics to apply based on remaining time, which is a novel approach in theorem proving.
- The augmentation strategy for training data is a significant contribution, providing the model with more examples to learn from and potentially improving performance.
- The paper achieves state-of-the-art results on standard benchmarks, demonstrating the effectiveness of the proposed method.
- The website developed for theorem proving in Lean is a valuable resource for the community, facilitating the submission and proof of mathematical theorems.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a comprehensive comparison with other recent works in the field, particularly with DT-Solver, which has similarities in its approach.
- The paper does not discuss the limitations of the proposed method, which could provide valuable insights for future research.
- The experimental results are not convincingly superior to existing methods, with only marginal improvements over state-of-the-art methods.
- The paper does not provide sufficient details on the training process, such as the loss function used and the specifics of the training algorithm.
- The novelty of the DS-Prover method is questioned due to its similarity to existing methods like DT-Solver, which also uses a dynamic approach for tactic selection.
- The paper does not adequately address the issue of limited formalized proofs in ITP libraries, which could limit the practical applicability of the proposed method.

**Questions:**
- How does DS-Prover compare to DT-Solver in terms of methodology and performance?
- Can the authors provide more details on the training process, such as the loss function used and the specifics of the training algorithm?
- What are the specific advantages of the DS-Prover method over existing methods like DT-Solver, especially in terms of novelty and performance?
- How does the DS-Prover method handle the issue of limited formalized proofs in ITP libraries?
- Can the authors clarify the role of the DS-Prover method in automating theorem proving and discuss its potential impact on the field?
