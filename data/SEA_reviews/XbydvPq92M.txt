 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel method for learning low-dimensional latent representations from high-dimensional data, focusing on the adaptive compression of latent variables ordered by likelihood maximization. This method, termed the information-ordered bottleneck (IOB), is designed to dynamically adjust the width of the latent bottleneck, thereby allowing for the capture of crucial information in the first latent variables. The IOB is shown to be effective in estimating global intrinsic dimensionality and can be applied to various datasets, including synthetic and real-world data. The method's effectiveness is demonstrated through experiments on different datasets, showcasing its ability to compress data into meaningful latent variables. However, the paper has been critiqued for its lack of clarity in explaining the method's operation and its limited comparison with existing methods.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, easy to follow, and includes a comprehensive literature review, effectively positioning the work within the existing body of knowledge.
- The method is novel and well-motivated, with a clear explanation of the intuition behind the method and its implementation.
- The paper includes a variety of experiments that demonstrate the effectiveness of the method in various applications, including synthetic and real-world data.
- The methodology for estimating intrinsic dimensionality using IOBs is novel and shows state-of-the-art performance on high-dimensional synthetic experiments.
- The paper includes a comprehensive set of baselines and a detailed experimental section that demonstrates the effectiveness of the method across different datasets.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks clarity in explaining the method's operation, particularly how the latent variables are ordered and how the method is used to estimate intrinsic dimensionality.
- There is a lack of detailed comparison with existing methods, such as nested dropout and triangular dropout, which are similar in approach.
- The paper does not provide sufficient details on the computational requirements and scalability of the method, which could limit its practical application in large-scale datasets.
- The paper does not adequately address the issue of interpretability, which is a critical aspect of the method's application in data exploration and model interpretability.
- The paper's claims of novelty and contribution are somewhat overstated, as the method builds on existing techniques and does not offer significant new insights or approaches.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors clarify how the latent variables are ordered and how this ordering is used to estimate intrinsic dimensionality?
- How does the method compare in terms of computational efficiency and scalability with existing methods, particularly in large-scale datasets?
- Can the authors provide more detailed results on the interpretability of the latent variables, especially in terms of their semantic meaning and the ability to guide data exploration?
- How does the method perform in more complex and realistic datasets, such as those used in other studies?
- Can the authors discuss the potential applications of the method in real-world scenarios and how it could be integrated into existing systems or workflows?
