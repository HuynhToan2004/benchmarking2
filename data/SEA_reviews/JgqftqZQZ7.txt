 **Summary:**
The paper introduces FLATTEN, a novel flow-guided attention mechanism designed to enhance visual consistency in text-to-video editing. By leveraging optical flow to guide attention mechanisms, FLATTEN ensures that patches across different frames maintain consistency, addressing a significant challenge in current text-to-video editing methods. The proposed method is training-free and can be seamlessly integrated into existing diffusion-based text-to-video editing methods, improving their visual consistency. Extensive experiments demonstrate that FLATTEN achieves state-of-the-art performance on benchmarks like TGVE-D and TGVE-V, and can be easily integrated into other diffusion-based T2V editing methods.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel flow-guided attention mechanism, FLATTEN, which is a significant advancement in the field of text-to-video editing.
- The method is training-free and can be easily integrated into existing diffusion-based text-to-video editing methods, enhancing their performance.
- The paper is well-written, making it easy to understand, and includes extensive experiments that demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed method.
- The use of optical flow to guide attention mechanisms is a novel approach that effectively addresses the issue of visual inconsistency in text-to-video editing.
- The method achieves state-of-the-art performance on benchmarks like TGVE-D and TGVE-V, and the results are visually consistent.
- The paper includes an ablation study that verifies the contributions of different modules to the overall performance.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed comparison with other state-of-the-art methods, particularly in terms of computational efficiency and visual fidelity.
- The integration of FLATTEN with other methods like ControlVideo shows only marginal improvements, raising questions about its effectiveness in different contexts.
- The method's dependency on the accuracy of optical flow estimation could affect the quality of the results, and the paper does not discuss potential failures or limitations of this approach.
- The paper does not provide sufficient details on the implementation of the method, such as the number of timesteps used for DDIM inversion and sampling.
- The paper does not discuss the limitations of the proposed method, which could help in understanding its applicability and potential challenges.
- The paper's claims about the method's ability to handle complex motions and long videos are not substantiated with sufficient evidence or analysis.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors provide a more detailed comparison with other state-of-the-art methods, particularly in terms of computational efficiency and visual fidelity?
- How does the proposed method handle scenarios where optical flow estimation is inaccurate?
- Could the authors discuss the potential limitations of the proposed method and provide examples or scenarios where it may not be effective?
- How does the proposed method compare to other methods in terms of handling complex motions and long videos?
- Could the authors provide more details on the implementation of the method, such as the number of timesteps used for DDIM inversion and sampling?
- Can the authors clarify the discrepancies in the results between the proposed method and other methods, particularly in the user study?
