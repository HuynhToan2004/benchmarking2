 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel representation space for image restoration tasks, termed aRGB, which aims to enhance the quality of restored images by integrating structural information with color preservation. This is achieved through a mixture of experts (MoE) encoder and a linear decoder. The aRGB space is designed to be orthogonally decomposable into color and structure encoding, allowing for better supervision of per-pixel distances. The authors demonstrate that this new representation space can improve performance across various image restoration tasks, including super-resolution, denoising, and deblurring, without modifying the architecture or other hyperparameters. However, the paper's claims of novelty and the effectiveness of the aRGB space are questioned, with concerns about the incremental nature of the contributions and the lack of comprehensive comparisons with existing methods.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel aRGB representation space that is orthogonally decomposable into color and structure encoding, providing a new perspective for image restoration.
- The use of a mixture of experts (MoE) for image encoding is innovative and potentially beneficial for future research.
- The paper is well-written, clear, and easy to follow, with a clear motivation and good experimental results.
- The proposed method is simple and effective, showing good performance across various tasks.
- The paper provides a clear and detailed explanation of the aRGB autoencoder and its training, which is crucial for understanding the proposed method.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a thorough comparison with existing methods, particularly in terms of the aRGB space's effectiveness and its contribution to image restoration.
- The novelty of the aRGB space is questionable, as it seems to be a straightforward application of existing techniques without significant innovation.
- The paper's claims about the aRGB space's superiority over traditional RGB representation are not convincingly supported by the experimental results.
- The paper does not sufficiently discuss the limitations of the proposed method, such as its applicability to different types of images and its potential impact on computational complexity.
- The paper's experimental results, while showing improvements, do not consistently outperform state-of-the-art methods, raising concerns about the effectiveness of the aRGB space.
- The paper's organization and structure could be improved, particularly in the presentation of results and the clarity of the experimental setup.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors provide a more detailed comparison with existing methods, particularly in terms of the aRGB space's effectiveness and its contribution to image restoration?
- How does the aRGB space perform in terms of computational complexity compared to traditional RGB representation?
- Is there a potential for the aRGB space to be used in other types of image processing tasks beyond restoration?
- Could the authors clarify the role and effectiveness of the aRGB space in guiding the training of image restoration models?
- How does the proposed method compare to other state-of-the-art methods in terms of computational efficiency and resource requirements?
- Can the authors provide more detailed experimental results, particularly in terms of the impact of different hyperparameters and training configurations on the performance of the aRGB space?
