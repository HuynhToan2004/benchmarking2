 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel method named Beam Enumeration for molecular design, aimed at improving sample efficiency and interpretability in molecular generation. This method exhaustively enumerates probable sub-sequences from language-based molecular generative models, extracting meaningful substructures that can be used to self-condition the model's generation. Beam Enumeration is demonstrated to enhance the performance of existing models like Augmented Memory, showing significant improvements in sample efficiency and interpretability. The method's effectiveness is validated through experiments on drug discovery case studies, demonstrating its practical applicability.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, clear, and easy to follow, with a comprehensive discussion on related work.
- The proposed method, Beam Enumeration, is novel and effectively improves sample efficiency in molecular design.
- The experiments are extensive, covering a variety of tasks and including ablation studies, which demonstrate the effectiveness of the method.
- The paper provides a detailed analysis of the behavior of Beam Enumeration and proposes a new metric, Oracle Burden, to measure sample efficiency.
- The code is provided, which is a significant contribution to the reproducibility of the research.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper does not sufficiently discuss the limitations of the proposed method, which could affect the reliability of the results.
- The evaluation of the method is limited to specific tasks, which may not fully demonstrate its general applicability.
- The paper lacks a detailed discussion on the computational complexity and scalability of Beam Enumeration, especially in larger molecular design tasks.
- The paper does not adequately address the interpretability aspect of the method, which is a significant claim.
- The paper does not compare the proposed method with other existing methods, which could provide a better understanding of its advantages and disadvantages.
- The paper does not include a discussion on the ethical implications of the research.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors provide a more detailed discussion on the limitations of the proposed method and how these might affect the reliability of the results?
- How does the computational complexity of Beam Enumeration scale with the size of the molecular design task?
- Can the authors provide a more detailed discussion on the interpretability aspect of the method and how it can be practically applied in molecular design?
- How does the proposed method compare with other existing methods in terms of sample efficiency and interpretability?
- Can the authors provide a more detailed comparison of the proposed method with other existing methods, including a discussion on the trade-offs between sample efficiency and interpretability?
