 **Summary:**
The paper investigates offline reinforcement learning (RL) in partially observable Markov decision processes (POMDPs) where the state is partially observed, and the dataset is collected offline. It demonstrates that standard offline RL algorithms suffer from poor sample complexity in POMDPs due to the lack of trajectory stitching. To address this, the paper introduces a bisimulation loss to learn a compact representation of observation histories, aiming to improve sample complexity. Theoretical analysis is provided to justify the use of this loss, and empirical results show its effectiveness across various tasks. However, the paper has been critiqued for its lack of clarity in explaining the theoretical results, the choice of environments, and the comparative analysis with other offline RL algorithms.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, clear, and easy to follow, with a clear motivation and a good literature review.
- It addresses a significant problem in offline RL by focusing on the use of observation histories, which is a novel approach.
- The paper provides both theoretical and empirical results, showing that the bisimulation loss can be used to improve sample complexity in POMDPs.
- The authors have demonstrated the effectiveness of their method through experiments in various environments, including a natural language game, which is a significant contribution.
- The paper introduces a new approach to improving sample complexity in offline RL by learning a compact representation of observation histories, which is a promising direction for future research.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a clear explanation of the theoretical results, particularly in how the bisimulation loss improves sample complexity.
- The environments used in the experiments are not clearly defined, and there is a lack of detail on how the datasets are collected and preprocessed.
- The paper does not compare its results with other offline RL algorithms, which could provide a more robust evaluation of the proposed method.
- The theoretical analysis is limited to tabular MDPs, which might not generalize well to more complex scenarios.
- The paper does not adequately address the limitations of its approach, such as the assumptions made about the dataset and the environment.
- There are some minor issues with the clarity and presentation of the paper, including unclear definitions and notation.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors clarify how the bisimulation loss improves sample complexity and provide a more detailed explanation of the theoretical results?
- How does the proposed method compare with other offline RL algorithms, especially in terms of sample complexity and computational efficiency?
- What are the specific environments and datasets used in the experiments, and how are they preprocessed to ensure a fair comparison with other algorithms?
- How does the proposed method handle the challenge of collecting a sufficient amount of data for offline RL, especially in scenarios where the data is sparse or noisy?
- Can the authors provide more details on the choice of environments and the rationale behind the experimental setup?
- How does the proposed method ensure that the learned representations accurately capture the relevant information from the observation histories, and what are the implications if this information is missing or incorrect?
