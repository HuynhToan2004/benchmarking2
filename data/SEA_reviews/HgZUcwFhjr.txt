 **Summary:**
The paper investigates the spatial relation prediction (SRP) task using a transformer-based architecture, specifically focusing on the RelatiViT model. The authors propose a novel benchmark dataset, SpatialSense+, to address the ambiguities in existing datasets. The RelatiViT model is designed to capture spatial relationships effectively by utilizing a transformer-based architecture, which is evaluated against various baselines including naive and existing models. The paper also provides a detailed analysis of the model's performance and compares it with other models like RegionViT and CrossAttnViT. The results show that RelatiViT outperforms these models, suggesting its effectiveness in spatial relation prediction.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, easy to follow, and provides a clear explanation of the proposed method and its contributions.
- The introduction of a new benchmark dataset, SpatialSense+, addresses the ambiguities in existing datasets and enhances the reliability of spatial relation prediction.
- The paper is original in its approach, focusing on the spatial relation prediction task, which is a significant and less explored area in computer vision.
- The proposed RelatiViT model is innovative and well-designed, utilizing a transformer-based architecture to capture spatial relationships effectively.
- The paper includes a comprehensive set of experiments and ablation studies, which provide a detailed analysis of the model's performance and the impact of different design choices.
- The results show that the proposed model outperforms existing models, demonstrating the effectiveness of the proposed method.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed discussion on the limitations of the proposed method and potential negative societal impacts.
- There is a need for more comparative analysis with other state-of-the-art models to establish the superiority of the proposed method more convincingly.
- The paper could benefit from a broader range of experiments, including different dataset sizes and varying model sizes to assess the scalability and robustness of the proposed method.
- The novelty of the proposed method is questionable as it primarily combines existing modules without significant innovation.
- The paper could provide more detailed explanations and visualizations, such as attention maps, to better illustrate the model's functionality and the spatial relationships it captures.
- The paper does not sufficiently discuss the challenges and complexities of the spatial relation prediction task, which could undermine the perceived difficulty and significance of the research.

**Questions:**
- How does the proposed method perform on other spatial relation datasets, and what are the implications for its generalizability?
- Can the authors provide more detailed explanations or visualizations to better illustrate the model's functionality and the spatial relationships it captures?
- How does the proposed method handle different types of spatial relationships, such as contact and non-contact relations?
- Could the authors elaborate on the challenges and complexities of the spatial relation prediction task and how their method addresses these challenges?
- How does the proposed method compare in terms of computational efficiency and model size with other state-of-the-art models?
- What are the potential negative societal impacts of the proposed method, and how are these addressed or mitigated?
