 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel framework, Individualized Markov Decision Processes (iMDPs), to model individual-specific factors that influence state transitions in reinforcement learning. The framework uses a Variational Autoencoder (VAE) to learn latent individual-specific factors from observed state-action trajectories, which are then used to train individualized policies. The authors provide theoretical guarantees for the identifiability of latent state-transition processes, even when these processes are nonlinear. The paper is supported by experiments on both synthetic and real-world datasets, demonstrating the effectiveness of the proposed method in identifying latent state-transition processes and learning individualized RL policies.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel approach to learning individualized state-transition processes from observed state-action trajectories, which is crucial for making individualized policies in RL.
- The framework integrates individual-specific factors into decision-making processes, modeling these factors as latent confounders, which is a novel approach.
- The paper provides theoretical guarantees, which are significant for the field and enhance the credibility of the proposed method.
- The method is supported by both synthetic and real-world experiments, demonstrating its effectiveness in identifying latent state-transition processes and learning individualized RL policies.
- The paper is well-written, with clear presentation of the problem and the proposed method, which aids in understanding the complex concepts discussed.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed discussion on the limitations of the proposed framework, which is crucial for understanding its applicability and potential drawbacks.
- The paper does not provide a clear explanation of how the method handles continuous latent variables, which are common in real-world applications.
- The paper does not discuss how the method can be applied to time-varying latent variables, which are prevalent in many real-world applications.
- The paper does not provide a detailed comparison with existing methods, which makes it difficult to assess the novelty and effectiveness of the proposed method.
- The paper does not discuss the computational complexity of the proposed method, which is a significant concern for practical applications.
- The paper does not discuss the scalability of the proposed method to large-scale applications, which is a critical factor for practical deployment.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors provide more details on how the method handles continuous latent variables and time-varying latent variables?
- How does the proposed method compare with existing methods in terms of computational complexity and scalability?
- Could the authors discuss the potential applications of the proposed method in different domains, such as healthcare, education, and e-commerce?
- How does the method ensure that the individualized policies are effective in real-world scenarios where data is limited?
- Can the authors provide more details on the limitations of the proposed framework and how these limitations affect the practical applicability of the method?
- How does the method handle the issue of data sparsity in real-world applications, where data is often limited and noisy?
