 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel watermarking algorithm for Large Language Models (LLMs) that leverages semantic embeddings to generate watermark logits. This approach aims to enhance both attack and security robustness by using semantic information from preceding tokens. The method involves training a watermark model to convert semantic embeddings into watermark logits, which are then used to embed watermarks into text. The robustness of the watermark is demonstrated through various experiments, including synonym substitution and text paraphrasing. However, concerns about the novelty and clarity of the method, as well as the depth of the experimental validation, were raised.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written and easy to follow, with a clear presentation of the proposed method and its components.
- The novel approach of using semantic embeddings to generate watermark logits is innovative and shows promise in improving robustness against semantic attacks.
- The paper provides a thorough evaluation of the proposed method, including robustness analysis and comparisons with existing watermarking algorithms.
- The experimental results demonstrate that the proposed method can achieve both attack robustness and security robustness, as shown in Table 1.
- The paper includes a detailed analysis of the proposed method, which helps in understanding its effectiveness and potential limitations.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed explanation of how the watermark logits are generated and how they are used to detect watermarks, which could confuse readers unfamiliar with the method.
- The novelty of the proposed method is questionable, as it appears to be a modification of existing methods such as KGW, with similarities in the watermark generation and detection processes.
- The experimental results do not convincingly demonstrate the superiority of the proposed method over existing methods, especially in terms of robustness and security.
- The paper does not adequately address the potential impact of the watermarking on text quality, which is crucial for maintaining the effectiveness and reliability of the watermark.
- There is a lack of clarity in the methodology and the explanation of certain terms and processes, which could hinder the understanding of the proposed method.
- The paper does not discuss the potential negative societal impacts of the proposed method, which is a significant omission in the ethical evaluation of the technology.

**Questions:**
- Can you provide a more detailed explanation of how the watermark logits are generated and how they are used to detect watermarks?
- How does the proposed method compare to existing methods in terms of robustness and security, especially under different attack scenarios?
- Can you provide more examples or case studies to illustrate the effectiveness of the proposed method in real-world scenarios?
- How does the proposed method perform under different decoding strategies, such as greedy decoding or beam search?
- Can you clarify the terms and processes used in the paper, particularly those that are not well-explained or are confusing?
- How does the proposed method address the trade-off between attack robustness and security robustness, and what are the implications of this trade-off for the practical use of the watermark?
- Can you discuss the potential negative societal impacts of the proposed method and how these impacts could be mitigated or addressed?
