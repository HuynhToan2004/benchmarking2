 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel method called Gode, which integrates graph representations of individual molecules with multi-domain biochemical data from knowledge graphs. This fusion enhances molecular property prediction by leveraging both chemical and biological information. The methodology involves pre-training two graph neural networks on different graph structures, combined with contrastive learning, to fuse molecular structures with their corresponding knowledge graph substructures. The approach is validated through extensive experiments on 11 chemical property tasks, showing that it outperforms existing benchmarks. However, concerns are raised about the novelty of the approach and the lack of comprehensive comparison with existing methods.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, with clear presentation and a clear methodology.
- The motivation is well-explained, and the methodology is straightforward and easy to follow.
- The proposed method outperforms existing benchmarks, demonstrating its effectiveness in molecular property prediction tasks.
- The use of a knowledge graph to enhance molecular representation is novel and interesting, showing potential for broader application in different domains.
- The paper provides extensive experiments and ablation studies, which help in understanding the contributions of different components of the proposed method.

**Weaknesses:**
- The novelty of the method is questionable, as similar approaches have been previously proposed, such as combining molecular graph and knowledge graph for molecular property prediction.
- The paper lacks a comprehensive comparison with existing methods, which makes it difficult to gauge the true advancement of the proposed method.
- There is a lack of detailed explanation for some experimental results and methodological choices, such as the selection of negative samples and the impact of different components in the ablation study.
- The paper does not provide a detailed analysis of the computational cost and training time, which are crucial for understanding the scalability of the proposed method.
- The paper does not sufficiently address the limitations of the proposed method, which could help in understanding its scope and applicability.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors provide a more detailed explanation for the selection of negative samples and their impact on the experimental results?
- How does the proposed method compare with other state-of-the-art methods in terms of computational cost and training time?
- Can the authors provide more details on the construction of the MolKG and the selection of negative samples for contrastive learning?
- Why is there a significant performance drop in certain datasets, such as BACE, and how does this affect the overall effectiveness of the proposed method?
- Can the authors provide a more detailed analysis of the limitations of the proposed method and suggest areas for future research?
