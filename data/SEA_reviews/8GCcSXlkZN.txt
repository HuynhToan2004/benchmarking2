 **Summary:**
The paper introduces Dense-JEPA, a variant of the JEPA model aimed at enhancing dense representation learning by aggregating features of neighboring patches as the masked targets. This approach addresses the limitations of the original JEPA model in capturing local semantics effectively. The methodology involves computing feature similarities between masked patches and their corresponding neighboring patches to select patches with semantically meaningful relations, which are then used to aggregate features using lightweight cross-attention heads. The paper presents extensive experiments demonstrating the effectiveness of Dense-JEPA across various visual benchmarks, including ImageNet-1K image classification, ADE20K semantic segmentation, and COCO object detection tasks.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, clear, and easy to follow, with a clear motivation and a well-designed methodology.
- The proposed method, Dense-JEPA, is simple yet effective, and the experiments are thorough, demonstrating significant improvements in dense representation learning.
- The paper introduces a novel masked modeling objective tailored for the joint-embedding predictive architecture, which enhances the ability to capture local semantics.
- The proposed method shows strong performance in dense prediction types of downstream tasks and demonstrates good performance in image classification tasks.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed analysis of the impact of different hyperparameters on the performance of the method.
- The experimental results show that the proposed method performs worse in image classification tasks compared to I-JEPA, which is not well explained.
- The novelty of the method is questioned, as it seems to be an extension of existing methods like MAE and I-JEPA, with minimal modifications.
- The paper does not sufficiently differentiate its contributions from existing methods, such as those mentioned in the references, which could lead to confusion about the originality of the proposed method.
- The method's computational efficiency and scalability are not discussed, which could be critical for practical implementation.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors provide more detailed analysis and insights into the impact of different hyperparameters on the performance of the method?
- Why does the proposed method perform worse in image classification tasks compared to I-JEPA? Can the authors provide more insights or experiments to support their claims?
- How does the proposed method differ from existing methods in terms of its technical contributions and novelty? Can the authors provide a more detailed comparison and discussion on these points?
- What are the computational costs and scalability of the proposed method, especially when applied to larger datasets or more complex models?
- Could the authors provide more visualization results, such as attention maps and semantic segmentation results, to better illustrate the effectiveness of the proposed method?
