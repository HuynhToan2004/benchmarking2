 **Summary:**
The paper addresses the privacy accounting of subsampled differentially private mechanisms, specifically focusing on the composition of such mechanisms. It critiques the common misconceptions and misapplications in privacy accounting, highlighting the discrepancies between theoretical and practical settings. The authors demonstrate that the privacy guarantees under composition can differ significantly based on the sampling scheme and the worst-case dataset pair used. They also provide a detailed analysis of the privacy curves and hockey-stick divergence under various sampling methods, emphasizing the importance of using accurate privacy accounting tools. The paper concludes by suggesting improvements in privacy accounting methods and encouraging transparency in privacy accounting hyperparameters.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, clear, and easy to follow, making complex concepts accessible to a broad audience.
- It addresses a significant and relevant problem in the field of privacy accounting, which is crucial for ensuring the accuracy and reliability of privacy guarantees in practical applications.
- The authors provide a detailed analysis of privacy curves and hockey-stick divergence under various sampling methods, which is both informative and insightful.
- The paper identifies and clarifies common pitfalls in privacy accounting, particularly concerning the composition of subsampled mechanisms, which is a critical area of confusion in the field.
- The findings of the paper are likely to be of interest to the broader NeurIPS community, as they touch on issues that are relevant to a wide range of researchers and practitioners in the field of privacy.

**Weaknesses:**
