 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel self-training framework for NeRF, termed Self-Evolving Neural Radiance Fields (SE-NeRF), aimed at improving the robustness of NeRF models trained with sparse views. The framework leverages a teacher-student model where the teacher NeRF generates pseudo labels from unseen views, which are then used to train a student NeRF. This process is iterative, with the student NeRF becoming the teacher in the next iteration, enhancing the model's performance. The paper also introduces a reliability estimation method to filter out unreliable rays based on consistency across views, thereby improving the quality of rendered images. The proposed method is evaluated on both synthetic and real-world datasets, showing state-of-the-art performance in multiple settings.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, with clear and detailed explanations of the proposed method.
- The motivation behind the paper is strong, focusing on the limitations of NeRF in handling sparse views and proposing a self-training framework to address these issues.
- The experimental results are impressive, demonstrating the effectiveness of the proposed method in improving NeRF performance with sparse views.
- The paper is organized well, with a clear presentation of the methodology and results, making it easy to follow.
- The idea of leveraging consistency across views to improve NeRF is novel and innovative, showing potential for significant impact in the field.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a comprehensive discussion on the limitations and potential negative societal impacts of the proposed method.
- The technical novelty of the paper is limited as it primarily builds upon existing self-training techniques without significant innovation.
- The paper does not provide sufficient comparisons with other self-training methods, which could have strengthened the argument for the effectiveness of the proposed method.
- There are issues with the clarity and presentation of some figures and tables, which could be improved for better comprehension.
- The paper does not discuss the limitations of the proposed method in detail, which is crucial for understanding its practical applicability and potential drawbacks.
- The paper does not provide a detailed analysis of the computational efficiency of the proposed method, which is important for practical implementation.

**Questions:**
- How does the proposed method compare to other self-training methods, particularly in terms of performance and computational efficiency?
- Can the authors provide more details on the computational complexity of the proposed method, especially in terms of memory and training time?
- How does the proposed method handle scenarios where the training data is not diverse enough, potentially leading to overfitting?
- Could the authors clarify the role and effectiveness of the unreliable ray distillation in the proposed method?
- How does the method ensure that the pseudo labels generated by the teacher NeRF are reliable, and what are the implications if these labels are inaccurate?
- Can the authors provide a more detailed comparison with other few-shot NeRF methods, particularly in terms of performance and computational efficiency?
