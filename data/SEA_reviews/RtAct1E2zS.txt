 **Summary:**
The paper investigates error propagation in diffusion models, a significant issue in the field of generative models. It proposes a novel theoretical framework to analyze error propagation, introducing key elements such as modular error, cumulative error, and a propagation equation. The authors also introduce a regularization term to address this issue, using a bootstrap algorithm to estimate its upper bound. Extensive experiments are conducted across multiple image datasets, demonstrating the effectiveness of the proposed method in reducing error propagation and enhancing the performance of diffusion models. The paper is well-structured, with clear theoretical explanations and empirical evidence supporting the proposed methods.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-organized and easy to follow, with clear explanations of the theoretical framework and empirical evidence supporting the proposed methods.
- The authors propose a novel theoretical framework for analyzing error propagation in diffusion models, which is supported by both theoretical and empirical evidence.
- The paper introduces a regularization term to reduce error propagation, which is shown to be effective through extensive experiments on multiple image datasets.
- The proposed method is simple and intuitive, with a clear motivation and logical derivation of the propagation equation.
- The paper is well-written and clearly explains the theoretical study and the proposed method, with a clear motivation and logical derivation of the propagation equation.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper could benefit from a more detailed explanation of the error propagation phenomenon and its implications, particularly in the introduction and related work sections.
- The assumption that the output of the neural network follows a standard Gaussian distribution may not hold in practice, which could affect the accuracy of the results.
- The paper lacks a detailed discussion on the limitations of the proposed method, particularly in scenarios where the error propagation might not be effectively addressed.
- The paper could benefit from a more comprehensive literature review, including a discussion on how the proposed method relates to and differs from existing methods in the field.
- There are some issues with the clarity and presentation of certain sections, such as the definition of cumulative error and the explanation of the error propagation equation.
- The paper could benefit from a more detailed comparison with existing methods, particularly in terms of the theoretical and empirical contributions.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors clarify the definition of cumulative error and how it relates to the propagation equation?
- How does the proposed method address the issue of error propagation in practical scenarios, and what are the limitations of this approach?
- Can the authors provide more details on the theoretical assumptions made in the paper and how they relate to the practical applicability of the method?
- How does the proposed method compare to other existing methods in terms of computational efficiency and practical effectiveness?
- Could the authors provide more details on the experimental setup and results, particularly in terms of the hyperparameters used and the statistical significance of the results?
- How does the proposed method handle the error propagation in different types of diffusion models, and what are the implications for other applications beyond image generation?
