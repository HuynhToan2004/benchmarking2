 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel approach for offline black-box optimization, focusing on the gradient matching problem between a surrogate model and the true objective function. The authors propose a theoretical framework to quantify the performance gap between the surrogate model and the true objective, which is upper-bounded by the difference in gradient fields. This theoretical analysis is supported by a gradient-matching algorithm, MATCH-OPT, which aims to minimize the performance gap by approximating the gradient field of the true objective. The paper also includes empirical results demonstrating the algorithm's effectiveness across various benchmarks. However, the paper's theoretical contributions are questioned due to their similarity to existing works, and the experimental results are seen as not convincingly superior to existing methods.

**Strengths:**
- The paper addresses an important and practical problem in offline black-box optimization, which is crucial for many real-world applications.
- The proposed algorithm, MATCH-OPT, is simple, intuitive, and easy to implement, making it accessible for practitioners.
- The paper provides a clear and well-structured presentation, making it easy to follow and understand the theoretical and practical aspects of the proposed method.
- The empirical results demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed algorithm, showing improvements over existing methods in certain benchmarks.
- The paper includes a theoretical analysis that is both rigorous and insightful, providing a solid foundation for the proposed method.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper's theoretical analysis is seen as a straightforward application of existing results from previous works, lacking significant novelty.
- The experimental results do not convincingly demonstrate the superiority of the proposed algorithm over existing methods, with some benchmarks showing no improvement or even inferior performance.
- The paper does not adequately address the limitations of the proposed method, which could impact its practical applicability in more complex or varied scenarios.
- There is a lack of detailed discussion on the hyper-parameters used in the experiments, which could affect the reproducibility and comparability of the results.
