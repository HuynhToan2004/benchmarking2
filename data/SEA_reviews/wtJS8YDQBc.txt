 **Summary:**
The paper introduces DEER (Delay-resilient Encoder-Enhanced RL), a framework designed to enhance the performance of reinforcement learning (RL) algorithms in scenarios with constant and random delays. DEER leverages a pretrained encoder to encode delayed states and past action sequences into a fixed-length hidden state, which is then used to train standard RL algorithms. This approach aims to improve interpretability and handling of variable delays, which is a significant challenge in RL. The paper includes extensive experiments on Gym and Mujoco environments, demonstrating that DEER outperforms state-of-the-art RL algorithms in both constant and random delay environments. Despite its strengths, the paper is criticized for its limited novelty and the lack of comparison with more recent and relevant offline-to-online RL methods.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, clear, and easy to follow, making it accessible to a broad audience.
- The problem addressed, which is the handling of delays in reinforcement learning, is significant and relevant, as delays are a common issue in real-world applications.
- The proposed solution, DEER, is innovative and practical, utilizing a pretrained encoder to encode delayed states and past action sequences into a fixed-length hidden state, which can be used as input to standard RL algorithms.
- The experiments are extensive and well-designed, including ablation studies that demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed method.
- The paper provides a comprehensive review of related works, which helps in understanding the context and contributions of the research.

**Weaknesses:**
- The novelty of the paper is limited as it primarily applies an existing method (offline-to-online RL) to a new domain (delayed RL) without significant modifications or new insights.
- The paper lacks a detailed comparison with more recent and relevant offline-to-online RL methods, which could have provided a more robust evaluation of the proposed method.
- The experimental results do not convincingly demonstrate the superiority of DEER over other methods, especially in environments where delays are constant.
- There is a lack of discussion on the generalization ability of DEER to different environments and the scalability of the pre-trained encoder.
- The paper does not sufficiently address the limitations of the proposed method, such as the reliance on a pretrained encoder which might not be readily available in all scenarios.
- The paper could benefit from more detailed explanations of the experimental settings and results, particularly in the appendix, to enhance reproducibility and transparency.

**Questions:**
- How does the proposed method perform in environments with a mix of constant and random delays?
- Could the authors provide more details on the training and testing data used in the experiments, including the number of trajectories and the distribution of delays?
- Is there a plan to release the code for DEER to facilitate reproducibility and further research?
- How does the proposed method handle different types of delays, such as those caused by network latency versus those caused by physical processes?
- Can the authors provide more detailed comparisons with other offline-to-online RL methods to clarify the advantages of DEER?
- How does the pre-trained encoder generalize to different environments, and what are the implications for its scalability and applicability?
