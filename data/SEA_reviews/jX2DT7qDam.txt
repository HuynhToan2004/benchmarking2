 **Summary:**
The paper introduces JEI-DNN, a method that jointly trains gating mechanisms and inference modules in early-exit dynamic neural networks (EDNNs) to address the train-test mismatch and improve uncertainty characterization. The authors propose a novel approach using bi-level optimization and learnable gates to enhance the performance and efficiency of EDNNs. The methodology involves optimizing the inference cost and accuracy simultaneously, which is different from traditional threshold-based methods. The paper provides a thorough analysis of the proposed method, including its effectiveness and limitations, and compares it with existing methods on various datasets. The experimental results demonstrate that JEI-DNN outperforms other methods in terms of accuracy-inference cost trade-offs and uncertainty characterization.

**Strengths:**
- The paper addresses a significant issue in early-exit dynamic neural networks (EDNNs) by focusing on the joint training of gating mechanisms (GMs) and inference modules (IMS), which is crucial for improving the performance of EDNNs.
- The methodology is well-structured, with clear explanations of the experimental setup and results, and the paper provides a comprehensive analysis of the proposed method.
- The paper introduces a novel approach using bi-level optimization and learnable gates, which are innovative in the field of EDNNs.
- The experiments conducted are extensive, covering a variety of datasets and architectures, which helps in validating the effectiveness of the proposed method.
- The paper is well-written, making it accessible and easy to understand, which is essential for a technical paper.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed comparison with other state-of-the-art methods, particularly those that use differentiable branching or other advanced techniques for training GMs and IMs.
- There is a lack of clarity in the presentation of the paper, especially in the introduction and related work sections, which could confuse readers unfamiliar with the field.
- The paper does not sufficiently address the limitations of the proposed method, which could affect the practical applicability of the findings.
- The method's dependence on the selection of hyperparameters such as λ could limit its generalizability and applicability in real-world scenarios.
- The paper could benefit from a more detailed discussion on the computational overhead and the practical implications of the proposed method.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors provide more detailed comparisons with other state-of-the-art methods, particularly those that use differentiable branching or other advanced techniques for training GMs and IMs?
- How does the proposed method compare in terms of computational overhead and practical applicability with other methods that use differentiable branching or other advanced techniques?
- What are the specific advantages of using JEI-DNN over other methods in terms of inference speed and computational efficiency?
- How does the proposed method handle the issue of train-test mismatch, and what are the implications of this mismatch for the performance of the model?
- Could the authors provide more detailed examples or case studies to illustrate the practical applications of the proposed method in real-world scenarios?
- How does the proposed method perform in terms of handling uncertainty in predictions, and what are the implications of this uncertainty for the practical applicability of the model?
