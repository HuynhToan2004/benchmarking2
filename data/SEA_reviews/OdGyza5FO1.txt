 **Summary:**
The paper introduces Motion PointNet, a novel method for point cloud video action recognition that formulates dynamic capture as a solvable partial differential equation (PDE) in feature space. This approach allows for the explicit modeling of motion in corresponding frames, which is a significant improvement over traditional methods that either rely on global supervision from action labels or track point trajectories. The proposed method achieves state-of-the-art results on various benchmarks, including MSRAction-3D, NTU RGB+D, and UTD-MHAD, with a lightweight PointNet-like encoder and a PDEs-solving module. Despite its novelty and effectiveness, concerns were raised about the clarity of the method's presentation and the depth of the experimental validation, particularly in terms of the generalizability of the approach and its comparison with other state-of-the-art methods.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel perspective on dynamic capture as a PDEs-solving problem, which is a pioneering approach in the field of point cloud video action recognition.
- The proposed method achieves state-of-the-art results on various benchmarks, demonstrating its effectiveness and applicability.
- The paper is well-written and easy to follow, with clear figures and tables that aid in understanding the proposed methodologies and results.
- The method is lightweight, both in terms of parameters and computational complexity, making it suitable for real-time applications.
- The paper includes extensive ablation studies that validate the effectiveness of the proposed components, such as the PointNet-like Encoder and the PDEs-solving module.
- The authors have committed to releasing the code and trained models, which enhances the reproducibility and transparency of the research.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks clarity in the presentation of the PDEs-solving module, particularly in the mathematical notation and the description of the spectral method used.
- There is a lack of detailed discussion on the limitations and potential negative societal impacts of the proposed method.
- The paper could benefit from a more thorough discussion on the related works, especially in the context of point cloud video action recognition and PDEs-solving with deep models.
- The generalizability of the proposed method to other point cloud video understanding tasks, such as segmentation, detection, and object tracking, is not adequately discussed.
- The paper does not provide a comprehensive comparison with other state-of-the-art methods in terms of computational efficiency, such as inference time and memory usage.
- The paper could benefit from a more detailed analysis of the limitations and potential negative societal impacts of the proposed method.

**Questions:**

- How does the proposed method perform when applied to other point cloud video understanding tasks, such as segmentation, detection, and object tracking?
- Can you provide a comparison of the inference time and memory usage of the proposed method with other state-of-the-art methods?
