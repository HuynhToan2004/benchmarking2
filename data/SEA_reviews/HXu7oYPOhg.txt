 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a memory-efficient particle filter recurrent neural network (mePFRNN) designed for object localization, particularly in environments with varying sizes. The mePFRNN model is based on a GRU RNN architecture and is compared to other models such as PFRNN and GRU-based models. It is evaluated in different scenarios, including symmetric and non-symmetric environments, and the results show that it requires fewer parameters and is faster than some baseline models. However, the paper is criticized for its limited novelty and the lack of detailed justification for the model's design choices. The experimental setup and the evaluation of the model's performance are also questioned, with concerns about the fairness of the comparisons and the clarity of the results.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a memory-efficient particle filter recurrent neural network (mePFRNN) designed for object localization, which is a significant advancement in the field.
- The paper is well-structured and easy to follow, making it accessible to readers.
- The authors provide detailed descriptions of the model's architecture, including the GRU-based model, which is a useful contribution to the community.
- The paper includes extensive experiments that demonstrate the model's effectiveness and efficiency, with results showing that mePFRNN outperforms other models in terms of MSE and parameter count.
- The paper is well-written, with clear figures and tables that aid in understanding the results and methodologies used.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a clear justification for why the mePFRNN model is more effective than existing models like PFRNN and GRU-based models. The motivation behind the model's design and the advantages it offers over other models are not well-explained.
- The experimental setup and evaluation of the model's performance are not convincing. The paper does not adequately compare the model with other state-of-the-art methods, and the results do not convincingly demonstrate the model's superiority or efficiency.
- There is a lack of detailed discussion on the limitations of the proposed method, which is crucial for understanding the scope and applicability of the findings.
- The paper does not provide sufficient details on the training and inference times of the models, which are essential for assessing the practicality and efficiency of the proposed methods.
- The paper lacks a thorough discussion on the generalizability of the results, particularly in real-world scenarios, which could affect the model's practical utility.

**Questions:**
- Could you provide more detailed justification for why the mePFRNN model is more effective than existing models like PFRNN and GRU-based models?
- How does the mePFRNN model handle different environments, particularly in terms of parameter count and performance?
- Can you clarify the discrepancies in the experimental results, such as the higher MSE values for mePFRNN compared to PFRNN in certain environments?
- How does the model perform in more complex and realistic scenarios, such as those involving more variables or more complex environments?
- Could you elaborate on the training and inference times of the models, and how these times compare to other state-of-the-art methods?
- How does the model generalize to real-world scenarios, and what are the limitations of its application in such environments?
