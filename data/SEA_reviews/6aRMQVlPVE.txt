 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel low-rank training method for convolutional neural networks (CNNs) by factorizing convolutions into tensor Tucker format and dynamically adjusting the Tucker ranks during training. This approach aims to maintain the convolutional structure and reduce memory consumption, which is crucial for handling large datasets. The methodology is supported by theoretical analysis, including the proof of loss descent and approximation of full baseline performance. The paper presents a variety of experiments demonstrating that the proposed method can significantly reduce training costs while maintaining or even improving accuracy compared to full models and other low-rank factorization strategies. However, concerns were raised about the clarity of the paper, the lack of comprehensive ablation studies, and the potential limitations of the method in more complex or diverse datasets.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel low-rank training method for convolutional neural networks (CNNs) that effectively handles the reduction of training costs while maintaining or even improving accuracy compared to full models.
- The methodology is supported by rigorous theoretical analysis, including the proof of loss descent and approximation of full baseline performance.
- The paper is well-structured and clearly presents the proposed method, which is easy to follow and understand.
- The authors provide a thorough rigorous analysis of the algorithm, demonstrating the well-approximation of the ideal full model and showing that the computed low-rank Tucker network approximates the ideal full model.
- The paper is well-written, with a clear introduction and a detailed related work section that effectively positions the paper within the existing literature.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a comprehensive ablation study, which could provide insights into the impact of different components of the method on performance and efficiency.
- The paper could benefit from more detailed explanations and justifications for the use of Tucker format over other tensor formats, such as CP or tensor train.
- The experiments are limited to simpler datasets like CIFAR10, and it would be beneficial to see how the method performs on more complex or diverse datasets like ImageNet.
- The paper is sometimes difficult to read, with some sections requiring more clarity and explanation, particularly around the Tucker decomposition and its practical implementation.
- The paper could benefit from a more detailed discussion on the limitations of the method and the potential challenges in extending it to more complex or diverse datasets.
- The paper could include more comparisons with other methods to better demonstrate the effectiveness and efficiency of the proposed method.
