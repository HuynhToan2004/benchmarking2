 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel method for post-training quantization of neural networks, specifically addressing the memory footprint issue by grouping similar neurons and optimizing codebooks and mappings. This method leverages a joint learning approach to optimize the codebooks and mappings, using a novel gradient update definition that enables proximal search of the codebooks and their mappings. The proposed method, termed JLCM, has been tested on various models including ResNet, ViT, and LLMs, demonstrating improvements in accuracy and memory efficiency. However, the paper lacks a detailed discussion on the limitations of the method, and the experimental results do not consistently outperform state-of-the-art methods.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel method for post-training quantization that addresses the memory footprint issue by grouping similar neurons and optimizing codebooks and mappings.
- The method leverages a joint learning approach to optimize the codebooks and mappings, which is a significant advancement in the field.
- The paper is well-written, clear, and easy to follow, with a detailed explanation of the methodology and experimental results.
- The method has been tested on various models including ResNet, ViT, and LLMs, demonstrating improvements in accuracy and memory efficiency.
- The paper provides a comprehensive evaluation of the proposed method, including a comparison with state-of-the-art methods and an ablation study on the different components of the method.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed discussion on the limitations of the proposed method, which could help in understanding its applicability and potential drawbacks.
- The experimental results do not consistently outperform state-of-the-art methods, raising questions about the effectiveness of the proposed method.
- The paper does not provide a detailed discussion on the computational cost of the proposed method, which is a significant concern given the potential for increased computational overhead.
- The paper does not compare the proposed method with the latest state-of-the-art methods such as SqueezeLLM, which could provide a more comprehensive evaluation of the proposed method.
- The paper does not discuss the impact of the proposed method on different types of neural networks, such as recurrent neural networks, which could limit the generalizability of the findings.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors provide a more detailed discussion on the limitations of the proposed method and how these limitations affect the applicability and effectiveness of the method?
- How does the proposed method compare with the latest state-of-the-art methods such as SqueezeLLM, particularly in terms of accuracy and memory efficiency?
- Can the authors provide a more detailed analysis of the computational cost of the proposed method, including the time required for training and inference?
- How does the proposed method perform on different types of neural networks, such as recurrent neural networks, and what are the implications of these results for the broader applicability of the method?
- Could the authors provide more detailed experimental results, including the number of iterations required for training and the accuracy of the compressed models at different compression rates?
