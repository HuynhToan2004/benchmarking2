 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel framework for modeling point processes with high-dimensional marks, using a conditional generator that takes the history of events as input to generate subsequent events. The framework is designed to handle complex event dynamics and marks, which are typically challenging for existing point process models. It employs a conditional variational autoencoder (CVAE) to model the conditional probability density function (PDF) of the next event, which is a departure from traditional methods that directly model the conditional intensity or PDF using parametric models. The framework's effectiveness is demonstrated through experiments on both synthetic and real-world data, showing improvements over existing methods. However, the paper's novelty is questioned due to its similarity to existing generative models, and concerns are raised about its computational efficiency and the clarity of its methodological contributions.

**Strengths:**
- The paper addresses the challenging problem of modeling point processes with high-dimensional marks, which is an important and relevant problem in the field.
- The proposed method is novel and offers a flexible framework for modeling point processes, which is a significant advancement in the field.
- The paper is well-written, clear, and easy to follow, making it accessible to a broad audience.
- The experimental results are compelling, demonstrating the effectiveness of the proposed method in generating high-quality event series.
- The method is general and model-agnostic, which enhances its applicability across various scenarios and domains.
- The paper provides a detailed comparison with existing methods, showing the superiority of the proposed method in terms of performance.

**Weaknesses:**
- The novelty of the method is questioned due to its similarity to existing generative models, particularly the conditional variational autoencoder (CVAE).
- The paper lacks a detailed discussion on the computational efficiency of the proposed method, which is a critical aspect for practical applications.
- There is a lack of clarity on the specific methodological contributions of the paper, particularly in how it differs from existing methods.
- The paper does not adequately address the limitations of the proposed method, which could help in understanding its scope and applicability.
- The experimental results are not convincingly superior to existing methods, especially in high-dimensional data scenarios.
- The paper does not provide sufficient detail on how the method handles high-dimensional data, which is a significant claim of the work.
- The method's applicability to different types of data, such as images and text, is not convincingly demonstrated, and the results on these types of data are not sufficiently detailed.

- Can the authors provide more details on the experimental setup, particularly how the data was generated and how the results were evaluated?
