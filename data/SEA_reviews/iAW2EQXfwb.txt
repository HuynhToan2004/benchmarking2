 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel ensemble reinforcement learning (ERL) approach for online diverse game level generation, leveraging a selector model to choose from multiple negatively correlated sub-policies. This method enhances diversity by incorporating negative correlation regularization, which is shown to be effective in generating diverse levels with competitive performance. The approach is evaluated against state-of-the-art policy ensemble methods and classic methods on well-known benchmarks, demonstrating its ability to generate diverse levels while maintaining competitive rewards. The paper also includes theoretical contributions in the form of policy iteration and policy gradient theorems, which are derived to optimize policy regularization in Markov decision processes.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, with a clear structure and well-presented figures, making it easy to follow and understand.
- The proposed method is innovative and effective, using multiple negatively correlated sub-policies and a selector model to generate diverse alternatives, which is a novel approach in the field.
- The paper includes thorough theoretical analysis, such as the derivation of policy iteration and policy gradient theorems, which provide a solid theoretical foundation for the proposed method.
- The experimental results are robust and demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed method, showing a significant improvement in diversity and competitive performance in terms of reward.
- The paper's methodology is well-explained, and the experiments are well-designed, providing a clear demonstration of the method's capabilities.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed comparison with existing methods, particularly in terms of computational efficiency and scalability, which could be crucial for real-world applications.
- The motivation behind the use of negative correlation regularization is not clearly articulated, and the paper could benefit from more detailed explanations and justifications for this approach.
- The paper does not sufficiently discuss the limitations of the proposed method, which could help in understanding its scope and applicability.
- The experimental setup and results could be more comprehensive, including more complex environments and a broader range of diversity metrics to better demonstrate the method's capabilities.
- The paper's theoretical contributions, while robust, are not sufficiently integrated into the practical application of the method, which could limit their impact and utility.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors clarify the motivation behind the use of negative correlation regularization and provide more detailed justifications for its effectiveness in enhancing diversity?
- How does the proposed method compare in terms of computational efficiency and scalability with other existing methods, particularly in more complex environments?
- Can the authors provide more detailed insights into the limitations of the proposed method and discuss potential avenues for future research to address these limitations?
- How does the diversity of the generated levels compare to other existing methods, and could the authors provide more detailed comparisons or additional metrics to better demonstrate the method's capabilities?
- Could the authors discuss the potential integration of the proposed method with other existing methods, such as multi-objective RL, to enhance its capabilities and applicability?
