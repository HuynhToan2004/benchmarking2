 **Summary:**
The paper introduces TangleNAS, a novel method that integrates weight-entanglement into single-stage NAS methods, aiming to reduce memory consumption and improve performance in both cell-based and weight-entangled search spaces. This approach allows for a unified application of single-stage methods across various NAS domains, including cell-based and weight-entangled spaces, and enhances the efficiency of NAS by reducing memory requirements and improving runtime performance. The methodology is validated through comprehensive experiments, demonstrating that TangleNAS outperforms traditional two-stage NAS methods and maintains the benefits of gradient-based NAS methods. However, the paper faces criticism for its lack of novelty, unclear experimental results, and potential issues in its methodological presentation.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel method of weight-entanglement in single-stage NAS, which is a significant advancement in the field.
- The proposed method is shown to be effective in both cell-based and weight-entangled search spaces, demonstrating its versatility.
- The paper is well-written and easy to follow, making it accessible to a broad audience.
- Comprehensive experiments are conducted, validating the effectiveness of the proposed method and providing a robust evaluation framework.
- The integration of weight-entanglement into single-stage NAS methods allows for a unified application across different NAS domains, enhancing the efficiency of NAS.
- The paper provides a thorough evaluation of the properties of single and two-stage approaches, including any-time performance, memory consumption, robustness to training fraction, and effect of fine-tuning, which helps in understanding the strengths and limitations of different NAS methods.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed discussion on the challenges of applying weight-entanglement to single-stage NAS methods, which could have provided a deeper understanding of the method's limitations.
- The method's novelty is questioned as it appears to be a straightforward combination of existing methods, such as weight-entanglement from two-stage NAS and single-stage NAS.
- The experimental results are not convincing, with the improvements shown being marginal and not consistently outperforming existing methods like SPOS.
- The paper does not adequately address the computational cost of the proposed method, which is crucial for assessing its practical applicability.
- The paper could benefit from a more detailed discussion on the methodological challenges and potential solutions for integrating weight-entanglement into single-stage NAS methods.
- The presentation of the paper could be improved, particularly in terms of clarity and organization, especially in the methodology section.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors provide a more detailed explanation of how weight-entanglement is integrated into single-stage NAS methods, particularly in terms of computational cost and methodological challenges?
- How does the proposed method compare in terms of computational cost and efficiency with existing methods, especially in large-scale applications?
- What are the specific advantages of using TangleNAS over other NAS methods, particularly in terms of performance and efficiency?
- How does the proposed method handle the challenges associated with weight-entanglement, such as the potential for increased computational complexity or reduced performance in certain scenarios?
- Could the authors clarify the experimental results, especially the marginal improvements shown in some comparisons, and discuss the statistical significance of these results?
- How does the proposed method perform in more complex and larger-scale search spaces, such as those involving more layers or more complex architectures?
