 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel federated semi-supervised learning (FSSL) method named FedAnchor, which leverages a double-head structure to train on labeled anchor data and generate pseudo labels for unlabeled data. This approach aims to improve the quality of pseudo-labels and enhance the performance of federated learning models. The methodology includes a label contrastive loss based on cosine similarity, which is designed to minimize the confirmation bias and overfitting issues. The paper presents experimental results on three datasets, showing that FedAnchor outperforms existing methods in terms of convergence rate and model accuracy. However, concerns were raised regarding the novelty of the method, the lack of comprehensive experimental validation, and the clarity of the presentation.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel method, FedAnchor, which integrates a double-head structure and label contrastive loss based on cosine similarity to generate pseudo labels, enhancing the quality of pseudo-labels and potentially reducing confirmation bias and overfitting issues.
- The methodology is well-explained, making it easy to follow, and the paper is well-organized, providing a clear and concise description of the proposed method.
- The paper is technically sound, and the experimental results demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed method.
- The paper addresses a significant problem in federated learning, which is the availability of labeled data, and proposes a novel solution to this problem.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a comprehensive comparison with existing methods, particularly those that also use pseudo-labeling in federated learning. This limits the understanding of the novelty and effectiveness of the proposed method.
- The experimental validation is insufficient, with only three datasets used and limited details provided on the experimental setup and results.
- The paper does not adequately address the issue of communication efficiency in federated learning, which is a critical concern in practical applications.
- The novelty of the proposed method is questioned as it appears to be a combination of existing techniques without significant innovation.
- The presentation of the paper is unclear in some sections, particularly in the description of the experimental setup and the results.
- The paper does not sufficiently discuss the limitations of the proposed method, which is crucial for understanding its applicability and potential drawbacks.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors provide a more comprehensive comparison with existing methods that also use pseudo-labeling in federated learning?
- How does the proposed method compare in terms of communication efficiency with other federated learning methods?
- Can the authors provide more detailed experimental results, including the standard deviation of the results and the performance of the method on a wider range of datasets?
- How does the proposed method address the issue of confirmation bias and overfitting, and what are the specific mechanisms used to mitigate these issues?
- Could the authors clarify the experimental setup, particularly the number of clients and the communication rounds used in the experiments?
- How does the proposed method handle the issue of non-IID data in federated learning, and what are the implications of this for the performance of the model?
