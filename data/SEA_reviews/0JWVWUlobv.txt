 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel machine learning approach, termed 4D tensor multi-task continual learning, aimed at predicting Alzheimer's disease (AD) progression using MRI data. This methodology integrates knowledge from all available data to continuously update and optimize prediction results, addressing challenges such as monotonic data, small datasets, and scarcity of time-continuous data. The approach involves a 4D tensor multi-task continual learning algorithm that quantifies multi-dimensional information on brain structural variation and knowledge sharing between patients. The paper evaluates this approach using data from the Alzheimer's Disease Neuroimaging Initiative (ADNI), demonstrating superior accuracy and stability in predicting various cognitive scores of AD progression compared to single-task learning, benchmark, and state-of-the-art multi-task regression methods.

**Strengths:**
- The paper addresses an important and timely problem in the field of Alzheimer's disease prediction, utilizing a novel 4D tensor multi-task continual learning approach to predict AD progression from MRI data.
- The methodology is well-described, and the paper is well-written, making it accessible and understandable.
- The proposed approach is novel and innovative, utilizing a 4D tensor multi-task continual learning algorithm to predict AD progression, which is a significant advancement in the field.
- The paper includes comprehensive experiments using data from the Alzheimer's Disease Neuroimaging Initiative (ADNI), demonstrating the superiority of the proposed approach over existing methods.
- The paper provides a clear and detailed description of the methodology, including the use of a 4D tensor multi-task continual learning algorithm and the quantification of multi-dimensional information on brain structural variation and knowledge sharing between patients.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed discussion on the limitations of the proposed method, which is crucial for understanding its applicability and potential drawbacks.
- The novelty of the approach is questionable as it primarily combines existing techniques without sufficient innovation or differentiation from existing methods.
- The paper does not adequately address the computational complexity of the proposed approach, which could limit its practical application in real-world scenarios.
- There is a lack of clarity in the experimental setup, including the specifics of the datasets used, the pre-processing steps, and the statistical analysis methods.
- The paper does not include a thorough comparison with state-of-the-art methods, which is necessary to establish the effectiveness and superiority of the proposed approach.
- The paper lacks a detailed discussion on the ethical considerations and data privacy, which are crucial in medical applications involving patient data.
