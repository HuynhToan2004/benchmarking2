 **Summary:**
The paper explores the impact of the ReLU activation function on the conditioning of the Neural Tangent Kernel (NTK) in neural networks. It demonstrates that the ReLU activation enhances the separation of similar data in the feature space of model gradients, thereby improving the conditioning of the NTK. This improvement is attributed to the non-linear nature of the ReLU function, which allows for better data separation and faster convergence rates in gradient descent algorithms. The theoretical analysis is supported by empirical evidence showing that deeper ReLU networks exhibit faster convergence and smaller NTK condition numbers compared to linear networks. The paper also discusses the implications of these findings for the optimization of neural networks and the expressivity of neural networks.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written and easy to follow, with clear theoretical results and well-designed experiments.
- The analysis of the ReLU activation function is novel and interesting, particularly the observation that ReLU networks have better separation and conditioning of the NTK.
- The theoretical analysis is sound, and the results are supported by both theoretical and empirical evidence.
- The paper provides a novel insight into the role of non-linear activations in the expressivity and optimization of neural networks.
- The findings on the condition number of the NTK matrix and the convergence of gradient descent algorithms are significant and could lead to further research in the field.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper's assumptions about the network width being infinite may not be practical or realistic, as it is not clear how the results would apply to networks with finite width.
- The paper's analysis is limited to the case where the input angle is small, which may not be representative of all scenarios.
- The paper does not discuss the limitations of its results, particularly how they might not apply to other types of neural networks or non-linear activation functions.
- The paper does not compare the results with other types of activation functions, such as sigmoid or tanh, which could provide a more comprehensive understanding of the role of non-linear activations in neural networks.
- The paper does not provide a detailed discussion on the practical implications of the findings, such as how they might be applied to improve the training of neural networks in real-world scenarios.
