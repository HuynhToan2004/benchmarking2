 **Summary:**
The paper investigates the security vulnerabilities in LLM-integrated applications, focusing on the risks posed by both insider and outsider threats. It identifies potential risks such as bias, toxic content, privacy, and disinformation, and proposes a defense mechanism, SHIELD, to mitigate these threats. The defense mechanism is designed to ensure the integrity and source identification of messages, enhance attack detectability, and preserve utility. The paper includes empirical evaluations using GPT-3.5 and GPT-4 to demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed defense. However, concerns were raised regarding the novelty of the defense mechanism and the depth of threat modeling, particularly in terms of adversarial capabilities and the potential for insider threats.

**Strengths:**
- The paper addresses a significant and timely issue of security in LLM-integrated applications, focusing on both insider and outsider threats.
- The proposed defense mechanism, SHIELD, is simple, lightweight, and effective in mitigating identified vulnerabilities, with empirical evaluations demonstrating its utility preservation and attack detection capabilities.
- The paper is well-written, easy to follow, and includes a detailed analysis of potential risks and the threat surface, which is crucial for understanding the security implications of LLM-integrated applications.
- The authors provide a comprehensive overview of potential risks and threats, which is crucial for understanding the security implications of LLM-integrated applications.

**Weaknesses:**
- The novelty of the defense mechanism is questionable, as it primarily relies on existing cryptographic methods without significant innovation.
- The paper lacks a detailed discussion on the specific adversarial capabilities and potential strategies for insider threats, which could significantly enhance the paper's depth and relevance.
- The evaluation of the proposed defense mechanism is limited to a single application domain, which may not fully demonstrate its generalizability or effectiveness across different scenarios.
- The paper does not sufficiently address the scalability of the proposed defense mechanism, which could be a critical concern for larger, more complex LLM-integrated applications.
- There is a lack of discussion on the limitations of the proposed defense mechanism and potential ethical implications, which could be crucial for understanding the practical applicability and societal impact of the proposed solution.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors clarify the specific adversarial capabilities and potential strategies for insider threats? How does the proposed defense mechanism address these threats effectively?
- How does the proposed defense mechanism perform in scenarios where there is a lack of trust in the application or the LLM provider? What are the potential risks and limitations in such scenarios?
- Can the authors provide more details on the scalability of the proposed defense mechanism? How does it perform in larger, more complex LLM-integrated applications?
- How does the proposed defense mechanism handle scenarios where the LLM may not accurately detect attacks, especially in cases where the LLM's output might be influenced by biases or other factors?
- Could the authors discuss the potential ethical implications of their proposed solution? How does it address the broader societal concerns related to the use of LLM-integrated applications?
