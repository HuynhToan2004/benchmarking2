 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel method for pruning large language models (LLMs) using a LoRA-guided criterion that estimates the importance of pre-trained weights based on LoRA gradients. This method avoids the need to compute gradients of pre-trained weights, which can be computationally and memory-intensive. The proposed method, termed LoRAPrune, is designed to perform iterative structured pruning while simultaneously conducting efficient fine-tuning, which can achieve high compression rates without compromising performance. Extensive experiments on the LLaMA series models demonstrate the effectiveness of LoRAPrune in reducing memory and computational costs while maintaining or even improving performance.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel LoRA-guided criterion for pruning LLMs, which is an innovative approach that leverages the gradients of LoRA to approximate the importance of pre-trained weights, thereby reducing the computational and memory requirements compared to traditional methods.
- The proposed method, LoRAPrune, is a structured pruning method that performs iterative pruning and fine-tuning, which allows for the efficient pruning of large language models while maintaining or even improving performance.
- The paper is well-written, clear, and easy to follow, with a clear motivation and explanation of the methodology, making it accessible to a broad audience.
- The experiments conducted on the LLaMA series models demonstrate the effectiveness of LoRAPrune in reducing memory and computational costs while maintaining or even improving performance, as shown in extensive ablation studies.
- The method is complementary to other PEFT techniques such as LoRA, making it a versatile tool for optimizing large language models.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed comparison with other pruning methods, such as LoRA-based pruning methods like QLoRA, which could provide a more comprehensive evaluation of the proposed method.
- The paper primarily focuses on the LLaMA series models and does not explore the applicability of the method to other large language models, which could limit the generalizability of the findings.
- The method requires fine-tuning to restore model performance, which might not be feasible in all scenarios, especially where fine-tuning is unavailable.
- The paper does not discuss the impact of different LoRA ranks on the performance of the pruning method, which could be a significant factor in practical deployments.
- The experimental results are not comprehensive, particularly in terms of the range of compression ratios tested, which could affect the reliability of the conclusions drawn.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors provide more details on how the LoRA-guided criterion is differentiated from the vanilla criterion, and how it impacts the pruning process?
- How does the proposed method compare to other pruning methods like LoRA-based pruning methods, and what are the advantages or disadvantages of using LoRAPrune over these methods?
- What are the specific challenges or limitations of applying the proposed method to other large language models beyond the LLaMA series?
- Can the authors provide insights into the impact of different LoRA ranks on the performance of the pruning method?
- Would it be possible to extend the experimental validation to include a broader range of compression ratios to ensure the robustness and reliability of the findings?
