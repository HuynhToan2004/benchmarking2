 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel method for incomplete multi-view clustering (IMVC) using a unified and robust representation learning framework. It employs a Transformer-based auto-encoder to fuse multi-view information and generate unified embeddings, which are robust against missing views through KNN imputation and data augmentation. The methodology is evaluated on four benchmark datasets, showing state-of-the-art performance and robustness against varying missing rates. The paper also includes extensive ablation studies to validate the effectiveness of its design choices, such as the use of KNN imputation and data augmentation, and the incorporation of a clustering module and dynamic initialization.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-organized, with clear and easy-to-follow writing, and the methodology is clearly explained.
- The proposed method is innovative, integrating a unified embedding that is robust to view missing conditions, which is a significant improvement over traditional methods.
- The paper is evaluated on four benchmark datasets and shows state-of-the-art performance, demonstrating the effectiveness of the proposed method.
- The ablation studies provide valuable insights into the contributions of each module of the proposed method.
- The paper is well-written, making it accessible and understandable, with a comprehensive related works section that positions the research within the existing literature.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed discussion on the limitations of the proposed method, which could provide a more balanced view of its contributions and shortcomings.
- There is a lack of comparison with more traditional methods such as matrix decomposition, kernel-based, and graph-based methods, which could provide a more comprehensive evaluation of the proposed method.
- The paper does not discuss the computational complexity of the proposed method, which could be a significant concern for practical applications.
- The paper does not provide code, which limits the reproducibility of the results.
- The experimental results are not convincing, particularly the results on the Scene15 dataset, which are significantly lower than those reported in other studies.
- The paper could benefit from a more thorough discussion on the related works, particularly in differentiating its contributions from existing methods.

**Questions:**
- How does the proposed method compare in terms of computational complexity and practicality with traditional methods such as matrix decomposition, kernel-based, and graph-based methods?
- Could you provide more details on the training process, such as the hyperparameters used and the training time required for the proposed method?
- How does the proposed method perform when the number of views increases, and what are the implications for the performance and computational efficiency?
- Can you provide more details on the experimental setup, such as the number of views used and the specifics of the data augmentation and KNN imputation techniques employed?
- How does the proposed method handle the issue of data heterogeneity across different views, and what are the implications for the clustering results?
- Could you clarify the differences between the proposed method and existing methods, particularly in terms of the novel contributions and improvements over existing methods?
