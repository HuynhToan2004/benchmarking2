 **Summary:**
The paper investigates the phenomenon of aliasing in semantic segmentation, focusing on the relationship between aliasing and boundary errors. It proposes a novel method to calculate the Nyquist frequency based on the kernel size and feature map size, aiming to enhance the accuracy of segmentation. The authors introduce two modules, De-aliasing Filter (DAF) and Frequency Mixing (FreqMix), to address aliasing-induced errors. These modules are tested on various datasets, including ADE20K, Cityscapes, and LIS, showing improved performance over baseline models. The paper also introduces a new metric for quantifying aliasing levels and provides a comprehensive analysis of boundary errors.

**Strengths:**
- The paper addresses a significant and less explored problem in semantic segmentation, focusing on the relationship between aliasing and boundary errors.
- The introduction of a new metric to quantify aliasing levels provides a novel approach to measuring the impact of aliasing on segmentation accuracy.
- The proposed method for calculating the Nyquist frequency is both novel and effective, offering a new perspective on how to improve segmentation performance.
- The paper is well-written, easy to follow, and provides a clear explanation of the proposed method, making it accessible to readers.
- The experimental results demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed method, showing improvements in various datasets.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed comparison with other methods designed to address aliasing, which could provide a more comprehensive understanding of the proposed method's advantages.
- The paper does not include a discussion on how the proposed method could be extended to other tasks such as object detection or panoptic segmentation, which could limit its applicability.
- The methodology for selecting the frequency cutoff in the DAF module is not clearly explained, which could lead to confusion about its implementation.
- The paper could benefit from a more thorough discussion on the limitations of the proposed method, especially in relation to its applicability in scenarios where aliasing is not a significant issue.
- The paper could be improved by providing more detailed experimental results, such as the impact of different parameters on the performance of the proposed modules.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors provide a more detailed comparison with other methods designed to address aliasing, such as those mentioned in the related works section?
- How does the proposed method compare in terms of computational efficiency and accuracy with other methods that address aliasing?
- Can the authors provide a more detailed explanation of how the frequency cutoff is selected in the DAF module, and how this affects the performance of the method?
- Could the authors discuss the potential applications of the proposed method in other tasks beyond semantic segmentation, such as object detection or panoptic segmentation?
- How does the proposed method perform in scenarios where aliasing is not a significant issue, and what are the implications of this for its applicability?
