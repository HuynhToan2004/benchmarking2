 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel training data synthesis framework using a latent diffusion model, focusing on distribution matching and conditional visual guidance. This approach aims to generate synthetic data that closely resembles real data distributions, thereby improving the performance of deep models trained on synthetic data. The methodology involves a theoretical analysis of distribution matching and a practical implementation using a text-to-image diffusion model. The paper also explores the benefits of this approach in terms of out-of-distribution generalization and privacy preservation. Extensive experiments across various datasets demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed method, although concerns about the novelty and clarity of presentation persist.

**Strengths:**
- The paper presents a novel approach to training data synthesis by using a latent diffusion model, which effectively aligns the synthetic data with real data distributions.
- Theoretical analysis is provided to support the proposed method, which is crucial for understanding the effectiveness of the approach.
- The paper is well-structured, making it easy to follow, and includes comprehensive experimental results that demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed method.
- The methodology is innovative, combining different existing techniques such as distribution matching, conditional visual guidance, and latent prior initialization to enhance the quality of synthetic data.
- The paper addresses an important and timely problem, which is the generation of high-quality synthetic data for various applications, including image classification and out-of-distribution generalization.
- The paper is well-written, clear, and easy to follow, which is crucial for academic publications.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a clear differentiation from existing methods in the field of training data synthesis, particularly in terms of novelty and technical contributions.
- The theoretical analysis, while sound, is not particularly novel as similar approaches have been previously explored in other works.
- The presentation of the paper could be improved, with some sections being difficult to understand due to complex mathematical expressions and lack of clarity in explanations.
- The experimental setup and results are not convincingly superior to existing methods, and the paper does not sufficiently address the limitations and potential negative societal impacts of its work.
- The paper could benefit from a more detailed discussion on the limitations of the proposed method and a more comprehensive comparison with existing methods in the field.

**Questions:**
- How does the proposed method compare to other state-of-the-art methods in terms of performance and computational efficiency?
- Can the authors provide more detailed experimental results, including the training and inference time for the proposed method?
- How does the proposed method address the limitations of existing methods, particularly in terms of distribution matching and conditional visual guidance?
- Could the authors clarify the technical contributions of their work and how these differ from existing methods in the field?
- How does the proposed method perform on different types of datasets, particularly those with different characteristics such as image size, resolution, and content?
- Can the authors provide more details on the privacy preservation aspect of their work, particularly how it compares to existing methods in this area?
