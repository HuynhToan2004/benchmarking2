 **Summary:**
The paper explores the use of stochastic neural networks (StoNet) as a bridge between deep neural networks (DNNs) and linear models, aiming to address challenges such as overparametrization, structural interpretability, and prediction uncertainty quantification. The authors adapt sparse learning theory from linear models to StoNet, establishing consistency in structure selection and prediction uncertainty quantification. The paper also introduces a post-StoNet procedure for uncertainty quantification in large-scale DNNs, which is demonstrated to be superior to the conformal method. However, the paper's novelty is questioned due to its reliance on existing methods like Lasso and conformal inference, and the lack of significant experimental validation.

**Strengths:**
- The paper addresses significant challenges in deep learning, including overparametrization, structural interpretability, and prediction uncertainty quantification.
- The adaptation of sparse learning theory from linear models to StoNet is innovative, providing a new approach to these issues.
- The paper is well-written, clear, and easy to follow, making complex concepts accessible to a broader audience.
- The proposed post-StoNet procedure for uncertainty quantification in large-scale DNNs is a valuable contribution, potentially enhancing the reliability and robustness of deep learning models.
- The theoretical results, such as the asymptotic equivalence between StoNet and DNN, are interesting and could be of broader interest to the deep learning community.

**Weaknesses:**
- The novelty of the paper is limited as it heavily relies on existing methods like Lasso and conformal inference, which have been previously used in similar contexts.
- The experimental validation is insufficient and does not convincingly demonstrate the superiority of the proposed methods over existing techniques.
- The paper lacks a comprehensive comparison with other state-of-the-art methods for uncertainty quantification, such as Bayesian neural networks and other post-training calibration methods.
- The assumptions made in the paper, such as the asymptotic equivalence between StoNet and DNN, are not thoroughly justified, and their practical implications are not clearly discussed.
- The paper could benefit from more detailed explanations and justifications for the theoretical results and assumptions, particularly in the areas of sparse learning theory and uncertainty quantification.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors clarify the novelty of their approach in comparison to existing methods like Lasso and conformal inference?
- How does the proposed post-StoNet procedure compare to other state-of-the-art methods for uncertainty quantification, such as Bayesian neural networks and other post-training calibration methods?
- Can the authors provide more detailed experimental results, including comparisons with other methods and a broader range of datasets to better demonstrate the effectiveness of their proposed methods?
- How do the authors justify the assumptions made in their theoretical results, particularly the asymptotic equivalence between StoNet and DNN?
- Could the authors elaborate on the practical implications of these assumptions and how they affect the applicability and reliability of their results?
