 **Summary:**
The paper introduces Geometric Bayesian Flow Networks (GeoBFN) for generating 3D molecular structures, leveraging Bayesian flow networks to address the challenges of multi-modality and noise sensitivity in molecular geometry. GeoBFN integrates Bayesian inference to modify parameters of collections of independent distributions, ensuring SE-(3) invariance and enhancing the generative process with iterative updates. The methodology is supported by extensive experiments on various datasets, demonstrating competitive performance against state-of-the-art models. However, the paper is criticized for its complexity, limited novelty, and lack of detailed comparative analysis with existing methods.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written and easy to follow, with a clear and concise presentation of the main ideas and methodology.
- The proposed GeoBFN is simple and effective, addressing the challenges of multi-modality and noise sensitivity in molecular geometry.
- The method is supported by extensive experiments that demonstrate its effectiveness in generating 3D molecular structures, outperforming existing models in some aspects.
- The paper provides a detailed description of the proposed method and its implementation, including the use of SE-(3) invariance and the Bayesian flow network for molecular generation.
- The authors have conducted extensive experiments to evaluate the performance of the proposed method, including comparisons with state-of-the-art models and ablation studies.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a clear explanation of the advantages of the proposed method over existing models, particularly in terms of performance and efficiency.
- The complexity of the method and the training process is not adequately addressed, with no discussion on the computational resources required or the scalability of the method to larger datasets.
- The paper does not provide sufficient details on the experimental setup, such as the architecture used for the equivariant graph neural network and the training details for the Bayesian flow network.
- The paper does not adequately address the limitations of the method, such as the lack of comparisons with other methods like GEDLDM and the potential for the method to collapse to a subset of the training data.
- The paper does not provide a detailed comparison of the proposed method with existing methods, particularly in terms of the training process and the generation of molecular structures.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors provide a more detailed comparison of the proposed method with existing methods, particularly in terms of the training process and the generation of molecular structures?
- How does the proposed method handle the multi-modality and noise sensitivity issues in molecular geometry, and what are the specific advantages of using Bayesian flow networks over other methods?
- What are the computational resources required for training the GeoBFN, and how does the performance of the model vary with different network architectures and hyperparameters?
- How does the GeoBFN ensure SE-(3) invariance during the generation process, and what are the implications of this invariance for the quality and diversity of the generated structures?
- Could the authors provide more details on the experimental setup, such as the architecture used for the equivariant graph neural network and the training details for the Bayesian flow network?
