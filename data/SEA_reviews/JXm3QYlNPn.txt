 **Summary:**
The paper introduces SignRound, a method aimed at optimizing weight rounding for large language models (LLMs) using signed gradient descent. This approach involves a block-wise tuning process to minimize the loss between full-precision and quantized weights, which is shown to be effective in reducing quantization errors. The method is evaluated against various existing techniques and shows promising results in terms of accuracy and efficiency. However, the paper's novelty is questioned due to its similarity to existing methods, and the experimental results are seen as incremental improvements over existing techniques. The methodology is straightforward, but the paper lacks a detailed discussion on the hyperparameter settings and their impact on performance.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written and easy to follow, making it accessible to readers.
- The proposed method, SignRound, is simple and effective, with clear motivation and straightforward methodology.
- The results demonstrate that the proposed method outperforms the baseline, showing a significant improvement in accuracy.
- The paper provides a detailed comparison of the proposed method with existing techniques, highlighting its effectiveness.
- The method does not introduce any additional overhead during inference, which is crucial for practical applications.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed discussion on the hyperparameter settings and their impact on performance, which is crucial for reproducibility and understanding the method's effectiveness.
- The novelty of the method is questioned as it appears to be a simple extension of existing methods, with the primary innovation being the use of signed gradient descent.
- The experimental results are seen as incremental improvements over existing techniques, with limited new insights or findings.
- The paper does not adequately address the scalability of the method to larger models or different types of tasks, which could limit its applicability.
- The paper does not discuss the limitations of the proposed method, which could help in understanding its scope and applicability.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors provide a more detailed discussion on the hyperparameter settings and their impact on performance?
- How does the proposed method compare to other existing techniques in terms of computational efficiency and practical deployment?
- Can the authors elaborate on the scalability of the method to larger models or different types of tasks?
- How does the method handle the scalability issues, especially when applied to larger models or different types of tasks?
- Could the authors discuss the limitations of the proposed method and suggest potential directions for future research?
