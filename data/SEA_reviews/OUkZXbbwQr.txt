 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel approach to reinforcement learning (RL) by integrating a debate-based reward model to enhance the justifiability of decisions made by RL agents. This model involves two agents debating evidence for their decisions, with a human judge evaluating the justifiability of the actions. The framework is tested in a sepsis treatment scenario, demonstrating that the justifiable agent's decisions are more likely to be favored by the judge compared to the baseline agent. The paper also explores the use of a judge model trained on human preferences and a debate game that quantifies the justifiability of decisions. Despite the innovative approach, concerns were raised about the scalability of the framework, the clarity of the experimental setup, and the justification for certain design choices.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, clearly presenting the proposed framework and its application to the sepsis treatment scenario, demonstrating a good understanding of the problem.
- The use of a debate-based reward model is innovative and provides a novel approach to enhancing the justifiability of decisions made by RL agents.
- The integration of a judge model trained on human preferences is a significant contribution, allowing for the evaluation of decisions based on human values and expectations.
- The paper provides a detailed discussion on the framework's limitations, which is essential for understanding the scope and applicability of the proposed method.
- The experimental results show that the justifiable agent's decisions are more likely to be favored by the judge, demonstrating the effectiveness of the proposed approach.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a clear justification for the specific design choices, such as the use of a judge model and the debate-based reward model, which could benefit from more detailed explanation.
- The scalability of the framework is a concern, particularly in terms of how it would perform in more complex or varied environments.
- The experimental setup and results are not clearly presented, making it difficult to understand the implications and contributions of the study.
- The paper does not adequately address the potential biases that might influence the judge's preferences, which could impact the validity of the results.
- The paper's reliance on a single dataset and a single judge model limits the generalizability of the findings.
- The paper could benefit from a more detailed discussion on the ethical implications of the proposed framework, particularly concerning the potential for misuse or manipulation of the justifiability mechanism.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors clarify the specific design choices, such as the use of a judge model and the debate-based reward model, and provide more detailed justification for these choices?
- How does the framework perform in more complex or varied environments, and what steps have been taken to ensure its scalability?
- What are the implications of using a single dataset and a single judge model for the generalizability of the findings?
- How does the proposed framework address the potential biases that might influence the judge's preferences, and what steps have been taken to mitigate these biases?
- Could the authors provide more details on the experimental setup, particularly how the judge model was trained and the specifics of the debate game used?
- How does the framework handle the potential for misuse or manipulation of the justifiability mechanism, and what measures have been put in place to prevent such misuse?
