 **Summary:**
The paper introduces RL4CO, a unified library for reinforcement learning in combinatorial optimization, which aims to address the challenges of reproducibility and fair comparison in the field. It provides a standardized framework for benchmarking different RL algorithms and decoding strategies across various combinatorial optimization problems. The library is designed to be flexible, extensible, and modular, allowing for the implementation of different RL algorithms and decoding schemes. The paper also explores the zero-shot performance of RL algorithms and evaluates different decoding strategies. Despite its strengths in providing a unified framework and benchmarking capabilities, the paper has been critiqued for its limited scope in terms of the number of RL algorithms and combinatorial optimization problems tested.
