 **Summary:**
The paper introduces GeONet, a novel neural operator network designed to compute the Wasserstein geodesic between probability distributions. It leverages a deep learning approach to model the optimal transport problem, specifically focusing on the fluid dynamics formulation of the Benamou-Brenier problem. The methodology involves training neural networks to learn the solution of the PDEs corresponding to the primal and dual problems, which are then used to compute the Wasserstein geodesic between two distributions. The paper demonstrates that GeONet can outperform traditional methods in terms of computational efficiency and accuracy, especially in high-dimensional settings. However, concerns about the clarity of the presentation, the depth of the theoretical analysis, and the practical applicability of the method, particularly in high-dimensional spaces, are noted.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel approach to solving the optimal transport problem using a deep learning method, specifically by training neural networks to learn the solution of the PDEs corresponding to the primal and dual problems. This is a significant advancement in the field, particularly in the context of high-dimensional data.
- The methodology is well-explained, with clear explanations of the mathematical formulations and the neural network architecture used.
- The paper demonstrates the effectiveness of the proposed method through numerical experiments, showing improvements in computational efficiency and accuracy compared to traditional methods.
- The use of a deep learning approach to solve the optimal transport problem is innovative and addresses the challenges of traditional methods, such as the curse of dimensionality.
- The paper is well-structured and easy to follow, making it accessible to readers.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed discussion on the theoretical properties of the proposed method, particularly in terms of convergence and generalization.
- There is a notable absence of comparisons with other state-of-the-art methods, which could have provided a better understanding of the relative performance of the proposed method.
- The paper could benefit from a more comprehensive evaluation of the method's performance in high-dimensional spaces, where the results are not convincingly presented.
- The presentation of the paper could be improved to better highlight the novelty and contributions of the research.
- The paper could benefit from a more detailed discussion on the limitations of the method, particularly in terms of its applicability to different types of data and the potential challenges in high-dimensional spaces.

**Questions:**

- What are the specific challenges in applying the method to high-dimensional data, and how does the method perform in such settings?
- Could you provide more details on the training process of the neural networks, particularly how the initial and terminal distributions are used to train the networks?
- How does the method handle the issue of the curse of dimensionality, and what are the implications for the method's performance in high-dimensional spaces?
- Could you elaborate on the theoretical underpinnings of the method, particularly in terms of convergence and generalization?
