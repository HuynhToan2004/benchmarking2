 **Summary:**
The paper introduces LoRA-FA, a novel adaptation of the LoRA method designed to reduce memory requirements during fine-tuning of large language models (LLMs). LoRA-FA achieves this by freezing the projection-down weight matrix A and updating only the projection-up weight B, thereby eliminating the need to store intermediate activations. This approach, while maintaining comparable performance to LoRA, significantly reduces memory usage and allows for efficient fine-tuning of LLMs. The paper presents extensive experiments across various model types and scales, demonstrating the method's effectiveness in reducing memory consumption and maintaining performance.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, with clear figures and tables that effectively illustrate the proposed method and its benefits.
- The idea of freezing A and updating only B is simple yet effective, and the method is easy to implement.
- Extensive experiments have been conducted on multiple model types and scales, demonstrating the effectiveness of the proposed method in reducing memory usage and maintaining performance.
- The paper provides a detailed analysis of the memory complexity of LoRA-FA, comparing it to other methods like full-parameter fine-tuning (FT) and LoRA.
- The method is compatible with other advanced memory optimization methods like FlashAttention, QLoRA, and ZeRO, which further enhances its utility.
- The paper presents results for various models, including RoBERTa, T5, and LLaMA, across multiple tasks, showing that LoRA-FA is effective in reducing memory consumption and maintaining performance.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper could benefit from a more detailed discussion on the limitations of the proposed method, particularly in scenarios where it may not perform as well as other methods.
- The experimental results show that LoRA-FA performs worse than LoRA in some cases, which is not adequately explained.
- The paper does not include a comparison with other parameter-efficient fine-tuning methods like adapter, prompt tuning, or LoRA-qv, which could provide a more comprehensive evaluation of the proposed method.
- There is a lack of discussion on the potential negative societal impacts of the proposed method, which could be a significant concern for some readers.
- The paper could benefit from a more thorough discussion on the convergence speed of LoRA-FA compared to LoRA, as this is a critical aspect of the proposed method.
- The paper does not provide a detailed analysis of the computational overhead introduced by LoRA-FA during the fine-tuning phase, which could affect its practical deployment in real-world scenarios.

**Questions:**
- Could you clarify why LoRA-FA performs worse than LoRA in some cases, and what specific scenarios or conditions lead to this outcome?
- How does the proposed method compare to other parameter-efficient fine-tuning methods like adapter, prompt tuning, or LoRA-qv?
- Can you provide a more detailed analysis of the computational overhead introduced by LoRA-FA during the fine-tuning phase, and how this might affect the practical deployment of the method?
- Is there a specific reason why LoRA-FA does not include a bias term in its equations?
- Can you provide a more detailed discussion on the convergence speed of LoRA-FA compared to LoRA, and how this might impact the practical deployment of the method?
- Could you elaborate on the potential negative societal impacts of the proposed method, and how these might be mitigated or addressed?
