 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel setwise contrastive learning method for neural topic models, aiming to enhance topic modeling by capturing shared semantics among documents. It formulates the training of contrastive neural topic models as a multi-objective optimization problem, optimizing for a Pareto stationary solution that balances the trade-off between the ELBO and contrastive objectives. The proposed methodology is evaluated across several datasets, showing improvements in topic coherence and topic diversity. The paper also includes an ablation study to demonstrate the effectiveness of the multi-objective optimization approach. However, concerns were raised about the clarity of the methodology, the lack of detailed comparisons with existing methods, and the potential for overfitting due to the complexity of the model.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel setwise contrastive learning algorithm for neural topic models, which addresses the problem of learning low-level mutual information in neural topic models.
- The proposed methodology is formulated as a multi-task learning problem and adapted multi-objective optimization algorithm to find a Pareto solution that moderates the effects of multiple objectives on the update of neural topic model parameters.
- The paper is well-written, easy to follow, and provides clear explanations of the proposed method and its advantages over existing methods.
- Extensive experiments demonstrate that the proposed approach can enhance contrastive neural topic models in terms of topic coherence, topic diversity, and downstream performance.
- The ablation study is well-designed and provides a clear comparison of the proposed method with other methods, showing the effectiveness of the multi-objective optimization approach.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed comparison with existing methods, particularly in terms of the experimental setup and the selection of hyperparameters.
- The use of ChatGPT for data augmentation raises concerns about the fairness of the comparison, as it might provide an unfair advantage over other methods.
- The paper does not provide sufficient evidence to support the claims made about the effectiveness of the proposed method in capturing shared semantics among documents.
- The methodology section could be clearer, particularly in explaining the optimization process and the selection of hyperparameters.
- The paper does not adequately address the potential for overfitting due to the complexity of the model, which could affect the generalizability of the results.
- The paper does not sufficiently discuss the limitations of the proposed method, which could help in understanding its applicability and potential drawbacks.

**Questions:**
- Could you clarify the specifics of the data augmentation process using ChatGPT, including the selection of options and the impact on the results?
- How do you ensure that the results are not biased by the use of ChatGPT, and what steps were taken to control for this in the experimental design?
- Can you provide more details on the experimental setup, particularly the selection of hyperparameters and the impact of these choices on the results?
- How do you address the potential for overfitting in your model, and what steps were taken to mitigate this risk?
- Could you elaborate on the potential limitations of your proposed method and how these might affect its practical application?
