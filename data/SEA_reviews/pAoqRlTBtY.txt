 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel framework called the Causal Modelling Agent (CMA), which integrates Large Language Models (LLMs) with Deep Structural Causal Models (DSCMs) to facilitate causal discovery. The CMA leverages LLMs to generate hypotheses about causal relationships and uses these to guide the training of DSCMs. The approach is evaluated on both synthetic and real-world datasets, demonstrating improvements over existing methods. The framework is designed to handle multi-modal data and complex causal structures, offering a method for discovering causal relationships that is both data-driven and metadata-informed.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel framework that integrates Large Language Models (LLMs) with Deep Structural Causal Models (DSCMs) to enhance causal discovery, providing a new methodology for handling complex causal relationships.
- The approach is evaluated on both synthetic and real-world datasets, demonstrating improvements over existing methods and providing a robust validation of the proposed method.
- The paper is well-written and easy to follow, making it accessible to a broad audience, including those with a background in causal discovery and machine learning.
- The methodology is innovative, combining LLMs with DSCMs to create a new framework for causal discovery, which is a significant contribution to the field.
- The paper includes a detailed discussion on the limitations and potential improvements of the proposed method, which enhances the credibility and applicability of the findings.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a thorough comparison with existing methods in terms of computational efficiency and scalability, particularly in handling large datasets.
- There is a need for more detailed explanations and justifications for certain methodological choices, such as the use of specific LLMs and the choice of metrics for evaluating causal discovery.
- The paper does not provide a comprehensive discussion on the limitations and potential biases of the LLMs used, which could affect the reliability and generalizability of the findings.
- The paper's evaluation primarily focuses on synthetic datasets, which may not fully reflect the performance of the method in real-world scenarios.
- The paper could benefit from a more detailed discussion on the methodology, including the specifics of how the LLMs are integrated with the DSCMs and how the causal graphs are constructed.

**Questions:**
- How does the proposed method compare in terms of computational efficiency and scalability with other existing methods, especially in handling large datasets?
- Can the authors provide more details on the specific LLMs used in the experiments and how they were chosen?
- How does the method handle the potential biases and limitations of the LLMs, particularly in terms of the accuracy and reliability of the causal hypotheses generated?
- Could the authors provide more examples or case studies to illustrate the practical applications and benefits of the proposed method in real-world scenarios?
- How does the method handle the potential for feedback loops in causal graphs, and what are the implications of this for the accuracy and validity of the causal relationships identified?
