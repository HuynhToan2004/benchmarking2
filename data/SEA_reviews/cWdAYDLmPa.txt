 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel self-supervised learning (SSL) method named DIM-UA, which utilizes an unbalanced atlas to enhance the representation learning of high-dimensional data. This method is adapted from the Spatiotemporal Deep Informax (ST-DIM) framework and aims to improve upon the state-of-the-art SSL approaches, particularly in the AtariARI benchmark. The methodology involves learning multiple output embeddings with membership probabilities, which are used to select the most probable chart for encoding, thereby improving the efficiency and effectiveness of the SSL process. The paper provides theoretical insights and empirical evidence to support the proposed method, although it has been critiqued for its limited experimental scope and potential over-reliance on the AtariARI benchmark.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel SSL method using an unbalanced atlas (UA) for representation learning, which is a significant advancement in the field.
- The methodology is well-articulated, with clear theoretical underpinnings and empirical evidence supporting the proposed approach.
- The experiments conducted on the AtariARI benchmark demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed method, showing improvements over existing SSL methods.
- The paper is well-written and easy to follow, with a clear presentation of the methodology and results.
- The introduction of the UA paradigm and the use of dilated prediction targets are innovative contributions to the field of SSL.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper's experimental evaluation is limited to the AtariARI benchmark, which may not generalize well to other datasets or real-world applications.
- The manuscript could benefit from a more comprehensive discussion on related work, particularly in the area of manifold-based SSL methods.
- The paper lacks a detailed discussion on the computational efficiency and resource requirements of the proposed method, which is crucial for practical implementation.
- The manuscript could be improved by providing a more detailed description of the experimental setup and methodology, including the specifics of the model architecture and training parameters.
- The paper's contribution to the field is seen as incremental, as it primarily adapts existing methods rather than introducing new theoretical insights.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors provide more details on the computational efficiency and resource requirements of the proposed method, particularly in comparison to other SSL methods?
- How does the proposed method perform on other datasets, and can the authors provide results on other benchmarks to demonstrate its generalizability?
- What is the impact of the number of output heads on the performance of the proposed method, and how does this compare to other SSL methods?
- How does the proposed method handle the potential issue of overfitting, especially in scenarios where the number of output heads is high?
- Could the authors clarify the role and impact of the dilated prediction targets in the performance of the SSL method?
- What are the specific advantages of using an unbalanced atlas over other SSL methods, and how does this approach contribute to more efficient and effective SSL?
