 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel method for estimating camera poses using a bundle of rays, which is a departure from traditional methods that focus on global parametrization of camera extrinsics. The approach involves predicting rays for each patch in an image, which are then used to infer camera poses. This method is tested on the CO3D dataset and shows improved performance over existing methods. The paper also explores the use of a denoising diffusion model to enhance the accuracy of the predicted rays. The method's novelty lies in its use of ray parametrization, which allows for a more precise estimation of camera poses from sparse views. However, the paper's experimental validation is limited to the CO3D dataset, and there are concerns about the computational complexity and the generalizability of the method to other datasets and real-world scenarios.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, with clear figures that effectively illustrate the proposed method.
- The idea of using rays to represent camera poses is novel and interesting, providing a new perspective on camera pose estimation.
- The paper demonstrates the effectiveness of the proposed method through experiments on the CO3D dataset, showing improved performance over existing methods.
- The use of a denoising diffusion model to enhance the accuracy of the predicted rays is a promising approach.
- The paper is generally well-motivated, with a clear and concise presentation that makes it easy to follow.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a comprehensive comparison with existing methods, particularly those that use different camera parametrizations or other approaches to pose estimation.
- The experimental results are not convincing, with insufficient validation on other datasets or real-world scenarios.
- The computational complexity of the proposed method is high, which could limit its practical application.
- The paper does not adequately address the issue of coordinate system ambiguity, which is a significant challenge in camera pose estimation.
- The method's generalizability is questionable, as it is tested only on the CO3D dataset, which may not be representative of real-world scenarios.
- There is a lack of discussion on the limitations of the proposed method, which is crucial for understanding its applicability and potential shortcomings.

**Questions:**
- Can the proposed method be extended to other datasets or real-world scenarios? If so, how does it perform in these settings?
- How does the method handle coordinate system ambiguity, and what are the implications of this on the accuracy of the estimated poses?
- What are the specific advantages of using a ray parametrization over other methods, such as those that directly regress camera extrinsics?
- How does the method perform when there is a significant amount of noise in the input images?
- Can the authors provide more details on the computational complexity of the proposed method and discuss potential strategies for reducing this complexity?
- How does the method perform when there are missing or occluded rays, and what are the implications of this on the accuracy of the estimated poses?
- Could the authors provide a more detailed comparison with existing methods, including those that use different camera parametrizations or other approaches to pose estimation?
