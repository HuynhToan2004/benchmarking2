 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel method for certifying the trustworthiness of explanations provided by machine learning models, focusing on the concept of a "trust region" around a given example. This method, named Ecertify, uses a series of algorithms to find the largest hypercube centered on an example where the explanation maintains a certain quality metric. The paper provides theoretical guarantees and empirical evidence, demonstrating the effectiveness of Ecertify on both synthetic and real-world datasets. It addresses the challenge of finding a region around a query where the explanation remains valid, thus enhancing the reliability of explanations in machine learning models.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel problem of black box (probabilistic) explanation certification, which is a significant advancement in the field of explainable AI.
- The proposed method, Ecertify, is innovative and provides theoretical guarantees, supported by empirical evidence showing its effectiveness on both synthetic and real data.
- The paper is well-written, clear, and easy to follow, with a clear presentation of the problem, methodology, and results.
- The methodology is general and applicable to various datasets and models, demonstrating its versatility and potential impact.
- The paper provides a comprehensive theoretical analysis, including finite sample exponentially decaying bounds that can be estimated in practice, which adds credibility to the proposed solutions.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed discussion on the limitations of the proposed method, particularly concerning the assumptions made and their impact on the applicability and effectiveness of the method.
- The paper could benefit from a more detailed comparison with other existing methods, especially those that also address the problem of explanation quality.
- The paper does not provide a clear explanation of how the quality metric is defined and how it impacts the results, which could limit the understanding and reproducibility of the findings.
- The paper does not discuss the computational complexity of the proposed method, which is crucial for understanding its scalability and efficiency.
- The paper could benefit from a more rigorous discussion on the assumptions made in the theoretical analysis and how they align with real-world scenarios.

**Questions:**
- Could you clarify the assumptions made in the theoretical analysis and how they align with real-world scenarios?
- How does the quality metric impact the results, and could you provide a more detailed explanation of its definition and calculation?
- Can you discuss the computational complexity of the proposed method and how it scales with the size of the dataset or the complexity of the model?
- How does the proposed method compare to other existing methods in terms of efficiency and effectiveness?
- Could you provide a more detailed discussion on the limitations of the proposed method and how these might affect its applicability and effectiveness in different scenarios?
