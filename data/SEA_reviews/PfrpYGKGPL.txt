 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel evaluation framework, the Entity-Deduction Arena (EDA), designed to assess the conversational reasoning and planning capabilities of Large Language Models (LLMs). The framework involves a game where LLMs interact with a judge to deduce entities through a series of questions, with the performance measured by metrics such as the number of turns, success rate, and score. The study includes experiments with various LLMs, including GPT-4, GPT-3.5, and Vicuna, and evaluates their performance against human baselines. The paper also explores the use of reinforcement learning to enhance the reasoning and planning capacity of LLMs.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel evaluation framework, the Entity-Deduction Arena (EDA), which is original and provides a new perspective on evaluating conversational reasoning and planning capabilities of LLMs.
- The framework is designed to assess the strategic planning and deductive reasoning abilities of LLMs in asking entity-deducing questions, which is a significant contribution to the field.
- The paper is well-written, clear, and easy to follow, with comprehensive details on the experimental setup, methodology, and results.
- The authors have conducted extensive experiments, comparing various LLMs including GPT-4, GPT-3.5, Claude-2, Vicuna, and an in-house 65B model, and have included a human baseline for comparison.
- The study includes a detailed analysis of the models' performance, including the number of turns, success rate, and score, which provides valuable insights into the capabilities of LLMs.
- The paper also explores the potential of using reinforcement learning to enhance the reasoning and planning capacity of LLMs, which is a promising area for further research.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a clear definition and explanation of the "judge" and "guesser" roles, which could lead to confusion about the roles and responsibilities of each participant in the game.
- There is a lack of clarity on the "human baseline" used in the study, particularly how it was established and what it entails.
- The paper does not discuss the potential societal impacts of the proposed framework, which could be significant given the potential applications in AI development.
- The study only evaluates LLMs in a single task, which might not fully demonstrate the generalizability of the models' capabilities across different tasks.
- The paper does not address the limitations of the proposed framework, such as its applicability to different types of entities or its scalability to larger or more complex scenarios.
- The paper could benefit from a more detailed discussion on the potential ethical considerations of the framework, particularly in terms of privacy and security.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors clarify the roles and responsibilities of the "judge" and "guesser" in the Entity-Deduction Arena (EDA) game?
- How was the "human baseline" established, and what does it entail in terms of performance metrics and methodology?
- What are the potential societal impacts of the proposed framework, and how might it be used to advance AI development?
- Could the authors provide more details on the training and testing data used in the study, and how they were selected and balanced?
- How does the proposed framework handle ambiguity in the entities being deduced, and what strategies are employed to ensure the accuracy of the deductions?
- What are the implications of the study's findings for the development of autonomous agents, and how might these agents be trained to behave more intelligently in ambiguous circumstances?
