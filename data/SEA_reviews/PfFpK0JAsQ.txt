 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel unsupervised feature selection method, GRSSLFS, which utilizes a basis of linearly independent features to represent the original feature space in a lower-dimensional space. This method combines subspace learning and matrix factorization of the basis matrix to optimize feature selection. The proposed method, GRSSLFS, is evaluated on several datasets and reportedly outperforms existing methods. However, the paper lacks a thorough theoretical analysis and comparative experiments with state-of-the-art methods, which could strengthen its claims. Additionally, the paper's presentation and clarity need improvement, particularly in explaining the mathematical notations and the experimental setups.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, easy to follow, and provides a comprehensive literature review, effectively positioning the proposed method within the current research landscape.
- The proposed method is novel and innovative, combining subspace learning and matrix factorization of the basis matrix to optimize feature selection.
- The paper presents a clear motivation and a well-defined problem statement, making it easy for readers to understand the methodology and the rationale behind the proposed method.
- The experimental results are promising, demonstrating the effectiveness of the proposed method across various datasets.
- The methodology section is detailed, and the paper provides a thorough description of the mathematical formulations, enhancing the reproducibility of the research.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed theoretical analysis and comparison with state-of-the-art methods, which could have strengthened the claims of the proposed method's effectiveness and novelty.
- The paper's presentation needs improvement, particularly in the explanation of mathematical notations and the experimental setup, which could enhance the clarity and accessibility of the content.
- The experimental results are not convincingly superior to existing methods, and the paper does not sufficiently discuss the limitations or potential negative societal impacts of the proposed method.
