 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel meta-adversarial multi-view representation learning framework named MAVRL, designed to address the vulnerability of meta-learners to adversarial attacks across unseen domains. MAVRL employs dual encoders and a label-free adversarial attack to maximize discrepancies between adversarial examples, enhancing the robustness of meta-learners against domain shifts. The framework is evaluated on multiple benchmarks, demonstrating significant improvements in adversarial robustness without compromising clean accuracy. The paper also discusses the limitations of existing meta-adversarial learning methods and the challenges of transferring robustness across unseen domains.

**Strengths:**
- The paper addresses an important and interesting problem in the field of meta-adversarial learning, focusing on the transferability of adversarial robustness across unseen tasks and domains with limited data.
- The proposed method is well-motivated and the paper is well-written, making it easy to follow and understand.
- The experiments conducted on multiple benchmarks demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed method, showing significant improvements in adversarial robustness and clean accuracy compared to existing methods.
- The ablation study is comprehensive and well-designed, providing insights into the effectiveness of the proposed method's components.
- The paper is novel in its approach to learning transferable robust representations via multi-view consistency, which is a significant contribution to the field.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a clear definition and explanation of the multi-view consistency objective, which could benefit from additional clarity.
- The experiments are limited to few-shot learning, and it is unclear how the proposed method would perform in other meta-learning settings such as few-shot detection or regression.
- The paper does not discuss the limitations of the proposed method, which could provide a more comprehensive understanding of its applicability and effectiveness.
- There is a lack of discussion on the computational complexity and efficiency of the proposed method, which could be a concern for practical implementation.
- The paper does not adequately address the limitations of existing meta-adversarial learning methods, which could have been discussed to highlight the novelty and effectiveness of the proposed method.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors provide a more detailed explanation of the multi-view consistency objective and its role in the proposed method?
- How does the proposed method perform in other meta-learning settings such as few-shot detection or regression?
- Could the authors discuss the limitations of the proposed method and how these might affect its practical applicability?
- What are the computational complexities and efficiency of the proposed method compared to existing methods?
- How does the proposed method compare to existing methods in terms of transferability of adversarial robustness across unseen tasks and domains?
- Can the authors provide more details on the training time and memory requirements of the proposed method compared to existing methods?
