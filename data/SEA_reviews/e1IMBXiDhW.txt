 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a unified matrix information-theoretic framework for analyzing contrastive and non-contrastive self-supervised learning methods, aiming to optimize matrix KL divergence and matrix cross-entropy. The authors propose a new method, Matrix-SSL, which integrates matrix-KL-uniformity and matrix-KL-alignment losses to achieve better performance in both linear evaluation and transfer learning tasks. The paper claims that Matrix-SSL outperforms existing state-of-the-art methods on the ImageNet dataset and MS-COCO dataset, demonstrating its effectiveness and novelty. However, concerns are raised about the novelty of the proposed method, the clarity of the theoretical explanations, and the comparative analysis with existing methods.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel matrix information-theoretic framework for analyzing contrastive and non-contrastive learning methods, providing a unified perspective on various self-supervised learning methods.
- The proposed method, Matrix-SSL, achieves state-of-the-art results on both linear evaluation and transfer learning tasks, demonstrating its effectiveness.
- The paper is well-written, with clear and concise explanations that aid in understanding the proposed methodologies and their applications.
- The integration of matrix-KL-uniformity and matrix-KL-alignment losses is innovative, offering a novel approach to addressing the challenges in self-supervised learning.
- The paper provides a detailed theoretical analysis of the proposed method, which is supported by empirical results showing the superiority of Matrix-SSL over existing methods.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a comprehensive review of existing works in the field, which could have strengthened the argument for the novelty and effectiveness of the proposed method.
- The theoretical explanations, particularly the connections between different matrix quantities and their implications, are not clearly articulated, leading to potential confusion.
- The paper does not adequately address the computational complexity of the proposed method, which could be a significant concern for practical applications.
- The paper does not include sufficient ablation studies to demonstrate the effectiveness of each component of the proposed method, which could undermine the credibility of the results.
- The paper does not provide sufficient comparisons with other methods, such as BYOL, which could have provided a more robust evaluation of the proposed method.
- The paper's claims of novelty are questionable as it appears to be a straightforward extension of existing methods without significant innovation.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors clarify the connection between the rank-increasing phenomenon and the proposed method, especially in terms of how it contributes to the effectiveness of the method?
- How does the proposed method perform when compared to other methods like BYOL, particularly in terms of computational efficiency and practical performance?
- Could the authors provide more detailed comparisons with other methods, including a broader range of self-supervised learning methods, to better establish the effectiveness and novelty of the proposed method?
- What are the specific contributions of the proposed method beyond existing methods, particularly in terms of computational efficiency, theoretical contributions, and practical performance?
- How does the proposed method address the challenges of non-contrastive learning, and what are the implications of this approach for the broader field of self-supervised learning?
