 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel approach to modeling nonlinear dynamical systems using Koopman autoencoders, specifically focusing on the issue of trajectory drift in the latent space. The authors propose a method called "Periodic Reencoding" to address this issue, which involves decoding and re-encoding the latent trajectories at regular intervals. This method is validated through experiments on both low-dimensional and high-dimensional systems, showing improvements over baseline methods. The paper is well-written and provides a comprehensive review of Koopman theory, although it could benefit from clearer explanations of certain terms and equations.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, clear, and easy to follow, with a comprehensive review of Koopman theory in the appendix.
- The proposed method, Periodic Reencoding, is novel and addresses a significant issue in the field of Koopman autoencoders, specifically the trajectory drift problem.
- The paper is supported by extensive experiments that demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed method.
- The authors have provided a thorough evaluation of the method, including comparisons with other baselines and detailed experimental results.
- The paper is well-organized, making it easy to navigate and understand the content.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper could benefit from clearer explanations and definitions of certain terms and equations, particularly in the context of Koopman theory and the use of the Koopman operator.
- The paper primarily focuses on state prediction, which might limit its applicability to other tasks such as control or optimization.
- The methodology section could be improved for clarity, especially in explaining the periodic reencoding process and the role of the Koopman operator.
- The paper's experimental setup and results could be better explained, particularly in terms of how the results were validated and the implications of the observed trajectory drift.
- There is a lack of discussion on the limitations of the proposed method, such as its applicability to high-dimensional systems or its computational complexity.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors clarify the definition and role of the Koopman operator in the context of their method?
- How does the proposed method compare to other methods that also address the trajectory drift problem in Koopman autoencoders?
- Can the authors provide more details on the experimental setup, particularly how the trajectory drift was observed and how it was validated?
- How does the method perform in terms of computational complexity and scalability, especially in high-dimensional systems?
- Is there a possibility to extend the method to include other types of tasks, such as control or optimization?
- Could the authors discuss the potential limitations of their method and how these might affect its applicability in real-world scenarios?
