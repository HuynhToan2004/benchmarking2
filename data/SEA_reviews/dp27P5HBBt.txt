 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel Periodicity Decoupling Framework (PDF) for long-term time series forecasting, which models 2D temporal variations to capture both short-term and long-term dependencies. The framework is structured into three main components: Multi-periodic Decoupling Block (MDB), Dual Variations Modeling Block (DVMB), and Variations Aggregation Block (VAB). The MDB decouples the input series into short-term and long-term components, DVMB models these variations, and VAB aggregates the outputs. The method is evaluated on seven datasets and shows superior performance over existing methods. The paper also includes ablation studies and computational complexity analyses to support its claims.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel framework, the Periodicity Decoupling Framework (PDF), which effectively models 2D temporal variations for long-term time series forecasting.
- The methodology is well-explained, with clear descriptions of the proposed components and their roles in the overall framework.
- The paper provides extensive ablation studies and computational complexity analyses, which support the claims of the proposed method.
- The experimental results demonstrate the superiority of the proposed method over existing state-of-the-art methods, showing both improved forecasting performance and computational efficiency.
- The paper is well-written, making it easy to follow and understand, and the experimental results are comprehensive, covering multiple datasets and metrics.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed discussion on the limitations and potential negative societal impacts of the proposed method.
- The computational complexity analysis in the paper is not convincing, as it does not consider the computational complexity of other components such as the convolution module.
- The paper does not compare the proposed method with other recent state-of-the-art methods, which could have provided a more robust evaluation of its effectiveness.
- The paper does not include code, which could hinder the reproducibility of the results.
- The paper does not provide a detailed discussion on the selection of hyperparameters and their impact on the results.
- The paper does not discuss the potential negative societal impacts of the proposed method, which could be a significant concern in some applications.

**Questions:**
