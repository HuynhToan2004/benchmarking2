 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel method, FreeDyG, for link prediction in dynamic graphs, focusing on continuous-time dynamic graphs. The approach leverages a frequency-enhanced MLP-Mixer layer to capture periodic temporal patterns and the "shift" phenomenon in the frequency domain, alongside a node interaction frequency encoding approach. This method aims to address the challenges of overfitting and optimization difficulties in existing methods by using a Fourier Transform to analyze the frequency domain, which is more nuanced than traditional methods. Extensive experiments on seven real-world datasets demonstrate the effectiveness of FreeDyG, showing improvements over state-of-the-art methods in both transductive and inductive settings.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-organized, making it easy to follow, and the proposed method is simple yet effective, with a clear and intuitive idea.
- The methodology is supported by extensive experiments that demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed method, outperforming state-of-the-art methods in both transductive and inductive settings.
- The paper is well-written, with detailed experimental settings and ablation studies that help understand the contributions of different components of the proposed method.
- The novel approach of using frequency domain analysis to capture periodic temporal patterns and the "shift" phenomenon is innovative and could potentially improve the performance of dynamic graph learning tasks.
- The paper is original in its approach, using Fourier Transform to analyze the frequency domain, which is more nuanced than traditional methods, and the proposed method is simple yet effective.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed discussion on the limitations and potential negative societal impacts of the proposed method.
- There is a lack of clarity on how the method performs on larger graphs and how it scales with the size of the graph.
- The paper does not sufficiently differentiate its approach from existing methods, particularly in terms of the novelty of using Fourier Transform for dynamic graph learning.
- The paper does not provide sufficient ablation studies to demonstrate the effectiveness of each component of the proposed method, such as the filter layer and the node interaction frequency encoding approach.
- The paper does not discuss the limitations of the proposed method, which could include potential challenges in handling large datasets or complex graph structures.
- The paper could benefit from a more detailed discussion on the computational complexity and time efficiency of the proposed method compared to existing methods.

**Questions:**
- How does the proposed method perform on larger graphs, and how does it scale with the size of the graph?
- Can the authors provide more details on the limitations of the proposed method and how these might impact its applicability in real-world scenarios?
- How does the proposed method compare in terms of computational complexity and time efficiency to existing methods, particularly in handling large and complex dynamic graphs?
- Could the authors provide more ablation studies to demonstrate the effectiveness of each component of the proposed method, particularly the filter layer and the node interaction frequency encoding approach?
- How does the proposed method handle the shift phenomenon in dynamic graphs, and can the authors provide more details on this aspect of the method?
