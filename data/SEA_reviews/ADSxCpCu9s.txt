 **Summary:**
The paper introduces LoTa-Bench, a benchmark system designed to evaluate the performance of task planning for home-service embodied agents using large language models (LLMs). It employs two pairs of datasets and simulators, ALFRED and AI2-THOR, and an extension of Watch-And-Help and VirtualHome. The benchmark system evaluates LLMs and prompts, exploring enhancements to the baseline planner, and provides extensive experiments to investigate the effects of various factors such as pre-trained model selection and prompt construction. The paper is commended for its comprehensive evaluation and the release of benchmark code and extended dataset, which could significantly advance research in this area.
