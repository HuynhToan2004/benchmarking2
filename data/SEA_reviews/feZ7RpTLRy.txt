 **Summary:**
The paper explores the application of hyperbolic embeddings in machine learning and algorithmic communities, specifically comparing the BFKL embedder with the Lorentz embedder. It highlights the computational efficiency of BFKL, which is about 100 times faster than Lorentz, and discusses the performance of both methods across various datasets. The paper also introduces a discrete hyperbolic random graph model (DHRG) to enhance the embedding quality. Despite its strengths in efficiency and clarity, the paper has been critiqued for its lack of novelty, inadequate discussion on the significance of its findings, and insufficient comparisons with other relevant methods.

- The paper is well-written, clear, and easy to follow, with a good presentation of the results.
- It provides a thorough comparison of various hyperbolic embedding methods, including the BFKL embedder and Lorentz embedder, and discusses their performance on different datasets.
- The paper is significant in bridging the gap between machine learning and algorithmic communities by introducing the BFKL embedder, which is less well-known in the machine learning community.
