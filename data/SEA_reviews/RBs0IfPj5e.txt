 **Summary:**
The paper introduces BackDiff, a novel generative model leveraging score-based diffusion models to perform backmapping from coarse-grained (CG) representations to all-atom structures. BackDiff addresses the challenge of transferability across various CG models and proteins by utilizing a self-supervised training framework that conditions on arbitrary CG auxiliary variables. This model is designed to generalize across different CG models, offering a plug-and-play solution for protein researchers. Extensive experiments demonstrate BackDiff's superior performance over existing methods, highlighting its flexibility and adaptability. Despite its strengths, concerns are raised about the clarity of presentation, the need for more comprehensive baseline comparisons, and the lack of detailed discussion on the model's limitations and societal impacts.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel approach, BackDiff, which achieves generalization and reliability in the protein backmapping problem by leveraging a score-based diffusion model with geometric representations.
- BackDiff is a plug-and-play solution that can be applied to various CG models without the need for retraining, which is a significant advantage in the field.
- The methodology is well-explained, and the paper is well-written, making it accessible and easy to understand.
- The proposed method demonstrates superior performance compared to existing methods, showcasing its effectiveness in the backmapping problem.
- The paper includes comprehensive experiments that validate the proposed method, demonstrating its effectiveness across different CG models and proteins.
- The inclusion of a self-supervised training framework and manifold constraint sampling allows for flexibility and adaptability to different CG models and proteins.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed discussion on the limitations of the proposed method, which is crucial for understanding its scope and applicability.
- There is a need for more comprehensive baseline comparisons to fully demonstrate the advantages of the proposed method.
- The presentation of the paper could be improved, particularly in the clarity of figures and the organization of content.
- The paper does not sufficiently discuss the societal impact of the proposed method, which is an important consideration for scientific advancements.
- There is a lack of discussion on the potential negative societal impacts of the proposed method, which should be addressed to ensure ethical considerations are met.
- The paper could benefit from a more detailed discussion on the challenges and complexities of the backmapping problem, which would enhance the reader's understanding of the problem and the proposed solution.
