 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel black-box adversarial attack method that focuses on region-wise perturbations, utilizing automatic region selection to optimize perturbations based on group sparsity constraints. The proposed method, which includes both overlapping and non-overlapping groups, is evaluated against various baseline methods on datasets like CIFAR-10, MNIST, and ImageNet. The paper provides theoretical guarantees and empirical evidence demonstrating the effectiveness of the proposed method in reducing the number of perturbations and queries needed for successful attacks. Despite its strengths in theoretical analysis and empirical evaluation, the paper is criticized for its limited novelty, insufficient comparison with recent region-wise attack methods, and potential issues in the clarity and organization of its content.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, with clear and detailed explanations of the proposed method and its theoretical guarantees.
- The proposed method is effective in reducing the number of perturbations and queries needed for successful attacks, as demonstrated by empirical results.
- The paper includes a comprehensive evaluation of the proposed method against various baseline methods, providing a thorough comparison.
- Theoretical performance bounds are provided, enhancing the understanding of the method's capabilities.
- The paper introduces a novel approach to adversarial attacks by using group sparsity constraints, which is both theoretically and practically sound.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed comparison with recent region-wise attack methods, which limits the understanding of its novelty and effectiveness in the current research landscape.
- The motivation behind the use of group sparsity constraints is not clearly explained, which could affect the reader's understanding of the method's rationale.
- There are inconsistencies in the experimental setup, particularly in the comparison of perturbation ratios and the number of queries used across different datasets.
- The paper could benefit from a clearer presentation and organization, especially in the introduction and methodology sections.
- The paper does not adequately address the potential negative societal impacts of its work, which is a significant omission in ethical considerations.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors clarify the motivation behind the use of group sparsity constraints and how they contribute to the effectiveness of the proposed method?
- How does the proposed method compare in terms of computational efficiency and practical implementation with existing region-wise attack methods?
- Could the authors provide more detailed explanations or examples to help understand the theoretical guarantees and their implications in practical scenarios?
- How does the proposed method perform when compared to other methods that use similar constraints, such as those mentioned in related works?
- Could the authors address the concerns regarding the clarity and organization of the paper, particularly in the introduction and methodology sections?
