 **Summary:**
The paper introduces two novel methods, node2ket and node2ket+, for network embedding that represent nodes as high-dimensional product quantum states in a super high-dimensional quantum Hilbert space. These methods aim to address the challenges of low-dimensional embedding spaces, such as overfitting and high computational complexity. Node2ket and node2ket+ are designed to be more efficient and effective than existing methods, with a focus on reducing the computational burden and improving model performance. The paper also introduces a C++ library, LIBN2K, for the efficient development of high-dimensional network embedding algorithms. The methods are evaluated against several benchmarks, showing superior performance in tasks like network reconstruction, link prediction, and node classification. However, concerns are raised regarding the lack of comparison with graph neural networks and the absence of quantum computing in the experiments.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, with clear and understandable content that effectively conveys the authors' ideas.
- The proposed method is novel, introducing a new paradigm for network embedding that is both efficient and effective.
- The method is theoretically sound, with a solid theoretical foundation and rigorous proofs provided in the appendix.
- The experiments are comprehensive and convincing, demonstrating the superiority of the proposed method over existing approaches in various network embedding tasks.
- The development of the LIBN2K library for high-dimensional network embedding is a significant contribution, enhancing the practical application of the proposed methods.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a comparison with graph neural networks (GNNs), which could provide a more comprehensive evaluation of the proposed method's effectiveness.
- The experiments are limited to specific datasets and do not include a broader range of network types, which could affect the generalizability of the results.
- There is no discussion on the limitations of the proposed methods, which could help in understanding the scope and applicability of the work.
- The paper does not include a discussion on the potential negative societal impacts of the research, which is a critical aspect of ethical considerations in scientific research.
- The paper does not provide a detailed analysis of the computational complexity of the proposed methods, which could be crucial for understanding their scalability and efficiency.

**Questions:**
- How does the proposed method compare with GNNs in terms of performance and computational efficiency?
- Can the authors provide more details on the hardware and software configurations used in the experiments, including the specifications of the CPU and the operating system?
- How does the method handle the issue of overfitting, especially in high-dimensional embeddings?
- Can the authors discuss the limitations of their proposed methods and suggest potential areas for future research?
- How does the method perform when applied to networks with different characteristics, such as different densities or sizes?
- Could the authors provide more insights into the computational complexity of the proposed methods and discuss any potential challenges in scaling up the method to larger networks?
