 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel method for latent graph inference using a trainable deep learning architecture called neural snowflakes. This method is designed to discover latent graph geometries by adaptively implementing fractal-like metrics on Rd. The paper demonstrates that any finite weighted graph can be isometrically embedded using a standard MLP encoder combined with the metric implemented by the neural snowflake. Theoretical guarantees are provided, showing that the neural snowflake and MLP encoder can break the curse of dimensionality by requiring only a polynomial number of parameters in the number of nodes. The method is evaluated against state-of-the-art latent graph inference models on graph benchmarks, showing predictive performance that either matches or surpasses existing models without requiring random search for optimal latent geometry.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel method for latent graph inference using a trainable deep learning architecture called neural snowflakes, which is a significant contribution to the field.
- The method is supported by theoretical guarantees, showing that it can break the curse of dimensionality by requiring only a polynomial number of parameters in the number of nodes.
- The paper provides a detailed theoretical analysis and empirical validation of the proposed method, demonstrating its effectiveness through various experiments.
- The concept of using a neural snowflake for latent graph inference is innovative and could potentially lead to new insights and applications in the field.
- The paper is well-written, clear, and easy to follow, making it accessible to a broad audience.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a comprehensive comparison with other existing methods for latent graph inference, which could help in understanding the advantages and limitations of the proposed method.
- The paper does not provide a detailed discussion on the limitations or potential drawbacks of the proposed method, which could affect its practical applicability.
- The paper does not include a detailed discussion on the computational complexity or scalability of the proposed method, which could be a concern for larger or more complex datasets.
- The paper could benefit from a more detailed explanation of the technical aspects of the neural snowflake model, particularly how it is trained and how it compares to other existing models.
- The paper does not include a discussion on the potential societal impacts of the proposed method, which could be an important consideration for some applications.
