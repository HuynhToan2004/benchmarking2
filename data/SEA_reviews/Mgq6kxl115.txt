 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel approach to ensemble learning using a diffusion-based method termed the Diffusion Bridge Network (DBN). This method aims to reduce computational overhead by approximating the output distribution of a deep ensemble using a single diffusion model, thereby avoiding the need to invoke forward passes through all ensemble models. The DBN learns a mapping from the output distribution of a single ensemble member to that of the ensemble, thereby allowing for efficient inference. The method is evaluated on various datasets including CIFAR-10, CIFAR-100, and TinyImageNet, showing improvements in inference speed and accuracy compared to traditional ensemble methods. However, the paper is criticized for its lack of clarity in presentation, particularly in the explanation of the diffusion process and the experimental results.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel method for ensemble learning using a diffusion-based approach, which is a departure from traditional methods such as deep ensembles and bridge networks.
- The methodology is well-explained, and the paper is easy to follow, making it accessible to readers.
- The experimental results demonstrate that the proposed method can achieve better performance and faster inference speed compared to other methods.
- The paper is well-written and the method is well-motivated, addressing the issue of computational overhead in ensemble learning.
- The use of diffusion models to learn the distribution of the ensemble is innovative and could be a significant contribution to the field.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks clarity in the presentation, particularly in the explanation of the diffusion process and the experimental results.
- The method's novelty is questioned as it seems to be a combination of existing methods such as bridge networks and diffusion models, without significant innovation.
- The paper does not provide a detailed comparison with other methods, particularly those that also use diffusion models for ensemble learning.
- The experimental results are not convincing, with marginal improvements over existing methods and a lack of detailed analysis of the results.
- The paper does not discuss the limitations of the proposed method, which could be a significant drawback for readers looking to understand the full implications of the work.
- The paper could benefit from a more thorough literature review and a more detailed comparison with existing methods to establish its novelty and effectiveness.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors clarify the role and impact of the temperature distribution in the diffusion process?
- How does the proposed method compare to other diffusion-based methods for ensemble learning, particularly in terms of performance and computational efficiency?
- Can the authors provide more detailed experimental results, including standard deviations and a more comprehensive comparison with other methods?
- How does the method handle the issue of computational overhead in deep ensembles, and what are the specific advantages of using a diffusion-based approach over other methods?
- Could the authors discuss the limitations of their method and how they might be addressed in future work?
