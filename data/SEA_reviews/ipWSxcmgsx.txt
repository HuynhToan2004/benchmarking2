 **Summary:**
The paper introduces NormIntSleep, a novel method aimed at enhancing the interpretability of deep learning models in sleep classification tasks. It leverages a combination of feature extraction and deep learning embeddings to create a linear projector that transforms embeddings into an interpretable feature space, which is then used to train a decision tree. The method is evaluated on two public datasets and is shown to outperform existing interpretable methods. The paper also proposes a new metric, Alignment_DT, to assess the interpretability of the model, which is claimed to align better with clinical guidelines. Despite the innovative approach and promising results, concerns were raised regarding the novelty of the method, the generalizability of the findings, and the depth of the interpretability analysis.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel method, NormIntSleep, which combines feature extraction and deep learning embeddings to create a linear projector that transforms embeddings into an interpretable feature space.
- The method is evaluated on two public datasets and shown to outperform existing interpretable methods, demonstrating its effectiveness in sleep classification tasks.
- The paper is well-written, clear, and easy to follow, making it accessible to a broad audience.
- The proposed Alignment_DT metric is a useful tool for evaluating the interpretability of models, providing a practical and relevant application in the medical domain.
- The use of a decision tree to interpret the model's decisions is a novel approach that adds depth to the interpretability analysis.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed comparison with state-of-the-art methods in terms of performance and interpretability, which could undermine the perceived effectiveness of the proposed method.
- The novelty of the method is questionable, as it appears to be a simple combination of existing techniques without significant innovation.
- The interpretability of the model is not thoroughly analyzed, with limited discussion on how the model's decisions align with clinical guidelines or how it could be improved to better align with these guidelines.
- The paper suffers from a lack of clarity in some sections, particularly in the description of the methodology and the results.
- The generalizability of the findings is limited due to the focus on sleep classification, and it is unclear how the method could be applied to other medical domains.
- The paper does not adequately address the limitations of the study, such as the potential bias in the datasets used and the generalizability of the findings to other datasets or clinical settings.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors provide more details on the selection of features for the linear projector and how these features align with clinical guidelines?
- How does the proposed method compare to other state-of-the-art methods in terms of performance and interpretability?
- Could the authors elaborate on how the Alignment_DT metric was calculated and how it contributes to the overall interpretability of the model?
- How does the proposed method address the potential bias in the datasets used, and what steps were taken to ensure the generalizability of the findings?
- In terms of the decision tree interpretability, could the authors provide more detailed examples or case studies to illustrate how the model's decisions align with clinical guidelines?
- Could the authors clarify the methodology and results sections to improve the clarity and accessibility of the paper?
