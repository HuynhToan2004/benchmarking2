 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel method called Perturb-then-Diagonalize (PTD) for diagonalizing matrices in state-space models (SSMs), particularly focusing on the HiPPO initialization. The PTD method aims to address the ill-conditioned nature of diagonalizing HiPPO matrices by perturbing them, thereby improving the stability and robustness of the models. This method is demonstrated through theoretical analysis and empirical results, showing that it outperforms existing methods like S4D and S5 in certain scenarios. The paper also discusses the practical implications of the PTD method, including its potential to enhance the robustness of SSMs against perturbations.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, clear, and easy to follow, with a clear motivation for the research and a logical structure that facilitates understanding.
- The proposed method, PTD, is novel and provides a new perspective on the diagonalization of non-normal matrices, which is significant for the field of state-space models.
- The paper includes a comprehensive theoretical analysis that supports the proposed method, including a detailed discussion on the convergence of the proposed method.
- The empirical results demonstrate the effectiveness of the PTD method, showing improvements over existing methods in certain scenarios.
- The paper provides a thorough literature review, positioning the work within the current state of the art and highlighting the novelty of the proposed method.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed discussion on the practical implications of the PTD method, particularly in terms of computational complexity and the choice of perturbation parameters.
- The empirical evaluation is limited to a few datasets, which might not fully demonstrate the generalizability of the proposed method.
- The paper could benefit from more detailed explanations and examples in certain sections, particularly in Section 3, where the theoretical results are presented.
- The experimental setup and results, such as the robustness test in Section 5.2, are not convincingly demonstrated, which could affect the credibility of the claims made.
- The paper could be more self-contained, as some necessary definitions and background information are relegated to the appendix, making the main text less accessible without additional references.

**Questions:**
- Could you provide more details on the computational complexity of the PTD method, particularly in terms of the size of the HiPPO matrix?
- How does the choice of perturbation parameters affect the performance of the PTD method? Is there a general guideline for setting these parameters?
- Can you clarify the experimental setup in Section 5.2, particularly the choice of parameters and the methodology used to generate the robustness test?
- How does the PTD method compare to other methods in terms of computational efficiency and practical implementation?
- Could you elaborate on the choice of datasets for the empirical evaluation and consider including additional datasets to demonstrate the generalizability of the method?
- In the theoretical analysis, could you provide more intuitive explanations or examples to help clarify the results and their implications?
