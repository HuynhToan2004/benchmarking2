 **Summary:**
The paper introduces NeuroBack, a novel approach to enhancing the performance of SAT solvers using Graph Neural Networks (GNNs). NeuroBack focuses on predicting variable phases based on the majority of satisfying assignments, which is crucial for CDCL SAT solving. The methodology involves training a GNN model to predict the phases of backbone variables, which are then used to initialize variables in a SAT solver. This approach allows for offline model inference, reducing the computational demands of frequent online inferences. The paper also introduces a new dataset, DataBack, which is used to train the GNN model. Experimental results show that NeuroBack significantly improves the performance of the Kissat SAT solver, demonstrating its practicality and effectiveness.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, clear, and easy to follow, making it accessible to a broad audience.
- The approach of using GNNs to enhance SAT solvers is novel and practical, particularly the idea of predicting variable phases before the solving process.
- The introduction of the new dataset, DataBack, is a significant contribution to the field, providing a valuable resource for future research.
- The paper presents a comprehensive evaluation of the proposed method, showing that NeuroBack can enhance the performance of the Kissat SAT solver by up to 7.4%.
- The paper effectively addresses the challenge of frequent online inferences in CDCL SAT solvers, which is a significant issue in practical applications.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed comparison with existing methods, particularly in terms of computational efficiency and the specific advantages of using GNNs over other methods.
- The experimental results are limited to a single SAT solver, which may not fully demonstrate the generalizability of the proposed method.
- The paper does not sufficiently discuss the limitations of the proposed method, particularly the potential for overfitting and the scalability of the model to larger datasets.
- The novelty of the approach is questioned as it appears to be a combination of existing techniques without significant new insights or contributions.
- The paper does not provide sufficient details on the training and evaluation of the GNN model, which could affect the reproducibility and understanding of the results.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors provide a more detailed comparison with existing methods, including computational efficiency and the specific advantages of using GNNs over other approaches?
- How does the proposed method perform when applied to different SAT solvers, and what are the potential benefits or challenges in integrating it with other solvers?
- Could the authors discuss the potential limitations of the proposed method, such as overfitting and scalability, and how these might be addressed in future work?
- What are the specific details of the GNN model training and evaluation, including the number of layers, training time, and evaluation metrics used?
- How does the proposed method handle the issue of backbone variables, which are crucial for the effectiveness of the CDCL algorithm?
