 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel method for detecting ordering mistakes in assembly tasks using a knowledge-grounded approach. It employs a BeliefBuilder to dynamically update and augment belief sets based on spatial and temporal constraints. The method is evaluated on both synthetic and real-world datasets, demonstrating superior performance in detecting ordering mistakes. The paper also introduces a new set of annotations for the Assembly101 dataset, enhancing its utility for error detection research. However, the paper's methodology and its application to real-world scenarios are questioned, with concerns about the clarity of the method's presentation and its practical implications.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel approach for detecting ordering mistakes in assembly tasks, focusing on the unique characteristics of assembly tasks such as spatial arrangement and ordering constraints.
- The methodology is well-organized, with clear explanations of the spatial and temporal beliefs, and the paper is generally well-written, making it easy to follow.
- The proposed method demonstrates superior performance in detecting ordering mistakes on both synthetic and real-world datasets, showing the effectiveness of the approach.
- The paper introduces a new set of annotations for the Assembly101 dataset, which could be beneficial for future research in this area.
- The method's ability to dynamically update and augment belief sets in an online manner is highlighted, demonstrating its practical applicability.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed explanation of the BeliefBuilder and Inferencer, which are crucial components of the proposed method. The absence of a detailed algorithm description and a lack of visual aids make it difficult to understand the full process of the method.
- There is a lack of clarity in the presentation of the method, particularly in the explanation of how the spatial and temporal beliefs are integrated and how the method handles different types of mistakes.
- The paper does not provide a detailed analysis of the limitations of the proposed method, which could help in understanding its scope and applicability.
- The paper does not include a comparison with more recent or advanced machine learning methods, which could provide a more robust evaluation of the proposed method.
- The paper's evaluation is limited to a single dataset, which may not fully demonstrate the generalizability of the proposed method.
- The paper does not discuss the potential societal impacts of the proposed method, which could be a significant consideration for the application of the technology.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors provide a more detailed explanation of the BeliefBuilder and Inferencer, possibly including a detailed algorithm description and visual aids to enhance understanding?
- How does the method handle different types of mistakes, such as misorientation mistakes, which are mentioned but not addressed in the experimental results?
- Could the authors clarify the integration of spatial and temporal beliefs in the method and provide a clearer explanation of how the method handles different types of mistakes?
- How does the method perform when applied to real-world scenarios, and what are the potential challenges or limitations in such applications?
- Could the authors discuss the potential societal impacts of the proposed method and how these might be mitigated or addressed?
- How does the proposed method compare to more recent or advanced machine learning methods, and what are the implications of these comparisons for the practical applicability of the method?
