 **Summary:**
The paper investigates the CLIP image encoder by decomposing its image representation into individual image patches, model layers, and attention heads. This decomposition is aimed at understanding the contributions of each component to the final representation, which is then used to interpret the model's behavior and improve its performance. The authors propose a method to find text representations that span the output space of each attention head, revealing the roles of different heads in image processing. The findings are applied to enhance CLIP's zero-shot image segmentation capabilities. Despite the innovative approach and potential utility, the paper is criticized for its limited novelty, unclear presentation, and lack of comprehensive experimental validation.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written and easy to follow, with clear explanations of the methodology and results.
- The findings about the role of individual attention heads in CLIP's image representation are novel and insightful, providing a new perspective on the model's functionality.
- The application of these findings to improve CLIP's zero-shot image segmentation performance demonstrates the practical utility of the research.
- The approach to decomposing image representations into text-interpretable elements is innovative and could be applied to other models like BLIP-2 and DINOv2.
- The paper is structured around a series of well-designed experiments that support the conclusions drawn, and the results are convincing and interesting.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a comprehensive analysis of the limitations and broader impacts of the proposed method, particularly in terms of its applicability to other models and its generalizability across different datasets.
- The novelty of the approach is questioned, as similar decomposition methods have been previously explored in other models.
- The presentation of the paper is unclear in places, particularly in the introduction and some sections of the methodology, which could benefit from clearer explanations and more detailed examples.
- The experimental validation is insufficient, with a lack of comparisons to other models and a limited number of experiments, which could undermine the credibility of the results.
- The paper does not sufficiently address the generalizability of the findings, especially in terms of whether the observed effects are specific to the CLIP model or can be applied to other models.
- There are concerns about the scalability of the proposed method to larger models and datasets, and the potential for overfitting to the specific dataset used in the study.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors clarify the exact methodology used in the decomposition of image representations into text-interpretable elements, particularly how this method compares to other decomposition methods used in similar models?
- How does the proposed method perform when applied to other models like BLIP-2 and DINOv2, and what are the expected outcomes in terms of interpretability and performance improvements?
- Can the authors provide more detailed examples and visualizations to illustrate the decomposition of image representations and the roles of different attention heads?
- How does the proposed method handle the potential for overfitting to the specific dataset used in the study, and what steps were taken to mitigate this risk?
- Can the authors elaborate on the limitations and broader impacts of their method, and discuss potential future directions for research in this area?
