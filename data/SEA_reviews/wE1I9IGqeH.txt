 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel method for continual learning in open-vocabulary image classification, combining a CLIP zero-shot model with an exemplar-based model to leverage the advantages of both systems. The method, which includes a "tree probe" exemplar model and an adaptive instance marginalization (AIM) mechanism, aims to improve upon existing methods by integrating a zero-shot model with an exemplar-based model, thereby enhancing learning efficiency and flexibility. The paper presents experiments across various continual learning settings and demonstrates competitive performance against baselines. However, it has been critiqued for its incremental nature, lack of comprehensive comparisons with existing methods, and potential issues with the scalability and practicality of the proposed methods.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, clear, and easy to follow, with a logical structure that effectively communicates the authors' ideas.
- The proposed method is innovative, combining a CLIP zero-shot model with an exemplar-based model, which is a novel approach to continual learning in open-vocabulary image classification.
- The paper includes a comprehensive set of experiments that demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed method across various continual learning settings, including data incremental, class incremental, and task incremental learning.
- The use of the CLIP model as a zero-shot classifier is a notable strength, as it allows for the classification of images based on textual descriptions, which can be beneficial in scenarios where labels are not readily available.
- The methodology is supported by thorough experimental evaluations and comparisons with existing methods, providing a robust validation of the proposed approach.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed comparison with existing methods, particularly in terms of computational efficiency and scalability.
- The methodology, while innovative, is seen as incremental and not sufficiently distinct from existing approaches, such as the use of CLIP for zero-shot classification and exemplar-based models.
- There are concerns about the practicality and scalability of the proposed method, particularly in terms of the storage and retrieval of exemplars, which could be a significant challenge in large-scale applications.
- The paper does not adequately address the issue of catastrophic forgetting, which is a critical aspect of continual learning.
- The paper lacks a discussion on the limitations of the proposed method, which could help in understanding its applicability and scope.
- The paper does not include a detailed discussion on the potential negative societal impacts of the proposed method, which is a critical consideration in the field of machine learning.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors provide more details on the computational efficiency and scalability of the proposed method, particularly in comparison to existing methods?
- How does the proposed method handle the issue of catastrophic forgetting, and what measures have been taken to mitigate this?
- Can the authors clarify the practical implementation of the exemplar-based model, particularly in terms of storage and retrieval of exemplars?
- How does the proposed method compare to other recent approaches in the field of continual learning, such as those mentioned in the review?
- Could the authors provide more detailed experimental results, possibly including a table with a more comprehensive set of results?
- How does the proposed method perform in scenarios where the CLIP model may not be as effective, such as in fine-grained classification tasks?
