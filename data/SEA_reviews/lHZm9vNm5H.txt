 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a new mode called Tune for ConvBN blocks, which aims to address the stability issues in deploy mode while maintaining the efficiency of the evalu mode. This is achieved by dynamically calculating transformed parameters during training, thereby reducing memory usage and improving training speed. The authors provide extensive experiments across various datasets and models, including object detection, classification, and adversarial example generation, demonstrating the effectiveness of the proposed method. Despite its theoretical grounding and practical implementation, the paper has been critiqued for its limited novelty and the need for more rigorous comparisons with existing methods.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written and easy to follow, making complex concepts accessible.
- The proposed method is simple and effective, with a clear motivation for reducing memory usage and improving training speed.
- Extensive experiments are conducted across various datasets and models, demonstrating the effectiveness of the proposed method.
- The paper provides a thorough analysis of the training stability in deploy mode, which is crucial for understanding the behavior of ConvBN blocks in different scenarios.
- The proposed Tune mode is a novel approach that addresses the trade-off between stability and efficiency in ConvBN blocks, providing a new perspective on optimizing these blocks for transfer learning and beyond.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed comparison with other methods for improving training efficiency, such as those mentioned in related works.
- The novelty of the proposed method is questioned, as it seems to be a straightforward extension of existing methods rather than a significant advancement.
- The paper could benefit from more rigorous theoretical analysis and empirical evidence to substantiate the claims made about the stability and efficiency of the proposed method.
- The experimental results presented in the paper are not convincingly superior to existing methods, and the paper lacks a detailed comparison with other methods in terms of memory usage and training speed.
- The paper's claims about the stability of Tune mode are not adequately supported by empirical evidence, and the method's effectiveness in real-world applications remains unclear.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors provide a more detailed comparison with other methods for improving training efficiency, such as those mentioned in related works?
- How does the proposed method compare in terms of memory usage and training speed with other state-of-the-art methods for optimizing ConvBN blocks?
- Can the authors provide more empirical evidence to support the claims made about the stability of Tune mode, particularly in real-world applications?
- How does the proposed method handle the train-inference mismatch, which is a known issue in deploying models on different devices?
- Can the authors clarify the discrepancies noted in the experimental results, particularly the differences in performance and memory usage between Tune mode and other modes like Deploy and Eval?
- How does the proposed method affect the training stability in different scenarios, and what measures can be taken to ensure that the stability is maintained across different devices and applications?
