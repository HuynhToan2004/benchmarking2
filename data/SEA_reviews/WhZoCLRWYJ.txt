 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel method for solving the Schrödinger Bridge (SB) problem using a lightweight, fast, and computationally efficient approach. It employs a Gaussian mixture parameterization for the Schrödinger potential and demonstrates that the proposed method can approximate any Schrödinger bridge with arbitrary accuracy. The approach is validated through experiments on both synthetic and real-world datasets, showing superior performance over existing methods. The method's simplicity and ease of implementation are highlighted, with the potential for broad applicability in various fields, including image-to-image translation.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, clear, and easy to follow, with a clear presentation of the problem and its solution.
- The proposed method is simple, elegant, and efficient, with a straightforward optimization objective and a focus on practical implementation.
- The method is validated through experiments on both synthetic and real-world datasets, showing superior performance over existing methods.
- The method is applicable in various fields, including image-to-image translation, and can be used to easily convert auto-encoders to translation models.
- The paper provides a closed-form expression for the drift of the process, which is a significant contribution to the field.
- The method is a universal approximator of Schrödinger bridges, which is a significant theoretical contribution.

**Weaknesses:**

- There is a lack of discussion on the computational complexity and scalability of the method, which could be important for understanding its practical limitations.
