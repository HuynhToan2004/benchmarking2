 **Summary:**
The paper introduces PECoRe, a framework designed to evaluate the context reliance of language models, specifically in machine translation. PECoRe identifies context-sensitive tokens and links them to contextual cues, allowing for quantitative assessments of plausibility in language generation beyond artificial settings. The framework is evaluated across various discourse phenomena in machine translation, demonstrating its ability to identify context-dependent errors and improve understanding of context usage in machine translation models. Despite its focus on machine translation, PECoRe's methodology and findings are potentially applicable to other context-dependent language generation tasks.

**Strengths:**
- The paper addresses a significant and relevant problem in the field of NLP, focusing on the plausibility of context usage in machine translation models.
- The proposed framework, PECoRe, is well-designed and well-explained, with clear methodology and evaluation results that demonstrate its effectiveness in identifying context-sensitive tokens and linking them to contextual cues.
- The paper is well-written, with a clear and concise presentation of the research, including detailed explanations of the proposed framework and its components.
- The evaluation of PECoRe across various discourse phenomena in machine translation demonstrates the framework's ability to identify context-dependent errors and improve understanding of context usage in machine translation models.
- The paper provides a valuable resource in the form of the SCAT+ dataset, which can be beneficial for further research in the field.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper's focus on machine translation might limit the perceived applicability of the proposed framework to other context-dependent language generation tasks.
- The evaluation of PECoRe is limited to machine translation models, and it is unclear how well the framework would perform with other types of language models or tasks.
- The paper lacks a comprehensive comparison with existing methods for detecting and explaining context-dependent errors in machine translation models.
- The paper does not discuss the limitations of the proposed framework, which could help in understanding its scope and applicability.
- The paper could benefit from more detailed explanations and examples of how PECoRe can be applied to other context-dependent language generation tasks, such as question answering and summarization.
