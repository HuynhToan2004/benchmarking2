 **Summary:**
The paper investigates the training dynamics of shallow linear Transformers applied to linear regression tasks, aiming to understand the optimization features and phenomena observed in full Transformers. The authors demonstrate that these simplified models can reproduce the distinctive features of Transformer training, such as heavy-tailed gradient noise, ill-conditioned landscapes, and directional smoothness. By focusing on linear Transformers, the paper provides insights into how these phenomena arise and how they might be influenced by factors like data distribution and network depth. The findings suggest that these simplified models could serve as a valuable tool for understanding Transformer optimization, although the paper's scope is limited to linear models and its practical implications for training full Transformers are not explored.

**Strengths:**
- The paper effectively summarizes and connects existing research on Transformer training dynamics, providing a comprehensive overview of the current state of understanding in the field.
- The authors introduce a novel approach by studying linear Transformers, which are simpler and more manageable than full Transformers, yet still capable of reproducing the distinctive features of Transformer training.
- The paper is well-written, with clear explanations and a logical flow that makes it easy to follow and understand.
- The experiments conducted are thorough and well-documented, demonstrating the reproducibility of Transformer training phenomena in simpler settings.
- The paper's findings could potentially lead to new insights and discoveries, as they provide a new perspective on Transformer training dynamics.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper's contribution is somewhat limited as it primarily demonstrates the existence of various Transformer training phenomena in linear Transformers, without providing significant new insights or explanations.
- The scope of the study is narrow, focusing only on linear Transformers and not exploring the implications for training full Transformers.
- The paper lacks a clear explanation for why the observed phenomena occur in linear Transformers, and the practical implications of these findings for training full Transformers are not explored.
- The paper could benefit from more detailed comparisons with existing works, such as those by Ahn et al. and von Oswald et al., to clarify the differences and similarities between their approaches and those presented in this paper.
- The paper's focus on linear Transformers might limit its relevance to the broader community, as these models are not commonly used in practice.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors provide a clearer explanation for why the observed phenomena occur in linear Transformers?
- How do the findings from this study translate to training full Transformers, and what are the practical implications of these findings for optimizing Transformer training?
- Could the authors clarify the differences and similarities between their approach and those of other recent works, such as those by Ahn et al. and von Oswald et al.?
- How does the data distribution affect the optimization features and phenomena in linear Transformers, and what insights can be gained from this?
- Can the authors elaborate on the practical implications of their findings for training full Transformers, and how these insights might be applied to improve training efficiency or accuracy?
