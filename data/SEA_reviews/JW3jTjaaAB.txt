 **Summary:**
The paper introduces AirPhyNet, a physics-guided neural network designed for air quality prediction, integrating physics principles such as diffusion and advection through differential equation networks. AirPhyNet leverages a graph structure to incorporate physical knowledge into its neural network architecture, aiming to improve prediction accuracy and reduce errors. The model's performance is validated on two real-world datasets, demonstrating its effectiveness in capturing spatio-temporal relationships and outperforming state-of-the-art models in various testing scenarios. Despite its strengths, the paper has been critiqued for its limited novelty, lack of detailed implementation and experimental results, and potential over-reliance on physical knowledge which may limit its applicability in real-world scenarios.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel approach, AirPhyNet, that integrates physics principles into neural network architectures, which is a significant advancement in air quality prediction.
- The methodology is well-explained, with a clear description of the physics-guided deep learning framework and its application to air quality prediction.
- The paper is well-written, making it accessible and easy to follow, with a clear presentation of the methodology and results.
- Extensive experiments are conducted on real-world datasets, demonstrating the effectiveness of the proposed method in capturing spatio-temporal relationships and outperforming state-of-the-art models.
- The paper includes an ablation study that shows the effectiveness of incorporating physical knowledge into the model.

**Weaknesses:**
- The novelty of the approach is limited as it mainly combines existing techniques such as differential equation networks and graph neural networks without significant innovation.
- The paper lacks detailed implementation and experimental results, which makes it difficult to fully understand the methodology and replicate the results.
- The applicability of the method is limited by its reliance on physical knowledge, which may not be sufficient for real-world scenarios where data is incomplete or inaccurate.
- The paper does not discuss the limitations of the proposed method, which is crucial for understanding its scope and applicability.
- There are concerns about the scalability of the method, particularly in handling large and complex datasets, and the computational efficiency of the model.
- The paper could benefit from a more thorough literature review and comparison with other relevant models to better position its contributions within the existing body of research.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors clarify the specific form of the diffusion equation used in their model and its impact on the model's performance?
- How does the model handle missing data, and what strategies are used to ensure the model's robustness in such scenarios?
- What are the limitations of the proposed method, and how do these impact its practical application in real-world scenarios?
- How does the model handle the scalability issues, particularly in terms of computational efficiency and handling large datasets?
- Could the authors provide more detailed implementation and experimental results, including the hyperparameters used and the computational resources required?
- How does the model compare with other relevant models in terms of performance and efficiency, and what are the specific advantages of the proposed method over these models?
