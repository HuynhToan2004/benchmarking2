 **Summary:**
The paper addresses the composed image retrieval problem, where the query image is associated with a textual description, and the goal is to retrieve images that match the description. The authors propose a method that introduces uncertainty into the retrieval process to model the coarse-grained matching, which is typically more challenging than fine-grained matching. This is achieved by adding noise to the features of the target image and adjusting the weights of the loss function to balance the importance of coarse and fine-grained matching. The method is evaluated on multiple datasets, showing some improvements over existing methods. However, the paper has been critiqued for its lack of clarity in presentation and the insufficient novelty of its approach.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, with clear explanations of the proposed method and its contributions to the field.
- The authors address a significant problem in the field of image retrieval, specifically the challenge of coarse-grained matching, which is often overlooked.
- The proposed method, which involves adding noise to the features of the target image and adjusting the weights of the loss function, is straightforward and effective in improving retrieval performance.
- The paper includes extensive experiments and ablation studies that demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed method across multiple datasets.
- The method's simplicity and ease of implementation make it accessible and practical for real-world applications.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a clear motivation for why coarse-grained matching is more challenging than fine-grained matching, which is a crucial aspect of the proposed method.
- The paper does not sufficiently differentiate its approach from existing methods, particularly in terms of its novelty and technical contributions.
- The presentation of the paper could be improved to enhance readability and clarity, especially in figures and tables.
- The method's reliance on a specific dataset (FashionIQ) for evaluation raises concerns about its generalizability to other datasets or real-world scenarios.
- There is a lack of detailed discussion on the impact of noise on retrieval performance and the choice of noise parameters, which could affect the reliability and reproducibility of the results.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors clarify the specific challenges and differences between coarse- and fine-grained matching, and how their proposed method addresses these challenges?
- How does the proposed method compare to existing methods in terms of technical novelty and performance on different datasets?
- Could the authors provide more details on the experimental setup, such as the selection criteria for the noise parameters and the impact of different noise levels on retrieval performance?
- How does the proposed method handle the issue of ambiguity in textual descriptions, and what measures are taken to ensure the effectiveness of the method in real-world applications?
- Given the concerns about the clarity and presentation of the paper, could the authors consider revising the manuscript to improve readability and address the reviewers' suggestions?
