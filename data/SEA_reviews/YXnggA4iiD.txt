 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel active learning (AL) framework that integrates Gaussian Mixture Models (GMMs) to address the distributional discrepancy between labeled and unlabeled datasets, which is a significant challenge in AL. The framework comprises several components: a regularizer that supervises GMM posteriors, a semi-supervised learning method that aligns latent distributions, and a hybrid informativeness metric that leverages GMM likelihoods. The methodology is evaluated on various datasets, showing improvements over existing methods. However, concerns are raised about the novelty of the approach, the clarity of the experimental results, and the potential for overfitting due to the use of GMM parameters.

**Strengths:**
- The paper addresses a significant problem in active learning (AL) by addressing the distributional discrepancy between labeled and unlabeled datasets, which is crucial for effective AL.
- The proposed method integrates Gaussian Mixture Models (GMMs) into the AL framework, which is novel and potentially impactful.
- The paper is well-written, making it easy to follow, and the proposed method is well-motivated and well-explained.
- The experimental results show improvements over existing methods, indicating the effectiveness of the proposed approach.
- The paper includes a good amount of visualizations that help in understanding the methodology and the results.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed discussion on the novelty of the proposed method, particularly in relation to existing works such as the WAAL paper.
- The methodology section is overly complex, with too many components, which might make it difficult for readers to grasp the main contributions.
- The paper does not adequately address the issue of overfitting, especially in the context of the use of GMM parameters.
- There are concerns about the computational complexity and scalability of the proposed method, particularly in large-scale datasets.
- The experimental results are not sufficiently clear, with issues such as overlapping lines in figures and unclear performance metrics.
- The paper does not adequately compare the proposed method with state-of-the-art methods, which could undermine the perceived effectiveness of the approach.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors clarify how the proposed method addresses the novelty concerns, particularly in relation to existing works like WAAL?
- How does the proposed method handle the computational complexity and scalability issues, especially in large-scale datasets?
- Can the authors provide more details on the choice of parameters, such as the number of Gaussian modality in GMM, and how these choices affect the performance of the model?
- How does the proposed method compare with other state-of-the-art methods in terms of computational efficiency, scalability, and performance?
- Can the authors provide more detailed explanations or examples to illustrate the concepts of "overfitting" and "distributional discrepancy" as used in the paper?
- How does the paper ensure that the use of GMM parameters does not lead to overfitting, especially in the early stages of the learning process?
