 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel approach to detecting and mitigating adversarial attacks on machine learning models using a two-stage adversarial training method. The first stage involves training a model on adversarial examples to enhance its robustness, followed by a second stage where the model is trained on clean data to restore its accuracy. The methodology is tested across various datasets and models, demonstrating the effectiveness of the proposed method. However, the paper suffers from significant presentation issues, including poor writing, unclear figures, and a lack of clarity in the methodology, which hampers understanding and evaluation.

**Strengths:**
- The paper addresses a significant and relevant problem in the field of machine learning, focusing on adversarial robustness.
- The proposed two-stage adversarial training method is novel and presents a potentially effective approach to enhancing the robustness of machine learning models against adversarial attacks.
- The paper is well-motivated and the experimental results are solid, showing the effectiveness of the proposed method.
- The paper is the first to propose a two-stage adversarial training method that enhances the robustness of machine learning models against adversarial attacks.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper suffers from poor writing and presentation, making it difficult to follow and understand the methodology and results.
- The figures and tables in the paper are not clearly labeled or explained, which hampers the ability to interpret the data and results.
- The paper lacks a clear definition and explanation of the adversarial attack used in the experiments, which is crucial for understanding the robustness of the proposed method.
- The paper does not sufficiently compare its method with existing approaches, which limits the evaluation of its effectiveness and novelty.
- There is a lack of clarity in the methodology and the presentation of results, making it difficult to replicate the experiments and understand the contributions of the paper.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors clarify the definition and characteristics of the adversarial attack used in the experiments?
- How does the proposed method compare with other existing methods in terms of robustness and efficiency?
- Could the authors provide more detailed explanations for the figures and tables in the paper to enhance their clarity and interpretability?
- How does the proposed method perform under different types and intensities of adversarial attacks?
- Can the authors provide a clearer explanation of the two-stage adversarial training process and its contributions to enhancing the robustness of machine learning models?
