 **Summary:**
The paper introduces InfoMLP, a novel method for node classification in graphs using MLPs, which maximizes the mutual information between node embeddings and graph structure. The authors propose a regularization method that encourages MLPs to learn node representations that capture graph structure information, thus potentially improving performance. The method is evaluated on several benchmark datasets and demonstrates competitive performance compared to GNNs and other MLP-based methods. The paper also includes theoretical analysis and empirical evidence to support the effectiveness of the proposed method. However, concerns were raised about the novelty and the direct comparison with other MLP-based methods, particularly in terms of computational efficiency and the generalization to heterophilic graphs.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel method for node classification using MLPs, which maximizes the mutual information between node embeddings and the graph structure, potentially improving performance.
- The paper is well-written, clear, and easy to follow, with a clear presentation of the proposed method and its motivation.
- The theoretical analysis and empirical evidence provided support the effectiveness of the proposed method.
- The proposed method is computationally efficient and does not require message-passing, which is a significant advantage over some other methods.
- The paper includes a comprehensive literature review and effectively positions the proposed method within the existing body of work.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper does not adequately compare the proposed method with other MLP-based methods, particularly in terms of computational efficiency and performance on heterophilic graphs.
- The theoretical analysis and empirical evidence provided are not sufficiently novel or insightful, as the main ideas and results are largely based on existing works.
- The paper lacks a thorough discussion on the limitations and potential negative societal impacts of the proposed method.
- The experimental results are not convincing, particularly in the inductive settings where the method performs worse than GNNs.
- The paper does not adequately address the scalability of the method to larger, more complex graphs, and does not compare the method with state-of-the-art GNNs.
- The paper contains several typographical errors and minor inconsistencies that need to be corrected.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors clarify the computational efficiency of the proposed method compared to other MLP-based methods, particularly in terms of training and testing times?
- How does the proposed method perform on heterophilic graphs, and can the authors provide evidence or theoretical insights into why it might not be effective in these settings?
- Can the authors provide more detailed comparisons with other MLP-based methods, particularly in terms of performance and computational efficiency?
- How does the proposed method handle the scalability issues on larger, more complex graphs, and what are the potential limitations in terms of performance and computational resources?
- Could the authors provide a more detailed discussion on the limitations and potential negative societal impacts of the proposed method?
