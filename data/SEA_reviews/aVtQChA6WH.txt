 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel method for off-policy evaluation in distributional reinforcement learning (DRL) named Energy Bellman Residual Minimizer (EBRM). This method aims to estimate the return distribution by minimizing the Bellman residual using an expectation-extended statistical distance, the energy distance. The paper provides theoretical analysis and finite-sample error bounds for the EBRM estimator under both realizable and non-realizable settings. It also introduces a multi-step extension to enhance the method's applicability in non-realizable scenarios. The paper is supported by empirical results showing the method's superiority over existing methods in certain scenarios. However, the paper's assumptions and the generalizability of the results to more complex environments are questioned.
