 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel interaction-centric hypersphere reasoning model for multi-person video Human-Object Interaction (HOI) recognition. The model incorporates a context fuser and an interaction state reasoner to learn context-rich and reasoning-aware entity representations, leveraging a hypersphere representation to visualize the structure bias of HOI. The method is evaluated on various datasets, including MPHOI-72, Bimanual Actions, and CAD-120, showing significant improvements over existing methods. Despite its innovative approach, the paper faces criticisms regarding its novelty and clarity in methodological descriptions.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel interaction-centric hypersphere reasoning model for multi-person video HOI recognition, which is a significant advancement in the field.
- The model effectively utilizes a context fuser and an interaction state reasoner to learn context-rich and reasoning-aware entity representations, enhancing the understanding of HOI interactions.
- The paper is well-written, making it easy to follow and understand the methodology and the results.
- The proposed method achieves state-of-the-art (SOTA) performance on multiple datasets, demonstrating its effectiveness.
- The use of hypersphere representation is innovative and provides a new perspective on modeling HOI interactions.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a clear explanation of the method's novelty, particularly in how it differs from existing methods such as 2G-GCN.
- The interaction-centric hypersphere representation and the interaction state reasoner are not adequately explained, leaving some ambiguity about their contributions to the model's performance.
- The paper does not provide sufficient visualization results, such as the interaction-centric hypersphere and the learned interaction representations, which are crucial for understanding the model's effectiveness.
- The experimental setup and results are not sufficiently detailed, making it difficult to assess the robustness and generalizability of the proposed method.
- The paper does not adequately address the limitations of the proposed method, which is essential for understanding its applicability and potential drawbacks.
- The paper does not provide the code, which limits the reproducibility of the results.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors clarify the novelty of their method compared to existing methods like 2G-GCN?
- How does the interaction-centric hypersphere representation differ from traditional methods, and what advantages does it offer?
- Can the authors provide more detailed explanations of the interaction state reasoner and its role in the model's performance?
- How does the model handle the influence of multiple persons on each other, especially in scenarios where interactions are complex?
- Can the authors provide more visualization results, such as the interaction-centric hypersphere and the learned interaction representations, to better illustrate the model's effectiveness?
- How does the model perform when the number of humans or objects in the scene varies?
- Could the authors discuss the potential limitations of their method and suggest areas for future research?
