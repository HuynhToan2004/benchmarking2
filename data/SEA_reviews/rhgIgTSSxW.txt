 **Summary:**
The paper introduces TabR, a novel retrieval-augmented deep learning model for tabular data, which leverages a feed-forward network with a k-Nearest-Neighbors-like component in the middle. This architecture utilizes a simple attention-like mechanism to retrieve the nearest neighbors and extract valuable signals from them, demonstrating superior performance over various baselines, including GBDT, on several public benchmarks. The authors have conducted extensive experiments to validate the model's effectiveness and efficiency, showing that it outperforms existing retrieval-based models and comes close to matching the performance of GBDT while being simpler and more efficient.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, clear, and easy to follow, making it accessible to a broad audience.
- The proposed model, TabR, is innovative, combining a feed-forward network with a k-Nearest-Neighbors-like component, which effectively enhances the performance of tabular deep learning models.
- Extensive experiments have been conducted, demonstrating the superiority of TabR over various baselines, including GBDT, on several public benchmarks.
- The paper provides a comprehensive analysis of the model's performance, including a detailed discussion on the limitations of retrieval-augmented models.
- The simplicity and efficiency of TabR are notable, making it a practical choice for many applications.
- The paper includes a detailed analysis of the model's components, such as the attention-like mechanism, which is crucial for understanding the model's effectiveness.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed discussion on the limitations of the proposed model, which could help in understanding its applicability and scope.
- The experiments are limited to specific datasets, and it would be beneficial to see how TabR performs on other datasets, especially those with larger scales.
- The novelty of the proposed model is questioned, as it seems to be a combination of existing techniques without significant innovation.
- There is a lack of clarity in some sections of the paper, particularly in the description of the retrieval module and the experimental setup.
- The paper does not provide a detailed comparison of the computational complexity of TabR with other models, which could be crucial for understanding its practical applicability.
- The paper could benefit from a more comprehensive discussion on the limitations of retrieval-augmented models and how TabR addresses these challenges.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors provide a more detailed discussion on the limitations of the proposed model and how these limitations affect its applicability and performance?
- How does TabR perform on other datasets, especially those with larger scales?
- Could the authors clarify the computational complexity of TabR compared to other models and discuss its practical implications?
- Is there a specific reason why the retrieval module was designed in a certain way, and how does this affect the model's performance and efficiency?
- How does the model handle the potential issues of overfitting, especially when the training dataset is large?
- Could the authors provide a more detailed comparison of TabR with other models, including a discussion on the model's efficiency and effectiveness in different scenarios?
