 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a method for identifying vulnerable regions in deep neural networks (DNNs) by employing adversarial perturbations at diverse locations, focusing on a one-pixel paradigm. This approach allows for detailed pixel-level vulnerability assessments, which is crucial for understanding the behavior of DNNs under adversarial conditions. The method uses a robust Sharing Differential Evolution Algorithm to identify multiple one-pixel perturbations, forming a vulnerable region. The paper includes extensive experiments across various network architectures and adversarial training techniques, demonstrating the effectiveness of the approach in identifying vulnerable regions and providing valuable insights into the inherent vulnerabilities of DNNs. However, the paper lacks a thorough comparison with existing methods, which limits the evaluation of its effectiveness and efficiency.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, making it easy to follow and understand.
- The proposed method is novel, focusing on identifying vulnerable regions through adversarial perturbations at diverse locations, which is a significant contribution to the field.
- The method is evaluated across various network architectures and adversarial training techniques, demonstrating its effectiveness in identifying vulnerable regions and providing valuable insights into the inherent vulnerabilities of DNNs.
- The paper includes extensive experiments, which are well-documented, and the results are clearly presented, making it easy for readers to understand the findings.
- The proposed algorithm is simple and easy to implement, which is beneficial for practical applications.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a thorough comparison with existing methods, particularly in terms of effectiveness and efficiency.
- There is a lack of discussion on the limitations of the proposed method, which is crucial for understanding its applicability and potential drawbacks.
- The paper does not adequately discuss the computational complexity of the method, which is a significant concern for practical applications.
- The paper does not provide a clear explanation of how the proposed method can be applied to other types of DNNs, such as transformer-based models, which limits its generalizability.
- The paper does not include a comprehensive evaluation of the method's performance on more complex datasets like ImageNet, which could provide a more robust validation of the proposed method.
- There is a lack of discussion on the transferability of the proposed method, which is a critical aspect for assessing its practical utility in real-world scenarios.

**Questions:**

- How does the proposed method perform on more complex datasets like ImageNet?

- Can the authors provide a more detailed discussion on the computational complexity of the method and how it scales with the size of the DNN?

- Can the authors provide more details on the transferability of the proposed method and how it can be applied in real-world scenarios?
