 **Summary:**
The paper investigates the effectiveness of retrieval-augmented language models (LLMs) compared to LLMs with extended context windows, focusing on the 43B GPT and 70B Llama2 models. It explores how retrieval-augmented LLMs perform on various long-context tasks, showing that they can outperform baseline models with larger context windows. The study also highlights the importance of retrieval in enhancing the performance of LLMs, especially in scenarios where the context window is not large enough. The paper provides a comprehensive evaluation across multiple datasets and tasks, demonstrating the benefits of retrieval-augmented LLMs in handling long contexts.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, clear, and easy to follow, making it accessible to a broad audience.
- The study is comprehensive, covering a wide range of tasks and datasets, providing a thorough evaluation of retrieval-augmented LLMs.
- The findings are robust and supported by extensive experiments, demonstrating the effectiveness of retrieval-augmented LLMs in handling long contexts.
- The paper presents an interesting observation about the "lost in the middle" phenomenon and provides a detailed analysis of retrieval-augmented LLMs.
- The experimental setup is well-designed, with a focus on the impact of retrieval on LLMs, and the results are convincingly presented.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a clear comparison with other retrieval-augmented LLMs, which could have provided a more robust evaluation of the proposed methods.
- There is a notable absence of discussion on the limitations and potential negative societal impacts of the proposed methods.
- The paper does not sufficiently discuss the technical details of the retrieval process, such as the number of retrieved chunks and the impact of different retrievers on performance.
- The results presented are somewhat limited in scope, focusing primarily on specific tasks and not generalizing well to other types of tasks.
- The paper could benefit from more detailed comparisons with other state-of-the-art models and a more comprehensive evaluation of the retrieval-augmented LLMs.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors provide a more detailed comparison with other retrieval-augmented LLMs to better contextualize the findings?
- How does the number of retrieved chunks impact the performance of the LLMs, and what are the implications for the "lost in the middle" phenomenon?
- Can the authors elaborate on the choice of the Dragon retriever and its impact on the results, especially in comparison to other retrievers?
- How do the findings of this study generalize to other types of tasks, and what are the potential implications for the broader application of retrieval-augmented LLMs?
- Could the authors provide more detailed metrics or analysis on the inference speed of the retrieval-augmented LLMs compared to the baseline models?
