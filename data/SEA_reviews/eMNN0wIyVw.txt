 **Summary:**
The paper introduces OAT, a method for augmenting offline trajectory data to enhance the performance of off-policy evaluation (OPE) algorithms. OAT utilizes a three-step process: first, it identifies potential sub-trajectories (PSTs) from the original trajectories based on their frequency and support in the dataset. Then, these PSTs are augmented using a Variational Autoencoder (VAE) trained on the PSTs to generate new trajectories. Finally, these augmented trajectories are fused with the original trajectories to form a new dataset. The paper presents extensive experiments across various domains, including robotics, healthcare, and e-learning, demonstrating the effectiveness of OAT in improving OPE performance.

**Strengths:**
- The paper addresses an important and relevant problem in the field of off-policy evaluation, focusing on the use of augmented trajectories to enhance the accuracy of OPE.
- The proposed method is well-motivated and supported by extensive experiments, which demonstrate its effectiveness across various domains.
- The paper is well-written and easy to follow, with clear explanations of the methodology and results.
- The use of potential sub-trajectories (PSTs) and their augmentation through a VAE model is a novel approach that adds value to the field of OPE.
- The paper provides a comprehensive evaluation of the proposed method, comparing it against various baselines and demonstrating its effectiveness through extensive experiments.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed comparison with existing data augmentation methods specifically designed for OPE, which could help in understanding the unique contributions of the proposed method.
- The methodology is complex and not well-explained, particularly the details of the VAE model and the augmentation process, which could confuse readers unfamiliar with the domain.
- There is a lack of clarity in the experimental setup, including the number of trajectories used and the specifics of the VAE model, which could affect the reproducibility and understanding of the results.
- The paper does not adequately address the issue of domain shift in OPE, which could limit the generalizability of the findings.
- There are several typographical and formatting errors that need correction to enhance the professional quality of the paper.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors clarify the role and impact of the VAE model in the augmentation process, specifically how it contributes to the generation of new trajectories?
- How does the proposed method handle the issue of domain shift in OPE, and what measures have been taken to ensure the generalizability of the findings?
- Could the authors provide more details on the experimental setup, including the number of trajectories used and the specifics of the VAE model, to enhance the reproducibility and transparency of the results?
- How does the proposed method compare with existing data augmentation methods specifically designed for OPE, and what are the unique advantages of the proposed method?
- Could the authors provide a more detailed explanation of the PST mining process and the selection of potential sub-trajectories, especially in terms of the threshold used and the criteria for selection?
