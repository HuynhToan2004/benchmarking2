 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel data-free fine-tuning framework, EfficientDM, for low-bit diffusion models. It leverages a quantization-aware variant of the low-rank adapter, QALoRA, to jointly quantize model weights and LoRA weights, thereby eliminating the need for training data. The framework also incorporates scale-aware LoRA optimization and temporal learned step-size quantization to address variations in weight quantization scales and activation distributions across denoising steps. Extensive experiments demonstrate that EfficientDM outperforms previous PTQ-based diffusion models while maintaining similar time and data efficiency. It also achieves QAT-level performance with PTQ-like efficiency, making it a significant advancement in the field.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a data-free, quantization-aware and parameter-efficient fine-tuning framework for low-bit diffusion models, which can achieve QAT-level performance with PTQ-like efficiency.
- The framework leverages a quantization-aware variant of the low-rank adapter, QALoRA, to jointly quantize model weights and LoRA weights, eliminating the need for training data.
- Scale-aware LoRA optimization is proposed to address ineffective learning of QALoRA due to variations in weight quantization scales across different layers.
- Temporal learned step-size quantization is introduced to handle notable variations in activation distributions across denoising steps.
- Extensive experiments demonstrate that the proposed EfficientDM significantly outperforms previous PTQ-based diffusion models while maintaining similar time and data efficiency.
- The paper is well-written, easy to follow, and provides a comprehensive experimental setup, including ablation studies and comparisons with state-of-the-art methods.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks clarity in some figures and sections, particularly in Section 3.2, where the explanation of the "data-free fine-tuning for diffusion models" is not sufficiently detailed.
- The paper does not sufficiently discuss the limitations of the proposed method, such as the potential impact of quantization errors on the model's performance and the scalability of the method to larger datasets.
- The paper does not provide sufficient comparisons with other quantization methods, particularly those that use different quantization techniques or different training datasets.
- The paper does not include experiments on larger datasets or with more recent diffusion models, which could enhance the generalizability and applicability of the proposed method.
- There are some inconsistencies and potential errors in the mathematical notations and equations used in the paper, which could affect the understanding and reproducibility of the results.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors provide more details on how the "data-free fine-tuning for diffusion models" works, particularly in terms of the inputs and outputs of the MSE loss function?
- How does the proposed method compare to other quantization methods, particularly in terms of performance and computational efficiency?
- Could the authors provide more details on the computational complexity and memory requirements of the proposed method, especially in comparison to other methods?
- What are the specific challenges associated with extending the method to larger datasets or more complex models?
- Can the authors clarify the mathematical notations and equations used in the paper to ensure consistency and accuracy?
