 **Summary:**
The paper introduces SpaFL, a federated learning framework that optimizes both personalized model parameters and sparse model structures with low computational overhead. SpaFL employs trainable thresholds for pruning parameters and communicates these thresholds between clients and the server, reducing communication costs. The methodology includes joint optimization of model parameters and thresholds, which are used to update model parameters based on parameter importance. Theoretical analysis and experimental results demonstrate that SpaFL achieves good accuracy while requiring less communication and computing resources compared to other baselines. However, the paper has been critiqued for its incremental contribution, lack of comprehensive theoretical analysis, and inadequate comparison with other state-of-the-art methods.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel approach to pruning by using trainable thresholds, which is a new idea in the field of federated learning.
- The proposed method significantly reduces communication overhead and computational resources, making it suitable for resource-constrained devices and systems.
- The paper provides a theoretical analysis of the convergence of thresholds, which is a significant contribution to the field.
- The experimental results demonstrate that SpaFL achieves good accuracy while requiring less communication and computing resources compared to other baselines.
- The paper is well-written, making it easy to follow, and includes a detailed description of the SpaFL algorithm.
- The convergence analysis of the proposed algorithm provides a theoretical guarantee for its effectiveness.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a comprehensive comparison with other state-of-the-art methods, which limits the understanding of its advantages over existing approaches.
- The contribution of the paper is incremental as it is a combination of existing techniques without significant innovation.
- The paper does not provide a detailed analysis of the training time of the proposed method, which is crucial for understanding its practical applicability.
- The paper lacks a thorough theoretical analysis of the proposed method, particularly in terms of how it handles personalized sparse model structures and the impact of these structures on convergence.
- The paper does not adequately address the potential limitations or drawbacks of the proposed method, which could impact its practical implementation.
- The paper's experimental setup and results are not sufficiently robust, with a limited number of datasets and models used, which could affect the generalizability of the findings.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors provide a more detailed comparison with other state-of-the-art methods, including the latest developments in the field?
- How does the proposed method compare in terms of computational efficiency and communication costs with other methods that also use sparse models in federated learning?
- What are the specific advantages of using trainable thresholds over other methods for pruning, such as binary masks?
- How does the proposed method handle the challenge of personalized sparse model structures, and what impact do these structures have on the convergence of the algorithm?
- Could the authors provide a more detailed analysis of the training time and the impact of different hyperparameters on the performance of the proposed method?
- How does the proposed method compare in terms of accuracy and resource usage with other methods that achieve similar levels of sparsity?
