 **Summary:**
The paper investigates ad hoc teamwork in multi-agent systems through the lens of cooperative game theory, focusing on the open stochastic Bayesian coalitional affinity game (OSB-CAG). It introduces a novel theoretical model and a practical algorithm called coalitional affinity GPL (CA-GPL), which aims to enhance teamwork performance by maximizing the social welfare of the team. The authors propose a star graph structure for the affinity graph, where the learner is the central node and teammates are leaves, to model the influence of the learner on teammates. The paper also provides a theoretical analysis and empirical validation of the proposed model, comparing it against existing methods like GPL and demonstrating its effectiveness through experiments.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, with clear and concise presentation, and a comprehensive literature review, making it accessible to readers.
- The theoretical analysis is robust, providing a solid foundation for the proposed methodology.
- The paper introduces a novel approach by formulating ad hoc teamwork as a coalitional affinity game, which is both innovative and relevant to the field.
- The experimental results demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed method, with a comprehensive comparison against existing methods.
- The authors have provided a detailed theoretical framework, including theorems and proofs, which enhance the credibility of the proposed model.
- The paper is well-organized, with clear explanations of the problem formulation and the proposed solutions, which aids in understanding the complex concepts presented.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed discussion on the limitations of the proposed method, particularly in scenarios where the assumptions made might not hold true.
- The motivation behind the choice of the star graph structure in the affinity graph is not adequately explained, which could lead to confusion about its applicability in different scenarios.
- The paper does not sufficiently discuss the computational complexity of the proposed method, which is crucial for understanding its practical applicability.
- The paper does not adequately address the issue of teammates' policies, which are crucial in real-world applications, and the impact of teammates' policies on the learner's performance is not thoroughly analyzed.
- There is a lack of clarity in the presentation of some sections, particularly in the experimental setup and the results, which could hinder the reader's understanding.
- The paper could benefit from a more thorough discussion on the assumptions made in the theoretical analysis and their implications in practical scenarios.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors clarify the assumptions made in the theoretical analysis and discuss their implications in practical scenarios?
- How does the proposed method perform when the team size is not fixed, and teammates join or leave the team during the learning process?
- Could the authors provide a more detailed explanation of the star graph structure in the affinity graph and its implications for the learner's influence on teammates?
- How does the proposed method handle scenarios where the learner's influence on teammates is not significant, and what are the implications for the team's performance?
- Can the authors discuss the computational complexity of the proposed method and its practical implications for real-world applications?
- How does the proposed method compare to other cooperative game theory-based methods for ad hoc teamwork, and what are the advantages of the proposed approach over these methods?
