 **Summary:**
The paper addresses the challenge of detecting AI-generated text, particularly in short texts, by proposing a Multiscale Positive-Unlabeled (MPU) training framework. This framework rephrases AI text detection as a partial Positive-Unlabeled (PU) problem, utilizing a length-sensitive Multiscale PU Loss and a Text Multiscaling module to enhance detection performance. The methodology aims to improve short-text detection while maintaining performance on long texts, using a recurrent model to estimate positive priors and a multiscaling module to augment training corpora. Experiments are conducted on datasets like TweepFake and HC3, demonstrating the effectiveness of the proposed approach. However, the paper has been critiqued for its clarity, the complexity of its methodology, and the lack of comprehensive comparisons with state-of-the-art methods.

**Strengths:**
- The paper addresses an important and timely problem of detecting AI-generated text, particularly focusing on short texts which are often overlooked in existing methods.
- The proposed MPU framework effectively models the task of AI text detection as a partial PU problem, which is a novel approach in the field.
- The paper is well-organized and easy to follow, with clear explanations of the proposed methods and their applications.
- The experiments conducted on datasets like TweepFake and HC3 demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed MPU method in improving short-text detection performance.
- The paper introduces a novel text multiscaling module to augment training corpora, which enhances the diversity and complexity of training data.
- The use of a recurrent model to estimate positive priors based on corpus length is an innovative approach that addresses the length-variant nature of text data.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed comparison with state-of-the-art methods in the field, which could undermine the perceived effectiveness of the proposed MPU framework.
- The methodology is complex and not well-explained, particularly the rationale behind using a recurrent model to estimate positive priors and the design of the text multiscaling module.
- The paper suffers from clarity issues, with some sections being difficult to understand without additional explanations or examples.
- The experimental results are not convincingly superior to existing methods, and the paper does not sufficiently demonstrate the superiority of the proposed method over simpler or more straightforward approaches.
- The paper does not adequately address the ethical implications of AI-generated text detection, particularly in terms of potential misuse or false positives.
- The paper's presentation could be improved, with more detailed explanations and examples needed to clarify the methodology and its applications.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors clarify the rationale behind using a recurrent model to estimate positive priors based on corpus length? How does this approach compare to simpler methods?
- How does the text multiscaling module affect the performance of the MPU framework, particularly in terms of the diversity and complexity of training data?
- Could the authors provide more detailed comparisons with state-of-the-art methods to better establish the effectiveness of the proposed MPU framework?
- How does the MPU framework handle the ethical implications of AI-generated text detection, particularly in terms of potential misuse or false positives?
- Can the authors provide more examples and detailed explanations to clarify the methodology and its applications in practical scenarios?
