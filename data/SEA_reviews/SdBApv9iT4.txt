 **Summary:**
The paper explores the sample complexity of linear Markov Decision Processes (MDPs) by proposing a new algorithm that achieves a horizon-free regret bound, which is independent of the planning horizon. This algorithm is designed to handle the inhomogeneity of value functions across different time steps, a challenge in previous works that required the estimation of transition models. The paper employs techniques such as maintaining multiple weighted least square estimators for value functions and a structural lemma to bound the variation of inhomogeneous value functions. However, the paper is criticized for its inefficiency in computation, lack of clear motivation, and insufficient justification for certain assumptions. The paper also lacks empirical results, which are crucial for validating the theoretical claims.

**Strengths:**
- The paper addresses a significant and challenging problem in the field of reinforcement learning by proposing a horizon-free regret bound for linear MDPs, which is a novel approach.
- The paper is well-written, clear, and easy to follow, making it accessible to a broad audience.
- The main technical idea of the paper is novel and interesting, providing a new perspective on the problem of linear MDPs.
- The paper provides a detailed technical discussion and analysis, which are crucial for understanding the complexities involved in the problem.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks empirical results, which are essential for validating the theoretical claims and demonstrating the practical applicability of the proposed algorithm.
- The motivation for the study is not clear, and the paper does not sufficiently justify the necessity or the advantages of achieving a horizon-free regret bound in linear MDPs.
- The paper relies heavily on existing techniques and does not sufficiently differentiate its contributions from prior works, such as those by Zhou and Gu (2022).
- The algorithm proposed is computationally inefficient, which may limit its practical applicability.
- The paper contains some inconsistencies and unclear definitions, such as the term "time-homogeneous" and the use of certain mathematical notations and definitions.
- The assumptions made in the paper, such as the bounded total rewards and the linear MDP assumptions, are not sufficiently justified or explained.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors clarify the main technical challenge mentioned in Section 3.1 and explain how it is addressed by the proposed algorithm?
- How does the paper differentiate its contributions from those of Zhou and Gu (2022), particularly in terms of the novelty and effectiveness of the proposed techniques?
- Could the authors provide a more detailed explanation of the assumptions made in the paper, especially the assumptions regarding the bounded total rewards and the linear MDP?
- What is the impact of the assumptions on the results, and how do the authors justify these assumptions in the context of linear MDPs?
- How does the proposed algorithm compare to other existing algorithms in terms of computational efficiency and practical applicability?
- Can the authors provide a more detailed comparison of their algorithm with those of Zhou and Gu (2022) to highlight the key differences and contributions?
- Is there a possibility to include empirical results to validate the theoretical claims and demonstrate the practical applicability of the proposed algorithm?
