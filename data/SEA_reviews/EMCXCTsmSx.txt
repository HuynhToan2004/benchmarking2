 **Summary:**
The paper introduces IRGen, a novel image retrieval system that frames the task as a variant of generative modeling, using a sequence-to-sequence model to predict discrete visual tokens that correspond to image identifiers. This approach allows for end-to-end differentiable search, improving precision scores and potentially eliminating the need for reranking stages. The methodology involves a semantic image tokenizer that reduces sequence length, enhancing inference speed. Extensive experiments across various datasets demonstrate significant performance improvements over existing methods. However, concerns are raised about the method's novelty, scalability, and practicality, particularly in terms of computational resources and the lack of detailed comparisons with state-of-the-art methods.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, making it easy to follow and understand the proposed methodology.
- The idea of using generative models for image retrieval is novel and interesting, potentially reducing the need for reranking stages.
- The method achieves state-of-the-art (SOTA) results on multiple datasets, demonstrating its effectiveness.
- The proposed semantic image tokenizer is a significant contribution, as it can be used in various retrieval tasks, potentially improving performance.
- The paper provides a thorough comparison with a range of baselines and datasets, including large-scale datasets, which strengthens the validation of the proposed method.
- The paper is well-motivated, addressing the limitations of existing methods and proposing a novel approach that integrates generative modeling with image retrieval.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks detailed explanations for some of the methodological choices, such as the use of a sequence-to-sequence model and the semantic image tokenizer.
- There is a significant computational cost associated with the method, which might not be practical for large-scale or real-time applications.
- The novelty of the method is questionable, as it appears to be a simple application of existing generative models without significant innovation.
- The paper does not provide sufficient evidence to support the claim that the proposed method eliminates the need for reranking stages, which is crucial for practical applications.
- The scalability of the method is not convincingly demonstrated, particularly in terms of handling large-scale datasets like ImageNet.
- The paper does not adequately compare the proposed method with state-of-the-art methods, which could have provided a clearer picture of its advantages and limitations.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors clarify the rationale behind using a sequence-to-sequence model and the semantic image tokenizer, and how these choices contribute to the overall effectiveness of the method?
- How does the proposed method handle the computational cost associated with the generative modeling approach, particularly in terms of inference speed and resource requirements?
- Could the authors provide more detailed evidence or experimental results to support the claim that the proposed method eliminates the need for reranking stages?
- How does the proposed method compare with other state-of-the-art methods in terms of scalability and performance on large-scale datasets like ImageNet?
- Can the authors discuss the potential limitations of the proposed method and suggest areas for future research or improvement?
