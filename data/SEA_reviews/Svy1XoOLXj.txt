 **Summary:**
The paper introduces BiLoRA, a novel method for fine-tuning large language models (LLMs) using bi-level optimization. BiLoRA separates the training of pseudo singular vectors and values on different subsets of the training data, aiming to alleviate overfitting and improve generalization performance. This approach contrasts with traditional methods that learn singular values and vectors simultaneously, which can lead to overfitting. BiLoRA is evaluated on various LLMs and tasks, showing improvements over LoRA and other baseline methods. However, concerns were raised about the clarity of the paper, particularly in explaining the rationale behind the bi-level optimization and the experimental setup.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel method, BiLoRA, which separates the learning of parameter subsets onto different datasets in different optimization problems, addressing the issue of overfitting in LoRA and its variants.
- BiLoRA demonstrates significant improvements over LoRA and other baseline methods across various LLMs and tasks, showcasing its effectiveness and potential for further research.
- The paper is well-organized, clearly written, and provides a detailed explanation of the proposed method, making it accessible and easy to follow.
- The experimental results are robust, demonstrating that BiLoRA outperforms LoRA and other baseline methods in terms of performance and training time, indicating its practical utility.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed explanation of the rationale behind using bi-level optimization for parameterizing pseudo singular values and vectors, making it difficult to understand the method's theoretical underpinnings.
- The experimental setup is not clearly described, particularly how the two datasets are split and the impact of this split on the results.
- The paper could benefit from more comprehensive ablation studies, including different parameterization methods for pseudo singular values and the effects of different hyperparameters.
- The paper's claims about overfitting and the generalization performance of LoRA and AdaLoRA are not adequately supported by experimental evidence.
- The paper suffers from inconsistencies and potential misleading statements, such as the claim that LoRA and AdaLoRA learn pseudo singular values and vectors simultaneously, which is not accurate according to the original papers.
- The paper's writing could be improved for clarity and precision, particularly in the use of terms and definitions.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors provide a more detailed explanation of the rationale behind using bi-level optimization for parameterizing pseudo singular values and vectors?
- How does the split of the two datasets affect the results, and could the authors provide a more detailed description of the experimental setup?
- Could the authors conduct additional ablation studies to explore the effects of different parameterization methods for pseudo singular values and the impact of different hyperparameters?
- In light of the concerns about overfitting and generalization performance, can the authors provide more experimental evidence to support these claims?
- How does the proposed method compare to other parameter-efficient fine-tuning methods, especially those that do not use a bi-level optimization approach?
- Could the authors clarify the inconsistencies and potential misleading statements in the paper, particularly regarding the learning process of pseudo singular values and vectors in LoRA and AdaLoRA?
