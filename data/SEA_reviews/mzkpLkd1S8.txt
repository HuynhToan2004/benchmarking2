 **Summary:**
The paper explores the robustness of Vision Transformers (ViTs) through the lens of nullspace, a concept from linear algebra. It demonstrates that a non-trivial nullspace exists in ViTs, primarily attributed to the patch embedding layer, and extends this concept to non-linear counterparts. The authors propose a fine-tuning method using approximate nullspace noise to enhance model robustness against various input perturbations. This method, applied for a single epoch, shows effectiveness in mitigating the adverse effects of distribution shifts and adversarial perturbations across multiple benchmark datasets. The paper also includes a theoretical analysis and empirical validation, showing that the method improves model robustness without requiring architectural modifications.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, clear, and easy to follow, with a comprehensive analysis of the existence of nullspace within transformer models, particularly at the patch embedding layer.
- The proposed method is simple and effective, requiring only fine-tuning with minimal additional data, which is beneficial for practical applications.
- The paper is theoretically sound, providing a novel approach to understanding and improving the robustness of transformer-based vision models.
- The method is shown to be effective in improving the robustness of ViTs without requiring architectural modifications, as demonstrated through extensive experimental results.
- The paper includes a comprehensive analysis of the existence of nullspace within transformer models, which is a novel approach to understanding and improving the robustness of transformer-based vision models.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed discussion on the limitations of the proposed method, particularly in scenarios where the method may not be effective or may even harm model performance.
- The method's effectiveness is not clearly justified, with insufficient experimental validation and comparison to existing methods such as robust ViTs.
- The paper's experimental evaluation is limited to specific datasets and does not include a broader range of adversarial attacks or robustness benchmarks, which could provide a more comprehensive understanding of the method's effectiveness.
- The paper does not adequately address the scalability of the proposed method, particularly in terms of its applicability to larger models or different architectures.
- The paper's theoretical analysis is not fully convincing, particularly in the definition and application of the nullspace in non-linear transformers.

**Questions:**
- Can the proposed method be extended to other architectures and layers of deep neural networks?
- What is the computational cost of computing the nullspace of the patch embedding layer?
- How does the proposed method perform in scenarios where the method may not be effective or may even harm model performance?
- How does the method perform when applied to larger models or different architectures?
- Can the method be adapted to other tasks such as object detection or segmentation?
- How does the method compare to other existing methods for improving the robustness of transformer-based vision models?
- Is there a possibility of extending the method to include a data-driven approach for learning the nullspace?
- Could the authors provide more details on the scalability and efficiency of the proposed method in practical applications?
