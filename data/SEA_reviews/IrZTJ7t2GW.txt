 **Summary:**
The paper addresses the intersection of adversarial training and fairness in machine learning, focusing on how adversarial perturbations can impact fairness metrics like Disparate Impact (DI) and Equalized Odds (EOd). It introduces a framework that aligns fairness robustness with accuracy robustness, theoretically proving the equivalence of adversarial attacks against different fairness notions. The authors propose a novel adversarial training technique that enhances fairness robustness by integrating fairness constraints with accuracy adversarial samples. The paper is supported by experiments that demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed method in improving fairness robustness. However, it faces criticisms for its clarity, particularly in the presentation of proofs and the empirical evaluation, which is seen as insufficient and not convincing.

**Strengths:**
- The paper addresses an important and timely problem in machine learning, specifically the impact of adversarial perturbations on fairness metrics.
- It introduces a novel adversarial training technique that aligns fairness robustness with accuracy robustness, which is a significant contribution to the field.
- The theoretical analysis provided is solid, and the proofs are well-presented, contributing to the understanding of the relationship between fairness and accuracy robustness.
- The paper is well-organized, making it easy to follow, and the inclusion of illustrative figures and tables enhances comprehension.
- The experimental results demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed method in improving fairness robustness, as evidenced by a decrease in disparate impact (DI) and equalized odds (EOd) under adversarial attacks.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper suffers from clarity issues, particularly in the presentation of proofs and the empirical evaluation. The proofs are not self-contained and lack detailed explanations, which could hinder understanding.
- The empirical evaluation is insufficient and does not convincingly demonstrate the superiority of the proposed method over existing approaches.
- The paper lacks a thorough discussion on the limitations of the proposed method and does not provide sufficient empirical evidence to support the theoretical claims.
- The paper could benefit from a more detailed discussion on the assumptions made in the theoretical analysis and how these assumptions affect the applicability of the results.
- The paper does not adequately address the practical implications of the proposed method, particularly in terms of computational efficiency and the scalability of the approach.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors clarify the notation and definitions used in the paper, particularly in Section 3.1 and 3.2, to enhance readability and understanding?
- How do the authors address the potential for adversarial attacks to exacerbate fairness issues, especially in scenarios where the attacker has access to sensitive attributes?
- Can the authors provide more detailed examples or case studies to illustrate the practical implications of the proposed method in real-world applications?
- How does the proposed method handle the scalability issues that might arise when applied to large datasets or complex models?
- Could the authors elaborate on the computational efficiency of the proposed method and discuss any potential trade-offs between accuracy robustness and fairness robustness?
- In light of the concerns about the empirical evaluation, could the authors consider additional experiments to more robustly demonstrate the effectiveness of their method?
