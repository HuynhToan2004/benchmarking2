 **Summary:**
The paper explores the problem of minimizing the maximum of N convex, Lipschitz functions, focusing on quantum algorithms that can provide quadratic speedup over classical methods. It introduces a quantum algorithm that utilizes a quantum zeroth-order oracle to find an approximate minimum of a softmax function, which is then used to minimize the maximum loss. The algorithm is based on the ball regularized optimization oracle (BROO) and achieves a query complexity of O~(√Nε−(2/3)+ε−(8/3)). The paper also provides a lower bound of Ω(√Nε−(2/3)) for the number of queries, demonstrating the near-optimality of the proposed quantum algorithm. The theoretical analysis is supported by a quantum lower bound, which is shown to be tight up to poly-logarithmic factors.

- The algorithm's implementation uses a quantum zeroth-order oracle, which is a novel approach in the field of quantum optimization.
- The paper provides a quantum lower bound for the problem, showing that the algorithm's performance is near-optimal.
