 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel planning framework, named Pylic, which leverages simulator source code to describe planning problems as meaningful events in the simulator's execution. This approach allows for a hierarchical planning process that decomposes complex tasks into manageable sub-goals, each associated with specific control events. The framework uses a tree-based planner to find sequences of meaningful events that lead to task completion, thereby improving planning efficiency compared to traditional methods like numerical search and reinforcement learning. The paper includes case studies in simulated robotic environments, demonstrating the framework's effectiveness in solving control and planning problems more efficiently than existing methods.
