 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a method for extracting generalizable interaction primitives from deep neural networks (DNNs) across different models, aiming to enhance the faithfulness and transferability of explanations. It addresses the challenges of ambiguity in interaction decomposition and noise sensitivity, proposing a new loss function to optimize the extraction of interactions. The authors claim that this method can improve the generalization of interaction primitives, which is crucial for explainable AI. The paper includes theoretical results and experimental validations on various datasets, demonstrating the method's effectiveness in extracting shared interactions across different models.

**Strengths:**
- The paper provides a clear explanation of the challenges in interaction extraction and proposes a novel method to address these challenges.
- The authors demonstrate the effectiveness of their method through experiments, showing that the extracted interactions are more generalizable than those extracted by traditional methods.
- The paper is well-written, making it easy to follow and understand the proposed methodology and results.
- Theoretical results are provided, which support the claims made in the paper.
- The paper addresses a significant problem in the field of explainable AI, focusing on the extraction of generalizable interaction primitives from DNNs.
- The method proposed is simple and easy to implement, making it accessible for practical applications.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed comparison with other existing methods for interaction extraction, which could have strengthened the argument for the superiority of the proposed method.
- The experimental setup is limited to specific tasks and models, which may not fully demonstrate the generalizability of the proposed method across different domains and model architectures.
- The paper does not sufficiently discuss the limitations of the proposed method, which could provide a more balanced view of its strengths and weaknesses.
- There are some issues with clarity and organization in the presentation of the paper, particularly in the explanation of certain mathematical formulations and the structure of the content.
- The paper does not sufficiently address how the proposed method handles the trade-off between sparsity and generalization, which is a critical aspect of the method's effectiveness.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors provide more details on how the proposed method compares to other existing methods for interaction extraction, particularly in terms of the generalization and faithfulness of the extracted interactions?
- How does the proposed method perform when applied to different types of models and tasks beyond those tested in the experiments?
- Could the authors clarify the mathematical formulations and definitions used in the paper, particularly in sections 2.1 and 2.2, to improve understanding?
- How does the proposed method address the trade-off between sparsity and generalization, and what are the implications of this trade-off for the practical applicability of the method?
- Can the authors provide more detailed examples or visualizations of the extracted interactions to help illustrate the method's effectiveness and generalizability?
