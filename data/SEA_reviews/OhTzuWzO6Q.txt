 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a Bayesian framework for federated learning, focusing on personalized models that adapt to different client needs and resource constraints. The approach utilizes a Bayesian inference framework to train local models with customized priors, enabling collaboration across heterogeneous clients via an unlabelled auxiliary dataset. The paper also addresses privacy concerns by incorporating differential privacy guarantees. Experiments are conducted to demonstrate the framework's effectiveness in both homogeneous and heterogeneous settings, showing improvements over strong baselines. However, the paper suffers from clarity issues, especially in the presentation of the methodology and the experimental setup, which could hinder understanding and reproducibility.

**Strengths:**
- The paper addresses a challenging problem of federated learning (FL) with heterogeneous clients, which is crucial for real-world applications.
- It introduces a novel Bayesian framework that enables collaboration across heterogeneous clients through a unique approach using prior distributions in the functional space of the networks.
- The methodology is well-articulated, providing a clear and intuitive explanation of the proposed framework.
- The paper includes a formal differential privacy guarantee, which is applicable across different client learning algorithms, enhancing the privacy and security of the system.
- Experiments are conducted on standard FL datasets, demonstrating the effectiveness of the proposed method in both homogeneous and heterogeneous settings and under strict privacy constraints.
- The paper provides characterizations of model uncertainties, which are essential for understanding the reliability and robustness of the model's predictions.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a clear comparison with existing Bayesian FL methods, making it difficult to assess the novelty and effectiveness of the proposed method.
- The experimental setup and results are not well-explained, particularly in terms of the hyperparameters used and the selection of datasets.
- The paper suffers from poor writing and lacks clarity in several sections, including the presentation of the methodology and the experimental setup.
- The paper does not adequately address the scalability of the proposed method, especially in terms of computational and memory requirements.
- There is a lack of detailed theoretical analysis, which could strengthen the paper's claims and provide a deeper understanding of the proposed method.
- The paper's claims about the efficiency of Bayesian methods and the necessity of using functional space priors are not convincingly argued, raising questions about the method's practicality and effectiveness.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors clarify the differences between their method and other Bayesian FL methods, particularly in terms of the use of functional space priors and the efficiency of Bayesian methods?
- How does the proposed method perform when compared to other Bayesian FL methods, especially in terms of computational efficiency and scalability?
- Can the authors provide more details on the experimental setup, including the selection of datasets, hyperparameters, and the rationale behind the experimental design?
- How does the proposed method ensure differential privacy, and what are the implications of this approach for the overall performance and security of the system?
- Could the authors elaborate on the choice of datasets and the experimental conditions, particularly in terms of how these conditions reflect real-world scenarios and how they affect the generalizability of the results?
