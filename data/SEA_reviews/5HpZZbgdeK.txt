 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel method for calibrating the confidence of neural network classifiers by treating the calibration problem as a binary classification task. This approach, termed top-versus-all (TvA), aims to address the challenges of calibration in high-class-count scenarios by leveraging binary calibration methods. The methodology is evaluated across various datasets and models, demonstrating improvements in calibration accuracy. The paper also includes a regularization term to prevent overfitting, which is particularly useful when the number of classes is high. Despite its innovative approach, the paper has been critiqued for its limited novelty and the lack of a comprehensive comparison with state-of-the-art methods.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel method for calibrating the confidence of neural network classifiers, which is particularly useful in high-class-count scenarios.
- The proposed method is evaluated across various datasets and models, demonstrating improvements in calibration accuracy.
- The paper is well-written, clear, and easy to follow, with a good balance between theoretical insights and practical applications.
- The use of regularization to prevent overfitting is a valuable addition, especially when the number of classes is high.
- The paper provides a clear and concise explanation of the proposed method, making it accessible to readers.

**Weaknesses:**
- The novelty of the proposed method is limited as it is a straightforward adaptation of existing methods, such as those used in binary classification.
- The paper lacks a comprehensive comparison with state-of-the-art methods, which could have provided a clearer picture of the method's performance and advantages.
- The paper does not discuss the potential limitations of the proposed method, which could have provided a more balanced view of its applicability and effectiveness.
- The paper does not sufficiently address the issue of overfitting, especially when the number of classes is high, which could lead to biased results.
- The paper could benefit from a more detailed discussion on the choice of calibration metrics, such as the Expected Calibration Error (ECE), and the potential impact of different binning strategies.

**Questions:**
- Could you provide a more detailed explanation of the regularization term and its impact on the calibration process?
- How does the proposed method compare to other state-of-the-art methods in terms of calibration accuracy and computational efficiency?
- Can you elaborate on the choice of the calibration metric (ECE) and discuss how different binning strategies might affect the results?
- How does the proposed method handle the issue of overfitting when the number of classes is high?
- Is there any experimental evidence to support the claim that the proposed method improves the performance of existing calibration methods?
