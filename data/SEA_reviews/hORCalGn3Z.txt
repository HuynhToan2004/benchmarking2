 **Summary:**
The paper investigates communication-efficient algorithms for solving distributed variational inequalities (VIPs) using a federated learning setup. It introduces a unified framework for analyzing distributed VIPs, focusing on communication-efficient methods that aim to improve communication complexity. The authors propose a ProxSkip algorithm that enhances the communication efficiency by skipping the proximal operator in most iterations, thus reducing the need for communication. Theoretical analysis and numerical experiments are provided to support the proposed methods, demonstrating their effectiveness in solving federated learning problems. However, the paper's novelty and the practical significance of the communication acceleration are questioned due to its similarity to existing works and the potential lack of significant improvement in communication complexity.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, with clear theoretical contributions and a comprehensive literature review.
- The authors provide a unified framework for analyzing distributed variational inequalities (VIPs), which is beneficial for understanding and solving various optimization problems.
- The paper introduces a novel algorithm, ProxSkip, which is designed to be communication-efficient and can be applied to various distributed VIPs.
- The paper presents a new result for communication complexity in distributed VIPs, which is a significant contribution to the field.
- The theoretical analysis is solid, with a detailed proof of the main theorem, and the paper includes numerical experiments to validate the theoretical results.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper's contribution is questioned due to its similarity to existing works, particularly in the use of ProxSkip and the focus on communication efficiency.
- The paper does not adequately address the practical significance of communication acceleration, as the improvement in communication complexity is not substantial.
- The paper could benefit from a more detailed comparison with existing methods, particularly in terms of communication complexity and the impact of different regularizers on the proximal operator.
- There is a lack of discussion on the practical implications of the proposed algorithms, such as the computational complexity and the scalability to larger datasets.
- The paper could be improved by including more detailed discussions on the limitations of the proposed methods and potential directions for future research.
