 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel approach to fine-tuning large language models (LLMs) using reinforcement learning (RL) by aligning the reward distribution of generated sentences with a predefined target reward distribution. This method aims to mitigate the issue of reward over-optimization, which can lead to unnatural language generations. The paper presents three use cases to demonstrate the effectiveness of this approach, comparing it with traditional methods like KL regularization and direct reward maximization. The proposed method is claimed to offer an a priori and parameter-free control over the reward distribution, potentially improving the reliability of RL-based fine-tuning. However, the paper has been critiqued for its lack of clarity in experimental details, insufficient comparison with existing methods, and the absence of significant novelty.

**Strengths:**
- The paper addresses a significant issue in the field of large language models (LLMs) by focusing on reward over-optimization, which is a crucial problem in RL-based fine-tuning.
- The method proposed is straightforward and effective, with results showing that it aligns the fine-tuned policy with the target distribution, potentially improving the reliability of RL-based fine-tuning.
- The paper is well-written, making it easy to follow, and provides a clear and detailed explanation of the methodology.
- The experimental results are promising, showing that the proposed method outperforms traditional methods in terms of reward distribution alignment and task success.
- The paper is well-organized, with a clear introduction, methodology, and experimental setup, which facilitates understanding and replication of the research.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks clarity in its experimental details, particularly in the explanation of the datasets used and the specifics of the reward model and LLM used in each experiment.
- The paper does not provide sufficient comparisons with existing methods, such as RLHF, which could have strengthened the argument for the effectiveness of the proposed method.
- The novelty of the proposed method is questionable, as it seems to be a simple modification of existing methods like KL regularization.
- The paper lacks a comprehensive discussion on the limitations of the proposed method, particularly in scenarios where the method might not be effective or where it could lead to suboptimal outcomes.
- The paper does not include a detailed analysis of the computational cost of the proposed method, which is crucial for practical implementation.
- The paper does not provide sufficient details on the implementation of the RL algorithms used, which could affect the reproducibility and understanding of the results.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors clarify the specifics of the datasets used in each experiment, including their size and characteristics?
- How does the proposed method compare with other existing methods, such as RLHF, in terms of effectiveness and computational efficiency?
- Could the authors provide more details on the computational cost of the proposed method, including the training time and resources required?
- How does the proposed method handle scenarios where the target distribution might not be optimal or where the reward model is imperfect?
- Could the authors elaborate on the implementation details of the RL algorithms used, particularly in terms of hyperparameter tuning and algorithmic specifics?
- How does the proposed method perform in scenarios where the target distribution is discontinuous or non-differentiable?
- Could the authors provide more details on the experimental setup, including the specifics of the LLM and reward model used in each experiment?
