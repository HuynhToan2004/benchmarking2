 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel framework leveraging diffusion models to generate complete, feasible solutions for Integer Programming (IP) problems. This method uses contrastive learning to establish relationships between IP instances and solutions, learning latent embeddings for both IP instances and their solutions. The framework employs diffusion models to learn the distribution of solution embeddings conditioned on IP representations, with a dedicated guided sampling strategy that accounts for both constraints and objectives. The method is evaluated against state-of-the-art methods on four typical datasets, demonstrating its effectiveness in generating high-quality solutions. However, concerns about the scalability of the method and the need for further validation against more traditional IP solvers were raised.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, easy to follow, and provides a clear motivation for the research.
- The method is innovative, using diffusion models to generate complete, feasible solutions for Integer Programming (IP) problems, which is a novel approach.
- The use of contrastive learning to establish relationships between IP instances and solutions is a smart and effective method.
- The paper is well-organized and provides detailed explanations of the methodology and experiments, including the use of a comprehensive framework for generating feasible solutions for IP problems.
- The method is evaluated against state-of-the-art methods on four typical datasets, demonstrating its effectiveness in generating high-quality solutions.
- The use of diffusion models to learn the distribution of solution embeddings is a novel approach that has shown promising results.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed discussion on the scalability of the method and how it performs on larger, more complex IP problems.
- There is a need for more comparative analysis with traditional IP solvers to validate the effectiveness of the proposed method.
- The paper does not adequately address the computational efficiency of the method, which is crucial for practical applications.
- The methodology section could benefit from more detailed explanations and a clearer presentation of the method's components and their roles.
- The paper does not sufficiently discuss the limitations of the method, such as its applicability to different types of IP problems or its scalability to larger, more complex problems.
- There is a lack of discussion on the potential negative societal impacts of the proposed method.

**Questions:**
