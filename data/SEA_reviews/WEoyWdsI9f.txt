 **Summary:**
The paper investigates the privacy risks associated with logit-based federated learning, where model outputs (logits) are shared instead of model weights or gradients. It introduces the Adaptive Model Stealing Attack (AdaMSA) to quantify the privacy risk and proposes a defense mechanism to mitigate this risk. The paper provides theoretical and empirical analyses, including experiments on various datasets like MNIST, EMNIST, and SVHN. It highlights the potential for semi-honest servers to infer clients' private models using logits, and discusses the trade-offs between privacy and utility. Despite its innovative approach, the paper faces criticism for its limited experimental scope, outdated baseline comparisons, and potential oversimplification of the privacy risks.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel privacy risk in logit-based federated learning, which is an interesting and unexplored area in the field.
- The theoretical and empirical analysis provided is solid, and the paper is well-organized, making it easy to follow.
- The proposed attack and defense mechanisms are innovative and could be of significant interest to the federated learning community.
- The paper is well-written, with clear and concise explanations of the proposed methods and their implications.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a thorough discussion on the potential impact of the proposed defense mechanisms on the utility of the models and the communication costs involved.
- The experimental evaluation is limited to small-scale datasets and simple architectures, which may not generalize well to more complex scenarios.
- The baseline comparisons are outdated, and the paper does not include comparisons with more recent and relevant works.
- The paper's assumptions and threat model are not clearly defined, which could limit the understanding and applicability of the results.
- The privacy risks discussed are not adequately differentiated from existing privacy risks in federated learning, which could lead to confusion about the novelty and significance of the findings.

**Questions:**
- Could you provide more details on the privacy risks associated with logit-based federated learning and how they differ from other privacy risks in federated learning?
- How does the proposed defense mechanism affect the utility of the models, and what are the potential trade-offs between privacy and utility?
- Can you clarify the assumptions made in the paper, particularly regarding the adversary's knowledge and capabilities?
- How does the proposed defense mechanism perform in larger-scale or more complex scenarios, and what are the potential limitations or challenges in such settings?
- Could you elaborate on the threat model and the assumptions made about the adversary's capabilities and knowledge?
- How does the proposed defense mechanism compare to other existing defense mechanisms in terms of effectiveness and practical implementation?
