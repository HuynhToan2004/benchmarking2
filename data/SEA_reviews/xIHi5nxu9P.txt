 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel framework for learning subtractive mixtures via squaring in the context of probabilistic circuits, which are a generalization of traditional mixture models. This approach allows for the representation of negative weights, enhancing the expressiveness of the model. The paper presents theoretical results on the expressiveness of these circuits and empirical evaluations demonstrating their superiority over traditional mixture models. The methodology is applied to both synthetic and real-world data, showcasing the potential of this approach in various applications. Despite the theoretical advancements and empirical evidence, the paper's presentation and the practical implications of the negative weights remain contentious.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel approach to learning negative mixture weights, which is a significant advancement in the field of mixture models.
- The theoretical results are well-presented, with clear proofs and a solid mathematical foundation.
- The empirical results demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed method, showing that it outperforms traditional mixture models in certain scenarios.
- The paper is well-written, with clear explanations of the methodology and the theoretical underpinnings.
- The connection between different models and the proof of the expressive power of the proposed model are interesting and provide a new perspective on mixture models.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper's presentation could be improved, with some sections being overly complex and difficult to follow, particularly the discussion on circuits and the experimental details.
- The practical implications of negative weights in the context of mixture models are not clearly explained, which could limit the understanding of the paper's contributions.
- The experimental setup and the results are not convincingly superior to existing models, with some results showing marginal improvements or even inferior performance in certain cases.
- The paper does not adequately address the computational complexity and the scalability of the proposed method, which is crucial for practical applications.
- There are several minor issues with the paper's presentation, such as missing references and typographical errors, which could detract from its professional quality.
