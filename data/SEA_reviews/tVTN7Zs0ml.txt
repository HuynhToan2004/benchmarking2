 **Summary:**
The paper introduces GraphCare, a framework designed to generate personalized knowledge graphs (KGs) using large language models (LLMs) and external biomedical KGs. This framework is aimed at enhancing predictions based on electronic health records (EHR) by integrating medical knowledge. The proposed Bi-attention Augmented (BAT) graph neural network (GNN) is used to train models for healthcare predictions, showing superior performance on various tasks such as mortality, readmission, length of stay, and drug recommendations. The framework is evaluated on two public datasets, MIMIC-III and MIMIC-IV, and the results indicate significant improvements over baseline models. However, concerns about the interpretability of the generated knowledge graphs and the generalizability of the approach to other healthcare settings were raised.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel framework, GraphCare, which effectively harnesses the wealth of clinical knowledge by integrating large language models (LLMs) to generate personalized knowledge graphs (KGs) tailored to medical concepts.
- The proposed Bi-attention Augmented (BAT) graph neural network (GNN) is innovative, offering a new approach to enhancing the predictive capabilities of healthcare models.
- The paper is well-structured, making it easy to follow, and provides a clear motivation for the proposed method.
- The framework is evaluated on two widely-used EHR datasets, MIMIC-III and MIMIC-IV, demonstrating its effectiveness and robustness.
- The authors have conducted a comprehensive ablation study, providing insights into the effectiveness of different components of the framework.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed discussion on the limitations of the proposed approach, particularly in terms of its generalizability to other healthcare settings and the interpretability of the generated knowledge graphs.
- The framework's reliance on LLMs and external biomedical KGs may introduce biases and errors, which could affect the accuracy and reliability of the predictions.
- There is a lack of comparison with other state-of-the-art methods, which could have provided a more robust evaluation of the proposed framework.
- The paper does not adequately address the issue of data sparsity in healthcare datasets, which could limit the practical applicability of the proposed method.
- The paper's claims of generating personalized KGs are not convincingly supported by evidence, and the methodology for generating these KGs is not clearly explained.
- The paper suffers from presentation issues, including unclear definitions and descriptions, which could hinder understanding of the methodology and results.

**Questions:**
- How does the proposed framework address the issue of data sparsity in healthcare datasets?
- Can the authors provide more details on the generation process of the personalized KGs, especially how they are tailored to individual patients?
- What are the specific biases and errors introduced by the reliance on LLMs and external biomedical KGs, and how are these addressed?
- How does the framework ensure the interpretability of the generated knowledge graphs, especially in terms of their clinical relevance and applicability?
- Can the authors provide more details on the experimental setup, particularly the data preprocessing and feature extraction steps?
- How does the framework handle missing or incomplete data in EHR records?
- Can the authors clarify the definitions and descriptions used in the paper, particularly in sections 1 and 2, to improve readability and understanding?
