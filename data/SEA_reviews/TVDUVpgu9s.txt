 **Summary:**
The paper introduces ZO-RankSGD, a novel zeroth-order optimization algorithm designed for scenarios where only ranking oracles are available to evaluate the objective function. The algorithm leverages a rank-based random estimator to determine the descent direction and guarantees convergence to a stationary point. It is applicable to policy optimization problems in reinforcement learning and can improve the quality of images generated by diffusion models with human ranking feedback. The paper also provides theoretical analysis and empirical validation of the algorithm's effectiveness, demonstrating its ability to enhance the detail of generated images with minimal human feedback. However, the paper lacks comprehensive comparisons with existing methods and could benefit from more detailed experimental setups and a broader range of applications.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel zeroth-order optimization algorithm, ZO-RankSGD, which utilizes a rank-based random estimator to determine the descent direction, ensuring convergence to a stationary point.
- The algorithm is applicable to policy optimization problems in reinforcement learning and can improve the quality of images generated by diffusion models with human ranking feedback.
- The paper provides theoretical analysis and empirical validation of the algorithm's effectiveness, demonstrating its ability to enhance the detail of generated images with minimal human feedback.
- The authors demonstrate the versatility of their algorithm by applying it to a variety of applications, including the optimization of latent embeddings in diffusion models.
- The paper is well-written, clear, and easy to follow, making it accessible to a broad audience.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a comprehensive comparison with existing methods, particularly in terms of the number of queries and the convergence rate.
- The experimental setup is limited, and the paper does not include a comparison with a broader range of applications or a more detailed analysis of the algorithm's performance under different conditions.
- The paper's application to reinforcement learning is not clearly explained, and the experimental results do not convincingly demonstrate the superiority of the proposed method over existing methods.
- The paper does not provide a detailed discussion on how the algorithm performs under noisy or uncertain ranking feedback, which is crucial for practical applications.
- There are concerns about the scalability of the algorithm, particularly in high-dimensional spaces, and the paper does not address this issue adequately.
- The paper could benefit from a more detailed analysis of the algorithm's performance under different conditions, such as noisy or uncertain ranking feedback, and a more thorough discussion of its limitations and potential improvements.
