 **Summary:**
The paper introduces Embodied Active Defense (EAD), a novel approach to enhancing the robustness of deep neural networks against adversarial patches in 3D real-world settings. EAD employs a recurrent feedback mechanism that contextualizes environmental information, integrating a perception module and a policy module to actively address misaligned adversarial patches. This methodology is tested across various scenarios such as face recognition and object detection, demonstrating significant improvements over existing defense methods. The paper also includes theoretical analysis and extensive experiments to validate the effectiveness of EAD, showing substantial reductions in attack success rates across a range of unseen adversarial attacks.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel approach to enhancing the robustness of deep neural networks against adversarial patches, specifically in 3D real-world settings, which is a significant advancement in the field of adversarial machine learning.
- The proposed methodology, EAD, is innovative and well-motivated, integrating a perception module and a policy module that recurrently process beliefs and observations, allowing for the development of strategic actions to counter adversarial patches.
- The paper is well-written, with clear explanations and detailed methodology, making it accessible and easy to understand.
- Extensive experiments are conducted to validate the effectiveness of EAD, demonstrating significant improvements in performance and robustness against various attacks.
- Theoretical analysis is provided to support the effectiveness of EAD, enhancing the paper's credibility and depth.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed discussion on the computational complexity and resource requirements of EAD, which could be a significant limitation for practical deployment.
- The experimental setup is primarily focused on synthetic datasets, which might not fully represent real-world scenarios, limiting the generalizability of the results.
- There is a lack of clarity in the motivation behind the use of a policy module, and the paper does not sufficiently explain the role of the policy module in enhancing the robustness of the system.
- The paper does not adequately address the potential limitations or negative societal impacts of the proposed method, which could be crucial for understanding the broader implications of the work.
- The paper's claims about the generalizability of the proposed method are not convincingly supported by the experimental results, particularly in real-world scenarios.

**Questions:**
- Could you provide more details on the computational complexity and resource requirements of EAD, especially in comparison to other defense methods?
- How does the performance of EAD vary with different patch sizes and attack iterations, and what are the optimal settings for these parameters?
- Can you explain the rationale behind the use of a policy module and how it specifically contributes to the robustness of the system?
- In the context of real-world applications, how does EAD perform against adaptive attacks, and what measures have been taken to ensure the system's effectiveness in such scenarios?
- How does the proposed method compare to other state-of-the-art defense methods in terms of computational efficiency and practical deployment?
- Could you provide more details on the experimental setup, particularly how the results were validated in real-world scenarios?
- What are the potential negative societal impacts of the proposed method, and how are these addressed in the paper?
