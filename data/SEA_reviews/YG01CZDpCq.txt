 **Summary:**
The paper introduces Adaptive Prompt Prototype Learning (APPLe) for Vision-Language Models (VLMs) to address the issue of using a single embedding for each class, which may not adequately capture visual variance. APPLe utilizes multiple prompts as class prototypes and an adaptive attention mechanism to weigh the importance of different prototypes. The method is tested on various datasets, demonstrating consistent performance improvements. However, concerns are raised about the novelty and the significant contribution of the proposed method over existing techniques like PLOT and CoOp. The paper is criticized for its lack of clarity in explaining the method's advantages over these methods and the potential limitations in scalability and generalization.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written and easy to follow, with a clear explanation of the proposed method.
- The proposed method is novel and makes sense, addressing the issue of using a single embedding for each class in Vision-Language Models (VLMs).
- Extensive experiments have been conducted to demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed method.
- The paper provides a detailed analysis of the proposed method, including an ablation study on the components of the method.
- The results of the proposed method are better than the baseline methods, indicating its effectiveness.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a clear explanation of the advantages of the proposed method over existing techniques like PLOT and CoOp, particularly in terms of novelty and performance.
- The motivation behind the use of multiple prompts as class prototypes is not well-explained, especially in terms of how they address the issue of using a single embedding for each class.
- The paper does not adequately address the limitations of the proposed method, particularly in terms of scalability and generalization to new classes.
- There is a lack of clarity in the experimental setup, including the details of the datasets used and the specifics of the experimental conditions.
- The paper does not provide a detailed discussion on the limitations of the proposed method, which is crucial for understanding its practical applicability.
- The paper could benefit from a more detailed comparison with existing methods, particularly in terms of computational efficiency and scalability.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors provide more details on how the proposed method addresses the issue of using a single embedding for each class in VLMs?
- How does the proposed method compare in terms of novelty and performance with existing techniques like PLOT and CoOp?
- Could the authors clarify the experimental setup, including the details of the datasets used and the specifics of the experimental conditions?
- How does the proposed method perform in terms of computational efficiency and scalability, particularly when applied to larger or more complex datasets?
- Can the authors provide more details on the limitations of the proposed method and how these limitations might affect its practical applicability?
