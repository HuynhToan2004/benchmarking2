 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel architecture called LMUFormer, which integrates Legendre Memory Units (LMU) with convolutional patch embedding and channel mixers, aiming to improve performance in sequence learning tasks. The authors also propose a spiking variant of this architecture, termed Spiking LMUFormer, which is evaluated on datasets like Speech Commands V2 and Long Range Arena. The model's efficiency is highlighted, showing significant reductions in parameters and computational complexity compared to existing models like AST. The paper also discusses the parallel training capabilities of LMU, which are beneficial for resource-constrained environments.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel architecture, LMUFormer, which integrates LMU with convolutional patch embedding and channel mixers, enhancing performance in sequence learning tasks.
- The authors propose a spiking variant of the LMUFormer, which is evaluated on the Speech Commands V2 dataset and the Long Range Arena benchmark, showing comparable performance to state-of-the-art models while maintaining efficiency.
- The paper is well-organized and clearly written, making it accessible and easy to understand.
- The LMUFormer architecture is designed to reduce computational complexity and improve efficiency, which is crucial for resource-constrained environments.
- The paper provides a comprehensive evaluation of the model's performance on various datasets, including a thorough ablation study, which supports the effectiveness of the proposed modifications.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed discussion on the limitations of the proposed model and potential future work, which could enhance its impact and applicability.
- The paper could benefit from a more comprehensive literature review, particularly in the area of SNNs, to position the work more effectively within the current research landscape.
- The experimental evaluation is limited to specific datasets and does not include a broader range of benchmarks, which could provide a more robust validation of the model's performance.
- The paper does not adequately discuss the implications of using SNNs in the proposed model, which could be a significant drawback for some applications.
- The paper does not provide a detailed comparison with other models in terms of energy efficiency, which is crucial for evaluating the practical benefits of the proposed model.

**Questions:**
- How does the proposed model perform on more complex datasets, such as those involving longer sequences or more complex data types?
- Can the authors provide a more detailed comparison of the proposed model with other models in terms of energy efficiency and computational complexity?
- How does the model handle the conversion from floating-point values to spikes, particularly in the context of SNNs?
- Could the authors elaborate on the potential limitations of the proposed model and suggest areas for future research?
- What are the specific advantages of using SNNs in the proposed model, and how do these advantages translate into practical benefits for applications?
