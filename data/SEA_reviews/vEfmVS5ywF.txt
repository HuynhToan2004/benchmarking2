 **Summary:**
The paper explores a novel setting of strategic classification in labor markets, where agents can directly manipulate their outcomes, a concept termed "reverse causal strategic classification." This setup extends beyond traditional models by allowing agents to adjust their labels, which in turn affects their features. The paper introduces a new algorithm for risk minimization and presents theoretical and empirical analyses of this model in the Coate-Loury framework. It discusses the implications of this model on employer and worker welfare, equity, and discrimination. The paper also includes experiments to validate the theoretical results, albeit with some concerns regarding the clarity and depth of the empirical results and the assumptions made.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel setting of strategic classification where agents can directly manipulate their outcomes, a concept termed "reverse causal strategic classification."
- The theoretical analysis provided is robust, and the paper includes a detailed empirical analysis that supports the theoretical results.
- The paper is well-written, with clear and concise language, and the results are well-presented.
- The authors introduce a new algorithm for risk minimization in this setting, which is a significant contribution to the field.
