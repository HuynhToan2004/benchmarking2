 **Summary:**
The paper introduces TorchRL, a comprehensive reinforcement learning (RL) library for PyTorch, designed to facilitate the development of RL algorithms. Key features include the TensorDict, a versatile data structure that supports both key-based and shape-based indexing, enhancing the communication between RL components. The library also integrates with various RL algorithms and environments, aiming to provide a unified interface for training and evaluation. Despite its potential utility and compatibility with existing frameworks like PyTorch, concerns were raised about the novelty and the lack of rigorous experimental validation against state-of-the-art libraries.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written and easy to follow, providing a clear and comprehensive description of the TorchRL library and its components.
- The TorchRL library is designed to be flexible and modular, integrating seamlessly with PyTorch and other RL libraries, which could benefit researchers and developers in the RL community.
- The library includes a variety of RL algorithms, environments, and tools, which can be useful for both research and practical applications.
- The paper provides a detailed explanation of the TensorDict, which is a novel data structure that supports both key-based and shape-based indexing, enhancing the communication between RL components.
- The library is open-source, which encourages collaboration and sharing of resources within the RL community.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a clear motivation for the development of TorchRL and does not provide sufficient justification for its use over existing libraries like RLlib.
- The experimental results are limited and do not compare the performance of TorchRL with state-of-the-art RL libraries, which could have validated its effectiveness and efficiency.
- The paper does not discuss the limitations of the TensorDict, which could be a significant drawback if it fails to meet the needs of RL researchers.
- The paper does not discuss the scalability of the library, which is crucial for handling large-scale RL tasks.
- The paper does not discuss the potential negative societal impacts of the library, which is an important consideration for any technological development.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors provide a clearer motivation for the development of TorchRL and compare its features and benefits with existing libraries like RLlib?
- How does the TensorDict compare with other data structures in terms of performance and functionality, especially in the context of RL applications?
- Can the authors provide more detailed experimental results, including comparisons with state-of-the-art RL libraries and discussions on the scalability of the library?
- How does the library handle the scalability challenges, especially in large-scale RL tasks?
- Are there any known limitations or drawbacks of the TensorDict that could affect its utility in RL applications?
- How does the library handle the integration of different RL algorithms and environments, and what are the implications for performance and efficiency?
