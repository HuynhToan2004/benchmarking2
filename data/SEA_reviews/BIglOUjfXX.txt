 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel conditional diffusion mechanism, Forked Diffusion Processes (FDP), designed to enhance conditional generation of molecular structures and properties. FDP extends the existing diffusion process by incorporating a parent process and multiple child processes, each conditioned on specific properties. This approach allows for more precise energy guidance and fine-grained control over the generation process. The method is evaluated across various datasets and shown to outperform existing methods in conditional molecule generation, although the novelty and the necessity of the forking mechanism are questioned.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, clear, and easy to follow, with a comprehensive literature review that positions the work within the existing field.
- The proposed Forked Diffusion Processes (FDP) are novel and innovative, providing a new perspective on conditional graph generation.
- The paper presents a strong mathematical foundation, leveraging tools from Stochastic Differential Equations (SDEs) to derive both the forward forking diffusion process and the corresponding reverse SDE.
- The empirical evaluations are robust, demonstrating the effectiveness of FDP across various datasets and tasks, showing significant improvements over existing methods.
- The paper includes a detailed theoretical framework and rigorous mathematical derivations, which are crucial for understanding the proposed method's theoretical underpinnings.
- The experiments are well-designed, with clear figures and tables that effectively illustrate the results, and the paper includes a detailed discussion on the limitations of the proposed approach.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper could benefit from a more thorough discussion on the limitations of the proposed approach, particularly concerning the computational cost and the scalability of the method.
- The novelty of the forking mechanism is questioned, as it seems to be an extension of existing methods rather than a groundbreaking innovation.
- The paper lacks a detailed discussion on the theoretical justification for the forking mechanism and its implications for the generation process.
- There is a need for a broader range of baselines, including more recent and relevant methods, to provide a more comprehensive comparison.
- The paper does not sufficiently explore the implications of the forking mechanism on the generation process, particularly how it affects the distribution of generated samples.
- The paper's structure and organization could be improved, particularly in the introduction and related work sections, to better guide the reader through the content.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors provide a detailed analysis of the computational cost associated with the FDP method and discuss any potential strategies for reducing this cost?
- How does the forking mechanism influence the generation process, particularly in terms of the distribution of generated samples?
- Could the authors elaborate on the theoretical justification for the forking mechanism and discuss its implications for the generation process?
- In terms of experimental design, how were the baselines selected, and why were certain methods chosen over others?
- How does the FDP method handle the scalability issues associated with the generation of larger graphs, and what strategies could be employed to address these challenges?
- Could the authors provide more details on the parameterization and implementation of the forking mechanism, particularly how it affects the training and inference processes?
