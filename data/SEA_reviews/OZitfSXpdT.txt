 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel approach to knowledge distillation (KD) by learning sample-wise knowledge fusion ratios using a neural network that takes trilateral geometry into account. This method, termed TGeo-KD, leverages both intra- and inter-sample geometric relations to adaptively adjust the fusion ratio, aiming to optimize the distillation process. The paper presents extensive experiments across various datasets and models, demonstrating improvements over existing methods. Despite the innovative approach, the paper faces criticism for its lack of clarity in motivation and methodological details, particularly in the experimental setup and the choice of baselines.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel approach to knowledge distillation (KD) by learning sample-wise knowledge fusion ratios using a neural network that takes trilateral geometry into account, which is a significant advancement in the field.
- The method is evaluated across a range of tasks, including image classification, attack detection, and click-through rate prediction, demonstrating its versatility and applicability.
- The experimental results show that the proposed method outperforms existing methods on all datasets, indicating its effectiveness.
- The paper is well-written, with clear and concise explanations that make it accessible to readers.
- The authors provide comprehensive ablation studies, which help in understanding the impact of different components of the proposed method.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks clarity in the motivation behind the proposed method, particularly the rationale for using trilateral geometry and the necessity of the proposed method over existing methods.
- The methodology section is not sufficiently detailed, with some parts of the methodology not adequately explained, such as the choice of baselines and the experimental setup.
- The paper does not provide sufficient theoretical justification for the proposed method, which could enhance the paper's credibility and understanding.
- The experimental results are not consistently strong across all datasets and models, with some datasets showing minimal improvements or even performance degradation.
- The paper does not address the limitations of the proposed method, which could provide a more balanced view of the research.

**Questions:**
- Could you clarify the motivation behind the proposed method and how it addresses the specific challenges in knowledge distillation?
- How does the proposed method compare to existing methods that also use sample-wise knowledge fusion ratios, such as those mentioned in the literature?
- Can you provide more details on the experimental setup, particularly the choice of baselines and the experimental conditions?
- How does the proposed method perform on larger datasets like ImageNet, and what are the implications of using different teacher and student architectures?
- Can you provide more theoretical justification for the proposed method, and how does it relate to existing methods in the field?
- Why were certain baselines not included in the experiments, and how do the results compare to these omitted baselines?
