 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel method for zero-shot out-of-distribution (OOD) detection using large language models (LLMs) to generate potential outlier class labels without requiring actual OOD data. The proposed method, termed DOS, utilizes a new score function based on the proportionality between potential outlier and ID class labels to distinguish hard OOD samples effectively. The approach is evaluated across various OOD detection tasks, including far, near, and fine-grained OOD detection, and is shown to achieve state-of-the-art performance. The method leverages expert knowledge from LLMs to dream potential outlier class labels for zero-shot OOD detection, which is a significant advancement in the field.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel approach to zero-shot OOD detection using LLM to generate potential outlier class labels, which is a significant advancement in the field.
- The proposed method is well-motivated, technically sound, and achieves good results, as demonstrated by extensive experiments and ablation studies.
- The paper is well-written, making it easy to follow, and provides a clear and detailed explanation of the proposed method.
- The use of LLM to generate potential outlier class labels is a novel and interesting approach that has the potential to enhance the performance of OOD detection models.
- The method's ability to distinguish between different types of OOD samples, such as far, near, and fine-grained OOD, is a valuable contribution to the field.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed discussion on the limitations of the proposed method, particularly concerning the generation of outlier class labels by LLM and the potential biases or errors in these labels.
- The evaluation of the method is somewhat limited, as it primarily focuses on CLIP-based methods and does not consider other types of OOD detection methods such as density-based or reconstruction-based methods.
- The method's generalizability to other datasets and its scalability to larger or more complex datasets are not thoroughly addressed.
- The paper does not provide a detailed analysis of the computational complexity or the inference time of the proposed method, which could be crucial for practical applications.
- There is a lack of discussion on how the method performs in scenarios where the ID and OOD classes overlap, which is a common issue in real-world applications.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors provide more details on the generation process of outlier class labels by LLM, including the potential biases or errors in these labels?
- How does the proposed method perform when the ID and OOD classes overlap?
- Can the authors provide a detailed analysis of the computational complexity and inference time of the proposed method?
- How does the proposed method compare to other types of OOD detection methods, such as density-based or reconstruction-based methods?
- Can the authors provide more details on the scalability of the method to larger or more complex datasets?
- How does the proposed method perform when the ID and OOD class labels are not well-defined or when there is a lack of expert knowledge in the LLM?
