 **Summary:**
The paper explores the possibility of detecting AI-generated text by analyzing the sample complexity of detectors. It leverages information theory to establish theoretical bounds on the sample size required for effective detection, demonstrating that the sample complexity increases as the text quality approaches human-like standards. Empirical evaluations across various datasets and state-of-the-art models support these theoretical findings. The paper also discusses the challenges and implications of detecting AI-generated text, particularly in scenarios where the AI-generated text closely resembles human-generated text.

**Strengths:**
- The paper addresses a significant and timely topic, focusing on the detection of AI-generated text, which is crucial for various applications such as content moderation and intellectual property protection.
- The authors provide a comprehensive theoretical analysis and empirical validation, demonstrating the feasibility of detecting AI-generated text even when it closely resembles human-generated text.
- The paper is well-written, with clear and concise explanations that facilitate understanding of the complex theoretical concepts and empirical results.
- The use of information theory to derive sample complexity bounds for detecting AI-generated text is innovative and provides a novel approach to the problem.
- The experimental results are robust and support the theoretical claims, showing that the detection of AI-generated text is possible under various conditions.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed discussion on the limitations and potential negative societal impacts of AI-generated text detection, which is crucial for a comprehensive understanding of the technology.
- There is a lack of clarity in some of the experimental results and theoretical claims, which could benefit from additional clarification or visual aids to enhance understanding.
- The paper could benefit from a more thorough review of related literature, particularly in the area of AI-generated text detection and the societal implications of such technology.
- The paper does not sufficiently address the practical challenges and limitations of AI-generated text detection in real-world scenarios, such as the availability of sufficient data and the potential for false positives and false negatives.
- The paper's theoretical analysis and empirical results do not convincingly demonstrate that AI-generated text detection is always possible, especially in scenarios where the AI-generated text closely resembles human-generated text.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors clarify the discrepancy between the theoretical results and the empirical observations, particularly regarding the practical feasibility of AI-generated text detection?
- How do the authors address the potential negative societal impacts of AI-generated text detection, such as privacy concerns and the potential for misuse?
- Could the authors provide more detailed examples or scenarios where AI-generated text detection could be practically applied, and discuss the implications of these applications?
- How do the authors respond to the concerns regarding the practicality and feasibility of AI-generated text detection in real-world scenarios, particularly in terms of the availability and quality of data?
- In light of the concerns about the practicality and theoretical soundness of the proposed methods, how do the authors justify the claims made in the paper, and what steps could be taken to address these concerns?
