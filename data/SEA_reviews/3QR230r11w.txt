 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel multi-fidelity active learning algorithm, MF-GFN, which integrates GFlowNets to sample both candidates and oracle indices proportionally to a given acquisition function. This method is designed to discover diverse, high-scoring candidates at a reduced computational cost compared to traditional single-fidelity methods. The evaluation of this algorithm is conducted across four scientific discovery tasks, demonstrating its ability to discover high-scoring candidates while maintaining diversity. The method's novelty lies in its application of GFlowNets in a multi-fidelity setting, which is less explored in the literature. However, the paper's presentation and the clarity of its contributions are noted as needing improvement.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel multi-fidelity active learning algorithm that effectively leverages GFlowNets to sample both candidates and oracle indices, enhancing the diversity of discovered samples.
- The evaluation of the proposed method is thorough, demonstrating its ability to discover high-scoring candidates at a reduced computational cost compared to traditional single-fidelity methods.
- The paper is well-written, clear, and easy to follow, with a comprehensive evaluation across four scientific discovery tasks, providing a robust assessment of the proposed method.
- The inclusion of an ablation study and additional results in the appendix offers valuable insights into the method's performance and components.
- The paper is well-motivated and provides a clear introduction of the background of GFlowNets and multi-fidelity active learning, making it accessible to readers unfamiliar with these concepts.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks clarity in describing the contributions of the proposed algorithm and the specific advantages of using GFlowNets in the multi-fidelity setting.
- The novelty of the paper is somewhat limited as it builds upon existing methods such as GFlowNets and multi-fidelity active learning, with the main innovation being the integration of these two approaches.
- The paper could benefit from more detailed explanations of the multi-fidelity GP kernel and the acquisition function, as well as the computational complexity of the proposed method.
- There is a need for more diverse benchmarks and experiments to validate the method's performance across different types of tasks and data sets.
- The paper does not adequately address the limitations of the proposed method, such as its applicability to continuous data and its scalability to large-scale problems.
- The presentation of the paper could be improved, particularly in the clarity of figures and the overall structure of the content.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors clarify the specific contributions of the proposed algorithm and how it advances the state-of-the-art in multi-fidelity active learning?
- How does the proposed method perform when applied to continuous data, and what are the implications for its applicability in different data domains?
- Can the authors provide more details on the computational complexity of the proposed method and how it scales with the size of the data set and the number of fidelity levels?
- How does the method handle the selection of the number of samples to be sampled from each fidelity level, and what are the implications for the diversity and quality of the samples?
- Could the authors discuss the potential limitations of the proposed method and suggest areas for future research to address these limitations?
- In terms of the presentation, could the authors consider simplifying the figures and improving the clarity of the content to enhance the readability and understanding of the paper?
