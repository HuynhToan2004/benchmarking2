 **Summary:**
The paper explores the training dynamics of a simplified transformer model, focusing on a linear self-attention layer with a single head, trained on a histogram problem. This model, which lacks softmax, MLP, layer-normalization, and positional information, is analyzed through a reduction to 2-layer linear neural networks, specifically df-linear networks. The authors investigate the gradient flow training of this model and provide a theoretical analysis of the training dynamics. The paper's primary contribution is the reduction of the training dynamics of transformers to a simpler linear neural network model, which allows for a more straightforward analysis. However, the paper's scope is limited to this specific model and task, and it does not provide a broader application or comparison to more complex models or tasks.
