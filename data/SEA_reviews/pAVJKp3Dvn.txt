 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel differentiable framework for learning structured matrices in deep neural networks (DNNs), focusing on the Generalized Block-Low-Rank (GBLR) matrix format. This framework aims to address the computational complexity of DNNs by optimizing the structure of weight matrices, including low-rank and block-sparse matrices, through a differentiable parameterization in the frequency domain. The methodology includes the use of Gaussian-Dirichlet (Gaudi) masks to ensure differentiability and the application of proximal gradient descent for learning these structures. The approach is tested on various DNN architectures, showing potential improvements in computational efficiency and accuracy. However, concerns are raised about the clarity of the experimental results, the practical implications of the proposed method, and the theoretical underpinnings of the GBLR matrix format.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel and generalized matrix format, the Generalized Block-Low-Rank (GBLR) matrix, which is a significant contribution to the field.
- The methodology of using Gaussian-Dirichlet (Gaudi) masks for differentiability in the frequency domain is innovative and well-executed.
- The paper is well-written, making it accessible and easy to understand, with a clear structure and well-organized presentation.
- The experimental results show that the proposed method can improve computational efficiency and accuracy, potentially reducing the number of multiplications required.
- The paper includes a comprehensive theoretical analysis, including theorems and proofs, which supports the proposed methodology.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a thorough discussion on the practical implications and benefits of the proposed method, particularly in terms of its applicability to different types of DNNs and its impact on training and inference times.
- The experimental results are not clearly presented or analyzed, making it difficult to assess the effectiveness and significance of the proposed method.
- There is a lack of comparison with state-of-the-art methods in terms of computational efficiency and accuracy, which limits the ability to evaluate the true impact of the proposed method.
- The paper could benefit from more detailed explanations and examples of the GBLR matrix format and its implementation, especially in the context of different types of DNNs.
- The paper does not adequately address the scalability and efficiency of the proposed method, particularly in larger or more complex DNNs.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors clarify the practical implications and benefits of the proposed method, especially in terms of its impact on training and inference times?
- How does the proposed method compare to other state-of-the-art methods in terms of computational efficiency and accuracy?
- Can the authors provide more detailed examples and explanations of the GBLR matrix format and its implementation in different types of DNNs?
- How does the proposed method handle the scalability and efficiency issues in larger or more complex DNNs?
- Could the authors provide more detailed experimental results, including the training and inference times, to better assess the effectiveness of the proposed method?
