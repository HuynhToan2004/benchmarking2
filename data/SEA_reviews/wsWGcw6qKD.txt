 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel teacher training method for knowledge distillation, termed _SoTeacher_, which focuses on improving student performance by training the teacher to approximate the true label distribution of its training data. This approach contrasts with traditional methods that optimize teacher performance, often leading to suboptimal student performance. The method incorporates Lipschitz and consistency regularization to ensure the teacher's feature extractor approximates the true label distribution, thereby enhancing student accuracy. Theoretical analysis and empirical results demonstrate the effectiveness of this approach, showing consistent improvements across various datasets and knowledge distillation algorithms.

**Strengths:**
- The paper provides a novel theoretical analysis of the teacher training process in knowledge distillation, which is both rigorous and insightful.
- The proposed _SoTeacher_ training method is simple, easy to implement, and empirically validated through extensive experiments across different datasets and knowledge distillation algorithms.
- The methodology is well-explained, with clear theoretical foundations and practical implications, making it accessible and understandable.
- The results show that _SoTeacher_ consistently improves student accuracy, indicating its effectiveness in real-world applications.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper's theoretical analysis assumes an idealized setting that may not align with practical scenarios, such as the assumption of a Lipschitz-continuous feature extractor which might not hold in real-world applications.
- The experimental results are not compelling, with marginal improvements observed in some cases, and the method's performance appears to be limited by the complexity of the teacher model and the dataset used.
- The paper's theoretical analysis is limited to the LDA model, which might not generalize well to other data distributions, and the assumptions made in the theoretical analysis are not adequately justified or discussed.
- There is a lack of discussion on the limitations of the proposed method, which could help in understanding its scope and applicability.
- The paper could benefit from a more detailed discussion on how the theoretical findings translate into practical improvements, particularly in terms of how the methodology addresses the challenges of teacher training in knowledge distillation.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors provide a more detailed discussion on the assumptions made in the theoretical analysis, particularly how these assumptions translate into practical implications for the methodology?
- How does the method perform on larger datasets such as ImageNet, and what are the implications of using different feature extractors?
- Can the authors clarify the discrepancy between the theoretical analysis and the practical implementation, especially regarding the assumptions made in the theoretical analysis?
- How does the method handle the complexity of the teacher model, and what are the implications of using different feature extractors?
- Could the authors provide a more detailed discussion on the limitations of the proposed method, and how these limitations might affect its applicability in different scenarios?
