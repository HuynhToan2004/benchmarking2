 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel approach to estimating longitudinal treatment effects using ordinary differential equations (ODEs) instead of traditional neural networks. The method, referred to as INSITE, involves discovering patient-specific ODEs from observed trajectories, which are then fine-tuned to estimate individual treatment effects. This approach aims to address the interpretability and irregular sampling challenges in longitudinal data analysis. The paper also discusses the identification assumptions required for this method and compares it with existing methods using synthetic datasets. Despite its innovative approach, the paper has been critiqued for its assumptions, lack of real data validation, and potential over-reliance on strong assumptions.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces an innovative approach to estimating treatment effects using ODEs, which is a novel application of this methodology.
- The methodology is well-presented, with clear explanations of the assumptions and methodologies used, and the paper is generally well-written.
- The paper provides a comprehensive comparison of the assumptions used in this study with those in existing literature, which aids in understanding the applicability and limitations of the proposed method.
- The approach offers a potentially more interpretable model compared to traditional neural network-based methods, which could be advantageous in certain applications.
- The paper is well-organized and includes detailed discussions on the methodology, assumptions, and comparisons with existing methods.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a clear justification for the choice of ODEs over other methods, such as neural networks, which could also model longitudinal data.
- The assumptions used in the method are strong and may not hold in all real-world scenarios, which could limit the generalizability of the findings.
- The paper does not provide a detailed discussion on the identification assumptions required for the method to work effectively, which could be crucial for understanding the method's limitations.
- The method's performance is not convincingly demonstrated, with results showing only marginal improvements over existing methods and no real data validation.
- The paper lacks a detailed discussion on the computational complexity and scalability of the proposed method, which could be a significant concern for practical applications.
- The paper does not adequately address the potential biases introduced by the method, such as those related to the assumption of linearity in the ODEs.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors clarify the choice of ODEs over other methods and provide a more detailed justification for this choice?
- How do the authors ensure that the assumptions required for the method are met in real-world scenarios, and what are the implications if these assumptions are violated?
- Can the authors provide more details on the identification assumptions required for the method and how these assumptions affect the validity of the results?
- How does the method perform in scenarios where the assumptions do not hold, and what are the implications of these violations?
- Can the authors provide more details on the computational complexity and scalability of the proposed method, and how does it compare to existing methods in terms of efficiency and practicality?
- How does the method handle potential biases introduced by the assumptions, such as the linearity assumption in the ODEs?
- Could the authors provide more detailed results on the method's performance, including comparisons with existing methods using real data?
