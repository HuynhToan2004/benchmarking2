 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel framework, LGPred, designed to adaptively generate linear predictors for time series forecasting. This framework decomposes time series into trend and seasonality components, processes them through tailored modules, and generates predictors accordingly. The LGPred model is evaluated across multiple datasets and shown to outperform existing state-of-the-art methods, including transformer-based models. The paper's approach is highlighted by its ability to handle long-term dependencies effectively and by its innovative use of a template predictor with a bottleneck architecture to enhance computational efficiency. However, concerns are raised about the novelty of the method, the lack of comprehensive comparisons with existing models, and the potential for overfitting due to the complexity of the model.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, with clear and easy-to-follow content, and the methodology is well-explained.
- The proposed framework, LGPred, is innovative and achieves state-of-the-art performance across six datasets, demonstrating its effectiveness.
- The paper provides a detailed description of the LGPred framework, including its design choices and components, which are well-explained.
- The experiments conducted are thorough and include a range of benchmarks, showing that LGPred outperforms existing methods in most cases.
- The paper addresses the long-term time series forecasting problem, which is crucial for many real-world applications, and proposes a novel approach to tackle this challenge.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed discussion on the limitations of the proposed method, which could provide a more balanced view of its capabilities and potential areas for improvement.
- The related work section is not comprehensive, and there is a lack of discussion on how the proposed method compares to existing models in terms of computational efficiency, scalability, and other relevant metrics.
- The paper could benefit from more extensive comparisons with other models, including those that do not rely on time series decomposition or linear predictors, to provide a more robust evaluation of the proposed method.
- There is a lack of discussion on the generalizability of the method to different types of time series, such as those with non-stationary or chaotic dynamics.
- The paper does not include a discussion on the limitations of the proposed method, which could provide a more balanced view of its capabilities and potential areas for improvement.
- The experimental results are not thoroughly discussed, and there is a lack of detailed analysis on the performance of the model on different types of time series and under various conditions.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors provide a more detailed comparison of the computational efficiency and scalability of LGPred with other models, especially in terms of parameter count and training time?
- How does LGPred perform on time series with non-stationary or chaotic dynamics? Have there been any experiments conducted to assess its performance in such scenarios?
- Could the authors elaborate on the choice of using a fully connected network for the trend component and a convolutional neural network for the seasonality component? What specific advantages do these architectures offer over other possible choices?
- Given the complexity of the model, how does the paper address the risk of overfitting, especially in long-term forecasting scenarios?
- The paper could benefit from a more detailed discussion on the limitations of the proposed method. Could the authors provide a more comprehensive analysis of these limitations and potential solutions for future work?
- How does the model perform on different types of time series, such as those with high noise or different statistical properties? Have there been any experiments conducted to assess its performance under such conditions?
