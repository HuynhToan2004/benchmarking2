 **Summary:**
The paper explores data augmentation in deep reinforcement learning (DRL) by analyzing the effects of different data augmentation techniques on the variance of Q-targets and critic losses. It introduces a novel regularization term called tangent prop, which is adapted from computer vision and applied in DRL to improve sample efficiency and generalization. The authors propose a generic algorithm that integrates data augmentation into actor-critic methods and conduct extensive experiments to demonstrate the effectiveness of their approach. The paper is praised for its comprehensive literature review, theoretical analysis, and empirical validation, although concerns are raised about the novelty and the depth of the theoretical contributions.

**Strengths:**
- The paper provides a comprehensive literature review on data augmentation in deep reinforcement learning (DRL), which is beneficial for understanding the current state of the field.
- The authors propose a novel regularization term called tangent prop, which is adapted from computer vision and applied in DRL to improve sample efficiency and generalization.
- The theoretical analysis of the variance of Q-targets and critic losses under different data augmentation techniques is insightful and helps in understanding the effects of different components.
- The empirical validation is extensive, covering a wide range of environments in the DeepMind Control Suite, and demonstrates the effectiveness of the proposed method in improving sample efficiency and generalization.
- The paper is well-written, making it easy to follow, and the theoretical analysis is supported by extensive empirical studies.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a thorough comparison with the latest state-of-the-art data augmentation methods in DRL, which could have provided a clearer picture of the method's position in the current landscape.
- The theoretical analysis, while insightful, is limited in scope and depth, and some of the theoretical results are already known in the literature.
- The paper does not adequately address the limitations of the proposed method, such as the potential negative effects of data augmentation on sample efficiency and generalization.
- The paper could benefit from a more detailed explanation of the experimental setup, including the hyperparameters used and the selection process for these parameters.
- The paper could be improved by providing more detailed visualizations, such as t-SNE plots, to better illustrate the effects of data augmentation on the feature space.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors clarify how the proposed method compares with the latest state-of-the-art data augmentation methods in DRL?
- What is the rationale behind the choice of hyperparameters, especially in the experimental setup?
- How does the proposed method handle the potential negative effects of data augmentation on sample efficiency and generalization?
- Could the authors provide more detailed visualizations, such as t-SNE plots, to better illustrate the effects of data augmentation on the feature space?
- What are the main differences between the proposed method and other existing methods, such as SVEA, and how does it address the limitations of these methods?
