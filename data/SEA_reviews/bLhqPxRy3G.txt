 **Summary:**
The paper investigates the application of overparametrized neural networks to solve linear programming (LP) problems, focusing on the use of diagonal linear networks and their implicit biases. It demonstrates that gradient descent with squared L2-norm of the slack variable optimization leads to solutions for entropically regularized LP problems. The paper provides convergence guarantees for both discrete-time and continuous-time dynamics, showing that both exhibit a linear rate of convergence under mild assumptions. It also explores the influence of initialization on the convergence of gradient descent to specific solutions and compares its results with mirror descent and the Sinkhorn algorithm.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, with clear exposition and a comprehensive literature review that effectively places the work within the current research landscape.
- The authors demonstrate the convergence of gradient descent (GD) on a reparametrized LP problem, showing that the limit of GD on a quadratic reparametrized LP is an approximate solution of the original LP.
- The paper includes rigorous proofs for convergence guarantees of both discrete-time and continuous-time dynamics, which are crucial for understanding the behavior of GD in solving LP problems.
- The analysis of the paper is robust, with a detailed examination of the influence of initialization on the convergence of GD to specific solutions, which is a significant contribution to the field.
- The paper also compares the results of GD with those of mirror descent and the Sinkhorn algorithm, providing a comprehensive evaluation of the proposed methods.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper's scope is somewhat limited as it primarily focuses on the use of overparametrized neural networks for solving LP problems, which may not be of significant interest to the broader ICLR community.
- The results presented in the paper, while interesting, are not surprising, as similar phenomena have been observed in previous works.
- The paper lacks a discussion on the limitations of the proposed method, which is crucial for understanding the practical applicability and potential drawbacks of the proposed approach.
- The paper could benefit from a more detailed comparison with existing methods, such as the Sinkhorn algorithm, to highlight the advantages and disadvantages of the proposed approach.
- The paper's technical contributions are somewhat limited, as the proof techniques and results are similar to those in previous works, which might not be sufficiently novel to warrant publication in a top-tier venue like ICLR.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors elaborate on the choice of using diagonal linear networks in solving LP problems and how this choice impacts the results?
- How does the proposed method compare with other recent works on reparametrized GD, particularly in terms of the convergence rate and the dependence on initialization?
- Is there a possibility to extend the analysis to include other types of linear programs, such as those with negative costs or different regularizations?
- Could the authors provide a more detailed discussion on the limitations of their proposed method and how these might affect its practical applicability?
- How does the proposed method compare with other existing methods for solving LP problems, such as those based on mirror descent or other optimization techniques?
