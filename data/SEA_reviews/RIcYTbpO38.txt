 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel data augmentation method called Motion Coherent Augmentation (MCA) for video recognition tasks. MCA leverages the observation that hue jittering, which is typically detrimental to image recognition, can be beneficial in video recognition by focusing on motion patterns rather than static appearances. The method involves a SwapMix operation to modify video sample appearances and Variation Alignment to address distribution shifts. The effectiveness of MCA is demonstrated through experiments on various video recognition tasks, showing consistent improvements over existing methods. The paper also discusses the practical implications of MCA, including its potential to reduce training time and memory usage compared to traditional methods.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-organized, making it easy to follow and understand.
- The proposed Motion Coherent Augmentation (MCA) is simple, effective, and can be integrated with other data augmentation techniques to improve video recognition performance.
- The method is supported by comprehensive experiments on multiple video recognition tasks and different model architectures, demonstrating its effectiveness.
- The paper includes a thorough analysis of the proposed method, including ablation studies, which help in understanding the contributions of different components.
- The proposed method is efficient in terms of computation and memory usage, making it practical for real-world applications.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed discussion on the limitations of MCA, which could help in understanding its scope and applicability.
- The experimental results are not sufficiently robust, with some results showing inconsistencies or marginal improvements over existing methods.
- There is a lack of comparison with the most recent video recognition methods, which could affect the perceived novelty and relevance of the proposed method.
- The paper could benefit from more detailed comparisons with other data augmentation methods, particularly those that also focus on motion information in videos.
- The paper could be improved by providing more detailed explanations of the mathematical notations and equations used, which could enhance clarity and understanding.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors provide more details on the limitations of MCA and its applicability in different scenarios?
- How does MCA perform when applied to other video recognition tasks beyond those tested?
- Could the authors clarify the experimental setup, particularly the inconsistencies noted in the results and the choice of baselines?
- How does the proposed method compare with other data augmentation methods that also focus on motion information in videos?
- Could the authors provide more detailed explanations of the mathematical notations and equations used in the paper?
