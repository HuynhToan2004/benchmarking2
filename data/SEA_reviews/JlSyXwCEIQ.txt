 **Summary:**
The paper introduces CodeIt, a novel program synthesis approach that leverages a higher level of abstraction through a domain-specific language (DSL) to solve tasks in the Abstraction and Reasoning Corpus (ARC). CodeIt iteratively samples new programs from a language model policy and learns from the outcomes, utilizing hindsight relabeling and program mutation. This method achieves state-of-the-art performance on the ARC benchmark, demonstrating the effectiveness of the approach. The paper is well-written, clear, and provides a comprehensive evaluation, although it lacks detailed comparisons with other recent works and a broader set of benchmarks.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, clear, and easy to follow, with a clear description of the methodology and a comprehensive evaluation.
- The proposed method is novel and effective, achieving state-of-the-art performance on the ARC benchmark, demonstrating the effectiveness of the approach.
- The paper provides a detailed evaluation of the proposed method, including ablation studies that demonstrate the importance of different components of the method.
- The use of a domain-specific language (DSL) allows for a higher level of abstraction, which is beneficial for solving complex tasks.
- The paper is original in its approach to program synthesis, using a combination of language model policies, hindsight relabeling, and program mutation, which is novel and effective.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed comparison with other recent works, particularly those that also use domain-specific languages for program synthesis.
- There is a lack of discussion on the limitations of the proposed method, which is crucial for understanding its applicability and potential drawbacks.
- The paper could benefit from a broader set of benchmarks to demonstrate the generalizability of the approach beyond the ARC benchmark.
- The paper does not adequately address the scalability of the proposed method to other domains or tasks, which could limit its practical applicability.
- The paper lacks a discussion on the ethical implications of the proposed method, which is an important consideration for any AI system.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors provide a more detailed comparison with other recent works, particularly those that also use domain-specific languages for program synthesis?
- How does the proposed method perform on other benchmarks beyond the ARC benchmark?
- Can the authors provide more details on the limitations of the proposed method and how these limitations affect the practical applicability of the approach?
- How does the proposed method compare to other recent works in terms of performance and scalability?
- Could the authors discuss the ethical implications of the proposed method and how these implications were considered in the development of the system?
