 **Summary:**
The paper addresses Bayesian bilevel optimization, focusing on the convergence of hyperparameters and model parameters in a nested optimization framework. It provides theoretical guarantees for the convergence of both inner and outer levels, modeling the excess risk of inner-level SGD as noise during Bayesian optimization. The paper proposes a new theoretical analysis that models the excess risk of inner-level SGD as evaluation noise during Bayesian optimization, establishing sublinear regret bounds for Bayesian bilevel optimization using the EI function. It also introduces adaptable balancing coefficients for the UCB acquisition function, which allows for fewer SGD steps while maintaining convergence. The paper is well-written and provides a comprehensive theoretical analysis, although it lacks empirical validation and assumes strong convexity, which may limit its applicability to more complex, non-convex scenarios.

**Strengths:**
- The paper provides a comprehensive theoretical analysis of the convergence guarantees for Bayesian bilevel optimization, which is a significant contribution to the field.
- It models the excess risk of inner-level SGD as noise during Bayesian optimization, which is a novel approach that bridges the analytical frameworks of Bayesian optimization and bilevel optimization.
- The paper establishes sublinear regret bounds for Bayesian bilevel optimization using the EI function, which is a notable theoretical contribution.
- The introduction of adaptable balancing coefficients for the UCB acquisition function allows for fewer SGD steps while maintaining convergence, which is a practical application of the theoretical results.
- The paper is well-written and easy to follow, with clear and concise explanations of the theoretical results and their implications.

**Weaknesses:**
