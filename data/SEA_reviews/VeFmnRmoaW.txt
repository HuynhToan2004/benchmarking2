 **Summary:**
The paper introduces MetroGNN, a graph-based reinforcement learning (RL) framework aimed at solving complex Markov Decision Processes (MDPs) in urban metro network expansion. The framework utilizes a novel graph neural network (GNN) and an attentive policy network with action masks to learn representations for urban regions and select new metro stations. The GNN encodes complicated features into unified graphical representations, while the policy network selects new stations based on these representations. The approach is evaluated on real-world urban data from Beijing and Changsha, showing substantial improvements in OD flow satisfaction compared to state-of-the-art methods. Despite its innovative approach, the paper has been critiqued for its lack of clarity in problem formulation and the absence of significant baseline comparisons.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel graph-based RL framework for solving complex MDPs, which is particularly relevant for addressing the challenging geometrical CO problem of metro network expansion.
- The proposed GNN model effectively unifies complicated features and learns effective representations for urban regions, which is crucial for metro network expansion.
- The paper presents a novel graph neural network and an attentive policy network with an action mask, which significantly enhances the search for the solution space and avoids infeasible solutions.
- Extensive experiments are conducted on real-world urban data of Beijing and Changsha, demonstrating the effectiveness of the proposed approach in improving OD flow satisfaction by over 30% compared to state-of-the-art methods.
- The paper is well-organized, easy to follow, and provides clear explanations of the methodology, making it accessible to a broad audience.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks clarity in problem formulation, particularly in the definition of the MDP and the action space, which is crucial for understanding the methodology.
- The novelty of the proposed GNN model is questionable, as similar models have been previously used in other contexts.
- The paper does not provide sufficient details on the training process, such as the number of epochs and the specifics of the training data.
- The baseline comparisons are not strong enough, and the paper does not adequately address the scalability of the proposed method, especially in larger or more complex scenarios.
- The paper could benefit from a more detailed discussion on the limitations of the proposed method and how it might perform in real-world applications.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors clarify the problem formulation, particularly the MDP definition and the action space, to enhance understanding of the methodology?
- How does the proposed GNN model differ from existing models, and what specific advantages does it offer over previous models?
- Could the authors provide more details on the training process, such as the number of epochs and the specifics of the training data?
- How does the proposed method perform in scenarios with a larger number of nodes or more complex constraints?
- Could the authors elaborate on the limitations of the proposed method and discuss potential improvements or modifications to address these limitations?
