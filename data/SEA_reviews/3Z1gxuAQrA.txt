 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel method named Positional Skip-wise Training (PoSE) for efficiently extending the context window of large language models (LLMs) without the need for full-length fine-tuning. This method involves partitioning the original context window into several chunks, adjusting position indices, and employing different skipping bias terms to manipulate these indices. The PoSE method is evaluated across various datasets and tasks, showing promising results in terms of memory and time efficiency. It is noted that the method is compatible with other RoPE-based LLMs and can potentially support extremely long contexts. However, the paper is criticized for its limited evaluation on only one model and a lack of comprehensive comparison with other methods.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, making it easy to follow and understand the proposed method.
- The proposed method, PoSE, is simple yet effective, requiring only the original context size for fine-tuning, which significantly reduces memory and time overhead.
- PoSE is compatible with all RoPE-based LLMs and can potentially support extremely long contexts, limited only by memory usage in inference.
- The method has been evaluated on various datasets and tasks, demonstrating its effectiveness and potential for extending context window sizes.
- The paper provides a clear and detailed explanation of the method, including the use of position interpolation and the adjustment of position indices.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a comprehensive comparison with other methods, particularly in terms of memory and time efficiency.
- The evaluation is limited to only one model (LLaMA), which limits the generalizability of the findings.
- The paper does not provide a detailed comparison of the computational cost of PoSE with other methods, such as full-length fine-tuning and RandPos.
- The methodology section is not clear in some parts, particularly the explanation of the training dataset and the sampling strategy for the length and skipping bias term.
- The paper does not provide a detailed analysis of the performance degradation of PoSE-extended models on the original context window, which could be crucial for understanding the method's effectiveness.
- The paper lacks a detailed discussion on the potential limitations or drawbacks of the proposed method.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors provide a more detailed comparison of the computational cost of PoSE with other methods, including full-length fine-tuning and RandPos?
- How does the proposed method compare in terms of memory and time efficiency with other methods, particularly in scenarios where the context window size is very large?
- Could the authors provide a detailed analysis of the performance degradation of PoSE-extended models on the original context window?
- How does the sampling strategy for the length and skipping bias term affect the performance of the model?
- Could the authors provide more details on the training dataset used in the experiments?
- How does the proposed method perform when applied to other models, such as Llama2 or GPT-J?
- Could the authors discuss the limitations or potential drawbacks of the proposed method in more detail?
