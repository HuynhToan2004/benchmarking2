 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel approach to machine unlearning in image-to-image generative models, focusing on the efficient removal of unwanted data samples while preserving the performance of retained samples. The methodology employs a combination of L2-loss and mutual information to optimize the unlearning process, demonstrating its effectiveness across various generative models like MAE, VQ-GAN, and diffusion models. The approach is tested on datasets such as ImageNet-1K and Places-365, showing minimal performance degradation on retained samples and significant forgetting of unwanted samples. The paper is well-received for its innovative approach, clarity in presentation, and robust experimental validation.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is the first to explore machine unlearning in image-to-image generative models, addressing a significant gap in the literature.
- The proposed method is well-motivated, theoretically grounded, and supported by rigorous empirical studies.
- The paper is clearly written, making the content accessible and easy to follow, with a well-structured presentation of the methodology and results.
- The experimental results are robust and demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed method across various generative models and datasets.
- The paper provides a novel approach to machine unlearning that is applicable to different generative models, including MAE, VQ-GAN, and diffusion models.
- The paper includes a thorough analysis of the proposed method, including an ablation study, which supports the claims made and contributes to the understanding of the method's effectiveness.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed discussion on the computational complexity and time efficiency of the proposed method, which could be crucial for practical applications.
- There is a need for more comparative analysis with existing machine unlearning methods, particularly those that have been adapted for image-to-image generative models.
- The paper does not adequately address the potential societal impacts of the proposed method, which could be significant given the application in generative models.
- The paper's methodology relies heavily on the availability of the retain set, which might not be practical in all scenarios.
- The paper's claims regarding the novelty of the approach are not convincingly supported, as similar methods have been previously proposed in other contexts.
- The paper does not sufficiently discuss the limitations of the proposed method, which could affect the generalizability and applicability of the findings.

**Questions:**
- How does the computational complexity and time efficiency of the proposed method compare to other machine unlearning methods, particularly those adapted for image-to-image generative models?
- Could you provide more details on the training time and computational resources required for the proposed method?
- How does the proposed method perform when applied to other types of generative models, such as GANs or other diffusion models?
- In the context of machine unlearning, how does the proposed method ensure that the retained samples are not biased towards specific classes or characteristics?
- Can you clarify the role and impact of the Gaussian noise in the unlearning process, particularly in relation to the forget set?
- How does the proposed method compare to other methods that have been adapted for machine unlearning in generative models, such as those discussed in related works?
- Could you provide more details on the practical implications of the method, especially in scenarios where the retain set is not available?
