 **Summary:**
The paper introduces PINNacle, a comprehensive benchmarking tool for evaluating Physics-Informed Neural Networks (PINNs) across various Partial Differential Equations (PDEs). PINNacle includes a diverse dataset of 20 PDEs from different domains, each presenting unique challenges such as complex geometry, multi-scale phenomena, nonlinearity, and high dimensionality. It also incorporates a toolbox of 10 state-of-the-art PINN methods for systematic evaluation, providing insights into their strengths and weaknesses. The paper aims to fill a gap in the literature by offering a standardized means of assessing performance and guiding future research.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a comprehensive benchmarking tool, PINNacle, which provides a diverse dataset and a user-friendly toolbox for evaluating the performance of PINNs across various PDEs.
- The dataset encompasses a wide range of PDEs, each presenting unique challenges, and the toolbox incorporates a variety of state-of-the-art PINN methods, enhancing the comprehensiveness of the evaluation.
- The paper conducts extensive experiments and ablation studies, providing insights into the strengths and weaknesses of different PINN methods and guiding future research.
- The benchmarking tool, PINNacle, is expected to foster further research in PINNs and potentially serve as a valuable resource for the scientific community.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a clear distinction between the contributions of PINNacle and the DeepXDE library, particularly in terms of novelty and methodological advancements.
- The paper does not adequately address the limitations of PINNacle, such as the potential bias in favor of certain PINN methods due to their inclusion in the toolbox.
- The evaluation metrics used in the paper are not clearly defined or justified, which could affect the reliability and comparability of the results.
- The paper does not sufficiently discuss the challenges associated with the implementation and evaluation of PINNs, which could limit the practical applicability of the results.
- The paper does not include a detailed comparison with other existing benchmarks or a discussion on the computational efficiency of the proposed toolbox, which are crucial for assessing the effectiveness and practicality of PINNacle.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors clarify the specific contributions of PINNacle beyond what is already provided by the DeepXDE library?
- How does the paper address the potential bias in favor of certain PINN methods due to their inclusion in the toolbox?
- Can the authors provide a more detailed explanation of the evaluation metrics used and their rationale behind their selection?
- How does PINNacle handle the challenges associated with the implementation and evaluation of PINNs, particularly in complex or high-dimensional problems?
- Could the authors include a comparison with other existing benchmarks to assess the relative strengths and weaknesses of PINNacle?
- What are the computational requirements and efficiency of PINNacle, and how do these compare to other benchmarking tools?
