 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel framework, Fair Disentangled Domain Generalization (FDDG), aimed at addressing fairness-aware domain generalization by integrating both covariate and dependence shifts. It proposes a novel approach that generates synthetic data through a transformation model, enabling the learning of a fairness-aware invariant classifier. The method is validated across four benchmark datasets, demonstrating its effectiveness in achieving fairness and accuracy. However, the paper's assumptions and methodological choices, such as the use of a transformation model and the handling of fairness constraints, are critically evaluated. The reviewers expressed concerns about the assumptions' realism and the method's practical applicability in more complex or varied real-world scenarios.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel approach to fairness-aware domain generalization by integrating both covariate and dependence shifts, which is a significant contribution to the field.
- The proposed framework is well-structured and clearly articulated, with a logical flow that enhances understanding.
- The methodology is supported by comprehensive theoretical analysis and empirical evidence, demonstrating the effectiveness of the proposed approach.
- The paper provides a detailed algorithm and theoretical analysis, which are useful for understanding the proposed method.
- The use of a transformation model to generate synthetic data is a novel and interesting approach, which could be further explored in future research.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper makes strong assumptions, such as the existence of a transformation model and the disentanglement of content, sensitive, and style factors, which may not hold in practical scenarios.
- The paper lacks a detailed discussion on the limitations of the proposed method, particularly concerning the assumptions and their practical implications.
- The experimental evaluation is limited to specific datasets and does not adequately address the scalability of the proposed method to more complex or varied real-world scenarios.
- The paper's contribution is seen as incremental, with similarities to existing works such as MBDG and DDG-FC, which could lead to questions about the novelty of the proposed method.
- The paper does not sufficiently address the practical implications of the assumptions made, such as the availability of a transformation model and the disentanglement of factors.

**Questions:**
- How does the proposed method perform when the assumptions do not hold, and what are the implications for the accuracy and fairness of the model?
- Can the authors provide more detailed discussions on the limitations of the proposed method and how these might affect its practical applicability?
- How does the proposed method scale to more complex or varied real-world scenarios, and what are the potential challenges in applying the method in such settings?
- Could the authors provide more detailed experimental results, including the performance of the method on different types of data and under different conditions?
- How does the proposed method compare to existing works in terms of novelty and effectiveness, and what are the specific advantages of the proposed approach over existing methods?
