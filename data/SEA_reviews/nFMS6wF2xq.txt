 **Summary:**
The paper introduces ContextDiff, a novel conditional diffusion model that integrates cross-modal context into both the forward and reverse processes of text-guided visual generation and editing. By aligning text and visual samples at each timestep, ContextDiff aims to enhance the semantic alignment and consistency between the text and generated visual content. This model is designed to generalize across various diffusion models, including DDPMs and DDIMs, and has been evaluated on tasks such as text-to-image and text-to-video editing. The results show that ContextDiff outperforms existing methods in terms of semantic alignment and image quality, as evidenced by both quantitative metrics and user studies.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written and easy to follow, with a clear motivation and logical structure.
- The proposed method, ContextDiff, is innovative and effective, enhancing the semantic alignment between text and generated visual content, as demonstrated by both quantitative and qualitative results.
- The method is generalizable and can be applied to various diffusion models, including DDPMs and DDIMs, and has been tested across different tasks such as text-to-image and text-to-video editing.
- The paper includes thorough evaluations and user studies, which support the effectiveness of the proposed method.
- The integration of cross-modal context into both the forward and reverse processes is a novel approach that could lead to significant improvements in text-guided visual generation and editing.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed comparison with similar works, particularly those that also incorporate text-visual relationships into both forward and reverse processes.
- There is a lack of discussion on the limitations and potential negative societal impacts of the proposed method.
- The paper does not provide sufficient details on the training process, such as the number of training epochs, batch size, and learning rate, which could affect the reproducibility and comparability of the results.
- The generalization ability of the proposed method is not adequately demonstrated, particularly in scenarios where the text-to-image diffusion model is not pre-trained with the same dataset.
- The paper could benefit from more comprehensive visualizations and comparisons, particularly in the text-to-video editing task, where the visual quality of the results is crucial.

**Questions:**
- Could you provide more details on the training process, such as the number of training epochs, batch size, and learning rate?
- How does the proposed method compare with similar works that also integrate text-visual relationships into both forward and reverse processes?
- Can you clarify the differences between the forward and reverse processes in the proposed method, and how these differences affect the overall performance and generalization of the model?
- How does the proposed method handle scenarios where the text-to-image diffusion model is not pre-trained with the same dataset?
- Could you provide more visualizations and comparisons, particularly in the text-to-video editing task, to better demonstrate the effectiveness and generalization of the proposed method?
