 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel approach to class-incremental learning (CIL) by integrating a task-specific model within a shared network for all tasks, utilizing an out-of-distribution (OOD) detector for both in-distribution (IND) class prediction and OOD detection. This method, named TPLR, employs a likelihood ratio to predict task-ids, which is claimed to be superior to traditional OOD detection. The paper presents extensive experiments across various datasets, including CIFAR-10, CIFAR-100, and TinyImageNet, demonstrating the effectiveness of the proposed method. However, the novelty of the approach is questioned due to its similarity to existing methods, and the paper's theoretical contributions are seen as limited.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, clear, and easy to follow, with a clear motivation and a well-designed method.
- The proposed method achieves good performance on various datasets, including CIFAR-10, CIFAR-100, and TinyImageNet, as demonstrated by extensive experiments.
- The paper provides a detailed ablation study and analysis of the proposed method, which helps in understanding the contributions and limitations of the approach.
- The method is principled and theoretically sound, with a novel approach to task-id prediction using a likelihood ratio, which is well-explained and supported by theoretical analysis.
- The paper is organized well, with clear figures and tables that aid in understanding the experimental results and methodological details.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed discussion on the limitations and potential negative societal impacts of the proposed method.
- The novelty of the approach is questioned due to its similarity to existing methods, particularly those by Kim et al. (2022a, 2023).
- The paper does not adequately address the computational complexity and memory requirements of the proposed method, which could be a significant concern in real-world applications.
- The paper's theoretical contributions are seen as limited, with the main theoretical results being straightforward and not providing significant insights.
- The paper's methodology and experimental setup could benefit from more detailed explanations and justifications, particularly in the use of pre-trained models and the choice of hyperparameters.
- The paper's claims about the novelty and effectiveness of the proposed method are not convincingly supported by the experimental results, which show only marginal improvements over existing methods.
