 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel diffusion-based generative model for 3D scenes, utilizing a neural scene representation called IB-planes to efficiently represent large 3D scenes. This model enables fast, detailed reconstruction and generation of real-world 3D scenes, leveraging a denoising-diffusion framework that learns a prior over the novel 3D scene representation without requiring additional supervision signals. The model's effectiveness is demonstrated through experiments on multiple datasets, showcasing superior results in generation, novel view synthesis, and 3D reconstruction. Despite its strengths, the paper is criticized for its limited novelty and potential lack of significant advancement over existing methods.

**Strengths:**
- The paper presents a novel diffusion-based generative model for 3D scenes, which effectively leverages a neural scene representation called IB-planes to efficiently represent large 3D scenes.
- The model supports conditioning on varying numbers of images, including prior generation of 3D scenes and reconstruction from one or several images.
- The paper is well-written, clear, and easy to follow, making it accessible to a broad audience.
- The proposed method shows promising results in both single-image and multi-image settings, demonstrating its effectiveness in real-world applications.
- The introduction of IB-planes, a novel neural scene representation, allows for more accurate representation of large 3D scenes and supports dynamic allocation of capacity to capture details visible in each image.
- The paper is the first to introduce a denoising-diffusion framework to learn a prior over the novel 3D scene representation, enabling fast and unified architecture for conditioning on varying numbers of images.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed comparison with recent generative 3D reconstruction methods, which could have provided a clearer understanding of its advantages and limitations.
- The novelty of the method is limited, as it builds upon existing methods like NeRF and diffusion models, with incremental improvements that might not be seen as significant advancements.
- The paper does not provide a detailed analysis of the limitations of the proposed method, which could help in understanding its applicability and potential drawbacks.
- The paper's reliance on a single dataset (MVImgNet) for most of its experiments raises concerns about the generalizability of the results.
- The paper does not include a discussion on the limitations of the proposed method, which could help in understanding its applicability and potential drawbacks.
- The paper's approach to unconditional generation of 3D scenes might not be as practical as conditional generation, as it requires a large amount of data for training, which could be a barrier for adoption.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors provide a detailed comparison with other generative 3D reconstruction methods to better understand the advantages and limitations of their proposed method?
- How does the proposed method compare to other methods that use diffusion models for 3D scene generation, such as those mentioned in the paper?
- Can the authors provide more details on the training process, particularly how the number of views affects the results and how the model handles different types of data (e.g., RGB images versus depth maps)?
- How does the proposed method handle unseen view synthesis, and what are the potential limitations in this regard?
- Could the authors discuss the potential applications of the proposed method in fields such as virtual/augmented reality, and how it could be adapted for these applications?
- How does the proposed method compare in terms of training and inference times with other generative 3D reconstruction methods, and what are the implications of these differences for practical deployment?
