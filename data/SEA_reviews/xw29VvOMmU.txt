 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel approach to memory-efficient finetuning of pretrained language models, focusing on the use of low-rank and quantized components. The methodology involves decomposing pretrained matrices into low-rank and quantized components, with the quantized component fixed during finetuning and the low-rank component updated. This approach is claimed to enhance performance and reduce memory requirements, particularly useful for models with high parameter counts. The paper presents empirical evidence supporting these claims, although some reviewers express concerns about the novelty and the scope of the contributions.

**Strengths:**
- The paper addresses the significant issue of memory requirements in large language models (LLMs), which is crucial for practical applications.
- The proposed method of decomposing pretrained matrices into low-rank and quantized components is novel and could potentially improve the efficiency of LLM finetuning.
- The paper is well-written, making it easy to follow, and includes a comprehensive empirical study that supports the claims made.
- The approach of using quantization and low-rank decomposition to enhance the efficiency of LLM finetuning is innovative and has the potential to significantly reduce memory requirements.
- The paper includes a robust empirical study that demonstrates the effectiveness of the proposed method across various NLP tasks, showing consistent improvements over existing methods like QLoRA.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a clear differentiation from existing methods such as QLoRA and Omniquant, which also use similar approaches.
- The novelty of the paper is questioned, as the use of low-rank and quantized components in LLM finetuning is not a new concept.
- The paper does not adequately address the issue of quantization error and its impact on model performance, especially in low-bit regimes.
- The methodology section could benefit from more detailed explanations, particularly regarding the initialization of L1 and L2 matrices and the role of the Fisher information matrix in model adaptation.
- The paper does not sufficiently discuss the limitations of the proposed method, such as its applicability to other parameter-efficient finetuning methods and the potential for overfitting due to low-rank updates.
- The paper could benefit from a more thorough literature review and a clearer presentation of the method's contributions and its novel aspects.

**Questions:**
- Could you clarify how the proposed method differs from existing methods like QLoRA and Omniquant, particularly in terms of the initialization and updating of the low-rank and quantized components?
- How does the paper address the issue of quantization error, especially in low-bit regimes?
- Can you provide more details on the initialization of the L1 and L2 matrices, and how this affects the performance of the model?
- What is the role of the Fisher information matrix in model adaptation, and how does it influence the weighting of the reconstruction objective during matrix decomposition?
- How does the paper ensure that the model does not overfit due to low-rank updates, and what measures are in place to prevent this?
- Could you expand on the limitations of the proposed method and discuss potential future directions for research in this area?
