 **Summary:**
The paper investigates the generalization of training-based ChatGPT detection methods, focusing on the impact of various factors such as prompts, text length, topics, and language tasks. It introduces a new dataset, HC-Var, which includes human and ChatGPT texts with variety, and uses this dataset to study the generalization behaviors of training-based methods under distribution shifts. The paper also proposes a theoretical analysis to explain the phenomena observed in the experiments. Despite the comprehensive empirical study and the introduction of a new dataset, concerns are raised about the novelty and the depth of the theoretical analysis.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a new dataset, HC-Var, which is significant for the research community as it enhances the availability of resources for studying ChatGPT detection.
- The study on the generalization of training-based ChatGPT detection methods is comprehensive, providing a detailed analysis of the factors influencing detection performance.
- The paper is well-written, making the content accessible and easy to follow, which is crucial for a broad audience.
- The experiments conducted are thorough, covering various aspects of ChatGPT detection, and the results are convincingly presented.
- The paper includes a theoretical analysis that helps explain the observed phenomena, providing a deeper understanding of the mechanisms at play.
- The discussion on the limitations of the study is appreciated, as it acknowledges the scope of the research and encourages further exploration.

**Weaknesses:**
- The novelty of the paper is limited, as it primarily focuses on analyzing existing training-based methods and does not introduce new detection methods or techniques.
- The theoretical analysis provided is not sufficiently novel or insightful, as it is based on existing models like MAUVE and does not significantly advance the understanding of the problem.
- The paper's contributions are seen as incremental, primarily extending prior works by Guo et al. (2023) without significantly advancing the state of the art.
- The study is limited to ChatGPT detection, which might not generalize well to other large language models, such as LLaMA2.
- The paper could benefit from a more detailed discussion on the limitations of the proposed dataset, HC-Var, and the potential impact of these limitations on the study's conclusions.
- The paper's scope is somewhat narrow, focusing on ChatGPT detection and not exploring broader applications or implications of the findings.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors elaborate on the novelty of their theoretical analysis, particularly how it differs from or builds upon existing models like MAUVE?
- How does the study's focus on ChatGPT detection specifically contribute to a broader understanding of large language model detection in general?
- What are the implications of the findings for the development of more robust and generalizable detection methods for other large language models?
- Could the authors discuss the potential impact of the findings on the broader research community, particularly in terms of practical applications or future research directions?
- In light of the concerns regarding the dataset's limitations, how might these affect the study's conclusions, and what steps could be taken to mitigate these issues?
