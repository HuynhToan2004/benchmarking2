 **Summary:**
The paper introduces SparseDFF, a method for distilling semantic features from sparse RGBD views to enable one-shot learning of dexterous manipulations. This method leverages a lightweight feature refinement network and a point pruning mechanism to enhance feature consistency and reliability. It also employs a contrastive loss to optimize feature similarity between views. The proposed approach is evaluated on both rigid and deformable objects, demonstrating its effectiveness in grasping and manipulation tasks. However, the paper is critiqued for its limited novelty, lack of comprehensive baseline comparisons, and insufficient discussion on the generalization of the method across different scenarios and object categories.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, with clear and understandable explanations of the methodology and results.
- The use of sparse views for feature distillation is innovative, addressing the challenge of obtaining dense views in many scenarios.
- The methodology is simple, yet effective, with a lightweight feature refinement network and a point pruning mechanism that enhance feature consistency and reliability.
- The experiments are well-designed, showing that the proposed method is effective for grasping and manipulation tasks, particularly in scenarios with sparse views.
- The paper provides a clear and concise explanation of the method, making it easy to follow and understand.
- The method's ability to generalize to different object categories, such as the transfer from bowl to mug, demonstrates its potential applicability in real-world scenarios.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a comprehensive discussion on the generalization of the method across different scenarios and object categories.
- The novelty of the work is limited, as the methodology appears to be a combination of existing techniques without significant innovation.
- The paper does not compare the proposed method with sufficient baseline methods, particularly in the grasping task, which could have provided a more robust validation of the method's effectiveness.
- The evaluation metrics used in the paper are not clearly defined, which could affect the interpretation of the results.
- The paper does not address the limitations of the method, which is crucial for understanding the scope and applicability of the proposed approach.
- The paper does not provide a detailed discussion on the challenges and limitations of the proposed method, which could hinder the understanding of its practical implications and potential improvements.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors provide a more detailed discussion on the generalization of the method across different scenarios and object categories?
- How does the method perform when the number of views increases?
- Is the method robust to different lighting conditions?
- Can the authors clarify the definition and role of the query points in the method?
- How does the method handle different object categories, especially those with significant differences in geometry and appearance?
- Can the authors provide a more detailed explanation of the role and impact of the feature pruning mechanism in the method?
- Can the authors provide a more detailed discussion on the limitations of the method and potential directions for future research?
