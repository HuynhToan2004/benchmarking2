 **Summary:**
The paper explores the generation of turbulent flows in 3D using a diffusion model, aiming to bypass the need for long roll-outs in autoregressive models. It introduces a novel dataset and two metrics specifically designed for evaluating turbulent flows. The model, trained using a U-Net architecture, is tested against two baselines, showing significant improvements in runtime and sample quality. The paper also discusses the challenges of predicting turbulent flows in 3D and proposes a generative model that directly learns the manifold of turbulent flow states. However, concerns were raised regarding the limited evaluation scope and the generalization of the model to different turbulent flow types.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel dataset of 3D turbulent flows, which is significant for the field and could be useful for other researchers.
- The authors propose two new metrics for evaluating turbulent flows, which are innovative and could be beneficial for the community.
- The paper is well-written, with clear explanations of the methodology and results.
- The approach of using a generative model to bypass the need for long roll-outs in autoregressive models for turbulent flow prediction is novel and could be of interest to the machine learning community.
- The paper provides a comprehensive evaluation of the proposed method, including comparisons with two baselines and a discussion on the advantages of the proposed method.
- The paper discusses the challenges of predicting turbulent flows in 3D and proposes a generative model that directly learns the manifold of turbulent flow states.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a discussion on the generalization of the model to different types of turbulent flows, which is a significant limitation given the variety of turbulent flow types.
- The evaluation scope is limited, with only two baselines considered, which may not fully demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed method.
- The paper does not discuss the limitations of the proposed method, which could help in understanding the scope and applicability of the model.
- The paper could benefit from a more detailed discussion on the computational resources required for training and inference, as well as the scalability of the model with different input sizes.
