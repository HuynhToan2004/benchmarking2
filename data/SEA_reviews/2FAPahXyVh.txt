 **Summary:**
The paper introduces OptiMUS, a novel approach that utilizes Large Language Models (LLMs) to formulate and solve Mixed Integer Linear Programming (MILP) problems from natural language descriptions. OptiMUS is designed to generate mathematical models, write and debug solver code, and create tests and solutions. The paper also introduces a new dataset, NLP4LP, which includes 52 LP and MILP problems annotated with solutions and code to check optimality. The methodology involves several stages, including data separation, formulation, code generation, testing, and revision, which are aimed at improving the accuracy and reliability of the solutions generated by LLMs. The paper evaluates the performance of OptiMUS using GPT-3.5 and GPT-4 models and demonstrates that it outperforms standard prompting strategies. However, the paper faces criticism for its limited dataset size and the potential for LLMs to be biased by the training data.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written and clear, making it easy to follow and understand.
- The proposed approach is novel and innovative, combining LLMs with traditional solvers to solve optimization problems, which is a significant advancement in the field.
- The dataset created for this study is comprehensive and includes a variety of LP and MILP problems, which can be useful for future research in the area.
- The paper is well-structured, with a clear introduction, methodology, and results sections, which help in understanding the research and its findings.
- The approach of using LLMs to formulate and solve MILP problems from natural language descriptions is a significant advancement in the field, and the paper provides a detailed methodology for this process.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a comparison with other state-of-the-art methods, which limits the evaluation of the proposed approach against existing solutions.
- The dataset used in the study is relatively small, which may not be sufficient to draw robust conclusions or to generalize the findings to larger or more complex problems.
- The paper does not discuss the potential biases that LLMs might have due to their training data, which could affect the reliability and accuracy of the solutions generated.
- The paper does not provide sufficient details on the time required for each stage of the proposed methodology, which is crucial for understanding the practical applicability and efficiency of the approach.
- The paper does not discuss the limitations of the approach, which could help in understanding the scope and applicability of the findings.

**Questions:**
- Could you clarify how the LLM determines the number of iterations required to solve a problem?
- How does the approach handle cases where the LLM generates incorrect or misleading tests, and what are the implications for the reliability of the solutions?
- What are the potential biases that LLMs might have due to their training data, and how do these biases affect the solutions generated by OptiMUS?
- How does the approach handle cases where the LLM fails to generate valid solutions, and what are the implications for the practical applicability of the approach?
- Can you provide more details on the time required for each stage of the proposed methodology, and how does this affect the practical applicability of the approach?
- How does the approach compare with other state-of-the-art methods, and what are the implications of using different solvers in the LLM-based approach?
