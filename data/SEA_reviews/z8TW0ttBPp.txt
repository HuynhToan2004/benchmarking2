 **Summary:**
The paper introduces MathCoder, a method that leverages GPT-4 to generate solutions for math problems, which are then used to train models like Llama-2 and CodeLlama. The approach involves a dataset construction process that interpolates math problems from GSM8K and MATH datasets, followed by a supervised fine-tuning and inference method that utilizes special tokens to differentiate between natural language, code, and execution results. The paper claims that MathCoder achieves state-of-the-art performance on various math benchmarks, including GSM8K and MATH, outperforming other open-source models. However, concerns are raised about the novelty and the direct transfer of GPT-4's capabilities to the trained models, which may not represent a significant advancement in model performance.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, clear, and easy to follow, with a clear motivation and a novel approach to using GPT-4 for dataset generation and model training.
- The proposed dataset, MathCodeInstruct, is comprehensive and well-constructed, with a focus on improving mathematical reasoning capabilities in large language models (LLMs).
- The methodology of integrating natural language, code, and execution results into open-source pre-trained LLMs is innovative and shows promising results in enhancing mathematical reasoning abilities.
- The paper provides a detailed analysis of the influence of problem interpolation and the effects of code execution, which adds depth to the understanding of the proposed method.
- The experimental results demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed method, particularly in improving the performance of open-source LLMs on math benchmarks.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a thorough comparison with other models like WizardMath and RFT, which could provide a more comprehensive evaluation of the proposed method.
- The use of GPT-4 for generating solutions and interpolating problems raises concerns about the novelty and the direct transfer of GPT-4's capabilities to the trained models.
- The paper does not adequately address the generalizability of the model to out-of-domain datasets, which could limit the practical applicability of the findings.
- There is a lack of detailed analysis on the effectiveness of the special tokens used for identifying natural language, code, and execution results, and the paper does not explore the potential of using these tokens to enhance model performance.
- The paper does not sufficiently discuss the limitations of the proposed method, such as the reliance on GPT-4 for data generation and the potential for the model to only solve specific types of math problems.

**Questions:**
- Could you clarify the differences between the proposed method and the WizardMath approach, particularly in terms of the integration of code and execution results?
- How does the proposed method compare to other open-source models like Galactica and Llama-1 RFT, especially in terms of performance and computational efficiency?
- What are the specific challenges in solving geometry problems, and how does the model handle these issues?
- Can you provide more details on the self-distillation process and how it affects the performance of the model?
- How do you ensure the quality of the solutions generated by GPT-4, and what steps are taken to verify the accuracy of these solutions?
- Could you elaborate on the use of special tokens for identifying natural language, code, and execution results, and how these tokens influence the model's performance?
