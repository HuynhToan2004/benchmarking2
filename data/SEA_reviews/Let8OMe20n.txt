 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel benchmark, TIA2, designed to assess the alignment between human judgment and reward models in text-to-image generation. The benchmark evaluates various state-of-the-art reward models and identifies issues such as overoptimization and misalignment with human preferences. To address these issues, the authors propose TextNorm, a method that calibrates reward models by normalizing scores across semantically different prompts. This approach aims to improve the alignment between the reward model and human judgment, thereby enhancing the quality and accuracy of text-to-image generation. The paper also includes a detailed analysis of the calibration of reward models and their impact on overoptimization, supported by both quantitative and qualitative results.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel benchmark, TIA2, which is a significant contribution to the field of text-to-image generation. This benchmark provides a comprehensive evaluation of various reward models and their alignment with human judgment, offering a valuable resource for future research.
- The proposed TextNorm method is innovative and effective, particularly in calibrating the confidence of reward models, which is crucial for improving the quality of text-to-image generation.
- The paper is well-written, clear, and easy to follow, making it accessible to a broad audience. The authors provide detailed explanations and examples, which aid in understanding the complex concepts and methodologies.
- The experimental results are robust and convincing, demonstrating the effectiveness of the TextNorm method in reducing overoptimization and improving the alignment between reward models and human judgment.
- The paper addresses a significant problem in the field of text-to-image generation, namely the overoptimization of reward models, which can lead to degraded model performance. The proposed method, TextNorm, offers a promising solution to this problem.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed discussion on the limitations of the proposed method, particularly in scenarios where it may not be effective or applicable.
- The methodology and its implementation, especially the use of ChatGPT for prompt generation and the selection of prompts, are not sufficiently detailed or justified.
- The paper does not sufficiently compare the proposed method with other existing methods, which could provide a more comprehensive understanding of its effectiveness and limitations.
- There is a lack of discussion on the generalizability of the findings and the methodology, which could affect the applicability of the results in different contexts or with different types of data.
- The paper does not provide sufficient details on the training and implementation of the TextNorm method, which could make it difficult for others to replicate or build upon the work.
- The paper does not adequately address the potential negative societal impacts of the proposed method, which could be significant given the potential applications in generating images from textual descriptions.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors provide more details on the selection and generation of contrastive prompts using ChatGPT? How were these prompts chosen, and what criteria were used to ensure their effectiveness?
- How does the TextNorm method perform when applied to different types of text-to-image generation models, such as those based on different architectures or trained on different datasets?
- What are the potential negative societal impacts of the proposed method, and how can these be mitigated or addressed?
- How does the TextNorm method compare with other existing methods for calibrating reward models or reducing overoptimization in text-to-image generation?
- Can the authors provide more details on the training and implementation of the TextNorm method, including the specifics of the training data, hyperparameters, and training algorithms?
- How generalizable is the TextNorm method across different types of text-to-image generation models and datasets?
- What are the potential limitations of the TextNorm method, and in what scenarios might it not be effective or applicable?
