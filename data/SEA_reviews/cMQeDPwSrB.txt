 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel method to quantify memorization in neural networks by evaluating the curvature of the loss function around training data points. This approach allows for the identification of memorized samples, which are critical for understanding dataset quality and model performance. The method is validated through experiments on various datasets, including MNIST, CIFAR-10, CIFAR-100, and ImageNet, demonstrating its effectiveness in detecting memorized samples and its correlation with existing metrics. Despite its innovative approach, the paper is critiqued for its lack of comprehensive baseline comparisons and its limited evaluation on more complex datasets like ImageNet.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel method for measuring memorization in deep neural networks by evaluating the curvature of the loss function around training samples, which is both innovative and relevant to the field.
- The method is scalable and efficient, requiring only the training of a single network, which is a significant advantage over traditional methods that might require extensive computational resources.
- The paper is well-written, making it easy to follow and understand, which enhances its readability and accessibility to a broader audience.
- The proposed metric is shown to be effective in identifying memorized samples, which is crucial for understanding dataset quality and model performance.
- The paper provides a detailed analysis of the method's effectiveness and its relationship with existing metrics, which helps in validating the proposed method.

**Weaknesses:**
