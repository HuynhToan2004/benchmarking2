 **Summary:**
The paper introduces M-BioBERTa, a modular transformer architecture designed for multimodal data integration, focusing on medical data such as genetics, demographics, laboratory results, and drug prescriptions. The model is evaluated using the UK Biobank dataset, demonstrating its effectiveness in handling diverse data types and predicting disease and drug burdens. Despite its innovative approach, the paper is criticized for its lack of novelty, insufficient comparisons with existing models, and unclear presentation. The reviewers also noted the absence of a baseline model and the need for more comprehensive evaluations and clearer methodological descriptions.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, easy to follow, and clearly presents the proposed model and its application to medical data.
- The model effectively integrates multiple data types, including genetics, demographics, laboratory results, and drug prescriptions, demonstrating its potential for predicting disease and drug burdens.
- The model's ability to handle missing data and its application to the UK Biobank dataset, one of the largest and most comprehensive biobanks, showcases its scalability and applicability to real-world scenarios.
- The paper provides a comprehensive evaluation of the model's performance, comparing it to various baseline models and demonstrating its superiority in certain tasks.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks novelty as it primarily builds upon existing models like RoBERTa and BERT, without introducing significant new methodologies or architectural innovations.
- The model's performance is not convincingly superior to existing models, as indicated by the lack of a comprehensive baseline comparison and the marginal improvements over simple models like logistic regression.
- The presentation of the paper is unclear, particularly in the methodology section, which lacks detailed explanations and formal mathematical definitions.
- The paper does not adequately address the issue of missing data in the datasets, which is crucial for the model's effectiveness.
- There is a lack of discussion on the societal impact of the model, which is essential for understanding the broader implications of the research.
- The paper does not sufficiently compare its model with other state-of-the-art models, which limits the assessment of its contribution to the field.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors clarify how the model handles missing data, particularly in the context of the UK Biobank dataset?
- How does the model compare to other state-of-the-art models in terms of performance and efficiency?
- Could the authors provide a more detailed explanation of the model's architecture, including the roles and interactions of the different components?
- What are the specific contributions of the model beyond existing models like RoBERTa, and how does it address the unique challenges of integrating diverse data types in medical applications?
- How does the model ensure the privacy and security of the sensitive medical data it processes?
- Can the authors discuss the potential societal impacts of their model, particularly in terms of healthcare outcomes and patient privacy?
