 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel test-time adversarial defense method called REAL, which utilizes entropy to enhance the robustness of neural networks against adversarial attacks. The method employs a two-stage self-adversarial rectification approach, involving entropy maximization followed by entropy minimization, to transform adversarial samples into mask samples and then into purified samples. The authors claim that this method can effectively generalize adversarial robustness by leveraging the properties of adversarial samples, particularly their low entropy despite high confidence misclassification. The method is evaluated on various datasets, including MNIST and CIFAR-10, showing improvements over existing methods in certain attack scenarios. However, the paper has been critiqued for its clarity, the necessity of its contributions, and the lack of comprehensive evaluation against a broader range of attacks and datasets.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel test-time adversarial defense method, REAL, which utilizes entropy to enhance the robustness of neural networks against adversarial attacks, focusing on the properties of adversarial samples.
- The method is well-motivated and theoretically grounded, with a focus on the entropy of adversarial samples, which is a significant aspect of adversarial sample rectification.
- The paper is well-written, with clear explanations of the methodology and the experimental results, which support the claims made.
- The proposed method achieves state-of-the-art performance in some attack scenarios, demonstrating its effectiveness in certain conditions.
- The paper provides a comprehensive evaluation of the proposed method against various adversarial attacks and datasets, including MNIST and CIFAR-10, which helps in validating the claims made.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks clarity in some sections, particularly in the descriptions of the entropy-based method and the experimental setup, which could confuse readers unfamiliar with the topic.
- The motivation behind the entropy-based method is not clearly articulated, and the paper does not adequately explain why this method is more effective than existing methods.
- The paper does not provide sufficient experimental evidence to support the claims made, such as the effectiveness of the method against a broader range of attacks and datasets.
- The method's reliance on auxiliary tasks for sample rectification may limit its generalizability and applicability to different types of neural networks.
- The paper does not sufficiently address the limitations of the method, such as the potential negative impact on clean sample accuracy and the generalizability of the findings across different types of neural networks.
- The paper's evaluation is limited to specific datasets and attack methods, which may not adequately represent the method's effectiveness in more diverse or complex attack scenarios.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors clarify the motivation behind the entropy-based method and how it addresses the specific challenges posed by adversarial samples?
- How does the proposed method perform against a broader range of attacks, including those that are not considered in the current evaluation?
- Could the authors provide more details on the experimental setup, such as the specifics of the auxiliary tasks used and the parameters of the adversarial attacks?
- How does the method handle different types of neural networks, such as those based on transformers, and what are the implications for their performance?
- Can the authors elaborate on the method's effectiveness in more complex or diverse attack scenarios, and how does it compare to other state-of-the-art defense methods?
- How does the method ensure that the improvements in robustness do not come at the expense of clean sample accuracy?
