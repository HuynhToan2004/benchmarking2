 **Summary:**
The paper explores the use of public data to enhance the privacy-utility trade-off in differentially private (DP) image generation, specifically focusing on Generative Adversarial Networks (GANs). The authors propose two methods, DP-MGE and DP-DRE, which model the private feature distribution using either a multivariate Gaussian or a density ratio estimator. These methods are evaluated against various benchmarks, showing that they can generate high-quality, photo-realistic images under DP constraints. The paper also discusses the challenges and potential solutions for handling different data distributions in the context of DP image generation.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel approach to using public data to enhance the privacy-utility trade-off in differentially private (DP) image generation, which is a significant contribution to the field.
- The proposed methods, DP-MGE and DP-DRE, are well-articulated and supported by a robust evaluation framework, showing state-of-the-art (SOTA) performance in terms of FID score and other metrics.
- The paper is well-written, making it accessible and easy to follow, which enhances its readability and comprehensibility.
- The use of a pre-trained IC-GAN to generate high-quality, photo-realistic images under DP constraints is a notable achievement, demonstrating the practical applicability of the proposed methods.
- The paper addresses a critical issue in DP image generation by relaxing the assumption that public data comes from the same distribution as private data, which is a significant advancement in the field.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a comprehensive discussion on the limitations of the proposed methods, particularly the assumptions made and their implications.
- The methodology, while innovative, does not significantly deviate from existing approaches, such as the use of public data for training GANs, which might limit its novelty.
- There are concerns about the privacy guarantees of the methods, particularly in the context of DP-MGE, which uses a multivariate Gaussian to model the private feature distribution, which might not adequately capture the complexity of the private data distribution.
- The paper could benefit from a more detailed comparison with existing methods, such as DP-GAN-FT and DP-MEPF, to better understand the advantages and disadvantages of the proposed methods.
- The paper does not adequately address how the proposed methods perform under different privacy levels, which is crucial for understanding their practical applicability.
- The paper could be improved by providing more detailed experimental results, including visualizations of generated images and a more comprehensive evaluation of the methods across different privacy levels.

**Questions:**
- How do the proposed methods perform under different privacy levels, and what are the implications for image quality and privacy?
- Can the authors provide more detailed visualizations of the generated images, including a comparison with non-private IC-GAN, to better understand the performance of the proposed methods?
- How does the paper address the potential issues of overfitting in the discriminator, especially in the context of the DP-DRE method?
- Could the authors clarify the assumptions made in the DP-MGE and DP-DRE methods, particularly regarding the support of the public data distribution containing that of the private?
- How does the paper ensure that the generated images are representative of the private data distribution, especially when the support of the public and private distributions significantly overlap?
- Could the authors provide a more detailed comparison with existing methods, such as DP-GAN-FT and DP-MEPF, to better understand the advantages and disadvantages of the proposed methods?
