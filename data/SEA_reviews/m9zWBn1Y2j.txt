 **Summary:**
The paper introduces PsiDiff, a novel diffusion model for ligand conformation generation that incorporates both ligand and target information, aiming to enhance the accuracy of ligand conformations in drug discovery. PsiDiff utilizes graph prompt learning to bridge the gap between ligand and target graphs, allowing for the capture of ligand-target pairwise interactions at each diffusion step. The model is evaluated against existing methods such as GeoDiff and TankBind, showing significant improvements in alignment RMSD. The methodology includes a complex branch network to model interactions between ligands and targets, and the use of an energy function to guide the diffusion process. The paper also addresses rotational and translational invariance in the diffusion process and provides a detailed theoretical framework for the model's operation.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel method for ligand conformation generation that integrates both ligand and target information, which is a significant advancement in the field of drug discovery.
- The use of graph prompt learning to bridge the gap between ligand and target graphs is a creative and effective approach that allows for the capture of ligand-target pairwise interactions at each diffusion step.
- The paper is well-written, clear, and easy to follow, making it accessible to a broad audience.
- The experimental results demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed method, showing improvements over existing methods in terms of alignment RMSD.
- The theoretical framework provided for the model's operation is detailed and well-explained, enhancing the paper's credibility and reproducibility.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a comprehensive evaluation of the method's performance in terms of other metrics such as RMSD and binding affinity, which are crucial for assessing the practical utility of the method in drug discovery.
- The complexity of the model, particularly the introduction of a complex branch network to model interactions between ligands and targets, raises concerns about the scalability and computational efficiency of the method, especially for larger ligands.
- The paper does not sufficiently compare the proposed method with other existing methods that also incorporate target information, which could provide a more robust evaluation of the method's effectiveness.
- There is a lack of clarity in some sections of the paper, particularly in the explanation of the energy function and its role in guiding the diffusion process.
- The paper does not adequately address the issue of rotational and translational invariance in the diffusion process, which is crucial for ensuring the model's robustness and generalizability.

**Questions:**
- Can you provide more details on how the energy function is defined and how it guides the diffusion process?
- How does the method handle the issue of rotational and translational invariance in the diffusion process?
- Could you clarify the role of the complex branch network in modeling interactions between ligands and targets, and how it impacts the scalability and computational efficiency of the method?
- How does the method perform in terms of RMSD and binding affinity, and what are the implications of these metrics for the practical utility of the method in drug discovery?
- Could you provide more details on the experimental setup, particularly how the target information is represented and how it affects the model's performance?
- How does the method handle the issue of missing atoms in the ligand graph, and what are the implications of this for the model's accuracy and reliability?
