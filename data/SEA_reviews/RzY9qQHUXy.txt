 **Summary:**
The paper explores the challenges of data augmentation in long-tailed learning, particularly focusing on the disparity in augmentation effectiveness across different classes. It proposes a novel method, Dynamic Optional Data Augmentation (DODA), which dynamically adjusts the augmentation strategies based on the specific needs of each class. The method is designed to address the imbalance in augmentation, which is a significant issue in long-tailed learning. The paper provides a theoretical analysis and empirical evidence, demonstrating the effectiveness of DODA through extensive experiments across various datasets. Despite some concerns about the novelty and the depth of theoretical contributions, the paper is generally well-received for its innovative approach and the practical benefits it offers in real-world applications.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, making it easy to follow and understand.
- The problem addressed is significant and relevant, with a clear motivation and a novel approach to solving it.
- The proposed method, DODA, is simple, intuitive, and effective, demonstrating state-of-the-art performance on various benchmarks.
- The methodology is technically sound, with a strong theoretical foundation that supports the proposed solutions.
- The paper provides extensive experimental results and ablation studies, which demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed method.
- The authors have made their code available, which is a commendable effort towards reproducibility.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper could benefit from more detailed explanations and justifications for certain theoretical claims, particularly in the proofs and assumptions used.
- There is a lack of clarity in the experimental setup, particularly in how the baseline methods were implemented and the specifics of the hyperparameters used.
- The novelty of the method is questioned, as it appears similar to existing methods like CUDA, with only minor differences.
- The paper does not sufficiently compare the proposed method with other state-of-the-art methods, which could have provided a more robust evaluation.
- The theoretical analysis could be more rigorous and comprehensive, particularly in the proofs and the assumptions made.
- The paper lacks a detailed discussion on the limitations of the proposed method, which is crucial for understanding its applicability and effectiveness in real-world scenarios.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors clarify how the baseline methods were implemented and how the hyperparameters were selected?
- How does the proposed method compare to other state-of-the-art methods in terms of performance and computational efficiency?
- Could the authors provide more detailed proofs or justifications for the theoretical claims made in the paper?
- What are the specific advantages of the proposed method over existing methods like CUDA, and how does it address the limitations of those methods?
- How does the proposed method handle the computational complexity and memory requirements, particularly in large-scale datasets?
- Can the authors provide a more detailed discussion on the limitations of the proposed method and how these might affect its practical applicability?
