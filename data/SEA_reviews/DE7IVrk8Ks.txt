 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel method, LASH, for converting unconditional generative models into conditional models without retraining or access to additional data. This is achieved by shattering the latent space into distinct clusters, each corresponding to a semantically meaningful mode in the output space. The approach utilizes a Gaussian mixture model to adaptively sample from these clusters, enhancing the model's ability to generate conditional samples. The method is demonstrated through experiments on datasets like MNIST, FashionMNIST, and CelebA, showing that LASH outperforms existing methods in unsupervised conditional sampling. However, the paper's novelty is questioned due to its similarity to previous works, and the experimental scope is limited to simple datasets, raising concerns about its applicability to more complex, real-world scenarios.

**Strengths:**
- The paper addresses an important and relevant problem in the field of generative models, specifically focusing on converting unconditional models into conditional models without retraining.
- The proposed method is novel and innovative, particularly in its approach to shattering the latent space into distinct clusters and using a Gaussian mixture model for adaptive sampling.
- The paper is well-written, making it clear and easy to follow, which is crucial for understanding complex theoretical concepts and methodologies.
- The experimental results are robust and demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed method, showing improvements over existing methods.
- The paper provides a comprehensive theoretical analysis, which is supported by empirical evidence, enhancing its credibility and applicability.

**Weaknesses:**
