 **Summary:**
The paper introduces TopoFR, a novel framework for face recognition (FR) that leverages topological structure alignment and hard sample mining strategies to enhance the encoding of critical structure information from large-scale datasets. TopoFR employs a Perturbation-guided Topological Structure Alignment (PTSA) strategy to align the topological structures of input and latent spaces, addressing the issue of structure collapse. Additionally, a Structure Damage Estimation (SDE) strategy is used to prioritize the optimization of hard samples, mitigating their adverse impact on the latent space structure. Extensive experiments on various face benchmarks demonstrate the effectiveness of TopoFR in improving the generalization performance of face recognition models.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces novel strategies for aligning the topological structures of input and latent spaces in face recognition, which is a significant advancement in the field.
- The use of persistent homology to align the topological structures of input and latent spaces is a novel approach that effectively preserves structure information and enhances the generalization performance of the face recognition model.
- The proposed methods, including PTSA and SDE, are well-motivated and technically sound, providing a robust framework for face recognition.
- The paper is well-written, with clear and concise explanations of the proposed methods and their contributions to the field.
- Extensive experiments are conducted to demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed methods, showing improvements over existing methods in terms of accuracy and generalization performance.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed discussion on how the topological structure discrepancy between input and latent spaces is quantitatively measured and how it affects the model's performance.
- The motivation behind the use of topological structure in face recognition is not clearly explained, and the paper does not provide a clear explanation of how the topological structure discrepancy is quantitatively measured.
- There is a lack of clarity in the presentation of the methodology, particularly in the explanation of the hard sample mining strategy and the alignment of topological structures.
- The paper does not include sufficient ablation studies to demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed methods, especially in comparison to existing methods.
- The paper does not adequately address the issue of overfitting, which is a significant concern in deep learning models.
- The paper could benefit from a more thorough discussion on the limitations of the proposed methods and potential areas for future research.
