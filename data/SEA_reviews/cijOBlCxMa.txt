 **Summary:**
The paper introduces CustomNet, a novel method for zero-shot object customization in text-to-image generation, leveraging 3D novel view synthesis capabilities to control viewpoints, locations, and backgrounds. This approach integrates a dual cross-attention module to manage background generation and foreground composition, aiming to preserve object identity and generate harmonious outputs. The method is evaluated against existing techniques, demonstrating improvements in identity preservation and viewpoint consistency. However, concerns are raised about the method's reliance on the Zero-1-to-3 model, the lack of detailed comparisons, and the method's generalization to different datasets and object classes.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel approach to object customization that effectively integrates 3D novel view synthesis capabilities, which is a significant advancement in the field.
- The methodology is well-explained, making it easy to follow and understand, and the paper provides a comprehensive evaluation against existing techniques, demonstrating improvements in identity preservation and viewpoint consistency.
- The use of a dual cross-attention module to manage background generation and foreground composition is innovative and shows promise in generating harmonious outputs.
- The paper is well-organized and the figures are clear, which aids in understanding the proposed methodology.
- The method allows for zero-shot object customization without test-time optimization, which is a practical advantage over some existing methods.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed comparison with inpainting-based methods, which are crucial for evaluating the effectiveness of the proposed method in handling complex backgrounds.
- The method's generalization to different datasets and object classes is not thoroughly evaluated, which is a significant limitation given the diversity of objects in real-world applications.
- The paper's reliance on the Zero-1-to-3 model raises concerns about the method's originality and its ability to handle objects in real-world scenarios where camera poses and object positions are not easily obtained.
- The method's performance in handling different object classes and the impact of the training data construction pipeline on the method's effectiveness are not adequately discussed.
- The paper could benefit from a more detailed discussion on the limitations of the method and how these might affect its practical application.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors provide a detailed comparison with inpainting-based methods to better understand how the proposed method handles complex backgrounds?
- How does the method perform when applied to different datasets and object classes, and what are the implications of using different training data construction pipelines?
- Can the authors discuss the limitations of the method and how these might affect its practical application in real-world scenarios?
- How does the method handle objects with complex shapes or intricate details, and what are the implications of using different object representations (e.g., segmentation masks, bounding boxes) for the method's effectiveness?
- Could the authors provide more details on the training data construction pipeline, particularly how it affects the method's performance and generalization?
