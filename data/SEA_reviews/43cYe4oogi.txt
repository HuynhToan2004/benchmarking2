 **Summary:**
The paper explores the expressivity of Graph Neural Networks (GNNs) in the context of knowledge graph (KG) reasoning, specifically focusing on the rule structures learned by GNNs. It introduces a unified framework, QL-GNN, which incorporates state-of-the-art GNNs for KG reasoning, and analyzes their logical expressivity. The paper also proposes a novel labeling strategy, EL-GNN, to enhance the learning of rule structures. Theoretical and experimental results demonstrate that EL-GNN can learn a broader range of rule structures compared to QL-GNN, and it outperforms existing methods on both synthetic and real datasets.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel labeling strategy, EL-GNN, which is a significant contribution to the field.
- The theoretical analysis of the expressivity of GNNs in the context of KG reasoning is thorough and insightful, providing a deeper understanding of the capabilities of GNNs.
- The experimental results demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed EL-GNN, showing its ability to outperform existing methods on both synthetic and real datasets.
- The paper is well-written, making it accessible and easy to follow, with clear logical structure and comprehensive experimental results.
- The introduction of a unified framework, QL-GNN, provides a useful tool for understanding the capabilities and limitations of GNNs in KG reasoning.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed discussion on the limitations of EL-GNN and its potential negative societal impacts.
- The experimental evaluation is somewhat limited, with a lack of comparison to other state-of-the-art methods and a narrow focus on accuracy as the sole evaluation metric.
- The paper does not adequately address the computational complexity and scalability of EL-GNN, particularly in large and complex knowledge graphs.
- The paper's claims about the expressivity of GNNs are not convincingly supported by the experimental results, which could benefit from more rigorous validation and broader evaluation metrics.
- The paper could benefit from a more detailed comparison with other GNN-based KG reasoning methods, such as AdaProp and A*Net, to better understand the relative advantages of EL-GNN.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors provide more details on the computational complexity and scalability of EL-GNN, particularly in comparison to QL-GNN?
- How does EL-GNN compare in terms of accuracy and efficiency to other state-of-the-art methods, especially on larger and more complex datasets?
- Can the authors clarify the relationship between the expressivity of GNNs and the ability to learn complex rule structures, particularly in the context of KG reasoning?
- How does EL-GNN handle the issue of over-smoothing in GNNs, and what are the implications for the learning of rule structures?
- Could the authors provide more detailed experimental results, including comparisons with other methods and a broader range of evaluation metrics?
