 **Summary:**
The paper investigates data selection strategies for machine learning models, focusing on the use of surrogate models to predict labels for a subset of data points. This approach aims to reduce the computational cost of labeling all data points, which is particularly beneficial in high-dimensional settings. The authors propose a theoretical framework that compares the performance of biased and unbiased sampling methods, showing that biased sampling can be more effective in certain scenarios. They also provide numerical experiments to support their theoretical claims, demonstrating that their methods can outperform traditional unbiased sampling strategies. The paper is structured around two asymptotic regimes, one for low-dimensional data and another for high-dimensional data, and explores the impact of various sampling strategies on model performance.

**Strengths:**
- The paper provides a thorough theoretical analysis of data selection strategies, which is a significant contribution to the field of machine learning.
- It offers a clear and well-structured presentation of the theoretical results, making the content accessible and understandable.
- The paper includes detailed proofs and mathematical derivations, which support the theoretical claims and provide a solid foundation for the proposed methods.
- The authors compare their proposed methods with existing approaches, highlighting the novelty and effectiveness of their approach.
- The paper includes both synthetic and real data experiments, demonstrating the practical applicability of the proposed methods.
- The theoretical results are supported by numerical experiments, which helps in validating the theoretical claims and providing a practical perspective.

**Weaknesses:**
