 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel prompting strategy called Re2, which involves re-reading the question to enhance the reasoning capabilities of large language models (LLMs). This strategy is inspired by human learning and problem-solving, aiming to align with the cognitive principle of reinforcement. The authors conducted extensive experiments across various benchmarks, demonstrating that Re2 can improve the reasoning performance of LLMs, particularly when used with other thought-eliciting prompting strategies. Despite its simplicity and intuitive nature, the paper's novelty is questioned due to the similarity of the re-reading approach to existing methods. Additionally, concerns about the lack of baseline comparisons and the potential for data leakage due to the use of closed-source models were raised.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel prompting strategy, Re2, which is a simple yet effective approach to enhance the reasoning capabilities of LLMs.
- The authors conducted extensive experiments across various benchmarks, demonstrating the effectiveness of Re2 in improving the reasoning performance of LLMs.
- The paper is well-written, with clear explanations of the methodology and experimental results, making it easy to follow and understand.
- The authors provide a comprehensive evaluation of the Re2 prompting strategy, showing its effectiveness across a variety of tasks, models, and prompting methods.
- The paper includes a detailed analysis of the impact of different re-reading instructions on the performance of the CoT prompting strategy, which is a significant contribution to the field.

**Weaknesses:**
- The novelty of the paper is limited as the idea of re-reading the question is not new, and similar approaches have been used in prior works.
- The paper lacks a detailed analysis of the impact of different re-reading instructions on the performance of the CoT prompting strategy, which is a significant omission given the focus on this aspect.
- There is a lack of baseline comparisons, particularly with other prompting strategies that might provide a more robust evaluation of the Re2 prompting strategy.
- The paper does not discuss the potential for data leakage due to the use of closed-source models, which could affect the reliability and validity of the results.
- The paper does not address the impact of different re-reading instructions on the performance of the CoT prompting strategy, which is a significant limitation given the focus on this aspect in the paper.
- The paper lacks a detailed analysis of the computational overhead and inference time introduced by the Re2 prompting strategy, which is crucial for understanding its practical applicability.
