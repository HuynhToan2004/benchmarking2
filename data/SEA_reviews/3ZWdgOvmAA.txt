 **Summary:**
The paper introduces LumiNet, a novel logit-based knowledge distillation method that leverages intra-class information to enhance the learning process. This method adjusts the logits of a batch by normalizing intra-class logits using variance and mean, aiming to improve the performance of student models. The paper presents extensive experiments across various datasets and architectures, demonstrating the effectiveness of LumiNet in tasks such as image classification, object detection, and transfer learning. However, concerns were raised about the clarity of the method's presentation, the novelty of the approach, and the robustness of the results, particularly in terms of the experimental setup and the comparison with existing methods.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written and easy to follow, making complex concepts accessible.
- The methodology is novel and well-motivated, particularly the use of variance and mean to normalize intra-class logits.
- Extensive experiments are conducted across various datasets and architectures, demonstrating the effectiveness of the proposed method.
- The paper includes a thorough analysis of the proposed method, with clear explanations and intuitive visualizations.
- The results show that the proposed method outperforms existing methods, indicating its effectiveness.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a clear description of the main idea and the mathematical definitions of key concepts such as K and R in Equation 2.
- The experimental results are not convincing, with insufficient comparisons to existing methods and a lack of detailed analysis on the impact of different parameters.
- The paper does not adequately address the limitations of the proposed method, which could affect the understanding and applicability of the findings.
- The paper does not provide sufficient details on the experimental setup, such as the training parameters and the number of epochs, which could affect the reproducibility and comparability of the results.
- The paper does not discuss the computational efficiency of the proposed method, which is crucial for practical applications.
- There are minor typographical and formatting errors that need correction.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors clarify the definitions of K and R in Equation 2 and how they relate to the overall methodology?
- How does the proposed method compare to existing methods, especially in terms of computational efficiency and practical applicability?
- Can the authors provide more detailed results on the impact of different parameters, such as batch size and temperature scaling factor, on the performance of the method?
- How does the proposed method address the challenges of logit-based knowledge distillation, particularly in terms of granularity and alignment issues?
- Could the authors discuss the limitations of their method and suggest potential directions for future research?
- Is there any theoretical analysis or additional empirical evidence that could support the claims made about the method's effectiveness and efficiency?
