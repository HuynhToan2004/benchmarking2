 **Summary:**
The paper introduces OCAtari, a novel framework designed to facilitate object-centric learning in Atari games. It provides a set of environments that generate object-centric states using both visual and RAM-based methods, aiming to enhance the evaluation of object-centric reinforcement learning (RL) methods. The framework is built upon existing Atari ARI and offers two methods for object detection, one based on visual processing and the other on RAM extraction. The paper also presents a dataset for training and evaluating object-centric RL agents, which includes a set of Atari frames along with object-centric states. The framework's utility is demonstrated through experiments that showcase its potential in training object-centric RL agents and evaluating object detection methods.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel framework for object-centric learning in Atari games, which is a significant contribution to the field.
- It provides a comprehensive set of environments and datasets for evaluating object-centric RL methods, enhancing the reproducibility and comparability of research in this area.
- The framework offers both visual and RAM-based methods for object detection, providing a versatile approach to object-centric learning.
- The paper is well-written, clear, and easy to follow, making it accessible to a broad audience.
- The introduction of a new benchmark for object-centric RL in Atari games is timely and relevant, addressing a gap in existing benchmarks.
- The experiments conducted are thorough, providing a robust evaluation of the framework's capabilities.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a clear motivation for why object-centric learning is crucial for Atari games, and the potential applications of this learning in such environments are not convincingly argued.
- The paper does not sufficiently compare its approach with existing methods like AtariARI, which could have provided a better context for the proposed framework.
- The framework's novelty is questioned as it appears to be a simple extension of existing methods, and the technical contributions are not clearly articulated.
- The paper could benefit from a more detailed discussion on the limitations of the proposed methods, particularly in terms of scalability and generalizability.
- There is a lack of discussion on the societal impact of the proposed framework, which is crucial for understanding the broader implications of the research.
- The paper does not adequately address the challenges of object detection in Atari games, particularly in games where objects are not well-defined or are static.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors clarify the specific advantages of using object-centric learning in Atari games over other environments like those used in previous studies?
- How does the proposed framework compare technically and qualitatively with existing methods like AtariARI?
- Can the authors provide a more detailed comparison of the computational efficiency and scalability of their methods compared to existing approaches?
- How do the authors address the challenges of object detection in games where objects are not well-defined or are static?
- Could the authors discuss the potential societal impacts of their research, particularly in terms of the applications of object-centric RL in real-world scenarios?
- How does the proposed framework handle the challenges of object detection in games with complex visuals or multiple overlapping objects?
