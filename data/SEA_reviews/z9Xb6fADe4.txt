 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a deep reinforcement learning (DRL) approach for managing departure metering in mixed-mode runway operations, focusing on the Singapore Changi Airport. It formulates the departure metering problem as a markov decision process, using a hotspot event graph to represent the state space. The DRL model is trained using historical data and evaluated against traditional methods and other baselines, demonstrating improvements in taxi-out delays, gate holding times, and runway throughput. The paper also discusses the reduction in fuel consumption and the application of the method to real-world airport scenarios. However, it lacks detailed discussion on the complexity of the DRL algorithm, the scalability of the method, and the generalizability of the results beyond the specific airport context.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, easy to follow, and provides a clear motivation for the study.
- The authors have developed a novel method for the departure metering problem, which is a significant contribution to the field.
- The method is evaluated against a good set of baselines, demonstrating the effectiveness of the proposed approach.
- The paper provides a detailed description of the methodology, including the event graph representation and the DRL-based algorithm, which are well-explained.
- The results show that the proposed method can significantly reduce taxi-out delays and improve runway throughput, with a reduction in fuel consumption.
- The paper is structured well, making it easy to understand and replicate the experiments.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed discussion on the complexity of the DRL algorithm and its scalability, especially in terms of computational resources and time required for training.
- The method's generalizability is limited, as it is primarily tested at a single airport (Singapore Changi Airport), raising concerns about its applicability to other airports with different characteristics.
- The paper does not discuss the limitations of the method, which could provide a more balanced view of its strengths and weaknesses.
- There is a lack of discussion on the impact of the method on other airport operations, such as air traffic control and aircraft maintenance.
- The paper does not include a discussion on the potential negative societal impacts of the method, such as increased noise pollution or increased emissions from idling aircraft.
- The results section is somewhat limited, with only a few metrics evaluated, which could be expanded to provide a more comprehensive evaluation of the method's effectiveness.

**Questions:**

- How does the method perform when applied to airports with different characteristics, such as different runway configurations or different traffic patterns?
- What are the limitations of the method, and how might these impact its practical application in real-world airport scenarios?
- Could you discuss the potential negative societal impacts of the method, such as increased noise pollution or increased emissions from idling aircraft?
- How does the method compare to other existing methods for managing departure metering, such as traditional rule-based systems or other machine learning approaches?
- Can you provide more details on the experimental setup, such as the simulation environment and the data used to train the model?
- How does the method handle uncertainty in the system, such as changes in weather conditions or unexpected delays in aircraft departures?
