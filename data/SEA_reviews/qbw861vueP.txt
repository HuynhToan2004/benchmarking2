 **Summary:**
The paper introduces BiDST, a novel dynamic sparse training framework that utilizes bi-level optimization to jointly optimize the mask and weights, aiming to improve the training efficiency and accuracy of sparse neural networks. BiDST formulates the dynamic sparse training process as a bi-level optimization problem, where the mask and weight updates are interdependent, thereby enhancing the exploration of sparse patterns and reducing system overhead. The paper presents a theoretical framework and empirical results demonstrating the effectiveness of BiDST over existing methods, showing improvements in accuracy, training speed, and system overhead. However, concerns were raised about the incremental nature of the contribution, the limited novelty, and the practical applicability of the proposed method.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel approach by formulating dynamic sparse training as a bi-level optimization problem, which is a significant advancement in the field.
- The proposed BiDST framework effectively optimizes the mask and weights simultaneously, leading to improved accuracy and training speed.
- The paper is well-written, easy to follow, and provides a comprehensive evaluation of BiDST, showing significant improvements over existing methods in accuracy, training speed, and system overhead.
- The paper addresses the critical issue of system overhead in dynamic sparse training, which is often overlooked by other methods.
- The authors provide a thorough analysis of the dynamic sparse training process and the impact of frequent mask updates on system overhead.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed discussion on the limitations of the proposed method, particularly concerning the practical applicability of the bi-level optimization approach in real-world scenarios.
- The novelty of the paper is limited as the bi-level optimization approach is not new, and the methodology seems to be a straightforward extension of existing methods.
- The paper does not adequately address the computational overhead introduced by the bi-level optimization approach, which could be a significant drawback in practical implementations.
- The paper's theoretical contributions are somewhat limited, and the empirical results do not convincingly demonstrate the superiority of BiDST over existing methods, especially in extreme sparsity settings.
- The paper could benefit from a more rigorous comparison with other recent methods, including those that use different optimization techniques or alternative sparsity patterns.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors clarify how the bi-level optimization approach can be practically applied in real-world scenarios, particularly in terms of computational efficiency and system overhead?
- How does the proposed method compare to other recent methods that use different optimization techniques or alternative sparsity patterns?
- In terms of practical implementation, how does the proposed method handle the computational overhead introduced by the bi-level optimization approach?
- Could the authors provide more detailed experimental results, especially in extreme sparsity settings, to better demonstrate the effectiveness of BiDST?
- How does the proposed method address the issue of layer collapse in high sparsity settings?
- Can the authors provide more insights into the computational efficiency of BiDST, particularly in terms of the computational cost and the practical applicability of the method?
