 **Summary:**
The paper introduces Denoised Predictive Imagination (DPI), a novel information-theoretic approach to learning world models that minimizes the mutual information about past observations and maximizes the mutual information about future outcomes. DPI leverages Soft Actor-Critic (SAC) agents augmented with an information-theoretic auxiliary loss to learn representations that disregard non-informative, temporally-correlated noise. The method is evaluated on six tasks from the DeepMind Control Suite (DMC) and is shown to outperform eight state-of-the-art approaches in various settings. Despite its innovative approach, the paper suffers from clarity issues in its presentation, particularly in the introduction and methodology sections, and lacks a comprehensive discussion on related works and its theoretical underpinnings.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel information-theoretic approach to learning world models, which is a significant advancement in the field of reinforcement learning.
- The proposed method, Denoised Predictive Imagination (DPI), is evaluated on six tasks from the DeepMind Control Suite (DMC) and shows promising results, outperforming eight state-of-the-art approaches in various settings.
- The paper is well-written, with clear and easy-to-follow sections, and the experiments are well-designed, providing a comprehensive evaluation of the proposed method.
- The approach is innovative, utilizing an information-theoretic auxiliary loss to augment Soft Actor-Critic (SAC) agents, which effectively disregards non-informative, temporally-correlated noise.
- The paper provides a good analysis of the method's performance and includes a comprehensive discussion on the experimental results, highlighting the method's effectiveness in noisy environments.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks clarity in its introduction and methodology sections, making it difficult for readers to understand the core ideas and the theoretical underpinnings of the proposed method.
- The related work section is insufficiently detailed, which could hinder the understanding of the paper's contributions in relation to existing literature.
- There is a lack of discussion on the limitations of the proposed method, which is crucial for understanding its applicability and potential drawbacks.
- The paper could benefit from more detailed explanations and examples in the methodology section to aid in understanding the complex mathematical notations and concepts.
- The paper's experimental setup and results are not sufficiently detailed, particularly in terms of the specifics of the environments and the parameters used, which could affect the reproducibility and generalizability of the results.
- The paper's theoretical claims, such as the minimization of mutual information about past and the maximization of predictive ability for future, are not adequately justified or supported by empirical evidence.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors provide more details on the specifics of the environments used in the experiments, including the dimensions of the observation and action spaces, the reward functions, and the dynamics of the environments?
- How does the proposed method compare to other state-of-the-art approaches in terms of computational efficiency and scalability, particularly in more complex or high-dimensional environments?
- Could the authors clarify the roles and interactions of the different components in the proposed method, such as the information bottleneck, the mutual information objectives, and the contrastive learning components?
- How does the proposed method handle the trade-off between maximizing predictive information and minimizing the mutual information about the past?
- Can the authors provide a more detailed discussion on the limitations of the proposed method and suggest potential directions for future research to address these limitations?
