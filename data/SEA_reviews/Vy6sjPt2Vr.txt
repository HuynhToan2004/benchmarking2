 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel approach to tokenization in Vision Transformers (ViTs) by utilizing superpixel tokenization, which decouples tokenization from feature extraction. This method is claimed to enhance interpretability and maintain competitive performance in classification tasks. The authors propose a modular framework, SPiT, which includes superpixel graph construction, feature extraction, and positional encoding, to improve the model's performance. Experiments demonstrate that the proposed method maintains competitive performance while enhancing interpretability. However, the paper has been critiqued for its lack of comprehensive experiments, particularly in terms of image resolution and the variety of datasets used.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel approach to tokenization in ViTs using superpixels, which is a significant advancement in the field.
- The authors have provided a comprehensive analysis of the proposed method, including a detailed description of the algorithm and its implementation.
- The paper is well-organized, clearly written, and includes a detailed description of the algorithm, which is supported by thorough experiments.
- The proposed method is shown to enhance the interpretability of the model, as demonstrated through experiments.
- The paper is original in its approach to tokenization and provides a valuable contribution to the field by proposing a modular framework that decouples tokenization from feature extraction.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks comprehensive experiments, particularly in terms of image resolution and the variety of datasets used.
- There is a lack of discussion on the limitations of the proposed method, which could be crucial for understanding its applicability and potential drawbacks.
- The paper could benefit from a more detailed comparison with existing methods, particularly in terms of computational efficiency and the impact of different feature extraction methods.
- The paper could be improved by including more detailed experimental results and a more thorough discussion on the limitations and potential improvements of the proposed method.
- The paper does not sufficiently discuss the impact of different feature extraction methods on the performance of the model.
- There is a lack of clarity in the presentation of results, particularly in tables, which could be improved for better readability and comprehension.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors clarify the differences between the proposed method and the work by Wei et al. (2018) mentioned in the paper?
- How does the proposed method perform with different image resolutions, and what are the implications for the model's performance?
- Could the authors provide a more detailed discussion on the limitations of the proposed method and potential areas for future research?
- How does the proposed method compare to other tokenization methods in terms of computational efficiency and model performance?
- Can the authors provide more detailed experimental results, particularly in terms of the impact of different feature extraction methods on the model's performance?
- How does the proposed method handle different types of images, such as those with complex textures or varying levels of detail?
- Can the authors clarify the notation and presentation in the paper, particularly in tables and figures, to improve readability and understanding?
