 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel Local and Smooth (LaS) attention mechanism aimed at enhancing the long-range modeling capabilities of transformers. By integrating a locality bias and a smoothness bias, the LaS attention mechanism aims to address the challenges posed by long-range dependencies in transformer architectures. The authors conduct a thorough empirical study, including extensive ablation studies and comparisons with state-of-the-art models, demonstrating the effectiveness of the proposed method. The paper also discusses the inductive biases necessary for capturing long-range dependencies and provides insights into the design choices that can improve transformer performance on long-range tasks.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, with clear figures and tables that aid in understanding the content.
- The proposed method is simple, intuitive, and effective, with a clear motivation for its design.
- The authors conduct extensive ablation studies and comparisons with state-of-the-art models, demonstrating the effectiveness of the proposed method.
- The paper provides insights into the design choices that can improve transformer performance on long-range tasks, and the proposed method is simple and easy to implement.
- The paper discusses the inductive biases necessary for capturing long-range dependencies, which is crucial for improving transformer performance on long-range tasks.
- The proposed method is empirically proven to be effective in capturing long-range dependencies, and the paper provides a thorough empirical study to validate the proposed method.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a comprehensive discussion on the limitations of the proposed method, such as its applicability to different domains and the potential negative societal impacts.
- The paper does not include experiments on natural language processing tasks, which could provide a broader validation of the proposed method.
- The paper does not provide a detailed analysis of the computational complexity introduced by the proposed method, which could be crucial for practical applications.
- The paper does not include a discussion on the potential negative societal impacts of the proposed method, which could be an important consideration for the ethical implications of the research.
- The paper could benefit from a more detailed discussion on the design choices made in the proposed method, particularly the choice of hyperparameters and their impact on performance.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors provide more details on the computational complexity introduced by the proposed method and how it compares to existing methods?
- How does the proposed method perform on natural language processing tasks, and could the authors provide additional results or insights on this?
- Could the authors discuss the potential negative societal impacts of the proposed method and how these might be mitigated or addressed?
- How sensitive is the proposed method to the choice of hyperparameters, and what impact do these choices have on performance?
- Could the authors provide more insights into the design choices made in the proposed method, particularly the choice of the locality bias and the smoothness bias?
- Could the authors clarify the statement regarding the expressivity of transformers, as mentioned in the paper, and provide more details on the theoretical underpinnings of this claim?
