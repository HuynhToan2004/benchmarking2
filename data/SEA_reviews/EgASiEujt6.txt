 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel framework named RGDM (Reward-Guided Diffusion Model) for training diffusion models, which guides the diffusion process towards desired samples using reinforcement learning (RL). The method leverages a payoff distribution to optimize the training objective and calculates policy gradients, thereby reducing gradient variance and improving the exploration of highly rewarded samples. The framework is tested on various tasks such as 3D shape and molecule generation, demonstrating significant improvements over existing conditional diffusion models. The paper also discusses the theoretical underpinnings of the approach, comparing it to existing methods, and provides experimental results to validate its effectiveness.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel approach to training diffusion models using reinforcement learning (RL), which is a significant departure from traditional methods.
- The proposed method is well-motivated, theoretically grounded, and effectively addresses the challenges of high gradient variance and non-stationary policies in diffusion models.
- The method is versatile and applicable to various tasks, including 3D shape and molecule generation, demonstrating its effectiveness across different domains.
- The paper is well-written, clear, and easy to follow, with a good balance between theoretical and practical aspects.
- The experimental results are strong, showing significant improvements over existing conditional diffusion models, and the method's ability to outperform baseline methods in various scenarios.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a thorough comparison with existing RL-based diffusion models, which could provide a better understanding of the method's novelty and advantages.
- The paper does not adequately address the computational efficiency of the method, which could be a significant concern for practical applications.
- There is a lack of discussion on how the method performs with different types of rewards, especially non-differentiable rewards, which could limit its applicability.
- The paper does not include a discussion on potential negative societal impacts, which could be a concern for some applications.
- The paper could benefit from a more detailed discussion on the limitations of the proposed method and how these could be addressed in future work.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors provide more details on the computational efficiency of the proposed method, particularly in terms of training and inference times compared to other methods?
- How does the method perform with different types of rewards, especially non-differentiable rewards, and what are the implications for the effectiveness of the method?
- Can the authors clarify how the method handles different types of rewards, such as those based on classifier outputs versus point cloud similarity?
- How does the method compare to other RL-based diffusion models, and what are the key differences in terms of performance and applicability?
- Could the authors provide more details on the training process, particularly how the method is initialized and how the reward function is integrated into the training process?
- In terms of practical implementation, how does the method perform in different scenarios, and what are the implications for its applicability in various domains?
