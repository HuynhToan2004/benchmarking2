 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel contribution valuation metric called the vertical federated Shapley value (VerFedSV) for vertical federated learning (VFL) scenarios. VerFedSV aims to address the challenges of computing Shapley values in VFL by proposing a method that does not require retraining the model, which is typically costly in terms of communication and computation overheads. The method is designed to be adaptable to both synchronous and asynchronous VFL settings, providing theoretical guarantees for its fairness and efficiency. The paper includes empirical evaluations across multiple datasets, demonstrating the practical applicability and effectiveness of VerFedSV. However, concerns were raised regarding the clarity and depth of the experimental results, the lack of discussion on limitations, and the need for a more detailed comparison with existing methods.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel contribution valuation metric called VerFedSV, which is specifically designed for vertical federated learning (VFL) scenarios.
- VerFedSV is computationally efficient and does not require retraining the model, which is a significant advantage over traditional methods.
- The paper provides both theoretical and empirical evidence to support the fairness and effectiveness of VerFedSV.
- VerFedSV can be adapted to both synchronous and asynchronous VFL settings, making it a versatile solution.
- The paper is well-written and easy to follow, with a clear structure and logical flow.
- The authors have conducted extensive experiments on multiple datasets to demonstrate the practical applicability and effectiveness of VerFedSV.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed discussion on the limitations of the proposed method, which could provide a more comprehensive understanding of its applicability and potential drawbacks.
- The experimental results are not sufficiently detailed, particularly the performance metrics and the impact of different settings on the results.
- The paper does not provide a clear comparison with existing methods, which could help in understanding the advantages and disadvantages of VerFedSV.
- The paper does not discuss the impact of different local update rules on the computation of VerFedSV, which could be a significant limitation in practical applications.
- The paper does not address the scalability of VerFedSV with the number of clients or the size of the data, which could limit its applicability in large-scale VFL scenarios.
- The paper lacks a detailed discussion on the privacy implications of the proposed method, which is a critical concern in VFL.

**Questions:**
- How does the computation of VerFedSV vary with different local update rules in VFL?
- Can the authors provide more detailed experimental results, including performance metrics and the impact of different settings on the results?
- How does the proposed method compare with existing methods in terms of computational efficiency, fairness, and effectiveness?
- Could the authors discuss the scalability of VerFedSV with the number of clients and the size of the data?
- How does the proposed method address privacy concerns in VFL?
- How does the proposed method ensure fairness in scenarios where clients have different computational resources?
- Could the authors provide a more detailed discussion on the limitations of the proposed method and its potential drawbacks?
