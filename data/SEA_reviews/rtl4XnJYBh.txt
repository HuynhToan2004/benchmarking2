 **Summary:**
The paper explores the robustness of multi-modal contrastive learning (MMCL) through two mechanisms: intra-class contrasting and inter-class feature sharing. These mechanisms are analyzed theoretically to explain why MMCL is more robust than supervised learning (SL) under distribution shift. The authors propose that MMCL's robustness stems from its ability to learn high-variance features and leverage inter-class feature sharing, which is not possible in SL. The paper also discusses the impact of rich captions on MMCL's robustness. Despite the theoretical insights, the paper is criticized for its lack of empirical evidence, particularly in real-world scenarios, and its reliance on simplified assumptions that may not reflect practical scenarios.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, clear, and easy to follow, with a comprehensive literature review that covers most related works.
- The theoretical analysis is solid, with theorems and proofs providing a clear explanation of MMCL's robustness mechanisms.
- The paper introduces two novel mechanisms, intra-class contrasting and inter-class feature sharing, which are crucial for MMCL's robustness under distribution shift.
- The theoretical analysis is supported by semi-synthetic experiments that demonstrate the benefits of rich captions and the role of intra-class contrasting in MMCL.
- The paper provides a detailed analysis of the benefits of annotating variations of a core feature and in general richer text annotations, which is crucial for understanding the robustness of MMCL.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks empirical evidence, particularly in real-world scenarios, which could strengthen the claims about MMCL's robustness.
- The assumptions made in the theoretical analysis are simplified and may not reflect practical scenarios, which could limit the generalizability of the findings.
- The paper does not discuss the limitations of MMCL, such as the potential for learning spurious correlations or the impact of different types of captions on MMCL's performance.
- The paper does not provide a detailed comparison of MMCL with other types of contrastive learning methods, which could help clarify the specific contributions of MMCL.
- The paper does not discuss the impact of different types of noise on MMCL's performance, which could be a significant limitation in practical applications.

**Questions:**
- Could you provide more empirical evidence to support the claims about MMCL's robustness in real-world scenarios?
- How do the theoretical findings compare with existing works that have also studied the robustness of MMCL?
- Can you discuss the limitations of MMCL and how they might affect its performance in practical applications?
- How do different types of captions impact MMCL's performance, and what are the implications of using different types of captions in different scenarios?
- Could you provide more details on the experimental setup, particularly the construction of captions and the impact of different types of noise on MMCL's performance?
