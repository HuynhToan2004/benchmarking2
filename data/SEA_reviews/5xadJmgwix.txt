 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel approach to sketch generation using a diffusion model, specifically designed to address the challenges of over-sketching and insufficient recognizability in generated sketches. It employs a three-phase sampling strategy and a scale-adaptive classifier-guided diffusion model to optimize the guidance scale dynamically, thereby improving the quality and diversity of generated sketches. The method is evaluated on the QuickDraw dataset, demonstrating superior performance over existing methods in terms of fidelity and mode coverage. The paper also includes an ablation study to analyze the effectiveness of different components and a detailed analysis of the sampling phases.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, clear, and easy to follow, with a comprehensive literature review and detailed experimental setup.
- The proposed method is innovative, utilizing a scale-adaptive classifier-guided diffusion model for pixel-level sketch generation, which is a significant advancement in the field.
- The three-phase sampling strategy effectively enhances sketch diversity and quality, and the use of a residual sketch for scale optimization is a novel and effective approach.
- The paper includes a thorough ablation study that demonstrates the effectiveness of each component and provides insights into the impact of different parameters on the generation process.
- The proposed method achieves state-of-the-art performance on the QuickDraw dataset, outperforming existing methods in terms of FID score and other metrics.
- The method's ability to balance recognizability and complexity in generated sketches is highlighted, with the use of a scaling indicator and a residual sketch contributing to this balance.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper could benefit from a more detailed discussion on the limitations of the proposed method, particularly concerning its applicability to different datasets and the potential for over-sketching in complex scenes.
- The method's dependence on a specific dataset (QuickDraw) and its potential transferability to other datasets or applications is not adequately explored.
- The computational cost of the method, particularly the use of a separate classifier for guidance, is not discussed, which could be a significant drawback for practical implementation.
- The paper lacks a detailed comparison with other diffusion-based methods, which could provide a more comprehensive evaluation of the proposed method's performance.
- There are some unclear aspects in the methodology, such as the definition and role of the "scale indicator" and the optimization of the guidance scale, which could be clarified with additional examples or mathematical formulations.
- The paper's claims about the method's ability to handle complex scenes are not convincingly supported by the examples provided, which are primarily simple sketches.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors provide more details on the limitations of the proposed method, particularly in terms of its applicability to different datasets and its potential for over-sketching in complex scenes?
- How does the method perform when applied to other datasets or in different applications, such as sketch-to-image generation?
- Can the authors clarify the role and definition of the "scale indicator" and the optimization process for the guidance scale?
- How does the method compare to other diffusion-based methods in terms of computational cost and practical implementation?
- Could the authors provide additional examples or visualizations to better illustrate the method's effectiveness in handling complex scenes and its ability to generate high-quality, recognizable sketches?
