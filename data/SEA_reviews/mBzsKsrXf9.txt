 **Summary:**
The paper introduces ArtWhisperer, a novel dataset and online game designed to investigate human-AI interactions in text-to-image model generation. The dataset consists of over 50,000 human-AI interactions, providing insights into user prompting strategies and model steerability. The authors propose a metric called "steerability" to quantify the ease with which users can guide AI models to generate desired images. They also discuss how different types of images affect model steerability and user prompting strategies. The paper is praised for its innovative approach, detailed analysis, and the potential of the dataset to advance research in human-AI collaborations. However, concerns are raised about the clarity of the metric's definition and the generalizability of the findings.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel dataset and a new metric, which are significant contributions to the field of human-AI collaborations.
- The dataset is large and diverse, containing over 50,000 human-AI interactions across 191 target images, which is a substantial resource for future research.
- The paper is well-written, clear, and easy to follow, making it accessible to a broad audience.
- The methodology is sound, and the findings are supported by a detailed analysis, including the use of CLIP text embedding to measure prompt diversity.
- The paper addresses an important and timely issue of human-AI collaborations, which is crucial for the development of AI systems that can effectively interact with humans.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks clarity in defining and measuring the "steerability" metric, which is crucial for understanding the user's ability to guide the AI model.
- The generalizability of the findings is limited due to the dataset's focus on a specific user population and the experimental setup.
- The paper does not sufficiently address the ethical implications of using AI models to generate images, particularly concerning copyright and intellectual property issues.
- The dataset's utility for training AI models to generate images is not clearly demonstrated, and the paper does not provide sufficient evidence that the dataset can improve the quality or diversity of AI-generated images.
- The paper could benefit from a more detailed discussion on how the findings can be applied to improve AI models, especially in terms of generating human-readable image captions.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors clarify the definition and measurement of the "steerability" metric, particularly how it relates to the user's ability to guide the AI model?
- How do the findings from this study generalize to other user populations or different experimental setups?
- What are the specific ethical considerations related to the use of AI models to generate images, and how are these addressed in the paper?
- Could the authors provide more detailed examples or case studies demonstrating how the dataset can be used to improve AI-generated images?
- How does the paper address the potential biases in the dataset, particularly concerning the representation of different user groups and the diversity of image categories?
