 **Summary:**
The paper introduces TransCues, a transformer-based architecture designed for semantic segmentation of transparent objects, specifically focusing on glass and mirrors. The architecture incorporates two modules: Boundary Feature Enhancement (BFE) and Reflection Feature Enhancement (RFE), which address the challenges of distinguishing transparent objects from opaque ones. The BFE module enhances boundary features, while the RFE module decomposes reflections into foreground and background layers, thereby improving segmentation accuracy. The method is tested on various datasets and demonstrates superior performance over existing methods. However, concerns are raised about the novelty of the approach, the limited generalization to other types of transparent objects, and the clarity of the experimental setup.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel transformer-based architecture, TransCues, which effectively segments transparent objects from color images, focusing on glass and mirror objects.
- The methodology is robust and has been tested on various benchmark datasets, demonstrating improved overall performance.
- The paper is well-written, with clear presentation and detailed experimental results.
- The proposed Boundary Feature Enhancement (BFE) and Reflection Feature Enhancement (RFE) modules are innovative and contribute to the field of transparent object segmentation.
- The method is versatile, applicable not only to transparent objects but also to general semantic segmentation tasks.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks clarity in explaining the design and operation of the BFE module, particularly the rationale behind the convolution layers and the selection of hyperparameters.
- The generalization of the method to other types of transparent objects, such as plastic, is not adequately addressed.
- The experimental setup is not detailed enough, and there is a lack of comparison with more recent and relevant methods.
- The paper does not sufficiently address the limitations of the method, such as the computational cost and the scalability to different resolutions.
- The novelty of the method is questioned due to similarities with existing approaches like GDNet and Trans2Seg.
- The paper could benefit from a more comprehensive ablation study and a deeper analysis of the failure cases.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors provide more detailed explanations on the design and operation of the BFE module, including the selection of hyperparameters and the role of convolution layers?
- How does the method perform on other types of transparent objects, such as plastic, and what are the implications for its generalization?
- Could the authors elaborate on the experimental setup, particularly the comparison with more recent and relevant methods?
- What are the specific contributions of the BFE and RFE modules, and how do they compare in terms of performance and efficiency?
- How does the method handle different resolutions, and what are the implications for its scalability?
- Can the authors provide more details on the training and testing phases, including the datasets used and the parameters of the loss function?
- How does the method compare in terms of computational cost and efficiency with other state-of-the-art methods?
