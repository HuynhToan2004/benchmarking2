 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel method for fine-tuning language models (LLMs) using reinforcement learning (RL) to mitigate the degeneration problem, where LLMs generate unnatural texts after RL fine-tuning. The proposed method, Degeneration-free Policy Optimization (DfPO), utilizes action-masked policies and clipped advantage functions to optimize likelihood max/minimization. DfPO is evaluated on the GRUE benchmark and reportedly achieves comparable performance to PPO and NLPO while preserving naturalness. However, the paper's claims about the effectiveness of DfPO in preserving naturalness are questioned, and the method's novelty is limited due to its similarity to existing methods like NLPO.

**Strengths:**
- The paper addresses an important and relevant issue in the field of LLM fine-tuning, specifically the degeneration problem that arises when optimizing LLMs for specific tasks.
- The proposed method, DfPO, is well-motivated and easy to follow, with a clear presentation of the algorithm and its components.
- The paper includes comprehensive experimental results on the GRUE benchmark, demonstrating that DfPO can achieve comparable performance to existing methods like PPO and NLPO without the need for hyperparameter tuning.
- The use of action-masked policies and clipped advantage functions is innovative and contributes to the field by potentially improving the naturalness of generated texts.
- The paper provides a thorough analysis of the degeneration problem and proposes a novel solution, which could be beneficial for future research in the area.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper's claims about the effectiveness of DfPO in preserving naturalness are not convincingly supported by the experimental results. Specifically, the perplexity scores of DfPO are higher than those of PPO and NLPO, which contradicts the assertion that DfPO preserves naturalness.
- The novelty of the proposed method is limited as it closely resembles existing methods like NLPO, with similar components and approaches.
- The paper lacks a detailed comparison with state-of-the-art methods like RLHF, which could provide a better understanding of DfPO's effectiveness and limitations.
- There is a lack of clarity and detailed explanation in some sections of the paper, particularly in the algorithmic descriptions and the motivation behind certain design choices.
- The paper does not adequately address the potential computational costs and inference overheads associated with the proposed method, which could impact its practical applicability.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors clarify how the action-masked policies are implemented in practice, especially in the context of the GPT-J-6B model used in the experiments?
- How does the proposed method compare to other state-of-the-art methods like RLHF, especially in terms of computational efficiency and practical applicability?
- Given the similarities to NLPO, how does DfPO differ in its approach and what are the specific advantages of using DfPO over NLPO?
- Could the authors provide more detailed experimental results, particularly in terms of perplexity scores and other metrics that directly assess the naturalness of generated texts?
- What are the specific advantages of DfPO over existing methods in terms of preserving naturalness, and how does it address the degeneration problem more effectively?
