 **Summary:**
The paper explores a zero-shot unlearning method using a discrete key-value bottleneck (DKVB) in a neural network. This method involves masking key-value pairs associated with a forget class to unlearn it, while retaining the performance on other classes. The paper demonstrates that this method can achieve effective unlearning without additional computational costs, which is a significant advantage over other unlearning techniques that require retraining or fine-tuning. The approach is evaluated on various datasets, showing competitive results with existing methods like SCRUB, although the novelty and applicability of the method are questioned due to its reliance on the DKVB architecture and its limited scalability.

**Strengths:**
- The paper addresses an important problem of unlearning, which is crucial for improving the reliability and privacy of machine learning models.
- The method proposed is novel, particularly the use of a discrete key-value bottleneck (DKVB) for unlearning, which is a creative application of this architecture.
- The paper is well-written, clear, and easy to follow, making it accessible to a broad audience.
- The method achieves zero-shot unlearning, which is a significant advancement in the field and reduces the computational cost associated with other unlearning methods.
- The experimental results demonstrate that the proposed method can achieve unlearning effectively without damaging the performance on the retain set, which is a critical aspect of unlearning.
- The paper includes a thorough analysis of the proposed method, including a comparison with state-of-the-art methods and a discussion on the limitations of the approach.

**Weaknesses:**
- The method's novelty is somewhat limited as it primarily builds upon the DKVB architecture, which might limit its broader applicability.
- The method's effectiveness is not convincingly demonstrated across a wide range of datasets or in more complex scenarios, such as incremental learning or large-scale datasets.
- The paper lacks a detailed discussion on the limitations of the method, particularly concerning the scalability and generalizability of the findings.
- The experimental setup is somewhat limited, with only a few datasets used, and the method's performance on these datasets is not consistently strong.
- There is a lack of discussion on the computational efficiency of the method, which is claimed to be "negligible," but this claim is not substantiated with detailed computational analysis.
- The paper could benefit from a more comprehensive discussion on the societal implications of unlearning, particularly in terms of privacy and data protection.

**Questions:**
- Can the method be adapted to other architectures beyond the DKVB, and if so, how might this affect its performance and applicability?
- How does the method perform when applied to different types of data, such as images versus text data?
- Could the authors provide more details on the computational efficiency of the method, particularly in terms of the actual computational resources required for unlearning?
- How does the method perform when applied to different types of unlearning tasks, such as data unlearning versus concept unlearning?
- Can the method be scaled up to handle larger datasets or more complex models, and what are the expected performance outcomes in such scenarios?
- How does the method compare to other unlearning methods that do not require additional computational resources, such as those based on knowledge distillation?
- Could the authors discuss the potential societal implications of their work, particularly in terms of privacy and data protection, and how their method might address or exacerbate these issues?
