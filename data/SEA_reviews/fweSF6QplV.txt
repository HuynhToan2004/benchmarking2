 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel optimization-based framework for learning coarsened graphs with desirable structures and properties, aiming to address scalability issues in Graph Neural Networks (GNNs) by reducing large graphs to smaller, tractable ones. The proposed framework utilizes block majorization-minimization, log determinant, structured regularization, and spectral regularization to optimize the coarsening process. This approach is validated through extensive experiments on real benchmark datasets, demonstrating improvements in node classification tasks across various GNN architectures. The framework is designed to preserve the structure of the coarsened graph, which is crucial for downstream applications. However, concerns about the novelty of the approach and the clarity of the experimental setup and results persist.
