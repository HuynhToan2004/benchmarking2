 **Summary:**
The paper introduces Conditional Slot Attention (CoSA), a novel approach to object-centric representation learning that utilizes a Grounded Slot Dictionary (GSD) to learn specialized slots for specific object types. CoSA extends the capabilities of Slot Attention (SA) by dynamically estimating the number of slots required for a given scene and enabling the learning of specialized slots that bind to specific object types, thereby addressing the limitations of SA. The GSD is derived from a codebook of canonical object-centric representations and parametric Gaussian distributions, which define a prior over the slots. The paper demonstrates the effectiveness of CoSA through extensive experiments on various benchmarks, showing improvements over SA in object discovery tasks and comparable performance in scene generation and composition tasks.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel approach to object-centric representation learning by combining slot attention with vector quantization, which is a significant contribution to the field.
- The Grounded Slot Dictionary (GSD) is a novel and interesting component that allows for the learning of specialized slots for specific object types, which is a significant advancement over previous methods.
- The paper is well-written and easy to follow, with clear explanations of the methodology and results.
- The proposed method is evaluated on various datasets and benchmarks, showing improvements over Slot Attention (SA) in object discovery tasks and competitive performance in scene generation and composition tasks.
- The use of vector quantization to learn specialized slot representations is a novel approach that could potentially improve the generalization and adaptability of object-centric models.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed comparison with existing methods like SlotVAE and other recent object-centric learning methods, which could have strengthened the argument for the superiority of the proposed method.
- The explanation of the Grounded Slot Dictionary (GSD) and its components, such as the canonical object-level property vectors and parametric Gaussian distributions, is not sufficiently detailed, making it difficult for readers to fully understand the methodology.
- The paper does not provide sufficient details on the computational efficiency of the proposed method compared to existing methods, which could be a significant concern for practical applications.
- The method's ability to handle more complex scenes with multiple overlapping objects or varying object appearances is not thoroughly addressed, which could limit its applicability in real-world scenarios.
- The paper does not discuss the limitations of the proposed method in detail, which could help in understanding the scope and applicability of the findings.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors provide a more detailed comparison with existing methods like SlotVAE and other recent object-centric learning methods to better establish the superiority of the proposed method?
- How does the proposed method handle scenes with multiple overlapping objects or varying object appearances? Are there any strategies or modifications to the method that could improve its performance in such scenarios?
- What are the specific advantages of the proposed method over existing methods in terms of computational efficiency and practical applicability?
- How does the proposed method perform in scenarios where the number of object instances is significantly larger or smaller than expected?
- Can the authors provide more detailed explanations of the Grounded Slot Dictionary (GSD) and its components, particularly the canonical object-level property vectors and parametric Gaussian distributions?
