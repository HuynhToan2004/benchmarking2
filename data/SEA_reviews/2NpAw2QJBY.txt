 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel learning-based approach for Multi-Agent Pathfinding (MAPF) using Large Neighborhood Search (LNS), specifically focusing on neural-guided LNS. The proposed method leverages a deep neural network to evaluate the improvement of agent subsets, which is then used to guide the LNS process. The architecture integrates convolution and attention mechanisms to efficiently represent MAPF subproblems, enabling practical guidance of LNS in benchmark settings. The approach demonstrates significant speedups over existing state-of-the-art LNS-based methods for MAPF and robustness to unseen settings. However, the paper has been critiqued for its lack of detailed theoretical analysis and comparison with more recent baselines.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel approach by combining convolution and attention mechanisms to represent MAPF subproblems, which is a significant advancement in the field.
- The proposed method demonstrates a significant speedup over existing state-of-the-art LNS-based methods for MAPF, highlighting its practical applicability and efficiency.
- The methodology is robust to unseen settings, showcasing its adaptability and potential for broader application in various path planning scenarios.
- The paper is well-written, making it easy to follow, and the experiments are thorough, providing a solid foundation for the claims made.
- The idea of using a deep neural network to guide LNS is innovative and could be a significant contribution to the field if the method can be further improved and applied to other combinatorial optimization problems.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed theoretical analysis, which could strengthen the understanding of the proposed method's effectiveness and efficiency.
- There is a need for more comprehensive comparisons with recent baselines, particularly those that have shown significant advancements in the field.
- The paper does not sufficiently discuss the scalability of the proposed method, which is crucial for its broader applicability.
- There are some presentation issues, such as minor typos and unclear figures, which could detract from the overall quality of the paper.
- The paper does not adequately address the computational overhead introduced by the neural network, which could be a significant drawback in real-world applications where computational resources are limited.
- The paper could benefit from a more detailed discussion on the limitations of the proposed method and potential avenues for future research.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors provide a detailed theoretical analysis of the proposed method, including its effectiveness and efficiency?
- How does the proposed method compare with other recent baselines, particularly those that have shown significant advancements in the field?
- Can the authors discuss the scalability of the proposed method and its potential applications in other combinatorial optimization problems?
- How does the proposed method handle different subset construction heuristics, and what are the implications of using different heuristics on the performance of the method?
- Could the authors clarify the presentation issues, such as the minor typos and unclear figures, to enhance the clarity and professionalism of the paper?
- How does the proposed method address the computational overhead introduced by the neural network, and what strategies could be employed to minimize this overhead in real-world applications?
