 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel method for sleep stage classification using a knowledge distillation framework that incorporates spatial and temporal knowledge modules. The framework aims to transfer knowledge from a teacher model to a student model, focusing on the extraction and transfer of spatial-temporal knowledge from multi-channel sleep signals. The experiments demonstrate that the proposed method achieves state-of-the-art performance on two datasets, ISRUC-III and MASS-SS3, while maintaining a lightweight model. The paper also includes an ablation study to validate the effectiveness of the proposed modules. However, the novelty of the method is questioned as it primarily builds on existing knowledge distillation techniques without significant innovation. The paper is well-written and the experiments are comprehensive, but the method's novelty and the depth of its technical contributions are limited.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written and easy to follow, with clear motivation and logical presentation of the proposed method.
- The experiments are comprehensive, including a detailed ablation study that supports the effectiveness of the proposed method.
- The method achieves state-of-the-art performance on two datasets, demonstrating its effectiveness in real-world applications.
- The proposed method is technically sound and innovative, utilizing spatial and temporal knowledge modules to enhance the performance of sleep stage classification.
- The paper is well-organized, with clear definitions and explanations of the methodology, making it accessible to a broad audience.

**Weaknesses:**
- The novelty of the method is limited as it primarily builds on existing knowledge distillation techniques without significant innovation.
- The paper lacks a thorough discussion on the limitations of the proposed method, which could help in understanding its applicability and potential drawbacks.
- The technical contribution of the paper is limited, as the methodology is similar to existing works, and the paper does not sufficiently distinguish its approach from previous research.
- The paper does not provide sufficient details on the implementation and training of the teacher model, which could affect the reproducibility and comparability of the results.
- The paper does not include a comparison with the latest state-of-the-art methods, which could provide a more robust evaluation of the proposed method's performance.
- The paper lacks a discussion on the potential societal impacts of the proposed method, which could be a significant consideration in healthcare applications.

**Questions:**
- How does the proposed method compare with other recent state-of-the-art methods, particularly those that use more advanced models or different distillation techniques?
- Can the authors provide more details on the implementation and training of the teacher model, including the choice of hyperparameters and the effects of different training strategies?
- How does the proposed method handle the variability in sleep data across different individuals, and what are the implications for the generalizability of the results?
- Could the authors elaborate on the potential societal impacts of the proposed method, particularly in the context of healthcare applications?
- What are the potential limitations of the proposed method, and how do these limitations affect the practical applicability of the method in real-world scenarios?
- Can the authors provide more details on the experimental setup, including the specifics of the datasets used and the preprocessing techniques employed?
