 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a method leveraging large language models (LLMs) to interpret neuron activations in vision models, specifically using GPT-3.5 to generate explanations based on neuron activations and weights. The approach involves training a vision-language model to associate image captions with neuron activations, which are then used to generate explanations. The paper also proposes a scoring metric based on correlation between simulated and actual neuron activations. The method is evaluated against existing approaches and shows promising results, although concerns are raised about the novelty and depth of the evaluation.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, easy to follow, and provides a clear methodology for generating explanations of neurons in vision models.
- The proposed method is simple and effective, with a straightforward approach to using LLMs for generating explanations.
- The paper includes a novel scoring metric that can be used to evaluate the quality of explanations, which is a significant contribution to the field.
- The method is evaluated against existing approaches and shows promising results, indicating its potential effectiveness.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks novelty as it primarily adapts the method from language models to vision models without significant innovation.
- The evaluation is limited to a single neuron in AlexNet, which does not adequately demonstrate the method's effectiveness across different models or neurons.
- The paper does not sufficiently address the issue of polysemantic neurons, which is a significant limitation in the interpretability of vision models.
- The use of LLMs for generating explanations is not new, and the paper does not sufficiently differentiate its approach from existing methods.
- The method's reliance on LLMs for generating explanations raises concerns about the accuracy and reliability of the explanations, especially in complex or ambiguous scenarios.
- The paper does not adequately address the scalability and computational efficiency of the method, which could be a significant limitation in practical applications.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors provide more details on how the explanations generated by the LLM align with the actual activations of the neurons?
- How does the method perform when applied to different layers of the network, especially those further away from the output layer?
- Could the authors elaborate on the methodology used for selecting neurons and the criteria for their selection?
- How does the method handle polysemantic neurons, and what steps are taken to ensure that the explanations accurately reflect the neuron's behavior?
- Could the authors provide a more detailed analysis of the computational cost and efficiency of the method, especially in comparison to other existing methods?
- Can the authors discuss the limitations of the method and potential directions for future research to address these limitations?
