 **Summary:**
The paper investigates the use of simple, low-rank kernel models in Graph Neural Networks (GNNs) for semi-supervised node classification (SSNC) tasks. It critiques the complexity of current GNN architectures and proposes a return to traditional methods, emphasizing the importance of evaluation conventions in recent performance improvements. The authors conduct extensive ablation studies to explore the impact of different hyperparameters on model performance, comparing these models against state-of-the-art methods. Despite the paper's focus on simplifying GNNs, concerns were raised about the novelty of the approach, the lack of significant performance improvements over existing methods, and the clarity of the experimental setup and results.

**Strengths:**
- The paper addresses the need for simpler and more interpretable models in the field of graph neural networks (GNNs), which is a significant issue in the community.
- It conducts an extensive ablation study, which is crucial for understanding the impact of different hyperparameters on model performance.
- The paper is well-written and easy to follow, with a clear motivation and a good introduction to the problem.
- The proposed method is simple and effective, with experiments demonstrating its practical utility.
- The authors provide a thorough analysis of the evaluation conventions, which is crucial for understanding the performance of different models.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper's main claim about the over-engineering of current GNN architectures is not convincingly supported by the results, which do not show significant performance improvements over existing methods.
- The novelty of the proposed method is questionable, as it appears to be a combination of existing techniques without significant new insights or contributions.
- There is a lack of clarity in the experimental setup and results, making it difficult to compare the proposed method with state-of-the-art GNNs.
- The paper does not adequately address the issue of interpretability in GNNs, which is a critical concern for many applications.
- The paper's focus on linear GNNs and the choice of kernels may limit its applicability to more complex GNN architectures and a broader range of datasets.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors clarify how their method compares with other state-of-the-art GNNs, especially in terms of performance and interpretability?
- How does the proposed method perform on larger datasets, and what are the implications for its scalability?
- Could the authors provide more details on the experimental setup, including the specifics of the datasets used and the methodology for selecting hyperparameters?
- How does the proposed method address the issue of over-engineering in current GNN architectures, and what are the specific benefits of using low-rank kernel models over more complex architectures?
- Can the authors elaborate on the choice of kernels and their impact on the performance of the model?
- How does the proposed method handle the issue of interpretability in GNNs, and what steps have been taken to ensure that the model's predictions are explainable and understandable?
