 **Summary:**
The paper introduces TILDE-Q, a novel loss function for time-series forecasting that addresses amplitude and phase distortions, enhancing the modeling of temporal dynamics. TILDE-Q is designed to be invariant to these distortions, ensuring that models trained with it can effectively capture the shapes of time-series sequences. The paper includes extensive experiments across various real-world applications, demonstrating that TILDE-Q outperforms other metrics like MSE and DILATE in terms of both short-term and long-term forecasting accuracy. Despite its strengths, the paper is criticized for its lack of clarity in some sections, particularly in the definition of terms and the structure of the experimental results.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel approach to time-series forecasting by focusing on the shape of the time-series, which is a significant advancement in the field.
- The TILDE-Q loss function is designed to be invariant to amplitude and phase distortions, ensuring that the model's predictions accurately reflect the underlying data.
- The paper is well-written, making it accessible and easy to understand, which is crucial for a broad audience.
- Extensive experiments are conducted across various real-world applications, demonstrating the effectiveness of TILDE-Q in both short-term and long-term forecasting scenarios.
- The paper includes a comprehensive review of existing literature, providing a solid foundation for the proposed methodology.
- The proposed method shows improvements over existing metrics like MSE and DILATE, indicating its practical utility.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks clarity in some sections, particularly in the definitions and explanations of terms such as "shape" and "distortion."
- There is a lack of detailed discussion on the choice of hyperparameters, which could affect the reproducibility and reliability of the results.
- The paper does not provide a detailed comparison with other shape-aware loss functions, which could help in understanding the relative advantages of TILDE-Q.
- The experimental results are presented in a confusing manner, with insufficient explanation and visualization of the results, making it difficult to assess the effectiveness of the proposed method.
- The paper does not adequately address the limitations of the proposed method, which could help in understanding its applicability and scope.
- The paper does not include a detailed discussion on the computational complexity of TILDE-Q, which could be a significant concern for practical applications.

**Questions:**
- Could you clarify the definitions of "shape" and "distortion" as used in the paper?
- How are the hyperparameters chosen, and what impact do they have on the results?
- Could you provide a more detailed comparison with other shape-aware loss functions, highlighting the advantages of TILDE-Q?
- How does TILDE-Q perform when compared to other state-of-the-art methods, and what are the specific advantages of using TILDE-Q over these methods?
- Could you provide a detailed explanation of the experimental results, including visualizations and a discussion on the significance of the improvements observed?
- How does TILDE-Q handle different types of distortions beyond amplitude and phase, and what are the implications of this for the model's effectiveness?
- What are the potential limitations of TILDE-Q, and under what conditions might it not be effective?
