 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel approach to constructing prediction sets under label shift conditions, leveraging PAC guarantees. The methodology involves estimating importance weights and their confidence intervals, which are then used to construct prediction sets. The paper provides theoretical guarantees and empirical evidence across multiple datasets, demonstrating the effectiveness of the proposed method in producing smaller prediction sets compared to existing methods. The approach is based on a Gaussian elimination algorithm and is evaluated against several baselines, showing promising results. However, the paper has been critiqued for its clarity in explaining the methodology, particularly in the algorithmic descriptions and the assumptions used.

**Strengths:**
- The paper addresses a significant and practical problem in the field of conformal prediction under label shift, which is both theoretically and empirically validated.
- The proposed algorithm is simple, intuitive, and provides PAC guarantees, which is a notable strength in the context of label shift.
- The paper is well-written, clear, and easy to follow, with a clear and well-structured presentation of the methodology and results.
- The methodology is novel, particularly the use of Gaussian elimination to propagate confidence intervals, which is a potentially useful approach in other prediction set constructions.
- The paper provides a comprehensive experimental evaluation across various datasets, demonstrating the effectiveness of the proposed method.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks clarity in explaining the algorithmic details, particularly in Algorithm 1, which makes it difficult to understand the methodology fully.
- There are several typographical errors and inconsistencies in the notation throughout the paper, which could confuse readers and undermine the credibility of the work.
- The paper's contribution is somewhat limited as it heavily relies on existing methods and does not sufficiently differentiate its approach from prior work.
- The paper could benefit from a more detailed discussion on the assumptions used, particularly Assumption 2.3, which is crucial for the method's applicability.
- The experimental setup and results could be improved by including more diverse baselines and a more rigorous comparison to state-of-the-art methods.
- The paper could benefit from a more thorough discussion on the limitations of the proposed method and its assumptions, as well as potential applications beyond the specific problem setting addressed.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors provide a clearer explanation of Algorithm 1, particularly the roles and interactions of the subroutines CPInterval and IWPredictionSet?
- How does the proposed method handle the assumption of invertible confusion matrices in practice, and what are the implications if this assumption is not met?
- Can the authors clarify the notation and definitions used in the paper, particularly the use of CPInterval and IWPredictionSet, and the meaning of certain variables like w^* and τ?
- How does the proposed method compare to other state-of-the-art methods in terms of performance and computational efficiency?
- Could the authors provide a more detailed discussion on the assumptions used in the method, particularly Assumption 2.3, and how these assumptions affect the applicability and effectiveness of the method?
- How does the proposed method perform in scenarios with extreme label shifts or small calibration datasets, and what are the implications of these conditions on the method's performance?
