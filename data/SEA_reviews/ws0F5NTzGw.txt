 **Summary:**
The paper introduces AdapTable, a novel test-time adaptation (TTA) method designed specifically for tabular data, which is known for its complexities and challenges in distribution shifts. The authors address these issues by proposing a two-step process: a shift-aware uncertainty calibrator and a label distribution handler. The calibrator adjusts output probabilities based on the estimated target label distribution, while the handler corrects initial probabilities using calibrated uncertainty. The paper provides extensive experimental results, demonstrating the effectiveness of AdapTable across various datasets and architectures. However, concerns about the clarity of the paper's structure and the necessity of certain components, such as the graph neural network, have been raised.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, with clear and comprehensive explanations of the proposed method, AdapTable, and its components.
- The authors have identified the challenges in tabular data test-time adaptation, such as skewed entropy, complex latent space decision boundaries, and confidence calibration issues, and have proposed a method to address these challenges.
- The paper provides extensive experimental results, demonstrating the effectiveness of AdapTable across various datasets and architectures, including MLP, TabNet, and FT-Transformer.
- The proposed method achieves state-of-the-art results on different model architectures and out-of-distribution benchmarks, showing significant improvements over existing methods.
- The paper includes a detailed analysis of the failure of existing entropy minimization-based test-time adaptation strategies on tabular data, providing valuable insights into the challenges of test-time adaptation in the tabular domain.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a clear motivation for the specific design of AdapTable, particularly the need for a graph neural network and the interpolation of the original label distribution and the estimated target label distribution.
- The experimental results, while promising, are not convincingly superior to existing methods, with some datasets showing only marginal improvements or even lower performance compared to baseline methods.
- The paper's structure could be improved for better clarity and coherence, particularly in the presentation of the methodology and experimental results.
- The paper does not sufficiently address the limitations of the proposed method, which could affect its practical applicability in real-world scenarios.
- The paper's focus on tabular data, while interesting, may limit its broader applicability to other domains, such as image classification, where test-time adaptation is more prevalent.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors clarify the necessity and design of the graph neural network in the shift-aware uncertainty calibrator?
- How does the paper address the potential issues of overfitting or underfitting in the test-time adaptation process, especially in scenarios where the training data and test data are not well-matched?
- What are the specific advantages of AdapTable over existing methods, particularly in terms of computational efficiency and practical applicability?
- Could the authors provide more detailed explanations or examples to support the claims made in the paper, especially in sections where the methodology and results are not clearly presented?
- How does the proposed method handle the challenge of label distribution shift in tabular data, which is a critical issue in many real-world applications?
