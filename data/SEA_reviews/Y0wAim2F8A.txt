 **Summary:**
The paper introduces PrivilegedDreamer, a model-based reinforcement learning framework that extends Dreamer by incorporating an explicit parameter estimation module. This module predicts hidden parameters from historical data, which are then used to condition the model, actor, and critic networks. The framework is evaluated on five Hidden-parameter Markov Decision Process (HIP-MDP) tasks and demonstrates superior performance compared to state-of-the-art model-based, model-free, and domain adaptation learning algorithms. The authors claim that this method enables more effective handling of hidden parameters in sequential decision problems, particularly in environments where rewards depend on these parameters. However, concerns are raised about the clarity of the paper's presentation, the novelty of the approach, and the robustness of the experimental results.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, making it easy to follow and understand.
- The authors provide a clear motivation for the research, highlighting the challenges posed by hidden parameters in MDPs.
- The proposed method, PrivilegedDreamer, is evaluated on five HIP-MDP tasks and shows superior performance compared to state-of-the-art model-based, model-free, and domain adaptation learning algorithms.
- The paper includes ablation studies that justify the design decisions of the framework.
- The introduction of an explicit parameter estimation module for hidden parameters in sequential decision problems is a novel approach.
- The method is demonstrated to be effective in solving HIP-MDP tasks, showing a significant improvement over existing methods.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed discussion on the limitations of the proposed method, which is crucial for understanding its applicability and effectiveness in various scenarios.
- The novelty of the approach is questioned as the concept of incorporating privileged information into world models is not new.
- The experimental results are not robust, with significant variance in the rewards reported, which undermines the reliability of the findings.
- The paper suffers from poor presentation, with unclear figures and a lack of detailed explanations for the experimental setup and results.
- There is a lack of comparison with other relevant works, such as "Meta-RL for Rapid Motor Adaptation in Unseen Environments," which also addresses similar issues.
- The paper does not adequately discuss the limitations of the proposed method, which could include the complexity of the model, the need for significant computational resources, and the potential for overfitting in complex environments.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors provide a detailed discussion on the limitations of the proposed method?
- How does the proposed method compare with other recent works such as "Meta-RL for Rapid Motor Adaptation in Unseen Environments"?
- What are the specific advantages of the proposed method over existing methods like DreamerV2, especially in terms of computational efficiency and accuracy?
- How does the method perform in environments where the reward does not depend on the hidden parameters?
- Can the authors clarify the experimental setup, particularly the role of the hidden variable in the tasks and the training conditions for the baselines?
- Could the authors provide more detailed explanations for the figures and results, especially those that are not clear or are misleading?
