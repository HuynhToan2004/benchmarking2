 **Summary:**
The paper introduces Mustard, a novel data generation framework that leverages a large language model (LLM) and a theorem prover to synthesize mathematical problems and their formal proofs. Mustard generates a diverse set of problems, including theorem-proving and word problems, across different educational levels, ensuring that each problem is accompanied by a formal proof that passes the Lean Prover. The framework is designed to enhance the quality and diversity of mathematical reasoning data, which is crucial for training and evaluating LLMs in mathematical reasoning tasks. The paper also introduces the MustardSauce dataset, which consists of 7,335 valid data points, and demonstrates that fine-tuning a 7B LLM on this dataset significantly improves its performance on various mathematical reasoning benchmarks.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel data generation framework called Mustard, which utilizes a combination of LLM and theorem prover to generate high-quality mathematical data.
- The framework is designed to generate diverse and challenging problems, which are crucial for training and evaluating LLMs in mathematical reasoning tasks.
- The proposed dataset, MustardSauce, is of high quality and diversity, and has been demonstrated to improve the performance of LLMs in mathematical reasoning tasks.
- The paper provides extensive experiments and analysis on the generated data, demonstrating the effectiveness of the framework in enhancing the mathematical reasoning capabilities of LLMs.
- The paper is well-organized, clearly written, and easy to follow, making it accessible to a broad audience.
- The methodology is well-explained, and the results are convincing, showing that the proposed framework can generate high-quality mathematical data that is useful for training and evaluating LLMs.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed discussion on how the proposed framework addresses the issue of insufficient training steps in current benchmarks, which is a significant motivation for the research.
- The evaluation of the generated data is limited to the performance of LLMs, and does not include a human evaluation which could provide valuable insights into the quality and clarity of the generated problems.
- The paper does not discuss the limitations of the proposed framework, such as potential biases introduced by the LLM or the theorem prover, and the scalability of the framework to more complex mathematical concepts.
- There is a lack of comparison with other data synthesis methods, which could help in understanding the advantages and disadvantages of the proposed method.
- The paper does not discuss the potential negative societal impacts of the proposed work, which is an important consideration for any technological innovation.

**Questions:**
- Could you provide more details on how the proposed framework addresses the issue of insufficient training steps in current benchmarks?
- How does the framework ensure that the generated problems are not biased towards certain mathematical concepts or educational levels?
- Can you elaborate on the steps taken to ensure the quality and clarity of the generated problems, particularly in terms of their readability and the complexity of the mathematical concepts involved?
- How does the framework scale to more complex mathematical concepts, and what are the potential limitations in this regard?
- Could you provide more details on the human evaluation process, including the number of evaluators, the criteria used for evaluation, and the inter-rater reliability?
- How does the proposed framework compare to other data synthesis methods in terms of effectiveness and efficiency?
- What are the potential negative societal impacts of the proposed work, and how are these being addressed?
