 **Summary:**
The paper investigates the use of Self-Repellent Random Walks (SRRW) in distributed stochastic optimization, focusing on the convergence properties of the SRRW-based token algorithm. It builds on the theoretical framework established by Doshi et al. (2023) and extends it to a distributed setting, showing that the optimization iterate errors converge to zero almost surely and proving a central limit theorem. The paper also provides explicit expressions for the asymptotic covariance matrix of the iterate errors, which is always smaller than that of an algorithm driven by a base Markov chain. Numerical simulations are conducted to support the theoretical findings, demonstrating the practical effectiveness of the proposed methods.

**Strengths:**
- The paper provides a detailed analysis of the convergence properties of the SRRW-based token algorithm, extending the theoretical framework established by Doshi et al. (2023) to a distributed setting.
- The authors have derived explicit expressions for the asymptotic covariance matrix of the iterate errors, which are always smaller than those of an algorithm driven by a base Markov chain.
- The paper is well-written, with clear and concise explanations of the theoretical results and the methodology used.
- The use of self-repellent random walks in token algorithms for distributed stochastic optimization is a novel approach that could potentially improve the performance of existing methods.
- The paper includes numerical simulations that support the theoretical findings, demonstrating the practical effectiveness of the proposed methods.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper could benefit from a more detailed discussion on the practical implementation of the SRRW-based token algorithm in distributed systems, particularly in terms of the computational complexity and the scalability of the method.
- The assumptions made in the paper, such as the boundedness of the iterates and the existence of at least one globally stable equilibrium, are quite strong and not well-justified.
- The paper's contributions are somewhat limited as it heavily relies on existing theoretical results from Doshi et al. (2023), with the main novelty being the extension to a distributed setting and the proof of a central limit theorem.
- The paper could benefit from a more thorough discussion of the limitations of the proposed method and a comparison with other existing methods in the field.
- The paper could be improved by including more detailed discussions on the proofs and the technical challenges involved in extending the results from Doshi et al. (2023) to the distributed setting.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors provide more details on the practical implementation of the SRRW-based token algorithm in distributed systems, including the computational complexity and scalability of the method?
- How does the choice of the step size affect the performance of the algorithm, and can the authors provide recommendations on the optimal step size for different scenarios?
- Is there a possibility to extend the analysis to non-ergodic Markov chains, and what are the main challenges in doing so?
- Could the authors clarify the role of the step size in the convergence results and whether the results hold for any step size?
- How does the paper's assumption of boundedness of the iterates affect the results, and can this assumption be relaxed?
- Can the authors provide a more detailed discussion on the technical challenges involved in extending the results from Doshi et al. (2023) to the distributed setting?
