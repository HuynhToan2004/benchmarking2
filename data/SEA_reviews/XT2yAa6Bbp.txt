 **Summary:**
The paper introduces Sinkhorn Output Perturbations (SOP), a novel method for semi-supervised segmentation that perturbs the output of segmentation models to enhance performance. SOP operates by optimizing the output of segmentation models using the Sinkhorn-Knopp algorithm, aiming to improve pseudo-labels by reallocating them to better match the predictions of a teacher model. The method is evaluated on standard datasets such as Cityscapes and Pascal VOC 2012, showing competitive performance and state-of-the-art results in some cases. The paper also explores the impact of various parameters and their ablations, demonstrating the method's effectiveness and robustness. However, concerns are raised about the novelty of the approach, as similar methods have been previously proposed, and the method's effectiveness is questioned when compared to existing state-of-the-art methods.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, making it easy to follow and understand.
- The proposed method, Sinkhorn Output Perturbations (SOP), is novel and effective, demonstrating state-of-the-art performance on standard datasets like Cityscapes and Pascal VOC 2012.
- The paper provides a comprehensive ablation study, which helps in understanding the impact of different components of the method.
- The method is simple yet effective, with a clear motivation and a well-designed objective function.
- The experiments are thorough, covering various datasets and different backbones, demonstrating the robustness and applicability of the method.
- The paper is well-organized, with clear figures and tables that aid in understanding the results and methodology.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed comparison with state-of-the-art methods, particularly in terms of performance metrics and methodological details.
- The novelty of the method is questionable, as similar methods have been previously proposed, such as the use of Sinkhorn-Knopp for pseudo-labeling in semi-supervised learning.
- The paper does not adequately address the potential negative societal impacts of the proposed method.
- The experimental results show that the proposed method is less effective compared to other state-of-the-art methods, particularly in terms of performance metrics like mIoU.
- The paper does not sufficiently discuss the limitations of the proposed method, which could affect its practical applicability and generalizability.
- The paper could benefit from more detailed explanations and justifications for certain methodological choices, such as the use of the Sinkhorn-Knopp algorithm and the design of the objective function.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors clarify the differences between their method and similar methods proposed in previous works, particularly in terms of methodology and results?
- How does the proposed method compare to other state-of-the-art methods in terms of performance and computational efficiency?
- Could the authors provide more details on the training procedures, such as the number of iterations and the computational requirements?
- How does the proposed method perform in scenarios with different data distributions or when the data is not well-balanced?
- Could the authors provide more detailed experimental results, including failure cases and additional datasets, to better demonstrate the robustness and applicability of the method?
- How does the proposed method handle class imbalance in the data, and what are the implications for the performance and generalizability of the method?
