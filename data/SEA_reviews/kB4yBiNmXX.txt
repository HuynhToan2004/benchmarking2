 **Summary:**
The paper introduces FasterViT, a hybrid architecture that integrates CNN and Vision Transformer (ViT) components, aiming to address the computational inefficiencies of pure ViT models. The primary innovation is the Hierarchical Attention (HAT) module, which decomposes global self-attention into a multi-level attention mechanism to reduce computational complexity. FasterViT demonstrates competitive performance in terms of accuracy and throughput on various benchmarks, including ImageNet-1K, MS COCO, and ADE20K. The model's architecture is designed to optimize memory and compute usage, particularly beneficial for high-resolution images. Despite its innovative approach and experimental validation, the paper has been criticized for its incremental novelty and lack of comprehensive comparisons with state-of-the-art models.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, with clear explanations of the proposed methodologies, and the figures effectively illustrate the model's components.
- The proposed FasterViT model achieves state-of-the-art performance on various benchmarks, including ImageNet-1K, MS COCO, and ADE20K, while maintaining high throughput.
- The Hierarchical Attention (HAT) module, which decomposes global self-attention into a multi-level attention mechanism, is a novel and effective approach to reduce computational complexity.
- The paper provides extensive experimental results, including ablation studies and comparisons with state-of-the-art models, which demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed method.
- The methodology is well-motivated and well-explained, with a clear focus on improving throughput in vision transformers.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a thorough comparison with state-of-the-art models, particularly in terms of throughput and accuracy. It would be beneficial to include more models in the comparisons, especially those that achieve high throughput while maintaining competitive accuracy.
- The novelty of the proposed method is questioned as it appears to be a combination of existing techniques such as Swin Transformer and the concept of carrier tokens from EdgeViT.
- The paper does not provide sufficient implementation details, such as the size of the carrier tokens and the specifics of the positional encoding used, which are crucial for reproducibility and further research.
- The experimental results, particularly in terms of throughput, are not convincing, with some models showing lower throughput than expected given their size and complexity.
- The paper could benefit from a more detailed discussion on the limitations of the proposed method and potential future research directions.

**Questions:**
- Could you provide more details on the implementation of the carrier tokens, such as their size and how they are initialized?
- How does the proposed method compare in terms of throughput and accuracy with state-of-the-art models like ConvNeXt and Swin Transformer, especially when fine-tuned on high-resolution datasets?
- Can you clarify the computational complexity of the Hierarchical Attention module and how it compares to other attention mechanisms in terms of efficiency?
- How does the proposed method handle the issue of memory-bound operations in high-resolution stages, and what are the implications for inference speed?
- It would be beneficial to see more detailed comparisons and ablation studies on the impact of different hyperparameters, such as the number of carrier tokens and window sizes, on the model's performance and throughput.
