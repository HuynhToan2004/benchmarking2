 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel model-free offline reinforcement learning (RL) algorithm named Relaxed State-Adversarial Offline RL (RAORL). This method addresses the challenge of robustness in offline RL by integrating a risk-aware policy and a state-adversarial method. It frames the problem within a state-adversarial context to eliminate the need for explicit environmental modeling, ensuring the policy's robustness and adaptability to varying transition dynamics. Theoretical analysis and empirical evaluations demonstrate that RAORL outperforms existing methods on established offline RL benchmarks, showcasing its potential for improving the robustness of offline RL policies.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, clear, and easy to follow, with a clear motivation and a clear presentation of the method.
- The paper introduces a novel method that integrates risk-aware and adversarial components to address the robustness of offline RL, which is a significant contribution to the field.
- The empirical evaluation is thorough and demonstrates that the proposed method outperforms existing baselines, indicating its practical effectiveness.
- The theoretical analysis is rigorous and provides a solid foundation for the proposed method, enhancing the credibility of the research.
- The paper addresses the problem of robustness in offline RL, which is crucial for practical applications, and proposes a method that can be integrated with existing offline RL algorithms.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed comparison with other state-of-the-art methods, particularly those that address similar challenges in offline RL, such as RORL and other robust RL methods.
- The paper does not provide a detailed analysis of the computational complexity or the scalability of the proposed method, which are important for practical applications.
- The empirical evaluation is limited to specific environments and does not include a broader range of tasks or more complex environments, which could affect the generalizability of the results.
- The paper does not discuss the limitations of the proposed method, which is crucial for understanding its applicability and potential drawbacks.
- There are some minor typographical and formatting errors that need correction.
