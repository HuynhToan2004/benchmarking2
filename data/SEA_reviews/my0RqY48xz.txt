 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel training strategy, Erase-and-Awaken Training Strategy (EATS), for enhancing the generalization capabilities of image super-resolution algorithms. This strategy, grounded in cooperative game theory, treats neurons as participants in a cooperative relationship, aiming to awaken suppressed neurons that hinder generalization. EATS is designed to improve the equitable contributions of all neurons, thereby boosting the generalization performance. Theoretical proofs are provided to support the effectiveness of EATS, which is validated through extensive experiments on various datasets. The methodology is shown to be effective in reducing correlations among channels, promoting feature diversity, and improving the generalization of super-resolution networks.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, clear, and easy to follow, with clear descriptions of the proposed method and its components.
- The Erase-and-Awaken Training Strategy (EATS) is novel and innovative, utilizing Shapley values to theoretically support its effectiveness in promoting equitable contributions among neurons.
- The methodology is simple yet effective, with clear visualizations and explanations that enhance understanding of the proposed approach.
- The experiments are comprehensive and convincing, demonstrating the effectiveness of EATS in enhancing generalization performance and reducing correlations among channels.
- The paper is significant in its application to super-resolution networks, showing potential for broader applicability across other computer vision tasks.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed discussion on the limitations of the proposed method, particularly in terms of its applicability to other tasks and its impact on training time and computational efficiency.
- The theoretical proofs provided are somewhat hand-wavy and could benefit from more rigorous mathematical formulation and proof.
- The experimental results do not show significant improvements over existing methods like Dropout, and the comparison with other state-of-the-art methods is missing.
- The paper does not adequately address the potential negative societal impacts of the proposed method.
- The paper could benefit from a more detailed comparison with other state-of-the-art methods, particularly in terms of training time and computational efficiency.

**Questions:**
