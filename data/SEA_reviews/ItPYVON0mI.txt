 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel coarse-grained (CG) model that integrates a graph neural network (GNN) potential with a high-frequency potential energy surface landscape function to reproduce the structure and dynamics of all-atom systems. The model is designed to capture essential features of fine-grained atomistic models and is evaluated using radial distribution functions (RDFs) and mean-squared displacements (MSDs) for various systems including water, argon, and methane. The authors argue that the high-frequency potential energy landscape term, which is orthogonal to the RDF, helps in accurately modeling the CG dynamics. However, the paper is criticized for its clarity, lack of detailed methodological explanations, and insufficient comparisons with existing models.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel approach to coarse-graining using a combination of a graph neural network (GNN) potential and a high-frequency potential energy surface landscape function, which is a significant advancement in the field.
- The authors provide a detailed explanation of the methodology and the results, which are supported by comprehensive experiments and comparisons with existing models.
- The paper addresses an important and relevant problem in the field of coarse-graining, which is crucial for understanding and modeling complex systems.
- The integration of a high-frequency potential energy surface landscape function into the CG model is a novel and promising approach, potentially enhancing the accuracy and fidelity of the model.
- The paper is well-written, making it accessible and understandable to a broad audience, which is essential for disseminating knowledge in the field.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks clarity in several sections, particularly in the methodological explanations and the definitions of key terms and equations.
- There is a notable absence of detailed comparisons with other existing models, which could have provided a more robust validation of the proposed model.
- The paper does not adequately address the computational efficiency of the proposed model, which is a critical aspect for practical applications.
- The paper does not provide sufficient justification or evidence for the choice of the high-frequency potential energy surface landscape function, which is a crucial component of the model.
- The paper suffers from several typographical and formatting errors, which could detract from its professional presentation and readability.
- The paper does not sufficiently address the limitations and potential negative societal impacts of the proposed model, which is a standard requirement for academic publications.

**Questions:**

- How does the proposed model compare in terms of computational efficiency and accuracy with other existing CG models, such as those based on neural network potentials or other methodologies?
- Could you clarify the definitions and roles of various terms and equations used in the paper, particularly those related to the high-frequency potential energy surface landscape function and the RDF?
- How does the proposed model handle the coarse-graining of different types of molecules or systems, and what are the implications of this for the model's applicability and accuracy?
