 **Summary:**
The paper introduces ERA-Solver, a novel method for fast sampling in diffusion models, leveraging an error-robust Adams solver that utilizes Lagrange interpolation and predictor-corrector sampling. The method aims to address the issue of inaccurate noise estimation in pretrained diffusion models, which is a common challenge in existing fast sampling methods. By using a predictor-corrector method and adaptively selecting Lagrange bases with lower estimation errors, ERA-Solver aims to enhance the robustness and accuracy of the sampling process. The paper presents empirical results demonstrating improvements in FID scores on various datasets, although it lacks a comprehensive theoretical analysis and detailed comparisons with state-of-the-art methods.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, clear, and easy to follow, making it accessible to a broad audience.
- The proposed method, ERA-Solver, is original and innovative, leveraging a predictor-corrector method and adaptively selecting Lagrange bases with lower estimation errors to enhance the robustness and accuracy of the sampling process.
- The method is applicable to any pretrained diffusion model without requiring additional training, which simplifies its implementation.
- The paper presents comprehensive experimental results, demonstrating the effectiveness of ERA-Solver in achieving better FID scores on various datasets compared to previous methods.
- The paper provides a theoretical analysis of the proposed method, which supports its soundness and contributes to its credibility.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed comparison with state-of-the-art methods, particularly in terms of computational efficiency and sampling speed.
- The selection strategy for the buffer of estimated noises and the choice of the power function in the selection process are not well-explained, which could limit the understanding and reproducibility of the results.
- There is a lack of discussion on the computational cost and memory usage of the proposed method, which are crucial for practical applications.
- The paper could benefit from a more detailed explanation of the theoretical underpinnings of the proposed method, particularly the use of implicit Adams solver and the error measure.
- The paper does not adequately address the limitations of the method, such as the potential for slower sampling times due to the use of implicit Adams solver and the need for additional computational resources.

**Questions:**
- How does the proposed method compare in terms of computational efficiency and sampling speed with other state-of-the-art methods?
- Can you provide more details on the selection strategy for the buffer of estimated noises and the choice of the power function in the selection process?
- How does the proposed method perform on more complex datasets like ImageNet-256 and ImageNet-1k, and what are the potential limitations in scaling up the method?
- Could you clarify the computational cost and memory usage of the proposed method, and how these compare to other methods in terms of practical applicability?
- How does the proposed method handle the issue of inaccurate noise estimation in pretrained diffusion models, and what are the potential implications for the robustness and accuracy of the sampling process?
