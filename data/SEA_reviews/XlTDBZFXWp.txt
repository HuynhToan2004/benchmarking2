 **Summary:**
The paper explores the effectiveness of feature preprocessing in differentially private optimization, specifically focusing on the linear classification setting. It introduces DPSGD-F, a new algorithm that combines DPSGD with feature preprocessing to achieve improved optimality gaps. Theoretical analysis supports the claim that the leading term of the error in DPSGD-F is proportional to the diameter of the features, while the error in DPSGD is proportional to the maximum Euclidean norm of features. The paper also presents empirical results on image classification benchmarks, showing that DPSGD-F outperforms DPSGD in terms of accuracy.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel algorithm, DPSGD-F, which combines feature preprocessing with DPSGD, and provides theoretical analysis that supports the claims of improved optimality gaps.
- Theoretical results are well-supported by proofs and experiments, and the paper is well-written with clear and concise explanations.
- The paper addresses a significant problem in the field of private optimization, focusing on the importance of feature preprocessing in achieving near-optimal results.
- The proposed algorithm, DPSGD-F, is shown to outperform DPSGD in terms of accuracy in certain scenarios, which is a practical improvement.
- The paper is well-organized and easy to follow, with clear presentation of the theoretical results and empirical findings.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper primarily focuses on linear classification settings, which may limit its applicability to more complex or non-linear models.
- The empirical results are based on a small set of datasets, which may not fully represent the performance of the proposed algorithm across a broader range of datasets.
- The paper does not discuss the computational complexity of the feature preprocessing steps, which could impact the practical implementation of the algorithm.
- The paper's theoretical contributions are somewhat limited by the restricted model settings, which may not generalize well to more complex models or different types of data.
- The paper could benefit from a more detailed discussion on the computational efficiency and scalability of the proposed algorithm, particularly in terms of memory usage and computational time.

**Questions:**

- Could the authors provide more detailed analysis on the computational complexity and memory usage of the feature preprocessing steps?
- How does the proposed algorithm perform when compared to other state-of-the-art algorithms in terms of accuracy and computational efficiency?
