 **Summary:**
The paper introduces Finite Scalar Quantization (FSQ), a novel approach to replace the vector quantization (VQ) in Variational Autoencoders (VQ-VAEs) with a simpler scheme that projects representations to a few dimensions and quantizes them to a small set of fixed values. This method aims to address the underutilization of codebooks in VQ-VAEs by avoiding complex machinery like auxiliary losses, commitment losses, codebook reseeding, and code splitting. FSQ is shown to achieve comparable performance to VQ-VAEs in various tasks, including image generation, multimodal generation, and dense prediction, without the risk of codebook collapse. The paper also explores the application of FSQ in MaskGIT and UViM, demonstrating its effectiveness and simplicity.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, clear, and easy to follow, with a comprehensive related work section that effectively positions the research within the existing literature.
- The proposed Finite Scalar Quantization (FSQ) method is simple, intuitive, and does not require complex machinery like auxiliary losses, commitment losses, codebook reseeding, and code splitting, which makes it more straightforward and potentially more efficient than traditional VQ-VAEs.
- FSQ achieves competitive performance in various tasks, including image generation, multimodal generation, and dense prediction, without the risk of codebook collapse.
- The paper provides a detailed analysis of the trade-offs between FSQ and VQ, including the scalability of the codebook size, the utilization of codewords, and the representation complexity from a compression angle.
- The FSQ method can be viewed as a drop-in replacement for VQ in various architectures, and its simplicity and effectiveness make it a promising alternative to VQ-VAEs.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper could benefit from a more detailed discussion on the limitations and potential negative societal impacts of the proposed method, particularly in the context of generative models.
- The comparison of FSQ with VQ is not comprehensive, as the paper does not consider other quantization methods like Product quantization or Residual quantization, which could provide a more robust evaluation of FSQ's performance.
- The experimental results are limited to two applications, which might not be sufficient to convincingly demonstrate the general applicability and effectiveness of FSQ across different tasks and datasets.
- The paper does not provide a clear explanation of how the FSQ method avoids codebook collapse, which is a critical issue in VQ-VAEs.
- There is a lack of discussion on the potential negative societal impacts of the proposed method, particularly in the context of generative models.

**Questions:**
- Could you provide more details on the limitations and potential negative societal impacts of the proposed FSQ method, especially in the context of generative models?
- How does the FSQ method avoid codebook collapse, and can you provide more insights into this mechanism?
- Can you expand the experimental evaluation to include more tasks and datasets to demonstrate the general applicability and effectiveness of FSQ?
- How does the FSQ method compare with other quantization methods like Product quantization or Residual quantization, particularly in terms of performance and computational efficiency?
- Can you clarify the experimental setup, especially the training details for MaskGIT and UViM, to ensure reproducibility and transparency?
