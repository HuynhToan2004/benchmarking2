 **Summary:**
The paper introduces RealSurf, a novel framework designed to process LiDAR point clouds using a point sliding module and surfel abstraction. RealSurf is built on a PointNet++ backbone and achieves state-of-the-art performance on benchmarks such as nuScenes, SemanticKITTI, and Waymo. The framework addresses challenges like sparsity, density variation, and large-scale point clouds by utilizing a jittering mechanism and a LiDAR-based surfel reconstruction process. RealSurf's effectiveness is demonstrated through extensive experiments and ablation studies, showing improvements over existing methods. However, the paper is criticized for its limited novelty and lack of comprehensive comparisons with more recent and relevant methods.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written and easy to follow, with clear explanations of the challenges in processing LiDAR point clouds and the proposed solutions.
- The proposed Point Sliding Module is novel and effective, demonstrating significant improvements over existing methods like PointNet++.
- The method achieves state-of-the-art performance on multiple benchmarks such as nuScenes, SemanticKITTI, and Waymo, without using complex model ensembles or multi-frame testing.
- The paper provides a detailed analysis of the challenges in LiDAR point cloud processing and proposes effective solutions, including a simple PointNet++ backbone.
- The proposed methods are general and can be easily integrated into any point-based network, enhancing the learning of geometric features from point clouds.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a comprehensive comparison with more recent and relevant methods, such as those mentioned in the review.
- The novelty of the proposed method is limited, as it is largely based on existing methods like RepSurf, with minor modifications.
- The paper does not adequately address the computational complexity of the proposed methods, which might not be suitable for real-time applications.
- The paper does not provide sufficient implementation details, such as the code for reproducibility, which is crucial for validating the results.
- The paper's claims of achieving state-of-the-art performance are not entirely accurate, as it does not compare favorably with the latest state-of-the-art methods.
- The paper does not sufficiently address the limitations of the proposed method, which could impact its practical applicability in real-world scenarios.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors clarify how the proposed method performs in real-time applications, considering the computational complexity?
- How does the proposed method compare to more recent and relevant methods like those mentioned in the review?
- Can the authors provide more detailed implementation details, including the code for reproducibility and a more comprehensive comparison with state-of-the-art methods?
- Could the authors elaborate on the limitations of the proposed method and discuss potential solutions or improvements for future work?
