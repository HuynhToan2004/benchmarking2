 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel 3D object detection framework based on the DETR architecture, which includes a new 3D Vertex Relative Position Encoding (3DV-RPE) method to enhance the locality of queries in 3D object detection. This approach aims to improve the performance of DETR in 3D scenarios by focusing on the relative position of points to predicted boxes, thereby reducing the attention to distant points. The methodology is tested on indoor 3D object detection benchmarks like ScanNetV2 and SUN RGB-D, demonstrating significant improvements in performance metrics such as AP25 and AP50. However, the paper is criticized for its lack of novelty, as it heavily relies on existing methods like DETR and does not sufficiently address the challenges of outdoor 3D object detection.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-organized and easy to follow, making it accessible to readers.
- The proposed 3DV-RPE is a novel method that effectively improves the performance of DETR in 3D object detection tasks, particularly in indoor scenarios.
- The method is simple yet effective, and the results show significant improvements over previous methods.
- The paper includes thorough ablation studies and detailed visualizations, which help in understanding the method's effectiveness.
- The approach is well-motivated and the paper is clearly written, making it easy to understand the contributions and the methodology.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks novelty as it heavily relies on existing methods like DETR, with the main contribution being the 3DV-RPE which is a minor modification.
- The methodology is not sufficiently detailed, particularly in the explanation of the DETR framework and the implementation of the 3DV-RPE.
- The paper does not adequately address the challenges of outdoor 3D object detection, which is a significant limitation given the increasing interest in this area.
- The paper's claims about the limitations of DETR in 3D object detection are not convincingly supported by the results, which show only marginal improvements over existing methods.
- The paper could benefit from a more comprehensive comparison with other state-of-the-art methods, including those that do not use DETR.

**Questions:**
- Could you clarify the role and implementation of the DETR framework in your method, especially how it is used to predict boxes?
- How does the 3DV-RPE method perform in outdoor scenarios, and what modifications would be necessary for it to be effective in such environments?
- Can you provide more details on the training data and the training process, particularly how the initial 3D object queries are generated and how the model is trained to predict these queries?
- How does the method handle occlusions and clutter in the point clouds, and what are the implications of these on the detection performance?
- Could you elaborate on the choice of using a canonical object space and the implications of this choice for the performance and generalizability of the method?
