 **Summary:**
The paper introduces Stable and Expressive Positional Encodings (SPE) for graph neural networks (GNNs), focusing on the stability and expressivity of positional encodings in GNNs. The authors argue that the stability of positional encodings is crucial for generalization and robustness to domain shifts. They propose a novel method that soft partitions eigenspaces based on eigenvalues, allowing for a more robust and expressive positional encoding. The paper presents theoretical results and empirical evidence supporting the stability and expressivity of SPE, demonstrating its effectiveness through various experiments on molecular property prediction and graph substructure counting tasks.

**Strengths:**
- The paper addresses a significant and timely problem in the field of graph neural networks (GNNs) by focusing on the stability of positional encodings, which is crucial for enhancing the generalization and robustness of GNNs.
- The proposed method is innovative and well-explained, providing a clear theoretical framework and empirical evidence to support its effectiveness.
- The paper is well-written and easy to follow, making it accessible to readers and contributing to the clarity and depth of the research.
- The experimental results are robust and demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed method, showing improvements over existing methods in terms of stability and expressivity.
- The paper contributes to the understanding of the expressive power of positional encodings in GNNs, which is a critical area of research in the field.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a comprehensive comparison with existing methods, particularly those that address similar issues in positional encodings. This makes it difficult to assess the true novelty and effectiveness of the proposed method.
- The experimental setup and results are not convincingly robust, with some results (such as those on the Alchemy dataset) not showing significant improvements or even deterioration compared to existing methods.
- The paper does not adequately address the computational complexity and scalability of the proposed method, especially in terms of the number of eigenvectors used and the scalability with larger graphs.
- There are some issues with the clarity and precision of the writing, which could be improved to enhance the paper's readability and comprehensibility.
- The paper does not sufficiently discuss the limitations of the proposed method, which is essential for a complete understanding of its applicability and potential drawbacks.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors provide a more detailed comparison with existing methods, particularly those that address similar issues in positional encodings?
- How does the proposed method compare in terms of computational complexity and scalability with other methods, especially in practical applications where large graphs are involved?
- Could the authors provide more detailed experimental results, including standard deviations and additional datasets, to better support the claims of the paper?
- How does the proposed method perform in scenarios where the graph structure is noisy or incomplete, and what are the implications for the stability and expressivity of the positional encodings?
- Could the authors clarify the relationship between the proposed method and other existing methods that address similar issues in positional encodings, such as those discussed in related works?
