 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel plug-and-play solution for post-training pruning of large language models (LLMs), specifically addressing the challenges of unstructured and N:M structured pruning. It proposes two components: the Relative Importance and Activations (RIA) metric and the Channel Permutation approach. The RIA metric evaluates the importance of each weight in a layer by considering both the weight and activation values, while Channel Permutation rearranges the weights to maximize the sum of retained weight importance under N:M sparsity. The paper claims that these methods outperform existing post-training pruning methods on various LLMs, including LLaMA and OPT, and can even surpass the performance of the original LLaMA2 70B on zero-shot tasks. However, concerns are raised about the clarity of the paper, the novelty of the contributions, and the practical implications of the proposed methods.

**Strengths:**
- The paper presents a novel plug-and-play solution for post-training pruning of LLMs, which is a significant advancement in the field.
- The proposed method, RIA, shows promising results in surpassing existing post-training pruning methods on prevalent LLMs, such as LLaMA and OPT, and can even outperform the original LLaMA2 70B on zero-shot tasks.
- The paper is well-written and easy to follow, making it accessible to a broad audience.
- The experiments are extensive, demonstrating the effectiveness of the proposed method across various models and datasets.
- The paper provides a good ablation study, which helps in understanding the impact of different components of the proposed method.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks clarity in some sections, particularly in the definitions and explanations of key terms and concepts, such as the perplexity (PPL) metric and the N:M sparsity.
- There is a notable absence of a related work section, which could have provided a more comprehensive context for the research.
- The paper does not sufficiently differentiate its contributions from existing methods, such as those by Pool & Yu (2021), which also use input channel permutation for N:M sparsity.
- The novelty of the proposed method is questioned, as it seems to be a combination of existing methods without significant innovation.
- The paper does not adequately address the practical implications of the proposed methods, particularly in terms of their applicability to real-world scenarios and their impact on model performance and efficiency.
- The paper does not provide sufficient details on the implementation of the proposed methods, which could hinder reproducibility and further research.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors clarify the definition and calculation of the perplexity (PPL) metric, especially in relation to the log-likelihood of the model's predictions?
- How does the proposed method compare to the Wanda method in terms of pruning time and the effectiveness of the pruning strategy?
- Can the authors provide more details on the implementation of the proposed methods, particularly the RIA and Channel Permutation, to facilitate reproducibility and further research?
- How does the proposed method address the issue of channel corruption in pruning, and what are the specific advantages of the RIA metric over other pruning metrics?
- In terms of practical applications, how does the proposed method perform in real-world scenarios, and what are the implications for model performance and efficiency?
