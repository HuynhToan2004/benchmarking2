 **Summary:**
The paper introduces CrossTVR, a novel re-ranking framework for text-video retrieval (TVR) that employs a multi-grained video text cross-attention module to capture fine-grained multimodal information from both frame and video levels. The method involves a two-stage training process, where the vision encoder is frozen, and the video text cross attention matching header is trained separately. The proposed method is evaluated on five TVR datasets and shows superior performance over existing state-of-the-art methods. The CrossTVR model is noted for its efficiency in inference and its ability to scale to larger pre-trained vision models. However, the novelty of the approach is questioned, as it is seen as an incremental improvement over existing methods rather than a groundbreaking innovation.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, easy to follow, and provides a clear explanation of the proposed method.
- The introduction of a multi-grained video text cross attention module is a novel approach that effectively captures fine-grained multimodal information from both frame and video levels.
- The CrossTVR model demonstrates significant performance improvements over existing state-of-the-art methods on five TVR datasets, showing its effectiveness and efficiency in inference.
- The method is simple and can be applied to any existing cosine similarity-based method, making it a versatile tool for improving retrieval performance.
- The paper includes comprehensive experimental results and ablation studies that validate the effectiveness of the proposed method.

**Weaknesses:**
- The novelty of the proposed method is limited as it primarily involves the use of multi-grained video text cross attention, which is a common technique in the field.
- The method's contribution to the field is not clearly distinguished from existing methods, particularly those that employ similar techniques.
- The training process of the CrossTVR model is complex, involving a two-stage training process which might be seen as a heuristic approach rather than a principled method.
- The paper lacks a detailed analysis of the limitations and potential negative societal impacts of the proposed method.
- The paper does not provide sufficient experimental results to support the claims made about the scalability and efficiency of the proposed method.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors provide more details on the specific contributions of the proposed method beyond the use of multi-grained video text cross attention?
- How does the CrossTVR model compare to other methods that employ similar techniques, such as those mentioned in related works?
- Can the authors clarify the training process of the CrossTVR model and explain why a two-stage training process was chosen over a more straightforward approach?
- How does the CrossTVR model perform when scaled to larger datasets or when used with different vision encoders?
- Could the authors provide more detailed experimental results, such as training and inference times, to support the claims of efficiency and scalability?
