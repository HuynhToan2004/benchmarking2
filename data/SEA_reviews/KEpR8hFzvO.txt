 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel neural operator architecture, clawNO, designed to learn complex physical systems that adhere to conservation laws. This architecture is achieved by adding an additional layer that employs numerical differentiation to evaluate spatial and temporal derivatives as weighted linear combinations of neighboring points. The methodology is demonstrated to be effective in scenarios with limited data availability, particularly in small-data regimes. The approach is validated through extensive experiments on various physical systems, including incompressible Navier-Stokes equations, radial dam break, and atmospheric modeling. Despite its innovative approach, the paper has been critiqued for its presentation clarity, the necessity of its contribution in light of existing methods, and the robustness of its experimental results.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel method for enforcing conservation laws in neural operators, which is a significant advancement in the field.
- The method is well-explained and the paper is easy to follow, with clear demonstrations of the method's application.
- The approach is generalizable and can be applied to a variety of physical systems, including those with irregular grids and non-uniform domains.
- The paper provides a theoretical framework for the method, which is well-supported by extensive experimental validation.
- The method is shown to be effective in scenarios with limited data availability, which is a significant contribution to the field.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a comprehensive review of related work, particularly in the area of neural operators and conservation laws, which could have strengthened the contextualization of the research.
- The presentation of the paper could be improved, with some sections being overly dense and others lacking clarity.
- The novelty of the method is questioned, as similar approaches have been previously proposed, such as the use of skew-symmetric matrices for divergence-free output functions.
- The paper's experimental results are not robustly validated, with some results showing significant error bars and a lack of detailed error analysis.
- The method's effectiveness is not convincingly demonstrated across all physical systems tested, and the paper could benefit from more comprehensive testing and validation.
- The paper's contribution is questioned in light of existing methods that also enforce conservation laws in neural operators, which could limit the perceived novelty and impact of the proposed method.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors clarify the differences between the proposed method and existing methods that use skew-symmetric matrices for divergence-free output functions?
- How does the proposed method compare in terms of computational efficiency and accuracy to other methods that also enforce conservation laws in neural operators?
- Could the authors provide more detailed error analysis and explain the discrepancies in performance between different physical systems and data regimes?
- How does the method handle the integration of additional layers in existing neural operator architectures, and what are the implications for model complexity and training time?
- Can the authors elaborate on the choice of physical systems for testing and the rationale behind the experimental design?
- How does the method perform in scenarios with more complex physical systems or larger data sets, and what are the potential limitations or challenges in such scenarios?
