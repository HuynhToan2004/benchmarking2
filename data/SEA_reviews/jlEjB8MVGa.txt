 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel learning framework, SAL, aimed at detecting out-of-distribution (OOD) data using unlabeled data. The framework is designed to filter candidate outliers from the unlabeled data using singular value decomposition and a gradient matrix, which is then used to train an OOD classifier. The paper provides theoretical analysis and empirical evidence to support the effectiveness of the proposed method. It demonstrates that the SAL framework can outperform existing methods on benchmarks such as Cifar-10 and Cifar-100. However, concerns were raised about the scalability of the method to high-dimensional data and its performance on more complex datasets like ImageNet. The paper also lacks a comprehensive discussion on the limitations and potential negative societal impacts of the proposed method.

**Strengths:**
- The paper addresses a significant and challenging problem of out-of-distribution (OOD) detection, which is crucial in machine learning and artificial intelligence.
- The proposed method is novel, well-motivated, and theoretically justified, providing a new perspective on how unlabeled data can be effectively utilized for OOD detection.
- The paper is well-written, with clear explanations and thorough experimental validation that demonstrates the effectiveness of the proposed method.
- The theoretical analysis is robust and convincing, offering a solid foundation for the proposed method.
- The method's performance is superior to existing methods, as demonstrated by empirical results on benchmarks such as Cifar-10 and Cifar-100.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a discussion on the limitations of the proposed method, particularly in terms of scalability and its applicability to more complex datasets.
- The experimental setup is somewhat limited, focusing primarily on Cifar-10 and Cifar-100, which may not adequately represent the performance on more challenging datasets like ImageNet.
- The method's performance on near OOD data and its ability to generalize to unseen OOD datasets are not thoroughly evaluated.
- The paper does not discuss potential negative societal impacts of the proposed method, which is a critical omission for a paper in this field.
- There are minor typographical and citation errors that need to be corrected.

**Questions:**
- How does the method perform on more complex datasets like ImageNet, and what are the potential challenges in scaling up the method to such datasets?
- Can the authors provide more details on the computational complexity of the method and the scalability of the SVD operation, especially in high-dimensional spaces?
- What are the potential negative societal impacts of the proposed method, and how might these be mitigated or addressed?
- Could the authors clarify the experimental setup, particularly the use of different datasets for training and testing, and the impact of this on the reported results?
- How does the method handle near OOD data and unseen OOD datasets, and what are the implications of these scenarios for the effectiveness of the method?
