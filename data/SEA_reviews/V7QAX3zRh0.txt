 **Summary:**
This paper investigates the catastrophic forgetting problem in continual learning from a theoretical perspective, focusing on parameter isolation methods. The authors propose a novel framework that unifies various parameter isolation strategies under a quadratic approximation of the loss function. Theoretical analyses are provided to demonstrate how these methods prevent forgetting by ensuring orthogonality between task updates. The paper validates its theoretical insights through empirical experiments, comparing the performance of different algorithms and architectures. Despite the innovative approach, the paper faces criticism for its assumptions, particularly the strong assumption of orthogonality between task updates, which may not hold in practical scenarios. The paper also lacks a comprehensive comparison with other continual learning algorithms and does not sufficiently address the computational efficiency of its proposed methods.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel framework for analyzing parameter isolation methods in continual learning, which is a significant contribution to the field.
- The theoretical analysis provided is comprehensive and insightful, offering a deeper understanding of the mechanisms behind parameter isolation methods.
- The empirical validation of the theoretical insights through experiments on different architectures and datasets adds credibility to the findings.
- The paper is well-written and structured, making it accessible and easy to follow, which is crucial for a broad audience.
- The theoretical results are not limited to specific architectures or task numbers, which enhances the generalizability of the findings.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper makes a strong assumption of orthogonality between task updates, which may not hold in practical scenarios, raising concerns about the real-world applicability of the proposed methods.
- The experimental results show that the proposed method does not significantly outperform other algorithms in terms of forgetting, which questions the practical utility of the approach.
- The paper lacks a comprehensive comparison with other continual learning algorithms, which is essential for understanding the relative advantages and disadvantages of the proposed method.
- The computational efficiency of the proposed method is not adequately addressed, which is a critical concern for practical implementations, especially in large-scale applications.
- The paper could benefit from a more detailed discussion on the limitations of the proposed method, particularly in scenarios where the assumptions do not hold.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors provide more details on how the strong assumption of orthogonality between task updates can be justified or relaxed in practical scenarios?
- How does the proposed method compare with other parameter isolation algorithms, particularly in terms of computational efficiency and practical performance?
- Could the authors elaborate on the choice of using SGD^ and the implications of using different numbers of eigenvectors in the experiments?
- What are the implications of the assumptions made in the paper, particularly Assumption 2, and how do these assumptions affect the practical applicability of the proposed method?
- Can the authors discuss the potential of extending the analysis to scenarios where the assumptions do not hold, and what insights might be gained from such an analysis?
