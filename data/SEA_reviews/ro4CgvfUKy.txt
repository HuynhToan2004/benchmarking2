 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel unsupervised perceptual grouping and segmentation method using neural noise in deep neural networks (DNNs). It proposes a method that segments images by adding noise to the latent space of a pre-trained autoencoder, which is then clustered to produce segmentation masks. The approach is evaluated on a set of datasets specifically designed to test perceptual grouping, and the results show that the method aligns with human perceptual grouping phenomena. The paper also discusses the ecological plausibility of the method and its potential benefits in real-world applications. However, concerns were raised about the clarity of the writing, the novelty of the approach, and the method's practical applicability.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel approach to unsupervised perceptual grouping and segmentation using neural noise, which is a significant contribution to the field.
- The method is well-motivated and clearly presented, with a focus on the role of noise in perceptual grouping, which is a relatively under-explored area in machine learning.
- The paper provides a comprehensive evaluation using a set of datasets specifically designed to test perceptual grouping, which is a valuable contribution to the field.
- The method's ecological plausibility is discussed, which is an important aspect of understanding the practical applicability of the findings.
- The paper is well-written, with clear explanations of the method and its implications, and includes a discussion on the limitations of the approach.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks clarity in some sections, particularly in the explanation of how the noise is added to the latent space and the rationale behind the noise addition.
- The paper could benefit from a more detailed discussion on how the noise is selected and its impact on the segmentation performance.
- The method's practical applicability is limited, as it requires a pre-trained autoencoder and does not scale well to more complex or realistic datasets.
- The paper's novelty is questioned, as similar approaches using noise for segmentation have been previously explored.
- The paper could benefit from a more detailed discussion on the limitations of the method, including its sensitivity to noise and the number of time steps required for segmentation.
- The paper's evaluation is limited to simple datasets and does not include comparisons with other methods or more complex datasets, which could affect the generalizability of the findings.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors clarify how the noise is added to the latent space and the rationale behind this approach?
- How does the segmentation performance vary with different model learning rules, noise levels, and the number of time steps?
- Could the authors provide more details on the selection of noise and its impact on segmentation performance?
- Is there a possibility to extend the method to more complex or realistic datasets, and if so, what would be the expected outcomes?
- How does the method compare to other unsupervised segmentation methods, and what are the specific advantages of using neural noise over other methods?
- Can the authors elaborate on the method's ecological plausibility and discuss how it might be applied in real-world scenarios?
