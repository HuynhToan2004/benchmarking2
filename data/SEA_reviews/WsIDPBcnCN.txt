 **Summary:**
The paper introduces Plasticity-Driven Sparsity Training (PlaD), a novel approach to sparse training in Deep Reinforcement Learning (DRL) that addresses the challenges of network plasticity and computational efficiency. PlaD leverages two innovative techniques: memory reset mechanisms to maintain consistency in the replay buffer and dynamic weight rescaling to mitigate training instability. The method is evaluated on various MuJoCo locomotion tasks, demonstrating competitive performance compared to dense models, even at high sparsity levels. The paper provides a comprehensive theoretical analysis, experimental results, and ablation studies to support the effectiveness of PlaD. However, concerns about the novelty and the generalizability of the findings to other environments and tasks are raised.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, making it easy to follow and understand the proposed methods.
- The authors provide a comprehensive theoretical analysis and experimental results to support the proposed method, which is a significant contribution to the field.
- The paper introduces two innovative techniques, memory reset and dynamic weight rescaling, which are crucial for maintaining network plasticity and mitigating training instability.
- The experiments demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed method, showing that it achieves performance on par with dense models, even at high sparsity levels.
- The paper addresses the important issue of reducing computational cost in DRL, which is crucial for practical applications.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a clear explanation of the connection between sparsity and the loss of neural plasticity, which is a central theme of the research.
- The generalizability of the findings to other environments and tasks beyond MuJoCo locomotion tasks is unclear.
- The paper does not sufficiently discuss the limitations of the proposed method, particularly in terms of its applicability to different types of neural networks and its scalability to larger, more complex environments.
- The paper could benefit from a more thorough discussion on the potential negative societal impacts of the research.
- There is a lack of clarity in some sections of the paper, particularly in the descriptions of the experimental setups and the theoretical underpinnings of the proposed method.
- The paper could benefit from a more detailed discussion on the scalability and generalizability of the findings, as well as a more comprehensive comparison with other sparse training methods.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors clarify the connection between sparsity and the loss of neural plasticity, and provide a more detailed explanation of how the proposed method addresses this issue?
- How does the proposed method perform in other environments and tasks beyond MuJoCo locomotion tasks?
- Could the authors provide more details on the computational requirements of the proposed method, particularly in terms of memory and computational resources?
- How does the proposed method compare to other sparse training methods in terms of performance and computational efficiency?
- Can the authors discuss the potential negative societal impacts of their research and how they plan to address these concerns?
- How does the proposed method perform in different types of neural networks, such as convolutional neural networks or transformer networks?
- Can the authors provide more details on the experimental setup, particularly in terms of the hyperparameters used and the training details for the baselines?
