 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel approach, Functional Bayesian Tucker Decomposition (FunBaT), aimed at handling continuous-indexed tensor data. FunBaT extends the traditional Tucker decomposition by treating continuous-indexed data as interactions between the Tucker core and a set of latent functions, modeled using Gaussian processes. The methodology involves a posterior approximation based on advanced message-passing techniques, which are evaluated on both synthetic and real-world datasets. The paper claims that FunBaT outperforms existing methods in terms of reconstruction error and can identify interpretable patterns that align with domain knowledge. However, the paper's contribution is questioned due to its similarity to existing methods, and the clarity and presentation of the methodology and experimental results need improvement.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, with a clear structure and good presentation.
- The proposed method is technically sound and the experiments demonstrate its effectiveness.
- The paper addresses an important problem of handling continuous-indexed tensor data, which is relevant in many real-world applications.
- The methodology is novel, particularly the application of Gaussian processes to model latent functions and the use of Kalman filter and RTS smoother for inference.
- The paper includes a detailed appendix with proofs and additional details, which is helpful for understanding the methodology.
- The paper provides a thorough comparison with other methods, including a lightweight alternative, and includes visualizations of the learned mode functions.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks clarity in some sections, particularly in the explanation of the state-space prior and the algorithm.
- The contribution of the paper is questioned due to its similarity to existing methods, particularly in the use of Gaussian processes and the Tucker decomposition.
- The paper could benefit from a more detailed discussion on the computational complexity and scalability of the proposed method.
- The experimental results are not convincingly superior to existing methods, and the paper does not adequately discuss the limitations of the proposed method.
- The paper could be improved by providing more detailed explanations of the algorithms and including more experimental results to support the claims.
