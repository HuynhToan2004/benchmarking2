 **Summary:**
The paper investigates the Hamiltonian inference problem, focusing on the convergence rates of quantum iterative scaling (QIS) and gradient descent (GD) algorithms. It introduces novel variants of QIS and GD, integrating Anderson mixing and the L-BFGS method, to enhance their performance. The authors provide a detailed analysis of the convergence rates, including lower and upper bounds on the spectral radius of the Jacobian matrix, which are crucial for understanding the algorithms' behavior. The paper also discusses the application of these methods to quantum computing, albeit with some limitations, and presents experimental results demonstrating the practical advantages of the proposed methods.
