 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel Shifted Non-Local Search (Shifted-NLS) module designed for space-time attention in video processing. This module aims to correct small spatial errors in predicted offsets, improving the alignment of video frames. The Shifted-NLS is integrated into a UNet-like architecture, enhancing video denoising results. The method is evaluated on various datasets, demonstrating its effectiveness in reducing computational costs while maintaining performance. The paper also discusses the integration of the Shifted-NLS into existing space-time attention modules, showcasing its potential for broader applications in video processing.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, clear, and easy to follow, making it accessible to a broad audience.
- The proposed method is simple yet effective, with a straightforward design that is easy to understand.
- The paper includes comprehensive experiments that demonstrate the method's effectiveness and efficiency, showing improvements over existing methods.
- The method is well-motivated, addressing the issue of motion in video processing, and is well-positioned within the existing literature.
- The paper provides a clear explanation of the methodology and its implementation, which is valuable for reproducibility and further research.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed discussion on the limitations of the proposed method, which could help in understanding its scope and applicability.
- The novelty of the method is questioned, as similar techniques have been previously used, and the paper does not sufficiently distinguish its contributions from existing works.
- The paper does not adequately discuss the computational complexity of the method, which could be a significant concern for practical applications.
- The experimental results are not convincingly superior to existing methods, particularly in terms of PSNR improvements, which are sometimes marginal.
- The paper could benefit from a more thorough comparison with state-of-the-art methods, especially in terms of computational efficiency and memory usage.
- The paper's presentation could be improved, particularly in the clarity of figures and the organization of content.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors provide a more detailed discussion on the limitations of the proposed method?
- How does the proposed method compare in terms of computational complexity and memory usage with existing methods?
- Can the authors clarify the novelty of their method and how it differs from existing techniques?
- What are the specific advantages of the Shifted-NLS over other methods in terms of computational efficiency and memory usage?
- How does the method perform in scenarios with large motion or complex optical flow patterns?
- Could the authors provide more detailed experimental results, including comparisons with state-of-the-art methods on various datasets?
- Can the authors elaborate on the integration of the Shifted-NLS into existing space-time attention modules and discuss its potential applications in other video processing tasks?
