 **Summary:**
The paper introduces TabGenDDPM, an extension of the TabDDPM model for tabular data generation, incorporating a transformer and a novel masking mechanism to handle both data imputation and generation tasks. The model is evaluated against various baselines, demonstrating improvements in machine learning efficiency and statistical similarity. However, the paper's contributions are seen as limited, primarily extending existing methods without significant innovation. The experimental setup and results are criticized for being unclear and potentially misleading, with concerns about the statistical significance and reproducibility of the results.

**Strengths:**
- The paper addresses an important and practical problem of data imputation and generation for tabular data, which is crucial for many real-world applications.
- The proposed method is technically sound, and the experiments demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed model.
- The paper is well-written and easy to follow, making it accessible to readers.
- The use of transformers in the diffusion model for tabular data is a novel approach, potentially improving the handling of inter-feature interactions.
- The paper conducts a comprehensive evaluation, comparing the proposed method with state-of-the-art techniques, and provides a detailed description of the proposed method.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks significant innovation, primarily extending existing methods like TabDDPM without substantial novel contributions.
- The experimental setup and results are unclear and potentially misleading. The paper does not report statistical significance of the results, which undermines the reliability of the findings.
- The methodology and results are not reproducible due to the lack of detailed information on the experimental setup, such as the number of trials, seeds, and hyperparameter tuning.
- The paper does not adequately address the privacy risks associated with data generation, which is a significant concern in many applications.
- The paper could benefit from a more thorough discussion of related works and a clearer differentiation from existing methods.
- The paper does not provide sufficient details on the computational cost and scalability of the proposed method, which is crucial for practical applications.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors clarify how the proposed method handles different types of missing data, such as missing values, missing columns, and missing rows?
- How does the proposed method compare in terms of computational cost and scalability to existing methods?
- Can the authors provide more details on the experimental setup, particularly the number of trials, seeds used, and hyperparameter tuning?
- How does the proposed method handle the privacy risks associated with data generation, and what measures are in place to mitigate these risks?
- Could the authors provide more detailed results, including statistical significance and reproducibility information, to strengthen the paper's claims?
- How does the proposed method compare to other methods in terms of data imputation and generation, especially in scenarios where data is incomplete or partially missing?
