 **Summary:**
The paper explores the impact of data quality on the performance of code generation models, specifically focusing on the APPS and Code-Contests datasets. The authors propose a data cleaning pipeline that includes renaming variables, modularizing code, and adding natural language-based planning annotations. This pipeline is evaluated using a CodeLlama-7B model, showing improvements in performance on these datasets compared to a larger AlphaCode model and other baselines. The paper also discusses the potential of using LLMs for data cleaning and the importance of data quality in improving model performance.

**Strengths:**
- The paper addresses a significant and timely issue in the field of code generation, focusing on the importance of data quality and its impact on model performance.
- The proposed data cleaning pipeline, including variable renaming, modularization, and planning annotations, is a novel approach that could potentially improve the quality of training data for code generation models.
- The paper is well-written, clear, and easy to follow, making it accessible to a broad audience.
- The experiments are thorough and demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed methods, showing improvements over existing baselines.
- The paper provides a detailed analysis of the transformed programs, which is beneficial for understanding the impact of the proposed methods.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper primarily uses a single model (CodeLlama-7B) for evaluation, which limits the generalizability of the findings to other models.
- The novelty of the approach is questionable as similar methods have been previously used in other domains.
- The paper lacks a detailed comparison with other state-of-the-art models and baselines, which could have provided a clearer picture of the relative performance of the proposed methods.
- The focus on a narrow set of benchmarks (APPS and Code-Contests) may limit the applicability of the findings to other domains or datasets.
- The paper does not adequately address the limitations of the proposed methods, particularly in terms of their scalability and applicability to more complex or diverse datasets.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors provide more detailed information on the effectiveness of the planning annotations, particularly in terms of their impact on model performance and the accuracy of the generated plans?
- How do the authors ensure that the improvements observed in model performance are not simply due to the increased size of the training dataset?
- Can the authors clarify the role and effectiveness of the oracle checker in maintaining functional equivalence between the original and transformed programs?
- How do the authors plan to address the limitations of the proposed methods, particularly in terms of scalability and applicability to more complex or diverse datasets?
- Could the authors provide more detailed comparisons with other state-of-the-art models and baselines to better establish the relative performance of the proposed methods?
