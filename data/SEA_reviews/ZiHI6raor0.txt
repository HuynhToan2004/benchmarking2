 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel multi-agent reinforcement learning (MARL) algorithm named CAMMARL, which employs conformal predictions to estimate the actions of other agents in the environment. This method predicts a set of possible actions that an agent might take, providing a measure of uncertainty. The algorithm is evaluated in two fully cooperative multi-agent tasks, demonstrating that it can improve the capabilities of an autonomous agent by predicting the behavior of other agents. The paper also discusses the limitations and potential extensions of the algorithm, including its scalability and the need for further validation in more complex or competitive scenarios.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel approach to multi-agent reinforcement learning (MARL) by integrating conformal predictions, which allows for the estimation of other agents' actions with a certain level of uncertainty.
- The methodology is clearly described, with a detailed algorithm and experimental setup, making it easy to follow and understand.
- The paper is well-written, with clear and concise descriptions of the experiments and results, which are supported by a comprehensive discussion and analysis.
- The proposed CAMMARL algorithm is innovative in its application of conformal predictions to MARL, offering a new perspective on how agents can reason about other agents' actions.
- The paper provides a theoretical guarantee for the conformal predictions, enhancing the reliability and robustness of the proposed method.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper's experimental setup is limited to fully cooperative multi-agent tasks, which might not adequately demonstrate the algorithm's effectiveness in more complex or competitive scenarios.
- The comparison baselines are not sufficiently robust, with some baselines (e.g., TAAM, TOAM) not directly addressing the same problem as the proposed method.
- The paper lacks a detailed discussion on how the proposed method can be extended to handle more complex or competitive scenarios, which could limit its practical applicability.
- The paper does not provide a detailed discussion on the limitations of the proposed method, which could be crucial for understanding its scope and applicability.
- The experimental results are not convincingly superior to existing methods, and the paper does not sufficiently address the scalability of the algorithm to more complex or larger-scale environments.

**Questions:**
- How does the algorithm perform in more complex or competitive scenarios, where the need for predicting other agents' actions might be more critical?
- Could the authors elaborate on how the proposed method can be extended to handle more complex or competitive scenarios, and discuss the potential challenges or limitations in doing so?
- What are the specific limitations of the proposed method, and how do these impact its practical applicability and effectiveness?
- How does the algorithm handle scenarios where the set of possible actions for an agent is very large, potentially leading to very large prediction sets?
- Could the authors discuss the potential benefits or drawbacks of using different types of conformal prediction models, such as those based on different types of data or different conformalization methods?
- How does the algorithm ensure that the predictions are accurate and reliable, particularly in scenarios where the prediction set might be large or the coverage might be low?
