 **Summary:**
The paper introduces PCME++, an improved version of the probabilistic cross-modal embedding method, which addresses the challenges of image-text matching by introducing a novel probabilistic distance and optimization techniques to enhance the model's performance. The methodology includes the use of pseudo-positives and mixed sample data augmentation to prevent loss saturation and improve training efficiency. Extensive experiments are conducted on various datasets, demonstrating the effectiveness of PCME++ over existing methods. The paper also explores the application of PCME++ in zero-shot classification, showing potential in this area. Despite its strengths, the paper is criticized for its limited novelty and the lack of a comprehensive comparison with other state-of-the-art methods.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, clear, and easy to follow, with a clear motivation and strong experimental results that support the claims.
- The proposed method, PCME++, is innovative, particularly in the introduction of a new probabilistic distance with a closed-form solution, which is computationally efficient.
- The method addresses the loss saturation issue effectively by incorporating pseudo-positives and mixed sample data augmentation, which enhances its robustness.
- The experimental results show that PCME++ outperforms existing methods, including VSE and InfoNCE, and demonstrates its effectiveness in handling false negatives and noisy correspondences.
- The paper provides a detailed analysis of the uncertainty in the matching process, which is useful for understanding the model's behavior and performance.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed discussion on the differences between PCME++ and PCME, which could help clarify the improvements and innovations of the new method.
- The novelty of the proposed method is questioned, as it appears to be a combination of existing techniques without significant new contributions.
- The paper does not adequately address the issue of loss saturation, which is a significant concern in the field.
- The experimental setup and results are not comprehensive enough, with some important comparisons and details missing, such as a comparison with other state-of-the-art methods and a more detailed analysis of the impact of different parameters on the performance.
- The paper suffers from a lack of clarity in some sections, particularly in the methodology and the description of the experimental setup, which could make it difficult for readers to fully understand the contributions and implications of the research.

**Questions:**
- Could you clarify the differences between PCME++ and PCME, particularly in terms of the new probabilistic distance and the optimization techniques introduced?
- How does the proposed method handle the loss saturation issue more effectively than existing methods, and what are the specific advantages of PCME++ in this regard?
- Can you provide more details on the experimental setup, such as the specifics of the dataset used and the parameters of the model?
- How does the proposed method perform in scenarios with varying noise ratios, and what are the implications of this for the model's performance and generalizability?
- Could you elaborate on the choice of metrics used in the evaluation and how they reflect the true effectiveness of the method in practical applications?
- How does the proposed method compare to other state-of-the-art methods in terms of computational efficiency and scalability, especially when applied to larger datasets or more complex models?
