 **Summary:**
The paper examines the performance of modern Generative Adversarial Networks (GANs) as density models, focusing on their ability to reconstruct images and their classification performance. It introduces a novel method using annealed importance sampling (AIS) to estimate the relaxed likelihood of GANs, which is crucial for understanding their behavior in outlier detection and classification tasks. The study shows that GANs often fail to reconstruct images from the training set effectively, which is a significant concern given their widespread use in image generation and manipulation. The paper also highlights that GANs' performance in these tasks is often worse than chance or simple baselines, suggesting that they do not accurately capture the underlying data distribution.

**Strengths:**
- The paper addresses a significant and important problem in the field of generative models, specifically focusing on the limitations of GANs as density models, which is a topic of current interest.
- The authors provide a novel methodology using annealed importance sampling (AIS) to estimate the relaxed likelihood of GANs, which is a valuable contribution to the field.
- The paper is well-written, with clear explanations and figures that aid in understanding the complex concepts discussed.
- The experimental results are well-designed and demonstrate the failure of GANs in outlier detection and classification tasks, which is a significant finding.
- The paper raises interesting questions about the fundamental limitations of GANs and their impact on the field, which could lead to further research and discussion.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper's conclusions are limited by the scope of the experiments, which are primarily focused on image datasets and do not generalize well to other types of data or datasets with different characteristics.
- The paper lacks a thorough discussion on the limitations of the methodology and its applicability to different types of data and GAN architectures.
- The paper does not adequately address the potential negative societal impacts of the findings, which could be significant given the widespread use of GANs in various applications.
- The paper could benefit from a more detailed comparison with existing works, particularly those that also use AIS for GANs, to establish a clearer position within the current literature.
- The paper's claims about the failure of GANs to capture the underlying data distribution are not convincingly supported by the evidence presented, which could be seen as a limitation.

**Questions:**
- How does the methodology compare with other existing methods for estimating the likelihood of GANs, such as those using variational inference?
- Could the authors clarify the role and impact of the observation noise in their model, particularly in relation to the GAN's performance in outlier detection and classification tasks?
- How do the findings of this study apply to other types of data or GAN architectures beyond those tested?
- What are the potential negative societal impacts of the findings, and how might they be mitigated or addressed?
- Could the authors provide more detailed comparisons with existing works to establish a clearer position within the current literature?
