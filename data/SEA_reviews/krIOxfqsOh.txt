 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel masked pretraining framework for multi-agent decision making, termed MaskMA, which leverages a transformer architecture and mask-based collaborative learning to address challenges in multi-agent settings. This framework is designed to handle varying agent numbers and action spaces, and it is evaluated on the StarCraft Multi-Agent Challenge (SMAC) benchmark. The approach employs a mask-based collaborative learning strategy to address the mismatch between centralized pretraining and decentralized execution, and a generalizable action representation to handle varying action spaces. Despite the innovative approach, concerns were raised regarding the clarity of the paper, the limited scope of experiments, and the novelty of the contributions compared to existing works.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written and easy to follow, making complex concepts accessible to a broad audience.
- The proposed method, MaskMA, demonstrates strong performance on the SMAC benchmark, particularly in zero-shot settings.
- The paper introduces a novel approach to pretraining multi-agent decision-making models, which is a significant contribution to the field.
- The use of mask-based collaborative learning and generalizable action representation is innovative and addresses specific challenges in multi-agent decision-making.
- The method's ability to handle varying agent numbers and action spaces is a notable advantage over many existing methods.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks clarity in some sections, particularly in the methodology and the experimental setup, which could confuse readers unfamiliar with the domain.
- The scope of the experiments is limited to a single environment (SMAC), which may not fully demonstrate the generalizability of the proposed method across different environments.
- The novelty of the proposed method is questioned due to similarities with existing works, such as MADT, which also use similar transformer architectures and mask-based collaborative learning strategies.
- The paper does not sufficiently differentiate its contributions from those of MADT, which could lead to confusion about the unique advantages of the proposed method.
- There are several grammatical errors and typos throughout the paper, which detract from its professional quality.
- The paper does not adequately address the scalability of the method to larger environments or the computational costs associated with pre-training.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors clarify the specific contributions of MaskMA compared to existing methods like MADT, especially in terms of novelty and performance?
- How does the proposed method handle the varying numbers of agents and actions in different environments, and what are the implications for scalability?
- Could the authors provide more detailed explanations of the mask-based collaborative learning strategy and the generalizable action representation, possibly with additional figures or examples?
- What are the computational costs associated with pre-training MaskMA, and how do these compare to other methods like MADT?
- How does the method perform in environments with larger numbers of agents or more complex action spaces, and what are the limitations in such scenarios?
- Can the authors provide more details on the experimental setup, particularly the training and testing phases, to clarify the conditions under which the model is evaluated?
