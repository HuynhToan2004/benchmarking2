 **Summary:**
The paper introduces EWoK, a novel method for learning robust policies in Robust Markov Decision Processes (RMDPs) by approximating the worst transition kernel. This approach allows for the application of the method on top of existing non-robust RL algorithms, enhancing their robustness without the need for modifications to the learning process. The method is evaluated across various domains including classic control environments, MinAtar games, and DeepMind Control Suite, demonstrating its effectiveness in high-dimensional settings. However, the paper is criticized for its limited experimental validation, focusing only on noise perturbations, and for not providing a comprehensive comparison with existing methods. The theoretical contributions, while significant, are seen as similar to prior works, and the paper lacks a detailed discussion on the computational efficiency and scalability of the proposed method.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel approach to learning robust policies in RMDPs by approximating the worst transition kernel, which is a significant contribution to the field.
- The method is theoretically sound and supported by a robust set of experiments, demonstrating its effectiveness across various domains including classic control environments, MinAtar games, and DeepMind Control Suite.
- The paper is well-written, making it easy to follow and understand, with clear explanations of the theoretical results and the experimental setup.
- The proposed method is flexible and can be integrated with any deep RL method, enhancing its applicability and scalability in high-dimensional domains.
- The paper addresses a significant problem in the field of RMDPs, which is crucial for real-world applications where the environment's dynamics are uncertain.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a thorough comparison with existing methods, particularly in terms of computational efficiency and scalability. It would be beneficial to see how the proposed method compares to other robust RL methods in terms of training time and performance.
- The experimental validation is limited, focusing primarily on noise perturbations and not exploring other types of perturbations such as additive noise or parameter shifts. This limits the understanding of the method's effectiveness in more diverse settings.
- The paper does not discuss the potential limitations of the proposed method, which could provide a more balanced view of its applicability and effectiveness.
- The theoretical contributions, while significant, are seen as similar to prior works, particularly in the characterization of the worst kernel for KL uncertainty sets.
