 **Summary:**
The paper introduces UStereo, a novel stereo matching model that leverages inductive biases to enhance scale invariance and capture long-range dependencies, aiming to address the limitations of traditional CNN-based methods. The model employs a dense scale-aware fusion strategy and a mixed direct long-range compensation module to integrate information across different resolutions and enhance feature extraction. Extensive experiments are conducted on various datasets, showing improvements over state-of-the-art methods. Despite the innovative approach, concerns are raised about the novelty and clarity of the paper, particularly in its presentation and the technical contributions over existing methods.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, with a clear and concise presentation of the proposed methodology.
- The authors provide a thorough review of related work, which is crucial for understanding the context and significance of their research.
- The paper includes extensive experimental results and ablation studies, which demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed components.
- The paper addresses a significant problem in stereo matching, focusing on the role of inductive bias in enhancing scale invariance and capturing long-range dependencies.
- The proposed method achieves state-of-the-art (SOTA) performance on various datasets, indicating its effectiveness.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed comparison with recent state-of-the-art methods, which could provide a better understanding of its competitive advantage.
- The motivation behind the design choices, such as the use of specific modules like MDLC and DSF, is not clearly explained, making it difficult to understand their necessity and effectiveness.
- The paper does not adequately address the computational complexity of the proposed method, which is crucial for real-time applications.
- There are numerous typographical errors and inconsistencies in the paper, which detract from its professional quality.
- The paper does not sufficiently discuss the limitations of the proposed method, which is essential for a comprehensive understanding of its applicability and potential shortcomings.
- The paper's experimental setup and results are not sufficiently detailed or convincing, particularly in the ablation studies and the comparisons with existing methods.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors provide a more detailed comparison with recent state-of-the-art methods, including a discussion on how their method compares in terms of performance, computational efficiency, and other relevant metrics?
- What are the specific advantages of the proposed method over existing methods, particularly in terms of inductive bias and feature extraction?
- Can the authors clarify the motivation behind the design choices, such as the use of MDLC and DSF, and how these components contribute to the overall effectiveness of the method?
- How does the proposed method handle the computational complexity, especially in real-time applications?
- Could the authors provide more detailed experimental results, including a broader range of datasets and a more thorough ablation study to better understand the contributions of different components of the method?
- The paper mentions several unclear terms and notations; could the authors clarify these to improve the readability and understanding of the paper?
