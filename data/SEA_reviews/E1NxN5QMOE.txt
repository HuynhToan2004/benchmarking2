 **Summary:**
The paper introduces Aranyani, a novel ensemble of oblique decision trees designed to address group fairness in online settings. Aranyani utilizes an oblique decision forest to manage group fairness constraints, enabling the computation of fairness gradients using aggregate statistics of previous decisions, thereby avoiding the need for additional storage or forward/backward passes. The paper provides theoretical analysis and empirical evidence demonstrating Aranyani's ability to achieve a better accuracy-fairness trade-off compared to baseline approaches. The approach is particularly notable for its ability to maintain group fairness in an online learning environment, which is a significant challenge in real-world applications.

**Strengths:**
- The paper addresses an important problem of group fairness in online learning, a critical area of research in machine learning.
- The proposed algorithm, Aranyani, is innovative and well-explained, with a clear methodology and theoretical analysis.
- The empirical results are compelling, demonstrating the effectiveness of Aranyani in achieving a better accuracy-fairness trade-off compared to baseline approaches.
- The paper is well-organized, making it easy to follow, and includes a thorough theoretical analysis and proofs of the proposed algorithm.
- The authors provide a detailed comparison with existing methods, highlighting the strengths and limitations of their approach.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed discussion on the limitations of the proposed approach, which could help in understanding the scope and applicability of Aranyani.
- The computational complexity of Aranyani is not thoroughly discussed, which is crucial for understanding its scalability and efficiency.
- The paper could benefit from a more comprehensive literature review, especially in the area of online learning and fairness.
- There is a lack of clarity in some sections of the paper, particularly in the definitions and explanations of certain concepts and equations.
- The paper does not sufficiently address the practical implications of the approach, such as its applicability in real-world scenarios and its impact on fairness in different settings.

**Questions:**

- How does the proposed approach handle scenarios where the group sizes are imbalanced?

- How does the proposed approach compare in terms of fairness and accuracy with other existing methods, especially in practical scenarios?
