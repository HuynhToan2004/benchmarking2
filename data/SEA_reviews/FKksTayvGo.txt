 **Summary:**
The paper introduces Denoising Diffusion Bridge Models (DDBMs), a novel approach to learn the score of a diffusion bridge from data, facilitating the translation of images from one distribution to another. This method leverages a stochastic differential equation (SDE) and an optimal transport problem to interpolate between two distributions, aiming to improve upon existing methods like OT-Flow-Matching and diffusion models. The proposed method is evaluated on various image datasets, showing competitive performance in image-to-image translation tasks. However, concerns are raised regarding the novelty of the approach, the clarity of its advantages over existing methods, and the fairness of the comparisons made.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel method for learning a score of a diffusion bridge from data, which can translate images from one distribution to another.
- The methodology is well-explained, with clear derivations and detailed explanations of the algorithm and its implementation.
- The paper provides extensive experimental evaluations, including ablation studies, and the results show competitive performance on image-to-image translation tasks.
- The proposed method is well-motivated and offers a new approach to the problem of image-to-image translation, which is a significant contribution to the field.
- The paper is well-written, making it easy to follow, and the authors have provided code, enhancing reproducibility.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a clear discussion on the advantages of the proposed method over existing methods like OT-Flow-Matching and diffusion models.
- The experiments are limited to image-to-image translation tasks, and it is unclear how the method would perform on other tasks such as unconditional image generation.
- The novelty of the proposed method is questionable, as it appears to be a combination of existing methods like EDM, OT-Flow-Matching, and diffusion bridges.
- There are concerns about the fairness of the comparisons made in the paper, particularly in terms of the computational resources and hyperparameters used.
- The paper does not sufficiently discuss the limitations of the proposed method, which could include its applicability to different types of data and its scalability to larger datasets.
- The paper could benefit from a more comprehensive discussion on the theoretical underpinnings of the method, especially in relation to the assumptions made and their implications.

**Questions:**
- Could you clarify the advantages of the proposed method over existing methods, particularly in terms of performance and efficiency?
- How does the method perform on other tasks beyond image-to-image translation, such as unconditional image generation?
- Can you provide more details on the computational resources and hyperparameters used in the experiments to ensure a fair comparison with other methods?
- How does the proposed method handle different types of data, such as text or audio, which may not fit well into the image-to-image translation framework?
- Could you discuss the limitations of the proposed method and how they might affect its practical applicability in real-world scenarios?
- How does the method handle the initialization of the diffusion bridge, especially in scenarios where the endpoints are not well-defined or not directly related?
