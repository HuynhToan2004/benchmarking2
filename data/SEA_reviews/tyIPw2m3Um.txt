 **Summary:**
The paper investigates the impact of gradient decay on the training of neural networks, focusing on the hyperparameter β in the Softmax loss function. By adjusting the gradient decay rate, the authors aim to control the confidence of neural networks, proposing a new approach to optimize the training process. Theoretical analysis and empirical results are provided to support the proposed method, which is claimed to improve model calibration and reduce overconfidence. The paper also introduces a warm-up strategy to dynamically adjust the gradient decay rate during training, enhancing the model's generalization and performance on various datasets. However, the paper has been criticized for its lack of clarity in explaining the theoretical contributions and the empirical results, and for not sufficiently differentiating its approach from existing methods.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a new hyperparameter β for gradient decay in the Softmax loss, which is a significant contribution to the field of neural network training.
- Theoretical analysis and empirical results are provided to support the proposed method, which is a rigorous approach to validating the proposed ideas.
- The paper explores the effect of gradient decay on model calibration, which is a critical aspect of neural network training.
- The authors propose a warm-up training strategy to dynamically adjust the gradient decay rate during training, which is a novel approach to optimizing the training process.
- The paper is well-written and easy to follow, which is essential for a clear understanding of the proposed methodologies and results.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a clear explanation of the theoretical contributions, particularly in the introduction and sections where the theoretical results are presented.
- The empirical results are not sufficiently explained, and it is unclear how the proposed method improves model calibration and reduces overconfidence.
- The paper does not sufficiently differentiate its approach from existing methods, such as the temperature parameter in the softmax function, which also affects model calibration.
- The paper could benefit from more detailed experimental results and a more comprehensive discussion of related work to better situate the proposed method within the existing literature.
- The paper's claims about the superiority of the proposed method over existing post-calibration methods are not convincingly supported by the experimental results.
- The paper's writing could be improved to better articulate the theoretical contributions and the practical implications of the proposed method.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors clarify the theoretical contributions of the paper, particularly in the introduction and sections where the theoretical results are presented?
- How does the proposed method improve model calibration and reduce overconfidence, and what specific improvements are observed in the experimental results?
- Could the authors provide more detailed experimental results, including the standard deviation of the results and the training curves for different values of β?
- How does the proposed method compare to existing post-calibration methods, and what specific advantages does it offer over these methods?
- Could the authors discuss the relationship between the proposed method and existing methods, such as the temperature parameter in the softmax function?
- How does the proposed method affect the convergence rate of the model, and what are the implications of this for practical applications?
