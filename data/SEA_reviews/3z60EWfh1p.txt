 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel transfer learning technique, GATE, for regression tasks using a Riemannian manifold approach. It proposes a method to transfer knowledge from source tasks to target tasks by aligning latent vectors and ensuring that arbitrary points map to locally flat coordinates in the overlapping region. This approach allows for the transfer of knowledge from source to target data, serving as a regularizer for the model's behavior in extrapolation regions. The method is evaluated on molecular graph datasets and shows competitive performance compared to existing methods. However, the paper lacks a comprehensive discussion on related works and does not include experiments on other types of datasets, which limits its generalizability.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel transfer learning technique based on differential geometry, specifically using Riemannian manifolds to align latent vectors and ensure transfer of knowledge.
- The proposed method, GATE, is evaluated on molecular graph datasets and shows competitive performance compared to existing methods.
- The paper is well-written and easy to follow, with a clear presentation of the methodology and results.
- The idea of using a Riemannian manifold to transfer knowledge from source to target data is innovative and addresses a significant problem in the field of transfer learning.
- The experimental results demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed method, particularly in molecular property prediction tasks.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a comprehensive discussion on related works, particularly in the area of transfer learning and geometric deep learning, which limits the understanding of the novelty and contributions of the proposed method.
- The experimental evaluation is limited to molecular graph datasets, which may not generalize well to other types of datasets such as image or language datasets.
- The paper does not include a detailed discussion on the limitations of the proposed method, which could help in understanding its applicability and potential drawbacks.
- There are several grammatical and typographical errors that need correction.
- The paper does not provide sufficient details on the experimental setup, such as the architecture of the encoder and the hyperparameters used, which could affect the reproducibility and comparability of the results.
- The paper does not compare the proposed method with a broader range of baselines, which could provide a more robust evaluation of its performance.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors provide a more detailed discussion on the related works, particularly in the areas of transfer learning and geometric deep learning, to clarify the novelty and contributions of their method?
- How does the proposed method compare in terms of performance and efficiency with other state-of-the-art methods, especially in different types of datasets beyond molecular graphs?
- Could the authors provide a detailed description of the experimental setup, including the architecture of the encoder and the hyperparameters used, to enhance the reproducibility and comparability of the results?
- How does the proposed method handle the transfer of knowledge from source to target tasks when the latent vectors are not aligned?
- Could the authors provide a more detailed explanation of the mathematical notations and equations used in the paper to improve the clarity and understanding of the methodology?
