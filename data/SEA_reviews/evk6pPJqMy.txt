 **Summary:**
The paper introduces Know2BIO, a comprehensive heterogeneous knowledge graph benchmark for the biomedical domain, integrating data from 30 diverse sources and encompassing 11 biomedical categories. This graph consists of approximately 219,000 nodes and 6.2 million edges, with node features including text descriptions, protein and compound sequences, and structures, which enable the utilization of emerging natural language processing methods and multi-modal data integration strategies. The paper evaluates various knowledge graph representation models on Know2BIO, demonstrating its effectiveness as a benchmark for knowledge graph representation learning in the biomedical field. However, the paper is criticized for its limited novelty, lack of comprehensive evaluation, and insufficient discussion on the challenges and limitations of the benchmark.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces Know2BIO, a comprehensive and evolving general-purpose knowledge graph benchmark for the biomedical domain, which integrates data from diverse sources and captures intricate relationships across various biomedical categories.
- Know2BIO is capable of user-directed automated updating to reflect the latest knowledge in biomedical science, making it a valuable resource for ongoing research in this field.
- The paper includes multi-modal data for the knowledge graph, enabling the utilization of emerging natural language processing methods and multi-modal data integration strategies.
- The Know2BIO dataset is comprehensive, integrating data from 30 diverse sources and encompassing 11 biomedical categories, which is a significant contribution to the field.
- The paper provides a detailed description of the dataset, including the number of nodes, edges, and node features, and provides a benchmark for knowledge graph representation learning in the biomedical field.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a comprehensive evaluation of Know2BIO, particularly in terms of its scalability and effectiveness in different biomedical applications.
- The paper does not sufficiently discuss the challenges and limitations of the benchmark, which is crucial for understanding the practical applicability and the potential impact of Know2BIO in the biomedical field.
- The novelty of the work is limited, as it primarily integrates existing data sources without introducing significant new methodologies or approaches.
