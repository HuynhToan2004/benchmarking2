 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel method for Out-of-Distribution (OOD) detection using feature maps, specifically focusing on the foreground-background spatial relationships. The proposed method, Feature Sim, is designed to measure the self-similarity of feature maps and is enhanced by a Threshold Activation module to suppress non-critical information. The paper also presents a fusion score that integrates knowledge from both feature map space and output space, aiming to improve OOD detection performance. The method is supported by theoretical analysis and extensive experiments across various datasets, demonstrating its effectiveness. However, concerns are raised about the clarity of the method's motivation and the novelty of the approach, as similar ideas have been explored in previous works.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel approach to Out-of-Distribution (OOD) detection using feature maps, which is a less explored area in this field.
- The proposed method is simple, intuitive, and supported by theoretical analysis, making it easy to understand and implement.
- The paper presents a comprehensive set of experiments across various datasets and models, demonstrating the effectiveness of the proposed method in OOD detection.
- The use of the Threshold Activation module to suppress non-critical information in feature maps is a novel and effective approach, enhancing the method's ability to discriminate between OOD and in-distribution (ID) data.
- The paper is well-written, clearly presenting the methodology and results, and includes a detailed analysis of the proposed method's effectiveness and its theoretical underpinnings.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks clarity in explaining the motivation behind using feature maps for OOD detection, especially in relation to the classifier's output.
- The novelty of the proposed method is questionable as similar ideas have been explored in previous works, particularly in the context of saliency detection.
- There is a lack of comparison with other methods that use feature maps for OOD detection, such as those mentioned in the references.
- The paper does not adequately address the computational complexity of the proposed method, which could be a significant drawback for its practical implementation.
- The paper could benefit from a more thorough discussion on the limitations of the proposed method and how it compares to existing methods in terms of computational efficiency and performance.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors clarify the motivation behind using feature maps for OOD detection and how it compares to using the classifier's output?
- How does the proposed method perform on datasets with different levels of noise and complexity, such as those with spurious correlations or high background noise?
- Could the authors provide a more detailed comparison with other methods that use feature maps for OOD detection, including a discussion on the computational complexity of these methods?
- How does the proposed method handle the issue of false positives in OOD detection, and what steps can be taken to mitigate this issue?
- Could the authors elaborate on the theoretical underpinnings of the Feature Sim score and how it relates to the GMM-based score?
- How does the proposed method perform when combined with other methods, such as those mentioned in the paper, and what are the potential benefits and drawbacks of such combinations?
