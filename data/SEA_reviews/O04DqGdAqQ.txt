 **Summary:**
The paper introduces Ada-Instruct, a method for generating diverse and complex instructions for various tasks using open-source large language models (LLMs). It contrasts with traditional self-instruct methods by utilizing a three-step process: first, fine-tuning an open-source LLM to generate instructions from a small set of samples, then using a closed-source LLM like ChatGPT to generate labels for these instructions, and finally fine-tuning the LLM on these labeled instructions for specific tasks. The method is evaluated across multiple benchmarks, demonstrating its effectiveness in generating long, complex instructions that align with target task distributions. However, concerns are raised about the cost-effectiveness and the novelty of the approach, as it appears similar to existing methods with minor modifications.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, clear, and easy to follow, making it accessible to a broad audience.
- The proposed method, Ada-Instruct, is a novel approach to generating complex instructions for various tasks using open-source LLMs, which is a significant contribution to the field.
- The paper provides extensive experiments and ablation studies, demonstrating the effectiveness of the method across different benchmarks and tasks.
- The method's ability to generate long, complex instructions that align with target task distributions is highlighted, showing a significant improvement over traditional self-instruct methods.
- The paper is well-motivated, addressing the limitations of current methods in generating complex instructions and providing a cost-effective solution using open-source LLMs.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a comprehensive comparison with existing methods, particularly those that also use open-source LLMs for instruction generation, which could undermine the perceived novelty and effectiveness of the proposed method.
- There is a noticeable absence of quantitative evaluations on the quality of generated instructions, which is crucial for assessing the practical utility and reliability of the method.
- The paper does not adequately address the cost implications of using both open-source and closed-source LLMs, which could limit the accessibility and affordability of the method for many researchers and organizations.
- The paper's reliance on closed-source LLMs like ChatGPT for generating labels raises concerns about the reproducibility and generalizability of the results, as these models are not publicly available.
- The method's complexity and the need for fine-tuning and labeling stages may limit its practical applicability and scalability, especially in real-world scenarios where data availability and diversity are challenges.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors provide more detailed comparisons with existing methods, particularly those that also use open-source LLMs for instruction generation?
- How does the proposed method compare in terms of cost-effectiveness and practical applicability with other methods that do not rely on closed-source LLMs for labeling?
- Can the authors clarify the rationale behind the choice of using ChatGPT for labeling and discuss potential alternatives that could ensure reproducibility and generalizability of the results?
- How does the method perform when applied to different types of tasks, such as those involving natural language understanding or reasoning, beyond the code completion tasks primarily evaluated?
- Could the authors provide more insights into the method's ability to handle complex instructions and its impact on the performance of downstream tasks?
