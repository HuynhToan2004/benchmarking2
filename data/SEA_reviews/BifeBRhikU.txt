 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel approach to compress Large Language Models (LLMs) by partially binarizing their weights, focusing on the most salient weights which are retained at high precision while the rest are binarized. This method is supported by a combination of post-training quantization and quantization-aware training techniques, including the use of a Hessian matrix and a compensation mechanism for binarized weights. The approach is evaluated on models like OPT-1.3B and LLaMA-7B, demonstrating significant improvements in compression rates and maintaining performance on various benchmarks. However, concerns are raised about the novelty of the approach, as it appears to be a combination of existing techniques without significant new insights or methodologies.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-organized, clearly written, and easy to follow, making it accessible to readers.
- The experiments are comprehensive, covering a variety of tasks and models, and the results are convincing.
- The paper provides a thorough analysis of the effectiveness of existing binarization algorithms for LLMs and introduces a novel approach that filters salient weights during binarization, which is a significant contribution to the field.
- The methodology is straightforward and effective, with the proposed PB-GPTQ and PB-LLM showing promising results, especially in terms of compression and performance.
- The paper is well-motivated and provides a comprehensive review of related work, highlighting the importance of the problem and the potential impact of the proposed solutions.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks novelty as it primarily combines existing techniques without introducing significant new insights or methodologies.
- The methodology, particularly the binarization of weights and the handling of salient weights, is not clearly explained, making it difficult for readers to understand the implementation details.
- The experimental setup is limited to smaller models, which might not adequately demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed method on larger models.
- The paper does not provide sufficient details on the implementation and training of the binarized weights, which could affect the reproducibility and applicability of the results.
- The novelty of the proposed approach is questionable as it appears to be a combination of existing techniques without significant new contributions.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors clarify how the binarization of weights is handled, especially in terms of the methodology used and its implementation details?
- How does the proposed method compare to other state-of-the-art methods in terms of performance and efficiency, especially on larger models?
- Could the authors provide more details on the training process of the binarized weights, including the optimization objectives and the specifics of the training algorithm?
- How does the method handle the selection of salient weights, and what criteria are used for this selection?
- Can the authors discuss the potential negative societal impacts of their work and how they address these concerns?
