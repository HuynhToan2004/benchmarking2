 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel activation mechanism named LayerAct, which is designed to enhance noise-robustness in convolutional neural networks (CNNs) using BatchNorm. LayerAct operates at a layer-level, utilizing layer normalization to achieve a zero-like mean activation, thereby improving robustness against noise. The authors demonstrate that LayerAct outperforms other activation functions in both clean and noisy conditions across various datasets, including MNIST, CIFAR, and ImageNet. The mechanism is also shown to reduce the variance of noise-robustness across samples, potentially improving the training efficiency. Despite its effectiveness, the paper's contribution is questioned due to the limited novelty in its approach, which is seen as a simple modification of existing activation functions.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, with clear and easy-to-follow content, making it accessible to a broad audience.
- The proposed LayerAct mechanism is novel and well-motivated, addressing the issue of noise-robustness in CNNs.
- The paper provides a theoretical analysis on the proposed LayerAct, which is supported by extensive empirical results, demonstrating its effectiveness in various settings.
- The experiments conducted are extensive, including both clean and noisy datasets, which showcase the robustness of the proposed mechanism.
- The paper addresses an important problem in CNNs, specifically focusing on the activation function, which is crucial for the robustness and efficiency of the model.

**Weaknesses:**
- The novelty of the proposed mechanism is limited as it is a simple modification of existing activation functions, such as SiLU and HardSiLU, which may not be considered groundbreaking.
- The paper lacks a comprehensive comparison with other existing layer-level activation functions, which could have provided a clearer picture of the proposed mechanism's advantages.
- The experimental setup and results could be more robust, especially concerning the choice of hyperparameters and the sensitivity of the proposed mechanism to different conditions.
- The paper does not adequately address the potential negative societal impacts of the proposed mechanism, which is a critical consideration in the deployment of such technologies.
- There is a need for a more detailed analysis of the computational complexity introduced by the proposed mechanism, especially in larger or more complex models.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors provide a more detailed comparison with other layer-level activation functions, such as those mentioned in the literature, to better understand the advantages of the proposed mechanism?
- How does the proposed mechanism perform when combined with different normalization techniques, such as LayerNorm or Decorrelated Batch Normalization?
- Can the authors clarify the definition and role of the constant alpha in the proposed mechanism, and discuss its impact on the activation function's behavior?
- Could the authors provide more detailed results on the performance of the proposed mechanism in different settings, such as different hyperparameters or different datasets?
- How does the proposed mechanism handle the potential negative societal impacts, and what steps are being taken to mitigate these risks?
