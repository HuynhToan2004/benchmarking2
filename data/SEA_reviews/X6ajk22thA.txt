 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel approach to GNN-to-MLP knowledge distillation, focusing on the hardness of samples rather than their correctness. It proposes a method that identifies hard samples and assigns them larger subgraphs to enhance the distillation process. This method, termed Hardness-aware GNN-to-MLP Distillation (HGMD), models both knowledge and distillation hardness, which are crucial for effective knowledge transfer from GNNs to MLPs. The proposed methodology involves two distillation schemes, HGMD-weight and HGMD-mixup, which outperform existing state-of-the-art competitors. Extensive experiments are conducted on multiple datasets to demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed method.

**Strengths:**
- The paper addresses an important problem in GNN-to-MLP knowledge distillation by focusing on the hardness of samples, which is a novel approach compared to traditional methods that focus on correctness.
- The proposed method is non-parametric, meaning it does not introduce additional learnable parameters beyond the student MLPs, which is advantageous in terms of computational efficiency.
- The paper is well-written and easy to follow, making it accessible to a broad audience.
- The proposed method, HGMD, outperforms most existing methods, demonstrating its effectiveness in improving the performance of distilled MLPs.
- The paper includes extensive experiments on multiple datasets and GNN architectures, which validate the effectiveness of the proposed method.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed discussion on the limitations of the proposed method, which could provide a more comprehensive understanding of its applicability and effectiveness.
- The computational complexity of the proposed method is not compared with existing methods, which could help in understanding the practical implications of using the proposed method.
- The paper could benefit from a more thorough analysis of the training curves and the impact of different distillation strategies on the performance of the student MLP.
- The paper does not provide a detailed discussion on the scalability of the proposed method with different GNN architectures and datasets, which could affect the generalizability of the findings.
- There is a lack of clarity in the definition and calculation of knowledge hardness, which could lead to confusion about the method's effectiveness and applicability.
- The paper does not discuss the potential negative societal impacts of the proposed method, which is a crucial consideration for any technological innovation.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors provide a detailed discussion on the limitations of the proposed method and how these limitations affect the practical applicability and effectiveness of the method?
- How does the computational complexity of the proposed method compare with existing methods, and what are the practical implications of using the proposed method in terms of computational resources and time?
- Can the authors provide a more detailed analysis of the training curves and the impact of different distillation strategies on the performance of the student MLP?
- How does the proposed method perform with different GNN architectures and datasets, and what are the implications of these variations on the method's effectiveness?
- Can the authors clarify the definition and calculation of knowledge hardness, and provide examples or theoretical proofs to support the method's effectiveness in improving the distillation process?
- How does the proposed method handle the scalability issues with larger datasets or more complex GNN architectures, and what are the potential solutions to these challenges?
