 **Summary:**
The paper introduces PnP Inversion, a novel method for image editing that disentangles the source and target diffusion branches in diffusion-based editing, aiming to improve content preservation and edit fidelity. This method, which involves adding the difference between the source and target latent vectors to the source latent, is designed to enhance the performance of diffusion-based image editing methods. The paper also presents PIE-Bench, a new benchmark for evaluating image editing performance, which includes 700 images with diverse scenes and editing types. The method is evaluated against various state-of-the-art optimization-based inversion techniques and shows significant improvements in content preservation and edit fidelity, as well as reduced inference time.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel method for inverting diffusion models, which is a significant contribution to the field.
- The method is described as simple and effective, with a clear motivation and explanation of the method's design.
- The paper provides a comprehensive evaluation using a large dataset and a variety of metrics, which demonstrates the effectiveness of the proposed method.
- The paper is well-written, with clear figures and tables that aid in understanding the content.
- The proposed PIE-Bench is a useful dataset for future research in this area.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed discussion on the limitations of the proposed method, which could provide a more balanced view of its capabilities.
- The paper could benefit from a more thorough comparison with existing methods, especially in terms of inference speed and image quality.
- The paper does not adequately address the scalability of the method across different datasets or the potential impact of different hyperparameters on performance.
- The paper does not sufficiently discuss the practical implications of the method, such as its computational efficiency and the impact on image quality.
- The paper does not provide enough implementation details, which could hinder reproducibility and further research.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors clarify the specific role and impact of the "source" and "target" branches in the proposed method?
- How does the proposed method compare in terms of inference speed and image quality with other methods, such as those mentioned in the paper?
- What are the specific hyperparameters used in the method, and how do they affect the performance of the method?
- Can the authors provide more details on the experimental setup, including the hardware and software configurations used?
- How does the method perform on different datasets, and what are the implications of using different hyperparameters for different datasets?
- Could the authors provide more details on the training and testing of the PIE-Bench dataset, including the data distribution and the metrics used for evaluation?
