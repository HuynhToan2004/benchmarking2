 **Summary:**
The paper explores the training of small transformers in arithmetic operations, specifically focusing on how different data formatting and sampling strategies affect performance. The authors investigate various techniques such as reversing the order of results, using chain-of-thought data, and integrating arithmetic with text data. They find that these methods can enhance the model's ability to learn arithmetic tasks more efficiently, with some showing phase transitions in performance. The paper also discusses the potential of these methods in larger models and their implications for the broader understanding of how transformers learn arithmetic operations.

**Strengths:**
- The paper provides a comprehensive and detailed investigation into the factors influencing the emergence of arithmetic capabilities in small transformers, including data formatting, sampling, and model size.
- The findings are supported by rigorous experiments and theoretical insights, which are well-organized and clearly presented.
- The paper is well-written, with clear explanations and detailed discussions on the implications of the results.
- The authors explore various data formatting and sampling strategies, which are novel and contribute to a better understanding of how transformers learn arithmetic operations.
- The paper offers a new perspective on the capabilities of small transformers in learning arithmetic operations, which is an important area of research in the field of machine learning.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper could benefit from a more detailed discussion on the limitations of the findings, particularly in terms of their applicability to larger models and more complex tasks.
- There is a lack of comparison with other models or methods, which could help to validate the effectiveness of the proposed techniques.
- The paper could provide more detailed explanations on how the findings can be generalized to other tasks or applications beyond arithmetic operations.
- The paper might be too specialized for a broader audience, focusing too much on a specific application (arithmetic operations) without sufficient broader implications or generalizations.
- Some sections of the paper, such as the explanation of certain experimental results and the discussion on the limitations, could be clearer and more detailed.
