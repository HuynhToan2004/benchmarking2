 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel approach to Multi-Agent Reinforcement Learning (MARL) by proposing a new condition called Individual-Global-Transform-Optimal (IGTO) and a corresponding method, Individual-Global Normalized Transformation (IGNT). This method aims to relax the constraint of identical individual-global actions while ensuring policy distribution equivalence. The authors propose a new algorithm, IGNT-MAC, which integrates IGNT with existing MARL algorithms such as VDN, QMIX, QTRAN, and FOP. The paper presents theoretical proofs and empirical results demonstrating the effectiveness of IGNT-MAC in various environments like StarCraft Multi-Agent Challenge (SMAC) and Multi-Agent Particle Environment (MPE). The methodology is designed to enhance the flexibility and applicability of MARL in scenarios where individual and global actions can be inconsistent.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel approach to Multi-Agent Reinforcement Learning (MARL) by proposing a new condition called Individual-Global-Transform-Optimal (IGTO) and a corresponding method, Individual-Global Normalized Transformation (IGNT).
- The paper is well-written, with clear and concise explanations of the proposed methods and theoretical proofs.
- The experiments conducted on SMAC and MPE environments demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed method, showing that it can outperform existing MARL algorithms.
- The paper provides a theoretical proof of the convergence of individual-global policies under the IGNT rule, which is a significant contribution to the field.
- The proposed method is flexible and can be integrated into various existing MARL algorithms, enhancing its applicability.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a comprehensive comparison with other existing MARL algorithms, particularly those that also aim to address the issue of individual-global action inconsistency.
- The paper does not provide a detailed comparison of the computational complexity or runtime of the proposed method against existing MARL algorithms.
- The experimental results are not comprehensive, and the paper does not discuss the performance of the proposed method in more challenging or complex scenarios.
- The paper does not provide a detailed discussion on the limitations of the proposed method, which is crucial for understanding its applicability and effectiveness in various scenarios.
- The paper's notation and terminology are sometimes confusing, and the definitions and explanations of key concepts (such as IGTO and IGNT) could be clearer and more intuitive.
