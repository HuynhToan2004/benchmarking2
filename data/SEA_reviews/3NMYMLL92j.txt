 **Summary:**
The paper investigates the use of multimodal feature alignment for predicting brain responses to videos, leveraging ImageBind to embed video, audio, and text captions into a unified space. The study employs a dataset of fMRI responses from subjects watching short video clips, generating detailed multi-event video captions and synthesizing audio from these captions using a text-to-speech model. The authors claim that multimodal feature alignment enhances predictivity of brain activations in multimodal areas like dorsal visual, middle temporal, and late language. However, the paper's novelty is questioned due to the use of existing models like ImageBind without significant modification, and the lack of a robust evaluation metric. The study also lacks a comprehensive analysis of the semantic information extracted from the models and fails to provide a detailed comparison with unimodal representations.

**Strengths:**
- The paper addresses an interesting problem by using AI to analyze brain responses to multimodal stimuli, specifically video, audio, and text captions.
- The use of pre-trained models for generating captions and synthesizing audio from these captions is a novel approach that adds value to the study.
- The paper is well-written and easy to follow, making the complex concepts accessible to readers.
- The application of ImageBind to jointly learn embeddings across text and video is a significant contribution to the field.
- The study provides a detailed analysis of the semantic information extracted from the models, which is crucial for understanding the effectiveness of the proposed methods.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a robust evaluation metric, relying solely on Pearson Correlation which may not adequately assess the quality of the predicted brain responses.
- The novelty of the study is questionable as it primarily uses existing models like ImageBind without significant modification or improvement.
- The paper does not adequately address the issue of semantic information extraction from the models, which is crucial for understanding the effectiveness of the proposed methods.
- The study does not provide a comprehensive comparison with unimodal representations, which is essential for establishing the superiority of the proposed multimodal approach.
- The dataset used in the study is limited, which may not be sufficient to draw robust conclusions about the effectiveness of the proposed methods.
- The paper does not sufficiently discuss the limitations of the study, which is crucial for understanding the scope and applicability of the findings.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors clarify the discrepancy in the number of voxels predicted by text and audio in Figure 5, and explain why these numbers are not consistent across different subjects?
- How does the paper address the issue of semantic information extraction from the models, and what measures were taken to ensure the accuracy and relevance of the extracted information?
- Could the authors provide a more detailed comparison with unimodal representations to establish the superiority of the proposed multimodal approach?
- How does the paper ensure the accuracy and reliability of the predicted brain responses, especially in light of the potential limitations of the evaluation metric used?
- Can the authors discuss the limitations of the study and how these limitations might affect the generalizability and applicability of the findings?
