 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel approach to weakly-supervised semantic segmentation by leveraging CLIP space for semantic prompt learning. It proposes a three-stage method involving segment-label matching, contrastive prompt learning, and class-associated semantic refinement to enhance semantic alignment and suppress background regions. The method is tested on the PASCAL VOC and MS COCO datasets, achieving state-of-the-art (SOTA) performance. The paper is commended for its clarity and the innovative use of CLIP space, although concerns are raised about the complexity of the method, the clarity of some figures, and the lack of comparisons with certain baselines.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, clear, and easy to follow, making complex concepts accessible to readers.
- The use of CLIP space for semantic prompt learning is innovative and effectively enhances the performance of weakly-supervised semantic segmentation.
- The methodology is well-motivated, with a clear description of the three-stage method and its components, which are well-explained and supported by both quantitative and qualitative results.
- The paper provides comprehensive ablation studies, which help in understanding the impact of different components of the proposed method.
- The visualization results are clear and help in understanding the effectiveness of the proposed method.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper could benefit from a clearer explanation of the motivation behind using CLIP space for semantic prompt learning and how it differs from existing methods.
- The complexity of the method, involving multiple stages and components, might make it difficult to understand and replicate.
- Some figures and diagrams (e.g., Figure 2) are not clear or well-explained, which could hinder the reader's comprehension.
- The paper lacks comparisons with certain baselines, such as CLIMS, which could provide a more robust evaluation of the proposed method.
- The paper does not discuss the limitations of the proposed method, which could provide valuable insights into its applicability and potential improvements.
- The performance improvement over existing methods like WeakTr is marginal, which might not fully justify the complexity of the proposed method.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors provide a clearer explanation of the motivation behind using CLIP space for semantic prompt learning and how it differs from existing methods?
- How does the proposed method compare to other state-of-the-art methods in terms of computational efficiency and training time?
- In Figure 2, could the authors clarify the meaning and significance of the different components and arrows?
- Why are there no comparisons with CLIMS in the experimental results, and how does the proposed method differ from or improve upon CLIMS?
- Can the authors discuss the limitations of the proposed method and suggest potential areas for future research?
