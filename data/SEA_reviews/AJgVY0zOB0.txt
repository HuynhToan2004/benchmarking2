 **Summary:**
The paper addresses the challenge of ground-to-satellite image registration, specifically focusing on refining camera poses using a weakly-supervised learning strategy. It introduces a method that leverages contrastive learning to learn feature representations for ground and satellite images, which are useful for translation estimation. The authors propose a pseudo image pair creation strategy for cross-view rotation estimation network training, demonstrating comparable performance to state-of-the-art methods on the VIGOR dataset. The methodology is designed to handle noisy pose labels for ground images and is evaluated on datasets such as KITTI and VIGOR. Despite the promising results, concerns are raised about the novelty of the approach, the clarity of presentation, and the generalization of the results to real-world scenarios.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, clear, and easy to follow, with a clear motivation and a well-structured methodology.
- The proposed method achieves comparable performance to state-of-the-art methods, showing good results on the VIGOR dataset.
- The paper introduces a novel method for camera pose refinement using ground-to-satellite image registration, which is a significant contribution to the field.
- The method is well-explained, and the authors provide a detailed ablation study and analysis of the limitations of their approach.
- The paper addresses the problem of camera pose refinement using a weakly-supervised learning strategy, which is a significant contribution to the field.

**Weaknesses:**
- The novelty of the approach is limited as similar methods have been previously explored in other contexts, such as self-supervised localization.
- The paper lacks a detailed discussion on the limitations of the proposed method, which could help in understanding its applicability and scope.
- The performance of the method is evaluated only on datasets with specific characteristics (e.g., KITTI and VIGOR), which may not generalize well to other datasets or real-world scenarios.
- The paper could benefit from a more thorough evaluation of the method's performance under different conditions, such as varying levels of noise in the input data.
- The presentation of the paper could be improved to make it more accessible to a broader audience, including more detailed explanations and visualizations.
- The paper does not adequately address how the method handles different types of cameras or scenarios, which could limit its applicability.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors provide more details on the datasets used in the experiments, particularly how they were collected and the specific characteristics of the images?
- How does the proposed method compare to other state-of-the-art methods in terms of computational efficiency and practical applicability?
- Can the authors elaborate on the limitations of their approach and how these might affect the practical implementation of their method?
- How does the method perform under different conditions, such as varying levels of noise in the input data or different types of cameras?
- Could the authors provide more visualizations and detailed explanations to enhance the clarity and accessibility of their paper?
