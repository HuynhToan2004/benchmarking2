 **Summary:**
The paper explores the use of generalized exact path kernels (gEPK) for out-of-distribution (OOD) detection in neural networks. It extends the Exact Path Kernel (EPK) framework to a more general setting, allowing for the decomposition of model predictions into localized input gradients or parameter gradients. This decomposition is used to explain the effectiveness of gradient-based methods in OOD detection and to estimate the signal manifold dimension. The paper also discusses the theoretical underpinnings of these methods, comparing them to existing works and proposing new theoretical justifications. Despite its theoretical contributions, the paper is criticized for its lack of empirical validation, limited experimental results, and unclear presentation in some sections.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a generalized exact path kernel (gEPK) which is a natural decomposition of model predictions in terms of parameter gradients, providing a theoretical justification for the success of gradient-based OOD detection methods.
- The gEPK is a novel approach that decomposes model predictions into localized input gradients or parameter gradients, which can be directly compared between models and is independent of architecture.
- The paper provides a theoretical justification for the success of gradient-based OOD detection methods, which is a significant contribution to the field.
- The authors demonstrate that the gEPK can be used to estimate the signal manifold dimension, which is a useful metric for understanding the model's behavior.
- The paper is well-written, making it easy to follow, and the theoretical contributions are significant, providing a deeper understanding of the model's behavior and the effectiveness of gradient-based OOD detection methods.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks empirical validation, particularly in terms of how the gEPK performs in practical OOD detection tasks compared to existing methods.
- The experimental results are limited and do not sufficiently demonstrate the practical applicability of the gEPK in real-world scenarios.
- The paper is not self-contained, requiring prior knowledge of EPK and tangent kernel methods, which might limit its accessibility to a broader audience.
- There are some presentation issues, such as unclear notation and definitions, which could confuse readers unfamiliar with the subject matter.
- The paper could benefit from a more comprehensive experimental validation and a clearer presentation to enhance its readability and applicability.

**Questions:**
- Could you provide more details on how the gEPK can be used in practical OOD detection tasks and how it compares to existing methods in terms of performance and computational efficiency?
- How does the gEPK handle the challenge of estimating the signal manifold dimension in more complex or high-dimensional datasets?
- Can you clarify the notation and definitions used in the paper, particularly in equations and sections where the notation is not standard or clear?
- How does the gEPK handle the issue of overfitting, especially in cases where the training data is limited or the model is complex?
- Could you provide more experimental results or comparisons with existing methods to demonstrate the practical applicability and effectiveness of the gEPK in real-world scenarios?
