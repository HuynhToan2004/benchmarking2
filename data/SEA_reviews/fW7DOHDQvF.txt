 **Summary:**
The paper introduces two methods, Classifier-Consistent Method (CCM) and Risk-Consistent Method (RCM), for multi-class classification from multiple unlabeled datasets. The CCM uses a probability transition function to map class posterior probabilities to surrogate labels, while RCM employs importance weighting to purify supervision information. Both methods aim to handle the challenges of multi-class classification from unlabeled datasets, where the class priors are provided. The paper provides theoretical analysis and empirical results to demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed methods. However, the paper is criticized for its lack of clarity in describing the problem and its contribution to the field, and the experimental results are not convincing enough.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel problem of multi-class classification from multiple unlabeled datasets, which is an important area of research.
- The authors propose two methods, Classifier-Consistent Method (CCM) and Risk-Consistent Method (RCM), to address this problem, which are both theoretically sound and empirically effective.
- The paper provides a comprehensive theoretical analysis and empirical results to demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed methods.
- The problem setting is interesting and relevant, as it addresses the challenge of learning from unlabeled datasets, which is a significant problem in the field of machine learning.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks clarity in describing the problem and its contribution to the field, making it difficult for readers to understand the novelty and significance of the proposed methods.
- The experimental results are not convincing enough, with the performance of the proposed methods not significantly better than existing methods, and the results being similar to those of the baseline methods.
- The paper does not adequately compare the proposed methods with existing methods, which could have provided a clearer picture of the advantages and disadvantages of the proposed methods.
- There is a lack of discussion on the limitations of the proposed methods, which could help in understanding the scope and applicability of the research.
- The paper could benefit from a clearer presentation and better organization to enhance readability and comprehension.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors clarify the problem setting and its contribution to the field of multi-class classification from multiple unlabeled datasets?
- How does the proposed method compare with existing methods, particularly in terms of performance and computational efficiency?
- Can the authors provide more detailed experimental results, including standard deviations and comparisons with other methods on different datasets?
- How does the proposed method handle noisy class priors, and what are the implications of using different class priors in the training process?
- Could the authors discuss the limitations of their proposed methods and how these limitations might affect the practical applicability of the research?
