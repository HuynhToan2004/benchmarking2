 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel method called Dynamic Adapter Merging (DAM) for continual video question-answering (VidQA) learning. This method addresses challenges such as catastrophic forgetting and the need for costly retraining by using domain-specific adapters trained sequentially on different datasets. During inference, these adapters are merged based on a non-parametric router function, which dynamically selects the most relevant adapters for each video-question sample. The approach is evaluated on six different VidQA datasets and demonstrates significant performance improvements over existing methods. The paper also explores the application of DAM in visual question-answering (VQA) and discusses its potential for broader applications.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel method, Dynamic Adapter Merging (DAM), for continual video question-answering (VidQA) learning, which is a significant advancement in the field.
- The method is evaluated on six different VidQA datasets and demonstrates significant performance improvements over existing methods, highlighting its effectiveness.
- The approach is innovative, leveraging domain-specific adapters and model merging techniques to tackle issues such as catastrophic forgetting and the need for costly retraining.
- The paper is well-written, clear, and easy to follow, making it accessible to a broad audience.
- The methodology is simple yet effective, with a straightforward approach to merging adapters that is easy to understand and implement.
- The paper provides a detailed analysis of the impact of different components of the method, such as the router function and adapter merging, which helps in understanding the contributions of each component.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper could benefit from a more comprehensive comparison with other methods, particularly those that use different approaches to continual learning.
- The experimental setup could be improved by including more diverse datasets and testing the method on larger, more complex datasets.
- There is a lack of discussion on the limitations of the proposed method, which is crucial for understanding its applicability and potential drawbacks.
- The paper could benefit from a more thorough analysis of the impact of different hyperparameters on the performance of the method.
- The novelty of the approach might be limited as similar methods have been previously explored in other domains.
- The paper could benefit from a more detailed discussion on the generalization of the method to other tasks and domains beyond VidQA.

**Questions:**
- Could you provide more details on the specific video-language router function used in the DAM method?
- How does the method perform when tested on larger, more complex datasets, such as those used in other continual learning studies?
- What are the specific hyperparameters used in the DAM method, and how do they impact the performance of the method?
- How does the method perform when applied to other tasks or domains, such as image classification or other types of question-answering tasks?
- Can you discuss the potential limitations of the DAM method and how these might impact its applicability in real-world scenarios?
- How does the method compare to other continual learning methods that do not use domain-specific adapters or model merging?
- Could you provide more details on the computational overhead of the DAM method during inference, especially in terms of additional computational costs compared to other methods?
