 **Summary:**
The paper introduces the Alice benchmarks, a new platform for evaluating domain adaptation algorithms in object re-identification, specifically focusing on the "synthetic to real" domain. It provides two datasets, AlicePerson and AliceVehicle, for person and vehicle re-identification, respectively. These datasets consist of real-world images annotated with bounding boxes and synthetic images generated from existing datasets. The paper evaluates various domain adaptation methods on these datasets and provides a comprehensive analysis of their performance. The benchmarks aim to facilitate research on domain adaptation and offer a platform for fair and convenient evaluations. However, concerns were raised about the novelty of the benchmark and the limited scope of the evaluation methods.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a new benchmark for domain adaptation in object re-identification, specifically focusing on the "synthetic to real" domain, which is a significant contribution to the field.
- It provides two datasets, AlicePerson and AliceVehicle, which are well-designed and include a large number of real-world images annotated with bounding boxes, enhancing the practical applicability of the research.
- The paper conducts a thorough evaluation of existing domain adaptation methods, comparing them across various datasets and providing a comprehensive analysis of their performance.
- The benchmarks are designed to facilitate research on domain adaptation and offer a platform for fair and convenient evaluations, which is beneficial for the community.
- The paper is well-written and easy to follow, making it accessible to a broad audience.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a clear motivation for why the "synthetic to real" domain is more suitable for object re-identification tasks compared to other domains.
- The novelty of the benchmark is questionable as it primarily combines existing synthetic and real-world datasets without significant innovation.
- The evaluation methods are limited to a few domain adaptation methods, which may not fully explore the capabilities of the benchmark.
- The paper does not provide a detailed comparison with other existing benchmarks, which could have helped in positioning the Alice benchmarks within the broader context of domain adaptation research.
- The datasets and the evaluation methods are limited to person and vehicle re-identification, which may not fully exploit the potential of the benchmark in other object re-identification tasks.
- The paper lacks a detailed discussion on the challenges and limitations of the benchmark, which could have provided a more comprehensive understanding of its applicability and utility.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors provide a more detailed discussion on the challenges and limitations of the Alice benchmarks, particularly in terms of their applicability and utility in different scenarios?
- How does the Alice benchmark compare with other existing benchmarks in terms of performance and practical applicability?
- What are the main contributions of the paper beyond the introduction of the Alice benchmarks?
- Can the authors clarify the motivation behind the "synthetic to real" domain and its relevance to object re-identification tasks?
- How do the authors address the concerns regarding the novelty and depth of evaluation in their benchmark?
- Could the authors provide more details on the data collection and annotation process for the AlicePerson and AliceVehicle datasets?
