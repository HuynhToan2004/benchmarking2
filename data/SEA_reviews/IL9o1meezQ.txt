 **Summary:**
The paper introduces ARROW-Diff, a novel random walk-based diffusion approach for large-scale graph generation. This method leverages an order-agnostic autoregressive diffusion model to generate graphs, which is trained on single or multiple graphs and can handle both directed and undirected graphs. The method's scalability is demonstrated through experiments on various datasets, showing improved performance over existing methods. However, concerns are raised about the novelty of the approach, as it primarily adapts existing models like OA-ARDM and EDGE without significant innovation. Furthermore, the paper's presentation could be clearer, and the experimental results do not convincingly outperform the baselines across all metrics.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, with clear and concise language that effectively conveys the main ideas and contributions.
- The method is well-explained, with a clear motivation and a detailed description of the methodology, making it easy to understand.
- The proposed method is novel, particularly in its application of OA-ARDM to random walks for graph generation, which is a novel approach.
- The experiments are well-designed, with a focus on large-scale graph generation, which is a significant and relevant problem in the field.
- The paper includes a complexity analysis, which helps in understanding the computational efficiency of the proposed method.
- The methodology is scalable and can handle large graphs, which is a significant advantage in the field of graph generation.

**Weaknesses:**
- The novelty of the method is limited as it primarily adapts existing models like OA-ARDM and EDGE without significant innovation.
- The paper lacks a thorough comparison with recent diffusion-based graph generation methods, which could have provided a more robust evaluation of the proposed method.
- The experimental results do not convincingly outperform the baselines across all metrics, raising questions about the effectiveness of the proposed method.
- The presentation of the paper could be improved, particularly in the introduction and methodology sections, to better highlight the contributions and the advantages of the proposed method over existing approaches.
- There are inconsistencies in the experimental results, such as the performance of ARROW-Diff not being consistently better than other methods in all datasets.
- The paper does not sufficiently address the limitations of the proposed method, which could help in understanding the scope and applicability of the work.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors clarify the novelty of the method and how it differs significantly from existing approaches like OA-ARDM and EDGE?
- How does the proposed method handle the scalability issues, especially in large graphs, and what are the specific challenges in scaling the method to very large graphs?
- Could the authors provide more details on the experimental setup, such as the specific datasets used and the hyperparameters chosen for the baseline models?
- How does the proposed method compare in terms of runtime and computational efficiency with other state-of-the-art methods, especially in large-scale graph generation?
- Could the authors provide more details on the training and evaluation of the OA-ARDM model, particularly in terms of the architecture and training parameters used?
- How does the proposed method handle the generation of graphs with varying node counts, and what are the implications of this limitation in practical applications?
