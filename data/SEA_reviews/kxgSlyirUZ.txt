 **Summary:**
The paper introduces Collie, a novel grammar-based framework designed to systematically generate constraints for text generation tasks. Collie enables the specification of diverse constraints across various text generation levels, such as word, sentence, paragraph, and passage, using a grammar-based approach. The framework also includes a dataset, Collie-v1, which comprises 2,080 instances with 13 constraint structures, evaluated against five state-of-the-art language models. The paper provides insights into the performance of these models under different constraints, revealing areas of improvement and potential for future research. Despite its innovative approach, concerns were raised regarding the novelty and depth of the experimental results, suggesting that the framework's utility could be enhanced by more varied and challenging constraints.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel grammar-based framework, Collie, which simplifies the process of creating constrained generation datasets by focusing on specifying high-level constraint structures, automatically extracting constraint values, rendering natural language instructions, and assessing model performance.
- Collie is a lightweight, extensible framework that allows researchers to easily specify constraint structures and automatically extract constraint values from text corpora, rendering constraints into natural language instructions, and evaluating model generations against constraints and ground truth.
- The framework is designed to be extensible, enabling the community to contribute additional constraints that can co-evolve with LLM capabilities over time, providing a convenient endpoint for users who only want to evaluate their model without developing their own constraints.
- The paper provides a comprehensive evaluation of five state-of-the-art LLMs of varying sizes, offering useful insights for both model and benchmark development in the future.
- The Collie framework is designed to be modular and extensible, allowing researchers to easily specify constraint structures and automatically extract constraint values from text corpora, rendering constraints into natural language instructions, and evaluating model generations against constraints and ground truth.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed discussion on how the constraint extraction process is performed, which is crucial for understanding the methodology behind the framework.
- There is a lack of clarity on the motivation for the specific constraint structures used in the dataset, and the paper does not provide insights into the extraction process or the criteria used for selecting constraints.
- The paper does not provide sufficient details on the evaluation methodology, such as the specific metrics used and the methodology for evaluating model performance against constraints.
- The dataset used in the study is relatively small (2,080 instances), which may not be sufficient for a comprehensive evaluation of the framework's capabilities.
- The paper lacks a detailed discussion on the limitations of the framework, such as the scalability and generalizability of the approach, and the potential biases in the dataset.
- The paper does not discuss the potential societal impacts of the framework, which could be significant given its potential applications in various domains.

**Questions:**
- Could you provide more details on the constraint extraction process, including the specific algorithms or techniques used?
- How were the constraint structures selected for the dataset, and what criteria were used to ensure their relevance and effectiveness?
- Could you clarify the evaluation methodology, particularly the metrics used and the methodology for evaluating model performance against constraints?
- How does the framework handle the generation of complex constraints that may not be directly represented in the dataset?
- Could you discuss the potential societal impacts of the framework, particularly in terms of its application in various domains and the potential biases or ethical considerations associated with its use?
- How does the framework ensure the quality and reliability of the extracted constraints, especially in cases where automated filtering may not be sufficient?
