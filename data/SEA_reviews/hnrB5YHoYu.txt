 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a method for fine-tuning text-to-image diffusion models to reduce biases in generated images, focusing on attributes such as gender, race, and age. The method employs a distributional alignment loss to adjust the target distribution of generated images, alongside a modified gradient computation to improve the stability of the training process. The authors also propose an adjusted DFT loss to fine-tune the sampling process of the diffusion model. Experimental results show that the proposed method effectively reduces biases in generated images, although the paper's clarity and the robustness of the results have been criticized.

**Strengths:**
- The paper addresses an important and timely issue of reducing biases in text-to-image diffusion models, which is crucial for fairness and ethical considerations in AI.
- The proposed method is simple, intuitive, and well-motivated, with a clear and well-structured presentation that makes it easy to follow.
- The experiments are well-designed, showing a good reduction in biases and demonstrating the effectiveness of the proposed method.
- The paper includes a thorough analysis of the training process and the design of the loss function, which is well-explained and supported by clear mathematical formulations.
- The results are convincing, with the proposed method showing significant improvements in reducing biases compared to existing methods.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks clarity in some parts, particularly in the explanation of certain technical details such as the adjustment of the gradient computation and the definition of terms like "Bt" and "Ai" in Figure 2.
- The experiments are limited to specific attributes (gender, race, and age) and do not include a broader range of attributes or a more diverse set of generated images.
- The paper does not adequately discuss the limitations of the proposed method, such as potential negative societal impacts, the generalizability of the method to other types of biases, and the scalability of the method to more complex scenarios.
- There is a lack of qualitative results and comparisons with existing methods, which makes it difficult to assess the effectiveness and novelty of the proposed method.
- The paper could benefit from more detailed explanations and examples of the training process, especially how the loss function is applied and how it affects the training of different components of the model.

**Questions:**
- Can the proposed method be extended to reduce other types of biases, such as those related to body shape or clothing?
- How does the method perform when applied to models that have already been trained on diverse datasets?
- What are the potential negative societal impacts of the proposed method, and how can these be mitigated?
- How does the method compare to other existing methods in terms of effectiveness and efficiency?
- Could you provide more detailed explanations and examples of how the loss function is applied during the training process, and how it affects the training of different components of the model?
- How does the method handle the trade-off between reducing biases and maintaining image quality?
