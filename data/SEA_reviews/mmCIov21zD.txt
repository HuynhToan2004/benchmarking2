 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel dynamic SLAM framework using neural radiance fields to address the challenges of dynamic environments in SLAM systems. The framework includes a motion mask generation method that combines optical flow and semantic masks to enhance motion mask precision, and a divide-and-conquer pose optimization algorithm for keyframes and non-keyframes. Extensive experiments on two datasets demonstrate the framework's effectiveness, showing improvements in both accuracy and robustness. However, the paper has been critiqued for its reliance on existing techniques, limited novelty, and potential inaccuracies in motion mask generation.

**Strengths:**
- The paper addresses the significant problem of dynamic environments in SLAM systems, proposing a novel approach using neural radiance fields.
- The proposed method is well-motivated, with a clear focus on improving the robustness of pose estimation in dynamic environments.
- The manuscript is well-written, making the content accessible and easy to follow.
- The experiments are comprehensive, including comparisons with state-of-the-art methods on challenging datasets, demonstrating the effectiveness of the proposed method.
- The paper is the first to utilize neural implicit representation for dynamic SLAM, a significant advancement in the field.
- The methodology is technically sound, with a focus on reducing computational complexity and enhancing efficiency.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a thorough discussion on the limitations of the proposed method, particularly in terms of its applicability to real-world scenarios and the handling of dynamic objects.
- The novelty of the work is limited as it primarily combines existing techniques without significant innovation.
- The paper could benefit from a more detailed comparison with traditional SLAM methods and a broader range of dynamic scene datasets.
- There is a noted inaccuracy in the motion mask generation method, which could lead to incorrect results in dynamic scenes.
- The paper does not adequately address the computational efficiency of the method, which might limit its practical application in real-time scenarios.
- The paper could benefit from a more detailed explanation of the methodology, particularly in the generation of motion masks and the use of optical flow masks.

**Questions:**
- How does the proposed method perform in scenarios with multiple dynamic objects, and how is it adapted to handle such complexities?
- Can the method be extended to handle dynamic scenes with more complex scenarios, such as multiple dynamic objects with varying speeds and trajectories?
- How does the proposed method compare with traditional SLAM methods in terms of computational efficiency and practical application?
- In the motion mask generation method, how is the threshold for dynamic object detection determined, and what impact does this have on the accuracy of the mask?
- Could you provide more details on the computational complexity of the proposed method and how it compares to other state-of-the-art methods?
- How does the proposed method handle scenarios where the dynamic object is partially occluded or only partially visible in the image?
- Can the authors clarify the role of the motion mask in the overall system and how it affects the accuracy of the reconstruction?
