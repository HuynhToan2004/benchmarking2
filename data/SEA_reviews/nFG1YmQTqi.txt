 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel score-based generative model (SGM) for time-series data, specifically designed for regular and irregular time-series synthesis. The model employs a denoising score matching loss, which is a first for this application, to enhance the diversity and quality of synthesized time-series. It also integrates a conditional score network to learn the conditional score function, which is crucial for generating time-series data. The model's effectiveness is demonstrated through experiments on various datasets, showing superior performance compared to existing methods. However, concerns were raised regarding the novelty of the method, the clarity of the presentation, and the depth of the experimental validation.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel approach to time-series generation using a score-based generative model, which is a first in this field.
- The methodology is well-explained, and the paper is generally well-written, making it accessible to readers.
- The proposed method achieves superior performance in generating time-series data, as evidenced by the results presented.
- The paper is original in its application of SGM to time-series generation, which is an innovative approach.
- The experiments are extensive, covering various datasets and baseline methods, which helps in validating the proposed method.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a clear differentiation between the proposed method and existing methods, particularly in terms of its novel contributions.
- The presentation of the paper is not clear, with some sections being difficult to follow due to unclear explanations and notations.
- The experimental setup and results are not convincingly superior to existing methods, with some results showing similar or even inferior performance compared to baselines.
- The paper does not adequately address the issue of mode collapse, which is a significant limitation in generative models.
- There are concerns about the scalability of the proposed method to more complex datasets and the generalizability of the results.
- The paper does not sufficiently discuss the limitations of the proposed method, which could affect its practical applicability.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors clarify the differences between the proposed method and existing methods, particularly in terms of the novel contributions?
- How does the proposed method handle the issue of mode collapse, which is a known limitation in generative models?
- In the experimental results, why does the proposed method sometimes perform worse than existing methods, such as TimeGAN and GT-GAN?
- How does the method perform when applied to other types of time-series data, such as video or audio?
- Could the authors provide more detailed explanations for the notations and equations used in the paper to improve clarity and understanding?
- How does the method perform when scaled to larger datasets or more complex time-series data?
- Can the authors discuss the limitations of their method and how these might affect its practical applicability?
