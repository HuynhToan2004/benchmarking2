 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel physics-guided learning framework, PhyDL-NWP, aimed at improving weather forecasting and downscaling tasks. This framework integrates deep learning with physical knowledge to enhance the understanding of meteorological dynamics and improve generalization capabilities. The methodology involves training a neural network to predict weather conditions using spatial-temporal coordinates and then utilizing auto-differentiation to obtain PDE terms, which are used to guide the optimization process. The paper presents extensive experiments across three datasets, showing significant performance improvements over existing methods. However, the paper is criticized for its limited novelty, unclear presentation, and lack of comprehensive comparison with state-of-the-art models.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel physics-guided learning framework, PhyDL-NWP, which integrates deep learning with physical knowledge to improve the understanding of meteorological dynamics and enhance the generalization capabilities for weather prediction tasks.
- The proposed method is versatile and can be applied to a wide range of tasks, including medium-range weather forecasting and weather downscaling with unlimited granularity.
- The paper is well-structured, providing clear explanations and detailed methodologies, which are supported by extensive experiments on three real-world datasets.
- The experimental results show significant performance improvements compared to existing methods, demonstrating the effectiveness of the proposed framework.
- The paper is well-written, with a clear and concise presentation that makes it easy to follow, and the experiments are well-documented, making it easy to reproduce the results.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a comprehensive comparison with state-of-the-art models, which limits the ability to assess the performance of the proposed method against the most advanced techniques.
- The novelty of the proposed method is limited as it primarily combines existing methods without introducing significant new techniques or insights.
- The presentation of the paper is unclear and difficult to follow, with many mathematical symbols and notations not well-explained, which could hinder understanding for readers unfamiliar with the field.
- The paper does not provide sufficient details on the experimental setup, such as the specifics of the datasets used and the preprocessing techniques employed, which could affect the reproducibility and generalizability of the results.
- The paper does not adequately address the limitations of the proposed method, which could help in understanding its applicability and potential drawbacks.
- The paper could benefit from a more rigorous evaluation, including a broader range of metrics and a more comprehensive analysis of the results to provide a more robust validation of the proposed method.
