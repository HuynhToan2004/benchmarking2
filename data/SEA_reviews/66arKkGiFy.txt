 **Summary:**
The paper explores the Plug-and-Play Unadjusted Langevin Algorithm (PnP-ULA) for posterior sampling in inverse problems, focusing on the mismatch between the data-fidelity term and the denoiser. It introduces a novel pseudometric to quantify the sensitivity of the sampling distribution to such mismatches. Theoretical analysis is provided to bound the total variation and Wasserstein distance between the invariant distributions of the Markov chain under different drift parameters. Numerical experiments are conducted to validate these theoretical findings, primarily using synthetic data and simplified models. The paper aims to bridge the gap in understanding the relationship between the sampling distribution of PnP-ULA and the mismatched data-fidelity and denoiser, which is crucial for practical applications in imaging inverse problems.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written and easy to follow, making complex concepts accessible to a broader audience.
- It addresses a significant gap in the literature by providing a theoretical analysis of the relationship between the sampling distribution of PnP-ULA and the mismatched data-fidelity and denoiser.
- The paper introduces a novel pseudometric to quantify the sensitivity of the sampling distribution to a mismatch in the measurement model and the denoiser, which is a crucial aspect of the PnP-ULA framework.
- The theoretical results are backed by numerical experiments, demonstrating the practical relevance and applicability of the proposed methods.
- The paper contributes to the understanding of the sensitivity of the sampling distribution of PnP-ULA to a mismatch in the measurement model and the denoiser, which is crucial for practical applications in imaging inverse problems.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper primarily focuses on the Plug-and-Play Unadjusted Langevin Algorithm (PnP-ULA), which might limit its applicability to other methods such as Gibbs sampling and conditional diffusion models.
- The theoretical results are derived under strong assumptions, such as the boundedness of the forward model, which might not hold in practical scenarios.
- The paper does not discuss the limitations of the proposed method, which could affect the practical applicability and generalizability of the findings.
- The experiments are limited to synthetic data and simplified models, which might not fully capture the complexity and variability of real-world imaging inverse problems.
- The paper does not provide a clear definition or justification for the pseudometric used, which could affect the interpretation and application of the results.

**Questions:**
- How does the proposed pseudometric compare to other metrics for evaluating the quality of the sampling distribution, such as the total variation distance or the Kullback-Leibler divergence?
- Can the results be extended to other methods besides PnP-ULA, such as Gibbs sampling or conditional diffusion models?
- How do the authors justify the assumptions made in the theoretical analysis, particularly the boundedness of the forward model and the smoothness of the log-likelihood?
- Could the authors provide more detailed discussions on the limitations of the proposed method and suggest potential avenues for future research?
- How do the constants A0, B0, A1, B1, A2, B2, A3, B3 depend on the data and the parameters of the Markov chain, and can these dependencies be quantified?
