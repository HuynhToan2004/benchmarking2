 **Summary:**
The paper introduces ProxyBench, a benchmark designed to evaluate the robustness of neural network proxies under optimization pressure, focusing on the text domain. It investigates the phenomenon of proxy gaming and its impact on the robustness of neural network proxies. The benchmark includes various optimization pressure sources and metrics to assess proxy reward model robustness. The paper also explores the correlation between the size of the proxy model and the extent of proxy gaming. Despite its novelty in addressing proxy gaming, the paper is criticized for its limited scope, lack of comprehensive evaluation, and potential misleading conclusions regarding the effectiveness of adversarial training in enhancing proxy robustness.

**Strengths:**
- The paper addresses an important and understudied problem in the field of AI: the robustness of neural network proxies, which is crucial for ensuring the reliability and trustworthiness of AI systems.
- The introduction of ProxyBench, a comprehensive benchmark for investigating the robustness of neural network proxies under various sources of optimization pressure in the text domain, is a significant contribution to the field.
- The paper includes a detailed analysis of the properties of the proxy gaming problem, which could provide valuable insights for future research and development in this area.
- The authors provide a clear and comprehensive description of the benchmark and the evaluation process, which could be useful for other researchers in the field.
- The paper is well-written and easy to follow, which is essential for making complex research accessible to a broader audience.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper is limited in scope as it only investigates proxy gaming in the text domain, which may not generalize to other domains or types of neural network proxies.
- The evaluation of proxy robustness is limited to a few models and optimization methods, which may not provide a comprehensive understanding of the robustness of different types of neural network proxies under various optimization pressures.
- The paper's conclusions regarding the effectiveness of adversarial training in enhancing proxy robustness are misleading and not supported by the data presented.
- The paper's reliance on a single model, the LLaMA-7B model, limits the generalizability of the findings to other models.
- The paper does not adequately address the potential negative societal impacts of its findings, which could be significant given the potential applications of the research.
- The paper's claims about the correlation between proxy gaming and the size of the proxy model are not convincingly supported by the data presented.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors clarify the relationship between the size of the proxy model and the extent of proxy gaming? What specific factors contribute to this correlation, and how does it vary across different types of neural network proxies?
- How does the paper's benchmark, ProxyBench, compare to other existing benchmarks for evaluating the robustness of neural network proxies? What are the advantages and disadvantages of using ProxyBench over these other benchmarks?
- Can the authors provide more details on the adversarial training methods used in the paper, particularly how they were implemented and what specific hyperparameters were used?
- In the paper's conclusions regarding the effectiveness of adversarial training in enhancing proxy robustness, what evidence supports these conclusions? Why do the authors believe that adversarial training is not effective in the BoN and RLHF settings?
- The paper mentions that adversarial training can help defend against downstream adversarial proxy optimizations in the white-box attack setting. Can the authors provide more details on these results, including the specific adversarial training methods used and the results of the attacks?
- Could the authors clarify the discrepancy between the final gold reward and the RMS metric in the paper? What do these metrics measure, and how do they relate to the overall robustness of the neural network proxies?
