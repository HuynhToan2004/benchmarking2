 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel method for achieving fairness in machine learning without the use of demographic information. It proposes minimizing the variance of training losses as a means of achieving fairness, which is a departure from traditional methods that rely on demographic data. The method is evaluated across four benchmark datasets, showing improvements in fairness metrics without compromising overall model performance. The approach is based on a dynamic weighting scheme that balances the variance of losses with the overall average loss, ensuring that the model remains fair and effective. The paper also includes a theoretical analysis and empirical evidence to support the proposed method.

**Strengths:**
- The paper addresses a significant and relevant problem in machine learning, namely achieving fairness without demographic information.
- The proposed method is novel and provides a new perspective on fairness in machine learning, focusing on the variance of losses rather than the expectation of losses.
- The paper is well-written, making it accessible and easy to understand, with clear explanations of the methodology and experimental results.
- The experimental results show that the proposed method outperforms existing methods in terms of fairness without compromising overall model performance.
- The paper provides a theoretical analysis that supports the proposed method, adding credibility to the approach.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper could benefit from a more detailed explanation of the theoretical underpinnings of the proposed method, particularly in how it achieves fairness without demographic information.
- The experimental setup and results are not sufficiently detailed or comprehensive, with some results being relegated to the appendix which could limit the accessibility of the findings.
- There is a lack of comparison with other fairness-without-demographics methods, which could provide a more robust evaluation of the proposed method.
- The paper could benefit from a more thorough discussion of related work, especially in the context of fairness without demographics.
- The paper's claims about the method's ability to achieve fairness without demographics are not convincingly supported by the experimental results, which do not convincingly demonstrate that the method is more effective than existing methods.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors provide a more detailed explanation of how the proposed method achieves fairness without demographic information?
- How does the proposed method compare to other fairness-without-demographics methods, particularly in terms of effectiveness and computational efficiency?
- Can the authors provide more detailed results and comparisons in the main body of the paper to support the claims made in the appendix?
- How does the proposed method handle different types of fairness criteria, such as individual fairness versus group fairness?
- Could the authors clarify the experimental setup and results, particularly in terms of how the method was applied and the specific outcomes observed?
