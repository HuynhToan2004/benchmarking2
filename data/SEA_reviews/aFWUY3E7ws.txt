 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel method for time series prediction named Global-Local Identification and Prediction (GLIP), which utilizes Fourier basis for global identification and local prediction. The GLIP model is designed to be computationally efficient, not requiring backpropagation, and can be implemented on CPUs, which is advantageous for practical applications. The method's effectiveness is demonstrated through experiments on benchmark datasets, showing improvements over existing methods. However, the paper suffers from significant issues in clarity, organization, and methodological detail, which hinder understanding and evaluation of the proposed method.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-motivated, focusing on the efficient and interpretable prediction of time series, which is a significant and relevant problem in the field.
- The proposed method, GLIP, is novel and well-articulated, with a clear explanation of its components and their roles in the overall process.
- The paper provides a comprehensive evaluation of GLIP, comparing it against state-of-the-art methods and demonstrating its superior performance in certain scenarios.
- The method's efficiency and the absence of backpropagation make it well-suited for CPU environments, which is a practical advantage.
- The paper includes a detailed description of the GLIP method, which aids in understanding its implementation and potential applications.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper suffers from a lack of clarity and organization, making it difficult to follow the methodology and understand the contributions of different components.
- There are numerous typographical and grammatical errors throughout the text, which detract from the professional quality of the paper.
- The paper lacks a detailed discussion on the limitations of the proposed method, which is crucial for a comprehensive understanding of its applicability and effectiveness.
- The experimental setup and results are not adequately explained or justified, which undermines the credibility of the findings.
- The paper does not sufficiently compare its method with existing state-of-the-art methods, particularly in terms of computational efficiency and performance metrics.
- The paper could benefit from a more rigorous validation of its claims, including a deeper exploration of the method's limitations and potential biases.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors clarify the role and significance of different components in the GLIP method, particularly in terms of their impact on the overall prediction accuracy?
- How does the GLIP method perform when compared to other state-of-the-art methods in terms of computational efficiency and prediction accuracy?
- Could the authors provide more detailed explanations or examples to illustrate the method's effectiveness in practical scenarios, especially in terms of its ability to handle long-term predictions?
- How does the GLIP method handle missing data points or noisy data, which are common in real-world time series data?
- What are the potential biases or limitations of the GLIP method, and how might these affect its performance in different scenarios?
- Could the authors elaborate on the experimental setup and results, particularly in terms of the choice of datasets and the methodology used for validation?
