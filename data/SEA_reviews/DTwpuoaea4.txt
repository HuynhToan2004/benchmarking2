 **Summary:**
The paper introduces PAGER, a novel framework designed to detect and categorize failure modes in deep regression models. PAGER integrates both epistemic uncertainties and non-conformity scores, leveraging the anchoring principle to systematically analyze model errors across different risk regimes. This approach allows for the identification of accurate generalization regions and detection of failure cases in out-of-distribution and out-of-support scenarios. The paper provides empirical evidence of PAGER's effectiveness, showcasing its ability to outperform existing methods in various datasets and tasks. Despite its strengths, the paper faces some limitations, including the lack of a comprehensive comparison with state-of-the-art methods and a need for more rigorous validation on larger, more complex datasets.
