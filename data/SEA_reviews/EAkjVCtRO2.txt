 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel time series forecasting model that integrates discrete state space hidden Markov models with neural network architectures, utilizing a two-stage training procedure based on the Evidence Lower Bound (ELBO). The model is designed to handle large datasets with heterogeneous time series, leveraging external signals and providing probabilistic predictions. The paper claims that the model outperforms existing state-of-the-art methods on several datasets, demonstrating its effectiveness. However, concerns were raised regarding the novelty of the approach, the lack of comprehensive theoretical analysis, and the need for more rigorous comparisons with existing methods.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel method that combines hidden Markov models with neural networks, which is a significant advancement in the field of time series forecasting.
- The methodology is well-explained, making it easy to understand and replicate, which is crucial for scientific progress.
- The paper presents a comprehensive experimental evaluation, demonstrating the effectiveness of the proposed method across various datasets.
- The use of a two-stage training procedure based on the Evidence Lower Bound (ELBO) is innovative and contributes to the paper's novelty.
- The paper is well-written, with clear explanations of the methodology and results, which enhances its readability and accessibility.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a thorough comparison with existing methods, particularly in terms of computational efficiency and theoretical underpinnings.
- The novelty of the approach is questionable as it appears to be a straightforward combination of existing models without significant innovation.
- The paper does not provide a detailed analysis of the computational complexity or the scalability of the proposed method, which is crucial for understanding its applicability in real-world scenarios.
- The paper does not adequately address the limitations of the proposed method, which is essential for understanding its scope and applicability.
- The paper could benefit from a more rigorous theoretical analysis and a clearer explanation of the method's theoretical foundations.
- The paper could be improved by including more comprehensive comparisons with existing methods, particularly in terms of computational efficiency and theoretical underpinnings.
