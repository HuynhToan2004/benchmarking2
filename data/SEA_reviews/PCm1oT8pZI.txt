 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel watermarking method for deep neural networks that utilizes a single out-of-distribution (OoD) image as a secret key, enhancing the security and robustness of the watermark against various removal attacks such as fine-tuning, pruning, and model extraction. The method involves augmenting the OoD image to create a surrogate dataset, which is then used for watermark injection through backdoor training. The paper demonstrates that the proposed method is time- and sample-efficient, and the watermark remains robust against these attacks, maintaining the model's utility. However, the paper's novelty is questioned due to its similarity to existing works, and the method's effectiveness is not convincingly demonstrated across a broader range of datasets and model architectures.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, easy to follow, and the methodology is clearly explained, making it accessible to readers.
- The proposed method is innovative, using a single out-of-distribution (OoD) image as a secret key, which is a novel approach in the field of watermarking.
- The paper demonstrates the robustness of the watermark against various removal attacks such as fine-tuning, pruning, and model extraction, which is crucial for maintaining the security of the watermark.
- The method is time- and sample-efficient, which is advantageous for practical applications where computational resources are limited.
- The paper provides extensive experiments to verify the effectiveness of the proposed method, demonstrating its utility and robustness.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a comprehensive comparison with existing methods, particularly in terms of the effectiveness of the watermarking method across different datasets and model architectures.
- The novelty of the proposed method is questioned due to its similarity to existing works, particularly in the use of OoD images for watermarking.
- The paper does not provide a detailed analysis of the impact of the weight perturbation strategy on the performance of the model, which is a critical aspect of the proposed method.
- The robustness of the watermark against different types of attacks, such as adversarial attacks, is not thoroughly evaluated, which could limit the understanding of the method's effectiveness in real-world scenarios.
- The paper does not discuss the potential negative societal impacts of the proposed method, which is a crucial aspect of ethical considerations in technology development.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors provide more details on the specific attacks used in the robustness evaluation and the rationale behind their selection?
- How does the weight perturbation strategy affect the performance of the model, and what are the implications of this strategy in practical applications?
- Can the authors provide a more detailed comparison with existing methods, particularly in terms of the effectiveness of the watermarking method across different datasets and model architectures?
- What are the potential negative societal impacts of the proposed method, and how does the paper address these concerns?
- How does the paper ensure the fairness and robustness of the evaluation, particularly in the comparison with existing methods?
