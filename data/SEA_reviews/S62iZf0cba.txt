 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel approach, Multi-Objective Molecular Design through Learning Latent Pareto Set (MLPS), which transforms discrete chemical space into a continuous latent space using an encoder-decoder model. This transformation facilitates the application of Bayesian optimization to identify local optimal solutions and learn the mapping between preferences and the Pareto set in the latent space. The MLPS model leverages a global Pareto set learning model and local Bayesian optimization models to efficiently search for local optimal solutions and adapt trust regions dynamically. The paper presents extensive experiments that demonstrate the superiority of MLPS over existing methods, although the approach's scalability and practical applicability are questioned.
