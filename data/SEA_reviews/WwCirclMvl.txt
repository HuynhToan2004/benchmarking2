 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel framework for posterior sampling in offline reinforcement learning (RL) using Langevin Monte Carlo (LMC) algorithms. The framework, which includes two variants tailored for linear MDPs and overparameterized neural networks, aims to address the challenges of posterior sampling in high-dimensional settings. Theoretical analysis provides sub-optimality bounds for the proposed algorithms, and empirical results demonstrate the efficiency and competitiveness of the methods. The paper also discusses the computational efficiency and stability issues associated with exact posterior sampling, highlighting the practical advantages of the proposed methods.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel framework for posterior sampling in offline RL, which is the first of its kind to leverage LMC algorithms.
- The authors provide theoretical analysis, including sub-optimality bounds for the proposed algorithms, which are supported by empirical results showing the efficiency and competitiveness of the methods.
- The paper is well-written, clear, and easy to follow, making it accessible to a broad audience.
- The algorithm's simplicity and the analysis's clarity are notable, which could be beneficial for practitioners seeking to implement the method in real-world scenarios.
- The paper addresses a significant gap in the literature by focusing on the use of posterior sampling in offline RL, a topic that has been under-explored.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a thorough comparison with existing methods, particularly in terms of computational efficiency and the practical applicability of the theoretical results.
- The assumptions made in the theoretical analysis, such as the linearity of the Bellman operator and the symmetry of network initialization, are not well justified or explained.
- There is a notable absence of empirical results for the neural-LMC algorithm, which is crucial for validating the claims made about its performance.
- The paper could benefit from a more detailed discussion on the computational complexity and practical implications of the proposed methods, especially in high-dimensional settings.
- The paper does not sufficiently address the limitations of the proposed methods, particularly in terms of their scalability and applicability to more complex MDPs.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors provide a more detailed comparison with existing methods, particularly in terms of computational efficiency and the practical applicability of the theoretical results?
- How do the authors justify the assumptions made in the theoretical analysis, particularly the linearity of the Bellman operator and the symmetry of network initialization?
- Could the authors elaborate on the empirical results for the neural-LMC algorithm and discuss the potential reasons for its absence in the experimental section?
- How does the proposed method compare in terms of computational complexity and stability with exact posterior sampling, especially in high-dimensional settings?
- Could the authors provide a more detailed discussion on the scalability and applicability of the proposed methods to more complex MDPs, especially those that do not conform to the linear or low-rank assumptions used in the analysis?
