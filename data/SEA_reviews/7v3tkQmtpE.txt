 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel approach to sequential decision making using the Decision Transformer (DT) framework, integrating it with Hierarchical Reinforcement Learning (HRL). The proposed method, named Autotuned Decision Transformer (ADT), introduces two new algorithms, Value-prompted Autotuned Decision Transformer (V-ADT) and Goal-prompted Autotuned Decision Transformer (G-ADT), to optimize high-level and low-level policies. These algorithms are tested against various baselines and demonstrate superior performance in stitching sub-optimal trajectories. The paper also discusses the limitations of DT and proposes a hierarchical framework to address these issues. However, concerns were raised about the novelty of the approach, the clarity of the methodology, and the comparative analysis with existing methods.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, with clear and concise explanations that make the content accessible and easy to understand.
- The authors provide a detailed analysis of the limitations of the Decision Transformer (DT) and propose a novel hierarchical framework to address these issues.
- The proposed method shows promising results, outperforming DT and other baselines in many tasks, demonstrating the effectiveness of the approach.
- The paper provides a comprehensive discussion on the limitations of DT and proposes a hierarchical framework to address these issues, which is a significant contribution to the field.
- The authors have conducted extensive experiments and ablation studies to validate their claims, which supports the robustness of their findings.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a clear comparison with existing methods, particularly in terms of the novelty of the proposed approach and its advantages over existing methods.
- The methodology is not well-explained, with several key components such as the "empirical MDP" and the "high-level policy" being confusing and not adequately defined.
- The paper does not adequately discuss the limitations of the proposed method, which is crucial for understanding the scope and applicability of the findings.
- The experimental results are not convincingly superior to existing methods, with some results showing marginal improvements or even inferior performance in certain environments.
- The paper does not sufficiently address the computational cost of the proposed method, which is a significant concern for practical applications.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors clarify the term "empirical MDP" and the role of the high-level policy in the proposed method?
- How does the proposed method compare to other methods that also use hierarchical reinforcement learning (HRL) to improve the performance of DT?
- Can the authors provide more detailed explanations of the experimental results, particularly in environments where the proposed method underperforms compared to DT?
- How does the computational cost of the proposed method compare to DT, and what are the implications for practical applications?
- Could the authors discuss the limitations of the proposed method and how these might affect the generalizability of the findings?
