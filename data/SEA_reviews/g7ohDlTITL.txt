 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel method for training continuous normalizing flows on manifolds, termed Riemannian Flow Matching (RFM), which leverages a premetric to define target vector fields. This method is simulation-free on simple geometries and does not require divergence computation, making it computationally efficient and scalable. The RFM method is evaluated against state-of-the-art baselines on various datasets, demonstrating superior performance in many cases. The method's theoretical framework is well-articulated, and the paper provides a clear and detailed description of the training procedure. The experiments conducted on both synthetic and real-world datasets showcase the method's effectiveness and scalability.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel method for training continuous normalizing flows on manifolds, which is a significant advancement in the field.
- The method is computationally efficient and scalable, as it is simulation-free on simple geometries and does not require divergence computation, making it suitable for high-dimensional and complex data distributions.
- The paper is well-written and clearly presents the methodology, making it accessible and understandable to a broad audience.
- The authors have provided a detailed description of the training procedure, which is crucial for reproducibility and practical implementation.
- The paper includes a comprehensive set of experiments that demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed method across various datasets and scenarios, showcasing its versatility and applicability.
- The theoretical framework of the method is well-articulated, providing a solid foundation for the proposed approach.

**Weaknesses:**
