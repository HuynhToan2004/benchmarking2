 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel frequency-aware transformer (FAT) block for learned image compression, which includes the frequency-decomposition window attention (FDWA) and frequency-modulation feed-forward network (FMFFN) modules. FDWA decomposes input images into four components, enabling multiscale and directional frequency component analysis. FMFFN adaptively modulates different frequency components, improving rate-distortion performance. The T-CA entropy model effectively exploits channel dependencies. The method achieves state-of-the-art rate-distortion performance, surpassing the latest standardized codec VTM-12.1 by significant margins on standard datasets. However, the paper lacks a detailed discussion on the computational complexity and memory requirements of the proposed modules, and it does not include comparisons with certain recent works.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel frequency-aware transformer (FAT) block, which includes frequency-decomposition window attention (FDWA) and frequency-modulation feed-forward network (FMFFN) modules. These modules enable multiscale and directional frequency component analysis, improving rate-distortion performance.
- The proposed T-CA entropy model effectively exploits channel dependencies, which is a significant contribution to the field of learned image compression.
- The paper achieves state-of-the-art rate-distortion performance, surpassing the latest standardized codec VTM-12.1 by significant margins on standard datasets.
- The FAT block is designed to capture multiscale and directional frequency components of natural images, which is a novel approach in the field of learned image compression.
- The paper is well-written, with clear explanations of the proposed methods and their contributions to the field.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed discussion on the computational complexity and memory requirements of the proposed modules, which are crucial for practical deployment in real-world applications.
- There is a lack of comparison with certain recent works, such as "A Comprehensive Survey on Learned Image Compression" and "A Survey on Transformer-based Image Compression," which could provide a more robust evaluation of the proposed methods.
- The paper does not provide a comparison with other transformer-based learned image compression methods, which could help in understanding the relative performance of the proposed method.
- The paper does not include experiments on large datasets like ImageNet or other standardized datasets like Tecnick, which could provide a more comprehensive evaluation of the proposed method.
- The paper does not discuss the limitations of the proposed method, which is crucial for understanding the scope and applicability of the findings.
