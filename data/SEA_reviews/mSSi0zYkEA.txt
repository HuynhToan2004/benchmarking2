 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel approach to layer-wise learning rate initialization, where each layer is assigned an individual learning rate based on the gradient magnitude at initialization. This method adjusts the learning rate inversely proportional to the gradient magnitude to address the uneven gradient magnitude across layers, aiming to improve training performance and stability. The proposed method was tested on ImageNet-1k using ResNet-50 and ViT-S, showing modest but consistent improvements in validation accuracy. The approach is compared against other layer-wise gradient normalization techniques and is evaluated against various datasets and models. However, the paper is criticized for its limited novelty, lack of comprehensive theoretical analysis, and insufficient experimental validation on more complex or larger-scale datasets.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, with a clear presentation of ideas and results.
- The method of layer-wise learning rate adjustment is novel and effective, as shown through empirical results.
- The proposed method is simple and easy to implement, making it accessible for practical applications.
- The paper provides a detailed explanation of the algorithm and its effects on training dynamics, which aids in understanding the method's impact.
- The method is compared against other layer-wise gradient normalization techniques, showing that it performs better in some scenarios.
- The paper is self-contained, with a clear description of the method and its effects on training dynamics.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a thorough theoretical analysis of the proposed method, particularly in explaining why the proposed method works and its implications for the training process.
- The experimental results are not sufficiently comprehensive, with limited datasets and models used, and a lack of comparison with more recent or larger-scale models.
- The paper does not discuss the limitations of the proposed method, which could provide a more balanced view of its applicability and effectiveness.
- The method's effectiveness is not clearly demonstrated across different architectures or datasets, which raises questions about its generalizability.
- The paper could benefit from a more detailed discussion on how the method handles different types of layers, such as batch normalization layers, and how it affects the training dynamics of transformer models.
- The paper lacks a discussion on the implications of the method for training stability and convergence, which are crucial aspects of the proposed approach.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors provide a more detailed theoretical analysis of the proposed method, explaining why it works and its implications for the training process?
- How does the proposed method compare to other layer-wise learning rate adjustment methods, particularly in terms of performance and computational efficiency?
- Could the authors provide more comprehensive experimental results, including comparisons with more recent or larger-scale models, and different datasets?
- How does the method perform when applied to different architectures, such as transformer models or other types of neural networks?
- Can the authors discuss the limitations of the proposed method and how these might affect its applicability in practical scenarios?
- How does the method handle the training dynamics of different types of layers, such as batch normalization layers, and what are the implications for training stability and convergence?
