 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel sampling algorithm that decomposes sampling from an unnormalized density into a sequence of sampling from log-concave conditional densities, leveraging a smoothing scheme using an isotropic Gaussian kernel with a fixed noise scale. This methodology is supported by theoretical analysis, particularly focusing on the improvement in conditioning numbers and the monotonic decrease of the Hessian of the log-density. The algorithm is tested against various Langevin MCMC algorithms, demonstrating its effectiveness in sampling from complex distributions. However, concerns are raised about the practicality of the method due to its complexity and the lack of comparison with simpler or more recent sampling methods.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, clear, and easy to follow, making it accessible to a broad audience.
- It introduces a novel approach to sampling from unnormalized densities, which is a significant problem in statistical physics and generative modeling.
- The theoretical analysis provided is robust, with proofs that are sound and well-explained.
- The paper demonstrates the effectiveness of the proposed method through experimental results, showing that it outperforms other sampling methods in certain scenarios.
- The paper is well-organized, with clear sections that are easy to follow, and includes a detailed analysis of the properties of the log-density.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed discussion on the practical implications of the method, particularly how it can be applied in real-world scenarios.
- There is a significant gap between the theoretical results and the practical implementation of the method, which could limit its applicability.
- The paper does not adequately compare the proposed method with simpler or more recent sampling methods, such as those based on diffusion models or other MCMC algorithms.
- The experiments conducted are limited in scope, and the paper does not provide a comprehensive evaluation of the method's performance across different types of distributions.
- The paper does not address the scalability of the method with respect to the dimensionality of the problem, which could limit its applicability in high-dimensional settings.
- There are several minor typographical and formatting errors that need to be corrected for clarity and professionalism.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors provide more details on how the proposed method can be practically applied in real-world scenarios, particularly in high-dimensional settings?
- How does the method perform in terms of computational efficiency, and what are the implications for scalability?
- Could the authors provide a more detailed comparison with other sampling methods, including recent diffusion models and other MCMC algorithms?
- What are the implications of the theoretical results for the practical implementation of the method, and how do these results translate into practical benefits?
- How does the method handle different types of distributions, particularly those that are not log-concave?
- Could the authors clarify the experimental setup and results, particularly in terms of the choice of hyperparameters and the comparison with other methods?
