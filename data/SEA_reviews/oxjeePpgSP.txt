 **Summary:**
The paper investigates backdoor attacks in contrastive learning (CL), focusing on the limitations of existing attacks that use fixed triggers. It proposes a novel bi-level optimization approach, Bi-Level Trigger Optimization (BLTO), to address these issues by optimizing the trigger to maintain a high similarity between the triggered and target-class data in the embedding space of CL. The method is designed to evade state-of-the-art defenses and achieves high attack success rates with a low poisoning rate. Extensive experiments are conducted to demonstrate the effectiveness and transferability of the proposed attack across various scenarios. The paper also discusses the challenges in designing effective triggers for CL and provides a detailed analysis of the attack's performance under different conditions.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, making it easy to follow and understand, with clear explanations and detailed experiments.
- The proposed bi-level optimization approach is novel and effective, as demonstrated by the high attack success rate and low poisoning rate achieved.
- The paper identifies the weaknesses of existing backdoor attacks on CL and proposes a new trigger design that can maintain a high similarity between the triggered and target-class data in the embedding space of CL.
- Extensive experiments are conducted to verify the effectiveness and transferability of the proposed backdoor attack, providing thorough analyses to justify the attack's ability to survive special CL mechanisms.
- The paper discusses the limitations of the proposed method and provides a detailed analysis of the attack's performance under different conditions, including the impact of data augmentation and uniformity.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a discussion on the limitations of the proposed method, which could help in understanding the scenarios where the attack might not be effective.
- The evaluation of the attack's performance against state-of-the-art defenses is not comprehensive, focusing only on a few defenses and not comparing the attack's effectiveness across different defense mechanisms.
- The paper does not discuss the potential negative societal impacts of the proposed attack, which could be a significant concern.
- The trigger design, while innovative, might not be practical for real-world applications due to its complexity and reliance on specific CL mechanisms.
- The paper does not provide sufficient evidence or analysis to support the claim that the proposed attack can evade existing state-of-the-art defenses.
- The method's reliance on the uniformity effect of CL for its effectiveness raises concerns about its robustness to changes in CL methods or data augmentation strategies.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors provide more details on the trigger design and its implementation, particularly how it is optimized and how it can be adapted to different CL frameworks?
- How does the proposed attack perform against a broader range of state-of-the-art defenses, and what are the specific defense mechanisms that the attack can evade?
- Can the authors discuss the potential negative societal impacts of the proposed attack and suggest ways to mitigate these risks?
- In the context of the paper's claims about the attack's effectiveness against state-of-the-art defenses, can the authors provide more evidence or analysis to support these claims?
- How does the proposed attack perform under different data augmentation strategies, and what are the implications of these variations on the attack's effectiveness?
- Can the authors provide more details on the experimental setup and the conditions under which the attack is tested, particularly the conditions under which the attack is considered successful?
