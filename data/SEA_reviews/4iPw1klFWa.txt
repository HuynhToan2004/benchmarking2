 **Summary:**
The paper introduces scalable neural network kernels (SNNKs) as an alternative to regular feedforward layers, aiming to approximate their functionality with computational advantages. SNNKs are designed to disentangle inputs from parameters, which are then connected via a dot-product kernel. The authors propose a bundling process to apply SNNKs across multiple layers, potentially compressing neural network architectures. They also introduce a mechanism of universal random features (URFs) to construct SNNKs and demonstrate their effectiveness in various applications, including point-wise kernel estimation and Transformer fine-tuning. The paper is supported by theoretical analysis and empirical evaluations, showcasing the potential of SNNKs in reducing computational complexity and improving model performance.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel concept of scalable neural network kernels (SNNKs) as an alternative to regular feedforward layers, which is a significant contribution to the field.
- SNNKs are designed to disentangle inputs from parameters, which is a novel approach that could potentially lead to more efficient and effective neural network architectures.
- The paper provides a comprehensive theoretical analysis and empirical evaluations, demonstrating the potential of SNNKs in reducing computational complexity and improving model performance.
- The concept of SNNKs is well-motivated, and the paper is well-written, making it easy to follow and understand.
- The paper is original in its approach, introducing a new methodology for designing neural network layers, which could be a significant step forward in the field.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed comparison with other compression techniques such as pruning, distillation, and quantization, which could help in understanding the advantages and disadvantages of SNNKs more clearly.
- The paper does not provide a detailed discussion on the limitations of SNNKs, which could help in understanding the practical challenges and potential drawbacks of the proposed method.
- The paper could benefit from a more detailed explanation of the SNNK-inspired adapter layers and their impact on model performance and training efficiency.
- The paper does not discuss the computational complexity of the SNNKs in terms of the number of parameters and the time required for training and inference.
- The paper does not compare SNNKs with other methods that use random features, such as the NTK, which could help in understanding the advantages and disadvantages of SNNKs more clearly.
- The paper does not provide a detailed analysis of the scalability of SNNKs with the size of the dataset, which is a critical factor in practical applications.

**Questions:**
- How does the SNNK method compare with other compression techniques such as pruning, distillation, and quantization in terms of effectiveness and efficiency?
- What are the potential limitations of SNNKs, and how do these limitations impact the practical applicability of the method?
- Could you provide more details on the SNNK-inspired adapter layers, particularly how they affect model performance and training efficiency?
- How does the computational complexity of SNNKs scale with the size of the dataset, and what are the implications of this for practical applications?
- Could you provide more details on the experimental setup, particularly how the random features are chosen and how they affect the performance of the SNNKs?
- How does the SNNK method compare with other methods that use random features, such as the NTK, in terms of effectiveness and efficiency?
