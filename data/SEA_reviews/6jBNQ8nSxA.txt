 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel security patch detection system, LLMDA, which utilizes Large Language Models (LLMs) to analyze patches and align various modalities, enhancing detection precision. The system leverages a PT-former to merge patches with code and a probabilistic batch contrastive learning mechanism to augment capabilities. The methodology includes data augmentation using LLMs and a stochastic batch contrastive learning mechanism. The paper presents an innovative approach by integrating LLMs with security patch detection, aiming to improve detection accuracy and efficiency. However, concerns about the novelty of the approach and the clarity of presentation have been raised.

**Strengths:**
- The paper presents a novel approach to security patch detection using Large Language Models (LLMs), which is a new direction in this field.
- The use of LLMs for data augmentation and feature combination is a significant innovation, enhancing the detection precision.
- The paper is well-written and easy to follow, making it accessible to a broad audience.
- The methodology is sound, and the results are promising, showing a significant improvement in detection precision compared to state-of-the-art techniques.
- The paper is well-structured, with clear motivation and a detailed explanation of the proposed method.
- The introduction of a probabilistic batch contrastive learning mechanism to augment capabilities is a notable contribution to the field.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a clear explanation of how the LLM is used for data augmentation, specifically how it aligns patches with code and how it generates labels.
- The novelty of the approach is questioned as it primarily involves the use of LLMs, which have been extensively used in similar tasks.
- The paper does not sufficiently differentiate its method from existing techniques, such as GraphSPD, which also uses LLMs.
- The methodology section is not clearly written, with unclear definitions and formulations, which could hinder understanding.
- The paper does not adequately address the issue of false positives, which is crucial in security patch detection.
- The evaluation metrics used are not standard in the field, which could limit the generalizability of the results.
- The paper does not discuss the limitations of the proposed method, which is essential for a complete evaluation.
- The paper does not include code for reproducibility, which is crucial for validation and further research.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors clarify how the LLM is used for data augmentation, specifically how it aligns patches with code and generates labels?
- How does the proposed method differentiate from existing techniques, particularly in terms of novelty and effectiveness?
- Could the authors provide more details on the methodology, especially in the methodology section, to enhance clarity and understanding?
- How does the paper address the issue of false positives in security patch detection?
- Why were the specific evaluation metrics chosen, and how do they compare to standard metrics in the field?
- Can the authors discuss the limitations of their proposed method and how these might impact its practical application?
- Would it be possible to include code for reproducibility and to facilitate further research and validation of the results?
