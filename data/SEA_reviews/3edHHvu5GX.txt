 **Summary:**
The paper addresses the challenge of Continual Scene Graph Generation (CSEGG), focusing on how AI systems can adapt to new objects and relationships in dynamic visual environments. It introduces a benchmark with three learning regimes to evaluate the performance of models in handling incremental relationships, scene increments, and relationship generalization. The authors propose a "Replays via Analysis by Synthesis" (RAS) strategy to enhance the efficiency of scene graph generation while maintaining privacy. The paper is well-structured, providing a comprehensive benchmark and baseline comparisons, along with an ablation study. However, concerns were raised about the novelty of the approach, the clarity of the methodology, and the depth of the experimental results.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-organized, easy to follow, and clearly presents the problem of continual learning in scene graph generation (SGG), which is a significant and relevant topic.
- The introduction of a benchmark with three learning regimes provides a comprehensive evaluation framework for continual learning in SGG, which is crucial for advancing research in this area.
- The paper proposes a "Replays via Analysis by Synthesis" (RAS) strategy, which is novel and innovative, potentially enhancing the efficiency of scene graph generation while preserving privacy.
- The authors conduct an ablation study to analyze the effectiveness of the RAS strategy, providing valuable insights into the method's components and their contributions.
- The paper is well-written, making it accessible and understandable, which is essential for advancing research and disseminating knowledge in the field.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a clear motivation and explanation for the necessity of continual learning in SGG, which could limit the understanding and relevance of the research for some readers.
- The methodology and its implementation details are not clearly explained, making it difficult for readers to fully grasp the approach and replicate the results.
- The paper does not sufficiently differentiate its contributions from existing works, particularly in terms of novelty and methodological advancements.
- The experimental results and their interpretation are not convincing, with some results showing inconsistencies or insufficient detail, which could undermine the credibility of the findings.
- The paper does not adequately address potential negative societal impacts of the proposed methods, such as privacy concerns and data biases, which are crucial for ensuring the ethical and responsible use of the technology.

**Questions:**
- Could you provide more details on the methodology, particularly how the RAS strategy is implemented and how it impacts the scene graph generation process?
- How does the RAS strategy compare to other existing methods in terms of efficiency and effectiveness?
- Can you clarify the role and impact of the "Replays via Analysis by Synthesis" (RAS) strategy in the context of continual learning in SGG?
- How does the paper address the potential negative societal impacts of the proposed methods, such as privacy concerns and data biases?
- Could you provide more detailed experimental results, including standard deviations and additional metrics, to better support the claims made in the paper?
