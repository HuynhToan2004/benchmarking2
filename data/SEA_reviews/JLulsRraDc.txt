 **Summary:**
The paper introduces RaFFM, a framework designed to adapt foundation models (FMs) for deployment in federated learning (FL) environments, addressing resource constraints through model compression and subnetwork extraction. RaFFM employs salient parameter prioritization and dynamic scaling techniques to optimize resource utilization, enhancing performance on heterogeneous devices. The framework is evaluated across various benchmarks, demonstrating its effectiveness in reducing resource consumption and improving model efficiency. However, the paper faces criticism for its limited novelty, unclear technical contributions, and insufficient experimental validation, particularly in terms of the effectiveness of subnetwork extraction and the generalizability of the results across different datasets and models.

**Strengths:**
- The paper addresses a significant and relevant problem in the field of federated learning (FL) by focusing on the efficient deployment of foundation models (FMs) in resource-constrained environments.
- The methodology is well-explained, and the paper is well-written, making it accessible and easy to understand.
- The proposed method, RaFFM, is novel in its approach to salient parameter prioritization and subnetwork extraction, which are critical for adapting FMs to FL settings.
- The paper provides a comprehensive evaluation across various datasets and models, demonstrating the effectiveness of RaFFM in reducing resource consumption and improving model efficiency.
- The paper addresses the significant challenge of deploying large foundation models in federated learning environments, which is a crucial issue in practical applications.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks clarity in explaining the technical contributions of the proposed method, particularly in how it differs from existing methods and why it is effective.
- The experimental results are not convincingly presented, with some figures and tables not clearly demonstrating the effectiveness of the proposed method.
- There is a lack of detailed analysis on the efficiency of subnetwork extraction and the impact of different model sizes on performance.
- The paper does not adequately address the limitations of the proposed method, such as the potential for increased training costs and the generalizability of the results across different datasets and models.
- The paper does not sufficiently compare the proposed method with existing state-of-the-art methods, which could help in understanding its relative effectiveness.
- The paper lacks a detailed discussion on the potential societal impacts of the proposed method, which is crucial for understanding the broader implications of the research.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors clarify the technical contributions of the proposed method and how it differs from existing methods?
- How does the proposed method handle the trade-off between model size and performance, especially in federated learning environments where resources are limited?
- Could the authors provide more detailed analysis on the efficiency of subnetwork extraction and its impact on model performance?
- How does the proposed method compare with existing state-of-the-art methods in terms of resource utilization and model efficiency?
- What are the potential societal impacts of the proposed method, and how do the authors address these in their research?
- Can the authors provide more detailed experimental results, especially in terms of the effectiveness of the proposed method across different datasets and models?
