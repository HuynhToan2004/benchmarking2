 **Summary:**
The paper investigates the application of Graph Neural Networks (GNNs) in flood forecasting using the LamaH-CE dataset, which includes river discharge and meteorological measurements. The study explores different adjacency definitions to leverage river network topology information, comparing the performance of GNNs with other models like MLPs and traditional LSTM networks. Despite the potential of GNNs to capture spatial dependencies, the results show that GNNs do not outperform other models, suggesting that the model's architecture might not effectively utilize the river network topology. The paper also discusses the limitations of GNNs in predicting sudden discharge spikes and the need for more data-rich regions for better model performance.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written and easy to follow, with a clear and well-organized structure.
- The authors explore the impact of different adjacency definitions on flood forecasting performance, providing a thorough analysis of the model's behavior.
- The paper includes a detailed analysis of the model's performance on the worst-performing gauge, which helps in understanding the model's limitations.
- The experimental setup is comprehensive, covering various aspects of the model's performance, and the results are presented in a clear and concise manner.
- The paper addresses the important and timely issue of flood forecasting, which is crucial for mitigating the impacts of floods on human life and property.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper does not adequately address the potential benefits of using GNNs in flood forecasting, as the results do not convincingly demonstrate the superiority of GNNs over other models like MLPs or LSTM networks.
- The paper lacks a detailed comparison with other state-of-the-art models, which could help in understanding the model's performance in a broader context.
- The paper's focus on a single dataset (LamaH-CE) limits the generalizability of the findings, as the results might not apply to other datasets or regions.
- There is a lack of discussion on the potential reasons why GNNs do not perform well in flood forecasting, which could be crucial for future research directions.
- The paper could benefit from a more comprehensive literature review and a clearer articulation of its contributions to the field.

**Questions:**

- How does the model perform on other datasets, and what are the implications of using different datasets for flood forecasting?
- Could the authors elaborate on the potential reasons why GNNs do not perform well in flood forecasting, and what could be done to address these issues?
- What are the implications of using different datasets for flood forecasting, and how do these affect the model's performance and generalizability?
- Could the authors discuss the potential of using other types of models, such as transformers or other types of GNNs, for flood forecasting, and what are the advantages and disadvantages of these models compared to the ones used in the study?
