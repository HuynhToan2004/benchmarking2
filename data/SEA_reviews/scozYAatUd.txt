 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel multiscale wavelet attention mechanism (MWA) for vision transformers (ViTs), leveraging wavelet neural operators to address the limitations of global convolution in AFNO. MWA transforms input images into wavelet space, performs convolutions, and then reconverts the output back to the original space. This method aims to improve performance on small-scale datasets like CIFAR and TinyImageNet, demonstrating superior accuracy over existing Fourier-based attention mechanisms. However, the paper has been critiqued for its lack of comprehensive ablation studies, insufficient justification for the use of wavelet transforms, and limited experimental validation on larger datasets.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, easy to follow, and provides a clear explanation of the proposed method.
- The use of wavelet transforms for multiscale attention in vision transformers is novel and interesting, potentially offering improvements over existing methods.
- The paper includes a thorough comparison of the proposed method with other attention mechanisms, demonstrating its effectiveness through experiments.
- The wavelet attention mechanism is well-motivated and shows potential for improving the performance of ViTs, especially on small-scale datasets.
- The paper is well-organized, making it easy to understand the proposed method and its implementation.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks comprehensive ablation studies, particularly on the impact of different wavelet transforms and the number of convolution layers in the skip connections.
- The justification for using wavelet transforms over other attention mechanisms is insufficient, and the paper does not convincingly demonstrate that wavelet transforms are superior for learning multiscale patterns in images.
- The experiments are limited to small-scale datasets, and the performance on larger datasets such as ImageNet is not reported, which could affect the generalizability of the results.
- The paper does not discuss the limitations of the proposed method, which could include computational complexity, scalability, or practical applicability.
- The comparison with other attention mechanisms like AFNO and GFN is not fair due to differences in model configurations, which could affect the validity of the results.
- The paper could benefit from additional experiments to validate the effectiveness of the proposed method on larger datasets and more complex tasks.

**Questions:**
- Could you provide more detailed ablation studies on the impact of different wavelet transforms and the number of convolution layers in the skip connections?
- How does the proposed method perform on larger datasets like ImageNet, and what are the implications for its scalability and performance?
- Can you clarify the comparison with other attention mechanisms like AFNO and GFN, particularly in terms of model configurations and experimental settings?
- How does the proposed method handle different input resolutions, and what are the implications for its applicability in real-world scenarios?
- Could you elaborate on the choice of wavelet transforms and their impact on the performance of the attention mechanism?
- What are the computational complexities of the proposed method compared to other attention mechanisms, and how does this affect its practical deployment?
