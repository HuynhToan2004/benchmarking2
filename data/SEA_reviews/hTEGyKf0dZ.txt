 **Summary:**
The paper investigates the risks associated with fine-tuning large language models (LLMs) by red teaming GPT-3.5 and Llama-2. It demonstrates that even with a few adversarial examples, LLMs can be compromised, leading to safety issues. The study also explores how fine-tuning with seemingly benign datasets can inadvertently degrade safety alignment. The authors propose several mitigation strategies to address these risks, including a comprehensive evaluation framework and policy-oriented safety benchmarks. The paper is well-written, clear, and presents a significant contribution to the field by highlighting the risks of fine-tuning LLMs and proposing potential solutions.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, clear, and easy to follow, making complex concepts accessible to a broad audience.
- The research addresses a significant and timely issue, focusing on the risks associated with fine-tuning large language models (LLMs) and the potential for these models to be compromised.
- The paper provides a comprehensive evaluation framework, including a policy-oriented safety benchmark and a GPT-4 Judge, which can be useful for evaluating the safety of LLMs.
- The study is original and contributes to the ongoing discussion on the safety and security of LLMs, particularly in the context of fine-tuning.
- The paper is well-organized, with clear figures and tables that aid in understanding the results and findings.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed discussion on the specific risks of fine-tuning LLMs, which could be more clearly articulated to enhance the reader's understanding of the threats.
- The paper could benefit from a more thorough analysis of the risks associated with fine-tuning, particularly in terms of the types of risks and their implications.
- The evaluation framework, while comprehensive, is not sufficiently detailed in some areas, particularly in the methodology used for evaluating the effectiveness of the proposed mitigation strategies.
- The paper does not sufficiently address the limitations of the study, which could include the generalizability of the findings to other types of LLMs or the effectiveness of the proposed mitigation strategies in different contexts.
- The paper could benefit from a more detailed discussion on the policy implications of the findings, particularly in terms of how the results might influence regulatory frameworks or industry standards.

**Questions:**
- Could you provide more details on the specific risks of fine-tuning LLMs and how these risks manifest in practice?
- How do the proposed mitigation strategies address these risks, and what are the limitations or potential drawbacks of these strategies?
- In terms of the evaluation framework, could you provide more details on the methodology used for evaluating the effectiveness of the proposed mitigation strategies?
- How do the findings of this study relate to ongoing discussions on the safety and security of LLMs, and what are the implications for future research in this area?
- Could you elaborate on the policy implications of the findings, particularly in terms of how the results might influence regulatory frameworks or industry standards?
