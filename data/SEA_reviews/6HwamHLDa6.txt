 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel Multi-In-Single-Out (MISO) network for video frame interpolation (VFI) that does not rely on optical flow estimation. Instead, it uses a perceptual loss to enhance the quality of interpolated frames. The proposed method, MISO-VFI, is tested on benchmarks such as Vimeo90K, Middlebury, and UCF101, demonstrating state-of-the-art performance. The MISO network is designed to accept multiple frames as input and generate a single frame as output, which is a departure from traditional VFI methods that typically use two frames. The paper also introduces a motion perceptual loss to better capture spatio-temporal correlations within video frames. Despite its innovative approach, the paper has been critiqued for its limited novelty, computational complexity, and lack of comparative analysis with existing methods.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel approach to video frame interpolation (VFI) by using a Multi-In-Single-Out (MISO) network, which does not rely on optical flow estimation.
- The MISO-VFI method achieves state-of-the-art results on benchmark datasets such as Vimeo90K, Middlebury, and UCF101, demonstrating its effectiveness.
- The introduction of a motion perceptual loss is a significant contribution, enhancing the capture of spatio-temporal correlations within video frames.
- The paper is well-written and easy to follow, making it accessible to readers.
- The experimental results are impressive, showing the superiority of the proposed method over existing approaches.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a clear motivation for using the MISO structure, which is not well-explained or justified.
- The novelty of the method is limited, as similar approaches have been previously explored in other works, such as the FLAVR paper.
- The computational complexity of the proposed method is high, which might limit its practical application, especially in real-time scenarios.
- The paper does not provide sufficient comparative analysis with existing methods, which is necessary to demonstrate the superiority of the proposed approach.
- The paper does not adequately address the limitations of the proposed method, such as its inability to handle non-linear motion effectively.
- The paper does not provide sufficient experimental evidence to support the claims made about the method's effectiveness in handling occlusions and non-linear motion.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors provide a more detailed explanation of the motivation behind using the MISO structure in the context of VFI?
- How does the proposed method compare to existing methods in terms of computational efficiency and practical application?
- Could the authors provide more experimental evidence to support the claims made about the method's effectiveness in handling occlusions and non-linear motion?
- How does the proposed method handle the trade-off between computational complexity and the accuracy of interpolated frames?
- Can the authors clarify the discrepancies in the experimental results, particularly the low PSNR values reported in the paper?
- How does the proposed method handle the issue of motion compensation in VFI, and what are the implications of this for the accuracy of interpolated frames?
