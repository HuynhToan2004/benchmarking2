 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel approach to skill learning in reinforcement learning (RL) by combining goal-conditioned RL with empowerment learning. This approach, termed Hierarchical Empowerment, aims to optimize the mutual information between skills and states by learning the distribution of achievable goals. The method involves a hierarchical architecture that optimizes empowerment over both short and long time horizons. The approach is evaluated in simulated environments, demonstrating superior performance compared to baseline methods. Despite its theoretical and empirical advancements, the paper is criticized for its limited experimental scope, lack of comparisons with state-of-the-art methods, and insufficient discussion on the scalability and generalizability of the proposed method.

**Strengths:**
- The paper presents a novel approach by combining empowerment learning with goal-conditioned RL, which is a significant advancement in the field of skill learning.
- The paper is well-written, with clear explanations of the concepts and methods used, making it accessible to a broad audience.
- The proposed method is evaluated in both simple and more complex environments, demonstrating its effectiveness and scalability.
- The use of a hierarchical architecture and a novel variational lower bound on mutual information are highlighted as innovative contributions to the field.
- The paper provides a comprehensive literature review and discusses the limitations of existing methods, which helps in positioning the proposed work within the current research landscape.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed comparison with state-of-the-art methods, particularly in terms of computational efficiency and scalability.
- The experimental setup is limited to simple environments, which may not fully demonstrate the generalizability of the proposed method to more complex scenarios.
- There is a noticeable absence of a discussion on the limitations of the proposed method, which is crucial for understanding its practical applicability and potential drawbacks.
- The paper does not provide a detailed analysis of the time complexity or computational requirements of the proposed method, which is essential for assessing its scalability.
- The paper does not sufficiently discuss the practical implications of the method, such as its applicability to real-world scenarios and the potential impact of domain-specific variations.
