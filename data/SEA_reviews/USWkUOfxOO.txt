 **Summary:**
The paper introduces PseudoCal, a novel post-hoc calibration framework aimed at addressing the predictive uncertainty calibration in unsupervised domain adaptation (UDA). PseudoCal leverages a mixup strategy during the inference stage to generate pseudo-target data, which is then used to calibrate the model using temperature scaling. This approach is designed to improve the calibration of models on target data, even in the absence of labeled data. The paper provides extensive empirical evaluations across various UDA scenarios, demonstrating the superior performance of PseudoCal over alternative calibration methods. However, concerns are raised regarding the novelty of the method, the lack of theoretical justification, and the potential for the proposed method to be applied to other calibration methods.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel approach to calibrating predictive uncertainty in unsupervised domain adaptation (UDA) by proposing PseudoCal, a post-hoc calibration framework that utilizes mixup and cluster assumption to generate pseudo-target data, which is then used for supervised calibration.
- The paper is well-written, making it easy to follow, and the experimental results are convincing, showing that PseudoCal outperforms alternative calibration methods in various UDA scenarios.
- The proposed method is simple, effective, and does not require model training or hyperparameter tuning, making it accessible and practical for real-world applications.
- The paper provides a comprehensive evaluation of PseudoCal, involving 5 calibration baselines and 10 UDA methods across 5 UDA scenarios, demonstrating its effectiveness and versatility.
- The paper is well-organized, with a clear structure and logical presentation that effectively communicates the main ideas and contributions of the research.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a clear explanation of how the cluster assumption is used to ensure sample-level correspondence between pseudo and real target data, which is crucial for the effectiveness of the proposed method.
- The novelty of the method is questionable, as it primarily combines existing techniques such as mixup and temperature scaling without providing significant new insights or theoretical justifications.
- The paper does not address the issue of pseudo-label noise in UDA, which could affect the performance of the proposed method.
- There is a lack of comparison with other calibration methods such as MCD and Ensemble, which could provide a more comprehensive evaluation of the proposed method.
- The paper does not provide a detailed analysis of the limitations of the proposed method, which could help in understanding its applicability and effectiveness in different scenarios.
- The paper lacks a thorough discussion on the potential negative societal impacts of the proposed method, which is an important consideration for ethical and responsible AI development.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors provide a detailed explanation of how the cluster assumption is used to ensure sample-level correspondence between pseudo and real target data?
- How does the proposed method handle the issue of pseudo-label noise in UDA?
- Could the authors provide more details on the limitations of the proposed method and how it might perform in different scenarios?
- How does the proposed method compare with other calibration methods such as MCD and Ensemble, especially in terms of performance and computational efficiency?
- Could the authors provide a more detailed comparison with other related works, especially those that address similar issues in UDA?
- How does the proposed method ensure the robustness of the calibration results, especially in scenarios where the model's performance on the target domain is low?
