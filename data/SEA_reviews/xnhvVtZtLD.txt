 **Summary:**
The paper addresses the local fairness problem in machine learning, focusing on ensuring fairness within subgroups of a population, which are unknown at training time. The authors propose a novel approach called ROAD (Robust Optimization for Adversarial Debiasing), which leverages the Distributionally Robust Optimization (DRO) framework to optimize fairness across different subgroups. ROAD aims to minimize the adversary's ability to reconstruct sensitive attributes from the predictions, thereby promoting fairness in unseen subgroups. The paper also introduces a novel objective function and a reweighting strategy to prioritize inputs likely to be locally unfair. Empirical evaluations are conducted on various datasets, demonstrating the effectiveness of the proposed method.

**Strengths:**
- The paper addresses a timely and important problem in the field of fair machine learning, focusing on local fairness which is often overlooked in traditional fairness metrics.
- The introduction of the ROAD framework, which integrates adversarial learning and distributionally robust optimization, is a novel and interesting approach to solving the local fairness problem.
- The paper is well-written, clear, and easy to follow, making it accessible to a broad audience.
- The experimental results are comprehensive and demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed method, showing its ability to improve fairness without compromising accuracy.
- The paper provides a clear motivation for its work, highlighting the limitations of traditional group fairness metrics and the need for a more nuanced approach to fairness.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a clear definition of the subgroups and their selection, which is crucial for the application of the proposed method. This ambiguity could lead to inconsistencies in the results.
- The experimental setup and the choice of datasets and baselines are not well-justified, which could affect the validity and reliability of the results.
- The paper does not provide a theoretical guarantee for the proposed method, which is a significant limitation given the importance of the problem.
- The paper does not discuss the limitations of the proposed method, which could help in understanding its applicability and potential drawbacks.
- There is a lack of clarity in the formulation of the problem and the role of the adversary, which could confuse readers and undermine the credibility of the proposed solution.
- The paper does not sufficiently compare its results with existing methods, which could provide a more robust evaluation of the proposed method's effectiveness.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors clarify the definition and selection of subgroups, and discuss how this affects the application of the proposed method?
- How does the proposed method ensure that the adversary focuses on the right subpopulations, and what are the implications of this focus on the overall fairness of the model?
- Can the authors provide a theoretical guarantee for the proposed method, and discuss any assumptions or limitations that might affect its applicability?
- How does the proposed method compare with existing methods in terms of fairness, accuracy, and computational efficiency?
- Could the authors discuss the limitations of their approach and suggest potential directions for future research?
