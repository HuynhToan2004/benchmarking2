 **Summary:**
The paper investigates the uncertainty quantification in evaluating face recognition systems, focusing on the Receiver Operating Characteristic (ROC) curves. It introduces a novel bootstrapping method to estimate the uncertainty of ROC curves, particularly addressing the issue of false acceptance and rejection rates. The paper also proposes a recentering technique to improve the accuracy of ROC curve estimation. Theoretical analyses are provided to support the method's consistency and validity, alongside empirical results demonstrating its practical applicability. The methodology is applied to various fairness metrics, showcasing its utility in quantifying uncertainty in fairness evaluations.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, clear, and easy to follow, with a logical structure that aids in understanding complex statistical concepts.
- The proposed methodology is simple, effective, and applicable to various scenarios, including fairness metrics, which is a significant contribution to the field.
- The paper provides a comprehensive theoretical analysis of the proposed method, including proofs of consistency and validity, which adds credibility to the proposed approach.
- Empirical results demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed method, showing that it can provide reliable estimates of uncertainty in ROC curves and fairness metrics.
