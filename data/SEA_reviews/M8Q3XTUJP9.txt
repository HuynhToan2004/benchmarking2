 **Summary:**
The paper explores the features learned by overparameterized and underparameterized neural networks, specifically focusing on how the expressivity of features varies between these models. The authors propose a novel method, Feature Span Error (FSE), to measure the expressivity of features in different models. They demonstrate that underparameterized networks, even when concatenated, cannot fully capture the expressivity of overparameterized networks. The paper also discusses the implications of these findings for the generalization and performance of neural networks. Despite the innovative approach and the clarity of the writing, the paper is criticized for its limited scope in terms of the models and datasets used, and the lack of a deeper theoretical analysis.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel and interesting method, Feature Span Error (FSE), which is used to quantify the expressivity of features in neural networks.
- The findings on the distinctiveness of features learned by overparameterized networks are intriguing and add to the understanding of neural network behavior.
- The paper is well-written, making the content accessible and easy to follow, with clear definitions and a logical structure that aids comprehension.
- The experimental setup is robust, with multiple experiments conducted to validate the claims, and the results are convincingly presented.
- The paper addresses a significant and relevant topic in the field of deep learning, focusing on the expressivity of features in neural networks, which is crucial for understanding the behavior and performance of models.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a deeper theoretical analysis, which could provide a more comprehensive understanding of the phenomena observed.
- The scope of the study is limited, focusing on specific models (VGG-16, ResNet-18, and a 4-layer transformer) and datasets (CIFAR-10 and MNLI), which might not generalize well to other models or tasks.
- The paper does not adequately address the issue of overfitting in overparameterized networks, which could affect the reliability of the results.
- There is a noticeable absence of related work discussion, which could help contextualize the findings and highlight the novelty of the contributions.
- The paper's experimental results, while interesting, are somewhat limited in scope and do not convincingly demonstrate the generalizability of the findings across different models and datasets.
- The presentation of some results, such as the correlation between features and labels, is confusing and could benefit from clearer visualization or explanation.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors provide a more detailed theoretical analysis to support the empirical findings?
- How do the results of the paper apply to other models and datasets beyond those used in the study?
- Is there a way to verify that the models are not overfitting during training?
- Could the authors elaborate on the role of different initialization methods in the learning of features by underparameterized networks?
- How does the paper's findings relate to or differ from existing works on the expressivity of neural network features?
- Can the authors clarify the role and significance of the "residuals" mentioned in the paper, and how they contribute to the overall understanding of feature learning in neural networks?
