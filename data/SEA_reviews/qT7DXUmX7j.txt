 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel framework called Nature-Powered Graph Learning (NP-GL), which extends the capabilities of Ising Graph Learning (IGL) to handle real-valued problems. NP-GL leverages a new Hamiltonian and training algorithms to enhance the expressivity and applicability of IGL, allowing it to process real-valued data effectively. This extension is crucial as most existing IGL methods are limited to binary data, which restricts their practical use. The framework is evaluated against Graph Neural Networks (GNNs) and other spatial-temporal GNNs, demonstrating significant improvements in speed and energy efficiency. However, the paper's novelty and the practical implications of its theoretical contributions are questioned, as the framework heavily relies on existing techniques and lacks comprehensive comparisons with other state-of-the-art methods.

**Strengths:**
- The paper presents a novel framework called NP-GL, which extends the capabilities of Ising Graph Learning (IGL) to handle real-valued problems, enhancing the applicability of IGL in real-world scenarios.
- The proposed Hamiltonian and training algorithms are well-designed, demonstrating a high level of engineering and theoretical insight.
- The paper is well-written, making it easy to follow, and the experimental results are robust, showing significant improvements over baseline methods in terms of speed and energy efficiency.
- The paper provides a comprehensive evaluation of the proposed framework, comparing it against a wide range of baselines and demonstrating its effectiveness in various real-world applications.
- The potential of the proposed method is highlighted by its application in nature-based computers, which could significantly reduce energy consumption and latency compared to traditional computing methods.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper's main theoretical contribution, particularly the analysis of the binary limitation of Ising Graph Learning, is not convincingly novel or groundbreaking. The theoretical insights and the proposed solutions are seen as incremental extensions of existing methods.
- The practical implications of the theoretical contributions are not clearly demonstrated, and the paper lacks a detailed discussion on the limitations and potential negative societal impacts of the proposed method.
- The experimental evaluation could be improved by including more diverse benchmarks and comparing the proposed method with a broader range of state-of-the-art methods.
- The paper does not sufficiently discuss the limitations of the proposed method, such as its applicability to different types of graph learning problems and the potential impact of hyperparameter tuning on its performance.
- There is a lack of clarity and detail in the presentation of the experimental results and the methodology used, which could hinder the reproducibility and understanding of the results.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors clarify the novelty and practical implications of the theoretical contributions, particularly in relation to the binary limitation of Ising Graph Learning?
- How does the proposed method perform in terms of accuracy compared to other methods, especially in real-world applications where energy efficiency is crucial?
- What are the specific advantages of using NP-GL over other methods in terms of energy efficiency and computational speed?
- How does the proposed method handle the issue of energy consumption reduction, and what are the implications of this reduction in practical applications?
- Can the authors provide more detailed comparisons with other state-of-the-art methods, especially in terms of energy efficiency and computational speed?
- How does the proposed method handle the issue of hyperparameter tuning, and what are the potential impacts of different hyperparameters on the performance of the method?
