 **Summary:**
The paper introduces SCALE, a collaborative framework that integrates Specialized Translation Models (STMs) with Large Language Models (LLMs) to enhance translation capabilities. SCALE leverages STMs to provide in-context demonstrations, refining and pivoting LLMs, thereby mitigating biases and enhancing translation quality. The framework demonstrates superior performance in low-resource settings, showing significant improvements over few-shot LLMs and specialized models. The paper is well-structured, providing comprehensive experimental results and detailed analyses, although it lacks clarity in some sections and could benefit from more detailed comparisons and further justification of its methodological choices.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel framework, SCALE, which effectively combines the strengths of Large Language Models (LLMs) and compact Specialized Translation Models (STMs) through in-context learning.
- SCALE demonstrates superiority in many scenarios including low-resource settings, multilingual translation, and model continual learning settings.
- The authors provide comprehensive experimental results and detailed analyses, which are well-presented and contribute to the understanding of the synergistic effects between LLMs and more specialized translation models.
- The paper is well-structured and easy to follow, with clear explanations of the SCALE framework and its components.
- The methodology is straightforward and innovative, utilizing the LLM's most enigmatic capability: in-context learning, to mitigate biases and enhance translation quality.
- The paper demonstrates significant improvements in translation quality over existing models, as shown in the experimental results.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks clarity in some sections, particularly in the explanation of the SCALE framework and the role of the LLM and STM in the refinement and pivoting processes.
- There is a need for more detailed comparisons and ablations to better understand the contributions of each component of the SCALE framework.
- The paper does not sufficiently discuss the limitations of the proposed method, which could help in understanding its applicability and potential challenges.
- The paper does not provide a detailed discussion on the computational resources required for the proposed method, which could be a significant limitation for practical implementation.
- The methodology section could benefit from more detailed explanations and justifications, particularly regarding the choice of in-context demonstrations and their impact on performance.
- The paper lacks a comprehensive discussion on the generalizability of the method across different languages and domains, which could affect the practical applicability of the findings.

**Questions:**
- Could you provide more detailed comparisons and ablations to better understand the contributions of each component of the SCALE framework?
- How does the method perform when applied to high-resource languages?
- What are the specific computational resources required for the proposed method, and how does this compare to other state-of-the-art methods?
- Can you clarify the role of the LLM and STM in the refinement and pivoting processes, and how these processes are executed within the SCALE framework?
- How does the SCALE framework handle the trade-off between specialization and generalization in translation tasks?
- What are the potential limitations of the proposed method, and how might these impact its practical application?
- Can you provide more details on the methodology used in the SCALE framework, particularly regarding the selection of in-context demonstrations and their impact on performance?
