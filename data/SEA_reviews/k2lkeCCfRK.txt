 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel approach to training Generative Flow Networks (GFlowNets) by formulating the training process as a reinforcement learning (RL) problem. It proposes new reward functions and training strategies that aim to optimize the flow balance in GFlowNets, thereby improving the efficiency and effectiveness of the training process. The paper provides theoretical analysis and empirical results across various datasets, including simulated and real-world, demonstrating the practical applicability of the proposed methods. Despite the innovative approach, the paper faces criticism for its clarity, the need for a more comprehensive literature review, and the lack of significant performance improvements over existing methods.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, making it easy to follow and understand the proposed methodologies.
- The introduction of policy-based training strategies for GFlowNets is a significant contribution to the field, offering a novel perspective on training these networks.
- The theoretical analysis provided is robust and supports the proposed methodologies, enhancing the credibility of the research.
- The empirical results are comprehensive, covering various datasets and scenarios, which effectively demonstrate the practical applicability of the proposed methods.
- The paper establishes a strong connection between GFlowNets and reinforcement learning, providing a new perspective on training these networks.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a comprehensive literature review, particularly in the area of reinforcement learning, which is crucial for positioning the research within the existing body of knowledge.
- The performance improvements shown in the experiments are not significant enough to justify the added complexity and computational overhead of the proposed methods.
- The clarity of the paper needs improvement, especially in the explanation of key concepts and methodologies, which could hinder the understanding of the proposed methods.
- The paper does not sufficiently address the potential limitations and challenges associated with the proposed methods, which could impact the practical applicability and scalability of the approach.
- The experimental setup and results are not convincingly superior to existing methods, raising questions about the effectiveness of the proposed approach.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors provide a more detailed literature review, particularly focusing on the intersection of GFlowNets and reinforcement learning?
- How does the proposed method compare in terms of computational efficiency and performance with other policy-based RL methods?
- Could the authors clarify the experimental setup, particularly the choice of datasets and the conditions under which the experiments were conducted?
- How does the proposed method address the potential limitations and challenges associated with policy-based training strategies for GFlowNets?
- Could the authors provide a more detailed explanation of the theoretical results and their implications for the practical application of the proposed methods?
