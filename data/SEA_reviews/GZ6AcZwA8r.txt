 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel approach to graph metric learning through the use of Maximum Mean Discrepancy (MMD) in graph kernels. The proposed methods include unsupervised and supervised versions, leveraging MMD to measure the distance between graphs, which is then used to quantify similarity. The approach is evaluated on various datasets and tasks, showing competitive performance against state-of-the-art methods. The paper provides theoretical analysis and robustness guarantees, aiming to address the limitations of traditional graph kernels. However, concerns were raised about the novelty of the approach, the robustness of the theoretical claims, and the clarity of presentation.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel approach using MMD for graph kernels, which is a significant departure from traditional methods.
- The methodology is well-explained, with clear definitions and theoretical analysis provided.
- The proposed method shows strong empirical performance, outperforming other baselines in some cases.
- The paper is well-written, with a clear structure and comprehensive experimental validation.
- Theoretical analysis is provided, including robustness and complexity analysis, which supports the theoretical underpinnings of the proposed methods.
- The paper addresses the limitations of traditional graph kernels, particularly in handling overlapping features and relying on manually crafted features.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed discussion on the limitations of the proposed methods, particularly in terms of scalability and applicability to large or complex graphs.
- The novelty of the approach is questioned, as the use of MMD for graph kernels has been previously explored.
- The robustness analysis and theoretical claims (e.g., Theorem 1) are not convincingly robust or well-supported, with potential issues in the assumptions and the mathematical formulations.
- There are presentation issues, including unclear definitions and notations, which could confuse readers.
- The paper does not sufficiently compare the proposed methods with state-of-the-art graph neural networks (GNNs) like GCN, GAT, and GraphSAGE, which could have strengthened the evaluation.
- The computational complexity of the proposed methods, particularly the deep MMD-GK, is high, which could limit its practical application.
- The paper does not sufficiently address the societal impact of the proposed methods, which is a significant concern in the field of machine learning.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors clarify the differences between the proposed method and previous works that have used MMD for graph kernels?
- How does the proposed method compare with state-of-the-art GNNs, particularly in terms of computational efficiency and effectiveness?
- Can the authors provide a more detailed comparison of the computational complexity of their methods with other existing graph kernels?
- In the robustness analysis, how does the method handle different types of perturbations, such as node addition/deletion, edge addition/deletion, and attribute/label changes?
- Could the authors elaborate on the choice of the Gaussian kernel and its impact on the performance of the MMD-GK?
- How does the proposed method perform when the graph size is large, and what are the scalability issues that might arise?
- Can the authors provide more details on the experimental setup, such as the specific hyperparameters used and their impact on the results?
