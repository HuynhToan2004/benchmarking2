 **Summary:**
The paper introduces Iterative Training for Stable RL (ITSRL), a novel framework aimed at enhancing the stability of reinforcement learning (RL) control policies. It establishes a reference system with a stable controller and demonstrates that if the state fitting error between the reference and real systems is bounded, the real system can be guaranteed to be uniformly ultimately bounded (UUB) stable. The framework employs an iterative training process to minimize the state fitting error, thereby improving the stability of RL controllers under perturbations. The paper provides theoretical analysis and empirical evidence to support the effectiveness of the proposed method.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-structured, clearly written, and easy to follow, making complex concepts accessible.
- It introduces a novel approach to combining stability analysis with reinforcement learning (RL), which is a significant advancement in the field.
- The paper presents a robust theoretical framework that supports the proposed method, enhancing its credibility and applicability.
- The iterative training framework for RL control policy is a promising approach to ensure stability in complex systems, which is a critical aspect of control theory.
- The paper provides empirical evidence that the proposed method can improve the performance of RL controllers under perturbations, demonstrating its practical utility.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed discussion on the limitations of the proposed method, which could help in understanding its applicability and potential drawbacks.
- There is a lack of comparison with other existing methods, which could help in positioning the proposed method within the current research landscape.
- The paper could benefit from more comprehensive empirical evaluations, particularly in more complex and realistic scenarios, to further validate the effectiveness of the proposed method.
- The paper's reliance on specific assumptions, such as the boundedness of the error between the reference and real systems, may limit its applicability in certain scenarios.
- The presentation of some mathematical formulas and the overall clarity of the paper could be improved to better guide readers through the complex theoretical content.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors provide a detailed discussion on the limitations of the proposed method, including scenarios where it may not be applicable or effective?
- How does the proposed method compare with other existing methods in terms of performance and computational efficiency?
- Can the authors provide more examples or case studies to illustrate the practical applicability and effectiveness of the proposed method in real-world scenarios?
- How does the stability analysis provided by the paper relate to the actual stability of the RL controller in real-world applications?
- Can the authors clarify the mathematical notations and assumptions used in the paper to improve the clarity and accessibility of the content?
