 **Summary:**
The paper introduces ReFeed, a novel pipeline for enhancing language model outputs by integrating retrieval feedback. This method involves generating initial outputs, retrieving relevant documents using a retrieval model, and refining the outputs based on the retrieved information. The approach includes two advanced modules: diversifying initial generation outputs and ensembling initial and post-feedback outputs. The paper claims that ReFeed can improve performance on knowledge-intensive tasks and demonstrates its effectiveness through experiments on various benchmarks. However, the paper has been critiqued for its limited novelty, unclear methodological details, and insufficient experimental validation.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written and easy to follow, with clear explanations of the proposed methodology and experiments.
- The idea of using retrieval feedback to refine the outputs of language models is innovative and has shown promising results in experiments.
- The integration of retrieval feedback into the language model output refinement process is a novel approach that has not been explored extensively in prior research.
- The paper is well-organized, with clear explanations of the proposed methodology and experiments.
- The experiments demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed method, showing significant improvements in performance compared to baseline methods.
- The methodology is simple and easy to implement, making it accessible for practical applications.

**Weaknesses:**
- The novelty of the proposed method is limited as it closely resembles existing methods like RePLUG, and the main differences are not clearly articulated.
- The paper lacks a comprehensive comparison with other retrieval-augmented generation (RAG) methods, which could have strengthened the argument for the effectiveness of the proposed method.
- There is a lack of detailed explanation on how the retrieval model is trained and how it is used to retrieve documents, which is crucial for understanding the method's effectiveness.
- The paper does not provide sufficient details on the experimental setup, such as the specific models used, hyperparameters, and training details, which could affect the reproducibility and comparability of the results.
- The paper does not discuss the limitations of the proposed method, which could provide valuable insights into its applicability and potential drawbacks.
- The paper lacks a detailed discussion on the computational complexity and resource requirements of the proposed method, which could affect its scalability and practicality in real-world applications.

**Questions:**
- Could you provide more details on the training of the retrieval model, including the specific algorithms and datasets used?
- How does the retrieval model handle cases where the initial output is not relevant to the question, and how does it ensure that the retrieved documents are indeed relevant and useful for refining the outputs?
- Can you explain the rationale behind the choice of using nucleus sampling for diversifying the initial generation outputs? How does this compare to other sampling methods like top-p sampling?
- How does the ensemble method work in practice, and what are the specifics of the probability-based ranking used to select the final output?
- Could you provide more details on the experimental setup, including the specific models used, hyperparameters, and training details? How were the results validated, and what were the statistical significance tests used to ensure the reliability of the findings?
- How does the proposed method perform on tasks that do not require text generation, such as binary classification?
- Could you discuss the potential limitations of the proposed method and how these might affect its practical applicability?
