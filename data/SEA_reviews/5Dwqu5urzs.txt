 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel physics-regulated deep reinforcement learning (DRL) framework, termed Phy-DRL, which integrates data-driven and model-based policies to ensure safety in autonomous systems. The framework includes a residual action policy, safety-embedded reward, and physics-knowledge-enhanced DNN, which are designed to guarantee safety and efficiency in control systems. The paper is supported by theoretical analysis and experimental validation, showing that Phy-DRL outperforms traditional DRL in safety-critical tasks. However, concerns were raised about the clarity of the writing, the complexity of the experimental setup, and the lack of a comprehensive comparison with state-of-the-art methods.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel framework, Phy-DRL, which integrates data-driven and model-based policies, enhancing safety and efficiency in control systems.
- The framework is supported by a strong theoretical foundation, with clear definitions and mathematical proofs provided.
- The paper is well-written, making it accessible and understandable to readers.
- The experimental results are robust, demonstrating the effectiveness of the proposed methods.
- The use of physics knowledge in the design of the DRL framework is a significant contribution to the field.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper is overly complex, with numerous variables and mathematical notations that could confuse readers.
- The experimental setup is not clearly described, making it difficult to replicate the results.
- The paper lacks a comprehensive comparison with state-of-the-art methods, which limits the assessment of its novelty and effectiveness.
- The paper suffers from poor writing quality, with unclear definitions and vague descriptions of methods and results.
- The paper lacks a detailed discussion on the limitations and potential negative societal impacts of the proposed framework.
- The paper does not provide a clear discussion on how the proposed framework could be generalized to other applications beyond the examples provided.

**Questions:**
- Could you clarify the role and definition of the variable "k" in equation (1)?
- How does the paper ensure that the residual action policy effectively integrates data-driven and model-based policies?
- Can you explain the choice of the safety envelope in equation (6) and how it relates to the safety set defined in equation (2)?
- How does the paper address the complexity introduced by the integration of data-driven and model-based policies in the residual action policy?
- Could you provide a more detailed comparison with state-of-the-art methods to better establish the novelty and effectiveness of your framework?
- How do the authors ensure that the framework is generalizable to other applications beyond the examples provided?
- Can you provide a clearer explanation of the experimental setup and results, especially in the context of the quadruped robot experiments?
- How does the paper address the potential negative societal impacts of the proposed framework, particularly in safety-critical applications?
