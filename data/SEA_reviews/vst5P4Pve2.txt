 **Summary:**
The paper explores the global interactions within graph neural networks (GNNs) by introducing a new metric called interaction efficiency, which is assessed using the Jacobian matrix of node features. This metric is used to evaluate the interaction sensitivity and expressiveness of GNNs, focusing on the limitations of existing models in handling diverse global interactions. The authors propose a novel architecture, Universal Interaction Graph Convolution (UIGC), which purportedly addresses these limitations by approximating all pairwise interactions. The paper provides theoretical insights and empirical validations to support these claims, albeit with some concerns regarding the novelty and practical implications of the proposed method.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel interaction efficiency metric, which is a significant contribution to the field of graph neural networks (GNNs).
- The theoretical insights provided, such as the analysis of global interactions and the definition of interaction expressiveness, are well-articulated and supported by empirical results.
- The paper is well-written and easy to follow, making complex concepts accessible to a broader audience.
- The proposed method of approximating interactions using a MLP is innovative and could potentially improve the expressiveness of GNNs.
- The paper provides a comprehensive theoretical analysis of global interactions in GNNs, which is a significant contribution to the field.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks clarity in some sections, particularly in the definitions and explanations of key terms and concepts such as the Jacobian matrix and the interaction space.
- The paper does not sufficiently differentiate its contributions from existing literature, particularly in terms of the novelty of the interaction sensitivity and expressiveness metrics.
- The empirical evaluations are limited, focusing only on a few datasets and not sufficiently comparing the proposed method with existing models.
- The paper does not adequately address the practical implications of the proposed method, particularly in terms of its scalability and applicability to larger or more complex datasets.
- The paper suffers from some presentation issues, such as unclear figures and inconsistencies in notation and terminology.
- The theoretical contributions, while interesting, are not sufficiently grounded in practical applications or real-world scenarios, which limits their relevance and applicability.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors clarify the definitions and roles of the Jacobian matrix and the interaction space in the context of their proposed method?
- How does the proposed method compare to existing models in terms of computational efficiency and scalability, especially when applied to larger or more complex datasets?
- Can the authors provide more detailed experimental results, including comparisons with existing models and ablation studies to better demonstrate the effectiveness of their proposed method?
- How does the proposed method address the practical issues of over-squashing and under-reaching in GNNs?
- Can the authors elaborate on the practical implications and real-world applications of their theoretical insights into global interactions in GNNs?
- Could the authors clarify the inconsistencies in notation and terminology throughout the paper to improve readability and understanding?
