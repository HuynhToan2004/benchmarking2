 **Summary:**
The paper introduces LangProp, a framework for iteratively optimizing code generated by large language models (LLMs) in a supervised or reinforcement learning setting. The framework is designed to evaluate code performance using a dataset of input-output pairs and exception handling, updating the LLM with the results to improve subsequent code generations. LangProp is tested in the CARLA autonomous driving simulator, demonstrating its ability to generate interpretable and transparent driving policies. The framework is evaluated against other methods such as Roach, TCP, TransFuser, InterFuser, and TF++, showing promising results. However, concerns were raised about the novelty of the approach and the clarity of the presentation, particularly in the methodology and experimental setups.

**Strengths:**
- The paper presents a novel approach to using LLMs for code generation, which is a promising direction for the field.
- The idea of using LLMs for optimizing code in a supervised or reinforcement learning setting is innovative and could be applied to various domains.
- The paper is well-written, with clear and concise explanations of the proposed method and its implementation.
- The experimental setup is comprehensive, including a variety of methods for comparison, which helps in understanding the effectiveness of the proposed method.
- The LangProp framework is versatile and adaptable to many application domains, including autonomous driving, where it shows potential for generating interpretable and transparent driving policies.
- The paper provides a detailed explanation of the LangProp framework, which could be beneficial for readers looking to implement similar systems in their own research.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed comparison with other LLM-based methods for code generation, which could have provided a clearer understanding of the advantages and disadvantages of the proposed method.
- The presentation of the paper is not clear, especially in sections 3.1 and 3.2, which are critical for understanding the methodology.
- There is a lack of novelty in the approach, as similar methods have been explored in the past, such as Voyager and other LLM-based code generation methods.
- The paper does not adequately address the generalizability of the LangProp framework to other domains or tasks beyond the specific examples provided.
- The experimental setup and results are not convincingly superior to existing methods, with marginal improvements reported.
- The paper does not sufficiently discuss the limitations of the proposed method, which is crucial for understanding its applicability and effectiveness in real-world scenarios.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors clarify the role and function of the LLM in the LangProp framework, especially in terms of optimizing the code?
- How does the LangProp framework handle the generalizability to other domains or tasks beyond the specific examples provided?
- Can the authors provide a more detailed comparison with other LLM-based methods for code generation, including a discussion on the advantages and disadvantages of each approach?
- How does the LangProp framework ensure the reliability and robustness of the generated code, especially in critical applications like autonomous driving?
- Could the authors elaborate on the experimental setup, particularly the choice of metrics and the methodology used in the comparisons with other methods?
- How does the LangProp framework handle the scalability issues that might arise when applying it to larger or more complex systems?
