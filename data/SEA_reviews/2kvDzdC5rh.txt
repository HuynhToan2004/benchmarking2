 **Summary:**
The paper introduces IntentGPT, a novel approach for intent discovery using large language models (LLMs) like GPT-4. The methodology involves a two-step process: first, an In-Context Prompt Generator (ICPG) is used to create prompts from a set of known intents and examples, and second, these prompts are utilized by an Intent Predictor to predict new intents. The paper claims that IntentGPT outperforms existing methods on benchmarks such as CLINC and BANKING, demonstrating its effectiveness in few-shot settings. However, the novelty of the approach is questioned as it primarily uses known intents and examples to generate new prompts, which may not represent true intent discovery.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, with a clear and concise presentation of the proposed method.
- The experiments are well-designed, showing that the proposed method outperforms baselines on benchmarks such as CLINC and BANKING.
- The paper effectively uses LLMs to generate prompts for intent discovery, which is a novel approach.
- The method's simplicity and its ability to outperform baselines in few-shot settings are highlighted.
- The paper provides a comprehensive analysis of hyperparameter influence on metric performance, which is beneficial for understanding the method's behavior.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks clarity on the definition and handling of "unknown" intents, which could affect the method's effectiveness in real-world scenarios.
- The novelty of the approach is limited as it primarily uses known intents and examples to generate new prompts, which may not represent true intent discovery.
- The paper does not sufficiently discuss the potential societal impacts of the proposed method, which could be significant given the use of LLMs.
- The method's reliance on LLMs for generating prompts might lead to biases and errors, which could impact the method's performance and reliability.
- The paper does not provide a detailed analysis of the prompts generated by the ICPG, which could provide insights into the method's effectiveness and limitations.
- The paper's claims about the method's ability to handle "open-world" intent discovery are not convincingly supported, as the method primarily uses known intents and examples.

**Questions:**
- Can you clarify the definition and handling of "unknown" intents in your method? How does the method handle the identification and classification of new, unseen intents?
- How does the method ensure that the prompts generated by the ICPG are effective and unbiased? Have you conducted any experiments to validate the quality and reliability of the generated prompts?
- Could you provide more details on the method's performance in scenarios where the known intents are not representative or are biased? How does the method handle such situations?
- What are the potential societal impacts of using LLMs for intent discovery, particularly in terms of privacy and security? Have you considered these implications in your methodology?
- Have you experimented with different LLMs, such as Llama 2, to evaluate their impact on the method's performance?
- How does the method handle the issue of out-of-distribution (OOD) examples, which are not covered in the training set?
- Could you provide more details on the method's scalability and generalization capabilities, particularly in terms of its applicability to different domains and the size of the training dataset?

**Paper Decision:**
- Decision: Reject

The method, while effective in few-shot settings, does not convincingly demonstrate true intent discovery as it primarily uses known intents and examples to generate new prompts.
