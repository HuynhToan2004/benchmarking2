 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel approach to self-supervised MRI reconstruction, termed BootRec, which models MRI reconstruction as a parameter estimation problem using bootstrap estimation. This methodology is designed to provide uncertainty quantification and is demonstrated to be effective in zero-shot reconstruction scenarios. The paper explores the connection between bootstrapping and re-undersampling in MRI, showing how this connection can be leveraged to estimate errors in MRI reconstruction. Experiments on the fastMRI dataset demonstrate the superiority of BootRec over existing methods, highlighting its potential as a significant advancement in the field.

**Strengths:**
- The paper provides a clear mathematical explanation of bootstrap estimation and its application to MRI reconstruction, which is novel and interesting.
- The proposed method is well-written, with clear explanations of the mathematical equivalence between bootstrapping and re-undersampling.
- The experiments conducted on the fastMRI dataset demonstrate the superiority of the proposed method over existing methods, showing a significant improvement in performance.
- The paper is well-organized, making it easy to follow, and provides a good balance between theoretical insights and practical applications.
- The proposed method can be applied to any existing MRI reconstruction model, enhancing its versatility and applicability.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed discussion on the limitations of the proposed method, which could be crucial for understanding its practical applicability and potential drawbacks.
- The method's reliance on a large number of observations for effective bootstrap estimation raises concerns about its practicality in real-world scenarios where data is limited.
- The paper could benefit from a more comprehensive literature review, particularly in the area of uncertainty quantification in MRI reconstruction, to position the work more effectively within the existing body of knowledge.
- The mathematical derivations and their explanations in the paper are sometimes unclear, which could hinder understanding of the proposed method.
- The paper could benefit from a more thorough evaluation of the proposed method, including a broader range of experiments and comparisons with more diverse datasets and methods.

**Questions:**
- How does the proposed method perform in scenarios where the number of observations is limited? Is there a threshold below which the method becomes ineffective?
- Can the authors provide a more detailed discussion on the limitations of the proposed method, particularly in terms of its practical applicability and potential drawbacks?
- How does the proposed method compare to other uncertainty quantification methods in MRI reconstruction, particularly those that do not require supervised training?
- Could the authors clarify the mathematical derivations and explanations in the paper to improve understanding of the proposed method?
- Is there a possibility to extend the proposed method to other imaging modalities or medical image analysis tasks beyond MRI?
- Can the authors provide a more detailed comparison of the proposed method with other state-of-the-art methods, particularly in terms of computational efficiency and resource requirements?
