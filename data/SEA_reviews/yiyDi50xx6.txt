 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel approach to vertical federated learning (VFL) for graph neural networks (GNNs) by proposing a model-splitting method and a communication-efficient algorithm named GLASU. This method aims to reduce communication overhead by allowing clients to maintain their own subgraphs and perform lazy aggregation, thereby reducing the number of communication rounds. The GLASU algorithm incorporates stale updates and lazy aggregation, which are shown to save communication while maintaining convergence guarantees. The paper provides theoretical analysis and empirical evidence demonstrating the effectiveness of the proposed method.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel approach to vertical federated learning (VFL) for graph neural networks (GNNs), which is a significant advancement in the field.
- The proposed method, GLASU, effectively reduces communication overhead in VFL by utilizing lazy aggregation and stale updates, which are innovative techniques for communication reduction.
- The paper provides a theoretical analysis of the convergence behavior of GLASU, which is a significant contribution to the understanding of the proposed method.
- The empirical results demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed method in reducing communication costs while maintaining accuracy, which is a crucial aspect of VFL.
- The paper is well-written, clear, and easy to follow, making it accessible to a broad audience.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed comparison with other related works, particularly those that address similar problems in VFL and GNNs.
- The experimental setup is limited to a small number of clients (M=3), which may not adequately represent real-world scenarios with a larger number of clients.
- The paper does not address privacy concerns in VFL, which is a significant issue in practical deployments.
- The theoretical analysis assumes constant step sizes, which may not be practical in real-world scenarios where the learning rate is typically adjusted during training.
- The convergence analysis is limited to the GLASU algorithm and does not extend to other algorithms that may be used in VFL.
- The paper does not provide a detailed comparison of the proposed method with other state-of-the-art methods in terms of communication savings and accuracy.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors provide a more detailed comparison with other related works, particularly those that address similar problems in VFL and GNNs?
- How does the proposed method compare in terms of communication savings and accuracy with other state-of-the-art methods?
- Can the authors provide a detailed analysis of the computational complexity of the proposed method compared to other methods?
- How does the proposed method perform in scenarios with a larger number of clients?
- Can the authors provide a more detailed discussion on the privacy concerns in VFL and how the proposed method addresses these concerns?
- Can the authors provide a more detailed discussion on the scalability of the proposed method with an increasing number of clients?
- Can the authors provide a more detailed discussion on the impact of different hyperparameters (K, Q) on the performance of the proposed method?
