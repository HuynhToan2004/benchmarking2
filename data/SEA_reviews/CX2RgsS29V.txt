 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel algorithm for updating truncated Singular Value Decomposition (SVD) of sparse matrices, leveraging sparsity in the orthogonalization process of augmented matrices and employing an extended decomposition to store projections in the column space of singular vectors. This approach significantly reduces the computational complexity and time required for updates compared to existing methods, maintaining comparable accuracy. The method's efficiency is demonstrated through numerical experiments, showcasing a substantial speed boost while maintaining accuracy. However, the paper lacks a detailed comparison with state-of-the-art methods and does not address potential numerical instabilities in the proposed method.

**Strengths:**

- The algorithm is supported by theoretical guarantees and is demonstrated to be efficient through numerical experiments, achieving a substantial speed boost while maintaining comparable accuracy.
- The paper is well-written, making it easy to follow and understand, and the algorithm is clearly explained with detailed pseudocode and a clear presentation of the methodology.
- The proposed method is an important contribution to the field of SVD-based representation learning, addressing the issue of updating truncated SVDs in sparse matrices, which is crucial for many real-world applications.
- The paper is well-organized and structured, with clear and concise explanations of the algorithm and its theoretical underpinnings, which enhances its accessibility and applicability to a broader audience.
