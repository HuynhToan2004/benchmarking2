 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel watermarking algorithm for large language models (LLMs) that utilizes two distinct neural networks for watermark generation and detection, eliminating the need for a shared key. This approach aims to enhance the security and robustness of watermarking by making it publicly verifiable and unforgeable. The methodology involves the use of a shared token embedding network, which is used for both watermark generation and detection, thereby improving efficiency and reducing the complexity of the training process. The paper presents experimental results demonstrating the effectiveness and robustness of the proposed method against various attacks, including attempts to reverse-engineer the watermarking process.

**Strengths:**
- The paper addresses the significant issue of watermarking for large language models (LLMs), which is crucial for detecting machine-generated content and protecting intellectual property.
- The use of a shared embedding network between the watermark generation and detection networks is innovative and enhances the efficiency of training the detector.
- The paper is well-written and easy to follow, making the complex concepts of watermarking and the proposed method accessible to a broader audience.
- The proposed method is robust against various attacks, demonstrating its effectiveness in real-world scenarios.
- The paper includes comprehensive ablation studies that validate the design choices and the necessity of the shared embedding layer.
- The methodology is novel and practical, with potential applications in various industries where LLM-generated content needs to be verified.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed discussion on the potential limitations or societal implications of the proposed watermarking algorithm.
- There is a lack of clarity in some parts of the paper, particularly in the description of the watermark generation process and the role of the shared embedding network.
- The experimental results are not sufficiently robust, with a limited number of datasets and a lack of comparison with more advanced models.
- The paper does not provide a detailed analysis of the computational complexity or the memory requirements of the proposed method, which could be crucial for practical implementation.
- The paper could benefit from a more comprehensive evaluation of the robustness of the watermarking algorithm against different types of attacks, including more advanced and realistic scenarios.
- The paper does not adequately address the potential for false positives or false negatives in the watermark detection process, which could lead to misleading conclusions.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors clarify the role and functionality of the shared embedding network in the watermark generation process?
- How does the proposed method ensure the robustness of the watermark against various types of attacks, including adversarial attacks?
- Can the authors provide more detailed experimental results, including a broader range of datasets and more advanced models for comparison?
- What are the computational and memory requirements of the proposed method, and how do they compare to other existing watermarking algorithms?
- How does the proposed method perform under different decoding strategies, such as greedy decoding or beam search with different beam sizes?
- Can the authors provide more details on the false positive and false negative rates observed in the experiments, and discuss their implications for the practical use of the watermarking algorithm?
