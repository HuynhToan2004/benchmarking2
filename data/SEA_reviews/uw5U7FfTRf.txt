 **Summary:**
The paper introduces BaDLoss, a method for detecting backdoor attacks in neural networks by analyzing the training dynamics of examples. It leverages a k-Nearest Neighbors (k-NN) classifier to distinguish between clean and backdoored examples based on the loss trajectories. The method involves inserting probe examples into the training set and retraining the model to identify and remove backdoored examples. BaDLoss claims to be effective against various backdoor attacks, including those that are not easily detected by existing methods. The evaluation, however, is limited to smaller datasets like CIFAR-10 and GTSRB, raising concerns about its scalability and generalization to larger, more complex datasets.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written and easy to follow, with clear explanations of the proposed method and its implementation.
- The motivation behind the research is strong, focusing on the detection of backdoor attacks in neural networks, which is a significant problem in the field.
- The method proposed, BaDLoss, is novel and effective, as demonstrated through experiments on CIFAR-10 and GTSRB datasets.
- The paper provides a comprehensive evaluation of the method, comparing it against various state-of-the-art backdoor detection methods.
- The method's simplicity and potential for practical application are highlighted, making it a valuable contribution to the field.

**Weaknesses:**
- The evaluation of the method is limited to smaller datasets, which may not adequately demonstrate its effectiveness on larger, more complex datasets.
- The paper lacks a detailed discussion on the choice of hyperparameters, such as the number of probe examples and the k-NN classifier, which could significantly impact the performance of the method.
- The method's assumptions about the training dynamics of backdoored examples may not hold true for all types of backdoor attacks, limiting its generalizability.
- The method's reliance on the availability of probe examples could limit its applicability in real-world scenarios where such examples are not readily available.
- The paper does not discuss the potential negative societal impacts of the proposed method, which is a significant omission.
- The method's performance is not compared against the latest backdoor attacks and defenses, which could affect the relevance and applicability of the results.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors clarify the choice of hyperparameters, particularly the number of probe examples and the k-NN classifier, and discuss how these choices affect the performance of the method?
- How does the method perform on larger, more complex datasets, such as ImageNet?
- Could the authors provide more details on the training dynamics of backdoored examples and how these dynamics are used to distinguish between clean and backdoored examples?
- How does the method handle different types of backdoor attacks that may not exhibit the same training dynamics as those assumed by the method?
- Can the authors discuss the potential negative societal impacts of the proposed method and how these impacts could be mitigated?
- Could the authors provide more details on the method's performance against the latest backdoor attacks and defenses to ensure the relevance and applicability of the results?
