 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel approach to constrained Bayesian optimization (CBO) by integrating active learning for level-set estimation with Bayesian optimization. The method, termed COBALT, identifies regions of interest (ROIs) for both the objective function and constraints, which are then used to optimize the objective within the feasible region. Theoretical analysis and empirical studies are provided to support the efficacy of the proposed method. The paper also discusses the practical implications of the method in various settings, including both synthetic and real-world scenarios. Despite the theoretical contributions and empirical validation, the paper has been critiqued for its lack of clarity in explaining the theoretical underpinnings, particularly the proof of key lemmas and theorems, and the necessity of the proposed method over existing approaches.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written and provides a clear introduction to constrained Bayesian optimization (CBO), highlighting the limitations of existing methods.
- The proposed COBALT algorithm integrates active learning for level-set estimation with Bayesian optimization, which is novel and addresses the challenges of adaptively balancing the active feasible region identification and the unknown objective optimization.
- Theoretical analysis is provided to support the efficacy of the proposed method, including a probabilistic regret analysis and a proof of the main theorem.
- Empirical studies demonstrate the efficiency and robustness of the proposed CBO framework across various optimization tasks, including both synthetic and real-world scenarios.
- The paper is structured well, with a clear presentation of the algorithm and its theoretical guarantees, making it accessible to readers.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks clarity in explaining the theoretical underpinnings, particularly the proof of key lemmas and theorems, which are crucial for understanding the theoretical contributions.
- The methodology section is not well-explained, particularly the process of identifying the region of interest (ROI) and the rationale behind certain design choices, such as the use of UCB and LCB.
- The paper does not adequately compare the proposed method with existing approaches, particularly in terms of computational efficiency and the scalability of the algorithm.
- The paper does not provide a detailed discussion on the limitations of the proposed method, which could help in understanding its applicability and potential challenges in real-world scenarios.
- The empirical studies are limited to a small number of trials, which may not provide a robust validation of the proposed method's efficacy.
- The paper does not sufficiently discuss the implications of the assumptions made, particularly Assumption 3, which is crucial for the theoretical guarantees.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors provide more details on how the ROI is identified and how it contributes to the overall methodology?
- How does the proposed method handle the identification of the feasible region, particularly in scenarios where the feasible region is not well-defined or when the constraints are not linearly separable?
- Could the authors clarify the proof of Lemma 1 and Theorem 1, particularly the role of the hyperparameters in the proofs?
- How does the proposed method compare with existing approaches in terms of computational efficiency and scalability?
- Could the authors provide a more detailed discussion on the limitations of the proposed method and how these might affect its practical applicability?
- How does the paper address the assumptions made, particularly Assumption 3, and what are the implications of these assumptions in real-world scenarios?
