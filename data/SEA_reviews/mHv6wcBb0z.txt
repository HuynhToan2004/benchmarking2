 **Summary:**
The paper addresses the model collapse issue in Deep Canonical Correlation Analysis (DCCA) by introducing a noise regularization approach, termed NR-DCCA. The authors demonstrate that the full-rank property of the transformation in CCA is crucial for preventing model collapse, and propose a novel noise regularization technique to maintain this property in DCCA. The paper includes both theoretical analysis and extensive experiments, showing that NR-DCCA outperforms other methods in terms of stability and performance. The approach is also generalized to other DCCA-based methods and tested on both synthetic and real-world datasets. However, the paper lacks a comprehensive comparison with state-of-the-art methods and does not adequately address the computational complexity of the proposed method.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, easy to follow, and includes a comprehensive theoretical analysis of the proposed method.
- The proposed noise regularization approach is novel and effectively prevents model collapse in DCCA, as demonstrated through extensive experiments on both synthetic and real-world datasets.
- The paper introduces a new noise regularization technique that can be applied to other DCCA-based methods, enhancing the generalizability of the proposed method.
- The authors provide a novel framework for constructing synthetic data with different common and complementary information, which is useful for evaluating multi-view representation learning methods comprehensively.
- The paper includes a detailed theoretical analysis of the full-rank property of the transformation in CCA, which is crucial for preventing model collapse.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a comprehensive comparison with state-of-the-art methods, particularly in terms of computational complexity and performance on larger, more complex datasets.
- The novelty of the proposed method is limited as similar approaches using noise regularization have been previously explored in other contexts.
- The paper does not adequately address the computational complexity of the proposed method, which could be a significant drawback for practical applications.
- The paper does not provide sufficient details on the implementation of the proposed method, such as the specifics of the noise generation and the hyperparameters used.
- The paper lacks a detailed discussion on the limitations of the proposed method and potential negative societal impacts.
- The paper's experimental setup and results are not sufficiently detailed, particularly in terms of the computational resources and settings used, which could affect the reproducibility and generalizability of the results.

**Questions:**

- How does the proposed method perform on larger, more complex datasets, and what are the potential limitations or challenges in scaling up the method?
- Can the authors provide more details on the implementation of the proposed method, particularly in terms of the noise generation process and the hyperparameters used?
- How does the proposed method compare in terms of performance and computational efficiency with other state-of-the-art methods, especially in scenarios where model collapse is not a significant issue?
- Could the authors discuss the limitations of the proposed method and potential negative societal impacts, particularly in the context of multi-view representation learning?
- How do the authors address the issue of model collapse in scenarios where it is not a significant issue, and what are the potential benefits or drawbacks of using the proposed method in such scenarios?
