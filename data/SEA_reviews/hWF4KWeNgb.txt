 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel method for multi-class anomaly detection using a hierarchical Gaussian mixture normalizing flow modeling approach. The method addresses the "homogeneous mapping" issue in existing normalizing flow-based anomaly detection methods by introducing multiple latent class centers and a mutual information maximization loss. This approach aims to enhance the representation of complex multi-class distributions in the latent space, thereby improving anomaly detection performance. The method was evaluated on four real-world datasets and demonstrated significant improvements over existing unified anomaly detection methods. However, the paper has been critiqued for its limited novelty, lack of comprehensive theoretical analysis, and insufficient comparison with relevant methods.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-organized, easy to follow, and the proposed method is clearly described.
- The method addresses the "homogeneous mapping" issue in normalizing flow-based anomaly detection, which is a significant challenge in the field.
- The experiments are conducted on multiple datasets, demonstrating the effectiveness of the proposed method.
- The paper includes comprehensive ablation studies, which provide a detailed analysis of the proposed method's components.
- The authors provide the code for reproducibility, which is a valuable resource for the community.
- The method's performance is validated on multiple datasets, showing improvements over existing methods.

**Weaknesses:**
- The novelty of the paper is limited, as it mainly combines existing methods such as normalizing flows and Gaussian mixture models without significant innovation.
- The paper lacks a thorough theoretical analysis and comparison with relevant methods, particularly those that address similar issues in anomaly detection.
- The experimental results are not sufficiently robust, with significant variations in performance across different datasets and a lack of comparison with the latest methods.
- The paper does not adequately address the computational complexity of the method, which could be a significant concern for practical applications.
- The paper could benefit from a more detailed discussion on the limitations of the method and its potential negative societal impacts.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors provide a more detailed comparison with other methods that address the "homogeneous mapping" issue in anomaly detection?
- How does the proposed method compare with the latest methods in terms of computational complexity and practical applicability?
- Could the authors provide a more detailed analysis of the experimental results, particularly the variations in performance across different datasets?
- What are the limitations of the proposed method, and how do these impact its practical applicability?
- Can the authors provide more details on the computational complexity of the method and discuss potential strategies for reducing this complexity in practical applications?
