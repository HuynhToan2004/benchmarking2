 **Summary:**
The paper introduces GraphChef, a novel GNN model that integrates decision trees into its message-passing framework to enhance explainability. GraphChef converts the internal states of GNNs into categorical states, which are then used to train decision trees. This approach aims to provide human-comprehensible explanations for graph classification tasks. The model's performance is evaluated on various benchmark datasets, demonstrating its potential to match or surpass the performance of non-self-explanatory GNNs while maintaining interpretability. However, concerns are raised about the method's scalability and the clarity of its explanations, particularly in complex datasets with large feature spaces.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, clear, and easy to follow, with a clear motivation for the proposed method.
- The proposed method is simple, intuitive, and easy to implement, with a focus on improving explainability in GNNs.
- The paper provides a user interface for interactive exploration of GraphChef recipes, enhancing the accessibility of the model's explanations.
- The method is evaluated on a wide range of datasets, including some with ground truth explanation, which helps in validating the model's effectiveness.
- The methodology is innovative, combining decision trees with GNNs to create a recipe for dataset-level explanations, which is a novel approach in the field.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed discussion on the limitations of the proposed method, particularly in terms of scalability and the complexity of the datasets it can handle.
- The method's reliance on decision trees for explanation might limit its ability to capture complex relationships and dependencies in graph data.
- The paper does not compare its method with other recent state-of-the-art explainability methods for GNNs, which could provide a better understanding of its relative effectiveness.
- The method's interpretability is questionable, as it does not provide clear insights into the relationships between features or subgraphs, and the explanations provided are sometimes overly simplistic or not actionable.
- The method's performance on complex datasets with large feature spaces is not thoroughly evaluated, raising concerns about its scalability and applicability in real-world scenarios.
- The paper does not adequately address how the method handles large datasets or how it might be adapted for different types of graph data, such as molecular graphs.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors provide more details on how the decision tree-based explanations capture the relationships between different features or subgraphs in the graph data?
- How does the proposed method compare with other recent state-of-the-art explainability methods for GNNs in terms of effectiveness and efficiency?
- Could the authors provide examples or case studies demonstrating how the method's explanations can be used to inform decision-making or improve understanding of graph data in real-world scenarios?
- How does the method handle large datasets with a large number of features, and what are the implications for its scalability and performance in such scenarios?
- Can the authors clarify how the method's interpretability is evaluated, particularly in terms of its ability to provide actionable insights or recommendations based on the explanations provided?
- How does the method perform on more complex datasets, such as those involving molecular graphs or other types of graph data with significant structural complexity?
