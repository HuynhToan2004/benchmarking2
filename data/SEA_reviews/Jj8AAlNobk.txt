 **Summary:**
The paper explores the connection between sequence modeling and policy gradients, proposing a novel action-sequence model (ASM) that directly predicts state dynamics given a sequence of actions. This approach allows for a more stable and accurate gradient estimation, particularly in scenarios with long-term credit assignment challenges. The authors demonstrate the utility of ASMs using both theoretical analysis and empirical results, showing that they can outperform traditional one-step models and other sequence-based models in various tasks. The paper also discusses the limitations of the model in more complex and stochastic environments, suggesting areas for future research.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written and structured, making it easy to follow and understand.
- The idea of connecting sequence modeling and policy gradients is novel and interesting, providing a fresh perspective on the policy gradient method.
- The experiments are well-designed, showing the advantages of the proposed action-sequence model (ASM) over traditional one-step models and other sequence-based models.
- The paper provides a theoretical analysis of the stability of the policy gradient, which is a significant contribution to the field.
- The empirical results are convincing, demonstrating the practical utility of the ASM in tasks where long-term credit assignment is challenging.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper's title and some of its claims are overly ambitious, potentially misleading readers about the scope and depth of the research.
- The experiments are somewhat limited in scope, focusing primarily on deterministic, low-dimensional environments, which may not fully demonstrate the model's capabilities in more complex, real-world scenarios.
- The paper does not adequately address the limitations of the ASM, particularly in stochastic or partially observable environments.
- The paper's theoretical analysis is somewhat limited, focusing primarily on the stability of the policy gradient without sufficiently exploring the practical implications or the limitations of the ASM in more complex scenarios.
- The paper lacks a detailed comparison with other sequence-based models, which could help clarify the unique advantages of the ASM.
- There is a need for more detailed experimental results and comparisons, especially in more complex environments, to fully demonstrate the effectiveness of the ASM.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors clarify how the ASM can be applied in stochastic environments, and whether it can be adapted to handle such scenarios?
- How does the ASM perform in more complex, real-world environments, and what are the potential limitations or challenges in such settings?
- Could the authors provide more details on the training of the ASM, particularly the training data and the methodology used?
- How does the ASM compare to other sequence-based models in terms of performance and efficiency?
- Is there a potential for the ASM to be adapted to handle partially observable MDPs, and if so, how might this be achieved?
- How does the ASM's performance compare to other model-based reinforcement learning methods, such as those that use history-sequence models?
- Could the authors elaborate on the practical implications of the theoretical analysis provided, particularly in terms of how it informs the design or implementation of the ASM?
