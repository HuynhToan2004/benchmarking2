 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel self-supervised learning approach using a GAN framework, where the discriminator is designed to learn semantic representations without relying on data augmentation. The method leverages a contrastive clustering target and a smoothness regularizer to guide the discriminator in learning both coarse and fine-grained features. The authors demonstrate that this method can achieve competitive results on CIFAR and ImageNet datasets compared to state-of-the-art contrastive learning methods. However, the paper lacks a comprehensive discussion on the limitations and potential negative societal impacts, and the experimental results are not convincingly superior to existing methods.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, with clear and logical organization that effectively communicates the authors' ideas.
- The methodology is innovative, combining GANs with self-supervised learning, and the proposed method shows promise in achieving competitive results on CIFAR and ImageNet datasets.
- The paper is well-motivated, with a clear distinction from existing work, and the experiments are well-designed to demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed method.
- The proposed method is simple and effective, and the paper provides a good discussion on the limitations of the proposed method.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a comprehensive discussion on the limitations and potential negative societal impacts of the proposed method.
- The experimental results are not convincingly superior to existing methods, particularly in terms of the quality of generated images and the superiority of the proposed method over other self-supervised learning methods.
- The paper could benefit from a more detailed comparison with other self-supervised learning methods and a discussion on the computational efficiency and scalability of the proposed method.
- The methodology section is difficult to follow, and there is a lack of clarity in some equations and notations used.
- The paper could benefit from additional experiments, such as those involving larger datasets like ImageNet, and a more thorough analysis of the limitations and potential improvements of the proposed method.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors provide a more detailed discussion on the limitations and potential negative societal impacts of the proposed method?
- How does the proposed method compare in terms of computational efficiency and scalability with other self-supervised learning methods?
- Could the authors elaborate on the motivation behind the choice of the Gaussian distance metric and its implications for the performance of the method?
- In the context of the experimental results, how does the proposed method perform when compared to other self-supervised learning methods, particularly in terms of the quality of generated images and the effectiveness of the learned representations?
- How does the proposed method handle the issue of mode collapse in GANs, and what are the potential impacts of the regularization scheme on the model's capacity and performance?
