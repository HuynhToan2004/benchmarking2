 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel Generative Adversarial Federated Model (GAFM) aimed at enhancing label privacy protection in Vertical Federated Learning (VFL) by integrating Generative Adversarial Networks (GANs) with splitNN. GAFM employs a GAN-based mechanism to learn label distributions indirectly, thereby mitigating the risk of label leakage from gradients. The model also incorporates a cross-entropy loss to improve prediction accuracy. The paper presents empirical results demonstrating that GAFM outperforms vanilla splitNN in terms of model utility and privacy protection. However, the novelty of the approach is questioned as it appears to be a combination of existing methods without significant innovation.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, making it easy to follow and understand, with clear explanations of the proposed methods.
- The proposed method is novel, integrating Generative Adversarial Federated Model (GAFM) with splitNN to enhance label privacy protection in Vertical Federated Learning (VFL).
- The paper provides empirical results that demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed method in mitigating label leakage from gradients.
- The methodology is supported by a theoretical analysis that provides insights into the proposed method.
- The paper introduces a new label stealing attack, enhancing the understanding of potential vulnerabilities in the proposed method.
- The authors have provided code for reproducibility, which is a valuable contribution to the research community.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a comprehensive literature review, particularly in the area of GANs in federated learning, which could have provided a better context for the research.
- The novelty of the proposed method is questionable as it seems to be a simple combination of existing methods without significant innovation.
- The paper does not adequately address the computational cost and scalability of the proposed method, especially in scenarios with a large number of clients.
- The theoretical analysis is limited, and the paper does not provide a rigorous proof of the effectiveness of the proposed method.
- The paper does not sufficiently compare the proposed method with other existing methods, particularly in terms of model utility and privacy protection.
- The paper does not discuss the potential negative societal impacts of the proposed method, which is a significant omission.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors provide a more detailed explanation of why the GAN-based approach is more effective in mitigating label leakage compared to other methods?
- How does the proposed method handle scenarios where the label distribution is highly imbalanced?
- What are the potential negative societal impacts of the proposed method, and how do the authors address these concerns?
- Can the authors provide a more comprehensive comparison of the proposed method with other existing methods in terms of model utility and privacy protection?
- How does the proposed method perform in scenarios with a large number of clients, and what are the implications for computational cost and scalability?
- Could the authors clarify the role and impact of the cross-entropy loss in the proposed method, particularly in terms of its contribution to model utility and privacy protection?
