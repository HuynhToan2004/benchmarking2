 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel framework called Hyper-Evidential Neural Network (HENN) designed to quantify uncertainty in classification tasks where data instances are labeled with composite sets of classes. This approach extends existing Evidential Neural Networks (ENNs) by utilizing a grouped Dirichlet distribution to model predictive uncertainty due to composite class labels in training data. The authors propose a new loss function, Uncertainty Partial Cross Entropy (UPCE), to train the network and demonstrate the effectiveness of HENN through experiments on four image datasets. The paper also discusses the theoretical aspects of the proposed method, including the use of Subjective Logic to model predictive uncertainty.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel framework, Hyper-Evidential Neural Network (HENN), which addresses a unique and challenging problem of quantifying uncertainty in classification tasks where data instances are labeled with composite sets of classes.
- The authors provide a comprehensive literature review, highlighting the gaps in existing methods and the novelty of their approach.
- The paper is well-written, making it easy to follow, and includes a detailed explanation of the proposed method, including the new loss function and the use of Subjective Logic to model predictive uncertainty.
- The paper includes extensive experiments and ablation studies that demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed method over existing methods.
- The authors provide the code and datasets, enhancing the reproducibility and transparency of their research.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper could benefit from a more detailed explanation of the motivation behind the use of composite class labels and how they are generated, particularly in the context of the experiments.
- There is a lack of discussion on the limitations of the proposed method, such as its applicability to different types of datasets and its computational efficiency.
- The paper does not adequately compare the proposed method with state-of-the-art methods, particularly those that also address classification uncertainty.
- The paper does not provide a detailed analysis of the computational complexity or scalability of the proposed method, which is crucial for practical applications.
- The paper lacks a thorough discussion on the ethical implications of the proposed method, which could be significant in real-world applications.
- The paper could benefit from a more rigorous evaluation of the proposed method, including more detailed experimental results and a broader range of datasets.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors provide a more detailed explanation of the motivation behind the use of composite class labels and how they are generated in the context of the experiments?
- How does the proposed method handle the computational complexity and scalability, particularly in larger and more complex datasets?
- Could the authors discuss the ethical implications of the proposed method and how it might be applied in real-world scenarios?
- How does the proposed method compare with state-of-the-art methods, particularly those that also address classification uncertainty?
- Could the authors provide a more detailed analysis of the limitations of the proposed method and suggest areas for future research?
- How does the proposed method handle the issue of label noise, which is common in real-world applications?
