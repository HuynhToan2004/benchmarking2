 **Summary:**
The paper presents a novel approach to estimating uncertainty in neural networks, specifically focusing on the propagation of uncertainty through various non-linear activation functions such as tanh, softplus, and ReLU. This is achieved using a stochastic linearization method that approximates the non-linearities with affine functions, thereby enabling the application of Kalman filtering techniques. The methodology is demonstrated through simulations, showing the effectiveness of the proposed approach in estimating mean and variance of the output distribution. However, the paper's experimental validation is limited to synthetic data, and the scalability and practical applicability of the method in real-world scenarios remain unclear.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel method for estimating the uncertainty of neural networks, which is a significant and under-explored area in the field.
- The method is clearly explained, with detailed mathematical derivations provided in the appendix, making it accessible to readers.
- The paper demonstrates the effectiveness of the proposed method through various simulations, showing improvements over existing methods like Jacobian Linearization (JL).
- The method's application to Kalman filtering is innovative and could potentially enhance the accuracy of state-estimation in dynamic systems.
- The paper is well-written, making it easy to follow and understand the proposed methodologies and their implications.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a comprehensive comparison with existing methods, particularly in terms of computational efficiency and accuracy.
- The experiments are limited to synthetic data, which may not fully represent the complexity and variability of real-world data.
- There is a noticeable gap in the literature review, with significant related works not adequately cited or discussed.
- The method's scalability and practical applicability in high-dimensional or large-scale problems are not thoroughly explored or justified.
- The paper does not provide sufficient details on the implementation and practical deployment of the proposed method, which could limit its adoption by practitioners.
- The paper's focus on mean and variance estimation might not fully capture the complexity of real-world data, which could affect the method's performance in more complex scenarios.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors provide a more detailed comparison of the computational efficiency and accuracy of their method against existing methods?
- How does the method perform in real-world scenarios, especially in high-dimensional or large-scale problems?
- Can the authors discuss the potential impact of the method on the deployment of neural networks in safety-critical applications, such as pedestrian detection or power management?
- What are the potential limitations of the method when applied to more complex neural network architectures or different types of data?
- How does the method handle the uncertainty in the initial conditions or parameters of the neural network, and what are the implications for the accuracy of the uncertainty estimates?
- Could the authors provide more details on the implementation and practical deployment of the proposed method, including any challenges or considerations for its adoption in real-world applications?
