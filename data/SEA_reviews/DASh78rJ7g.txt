 **Summary:**
The paper investigates the problem of selective classification with out-of-distribution (OOD) detection, proposing a unified framework that integrates both tasks. It derives the Bayes-optimal solution for this problem, which combines confidence scores for selective classification and density ratio scores for OOD detection. The paper then introduces a plug-in estimator to approximate this solution, using existing methods for selective classification and OOD detection. Experiments are conducted on various datasets, including CIFAR and ImageNet, demonstrating the effectiveness of the proposed method. The paper also discusses the theoretical underpinnings of the problem and provides a statistical formulation for the SCOD problem.

**Strengths:**
- The paper addresses an important and practical problem of selective classification with out-of-distribution (OOD) detection, which is relevant to real-world scenarios.
- It provides a unified framework for studying the problem, which is a significant contribution to the field.
- The theoretical analysis is thorough, including a Bayes-optimal solution for the problem, and the paper is well-written, making the theoretical results clear and accessible.
- The empirical results are strong, demonstrating the effectiveness of the proposed methods.
- The paper includes a comprehensive literature review, which is beneficial for understanding the current state of research in the field.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper could benefit from a more detailed discussion on the practical implications of the theoretical results, particularly in terms of how they guide the design of practical algorithms.
- The experimental setup and the choice of hyperparameters, such as the cost parameters and the threshold t_BB, are not clearly justified or explained.
- There is a lack of comparison with a broader range of existing methods, particularly those that address similar problems but use different approaches.
- The paper could benefit from a clearer explanation of the assumptions made in the theoretical analysis, especially how these assumptions relate to real-world scenarios.
- The experimental results are not consistently strong across all datasets, and the paper could benefit from more detailed reporting and analysis of the results.
- The paper could be improved by providing more detailed explanations and justifications for the experimental setup and the choice of hyperparameters.
