 **Summary:**
The paper introduces FedWon, a novel federated learning (FL) method that eliminates normalization layers and replaces them with scaled weight standardization to address the multi-domain FL problem. FedWon is evaluated across five datasets and five models, demonstrating superior performance over existing methods like FedAvg and FedBN. It is designed to handle diverse data domains and small batch sizes, making it suitable for both cross-silo and cross-device FL scenarios. The method's effectiveness is highlighted by its ability to outperform existing methods in terms of accuracy improvements and its applicability to various FL settings. However, the paper's contribution is questioned due to its limited novelty and the lack of comprehensive theoretical analysis and comparisons with a broader range of methods.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel method, FedWon, that effectively addresses the multi-domain federated learning problem by removing normalization layers and replacing them with scaled weight standardization, which is a simple yet effective approach.
- Extensive experiments are conducted across five datasets and five models, demonstrating the superior performance of FedWon over existing methods like FedAvg and FedBN.
- The paper is well-organized, easy to follow, and provides a clear explanation of the proposed method, making it accessible to a broad audience.
- FedWon's versatility is highlighted by its ability to support both cross-silo and cross-device federated learning, and its effectiveness is shown even with small batch sizes.
- The paper includes a comprehensive evaluation of FedWon's performance under different settings, such as small batch sizes and skewed label distributions, which demonstrates its robustness and adaptability.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed analysis of the differences between FedWon and other methods like FedAvg and FedBN, which could provide a deeper understanding of the advantages of FedWon.
- There is a limited theoretical analysis of the proposed method, which could enhance the paper's credibility and depth.
- The experiments are limited to a narrow range of datasets and models, which may not fully demonstrate the generalizability of the method.
- The paper does not sufficiently address the scalability of FedWon, especially in large-scale federated learning scenarios.
- The paper lacks a comparison with a broader range of methods, including other normalization-free methods, which could provide a more comprehensive evaluation of FedWon's performance.
- The paper does not discuss the limitations of FedWon, which could provide valuable insights for future research and improvements.

**Questions:**
- Can you provide a detailed analysis of the differences between FedWon and other methods like FedAvg and FedBN?
- How does FedWon handle the scalability issues in large-scale federated learning scenarios?
- Could you include a comparison with a broader range of methods, including other normalization-free methods, to provide a more comprehensive evaluation of FedWon's performance?
- How does FedWon perform under different communication round settings?
- Can you discuss the limitations of FedWon and suggest potential directions for future research and improvements?
