 **Summary:**
The paper introduces DeeDiff, a novel early exiting framework designed to improve the generation efficiency of diffusion models by dynamically allocating computation resources at each sampling step. This framework incorporates a timestep-aware uncertainty estimation module to estimate the prediction uncertainty of each layer, enabling early exiting decisions based on this uncertainty. Additionally, an uncertainty-aware layer-wise loss strategy is employed to fill the performance gap between full models and early-exited models. The methodology is validated through extensive experiments on various datasets, demonstrating state-of-the-art performance and efficiency compared to existing early exiting methods. The paper is well-received for its innovative approach, extensive experimental validation, and the release of code and models for reproducibility.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, easy to follow, and the experimental results are impressive, demonstrating state-of-the-art performance and efficiency.
- The proposed method is intuitive and well-motivated, with a clear explanation of the methodology and its application to diffusion models.
- The uncertainty estimation and layer-wise loss are well-designed and effectively enhance the performance of the model.
- The paper is reproducible, with all code and models released for public use, which is crucial for the reproducibility and further research in the field.
- The method is simple yet effective, and the uncertainty estimation and layer-wise loss are well-designed and effectively enhance the performance of the model.
- The paper is well-organized, with a clear summary of contributions and an extensive experimental section that validates the proposed method.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed analysis of the computational cost of the additional modules introduced, such as the uncertainty estimation module and the layer-wise loss.
- The paper does not provide a detailed comparison with other early exiting methods that use different uncertainty estimation techniques or layer-wise loss strategies.
- The experimental results on larger datasets like ImageNet show a significant performance drop, which might limit the practical applicability of the method.
- The paper does not discuss the potential limitations or societal impacts of the proposed method, which could be important considerations for the deployment of the technology.
- There is a lack of qualitative results and comparisons with other methods, which could provide a more comprehensive understanding of the performance and effectiveness of the proposed method.
- The paper could benefit from a more detailed discussion on the assumptions made by the uncertainty estimation module and the potential impact of these assumptions on the performance of the model.

**Questions:**
- How does the performance of the model change when different threshold values are used for early exiting?
- What are the specific computational costs of the additional modules introduced, and how do these costs compare to the computational savings achieved by the early exiting strategy?
- How does the proposed method compare to other early exiting methods that use different uncertainty estimation techniques or layer-wise loss strategies?
- Can the authors provide more details on the training process, particularly how the uncertainty estimation module is trained and how it interacts with the rest of the model?
- How does the proposed method perform on larger datasets like ImageNet, and what are the potential reasons for the observed performance drop?
- Could the authors provide more qualitative results, such as visual comparisons of the generated images, to better understand the performance and effectiveness of the proposed method?
