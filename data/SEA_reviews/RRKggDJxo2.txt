 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel reservoir computing architecture, termed reservoir-in-reservoir (R-i-R), aimed at modeling the decay trajectories of Higgs bosons. This architecture integrates multiple reservoirs, each specialized for different aspects of the decay process, with a primary reservoir learning from these secondary reservoirs. The R-i-R model is evaluated against existing reservoir architectures and other system identification methods, showing potential for improved performance in modeling complex dynamical systems. However, concerns are raised regarding the clarity of the methodology, the choice of baselines, and the generalizability of the approach beyond the specific application to Higgs boson decay.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel reservoir computing architecture, reservoir-in-reservoir (R-i-R), which is a significant contribution to the field.
- The methodology is well-explained, making it accessible to readers.
- The proposed architecture is evaluated against state-of-the-art reservoir computing methods, demonstrating its effectiveness in modeling complex dynamical systems.
- The paper is well-written, with clear explanations of the proposed method and its implementation.
- The application of the proposed method to the Higgs boson decay problem is interesting and adds a new perspective to the application of machine learning in physics.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a clear definition and explanation of the decay trajectory of the Higgs boson, which is crucial for understanding the application of the model.
- The choice of baselines for comparison is not justified, and the paper could benefit from a more comprehensive comparison with other machine learning methods.
- The paper suffers from poor writing, with unclear explanations and inadequate descriptions of the methodology and results.
- The evaluation of the method is limited to a small dataset, which may not be representative of real-world applications.
- The paper lacks a detailed discussion on the generalizability of the proposed method to other dynamical systems, which is a critical aspect for broader applicability.
- There is a lack of comparison with more advanced neural network architectures, which could provide a better understanding of the model's effectiveness.

**Questions:**
- Could you provide a clearer definition and explanation of the decay trajectory of the Higgs boson?
- How does the proposed method compare to other machine learning methods in terms of performance and efficiency?
- Can you clarify the role and functionality of the different components of the reservoir-in-reservoir architecture, particularly the learner and generator reservoirs?
- How does the model handle the complex dynamics of the Higgs boson decay, and what insights does it provide into the physical properties of the decay process?
- Could you discuss the generalizability of the proposed method to other dynamical systems, and provide examples or scenarios where it could be applied?
- How does the model perform in terms of computational efficiency and resource requirements, especially in real-time applications?
