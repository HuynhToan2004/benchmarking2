 **Summary:**
The paper presents a novel approach using BERT for predicting bankruptcy risks based on the textual content of financial reports, specifically the annexes of annual accounts from the Luxembourg Business Registers. The methodology involves segmenting long texts into manageable chunks, processing them with BERT, and integrating the outputs for prediction. The paper claims that the proposed method achieves a precision of approximately 73% in predicting risky companies. However, the experimental setup is criticized for being too simple, lacking in baseline comparisons, and not providing sufficient detail on the dataset and model training. The paper also lacks clarity in its presentation and does not sufficiently address the societal impact of its work.

**Strengths:**
- The paper addresses an important and relevant problem, focusing on the prediction of bankruptcy using textual data from financial reports.
- The approach is innovative, using BERT for processing long textual data, which is a novel application in this domain.
- The paper is well-written and clear, making it accessible to a broad audience.
- The use of BERT to process financial statements and annual reports is a significant contribution, as it is a relatively new application.
- The dataset used is publicly available, enhancing the reproducibility and transparency of the research.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed description of the dataset, including its size, language distribution, and characteristics, which is crucial for understanding the scope and applicability of the study.
- The experimental setup is too simplistic, with only one baseline comparison and no discussion on the choice of parameters such as the number of segments and the LSTM layers.
- The paper does not adequately address the societal impact of its work, which is a significant omission in ethical and responsible AI research.
- The methodology is not clearly explained, particularly the segmentation process and the integration of BERT outputs, which could benefit from more detailed illustrations or explanations.
- The paper does not include a discussion on the limitations of the work, which is essential for understanding the scope and applicability of the findings.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors provide more details on the dataset, such as its size, language distribution, and characteristics?
- How were the segments defined in the segment analysis phase, and how were the outputs integrated in the integrated prediction phase?
- Could the authors clarify the experimental setup, particularly the choice of baseline and the rationale behind the parameter settings?
- How does the paper address the societal impact of its work, and what steps are being taken to ensure ethical and responsible AI practices?
- Could the authors provide a more detailed description of the methodology, including possibly a step-by-step guide or a more detailed illustration of the process?
