 **Summary:**
The paper introduces NeuralQP, a novel optimization framework for large-scale Quadratically Constrained Quadratic Programs (QCQPs) using a hypergraph-based approach. NeuralQP consists of two stages: Hypergraph-based Neural Prediction, which generates embeddings for QCQPs, and Iterative Neighborhood Optimization, which repairs solutions using a McCormick relaxation-based repair strategy. The framework is tested against state-of-the-art solvers like Gurobi and SCIP, demonstrating superior performance in terms of solution quality and computational efficiency. However, the paper is criticized for its lack of clarity in explaining the hypergraph-based approach, insufficient discussion on the scalability of the proposed method, and the absence of a broader experimental validation on real-world QCQP datasets.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel hypergraph-based approach for solving large-scale Quadratically Constrained Quadratic Programs (QCQPs), which is a significant advancement in the field of optimization.
- The proposed method, NeuralQP, is well-structured and clearly explained, with a detailed algorithm description and pseudocode provided in the appendix.
- The paper provides extensive experimental results demonstrating the effectiveness of NeuralQP compared to state-of-the-art solvers like Gurobi and SCIP.
- The use of a hypergraph-based approach to represent QCQPs is innovative and potentially more efficient than traditional graph-based methods.
- The paper is well-written, making it accessible and easy to follow, with clear explanations of the methodology and results.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks clarity in explaining the hypergraph-based approach, particularly how it differs from traditional graph-based methods and why it is more suitable for QCQP problems.
- There is a lack of discussion on the scalability of the proposed method, especially concerning the complexity of the hypergraph and the computational cost of hypergraph convolution.
- The experimental setup is limited to synthetic QCQP instances, which may not adequately represent real-world scenarios.
- The paper does not discuss the limitations of the proposed method, which is crucial for understanding its applicability and potential drawbacks.
- The paper could benefit from a more detailed discussion on the related work, especially in the context of other hypergraph-based optimization methods.
- The paper lacks a broader discussion on the societal impact of the proposed method, which is essential for understanding the broader implications of the research.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors provide a clearer explanation of how the hypergraph-based approach differs from traditional graph-based methods and why it is more suitable for QCQP problems?
- How does the size and complexity of the hypergraph affect the performance of NeuralQP? What are the computational costs associated with hypergraph convolution?
- Could the authors elaborate on the scalability of NeuralQP, particularly in terms of handling larger, more complex QCQP instances?
- Is there a specific reason why the experimental setup was limited to synthetic QCQP instances? How do the authors plan to validate the effectiveness of NeuralQP on real-world QCQP datasets?
- Could the authors discuss the limitations of the proposed method and how these might affect its applicability in different scenarios?
- How does NeuralQP compare to other hypergraph-based optimization methods, and what are the advantages of using a hypergraph-based approach over other methods?
