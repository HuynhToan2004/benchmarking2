 **Summary:**
The paper introduces the Spatial and Temporal Understanding of Prepositions Dataset (STUPD), a synthetic dataset designed to enhance the understanding of spatial and temporal relationships in visual scenes. The dataset, which includes both static and dynamic spatial prepositions and temporal relations, is constructed using a physics engine and Unity3D, providing 3D object interactions and bounding box annotations. The authors demonstrate that pre-training on STUPD can improve performance on various visual reasoning tasks, including SpatialSense and ImageNet-VidVRD. However, the paper has been criticized for its limited real-world applicability, lack of diversity in prepositions, and insufficient comparison with existing datasets.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel dataset, STUPD, which includes both static and dynamic spatial prepositions and temporal relations, providing a comprehensive resource for understanding visual relationships.
- The dataset is well-constructed using a physics engine and Unity3D, ensuring realistic and diverse object interactions, and includes 3D object interactions and bounding box annotations.
- The paper is well-written, with clear and comprehensive explanations of the dataset's construction and its intended use in pretraining models for visual reasoning tasks.
- The paper demonstrates the effectiveness of the dataset in improving performance on various visual reasoning tasks, such as SpatialSense and ImageNet-VidVRD, when pretrained on STUPD.
- The inclusion of temporal prepositions in the dataset is a significant contribution, as it addresses a gap in existing datasets.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed discussion on how the dataset's prepositions are selected, which could affect the dataset's generalization and applicability.
- The paper does not compare its dataset with existing datasets such as CLEVR, which could provide a clearer picture of the dataset's advantages and limitations.
- The paper does not include a comprehensive evaluation of the dataset's utility in real-world applications, which is crucial for demonstrating the practical value of the dataset.
- The paper's evaluation is limited to a few baseline models and datasets, which may not adequately demonstrate the dataset's effectiveness across a broader range of scenarios.
- The paper does not discuss the limitations of the dataset, such as the limited diversity of prepositions and the potential for biases in the dataset's construction.
- The paper's claims regarding the dataset's ability to enhance visual reasoning in real-world settings are not convincingly supported by empirical evidence.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors clarify the selection process for the prepositions included in the dataset and discuss how this affects the dataset's generalization and applicability?
- How does the dataset compare to existing datasets such as CLEVR in terms of its contribution to the field of visual relationship reasoning?
- Could the authors provide more details on the evaluation protocols used in the paper, particularly how the performance improvements are attributed to the dataset versus other factors?
- How does the dataset perform when evaluated on a broader range of models and datasets, particularly those that are not explicitly designed for visual reasoning tasks?
- Could the authors discuss the potential for bias in the dataset's construction and how this might affect the dataset's utility and generalizability?
- Can the authors provide more detailed examples and explanations of the dataset's construction and evaluation to better illustrate its contributions and limitations?
