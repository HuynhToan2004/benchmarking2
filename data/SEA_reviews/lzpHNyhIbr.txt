 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel dataset, CropNet, designed for crop yield predictions in the United States. This dataset integrates three modalities of data: Sentinel-2 imagery, WRF-HRRR computed datasets, and USDA crop datasets, covering 2291 counties from 2017 to 2022. The dataset is notable for its large size and multi-modal nature, which allows for the development of versatile deep learning models. The paper also provides APIs for accessing the dataset, facilitating its use in various research applications. Despite its strengths, the paper is criticized for its limited novelty, lack of detailed methodology, and insufficient discussion on the dataset's limitations and potential societal impacts.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a significant dataset, CropNet, which is a large-scale, multi-modal dataset that covers the entire United States from 2017 to 2022, providing a valuable resource for researchers in the field of crop yield prediction.
- The dataset is well-documented, with clear descriptions of the data sources and processing, making it accessible and usable by a wide range of researchers.
- The paper includes a comprehensive set of experiments using various deep learning models, demonstrating the versatility and applicability of the dataset in different predictive scenarios.
- The availability of the dataset and the CropNet package in open-source formats enhances its accessibility and utility for the broader research community.
- The paper is well-written, clear, and easy to follow, which is crucial for a dataset paper to effectively communicate its value and usage to potential users.

**Weaknesses:**
- The novelty of the dataset is limited, as it primarily aggregates existing datasets without significant additional processing or analysis.
- The paper lacks a detailed discussion on the limitations of the dataset, which is essential for users to understand the dataset's scope and applicability.
- There is a notable absence of a discussion on the potential societal impacts of the dataset, which is crucial for understanding the broader implications of its use.
- The paper does not provide a detailed methodology for using the dataset, which could hinder its adoption by less experienced researchers or practitioners.
- The dataset's utility is limited by its focus on a specific region (the United States) and specific crops, which might not be generalizable to other agricultural contexts or crops.
- The paper's experimental setup and results lack depth and clarity, particularly in the description of the models used and the results presented.
