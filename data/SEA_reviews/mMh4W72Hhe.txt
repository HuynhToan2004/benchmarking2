 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel method for reducing the number of subproblems in neural network verification by utilizing bound implications among neurons. This method is designed to improve the efficiency of branch-and-bound (BaB) algorithms by identifying dependencies between neurons, which can be used to prune branches in the verification process. The method is applied to various benchmarks, including VeriHard, and shows promising results in reducing the number of branches and improving verification times. The approach is based on an optimization formulation that considers bound implications between neurons from arbitrary layers, which is a significant advancement over previous methods that only considered implications within the same or consecutive layers. The paper also proposes a scalable method for solving optimization problems among neurons, which is less restrictive and produces more bound implications compared to prior work.
