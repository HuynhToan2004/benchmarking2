 **Summary:**
The paper introduces S-TLLR, a novel three-factor temporal local learning rule inspired by the Spike-Timing Dependent Plasticity (STDP) mechanism, designed to train deep Spiking Neural Networks (SNNs) on event-based learning tasks. S-TLLR aims to address the challenges of training SNNs by utilizing a combination of causal and non-causal relationships, which is claimed to enhance generalization and task performance. The method is intended to reduce memory and computational requirements, making it suitable for online learning on low-power edge devices. The paper presents experimental results across various datasets, demonstrating the effectiveness of S-TLLR in achieving high accuracy with reduced memory and computational demands compared to traditional methods like BPTT.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel temporal local learning rule, S-TLLR, for spiking neural networks, which is inspired by the STDP mechanism and addresses the challenges of training SNNs by utilizing both causal and non-causal relationships.
- The methodology is well-explained, and the paper is easy to follow, making it accessible to a broad audience.
- The paper presents a clear motivation for the research and provides a comprehensive background on the challenges of training SNNs.
- The experimental results demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed learning rule across a diverse range of network topologies, including VGG, U-Net-like, and recurrent architectures.
- The paper provides a detailed comparison of the computational and memory requirements of S-TLLR with other methods, highlighting its efficiency.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed explanation of the non-causal term in the learning rule and its impact on performance, which could be crucial for understanding the effectiveness of the proposed method.
- The experimental results are not convincing enough, particularly in the accuracy comparisons with existing methods like BPTT and other local learning rules.
- The paper does not provide sufficient evidence to support the claim that the method is suitable for online learning on low-power edge devices, which is a significant claim.
- The paper could benefit from more comprehensive comparisons with other local learning rules and a broader range of datasets to strengthen the claims of the method's effectiveness and generalizability.
- There are inconsistencies in the notation and definitions used in the paper, which could lead to confusion and misunderstanding.

**Questions:**
- Can you provide a detailed explanation of the non-causal term in the learning rule and its impact on performance?
- How does the proposed method compare to other local learning rules in terms of accuracy and computational efficiency?
- Can you provide more evidence to support the claim that the method is suitable for online learning on low-power edge devices?
- Why is the method named S-TLLR, and what does it mean in the context of the paper?
- Could you clarify the inconsistencies in the notation and definitions used in the paper?
- How does the method perform on more complex datasets, and what are the implications for its generalizability?
