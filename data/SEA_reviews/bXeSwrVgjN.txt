 **Summary:**
The paper introduces the TRACE framework, a methodology aimed at optimizing feature attribution by addressing the limitations of existing metrics like MoRF and LeRF. The authors propose a new methodology that systematically evaluates the impact of feature deletion on model predictions, aiming to improve the consistency and interpretability of model explanations. The paper also includes a benchmarking study across various settings and scenarios, providing insights into the application of these metrics in practice. The methodology is supported by a theoretical analysis and empirical evidence, demonstrating its potential to enhance the understanding and evaluation of model explanations.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel methodology, TRACE, for evaluating feature attribution, which is a significant contribution to the field of model explanation.
- The authors provide a theoretical analysis of the limitations of existing metrics and propose a new approach to address these issues.
- The paper is well-written, clear, and easy to follow, making it accessible to a broad audience.
- The proposed methodology is evaluated across various settings and scenarios, providing a comprehensive analysis of its effectiveness and applicability.
- The paper includes a benchmarking study that helps in understanding the performance of different models under different conditions, contributing to the practical applicability of the proposed methodology.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper could benefit from a more detailed discussion on the computational complexity of the proposed methodology, particularly the optimization problem and the impact of different feature sizes.
- The paper does not sufficiently address the limitations of the proposed methodology, such as its scalability and the potential for overfitting to specific datasets.
- The paper lacks a broader comparison with existing methods, particularly those that address similar issues in model explanation, which could help in positioning the proposed methodology within the existing literature.
- The paper's focus on image datasets might limit its applicability to other types of data, such as text or tabular data.
- The paper could benefit from a more detailed discussion on the implications of the proposed methodology for different types of models, such as transformer-based models, which are increasingly popular in various applications.
