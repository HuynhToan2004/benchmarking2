 **Summary:**
The paper introduces RePLan, a novel framework that integrates vision language models (VLMs) with large language models (LLMs) to enable real-time replanning capabilities for long-horizon tasks in robotics. The system utilizes VLMs to perceive the environment and generate high-level plans, which are then refined into low-level reward functions by LLMs. These reward functions guide the robot's motion planning and execution, with a focus on handling unforeseen obstacles and achieving long-term goals. The paper presents a comprehensive evaluation across multiple tasks, demonstrating RePLan's effectiveness compared to existing methods. However, concerns were raised about the clarity of the paper, the depth of the experimental validation, and the generalizability of the approach.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel approach that effectively integrates large language models (LLMs) and vision language models (VLMs) to enhance robot planning and execution.
- The framework is well-structured, with a clear presentation of the methodology and results, which facilitates understanding and reproducibility.
- The evaluation is thorough, demonstrating the effectiveness of the proposed method across a range of tasks.
- The paper provides detailed examples of the proposed method's application, which aids in understanding its functionality and potential.
- The approach is innovative, combining the strengths of LLMs and VLMs to address the challenges of long-horizon tasks in robotics.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper suffers from poor writing, with unclear descriptions and insufficient details, which hampers the understanding of the methodology and its implementation.
- The experimental setup is limited, with only a few tasks evaluated, which does not convincingly demonstrate the generalizability of the approach.
- The paper lacks a detailed discussion on the limitations and potential negative societal impacts of the proposed method.
- The paper does not adequately compare its method with existing works, particularly those that have explored similar aspects of robot planning and execution.
- The paper's reliance on specific models, such as GPT-4 and Qwen-VL-Chat-7B, may limit its applicability to other scenarios or models, which could affect its scalability and adaptability.
- The paper does not provide sufficient details on the training and implementation of the LLM and VLM models, which are crucial for replication and further research.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors clarify how the system handles situations where the generated plan does not match the actual environment, especially when the plan is based on incorrect object recognition or state estimation?
- How does the system ensure the robustness of the replanning process when there are significant discrepancies between the planned actions and the actual execution?
- Could the authors provide more details on the training and implementation of the LLM and VLM models, including the datasets used and the specifics of the training procedures?
- How does the system handle the trade-off between exploration and exploitation in long-horizon tasks, and what mechanisms are in place to prevent the agent from getting stuck in suboptimal solutions?
- The paper mentions the use of GPT-4 and Qwen-VL-Chat-7B models. Could the authors discuss the potential implications of using different models or open-source alternatives, and how this might affect the performance and applicability of the proposed method?
