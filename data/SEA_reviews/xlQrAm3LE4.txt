 **Summary:**
The paper introduces DiffSim, a hybrid approach integrating diffusion models with molecular dynamics (MD) simulations for blind docking. DiffSim uses a novel loss function to align reverse diffusion sampling with MD trajectories, enhancing the accuracy of ligand conformations. This integration leverages the strengths of both methods, with the diffusion model providing initial conformation sampling and MD simulations refining these conformations based on protein-ligand interactions. The method is evaluated on the PDBBind dataset, showing improvements over existing methods. However, concerns are raised about the method's novelty, the complexity of the MD simulations, and the need for a broader evaluation on larger and more diverse datasets.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, clear, and easy to follow, making it accessible to a broad audience.
- The integration of diffusion models with molecular dynamics (MD) simulations is a novel and innovative approach, offering a new perspective on the application of these models in blind docking.
- The proposed loss function is well-designed and effectively guides the diffusion model to generate conformations that align with those from MD simulations.
- The theoretical analysis provided offers a deeper understanding of the dynamics between diffusion models and MD simulations, enhancing the paper's scientific rigor.
- The method shows improvements over existing models in terms of commonly used evaluation metrics, indicating its effectiveness.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed comparison with other state-of-the-art models, which could have provided a clearer picture of DiffSim's advantages and disadvantages.
- The method's reliance on MD simulations for refining conformations might limit its applicability in scenarios where MD simulations are not feasible or practical.
- The paper's experimental setup and results are somewhat limited, focusing only on a subset of the PDBBind dataset and not including larger or more diverse datasets which could better demonstrate the method's generalizability.
- The paper's claims about the method's novelty and its theoretical contributions are not convincingly supported by the empirical results, raising questions about the practical benefits of the proposed method.
- The paper's presentation of results and methodology could be improved, particularly in terms of clarity and detail, especially in the description of the MD simulation setup and the experimental results.

**Questions:**
- Could you provide a more detailed comparison with other state-of-the-art models, including DiffDock, TANKBind, and EQUIBIND, to better understand the advantages and disadvantages of your method?
- How does the proposed method perform on larger and more diverse datasets, such as those from the CASF benchmark?
- Can you clarify the method's dependence on MD simulations and discuss potential strategies for reducing this dependence, such as using more advanced or specialized MD simulations?
- How does the method handle scenarios where the MD simulations are not feasible or practical, and what are the implications for the method's applicability in such cases?
- Could you provide a more detailed description of the MD simulation setup, including the specific conditions and parameters used, to better understand the experimental conditions and results?
