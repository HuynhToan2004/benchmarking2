 **Summary:**
The paper explores the generation of differentially private synthetic data using a pre-trained language model, specifically focusing on text classification tasks. It employs a novel approach by fine-tuning the model on a sensitive dataset while ensuring privacy through differential privacy techniques. The paper evaluates various methods such as full fine-tuning, prompt tuning, and LoRA tuning, demonstrating that LoRA tuning provides the best results in terms of privacy and utility. The authors also address the issue of privacy leakage by ensuring that the pre-training data does not overlap with the sensitive dataset, thus maintaining the privacy guarantees. The paper includes extensive experimental results and discussions on the utility of synthetic data, comparing it with traditional differential privacy methods.

**Strengths:**
- The paper addresses a timely and important problem of generating synthetic data while ensuring differential privacy, which is crucial for maintaining data privacy in various applications.
- The authors conduct extensive experiments and provide a detailed discussion on the utility of synthetic data, which is a significant contribution to the field.
- The paper is well-written, clear, and easy to follow, making it accessible to a broad audience.
- The proposed methods, such as LoRA tuning, demonstrate promising results, showing that it is a viable approach for generating differentially private synthetic data.
- The paper is original in its approach, providing a novel perspective on the use of differentially private language models for generating synthetic data.
- The paper is well-motivated and addresses a significant gap in the literature by providing a clear and comprehensive explanation of the methodology and its implications.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed discussion on the privacy guarantees provided by the differentially private language model, which is a critical aspect of the research.
- There is an absence of a theoretical analysis on the utility of the synthetic data, which could strengthen the paper's claims.
- The paper does not sufficiently discuss the computational costs and limitations of the proposed methods, which could affect the practical implementation of the solutions.
- The paper does not compare its methods with other existing methods, such as GAN-based approaches, which could provide a more comprehensive evaluation of the proposed solutions.
- The paper does not address the issue of privacy leakage from the pre-training data, which is a significant concern in the context of differential privacy.
- The paper could benefit from a more thorough discussion on the privacy guarantees and the implications of the privacy budget allocation.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors provide a more detailed discussion on the privacy guarantees provided by the differentially private language model?
- How does the proposed method compare in terms of utility and privacy with other existing methods, such as GAN-based approaches?
- Could the authors elaborate on the computational costs and limitations of the proposed methods, particularly in terms of scalability and practical implementation?
- How does the paper address the issue of privacy leakage from the pre-training data, and what steps are taken to ensure that the synthetic data does not reveal sensitive information?
- Could the authors provide a more detailed explanation of the privacy budget allocation and its implications on the utility and privacy of the synthetic data?
- How does the paper ensure that the synthetic data generated is not biased or discriminatory, particularly in terms of fairness and non-discrimination?
