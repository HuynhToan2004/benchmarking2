 **Summary:**
The paper explores the robustness of predictive models under missing data scenarios, focusing on the impact of different missing data mechanisms on predictive performance. It introduces a novel method, NeuMISE, which aims to improve the handling of missing data by adjusting the imputation process based on the missingness mechanism. The paper also discusses the theoretical implications of missingness shifts and their impact on predictive accuracy, particularly under non-ignorable missingness shifts. Empirical results demonstrate that NeuMISE outperforms other methods in some scenarios, although the paper is criticized for its lack of clarity in explaining the theoretical contributions and the practical implications of the proposed methods.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, making complex concepts accessible and understandable.
- The problem of handling missing data in machine learning models is addressed, which is highly relevant and timely.
- The authors provide a detailed analysis of the robustness of predictive models under different missing data mechanisms, including MCAR, MAR, and MNAR.
- The paper introduces a novel method, NeuMISE, which shows promising results in empirical evaluations, particularly in handling missing data.
- The theoretical framework developed in the paper is comprehensive and well-articulated, providing a solid foundation for further research in the field.
- The paper addresses an important and relevant problem in machine learning, which is the handling of missing data in real-world applications.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks clarity in explaining the theoretical contributions, particularly the relationship between missingness shifts and the Bayes predictor.
- The paper could benefit from a more detailed discussion on the practical implications of the findings, especially in terms of their applicability to real-world scenarios.
- The experimental results presented are not convincingly robust, with some results showing inconsistencies and potential errors.
- The paper does not adequately address the limitations of the proposed methods, such as the assumptions made about the data-generating process and the potential biases introduced by the missing data mechanisms.
- The paper could benefit from a more comprehensive literature review, especially in the context of missing data mechanisms and their impact on predictive accuracy.
- The paper lacks a discussion on the broader implications of the findings, such as the societal impact of the proposed methods.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors clarify the relationship between missingness shifts and the Bayes predictor, particularly in terms of how the shifts affect the predictive accuracy?
- How does the proposed method, NeuMISE, compare to other existing methods in terms of computational efficiency and practical applicability?
- Could the authors provide more detailed examples or case studies to illustrate the practical implications of their findings?
- How do the authors address the potential biases introduced by the missing data mechanisms, and what steps can be taken to mitigate these biases in real-world applications?
- What are the specific assumptions made about the data-generating process, and how do these assumptions affect the validity of the results?
- Can the authors provide a more detailed discussion on the limitations of their methods and the potential areas for future research?
