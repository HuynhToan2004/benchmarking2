 **Summary:**
The paper introduces ESP, a novel method for predicting cryptic binding pockets in proteins using Protein Language Models (PLMs). ESP leverages pre-trained PLMs to extract features from protein sequences, which are then used to predict cryptic pockets. The model's performance is evaluated using the PocketMiner test set, showing superior results compared to existing methods. The paper explores the use of PLMs in predicting cryptic pockets, demonstrating the utility of these models in identifying cryptic pockets without the need for molecular dynamics simulations. However, the paper's scope is limited to a single test set, and the method's generalizability and scalability are not thoroughly evaluated.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, clear, and easy to follow, with a clear motivation and a straightforward approach to the problem.
- The methodology is innovative, using pre-trained protein language models (PLMs) to predict cryptic pockets, which is a novel application in the field.
- The paper demonstrates the utility of PLMs in predicting cryptic pockets without the need for molecular dynamics simulations, which is a significant advancement in the field.
- The results show that the proposed method outperforms existing methods, providing a significant contribution to the field.

**Weaknesses:**
