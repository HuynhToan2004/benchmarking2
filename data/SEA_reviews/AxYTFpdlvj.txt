 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel decoder approach for graph tasks using the Generalized Random Dot Product Graph (GRDPG) as a generative model for graph decoding. This method aims to enhance the performance of encoder-decoder architectures by capturing intricate structures within adjacency matrices more effectively. The authors propose a new benchmark for molecular graphs to evaluate the effectiveness of their approach, showcasing its ability to preserve the structural integrity of original graphs while improving performance metrics. Despite the innovative approach, the paper's contribution is questioned due to its limited novelty and experimental validation, as it heavily relies on existing methods like GRDPG and does not sufficiently differentiate its experimental setup from previous works.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel approach using GRDPG for graph decoding, which is a significant contribution to the field of graph neural networks (GNNs).
- The paper is well-written, with clear and concise explanations that make it accessible to readers.
- The experimental results demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed method in improving the performance of encoder-decoder architectures.
- The paper addresses the limitations of existing decoders in GNNs by proposing a new method that captures the intricate structures within adjacency matrices more effectively.
- The motivation behind the research is clear, and the paper provides a comprehensive review of the background and related work.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed comparison with other state-of-the-art methods for graph decoding, which could provide a better understanding of the proposed method's advantages and disadvantages.
- The novelty of the paper is questioned as the GRDPG method used is not new, and the experimental validation does not sufficiently differentiate it from previous works.
- The paper's contribution is seen as limited as it heavily relies on existing methods like GRDPG without significantly advancing the state of the art.
- The experimental results are not convincing enough, particularly in larger datasets, where the performance of the proposed method is not significantly better than existing methods.
- The paper's focus on molecular graphs limits its applicability to other types of graphs, and the benchmark used for evaluation is not sufficiently diverse or challenging.
- The paper's writing could be improved for clarity and coherence, particularly in the introduction and methodology sections.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors provide a more detailed comparison with other state-of-the-art methods for graph decoding to better understand the advantages and disadvantages of the proposed method?
- How does the proposed method perform in larger datasets, and what are the implications for its scalability?
- Could the authors elaborate on the differences between the proposed method and existing methods like GRDPG, particularly in terms of the experimental setup and results?
- How does the proposed method handle the limitations of existing decoders in GNNs, and what specific improvements does it offer?
- Can the authors provide more detailed experimental results, including standard deviations and error bars, to better support the claims made in the paper?
- How does the proposed method perform on other types of graphs beyond molecular graphs, and what are the implications for its applicability across different domains?
