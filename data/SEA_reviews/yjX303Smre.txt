 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel approach to contextual reinforcement learning using a mixture of experts (MoE) with energy-based models for context distributions and trust-region layers for expert parameterization. The methodology aims to learn diverse skills in environments with continuous contexts, employing automatic curriculum learning to optimize context distributions. The approach is evaluated against existing methods on various robotics tasks, demonstrating its ability to learn diverse skills and outperform baseline methods in some cases. However, the paper is criticized for its lack of clarity in presentation and the need for a more rigorous comparison with other recent methods. Concerns about the method's scalability, especially in high-dimensional context spaces, and the complexity of the training process are also raised.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, with clear and concise explanations of the method and the experiments, making it easy to follow.
- The proposed method is novel, integrating energy-based models for context distribution and trust-region layers for expert parameterization, which are not commonly used in similar studies.
- The experimental results show that the proposed method outperforms existing methods, demonstrating its effectiveness.
- The paper provides a comprehensive review of related work, highlighting the novelty of the proposed approach.
- The use of energy-based models (EBMs) for context distribution is a significant contribution, as it allows for the representation of complex and multi-modal distributions, which is crucial for diverse skill learning.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks clarity in presentation, particularly in the explanation of the methodology and the experimental results. There are numerous typos and grammatical errors that need correction.
- The paper does not sufficiently compare the proposed method with other recent relevant methods, such as those mentioned in the review of related work.
- The method's scalability is questionable, especially in high-dimensional context spaces, where the number of experts required for effective learning might be prohibitively large.
- The paper does not adequately address the issue of replanning, which is crucial for handling unexpected events or changes in the environment.
- The paper's experimental setup and results are not sufficiently robust or convincing, with some results showing marginal improvements over existing methods.
- The paper lacks a detailed discussion on the limitations and potential negative societal impacts of the proposed method.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors clarify the role and impact of the energy-based model (EBM) in the proposed method, particularly in terms of its effectiveness in handling complex and multi-modal context distributions?
- How does the proposed method compare with other recent relevant methods, such as those mentioned in the review of related work?
- In the context of diverse skill learning, how does the proposed method ensure that the learned skills are not only diverse but also applicable in varied contexts?
- What are the specific advantages of using trust-region layers for expert parameterization over other optimization methods?
- How does the proposed method handle the issue of replanning, which is crucial for adapting to unexpected events or changes in the environment?
- Can the authors provide more detailed results and comparisons, particularly in the box pushing environment, to better demonstrate the effectiveness of their method?
