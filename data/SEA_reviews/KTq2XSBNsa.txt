 **Summary:**
The paper introduces MOESART, a novel k-sparse routing approach for Mixture-of-Experts (MoE) that maintains k-sparsity during both training and inference. This method aims to address the limitations of existing routers by ensuring sparsity and mitigating performance issues. MOESART achieves this by learning a good k-sparse approximation of the classical softmax router through sampling and expert weighting strategies. The approach is evaluated across various datasets in recommender systems, vision, and natural language processing, showing improvements over existing k-sparse routers. Despite its innovative approach, concerns are raised about the clarity of the paper, the selection of datasets, and the theoretical underpinnings of the method.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel k-sparse routing approach, MOESART, which maintains k-sparsity during both training and inference, allowing for conditional training and inference.
- MOESART outperforms existing k-sparse routers on various datasets in recommender systems, vision, and natural language processing, demonstrating its effectiveness.
- The paper is well-written and easy to follow, providing a clear exposition of the methodology and results.
- The proposed method is simple and intuitive, with a clear objective that is easy to understand and implement.
- The experiments are comprehensive and convincing, demonstrating the effectiveness of the proposed method across different domains.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks clarity in some sections, particularly in the descriptions of the methodology and the experimental setup.
- The choice of datasets and their relevance to the research question is questionable, as some datasets are not well-suited for the task of sparse MoE.
- The paper does not adequately compare its method with other relevant baselines, such as those that use optimal transport for sparse MoE.
- The theoretical underpinnings of the method, particularly the use of the softmax function and the k-sparsity constraints, are not well-explained.
- The paper could benefit from more detailed explanations and examples to clarify the complexities involved in implementing the method.
- The paper's claims about the method's effectiveness are not convincingly supported by the experimental results, which show only marginal improvements over existing methods.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors clarify the choice of datasets and their relevance to the research question?
- How does the proposed method compare with other relevant baselines, such as those that use optimal transport for sparse MoE?
- Can the authors provide more detailed explanations and examples to clarify the complexities involved in implementing the method?
- How does the proposed method address the selection bias prevalent in Top-k based selection approaches?
- Can the authors provide more theoretical insights into the use of the softmax function and the k-sparsity constraints in their method?
- How does the proposed method perform in terms of computational efficiency and practical applicability, especially in large-scale MoE models?
