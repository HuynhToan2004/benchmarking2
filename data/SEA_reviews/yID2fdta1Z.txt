 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel approach, Robust Unbiased Aggregation (RUNG), to enhance the robustness of Graph Neural Networks (GNNs) against adversarial attacks. The authors argue that existing robust GNNs, such as SoftMedian, TWIRLS, and ElasticGNN, are vulnerable to large attack budgets due to an accumulated estimation bias, which they address by proposing a robust and unbiased graph signal estimator. The paper also introduces a Quasi-Newton IRLS algorithm to solve the non-smooth and non-convex estimation problem, offering a theoretical convergence guarantee. The proposed method is tested against various attacks and is shown to outperform existing defenses, particularly under large attack budgets.

**Strengths:**
- The paper provides a comprehensive study on the limitations of existing defenses and offers a unified view of ℓ1-based robust graph signal smoothing to justify the improved and closely aligned robustness of representative robust GNNs.
- It introduces a novel robust and unbiased graph signal estimator to mitigate the estimation bias in ℓ1-based graph signal smoothing and designs an efficient Quasi-Newton IRLS algorithm to solve the non-smooth and non-convex estimation problem with a theoretical convergence guarantee.
- The paper provides extensive experiments demonstrating that the proposed GNN significantly improves the robustness against large-budget adaptive attacks, outperforming the best existing model by 16% and 28.6% under local attack of budget 100% and 200% on Cora ML.
- The proposed algorithm can be readily unfolded as feature aggregation layers in GNNs, providing clear interpretability and covering many classic GNNs as special cases.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a comprehensive comparison with other robust GNNs, particularly those that have not been evaluated in the experiments.
- The robustness of the proposed method is only tested on two datasets, which might not be sufficient to validate its effectiveness across different scenarios.
- The paper does not provide a detailed comparison of the computational complexity and memory requirements of the proposed method against existing robust GNNs.
- The paper does not discuss the limitations of the proposed method, which could provide a more balanced view of its contributions and potential challenges.
- The paper could benefit from a more detailed analysis of the convergence of the proposed method and its sensitivity to hyperparameters.
- The paper lacks a discussion on the limitations of the proposed method and its potential negative societal impact.

**Questions:**
- How does the proposed method perform against other robust GNNs that have not been evaluated in the experiments?
- Can the authors provide a detailed comparison of the computational complexity and memory requirements of the proposed method against existing robust GNNs?
- How sensitive is the proposed method to the choice of hyperparameters, and what are the potential implications of this sensitivity in practical applications?
- Could the authors provide a more detailed analysis of the convergence of the proposed method and its sensitivity to hyperparameters?
- What are the potential limitations of the proposed method, and how might these limitations affect its practical applicability?
- How does the proposed method compare in terms of computational efficiency and accuracy with other state-of-the-art GNNs, especially under different attack scenarios?
