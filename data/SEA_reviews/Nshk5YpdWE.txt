 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel method for modeling fluid dynamics using Lagrangian flow networks (LFlows), a type of normalizing flow that satisfies the continuity equation. The method models the velocity and density of fluids by transforming a base density with parameterized diffeomorphisms conditioned on time. This approach is computationally efficient and demonstrates superior predictive accuracy compared to existing methods in both 2D and 3D scenarios. The method is validated on both synthetic and real-world data, including bird migration patterns derived from weather radar measurements, showing better performance than traditional methods like PINNs. However, the paper's presentation and clarity need improvement, with concerns regarding the experimental setup, the novelty of the method, and the comparison with existing works.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, clear, and easy to follow, with a clear presentation of the methodology and results.
- The proposed method is innovative and well-motivated, providing a novel approach to modeling fluid dynamics using normalizing flows, which is computationally efficient and shows superior predictive accuracy compared to existing methods.
- The method is applicable to both synthetic and real-world data, demonstrating its versatility and practical utility.
- The paper provides a detailed mathematical proof and a clear explanation of the methodology, making it accessible to readers with varying backgrounds in the field.
- The experiments conducted are thorough and demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed method, showing a clear improvement over existing methods in certain scenarios.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed comparison with existing works, particularly with those that use similar approaches such as SLDA and divergence-free neural networks (DFNNs).
- The presentation of the methodology and the experimental setup could be improved to enhance clarity and reproducibility. More detailed explanations and visual aids are needed to better understand the method and its implementation.
- The paper's novelty is questioned as it appears to be a simple extension of existing methods, with some elements being similar to those in other works.
- The experimental setup and the choice of hyperparameters are not adequately justified, which could affect the reliability and generalizability of the results.
- The paper does not adequately address the limitations of the method, particularly concerning the assumptions made about the initial conditions and the physical constraints of the fluid dynamics.
- The paper could benefit from a more rigorous validation of the results, especially in scenarios where the method's assumptions are not met.

**Questions:**
- How does the proposed method compare to SLDA and DFNNs in terms of computational efficiency and predictive accuracy?
- Can the authors provide a more detailed comparison of the results with existing methods, particularly in the bird migration experiment?
- How does the method perform in scenarios where the initial conditions are not known or not well-defined?
- Could the authors clarify the role and impact of the base density in the model's performance?
- What are the implications of using a normalizing flow as a model for fluid dynamics, and how does this compare to other models like PINNs or SLDA?
- How are the hyperparameters chosen in the experiments, and what is the impact of these choices on the results?
- Can the authors provide more details on the experimental setup, particularly the choice of initial conditions and the validation of the results?
