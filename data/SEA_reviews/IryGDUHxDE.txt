 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel method for zero-shot action recognition using an autoregressive Vision-Language model, specifically BLIP. The approach involves a self-training mechanism where the model generates pseudo-captions from its own outputs, which are then refined using CLIP-based retrieval. The method is evaluated on various datasets such as Kinetics-400 and HMDB-51, showing competitive results against existing methods. The paper also explores the use of a retrieval mechanism to enhance the diversity and accuracy of the generated captions. Despite the innovative approach, the paper has been critiqued for its limited novelty, lack of comprehensive comparisons with state-of-the-art methods, and insufficient analysis of the impact of the retrieval mechanism.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel method for zero-shot action recognition using an autoregressive Vision-Language model, which is a significant departure from traditional methods.
- The methodology is clearly explained, making it easy to follow and understand the proposed approach.
- The paper is well-organized and includes extensive ablation studies and analysis, which support the effectiveness of the proposed method.
- The results demonstrate that the proposed method achieves state-of-the-art performance on various benchmarks, indicating its effectiveness.
- The paper is well-written, making it accessible and understandable to readers.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed analysis of the retrieval mechanism, which is a critical component of the proposed method. More insights into how this mechanism contributes to the overall performance would be beneficial.
- There is a lack of comprehensive comparisons with state-of-the-art methods, particularly in the few-shot setting. Including more recent and relevant comparisons would strengthen the paper's claims.
- The paper does not sufficiently address the issue of overfitting, which is a significant concern in zero-shot learning. More detailed analysis and mitigation strategies are needed.
- The paper could benefit from a more thorough evaluation, including additional metrics and a broader range of datasets to validate the proposed method's effectiveness.
- The paper lacks a discussion on the limitations of the proposed method, which is essential for a complete understanding of its applicability and potential drawbacks.
- The paper does not include a discussion on the computational efficiency of the proposed method, which is crucial for practical deployment.

- How does the proposed method compare to other state-of-the-art methods in terms of computational efficiency and resource requirements?
- Can you include more recent and relevant comparisons in the few-shot setting to strengthen the paper's claims?
- How does the proposed method address the issue of overfitting, which is a significant concern in zero-shot learning?
- Could you include more detailed analyses and mitigation strategies to address the issue of overfitting?
