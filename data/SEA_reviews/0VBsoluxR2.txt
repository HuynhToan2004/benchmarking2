 **Summary:**
The paper introduces MoFDiff, a novel diffusion model designed to generate coarse-grained Metal-Organic Frameworks (MOFs) through a denoising diffusion process. The model leverages a contrastive learning approach to represent building blocks, which are then assembled using a novel algorithm to recover atomic MOF structures. The method's effectiveness is demonstrated by generating valid and novel MOFs with optimized properties for carbon capture applications, showcasing a significant reduction in the number of GCMC simulations required compared to traditional methods. Despite its innovative approach, the paper faces criticisms regarding its novelty, the depth of experimental validation, and the clarity of its presentation.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel method for generating coarse-grained MOFs using a diffusion model, which is innovative and potentially beneficial for material design.
- The use of contrastive learning to represent building blocks is a novel and effective approach, which enhances the model's ability to generate valid and novel MOF structures.
- The method is evaluated on a challenging task, specifically for carbon capture applications, demonstrating its potential practical utility.
- The paper is well-written and provides a clear explanation of the methodology, making it accessible to readers.
- The approach shows promise in reducing the number of GCMC simulations required to find MOFs with desirable properties, indicating potential efficiency and cost savings.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper's novelty is questioned as it primarily applies existing methods to a new problem, without significant innovation in the underlying techniques.
- The experimental validation is limited, with only a single task evaluated, which may not fully demonstrate the general applicability of the method.
- The paper lacks a thorough comparison with other state-of-the-art methods, which could have provided a better understanding of the model's relative performance.
- The presentation of the paper could be improved, with some figures and text being unclear or difficult to understand, which might hinder comprehension.
- The paper's reliance on a coarse-grained representation raises concerns about the accuracy and completeness of the generated MOF structures, especially in terms of their physical properties and the chemical reactions they might undergo.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors clarify the novelty of their approach and how it differs from existing methods in terms of generating coarse-grained MOFs?
- How does the model perform when applied to other MOF-related tasks beyond carbon capture?
- What is the impact of the coarse-grained representation on the accuracy and completeness of the generated MOF structures?
- Can the authors provide more details on the experimental setup, particularly the validation and novelty criteria used to assess the generated MOFs?
- How does the model handle the physical properties of the generated MOFs, and what steps are taken to ensure their validity and applicability in real-world scenarios?
- Could the authors provide a more detailed comparison with other state-of-the-art methods to better establish the relative performance of their model?
