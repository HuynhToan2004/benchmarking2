 **Summary:**
The paper investigates the capabilities of transformer models in handling arithmetic tasks, focusing on positional encoding and data format modifications to improve performance. The authors explore how transformers process arithmetic operations by examining their reliance on positional information and proposing modifications such as randomized embeddings and data formatting to enhance model performance. The paper presents experiments on various tasks including multiplication and addition, demonstrating that these modifications can lead to significant improvements in model accuracy. However, the paper is criticized for its limited scope, focusing on small models and a narrow range of arithmetic tasks, which may not generalize well to more complex or varied scenarios.
