 **Summary:**
The paper introduces AlignDiff, a novel framework designed to align agent behaviors with diverse human preferences using a combination of RLHF and diffusion models. AlignDiff quantifies human preferences through a multi-perspective human feedback dataset and an attribute strength model, which guides diffusion planning for zero-shot behavior customization. The framework is evaluated on various locomotion tasks and demonstrates superior performance in preference matching, switching, and covering compared to other baselines. Despite its innovative approach, the paper has been criticized for its clarity in presentation and the complexity of its methodology, which may hinder its accessibility to a broader audience.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel framework, AlignDiff, which aligns agent behaviors with diverse human preferences using RLHF and diffusion models, demonstrating significant performance improvements over existing methods.
- The creation of multi-perspective human feedback datasets through crowdsourcing is a valuable contribution, providing a robust evaluation benchmark for future research in this area.
- The paper is well-written, clear, and easy to follow, making it accessible to a broad audience.
- The framework is designed to handle abstractness and mutability of human preferences effectively, which is a significant challenge in the field of RLHF.
- The experiments conducted on various locomotion tasks showcase the superior performance of AlignDiff, demonstrating its practical applicability.
- The paper provides a comprehensive evaluation of the framework, including preference matching, switching, and covering, which are crucial aspects of RLHF.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed discussion on the limitations of the proposed framework, which could provide valuable insights for future research.
- The complexity of the framework, involving multiple components such as RLHF, diffusion models, and attribute strength models, might make it challenging for non-experts to understand and replicate.
- The paper could benefit from more clarity in presentation, particularly in the explanation of the algorithm and the use of complex mathematical notations.
- There is a lack of comparison with other advanced RLHF methods, which could help in understanding the relative advantages of AlignDiff.
- The evaluation is limited to locomotion tasks, and it would be beneficial to test the framework in more complex and varied environments to demonstrate its generalizability.
- The paper does not discuss the potential negative societal impacts of the framework, which could be a concern for some readers.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors provide more details on the limitations of the proposed framework and how these might affect its practical application?
- How does the framework handle scenarios where human preferences are not clearly defined or are in conflict?
- Can the authors clarify the role and impact of the attribute strength model in the overall performance of the framework?
- How does the framework handle the complexity of human preferences, especially when they are not clearly defined or are in conflict?
- Could the authors discuss the potential negative societal impacts of the framework and how these might be mitigated?
- How does the framework ensure the fairness and accuracy of the human evaluations, especially when these are conducted remotely?
- Could the authors provide more details on the computational costs associated with using diffusion models for RLHF?
- How does the framework handle the challenge of aligning agent behaviors with diverse human preferences, especially when these preferences are complex or evolving?
