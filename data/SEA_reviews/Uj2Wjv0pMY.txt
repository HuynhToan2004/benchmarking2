 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a new dataset of egocentric videos featuring cooking activities, focusing on errors and their categorization. The dataset includes 384 recordings, providing both coarse-grained and fine-grained action annotations, and is designed to aid in the development of AI systems for error recognition and anticipation in procedural activities. The dataset is intended to advance research in this area by providing a benchmark for error recognition and multi-step localization tasks. Despite its strengths in dataset collection and annotation, the paper is criticized for its lack of novelty in methodology, insufficient comparison with existing datasets, and inadequate evaluation of its contributions.

**Strengths:**
- The dataset is well-collected with 384 recordings and 5.3K step annotations, which is a significant contribution to the field.
- The paper provides a detailed description of the dataset, including its construction, annotation, and evaluation, which is essential for understanding its utility and applicability.
- The dataset includes both normal and error recordings, which are crucial for training AI systems to recognize and anticipate errors in procedural activities.
- The dataset is diverse, covering a wide range of errors and error types, which enhances its utility in advancing research in this area.
- The paper is well-written, making it easy to follow and understand, and the dataset is annotated with both coarse-grained and fine-grained actions, which are beneficial for training AI systems.

**Weaknesses:**
- The dataset is limited to a specific domain (cooking) and a specific setting (kitchen), which may limit its applicability to other domains or settings.
- The dataset's evaluation is limited, focusing only on error recognition and multi-step localization tasks. There is a lack of evaluation on other tasks such as procedure learning, which could provide a more comprehensive assessment of the dataset's utility.
- The paper does not sufficiently differentiate its contributions from existing datasets, particularly in terms of dataset size and diversity.
- The methodology for collecting and annotating the dataset is not novel, and the paper does not provide a detailed analysis of the collected data, which could help in understanding the dataset's strengths and limitations.
- The paper lacks a detailed discussion on the limitations of the dataset and the potential biases that could affect its utility.
- There is a lack of comparison with existing datasets, which is crucial for establishing the novelty and utility of the proposed dataset.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors provide a more detailed comparison of their dataset with existing datasets, highlighting the unique contributions of their dataset?
- How does the dataset perform in terms of error recognition and multi-step localization tasks compared to other datasets?
- Can the authors provide more details on the methodology for collecting and annotating the dataset, including any potential biases or limitations in the data collection process?
- How does the dataset handle the issue of error anticipation, and could it be used for this purpose?
- What are the potential applications of the dataset beyond error recognition and multi-step localization tasks?
- Could the authors provide more details on the dataset's limitations and potential biases, and discuss how these could affect the utility of the dataset?
