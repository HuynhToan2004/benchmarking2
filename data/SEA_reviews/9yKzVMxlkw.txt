 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel method called Target Inner-Geometry learning (TIG-BEV) to enhance camera-based BEV detectors by leveraging LiDAR features. This approach involves an inner-depth supervision module for learning low-level relative depth relations and an inner-feature BEV distillation module for imitating high-level semantics of different keypoints within foreground targets. The methodology aims to bridge the modality gap between camera and LiDAR features, enhancing the performance of camera-based detectors. The paper presents a comprehensive evaluation on the nuScenes dataset, demonstrating significant improvements over existing methods.

**Strengths:**
- The paper addresses the modality gap between camera and LiDAR features, which is a significant issue in multi-view BEV 3D object detection.
- The proposed method is simple yet effective, with a clear motivation and a well-designed framework.
- The paper is well-written, making it easy to follow, and the experiments are comprehensive, including ablation studies that demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed method.
- The method achieves state-of-the-art performance on the nuScenes dataset, demonstrating its practical applicability.
- The paper introduces a novel inner-depth supervision module that captures internal depth relations between different parts of each foreground target, enhancing target depth map prediction.
- The inner-feature BEV distillation module transfers well-learned knowledge from the LiDAR modality to camera-based BEV representations, reducing the domain gap between two modalities.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed comparison with other methods that also use LiDAR features for camera-based detectors, such as those mentioned in references [1], [2], and [3].
- There is a lack of discussion on the limitations of the proposed method, which is crucial for understanding its applicability and potential drawbacks.
- The paper does not provide sufficient qualitative results, such as visualizations of depth maps or other visual aids that could help in understanding the method's effectiveness.
- The paper does not address the scalability of the method to other datasets or scenarios beyond those tested.
- There is a need for more detailed explanations of the inner-depth supervision and inner-feature BEV distillation modules, including the selection criteria for adaptive depth reference points and the rationale behind the choice of keypoints.
- The paper lacks a discussion on the potential negative societal impacts of the proposed method.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors provide a more detailed comparison with other methods that use LiDAR features for camera-based detectors, such as those mentioned in references [1], [2], and [3]?
- How does the proposed method perform when the camera-based detectors are pre-trained with BEVDepth?
- What are the limitations of the proposed method, and how do these impact its practical applicability?
- Can the authors provide more qualitative results, such as visualizations of depth maps, to better illustrate the method's effectiveness?
- How does the method perform on other datasets or scenarios beyond those tested?
- Could the authors elaborate on the selection criteria for adaptive depth reference points and the rationale behind the choice of keypoints?
- How does the proposed method address the potential negative societal impacts, and what steps have been taken to mitigate these impacts?
