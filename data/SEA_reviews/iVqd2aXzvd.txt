 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel approach to learn the dynamics of black-box ODE implementations using abstract automata learning and Neural Event ODEs. It proposes a method that extracts event specifications and state update functions from a black-box ODE, generating a high-coverage training dataset through abstract automata learning. The method is tested on various systems, including a bouncing ball, a thermostat-controlled water heater, and a switching dynamical system, demonstrating significant improvements in training time and computational resource requirements. The paper also discusses the interpretability of the learned models and the potential for broader application in hybrid systems.

**Strengths:**
- The paper presents a novel approach using automata learning to infer both event and state update functions from black-box piecewise ODEs, which is a significant advancement in the field.
- The methodology is well-explained, making it easy to follow, and the experiments are well-designed, demonstrating the effectiveness of the proposed approach.
- The paper is well-written, with clear explanations of the methodology and the results, and the use of a bouncing ball as a running example throughout the paper is effective in illustrating the key ideas.
- The approach shows promise in improving training time and computational resource requirements compared to existing methods, and the results are convincing, particularly in the context of hybrid systems.
- The paper is original in its application of automata learning to piecewise ODEs, and the methodology is well-grounded in theoretical principles, providing a solid foundation for further research.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a thorough comparison with existing methods, particularly in terms of computational efficiency and the accuracy of the learned models.
- There is a need for more detailed discussion on the limitations of the proposed method, including scenarios where the method may not be effective or where it may fail.
- The paper could benefit from more extensive experimental validation, particularly in more complex and realistic scenarios.
- The paper's writing could be improved for clarity, particularly in the definitions and explanations of key terms and concepts.
- The paper does not adequately address the interpretability of the learned models, which is a critical aspect for the application of the method in real-world scenarios.
- There is a lack of discussion on the limitations of the proposed method, which could impact its applicability and effectiveness in different contexts.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors provide more details on the computational efficiency of the proposed method compared to existing methods, particularly in terms of training time and resource requirements?
- How does the proposed method compare in terms of accuracy and interpretability to other methods for learning piecewise ODEs?
- Could the authors elaborate on the limitations of the proposed method and discuss scenarios where it may not be effective or where it may fail?
- How does the proposed method handle noisy or incomplete data, and what are the implications for the accuracy and interpretability of the learned models?
- Can the authors provide more detailed definitions and explanations of key terms and concepts used in the paper, particularly those related to automata learning and hybrid systems?
- How does the proposed method handle systems with more complex dynamics or multiple variables, and what are the implications for the complexity and interpretability of the learned models?
