 **Summary:**
The paper explores the application of large language models (LLMs) in predicting stock market trends by integrating various factors such as moving averages, news sentiment, options trading volume, and dependency graphs. It proposes a methodology that utilizes LLMs to analyze news sentiment, options trading volume, and dependency graphs, aiming to enhance predictive accuracy. The authors also discuss the potential of using LLMs to interpret complex financial data and improve investment strategies. However, the paper lacks rigorous experimentation, clear methodological details, and a comprehensive evaluation of its contributions, which limits its impact and relevance to the ICLR community.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces an innovative approach to using large language models (LLMs) in financial markets, particularly in predicting stock market trends, which is a novel and relevant application.
- The integration of LLMs with financial data such as news sentiment, options trading volume, and dependency graphs is highlighted as a promising direction for enhancing predictive accuracy in financial markets.
- The paper is well-structured and easy to follow, making it accessible to a broad audience.
- The topic of using LLMs in financial markets is timely and relevant, given the increasing interest in AI applications in finance.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks rigorous experimentation and detailed methodology, which makes it difficult to assess the effectiveness and reliability of the proposed method.
- The paper does not provide a clear explanation of how the LLMs are trained or used in the experiments, which is crucial for understanding the methodology and its applicability.
- The paper does not contribute significantly to the ICLR community as it primarily focuses on financial applications, which may not align with the conference's focus on computer vision and machine learning.
- The paper lacks a comprehensive evaluation of its contributions, particularly in terms of its novelty and the depth of its analysis.
- The paper does not provide a detailed comparison with existing methods or benchmarks, which is necessary to assess the effectiveness and superiority of the proposed approach.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors provide more details on how the LLMs were trained and used in the experiments?
- How does the paper handle the integration of LLMs with financial data, particularly in terms of data preprocessing and feature engineering?
- What specific LLMs were used in the experiments, and how were they chosen?
- How does the paper address the potential for biases in the LLMs, especially given the dynamic nature of LLM training?
- Can the authors clarify the methodology used in the experiments and provide a more detailed explanation of the experimental setup and results?
- How does the paper compare its results with existing methods, and what benchmarks were used for evaluation?
