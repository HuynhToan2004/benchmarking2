 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel distillation framework, Scene Flow via Distillation (SFvD), which generates pseudo-labels from a teacher model, NSFP, to train a faster student model, ZeroFlow. ZeroFlow achieves state-of-the-art performance on the Argoverse 2 Self-Supervised Scene Flow Challenge, significantly outperforming existing methods like Chodosh et al. (2023) and NSFP. The approach leverages a label-free optimization method to produce pseudo-labels, which are then used to supervise a feedforward model, achieving a significant speedup in inference. The methodology is evaluated on Argoverse 2 and Waymo Open datasets, demonstrating its effectiveness and scalability.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel distillation framework, Scene Flow via Distillation (SFvD), which effectively utilizes a label-free optimization method to produce pseudo-labels, enhancing the training of feedforward models.
- ZeroFlow, the proposed method, achieves state-of-the-art performance on the Argoverse 2 Self-Supervised Scene Flow Challenge, demonstrating significant improvements in speed and accuracy.
- The paper is well-written, clear, and easy to follow, with comprehensive experiments that include the release of pseudo-labels for Argoverse 2 and Waymo Open datasets, along with the code and trained model weights.
- The methodology of SFvD is innovative, leveraging the strengths of both optimization-based and feedforward methods to achieve a balance between efficiency and accuracy.
- The paper provides a detailed evaluation of the proposed method, including a comparison with state-of-the-art methods and an analysis of the impact of different components and hyperparameters.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper primarily focuses on the Argoverse 2 dataset, which might limit the generalizability of the results to other datasets.
- There is a lack of discussion on the limitations and potential negative societal impacts of the proposed method.
- The paper does not provide a detailed comparison of training times and costs between different methods, which could affect the evaluation of the proposed method's efficiency.
- The novelty of the proposed method is questioned as it seems to be a simple combination of existing methods without significant innovation.
- There is a lack of discussion on the potential negative societal impacts of the proposed method, which is crucial for understanding the broader implications of the research.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors provide more details on the limitations and potential negative societal impacts of the proposed method?
- How does the proposed method compare in terms of training times and costs to other state-of-the-art methods?
- Could the authors elaborate on the novelty of the proposed method, particularly how it innovates beyond the existing distillation techniques?
- In Section 4.4, the paper mentions that the teacher quality does not significantly impact ZeroFlow's performance. Could the authors provide more insights into this observation?
- The paper mentions the use of a larger backbone for ZeroFlow XL. How does this affect the model's performance and inference speed?
- How does the proposed method handle scenarios where the pseudo-labels generated by NSFP might introduce biases or inaccuracies?
