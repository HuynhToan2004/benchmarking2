 **Summary:**
The paper introduces Diffusion-TS, a novel diffusion-based framework for generating multivariate time series with interpretable temporal representations. It utilizes an encoder-decoder Transformer architecture and a Fourier-based loss term to reconstruct time series from noise, thereby capturing both semantic meaning and detailed sequential information. The model is designed to handle conditional generation tasks such as forecasting and imputation, and is evaluated on several real-world datasets. Despite its innovative approach, the paper faces criticism for its limited novelty, lack of comparison with recent relevant works, and insufficient discussion on the interpretability of the generated time series.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel diffusion-based framework for time series generation, integrating disentangled temporal representations which is innovative.
- The proposed method is simple, easy to implement, and can be applied to conditional generation tasks such as forecasting and imputation.
- The paper provides a detailed explanation of the model architecture, including the use of a transformer-based deep decomposition architecture, which enhances the understanding of the methodology.
- The experiments conducted on both real and synthetic datasets demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed method, showing superior performance compared to existing methods.
- The paper is well-written, making it easy to follow, and the authors provide a comprehensive ablation study to validate the effectiveness of the proposed method.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a clear discussion on the interpretability of the generated time series, which is a critical aspect of the proposed method.
- There is a notable absence of recent relevant works in the literature review, which could have provided a more robust comparison and validation of the proposed method.
- The paper does not adequately discuss the limitations of the proposed method, which could help in understanding its applicability and potential drawbacks.
- The experimental results are not convincing, particularly in the context of conditional generation tasks, and the paper does not include a comprehensive comparison with state-of-the-art methods.
- The paper could benefit from a more detailed discussion on the computational efficiency of the proposed method, especially in terms of inference time and resource requirements.
- The paper's title and some of its claims (e.g., "highly interpretable") are overstated, which could mislead readers about the true capabilities of the proposed method.

**Questions:**
- How can the authors ensure that the generated time series are interpretable, especially in terms of the semantic meaning captured by the model?
- Could the authors provide a more detailed discussion on the limitations of the proposed method and how these might affect its practical application?
- How does the proposed method compare to other recent diffusion-based methods for time series generation, such as those mentioned in recent literature?
- Could the authors clarify the computational efficiency of the proposed method, particularly in terms of inference time and resource requirements?
- How does the proposed method handle irregularly sampled time series, and what are the implications of this for the model's performance?
- The paper mentions using a polynomial regressor for trend modeling. Could the authors discuss the choice of this method over other potential models like ARIMA?
