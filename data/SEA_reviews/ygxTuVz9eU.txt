 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel approach to detecting dirty samples in datasets using a versatile data cleanser (VDC) that leverages multimodal large language models (MLLM) to identify visual-linguistic inconsistencies. The VDC framework consists of three modules: a visual question generation module, a visual question answering module, and a visual answer evaluation module. These modules are designed to detect various types of dirty samples, including poisoned samples, noisy labels, and hybrid samples. The methodology is evaluated on datasets such as CIFAR-10 and ImageNet, demonstrating superior performance compared to existing methods. However, the paper has been critiqued for its limited evaluation on small datasets, lack of comparisons with state-of-the-art methods, and potential over-reliance on LLM outputs without sufficient ablation studies.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel approach by leveraging large language models (LLMs) to detect dirty samples in datasets, which is a significant advancement in the field.
- The methodology is well-articulated, with a clear presentation of the proposed framework and its components.
- The paper demonstrates the effectiveness of the proposed method across various types of dirty samples, including poisoned samples, noisy labels, and hybrid samples, which is a significant strength.
- The paper is well-organized and easy to follow, making it accessible to a broad audience.
- The idea of using LLMs to detect dirty samples is innovative and has the potential to significantly impact the field.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper's evaluation is limited to small datasets such as CIFAR-10 and ImageNet, which may not adequately represent real-world scenarios with larger, more complex datasets.
- There is a lack of comparison with state-of-the-art methods, which is crucial for establishing the effectiveness and novelty of the proposed method.
- The paper does not provide sufficient ablation studies to understand the impact of different components of the proposed method, such as the number of visual questions or the choice of LLM.
- The paper's reliance on LLM outputs without sufficient validation or error analysis could lead to incorrect conclusions or misleading results.
- The paper does not address the potential biases or errors in LLM outputs, which could significantly affect the accuracy and reliability of the results.
- The paper does not adequately address the computational complexity and cost associated with the use of LLMs in the proposed framework.
