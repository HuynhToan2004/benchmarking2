 **Summary:**
The paper investigates the use of correlated noise in differential privacy (DP) settings, specifically focusing on DP-FTRL and its comparison to DP-SGD. It introduces a novel approach to finding noise correlations that optimize utility in DP-FTRL, which is shown to outperform DP-SGD in certain scenarios. The authors provide theoretical analysis and empirical evidence, including experiments on image and text classification tasks, to support their claims. The paper also discusses the computational efficiency of DP-FTRL, which is claimed to be more efficient than DP-SGD. However, the paper's claims of significant improvements over existing methods are questioned due to the limited scope of the experiments and the lack of detailed comparisons with state-of-the-art methods.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, clear, and easy to follow, with a good balance of theoretical results and empirical evidence.
- The proposed method, ν-DP-FTRL, is a simple and efficient alternative to DP-SGD, particularly in terms of computational efficiency.
- The paper provides a theoretical analysis of the behavior of DP-FTRL with correlated noise, which is an interesting and understudied area in differential privacy.
- The experiments conducted are extensive and well-documented, including a comparison with other efficient DP algorithms and a detailed analysis of the performance of ν-DP-FTRL.
- The paper offers a novel approach to finding noise correlations for DP-FTRL, which is more computationally efficient than previous methods.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper's claims of significant improvements over existing methods are not convincingly supported by the experimental results, which are limited to specific datasets and do not include comparisons with state-of-the-art methods such as those mentioned in the review.
- The theoretical analysis is not clearly explained in some sections, particularly in the introduction and in the explanation of certain results like Theorem 2.1.
- The paper lacks a comprehensive comparison with other methods that use correlated noise in DP settings, such as those mentioned in the review.
- There is a potential issue with the practical applicability of the proposed method, as it requires the computation of a sensitivity parameter which might not be feasible in all scenarios.
- The paper's experimental setup and results are not clearly presented, which could lead to confusion about the actual performance of the proposed method.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors clarify the discrepancy between the theoretical results and the practical performance of the proposed method, especially in terms of the computational efficiency claimed?
- How does the proposed method compare to other methods that use correlated noise in DP settings, particularly those mentioned in the review?
- Could the authors provide more detailed comparisons with state-of-the-art methods, including the experimental setup and results?
- How does the proposed method handle the practical issue of computing the sensitivity parameter in real-world scenarios?
- Could the authors provide a more detailed explanation of the theoretical results, particularly in the introduction and in the explanation of Theorem 2.1?
