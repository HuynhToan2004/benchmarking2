 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel framework called Fairret, designed to quantify bias in machine learning models as a modular, flexible objective that can be easily integrated into automatic differentiation pipelines. Fairret supports a wide range of fairness definitions, including those that consider linear-fractional statistics, which are more expressive than traditional linear statistics. The framework includes two types of fairness regularization terms: violation and projection, which are evaluated against existing fairness tools using datasets like Bank, CreditCard, LawSchool4, and ACSIncome. The paper claims that fairret improves upon existing fairness tools by providing a more general and expressive set of fairness definitions, although it does not offer significant empirical improvements over existing methods.
