 **Summary:**
The paper introduces NodeDup, a novel augmentation method aimed at enhancing the performance of Graph Neural Networks (GNNs) in cold-start link prediction tasks. By duplicating low-degree nodes and creating links between the original and duplicated nodes, NodeDup provides additional views for nodes, which can improve the training process by enriching the neighborhood aggregation and supervision signals. The method is evaluated on seven benchmark datasets and shows significant improvements in performance, particularly for low-degree nodes, without compromising the performance on high-degree nodes. The paper also discusses the method's efficiency and its effectiveness in both transductive and inductive settings.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, making it easy to follow, and the proposed method is simple and effective.
- The method is evaluated on a large number of datasets and across different settings, including both transductive and inductive settings.
- The paper provides a detailed explanation of the method's workings and its impact on cold nodes, and includes a comprehensive ablation study that shows the effectiveness of the method.
- The method is shown to be efficient in terms of training time and can be integrated into existing GNNs without significant computational overhead.
- The paper includes a detailed analysis of the method's impact on different types of nodes (isolated, low-degree, and warm) and its efficiency in terms of training time.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed discussion on the limitations of the proposed method and its potential negative societal impacts.
- The paper could benefit from a more thorough discussion on the related work, particularly in the area of graph augmentation.
- The paper's claims about the method's efficiency are not convincingly supported by the experimental results, which do not show a significant improvement in efficiency over existing methods.
- The paper does not adequately address the potential issues of over-smoothing in GNNs and the impact of the method on the overall performance of the GNN.
- The paper's discussion on the method's impact on warm nodes is limited, and it does not explore the method's effectiveness in other graph learning tasks beyond link prediction.
- The paper's experimental results do not convincingly demonstrate that the method significantly outperforms existing methods, and there is a lack of comparison with more recent and relevant baselines.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors provide more details on the limitations of the proposed method and its potential negative societal impacts?
- How does the method perform when applied to other graph learning tasks beyond link prediction?
- Can the authors provide more details on the experimental setup, such as the number of negative samples used and the specifics of the GNN architecture used?
- How does the method compare to other state-of-the-art methods in terms of efficiency and performance?
- Can the authors provide more insights into the method's impact on warm nodes and the overall performance of the GNN?
- Can the authors clarify the discrepancies in the performance metrics reported in the paper and provide more detailed explanations for the observed results?
