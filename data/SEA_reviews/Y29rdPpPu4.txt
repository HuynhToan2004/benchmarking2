 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel loss function, Mean Logarithmic Square Error (MLSE), designed to enhance the performance of long-term forecasting in time series data. This loss function is a simple modification of the Mean Square Error (MSE) by incorporating a logarithmic function, which is shown to effectively mitigate the accumulation of errors in long-term forecasts. The authors provide a theoretical analysis and extensive experiments across various datasets, including weather forecasting and time series data, demonstrating the effectiveness of MLSE in reducing forecast errors. The paper also discusses the practical implications and potential limitations of the proposed method, suggesting that it could be particularly beneficial in scenarios where long-term forecasts are critical.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel loss function, Mean Logarithmic Square Error (MLSE), which is a simple yet effective modification of the standard Mean Square Error (MSE) loss function, showing promising results in long-term forecasting tasks.
- The authors provide a comprehensive theoretical analysis, including a phenomenological theory that explains the feasibility and limitations of MLSE, offering a deeper understanding of error behavior in autoregressive tasks.
- The paper includes extensive experiments on various datasets, demonstrating the effectiveness of MLSE in reducing forecast errors across different tasks and datasets.
- The paper is well-written, clear, and easy to follow, making it accessible to a broad audience.
- The authors have conducted ablation studies to identify the most efficient way to incorporate the information from higher-order losses, which is crucial for understanding the impact of different loss functions on model performance.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper primarily focuses on weather forecasting tasks, which might limit the generalizability of the findings to other types of time series data.
- The theoretical analysis and the experimental setup could benefit from a more detailed discussion on the choice of hyperparameters and their impact on model performance.
- The paper lacks a detailed comparison with other state-of-the-art methods, which could provide a clearer picture of the advantages and disadvantages of the proposed method.
- There is a lack of discussion on the computational complexity of the proposed method, which is crucial for understanding its practical applicability in different scenarios.
- The paper does not include a comprehensive discussion on the limitations of the proposed method, which could help in understanding its scope and applicability.
