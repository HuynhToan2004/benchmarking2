 **Summary:**
The paper investigates the impact of incomplete client participation in federated learning (FL) and proposes a server-assisted FL framework (SA-FL) to address this issue. It establishes that FL is not PAC-learnable under incomplete client participation, but this limitation can be overcome by SA-FL, which is shown to be PAC-learnable. The paper introduces a new algorithm, SAFARI, which achieves linear convergence speedup guarantees, and presents numerical experiments to validate these theoretical findings. The authors argue that SA-FL can revive the PAC-learnability of FL with incomplete client participation, and the paper provides a convergence rate for the SAFARI algorithm. However, the paper is criticized for its lack of novelty, the simplicity of the experimental setup, and the absence of a comparison with existing SA-FL algorithms.

**Strengths:**
- The paper addresses a significant and timely issue in federated learning (FL) by focusing on the impact of incomplete client participation and proposing a server-assisted FL framework (SA-FL) to address this challenge.
- The paper is well-written, making it easy to follow, and the theoretical analysis is rigorous, with proofs that are clearly explained.
- The introduction of the SAFARI algorithm, which is the first SA-FL algorithm with convergence guarantees, is a notable contribution to the field.
- The paper provides a new generalization error bound for SA-FL, which is a significant theoretical contribution to the field.
- The experimental results demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed methods, validating the theoretical findings.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks novelty as it does not introduce new theoretical or methodological contributions beyond existing works in the field.
- The experimental setup is too simple and does not include a comparison with other SA-FL algorithms, which limits the evaluation of the proposed method.
- The paper does not adequately address the practical implications of incomplete client participation in FL, such as the impact of client churn and the handling of non-i.i.d. data.
- There is a lack of discussion on the practical deployment of SA-FL and its scalability to larger datasets and more complex models.
- The paper could benefit from a more detailed discussion on the assumptions made, particularly Assumption 2, and the implications of these assumptions on the results.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors clarify how the assumptions made in the paper, particularly Assumption 2, affect the results and the applicability of the findings?
- How does the proposed method perform in more complex scenarios, such as when the data is highly non-i.i.d. or when there is a large number of clients with different data distributions?
- Can the authors provide a more detailed discussion on the practical deployment of SA-FL and its scalability to larger datasets and more complex models?
- How does the proposed method compare to existing SA-FL algorithms, and what are the specific advantages of the SAFARI algorithm over these methods?
- Can the authors elaborate on the choice of the auxiliary dataset and its impact on the performance of the SA-FL framework?
- In the experimental setup, how were the hyperparameters chosen, and what were the considerations behind these choices?
