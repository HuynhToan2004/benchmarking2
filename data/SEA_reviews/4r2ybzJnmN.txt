 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel approach to learning delays in spiking neural networks (SNNs) using dilated convolutions with learnable spacings (DCLS). This method models each synaptic connection as a 1D Gaussian kernel centered on the delay position, enabling the learning of kernel positions (delays) through DCLS. The method is evaluated on several benchmark datasets, including the Spiking Heidelberg Dataset, Spiking Speech Commands, and Google Speech Commands, showing improvements in performance and parameter efficiency compared to existing methods. The paper also discusses the biological plausibility of the approach, which could potentially enhance the expressivity of SNNs by incorporating delays.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel method for learning delays in SNNs using dilated convolutions with learnable spacings, which is a significant contribution to the field.
- The proposed method shows improved performance and parameter efficiency on several benchmark datasets, demonstrating its effectiveness.
- The approach is biologically plausible, as delays in neural connections are a biological feature, which could enhance the expressivity of SNNs.
- The paper is well-written, with clear figures and a comprehensive literature review, making it accessible and understandable.
- The experimental results are convincing, with a thorough ablation study that supports the effectiveness of the proposed method.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a clear explanation of how the method differs from existing approaches, particularly those that also use Gaussian kernels to learn delays.
- The motivation behind the use of DCLS for learning delays is not well-explained, and the paper does not adequately compare its method with other recent works that also learn delays in SNNs.
- The paper does not address the scalability of the method, especially in terms of the number of neurons and layers, which could affect the accuracy and efficiency of the model.
- There is a lack of discussion on the limitations of the proposed method, such as its applicability to different types of SNNs and its compatibility with other learning rules.
- The paper does not include a discussion on the biological plausibility of the method, which could be a significant concern for some readers.
- The method's performance is not consistently better across all datasets, and there is a need for more comprehensive comparisons with other methods to establish its superiority.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors clarify the differences between their method and other recent works that use Gaussian kernels to learn delays in SNNs?
- How does the method perform when the number of neurons and layers is increased, and what are the implications for accuracy and efficiency?
- Can the authors provide a more detailed comparison with other methods that also learn delays in SNNs, particularly those mentioned in the literature review?
- What are the specific advantages of using DCLS over other methods for learning delays in SNNs?
- How does the method handle the biological plausibility concerns raised by some readers, and what evidence supports the biological plausibility of the approach?
- Can the authors explain the rationale behind the choice of using DCLS for learning delays, and how does this method compare to other learning rules for delays in SNNs?
