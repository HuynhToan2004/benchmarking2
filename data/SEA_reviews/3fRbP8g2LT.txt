 **Summary:**
The paper investigates the redundancy in message passing in Graph Neural Networks (GNNs) and proposes a novel approach to address this issue by introducing Directed Line Graph (DLG) and Directed Acyclic Line Graph (DALG) structures. These structures aim to eliminate redundancy in message passing by focusing on non-backtracking edges and cycles. The proposed methods, including Directed Line Graph Network (DLGN) and Efficient Redundancy-Free Graph Network (ERFGN), are evaluated against various baselines and shown to improve expressiveness and reduce redundancy. The paper also introduces a Global Attention module to model node-to-node and node-to-cycle interactions, further enhancing the model's capabilities. Despite these strengths, the paper faces criticism for its complexity, scalability, and the novelty of its contributions.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, easy to follow, and provides a clear and intuitive explanation of the proposed methods.
- The motivation behind the research is compelling, addressing the critical issue of redundancy in message passing in Graph Neural Networks (GNNs).
- The proposed DLGN and ERFGN models are innovative, focusing on directed edges and non-backtracking edges, which are different from traditional methods that rely on bidirectional edges and self-loops.
- The theoretical analysis provided is robust, with detailed proofs in the appendix, supporting the claims made about the expressiveness and redundancy-free nature of the proposed methods.
- The paper includes comprehensive experimental evaluations, demonstrating the effectiveness of the proposed methods across various datasets and tasks.

**Weaknesses:**
- The complexity of the proposed methods, particularly the DALG conversion, is a significant concern. The paper does not adequately address the computational efficiency of this process, especially in large-scale or dense graphs.
- The paper lacks clarity in some sections, particularly in the definitions and explanations of certain terms and processes, which could confuse readers.
- The scalability of the proposed methods is questionable, as the complexity analysis suggests that the time and space requirements could increase exponentially with the size of the graph, which may limit their practical applicability.
- The novelty of the contributions is limited, as similar approaches have been explored in previous works, such as SPAGAN and RFGNN.
- The paper could benefit from a more detailed discussion on the limitations and potential negative societal impacts of the proposed methods.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors clarify the computational efficiency of the DALG conversion process, especially in large-scale or dense graphs?
- How does the proposed method handle the scalability issues, particularly in terms of computational complexity and memory requirements?
- Can the authors provide a more detailed comparison of the proposed methods with existing approaches like RFGNN, highlighting the key differences and improvements?
- How does the paper address the potential negative societal impacts of the proposed methods, particularly in terms of data privacy and fairness in machine learning applications?
- Could the authors provide a more detailed explanation of the cycle modeling and global attention modules, especially the role of the 1-to-1 scheme in preserving details?
