 **Summary:**
The paper introduces M3C, a method for joint graph matching and clustering, which addresses the limitations of existing methods by integrating a learning-free approach with a minorize-maximization framework. M3C is designed to handle diverse graph modes and is theoretically guaranteed to converge. The method is evaluated on benchmarks and shows improved performance over state-of-the-art methods. Additionally, the paper introduces UM3C, an unsupervised model that incorporates edge-wise affinity learning and pseudo label selection, further enhancing the robustness of the method. The paper is praised for its clarity, thorough literature review, and detailed experimental validation.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written and easy to follow, with a comprehensive literature review that positions the work effectively within the field.
- The proposed method, M3C, is simple, intuitive, and effective, with a theoretical guarantee of convergence within the minorize-maximization framework.
- UM3C, an unsupervised model that integrates M3C with an unsupervised pipeline, introduces edge-wise affinity learning and pseudo label selection for robustness and learning quality.
- Extensive experimental results demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed method, showing improvements over state-of-the-art methods.
- The paper includes an ablation study to analyze the contributions of different components of the proposed method.
- The paper is well-structured, with a clear problem formulation and a detailed description of the proposed method.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed discussion on the computational complexity of the proposed method compared to existing methods.
- There is a need for a more comprehensive comparison with recent graph matching methods, especially those that leverage deep learning for graph matching and clustering.
- The paper does not provide a detailed analysis of the time complexity of the proposed method, which could be crucial for understanding its scalability.
- The paper could benefit from a more thorough discussion of related works, particularly those that address similar problems using different approaches.
- The paper does not discuss the limitations of the proposed method, which could provide a more balanced view of its contributions and potential challenges.
- The paper's presentation could be improved by including a table of notations to aid in understanding complex mathematical expressions.

**Questions:**
- Could you provide a detailed analysis of the computational complexity of the proposed method compared to existing methods?
- How does the proposed method perform on larger datasets or more complex graph matching scenarios?
- Can you discuss the limitations of the proposed method and how these might affect its practical application?
- How does the proposed method compare to other recent graph matching methods that leverage deep learning for graph matching and clustering?
- Could you clarify the definition and role of the cluster indicator in the proposed method?
- How does the proposed method handle the initialization of the cluster indicator, and what are the implications of this initialization on the performance of the method?
