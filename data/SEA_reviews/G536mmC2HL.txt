 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel method for generating molecular conformers, specifically focusing on the prediction of torsion angles. This method employs a sequence-to-sequence model that models the joint distribution of torsion angles using a bidirectional LSTM, conditioned on the molecular graph. The authors also introduce a method to address the vanishing gradient problem in the model by adding a random deviation to the torsion angle predictions. The method is evaluated against several baselines on two datasets, showing improved performance in terms of RMSD and runtime. The paper also includes an ablation study to analyze the impact of different components of the model. However, the novelty of the method is questioned, and the paper lacks a thorough comparison with existing methods and a detailed discussion on the limitations and societal impacts of the work.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, clear, and easy to follow, with a clear motivation for the work.
- The proposed method is simple and effective, with a focus on the importance of torsion angles in molecular conformer generation.
- The paper includes an ablation study to analyze the impact of different components of the model, and the results are promising.
- The method is efficient in terms of runtime, which is a significant advantage over diffusion models.
- The paper addresses the vanishing gradient problem in the model by adding a random deviation to the torsion angle predictions, which is a novel approach.
- The authors have conducted experiments on two datasets, showing that their method performs better than baselines in terms of RMSD and runtime.

**Weaknesses:**
- The novelty of the method is limited as it primarily uses existing techniques such as LSTM and Earth Mover's Distance loss.
- The paper lacks a detailed comparison with other sequence-based methods for torsion angle prediction and does not discuss the limitations of the proposed method.
- There is a lack of discussion on the societal impact of the work, which is crucial for assessing the broader implications of the research.
- The paper does not provide sufficient details on the architecture of the MPNN used in the model, which is a critical component of the method.
- The paper does not compare the model's performance with different diffusion steps, which could provide insights into the effectiveness of the model under varying conditions.
- The paper does not discuss the limitations of the proposed method, which is essential for understanding the scope and applicability of the work.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors provide more details on the architecture of the MPNN used in the model, including the number of layers and the number of hidden units in each layer?
- How does the proposed method compare with other sequence-based methods for torsion angle prediction in terms of performance and efficiency?
- Could the authors discuss the limitations of the proposed method and how these limitations affect the practical applicability of the work?
- How does the proposed method handle the vanishing gradient problem in different scenarios, and what are the implications of this for the model's performance?
- Can the authors provide more insights into the choice of using Earth Mover's Distance loss over other loss functions, and how this choice impacts the model's performance?
- How does the proposed method handle the issue of torsional ambiguity, and what are the implications of this for the model's accuracy and applicability?
