 **Summary:**
The paper investigates the geometry of feature representations learned through the Supervised Contrastive Loss (SCL) under class imbalance. It demonstrates that the addition of a ReLU activation at the final layer restores symmetry in the learned representations, which is critical for maintaining orthogonality in feature vectors. The authors also explore the impact of batching strategies on the symmetry of SCL-learned representations, proposing a batch-binding strategy to ensure invariant symmetric representations. Theoretical and empirical evidence supports these findings, which are significant for understanding the behavior of SCL under imbalanced data conditions.

**Strengths:**
- The paper provides a comprehensive theoretical analysis of the geometry of representations learned by the Supervised Contrastive Loss (SCL), specifically focusing on the impact of batching strategies and the addition of a ReLU activation at the final layer.
- The paper is well-written, with clear explanations of the theoretical concepts and experimental setups, making it accessible to readers.
- The empirical results are robust, demonstrating the symmetry of SCL-learned representations under class imbalance and the impact of batching strategies on the symmetry of these representations.
- The paper presents novel contributions to the understanding of SCL and its behavior under imbalanced data conditions, which are of significant interest in the field of machine learning.
- The theoretical analysis is supported by empirical evidence, which strengthens the credibility of the findings.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper's main theoretical analysis is limited to the full-batch setting, which may not be practical or applicable in real-world scenarios where mini-batch optimization is more common.
- The paper could benefit from a more detailed discussion on the practical implications of the theoretical results, especially in the context of mini-batch optimization.
- The experimental setup and results could be improved by including more complex datasets and architectures, as well as a broader range of batching strategies and imbalance ratios.
- The paper lacks a detailed discussion on the practical significance of the findings, particularly how the insights from the analysis of SCL with ReLU could be applied in practical scenarios.
- There is a need for more comprehensive comparisons with existing methods, such as those mentioned in the related work section, to better position the paper within the current research landscape.

**Questions:**
- How does the addition of a ReLU activation at the final layer affect the generalization performance of the model, as suggested by the empirical results presented in Table 1?
- Can the authors clarify the differences between the batching strategies discussed in the paper and those mentioned in related works, such as those by Khosla et al. (2020) and Graf et al. (2021)?
- How does the batch-binding strategy perform when applied to more complex datasets and architectures, such as those used in the CIFAR-100 and TinyImageNet datasets?
- Could the authors provide more details on the experimental setup and results, particularly in terms of the specific batching strategies used and their impact on the symmetry of the SCL-learned representations?
- How does the paper's analysis of the SCL with ReLU contribute to the practical application of SCL in real-world scenarios, and what are the potential implications for improving the performance of SCL in imbalanced data conditions?
