 **Summary:**
The paper investigates the role of activation functions in continual learning, specifically focusing on the elephant activation function, which is designed to address catastrophic forgetting. This function is characterized by its ability to generate both sparse representations and sparse gradients, which are crucial for reducing forgetting in neural networks. The paper presents theoretical insights into the properties of activation functions that aid in continual learning and empirical results demonstrating the effectiveness of the elephant activation function across various tasks, including regression, classification, and reinforcement learning. The study also discusses the limitations and potential future directions for research in this area.

**Strengths:**
- The paper addresses an important and timely problem in the field of continual learning, specifically focusing on the role of activation functions in mitigating catastrophic forgetting.
- The proposed elephant activation function is novel and well-motivated, providing a theoretical justification for its effectiveness in reducing forgetting.
- The paper is well-written, clear, and easy to follow, with a good balance between theoretical analysis and empirical validation.
- The experimental results are strong and convincing, demonstrating the practical utility of the proposed method across different tasks and settings.
- The paper provides a novel approach to continual learning by focusing on the activation function, a topic that has not been extensively explored in this context.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a comprehensive comparison with existing methods, particularly in the regression and reinforcement learning settings, which limits the understanding of the method's relative performance.
- The theoretical analysis, while sound, is somewhat limited in scope and depth, and could benefit from a broader discussion on the implications of the proposed properties.
- The experimental design and results presentation could be improved, particularly in terms of clarity and reproducibility. More detailed experimental setups and results, including standard deviations and comparisons with baseline methods, are needed.
- The paper's focus on a single activation function might limit its applicability to more complex or varied neural network architectures.
- There is a need for more detailed analysis and discussion on the practical implications and limitations of the elephant activation function, especially in terms of its computational efficiency and scalability.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors clarify the differences in the experimental setups and results between the regression and classification tasks, particularly in terms of the neural network architectures used?
- How does the elephant activation function perform in more complex or varied neural network architectures, such as those used in larger-scale datasets like CIFAR-100 or Tiny ImageNet?
- Can the authors provide more detailed experimental results, including standard deviations and comparisons with baseline methods, to better understand the practical impact of the proposed method?
- How does the elephant activation function compare in terms of computational efficiency and scalability with other activation functions, particularly in large-scale or complex neural network architectures?
- Are there any specific scenarios or types of data where the elephant activation function might not be as effective, and if so, what are the implications for its practical applicability?
