 **Summary:**
The paper introduces BruSLEAttack, a novel score-based sparse adversarial attack method that significantly reduces the number of queries needed to generate adversarial samples compared to existing methods. The method employs a Bayesian framework to iteratively select and perturb pixels based on their influence on model predictions, aiming to minimize the number of perturbed pixels while maintaining high attack success rates. Extensive experiments are conducted across various datasets and model architectures, including ViT and CNNs, demonstrating the effectiveness and efficiency of the proposed attack. However, concerns were raised regarding the clarity and comprehensibility of the methodology, particularly the explanation of the probabilistic framework and the optimization process, which could hinder the understanding and reproducibility of the results.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel sparse attack method that is query-efficient, significantly reducing the number of queries needed compared to existing methods.
- The methodology is well-explained, making the paper easy to follow, and the experiments are extensive, covering various datasets and model architectures.
- The proposed attack is effective, as demonstrated by its ability to achieve high attack success rates with fewer queries compared to other methods.
- The paper is well-written, with clear explanations of the proposed method and its contributions to the field of sparse adversarial attacks.
- The experiments are well-designed and include both white-box and black-box settings, demonstrating the robustness of the proposed method against various defenses.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed discussion on the limitations of the proposed method, which could affect the reader's understanding of its applicability and effectiveness.
- There are several notations and equations that are not clearly explained or referenced, making it difficult to follow the methodology and replicate the results.
- The paper does not adequately compare the proposed method with other state-of-the-art methods, particularly in terms of query efficiency and sparsity.
- The probabilistic framework used in the method is not well-explained, and the optimization process is not clearly described, which could hinder the understanding and reproducibility of the results.
- The paper lacks a thorough discussion on the societal impact of the proposed method, which is crucial for understanding the broader implications of the research.

**Questions:**
- Could you clarify the notations and equations used in the methodology, particularly those mentioned in the weaknesses section?
- How does the proposed method compare in terms of query efficiency and sparsity with other state-of-the-art methods?
- Can you provide more details on the probabilistic framework used in the method, especially how it influences the optimization process?
- How does the method perform against different types of defenses, such as adversarial training and robustness techniques?
- Could you elaborate on the societal impact of the proposed method and discuss any potential negative consequences of its use?
- How does the method handle different types of adversarial attacks, such as untargeted attacks, and what are the implications of these variations on the attack success rates?
