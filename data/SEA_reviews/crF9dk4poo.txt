 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel deep clustering framework for tabular data that utilizes a self-supervised approach to identify informative features for clustering. The framework incorporates a gating mechanism to selectively activate features, enhancing interpretability by providing both instance-level and cluster-level explanations. The methodology involves training an autoencoder to learn a latent representation of data, followed by training a clustering head to predict cluster assignments. The paper presents experiments on various datasets, demonstrating the framework's effectiveness in identifying informative features and improving clustering accuracy. However, concerns are raised about the clarity of the experimental setup, the necessity of the proposed method over existing techniques, and the lack of comprehensive comparisons with state-of-the-art methods.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-organized and easy to follow, with clear explanations and detailed descriptions of the proposed method.
- The problem addressed, i.e., interpretable clustering of high-dimensional tabular data, is both interesting and relevant to the community.
- The proposed method achieves better clustering accuracy compared to existing methods, demonstrating its effectiveness.
- The use of a self-supervised approach to identify informative features is innovative and could be beneficial for various downstream tasks.
- The paper provides a comprehensive evaluation of the proposed method, including experiments on both synthetic and real-world datasets.
- The inclusion of an ablation study and visualizations of selected features helps in understanding the method's impact.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a clear definition and motivation for the problem setting, which could lead to confusion about the purpose and application of the proposed method.
- The experimental setup and comparisons are not sufficiently robust or fair, particularly in the evaluation of clustering accuracy and the selection of hyperparameters.
- The novelty of the proposed method is questioned, as it appears to be a combination of existing techniques without significant advancements.
- The paper does not adequately address the issue of correlated variables, which could affect the reliability of the results.
- The presentation of results and figures could be improved for better clarity and readability.
- The paper does not sufficiently discuss the limitations of the proposed method, which is crucial for understanding its applicability and effectiveness.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors clarify the definition and motivation behind the problem setting, particularly the distinction between instance-level and cluster-level explanations?
- How does the proposed method handle correlated variables, and what are the implications of this on the results?
- Could the authors provide more detailed explanations on the experimental setup, including the selection of hyperparameters and the rationale behind the experimental design?
- How does the proposed method compare to state-of-the-art methods in terms of clustering accuracy and interpretability?
- Can the authors elaborate on the choice of the synthetic dataset and its relevance to the problem at hand?
- In the context of the real-world datasets, can the authors provide more detailed descriptions and justifications for their selection?
- How does the proposed method handle the issue of overfitting, particularly in the context of the self-supervised learning approach?
