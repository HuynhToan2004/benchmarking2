 **Summary:**
The paper explores a novel active learning scenario where the labeler strategically abstains from providing labels to maximize query complexity, thereby potentially delaying the learning process. This approach challenges the traditional assumptions of active learning, where the labeler is assumed to be cooperative. The paper introduces a new complexity measure, GIC, and proposes an algorithm, E-VS Bisection, to address this issue. The algorithm aims to identify the hypothesis by querying the most informative samples, thereby reducing the complexity of learning. The paper also discusses the implications of this setting in multi-task learning and extends the analysis to scenarios where the labeler may not be identifiable. The theoretical contributions are supported by proofs and discussions on the optimality of the proposed algorithm.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel active learning setting where the labeler may not be identifiable, which is a significant departure from traditional active learning assumptions.
- The authors propose a new complexity measure, GIC, and an algorithm, E-VS Bisection, which are specifically designed to address this new setting.
- The paper provides a comprehensive theoretical analysis, including proofs that support the optimality of the proposed algorithm.
- The paper is well-written, clear, and easy to follow, making it accessible to a broad audience.
- The paper extends the analysis to other learning setups and scenarios, demonstrating the versatility of the proposed approach.
- The paper discusses the implications of the setting in multi-task learning, providing insights into how the strategic labeler might affect learning in different tasks.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper's assumptions about the labeler's behavior and the incentives behind strategic labeling are not well-defined, which could limit the practical applicability of the proposed algorithm.
- The paper lacks empirical results, which are crucial for validating the theoretical claims and demonstrating the effectiveness of the proposed algorithm in real-world scenarios.
- The paper's focus on the exact learning setting might limit its relevance to more practical scenarios, such as agnostic learning.
- The paper does not sufficiently discuss the limitations of its approach, which could help in understanding the scope and applicability of the proposed algorithm.
- The paper does not compare its results with existing algorithms, which could help in understanding the relative performance of the proposed algorithm.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors clarify the assumptions about the labeler's behavior and the incentives behind strategic labeling?
- How does the proposed algorithm perform in real-world scenarios, especially in practical applications where the labeler's behavior might not be as strategic as assumed?
- Can the authors provide empirical results to validate the theoretical claims and demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed algorithm in practice?
- How does the proposed algorithm compare with existing active learning algorithms, especially in terms of query complexity and sample efficiency?
- Could the authors discuss the limitations of their approach and how these might affect the practical applicability of the proposed algorithm?
