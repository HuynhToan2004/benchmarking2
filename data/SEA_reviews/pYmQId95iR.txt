 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel benchmark for evaluating reinforcement learning (RL) algorithms in algorithmic reasoning tasks, using puzzles from Simon Tatham's Portable Puzzle Collection. The benchmark includes 40 logic puzzles of varying complexity levels, which are designed to challenge the capabilities of RL agents in algorithmic reasoning. The authors demonstrate that current RL methods struggle with these tasks, highlighting the need for further research in this area. The benchmark is designed to be scalable and adaptable, allowing for the evaluation of RL agents across different difficulty levels. The paper also provides code and software for the benchmark, which is available to the public.
