 **Summary:**
The paper explores the problem of deleting sensitive information from large language models (LLMs) by proposing a framework that includes both attack and defense methods. The attacks aim to recover sensitive information from LLMs by leveraging techniques like logit lens and rephrasing, while the defenses focus on reducing the success rate of these attacks. The paper evaluates the effectiveness of these methods using a variety of LLMs and datasets, demonstrating that the proposed defenses can significantly reduce the attack success rate. However, the paper also highlights the challenges in completely removing sensitive information and the potential limitations of the defense methods.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, clear, and easy to follow, with a comprehensive related work section that effectively situates the research within the existing literature.
- The proposed defense methods are simple and effective, with the max entropy method being particularly highlighted for its ability to reduce attack success rates.
- The paper presents a thorough evaluation of the proposed methods, including a detailed analysis of the effectiveness of the defense methods against various attacks.
- The paper addresses a significant and timely issue of data privacy in LLMs, which is of increasing concern given the widespread use of LLMs.
- The paper is well-organized and provides a clear and concise explanation of the attack and defense methods, making it accessible to a broad audience.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper could benefit from a more detailed explanation of the threat model, particularly the definition of "sensitive information" and the threat model's assumptions.
- The paper's scope is somewhat limited, focusing primarily on factual information and not addressing other types of sensitive information that LLMs might memorize.
- The evaluation of the defense methods is limited to a few datasets, which might not fully represent the effectiveness of the methods across different types of data.
- The paper does not adequately address the potential negative societal impacts of the research, such as the potential for the findings to be used to circumvent privacy protections.
- The paper's title and some of its claims (e.g., "deleting sensitive information") are overly broad and potentially misleading, as they do not adequately reflect the limitations of the proposed methods.
- The paper's experimental setup and results are not sufficiently detailed, making it difficult to fully understand the conditions under which the experiments were conducted and the implications of the results.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors clarify the definition of "sensitive information" and the specific types of information that are considered sensitive in the context of the research?
- How does the proposed framework handle the issue of overfitting, particularly in the context of the defense methods?
- In the context of the threat model, how does the paper define the "budget" and the set of candidates (C_i)?
- What are the specific limitations of the proposed defense methods, and how do these limitations impact the practical applicability of the findings?
- How does the paper address the potential for the findings to be used to circumvent privacy protections or other ethical concerns?
- Can the authors provide more details on the experimental setup, including the specific conditions under which the experiments were conducted and the implications of the results?
