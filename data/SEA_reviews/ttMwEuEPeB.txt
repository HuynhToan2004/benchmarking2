 **Summary:**
The paper introduces 3D-GPT, a framework that leverages large language models (LLMs) to facilitate instruction-driven 3D modeling, integrating procedural generation to enhance user instructions into detailed 3D models. The system consists of three agents: a task dispatch agent, a conceptualization agent, and a modeling agent, each performing specific roles in the modeling process. The approach is tested on a set of 100 scene descriptions and demonstrates the capability to generate 3D models that align closely with user instructions. The paper also explores the use of LLMs to generate Python code for Blender, enhancing the system's flexibility and adaptability. However, the paper lacks a detailed discussion on the limitations and potential negative societal impacts of the technology, and the experimental validation could be strengthened with more comprehensive quantitative evaluations and comparisons with baseline methods.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel approach to 3D modeling using large language models (LLMs), which is a significant advancement in the field.
- The integration of procedural generation with LLMs is a promising avenue for future research, as it allows for the generation of detailed 3D models from simple user instructions.
- The system is flexible and adaptable, with the capability to generate Python code for Blender, enhancing its applicability and practicality.
- The paper is well-written and easy to follow, making it accessible to a broad audience.
- The system demonstrates the potential of LLMs in 3D modeling, particularly in terms of reasoning, planning, and tool-using capabilities, which could lead to significant advancements in the field.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed discussion on the limitations and potential negative societal impacts of the technology, which is crucial for a comprehensive understanding of the system's implications.
- The experimental validation is limited, with only a qualitative evaluation of the generated models, which does not provide a robust assessment of the system's effectiveness.
- The paper does not compare the proposed system with baseline methods, which makes it difficult to evaluate its performance relative to existing approaches.
- The system's reliance on LLMs for generating procedural generation algorithms could limit its applicability and effectiveness, particularly in scenarios where the generated algorithms are not effective.
- The paper does not discuss how the system handles multi-modal inputs, which could be a significant limitation in practical applications.
- The paper does not include a user study, which is essential for evaluating the system's usability and practicality in real-world scenarios.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors provide more details on how the system handles multi-modal inputs, particularly in terms of ensuring accurate and effective processing of these inputs?
- How does the system ensure the quality and accuracy of the generated procedural generation algorithms, especially when they are not effective?
- Could the authors provide more details on the experimental setup, particularly the number of user instructions used and the parameters involved in the evaluation?
- How does the system handle scenarios where the generated 3D models do not align closely with the user instructions, and what steps are taken to correct these discrepancies?
- Can the authors discuss the potential societal impacts of the technology, particularly in terms of its applicability and ethical considerations?
- How does the system compare to other baseline methods in terms of performance and effectiveness, and what are the key differences between the proposed system and these baseline approaches?
