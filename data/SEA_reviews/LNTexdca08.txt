 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel iterative learning framework, Point to Prompt (P2P), for point-supervised object detection and segmentation. This framework leverages the outstanding generalization ability of the foundation model Segment Anything Model (SAM) to refine object regions and generate semantic-explicit prompts. The iterative refinement process consists of two stages: Semantic Explicit Prompt Generation (SEPG) and Prompt Guided Spatial Refinement (PGSR). SEPG serves as a prompt generator for generating semantic-explicit prompts from point inputs, while PGSR guides the visual foundation model to refine object regions. The proposed method achieves state-of-the-art performance on both detection and segmentation tasks, significantly narrowing the performance gap with fully-supervised methods.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, easy to follow, and provides a clear motivation for the proposed method.
- The proposed method is novel, leveraging the outstanding generalization ability of the foundation model Segment Anything Model (SAM) to refine object regions and generate semantic-explicit prompts.
- The method achieves state-of-the-art performance on both detection and segmentation tasks, significantly narrowing the performance gap with fully-supervised methods.
- The paper includes a comprehensive ablation study that demonstrates the effectiveness of each component of the proposed method.
- The idea of using prompts to guide the foundation model to refine object regions is innovative and has the potential to be applied in various downstream tasks.
- The iterative learning framework using visual foundation model to achieve a semantic-explicit output is a significant contribution to the field.

**Weaknesses:**
