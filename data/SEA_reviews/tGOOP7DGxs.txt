 **Summary:**
The paper introduces LargeGT, a novel graph transformer architecture designed for large-scale graph learning, which addresses the computational challenges of global attention mechanisms by employing a 4-hop reception field through 2-hop operations. This approach, coupled with an approximate global codebook, enables efficient sampling and representation learning on large graphs. The model is tested on several large-scale node classification benchmarks, demonstrating a significant speedup and performance gain over existing methods. Despite its innovative approach, the paper is criticized for its limited novelty, as it heavily borrows from existing works, and its evaluation is limited to a few datasets and tasks.

**Strengths:**
- The paper addresses a significant problem in the field of graph learning, specifically focusing on large-scale graphs, and proposes a novel solution by integrating local and global graph representations.
- The proposed LargeGT architecture is well-motivated, with a clear and well-organized presentation that effectively communicates the challenges and design principles of graph learning models.
- The paper provides a detailed description of the LargeGT framework, including the LocalModule and GlobalModule, which are designed to handle local and global information exchange efficiently.
- The empirical results demonstrate a 3x speedup and a 16.8% performance gain on ogbn-products and snap-patents compared to their best baselines, and a 5.9% improvement in performance on ogbn-papers100M.
- The paper is well-written, making it accessible and easy to follow, and the experimental results are convincing, showing the effectiveness of the proposed method.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper heavily borrows from existing works, such as Kong et al. (2023), and does not sufficiently differentiate its contributions from these prior works.
- The evaluation of the proposed method is limited to a few datasets and tasks, which may not fully demonstrate the generalizability of the LargeGT architecture across different graph learning tasks.
- The paper lacks a thorough discussion on the limitations of the proposed method, which could help in understanding its applicability and potential drawbacks.
- The novelty of the proposed method is questioned, as the core ideas and components are derived from existing works, such as the GlobalModule which closely resembles the codebook module from Kong et al. (2023).
- The paper could benefit from a more detailed analysis of the computational complexity and a comparison with other methods that use different sampling strategies or attention mechanisms.
- The paper does not sufficiently address the scalability of the proposed method to even larger graphs, and the scalability evaluation is limited to the ogbn-papers100M dataset.

**Questions:**
- Could you clarify the differences between the LocalModule and the GlobalModule, and how they contribute uniquely to the LargeGT architecture?
- How does the LocalModule integrate with the GlobalModule to provide a more comprehensive representation of graph data?
- Can you provide more details on the computational complexity of the LargeGT architecture and compare it with other methods that use different sampling strategies or attention mechanisms?
- How does the proposed method scale to even larger graphs, and what are the potential limitations or challenges in scaling up the method?
- Could you elaborate on the choice of datasets and tasks for evaluation, and consider expanding the evaluation to include more diverse graph learning tasks?
- How does the proposed method handle the issue of scalability in graph learning, particularly in terms of the computational cost and the efficiency of the sampling strategy?
