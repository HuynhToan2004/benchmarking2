 **Summary:**
The paper introduces MUBen, a benchmark designed to evaluate the performance of uncertainty quantification (UQ) methods in molecular property prediction tasks. It evaluates various UQ methods across different molecular representation models, including pre-trained models like ChemBERTa and GROVER, and non-pretrained models like DNN and GIN. The benchmark assesses UQ methods using metrics such as negative log-likelihood, Brier score, and calibration error. The study aims to provide insights into the selection of UQ methods for molecular property prediction tasks, which is crucial for applications in fields like materials science and drug discovery.

**Strengths:**
- The paper addresses an important problem in molecular property prediction tasks, specifically focusing on uncertainty quantification (UQ) methods, which is a critical area for improving the reliability and robustness of predictions.
- The introduction of MUBen, a benchmark for evaluating UQ methods, is a significant contribution to the field. This benchmark can facilitate research and development in UQ for molecular property prediction tasks.
- The paper is well-organized and easy to follow, making it accessible to a broad audience. It provides a clear overview of the problem and the proposed solution, which is a benchmark for evaluating UQ methods.
- The paper includes a comprehensive evaluation of various UQ methods across different molecular representation models, which can help guide the selection of UQ methods for specific applications.
- The authors have conducted a thorough evaluation of UQ methods, comparing them across different molecular representation models and tasks, which can provide valuable insights for researchers and practitioners in the field.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper does not adequately address the limitations of the proposed benchmark, such as the potential for overfitting on the MoleculeNet dataset and the generalizability of the results to other datasets or real-world scenarios.
- The paper lacks a detailed discussion on the choice of UQ methods and their effectiveness, particularly in relation to the specific molecular representation models used.
- The paper could benefit from a more detailed analysis of the results, including a comparison of the performance of different UQ methods across different tasks and datasets.
- There is a lack of clarity on how the UQ methods are integrated into the molecular representation models and how this integration affects the model's performance.
- The paper does not sufficiently address the potential societal impacts of the research, which could be significant given the applications in fields like drug discovery and materials science.
- The paper does not provide enough detail on how the uncertainty metrics are calculated, which could limit the reproducibility and transparency of the results.

**Questions:**
