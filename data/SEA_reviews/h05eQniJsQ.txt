 **Summary:**
The paper explores the phenomenon of Interval Bound Propagation (IBP) in the context of certified training, focusing on the tightness of IBP bounds and their impact on robustness. It introduces a new metric, propagation tightness, which measures the gap between optimal and IBP bounds. Theoretical insights are provided on how IBP training affects the tightness of bounds in deep linear models, and how this relates to the robustness of ReLU networks. The paper also includes empirical studies that demonstrate how IBP training influences the tightness of bounds and the performance of certified training methods.

**Strengths:**
- The paper provides a comprehensive analysis of Interval Bound Propagation (IBP) and its tightness, which is crucial for understanding the effectiveness of certified training methods.
- The introduction of the propagation tightness metric offers a new perspective on how IBP bounds can be optimized, potentially leading to more efficient and effective training strategies.
- The theoretical insights presented, particularly in the context of deep linear models, provide a deeper understanding of the mechanisms underlying IBP training.
- The paper is well-structured, with clear explanations and visualizations that aid in understanding complex concepts related to IBP and propagation tightness.
- The experimental results are robust and support the theoretical claims, providing empirical evidence for the effectiveness of IBP-based training methods.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper primarily focuses on deep linear models, which may not fully represent the behavior of IBP in more complex network architectures like CNNs and RNNs.
- The theoretical analysis is limited to two-layer networks, which might not generalize well to deeper or more complex architectures.
- The paper could benefit from a more detailed discussion on the practical implications of the theoretical findings, particularly how they can be applied to improve existing certified training methods.
- The novelty of the paper is questioned, as it primarily extends existing work on tightness in IBP training to a new metric, which may not be sufficiently distinct to warrant a new submission.
- The paper could be clearer in its presentation, especially in the introduction and background sections, to better guide readers unfamiliar with IBP and certified training.

**Questions:**
- How does the tightness of IBP bounds vary with different training methods, such as adversarial training and robust training?
- Can you clarify the definition and role of propagation tightness in the context of IBP training?
- How does the paper's theoretical analysis translate to more complex network architectures, such as CNNs and RNNs?
- Could you provide more details on how the theoretical insights can be applied to improve existing certified training methods?
- Is there a possibility to extend the theoretical analysis to deeper or more complex network architectures?
- How does the paper's findings compare to other metrics or approaches for evaluating the effectiveness of IBP in certified training?
