 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel approach to modeling irregular time series data using Neural Stochastic Differential Equations (Neural SDEs), specifically focusing on three classes: Langevin-type SDE, Linear Noise SDE, and Geometric SDE. These classes are designed to address the challenges posed by irregular sampling intervals and missing values in real-world time series data. The paper provides a theoretical analysis of the existence and uniqueness of solutions for these SDEs, alongside empirical evaluations demonstrating their robustness and effectiveness. The authors claim that these methods outperform existing models in handling irregular time series data, although concerns are raised about the novelty and the depth of the theoretical analysis.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel approach using Neural SDEs to model irregular time series data, which is a significant advancement in the field.
- The theoretical analysis provided is robust, including proofs of the existence and uniqueness of solutions for the proposed SDEs, which is crucial for understanding their behavior and reliability.
- The paper is well-written, making it accessible and easy to follow, which is essential for a broad audience.
- The experimental results are comprehensive, demonstrating the effectiveness of the proposed methods across various tasks and datasets.
- The paper provides a detailed analysis of the robustness of the proposed methods, particularly under distribution shift, which is a critical aspect for practical applications.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a thorough literature review, particularly in the area of stochastic differential equations, which could have provided a richer context and a clearer differentiation from existing works.
- The novelty of the approach is questionable as the classes of SDEs proposed appear to be well-known, and the theoretical contributions are not sufficiently novel or substantial.
- The paper does not adequately address the computational complexity and memory requirements of the proposed methods, which could limit their practical applicability in real-world scenarios.
- The theoretical analysis provided, while robust, is somewhat shallow and does not sufficiently explore the complexities and nuances of the proposed methods.
- The paper does not provide a detailed discussion on the limitations of the proposed methods, which is crucial for understanding their applicability and scope.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors provide a more detailed discussion on the novelty of the approach, particularly in comparison to existing works in the field of Neural SDEs?
- How does the proposed method compare in terms of computational complexity and memory requirements to other existing methods?
- Can the authors elaborate on the theoretical contributions of their work, particularly in terms of how these advancements address the specific challenges posed by irregular time series data?
- How do the proposed methods perform when the drift and diffusion functions are non-differentiable, and what are the implications of this for the model's stability and accuracy?
- Could the authors provide a more detailed analysis of the practical implications of the theoretical results, particularly in terms of how these findings can be applied in real-world scenarios?
