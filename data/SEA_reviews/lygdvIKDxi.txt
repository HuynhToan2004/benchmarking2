 **Summary:**
The paper introduces SEEKER, a query-efficient model extraction attack that utilizes semantic alignment to learn generalizable image encoding patterns from publicly available data. This method optimizes the substitute model without interacting with the victim model, aiming to reduce the number of queries required for constructing the substitute model. Extensive experiments demonstrate that SEEKER achieves state-of-the-art performance with significantly fewer queries, improving query-efficiency by up to 50x and maintaining high accuracy. The methodology includes a query generator that enhances information density and a semantic alignment approach that trains the substitute model based on semantic consistency of neural networks. The paper also discusses the potential for bypassing various defense mechanisms.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel approach for query-efficient model extraction, leveraging publicly available data to reduce the number of queries required for constructing the substitute model.
- The proposed method is technically sound, with a focus on improving query-efficiency in model extraction attacks, a topic that has received limited attention in the literature.
- The experiments are well-designed, and the results are strong, demonstrating the effectiveness of the proposed method in reducing the number of queries needed to extract a model with high accuracy and attack success rate.
- The paper is well-written and easy to follow, with clear explanations of the methodology and results.
- The use of publicly available data in model extraction is a novel approach, which could potentially reduce the number of queries needed to extract a model.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks clarity in some sections, particularly in the presentation of the query generation process and the details of the semantic alignment approach.
- The assumption that the victim model maintains semantic consistency may not hold in practical scenarios, which could limit the applicability of the proposed method.
- The paper does not adequately address the potential limitations of the proposed method, such as its performance on different model architectures and its robustness against various defense mechanisms.
- The paper does not provide a detailed discussion on the computational complexity of the proposed method, which could be a significant concern for practical implementation.
- There is a lack of comparative analysis with other methods that also use publicly available data for model extraction, which could help in understanding the relative advantages of the proposed method.

**Questions:**
- Could you provide a more detailed explanation of the query generation process and the specifics of the semantic alignment approach?
- How does the proposed method perform when the victim model does not maintain semantic consistency?
- Could you provide a more detailed discussion on the computational complexity of the proposed method and how it compares to other model extraction methods?
- How does the proposed method perform against different defense mechanisms, and what are the specific strategies for bypassing these defenses?
- Could you provide a comparative analysis with other methods that also use publicly available data for model extraction, to better understand the relative advantages of the proposed method?
