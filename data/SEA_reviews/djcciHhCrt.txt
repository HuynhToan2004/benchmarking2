 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel attack method leveraging adversarial images to manipulate Large Language Models (LLMs) into executing malicious tool invocations. The attack is designed to be stealthy, maintaining the image's original appearance and not altering the conversation flow. The methodology involves optimizing the adversarial perturbations to the input image while ensuring the responses remain natural and indistinguishable from clean responses. The paper presents an evaluation using both human and automated metrics, demonstrating the attack's effectiveness and stealth. However, concerns are raised about the generalizability of the attack across different models and the need for a broader evaluation on more diverse datasets.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel attack method that leverages adversarial images to manipulate LLMs, which is a significant advancement in the field of security against LLMs.
- The methodology is well-explained, with a clear description of the attack and the optimization process, making it accessible to readers.
- The paper is well-written, with a clear presentation of the experimental setup, including the evaluation metrics used.
- The paper demonstrates the effectiveness of the proposed attack method through a variety of experiments, including human evaluations, which support the claims of the paper.
- The paper is timely and addresses a critical issue of security against LLMs, which is increasingly relevant as these models become more integrated into various applications.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper primarily evaluates the attack on a single LLM, which limits the generalizability of the results to other models.
- The paper lacks a broader evaluation on more diverse datasets, which could provide a more comprehensive understanding of the attack's effectiveness and generalizability.
- There is a concern about the stealthiness of the attack, as the attack's success rates are high, which could suggest that the attack might not be entirely stealthy.
- The paper could benefit from a more detailed discussion on the limitations of the proposed attack, including potential countermeasures and the impact of different hyperparameters on the attack's effectiveness.
- The paper could be improved by including more diverse attack variants and a more comprehensive evaluation of the attack's robustness against different types of defenses.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors provide more details on how the attack might be adapted to other LLMs, especially those with different architectures or training methodologies?
- How does the proposed attack perform against different types of defenses, such as those designed to detect or prevent adversarial attacks?
- Could the authors discuss the potential impact of different hyperparameters on the attack's effectiveness and provide guidelines for selecting these parameters in practical applications?
- How does the proposed attack compare to other existing attack methods in terms of effectiveness and stealthiness?
- Can the authors provide more examples or case studies to demonstrate the attack's effectiveness and generalizability across different scenarios?
- Could the authors clarify the methodology used in the evaluation, particularly the selection of the test set and the criteria for evaluating the attack's success?
