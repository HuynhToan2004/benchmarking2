 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel method for audio-visual navigation, specifically focusing on the "Find Fallen Object" task. It proposes a Disentangled Acoustic Field (DAF) model that disentangles the latent space to predict object properties such as type, position, and material, leveraging a power spectral density (PSD) representation for audio signals. The model is trained on a dataset of 8000 instances, demonstrating improved performance over existing methods like Gan et al. (2022). The paper also discusses the use of this model for navigation and object localization, using a combination of audio and visual cues. The methodology involves a neural network that encodes audio signals into a latent space, which is then used to predict object properties and navigate to the object's location.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, making it easy to follow and understand, with clear explanations of the methodology and results.
- The proposed method is novel, introducing a new approach to audio-visual navigation that effectively disentangles audio signals, which is a significant advancement in the field.
- The paper provides strong experimental results, demonstrating the effectiveness of the proposed method in object localization and navigation tasks.
- The use of a power spectral density (PSD) representation for audio signals is innovative and shows potential for improving the accuracy and generalization of audio-visual navigation models.
- The paper is well-organized, with clear explanations of the methodology, experimental setup, and results, which are supported by detailed figures and tables.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed discussion on the limitations of the proposed method, which could help in understanding the scope and applicability of the findings.
- There is a lack of comparison with more recent and relevant methods, which could provide a clearer picture of the method's position within the current research landscape.
- The paper could benefit from more comprehensive ablation studies to better understand the contributions of different components of the model.
- The paper does not adequately address the issue of cross-scene generalization, which is crucial for practical applications.
- The paper's focus on a specific task (Find Fallen Object) might limit its broader applicability, and the generalization of the findings to other tasks or scenarios is not clearly demonstrated.
- There are some minor issues with the presentation, such as unclear descriptions and inconsistencies in the use of terminology and notation.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors clarify the choice of using a power spectral density (PSD) representation for audio signals and discuss any potential limitations or challenges associated with this approach?
- How does the proposed method compare to more recent and relevant methods in terms of performance and efficiency?
- Could the authors provide more detailed results on the cross-scene generalization of the model, particularly how it performs when tested on datasets that differ significantly from the training data?
- How does the method handle scenarios where the audio signal is noisy or incomplete, and what are the implications for the accuracy and reliability of the predictions?
- Can the authors discuss the potential applications of the proposed method beyond the specific task of audio-visual navigation, and how it might be adapted or extended to other domains or tasks?
