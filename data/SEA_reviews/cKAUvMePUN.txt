 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel end-to-end visual prosthesis framework named StimuSEE, which generates stimulation patterns based on V1 neuron spike patterns to restore vision in blind individuals. The framework comprises a retinal network, a phosphene model, and a primary visual system network (PVS-net), designed to mimic the neural response in the visual cortex. The proposed method aims to predict stimulation patterns that elicit neural responses similar to those observed in normal vision, thus potentially improving the quality of vision restoration. The framework is evaluated using the DVS-V1 dataset, showing promising results in terms of neural response prediction. However, concerns are raised about the practical applicability of the framework, the clarity of the presentation, and the limited scope of the evaluation.

**Strengths:**
- The paper addresses an important and relevant problem in the field of visual prostheses, which is crucial for restoring vision in blind individuals.
- The proposed framework, StimuSEE, is innovative, integrating a retina network, a phosphene model, and a primary visual system network (PVS-net) to generate stimulation patterns that mimic neural responses in the visual cortex.
- The methodology is well-explained, with clear figures and detailed descriptions of the components of the framework, making it accessible and understandable.
- The paper presents a novel approach by utilizing neural spike patterns as supervision to generate stimulation patterns, which is a significant advancement in the field.
- The evaluation of the proposed method using the DVS-V1 dataset shows promising results, indicating the potential effectiveness of the framework.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks clarity in explaining the methodology, particularly in how the stimulation patterns are generated and how they relate to the neural responses observed.
- The practical applicability of the framework is questionable, as it requires a large amount of neural data, which may not be available or practical for real-world applications.
- The experimental setup and the evaluation of the framework are limited, with only a single dataset used and no real-world testing or validation.
- The paper does not adequately address the computational efficiency or power consumption of the framework, which is critical for real-world applications.
- There are numerous typos and grammatical errors throughout the paper, which detract from its professionalism and readability.
- The paper lacks a comprehensive literature review, which could have provided a better context for the research and highlighted the novelty of the contributions.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors clarify how the stimulation patterns are generated and how they relate to the neural responses observed in the V1 cortex?
- How does the framework handle variations in neural responses due to different stimuli or individual differences in neural responses?
- What are the specific advantages of using V1 neuron spike patterns as supervision over other types of neural responses or image-based supervision?
- How does the framework ensure that the stimulation patterns are effective in restoring vision, especially in terms of spatial and temporal resolution?
- Can the authors provide more details on the computational efficiency and power consumption of the framework, especially in comparison to other state-of-the-art methods?
- How does the framework handle the complexity of neural responses in the visual cortex, particularly in terms of the number of neurons and the diversity of responses?
- Can the authors provide a more detailed explanation of the biological plausibility of the phosphene model and how it relates to real-world neural responses?
