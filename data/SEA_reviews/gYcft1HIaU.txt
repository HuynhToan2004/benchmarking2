 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a new evaluation framework, MedDisKEval, designed to assess the clinical knowledge mastery of Large Language Models (LLMs) by constructing a comprehensive medical disease-based knowledge base, MedDisK. This framework evaluates LLMs by prompting them to retrieve information related to 18 clinical knowledge aspects and comparing their outputs to the knowledge base. The results indicate that over 50% of the generated clinical information by LLMs is inconsistent with the knowledge base. Furthermore, the paper demonstrates that most current medical LLMs underperform compared to their backbone models, suggesting a lack of mastery of clinical knowledge. The framework's novelty lies in its ability to evaluate LLMs based on a large-scale medical disease-based knowledge base, which is a significant advancement in the field.

**Strengths:**
- The paper presents a novel framework for evaluating the clinical knowledge mastery of LLMs, which is crucial for their application in real-world clinical scenarios.
- The construction of a large-scale medical disease-based knowledge base, MedDisK, is a significant contribution to the field, providing a valuable resource for evaluating LLMs' clinical knowledge.
- The evaluation methodology is well-designed, employing multiple metrics and expert-aligned grading to ensure a comprehensive assessment of LLMs' clinical knowledge.
- The paper is well-written, clear, and easy to follow, making it accessible to a broad audience.
- The results are well-presented, and the analysis is clear, providing insights into the current state of LLMs in terms of clinical knowledge mastery.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper's reliance on a single knowledge base, MedDisK, raises concerns about the generalizability of the findings to other knowledge bases or domains.
- The evaluation methodology is limited to a specific set of clinical knowledge aspects, which might not fully capture the breadth of clinical knowledge required for real-world applications.
- The paper does not include a detailed comparison with existing evaluation frameworks, which could have provided a clearer context for the proposed method's contributions.
- The paper lacks a discussion on how the results might vary with different knowledge bases or domains, which could affect the robustness of the findings.
- The methodology does not include a validation process to ensure the accuracy and reliability of the knowledge base, which is crucial for the credibility of the results.
- The paper does not address the potential limitations of the proposed evaluation framework, which could affect the practical applicability and the generalizability of the findings.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors provide a more detailed explanation of how the prompting strategy for different categories of LLMs was determined?
- How does the knowledge base, MedDisK, ensure its accuracy and reliability? Could the authors provide more details on the validation process for the knowledge base?
- Could the authors clarify the methodology used for calculating the similarity between LLM-generated information and the content within the knowledge base?
- How do the authors address the potential limitations of the proposed evaluation framework, and what steps are being taken to ensure the framework's applicability and generalizability?
- The paper mentions that the knowledge base, MedDisK, is constructed with a collaborative effort between clinical experts and machine assistance. Could the authors provide more details on this process, including the number of experts involved and the specifics of the machine assistance used?
- The paper discusses the potential limitations of the proposed evaluation framework. Could the authors elaborate on these limitations and how they might affect the practical applicability and the generalizability of the findings?
