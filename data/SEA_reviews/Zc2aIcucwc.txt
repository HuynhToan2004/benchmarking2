 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a suite of molecular datasets, ranging from 100k to 13B data points, along with a Graphium library for training large-scale graph neural networks (GNNs) on these datasets. The datasets cover a variety of tasks such as quantum and biological properties, and the library facilitates the handling of large-scale datasets and complex feature interactions. The paper claims that the datasets are orders of magnitude larger than current state-of-the-art datasets and that the Graphium library simplifies the process of building and training molecular machine learning models. However, the paper lacks detailed comparisons with existing datasets and libraries, and the experimental results do not convincingly demonstrate the superiority of the proposed methods over existing ones.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a suite of molecular datasets that are orders of magnitude larger than current state-of-the-art datasets, providing a significant advancement in the field.
- The Graphium library is a novel contribution that simplifies the process of building and training molecular machine learning models, offering a streamlined approach for researchers and practitioners.
- The datasets are meticulously curated and enhanced, ensuring high quality and relevance to the field of molecular machine learning.
- The paper provides a comprehensive overview of the datasets, their characteristics, and the Graphium library, making it accessible and useful for a broad audience.
- The datasets cover a wide range of molecular properties, including quantum and biological properties, and are designed to improve the generalization ability of foundation models for molecular modeling.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks detailed comparisons with existing datasets and libraries, making it difficult to assess the relative advantages of the proposed datasets and library.
- The experimental results presented in the paper do not convincingly demonstrate the superiority of the proposed methods over existing ones, especially in terms of performance metrics like MAE, Pearson, and R2.
- The paper does not provide a detailed discussion on how the Graphium library handles missing data and joint training, which are critical aspects of molecular machine learning.
- The datasets, while large, do not necessarily represent a significant improvement in terms of the number of molecules or tasks compared to existing datasets like OGB-LSC PCQM4MV2 and QM1B.
- The paper does not adequately address the challenges posed by the complexity of molecular data, which could limit the applicability of the proposed datasets and library.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors provide a more detailed comparison of the proposed datasets and Graphium library with existing datasets and libraries, highlighting the unique advantages and contributions of the proposed approach?
- How does the Graphium library handle missing data and joint training, and what are the implications of these features for the performance and applicability of the library?
- In the experimental results presented, what are the specific contributions of the Graphium library to the observed improvements in performance, and how do these improvements compare to the baseline models used?
- Could the authors provide more detailed insights into the handling of quantum and biological properties in the datasets, and how these properties are integrated into the training and prediction processes?
- How do the authors envision the use of these datasets and the Graphium library in real-world applications, and what are the potential limitations or challenges that might arise in practical implementations?
