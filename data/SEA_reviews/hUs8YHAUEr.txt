 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel method for enhancing large language models (LLMs) in code generation tasks by leveraging multi-perspective self-consistency (MPSC). This approach involves generating diverse outputs from multiple perspectives, constructing a multipartite graph based on these outputs, and embedding consistency information into the graph. The method is evaluated across several benchmarks and demonstrates significant improvements over existing models like GPT-4. The MPSC framework is designed to incorporate both inter-consistency across outputs from different perspectives and intra-consistency within a single perspective, using a graph-based representation to optimize the selection of outputs based on consistency measures.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel method for enhancing the performance of LLMs in complex reasoning tasks, particularly in code generation, by leveraging multi-perspective self-consistency (MPSC).
- The MPSC framework is a model-agnostic and task-agnostic approach that can be applied to various textual generation tasks, making it versatile and adaptable.
- The paper is well-written, easy to follow, and provides a comprehensive evaluation of the MPSC framework on several popular benchmarks, demonstrating its effectiveness in improving the performance of LLMs.
- The authors have conducted a thorough analysis of the MPSC framework, including an ablation study and analysis of different perspectives, which helps in understanding the impact of different components of the framework.
- The MPSC framework has shown significant improvements over existing models like GPT-4, indicating its potential to outperform state-of-the-art models.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed discussion on the limitations and potential negative societal impacts of the MPSC framework, which could be crucial for understanding its broader implications.
- The methodology section is not clearly explained, particularly the process of constructing a multipartite graph based on outputs and the implementation details of the graph.
- The paper does not adequately discuss the scalability of the MPSC framework, especially in terms of computational complexity and resource requirements.
- There is a lack of clarity in the evaluation section regarding the selection criteria for the benchmarks and the significance of the improvements achieved.
- The paper does not sufficiently compare the MPSC framework with other existing methods, which could provide a better understanding of its advantages and disadvantages.
- The paper does not discuss the potential limitations of the MPSC framework, such as its applicability to different types of LLMs and its performance in scenarios where there is a lack of consistency in the generated outputs.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors provide more details on how the MPSC framework handles scenarios where there is a lack of consistency in the generated outputs?
- How does the MPSC framework ensure the reliability and accuracy of the generated outputs, especially when there are significant variations in the outputs from different perspectives?
- Could the authors elaborate on the scalability of the MPSC framework, particularly in terms of computational complexity and resource requirements?
- What are the specific implementation details of the graph used in the MPSC framework, and how are the outputs and edges constructed?
- How does the MPSC framework compare with other existing methods for improving the performance of LLMs in code generation tasks?
- Could the authors provide more insights into the limitations and potential negative societal impacts of the MPSC framework?
