 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel sampling strategy called Condition-Annealed Diffusion Sampling (CADS) to address the issue of low diversity in conditional diffusion models. This method perturbs the conditioning signal with Gaussian noise, which is gradually reduced during the sampling process, allowing the model to explore a broader range of outputs. The proposed method is evaluated across various conditional generation tasks and shown to enhance diversity while maintaining sample quality. The paper also explores the impact of noise injection on the alignment between the input condition and the generated image, suggesting that the proposed method does not compromise this alignment significantly. Despite its effectiveness in increasing diversity, the paper is criticized for its simplicity and lack of comprehensive analysis, particularly in the context of larger datasets and more complex models.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel method, CADS, which is simple yet effective in enhancing the diversity of outputs in conditional diffusion models.
- The method is straightforward to implement and can be integrated with any pretrained model and sampling algorithm, making it accessible and versatile.
- The experimental results demonstrate that CADS significantly improves the diversity of diffusion models in various conditional generation tasks, as shown in Figure 1 and Table 1.
- The proposed method can be applied to any pretrained model and sampling algorithm, making it a versatile approach that can be adopted in a wide range of applications.
- The paper provides a comprehensive evaluation of the proposed method, including a comparison with DDPM and other sampling strategies, and shows that CADS achieves a new state-of-the-art FID on class-conditional ImageNet generation at 256x256 and 512x512 resolutions.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed analysis of the proposed method, particularly in terms of the impact of noise injection on the alignment between the input condition and the generated image.
- The method's effectiveness is not thoroughly analyzed in scenarios where the training dataset is large, such as ImageNet, which could limit its applicability in practical settings.
- The paper does not include a comprehensive comparison with other methods for enhancing the diversity of diffusion models, which could provide a more robust evaluation of the proposed method.
- The paper lacks a detailed discussion on the limitations of the proposed method, particularly in terms of its applicability to broader conditioning contexts and the potential for increased computational cost.
- The paper could benefit from a more detailed discussion on the theoretical implications of the proposed method, particularly in relation to the Bayesian formulation of diffusion models.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors provide a more detailed analysis of the impact of noise injection on the alignment between the input condition and the generated image?
- How does the proposed method perform in scenarios where the training dataset is very large, such as ImageNet?
- Could the authors provide more details on the computational cost of the proposed method compared to other sampling strategies?
- How does the proposed method compare to other methods for enhancing the diversity of diffusion models, particularly in terms of effectiveness and computational efficiency?
- Could the authors provide more theoretical insights into the proposed method, particularly in relation to the Bayesian formulation of diffusion models?
