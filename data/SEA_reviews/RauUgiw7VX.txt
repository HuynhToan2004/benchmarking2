 **Summary:**
The paper introduces SeReDiff, a diffusion-based method for fine-grained text-to-image synthesis, which enhances the semantic detailing of text prompts by emphasizing specific words. This approach utilizes a semantic-induced gradient to guide the diffusion process, allowing for the synthesis of images with higher semantic detail. The methodology involves a two-stage denoising process and a semantic-induced gradient computation from reference images, which helps in refining the generated images. The paper also explores the use of gradient as a condition to guide the diffusion process, which is a novel approach in the field. Despite its innovative approach, the paper has been critiqued for its limited evaluation on a small dataset, lack of clarity in some methodological explanations, and potential issues with the visual quality of generated images.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel approach using a gradient as a condition to guide the diffusion process, which is a significant advancement in the field of text-to-image synthesis.
- The methodology is well-explained, with clear figures and detailed descriptions that facilitate understanding.
- The proposed method achieves good performance on the CUB dataset and shows promising results in generating fine-grained details.
- The paper is well-written, making it easy to follow, and provides a comprehensive literature review.
- The introduction of a semantic-induced gradient to guide the diffusion process is a novel and interesting contribution to the field.
- The paper includes a user study to assess the photorealism, text alignment, and detail matching of the generated images, which adds to the credibility of the results.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a comprehensive evaluation across various datasets, particularly open-world datasets, which limits the generalizability of the findings.
- The visual quality of the generated images is not consistently high, with some images appearing blurry or lacking detail.
- The explanation of the semantic-induced gradient and its computation from reference images is not clear, which could hinder understanding of the method.
- The paper does not provide sufficient details on the retrieval of reference images from the database, which is crucial for the method's effectiveness.
- The paper's methodology section could benefit from more detailed explanations and clarifications, particularly regarding the use of gradient as a condition and the computational details of the semantic-induced gradient.
- The paper does not sufficiently address the limitations of the proposed method, such as the potential for over-emphasizing specific semantics at the expense of others.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors clarify the computational details of the semantic-induced gradient, particularly how it is computed from reference images?
- How are the reference images retrieved from the database, and what are the criteria for their selection?
- What is the impact of the semantic-induced gradient on the overall performance of the model, and how does it compare to other methods like TediGAN or LAFITE?
- Can the authors provide more details on the retrieval process for reference images, possibly including visual examples or a detailed algorithm description?
- How does the method handle the potential for over-emphasizing specific semantics, and what measures are in place to prevent this?
- Could the authors provide more qualitative results, such as additional images or comparisons, to better demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed method?
