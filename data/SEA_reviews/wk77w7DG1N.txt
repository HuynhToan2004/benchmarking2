 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel framework for evaluating the consistency of text generated by Large Language Models (LLMs) using a divide-and-conquer strategy. The approach involves breaking down the comparison into individual sentences, each evaluated based on predefined criteria. The framework includes an automatic metric converter that translates the output from the Divide-Conquer Evaluator (DCE) into an interpretable numeric score. The paper also presents a reason-assisted improver (RAI) that leverages analytical reasons with explanations identified by DCE to generate new responses aimed at reducing inconsistencies. The framework has been tested on various benchmarks and has shown significant improvements over existing methods in evaluating the consistency of LLM generation.

**Strengths:**
- The paper addresses an important and relevant problem in the field of Large Language Models (LLMs) by focusing on the evaluation of consistency in generated text, which is crucial for ensuring the reliability and safety of LLMs in high-stakes applications.
- The proposed framework is innovative, utilizing a divide-and-conquer strategy that breaks down the comparison between two generated responses into individual sentences, which is a novel approach that could be more effective in evaluating consistency than traditional methods.
- The paper is well-written, clear, and easy to follow, with comprehensive experimental results that demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed method.
- The methodology is adaptable and can be applied to various tasks, including summarization and factual consistency evaluations, showing significant improvements over existing methods.
- The paper provides a detailed analysis of the proposed framework, including the use of an automatic metric converter that translates the output from the Divide-Conquer Evaluator (DCE) into an interpretable numeric score, which is a useful feature for evaluating the consistency of LLM-generated text.

**Weaknesses:**
