 **Summary:**
The paper introduces LayoutDETR, a novel approach for multimodal graphic layout design that leverages a DETR-based transformer to detect and generate layouts conditioned on multimodal inputs, including text and images. This methodology integrates a generative model with a transformer architecture, which allows for the detection of layout parameters and the generation of realistic and aesthetically pleasing designs. The paper also presents a new ad banner dataset with rich semantic annotations, which is used to benchmark the performance of the proposed method against existing baselines. The evaluation shows that LayoutDETR outperforms state-of-the-art methods in terms of realism, accuracy, and regularity, as measured by various metrics.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel approach by combining a DETR-based transformer with a generative model, which effectively addresses the challenges of multimodal graphic layout design.
- The methodology is well-documented, with a clear and detailed explanation of the proposed framework, including the use of a large-scale ad banner dataset with rich semantic annotations.
- The paper is well-written, making it easy to follow, and includes a comprehensive evaluation that demonstrates the superiority of the proposed method over existing baselines.
- The integration of a graphical system for user studies adds a practical application aspect, allowing for a more comprehensive evaluation of the generated designs.
- The paper presents a significant contribution to the field by proposing a new dataset and methodology that can potentially advance research in multimodal graphic layout design.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed discussion on the limitations of the proposed method, which could provide valuable insights for future research and development.
- The evaluation metrics used are not clearly defined or justified, which could limit the understanding and reproducibility of the results.
- The paper does not sufficiently differentiate its approach from existing methods, particularly in terms of its technical novelty and the specific contributions of the DETR-based transformer.
- There is a lack of discussion on the societal implications and potential negative societal impacts of the proposed method, which is crucial for ensuring ethical and responsible use.
- The paper could benefit from more detailed comparisons with existing methods, particularly in terms of the technical challenges and advantages of the proposed method over these baselines.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors clarify the specific technical challenges addressed by the proposed method and how it compares to existing methods in terms of performance and efficiency?
- How does the DETR-based transformer contribute uniquely to the proposed method, and what specific advantages does it offer over other potential architectures?
- Could the authors provide more detailed results and comparisons with existing methods, particularly in terms of the impact of different input modalities on the performance of the system?
- What are the potential negative societal impacts of the proposed method, and how do the authors plan to address these concerns?
- How does the paper ensure the fairness and accuracy of the user study, and what measures were taken to prevent bias or manipulation in the results?
