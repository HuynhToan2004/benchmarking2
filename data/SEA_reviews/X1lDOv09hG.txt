 **Summary:**
The paper investigates the generalization ability of diffusion models, focusing on the high variance of score function estimates at early times. It proposes that this variance is crucial for diffusion models to generalize effectively. The authors provide a theoretical analysis of the score function and the sampling process, highlighting how the variance of the score function during early sampling times contributes to the generalization of diffusion models. They argue that this variance, which is similar to a kernel in kernel density estimation, is a key factor in diffusion models' ability to generalize beyond their training set. The paper also discusses the role of time sampling and variance normalization in mitigating the large variance issue.

**Strengths:**
- The paper provides a clear and concise explanation of the main ideas, making it easy to follow and understand.
- The theoretical analysis is well-structured and supported by rigorous proofs, which enhance the credibility of the claims.
- The paper introduces a novel perspective on the generalization ability of diffusion models, focusing on the high variance of score function estimates at early times.
- The authors present a clear and coherent argument, supported by examples and theoretical analysis, which helps in understanding the implications of their findings.
- The paper is well-written, with clear definitions and mathematical formulations that aid in understanding the theoretical underpinnings of the study.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper could benefit from more comprehensive empirical evidence to support the theoretical claims, particularly in demonstrating the generalization capabilities of diffusion models.
- The paper primarily focuses on the theoretical aspects of diffusion models, with limited discussion on the practical implications or potential applications of the findings.
- The assumption that the score estimator is linear in features may not be realistic in practical scenarios, which could limit the applicability of the results.
- The paper lacks a detailed discussion on how the theoretical insights could be applied to improve diffusion models in practice.
- There is a lack of comparison with existing methods, which could help in understanding the novelty and effectiveness of the proposed approach.
- The paper does not adequately address the limitations of the study, particularly the assumptions made and the potential impact of these assumptions on the validity of the results.
