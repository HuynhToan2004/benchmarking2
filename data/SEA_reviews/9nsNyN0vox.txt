 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel method called "Neural Comprehension" that integrates Compiled Neural Networks (CoNNs) with large language models (LLMs) to enhance the models' ability to perform symbolic reasoning and arithmetic operations. CoNNs are designed to execute rules through artificially generated attention weights, and their integration allows for the handling of symbolic reasoning tasks more effectively than traditional LLMs. The methodology involves training CoNNs on specific tasks and then integrating them with LLMs to improve performance on these tasks. The paper presents several experiments demonstrating the effectiveness of this approach, comparing it to other methods like few-shot learning and vanilla fine-tuning. However, concerns were raised about the scalability and generalizability of the CoNNs, as well as the clarity of the presentation and the methodological details.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, clear, and easy to follow, making it accessible to a broad audience.
- The proposed method of integrating Compiled Neural Networks (CoNNs) with Large Language Models (LLMs) is innovative and has shown promising results in improving the performance of LLMs on symbolic reasoning tasks.
- The experiments conducted are comprehensive and demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed method, particularly in the context of symbolic reasoning tasks.
- The paper provides detailed implementation details, which are crucial for reproducibility and further research.
- The AutoCoNN toolkit introduced in the paper is a significant contribution, allowing for the automatic generation of CoNNs, which is a practical and scalable approach.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a clear motivation for the choice of CoNNs over other methods like PAL or ToolFormer, and does not sufficiently compare the performance of CoNNs with these alternatives.
- The scalability and generalizability of CoNNs are questionable, as they are currently limited to specific tasks and may not generalize well to other types of symbolic reasoning or arithmetic operations.
- The paper could benefit from a more detailed discussion on the limitations of the proposed method, including potential negative societal impacts and ethical considerations.
- The presentation of the paper could be improved, particularly in terms of clarity and the organization of figures and tables.
- There are several minor typographical and formatting errors that need to be corrected.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors clarify the choice of CoNNs over other methods like PAL or ToolFormer, and provide a more detailed comparison of their performance?
- How do the authors ensure the scalability and generalizability of CoNNs to other types of symbolic reasoning or arithmetic operations?
- Could the authors provide more details on the training process for CoNNs, including the data used and the hyperparameters employed?
- How does the proposed method handle the integration of CoNNs with LLMs, particularly in terms of training and inference?
- Can the authors provide more examples or case studies to illustrate the practical applications of the AutoCoNN toolkit?
- How do the authors address the potential negative societal impacts and ethical considerations of their proposed method?
