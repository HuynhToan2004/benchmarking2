 **Summary:**
The paper addresses the challenges of over-correlation and over-smoothing in graph neural network (GNN) collaborative filtering models for recommender systems. It introduces the Adaptive Feature De-correlation Graph Collaborative Filtering (AFDGCF) framework, which dynamically applies correlation penalties to the feature dimensions of the representation matrix to mitigate these issues. The paper provides both theoretical and empirical evidence to substantiate the prevalence of over-correlation in GNN-based CF models and its negative impact on model performance. Extensive experiments are conducted across four different graph collaborative filtering models, demonstrating the effectiveness of the proposed method. However, the paper is criticized for its limited novelty and the lack of comprehensive comparison with existing methods, particularly in terms of computational complexity and parameter count.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, making it easy to follow, and provides both theoretical and empirical evidence to substantiate the prevalence of over-correlation in GNN-based CF models and its negative impact on model performance.
- The proposed AFDGCF framework is model-agnostic and effectively mitigates the influence of over-correlation by strategically managing feature correlations.
- Extensive experiments are conducted across four different graph collaborative filtering models, demonstrating the effectiveness of the proposed method.
- The paper provides a novel approach to address the over-correlation problem in GNN-based CF models, which is a significant issue in the field of recommender systems.
- The paper is well-organized and easy to understand, with a clear motivation and a detailed analysis of the relationship between over-correlation and over-smoothing.

**Weaknesses:**
- The novelty of the proposed method is limited, as it mainly introduces a regularization term to penalize the correlation between features, which has been previously explored in other works.
- The paper lacks a comprehensive comparison with existing methods in terms of computational complexity and parameter count, which are crucial for evaluating the efficiency and scalability of the proposed method.
- The motivation behind the proposed method is not convincingly argued, particularly in relation to the claims about over-correlation being a more significant issue than over-smoothing.
- The paper could benefit from a more thorough comparison with other state-of-the-art methods in terms of performance metrics and experimental settings.
- The paper does not adequately address the issue of over-smoothing, which is crucial for understanding the performance of GNN-based CF models.
- The experimental results and the comparisons with baseline models are not sufficiently robust, with some results showing marginal improvements or even degraded performance.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors provide a more detailed comparison of the computational complexity and parameter count of their method with existing methods to demonstrate the efficiency and scalability of their approach?
- How does the proposed method compare in terms of performance with other state-of-the-art methods in terms of performance metrics such as NDCG@K, Recall@K, and MAP@K?
- Could the authors elaborate on the motivation behind their proposed method and why they believe that over-correlation is a more significant issue than over-smoothing?
- How does the proposed method perform in scenarios with varying numbers of layers in the GNN-based CF models, and what are the implications for performance and computational efficiency?
- The paper claims that the proposed method addresses the over-correlation problem in GNN-based CF models. Can the authors provide more detailed experimental results and comparisons to substantiate this claim?
