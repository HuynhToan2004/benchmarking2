 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel framework named Compose and Conquer (CnC) for text-conditional image synthesis, focusing on the integration of depth maps and CLIP image embeddings to enhance control over object placement and global semantics. The framework leverages depth disentanglement training and soft guidance mechanisms to manage depth information and global semantics, respectively. The CnC model is designed to handle multiple conditions and is evaluated against various baselines, demonstrating its effectiveness in controlling depth and global semantics. Despite its innovative approach, the paper is criticized for its limited evaluation, lack of clarity in certain methodological details, and potential issues with the generalization of its components.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel approach to depth disentanglement training and soft guidance, which are significant contributions to the field of text-to-image synthesis.
- The methodology is well-articulated, with clear descriptions of the training process and the integration of depth maps and CLIP image embeddings.
- The proposed framework shows effectiveness in controlling depth and global semantics, as demonstrated by qualitative and quantitative results.
- The paper is well-written and easy to follow, with comprehensive explanations of the model's architecture and training processes.
- The use of depth disentanglement training and soft guidance allows for the handling of multiple conditions and the control of depth and global semantics in text-to-image synthesis.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a thorough evaluation of the depth disentanglement training and soft guidance mechanisms, which are crucial components of the proposed framework.
- The generalization of the proposed components, such as the local and global fusers, is not adequately addressed, raising concerns about their applicability in different scenarios.
- The paper could benefit from more diverse and challenging datasets to better evaluate the model's performance across various scenarios.
- The integration of depth information and CLIP image embeddings is not clearly described, and the paper could benefit from a more detailed explanation of how these components interact within the model.
- The paper's focus on depth control might limit its applicability to other image attributes, such as color or texture.
- There are some issues with the clarity of the writing, particularly in the methodology section, which could be improved for better understanding.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors clarify the training details for the local and global fusers, including the specifics of how they are initialized and trained?
- How does the proposed framework handle multiple depth maps, and what are the implications for the model's performance in such scenarios?
- Could the authors provide more details on the integration of depth information and CLIP image embeddings within the model, and how these components influence the final output?
- How does the proposed method compare in terms of performance and efficiency with other recent works in the field of text-to-image synthesis?
- Can the authors elaborate on the methodology used for generating the depth maps from inpainted images, and how these maps are used to control the depth of objects in the generated images?
- How does the model handle scenarios where the depth information is not accurate or where multiple depth maps conflict with each other?
