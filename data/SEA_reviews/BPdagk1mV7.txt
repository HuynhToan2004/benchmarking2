 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel implicit semi-auto-regressive approach for image-to-video (I2V) synthesis using diffusion models. This methodology involves a look-back convolution layer to capture temporal motion after each layer of diffusion U-Net, enhancing temporal consistency and relevance to the given image. The approach is supported by a hybrid input initialization strategy that improves information propagation within the look-back module. The paper also includes extensive experiments demonstrating the method's ability to generate video clips with higher detail consistency relative to the provided image, outperforming previous methods. Despite its strengths, the paper is critiqued for its limited novelty, lack of comprehensive comparative analysis with state-of-the-art methods, and insufficient discussion on its limitations and societal impacts.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel implicit semi-auto-regressive approach for diffusion-based image-to-video synthesis, which effectively improves both temporal consistency and relevance to the given image.
- The proposed look-back recurrent convolutional module inserted to diffusion U-Net provides coherent generation results with high computation efficiency.
- The hybrid input initialization strategy for training and inference enhances information propagation within the look-back module, leading to more detailed and consistent video generation.
- The paper is well-organized, easy to follow, and provides extensive experiments that demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed method.
- The proposed ISIM metric is simple yet effective for evaluating the similarity between the generated video and the input image.
- The method is efficient, allowing for the generation of long videos without significant computational overhead.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a comprehensive comparative analysis with state-of-the-art methods, particularly in terms of visual results and efficiency.
- The novelty of the proposed method is limited, as the look-back module is similar to the global attention mechanism used in other works.
- The paper does not adequately address the limitations of the proposed method, such as the potential for error accumulation when processing long videos and the lack of a discussion on societal impacts.
- The paper could benefit from more detailed explanations of the look-back module and the hybrid image-noise initialization, as well as a more thorough discussion on the method's limitations and potential future work.
- The experiments are not sufficiently comprehensive, particularly in terms of the variety of images used and the evaluation metrics employed.
- The paper could benefit from a more rigorous comparative analysis with other diffusion-based I2V methods, including those that use different training strategies or different types of diffusion models.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors provide more details on how the look-back module is implemented, particularly how it captures temporal motion and improves temporal consistency?
- How does the hybrid image-noise initialization strategy affect the model's performance, especially in terms of the propagation of information and the model's ability to learn special representations of the first frame?
- Could the authors clarify the differences between the proposed method and other diffusion-based I2V methods, especially in terms of the novelty and effectiveness of the look-back module?
- How does the proposed method handle the potential for error accumulation when processing long videos, and what steps could be taken to mitigate this issue?
- Could the authors provide more examples of generated videos, especially those that are not generated from images generated by Stable Diffusion, to demonstrate the generalizability of the method?
- How does the method perform when using different types of diffusion models, such as DDIM or DDPM, and what are the implications of these choices for the model's performance?
- Could the authors discuss the potential societal impacts of their method, particularly in terms of its applications and limitations?
