 **Summary:**
The paper introduces DisFormer, a method for learning disentangled object representations in visual dynamics prediction. It extends the slot attention mechanism by iteratively refining object representations into blocks, each representing different attributes like color, size, and shape. These blocks are then linearly combined to form a disentangled representation, which is used to predict future dynamics. The method uses a novel permutation module to align object masks with those extracted from ground truth images, ensuring canonical ordering. The paper presents experiments on various datasets, showing improvements over existing baselines in both standard and transfer settings. However, concerns were raised about the clarity of the method's technical contributions, the novelty of the approach, and the fairness of the experimental comparisons.

**Strengths:**
- The paper addresses an important problem in visual dynamics prediction, specifically focusing on disentangled object representation learning, which is a significant area of research.
- The proposed method, DisFormer, is technically sound and well-presented, with a clear and logical structure that facilitates understanding.
- The use of a novel permutation module to align object masks with those extracted from ground truth images is a notable contribution, enhancing the method's effectiveness.
- The paper provides extensive experiments and comparisons with state-of-the-art methods, demonstrating the superiority of DisFormer in various settings, including standard and transfer scenarios.
- The analysis of the learned representations in terms of disentanglement is insightful, providing a deeper understanding of the model's capabilities.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks clarity in distinguishing its technical contributions from existing methods, particularly those discussed in the related work section.
- The novelty of the method is questioned, with concerns about its similarity to previous works such as Slot Attention and the use of disentangled object representation learning.
- The paper's experimental setup and comparisons are not always fair or well-explained, particularly in the transfer learning experiments where the baselines were not given access to the same object masks.
- There is a lack of detailed ablation studies and hyperparameter tuning, which could provide a deeper understanding of the model's performance and limitations.
- The paper could benefit from a more comprehensive discussion on the limitations of the proposed method and potential areas for future research.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors clarify how the proposed method's technical contributions differ from those discussed in the related work section, particularly in terms of the slot attention mechanism and the use of disentangled object representation learning?
- How does the method handle the initialization of block vectors in Algorithm 1, and what impact does this have on the model's performance?
- Could the authors provide more details on the experimental setup, particularly in the transfer learning experiments, to ensure a fair comparison with the baselines?
- How does the method perform in more complex 3D scenes, and what are the implications of using different object extractors?
- Can the authors elaborate on the choice of hyperparameters and their impact on the model's performance?
- What are the specific advantages of using DisFormer over other methods like SlotFormer, especially in terms of computational efficiency and model complexity?
