 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel framework that utilizes a spiking transformer to generate synthetic time-series data from VIP neurons, enhancing the performance of spiking neural networks (SNNs) in decoding heading direction. The framework, named Temporal Spiking Generative Adversarial Networks (T-SGAN), incorporates a transformer-based GAN model that generates synthetic spike trains from real neuronal data. The SNN decoder, integrated with spatial and temporal attention mechanisms, is used for decoding. The paper demonstrates that the proposed model can improve decoding accuracy by up to 1.75% compared to other decoding models, showcasing its potential in neuromorphic computing and other applications. However, the paper suffers from clarity issues, particularly in the methodology section, and lacks a comprehensive comparison with state-of-the-art methods.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel approach using spiking transformers for generating synthetic time-series data, which is a significant advancement in the field of spiking neural networks (SNNs).
- The methodology is well-articulated, and the paper is generally well-written, making it accessible and understandable.
- The proposed T-SGAN model is innovative and could have broad applications in neuromorphic computing and other fields where spike trains are used.
- The experimental results demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed method, showing improvements in decoding accuracy and computational efficiency.
- The paper is well-motivated, addressing the need for synthetic data to enhance the training and performance of SNNs.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a comprehensive comparison with state-of-the-art methods, particularly in terms of computational efficiency and accuracy.
- There is a significant lack of clarity in the methodology section, particularly in the description of the architecture and the roles of different components.
- The paper does not adequately address the ethical implications of using animal data, and there is a need for more detailed information on the ethical considerations.
- The paper does not provide sufficient details on the training process, such as the loss function and the parameters used, which could affect the reproducibility and understanding of the results.
- The paper lacks a discussion on the limitations of the proposed method, which is crucial for understanding its applicability and potential improvements.
- The paper could benefit from a more thorough analysis of the generated data, including comparisons with real data and a discussion on the diversity and quality of the synthetic data.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors clarify the roles and functions of different components in the proposed architecture, particularly in the methodology section?
- How does the proposed method compare with other state-of-the-art methods in terms of computational efficiency and accuracy?
- Can the authors provide more details on the training process, including the loss function and the parameters used?
- How do the authors ensure the ethical handling of animal data, and what steps are taken to minimize animal suffering?
- Could the authors provide more details on the synthetic data generation process, including the diversity and quality of the generated data compared to real data?
- How does the proposed method perform in terms of energy efficiency, and what are the specific energy consumption estimates?
- Can the authors provide more comparative analysis with other models to better understand the effectiveness of the proposed method?
