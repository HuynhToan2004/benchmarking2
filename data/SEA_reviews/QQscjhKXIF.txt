 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel approach to class-incremental continual multi-view clustering, addressing the challenges of learning from streaming multi-view data with incremental semantic classes. The proposed method, named Class-incremental Continual Multi-View Clustering (CCMVC), conducts two iterative optimization phases: multi-view cluster search and multi-view cluster consolidation. CCMVC learns a common feature space for multi-view data, searches for clusters in incoming data, and harmonizes and consolidates all learned clusters into a unified MVC model. The method also introduces a cross-view synchronous loss to mitigate asynchronous convergence issues in multi-view continual learning. The paper presents extensive experiments on six public MVC datasets, demonstrating the superiority of CCMVC over existing methods.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, easy to follow, and presents a novel approach to class-incremental continual multi-view clustering, which is a challenging problem.
- The proposed method, CCMVC, is comprehensive, including multi-view cluster search and multi-view cluster consolidation, which are both innovative and effective.
- The paper conducts extensive experiments on six public MVC datasets, demonstrating the superiority of CCMVC over existing methods.
- The methodology includes a self-supervised data replay mechanism and a cross-view synchronous loss, which are well-motivated and contribute to the field.
- The paper is the first to study class-incremental continual learning for multi-view clustering, which is a significant contribution to the field.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed analysis of the computational complexity of the proposed method, which is crucial for understanding its scalability and efficiency.
- The paper does not provide a clear explanation of how the model manages the imbalanced learning rhythm across different views, which is a critical issue in multi-view learning.
- The paper does not discuss the limitations of the proposed method, which is essential for understanding its applicability and potential improvements.
- The paper does not compare the proposed method with the most recent and relevant works, such as those published in 2022 and 2023, which could provide a more comprehensive evaluation of the method's effectiveness.
- The paper does not include a detailed discussion on the potential negative societal impacts of the proposed method, which is a critical consideration for ethical AI applications.

**Questions:**
- How does the proposed method ensure that the learning rhythm is balanced across different views, especially when the data distribution is imbalanced?
- Can the authors provide a more detailed analysis of the computational complexity of the proposed method, including the time and space complexity of each component?
- How does the proposed method compare with the most recent and relevant works published in 2022 and 2023?
- Can the authors provide a detailed discussion on the potential negative societal impacts of the proposed method, and how these impacts could be mitigated or avoided?
- Can the authors provide more experimental results, such as the impact of varying the number of samples stored in the task memory and the effect of different memory sizes on the performance?
- Can the authors provide more detailed explanations of the experimental results, particularly the discrepancies in performance between different tasks and different datasets?
