 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel method called GML-NeRF, which addresses the rendering defects in complex scenes by allocating model parameters along the ray dimension. This approach allows for more effective handling of occlusions and improves the rendering quality by integrating a soft gate module for parameter assignment and a depth-based mutual learning method for consistency enhancement. The method is evaluated against several baseline methods, demonstrating its effectiveness in various scene types. However, the paper has been critiqued for its limited novelty and the need for more comprehensive ablation studies to support its claims.

**Strengths:**
- The paper addresses a significant problem in NeRF, specifically the rendering of complex scenes with many occlusions, by proposing a method that is visibility-aware and easy to apply to different types of scenes.
- The proposed method is well-motivated and supported by extensive ablation studies, demonstrating its effectiveness in improving rendering quality.
- The paper is well-written and easy to follow, with clear and convincing figures that aid in understanding the proposed method and its results.
- The introduction of a soft gate module and depth-based mutual learning method enhances the rendering consistency and reduces depth ambiguity, leading to improved generalization to novel views.
- The paper provides extensive experiments and ablation studies, demonstrating the effectiveness of the proposed method across various datasets and scene types.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a comprehensive ablation study, particularly in terms of the contribution of the depth-based mutual learning method and the number of sub-NeRFs used.
- The method's performance is not significantly better than existing methods like Switch-NeRF, and the improvements are marginal in some cases.
- The paper does not discuss the limitations of the proposed method, which could help in understanding its applicability and potential drawbacks.
- The paper does not provide a detailed comparison with state-of-the-art methods, which could help in understanding the relative performance of the proposed method.
- The paper could benefit from a more detailed discussion on the scalability and efficiency of the method, especially in terms of memory and training time.
- The paper's novelty is questioned as it builds on existing methods like Switch-NeRF and Instant-NGP, and the contributions are seen as incremental rather than groundbreaking.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors provide more detailed ablation studies on the depth-based mutual learning method and the number of sub-NeRFs used?
- How does the proposed method compare with state-of-the-art methods in terms of rendering quality and efficiency?
- What are the limitations of the proposed method, and how do these impact its applicability and effectiveness?
- How does the proposed method scale with an increasing number of sub-NeRFs, and what are the potential issues that could arise?
- Could the authors clarify the role and effectiveness of the depth-based mutual learning method in improving rendering consistency and reducing depth ambiguity?
- How does the proposed method handle the issue of depth ambiguity, and what are the potential solutions to this problem?
