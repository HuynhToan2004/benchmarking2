 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel approach to enhance the reasoning capabilities of large language models (LLMs) by incorporating "planning tokens" at the beginning of each reasoning step. These tokens guide the model's generation process by indicating the type of mathematical operation to be performed, such as addition or subtraction. The method is tested on various math datasets, demonstrating improvements over baseline methods. The approach involves learning an embedding for each planning token and is implemented through a low-rank adapter to minimize parameter overhead. Despite the method's simplicity and potential, concerns are raised about its generalizability beyond math problems and its scalability to more complex or diverse reasoning tasks.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, with clear and well-organized content, making it easy to follow and understand.
- The introduction of planning tokens is a novel and straightforward approach that can be applied to any reasoning dataset, enhancing the model's ability to perform complex mathematical operations.
- The method is simple yet effective, with a minimal increase in parameters, making it practical for implementation.
- The paper demonstrates the effectiveness of the proposed method through various experiments, showing improvements over baseline methods.
- The use of clustering algorithms to learn planning tokens from embedding space is an interesting approach that adds depth to the methodology.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper primarily focuses on math problems, limiting its applicability to other types of reasoning tasks.
- The method's effectiveness is not convincingly demonstrated across all datasets, particularly on the AQUA dataset, which shows only marginal improvements.
- The paper lacks a broader evaluation of the method's applicability to other domains or tasks beyond math problems, which could enhance its relevance and impact.
- The scalability of the method to more complex or diverse reasoning tasks is not discussed, raising concerns about its generalizability.
- The paper does not include a detailed discussion on the limitations of the proposed method, which is crucial for understanding its scope and applicability.
- There is a lack of comparison with other methods that use planning tokens or similar approaches, which could provide a more robust evaluation of the proposed method.

**Questions:**
