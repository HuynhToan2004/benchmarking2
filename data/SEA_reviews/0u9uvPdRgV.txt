 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel semi-supervised diffusion solver for the Traveling Salesman Problem (TSP), leveraging a transition matrix and a tailored graph neural network to model the generative process. The approach utilizes a diffusion model to learn from both labeled and unlabeled data, aiming to improve performance on large-scale TSP instances. The methodology is supported by extensive experiments, demonstrating the effectiveness of the proposed model against various state-of-the-art methods. Despite its strengths, the paper has been critiqued for its limited novelty and the lack of detailed comparisons with other semi-supervised learning methods.

**Strengths:**
- The paper addresses the challenging problem of the TSP, proposing a semi-supervised diffusion solver that effectively utilizes both labeled and unlabeled data, demonstrating a novel approach to data-driven TSP solvers.
- The methodology is supported by extensive experiments, showcasing the effectiveness of the proposed model across various TSP instances, particularly on large-scale problems.
- The paper is well-written, providing clear explanations of the methodology and results, and is structured in a logical and coherent manner.
- The use of a diffusion model to learn from both labeled and unlabeled data is a significant contribution, potentially enhancing the scalability and applicability of the model to other combinatorial optimization problems.
- The paper includes a comprehensive set of experiments and ablation studies, which are crucial for validating the proposed model and understanding its performance under different conditions.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed comparison with other semi-supervised learning methods, which could have provided a clearer understanding of the advantages and disadvantages of the proposed method.
- There is a need for more detailed explanations of the model's components, particularly the transition matrix and the diffusion model, to aid in understanding the model's operation and effectiveness.
- The paper could benefit from a more thorough discussion of related works, especially those that have also explored semi-supervised learning for TSP or similar problems.
- The novelty of the proposed method is questioned, as it appears to be a combination of existing methods without significant new insights or contributions.
- The paper does not sufficiently address the scalability of the model, particularly in terms of the computational resources required for training and inference, which could limit its practical applicability in real-world scenarios.
- The paper could benefit from a more detailed discussion of the limitations and potential negative societal impacts of the proposed method.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors provide a detailed comparison of their method with other semi-supervised learning methods, highlighting the advantages and disadvantages of their approach?
- How does the proposed method handle the scalability issues, particularly in terms of computational resources and training time?
- Can the authors provide a more detailed explanation of the transition matrix and the diffusion model, including how these components contribute to the model's effectiveness?
- How does the proposed method compare in terms of performance and computational efficiency with other state-of-the-art methods, particularly those that do not rely on semi-supervised learning?
- Could the authors discuss the potential negative societal impacts of their method and outline strategies to mitigate these risks?
- How does the proposed method handle the generation of unlabeled data, and what are the implications of this process on the model's performance and generalizability?
