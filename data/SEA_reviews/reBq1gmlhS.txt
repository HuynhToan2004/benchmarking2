 **Summary:**
The paper explores the application of differential privacy (DP) in reinforcement learning from human feedback (RLHF), specifically focusing on the Plackett-Luce model and Bradley-Terry-Luce model. It presents a novel algorithm that ensures DP while maintaining performance comparable to non-private methods. The paper discusses the challenges of extending existing results to the DP setting and provides theoretical proofs of differential privacy and performance guarantees. The authors aim to bridge the gap between differential privacy and RLHF, showing how privacy can be preserved while maintaining statistical performance.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel algorithm that preserves differential privacy (DP) while maintaining performance comparable to non-private methods, which is significant for applications in reinforcement learning from human feedback (RLHF).
- The authors provide theoretical proofs of differential privacy and performance guarantees, which are crucial for the validity and reliability of the proposed algorithm.
- The paper addresses the challenge of extending existing results to the DP setting, which is a critical area of research in privacy-preserving machine learning.
- The algorithm design and theoretical analysis are well-articulated, making the paper accessible and understandable to a broad audience.
- The paper is well-written, with clear explanations of the technical challenges and the solutions employed to address them.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper primarily focuses on linear parametrization in a fixed feature space, which may limit its applicability to more general settings.
- The technical contributions are somewhat limited, with the main algorithm being a straightforward combination of existing methods.
- The paper could benefit from a more detailed discussion on the limitations of the proposed algorithm and potential avenues for future research.
- The paper does not provide empirical results or experiments to support the theoretical claims, which could enhance the credibility of the findings.
- There is a lack of clarity in some sections of the paper, particularly in the discussion on the privacy parameters and the assumptions made.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors clarify the definition and use of the privacy parameters in the paper?
- How does the proposed algorithm compare to other existing methods in terms of performance and privacy guarantees?
- Could the authors provide empirical results or experiments to support the theoretical claims made in the paper?
- How does the paper handle the potential limitations of the proposed algorithm, and what are the implications of these limitations for practical applications?
- Can the authors discuss the potential impact of the algorithm's assumptions on its applicability and effectiveness in different scenarios?
- How does the paper address the issue of maintaining privacy when the data is perturbed, as suggested by the title and abstract?
