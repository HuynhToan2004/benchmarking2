 **Summary:**
The paper introduces Energy-based Concept Bottleneck Models (ECBMs) as a novel approach to interpretable deep learning, focusing on concept-based interpretations for black-box models. ECBMs utilize a set of neural networks to define the joint energy of candidate (input, concept, class) tuples, enabling prediction, concept correction, and conditional dependency quantification through conditional probabilities. This method aims to address the limitations of existing concept bottleneck models (CBMs) by enhancing interpretability and capturing complex, nonlinear interactions between concepts. The empirical results show that ECBMs outperform state-of-the-art methods on real-world datasets, demonstrating their effectiveness and potential for broader application in interpretable deep learning.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel method for concept-based interpretations in deep learning models, focusing on the use of energy-based models to enhance interpretability.
- The methodology is well-structured and clearly presented, with a comprehensive empirical evaluation that demonstrates the effectiveness of the proposed approach.
- The paper addresses a significant and timely issue in the field of interpretable machine learning, focusing on the limitations of existing concept bottleneck models (CBMs) and proposing a novel approach to address these challenges.
- The proposed ECBM model is evaluated on various datasets, showing its potential for broader application and demonstrating its effectiveness in capturing complex, nonlinear interactions between concepts.
- The paper provides a thorough empirical evaluation, including an ablation study and comparisons with state-of-the-art methods, which supports the claims made and provides a robust validation of the proposed approach.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed discussion on the limitations of the proposed method, particularly in terms of computational complexity and scalability, which could affect the practical applicability of ECBMs.
- The paper does not sufficiently compare the proposed method with existing energy-based models, which could provide a clearer context for the novelty and effectiveness of ECBMs.
- The paper does not adequately address the issue of concept leakage, which is a critical concern in concept-based interpretations.
- The paper could benefit from a more detailed discussion on the differences between the proposed method and existing concept bottleneck models, particularly in terms of how ECBMs address the limitations of these models.
- The paper's presentation and writing could be improved for better clarity and accessibility, particularly in the introduction and related work sections.
- The paper does not discuss the potential negative societal impacts of the proposed method, which is a significant omission in the ethical considerations of the work.

**Questions:**
- How does the proposed method address the issue of concept leakage, particularly in datasets where concepts might be highly correlated or overlapping?
- Can the authors provide a more detailed comparison between the proposed method and existing energy-based models, highlighting the unique contributions and advantages of ECBMs?
- How does the proposed method handle the trade-off between interpretability and performance, particularly in terms of the computational complexity introduced by the energy-based approach?
- Could the authors elaborate on the differences between the proposed method and existing concept bottleneck models, particularly in terms of how ECBMs address the limitations of these models?
- How does the proposed method ensure the reliability and robustness of the concept-based interpretations, particularly in scenarios where the model's predictions might be influenced by biases or other factors?
- Can the authors provide more details on the training process for the energy-based models, including the optimization of the energy function and the selection of hyperparameters?
- How does the proposed method handle the issue of out-of-distribution (OOD) samples, and what measures are in place to ensure the model's accuracy and interpretability in such scenarios?
