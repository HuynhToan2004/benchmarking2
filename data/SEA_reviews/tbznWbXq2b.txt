 **Summary:**
The paper introduces GPT-FL, a novel federated learning (FL) framework that leverages generative pre-trained models to generate synthetic data, which is then used to train a downstream model on the server and fine-tuned with private client data. This approach aims to enhance model performance, communication efficiency, and client sampling efficiency. The framework's effectiveness is demonstrated through extensive experiments across various datasets and model architectures, showcasing improvements over existing FL methods. The paper also discusses the potential of GPT-FL in handling non-IID data and its compatibility with secure aggregation protocols.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, making it easy to follow, and the experiments are comprehensive, covering various datasets and model architectures.
- The proposed GPT-FL framework is innovative, utilizing generative pre-trained models to generate synthetic data, which is then used to train a downstream model on the server, enhancing model performance.
- The paper provides a thorough analysis of the proposed method, including a detailed ablation study that shows the effectiveness of the proposed method.
- The methodology is well-explained, and the paper is well-structured, making it easy for readers to understand the proposed approach.
- The paper includes extensive experiments that demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed method, showing improvements over existing federated learning methods.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a discussion on the computational cost associated with the generation of synthetic data, which could be a significant limitation in practical applications.
- The methodology section could be improved by including more detailed explanations of the generation process for synthetic data and the fine-tuning of models on the server.
- The paper does not adequately address the potential negative societal impacts of the proposed method, which is a critical oversight.
- The paper does not provide a detailed comparison with other existing federated learning methods that leverage pre-trained models, which could limit the understanding of the novelty and effectiveness of the proposed method.
- The paper's claims about the novelty and effectiveness of the proposed method are not convincingly supported by the experimental results, particularly in terms of the improvements over existing methods.
- The paper does not discuss the potential limitations of the proposed method, such as the quality of synthetic data and its impact on model performance.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors provide a detailed comparison of the computational cost associated with the generation of synthetic data with the cost of training on real data?
- How does the proposed method handle non-IID data, and what are the potential impacts on model performance?
- Can the authors provide more details on the generation process for synthetic data and the fine-tuning of models on the server?
- How does the proposed method ensure the security and privacy of client data during the federated learning process?
- Can the authors discuss the potential negative societal impacts of the proposed method and how these impacts could be mitigated?
- How does the proposed method compare with other existing federated learning methods that leverage pre-trained models, particularly in terms of novelty and effectiveness?
- Could the authors provide a more detailed discussion on the potential limitations of the proposed method, such as the quality of synthetic data and its impact on model performance?
