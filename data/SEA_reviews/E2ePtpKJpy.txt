 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a method for enhancing compositional text-to-image generation by leveraging large vision-language models (LVLMs) to assess and correct misalignments between generated images and input text. The approach involves fine-tuning the diffusion model using LVLM-based evaluations and employing an iterative correction process during inference. The paper claims that this method improves text-image alignment in terms of object number, attribute binding, spatial relationships, and aesthetic quality. However, the paper suffers from a lack of comprehensive comparisons with existing methods, insufficient experimental validation, and unclear methodological descriptions, which limits its impact and the understanding of its contributions.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel approach to improving the compositional text-to-image generation process by utilizing large vision-language models (LVLMs) to evaluate and correct misalignments between generated images and input text.
- The use of LVLMs for generating question-answer pairs from input text and analyzing generated images is innovative and could potentially enhance the quality of generated images.
- The iterative correction process during the inference phase, guided by LVLMs, is a significant contribution that could potentially improve the alignment between generated images and input text.
- The paper is well-written, making it easy to follow, and the idea of using LVLMs for evaluating and refining text-to-image generation models is promising.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a clear explanation of how the LVLM-based evaluation is conducted and the role of the LVLM in the evaluation process is not clearly defined.
- There is a lack of comprehensive comparison with existing methods, which limits the understanding of the paper's contributions and the effectiveness of the proposed method.
- The experimental results are not convincing, and the paper does not provide a detailed analysis of the impact of different components of the proposed method.
- The paper does not provide sufficient details on the implementation of the LVLM-based evaluation, which is crucial for reproducibility and understanding the method's effectiveness.
- The method's dependence on the performance of LVLMs raises concerns about the reliability of the results, especially if the LVLMs are not accurate.
- The paper does not discuss the computational cost of the proposed method, which is crucial for practical implementation.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors provide more details on how the LVLM-based evaluation is conducted and the specific role of the LVLM in this process?
- How does the proposed method compare to existing methods, especially in terms of text-image alignment and image quality?
- Can the authors provide a detailed analysis of the impact of different components of the proposed method on the performance of the text-to-image generation model?
- How does the proposed method handle different types of misalignments between generated images and input text, especially in complex compositions?
- What are the specific questions generated by the LVLM for each input text, and how do these questions affect the evaluation results?
- How does the proposed method handle cases where the LVLM is not accurate, and what are the potential impacts on the quality of the generated images?
- Can the authors provide more details on the computational cost of the proposed method and discuss potential strategies for reducing this cost?
