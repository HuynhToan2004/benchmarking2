 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a method for generating synthetic anomalies using a diffusion model to evaluate anomaly detection models without requiring labeled validation data. The approach involves generating anomalies by interpolating between normal images and using these synthetic anomalies to train a detection model. The method is tested across various datasets and shows promise in selecting the best model for anomaly detection tasks. However, concerns were raised about the novelty of the method, the quality of the generated anomalies, and the generalizability of the findings.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written and easy to follow, making it accessible to a broad audience.
- The proposed method of generating synthetic anomalies using a diffusion model is innovative and could be useful for training anomaly detection models without labeled data.
- The paper presents a novel approach to model selection for anomaly detection using synthetic anomalies, which could be beneficial in scenarios where labeled data is scarce.
- The experiments are comprehensive, covering a variety of datasets and tasks, and demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed method in selecting the best model for anomaly detection.
- The paper is well-organized, with clear figures and a logical structure that facilitates understanding of the proposed method and its application.

**Weaknesses:**
- The novelty of the method is questioned as it largely builds upon existing works, particularly DiffStyle, and the contributions are not clearly articulated.
- The quality of the generated anomalies is not convincingly demonstrated to be suitable for anomaly detection tasks, as they may not adequately represent real-world anomalies.
- The paper lacks a comprehensive comparison with other baseline methods, particularly in the selection of CLIP prompts, which could undermine the effectiveness of the proposed method.
- The methodology is not well-explained in some sections, particularly in Section 3.2, which could confuse readers about the implementation details.
- The paper does not adequately address the potential negative societal impacts of the proposed method, which could be significant in applications where false positives or false negatives in anomaly detection could have serious consequences.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors clarify the specific contributions of their work beyond the existing DiffStyle method?
- How do the authors ensure that the generated anomalies are of sufficient quality for anomaly detection tasks, and what steps are taken to validate their effectiveness in real-world scenarios?
- Can the authors provide more details on the implementation of the diffusion model used for generating anomalies, particularly in Section 3.2?
- How does the proposed method compare to other baseline methods in terms of effectiveness and efficiency in model selection and anomaly detection?
- Could the authors address the concerns about the novelty and the potential negative societal impacts of their proposed method?
