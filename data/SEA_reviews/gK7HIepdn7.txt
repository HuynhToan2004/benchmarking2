 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel method for text-to-3D generation, focusing on the material aspect of objects by leveraging a latent BRDF auto-encoder trained on real-world BRDF data. This approach allows for the generation of photorealistic materials, which is a significant advancement over existing methods that either do not consider material properties or entangle them with environment lighting. The method is evaluated against state-of-the-art baselines and shows superior performance in terms of material realism and disentanglement. The paper also explores the use of a semantic-aware material regularization to improve material consistency across different parts of the object. Despite its strengths, the paper suffers from some presentation issues and lacks a thorough discussion on the limitations and societal impacts of the proposed method.

**Strengths:**
- The paper addresses an important problem in text-to-3D generation by focusing on the material aspect of objects, which is a significant limitation in existing methods.
- The proposed method is technically sound, with a clear presentation that aids in understanding the complex ideas.
- The use of a latent BRDF auto-encoder trained on real-world BRDF data is innovative and effective in generating photorealistic materials.
- The paper includes comprehensive experiments and ablation studies that demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed method, showing superior performance compared to state-of-the-art baselines.
- The method's capability to generate high-quality and coherent object materials in text-to-3D synthesis is highlighted, which is crucial for various downstream applications such as relighting and material editing.
- The paper is well-written, making it easy to follow and understand the methodology and results.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a thorough discussion on the limitations of the proposed method, which is crucial for understanding the scope and applicability of the work.
- The paper could benefit from more detailed explanations and examples of the semantic-aware material regularization, particularly how it is applied and its impact on material consistency across different parts of the object.
- The presentation of the paper could be improved, particularly in figures and tables, to make the content clearer and more accessible.
- There is a lack of discussion on the societal impacts of the proposed method, which is an important consideration for any technological innovation.
- The paper could benefit from a more detailed comparison with other methods, including a discussion on the advantages and disadvantages of each approach.
- The method's reliance on a pre-trained BRDF auto-encoder raises concerns about the generalizability of the method, especially for objects not present in the training dataset.

**Questions:**
- How does the proposed method handle the issue of geometry/material entanglement, particularly in cases where the material properties are not adequately represented by the BRDF data?
- Can you provide more details on the semantic-aware material regularization, including how it is applied and its impact on material consistency across different parts of the object?
- How does the method ensure that the generated materials are not overly simplistic or generic, especially in cases where the BRDF data may not be sufficient to capture the complexity of real-world materials?
- Could you elaborate on the training process for the BRDF auto-encoder, particularly how it is initialized and how the weights are balanced to ensure optimal performance?
- How does the method handle variations in material properties across different parts of the object, especially in cases where the same material may have different properties in different contexts?
- Can you provide more details on the user study conducted, including the sample size and the criteria used for selecting participants?
- How does the method handle the issue of material disentanglement, particularly in cases where the material properties are not adequately represented by the BRDF data?
