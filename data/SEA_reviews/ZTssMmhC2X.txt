 **Summary:**
The paper investigates the performance of SGD versus AdamW in fine-tuning neural networks, particularly focusing on the embedding layer of vision transformers. It highlights that AdamW often outperforms SGD due to larger gradients in the embedding layer, which can lead to overfitting. The authors propose a method to freeze the embedding layer during fine-tuning, which can improve the performance of SGD and reduce memory usage. This approach is validated across several datasets and models, showing that it can lead to comparable or better performance than AdamW. The paper also discusses the memory efficiency of SGD, which is crucial for practical applications.

**Strengths:**
- The paper provides a detailed analysis of the performance of SGD and AdamW in fine-tuning neural networks, particularly focusing on the embedding layer of vision transformers.
- The proposed method of freezing the embedding layer during fine-tuning with SGD is simple, effective, and has been validated across multiple datasets and models.
- The paper is well-written, easy to follow, and includes detailed explanations and experiments that support the findings.
- The results show that the proposed method can lead to comparable or better performance than AdamW, and the memory efficiency of SGD is a significant advantage.
- The findings are relevant for practical applications where memory usage is a critical concern, and the paper provides a clear and logical presentation of the results.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper could benefit from a more comprehensive comparison with other optimizers like LAMB and LARS, especially in terms of memory efficiency and performance.
- The paper's claim that SGD performs better than AdamW due to larger gradients in the embedding layer is not entirely novel, as similar findings have been reported in previous works.
- The paper does not adequately address the scalability of the proposed method to larger models or datasets, which could limit its applicability in more complex scenarios.
- The paper's experimental setup and results are somewhat limited, and the generalizability of the findings to other datasets and models is not clearly established.
- The paper lacks a deeper analysis of the reasons why freezing the embedding layer improves performance, and the results do not convincingly demonstrate that this method is superior to other fine-tuning methods like LP-FT.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors provide more detailed comparisons with other optimizers like LAMB and LARS, especially in terms of memory efficiency and performance?
- How does the proposed method scale to larger models or datasets, and what are the potential limitations or challenges in such scenarios?
- Could the authors clarify the role of the "embedding" layer in their experiments and provide a more detailed explanation of its significance?
- How does the proposed method compare to other fine-tuning methods like LP-FT, and what are the implications of using these methods together?
- Can the authors provide more examples or case studies where the proposed method is particularly effective, and how does it perform in scenarios where the fine-tuning data is similar to the pretraining data?
