 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel diffusion model named Topo-Diffusion, which integrates topological representation learning with diffusion models to enhance the stability and performance of generative models. This integration allows for the preservation of topological structures during the diffusion process, addressing the limitations of traditional diffusion models in maintaining shape integrity. The paper presents theoretical guarantees and empirical evidence of the model's effectiveness, showing improvements over baseline models on various datasets. Despite its innovative approach, the paper has been criticized for its clarity in presentation, lack of comprehensive experimental validation, and the need for more detailed comparisons with existing models.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel approach by integrating topological representation learning with diffusion models, which is a significant advancement in the field of generative models.
- The integration of topological representation learning into diffusion models is innovative and could potentially lead to more stable and robust models.
- The paper provides theoretical guarantees for the proposed topological features, which is a strong contribution to the field.
- The experimental results demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed method, showing improvements over baseline models in various datasets.
- The integration of topological features into diffusion models is a promising direction for improving the stability and performance of generative models.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a clear motivation for the use of topological representation learning in diffusion models, which could be better articulated to enhance understanding of the paper's contributions.
- The experimental setup and results are not convincing, with some figures and results (e.g., Table 1 and Figure 4) being misleading or not well-explained.
- The paper lacks a comprehensive comparison with existing models, particularly in terms of performance metrics and the inclusion of more diverse datasets.
- The presentation of the paper could be improved, with some sections being unclear or difficult to follow, particularly in the mathematical formulations and experimental setups.
- The paper does not adequately address the computational complexity or scalability of the proposed model, which could limit its practical application in real-world scenarios.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors clarify the role and impact of the weighting function w in equation 2, particularly in relation to the persistence points?
- How does the proposed model handle the computational complexity and scalability, especially in terms of its practical application in real-world scenarios?
- Could the authors provide more detailed explanations and examples of how the topological features are integrated into the diffusion models, particularly in the forward and reverse processes?
- How does the proposed model compare with existing models in terms of performance metrics, especially in scenarios where the data is not topological in nature?
- Can the authors elaborate on the choice of datasets and the experimental setup, particularly in how they validate the effectiveness of the model in different scenarios?
