 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a unified framework for consistency generative modeling, which extends beyond the predefined diffusion process by directly constructing a probability density path bridging two distributions. This framework is demonstrated through the introduction of two novel models: Poisson Consistency Models (PCMs) and Coupling Consistency Models (CCMs), which extend the prior distribution of latent variables beyond the Gaussian form. The framework leverages the principles of Optimal Transport (OT) to mitigate variance during consistency training, enhancing convergence and generative quality. Extensive experiments across synthetic and real-world datasets, as well as image-to-image translation tasks, validate the effectiveness of the proposed models.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel framework for consistency generative modeling that does not rely on the predefined diffusion process, offering a new perspective on consistency modeling.
- It presents two novel models, Poisson Consistency Models (PCMs) and Coupling Consistency Models (CCMs), which extend the prior distribution of latent variables beyond the Gaussian form, enhancing the flexibility of generative modeling.
- The use of Optimal Transport (OT) to mitigate variance during consistency training is a significant contribution, improving convergence and generative quality.
- Extensive experiments across various datasets, including image-to-image translation tasks, demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed approaches.
- The paper is well-written, making it easy to follow, and provides a unified framework for consistency generative modeling, which can be used to understand and explore various consistency generation models.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper does not sufficiently discuss the limitations or potential drawbacks of the proposed framework, such as its applicability to different types of data or its scalability to larger datasets.
- The experimental results, particularly on the CIFAR-10 dataset, show that the proposed models do not significantly outperform existing methods, raising questions about their practical utility.
- The paper does not provide sufficient detail on the computational resources required for training and inference, which is crucial for understanding the scalability and accessibility of the proposed methods.
- The novelty of the proposed framework is questionable, as it appears to be a combination of existing ideas without sufficient differentiation or innovation.
- The paper lacks a detailed comparison with state-of-the-art methods, particularly in terms of performance metrics and computational efficiency.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors provide more details on the limitations or potential drawbacks of the proposed framework?
- How does the proposed framework perform on larger datasets, such as ImageNet, and what are the computational requirements for training and inference?
- What are the specific advantages of the proposed models over existing consistency models, and how do they compare in terms of performance and computational efficiency?
- Could the authors provide more qualitative results, such as visual comparisons of generated images, to better demonstrate the effectiveness of their models?
- How does the framework handle the generation of different types of data, such as text or audio, and what modifications would be required for these applications?
