 **Summary:**
The paper introduces an in-context learning algorithm for offline reinforcement learning (RL) named ICEE (In-context Exploration-Exploitation), which leverages Transformer models to address exploration-exploitation trade-offs without Bayesian inference. The algorithm uses a sequence model to predict the action distribution based on historical trajectories, enabling efficient learning from multiple episodes of the same task. This methodology is tested in grid world environments, demonstrating significant improvements in sample efficiency over existing methods. The paper also explores the use of epistemic uncertainty in sequence models to enhance exploration-exploitation behavior, although it is limited to simple environments and lacks a broader empirical validation.

**Strengths:**
- The paper presents a novel approach to in-context learning in offline RL, which is a significant advancement in the field.
- The use of Transformers to model historical trajectories and predict action distributions is a novel and interesting application.
- The paper is well-written, with clear explanations and a logical flow, making it easy to understand.
- The empirical results are compelling, demonstrating the effectiveness of the proposed method.
- The idea of using Transformers to predict action distributions and incorporate exploration-exploitation trade-offs is innovative and could be a significant contribution to the field.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper is limited to simple grid-world environments, which may not adequately represent real-world applications, and the experiments are not extensive enough to validate the claims fully.
- The methodology relies heavily on historical trajectories, which may limit its applicability in more complex environments where such data is not readily available.
- The paper lacks a thorough discussion on the limitations and potential negative societal impacts of the proposed method.
- The empirical results are not sufficiently robust, with insufficient statistical analysis and a lack of comparison with more recent and relevant baselines.
- The paper could benefit from a more detailed discussion on the limitations and potential negative societal impacts of the proposed method.
- The paper does not adequately address how the method performs in more complex environments or how it scales to larger datasets.

**Questions:**
- How does the method perform in more complex environments, and what are the potential limitations in such settings?
- Can the authors provide more details on how the method scales to larger datasets and more complex environments?
- How does the method compare to other in-context learning methods, particularly in terms of computational efficiency and sample efficiency?
- Can the authors provide a more detailed analysis of the empirical results, including statistical significance and a broader range of baseline comparisons?
- How does the method address the issue of bias in historical trajectories, and what steps are taken to ensure that the model does not overfit to these biases?
- Can the authors provide a more detailed discussion on the limitations and potential negative societal impacts of the proposed method?
