 **Summary:**
The paper introduces ACES, a novel method for generating diverse programming puzzles using semantic descriptors derived from a large language model (LLM). The approach leverages LLM's ability to label puzzles based on ten predefined skills, thereby optimizing for diversity in puzzle generation. The method involves a goal-directed approach where LLM is prompted to generate puzzles that meet specific semantic descriptions, which are then evaluated for validity and added to the dataset. The paper presents several experiments demonstrating the diversity of the generated puzzles and their potential for training models to solve them. However, the paper faces criticism for its lack of novelty in approach, the limited evaluation metrics, and the absence of a comprehensive discussion on the semantic descriptors' effectiveness.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, easy to follow, and presents a novel approach to generating diverse programming puzzles using semantic descriptors derived from a large language model (LLM).
- The experiments conducted demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed method in generating diverse puzzles, which is a significant contribution to the field.
- The paper introduces a novel methodology for generating diverse programming puzzles, which is a significant contribution to the field.
- The paper is well-organized and easy to follow, making it accessible to a broad audience.
- The use of semantic descriptors derived from LLM to guide puzzle generation is an innovative approach that could be applied in other areas of programming and AI.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a thorough discussion on the effectiveness of the semantic descriptors in guiding puzzle generation, particularly how these descriptors ensure the diversity and relevance of the generated puzzles.
- The evaluation metrics used are limited, focusing primarily on diversity and not adequately assessing the quality or difficulty of the generated puzzles.
- The paper does not provide a detailed discussion on the potential negative societal impacts of the proposed method, which is a significant omission.
- The paper's novelty is questionable as it builds on existing methods such as ELM, and the contributions appear incremental rather than groundbreaking.
- The paper does not adequately address the limitations of the proposed method, particularly in terms of its scalability and applicability to more complex or varied programming tasks.
- The methodology used in the experiments, such as the selection of semantic descriptors and the evaluation of puzzle diversity, is not sufficiently detailed or explained, which makes it difficult to replicate or validate the results.

**Questions:**
- Can you provide more details on how the semantic descriptors were selected and how they guide the puzzle generation process?
- How do you ensure that the generated puzzles are both diverse and of high quality, and what metrics are used to assess this?
- Can you discuss the potential negative societal impacts of the proposed method, particularly in terms of how it might affect the education or training of programmers?
- How does the proposed method compare to other existing methods for generating diverse programming puzzles, and what are the advantages or disadvantages of using LLM-derived semantic descriptors compared to other methods?
- Could you clarify the methodology used in the experiments, particularly in terms of how the puzzles were generated and evaluated, and how the results were analyzed and interpreted?
- How do you address the concerns regarding the novelty and incremental nature of the contributions made by the proposed method?
