 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel method for estimating Frechet bounds on performance metrics of machine learning models trained with programmatic weak supervision, addressing the challenge of validating models without labeled data. The authors propose a convex optimization-based algorithm that estimates sharp lower and upper bounds for metrics such as accuracy, precision, recall, and F1 score. The method is validated through theoretical analysis and experiments on datasets from the Wrench benchmark, demonstrating its effectiveness in estimating bounds and its potential for model selection and risk assessment. Despite its theoretical and experimental strengths, the paper is criticized for its limited scope to binary classification and its experimental setup, which is considered too simplistic and not sufficiently challenging.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel method for estimating Frechet bounds on performance metrics of machine learning models trained with programmatic weak supervision, addressing the challenge of validating models without labeled data.
- The authors propose a convex optimization-based algorithm for estimating the Frechet bounds, which is theoretically sound and supported by experimental results.
- The methodology is well-presented, with clear theoretical results and practical applications, and the paper is well-written, making it accessible and easy to follow.
- The paper is innovative in its application of Frechet bounds to weakly supervised learning, providing a new perspective on the validation of models trained with weak supervision.
- The paper includes a comprehensive theoretical analysis, which is supported by experimental results demonstrating the effectiveness of the proposed method.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper is limited to binary classification, which may not generalize well to multi-class classification scenarios.
- The experimental setup is somewhat simplistic, using datasets with a limited number of classes, which may not adequately challenge the proposed method.
- The paper does not sufficiently discuss the limitations of the proposed method, particularly concerning its applicability to more complex or realistic scenarios.
- The paper's assumptions, such as the boundedness of P(Y=y|Z=z) and the boundedness of the conditional distribution PY|Z, are not adequately justified or discussed.
- The paper lacks a detailed discussion on the convergence of the label model to PY|Z, which is a critical aspect of the proposed method.
- The paper's contribution to the field is not clearly articulated, and it does not adequately compare its method with existing works, particularly those that address similar problems in weak supervision.

**Questions:**
- How does the proposed method handle the case where PY|Z is not identifiable from the marginal distributions PX,Z and PY,Z?
- Can the proposed method be extended to handle multi-class classification scenarios?
- How does the method perform when the label model is misspecified?
- Is there a way to adapt the proposed method to handle more complex or realistic scenarios, such as those involving larger or more complex datasets?
- Could the authors provide more details on the convergence of the label model to PY|Z, and discuss the implications of this convergence for the validity of the estimated bounds?
- How does the proposed method compare with existing works in terms of computational complexity and performance?
- Could the authors clarify the assumptions made in the paper and discuss their implications for the validity and applicability of the proposed method?
