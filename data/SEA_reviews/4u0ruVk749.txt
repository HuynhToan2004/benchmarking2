 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel approach to estimating individual treatment effects (ITE) using a diffusion model to capture unobserved confounders. The method involves a reverse diffusion process to generate unobserved confounders, which are then used to estimate ITE. The paper provides theoretical proofs and empirical evaluations, demonstrating the method's effectiveness in various scenarios. It compares the proposed method with existing baselines and shows promising results. However, the paper has been critiqued for its clarity, with some reviewers expressing concerns about the writing and the presentation of technical details.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel approach using a diffusion model to capture unobserved confounders, which is a significant advancement in the field.
- The methodology is well-articulated, with clear theoretical proofs and empirical evaluations that demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed method.
- The paper is well-written, making it accessible and easy to understand, which is crucial for a broad audience.
- The proposed method shows superior performance compared to existing methods, as evidenced by the experimental results.
- The paper is well-motivated, with a clear explanation of the problem setting and the proposed method, which is well-suited to the task of estimating individual treatment effects.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper could benefit from more detailed explanations and clarifications, particularly in the methodology section where the generation of unobserved confounders is not adequately explained.
- There are some inconsistencies and potential errors in the paper, such as incorrectly labeled equations and missing definitions.
- The paper lacks a discussion on the limitations of the proposed method, which is crucial for understanding its applicability and potential drawbacks.
- The paper's novelty is somewhat limited as it builds upon existing methods like QHTE, and the technical contributions are not clearly delineated.
- There are some typographical and formatting errors that need to be corrected.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors clarify how the unobserved confounders are generated and how they relate to the observed confounders?
- How does the paper handle the generation of unobserved confounders, particularly in terms of the model's assumptions and the impact of different hyperparameters?
- Could the authors provide more details on the experimental setup, such as the specifics of the datasets used and the methodology for generating the unobserved confounders?
- How does the proposed method compare to existing methods in terms of computational efficiency and scalability?
- Can the authors elaborate on the theoretical guarantees of the method and discuss the conditions under which the assumptions hold?
- Could the authors provide more detailed explanations of the equations and terms used in the paper, particularly those that are not standard or require additional explanation?
