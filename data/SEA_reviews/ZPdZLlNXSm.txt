 **Summary:**
The paper explores the application of mean field theory from statistical physics to deep metric learning, specifically focusing on the Contrastive and Class-wise multi-similarity loss functions. The authors propose new loss functions, MeanFieldContrastive and MeanFieldClassWiseMultiSimilarity, which aim to reduce training complexity by approximating interactions between data points with an average field generated by the rest of the system. The proposed losses are evaluated against existing baselines on image retrieval datasets, showing competitive performance and potentially faster convergence. The paper also discusses the application of mean field theory to reduce the training complexity of pair-based loss functions, providing a novel perspective on deep metric learning.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel approach by applying mean field theory from statistical physics to deep metric learning, which is an innovative direction for reducing training complexity.
- The proposed MeanFieldContrastive and MeanFieldClassWiseMultiSimilarity losses are novel and show promising performance, particularly in terms of faster convergence.
- The paper is well-written, making it easy to follow, and the experiments are extensive, demonstrating the effectiveness of the proposed losses.
- The application of mean field theory to simplify deep metric learning is a significant contribution to the field, and the paper effectively leverages this concept to derive new losses.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a comprehensive discussion on the limitations of the proposed approach, particularly in terms of its applicability to other types of loss functions and its generalization to different datasets and tasks.
- The paper could benefit from a more detailed comparison with other existing methods, such as the proxy-based method, to clarify the advantages and disadvantages of the proposed approach.
- The experimental results, while promising, are not consistently superior across all datasets, and the paper does not sufficiently discuss the failure cases or the impact of different hyperparameters on the performance.
- There is a need for more extensive comparisons with other state-of-the-art methods to validate the effectiveness of the proposed losses, especially in terms of their ability to improve the training process and generalization to different tasks and datasets.
- The paper could benefit from a more detailed discussion on the theoretical underpinnings of the mean field theory and its application to deep metric learning, particularly in terms of its effectiveness and limitations.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors elaborate on the specific advantages of using mean field theory over other methods like the proxy-based method, particularly in terms of training complexity and performance?
- How does the proposed approach generalize to different types of loss functions and datasets beyond those tested?
- Can the authors provide a more detailed discussion on the failure cases and the impact of different hyperparameters on the performance of the proposed losses?
- Is there a potential for the proposed losses to be adapted for use in other deep learning tasks beyond metric learning?
- How does the proposed approach compare in terms of computational efficiency and practical implementation to other state-of-the-art methods in deep metric learning?
