 **Summary:**
The paper introduces LLM+A, a novel framework that leverages large language models (LLMs) for robotic manipulation tasks. The system employs affordance prompting to enhance the physical executability of sub-task plans and control sequences generated by LLMs. The approach is tested across various simulated environments, demonstrating improvements over baseline methods. However, concerns are raised about the generalizability of the method to real-world scenarios and the need for more robust experimental validations.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel approach using LLMs for robotic manipulation tasks, demonstrating the potential of LLMs in generating control sequences for robots without requiring extensive training.
- The affordance prompting technique is innovative, allowing LLMs to infer goal-conditioned affordance values, which are crucial for generating executable control sequences.
- The method is evaluated on various simulated tasks, showing promising results and outperforming baseline methods.
- The paper is well-written, clear, and easy to follow, making it accessible to a broad audience.
- The use of LLMs for robotic manipulation tasks is a significant step towards advancing the field, and the proposed method is a valuable contribution to this area.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed discussion on how the affordance prompting technique is implemented and the specifics of the LLM's training and inference.
- The experimental setup is limited to simulated environments, which may not adequately reflect the performance in real-world scenarios.
- The generalizability of the method to more complex, real-world tasks is questionable, as the experiments are primarily focused on simple tasks.
- There is a lack of detailed implementation details and experimental settings, which makes it difficult to reproduce the results and assess the method's effectiveness in diverse scenarios.
- The paper does not adequately address the potential negative societal impacts of the proposed method.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors provide more details on how the affordance prompting technique is implemented, including the specific LLM used and its training and inference details?
- How does the method perform in more complex, real-world scenarios, and what are the potential challenges or limitations in such environments?
- What are the specific failure modes of the method, and how do they impact the overall performance?
- How does the method compare to other state-of-the-art methods in terms of performance and efficiency?
- Can the authors discuss the potential societal impacts of the proposed method, particularly in terms of safety and ethical considerations?
- Could the authors provide more details on the experimental setup, such as the specific simulators used and the parameters of the LLM?
