 **Summary:**
The paper introduces AgentTuning, a method aimed at enhancing the agent capabilities of large language models (LLMs) while maintaining their general abilities. It involves the creation of a new dataset, AgentInstruct, which is used to fine-tune LLMs. The methodology includes a hybrid instruction-tuning strategy that combines AgentInstruct with general-domain instructions, demonstrating that AgentTuning can improve the agent capabilities of LLMs, as evidenced by its performance on various agent tasks. The paper also provides a detailed evaluation of AgentTuning across different model scales and tasks, showing that it can match or even surpass the performance of commercial models like GPT-3.5 on unseen agent tasks. However, concerns were raised about the novelty of the approach and the limited scope of tasks evaluated.

**Strengths:**
- The paper addresses an important and timely problem by focusing on enhancing the agent capabilities of open-source LLMs without compromising their general abilities.
- The introduction of the AgentInstruct dataset, which is made publicly available, will be beneficial for the research community.
- The methodology is clearly described, making it easy to understand and replicate.
- The paper provides a thorough evaluation of AgentTuning across different model scales and tasks, demonstrating its effectiveness and generalization capabilities.
- The paper is well-written, with clear explanations of the dataset construction and the training strategy.
- The results are promising, showing that AgentTuning can improve the agent capabilities of LLMs, and the analysis is insightful.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a clear definition and explanation of the term "agent" used in the context of LLMs, which could lead to confusion.
- The novelty of the approach is questionable as it primarily involves the construction of a new dataset and the use of a standard fine-tuning method.
- The tasks used in the evaluation are limited, and the results do not convincingly demonstrate the effectiveness of the method across a broader range of tasks.
- The paper does not provide sufficient details on the model training, such as the learning rate, optimization method, and training time, which could affect the reproducibility and comparison of results.
- The paper does not discuss the limitations of the proposed method, which is crucial for understanding its applicability and potential drawbacks.
- The paper does not compare the proposed method with other existing methods, such as those mentioned in related works, which could provide a more robust evaluation of its effectiveness.

**Questions:**
- Could you clarify the definition and usage of the term "agent" in the context of LLMs?
- How does the proposed method compare with other existing methods, such as those mentioned in related works?
- Can you provide more details on the model training, such as the learning rate, optimization method, and training time?
- How do you address the potential data leakage issue discussed in the paper?
- In the context of the evaluation, why were only specific tasks chosen, and how do these tasks reflect the general capabilities of the model?
- How does the proposed method perform when applied to different model architectures or training strategies?
- Could you elaborate on the limitations of the proposed method and discuss potential areas for future research?
