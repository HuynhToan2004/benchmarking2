 **Summary:**
The paper introduces DriveGPT4, an innovative multimodal large language model (LLM) designed for interpretable end-to-end autonomous driving. DriveGPT4 utilizes a novel dataset generated from the BDD-X dataset, which is enhanced by ChatGPT for instruction tuning. The model is capable of interpreting vehicle actions, generating text responses, and predicting control signals based on video inputs. It outperforms existing methods in various tasks such as vehicle action description, action justification, and control signal prediction. However, concerns about the novelty of the approach and the generalization ability of the model to real-world scenarios are highlighted.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel approach to autonomous driving by leveraging large language models (LLMs) for interpretable end-to-end autonomous driving, which is a significant advancement in the field.
- The creation of a new dataset for autonomous driving interpretation, with the assistance of ChatGPT, is a valuable contribution to the research community.
- The paper is well-structured, clear, and easy to follow, making it accessible to a broad audience.
- The proposed model, DriveGPT4, demonstrates superior performance in various tasks such as vehicle action description, action justification, and control signal prediction, as evidenced by both qualitative and quantitative results.
- The paper includes a comprehensive evaluation of the model, demonstrating its effectiveness in handling unseen scenarios and its generalization ability to some extent.
- The use of a new dataset and the innovative application of LLMs in autonomous driving are significant contributions to the field, potentially inspiring further research and development.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed discussion on the limitations of the proposed method, which is crucial for understanding the scope and applicability of the findings.
- The novelty of the approach is questionable as it appears to be a direct application of existing techniques, such as LLaVA and Valley, without significant innovation.
- The paper does not sufficiently address the generalization ability of the model to real-world scenarios, which is a critical concern for the practical deployment of autonomous driving systems.
- The use of ChatGPT for generating instruction-tuning data raises concerns about the reliability and accuracy of the generated data, particularly in the context of autonomous driving where safety is paramount.
- The paper does not compare the proposed method with other state-of-the-art methods, which could provide a more robust evaluation of the model's performance.
- There is a lack of discussion on the ethical implications of using LLMs in autonomous driving, which is a significant and timely issue.
- The paper could benefit from more detailed explanations and examples in the methodology section, particularly in the video tokenizer and text and control signals sections.

**Questions:**
- Could you provide more details on the specific improvements or innovations made by DriveGPT4 compared to existing methods, particularly in terms of the LLM's interpretability and end-to-end autonomous driving capabilities?
- How does the model handle unseen scenarios, and what measures are in place to ensure its generalization ability to real-world conditions?
- Can you elaborate on the limitations of the proposed method and discuss how these limitations might affect the practical deployment of autonomous driving systems?
- How does the model handle situations where the control signals are missing, and what strategies are employed to ensure safe and effective driving in such scenarios?
- Can you provide more details on the dataset used for training and testing, particularly in terms of its size, diversity, and quality?
- What are the specific advantages of using DriveGPT4 over other existing methods, and how does it compare in terms of performance, interpretability, and generalization ability?
- Could you discuss the ethical implications of using LLMs in autonomous driving, and how these are addressed in your research?
