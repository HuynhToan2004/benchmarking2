 **Summary:**
The paper introduces ReForm-Eval, a benchmark designed to evaluate the capabilities of large vision-language models (LVLMs) by re-formatting existing benchmarks into unified LVLM-compatible formats. This benchmark, which encompasses 61 datasets and 8 evaluation dimensions, aims to provide a comprehensive assessment of LVLMs across various capabilities. The authors conducted extensive experiments, comparing 16 models across different architectures and datasets, and analyzed the performance of LVLMs using both black-box and white-box evaluation methods. The paper also explores the impact of different model architectures, pre-training datasets, and instruct-tuning datasets on LVLM performance.

**Strengths:**
- The paper presents a comprehensive evaluation of 16 models across different architectures and datasets, providing a robust analysis of LVLM performance.
- The proposed ReForm-Eval benchmark is a valuable tool for evaluating LVLMs, offering a unified framework for assessing their capabilities.
- The paper is well-written, clear, and easy to follow, making it accessible to a broad audience.
- The inclusion of both black-box and white-box evaluation methods adds depth to the analysis, providing insights into the performance of LVLMs under different conditions.
- The comprehensive analysis of different factors affecting LVLM performance, such as model architecture, pre-training datasets, and instruct-tuning datasets, is thorough and informative.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper's main contribution, the ReForm-Eval benchmark, primarily involves reformatting existing benchmarks, which may not be considered a significant technical contribution.
- The benchmark is limited to text-based evaluation and does not include visual evaluations, which could provide a more comprehensive assessment of LVLMs' capabilities.
- The paper lacks a detailed discussion on how the benchmark's design addresses the limitations of existing LVLM benchmarks, which could enhance the paper's impact.
- The analysis of different factors affecting LVLM performance is somewhat limited, as it does not explore the impact of different evaluation strategies or the effectiveness of different connection modules.
- The paper could benefit from a more in-depth analysis of the limitations and potential biases introduced by the re-formulation of existing benchmarks.

**Questions:**
- How does the ReForm-Eval benchmark address the limitations of existing LVLM benchmarks, particularly in terms of evaluation scope and methodology?
- Can you elaborate on the design choices behind the ReForm-Eval benchmark, especially how it overcomes the challenges posed by the structural gap between existing task-oriented multi-modal benchmarks and LVLMs?
- How do the authors envision the use of ReForm-Eval in future research, and what impact do they anticipate it will have on the field of LVLM development?
- Could you provide more details on the evaluation methodologies used in ReForm-Eval, particularly how they ensure the benchmark's effectiveness in assessing LVLMs' capabilities?
- How does the paper address the potential biases introduced by the re-formulation of existing benchmarks, and what steps have been taken to mitigate these biases?
