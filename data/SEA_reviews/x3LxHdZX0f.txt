 **Summary:**
The paper introduces PUMA, a novel framework designed for secure inference of Transformer models, focusing on the GeLU and softmax operations. It proposes polynomial approximations to enhance computational efficiency and reduces inference costs by 2x compared to existing methods like MPCFormer. PUMA is evaluated on various transformer models and datasets, demonstrating its effectiveness and efficiency. The framework is open-sourced, and the authors have made significant efforts to improve the security and performance of secure inference. However, the paper has been critiqued for its limited novelty and the need for more comprehensive evaluations, including comparisons with other secure inference frameworks and larger language models.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written and easy to follow, with clear explanations of the proposed methods and their implementation.
- The evaluation is comprehensive, covering a variety of transformer models and datasets, and the results demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed methods.
- The paper introduces novel polynomial approximations for GeLU and softmax operations, which are not present in previous works.
- The framework is open-sourced, allowing for reproducibility and further development by the community.
- The paper addresses an important problem in secure inference for large language models, which is crucial for privacy-preserving applications.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed comparison with other secure inference frameworks, which limits the understanding of PUMA's relative performance.
- The novelty of the polynomial approximations for GeLU and softmax operations is questionable, as similar methods have been previously proposed.
- The evaluation is limited to smaller language models, and it would be beneficial to test PUMA on larger models like LLaMA-13B or LLaMA-70B to assess its scalability.
- The paper does not discuss the impact of approximation error on the performance of the models, which is a significant concern for secure inference.
- The paper could benefit from more detailed comparisons with other secure inference methods, such as those that use secure multi-party computation (MPC) or other techniques like homomorphic encryption.
- The paper does not sufficiently discuss the security implications of the proposed methods, which could be a critical concern for practical applications.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors provide more details on the polynomial approximations for GeLU and softmax operations, particularly how they differ from existing methods?
- How does PUMA compare in terms of performance and security with other secure inference frameworks like MPCFormer?
- Could the authors discuss the potential impact of approximation error on the performance of the models and provide more details on the security implications of the proposed methods?
- How does the performance of PUMA scale with larger language models, and what are the expected runtime and communication costs for these models?
- Can the authors clarify the differences in the proposed GeLU and softmax approximations compared to previous works, and provide a detailed comparison with MPCFormer?
