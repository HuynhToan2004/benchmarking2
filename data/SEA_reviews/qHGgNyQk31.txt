 **Summary:**
The paper introduces SEER, a novel method for text-conditioned video prediction that leverages pretrained text-to-image models to enhance the temporal consistency of video generation. SEER incorporates a Frame Sequential Text Decomposer to dissect global text instructions into temporally aligned sub-instructions, which are then integrated into the video generation process. This approach is aimed at improving the fidelity and coherence of video frames, making it computationally efficient and effective in handling tasks such as robot planning and video editing. The methodology involves a novel attention mechanism, Scaled Autoregressive Window Temporal Attention, and a temporal text decomposer to handle the temporal dynamics of video generation. The paper provides comprehensive experimental results, demonstrating SEER's superiority over existing methods in terms of computational efficiency and video quality.

**Strengths:**
- The paper addresses an important problem in text-conditioned video prediction, which is crucial for various applications such as robot planning and video editing.
- The proposed method is novel and innovative, particularly in its use of pretrained text-to-image models to enhance temporal consistency in video generation.
- The paper is well-written, clear, and easy to follow, with detailed experimental results that demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed method.
- The introduction of the FSText Decomposer and the Scaled Autoregressive Window Temporal Attention is a significant contribution to the field, providing a new approach to handling textual instructions in video generation.
- The method's ability to generate high-fidelity and coherent video frames, as shown in the experiments, is a notable strength.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed discussion on the limitations of the proposed method, particularly in terms of its applicability to different types of videos or scenarios.
- There is a need for more quantitative and qualitative results to support the claims of improved computational efficiency and video quality.
- The paper could benefit from a more comprehensive comparison with existing methods, especially in terms of computational efficiency and video quality metrics.
- Some figures and descriptions in the paper are not clear or require further explanation, such as the role of certain components in the video generation process.
- The paper does not discuss the potential societal impacts of the proposed method, which could be a significant concern for some applications.
