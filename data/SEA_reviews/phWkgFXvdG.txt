 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel method for incremental object detection using a hybrid knowledge representation and task-regularized distillation loss to address the issues of catastrophic forgetting and knowledge fuzziness. It proposes a knowledge selection strategy and knowledge transfer strategy to balance the learning of old and new classes, aiming to maintain the stability of old knowledge while allowing for the plasticity of new knowledge. The method is evaluated on datasets like MS COCO and Pascal VOC, showing significant improvements over existing methods in terms of performance metrics like mAP and AbsGap. Despite its effectiveness, the paper has been critiqued for its lack of theoretical analysis, unclear methodological explanations, and potential over-reliance on specific datasets.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written and easy to follow, making complex concepts accessible to a broader audience.
- The proposed method is innovative, integrating knowledge selection and transfer strategies to address the challenges of incremental object detection, particularly in scenarios where data distribution is non-stationary.
- The method achieves state-of-the-art performance on benchmarks such as MS COCO and Pascal VOC, demonstrating its effectiveness in real-world applications.
- The paper includes extensive experiments and ablation studies, providing a comprehensive evaluation of the proposed method.
- The use of hybrid knowledge representation and task-regularized distillation loss is a novel approach that effectively balances the learning of old and new classes, addressing the issues of catastrophic forgetting and knowledge fuzziness.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed theoretical analysis, which could enhance the understanding of the proposed method and its effectiveness.
- There is a noticeable absence of comparisons with other state-of-the-art methods, particularly in the context of open-world object detection, which limits the evaluation of the proposed method's performance against current leading techniques.
- The method's reliance on specific datasets, such as MS COCO and Pascal VOC, raises concerns about its generalizability to other datasets or scenarios.
- The paper does not sufficiently address the issue of catastrophic forgetting in the context of incremental object detection, which is a critical aspect of the research.
- The experimental results are not sufficiently detailed, particularly in terms of the standard deviation of the results and the specifics of the experimental setup.
- There is a lack of clarity in some methodological explanations, such as the calculation of confidence differences and the role of the hybrid knowledge representation in maintaining stability and plasticity.

**Questions:**
- Could you provide a more detailed theoretical analysis to support the proposed method?
- How does the proposed method compare with other state-of-the-art methods in terms of performance and computational efficiency?
- What are the specific challenges in applying the proposed method to other datasets or scenarios, and how does it perform in these contexts?
- How does the hybrid knowledge representation ensure a balance between stability and plasticity in learning?
- Could you clarify the calculation of confidence differences and their impact on the learning process?
- What are the implications of the experimental results, particularly in terms of the generalizability of the findings?
