 **Summary:**
The paper introduces FIITED, a method for optimizing embedding dimensions in-training to reduce memory consumption during the training of Deep Learning Recommender Models (DLRMs). FIITED assigns different dimensions to each embedding vector based on their importance, which is determined by access frequency and gradient norms. The method is designed to adapt to dynamic changes in data and is evaluated on both industry and public datasets, demonstrating significant memory savings and performance improvements. However, the paper's novelty is questioned due to its close resemblance to existing methods like AdaEmbed, and the evaluation primarily focuses on click-through rate prediction tasks, which may limit its applicability to other recommendation tasks.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, with a clear motivation and logical structure that effectively conveys the importance of the problem and the proposed solution.
- The proposed method, FIITED, is innovative and addresses the significant issue of memory consumption in training DLRMs, which is crucial for large-scale applications.
- The method's ability to dynamically adjust the embedding dimensions based on data characteristics during training is a notable strength, particularly in scenarios where data is not fully observable or changes rapidly.
- The paper provides extensive experimental results that demonstrate the effectiveness of the method in reducing memory consumption and maintaining model performance, as evidenced by the experiments conducted on both industry and public datasets.
- The paper is well-organized and includes clear figures and tables that aid in understanding the proposed method and its evaluation.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper primarily focuses on click-through rate prediction tasks, which may limit its applicability to other types of recommendation tasks such as rating prediction or graph recommendation.
- The novelty of the method is questionable as it appears to be a direct extension of AdaEmbed, with similarities in methodology and experimental setup.
- The paper lacks a detailed comparison with other state-of-the-art methods, particularly in terms of memory savings and performance improvements.
- There is a lack of clarity in the experimental setup, particularly in the description of the datasets used and the specifics of the model configurations.
- The paper could benefit from a more comprehensive analysis of the trade-offs between memory savings and performance, as well as a discussion on the potential negative societal impacts of the proposed method.

**Questions:**
- How does the proposed method perform when applied to other types of recommendation tasks beyond click-through rate prediction?
- Can the authors provide more details on the datasets used in the experiments, including their size, characteristics, and any preprocessing steps that were applied?
- How does the proposed method compare to other state-of-the-art methods in terms of memory savings and performance improvements?
- Could the authors provide a more detailed analysis of the trade-offs between memory savings and performance, particularly in terms of how these trade-offs vary across different types of datasets and model configurations?
- How does the proposed method handle the potential negative societal impacts, such as biases in recommender systems?
