 **Summary:**
The paper introduces SliceGPT, a novel post-training sparsification scheme for large language models (LLMs) that focuses on reducing model parameters by up to 25% without significant loss in accuracy. This is achieved by decomposing weight matrices into smaller matrices, effectively reducing the embedding dimension. The method leverages computational invariance in transformer networks to maintain performance, and it has been tested on models like OPT 66B and Llama-2 70B, demonstrating a modest loss in accuracy and significant reductions in compute and memory requirements. The paper also explores the potential for further improvements in throughput and energy efficiency.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, making it easy to follow, and the methodology is clearly presented.
- The proposed method, SliceGPT, is novel and innovative, particularly in its application of computational invariance in transformer networks.
- The experiments conducted are thorough and demonstrate that SliceGPT can reduce compute and memory requirements significantly, with minimal loss in accuracy.
- The paper provides a detailed analysis of the method, including its theoretical underpinnings and practical implications, which are supported by comprehensive empirical results.
- The reduction in model size and computational requirements is substantial, which is particularly beneficial for deploying LLMs on resource-constrained devices.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a comprehensive comparison with other sparsification techniques, which could have provided a more robust evaluation of the proposed method.
- The method's scalability to larger models is not adequately addressed, raising concerns about its applicability to very large models.
- The paper does not discuss the potential impact of the method on fine-tuning, which could be a significant limitation for practical applications.
- There is a lack of discussion on the computational overhead introduced by the method, particularly in terms of memory and computational resources required for the PCA computation.
- The paper does not provide sufficient details on the training process, which could affect the reproducibility of the results.
- The method's effectiveness in terms of energy efficiency is not clearly demonstrated, which could be a critical concern for practical deployment.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors provide more details on how the method performs on larger models, such as those with over 100B parameters?
- Is there a potential for the method to be applied to models that use LayerNorm instead of RMSNorm?
- How does the method perform when applied to different model architectures, such as those with multiple attention layers or different FFN configurations?
- Can the authors provide a more detailed analysis of the computational overhead introduced by the method, particularly in terms of memory and computational resources?
- How does the method compare in terms of energy efficiency and practical deployment to other sparsification techniques?
- Could the authors provide more details on the training process, particularly how the PCA computation is integrated into the training pipeline?
- Is there a possibility of extending the method to include a fine-tuning step to potentially improve performance?
