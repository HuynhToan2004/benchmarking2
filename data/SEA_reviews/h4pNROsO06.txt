 **Summary:**
The paper explores a novel framework for sampling from unnormalized densities using a path-space perspective, which unifies various existing diffusion-based sampling methods such as the Schrödinger Bridge problem, diffusion-based generative modeling, and time-reversal of reference processes. It introduces a log-variance divergence loss as an alternative to the KL divergence, which is shown to provide better performance and stability. The paper also discusses the challenges of mode collapse and numerical instability, which are addressed by the proposed loss function. Theoretical and numerical results are provided to support the effectiveness of the log-variance loss over the KL divergence, although the paper could benefit from more extensive numerical experiments and a clearer presentation of the theoretical connections.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel perspective on sampling from unnormalized densities using a path-space viewpoint, which unifies various existing diffusion-based sampling methods.
- The proposed log-variance divergence loss is shown to provide better performance and stability compared to the KL divergence, which is a significant contribution to the field.
- The paper is well-written and provides a comprehensive theoretical framework, making it accessible and understandable for readers.
- The authors have successfully demonstrated the effectiveness of the log-variance loss through both theoretical analysis and numerical experiments, showing improvements over the KL divergence in certain scenarios.
- The paper addresses the significant issue of mode collapse in diffusion-based sampling methods, which is a crucial aspect for practical applications.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks sufficient numerical experiments to convincingly demonstrate the superiority of the log-variance loss over the KL divergence in general settings.
- The presentation of the paper could be improved, particularly in the explanation of the theoretical connections and the derivation of the log-variance loss.
- The paper does not provide a clear explanation of how the log-variance loss avoids differentiation through the SDE solver, which is a crucial aspect of its claimed benefits.
- There is a lack of discussion on the limitations and potential drawbacks of using the log-variance loss, which could provide a more balanced view of its applicability.
- The paper could benefit from more extensive numerical experiments to validate the claims made about the log-variance loss, particularly in high-dimensional settings.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors provide more details on how the log-variance loss avoids differentiation through the SDE solver, and clarify the computational implications of this approach?
- How does the proposed log-variance loss compare to other divergence losses, such as the F-divergence, in terms of performance and computational efficiency?
- Can the authors provide a more detailed explanation of the theoretical connections between the different methods discussed in the paper, particularly the derivation of the log-variance loss from the KL divergence?
- How does the choice of the reference measure affect the performance of the log-variance loss, and what are the implications of this choice in practical applications?
- Could the authors provide more examples or scenarios where the log-variance loss might not be the most effective choice, and discuss the conditions under which the KL divergence might be preferred?
