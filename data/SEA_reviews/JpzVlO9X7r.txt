 **Summary:**
The paper explores the function modeling capabilities of GPT-4, specifically its ability to leverage domain knowledge to model functions effectively. It evaluates GPT-4 against traditional machine learning models using both synthetic and real-world datasets, demonstrating its capacity to outperform these models in certain scenarios. The paper also investigates the impact of domain knowledge on GPT-4's performance and proposes a framework for evaluating LLMs' function modeling capabilities. However, concerns are raised about the lack of comparison with other LLMs, the potential for data contamination, and the generalizability of the findings.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written and easy to follow, with clear and well-organized sections.
- It presents a novel and interesting approach to understanding the function modeling capabilities of large language models (LLMs), focusing on the intuitive understanding of functions without gradient-based learning.
- The experiments are comprehensive, covering both synthetic and real-world datasets, and demonstrate the potential of LLMs in leveraging domain knowledge for function modeling tasks.
- The paper includes a detailed comparison of GPT-4 with MLP models, highlighting the advantages of LLMs in terms of domain knowledge utilization and generalization.
- The proposed framework for evaluating LLMs' function modeling capabilities is comprehensive and could be useful for future research in this area.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a thorough comparison with other LLMs, limiting the generalizability of the findings.
- The methodology is not clearly defined, particularly how GPT-4 was prompted and the specifics of the model's performance metrics (e.g., MSE) are not adequately explained.
- The paper does not sufficiently address potential data contamination issues, especially in real-world datasets where data leakage could significantly impact the results.
- The evaluation is limited to a single model (GPT-4), which might not be generalizable to other LLMs or models trained differently.
- The paper does not adequately discuss the limitations of the study, such as the potential for data leakage and the generalizability of the findings to other LLMs or models trained differently.
- There is a lack of discussion on how the results might be influenced by different prompting strategies or the specifics of the LLM's training data.

**Questions:**
- How does the performance of GPT-4 vary with different prompting strategies?
- Could the authors provide more details on how they ensured that the LLM did not use any data from the test set during training?
- How does the paper's methodology compare to other LLMs, particularly in terms of domain knowledge utilization and generalization?
- Can the authors discuss the potential for data leakage in their experiments and how they addressed this issue?
- How does the paper's evaluation methodology apply to other LLMs, and what are the implications for the generalizability of the findings?
- Could the authors clarify the specifics of the LLM's training data and how this might have influenced the results?
