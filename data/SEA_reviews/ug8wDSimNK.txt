 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel approach using GPT-4 to model agents in imperfect information games, specifically focusing on Leduc Hold'em. It proposes a methodology that leverages GPT-4's ability to understand game rules and opponent behavior, enhancing its performance against traditional algorithms. The authors demonstrate that GPT-4 can outperform other models like CFR+ and NFSP, showcasing its potential in complex decision-making environments. The paper also discusses the challenges of implementing GPT-4 in different game contexts and the need for further research to improve its performance and adaptability.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, clear, and easy to follow, with a clear motivation and a novel approach to using LLMs in imperfect information games.
- The proposed method is innovative, particularly in its use of GPT-4 for theory of mind (ToM) modeling, which is a significant advancement in the field.
- The experiments are well-designed, with detailed explanations of the methodology and results, including an ablation study that provides valuable insights into the effectiveness of different components of the model.
- The paper provides a comprehensive evaluation, including both quantitative and qualitative assessments, which helps to validate the claims made about the effectiveness of the proposed method.
- The paper makes a significant contribution to the field by demonstrating that GPT-4 can outperform traditional algorithms in imperfect information games, which is a promising direction for future research.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed discussion on the limitations and challenges of using GPT-4 in different game contexts, particularly in terms of its adaptability and generalizability.
- There is a noticeable absence of a theoretical analysis or a deeper exploration into the reasons behind GPT-4's performance, which could enhance the understanding of its capabilities and limitations.
- The paper does not sufficiently compare its results with other state-of-the-art methods, which could provide a more comprehensive evaluation of the proposed method's effectiveness.
- The paper does not discuss the potential negative societal impacts of using GPT-4 in game environments, which could be a significant concern for some readers.
- The paper could benefit from more detailed explanations and examples of how GPT-4's theory of mind modeling works in practice, particularly in the context of different game scenarios.

**Questions:**
- Could you provide more details on how GPT-4's theory of mind modeling operates in practice, especially in scenarios where opponents have different behavioral patterns?
- How does the performance of GPT-4 compare when playing against other agents trained using different methods, such as those based on reinforcement learning or game theory?
- What are the specific challenges and limitations you have encountered when implementing GPT-4 in different game contexts, and how do you address these issues?
- Can you discuss the potential negative societal impacts of using GPT-4 in game environments, and how these impacts could be mitigated?
- How do you ensure that the results of your experiments are robust and not influenced by the specific prompts or configurations used in your experiments?
- Could you provide more examples or case studies of how GPT-4's theory of mind modeling is applied in different game scenarios, particularly in situations where the opponent's behavior is not immediately apparent?
