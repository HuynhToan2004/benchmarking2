 **Summary:**
The paper introduces Crystalformer, a novel Transformer-based architecture for predicting material properties from crystal structures. Crystalformer leverages an "infinitely connected attention" mechanism to model the periodic nature of crystal structures, which is a significant departure from traditional methods like Matformer that rely on hybrid Transformer-GNN architectures. This new approach is claimed to be more computationally efficient and physically interpretable, with experiments demonstrating its effectiveness on datasets like Materials Project and JARVIS-DFT. The paper also discusses the challenges of integrating the infinitely connected attention mechanism with the Transformer architecture and provides a detailed analysis of the computational implications.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel Transformer-based architecture for crystal structures, which is a significant departure from existing methods that rely on hybrid Transformer-GNN architectures.
- The proposed model is shown to be more computationally efficient and physically interpretable, which is a notable improvement over previous methods.
- The paper is well-written, with clear explanations of the model's components and their motivations, making it accessible to a broad audience.
