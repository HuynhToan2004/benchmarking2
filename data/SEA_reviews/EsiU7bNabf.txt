 **Summary:**
The paper addresses the challenge of fine-tuning large language models (LLMs) with multiple tasks, aiming to reduce negative interference and improve performance. It introduces a novel clustering algorithm that groups tasks based on affinity scores, which are then used to optimize the training process. This approach is validated through extensive experiments, demonstrating improvements over traditional methods such as spectral clustering and Lloyd's algorithm. The paper also constructs a new evaluation benchmark, enhancing the reproducibility and utility of the research. The proposed methodology, although innovative, is critiqued for its computational complexity and the limited scope of its evaluations.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, clear, and easy to follow, making it accessible to a broad audience.
- The introduction of a new clustering algorithm to group tasks based on affinity scores is a significant contribution to the field, addressing the issue of negative interference in multitask learning.
- The experimental results are robust and demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed method, showing improvements over existing methods like spectral clustering and Lloyd's algorithm.
- The paper constructs a new evaluation benchmark, which enhances the reproducibility and utility of the research, facilitating further discussions and potential improvements in the field.
- The proposed method is technically sound, and the paper provides a clear and well-structured presentation, making it easy to understand and apply the proposed methodology.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed discussion on the computational complexity of the proposed method, which is crucial for understanding its practical applicability in real-world scenarios.
- The experimental evaluation is limited, focusing primarily on a single model (T5-Base) and a few datasets. Expanding the evaluation to include a broader range of models and datasets would strengthen the paper's claims.
- The paper does not sufficiently discuss the limitations of the proposed method, which is essential for understanding its applicability and potential drawbacks.
- There is a lack of comparison with other state-of-the-art methods, which could help in positioning the proposed method within the current research landscape.
- The paper does not provide a detailed comparison of the performance of the proposed method across different models and datasets, which is necessary to assess its generalizability and effectiveness.

**Questions:**
- Could you provide more details on the computational complexity of the proposed method, especially in terms of time and memory requirements?
- How does the proposed method perform when applied to larger and more complex models, such as those based on the T5 architecture?
- Is there a possibility to extend the proposed method to handle hierarchical relationships between tasks, as suggested by the experimental results?
- Could you clarify the experimental setup, particularly the selection of parameters and the rationale behind the choices made?
- How does the proposed method compare to other state-of-the-art methods, and what are the specific advantages or disadvantages of each approach?
- Could you provide more details on the experimental results, particularly the statistical significance of the improvements observed and the variability in performance across different datasets and model configurations?
