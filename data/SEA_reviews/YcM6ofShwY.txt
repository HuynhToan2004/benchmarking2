 **Summary:**
The paper introduces BayesDiff, a novel method for estimating pixel-wise uncertainty in diffusion models using Bayesian inference. This method leverages the last-layer Laplace approximation to perform Bayesian inference of pre-trained score models, enabling simultaneous image generation and uncertainty estimation. The approach is designed to enhance image quality by identifying and filtering out low-fidelity images and improving the diversity and quality of generated images. Extensive experiments are conducted across various datasets and diffusion models to validate the effectiveness of BayesDiff. Despite its innovative approach, the paper faces criticism for its limited novelty and insufficient comparison with existing methods.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel method for estimating pixel-wise uncertainty in diffusion models, which can be aggregated into a sample-wise metric for filtering out low-fidelity images.
- The method is well-articulated and supported by extensive experiments that demonstrate its effectiveness in enhancing the quality of generated images.
- The authors have provided a clear and detailed explanation of the method, including a step-by-step procedure and a clear algorithm presentation.
- The paper is well-written and easy to follow, with a comprehensive experimental setup that includes diverse datasets and various diffusion models.
- The method's potential applications include improving the quality of generated images and filtering out low-fidelity images, which could be beneficial for various downstream tasks.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper's novelty is limited as it primarily applies Bayesian inference to diffusion models, which has been explored in previous works.
- The comparison with existing methods is insufficient, and the paper lacks a detailed discussion on how the proposed method differs from or improves upon existing approaches.
- The paper does not provide a detailed analysis of the computational cost and training time associated with the proposed method, which could be crucial for practical implementation.
- The paper does not adequately address the scalability of the method to larger models, which could limit its applicability in more complex scenarios.
- The paper's focus on pixel-wise uncertainty estimation might not be practical for real-world applications where image-level uncertainty is more relevant.
- There is a lack of detailed discussion on the limitations of the proposed method and potential negative societal impacts.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors provide a more detailed comparison with existing methods, particularly in terms of the methodology and results?
- How does the proposed method perform in scenarios where the uncertainty is high, and how can this be mitigated to ensure the quality of the generated images?
- Could the authors provide more details on the computational cost and training time associated with the proposed method, and how this compares to existing methods?
- How does the proposed method scale to larger models, and what are the potential challenges or limitations in such scenarios?
- Could the authors provide more insights into the practical applications of pixel-wise uncertainty estimation, and how this can be translated into image-level uncertainty estimation for real-world applications?
- Can the authors discuss the potential limitations of the proposed method and any negative societal impacts that might arise from its use?
