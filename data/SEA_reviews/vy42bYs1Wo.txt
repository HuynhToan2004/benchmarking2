 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel off-policy primal-dual safe RL method aimed at improving constraint satisfaction in reinforcement learning (RL) by addressing the issue of cost underestimation in naive off-policy primal-dual methods. The method employs two key components: conservative policy optimization using an upper confidence bound of the true cost value and local policy convexification using the augmented Lagrangian method. These components aim to enhance policy optimization by reducing estimation uncertainty and expanding the search space for high-reward policies. The paper validates the method's effectiveness through empirical results on benchmark tasks and a real-world auto-bidding task, demonstrating superior sample efficiency and constraint satisfaction compared to existing methods.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel off-policy primal-dual method that effectively addresses the challenge of cost underestimation in naive off-policy primal-dual methods, providing a significant contribution to the field of safe RL.
- The methodology is supported by both theoretical analysis and empirical results, demonstrating the effectiveness of the proposed approach.
- The paper is well-written, clear, and easy to follow, making it accessible to a broad audience.
- The experiments conducted on benchmark tasks and a real-world auto-bidding task show that the proposed method achieves superior sample efficiency compared to existing methods, with fewer constraint violations during training.
- The paper provides a detailed description of the proposed algorithm, which can be easily implemented and reproduced, enhancing its practical applicability.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed discussion on the computational complexity and resource requirements of the proposed method, which could affect its scalability and applicability in more complex or resource-constrained environments.
- There is a lack of clarity in the experimental setup, particularly in the semi-batch setting, which is crucial for understanding the practical implications of the proposed method.
- The paper does not adequately address the limitations of the proposed method, such as potential instability issues and the sensitivity to hyperparameters.
- The paper does not include a discussion on the limitations of the method, which could help in understanding the scope and applicability of the proposed approach.
- The paper does not provide a detailed comparison with on-policy baselines, which could help in understanding the relative advantages and disadvantages of the proposed off-policy method.
- The paper does not discuss the limitations of the method in terms of its scalability, applicability to different types of constraints, and the impact of different types of cost functions.

**Questions:**

- How does the proposed method perform in more complex or resource-constrained environments?

- How does the proposed method compare to on-policy baselines, and what are the specific advantages of using an off-policy method in this context?
- Can the authors discuss the limitations of the proposed method and provide insights into potential future research directions?
- How does the proposed method handle different types of constraints, and what are the implications for its applicability in various real-world scenarios?
