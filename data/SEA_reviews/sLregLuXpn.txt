 **Summary:**
The paper explores the impact of Gaussian noise injection during training on GAN-based image-to-image translation models, focusing on how this noise affects the alignment of distributions and the model's robustness to unseen noise types. Theoretical insights are provided through the lens of f-divergence, demonstrating how noise variance influences the divergence between real and generated distributions. Extensive experiments validate these theoretical findings, showing that the proposed method can enhance noise robustness in various I2I translation models. However, concerns are raised about the generalizability of the findings to more complex noise types and the potential limitations of the method in real-world applications.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written and structured, making it easy to follow and understand the content.
- The theoretical analysis is rigorous and provides a clear understanding of the impact of Gaussian noise injection on image-to-image translation models.
- The experimental results are extensive and demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed method in improving noise robustness in various I2I translation models.
- The paper introduces a novel connection between f-divergence and score matching, which sheds light on the implications of Gaussian noise injection on I2I model training.
- The theoretical insights into the impact of Gaussian noise on aligning probability distributions are valuable and provide a sophisticated theoretical understanding beyond heuristic applications.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed discussion on the limitations of the proposed method, particularly in handling more complex noise types beyond Gaussian noise.
- The generalizability of the findings to more complex noise types and real-world applications is not adequately addressed.
- The experimental setup is somewhat limited, focusing mainly on supervised I2I tasks and not exploring unsupervised or more challenging scenarios like domain adaptation.
- The paper does not sufficiently compare the proposed method with existing noise injection methods, which could have provided a more robust evaluation of its effectiveness.
- The theoretical analysis is limited to isotropic Gaussian noise, which may not fully capture the complexities of real-world noise distributions.
- The paper does not sufficiently discuss the potential negative societal impacts of the proposed method, which is an important consideration for any technological advancement.

**Questions:**
- How does the proposed method perform when the noise distribution during inference is different from that during training?
- Can the method be adapted to handle more complex noise types, such as those found in real-world scenarios?
- Is there a way to determine the optimal noise variance for different noise types during inference?
- How does the method compare to other noise injection methods, such as those based on adversarial training or robustness techniques?
- Could the authors provide more details on the experimental setup, including the specific GAN architectures used and the training conditions?
- How does the method perform in more challenging scenarios, such as domain adaptation, where the noise conditions vary significantly across different domains?
- Can the authors discuss the potential negative societal impacts of the proposed method and how these might be mitigated?
