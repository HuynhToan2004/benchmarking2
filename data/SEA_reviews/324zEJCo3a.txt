 **Summary:**
The paper investigates the computational complexity of different forms of explanations in machine learning models, focusing on both local and global interpretability. It introduces a framework that systematically evaluates various forms of explanations, such as sufficient reasons, redundant features, and completion counts, using computational complexity theory. The authors demonstrate that the computational complexity of global explanations can vary across different model types, highlighting interesting phenomena like the computational tractability of global explanations for certain models. The paper also explores the duality between local and global explanations and provides a complexity hierarchy of explaining different models.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written and clearly articulates the problem of evaluating interpretability from a computational complexity perspective, providing a novel approach to the study of interpretability in machine learning.
- It introduces a formal framework for evaluating the local and global interpretability of ML models, which is a significant contribution to the field.
- The authors have conducted a comprehensive study on the computational complexity of obtaining different forms of explanations for various ML models, offering novel insights into the overarching characteristics of these types of explanations.
- The paper presents interesting results, such as the duality relationship between local and global forms of explanations and the uniqueness of certain global forms of explanations, which are compelling evidence of the importance of analyzing ML explainability from a computational complexity perspective.
- The paper is well-organized, with clear sections and a logical flow that makes it easy to follow and understand.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a clear motivation for studying the computational complexity of obtaining different forms of explanations, and it does not provide sufficient intuition or real-world examples to justify the practical relevance of these results.
- The computational complexity results are not presented in a way that is accessible to readers without a strong background in computational complexity theory, making the paper less accessible to a broader audience.
- The paper does not provide sufficient experimental validation or empirical evidence to support the theoretical claims, which could be crucial for demonstrating the practical applicability and effectiveness of the proposed framework.
- The paper does not address the limitations of its framework, which could help in understanding the scope and applicability of the proposed approach.
- The paper could benefit from a more thorough discussion of the implications of its findings for practical applications in machine learning, particularly in terms of how these results could be used to improve the interpretability of models in real-world scenarios.
