 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a Texture Transformer Network (TxTN) aimed at enhancing lesion identification in medical imaging by integrating texture patterns and features into Vision Transformer (ViT) architecture. This novel approach aims to address the limitations of ViT by integrating texture features, which are typically represented as vectors or matrices, into the transformer network. The TxTN is designed to replace the pixel projection layer in ViT, thereby improving the model's ability to capture both local and global texture information. The paper presents an ablation study and experiments using two public datasets, demonstrating the effectiveness of the proposed method. However, the paper has been criticized for its lack of clarity in methodological details, insufficient comparison with state-of-the-art methods, and limited experimental validation on diverse datasets.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel method by integrating texture patterns into the Vision Transformer (ViT) architecture, which is a significant advancement in medical image analysis.
- The methodology is well-presented, with clear explanations of the proposed method and its components, making the paper easy to follow.
- The paper provides an ablation study that shows the effectiveness of the proposed method in enhancing lesion identification in medical imaging.
- The integration of texture features into the transformer network is a novel approach that could potentially improve the performance of existing models.
- The paper is well-written, making it accessible to readers and facilitating understanding of the proposed method.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks clarity in some methodological details, particularly in the explanation of how texture patterns are integrated into the transformer network and how they are represented as vectors or matrices.
- The comparison with state-of-the-art methods is insufficient, and the experimental results are not convincingly superior to existing methods.
- The paper does not provide a detailed analysis of the limitations of the proposed method, which could help in understanding its applicability and effectiveness in different scenarios.
- The paper's claims about the limitations of ViT and the benefits of the proposed method are not convincingly supported by the experimental results.
- The paper lacks a comprehensive evaluation on diverse datasets, which could help in validating the generalizability of the proposed method.
- The paper does not sufficiently discuss the computational complexity of the proposed method, which could be a significant concern in practical applications.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors provide more details on how texture patterns are integrated into the transformer network and how they are represented as vectors or matrices?
- How does the proposed method compare with other state-of-the-art methods in terms of computational efficiency and effectiveness in different medical imaging modalities?
- Can the authors provide a more detailed analysis of the limitations of the proposed method and discuss its applicability in different scenarios?
- How does the proposed method perform on different medical imaging modalities, such as MRI and CT scans?
- Could the authors provide a more detailed discussion on the computational complexity of the proposed method and how it compares with existing methods in terms of training and inference times?
- How does the proposed method handle different image resolutions and sizes, and what are the implications for the performance of the model?
