 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel sampling method named Basis-Aware Threshold (BAT) sampling, which aims to address the softmax bottleneck in truncation sampling. BAT sampling employs a linear program to identify tokens that are in the true support of the language model's predicted distribution, thereby improving the quality of generated text. Theoretical proofs and empirical evidence are provided to support the effectiveness of BAT sampling, particularly in low-entropy generation settings. The method is evaluated against various truncation sampling methods and shows promising results, although it requires significant computational resources and may not be practical for all settings.

**Strengths:**
- The paper provides a clear theoretical explanation for truncation sampling, which is crucial for understanding the behavior of language models in autoregressive generation.
- The proposed method, BAT sampling, is innovative and well-explained, offering a novel approach to addressing the softmax bottleneck in truncation sampling.
- The empirical results demonstrate the effectiveness of BAT sampling in low-entropy generation settings, showing improvements over traditional truncation sampling methods.
- The paper is well-written, making complex concepts accessible and understandable, which is essential for a broad audience in the field of machine learning and natural language processing.
- The theoretical insights provided are robust and contribute significantly to the understanding of truncation sampling methods.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper primarily focuses on low-entropy generation settings, which may not generalize well to other settings, such as high-entropy generation, where truncation sampling is less effective.
- The computational cost of BAT sampling is high, making it impractical for real-time applications or in settings where fast generation is necessary.
- The empirical evaluation is limited to GPT-2, which may not fully demonstrate the effectiveness of BAT sampling across different models or datasets.
- The paper could benefit from a more detailed comparison with other sampling methods, such as nucleus sampling, to better understand the advantages and limitations of BAT sampling.
- The method's practical applicability is limited due to its high computational cost, which may not be feasible for all settings.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors provide more details on the computational cost of BAT sampling and discuss potential strategies for reducing this cost?
- How does BAT sampling compare to other sampling methods, such as nucleus sampling, in terms of practicality and effectiveness?
- Could the authors clarify the definition of MAUVE in the context of the paper and provide more detailed explanations for the MAUVE scores and their interpretation?
- Is there a possibility to extend the evaluation to include other models and datasets to validate the generalizability of the findings?
- Can the authors discuss the practical implications of the theoretical results and how they translate into real-world applications?
