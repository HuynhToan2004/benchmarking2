 **Summary:**
The paper investigates the learning dynamics in overparameterized neural networks, focusing on the implicit regularization effects of noise in gradient descent. It builds on previous works by Li et al. (2022) and Blanc et al. (2020), employing multiscale methods for elliptic partial differential equations (PDEs) to analyze the learning dynamics in the small learning rate limit. The authors aim to derive the effective stochastic dynamics at the O(1/η²) step, providing an alternative derivation to Li et al. (2022). Despite its theoretical contributions, the paper is critiqued for its limited novelty, as it primarily offers a different perspective on existing results without introducing significant new insights or methodologies.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, making the results easy to follow, and the authors have provided clear explanations of their results.
- The application of multiscale methods to the analysis of learning dynamics in overparameterized neural networks is a significant contribution, providing a new perspective on understanding the dynamics of gradient descent.
- The paper introduces a novel approach to analyzing the learning dynamics by employing multiscale methods, which is a promising direction for future research in the field.
- The paper provides a clear and detailed derivation of the results, which is a valuable resource for researchers in the field.

**Weaknesses:**
- The novelty of the paper is limited as it primarily offers an alternative derivation to existing results without introducing new insights or methodologies.
- The paper lacks empirical validation of its theoretical claims, which is crucial for establishing the practical applicability and effectiveness of the proposed methods.
- The paper's contribution to the field is seen as incremental, with the primary focus being on providing an alternative derivation rather than introducing new theoretical insights or methodologies.
- The paper does not sufficiently differentiate its contributions from those of previous works, which may lead to confusion about the originality and significance of the research.
- The paper's relevance to the ICLR community is questionable, as the theoretical contributions may not be of significant interest or relevance to the majority of the conference attendees.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors clarify the differences between the results presented in their paper and those of Li et al. (2022)?
- How does the paper's contribution differ from the existing literature, particularly in terms of the theoretical insights and methodologies used?
- Could the authors provide empirical evidence or simulations to support their theoretical claims, especially regarding the effectiveness of the proposed multiscale methods in real-world scenarios?
- In light of the concerns about the novelty and relevance of the paper, can the authors elaborate on the unique contributions and benefits of their research to the ICLR community?
