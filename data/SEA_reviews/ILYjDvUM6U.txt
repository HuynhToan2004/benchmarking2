 **Summary:**
The paper introduces Uncertainty-Aware Inverse Constrained Reinforcement Learning (UAICRL), a novel framework that addresses both aleatoric and epistemic uncertainties in inverse constrained reinforcement learning (ICRL). It incorporates distributional Bellman updates and flow-based generative data augmentation to manage these uncertainties. The paper demonstrates the effectiveness of UAICRL through empirical results, showing that it outperforms existing methods in various environments, including discrete and continuous spaces. However, the paper has been critiqued for its lack of theoretical analysis, the need for clearer presentation, and the absence of comparisons with certain baseline methods.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, with clear explanations of the proposed methods and the experiments.
- The motivation for the study is clear, and the experiments are comprehensive, covering both discrete and continuous spaces.
- The paper provides a detailed explanation of the proposed method, including the formulation of the risk-sensitive constraint and the use of distributional Bellman updates.
- The inclusion of empirical results that demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed method in reducing constraint violation rates and improving feasible rewards is a significant strength.
- The paper addresses both aleatoric and epistemic uncertainties, which are crucial for safe and robust constraint inference in inverse constrained reinforcement learning.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a theoretical analysis of the proposed method, which could strengthen the paper's contributions.
- The presentation could be improved by reducing the use of abbreviations and acronyms, which can make the paper less accessible to readers.
- The paper does not compare the proposed method with certain baseline methods, such as the Variational Inverse Constrained Reinforcement Learning (VICRL), which could have provided a more robust evaluation of the proposed method.
- The paper does not address the computational efficiency of the proposed method, which could be a significant concern for real-world applications.
- The paper could benefit from a more detailed discussion on the limitations of the proposed method, including its applicability to different types of constraints and its scalability to more complex environments.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors provide a more detailed discussion on the limitations of the proposed method, including its applicability to different types of constraints and its scalability to more complex environments?
- How does the proposed method compare with the Variational Inverse Constrained Reinforcement Learning (VICRL) in terms of performance and computational efficiency?
- Could the authors clarify the role of the epistemic uncertainty in the proposed method and how it affects the learning process?
- How does the paper ensure that the generated trajectories by the FTG are not redundant and contribute to the learning process?
