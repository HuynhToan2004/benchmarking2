 **Summary:**
The paper investigates the calibration of CLIP models during test-time prompt tuning, focusing on the relationship between prompt choice and calibration error. It introduces the Average Text Feature Dispersion (ATFD) as a metric to optimize prompt tuning, aiming to enhance model calibration without the need for labeled data. The proposed method, Calibrated Test-time Prompt Tuning (C-TPT), is evaluated across various datasets and CLIP architectures, showing improved calibration performance compared to traditional methods. The paper also provides a theoretical analysis and empirical evidence supporting the effectiveness of C-TPT.

**Strengths:**
- The paper addresses an important problem of calibration in CLIP models during test-time prompt tuning, which is crucial for applications in real-world scenarios.
- The proposed method, C-TPT, is simple, intuitive, and easy to implement, making it accessible for practitioners.
- The paper provides a thorough theoretical analysis and empirical evidence to support the effectiveness of the proposed method, which is well-motivated and well-explained.
- The paper is well-written, with clear figures and tables that aid in understanding the results and methodologies.
- The proposed metric, ATFD, is a simple yet effective way to optimize prompt tuning, and the method shows promising results in improving the calibration of CLIP models.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a comprehensive comparison with other calibration methods, particularly those that do not require labeled data, which could provide a more robust evaluation of the proposed method.
- The paper does not discuss the limitations of the proposed method, which could provide insights into its applicability and potential drawbacks.
- The paper could benefit from a more detailed discussion on the relationship between ATFD and ECE, as well as the impact of different prompt tuning methods on calibration performance.
- The paper's results are not consistently superior across all datasets and CLIP architectures, suggesting that the method may not be universally effective.
- The paper could be improved by including a more detailed discussion on the potential negative societal impacts of the proposed method.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors clarify the relationship between ATFD and ECE, particularly how ATFD maximization leads to calibration?
- How does the proposed method compare to other calibration methods, especially those that do not require labeled data?
- What are the potential negative societal impacts of the proposed method, and how can these be mitigated?
- How does the proposed method perform when applied to different types of datasets and CLIP architectures, and what are the implications for its generalizability?
- Could the authors provide more details on the experimental setup, such as the specific hyperparameters used and the computational resources required?
