 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel algorithm for learning the Pareto front in multi-objective Markov decision processes (MOMDPs) through a decomposition of the problem into single-objective sub-problems. The proposed algorithm, IPRO, utilizes a Pareto oracle to iteratively improve the approximation of the Pareto front, offering theoretical guarantees for convergence and an upper bound on the approximation error. The paper is structured around the IPRO algorithm, which involves selecting reference points and refining them through iterative processes to approach the Pareto front. Despite its theoretical contributions, the paper lacks comprehensive experimental validation and detailed comparative analysis with existing methods, which limits its practical applicability and the clarity of its advantages over existing approaches.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written and easy to follow, with clear explanations and well-designed figures that aid in understanding the proposed method.
- The authors introduce a novel approach to learning the Pareto front in multi-objective Markov decision processes (MOMDPs), which is a significant contribution to the field of reinforcement learning.
- The paper provides theoretical guarantees for the convergence of the proposed algorithm and an upper bound on the approximation error, which is a strong theoretical contribution.
- The methodology is clearly presented, with detailed explanations of the algorithm's components and their theoretical underpinnings.
- The paper is well-structured, making it accessible to readers not familiar with the specifics of multi-objective reinforcement learning (MORL).

**Weaknesses:**
