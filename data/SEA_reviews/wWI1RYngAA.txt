 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel adaptive offline data replay method for offline-to-online reinforcement learning (RL), where the mixing ratio of offline and online data is dynamically adjusted based on the exploration reward of the agent in the online learning phase. This method is evaluated across various environments and datasets, demonstrating its effectiveness compared to conventional methods. The approach utilizes a bandit model to explore and exploit different mixing ratios, aiming to optimize the utilization of offline data in the online training phase. Despite its empirical strengths, the paper is criticized for its limited theoretical analysis and lack of comparison with more advanced offline RL algorithms.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces an adaptive offline data replay method for offline-to-online RL, which is a novel approach that dynamically adjusts the mixing ratio of offline and online data based on the exploration reward of the agent in the online learning phase.
- The method is evaluated across various environments and datasets, demonstrating its effectiveness compared to conventional methods.
- The paper is well-written, easy to follow, and provides a clear motivation for the proposed method.
- The adaptive mixing ratio selection is a valuable contribution, which is particularly beneficial for environments where the optimal mixing ratio varies across different tasks and datasets.
- The experimental results are impressive, showing that the proposed method outperforms conventional methods in various environments and datasets.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a thorough theoretical analysis of the proposed method, which could enhance the understanding and credibility of the approach.
- The paper does not compare the proposed method with more advanced offline RL algorithms, which could provide a more robust evaluation of the method's performance.
- The experimental setup and results are not clearly presented, with some figures and results being confusing or inadequately explained.
- The paper does not discuss the potential limitations or negative societal impacts of the proposed method, which could be a concern for some readers.
- The paper could benefit from a more detailed discussion on the computational demands and potential limitations posed by offline data, as well as the generalization ability of the proposed method across different tasks and datasets.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors provide a more detailed theoretical analysis of the proposed method to enhance the understanding and credibility of the approach?
- How does the proposed method compare with more advanced offline RL algorithms in terms of performance and efficiency?
- In the experimental setup, how are the results presented in Figure 3 and Figure 4 averaged across different seeds, and what is the standard deviation of the results?
- How does the proposed method handle the distribution shift between offline data and samples from the online environment?
- Is there a possibility to use importance sampling techniques to address the distribution shift between offline data and online data?
- Could the authors provide a clearer explanation of the experimental results, particularly in Figure 3 and Figure 4, to improve the readability and understanding of the paper?
