 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel learning framework termed "Big Learning," which aims to model multiple conditional and marginal data distributions using a universal foundation model. This approach extends beyond traditional methods like GANs and VAEs by incorporating the concept of "Big Learning," which can be applied to various models including GANs, VAEs, and BERTs. The paper explores the application of this framework in unsupervised/unimodal and supervised/multimodal settings, demonstrating its flexibility and potential for diverse applications. However, the paper suffers from a lack of clarity in presentation and a limited evaluation scope, which raises concerns about its practical applicability and the depth of its theoretical contributions.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel learning framework, "Big Learning," which is a significant advancement in the field of generative models.
- The framework is flexible and can be applied to various models such as GANs, VAEs, and BERTs, demonstrating its broad applicability.
- The authors provide a clear and detailed explanation of the Big Learning framework, including its theoretical underpinnings and practical applications.
- The paper includes comprehensive experiments that validate the effectiveness of the proposed framework, showing that it can handle diverse tasks and data types.
- The manuscript is well-written, making it accessible and understandable to a broad audience, including those new to the field.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks clarity in its presentation, particularly in the introduction and some sections of the methodology, which could benefit from more detailed explanations and examples.
- There is a limited scope of evaluation, focusing primarily on the application of Big Learning in GANs and not exploring its potential in other areas such as language models or other generative models.
- The paper does not provide a comprehensive comparison with existing methods, which could help in understanding the unique contributions of Big Learning.
- There is a lack of discussion on the computational complexity and scalability of the proposed framework, which is crucial for understanding its practical implications.
- The paper does not adequately address the societal implications of Big Learning, which is a significant omission given the potential impact of such a framework.

**Questions:**
- Could you provide more details on how Big Learning can be applied to other types of generative models beyond GANs, such as diffusion models?
- How does the proposed framework handle the complexity and scalability issues, especially in large-scale applications?
- Can you clarify the role and impact of the "communication discriminator" in the Big Learning framework?
- How does the framework handle the potential for overfitting or underfitting in different scenarios, especially when dealing with incomplete data?
- Could you elaborate on the theoretical underpinnings of the Big Learning framework, particularly in relation to existing methods like GANs and VAEs?
- How does the framework ensure that the model's outputs are consistent with the original data distribution, especially in cases where the data distribution might not be fully understood?
