 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a self-supervised pre-training model for time series classification, focusing on data pre-processing and contrastive learning. The proposed method includes a data pre-processing module that utilizes a Self-Adaptive FIR Filter to enhance the quality of data, followed by a sorting similarity method for contrastive learning. The model is trained on a large dataset and fine-tuned on smaller datasets, demonstrating improved performance over existing methods. The methodology is validated through experiments on various real-world datasets, showcasing its effectiveness and efficiency. However, the paper lacks a comprehensive comparison with state-of-the-art methods and does not sufficiently discuss the limitations and societal impact of the proposed model.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written and easy to follow, making it accessible to a broad audience.
- The proposed method, which includes a data pre-processing module and a sorting similarity method for contrastive learning, is novel and contributes to the field of time series classification.
- The paper provides a comprehensive experimental evaluation, comparing the proposed method with existing methods on various real-world datasets, demonstrating its effectiveness and efficiency.
- The methodology is supported by a theoretical framework, which enhances its credibility and applicability.
- The paper introduces a new sorting similarity method for contrastive learning, which is a significant contribution to the field.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed discussion on the limitations and potential negative societal impacts of the proposed model.
- There is a lack of comparison with state-of-the-art methods, which could provide a clearer picture of the model's performance and novelty.
- The paper does not sufficiently discuss the computational complexity of the proposed method, which is crucial for understanding its practical applicability.
- The experimental results are not sufficiently detailed, particularly in terms of the statistical significance of the results and the comparative analysis with other methods.
- The paper does not adequately address the potential biases in the datasets used, which could affect the generalizability and applicability of the findings.
- There is a lack of discussion on the theoretical underpinnings of the proposed method, which could enhance its credibility and applicability.

**Questions:**
