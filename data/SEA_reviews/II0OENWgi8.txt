 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel method called Skill-Conditioned Optimal Agent (SCOPA), which leverages successor features representations to learn a continuous range of skills that solve tasks. SCOPA extends the generalized policy iteration framework with a policy skill improvement update based on successor features, which is analogous to the classic policy improvement update. The method is evaluated on various continuous control locomotion tasks and demonstrates significant performance improvements over existing methods. SCOPA's approach allows for the efficient learning of skills while maintaining a balance between performance and skill execution. The methodology is supported by extensive theoretical and empirical analysis, showcasing its effectiveness and potential for broader applications.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel method called Skill-Conditioned Optimal Agent (SCOPA), which leverages successor features representations to learn a continuous range of skills that solve tasks. This is a significant contribution to the field of reinforcement learning.
- The proposed method is well-motivated and well-explained, with clear mathematical formulations and a logical structure that makes it easy to follow.
- The paper provides extensive theoretical analysis and empirical results, demonstrating the effectiveness of the method.
- The method is evaluated on challenging continuous control locomotion tasks and shows significant performance improvements over existing methods, indicating its practical applicability.
- The paper includes a detailed discussion on the limitations of the proposed method, which is a commendable approach to transparency and credibility.
- The use of a world model for training and the integration of successor features into the policy skill improvement update are innovative and contribute to the efficiency and effectiveness of the method.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a clear explanation of the main innovation of the method, particularly the role and function of the Lagrange multiplier in the policy skill improvement update.
- The method's reliance on a pre-defined feature space for skills limits its adaptability to new environments or tasks.
- There is a lack of comparison with other methods such as DOMiNO, which could provide a better understanding of the method's relative performance and advantages.
- The paper does not sufficiently address the issue of out-of-distribution (OOD) skills, which is a critical aspect of skill generalization.
- The method's dependence on a world model for training raises concerns about its scalability and applicability in more complex environments.
- The paper does not provide sufficient details on how the Lagrangian multiplier is optimized and how it affects the learning process, which could affect the reproducibility and understanding of the results.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors clarify the role and function of the Lagrange multiplier in the policy skill improvement update?
- How does the method handle OOD skills, and what measures are in place to ensure robust skill generalization?
- Can the authors provide more details on the training process, particularly how the Lagrangian multiplier is optimized and how it affects the learning process?
- How does the method compare to other methods like DOMiNO, and what are the specific advantages of using SCOPA over other methods?
- Could the authors elaborate on the scalability of the method and how it can be adapted to more complex environments or tasks?
- How does the method ensure the diversity and quality of the learned skills, and what measures are in place to prevent the learning of suboptimal or redundant skills?
