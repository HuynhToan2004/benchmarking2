 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel training-free framework for video editing named Ground-A-Video, which leverages a diffusion model to perform multi-attribute editing in a temporally consistent manner. This is achieved by integrating grounding information into the latent representations, guided by optical flow and depth maps. The methodology includes the use of cross-frame gated attention and modulated cross-attention to enhance the interaction between different frames and maintain consistency. The paper also employs a control net to preserve the structural integrity of the video. Extensive experiments and comparisons with state-of-the-art methods are provided, demonstrating the effectiveness of the proposed approach. However, the paper is criticized for its complexity, lack of clarity in presentation, and limited novelty in its contributions.

**Strengths:**
- The paper presents a novel training-free framework for multi-attribute video editing, which is a significant contribution to the field.
- The methodology is supported by extensive experiments and comparisons with state-of-the-art methods, demonstrating the effectiveness of the proposed approach.
- The paper is well-written, making it easy to follow, and includes clear figures and diagrams that aid in understanding the proposed method.
- The paper introduces a novel use of cross-frame gated attention and modulated cross-attention, which are crucial for maintaining consistency and accuracy in video editing.
- The methodology is robust, with a focus on temporal consistency and the use of grounding information to guide the editing process.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper is overly complex, with multiple components and a high degree of technical detail which might make it difficult for readers to fully grasp.
- The presentation of the paper could be improved, particularly in terms of clarity and organization. The use of excessive technical details and complex diagrams detracts from the readability.
- The novelty of the contributions is limited, as many components of the proposed method have been previously introduced in other works.
- The paper lacks a detailed discussion on the limitations of the proposed method, which is crucial for understanding its applicability and potential drawbacks.
- The evaluation of the method is insufficient, with a limited number of videos used for testing and a lack of comparison with more recent or relevant methods.
- The paper could benefit from a more comprehensive discussion on the computational cost of the proposed method, including the time and resources required for each step.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors provide a more detailed explanation of how the grounding information is integrated into the latent representations?
- How does the proposed method handle the issue of multi-attribute editing in scenarios where attributes are not clearly defined or overlapping?
- What are the specific advantages of the proposed method over other existing methods, particularly in terms of computational efficiency and practical applicability?
- Could the authors provide more details on the experimental setup, including the number of videos used for testing and the criteria for selecting these videos?
- How does the proposed method perform when applied to longer video sequences, and what are the potential challenges or limitations in such scenarios?
- Could the authors clarify the role and impact of the 'ControlNet Scale' hyperparameter on the results, and provide more details on how it is adjusted or optimized?
