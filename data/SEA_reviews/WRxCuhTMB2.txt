 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a methodology for evaluating the effectiveness of uncertainty quantification (UQ) in machine learning models, specifically focusing on the disentanglement of epistemic and aleatoric uncertainties. The approach involves a novel evaluation methodology using synthetic data to assess the decomposition of uncertainty, comparing various UQ methods across different datasets. The paper proposes a new evaluation metric, the dE-Indicator, and employs statistical tests to assess the effectiveness of different UQ methods. However, the paper is criticized for its limited experimental scope, lack of novelty in methodology, and unclear presentation, which hampers its impact and readability.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, with clear explanations and a well-organized structure.
- The proposed evaluation methodology is novel and could be useful for assessing the effectiveness of uncertainty quantification (UQ) methods.
- The paper provides a comprehensive review of existing UQ methods and evaluates them using a new evaluation methodology.
- The use of synthetic data for uncertainty decomposition is innovative and could be beneficial for understanding the behavior of UQ methods in controlled environments.
- The inclusion of statistical tests and a detailed discussion on the results provides a robust analysis of the effectiveness of UQ methods.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a clear and coherent presentation, with some sections being hard to follow and others containing irrelevant content.
- The experimental setup is limited, focusing only on a few datasets and UQ methods, which limits the generalizability of the findings.
- The novelty of the proposed methodology is questionable, as it primarily involves a new evaluation metric without significant advancements in the underlying methodology.
- The paper does not adequately address the limitations of the proposed methodology, such as the assumption of Gaussian uncertainty and the lack of applicability to other types of data.
- The paper could benefit from a more detailed discussion on the related work and a clearer presentation of the results to enhance its readability and impact.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors clarify the relevance and contribution of the proposed methodology, especially in terms of its novelty and applicability to other types of data?
- How do the authors justify the choice of the dE-Indicator as the primary evaluation metric, and what are the implications of using different metrics?
- Could the authors provide more detailed explanations for the results, particularly the discrepancies observed in the performance of different UQ methods across different datasets?
- Is there a plan to extend the evaluation to other types of data or UQ methods to enhance the generalizability of the findings?
- How do the authors address the limitations of the proposed methodology, such as the assumptions of Gaussian uncertainty and the limited applicability to other types of data?
