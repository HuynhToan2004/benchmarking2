 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel method for single-positive multi-label learning (SPMLL) that estimates class-priors to improve the performance of multi-label classification. The authors propose a class-priors estimator that converges to the ground-truth class-prior, and an unbiased risk estimator is derived based on these estimates. The methodology is supported by theoretical guarantees and extensive experiments on ten multi-label learning benchmark datasets, showing superior performance compared to existing SPMLL approaches. Despite the theoretical and experimental strengths, the paper faces criticism for its lack of clarity in presentation, insufficient novelty, and inadequate comparison with recent works.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, with clear and concise presentation of the methodology and experimental results.
- The proposed method is novel, providing a new perspective on single-positive multi-label learning (SPMLL) by considering class-priors, which is a significant advancement over existing methods.
- Theoretical analysis is robust, with proofs and theoretical guarantees that support the proposed method's effectiveness.
- Extensive experiments are conducted on ten multi-label learning benchmark datasets, demonstrating the method's superiority over existing SPMLL approaches.
- The paper is well-structured, with clear definitions and explanations of the problem, methodology, and results, making it accessible to readers.
- The use of an unbiased risk estimator based on estimated class-priors is a novel approach that enhances the method's reliability and accuracy.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks clarity in presentation, particularly in the description of the methodology and the algorithm, which could hinder understanding for readers.
- The novelty of the method is questioned, as it appears to be a combination of existing techniques without significant innovation.
- The comparison with recent works, such as those by Zhou et al. and Xu et al., is insufficient, which could mislead the reader about the method's relative performance.
- The paper does not adequately address the issue of noisy labels, which is crucial in SPMLL scenarios.
- The theoretical analysis and proofs, particularly in theorems and lemmas, are not sufficiently detailed or rigorous, which could undermine the credibility of the results.
- The paper does not sufficiently address the limitations of the proposed method, which could provide valuable insights for future research.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors clarify the computational complexity of the proposed method and compare it with existing methods to demonstrate its efficiency?
- How does the proposed method handle the issue of noisy labels, particularly in scenarios where the class-prior estimation might be affected by label noise?
- Can the authors provide more detailed comparisons with recent works by Zhou et al. and Xu et al., especially in terms of experimental results and methodological innovations?
- Could the authors elaborate on the theoretical underpinnings of the method, particularly in the proofs and assumptions made in theorems and lemmas?
- How does the proposed method perform in scenarios where the class-prior estimation is not accurate, and what are the potential impacts on the overall performance?
- Can the authors discuss the potential limitations of the proposed method and suggest areas for future research to address these limitations?
