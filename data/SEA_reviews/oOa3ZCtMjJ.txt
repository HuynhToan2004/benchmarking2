 **Summary:**
The paper introduces HSPC-GAN, a method that integrates CLIP models with GANs to enhance text-to-image synthesis. HSPC-GAN utilizes a hierarchical semantic prompt generator and adaptor to guide CLIP's adjustment of visual features, aiming to improve semantic consistency between text and images. The methodology includes a Hard Mining Matching-Aware Loss to enhance training efficiency and reduce computational demands. Despite the innovative approach, concerns were raised regarding the clarity of the methodology, the necessity of the proposed losses, and the lack of comprehensive experimental validation.

**Strengths:**
- The paper addresses an important problem in text-to-image synthesis and proposes a novel approach using CLIP-based GANs.
- The methodology is well-explained, and the paper is generally well-written, making it easy to follow.
- The use of hierarchical semantic prompts and the Hard Mining Matching-Aware Loss are innovative and contribute to the field.
- The results, as shown in the figures, are impressive, demonstrating the effectiveness of the proposed methods.
- The paper is well-organized, with clear figures that aid in understanding the proposed method.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed explanation of the hard negative mining and the Hard Mining Matching-Aware Loss, making it difficult for readers to understand their impact on the model's performance.
- The necessity and effectiveness of the proposed losses are not convincingly demonstrated, especially in the ablation studies.
- The experimental setup and comparisons are not comprehensive, with insufficient details on the training conditions and dataset specifics.
- The paper does not adequately compare its results with existing methods, such as GALIP, which could have provided a clearer picture of the model's performance.
- The paper suffers from some typos and grammatical errors, which could detract from its professionalism.
- The paper does not sufficiently address the limitations of the proposed method, which is crucial for understanding its applicability and scope.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors provide more details on the hard negative mining and the Hard Mining Matching-Aware Loss, possibly including a detailed algorithm or a more thorough explanation?
- How does the proposed method compare to other state-of-the-art methods, particularly in terms of semantic consistency and image quality?
- Could the authors clarify the experimental setup, including the specifics of the datasets used and the training conditions?
- What are the implications of using different numbers of local prompts in the ablation studies, and how do these affect the model's performance?
- How does the model handle scenarios where the text description does not contain sufficient semantic information for effective prompting?
- Can the authors provide more details on the computational resources and training time required for the proposed method?
