 **Summary:**
The paper investigates the power of quantum kernel methods under noisy conditions, specifically focusing on global depolarization noise. It provides theoretical bounds on the prediction error of noisy quantum kernels, demonstrating how these errors depend on various parameters such as the number of layers affected by noise, the size of training samples, and the number of qubits. The paper also includes numerical simulations to validate these theoretical findings. Despite the paper's technical soundness and the novelty of its approach, concerns were raised about the practical applicability of the results, particularly due to the strong assumptions and idealized settings used.

**Strengths:**
- The paper addresses a timely and relevant problem by investigating the power of quantum kernel methods under noise, which is a significant issue in the field of quantum machine learning.
- The authors provide a clear and detailed analysis, including proofs and a discussion of the limitations of noisy quantum kernels, which could guide the development of more robust quantum kernel algorithms.
- The paper is well-written, with a clear and concise presentation of the main results and a logical structure that aids in understanding the complex theoretical concepts.
- The authors provide a comprehensive overview of the limitations of noisy quantum kernels, which could be useful for practitioners in the field of quantum machine learning.
- The paper includes a novel figure of merit that characterizes the power and limitations of quantum kernel methods, which could be a useful tool for evaluating the performance of quantum kernels in practical scenarios.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper makes strong assumptions, such as the unitarity of the encoding circuit and the absence of noise in the encoding process, which may not be realistic in practical scenarios.
- The results are idealized and may not hold in more realistic settings, such as those with balanced or imbalanced labels, which could significantly affect the performance of quantum kernels.
- The paper primarily focuses on global depolarization noise, which may not be the most relevant or practical noise model for quantum machine learning applications.
- The numerical experiments are limited and do not convincingly demonstrate the practical applicability of the theoretical results.
- The paper lacks a discussion on the generalization error, which is a critical aspect of quantum kernel methods, and does not compare its results with existing literature, which could provide a more robust validation of the findings.
- The paper could benefit from a more detailed discussion on the practical implications of the theoretical results and how they might be applied in real-world scenarios.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors clarify the assumptions made in the paper, particularly regarding the unitarity of the encoding circuit and the absence of noise in the encoding process?
- How do the results of the paper hold up under more realistic noise models, such as those that are more relevant to practical quantum machine learning applications?
- Can the authors provide a more detailed discussion on the generalization error and how it affects the performance of quantum kernels in practical scenarios?
- How do the results of the paper compare with existing literature, particularly in terms of the theoretical bounds on the prediction error under different noise conditions?
- Could the authors elaborate on the practical implications of their findings and discuss how they might be applied in real-world scenarios to improve the performance of quantum kernels?
