 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel approach to address the oversmoothing problem in Transformers by interpreting the self-attention mechanism as a graph filter and proposing a graph filter-based self-attention (GFSA) mechanism. This GFSA mechanism enriches the self-attention in Transformers with more diverse frequency information, which is crucial for mitigating the oversmoothing problem and enhancing the learning of latent representations. The paper presents extensive experiments across various domains, including computer vision, natural language processing, graph pattern classification, speech recognition, and code classification, demonstrating the effectiveness of the proposed method. Despite the significant performance improvements, the paper has been critiqued for its limited novelty, as the idea of using graph convolution to replace self-attention is not entirely new.

**Strengths:**
- The paper addresses a significant issue in Transformers, the oversmoothing problem, which is crucial for the field.
- The proposed graph filter-based self-attention (GFSA) mechanism is simple to implement and can be used in conjunction with other techniques to further improve the performance of Transformers.
- The paper provides extensive experiments across various domains, demonstrating the effectiveness of the proposed method.
- The experiments are comprehensive, covering a wide range of tasks, and the results are promising.
- The paper is well-written, easy to follow, and includes detailed experimental settings, which enhances the reproducibility of the results.

**Weaknesses:**
- The novelty of the proposed method is limited as the idea of using graph convolution to replace self-attention is not entirely new.
- The paper lacks a thorough discussion on related works, particularly in the area of graph convolutional networks (GCNs), which could provide a better context for the proposed method.
- The paper does not provide a detailed analysis of the computational complexity of the proposed method, which could affect its scalability in large-scale applications.
- The experimental results are not compared with the latest state-of-the-art methods, which could limit the evaluation of the proposed method's effectiveness.
- The paper does not discuss the potential limitations or negative societal impacts of the proposed method, which could be crucial for understanding its broader implications.
