 **Summary:**
The paper introduces MapSelect, a novel sparse attention mechanism designed to enhance the interpretability of Graph Attention Networks (GATs) by controlling the sparsity of attention weights. MapSelect utilizes a differentiable sparse attention mechanism, SparseMAP, to manage the attention density both locally and globally. This mechanism allows for precise control over the number of active connections per node and the percentage of active edges in the full graph. The method is evaluated against five baseline methods, demonstrating improved interpretability and competitive performance on real-world datasets. Despite its innovative approach, concerns are raised about the clarity of the paper's presentation, the need for more comprehensive comparisons with existing methods, and the potential for over-smoothing in GATs.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, with clear explanations of the proposed method and its application to Graph Attention Networks (GATs).
- The introduction of a novel sparse attention mechanism, MapSelect, which is differentiable and allows for both local and global control of attention density, is a significant contribution.
- MapSelect shows improved interpretability compared to baseline methods and competitive performance on real-world datasets.
- The paper provides a comprehensive evaluation of the sparsity-accuracy and sparsity-interpretability trade-offs, which is crucial for understanding the impact of sparsity on model performance.
- The experimental results demonstrate that MapSelect can maintain high task accuracy while improving interpretability, which is a notable achievement.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed comparison with existing methods like KEdge and PTD-Net, which also focus on interpretable GNNs, which could have provided a more robust validation of the proposed method.
- The presentation of the paper could be improved, particularly in the experimental section, which is currently unclear and could benefit from more detailed explanations.
- There is a need for a more thorough discussion on the potential issues of over-smoothing in GATs, which could affect the model's performance and interpretability.
- The paper's experimental setup and results are not sufficiently detailed, making it difficult to assess the robustness and effectiveness of the proposed method.
- The paper could benefit from a more comprehensive discussion on the limitations and potential negative societal impacts of the proposed method.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors provide a detailed comparison with KEdge and PTD-Net to highlight the unique advantages of MapSelect?
- How does MapSelect compare to other methods that also focus on interpretable GNNs, such as GraphMask and SEGNN?
- Can the authors clarify the experimental setup and results, particularly in terms of the sparsity levels used and the impact of these on model performance and interpretability?
- How does the proposed method address the potential issues of over-smoothing in GATs, and what measures have been taken to mitigate these effects?
- Could the authors provide more detailed explanations and examples of the explanations generated by the model to better understand the interpretability of the model's outputs?
