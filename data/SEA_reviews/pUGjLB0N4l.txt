 **Summary:**
The paper introduces BigLearn-VAE, a novel variant of the Variational Auto-Encoder (VAE) that leverages the big learning principle to enhance the modeling of conditional and marginal distributions. It proposes new ELBOs and models to handle these distributions, which are demonstrated to improve the joint ELBO and perform well in incomplete data settings. The model's architecture includes a transformer-based model for handling diverse inference arms, and the paper provides empirical evidence of its effectiveness through experiments on datasets like MNIST and FashionMNIST. However, the paper's clarity and the experimental setup's fairness are questioned, with concerns about the scalability and generalization of the results.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel approach to modeling marginal and conditional distributions using a big learning perspective, which is a significant advancement in the field.
- The authors provide a clear and detailed explanation of the BigLearn-VAE model, its architecture, and its training objectives, which are crucial for understanding the model's functionality and effectiveness.
- The empirical results demonstrate that the BigLearn-VAE model outperforms the vanilla VAE model in terms of ELBO, showing a notable improvement in performance.
- The paper is well-written, with clear and detailed explanations that make it accessible to readers not familiar with the big learning perspective.
- The experiments conducted show that the BigLearn-VAE model can handle incomplete data and can be used as a feature extractor for downstream tasks, highlighting its versatility and applicability.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed comparison with other existing VAE variants, which could have provided a better understanding of the model's advantages and limitations.
- The scalability of the model to larger datasets and more complex tasks is not adequately addressed, raising concerns about its practical applicability in real-world scenarios.
- The experimental setup and results are not sufficiently detailed or fair, making it difficult to assess the model's performance and reliability.
- The paper's claims about the model's capabilities and the benefits of the big learning principle are not convincingly supported by the experimental results, which could limit the credibility of the proposed model.
- There are several typographical and notation errors throughout the paper, which could detract from its professionalism and clarity.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors clarify how the BigLearn-VAE model handles the inference of conditional distributions, particularly in terms of the computational efficiency and accuracy of the results?
- How does the BigLearn-VAE model compare to other existing VAE variants in terms of performance and efficiency?
- Could the authors provide more detailed experimental results, including the training and testing loss curves, to better understand the model's behavior during training and its generalization to new data?
- How does the model perform when trained on larger, more complex datasets, and what are the implications of this for its practical applicability?
- Can the authors address the typographical and notation errors in the paper to improve its clarity and professionalism?
