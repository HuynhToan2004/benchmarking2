 **Summary:**
The paper introduces ConsisGAD, a novel model for node-level graph anomaly detection under limited supervision. ConsisGAD employs consistency training and learnable data augmentation to address challenges such as limited supervision and class imbalance. The model leverages a homophily-aware GNN backbone to differentiate between normal and anomalous nodes, utilizing a novel learnable data augmentation mechanism to introduce controlled noise into the dataset. The model's effectiveness is demonstrated through extensive experiments on benchmark datasets, showing superior performance compared to state-of-the-art baselines.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-organized and easy to follow, making it accessible to readers.
- The proposed method is technically sound and innovative, particularly in the use of learnable data augmentation and consistency training.
- The experimental results are robust and demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed method across multiple benchmark datasets.
- The paper includes a detailed analysis of the proposed method, which provides insights into its design and performance.
- The code is provided, which facilitates reproducibility and further research.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed discussion on the limitations and potential negative societal impacts of the proposed method.
- The motivation behind the use of learnable data augmentation and consistency training is not clearly explained, which could confuse readers.
- The novelty of the proposed method is questionable, as it appears to be a combination of existing techniques without significant innovation.
- The paper could benefit from more comprehensive comparisons with other graph augmentation methods and a broader range of baseline models.
- The paper could provide more details on the computational complexity and memory usage of the proposed method, which are critical for practical applications.
- The presentation of the paper could be improved by addressing minor typographical and formatting errors.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors provide a more detailed explanation of the motivation behind the use of learnable data augmentation and consistency training?
- How does the proposed method compare to other graph augmentation methods, particularly in terms of performance and computational efficiency?
- What are the specific advantages of the proposed method over other state-of-the-art models, especially in terms of computational complexity and memory usage?
- How does the proposed method perform under different levels of supervision, and what are the implications for its practical applicability?
- Can the authors provide more details on the limitations of the proposed method and any potential negative societal impacts?
