 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel approach to enhancing text-to-layout models by integrating human feedback in the form of revision sequences. It proposes a reward model trained on a dataset of revisions made by professional designers to a layout model, which is then used to optimize the model via reinforcement learning from human feedback (RLHF). The method aims to improve the model's alignment with human preferences, as demonstrated through experiments showing that the proposed RARE method outperforms baseline methods. However, the paper has been critiqued for its lack of clarity in defining the task, insufficient evaluation, and potential issues with the experimental setup and reward model's effectiveness.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel method of leveraging human feedback to improve text-to-layout models, specifically through the use of revision sequences, which is a new and innovative approach.
- The authors have collected a high-quality dataset of revisions made by professional designers, which is a significant contribution to the field.
- The paper is well-written and easy to follow, with clear explanations of the proposed method and its implementation.
- The experiments conducted demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed method, showing that it outperforms baseline methods in terms of both qualitative and quantitative metrics.
- The paper addresses an important issue in the field of generative design, specifically focusing on the task of layout generation, which is crucial for improving the alignment of AI-generated designs with human preferences.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks clarity in defining the task and the methodology, particularly in how the task of layout generation is defined and how the model's outputs are evaluated.
- The experimental setup and evaluation are insufficient, with limited baseline comparisons and a lack of detailed analysis of the reward model's effectiveness.
- The paper does not adequately address the scalability and generalizability of the proposed method, especially in terms of its applicability to other domains or models.
- There are concerns about the reliability of the reward model, which is based on human revisions, and the potential for overfitting or bias in the model's training data.
- The paper could benefit from more detailed explanations and justifications for certain methodological choices, such as the use of the Chamfer distance and the choice of reward functions.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors clarify the definition of the task and the methodology, specifically how the task of layout generation is defined and how the model's outputs are evaluated?
- How does the proposed method compare to other state-of-the-art methods in terms of performance and efficiency?
- Can the authors provide more detailed analysis on the effectiveness of the reward model, particularly in terms of its ability to predict the amount of effort needed to improve a layout?
- How does the proposed method handle the scalability and generalizability issues, especially in terms of its applicability to other domains or models?
- Could the authors address the concerns regarding the reliability of the reward model and the potential for overfitting or bias in the training data?
