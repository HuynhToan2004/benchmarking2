 **Summary:**
The paper explores the equilibrium states of weight vectors in neural networks under different optimizers, focusing on the rotational equilibrium achieved through weight decay. It introduces a novel rotational variant of AdamW, which aims to balance the rotation of weight vectors and eliminates the chaotic transient phase observed in traditional optimizers. The paper provides theoretical insights into the equilibrium states and empirical validations using various datasets and models, including ResNets and Transformers. The authors propose that this approach could potentially enhance the performance of deep learning models by balancing the learning dynamics across different layers and neurons.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written and the presentation is clear, making it easy to follow and understand.
- The authors have conducted a thorough investigation into the rotational equilibrium in neural networks, which is a novel approach to understanding the dynamics of weight decay.
- The theoretical analysis and empirical results are well-aligned, providing a robust validation of the proposed methods.
- The paper introduces a novel rotational variant of AdamW, which is a significant contribution to the field of optimization in deep learning.
- The paper provides a clear motivation for the proposed method and presents a comprehensive analysis of the equilibrium states, which is both insightful and innovative.
- The experimental results show that the proposed method can improve the performance of deep learning models, especially in scenarios where the equilibrium dynamics are not balanced.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a comprehensive comparison with other optimizers, particularly those that have been shown to perform well in similar scenarios, such as NEROS and LARS.
- The experimental results are not robust enough, with some results showing marginal improvements or even degradation in performance compared to existing methods.
- The paper does not adequately address the practical implications of the proposed method, particularly how it would perform in real-world scenarios where the equilibrium dynamics are not ideal.
- The theoretical analysis is based on simplified assumptions that may not hold in practical settings, such as the assumption of scale-invariant weights which may not always be the case.
- The paper does not provide a clear explanation of how the proposed method could be applied in more complex scenarios, such as large-scale networks or different types of normalization layers.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors provide a more detailed comparison of their method with other optimizers like NEROS and LARS, especially in terms of the equilibrium dynamics and performance?
- How does the proposed method perform in scenarios where the equilibrium dynamics are not balanced, such as in networks with non-scale-invariant weights?
- Can the authors provide a more detailed analysis of the equilibrium dynamics in more complex scenarios, such as in large-scale networks or different types of normalization layers?
- How does the proposed method handle the practical implications of weight decay, especially in scenarios where the decay rate is not optimal?
- Can the authors provide a more detailed explanation of the theoretical assumptions made in the analysis, particularly in relation to the scale-invariance of weights?
- How does the proposed method compare in terms of computational efficiency and practical applicability with other optimization techniques, such as those based on relative optimizers?
