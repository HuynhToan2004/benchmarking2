 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel framework, LatentDiffuser, for offline reinforcement learning (RL) that leverages latent, score-based diffusion models to represent a continuous latent action space. This approach enables efficient planning in latent action space, which is shown to be equivalent to energy-guided sampling with a pretrained diffusion model. The framework is evaluated on various tasks, including locomotion control and AntMaze, demonstrating competitive performance against existing methods. The methodology involves a unified training process for the latent action space, which is then used for planning in the latent space. Despite its strengths, the paper is criticized for its complexity, high computational cost, and limited novelty in its training methodology.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel framework for offline RL that leverages latent, score-based diffusion models to represent a continuous latent action space, which is a significant advancement over traditional methods.
- Theoretical analysis is provided to show the equivalence between planning in the latent action space and energy-guided sampling with a pretrained diffusion model, which is a non-trivial contribution.
- The proposed method is evaluated on various tasks, including low-dimensional locomotion control and high-dimensional tasks like Adroit and AntMaze, demonstrating competitive performance against existing methods.
- The paper is well-written, with clear explanations and a logical flow that makes it easy to follow.
- The use of latent action space in offline RL is innovative and addresses the challenge of planning in raw action space, which is a significant improvement over existing methods.
- The experimental results show that the proposed method outperforms existing methods in high-dimensional tasks, demonstrating the effectiveness of the approach.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper is complex and difficult to follow, especially due to the multiple neural networks involved and the complexity of the training process.
- The training process is described as inefficient and ineffective, with a lack of detailed analysis on how to train the diffusion model effectively.
- The paper lacks a clear comparison with other offline RL methods, particularly those that do not rely on hierarchical approaches.
- The computational cost of the proposed method is high, which could limit its practical application in real-world scenarios.
- The paper could benefit from a more comprehensive comparison with existing methods, particularly in terms of the computational efficiency and practical applicability of the proposed method.
- The novelty of the training methodology is limited, as it primarily adapts existing methods such as TAP and QGPO, which might not be sufficiently distinct to warrant the claim of a significant advancement.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors clarify the computational cost and efficiency of the proposed method, particularly in comparison to other existing methods?
- How does the proposed method compare in terms of practical applicability and real-world deployment?
- Could the authors provide a more detailed analysis of the training process for the diffusion model, including any strategies or techniques that could improve its effectiveness?
- How does the proposed method handle the issue of planning in raw action space, which is a significant challenge in offline RL?
- Could the authors elaborate on the differences between the proposed method and existing methods, particularly in terms of the novelty and effectiveness of the training process?
