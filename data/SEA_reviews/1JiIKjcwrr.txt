 **Summary:**
The paper introduces BFTNet, a novel self-supervised learning framework for heterogeneous information networks (HINs), which separates graph topology and node features to address the challenges of noisy and heterogeneous data. BFTNet employs a knowledge graph embedding module to learn graph topology and a contrastive learning module to learn node features, aiming to improve performance and robustness. The framework is evaluated on four benchmark datasets and demonstrated to outperform existing methods in object classification and clustering tasks. The paper also discusses the importance of balancing graph topology and node features, which is crucial for handling skewed datasets in real-world scenarios.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel SSL framework, BFTNet, which separates graph topology and node features to address the challenges of noisy and heterogeneous data.
- The framework is evaluated on four benchmark datasets and demonstrated to outperform existing methods in object classification and clustering tasks.
- The proposed methodology is clear and easy to follow, with a well-structured presentation that aids in understanding the complex concepts.
- The paper addresses an important and timely problem in the field of heterogeneous information networks, focusing on the balance between node features and graph topology.
- The experimental results show that the proposed method outperforms existing methods, demonstrating the effectiveness of the proposed approach.

**Weaknesses:**
