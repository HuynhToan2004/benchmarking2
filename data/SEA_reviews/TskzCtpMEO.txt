 **Summary:**
The paper introduces Sparse Subspace Variational Inference (SSVI), a novel method for training sparse Bayesian Neural Networks (BNNs) by optimizing the sparse subspace and variational parameters alternately. SSVI uses a removal-and-addition strategy based on weight distribution statistics to manage the sparsity throughout the training and inference phases. The method is evaluated on CIFAR-10 and CIFAR-100 datasets, demonstrating significant improvements in model size, training costs, and uncertainty quantification, while maintaining competitive accuracy levels. Despite its strengths, the paper has been critiqued for its limited evaluation on only two datasets, and the absence of comparisons with other sparse BNN methods.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, with a clear and organized structure, and the presentation is easy to follow.
- The proposed method, SSVI, is novel and effective, achieving a 10-20x compression in model size with under 3% performance drop, and up to 20x FLOPs reduction during training compared with dense VI training.
- The method is well-motivated, with a clear rationale for its design and a logical approach to managing sparsity in BNNs.
- The paper includes a thorough ablation study that helps in understanding the design choices and the impact of different components of the method.
- The experimental results are strong, demonstrating the effectiveness of the proposed method in terms of model size, training costs, and uncertainty quantification.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed comparison with other sparse BNN methods, which could provide a better understanding of the method's advantages and limitations.
- The evaluation is limited to two datasets, which may not fully demonstrate the generalizability of the method across different domains or network architectures.
- The paper could benefit from a more comprehensive discussion on the limitations of the method and potential areas for future research.
- There are some minor issues with the presentation, such as inconsistencies in the notation and some typographical errors.
- The paper does not adequately address the issue of the method's reliance on the choice of the sparsity level, which could be seen as a limitation.

**Questions:**
- How does the method compare in terms of performance and efficiency with other sparse BNN methods, particularly those that use different sparsity-promoting priors?
- Can the method be adapted to other network architectures beyond the ResNet-18 used in the experiments?
- How does the method perform on larger and more complex datasets, such as ImageNet?
- Is there a way to automate the selection of the sparsity level, or is it necessary to manually tune this parameter?
- Could the authors provide more details on the initialization of the variational parameters and the impact of this initialization on the method's performance?
- How does the method handle the uncertainty quantification in the context of sparse BNNs, and what are the implications of this for practical applications?
