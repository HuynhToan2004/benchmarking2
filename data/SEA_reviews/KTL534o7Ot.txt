 **Summary:**
The paper introduces ProgSyn, a novel framework for generating synthetic tabular data that can be customized with programmable specifications such as fairness, privacy, and logical constraints. It uses a differentiable loss derived from these specifications to fine-tune a pre-trained generative model, ensuring the generated data adheres to the desired properties. The framework is evaluated across multiple datasets and shown to outperform existing methods in various tasks, including fairness and privacy. The approach is flexible and can handle a wide range of constraints, making it a valuable tool for data privacy and fairness in machine learning applications.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel approach for generating synthetic tabular data with programmable specifications, which is a significant advancement in the field.
- The methodology is well-explained, with clear presentation of the framework and the training procedure, making it easy to understand.
- The paper includes a comprehensive evaluation across multiple datasets, demonstrating the effectiveness of the proposed method in various scenarios.
- The flexibility of the framework in handling a wide range of constraints and customizations is highlighted, which is crucial for applications where data privacy and fairness are critical.
- The use of differentiable loss derived from programmable specifications to fine-tune the generative model is a novel and effective approach, ensuring that the generated data adheres to the desired properties.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed discussion on the limitations of the proposed method, which is essential for understanding its applicability and scope.
- The experimental results are not robustly compared with state-of-the-art methods, especially in terms of statistical significance and the inclusion of confidence intervals.
- The evaluation primarily focuses on accuracy and fairness, with limited exploration of other properties such as privacy and utility.
- The paper does not sufficiently discuss the scalability of the method to large datasets or the complexity of generating complex logical constraints.
- The paper's reliance on a pre-trained generative model raises concerns about the novelty of the approach and the potential for overfitting, especially in different domains.
- The paper could benefit from a more detailed discussion on the choice of hyperparameters and their impact on the performance of the model.

**Questions:**

- How does the proposed method compare with state-of-the-art methods in terms of statistical significance and the inclusion of confidence intervals?
- What are the specific use cases where the generated data is likely to be useful, and how does the method perform in these scenarios?
- How does the method handle the scalability issues, especially when dealing with large datasets?
- Can you discuss the potential for overfitting when using a pre-trained generative model across different domains?
- How do you ensure the fairness and privacy of the generated data, especially when dealing with complex logical constraints?
- Could you provide more details on the choice of hyperparameters and their impact on the performance of the model?
