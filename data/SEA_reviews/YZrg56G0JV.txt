 **Summary:**
The paper explores the theoretical aspects of sample efficiency in multitask reinforcement learning (MTRL) through a novel approach that leverages a diverse set of tasks to enhance exploration. It introduces a new algorithm that employs a mixture of policies, where each policy corresponds to a task, to facilitate efficient exploration. Theoretical analysis is provided to demonstrate the sample efficiency of this approach, and empirical evidence is presented in the form of synthetic experiments. The paper also discusses the implications of its findings for deep reinforcement learning, suggesting that the diversity of tasks can influence the efficiency of exploration. However, the paper lacks comprehensive empirical evaluations and a detailed discussion on the practical implications of the proposed method.

**Strengths:**
- The paper provides a comprehensive theoretical analysis of the sample complexity of multitask reinforcement learning (MTRL), which is a significant contribution to the field.
- The authors have introduced a novel algorithm for MTRL that utilizes a diverse set of tasks to enhance exploration, which is a novel approach in the context of MTRL.
- The paper is well-structured and easy to follow, making it accessible to readers.
- The theoretical results are supported by empirical evidence, which adds credibility to the claims made.
- The paper addresses an important problem in MTRL, which has been overlooked in previous studies.
- The results of the paper are novel and potentially impactful, offering a new perspective on the exploration benefits of MTRL.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed discussion on the practical implications of the proposed algorithm, particularly in terms of its applicability and effectiveness in real-world scenarios.
- The empirical evaluations presented in the paper are limited and do not adequately support the claims made about the algorithm's performance.
- There is a lack of comparison with existing algorithms, which makes it difficult to assess the relative performance of the proposed method.
- The paper does not sufficiently discuss the assumptions made in the theoretical analysis, which could limit the generalizability of the findings.
- The paper does not adequately address the limitations of its method, which could impact the practical applicability of the proposed approach.
- The paper could benefit from a more detailed discussion on the practical implications of the theoretical results, particularly in terms of how they can be applied in real-world scenarios.
