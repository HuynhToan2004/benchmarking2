 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel short-to-long video diffusion model named SEINE, designed to generate transition videos between different scenes using textual descriptions. The model leverages a random-mask video diffusion process to ensure temporal consistency, semantic similarity, and video-text alignment. The methodology involves the use of a text-to-video base model conditioned on a random mask, which is trained on the WebVid10M dataset. The paper evaluates the model against existing methods, demonstrating its superiority in generating transitions and long videos. However, concerns were raised regarding the novelty of the approach, the limited comparison with existing methods, and the lack of detailed experimental results.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel task of short-to-long video diffusion, which is a significant advancement in the field of video generation.
- The proposed method is simple yet effective, utilizing a random mask to guide the diffusion process, which is a novel approach.
- The paper is well-written, easy to follow, and provides extensive experiments to demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed method.
- The method is capable of generating smooth transitions between different scenes, enhancing the continuity and coherence of video content.
- The paper includes a variety of visual results and comparisons with existing methods, showcasing the superiority of the proposed method.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed description of the training process and the specifics of the model architecture, making it difficult to replicate or understand the method fully.
- The comparison with existing methods is limited, and the paper does not adequately discuss how the proposed method differs or improves upon these existing approaches.
- The novelty of the approach is questionable, as the use of a random mask to guide diffusion is a common technique in image-to-video models.
- The paper does not provide sufficient quantitative results or detailed metrics to support the claims of superiority over existing methods.
- There is a lack of discussion on the limitations of the proposed method and its potential societal impacts.
- The paper does not adequately address the issue of blurry and low-quality video generation, which is a significant concern.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors provide more details on the training process, including the specifics of the model architecture and the training data?
- How does the proposed method differ from existing approaches in terms of effectiveness and novelty?
- Can the authors provide more quantitative results, such as FVD scores, to support the claims of superiority over existing methods?
- How does the proposed method handle the issue of blurry and low-quality video generation?
- Could the authors clarify the role and effectiveness of the random mask in the diffusion process?
- How does the proposed method compare to other recent advancements in video generation, such as those mentioned in the review?
- Can the authors provide more detailed metrics and comparisons to better establish the superiority of their method?
