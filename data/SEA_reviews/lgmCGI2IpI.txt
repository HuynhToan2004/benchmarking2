 **Summary:**
The paper introduces an active learning strategy named Active Query by Optimal Transport (AQOT), which integrates both informativeness and representativeness to enhance query selection efficiency. AQOT leverages optimal transport (OT) to measure the difference between distributions of instances, enabling efficient querying based on sparse solutions of OT. The method is tested across various datasets and compared against several active learning baselines, showing improvements in performance and computational efficiency. However, concerns are raised about the novelty of the approach, as it appears to be a simple combination of existing methods, and the experimental validation is limited to small-scale datasets.

**Strengths:**
- The paper addresses an important problem in active learning by integrating both informativeness and representativeness, which is crucial for efficient query selection.
- The use of optimal transport (OT) to measure the difference between distributions of instances is innovative and provides a novel approach to query selection.
- The proposed AQOT method is well-explained, easy to follow, and has been tested across a variety of datasets, demonstrating its effectiveness.
- The paper is well-written, making it accessible and understandable, which is essential for a broad audience.
- The method's performance is evaluated on several datasets, including some challenging ones, which shows its robustness and applicability.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a clear motivation for using optimal transport (OT) specifically and does not sufficiently differentiate its approach from existing methods, particularly in terms of novelty and computational efficiency.
- The experimental validation is limited to small-scale datasets, which might not adequately demonstrate the method's effectiveness in real-world scenarios with large datasets.
- The paper could benefit from a more comprehensive comparison with state-of-the-art active learning methods to better establish its position in the field.
- There is a lack of discussion on the sensitivity of the results to the choice of hyperparameters, which is crucial for practical applications.
- The paper does not adequately address the computational complexity of the proposed method, which could be a significant concern for large-scale applications.
- The paper's writing style needs improvement, particularly in terms of clarity and grammar, which could hinder understanding and engagement.

**Questions:**
- Could you clarify the choice of using optimal transport (OT) over other methods for measuring the difference between distributions of instances? What specific advantages does OT offer in this context?
- How does the proposed AQOT method compare in terms of computational efficiency with other active learning methods, especially in scenarios with large datasets?
- Can you provide more details on the sensitivity of the results to the choice of hyperparameters, such as the regularization parameter λ and the weight parameter α?
- How does the proposed method perform when applied to more complex and larger datasets, such as those commonly used in machine learning competitions?
- Could you discuss the limitations of the proposed method and suggest potential directions for future research?
- How does the proposed method handle different types of noise in the data, and what are the implications of this for the effectiveness of the active learning process?
- The paper mentions using entropy regularization to solve the OT problem. Could you elaborate on this choice and its impact on the computational complexity and performance of the method?
