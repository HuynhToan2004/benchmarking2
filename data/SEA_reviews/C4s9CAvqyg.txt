 **Summary:**
The paper introduces MPformer, a novel approach for graph transformer models aimed at addressing the limitations of existing models in handling heterophilous relationships among nodes. MPformer incorporates a Tree2Token module for aggregating node and neighbor information across various hop distances, represented as token sequences, and a HeterPos module for positional encoding that considers the shortest path distance and feature differences between nodes. The model is validated through extensive experiments on various datasets, demonstrating superior performance compared to existing models. Despite its innovative approach, the paper faces criticism for its lack of clarity in methodology and presentation, particularly in the description of the Tree2Token module and the theoretical underpinnings of the HeterPos module.

**Strengths:**
- The paper addresses the significant issue of heterophilous relationships in graph modeling, which is a critical and under-explored area in graph learning.
- The proposed method, MPformer, is innovative and well-motivated, offering a new perspective on how to handle heterophilous relationships in graph transformer models.
- The paper is well-written, making it accessible and easy to understand, which is crucial for advancing research in the field.
- Extensive experiments are conducted to validate the effectiveness of the proposed method, showing superior performance compared to existing models.
- The introduction of the Tree2Token module for aggregating node and neighbor information at various hop distances is a novel approach that could potentially enhance the modeling capabilities of graph transformer models.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks clarity in the methodology, particularly in the description of the Tree2Token module and the HeterPos module. More detailed explanations and visual aids are needed to enhance understanding.
- The theoretical analysis provided is insufficient, particularly regarding the effectiveness of the HeterPos module and its contribution to the overall performance of the model.
- The paper does not adequately address the scalability of the proposed method, especially in large-scale datasets, which could limit its practical applicability.
- The experimental results are not convincingly superior to existing models, with only marginal improvements in some cases.
- The paper does not sufficiently discuss the limitations of the proposed method, which is crucial for understanding its applicability and potential drawbacks.
- There are several instances of incorrect or inappropriate mathematical notation and definitions, which could mislead readers or confuse the understanding of the proposed method.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors provide a more detailed explanation of the Tree2Token module, including how it aggregates node and neighbor information at various hop distances?
- How does the HeterPos module effectively capture the heterophilous relationships in graph modeling, and what are the specific contributions of this module to the overall performance of the model?
- Can the authors clarify the theoretical underpinnings of the HeterPos module and how it facilitates a more favorable trade-off between stability and empirical risk minimization?
- How does the proposed method perform on large-scale datasets, and what are the potential scalability issues that need to be addressed?
- Could the authors discuss the limitations of the proposed method and suggest areas for future research to address these limitations?
- In light of the concerns about the clarity and mathematical correctness of the paper, could the authors revise the paper to address these issues and ensure the accuracy and clarity of the mathematical notation and definitions?
