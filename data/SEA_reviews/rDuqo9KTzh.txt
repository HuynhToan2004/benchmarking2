 **Summary:**
The paper introduces Uncertainty-Aware Prompted Meta-Learning (UAPML), a novel method that leverages task-specific prompts to extract meta-knowledge for few-shot tasks, thereby reducing computational costs. This approach contrasts traditional methods that adapt all meta-knowledge, which can be computationally expensive. UAPML employs a Bayesian meta-prompt to initialize task-specific prompts and uses theoretical analysis to demonstrate the alignment between the posterior uncertainty of the Bayesian meta-prompt and the task-specific prompt. The method achieves comparable or better performance with significantly reduced computational costs, as demonstrated in extensive experiments. However, the paper's novelty is questioned due to its reliance on existing methods like prompt tuning and Bayesian meta-learning, and the method's effectiveness in more complex scenarios remains unclear.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written and easy to follow, with clear and logical presentation of the methodology and results.
- The proposed method is novel and effective, demonstrating a significant reduction in computational cost and comparable or better performance compared to existing methods.
- The paper provides a comprehensive theoretical analysis of the proposed method, including an ablation study and extensive experiments to validate the effectiveness of the proposed method.
- The use of Bayesian meta-prompt to initialize task-specific prompts is a novel approach that aligns with the uncertainty of task-specific prompts, which is a significant contribution to the field.
- The paper demonstrates the efficiency of the meta-knowledge extraction paradigm through extensive experiments, showcasing the method's effectiveness and efficiency.

**Weaknesses:**
- The novelty of the paper is limited as it primarily combines existing techniques like prompt tuning and Bayesian meta-learning, which have been previously explored.
- The method's effectiveness in more complex scenarios, such as more complex datasets or more challenging tasks, is not thoroughly explored.
- The paper lacks a detailed discussion on the limitations and potential negative societal impacts of the proposed method.
- There is a lack of comparison with other state-of-the-art meta-learning methods that also use prompt tuning, which could provide a more comprehensive evaluation of the proposed method.
- The paper does not provide a detailed analysis of the computational efficiency of the proposed method, which is crucial for understanding its practical applicability.
- The paper could benefit from a more thorough discussion on the limitations and potential negative societal impacts of the proposed method.

**Questions:**
- Can the proposed method be adapted to other types of meta-learning tasks, such as regression or reinforcement learning?
- How does the proposed method perform in more complex scenarios, such as more complex datasets or more challenging tasks?
- Could the authors provide a detailed analysis of the computational efficiency of the proposed method, including a comparison with other state-of-the-art meta-learning methods?
- How does the proposed method compare with other meta-learning methods that also use prompt tuning?
- Could the authors discuss the potential negative societal impacts of the proposed method and provide strategies for mitigating these impacts?
