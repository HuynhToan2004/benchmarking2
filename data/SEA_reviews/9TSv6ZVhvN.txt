 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel Federated Learning (FL) algorithm that integrates local training, client sampling, and communication compression. It proposes two algorithms, 5GCS-CC and 5GCS-AB, which utilize these techniques to achieve convergence guarantees under standard assumptions. The authors aim to address the challenges of heterogeneity in client gradients by leveraging importance sampling to ensure unbiasedness. Theoretical analysis and numerical experiments are provided to support the claims, demonstrating the method's effectiveness in handling client sampling and communication compression. However, the paper is criticized for its presentation clarity, lack of novelty, and insufficient experimental validation.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-organized, making it easy to follow, and the problem addressed is of significant importance in federated learning (FL).
- The theoretical analysis is comprehensive, covering various sampling techniques, and the paper provides a clear and concise explanation of the methodology.
- The proposed method achieves state-of-the-art convergence guarantees in the considered setting, and the paper includes both theoretical and numerical results, which are convincing.
- The method is simple to implement and has been experimentally validated, showing good performance in terms of convergence speed.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks novelty as it primarily combines existing techniques without introducing significant new insights or methodologies.
- The presentation of the paper is not clear, with numerous typos and unclear explanations, which makes it difficult to understand the main contributions and the implications of the theoretical results.
- The experimental validation is insufficient, and the paper does not adequately compare its results with existing methods, such as 5GCS, which could undermine the perceived effectiveness of the proposed method.
- The paper does not discuss the limitations of the proposed method, which could provide valuable insights into its applicability and effectiveness in different scenarios.
- The paper could benefit from a more thorough discussion of related work and a clearer presentation of the main results and their implications.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors clarify the role and impact of the parameters $k$ and $C$ in the proposed method?
- How does the proposed method compare to other existing methods, such as 5GCS, in terms of convergence rate and practical performance?
- In the context of the theoretical results, what are the implications of the assumptions made, and how do they affect the practical applicability of the method?
- Could the authors provide more details on the experimental setup and results, including the specific conditions under which the experiments were conducted and the metrics used to evaluate performance?
- How does the paper address the challenges of client heterogeneity, and what are the potential limitations or drawbacks of the proposed method in this context?
