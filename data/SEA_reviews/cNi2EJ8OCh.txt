 **Summary:**
The paper addresses the classification of functional data under local differential privacy (LDP), employing model reversal and model averaging techniques to enhance classifier performance. The authors propose a framework for functional data classification in both single-server and multi-server settings, utilizing model reversal and model averaging to improve classification accuracy. The paper also introduces a novel approach to functional data classification by integrating LDP into the model selection process, which is crucial for maintaining privacy in data-intensive applications. The theoretical analysis and experimental results are provided to support the proposed methods, although the paper's presentation and clarity need improvement.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is the first to address functional data classification under LDP, which is an innovative approach to privacy-preserving functional data analysis.
- The proposed method of model reversal and model averaging is novel and effective in enhancing the performance of weak classifiers, contributing to the advancement of privacy-preserving methodologies in functional data analysis.
- The paper provides a comprehensive theoretical analysis and experimental results to support the proposed methods, which are well-motivated and backed by a thorough literature review.
- The paper addresses a significant and relevant problem in the field of functional data analysis, focusing on privacy-preserving methodologies which are crucial in data-intensive applications.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper suffers from poor writing, with unclear definitions and notation, which makes it difficult to follow.
- The motivation behind the problem setting is not clearly articulated, which could limit the paper's impact and relevance.
- The experimental results are not convincing, particularly in the real application section, which lacks sufficient detail and comparison with other methods.
- The paper could benefit from a clearer explanation of the model selection process and its impact on privacy, especially in terms of the privacy budget.
- The paper lacks a detailed discussion on the computational complexity of the proposed methods and the scalability of the algorithms, which are crucial for practical implementation.
- The paper does not provide a detailed comparison with other methods in the literature, which could help in understanding the relative performance of the proposed methods.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors clarify the definitions and notation used in the paper, particularly in the context of functional data and LDP?
- How does the proposed model selection process impact the privacy budget, and what measures are in place to ensure that privacy is not compromised?
- Can the authors provide more details on the experimental setup, particularly in the real application section, to better understand the results and their implications?
- How does the proposed method compare with other existing methods in terms of computational complexity and scalability?
- Could the authors elaborate on the theoretical underpinnings of the model reversal and model averaging techniques, and how they contribute to the overall performance of the classifiers?
