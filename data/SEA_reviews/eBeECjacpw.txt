 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel method for refining camera poses in NeRF-based neural surface reconstruction, using a Pose Residual Field (PoRF) and an epipolar geometry loss. This method leverages MLP to learn pose residuals, which are then combined with initial poses to refine them. The authors demonstrate that their approach can significantly reduce rotation errors and improve reconstruction accuracy, as evidenced by experiments on the DTU and MobileBrick datasets. The method is claimed to be more robust and efficient than previous methods, although concerns are raised about the clarity of the motivation, the experimental setup, and the novelty of the approach.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, making it easy to follow and understand the proposed method.
- The method is straightforward and effective, as demonstrated by its ability to reduce rotation errors and improve reconstruction accuracy.
- The use of an MLP to learn pose residuals is a novel approach that leverages global information across the entire sequence, enhancing the robustness of the method.
- The proposed epipolar geometry loss is robust and effective in supervising the optimization process.
- The paper provides a comprehensive evaluation of the method, comparing it with multiple state-of-the-art methods and demonstrating its superiority.
- The method's robustness to initial pose noise is highlighted, showing its ability to handle noisy initial poses effectively.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed comparison with other related works, particularly in the area of NeRF-based neural surface reconstruction.
- The motivation behind the use of an MLP to learn pose residuals is not clearly articulated, and the paper does not provide sufficient analysis or intuition on how this method contributes to the overall effectiveness of the approach.
- The experimental setup and results are not convincingly presented, with some results (e.g., Table 2) being confusing or not well-explained.
- The paper does not adequately address the computational complexity or efficiency of the proposed method, especially in comparison to other methods.
- There is a lack of discussion on the limitations and potential negative societal impacts of the proposed method.
- The paper does not include a comparison with the most recent and relevant methods, such as Neuralangelo, which might affect the perceived novelty and relevance of the work.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors clarify the motivation behind using an MLP to learn pose residuals and how this contributes to the overall effectiveness of the method?
- How does the proposed method compare in terms of computational efficiency and complexity with other state-of-the-art methods?
- Could the authors provide more detailed results and comparisons, especially in scenarios where the method does not perform as well (e.g., in scenes where the initial pose is already good)?
- How does the method handle different types of initial poses, such as those from COLMAP versus ARKit, and what are the implications of these differences?
- What is the role of the MLP in the optimization process, and how does it contribute to the refinement of the initial pose?
- Can the authors provide a more detailed comparison with the most recent and relevant methods, such as Neuralangelo, to clarify the novelty and relevance of their work?
