 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel method called Twin TD-regularized Actor-Critic (TDR) to address estimation biases in deep reinforcement learning (DRL). TDR utilizes a double critic network, where the critic with the smaller TD error is chosen to update the actor, and a TD regularization term is added to the actor's update rule. Theoretical analysis and empirical results demonstrate that TDR mitigates both overestimation and underestimation biases, enhancing the performance of existing algorithms like SAC, TD3, and D4PG. The method is validated through experiments on the DeepMind Control Suite, showing improved performance in challenging environments.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written and easy to follow, with clear explanations of the proposed method and its theoretical underpinnings.
- The empirical results are robust, showing that the proposed method, TDR, outperforms existing baselines in various environments.
- The paper provides a comprehensive ablation study, demonstrating the effectiveness of each component of TDR.
- Theoretical analysis is provided to support the proposed method, enhancing the paper's credibility.
- The experimental results are extensive, covering a wide variety of control tasks, and the paper is well-motivated, addressing the significant problem of estimation bias in deep RL.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed discussion on the limitations of the proposed method, which is crucial for understanding its applicability and scope.
- The theoretical analysis, while present, is somewhat limited in depth and scope, particularly in terms of its applicability to different types of reward structures.
- The paper does not adequately compare the proposed method with other existing methods, such as the TD-regularized actor-critic method, which could have provided a more robust validation of the proposed approach.
- There are some concerns about the novelty of the approach, as similar methods have been previously explored, which could affect the perceived originality of the work.
- The experimental setup and results could be improved by including more complex environments and a broader range of reward structures to test the robustness of the proposed method.

**Questions:**
- How does the proposed method compare to other existing methods, particularly in terms of computational efficiency and performance?
- Can the authors provide a more detailed discussion on the limitations of the proposed method and its applicability in different scenarios?
- How does the regularization coefficient impact the performance of the proposed method, and can the authors provide more detailed insights into its selection and impact on performance?
- Could the authors clarify the relationship between the proposed method and existing methods, particularly in terms of the theoretical contributions and practical implications?
- How does the proposed method handle different types of reward structures, and can the authors provide examples or experimental results to support its effectiveness in such scenarios?
