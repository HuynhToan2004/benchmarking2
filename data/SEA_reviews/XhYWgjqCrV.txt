 **Summary:**
The paper introduces MogaNet, a novel convolutional neural network (CNN) architecture designed to address the representation bottleneck of modern CNNs by focusing on middle-order interactions. MogaNet incorporates a multi-order gated aggregation module, which is claimed to enhance the model's ability to capture complex visual concepts and achieve state-of-the-art performance on various vision tasks. The architecture is evaluated across multiple benchmarks, including ImageNet, COCO, ADE20K, and others, demonstrating significant improvements in accuracy and efficiency compared to existing models like ViTs and CNNs. However, the paper has been critiqued for its complex architecture, potential computational inefficiencies, and lack of clarity in certain sections.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel convolutional neural network (CNN) architecture, MogaNet, which addresses the representation bottleneck of modern CNNs by focusing on middle-order interactions.
- MogaNet achieves state-of-the-art performance on various vision tasks, including image classification, object detection, semantic segmentation, 2D and 3D human pose estimation, and video prediction.
- The paper is well-written, clearly presenting the motivation, methodology, and results, with detailed experimental setups and clear illustrations that aid in understanding the proposed methods.
- MogaNet is evaluated on a wide range of benchmarks, demonstrating its effectiveness across different tasks and showing significant improvements over existing models like ViTs and CNNs.
- The paper includes comprehensive ablation studies and visualizations, which help in understanding the contributions of different components of the model and the impact of the proposed modifications.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed analysis of the computational complexity of MogaNet, particularly in terms of FLOPs and memory usage, which is crucial for understanding its efficiency.
- There is a lack of clarity in some sections of the paper, particularly in the definitions and roles of different components such as FD(.), GAP, and Moga(), which could confuse readers.
- The paper does not adequately address the scalability of MogaNet, especially in terms of parameter count and computational cost, which could limit its applicability in real-world scenarios.
- The novelty of the proposed modules, such as the multi-order gated aggregation and channel aggregation, is questioned, as similar approaches have been previously explored in other models like Vision Transformers.
- The paper does not provide a detailed comparison of MogaNet with the latest models like ConvNeXt, which might be more relevant in terms of performance and computational efficiency.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors provide more detailed analysis on the computational complexity of MogaNet, including FLOPs, memory usage, and other relevant metrics?
- How does MogaNet perform in terms of scalability, especially in large-scale applications where computational resources are limited?
- Can the authors clarify the roles and definitions of different components such as FD(.), GAP, and Moga() in the context of MogaNet?
- How does MogaNet compare to the latest models like ConvNeXt in terms of performance and computational efficiency?
- What are the specific advantages of MogaNet over existing models in terms of its ability to capture middle-order interactions, and how does this translate into practical improvements in performance?
