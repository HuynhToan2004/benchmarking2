 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel neural network-based rate control system for learned video compression, aiming to optimize the bitrate allocation and achieve more efficient video compression. The system comprises a rate allocation network and a rate implementation network, which operate at the mini-GOP and frame levels, respectively. The rate allocation network determines the optimal bitrate for each frame based on spatiotemporal features, while the rate implementation network maps the target bitrate to the appropriate coding parameters. The method has been evaluated across various datasets and baseline models, demonstrating improvements in bitrate savings and rate control accuracy. However, the paper lacks a comprehensive comparison with existing rate control methods and does not include subjective visual quality evaluations.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel neural network-based rate control system for learned video compression, which is the first of its kind.
- The proposed method achieves accurate rate control with only 2% average bitrate error and nearly 10% bitrate savings compared to various baseline methods.
- The method is agnostic to the existing learning-based video compression method and only requires a small additional computational overhead on the encoding side.
- The paper is well-written, clear, and easy to follow, making it accessible to a broad audience.
- The proposed method is evaluated on four baseline models and four datasets, demonstrating its effectiveness and applicability across various scenarios.
- The paper includes an ablation study that shows the effectiveness of the proposed method in improving the bitrate savings and rate control accuracy.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a comprehensive comparison with existing rate control methods, which limits the understanding of its advantages over existing methods.
- The methodology section is not clearly presented, making it difficult to understand the specific contributions of the proposed method.
- The paper does not include subjective visual quality evaluations, which are crucial for assessing the practical effectiveness of the proposed method.
- The paper only evaluates the proposed method on HEVC, which might limit its applicability to other video compression standards.
- The paper does not provide a detailed comparison of the inference time and model complexity of the proposed method with existing methods, which could affect the practical deployment of the proposed method.
- The paper does not discuss the limitations of the proposed method, which could help in understanding its applicability and potential improvements.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors provide a more detailed comparison with existing rate control methods to highlight the advantages of the proposed method?
- How does the proposed method perform when integrated with other video compression standards, such as VVC?
- Could the authors include subjective visual quality evaluations to provide a more comprehensive assessment of the practical effectiveness of the proposed method?
- How does the proposed method compare in terms of inference time and model complexity with existing methods?
- What are the limitations of the proposed method, and how might these limitations affect its practical deployment?
- Could the authors provide more details on the methodology, particularly how the rate implementation network maps the target bitrate to the appropriate coding parameters?
