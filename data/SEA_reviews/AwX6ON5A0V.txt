 **Summary:**
The paper investigates the sample complexity of Gaussian Mixture Models (GMMs) under the uniform spherical Gaussian mixture, focusing on the relationship between the distribution of spacings among the means and the sample complexity. The authors derive an upper bound for the number of samples required to learn the parameters of a GMM with epsilon-precision, which is optimal in this setting. They also explore the impact of pair correlations of means on sample complexity, showing that the distribution of spacing between means influences the sample complexity. The paper is technically sound, providing new insights into the sample complexity of GMMs, but it lacks empirical validation and a broader discussion on the practical implications of the theoretical results.

**Strengths:**
- The paper addresses a significant and interesting problem, providing a rigorous mathematical analysis and deriving the optimal upper bound for the number of samples required to learn the parameters of a Gaussian Mixture Model (GMM) with epsilon-precision in the uniform spherical case.
- The authors introduce a novel approach by considering the relationship between pair correlations of means and sample complexity, which is a significant contribution to the field.
- The paper is well-written, with clear definitions and proofs, making the content accessible and understandable.
- The theoretical results are robust and could potentially be useful in practical applications, particularly in scenarios where GMMs are used extensively.
- The paper is well-positioned within the literature, providing a clear overview of related work and positioning its contributions effectively.
