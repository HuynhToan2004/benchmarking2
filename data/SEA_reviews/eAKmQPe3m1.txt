 **Summary:**
The paper introduces PixArt-\u03b1, a Transformer-based diffusion model for text-to-image (T2I) synthesis, which employs a three-stage training strategy to optimize pixel dependency, text-image alignment, and image aesthetic quality. It utilizes a cross-attention module to enhance the integration of text conditions and streamline the computation-intensive class-condition branch. The model leverages a large vision-language model to auto-label dense pseudo-captions, improving text-image alignment learning. The training strategy and the use of a large dataset with high-quality captions are highlighted as significant contributions. However, concerns were raised about the novelty of the proposed components and the lack of a comprehensive comparison with existing models.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, with clear motivation and a clear structure.
- The proposed training strategy is innovative, decomposing the training process into three stages, which is a novel approach in text-to-image (T2I) generation.
- The use of a large dataset with high-quality captions is a significant contribution, enhancing the model's performance.
- The paper introduces a cross-attention module to enhance the integration of text conditions and streamline the computation-intensive class-condition branch, which is a novel approach.
- The methodology is supported by extensive experiments and ablation studies, demonstrating the effectiveness of the proposed components.
- The paper provides a detailed analysis of the dataset, showing the benefits of using high-quality captions.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a comprehensive comparison with existing models, particularly in terms of training time and memory usage.
- The novelty of the proposed components, such as the cross-attention module and the training strategy, is questionable as similar approaches have been previously explored in other models.
- The paper does not sufficiently discuss the limitations of the proposed method, particularly the potential impact of the training strategy on the model's performance.
- The paper does not provide sufficient visual comparisons with other models, which could help in evaluating the effectiveness of the proposed method.
- There is a lack of discussion on the generalizability of the model to other datasets and the potential impact of the training strategy on the model's performance.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors provide more details on the training time and memory usage of the proposed model compared to other models?
- How does the model perform when trained on other datasets, and what are the implications of the training strategy on the model's performance?
- Can the authors provide more visual comparisons with other models to better demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed method?
- What are the specific benefits of using the cross-attention module over other methods for integrating text conditions?
- How does the model handle the potential impact of the training strategy on the model's performance, particularly in terms of generalizability to other datasets?
