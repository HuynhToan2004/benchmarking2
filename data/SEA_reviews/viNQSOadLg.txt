 **Summary:**
The paper introduces GFNSeqEditor, a novel sequence-editing algorithm that leverages pre-trained GFlowNet models to identify and edit sub-optimal positions in biological sequences, aiming to enhance specific biological properties. The algorithm identifies sub-optimal positions and then edits them based on a learned stochastic policy, which can be tuned by hyperparameters to control the number of edits. GFNSeqEditor's performance is evaluated on several datasets, showing improvements over existing state-of-the-art methods. Theoretical analyses are provided to justify the algorithm's design and the impact of hyperparameters on performance.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel approach to sequence editing using GFlowNet, which is a significant advancement in the field.
- The algorithm's design is well-motivated and theoretically justified, providing a strong foundation for its application.
- The experiments conducted are comprehensive, demonstrating the algorithm's effectiveness across various datasets and biological properties.
- The paper is well-written, making the content accessible and easy to understand, which is crucial for a broad audience.
- The proposed method has the potential to be widely used in sequence editing, which is a significant contribution to the field.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed comparison with state-of-the-art sequence editing methods, which could have provided a clearer picture of GFNSeqEditor's advantages and disadvantages.
- The experimental results are not convincing, with marginal improvements over existing methods, which raises questions about the algorithm's effectiveness.
- The paper could benefit from a more thorough discussion on the limitations of the proposed method, including the impact of hyperparameters and the algorithm's scalability with sequence length.
- There is a need for a more comprehensive evaluation of the algorithm's performance on larger, more complex datasets to validate its effectiveness.
- The paper could improve by including a more detailed comparison with other sequence editing methods, such as those mentioned in the literature review, to better position the proposed method within the current research landscape.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors provide a more detailed comparison with state-of-the-art sequence editing methods, including a discussion on the specific advantages and disadvantages of GFNSeqEditor compared to these methods?
- How does the proposed method perform on longer sequences, and what are the potential limitations or challenges associated with editing such sequences?
- Can the authors provide more insights into the impact of hyperparameters on the algorithm's performance and discuss any potential strategies for optimizing these parameters?
- How does the proposed method compare to other sequence editing methods in terms of computational efficiency and resource requirements?
- Could the authors elaborate on the theoretical underpinnings of the algorithm, particularly how it balances the number of edits with the improvement in biological properties?
- What are the potential applications of the proposed method beyond sequence editing, and how might it be adapted or modified for these applications?
