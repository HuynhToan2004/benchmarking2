 **Summary:**
The paper investigates the utility of machine learning algorithms in predicting genitourinary involvement in gastrointestinal stromal tumor (GIST) patients. It employs three supervised machine learning algorithms, namely Logistic Regression, XGBoost Regressor, and Random Forests, to analyze data from a specialist research center in Saudi Arabia. The study focuses on a dataset of 170 patients, with 58.8% being male, and the majority having gastric GISTs. Despite the potential of machine learning in predicting GU involvement, the paper's methodology and findings are criticized for being underwhelming and not sufficiently robust. The paper is criticized for its lack of originality, as similar studies have been conducted previously, and its methodological soundness is questioned due to insufficient details on data preprocessing, model selection, and evaluation metrics.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written and easy to understand, making it accessible to a broad audience.
- The topic of using machine learning to predict genitourinary involvement in GIST patients is relevant and interesting, particularly in the context of improving patient outcomes.
- The study includes a variety of machine learning algorithms, demonstrating a comprehensive approach to the analysis.
- The dataset used in the study is diverse, including patients with different GIST primary sites, which enhances the generalizability of the findings.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks novelty as similar studies have been conducted previously, which undermines its originality.
- The methodology is not sufficiently detailed, particularly in terms of data preprocessing, model selection, and evaluation metrics, which limits the reproducibility and robustness of the findings.
- The paper does not provide sufficient details on the dataset, such as the number of patients with GU involvement and the specific machine learning algorithms used.
- The paper does not adequately address the potential biases in the dataset, which could affect the validity of the results.
- The paper does not compare the machine learning models with existing methods or provide a detailed discussion on the model's performance, which is necessary for a comprehensive evaluation.
- The paper's contribution to the field is limited, as the use of machine learning algorithms in predicting GU involvement in GIST patients is already well-established.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors provide more details on the data preprocessing, model selection, and evaluation metrics used in the study?
- How does the paper address potential biases in the dataset, and what steps were taken to ensure the validity of the results?
- Can the authors clarify the specific machine learning algorithms used in the study and how they were chosen?
- How does the paper compare the machine learning models with existing methods, and what are the implications of using these models in clinical practice?
- Can the authors provide a more detailed discussion on the model's performance and the features that contribute most to the prediction of GU involvement?
- Could the authors explain the low incidence of GU involvement in the dataset and its implications for the study's findings?
