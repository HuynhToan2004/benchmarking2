 **Summary:**
The paper introduces stack attention, a novel mechanism for handling hierarchical patterns in transformers, inspired by differentiable stacks and pushdown automata (PDAs). This mechanism, which can be integrated into transformers as a replacement for standard attention, is designed to enhance their ability to model complex syntactic structures, particularly context-free languages (CFLs). The authors demonstrate that stack attention can outperform standard transformers in various tasks, including language modeling and machine translation, although the improvements are not consistently significant across all settings. The paper also explores the theoretical connections between stack attention and PDAs, highlighting its potential to recognize CFLs. However, the paper's practical applications and the scalability of the approach are questioned, particularly in larger models and more complex tasks.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel approach to incorporating differentiable stacks into transformers, which is a significant advancement in the field of natural language processing (NLP).
- The proposed stack attention mechanism is shown to be effective in modeling hierarchical structures in transformers, enhancing their ability to recognize and process complex syntactic patterns.
- The paper is well-written, with clear explanations and a thorough literature review, making it accessible and informative for readers.
- The experimental results demonstrate the effectiveness of stack attention in various tasks, including language modeling and machine translation, showing improvements over standard transformers in some cases.
- The paper provides a detailed comparison with existing models, highlighting the advantages and limitations of stack attention.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed discussion on the practical implications and limitations of stack attention, particularly in larger models and more complex tasks.
- The theoretical connections between stack attention and pushdown automata (PDAs) are not fully explored, which could limit the understanding of the mechanism's capabilities and limitations.
- The paper's experimental setup is somewhat limited, focusing primarily on small-scale models and simple tasks, which may not adequately demonstrate the scalability or general applicability of stack attention.
- There are concerns about the computational efficiency and scalability of stack attention, particularly in terms of memory and computational complexity.
- The paper does not sufficiently address how stack attention performs in more realistic settings, such as with longer sequences or in more complex language modeling tasks.
- The paper does not include a detailed analysis of the model parameters and their impact on performance, which could provide insights into the efficiency and effectiveness of stack attention.
