 **Summary:**
The paper addresses the "edge-of-reach" problem in offline model-based reinforcement learning, where values of states that are only reachable in the final step of rollouts are overestimated, similar to the out-of-sample problem in model-free methods. The authors propose a new algorithm, Reach-Aware Value Learning (RAVL), to capture value uncertainty at edge-of-reach states. This algorithm is tested on the standard D4RL benchmark, showing promising results. The paper also discusses the limitations of existing methods and provides a theoretical analysis of the edge-of-reach problem.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel problem in offline model-based reinforcement learning, termed the "edge-of-reach" problem, which is both significant and overlooked in the existing literature.
- The proposed solution, Reach-Aware Value Learning (RAVL), is simple yet effective, achieving state-of-the-art results on the D4RL benchmark.
- The paper is well-written, making it easy to follow, and the experiments are well-designed and convincing, demonstrating the effectiveness of RAVL.
- The authors provide a thorough theoretical analysis and empirical evidence, including a simple environment to illustrate the phenomenon, which supports their claims.
- The paper offers a fresh perspective on the out-of-sample problem in offline model-based reinforcement learning, connecting the strengths of model-free and model-based approaches.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a comprehensive comparison with other model-based methods, particularly those that use dynamics uncertainty penalization.
- The presentation of the paper could be improved, especially in terms of clarity and organization, particularly in sections 4.2 and 4.3.
- The experimental results are limited to a few environments, which might not fully demonstrate the generalizability of the proposed method.
- The paper does not provide a detailed analysis of the computational cost and training time of the proposed method, which could be crucial for practical applications.
- The theoretical analysis of the edge-of-reach problem and its connection to the out-of-sample problem could be more clearly articulated, especially in terms of how it affects model-free methods.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors provide a more detailed comparison with other model-based methods, particularly those that use dynamics uncertainty penalization?
- How does the proposed method perform in environments where the learned dynamics model is not accurate?
- Could the authors clarify the definition and role of edge-of-reach states in the context of model-based reinforcement learning?
- How does the proposed method compare in terms of computational cost and training time with other methods?
- Can the authors provide more detailed experimental results, possibly including a broader range of environments and a more comprehensive evaluation of the method's performance?
