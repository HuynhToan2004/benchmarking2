 **Summary:**
The paper explores the theoretical foundations of outlier transfer learning by establishing information-theoretic limits and providing adaptive procedures to achieve these limits. It discusses the transferability of knowledge from a source to a target outlier detection task, focusing on the Neyman-Pearson classification framework. The authors demonstrate that seemingly different source and target distributions can yield identical optimal classifiers, and they provide a minimax lower bound for outlier transfer learning. The paper also discusses the practical implications of these theoretical findings, though it lacks empirical validation.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces the concept of outlier transfer learning, which is a novel and significant contribution to the field.
- It provides a theoretical analysis of the information-theoretic limits of outlier transfer learning, which is a significant contribution to the understanding of this problem.
- The paper is well-written, making it easy to follow and understand the complex theoretical concepts presented.
- The authors demonstrate that even with different source and target distributions, optimal classifiers can be transferred, which is a surprising and interesting finding.
- The paper introduces a novel notion of outlier transfer exponent, which measures the discrepancy between source and target distributions, and provides a lower bound for the outlier transfer learning problem.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks empirical validation, which is crucial for assessing the practical applicability and effectiveness of the proposed theoretical findings.
- The assumptions made, such as the mutual dominance of distributions, may not hold in practical scenarios, which could limit the generalizability of the results.
- The paper does not provide a concrete algorithm for outlier transfer learning, which could be a significant limitation for practitioners seeking practical applications.
- The paper does not adequately address the practical implications of the theoretical findings, such as how to apply the minimax lower bound in real-world scenarios.
- The paper could benefit from more detailed discussions on the practical implications and limitations of the theoretical results.

**Questions:**
- How can the minimax lower bound be applied in practical scenarios, and what are the implications of this application?
- What are the practical implications of the theoretical results presented in the paper?
- How can the theoretical findings be used to develop practical algorithms for outlier transfer learning?
- Can the authors clarify the assumptions made in the paper, particularly the mutual dominance of distributions, and discuss how these assumptions affect the generalizability of the results?
- How does the proposed outlier transfer learning approach compare with other existing methods, and what are the advantages or disadvantages of using this approach over others?
