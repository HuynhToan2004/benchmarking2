 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel method for solving inverse problems using latent diffusion models, specifically focusing on prompt-tuning and VAE generative prior to enhance performance. The method optimizes text embeddings in real-time while running the reverse diffusion process, which is claimed to improve image quality by reducing artifacts. The authors demonstrate the effectiveness of their approach through experiments on various inverse problems such as super-resolution, deblurring, and inpainting, showing superior performance over existing methods. Despite its strengths, the paper has been criticized for its limited novelty, insufficient experimental validation, and lack of detailed implementation and hyperparameter settings.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel method for solving inverse problems using latent diffusion models, which is a significant advancement in the field.
- The proposed prompt-tuning method is innovative and effective, demonstrated by its superior performance in various inverse problem-solving tasks.
- The use of a VAE generative prior in conjunction with the diffusion prior is a promising approach that helps reduce artifacts in the output images.
- The paper is well-written, clear, and easy to follow, with a comprehensive experimental evaluation that demonstrates the effectiveness of the proposed method.
- The idea of prompt tuning is novel and interesting, showing a significant impact on the performance of latent diffusion models in solving inverse problems.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks sufficient experimental validation, particularly in terms of the generalization ability of the method to different datasets and the impact of hyperparameters.
- The novelty of the method is questioned, as it primarily involves the use of pre-trained models and a VAE prior, which have been previously explored.
- There is a lack of detailed implementation and hyperparameter settings, which is crucial for reproducibility and understanding the full impact of the method.
- The paper could benefit from a more thorough discussion on the limitations of the proposed method, including its computational complexity and the potential for overfitting.
- The paper's claims about the performance of the method are not convincingly supported by the provided experimental results, which may not adequately demonstrate the superiority of the proposed method over existing techniques.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors provide more detailed experimental results, particularly on different datasets and with varying hyperparameters, to demonstrate the generalization ability and robustness of the proposed method?
- How does the proposed method compare in terms of computational complexity and inference speed to other existing methods, especially in practical applications?
- Could the authors clarify the role and impact of the VAE prior in the performance of the method, and discuss how it interacts with the diffusion prior?
- Is there a possibility to include more recent and relevant literature in the comparative analysis to better position the paper within the current research landscape?
- Could the authors provide more detailed implementation and hyperparameter settings to facilitate reproducibility and further research?
