 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel method, GNNBoundary, aimed at understanding the decision boundaries of Graph Neural Networks (GNNs) by generating boundary graphs that represent the decision-making process of GNNs. The approach involves identifying adjacent class pairs and optimizing an objective function to generate graphs that closely resemble the decision boundary. The paper evaluates this method on both synthetic and real-world datasets, showing promising results in generating faithful near-boundary graphs. The methodology is designed to provide insights into the robustness and generalizability of GNNs by analyzing these boundary graphs. Despite its innovative approach, the paper has been critiqued for its lack of comprehensive experimental validation, insufficient comparative analysis with existing methods, and unclear presentation of certain technical aspects.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, making it easy to follow and understand, with a clear structure and logical flow.
- The introduction of the concept of decision boundary analysis for GNNs is novel and innovative, providing a new perspective on explaining GNNs.
- The proposed method, GNNBoundary, is technically sound and the experimental results show that it can generate faithful near-boundary graphs, which are useful for analyzing decision boundaries.
- The paper addresses an important and timely topic, focusing on the transparency and explainability of GNNs, which is crucial for their adoption in real-world applications.
- The methodology introduces an algorithm to identify adjacent class pairs and generates graphs that closely resemble the decision boundary, which is a significant contribution to the field.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a comprehensive evaluation of the proposed method, particularly in terms of its scalability and robustness, especially when applied to larger and more complex datasets.
- There is a notable absence of comparative analysis with existing methods, which could have provided a better understanding of the method's advantages and limitations.
- The presentation of the paper could be improved, particularly in the clarity and explanation of technical aspects such as the objective function and the methodology used.
- The paper does not sufficiently address the limitations of the proposed method, which could help in understanding its scope and applicability.
- There is a lack of detailed explanation and justification for certain methodological choices, such as the decision to use the Gumbel-Softmax trick and the choice of hyperparameters.
- The paper could benefit from a more comprehensive discussion on the potential societal impacts and ethical considerations of the proposed method.

**Questions:**
- How does the proposed method compare with other existing methods for explaining GNNs, particularly in terms of its effectiveness and efficiency?
- Can you provide more details on the computational complexity and scalability of the proposed method, especially when applied to larger and more complex datasets?
- How does the method handle scenarios where the decision boundaries are not linear, and what adjustments, if any, are necessary in such cases?
- In the experimental results, how are the metrics (boundary thickness, boundary margin, and boundary complexity) computed, and what do they represent in the context of the decision boundaries?
- Could you elaborate on the decision to use the Gumbel-Softmax trick and the choice of hyperparameters in the methodology?
- How does the proposed method ensure that the generated graphs are faithful to the original decision boundaries, and what measures are in place to prevent overfitting or other biases?
