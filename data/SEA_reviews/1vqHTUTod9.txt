 **Summary:**
The paper introduces PrivQA, a benchmark designed to evaluate the ability of language models to protect personal information while maintaining utility. It assesses the trade-off between privacy and utility by evaluating models' responses to protected groups and control groups. The benchmark includes a self-moderation technique to improve privacy protection, but also highlights the vulnerabilities of these models to adversarial attacks. The paper also discusses the limitations of current models in handling privacy concerns, particularly in the context of public figures and protected information.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel benchmark, PrivQA, which assesses the trade-off between privacy protection and model utility, a significant issue in the field of language models.
- The paper is well-written, clearly presenting the research questions and methodology, and includes a comprehensive literature review.
- The experiments are well-designed, with a focus on both protected and control groups, and the use of self-moderation to improve privacy protection is innovative.
- The paper addresses a timely and relevant topic, as the use of language models in handling personal information raises significant privacy concerns.
- The paper is original in its approach, providing a new benchmark and methodology for evaluating the privacy-utility trade-off in language models.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a comprehensive discussion on the limitations of the proposed benchmark, which is crucial for understanding its applicability and effectiveness.
- The technical contribution of the paper is limited, with the proposed self-moderation technique not being particularly novel or surprising.
- The paper does not adequately address the ethical implications of the research, particularly concerning the use of protected groups and the potential for data leakage.
- There is a lack of clarity in some sections of the paper, which could hinder understanding and reproducibility.
- The paper does not sufficiently discuss the generalizability of the findings, which is crucial for understanding the broader applicability of the results.
- The paper could benefit from a more thorough discussion on the potential societal impacts of the research, particularly concerning the use of language models in handling personal information.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors clarify the definition and selection process for protected groups, particularly how they ensure that the benchmark does not inadvertently protect groups that should not be protected?
- How does the paper address the potential for data leakage, especially given the focus on protected groups?
- Could the authors provide more details on the self-moderation technique, including the specific prompts used and how they were selected?
- How do the authors ensure the generalizability of the findings, particularly given the use of specific models and datasets?
- Could the authors expand on the potential societal impacts of the research, particularly concerning the use of language models in handling personal information?
- How do the authors plan to address the limitations discussed, particularly concerning the ethical implications and the clarity of the presentation?
