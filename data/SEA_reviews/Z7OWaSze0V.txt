 **Summary:**
The paper introduces the Multi-view Cross-domain Item-sharing Recommendation (MCIR) framework, which integrates user preferences from user-rating and user-comment networks with critic opinions from a critic network. The MCIR framework employs distinct embedding networks for each perspective, integrating an attentive mechanism and contrastive learning to align embeddings across different views. The framework's effectiveness is demonstrated through experiments on three real-world datasets, showing superiority over state-of-the-art methods. Despite its strengths in methodology and experimental validation, the paper suffers from issues in clarity and depth in the discussion of related work and the necessity of the proposed method in real-world scenarios.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-organized and easy to follow, with a clear problem definition and a novel approach to handling cross-domain recommendation with distinct user groups.
- The proposed method is sound and well-motivated, with a focus on integrating user preferences and critic opinions through distinct embedding networks and a cross-view contrastive learning mechanism.
- Extensive experiments are conducted on real-world datasets, demonstrating the effectiveness of the proposed method in improving recommendation performance.
- The paper provides a detailed description of the proposed framework, including the user-rating network, user-comment network, and critic network, which are all well-explained and justified.
- The use of a graph convolutional network to capture neighborhood information from the user-item-critic graph is a novel approach that enhances the recommendation system's capabilities.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a clear explanation of how the proposed method can be applied to real-world scenarios, particularly in the absence of a detailed discussion on the necessity and practical implications of the proposed method.
- The related work section is inadequately discussed, missing out on significant existing methods in cross-domain recommendation and multi-view learning.
- The performance of the proposed method is not consistently superior across all datasets and metrics, with some variations in performance noted.
- The paper lacks a detailed discussion on the limitations of the proposed method and the potential negative societal impacts of the work.
- There are several instances of unclear or ambiguous statements in the paper, which could confuse readers or require further clarification.
- The paper could benefit from a more comprehensive evaluation, including comparisons with state-of-the-art methods and a more detailed analysis of the results.
