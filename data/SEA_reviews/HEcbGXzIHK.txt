 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel approach to interpreting Recurrent Neural Networks (RNNs) by viewing them as discrete-time analogs of the General Sequential Episodic Memory Model (GSEMM). This perspective allows for the formulation of a mathematically rigorous circuit for variable binding in RNNs, which is empirically validated through tasks designed to probe variable binding behavior. The authors propose a method for identifying a "privileged basis" of latent neurons that play a crucial role in temporal storage and composition of variables. This approach not only enhances the interpretability of RNNs but also bridges the gap between artificial neural networks and neural memory models. The paper's theoretical contributions are supported by empirical evidence, and the methodology is validated through a series of experiments.

**Weaknesses:**

- The paper's scope is limited to linear RNNs, which may not generalize well to non-linear RNNs.
- The paper does not adequately address the limitations of the variable memory approach, such as the assumption of linear dynamical systems and the storage of external information as a linear superposition of variable memories in the hidden state.
