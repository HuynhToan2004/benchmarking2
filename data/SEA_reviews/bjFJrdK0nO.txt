 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel framework for object view editing, integrating viewpoint information to enhance the control of image editing tasks. It employs a two-stage process: first, an LLM planner extracts pose information from user instructions, followed by a pose estimation module to obtain the pose of the object in the source image. The framework then synthesizes the reference object with the desired pose and inpaints the background using a diffusion model. The methodology is evaluated against existing techniques, showing superior performance in terms of consistency, controllability, and harmony. However, the paper has been criticized for its complexity, the lack of comparisons with stronger baselines, and insufficient experimental validation.

**Strengths:**
- The paper addresses a novel problem of object view editing, which is a significant challenge in image processing.
- The proposed framework integrates viewpoint information to enhance the control of image editing tasks, providing a more precise and controllable approach to object manipulation.
- The paper is well-organized, clearly written, and easy to follow, making it accessible to a broad audience.
- The methodology is evaluated against existing techniques, demonstrating superior performance in terms of consistency, controllability, and harmony.
- The paper presents a novel approach to object view editing, which is both innovative and technically sound, and the results showcase the effectiveness of the proposed method.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper is complex and difficult to understand, with a lack of clarity in the methodology and a need for more detailed explanations of the modules used.
- The evaluation of the method is limited, with only a few comparisons made against existing techniques, and no comparisons with stronger baselines such as GLIGEN, ControlNet, or DreamPaint.
- The paper does not discuss the limitations of the proposed method, which is crucial for understanding its applicability and potential drawbacks.
- The paper lacks a detailed analysis of the computational efficiency of the proposed method, which is essential for practical applications.
- There is a lack of detailed experimental results, including the absence of quantitative comparisons and a discussion on the effectiveness of the pose estimation module.
- The paper does not adequately address the limitations of the method, such as the complexity of the framework and the lack of comparison with stronger baselines.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors clarify the role and effectiveness of the LLM Planner in the proposed method?
- How does the paper ensure that the synthesized objects harmoniously integrate with their surroundings, and what metrics were used to measure this harmony?
- Could the authors provide more detailed information on the training process of the pose estimation model, including the size and diversity of the training dataset?
- How does the paper ensure that the synthesized objects are consistent with the reference objects, and what measures are taken to maintain this consistency?
- The paper mentions the use of a two-stage synthesis approach. Can the authors explain why a one-stage synthesis approach was not considered, and what are the implications of this choice for the computational efficiency and practical applicability of the method?
