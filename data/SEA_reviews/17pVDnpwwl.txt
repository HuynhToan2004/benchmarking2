 **Summary:**
The paper introduces Depth-uP, a novel scaling method for training arbitrarily deep neural networks, focusing on residual networks. This method, which is a generalization of the maximal update parametrization (uP), is designed to maximize feature learning and diversity among layers. It proposes a parameter scaling strategy and a learning rate scaling strategy that are theoretically justified and empirically validated through experiments on CIFAR-10. The method's effectiveness is demonstrated by showing that it allows for the training of networks with thousands of layers, maintaining feature learning and diversity. However, the paper's scope is limited to residual networks, and it lacks a comprehensive empirical validation across different architectures and datasets.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, clearly presenting the methodology and its implications, with a clear and logical flow of ideas.
- The proposed scaling method for depth is novel and theoretically grounded, showing potential for transferability of hyperparameters across depths.
- The experiments are well-designed, demonstrating the effectiveness of the proposed method in maintaining feature learning and diversity across layers.
- The theoretical analysis, particularly in the linear case, is thorough and provides a solid foundation for the proposed method.
- The paper introduces a new scaling strategy that maximizes feature learning and diversity among layers, which is significant for the field of neural network training.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper primarily focuses on residual networks, which limits the generalizability of the findings to other network architectures such as transformers.
- The empirical validation is limited to CIFAR-10, and it would be beneficial to see how the method performs on more complex datasets like ImageNet.
- The paper's scope is somewhat narrow, focusing only on the training dynamics of linear networks and not extending to nonlinear networks, which could limit the applicability of the findings.
- There is a lack of discussion on how the proposed method might affect the training dynamics of non-linear networks, which is a critical aspect for practical applications.
- The paper does not provide a detailed comparison with existing methods, which could help in understanding the novelty and effectiveness of the proposed method.

**Questions:**
- How does the proposed method perform on non-linear networks, particularly in terms of training dynamics and feature learning?
- Could the authors provide more details on how the method might be adapted for transformer architectures?
- Is there a possibility to extend the theoretical analysis to non-linear networks, and what would be the expected outcomes?
- How does the proposed method compare with other scaling strategies for residual networks, particularly in terms of feature learning and diversity?
- In practical applications, how can the optimal learning rate and block multiplier be determined for different depths and datasets?
- Could the authors discuss the implications of the proposed method for the training of very deep networks, and how it might affect the performance on larger datasets like ImageNet?
