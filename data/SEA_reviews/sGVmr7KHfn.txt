 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel method called Memory-Assisted Sub-Prototype Mining (MemSPM) for Universal Domain Adaptation (UniDA), focusing on the intra-class structure within categories to address significant concept shifts. MemSPM utilizes a memory mechanism to learn sub-prototypes that represent sub-classes within categories, enhancing the model's ability to distinguish between similar but distinct classes. The method is evaluated across multiple benchmarks, showing promising results in most cases, although some concerns about the novelty and clarity of the method's contributions remain.

**Strengths:**
- The paper addresses an important and timely topic, focusing on the intra-class structure within categories, which is often overlooked in UniDA.
- The proposed method, Memory-Assisted Sub-Prototype Mining (MemSPM), is novel and effectively leverages a memory mechanism to learn sub-prototypes that represent sub-classes, enhancing the model's adaptability and interpretability.
- The paper is well-written, with clear explanations of the methodology and results, and includes a comprehensive experimental evaluation that demonstrates the effectiveness of MemSPM.
- MemSPM achieves state-of-the-art performance in most cases, as shown in the experimental results.
- The paper is well-organized and provides a detailed explanation of the proposed method, including its components and their roles in the overall framework.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper's contribution is somewhat limited by its reliance on existing methods like CLIP for pre-trained feature extraction, which may not be seen as a significant advancement over existing methods.
- The novelty of the method is questioned, as it appears to be a combination of existing techniques like the memory structure and the adaptive threshold, which may not be sufficiently distinct to warrant a new method.
- The paper lacks a detailed comparison with existing methods, particularly in terms of the use of CLIP for feature extraction, which could affect the perceived novelty and effectiveness of the proposed method.
- The method's complexity, particularly in terms of the memory mechanism and the use of multiple sub-prototypes, may make it difficult to apply in practical scenarios.
- The paper does not adequately address the computational costs associated with the memory mechanism, which could be a significant drawback in terms of practical implementation.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors clarify how the memory structure and adaptive threshold are combined to form the MemSPM method, and how these components contribute uniquely to the proposed method?
- How does the proposed method perform when compared to other existing methods, particularly those that do not use CLIP for feature extraction?
- Could the authors provide more detailed comparisons with existing methods, including a more detailed analysis of the computational costs associated with the memory mechanism?
- In the context of the UniDA problem, how does the proposed method address the challenges of domain shift and the lack of labeled data in the target domain?
- How does the proposed method handle the issue of concept shift within categories, and can it effectively identify and classify samples from different sub-classes?
- Could the authors provide more details on the experimental setup, particularly the training and testing data used in the experiments, and the hyperparameters employed for different datasets?
