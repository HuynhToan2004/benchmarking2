 **Summary:**
The paper introduces SCoRF, a novel method for 3D scene reconstruction and view synthesis using a single-stage convolutional neural network. This approach aims to address the limitations of hierarchical architectures in NeRF by reducing noise and irrelevant features, thereby enhancing the robustness of the model. The proposed method leverages a convolutional neural network to jointly learn the relative position of three-dimensional rays and their relative color and density, which is a significant improvement over traditional NeRF methods. The paper provides extensive experiments and comparisons with state-of-the-art methods, demonstrating the superiority of the proposed method in terms of performance and efficiency.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written and easy to follow, with clear explanations of the proposed method.
- The proposed method, SCoRF, effectively reduces noise and irrelevant features in 3D scene reconstruction, improving the robustness of the model.
- The experiments conducted are extensive, including comparisons with state-of-the-art methods and an ablation study, which demonstrate the superiority of the proposed method.
- The paper provides a novel approach to 3D scene reconstruction and view synthesis by using a single-stage convolutional neural network, which is more efficient than traditional hierarchical architectures.
- The methodology is well-explained, making it easy to understand and implement, and the paper provides a clear explanation of the motivation behind the proposed method.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed discussion on the limitations of the proposed method, which is crucial for a comprehensive understanding of its applicability and performance.
- There are minor typographical errors and inconsistencies in the manuscript, which could detract from its professionalism.
- The paper does not include a discussion on the scalability of the proposed method, which is a significant concern for large-scale applications.
- The paper does not provide sufficient details on the implementation of the method, such as the number of parameters used and the training time required.
- The paper could benefit from more visual results, especially for synthetic data, to better demonstrate the effectiveness of the method.
- The paper does not sufficiently differentiate its contributions from existing works, such as Tensorf, which also uses a convolutional neural network for 3D scene reconstruction.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors provide a detailed discussion on the limitations of the proposed method, including its scalability and performance in different scenarios?
- How does the proposed method compare in terms of training time and computational complexity to existing methods like Instant-NGP and NeRF?
- Could the authors provide more visual results, especially for synthetic data, to better demonstrate the effectiveness of the method?
- How does the proposed method handle different types of scenes, such as those with complex backgrounds or crowded objects, which are known to be challenging for NeRF-based methods?
- Could the authors clarify the role and impact of the adaptive position loss in the proposed method, especially in scenarios where the sequence of position-related values does not exhibit a monotonic increase?
- How does the proposed method compare in terms of generalization ability to other types of scenes, such as those with different illumination conditions or varying levels of complexity?
