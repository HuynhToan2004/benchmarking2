 **Summary:**
The paper investigates the application of dropout in bilevel optimization, focusing on the convergence analysis of a dropout variant of the FSLA algorithm. It introduces a stochastic bilevel model that incorporates the distribution of dropout masks, proposing a method to solve the dropout bilevel optimization model. Theoretical analysis is provided to characterize the inductive relations between variables affected by dropout masks and the variance term in the inductive relations. Empirical results demonstrate that the proposed method mitigates overfitting in data cleaning and meta-learning tasks. However, the paper's contribution is seen as limited as it primarily adapts existing methods without significant innovation.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, making it easy to follow and understand the proposed methodologies and theoretical analyses.
- It provides a comprehensive theoretical analysis of the proposed method, including a convergence rate analysis for the proposed algorithm.
- The paper introduces a novel approach to bilevel optimization by incorporating dropout methods, which is a significant advancement in the field.
- The authors have demonstrated the practical utility of their method through experiments that show the effectiveness of the proposed method in mitigating overfitting in data cleaning and meta-learning tasks.
- The paper includes a detailed analysis of the complexity of the dropout bilevel method, which is an important aspect of the study.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed discussion on the computational complexity of the proposed method, which is crucial for understanding its practical applicability.
- The novelty of the proposed method is limited as it primarily adapts existing methods without significant innovation.
- There is a lack of comprehensive empirical evidence to support the claims made in the paper, particularly concerning the overfitting problem and the effectiveness of the proposed method in addressing it.
- The paper does not adequately address the generalizability of the proposed method to other types of bilevel optimization problems or different datasets.
- The theoretical analysis is somewhat limited, particularly in terms of its applicability to other types of bilevel optimization problems and the lack of detailed discussion on the assumptions made in the analysis.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors provide more detailed discussions on the computational complexity of the proposed method, including the time and space complexity?
- How does the proposed method compare in terms of computational efficiency and convergence rate with existing methods, especially in scenarios where overfitting is not a significant issue?
- Can the authors elaborate on the assumptions made in the theoretical analysis and discuss how these assumptions might affect the applicability of the results to other types of bilevel optimization problems?
- Is there a possibility to extend the proposed method to other types of bilevel optimization problems, such as those involving non-convex lower-level problems?
- Could the authors provide more detailed experimental results, including comparisons with other methods and a broader range of datasets to validate the effectiveness of the proposed method?
