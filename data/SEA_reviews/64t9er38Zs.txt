 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel method for learning O(n)-equivariant deep features using deep equivariant hyperspheres, which are a generalization of spherical neurons to any dimension. This method employs regular n-simplexes to construct an O(n)-equivariant network, which is capable of handling higher-dimensional data. The paper presents a theoretical framework that extends the concept of steerable 3D spherical neurons to higher dimensions, using regular n-simplexes to maintain equivariance. The method is evaluated on both synthetic and real-world data, showing superior performance and a better speed/performance trade-off compared to existing methods. However, the paper's writing and presentation are criticized for being unclear and lacking in depth in some sections, particularly in the experimental setup and the discussion of related works.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel method for learning O(n)-equivariant deep features using deep equivariant hyperspheres, which is a significant contribution to the field of geometric deep learning.
- The method is well-explained, making it accessible to readers, and the paper provides a clear and detailed explanation of the theoretical framework, including proofs of theorems and propositions.
- The proposed method is evaluated on both synthetic and real-world data, demonstrating its effectiveness and robustness across different scenarios.
- The paper presents a novel approach to steerable n-simplexes, which could potentially be useful in other areas of machine learning and deep learning.
- The method's ability to maintain equivariance in higher dimensions is a significant advancement over existing methods, which are typically limited to lower dimensions.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a thorough discussion on the limitations of the proposed method, which could help in understanding its applicability and potential drawbacks.
- The experimental section could be improved by providing more details on the experimental setup, such as the number of trials, the size of the datasets used, and the hyperparameters of the models.
- There is a need for more clarity in the presentation of the method, particularly in the explanation of the matrices and the use of symbols in the equations.
- The paper could benefit from a more comprehensive literature review, especially regarding the use of spherical neurons in the literature and their potential applications.
- The paper's writing and presentation need improvement, with some sections being unclear or difficult to follow, and the paper could benefit from a more polished presentation.
- The experimental results are not sufficiently convincing, with some figures being too small to read and the results not showing a significant advantage over existing methods in all cases.
