 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel framework named SpikSLC-Net, designed for sound source localization and classification using spiking neural networks (SNNs). The framework incorporates a Spiking Hybrid Attention Fusion (SHAF) mechanism, which integrates spiking self-attention and spiking cross-attention modules to capture temporal dependencies and align relationships among diverse features. Additionally, a training-inference-decoupled Layer Normalization (DSLN) method is proposed to optimize the computational efficiency of SNNs. The framework is evaluated on the SLoClas dataset, showing state-of-the-art performance with minimal time steps. However, the paper is criticized for its lack of clarity in explaining the biological inspiration and the practical implications of the proposed methods, particularly the use of spiking neural networks in real-world scenarios.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel Spiking Hybrid Attention Fusion (SHAF) mechanism, which effectively integrates spiking self-attention and spiking cross-attention modules to capture temporal dependencies and align relationships among diverse features.
- The proposed training-inference-decoupled Layer Normalization (DSLN) method is innovative and optimizes the computational efficiency of SNNs.
- The paper is well-written, easy to follow, and provides a clear motivation for the research.
- The methodology is novel and the results show that the proposed method achieves state-of-the-art performance on the SLoClas dataset.
- The paper effectively utilizes spiking neural networks (SNNs) for sound source localization and classification, which is a significant contribution to the field.

**Weaknesses:**
