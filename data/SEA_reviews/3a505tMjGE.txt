 **Summary:**
The paper addresses the issue of overestimation in Variational Autoencoders (VAEs) for unsupervised out-of-distribution (OOD) detection, focusing on two primary factors: the design of the prior distribution and the entropy-mutual integration gap between in-distribution (ID) and out-of-distribution (OOD) datasets. The authors propose a new score function, AVOID, which includes two novel techniques: post-hoc prior and dataset entropy-mutual calibration, to mitigate these issues. Theoretical analysis is provided to support these techniques, and extensive experiments are conducted to validate their effectiveness. However, the paper has been critiqued for its clarity in presentation, potential lack of novelty, and insufficient experimental validation, particularly in terms of the scalability and generalizability of the proposed methods.

**Strengths:**
- The paper addresses the critical issue of overestimation in Variational Autoencoders (VAEs) for unsupervised out-of-distribution (OOD) detection, which is a significant and relevant problem in the field.
- The authors provide a comprehensive theoretical analysis and empirical validation of the proposed methods, which are well-articulated and supported by extensive experiments.
- The paper is well-written, clear, and easy to follow, making it accessible to a broad audience.
- The proposed methods, including post-hoc prior and dataset entropy-mutual calibration, are innovative and offer a new perspective on addressing the overestimation problem in VAEs.
- The paper includes a detailed analysis of the overestimation issue, which is crucial for understanding and improving the performance of VAEs in OOD detection tasks.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper could benefit from improved clarity and presentation, particularly in sections where the content is dense or the logical flow is unclear.
- The novelty of the proposed methods is questionable, as similar approaches have been previously explored in the literature.
- The experimental validation is insufficient, with a lack of comparison to more recent state-of-the-art methods and a limited evaluation across a variety of datasets.
- There is a potential issue with the scalability and generalizability of the proposed methods, particularly in more complex or high-dimensional datasets.
- The paper does not sufficiently address the limitations of the proposed methods, such as the computational complexity and the need for additional hyperparameters, which could impact the practical applicability of the proposed solutions.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors clarify the rationale behind the choice of the SVD-based calibration function and discuss its effectiveness compared to other potential calibration methods?
- How does the proposed method perform when the in-distribution (ID) and out-of-distribution (OOD) datasets are not distinct, such as in cases where OOD data is a subset of ID data?
- Could the authors provide more detailed experimental results, including comparisons with more recent state-of-the-art methods and a broader range of datasets?
- How does the proposed method handle scenarios where the in-distribution (ID) and out-of-distribution (OOD) datasets are not well-separated, such as in the case of adversarial examples?
- Can the authors elaborate on the potential applications of the proposed method in more complex or high-dimensional datasets, and discuss any challenges or limitations in scaling up the method?
