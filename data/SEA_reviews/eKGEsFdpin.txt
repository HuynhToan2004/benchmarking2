 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel watermarking method for detecting machine-generated texts by manipulating the token sampling process during text generation. The method involves calculating a "secret number" for each token, which is used to determine the likelihood of a token being chosen during sampling. This method is designed to be robust against paraphrasing attacks and maintains textual quality. The paper presents a comprehensive experimental evaluation, comparing the proposed method with a state-of-the-art watermarking method in terms of robustness and detectability. Despite the promising results, concerns were raised about the novelty of the method and its potential impact on text quality, especially when applied to smaller models.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, with a clear and concise presentation of the method and results.
- The proposed method is simple, easy to understand, and does not require retraining or fine-tuning of large language models (LLMs).
- The method is robust to paraphrasing attacks, maintaining the text's natural readability and coherence.
- The paper includes a comprehensive experimental evaluation, comparing the proposed method with a state-of-the-art watermarking method in terms of robustness and detectability.
- The methodology is model-agnostic, which allows for its application across different LLMs.
- The paper is reproducible, as the authors have shared their code and dataset, which can aid in further research and validation of the proposed method.

**Weaknesses:**
- The novelty of the method is questionable as it seems to be a variation of existing methods like the "Maryland Watermark" and "Adversarial Watermarking Transformer," with similar methodologies and results.
- The paper lacks a detailed analysis of the trade-off between text quality and watermarking robustness, which is crucial for understanding the practical implications of the proposed method.
- The experimental results do not convincingly demonstrate a significant improvement over existing methods, especially when applied to smaller models.
- The paper does not sufficiently address the potential negative societal impacts of the proposed method, such as its use in malicious applications like plagiarism or misinformation.
- The method's effectiveness is limited by the model size, as shown in the experimental results, which raises concerns about its applicability to smaller models.
- The paper does not adequately discuss the limitations of the proposed method, such as its potential to introduce biases or affect the diversity of generated texts.

**Questions:**
- How does the proposed method compare to existing methods in terms of computational overhead and practical deployment?
- Can the authors provide more detailed insights into the trade-off between text quality and watermarking robustness, especially in practical scenarios?
- How does the proposed method perform when applied to smaller models, and what are the implications for its practical utility?
- Can the authors provide a more detailed analysis of the potential negative societal impacts of the proposed method, and discuss strategies to mitigate these risks?
- How does the proposed method affect the diversity of generated texts, and what steps can be taken to ensure that the watermarking process does not bias the output?
