 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel dataset of paired visual and tactile data, which is used to train a model that predicts tactile signals from visual inputs and vice versa. The model uses a shared latent space to represent both modalities, which is projected onto separate sub-manifolds for each modality to preserve local structure. The paper demonstrates that the proposed method outperforms existing multi-modal representation works, showing potential in various robotic and vision applications. However, the paper is criticized for its limited experimental validation, lack of comparison with state-of-the-art methods, and insufficient discussion on the societal impact and ethical considerations.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel dataset of paired visual and tactile data, which is significant for advancing research in robotics and computer vision.
- The proposed method for learning a shared latent space across visual and tactile modalities is novel and shows potential for various applications, including in robotics.
- The paper is well-written, making it easy to follow, and the methodology is clearly explained, contributing to its readability and comprehensibility.
- The experiments conducted demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed method in handling cross-modal prediction and retrieval tasks, showcasing its utility in various applications.
- The paper provides a thorough evaluation of the method, which is crucial for understanding its strengths and limitations.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a comprehensive comparison with state-of-the-art methods, which is crucial for establishing the superiority of the proposed method.
- The dataset used in the experiments is not sufficiently detailed, and there is a lack of discussion on how the dataset was collected and its potential biases or limitations.
- The paper does not discuss the societal impact or ethical considerations of the work, which are essential for ensuring the responsible development and deployment of the technology.
- The methodology section is not clearly explained, particularly the details of the neural fields used and the training process, which could hinder reproducibility and understanding of the results.
- The paper does not provide sufficient qualitative results or ablation studies, which are necessary for a deeper understanding of the method's effectiveness and limitations.
- The paper does not discuss the potential biases or limitations of the dataset, which could affect the generalizability and reliability of the results.

**Questions:**
- Could you provide more details on how the dataset was collected, including the number of participants, the data collection process, and any potential biases or limitations of the dataset?
- How do you address the issue of domain gaps between different modalities, especially when the data modalities are very different?
- How does the proposed method handle the variability in data modalities, especially when the visual and tactile data may not be directly comparable?
- Can you provide more details on the neural fields used in the method, including their architecture and training process?
- How do you ensure the reproducibility of the results, and what steps have been taken to validate the method's effectiveness across different datasets and conditions?
- Could you discuss the potential societal impacts and ethical considerations of the work, particularly in the context of robotics and computer vision applications?
