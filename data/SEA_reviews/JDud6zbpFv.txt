 **Summary:**
The paper introduces Cooperative Coevolution Quality-Diversity (CCQD), a novel approach to address the sample efficiency issues in Quality-Diversity (QD) algorithms. CCQD decomposes a policy network into two types of layers: representation and decision, allowing for a more efficient search process. The methodology is evaluated against existing QD algorithms and shows significant improvements in sample efficiency and performance on various tasks. However, the paper has been critiqued for its lack of theoretical justification, insufficient empirical evaluation, and unclear presentation, particularly in the introduction and related work sections.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel approach to QD by decomposing a policy network into representation and decision layers, which is a significant contribution to the field.
- The method is evaluated on a variety of tasks and shows promising results, outperforming other methods in terms of sample efficiency and performance.
- The paper is well-written, making it easy to follow, and includes a detailed ablation study that helps in understanding the method's components.
- The proposed method is compatible with existing QD techniques, which allows for its integration into existing frameworks.
- The paper provides a detailed explanation of the methodology, including the use of different selection and variation operators, which enhances its applicability and reproducibility.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a clear justification for the proposed method, particularly in terms of its theoretical underpinnings and its comparison with other existing methods.
- The empirical evaluation is limited, with insufficient comparisons to state-of-the-art methods and a lack of detailed analysis of the results.
- The paper suffers from unclear writing and presentation, particularly in the introduction and related work sections, which makes it difficult for readers to understand the context and contributions of the research.
- The paper does not adequately address the limitations of the method, such as the scalability of the approach and the potential for overfitting due to the use of multiple critics.
- The paper's experimental setup and results are not sufficiently robust, with some results not being statistically significant and others being potentially biased due to the use of specific hyperparameters.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors clarify the theoretical justification for the proposed method and its comparison with existing methods?
- How does the method handle the potential for overfitting due to the use of multiple critics?
- Could the authors provide more detailed comparisons with state-of-the-art methods, including a more comprehensive analysis of the results?
- How does the method perform on larger, more complex environments, and what are the scalability limitations?
- Can the authors provide more detailed information on the experimental setup, such as the selection of hyperparameters and the statistical significance of the results?
