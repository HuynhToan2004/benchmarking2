 **Summary:**
The paper introduces Guided Positive Sampling Self-Supervised Learning (GPS-SSL), a novel method for positive sample selection in Self-Supervised Learning (SSL). GPS-SSL utilizes a pre-trained network to calculate the nearest neighbors of each input, which are then used to construct positive pairs. This method aims to reduce the dependence on data augmentation by leveraging prior knowledge, which is particularly beneficial for understudied datasets. The authors demonstrate the effectiveness of GPS-SSL across various datasets and SSL methods, showing improvements over traditional SSL methods. However, the paper is critiqued for its limited novelty, unclear presentation, and insufficient experimental validation.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel approach to positive sample selection in SSL by leveraging a pre-trained network, which is a significant departure from traditional methods that heavily rely on data augmentation.
- The methodology is clearly explained, with detailed examples and illustrations that aid in understanding the proposed method.
- The paper is well-written, making it accessible and easy to follow, with a clear structure that facilitates comprehension.
- The empirical results are robust, demonstrating the effectiveness of the proposed method across various datasets and SSL methods.
- The paper presents a potentially impactful contribution to the field of SSL by reducing the dependence on data augmentation, which could be particularly beneficial for understudied datasets.

**Weaknesses:**
- The novelty of the method is questioned as it appears to be a simple extension of existing methods like NNCLR, with similarities that are not adequately distinguished.
- The paper lacks a thorough analysis of the computational cost and training time associated with the use of a pre-trained network for positive sample selection.
- The paper's experimental validation is limited, with insufficient comparisons to state-of-the-art methods and a lack of comprehensive ablation studies.
- The presentation of the paper needs improvement, particularly in terms of clarity and organization, especially in the introduction and related work sections.
- The paper's methodology and results are not sufficiently novel or significant to justify a high rating, as the improvements over existing methods are not substantial enough.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors clarify the specific differences between GPS-SSL and NNCLR, particularly in terms of the use of a pre-trained network and the methodology for positive sample selection?
- How does the computational cost and training time of GPS-SSL compare to traditional SSL methods, especially when using a pre-trained network?
- Can the authors provide more detailed comparisons to state-of-the-art methods, including a broader range of datasets and SSL methods?
- How does the performance of GPS-SSL vary with different pre-trained networks, and what are the implications for the effectiveness of the method?
- Could the authors elaborate on the methodology used for positive sample selection and the impact of different pre-trained networks on the performance of GPS-SSL?
