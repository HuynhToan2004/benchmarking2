 **Summary:**
The paper introduces Signed Binarization, a novel method for binary neural network (BNN) quantization that leverages the repetition-sparsity trade-off to enhance computational efficiency. This method allows for the effective utilization of both repetition and sparsity in binary networks, which can be beneficial for deploying efficient models in resource-limited environments. The approach, which involves signed binary quantization and ternary kernels, aims to improve inference efficiency by reducing memory I/O and enhancing hardware-software system co-design. The paper presents empirical results demonstrating the method's effectiveness, showing improvements in accuracy and efficiency compared to traditional binary methods. However, it also acknowledges the limitations of the method, such as the requirement for training from scratch and potential issues with model bias.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, clear, and easy to follow, with a clear motivation and a novel approach that effectively addresses the repetition-sparsity trade-off in binary neural networks (BNNs).
- The proposed method, Signed Binarization, is technically sound and shows good empirical results, demonstrating improvements in accuracy and efficiency compared to traditional binary methods.
- The paper provides a comprehensive review of related work and effectively positions its contributions within the existing literature.
- The authors have conducted a thorough empirical evaluation, including detailed ablation studies, which support the effectiveness of their approach.
- The paper introduces a novel signed binary quantization function and a new method to prevent gradient information loss during backward propagation, which are significant contributions to the field.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed discussion on the limitations of the proposed method, such as the requirement for training from scratch and potential issues with model bias.
- The method's effectiveness in terms of inference speed is not convincingly demonstrated, with inconsistencies in reported speedups across different experiments.
- The paper does not sufficiently discuss the implications of signed binary quantization on model accuracy and bias, which are critical aspects of BNNs.
- The paper could benefit from a more detailed explanation of the experimental setup and results, particularly in the supplementary materials, to enhance reproducibility and transparency.
- The paper does not adequately address the generalizability of the method beyond convolutional layers, which limits its applicability to other types of neural networks.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors clarify the inconsistencies in reported speedups across different experiments and explain why the method is not consistently faster than binary methods?
- How does the proposed method perform in terms of accuracy and efficiency when applied to other types of neural networks beyond convolutional layers?
- What are the specific benefits of using signed binary quantization over other methods like ternary quantization, especially in terms of model accuracy and efficiency?
- Can the authors provide more details on the experimental setup, particularly the hardware and software configurations used, to enhance reproducibility and transparency?
- How does the proposed method address the repetition-sparsity trade-off in practice, and what are the implications of this trade-off for model accuracy and efficiency?
- Could the authors elaborate on the potential impact of signed binary quantization on model bias and provide empirical evidence to support their claims?
