 **Summary:**
The paper introduces Distributional Edge Layouts (DELs), a novel method for enhancing Graph Neural Networks (GNNs) by integrating edge features derived from graph layouts sampled from the Boltzmann distribution. The DELs are designed to improve GNNs by providing a richer representation of graph topology, which can be integrated into existing GNN models as a pre-processing step. The method has been tested on several benchmark datasets and shown to outperform baseline models, although concerns about the clarity of the experimental setup and the novelty of the approach have been raised.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written and easy to follow, making complex concepts accessible.
- The proposed method is simple and effective, with a straightforward integration into existing GNN models.
- The method demonstrates improvements over baseline models on several benchmark datasets, showing practical utility.
- The paper is well-organized and includes comprehensive experimental results, which support the effectiveness of the proposed method.
- The idea of using graph layouts to learn graph representations is novel and has the potential to enhance the performance of GNNs.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks clarity in explaining the motivation behind using DELs, particularly how they improve GNN performance.
- The experimental setup is not clearly described, making it difficult to replicate the results or understand the impact of different parameters.
- The paper does not adequately discuss the limitations of the proposed method, which could help in understanding its applicability and performance in different scenarios.
- The novelty of the approach is questionable as similar methods have been previously explored, such as using graph layouts to learn graph representations.
- The paper could benefit from a more detailed discussion on the computational complexity and scalability of the proposed method, especially in large-scale applications.
- The method's reliance on the Boltzmann distribution and the physical interpretation of graph layouts are not convincingly justified, raising concerns about the method's theoretical foundation.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors clarify the motivation behind using DELs and how they specifically improve GNN performance?
- How does the proposed method compare to other existing methods that use graph layouts to learn graph representations?
- What are the specific advantages of using the Boltzmann distribution in the context of graph layouts, and how does this choice affect the method's performance?
- How does the computational complexity of the proposed method scale with the size of the graph, and what are the practical implications of this complexity?
- Could the authors provide more details on the experimental setup, such as the specific GNN models used and the training parameters for the baseline models?
- How does the proposed method perform on larger, more complex graphs, and what are the potential limitations in such scenarios?
