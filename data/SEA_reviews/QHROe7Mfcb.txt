 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel one-shot-subgraph link prediction method for large-scale knowledge graphs (KGs), aiming to enhance efficiency and scalability. The approach involves sampling a subgraph based on Personalized PageRank (PPR) and then using a structural GNN for reasoning. This method is designed to address the scalability issues in existing KG link prediction methods by focusing on a smaller, query-dependent subgraph. The method's effectiveness is demonstrated through experiments on five large-scale benchmarks, showing improvements in efficiency and performance. However, concerns are raised regarding the novelty of the approach, as it seems to resemble existing methods, and the theoretical analysis provided is not robust enough to support the claims fully.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, making it easy to follow and understand.
- The proposed method is simple, intuitive, and effective, with a clear explanation of the advantages of the one-shot-subgraph link prediction approach.
- Extensive experiments are conducted, demonstrating the method's effectiveness and efficiency across five large-scale benchmarks.
- The use of PPR for subgraph sampling is innovative and contributes to the method's efficiency.
- The paper provides a comprehensive ablation study and theoretical analysis, which help in understanding the method's components and their contributions.
- The method's efficiency is highlighted through a detailed analysis of running time and memory usage, showing significant improvements in training and inference times.

**Weaknesses:**
- The novelty of the method is limited, as it appears to resemble existing methods such as GraIL and CompGCN, with similar subgraph sampling techniques.
- The paper lacks a robust theoretical analysis to support the claims of the method's efficiency and effectiveness, particularly in terms of scalability and the impact of subgraph size on performance.
- There is a lack of discussion on the limitations of the proposed method, which is crucial for understanding its applicability and potential drawbacks.
- The method's reliance on PPR for subgraph sampling might not be optimal for all types of queries or graphs, and the paper does not discuss the implications of this choice.
- The paper does not provide a detailed comparison with state-of-the-art methods, which could help in understanding the method's relative performance.
- There are some typographical errors and unclear descriptions in the paper, which could confuse readers or mislead the understanding of the method.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors clarify the novelty of the proposed method compared to existing methods like GraIL and CompGCN?
- How does the method handle the scalability issues when the graph size increases?
- What are the implications of using PPR for subgraph sampling, especially in terms of its effectiveness across different types of queries and graphs?
- Could the authors provide more details on the computational complexity of the method, particularly in terms of the time and space required for subgraph sampling and reasoning?
- How does the method perform when the subgraph size is varied, and what are the observed trends in performance?
- Could the authors provide more examples or case studies to illustrate the method's effectiveness in practical scenarios?
- Can the authors discuss the limitations of the proposed method and provide suggestions for future research to address these limitations?
