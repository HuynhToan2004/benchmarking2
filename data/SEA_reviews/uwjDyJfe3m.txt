 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a benchmark suite, B4MRL, designed to evaluate the performance of reinforcement learning (RL) agents in real-world environments with biased offline data and imperfect simulators. It highlights four challenges: simulator modeling error, partial observability, state and action discrepancies, and confounding bias. The benchmark includes datasets and simulators tailored to these challenges, and the paper presents a novel hybrid algorithm, HyCORL, to address these issues. The benchmark suite is evaluated using a variety of RL algorithms, and the results demonstrate the challenges of hybrid RL and the impact of various biases on performance. Despite its novelty, the paper has been critiqued for its lack of originality, the clarity of its presentation, and the depth of its experimental validation.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a new benchmark suite, B4MRL, which addresses significant challenges in reinforcement learning (RL) by combining offline data with imperfect simulators, focusing on simulator modeling error, partial observability, state and action discrepancies, and confounding bias.
- The paper provides a comprehensive evaluation of RL algorithms, including both model-based and model-free approaches, and compares their performance under various conditions of bias and simulator imperfections.
- The introduction of a novel hybrid algorithm, HyCORL, which learns and corrects for discrepancies between offline data and simulation, demonstrates significant performance increases on some benchmarks.
- The paper is well-structured, with a clear exposition of the challenges and a detailed description of the benchmark suite and the experimental setup.
- The inclusion of a new benchmark suite, B4MRL, and the proposed HyCORL algorithm, provide valuable resources for the RL community, potentially driving future research in this area.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks originality as it primarily evaluates existing algorithms without introducing new methods to address the challenges posed by the benchmark.
- The paper's writing and presentation are criticized for being unclear and confusing, particularly in the explanation of the benchmark challenges and the experimental setup.
- The experimental design is limited to a single environment (HalfCheetah), which may not sufficiently validate the proposed benchmarks and algorithms across different environments.
- The paper does not adequately address the issue of confounding bias, particularly in the context of offline RL, which is crucial for understanding the impact of hidden variables on performance.
- The paper's focus on a limited set of RL algorithms (TD3 and SAC) and environments (HalfCheetah) may not convincingly demonstrate the generalizability of the findings.
- The paper's claims about the necessity of the benchmarks and the novelty of the approach are not convincingly supported, and the experimental results are not sufficiently robust to validate the proposed methods.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors clarify the experimental setup and the benchmark challenges, particularly in the context of the HalfCheetah environment and the different types of biases introduced?
- How does the proposed hybrid algorithm, HyCORL, compare to other existing methods in terms of performance and computational efficiency?
- Can the authors provide a more detailed explanation of the confounding bias and its impact on the results, especially in the context of offline RL?
- Would it be possible to expand the experimental validation to include more diverse environments and a broader range of RL algorithms to better establish the generalizability of the findings?
- How do the authors address the concerns regarding the originality and depth of the experimental validation, particularly in light of the limited scope of the current study?
