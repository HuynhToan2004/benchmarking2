 **Summary:**
The paper introduces Habitat 3.0, a simulation platform designed for studying collaborative tasks between humans and robots in home environments. The platform incorporates realistic humanoid simulation, a human-in-the-loop infrastructure for real-time human-robot interaction, and supports two collaborative tasks: social navigation and social rearrangement. The paper evaluates various baselines and presents findings on the effectiveness of different policies in collaboration with humanoid agents. Despite its technical advancements and the introduction of novel features, the paper is criticized for its limited scope, lack of novelty, and inadequate comparisons with existing simulators.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, with clear descriptions of the simulator and tasks, making it easy to follow.
- The simulator, Habitat 3.0, is a significant contribution to the field of embodied AI, providing a robust framework for simulating humanoid agents in home environments.
- The inclusion of a human-in-the-loop infrastructure allows for real-time human-robot interaction, enhancing the study of collaborative tasks.
- The paper introduces two collaborative tasks, social navigation and social rearrangement, which are well-explained and supported by comprehensive evaluations and ablations.
- The paper presents a variety of baselines and conducts human-in-the-loop experiments, which are valuable for understanding the performance of different policies in collaboration with humanoid agents.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed comparison with existing simulators, particularly in terms of performance, speed, and feature sets.
- The novelty of the paper is questioned, as it primarily introduces new features to an existing simulator (Habitat 3.0) without significant innovation in the underlying technology.
- The scope of the paper is limited, focusing on a narrow set of tasks and not exploring a broader range of collaborative tasks or environments.
- The paper does not adequately address the generalization of the simulated environment to real-world scenarios, which is crucial for practical applications of the research.
- The paper's evaluation methods and metrics are not thoroughly explained or justified, which makes it difficult to assess the validity and reliability of the results.
- There is a lack of discussion on the limitations of the proposed approach, which could help in understanding the scope and applicability of the findings.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors provide a detailed comparison of Habitat 3.0 with other existing simulators, particularly in terms of performance, speed, and feature sets?
- How does the paper address the generalization of the simulated environment to real-world scenarios?
- Can the authors clarify the novelty of their contributions to the field of embodied AI and discuss how these contributions advance the state-of-the-art?
- How does the paper ensure that the simulated environment accurately models real-world scenarios, and what steps are taken to validate the simulation results against real-world data?
- Could the authors provide more details on the evaluation methods and metrics used in the paper, particularly how these metrics are derived and how they contribute to the overall assessment of the system's performance?
- How does the paper address the limitations of its approach, and what are the potential impacts of these limitations on the practical application of the findings?
