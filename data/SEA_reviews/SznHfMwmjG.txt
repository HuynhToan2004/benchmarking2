 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel set of metrics to evaluate the sparsity and linearity of neural network activations in language models, particularly focusing on sparse coding. The authors propose four metrics to measure the success of sparse coding and demonstrate their effectiveness on synthetic data and various language models. The study reveals that activations are most sparse in the first and last layers, with intermediate layers showing less sparsity. The paper also discusses the relationship between sparsity and model depth, suggesting that sparsity decreases as layers deepen before increasing again. The proposed metrics are evaluated against both synthetic and real-world data, showing promising results. However, the paper's novelty is questioned due to the similarity of some metrics to existing works, and the clarity of the paper's contributions and methodology could be improved.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a new set of metrics to evaluate the sparsity of neural network activations, which is a significant contribution to the field of neural network interpretability.
- The proposed metrics are tested on both synthetic and real-world data, providing a robust validation of their effectiveness.
- The paper is well-written and easy to follow, making it accessible to a broad audience.
- The experiments are thorough and demonstrate that the proposed metrics can accurately measure the sparsity of activations in language models.
- The paper provides a novel approach to measuring sparsity in neural network activations, which is crucial for understanding and interpreting the behavior of neural networks.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper's novelty is limited as some of the proposed metrics are similar to those in previous works, such as Sharkey et al. (2022), which were not adequately cited or compared.
- The paper lacks a clear explanation of its contributions and how they differ from existing methods.
- The paper's methodology and the definitions of key concepts, such as sparsity, are not clearly articulated, making it difficult for readers to fully understand the authors' claims.
- The paper's focus on sparse coding as the sole method for interpreting neural network activations may be too narrow, as other methods like attention maps and saliency maps could also be relevant.
- The paper's claims about the sparsity of activations in different layers of neural networks are not convincingly supported by the data, and the results are not adequately explained or interpreted.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors clarify the novelty of their metrics compared to those in previous works, such as Sharkey et al. (2022)?
- How do the authors define and measure sparsity in their experiments, and how does this definition compare to other definitions in the literature?
- Could the authors provide more detailed explanations of the sparsity metrics and how they are calculated, especially in the context of the experiments presented?
- How do the authors address the potential limitations of their metrics, such as their applicability to different types of neural networks or their effectiveness in different scenarios?
- Can the authors provide more detailed interpretations of the results presented in the paper, particularly the findings on the sparsity of activations in different layers of neural networks?
