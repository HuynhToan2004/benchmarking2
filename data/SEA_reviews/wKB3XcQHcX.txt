 **Summary:**
The paper explores the training dynamics of neural networks from a thermodynamic perspective, focusing on the speed at which networks transition from initial to final distributions. It derives theoretical expressions for the speed limits of learning using both gradient-flow and Langevin dynamics, particularly in the context of linear and linearizable neural networks. The authors apply these concepts to practical settings like the Neural Tangent Kernel (NTK) regime, demonstrating that the training process can be optimized within a constant factor. The paper also includes numerical experiments on CIFAR-10, albeit with limited scope and practical applicability. Despite its theoretical depth, the paper is criticized for its limited empirical validation and the unclear relevance of the derived speed limits to practical neural network training scenarios.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel application of thermodynamic speed limits to the training of neural networks, providing a unique perspective on the optimization of learning dynamics.
- The theoretical results are well-presented, with clear explanations of the underlying concepts and their implications, making the paper accessible to a broad audience.
- The connection between neural network training and thermodynamics is explored in depth, offering a fresh perspective on the optimization of learning dynamics.
- The paper is well-written, with clear explanations and intuitive examples that aid in understanding complex concepts.
- The authors provide a detailed analysis of the speed limits in learning, which could potentially influence the design of more efficient training algorithms.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper's practical relevance is limited, as the derived speed limits do not provide concrete guidance for improving practical training algorithms.
- The empirical validation of the theoretical results is insufficient, with only small-scale experiments conducted on CIFAR-10, which may not adequately represent real-world neural network training scenarios.
- The paper lacks a comprehensive literature review, missing out on relevant works that could have enriched the discussion and contextualized the research.
- The relevance of the derived speed limits to practical neural network training is questionable, as the theoretical results are not directly applicable to typical training scenarios.
- The paper's focus on the Neural Tangent Kernel (NTK) regime may limit its applicability to more general neural network training scenarios.
- The paper's experimental setup and the choice of parameters are not clearly justified, which could limit the generalizability of the findings.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors clarify the relevance and practical implications of the derived speed limits for neural network training?
- How do the theoretical results compare to typical neural network training scenarios, particularly in terms of the applicability of the derived speed limits?
- Could the authors provide more detailed experimental results, possibly including larger-scale experiments or a broader range of network architectures to validate the theoretical findings?
- How does the paper's theoretical framework relate to existing works on the optimization of learning dynamics, and what are the novel contributions of this study?
- Could the authors elaborate on the choice of parameters and experimental settings, particularly in the context of the NTK regime, to better understand the assumptions and limitations of the theoretical analysis?
