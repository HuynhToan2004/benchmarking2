 **Summary:**
The paper explores the internal spatial and temporal representations in large language models (LLMs) by training linear probes on hidden activations to predict real-world locations and time stamps. The authors demonstrate that these representations are linear and robust to variations in prompts, suggesting that LLMs can learn world models. They also identify "space neurons" and "time neurons" that encode spatial and temporal coordinates, respectively. The study is based on six datasets, including geographical and historical data, and shows that LLMs can learn representations that generalize across different types of entities. However, the paper's conclusions are limited by the small size of the datasets and the simplicity of the probing method, which may not fully capture the complexity of the model's internal representations.

**Strengths:**
- The paper addresses an important question about the internal representations of large language models (LLMs) and provides a clear and well-structured presentation of the results.
- The experiments are well-designed, and the paper includes a variety of datasets and model sizes, which supports the generalization of the findings across different models and datasets.
- The paper includes a thorough discussion of the limitations, which is commendable for its transparency and honesty in reporting the scope of the work.
- The results are presented in a clear and organized manner, with figures and tables that effectively illustrate the findings.
- The paper includes a range of probing methods, such as linear probing, MLP probing, and principal component analysis (PCA), which provides a comprehensive understanding of the model's internal representations.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper's conclusions are limited by the small size of the datasets used, which may not adequately represent the complexity of the real world.
- The probing method used is quite simple, and it is unclear whether more complex methods could reveal more nuanced or detailed information about the model's internal representations.
- The paper's findings are somewhat limited to the specific datasets and models used, and it is unclear whether the conclusions would hold for other types of data or models.
- The paper's discussion on the limitations of the work is detailed but could be better integrated into the main text to enhance the flow of the paper.
- The paper lacks a detailed discussion on how the findings relate to prior work on LLMs, which could help contextualize the current study within the existing literature.
- The paper does not adequately address the issue of train-test leakage, which could affect the reliability of the results.
