 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel conditional generative model named RAISE, designed to solve abstract reasoning problems using Raven's Progressive Matrices (RPM). RAISE encodes image attributes as latent concepts and decomposes underlying rules into atomic rules, abstracted as global learnable parameters. The model predicts target images by selecting appropriate rules from a global knowledge set, thereby generating answers in the latent space. RAISE demonstrates superior performance compared to other generative solvers in tasks involving generating bottom-right and arbitrary-position answers. It also shows effectiveness in handling unseen combinations of rules and attributes, showcasing its ability to generalize well. Despite its strengths, the paper could benefit from clearer presentation and a more comprehensive discussion on the limitations and potential societal impacts of the model.

**Strengths:**
- The proposed RAISE model is innovative, utilizing a conditional generative model to solve answer generation problems through rule abstraction and selection in the latent space.
- RAISE is well-motivated, with a clear and straightforward methodology, and the paper is well-written, making it easy to follow.
- The model demonstrates superior performance compared to other generative solvers, particularly in tasks involving generating bottom-right and arbitrary-position answers.
- RAISE shows effectiveness in handling unseen combinations of rules and attributes, demonstrating its ability to generalize well.
- The paper provides a detailed description of the methodology, including the derivation of the ELBO, which is a significant contribution to the field.
- The results show that RAISE outperforms other models in both bottom-right answer selection and arbitrary-position answer selection, indicating its effectiveness in solving RPM problems.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper could benefit from a clearer presentation, especially in the methodology section, to make it more accessible to readers.
- The paper lacks a discussion on the limitations of the proposed method, which is crucial for understanding the scope and applicability of the model.
- There is a need for more comprehensive comparisons with other generative models, especially in terms of performance and computational efficiency.
- The paper does not sufficiently discuss the potential societal impacts of the model, which is a significant omission given the potential applications of the technology.
- The paper could benefit from a more detailed explanation of the technical details, such as the role of the encoder in the model and the computational complexity of the model.
- The experimental setup could be improved by including more diverse datasets and scenarios to better evaluate the model's performance and generalizability.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors clarify the role of the encoder in the model and how it impacts the performance of RAISE?
- How does RAISE handle scenarios where the rules are not well-defined or ambiguous?
- Can the authors provide more details on the computational complexity of the model and how it scales with the size of the input data?
- How does RAISE perform in scenarios where the rules are not well-defined or ambiguous?
