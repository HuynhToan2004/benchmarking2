 **Summary:**
The paper investigates the memorization behavior in diffusion models, specifically focusing on the Effective Model Memorization (EMM) metric which quantifies the maximum size of training data at which a model approximates its theoretical optimum. The study explores the impact of various factors such as data distribution, model configuration, and training procedures on EMM. It finds that conditioning training data on uninformative random labels can trigger memorization in diffusion models. The paper also introduces the concept of EMM to measure the maximum dataset size at which diffusion models demonstrate behavior similar to the theoretical optimum, which is crucial for understanding the memorization behavior in diffusion models.

**Strengths:**
- The paper provides a comprehensive empirical study on memorization behavior in diffusion models, which is an interesting and important topic for the research community.
- The authors introduce the concept of Effective Model Memorization (EMM) to measure the maximum dataset size at which diffusion models demonstrate behavior similar to the theoretical optimum, which is a significant contribution to the field.
- The paper is well-written, clear, and easy to follow, with detailed descriptions of experiments and results.
- The study includes various factors such as data distribution, model configuration, and training procedures, which helps in understanding the memorization behavior in diffusion models.
- The paper is well-motivated and addresses an important problem in the field of diffusion models, specifically focusing on the memorization behavior which is crucial for understanding the generalization ability of these models.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper could benefit from a more detailed discussion on the limitations and assumptions of the EMM metric, particularly how it applies to different types of diffusion models and data distributions.
- The paper lacks a thorough theoretical analysis on the memorization behavior in diffusion models, which could provide a deeper understanding of the observed phenomena.
