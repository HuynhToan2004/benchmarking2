 **Summary:**
The paper addresses the issue of disparate impact in model pruning, focusing on the accuracy gaps between dense and sparse models across different subgroups. It introduces a constrained optimization problem to mitigate these disparities by controlling the group-level accuracy gaps. The proposed method involves a replay buffer to enhance the accuracy of the constraints during training, which is demonstrated to be effective in reducing disparate impact on training data. The paper also discusses the limitations of the approach, including potential generalization issues and the need for further validation on more diverse datasets.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, clear, and easy to follow, with a clear motivation and well-structured presentation.
- The proposed method is novel, addressing the disparate impact of model pruning by controlling the group-level accuracy gaps, which is a significant contribution to the field.
- The use of a replay buffer to enhance the accuracy of the constraints during training is a practical and innovative approach that enhances the training dynamics.
- The paper provides a thorough discussion on the limitations of the proposed method, which shows a commitment to transparency and honesty in reporting.
- The experiments demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed method in reducing disparate impact on training data, showing a clear improvement over baseline methods.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed discussion on the generalization of the proposed method to unseen data, which is a critical aspect for practical applications.
- The methodology section could benefit from more detailed explanations, particularly regarding the choice of the replay buffer and its impact on the training dynamics.
- The paper primarily focuses on the disparate impact of model pruning, which may limit its applicability to other fairness notions.
- There is a need for more extensive experiments on diverse datasets and different model architectures to validate the effectiveness of the proposed method across different scenarios.
- The paper does not adequately address the scalability of the proposed method, particularly in terms of computational and memory costs, which could limit its practical implementation in resource-constrained environments.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors elaborate on the choice of the replay buffer and its impact on the training dynamics?
- How does the proposed method perform when applied to other fairness notions beyond disparate impact?
- Can the authors provide more details on the generalization of the proposed method to unseen data, and discuss potential strategies to improve its generalization performance?
- How does the proposed method compare in terms of computational and memory costs to other existing methods, and what are the implications for practical deployment in resource-constrained environments?
- Could the authors discuss the potential of extending the proposed method to other types of fairness notions, such as demographic parity or equalized odds?
