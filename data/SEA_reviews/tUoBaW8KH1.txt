 **Summary:**
The paper investigates the impact of activation functions on the smoothness of node features in Graph Convolutional Networks (GCNs), specifically focusing on ReLU and leaky ReLU. It introduces a novel method called Smoothness Control Term (SCT) to manage the smoothness of node features, aiming to prevent over-smoothing and improve performance in graph node classification tasks. The authors demonstrate that SCT can be integrated into various GCN-style models to enhance their performance on datasets like Cora, Citeseer, and Pubmed. Theoretical insights are provided on how activation functions affect smoothness, and empirical results show improvements in accuracy and computational efficiency.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, making it easy to follow, and the theoretical proofs are rigorous and sound.
- The introduction of a new method called SCT to control the smoothness of node features in GCNs is a significant contribution to the field.
- The experimental results demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed SCT, showing improvements in accuracy and computational efficiency.
- The paper provides a clear explanation of how activation functions affect the smoothness of node features, which is a novel perspective in understanding GCNs.
- The paper is well-organized, with a clear structure and logical flow, making it accessible to a broad audience.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a thorough discussion on how the proposed method compares to other state-of-the-art methods, particularly in terms of computational efficiency and accuracy.
- There is a lack of clarity on how the smoothness of node features is measured and whether it is a desirable property for all datasets.
- The paper does not sufficiently address the limitations of the proposed method, particularly in scenarios where it may not perform optimally.
- The experimental results do not convincingly demonstrate the superiority of the proposed method over existing methods, and the improvements in accuracy are marginal in some cases.
- The paper could benefit from a more comprehensive analysis of the computational complexity and memory requirements of the proposed method compared to other GCN variants.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors provide more details on how the smoothness of node features is measured and whether it is a desirable property for all datasets?
- How does the proposed method compare to other state-of-the-art methods in terms of computational efficiency and accuracy?
- Could the authors provide a more detailed discussion on the limitations of the proposed method and when it might not perform optimally?
- How does the proposed method handle the issue of over-smoothing in GCNs, and can it be extended to other activation functions beyond ReLU and leaky ReLU?
- Can the authors clarify the experimental setup, particularly the choice of hyperparameters and the dataset splits used?
- How does the proposed method scale with larger graph sizes and more complex datasets, and what are the computational and memory requirements compared to other GCN variants?
