 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a multi-resolution pre-training framework for speech, named Multi-Resolution HuBERT (MR-HuBERT), which processes speech signals at multiple resolutions, enhancing the efficiency and performance of speech representation learning. MR-HuBERT leverages a hierarchical Transformer architecture and HuBERT-style masked prediction objectives to process speech signals at various resolutions, achieving superior or comparable performance to the original HuBERT model across various tasks such as speech recognition and enhancement. The model is trained on datasets like LibriSpeech, LibriLight, and Voxpopuli, demonstrating improvements in tasks like speech recognition and enhancement.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel multi-resolution pre-training framework for speech, which effectively enhances the efficiency and performance of speech representation learning.
- The proposed Multi-Resolution HuBERT (MR-HuBERT) model shows superior or comparable performance to the original HuBERT model across various tasks, demonstrating its effectiveness.
- The paper is well-written, easy to follow, and provides extensive experimental results and ablation studies, which support the proposed method's effectiveness.
- The introduction of a hierarchical Transformer architecture and HuBERT-style masked prediction objectives allows for the processing of speech signals at multiple resolutions, which is a significant advancement in speech representation learning.
- The paper includes comprehensive experimental results, including those from the SUPERB benchmark, which demonstrate the robustness and effectiveness of the proposed method.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a clear motivation for the multi-resolution pre-training, and the improvements in performance are not consistently significant across all tasks.
- The novelty of the paper is limited as similar ideas have been previously explored in other works, such as Shi et al. (2023c).
- The paper could benefit from more detailed explanations of the model's architecture and the training process, particularly the role of the K-means model in the pre-training stage.
- The paper does not provide sufficient comparative analysis with other existing methods, such as WavLM, which also processes speech signals at multiple resolutions.
- The paper lacks a discussion on the potential limitations or challenges associated with the proposed method, which could help in understanding its applicability and effectiveness in real-world scenarios.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors provide more detailed explanations of the model's architecture and the training process, particularly the role of the K-means model in the pre-training stage?
- How does the proposed method compare to other existing methods, such as WavLM, in terms of performance and efficiency?
- What are the specific advantages of using a multi-resolution approach over a single-resolution approach in speech processing?
- How does the proposed method handle the issue of short-term stationarity in speech signals, which could affect the performance of speech recognition tasks?
- Can the authors discuss the potential limitations or challenges associated with the proposed method, and how these might affect its applicability and effectiveness in real-world scenarios?
