 **Summary:**
The paper introduces MgNO, a novel neural operator architecture that leverages multigrid structures to parameterize linear operators between neurons, offering a more efficient and expressive approach to operator learning. The authors claim that MgNO eliminates the need for conventional lifting and projecting operators, improving the efficiency and expressivity of neural operators. The method is evaluated across various partial differential equations (PDEs) and shows superior performance in terms of training ease and resistance to overfitting. Despite these strengths, concerns are raised about the clarity of the presentation, the completeness of the experimental validation, and the novelty of the method compared to existing approaches like Fourier neural operators (FNO).

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel neural operator architecture, MgNO, which utilizes multigrid structures to parameterize linear operators between neurons, offering a more efficient and expressive approach to operator learning.
- MgNO eliminates the need for conventional lifting and projecting operators, which is a significant improvement in the field of neural operators.
- The paper provides a thorough literature review, positioning the proposed method within the context of existing approaches and highlighting its advantages.
- The paper includes comprehensive experimental results and ablation studies, demonstrating the effectiveness of the proposed method in various PDEs and benchmarks.
- The paper is well-written, making it easy to follow and understand, which is crucial for a technical paper.
- The method shows superior ease of training compared to CNN-based models and reduced susceptibility to overfitting when contrasted with spectral-type neural operators.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks clarity in explaining the key differences between the proposed method and FNO, particularly in how MgNO achieves a universal approximation property.
- The paper does not adequately address the limitations of the proposed method, which could include the handling of non-linear PDEs, the complexity of the multigrid structure, and the computational efficiency compared to other methods.
- The paper does not include a detailed comparison with other methods, such as DiResNet and MWT, which could provide a more comprehensive evaluation of the proposed method.
- The paper does not discuss the limitations of the proposed method in detail, which is crucial for understanding its applicability and potential drawbacks.
- The paper does not provide a clear explanation of how the method can be applied to different types of PDEs, especially those with complex boundary conditions.
- The paper does not include a discussion on the potential negative societal impact of the proposed method.

**Questions:**
- How does the proposed method compare to other methods like FNO, particularly in terms of computational efficiency and expressivity?
- Can you provide more details on how the multigrid structure is implemented in the MgNO architecture, including the number of levels and channels used?
- How does the proposed method handle non-linear PDEs, and what are the potential challenges or limitations in such scenarios?
- Can you clarify the role of the non-linear point-wise activation function in the MgNO architecture, and how it contributes to the overall performance of the method?
- How does the method perform when applied to different types of PDEs, especially those with complex boundary conditions?
- Could you provide more details on the computational complexity of the MgNO architecture compared to other methods, and how this affects the practical applicability of the method?
- Can you discuss the potential negative societal impact of the proposed method, and how this was considered in the development and evaluation of the method?
