 **Summary:**
The paper investigates the effectiveness of visual prompt tuning (VPT) in vision transformers, comparing it to traditional full finetuning (FT) across various datasets and tasks. The study explores scenarios where VPT outperforms FT, particularly when there is a significant disparity in task objectives or similarities in data distributions. The authors propose that VPT's success is not solely attributed to overfitting or optimization considerations but rather its ability to preserve original features and introduce new parameters. The paper also discusses the impact of dataset disparities and data scales on the performance of VPT.

**Strengths:**
- The paper addresses a significant and relevant topic by exploring the conditions under which visual prompt tuning (VPT) is most effective.
- The experiments are well-designed, with a thorough analysis of the results, and the paper is well-written, making it easy to follow.
- The study provides valuable insights into the performance of VPT under different conditions, which can guide researchers in choosing the most appropriate method for their specific tasks.
- The paper includes a comprehensive evaluation across multiple datasets and tasks, offering a robust assessment of VPT's effectiveness.
- The visualizations and explanations provided, such as the GradCAM results, help to clarify the contributions of VPT in improving model performance.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed discussion on the limitations of VPT, particularly in scenarios where it may not be the most effective approach.
- There is a need for more diverse and extensive experimental setups, including different backbone models and training objectives, to validate the findings more robustly.
- The paper could benefit from a more in-depth analysis of the reasons behind VPT's success, particularly the role of the pretrained model in capturing general features and the additional parameters in encoding task information.
- The presentation of some figures and tables, such as Figure 3 and Table 2, could be improved for better clarity and understanding.
- The paper does not adequately address the impact of different data distributions on the performance of VPT, which is crucial for understanding its applicability across various datasets.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors provide more details on the specific scenarios where VPT fails to outperform FT, and what factors contribute to this?
- How does the performance of VPT compare when using different backbone models, such as Swin, and different training objectives, like MAE?
- Could the authors elaborate on the role of the pretrained model in capturing general features and the additional parameters in encoding task information?
- Is there a possibility to include more diverse datasets in future experiments to validate the findings more comprehensively?
- Can the authors clarify the discrepancies in the performance of VPT across different datasets, particularly those with similar data distributions?
- How does the paper address the potential for overfitting in VPT, especially in scenarios where the data distribution is similar?
