 **Summary:**
The paper introduces AutoVP, a framework designed to automate visual prompting (VP) for image classification tasks. AutoVP optimizes hyperparameters such as input scaling, visual prompts, pre-trained models, and output label mapping, offering a benchmark for evaluating VP performance across 12 datasets. The framework is claimed to outperform existing VP methods and linear probing, demonstrating a significant improvement in accuracy in some cases. However, concerns are raised about the novelty of the approach, as it primarily combines existing methods without significant innovation. The paper's contribution is seen as incremental rather than groundbreaking, and the experimental validation, while thorough, does not fully address the claimed performance improvements.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-organized and clearly written, making it easy to follow.
- It addresses an important problem in the field of visual prompting by combining multiple design choices into a single framework.
- The proposed method shows promising results, particularly in terms of performance improvements over existing methods.
- The paper includes a comprehensive set of experiments and ablation studies, which provide valuable insights into the effectiveness of the proposed method.
- The methodology is straightforward and intuitive, which could make it accessible for further research and application.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks novelty as it primarily combines existing methods without significant innovation.
- The methodology's contribution is seen as incremental rather than groundbreaking, with the primary focus being on the automation of hyperparameter tuning rather than the development of new techniques.
- The paper does not sufficiently address the limitations of the proposed method, particularly concerning the scalability and generalizability of the findings.
- There is a lack of detailed analysis and discussion on the performance improvements and the role of different components of the framework in achieving these improvements.
- The paper could benefit from a more thorough discussion on the limitations of the proposed method and a more detailed analysis of the experimental results.

**Questions:**
- How does the proposed method compare to other state-of-the-art methods in terms of performance and computational efficiency?
- Can the authors provide more details on the experimental setup, particularly how the hyperparameters were tuned and how this affects the results?
- How does the proposed method perform in more complex or diverse datasets, and what are the implications of these findings for the generalizability of the results?
- What are the specific contributions of the proposed method beyond the automation of hyperparameter tuning, and how do these contributions advance the field of visual prompting?
- Could the authors elaborate on the role of different components of the framework in achieving the observed performance improvements, and discuss potential limitations or challenges in applying the method to other tasks or datasets?
