 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel methodology, MC-SMoE, for merging and compressing Sparsely Activated Mixture-of-Experts (SMoE) to address the challenges of high memory usage and redundancy in SMoE models. The methodology involves merging experts based on routing statistics and compressing the merged experts using low-rank decomposition. The proposed methodology is evaluated on eight benchmarks, showing significant improvements in memory and computational efficiency without significant loss in performance. The paper also provides extensive experimental results and ablation studies, which demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed method.

**Strengths:**
- The paper addresses a significant and timely problem in the field of large language models (LLMs) by proposing a novel methodology, MC-SMoE, for merging and compressing SMoE models.
- The methodology is well-motivated, clearly explained, and supported by extensive experimental results and ablation studies, demonstrating the effectiveness of the proposed approach.
- The paper is well-written, making it accessible and easy to follow, with clear explanations of the methodology and its implementation.
- The proposed methodology is novel, combining existing ideas in a novel way to achieve significant improvements in memory and computational efficiency.
- The paper provides a thorough evaluation of the proposed methodology, demonstrating its effectiveness across a variety of benchmarks and scenarios.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed discussion on the limitations of the proposed methodology, which could help in understanding its applicability and potential drawbacks.
- The methodology assumes a fixed number of experts per layer, which may not be optimal in all scenarios. A more adaptive approach could potentially improve the results.
- The paper could benefit from a more detailed comparison with other baseline models, particularly in terms of model size and performance.
- The paper could provide more insights into the impact of the proposed methodology on different types of LLMs, such as encoder-only or decoder-only models.
- The paper could benefit from a more detailed explanation of the experimental setup and the results, particularly in terms of the impact of different hyperparameters and model configurations.
- The paper could provide more details on the computational overhead and inference time of the proposed methodology, which could be crucial for practical applications.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors provide more details on the computational overhead and inference time of the proposed methodology?
- How does the proposed methodology perform when applied to different types of LLMs, such as encoder-only or decoder-only models?
- Could the authors elaborate on the impact of different hyperparameters and model configurations on the performance of the proposed methodology?
- How does the proposed methodology compare with other baseline models in terms of model size and performance?
- Could the authors provide more insights into the limitations of the proposed methodology and potential areas for future research?
