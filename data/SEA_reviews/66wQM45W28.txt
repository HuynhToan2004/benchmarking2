 **Summary:**
The paper introduces CEDNet, a cascade encoder-decoder network designed for dense prediction tasks such as object detection, instance segmentation, and semantic segmentation. CEDNet utilizes a novel multi-scale feature fusion mechanism within the decoder, which is claimed to be more effective than traditional methods that fuse features after the classification backbone. The network architecture is evaluated on various datasets, showing improvements over existing methods. However, the novelty of the approach is questioned due to its similarity to existing methods like FPN, and the paper's experimental validation is considered insufficient.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written and easy to follow, with a clear introduction of the CEDNet model.
- The proposed method, CEDNet, is simple and effective, and shows good performance on dense prediction tasks such as object detection, instance segmentation, and semantic segmentation.
- Extensive experiments are conducted to verify the effectiveness of the proposed method, including detailed ablation studies.
- The paper provides a thorough evaluation of the proposed method, demonstrating its effectiveness and potential for application in various dense prediction tasks.

**Weaknesses:**
- The novelty of the proposed method is limited as it resembles existing methods like FPN, particularly in the feature fusion process.
- The paper lacks sufficient experimental validation, particularly in terms of comparisons with state-of-the-art methods and a detailed analysis of the results.
- The paper does not adequately address the computational complexity of the proposed method, which could be a significant concern for real-time applications.
- The motivation behind the proposed method is not convincingly explained, particularly the rationale behind the design choices such as the cascade encoder-decoder structure.
- The paper could benefit from a more comprehensive literature review and a clearer explanation of the differences between the proposed method and existing methods like FPN.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors provide a more detailed comparison with state-of-the-art methods, including a head-to-head comparison in terms of performance and computational efficiency?
- How does the proposed method perform in terms of inference speed, and what are the specific computational costs compared to other methods?
- Could the authors provide a more detailed analysis of the results, particularly the performance gains and the impact of different design choices on the results?
- How does the proposed method compare to other existing methods in terms of feature fusion efficiency and the integration of high-level features into low-level feature learning?
- Can the authors clarify the motivation behind the cascade encoder-decoder structure and the specific advantages it offers over other existing methods?
