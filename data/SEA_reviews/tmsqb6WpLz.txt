 **Summary:**
The paper investigates the impact of fine-tuning language models (LMs) on topic, style, and factual knowledge, employing ChatGPT to generate controlled-variable text examples. The study reveals that fine-tuning shifts language models' topic and style priors, with factual knowledge learning contributing minimally to the total probability change. It also explores how different factors like learning rate and model capacity influence the learning dynamics. The paper is well-organized, with clear visualizations and methodologies, and provides valuable insights into the effects of fine-tuning on LMs. However, the paper's generalizability to larger models and different domains is questioned, and the methodology's novelty and the depth of analysis are seen as limited.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-organized, easy to follow, and the methodology is clearly described, making it accessible to a broad audience.
- The experiments are well-designed, and the findings are supported by clear visualizations, which aid in understanding the results.
- The topic of the paper is relevant and timely, addressing an important aspect of language model fine-tuning, which is crucial for practical applications.
- The paper provides a detailed analysis of how different factors, such as learning rate, model capacity, and token position, influence the learning dynamics of language models.
- The paper is well-written, with a clear structure and logical flow, which enhances the reader's understanding of the content.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper's findings are limited to smaller models (7B and 13B), and it's unclear how the results would generalize to larger models.
- The methodology used to generate the derived datasets is not thoroughly discussed, which could lead to questions about the reliability and validity of the results.
- The novelty of the paper is questioned, as the methodology and findings are seen as similar to previous works, particularly in the area of continual learning.
- The paper does not adequately address how the findings could be applied to improve language models or mitigate the observed issues such as forgetting.
- The analysis is limited to specific domains and datasets, which may not be generalizable to other types of data or domains.
- The paper's contributions are seen as incremental and not sufficiently novel to warrant high impact or acceptance at the conference.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors clarify how the derived datasets were generated and ensure that they are not biased towards certain topics or styles?
- How do the authors justify the use of specific models (GPT-2 XL, LLaMA 2 7B, and LLaMA 2 13B) for their experiments, and what would be the impact if different models were used?
- How does the paper's methodology compare to other existing methods in the field of continual learning, and what are the unique contributions of this study?
- Could the authors provide more detailed insights into how the findings could be applied to improve language models or mitigate the observed issues?
- The paper's conclusions suggest that factual knowledge is learned slowly and requires significant model capacity. Can the authors elaborate on how this finding could be leveraged to improve the learning of factual knowledge in language models?
- Given the limitations of the study, such as its focus on smaller models and specific datasets, how do the authors plan to extend their research to larger models and more diverse datasets?
