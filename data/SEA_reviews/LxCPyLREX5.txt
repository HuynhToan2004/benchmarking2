 **Summary:**
The paper explores the challenges of federated learning (FL) under label shifts, focusing on the estimation of the overall true risk minimizer across multiple clients. It introduces a novel method called Variational Regularized Label Shift (VRLS), which leverages a regularization term to optimize the ratio of marginal label distributions. The VRLS method is evaluated on various datasets, including MNIST, Fashion MNIST, and CIFAR-10, demonstrating superior performance over existing methods. The paper also provides theoretical guarantees on the estimation errors and communication overhead, which are crucial for federated learning applications. The methodology is supported by both theoretical analysis and empirical results, highlighting its practical utility in real-world scenarios.

**Strengths:**
- The paper addresses a significant and relevant problem in federated learning, focusing on the estimation of the overall true risk minimizer under label shifts, which is a critical aspect of federated learning.
- The methodology is well-explained, with clear theoretical analysis and empirical results that demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed method.
- The paper is well-structured and easy to follow, with clear notation and a clear presentation of the methodology and results.
- The proposed method, VRLS, is novel and shows superior performance over existing methods, particularly in handling label shifts in federated learning.
- The paper provides both theoretical analysis and empirical results, which are critical for understanding the practical applicability and effectiveness of the proposed method.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper could benefit from a more detailed discussion on the limitations of the proposed method, particularly in scenarios where the method may not perform optimally.
- There are some notational issues and inconsistencies in the paper that could confuse readers. For example, the notation for the loss function and the estimation of the overall true risk minimizer is not clearly defined.
- The paper lacks a comprehensive comparison with other existing methods, which could help to better understand the relative performance of the proposed method.
