 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel method for single-image 3D reconstruction, utilizing a two-stage process that optimizes both 3D structural and 2D texture details. The method leverages a CLIP-based adaptive detection mechanism to ensure a case-aware approach, allowing for dynamic prior optimization based on the progress of the optimization process. The paper also includes extensive experiments and ablation studies, demonstrating the method's effectiveness in generating 3D assets from a single image. Despite the innovative approach and the method's reported effectiveness, concerns were raised regarding the novelty and clarity of the presentation, with some reviewers questioning the method's incremental nature and the lack of detailed comparative analysis with existing methods.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel approach to single-image 3D reconstruction, employing a two-stage method that effectively optimizes both 3D structural and 2D texture details.
- The method's use of a CLIP-based adaptive detection mechanism for case-aware optimization is innovative and enhances the method's applicability across various objects.
- The paper is well-written, making it easy to follow, and includes extensive experiments and ablation studies that demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed method.
- The paper's results show that the proposed method outperforms existing state-of-the-art methods, as evidenced by both quantitative and qualitative comparisons.
- The authors provide a detailed analysis of the limitations of their method, which is commendable for its transparency and honesty.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed discussion on the limitations of the method, which could provide valuable insights for future research.
- The method's novelty is questioned due to its similarity to existing methods such as DreamFusion, with the main difference being the use of a CLIP-based adaptive detection mechanism.
- The paper does not provide sufficient quantitative comparisons with existing methods, which makes it difficult to assess the method's performance objectively.
- There is a lack of clarity in the presentation of the method, particularly in the description of the optimization boundary judgment and dynamic prior, which could benefit from more detailed explanations or illustrations.
- The paper does not adequately address the generalization ability of the method across different types of objects, raising concerns about its applicability in real-world scenarios.
- The paper's reliance on pre-trained models for certain components, such as depth and binary mask estimation, could limit its flexibility and applicability in different scenarios.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors provide more detailed explanations or illustrations for the optimization boundary judgment and dynamic prior sections to enhance clarity and understanding?
- How does the proposed method compare quantitatively with existing methods in terms of performance metrics such as CLIP-similarity, PSNR, and LPIPS?
- What are the specific advantages of using a CLIP-based adaptive detection mechanism over other methods for case-aware optimization?
- How does the method handle the generalization ability across different types of objects, and what steps have been taken to ensure its applicability in diverse scenarios?
- Could the authors elaborate on the limitations of their method and discuss potential future research directions to address these limitations?
