 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel approach to visual localization by leveraging synthetic images generated using text-to-image models to enhance the robustness of retrieval models against variations in weather, time of day, and season. This is achieved by integrating these synthetic images into the training dataset, which helps in improving the model's performance under different conditions. The paper also explores the use of geometric consistency scores to filter out synthetic images that do not preserve key scene features. Extensive experiments on various datasets demonstrate the effectiveness of this approach, showing significant improvements over existing methods. The methodology is straightforward, using existing models like HOW and InstructPix2Pix, and the results are promising, although some concerns about the novelty and the practical implications of using synthetic data are raised.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, clear, and easy to follow, making complex concepts accessible.
- The proposed method is simple yet effective, utilizing a straightforward approach that enhances the retrieval model's robustness to variations in weather, time of day, and season.
- The paper is well-motivated, with a clear focus on improving the robustness of retrieval models to adversarial conditions.
- The methodology is well-supported by extensive experiments, which demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed approach.
- The use of synthetic images generated by text-to-image models is a novel approach that has not been extensively explored in the context of visual localization.
- The paper provides a detailed analysis of the method's effectiveness, including the use of geometric consistency scores to filter out synthetic images that do not preserve key scene features.

**Weaknesses:**
- The novelty of the paper is somewhat limited as the use of synthetic images and generative models has been explored in other contexts.
- The paper does not sufficiently compare the proposed method with other state-of-the-art methods, such as those using semantic-aware detectors or other generative models.
- There is a lack of discussion on the potential negative societal impacts of using synthetic data, particularly in real-world applications.
- The paper could benefit from a more detailed analysis of the limitations of the proposed method, such as its applicability in different scenarios and its sensitivity to the quality of synthetic data.
- The paper does not sufficiently address the issue of domain shift in the training data, which could affect the model's performance in real-world scenarios.
- The paper does not provide enough details on the computational cost and resource requirements of the proposed method, which could be a significant concern for practical deployment.

**Questions:**
- How does the proposed method compare with other state-of-the-art methods, particularly those that use semantic-aware detectors or other generative models?
- Can the authors provide more details on the computational cost and resource requirements of the proposed method, especially in terms of training and inference times?
- How sensitive is the proposed method to the quality of synthetic data? What are the potential implications if the synthetic data does not accurately represent real-world conditions?
- Could the authors discuss the potential negative societal impacts of using synthetic data in real-world applications, and how these impacts could be mitigated?
- The paper mentions that the synthetic images are generated using text-to-image models. How does the choice of model affect the quality and accuracy of the synthetic images?
- The paper uses geometric consistency scores to filter out synthetic images that do not preserve key scene features. How does the method ensure that these scores accurately reflect the preservation of key scene features?
