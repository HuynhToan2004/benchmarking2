 **Summary:**
The paper introduces ParFam, a novel method for symbolic regression that converts the discrete symbolic regression problem into a continuous one using parametric families of symbolic functions. This approach leverages a global optimizer to find the optimal parameters, demonstrating state-of-the-art performance on benchmarks such as the SRBench. The paper also proposes an extension, DL-ParFam, which integrates a pre-trained neural network to predict sparsity patterns, reducing the optimization problem dimensions. Despite its innovative approach, the paper faces criticism for its limited novelty, the lack of comprehensive comparison with existing methods, and the restricted applicability of the parametric family used.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, clear, and easy to follow, with a clear motivation and a novel approach to symbolic regression (SR) using parametric families of symbolic functions.
- The method achieves state-of-the-art performance on SRBench, demonstrating its effectiveness.
- The paper provides a detailed analysis of the methodology, including the use of a global optimizer and the integration of a pre-trained neural network to predict sparsity patterns.
- The experimental results are comprehensive and well-documented, providing a robust evaluation of the proposed methods.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a comprehensive comparison with existing methods, particularly those that also use parametric families for SR, such as EQL.
- The novelty of the approach is questioned as similar methods have been previously explored, such as the use of parametric families and global optimization.
- The paper's focus on specific parametric families limits its applicability to a broader range of problems.
- The experimental results, while promising, are not sufficiently robust to fully support the claims made, particularly regarding the effectiveness of the proposed methods in more complex or varied settings.
- The paper's methodology and results are not sufficiently differentiated from existing methods, particularly those that also use parametric families and global optimization.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors provide a more detailed comparison with existing methods, particularly EQL, to clarify the differences and advantages of the proposed approach?
- How does the paper address the issue of interpretability, especially in the context of the parametric families used?
- Can the authors discuss the choice of parametric families and how it impacts the applicability and effectiveness of the proposed methods?
- In the experimental results, could the authors clarify the differences in performance between ParFam and DL-ParFam, and provide more detailed results to support the claims made?
- How does the paper address the potential issues with the expressivity of the parametric families, especially in more complex or varied settings?
