 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel method for grounding manipulation concepts in robotic tasks using a large language model (LLM) and a vision-language model (VLM). It proposes a key state localization network that maximizes the information boost between key states and their preceding states, enhancing the semantic compatibility between textual descriptions and physical states. The method is evaluated on the ManiSkill2 dataset, demonstrating superior performance compared to baseline methods. The approach leverages an information-theoretic criterion to select key states, which are crucial for successful task completion. It also introduces a novel training objective that combines semantic compatibility, chronological correctness, and maximal information boost to optimize the grounding of LLM-generated concepts in physical states.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel approach for key state localization, which is crucial for task completion in robotic manipulation tasks.
- The proposed method is well-motivated and the experimental results are promising, demonstrating the effectiveness of the proposed approach.
- The paper is well-written, making it easy to follow, and the experimental results are comprehensive, covering both seen and unseen environments.
- The introduction of the concept of "Maximal Information Boost" is innovative and helps in selecting key states, which is a significant contribution to the field.
- The method's ability to handle unseen environments and its robustness to variations in physical states are highlighted, which is crucial for practical applications in real-world scenarios.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed discussion on the limitations of the proposed method, which could help in understanding its scope and applicability.
- The experimental setup is limited to specific tasks, which might not generalize well to other types of tasks.
- The paper does not provide a detailed analysis of the computational complexity or the scalability of the proposed method, which could limit its applicability in more complex or larger-scale tasks.
- The paper could benefit from more detailed explanations of the key components, such as the "key state localization network" and the "Maximal Information Boost" criterion.
- The paper does not sufficiently compare the proposed method with existing approaches, which could help in understanding its advantages and disadvantages.
- The paper's reliance on a single model (CLIP) for key state selection might limit its applicability to other models, and it would be beneficial to evaluate the method using different models to ensure its robustness.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors provide a more detailed explanation of the "key state localization network" and how it operates?
- How does the proposed method handle variations in physical states that are not directly related to the key states?
- Could the authors provide a more detailed analysis of the computational complexity and scalability of the proposed method?
- How does the proposed method compare with existing approaches in terms of performance and computational efficiency?
- Could the authors provide more details on the experimental setup, particularly how the key states are selected and how the success rate is measured?
- How does the proposed method perform when using different models for key state selection, and what are the implications of this on the method's effectiveness?
