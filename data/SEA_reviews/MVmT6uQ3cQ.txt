 **Summary:**
The paper introduces OPTIN, a one-shot pruning technique for Transformer models across various domains, including natural language processing and image classification. The method employs a trajectory metric to measure parameter importance, which is used to prune models without retraining. The experiments demonstrate competitive performance compared to state-of-the-art methods, showing that OPTIN can be effective in reducing computational costs. However, the paper has been critiqued for its lack of novelty, insufficient comparison with recent works, and unclear presentation in some sections.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, making it easy to follow and understand, with a clear structure that aids in comprehension.
- The proposed method, OPTIN, is simple yet effective, demonstrated by its ability to perform well on various tasks without retraining, which is a significant advantage over other pruning methods.
- The method's application is broad, as shown by its effectiveness across multiple domains, including language and vision tasks, which demonstrates its versatility and applicability.
- The paper provides extensive experimental results and thorough ablation studies, which help in understanding the method's effectiveness and its impact on different components.
- The paper is well-organized, with a clear presentation of the methodology and results, which enhances its readability and accessibility.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks novelty as it primarily focuses on measuring the trajectory of prunable components, which has been previously explored in other works.
- The comparison with recent works is insufficient, which limits the understanding of how the proposed method performs against the most current state-of-the-art techniques.
- The paper could benefit from a more detailed comparison with other pruning methods, particularly those that also employ the trajectory metric, to clarify the advantages of the proposed method.
- The paper's presentation in some sections is unclear, particularly in the introduction and the description of the methodology, which could hinder understanding for readers.
- The paper lacks a detailed discussion on the computational cost of the proposed method, which is crucial for understanding its practical applicability.
- The paper's claims about the generalizability of the method across different tasks and domains are not adequately supported by the experimental results, which limits the convincing nature of these claims.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors clarify the differences between the trajectory metric used in this work and those used in previous works, particularly in terms of how it contributes to the novelty and effectiveness of the proposed method?
- How does the proposed method compare with other pruning methods, particularly those that also employ the trajectory metric? What specific advantages does OPTIN offer over these methods?
- Can the authors provide more details on the computational cost of the proposed method, including the time and resource requirements for applying the method to different models and tasks?
- How does the method perform when applied to larger models or more complex tasks, such as those involving generative models or more advanced vision tasks?
- Could the authors provide more experimental evidence to support the claims of the method's generalizability across different tasks and domains?
