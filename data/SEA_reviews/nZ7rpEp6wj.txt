 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel architecture, DON-LSTM, which integrates DeepONet with an LSTM layer to address the limitations of DeepONet in handling long-term temporal dependencies. This architecture is designed to leverage both low- and high-resolution data, utilizing a multi-resolution training strategy. The proposed method is evaluated on various non-linear PDEs, showing improved generalization performance and requiring fewer high-resolution samples compared to baseline methods. However, concerns are raised about the novelty of the approach, the clarity of the presentation, and the lack of comprehensive comparisons with existing methods.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel architecture by combining DeepONet with an LSTM layer, which effectively utilizes multi-resolution data and captures long-term temporal dependencies, enhancing the model's ability to handle complex dynamical systems.
- The proposed method achieves significantly lower generalization error and requires fewer high-resolution samples compared to vanilla counterparts, demonstrating its efficiency and effectiveness in handling limited high-resolution data.
- The paper is well-written, making it easy to follow, and the experimental results are convincing, showing the superior performance of the proposed method over baseline methods in various scenarios.
- The integration of DeepONet with LSTM is a natural approach that leverages the strengths of both models, addressing the limitations of DeepONet in capturing long-term temporal dependencies.

**Weaknesses:**
- The novelty of the approach is limited as the combination of DeepONet with an LSTM layer is a straightforward extension of existing methods.
- The paper lacks clarity in its presentation, particularly in the methodology section, where it is difficult to understand the specifics of the DeepONet training and the integration of LSTM.
- The paper does not adequately compare the proposed method with existing state-of-the-art methods, which could have provided a more robust validation of the proposed approach.
- There is a lack of discussion on the computational cost of the proposed method compared to other methods, which is crucial for understanding its practical applicability.
- The paper does not address the limitations of the proposed method, which could have provided insights into its potential drawbacks and areas for future research.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors provide more detailed explanations on how the LSTM is integrated into the DeepONet framework, particularly in the methodology section?
- How does the proposed method compare in terms of computational cost and efficiency with other state-of-the-art methods, especially in handling long-term temporal dependencies?
- Could the authors discuss the limitations of the proposed method and suggest potential avenues for future research to address these limitations?
- In the experimental section, can the authors include a comparison with other multi-resolution learning methods to better position their work within the existing literature?
- How does the proposed method perform when compared to other models that leverage multi-resolution data, such as those using encoder-decoder architectures or other neural operator architectures?
