 **Summary:**
The paper explores the use of tensor-train (TT) low-rank tensor decomposition to efficiently represent and compress point clouds, aiming to enhance nearest-neighbor search in large vector databases. It introduces a method for training TT decompositions using a combination of Sliced Wasserstein loss and Nearest Neighbor Distance loss, which allows for the implicit extraction of hierarchical structures within the TT point clouds. The proposed method demonstrates robustness in out-of-distribution (OOD) problems and shows potential in approximate nearest-neighbor (ANN) search tasks. However, the paper's claims about the method's ability to directly apply to ANN search are questioned, and the experimental results are limited to specific datasets, raising concerns about the generalizability of the findings.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, with clear and easy-to-follow descriptions of the proposed method, making it accessible to readers.
- The use of Tensor-Train (TT) decomposition for point cloud representation and approximate nearest neighbor search is novel and innovative, particularly the hierarchical structure and the use of Sliced Wasserstein loss for training.
- The method is shown to be effective in out-of-distribution (OOD) problems and approximate nearest-neighbor (ANN) search tasks, demonstrating its practical utility.
- The paper includes comprehensive comparisons with existing methods, providing a robust evaluation of the proposed approach.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed discussion on the limitations of the proposed method, which is crucial for understanding its applicability and scope.
- The experimental results are limited to specific datasets, raising concerns about the generalizability of the findings.
- There is a discrepancy between the claims made in the abstract and the actual implementation of the method, particularly regarding the direct application of TT point clouds to ANN search.
- The paper does not sufficiently discuss the time complexity of the proposed method, which is crucial for understanding its computational efficiency.
- The paper could benefit from a more thorough comparison with other methods, including the use of more recent and relevant datasets.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors clarify how the hierarchical structure of the TT point clouds is utilized for approximate nearest-neighbor search?
- Is there a specific reason why the method is not directly applicable to ANN search, as claimed in the abstract?
- How does the proposed method compare in terms of time complexity and computational efficiency with other existing methods?
- Could the authors provide more details on the limitations of the proposed method and discuss how these limitations affect its applicability in various scenarios?
- It would be beneficial if the authors could expand on the experimental results by including more datasets and a broader range of comparisons to strengthen the validity of their findings.
