 **Summary:**
The paper introduces CAMEL, a novel meta-learning architecture designed for learning physical systems, which leverages an affine structure with respect to the learning task. This approach allows for efficient learning from multiple environments and enables interpretability by identifying physical parameters. The methodology is validated through experiments on physical systems, ranging from toy models to complex, non-analytical systems, demonstrating competitive generalization performance and low computational cost. The paper also discusses the challenges in meta-learning physical systems and the importance of interpretability, particularly in physical systems where understanding the model's behavior is crucial.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, clear, and easy to follow, with a comprehensive literature review and a clear motivation for the problem addressed.
- The proposed method, CAMEL, is innovative and effectively addresses the challenge of learning physical systems, which is crucial for scientific discovery and real-world applications.
- The paper provides a theoretical framework for the identifiability of physical parameters and demonstrates the effectiveness of the proposed method through a series of experiments on physical systems, showing that CAMEL outperforms baseline methods.
- The interpretability of the model is highlighted, which is essential in physical systems where understanding the model's behavior is crucial.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a thorough discussion on the limitations of the proposed method, particularly in terms of its applicability to more complex physical systems and its scalability to larger datasets.
- The paper could benefit from a more detailed comparison with existing methods, particularly in terms of computational cost and interpretability.
- The experimental results, while showing some advantages, do not convincingly demonstrate significant improvements over existing methods, especially in complex or real-world scenarios.
- The paper could be improved by including more realistic examples and a more detailed discussion on the assumptions made in the theoretical analysis.
- The paper's focus on physical systems might limit its broader applicability to other domains where meta-learning could be beneficial.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors provide more details on how the proposed method can be scaled up to handle larger datasets and more complex physical systems?
- How does the computational cost of CAMEL compare to other meta-learning methods, particularly in terms of training and inference times?
- Could the authors discuss the generalizability of the proposed method to other domains beyond physical systems?
- In the experiments, how does the method perform when the physical parameters are not linearly parametrized?
- How does the method compare in terms of interpretability and system identification capabilities to other meta-learning methods?
- Can the authors provide more realistic examples or scenarios where the proposed method could be applied to demonstrate its practical utility?
