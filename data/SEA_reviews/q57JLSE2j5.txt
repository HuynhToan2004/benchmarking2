 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel diffusion-based method for generating 3D objects using a triplane representation, which is a more efficient alternative to traditional voxel-based methods. This approach allows for the generation of a large vocabulary of 3D objects across various categories, including ShapeNet and OmniObject3D, with impressive results in terms of both geometry and texture quality. The method leverages a 3D-aware transformer to enhance the generalization capabilities of the diffusion model, which is crucial for handling the diversity and complexity of real-world 3D objects. Despite the promising results, the paper faces criticism for its lack of detailed technical explanations, insufficient comparisons with existing methods, and limited discussion on the societal impact of the proposed technology.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel method for generating 3D objects using a triplane representation, which is more efficient and effective compared to traditional voxel-based methods.
- The methodology includes the use of a 3D-aware transformer to enhance the generalization capabilities of the diffusion model, which is crucial for handling the diversity and complexity of real-world 3D objects.
- The paper is well-written, easy to follow, and provides a clear explanation of the methodology, including the use of a diffusion model with a transformer for 3D object generation.
- The results show a good performance in terms of geometry and texture quality, and the method is capable of generating a large vocabulary of 3D objects across various categories, including ShapeNet and OmniObject3D.
- The paper includes an ablation study that demonstrates the effectiveness of the proposed method, and the authors have provided code and pretrained models to support their claims.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks detailed technical explanations and discussions, particularly regarding the novelty of the 3D-aware transformer and the specifics of the triplane fitting process.
- There is a lack of comparative analysis with existing methods, which makes it difficult to assess the relative performance and effectiveness of the proposed method.
- The paper does not discuss the limitations of the proposed method, such as its applicability to other 3D representation formats or its scalability to larger datasets.
- The paper does not provide sufficient details on the training and inference time for the proposed method, which is crucial for evaluating its practical applicability.
- The paper does not adequately address the societal impact of the proposed technology, particularly concerning the potential misuse of the generated 3D objects for malicious purposes.
- The paper could benefit from a more thorough evaluation and discussion of the results, including a comparison with other state-of-the-art methods and a more detailed analysis of the generated 3D objects' quality and diversity.
