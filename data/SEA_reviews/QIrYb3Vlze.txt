 **Summary:**
The paper introduces Isometric Diffusion, a novel method for training diffusion models that utilizes isometric representation learning to enhance the latent space's alignment with human perception and semantic understanding. This approach aims to improve the interpolation process in diffusion models by making the latent space more isometric to the data manifold, thereby promoting smoother transitions between latent vectors. The methodology involves a regularization term that guides the encoder of the score model to map from the latent space to the semantic space, preserving geodesic distances. The paper includes experimental results that demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed method, although it requires further validation on more diverse datasets and with comparisons to other state-of-the-art methods.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, clearly presenting the problem and its motivation, making it accessible and understandable to readers.
- The introduction of the isometric representation learning concept is novel and innovative, offering a new perspective on training diffusion models.
- The methodology is supported by a solid theoretical foundation, which is well-explained and easy to follow.
- The experimental results demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed method, showing improvements in the latent space's disentanglement and the smoothness of interpolations.
- The paper includes a new metric, mRTL, which is useful for evaluating the quality of the learned latent space.
- The use of isometric learning in diffusion models is a significant advancement, potentially enhancing the utility of these models for tasks like semantic editing and controllable generation.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed comparison with other state-of-the-art methods, which could provide a more robust validation of the proposed method.
- The experimental results are limited to specific datasets, and it would be beneficial to see how the method performs on a more diverse set of images.
- There is a noticeable increase in FID scores, which raises concerns about the method's effectiveness and the potential trade-offs involved.
- The paper does not sufficiently address the computational costs associated with the proposed method, which could be a significant drawback for practical applications.
- The paper could benefit from a more thorough discussion on the limitations of the proposed method, particularly concerning its applicability to different types of diffusion models and datasets.
- The paper could be improved by providing more detailed visualizations and comparisons, especially in the interpolation and latent space visualization results.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors provide more detailed visualizations and comparisons to better illustrate the interpolation and latent space visualization results?
- How does the proposed method perform on a more diverse set of images, and what are the implications of this diversity for the method's effectiveness?
- Could the authors elaborate on the computational costs associated with the proposed method and discuss potential strategies for mitigating these costs?
- In light of the observed increase in FID scores, can the authors provide more detailed insights into the trade-offs involved in using the proposed method?
- How does the proposed method compare to other state-of-the-art methods in terms of computational efficiency and effectiveness in achieving isometric representation learning?
- Can the authors clarify the potential limitations of the proposed method and discuss how these limitations might affect the practical applicability of the method?
