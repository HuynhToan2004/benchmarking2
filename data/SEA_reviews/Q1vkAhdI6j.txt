 **Summary:**
The paper introduces MixSup, a novel label-efficient LiDAR-based 3D object detection method that utilizes a combination of coarse cluster-level and accurate box-level labels. The approach leverages the Segment Anything Model (SAM) to automatically generate coarse labels, aiming to reduce annotation costs. The methodology is tested across various benchmarks such as nuScenes, Waymo Open Dataset, and KITTI, demonstrating competitive performance with minimal annotation costs. However, the paper's experimental results are not consistently strong, and the method's scalability and generalization across different datasets are questioned.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, with clear figures and tables that aid in understanding the content.
- The proposed method is practical and intuitive, with a focus on utilizing both coarse and accurate labels, which is well-motivated.
- The use of the Segment Anything Model (SAM) for coarse label generation is innovative and could potentially reduce annotation costs.
- The paper presents a comprehensive experimental setup, including a pilot study and comparisons with various label-efficient learning settings, which demonstrates the method's effectiveness.
- The method's compatibility with different detectors such as CenterPoint, SECOND, PV-RCNN, and FSD, shows its versatility and applicability.

**Weaknesses:**
- The performance of the proposed method is not consistently strong, with results on the nuScenes dataset being particularly weak.
- The paper lacks a thorough comparison with existing label-efficient methods, which could have strengthened the argument for the effectiveness of the proposed method.
- The paper does not provide a detailed analysis of the scalability and generalization of the proposed method across different datasets, which is crucial for understanding its applicability.
- The paper does not include a discussion on the limitations of the proposed method, which could help in understanding its scope and applicability.
- The paper does not sufficiently address the potential impact of using different IoU thresholds for different classes, which could affect the fairness and effectiveness of the proposed method.

**Questions:**
- Could you clarify how the cluster-level labels are generated using SAM and how the SAR module is utilized in this process?
- How does the proposed method perform when using a larger number of box-level labels?
- Why is the performance on the nuScenes dataset significantly lower than on other datasets?
- How does the method handle the scalability and generalization across different datasets, especially those with varying characteristics such as Waymo Open Dataset and nuScenes?
- Could you provide more details on the training schedule and hyperparameters used in your experiments to ensure reproducibility?
- How does the proposed method compare in terms of annotation cost and performance with other label-efficient methods, especially those that also leverage semi-supervised learning techniques?
