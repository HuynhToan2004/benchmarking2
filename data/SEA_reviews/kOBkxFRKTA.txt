 **Summary:**
The paper introduces SRigL, a novel dynamic sparse training method that integrates structured sparsity with constant fan-in constraints to enhance the efficiency and generalization of neural networks. SRigL builds upon the RigL method, employing a neuron ablation technique to maintain performance at high sparsity levels. The method is evaluated across various network architectures and datasets, demonstrating improvements in computational efficiency and generalization. The authors also provide empirical evidence of the method's effectiveness, particularly in terms of reducing computational overhead and maintaining accuracy. Despite its strengths, the paper is criticized for its limited novelty and the need for more comprehensive hardware evaluations.

**Strengths:**
- The paper presents a well-structured and clear presentation of the proposed method, with a thorough review of related literature.
- The empirical results are convincing, showing that SRigL maintains high accuracy while achieving significant sparsity, even at high sparsity levels.
- The paper introduces a novel approach by combining the advantages of structured sparsity and dynamic sparse training, which is a significant contribution to the field.
- The authors have provided a detailed analysis of the training dynamics and generalization performance of the proposed method, which is well-documented and supported by empirical evidence.
- The method is shown to be effective in reducing computational overhead, which is a significant advantage for practical applications.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a comprehensive discussion on the limitations of the proposed method, particularly in terms of hardware support and the practical implications of using SRigL in different hardware environments.
- The novelty of the method is limited as it mainly builds upon existing techniques like RigL and neuron ablation, which have been previously explored.
- The paper does not adequately address the scalability of the method, particularly in terms of training time and the impact of hyperparameters on performance.
- The paper's evaluation is limited to specific datasets and network architectures, which may not generalize well to other scenarios.
- The paper does not provide a detailed discussion on the impact of the proposed method on the generalization performance of the models, which is a critical aspect of the study.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors provide a more detailed discussion on the limitations of the proposed method, particularly in terms of hardware support and practical implications?
- How does the proposed method compare to other dynamic sparse training methods in terms of computational efficiency and generalization performance?
- Could the authors clarify the impact of the hyperparameter γsal on the performance of the model, and provide more insights into its optimal value?
- In light of the empirical results, how does the proposed method compare to other structured sparsity methods in terms of computational efficiency and generalization performance?
- How does the proposed method handle the scalability issues, particularly in terms of training time and the impact of hyperparameters on performance?
- Can the authors provide a more detailed analysis of the generalization performance of the models trained using SRigL, and discuss the implications of these findings?
