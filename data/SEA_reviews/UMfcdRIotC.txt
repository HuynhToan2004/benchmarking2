 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel method for generating counterfactuals in Natural Language Processing (NLP) models using Large Language Models (LLMs) and a matching-based approach. The authors propose a causal representation learning model that matches counterfactual examples with control samples, which are then used to approximate counterfactuals. This method is evaluated using the CEBaB benchmark and demonstrates the effectiveness of the proposed approach. The paper also introduces a new dataset for stance detection, leveraging LLMs to generate counterfactual examples. The methodology is designed to be model-agnostic and addresses the challenge of generating counterfactuals in NLP, which is crucial for understanding and improving model predictions.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel method for generating counterfactuals using LLMs, which is a significant contribution to the field of NLP.
- The methodology is well-motivated and theoretically grounded, with a focus on causal representation learning and the use of LLMs for generating counterfactuals.
- The paper is well-written, making it accessible and easy to understand, with clear explanations of the methodology and results.
- The proposed method is evaluated using a benchmark dataset, providing a robust evaluation of its effectiveness.
- The paper addresses an important and timely issue in the field of NLP, focusing on the generation of counterfactuals which is crucial for understanding and improving model predictions.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper's reliance on LLMs for generating counterfactuals raises concerns about the method's effectiveness and efficiency, particularly in scenarios where LLMs might not be the most suitable or cost-effective solution.
- The paper's methodology is somewhat limited by the need for a predefined candidate set of counterfactual examples, which may not be feasible in all scenarios.
- The paper's evaluation is limited to the CEBaB benchmark, which may not fully demonstrate the generalizability of the proposed method.
- The paper's assumptions about the causal graph and the availability of a predefined set of counterfactual examples may not hold in all real-world scenarios, limiting the practical applicability of the proposed method.
- The paper's methodology and results could benefit from a more detailed discussion of the limitations and assumptions, as well as a broader evaluation across different datasets and scenarios.

**Questions:**
- How does the proposed method compare to other methods for generating counterfactuals, particularly in terms of efficiency and effectiveness?
- Can the authors provide more details on the training and evaluation of the causal representation model, including the size and quality of the training data and the evaluation metrics used?
- How does the method handle the generation of counterfactuals for different classes of text, such as product reviews versus news articles?
- Could the authors discuss the potential impact of the method's assumptions about the causal graph and the availability of a predefined set of counterfactual examples on the practical applicability of the method?
- How does the method handle the generation of counterfactuals for text that does not fit neatly into the predefined categories used in the experiments?
