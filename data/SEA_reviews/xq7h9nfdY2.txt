 **Summary:**
The paper introduces SmoothLLM, a novel algorithm designed to mitigate jailbreaking attacks on large language models (LLMs) by perturbing input prompts and aggregating responses. The algorithm is based on the observation that adversarial suffixes are brittle to character-level perturbations. SmoothLLM has been tested against various LLMs, demonstrating a significant reduction in attack success rates (ASRs) while maintaining high nominal performance. The method is compatible with any LLM and is efficient in terms of query usage, showing a significant reduction in the number of queries needed compared to existing attacks. The paper also provides a theoretical analysis of the defense's effectiveness and discusses its compatibility with different LLMs.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, clear, and easy to follow, with a comprehensive experimental evaluation that supports the claims effectively.
- The introduction of SmoothLLM, a novel algorithm for defending against jailbreaking attacks on LLMs, is a significant contribution to the field.
- The paper provides a theoretical analysis of the defense's effectiveness and discusses its compatibility with different LLMs, enhancing the understanding of the algorithm's applicability.
- The experimental results show that SmoothLLM is effective in reducing the attack success rate (ASR) on various LLMs, maintaining high nominal performance, and being more query-efficient than existing attacks.
- The paper is well-organized, with a clear presentation of the methodology, experimental results, and theoretical analysis, making it accessible to readers.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper primarily focuses on defending against a specific type of attack, which might limit its broader applicability to other types of adversarial attacks on LLMs.
- There is a lack of discussion on the potential negative societal impacts of the proposed method, which could be crucial for understanding the broader implications of the work.
- The paper does not provide a detailed comparison with other existing defense methods, which could help in understanding the advantages and disadvantages of SmoothLLM more clearly.
- The theoretical analysis is limited to swap perturbations, and it would be beneficial to see how the proposed algorithm performs with other types of perturbations.
- The paper could benefit from a more detailed discussion on the limitations and potential negative societal impacts of the proposed method.

**Questions:**
- How does SmoothLLM perform against other types of adversarial attacks on LLMs, such as those involving token-level perturbations?
- Can the authors provide more details on the computational burden of the SmoothLLM defense, particularly how it affects the performance of the LLM?
- Is there a possibility to extend the theoretical analysis to other types of perturbations besides swap?
- Could the authors discuss the potential negative societal impacts of the proposed method and how these might be mitigated or addressed?
- How does the proposed algorithm compare in terms of computational efficiency and effectiveness with other existing defense methods against adversarial attacks on LLMs?
