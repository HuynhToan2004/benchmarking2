 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel approach to perceptual image compression by integrating a generative model to ensure idempotence, a crucial property for image compression. The proposed method leverages an unconditional generative model to optimize perceptual quality, showing superior performance over existing methods in terms of FID and other metrics. Theoretical insights connect idempotence with perceptual quality, providing a theoretical foundation for the proposed method. Despite the theoretical and empirical strengths, the paper faces criticism for its clarity in presentation, particularly in explaining the relationship between idempotence and perceptual quality, and for the complexity introduced by the additional steps required for inversion.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel approach to perceptual image compression by combining generative models with idempotence constraints, which is a novel and interesting approach.
- The theoretical proofs provided are solid and well-articulated, contributing to the understanding of the proposed method.
- The empirical results show that the proposed method outperforms state-of-the-art methods in terms of FID and other metrics, indicating its effectiveness.
- The paper is well-written, making it easy to follow and understand, with clear explanations of the methodology and experimental setups.
- The connection between idempotence and perceptual image compression is insightful, providing a theoretical framework for understanding the proposed method.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks clarity in explaining the relationship between idempotence and perceptual quality, particularly in how the idempotence constraint ensures perceptual quality.
- There is a need for more detailed explanations of the inversion process and the practical implications of using different generative models.
- The paper could benefit from more comprehensive evaluations, including comparisons with other metrics like KID and LPIPS, and additional datasets to validate the generalizability of the results.
- The complexity of the proposed method, which involves additional steps for inversion, could be a drawback, particularly in terms of computational efficiency and practical implementation.
- The paper could be improved by better integrating the theoretical insights into the practical implementation, and by providing more detailed explanations of the experimental setups and results.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors provide more detailed explanations of how the idempotence constraint ensures perceptual quality, and clarify the relationship between idempotence and perceptual quality?
- How does the choice of generative model affect the performance of the proposed method? Could the authors provide more detailed comparisons of different generative models in practical applications?
- Could the authors elaborate on the inversion process and the practical implications of using different generative models in the context of perceptual image compression?
- How does the proposed method compare with other metrics like KID and LPIPS, and could the authors include these comparisons in future evaluations?
- Could the authors provide more detailed explanations of the experimental setups and results, including the specifics of the datasets used and the experimental conditions?
