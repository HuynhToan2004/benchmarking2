 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel method for integrating large language models (LLMs) into task-oriented agents, focusing on environmental modeling. The approach involves a two-stage process: pre-task exploration and in-task updates. During pre-task exploration, the LLM is used to generate actions that lead to a richer initial state description. In the in-task phase, the LLM's output is used to update the state description, allowing the agent to refine its understanding of the environment. The method is tested on the ALFWorld-Eco benchmark and shows promising results, particularly in improving the agent's performance on tasks. However, concerns were raised about the clarity of the method's presentation, the depth of the experimental validation, and the generalizability of the findings.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel approach to modeling environments using LLMs, which is a significant step towards integrating LLMs into task-oriented agents.
- The methodology, while simple, is effective in improving task performance, as demonstrated by the results on the ALFWorld-Eco benchmark.
- The paper is well-written, clear, and easy to follow, making it accessible to a broad audience.
- The authors have proposed a new benchmark, ALFWorld-Eco, which is a significant contribution to the field and can be useful for future research.
- The paper addresses an important and timely problem of enabling LLMs to learn the environment, which is crucial for improving the performance of LLM-based agents.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a thorough evaluation of the method, particularly in terms of its generalizability and effectiveness across different environments.
- There is a lack of clarity in the presentation of the method, particularly in the explanation of how the LLM is used to generate actions and how the initial state description is obtained.
- The experimental setup is limited, and the results are not sufficiently validated against existing methods, making it difficult to assess the true impact of the proposed method.
- The paper does not sufficiently address the scalability of the proposed method, especially in terms of its applicability to more complex environments.
- There are several minor presentation issues, such as typos and unclear figures, which detract from the professional quality of the paper.

**Questions:**
- How does the proposed method compare to existing methods for task-oriented agents, particularly in terms of performance and generalizability?
- Can the authors provide more details on how the LLM is used to generate actions and how the initial state description is obtained?
- How does the method handle the scalability issue, especially in more complex environments?
- Could the authors clarify the presentation of the method, particularly in sections where the explanation of the LLM's role is unclear?
- How does the proposed method handle the issue of LLMs forgetting previous information when generating actions, and how does it ensure that the LLM is using the most up-to-date information?
