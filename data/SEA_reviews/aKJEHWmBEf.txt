 **Summary:**
The paper introduces APEN, a novel framework designed to construct approximately piecewise E(3) equivariant point networks. This framework is aimed at handling part-based symmetry in point clouds, a significant challenge in point cloud recognition tasks. APEN leverages a compositional network architecture to gradually refine the partitioning of point clouds, maintaining equivariance with respect to the current partition. The authors provide theoretical insights and experimental validations, showcasing the framework's effectiveness in tasks such as point cloud segmentation and classification. Despite its innovative approach, the paper has been critiqued for its presentation clarity, particularly in explaining the architecture and its novel contributions, and the experimental setup which lacks comprehensive comparisons with state-of-the-art methods.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel compositional network architecture for handling part-based symmetry in point clouds, which is a significant advancement in the field of point cloud recognition.
- Theoretical insights are provided to justify the proposed approach, enhancing the paper's credibility and depth.
- The experimental results are robust, demonstrating the effectiveness of the proposed method in tasks such as point cloud segmentation and classification.
- The paper is well-written, making it accessible and easy to follow, which is crucial for academic papers.
- The framework is general and applicable to other tasks, indicating its potential for broader applications in the field.

**Weaknesses:**
- The presentation of the paper is not clear, particularly in explaining the architecture and its novel contributions, which could hinder understanding and reproducibility.
- The experimental setup lacks comprehensive comparisons with state-of-the-art methods, which could undermine the validity of the results.
- The paper does not sufficiently discuss the limitations of the proposed method, which is essential for a complete evaluation of its effectiveness and applicability.
- The paper's contribution to the field is not clearly articulated, which could lead to confusion about the novelty and significance of the work.
- The paper could benefit from more detailed explanations and examples to clarify the concepts and methodologies used, particularly in the introduction and methodology sections.

**Questions:**
