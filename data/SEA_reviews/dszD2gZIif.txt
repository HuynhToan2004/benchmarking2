 **Summary:**
The paper introduces Swin4TS, a novel approach for long-term time series forecasting inspired by the Swin Transformer, which is known for its window-based attention and hierarchical representation. The model divides time series data into windows and patches, allowing for linear computational complexity and effective handling of both channel-dependent and channel-independent strategies. Swin4TS is evaluated across eight datasets and demonstrates superior performance compared to state-of-the-art models. The paper also discusses the potential of applying Swin4TS to other time series tasks like anomaly detection, classification, and imputation. However, concerns were raised about the novelty of the approach, the depth of the analysis, and the clarity of the presentation.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written and easy to follow, with a clear and concise presentation of the proposed method, Swin4TS.
- Swin4TS demonstrates strong empirical performance, achieving state-of-the-art results on multiple benchmarks.
- The use of window-based attention and hierarchical representation from the Swin Transformer is innovative and effectively adapted to the time series forecasting problem.
- The paper provides a comprehensive evaluation across multiple datasets, showing the model's ability to handle both channel-dependent and channel-independent strategies.
- The method's computational efficiency is highlighted, with a linear computational complexity that enables processing of longer inputs.

**Weaknesses:**
- The novelty of the approach is questionable as it primarily adapts existing techniques from the Swin Transformer without significant modification or innovation.
- The paper lacks a thorough analysis of the model's limitations and failure cases, which is crucial for understanding its practical applicability.
- There is a lack of comparative analysis with other state-of-the-art models, particularly those that use similar techniques like patch-based transformers.
- The paper does not adequately address the limitations of the proposed method, which could include the model's performance on different types of time series data and its scalability to larger datasets.
- The paper could benefit from a more detailed explanation of the model's components, such as the role of the linear layer in the hierarchical representation and the impact of different window sizes on the model's performance.
- The paper's presentation and structure could be improved to better guide the reader through the content and to enhance the clarity of the figures and tables.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors provide more details on how the hierarchical representation, specifically the linear layer, contributes to the model's performance?
- How does the proposed method compare in terms of computational efficiency and performance with other models like PatchTST and Crossformer, especially in scenarios where the number of channels and time steps varies?
- What are the specific advantages of Swin4TS over other models in terms of handling different types of time series data, such as those with varying frequencies or non-stationary patterns?
- Can the authors clarify the impact of different window sizes on the model's performance and provide insights into how these parameters should be chosen for optimal results?
- How does the model handle the potential for overfitting due to the fixed window size, and what measures are in place to prevent this?
- Could the authors elaborate on the model's transferability to other time series tasks like anomaly detection, classification, and imputation?
