 **Summary:**
The paper introduces HoloNets, a novel approach to spectral convolutional networks for directed graphs, which extends beyond the traditional reliance on the graph Fourier transform. This extension is facilitated by the holomorphic functional calculus, allowing for the definition of filters that are not contingent on the self-adjointness of the characteristic operator. The paper presents two specific instantiations of HoloNets, FaberNet and Dir-ResolvNet, which are tested on real-world datasets, demonstrating state-of-the-art results in heterophilic node classification and stability against topological perturbations. The theoretical underpinnings of HoloNets are explored, and the paper discusses the importance of basis selection in expressing filters.
