 **Summary:**
The paper introduces Dyna-PSRO, a novel algorithm that co-learns an empirical game and a world model to enhance strategic decision-making in multi-agent reinforcement learning (MARL) environments. Dyna-PSRO leverages strategic diversity from empirical games to train more effective world models and employs these models to reduce computational costs in response calculations. The algorithm is evaluated against the baseline PSRO and shows improvements in both computational efficiency and regret minimization across various games. The paper also discusses the potential of Dyna-PSRO in handling partial observability and general-sum games, although the experimental scope is limited.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, with clear and concise language that effectively communicates the main ideas.
- The integration of world models and empirical games is a novel approach that enhances the understanding of strategic interactions in multi-agent reinforcement learning (MARL) environments.
- The experimental results demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed algorithm, Dyna-PSRO, in improving computational efficiency and reducing regret in various games compared to the baseline PSRO.
- The paper provides a clear motivation for the proposed algorithm and includes a detailed discussion of the experimental results, which aids in understanding the implications of the findings.
- The use of strategic diversity to enhance the training of world models is a novel and interesting approach that could potentially be applied in other domains.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed discussion on how the proposed algorithm handles partial observability, which is crucial in many MARL environments.
- The experimental evaluation is limited to a few games, which might not fully demonstrate the generalizability of the proposed algorithm.
- The paper does not adequately address the limitations of its approach, particularly in terms of scalability and the impact of model prediction errors on the effectiveness of the algorithm.
- The experimental setup and the selection of baselines could be improved to better demonstrate the advantages of the proposed algorithm.
- The paper could benefit from a more thorough discussion on the relationship between the proposed algorithm and existing works, such as the use of world models in MARL and the role of strategic diversity in game modeling.

**Questions:**
- Could you clarify how Dyna-PSRO handles partial observability in MARL environments?
- How does the strategic diversity of the training data affect the performance of the world model, and could you provide more detailed results on this aspect?
- Is there a specific reason for using a deterministic world model, and how does this affect the performance of the algorithm in stochastic environments?
- Could you provide more details on the implementation of the world model, particularly how it handles different types of games and the impact of model prediction errors?
- How does the proposed algorithm compare to other existing methods in terms of computational efficiency and regret minimization in different types of games?
- Could you discuss the potential applications of the proposed algorithm in more complex or varied game scenarios, such as those involving more players or different types of interactions?
