 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel stochastic vision transformer for self-supervised learning, leveraging elliptical Gaussian distributions and Wasserstein distance-based attention to enhance uncertainty and distance awareness. This approach aims to improve robustness and generalization capabilities of vision transformers by embedding images into Gaussian distributions and using Wasserstein distance for attention calculation. The methodology involves regularization terms based on Wasserstein distance for both pre-training and fine-tuning, aiming to enhance the model's performance in various tasks such as in-distribution generalization, out-of-distribution detection, and semi-supervised learning. Despite its innovative approach, the paper faces criticism for its limited experimental scope, lack of detailed ablation studies, and insufficient justification of certain design choices.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel approach using Gaussian distributions for embedding image patches, which is an innovative approach to self-supervised learning.
- The methodology of using Wasserstein distance-based attention to compute attention matrices from stochastic representations is a significant contribution to the field.
- The paper is well-written and easy to follow, making it accessible to readers.
- The experiments conducted demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed method, showing improvements over baseline methods in various tasks.
- The paper presents a comprehensive experimental setup, including multiple tasks and datasets, which supports the robustness of the proposed method.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks detailed ablation studies, particularly concerning the design choices of the regularization term and the impact of different hyperparameters.
- The paper does not provide sufficient justification for the design choices, such as the use of elliptical Gaussian distributions and the specific choice of hyperparameters.
- The experimental results are not compelling, with marginal improvements over existing methods and inconsistencies in performance across different metrics.
- The paper does not sufficiently compare its method with state-of-the-art methods in the field, which could undermine the perceived effectiveness of the proposed approach.
- The paper does not provide a comprehensive analysis of the computational cost of the proposed method, which is crucial for practical applications.
- The paper lacks a thorough discussion on the limitations and potential negative societal impacts of the proposed method.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors provide a more detailed explanation of the design choices, particularly the use of elliptical Gaussian distributions and the specific hyperparameters chosen?
- Why was the decision made to use a single seed for the experiments, and how does this affect the reliability of the results?
- How does the proposed method compare to state-of-the-art methods in terms of computational efficiency and practical applicability?
- Can the authors provide a more detailed analysis of the experimental results, particularly the inconsistencies observed in the performance metrics?
- How does the proposed method handle the issue of overfitting, especially in the context of semi-supervised learning?
- Could the authors clarify the role and impact of the regularization terms in the model's performance, particularly in terms of their contribution to the model's robustness and generalization capabilities?
