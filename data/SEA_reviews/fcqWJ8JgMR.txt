 **Summary:**
The paper introduces AuG-KD, a novel method for out-of-domain (OOD) knowledge distillation, addressing the performance degradation caused by domain shifts. It utilizes an anchor-based mixup generation to align student-domain data with teacher-domain data, aiming to mitigate the impact of domain-specific information on student model performance. The methodology involves a data-free learning module, an anchor learning module, and a mixup learning module, which are evaluated on three datasets and eight settings, demonstrating superior performance over existing methods. The paper is commended for its comprehensive experimental setup and clear presentation, although concerns are raised about the novelty and the clarity of some methodological descriptions.

**Strengths:**
- The paper addresses a novel problem of out-of-domain knowledge distillation, which is an important and practical problem.
- The proposed method, AuG-KD, is simple yet effective, and the paper is well-written, making it easy to follow.
- Comprehensive experiments are conducted on three datasets and eight settings, demonstrating the superior performance of the proposed method.
- The authors have provided a detailed analysis of the experimental results, which is helpful in understanding the effectiveness of the proposed method.
- The paper introduces a novel method of out-of-domain knowledge distillation, which is an interesting approach that could potentially advance the field.
- The authors have provided a detailed explanation of the methodology, including the use of an anchor-based mixup generation and an uncertainty-guided anchor to align student-domain data with the teacher domain.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a clear definition of the term "domain," which is used frequently without adequate explanation.
- The methodology section is not clearly explained, particularly the roles and interactions of different components such as the anchor learning module and the mixup learning module.
- The paper does not sufficiently discuss the limitations of the proposed method, such as the impact of domain shifts on the performance of the student model.
- The paper does not provide sufficient details on the implementation of the anchor learning module, including the structure and training details, which are crucial for reproducibility.
- The novelty of the proposed method is questionable as it seems to be a combination of existing techniques without significant innovation.
- The paper could benefit from a more detailed comparison with state-of-the-art methods, particularly in terms of performance metrics and experimental setups.
- The paper suffers from some typographical errors and lacks clarity in certain sections, which could affect the reader's understanding.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors clarify the term "domain" and provide a more detailed explanation of how it is used in the context of the paper?
- How does the proposed method perform when the domain shift is significant, and what are the potential impacts on the student model's performance?
- Can the authors provide more details on the implementation of the anchor learning module, including the structure and training details?
- How does the proposed method compare with state-of-the-art methods in terms of performance metrics and experimental setups?
- Could the authors provide a more detailed explanation of the methodology, particularly the roles and interactions of different components such as the anchor learning module and the mixup learning module?
- How does the proposed method address the issue of domain shift, and what are the potential limitations or drawbacks of the method?
- Can the authors provide more details on the experimental setup, such as the specifics of the datasets used and the experimental conditions?
