 **Summary:**
The paper introduces the Differentiable Cell Complex Module (DCM), a novel approach for latent graph inference that learns higher-order topological structures, specifically cell complexes, to describe multi-way interactions among data points. DCM extends the Differentiable Graph Module (DGM) by integrating a polygon inference module to sample higher-order cells, which is crucial for learning complex interactions. The paper is structured around the DCM and its integration with cell complex message-passing networks, showcasing its effectiveness across various datasets, including homophilic and heterophilic graphs. The methodology is evaluated through extensive experiments, demonstrating the superiority of DCM over existing models. However, the paper suffers from clarity issues, particularly in the mathematical definitions and the explanation of certain modules, which could hinder understanding.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces the Differentiable Cell Complex Module (DCM), a novel approach that dynamically learns higher-order topological structures, which is a significant advancement in the field of graph neural networks (GNNs).
- The DCM is shown to be effective in various tasks, including node classification, demonstrating its versatility and applicability.
- The paper is well-structured and provides a comprehensive evaluation, including a detailed analysis of the limitations and potential future directions, which adds to the credibility of the research.
- The authors have addressed the limitations of existing methods by proposing a novel module, the alpha-DGM, which overcomes the constraints of learning only regular graphs.
- The paper is well-written and provides a clear description of the proposed method, making it accessible and understandable to a broad audience.
