 **Summary:**
The paper investigates the asymptotic properties of robust regression estimators under heavy-tailed data distributions, focusing on the Huber loss and ridge regression. The authors provide theoretical insights into the performance of these estimators in high-dimensional settings, showing that the decay rate of excess risk is influenced by the tail behavior of the covariate distribution. The paper also introduces a novel approach to characterize the performance of M-estimators and Bayes optimal estimators in high-dimensional regimes, demonstrating the impact of heavy-tailed covariate and response contamination on the performance of M-estimators. Despite its technical contributions, the paper is criticized for its lack of clarity in presentation and the need for a more rigorous discussion on the assumptions and results.
