 **Summary:**
The paper investigates the use of GPT-4's code interpreter for solving math problems, particularly focusing on its ability to generate and execute code, adjust solutions when faced with implausible outputs, and employ a self-verification mechanism. The authors propose a novel prompting method, CSV, which encourages GPT-4 to use code for self-verification, potentially improving accuracy. The paper provides extensive experiments across various datasets, including MATH, GSM8K, and MMLU-Math, demonstrating that GPT-4's performance is significantly enhanced by the CSV prompt. The study also explores the impact of code usage frequency on model performance and introduces a verification-guided weighted majority voting strategy to enhance accuracy.

**Strengths:**
- The paper provides a thorough analysis of GPT-4's code usage frequency and its correlation with accuracy, which is a significant contribution to the understanding of GPT-4's performance.
- The proposed CSV prompt is innovative and effective, enabling GPT-4 to self-verify its answers, which is a novel approach in mathematical problem-solving.
- The paper is well-written, clear, and easy to follow, with comprehensive experiments conducted on three datasets, demonstrating the effectiveness of the proposed method.
- The introduction of verification-guided weighted majority voting is a simple yet effective method that enhances the accuracy of GPT-4's mathematical problem-solving capabilities.
- The study is significant as it is the first to systematically analyze the role of code generation, execution, and self-debugging in mathematical problem-solving, providing a novel perspective on enhancing GPT-4's performance.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed explanation of the self-verification mechanism and the code verification process, which could benefit from additional examples or a more detailed explanation.
- The comparison with other models like Llama 2 and Claude 2 is missing, which could provide a more comprehensive evaluation of the proposed method.
- The paper does not sufficiently discuss the limitations of the proposed method, particularly in scenarios where the self-verification mechanism might fail or be less effective.
- There is a lack of discussion on the limitations of GPT-4's code interpreter, which could impact the generalizability of the findings.
- The paper does not sufficiently compare the proposed method with other existing methods, such as CoT or self-consistency, which could provide a more robust evaluation of the proposed method.
- The paper could benefit from a more detailed discussion on the potential negative societal impacts of the proposed method.

**Questions:**
- Can you provide more details on the self-verification mechanism and the code verification process, possibly with additional examples or a more detailed explanation?
- How does the CSV prompt compare with other prompting methods, such as those used in CoT or self-consistency?
- Could you elaborate on the limitations of the proposed method and discuss scenarios where it might not be effective or could potentially fail?
- How does the proposed method compare with other methods in terms of computational efficiency and scalability, especially when applied to larger datasets or more complex problems?
- Can you provide a more detailed discussion on the potential negative societal impacts of the proposed method, especially in terms of its application in educational or assessment contexts?
