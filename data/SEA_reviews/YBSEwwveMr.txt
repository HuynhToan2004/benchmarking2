 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel approach to multimodal Variational Autoencoders (VAEs) by integrating a score-based model to learn the latent space of unimodal VAEs, aiming to enhance multimodal coherence and improve generative performance. This methodology is evaluated on the Extended PolyMNIST and CelebAMask-HQ datasets, showing promising results in terms of coherence and FID scores. The authors claim that this approach improves upon existing methods by avoiding the need to construct a joint multimodal ELBO, which can lead to scalability issues. However, the paper's novelty is questioned due to its similarity to previous works, and the experimental evaluation is seen as insufficient.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, making it easy to follow and understand.
- The proposed method is simple, effective, and supported by comprehensive experimental studies.
- The use of score-based models to learn the latent space of unimodal VAEs is a novel approach that enhances the coherence of multimodal data.
- The paper addresses the scalability issue of multimodal VAEs by avoiding the need to construct a joint multimodal ELBO.
- The methodology is supported by a clear and concise mathematical notation, and the experimental results are well-presented.
- The paper demonstrates the effectiveness of the proposed method in handling different types of modalities, such as images and text, and shows that it can handle a large number of modalities effectively.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed discussion on the limitations of the proposed method, particularly concerning its scalability and the computational cost of training and inference.
- The experimental evaluation is insufficient, with the baselines not being strong enough to demonstrate the superiority of the proposed method.
- The paper does not adequately compare its method with other state-of-the-art methods, such as those that use hierarchical VAEs or other multimodal VAEs that have been proposed recently.
- The paper's claim of being the first to use a score-based model to learn the latent space of unimodal VAEs is not entirely accurate, as similar methods have been previously proposed.
- The paper does not sufficiently address the issue of missing modalities during training, which is a significant limitation.
- The paper's experimental results are not convincing, with some figures (e.g., Figure 4) being misleading, and the results not being consistently superior to existing methods.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors provide a detailed discussion on the limitations of the proposed method, particularly concerning its scalability and computational cost?
- How does the proposed method compare with other state-of-the-art methods, especially those that use hierarchical VAEs or other multimodal VAEs that have been proposed recently?
- Why was the decision made to not include a comparison with other baseline methods, such as those mentioned in the review?
- Can the authors clarify the role of the coherence guidance in the method, particularly how it affects the results, especially in scenarios where it is not used?
- How does the proposed method handle the issue of missing modalities during training, and what are the implications of this limitation on the model's performance?
- Can the authors provide more detailed experimental results, especially for the CelebAMask-HQ dataset, to better demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed method?
