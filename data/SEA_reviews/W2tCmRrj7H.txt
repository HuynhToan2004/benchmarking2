 **Summary:**
The paper introduces FlexGen-EHR, a novel generative model designed to handle missing modalities in heterogeneous tabular EHR data. It employs a latent diffusion model and an optimal transport module to ensure the consistency of static and temporal features across different modalities. The model is evaluated against various baselines and shows superior performance in terms of fidelity and utility. The paper also discusses the challenges of handling missing data in EHR and provides a method for handling such data using a latent space alignment technique. However, concerns are raised about the novelty of the approach and the clarity of the presentation, which may hinder understanding of the method's contributions and its applicability in real-world scenarios.

**Strengths:**
- The paper addresses a significant problem in the healthcare domain by focusing on the generation of heterogeneous tabular EHRs, which is a crucial area for medical research and data privacy.
- The proposed method is technically sound, with a clear presentation of the methodology and results, making it accessible to readers.
- The paper is well-organized and easy to follow, with a comprehensive literature review that positions the work within the existing body of research.
- The experimental results demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed method in handling missing modalities and generating realistic and useful synthetic EHRs.
- The paper includes a detailed evaluation of the model's performance against various baselines, showing that the proposed method outperforms existing models in terms of fidelity and utility.
- The use of an optimal transport module to align static and temporal features is a novel approach that enhances the model's ability to handle heterogeneous EHR data.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a clear explanation of the key contributions, particularly how the proposed method addresses the challenges of handling missing modalities and the specific advantages of using a latent diffusion model.
- There is a lack of detailed discussion on the limitations of the method, including the assumptions made and the potential biases introduced by the model.
- The paper does not sufficiently compare the proposed method with other state-of-the-art methods in the field, which could have provided a more robust evaluation of the model's effectiveness.
- The presentation of the paper could be improved, particularly in terms of clarity and coherence, especially in the methodology section.
- The paper does not sufficiently address the ethical considerations of generating synthetic EHRs, which is a critical aspect of medical data privacy and security.
- The paper's claims about the novelty of the approach are not well-supported, and it does not sufficiently distinguish its contributions from existing methods.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors provide more details on the specific advantages of using a latent diffusion model over other methods, and how this model addresses the challenges of handling missing modalities?
- How does the proposed method handle the issue of mode collapse, which is a common problem in generative models?
- Could the authors clarify the methodology used in the experiments, particularly the settings and parameters used in the ablation studies?
- How does the proposed method ensure the privacy and security of the synthetic EHRs, especially in light of potential re-identification risks?
- Could the authors provide a more detailed comparison with other state-of-the-art methods in the field, including a discussion on the ethical implications of generating synthetic EHRs?
- How does the proposed method handle the issue of missing modality in the data, and what are the implications of this on the model's performance and the accuracy of the generated data?
