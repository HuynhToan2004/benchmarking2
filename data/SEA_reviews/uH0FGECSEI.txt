 **Summary:**
The paper introduces Expected Flow Networks (EFlowNets) and Adversarial Flow Networks (AFlowNets) to extend Generative Flow Networks (GFlowNets) for stochastic and adversarial environments. EFlowNets aim to satisfy detailed balance constraints under stochastic transitions, while AFlowNets are used for two-player zero-sum games. The proposed methods are evaluated on tasks such as protein design and games like tic-tac-toe and Connect-4, showing competitive performance against existing methods like AlphaZero. The paper also discusses the theoretical underpinnings of these models and their implications for robustness in stochastic environments.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, with clear explanations of the problem and the proposed solutions, making it accessible to a broad audience.
- The authors introduce a novel approach by integrating GFlowNets with stochastic and adversarial environments, which is a significant advancement in the field.
- The theoretical analysis provided is robust, with proofs and propositions that support the claims made.
- The experimental results demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed methods, showing competitive performance against established baselines.
- The paper addresses a timely and important problem in the field of reinforcement learning, focusing on robustness in stochastic environments.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper could benefit from a more thorough discussion of related work, particularly in the context of GFlowNets and their extensions.
- The experimental evaluation could be improved by including more diverse tasks and baselines, especially in stochastic environments where the proposed methods are applied.
- The paper lacks a detailed discussion on the limitations of the proposed methods, which could help in understanding their applicability and potential drawbacks.
- There is a need for clearer explanations and examples to aid in understanding the mathematical formulations and their implications.
- The paper could benefit from additional experiments and discussions to better illustrate the advantages of the proposed methods over existing ones.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors provide more detailed explanations of the mathematical formulations, particularly those related to the stochastic environment and the reward functions used in the experiments?
- How do the authors justify the choice of tasks for evaluating the robustness of the proposed methods, and could additional tasks be included to strengthen the evaluation?
- Is there a plan to open-source the code for the proposed methods to facilitate further research and reproducibility?
- Could the authors clarify the role and impact of the hyperparameter λ in the experiments, especially in relation to the performance of AFlowNets in different scenarios?
- How do the authors address the potential limitations of their methods, and what are the implications of these limitations in practical applications?
