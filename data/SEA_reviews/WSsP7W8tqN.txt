 **Summary:**
The paper investigates the phenomenon of "grokking" in neural networks through the lens of the lottery ticket hypothesis. It proposes that finding good subnetworks, termed "grokking tickets," is critical for understanding the transition from memorization to generalization in neural networks. The paper conducts experiments on various architectures and datasets, including modular addition and MNIST, to demonstrate that grokking tickets can accelerate the generalization process compared to dense networks or lottery tickets identified before generalization. It also explores the relationship between grokking and sparsity, showing that the sparsity of networks is crucial for achieving the generalized solution. Despite its innovative approach, the paper faces criticism for its limited scope, focusing on only a few datasets and architectures, and for not sufficiently exploring the underlying mechanisms of the grokking phenomenon.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel perspective by connecting the grokking phenomenon with the lottery ticket hypothesis, offering a fresh angle for understanding the dynamics of neural network training.
- The experimental setup is well-designed, with a focus on identifying the critical pruning rate for achieving grokking, which is a significant contribution to the field.
- The paper is well-written, making it easy to follow and understand, with clear figures and a logical structure that aids in comprehension.
- The study provides a comprehensive analysis of the grokking phenomenon, including a thorough investigation into the relationship between grokking and sparsity, which is a critical aspect of neural network training.
- The paper is the first to study the grokking phenomenon using the lottery ticket hypothesis, which is a significant contribution to the field of neural network research.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper's scope is limited, focusing on only a few datasets and architectures, which may not adequately represent the generalizability of the findings.
- There is a lack of exploration into the underlying mechanisms of the grokking phenomenon, which could limit the depth of understanding and the applicability of the findings.
- The paper's reliance on empirical evidence without a deeper theoretical analysis may limit its impact and relevance to the broader neural network community.
- The paper does not adequately address the limitations of its work, such as the lack of a comprehensive theoretical framework or the generalizability of the findings to more complex or varied datasets and architectures.
- There is a need for more detailed explanations and justifications for certain experimental choices and methodologies used in the study.
