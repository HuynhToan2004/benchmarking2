 **Summary:**
The paper introduces VTruST, a framework designed to enhance the trustworthiness of AI models by focusing on data selection for training. VTruST employs a novel online version of the OMP algorithm to solve the online sparse approximation problem, which is crucial for ensuring that selected data points contribute effectively to the model's training. The framework aims to balance fairness, robustness, and accuracy by defining value functions for each metric and allowing users to specify the importance of each. Experiments are conducted on various datasets to demonstrate the effectiveness of VTruST, showing that it can outperform state-of-the-art baselines in terms of fairness and robustness. However, the paper's contribution is questioned due to its similarity to existing methods and the limited scope of its experiments.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel online version of the OMP algorithm for solving the online sparse approximation problem, which is a significant contribution to the field.
- The framework proposed, VTruST, is designed to balance fairness, robustness, and accuracy by defining value functions for each metric and allowing users to specify the importance of each.
- The paper is well-written, easy to follow, and provides a clear motivation for the proposed framework.
- Experiments are conducted on various datasets, demonstrating the effectiveness of VTruST in terms of fairness and robustness.
- The paper addresses a timely and important problem in AI, focusing on data-centric approaches for trustworthy AI.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper does not sufficiently differentiate its method from existing methods like OMP, and the novelty of the proposed algorithm is not clearly established.
- The experimental results are not comprehensive enough, with limited datasets used and a lack of comparisons with other relevant methods.
- The paper lacks a detailed discussion on the computational complexity and scalability of the proposed method, which is crucial for practical applications.
- The paper could benefit from more detailed theoretical analysis and a broader range of experiments to validate the effectiveness of the proposed method across different scenarios and datasets.
- The writing quality of the paper needs improvement, particularly in the clarity and organization of the content.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors clarify the novelty of their method compared to existing methods like OMP?
- How does the proposed method perform in terms of computational complexity and scalability, especially when dealing with large datasets?
- What is the rationale behind the choice of datasets for the experiments, and why were only certain metrics used?
- Could the authors provide more detailed comparisons with other relevant methods to establish the effectiveness of their proposed method?
- How does the proposed method handle the trade-offs between different trustworthiness metrics, especially in scenarios where these metrics conflict?
- Can the authors provide more detailed explanations for the experimental results, especially the differences in performance between VTruST and other methods?
