 **Summary:**
The paper explores the relationship between Wasserstein GANs (WGANs) and the Wasserstein distance, focusing on how different discriminator architectures influence the minimized distance. Specifically, it investigates the behavior of WGANs when the discriminator is either fully connected or convolutional, demonstrating that the former minimizes the image Wasserstein distance while the latter minimizes the patch Wasserstein distance. The authors argue that this discrepancy in behavior is crucial for understanding the effectiveness of WGANs in generating sharp, realistic images. Theoretical insights are provided alongside empirical evidence, showing that WGANs can indeed minimize the Wasserstein distance, albeit in different forms depending on the discriminator's architecture.

**Strengths:**
- The paper provides a novel perspective on the success of WGANs by demonstrating that they can effectively minimize the Wasserstein distance, contrary to some claims that they do not.
- The theoretical analysis and empirical evidence presented are compelling, showing that the choice of discriminator architecture significantly influences the type of Wasserstein distance minimized by WGANs.
- The paper is well-written, clearly explaining complex concepts and providing intuitive insights into the behavior of WGANs.
- The authors have conducted extensive experiments and comparisons, which support their claims and provide valuable insights into the generation of sharp, realistic images.
- The paper introduces the concept of "location-specific patch Wasserstein distance," which is an interesting and potentially impactful contribution to the field.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper's scope is somewhat limited, focusing primarily on WGANs and not exploring other types of GANs or more recent advancements in GAN research.
- The theoretical results are somewhat hand-wavy and lack rigorous mathematical proofs, which could undermine the paper's credibility.
- The experiments are limited to simple GANs, and it is unclear how the results would apply to more complex or state-of-the-art GANs.
- The paper does not adequately address the implications of its findings for practical applications, such as improving GAN training or designing better discriminator architectures.
- There are some typographical errors and unclear explanations in the text, which could confuse readers.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors elaborate on how the findings of this paper could be applied to improve GAN training or design better discriminator architectures?
- How do the results of this paper compare to other types of GANs, such as those that do not use the Wasserstein distance?
- In light of the limitations in the scope of the experiments, how do the authors plan to extend their research to include more complex GANs and real-world applications?
- Can the authors provide more detailed explanations or mathematical proofs for the theoretical results presented in the paper?
- How do the authors address the potential issues with overfitting in WGANs when the latent vector is continuous?
- Could the authors clarify the role of the GAP layer in the discriminator and its impact on the minimized Wasserstein distance?
