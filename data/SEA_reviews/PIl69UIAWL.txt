 **Summary:**
The paper introduces GraphLLM, a novel approach that integrates graph learning models with large language models (LLMs) to enhance graph reasoning capabilities. GraphLLM leverages a graph transformer to learn graph structures and a textual transformer for node descriptions, which are then integrated through prefix tuning. The model is evaluated on four graph reasoning tasks, demonstrating significant improvements over baseline methods, particularly in context reduction and computational efficiency. The authors argue that Graph2Text, a common method for converting graphs to text, is a significant bottleneck in LLM performance on graph reasoning tasks. However, the paper is criticized for its limited scope in terms of the complexity and diversity of graph reasoning tasks, and the lack of comparison with more recent and diverse models.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, clear, and easy to follow, with a clear motivation and a novel approach that integrates graph learning models with LLMs.
- The proposed GraphLLM achieves significant improvements in graph reasoning tasks, particularly in context reduction and computational efficiency.

- The experimental results demonstrate that GraphLLM outperforms the baseline method in terms of performance and efficiency, showcasing its practical utility.
