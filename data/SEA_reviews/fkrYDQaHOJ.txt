 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel method for modeling nonlinear dynamics in reinforcement learning environments using Koopman theory, a mathematical framework for studying the dynamics of nonlinear systems. The authors propose a method that learns a linear dynamics model in a high-dimensional latent space, which can be efficiently parallelized during training for long-range dynamics modeling. The approach is supported by empirical evaluations on various MuJoCo tasks, demonstrating its effectiveness in modeling dynamics over extended horizons. The paper also discusses the potential benefits of this method for reinforcement learning, such as enhanced training stability and gradient control.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel approach to modeling long-range dynamics in reinforcement learning environments, leveraging Koopman theory to create a bijective mapping to linear dynamics in a high-dimensional latent space.
- The proposed method is computationally efficient and can be easily incorporated into dynamics modeling for model-based planning and model-free RL, potentially improving the efficiency and accuracy of dynamics modeling.
- The paper is well-written, clear, and provides a comprehensive background on Koopman theory, making it accessible to readers unfamiliar with the subject.
- The method is empirically evaluated on several MuJoCo environments, showing promising results in forward state and reward prediction.
- The paper provides a theoretical analysis of the gradient flow through time, which is crucial for understanding the dynamics of the model.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed discussion on the choice of the Koopman operator and its impact on the model's performance, particularly in relation to the selection of eigenvalues.
- There is a lack of clarity on how the model handles stochastic environments, which is a significant limitation given the prevalence of stochastic dynamics in many real-world applications.
- The paper does not provide sufficient ablation studies to demonstrate the effectiveness of the Koopman-based model over other baseline models, particularly in scenarios where the model's performance is marginal or comparable to existing methods.
- The paper's claims about the linearization of nonlinear dynamics in a high-dimensional latent space are not adequately supported by empirical evidence, which could undermine the credibility of the proposed method.
- There is a lack of discussion on the limitations of the proposed method, particularly in terms of its applicability to more complex or stochastic environments.

**Questions:**
- How is the Koopman operator chosen, and what are the implications of this choice on the model's performance?
- Can the authors provide more details on how the model handles stochastic environments, particularly in terms of modeling uncertainty in dynamics?
- What are the specific advantages of using a Koopman-based model over other existing models, such as those based on Kalman filters or other linear dynamical systems?
- How does the proposed method compare to other state-of-the-art methods in terms of performance and computational efficiency?
- Can the authors clarify the method's handling of stochastic dynamics and discuss potential modifications or extensions of the method to handle more complex or stochastic environments?
- What are the implications of the model's assumptions regarding the linearity of dynamics in the latent space, and how do these assumptions affect the model's applicability and effectiveness?
