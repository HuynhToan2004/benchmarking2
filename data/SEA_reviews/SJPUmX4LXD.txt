 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel approach to reconstruct music from human brain activity using functional magnetic resonance imaging (fMRI) data. This method employs either music retrieval or the MusicLM model conditioned on embeddings derived from fMRI data to generate music that resembles the original stimuli in terms of semantic properties like genre, instrumentation, and mood. The study also explores the relationship between different components of MusicLM and brain activity through voxel-wise encoding modeling analysis. Despite the innovative application of deep learning models to neuroscience, the paper has been critiqued for its limited novelty, the lack of clarity in methodological descriptions, and insufficient experimental validation.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is the first to use a deep learning model to decode music from human brain activity, which is a novel and interesting approach.
- The research question is well-motivated, and the paper is well-written, making it easy to follow.
- The results are promising, showing that the model can effectively predict music embeddings from fMRI signals, which is a significant achievement in the field.
- The paper includes a detailed description of the dataset, which is beneficial for reproducibility and transparency.
- The study addresses an important and timely topic in the field of music and brain science, which has significant implications for understanding the neural basis of music perception and generation.

**Weaknesses:**
- The novelty of the paper is limited as it primarily uses existing models like MusicLM and MuLan without significant modification or innovation.
- The paper lacks clarity in methodological descriptions, particularly in the decoding process, which is crucial for understanding the model's effectiveness.
- The experimental validation is insufficient, with only five participants used in the study, which limits the generalizability of the findings.
- The paper does not adequately address the limitations of its method, such as the potential for overfitting due to the small size of the training dataset.
- The paper does not include a direct comparison of the generated music with the original stimuli, which is crucial for evaluating the model's effectiveness in reconstructing the original music.
- The paper's focus on semantic properties of music, such as genre and instrumentation, may not fully capture the complexity and richness of musical experiences.

**Questions:**
- Can you clarify the decoding process, particularly how the model predicts music embeddings from fMRI signals?
- How does the model handle the potential for overfitting given the small size of the training dataset?
- Could you provide more details on the selection of the top six ROIs for the ensemble model, and how this affects the model's performance?
- How does the model ensure that the generated music is not just a random combination of musical concepts but accurately represents the original stimuli?
- Given the limitations of the current model, what are the potential directions for future research to improve the model's performance and generalizability?
