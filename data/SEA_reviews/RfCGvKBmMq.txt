 **Summary:**
The paper introduces the Representation Matching Information Bottleneck (RMIB) to align the distribution of text representations in asymmetrical domains, aiming to improve text matching performance. It theoretically proves that RMIB is equivalent to optimizing the information bottleneck, enhancing the generalization ability of text matching in different domains. The authors provide experimental evidence supporting the effectiveness of RMIB across various text matching models and datasets. Despite its innovative approach, the paper faces criticism for its limited novelty, insufficient theoretical proof, and lack of clarity in some sections.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel approach, the Representation Matching Information Bottleneck (RMIB), which aligns the distribution of text representations to enhance the generalization ability of text matching in asymmetrical domains.
- The theoretical proof that RMIB is equivalent to optimizing the information bottleneck is well-articulated, providing a solid foundation for the proposed method.
- The paper is well-organized, with clear figures and tables that aid in understanding the proposed method and its experimental results.
- The experimental results demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed method, showing improvements in text matching performance across various models and datasets.
- The paper addresses the significant issue of domain matching in text matching, which is crucial for improving the generalization ability of text matching tasks.

**Weaknesses:**
- The novelty of the paper is questioned, as the concept of aligning the distribution of text representations is similar to existing methods like DDR-Match, and the theoretical proof lacks depth and clarity.
- The paper suffers from unclear writing in some sections, particularly in the explanation of key concepts such as the interaction between text representations and the necessity of aligning text representations.
- The experimental results, while showing improvements, are not sufficiently convincing due to the lack of detailed comparisons with existing methods and insufficient statistical analysis.
- The paper does not adequately address the computational complexity of the proposed method, which could limit its practical application in large-scale text matching scenarios.
- The paper lacks a comprehensive literature review, particularly in the area of text matching and information bottleneck theory, which could enhance the paper's contribution to the field.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors clarify the differences between RMIB and IB, and provide a more detailed explanation of the interaction between text representations and the necessity of aligning text representations?
- How does the paper address the computational complexity of the proposed method, and what are the practical implications for its application in large-scale text matching scenarios?
- Could the authors provide a more detailed comparison with existing methods, particularly in terms of the experimental setup and results, to better demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed method?
- How does the paper address the limitations discussed, such as the lack of a comprehensive literature review and the insufficient theoretical proof?
- Could the authors elaborate on the choice of the prior distribution and its impact on the results, and provide more insights into the practical implications of the proposed method in real-world applications?
