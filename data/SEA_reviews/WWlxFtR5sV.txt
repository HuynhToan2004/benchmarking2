 **Summary:**
This paper explores the challenges in training physics-informed neural networks (PINNs) by focusing on the conditioning of the operator associated with the Hermitian square of the differential operator of the underlying PDE. The authors demonstrate that the training difficulties are closely linked to the conditioning of this operator, and propose a preconditioning strategy to improve training. The paper provides both theoretical analysis and empirical evaluations to support the proposed method, showing that it can effectively precondition the critical operator and enhance training. The methodology is supported by a rigorous mathematical analysis and empirical results, which suggest that the preconditioned operator can significantly improve the training process.

**Strengths:**
- The paper provides a rigorous mathematical analysis to explain the training difficulties of physics-informed neural networks (PINNs) and proposes a preconditioning strategy to address these issues.
- The preconditioning strategy is supported by both theoretical analysis and empirical evaluations, demonstrating its effectiveness in improving the training process.
- The paper is well-written, clear, and easy to follow, making it accessible to a broad audience.
- The proposed preconditioning strategy is novel and could be applied to other machine learning models that involve differential operators.
- The paper is significant as it addresses a critical issue in the training of PINNs, which is crucial for advancing the application of these models in scientific computing.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper is limited to linear PDEs, which may not generalize well to nonlinear PDEs, where the training difficulties are more pronounced.
- The preconditioning strategy is specific to the linear PDEs and may not be applicable to nonlinear models such as neural networks, which are widely used in PINNs.
- The paper does not provide a clear discussion on how the preconditioning strategy can be extended to nonlinear models or how it compares to existing methods like hard boundary conditions or domain decomposition.
- The paper could benefit from a more detailed discussion on the limitations of the proposed preconditioning strategy, particularly in the context of nonlinear models and high-dimensional problems.
- The paper does not include a comprehensive discussion on the computational complexity of the preconditioning strategy, which could be a significant concern for practical applications.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors provide a detailed discussion on how the preconditioning strategy can be extended to nonlinear models?
- How does the proposed preconditioning strategy compare to existing methods like hard boundary conditions or domain decomposition?
- Can the authors provide a detailed discussion on the computational complexity of the preconditioning strategy and how it scales with the size of the problem?
- How does the proposed preconditioning strategy perform when applied to nonlinear PDEs, and what are the limitations of its application in such cases?
- Can the authors clarify the assumptions made in the paper and how they affect the applicability of the results to different types of PDEs?
