 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel regularization method termed "activation hue" to optimize the learning process in deep convolutional neural networks (CNNs). This method is inspired by the concept of hue in RGB color space, aiming to enhance the learning of activation hue angles in both the image plane and the multi-channel activation space. The authors propose a new loss function, hue loss, which is added to the standard one-hot loss to improve classification performance. The method's effectiveness is demonstrated through experiments on various datasets, including ImageNet, and the paper includes a detailed analysis of the impact of the activation hue on CNN performance.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel regularization method, "activation hue," which is a significant contribution to the field of deep learning, particularly in the context of optimizing the learning process in deep convolutional neural networks (CNNs).
- The method is well-explained and supported by a detailed analysis, including experiments that demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed activation hue loss in improving classification performance on various datasets.
- The paper is well-written, making it accessible and easy to follow, which is crucial for a broad audience in the field of computer vision.
- The proposed method is simple and effective, with a focus on the use of activation hue loss to complement standard one-hot loss, which enhances the performance of CNNs.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a thorough comparison with existing methods, particularly in terms of performance and computational efficiency. This limits the ability to fully assess the effectiveness and practicality of the proposed method.
- The methodology and experimental setup are not clearly explained, making it difficult for readers to fully understand the experimental conditions and the implications of the results.
- The paper does not provide a comprehensive analysis of the limitations of the proposed method, which could help in understanding its scope and applicability.
- The paper's structure and presentation could be improved, particularly in the introduction and methodology sections, to better guide the reader through the content and highlight the key contributions and findings.
- The paper lacks a detailed discussion on the potential negative societal impacts of the proposed method, which is an important consideration for ethical and responsible AI development.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors provide more details on the computational efficiency of the proposed method compared to existing methods?
- How does the proposed method compare to other regularization methods, such as those based on geometric regularization?
- Could the authors clarify the experimental setup, particularly the conditions under which the proposed method was tested and the specifics of the datasets used?
- Is there a possibility to extend the proposed method to other types of neural networks, such as transformers?
- How does the proposed method perform in scenarios where the activation hue is not well-defined or in the presence of noise or other disturbances?
- Can the authors provide more details on the training procedure, particularly how the angular labels are assigned and how they influence the training process?
- What are the potential negative societal impacts of the proposed method, and how are these addressed in the paper?
