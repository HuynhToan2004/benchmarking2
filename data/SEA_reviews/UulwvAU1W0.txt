 **Summary:**
The paper introduces FourTran, a novel method for pick-and-place tasks in 2D and 3D environments, leveraging bi-equivariance to improve sample efficiency in robot learning. FourTran uses a fiber space Fourier transformation to construct a bi-equivariant architecture, enabling the handling of high resolution features without extensive sampling. The method is tested on the RLbench benchmark and shows state-of-the-art results, demonstrating its effectiveness in complex robotic manipulation tasks. The paper also provides theoretical analysis and empirical evidence, highlighting the method's potential in various applications.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written and provides a clear theoretical analysis, making it accessible to readers.
- The proposed method, FourTran, is innovative and shows promising results, particularly in improving sample efficiency for pick-and-place tasks.
- The empirical results demonstrate significant improvements over existing methods, highlighting the effectiveness of the proposed approach.
- The use of bi-equivariant architecture in robot learning is a novel and significant contribution to the field.
- The paper provides a detailed mathematical analysis of the method, which enhances the understanding of the proposed approach.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed discussion on the limitations of the method, which could provide a more comprehensive understanding of its applicability and potential drawbacks.
- There is a need for more detailed explanations and examples in the method section, particularly in the definitions and usage of symbols and terms.
- The paper does not provide sufficient implementation details, which could hinder reproducibility and further research.
- The experimental setup and comparisons are limited, and the paper could benefit from more extensive testing across different scenarios and baseline methods.
- The paper could be improved by addressing the minor typographical and formatting errors noted in the manuscript.
