 **Summary:**
The paper presents two new algorithms, DS-DQN and AS-PPO, designed to enhance the robustness of deep reinforcement learning (DRL) agents. It critiques the existing CROP method, highlighting issues such as a significant trade-off between utility and robustness, overestimation of robustness, and ineffective adversarial attacks. The authors propose new smoothing strategies and stronger adversarial attacks to address these issues. DS-DQN and AS-PPO are evaluated against the CROP method and other baselines, showing improvements in both clean reward and robustness certification. However, concerns were raised about the clarity of the paper's presentation, the robustness of the results, and the need for a broader evaluation across different environments and attack methods.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, with a clear and detailed presentation of the proposed methods.
- The introduction of denoised smoothing and the use of a denoiser to remove noise from the input state is a novel approach.
- The paper addresses the significant trade-off between utility and robustness in CROP, proposing new smoothing strategies and stronger adversarial attacks to mitigate this issue.
- The proposed algorithms, DS-DQN and AS-PPO, demonstrate better trade-offs between clean reward and robustness certification compared to existing methods.
- The paper includes extensive experimental results and ablation studies, which support the claims made.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper's presentation could be improved, particularly in the clarity and organization of the content, especially in sections 3.1 and 3.2.
- The experiments are limited to a few environments (Atari and Mujoco), and the results are not convincingly robust across all tested scenarios.
- There is a lack of clarity regarding the robustness of the proposed methods, particularly in how the robustness is evaluated and what constitutes a robust policy.
- The paper does not adequately address the issue of overestimation of robustness in CROP, and the proposed methods may not fully resolve this issue.
- The paper does not discuss the computational efficiency of the proposed methods, which could be a significant concern for practical applications.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors clarify the role and impact of the denoiser in the proposed methods?
- How does the proposed method ensure that the policy remains robust across different attack methods, not just the one used in the evaluation?
- Can the authors provide more details on the experimental setup, particularly the number of trials and the statistical significance of the results?
- What are the implications of using a smaller smoothing factor, and how does this affect the clean reward and certified radius?
- How does the proposed method compare to other existing robust DRL methods, such as those mentioned in the literature review?
- Could the authors elaborate on the computational efficiency of their methods and discuss any potential trade-offs between computational cost and robustness?
