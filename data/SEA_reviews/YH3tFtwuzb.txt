 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a differentially private (DP) variant of the BiTFiT method, which optimizes the bias terms of pretrained models to achieve parameter and computational efficiency. The proposed method, named DP-BiTFiT, does not require forward caching of activation tensors, thereby avoiding the memory overhead associated with full fine-tuning. It is tested across various tasks including natural language and computer vision, showing improvements in accuracy and efficiency compared to existing methods. The paper also discusses the trade-offs between privacy and utility, providing a detailed analysis of the method's performance under different privacy settings.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, with clear and concise explanations of the proposed method and its advantages over existing methods.
- The authors provide a thorough analysis of the time and space complexity of their method, demonstrating its efficiency in terms of computational resources and memory usage.
- The paper includes a comprehensive comparison with state-of-the-art methods, showing that DP-BiTFiT outperforms them in terms of accuracy and efficiency.
- The paper is well-structured, making it easy to follow, and includes detailed explanations of the methodology and its implementation.
- The proposed method is model-agnostic, making it applicable to a wide range of models and tasks, and does not require significant modifications to the model architecture.
- The paper includes a detailed discussion on the trade-offs between privacy and utility, which is crucial for understanding the implications of using DP-BiTFiT in practical scenarios.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed discussion on the privacy guarantees of the method, which is crucial for understanding the implications of using DP-BiTFiT in practical scenarios.
- There is a lack of experimental results under different privacy settings, which is necessary to demonstrate the robustness of the method under varying levels of privacy constraints.
- The paper does not provide a comprehensive comparison with other differential privacy fine-tuning methods, which could help in understanding the relative performance of DP-BiTFiT.
- The paper could benefit from a more detailed discussion on the limitations of the proposed method, particularly in scenarios where it may not perform as well as other methods.
- The paper does not provide code, which could limit the reproducibility of the results.
