 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel method for enhancing the robustness of neural networks against adversarial attacks by selectively injecting noise into non-critical neurons. This approach is supported by a learning-based method that identifies essential neurons and perturbs non-essential ones, aiming to preserve clean accuracy while improving adversarial robustness. The method is validated through experiments on various datasets and models, showing improvements in robustness against different attack methods. However, concerns were raised about the clarity of the methodology, the robustness of the results, and the overall novelty of the approach.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, easy to follow, and provides a clear motivation for the proposed method.
- The proposed method is simple and effective, with good results demonstrated across various datasets and models.
- The approach of selectively injecting noise into non-critical neurons is novel and potentially beneficial for practical applications.
- The paper includes both theoretical and empirical evidence to support the proposed method.

**Weaknesses:**
- The methodology and some experimental results are not clearly presented, making it difficult to understand the specifics of the experiments and their implications.
- The paper lacks a comprehensive comparison with existing methods, which limits the understanding of the proposed method's advantages and disadvantages.
- The theoretical underpinnings of the method, particularly the claims about the importance of non-critical neurons, are not well-supported.
- The paper does not adequately address the potential negative societal impacts of the proposed method.
- The paper could benefit from more detailed explanations of the experimental setup and results, including a clearer presentation of the noise injection process and its effects on different model architectures.

**Questions:**
- Could you clarify the experimental setup and results, particularly how the noise is injected into non-critical neurons and how this affects the model's performance?
- How does the proposed method compare to other noise injection methods, such as those that use uniform noise or other types of noise?
- What are the specific advantages of the proposed method over other methods in terms of computational efficiency and robustness?
- Can you provide more detailed theoretical support for the claims about the importance of non-critical neurons and the effectiveness of the proposed method?
- How does the method perform under different attack strengths and model architectures, and what are the implications of these findings for practical applications?
