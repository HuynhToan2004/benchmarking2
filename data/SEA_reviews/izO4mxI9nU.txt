 **Summary:**
The paper introduces HAICO-CN, a novel human-AI collaborative framework aimed at enhancing joint decision-making through personalized model training and a cluster-wise noisy-label augmentation technique. This methodology clusters raters based on their labeling patterns to generate a sufficient number of labels for training, allowing for the selection of a cluster-specific human-AI model during inference. The framework is evaluated across various datasets, including CIFAR-10N, CIFAR-10H, Fashion-MNIST-H, and Chaoyang histopathology, demonstrating superior performance compared to existing methods. The paper also proposes new evaluation criteria and a model-agnostic approach, which is simple to implement and does not require ground truth labels.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel human-AI collaborative framework, HAICO-CN, which personalizes models based on user patterns, enhancing joint decision-making performance.
- It proposes a new noisy-label augmentation technique that generates sufficient noisy labels for training, and a new evaluation criteria for assessing human-AI collaborative frameworks.
- The method is model-agnostic and does not require ground truth labels, making it simple to implement and applicable to various datasets.
- The paper is well-written, easy to follow, and provides extensive experiments on multiple datasets, including CIFAR-10N, CIFAR-10H, Fashion-MNIST-H, and Chaoyang histopathology, demonstrating the effectiveness of the proposed method.
- The proposed method is evaluated using new evaluation criteria, which are innovative and contribute to the field of human-AI collaboration.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a clear explanation of the rationale behind the proposed method, particularly the choice of using a cluster-wise noisy-label augmentation technique and the selection of a cluster-specific human-AI model based on user patterns.
- The method's reliance on the assumption that noise patterns vary across different user groups may not hold true in real-world scenarios where users may exhibit similar noisy patterns.
- The method's effectiveness is not adequately demonstrated through experiments, and the paper does not include a comparison with state-of-the-art methods or a detailed analysis of the performance of the proposed method.
- The paper lacks a discussion on the limitations of the proposed method, which is crucial for understanding its applicability and potential drawbacks.
- The presentation of the paper could be improved by reducing the use of abbreviations and acronyms, and by including more figures and tables to better illustrate the results.
