 **Summary:**
The paper introduces StyleAdapter, a method designed to generate stylized images using a unified model that integrates textual prompts with style references without requiring test-time fine-tuning. The method employs a two-path cross-attention module and a semantic suppressing vision model to process textual prompts and style references separately, enhancing the control over content and suppressing semantic information from style images. The model is evaluated against various baselines and shown to produce high-quality images that balance content and style faithfully. The paper also discusses the method's integration with existing controllable synthesis methods and its potential to improve the efficiency and flexibility of text-to-image synthesis.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel method for stylized image generation that does not require test-time fine-tuning, which is a significant advancement over existing methods.
- The method is evaluated against various baselines and shown to produce high-quality images that balance content and style faithfully.
- The paper is well-written, clear, and easy to follow, making it accessible to a broad audience.
- The proposed method is innovative and addresses the challenge of accurately extracting style information from style images, enhancing the control over content and suppressing semantic information from style references.
- The paper includes a detailed ablation study and user study, providing a robust evaluation of the method's effectiveness and user preference.
- The method can be integrated with existing controllable synthesis methods, such as T2I-adapter and ControlNet, to enhance the controllability and stability of text-to-image synthesis.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper does not discuss the potential limitations of the method, such as the generalization of the proposed method to other styles or the potential for unethical image generation.
- The paper lacks a detailed discussion on how the method handles different styles and the potential for overfitting to the training set.
- The method's performance compared to other methods like LoRA and TI is not discussed, which could provide valuable insights into the method's effectiveness.
- The paper does not provide a detailed analysis of the computational efficiency or the scalability of the method, which are crucial for practical applications.
- The method's reliance on a pre-trained stable diffusion model could limit its applicability to different datasets, and the generated data is constrained by the dataset distribution used for training the stable diffusion.
- The paper does not provide a detailed analysis of the method's limitations, which could help in understanding its scope and applicability.

**Questions:**
- How does the method handle different styles, and what are the potential limitations in generalizing the proposed method to other styles?
- Could the authors provide a more detailed analysis of the computational efficiency and scalability of the method?
- How does the method compare in terms of performance to other methods like LoRA and TI, particularly in handling different styles and computational efficiency?
- What are the potential limitations of the method, and how do these affect its practical applicability and generalization to different datasets?
- Could the authors discuss the potential ethical implications of the method, particularly concerning the generation of unethical images?
