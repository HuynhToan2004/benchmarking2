 **Summary:**
The paper investigates the expressive power of graph neural networks (GNNs) through the lens of the Weisfeiler-Lehman (WL) test, focusing on the WL-dimension of graph motif parameters. It characterizes the WL-dimension for subgraph and induced subgraph counting problems, demonstrating that these problems are interconnected components of a broader problem termed "graph motif parameters." The paper also proposes a polynomial time algorithm for determining the WL-dimension of subgraph counting, resolving an open question from prior work. It further explores the computational aspects of graph motif parameters and their relationship to GNNs.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel perspective on understanding the expressive power of GNNs, focusing on the WL-dimension of graph motif parameters, which is a significant contribution to the field.
- It presents a polynomial time algorithm for determining the WL-dimension of subgraph counting, which is a notable advancement in the field.
- The paper is well-written, with clear and detailed explanations of the results and proofs, making it accessible to readers.
- The authors have effectively integrated existing results from graph theory and counting complexity to provide a comprehensive understanding of the WL-dimension of graph motif parameters.
- The paper addresses an important problem in the expressive power of GNNs, which is crucial for the development of more effective and efficient GNNs.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper's main contribution, Theorem 2, is seen as a simple extension of prior work, which might limit its perceived novelty.
- There is a lack of discussion on the practical implications of the results, particularly how the theoretical findings can be applied in real-world scenarios.
- The paper could benefit from a more detailed comparison with existing methods, such as the oblivious k-WL test, to highlight the advantages of the proposed approach.
- Some sections of the paper, particularly the discussion on graph motif parameters, are not as clear or detailed as they could be, which might hinder understanding for readers not familiar with the topic.
- There is a lack of experimental validation, which could have strengthened the paper's claims by demonstrating the practical applicability of the theoretical results.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors provide a more detailed comparison with the oblivious k-WL test to highlight the advantages of the proposed approach?
- How does the proposed method compare in terms of computational efficiency and practical applicability to existing methods for counting subgraphs and induced subgraphs?
- Can the authors provide examples or scenarios where the theoretical results of the paper can be applied in real-world scenarios?
- What are the potential applications of the proposed method in graph-based machine learning tasks, and how does it compare to other methods in terms of performance and efficiency?
- Could the authors clarify the relationship between graph motif parameters and the expressive power of GNNs, particularly how the results relate to the expressive power of GNNs?
