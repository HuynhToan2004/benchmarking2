 **Summary:**
The paper introduces EZ-CLIP, a novel adaptation of the CLIP model for zero-shot video action recognition. EZ-CLIP leverages temporal visual prompting and motion loss to enhance the model's ability to learn temporal aspects in videos, while preserving its generalization capabilities. The model is trained with minimal learnable parameters, making it computationally efficient and capable of outperforming existing methods in terms of performance and efficiency. Extensive experiments on five benchmark datasets demonstrate the model's robustness and generalization capabilities, particularly in scenarios where motion plays a crucial role. However, concerns about the clarity of the paper's presentation and the fairness of the comparison with other models, particularly in terms of computational efficiency and parameter count, have been raised.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written and easy to follow, with clear explanations and detailed supplementary materials.
- The proposed EZ-CLIP model is efficient and effective, demonstrating superior performance on multiple benchmark datasets.
- The use of temporal visual prompting and motion loss is novel and contributes to the model's ability to learn temporal aspects in videos.
- The model is computationally efficient, requiring minimal learnable parameters and capable of training on a single GPU, which is a significant advantage in terms of practical application.
- The paper includes a comprehensive ablation study that supports the effectiveness of the proposed components.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks clarity in some sections, particularly in the descriptions of the model's architecture and the training process.
- The comparison with other models in terms of computational efficiency and parameter count may not be entirely fair, as the model's efficiency is not fully compared with other models under similar conditions.
- The paper does not adequately address the scalability of the model to larger video datasets or the impact of different frame sampling strategies on performance.
- The novelty of the approach is questioned as similar techniques have been previously used in other models like ViT-CLIP.
- The paper does not sufficiently address the limitations of the proposed model, such as its performance on different video resolutions and its applicability to other video action recognition tasks.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors clarify how the model's efficiency is compared with other models, particularly in terms of computational resources and parameter count?
- How does the model perform when applied to other video action recognition tasks, and what are the limitations of its applicability?
- Could the authors provide more details on the training process, such as the specifics of the adapter placement and the temporal visual prompt learning?
- How does the model handle different video resolutions, and what are the implications of using different frame sampling strategies?
- In the context of the motion loss, can the authors elaborate on how it ensures that the motion is captured effectively across all frames in the video?
- Why was the decision made to train the model on only 8 frames per video, and what are the implications of this choice on the model's performance and generalization capabilities?
