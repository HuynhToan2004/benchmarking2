 **Summary:**
The paper introduces Voila-A, a novel approach that aligns Vision-Language Models (VLMs) with human gaze attention using gaze information collected through AR or VR devices. This alignment aims to enhance the interpretability and effectiveness of VLMs in real-world applications by mimicking human gaze modalities using localized narratives. The authors propose an automatic data annotation pipeline using GPT-4 to generate the VOILA-COCO dataset and a new attention mechanism, the Voila Perceiver Resampler, to integrate gaze information into VLMs while preserving pretrained knowledge. The paper presents a comprehensive evaluation using a hold-out validation set and a newly collected VOILA-GAZE testset, demonstrating that Voila-A outperforms several baseline models.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel approach, Voila-A, which aligns Vision-Language Models (VLMs) with human gaze attention, enhancing interpretability and effectiveness in real-world applications.
- The methodology involves collecting hundreds of minutes of gaze data to mimic human gaze modalities using localized narratives, which is a significant advancement in the field.
- The paper is well-written, with clear explanations of the methodology and experimental results, and includes a comprehensive evaluation using a hold-out validation set and a newly collected VOILA-GAZE testset.
- The proposed method demonstrates significant outperformance compared to several baseline models, indicating its effectiveness.
- The integration of gaze information into VLMs while preserving pretrained knowledge is a valuable contribution to the field.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed discussion on the limitations and challenges of the proposed approach, which is crucial for understanding the practical applicability and potential drawbacks of the method.
- There is a lack of clarity on the novelty of the method, particularly in terms of how it differs from existing methods and what specific advantages it offers.
- The evaluation metrics used in the study, such as GPT-4 RANKING and REWARD SCORE, are not convincingly robust or generalizable to real-world scenarios, which could limit the reliability of the results.
- The paper does not adequately address how the model handles scenarios where the gaze data is not available or is noisy, which could significantly impact the performance and applicability of the model.
- The paper does not provide a detailed comparison with other state-of-the-art methods, which could help in understanding the relative advantages and disadvantages of the proposed method.
- The methodology section is not detailed enough, particularly in explaining the process of transforming trace data to make it more gaze-like and the details of the Voila Perceiver Resampler.
- The paper does not discuss the potential societal impacts of the proposed method, which is a significant omission in the ethical considerations of the work.

**Questions:**
- How does the proposed method handle scenarios where the gaze data is not available or is noisy?
- Could the authors provide a detailed comparison with other state-of-the-art methods to help in understanding the relative advantages and disadvantages of the proposed method?
- How does the model ensure that it is not overly reliant on gaze data, which could limit its ability to adapt to different users or scenarios?
- What are the potential societal impacts of the proposed method, particularly in terms of privacy and security concerns?
- Could the authors provide more details on the methodology, particularly in the process of transforming trace data to make it more gaze-like and the details of the Voila Perceiver Resampler?
- How does the model handle scenarios where the gaze data does not align with the user's actual attention or intent?
- Could the authors clarify the novelty of the method and what specific advantages it offers over existing methods?
