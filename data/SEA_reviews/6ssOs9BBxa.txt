 **Summary:**
The paper introduces AnonymizedAI, a deep reinforcement learning (DRL) agent that has won the IEEE microRTS competition, marking the first victory for a DRL agent in this domain. The agent employs a combination of transfer learning, behavior cloning, and fine-tuning to achieve its success. The paper provides a detailed analysis of the agent's architecture, training processes, and performance across various maps. It also explores the use of different reward functions and neural network architectures to optimize the agent's behavior. Despite its strengths in experimental validation and detailed methodological description, the paper suffers from issues in presentation clarity, lack of novelty, and potential over-reliance on existing techniques.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-organized, clear, and easy to follow, making it accessible to a broad audience.
- The experimental results are comprehensive and demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed methods, showing that AnonymizedAI can outperform existing agents in the IEEE microRTS competition.
- The paper provides a detailed analysis of the training process, including the use of transfer learning, behavior cloning, and fine-tuning, which are critical for the agent's success.
- The authors have made significant efforts to improve the agent's performance by fine-tuning on specific maps and using different reward functions, which are crucial for the agent's adaptability and effectiveness.
- The paper is a significant contribution to the field of RTS games, demonstrating the potential of deep reinforcement learning in complex game environments.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks novelty as it primarily builds upon existing methods such as transfer learning, behavior cloning, and fine-tuning, which have been previously used in similar contexts.
- The presentation of the paper could be improved to make it more accessible to a broader audience, especially those not familiar with the specifics of RTS games and deep reinforcement learning.
- There is a significant reliance on existing techniques without sufficient discussion or justification of their effectiveness or novelty.
- The paper could benefit from more detailed explanations of the training process, such as the specifics of the transfer learning and fine-tuning methods used.
- The paper does not sufficiently discuss the limitations of the proposed methods, which could help in understanding the scope and applicability of the findings.
- The paper could be improved by including more comparative analysis with other state-of-the-art methods to better establish the superiority of the proposed approach.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors provide more details on the specific transfer learning and fine-tuning methods used, including how they were implemented and their impact on the agent's performance?
- How does the agent perform on maps that were not used for training, and what are the implications of this for its generalizability?
- Could the authors discuss the limitations of their approach and how these might affect the practical application of the findings in real-world scenarios?
- How does the agent's performance compare to other state-of-the-art methods, and what are the key differences in terms of training time, computational resources, and performance metrics?
- Could the authors provide more detailed explanations of the reward functions used and their impact on the agent's behavior and performance?
- How does the agent's performance vary with different hyperparameters, and what are the implications of these variations for the generalizability and robustness of the findings?
