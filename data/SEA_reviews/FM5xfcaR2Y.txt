 **Summary:**
The paper explores the Bayes optimal classifier under group fairness constraints, specifically focusing on demographic parity (DP), equalized odds (EO), and equality of opportunity (EOp). It introduces a novel post-processing method that modifies the Bayes optimal classifier using a linear rule based on bias scores. These scores are derived from the estimated conditional probabilities of the sensitive attribute and the target variable, and they allow for flexible adjustment of the trade-off between accuracy and fairness without requiring access to sensitive attributes during inference. The method is validated through experiments on three datasets, showing competitive or better performance compared to in-processing and post-processing methods. However, the paper is criticized for its limited novelty and lack of comprehensive experimental evaluation, particularly concerning the scalability and computational efficiency of the proposed method.

**Strengths:**
- The paper provides a clear and concise summary of the proposed method, which is easy to follow and understand.
- The experiments are well-conducted, with results showing that the proposed method can achieve better trade-offs between accuracy and fairness compared to existing methods.
- The paper introduces a novel post-processing method that allows for the flexible adjustment of the trade-off between accuracy and fairness without requiring access to sensitive attributes during inference.
- The method's simplicity and the clarity of its presentation are commendable, making it accessible to a broad audience.
- The paper introduces a bias score that captures both predictive uncertainty and the inference of the sensitive group on the instance level, which is a unique approach to measuring fairness.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a comprehensive evaluation of the proposed method, particularly in terms of scalability and computational efficiency, especially for large datasets.
- The novelty of the method is questioned, as it seems to resemble existing methods like those by Menon & Williamson (2018), with the main difference being the use of bias scores.
- The paper does not adequately address the limitations of the proposed method, such as its applicability to other fairness definitions and its potential impact on performance when the estimated conditional probabilities are inaccurate.
- The paper's theoretical contributions are seen as limited, with the main theoretical result being a restatement of known results from Menon & Williamson (2018).
- The paper lacks a detailed discussion on the computational efficiency and scalability of the proposed method, which is crucial for its practical application.
- The paper does not include a discussion on the limitations of the proposed method, which could affect the reader's understanding of its applicability and effectiveness.
