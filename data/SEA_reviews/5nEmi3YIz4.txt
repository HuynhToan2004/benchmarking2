 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel method for converting a trained black-box image classifier into an interpretable model by leveraging non-negative matrix factorization (NMF) to extract prototypes from feature vectors. These prototypes are then used to reconstruct the classifier's output, providing a transparent reasoning process for image classification. The approach aims to maintain the performance of the original model while enhancing interpretability, which is typically a challenge in deep learning models. The methodology is tested on various datasets, including CUB and ImageNet, demonstrating competitive results with existing interpretable models. However, the paper lacks a comprehensive comparison with other interpretable models and does not convincingly demonstrate superior interpretability or performance over existing methods.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, with clear and concise sections, and the methodology is easy to follow.
- The idea of converting a trained black-box model into an interpretable model is innovative and addresses a significant challenge in deep learning.
- The use of non-negative matrix factorization (NMF) to extract prototypes from feature vectors is a novel approach, and the method is shown to be effective in maintaining model performance while enhancing interpretability.
- The paper includes a variety of experiments on different architectures, which demonstrate the versatility of the proposed method.
- The methodology is simple and effective, making it accessible and potentially useful for a wide range of applications.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a comprehensive comparison with other interpretable models, which limits the evaluation of its performance and interpretability against existing methods.
- The performance of the proposed method is not consistently superior to existing methods, particularly in terms of accuracy on the ImageNet dataset.
- The paper does not convincingly demonstrate that the extracted prototypes are meaningful or interpretable, as the visualizations and explanations provided are insufficient.
- The paper does not discuss the limitations of the proposed method, which could help in understanding its applicability and potential drawbacks.
- The method's reliance on NMF, which is known to have issues with local minima and scalability, is not adequately addressed, which could limit its practical applicability.
- The paper does not provide a detailed analysis of the computational overhead or the scalability of the method, which are crucial for understanding its practical deployment.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors provide a more detailed comparison with other interpretable models, including a discussion on how their method compares in terms of performance and interpretability?
- How does the proposed method compare to other post-hoc methods for interpretability, particularly in terms of computational overhead and scalability?
- Can the authors provide more detailed visualizations and explanations of the extracted prototypes to demonstrate their interpretability and relevance to the image data?
- What are the limitations of the proposed method, and under what conditions might it not be applicable or effective?
- How does the proposed method handle negative values in feature vectors, and what are the implications of this approach on the model's performance and interpretability?
- Could the authors discuss the potential for extending the method to other types of data or tasks, and what modifications might be necessary for such applications?
