 **Summary:**
The paper introduces "What's In My Big Data?" (WIMBD), a platform designed to analyze large text corpora used in training language models. WIMBD offers sixteen analyses to examine various aspects of these corpora, including document length distribution, data quality, and community-relevant measurements. The platform is tested on ten datasets, revealing insights such as the prevalence of duplicate content and the presence of toxic language. The authors plan to open-source the code, aiming to facilitate further research and transparency in dataset analysis. However, concerns were raised about the novelty of the analyses and the potential for overfitting due to the limited diversity of datasets used.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel platform, WIMBD, which offers a comprehensive set of sixteen analyses to investigate large text corpora, providing valuable insights into the data used to train large language models (LLMs).
- The authors have conducted extensive analyses on ten different English corpora, revealing surprising and previously undocumented findings, which are crucial for understanding the content and quality of these corpora.
- The paper is well-organized, clearly written, and easy to follow, with a focus on transparency and reproducibility, which is essential for advancing research in the field.
- The findings, such as the high prevalence of duplicate content and the presence of toxic language in certain datasets, are significant and have important implications for the development and deployment of LLMs.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a clear differentiation from existing works, particularly those that have already explored similar analyses in large text corpora. The novelty of the analyses and the insights provided is not convincingly demonstrated.
- The methodology used in the analyses, such as the use of Elasticsearch and map-reduce for data processing, is not sufficiently detailed or justified, which could lead to questions about the efficiency and effectiveness of the chosen methods.
- There is a potential for overfitting due to the limited diversity of datasets used in the analyses. The inclusion of a broader range of datasets could enhance the generalizability and applicability of the findings.
- The paper does not sufficiently address the limitations of its methodology or the potential biases that could be introduced by the use of specific tools or datasets.
- The paper could benefit from a more comprehensive discussion on the implications of the findings and the potential for future research directions.

**Questions:**
- Could you provide more details on the specific analyses conducted using WIMBD and how these analyses differ from those previously reported in the literature?
- How does WIMBD compare in terms of efficiency and effectiveness to other tools or methods for analyzing large text corpora?
- What are the potential biases introduced by the use of specific datasets or tools in the analysis, and how might these biases affect the findings?
- How do the findings of WIMBD contribute to a deeper understanding of the content and quality of large text corpora, and what are the implications for the development and deployment of LLMs?
- Could you elaborate on the potential limitations of the methodology used in WIMBD and how these limitations might impact the generalizability and applicability of the findings?
