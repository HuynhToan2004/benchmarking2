 **Summary:**
The paper introduces Mul2Vec, a novel framework designed to learn modality-free representations for various entity types, including text, images, and objects, under conditions of incomplete data. Mul2Vec utilizes a non-negative tensor factorization model to extract semantic representations from text and contextual representations from images, which are then aligned using dual-level matching loss. The framework is evaluated on datasets such as Flickr30k and MSCOCO, showing improvements in multi-modal search tasks. Despite its innovative approach, the paper suffers from unclear writing, insufficient experimental validation, and a lack of comprehensive comparison with existing methods.

**Strengths:**
- The paper addresses the significant problem of learning modality-free representations for various entity types under incomplete data, which is crucial for advancing research in multimodal AI.
- The proposed framework, Mul2Vec, is innovative and well-articulated, providing a novel approach to learning semantic representations that can be applied to various downstream tasks.
- The paper is well-written, making it accessible and easy to follow, which is a critical factor for academic publications.
- The experiments conducted on datasets like Flickr30k and MSCOCO demonstrate the effectiveness of Mul2Vec in enhancing multimodal search tasks.
- The methodology of Mul2Vec is straightforward and easy to implement, making it a practical solution for real-world applications.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a clear and concise description of the Mul2Vec model, particularly in terms of its architecture and training objectives. This makes it difficult for readers to fully understand the proposed method.
- The experimental validation is insufficient, with only one dataset used for evaluation, and the results are not convincingly superior to existing methods.
- There is a significant gap in the literature review, with many relevant studies missing, which limits the contextual understanding of the proposed method.
- The paper does not adequately address the limitations of the proposed method, which is essential for a comprehensive understanding of its applicability and potential drawbacks.
- The writing quality needs improvement, particularly in terms of clarity and coherence, as the paper suffers from unclear explanations and repetitive content.
- The paper lacks a detailed comparison with existing methods, which is crucial for establishing the novelty and effectiveness of Mul2Vec.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors clarify the architecture of Mul2Vec and the specifics of its training objectives?
- How does Mul2Vec handle different types of incomplete data, and what are the implications of this on the quality of the learned representations?
- Can the authors provide a more detailed comparison with existing methods, including a discussion on how Mul2Vec addresses the limitations of these methods?
- How does Mul2Vec ensure the quality of the learned representations, especially in cases where the data is noisy or incomplete?
- Can the authors elaborate on the rationale behind the choice of datasets and the specific experimental settings used in their study?
- How does Mul2Vec perform under different conditions of data incompleteness, and what are the implications of this for its practical application?
