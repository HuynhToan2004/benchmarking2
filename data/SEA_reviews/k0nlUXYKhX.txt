 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel two-dimensional optimization approach aimed at addressing the challenges of highly imbalanced data in the context of fault detection in the automotive industry. The approach employs a heuristic optimization algorithm, Genetic Algorithm (GA), to concurrently reduce both the number of data tuples and features. The methodology is evaluated using datasets from thousands of vehicles, demonstrating promising results. However, the paper suffers from significant issues in clarity, presentation, and methodological rigor. The lack of detailed algorithmic descriptions, insufficient experimental validation, and inadequate comparison with state-of-the-art methods undermine the paper's contribution.

**Strengths:**
- The paper addresses an important and timely issue in the field of fault detection in the automotive industry, focusing on the challenges posed by highly imbalanced data.
- The application of optimization algorithms such as Genetic Algorithm (GA), Particle Swarm Optimization (PSO), and Whale Optimization Algorithm (WOA) to select optimal tuples and features for predictive models is innovative and relevant.
- The experimental results, while not extensively validated, demonstrate the potential of the proposed approach in improving fault detection performance.
- The paper is well-structured, providing a clear description of the proposed method and its application in the context of the automotive industry.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks clarity and detail in describing the proposed method, particularly in the algorithmic descriptions and the formulation of the optimization problem.
- The experimental evaluation is insufficiently detailed, with a lack of standard deviation reporting and a lack of comparison with state-of-the-art methods.
- The paper suffers from poor presentation quality, with numerous grammatical errors and unclear figures and tables.
- The paper does not adequately address the issue of imbalanced data, which is critical for the application of the proposed method.
- The novelty of the proposed method is questionable, as it seems to resemble existing methods with minor modifications.
- The paper does not sufficiently discuss the limitations of the proposed method, which is crucial for understanding its applicability and effectiveness.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors provide a more detailed description of the proposed method, including a clear formulation of the optimization problem and a step-by-step algorithmic description?
- How does the proposed method address the issue of imbalanced data in a robust and effective manner?
- Could the authors provide a more detailed discussion on the limitations of the proposed method and its potential impacts on the application of the method in real-world scenarios?
- How do the authors justify the use of the GA algorithm as the primary optimization method, given the potential advantages of other methods like PSO and WOA?
- Could the authors provide a more detailed comparison with state-of-the-art methods to better establish the effectiveness and novelty of their approach?
- How do the authors address the potential issues of overfitting and underfitting in their method, particularly in the context of the imbalanced data used?
