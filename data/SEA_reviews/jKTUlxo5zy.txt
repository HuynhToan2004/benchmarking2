 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel approach to image attribution by framing it as a submodular subset selection problem. The method aims to identify and highlight informative image regions by optimizing a submodular function that evaluates the importance of different subsets of regions based on criteria such as confidence, effectiveness, consistency, and collaboration. The proposed method is evaluated across various datasets, showing improvements over existing methods in terms of faithfulness and interpretability. Despite its innovative approach, the paper is criticized for its limited evaluation scope, focusing only on a few datasets, and for not sufficiently distinguishing its contributions from existing methods.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel approach to image attribution by framing it as a submodular subset selection problem, which is both innovative and effective.
- The methodology is well-supported by theoretical analysis and extensive experimental results, demonstrating the practical applicability of the proposed method.
- The paper is well-written, making it accessible and easy to follow, which is crucial for a broad audience.
- The approach shows promise in improving the faithfulness of attribution methods, as evidenced by the experimental results.
- The paper's theoretical analysis provides a solid foundation for the proposed method, and the experimental results are convincing, showing improvements over existing methods.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper's evaluation is limited to a few datasets, which may not adequately demonstrate the generalizability of the proposed method across different types of images and datasets.
- The novelty of the approach is questionable as it appears to be a combination of existing methods without sufficient differentiation or innovation.
- The paper lacks a comprehensive discussion on the limitations of the proposed method, which is crucial for understanding its applicability and effectiveness in real-world scenarios.
- The computational cost of the proposed method is not discussed, which is important for practical implementation and comparison with other methods.
- The paper does not sufficiently distinguish its contributions from existing methods, which may lead to confusion about the unique advantages of the proposed approach.
- The paper could benefit from more detailed comparisons with existing methods, especially in terms of computational efficiency and practical applicability.
