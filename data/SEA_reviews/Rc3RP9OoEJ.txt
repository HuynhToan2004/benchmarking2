 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel test-time prompt tuning method, termed in-context prompt learning (InCP), which leverages a token network to translate text prompts into visual prompts, enhancing the CLIP model's adaptability to various downstream tasks. The approach optimizes these visual prompts using a semi-supervised loss, enabling the CLIP model to adapt to new tasks without fine-tuning its parameters. The methodology is supported by extensive experiments across multiple datasets, demonstrating the effectiveness of the proposed method. However, concerns were raised about the clarity of the methodology, particularly the role of different loss functions and the lack of comprehensive comparisons with existing methods.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel method of in-context prompt learning, which effectively adapts the CLIP model to new tasks without fine-tuning, enhancing its adaptability and zero-shot generalization capabilities.
- The use of a token network to translate text prompts into visual prompts is innovative and contributes significantly to the field of visual prompting.
- The paper is well-written, making it easy to follow and understand the proposed methodology.
- The experimental results are comprehensive and convincing, demonstrating the effectiveness of the proposed method across various datasets and tasks.
- The integration of in-context learning with visual prompting is a novel approach that bridges the gap between natural language processing and visual recognition, showing potential for broader applications in vision-language models.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks clarity in the description of the token network, particularly how it translates text prompts into visual prompts, which could benefit from additional illustrations or detailed explanations.
- There is a need for more comprehensive comparisons with existing methods, especially those that use similar prompting strategies, to establish the superiority of the proposed method.
- The paper could benefit from a more detailed discussion on the selection of in-context examples and their impact on the performance of the model.
- The role and impact of different loss functions, such as the semi-supervised loss and the supervised loss, are not clearly explained, which could confuse the reader about their contributions to the model's performance.
- The paper does not adequately address the potential limitations or societal implications of the proposed method, which could be a significant concern for practical applications.

**Questions:**
- Could you provide more details on how the token network translates text prompts into visual prompts, perhaps through additional illustrations or a step-by-step explanation?
- How does the proposed method compare to other in-context learning methods, particularly those that use similar prompting strategies?
- Could you clarify the role and impact of the different loss functions, specifically the semi-supervised loss and the supervised loss, on the model's performance?
- How are the in-context examples selected, and what criteria are used to ensure their effectiveness in enhancing the model's performance?
- Can you discuss the potential limitations or societal implications of the proposed method, and how these might be addressed in future research or practical applications?
