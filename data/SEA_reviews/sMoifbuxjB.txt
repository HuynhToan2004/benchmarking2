 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel methodology for structured pruning in neural networks, termed Intra-Fusion, which leverages optimal transport for model fusion to enhance pruning efficiency. This approach aims to reduce the computational burden and improve performance by fusing neurons rather than discarding them, thereby potentially reducing the training data needed for pruning. The methodology is evaluated on various datasets including CIFAR-10, CIFAR-100, and ImageNet, showing improvements in pruning efficiency and training speed. However, the paper is criticized for its lack of clarity in presentation, insufficient experimental validation, and unclear benefits compared to existing methods.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel approach to structured pruning by fusing neurons instead of discarding them, which is a significant departure from traditional methods.
- The use of optimal transport for model fusion is a novel application that adds value to the field of pruning.
- The paper provides a detailed algorithm for Intra-Fusion, which could potentially be applied to other areas beyond pruning, enhancing its versatility.
- The proposed methodology is evaluated on various datasets including CIFAR-10, CIFAR-100, and ImageNet, demonstrating its effectiveness across different architectures.
- The paper addresses the challenge of pruning neural networks without fine-tuning, which is a significant issue in the field.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks clarity in its presentation, making it difficult to follow and understand the methodology and results.
- The experimental validation is insufficient, with the paper not comparing its results against state-of-the-art methods, nor providing a comprehensive analysis of the results.
- The paper does not adequately address the computational complexity and time efficiency of the proposed method, which is crucial for practical implementation.
- The benefits of using Intra-Fusion are not clearly demonstrated, and the paper does not convincingly argue why its approach is superior to existing methods.
- The paper's writing style is verbose and lacks precision, which detracts from its readability and scientific rigor.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors clarify the main differences between their method and existing methods, particularly in terms of computational efficiency and effectiveness?
- How does the proposed method compare to other structured pruning methods, such as those based on sparse convolution?
- What are the specific benefits of using Intra-Fusion over other pruning methods, and how does it address the challenges of pruning without fine-tuning?
- Can the authors provide more detailed experimental results, including comparisons with state-of-the-art methods and a more comprehensive analysis of the results?
- How does the computational complexity and time efficiency of the proposed method compare to other pruning methods, and what are the practical implications of these factors?
