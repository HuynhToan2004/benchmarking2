 **Summary:**
The paper introduces TransductGAN, a novel generative adversarial network (GAN) designed for transductive positive-unlabeled (PU) learning, specifically for novelty detection in machine vision. This model utilizes a GAN to learn a latent space that separates novel and non-novel classes, leveraging a latent space where the distributions of the two classes do not overlap. The model is trained using a mixture of two Gaussian distributions and is evaluated on various datasets, demonstrating superior performance over existing methods. Despite its innovative approach and strong experimental results, the paper is criticized for its lack of clarity in presentation and the need for more comprehensive comparisons with state-of-the-art methods.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, clear, and easy to follow, making it accessible to a broad audience.
- The proposed method is novel, introducing a new GAN-based model for learning how to generate novelties in a transductive/PU learning setup, which is innovative in its application of a latent space for separating novel and non-novel classes.
- The experimental results are strong, showing superior performance over state-of-the-art unsupervised and semi-supervised models across multiple datasets.
- The paper is well-organized, with a clear structure and effective use of visual aids like Figure 1, which helps in understanding the proposed method.
- The use of a latent space to learn a mixture of two Gaussian distributions to represent positive and negative classes is a significant contribution to the field.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a thorough discussion on the limitations of the proposed method, which could be a significant drawback for potential users or researchers looking to build upon this work.
- The presentation of the paper could be improved, with some sections being difficult to understand due to complex mathematical notations and a lack of clarity in the presentation of the method.
- The paper does not adequately compare its method with the most relevant and recent state-of-the-art methods, which could weaken the argument for the superiority of the proposed approach.
- The experimental results are not convincingly superior to existing methods, as shown in Table 3, which could undermine the claimed effectiveness of the proposed method.
- The paper does not discuss the computational complexity or the scalability of the proposed method, which could limit its practical application in large-scale or real-world scenarios.
- The paper lacks a detailed discussion on the potential negative societal impacts of the proposed method, which is a critical consideration for ethical AI applications.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors provide more details on how the method performs when the contamination rate is not known?
- How does the proposed method compare with the most relevant and recent state-of-the-art methods in terms of performance and computational complexity?
- Could the authors elaborate on the potential negative societal impacts of the proposed method and how these might be mitigated or addressed?
- How does the proposed method handle the issue of mode collapse, which is a common problem in GANs?
- Could the authors discuss the potential of using the latent space learned by the proposed method for other tasks, such as anomaly detection or generation?
- What are the specific advantages of using a GAN-based model over other methods like autoencoders for learning the latent space?
