 **Summary:**
The paper addresses the challenges in the federated linear contextual bandits problem under differential privacy (DP) constraints, particularly focusing on the limitations of existing state-of-the-art algorithms. It identifies issues such as privacy leakage, incorrect regret bounds, and unrealistic communication costs and proposes a new algorithmic framework to address these issues. The authors introduce a generic federated linear contextual bandits algorithm and flexible privacy protocols, which are applied under two different privacy constraints: silo-level local DP and shuffle DP. The paper provides theoretical analysis, including privacy and regret guarantees, and supports these with numerical evaluations on synthetic and real-life data.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, clear, and easy to follow, with a clear motivation and good theoretical analysis.
- It identifies and addresses several limitations in the state-of-the-art algorithm, providing a detailed analysis of the issues and proposing a new algorithm to address these.
- The paper provides theoretical analysis for the proposed algorithm and supports the theoretical results with numerical evaluations.
- The authors have conducted a thorough literature review and have compared their results with prior works, highlighting the novelty and contributions of their research.
- The paper is structured well, with a clear introduction, detailed discussion of related work, and a comprehensive analysis of the limitations of existing methods.
- The proposed algorithm is general and applicable to different privacy notions, making it a valuable contribution to the field.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper does not provide a detailed comparison of the proposed algorithm with existing algorithms, which could help in understanding the improvements and advantages of the proposed method.
- The numerical evaluations are limited to small datasets, which might not fully demonstrate the scalability and performance of the proposed algorithm in larger, more complex scenarios.
- There are concerns about the practicality of the algorithm, particularly in terms of its applicability in real-world scenarios where privacy is a significant concern.
- The paper lacks a detailed discussion on the limitations of the proposed algorithm, which could help in understanding the scope and applicability of the research.
- There are minor issues with the presentation of the paper, such as typos and inconsistencies in notation, which could be confusing for readers.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors provide a detailed comparison of the proposed algorithm with existing algorithms, highlighting the improvements and advantages of their method?
- How does the proposed algorithm perform in larger, more complex scenarios, and what are the scalability limitations, if any?
- Could the authors discuss the limitations of the proposed algorithm and how these might affect its practical application in real-world scenarios?
- How does the proposed algorithm handle the issue of privacy leakage through the side channel of communication schedule, and what measures are in place to prevent this?
- In the context of the proposed algorithm, how does the fixed-batch schedule help avoid privacy leakage from the side channel, and what are the implications of this approach on the overall performance of the algorithm?
- Can the authors clarify the discrepancies in the regret bounds mentioned in the paper and provide a detailed explanation of how the proposed algorithm addresses these issues?
