 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel Bayesian bi-clustering method aimed at analyzing neural spiking activity across multiple brain regions and time periods. The method employs a mixture of finite mixtures model for subject clustering and a sticky hierarchical Dirichlet process hidden Markov model for state clustering, leveraging negative binomial distributions to model the neural spiking counts. This approach allows for the identification of spatio-temporal clusters in neural data, which is crucial for understanding complex neural dynamics. The paper validates the proposed method through simulations and real-world applications, demonstrating its ability to recover latent structures and provide insights into neural population dynamics. However, the method's assumptions, such as the linear dynamics and the use of negative binomial distributions, are critiqued for their realism and appropriateness in modeling neural data.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel bi-clustering method for analyzing neural spiking activity, which is both innovative and applicable to large-scale neural data.
- The methodology is well-explained, with clear descriptions of the Bayesian inference procedure and the use of MCMC algorithms.
- The paper is well-written, with clear and concise language, and includes a comprehensive literature review that effectively positions the research within the context of existing studies.
- The proposed method is supported by simulations and real-world applications, demonstrating its ability to recover latent structures and provide insights into neural population dynamics.
- The paper is significant in its potential to advance the understanding of neural dynamics by identifying spatio-temporal clusters in neural data, which could lead to new discoveries in neuroscience.

**Weaknesses:**
- The assumptions made in the model, such as the linear dynamics and the use of negative binomial distributions, are not convincingly justified for modeling neural data, which could limit the generalizability of the findings.
- The paper lacks a thorough discussion on the limitations of the proposed method, particularly in terms of its assumptions and the potential biases introduced by these assumptions.
- The application of the method to real-world datasets is limited, and the results are not sufficiently validated against ground truth data, which could undermine the credibility of the findings.
- The paper could benefit from a more rigorous comparison with existing methods, such as the mixPLDS and mixDPFA, to demonstrate the superiority or novelty of the proposed method.
- The presentation of the paper could be improved by including more detailed descriptions of the model components and the assumptions made, as well as by providing clearer visualizations and explanations of the results.
