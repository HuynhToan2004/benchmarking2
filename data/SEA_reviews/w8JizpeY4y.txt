 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel method for time series imputation and forecasting using a continuous-time-dependent model that leverages adaptations of conditional, implicit neural representations for sequential data. This method, termed TimeFlow, is designed to handle irregular and unaligned data, which is a significant challenge in real-world applications. The authors claim that TimeFlow outperforms existing models on various benchmarks, demonstrating its effectiveness in handling missing data and long-term predictions. However, the paper's novelty is questioned, as it appears to be a combination of existing techniques without significant innovation. Additionally, the clarity of the paper could be improved, particularly in the explanation of key components like the modulation mechanism and the training and inference processes.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, making it easy to follow, and the experimental results are impressive, demonstrating the effectiveness of the proposed method.
- The proposed method, TimeFlow, is innovative in its approach to handling irregular and unaligned data, which is a significant challenge in real-world applications.
- The paper includes a thorough comparison with state-of-the-art continuous and discrete models, demonstrating the superior performance of TimeFlow in handling missing data and long-term predictions.
- The methodology is clearly presented, and the paper includes comprehensive experiments and ablation studies, which support the claims made.
- The paper addresses a significant and relevant problem in the field of time series analysis, which is the handling of irregular and unaligned data.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed description of the modulation mechanism and the training and inference processes, which could confuse readers.
- The novelty of the proposed method is questionable, as it appears to be a combination of existing techniques without significant innovation.
- The paper does not adequately address the issue of overfitting, which is a critical concern in time series forecasting.
- The paper's claim of being the first to adapt INRs for both imputation and forecasting is not entirely accurate, as similar approaches have been explored in previous works.
- The clarity of the paper could be improved, particularly in the explanation of key components and the training and inference processes.
- The paper's evaluation could be enhanced by including more diverse datasets and scenarios to better demonstrate the generalizability of the proposed method.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors provide a more detailed description of the modulation mechanism and the training and inference processes to clarify how these components work?
- How does the proposed method handle overfitting, especially in scenarios where the training data is limited?
- What are the specific advantages of using TimeFlow over other existing methods, particularly in terms of handling irregular and unaligned data?
- Could the authors elaborate on the choice of datasets and the rationale behind the selection of benchmarks for evaluating the proposed method?
- How does the proposed method compare to other state-of-the-art methods in terms of computational efficiency and scalability, especially when dealing with large-scale datasets?
