 **Summary:**
The paper introduces ControlVideo, a training-free method for text-driven video synthesis that leverages a pre-trained text-to-image model (ControlNet) to generate videos from text prompts and motion sequences. ControlVideo employs a novel interleaved-frame smoother to ensure video continuity and a hierarchical sampler for efficient long-video generation. The methodology involves cross-frame interaction mechanisms to enhance frame consistency and a fully cross-frame interaction mechanism to improve video quality. Extensive experiments demonstrate the effectiveness of ControlVideo, showcasing its potential in generating high-quality videos with minimal computational resources. However, concerns about the novelty and the depth of experimental validation were raised, suggesting that the paper could benefit from more comprehensive comparisons and a deeper exploration of its technical contributions.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, making it easy to follow and understand.
- The proposed method, ControlVideo, is a training-free approach that can generate high-quality videos using only text prompts and motion sequences, demonstrating significant potential for practical applications.
- The paper provides extensive experimental results, including qualitative and quantitative comparisons, and ablation studies, which support the effectiveness of the proposed method.
- The methodology is straightforward and easy to implement, making it accessible to a broad audience.
- The paper addresses the significant issue of training costs associated with text-to-video synthesis, which is a major bottleneck in the field.
- The proposed interleaved-frame smoother is a novel technique that ensures video continuity and is a significant contribution to the field.

**Weaknesses:**
- The novelty of the proposed method is limited as it primarily adapts existing models like ControlNet for text-to-video synthesis and does not introduce significant new technical contributions.
- The paper lacks a detailed discussion on the limitations of the proposed method, which could help in understanding its scope and applicability.
- The experimental validation could be improved by including more comprehensive comparisons with existing methods and evaluating the method's performance on a wider range of datasets and conditions.
- The paper does not provide sufficient details on the computational resources required for generating long videos, which could limit its practical applicability.
- The paper does not sufficiently discuss the potential societal impacts of the proposed method, which is a critical aspect of any technological innovation.
- The paper could benefit from a more detailed discussion on the technical challenges faced during the development of the method and how these were addressed.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors clarify the specific technical challenges they faced during the development of ControlVideo and how these were addressed?
- How does the proposed method compare in terms of computational resources and efficiency with other training-free methods like Text2Video-Zero?
- Could the authors provide more details on the experimental setup, particularly the conditions under which the experiments were conducted and the specific datasets used?
- How does the method perform when generating videos with complex motion or long durations?
- Could the authors provide more quantitative results, such as the time taken to generate long videos and the computational resources required?
- How does the method handle different types of motion sequences, such as those with complex or repetitive patterns?
- Could the authors discuss the potential societal impacts of their method and how these were considered during its development?
