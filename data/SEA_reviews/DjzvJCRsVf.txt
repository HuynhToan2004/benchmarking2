 **Summary:**
The paper introduces CLIPSelf, a novel self-distillation approach that enhances the region-language alignment in CLIP models, aiming to improve open-vocabulary dense prediction tasks such as object detection and semantic segmentation. By aligning the dense features extracted from CLIP ViTs with image-level representations, CLIPSelf aims to bridge the gap between global and local representations, thereby enhancing the model's ability to perform dense prediction tasks. The methodology involves dividing images into patches, aligning dense features with image-level representations, and fine-tuning the model without the need for region-text pairs. The paper provides a comprehensive evaluation across various benchmarks, demonstrating significant improvements over existing methods. However, concerns about the novelty and the generalizability of the approach, especially in different model architectures, are highlighted.
