 **Summary:**
The paper introduces VideoClusterNet, a self-supervised and adaptive face clustering method for video face clustering. This method is designed to adapt a generic face ID model to new video face tracks, eliminating the need for manual ground truth labels. The proposed clustering algorithm is parameter-free and can adapt to the finetuned model's embedding space, making it applicable to any input video. The paper also introduces a new dataset, MovieFaceCluster, specifically curated for video face clustering, which is evaluated against traditional TV series datasets. Despite its novel approach and the creation of a new dataset, the paper is criticized for its lack of clarity in explaining the dataset and its contributions, and the absence of comparisons with state-of-the-art methods.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel self-supervised and adaptive face clustering method, VideoClusterNet, which adapts a generic face ID model to new video face tracks without requiring manual ground truth labels.
- The proposed clustering algorithm is parameter-free, which eliminates the need for manual hyperparameter tuning and makes it applicable to any input video.
- The creation of a new dataset, MovieFaceCluster, specifically curated for video face clustering, enhances the evaluation of the proposed method.
- The paper is well-written, clearly articulating the problem and its significance, and includes comprehensive evaluations and ablation studies that demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed method.
- The use of a self-supervised and adaptive face clustering method, along with a new dataset, contributes to the field by addressing the challenges of video face clustering.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks clarity in explaining the dataset, particularly in terms of its creation process and the criteria used for selecting the dataset.
- There is a significant gap in the literature review, with many relevant works not cited, which could have provided a more robust context for the research.
- The methodology of the proposed clustering algorithm, which uses a multivariate normal distribution, is not adequately justified or compared to other methods.
- The paper does not provide a clear definition of the "Unknown" cluster and does not compare the proposed method with state-of-the-art methods, which limits the evaluation of its effectiveness.
- The paper's presentation suffers from inconsistencies in referencing figures and tables, and some parts of the methodology are not well-explained, leading to potential confusion.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors clarify the process of creating the MovieFaceCluster dataset and the criteria used for its selection?
- How does the proposed clustering algorithm perform when compared to other state-of-the-art methods, particularly in terms of accuracy and efficiency?
- What is the specific role and definition of the "Unknown" cluster in the clustering process?
- How does the proposed method handle the potential bias from the generic face ID model, and what measures are in place to mitigate this?
- Could the authors provide more details on the dataset, including its size, diversity, and the criteria for selecting the movies and characters included?
- In the context of the clustering algorithm, could the authors provide a more detailed explanation of the multivariate normal distribution used and its impact on the clustering results?
