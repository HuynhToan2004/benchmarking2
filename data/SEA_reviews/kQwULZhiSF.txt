 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel method for unsupervised discovery of 3D object-centric scene representations from a single image, focusing on the disentanglement of object intrinsic and extrinsic properties. The approach involves a three-stage training pipeline on synthetic scenes to learn object priors, followed by training on real-world images to predict object positions and segmentation. The methodology includes the use of object-centric prior learning and object-centric sampling techniques to improve generalization and reconstruction quality. The paper presents new datasets and demonstrates the effectiveness of the proposed method through extensive experiments, showing superior performance over existing methods in tasks like scene segmentation, novel view synthesis, and scene manipulation.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel approach to unsupervised discovery of 3D object-centric scene representations from a single image, focusing on the disentanglement of object intrinsic and extrinsic properties.
- The proposed method achieves state-of-the-art results in scene segmentation, novel view synthesis, and scene manipulation, demonstrating its effectiveness.
- The paper is well-written, making it easy to follow, and the methodology is clearly explained with detailed technical descriptions.
- The introduction of new datasets and the use of a three-stage training pipeline on synthetic scenes to learn object priors are innovative approaches that contribute to the field.
- The paper is evaluated on multiple datasets, including real-world scenes, which enhances the robustness of the results.
- The inclusion of ablation studies and additional experiments in the appendix provides a deeper understanding of the method's components and their impact on performance.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a comprehensive discussion on the limitations of the proposed method, particularly in scenarios where the method might fail or underperform.
- The comparison with existing methods is not sufficiently robust, as it only includes a few recent works and does not include comparisons with more established methods like AutoRF.
- The generalization ability of the method to scenes with a large number of objects is not thoroughly evaluated, which is a significant limitation given the method's focus on object-centric modeling.
- The paper does not adequately address the scalability of the method to scenes with a large number of objects or complex backgrounds, which could limit its practical applicability.
- The method's dependence on the pre-trained DINO-ViT model and its performance on scenes with different object categories or complex backgrounds is not thoroughly explored.
- The paper could benefit from a more detailed discussion on the technical contributions and the rationale behind the design choices, such as the use of object-centric prior learning and object-centric sampling.
