 **Summary:**
The paper introduces MetaPhysiCa, a novel method for out-of-distribution (OOD) forecasting in physics-informed machine learning (PIML), utilizing a meta-learning approach for causal structure discovery. This method aims to enhance OOD robustness by learning from diverse training trajectories of the same dynamical system and leveraging a causal structure discovery approach with an invariant risk minimization penalty. The method is evaluated across three common OOD tasks, demonstrating significant improvements over existing PIML and deep learning methods. Despite its innovative approach, the paper has been critiqued for its lack of comparison with a broader range of baselines, including more recent and relevant works, and for not addressing the computational efficiency of its method.

**Strengths:**
- The paper addresses an important problem of out-of-distribution (OOD) forecasting in physics-informed machine learning (PIML), which is crucial for accurately modeling dynamical systems.
- The proposed method, MetaPhysiCa, is innovative and well-explained, with clear motivations and a logical flow that makes the content accessible to readers.
- The paper includes a thorough evaluation across three common OOD tasks, demonstrating significant improvements over existing PIML and deep learning methods.
- The use of causal structure discovery for physics-informed machine learning is a novel approach that enhances the understanding and modeling of dynamical systems.
- The paper is well-written, making it easy to follow and understand, with clear figures and explanations that aid in understanding the proposed methodologies.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a comprehensive comparison with a broader range of baselines, particularly recent and relevant works in the field of PIML and OOD forecasting.
- There is a need for more detailed explanations and examples of the causal structure discovery process and the choice of basis functions, which are crucial for the method's effectiveness.
- The paper could benefit from more detailed discussions on the limitations of the proposed method, such as its applicability to more complex dynamical systems and its computational efficiency.
- The experimental setup could be improved by including more realistic OOD scenarios and comparing the method's performance against a wider range of methods, including those that do not use causal structure discovery.
- There is a lack of clarity in some sections of the paper, particularly in the discussion of the causal structure discovery and the choice of basis functions, which could confuse readers.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors provide more details on the causal structure discovery process, including how the basis functions are chosen and the specifics of the discovery method used?
- How does the proposed method compare to other methods that do not use causal structure discovery, such as those that rely on physics-informed neural networks or other PIML methods?
- Can the authors clarify the computational efficiency of the method, particularly in terms of training and inference times, and how these compare to other methods in the field?
- How does the method perform in more complex dynamical systems, and what are the limitations in terms of model complexity and parameter estimation?
- Could the authors provide more examples or case studies to illustrate the practical applications and effectiveness of the method in real-world scenarios?
