 **Summary:**
The paper addresses the challenge of overfitting in vision-language models (VLMs) during fine-tuning, focusing on the out-of-distribution (OOD) generalization. It introduces a novel method called OGEN, which synthesizes OOD features using a class-conditional feature generator and employs an adaptive self-distillation mechanism to prevent overfitting. The approach is validated through experiments on various datasets, showing improvements in OOD generalization performance. The paper also discusses the limitations of existing VLM fine-tuning methods and proposes a new regularization strategy to address these issues.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel method, OGEN, for synthesizing OOD features using a class-conditional feature generator, which is a significant contribution to the field.
- The adaptive self-distillation mechanism is a novel approach that effectively prevents overfitting during joint optimization, enhancing the method's effectiveness.
- The paper is well-written, clearly explaining the proposed method and its implementation, making it accessible to readers.
- The experiments conducted are comprehensive, demonstrating the effectiveness of the proposed method across various datasets and baseline methods.
- The paper provides a thorough analysis of the limitations of existing VLM fine-tuning methods, highlighting the need for a new regularization strategy to address these issues.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper's novelty is questioned as it primarily focuses on synthesizing OOD features from a class-conditional feature generator, which is similar to previous methods.
- The comparison with other methods, such as VOS and non-parametric outlier synthesis, is lacking, which could provide a more robust evaluation of the proposed method.
- The paper does not adequately discuss the limitations of the proposed method, which is crucial for understanding its applicability and effectiveness in different scenarios.
- The paper's methodology and results are not sufficiently novel or surprising, as the use of CLIP models and the focus on out-of-distribution generalization are not new.
- The paper does not include a discussion on how the proposed method could be adapted or extended to other vision-language models, which could limit its applicability.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors clarify how the proposed method differs from previous methods, particularly in terms of the synthesis of OOD features?
- How does the proposed method handle the generation of features for classes that are not well-represented in the dataset, such as "unknown unknowns"?
- Could the authors provide a more detailed comparison with other methods, such as VOS and non-parametric outlier synthesis, to better understand the advantages and disadvantages of the proposed method?
- How does the proposed method perform in scenarios where the training dataset is limited, and the number of classes is large?
- Can the authors discuss the limitations of the proposed method and provide insights into how these could be addressed in future research?
