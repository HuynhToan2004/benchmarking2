 **Summary:**
The paper introduces AutoCast++, a zero-shot ranking-based context retrieval system designed for event forecasting, which enhances the performance of the AutoCast benchmark by incorporating a task-aligned retrieval module, an enhanced neural article reader, and a human-aligned loss function. This system leverages pre-trained language models (LLMs) like GPT-3 and GPT-4 to perform retrieval and summarization tasks, aiming to improve the accuracy of event forecasting. The methodology involves a novel ranking-based context retrieval approach that utilizes LLMs for relevance assessment and summarization, which are then integrated into the forecasting model to generate predictions. The paper presents extensive experiments demonstrating the effectiveness of these components, although it has been critiqued for its lack of clarity in some sections and the potential for over-reliance on LLMs, which could limit the generalizability of the findings.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, easy to follow, and presents a novel ranking-based context retrieval approach for event forecasting, which is a significant advancement in the field.
- The introduction of the AutoCast++ system, which integrates task-aligned retrieval, an enhanced neural article reader, and a human-aligned loss function, is a substantial contribution to the field.
- The paper demonstrates the effectiveness of the proposed methods through extensive experiments, showing improvements in event forecasting accuracy.
- The use of pre-trained language models (LLMs) like GPT-3 and GPT-4 for retrieval and summarization tasks is a novel application that enhances the system's performance.
- The paper is well-structured, with clear explanations of the methodology and results, which are supported by ablation studies that help in understanding the contributions of different components.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper could benefit from more detailed explanations and clarifications in certain sections, particularly around the use of GPT-3 and GPT-4, and the specifics of the LLM-based relevance assessment and summarization processes.
- The novelty of the approach is somewhat limited as it primarily builds upon existing methods like the FiD Static model, with minor modifications.
- The reliance on LLMs for retrieval and summarization might limit the generalizability of the findings, as the performance of the system could vary significantly depending on the quality and coverage of the LLM's knowledge.
- There are concerns about the fairness of the comparisons made in the paper, particularly regarding the use of different model sizes and the potential for over-optimization of the FiD Static baseline.
- The paper could benefit from more diverse datasets and comparisons with different models to better establish the generalizability and effectiveness of the proposed methods.

**Questions:**
- Can you provide more details on the specific LLM-based relevance assessment and summarization processes used in the AutoCast++ system?
- How does the system perform when using different LLMs, and what are the implications of using different model sizes?
- Could you clarify the rationale behind the choice of using GPT-3 and GPT-4 for retrieval and summarization, and how these choices affect the system's performance?
- How does the AutoCast++ system handle cases where the retrieved articles are not relevant or where the summarization process fails to capture the essential information?
- Could you provide more detailed results on the performance of the system when using different LLMs and when compared to other state-of-the-art models in the field?
- How does the AutoCast++ system handle the potential for over-optimization of the FiD Static baseline, and what steps have been taken to ensure a fair comparison with other models?
