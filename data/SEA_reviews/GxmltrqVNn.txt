 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel approach to monocular depth estimation by converting the problem from a regression task to a classification problem using a Gated Attention Bin (GABIN) module. This module divides the depth range into adaptive bins based on the image's features, which are then used to estimate depth values. The approach employs a single-head gated attention mechanism and a Laplace normalization function to enhance model training stability. The method is tested on popular datasets like NYUv2 and KITTI, showing improved performance over some baselines. However, the paper lacks in-depth analysis and fails to provide significant improvements over state-of-the-art methods. The methodology is criticized for being overly simplistic and not sufficiently novel, with the paper being described as difficult to follow and lacking in clarity.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-organized and easy to follow, making it accessible to readers.
- The proposed method of converting a regression problem to a classification problem is novel and effective, as demonstrated by the use of the GABIN module.
- The paper includes a thorough comparison with state-of-the-art methods, showing the effectiveness of the proposed method in terms of both quantitative and qualitative results.
- The method's simplicity and efficiency are highlighted, particularly in the use of a single-head gated attention mechanism and the Laplace normalization function for model training stability.
- The paper demonstrates the method's ability to generalize well to unseen datasets, indicating its potential applicability in various scenarios.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed analysis of the proposed method, particularly in terms of how the GABIN module works and how it compares to other depth discretization methods.
- The paper's novelty is questioned as the use of gated attention and depth discretization are not entirely new concepts.
- The writing and presentation of the paper are criticized for being unclear and difficult to follow, particularly in the explanation of the methodology and the experimental results.
- The paper's performance improvements over state-of-the-art methods are minimal, and in some cases, the proposed method performs worse than existing methods.
- The paper does not sufficiently address the limitations of the proposed method, which could be crucial for understanding its applicability and effectiveness in different scenarios.
- The paper's contribution to the field is seen as limited due to the incremental nature of the proposed method and the lack of significant performance improvements.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors provide a more detailed explanation of how the GABIN module operates and how it contributes to the overall performance of the method?
- How does the proposed method compare to other depth discretization methods, particularly in terms of accuracy and efficiency?
- Could the authors clarify the experimental setup and results, particularly in the comparison with state-of-the-art methods and the generalization to unseen datasets?
- How does the method handle the discretization of depth values, and what are the implications of this discretization on the overall performance and accuracy of the depth estimation?
- Could the authors elaborate on the method's limitations and potential areas for future research and improvement?
