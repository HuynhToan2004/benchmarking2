 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel method called the Stable Prefix Policy (SPP) aimed at enhancing exploration in multi-agent reinforcement learning (MARL) algorithms. SPP employs a Monte-Carlo Trajectory Tree (MCT2) to maintain a stable trajectory for agents, balancing exploration and exploitation effectively. This method is tested in the SMAC environment, showing improvements over existing algorithms like QMIX and MACPF. However, concerns were raised about the clarity of the paper, particularly in the explanation of key concepts like the Monte-Carlo Trajectory Tree and the Stable Prefix Policy, and the novelty of the proposed method.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, making it easy to follow, and the methodology is clearly presented.
- The proposed method, SPP, is novel and addresses an important issue in MARL, namely the balance between exploration and exploitation.
- The paper includes a comprehensive set of experiments and ablation studies, which demonstrate the effectiveness of SPP.
- The theoretical analysis provided supports the proposed method, enhancing the paper's credibility.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks clarity in explaining key concepts such as the Monte-Carlo Trajectory Tree and the Stable Prefix Policy, making it difficult for readers to fully understand the proposed method.
- The paper does not adequately differentiate its contributions from existing works, particularly in the area of exploration in MARL.
- The paper suffers from several grammatical errors and unclear figures, which detract from its professional presentation.
- The paper's experimental setup and results are not convincingly superior to existing methods, and the improvements are marginal in some cases.
- The paper's assumptions and theoretical underpinnings, such as the assumption of a perfect clustering mapping and the lack of a rigorous exploration strategy, are not well-explained or justified.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors clarify the term "trembling hands" and its relevance to the paper's content?
- How does the proposed method compare to existing works, particularly in terms of novelty and effectiveness?
- Can the authors provide a clearer explanation of the Monte-Carlo Trajectory Tree and the Stable Prefix Policy, possibly with additional figures or examples?
- How does the paper's method ensure that the clustering mapping is perfect, and what are the implications of this assumption on the practical application of the method?
- Could the authors elaborate on the method's theoretical underpinnings, particularly in relation to the assumptions made and their implications on the method's applicability?
- How does the proposed method handle scenarios where the agent's exploration is insufficient, and what are the implications of this on the overall training process?
