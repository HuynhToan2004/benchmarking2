 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel adversarial training method that involves a specially designed input layer to enhance the robustness of neural networks against adversarial attacks. This layer includes a 3x3 convolutional layer followed by a ReLU activation, a sigmoid function, and two additional random perturbations. The authors claim that this method, which can be applied during standard training without additional computational overhead, significantly improves the robustness of the model against various attacks. However, the paper has been criticized for its lack of clarity, insufficient experimental validation, and failure to convincingly demonstrate the method's effectiveness against a broader range of attacks.

**Strengths:**
- The proposed method is simple and effective, with the ability to enhance the robustness of neural networks without requiring additional computational resources or adversarial training.
- The paper is well-structured and easy to follow, with clear explanations of the method and its implementation.
- The method's simplicity and efficiency make it a valuable contribution to the field, particularly in its ability to enhance robustness without increasing training costs.
- The paper addresses an important and relevant problem in the field of adversarial robustness, which is crucial for improving the security and reliability of machine learning models.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks clarity in several sections, including the description of the input layer and the methodology used in the experiments.
- The experimental results are not convincing, with some results showing marginal improvements over existing methods, and others being inconsistent or not well-explained.
- The paper does not provide a comprehensive theoretical analysis of the method's effectiveness or robustness, which is crucial for understanding its limitations and potential applications.
- There is a lack of discussion on the limitations of the proposed method, which could include scenarios where the method may not be effective or where it may introduce new vulnerabilities.
- The paper does not include a comparison with a broader range of attacks, which limits the understanding of the method's effectiveness against different types of adversarial threats.
- The method's performance against stronger adversarial attacks, such as those that employ gradient obfuscation or adaptive defenses, is not adequately addressed.

**Questions:**
- Could you clarify the description of the input layer and the methodology used in the experiments, particularly in Section 4.1 and 4.2?
- How does the proposed method compare to other existing methods in terms of computational efficiency and effectiveness against different types of adversarial attacks?
- Can you provide a more detailed analysis of the experimental results, particularly in Table 1 where the proposed method shows lower robustness compared to existing methods?
- How does the method perform against stronger adversarial attacks, such as those that employ gradient obfuscation or adaptive defenses?
- Could you include a comparison with a broader range of attacks, including both white-box and black-box attacks, to better understand the method's effectiveness against different types of adversarial threats?
- How does the method's performance vary with different hyperparameters, such as the threshold beta and the weight alpha, and how sensitive is the method to these parameters?
