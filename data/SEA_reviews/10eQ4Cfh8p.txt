 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel reinforcement learning (RL) framework aimed at solving the Flexible Job Shop Problem (FJSP) by integrating both generative and improvement models. The generative model generates initial solutions, while the improvement model refines these solutions to optimize makespan. The approach leverages graph neural networks (GNN) to extract node features and uses a GAT module for feature aggregation. The proposed method is tested against various baselines, including heuristic rules and other RL-based methods, showing promising results. However, the paper's experimental evaluation is limited to small-scale problems, and it lacks comparisons with state-of-the-art methods, raising concerns about the generalizability and scalability of the proposed solution.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel approach using reinforcement learning (RL) for the Flexible Job Shop Problem (FJSP), which is a significant advancement in the field.
- The methodology is well-structured and clearly explained, with a focus on using graph neural networks (GNN) and deep reinforcement learning (DRL) for solution generation and improvement.
- The experimental results show that the proposed method outperforms traditional methods and other RL-based methods, indicating the effectiveness of the approach.
- The paper is well-written, making it accessible and easy to follow, which is crucial for a technical paper.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a comprehensive review of existing literature, particularly in the area of RL-based methods for FJSP, which could provide a better context for the proposed work.
- The experimental evaluation is limited to small-scale problems, which may not adequately demonstrate the method's performance on larger, more complex instances.
- There is a lack of comparisons with state-of-the-art methods, which is crucial for establishing the method's competitiveness and effectiveness.
- The paper does not provide sufficient details on the computational resources used, such as the GPU used for training, which could affect the reproducibility of the results.
- The paper does not address the scalability of the method to larger instances, which is a significant limitation given the method's potential applicability in larger industrial settings.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors clarify how the proposed method can be adapted to solve larger, more complex instances of FJSP?
- How does the proposed method compare to state-of-the-art methods in terms of performance, particularly in terms of solution quality and computational efficiency?
- Can the authors provide more details on the computational resources used for training and testing the model, including the specifications of the GPU used?
- How does the proposed method handle different types of constraints that may be present in real-world FJSP instances?
- Can the authors elaborate on the novelty of their approach compared to existing methods, particularly in terms of the use of graph neural networks and reinforcement learning?
