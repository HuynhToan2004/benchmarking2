 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel approach to the simplex method in linear programming by integrating reinforcement learning (RL) to enhance pivot selection. This method, named RL Simplex, utilizes a UCB-based exploration strategy to balance exploration and exploitation, which is particularly effective in the context of the Euclidean Traveling Salesman Problem (TSP). The authors demonstrate that RL Simplex can reduce the number of iterations required compared to traditional methods, although its scalability and applicability to larger, more complex problems remain limited. The approach is tested against various baselines, including commercial solvers and other RL-based methods, showing some improvements but not consistently outperforming state-of-the-art methods.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, making it easy to follow and understand the proposed method.
- The integration of reinforcement learning (RL) with the simplex method is a novel and interesting approach, particularly in the context of the TSP problem.
- The experimental results demonstrate that the proposed RL Simplex method can reduce the number of iterations required compared to traditional methods, which is a significant improvement.
- The paper provides a clear and concise explanation of the methodology, including the use of the UCB algorithm for exploration and exploitation in RL.
- The results are promising, showing that the proposed method can outperform some baselines, although it falls short of state-of-the-art methods.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a clear motivation for using RL to improve the simplex method, especially in terms of how it addresses specific challenges or limitations of the simplex method.
- The scalability of the RL Simplex method is limited, particularly in larger, more complex problems such as those with 50 cities or more.
- The paper does not adequately compare the proposed method with other state-of-the-art methods, particularly those that use different pivot selection rules or other optimization techniques.
- The experimental design and results are not convincing, with some results showing that the proposed method is not significantly better than existing methods, and others showing that it is not scalable to larger problems.
- The paper does not sufficiently discuss the limitations of the proposed method, particularly in terms of its applicability to more complex or real-world problems.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors clarify the motivation behind using RL to improve the simplex method, especially in terms of how it addresses specific challenges or limitations of the simplex method?
- How does the proposed RL Simplex method compare to other state-of-the-art methods, particularly those that use different pivot selection rules or other optimization techniques?
- Why is the RL Simplex method not scalable to larger problems, and what are the specific challenges or limitations that prevent it from being applied to more complex problems?
- How does the RL Simplex method handle the exploration-exploitation tradeoff in the context of the TSP problem, and what are the implications of this tradeoff on the performance of the method?
- Can the authors provide more details on the experimental setup, particularly how the RL Simplex method was trained and tested, and what the specific conditions were for the experiments?
