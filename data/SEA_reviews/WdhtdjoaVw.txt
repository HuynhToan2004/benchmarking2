 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel functional variational inference method named Functional Wasserstein Bridge Inference (FWBI) to address the challenges of defining meaningful priors and avoiding pathological behaviors in Bayesian deep learning (BDL). FWBI employs a Wasserstein distance-based bridge to replace the KL divergence, providing a lower bound of the model evidence. This method is evaluated on various tasks, demonstrating its effectiveness in predictive performance and uncertainty quantification compared to parameter-space and function-space variational methods. The paper is well-written, clearly presenting the methodology and experimental results, although it lacks a detailed discussion on computational complexity and the scalability of the method to high-dimensional datasets.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel method, Functional Wasserstein Bridge Inference (FWBI), which addresses the challenges of defining meaningful priors and avoiding pathological behaviors in Bayesian deep learning (BDL).
- FWBI offers a lower bound of the model evidence, enhancing its theoretical foundation and practical applicability.
- The method is evaluated on various tasks, demonstrating its effectiveness in predictive performance and uncertainty quantification compared to parameter-space and function-space variational methods.
- The paper is well-written, clearly presenting the methodology and experimental results, making it accessible and understandable.
- The proposed method is the first to use the Wasserstein distance as a distance measure in function-space variational inference, which is a significant contribution to the field.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed discussion on the computational complexity of the proposed method, which is crucial for understanding its scalability and applicability in real-world scenarios.
- The method's performance on high-dimensional datasets is not thoroughly evaluated, which is a limitation given the increasing interest in handling high-dimensional data in machine learning.
- The paper does not provide a detailed comparison with other state-of-the-art methods, which could help in understanding the relative performance of FWBI more clearly.
- There are some typographical and formatting errors in the paper, which could detract from its professional presentation.
- The paper does not adequately address the issue of pathologies from over-parametrization, which is a significant challenge in BDL.
- The method's reliance on Wasserstein distances may not be as straightforward or effective as using KL divergence in certain scenarios, which could limit its applicability.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors provide more details on the computational complexity of the proposed method and discuss its scalability to high-dimensional datasets?
- How does the proposed method compare with other state-of-the-art methods in terms of performance and efficiency?
- Can the authors elaborate on the choice of using Wasserstein distances over KL divergence and discuss the potential advantages or disadvantages of this choice?
- How does the proposed method handle the issue of pathologies from over-parametrization in BDL?
- Could the authors provide a more detailed comparison of the predictive performance and uncertainty quantification of FWBI with other methods, particularly in high-dimensional datasets?
- Is there a possibility to extend the method to handle more complex tasks beyond regression, such as classification or generation?
