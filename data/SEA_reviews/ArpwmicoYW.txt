 **Summary:**
The paper introduces FairTune, a framework that optimizes parameter-efficient fine-tuning (PEFT) strategies for fairness in medical image analysis. FairTune employs bi-level optimization to search for the optimal PEFT mask that maximizes fairness on validation sets, aiming to minimize bias during training-test generalization. The framework is tested on several medical imaging datasets, demonstrating its effectiveness in improving fairness and performance. Despite its innovative approach, the paper has been criticized for its lack of novelty, limited scope, and insufficient comparative analysis with other fairness interventions. Additionally, concerns were raised about the scalability and generalizability of the proposed method, especially in broader, more diverse datasets.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-organized and clearly written, making it easy to follow.
- The authors have conducted extensive experiments on multiple datasets, demonstrating the effectiveness of their proposed method.
- The paper addresses the important and timely issue of fairness in medical image analysis, which is crucial for ensuring equitable outcomes in healthcare.
- The methodology is sound, and the paper provides a comprehensive review of related work, which helps in understanding the context and contributions of the study.
- The experimental results are robust, showing that the proposed method can lead to improved fairness on a range of medical imaging datasets.

**Weaknesses:**
- The novelty of the paper is limited, as it primarily combines existing techniques such as PEFT and bi-level optimization without significant innovation.
- The paper lacks a clear explanation of how the proposed method differs from existing approaches, such as those by Zong et al. (2023).
- The scope of the study is limited to medical imaging datasets, which may not generalize well to other domains or types of datasets.
- The paper does not provide a detailed comparison with other fairness interventions, which is crucial for understanding the relative effectiveness of the proposed method.
- The computational cost of the proposed method is high, which may limit its practical applicability in real-world scenarios.
- The paper does not discuss the limitations of the proposed method, such as scalability and generalizability, which are critical for understanding the practical implications of the findings.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors clarify how their method differs from those by Zong et al. (2023) and provide a more detailed explanation of the novel contributions of their work?
- How does the proposed method perform when applied to datasets with overlapping sensitive attributes?
- Can the authors provide more detailed comparisons with other fairness interventions to better understand the relative effectiveness of their method?
- How does the computational cost of the proposed method compare to other fairness interventions, and what are the practical implications of these costs in real-world scenarios?
- Can the authors discuss the scalability and generalizability of their method in more detail, and how these limitations might affect the practical applicability of the findings?
