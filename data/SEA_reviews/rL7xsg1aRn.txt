 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel method for progressively expanding the size of Transformer models during pre-training, focusing on achieving strict function preservation and efficient growth schedules. This method, named Masked Structural Growth (MSG), employs a masking strategy to ensure that the output of the model remains the same as the smaller version, even when new parameters are initialized arbitrarily. The MSG method is compared against existing methods like Net2Net and other baselines, demonstrating significant improvements in training speed and performance across various tasks such as BERT and GPT-2. The paper also discusses the impact of different growth schedules and initialization strategies on the training dynamics and the overall performance of the models.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, easy to follow, and includes clear explanations of the proposed methods and their implications.
- The authors provide a comprehensive overview of existing methods and clearly distinguish their approach from previous works.
- The proposed method achieves state-of-the-art speed-up while maintaining comparable or better downstream performance, demonstrating its effectiveness.
- The paper includes extensive experiments, showing that the proposed method achieves state-of-the-art speed-up while maintaining comparable or better downstream performance.
- The method is novel in its approach to function preservation, which is crucial for maintaining model performance during growth.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed comparison with other recent works, such as LiGO, which could have provided a more robust evaluation of the proposed method.
- The paper does not provide sufficient evidence to support the claim that the MSG method is strictly function-preserving in arbitrary expansion, especially concerning the impact of layer normalization.
- The method's effectiveness is limited to multi-stage growth, and it does not compare with one-stage growth methods like LiGO, which could be crucial for understanding the trade-offs between different growth strategies.
- The paper does not sufficiently address the scalability of the proposed method to larger models, which is crucial for practical applications.
- The paper lacks a detailed discussion on the generalizability of the proposed method to other model architectures and the impact of different initialization strategies on the training dynamics.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors provide a more detailed comparison with LiGO, including the performance on different tasks and the impact of the growth schedule on the performance?
- How does the proposed method perform when compared to one-stage growth methods, and what are the trade-offs between these different growth strategies?
- Can the authors provide more evidence to support the claim that the MSG method is strictly function-preserving in arbitrary expansion, especially concerning the impact of layer normalization?
- How does the proposed method scale to larger models, and what are the potential challenges or limitations in applying this method to such models?
- Could the authors discuss the impact of different initialization strategies on the training dynamics and the overall performance of the models, and provide suggestions for practitioners on how to optimize these strategies?
