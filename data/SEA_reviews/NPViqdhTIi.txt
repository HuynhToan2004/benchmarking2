 **Summary:**
The paper introduces MolZip and MolZip-Vec, two methods that leverage Gzip compression to represent molecules for various tasks such as classification, regression, and binding affinity prediction. These methods aim to simplify molecular representation by compressing SMILES strings and other numerical descriptors into a single string, which is then used for classification and regression tasks. The authors claim that these methods can perform comparably or even better than other models like ChemBERTa and GROVER, although they acknowledge that the performance is not always superior. The paper also discusses the computational efficiency and the potential of these methods in chemical information retrieval. However, concerns are raised about the novelty of the approach, the lack of detailed experimental setups, and the potential limitations of the method in terms of scalability and interpretability.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel method using Gzip compression to represent molecules, which is a novel approach in the field of molecular representation learning.
- The method shows comparable or better performance than some baseline models, indicating its potential effectiveness.
- The paper is well-written, clear, and easy to follow, making it accessible to a broad audience.
- The proposed method is simple and efficient, which could be a significant advantage in practical applications.

**Weaknesses:**
