 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel method for Bayesian phylogenetic inference called VBPI-Mixtures, which integrates mixture learning with the Variational Bayesian Phylogenetic Inference (VBPI) framework. This approach aims to address the multimodality issues in tree-topology posteriors, which are typically challenging for sampling-based learning techniques. The method leverages mixtures of subsplit Bayesian networks to capture complex distributions over tree-topologies that VBPI fails to model. The paper presents experimental results demonstrating the effectiveness of VBPI-Mixtures across various real phylogenetic datasets, showing improvements in accuracy and efficiency compared to existing methods.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, clear, and easy to follow, with a good balance of background information, motivation, and methodological details.
- The proposed method is simple and effective, with a clear and intuitive explanation of the rationale behind using a mixture of subsplit Bayesian networks (SBNs) to address the multimodality issue in tree-topology posteriors.
- The experimental results are convincing, showing significant improvements in marginal log-likelihood estimates and the accuracy of tree-topology posterior approximations compared to existing methods.
- The paper includes a thorough literature review, which is beneficial for readers unfamiliar with the field.
- The authors provide code, which enhances reproducibility and allows for further validation and exploration of the proposed method.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper could benefit from more detailed explanations and examples of the tree-topology posterior and the multimodality issues, particularly in the context of real phylogenetic datasets.
- The experiments are limited to a small number of datasets, which might not fully demonstrate the generalizability of the proposed method.
- The paper could include more comparisons with other methods, such as VBPI-GNN and VBPI-NF, to provide a more comprehensive evaluation of the proposed method.
- The paper lacks a detailed discussion on the computational complexity and scalability of the proposed method, which is crucial for understanding its practical applicability in larger and more complex phylogenetic problems.
- The paper could benefit from a more comprehensive evaluation of the method's performance on larger and more complex datasets to demonstrate its scalability and effectiveness in more challenging scenarios.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors provide more detailed examples or visualizations of the tree-topology posterior and the multimodality issues in real phylogenetic datasets?
- How does the proposed method perform on larger and more complex datasets, and what are the computational requirements and limitations?
- Could the authors elaborate on the specific advantages of using a mixture of SBNs over other methods, such as VBPI-GNN and VBPI-NF?
- How does the method handle the selection of the number of mixture components, and what are the implications of choosing different numbers of components on the performance and interpretability of the results?
- The paper mentions the use of a mixture of SBNs to capture distributions over tree-topologies that VBPI fails to model. Can the authors provide examples or theoretical justifications for these cases?
- Can the authors discuss the potential societal impact of their work, particularly in the context of phylogenetic inference and its applications in fields like evolutionary biology and public health?
