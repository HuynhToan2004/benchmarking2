 **Summary:**
The paper introduces two novel methods, VCC (Variational Confidence Calibration) and INFUSE (INfluence-Function-based Unlabeled Sample Elimination), aimed at enhancing the performance of semi-supervised learning (SSL) algorithms. VCC addresses the issue of pseudo-labeling by calibrating confidence scores, which helps in selecting more accurate pseudo-labels. INFUSE, on the other hand, dynamically prunes unimportant unlabeled examples, reducing the computational cost and improving training efficiency. These methods are validated through extensive experiments on various datasets, demonstrating their effectiveness in improving SSL performance.

**Strengths:**
- The paper addresses the significant problem of leveraging unlabeled data effectively in semi-supervised learning, which is crucial for improving the accuracy of models with limited labeled data.
- The proposed methods, VCC and INFUSE, are well-motivated and technically sound, offering innovative approaches to calibrate confidence scores and prune unlabeled data, respectively.
- The paper is well-written, clearly presenting the problem, motivation, and methodology, making it accessible to readers and enhancing its readability.
- The experiments conducted are extensive and convincing, demonstrating the effectiveness of the proposed methods across various datasets and settings.
- The paper provides a clear and detailed explanation of the methods, including their theoretical underpinnings and practical applications, which aids in understanding and replicating the results.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a comprehensive literature review, particularly in the area of confidence calibration, which is crucial for positioning the work within the existing body of knowledge.
- The explanation of some experimental results and methodological choices, such as the choice of the number of views in the VCC method and the results in certain tables, is not clear or convincing.
- There are inconsistencies and potential errors in the experimental setup and results reporting, which need clarification to ensure the reliability and reproducibility of the findings.
- The paper does not adequately address the computational complexity and memory requirements of the proposed methods, which could be a significant limitation for their practical implementation.
- The paper does not sufficiently discuss the limitations of the proposed methods, which is essential for understanding their scope and applicability.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors clarify the choice of the number of views in the VCC method and its impact on the results?
- What is the rationale behind the results shown in Table 1, particularly the performance of VCC-SimMatch compared to SimMatch?
- How does the VCC method handle the issue of pseudo-labeling, especially in scenarios where the pseudo-labels are incorrect?
- Can the authors provide more details on the computational complexity and memory requirements of the VCC and INFUSE methods?
- How does the proposed VCC method compare to other confidence calibration methods, especially in the context of semi-supervised learning?
- Could the authors elaborate on the experimental setup and results, particularly in the class imbalance SSL experiments and the results in Table 6?
