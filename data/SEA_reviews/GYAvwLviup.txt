 **Summary:**
The paper explores a method for aligning brain responses across subjects to enhance video decoding from functional Magnetic Resonance Imaging (fMRI) signals. The authors propose using a combination of anatomical and functional alignment methods to align brain responses, demonstrating that this approach can improve decoding performance in out-of-subject scenarios. The study utilizes a dataset from Wen et al. (2017) and employs a novel approach that leverages pre-trained models to align brain responses, which is shown to be effective in scenarios where data from a reference subject is available. The method's effectiveness is evaluated using a variety of metrics, including median rank and accuracy, and is compared against a baseline model that does not use alignment. The paper also discusses the potential for this approach to generalize to other subjects and improve decoding performance with limited data.

**Strengths:**
- The paper presents a novel approach to aligning brain responses across subjects, which is a significant advancement in the field of brain decoding from fMRI.
- The methodology is well-described, making it clear and easy to follow, with a clear presentation of the experimental setup and results.
- The paper demonstrates that the proposed method can improve out-of-subject decoding performance and generalize to novel subjects, even with limited data.
- The use of a novel multi-subject alignment method that aligns neural representations according to brain anatomy is highlighted, which is a significant contribution to the field.
- The paper is well-organized and provides a clear and concise description of the methodology, making it accessible to readers.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed discussion on the limitations of the proposed method, which could provide a more comprehensive understanding of its applicability and potential challenges.
- The paper does not adequately compare the proposed method with other state-of-the-art methods, which could help in understanding its relative effectiveness and positioning within the field.
- The evaluation metrics used in the study, such as median rank and accuracy, are not well-explained or justified, which could limit the understanding of the results.
- The paper does not discuss the computational complexity or the scalability of the proposed method, which could be critical for practical applications in larger or more complex datasets.
- The methodology is not sufficiently novel, as it relies on existing methods such as FUGW and does not introduce significant new contributions beyond these.
- The paper lacks a comprehensive discussion on the ethical implications of the work, which is crucial given the potential applications in brain decoding.
