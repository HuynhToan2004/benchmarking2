 **Summary:**
The paper introduces EmerNeRF, a novel method for learning spatial-temporal representations of dynamic driving scenes, leveraging a hybrid static-dynamic world representation and a flow field to aggregate temporal features. EmerNeRF effectively separates static and dynamic scene components and estimates scene flow, all through self-supervision. It introduces a new dataset, NOTR, for assessing neural fields under extreme and highly-dynamic conditions. The method achieves state-of-the-art performance in scene reconstruction, novel view synthesis, and scene flow estimation. Despite its strengths, the paper is criticized for its incremental novelty, lack of detailed analysis, and inadequate discussion on the limitations of the proposed method.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel hybrid spatial-temporal representation, which effectively separates static and dynamic scene components, enabling the estimation of scene flow through self-supervision.
- It proposes a new dataset, NOTR, which is a valuable resource for assessing neural fields under extreme and highly-dynamic conditions.
- The method achieves state-of-the-art performance in scene reconstruction, novel view synthesis, and scene flow estimation, demonstrating the effectiveness of the proposed approach.
- The paper is well-written, easy to follow, and includes a comprehensive discussion on the limitations of the proposed method, which is crucial for understanding the scope and applicability of the research.
- The introduction of a new dataset, NOTR, is a significant contribution to the field, providing a benchmark for evaluating neural fields under challenging conditions.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed analysis of the proposed method, particularly in the ablation study section, which is crucial for understanding the effectiveness and robustness of the proposed approach.
- The novelty of the method is questioned, with some reviewers pointing out that similar approaches have been previously explored, such as the use of a hybrid static-dynamic world representation and the estimation of scene flow.
- The paper does not provide a detailed discussion on the limitations of the proposed method, which could help in understanding the scope and applicability of the research.
- The incremental nature of the proposed method, which builds upon existing works like Instant-NGP, raises concerns about the true novelty of the contributions.
- The paper could benefit from a more thorough discussion on the limitations of the proposed method, including its applicability in different scenarios and its potential negative societal impacts.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors provide a detailed analysis of the proposed method, particularly in the ablation study section, to better understand the effectiveness and robustness of the proposed approach?
- How does the proposed method compare to other state-of-the-art methods in terms of computational efficiency and resource requirements?
- The paper mentions the use of a hybrid static-dynamic world representation and the estimation of scene flow. Can the authors provide a more detailed explanation of these components and how they contribute to the overall effectiveness of the method?
- The paper mentions the use of a new dataset, NOTR, which is a significant contribution to the field. Can the authors provide more details on the dataset, including its size, diversity, and the selection criteria used to create it?
- Can the authors discuss the potential limitations of the proposed method and how these might affect its applicability in different scenarios?
