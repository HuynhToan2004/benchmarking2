 **Summary:**
The paper introduces S-ViLM, a novel video-language pre-training framework that leverages fine-grained structures in videos and text to learn region-object correspondences and temporal-aware features simultaneously. Key components include inter-clip spatial grounding, intra-clip temporal grouping, and global contrastive learning. These components aim to enhance the model's understanding of video content by focusing on spatial and temporal details. The framework is evaluated on four downstream tasks, demonstrating significant improvements over existing methods. However, concerns are raised regarding the clarity of the methodology, the complexity of the model, and the lack of comparative analysis with other recent methods.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, clear, and easy to follow, making it accessible to readers.
- The motivation behind the research is strong, focusing on the importance of learning fine-grained structures in video-language models.
- The proposed method, S-ViLM, is innovative and demonstrates significant improvements over existing methods in various downstream tasks.
- The paper includes comprehensive evaluations and ablation studies, providing a robust validation of the proposed method.
- The methodology is technically sound, with a well-defined objective function and clear explanations of the proposed methods.
- The paper addresses an important problem in video-language understanding and presents a novel approach to video-language pre-training.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a clear explanation of how the cut-and-paste operation and intra-clip temporal grouping are connected to the global contrastive learning objective.
- The methodology section is complex and difficult to understand, particularly the explanation of the grouping module and the inter-clip spatial grounding.
- The paper does not adequately compare its method with other recent methods, such as TemPVL and LAVILA, which also focus on fine-grained video-text alignment.
- There is a lack of discussion on the limitations of the proposed method, which could provide a more balanced perspective.
- The paper does not include sufficient visualizations or examples to illustrate the effectiveness of the proposed method, particularly in the context of temporal action localization.
- The paper could benefit from a more detailed analysis of the computational efficiency and scalability of the proposed method.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors clarify the connection between the cut-and-paste operation and intra-clip temporal grouping to the global contrastive learning objective?
- How does the proposed method handle the complexity introduced by the grouping module and the inter-clip spatial grounding?
- Could the authors provide more detailed explanations or examples to illustrate the effectiveness of the proposed method, particularly in the context of temporal action localization?
- How does the proposed method compare with other recent methods in terms of computational efficiency and scalability?
- Can the authors discuss the potential limitations of their proposed method and how these could be addressed in future research?
