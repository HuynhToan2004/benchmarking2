 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel probabilistic perspective for generating adversarial examples, aiming to maintain the original semantics of input data while deceiving classifiers. It proposes a method that interprets geometric constraints on adversarial examples as distributions, enabling a transition from geometric to semantic constraints. The approach uses an energy-based model to train on a dataset of image transformations, which is then used to generate adversarial examples that preserve the original semantics. The paper presents a method that optimizes the generation of adversarial examples using a probabilistic approach, which is evaluated on various datasets including MNIST, SVHN, and CIFAR10. Despite the innovative approach, the paper has been critiqued for its limited evaluation on high-resolution datasets and its reliance on human annotations for success rate measurements.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel probabilistic perspective for generating adversarial examples, which is a significant contribution to the field.
- The approach of using an energy-based model to train on a dataset of image transformations is innovative and potentially effective.
- The paper is well-written, making it easy to follow, and the experiments are conducted on multiple datasets, demonstrating the versatility of the proposed method.
- The method is described as interesting and novel, offering a new approach to generating adversarial examples that maintain the original semantics of input data.
- The paper includes a comprehensive evaluation across different datasets and models, which helps in validating the effectiveness of the proposed method.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed discussion on the limitations of the proposed method, particularly in terms of its applicability to high-resolution datasets and its dependence on human annotations for success rate measurements.
- The paper does not provide sufficient experimental evidence to support the claim that the proposed method can generate adversarial examples that are more successful than existing methods.
- The paper's evaluation is limited to low-resolution datasets, which may not adequately demonstrate the effectiveness of the method on more complex or high-resolution images.
- The methodology and some experimental results are not clearly explained, making it difficult for readers to fully understand the implications and contributions of the research.
- The paper does not sufficiently compare its results with existing methods, which could undermine the perceived effectiveness of the proposed method.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors provide more details on how the method performs on high-resolution datasets and whether it can generate adversarial examples that are imperceptible to humans?
- How does the proposed method compare to other existing methods in terms of generating adversarial examples that maintain the original semantics of input data?
- Could the authors clarify the experimental setup and results, particularly in figures where the success rates are not clearly indicated?
- Is there a possibility to include a broader range of datasets and models in future evaluations to better demonstrate the generalizability of the proposed method?
