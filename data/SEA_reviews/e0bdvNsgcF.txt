 **Summary:**
The paper addresses the problem of finding the k largest/smallest elements in a factorized tensor, particularly in the CANDECOMP/PARAFAC (CP) format. It proposes two algorithms: an alternating iterative method combined with the Maximal Block Increasing (MBI) approach and a block-search strategy to improve accuracy. Theoretical analysis is provided for the convergence behavior of the alternating iterative algorithm, and numerical experiments demonstrate the efficiency and accuracy of the proposed methods. The paper also discusses the computational complexity and the memory requirements of the proposed algorithms, comparing them to existing methods.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, with clear and concise descriptions of the proposed algorithms and their theoretical underpinnings.
- The algorithms are easy to implement and provide a theoretical guarantee of convergence.
- The proposed methods are efficient and show good performance in numerical experiments, particularly in finding the largest and smallest elements in a factorized tensor.
- The paper introduces a general continuous optimization model for the task of locating the k largest/smallest elements in a factorized tensor, which is a significant contribution to the field.
- The paper includes a convergence analysis of the alternating iterative algorithm, which is a valuable contribution to the literature.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed discussion on the computational complexity and memory requirements of the proposed algorithms, which could be crucial for practical applications.
- There is a lack of comparison with other existing methods for finding the k largest/smallest elements in a tensor, such as those based on the Tucker or tensor-train formats.
- The paper does not adequately address the scalability of the proposed algorithms, particularly in high-dimensional or large-scale tensor problems.
- The convergence analysis provided is limited, and the paper does not discuss the convergence rate of the proposed algorithms.
- The paper does not sufficiently address the limitations of the proposed methods, particularly in terms of their applicability to high-dimensional or large-scale tensor problems.
- The paper lacks a detailed discussion on the impact of initialization on the performance of the proposed algorithms.

**Questions:**
