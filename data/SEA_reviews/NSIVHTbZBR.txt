 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel method for image inpainting using Tractable Probabilistic Models (TPMs) to guide the diffusion process. By combining the output of a diffusion model with that of a TPM, the method aims to generate more semantically consistent images. The approach is demonstrated to be effective in high-resolution image inpainting tasks, showing improved semantic coherence and visual quality. The paper also explores the use of TPMs to control the diffusion model's output, which is a significant advancement in the field. Despite its strengths, the paper has been criticized for its limited novelty, the complexity of the method, and the need for more extensive comparisons with other state-of-the-art methods.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written and easy to follow, making it accessible to a broad audience.
- The proposed method effectively combines the output of a diffusion model with that of a Tractable Probabilistic Model (TPM) to improve the semantic coherence of generated images.
- The method is innovative, particularly in its application of TPMs to guide the diffusion process, which is a novel approach in image inpainting.
- The paper provides a clear and detailed explanation of the methodology, including the theoretical underpinnings and practical implementation.
- The experimental results are convincing, demonstrating the effectiveness of the proposed method in improving the quality and semantic coherence of inpainted images.
- The paper is well-motivated, with a clear focus on improving the semantic coherence of inpainted images.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a comprehensive comparison with other state-of-the-art methods, which could help establish the superiority or novelty of the proposed method.
- The methodology is complex, requiring a deep understanding of TPMs and their application in image inpainting, which may limit its accessibility to a broader audience.
- The paper does not provide a detailed analysis of the computational complexity or the runtime of the proposed method, which is crucial for practical implementation.
- The novelty of the method is questionable, as it appears to be a simple combination of existing techniques without significant innovation.
- The paper could benefit from a more thorough discussion on the limitations and potential negative societal impacts of the proposed method.
- There are some typographical and formatting errors in the manuscript, which need to be corrected for clarity and professionalism.
