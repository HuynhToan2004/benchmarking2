 **Summary:**
The paper presents a novel approach for converting pre-trained transformer models into spiking neural networks (SNNs) without the need for additional training or fine-tuning. The authors propose a training-free conversion method that approximates non-linear operations in both spatial and temporal dimensions using Universal Group Operators (UGOs) and Temporal-Corrective Self-Attention Layers (TCSA). This method is demonstrated to be effective in retaining the generalization performance of the original models, which is crucial for neuromorphic hardware deployment. The paper also includes theoretical analysis and empirical results showing the effectiveness of the proposed method.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel approach to convert pre-trained transformer models into SNNs without training or fine-tuning, which is a significant advancement in the field of neuromorphic computing.
- The proposed method, Spatio-Temporal Approximation (STA), is a training-free pipeline that can convert ANN Transformers to SNNs via universal approximations in both spatial and temporal domains.
- The paper provides theoretical analysis on the error bounds and convergence rates of both key modules in STA, enhancing the credibility and applicability of the proposed method.
- The conversion of a pre-trained mainstream Transformer (ViT-B/32 from CLIP) into an SNN without additional training or fine-tuning is a significant achievement, demonstrating the practical utility of the proposed method.
- The paper is well-organized, clearly written, and includes detailed descriptions of the conversion process, making it accessible to readers with diverse backgrounds.
- The empirical results show that the proposed method achieves state-of-the-art accuracy for SNNs on multiple benchmarks after supervised fine-tuning, demonstrating the effectiveness of the method.
