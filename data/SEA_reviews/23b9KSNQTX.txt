 **Summary:**
The paper introduces RETSim, a novel deep learning model designed for robust text similarity detection, specifically for near-duplicate text retrieval, clustering, and dataset deduplication. RETSim utilizes a combination of RETVec text vectorizer and a transformer block to generate robust neural embeddings, which are then used to address challenges such as typo-laden near-duplicate text retrieval in a multilingual setting. The paper also introduces the W4NT3D benchmark, which allows for the evaluation of models under adversarial conditions. RETSim's performance is demonstrated to surpass that of MinHash and other neural text embeddings, as evidenced by its superior results on various benchmarks and real-world applications.

**Strengths:**
- The paper addresses an important problem of robust text similarity detection, which is crucial for applications such as near-duplicate text retrieval, clustering, and dataset deduplication.
- The proposed RETSim model is innovative, combining the RETVec text vectorizer with a transformer block to generate robust neural embeddings, which are particularly effective in handling typo-laden near-duplicate text retrieval in a multilingual setting.
- The paper introduces the W4NT3D benchmark, a significant contribution to the field, which allows for the evaluation of models under adversarial conditions, enhancing the understanding and evaluation of models' performance under challenging conditions.
- The experimental results demonstrate that RETSim outperforms existing methods in various tasks, showing its effectiveness and robustness.
- The paper is well-organized, clearly written, and provides a detailed discussion on the RETSim model, including its architecture and training procedure, which is beneficial for understanding and replicating the model.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper could benefit from a more comprehensive discussion on related works, particularly on the use of character-level embeddings and the application of similar techniques in other domains.
- There is a lack of clarity on the specific contributions of the RETSim model compared to existing methods, such as RETVec, which might lead to confusion about the novelty and effectiveness of the proposed model.
- The paper does not sufficiently discuss the limitations of the RETSim model, which could provide a more balanced view of its capabilities and potential improvements.
- The paper does not adequately address the scalability of the RETSim model to larger datasets or more complex scenarios, which could limit its applicability in real-world settings.
- There is a need for more detailed comparisons with other state-of-the-art methods, particularly in terms of computational efficiency and robustness under different conditions.

**Questions:**
- Could you clarify the specific contributions of the RETSim model compared to existing methods, such as RETVec? How does RETSim improve upon or differ from these methods?
- How does the RETSim model handle different types of noise or adversarial attacks beyond typos? Is it robust against other forms of perturbations?
- What are the limitations of the RETSim model, and under what conditions might it fail or perform less effectively?
- Could you provide more details on the scalability of the RETSim model to larger datasets or more complex scenarios?
- How does the RETSim model compare in terms of computational efficiency and robustness under different conditions to other state-of-the-art methods?
- The paper mentions the use of the W4NT3D benchmark for evaluating models under adversarial conditions. Could you elaborate on how this benchmark enhances the evaluation of models and what specific insights it provides into the models' performance?
