 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel method for learning from label proportions, leveraging a belief propagation algorithm to estimate pseudo-labels from aggregated labels. This approach involves a two-step process: first, using a Gibbs distribution to assign pseudo-labels based on bag-level and pair-wise constraints, and then refining these labels through a deep neural network that considers both instance-level and bag-level information. The methodology is tested across various datasets and shows promising results, although the paper is criticized for its clarity, the need for more extensive comparisons with existing methods, and the potential for misleading terminology.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel method for learning from label proportions, which is a significant contribution to the field.
- The methodology is well-described, with clear explanations of the steps involved, and the paper provides extensive details on the experimental setup and results.
- The paper is well-written, making it accessible and easy to follow, with a clear and logical structure.
- The use of belief propagation to estimate pseudo-labels is a novel and interesting approach, and the paper provides a detailed analysis of the performance of the belief propagation algorithm.
- The paper includes a comprehensive set of experiments and ablation studies, which demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed method.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper could benefit from improved clarity and writing, particularly in the description of the methodology and the experimental results.
- There are several instances of potentially misleading or incorrect terminology, such as the use of "belief propagation" and "Gibbs distribution" in the context of learning from label proportions, which may not accurately reflect the true nature of the approach.
- The paper lacks a thorough discussion of related work and does not adequately compare its methodology with existing methods, which could lead to a misunderstanding of the novelty and effectiveness of the proposed approach.
- The paper's experimental setup and results are not sufficiently detailed or clear, making it difficult to fully understand the implications of the findings.
- The paper does not discuss the limitations of its methodology or potential negative societal impacts, which could be important considerations for the application of the proposed method.

**Questions:**
- Could you clarify the terminology used in the paper, particularly the use of "belief propagation" and "Gibbs distribution" in the context of learning from label proportions?
- How does the proposed method compare to existing methods in terms of computational efficiency and scalability, especially in large datasets?
- Can you provide more details on the experimental setup, including the specifics of the datasets used and the hyperparameters tuned?
- How does the proposed method perform in scenarios with imbalanced class proportions, and what are the potential implications of this for the effectiveness of the method?
- Could you elaborate on the potential negative societal impacts of the proposed method, particularly in terms of privacy and data security?
