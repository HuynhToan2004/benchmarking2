 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel optimization algorithm, Loss-Controlled Asymmetric Momentum (LCAM), aimed at improving the performance of stochastic gradient descent (SGD) by utilizing different momentum coefficients for non-sparse and sparse weights. The algorithm is designed to adaptively adjust the momentum based on the loss value, aiming to enhance the training of parameters that change frequently and slowly. Theoretical analysis and experimental results on various datasets demonstrate the potential of LCAM to reduce the number of training iterations while maintaining or improving test accuracy. However, concerns were raised about the clarity and depth of the theoretical analysis, the empirical validation of the algorithm's effectiveness, and the generalizability of the findings across different datasets and model architectures.

**Strengths:**
- The paper addresses an important and interesting problem in the field of optimization in deep learning, specifically focusing on the limitations of adaptive optimizers and the potential of asymmetric momentum.
- The authors provide a clear and detailed explanation of the LCAM algorithm, including its theoretical underpinnings and practical implementation, which could be beneficial for further research and development in this area.
- The paper includes a comprehensive set of experiments on various datasets and models, demonstrating the effectiveness of the proposed method in reducing the number of training iterations while maintaining or improving test accuracy.
- The paper is well-structured and easy to follow, making it accessible to a broad audience, including both experts and non-experts in the field.
- The authors have provided the code for their experiments, enhancing the reproducibility and transparency of their research.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a thorough theoretical analysis, particularly in terms of the convergence guarantees of the proposed method and the generalizability of the findings across different datasets and model architectures.
- The experimental results presented are somewhat limited and do not convincingly demonstrate the superiority of the proposed method over existing optimizers like SGD with momentum or Adam.
- The paper suffers from a lack of clarity in certain sections, particularly in the explanation of the algorithm's components and the theoretical underpinnings of the LCAM algorithm.
- There are several typos and grammatical errors throughout the paper, which could detract from the overall quality and professionalism of the work.
- The paper does not adequately address the potential negative societal impacts of the proposed method, which could be a significant concern for some readers and reviewers.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors provide a more detailed analysis of the convergence properties of the LCAM algorithm, particularly in terms of the rate and global convergence guarantees?
- How does the proposed method perform on more complex datasets and models, such as those used in large-scale image classification tasks like ImageNet?
- Could the authors clarify the algorithmic and theoretical details of the LCAM algorithm, particularly in sections where the explanations are unclear or incomplete?
- How does the proposed method compare to other state-of-the-art optimizers in terms of computational efficiency and memory requirements?
- Can the authors provide more detailed experimental results, including standard deviations and a broader range of datasets, to better demonstrate the effectiveness and generalizability of their method?
- How does the proposed method address the potential negative societal impacts, particularly in terms of fairness and bias in machine learning models?
