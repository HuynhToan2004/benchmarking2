 **Summary:**
The paper investigates the application of Explainable AI (XAI) techniques to predict and manage crowding in subway stations, using a rule-based model and counterfactual explanations. The study leverages a dataset derived from simulations to train the model, which predicts the likelihood of crowding and suggests countermeasures to prevent it. The authors conducted a survey with transportation experts to assess the model's applicability and realism of suggestions. The paper's main contributions include the use of XAI to enhance transparency and interpretability in AI systems, which is crucial for improving public transportation management. However, the paper suffers from several issues, including a lack of clarity in methodology, insufficient experimental validation, and a limited scope that does not adequately address the complexity of real-world scenarios.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces an innovative approach by integrating Explainable AI (XAI) techniques with counterfactual explanations to predict and manage crowding in subway stations, which is a significant contribution to the field.
- The authors have conducted a survey with transportation experts to assess the applicability and realism of the suggestions provided by the model, which adds a practical dimension to the research.
- The paper addresses an important problem in the context of smart cities, focusing on improving public transportation management, which is crucial for enhancing urban sustainability.
- The use of a dataset derived from simulations allows for a controlled and realistic evaluation of the proposed methods, which is a strength in terms of methodological rigor.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks clarity in explaining the methodology, particularly the choice of variables and the training of the LLM, which are crucial for understanding the experimental setup and results.
- The experimental validation is insufficient, with the paper not providing sufficient details on how the results were validated or the methodology's effectiveness compared to existing methods.
- The scope of the study is limited, focusing on a single station and not considering real-world variations or the impact of external factors, which could affect the generalizability of the findings.
- The paper does not adequately address the complexity of real-world scenarios, such as the presence of multiple trains or variations in passenger behavior, which could significantly affect the model's performance.
- The paper's presentation could be improved, particularly in terms of clarity and organization, to better convey the research's contributions and findings.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors clarify how the LLM was trained, specifically whether it was trained on the same dataset used for prediction?
- How does the paper address the issue of overfitting, especially given the limited size of the dataset?
- What is the rationale behind the choice of variables used in the LLM, and how were they selected?
- How does the model handle variations in passenger behavior, such as unexpected surges in demand or changes in passenger preferences?
- Could the authors provide more details on the experimental setup and validation, including how the results were validated and the methodology's effectiveness compared to existing methods?
- How does the paper address the potential limitations of the simulation-based dataset, such as its realism and applicability to real-world scenarios?
