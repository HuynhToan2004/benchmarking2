 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel linear attention-based Large Language Model (LLM) named TransNormerLLM, which integrates several enhancements over the TransNormer architecture. These improvements include a gating mechanism, positional embedding, tensor normalization, and inference acceleration techniques. The model's performance is evaluated against various benchmarks, demonstrating competitive results with state-of-the-art models while maintaining faster inference speeds. The paper also discusses the challenges in scaling the model to larger sizes and provides a detailed analysis of the model's performance and efficiency.

**Strengths:**
- The paper presents a significant contribution to the field by proposing a new linear attention-based LLM that achieves both accuracy and efficiency.
- The authors have conducted extensive ablation studies to demonstrate the effectiveness of each proposed component, which is crucial for understanding the impact of each modification.
- The paper is well-written, making it accessible and easy to follow, with a clear presentation of the methodology and results.
- The proposed method, TransNormerLLM, is evaluated on various benchmarks, demonstrating its competitive performance against state-of-the-art models.
- The paper introduces a novel Lightning Attention technique that significantly accelerates linear attention, enhancing the model's performance.
- The model's scalability is highlighted, with the potential to scale to even larger models, which is a significant advantage for future applications.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a thorough discussion on the limitations of the proposed method, particularly concerning its scalability to larger models and its performance on more complex tasks.
- There is a noticeable absence of comparisons with other efficient attention mechanisms, which could provide a more comprehensive understanding of the model's advantages.
- The paper does not provide sufficient details on the training and inference speeds, which are crucial for evaluating the model's efficiency.
- The model's performance on more complex tasks such as summarization, translation, and code generation is not evaluated, which could limit the understanding of its applicability in practical scenarios.
- There are concerns about the model's reproducibility due to the lack of detailed implementation and training specifics.
- The paper could benefit from a more detailed discussion on the system optimization techniques used, particularly in relation to the Megatron-LM model parallelism.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors provide more details on the training and inference speeds of the proposed model, especially in comparison to other models?
- How does the proposed model perform on more complex tasks such as summarization, translation, and code generation?
- Can the authors elaborate on the model's scalability, particularly in terms of its ability to handle larger models?
- How does the model perform in terms of computational efficiency and memory usage, especially when compared to other efficient attention mechanisms?
- Could the authors provide a more detailed comparison with other models, including a discussion on the model's performance in terms of perplexity and other relevant metrics?
- How does the model handle the scalability issues mentioned in the paper, particularly in relation to the model's performance on larger datasets?
