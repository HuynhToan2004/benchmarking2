 **Summary:**
The paper introduces CoarsenConf, a novel method for molecular conformer generation using an SE(3)-equivariant hierarchical Variational Autoencoder (VAE). CoarsenConf employs a coarse-graining technique to aggregate atomic coordinates into coarse-grained representations, which are then refined using a channel selection mechanism and a coordinate refinement process. This method is evaluated on various datasets including GEOM-QM9 and GEOM-DRUGS, showing improvements in RMSD precision and property prediction benchmarks. The paper also discusses the potential of CoarsenConf in downstream applications such as protein docking, although the evaluation in this area is limited. The methodology, while innovative, is critiqued for its lack of novelty in certain aspects and its reliance on pre-existing methods like RDKit for initial conformer generation.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, with clear explanations of the methodology and results, and includes a detailed description of the model architecture.
- The use of coarse-graining in molecular conformer generation is novel and provides a flexible representation that can accommodate molecules of varying sizes and shapes.
- The evaluation of the method is extensive, covering various datasets and downstream applications, demonstrating the robustness of the proposed method.
- The paper provides a thorough discussion of related work and includes a detailed appendix with additional experiments, enhancing the credibility of the results.
- The method shows significant improvements over previous state-of-the-art methods on benchmarks such as GEOM-QM9 and GEOM-DRUGS, and is shown to be effective in downstream tasks like protein docking.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed discussion on the coarse-graining procedure, particularly the rationale behind the choice of torsional angles and the impact of different coarse-graining strategies on the results.
- There is a lack of novelty in some aspects of the method, such as the use of hierarchical VAEs and channel selection mechanisms, which have been previously explored in other works.
- The method's dependence on RDKit for initial conformer generation raises concerns about the fairness of the comparison with other methods that do not use pre-generated conformers.
- The paper does not provide a detailed analysis of the computational cost and training time of the proposed method, which could be crucial for practical applications.
- The evaluation of the method in downstream applications such as protein docking is limited, and the results are not convincingly superior to existing methods.
- The paper does not adequately address the limitations of the method, particularly in terms of the generalizability of the results to other datasets or molecular structures.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors provide a more detailed discussion on the coarse-graining procedure, including the rationale behind the choice of torsional angles and the impact of different coarse-graining strategies on the results?
- How does the proposed method compare in terms of computational cost and training time with other existing methods, especially those that do not use pre-generated conformers?
- Can the authors provide a more detailed analysis of the results in downstream applications, particularly in protein docking, to clarify the superiority of the proposed method over existing methods?
- How does the method perform when applied to other datasets or molecular structures, and what are the limitations of the method in terms of generalizability?
- Can the authors clarify the role of the RDKit conformer in the evaluation of the method and discuss the implications of using pre-generated conformers for the fairness of the comparison?
