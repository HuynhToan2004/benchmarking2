 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel method for positive unlabeled (PU) learning, focusing on the case where only positive samples are labeled and unlabeled samples are either positive or negative. The authors propose a contrastive learning objective that utilizes labeled positive samples to learn a cluster-preserving representation, which is then used to assign pseudo-labels to unlabeled samples. This approach is supported by theoretical analysis and empirical results showing improvements over existing methods. The paper also discusses the challenges in risk estimation and proposes a method to address these issues, though the theoretical guarantees are limited to specific assumptions.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, clear, and easy to follow, with a clear motivation and a well-structured presentation.
- The proposed method is novel and innovative, introducing a contrastive learning objective that utilizes labeled positive samples to learn a cluster-preserving representation, which is then used to assign pseudo-labels to unlabeled samples.
- The paper provides a theoretical analysis of the proposed method, including proofs of theoretical guarantees and a discussion on the bias-variance trade-off.
- Extensive experiments are conducted on several benchmark datasets, demonstrating the effectiveness of the proposed method.
- The paper discusses the limitations of existing methods and provides a novel approach to address these limitations.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a thorough discussion on related work, particularly recent works that have also addressed similar problems.
- The theoretical guarantees provided are limited to specific assumptions, and the paper does not sufficiently discuss the limitations of these assumptions.
- The experimental setup and results are not convincingly presented, with some results not showing significant improvements over existing methods.
- The paper does not adequately address the practicality of the proposed method in scenarios where the dataset does not naturally form separate clusters for positive and negative samples.
- There is a lack of discussion on the limitations of the proposed method, particularly in scenarios where the method may not be effective.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors provide more details on the related work, especially recent works that have addressed similar problems?
- How does the proposed method perform when the dataset does not naturally form separate clusters for positive and negative samples?
- Could the authors discuss the limitations of the proposed method and provide examples or scenarios where the method may not be effective?
- How does the method perform when the number of labeled positive samples is very small?
- Can the authors provide more detailed experimental results, including the standard deviation of the results and a discussion on the significance of the improvements over existing methods?
