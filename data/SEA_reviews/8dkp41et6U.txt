 **Summary:**
The paper introduces LongLLMLingua, a method for prompt compression in long context scenarios, addressing challenges such as higher computational costs, longer latency, and inferior performance. LongLLMLingua employs a question-aware coarse-to-fine compression method, document reordering, dynamic compression ratios, and a post-compression subsequence recovery strategy to enhance the perception of key information in LLMs. The method is evaluated across various tasks including single-/multi-document QA, few-shot learning, summarization, and code completion, demonstrating improvements in performance and efficiency. The paper also includes an ablation study to understand the impact of individual components.

**Strengths:**
- The paper addresses a significant and relevant problem of long context scenarios, where LLMs face challenges such as higher computational costs, longer latency, and inferior performance.
- The proposed method, LongLLMLingua, is well-motivated and designed, with a focus on improving the perception of key information in LLMs.
- The paper is well-written, clear, and easy to follow, with a clear presentation of the methodology and results.
- The experiments are comprehensive, covering a wide range of long context scenarios and demonstrating the effectiveness of the proposed method in improving performance and reducing costs.
- The ablation study provides insights into the individual contributions of each component of the method.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed discussion on the computational costs of the proposed method, particularly the inference cost of the small language model used for compression.
- There is a potential issue with the reproducibility of the results due to the reliance on specific models and datasets.
- The paper could benefit from a more thorough comparison with existing methods, particularly in terms of computational efficiency and performance.
- The novelty of the proposed method is questioned, as it appears to be a simple extension of existing methods without significant technical innovation.
- The paper could provide more detailed explanations and justifications for the choices made in the methodology, particularly the use of perplexity-based metrics and the design of the compression algorithm.

**Questions:**
- Could you provide more details on the computational costs of the proposed method, particularly the inference cost of the small language model used for compression?
- How does the performance of the proposed method compare when using different models or datasets?
- Can you clarify the differences between the proposed method and existing methods, particularly in terms of technical innovation and effectiveness?
- How does the proposed method handle the trade-off between compression ratio and performance?
- Could you provide more insights into the design choices, particularly the use of perplexity-based metrics and the dynamic compression ratios?
- In the ablation study, could you provide results for the baseline without document reordering to better understand the impact of this component?
