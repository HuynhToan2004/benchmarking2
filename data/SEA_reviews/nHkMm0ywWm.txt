 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a method for identifying causal structures in the presence of latent variables, which are assumed to follow a linear non-Gaussian acyclic model. The authors propose a novel algorithm that iteratively identifies atomic units and their causal relationships, leveraging the Generalized Independent Noise (GIN) condition. The method's theoretical soundness is demonstrated through the use of the GIN condition and identifiability conditions, which are supported by both synthetic and real-world data experiments. The paper is well-structured, providing clear explanations and examples, but it lacks a detailed discussion on the computational complexity and the robustness of the algorithm against noisy or incomplete data.
