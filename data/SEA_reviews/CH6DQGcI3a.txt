 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel family of adversarial attacks, named SuperDeepFool (SDF), which aims to enhance the efficiency and accuracy of adversarial perturbations in deep neural networks. The proposed method is designed to address the limitations of existing attacks such as DeepFool by ensuring the perturbations are orthogonal to the decision boundary, thereby improving the robustness of adversarial examples. The paper also presents a new adversarial training method using SDF, which is evaluated against state-of-the-art attacks and shows significant improvements in terms of both effectiveness and computational efficiency. The method's theoretical underpinnings and experimental validations are discussed, highlighting its potential to advance the field of adversarial machine learning.

**Strengths:**
- The paper addresses the significant issue of adversarial robustness in deep learning, which is crucial for the security and reliability of AI systems.
- The proposed method, SuperDeepFool (SDF), is simple, efficient, and effective, offering a novel approach to generating adversarial perturbations that are orthogonal to the decision boundary, thereby improving the accuracy and efficiency of adversarial attacks.
- The paper is well-written, making it accessible and easy to follow, which is essential for a broad audience in the field of adversarial machine learning.
- The experiments are comprehensive, covering various datasets and models, providing a robust validation of the proposed method's effectiveness and efficiency.
- The theoretical analysis and empirical results support the claims made by the authors, enhancing the credibility of the proposed method.
- The paper presents a significant contribution by improving the computational efficiency of adversarial attacks, which is crucial for practical applications and future research.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a thorough discussion on the limitations of the proposed method, particularly in terms of its applicability to different types of networks and the potential impact of hyperparameters on the attack's effectiveness.
- There is a noticeable absence of comparisons with other popular adversarial attacks such as AutoAttack, PGD, and C&W, which could provide a more comprehensive evaluation of the proposed method's performance.
- The paper does not sufficiently discuss the potential negative societal impacts of the proposed method, which is a crucial aspect of ethical considerations in AI research.
- The method's reliance on a linear approximation of the decision boundary could limit its effectiveness on more complex networks, and the paper does not adequately address this limitation.
- The paper does not include a detailed discussion on the computational complexity and scalability of the proposed method, which are crucial for practical applications and future research.
