 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel Hessian-free algorithm, LV-HBA, for solving constrained bi-level optimization problems, particularly focusing on the lower-level problem involving constraints that couple both upper-level and lower-level variables. The authors develop a smooth proximal Lagrangian value function to handle these constraints and transform the original problem into an equivalent optimization problem with smooth constraints. The paper provides non-asymptotic convergence analysis for LV-HBA, which does not require strong convexity assumptions for the lower-level problem and can accommodate non-singleton scenarios. Empirical results demonstrate the practical performance of the algorithm. However, the paper lacks a detailed comparison with existing methods, and the experimental results are not convincingly superior to state-of-the-art methods.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel Hessian-free algorithm, LV-HBA, for solving constrained bi-level optimization problems, which is a significant contribution to the field.
- The paper provides a detailed theoretical analysis of the proposed algorithm, including non-asymptotic convergence analysis, which is crucial for understanding the algorithm's behavior and performance.
- The algorithm is well-explained, with clear and concise explanations that aid in understanding the methodology and its implementation.
- The paper is well-structured, with a clear flow from problem formulation to algorithm development and analysis, making it accessible and understandable.
- The proposed algorithm does not require strong convexity assumptions for the lower-level problem, which is a notable advantage over many existing methods.
- The paper includes a detailed convergence analysis, which is a strong point as it provides a rigorous theoretical foundation for the algorithm's effectiveness.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed comparison with existing methods, particularly in terms of computational efficiency and performance metrics such as the number of iterations and wall-clock time.
- The experimental results do not convincingly demonstrate the superiority of the proposed algorithm over state-of-the-art methods, which undermines the paper's claims.
- The paper could benefit from a more comprehensive literature review and a clearer explanation of the algorithm's advantages over existing methods.
- There are several typographical and formatting errors throughout the paper, which detract from its professional presentation.
- The paper does not provide sufficient details on the experimental setup and parameter choices, which makes it difficult to replicate the results and understand the impact of different parameter choices on the algorithm's performance.
