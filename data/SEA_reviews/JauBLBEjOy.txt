 **Summary:**
The paper introduces AvatarStudio, a novel method for generating high-fidelity, animatable 3D avatars from textual descriptions. AvatarStudio leverages a coarse-to-fine generative model, combining a NeRF-based representation for initial coarse generation with SMPL-guided articulation for precise pose control and high-resolution rendering. The methodology includes a DensePose-conditioned ControlNet for SDS guidance, enhancing pose control and reducing the Janus problem. The paper presents comprehensive experiments and ablation studies, demonstrating the superiority of AvatarStudio over existing methods. The generated avatars are evaluated for their quality, detail, and pose control, showcasing the potential of the proposed method in various applications, such as multimodal avatar animations and style-guided avatar creation.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel method for generating high-quality, animatable 3D avatars from textual descriptions, which is a significant advancement in the field.
- The proposed method leverages a coarse-to-fine generative model, which effectively combines a NeRF-based representation for initial coarse generation with SMPL-guided articulation for precise pose control and high-resolution rendering.
- The paper presents a comprehensive set of experiments and ablation studies, demonstrating the effectiveness of the proposed method and highlighting the impact of each component.
- The paper is well-written, clear, and easy to follow, making it accessible to a broad audience.
- The results are visually impressive, showing the potential of the method in various applications, such as multimodal avatar animations and style-guided avatar creation.
- The paper addresses limitations and discusses potential societal impacts, demonstrating a thoughtful approach to the ethical implications of the technology.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper could benefit from a more detailed comparison with other methods, particularly in terms of computational efficiency and the quality of animations.
- The paper does not provide sufficient details on the training data used for the 2D diffusion model, which could introduce bias in the generated avatars.
- The paper lacks a detailed discussion on the limitations of the method, particularly concerning the generation of fine-grained motions and the efficiency of the model.
- The paper does not sufficiently address the potential societal impacts of the technology, particularly concerning the generation of fake videos or manipulating authentic videos for illicit purposes.
- The paper does not compare the proposed method with more recent and relevant methods, such as those mentioned in the review, which could provide a more comprehensive evaluation of the proposed method.

**Questions:**
- How does the proposed method compare with other recent methods, such as those mentioned in the review, particularly in terms of computational efficiency and the quality of animations?
- Can the authors provide more details on the training data used for the 2D diffusion model and how bias is addressed in the generation process?
- How does the proposed method address the limitations mentioned, particularly concerning the generation of fine-grained motions and the efficiency of the model?
- Could the authors provide more detailed comparisons with other methods, including those that do not use SMPL-guided articulation, to better understand the advantages of the proposed method?
- How does the proposed method handle the generation of avatars with different body shapes and sizes, and what are the implications for the generalizability of the method?
- Could the authors provide more detailed results on the generation of avatars with different body shapes and sizes, and how does the method handle such variations?
