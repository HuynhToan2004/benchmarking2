 **Summary:**
The paper introduces MPDistil, a meta-policy distillation technique aimed at enhancing knowledge distillation between teacher and student models. MPDistil utilizes a curriculum learning framework for the student model, enabling it to surpass the teacher model's performance on various tasks. This methodology includes a meta-teacher learning step where the teacher model is fine-tuned on a quiz dataset to optimize outputs for both teacher and student models. The student model undergoes curriculum learning, where it is fine-tuned on a curriculum of tasks to maximize rewards. Extensive experiments on SuperGLUE and GLUE benchmarks demonstrate the effectiveness of MPDistil compared to traditional knowledge distillation and meta-knowledge distillation methods. The paper also includes a meta-policy knowledge distillation framework that optimizes a shared utility function to enhance the teacher's predictive capabilities and ability to impart knowledge to the student model.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-organized, clearly written, and easy to follow, making complex concepts accessible to a broad audience.
- The proposed method is innovative, integrating meta-policy knowledge distillation and curriculum learning, which is novel in the context of knowledge distillation.
- The experimental results are strong, demonstrating the effectiveness of the proposed method in enhancing the performance of student models compared to traditional knowledge distillation and meta-knowledge distillation methods.
- The paper provides a comprehensive evaluation of the proposed method, including a detailed comparison with existing methods and a thorough analysis of the results.
- The methodology is flexible and can be applied to different types of teacher models, including large language models, making it a versatile tool for various applications.

**Weaknesses:**
