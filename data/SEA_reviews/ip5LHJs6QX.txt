 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel modulation mechanism for efficient vision networks, focusing on the integration of attention mechanisms with convolutional blocks. This approach, termed EfficientMod, aims to enhance the performance and efficiency of vision networks by combining the benefits of both convolutional and attention mechanisms. The authors have conducted extensive experiments across various tasks including image classification, object detection, and semantic segmentation, demonstrating the effectiveness of their method. The paper also discusses the computational complexity and latency of the proposed model, providing a detailed analysis of its performance on different hardware platforms.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel modulation mechanism that effectively integrates convolutional and attention mechanisms, which is a significant advancement in the field of vision networks.
- The authors have conducted extensive experiments across various tasks, including image classification, object detection, and semantic segmentation, demonstrating the effectiveness of their method.
- The paper is well-written and provides a clear presentation of the proposed method, making it accessible and understandable to readers.
- The methodology is simple yet effective, with a focus on reducing computational complexity and latency, which is crucial for real-time applications.
- The paper provides a detailed analysis of the computational complexity and latency of the proposed model, which is crucial for understanding its practical implications.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a thorough discussion on the limitations of the proposed method, particularly in terms of scalability and generalization to other tasks or datasets.
- The novelty of the method is questionable as it appears to be a combination of existing techniques, such as VAN and FocalNet, without sufficient differentiation or innovation.
- There is a lack of detailed comparison with other efficient vision transformers, which could provide a clearer picture of the method's relative performance and advantages.
- The paper does not sufficiently discuss the design choices of the EfficientMod block, such as the specific roles of the "ctx" and "v" branches, which could confuse readers about the actual contributions of the proposed method.
- The paper's claims about the efficiency of the proposed method are not convincingly supported by the results, which could lead to misleading conclusions about its practical utility.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors provide more detailed comparisons with other efficient vision transformers to clarify the advantages of their method?
- How does the proposed method perform when scaled up to larger models or more complex tasks, such as high-resolution image classification or large-scale object detection?
- Can the authors provide a more detailed analysis of the computational complexity and latency of the proposed method under different hardware conditions, particularly in real-world scenarios?
- How does the EfficientMod block compare in terms of performance and efficiency with other state-of-the-art methods, especially in tasks where efficiency is critical?
- Could the authors clarify the design choices of the EfficientMod block, particularly the roles of the "ctx" and "v" branches, and how these contribute to the overall effectiveness of the method?
