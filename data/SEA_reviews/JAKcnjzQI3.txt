 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel framework for data transformation aimed at preserving privacy by suppressing sensitive attributes while maintaining utility in data. It proposes a methodology that utilizes mutual information to balance the preservation of sensitive and useful attributes, supported by theoretical analysis and empirical validation. The framework is tested across multiple datasets, demonstrating its effectiveness in preserving privacy and utility. However, concerns are raised about the clarity of the presentation, the robustness of the experimental setup, and the generalizability of the results. The paper also lacks a detailed discussion on the limitations and societal impacts of the proposed method.

**Strengths:**
- The paper presents a novel approach to privacy-preserving data transformation, focusing on the balance between privacy and utility, which is a significant and relevant issue in the field.
- The proposed method is theoretically sound, with a clear mathematical formulation and detailed proofs, which enhances its credibility and reproducibility.
- The paper provides a comprehensive evaluation across multiple datasets and modalities, demonstrating the effectiveness of the proposed method in various scenarios.
- The use of mutual information for privacy-preserving data transformation is a novel and interesting approach that has been validated through empirical results.
- The paper is well-written, making it accessible and easy to follow, which is crucial for a broad audience.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed discussion on the limitations of the proposed method, which could help in understanding its applicability and potential drawbacks.
- There is a need for more comprehensive experiments to validate the effectiveness of the method across a wider range of datasets and scenarios, particularly those involving more complex and diverse data types.
- The paper could benefit from a more detailed comparison with existing methods, including both theoretical and empirical evaluations, to clearly establish the advantages of the proposed method.
- The presentation of the paper could be improved to enhance clarity and readability, particularly in the introduction and related works sections, which are currently too brief and do not adequately contextualize the research.
- There are concerns about the robustness of the experimental setup, as the results are not consistently superior to existing methods, and the method's performance varies significantly across different datasets.
- The paper does not sufficiently discuss the societal impacts and ethical considerations of the proposed method, which is crucial for understanding the broader implications of the research.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors provide a more detailed discussion on the limitations of the proposed method and how these might affect its practical applicability?
- How does the proposed method perform in scenarios where the data is highly imbalanced or where the sensitive and useful attributes are not clearly distinguishable?
- Could the authors clarify the differences between the proposed method and existing methods, particularly in terms of theoretical contributions and practical implications?
- In the experimental setup, why were some datasets and attributes chosen over others, and how do these choices affect the generalizability of the results?
- Could the authors provide more detailed results and analysis for the experiments involving facial images and other modalities to better understand the method's performance in diverse settings?
- How does the proposed method handle the trade-off between privacy and utility, especially in scenarios where these goals conflict?
- Could the authors discuss the potential societal impacts and ethical considerations of the proposed method, particularly in terms of privacy preservation and data utility?
