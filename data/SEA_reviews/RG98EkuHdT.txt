 **Summary:**
The paper introduces ArtiHippo, a novel method for lifelong learning in Vision Transformers (ViTs) aimed at addressing catastrophic forgetting. ArtiHippo identifies the final projection layer of the multi-head self-attention block as the Artificial Hippocampal (ArtiHippo) of ViTs. The method employs a hierarchical exploration-exploitation sampling-based single-path one-shot Neural Architectural Search (NAS) to grow ArtiHippo dynamically. The paper proposes four operations: Skip, Reuse, Adapt, and New, which are used to manage plasticity and stability in the model. Extensive experiments are conducted on the Visual Domain Decathlon (VDD) and 5-Dataset benchmarks, demonstrating the method's effectiveness in lifelong learning scenarios. However, concerns about the scalability of the proposed method to larger datasets and the complexity of the model architecture are raised.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel method for lifelong learning in ViTs, specifically focusing on the final projection layer of the multi-head self-attention block, which is identified as the ArtiHippo of ViTs.
- The method employs a hierarchical exploration-exploitation sampling-based single-path one-shot Neural Architectural Search (NAS) to grow ArtiHippo dynamically, which is a significant advancement in the field.
- Extensive experiments are conducted on the Visual Domain Decathlon (VDD) and 5-Dataset benchmarks, demonstrating the method's effectiveness in lifelong learning scenarios.
- The paper is well-written and easy to follow, with clear explanations of the proposed method and its components.
- The use of prompt-based methods in conjunction with ArtiHippo is explored, which shows promise in enhancing performance.
- The paper is the first to evaluate lifelong learning with ViTs on the large-scale, diverse, and imbalanced VDD benchmark, demonstrating strong empirical performance.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a thorough comparison with state-of-the-art methods, particularly in terms of computational efficiency and scalability to larger datasets.
- The scalability of the proposed method to larger datasets and more complex model architectures is not adequately addressed.
- The paper does not discuss the limitations of the proposed method, which could provide valuable insights for future research.
- The methodology section is not detailed enough, and the paper could benefit from more comprehensive explanations and detailed implementation descriptions.
- The paper does not sufficiently address the complexity of the model architecture, which could impact the practical applicability of the proposed method.
- The paper lacks a discussion on the potential societal impacts of the proposed method, which is an important consideration for any AI technology.
