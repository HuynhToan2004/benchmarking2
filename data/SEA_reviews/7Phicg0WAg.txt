 **Summary:**
The paper introduces FlexCap, a method for generating localized descriptions for images using a combination of an open-vocabulary object detector and a text decoder. This approach allows for the generation of controllable captions that can be used for various tasks such as visual question answering and dense captioning. The model is trained on a large dataset of image-box-caption triplets derived from web-scale text-image pairs, which is claimed to be a significant contribution. The method's performance is evaluated on several benchmarks, including zero-shot VQA, dense captioning, and ablation studies. Despite the innovative approach, concerns were raised about the novelty and the effectiveness of the dataset generation method.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel approach to generate controllable localized visual descriptions using a length prefix, which is a significant advancement in the field of image captioning and visual question answering.
- The creation of a large-scale dataset of image-box-caption triplets from web-scale image-text pairs is a substantial contribution to the research community.
- The paper is well-written, with clear explanations of the methodology and the dataset, making it easy to follow and understand.
- The method achieves state-of-the-art (SOTA) performance on several benchmarks, including zero-shot VQA and dense captioning, demonstrating its effectiveness.
- The paper presents a comprehensive set of experiments and ablations, providing a thorough evaluation of the proposed method.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a clear justification for using a single object detector (OWL-ViT) for dataset generation, which might limit the diversity and accuracy of the dataset.
- There is a lack of detailed analysis on the impact of different object detectors and the robustness of the method against noisy or inaccurate bounding boxes.
- The method's reliance on a single model (OWL-ViT) for both dataset generation and downstream tasks might limit its applicability and robustness.
- The paper does not provide sufficient qualitative results to support the claims of controllable length and diversity in the generated captions.
- There is a lack of comparison with other datasets and methods, such as GRiT, which could have provided a more robust evaluation of the proposed method.
- The paper does not adequately address the potential limitations of the method, such as its performance under varying conditions or with different datasets.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors clarify the choice of using a single object detector (OWL-ViT) for dataset generation and discuss the potential impact on dataset diversity and accuracy?
- How does the method handle noisy or inaccurate bounding boxes, and what are the effects on the generated captions?
- Can the authors provide more detailed analysis on the impact of different object detectors and the robustness of the method against noisy or inaccurate bounding boxes?
- Why was a single model (OWL-ViT) chosen for both dataset generation and downstream tasks, and what are the implications for the method's applicability and robustness?
- Can the authors provide more qualitative results to support the claims of controllable length and diversity in the generated captions?
- Could the authors compare the performance of their method with other datasets and methods, such as GRiT, to provide a more robust evaluation of the proposed method?
