 **Summary:**
The paper explores the hyperparameter transfer across both width and depth in deep residual networks using a novel parameterization that scales the learning rate and network weights by the square root of depth. This approach is supported by both empirical evidence and theoretical analysis, including the application of dynamical mean field theory (DMFT). The authors demonstrate that this method can effectively transfer hyperparameters from smaller to larger networks, thereby reducing computational costs associated with hyperparameter tuning. The paper also discusses the theoretical underpinnings of this method, including the scaling limits and the dynamics of neural networks.

**Strengths:**
- The paper addresses an important and relevant problem in deep learning, specifically the transfer of hyperparameters across different network architectures, which is crucial for reducing computational costs and enhancing scalability.
- The authors provide a comprehensive theoretical framework and empirical evidence to support their claims, including a novel parameterization that scales learning rates and network weights by the square root of depth.
- The empirical results are convincing and demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed method across various architectures and datasets, including CIFAR-10 and ImageNet.
- The paper is well-written, clear, and easy to follow, with a clear presentation of the problem and the proposed solution.
- The theoretical analysis is rigorous and provides insights into the dynamics of neural networks, contributing to the understanding of feature learning in deep networks.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a thorough comparison with existing methods, particularly in terms of the transfer of hyperparameters across depth and width. This could be addressed by including more detailed comparisons and discussions on the limitations of previous approaches.
- The empirical results are based on a limited number of training epochs, which might not fully demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed method in practical scenarios.
- The paper does not discuss the potential impact of different learning rate schedules on the transferability of hyperparameters, which could be a significant factor in practical applications.
- The paper does not include experiments on more complex datasets or models, which could help validate the findings in more challenging scenarios.
- The paper could benefit from a more detailed discussion on the practical implications of the theoretical results and the limitations of the proposed method.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors provide a more detailed comparison with existing methods, particularly in terms of the transfer of hyperparameters across depth and width?
- How does the proposed method perform when applied to more complex datasets or models, such as those used in large-scale image classification tasks?
- What are the specific advantages of the proposed method compared to other hyperparameter transfer methods, such as those based on adaptive kernel machines?
- How does the proposed method handle the transfer of hyperparameters in transformer architectures, given the different dynamics in these models compared to residual networks?
- Could the authors elaborate on the choice of scaling factors in their experiments and how they were determined?
- How does the proposed method perform in practical scenarios where the number of training epochs is not limited?
- Could the authors discuss the potential impact of different learning rate schedules on the transferability of hyperparameters and how these schedules were chosen?
