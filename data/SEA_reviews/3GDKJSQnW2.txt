 **Summary:**
The paper introduces Pivotal Dynamic Editing (PDEdit), a novel text-based video editing framework that enables non-rigid video editing by generating a pivotal prompt from a target prompt and a video. This pivotal prompt is used to guide the video editing process, ensuring that the edited video remains consistent with the original content. The methodology involves a two-stage process: first, generating a pivotal prompt from the target prompt and video, and then using this prompt to guide the editing process. The paper demonstrates the effectiveness of PDEdit through various experiments and comparisons with existing methods, showcasing its ability to perform complex edits such as motion changes and object overlay while preserving the temporal consistency of the edited motions.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, with clear descriptions of the proposed methods and a clear presentation of the motivation behind the research.
- The proposed method, PDEdit, is novel and interesting, addressing the challenges of non-rigid video editing, which is an important and relevant topic in the field of video editing.
- The paper includes comprehensive experiments and ablation studies, which help in understanding the effectiveness of the proposed method.
- The methodology of PDEdit is straightforward and easy to understand, making it accessible to a broader audience.
- The paper includes a variety of examples and demonstrations, which help in illustrating the capabilities of the proposed method.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed discussion on the limitations of the proposed method, which is crucial for understanding its applicability and potential drawbacks.
- There is a noticeable absence of comparisons with existing methods in the field, particularly those that use source prompts for video tuning, which could provide a more comprehensive evaluation of the proposed method.
- The paper does not include a user study to assess the perceptual quality of the edited videos, which is essential for evaluating the practical applicability of the method.
- The experimental results and their interpretations are not convincing, with some results showing marginal improvements over existing methods.
- The paper lacks a discussion on the computational complexity of the proposed method, which is crucial for understanding its scalability and practicality.
- The paper does not address how the method handles the complexity of video editing tasks, especially in terms of handling multiple moving objects in a video.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors clarify the term "rigid edit" as used in the paper?
- How does the proposed method handle the complexity of video editing tasks, especially in terms of handling multiple moving objects in a video?
- Can the authors provide more details on the computational complexity of the proposed method, including the time and memory requirements for different video editing tasks?
- How does the proposed method compare with existing methods that use source prompts for video tuning?
- Could the authors provide more detailed examples or demonstrations of the method's capabilities, particularly in scenarios where the method might struggle or fail to produce satisfactory results?
- How does the method handle the trade-off between editing quality and video fidelity, especially in scenarios where the editing might require significant changes to the video content?
- Can the authors provide a more detailed discussion on the limitations of the proposed method and how these might impact its practical applicability?
