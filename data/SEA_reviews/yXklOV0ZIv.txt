 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a learnable counterfactual attention (LCA) mechanism aimed at enhancing the performance of singer identification (SID) tasks by focusing attention on meaningful but not overly discriminative regions. This mechanism is designed to guide the attention model to learn more discriminative features, thereby improving the identification of fine-grained vocals. The LCA mechanism is integrated into an attention-based deep-net model, and its effectiveness is evaluated on the artist20 dataset, showing improvements over existing models. However, the paper is critiqued for its limited novelty, as it primarily modifies the loss function to guide attention learning, and its generalizability to other tasks is unclear.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel learnable counterfactual attention (LCA) mechanism, which guides the attention model to learn more discriminative features, enhancing the identification of fine-grained vocals.
- The proposed method is evaluated on the artist20 dataset and shows improvements over existing models, demonstrating the effectiveness of the LCA mechanism.
- The paper is well-organized, easy to follow, and the experimental results are promising, showing the effectiveness of the proposed method.
- The paper is significant in addressing the limitations of existing counterfactual attention by proposing a mechanism that learns counterfactual attention directly from the network, rather than relying on random attention.
- The motivation behind the proposed method is clear, and the paper is well-written, making it accessible and understandable.

**Weaknesses:**
- The novelty of the paper is limited as it primarily modifies the loss function to guide attention learning, which is a common approach in the field.
- The paper's evaluation is limited to the SID task and the artist20 dataset, raising concerns about the generalizability of the proposed method to other tasks and datasets.
- The paper does not provide a detailed comparison with other attention mechanisms, such as self-attention, which could help in understanding the advantages of the proposed method.
- The paper lacks a comprehensive discussion on the limitations of the proposed method, which is crucial for understanding its applicability and potential drawbacks.
- The paper could benefit from a more thorough analysis of the attention maps to provide deeper insights into the effectiveness of the proposed method.
- The paper does not include a discussion on the computational cost of the proposed method, which is an important consideration for practical applications.

**Questions:**
- How does the proposed method compare with other attention mechanisms, such as self-attention?
- Can the proposed method be applied to other tasks beyond SID? If so, how does it perform on those tasks?
- How does the proposed method perform on other datasets beyond artist20?
- What are the limitations of the proposed method, and how do these limitations impact its applicability and effectiveness?
- Could the authors provide a detailed analysis of the attention maps to provide deeper insights into the effectiveness of the proposed method?
- How does the proposed method perform under different noise conditions, and what are the implications of these conditions on the model's performance?
