 **Summary:**
The paper introduces StructChart, a novel framework aimed at enhancing the understanding and reasoning of visual charts. StructChart transforms chart information from a tabular format into Structured Triplet Representations (STR), which are more conducive to chart perception and reasoning tasks. The framework includes a Structuring Chart-oriented Representation Metric (SCRM) for evaluating chart perception, and leverages Large Language Models (LLMs) to generate synthetic chart data. The approach shows promise in improving the performance of chart perception and reasoning tasks, as demonstrated through experiments on various datasets. However, the paper has been critiqued for its reliance on existing models like GPT-3.5 and the lack of comprehensive comparisons with other methods.

**Strengths:**
- The paper addresses the significant challenge of chart understanding, which is crucial for various applications in science and industry.
- The proposed methodology, including the Structured Triplet Representations (STR) and the Structuring Chart-oriented Representation Metric (SCRM), is innovative and shows potential for advancing the field.
- The paper is well-written, making it accessible and easy to follow, which is crucial for a broad audience.
- The use of Large Language Models (LLMs) for data generation and the introduction of a synthetic dataset (SimChart9K) are valuable contributions to the field.
- The methodology is supported by extensive experiments, which demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed approach.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a thorough comparison with existing methods, particularly in terms of the performance of the proposed method against state-of-the-art approaches.
- There is an overreliance on LLMs, which might limit the generalizability of the findings and the applicability of the method in real-world scenarios.
- The paper could benefit from more comprehensive evaluations, including a broader range of datasets and metrics to better assess the robustness and effectiveness of the proposed method.
- The paper's presentation could be improved to better highlight the novelty and contributions of the proposed method, particularly in the introduction and related work sections.
- The use of LLMs for chart generation and the evaluation of the proposed method using the same model (GPT-3.5) raises concerns about the objectivity and fairness of the results.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors provide a more detailed comparison with existing methods, particularly in terms of performance and the effectiveness of the proposed STR and SCRM?
- How does the proposed method perform on a wider range of datasets, including those that might not be as diverse as those used in the current study?
- Can the authors clarify the role and impact of the LLM-based self-inspection data production scheme on the overall performance and generalizability of the method?
- How does the method perform when compared to other models, such as GPT-4V and LLaVA-1.5, which might offer different capabilities or perspectives?
- Could the authors elaborate on the decision to use GPT-3.5 for both data generation and evaluation, and consider using different models for these tasks to ensure a more objective assessment?
