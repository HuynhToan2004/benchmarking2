 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a new kernel, labeled pseudo Neural Tangent Kernel (lpNTK), which integrates label information to measure sample interactions during learning. It proposes a method to identify and remove data items with similar lpNTK feature representations, demonstrating that this approach can maintain or even enhance generalization performance. The paper also explores how lpNTK can help understand learning phenomena such as the learning difficulty of samples and forgetting events. Experiments are conducted on MNIST and CIFAR-10 datasets, showing that lpNTK can be used to predict forgetting events and improve generalization performance by removing redundant samples. However, the paper has been critiqued for its limited scope in terms of the datasets used and the lack of a thorough comparison with existing methods.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, clear, and easy to follow, with a clear structure and well-organized presentation.
- The introduction of the labeled pseudo Neural Tangent Kernel (lpNTK) is a novel approach that integrates label information into the NTK, providing a new perspective on sample interactions.
- The paper demonstrates that lpNTK can be used to identify and remove poisoning training samples without affecting generalization performance, showing practical utility.
- The theoretical analysis and empirical results are convincing, with a clear explanation of the methodology and its implications.
- The paper addresses an important and timely topic, namely the role of data relationships in generalization performance, which is of significant interest to the community.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper is limited to simple datasets such as MNIST and CIFAR-10, which may not adequately represent real-world scenarios.
- The paper lacks a thorough comparison with existing methods, particularly in terms of the scalability and computational efficiency of lpNTK compared to other kernel methods.
- The practical applicability of lpNTK is limited due to its high computational complexity and the need for accurate model parameters, which may not be feasible in all scenarios.
- The paper does not sufficiently address the limitations of lpNTK, such as its dependency on accurate model parameters and its scalability.
- The paper could benefit from a more detailed discussion on the theoretical underpinnings of lpNTK, particularly in relation to the Neural Tangent Kernel (NTK) and the assumptions made.

**Questions:**
- How does the lpNTK perform on more complex datasets such as ImageNet or other large-scale datasets?
- Can the authors provide a detailed comparison of the computational complexity and scalability of lpNTK compared to other kernel methods?
- How does the lpNTK compare to other methods that use label information to measure sample interactions, such as those mentioned in related works?
- What are the specific assumptions made in the theoretical analysis of lpNTK, and how do these assumptions affect the applicability and reliability of the results?
- Can the authors elaborate on the practical implications of lpNTK, particularly in terms of its scalability and computational efficiency in real-world applications?
