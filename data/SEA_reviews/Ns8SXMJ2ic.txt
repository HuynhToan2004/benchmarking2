 **Summary:**
The paper presents a benchmarking study on local zeroth-order optimizers for quantum learning tasks, focusing on Hamiltonian minimization and quantum generative modeling. The study evaluates seven optimizers, including SPSA, AdamSPSA, 2-SPSA, QNSPSA, GES, xNES, and sNES, using randomized quantum circuits and randomized objective functions. The primary findings suggest that SPSA is the most effective optimizer across the tested tasks, although its performance is not consistently superior to other methods. The paper also discusses the importance of hyperparameter tuning and the limitations of current optimization strategies. However, the paper's contributions are seen as limited due to its focus on a narrow set of optimizers and its lack of novel insights.

**Strengths:**
- The paper provides a comprehensive benchmarking of various local zeroth-order optimizers, which is a significant contribution to the field.
- The results are presented clearly, with good visual aids that facilitate understanding.
- The study includes a variety of tasks, which enhances the generalizability of the findings.
- The paper highlights the importance of hyperparameter tuning and the need for more robust or adaptive optimization heuristics.
- The authors have conducted a large number of experiments, which supports the validity of their conclusions.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks novel insights and fails to provide new or significant contributions to the field.
- The choice of optimizers and tasks is limited, which may not generalize well to other quantum learning problems.
- The paper does not adequately address the limitations of the chosen optimizers, which could have been discussed in the introduction.
- The paper does not sufficiently differentiate its approach from existing benchmarks, making it difficult to understand the unique contributions of the study.
- The paper could benefit from a more detailed discussion on the limitations of the benchmarking study and a more thorough analysis of the results.
- The presentation could be improved by providing more detailed explanations of the experiments and results, and by including a more comprehensive literature review.

**Questions:**
- Could you clarify the term "randomized objective" as used in the paper?
- What are the specific advantages of using SPSA over other optimizers, and why does it perform well in general?
- How do the authors justify the choice of tasks and optimizers, and what criteria were used for their selection?
- Can you provide a more detailed explanation of the experiments, including the randomization process and the hyperparameter tuning methodology?
- How do the results of this study compare to similar studies in the literature, and what are the implications of these comparisons?
- Could you elaborate on the potential future directions for research based on the findings of this study?
