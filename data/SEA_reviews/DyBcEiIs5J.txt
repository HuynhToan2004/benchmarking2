 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a two-stage method, Multi-queue Contrastive Learning (MQCL), aimed at enhancing crowd counting performance in adverse weather conditions. The method leverages a novel multi-queue MoCo contrastive learning strategy to address the issue of weather class imbalance, and employs a refinement module guided by supervised contrastive learning to convert adverse weather representations to the normal domain. The paper also introduces a new synthetic adverse weather dataset, which is synthesized using a new synthetic method. The proposed method, MQCL, shows competitive performance on benchmark datasets, although it falls short of state-of-the-art methods in terms of accuracy. The method's novelty lies in its two-stage approach, which includes a multi-queue MoCo strategy and a supervised contrastive learning refinement process, aimed at improving robustness in adverse weather conditions.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel two-stage method for crowd counting in adverse weather conditions, which is a significant and practical problem in the field.
- The introduction of a new synthetic adverse weather dataset is a valuable contribution to the research community, enhancing the availability of data for training and testing models.
- The paper is well-written, clear, and easy to follow, making it accessible to a broad audience.
- The proposed method, Multi-queue Contrastive Learning (MQCL), is innovative and effectively addresses the issue of weather class imbalance through the use of multi-queue MoCo and supervised contrastive learning.
- The paper provides a detailed analysis of the proposed method, including ablation studies and comparisons with state-of-the-art methods, which supports the validity of the proposed approach.
- The introduction of a new synthetic adverse weather dataset, which is synthesized using a new synthetic method, is a significant contribution to the field, providing a valuable resource for future research.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed discussion on the limitations and potential negative societal impacts of the proposed method, which is crucial for understanding the broader implications of the research.
- The novelty of the proposed method is questioned as it combines existing techniques such as multi-queue MoCo and supervised contrastive learning without sufficient justification or innovation.
- The performance of the proposed method falls short of state-of-the-art methods, and the reasons for this are not thoroughly explored or explained.
- The paper does not adequately address the computational complexity of the proposed method, which could be a significant limitation for practical applications.
- The synthetic method used to create the new adverse weather dataset is not sufficiently detailed, which raises concerns about the validity and applicability of the dataset.
- The paper does not adequately compare the proposed method with existing methods, particularly those that also address adverse weather conditions, which could provide a clearer picture of the method's effectiveness.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors provide more details on the synthetic method used to create the new adverse weather dataset, including the specifics of the data generation process and the parameters used?
- How does the proposed method perform when compared to existing methods that also address adverse weather conditions? Could the authors provide a more detailed comparison in this regard?
- Could the authors clarify the computational complexity of the proposed method and discuss potential strategies for reducing computational requirements without sacrificing performance?
- The paper mentions that the method's performance falls short of state-of-the-art methods. Could the authors elaborate on the reasons for this and discuss potential improvements or modifications to the method that could address these shortcomings?
- Could the authors provide more detailed comparisons with other methods, including the baseline model used in the experiments, to better understand the relative performance of the proposed method?
