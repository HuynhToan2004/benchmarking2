 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel method for evaluating the alignment between conditions and generated outputs using a diffusion model. The method leverages the conditional probability measurable through the diffusion process to score the alignment, which does not require additional models or training datasets. This approach, termed the Condition Alignment Score (CAS), is demonstrated to be effective across various modalities including text-to-image, image-to-image, and text-to-audio. The paper also proposes a recursive inversion method to improve the accuracy of the CAS metric. Despite its simplicity and applicability, the method's effectiveness is questioned, with some reviewers expressing concerns about its performance compared to existing metrics.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel method to score the alignment between conditions and generated outputs, which does not require additional models or training datasets.
- The method is simple, elegant, and applicable across various modalities such as text-to-image, image-to-image, and text-to-audio.
- The paper is well-written, clear, and easy to follow, with a clear motivation and logical structure.
- The proposed method is original and innovative, leveraging conditional probability measurable through the diffusion process to evaluate the alignment, which is a significant contribution to the field.
- The experiments conducted on various models and modalities demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed method, showing promising results.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed discussion on the limitations of the proposed method, particularly in scenarios where the method might not perform well.
- The method's performance is not convincingly superior to existing metrics, and in some cases, it performs worse, which needs further explanation.
- The paper could benefit from more comprehensive experimental validation, including a broader range of models and conditions, and a more detailed analysis of the results.
- There are several typos and minor errors in the manuscript that need correction.
- The paper does not adequately address the potential negative societal impacts of the proposed method, which could be a significant concern.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors provide more details on the limitations of the proposed method and when it might not perform well?
- How does the proposed method compare to existing metrics in terms of performance and computational efficiency?
- Could the authors provide more details on the experimental setup, particularly the conditions used for evaluation and the selection of images for testing?
- How does the proposed method handle different types of conditions, such as textual and visual conditions?
- Could the authors clarify the computational complexity and time required for the recursive inversion process, especially in comparison to other methods?
- How does the proposed method handle the potential for bias in the diffusion model's output, particularly in scenarios where the model might not accurately reflect the true conditional likelihood?
