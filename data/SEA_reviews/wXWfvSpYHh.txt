 **Summary:**
The paper introduces MVSformer++ as an advanced transformer-based model for Multi-View Stereo (MVS) tasks. It builds upon the MVSFormer framework, integrating the DINOv2 model for feature extraction and proposing new attention mechanisms like Side View Attention (SVA) and Frustoconical Positional Encoding (FPE). These innovations aim to enhance the model's capability to process cost volumes and handle cross-view information effectively. The paper also explores various attention mechanisms, including linear attention, and presents extensive ablation studies to validate the contributions. Despite its technical merits, the paper is criticized for its limited novelty and marginal performance improvements over existing methods.

**Strengths:**
- The paper provides a comprehensive review of transformer-based approaches for Multi-View Stereo (MVS) and presents a novel approach to integrating cross-view information into the pre-trained DINOv2 model.
- The paper is well-written, making it easy to follow, and includes extensive ablation studies that validate the proposed components, such as the SVA module and the use of linear attention.
- The proposed 3D Frustoconical Positional Encoding (FPE) and Adaptive Attention Scaling (AAS) are innovative and show significant improvements in depth estimation accuracy.
- The paper achieves state-of-the-art results on the DTU and Tanks-and-Temples benchmarks, demonstrating the effectiveness of the proposed methods.
- The paper is well-organized, with a clear introduction and detailed explanations of the proposed methods, which are supported by comprehensive experiments and ablation studies.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed comparison with the latest state-of-the-art methods, such as those from the NeurIPS 2022 conference, which could have provided a clearer picture of the paper's contributions.
- The novelty of the paper is limited, as it primarily builds on existing methods like MVSFormer and DINOv2, without introducing fundamentally new concepts or approaches.
- The performance improvements over existing methods are marginal, which raises questions about the practical significance of the proposed enhancements.
- The paper could benefit from a more detailed discussion on the limitations of the proposed methods and potential areas for future research.
- The paper's presentation could be improved by better organization of figures and tables, especially in the appendix, to enhance readability and clarity.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors clarify the rationale behind the selection of linear attention over other attention mechanisms, particularly in the context of the ablation studies presented?
- How does the paper address the issue of attention dilution, especially in high-resolution images, and what are the implications for the performance of the model?
- Can the authors provide a more detailed comparison with the latest state-of-the-art methods to better establish the paper's contributions?
- How do the authors justify the marginal performance improvements over existing methods, and what are the potential reasons for these limitations?
- Could the authors discuss the potential limitations of the proposed methods and suggest areas for future research to address these challenges?
