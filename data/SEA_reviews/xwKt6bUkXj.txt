 **Summary:**
The paper investigates the impact of different training curricula on the learning dynamics of recurrent neural networks (RNNs) with trainable leak parameters, focusing on memory tasks. The authors explore how these parameters influence the network's ability to solve tasks with increasing memory requirements. They compare two training methods: single-head, where the network is trained on a single task at a time, and multi-head, where multiple tasks are trained concurrently. The results suggest that the multi-head curriculum can enhance the network's performance, stability, and generalization capabilities. The paper also discusses the implications of these findings for the development of RNNs that can handle complex tasks effectively.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, clear, and easy to follow, making it accessible to a broad audience.
- The research addresses a significant and timely problem in the field of recurrent neural networks (RNNs), focusing on the development of longer timescales for solving memory-dependent tasks.
- The study provides a comprehensive analysis of the impact of training curricula on the learning dynamics of RNNs, comparing different mechanisms such as single-neuron timescales and network-mediated timescales.
- The paper includes a thorough discussion on the implications of the findings for both computational and neuroscience communities, highlighting the relevance and applicability of the research.
- The experiments are well-designed, and the results are convincingly presented, supporting the claims made in the paper.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a comprehensive literature review, particularly in the area of RNNs and timescales, which could have strengthened the contextualization of the research.
- The experimental setup is limited to simple tasks, which might not adequately demonstrate the generalizability of the findings to more complex or real-world scenarios.
- The paper does not sufficiently discuss the limitations of the work, which could include the generalizability of the findings to other architectures or more complex tasks.
- There is a lack of comparison with other mechanisms that could contribute to the development of long timescales, such as synaptic delays or short-term plasticity.
- The paper could benefit from a more detailed discussion on the implications of the findings for practical applications, such as in the design of RNNs for specific tasks.

**Questions:**
- How do the findings of this study compare to other mechanisms that could contribute to the development of long timescales in RNNs, such as synaptic delays or short-term plasticity?
- Can the multi-head curriculum be applied to other architectures or tasks beyond the specific scenarios tested in this study?
- How does the network handle the trade-off between learning new tasks and retaining previously learned information?
- What are the implications of the findings for the design of RNNs for specific tasks, particularly in terms of the choice of training curriculum?
- How does the network's performance on the N-parity task compare to other tasks, and what are the specific challenges in training on this task?
- Could the authors provide more detailed explanations or examples to clarify the role of single-neuron timescales in the N-parity task, especially in relation to the multi-head curriculum?
