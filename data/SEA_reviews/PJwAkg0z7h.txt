 **Summary:**
The paper introduces EasyTPP, a benchmark repository for temporal point processes (TPPs), aimed at facilitating reproducible research and comparative analysis across various TPP models. The repository includes a unified interface for datasets, models, and evaluation programs, supported by a rich library of modules and implementations of popular neural TPPs. The benchmarking framework is designed to enhance research by providing a standardized platform for model evaluation and comparison. It also includes a diverse set of datasets and models, along with a comprehensive evaluation suite, which is expected to accelerate research progress and improve real-world impacts.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces EasyTPP, a valuable benchmarking repository for TPP models, which is a significant contribution to the field.
- The repository provides a unified interface for datasets, models, and evaluation programs, which is crucial for reproducible research and comparative analysis.
- The paper is well-written and easy to follow, making it accessible to a broad audience.
- The benchmarking framework is comprehensive, including a diverse set of datasets and models, which will accelerate research progress and improve real-world impacts.
- The implementation of popular neural TPPs and the provision of a rich library of modules facilitate the development of complex models.
- The paper is well-organized and provides a clear overview of the project, making it easy for readers to understand the contributions and benefits of the repository.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a clear motivation for the need for a benchmarking repository for TPP models, which could be better articulated to emphasize the importance of this work.
- The evaluation protocol is not thoroughly explained, particularly the criteria for model selection and the justification for the chosen metrics.
- The paper does not sufficiently address the limitations of the benchmark, which could include the diversity of datasets and models, the scalability of the benchmark, and the generalizability of the results.
- There is a lack of discussion on the potential negative societal impacts of the benchmark, which should be considered in the context of ethical implications.
- The paper does not include a detailed comparison with existing benchmarking frameworks, which could provide a better understanding of the contributions and innovations of EasyTPP.

**Questions:**
- How does the benchmark handle the diversity of datasets and models, particularly in terms of ensuring fairness and generalizability of the results?
- Can the authors clarify the criteria for model selection and the justification for the chosen metrics in the evaluation protocol?
- How does EasyTPP compare to existing benchmarking frameworks, particularly in terms of the contributions and innovations it offers?
- What are the potential negative societal impacts of the benchmark, and how are these being addressed or mitigated?
- How does EasyTPP address the scalability of the benchmark, particularly in terms of handling larger and more complex datasets and models?
