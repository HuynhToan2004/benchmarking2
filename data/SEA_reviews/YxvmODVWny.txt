 **Summary:**
The paper introduces RT-Sketch, a novel goal-conditioned policy for manipulation that utilizes hand-drawn sketches as a modality for specifying task goals. RT-Sketch leverages an image-to-sketch translation network to convert images of desired states into sketches, which are then used to condition a policy. The approach was tested on a robot manipulator, demonstrating comparable performance to language or image-conditioned policies in straightforward settings. The method also showed robustness to visual distractors and ambiguous language, which are common challenges in goal-conditioned imitation learning. The paper presents a detailed experimental setup and evaluates the system's performance using both quantitative and qualitative metrics.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel approach to goal-conditioned imitation learning by utilizing hand-drawn sketches, which is a less explored area in robotics.
- The methodology is well-articulated, with clear descriptions of the dataset generation, image-to-sketch translation network, and the RT-Sketch model architecture.
- The paper provides a detailed experimental setup, including the use of human annotators for evaluating the system, which adds a level of realism to the evaluation.
- The paper is well-written, with clear explanations of the methodology and results, making it accessible and understandable.
- The paper presents a novel application of sketches in goal-conditioned imitation learning, which is a promising direction for future research in robotics.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a thorough discussion on the limitations of the RT-Sketch system, which is crucial for understanding the scope and applicability of the proposed method.
- The paper does not provide a detailed analysis of the image-to-sketch translation network, which is a critical component of the system.
- The evaluation metrics used in the paper are not clearly defined or justified, which could affect the interpretation of the results.
- The paper does not discuss the potential societal impacts of the proposed method, which is an important consideration for any technological innovation.
- The paper could benefit from a more detailed discussion on the failure modes of the RT-Sketch system and how these might be addressed in future work.
- The paper does not compare the proposed method with other state-of-the-art methods in the field, which could provide a better understanding of the method's relative performance.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors provide more details on the image-to-sketch translation network, including the training data, architecture, and evaluation metrics?
- How does the RT-Sketch system handle different styles of sketches, especially those that are not black and white?
- What are the failure modes of the RT-Sketch system, and how do these impact the performance of the system?
- How does the RT-Sketch system compare with other state-of-the-art methods in the field of goal-conditioned imitation learning?
- Can the authors provide more details on the dataset used for training the image-to-sketch translation network, including the size and diversity of the dataset?
- How does the RT-Sketch system handle different levels of detail in the sketches, and how does this affect the performance of the system?
- Could the authors provide more details on the experimental setup, including the hardware and software used, and the specifics of the robot manipulator used in the experiments?
