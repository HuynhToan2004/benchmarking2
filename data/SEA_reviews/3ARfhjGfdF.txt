 **Summary:**
The paper introduces ReBis, a novel method for learning control-centric representations in reinforcement learning from images. ReBis integrates reward-free control information with reward-specific knowledge, using a transformer architecture to model dynamics and block-wise masking to reduce spatiotemporal redundancy. The method aims to address the limitations of existing bisimulation-based approaches by incorporating a highly expressive dynamics model and capturing reward-free control information. Empirical studies on Atari games and DeepMind Control Suit demonstrate ReBis's superior performance compared to existing methods. The paper also provides theoretical analysis and empirical results to support its claims, although it lacks detailed implementation and experimental setup descriptions.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel method called ReBis, which addresses the limitations of previous work by adhering to the bisimulation principle for RL representation learning.
- ReBis is designed to learn state representations tailored to vision-based RL, encompassing both spatial-temporal consistency and long-term behavior similarity.
- The paper provides a theoretical analysis and empirical results to support its claims, demonstrating the superior performance of ReBis on large benchmarks such as Atari games and DeepMind Control Suit.
- The use of a transformer architecture to model dynamics and block-wise masking to reduce spatiotemporal redundancy is innovative and addresses a significant challenge in RL from images.
- The paper is well-written, making it easy to follow, and the methodology is clearly explained, enhancing its accessibility and reproducibility.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed description of the implementation and experimental setup, making it difficult to fully understand the methodology and replicate the experiments.
- There is a lack of clarity on the specifics of how the method is implemented, such as the dimensions of the latent representations and the details of the transformer architecture used.
- The paper does not provide a detailed comparison with other state-of-the-art methods, which could help in understanding the relative performance of ReBis.
- The paper's theoretical analysis, while interesting, is somewhat disconnected from the main methodological contributions and could benefit from more integration into the main text.
- The paper could benefit from a more comprehensive discussion on the limitations of the proposed method and potential areas for future research.

**Questions:**
- Could you provide more details on the implementation of the method, including the dimensions of the latent representations and the specifics of the transformer architecture used?
- How does the method perform in terms of computational efficiency, and what are the implications of using a transformer architecture in the context of RL from images?
- Can you elaborate on the choice of using a transformer architecture over other models for modeling dynamics, and how this choice impacts the performance and applicability of the method?
- How does the method handle the balance between the behavioral loss and the reconstruction loss, and what are the implications of this balance on the performance of the method?
- In the context of the experimental results, how do the performance improvements of ReBis compare to other state-of-the-art methods, and what are the specific contributions of the method that lead to these improvements?
