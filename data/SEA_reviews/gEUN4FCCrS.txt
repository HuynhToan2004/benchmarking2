 **Summary:**
The paper introduces Value Bonuses with Ensemble Errors (VBE), a novel exploration strategy for reinforcement learning (RL) that utilizes an ensemble of random action-value functions (RQFs) to compute value bonuses. These bonuses are designed to encourage exploration by reducing to zero when the primary value function converges. VBE is tested against other exploration strategies like BDQN and reward bonus approaches in both classic gridworlds and Atari environments. The experiments show that VBE can effectively explore state spaces and learn faster than other methods, although it may not always outperform in terms of final performance.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, with clear explanations of the algorithm and its components, making it accessible to readers.
- The idea of using an ensemble of value functions to encourage exploration is innovative and well-explained, particularly the use of a maximum over an ensemble to set the value bonus.
- The experiments are well-designed and thorough, comparing the proposed method to existing baselines and showing that the method outperforms them in most cases.
- The paper introduces a novel approach to exploration in RL, which is a significant contribution to the field.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed theoretical analysis, particularly in the convergence of the value bonus to zero and the impact of this on the exploration process.
- The method's connection to existing methods like BDQN is not adequately explained, which could lead to confusion about the novelty of the approach.
- The paper could benefit from more detailed explanations and justifications for certain design choices, such as the use of a maximum over an ensemble for the value bonus.
- The experiments are limited to specific environments and do not include more challenging benchmarks like the DMC or Mujoco domains, which could affect the generalizability of the results.
- The paper does not discuss the computational cost of the method, which could be a significant concern for practical implementation.

**Questions:**
- Could you provide more details on the convergence of the value bonus to zero and how this impacts the exploration process?
- How does the proposed method compare to other methods like BDQN, particularly in terms of computational cost and practical implementation?
- Can you clarify the connection between VBE and BDQN, and explain why VBE is considered a new approach despite similarities to BDQN?
- How does the method handle the exploration of unseen states, and what happens if the value bonus does not converge to zero?
- In the experiments, why were some baselines not included in certain comparisons, such as VB RND in Figure 2 and VB ACB in Figure 3?
- Could you provide more details on the computational cost of the method and how it scales with the size of the ensemble?
