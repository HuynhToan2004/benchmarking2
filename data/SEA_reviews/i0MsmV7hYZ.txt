 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel approach to counterfactual inference by formulating it as a quantile regression problem, leveraging neural networks to estimate quantile functions and quantile regression parameters. This method avoids the need for structural causal models, offering a potentially more efficient and generalizable solution. Theoretical insights are provided, including a theorem that connects counterfactual inference with quantile regression and a generalization bound for the empirical estimator. Experiments demonstrate the method's effectiveness across various datasets, although the paper could benefit from more detailed comparisons with existing methods and a deeper exploration of the assumptions and limitations.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel approach to counterfactual inference using quantile regression, which is both innovative and theoretically grounded.
- The method is shown to be more sample-efficient than existing approaches, demonstrating its practical utility.
- The paper is well-written and clear, making it accessible to readers with varying backgrounds in the field.
- Theoretical insights provided, such as Theorem 1, offer a strong foundation for the proposed method.
- The experiments conducted on various datasets demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed method, showcasing its potential applicability.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed discussion on the assumptions required for the method to be effective, particularly the monotonicity assumption.
- The paper does not sufficiently compare the proposed method with existing approaches, especially in terms of computational efficiency and the generalizability of results.
- The paper does not address the issue of counterfactual inference in the presence of multiple treatments, which is a significant limitation.
- The presentation of the paper could be improved to enhance clarity and accessibility, especially in sections that are currently hard to follow.
- There is a lack of discussion on the limitations of the proposed method and potential negative societal impacts, which are crucial for understanding the broader implications of the research.
