 **Summary:**
The paper investigates the impact of symmetry in the loss function on the learning dynamics of neural networks, focusing on the effects of rescaling, rotation, and permutation symmetries. It proposes a general theorem that demonstrates how these symmetries can lead to structured constraints in the learning process, influencing the behavior of models under different conditions such as weight decay or gradient noise. The paper provides theoretical insights into how these symmetries affect the optimization landscape and the convergence properties of neural networks, offering practical implications for designing algorithms that enforce hard constraints in a differentiable manner. The theoretical framework is supported by numerical experiments, though the paper's experimental validation is considered limited and its empirical evidence is not convincingly robust.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written and easy to follow, with clear explanations of theorems and their implications.
- It presents a unified framework for analyzing the effects of symmetries in loss functions, which is novel and potentially useful for understanding the behavior of neural networks.
- The theoretical insights provided are significant, particularly the identification of symmetry as a cause of collapse phenomena in neural networks.
- The paper is structured in a logical and coherent manner, with a clear presentation of theorems and their implications.
- The proposed algorithm for enforcing structured constraints in a differentiable manner is a notable contribution, potentially useful for practitioners.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper's claims are considered overly broad and lack sufficient empirical evidence to support them. The experiments provided are limited and do not convincingly demonstrate the general applicability of the proposed framework.
- The paper's theoretical contributions are seen as incremental and not sufficiently novel, as many of the insights provided are already known in the literature.
- There is a lack of clarity in some sections of the paper, particularly in the definitions and explanations of certain terms and concepts.
- The paper's discussion on the impact of symmetries on the optimization landscape and the convergence properties of neural networks is not sufficiently detailed or convincing.
- The paper's experimental results are not convincingly robust, with some figures and results not adequately explained or validated.

**Questions:**

- How do the authors respond to the criticism that their paper's claims are overly broad and lack sufficient empirical evidence?
