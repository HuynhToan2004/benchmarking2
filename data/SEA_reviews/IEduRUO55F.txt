 **Summary:**
The paper introduces Eureka, a novel algorithm that utilizes large language models (LLMs) to design reward functions for reinforcement learning (RL) tasks. Eureka employs an evolutionary search strategy to iteratively refine reward functions based on LLM outputs, which are evaluated using a fitness function. This approach allows for the generation of complex reward functions that can be used to train agents in various simulated environments, including IsaacGym and Dexterity. The method is evaluated against human-designed rewards and existing LLM-based methods, showing significant performance improvements in most tasks. The paper also discusses the potential of using Eureka for in-context learning from human feedback, although this aspect is not extensively explored.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel approach using large language models (LLMs) for reward design, which is a significant advancement in the field of reinforcement learning (RL).
- The method is evaluated against human-designed rewards and existing LLM-based methods, showing significant performance improvements in most tasks.
- The paper is well-written, easy to follow, and provides comprehensive details on the experiments and implementation of the method.
- The methodology is simple yet effective, with a clear focus on using LLMs for reward design, which is a promising direction for future research.
- The paper includes a diverse set of environments and tasks, which demonstrates the general applicability of the method.
- The use of evolutionary search to improve reward functions is highlighted as a novel approach, which could be beneficial for future research.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper does not adequately discuss the limitations of the method, which could include the reliance on LLMs for reward design, the potential for sub-optimal rewards, and the lack of interpretability of the generated rewards.
- There is a lack of discussion on how the method could be adapted for real-world applications, such as in robotic manipulation.
- The paper could benefit from a more detailed discussion on how the method performs in different scenarios, particularly in environments where the reward functions are more complex or where the LLM's knowledge cut-off might affect performance.
- The paper does not sufficiently compare the performance of Eureka with other LLM-based methods, such as L2R, which might be crucial for understanding the relative effectiveness of the proposed method.
- The paper could be improved by including more detailed experimental results, such as the performance of Eureka in specific environments or tasks, and a more comprehensive comparison of the generated rewards with human-designed rewards.

**Questions:**
