 **Summary:**
The paper introduces AdamQLR, a novel optimization algorithm that combines the adaptive learning rate of Adam with the damping and learning rate selection strategies from K-FAC. The authors argue that AdamQLR can effectively address the challenges of tuning hyperparameters in Adam, thereby improving the performance of neural networks. The paper presents a detailed theoretical analysis and empirical evidence, including sensitivity analyses, to support the efficacy of AdamQLR. The proposed method aims to bridge the gap between first-order and second-order optimization methods, offering a potentially more robust and efficient approach to neural network training.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written and easy to follow, with a clear exposition of the proposed method, AdamQLR.
- The authors provide a detailed analysis of the AdamQLR algorithm, including its theoretical underpinnings and empirical performance, which demonstrates its effectiveness.
- The proposed algorithm, AdamQLR, is a significant contribution to the field, combining the strengths of Adam and K-FAC to potentially improve neural network training.
- The paper includes comprehensive sensitivity analyses and empirical results that validate the effectiveness of AdamQLR across various datasets and settings.
- The authors have effectively addressed the challenges of hyperparameter tuning in Adam, which is a common issue in machine learning applications.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a thorough discussion on the limitations and potential drawbacks of the proposed method, such as increased computational complexity and potential issues with overfitting in certain scenarios.
- The experimental results, while promising, are not sufficiently robust to conclusively demonstrate the superiority of AdamQLR over existing methods. The results show some variability and do not convincingly outperform other methods in all cases.
- The paper could benefit from a more detailed comparison with other optimization methods, including a broader range of datasets and architectures to better establish the generalizability of the findings.
- There are some typographical and formatting errors that need correction to improve the professional quality of the paper.
- The paper does not adequately address the potential impact of different data augmentation strategies on the performance of AdamQLR, which could be a significant factor in practical applications.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors provide more detailed results on the performance of AdamQLR on larger datasets such as ImageNet, especially in terms of computational efficiency and generalization performance?
- How does the proposed method perform when using different data augmentation strategies, and what are the implications for the robustness and generalizability of the results?
- Can the authors clarify the computational cost and memory requirements of AdamQLR compared to other optimization methods, especially in large-scale applications?
- How does the performance of AdamQLR compare when using different batch sizes, and what are the optimal batch sizes for achieving the best results?
- Can the authors provide a more detailed comparison of the training and testing performance of AdamQLR with other methods, particularly in terms of accuracy and loss metrics?
