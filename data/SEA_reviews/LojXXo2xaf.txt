 **Summary:**
The paper introduces MathGLM, a transformer-based model designed to handle arithmetic tasks and mathematical word problems. The model is trained on a dataset constructed using a step-by-step strategy to enhance its ability to perform arithmetic operations, including those involving large numbers and fractions. MathGLM is evaluated against existing models like GPT-4 and ChatGPT, demonstrating superior performance in arithmetic tasks and mathematical word problems. The paper also explores the impact of model size and training data on MathGLM's performance, showing that it can achieve high accuracy even with a small training set. Despite its strengths, the paper is criticized for its limited novelty, as it primarily focuses on a specific task without broader implications, and for its presentation, which lacks clarity and depth in certain sections.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a new model, MathGLM, which can handle arithmetic tasks without relying on external tools, demonstrating a significant advancement in the field.
- The model is trained on a dataset constructed using a step-by-step strategy, which enhances its ability to perform complex arithmetic operations and mathematical word problems.
- The paper provides a comprehensive evaluation of the model, comparing it to various baselines and demonstrating its superior performance in arithmetic tasks and mathematical word problems.
- The results show that the model can achieve high accuracy even with a small training set, indicating its robustness and effectiveness.
