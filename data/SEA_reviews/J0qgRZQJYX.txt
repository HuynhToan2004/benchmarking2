 **Summary:**
The paper introduces an axiomatic approach for model-agnostic concept explanations, focusing on the axioms of linearity, recursivity, and similarity. This framework aims to measure the importance of concepts in model predictions, which can be applied to various tasks such as model selection, optimizer selection, and model improvement. The approach is demonstrated through experiments on datasets like a-Pascal, showing the utility of the proposed method in understanding model behavior and improving model performance. The paper also discusses the application of the axiomatic approach to existing methods like TCAV and completeness-aware explanations, providing a theoretical grounding for these methods.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel axiomatic approach for concept explanations, which is model-agnostic and applicable across various tasks.
- The proposed method is efficient and easy to implement, making it accessible for practical applications.
- The paper provides a clear and concise explanation of the axioms and their implications, which are well-supported by theoretical proofs and experimental validations.
- The axiomatic approach allows for a deeper understanding of the semantic relationships between concepts and their impact on model predictions, which is crucial for model interpretability.
- The method is shown to be effective in scenarios such as model selection, optimizer selection, and model improvement, demonstrating its practical utility.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper's focus on binary classification might limit its applicability to more complex scenarios involving multiple classes.
- The paper's experimental setup is limited to specific datasets and models, which may not fully demonstrate the generalizability of the proposed method.
- The axioms and their implications are not sufficiently motivated or explained, which could hinder the understanding of the theoretical underpinnings of the method.
- The paper does not adequately address the issue of spurious correlations, which is a significant challenge in model interpretability.
- The paper's claims about model-agnosticness and the efficiency of the proposed method are not convincingly supported by the experimental results.
- The paper could benefit from a more detailed discussion on the limitations of the proposed method and how these might impact its practical application.
