 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel test-time adaptation (TTA) method, Reliable Fusion and Robust Adaptation (RFRA), designed to address the reliability bias in multi-modal scenarios. RFRA utilizes a self-adaptive attention module for reliable fusion across modalities and a confidence-aware loss function for robust adaptation. The method is evaluated on two benchmarks, Kinetics50-C and VGGSound-C, demonstrating its effectiveness in handling distribution shifts in multi-modal settings. The paper also discusses the challenges of reliability bias in TTA and provides insights into the dynamics of attention mechanisms in multi-modal models.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, easy to follow, and provides clear explanations of the proposed method and its components.
- The proposed method, RFRA, is novel and addresses a significant problem in multi-modal test-time adaptation, which is often overlooked in current TTA methods.
- The experiments are comprehensive, demonstrating the effectiveness of the proposed method across various settings and scenarios, including both audio-only and video-only cases.
- The paper introduces a new benchmark for multi-modal TTA under reliability bias, which is valuable for further research in this area.
- The theoretical analysis of the loss function and the attention mechanism provides a deeper understanding of the proposed method's operation.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed comparison with existing methods, particularly those that address similar issues in multi-modal TTA, such as MM-TTA.
- The experiments are limited to specific datasets (Kinetics and VGGSound) and do not include a broader range of corruptions or datasets to validate the generalizability of the proposed method.
- The paper does not sufficiently address the limitations of the proposed method, such as its applicability to different model architectures and the potential for overfitting to specific datasets.
- There is a lack of discussion on the limitations of existing TTA methods and how they might be adapted to handle reliability bias.
- The paper could benefit from a more detailed discussion on the dynamics of attention mechanisms in multi-modal models and how they relate to the proposed method.

**Questions:**
- How does the proposed method compare with MM-TTA, particularly in terms of the methodology and experimental results?
- Could the proposed method be adapted to handle different model architectures, such as those using different attention mechanisms or fusion strategies?
- How does the method perform when applied to different types of corruptions, such as those that affect both audio and video modalities simultaneously?
- Can the authors provide more details on the computational efficiency of the proposed method compared to existing TTA methods?
- How does the proposed method address the issue of overfitting, particularly in scenarios where the model is trained on a specific dataset?
- In the experiments, how does the proposed method compare in terms of computational efficiency and resource requirements with other TTA methods?
