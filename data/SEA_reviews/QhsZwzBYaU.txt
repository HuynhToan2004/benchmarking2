 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel method for data augmentation called Kernel Warping Mixup, which dynamically adjusts interpolation coefficients based on the similarity between data points. This method aims to improve model performance and calibration by ensuring that similar points are mixed more frequently, while less similar points are mixed less often. The authors demonstrate the effectiveness of their method through extensive experiments on both classification and regression tasks, showing improvements over existing methods like Mixup and C-Mixup. The paper also discusses the potential for extending this method to other data augmentation techniques and tasks.

**Strengths:**
- The paper presents a novel approach to data augmentation by adjusting interpolation coefficients based on the similarity between data points, which is a significant advancement over traditional methods.
- The experiments conducted are extensive, covering both classification and regression tasks, and demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed method in improving model performance and calibration.
- The paper is well-written, clear, and easy to follow, making it accessible to a broad audience.
- The proposed method is flexible and can be applied to different tasks by changing the similarity function, which enhances its applicability.
- The methodology is supported by a robust theoretical framework and empirical evidence, which validates its effectiveness.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed discussion on the computational overhead of the proposed method compared to existing methods like Mixup and C-Mixup, which could be a significant concern for practical applications.
- The paper does not adequately address the potential limitations of the proposed method, such as its applicability to other data augmentation techniques or tasks.
- The experimental results do not consistently show significant improvements over existing methods like MIT, and the paper does not provide a clear explanation for these discrepancies.
- The paper does not sufficiently compare the proposed method with other advanced data augmentation techniques like R-Mixup, which could have provided a more robust validation of its effectiveness.
- The paper does not include a discussion on the potential negative societal impacts of the proposed method, which is a critical omission for a paper in this field.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors provide a detailed comparison of the computational overhead of the proposed method compared to existing methods like Mixup and C-Mixup?
- How does the proposed method perform when combined with other data augmentation techniques like R-Mixup?
- Can the authors clarify the discrepancies in the experimental results, particularly the lack of significant improvement over MIT in certain scenarios?
- Could the authors discuss the potential limitations of the proposed method and suggest areas for future research?
- How does the proposed method address the issue of manifold intrusion in mixup, and what are the implications of this for model calibration?
- Can the authors provide a more detailed explanation of the experimental setup and results, particularly in the classification experiments, to help clarify the effectiveness of the proposed method?
