 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel diffusion-based method for solving inverse problems, focusing on the replacement of traditional measurement-based likelihood with a restoration-based likelihood. This approach leverages pre-trained restoration models to guide the diffusion process, aiming to enhance the model's ability to handle variational inverse problems. The paper presents a theoretical framework that extends the diffusion model's capabilities beyond deterministic processes, enabling it to handle more complex scenarios. Empirical results demonstrate the method's effectiveness in various applications, including image deblurring, rain streak removal, and dehazing. The approach is supported by a detailed mathematical formulation and experimental validation, though it faces criticism for its dependence on the quality of the restoration model and the complexity of the diffusion process.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel approach to solving inverse problems using diffusion models, which is a significant contribution to the field.
- The methodology is supported by a strong theoretical foundation, including detailed mathematical formulations that are well-explained.
- The paper is well-written, making it accessible and understandable to readers, which is crucial for disseminating the research findings.
- The empirical results are convincing, demonstrating the effectiveness of the proposed method in various applications, including image deblurring, rain streak removal, and dehazing.
- The paper is technically sound, with a clear explanation of the methodology and its application, which makes it easier for readers to understand and replicate the research.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed comparison with existing methods, particularly in terms of computational complexity and the impact of different restoration models on performance.
- There is a notable absence of discussion on the limitations of the proposed method, which is crucial for understanding its applicability and potential drawbacks.
- The paper could benefit from more extensive experimental validation, including additional datasets and baseline methods to provide a more comprehensive evaluation of the proposed method.
- The paper's reliance on the quality of the restoration model and the complexity of the diffusion process might limit its applicability in real-world scenarios where such models might not be available or practical.
- The paper's presentation could be improved by addressing minor issues such as typos and grammatical errors, which could detract from the overall quality of the manuscript.
