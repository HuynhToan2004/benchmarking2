 **Summary:**
The paper introduces STAC, a novel framework for modeling long-term dynamics in dynamical systems using a combination of spatial and temporal modules. The spatial module employs a Fourier neural operator to capture spectral information, while the temporal module utilizes a neural ODE to model continuous dynamics. A cache-based recurrent propagator is integrated to enhance long-term prediction capabilities. The framework is evaluated on 14 benchmarks, including a newly proposed fire dynamics benchmark, and is shown to outperform existing methods. However, the paper has been criticized for its complexity, lack of clarity in presentation, and insufficient discussion on related works. The methodology's novelty and the justification for its components are also questioned.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel benchmark for fire dynamics, which is a significant contribution to the field.
- The proposed method, STAC, demonstrates superior performance across multiple benchmarks, indicating its effectiveness.
- The integration of a frequency-enhanced spatial module and an ODE-enhanced temporal module provides a comprehensive approach to modeling long-term dynamics.
- The use of a cache-based recurrent propagator is innovative and enhances the model's ability to capture long-term dynamics.
- The paper is well-structured and includes detailed experimental results, which support the claims made about the method's effectiveness.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks clarity in its presentation, with complex figures and a dense text that makes it difficult to follow.
- The novelty of the proposed method is questioned, as it appears to be a combination of existing techniques without significant innovation.
- The paper does not adequately discuss the limitations of the method, which is crucial for understanding its applicability and scope.
- There is a lack of comparison with more recent and relevant baselines, which could have provided a more robust evaluation of the method.
- The method's complexity and the number of hyperparameters make it challenging to implement and tune, which could limit its practical application.
- The paper does not sufficiently discuss the rationale behind the design choices, particularly the use of the ODE-based temporal module and the frequency-enhanced spatial module.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors clarify the rationale behind the design choices, particularly the use of the ODE-based temporal module and the frequency-enhanced spatial module?
- How does the proposed method handle different types of dynamical systems, and what are the specific advantages it offers over existing methods?
- Can the authors provide a more detailed discussion on the limitations of the method and how these might affect its practical application?
- Why were certain baseline models not included in the comparisons, and how do the authors justify the choice of benchmarks used?
- Can the authors provide more detailed ablation studies to better understand the contributions of each component of the method?
- How does the proposed method handle the computational complexity associated with its design, and what strategies can be employed to mitigate this?
