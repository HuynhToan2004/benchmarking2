 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel adversarial defense mechanism called Targeted Manifold Manipulation (TMM), aimed at detecting adversarial attacks by manipulating the data manifold around genuine data. The TMM approach involves adding a trapdoor class, which can be detected when an adversarial attack enters the trapdoor region. This method is evaluated on four image datasets using six state-of-the-art adversarial attacks, showing promising results in detecting adversarial attacks with minimal impact on clean accuracy. The paper also discusses the practical implementation and computational efficiency of the TMM defense, comparing it with existing methods.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, clear, and easy to follow, making it accessible to a broad audience.
- The proposed method, Targeted Manifold Manipulation (TMM), is novel and offers a fresh perspective on adversarial defense, particularly in the context of trapdoors.
- The method is evaluated on four different datasets using six state-of-the-art adversarial attacks, providing a robust experimental validation.
- The TMM defense can effectively detect adversarial attacks with minimal impact on clean accuracy, which is crucial for practical applications.
- The paper includes comprehensive evaluations, including comparisons with other detection methods and baseline models, which helps in validating the effectiveness of the proposed approach.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a thorough discussion on the limitations of the proposed method, particularly in terms of its generalizability and applicability to different model architectures and datasets.
- The paper does not provide sufficient details on the computational complexity and memory requirements of the TMM defense, which could affect its practical implementation.
- The paper does not adequately address the issue of false positives, which is a critical concern in any adversarial defense system.
- The method's reliance on the robustness of the backbone model to adversarial attacks is not clearly addressed, which could limit its effectiveness in real-world scenarios.
- The paper could benefit from a more detailed explanation of the trapdoor generation process and the impact of different trigger patterns on the effectiveness of the defense.
- The experimental setup and results could be improved by including more diverse datasets and attack scenarios, such as adaptive attacks and transfer attacks.

**Questions:**
- How does the TMM defense perform against adaptive attacks, where the adversary is aware of the defense mechanism and tries to adapt the attack to bypass it?
- Could the TMM defense be adapted to work with different model architectures, such as ResNet, and how would this affect its performance?
- How does the TMM defense handle different trigger patterns, and what is the impact of these patterns on the effectiveness of the defense?
- Can the authors provide more details on the computational complexity and memory requirements of the TMM defense, particularly in comparison to other defense methods?
- How does the TMM defense handle false positives, and what measures are in place to minimize their occurrence?
- Could the authors include more diverse datasets and attack scenarios in future experiments to better evaluate the robustness and generalizability of the TMM defense?
