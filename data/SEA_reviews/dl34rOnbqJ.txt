 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel model for video action anticipation that integrates recurrent and attention mechanisms, leveraging inductive attention to enhance the model's ability to predict future actions based on historical video sequences. This model is designed to capture temporal information effectively and infers from historical data, setting new benchmarks in accuracy on extensive egocentric video datasets. The proposed inductive attention mechanism allows the model to handle longer contexts in videos, which is a significant improvement over existing methods. The paper presents extensive experiments on multiple datasets, including EPIC-Kitchens-100, EPIC-Kitchens-55, and EGTEA Gaze+, demonstrating the model's effectiveness and efficiency.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel inductive attention mechanism that leverages historical video sequences to predict future actions, which is a significant advancement in the field of video action anticipation.
- The model's architecture is well-articulated, with clear descriptions of the inductive attention mechanism and its integration into the model.
- The paper presents extensive experiments on multiple datasets, demonstrating the effectiveness of the proposed model in handling longer contexts in videos and its superior performance compared to existing methods.
- The paper is well-written, making it easy to follow, and the results are convincing, supported by extensive experiments and analysis.
- The model's design is innovative, utilizing prior anticipation results to refine subsequent action predictions, which significantly enhances the model's generalizability.
- The model's performance is competitive, achieving state-of-the-art results on multiple benchmarks, including EPIC-Kitchens-100, EPIC-Kitchens-55, and EGTEA Gaze+.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed discussion on the limitations and potential negative societal impacts of the proposed model, which could be a significant drawback for readers seeking a comprehensive understanding of the technology's implications.
- The paper does not provide sufficient ablation studies to demonstrate the impact of the proposed inductive attention mechanism, which could have strengthened the paper's argument for the model's effectiveness.
- The paper's experiments are limited to a few datasets, which may not fully demonstrate the model's generalizability across different types of video data.
- The paper could benefit from a more comprehensive analysis of the model's performance across different datasets and a deeper exploration of the model's limitations and potential improvements.
- The paper does not include a discussion on the limitations of the proposed model, which could provide valuable insights into the challenges and future directions for research in this area.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors provide more details on the limitations of the proposed model and discuss potential directions for future research?
- How does the model perform on different types of video data, such as non-egocentric videos or videos with different resolutions?
- Can the authors provide more ablation studies to demonstrate the impact of the proposed inductive attention mechanism on the model's performance?
- How does the model handle different types of actions, such as fine-grained actions, and what are the implications of this for the model's performance?
- Could the authors clarify the notations used in the paper, especially in the equations and the captions of figures, to improve the paper's readability and comprehensibility?
