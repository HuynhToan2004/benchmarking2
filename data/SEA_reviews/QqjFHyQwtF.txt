 **Summary:**
The paper introduces Uncertainty-Aware Dynamic Fusion (UADF), a novel late fusion method for Large Language Models (LLMs) in Automatic Speech Recognition (ASR). UADF dynamically adjusts the weighting of LLM and ASR model outputs based on the uncertainty of the LLM's predictions, aiming to improve performance over static fusion methods. The paper presents a comprehensive set of experiments, including ablation studies and comparisons with various fusion strategies, demonstrating the effectiveness of UADF in reducing word error rates (WER) and improving generalization capabilities. The methodology is particularly notable for its simplicity and the use of uncertainty-aware dynamic fusion, which allows for adaptive weighting of LLM and ASR model outputs.

**Strengths:**
- The paper presents a novel late fusion method, UADF, which dynamically adjusts the weighting of LLM and ASR model outputs based on the uncertainty of the LLM's predictions, enhancing the fusion of acoustic information into LLMs.
- The paper is well-written, making it easy to follow, and includes comprehensive ablation studies that demonstrate the effectiveness of each component of the proposed method.
- The experimental results show that the proposed method outperforms existing fusion mechanisms in terms of WER reduction and generalization capabilities.
- The paper addresses the challenge of integrating acoustic information into LLMs for GER-based speech recognition tasks, which is a significant and relevant research topic.
- The paper is well-motivated, well-written, and provides a comprehensive evaluation of the proposed method, comparing it with other fusion strategies and demonstrating its effectiveness in audio-visual speech recognition.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks clarity in some areas, particularly in the description of the ASR-only model and the role of the ASR model in the proposed method.
- The methodology and experimental setup could benefit from more detailed explanations, especially concerning the training and evaluation of the ASR model and the hyperparameter tuning.
- The paper does not sufficiently discuss the limitations of the proposed method, such as the potential impact of different data distributions on the performance of the ASR model.
- The paper could benefit from a more thorough discussion of the related work, particularly in the context of late fusion and uncertainty-aware dynamic fusion.
- The paper could be improved by providing more detailed comparisons with other methods, such as those mentioned in the literature, and by including more diverse datasets in the experiments.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors clarify the role and functionality of the ASR-only model in the context of the proposed method?
- How were the hyperparameters tuned for the UADF method, and what impact did this have on the performance?
- Could the authors provide more details on the training and evaluation of the ASR model, particularly in terms of the data used and the decoding settings?
- How does the proposed method handle different data distributions, and what are the potential implications for the performance of the ASR model?
- Could the authors expand on the potential applications of the proposed method beyond ASR, and discuss how it might be adapted for other tasks?
- How does the proposed method compare with other late fusion methods, and what are the key advantages of using UADF over these alternatives?
