 **Summary:**
The paper introduces two novel deep anomaly detection methods, DOHSC and DO2HSC, aimed at addressing the challenges of hypersphere learning in high-dimensional spaces. These methods leverage an orthogonal projection layer to ensure the training data distribution aligns with the hypersphere hypothesis, thereby enhancing the decision-making process. The authors also propose a bi-hypersphere compression method to construct a more compact decision region. The paper is evaluated on various datasets, including image, tabular, and graph data, and demonstrates superior performance compared to state-of-the-art methods. The theoretical analysis supports the proposed methods, and the paper includes extensive experimental validations and visualizations to substantiate the claims.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces two novel methods, DOHSC and DO2HSC, which address the challenges of hypersphere learning in high-dimensional spaces, improving the decision-making process and reducing false negatives.
- The proposed methods are theoretically grounded and demonstrate effectiveness across various datasets, including image, tabular, and graph data.
- The paper provides extensive experimental validations and visualizations, enhancing the understanding and applicability of the proposed methods.
- The authors have provided a detailed theoretical analysis and empirical validation, supporting the effectiveness of the proposed methods.
- The paper is well-organized and easy to follow, making it accessible to readers and potentially useful for practitioners in the field of anomaly detection.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper does not sufficiently compare the proposed methods with state-of-the-art anomaly detection methods, such as those mentioned in recent reviews, which could provide a more comprehensive evaluation of the proposed methods.
- The methodology and its implementation details are not clearly explained, particularly the use of orthogonal projection layers and the decision-making process based on different scores.
- The paper does not discuss the computational complexity or scalability of the proposed methods, which could limit their practical application in real-world scenarios with large datasets.
- The paper does not address the potential limitations or negative societal impacts of the proposed methods, which could be critical for understanding the broader implications of the research.
- There are several typographical and grammatical errors throughout the paper, which detract from the professional quality of the manuscript.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors provide a more detailed comparison with state-of-the-art anomaly detection methods, particularly those mentioned in recent reviews?
- How do the proposed methods compare in terms of computational complexity and scalability with other methods in the field?
- Can the authors clarify the implementation details and the rationale behind the use of orthogonal projection layers in their methods?
- How do the proposed methods handle different types of data, such as image, tabular, and graph data, and what are the specific challenges and solutions in each case?
- Could the authors discuss the potential limitations or negative societal impacts of their proposed methods, and how these might be mitigated or addressed?
