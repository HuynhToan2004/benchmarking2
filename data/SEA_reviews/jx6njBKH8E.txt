 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel method for enhancing the effectiveness of training data extraction (TDE) attacks by fine-tuning large language models (LLMs) with pseudo-labeled memberships. The authors leverage the DetectGPT model to assign pseudo-labels to generated texts, which are then used to fine-tune the LLM using reinforcement learning from human feedback (RLHF). This approach aims to amplify the exposure of training data, potentially increasing the vulnerability of LLMs to TDE attacks. The paper presents a comprehensive evaluation across various model scales, showing promising results in increasing the amount of training data exposed. However, concerns were raised about the novelty and practical applicability of the method, particularly in scenarios where the attacker does not have direct access to the LLM's parameters.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel method for enhancing the effectiveness of training data extraction (TDE) attacks by fine-tuning large language models (LLMs) with pseudo-labeled memberships.
- The proposed method is well-explained, and the experimental results are convincing, showing a significant increase in the amount of training data exposed when fine-tuning LLMs.
- The paper provides a comprehensive evaluation across various model scales, which helps in understanding the impact of model size on the effectiveness of the proposed method.
- The authors have made significant efforts to ensure the reproducibility of their work, including providing detailed experimental setups and code availability.
- The paper is well-written, making it easy to follow, and the motivation behind the research is clearly articulated.

**Weaknesses:**
- The novelty of the method is limited as it primarily involves the use of DetectGPT to assign pseudo-labels to generated texts, which is a known technique.
- The paper lacks a detailed discussion on the practical applicability of the proposed method, particularly in scenarios where the attacker does not have direct access to the LLM's parameters.
- There is a significant reliance on the accuracy of the DetectGPT model, which could introduce errors in the pseudo-labeling process.
- The paper does not adequately address the potential limitations of the proposed method, such as the impact of fine-tuning on the generalized performance of the LLM and the scalability of the method to larger or more complex datasets.
- The paper could benefit from a more thorough comparison with existing TDE attack methods, particularly in terms of the effectiveness and efficiency of the proposed method.

**Questions:**
- How does the proposed method compare with other TDE attack methods in terms of effectiveness and efficiency?
- Can the authors provide more details on the practical applicability of the proposed method, particularly in scenarios where the attacker does not have direct access to the LLM's parameters?
- How does the accuracy of the DetectGPT model affect the effectiveness of the pseudo-labeling process?
- What are the potential mitigations against the proposed attack method, and how effective are these mitigations in practice?
- Can the authors clarify the discrepancy between the results presented in the paper and those reported in previous studies, particularly in terms of the effectiveness of the proposed method?
