 **Summary:**
The paper investigates the impact of data accumulation on machine learning models, specifically focusing on how adding more data from different sources can affect model performance. The authors present two scenarios: Mixture and Sequential, which model the acquisition of data from multiple sources. The experiments conducted on three datasets show that while increasing data size can improve model performance in some cases, it can also lead to decreased accuracy in others, particularly when the data sources are significantly different. The paper proposes a heuristic based on KL divergence to determine when adding more data might be detrimental. However, the paper's methodology and experimental setup have been critiqued for lacking clarity, depth, and a strong theoretical foundation.

**Strengths:**
- The paper addresses an important and relevant question regarding the impact of data composition on machine learning model performance, which is a critical aspect of data-driven decision-making.
- The experimental results are well-presented, and the paper is generally easy to follow, making it accessible to a broad audience.
- The authors have conducted experiments on real-world datasets, which adds credibility to their findings.
- The paper is well-written, with clear explanations of the problem, methodology, and results, which are supported by figures and tables.
- The authors provide a heuristic for determining when to add more data, which could be beneficial for practitioners in real-world applications.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a clear and concise presentation of the methodology, making it difficult to follow and understand the contributions fully.
- The theoretical framework is weak, with unclear connections between the theoretical models and the empirical results.
- The paper does not adequately address the limitations of the proposed methodologies, nor does it provide a detailed discussion on how these limitations might affect the practical applicability of the findings.
- The experiments are limited to tabular data and do not include more complex datasets such as image or text data, which could provide a more comprehensive understanding of the effects of data composition on model performance.
- The paper does not sufficiently explore the impact of data composition on model performance across different data modalities, which could limit the generalizability of the findings.
- The paper could benefit from a more rigorous theoretical analysis and a deeper exploration of the theoretical underpinnings of data composition and its effects on model performance.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors clarify the connection between the theoretical models and the empirical results, especially how the findings from the Mixture case relate to the theoretical framework?
- How does the proposed heuristic based on KL divergence compare to other methods for determining when to add more data, and what are the advantages or disadvantages of this approach?
- Could the authors provide more detailed examples or case studies to illustrate the practical implications of their findings, particularly in real-world scenarios where data composition is a critical concern?
- How does the paper address the potential limitations of the proposed methodologies, and what steps have been taken to ensure the robustness and generalizability of the findings?
- Could the authors consider expanding the experimental setup to include more complex datasets and different data modalities to provide a more comprehensive understanding of the effects of data composition on model performance?
