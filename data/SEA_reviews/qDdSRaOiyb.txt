 **Summary:**
The paper introduces ContraLSP, a method for explaining multivariate time series data using counterfactual samples and sparse masks. This approach employs contrastive learning to ensure perturbations are uninformative and in-distribution, while sparse gates are used to generate masks that align with latent time series patterns. The methodology is evaluated on both synthetic and real-world datasets, demonstrating its effectiveness in explaining time series models. The paper also discusses the challenges of existing methods and provides a detailed literature review, highlighting the need for more robust explanations in time series data.

**Strengths:**
- The paper addresses a significant and relevant issue in the field of time series data, focusing on the explanation of multivariate time series, which is crucial for understanding complex temporal patterns.
- The proposed method is well-articulated, with a clear explanation of the problem formulation, methodology, and experimental setup.
- The paper includes a comprehensive literature review, which effectively positions the work within the existing body of research.
- The use of counterfactual samples and sparse gates to generate uninformative perturbations and align with latent time series patterns is innovative and could be beneficial for explaining time series models.
- The paper is well-written, making it easy to follow and understand, which is essential for readers to engage with the content effectively.
- The experiments conducted on both synthetic and real-world datasets demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed method, providing empirical evidence of its utility.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a clear definition and explanation of the "counterfactual perturbations" used in the methodology, which could lead to confusion about their role and impact in the explanation process.
- There is a lack of discussion on the limitations and potential drawbacks of the proposed method, which is crucial for understanding its applicability and scope.
- The paper does not provide sufficient details on the computational complexity and efficiency of the method, which could be a significant concern for practical implementation.
- The paper could benefit from a more thorough comparison with other state-of-the-art methods, especially those specifically designed for time series data, to establish a clearer competitive advantage.
- The paper's use of metrics for evaluating the effectiveness of the method could be more clearly defined and justified, particularly in terms of their relevance and reliability in assessing the method's performance.
- The paper's focus on a specific type of time series data (multivariate) might limit its applicability to other types of time series data, such as univariate or categorical time series, which could affect its broader utility.

**Questions:**

- Can you provide more details on the computational complexity and efficiency of the method, particularly in terms of training and inference times?
- How does the method perform when applied to different types of time series data, such as univariate or categorical time series?
- Could you discuss the potential limitations and drawbacks of the proposed method, and how these might impact its practical applicability?
