 **Summary:**
The paper introduces MIntRec2.0, a large-scale benchmark dataset for multimodal intent recognition in multi-party conversations. This dataset includes 1,245 dialogues with 15,040 samples, each annotated with 30 fine-grained intent classes across text, video, and audio modalities. The dataset is designed to address the limitations of existing benchmarks by including a broader range of intent classes and a larger number of samples. It also includes out-of-scope samples, which are crucial for evaluating the robustness of models in real-world scenarios. The paper proposes a general framework for multimodal intent recognition and out-of-scope detection, and conducts experiments to demonstrate the effectiveness of multimodal information in intent recognition.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces MIntRec2.0, a large-scale benchmark dataset for multimodal intent recognition in multi-party conversations, which is a pioneering foundation for research in human-machine conversational interactions.
- The dataset is the first large-scale multimodal multi-party conversational intent dataset, providing detailed annotations for both intent and speaker identity for each utterance within multimodal contexts and enabling out-of-scope detection in open-world scenarios.
- The dataset is comprehensive, covering a wide range of intent classes and providing a large number of samples, which is crucial for training and testing models in multimodal intent recognition.
- The paper establishes a universal framework for in-scope classification and out-of-scope detection, applicable to both single-turn and multi-turn conversations, and introduces strong benchmark baselines.
- The paper is well-organized and easy to follow, making it accessible to a broad audience.
- The dataset and code will be released, which will benefit the research community.

**Weaknesses:**
