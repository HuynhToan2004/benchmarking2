 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel method for handling missing values in multivariate time series data using a combination of Radial Basis Function Neural Network (RBFNN) and Recurrent Neural Network (RNN). The proposed method, named MIM-RBFNN and MIRNN-CF, leverages RBFNN to model local covariance structures and RNN to capture temporal dependencies. The method was evaluated on two real-world datasets, demonstrating improved performance over existing baselines. However, concerns were raised about the novelty of the approach, as similar methods have been previously explored. The paper also lacks a detailed comparison with state-of-the-art methods and does not sufficiently address the generalizability of the findings.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel approach by combining RBFNN and RNN to handle missing values in multivariate time series data, which is a significant contribution to the field.
- The proposed method demonstrates improved performance over existing baselines, as evidenced by the experimental results.
- The paper is well-organized and easy to follow, making it accessible to a broad audience.
- The methodology is well-motivated, with a clear description of the proposed method and its advantages over existing methods.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed comparison with state-of-the-art methods, which could have provided a better understanding of the method's performance relative to the current best practices.
- The experimental results are not sufficiently robust, with only two datasets used, and the results are not convincingly superior to existing methods.
- The novelty of the approach is questionable as similar methods combining RBFNN and RNN have been previously explored.
- The paper does not adequately address the generalizability of the findings, which is a critical issue in machine learning research.
- The presentation of the paper could be improved, particularly in terms of clarity and organization, which could enhance its readability and accessibility.
- The paper does not sufficiently address the limitations of the proposed method, which could help in understanding its applicability and scope.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors provide a more detailed comparison with state-of-the-art methods to better understand the method's performance relative to current best practices?
- How does the proposed method compare to other deep learning models for time series data imputation?
- Could the authors provide a more detailed description of the datasets used, including their characteristics and how they were selected?
- How does the proposed method handle different types and patterns of missing data, such as missing at random (MAR) and missing completely at random (MCAR)?
- What are the specific advantages of using RBFNN and RNN over other machine learning models for handling missing values in time series data?
- Can the authors provide a more detailed explanation of the methodology, particularly the use of RBFNN and RNN, to help readers better understand the proposed approach?
