 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel adversarial purification method named ZeroPur, which aims to remove adversarial perturbations from attacked images without the need for a generative model or retraining. The method leverages a two-stage approach: coarse shifting and fine alignment, utilizing a blurring operator to restore the embedding vector of adversarial examples to a natural image manifold. This method is tested on various datasets including CIFAR-10, CIFAR-100, and ImageNet, demonstrating its effectiveness against various attacks. Despite its innovative approach, concerns about the method's effectiveness against certain types of attacks and its practicality in real-world scenarios remain.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel method for adversarial purification that does not require a generative model or retraining, which is a significant departure from existing methods.
- The method is supported by extensive experiments on three datasets and various classifier architectures, demonstrating its robustness and generalizability.
- The paper is well-written, clear, and easy to follow, with a comprehensive literature review that effectively positions the proposed method within the current research landscape.
- The method's simplicity and the absence of the need for additional knowledge or models make it potentially more accessible and practical for real-world applications.
- The paper presents a new hypothesis about the natural image manifold, which is both intriguing and relevant to the field of adversarial purification.

**Weaknesses:**
- The method's effectiveness is not convincingly demonstrated against all types of attacks, particularly strong adaptive attacks such as BPDA+EOT.
- There is a lack of clarity regarding the choice of the blurring operator and its impact on the method's performance. The paper does not provide a detailed ablation study on different types of blurring operators.
- The paper's claims about the method's effectiveness in real-world scenarios are not substantiated with practical examples or empirical evidence.
- The paper could benefit from a more rigorous comparison with existing methods, particularly in terms of computational efficiency and practical applicability.
- There is a concern about the method's ability to handle adversarial examples that are not initially beyond the natural image manifold, which could limit its practical utility.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors provide more details on the choice of the blurring operator and its impact on the method's performance?
- How does the method perform against different types of adversarial attacks, particularly strong adaptive attacks?
- Could the authors clarify the hypothesis about the natural image manifold and its implications for the method's effectiveness?
- How does the method compare in terms of computational efficiency and practical applicability with existing methods?
- Can the authors provide more empirical evidence or practical examples to support the method's effectiveness in real-world scenarios?
