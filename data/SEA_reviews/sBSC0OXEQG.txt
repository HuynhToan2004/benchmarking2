 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel associative memory model named Correlated Dense Associative Memory (CDAM), which integrates both auto- and hetero-association in a unified framework. The model employs an arbitrary graph structure to semantically link memory patterns, enabling the extraction of multiscale representations of community structures in graphs and stabilizing the recall of temporal sequences. CDAM's theoretical and numerical analysis reveals four distinct dynamical modes: auto-association, narrow hetero-association, wide hetero-association, and neutral quiescence. The model's effectiveness is demonstrated through experiments on real video data and a classical neuroscience experiment, showcasing its potential for handling real-world data and replicating experimental results.
