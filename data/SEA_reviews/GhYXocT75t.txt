 **Summary:**
The paper introduces FOBAR, a method that combines forward and backward reasoning to improve the accuracy of mathematical reasoning in large language models (LLMs). FOBAR leverages a novel approach by masking a number in the question and asking the LLM to predict its value given a candidate answer. This method is designed to enhance the verification process by integrating backward reasoning with forward reasoning, which is shown to outperform existing methods like Self-Consistency and Self-Verification. The paper includes comprehensive experiments across various mathematical datasets and LLMs, demonstrating the effectiveness of FOBAR in improving mathematical reasoning accuracy.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, easy to follow, and presents clear, well-organized content.
- The proposed FOBAR method is simple yet effective, combining forward and backward reasoning to improve mathematical reasoning in LLMs.
- Extensive experiments are conducted on six mathematical datasets and three LLMs, demonstrating the effectiveness of FOBAR in enhancing mathematical reasoning accuracy.
- The paper provides a detailed analysis of FOBAR's performance across different datasets and LLMs, including ablation studies that validate the effectiveness of combining forward and backward reasoning.
- The paper is well-motivated, with a clear focus on improving mathematical reasoning in LLMs, and the proposed FOBAR method is well-explained and easy to understand.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed discussion on the limitations of FOBAR, particularly how it might perform in scenarios with complex mathematical questions or in domains outside of mathematics.
- There is a need for more comprehensive comparisons with other LLM-based mathematical reasoning methods, such as those using LLMs as a solver or LLMs for generating reasoning chains.
- The paper does not provide sufficient analysis on the computational cost of FOBAR compared to other methods, which is crucial for understanding its practical applicability.
- The paper could benefit from a more detailed discussion on the impact of different hyperparameters on FOBAR's performance and a more rigorous evaluation of its generalization ability across different mathematical domains.
- The novelty of the FOBAR method is questioned, as similar methods have been previously proposed, such as Self-Verification, which also uses a similar approach to verify answers.

**Questions:**
- How does FOBAR perform on more complex mathematical questions, and what are the potential limitations of the method in such scenarios?
- Could FOBAR be adapted for use in domains outside of mathematics, such as natural language inference or other reasoning tasks?
- How does FOBAR compare to other LLM-based mathematical reasoning methods, particularly in terms of computational efficiency and generalization ability?
- Can the authors provide more insights into the impact of different hyperparameters on FOBAR's performance and discuss the potential for tuning these parameters to optimize performance?
- In light of the similarities to Self-Verification, how does FOBAR differentiate itself and offer a unique advantage in mathematical reasoning?
