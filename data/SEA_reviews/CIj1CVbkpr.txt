 **Summary:**
The paper introduces two novel online training techniques for spiking neural networks (SNNs) to address the challenges of memory efficiency and biological plausibility. It proposes the Online Spiking Renormalization (OSR) method to ensure consistent parameters between testing and training, and the Online Threshold Stabilizer (OTS) to stabilize neuron firing rates across time steps. The OSR method computes variable mean and variance over time, while the OTS method adjusts the threshold to maintain stable firing rates. These methods are evaluated against existing algorithms and show superior performance in terms of accuracy and memory efficiency. However, the paper lacks detailed comparisons with other online training methods and does not thoroughly discuss the biological plausibility of the proposed methods.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces two novel online training techniques for spiking neural networks (SNNs): Online Spiking Renormalization (OSR) and Online Threshold Stabilizer (OTS).
- The proposed methods are biologically plausible, which is a significant strength in the field of SNNs.
- The paper is well-written and easy to follow, making it accessible to a broad audience.
- The experimental results demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed methods, showing improvements in accuracy and memory efficiency.
- The integration of batch normalization into the online training process is innovative and addresses a significant challenge in SNN training.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed comparison with other online training methods, which could provide a more comprehensive understanding of the proposed methods' advantages and limitations.
- The biological plausibility of the proposed methods is not thoroughly discussed, leaving questions about their adherence to biological principles.
- The paper does not sufficiently address the limitations of the proposed methods, such as their applicability to different types of neuron models and the potential for overfitting due to the absence of batch normalization.
- There are concerns about the practicality of the methods in real-world applications, particularly in scenarios where memory and computational efficiency are critical.
- The paper does not provide a detailed discussion on the limitations of the proposed methods, which could hinder the understanding of their applicability and effectiveness.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors provide a more detailed comparison with other online training methods, including the specific advantages and disadvantages of their proposed methods?
- How do the proposed methods compare in terms of biological plausibility with other existing methods?
- What are the specific advantages of using OSR and OTS over other existing methods, such as tdBN and TEBN?
- Can the authors discuss the limitations of their methods and how they might be addressed in future research?
- How do the proposed methods perform in terms of computational efficiency, especially in scenarios where memory and computational resources are limited?
- Can the authors provide more details on the biological plausibility of their methods, particularly in relation to how they mimic the behavior of biological neurons?
