 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel entropic risk measure to quantify the robustness of counterfactual explanations against model changes. It proposes a convex risk measure that balances the cost of counterfactuals and their robustness, connecting worst-case robust and robustness-constrained counterfactuals. The authors provide a constrained optimization algorithm with probabilistic guarantees, demonstrating its effectiveness on several datasets. The paper also discusses the integration of this measure with min-max optimization, aiming to enhance the robustness of counterfactual explanations. Despite the theoretical advancements, the paper's experimental results are somewhat limited, focusing on a few datasets and not extensively comparing with existing methods.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel entropic risk measure for quantifying the robustness of counterfactuals, which is a significant contribution to the field.
- The proposed method is theoretically grounded and provides a unifying connection between existing approaches for worst-case robust and robustness-constrained counterfactuals.
- The paper is well-written, clear, and easy to follow, making it accessible to a broad audience.
- The methodology is innovative, combining worst-case robustness and robustness-constrained approaches, which is a first in the literature.
- The paper addresses a significant and relevant problem in the field of counterfactual explanations, which is crucial for ensuring trustworthy AI systems.
- The theoretical results are robust, providing a solid foundation for the proposed method.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a thorough comparison with existing methods, particularly in terms of experimental results. It only includes results for a few datasets and does not provide a comprehensive comparison with state-of-the-art methods.
- The assumption of Lipschitz continuity for the model might not hold for complex models like deep neural networks, which could limit the applicability of the proposed method.
- The paper does not provide a detailed discussion on the computational complexity of the proposed method, which is a critical aspect for practical implementation.
- There is a lack of discussion on the limitations of the proposed method, particularly in terms of its applicability to different model types and its sensitivity to hyperparameter choices.
- The paper does not sufficiently address the potential societal impacts of the proposed method, which could be a significant concern in practical applications.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors provide more details on how the proposed method performs compared to other state-of-the-art methods, especially in terms of experimental results?
- How does the proposed method handle the trade-off between cost and robustness in practical scenarios, and what are the implications of this trade-off for the effectiveness of the counterfactual explanations?
- Could the authors clarify the role and impact of the hyperparameter γ on the performance of the method?
- How does the method handle different types of model changes, and what are the implications of these changes on the robustness of the counterfactual explanations?
- Can the authors discuss the potential societal impacts of the proposed method, particularly in terms of its application in high-stakes decision-making scenarios?
