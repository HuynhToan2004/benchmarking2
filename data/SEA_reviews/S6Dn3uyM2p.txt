 **Summary:**
The paper introduces differentially private variants of the MinHash algorithm, specifically focusing on the One Permutation Hashing (OPH) framework for Jaccard similarity. The authors propose three methods: DP-OPH-fix, DP-OPH-re, and DP-OPH-rand, each utilizing different densification strategies to achieve differential privacy. The paper includes theoretical analysis and empirical evaluations, comparing the proposed methods against existing DP-MinHash. The experiments demonstrate that DP-OPH-re outperforms DP-MH in terms of privacy and utility. However, concerns were raised about the practical applicability of the results due to the small dataset sizes used in the experiments and the limited discussion on the real-world applicability of the proposed methods.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-organized, making it easy to follow, and the algorithms are clearly presented.
- The proposed methods are evaluated against a relevant baseline, providing a clear comparison.
- The paper addresses an important problem in the intersection of hashing and differential privacy, which is not well-studied.
- The paper provides theoretical analysis, which is complemented by empirical evaluations.
- The experimental results show that the proposed methods perform well, particularly in terms of precision and recall.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed discussion on the real-world applicability of the proposed methods, particularly in scenarios where privacy is a concern.
- The datasets used in the experiments are relatively small, which might not adequately demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed methods in larger, more realistic settings.
- The paper does not provide a detailed discussion on the limitations of the proposed methods, which is crucial for understanding their applicability and effectiveness.
- There is a lack of discussion on the computational overhead of the proposed methods, which could be a significant concern in practical implementations.
- The paper could benefit from a more detailed comparison with other related works, particularly those that address similar issues in the intersection of hashing and differential privacy.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors provide more detailed examples of real-world applications where the proposed methods could be useful?
- How do the computational costs of the proposed methods compare to those of other state-of-the-art methods in terms of both time and space complexity?
- Could the authors clarify the definition of "neighboring" data vectors and how they relate to the differential privacy guarantees provided by the proposed methods?
- In the experimental results, why does DP-OPH-rand perform better than DP-OPH-re for small values of ε?
- How do the authors address the potential negative societal impacts of their work, particularly in terms of privacy and data security?
