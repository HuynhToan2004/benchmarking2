 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a method for extending optimal transport (OT) methods to unbalanced settings, which is crucial for handling class imbalances in real-world applications. It proposes a framework that integrates unbalancedness into Monge map estimators, demonstrating its effectiveness in various scenarios such as single-cell trajectory inference and unpaired image translation. The approach, which includes an entropy-regularized unbalanced Kantorovich problem, is theoretically grounded and empirically validated across multiple tasks. The method's novelty lies in its ability to handle unbalanced distributions, which is a significant challenge in traditional OT methods. The paper also discusses the limitations and potential future directions, including the choice of cost functions and the generalization of the method to other neural Monge map estimators.

**Strengths:**
- The paper addresses a significant problem in the field of optimal transport (OT) by focusing on unbalanced settings, which are crucial for handling class imbalances in real-world applications.
- The authors provide a clear and detailed theoretical explanation of the method, supported by a comprehensive literature review and comparisons with related works.
- The paper includes empirical validations of the method across multiple tasks, demonstrating its effectiveness and versatility.
- The approach is well-written, easy to follow, and includes a detailed discussion on the limitations and potential future directions of the research.
- The methodology is innovative, as it proposes a novel way to incorporate unbalancedness into any Monge map estimator, which is a significant advancement in the field.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a clear motivation for why unbalancedness is necessary in the context of Monge maps, and the benefits of this approach are not convincingly demonstrated across all tasks.
- The choice of cost functions (e.g., Euclidean distance) and their impact on the performance of the method are not thoroughly discussed, which could limit the generalizability of the findings.
- The paper does not include a comprehensive discussion on the computational complexity and scalability of the proposed method, which could be a significant concern for larger datasets or more complex models.
- The paper could benefit from a more detailed comparison with other existing methods that address unbalancedness in OT, particularly in terms of their theoretical contributions and practical applications.
- The method's reliance on specific neural Monge map estimators (e.g., OT-ICNN, OT-MG, and OT-FM) may limit its applicability to other estimators, which could be a significant drawback for broader adoption.
