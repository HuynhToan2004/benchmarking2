 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel conditional diffusion model, CDGraph, designed for generating social graphs that satisfy specific conditions such as users' membership and financial status. CDGraph employs a co-evolution dependency to capture mutual dependencies between dual conditions and integrates social homophily and social contagion to preserve connectivity and satisfy conditions. The model is evaluated against several baselines, showing improved performance in terms of dual-conditional validity and reduced discrepancy in various network metrics. However, the paper has been critiqued for its lack of clarity in technical details, especially the definitions and roles of social homophily and social contagion. Additionally, the method's applicability to real-world datasets and its scalability to larger graphs are questioned.

**Strengths:**
- The paper addresses the generation of social graphs with dual conditions, which is a significant and relevant problem in the field.
- The proposed method, CDGraph, is novel and technically sound, introducing a novel co-evolution dependency to capture mutual dependencies between dual conditions.
- The paper is well-written and easy to follow, with clear explanations of the methodology and results.
- The experiments demonstrate that CDGraph outperforms several baselines in terms of dual-conditional validity and reduced discrepancy in various network metrics.
- The paper introduces a dual-condition classifier to guide the denoising process, ensuring that the discrepancy in the diffusion process and condition fulfillment can be jointly optimized.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks clarity in technical details, particularly the definitions and roles of social homophily and social contagion, and the relationship between these concepts and the proposed method.
- There is a lack of discussion on the limitations of the proposed method, including its scalability to larger graphs and its applicability to real-world datasets.
- The paper could benefit from a more detailed explanation of the co-evolution dependency and the dual-condition classifier, as well as the rationale behind the choice of datasets and evaluation metrics.
- The paper does not provide sufficient evidence to support the claim that the proposed method is superior to existing baselines, and the experimental results are not convincingly superior.
- There are issues with the presentation and organization of the paper, including unclear figures and a lack of detailed explanation of the methodology in some sections.

**Questions:**
- Could you clarify the definitions and roles of social homophily and social contagion in the context of the proposed method?
- How does the co-evolution dependency capture mutual dependencies between dual conditions, and how does the dual-condition classifier guide the denoising process?
- How does the proposed method compare to other diffusion models in terms of performance and efficiency?
- Can you provide more details on the datasets used, including their characteristics and the reasons for their selection?
- How does the proposed method handle different types of conditions, and what are the implications for the model's performance and applicability?
- Could you elaborate on the limitations of the proposed method and suggest potential directions for future research?
