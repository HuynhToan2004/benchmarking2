 **Summary:**
The paper presents a novel application of multi-agent reinforcement learning (MARL) to address the HIV epidemic, focusing on the jurisdictional interactions in the United States. It proposes a model where jurisdictions are treated as agents, each with distinct objectives, and uses a PPO-based algorithm to optimize interventions across these jurisdictions. The model is evaluated against single-agent RL baselines, showing that MARL can lead to better outcomes. The paper is commended for its innovative approach and its potential to inform public health policies. However, it is criticized for its limited evaluation scope, lack of novelty, and insufficient comparative analysis with other multi-agent RL methods.
