 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel robust fine-tuning algorithm, Lipsum-FT, designed to enhance the zero-shot ability of pre-trained vision-language models like CLIP. This method is inspired by the joint energy-based model and aims to minimize the energy gap between the fine-tuned model and the zero-shot model. The algorithm utilizes random text guidance to align the vision model with the language model, which is hypothesized to reduce the energy gap and improve robustness. The paper presents extensive experiments on DomainNet and ImageNet, demonstrating that Lipsum-FT outperforms existing robust fine-tuning methods in handling distribution shifts. However, the paper has been critiqued for its limited novelty, as it primarily combines existing techniques without significant innovation, and for its experimental setup, which may not fully support the claims made.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, clear, and easy to follow, making complex concepts accessible to a broad audience.
- The introduction of Lipsum-FT is novel and addresses the challenge of robust fine-tuning in vision-language models.
- Extensive experiments conducted on DomainNet and ImageNet demonstrate the effectiveness of Lipsum-FT in handling distribution shifts.
- The paper provides a comprehensive analysis of the impact of fine-tuning on the robustness of pre-trained models, which is crucial for understanding the behavior of models under different conditions.
- The use of joint energy-based models to explain the robustness of vision-language models is a significant contribution to the field.

**Weaknesses:**
- The novelty of the proposed method is limited as it primarily combines existing techniques (joint energy-based model and random text guidance) without significant innovation.
- The paper lacks a detailed discussion on the limitations of the proposed method, particularly concerning the scalability and generalizability of the findings.
- There is a significant gap in performance between the proposed method and strong baselines like WiSE, which is not adequately explained.
- The experimental setup and results are not convincingly presented, with inconsistencies in the reported results and a lack of detailed comparisons with other methods.
- The paper could benefit from a more rigorous evaluation of the proposed method, including additional datasets and a more thorough analysis of the results.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors clarify the discrepancies in the reported results, especially in Table 1 and Figure 1, and provide a more detailed explanation of the experimental setup and results?
- How does the proposed method compare to other robust fine-tuning methods, especially in terms of scalability and performance on different datasets?
- Could the authors provide a more detailed discussion on the limitations of the proposed method and how these might affect its practical application?
- Is there a specific reason for the observed gap in performance between Lipsum-FT and WiSE, and how does this impact the perceived effectiveness of the proposed method?
- Can the authors elaborate on the choice of using random text guidance and its impact on the performance of the model?
