 **Summary:**
The paper introduces Latent Diffusion Counterfactual Explanations (LDCE), a novel method that leverages latent diffusion models to generate counterfactual explanations. LDCE operates by adding noise to the original image and then guiding the reverse diffusion process to generate a counterfactual image that elicits a desired output from a target model while staying close to the original image. The method includes a consensus guidance mechanism that uses the implicit classifier of a class- or text-conditional diffusion model to ensure semantic meaningfulness in the counterfactual explanations. The paper claims that LDCE can be applied to any classifier, regardless of the learning paradigm, and demonstrates its effectiveness through experiments on various datasets. However, concerns are raised about the method's practicality due to its slow generation speed and the need for hyperparameter optimization.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, clear, and easy to follow, with a clear introduction of the problem and a comprehensive background section.
- The proposed method is novel and innovative, utilizing a consensus guidance mechanism and leveraging the implicit classifier of a class- or text-conditional diffusion model to ensure semantic meaningfulness in the counterfactual explanations.
- The method is demonstrated to be effective and versatile, applicable to any classifier and capable of generating high-quality visual counterfactual explanations.
- The paper includes comprehensive experiments on various datasets and models, demonstrating the effectiveness of the proposed method.
- The paper is well-organized and includes a detailed appendix, making it easy for readers to understand the method and its implications.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed discussion on the limitations of the proposed method, such as its slow generation speed and the need for hyperparameter optimization.
- The novelty of the consensus guidance mechanism is questionable, as similar mechanisms have been previously proposed.
- The paper does not compare the proposed method with other existing counterfactual explanation methods, which could provide a more robust evaluation of its effectiveness.
- The paper does not discuss the potential negative societal impacts of the proposed method, such as its potential for misuse or manipulation.
- There are some technical errors and inconsistencies in the paper, such as missing references and unclear equations.

**Questions:**
- How does the proposed method compare with other existing counterfactual explanation methods, especially in terms of effectiveness and efficiency?
- Can the authors provide more details on the consensus guidance mechanism, including how it differs from existing methods and how it ensures semantic meaningfulness in the counterfactual explanations?
- How does the paper address the potential negative societal impacts of the proposed method?
- Can the authors clarify the technical details of the method, especially in the equations and the implementation of the consensus guidance mechanism?
- How does the proposed method handle different types of data, such as tabular data or non-image data?
- Can the authors provide more examples of counterfactual explanations that are generated by the method, including both successful and unsuccessful examples?
