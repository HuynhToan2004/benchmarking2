 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel black-box model inversion attack named Targeted Model Inversion (TMI), which utilizes a StyleGAN model to generate a mapping network that transforms prediction vectors into StyleGAN latent representations. This approach allows for the generation of high-quality images that closely match the original input images, thereby reducing the number of necessary queries to the target model. The TMI method is evaluated against state-of-the-art model inversion attacks and is shown to outperform them in terms of image quality and the number of queries required. However, concerns are raised about the practicality of the attack due to the need for extensive training and the potential for detection due to high query volumes.

**Strengths:**
- The paper addresses a significant and practical problem by focusing on reducing the number of queries needed for model inversion attacks, which is crucial for real-world applications.
- The proposed method, TMI, demonstrates a novel approach by using StyleGAN for model inversion, which is both innovative and effective.
- The paper is well-written, clear, and easy to follow, making it accessible to a broad audience.
- The experimental results are robust and convincing, showing that TMI outperforms existing state-of-the-art methods in terms of both image quality and the number of queries required.
- The paper provides a thorough evaluation of the proposed method, including a comparison with various existing methods and an analysis of the number of queries needed for different datasets.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a comprehensive discussion on the limitations and potential negative societal impacts of the proposed method, which is crucial for understanding the broader implications of the work.
- The experimental setup could be improved by including more diverse datasets and different architectures to better demonstrate the generalizability of the findings.
- The paper does not sufficiently address the practicality of the attack, particularly the high query volumes needed for the attack, which could raise suspicion and be detected.
- The paper does not adequately discuss the potential for adversarial attacks on the StyleGAN model used in the preparation phase, which could affect the reliability of the results.
- The paper does not provide sufficient details on the training process of the StyleGAN model, which is crucial for reproducibility and understanding the method's effectiveness.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors provide more details on the training process of the StyleGAN model, including the specifics of the loss function and the training parameters used?
- How does the TMI method perform when using different architectures, such as ResNet-50, and what are the implications of using different architectures on the attack's effectiveness?
- What are the specifics of the adversarial attacks used to evaluate the robustness of the StyleGAN model, and how do these attacks impact the results of the model inversion process?
- Could the authors discuss the potential negative societal impacts of the proposed method and provide suggestions for mitigating these risks?
- How does the TMI method perform when using different datasets, particularly those that are not as diverse as those used in the current study?
- Can the authors provide more details on the experimental setup, such as the specifics of the datasets used and the training parameters for the baseline methods?
