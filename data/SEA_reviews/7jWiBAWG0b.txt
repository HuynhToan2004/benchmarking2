 **Summary:**
The paper investigates the generalization and optimization properties of non-convex pairwise stochastic gradient descent (SGD) in the presence of heavy-tailed sub-Weibull gradient noise. It establishes stability-based learning guarantees for non-convex pairwise SGD by bridging the relationship between l1 on-average model stability and generalization error. The paper also introduces refined generalization bounds and error bounds for optimization, which are competitive with existing results in the literature. The theoretical contributions are supported by comparisons with prior works, highlighting the novelty and relevance of the study.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, with clear and concise definitions, assumptions, and theorems, making it accessible and easy to understand.
- The authors provide a detailed comparison with previous works, highlighting the novelty and relevance of the study.
- The paper introduces refined generalization bounds and error bounds for optimization, which are competitive with existing results.
- The theoretical contributions are significant, especially in the context of pairwise learning, which is an important but understudied area.
- The paper is well-structured, with a clear introduction and logical flow of ideas, making it easy to follow.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks numerical experiments to validate the theoretical results, which could enhance its credibility and practical applicability.
- The assumptions made in the paper, such as the PL condition and the gradient dominance condition, are not clearly justified or explained.
- The paper does not sufficiently discuss the limitations of its approach or the potential negative societal impacts of its findings.
- The technical contributions of the paper are not clearly articulated, and the paper does not sufficiently distinguish its novel contributions from existing literature.
- The paper could benefit from a more thorough discussion of the limitations of its assumptions and the practical implications of its results.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors provide more details on the assumptions made in the paper, particularly the PL condition and the gradient dominance condition?
- How do the authors justify the assumptions made in the paper, and what are the implications if these assumptions do not hold?
