 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel message-passing approach for coreset selection, focusing on balancing data diversity and difficulty in large datasets. The proposed method, D2 Pruning, constructs a graph where each node represents a data point, and message passing is used to update the difficulty scores based on the neighbors' scores. The algorithm then selects a subset of data points based on these updated scores, aiming to maintain a balance between diversity and difficulty. The method is tested on various datasets including CIFAR10, CIFAR100, ImageNet, and NLP datasets, demonstrating improvements over existing methods in low-to-medium pruning rates. However, the paper is criticized for its complexity, lack of clarity in experimental results, and insufficient discussion on the choice of hyperparameters.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel approach to coreset selection by combining data diversity and difficulty, which is a significant advancement in the field.
- The methodology is clearly explained, making it easy to follow and understand.
- The experimental results are comprehensive, covering a variety of datasets and scenarios, which demonstrates the robustness and applicability of the proposed method.
- The paper provides a good explanation of the intuition behind the method, which enhances its credibility and practical relevance.
- The paper is well-written, making it accessible and easy to understand, which is crucial for a broad audience.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper is complex, with many hyperparameters that require careful tuning, which could make it difficult for practitioners to adopt without extensive experimentation.
- The experimental results are not clearly presented, making it difficult to draw meaningful conclusions from the data.
- The paper lacks a thorough discussion on the choice of hyperparameters and their impact on the results, which could be critical for understanding the robustness and reliability of the proposed method.
- The paper does not adequately compare its results with existing methods, particularly in terms of accuracy and performance metrics, which could undermine the claims of its effectiveness.
- The paper's presentation and organization need improvement, with some sections appearing redundant or confusing, which could detract from the overall impact of the research.
- The paper does not sufficiently discuss the limitations of its approach, which could help in understanding the scope and applicability of the proposed method.
