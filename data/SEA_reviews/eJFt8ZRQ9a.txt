 **Summary:**
The paper introduces IMProv, a novel approach to in-context learning (ICL) in computer vision tasks using a generative model that leverages both textual and visual prompts. IMProv is trained on a new dataset, S2CV, which is a significant extension of existing datasets, and is evaluated on various computer vision tasks such as foreground segmentation, object detection, and colorization. The model's effectiveness is demonstrated through comprehensive experimental results, showing improvements over existing methods. However, the paper's novelty is questioned due to its similarity to existing models like MAE-VQGAN, and the lack of significant technical innovation is noted. Additionally, the paper's methodology and the evaluation of its contributions are seen as somewhat limited, with concerns about the clarity of the experimental setup and the generalizability of the results.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, easy to follow, and the authors provide clear explanations of the method and experimental results.
- The proposed IMProv model achieves good performance on various computer vision tasks, and the experimental results demonstrate its effectiveness.
- The paper introduces a new dataset, S2CV, which is a significant contribution to the field and could be beneficial for future research.
- The use of multimodal prompting, combining both visual and textual prompts, is a novel approach that enhances the model's ability to learn from in-context examples.
- The paper provides comprehensive evaluations, including ablation studies and comparisons with existing methods, which help in understanding the model's performance and contributions.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks significant technical innovation, as the proposed method is similar to existing models like MAE-VQGAN, with the main difference being the use of textual prompts.
- The novelty of the IMProv model is limited, as it mainly uses a combination of existing models without significant modifications or improvements.
- The paper does not adequately address the limitations of the IMProv model, which could affect the generalizability of the findings.
- The experimental setup and the evaluation of the model's performance are not clearly explained, making it difficult to assess the model's effectiveness and the validity of the results.
- The paper could benefit from a more thorough discussion of the limitations and potential negative societal impacts of the proposed method.

**Questions:**
- Could you clarify the technical contributions of the IMProv model compared to existing models like MAE-VQGAN?
- How does the IMProv model handle inconsistencies between textual and visual prompts, as shown in Figure 6?
- Can you provide more details on the experimental setup, such as the training and evaluation procedures, to enhance the reproducibility and transparency of the research?
- What are the potential negative societal impacts of the IMProv model, and how do the authors address these concerns?
