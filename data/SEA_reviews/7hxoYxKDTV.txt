 **Summary:**
The paper introduces PQDiff, a novel diffusion-based method for image outpainting that achieves state-of-the-art performance on three benchmark datasets, surpassing existing methods like QueryOTR. PQDiff utilizes a relative positional query scheme, which allows for the generation of images with arbitrary expansion multiples in a single step, without requiring pre-trained backbone networks. The method's efficiency is highlighted by its ability to outpaint images in one step, which is a significant improvement over traditional methods that require multiple iterations. The paper also discusses the limitations and potential negative societal impacts of the proposed method.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-organized, with clear explanations of the method and its contributions.
- The proposed method, PQDiff, achieves state-of-the-art performance on three benchmark datasets and is the first to achieve one-step image outpainting, which is a significant advancement over existing methods.
- The paper is technically sound, with a clear motivation and a novel approach to image outpainting using relative positional embedding and a diffusion-based generator.
- The experiments are comprehensive, including ablation studies that demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed method.
- The paper is well-written, making it easy to follow, and includes a detailed discussion on the limitations and potential negative societal impacts of the proposed method.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed comparison with existing methods like RPE, which also uses positional encoding for image outpainting.
- The method's ability to handle arbitrary and continuous multiples of outpainting is not clearly distinguished from existing methods like QueryOTR.
- The paper could benefit from more detailed explanations and examples of the "copy" operation and the "relative positional embedding" used in the method.
- The paper does not adequately address the potential negative societal impacts of the proposed method, such as its application in creating deepfakes or other malicious uses.
- The paper could benefit from more detailed discussions on the limitations of the proposed method, such as its performance on different datasets and the potential for overfitting.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors provide more details on the "copy" operation and how it affects the quality of the outpainting results?
- How does the proposed method handle the outpainting of images with different scales or aspect ratios?
- Could the authors clarify the differences between the "relative positional embedding" used in the proposed method and other methods like QueryOTR?
- How does the method handle the generation of images with different aspect ratios, and what are the implications for the quality of the generated images?
- Could the authors provide more details on the training and sampling details, including hyper-parameters and the impact of different parameters on the performance of the method?
