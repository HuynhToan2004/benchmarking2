 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel algorithm called Incremental Successive Halving (iSHA), an extension of the Successive Halving Algorithm (SHA), designed for hyperparameter optimization (HPO) under resource constraints. The algorithm is aimed at improving the efficiency of HPO by allowing the budget to be increased incrementally, which is a significant departure from traditional methods that either use a fixed budget or allow for unbounded budgets. The paper provides theoretical and empirical evidence to support the algorithm's effectiveness, showing that it outperforms existing methods such as ASHA and PASHA in terms of resource utilization and performance. The algorithm's theoretical analysis includes a proof of its soundness and resource efficiency, backed by extensive empirical studies.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, clear, and easy to follow, with a clear motivation for the research.
- The proposed algorithm, iSHA, is simple, intuitive, and easy to implement, making it accessible for practical applications.
- The theoretical analysis of ASHA is novel and provides valuable insights into the behavior of asynchronous algorithms in resource-constrained environments.
- The empirical results show that iSHA outperforms existing methods such as ASHA and PASHA, demonstrating its practical utility and effectiveness.
- The paper provides a comprehensive theoretical analysis of iSHA, including a proof of its soundness and resource efficiency, which is supported by empirical studies.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed discussion on the limitations of the proposed method, particularly in scenarios where it may not perform optimally or where it may not be the most effective choice compared to other methods.
- The empirical evaluation is limited to a small number of datasets, which may not be sufficient to convincingly demonstrate the generalizability of the findings.
- The paper does not compare the proposed method with other state-of-the-art algorithms such as HyperJump, which could provide a more robust evaluation of its performance.
- The theoretical analysis is limited to the incremental version of the algorithm, and it would be beneficial to extend this analysis to other variants of the algorithm.
- The paper could benefit from a more detailed discussion on the practical implications of the algorithm's assumptions, particularly the assumption that the proportion of ε-optimal configurations in the sampled set is high enough.

**Questions:**
- Can you provide a more detailed discussion on the limitations of the proposed method and scenarios where it may not be the most effective choice?
- How does the proposed method perform in scenarios with varying budgets, and how does it compare to other methods in such scenarios?
- Could you elaborate on the practical implications of the assumption that the proportion of ε-optimal configurations in the sampled set is high enough?
- How does the proposed method perform in terms of computational complexity and runtime compared to other methods?
- Can you provide a more detailed discussion on the practical implications of the theoretical results, particularly in terms of their applicability and relevance to real-world scenarios?
