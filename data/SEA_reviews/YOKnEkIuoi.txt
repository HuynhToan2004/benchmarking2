 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel method for learning variance schedules in diffusion models, addressing the challenge of manually tuning these schedules for specific applications. This approach extends the Variational Diffusion Models (VDMs) to the conditioned case, allowing for the schedule to be learned during training, thus improving performance and adaptability to various applications. The method is tested across three different benchmarks, including super-resolution microscopy, quantitative phase imaging, and image super-resolution, demonstrating competitive results against existing methods. The paper also includes theoretical insights and experimental validations, highlighting the importance of the variance schedule in diffusion models and its impact on performance.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel method for learning the variance schedule in diffusion models, which is a significant advancement in the field.
- The proposed method is demonstrated to outperform existing methods across various applications, including super-resolution microscopy and quantitative phase imaging.
- The paper is well-written, with clear explanations of the theoretical underpinnings and practical applications of the proposed method.
- The approach of learning the schedule instead of manually tuning it as a hyperparameter is a practical and effective solution for improving the performance of diffusion models.
- The paper includes a comprehensive set of experiments and ablation studies, which provide a robust validation of the proposed method.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed discussion on the limitations and potential negative societal impacts of the proposed method.
- There is a need for more comprehensive comparisons with other state-of-the-art methods, particularly in terms of computational efficiency and scalability.
- The paper could benefit from a more thorough analysis of the impact of the regularization term on the learning of the variance schedule.
- The paper does not adequately address the computational complexity of the proposed method, which could be a significant drawback for practical applications.
- The paper could be improved by including more detailed experimental results and comparisons, particularly in the image super-resolution benchmark.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors provide more detailed insights into the computational efficiency and scalability of the proposed method?
- How does the proposed method compare to other state-of-the-art methods in terms of computational complexity and training time?
- Can the authors provide more detailed results on the impact of the regularization term on the learning of the variance schedule?
- How does the proposed method perform in terms of sample efficiency and stability during training?
- Can the authors provide more examples or visualizations of the learned variance schedules to better understand their impact on the performance of the model?
- Could the authors clarify the discrepancies in the results reported in the paper, particularly in the image super-resolution benchmark?
