 **Summary:**
The paper introduces Graph Metanetworks (GMNs), a novel approach for processing diverse neural architectures by converting neural networks into graphs. GMNs utilize graph neural networks (GNNs) to process these graphs, allowing for the handling of diverse architectures including MLPs, CNNs, transformers, and more. The methodology is supported by theoretical proofs and empirical evidence, showing the expressiveness and equivariance of GMNs to permutation symmetries. The paper also includes extensive experiments that demonstrate the effectiveness of GMNs across various neural network architectures, outperforming existing methods in some cases. However, the scalability and efficiency of GMNs, particularly with large networks, are noted as limitations.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written and easy to follow, with a clear and concise presentation of the proposed method.
- The authors demonstrate a strong understanding of the problem and the field, providing a comprehensive overview of related works and a thorough discussion on the limitations of prior approaches.
- The proposed method is novel, generalizing to a wide range of neural architectures and showing empirical performance superior to existing methods.
- The paper includes a theoretical analysis of the proposed method, supported by empirical evidence, which enhances the credibility of the proposed approach.
- The experiments are well-designed and convincing, demonstrating the effectiveness of the proposed method across various tasks and datasets.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a clear definition and motivation for the proposed task, which could be better articulated to enhance the reader's understanding of its relevance and significance.
- There is a need for more detailed explanations and justifications for the choices made in the design of the parameter graph, particularly how it affects the expressivity of the GNN and the scalability of the method.
- The paper does not adequately address the scalability and efficiency of the proposed method, especially in handling large neural networks, which could be a significant limitation for practical applications.
- The novelty of the method is questioned, as it seems to be an extension of existing methods rather than a fundamentally new approach.
- The paper could benefit from a more detailed comparison with other methods, particularly in terms of computational resources and performance metrics.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors clarify the definition and motivation of the proposed task? How does it differ from existing tasks, and what are its practical applications?
- How does the choice of the parameter graph affect the expressivity of the GNN, and what are the implications for the scalability and efficiency of the method?
- Could the authors provide more details on the scalability and efficiency of the proposed method, particularly in handling large neural networks?
- How does the proposed method compare with other existing methods in terms of computational resources and performance metrics?
- Can the authors elaborate on the novelty of their approach and how it differs from existing methods, especially in terms of theoretical contributions and practical applications?
