 **Summary:**
The paper investigates the anchoring technique, which involves replacing the input to a neural network with a tuple comprising an anchor and the residual between the input and the anchor. This method, first introduced by Thiagarajan et al., has shown promise in improving out-of-distribution (OOD) generalization and anomaly rejection. The authors propose a new variant called Random Anchor Masking (RAM), which masks the anchor with a noise vector sampled from the same distribution as the anchor. This approach is aimed at improving the diversity of residuals and reducing the model's sensitivity to distribution shifts. The paper presents a comprehensive empirical evaluation across various datasets and models, demonstrating the effectiveness of the anchoring method in enhancing generalization and safety. However, the theoretical underpinnings of the anchoring method are not fully explored, and the paper could benefit from a deeper exploration of the mechanisms behind the observed improvements.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel approach to anchoring by masking the anchor with a noise vector, which is a simple yet effective method for improving the diversity of residuals.
- The paper is well-written, clear, and easy to follow, making it accessible to a broad audience.
- The empirical evaluation is comprehensive, covering various datasets, models, and tasks, providing a robust validation of the proposed method.
- The paper provides a detailed analysis of the mechanisms behind the observed improvements, including the impact of dataset size and label noise on the anchoring method.
- The paper is significant as it addresses the critical issue of out-of-distribution (OOD) generalization, which is crucial for safe and effective deployment of neural networks.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a thorough theoretical analysis of the anchoring method, which could provide deeper insights into the mechanisms behind the observed improvements.
- The novelty of the proposed method is limited, as it builds upon existing techniques like anchoring and masking, which have been previously explored.
- The paper could benefit from a more detailed comparison with existing methods, such as those using data augmentation techniques, to better contextualize the contributions of the proposed method.
- The paper does not sufficiently address the computational cost and memory requirements of the anchoring method, which could be a significant concern for large-scale models.
- The paper does not adequately discuss the limitations of the proposed method, which could help in understanding the scope and applicability of the findings.

**Questions:**
- Could you provide a more detailed theoretical analysis of the anchoring method, particularly how it impacts the generalization and safety of the trained models?
- How does the proposed method compare with other data augmentation techniques in terms of computational cost and memory requirements?
- Can you clarify the rationale behind the choice of parameters such as the alpha value in the RAM regularizer?
- How does the proposed method perform when applied to other types of data beyond images, such as text or audio?
- In light of the observed improvements in OOD generalization and anomaly rejection, can you provide insights into how the anchoring method affects the model's ability to generalize to unseen combinations of anchors and residuals?
- How does the anchoring method perform when applied to different architectures and datasets, especially those that are not typically used in the vision domain?
