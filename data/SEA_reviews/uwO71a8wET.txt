 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a Bayesian neural controlled differential equation (BNCDE) framework for estimating treatment effects in continuous time. It builds upon the TE-CDE model, integrating Bayesian inference to quantify uncertainty in treatment outcomes. The methodology involves a coupled system of neural controlled differential equations and neural stochastic differential equations, which allows for variational Bayesian inference. The paper evaluates the model using a simulated dataset, comparing it to existing methods and demonstrating improvements in uncertainty quantification. However, the experimental validation is limited to a single dataset, and the paper lacks a broader comparison with existing methods and a more rigorous theoretical analysis.

**Strengths:**
- The paper addresses a significant gap in the literature by integrating Bayesian inference into neural controlled differential equations (CDEs), enhancing the quantification of uncertainty in treatment outcomes.
- The methodology is well-articulated, with clear explanations of the Bayesian neural CDEs and the Bayesian neural SDEs, which are novel in their application to treatment effect estimation.
- The paper is well-written, with a clear structure that makes it easy to follow, and includes a comprehensive literature review that effectively positions the research within the current state of the art.
- The proposed method is novel and well-motivated, providing a clear explanation of its components and how they contribute to the overall model.
- The paper addresses a relevant and timely topic, with potential applications in various fields, including personalized medicine.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a thorough comparison with existing methods, particularly in terms of uncertainty quantification. It only compares against a single baseline method (TE-CDE), which may not adequately demonstrate the superiority or generalizability of the proposed method.
- The experimental validation is limited to a single dataset, which may not be sufficient to establish the robustness and applicability of the method across different scenarios.
- There is a lack of detailed discussion on the assumptions made by the model and the implications of these assumptions on the model's applicability and generalizability.
- The paper does not provide a detailed discussion on the computational complexity or scalability of the proposed method, which is crucial for understanding its practical implementation in real-world scenarios.
- There are minor typographical and formatting errors that need to be corrected.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors provide a more detailed comparison with existing methods, particularly in terms of uncertainty quantification and model performance?
- How does the proposed method perform in scenarios where the data is noisy or incomplete?
- What are the implications of the assumptions made by the model on the model's applicability and generalizability?
- Could the authors provide more details on the computational complexity and scalability of the proposed method?
- How does the proposed method compare in terms of computational efficiency and model performance with other Bayesian neural network methods, such as those using MCMC or variational inference?
- Can the authors clarify the role and impact of the neural SDEs in approximating the posterior distribution of the neural CDE weights?
