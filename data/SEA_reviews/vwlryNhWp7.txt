 **Summary:**
The paper introduces MMLoRA, a method for improving discriminative multi-modal learning by leveraging large-scale pre-trained uni-modal models. It addresses the issue of insufficient learning of uni-modal features in multi-modal models by freezing the weights of uni-modal fine-tuned models and introducing additional trainable rank decomposition matrices. The method involves joint multi-modal training, which enhances adaptation between modalities and improves overall performance. The paper demonstrates the effectiveness of MMLoRA across various datasets, including audio-visual, vision-language, and RGB-optical flow datasets, showing consistent improvements over baseline methods.

**Strengths:**
- The paper addresses a significant problem in the field of multi-modal learning by improving the generalization ability of multi-modal models, particularly in audio-visual and vision-language tasks.
- The proposed method, MMLoRA, is simple, effective, and easy to implement, making it accessible for practical applications.
- The paper is well-written, with clear and convincing experimental results that demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed method.
- The method's adaptability to various datasets and its potential for improving the performance of existing models are notable.
- The introduction of MMLoRA, which adds trainable rank decomposition matrices to specific modality or all modalities' models, is a novel approach that enhances the adaptation between modalities.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed discussion on the choice of rank value in MMLoRA, which is crucial for the method's effectiveness.
- There is a lack of clarity regarding the selection of datasets and the rationale behind the choice of specific models, such as BERT-Base over other text encoders.
- The method's performance is not consistently superior across all datasets, and in some cases, it performs worse than baseline methods.
- The paper does not provide a detailed comparison with other parameter-efficient fine-tuning methods, which could have strengthened the argument for the superiority of MMLoRA.
- The novelty of the method is questioned as it closely resembles existing methods like LoRA, and the improvements over these methods are not clearly articulated.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors provide more details on the selection of the rank value in MMLoRA and its impact on the method's performance?
- How does MMLoRA compare to other parameter-efficient fine-tuning methods like LoRA and PEFT, particularly in terms of computational efficiency and effectiveness?
- In the context of multi-modal learning, what specific advantages does MMLoRA offer over other methods, especially in scenarios where it underperforms compared to baseline methods?
- Could the authors elaborate on the choice of datasets and the rationale behind the selection of specific models for each dataset?
- Given the similarities to existing methods like LoRA, how does MMLoRA differentiate itself and offer unique benefits to the field of multi-modal learning?
