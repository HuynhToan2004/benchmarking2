 **Summary:**
The paper introduces CodeSage, a novel approach to code representation learning that leverages a two-stage pretraining scheme. The first stage involves a mix of random masking and identifier deobfuscation to train encoders, followed by a second stage of contrastive learning to enhance representations. This methodology is designed to improve the performance of code representation models on various downstream tasks such as semantic search and classification. The paper presents a detailed analysis of the pretraining schemes and their impact on downstream task performance, demonstrating that CodeSage outperforms existing models across multiple languages and tasks.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, making it easy to follow and understand the proposed methodologies.
- The experiments are comprehensive and demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed method across various downstream tasks.
- The paper provides a detailed analysis of the pretraining schemes, including ablation studies and insights into the factors influencing downstream task performance.
- The methodology is novel, combining mask language modeling with identifier deobfuscation and contrastive learning, which enhances the representation learning for code.
- The paper addresses an important and timely problem in code representation learning, which is crucial for the development of advanced software engineering tools.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed comparison with existing methods, particularly in terms of the novelty of the proposed method and its performance relative to state-of-the-art models.
- There is a lack of clarity in the methodology, particularly in the definitions and roles of different components such as "hard positive" and "hard negative."
- The paper does not adequately address the limitations of the proposed method, which could include its applicability to different programming languages or the generalizability of the results.
- The paper does not sufficiently discuss the ethical implications of the work, which could be significant given the potential impact on software development and maintenance.
- There are some inconsistencies and potential errors in the paper, such as typographical errors and unclear definitions.
