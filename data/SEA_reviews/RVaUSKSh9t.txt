 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel approach to thermal analysis using Graph Neural Networks (GNNs) for the prediction of thermal conductivity in composite materials, focusing on the block decomposition and continual graph learning methods. It aims to enhance the efficiency and accuracy of thermal predictions by partitioning the system into sub-blocks based on material properties and using a physics-constrained GNN to integrate local models into a system, with edges representing thermal interactions. The methodology includes a continual adaptation of the GNN model to accommodate random variations, which is evaluated through experiments showing improvements over traditional methods like FEM. Despite its innovative approach, the paper faces criticism for its limited novelty and lack of comprehensive comparative analysis with other machine learning methods.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-structured, clearly written, and easy to follow, with a clear motivation and detailed methodology.
- The proposed method is innovative, combining block decomposition and continual graph learning, which is not commonly seen in similar applications.
- The experimental results show promising outcomes, with the proposed method demonstrating better performance than traditional methods like FEM in terms of speed and accuracy.
- The application of GNNs in thermal analysis is novel and relevant, addressing the challenges of complex systems with diverse thermal conductivity and interface variations.
- The paper includes code for reproducibility, which is a valuable asset for further research and validation of the proposed methods.

**Weaknesses:**
- The novelty of the work is limited, as the approach primarily builds upon existing methods like block decomposition and continual graph learning without significant innovation.
- The experimental evaluation is limited, lacking a comprehensive comparison with other machine learning methods and not including real-world data which could enhance the validity of the results.
- The paper lacks a discussion on the limitations of the proposed method, which could provide a more balanced perspective on its applicability and effectiveness.
- There is a lack of detailed explanation and justification for the method's assumptions and the physical interpretations provided, which could be confusing and not well-aligned with the actual physical processes.
- The paper does not adequately address the scalability of the method to larger or more complex systems, which is a significant concern given the potential limitations of GNNs in handling high-dimensional data.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors clarify the physical interpretation of the GNN in the context of thermal analysis and how it aligns with the actual physical processes?
- How does the proposed method handle variations in thermal conductivity and material properties within a single sub-block, and what are the implications for the overall system's performance?
- Could the authors provide more details on the experimental setup, particularly the generation of the power map and the random assignment of conductivity values?
- What are the specific limitations of the proposed method, and how do these affect its applicability in real-world scenarios?
- How does the proposed method compare in terms of computational efficiency and accuracy with other machine learning methods, especially in the context of large-scale or complex systems?
- Can the authors discuss the potential scalability issues of the GNN approach and propose strategies to mitigate these challenges?
