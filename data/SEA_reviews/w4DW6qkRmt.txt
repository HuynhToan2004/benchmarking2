 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel framework named Summarized Retrieval (SuRe) aimed at enhancing the performance of open-domain question-answering (ODQA) systems using large language models (LLMs). The core idea behind SuRe is to construct summaries of retrieved passages for each answer candidate, which are then used to evaluate the validity and ranking of the summaries to determine the most plausible answer. This approach allows LLMs to focus on contexts relevant to the given candidate, providing more discriminative viewpoints for the question. The framework is evaluated across multiple datasets and LLMs, demonstrating significant improvements in ODQA performance. The method's effectiveness is highlighted by its ability to improve the zero-shot ODQA performance of retrieval-augmented LLMs, making it applicable to various LLMs and retrieval methods.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel approach by utilizing LLMs to summarize retrieved passages for each answer candidate, which is a significant departure from traditional methods that rely on retrieval-augmented LLMs without additional training.
- The proposed method is simple yet effective, with clear and detailed explanations of the methodology and experiments, which are well-organized and easy to follow.
- The paper demonstrates the effectiveness of the proposed method across various datasets and LLMs, showing significant improvements in zero-shot ODQA performance.
- The method is adaptable and can be integrated with a wide range of retrieval methods and LLMs, making it a versatile solution for enhancing the performance of ODQA systems.
- The generated summaries from the SuRe framework can be used to measure the importance of retrieved passages and serve as more preferred rationales by models and humans, providing additional benefits for evaluating the effectiveness of the retrieval process.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks detailed analysis on how the method performs in scenarios where the retrieved passages do not fully support the answer candidates, which could limit its applicability in real-world scenarios.
- There is a lack of clarity on how the method handles scenarios where the retrieved passages contain contradictory or misleading information, which could potentially lead to incorrect or misleading answers.
- The paper does not discuss the potential negative societal impacts of the proposed method, which is a significant oversight given the potential applications of the method in various real-world scenarios.
- The paper's focus on improving the performance of LLMs in ODQA tasks without sufficient discussion on the limitations and potential negative societal impacts of these models could be seen as a weakness.
- The paper does not provide sufficient details on the implementation and optimization of the method, which could limit its practical applicability in real-world scenarios.

**Questions:**
- How does the method handle scenarios where the retrieved passages do not fully support the answer candidates?
- Can the method be adapted to handle scenarios where the retrieved passages contain contradictory or misleading information?
- How does the method perform in scenarios where the retrieved passages are of varying quality or relevance to the question?
- Could the authors provide more details on the implementation and optimization of the method, particularly in terms of computational resources and training requirements?
- What are the potential negative societal impacts of the proposed method, and how are these addressed in the paper?
- How does the method compare to other existing methods for enhancing the performance of LLMs in ODQA tasks, particularly in terms of computational efficiency and practical applicability?
