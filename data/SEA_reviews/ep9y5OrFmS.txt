 **Summary:**
The paper investigates the relationship between parameter pruning masks and the top Hessian eigenspace in neural networks, focusing on how these spaces overlap and evolve during training. It proposes a novel method using Grassmannian metrics to quantify the similarity between these spaces, suggesting that the largest parameter magnitudes correspond to the directions of highest loss curvature. The study is based on experiments using a small MLP trained on MNIST, showing that the overlap between pruning masks and Hessian eigenspaces is significant and stabilizes early in training. The paper also discusses the implications of these findings for pruning and optimization methods, although it faces criticism for its limited scope, lack of theoretical backing, and unclear experimental setups.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel perspective on the connection between pruning masks and Hessian eigenspaces, which is a significant contribution to the field.
- The experimental results are well-supported by clear and well-presented figures, enhancing the paper's readability and comprehensibility.
- The authors effectively use Grassmannian metrics to quantify the similarity between these spaces, which is a useful methodological contribution.
- The paper is well-written and easy to follow, with clear explanations of the methods and results.
- The paper's findings, such as the early stabilization of the Hessian spectrum and the overlap between pruning masks and Hessian eigenspaces, are intriguing and could lead to new insights in the field.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a theoretical foundation for its claims, which could be strengthened by providing a more rigorous theoretical analysis.
- The experimental setup is limited and does not convincingly demonstrate the generalizability of the findings to larger or more complex neural networks.
- The paper does not sufficiently address the computational complexity of the proposed methods, which is a significant concern for practical applications.
- There is a lack of clarity in the experimental setup, particularly regarding the computational methods used and the selection of hyperparameters.
- The paper's claims about the early stabilization of pruning masks and Hessian eigenspaces are not sufficiently supported by the experimental evidence provided.
- The paper's relevance to the conference is questionable, as it primarily focuses on a specific aspect of neural network training without a clear connection to broader ICLR topics.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors provide more details on how the pruning masks are generated and how they relate to the Hessian eigenspaces?
- How does the paper address the computational complexity of the proposed methods, especially in terms of scalability to larger neural networks?
- Can the authors clarify the experimental setup, particularly how the hyperparameters were selected and how the results were validated?
- How do the authors justify the choice of the MLP model and the MNIST dataset for their experiments, and what implications do these choices have for the generalizability of the findings?
- Could the authors provide more theoretical insights into the observed phenomena, such as the early stabilization of pruning masks and Hessian eigenspaces?
