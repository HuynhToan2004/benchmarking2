 **Summary:**
The paper addresses the multi-label classification problem under the macro-at-k metric, which requires predicting exactly k labels for each instance. The authors propose a statistically consistent algorithm using the Frank-Wolfe method to optimize these metrics, demonstrating its effectiveness through empirical results. Theoretical analysis provides insights into the form of the optimal classifier and the convergence of the proposed algorithm. The paper also discusses the challenges posed by the at-k constraint, which couples binary classification tasks, making the optimization problem more complex.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel approach to multi-label classification, focusing on the macro-at-k metric, which is both theoretically and empirically validated.
- The authors provide a clear theoretical framework, including proofs for the existence and form of the optimal classifier, and a statistically consistent and practical learning algorithm.
- The paper is well-written, making it accessible and understandable, with a clear presentation of the problem and proposed solutions.
- The empirical results demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed method, showing that it outperforms baseline methods in many cases.
- The paper addresses the macro-at-k metric, which is a significant contribution to the field of multi-label classification.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper does not sufficiently compare its results with existing methods, such as those mentioned in related works, which could provide a clearer context for the novelty and effectiveness of the proposed approach.
- The experimental setup and results lack detailed explanation, particularly in the methodology used for estimating label probabilities and the specifics of the experimental conditions.
- The paper could benefit from a more detailed discussion on the practical implications and real-world applications of the macro-at-k metric.
- The paper's theoretical contributions, while significant, are not fully integrated with the practical implications, and the connection between the theoretical results and the proposed algorithm is not adequately explained.
- The paper's presentation could be improved to better highlight the novelty and contributions of the work, particularly in the abstract and introduction sections.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors clarify how the proposed algorithm handles the at-k constraint, especially in terms of its impact on the optimization process?
- How does the paper's theoretical framework apply to more general metrics beyond those that are linearly decomposable?
- Could the authors provide a more detailed explanation of the experimental setup, including the methodology used for estimating label probabilities and the specifics of the experimental conditions?
- How does the proposed algorithm compare to existing methods in terms of computational efficiency and scalability, particularly in scenarios with a large number of labels?
- Could the authors discuss the practical implications and real-world applications of the macro-at-k metric, and how the proposed algorithm might be applied in these contexts?
