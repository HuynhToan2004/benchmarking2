 **Summary:**
The paper investigates the application of dropout regularization in parameter-efficient fine-tuning (PEFT) scenarios, specifically focusing on the LoRA method. The authors demonstrate that PEFT is susceptible to overfitting, despite its efficiency, and propose a new dropout method called HiddenKey. This method combines existing dropout techniques like DropKey and HiddenCut with a new KL loss to enhance model performance. The paper provides empirical evidence of HiddenKey's effectiveness across various tasks, including both natural language understanding (NLU) and natural language generation (NLG). The proposed method aims to address the challenges of overfitting in PEFT by integrating dropout techniques in a novel way.

**Strengths:**
- The paper provides a thorough analysis of the application of dropout in LoRA-based PEFT, highlighting the potential contradiction between PEFT and dropout.
- The authors propose a new dropout method, HiddenKey, which integrates DropKey and HiddenCut with KL loss, demonstrating its effectiveness on both NLU and NLG tasks.
- The paper is well-written, clear, and easy to follow, making complex concepts accessible to a broad audience.
- The empirical results show that the proposed method, HiddenKey, outperforms other dropout methods, including DropKey and HiddenCut, in various settings.
- The analysis of the training and inference gap is interesting and provides insights into the application of dropout techniques in PEFT scenarios.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a comprehensive comparison with other PEFT methods such as Adapter, RepLKD, and others, which could have provided a more robust validation of the proposed method.
- The paper does not include a theoretical analysis of HiddenKey, which could have strengthened the argument for its effectiveness.
- The experimental settings and comparisons are not sufficiently detailed or clear, particularly in how different dropout methods are applied across various tasks and datasets.
- The paper does not sufficiently address the computational costs associated with HiddenKey, which could be a significant concern for practical applications.
- There are several grammatical and typographical errors that need correction to improve the readability and professionalism of the paper.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors provide a more detailed comparison with other PEFT methods, including Adapter, RepLKD, and others?
- How does HiddenKey compare in terms of computational cost and efficiency with other PEFT methods?
- Can the authors clarify the differences between DropKey and DropAttention, especially in terms of their forward and backward passes?
- How does HiddenKey address the overfitting issue in PEFT, and what specific mechanisms does it employ to prevent overfitting?
- Could the authors provide more details on the experimental settings, such as how different dropout methods were applied across various tasks and datasets?
- How does HiddenKey perform in different model sizes and training regimes, and what are the implications for its practical application?
