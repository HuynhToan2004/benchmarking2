 **Summary:**
The paper introduces NuNet, a novel method for causal effect estimation that combines inverse probability weighting and adversarial training to address the instability and uncertainty issues in causal inference. NuNet aims to improve upon existing methods by integrating adversarial training with the estimation of propensity scores, which is claimed to enhance the robustness and stability of the estimation. The methodology is supported by theoretical analysis and empirical results, demonstrating NuNet's effectiveness in handling heterogeneous noise and improving estimation accuracy. The paper also discusses the limitations and potential improvements of the approach, including the need for further theoretical analysis and broader empirical validation.

**Strengths:**
- The paper addresses a significant problem in causal inference, namely the instability and uncertainty in propensity score estimation, which is a crucial aspect of the method.
- The proposed method, NuNet, integrates adversarial training with inverse probability weighting, providing a novel approach to addressing these issues.
- The theoretical analysis and empirical results are robust, demonstrating the effectiveness of NuNet in handling heterogeneous noise and improving estimation accuracy.
- The paper is well-written, clear, and easy to follow, making it accessible to a broad audience.
- The methodology is supported by a theoretical framework and empirical evidence, which strengthens the credibility of the proposed approach.
- The paper is the first to apply pessimism in offline reinforcement learning to weighted estimation in causal inference, offering a novel perspective on the problem.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed comparison with other state-of-the-art methods, such as DeR-CFR, which could have provided a more comprehensive evaluation of NuNet's performance.
- The theoretical analysis is not fully self-contained, requiring referencing to previous works for a complete understanding of the theoretical underpinnings.
- The paper does not provide sufficient empirical evidence to support the claim that NuNet outperforms other methods in all scenarios, particularly in datasets with non-heterogeneous noise.
- The paper could benefit from a more rigorous discussion on the limitations of the proposed method and potential areas for future research.
- The experimental setup and the choice of datasets could be expanded to include more diverse and realistic scenarios, such as semi-synthetic datasets, to better validate the effectiveness of NuNet.
- The paper could provide more detailed insights into the computational complexity and the practical implications of using NuNet in real-world applications.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors provide a more detailed comparison with other state-of-the-art methods, such as DeR-CFR, to better understand the relative performance of NuNet?
- How does NuNet perform in scenarios with non-heterogeneous noise, and what are the implications of this for the robustness of the method?
- Can the authors clarify the role and impact of the hyperparameter c in the theoretical analysis and its practical implementation?
- How does the proposed method compare with other robust methods like DeR-CFR, and what are the specific advantages of NuNet over these methods?
- Could the authors provide more detailed insights into the computational complexity and the practical implications of using NuNet in real-world applications?
- Is there a possibility of extending the method to scenarios where the representation extractor is not invertible, and how might this affect the robustness and stability of the estimation?
