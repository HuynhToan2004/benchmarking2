 **Summary:**
The paper investigates the vulnerability of AI-generated text detectors to paraphrasing attacks, demonstrating that these detectors can be evaded by recursively paraphrasing AI-generated texts. The authors propose a theoretical framework connecting the detection difficulty with the Total Variation distance between AI-generated and human-written texts. They conduct experiments using a paraphrasing model to show that existing detectors can be evaded, and propose a new detector that is less susceptible to paraphrasing. The paper also discusses the implications of these findings for the development of more robust AI-generated text detectors.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, making it easy to follow, and the experiments are clearly presented.
- The topic is relevant and timely, addressing the critical issue of AI-generated text detection, which is crucial for maintaining the integrity of AI-generated content.
- The paper provides a comprehensive analysis of the performance of various AI-generated text detectors and proposes a novel recursive paraphrasing attack to evaluate their effectiveness.
- The authors establish a theoretical connection between the AUROC of the best possible detector and the Total Variation distance between human and AI-text distributions, offering a novel perspective on the fundamental hardness of the reliable text detection problem.
- The proposed recursive paraphrasing attack is innovative and shows promise in evading existing AI-generated text detectors, highlighting the need for more robust detection methods.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a comprehensive discussion on the limitations of the proposed attack and the conditions under which it might not be effective.
- The experiments are limited in scope, focusing primarily on one paraphrasing model (DIPPER) and not comparing the effectiveness of different paraphrasing models against various detectors.
- The paper does not provide a detailed analysis of the trade-offs between false positive rates and true positive rates in the proposed detector, which could be crucial for practical applications.
- The paper could benefit from a more thorough comparison with existing paraphrasing attacks and a broader range of paraphrasing models to establish the effectiveness and generalizability of the proposed method.
- The theoretical analysis could be improved by including a more detailed discussion on the assumptions and implications of the Total Variation distance used in the analysis.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors provide more details on the conditions under which the proposed attack might not be effective?
- How does the proposed recursive paraphrasing attack compare with other paraphrasing attacks in terms of effectiveness and generalizability?
- Could the authors discuss the potential implications of using different paraphrasing models on the effectiveness of the proposed attack?
- How does the proposed detector perform when faced with adversarial human-written text, and what are the trade-offs between false positive rates and true positive rates in practical applications?
- Could the authors provide a more detailed analysis of the assumptions and implications of the Total Variation distance used in the analysis, particularly in relation to the practical applicability of the theoretical results?
