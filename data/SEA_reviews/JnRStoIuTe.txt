 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel method called Repeated Sampling of Random Subsets (RS2) for data subset selection in deep learning training. RS2 randomly selects a subset of the dataset for each epoch, aiming to minimize the time-to-accuracy. This method is evaluated against various data pruning and distillation methods across multiple datasets, showing promising results in terms of accuracy and runtime efficiency. The authors provide theoretical insights and empirical evidence to support the effectiveness of RS2, particularly in scenarios where the training data is limited. Despite its simplicity and practicality, the paper acknowledges that RS2 may not be suitable for all scenarios, such as those requiring minimal dataset storage or labeling costs.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, clear, and easy to follow, making complex concepts accessible to a broad audience.
- The proposed method, Repeated Sampling of Random Subsets (RS2), is simple yet effective, with good empirical performance across various datasets and models.
- The paper provides a comprehensive empirical evaluation, comparing RS2 against 32 state-of-the-art data pruning and distillation methods across four datasets, demonstrating its effectiveness in reducing training time while maintaining accuracy.
- The theoretical analysis of RS2's convergence rate and generalization error is insightful, providing a deeper understanding of the method's behavior and performance.
- The method's practicality is highlighted by its simplicity and ease of implementation, which could make it appealing for practitioners seeking to optimize their training processes.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed discussion on the limitations and potential drawbacks of RS2, such as its applicability in scenarios requiring minimal dataset storage or labeling costs.
- There is a need for more experimental results, particularly on larger datasets like ImageNet, to validate the method's effectiveness on a broader scale.
- The paper could benefit from a more rigorous comparison with other baseline methods, such as those using dynamic sampling strategies or other data pruning techniques.
- The theoretical analysis could be strengthened by including more rigorous proofs or additional theoretical insights to solidify the claims made about RS2's convergence and generalization properties.
- The paper's claims about the method's novelty and effectiveness are not adequately supported by the experimental results, which may not convincingly demonstrate that RS2 outperforms existing methods.
