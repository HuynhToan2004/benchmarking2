 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel perspective on interpreting deep neural networks (DNNs) as dynamical systems, proposing a new metric called "dynamical discrepancy" to quantify the network's representational capacity. This metric is derived from the dynamical properties of neurons within the network, and its correlation with the model's performance is explored through various experiments, including the replacement of network components with dynamical systems and the computation of dynamical discrepancies during training and fine-tuning. The paper also presents a theoretical framework that connects the dynamical discrepancy with the model's generalization capability and the evolution of its representational capacity. Despite its innovative approach, the paper has been critiqued for its lack of clarity in defining key terms, the necessity of additional datasets for validation, and the potential oversimplification of complex neural network dynamics.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel perspective on interpreting DNNs as dynamical systems, which is a significant contribution to the field.
- The proposed metric, dynamical discrepancy, is a novel approach to quantifying a model's representational capacity, which is both theoretically sound and practically applicable.
- The experiments conducted are comprehensive and provide valuable insights into the relationship between dynamical discrepancy and model performance.
- The paper is well-structured, with clear explanations and a logical flow that makes it easy to follow.
- The authors have made a substantial effort to connect the dynamical discrepancy with the model's generalization capability, which is a crucial aspect of neural network behavior.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper could benefit from a more detailed explanation of the dynamical discrepancy and its relationship with the model's representational capacity.
- There is a lack of clarity in some sections, particularly in the definitions and explanations of key terms such as "dynamical neurons" and "dynamical discrepancy."
- The paper's claims about the model's representational capacity being strongly correlated with its dynamical attributes are not convincingly supported by the experimental results.
- The paper's scope is somewhat limited, focusing primarily on feedforward networks, which may not fully capture the complexity of neural network dynamics.
- The experimental results are not sufficiently robust to support the claims made, particularly in the comparison between dynamical systems and neural networks.
- The paper could benefit from a more rigorous definition and justification of the dynamical systems used, as well as a clearer explanation of how they relate to the neural networks being studied.

**Questions:**
- Could you clarify the definition and role of "dynamical neurons" in your model? How do they relate to the neurons in a typical neural network?
- How do the dynamical systems used in your experiments relate to the neural networks being studied? Are they intended to be an approximation or a direct mapping?
- In your experiments, how do you ensure that the dynamical systems accurately represent the behavior of the neural networks they are replacing?
- Can you provide more details on the computational complexity and practical implementation of the coordinate learning algorithm used in your model?
- How do you address the issue of the model's representational capacity being limited by the dimensionality of the Euclidean space in which the dynamical systems are embedded?
- Could you elaborate on the necessity of using additional datasets for validation, and how this addresses the potential biases and noise sources mentioned in the paper?
