 **Summary:**
The paper introduces LEO, a novel embodied multi-modal and multi-task general agent designed to perceive, ground, reason, plan, and act in the 3D world. LEO leverages a combination of 3D vision-language alignment and 3D vision-language-action instruction tuning to achieve state-of-the-art results across various 3D tasks, including 3D captioning, question answering, embodied reasoning, embodied navigation, and robotic manipulation. The authors curate a large-scale dataset for training LEO and propose several techniques aimed at enhancing the quality of prompted data from LLMs. The paper demonstrates LEO's proficiency on diverse tasks and its capability in scene-aware planning and dialogue, showing consistent performance gains while scaling up the training data.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces LEO, a novel embodied generalist agent that can perceive, ground, reason, plan, and act in the 3D world, which is a significant advancement in the field of embodied AI.
- LEO is trained using a large-scale dataset with object-level and scene-level tasks, which is meticulously curated and generated, necessitating a deep understanding of and interaction with the 3D world.
- The paper demonstrates LEO's proficiency on diverse tasks and its capability in scene-aware planning and dialogue, showing consistent performance gains while scaling up the training data.
- The authors propose a two-stage training scheme that is crucial for LEO's learning, and they conduct a comprehensive evaluation of LEO's performance on various tasks, including 3D captioning, question answering, embodied reasoning, embodied navigation, and robotic manipulation.
- The paper provides a detailed analysis of the results, including an ablation study on different data configurations and scaling law analysis, which provides valuable insights for the development of future embodied generalist agents.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed comparison of LEO with other state-of-the-art embodied agents, which could provide a clearer understanding of LEO's advantages and disadvantages.
- The training and inference details of LEO are not clearly explained, particularly the architecture of the 2D image encoder and the specifics of the training and inference process.
- The paper does not discuss the limitations of LEO, which could provide a more balanced view of the model's capabilities and potential areas for improvement.
- The evaluation metrics used in the paper are not clearly defined, and the results are not compared against other state-of-the-art models, which could limit the understanding of LEO's performance.
- The paper does not provide a detailed discussion on the limitations of the proposed model and the future directions of research, which could be crucial for advancing the field.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors provide a more detailed comparison of LEO with other state-of-the-art embodied agents, including a discussion of the advantages and disadvantages of each model?
- Can the authors clarify the training and inference details of LEO, particularly the architecture of the 2D image encoder and the specifics of the training and inference process?
- How do the authors address the limitations of LEO, and what are the future directions of research in this area?
- Could the authors provide a more detailed discussion on the limitations of the proposed model and the future directions of research?
- In the ablation study, what is the effect of using different datasets for training, and how does this affect the performance of LEO?
- How does LEO perform on more complex tasks that require more advanced reasoning and planning capabilities, such as those found in the Habitat Challenge?
- Can the authors provide more detailed examples of LEO's outputs, including both successful and unsuccessful cases, to better understand the model's performance?
