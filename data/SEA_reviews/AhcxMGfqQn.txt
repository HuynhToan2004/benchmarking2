 **Summary:**
The paper introduces Collaborative World Models (CoWorld), a novel approach for offline visual reinforcement learning (RL) that leverages an auxiliary online simulator to address overfitting and value function overestimation issues in offline datasets. The method involves training a world model on the source domain and aligning it with the target domain to mitigate domain discrepancies. The approach uses a min-max value regularization technique to ensure the target critic does not overestimate values, and a target-inclined source model tuning to adjust the source model to the target domain. Experiments across various benchmarks such as Meta-World, RoboDesk, and DeepMind Control Suite demonstrate the effectiveness of CoWorld in improving offline RL performance.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel approach, CoWorld, which addresses the challenges of overfitting and value function overestimation in offline visual RL by leveraging an auxiliary online simulator.
- The methodology is well-explained, with clear figures and a detailed algorithm, making it easy to understand and replicate.
- The experiments are robust, demonstrating the effectiveness of the proposed method across various benchmarks, including Meta-World, RoboDesk, and DeepMind Control Suite.
- The paper provides a comprehensive evaluation, including ablation studies and a detailed analysis of the effectiveness of each component of the proposed method.
- The approach is innovative, using an auxiliary domain to mitigate overfitting and value function overestimation, which is a novel application of offline RL to the visual control domain.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a thorough discussion on related work, particularly in the area of transfer learning and domain adaptation, which are closely related to the proposed method.
- The computational complexity of the training phase in the auxiliary source domain is not addressed, which could limit the scalability of the proposed method.
- The paper does not provide sufficient details on the implementation of the online simulator, which is crucial for reproducibility and understanding the effectiveness of the method.
- The paper's claims of novelty are somewhat overstated, as the use of an auxiliary domain for offline RL is not entirely new, and the paper does not adequately distinguish its contributions from existing methods.
- The paper could benefit from more detailed explanations and visualizations, particularly in the experimental results and methodology sections, to enhance clarity and understanding.

**Questions:**
- How does the computational complexity of the training phase in the auxiliary source domain impact the scalability and practical applicability of the proposed method?
- Can you provide more details on the implementation of the online simulator, including its specifications and how it is integrated into the training process?
- How does the proposed method perform when the source domain and target domain are significantly different, such as in cross-domain scenarios?
- In the context of the min-max value regularization, how does the regularization weight (\(\alpha\)) affect the performance of the method?
- How is the source domain selected for the auxiliary domain, and what criteria are used to determine its suitability?
- Could you clarify the role and impact of the target-inclined source model tuning in the overall performance of the method?
- In the experimental results, how does the proposed method compare to other model-based offline RL methods, particularly in terms of computational efficiency and performance?
