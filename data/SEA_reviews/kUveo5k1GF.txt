 **Summary:**
The paper investigates the challenges of Equilibrium Propagation (EP) in neural networks, focusing on the asymmetry in Jacobians and the finite nudge amplitude. It proposes a novel homeostatic objective to minimize asymmetry and a method to estimate gradients using holomorphic EP, which does not require weight symmetry. The paper demonstrates these methods on datasets such as Fashion MNIST and CIFAR-10, showing improvements over traditional EP methods. However, the paper's claims about the necessity of weight symmetry and the practical implications of its methods on more complex datasets like ImageNet are questioned. The theoretical analysis is praised for its clarity and depth, but the empirical results are seen as insufficient to fully substantiate the claims.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, clear, and easy to follow, making complex concepts accessible to readers.
- The theoretical analysis is thorough, providing a solid foundation for the proposed methods.
- The paper introduces a novel method for estimating gradients in neural networks without requiring weight symmetry, which is a significant contribution to the field.
- The paper provides a clear exposition of the theoretical results, which are well-integrated with practical demonstrations.
- The empirical results, while not extensive, are convincing and support the theoretical claims.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper's claims about the necessity of weight symmetry are overstated and not adequately supported by empirical evidence.
- The empirical results are limited to small-scale datasets like Fashion MNIST and CIFAR-10, which may not adequately demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed methods on more complex datasets.
- There is a lack of comparison with other methods that do not require weight symmetry, which could provide a more robust evaluation of the proposed method's effectiveness.
- The paper does not sufficiently discuss the limitations of its work, particularly in terms of the practical implications of its findings.
- Some sections of the paper, such as the discussion on the practical implications of the findings, are not sufficiently detailed, which could limit the understanding of the paper's contributions.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors clarify the necessity of weight symmetry in their proposed method and provide empirical evidence to support this claim?
- How does the proposed method compare to other methods that do not require weight symmetry in terms of performance and computational efficiency?
- Could the authors provide more detailed empirical results on the effectiveness of the proposed method on larger, more complex datasets like ImageNet?
- What are the practical implications of the findings, particularly in terms of their applicability to real-world neural network architectures and training scenarios?
- How does the paper address the practical challenges associated with implementing the proposed methods, such as the need for additional computational resources or specialized hardware?
