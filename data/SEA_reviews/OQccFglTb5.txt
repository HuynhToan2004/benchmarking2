 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel watermarking method, FT-Shield, designed to protect images from unauthorized fine-tuning in text-to-image diffusion models. The method involves training a binary watermark detector using a combination of clean and watermarked images, along with generated images from the fine-tuned text-to-image model. The watermark is optimized to be quickly learned by the diffusion model during fine-tuning, ensuring its transfer to the generated images. The method's effectiveness is demonstrated through experiments that showcase its ability to detect watermarks even when the fine-tuning steps are limited. However, concerns were raised about the method's reliance on the specific fine-tuning method used by the offender and the potential for the watermark to be removed or altered by attackers.

**Strengths:**
- The paper addresses a significant and timely issue of protecting intellectual property rights in text-to-image diffusion models, which is crucial for the future development of this technology.
- The proposed method is novel and effective, demonstrating its ability to detect watermarks even with limited fine-tuning steps, which is a notable improvement over existing methods.
- The paper is well-organized, easy to follow, and includes comprehensive experiments that validate the effectiveness of the proposed method.
- The method's robustness against image corruptions is highlighted, showing its ability to maintain effectiveness in real-world scenarios.
- The paper includes ablation studies that provide insights into the method's performance under different conditions, which is valuable for understanding the method's limitations and potential improvements.

**Weaknesses:**
- The method's reliance on the specific fine-tuning method used by the offender could limit its effectiveness if the offender uses a different method.
- The method's performance under different watermark rates and the impact of watermark strength on detection accuracy are not thoroughly explored.
- The paper lacks a discussion on the potential negative societal impacts of the proposed method, which is an important consideration for ethical implications.
- The method's effectiveness in scenarios where the watermarked images are significantly different from the generated images is not adequately addressed, which could limit its applicability in real-world scenarios.
- The paper does not discuss the method's robustness against advanced attacks, which could be a significant limitation in practical applications.
- The method's transferability across different fine-tuning methods and its robustness against various types of corruptions are not thoroughly explored, which could limit its practical applicability.

**Questions:**
- How does the proposed method perform under different watermark rates, and what are the implications of varying watermark strengths on detection accuracy?
- Can the method be adapted to protect images in scenarios where the watermarked images are significantly different from the generated images?
- How does the method handle advanced attacks that aim to remove or alter the watermark?
- Could the authors provide more details on the training process of the watermark detector, particularly how it is trained using generated images from the fine-tuned text-to-image model?
- How does the method ensure that the watermark remains effective when the offender uses a different fine-tuning method?
- Could the authors discuss the potential negative societal impacts of the proposed method and how these impacts might be mitigated?
