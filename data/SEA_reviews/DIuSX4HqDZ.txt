 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel approach to abductive reasoning over knowledge graphs (KGs), focusing on generating logical hypotheses from observations. The authors employ a generative model trained using reinforcement learning to minimize the discrepancy between observations and conclusions drawn from generated hypotheses. This method is evaluated against several existing models, showing improved performance in some cases. The paper also explores the use of a transformer-based model for generating logical expressions from observations, which are then validated using a Jaccard index-based metric. Despite the innovative approach, concerns are raised about the clarity of the methodology, the fairness of the comparisons, and the generalizability of the results.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel task of abductive reasoning on knowledge graphs, which is a significant contribution to the field.
- The proposed method is well-motivated and provides a clear explanation of the challenges and solutions in the context of abductive reasoning over knowledge graphs.
- The methodology is well-articulated, with clear explanations of the method's components and their functions.
- The paper is well-written, making it accessible and easy to follow, with a comprehensive appendix that provides additional details and examples.
- The experimental results show that the proposed method outperforms existing models on some datasets, demonstrating the effectiveness of the approach.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks clarity in defining the task and its differences from other related tasks, which could lead to confusion about the specific contributions and applications of the proposed method.
- The evaluation of the method is limited to a small number of datasets, which may not sufficiently demonstrate the generalizability of the findings.
- The comparisons made in the paper are not fair, as they do not consider state-of-the-art methods or recent advancements in the field.
- The methodology section is not detailed enough, particularly in explaining the reinforcement learning process and the tokenization of hypotheses.
- The paper's focus on specific datasets and logical patterns may limit the applicability of the findings to more general knowledge graph reasoning tasks.
- The paper does not sufficiently discuss the limitations of the proposed method, which is crucial for understanding its scope and applicability.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors clarify the specific differences between the task of abductive reasoning on knowledge graphs and other related tasks, such as knowledge graph completion or logical query answering?
- How does the proposed method compare to state-of-the-art methods in terms of performance and computational efficiency?
- Could the authors provide more details on the tokenization process for hypotheses, particularly how it is adapted for different knowledge graphs?
- How does the method handle the complexity of logical hypotheses, especially when they involve multiple variables and relations?
- Could the authors provide more qualitative examples of generated hypotheses to better understand the method's outputs?
- How does the method ensure that the generated hypotheses are not overly simplistic or biased towards specific logical patterns?
- Can the authors discuss the potential limitations of the proposed method and how these might affect its applicability in real-world scenarios?
