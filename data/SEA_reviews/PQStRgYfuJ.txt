 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel approach to continual learning on expanding graphs by addressing the memory explosion problem through a framework called Parameter Decoupled Graph Neural Networks (PDGNNs) with Topology-aware Embedding Memory (TEM). This framework decouples trainable parameters from the computation ego-subgraph, thereby reducing memory complexity from O(nd^L) to O(n). The TEM module encodes topological information into compact vectors, which are stored in a memory buffer, facilitating memory replay. The proposed method also includes a coverage maximization sampling strategy to optimize the use of memory. Experiments demonstrate that the proposed method outperforms existing techniques in both class-incremental and task-incremental scenarios, showcasing its effectiveness and efficiency.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-organized, clearly written, and easy to follow, making it accessible to a broad audience.
- The proposed method is novel and provides a significant advancement in the field of continual learning on expanding graphs.
- The experimental results are robust, demonstrating the effectiveness of the proposed method across various datasets and scenarios.
- The paper includes a comprehensive discussion of related works, providing a thorough review of existing literature.
- The theoretical analysis and proofs are sound, contributing to the understanding and validation of the proposed method.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed discussion on the limitations of the proposed method, which is crucial for understanding its applicability and potential drawbacks.
- The comparison with other methods, particularly those that use more advanced GNN architectures like Graph Transformers, is insufficient.
- The paper does not discuss the scalability of the proposed method, which is a critical aspect for practical applications.
- The experimental setup and results are not convincingly superior to existing methods, particularly in terms of memory consumption and performance improvements.
- The paper does not adequately address the generalizability of the proposed method across different graph structures and sizes.
- The theoretical analysis and proofs, while sound, could benefit from a more detailed and rigorous presentation to enhance their clarity and accessibility.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors clarify the definitions and notations used in the paper, particularly those related to the topology-aware embedding and the coverage ratio?
- How does the proposed method compare with other advanced GNN architectures, such as Graph Transformers?
- What are the limitations of the proposed method, and under what conditions might it not be effective?
- Could the authors provide more details on the scalability of the proposed method, including its performance on larger and more complex graphs?
- How does the proposed method handle the scalability issues, especially in terms of computational complexity and memory requirements?
- Can the authors provide more evidence or theoretical proofs to support the claims made about the memory efficiency and performance improvements of the proposed method?
