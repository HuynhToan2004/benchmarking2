 **Summary:**
The paper investigates the self-explanation capabilities of large language models (LLMs) using a novel dataset, ArticulateRules, which includes few-shot text-based classification tasks. The dataset is designed to evaluate whether LLMs can provide accurate, high-level explanations of their internal processes by associating simple rules with natural language explanations. The study examines various models, including GPT-3 and GPT-4, and reveals that while GPT-4 shows some self-explanation capabilities, GPT-3 fails to provide accurate explanations even after fine-tuning. The paper also introduces a methodology for evaluating the faithfulness of LLM explanations, which could be useful for future research in this area.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel dataset, ArticulateRules, which includes adversarial inputs and multiple rule functions, providing a comprehensive evaluation of self-explanation capabilities in large language models (LLMs).
- The methodology for evaluating the faithfulness of LLM explanations is well-defined and could be useful for future research in this area.
- The paper provides a detailed analysis of the performance of different models on the dataset, including GPT-3 and GPT-4, which helps in understanding the capabilities and limitations of these models.
- The dataset and methodology are designed to be reproducible, enhancing the transparency and reliability of the research.
- The paper is well-written, making it accessible and easy to follow, which is crucial for a broad audience.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a thorough comparison with other models like Claude, Llama, and Falcon, which could provide a more comprehensive evaluation of the models' self-explanation capabilities.
- The dataset's size and complexity are limited, which might not adequately challenge the models or provide a comprehensive evaluation of their capabilities.
- The paper's reliance on GPT-4 for evaluating the accuracy of LLM explanations raises concerns about the objectivity and transparency of the evaluation process.
- The methodology for evaluating the faithfulness of LLM explanations is not sufficiently robust, as it does not adequately address the potential for model hallucination or the impact of different prompts on the model's behavior.
- The paper's focus on binary classification tasks might not fully capture the complexity and nuance of real-world applications, limiting the generalizability of the findings.

**Questions:**
- Could you elaborate on the specific reasons why GPT-3 fails to provide accurate self-explanations, even after fine-tuning?
- How does the size and complexity of the dataset impact the evaluation of the models' self-explanation capabilities?
- Could you discuss the potential implications of using different prompts for evaluating the faithfulness of LLM explanations?
- How does the paper address the issue of model hallucination in the evaluation of LLM explanations?
- Can you provide more details on the methodology used for evaluating the faithfulness of LLM explanations, particularly how this methodology ensures the accuracy and objectivity of the evaluations?
