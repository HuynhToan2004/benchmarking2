 **Summary:**
The paper investigates the convergence of deep residual networks to neural ordinary differential equations (ODEs) under specific conditions such as large depth, finite training time, and linear overparameterization. It establishes that these networks converge to a neural ODE that interpolates the training data, demonstrating the theoretical connection between deep residual networks and neural ODEs. The paper also discusses the implications of these findings, including the potential for improving training efficiency and understanding the implicit regularization effects in deep learning. Despite some concerns about the novelty and the generalizability of the results, the paper is well-written and contributes to the understanding of the implicit regularization effects in deep learning.

**Strengths:**
- The paper addresses an important and timely problem, investigating the convergence of deep residual networks to neural ODEs, which is a significant area of interest in the deep learning community.
- The theoretical results are well-articulated and supported by clear, rigorous proofs, contributing to the understanding of the implicit regularization effects in deep learning.
- The paper is well-written, making it accessible and easy to follow, with clear explanations of the theoretical results and their implications.
- The experimental results are convincing and support the theoretical claims, providing empirical evidence for the theoretical results.
- The paper introduces a novel approach to analyzing the implicit regularization effects in deep learning, which is a significant contribution to the field.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a clear discussion on the novelty and contributions compared to existing literature, particularly in relation to previous works that have explored similar topics.
- The assumptions made in the paper, such as the PL condition, are not thoroughly justified, and their practical implications are not clearly discussed.
- The experimental setup and results are somewhat limited, focusing primarily on synthetic data and not adequately addressing the performance of the models on real-world datasets.
- The paper could benefit from a more detailed discussion on the practical implications of the theoretical results, particularly in terms of their applicability to real-world scenarios.
- The paper could be improved by including more comprehensive discussions on the limitations of the results and the potential for future research directions.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors clarify the relationship between the results presented in this paper and those of previous works, particularly in terms of novelty and contributions?
- How do the assumptions made in the paper, such as the PL condition, affect the practical applicability of the results?
- Can the authors provide more detailed explanations or examples to illustrate the practical implications of the theoretical results?
- In terms of future research, what directions do the authors see as most promising for extending or applying the results presented in this paper?
