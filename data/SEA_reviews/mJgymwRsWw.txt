 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel active probabilistic drug discovery (APDD) method aimed at enhancing the efficiency and accuracy of drug discovery by integrating intelligent computing and automation into end-to-end operations. APDD comprises three primary steps: probabilistic clustering, selective docking, and active wet-experiment. The method is evaluated using datasets from DUD-E and LIT-PCBA, showing significant reductions in computational and wet experimental costs, while maintaining comparable screening accuracy. However, the paper is criticized for its lack of novelty, insufficient literature review, and inadequate comparison with existing methods. The method's scalability and its applicability to larger datasets are also questioned.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel method for drug discovery that integrates intelligent computing and automation into end-to-end operations, which is a promising approach for enhancing the efficiency and effectiveness of drug discovery.
- The method is evaluated using datasets from DUD-E and LIT-PCBA, demonstrating significant reductions in computational and wet experimental costs while maintaining comparable screening accuracy.
- The paper is well-written and easy to follow, making it accessible to a broad audience.
- The proposed method shows potential for reducing the number of docking and wet experiments needed to achieve a certain recall rate, which is crucial for drug discovery applications.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks novelty as the proposed method appears to be a combination of existing techniques without significant advancement or innovation.
- The literature review is insufficient, with many related works missing, which limits the contextual understanding of the proposed method.
- The method's scalability and applicability to larger datasets are questionable, as the paper primarily focuses on small datasets and does not address how the method would perform on larger, more complex datasets.
- The paper does not adequately compare its method with existing methods, which makes it difficult to assess its relative performance and advantages.
- There are several grammatical and formatting errors throughout the paper, which detract from its professionalism and readability.
- The paper does not sufficiently discuss the limitations and potential negative societal impacts of the proposed method, which is crucial for ensuring ethical and responsible application.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors provide more details on how the proposed method is different from existing methods, particularly in terms of its novel contributions and advantages?
- How does the proposed method perform on larger, more complex datasets, and what steps are being taken to address scalability issues?
- Can the authors clarify the role and impact of the MPC algorithm in the method, particularly in terms of its contribution to the overall performance and efficiency?
- How does the method handle the issue of outliers in molecular datasets, and what steps are taken to mitigate their impact on the clustering and docking processes?
- Could the authors provide a more detailed discussion on the limitations and potential negative societal impacts of the proposed method, and how these are being addressed or mitigated?
