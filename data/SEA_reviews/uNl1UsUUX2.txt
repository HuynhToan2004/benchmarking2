 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel iterative training framework, Selective Knowledge Evolution (SKE), aimed at enhancing generalization in deep neural networks trained on small datasets. SKE employs a data-aware dynamic masking scheme to adaptively eliminate redundant connections, which in turn increases the model's capacity for further learning through random weight reinitialization. This method is inspired by the process of neurogenesis in the brain and is evaluated across various datasets, showing improvements in accuracy and robustness compared to existing methods. The approach is designed to address the challenges of training neural networks on small datasets, which is a significant issue in real-world applications where data availability is limited.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, easy to follow, and provides a clear motivation for the research.
- The proposed method is novel, well-explained, and effectively tackles the challenges of training neural networks on small datasets.
- The methodology is supported by extensive experiments and ablation studies, demonstrating the effectiveness of the proposed approach.
- The paper provides a clear and detailed explanation of the proposed method, including a comprehensive literature review and a clear distinction from related works.
- The authors have made their code available, enhancing the reproducibility and transparency of their research.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed discussion on the computational cost and time complexity of the proposed method compared to existing methods, which is crucial for understanding its practical applicability.
- There is a lack of detailed analysis of the scalability of the proposed method with varying dataset sizes, which is essential for demonstrating its effectiveness across different scenarios.
- The paper does not sufficiently discuss the limitations of the proposed method, which could help in understanding its applicability and potential drawbacks.
- The paper does not provide a clear comparison of the performance of the proposed method with other state-of-the-art methods, which is necessary for evaluating its effectiveness and efficiency.
- The paper could benefit from a more detailed discussion on the impact of the proposed method on the interpretability of the models, as dynamic reinitialization could potentially affect the interpretability of the models.
