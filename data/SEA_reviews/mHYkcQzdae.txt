 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel method for micro-expression recognition, leveraging a dual-stream neural network that integrates vertical attention mechanisms and facial position localization. The Continuous Vertical Attention (CVA) module is designed to model muscle movements, while the Facial Position Focalizer (FPF) uses Swin Transformer for spatial information. The methodology includes Action Unit (AU) embeddings and a dual-frame input to enhance recognition accuracy. The proposed model achieves state-of-the-art results on standard datasets like CASME II and SAMM, demonstrating improvements over existing methods. However, concerns were raised about the novelty of the approach, the limited evaluation on different datasets, and the lack of comprehensive ablation studies.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel method for micro-expression recognition, which is a significant and challenging task in the field of facial expression analysis.
- The use of Swin Transformer for facial position localization and the incorporation of vertical attention mechanisms are innovative approaches that enhance the model's ability to capture subtle facial movements.
- The proposed method achieves state-of-the-art results on standard datasets like CASME II and SAMM, demonstrating its effectiveness in real-world applications.
- The paper is well-structured and easy to follow, providing clear explanations of the methodology and its implementation.
- The methodology is supported by extensive experiments and ablation studies, which validate the effectiveness of the proposed components.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a comprehensive comparison with state-of-the-art methods, particularly in terms of computational efficiency and generalizability across different datasets.
- The novelty of the method is questioned, as the integration of AU embeddings and the use of a dual-frame input are not entirely novel.
- The paper does not provide sufficient details on the training process, such as the number of training epochs and the learning rate, which could affect the reproducibility and comparability of the results.
- There is a lack of visualization and detailed analysis of the model's attention maps, which could provide insights into the model's decision-making process and the importance of different facial features.
- The paper could benefit from a more thorough discussion on the limitations of the proposed method and its potential societal impacts.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors provide more details on the training process, such as the number of training epochs and the learning rate, to enhance the reproducibility and comparability of the results?
- How does the model perform on other datasets, particularly those with different characteristics or larger sample sizes?
- Could the authors provide visualizations or detailed analyses of the model's attention maps to better understand the model's decision-making process and the importance of different facial features?
- What are the specific challenges in micro-expression recognition that the proposed method addresses, and how does it compare to existing methods in terms of computational efficiency and generalizability?
- Could the authors discuss the potential societal impacts of their method, particularly in applications where privacy concerns might be raised?
