 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel Common Weakness Attack (CWA) for transfer-based adversarial attacks, leveraging the flatness of loss landscapes and proximity to local optima of models within an ensemble to enhance adversarial transferability. The CWA is composed of Sharpness Aware Minimization (SAM) and Cosine Similarity Encourager (CSE) to optimize these properties. Extensive experiments on image classification and object detection tasks demonstrate the effectiveness of CWA, showing improvements over existing methods. The theoretical analysis supports the proposed method, though concerns about computational efficiency and the necessity of ensemble methods remain.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, easy to follow, and presents a novel approach to transfer-based adversarial attacks using ensemble models.
- The proposed method is technically sound, with a clear motivation and theoretical analysis that supports the effectiveness of the approach.
- Extensive experiments are conducted, demonstrating the effectiveness of the proposed method across various tasks and datasets, including image classification and object detection.
- The paper provides a detailed theoretical analysis of the flatness of the loss landscape and the proximity of the local optima, which is crucial for improving adversarial transferability.
- The method is shown to be effective in improving the transferability of adversarial examples, which is a significant issue in the field of adversarial machine learning.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a comprehensive comparison with other state-of-the-art methods, particularly in terms of computational efficiency and the necessity of using ensemble methods.
- The experimental setup could be improved to include more diverse datasets and a broader range of models to enhance the generalizability of the results.
- There are some minor issues with the clarity and organization of the paper, such as unclear figures and a lack of detailed explanations in certain sections.
- The paper does not sufficiently address the limitations of the proposed method, particularly the potential for overfitting and the computational cost of the optimization process.
- The theoretical analysis could be strengthened by including more detailed proofs and a broader range of scenarios to ensure the robustness of the findings.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors provide a detailed comparison of the computational efficiency of the proposed method with other state-of-the-art methods, including the number of iterations required to achieve similar levels of transferability?
- How does the proposed method perform when using a different number of surrogate models, and what is the impact on the transferability of the adversarial examples?
- Could the authors clarify the use of the term "ensemble" in the context of the proposed method, and how it differs from traditional ensemble methods?
- Is there a possibility to incorporate the proposed method into other existing adversarial attack methods, and if so, how does this affect the computational efficiency and transferability?
- Can the authors provide a more detailed explanation of the experimental setup, including the choice of datasets and models, and the rationale behind the experimental design?
- How does the proposed method handle the potential for overfitting, and what measures have been taken to ensure that the optimization process does not lead to overly complex adversarial examples?
