 **Summary:**
The paper introduces LLM-Bar, a novel benchmark designed to assess the capabilities of LLM-based evaluators in discerning instruction-following outputs. It differentiates from existing benchmarks by manually curating 419 pairs of outputs, half adhering to instructions and the other half diverging, yet potentially misleading due to their superficial qualities. The authors test various LLM evaluators on this benchmark and propose new prompting strategies to improve their performance. The study highlights that even the most powerful LLM evaluators like GPT-4 exhibit significant room for improvement, demonstrating the need for continuous research in this area.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, with clear figures and tables that effectively convey the results and methodologies.
- The introduction of a new benchmark, LLM-Bar, for evaluating LLM-based evaluators is innovative and addresses a significant gap in the current evaluation landscape.
- The paper provides a comprehensive analysis of the performance of LLM evaluators and proposes new prompting strategies to improve their performance, which could be valuable for future research.
- The use of adversarial examples and the manual curation of the dataset add a layer of rigor and detail to the study.
- The paper includes a thorough evaluation of different LLM evaluators and prompting strategies, which provides a robust analysis of their strengths and weaknesses.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper's claims about the superiority of LLM-Bar over other benchmarks are not convincingly supported by the data provided.
- The manual curation of the dataset raises concerns about the reproducibility and scalability of the research.
- The paper does not adequately address the potential biases introduced by the manual curation process, which could affect the generalizability of the findings.
- The paper lacks a detailed discussion on the potential limitations and societal impacts of the research.
- The novelty of the work is questioned due to the similarities with existing benchmarks like LLMEval2, and the paper does not sufficiently distinguish its contributions from these prior works.
- The paper's reliance on proprietary models like GPT-4 and ChatGPT for evaluation raises concerns about the accessibility and reproducibility of the research.

**Questions:**
- Can you clarify the discrepancies in the performance of GPT-4 as depicted in Figure 4 and Table 2?
- How do you address the concerns regarding the reproducibility and scalability of the research, particularly given the manual curation of the dataset?
- What are the specific guidelines or criteria used by the authors for manual curation, and how do these ensure the quality and consistency of the dataset?
- Could you provide more details on the potential biases introduced by the manual curation process and how these were mitigated?
- How do you plan to make the dataset and the prompting strategies publicly available to ensure the reproducibility and accessibility of your research?
- Given the similarities with existing benchmarks like LLMEval2, how do you distinguish the contributions of your work from these prior studies?
