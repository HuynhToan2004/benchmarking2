 **Summary:**
The paper introduces Adaptive Feature Transfer (AFT), a novel method for transfer learning that leverages pre-trained features to enhance downstream model performance. AFT optimizes a regularization term that minimizes the mutual information between the pre-trained features and the downstream model, thereby encouraging the model to use only the most relevant features for prediction. The method is evaluated across various vision, language, and multi-modal datasets, demonstrating improvements over standard transfer learning and knowledge distillation methods. The approach is particularly notable for its ability to transfer features from multiple pre-trained models into a single downstream model, which is beneficial for scenarios where large pre-trained models are not feasible.

**Strengths:**
- The paper addresses an important and timely problem in transfer learning, focusing on the efficient use of pre-trained models, which is a significant area of interest.
- The proposed method, Adaptive Feature Transfer (AFT), is novel and well-explained, with a clear methodology and strong empirical results that outperform existing methods like B-Tuning and Knowledge Distillation (KD).
- The paper is well-written, making it accessible and easy to follow, which is crucial for a broad audience.
- The experiments are comprehensive, covering a wide range of datasets and models, including vision, language, and multi-modal tasks, which demonstrates the versatility and applicability of the proposed method.
- The use of a kernel formulation and mutual information to guide the transfer of pre-trained features is a novel and effective approach, enhancing the relevance and efficiency of the transferred features.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a thorough comparison with other state-of-the-art methods in transfer learning, such as Bayesian transfer learning, which could provide a more robust evaluation of the proposed method.
- There is a lack of detailed analysis on the computational efficiency of AFT compared to other methods, particularly in terms of memory and time complexity.
- The paper does not adequately address the scalability of AFT with larger pre-trained models, which could limit its applicability in more demanding scenarios.
- The method's effectiveness in scenarios with a large number of pre-trained models or in data-scarce settings is not explored, which could limit its practical utility.
- The paper does not provide a detailed discussion on the potential negative societal impacts of the proposed method, which could be a significant concern in some applications.

**Questions:**
- Can you provide a more detailed comparison with other state-of-the-art methods in transfer learning, particularly in terms of computational efficiency and performance on larger, more complex datasets?
- How does the performance of AFT scale with the size and complexity of the pre-trained models, and what are the practical limits for its application?
- Could you discuss the potential negative societal impacts of the proposed method, and how these might be mitigated or addressed in future work?
- How does the proposed method handle the transfer of features from models trained on different datasets or domains, especially in scenarios where the data distribution might be significantly different?
- Could you clarify the computational efficiency of AFT compared to other methods, particularly in terms of memory and time complexity?
