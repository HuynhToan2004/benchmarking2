 **Summary:**
The paper introduces Auto-Bench, an automated benchmarking framework that utilizes large language models (LLMs) to generate and evaluate visual question-answering (VQA) datasets. The system generates VQA data using GPT-4, which is then assessed by GPT-3.5 to provide both quantitative and qualitative evaluations. The dataset generated by Auto-Bench includes 28,500 human-verified and 3.5 million unfiltered question-answer-reasoning triplets, covering 4 primary abilities and 16 sub-abilities. The authors claim that this approach offers a scalable, labor-efficient, and comprehensive method for evaluating the alignment of VLMs with human intelligence and values. However, concerns are raised about the potential for overfitting and the reliance on GPT-4 for data generation, which may limit the dataset's generalizability and applicability.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces an innovative approach by using large language models (LLMs) to generate and evaluate visual question-answering (VQA) datasets, which is a significant advancement in the field.
- The dataset generated by Auto-Bench is extensive, covering 4 primary abilities and 16 sub-abilities, and is evaluated using both quantitative and qualitative methods, providing a comprehensive assessment of VLMs.
- The paper is well-written, clear, and easy to follow, making it accessible to a broad audience.
- The evaluation of VLMs using LLMs is a novel and promising direction for benchmarking, which could potentially enhance the evaluation of VLMs.
- The use of human verification and the comparison with other datasets such as VQAv2 and GQA demonstrates the effectiveness and quality of the generated dataset.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper does not sufficiently discuss the potential for overfitting due to the use of GPT-4 for data generation, which could limit the generalizability and applicability of the dataset.
- The reliance on GPT-4 for data generation raises concerns about the dataset's diversity and the potential for biases in the evaluation process.
- The evaluation methodology, which involves LLMs as judges, could lead to biased results due to the similarity between the training and evaluation data.
- The paper does not provide a detailed comparison with other datasets in terms of diversity and quality, which could help establish the superiority of the Auto-Bench dataset.
- There is a lack of discussion on the limitations of the proposed method, which could help readers understand the scope and applicability of the Auto-Bench framework.
- The paper could benefit from a more thorough discussion on the potential societal impacts of the generated dataset, particularly in terms of privacy and security concerns.

**Questions:**
- How does the paper address the potential for overfitting due to the use of GPT-4 for data generation?
- Could the authors provide more details on the diversity of the generated dataset and how it compares to other datasets in terms of quality and representation?
- How does the evaluation methodology using LLMs as judges ensure fairness and avoid biased results?
- Can the authors provide more details on the supervised fine-tuning process and how it impacts the performance of VLMs?
- How does the Auto-Bench framework ensure that the generated questions are of high quality and diverse enough to cover a wide range of scenarios?
- What are the specific steps taken to ensure that the generated data does not contain biases or inappropriate content?
- How does the Auto-Bench framework handle scenarios where the LLM may not be able to provide accurate evaluations, and what measures are in place to mitigate this risk?
