 **Summary:**
The paper investigates the weight decay mechanism in deep learning, proposing a novel approach termed layer-wise pre-weight decay (LPWD) to address issues such as the delay defect and distribution distortion caused by traditional weight decay. The authors introduce RWD and PWD to modify the weight decay process, focusing on real-time weight decay and pre-weight decay, respectively. The LPWD method adjusts the decay rate based on layer index to enhance model robustness. The paper presents a theoretical analysis and extensive experiments, including on the CIFAR-10 and CIFAR-100 datasets, demonstrating improvements in generalization performance. However, the paper has been critiqued for its lack of clarity in mathematical notation, insufficient experimental validation on larger, more complex datasets, and potential over-reliance on specific model architectures.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel approach to weight decay in deep learning, specifically focusing on the delay defect and distribution distortion caused by traditional weight decay.
- The authors propose a layer-wise pre-weight decay (LPWD) method, which adjusts the decay rate based on layer index, enhancing model robustness.
- The paper provides a theoretical analysis and extensive experiments, including on the CIFAR-10 and CIFAR-100 datasets, demonstrating improvements in generalization performance.
- The authors have conducted a comprehensive literature review, and the paper is well-organized, making it easy to follow.
- The proposed method is simple and effective, with significant improvements in generalization performance.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks clarity in mathematical notation and explanation, particularly in the equations and their interpretations.
- The experimental validation is insufficient, focusing only on the CIFAR-10 and CIFAR-100 datasets and not including larger, more complex datasets like ImageNet.
- The paper does not provide sufficient empirical evidence to support the claims made about the delay defect and distribution distortion caused by traditional weight decay.
- The paper primarily uses tiny models, and it is unclear whether the proposed method would be effective with larger, more complex models.
- The paper does not adequately address the potential negative societal impacts of the proposed method.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors clarify the mathematical notation and provide a clearer explanation of the equations and their interpretations?
- Why was the decision made to use tiny models for the experiments, and what are the implications of this choice for the generalizability of the results?
- How does the proposed method perform with larger, more complex models like those used in large-scale datasets such as ImageNet?
- Could the authors provide more detailed empirical evidence to support the claims made about the delay defect and distribution distortion caused by traditional weight decay?
- What are the specific advantages of the proposed method over traditional weight decay, and how does it compare in terms of computational efficiency and resource requirements?
- Could the authors discuss the potential negative societal impacts of the proposed method and how these might be mitigated or addressed?
