 **Summary:**
The paper introduces SEVAL, a method designed to address class imbalance in semi-supervised learning (SSL) by refining pseudo-labels and adjusting class-specific thresholds. SEVAL operates by optimizing logit offsets and thresholds to enhance the accuracy of pseudo-labels, thereby improving SSL performance. It is evaluated on several benchmarks, showing promising results, but concerns are raised about its novelty and the strength of its experimental validations. The method is criticized for its lack of originality in its approach and the clarity of its experimental setups, particularly in how it handles class imbalance in SSL scenarios.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written and easy to follow, with clear explanations of the methodologies and results.
- The proposed method, SEVAL, is simple and effective, with improvements over existing methods in both pseudo-label refinement and threshold adjustment.
- The method's performance is validated across several benchmarks, demonstrating its effectiveness in handling class imbalance in SSL.
- The method's design is intuitive and well-motivated, with theoretical justifications provided for the use of logit adjustment and class-specific thresholds.
- The paper provides a comprehensive evaluation of the method, including ablation studies and comparisons with state-of-the-art methods.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a thorough discussion on related works, particularly in the area of pseudo-label refinement and threshold adjustment, which could enhance the understanding of the proposed method's contributions.
- The experimental setup and results are not clearly presented, making it difficult to assess the method's effectiveness and to compare it with other methods.
- There is a lack of clarity in how the method handles class imbalance in SSL, and the paper does not adequately compare its approach with existing methods that address similar issues.
- The paper does not provide sufficient details on the experimental setup, such as the number of samples used in each dataset and the specifics of the model architectures and hyperparameters.
- The method's reliance on a validation dataset for threshold adjustment and pseudo-label refinement may limit its applicability in scenarios where such data is not available or is limited.
- The paper does not sufficiently discuss the limitations of the proposed method, which could help in understanding its scope and applicability.

**Questions:**
- Could you clarify the experimental setup, particularly how the method was applied to the CIFAR-100 dataset and how the results were validated?
- How does the method handle class imbalance in SSL, and what are the specific advantages of using logit adjustment and class-specific thresholds over other existing methods?
- Can you provide more details on the experimental setup, such as the number of samples used in each dataset and the specifics of the model architectures and hyperparameters?
- How does the method perform when applied to other datasets or models, and what are the implications of these variations on the method's effectiveness?
- Could you discuss the limitations of the proposed method and how these might affect its practical application?
