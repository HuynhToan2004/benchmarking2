 **Summary:**
The paper investigates the generalization of overparameterized neural networks using a univariate two-layer ReLU network trained with a minimum L2 norm constraint. It focuses on the tempered overfitting behavior of such networks, particularly in the context of L1 loss, and contrasts this with the catastrophic overfitting observed with L2 loss or when averaging over the training set. The authors provide theoretical insights into the behavior of these networks under different loss functions and noise conditions, comparing the results to previous studies. Despite the paper's theoretical rigor, it is noted for its simplicity and the clarity of its presentation, which aids in understanding complex concepts.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written and provides a clear, concise explanation of the results, making it accessible to a broad audience.
- It offers a rigorous analysis of the overfitting behavior of regression with minimum norm (L2 of weights), focusing on univariate two-layer ReLU networks.
- The paper demonstrates the tempered overfitting behavior of such networks with respect to L1 loss, and contrasts this with the catastrophic overfitting observed with L2 loss or when taking an expectation over the training set.
- The theoretical results are supported by rigorous proofs, and the paper provides a detailed discussion of the limitations of its approach.
- The paper is notable for its simplicity and clarity, which makes it easier to understand complex concepts related to neural network generalization.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper's contribution is seen as somewhat limited due to its focus on a simplified setting, specifically a univariate two-layer ReLU network, which might not generalize well to more complex or practical scenarios.
- The paper's analysis is restricted to a specific loss function (L1 loss) and does not consider other loss functions such as MSE, which could provide a more comprehensive understanding of the network's behavior.
- The paper's results are not surprising, as similar phenomena have been observed in previous works, which might diminish the perceived novelty of the findings.
- The paper's theoretical results are limited to the L2 norm of the weights, which might not capture the full complexity of the network's behavior.
- The paper does not adequately discuss the implications of its findings for practical applications, which could limit its relevance to real-world problems.

- Could the authors discuss the implications of their findings for practical applications, particularly in terms of how the insights from this study could be used to improve the performance of neural networks in real-world settings?
