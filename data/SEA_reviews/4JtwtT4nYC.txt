 **Summary:**
The paper presents a novel approach to multi-task reinforcement learning (MTRL) by integrating task-specific embeddings and task-aware prioritized experience replay. The proposed method aims to address the task performance imbalance problem by preserving unique features of each task and prioritizing training across tasks. The approach is evaluated on the Meta-World benchmark, showing state-of-the-art average success rates and stable performance across tasks. Despite these strengths, the paper suffers from issues such as unclear writing, insufficient explanation of key components, and a lack of comprehensive experimental validation. The method's scalability and generalization to other benchmarks are also questioned, and the paper does not adequately address the potential societal impacts of its work.

**Strengths:**
- The paper addresses the important and challenging task performance imbalance problem in multi-task reinforcement learning (MTRL), which is crucial for practical applications.
- The proposed method is simple yet effective, and the paper is well-written, making it easy to follow.
- The experiments are solid, with results demonstrating the effectiveness of the proposed method in achieving state-of-the-art average success rates on the Meta-World benchmark.
- The paper includes a comprehensive ablation study, which provides insights into the effectiveness of the proposed method's components.
- The approach is novel, integrating task-specific embeddings and task-aware prioritized experience replay, which are not commonly used in MTRL.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a clear explanation of how the unique feature is determined for each task and how it is trained using triplet loss.
- There is insufficient discussion on the limitations of the proposed method, such as its scalability and generalization to other benchmarks.
- The paper does not adequately address the potential societal impacts of its work, which is a significant omission.
- The writing quality of the paper is poor, with several grammatical errors and unclear explanations, particularly in the methodology section.
- The paper does not provide sufficient evidence to support the claims made about the performance imbalance problem, and the results are not convincingly superior to existing methods.
- The paper lacks a detailed discussion on the relationship between the proposed method and other existing methods, such as those mentioned in related works.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors clarify how the unique feature is determined for each task and how it is trained using triplet loss?
- How does the proposed method compare to other methods that use shared-unique features in MTRL, such as those mentioned in related works?
- Can the authors provide more detailed explanations for the poor performance of the peg-insert-side task in the MT10-rand environment?
- How does the proposed method address the task performance imbalance problem more effectively than existing methods, especially in the context of the MT10-rand environment?
- Could the authors discuss the potential societal impacts of their work and how they plan to address these in future research?
- Why were the results of the MT50-rand environment not included in the paper, and what were the reasons for not including these results in the main text?
