 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel approach to enhancing the modeling of temporal signals in neural fields by incorporating residual layers and low-rank parameterization. This method, termed ResFields, is designed to increase the capacity of MLPs without expanding their architecture, thereby improving generalization capabilities and reducing computational costs. The approach is demonstrated across various tasks, including 2D video approximation, temporal SDFs, dynamic NeRF reconstruction, and 3D scene flow, showing significant improvements over existing methods. The paper is commended for its clarity, thorough experimental validation, and potential for practical applications in fields like computer vision and computer graphics.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, clear, and easy to follow, with detailed explanations of the proposed method and its implementation.
- The method is simple, versatile, and can be applied to various MLP-based methods for spatiotemporal signals, demonstrating broad applicability.
- Extensive experiments are conducted across various tasks, including video approximation, temporal SDF, dynamic NeRF reconstruction, and 3D scene flow, showing consistent improvements over baseline methods.
- The use of low-rank parameterization to reduce the number of trainable parameters is a novel and effective approach, enhancing the generalization capabilities of the model.
- The paper provides a comprehensive analysis of the properties of the proposed method and demonstrates its effectiveness in capturing dynamic 3D scenes from sparse RGBD cameras of a lightweight capture system.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a thorough analysis of the limitations of the proposed method, particularly in terms of its scalability and applicability to more complex or diverse datasets.
- The factorization technique used in the method is not well-explained, which could hinder the understanding of the model's design choices and their impact on performance.
- There is a lack of comparison with other related methods that use residual connections in neural fields, which could provide a clearer context for the novelty and effectiveness of the proposed method.
- The paper does not discuss potential societal impacts of the work, which could be a significant limitation for some readers.
- Some technical details, such as the choice of factorization rank and the effects of varying this parameter, are not thoroughly explored, which could affect the reproducibility and generalizability of the results.

**Questions:**
- Can the proposed method be applied to video generation tasks, and how might it perform in such applications?
- How does the method compare in terms of computational efficiency and performance with other methods that use residual connections in neural fields?
- Is there a possibility to apply the method to other types of neural fields, such as those used in inverse problems?
- Could the authors provide more details on the factorization technique used and its impact on the model's performance and generalization?
- How does the method handle the trade-off between generalization and capacity, and what factors influence this trade-off?
- Could the authors provide a more detailed explanation of the experimental setup and results, particularly in the supplementary materials, to enhance the clarity and reproducibility of the results?
