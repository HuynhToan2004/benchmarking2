 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel dynamic sparse training method called Guided Stochastic Exploration (GSE), which maintains sparsity throughout training by randomly sampling a subset of connections for gradient computation. GSE is designed to improve the accuracy of existing methods like RigL by growing connections with the largest gradients from this subset. The method is tested on various datasets, including CIFAR and ImageNet, and is shown to outperform other sparse training methods in terms of accuracy and FLOPs. The authors also explore the impact of different subset sampling distributions and demonstrate that GSE can be applied to different architectures like CNNs and ViTs, achieving state-of-the-art results.
