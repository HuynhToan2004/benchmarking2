 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a method for training a Concept Question-Answering (Concept-QA) network using pseudo-labels derived from both GPT and CLIP to address the limitations of Variational Information Pursuit (V-IP) in large-scale datasets. The proposed method leverages GPT to generate semantic concepts as queries and CLIP to evaluate their presence in images, thereby providing binary answers. This approach allows for the generation of interpretable query chains that are shorter and more accurate than those obtained using CLIP-based answering systems. The paper also discusses the challenges of interpreting CLIP's continuous-valued answers and proposes a new method to improve the interpretability of V-IP in image classification tasks.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, easy to follow, and provides a clear motivation for the research.
- The proposed method is novel, using a Concept Question-Answering (Concept-QA) network to address the interpretability issues in Variational Information Pursuit (V-IP) by leveraging GPT and CLIP for pseudo-labeling.
- The experiments conducted are thorough, demonstrating the effectiveness of the proposed method in improving the interpretability of V-IP.
- The paper is well-structured, with a clear presentation of the methodology and results.
- The methodology is simple yet effective, and the paper provides a comprehensive evaluation of the proposed method.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed discussion on the limitations of the proposed method, particularly in terms of its scalability and generalizability.
- The method's dependence on GPT and CLIP for generating pseudo-labels raises concerns about the reliability and robustness of the method, especially in scenarios where these models might fail.
- There is a lack of comparison with other existing methods, which could have provided a more comprehensive evaluation of the proposed method.
- The paper does not adequately address the computational cost and efficiency of the proposed method, especially in large-scale applications.
- The paper could benefit from a more detailed analysis of the interpretability of the V-IP method and a more thorough discussion on the impact of the Concept-QA network on the overall interpretability of the V-IP framework.

**Questions:**
- How does the proposed method handle the issue of GPT and CLIP potentially providing incorrect or misleading pseudo-labels?
- Can the authors provide more detailed explanations on how the Concept-QA network is trained and how it contributes to the overall interpretability of the V-IP method?
- How does the proposed method compare in terms of computational cost and efficiency with other existing methods, especially in large-scale applications?
- Is there a possibility to extend the method to other types of datasets or tasks beyond image classification?
- Can the authors provide a more detailed analysis of the interpretability of the V-IP method, particularly how the Concept-QA network affects the overall interpretability?
