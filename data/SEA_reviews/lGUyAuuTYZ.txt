 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel training framework for binary activation neural networks (BANNs) that combines elements of spiking neural networks (SNNs) and binary neural networks (BNNs). The framework utilizes a Hoyer regularizer to train the activation thresholds and achieve sparsity in the network. This approach is claimed to improve the accuracy-FLOPs tradeoff over existing methods, including SNNs and BNNs. The paper also proposes a new Hoyer threshold layer and a Hoyer regularized training method, which are evaluated on various image recognition tasks. The framework's performance is compared against state-of-the-art methods, showing improvements in accuracy and energy efficiency. However, the paper has been critiqued for its limited novelty and the lack of a clear justification for its design choices.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel training framework for binary activation neural networks (BANNs) using a Hoyer regularizer, which is a significant contribution to the field.
- The proposed method achieves state-of-the-art (SOTA) accuracy-FLOPs tradeoff on image recognition tasks, demonstrating the effectiveness of the proposed approach.
- The paper is well-written, making it easy to follow and understand the proposed methods and their implications.
- The methodology is supported by extensive experiments, including both accuracy and energy efficiency evaluations, which are critical for assessing the practical applicability of the proposed framework.
- The paper provides a clear and detailed explanation of the proposed methods, including the use of a Hoyer threshold layer and a Hoyer regularized training method, which are crucial for understanding the proposed approach.

**Weaknesses:**
- The novelty of the proposed method is limited as it combines existing techniques such as Hoyer regularization and BANNs, which have been previously explored.
- The paper lacks a clear justification for the design choices, particularly the use of Hoyer regularization and the training of activation thresholds, which are crucial for the effectiveness of the proposed method.
- The paper's comparisons with other methods, such as AddNNs and BNNs, are not convincingly superior, and the results are not consistently better across different datasets.
- The paper's focus on binary activation neural networks (BANNs) is somewhat misleading as the proposed method is more closely related to spiking neural networks (SNNs) than to traditional BNNs.
- The paper lacks a thorough analysis of the computational complexity and energy efficiency of the proposed method, which are critical for understanding its practical applicability.
- The paper's claims of significant improvements in energy efficiency are not convincingly supported by the experimental results, which do not show a significant reduction in energy consumption compared to existing methods.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors provide a more detailed explanation of the design choices, particularly the use of Hoyer regularization and the training of activation thresholds?
- How does the proposed method compare to other methods that use similar regularization techniques, such as the L1-norm regularization used in Sparse-BNNs?
- In the energy efficiency evaluations, how were the results normalized to account for the differences in network sizes and architectures?
- Could the authors clarify the discrepancies in the energy efficiency results between different datasets and network architectures?
- How does the proposed method handle the trade-off between accuracy and energy efficiency, particularly in scenarios where the energy efficiency gains may not be significant?
- Can the authors provide more details on the computational complexity of the proposed method, particularly in terms of FLOPs and memory usage?
