 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel federated learning approach for multi-agent reinforcement learning (MARL) using the A2FC algorithm, which aggregates the critic model weights while keeping the actor model weights local to each agent. This method aims to address challenges such as communication overhead, privacy concerns, and the need for identical action spaces among agents. The approach is tested in a traffic signal control scenario, showing some improvements over existing methods. However, the paper is criticized for its limited evaluation, lack of comparison with more recent methods, and insufficient experimental details. The paper also lacks a comprehensive discussion on the scalability and generalizability of the proposed method.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-organized and clearly written, making it easy to follow.
- The proposed method, A2FC, is novel and innovative, particularly in its approach to aggregating only the critic model weights while keeping the actor model weights local.
- The paper addresses significant issues in MARL, such as communication overhead, privacy concerns, and the need for identical action spaces.
- The experimental results demonstrate the effectiveness of the A2FC algorithm, showing improvements over existing methods in the traffic signal control scenario.

**Weaknesses:**
- The evaluation is limited to a single scenario, which may not adequately demonstrate the generalizability or robustness of the proposed method.
- The paper lacks a thorough comparison with recent methods, particularly in terms of performance and scalability.
- There is a lack of detailed experimental information, including the network architecture, learning rate schedules, and hyperparameters used in the experiments.
- The paper does not adequately address the scalability of the A2FC algorithm to more complex or larger-scale environments.
- The paper does not sufficiently discuss the limitations of the proposed method, such as the potential for suboptimal solutions due to local actor training.
- The paper does not provide sufficient ablation studies or theoretical guarantees to support the claims made about the advantages of the A2FC algorithm.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors provide more details on the network architecture, learning rate schedules, and hyperparameters used in the experiments?
- How does the A2FC algorithm perform in more complex or larger-scale environments, and what are the potential challenges or limitations in such scenarios?
- Could the authors provide additional experimental results or comparisons with other methods to further validate the effectiveness of the A2FC algorithm?
- How does the A2FC algorithm handle scenarios where agents have different action spaces or different reward functions?
- Could the authors clarify the definitions and roles of different parameters used in the A2FC algorithm, such as the value of T and the learning rates for the actor and critic models?
- How does the A2FC algorithm ensure the privacy of agents' data, and what measures are in place to prevent information leakage?
