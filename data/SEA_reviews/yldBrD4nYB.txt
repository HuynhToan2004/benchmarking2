 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel variant of Variational Autoencoder (VAE) named Class-Informed VAE (CI-VAE), which integrates a linear discriminator layer in the latent space to enhance class separability. This modification aims to improve the linear separability of classes within the latent space, facilitating class-specific data generation and interpolation. The CI-VAE is evaluated on the MNIST dataset, demonstrating superior performance in linear separability and data generation compared to traditional VAEs. However, the paper's novelty is questioned due to its similarity to existing methods, and the evaluation is limited to one dataset, raising concerns about the generalizability of the findings.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, clear, and easy to follow, making complex concepts accessible to readers.
- The proposed CI-VAE model is innovative, particularly in its application of a linear discriminator to ensure class separability in the latent space, which is a novel approach.
- The methodology is simple and effective, with the inclusion of a linear discriminator layer in the latent space enhancing the model's ability to generate class-specific data.
- The paper provides a detailed explanation of the CI-VAE model, its components, and their functions, which aids in understanding the proposed methodology.
- The experimental results demonstrate the effectiveness of the CI-VAE in enhancing class separability and linear separability, showing improvements over traditional VAEs.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a thorough comparison with existing methods, particularly those that also focus on class separability in the latent space. This limits the understanding of how the proposed method performs relative to existing solutions.
- The evaluation is limited to one dataset (MNIST), which may not adequately demonstrate the generalizability of the findings.
- The novelty of the paper is questioned due to similarities with existing methods, particularly those that use a linear discriminator for class separability.
- The paper does not provide sufficient evidence to support claims of superior performance, such as the claim of better linear separability, which is not substantiated by the results presented.
- There are concerns about the clarity and presentation of the paper, with some sections being difficult to understand, and the figures not being of high quality.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors provide a more detailed comparison with existing methods that also focus on class separability in the latent space?
- How does the performance of the CI-VAE compare when evaluated on other datasets, such as CIFAR-10 or ImageNet?
- Could the authors provide more evidence to support the claims of superior performance, particularly in terms of linear separability and class-specific data generation?
- Is there a plan to release the code for the CI-VAE to facilitate reproducibility and further research by the community?
- How does the CI-VAE handle potential issues such as discontinuities or holes in the latent space?
- Can the authors clarify the discrepancies in the results presented, such as the lower MAE in CI-VAE compared to VAE despite the inclusion of an additional loss term?
