 **Summary:**
The paper introduces CathSim, an open-source simulator for endovascular intervention, designed to facilitate rapid development and testing of learning algorithms. The simulator models the aorta as a collection of convex hulls, guidewires as rigid segments, and blood as an incompressible Newtonian fluid. It also includes an expert navigation network for autonomous catheterization, validated against real robots and human experts. The simulator is intended to enhance research in autonomous catheterization by providing a realistic and efficient training environment. However, the paper's novelty is questioned due to its reliance on existing simulator components and its limited comparison with other simulators. The paper's contribution is seen as primarily in the development of the simulator, rather than in the machine learning algorithms used.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces an open-source simulator, CathSim, which is a significant contribution to the community and addresses the need for more open-source simulators in the field.
- The simulator is designed for real-time performance, which is crucial for rapid development and testing of learning algorithms.
- The paper is well-written, clear, and easy to follow, making it accessible to a broad audience.
- The inclusion of an expert navigation network and the use of an open-source simulator are novel contributions that could potentially accelerate research in the autonomous catheterization field.
- The paper provides a detailed description of the simulator and its components, which could be beneficial for those looking to use or develop similar systems.

**Weaknesses:**
- The novelty of the paper is limited as it primarily combines existing simulator components without significant innovation.
- The comparison with other simulators is insufficient, making it difficult to evaluate the performance of CathSim against state-of-the-art simulators.
- The paper lacks a comprehensive discussion on the limitations of the simulator, particularly in terms of its realism and applicability to real-world scenarios.
- The paper's contribution is seen as primarily in the development of the simulator, rather than in the machine learning algorithms used, which may limit its impact on the field.
- The paper does not adequately address the realism of the simulator, particularly in terms of the simplification of the aorta model and the use of rigid bodies, which could affect the accuracy of the simulations.
- The paper's claims of superior performance over human operators are not convincingly supported by the data provided.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors provide more details on the differences between CathSim and other simulators, particularly in terms of performance and functionality?
- How does the simulator handle the deformation of the aorta, and what are the implications of this simplification on the realism of the simulations?
- What are the specific advantages of using CathSim over other simulators, and how does it compare in terms of realism and applicability to real-world scenarios?
- Could the authors provide more detailed results on the performance of the expert navigation network, particularly in comparison to other algorithms and human operators?
- How does the simulator handle the interaction between the guidewire and the aorta, and what are the implications of this interaction on the realism of the simulations?
