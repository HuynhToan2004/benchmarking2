 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel approach to classifier-free guidance (CFG) in diffusion models, focusing on the second-order Taylor expansion of ICFG. This method is proposed to enhance the balance between fidelity and diversity in generated samples, particularly when the condition space is continuous. The authors demonstrate that CFG is a first-order Taylor expansion of ICFG, and provide a theoretical analysis of the convergence of ICFG's Taylor expansion. The paper also includes experiments on the MS-COCO dataset, showing improved performance over CFG in certain scenarios. However, concerns were raised about the clarity of the paper, particularly in explaining the mathematical notations and the assumptions made.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written and easy to follow, making complex concepts accessible.
- The proposed method is innovative, particularly in the use of second-order Taylor expansion to improve the balance between fidelity and diversity in generated samples.
- The theoretical analysis is sound, providing a solid foundation for the proposed method.
- The paper demonstrates improved performance over the baseline method, CFG, as shown in experiments.
- The use of a structured continuous condition space enhances the performance of Stable Diffusion, as evidenced by the experimental results.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks clarity in some sections, particularly in the mathematical notations and assumptions, which could confuse readers.
- The paper does not provide sufficient empirical evidence to support the claim that the proposed method consistently improves the balance between fidelity and diversity across different datasets.
- The paper does not compare the proposed method with other state-of-the-art diffusion models, which limits the understanding of its performance relative to the current state of the art.
- The paper does not discuss the limitations of the proposed method, which is crucial for understanding its applicability and potential drawbacks.
- The paper's experimental setup is limited to the MS-COCO dataset, and it would be beneficial to see results on other datasets such as FFHQ or ImageNet to validate the generalizability of the findings.
- The paper lacks a detailed discussion on the computational complexity of the proposed method, which is crucial for practical implementation.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors provide more detailed explanations for the mathematical notations and assumptions used in the paper?
- How does the proposed method perform when applied to other datasets beyond MS-COCO?
- Can the authors provide more empirical evidence to support the claim that the proposed method consistently improves the balance between fidelity and diversity across different datasets?
- How does the proposed method compare with other state-of-the-art diffusion models in terms of performance?
- What are the potential limitations of the proposed method, and how might these limitations affect its practical application?
- Can the authors provide more details on the computational complexity of the proposed method, including the time and memory requirements for training and inference?
