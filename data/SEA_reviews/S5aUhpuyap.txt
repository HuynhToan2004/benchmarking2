 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel neural circuit model inspired by diffusion models (DMs), designed to represent and manipulate complex priors and perform flexible inference. The model utilizes a recurrent network with dendritic nonlinearities, which can implicitly represent prior distributions over latent variables. The authors demonstrate how this architecture can sample from low-dimensional nonlinear manifolds and multimodal posteriors, showing its potential for probabilistic inference in high-dimensional spaces. The model's flexibility allows it to adapt to various tasks by integrating sensory and contextual information, and it provides a theoretical framework for understanding how the brain represents and uses structured priors.

**Strengths:**
- The paper presents a novel neural circuit model that combines dendritic nonlinearities optimized for denoising with stochastic somatic integration, inspired by diffusion models (DMs). This model can implicitly represent priors over latent variables and combine them with sensory and contextual sources of information to encode task-specific posteriors.
- The paper is well-written, clear, and easy to follow, with a strong focus on the implementation of the model and the experimental validation of its properties.
- The authors demonstrate that their model can sample from low-dimensional nonlinear manifolds and multimodal posteriors, which is a significant contribution to the field of neural computation.
- The paper introduces a new framework for circuit-level representation of probabilistic information, which facilitates flexible inference and provides a novel approach to understanding how the brain represents and uses structured priors.
- The model's architecture is flexible, allowing for the integration of various sources of information, including sensory and top-down contextual information, which enhances its applicability across different tasks.
- The paper is well-motivated, with a clear introduction of the problem and a well-defined objective, and the methodology is sound and well-explained.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed discussion on the limitations of the model, which is crucial for understanding its scope and applicability.
- The biological plausibility of the model is questionable, as it does not fully adhere to the biological constraints of neural circuits, such as the absence of all-to-all connectivity and the use of ReLU nonlinearities which are not biologically plausible.
- The paper does not provide a clear explanation of how the model's parameters are optimized or how the model is trained, which is essential for reproducibility and understanding the model's performance.
- The paper lacks a thorough comparison with other models, which makes it difficult to assess the model's relative performance and effectiveness.
- The paper does not discuss the implications of the model's architecture on computational complexity, which is a significant concern for large-scale applications.
- The paper does not provide a detailed analysis of the computational efficiency of the model, which is crucial for understanding its practical applicability.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors provide a more detailed discussion on the limitations of the model and how these limitations affect its applicability and performance?
- How does the model's performance compare to other models in terms of computational efficiency and biological plausibility?
- Could the authors clarify how the model's parameters are optimized and how the model is trained, including any assumptions made during the training process?
- How does the model's architecture influence the computational complexity, and what are the implications of this for large-scale applications?
- Can the authors provide more details on how the model's performance is evaluated, including a comparison with other models and a discussion of the metrics used to assess its effectiveness?
- How does the model's performance scale with the size and complexity of the input data, and what are the practical implications of this for real-world applications?
