 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel unsupervised method for implicit alignment of sequence data with low-dimensional dynamics, which allows for the reuse of trained generative models. This approach is particularly relevant for neuroscience applications where large-scale inference models are used to extract latent representations from high-dimensional neural recordings. The method is validated on both synthetic and real data, demonstrating its effectiveness in aligning neural time series data and improving the performance of pre-trained models. The paper also discusses the challenges of training models from scratch and the benefits of reusing pre-trained models, which is crucial in scenarios where data is limited or complex.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, with clear explanations of the proposed method and its applications, particularly in neuroscience.
- The experimental results are convincing, showing that the proposed method outperforms other baselines in terms of reconstruction and forecasting performance.
- The paper introduces a novel unsupervised method for implicit alignment of sequence data with low-dimensional dynamics, which is significant for neuroscience applications where large-scale inference models are used to extract latent representations from high-dimensional neural recordings.
- The paper demonstrates the method's ability to handle different recording modalities and its effectiveness in real-world scenarios, such as aligning neural recordings from different monkeys.
- The use of synthetic data to validate the method's performance and the comparison with other existing methods like ERDiff and NoMAD provide a robust evaluation of the proposed approach.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed discussion on the limitations of the proposed method, which could include the assumptions made and their implications, the sensitivity of the method to hyperparameters, and the generalizability of the findings to other datasets or tasks.
- The paper could benefit from more extensive evaluations, such as using different datasets and comparing the results with other methods like Procrustes.
- There is a lack of clarity in the presentation of the method, particularly in the description of the alignment function and the training details, which could be improved for better reproducibility.
- The paper could provide more details on the computational costs of the proposed method compared to other methods, especially in terms of training and inference times.
- The paper does not adequately address the potential societal impacts of the research, which is a significant omission in the ethical considerations section.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors provide a more detailed discussion on the limitations of the proposed method, including the assumptions made and their implications, the sensitivity of the method to hyperparameters, and the generalizability of the findings to other datasets or tasks?
- How does the proposed method compare in terms of computational costs, such as training and inference times, to other methods like ERDiff and NoMAD?
- Can the authors clarify the alignment function and the training details to improve reproducibility and understanding of the method?
- How does the proposed method perform in scenarios where the latent dynamics are not exactly the same across datasets?
- Could the authors provide more details on the ethical considerations and potential societal impacts of the research?
