 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel auxiliary self-supervised learning (SSL) module, Transition-Informed Multi-Agent Representations (TIMAR), designed to enhance the performance and data efficiency of multi-agent reinforcement learning (MARL) algorithms. TIMAR leverages a joint transition model to predict future transitions based on sequential observations and actions, allowing agents to infer the latent state of the system and consider the influences of others. This module is integrated with existing MARL algorithms such as QMIX and MAT, showing improved performance and data efficiency in various MARL environments. The paper also includes comprehensive experimental evaluations across different MARL environments, demonstrating the effectiveness of TIMAR.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, clear, and easy to follow, with a clear presentation of the proposed method and its implementation.
- The TIMAR method is novel and well-motivated, effectively addressing the issue of data efficiency in MARL by learning from transitions.
- The paper includes comprehensive experimental evaluations, comparing TIMAR with multiple state-of-the-art MARL algorithms, demonstrating its effectiveness in improving performance and data efficiency.
- The inclusion of a world model to learn representations is a reasonable approach that enhances the understanding of multi-agent interactions.
- The proposed method is applicable to various MARL algorithms, including CTDE and MAT, and can be applied to both state-based and vision-based settings.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed analysis of the impact of hyperparameters, which could be crucial for understanding the performance of TIMAR across different settings.
- There is a lack of discussion on the limitations of the proposed method, particularly concerning its applicability to other MARL algorithms and its performance in different scenarios.
- The paper does not include a detailed comparison with existing methods like MA2CL, which could provide a clearer understanding of the advantages and disadvantages of TIMAR.
- The paper does not provide a detailed discussion on the computational complexity or the impact of TIMAR on the computational requirements of MARL algorithms.
- The paper does not include a discussion on the potential negative societal impacts of the proposed method.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors provide more details on the impact of hyperparameters, particularly how different settings affect the performance of TIMAR?
- How does TIMAR perform when applied to other MARL algorithms, and what are the observed improvements or challenges?
- Could the authors discuss the potential limitations of the proposed method and how these might affect its applicability in various scenarios?
- Is there a possibility to include a detailed comparison with existing methods like MA2CL, particularly in terms of computational efficiency and performance?
- How does the computational complexity of TIMAR compare to other MARL algorithms, and what are the implications for the scalability of the method?
- Can the authors provide insights into how the Transformer architecture contributes to the performance improvements observed with TIMAR?
- How does TIMAR handle the issue of permutation invariance, particularly in scenarios where the order of observations and actions is not known?
