 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel multivariate temporal-contextual adaptive learning method, ARM, designed to address the challenges in modeling series-wise relationships in multivariate time series forecasting (LTSF). The ARM framework incorporates Adaptive Univariate Effect Learning (AUEL), Random Dropping (RD), and Multi-kernel Local Smoothing (MKLS) to enhance the handling of individual series temporal patterns and inter-series dependencies. The methodology is evaluated against various benchmarks, demonstrating its effectiveness in improving forecasting performance. However, the paper has been critiqued for its complexity, lack of clarity in some sections, and limited novelty in some of its components.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel multivariate temporal-contextual adaptive learning method, ARM, which addresses the challenges in modeling series-wise relationships in multivariate time series forecasting.
- The proposed methodology is well-motivated, with clear explanations of the challenges and the proposed solutions, and is supported by extensive experiments that demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed method.
- The paper is well-written, making it easy to follow, and the authors provide a comprehensive ablation study to analyze the contributions of each component.
- The introduction of Adaptive Univariate Effect Learning (AUEL) and Multi-kernel Local Smoothing (MKLS) are innovative contributions that enhance the handling of individual series temporal patterns and inter-series dependencies.
- The paper provides a detailed description of the proposed method, including figures that aid in understanding the methodology.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper is complex and challenging to understand, with a large number of components and notations, which could be simplified to enhance readability.
- The paper lacks clarity in some sections, particularly in the introduction and the description of the methodology, which could benefit from more detailed explanations and examples.
- The novelty of the proposed method is limited as it combines existing techniques such as AUEL, RD, and MKLS, which have been previously proposed in other studies.
- The paper does not adequately address the computational complexity of the proposed method, which could impact its practical applicability, especially in real-world scenarios with large datasets.
- The paper does not provide sufficient experimental details, such as the dataset size and the specifics of the experiments, which could limit the reproducibility of the results.
- The paper could benefit from a more detailed discussion on the limitations of the proposed method and potential future research directions.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors provide more detailed explanations of the methodology, particularly in the introduction and the description of the components such as AUEL and MKLS?
- How do the authors address the computational complexity of the proposed method, and what are the specific computational requirements for implementing ARM?
- Can the authors clarify the novelty of the proposed method, particularly in relation to the existing literature on time series forecasting?
- How does the proposed method compare to other state-of-the-art methods in terms of computational efficiency and forecasting accuracy?
- Could the authors provide more details on the experimental setup, such as the dataset size and the specifics of the experiments, to enhance the reproducibility of the results?
- How does the proposed method handle the issue of overfitting, especially in scenarios where the dataset size is limited?
- Could the authors discuss the limitations of their proposed method and potential future research directions to address these limitations?
