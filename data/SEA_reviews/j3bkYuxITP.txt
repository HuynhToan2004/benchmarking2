 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel method called RP-MGDA, which leverages refined partitioning to enhance the performance of the Multiple Gradient Descent Algorithm (MGDA) in multi-objective optimization tasks. This method strategically partitions and groups variables, aiming to improve the optimization process by considering variable dependencies and structures. The paper presents several motivating examples and theoretical analysis to demonstrate the effectiveness of RP-MGDA compared to MGDA. Despite its innovative approach, the paper has been critiqued for its limited experimental scope, lack of comprehensive theoretical analysis, and potential over-simplification of the variable partitioning process.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, clear, and easy to follow, with a clear presentation of the problem and the proposed solution.
- The authors have identified a significant limitation in the Multiple Gradient Descent Algorithm (MGDA) and have proposed a novel approach to address it.
- The proposed method is intuitive and easy to implement, with a theoretical guarantee of finding Pareto stationary solutions.
- The paper provides examples that demonstrate the limitations of MGDA and how these can be addressed by the proposed method.
- The idea of partitioning variables to improve the performance of MGDA is innovative and could potentially be applied to other multi-objective gradient methods.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a comprehensive experimental evaluation, particularly in terms of the number and complexity of the multi-objective optimization problems tested.
- The theoretical analysis provided is limited, with a lack of detailed proofs and a clear explanation of how the proposed method enhances MGDA's performance.
- The paper does not adequately address the scalability of the proposed method, particularly in scenarios with a large number of variables.
- The presentation of the paper could be improved, with some sections appearing rushed and the figures and formulas not being clearly explained.
- The paper's contribution is seen as limited, as it primarily focuses on a specific aspect of MGDA without addressing broader issues related to multi-objective optimization.
- There is a lack of discussion on the computational complexity of the proposed method and its scalability to large-scale problems.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors provide more details on the computational complexity of the proposed method and its scalability to large-scale problems?
- How does the proposed method handle scenarios where the variable dependency structure is unknown or not easily discernible?
- Could the authors clarify the relationship between the proposed method and the existing literature on multi-objective optimization?
- Is there a specific reason why the proposed method was not tested on a broader range of multi-objective optimization problems?
- How does the proposed method compare in terms of performance and computational efficiency with other state-of-the-art multi-objective optimization algorithms?
- Could the authors provide a more detailed explanation of the experimental setup and results, particularly in the figures and formulas presented in the paper?
