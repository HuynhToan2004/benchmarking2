 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel attention mechanism, One-Versus-Others (OvO) attention, designed to scale linearly with the number of modalities in multimodal learning, addressing the computational complexity issues associated with quadratic scaling in traditional methods. OvO attention computes the dot product between one modality's output and the average of others, which is shown to outperform self-attention and cross-attention in terms of performance and computational efficiency across multiple datasets. The methodology is validated through extensive experiments on various datasets, demonstrating its effectiveness and scalability.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel attention mechanism, One-Versus-Others (OvO) attention, which is a significant contribution to the field of multimodal learning.
- The method is well-motivated and addresses the challenge of scalability in multimodal learning, which is crucial for practical applications.
- The paper is well-written, with clear and detailed explanations of the proposed method and its implementation.
- Extensive experiments are conducted across various datasets, demonstrating the effectiveness of the proposed method and its ability to outperform self-attention and cross-attention in terms of performance and computational efficiency.
- The paper provides a comprehensive analysis of the computational complexity of different attention mechanisms, which is crucial for understanding the scalability of multimodal learning systems.
- The authors have provided the code for their experiments, enhancing the reproducibility of the research.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed discussion on the limitations of the proposed method, which could help in understanding its scope and applicability.
- There is a need for more comprehensive comparisons with state-of-the-art models to establish the superiority of the proposed method.
- The experimental results are not always convincing, particularly in cases where the proposed method does not outperform cross-attention or other baselines.
- The paper could benefit from a more thorough analysis of the attention weights to better understand the model's decision-making process.
- The paper's focus on computational complexity might overshadow other important aspects of multimodal learning, such as the quality of the learned representations.
- The paper's scope is somewhat limited, focusing primarily on text and image modalities, which might not generalize well to other types of modalities.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors provide more details on the limitations of the proposed method and its potential applications?
- How does the OvO attention mechanism compare to other state-of-the-art models in terms of performance and computational efficiency?
- Could the authors elaborate on the experimental results where the proposed method does not outperform other baselines?
- How does the proposed method handle different types of modalities, especially those that are not text or image-based?
- Could the authors provide more insights into the attention weights and their impact on the model's decision-making process?
- How does the computational complexity of the OvO attention mechanism scale with the number of modalities, and what are the practical implications of this scaling?
