 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a method for detecting variance change points in organ viability assessment using a dual control window scheme. The method aims to improve computational efficiency by focusing on regions where change points are likely to occur, thereby reducing the need to scan the entire data profile. This approach is validated through simulations and real-world applications, showing that it can detect variance changes more efficiently than existing methods. However, the paper suffers from clarity issues, particularly in the presentation of algorithms and experimental results, which makes it difficult to follow and assess the method's effectiveness comprehensively.

**Strengths:**
- The paper addresses a significant problem in organ viability assessment, which is crucial for improving the efficiency and accuracy of organ transplantation.
- The proposed method is well-motivated and the application is interesting, with potential real-world impact.
- The paper is well-written and easy to follow, making it accessible to readers.
- The methodology is well-explained, with clear descriptions of the dual control window scheme and the variance change point detection algorithm.
- The paper provides both simulation and real-world application results, which demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed method in detecting variance change points.
- The paper is original in its approach, introducing a novel method for variance change point detection in organ viability assessment.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a clear comparison with existing methods, particularly in terms of computational efficiency and detection accuracy.
- The paper does not adequately address the choice of hyperparameters, such as the window size and the number of random samples used in the algorithm.
- The experimental results are not convincingly presented, with some figures and tables being too small or lacking in detail.
- The paper suffers from clarity issues, particularly in the presentation of algorithms and experimental results, which makes it difficult to follow and assess the method's effectiveness comprehensively.
- The paper does not sufficiently address the limitations of the proposed method, such as the assumptions made about the data distribution and the potential impact of these assumptions on the method's effectiveness.
- The paper does not provide a detailed comparison of computational efficiency and detection accuracy with other state-of-the-art methods, which is crucial for establishing the method's effectiveness.

**Questions:**
- Could you clarify the choice of hyperparameters, particularly the window size and the number of random samples used in the algorithm?
- How does the proposed method compare in terms of computational efficiency and detection accuracy with other state-of-the-art methods for variance change point detection?
- Can you provide more details on the experimental setup and results, particularly in terms of the number of temperature profiles analyzed and the specific conditions under which the experiments were conducted?
- How does the proposed method perform under different data distributions, particularly those that do not conform to the assumptions made in the methodology?
- Can you elaborate on the methodology used in the liver experiment and how it differs from the methodology used in the simulation studies?
- How does the method handle the variability in the data, especially in the context of the organ viability assessment?
