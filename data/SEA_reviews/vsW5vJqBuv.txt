 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel framework named Diffusion for Open-ended Goals (DOG) aimed at enabling embodied AI to plan and act flexibly and dynamically for open-ended tasks. The framework leverages diffusion models to learn world knowledge from offline experience and uses this knowledge to adaptively perform online planning and control. The methodology is evaluated across various embodied decision-making tasks, demonstrating its effectiveness in handling novel goals not encountered during training. The framework's approach is innovative, integrating training-free guidance techniques with diffusion models to adaptively perform online planning and control. Despite its strengths, the paper is criticized for its limited novelty and lack of comprehensive evaluation on complex tasks.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel framework, DOG, which effectively handles open-ended tasks in embodied AI by leveraging diffusion models to learn world knowledge from offline experience without goal conditioning.
- The methodology is evaluated across various embodied decision-making tasks, demonstrating its effectiveness in handling novel goals not encountered during training.
- The framework is evaluated on a wide range of tasks, including maze navigation, robot movement control, and robot arm manipulation, which showcases its versatility and applicability.
- The paper is well-written, clear, and easy to follow, making the complex concepts accessible and understandable.
- The use of diffusion models for open-ended tasks is a novel and promising approach, offering a significant contribution to the field of embodied AI.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks comprehensive evaluation on complex tasks, which limits the assessment of the framework's performance in more challenging scenarios.
- The novelty of the paper is questioned as it builds upon existing works like Diffuser and AdaptDiffuser, with similar methodologies and objectives.
- The paper could benefit from a more detailed discussion on the limitations of the proposed method and the challenges it might face in real-world applications.
- There is a need for more diverse experiments to demonstrate the generalizability of the framework across different environments and tasks.
- The paper could improve by providing a more detailed comparison with related works and a clearer explanation of the differences and advantages of the proposed method over existing approaches.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors clarify the main differences between this paper and the previous works mentioned, particularly in terms of methodology and objectives?
- How does the proposed framework compare to other existing methods in terms of performance and efficiency?
- What are the specific challenges or limitations of the proposed framework, and how might these impact its practical application in real-world scenarios?
- Could the authors provide more details on the training and testing stages, especially how the agent interprets goal descriptions and generates plans?
- How does the framework handle scenarios where the goal is not achievable or contradictory, and what are the implications of such scenarios for the framework's performance?
