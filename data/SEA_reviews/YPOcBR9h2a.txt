 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel data-dependent long-range convolution network, termed DLCNet, which aims to address the challenges of processing lengthy sequences by incorporating data dependency through three key modules: Layer-Wise Mapping, Rectify SideNet, and SWEAP Operator. These modules facilitate in-context learning while maintaining a reasonable parameter scale. The network is evaluated on various tasks, including super-glue and wikitext-103, showing promising results. However, the paper is criticized for its unclear presentation and lack of detailed experimental results, particularly concerning the computational complexity and the fairness of comparisons with other models.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel approach to long-range convolution, which is an interesting and potentially impactful area of research.
- The proposed method, DLCNet, shows promising results in terms of performance on various tasks, including super-glue and wikitext-103.
- The paper is well-organized and easy to follow, with clear descriptions of the proposed method and its implementation.
- The inclusion of an ablation study provides insights into the contributions of different components of the model.
- The paper is significant in addressing the challenges of processing lengthy sequences, which is a critical issue in many real-world applications.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks detailed information on the computational complexity of the proposed method, particularly concerning the time and memory required for different tasks.
- There are inconsistencies and potential errors in the paper, such as missing or incorrect equations and unclear descriptions in figures and tables.
- The comparisons with other models, such as Hyena, are not fair due to differences in parameter counts and training data.
- The paper does not sufficiently discuss the limitations of the proposed method, which is crucial for understanding its applicability and potential drawbacks.
- There is a lack of detailed experimental results, particularly concerning the performance of the model on longer sequences and the impact of different hyperparameters.
- The paper could benefit from a more rigorous evaluation, including a broader set of benchmarks and a more comprehensive analysis of the results.

**Questions:**
- Can you clarify the computational complexity of the proposed method, particularly concerning the time and memory required for different tasks?
- How does the performance of the model scale with the length of the input sequence? Have you tested the model with sequences longer than those used in the experiments?
- What is the impact of different hyperparameters on the performance of the model? Have you conducted a sensitivity analysis to understand their effects?
- How does the model compare to other methods in terms of generalization and extrapolation, particularly when applied to sequences not encountered during training?
- Could you provide more details on the training and evaluation of the model, including the specifics of the training data, hyperparameters, and evaluation metrics used?
- How does the model perform on different types of data, such as non-textual data? Have you tested the model on such data, and if so, what were the results?
