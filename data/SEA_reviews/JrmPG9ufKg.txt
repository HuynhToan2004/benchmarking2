 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel federated learning approach based on SimCLR, focusing on maximizing mutual information (MI) between two views of data. It proposes a new loss function, the user verification (UV) loss, to handle non-i.i.d. data and extends SimCLR to a federated semi-supervised setting. The paper derives a lower bound for the MI of the two views of data, showing that it is equivalent to a sum of local objectives involving parameters in the encoder and classifier. The experiments conducted on CIFAR10 and CIFAR100 datasets demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed method. However, concerns were raised regarding the novelty of the proposed method, the limited scope of the experiments, and the clarity of the theoretical derivations.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written and easy to follow, with a clear motivation and a well-defined problem.
- The proposed method is intuitive and easy to understand, with a clear description of the derivation process.
- The paper provides a theoretical analysis on the lower bound of the proposed method, which is supported by experimental results on CIFAR10 and CIFAR100.
- The paper includes ablation studies that demonstrate the impact of different parameters on the performance of the method.
- The method is simple to implement and does not require significant computational resources, making it accessible for practical applications.

**Weaknesses:**
- The novelty of the proposed method is questionable, as it appears to be a straightforward extension of existing methods like SimCLR and FedAvg.
- The paper lacks comprehensive experimental validation, particularly in terms of the scalability and robustness of the method across different datasets and hyperparameters.
- The theoretical derivations, particularly the use of the chain rule and the derivation of the lower bound, are not convincingly explained or justified.
- There is a lack of discussion on the limitations of the proposed method, which could affect its practical applicability in real-world scenarios.
- The paper does not adequately address the impact of the proposed method on the privacy and security of federated learning systems.

**Questions:**
- How does the proposed method compare to existing methods in terms of computational efficiency and scalability, particularly when dealing with larger datasets?
- Can the authors provide more detailed theoretical justifications for the use of the chain rule and the derivation of the lower bound?
- How does the method perform when dealing with datasets that are not as balanced as CIFAR10 and CIFAR100, and what are the potential implications for the model's generalization ability?
- What are the specific hyperparameters used in the experiments, and how do they affect the performance of the method?
- Can the authors discuss the potential limitations of the proposed method and suggest areas for future research to address these limitations?
