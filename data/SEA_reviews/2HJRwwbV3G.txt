 **Summary:**
The paper critically evaluates the Knowledge Neuron (KN) thesis, which proposes that facts are stored in specific MLP weights within large language models (LLMs), thereby influencing their output. The authors challenge this thesis by demonstrating that the effects of editing MLP weights are limited to local changes in probability distributions, without significantly altering the model's overall behavior. They argue that the KN thesis oversimplifies the complex mechanisms of LLMs, particularly in how they process and recall information. The paper presents empirical evidence showing that the KN thesis does not adequately explain the storage and retrieval of facts in LLMs, suggesting a need for more nuanced theories.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, clear, and easy to follow, making complex concepts accessible to a broader audience.
- It provides a comprehensive overview of the Knowledge Neuron (KN) thesis and critiques the methodology used in previous studies, highlighting the limitations of the KN thesis.
- The empirical evaluation is robust, demonstrating that the KN thesis does not adequately explain the process of factual expression.
- The paper introduces new metrics for evaluating model-editing methods, which could be beneficial for future research in this area.
- The authors challenge the oversimplification of the KN thesis and encourage a more nuanced understanding of how LLMs process and recall information.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper primarily focuses on a specific model (GPT-2 XL), which might limit the generalizability of the findings to other models.
- The conclusions drawn from the empirical evaluations are not clearly articulated, making it difficult to discern the authors' position on the KN thesis.
- The paper's claims about the limitations of the KN thesis might be overstated, as the empirical evidence does not convincingly demonstrate that the KN thesis is fundamentally flawed.
- The paper's structure and presentation could be improved to better highlight the contributions and conclusions of the research.
- There is a lack of clarity regarding the implications of the findings for the broader understanding of LLMs and the nature of knowledge representation in these models.

**Questions:**
- How do the authors envision the future of research in this area, particularly in light of the limitations of the KN thesis?
- Could the authors clarify the implications of their findings for the broader understanding of LLMs and the nature of knowledge representation in these models?
- What specific changes or improvements would the authors suggest to the KN thesis to make it more robust and accurate?
- Given the limitations of the empirical evidence, how do the authors justify their strong claims about the inadequacy of the KN thesis?
- Could the authors provide more detailed examples or case studies to illustrate the practical implications of their findings for the development and deployment of LLMs?
