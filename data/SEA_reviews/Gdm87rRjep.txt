 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a method for improving the accuracy-efficiency trade-off in compressed large language models (LLMs) by employing learnable prompts. These prompts are trained on a dataset and then used to enhance the performance of compressed models, such as those pruned using SparseGPT or quantized using GPTQ. The methodology involves optimizing the prompt matrix to maximize the likelihood of the next token given the preceding tokens, including the prompt. The approach is evaluated across various datasets and compression levels, demonstrating improvements in perplexity and potential for transfer across different tasks and datasets. The paper also explores the transferability of these prompts, suggesting that they can be effectively utilized in other compressed models.

**Strengths:**
- The paper addresses an important problem by improving the accuracy-efficiency trade-off in compressed models, which is a significant issue in the field of large language models (LLMs).
- The proposed method of using learnable prompts to enhance the performance of compressed models is innovative and shows promising results, particularly in improving perplexity and potentially transferring across different datasets and tasks.
- The paper is well-organized, clearly written, and provides a detailed analysis of the trade-offs involved in model compression, which is crucial for understanding the limitations and potential of the proposed method.
- The experiments are extensive and demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed method, showing that it can be transferred across different datasets and tasks, enhancing the generalizability of the findings.
- The paper provides a valuable contribution to the community by sharing the learned prompts, which can be used to improve the performance of other compressed models, thus reducing the need for retraining.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a thorough comparison with existing methods, particularly in terms of the efficiency and effectiveness of the proposed method compared to other compression techniques like LoRA or full fine-tuning.
- The evaluation of the method's effectiveness is limited to perplexity, which may not fully capture the quality of the generated text. Additional metrics such as BLEU scores or human evaluations could provide a more comprehensive assessment.
- The paper does not provide a detailed analysis of the computational overhead or the memory requirements of the proposed method, which could limit its practical applicability in real-world scenarios.
- The transferability of the learned prompts across different tasks and datasets is not adequately explored, and the paper does not discuss the potential for adapting the prompts to specific tasks or domains.
- The paper does not discuss the limitations of the proposed method, such as the potential for overfitting or the scalability of the method to larger models.

**Questions:**
- How does the proposed method compare in terms of efficiency and effectiveness with other compression techniques like LoRA or full fine-tuning?
- Could you provide more details on the computational overhead and memory requirements of the proposed method, especially in comparison to other compression techniques?
- How do the learned prompts perform when transferred to different tasks or datasets, and what are the potential limitations or challenges in adapting the prompts to specific tasks or domains?
- Are there any potential negative societal impacts of the proposed method, and how do the authors address these concerns?
- Could you elaborate on the transferability of the learned prompts across different tasks and datasets, and discuss any potential strategies for adapting the prompts to specific tasks or domains?
