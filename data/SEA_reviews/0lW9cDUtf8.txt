 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel pre-processing algorithm called FairReweighing, aimed at achieving fairness in regression problems by ensuring the separation criterion. This criterion, adapted from classification settings, is applied through density estimation to adjust the weights of training data, thus potentially improving fairness in predictions. The algorithm is tested on both synthetic and real-world datasets, demonstrating its effectiveness in reducing unfairness in predictions. However, the paper is criticized for its lack of theoretical analysis and for not thoroughly addressing the practical implications of its assumptions, such as the independence of A and Y given X. The method's generalization and scalability are also questioned, particularly in high-dimensional datasets and complex regression models.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, making it easy to follow and understand.
- The proposed method is straightforward and can be applied to various regression problems.
- The method achieves good results on real-world data, demonstrating its practical applicability.
- The paper introduces a novel approach to fairness in regression problems, which is an important and relevant area of research.
- The methodology is simple and effective, with the potential to improve fairness in regression models.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a thorough theoretical analysis, particularly regarding the assumptions made about the independence of A and Y given X.
- The method's generalization to high-dimensional datasets and complex regression models is unclear.
- The paper does not sufficiently compare its method with existing approaches, such as those by Steinberg et al. (2020), which also address fairness in regression.
- The paper's experimental setup is limited, with only two datasets used for testing, which may not be sufficient to validate the method's effectiveness across different scenarios.
- The paper does not adequately address the practical implications of its assumptions, particularly the independence of A and Y given X, which is a critical assumption for the method's applicability.
- The paper's contributions are seen as incremental and not sufficiently distinct from existing methods, particularly those by Steinberg et al. (2020).

**Questions:**

- How does the method handle the practical challenges of ensuring the independence of A and Y given X in real-world datasets?
