 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel method called METERN for learning multiplex embeddings on text-rich networks using a single text encoder and relation prior tokens. This method aims to capture relation-specific signals and shared knowledge across relations, enhancing the representation of nodes in multiplex networks. The authors demonstrate the effectiveness of their approach through experiments on various datasets and tasks, showing that METERN outperforms existing methods. Despite its innovative approach, the paper is critiqued for its limited novelty in using relation prior tokens and its scalability issues, particularly in handling large multiplex networks.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, easy to follow, and the experimental results are solid, showing the effectiveness of the proposed method.
- The proposed method is innovative, using only one shared text encoder and relation prior tokens to learn multiplex embeddings, which is a novel approach.
- The paper conducts extensive experiments on nine downstream tasks and five networks from both academic and e-commerce domains, demonstrating the versatility and applicability of the proposed method.
- The proposed method is intuitive and makes sense, with a focus on learning relation-specific signals and shared knowledge across relations.
- The paper provides a comprehensive evaluation of the proposed method, comparing it against various baseline methods and demonstrating its superiority.

**Weaknesses:**
- The novelty of the method is limited, as the use of relation prior tokens is similar to other methods that use learnable "prompt tokens" to enhance text representation.
- The paper does not discuss the scalability of the method, particularly in handling large multiplex networks, which could affect its practical application.
- There is a lack of detailed explanation regarding the effectiveness of the relation prior tokens and the method's ability to capture relation-specific signals.
- The paper does not sufficiently compare the proposed method with other recent methods that use pre-trained language models (PLMs) to generate node embeddings, which could provide a more robust evaluation.
- The paper lacks a detailed discussion on the limitations of the proposed method and potential negative societal impacts.
- The presentation of the paper could be improved, particularly in terms of clarity and organization, especially in the appendices where the experimental settings are not clearly explained.

**Questions:**
- How does the proposed method compare with other recent methods that use PLMs to generate node embeddings, such as MTDNN?
- Can the authors provide more detailed explanations on the effectiveness of the relation prior tokens and how they contribute to the method's performance?
- How does the proposed method handle the scalability issues when applied to large multiplex networks?
- Could the authors discuss the limitations of their proposed method and potential negative societal impacts in more detail?
- In the experimental section, how does the proposed method perform when compared with other methods that use PLMs for node embedding generation?
- How does the method handle the scalability issues when applied to large multiplex networks, and what are the potential solutions to address these challenges?
