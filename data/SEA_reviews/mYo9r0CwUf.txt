 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel method for rendering semi-transparent scenes using Neural Density-Distance Fields (NeDDF), which optimizes the bisection sampling method to enhance the rendering of semi-transparent objects. This approach involves the use of multi-resolution hash encoding to separate high-frequency components and maintain low-frequency information, which is critical for handling complex shapes and transparency effects. The methodology aims to improve the rendering of NeDDF by addressing issues related to transparency and distance field representations, thereby potentially outperforming existing methods in terms of image quality. However, the paper's experimental validation is limited to synthetic data, which may not fully demonstrate the method's effectiveness in real-world scenarios.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, making it easy to follow and understand the methodologies and results presented.
- The proposed method effectively handles the limitations of NeDDF by optimizing the bisection sampling method and introducing multi-resolution hash encoding.
- The method is capable of rendering semi-transparent objects with good quality, particularly in handling complex shapes and transparency effects.
- The paper provides a detailed analysis of the limitations of NeDDF and proposes solutions to address these issues, which are supported by experimental results.
- The use of multi-resolution hash encoding for frequency separation is a novel approach that allows for the representation of thin shapes and detailed shapes in distance fields.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a clear explanation of the advantages of using distance fields over density fields, which are more commonly used in similar applications.
- The experimental validation is limited to synthetic data, which may not fully demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed method in real-world scenarios.
- There is a lack of discussion on the limitations of the proposed method, which is crucial for understanding its applicability and potential drawbacks.
- The paper could benefit from a more detailed comparison with existing methods, particularly in terms of rendering speed and memory usage.
- The use of multi-resolution hash encoding for frequency separation is not a new concept and has been previously used in other methods.
- The paper does not provide sufficient implementation details, which makes it difficult to replicate the results.

**Questions:**
- Can the proposed method be applied to other distance field-based methods, such as NeuS, to potentially improve their rendering quality?
- How does the proposed method compare to other methods in terms of rendering speed and memory usage?
- Could the authors provide more details on the training and inference times for the proposed method?
- Is there a possibility to use the proposed method for other applications beyond rendering, such as scene completion or shape reconstruction?
- How does the proposed method handle the representation of thin shapes, and can it effectively capture the fine details of these shapes?
- Can the authors provide more implementation details, such as the specifics of the MLP used for rendering and the training parameters?
- How does the proposed method handle the representation of distant landscapes, and can it effectively capture the details of these scenes?
