 **Summary:**
The paper presents a comparative analysis between Graph Neural Networks (GNNs) and shallow embedding methods, focusing on node representation learning. It systematically explores the differences in learning paradigms and neighborhood aggregation mechanisms, revealing when GNNs may underperform compared to shallow embedding methods. The study identifies scenarios such as attribute-poor networks and heterophilic nodes where GNNs may struggle. The paper also proposes a unified framework for evaluating GNNs and shallow embedding methods, providing insights into when each approach might be advantageous. Despite the comprehensive empirical analysis, the paper is criticized for its limited theoretical insights and the potential for overgeneralization from the datasets used.

**Strengths:**
- The paper presents a comprehensive empirical analysis of GNNs and shallow embedding methods, comparing their performance in various scenarios.
- The authors provide a clear and structured presentation of the differences between GNNs and shallow embedding methods, which is beneficial for understanding the trade-offs between these approaches.
- The paper introduces a novel approach to unifying the evaluation of GNNs and shallow embedding methods, which could be useful for practitioners in selecting the most appropriate method for their specific use case.
- The discussion on the impact of node attributes and homophily on GNNs' performance is insightful and contributes to a better understanding of when GNNs may underperform compared to shallow embedding methods.
- The paper provides a thorough analysis of the strengths and weaknesses of both GNNs and shallow embedding methods, highlighting the need for careful consideration in choosing between these approaches.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a theoretical analysis of the differences between GNNs and shallow embedding methods, which could provide deeper insights into the observed phenomena.
- The focus on self-supervised learning methods for GNNs may limit the generalizability of the findings to other types of GNNs trained using different objectives.
- The empirical analysis is limited to a few datasets, which may not adequately represent the performance of GNNs and shallow embedding methods across a broader range of datasets.
- The paper does not sufficiently address the computational complexity of GNNs compared to shallow embedding methods, which could be a significant concern in large-scale applications.
- The paper's conclusions about the superiority of shallow embedding methods in certain scenarios (e.g., attribute-poor networks) are not sufficiently supported by the data presented, and the results may be overgeneralized.
- The paper could benefit from a more detailed discussion on how to combine the outputs of GNNs and shallow embedding methods, as this is a promising area for future research.

**Questions:**
- How does the performance of GNNs compare to shallow embedding methods in inductive settings, where nodes are added or removed during training?
- Can the authors provide more details on the computational complexity of GNNs compared to shallow embedding methods, particularly in large-scale applications?
- How do the findings of this paper generalize to other types of GNNs trained using different objectives?
- Could the authors clarify the choice of datasets used in the empirical analysis and discuss how the results might vary on other datasets?
- How does the paper's conclusion regarding the superiority of shallow embedding methods in attribute-poor networks align with the empirical results presented?
- Can the authors provide more theoretical insights into the observed differences between GNNs and shallow embedding methods, particularly in terms of how these differences impact performance in different scenarios?
