 **Summary:**
The paper introduces NeuFace, a novel optimization-based method for generating accurate and spatio-temporally consistent 3D face meshes from 2D face videos. This method leverages a neural network to parameterize the 3D face mesh and optimizes for temporal and multi-view consistency. The paper also introduces the NeuFace dataset, a large-scale 3D face mesh pseudo-annotation dataset constructed using this method, which is claimed to be the largest of its kind. The dataset is used to validate the effectiveness of NeuFace and to demonstrate its utility in various applications such as improving face reconstruction and learning facial motion priors. The paper also discusses the limitations of the dataset, including potential issues with identity preservation and the quality of the reconstructed meshes, particularly in regions with occlusions.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel optimization-based method for generating accurate and spatio-temporally consistent 3D face meshes from 2D face videos, which is a significant advancement in the field.
- The proposed NeuFace dataset is the largest of its kind, providing a valuable resource for the research community.
- The paper is well-written, easy to follow, and provides a comprehensive evaluation of the method, demonstrating its effectiveness in various applications.
- The methodology is technically sound, with clear explanations of the neural re-parameterization approach and the optimization process.
- The paper presents a clear motivation and provides a detailed analysis of the dataset's quality, including comparisons with existing datasets and baselines.
- The authors have made the code and dataset publicly available, which is a commendable effort towards promoting transparency and reproducibility in research.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed discussion on the limitations of the NeuFace dataset, particularly concerning identity preservation and the quality of the reconstructed meshes in regions with occlusions.
- The method's reliance on existing datasets raises concerns about the novelty and the extent to which it represents a significant advancement over existing methods.
- The paper does not sufficiently address the issue of identity preservation in the dataset, which could be a critical concern for applications involving face recognition or other identity-sensitive tasks.
- The method's dependence on existing datasets also questions the extent to which it represents a significant advancement over existing methods, as it primarily leverages pre-trained models and existing datasets without significant modifications or innovations.
- There is a lack of discussion on the potential negative societal impacts of the dataset and the method, which could be a significant concern given the sensitive nature of face data.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors clarify the extent to which the NeuFace dataset preserves identity and how this impacts the quality of the reconstructed meshes?
- How does the proposed method compare to existing methods in terms of computational efficiency and resource requirements?
- Could the authors provide more details on the dataset filtering process and the criteria used to determine the final dataset quality?
- How does the method handle occlusions in the input videos, and what impact does this have on the quality of the reconstructed meshes?
- Are there any plans to release the dataset in a format that preserves identity, such as a per-identity video dataset?
- How does the method perform when applied to videos with significant variations in lighting conditions or other environmental factors?
- Could the authors provide more details on the training process for the DECA model and the specifics of the dataset used for training?
