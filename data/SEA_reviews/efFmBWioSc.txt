 **Summary:**
The paper introduces WebGUM, a multimodal agent designed for autonomous web navigation, leveraging a large corpus of demonstrations to train an instruction-finetuned language model and a vision encoder. This model, trained offline, is evaluated on benchmarks such as MiniWoB and WebShop, showing significant improvements over existing methods. The authors also discuss the scalability and generalization of their approach, suggesting that it could be applied to real-world settings. The paper includes a detailed analysis of the dataset and model architecture, with a focus on the importance of multimodal data and the role of instruction-finetuning in enhancing the model's performance.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, clear, and easy to follow, with a clear presentation of the methodology and results.
- The proposed method achieves state-of-the-art (SOTA) results on MiniWoB, demonstrating the effectiveness of the proposed approach.
- The paper provides a thorough analysis of the dataset, including the number of examples and the impact of multimodal data, which is crucial for understanding the model's performance.
- The use of instruction-finetuned language models is highlighted as a significant factor in improving the model's performance, showing a 25% improvement over vanilla models.
- The paper includes comprehensive ablation studies that help in understanding the individual contributions of different components of the model.
- The dataset and code will be made publicly available, which is a valuable resource for the community.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed discussion on the limitations of the proposed method, particularly in terms of its scalability and applicability to real-world settings.
- The paper does not adequately address the generalization capabilities of the model, which is a critical concern given the limited size of the dataset used.
- The paper does not include a comparison with other large language models (LLMs) like GPT-4, which could provide a more robust evaluation of the model's performance.
- The paper does not discuss the potential societal impacts of the work, which is a significant omission.
- The paper could benefit from more detailed discussions on the model's performance in real-world settings and the potential challenges in deploying the model in such environments.
- The paper does not discuss the computational requirements of the model, which could limit its practical application.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors provide more details on the generalization capabilities of the model, especially in terms of its performance on tasks that were not part of the training set?
- How does the model perform in real-world settings, and what are the potential challenges in deploying the model in such environments?
- Could the authors elaborate on the computational requirements of the model and discuss potential strategies for reducing these requirements?
- How does the model handle the scalability issues, especially in terms of its performance on larger and more complex datasets?
- Could the authors discuss the potential societal impacts of their work and how they plan to address these impacts?
- Could the authors provide more details on the model's performance on the WebShop benchmark, especially in comparison to other models like Synapse?
