 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel framework for nighttime optical flow estimation, leveraging a common latent space to adapt from daytime to nighttime scenes. This approach utilizes event cameras to capture sharp boundaries and enhance the accuracy of motion estimation in low-light conditions. The methodology involves a two-stage adaptation process, first aligning the reflectance of daytime and nighttime images and then aligning motion features. The paper claims significant performance improvements over existing methods, although the novelty and clarity of the method's contributions are questioned. The paper also includes a new dataset, LLFE, which is a paired image-event dataset, and employs a contrastive loss to transfer motion features from event cameras to nighttime images.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel approach by using event cameras to capture sharp motion boundaries and enhance the accuracy of motion estimation in low-light conditions.
- The proposed method demonstrates significant performance improvements over existing methods, particularly in nighttime optical flow estimation.
- The paper is well-written and easy to follow, with clear and logical presentation of the methodology and results.
- The introduction of a new dataset, LLFE, which is a paired image-event dataset, is a valuable contribution to the field.
- The method's novelty lies in its use of event cameras to capture sharp motion boundaries and its approach to aligning the reflectance of daytime and nighttime images, which is a new idea.
- The paper includes an ablation study that demonstrates the effectiveness of the proposed method.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a clear motivation for using event cameras for motion boundary detection, which could be better explained or justified.
- The technical contributions of the paper are not clearly articulated, making it difficult to understand the novelty and significance of the proposed method.
- The paper does not compare its method with the latest event-based optical flow methods, which could provide a more robust evaluation of the proposed method.
- The dataset used in the experiments, LLFE, is not adequately described, which could limit the reproducibility and understanding of the results.
- The paper does not discuss the limitations of the proposed method, which could provide valuable insights into its applicability and potential improvements.
