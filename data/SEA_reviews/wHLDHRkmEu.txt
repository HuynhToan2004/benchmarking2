 **Summary:**
The paper introduces BarLeRia, a novel Parameter-Efficient Tuning (PET) framework for Referring Image Segmentation (RIS), leveraging bi-directional intertwined vision language adapters to adapt pre-trained models for cross-modal dense prediction tasks. The framework incorporates two tuning modules for efficient attention, one for global and one for local, alongside an intertwined vision language tuning module for efficient modal fusion. The method demonstrates superior performance on RIS benchmarks compared to previous PET methods, achieving an average improvement of 5.6%. Despite its innovative approach, the paper is criticized for its limited novelty, lack of clarity in methodological descriptions, and insufficient comparisons with state-of-the-art methods.

**Strengths:**
- The paper addresses the RIS task, which is not well-explored in the PET domain, and introduces a novel approach to parameter-efficient tuning for this task.
- The proposed method is simple and effective, with a focus on efficient parameter-efficient tuning for the RIS task.
- The paper is well-written, easy to follow, and includes comprehensive experiments and ablation studies that demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed method.
- The authors conduct experiments on three datasets, including the RefCOCO, RefCOCO+, and G-Ref datasets, and achieve state-of-the-art performance.
- The paper includes a novel approach to parameter-efficient tuning for the RIS task, which is a significant contribution to the field.

**Weaknesses:**
- The novelty of the paper is limited as it primarily builds upon existing methods like ETRIS, with minor modifications and additional components.
- The paper lacks a thorough comparison with other state-of-the-art methods, particularly in terms of computational efficiency and model parameters.
- The methodology section is not clearly written, making it difficult to understand the specific contributions of the proposed method.
- The paper does not include a detailed analysis of the computational efficiency or the impact of different model sizes on performance.
- The paper does not sufficiently discuss the limitations of the proposed method, which is crucial for understanding its applicability and potential drawbacks.
- The paper could benefit from a more detailed discussion on the design choices and the rationale behind the proposed method, particularly in the methodology section.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors provide a more detailed comparison with other state-of-the-art methods, including a discussion on the computational efficiency and model parameters?
- How does the proposed method compare with other PET methods in terms of performance and efficiency?
- Can the authors clarify the design choices and the rationale behind the proposed method, particularly in the methodology section?
- Could the authors provide a more detailed analysis of the limitations of the proposed method and discuss potential directions for future research?
- How does the proposed method handle the issue of feature adaption, and what are the specific advantages over other PET methods in this regard?
