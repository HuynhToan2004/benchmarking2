 **Summary:**
The paper introduces PerSAM, a training-free method that personalizes the Segment Anything Model (SAM) for object segmentation using a single reference image and a corresponding mask. PerSAM utilizes a location confidence map to guide attention and semantic prompting to enhance segmentation accuracy. The method also includes a fine-tuning variant, PerSAM-F, which optimizes two parameters to improve performance. Experiments were conducted on the newly created PerSeg dataset and existing benchmarks, showing competitive results. The paper also discusses potential applications in personalized text-to-image synthesis using DreamBooth. However, concerns were raised about the novelty and clarity of the method's contributions, the need for a broader range of experiments, and the method's scalability and generalizability.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, easy to follow, and includes a comprehensive ablation study.
- The proposed method is straightforward and effective, achieving good results with minimal parameters and computational cost.
- The method is capable of segmenting objects in various poses or scenes and can handle multiple identical objects in the same image.
- The PerSeg dataset, specifically curated for personalized segmentation, is a valuable contribution to the field.
- The paper introduces a novel approach to personalized segmentation, leveraging the Segment Anything Model (SAM) without training, which is a significant advancement in the field.
- The proposed method is simple yet effective, demonstrating strong performance on the PerSeg dataset and other benchmarks.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a comprehensive comparison with existing methods, particularly in terms of novelty and scalability.
- The method's reliance on a reference image and a mask may limit its generalizability to different scenarios.
- The paper does not sufficiently address the scalability and generalizability of the proposed method, especially in terms of its applicability to different datasets and scenarios.
- The method's performance in handling multiple objects in the same image is not adequately addressed, and the potential for confusion or misclassification of similar objects is not discussed.
- There is a lack of detailed analysis on the method's robustness to variations in the reference image or the quality of the mask, which could affect its practical applicability.
- The paper does not discuss the potential societal impacts of the proposed method, which could be significant given its potential applications.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors provide more details on the novelty of their method compared to existing works, particularly in terms of the use of reference images and masks?
- How does the proposed method handle variations in the reference image or the quality of the mask? What are the potential impacts on the segmentation accuracy?
- Could the authors elaborate on the scalability and generalizability of their method, especially in terms of its applicability to different datasets and scenarios?
- How does the method perform when handling multiple objects in the same image, particularly when objects are similar or overlapping?
- Can the authors provide more details on the method's robustness to noisy or inaccurate masks, and how the method handles such scenarios?
- How does the method compare in terms of computational efficiency and performance with other state-of-the-art methods, particularly in scenarios involving multiple objects or complex backgrounds?
