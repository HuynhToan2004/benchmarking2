 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel method for detecting deepfake videos by enhancing the visibility of artifacts through a caricature generation process. This approach, termed "Deepfake Caricatures," is designed to make deepfake artifacts more noticeable, potentially improving human detection. The methodology involves a semi-supervised learning approach, leveraging human annotations to guide the model's attention towards areas of interest in deepfake videos. The paper presents several experiments, including a user study and comparisons with existing methods, demonstrating the effectiveness of the proposed method. However, concerns are raised about the method's reliance on specific artifacts and its generalizability across different deepfake generation methods.

**Strengths:**
- The paper addresses an important and timely issue of deepfake detection, which is crucial in the current digital age.
- The proposed method is well-motivated and novel, providing a clear methodology and detailed descriptions of the model architecture and training procedures.
- The paper includes extensive experiments and ablation studies, demonstrating the effectiveness of the proposed method in enhancing deepfake detection.
- The use of caricature as a visual indicator is innovative and could potentially improve human detection of deepfakes.
- The paper is well-written, making it easy to follow and understand, and includes a user study to evaluate the effectiveness of the proposed method.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper does not provide sufficient justification for the choice of artifacts used in the caricature generation process, which could limit the generalizability of the results.
- The methodology section is not clearly explained, particularly the details of the caricature generation module and the training process.
- The paper lacks a comparison with some of the latest state-of-the-art methods, which could have strengthened the claims of the proposed method.
- There is a lack of clarity in the presentation of the results, particularly in figures and tables, which could hinder understanding and reproducibility.
- The paper does not adequately address the potential negative social impacts of the proposed method, such as the potential misuse of deepfake caricatures for malicious purposes.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors provide more details on the specific artifacts used in the caricature generation process and how they were selected?
- How does the proposed method perform when applied to deepfakes generated by different methods, especially those that do not exhibit the same artifacts used in the training data?
- Could the authors clarify the training process for the caricature generation module and explain how it is integrated with the rest of the model?
- How does the proposed method compare with other state-of-the-art methods, especially in terms of detection accuracy and computational efficiency?
- Could the authors provide more detailed results and comparisons, particularly in the user study and the experimental setup, to better support the claims made in the paper?
