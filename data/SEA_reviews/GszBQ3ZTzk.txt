 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel approach to Traffic State Estimation (TSE) by integrating data-driven and physics-based models using an ensemble distillation framework. This method aims to address the challenges of submerged physical knowledge in data information, which is a common issue in traditional physics-informed deep learning methods. The proposed framework, PDED, separates data-driven and physics-based models, then combines them through ensemble learning and knowledge distillation to enhance the model's performance. Extensive experiments on both synthetic and real-world datasets demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed method, showing improvements over existing state-of-the-art methods. However, concerns were raised about the novelty of the ensemble learning and knowledge distillation techniques and the lack of detailed comparisons with other state-of-the-art methods.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-organized and clearly presented, making it easy to follow and understand the proposed methodologies and results.
- The integration of data-driven and physics-based models through ensemble learning and knowledge distillation is a novel approach, which addresses the challenges of submerged physical knowledge in data information.
- The experimental results demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed method, showing improvements over existing state-of-the-art methods.
- The paper provides a comprehensive comparison of the proposed method with other state-of-the-art methods, highlighting the advantages and limitations of each approach.
- The theoretical analysis and mathematical proofs provided in the paper support the proposed methodologies, enhancing the credibility of the research.

**Weaknesses:**
- The novelty of the ensemble learning and knowledge distillation techniques is questionable, as these techniques have been extensively studied in previous works.
- The paper lacks a detailed comparison with other state-of-the-art methods, particularly in terms of performance metrics and experimental setups.
- There is a lack of discussion on the limitations and potential negative societal impacts of the proposed method.
- The paper could benefit from a more thorough discussion on the challenges and limitations of the proposed method, as well as suggestions for future research directions.
- The paper does not provide the source code, which could limit the reproducibility of the results.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors provide more details on the novelty of the ensemble learning and knowledge distillation techniques, particularly how they differ from existing methods?
- How do the authors address the challenges and limitations of the proposed method, and what are their suggestions for future research directions?
- Could the authors provide the source code for their experiments to enhance the reproducibility of the results?
- In terms of the experimental results, could the authors provide more detailed comparisons with other state-of-the-art methods, particularly in terms of performance metrics and experimental setups?
- How do the authors ensure the robustness of the model against different types of noise and uncertainties in real-world traffic data?
