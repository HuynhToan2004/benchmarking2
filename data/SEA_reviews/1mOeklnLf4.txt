 **Summary:**
The paper introduces FroSSL, a novel self-supervised learning (SSL) method that utilizes the Frobenius norm of the covariance matrix to minimize redundancy in feature dimensions, thereby enhancing the convergence speed of SSL models. FroSSL is designed to be both sample-contrastive and dimension-contrastive, which allows it to converge more quickly than other SSL methods. The method's effectiveness is demonstrated through experiments on various datasets, including CIFAR-10, CIFAR-100, STL-10, and ImageNet, showing competitive performance with other SSL methods. However, the paper is critiqued for its limited novelty, as it shares similarities with existing methods like CorInfoMax and TiCo, and for not sufficiently differentiating itself in terms of theoretical analysis and experimental validation.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, clear, and easy to follow, with a clear motivation and a logical flow of content.
- The proposed FroSSL method is innovative in its approach to using the Frobenius norm of the covariance matrix to minimize redundancy in feature dimensions, which is a novel application in the context of SSL.
- The method's ability to converge more quickly than other SSL methods is a significant advantage, potentially reducing training time and computational costs.
- The paper includes both theoretical and empirical evidence to support the claims of faster convergence, which is a strong point in favor of the proposed method.
- The method's performance on various datasets, including CIFAR-10, CIFAR-100, STL-10, and ImageNet, demonstrates its effectiveness and applicability across different domains.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed comparison with existing methods like CorInfoMax and TiCo, which also use similar approaches, and does not sufficiently differentiate its method from these prior works.
- The theoretical analysis provided is insufficient, with only a few theorems and propositions that do not fully explain the method's effectiveness or the intuition behind its design choices.
- The experimental results are limited to small datasets and do not include a broader range of datasets or comparisons with a more diverse set of SSL methods, which could strengthen the claims of the paper.
- The paper does not adequately address the limitations of the proposed method, which could help in understanding its scope and applicability.
- The method's performance on larger datasets like ImageNet is not convincingly superior to other methods, which raises concerns about its scalability and effectiveness in more complex scenarios.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors provide a more detailed comparison with CorInfoMax and TiCo, highlighting the specific differences and advantages of their method?
- How does the FroSSL method compare in terms of training time and computational cost to other SSL methods, especially in scenarios where training for a large number of epochs is necessary?
- Can the authors provide more theoretical insights into the method's design choices, particularly why the Frobenius norm of the covariance matrix is effective in minimizing redundancy?
- How does the FroSSL method perform when combined with other SSL methods, and what are the potential benefits or drawbacks of such integration?
- Could the authors provide additional experimental results, especially on larger datasets like ImageNet, to validate the method's effectiveness in more complex scenarios?
