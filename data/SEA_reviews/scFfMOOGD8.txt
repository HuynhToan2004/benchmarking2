 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel method for inserting invisible triggers into diffusion models, enhancing their vulnerability to backdoor attacks. The proposed framework, formulated as a bi-level optimization problem, is designed to optimize the diffusion model to generate target images when triggered, while keeping the perturbations imperceptible. The method is evaluated across various datasets and diffusion models, including both unconditional and conditional models, and demonstrates the effectiveness of the proposed method in achieving high utility and specificity. The paper also discusses the potential implications of these findings for the security of diffusion models in real-world applications.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel approach to injecting invisible triggers into diffusion models, which is a significant advancement in the field of backdoor attacks.
- The method is formulated as a bi-level optimization problem, which provides a clear and structured approach to the problem.
- The paper is well-written, with clear explanations of the methodology and results, making it accessible and understandable to a broad audience.
- The proposed method achieves high utility and high specificity, as demonstrated by the experimental results.
- The paper provides a comprehensive evaluation of the proposed method, including detailed experimental setups and results, which helps in validating the effectiveness of the approach.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed discussion on the limitations of the proposed method, which could help in understanding its practical applicability and potential drawbacks.
- The evaluation of the method is limited to a few datasets and diffusion models, which may not fully demonstrate the generalizability of the approach.
- There is a lack of comparison with existing methods, which could help in positioning the proposed method within the current research landscape.
- The paper does not adequately address the potential negative societal impacts of the proposed method, which is a critical consideration for any technology that could be used maliciously.
- The methodology and some of the experimental setups are not clearly explained, which could hinder the reproducibility and understanding of the results.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors provide a more detailed discussion on the limitations of the proposed method and how these might affect its practical application?
- How does the proposed method compare to existing methods in terms of effectiveness and efficiency?
- Can the authors provide more details on the experimental setup, particularly in the conditional generation case, to clarify the conditions under which the method was tested?
- How does the method perform when the poison rate is varied, and what are the implications of this for the effectiveness of the attack?
- Could the authors elaborate on the potential negative societal impacts of the proposed method and discuss any mitigation strategies that could be employed to minimize these risks?
