 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a method for generating state-action sequences from video demonstrations, utilizing a pre-trained Vision-Language Model (VLM) and a large language model (GPT-4). The approach involves GPT-4 asking VLM questions to extract salient information from images, which are then used to predict current states and actions. This method is evaluated on the EPIC-Kitchens dataset, showing improved performance over existing methods. The paper also introduces a new dataset for state-action generation, which is annotated with human-labeled state predicates. Despite its innovative approach and experimental validation, the paper is criticized for its lack of novelty, as similar methods have been previously explored, and for not sufficiently addressing the generalization of the proposed method to other datasets.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, with a clear and easy-to-follow structure, making it accessible to readers.
- The idea of using large language models (LLMs) to ask questions about images is innovative and shows potential for future research.
- The method demonstrates improved performance over existing methods, as shown in the experimental results.
- The paper introduces a new dataset for state-action generation, which is annotated with human-labeled state predicates, enhancing the dataset's utility.
- The use of a pre-trained Vision-Language Model (VLM) and a large language model (GPT-4) to extract salient information from images and generate state-action sequences is a promising approach.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a thorough analysis of the performance of GPT-4 in generating state-action sequences, which is crucial for understanding the effectiveness of the proposed method.
- The paper does not sufficiently address the generalization of the proposed method to other datasets, raising concerns about its applicability beyond the EPIC-Kitchens dataset.
- The novelty of the proposed method is questionable, as similar approaches have been previously explored, such as using LLMs to ask questions about images.
- The paper does not provide a detailed analysis of the computational resources required for the proposed method, which could limit its practical implementation.
- There is a lack of clarity in the experimental setup, particularly in the description of the dataset and the evaluation metrics used.
- The paper does not adequately discuss the limitations of the proposed method, which could hinder its adoption in practical applications.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors provide a more detailed analysis of the performance of GPT-4 in generating state-action sequences, including the accuracy and efficiency of the model?
- How does the proposed method generalize to other datasets beyond EPIC-Kitchens, and what steps are being taken to address the potential limitations in generalization?
- Could the authors clarify the experimental setup, particularly the dataset used and the evaluation metrics, to ensure reproducibility and transparency?
- How does the proposed method compare to other state-of-the-art methods in terms of performance and computational efficiency?
- What are the specific computational resources required for the proposed method, and how does this impact its practical implementation?
- Can the authors provide more details on the annotation process for the new dataset, including the inter-annotator agreement and the criteria for labeling state predicates?
