 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel method for constructing instruction-following datasets by diversifying tasks per input, termed "scaling tasks per input." This method contrasts with traditional approaches that either scale inputs or tasks independently. The proposed method is evaluated on several benchmarks, showing improved performance compared to existing methods. The dataset, named MUFFIN, is introduced, which is claimed to be the first of its kind to align with the scaling tasks per input paradigm. The paper is well-written and includes extensive evaluations, both automatic and human, demonstrating the dataset's effectiveness in enhancing the instruction-following capabilities of large language models (LLMs).
