 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel framework called ManyFairHPO, designed to optimize fairness metrics in machine learning models by treating fairness as a multi-objective optimization problem. This approach allows for the exploration of various fairness metrics and their trade-offs with model performance. The paper employs a state-of-the-art many-objective hyper-parameter optimization (MOHPO) method to manage these complexities, demonstrating its effectiveness through a variety of experiments. The framework's utility is highlighted by its ability to identify and address conflicts between different fairness metrics, which is crucial for ensuring fairness in machine learning applications. However, the paper's novelty is questioned due to its reliance on existing methods, and the clarity of its presentation needs improvement.

**Strengths:**
- The paper addresses a critical issue in machine learning, specifically the optimization of fairness metrics, which is an area of increasing importance.
- The proposed method, ManyFairHPO, is a well-executed, well-presented approach that provides a clear solution to the problem of managing multiple fairness metrics.
- The paper includes a thorough set of experiments, which are detailed and provide a comprehensive understanding of the framework's capabilities.
- The paper is well-written, making it accessible and understandable to a broad audience, which is crucial for advancing the understanding and application of fairness in machine learning.
- The use of a multi-objective hyperparameter optimization (MOHPO) approach to manage the complexities of fairness optimization is innovative and provides a solid theoretical foundation for the work.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper's novelty is limited as it primarily builds upon existing methods like NSGA-III and hyperparameter optimization techniques, which have been previously applied in similar contexts.
- The clarity of the paper needs improvement, particularly in the presentation of figures and the explanation of certain methodologies and results.
- The paper's contribution to the field is not clearly articulated, which makes it difficult to assess the significance of the work.
- The experiments are limited to a small set of datasets and models, which might not adequately demonstrate the framework's effectiveness across a broader range of applications.
- The paper does not sufficiently discuss the limitations of the proposed method, which could help in understanding its applicability and potential challenges.
- The paper's reliance on a single dataset and model for some of its experiments weakens the validity of the results and the generalizability of the findings.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors provide a more detailed comparison with existing methods, particularly in terms of the specific advantages and disadvantages of ManyFairHPO over these approaches?
- How does the paper address the issue of conflicting fairness metrics, and what are the implications of these conflicts for the overall fairness of the models produced?
- Could the authors clarify the methodology used in the experiments, particularly the selection of datasets and models, and the rationale behind these choices?
- How does the paper ensure that the fairness metrics used are not only statistically valid but also practically relevant and effective in real-world applications?
- Can the authors discuss the limitations of their method and how these might affect its applicability and effectiveness in different scenarios?
- How does the paper address the potential for overfitting or other biases in the models produced, especially in scenarios where fairness metrics are in conflict?
