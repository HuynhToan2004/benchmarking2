 **Summary:**
The paper addresses the issue of threshold inconsistency in deep metric learning (DML) by introducing a novel variance-based metric named Operating-Point-Inconsistency-Score (OPIS) to quantify this inconsistency. The authors propose a Threshold-Consistent Margin (TCM) loss to address this issue, which aims to improve threshold consistency while maintaining accuracy. The TCM loss is designed to penalize hard sample pairs, promoting uniformity in representation structures across classes. Extensive experiments demonstrate the effectiveness of TCM in enhancing threshold consistency while preserving accuracy. The paper also discusses the limitations of OPIS and TCM, including their suitability for few-shot evaluation scenarios and their potential failure in scenarios with significant distribution shifts or label noise.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel variance-based metric named Operating-Point-Inconsistency-Score (OPIS) to quantify the threshold inconsistency of a DML model, which is a significant contribution to the field.
- The authors propose a Threshold-Consistent Margin (TCM) loss, a simple yet effective regularization technique that can be combined with any base losses and backbone architectures to improve threshold consistency in DML.
- The paper is well-written, with clear explanations of the motivation, methodology, and experimental results, making it accessible and understandable to a broad audience.
- The paper provides extensive experiments that demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed TCM loss in enhancing threshold consistency while maintaining or even enhancing accuracy.
- The paper discusses the limitations of OPIS and TCM, which shows a high level of self-awareness and honesty in the research.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed comparison with other metrics like OneFace and One-Threshold-for-All, which are closely related to OPIS.
- The paper does not provide a detailed discussion on the limitations of the proposed method, such as the high computational cost and the need for large-scale datasets for training.
- The paper could benefit from more detailed visualizations and explanations of the TCM loss and its impact on model performance.
- The paper does not sufficiently address the scalability of the proposed method for large-scale datasets, which could be a significant limitation in practical applications.
- The paper could benefit from a more detailed discussion on the limitations of the proposed method, such as its applicability in few-shot evaluation scenarios and its potential failure in scenarios with significant distribution shifts or label noise.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors provide more details on how the TCM loss is designed to address the threshold inconsistency issue in DML?
- How does the TCM loss compare with other existing methods like OneFace and One-Threshold-for-All in terms of effectiveness and computational efficiency?
- Could the authors provide more visualizations and explanations of the TCM loss and its impact on model performance?
- How does the TCM loss handle the scalability issues in large-scale datasets, and what are the implications for practical applications?
