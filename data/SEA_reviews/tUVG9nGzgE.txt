 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel approach to learning conditional invariances in domain adaptation, focusing on non-commutative invariance (NCI) that leverages semantic complementarity between domains. The authors propose a theoretical framework that bounds the risk of the optimal NCI encoder, demonstrating that NCI can achieve state-of-the-art (SOTA) performance in domain adaptation. The paper provides a comprehensive theoretical analysis and empirical evidence to support the effectiveness of NCI, showing that it can outperform traditional methods in various benchmarks. However, concerns are raised about the practicality of the assumptions, the clarity of the presentation, and the scope of the experimental validation.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, clear, and easy to follow, with a clear motivation and a novel approach to learning conditional invariances through non-commutative invariance (NCI).
- The theoretical analysis is rigorous, and the experimental results are convincing, showing that the proposed method outperforms existing methods in domain adaptation.
- The paper introduces a new approach to learning conditional invariances, which is a significant contribution to the field of domain adaptation and invariance learning.
- The results are promising, and the paper provides a comprehensive theoretical analysis that supports the proposed method.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed discussion on the practical implications of the assumptions made, such as the assumption of semantic complementarity between domains, which might not hold in all scenarios.
- The experimental validation is limited, and the paper does not compare the proposed method with the latest state-of-the-art methods, which could undermine the claimed improvements.
- The paper does not discuss the limitations of the proposed method, which could be a significant drawback for readers looking for a comprehensive understanding of the method's applicability and effectiveness.
- There are some typographical errors and inconsistencies in the presentation of the paper, which could detract from its professionalism and clarity.
- The paper does not adequately address the practical implications of the theoretical results, particularly in real-world applications where the assumptions might not hold.

**Questions:**
