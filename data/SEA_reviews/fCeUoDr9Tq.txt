 **Summary:**
The paper introduces RoboShot, a method designed to improve the robustness of pre-trained models in zero-shot settings without the need for labeled data or fine-tuning. It leverages insights from language models to identify and neutralize harmful components in model embeddings, thereby enhancing performance on various image and NLP classification tasks. The method involves extracting harmful and helpful components from language models and using them to adjust the model's embeddings, aiming to reduce the impact of harmful correlations. Theoretical analysis and extensive experiments across multiple datasets support the effectiveness of the proposed method.

**Strengths:**
- The paper addresses a significant and challenging problem in the field of machine learning, focusing on the robustification of zero-shot models without the need for labeled data, training, or manual specification.
- The proposed method, RoboShot, is novel and innovative, utilizing language models to extract harmful and helpful components from task descriptions, which are then used to adjust model embeddings without requiring manual intervention.
- Theoretical analysis provided supports the method's effectiveness in reducing the impact of harmful correlations, and empirical results demonstrate significant improvements in worst-group accuracy across various datasets.
- The paper is well-written, with clear explanations and visualizations that aid in understanding the methodology and its implications.
- The method's simplicity and the ability to be applied to a variety of pretrained and language models make it a versatile tool for improving zero-shot model robustness.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed discussion on the limitations and potential negative societal impacts of the proposed method.
- There is a lack of clarity on how the method would perform in scenarios with more complex or nuanced spurious correlations, such as those involving multiple attributes or subtle biases.
- The paper does not sufficiently address the potential biases that could be introduced by the language models used to extract insights, which could impact the reliability and generalizability of the results.
- The method's effectiveness is not thoroughly evaluated on more complex or nuanced datasets, which could better demonstrate its robustness and applicability in real-world scenarios.
- The paper does not provide a comprehensive comparison with existing methods, which could help in understanding the relative advantages and disadvantages of the proposed method.
- The paper's reliance on a single language model for insight extraction may limit its applicability and robustness, as different language models might produce different insights.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors provide more details on how the method would perform in scenarios with more complex or nuanced spurious correlations, such as those involving multiple attributes or subtle biases?
- How does the proposed method address potential biases that could be introduced by the language models used to extract insights?
- Could the authors elaborate on the method's performance when applied to more complex or nuanced datasets, and discuss any potential limitations or challenges in such applications?
- What are the potential societal impacts of the proposed method, and how might these impacts be mitigated or addressed?
- How does the method compare to other existing methods in terms of performance, robustness, and applicability?
- Can the authors provide a more detailed explanation of the method's theoretical underpinnings and how they relate to practical applications?
- What are the implications of using different language models for insight extraction, and how might this affect the method's performance and applicability?
