 **Summary:**
The paper explores the transferability of availability poisoning attacks across different learning paradigms, focusing on the generation of high-frequency perturbations through a combination of supervised and unsupervised contrastive learning. It highlights the ineffectiveness of existing attacks when the victim employs a different learning method and proposes a novel method, Transferable Poisoning, to address this issue. The paper presents extensive experiments on image datasets, demonstrating the superior transferability of the proposed method. It also discusses the impact of different learning paradigms on the transferability of poisoning attacks, offering insights into the effectiveness of attacks across various models and datasets.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, making it easy to follow, and provides a clear motivation for the research.
- The authors effectively address the problem of transferability in poisoning attacks across different learning paradigms, which is a significant issue in machine learning security.
- The proposed method is simple yet effective, and the experiments are extensive, demonstrating the effectiveness of the proposed method.
- The paper provides a detailed analysis of the transferability of poisoning attacks across different learning paradigms, which is a crucial aspect of machine learning security.
- The paper is well-organized, with clear figures and tables that aid in understanding the experimental results.
- The authors have conducted a comprehensive literature review, discussing various types of poisoning attacks and their transferability.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed analysis of the limitations of the proposed method, which could help in understanding its practical applicability and potential weaknesses.
- The paper does not sufficiently discuss the computational complexity of the proposed method, which is crucial for understanding its scalability and efficiency.
- The methodology used in the paper, particularly the use of TAP and CP, is not sufficiently differentiated from existing methods, which could lead to confusion about the novelty of the proposed approach.
- The paper does not adequately address the generalizability of the findings to other learning paradigms beyond those tested.
- The paper could benefit from a more detailed discussion on the transferability of the proposed method to different model architectures and datasets, especially those beyond image datasets.
- There is a lack of discussion on the potential societal impacts of the proposed method, which could be a significant concern for the application of the findings in real-world scenarios.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors provide more details on the computational complexity of the proposed method and discuss its scalability in terms of training time and resource requirements?
- How does the proposed method perform when applied to other learning paradigms beyond those tested, such as reinforcement learning or unsupervised learning?
- Can the authors clarify the specific contributions of the proposed method beyond the existing methods, particularly in terms of the novelty and effectiveness of the approach?
- How does the proposed method perform when transferred to other model architectures, such as those used in natural language processing or other domains?
- Could the authors provide more details on the generalizability of the findings to other datasets and models, especially those that are not image-based?
- What are the potential societal impacts of the proposed method, and how can these be mitigated to ensure the ethical use of the findings?
