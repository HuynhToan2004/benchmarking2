 **Summary:**
The paper introduces WebArena, a realistic and reproducible web environment designed to evaluate the performance of autonomous agents in executing tasks on the web. This environment includes four websites from different domains: e-commerce, social forum discussions, collaborative software development, and content management. The authors have curated a benchmark of 812 tasks and conducted experiments using various baseline agents to assess their performance. The results indicate that current large language models (LLMs) struggle to achieve high success rates, highlighting the need for further research and development in this area. The paper also discusses the challenges in evaluating web-based tasks and provides insights into the limitations of current LLMs.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces WebArena, a realistic and reproducible web environment designed for evaluating autonomous agents, which includes four websites from common domains and tools for task execution.
- It provides a comprehensive benchmark of 812 tasks, which are diverse, long-horizon, and designed to emulate tasks humans perform on the internet.
- The paper includes detailed evaluations and ablation studies, which demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed environment in assessing the performance of LLMs in executing web-based tasks.
- The evaluation is extensive, covering a wide range of tasks and models, and the results show that current state-of-the-art LLMs perform significantly worse than human agents, highlighting the need for further research and development.
- The paper is well-written, clear, and easy to follow, with a good balance of technical details and high-level insights, making it accessible to a broad audience.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a clear discussion on the limitations of the proposed environment, which is crucial for understanding its applicability and scope.
- There is a lack of comparison with other environments, which could help in evaluating the advantages and disadvantages of WebArena.
- The paper does not provide sufficient details on the process of collecting intents and the evaluation annotation, which are crucial for understanding the validity and reliability of the results.
- The paper does not include a detailed discussion on how the results of the experiments could be applied in practical scenarios, which could limit the impact of the research.
- The paper does not address the potential societal impacts of the research, which is a significant omission in the ethical considerations of AI research.

**Questions:**

- How do you ensure the reliability and validity of the results, especially in terms of the annotation process and the evaluation metrics used?

- Can you provide more details on how the environment was built, including the tools and frameworks used, and how the data was collected and processed?
