 **Summary:**
The paper introduces AutoParLLM, a novel framework that leverages Graph Neural Networks (GNNs) and Large Language Models (LLMs) to automatically discover parallelism in sequential programs and generate parallel versions. The framework consists of two main components: a GNN-based parallelism discovery and pattern detection module, and an LLM-based code generator. The methodology is evaluated on benchmarks such as NAS Parallel Benchmark and Rodinia Benchmark, demonstrating improvements in runtime performance and code quality. However, concerns are raised about the novelty of the approach, the depth of evaluation, and the clarity of presentation.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, with clear and detailed descriptions of the methodology, including the use of Graph Neural Networks (GNNs) and Large Language Models (LLMs) for parallelization.
- The motivation behind the research is clearly articulated, highlighting the potential of LLMs to generate parallel code more effectively with the aid of GNNs.
- The experimental setup is robust, involving a variety of benchmarks and different LLMs, which helps in validating the effectiveness of the proposed method.
- The paper introduces a new evaluation metric, OMPScore, which is shown to have a higher correlation with human judgment compared to existing metrics.
- The paper demonstrates that the proposed method can improve the runtime performance of LLM-generated parallel code, as shown by experiments on NAS Parallel Benchmark and Rodinia Benchmark.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a thorough comparison with existing methods, particularly in terms of runtime performance and the quality of generated code.
- The novelty of the approach is questioned, as similar methods using GNNs and LLMs for code parallelization have been previously explored.
- The evaluation section is limited in scope, focusing only on a few benchmarks and not providing a comprehensive comparison across different LLMs and benchmarks.
- The paper does not adequately address the issue of over-optimistic evaluation metrics, which might not accurately reflect the practical performance of the generated code.
- The presentation of the paper could be improved to better convey the technical details and the contributions of the research.
- There are concerns about the generalizability of the method to larger or more complex programs, as the evaluation is limited to small-scale benchmarks.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors clarify the specific contributions of their method compared to existing works, particularly in terms of novelty and effectiveness?
- How does the proposed method compare in terms of runtime performance and code quality with other existing methods, especially when evaluated on a broader set of benchmarks?
- What are the specific advantages of using GNNs over other methods for parallelism discovery and pattern detection?
- How does the proposed method handle the issue of over-optimistic evaluation metrics, and what steps are taken to ensure that the generated code performs well in practical scenarios?
- Can the authors provide more detailed comparisons with other LLM-based methods, particularly in terms of the effectiveness of the GNN-guided prompting in improving the performance of LLMs?
- Could the authors discuss the potential of extending the method to support more types of patterns or to include auto-tuning information in the prompt?
