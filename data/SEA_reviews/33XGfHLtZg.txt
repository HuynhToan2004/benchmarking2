 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel approach to conformal risk control, extending the traditional conformal prediction framework to handle various monotone loss functions. This method, which includes the split conformal prediction algorithm, provides a guarantee of controlling the expected value of the loss function at a specified level. The authors demonstrate the application of this approach in different scenarios, including tumor segmentation, multilabel classification, and hierarchical image classification. The paper also discusses extensions to non-monotone loss functions and other practical applications, although it has been critiqued for its limited novelty and the lack of significant new theoretical contributions.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel approach to conformal risk control, which is an extension of conformal prediction, and provides a guarantee of controlling the expected value of a monotone loss function.
- The paper is well-written, with clear and concise explanations of the proposed method and its theoretical underpinnings, making it accessible to a broad audience.
- The paper includes practical examples and demonstrates the application of the proposed method in various real-world scenarios, which enhances its relevance and applicability.
- The paper is well-motivated, with a clear rationale for extending conformal prediction to other loss functions, which is supported by a range of examples and theoretical justifications.
- The paper provides a comprehensive set of theoretical results, including proofs of the main theorems, which are supported by rigorous mathematical analysis.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks significant novelty as it primarily extends existing conformal prediction techniques to handle different loss functions, which might not be perceived as a significant advancement in the field.
- The paper does not include a comparison with other existing methods for conformal risk control, which could have provided a more robust evaluation of the proposed method.
- The paper does not adequately address the computational complexity of the proposed method, which could be a significant limitation in practical applications.
- The paper's theoretical contributions are seen as incremental, with the proofs and theoretical results not sufficiently distinct from existing works.
- The paper's experimental results are not sufficiently detailed, and the results presented are not convincingly superior to existing methods, which could undermine the paper's claims.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors clarify how the proposed method can be extended to handle non-monotone loss functions?
- Could the authors provide a more detailed comparison with other existing methods for conformal risk control, including a discussion of the computational efficiency and practical applicability of their approach?
- How does the proposed method perform in terms of computational complexity, and what are the implications of this for its practical use?
- Can the authors provide a more detailed discussion on the practical implications of the assumptions made in the paper, particularly in scenarios where these assumptions might not hold?
- How does the proposed method compare in terms of theoretical contributions with existing works, and what are the specific advantages or disadvantages of the proposed method compared to these?
