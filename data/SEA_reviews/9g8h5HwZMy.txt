 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel diffusion model named SubgDiff, which integrates discrete masking and continuous diffusion processes for molecular representation learning. This model allows for the selective addition of noise to specific molecular substructures, enhancing the understanding of molecular conformation generation and 3D molecular property prediction. The authors propose a new diffusion framework, MaskedDiff, which extends the capabilities of diffusion models in molecular representation learning. The paper also includes a mask predictor to estimate mask vectors during training, which is crucial for molecular generation. The methodology is supported by extensive experiments, demonstrating the superior performance of SubgDiff over existing methods.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel diffusion framework, MaskedDiff, which extends the capabilities of diffusion models in molecular representation learning.
- The SubgDiff model, a specific application of MaskedDiff, is highlighted for its superior performance in molecular conformation generation and 3D molecular property prediction tasks.
- The paper is well-organized and clearly written, making the complex concepts accessible and understandable.
- Extensive experiments are conducted to demonstrate the superior performance of the proposed method, including comparisons with existing methods and ablation studies.
- The paper includes a mask predictor to estimate mask vectors during training, which is crucial for molecular generation.
- The use of discrete masking in conjunction with continuous diffusion processes is innovative and potentially impactful for molecular representation learning.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper could benefit from more detailed explanations of the masked diffusion model and its implications for molecular generation.
- The experimental results are not consistently superior across all tasks, and the improvements over existing methods are sometimes marginal.
- The paper lacks a thorough discussion on the limitations of the proposed method, which is crucial for understanding its applicability and potential drawbacks.
- There are some minor issues with the presentation, such as unclear figures and a lack of clarity in some sections of the paper.
- The paper could benefit from more detailed comparisons with existing methods, particularly in terms of computational efficiency and resource requirements.

**Questions:**
- Could you clarify the role and impact of the mask predictor in the molecular generation process?
- How does the discrete masking affect the performance and applicability of the model in different molecular structures?
- Can you provide more details on the computational efficiency and resource requirements of the proposed method compared to existing methods?
- How does the proposed method handle the issue of independent noise injection in previous diffusion models?
- Can you discuss the potential limitations of the proposed method and how they might impact its practical application in real-world scenarios?
- How does the proposed method compare in terms of computational cost and performance with other state-of-the-art methods?
- Can you provide more details on the training and sampling algorithms of SubgDiff, particularly in the context of molecular generation?
