 **Summary:**
The paper introduces ESSformer, a segment-based sparse Transformer designed for multivariate long-term time-series forecasting. It employs two novel attention mechanisms: Periodic Attention (PeriA) and Random-Partition Attention (R-PartA) to address computational efficiency and performance. PeriA leverages a dilated attention mechanism to capture temporal dependencies between segments, while R-PartA partitions features randomly to capture inter-feature dependencies. The model is tested across seven datasets, demonstrating competitive performance and computational efficiency compared to existing methods. However, concerns were raised regarding the novelty of the approach, as similar mechanisms have been previously explored in other models.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, easy to follow, and the motivation is clearly articulated.
- The proposed method, ESSformer, demonstrates competitive performance compared to other state-of-the-art models in time-series forecasting.
- The introduction of sparsity into attention mechanisms is an innovative approach that helps reduce computational complexity.
- The paper provides a comprehensive analysis of the proposed methods, including ablation studies, and demonstrates the efficiency of the model through complexity analysis.
- The model's robustness under feature dropping is highlighted, showcasing its applicability in real-world scenarios.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed comparison with the latest state-of-the-art models, particularly those that have been released after the submission of the paper.
- The novelty of the approach is questioned as similar mechanisms have been explored in previous models, such as the use of dilated attention for periodic dependencies and random partitioning for feature interactions.
- The paper does not adequately address the computational cost reduction claims, and the complexity analysis may not accurately reflect the practical computational efficiency.
- There is a lack of discussion on the limitations of the proposed model and the potential societal impact of the research.
- The paper does not sufficiently compare the proposed model with other sparse attention mechanisms that have been previously proposed.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors clarify how the proposed model performs against the latest state-of-the-art models, especially those that have been released after the submission of the paper?
- How does the model handle scenarios where the number of features is not a multiple of the number of groups, and what are the implications of this limitation?
- Can the authors provide more details on how the model handles irregular time series, particularly in terms of missing data?
- Is there a plan to release the code for the proposed model to facilitate reproducibility and further research?
- Can the authors elaborate on the computational efficiency claims and provide more detailed experimental results to support these claims?
- Could the authors discuss the potential societal impacts of their research and how they plan to mitigate any negative consequences?
