 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel method called Evol-Instruct, designed to automatically generate diverse and complex instruction data for large language models (LLMs). This method iteratively evolves simple instructions into more complex ones, using a combination of in-depth and in-breadth evolving strategies. The evolved instruction data is then used to fine-tune the LLaMA model, resulting in a new model named WizardLM. The paper claims that WizardLM outperforms existing models like Alpaca and Vicuna across various benchmarks, demonstrating the effectiveness of the proposed method. However, concerns are raised about the method's reliance on ChatGPT for data generation and the potential for overfitting due to the similarity of the evolved data to the training data.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel method, Evol-Instruct, for generating diverse and complex instruction data for large language models (LLMs), which is a significant contribution to the field.
- The methodology is well-articulated, with clear descriptions of the evolutionary process and the use of a variety of prompts to ensure a diverse set of instructions.
- The paper provides a comprehensive evaluation of the generated data, comparing it against existing models like Alpaca and Vicuna, and demonstrating the effectiveness of the generated data through both automatic and human evaluations.
- The approach is scalable and can be used to generate large amounts of instruction data, which is crucial for training LLMs.
- The paper is well-written, making it accessible and easy to follow, and includes a detailed analysis of the method's impact on model performance.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper's reliance on ChatGPT for data generation raises concerns about the method's ability to generate truly diverse and original instructions, as the data may be overly influenced by the biases and limitations of the model used.
- There is a lack of detailed analysis on the quality and diversity of the evolved instructions, particularly in terms of how they compare to existing datasets like ShareGPT.
- The paper does not provide sufficient details on the quality control measures used to ensure the accuracy and relevance of the generated instructions, which is crucial for ensuring the effectiveness of the fine-tuning process.
- The paper's claims about the superiority of the generated data over existing datasets are not adequately supported by evidence, particularly in terms of how the data was evaluated and the criteria used for evaluation.
- There is a lack of transparency in the methodology, particularly in the details of the evolutionary process and the evaluation of the generated data, which could limit the reproducibility and validation of the results.

**Questions:**
- How does the paper ensure that the generated instructions are not overly influenced by the biases and limitations of ChatGPT?
- Can the authors provide more details on the quality control measures used to ensure the accuracy and relevance of the generated instructions?
- How does the paper address the potential for overfitting due to the similarity of the evolved data to the training data?
- Could the authors provide more detailed examples of the evolved instructions and the evaluation criteria used to assess their quality?
- How does the paper address the concerns about the transparency and reproducibility of the results, particularly in terms of the methodology and the evaluation process?
