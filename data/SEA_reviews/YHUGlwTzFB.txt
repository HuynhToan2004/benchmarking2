 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel problem setting, Active Test-Time Adaptation (ATTA), which integrates active learning with fully test-time adaptation. The authors propose a method, SimATTA, which utilizes an incremental clustering technique and selective entropy minimization to address catastrophic forgetting and distribution shifts. Theoretical analysis and extensive experiments are conducted to validate the proposed method's effectiveness and efficiency. The paper also discusses the challenges of catastrophic forgetting and distribution shifts in the ATTA setting and provides a learning theory analysis to demonstrate the effectiveness of ATTA.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel problem setting, Active Test-Time Adaptation (ATTA), which is original and addresses the challenges of catastrophic forgetting and distribution shifts in test-time adaptation.
- The authors provide a learning theory analysis to demonstrate the effectiveness of ATTA, which is a significant contribution to the field.
- The proposed ATTA method is simple yet effective, as shown by experimental results that are consistent with theoretical analyses.
- The paper is well-written, clear, and easy to follow, making it accessible to readers.
- The methodology is well-motivated, with a clear explanation of the rationale behind the design choices, and the experimental results are convincing.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper does not adequately address the privacy concerns related to active learning, particularly the potential for privacy leakage when using ATTA.
- The theoretical analysis in the paper is limited, with some assumptions (such as the VC-dimension of the hypothesis class) that may not be practical in real-world applications.
- The paper lacks a detailed discussion on the limitations of the proposed method, which is crucial for understanding its applicability and scope.
- The paper does not sufficiently compare the proposed method with other active learning methods, which could provide a more comprehensive evaluation of its effectiveness.
- There is a lack of clarity in some parts of the paper, particularly in the definitions and explanations of key terms and concepts.
- The paper could benefit from a more detailed discussion on the computational complexity of the proposed method and its scalability to larger datasets.

**Questions:**
- Could you provide more details on the privacy concerns related to active learning in the ATTA setting? How does the paper address these concerns?
- How does the proposed method perform when compared to other active learning methods, particularly in terms of privacy and computational efficiency?
- Could you clarify the assumptions made in the theoretical analysis, particularly the VC-dimension of the hypothesis class and its practical applicability?
- How does the proposed method handle the trade-off between the number of labeled samples and the number of unlabeled samples, especially in scenarios where the number of labeled samples is limited?
- Could you provide more details on the computational complexity and scalability of the proposed method, especially in relation to the size of the dataset?
- How does the paper ensure the reproducibility of the results, and what steps have been taken to validate the effectiveness of the proposed method across different datasets and scenarios?
