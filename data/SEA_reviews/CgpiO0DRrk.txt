 **Summary:**
The paper explores the challenges of video caching in a collaborative edge network, focusing on the impact of request characteristics on different servers. It proposes a Knowledge Distillation-based Cross-domain Collaborative Edge Caching (KDCdCEC) framework to address the issue of request-sparse EBSs and improve overall cache hit rates. The framework includes a DRL-based KD-enhanced caching agent, a deep deterministic policy gradient-based algorithm, and a content-aware request routing mechanism. The authors have conducted experiments using real-world data from Kuaishou, demonstrating the effectiveness of the proposed solution in enhancing cache hit rates, reducing latency, and minimizing traffic costs. However, the paper's contribution is questioned due to its similarity to existing works and the lack of clarity in its methodological contributions.

**Strengths:**
- The paper addresses a significant and timely problem of video caching at the edge network, focusing on the impact of request characteristics on different servers.
- It proposes a knowledge distillation-based cross-domain collaborative edge caching framework, which is innovative in its approach to addressing the challenges of video caching.
- The paper is well-written and easy to follow, making it accessible to a broad audience.
- The authors have conducted experiments using real-world data from Kuaishou, which adds credibility to the findings.
- The paper presents a comprehensive study of the impact of request characteristics on different servers and proposes a knowledge distillation-based cross-domain collaborative edge caching framework to address the identified challenges.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed explanation of the differences and contributions of the proposed framework compared to existing works, particularly in terms of the use of DRL and knowledge distillation.
- The paper's methodology section is not clear, and the specific contributions of the proposed framework are not well-defined.
- The evaluation metrics used in the paper are not clearly defined, making it difficult to assess the effectiveness of the proposed framework.
- The paper's assumptions about the storage capacity of mobile edge caching servers and the collaboration between servers are not realistic or well-explained.
- The paper does not provide sufficient details about the data used in the experiments, such as the number of videos and requests, which could affect the reproducibility and generalizability of the results.
- The paper does not adequately address the limitations of the proposed framework, such as the assumptions made about the storage capacity and the collaboration between servers.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors provide a detailed comparison of the proposed framework with existing works, highlighting the specific differences and contributions?
- How does the proposed framework address the challenges of request-sparse EBSs, and what are the specific advantages over existing solutions?
- Could the authors clarify the methodology used in the experiments, including the selection of hyperparameters and the details of the data used?
- How does the proposed framework handle the spatial and temporal drift in video requests, and what are the implications for the overall cache hit rate?
- Can the authors provide more details on the data used in the experiments, including the number of videos and requests, and the specifics of the data collection and processing?
- How does the proposed framework scale with increasing numbers of servers and requests, and what are the expected performance improvements in such scenarios?
