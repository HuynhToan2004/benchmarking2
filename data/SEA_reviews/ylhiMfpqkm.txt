 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel approach to pre-training and fine-tuning Generative Flow Networks (GFlowNets) for unsupervised learning of compositional objects. The authors propose an outcome-conditioned GFlowNet (OC-GFN) that learns to reach specified outcomes in a self-supervised manner, using a contrastive learning approach to handle sparse rewards. The methodology includes an amortized predictor to approximate the marginalization required for fine-tuning, which is crucial for adapting to downstream tasks with new rewards. Extensive experiments across various tasks demonstrate the efficacy of the proposed method, showing improvements over strong baselines. However, the paper's presentation and clarity in some sections need improvement, and the experimental setup could be enhanced to include more diverse and challenging tasks.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, clear, and easy to follow, with a clear motivation and a solid theoretical foundation.
- The introduction of outcome-conditioned GFlowNets (OC-GFNs) is a novel approach that addresses the challenge of adapting GFlowNets to unseen reward functions.
- The use of a contrastive learning procedure and outcome teleportation scheme are innovative methods for handling sparse rewards and long-horizon tasks.
- Extensive experiments demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed method, showing improvements over strong baselines.
- The paper provides a theoretical justification for the proposed method and includes proofs in the appendix, enhancing the credibility of the research.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper could benefit from better presentation, particularly in the clarity of figures and the organization of content.
- The experimental setup is somewhat limited, focusing mainly on simple tasks like gridworld and bit sequence generation, which might not adequately demonstrate the method's effectiveness in more complex or realistic scenarios.
- There is a lack of discussion on the limitations of the proposed method, which could provide a more balanced view of its capabilities.
- The paper does not sufficiently compare the proposed method with existing methods, such as GFlowNets, which could help in understanding its advantages and disadvantages more clearly.
- The paper's methodology and some of its claims (e.g., about the generalizability of OC-GFNs) are not sufficiently justified or explained, which could limit the reader's understanding and acceptance of the results.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors clarify the role and impact of outcome teleportation in the pre-training stage, as it seems to significantly affect the learning process?
- How does the amortized predictor ensure the correct estimation of the marginalization required for fine-tuning, and what are the implications if this estimation is inaccurate?
- Could the authors provide more details on the computational complexity of the proposed method, particularly in terms of training and inference times?
- How does the proposed method compare with other goal-conditioned reinforcement learning methods, and what are the specific advantages of using OC-GFNs over these approaches?
- Can the authors discuss the potential limitations of the proposed method, such as its applicability to other types of tasks or its scalability to larger or more complex datasets?
