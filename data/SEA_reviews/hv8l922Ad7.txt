 **Summary:**
The paper introduces two new metrics, Single-neuron Classification (SNC) and Neuron Knockout (NK), to assess the disentanglement of neural representations. These metrics aim to address the limitations of existing metrics by ensuring that each neuron corresponds to a unique factor and by measuring the impact of neuron removal on the prediction of generative factors. The proposed metrics are evaluated on several datasets, showing their effectiveness in measuring disentanglement and their correlation with compositional generalization performance. However, concerns were raised about the limited scope of the experiments, particularly the focus on specific datasets and the lack of comparison with other metrics.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces two new metrics, SNC and NK, which address the limitations of existing metrics by ensuring that each neuron corresponds to a unique factor and by measuring the impact of neuron removal on the prediction of generative factors.
- The proposed metrics are evaluated on several datasets, showing their effectiveness in measuring disentanglement and their correlation with compositional generalization performance.
- The paper is well-written, with clear and concise explanations of the proposed metrics and their implications for disentanglement research.
- The paper provides a thorough review of related work, including a detailed discussion on the limitations of existing metrics.
- The experiments are conducted on several datasets, demonstrating the practical applicability of the proposed metrics.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper's experiments are limited to specific datasets, which may not adequately represent the generalizability of the proposed metrics across different domains.
- The paper does not sufficiently compare the proposed metrics with other metrics, which could have provided a more comprehensive evaluation of their effectiveness.
- The paper's focus on strong disentanglement may not be applicable in all scenarios, as weak disentanglement could also be relevant in certain contexts.
- The paper lacks a detailed discussion on the limitations of the proposed metrics and does not include a discussion on how these limitations could be addressed in future research.
- The paper's writing could be improved in terms of clarity and consistency, particularly in the use of terminology and the presentation of results.

**Questions:**
- How do the proposed metrics perform when applied to other datasets beyond those used in the experiments?
- Can the authors provide more details on the computational complexity and efficiency of the proposed metrics compared to existing metrics?
- How do the proposed metrics compare to other metrics in terms of their ability to measure weak disentanglement, and could the authors suggest modifications to make the metrics applicable to weak disentanglement?
- Could the authors clarify the terminology used in the paper, particularly the use of "neurons" and "neuron alignment," to ensure that the paper is consistent and clear in its definitions?
- How do the proposed metrics perform when applied to different types of neural networks, such as those using different activation functions or architectures?
