 **Summary:**
The paper investigates the application of large language models (LLMs) in constructing knowledge graphs (KGs) from electronic medical records (EMRs), focusing on diseases, factors, treatments, and manifestations associated with age-related macular degeneration (AMD). It evaluates 12 LLMs using precision and recall metrics and explores the effectiveness of different LLM architectures. The study highlights the potential of LLMs in automating KG construction and identifies encoder-decoder models as the most suitable for this task. However, concerns are raised about the clarity of the methodology, the lack of rigorous evaluation, and the generalizability of the findings beyond AMD.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel approach to using LLMs for knowledge graph (KG) construction from electronic medical records (EMRs), focusing on diseases, factors, treatments, and manifestations associated with AMD.
- It evaluates 12 LLMs using precision and recall metrics, providing a comprehensive analysis of their performance.
- The paper is well-organized, clearly written, and easy to follow, making it accessible to a broad audience.
- The use of LLMs to automatically construct KGs from EMR notes is highlighted as a potentially valuable application in the medical domain.
- The paper is the first to evaluate the performance of LLMs for KG construction, which is a significant contribution to the field.

**Weaknesses:**
- The methodology section is not clearly described, making it difficult to understand the specifics of how the LLMs are utilized for KG construction.
- The evaluation of the LLMs is limited, focusing only on AMD and not comparing the results with other diseases or medical conditions.
- The paper lacks a comprehensive comparison with existing methods such as SemRep, which could provide a more robust evaluation of the proposed approach.
- The generalizability of the findings is questionable, as the study is limited to AMD and does not explore how the proposed method could be applied to other diseases or medical conditions.
- The paper does not provide sufficient details on the dataset used, including its size, source, and composition, which could affect the reproducibility and reliability of the results.
- The evaluation metrics used, such as precision and recall, may not be sufficient to assess the quality of KG construction, as they do not account for the completeness or accuracy of the KGs.
- The paper does not adequately address the potential biases in LLMs, which could affect the reliability and validity of the KGs constructed.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors clarify the specifics of how the LLMs are used for KG construction, including the input and output formats, and the details of the prompting and in-context learning methods?
- How do the authors ensure the accuracy and completeness of the KGs constructed by LLMs, especially in cases where the models may generate incorrect or incomplete information?
- Could the authors provide more details on the dataset used, including its size, source, and composition, to enhance the reproducibility and reliability of the results?
- How do the authors address the potential biases in LLMs, and what steps are taken to ensure that the KGs constructed are free from these biases?
- In light of the limitations of the evaluation metrics used, how do the authors plan to expand the evaluation to include other metrics that could better assess the quality of KG construction?
- Given the specific focus on AMD, how do the authors plan to generalize the findings to other medical conditions, and what steps are being taken to ensure the applicability of the proposed method to a broader range of diseases?
