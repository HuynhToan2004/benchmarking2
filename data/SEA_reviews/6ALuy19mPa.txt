 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel training-free method for image restoration called DreamClean, which leverages a pre-trained diffusion model to restore images from various degradations without the need for specific degradation prior knowledge. The method employs a Variance Preservation Sampling (VPS) algorithm to guide low-probability latents towards high-probability regions, ensuring fidelity to the input degraded image. The paper provides both theoretical and empirical evidence supporting the convergence of the proposed method and its effectiveness in handling diverse image degradations. The methodology is supported by extensive experiments that demonstrate the superiority of DreamClean over existing methods in terms of visual and quantitative metrics.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel training-free method for image restoration using a diffusion model, which is a significant advancement in the field.
- The proposed method does not require specific degradation prior knowledge, making it a versatile solution for various image restoration tasks.

- Extensive experiments are conducted to validate the effectiveness of the proposed method, showing its superior performance compared to existing methods in terms of both visual and quantitative metrics.
