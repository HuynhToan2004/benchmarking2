 **Summary:**
The paper introduces the Cross-Query Residual Transformer (CResT) for object goal navigation, focusing on extracting sufficient visual features from RGB images. The CResT model incorporates a Residual Transformer to address gradient vanishing issues and a Random Mask for data augmentation, enhancing the network's generalization capabilities. The experiments, conducted on the AI2-THOR dataset, demonstrate that CResT outperforms existing methods, achieving state-of-the-art results. However, the paper is criticized for its limited experimental validation, lack of comparison with more recent works, and insufficient discussion on the novelty and significance of the proposed methods.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written and easy to follow, making it accessible to readers.
- The proposed method, Cross-Query Residual Transformer (CResT), is technically sound and introduces innovative components such as the Residual Transformer and Random Mask, which are novel contributions to the field.
- The experimental results show that CResT outperforms existing methods on the AI2-THOR dataset, demonstrating its effectiveness in object goal navigation.
- The paper provides a comprehensive ablation study that supports the design choices of CResT and helps in understanding the contributions of its individual components.
- The use of a residual transformer to address the gradient vanishing problem is a significant contribution to the field, as it enhances the backpropagation in the Transformer, enabling one-stage training.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed discussion on the novelty and significance of the proposed methods, particularly the Residual Transformer and Random Mask, which are not sufficiently distinguished from existing methods.
- The experiments are limited to a single dataset (AI2-THOR), and there is no comparison with more recent works such as HOZ, which could have provided a more comprehensive evaluation.
- The paper does not adequately address the computational efficiency of CResT compared to existing methods, which is crucial for practical deployment in real-world scenarios.
- The paper does not provide sufficient visualization or detailed analysis to support the effectiveness of the Random Mask module, which is a critical component of the proposed method.
- The paper lacks a thorough comparison with more advanced baselines and a discussion on the limitations of the proposed method, which could help in understanding its applicability and potential improvements.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors provide a more detailed discussion on the novelty and significance of the proposed methods, particularly the Residual Transformer and Random Mask, compared to existing methods?
- How does the computational efficiency of CResT compare to existing methods, especially in terms of training and inference times?
- Could the authors provide visualization or more detailed analysis to support the effectiveness of the Random Mask module?
- How does the proposed method compare to more advanced baselines such as HOZ, and what are the potential limitations of the proposed method that could affect its applicability in different scenarios?
- Can the authors provide more results on other datasets to validate the generalization ability of the proposed method?
- How does the proposed method handle different levels of noise or distractions in the input images, and what are the potential impacts on the navigation performance?
