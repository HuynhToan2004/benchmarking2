 **Summary:**
The paper investigates the impact of data augmentation on diffusion models, specifically focusing on the timestep-aware augmentation to prevent distribution shifts. The authors propose a method where augmentation strength is adjusted based on the timestep, aiming to improve model performance and prevent overfitting. Extensive experiments are conducted to demonstrate the effectiveness of this method across various settings, including different noise schedules, model sizes, and sampling steps. The paper also addresses the issue of distribution shift caused by data augmentation, showing that it is primarily due to specific timesteps rather than the entire process. Despite its novel approach, the paper has been critiqued for its limited novelty, the lack of comprehensive comparison with state-of-the-art methods, and the clarity of its presentation.

**Strengths:**
- The paper addresses an important and relevant problem in the field of data augmentation for diffusion models, which is crucial for improving model performance and preventing overfitting.
- The experiments are well-designed, comprehensive, and cover a wide range of settings, including different noise schedules, model sizes, and sampling steps.
- The proposed method is simple, intuitive, and does not require additional modules or conditioning inputs, making it easy to implement and use.
- The paper is well-written, clear, and easy to follow, with a clear motivation and a strong empirical validation of the proposed method.
- The findings on the relationship between distribution shifts and data augmentation are novel and provide a significant contribution to the field.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a comprehensive comparison with state-of-the-art methods, which limits the understanding of the proposed method's effectiveness and innovation.
- The novelty of the paper is questioned, as the method of adjusting augmentation strength based on timesteps is similar to existing methods.
- The presentation of the paper could be improved, particularly in the clarity of the figures and the explanation of the methodology.
- The paper does not sufficiently address the issue of distribution shifts caused by data augmentation, especially in the context of diffusion models.
- The paper's experimental setup and results are not sufficiently robust, with some results showing inconsistencies or lack of statistical significance.
- The paper does not sufficiently discuss the limitations of the proposed method, such as its applicability to different types of diffusion models and its potential impact on model training and inference efficiency.

**Questions:**
- How does the proposed method compare to other state-of-the-art methods in terms of effectiveness and efficiency?
- Could the authors provide more detailed explanations or examples of how the method works in practice, particularly in the context of different types of data augmentations?
- How does the proposed method address the issue of distribution shifts caused by data augmentation, especially in the context of diffusion models?
- Can the authors provide more detailed results on the distribution shifts observed in the experiments, and discuss how these shifts affect the model's performance and generalization?
- How does the proposed method impact the model training and inference efficiency, and what are the potential trade-offs between performance and efficiency?
- Can the authors clarify the experimental setup and results, particularly in the context of different noise schedules and model sizes, to ensure the robustness and reproducibility of the findings?
