 **Summary:**
The paper introduces FreqTST, a novel transformer-based model for time series forecasting, which utilizes a frequency-based tokenization method to represent time series data in the frequency domain. This method involves using sinusoidal embeddings to tokenize time series, which are then processed through a transformer encoder. The model is trained using a combination of supervised learning and pre-training tasks, including time series reconstruction and maximum position prediction. The results demonstrate that FreqTST outperforms existing models on various benchmark datasets, showing improvements in long-term forecasting and transfer learning capabilities. However, concerns were raised about the novelty of the frequency tokenization approach and the clarity of the experimental setup and baseline comparisons.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel frequency-based tokenization method for time series data, which is a significant advancement in the field of time series forecasting.
- The proposed FreqTST model effectively utilizes transformer layers to process tokenized frequency spectrum sequences, which enhances the model's ability to handle long-term forecasting tasks.
- The paper provides a comprehensive evaluation of FreqTST, including extensive experiments on multiple datasets and various evaluation metrics, which demonstrates the model's effectiveness and robustness.
- The methodology is well-explained, making the paper accessible and understandable, which is crucial for reproducibility and further research.
- The paper presents a clear and logical structure, with well-organized figures and tables that aid in understanding the proposed methodology and results.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed discussion on the choice of frequency tokenization as the primary method for time series tokenization, which could benefit from a more thorough comparison with other tokenization methods.
- There is a lack of novelty in the frequency tokenization approach, as similar methods have been previously explored in other works.
- The paper could benefit from a more rigorous discussion on the limitations of the proposed method, including potential societal impacts and ethical considerations.
- The experimental setup and baseline comparisons are not clearly defined, which makes it difficult to assess the true effectiveness of the proposed method against existing models.
- The paper does not sufficiently address the computational efficiency of the proposed method, which is crucial for practical applications.
- There are some typographical and grammatical errors that need correction to improve the readability and professionalism of the paper.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors clarify the computational complexity of the proposed method and how it compares to existing models in terms of efficiency?
- How does the choice of frequency tokenization compare to other tokenization methods, and what are the specific advantages of using this approach?
- Could the authors provide a more detailed discussion on the limitations of the proposed method and how these might impact its practical application?
- In terms of baseline comparisons, how does the proposed method compare to other state-of-the-art models, particularly in terms of computational efficiency and long-term forecasting capabilities?
- How does the proposed method perform when applied to non-stationary time series, and what modifications, if any, are required to adapt the method to such scenarios?
- Could the authors provide more details on the pre-training tasks and their impact on the model's performance, particularly in terms of the weighting of different tasks and their individual contributions to the model's effectiveness?
