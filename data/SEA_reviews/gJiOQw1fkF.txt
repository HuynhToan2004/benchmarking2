 **Summary:**
The paper introduces GADGET, a novel method for tabular data synthesis that decomposes the original distribution into K Gaussian-like distributions. This method uses a self-paced learning algorithm to train K smaller models, each specialized in a single Gaussian distribution. The proposed framework is designed to be model-agnostic and can be applied to various generative models for tabular data. The experiments conducted on 11 datasets and 8 baselines demonstrate that GADGET significantly improves the performance of existing tabular data synthesis methods, particularly in terms of sampling quality and diversity. The method is also shown to reduce the number of learnable parameters, enhancing efficiency.
