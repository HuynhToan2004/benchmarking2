 **Summary:**
The paper introduces 3D-GOI, a framework designed for multi-object scene editing using a 3D GAN model, specifically GIRAFFE. The methodology involves a three-stage process: segmentation, coarse estimation, and precise optimization. The segmentation phase separates objects from the background, followed by coarse estimation using a custom neural inversion encoder to extract codes for each object. The final stage employs a round-robin optimization algorithm to refine these codes, allowing for multifaceted editing of multiple objects within a scene. Despite its innovative approach, the paper has been criticized for its limited scope, as it only evaluates the method on specific datasets and lacks a broader evaluation across different GAN models. Additionally, the paper's presentation and clarity need improvement, with concerns about the complexity of the method and the need for more comprehensive experimental validation.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is the first to explore multi-object scene editing, a novel and significant direction in 3D scene generation.
- The proposed method is technically sound, with a clear presentation of the methodology and a detailed explanation of the round-robin optimization algorithm.
- The paper includes a comprehensive evaluation, comparing the proposed method with existing baselines, and demonstrates the effectiveness of the proposed method through qualitative and quantitative results.
- The use of GIRAFFE as the generator model is reasonable, and the method's approach of using a GAN-based inversion method is innovative.
- The paper is well-written, making it easy to follow, and includes detailed explanations of the method's components and experimental setups.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed discussion on the limitations of the method, particularly concerning the segmentation step and the generalization of the method to different datasets.
- The method's complexity and the need for multiple encoders for different attributes could limit its applicability and scalability.
- The experimental evaluation is limited to specific datasets, and the method's performance on more diverse or complex datasets is not explored.
- The paper does not discuss potential negative societal impacts of the proposed method.
- The paper could benefit from clearer figures and better organization of content to enhance readability and understanding.
- The paper's presentation and clarity could be improved, particularly in explaining the optimization process and the roles of different components in the method.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors provide more details on the segmentation process, particularly how it affects the accuracy and efficiency of the method?
- How does the method perform when applied to different GAN models, and what are the implications of using different models for the inversion process?
- Could the authors elaborate on the decision-making process for the optimization order and the rationale behind the round-robin optimization strategy?
- What are the potential societal impacts of the proposed method, and how might these impacts be mitigated or addressed?
- How does the method handle the editing of multiple objects simultaneously, and what are the implications of this approach for the overall performance and efficiency of the method?
- Could the authors provide more qualitative results, especially for multi-object editing scenarios, to better demonstrate the method's capabilities?
