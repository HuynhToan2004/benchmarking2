 **Summary:**
The paper introduces the Planner-Actor-Evaluator (PAE) framework, designed to enhance exploration in reinforcement learning (RL) by integrating external knowledge into the learning process. The framework comprises a Planner that selects relevant knowledge, an Actor that utilizes this knowledge to make decisions, and an Evaluator that rewards the Planner and Actor based on their performance. The PAE is evaluated across 11 environments from BabyAI and MiniHack, demonstrating improved performance and convergence speed compared to baseline methods. The framework utilizes a cross-attention mechanism to align knowledge with the current state, enhancing the agent's ability to understand and apply external knowledge effectively.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, clear, and easy to follow, with a clear motivation and a well-designed framework that effectively integrates external knowledge into the RL process.
- The PAE framework is innovative, introducing a novel approach to incorporating external knowledge into the RL process, which is a significant contribution to the field.
- The methodology is well-explained, and the paper provides a detailed description of the model architecture and its components, which aids in understanding the proposed approach.
- The experiments conducted are thorough, comparing the PAE with various baselines and demonstrating its effectiveness in improving exploration efficiency and task performance.
- The paper provides source code and detailed experimental results, which support the reproducibility of the research and facilitate further study and application of the proposed framework.

**Weaknesses:**

- The evaluation is limited to a small number of environments, which might not adequately demonstrate the generalizability of the PAE framework across diverse scenarios.
- There is a lack of detailed analysis on the computational efficiency of the PAE framework, which is crucial for understanding its scalability and applicability in more complex environments.
