 **Summary:**
The paper explores the application of contrastive reinforcement learning (RL) for robotic manipulation tasks, focusing on the design decisions that enhance the stability and performance of the contrastive RL algorithm. These decisions include neural network architecture, batch size, layer normalization, initialization, and data augmentation. The paper presents a comprehensive set of experiments, including both simulated and real-world scenarios, to validate the effectiveness of these design choices. The experiments demonstrate that the proposed methods can outperform existing baselines, providing a practical approach to deploying self-supervised RL methods on robotic systems.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, clear, and easy to follow, with a comprehensive set of experiments that validate the proposed method's effectiveness.
- The authors provide a thorough investigation into the design decisions necessary for contrastive RL to be effective in robotic manipulation tasks, which is a significant contribution to the field.
- The paper includes a variety of experiments on simulated and real-world robotic manipulation tasks, demonstrating the practical applicability of the proposed method.
- The visualizations and explanations of the method's operation are well-presented, making it accessible and understandable to a broad audience.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed discussion on the limitations of the proposed method and how it compares to other existing methods, which could provide a more comprehensive understanding of its applicability and effectiveness.
- There is a lack of discussion on the potential societal implications of the work, which is crucial for ensuring the responsible deployment of the technology.
- The paper does not sufficiently address the scalability of the method to more complex environments or tasks, which is a significant limitation given the complexity of real-world robotic applications.
- The paper does not include a detailed discussion on the computational efficiency of the method, which is a critical factor in practical applications.
- The method's performance on more complex tasks and its generalization to different environments are not adequately addressed, which could limit the understanding of its applicability and effectiveness.

**Questions:**
- How does the method perform in environments with more complex dynamics, such as those found in humanoid robots or robots in cluttered environments?
- Could the authors provide more details on the computational efficiency of the method, particularly in terms of training and inference times?
- How does the method handle the generalization to different environments, and what steps are taken to ensure that the learned representations are robust across different scenarios?
- Could the authors discuss the potential societal implications of their work and how they plan to address these in future deployments of the technology?
- How does the method compare to other existing methods in terms of performance and computational efficiency, and what are the specific advantages of using contrastive RL over other approaches?
