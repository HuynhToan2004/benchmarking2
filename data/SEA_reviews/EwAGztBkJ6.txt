 **Summary:**
The paper explores the generalization of gradient-based saliency maps across different training datasets, demonstrating that the number of training samples significantly impacts the consistency of these maps. The study presents theoretical bounds for gradient-based saliency maps, including simple gradient, integrated gradient, and SmoothGrad, and empirically validates these bounds using various datasets and neural network architectures. The paper also investigates the role of spectral normalization in enhancing the generalization of saliency maps and the impact of training set size on the performance of saliency maps. Theoretical results show that more training samples are required for accurate gradient-based interpretations compared to standard deep learning problems.
