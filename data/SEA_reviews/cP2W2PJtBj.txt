 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel approach to deepfake detection by leveraging Vision-Language Models (VLMs) and prompt tuning techniques. It formulates the deepfake detection problem as a Visual Question Answering (VQA) task, using soft prompts to optimize the VLM's question-answering capability. The proposed method, named AntifakePrompt, is evaluated on various datasets and generative models, demonstrating significant improvements in detection accuracy compared to existing methods. Despite its innovative approach, the paper is criticized for its limited scope in terms of dataset diversity and lack of robustness against advanced generative models.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel approach to deepfake detection using Vision-Language Models (VLMs) and prompt tuning techniques, which is a significant advancement in the field.
- The formulation of the deepfake detection problem as a Visual Question Answering (VQA) task is innovative and provides a new perspective on the application of VLMs in this domain.
- The proposed method shows promising results, outperforming several state-of-the-art methods on various datasets, demonstrating its effectiveness.
- The paper is well-written, making it easy to follow, and includes extensive experiments and ablation studies that support the proposed method's effectiveness.
- The use of prompt tuning to optimize the VLM's question-answering capability is a novel approach that could inspire further research in this area.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a comprehensive evaluation on diverse datasets, particularly those containing images from different domains such as medical images or images with different resolutions.
- The method's robustness against advanced generative models like DALLE-2 or DALLE-3 is not thoroughly tested, which is a significant limitation given the rapid advancements in generative AI.
- The paper does not adequately discuss the limitations of the proposed method, which could hinder the understanding of its applicability and effectiveness in real-world scenarios.
- The formulation of the deepfake detection problem as a VQA task is seen as overly simplistic and may not capture the complexity of the problem adequately.
- The paper lacks a detailed discussion on how the proposed method could be adapted to different types of deepfakes or how it might perform in scenarios with varying image quality or resolution.

**Questions:**
- How does the proposed method perform on datasets containing images from different domains, such as medical images or images with different resolutions?
- Can the authors provide more details on the training process, particularly how the pseudo-word is optimized and how it impacts the model's performance?
- How does the proposed method compare to other state-of-the-art methods in terms of computational efficiency and scalability, especially when applied to large-scale datasets?
- Given the limitations in dataset diversity and robustness against advanced generative models, how do the authors plan to address these issues in future work?
- Could the authors discuss potential negative societal impacts of their work, particularly in terms of deepfake detection and its implications for privacy and security?
