 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel regularization technique called Periodic Regularization, which involves dynamically altering hyperparameters during training to enhance model generalization. This approach is demonstrated to be effective in reducing overfitting and improving model performance across various datasets, including CIFAR100 and FER2013. The authors propose a combination of Periodic Regularization with reinforcement learning (RL) and transfer learning, showing significant improvements in model performance. The methodology is supported by empirical evidence, although the paper could benefit from a more rigorous theoretical analysis and a broader range of experimental validations.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel regularization technique, Periodic Regularization, which dynamically alters hyperparameters during training, reducing overfitting and improving model performance.
- The paper is well-written, with clear explanations of the proposed methodology and its implementation.
- The experimental results demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed method, showing significant improvements in model performance across various datasets.
- The paper includes a comprehensive review of related works, providing a solid foundation for the research.
- The use of reinforcement learning and transfer learning in conjunction with Periodic Regularization is a novel approach that enhances the model's ability to adapt to different tasks and datasets.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a thorough theoretical analysis of the proposed method, which could strengthen the paper's contribution to the field.
- The experimental validation is limited to a few datasets, which may not sufficiently demonstrate the generalizability of the proposed method.
- The paper does not provide a detailed discussion on the computational complexity or training time implications of the proposed method, which could be a concern for large-scale applications.
- The paper could benefit from a more comprehensive literature review, particularly in the area of regularization techniques and their impact on model performance.
- The experimental results, while showing improvements, are not always statistically significant, which could limit the confidence in the results.
- The paper could benefit from a more detailed explanation of the hyperparameter tuning process and its impact on model performance.
