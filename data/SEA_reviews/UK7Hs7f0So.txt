 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel probabilistic forecasting model named VMFTransformer, which leverages the von Mises-Fisher (VMF) distribution to model the direction and length of multivariate time series data. This model uses a Transformer architecture to optimize the upper bound of the logarithm of the Bessel function, enhancing its efficiency and stability. The methodology includes a novel "Angle&Scale" similarity measurement for attention modules and an efficient optimization technique for the Bessel function. The model is evaluated on three benchmark datasets, showing promising results compared to existing methods. However, the paper is critiqued for its lack of clarity in certain sections, limited novelty, and insufficient comparison with other state-of-the-art methods.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel probabilistic forecasting model, VMFTransformer, which integrates the von Mises-Fisher (VMF) distribution with Transformer architecture, providing a unique approach to modeling multivariate time series data.
- The authors propose a new "Angle&Scale" similarity measurement for attention modules, which is shown to be effective in capturing the temporal dependence of multivariate time series data.
- The paper includes an efficient optimization technique for the Bessel function, which is crucial for the stability and scalability of the model.
- The methodology is well-explained, with clear descriptions of the model's components and their functions, which aids in understanding the proposed approach.
- The paper is well-written, making it accessible and easy to follow, which is crucial for reproducibility and further research.
- The empirical results demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed model, showing promising performance compared to existing methods.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks clarity in certain sections, particularly in the explanation of the VMF distribution and its application to time series forecasting. This could confuse readers and hinder the understanding of the proposed method.
- The novelty of the proposed model is limited as it primarily combines existing models (VMF distribution and Transformer) without significant modifications to the underlying algorithms or architectures.
- The comparison with other state-of-the-art methods is insufficient, with only a few methods being included in the experiments. This limits the ability to assess the model's performance against the current leading methods.
- The paper does not adequately address the computational complexity of the model, which could be a significant limitation for its practical application, especially in real-time or large-scale forecasting scenarios.
- The empirical results are not convincingly superior to existing methods, particularly in terms of quantile risk, which is a critical metric for probabilistic forecasting.
- The paper could benefit from more detailed discussions on the limitations of the proposed method and potential future research directions.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors clarify the role and significance of the VMF distribution in the context of time series forecasting, especially in terms of its application to multivariate data?
- How does the "Angle&Scale" similarity measurement compare to other attention similarity measures in terms of effectiveness and computational efficiency?
- What are the specific advantages of using the VMF distribution over other probability distributions for modeling multivariate time series data?
- How does the proposed model handle the issue of conditional independence among time points, which is a known challenge in time series forecasting?
- Can the authors provide more details on the computational complexity of the model and discuss potential strategies for reducing it?
- How does the model perform when applied to time series with different characteristics, such as those with varying seasonalities or non-stationary behaviors?
- Could the authors provide a more detailed comparison with other state-of-the-art methods, including recent developments in probabilistic forecasting models?
