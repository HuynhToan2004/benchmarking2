 **Summary:**
The paper introduces Time-LLM, a novel framework that leverages large language models (LLMs) for time series forecasting without altering the pre-trained backbone model. It transforms time series data into textual representations using a patch reprogramming module and a prompt-as-prefix technique, which is then processed by the LLM. The framework demonstrates competitive performance against state-of-the-art models across various benchmarks, including long-term, short-term, few-shot, and zero-shot forecasting scenarios. The methodology is supported by extensive experimental evaluations and ablation studies, showcasing the effectiveness of the proposed components. However, concerns are raised about the novelty of the approach, the limited scope of the experiments, and the lack of comparison with more recent and relevant baselines.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, clear, and easy to follow, with comprehensive experimental evaluations and ablation studies that demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed components.
- The idea of using LLMs for time series forecasting is novel and innovative, showing potential for significant advancements in the field.
- The methodology is straightforward and the proposed framework, Time-LLM, is simple yet effective, requiring minimal parameter updates while maintaining high efficiency.
- The paper provides a detailed discussion on the limitations of the proposed method, which aids in understanding the scope and applicability of the model.
- The experimental results show that Time-LLM outperforms existing state-of-the-art models, indicating its practical utility and potential for real-world applications.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed discussion on the limitations and potential negative societal impacts of the proposed method, which is crucial for a comprehensive understanding of its applicability and ethical considerations.
- The novelty of the approach is questionable as similar methods have been explored in other domains, such as text-to-image generation and cross-modal representation learning.
- The experimental setup is limited, with insufficient comparison to more recent and relevant baselines, particularly in the context of few-shot and zero-shot learning.
- The paper does not sufficiently discuss the scalability and generalizability of the model, especially in scenarios where the input time series length is different from the pre-trained LLM's input length.
- The paper could benefit from a more detailed comparison with other recent models that utilize LLMs for time series forecasting, such as LLMTime and GPT4TS.

**Questions:**
- How does the proposed method perform when compared to other LLM-based models, particularly in scenarios where the input time series length is not the same as the pre-trained LLM's input length?
- Can the authors provide more details on the training and inference time for Time-LLM, especially in comparison to other models?
- How does the model handle different types of time series data, such as univariate versus multivariate time series, and what are the implications for the model's performance in these scenarios?
- Could the authors discuss the potential for extending the model to handle different data modalities beyond time series data, such as images or text?
- In the context of the experimental results, how does the model perform when compared to other models that use similar or identical backbone LLMs, such as GPT-4?
