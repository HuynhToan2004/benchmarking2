 **Summary:**
The paper introduces RealFM, a novel federated learning mechanism designed to address the free-rider problem in federated learning. RealFM incentivizes data contribution and device participation by modeling device utility in a non-linear manner, which is a significant departure from traditional linear models. The mechanism is designed to ensure that devices contribute more data points than they would locally, thereby enhancing the utility for both the server and the participating devices. The paper presents theoretical contributions and empirical results showing that RealFM outperforms existing methods in terms of device and server utility. However, the paper's presentation and the clarity of its contributions are noted as areas for improvement.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel and interesting approach to modeling device utility, which is a significant departure from traditional linear models.
- The proposed RealFM mechanism is theoretically sound and empirically evaluated, showing promising results in improving both server and device utility.
- The paper is well-written, clear, and easy to follow, making it accessible to readers.
- The authors provide a comprehensive theoretical analysis, including proofs for the existence of pure equilibrium and the accuracy-shaping guarantee.
- The paper addresses the important problem of incentivizing data contribution and device participation in federated learning, which is crucial for the success of federated learning systems.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a clear and concise summary of its contributions, making it difficult for readers to grasp the key takeaways.
- The experimental results are not convincingly superior to existing methods, and the paper does not adequately address the limitations of its approach.
- The paper's presentation could be improved to make the contributions and the theoretical results more accessible and understandable to a broader audience.
- There is a lack of discussion on the limitations of the proposed approach, which is crucial for understanding the scope and applicability of the findings.
- The paper could benefit from a more detailed discussion on the assumptions made and their implications, particularly in the context of federated learning.
- The paper's claims of being the first to incentivize data contribution and device participation in federated learning are not fully supported by the literature review.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors clarify how the proposed RealFM mechanism incentivizes data contribution and device participation more effectively than existing methods, particularly in terms of the theoretical contributions?
- How does the paper address the issue of data privacy in federated learning, especially considering the potential for data leakage in the proposed mechanism?
- What are the specific advantages of the proposed mechanism over existing methods, particularly in terms of practical implementation and scalability?
- How do the authors justify the assumptions made in their model, particularly in the context of federated learning where data distributions can vary significantly across devices?
- Can the authors provide more detailed experimental results, including comparisons with a broader range of baseline methods, to better demonstrate the effectiveness of their approach?
