 **Summary:**
The paper introduces the Question Awareness Temperature (QAT) sampling method to enhance the question-awareness capabilities of Large Language Models (LLMs). This method adjusts the answer distributions based on question features, aiming to improve the performance of LLMs in various benchmarks. The authors propose a dataset for evaluating question awareness and demonstrate that LLMs can learn to adjust their output distributions based on question types. The experiments show that QAT sampling can enhance the question-awareness ability of LLMs, although the method's novelty and the clarity of its presentation have been questioned.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, clear, and easy to follow, with a clear motivation and well-defined problem setting.
- The proposed method is simple, intuitive, and well-explained, making it easy to understand and implement.
- The experimental results are promising, showing that the proposed method can enhance the performance of LLMs in various benchmarks.
- The introduction of the QAT sampling method is a novel approach that allows LLMs to autonomously adjust their response determinacy based on question type, which is a significant contribution to the field.
- The paper provides a detailed analysis of the results, including comparisons with baseline models and a discussion on the limitations of the proposed method.

**Weaknesses:**
- The novelty of the paper is limited as the idea of using kurtosis to measure the question awareness and temperature sampling to control the output distribution is not new.
- The methodology lacks clarity in some sections, particularly in the description of the dataset used for training and the experimental setup.
- The paper does not provide a detailed analysis of the limitations of the proposed method, which could be crucial for understanding its applicability and effectiveness.
- There is a lack of comparison with other existing methods, such as Top-k/p sampling and other temperature scaling methods, which could provide a more comprehensive evaluation of the proposed method.
- The paper does not discuss the potential negative societal impacts of the proposed method, which could be a significant concern for some readers.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors clarify how the dataset used for training is constructed and how it is used to train the model?
- How does the proposed method compare with other existing methods, such as Top-k/p sampling and other temperature scaling methods?
- Could the authors provide more detailed analysis on the limitations of the proposed method and how these limitations affect its applicability and effectiveness?
- How does the proposed method perform in scenarios where the question awareness is not clear or when the question types are ambiguous?
- Can the authors discuss the potential negative societal impacts of the proposed method and how these impacts could be mitigated?
