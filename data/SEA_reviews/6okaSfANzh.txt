 **Summary:**
The paper explores the use of a cascade of Large Language Models (LLMs) to reduce costs by employing a weaker, less expensive LLM for simpler questions and a stronger, more expensive LLM for complex ones. It introduces a decision-making process based on the consistency of answers generated by the weaker LLM, using both vote-based and verification-based methods. The approach is tested across six reasoning datasets and demonstrates comparable performance to using the stronger LLM while reducing costs by up to 60%. The paper also discusses the use of different thought representations (CoT and PoT) to enhance the consistency and accuracy of answers.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, with clear and concise language, and the results are well-presented with figures that effectively illustrate the findings.
- The proposed method is simple, intuitive, and easy to understand, making it accessible to a broad audience.
- The idea of leveraging answer consistency for decision-making is innovative and shows potential for practical applications.
- The experiments are well-designed, and the results demonstrate that the proposed method can save costs without compromising performance.
- The paper addresses a timely and relevant topic, focusing on the cost-efficiency of Large Language Models (LLMs), which is a critical issue for organizations with limited budgets.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a comprehensive discussion on the limitations of the proposed method, particularly in scenarios where the method might not perform as well.
- The novelty of the approach is questionable, as it seems to be a straightforward combination of existing techniques without significant new insights or contributions.
- There is a lack of comparative analysis with other cost-saving techniques, such as those mentioned in related works, which could have provided a more robust evaluation of the proposed method.
- The paper could benefit from a more detailed discussion on the challenges and complexities involved in implementing the proposed method in real-world scenarios.
- The paper could be more rigorous in its experimental setup and analysis, particularly in ensuring that the results are robust and reproducible.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors provide more details on the specific scenarios where the proposed method might not perform as well, and what steps could be taken to address these limitations?
- How does the proposed method compare with other cost-saving techniques, particularly in terms of effectiveness and efficiency?
- Could the authors discuss the potential impact of the proposed method on the accessibility and affordability of LLMs for organizations with limited budgets?
- How do the authors address the issue of potential hallucinations in the weaker LLM's answers, especially in scenarios where the method might not perform as well?
- Can the authors provide more details on the experimental setup and analysis, particularly in how the results were obtained and how the experiments were designed to ensure robustness and reproducibility?
