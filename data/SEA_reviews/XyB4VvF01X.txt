 **Summary:**
The paper introduces Graph2Tac, a novel graph neural network (GNN) model designed to enhance theorem proving in Coq by leveraging a graph-based dataset. Graph2Tac operates by integrating a neural network that learns from new definitions and theorems in real-time, complementing existing online models like k-NN. The model's performance is evaluated against various baselines, including other symbolic and machine learning solvers, showing improvements in theorem proving. The authors also discuss the challenges of adapting to new Coq projects and the potential of their approach in handling complex mathematical concepts and lemmas.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel graph neural network (GNN) model, Graph2Tac, which effectively integrates with Coq's tactician framework, enhancing theorem proving capabilities.
- The authors have conducted extensive experiments, comparing Graph2Tac with various baselines, including other symbolic and machine learning solvers, and demonstrating its effectiveness.
- The paper is well-written, providing a clear and detailed explanation of the model's architecture, training methodology, and evaluation.
- The authors have made significant efforts to ensure the reproducibility of their work by providing detailed descriptions of the experimental setup and data used.
- The proposed method of using a graph neural network to handle the hierarchical relationships between definitions and theorems is innovative and potentially impactful for the field of theorem proving.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a comprehensive comparison with existing machine learning-based theorem provers, particularly those that use large language models (LLMs) like Proverbot9001.
- The paper does not adequately discuss the limitations of the Graph2Tac model, such as its inability to handle new tactics and its reliance on a specific graph representation for Coq terms.
- There is a lack of clarity regarding the specific contributions of the GNN model in theorem proving, as it primarily relies on the definition task for performance improvements.
- The paper does not provide a detailed discussion on how the model handles the hierarchical relationships between definitions and theorems, which is a key aspect of the proposed method.
- The paper could benefit from a more detailed discussion on the potential societal impacts of the proposed technology, particularly in the context of automated theorem proving.

**Questions:**
- How does Graph2Tac compare to other machine learning-based theorem provers that use LLMs? What are the specific advantages or disadvantages of using a GNN over an LLM in this context?
- Could you provide more details on how Graph2Tac handles new tactics that are not part of the training data?
- How does the model's performance vary when using different graph representations for Coq terms?
- In the context of the definition task, how does the model ensure that it is learning meaningful representations of definitions and not just memorizing specific examples?
- Can you elaborate on the potential societal impacts of the proposed technology, particularly in terms of automating theorem proving and its implications for the verification of complex systems?
