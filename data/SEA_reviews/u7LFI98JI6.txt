 **Summary:**
The paper introduces GraphDeepONet, a novel model that integrates Graph Neural Networks (GNNs) with DeepONet to enhance the simulation of time-dependent Partial Differential Equations (PDEs). This model allows for the handling of irregular grids and enables time extrapolation, which is a significant advancement over traditional DeepONet and its variants. The theoretical analysis provided demonstrates the universal approximation capability of GraphDeepONet across arbitrary time intervals. The paper presents experiments that showcase the model's superiority over existing methods, although there are concerns regarding the clarity of presentation and the depth of the experimental validation.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel approach by integrating GNNs with DeepONet, which is innovative and well-motivated.
- The theoretical analysis provided is robust, demonstrating the universal approximation capability of GraphDeepONet across arbitrary time intervals.
- The paper is well-written, clear, and easy to follow, making it accessible to readers.
- The experimental results are promising, showing the superiority of GraphDeepONet over existing methods in predicting the solution operator at arbitrary positions of the input on irregular grids.
- The paper addresses an important problem of time-dependent PDEs, which is crucial for scientific computing and has significant real-world applications.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed comparison with existing GNN-based PDE solvers, which could have provided a clearer understanding of the advantages of the proposed method.
- The presentation of the paper needs improvement, particularly in the clarity of figures and the explanation of symbols and notations.
- The experimental validation could be strengthened by including more comprehensive comparisons with other state-of-the-art models and by addressing the limitations of the experiments.
- There is a need for more detailed discussions on the limitations and potential negative societal impacts of the proposed method.
- The paper could benefit from more thorough theoretical analysis and proofreading to ensure the accuracy and clarity of the theoretical claims and proofs.

**Questions:**
- Could you clarify the role and impact of the 'branch net' in the model's performance, particularly in terms of its contribution to the model's ability to handle irregular grids?
- How does the model perform when trained and tested on different grids, and what are the implications of this setup for the model's generalization capabilities?
- Can you provide more details on the experimental setup, particularly the dataset used and the conditions under which the model was tested?
- How does the model handle boundary conditions, and what are the implications of these conditions on the model's predictions?
- Could you elaborate on the choice of using an autoregressive method for time prediction and discuss its impact on the model's performance and computational efficiency?
- How does the model perform when trained and tested on different types of PDEs, and what are the implications of these variations for the model's applicability and effectiveness?
