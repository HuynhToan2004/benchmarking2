 **Summary:**
The paper introduces MINERVA, a novel feature selection method that utilizes a neural network to estimate mutual information between features and labels. This approach allows for the selection of relevant features based on their mutual information, which is estimated using a neural network trained on the entire dataset. The methodology is demonstrated through experiments on synthetic datasets, showing that MINERVA can achieve exact feature selection, while other methods fail. However, the paper's experimental scope is limited to synthetic data, and it lacks a broader validation on real-world datasets, which could limit its applicability and generalizability. The method's computational efficiency and scalability are also concerns, as it requires training a neural network on the entire dataset, which could be impractical for large datasets.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel approach to feature selection using a neural network to estimate mutual information, which is a significant advancement over traditional methods.
- The method is demonstrated to be effective in achieving exact feature selection, which is a notable achievement.
- The paper is well-written, with clear and concise language, making it easy to follow.
- The use of mutual information for feature selection is a promising direction, and the method's application to real-world datasets could provide valuable insights.
- The method's ability to handle complex relationships between features and targets is highlighted, which is a significant advantage over traditional methods.

**Weaknesses:**
