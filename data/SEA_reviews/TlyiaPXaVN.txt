 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel approach to computing generalized Nash equilibria (GNE) and competitive equilibria (CE) in pseudo-games using a Generative Adversarial Equilibrium Solver (GAES). This methodology leverages a Generative Adversarial Network (GAN) to predict GNE and CE from a sample of problem instances, addressing the computational and sample complexity issues in pseudo-games. The authors provide theoretical and empirical evidence supporting the effectiveness of their approach, demonstrating that GAES can outperform existing methods in practical applications. The paper also discusses the convergence and sample complexity of the proposed method, supported by both theoretical and empirical evidence.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel approach to computing GNE and CE in pseudo-games using a Generative Adversarial Equilibrium Solver (GAES), which is a significant advancement in the field.
- The authors provide both theoretical and empirical evidence to support the effectiveness of their approach, which is crucial for validating the proposed method.
- The paper is well-written, clear, and easy to follow, which enhances its readability and accessibility to a broader audience.
