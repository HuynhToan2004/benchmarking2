 **Summary:**
The paper investigates the training instability in behavior cloning (BC) tasks, focusing on the oscillations in long-horizon rewards despite negligible effects on the BC loss. It attributes these oscillations to gradient variance amplification (GVA) caused by minibatch SGD noise propagating through unstable closed-loop dynamics. The authors propose using exponential moving averages (EMA) to mitigate these oscillations, demonstrating its effectiveness in both MuJoCo control and autoregressive language generation tasks. The paper also discusses the theoretical implications of EMA in convex models, suggesting that it might be beneficial for reducing variance in stochastic convex optimization.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, clear, and easy to follow, with a clear problem statement and motivation.
- The authors have conducted extensive experiments to investigate the training instability in behavior cloning, which is a significant and relevant problem in the field.
- The paper provides a thorough analysis of the causes of training instability in behavior cloning, particularly focusing on gradient variance amplification (GVA) and its impact on long-horizon rewards.
- The authors propose a novel approach using exponential moving averages (EMA) to mitigate the effects of GVA, which is supported by empirical evidence and theoretical analysis.
- The paper is well-organized, with clear figures and a logical structure that facilitates understanding of the complex concepts discussed.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed discussion on the limitations of the proposed method, particularly in terms of its applicability to different types of tasks and its scalability.
- The experiments are limited to specific tasks and models, which may not fully generalize to other scenarios or architectures.
- There is a lack of comparison with other methods, such as learning rate schedules or different optimizers, which could provide a more comprehensive evaluation of the proposed approach.
- The paper does not adequately address the potential statistical and computational challenges associated with the proposed method, particularly in terms of its practical implementation and the computational resources required.
- The paper could benefit from a more detailed discussion on the theoretical underpinnings of GVA and its relationship to other phenomena like stochastic optimization and convex theory.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors clarify whether the behavior cloning loss is averaged over the whole trajectory or per-step?
- How does the proposed method perform in tasks with different types of rewards, such as sparse rewards or those with high-dimensional state spaces?
- Could the authors provide more details on the computational requirements and practical implementation of the proposed method, particularly in terms of its scalability and resource needs?
- How does the proposed method compare to other optimization techniques, such as those that use different learning rate schedules or optimizers?
- Can the authors elaborate on the theoretical implications of GVA and its relationship to other phenomena in stochastic optimization and convex theory?
