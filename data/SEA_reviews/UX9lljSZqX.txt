 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel Unified Static and Dynamic Network (UniSDNet) designed for efficient video grounding in both natural language and spoken language scenarios. It employs a ResMLP architecture for static modeling, integrating multiple queries and video interactions, and a Dynamic Temporal Filtering Network (DTFNet) for dynamic modeling, leveraging a multi-kernel Temporal Gaussian Filter to enhance contextual understanding. The network is evaluated on various datasets, including ActivityNet Captions, Charades-STA Speech, and TACoS Speech, demonstrating state-of-the-art performance. The paper also introduces new datasets for spoken language video grounding.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel approach to video grounding, integrating static and dynamic modeling through a ResMLP architecture and a Dynamic Temporal Filtering Network (DTFNet), respectively.
- The proposed method achieves state-of-the-art (SOTA) performance on multiple datasets, demonstrating its effectiveness.
- The paper is well-written, making the methodology and results clear and accessible.
- The authors collect two new datasets for spoken language video grounding, which could be beneficial for future research in this area.
- The model is lightweight, which is advantageous for practical applications.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed comparison with existing state-of-the-art methods, particularly in terms of computational efficiency and model complexity.
- The ablation studies are insufficient to fully understand the contributions of different components of the model.
- The paper does not provide sufficient visualization results, which could help in understanding the model's performance and effectiveness.
- The paper does not discuss the limitations of the proposed method, which is crucial for understanding its applicability and potential improvements.
- There are several typographical errors and inconsistencies in the paper, which could detract from its professionalism and clarity.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors provide a more detailed comparison with existing state-of-the-art methods, including computational efficiency and model complexity?
- Can the authors provide more visualization results to better illustrate the model's performance and effectiveness?
- How does the model handle different types of queries and videos, particularly those with varying levels of complexity or semantic similarity?
- Could the authors discuss the limitations of the proposed method and suggest potential areas for future research or improvement?
- How does the model perform when different pre-trained models are used for feature extraction, and what are the implications for the model's performance?
