 **Summary:**
The paper introduces CONFIDE, a novel method for inferring an explicit PDE from data samples generated by previously unseen dynamics, integrating knowledge of the PDE form with a differential scheme. The method is designed to predict signals and explain dynamics, using a context-aware autoencoder and a coefficient estimator. The authors demonstrate the effectiveness of their method through experiments on three different PDE systems, comparing it to several state-of-the-art approaches. The paper is commended for its clarity, originality, and methodological soundness, although some concerns are raised about the complexity of the method, the lack of a comprehensive comparison with existing methods, and the limited scope of the experiments.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, with clear and easy-to-follow descriptions of the proposed method and the experimental results.
- The method is novel, combining a context-aware autoencoder and a coefficient estimator to infer an explicit PDE from data samples, which is a significant contribution to the field.
- The paper demonstrates the effectiveness of the method through experiments on three different PDE systems, showing good performance compared to several state-of-the-art approaches.
- The authors provide a detailed discussion on the challenges and potential limitations of the proposed method, which adds depth to the paper.
- The paper is well-motivated, with clear explanations of the problem setting and the proposed method, making it accessible and understandable to a broad audience.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a comprehensive comparison with existing methods, particularly in terms of computational complexity and the specific advantages of the proposed method over existing approaches.
- The experiments are limited to only three PDE systems, which may not adequately demonstrate the generalizability of the method across a broader range of PDEs.
- The method's complexity is not adequately addressed, with the paper not discussing the computational resources required for training and inference, which could be a significant barrier for practical implementation.
- The paper does not include a discussion on the limitations of the proposed method, which could provide valuable insights into its applicability and potential drawbacks.
- The paper's presentation could be improved, particularly in terms of the clarity and organization of the figures and the overall structure of the paper.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors provide a more detailed comparison with existing methods, including computational complexity and specific advantages of the proposed method?
- How does the proposed method perform when applied to PDEs with higher-order derivatives, and how does it handle more complex PDEs with multiple dependent variables?
- What are the computational resources required for training and inference with the proposed method, and how do these compare to other methods in terms of practical feasibility?
- How does the method handle the identification of PDEs with different orders or different numbers of dependent variables?
- Could the authors provide a discussion on the limitations of the proposed method and potential future directions for research?
