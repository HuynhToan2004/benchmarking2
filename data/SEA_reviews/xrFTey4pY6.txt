 **Summary:**
The paper introduces Clarify, a novel method that leverages natural language descriptions to correct model misconceptions in supervised learning. By allowing users to describe model misconceptions in natural language, Clarify facilitates the reweighting of training data or the collection of targeted data, enhancing model robustness. The method has been evaluated on various datasets, demonstrating its effectiveness in improving model performance. The paper also discusses the scalability of the method and its potential for broader application, although it acknowledges limitations such as its applicability in specialized domains and the need for further research in these areas.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel approach to correcting model misconceptions using natural language descriptions, which is both innovative and practical.
- The methodology is well-explained, making it accessible and understandable to a broad audience.
- The paper is well-written, with clear explanations of the methodology, experiments, and results.
- The approach shows potential for scalability and broader application, particularly in areas like medical imaging or scientific domains.
- The method's simplicity and the use of a web app make it accessible and easy to implement.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper could benefit from a more detailed discussion on the scalability of the method, particularly in terms of the number of users and the amount of feedback required to achieve significant improvements.
- There is a lack of comparison with other methods for mitigating spurious correlations, which could provide a more comprehensive evaluation of the proposed method.
- The paper could be improved by providing more detailed information on the datasets used, including their size, diversity, and characteristics.
- The evaluation of the method's effectiveness is limited to a few datasets and does not include a broader range of benchmarks or real-world applications.
- The paper lacks a detailed discussion on the limitations of the proposed method, particularly in terms of its applicability in specialized domains and its scalability.
- There is a need for more rigorous evaluation of the method's effectiveness, especially in terms of its ability to handle complex or nuanced failure modes.

**Questions:**
- How does the method perform in scenarios where the model's failure mode is not easily described in natural language?
- Can the method be adapted to handle different types of models beyond image classifiers, such as language models or graph neural networks?
- How does the method handle cases where the model's failure mode is not directly related to the image content, such as in cases of adversarial attacks or concept drift?
- Could the method be enhanced by incorporating additional data sources or types of feedback, such as user-generated content or expert annotations?
- How does the method compare to other methods for mitigating spurious correlations, particularly in terms of computational efficiency and effectiveness?
- What are the specific limitations of the method, and how can these be addressed in future research or applications?
