 **Summary:**
The paper investigates the challenges in off-policy deep reinforcement learning (DRL) when dealing with mixed-sign rewards, which include both positive incentives and negative costs. The authors propose a novel off-policy actor-critic method, OPAC2, to address these issues, specifically focusing on the problem of value estimation errors that can skew the learning towards either incentives or costs. The method is tested in both unconstrained and constrained settings, demonstrating its effectiveness in environments like the Safety Gym. The paper also explores the use of resetting strategies to improve learning, comparing these to existing methods like SAC and TD3. Despite its strengths in addressing mixed-sign rewards and its experimental validation, the paper is criticized for its limited novelty and lack of comprehensive theoretical justification.

**Strengths:**
- The paper addresses an important problem in reinforcement learning (RL) by focusing on the challenges posed by mixed-sign rewards, which are a common issue in real-world applications.
- The authors provide a clear and well-structured presentation, with a detailed discussion of the limitations of current off-policy methods and the potential for bias in Q-value estimation.
- The paper includes extensive experiments across multiple environments, demonstrating the effectiveness of the proposed method, OPAC2, in both unconstrained and constrained settings.
- The paper provides a thorough review of related work, positioning the proposed method within the context of existing literature.
- The inclusion of source code enhances the reproducibility of the results, which is crucial for advancing the field.

**Weaknesses:**
- The novelty of the paper is limited as the proposed method, OPAC2, is a direct application of the actor-critic method from 2012, with only minor modifications.
- The paper lacks a comprehensive theoretical analysis, relying heavily on empirical results which may not generalize well across different environments or reward structures.
- There is a significant gap in the discussion of related work, particularly in the context of constrained RL and the use of dual methods for handling mixed-sign rewards.
- The paper's claims about the impact of mixed-sign rewards on off-policy learning are not well-supported by the experiments, which are limited to specific environments.
- The paper does not adequately address the issue of bias in Q-value estimation, which is a known problem in off-policy learning.
- There is a lack of discussion on the potential negative societal impacts of the work, which is a critical consideration for any technological advancement.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors clarify the choice of environments for the experiments and how they were selected? How do these environments reflect the challenges posed by mixed-sign rewards?
- How does the proposed method, OPAC2, compare to other existing methods like PPO and TRPO, especially in terms of sample efficiency and performance?
- What is the rationale behind the choice of resetting in the experimental setup, and how does it affect the results?
- Could the authors provide more details on the experimental setup, particularly the parameters used for different methods and the computational resources required?
- How does the proposed method address the issue of bias in Q-value estimation, and what steps have been taken to mitigate this bias in the experiments?
- Can the authors provide a more detailed theoretical analysis to support the claims made about the impact of mixed-sign rewards on off-policy learning?
