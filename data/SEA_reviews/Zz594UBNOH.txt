 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel equivariant message passing framework on simplicial complexes, integrating the expressive power of Clifford group-equivariant layers with simplicial message passing. The framework represents simplicial features using geometric products of their vertices and shares parameters of the message network across different dimensions, enabling efficient simplicial message passing. The methodology is evaluated across various geometric tasks, demonstrating its effectiveness in handling higher-dimensional simplices and maintaining geometric symmetries. Despite its innovative approach, the paper is criticized for its lack of clarity in technical details and the complexity of its notation, which could hinder understanding. Additionally, the paper's experimental validation is seen as insufficient, particularly in terms of the number of datasets used and the complexity of the tasks.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel approach by combining Clifford group equivariant layers with simplicial message passing, which is a significant advancement in the field of geometric deep learning.
- The proposed method is well-motivated, with a clear and well-structured presentation that effectively communicates the methodology and its applications.
- The experimental results demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed method, showing superior performance compared to existing methods in various geometric tasks.
- The integration of simplicial message passing with Clifford group equivariant layers enhances the expressive power of the model, enabling the representation of geometric features more accurately.
- The paper is well-written, with a clear and concise presentation that effectively communicates the methodology and its applications.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper suffers from a lack of clarity in technical details, particularly in the definitions and explanations of key concepts such as the simplicial adjacencies and the geometric product in Clifford algebra.
- The notation used in the paper is complex and sometimes confusing, making it difficult for readers to follow the explanations and understand the methodology.
- The experimental validation of the proposed method is insufficient, with only a few datasets used, and the complexity of the tasks is not adequately addressed.
- The paper's contribution is not clearly articulated, and the novelty of the method is not convincingly demonstrated.
- There are several instances of incorrect or incomplete citations, which undermine the credibility of the paper.
- The paper lacks a detailed discussion on the limitations of the proposed method, which could help in understanding its applicability and potential drawbacks.
