 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel approach to personalized federated learning (PFL) by leveraging Neural Tangent Kernel (NTK) to quantify the importance of local and global updates, which are mixed based on their importance. The authors propose an efficient approximation to calculate the trace of a feature matrix instead of the NTK matrix, which significantly reduces computational costs. Theoretical analysis in the over-parameterized regime and extensive experiments on various datasets demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed method. The paper also discusses the convergence of the proposed method and provides a detailed experimental setup and results, comparing the proposed method with several existing approaches.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, making it easy to follow and understand.
- The proposed method is novel, particularly the use of Neural Tangent Kernel (NTK) to determine the importance of local and global updates, which is both innovative and effective.
- The paper includes a comprehensive experimental setup with a large number of clients and datasets, demonstrating the effectiveness of the proposed method.
- Theoretical analysis in the over-parameterized regime is provided, which is crucial for understanding the theoretical foundations of the proposed method.
- The paper includes a detailed discussion on the convergence of the proposed method, which is a significant contribution to the field.
- The use of the NTK matrix to analyze the convergence rate of the proposed method is a notable contribution, providing a deeper understanding of the method's efficacy.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed discussion on the limitations of the proposed method, which is crucial for understanding its applicability and potential drawbacks.
- The experimental setup could be improved by including a broader range of datasets and more diverse experimental conditions to validate the robustness of the proposed method.
- The paper does not sufficiently compare the proposed method with existing approaches, particularly in terms of computational efficiency and theoretical guarantees.
- The paper does not provide a clear explanation of the rationale behind the specific design choices of the proposed method, which could hinder the reproducibility and understanding of the results.
- There are some minor issues with the presentation, such as typographical errors and unclear descriptions in figures and tables, which could detract from the paper's professionalism and clarity.
