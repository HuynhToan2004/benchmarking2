 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel method for verifying recourse in machine learning models, focusing on whether a model's prediction is fixed or can be altered by actions within a set of constraints. This verification process is crucial in scenarios where individuals can change their features to influence model predictions, ensuring fairness and accessibility. The approach uses reachable sets to determine whether a model's prediction is infeasible, and the paper provides empirical evidence demonstrating the prevalence of fixed predictions in real-world datasets. The methodology is designed to be model-agnostic and applicable to various machine learning models, enhancing the reliability and trustworthiness of AI systems in critical applications like lending and hiring.

**Strengths:**
- The paper addresses a significant and timely issue of verifying recourse in machine learning models, which is crucial for ensuring fairness and accessibility in AI systems.
- The proposed method is novel and well-motivated, focusing on verifying whether a model's prediction is fixed or can be altered by actions within a set of constraints, which is a novel approach in the field.
- The paper is well-written, clear, and easy to follow, with a comprehensive discussion on the related work, making it accessible to a broad audience.
- The experiments conducted are thorough, demonstrating the effectiveness of the proposed method in real-world datasets, and the methodology is designed to be model-agnostic, enhancing its applicability across various machine learning models.
- The paper provides a theoretical framework and practical algorithms for constructing reachable sets from complex actionability constraints, which is a significant contribution to the field.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed comparison with existing methods, particularly in terms of computational efficiency and the ability to handle different types of constraints.
- The methodology's scalability is questionable, as it might not be practical for high-dimensional datasets or models with complex actionability constraints.
- The paper does not provide a detailed discussion on the societal implications of the proposed method, which could be a significant limitation for its broader adoption.
- The experimental setup is limited to a few datasets, which might not adequately represent the diversity and complexity of real-world scenarios.
- The paper does not provide a comprehensive discussion on the limitations of the proposed method, which could affect its practical applicability and reliability.
