 **Summary:**
The paper explores the estimation of long-memory parameters in time series models using deep neural networks, specifically focusing on the Hurst exponent of fractional Brownian motion and the parameters of ARFIMA and fOU processes. It employs a novel approach by leveraging efficient process generators to create synthetic training data, which is then used to train neural network models like CNN and LSTM. The paper claims that these models outperform traditional statistical methods in terms of precision, speed, consistency, and robustness. The methodology involves a combination of training and fine-tuning on synthetic data to optimize the neural network's performance. Despite the innovative approach and the potential utility of the proposed method, the paper suffers from several issues including unclear methodology, lack of detailed theoretical analysis, and insufficient empirical validation.

**Strengths:**
- The paper addresses a significant problem in the field of time series modeling and parameter estimation, focusing on long-memory parameters which are crucial in various scientific fields.
- The approach of using deep neural networks (DNNs) to estimate long memory parameters is innovative and has the potential to improve upon traditional statistical methods.
- The paper is well-written and easy to follow, making it accessible to a broad audience.
- The authors have provided detailed explanations of their methodology and experimental results, which are supported by comprehensive experiments.
- The use of synthetic training data to train neural networks is a novel and potentially effective approach, especially in scenarios where real data is scarce or difficult to obtain.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed theoretical analysis, which could strengthen the claims made about the superiority of the proposed method over traditional statistical methods.
- There is a lack of clarity in the methodology, particularly in the training and fine-tuning processes, which could benefit from more detailed explanations and potentially the inclusion of a flowchart or pseudocode.
- The paper does not adequately address the scalability of the proposed method, especially in terms of computational resources and data requirements.
- The empirical validation of the method is insufficient, with a limited number of experiments and a lack of comparison with other neural network architectures and different datasets.
- There are several grammatical errors and typos throughout the paper, which detract from its professionalism and readability.
- The paper does not sufficiently discuss the limitations of the proposed method, which is crucial for understanding its applicability and potential drawbacks.

**Questions:**
- Could you clarify the training and fine-tuning processes, particularly how the models are trained on different sequence lengths and how the validation loss is computed?
- How does the proposed method compare in terms of computational efficiency and resource requirements to traditional statistical methods, especially in scenarios where data is scarce?
- What are the specific advantages of using DNNs over other neural network architectures for estimating long memory parameters?
- How does the method perform when applied to real-world datasets, and what are the implications of using synthetic data for training?
- Could you provide more details on the scalability of the proposed method, including the computational resources required and the data requirements for effective training?
- How does the method handle different types of long-memory parameters beyond those discussed in the paper, and what are the implications for the generalizability of the findings?
