 **Summary:**
The paper explores the concept of adaptive models in deep learning, focusing on Vision Transformers (ViTs) and their training efficiency. The authors propose an adaptive model that adjusts its parameters, such as patch size and model width, during training to optimize performance. This approach is supported by a theoretical framework that suggests the optimal trajectory of model configuration during training, aiming to reduce computational costs. The paper presents experiments on ImageNet-1k and ImageNet-21k datasets, demonstrating that the proposed methods can achieve significant computational savings while maintaining or even improving performance. However, the paper is critiqued for its limited novelty, as similar approaches have been previously explored, and its practical applicability, particularly in terms of the complexity of the proposed methods and their generalizability.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, easy to follow, and provides a comprehensive analysis of the proposed methods, supported by extensive experiments.
- The concept of an adaptive model is innovative and well-explained, demonstrating how it can optimize the use of computational resources during training.
- The experiments are well-designed and demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed methods in achieving significant computational savings while maintaining or improving performance.
- The paper introduces a novel method of traversing scaling laws to optimize the training process, which is a significant contribution to the field.
- The authors provide a detailed comparison of their methods with existing approaches, such as FlexiViT, which helps in understanding the relative advantages of the proposed methods.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a thorough discussion on the limitations of the proposed methods, particularly in terms of their generalizability and practical applicability.
- The novelty of the paper is limited, as similar approaches have been previously explored, such as the adaptive model and the use of scaling laws.
- The paper does not adequately address the complexity of the proposed methods, which might make it difficult for practitioners to implement the proposed strategies in real-world scenarios.
- There is a lack of comparison with other methods, such as those that use data pruning or other techniques to optimize training efficiency, which could have provided a more comprehensive evaluation of the proposed methods.
- The paper does not sufficiently discuss the practical implications of the proposed methods, such as the scalability and applicability to different datasets and architectures.
- The paper could benefit from a more detailed discussion on the theoretical underpinnings of the proposed methods, particularly in terms of how they relate to existing scaling laws and the practical implications of these laws.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors clarify the specific contributions of their work compared to existing literature, particularly in terms of novelty and practical applicability?
- How do the authors justify the complexity of the proposed methods, and what steps are being taken to make these methods more accessible to practitioners?
- In terms of the theoretical framework, how does the proposed method relate to existing scaling laws, and what are the implications of these laws for the practical implementation of the proposed methods?
- How do the authors address the practical challenges of implementing adaptive models in real-world scenarios, such as the need for real-time adjustments during training?
- Could the authors provide more detailed examples or case studies that demonstrate the practical benefits of the proposed methods in different application areas?
- How do the authors plan to address the limitations of their current approach, such as the need for more extensive validation across different datasets and architectures?
