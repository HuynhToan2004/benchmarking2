 **Summary:**
The paper explores the robustness of labeled data from a novel perspective, focusing on the curvature of the data manifold as perceived by a model during training. The authors argue that adversarial training is less effective in regions of nearby differently labeled data points, which they attribute to the model's perception of curvature. They propose a method that emphasizes non-robust regions during adversarial training to enhance robustness. The paper also introduces a new geometric understanding of data robustness, which is supported by both theoretical analysis and empirical evidence. Despite the novel approach, the paper faces criticism for its lack of clarity in explaining certain concepts, such as the model's perception of curvature and the robustness of data points.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel perspective on adversarial training, focusing on the curvature of the data manifold as perceived by a model during training. This new geometric understanding of data robustness is both innovative and insightful.
- The authors provide a clear and comprehensive explanation of the phenomenon of adversarial training's weaker effect in non-robust regions, which is supported by both theoretical analysis and empirical evidence.
- The paper is well-written, making it accessible and easy to understand, with clear illustrations and a logical flow that enhances comprehension.
- The proposed method of focusing on non-robust regions during adversarial training shows promising results, potentially leading to improvements in robustness for unseen data.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks clarity in explaining certain concepts, such as the model's perception of curvature and the robustness of data points. More detailed explanations and examples are needed to enhance understanding.
- The experimental setup and results are not convincingly presented, with some results showing inconsistencies or insufficient detail, which undermines the credibility of the findings.
- The paper does not adequately discuss the limitations of its approach, which could help in understanding the scope and applicability of the proposed method.
- The paper's empirical results do not convincingly demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed method, particularly in terms of its ability to improve adversarial robustness across different datasets and model architectures.
- The paper does not sufficiently address the computational costs associated with the proposed method, which could be a significant drawback for practical implementation.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors provide more detailed explanations and examples to clarify the model's perception of curvature and the robustness of data points?
- How does the proposed method compare to other existing methods in terms of computational efficiency and effectiveness in improving adversarial robustness?
- Could the authors elaborate on the experimental setup and results, particularly in sections where the results are inconsistent or unclear?
- How does the proposed method perform on other datasets and model architectures, and what are the implications of these results for the generalizability of the findings?
- Can the authors discuss the potential limitations of their approach and how these might affect the practical applicability of their method?
