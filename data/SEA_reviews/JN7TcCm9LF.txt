 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel complexity bound for neural networks using Koopman operators, which are linear operators that map a function space to itself via function composition. This bound is different from traditional norm-based bounds in that it incorporates the determinant of the weight matrix, which can be small if the singular values are large and the condition number is small. The authors argue that this bound is more effective when the weight matrices are orthogonal or have small singular values, which is a common feature in well-generalizing networks. The paper also discusses the practical implications of these theoretical results, suggesting that the determinant term could be used to design regularization terms for neural networks.
