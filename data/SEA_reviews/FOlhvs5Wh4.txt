 **Summary:**
The paper investigates the generalization performance of smoothed classifiers, specifically focusing on the certified robust radius and model weights. It extends the concepts of margin-based generalization analysis to the smooth setting, providing a theoretical framework that includes a margin-based generalization bound with a certified robust radius for smoothed classifiers. The theoretical results are supported by empirical evidence demonstrating the effectiveness of the proposed spectral regularizer in enhancing certified robustness. The paper also introduces a novel method for spectral regularization in smooth training, aiming to improve the generalization and robustness of smoothed classifiers. Despite its theoretical contributions, the paper has been critiqued for its empirical results, which are not convincing enough to substantiate the claims fully.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel method for spectral regularization in smooth training, which is effective in enhancing certified robustness.
- The theoretical results are supported by empirical evidence, demonstrating the effectiveness of the proposed spectral regularizer in improving certified robustness.
- The paper is well-written and easy to follow, making complex theoretical concepts accessible.
- The extension of the concepts of margin-based generalization analysis to the smooth setting is a significant contribution, providing a theoretical framework for analyzing the generalization performance of smoothed classifiers.
- The paper is original in its approach, combining the PAC-Bayes framework with the randomized smoothing technique to provide a new perspective on the generalization and robustness of smoothed classifiers.

**Weaknesses:**
- The empirical results presented in the paper are not convincing enough to substantiate the claims fully. The experiments are limited to specific datasets, and the results do not clearly demonstrate the superiority of the proposed method over existing approaches.
- The paper lacks a detailed comparison with other methods, such as the randomized smoothing method proposed by Cohen et al. (2019), which could provide a clearer picture of the advantages of the proposed method.
- The paper could benefit from more extensive empirical evaluations on larger datasets like CIFAR-100 and ImageNet to validate the claims more robustly.
- There is a lack of clarity in some sections of the paper, particularly in the definitions and explanations of key terms and concepts, which could hinder understanding.
- The paper could be improved by including a more detailed discussion on the limitations of the proposed method and potential directions for future research.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors provide more detailed comparisons with other methods, such as the randomized smoothing method proposed by Cohen et al. (2019), to clearly demonstrate the advantages of the proposed method?
- How does the proposed method perform on larger datasets like CIFAR-100 and ImageNet?
