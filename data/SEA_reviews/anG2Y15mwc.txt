 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel diffusion-based face privacy protection method, named Diff-Privacy, which integrates anonymization and visual identity information hiding. The method leverages a multi-scale image inversion module (MSI) to learn conditional embeddings from original images, which are then used to guide the denoising process in the diffusion model. This approach allows for flexible privacy protection tailored to different requirements, including security, utility, diversity, and controllability. The method has been evaluated on publicly available facial datasets and shows superior performance compared to existing methods. However, concerns about the clarity of the method's presentation and the depth of experimental validation persist, with some reviewers suggesting the need for more rigorous testing and comparative analysis.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel diffusion-based face privacy protection method, which unifies anonymization and visual identity information hiding, offering a flexible approach to privacy protection tailored to various requirements.
- The methodology is well-articulated, with a clear and logical structure that makes it easy to follow.
- The paper is well-written, with high-quality figures that effectively illustrate the method's functionality.
- The proposed multi-scale image inversion module (MSI) is innovative, effectively learning conditional embeddings from original images and guiding the denoising process.
- The experiments conducted on publicly available facial datasets demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed method, showcasing its ability to maintain photo-realism and provide high-quality recovery results.
- The paper addresses an important problem in face privacy protection, which is of significant interest to both academia and industry.
