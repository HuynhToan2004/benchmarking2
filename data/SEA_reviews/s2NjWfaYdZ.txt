 **Summary:**
The paper introduces KPrune, a retraining-free structured pruning method for encoder-based language models, aiming to minimize accuracy degradation during pruning. KPrune is designed to iteratively prune attention heads and neurons, using a knowledge-preserving approach that includes knowledge measurement, mask search, and weight-tuning to maintain model performance. The method is evaluated on datasets like GLUE and SQuAD, showing improvements in accuracy and efficiency compared to existing methods. However, the paper has been critiqued for its incremental novelty and limited experimental validation, particularly in terms of the datasets used and the depth of analysis.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel approach to structured pruning that considers the knowledge of the pretrained model, which is a significant advancement in the field.
- The methodology is well-articulated, with clear explanations of the proposed techniques and their contributions to the overall performance of the model.
- The paper is well-written, making it easy to follow and understand, which is crucial for reproducibility and dissemination of the research.
- The experiments conducted are thorough, demonstrating the effectiveness of the proposed method across various datasets and models.
- The paper provides a detailed description of the proposed techniques, including the iterative pruning process and the use of knowledge-preserving mask search and weight-tuning.

**Weaknesses:**
- The novelty of the proposed method is questioned, as it appears to be a combination of existing techniques without significant new contributions.
- The paper lacks a detailed analysis of the hyperparameters used in the experiments, which could affect the reproducibility and generalizability of the results.
- The datasets used in the experiments are limited, which might not fully demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed method across a broader range of scenarios.
- The paper does not sufficiently discuss the limitations of the proposed method, which could help in understanding its applicability and potential drawbacks.
- There is a lack of comparison with other pruning methods, which could provide a more comprehensive evaluation of the proposed method's effectiveness.
- The paper's claims of superiority over existing methods are not convincingly supported by the experimental results, which do not consistently outperform other methods.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors provide a more detailed analysis of the hyperparameters used in the experiments, including their impact on the results and the choice of values?
- How does the proposed method compare to other pruning methods, particularly in terms of accuracy and efficiency?
- Can the authors discuss the limitations of their method and how these might affect its applicability in different scenarios?
- How does the proposed method handle the trade-off between pruning speed and model accuracy?
- Could the authors clarify the discrepancies in the experimental results, particularly the inconsistencies in the performance of KPrune compared to other methods?
- How does the proposed method perform on larger language models, such as those with more than 100M parameters, and what are the implications for pruning these models?
