 **Summary:**
The paper introduces prompt sketching, a novel method for conducting inference in large language models (LLMs) using templates that guide the model's output. This approach, which involves a sequence of prompts, allows for more controlled and structured generation of responses, particularly useful for tasks requiring specific formats or structures. The authors demonstrate the effectiveness of this method through various experiments, comparing it to traditional methods like chain-of-thought prompting and other sequential approaches. They also provide a library, DCTIB, to facilitate the implementation of prompt sketching in different LLMs. Despite its innovative approach, the paper has been critiqued for its limited novelty, potential computational overhead, and lack of comprehensive evaluation against other advanced decoding techniques.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel method for conducting inference in large language models (LLMs) using templates, which is a creative and potentially impactful approach.
- The proposed method is well-motivated, with a clear rationale for its design and implementation, and the experiments are comprehensive, demonstrating the effectiveness of the proposed method.
- The paper is well-written, making it accessible and easy to understand, and the authors provide a library (DCTIB) to facilitate the implementation of their method, which is a valuable resource for the community.
- The paper includes a good collection of ready-to-use, generic prompt sketches that work well with a variety of hard LLM reasoning tasks, which can be easily adapted to other tasks.
- The experiments are well-designed, and the results are convincing, demonstrating the effectiveness of the proposed method in a zero-shot setting and on a variety of benchmarking tasks.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a thorough comparison with other advanced decoding techniques, such as lattice decoding, which could have provided a more robust evaluation of the proposed method.
- The method's effectiveness is limited to tasks where the template is known, which may not be applicable to all types of tasks, particularly those involving free-form text generation.
- The paper does not provide a detailed comparison of the computational overhead of the proposed method, which is crucial for understanding its practical applicability.
- The novelty of the paper is somewhat limited as it builds upon existing works such as LMQL, and the technical contributions are not clearly distinguished.
- The paper could benefit from a more detailed discussion on the limitations of the proposed method, including its applicability to different types of tasks and the potential for overfitting to specific templates.
- The paper does not adequately address the potential negative societal impacts of the proposed method, which is a significant omission in the ethical considerations section.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors provide a more detailed comparison of the computational overhead of the proposed method against other decoding techniques, such as lattice decoding?
- How does the proposed method compare to other advanced decoding techniques in terms of efficiency and effectiveness?
- Can the authors clarify the technical contributions of their work and how they differ from or build upon existing methods like LMQL?
- How does the proposed method handle tasks where the template is not known or where the template needs to be generated dynamically?
- Could the authors elaborate on the limitations of their method and discuss potential ways to address these limitations in future work?
- In the context of the ethical considerations, how does the proposed method address or mitigate potential negative societal impacts, particularly in terms of bias and fairness in LLM outputs?
