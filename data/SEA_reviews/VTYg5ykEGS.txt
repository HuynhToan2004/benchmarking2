 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a new dataset, ImageNet-OOD, designed to evaluate out-of-distribution (OOD) detection algorithms by focusing on semantic shifts rather than covariate shifts, which are prevalent in existing datasets. This dataset is curated from ImageNet-21K, ensuring minimal covariate shift, and is used to evaluate various OOD detection algorithms against the maximum softmax probability baseline. The study highlights the potential limitations of current OOD detection methods, particularly in their sensitivity to covariate shifts, and suggests that the performance of these methods on semantic shifts is minimal. The paper also critiques the methodologies used in existing OOD datasets, proposing a more rigorous approach to OOD detection evaluation.

**Strengths:**
- The paper addresses a significant and timely issue in the field of OOD detection by critically examining the current evaluation methodologies and datasets.
- The introduction of the ImageNet-OOD dataset, which minimizes covariate shifts, is a valuable contribution to the community, potentially improving the evaluation of OOD detection algorithms.
- The paper provides a detailed analysis of the limitations of existing OOD datasets and proposes a new dataset that could potentially enhance the evaluation of OOD detection algorithms.
- The paper is well-written, clear, and easy to follow, making it accessible to a broad audience.
- The authors have conducted extensive experiments to validate their claims, providing empirical evidence to support their arguments.
- The findings of the paper, such as the sensitivity of OOD detection algorithms to covariate shifts, are significant and could lead to new research directions in the field.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed explanation of the methodology used to select the 637 classes from ImageNet-21K, which is crucial for understanding the dataset's construction and reliability.
- The paper's focus on semantic shifts rather than covariate shifts is not convincingly justified, especially given the potential for significant covariate shifts in real-world scenarios.
- The paper does not adequately address the potential biases introduced by the ImageNet dataset, which could limit the generalizability of the findings.
- The paper's claims about the performance of OOD detection algorithms are not convincingly supported by the data presented, particularly in the context of the new dataset's effectiveness in evaluating these algorithms.
- The paper's contribution to the field is somewhat limited as it primarily introduces a new dataset without proposing significant new methods or insights.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors provide more details on the methodology used to select the 637 classes from ImageNet-21K, including any statistical analysis or validation measures used?
- How do the authors justify the focus on semantic shifts over covariate shifts, especially given the potential for significant covariate shifts in real-world scenarios?
- In the context of the new dataset, how do the authors address the potential biases introduced by the ImageNet dataset, and what steps have been taken to ensure the dataset's reliability and generalizability?
- Can the authors clarify the discrepancies noted in the performance of OOD detection algorithms on the new dataset compared to existing datasets, and what insights can be gained from these differences?
- How do the authors envision the use of this dataset in future research, and what specific contributions do they see it making to the field of OOD detection?
