 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel task, "PersonalityEdit," which involves editing the personality traits of Large Language Models (LLMs) to align with specified topics, focusing on the Big Five personality traits: Neuroticism, Extraversion, and Agreeableness. The authors construct a new benchmark dataset, "PersonalityEdit," to address this task, using GPT-4 to generate responses that embody the targeted personality traits. The paper evaluates various model editing methods and proposes new metrics to measure personality traits in generated text. Despite its innovative approach, the paper faces criticism for its limited scope, focusing on only three personality traits, and the potential lack of novelty in its methodology. The paper also lacks a thorough comparison with existing datasets and a comprehensive analysis of the ethical implications of editing LLM's personality traits.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a new task, "PersonalityEdit," which involves editing the personality traits of Large Language Models (LLMs), which is a significant and innovative area of research.
- The authors construct a new benchmark dataset, "PersonalityEdit," which is well-designed and includes a variety of topics, making it a valuable resource for future research.
- The paper proposes new metrics to measure personality traits in generated text, which are novel and could be useful for evaluating the effectiveness of model editing methods.
- The experiments conducted are comprehensive, covering various model editing methods, and the results provide insights into the challenges and potential of personality editing in LLMs.
- The paper is well-written, making it easy to follow, and the methodology is clearly explained, enhancing the paper's readability and comprehensibility.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper's focus on only three personality traits, Neuroticism, Extraversion, and Agreeableness, limits the scope of its findings and the generalizability of its results.
- The paper lacks a thorough comparison with existing datasets, particularly in terms of the number of topics and the variety of personality traits covered.
- The methodology of the paper is not novel, as it primarily uses existing techniques such as GPT-4 for data generation and RoBERTa for classification, without significant innovation.
- The paper's evaluation is limited to a few models, and it does not include a broader range of models or a more diverse set of experimental setups, which could provide more robust results.
- The ethical implications of editing LLM's personality traits are not adequately addressed, raising concerns about the potential misuse of these capabilities.
- The paper could benefit from a more detailed analysis of the data generation process, particularly how the quality of generated data impacts the results.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors provide more details on how the selection of topics for the dataset was conducted and how the quality control process was implemented?
- How do the authors ensure that the generated data is diverse enough to capture the full range of personality traits, especially considering the limited scope of the dataset?
- What are the specific challenges in editing LLM's personality traits, and how do these compare to other model editing tasks?
- How do the authors address the ethical concerns raised by the ability to edit LLM's personality traits?
- Could the authors clarify the role and impact of the "edit descriptor" in the model editing process, especially in terms of its influence on the model's behavior?
- How do the authors plan to expand the dataset to include a broader range of personality traits and topics in future work?
