 **Summary:**
The paper introduces Prompt2Rec, a novel method that leverages prompt-based learning to generate key factors from review texts, enhancing the performance of recommendation systems. It uses a pre-trained language model, RoBERTa, to extract essential user and item characteristics from reviews, which are then used to train a recommendation model. The paper claims that these key factors improve the prediction of user ratings and offer insights into user preferences and item attributes. Despite the promising results shown in experiments, the paper is criticized for its lack of clarity in presentation, insufficient discussion on related works, and the need for more comprehensive evaluations and comparisons with state-of-the-art methods.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel approach using prompt-based learning to generate key factors from review texts, which is a significant advancement in the field of recommendation systems.
- The methodology is well-structured, with clear explanations of the key factors and their extraction using pre-trained language models.
- The paper demonstrates promising experimental results, showing that the proposed method outperforms existing deep learning models in terms of Mean Squared Error (MSE) on five datasets.
- The use of prompt-based learning in the context of recommendation systems is a novel and interesting approach that could potentially enhance the performance and interpretability of models.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks clarity in presentation, with many technical terms and concepts introduced without adequate explanation, making it difficult for readers to fully understand the methodology and results.
- The evaluation metrics used, such as Mean Squared Error (MSE), may not adequately reflect the practical performance of the recommendation system, as it does not consider the ranking aspect of the problem.
- The paper does not sufficiently discuss related works, particularly those that use prompt-based learning in recommendation systems, which limits the contextual understanding of the proposed method.
- The paper's experimental setup and baseline comparisons are considered weak, with insufficient details on hyperparameter tuning and a lack of comparison with state-of-the-art methods.
- There is a need for more comprehensive evaluations, including comparisons with other datasets and more diverse baseline models, to strengthen the paper's claims and contributions.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors provide a more detailed explanation of the prompt-based learning method used in the paper, particularly how it differs from other prompt-based learning methods in the field?
- How do the authors justify the choice of Mean Squared Error (MSE) as the primary evaluation metric, especially considering its limitations in reflecting the practical performance of the recommendation system?
- Can the authors clarify the role and impact of user and item ID embedding in the model's performance?
- Why were specific datasets chosen for evaluation, and how do the results on these datasets generalize to other datasets or real-world scenarios?
- How do the authors address the issue of overfitting in their model, especially given the complexity of the proposed method?
- Could the authors provide more detailed experimental results, including standard deviations and comparisons with a wider range of baseline models, to better establish the effectiveness of their method?
