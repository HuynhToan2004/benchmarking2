 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel certifiably robust conformal prediction framework called COLEP, which integrates probabilistic circuits to enhance conformal prediction's robustness against adversarial attacks. COLEP leverages logical reasoning to encode domain knowledge into probabilistic circuits, which then adjust the model's predictions based on this knowledge. This framework is evaluated across various datasets, showing improved certified coverage and accuracy compared to existing methods. The paper also provides theoretical guarantees for the robustness of COLEP under adversarial perturbations.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel approach to certifiably robust conformal prediction by integrating probabilistic circuits with logical reasoning, which is a significant advancement in the field.
- The use of probabilistic circuits to encode domain knowledge and logical reasoning is a novel and effective method, providing a robust framework for conformal prediction.
- The paper is well-written, with clear and concise explanations of the proposed methodologies and theoretical results, making it accessible to a broad audience.
- The integration of probabilistic circuits with logical reasoning enhances the expressiveness of the reasoning component, potentially improving the accuracy and robustness of conformal prediction.
- The paper includes comprehensive theoretical analysis and empirical evaluations that demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed method, with detailed proofs and experimental setups provided in the appendix.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper could benefit from more detailed explanations and examples of the probabilistic circuits and their construction, particularly in the appendix, to aid in understanding the methodology.
- The paper's reliance on the accuracy of the main model and the knowledge models could limit its applicability in scenarios where these models are not sufficiently accurate or where the domain knowledge is not well-defined.
- The complexity of the method, including the construction of probabilistic circuits and the integration of knowledge models, could pose challenges in practical implementation and scalability.
- The paper's theoretical analysis and experimental setup could be improved by including more detailed discussions on the limitations and assumptions of the proposed method, as well as comparisons with other state-of-the-art methods.
- The paper could benefit from a more comprehensive discussion on the ethical implications of the proposed method, particularly in terms of fairness and potential biases in the encoded knowledge.

**Questions:**
- Could you provide more details on how the probabilistic circuits are constructed and how the knowledge rules are encoded?
- How do you ensure that the knowledge models and the main model are accurate, and what are the implications if they are not?
- How does the proposed method handle scenarios where the encoded knowledge is biased or incomplete?
- Can you discuss the scalability and computational efficiency of the proposed method, particularly in large-scale applications?
- How does the method perform under different adversarial attack scenarios, and what are the implications for the robustness of the predictions?
- Can you provide more details on the experimental setup, including the choice of datasets and the hyperparameters used?
- How do the weights assigned to different knowledge models affect the overall performance of the method, and what are the implications of varying these weights?
