 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel image clustering pipeline, CPP, which leverages pre-trained CLIP features to enhance clustering accuracy and efficiency on large-scale datasets. The CPP pipeline refines CLIP features using the MLC objective and a self-labeling algorithm to generate text labels for clusters. This methodology is validated on standard datasets like CIFAR and ImageNet, showing improved clustering performance over existing methods. The paper also proposes a mechanism for estimating the optimal number of clusters without retraining, which is a significant contribution to the field. Despite its strengths, the paper is criticized for lacking a thorough literature review, not including certain relevant baselines, and having unclear descriptions in some sections.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel image clustering pipeline, CPP, which effectively leverages pre-trained CLIP features to enhance clustering accuracy and efficiency.
- The use of CLIP features as an initialization for MLC is a significant contribution, demonstrating a smart approach to clustering.
- The paper is well-written, making it easy to follow, and includes a variety of experiments that showcase the effectiveness of the proposed method.
- The CPP pipeline is innovative, utilizing pre-trained CLIP features as a foundation for clustering, which is a novel approach in the field.
- The paper includes a mechanism for estimating the optimal number of clusters without retraining, which is a significant contribution to the field.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a thorough literature review, particularly in the area of image clustering, which could have provided a more robust context for the proposed method.
- There is a notable absence of several relevant baselines in the experimental comparisons, which could have strengthened the validity of the results.
- The description of the self-labeling algorithm and its implementation details are unclear, making it difficult to understand the specifics of how the text labels are generated.
- The paper does not adequately discuss the limitations of the proposed method, which could provide valuable insights into its applicability and potential improvements.
- The paper's claims about the clustering accuracy on ImageNet-1k being significantly higher than reported in previous studies are not substantiated with evidence or further explanation.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors provide a more detailed explanation of how the self-labeling algorithm works, particularly the process of generating text labels from CLIP features?
- Why were certain relevant baselines, such as those by Li et al. and Niu et al., not included in the experimental comparisons?
- How does the proposed method compare in terms of computational efficiency and resource requirements when scaled up to even larger datasets?
- Could the authors elaborate on the potential limitations of the proposed method and discuss how these could be addressed in future work?
- Why does the paper claim significantly higher clustering accuracy on ImageNet-1k than previously reported, and can this claim be substantiated with additional evidence or explanation?
