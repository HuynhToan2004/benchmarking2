 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel adversarial attack strategy called Efficient Diversified Attack (EDA), which combines multiple search directions and objective functions to enhance the diversity and efficiency of adversarial attacks. EDA leverages the Multi-directions/objectives (MDO) strategy, which includes the Automated Diversified Selection (ADS) algorithm and the GS+LS framework, to improve upon the Adaptive Auto Attack (A3). The approach is validated through extensive experiments, demonstrating that EDA outperforms A3 in terms of diversification and attack performance. However, the paper's empirical evaluation and theoretical underpinnings are criticized for being inadequate, with concerns about the practical applicability of the proposed methods and the lack of comprehensive theoretical analysis.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-organized, clearly written, and easy to follow, making it accessible to a broad audience.
- The proposed EDA is innovative, combining multiple search directions and objective functions to enhance the diversity of adversarial attacks, which is a significant advancement in the field.
- Extensive experiments are conducted, demonstrating the effectiveness of the proposed methods through a series of experiments on various models and datasets.
- The paper includes detailed experimental results and ablation studies, which support the claims made and provide insights into the performance of the proposed methods.
- The paper introduces a novel approach to adversarial attacks by considering both intensification and diversification phases, which is a significant contribution to the field.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a comprehensive theoretical analysis, which could strengthen the argument for the proposed methods and provide a deeper understanding of their effectiveness.
- The practical applicability of the proposed methods is questionable, particularly in scenarios where the attacker has limited resources or time constraints.
- The paper's empirical evaluation is limited, focusing primarily on a subset of models and not including a broader range of models trained on different datasets.
- The paper does not adequately compare the proposed methods with state-of-the-art attacks, which could undermine the claims of superiority.
- There are several minor issues with the presentation of the paper, including grammatical errors and inconsistencies in notation and terminology.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors provide a more detailed explanation of how the Automated Diversified Selection (ADS) algorithm works, particularly in terms of its implementation and effectiveness?
- How does the paper address the practical applicability of the proposed methods, especially in scenarios where resources or time constraints are significant?
- Could the authors clarify the experimental setup, particularly the choice of models and datasets used, and explain why certain models were not included in the evaluation?
- How does the paper address the issue of adversarial transferability, especially in the context of the EDA method?
- Can the authors provide a more detailed comparison with other state-of-the-art attacks, particularly in terms of computational efficiency and attack success rates?
- The paper mentions several acronyms without initial definition; could these be clarified to improve readability?
