 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel evaluation paradigm for Vision-Language Instruction Tuning (VLIT) datasets, focusing on the quality of datasets and samples. It proposes a method for evaluating and refining VLIT datasets using a "tune-cross-evaluation" paradigm, which involves tuning on one dataset and evaluating on others to measure dataset and sample quality. The authors construct a new dataset, REVO-LION, by selecting samples with high sample quality from existing datasets, which is shown to perform comparably to models trained on all VLIT datasets. The paper also discusses the limitations and potential negative societal impacts of the proposed method, suggesting a need for further research and discussion.

**Strengths:**
- The paper provides a comprehensive evaluation of the quality of existing VLIT datasets and proposes a new dataset, REVO-LION, which is a significant contribution to the field.
- The proposed evaluation paradigm is innovative and offers a novel approach to measuring dataset and sample quality in VLIT models.
- The paper is well-written, clear, and easy to follow, making it accessible to a broad audience.
- The experiments conducted are robust, demonstrating the effectiveness of the proposed method in improving the quality of VLIT datasets.
- The paper addresses an important and relevant problem in the field of VLIT, which is crucial for advancing the development of these models.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed discussion on the limitations of the proposed evaluation paradigm and the potential negative societal impacts of the refined dataset.
- The paper does not provide a comprehensive comparison with other benchmarks like LVLM-eHub, which could help in understanding the relative performance of the proposed method.
- The paper does not include a discussion on the potential negative societal impacts of the proposed method, which is a significant omission.
- The paper's methodology and dataset construction could be seen as overly specific to the VLIT domain, limiting its applicability to other domains.
- The paper does not adequately address the potential for bias in the evaluation process, which could impact the perceived quality of datasets.
- The paper's reliance on a single model for evaluation and the use of a specific set of datasets for tuning could limit the generalizability of the findings.

**Questions:**
- Can the proposed evaluation paradigm be applied to other domains beyond VLIT, and how might it need to be adapted for other contexts?
- How does the proposed evaluation paradigm compare to other existing benchmarks like LVLM-eHub, and what are the key differences in terms of methodology and results?
- Could the authors provide more details on the potential negative societal impacts of the refined dataset, and how these risks might be mitigated?
- How does the proposed method handle the potential for bias in the evaluation process, and what steps are being taken to ensure that the evaluation is fair and unbiased?
- Can the authors discuss the potential implications of using different models or datasets for tuning and evaluation, and how this might affect the results and conclusions drawn from the study?
