 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel approach to anomaly detection by leveraging a B-cos network to differentiate between familiar and novel features in input data. This methodology aims to address the limitations of existing models that heavily rely on familiar features, potentially leading to false negatives when encountering novel anomalies. By integrating a novelty score derived from the B-cos network, the proposed method aims to improve anomaly detection performance by reducing false negatives. The approach is validated through experiments on various datasets, demonstrating its effectiveness in identifying anomalies and outperforming state-of-the-art methods in some cases. However, concerns were raised about the theoretical justification of the method, the clarity of the experimental setup, and the generalizability of the results.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, with clear explanations and a well-structured presentation that enhances understanding.
- The problem addressed is significant and relevant, focusing on the limitations of existing anomaly detection methods that heavily rely on familiar features, potentially leading to false negatives.
- The proposed method of integrating a novelty score derived from a B-cos network is innovative and addresses this issue effectively.
- The paper demonstrates promising results in various datasets, showing that the proposed method can outperform existing methods in some scenarios.
- The inclusion of a novelty score derived from a B-cos network is a significant contribution to the field of anomaly detection.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a theoretical justification for the proposed method, which is a significant drawback in a field that heavily relies on theoretical underpinnings.
- The experimental setup and results are not clearly presented, making it difficult to understand the contributions of the proposed method and compare it with existing methods.
- There is a lack of detailed analysis and discussion on the limitations of the proposed method, which could undermine the credibility of the results.
- The paper does not adequately address the issue of false positives, which is a critical aspect of anomaly detection.
- The paper's contribution is not clearly defined, and it is unclear how the proposed method significantly advances the state-of-the-art in anomaly detection.
- There are inconsistencies and potential errors in the experimental setup and results, such as the use of different training datasets and the inconsistency in the performance metrics reported.

**Questions:**
- Can you provide a more detailed theoretical justification for the proposed method?
- How does the proposed method perform when dealing with novel anomalies that are not present in the training data?
- Could you clarify the experimental setup, particularly the differences in training datasets and the performance metrics reported?
- How does the method perform when the background class is not available?
- Can you discuss the potential impact of using different training datasets on the results, and how this affects the generalizability of the findings?
- What are the specific advantages of the proposed method over existing methods, especially in terms of reducing false negatives?
