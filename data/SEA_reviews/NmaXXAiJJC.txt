 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel vector quantization (VQ) method for compressing and accelerating deep neural networks (DNNs), specifically focusing on linear and convolutional layers. The method involves dividing weight parameters into segments and assigning them to a compact codebook, which is learned alongside the model weights. During training, the model learns both the codebook values and the mapping to model weights, reducing computational costs by optimizing matrix multiplications and convolution operations. The paper presents a range of experiments across various datasets and models, demonstrating the effectiveness of the VQ-DNN method in reducing model size and computational requirements. However, concerns are raised about the novelty of the approach, the clarity of the presentation, and the depth of experimental validation.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel approach to vector quantization (VQ) in deep neural networks (DNNs), which is both innovative and effective in reducing model size and computational requirements.
- The paper is well-written, clear, and easy to follow, with comprehensive experimental results that demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed method.
- The authors provide a detailed explanation of the training process, including the use of two different approaches for updating the codebook during training, which helps in achieving good results.
- The paper includes a comprehensive set of experiments across various datasets and models, including VGG16, ResNet, and ViT models, which show the effectiveness of the proposed method in reducing model size and computational requirements.
- The paper is well-structured and provides a detailed analysis of the proposed method, which is supported by a thorough literature review.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a clear differentiation from previous works on vector quantization for neural networks, particularly in terms of the novelty and technical contributions.
- The presentation of the paper is not clear, with inconsistent terminology and unclear explanations of key concepts such as "vector quantization" and "codebook."
- The experimental results are not convincing, with some results showing significant accuracy drops and others not demonstrating significant improvements over existing methods.
- The paper does not adequately address the scalability of the proposed method to larger models and datasets, which is crucial for practical applications.
- There are several typographical errors and inconsistencies in the paper, which detract from its professionalism and clarity.
- The paper does not provide sufficient details on the training process, such as the size of the codebook and the number of codewords used, which are critical parameters for evaluating the effectiveness of the proposed method.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors clarify the novelty and technical contributions of their work compared to previous studies on vector quantization for neural networks?
- How does the proposed method perform in terms of model size and computational efficiency compared to other state-of-the-art methods, especially in larger models and datasets?
- Can the authors provide more details on the training process, such as the size of the codebook and the number of codewords used, and how these parameters affect the performance of the model?
- How does the proposed method handle the scalability issues, particularly in large models and datasets?
- Could the authors provide more insights into the experimental results, especially those showing significant accuracy drops or marginal improvements?
- How does the proposed method compare to other low-precision quantization methods, such as 8-bit quantization, in terms of accuracy and computational efficiency?
- Could the authors provide more detailed results on the transferability of the learned codebooks between different models and datasets?
