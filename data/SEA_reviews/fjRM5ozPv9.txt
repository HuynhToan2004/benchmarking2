 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel biologically plausible reinforcement learning algorithm, Local-Forward, which replaces backpropagation with local updates and forward connections. This approach is designed to be more biologically plausible, as it does not require caching local outputs or propagating global error derivatives. The algorithm is tested in the MinAtar environment and shows comparable performance to DQN in most tasks. The paper also discusses the limitations and potential improvements of the Local-Forward algorithm, such as its reliance on a specific error measure and its comparison with other biologically plausible algorithms.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written and easy to follow, with clear explanations of the proposed Local-Forward algorithm and its components.
- The motivation for the work is strong, focusing on the biological plausibility of the Local-Forward algorithm, which could be particularly useful in the field of neuroscience.
- The paper includes a comprehensive set of experiments and ablations, which help in understanding the algorithm's behavior and performance.
- The use of forward connections between layers is a novel approach that could potentially lead to more efficient and biologically plausible neural network architectures.
- The Local-Forward algorithm shows comparable performance to DQN in some tasks, indicating its potential effectiveness.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a thorough comparison with other biologically plausible algorithms, such as Hebbian learning or spike-timing-dependent plasticity, which could provide a better understanding of the Local-Forward algorithm's advantages and limitations.
- The paper does not adequately address the scalability of the Local-Forward algorithm to larger, more complex tasks, which is a significant concern given the computational demands of biologically plausible algorithms.
- The paper does not sufficiently address the limitations of the Local-Forward algorithm, such as its reliance on a specific error measure and its potential inefficiencies compared to more conventional algorithms like DQN.
- The paper's experimental setup and results are somewhat limited, with only a few tasks tested and no comparison to state-of-the-art algorithms.
- The paper could benefit from a more detailed discussion on the biological plausibility of the Local-Forward algorithm and a comparison with other biologically plausible algorithms in terms of performance and computational efficiency.
