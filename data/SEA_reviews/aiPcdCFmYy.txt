 **Summary:**
The paper introduces Sinkhorn distributional RL (SinkhornDRL), a novel approach that leverages the Sinkhorn divergence to learn statistics from return distributions in distributional RL. This method aims to minimize the difference between current and target Bellman return distributions, which is theoretically proven to be equivalent to a regularized MMDDRL algorithm. The paper provides a comprehensive empirical evaluation across 55 Atari games, demonstrating the algorithm's performance against existing distributional RL algorithms. Theoretical analysis is provided to support the convergence of SinkhornDRL, and the paper also discusses the practical implications of using Sinkhorn divergence in distributional RL.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel approach to distributional RL by utilizing Sinkhorn divergence, which offers a unique perspective and potential for future research in this area.
- Theoretical analysis is provided to support the convergence of SinkhornDRL, which is crucial for understanding the robustness and reliability of the proposed method.
- The empirical evaluation is comprehensive, covering a large number of Atari games, which demonstrates the practical applicability and effectiveness of the proposed method.
- The paper is well-written and easy to follow, making it accessible to a broad audience, including those unfamiliar with the specifics of distributional RL.
- The methodology is innovative, using Sinkhorn divergence to approximate Wasserstein distance and MMD, which could potentially improve the performance of distributional RL algorithms.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a direct comparison with other distributional RL algorithms that utilize different divergence metrics, such as the Wasserstein distance, which could provide a more comprehensive evaluation of the proposed method.
- Theoretical analysis, particularly the proofs in the appendix, are not thoroughly explained, making it difficult for readers to fully understand the theoretical underpinnings of the method.
- The paper does not provide a detailed comparison of the computational efficiency or wall-clock time of SinkhornDRL compared to other distributional RL algorithms, which could be crucial for practical deployment.
- The paper could benefit from a more detailed discussion on the limitations and potential drawbacks of using Sinkhorn divergence in distributional RL, especially in terms of computational complexity and the need for specific kernel functions.
- The experimental results, while comprehensive, do not consistently outperform other distributional RL algorithms across all Atari games, raising questions about the generalizability and effectiveness of the proposed method.
