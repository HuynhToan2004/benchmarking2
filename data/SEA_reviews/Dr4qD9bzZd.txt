 **Summary:**
The paper introduces NAEPro, a method designed to co-optimize protein sequence and backbone structure using a novel architecture that integrates neighborhood attentive equivariant layers (NAELs). NAELs leverage global attention to capture sequence-level correlations and neighborhood equivariance to consider local structural influences. The method is evaluated on two datasets, demonstrating improvements in binding affinity and efficiency compared to existing methods. The paper also discusses the limitations of the approach, including the lack of generalization to other protein types and the need for further testing in wet-lab scenarios.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written and clearly presents the methodology, making it accessible to readers.
- The use of neighborhood attentive equivariant layers (NAELs) is innovative, allowing for the capture of both global sequence and local structural information.
- The method achieves significant improvements in binding affinity and efficiency compared to existing methods, as demonstrated through extensive experiments.
- The paper provides a thorough analysis of the protein design process, including the use of meaningful protein fragments and the integration of global and local information.
- The method is designed to be computationally efficient, which is crucial for practical applications.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a broader evaluation on other types of proteins, limiting the generalizability of the findings.
- There is a concern about the scalability of the method to longer protein sequences and more complex structures.
- The paper does not adequately address the limitations of the method, particularly in terms of its applicability to different protein types and the potential for overfitting.
- The paper does not discuss the societal impact of the work, which is an important consideration for scientific research.
- There are minor issues with the clarity and organization of the paper, including inconsistencies in notation and the placement of figures.

**Questions:**
- Could you clarify the specific role of the global attention sub-layer in the NAELs? Is it solely responsible for capturing global correlations across the entire protein sequence?
- How does the method handle the generalization to other protein types, especially those not included in the initial evaluation?
- Could you provide more details on the scalability of the method to longer protein sequences and more complex structures?
- How does the method ensure that the designed proteins are not overfitted to the training data?
- In the ablation studies, why were only the top-5 and top-10 candidates considered? Could you provide results for the top-30 candidates as well?
- Could you discuss the potential societal impacts of your work, particularly in terms of protein design for biotechnological applications?
