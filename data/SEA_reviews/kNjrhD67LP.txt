 **Summary:**
The paper introduces ITIT (Integrating Image-Text), a novel training paradigm that leverages cycle consistency to enable vision-language training on unpaired image and text data. ITIT integrates a joint image-text encoder with disjoint image and text decoders, allowing bidirectional image-to-text and text-to-image generation within a single framework. The approach utilizes a small set of paired image-text data to ensure output matches the input reasonably well in both directions and enforces cycle consistency between original unpaired samples and cycle-generated counterparts. The paper demonstrates that ITIT can achieve performance on par with state-of-the-art methods on common text-to-image and image-to-text benchmarks with significantly less paired data.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, easy to follow, and presents a novel approach to leveraging unpaired data for vision-language training, which is a significant challenge in the field.
- The proposed method, ITIT, shows promising results in achieving performance on par with state-of-the-art methods on common text-to-image and image-to-text benchmarks with much lesser paired data.
- The use of cycle consistency regularization to address the lack of supervision when dealing with unpaired data is a novel and effective approach.
- The paper provides a comprehensive evaluation of the proposed ITIT framework and the image-text cycle consistency method, demonstrating significant enhancements in model performance.
- The proposed method can achieve performance on par with state-of-the-art methods on common text-to-image and image-to-text benchmarks with much lesser paired data, which is a significant advantage over existing methods.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper could benefit from a more detailed explanation of the cycle consistency loss and its relationship to the contrastive loss.
- The use of cycle consistency loss in conjunction with contrastive loss could be seen as a double-counting of similar information, which might affect the performance and generalization of the model.
- The paper could provide more clarity on the technical details, particularly in the implementation of the image-text encoder and the use of cycle consistency loss.
- There is a need for a more thorough analysis of the limitations of the proposed method and how it might perform in different scenarios, such as when paired data is of low quality or when the unpaired data is highly diverse.
- The paper lacks a comparison with other models that leverage unpaired data, such as those mentioned in the literature review, which could help to contextualize the contributions of the proposed method.
- The paper could benefit from a more detailed discussion on the computational efficiency of the model, especially in terms of memory and computation overheads.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors provide more details on the implementation of the image-text encoder, particularly how it handles the concatenation of image and text embeddings?
- How does the use of cycle consistency loss affect the model's performance and generalization, especially when combined with contrastive loss?
- Can the authors clarify the technical details of the cycle consistency loss, particularly how it is computed and how it influences the model's training?
- How does the proposed method compare with other models that leverage unpaired data, such as those mentioned in the literature review?
- Could the authors provide more detailed ablation studies to better understand the contributions of different components of the model, such as the cycle consistency loss and the use of unpaired data?
- How does the proposed method perform when dealing with paired data of low quality or highly diverse unpaired data?
- Could the authors discuss the potential limitations of the proposed method and how they might affect its performance in different scenarios?
