 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel Bayesian optimization (BO) framework for constrained optimization problems, focusing on a decoupled setting where function and constraint evaluations are independent. The proposed method, UCB-D, is designed to adaptively select inputs and functions, leveraging a novel exploration-exploitation trade-off termed "horizontal exploration." Theoretical analysis supports the sublinear regret of the proposed method, and empirical results demonstrate its effectiveness across both synthetic and real-world problems. The paper also discusses the potential for further theoretical exploration and practical applications in various fields, including machine learning and physics.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written and clearly presents the problem and proposed solution, with a clear distinction between coupled and decoupled query settings.
- The proposed method, UCB-D, is simple and effective, supported by both theoretical analysis and empirical results that demonstrate its superiority over existing methods.
- The paper is well-organized, with clear and concise explanations of the proposed algorithm and its theoretical foundations.
- The experimental results are robust and convincing, showcasing the effectiveness of the proposed method across various scenarios.
- The paper provides a novel perspective on constrained Bayesian optimization, focusing on the decoupled setting which is often overlooked in existing literature.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed discussion on the limitations and potential negative societal impacts of the proposed method, which could be critical for understanding its broader implications.
- The paper does not sufficiently discuss the practical implementation challenges of the proposed method, particularly in scenarios where constraints are not known a priori.
- The paper could benefit from a more thorough comparison with existing methods, especially those that handle coupled queries, to better establish the novelty and effectiveness of the proposed approach.
- The theoretical analysis and proofs, while sound, could be more clearly explained and linked to practical scenarios to enhance the reader's understanding.
- There are some minor issues with the presentation, such as unclear definitions and notations, which could confuse readers and detract from the paper's clarity.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors provide a more detailed discussion on the practical implementation challenges of the proposed method, particularly in scenarios where constraints are not known a priori?
- How does the proposed method compare to other existing methods in terms of computational efficiency and scalability, especially in high-dimensional or complex optimization problems?
- Could the authors elaborate on the potential negative societal impacts of the proposed method and discuss strategies for mitigating these risks?
- How does the proposed method handle scenarios where the constraint functions are not known a priori, and what are the implications for the algorithm's performance and convergence?
- Can the authors clarify the definitions and notations used in the paper, particularly those that are not standard in the field of Bayesian optimization?
- How does the proposed method perform in scenarios where the constraint functions are noisy or uncertain, and what are the implications for the algorithm's ability to identify feasible solutions?
