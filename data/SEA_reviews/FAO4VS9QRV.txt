 **Summary:**
The paper introduces Delta-LoRA, a novel parameter-efficient fine-tuning (PEFT) method for large language models (LLMs) that updates both low-rank matrices and pre-trained weights by utilizing the delta of the product of these matrices. This approach aims to enhance the learning capabilities of LLMs by incorporating pre-trained weights into the optimization process, which is not typically done in previous PEFT methods like LoRA. Delta-LoRA shows promising results in improving performance over LoRA on various natural language processing (NLP) tasks, although its effectiveness varies across different models and datasets. The paper is well-written, with clear experimental results and a detailed analysis of the method's components.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel approach, Delta-LoRA, which updates both low-rank matrices and pre-trained weights, offering a new perspective on parameter-efficient fine-tuning (PEFT).
- The proposed method is well-motivated, with a clear explanation of the methodology and its advantages over existing methods like LoRA.
- The paper is well-written, making it easy to follow, and the experiments are well-designed, providing a comprehensive analysis of the proposed method's components.
- Delta-LoRA shows promising results in improving performance over LoRA on various natural language processing (NLP) tasks, as demonstrated by extensive experiments.
- The paper provides a detailed analysis of the gradient flow and the importance of each component, which helps in understanding the method's effectiveness.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a thorough comparison with other PEFT methods such as adapter, prefix-tuning, and prompt-tuning, which could have provided a more comprehensive evaluation of the proposed method.
- The novelty of the proposed method is questionable, as it appears to be a combination of existing methods like LoRA and AdaLoRA, with marginal improvements over LoRA.
- The paper does not discuss the limitations of the proposed method, which could help in understanding its applicability and potential drawbacks.
- There is a lack of detailed discussion on the computational cost and memory efficiency of Delta-LoRA compared to LoRA, which is crucial for understanding the practical implications of the method.
- The paper does not include experiments on larger models or different domains, which could have provided insights into the method's scalability and generalizability.

**Questions:**
- Can you provide more details on the computational cost and memory efficiency of Delta-LoRA compared to LoRA?
- How does Delta-LoRA compare with other PEFT methods like adapter, prefix-tuning, and prompt-tuning in terms of performance and efficiency?
- Is there a possibility to extend the proposed method to other PEFT methods, such as adapters or prefix-tuning?
- How does the method perform on larger models, and what are the potential limitations or challenges in scaling up the method?
- Could you clarify the experimental setup and results, particularly in the tables and figures where the results are not clearly presented or interpreted?
- How does the method handle the update of the pre-trained weights, and what are the implications of this update on the model's performance and generalization?
