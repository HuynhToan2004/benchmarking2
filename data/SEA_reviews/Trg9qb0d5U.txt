 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel method for estimating the test accuracy of a neural network without the use of a test dataset. The approach involves generating prototype vectors and evaluating the feature extractor and classifier separately. The methodology is supported by theoretical analysis and empirical results on CIFAR-10 and CIFAR-100 datasets, demonstrating that the proposed metrics can estimate the test accuracy bounds of a trained DNN. However, the paper suffers from clarity issues in its presentation, and the experimental validation is limited to small-scale datasets. The method's generalizability to larger or more complex datasets remains unclear, and the paper's contributions are not sufficiently distinguished from existing works.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces an innovative method to estimate test accuracy bounds of a given DNN without a test dataset, which is a significant contribution to the field.
- The methodology is supported by a theoretical analysis, which adds credibility to the proposed approach.
- The paper is well-written, making it easy to follow, and includes empirical results that validate the effectiveness of the proposed metrics.
- The problem addressed is both interesting and relevant, as it tackles the challenge of evaluating a neural network's performance without testing data, which is crucial in scenarios where data is scarce or unavailable.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks clarity in its presentation, with some sections being difficult to understand due to complex notations and unclear definitions.
- The experimental validation is limited to small-scale datasets, which may not adequately demonstrate the method's effectiveness on larger or more complex datasets.
- The paper does not sufficiently distinguish its contributions from existing works, particularly those that have explored similar concepts in the context of generalization bounds.
- The paper's methodology and results are not well-integrated, with some sections appearing disconnected from the overall narrative.
- The paper's claims regarding the estimation of test accuracy without a test dataset are not convincingly supported by the empirical evidence provided.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors clarify the definitions and notations used in the paper, particularly those that are not standard or could be confusing to readers?
- How do the authors justify the choice of the CIFAR-10 and CIFAR-100 datasets for experimental validation, and what were the considerations for not using larger or more complex datasets?
- In light of the concerns about the method's generalizability, how do the authors plan to validate the method's effectiveness on larger or more complex datasets?
- Can the authors provide more detailed explanations for the methodology and results, especially in sections where the connections between different parts of the paper are not clear?
- How do the authors address the issue of the method's applicability to datasets with different characteristics or distributions, such as those with higher dimensionality or more classes?
