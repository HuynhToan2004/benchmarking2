 **Summary:**
The paper introduces End-to-end Hierarchical Indexing (EHI), a novel method for learning dense retrieval models that jointly train the query/document encoder and the search indexer. This approach aims to optimize both the embedding and the ANNS structure, enhancing retrieval performance by aligning the encoder's output with the ANNS method. EHI utilizes a hierarchical tree-based indexer with classifiers at each node, which allows for efficient learning of the ANNS structure. The method is evaluated on several benchmarks, including MS MARCO and TREC DL19, demonstrating improvements over state-of-the-art methods. Despite its innovative approach, concerns were raised about the scalability of the method to larger datasets and the clarity of the motivation behind the proposed method.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, making it easy to understand and follow, with clear explanations of the proposed method and its components.
- The approach of jointly training the encoder and indexer is novel and innovative, particularly the use of a hierarchical tree-based indexer with classifiers at each node.
- The paper includes extensive experimental evaluations on various datasets, demonstrating the effectiveness of the proposed method over state-of-the-art approaches.
- The proposed method is well-motivated, with clear explanations of the advantages of the proposed method over existing techniques.
- The paper is well-organized, with a clear and comprehensive presentation of the methodology and results.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed comparison with similar existing methods like RepConc and SPLADE, which could have provided a better understanding of the novelty and effectiveness of the proposed method.
- The scalability of the proposed method to larger datasets and more complex queries is not adequately addressed, raising concerns about its practical applicability in real-world scenarios.
- The motivation behind the proposed method is not clearly articulated, making it difficult to understand the rationale behind the method's design.
- The paper lacks a thorough analysis of the limitations and potential negative societal impacts of the proposed method.
- The paper could benefit from a more detailed discussion on the computational complexity and resource requirements of the proposed method, especially in comparison to existing methods.
- There is a need for more detailed explanations of the technical contributions and the intuition behind the proposed method, particularly the path embedding and the use of triplet loss.

**Questions:**

- How does the proposed method perform on larger datasets, such as those used in the CCSA paper? What are the scalability limitations of the method?
- What are the specific advantages of using a hierarchical tree-based indexer over other types of indexers, such as those used in existing methods like RepConc and SPLADE?
- Could you provide more details on the computational complexity and resource requirements of the proposed method, especially in comparison to existing methods?
