 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel post-training quantization method for neural network model compression, focusing on mixed-precision quantization and the concept of rotation-invariant quantization (RIQ). This approach utilizes a single parameter to quantize each layer differently, optimizing the compression rate and distortion under a deviation constraint. Theoretical analysis supports the optimality of RIQ, and empirical results demonstrate its effectiveness in achieving high compression ratios with minimal accuracy loss. The method's applicability is demonstrated across various models and tasks, including computer vision and natural language processing, although it lacks a direct comparison with some state-of-the-art methods.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel post-training quantization method for neural network model compression, focusing on mixed-precision quantization and the concept of rotation-invariant quantization (RIQ).
- Theoretical analysis supports the optimality of RIQ, and empirical results demonstrate its effectiveness in achieving high compression ratios with minimal accuracy loss.
- The method is applicable to a wide range of models and tasks, including computer vision and natural language processing.
- The paper is well-written, making it easy to follow, and includes a comprehensive theoretical analysis and empirical results that demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed method.
- The proposed method achieves a higher compression ratio compared to existing methods, and the theoretical analysis is sound, providing a solid foundation for the proposed method.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a direct comparison with some state-of-the-art methods, such as LoRA and BERT-Prime, which could have strengthened the argument for the effectiveness of the proposed method.
- The empirical results are not comprehensive, particularly in terms of the number of models and tasks tested.
- The method's applicability to different architectures and its efficiency in terms of computation time are not discussed in detail.
- The paper does not provide a detailed comparison with other quantization methods like quantization-aware training, which could have provided a more complete picture of the method's effectiveness.
- There are some typographical errors and potential issues with the clarity of some sections of the paper, which could affect the reader's understanding.

**Questions:**
- How does the proposed method compare to other quantization methods like quantization-aware training, and what are the implications for computation time and model efficiency?
- Can the authors provide more details on the efficiency of the proposed method, particularly in terms of computation time and memory usage?
- How does the proposed method perform on other architectures beyond those tested, and what are the implications for its applicability to different types of neural networks?
- Could the authors elaborate on the choice of the deviation constraint and its impact on the compression ratio and accuracy loss?
- In terms of the theoretical analysis, how does the proposed method ensure the rotation-invariant nature of the quantization, and what are the implications for the model's performance under different conditions?
