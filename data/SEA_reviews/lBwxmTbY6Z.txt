 **Summary:**
The paper introduces TacSas, a novel approach to time-series forecasting and anomaly detection using tensor time-series data. It employs a bi-level optimization method to discover causal structures, integrating both instantaneous and time-lagged causal effects. TacSas utilizes a deep generative model, which includes a variational autoencoder for preprocessing and a graph recurrent neural network for causal discovery. The model is evaluated on both synthetic and real-world datasets, demonstrating its effectiveness in forecasting and anomaly detection tasks. Despite its innovative approach, the paper faces criticism for its lack of clarity in methodological descriptions and the limited scope of its experiments.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel method for discovering causal structures in time-series data, which is both innovative and well-articulated.
- The use of a variational autoencoder for preprocessing and a graph recurrent neural network for causal discovery is a notable contribution to the field.
- The experimental results showcase the effectiveness of the proposed method, demonstrating its potential for real-world applications.
- The paper is well-written, making it accessible and easy to follow, which is crucial for a technical paper.
- The authors have provided a detailed discussion on the limitations of their work, which is commendable for transparency and accountability.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks clarity in explaining the methodological details, particularly in the equations and their interpretations, which could confuse readers.
- The experimental setup is limited, focusing only on a few datasets and not including more diverse or larger datasets, which could affect the generalizability of the findings.
- There is a noticeable absence of comparisons with other causal discovery methods, which is crucial for validating the proposed approach.
- The paper does not adequately address the computational complexity and scalability of the proposed method, which is a significant concern for practical applications.
- The paper's reliance on a pre-trained autoencoder for anomaly detection raises questions about the method's robustness and the potential for overfitting or underfitting.
- The paper lacks a detailed discussion on the limitations of the proposed method, which is essential for a comprehensive understanding of its applicability and potential shortcomings.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors provide a more detailed explanation of the methodology, particularly the equations and their interpretations, to enhance the clarity of the paper?
- How does the proposed method handle the computational complexity and scalability, especially in large-scale applications?
- What are the specific advantages of using a variational autoencoder for preprocessing over other methods, and how does it contribute to the overall effectiveness of the model?
- Can the authors elaborate on how the method performs when the ground-truth causal structures are not available, and how this affects the accuracy of the forecasting and anomaly detection?
- How does the method handle the potential for overfitting or underfitting in the anomaly detection component, especially when using a pre-trained autoencoder?
- Could the authors consider including more diverse datasets and expanding the experimental setup to include more baseline models for a more robust comparison?
