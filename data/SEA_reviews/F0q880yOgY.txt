 **Summary:**
The paper explores the potential of language agents as alternatives to PPO agents in traditional sequential decision-making tasks by introducing a benchmark named TextGym. This benchmark translates OpenAI Gym environments into textual environments using GPT-4, enabling direct comparisons between language agents and PPO agents. The authors propose an innovative EXE agent to address tasks within TextGym and conduct numerical experiments to evaluate the decision-making capabilities of language agents. The study also introduces a unified framework for language agents, aiming to ensure fair and effective benchmarking. Despite its innovative approach, the paper faces criticism for its limited novelty, lack of detailed experimental results, and insufficient comparison with existing methods.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces an innovative benchmark named TextGym, which translates OpenAI Gym environments into textual environments using GPT-4, allowing for direct comparisons between language agents and PPO agents.
- The authors propose an innovative EXE agent to address tasks within TextGym, which is designed to tackle partially observable, sparse reward tasks.
- The paper provides a unified RL-inspired framework for language agents, ensuring fair and effective benchmarking.
- The authors conduct numerical experiments and ablation studies to extract valuable insights into the decision-making capabilities of language agents and evaluate their potential as alternatives to PPO in classical sequential decision-making problems.
- The paper is well-written, easy to follow, and the idea is interesting and reasonable, providing a novel approach to evaluating language agents in a text-based environment.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks detailed experimental results, particularly concerning the performance of the proposed EXE agent and the comparison with existing methods like Reflexion.
- The novelty of the work is limited, as the EXE agent appears to be a direct application of existing exploration and exploitation strategies from reinforcement learning.
- The paper does not adequately address the limitations of the proposed method, such as its applicability to more complex or realistic environments and the potential impact of different language models on the results.
- The comparison with PPO is not fair as PPO requires significant data and computational resources, while the language agents do not.
- The paper does not sufficiently discuss the limitations of the proposed method, particularly its applicability to more complex or realistic environments and the potential impact of different language models on the results.
- The paper does not include a discussion on the limitations of the proposed method, which is crucial for understanding the scope and applicability of the findings.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors provide more detailed experimental results, particularly concerning the performance of the proposed EXE agent and the comparison with existing methods like Reflexion?
- How does the EXE agent compare to other language agents in terms of decision-making capabilities and performance in different environments?
- Can the authors clarify the role and impact of different language models on the results, especially in environments where the performance varies significantly?
- How does the proposed EXE agent handle exploration and exploitation in different scenarios, and what are the specific strategies used for these processes?
- Could the authors discuss the limitations of the proposed method and how these might affect the applicability and generalizability of the findings?
- How does the proposed EXE agent compare to other existing methods for evaluating language agents in text-based environments, and what are the advantages or disadvantages of using this approach compared to other methods?
