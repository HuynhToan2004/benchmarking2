 **Summary:**
The paper introduces DICE, a method aimed at debiasing language models by leveraging Energy-Based Models (EBMs) and Ordinary Differential Equations (ODEs) to navigate the latent space of a Variational Autoencoder (VAE). The approach involves training classifiers within the latent space to identify and mitigate biases, which are then used to guide the ODE solver towards less biased samples. The method is evaluated across various models, including GPT-2, LLaMA, and LLaMA-2, showing promising results in reducing biases while maintaining semantic content. However, concerns were raised about the clarity of the method's implementation, the generalizability of the results, and the method's effectiveness in large language models (LLMs).

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces an innovative approach using Energy-Based Models (EBMs) and Ordinary Differential Equations (ODEs) to debias language models, which is a novel application of these models in this context.
- The method is well-motivated, theoretically sound, and clearly presented, making it accessible and understandable.
- The paper provides a comprehensive evaluation across various models and datasets, demonstrating the effectiveness of the proposed method in reducing biases.
- The use of a universal training framework that operates in the latent space allows for flexibility in adapting to different biases and enhances the method's applicability.
- The integration of classifiers within the EBM framework and the use of ODEs for deterministic sampling offer computational advantages and balance in bias mitigation.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks clarity in explaining the method's implementation, particularly how the ODE is used to navigate the latent space and how the classifiers are integrated into the EBM framework.
- The generalizability of the results is limited as the method's effectiveness varies across different models, and it is not clear whether the observed improvements are due to the model's scale or the method's efficacy.
- There is a lack of discussion on the method's limitations, including potential issues with the method's effectiveness in large language models (LLMs) and the potential for the method to introduce new biases or alter semantic information.
- The paper does not adequately address the computational complexity of the method, which could be a significant drawback in practical applications.
- The method's reliance on a specific model architecture (BERT) and its integration with the VAE decoder could limit its applicability to other models or decoder architectures.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors provide a more detailed explanation of how the ODE is used to navigate the latent space and how the classifiers are integrated into the EBM framework?
- How does the method handle the trade-off between bias mitigation and semantic information preservation, particularly in large language models?
- Can the authors discuss the potential for the method to introduce new biases or alter semantic information in the debiased samples?
- How does the method perform when applied to different model architectures or decoder structures, and what are the implications for its generalizability?
- Could the authors provide more details on the computational complexity of the method and discuss potential strategies for reducing this complexity in practical applications?
