 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel object-level data augmentation method for 3D object detection in autonomous driving, leveraging a neural scene renderer to transform point clouds into 2D images. The method involves a two-step process: first, a dense point cloud is generated by accumulating point clouds from multiple frames, and then, a neural scene renderer is used to project these point clouds onto 2D images. This process allows for flexible object manipulation and the generation of augmented images with corresponding labels, enhancing the diversity and complexity of training data. The proposed method has been tested on the nuScenes dataset, showing improved performance over existing detectors. However, concerns were raised regarding the novelty of the method, the limited evaluation on different datasets, and the computational efficiency of the neural scene renderer.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel object-level data augmentation method for 3D object detection in autonomous driving, which is a significant contribution to the field.
- The method effectively utilizes a neural scene renderer to transform point clouds into 2D images, enabling flexible object manipulation and the generation of augmented images with corresponding labels.
- The paper is well-written, with clear and detailed explanations of the methodology, including the use of a cell-based z-buffer to address the bleeding problem.
- The experimental results demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed method in improving the performance of multi-camera detectors, as shown in Table 1 and Table 2.
- The paper includes a comprehensive evaluation on the nuScenes dataset, which is a widely-used benchmark for autonomous driving, and shows improvements over existing detectors.

**Weaknesses:**
- The novelty of the method is questioned as it resembles existing methods such as those described in "A point-based method for novel view synthesis" and "Point-NeRF: Learning to Render 3D Scenes with Point Clouds," which also use similar techniques for neural scene rendering and point cloud descriptors.
- The paper lacks a thorough evaluation on different datasets, particularly those that are more diverse or challenging, such as Waymo Open or ApolloScape.
- The computational efficiency of the neural scene renderer is not discussed, raising concerns about the practicality and scalability of the method.
- The methodology section is not well-organized, making it difficult to follow the training process of the neural scene renderer and point cloud descriptor.
- The paper does not adequately address how the method handles the bleeding problem, which is critical for the accuracy and effectiveness of the method.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors clarify the differences between their method and existing methods, particularly in terms of novel contributions and improvements over existing techniques?
- How does the proposed method handle the bleeding problem more effectively than previous methods, and what are the specific advantages in terms of accuracy and efficiency?
- What are the specific contributions of the proposed method that make it more effective than existing methods, especially in terms of data augmentation and neural scene rendering?
- How does the proposed method handle different scenarios, such as varying camera angles and object positions, and what are the implications for the quality and diversity of the generated data?
- Can the authors provide more details on the computational efficiency of the neural scene renderer and discuss potential strategies for improving efficiency?
- How does the method ensure the consistency and accuracy of the generated data across different views and frames, and what are the implications for the performance of the detection models?
