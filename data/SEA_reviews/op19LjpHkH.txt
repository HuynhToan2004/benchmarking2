 **Summary:**
The paper introduces Decoupled Actor-Critic (DAC), a novel off-policy algorithm designed to address the exploration-exploitation dilemma in actor-critic reinforcement learning (RL). DAC employs two distinct actors: a conservative actor for TD learning and evaluation, and an optimistic actor for exploration. The optimistic actor is trained to maximize an optimistic Q-value upper bound, while the conservative actor is trained using standard lower bound soft policy learning. DAC demonstrates state-of-the-art performance and sample efficiency on locomotion tasks in DeepMind Control tasks, showing improvements over existing methods like OAC and SAC. The paper also includes a comprehensive set of experiments and ablations, demonstrating the robustness of the proposed method.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel off-policy dual-actor actor-critic setup, where each actor is trained via gradient backpropagation of a specialized objective, which is a significant contribution to the field.
- The proposed method, DAC, is well-motivated and well-explained, with clear explanations of the challenges it addresses and how it addresses them.
- The paper is well-written, making it easy to follow, and the experiments are comprehensive, including a large number of ablations, which demonstrate the robustness of the proposed method.
- DAC outperforms model-free benchmarks in terms of both sample efficiency and final performance, showing significant improvements over existing methods like SAC and OAC.
- The paper provides a detailed comparison with OAC, highlighting the differences and advantages of DAC, and includes a detailed appendix that provides additional insights into the design choices and implementation details.
- The paper is well-organized, with a clear structure and a logical flow, which facilitates understanding of the proposed method and its implications.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed discussion on the limitations of the proposed method, which could help in understanding its applicability and potential drawbacks.
- There is a lack of clarity in some of the figures, particularly in Figure 1, which could benefit from more detailed explanations or additional visual aids.
- The paper could benefit from more detailed explanations of the experimental setup, including the specifics of the hyperparameters used and the rationale behind their choices.
- The paper does not discuss the computational overhead of DAC compared to other methods, which could be a significant concern for practical applications.
- There are some minor errors and inconsistencies in the text and figures, which could be corrected for clarity and accuracy.
- The paper could benefit from a more detailed discussion on the relation of the proposed method to other recent works, such as those mentioned in the review.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors provide more details on the computational overhead of DAC compared to other methods, particularly in terms of memory and computational resources?
- How does the proposed method compare to other recent works that also address the exploration-exploitation dilemma in actor-critic algorithms?
- Could the authors provide a more detailed discussion on the limitations of the proposed method and how they might affect its applicability in different scenarios?
- How does the paper address the potential issues of overestimation and underestimation in the Q-value estimation, particularly in the context of the optimistic and conservative actors?
- Could the authors clarify the discrepancies and inconsistencies noted in the text and figures, such as the missing legends in Figure 2 and the apparent inconsistencies in the experimental results?
- How does the proposed method handle the exploration-exploitation trade-off in more complex tasks, such as those involving stochastic environments or long-horizon tasks?
