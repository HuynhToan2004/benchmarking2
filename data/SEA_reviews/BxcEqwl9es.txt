 **Summary:**
The paper introduces MeFlow, a novel framework for protein sequence design using a diffusion model. MeFlow processes local micro-environments to generate diverse and high-quality sequences, aiming to improve protein design and engineering. The methodology involves training a neural network to predict amino acid sequences based on local micro-environments, which are then used to guide sequence generation. The paper also explores the use of data augmentation to enhance sequence diversity and the application of MeFlow in zero-shot prediction tasks. Despite its innovative approach, the paper faces criticism for its limited novelty, lack of comparison with state-of-the-art methods, and insufficient experimental validation.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel approach to protein sequence design using a diffusion model, which is a significant contribution to the field.
- The methodology is well-articulated, with clear explanations of the training process, the neural network architecture, and the experimental setup.
- The paper is well-written, making it accessible and easy to follow, which is crucial for a broad audience.
- The application of MeFlow in zero-shot prediction tasks demonstrates its versatility and potential for broader applications in protein engineering.
- The paper includes a comprehensive set of experiments, which provides a robust validation of the proposed method.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a thorough comparison with state-of-the-art methods, which limits the assessment of its relative performance and contribution.
- The novelty of the method is questionable as similar approaches using micro-environments and diffusion models have been explored in previous works.
- The paper does not sufficiently discuss the limitations of the proposed method, which is essential for understanding its applicability and potential drawbacks.
- The evaluation metrics used, such as RMSD and recovery ratio, are not convincingly superior to existing methods, and the paper does not provide a clear demonstration of its advantages over other approaches.
- There is a lack of clarity in the description of the experimental setup and the methodology, which could hinder the reproducibility and understanding of the results.
- The paper does not adequately address the computational cost and complexity of the proposed method, which is crucial for assessing its practicality and scalability.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors provide a more detailed comparison with state-of-the-art methods, including a head-to-head comparison of performance metrics and a discussion on the methodological differences?
- How does the proposed method handle the complexity of micro-environments, particularly in terms of the number of atoms and their types?
- Could the authors elaborate on the choice of using a diffusion model over other generative models and discuss the specific advantages of this approach?
- How does the method ensure the diversity of generated sequences, and what are the implications of this diversity in practical applications?
- Could the authors clarify the experimental setup, particularly the use of datasets and the methodology for generating micro-environments?
- What are the potential applications of MeFlow beyond the inverse folding problem, and how does it compare to other methods in these applications?
