 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel regularizer, the weighted group sparse envelope function (WGSEF), designed to induce structured sparsity in neural networks. This regularizer is derived from the sparse envelope function (SEF) and is adapted to handle group-structured sparsity, which is crucial for hardware-friendly sparsity in deep neural networks. The method is demonstrated to maintain network accuracy while reducing computational complexity, as shown through experiments on various datasets. The paper also discusses the theoretical aspects of the regularizer and its application, including a proximal gradient method for optimization. However, the paper's scope is limited to simple datasets and the empirical results are not convincing enough to support the claims of efficiency and effectiveness.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel regularizer, WGSEF, which is derived from the sparse envelope function (SEF) and adapted to handle group-structured sparsity, enhancing the theoretical foundation.
- The authors provide a detailed mathematical derivation of the WGSEF and its proximal operator, which is crucial for understanding the theoretical underpinnings of the proposed method.
- The proposed method is shown to be efficient, with a worst-case complexity of O(n), which is a significant advantage over other methods.
- The paper includes both theoretical and experimental results, which support the effectiveness of the proposed method.
- The authors have conducted experiments on various datasets and models, including CIFAR10, Fashion-MNIST, LeNet-5, VGG16, and ResNet50, which demonstrate the practical applicability of the method.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper's empirical results are not convincing enough to support the claims of efficiency and effectiveness. The results do not show a significant improvement over existing methods, and the experiments are limited to simple datasets.
- The paper lacks a detailed comparison with other state-of-the-art methods, which could have provided a clearer picture of the proposed method's advantages and disadvantages.
- The paper does not provide a detailed analysis of the computational complexity of the optimization process, which is crucial for understanding the practical feasibility of the proposed method.
- The paper's scope is limited to simple datasets, which may not adequately demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed method in more complex scenarios.
- There is a lack of discussion on the limitations of the proposed method, which is crucial for understanding its applicability and potential drawbacks.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors provide a detailed comparison of the proposed method with other state-of-the-art methods in terms of computational complexity, accuracy, and efficiency?
- How does the proposed method compare to other methods that use similar techniques, such as sparse group lasso and other nonconvex regularizers?
- Can the authors clarify the definition of structured sparsity and how it differs from unstructured sparsity?
- How does the proposed method handle the computational complexity of the optimization process, particularly in large-scale neural networks?
- Can the authors provide more detailed results on the computational efficiency and accuracy of the proposed method, especially in more complex scenarios?
