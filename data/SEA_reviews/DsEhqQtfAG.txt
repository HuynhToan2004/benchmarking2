 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel diffusion sampling strategy that integrates Krylov subspace methods with diffusion models to address large-scale linear inverse problems, specifically in medical imaging. It proposes a method that leverages the Krylov subspace to ensure data consistency updates remain within the tangent space, thereby eliminating the need for computationally demanding manifold-constrained gradients. The approach is validated through extensive experiments on real-world medical imaging tasks such as multi-coil MRI reconstruction and 3D CT reconstruction, demonstrating state-of-the-art reconstruction quality with significant speedup over existing methods. The methodology is supported by theoretical analysis and empirical evidence, showing a significant reduction in computational costs and an improvement in reconstruction quality.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel diffusion sampling strategy that synergistically combines diffusion sampling and Krylov subspace methods, providing a new perspective on solving large-scale linear inverse problems.
- The methodology is supported by extensive theoretical analysis and empirical evidence, demonstrating its effectiveness in real-world medical imaging tasks such as multi-coil MRI reconstruction and 3D CT reconstruction.
- The paper is well-organized, with clear and concise writing that effectively communicates the main ideas and contributions.
- The proposed method achieves state-of-the-art reconstruction quality with significant speedup over existing methods, which is crucial for practical applications in medical imaging.
- The theoretical analysis provided in the paper is solid, and the empirical results are convincing, supporting the effectiveness of the proposed method.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed discussion on the limitations of the proposed method, particularly in scenarios where the clean data manifold may not be an affine subspace.
- The experimental results presented in the paper do not clearly demonstrate the superiority of the proposed method over existing methods, particularly in terms of computational efficiency and reconstruction quality.
- The paper does not provide a detailed comparison of the proposed method with other state-of-the-art methods in terms of computational complexity and memory usage, which could affect the practical applicability of the method.
- The paper lacks a discussion on the potential negative societal impacts of the proposed method, which could be significant given its potential applications in medical imaging.
- The paper could benefit from a more detailed discussion on the scalability of the proposed method, particularly in terms of its applicability to larger and more complex imaging datasets.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors provide more detailed explanations on how the clean data manifold is represented as an affine subspace in practical scenarios?
- How does the proposed method compare in terms of computational complexity and memory usage with other state-of-the-art methods, particularly in large-scale medical imaging applications?
- Can the authors provide a detailed comparison of the proposed method with other diffusion-based methods in terms of reconstruction quality and computational efficiency?
- How does the proposed method handle scenarios where the clean data manifold is not an affine subspace?
- Could the authors discuss the potential negative societal impacts of the proposed method, particularly in terms of patient data privacy and the potential for misinterpretation of generated images?
