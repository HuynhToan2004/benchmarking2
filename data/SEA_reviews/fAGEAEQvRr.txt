 **Summary:**
The paper explores the behavior of gradient descent (GD) in matrix factorization problems, focusing on the dynamics of GD with large initialization. It introduces a novel signal-to-noise ratio (SNR) argument and an inductive argument to analyze the trajectory of GD from the initial point to the global minima. The authors demonstrate that GD can exhibit incremental learning, which aligns with experimental observations. They also discuss the convergence properties of GD with large initialization, showing that it can achieve fast convergence to the global minima. However, the paper is criticized for its limited scope, as it primarily focuses on symmetric positive semi-definite (PSD) matrix factorization and does not generalize to other types of matrices. Furthermore, the paper's assumptions, such as the strictly decreasing top eigenvalues, are seen as too restrictive.

**Strengths:**
- The paper provides a comprehensive analysis of the trajectory of gradient descent (GD) in addressing matrix factorization issues, focusing particularly on instances with large initialization.
- The authors introduce a novel signal-to-noise (SNR) analysis and an inductive argument to understand the dynamics of GD in matrix factorization problems.
- The paper is well-written, with clear figures and detailed explanations that aid in understanding the results.
- The analysis of GD with large initialization is a significant contribution to the understanding of matrix factorization, which is a simplified model for neural network study.
- The paper is original in its approach, using SNR analysis to prove the local linear convergence of GD in general rank problems, and provides a detailed sketch of proof for the results.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper's analysis is limited to symmetric positive semi-definite (PSD) matrix factorization, which may not generalize to other types of matrices.
- The assumptions made in the paper, such as the strictly decreasing top eigenvalues, are too restrictive and may not hold in practical scenarios.
- The paper does not adequately address the practical implications of its findings, particularly how the results apply to real-world scenarios.
- The paper's theoretical contributions are seen as incremental, as the analysis closely resembles that of previous works, and the proofs are not sufficiently distinct.
- The paper's scope is limited to symmetric positive semi-definite (PSD) matrix factorization, which may not be representative of more complex or realistic matrix factorization problems.
- The paper's assumptions, such as the requirement that the top eigenvalues be strictly decreasing, are not practical and may not hold in real-world scenarios.
