 **Summary:**
The paper introduces RedMotion, a transformer-based model for motion prediction in autonomous driving, utilizing two types of redundancy reduction. The first is an internal redundancy reduction within the transformer decoder, converting variable-sized road environment tokens into a fixed-sized embedding. The second is a self-supervised learning objective that applies redundancy reduction to embeddings from augmented road environment views. The model's performance is evaluated on the Waymo Open Motion dataset, showing improvements over existing methods like PreTAM, Traj-MAE, and GraphDINO. The paper is well-organized, with clear explanations of the methodology and results, although it lacks a detailed comparison with recent state-of-the-art methods and does not provide the code for reproducibility.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-organized, making it easy to follow, and the methodology is clearly explained.
- The proposed model is simple, intuitive, and effective, with good performance reported.
- The paper provides a comprehensive evaluation, comparing the proposed method with various baselines, and includes detailed ablation studies.
- The introduction of redundancy reduction in motion prediction is novel and interesting, contributing to the field.
- The paper is well-written, with clear figures and a logical flow of information.
- The results are promising, showing improvements over existing methods like PreTAM, Traj-MAE, and GraphDINO.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed comparison with recent state-of-the-art methods, which could enhance the understanding of the proposed model's advantages and limitations.
- The paper does not provide the code for reproducibility, which is crucial for validating the results and allowing others to build upon the work.
- The paper could benefit from more detailed discussions on the limitations of the proposed method and potential avenues for future research.
- The paper primarily uses the Waymo Open Motion dataset for evaluation, which might not fully demonstrate the model's effectiveness across different datasets and real-world scenarios.
- There are some typographical and formatting errors that need correction.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors provide a more detailed comparison with recent state-of-the-art methods to better understand the advantages and limitations of the proposed model?
- Could the authors provide the code for reproducibility and to allow others to build upon the work?
- How does the proposed method perform on other datasets beyond the Waymo Open Motion dataset?
- Can the authors discuss the potential limitations of the proposed method and suggest areas for future research?
