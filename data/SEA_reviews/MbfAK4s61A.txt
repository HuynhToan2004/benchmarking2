 **Summary:**
The paper explores the vulnerability of large language models (LLMs) to adversarial prompts in the form of ciphers, which can bypass safety mechanisms. The authors propose a framework called CipherChat, which includes a system prompt to guide LLMs to understand and generate ciphered responses. They demonstrate that LLMs can be manipulated into generating unsafe responses by encoding unsafe demonstrations in different ciphers. The study also introduces a new cipher called SelfCipher, which does not require explicit cipher rules, and shows that it can be as effective as human ciphers in certain scenarios. The paper provides experimental evidence that LLMs can be influenced by ciphered prompts, highlighting a significant safety concern.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, making it easy to follow and understand the proposed methodologies and results.
- The authors have conducted extensive experiments to demonstrate the effectiveness of their proposed method, which includes a new cipher called SelfCipher.
- The findings of the paper are significant and contribute to the ongoing research on safety and alignment of large language models (LLMs).
- The paper provides a novel approach to understanding the vulnerabilities of LLMs, which is crucial for improving their safety and reliability.
- The authors have conducted a thorough analysis of the results, which helps in understanding the implications of their findings.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed explanation of how the SelfCipher works, which could limit the reproducibility and understanding of the results.
- There is a concern about the generalizability of the findings to other types of ciphers or adversarial prompts, which could limit the scope of the study.
- The paper does not adequately address the ethical implications of the research, particularly the potential misuse of the findings to create harmful content.
- The paper's claims about the effectiveness of the proposed methods are not sufficiently supported by quantitative evidence, which could undermine the credibility of the results.
- The paper does not sufficiently differentiate its approach from existing methods, which could lead to confusion about the novelty and significance of the contributions.

**Questions:**
- Could you provide more details on how the SelfCipher works and how it is implemented in the experiments?
- How do the authors ensure the reliability and validity of the results, especially in light of the potential for misinterpretation or misuse of the findings?
- Can the proposed method be generalized to other types of ciphers or adversarial prompts, or is it specific to the ciphers used in the study?
- How do the authors address the ethical concerns raised by the research, particularly in terms of preventing the misuse of the findings?
- Could you clarify the definitions and roles of different ciphers used in the study, especially in relation to the proposed SelfCipher?
- How do the authors plan to address the potential for misuse of the findings, and what steps are being taken to ensure the safety and ethical implications of the research are considered?
