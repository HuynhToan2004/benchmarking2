 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel non-stationary contextual bandit algorithm, NeuralPES, which integrates a scalable deep-neural-network-based architecture with a strategic exploration mechanism that prioritizes collecting information of lasting value in non-stationary environments. The algorithm is evaluated on two real-world recommendation datasets, demonstrating significant improvements over state-of-the-art baselines. The theoretical analysis provided supports the algorithm's ability to prioritize lasting information, and empirical results showcase its effectiveness. However, the paper's scope is limited to contextual bandits, and the theoretical analysis is restricted to linear contextual bandits, which may limit its applicability to more complex or general settings.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, easy to follow, and the theoretical analysis is clear and correct.
- The empirical results are strong, showing that the proposed method outperforms baselines on two real-world recommendation datasets.
- The novel approach of combining a scalable deep-neural-network-based architecture with a strategic exploration mechanism that prioritizes collecting information of lasting value in non-stationary environments is innovative.
- The theoretical analysis provided supports the algorithm's ability to prioritize lasting information.
- The paper is well-structured and includes a detailed related work section, which is helpful for readers unfamiliar with the field.
- The proposed algorithm is shown to be effective in real-world settings, which is a significant strength.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper's contribution is somewhat limited as the proposed method is not entirely novel, as similar ideas have been explored in previous works.
- The theoretical analysis is restricted to linear contextual bandits, which may limit the applicability of the results to more general or complex settings.
- The paper's focus is narrow, as it only considers contextual bandits and does not explore the application of the proposed method in other types of bandits or more general settings.
- The paper lacks a detailed discussion on the limitations of the proposed method and potential future directions for research.
- The empirical evaluation is limited to two datasets, which may not be sufficient to fully assess the algorithm's performance across different scenarios.
- The paper does not provide a detailed discussion on the limitations of the proposed method and potential future directions for research.
- There is a lack of clarity in some sections of the paper, such as the explanation of certain figures and the use of terminology.
