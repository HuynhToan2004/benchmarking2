 **Summary:**
The paper introduces Sorted Adaptive Prediction Sets (SAPS), a novel conformal prediction algorithm that discards probability values in favor of label ranking. SAPS is designed to minimize the dependence on softmax probabilities, aiming to generate smaller prediction sets. Theoretical analysis and empirical results demonstrate that SAPS can achieve smaller prediction sets and higher conditional coverage rates compared to existing methods like Adaptive Prediction Sets (APS) and Regularized Adaptive Prediction Sets (RAPS). The paper also discusses the practical implications of using SAPS in real-world applications, although it lacks a detailed discussion on the computational complexity and scalability of the algorithm.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, clear, and easy to follow, making it accessible to a broad audience.
- The motivation behind the proposed method is well-articulated, and the theoretical analysis provided is solid.
- The empirical results are convincing, showing that the proposed method outperforms existing methods in terms of prediction set size and conditional coverage rate.
- The paper presents a novel approach to conformal prediction by discarding probability values and focusing on label ranking, which is a significant departure from traditional methods.
- The experimental setup is comprehensive, covering a range of models and datasets, and the results are clearly presented with well-designed figures and tables.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed discussion on the computational complexity of SAPS, which is crucial for understanding its scalability and practical applicability.
- The paper does not compare SAPS with other methods like MC-dropout and other uncertainty quantification methods, which could provide a more comprehensive evaluation of its performance.
- The paper could benefit from a more detailed discussion on the limitations and potential drawbacks of SAPS, especially in terms of its applicability and effectiveness in real-world scenarios.
- The paper does not discuss the societal impact of conformal prediction, which is an important consideration in the field.
- The theoretical analysis and proofs provided in the paper are not always clear or intuitive, which could limit the paper's accessibility to readers not familiar with the underlying concepts.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors provide a more detailed analysis of the computational complexity of SAPS and discuss its scalability in more detail?
- How does SAPS compare with other methods like MC-dropout and other uncertainty quantification methods?
- Can the authors discuss the limitations and potential drawbacks of SAPS, especially in terms of its applicability and effectiveness in real-world scenarios?
- How does the paper address the issue of calibration in the models used, and what are the implications of this for the performance of SAPS?
- Could the authors provide a more detailed explanation of the theoretical results and proofs, especially in the appendix, to make them more accessible to readers?
