 **Summary:**
The paper introduces SlimTTT, a resource-adaptive test-time training framework designed to address the computational resource constraints in real-world scenarios. It leverages the slimmable network architecture to optimize model performance across diverse devices, enabling the switching between different sub-networks based on computational budgets. The framework incorporates techniques such as Width-enhanced Contrastive Learning, Logits Consistency Regularization, and Global Feature Alignment to enhance representation consistency and performance. The experiments conducted across multiple datasets demonstrate the effectiveness of SlimTTT in achieving state-of-the-art results with reduced computational complexity. However, concerns were raised about the novelty of the method, the clarity of its contribution to the field, and the lack of comprehensive comparisons with state-of-the-art methods.

**Strengths:**
- The paper addresses a practical and significant issue in the field of test-time training by focusing on the computational resource constraints, which is often overlooked in existing methods.
- The use of slimmable networks to adapt to varying computational budgets during inference is a novel and practical approach.
- The paper is well-written, making it easy to follow, and includes a comprehensive set of experiments that demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed method.
- The introduction of Width-enhanced Contrastive Learning, Logits Consistency Regularization, and Global Feature Alignment provides a comprehensive approach to enhancing representation consistency and improving model performance.
- The methodology is simple and intuitive, with a clear explanation of the motivation and the technical details of the proposed framework.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a thorough comparison with state-of-the-art methods, particularly in terms of computational efficiency and model performance.
- The novelty of the method is questionable as it primarily builds upon existing techniques such as slimmable networks and contrastive learning, with limited new insights or methodologies introduced.
- The paper could benefit from more comprehensive ablation studies to better understand the impact of each component of the proposed framework.
- The presentation of the paper could be improved, particularly in terms of clarity and organization, especially in the introduction and methodology sections.
- The paper does not sufficiently address the practical implications of the proposed method, such as the scalability of the framework across different devices and the practical deployment scenarios.
- The experimental results and comparisons are not convincing, with some results showing a lack of statistical significance or a significant gap compared to state-of-the-art methods.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors clarify the practical scenarios where the proposed method would be most beneficial and how it addresses the specific challenges in these scenarios?
- How does the proposed method compare in terms of computational efficiency and model performance with state-of-the-art methods, especially in resource-constrained environments?
- Could the authors provide more detailed comparisons and analyses to demonstrate the superiority of their method over existing approaches?
- Is there a possibility to include a more detailed ablation study to better understand the individual contributions of each component of the proposed framework?
- How does the proposed method handle the computational overhead during the test-time training phase, and what are the implications for practical deployment?
- Can the authors elaborate on the practical implications of the proposed method, such as its scalability and adaptability to different devices and scenarios?
