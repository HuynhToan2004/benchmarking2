 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel approach, OLS-OFU, for online label shift adaptation, leveraging self-supervised learning to enhance feature extraction from unlabeled data at test time. This method refines feature representations to adapt to changing label distributions, thereby improving model performance. Theoretical analysis and empirical results on CIFAR-10 and CIFAR-10C datasets demonstrate the effectiveness of OLS-OFU in reducing algorithmic regret and enhancing model robustness. The methodology integrates self-supervised learning with online label shift adaptation, providing a novel perspective on handling distribution shifts in online learning scenarios.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel approach, OLS-OFU, which integrates self-supervised learning with online label shift adaptation, offering a fresh perspective on handling distribution shifts in online learning scenarios.
- The methodology is well-motivated, and the paper is well-written, making it easy to follow.
- The proposed method, OLS-OFU, demonstrates effectiveness in reducing algorithmic regret and enhancing model robustness, as evidenced by theoretical analysis and empirical results on CIFAR-10 and CIFAR-10C datasets.
- The paper is the first to apply self-supervised learning to online label shift adaptation, which is a significant contribution to the field.
- The theoretical results are well-presented, and the paper provides a clear explanation of the method's components and their integration.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a comprehensive discussion on the limitations and potential drawbacks of the proposed method, OLS-OFU.
- The empirical results are not sufficiently robust, with the experiments being limited to CIFAR-10 and CIFAR-10C datasets, which may not adequately represent real-world scenarios.
- There is a need for more extensive empirical evaluations to validate the effectiveness of OLS-OFU in more diverse and complex scenarios.
- The paper does not sufficiently address the computational efficiency of the method, which could be a significant concern in practical applications.
- The paper could benefit from a more thorough discussion of related work, particularly in the area of online label shift adaptation, to better position its contributions within the existing literature.
- The paper could be improved by providing a more detailed explanation of the self-supervised learning methods used and their integration with the proposed method.

**Questions:**
- How does the proposed method, OLS-OFU, perform in scenarios where the label shift is more severe or less frequent?
- Could you elaborate on the computational efficiency of the method, particularly how it scales with the size of the dataset or the complexity of the model?
- How does the method perform when compared to other state-of-the-art methods in terms of computational efficiency and practical applicability?
- What are the potential limitations or drawbacks of the proposed method, and how might these affect its practical utility?
- In the context of the experimental results, how does the performance of OLS-OFU compare to other methods when using different SSL techniques or under varying conditions of domain shift severity?
