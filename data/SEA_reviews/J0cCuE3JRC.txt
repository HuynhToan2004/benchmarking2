 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel feature-based approach for link prediction, focusing on the integration of structural and domain features. It contrasts this method with Graph Neural Networks (GNNs) by highlighting the potential of feature engineering in link prediction tasks. The authors propose a Bag of Features (BFLP) model that leverages both structural and domain features to achieve competitive performance on various datasets. The paper also discusses the limitations of GNNs in terms of expressivity and suggests that feature engineering could potentially outperform GNNs in certain scenarios. However, the novelty of the approach is questioned due to its reliance on existing feature engineering techniques and the lack of significant innovation.

**Strengths:**
- The paper provides a comprehensive review of existing feature engineering techniques for link prediction, which is beneficial for understanding the current state of the field.
- The proposed BFLP model effectively combines local neighborhood information and domain-specific node features, demonstrating competitive performance on benchmark datasets.
- The paper is well-structured, easy to follow, and includes a detailed discussion on the limitations of the proposed method.
- The authors provide detailed descriptions of their method, including the feature extraction process, which is crucial for reproducibility and transparency.
- The paper is significant in its exploration of feature engineering for link prediction, which is often overlooked in favor of more complex models like GNNs.

**Weaknesses:**
- The novelty of the paper is limited as the proposed BFLP model primarily combines existing feature engineering techniques without significant innovation.
- The paper does not sufficiently discuss the limitations of the BFLP model, which could affect its generalizability and applicability in different scenarios.
- The performance of the BFLP model on larger datasets is not reported, which raises concerns about its scalability.
- The paper's claims about the limitations of GNNs in link prediction are not convincingly supported by empirical evidence, particularly in light of recent advancements in GNN models.
- The paper does not sufficiently differentiate its approach from existing methods, which could lead to confusion about the unique contributions of the proposed model.
- The paper's focus on link prediction may limit its applicability to other graph-related tasks, such as node classification and graph classification.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors clarify the distinction between their proposed method and existing feature engineering methods, particularly in terms of the novelty and effectiveness of the BFLP model?
- How does the BFLP model perform on larger datasets, and what are the computational implications of scaling up the model?
- Could the authors provide more detailed experimental results, including the performance of the BFLP model on different types of datasets and the impact of different hyperparameters on its performance?
- How do the authors address the potential limitations of their model, such as its generalizability and scalability?
- Could the authors discuss the potential of integrating the BFLP model with GNNs to enhance its performance, and what are the challenges associated with such an integration?
