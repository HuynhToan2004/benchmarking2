 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel approach to data augmentation for graph learning, specifically focusing on out-of-distribution (OOD) generalization. The method involves the use of linear extrapolation in both the structural and feature spaces of graphs, which is facilitated by the use of a conditional variational autoencoder (cVAE) and a pre-trained graph neural network (GNN). The authors propose two methods: G-Splice for structural extrapolation and FeatX for feature extrapolation. Theoretical justifications are provided, and extensive experiments are conducted to demonstrate the effectiveness of these methods. The paper also introduces a new dataset, FSMotif, for evaluating the proposed methods. Despite its innovative approach, the paper has been critiqued for its clarity in explaining the methodology, particularly the role of the conditional variational autoencoder (cVAE) and the lack of comprehensive comparisons with existing methods.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, easy to follow, and presents a novel approach to graph data augmentation, focusing on both structural and feature extrapolation.
- The authors introduce a new dataset, FSMotif, which can be beneficial for future research in the field.
- The methodology is theoretically justified, with theorems that support the effectiveness of the proposed methods.
- The paper is original in its approach to graph data augmentation, specifically in its focus on extrapolation rather than interpolation, which is a novel aspect in this context.
- The paper provides a clear and comprehensive explanation of the methodology, making it accessible to readers.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a clear explanation of how the conditional variational autoencoder (cVAE) is used to generate bridges between component graphs, which is a critical component of the proposed method.
- There is a lack of comparison with existing methods, particularly in terms of the use of cVAE and the selection of non-causal features.
- The paper does not adequately address the issue of causal and non-causal features, which is crucial for the effectiveness of the proposed method.
- The experimental results are not convincing, particularly in the comparisons with existing methods, and the paper does not provide a comprehensive ablation study to demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed methods.
- The paper suffers from clarity issues, particularly in the introduction and explanation of key concepts and methodologies.
- The paper lacks a discussion on the limitations of the proposed method, which is essential for a comprehensive understanding of its applicability and effectiveness.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors provide a clearer explanation of how the cVAE is used to generate bridges between component graphs?
- How does the selection of non-causal features affect the performance of the proposed method?
- What is the rationale behind the use of cVAE in the proposed method, and how does it contribute to the effectiveness of the method?
- Could the authors clarify the role of causal and non-causal features in the proposed method, and how they are identified and utilized?
- How does the proposed method handle the issue of causal and non-causal features in real-world datasets?
- Can the authors provide more details on the training and inference processes for the cVAE, particularly how it is trained and how it is used in the proposed method?
- What are the specific advantages of the proposed method over existing methods, and how does it address the limitations of these methods?
