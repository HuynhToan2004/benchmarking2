 **Summary:**
The paper investigates the effectiveness of concept erasure methods in text-to-image generative models, specifically focusing on Stable Diffusion models. It critically evaluates seven recently proposed methods, demonstrating that these methods do not fully erase targeted concepts. The authors propose a Concept Inversion (CI) method to retrieve "erased" concepts from sanitized models without altering their weights. The study highlights the limitations of current concept erasure techniques and suggests that new approaches are necessary to ensure the safety and ethical use of these models. Despite the paper's comprehensive evaluation, it has been criticized for its lack of novelty, limited scope, and insufficient exploration of potential solutions.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-organized and clearly written, making it accessible and easy to follow.
- The research addresses a significant and relevant topic, namely the limitations of concept erasure methods in text-to-image generative models.
- The experiments are thorough and convincingly demonstrate the ineffectiveness of existing concept erasure methods.
- The paper provides a comprehensive evaluation of seven recently proposed concept erasure methods, showcasing their limitations and potential vulnerabilities.
- The proposed Concept Inversion (CI) method is innovative and offers a novel approach to retrieving "erased" concepts from sanitized models without altering their weights.
- The paper raises important questions about the safety and ethical implications of text-to-image generative models, emphasizing the need for more robust and reliable concept erasure methods.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks novelty as the Concept Inversion (CI) method closely resembles existing techniques such as textual inversion, which have been previously explored.
- The methodology and results are limited to Stable Diffusion models, which may not generalize to other types of generative models.
- The paper does not propose a solution to the problem it identifies, which could be seen as a significant shortcoming.
- The evaluation of the CI method is limited to a few concepts and models, which may not sufficiently demonstrate its effectiveness or generalizability.
- The paper could benefit from a more comprehensive discussion on the implications of its findings and the potential directions for future research.
- The presentation of results, particularly in tables and figures, could be improved to enhance readability and clarity.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors clarify the main differences between their CI method and existing textual inversion methods?
- How does the CI method perform when applied to different types of generative models beyond Stable Diffusion?
- Could the authors provide more details on the selection and evaluation of the special word embeddings used in the CI method?
- How does the CI method handle the retrieval of erased concepts from models that have undergone multiple rounds of concept erasure?
- Can the authors discuss the implications of their findings for the development of more effective and reliable concept erasure methods in the future?
- How does the CI method compare in terms of effectiveness and efficiency with other existing methods for retrieving erased concepts from sanitized models?
