 **Summary:**
The paper introduces UGradSL, a novel method for machine unlearning that integrates gradient ascent with label smoothing. This approach aims to improve the unlearning process by reducing the confidence of the model on the data to be forgotten, thus enhancing the model's performance on remaining data. Theoretical analysis and extensive experiments on various datasets demonstrate the effectiveness of UGradSL in achieving high unlearning accuracy with minimal computational overhead. The method is validated against multiple baselines, showing improvements in unlearning performance. However, concerns are raised about the practicality of the method due to its reliance on a large unlearning dataset, which may not be available in real-world scenarios.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-organized, easy to follow, and provides a clear and detailed explanation of the proposed method.
- The proposed method, UGradSL, is simple, practical, and achieves significant improvements over existing methods, as demonstrated by comprehensive experiments.
- The theoretical analysis of the proposed method is solid and provides valuable insights into the influence of label smoothing on machine unlearning.
- The paper addresses a critical and under-explored problem in machine unlearning, focusing on the practical implementation of the method.
- The methodology is supported by thorough experiments that demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed method across various datasets and tasks.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a clear discussion on the practicality of the method, particularly concerning the availability of a large unlearning dataset which may not be feasible in real-world scenarios.
- The theoretical analysis, while solid, does not provide a clear intuitive explanation of the proposed method's effectiveness, which could hinder understanding for readers less familiar with the theoretical underpinnings.
- The experimental setup and results could be more convincing if the paper included comparisons with more recent and relevant unlearning methods, such as those based on influence functions.
- The paper's contribution to the field is somewhat limited as the proposed method primarily builds upon existing techniques like gradient ascent and label smoothing, without introducing significant new concepts.
- The paper's presentation could be improved by addressing minor typographical errors and enhancing the clarity of some sections, particularly in the theoretical analysis.

**Questions:**
- How does the proposed method perform in real-world scenarios where the unlearning dataset is not as large or varied as in the experiments?
- Could the authors provide more detailed comparisons with recent unlearning methods based on influence functions to better establish the method's effectiveness?
- How does the proposed method compare in terms of computational efficiency and practical implementation to other unlearning methods that do not require a large unlearning dataset?
- In the theoretical analysis, could the authors provide more intuitive explanations or examples to help readers better understand the implications of the mathematical derivations?
- How does the proposed method handle different types of data, such as textual or time-series data, which may not be as easily unlearned using the current methodology?
