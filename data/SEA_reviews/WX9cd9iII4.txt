 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel framework, FairPol, for fair off-policy learning from observational data. It addresses the challenges of ensuring fairness in decision-making by proposing two fairness notions: action fairness and value fairness. The framework involves a two-step process: first, learning a fair representation of data using a neural network, and second, training a policy network that outputs fair actions. Theoretical guarantees are provided in the form of generalization bounds, supported by both synthetic and real-world data experiments. The paper aims to enhance fairness in off-policy learning by addressing the potential biases in observational data, which are often collected under discriminatory behavioral policies.

**Strengths:**
- The paper addresses an important and relevant problem of fair off-policy learning, which is crucial in real-world applications.
- The proposed framework, FairPol, is novel and provides a flexible instantiation of the framework using machine learning, which is supported by theoretical guarantees.
- The paper is well-written, with clear and concise definitions and explanations of the two fairness notions, action fairness and value fairness.
- The experiments conducted, both synthetic and real-world, support the effectiveness of the proposed framework in achieving fairness.
- The paper is well-organized and easy to follow, with a clear structure that makes it accessible to readers.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a thorough discussion on the limitations and potential negative societal impacts of the proposed framework.
- The relationship between the two fairness notions, action fairness and value fairness, is not adequately explored, and the implications of combining these notions are not discussed.
- The paper does not provide a comprehensive discussion on the trade-offs between fairness and performance, which is crucial for practical applications.
- The theoretical guarantees provided are limited, and the paper does not adequately address the potential for fairness and performance trade-offs in different scenarios.
- The paper does not discuss the scalability of the proposed method to larger datasets or more complex scenarios, which could limit its applicability in real-world settings.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors provide a more detailed discussion on the limitations and potential negative societal impacts of the proposed framework?
- How does the paper address the trade-offs between fairness and performance, especially in practical applications?
- Could the authors elaborate on the relationship between action fairness and value fairness, and discuss the implications of combining these notions?
- How does the proposed method perform in scenarios where the dataset is imbalanced or the sensitive attributes are highly correlated with the outcomes?
- Could the authors provide a more detailed discussion on the scalability of the proposed method to larger datasets or more complex scenarios?
- In the experiments, how do the results vary with different hyperparameter settings, and what are the optimal settings for achieving the best trade-off between fairness and performance?
