 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel dataset, FruitBin, specifically designed for 6D pose estimation in fruit bin picking scenarios. This dataset, which includes over a million images and 40 million instance-level 6D poses, is notable for its extensive annotations and metadata, such as 6D pose, depth, segmentation masks, point clouds, 2D and 3D bounding boxes, and occlusion rates. The dataset is constructed using PickSim and is compatible with robotic software, enabling direct testing of trained models in dynamic grasping tasks. The paper proposes two benchmarks, one for novel scene generalization and another for novel camera viewpoint generalization, with four levels of occlusion to facilitate the study of occlusion robustness. The dataset is claimed to be the largest of its kind, with extensive comparisons to existing datasets. However, the paper is criticized for its limited novelty, as the dataset primarily uses existing simulators and does not introduce significant new methodologies.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel dataset specifically designed for 6D pose estimation in fruit bin picking, which is a unique and significant application in the field of robotics.
- The dataset is extensive, featuring over 1 million images and 40 million instance-level 6D poses, making it the largest of its kind.
- The dataset includes various annotations and metadata, such as 6D pose, depth, segmentation masks, point clouds, 2D and 3D bounding boxes, and occlusion rates, which are crucial for evaluating the performance of 6D pose estimation models.
- The paper proposes two benchmarks, one for novel scene generalization and another for novel camera viewpoint generalization, with four levels of occlusion, which facilitate the study of occlusion robustness.
- The dataset is compatible with robotic software, allowing for direct testing of trained models in dynamic grasping tasks, which is crucial for practical applications in robotics.
- The paper is well-written and provides a comprehensive comparison with existing datasets, highlighting the unique challenges and advantages of the FruitBin dataset.

**Weaknesses:**
- The novelty of the dataset is limited, as it primarily uses existing simulators (PickSim) and does not introduce significant new methodologies for data collection or annotation.
- The paper lacks a thorough discussion on the challenges specific to fruit bin picking scenarios and how the dataset addresses these challenges.
- The dataset is synthetic, which may not fully address the sim2real gap that is prevalent in real-world applications of 6D pose estimation.
- The paper does not provide a detailed analysis of the challenges of fruit bin picking scenarios, which could have been included in the dataset description.
- The paper does not include a discussion on the limitations of the dataset, such as the synthetic nature of the data and the potential for overfitting due to the extensive annotations.
- The paper does not provide a detailed comparison with other datasets, such as the SynPick dataset, which also focuses on 6D pose estimation in fruit picking scenarios.
- The paper does not include a discussion on the potential negative societal impacts of the research, which is a critical consideration in ethical research.

**Questions:**
- How does the dataset address the specific challenges of fruit bin picking scenarios, such as occlusion, texture, and lighting conditions?
- What are the potential applications of the dataset beyond fruit bin picking, and how does it compare to other datasets in terms of generalizability?
- How does the dataset handle the sim2real gap, and what steps are being taken to ensure the dataset's applicability in real-world scenarios?
- Can the authors provide a detailed comparison of the FruitBin dataset with other datasets, such as the SynPick dataset, in terms of dataset size, annotations, and challenges?
- How does the dataset ensure that the 6D poses are accurate, especially in scenarios with high occlusion rates?
- Could the authors discuss the potential negative societal impacts of the research, such as the automation of fruit picking jobs, and how these impacts are being mitigated?
