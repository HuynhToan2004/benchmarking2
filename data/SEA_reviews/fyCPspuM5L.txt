 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a new dataset named PowerGraph, specifically designed for cascading failure events in power grids, using a physics-based cascading failure model. This dataset, which includes several graph-level tasks, aims to train GNN models and enhance explainability methods. The paper evaluates several GNN models and explainability methods on this dataset, showing the utility of GNNs in predicting cascading failures and providing explanations for these predictions. The dataset is publicly available and is expected to be beneficial for further research in the field. However, the paper is criticized for its limited novelty, as it primarily uses existing models and datasets, and for not sufficiently demonstrating the practical utility of its contributions.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a new dataset, PowerGraph, which is the first of its kind to be publicly available, providing a valuable resource for the community.
- The dataset is well-documented, with clear explanations of the features and tasks, making it accessible for various applications.
- The paper provides a comprehensive evaluation of different GNN models and explainability methods, which is crucial for understanding the effectiveness of these tools in predicting cascading failures.
- The dataset is designed to be useful for the community, with potential applications in various fields beyond power systems, such as chemistry and biology.
- The paper is well-written and easy to follow, which enhances its readability and accessibility.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper's contribution is limited as it primarily uses existing models and datasets, without significantly advancing the state of the art.
- The practical utility of the dataset is not clearly demonstrated, particularly in terms of how it can be applied to real-world power systems to improve performance.
- The paper does not adequately address the generalization of GNN models to unseen power systems, which is a significant limitation for the practical application of the findings.
- The dataset's size and complexity are not sufficiently justified, raising questions about its ability to capture the complexity of real-world power systems.
- The paper lacks a detailed discussion on the limitations of the dataset and the potential biases that could affect the results.
- The paper's methodology and results are not sufficiently novel, as many of the models and methods used are already well-known in the field.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors clarify how the dataset's features and tasks can be applied to real-world power systems to improve performance?
- How do the authors address the generalization of GNN models to unseen power systems?
- Can the authors provide more detailed justification for the choice of the Cascades model and the specific features included in the dataset?
- How does the dataset's complexity and size compare to real-world power systems, and what steps were taken to ensure that the dataset captures the necessary complexity?
- Could the authors discuss the potential biases in the dataset and how these might affect the results?
- How does the paper's methodology and results contribute novel insights or advancements beyond what is already known in the field?
