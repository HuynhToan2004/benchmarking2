 **Summary:**
The paper introduces DEEP-ALIGN, a novel approach to weight alignment in deep neural networks, leveraging equivariant architectures to learn optimal permutations between weight matrices. DEEP-ALIGN is designed to be computationally efficient, making it suitable for applications where weight alignment is critical but time-consuming. The methodology is supported by theoretical analysis and extensive experiments across various datasets and network architectures, demonstrating the approach's effectiveness and efficiency compared to existing methods. The paper also discusses the potential of DEEP-ALIGN as a tool for model merging and enhancing the optimization landscape of deep neural networks.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel approach to weight alignment using equivariant architectures, which is a significant contribution to the field.
- The proposed method is computationally efficient, which is crucial for practical applications where weight alignment is required.
- The paper is well-written, clear, and easy to follow, with a logical structure that aids in understanding the complex concepts presented.
- Theoretical analysis and extensive experiments are provided to support the claims made about the effectiveness and efficiency of the proposed method.
- The methodology is original, providing a new perspective on weight alignment and potentially opening new avenues for research in this area.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a thorough discussion on the limitations of the proposed method, which could provide a more balanced view of its capabilities and potential drawbacks.
- There is a lack of clarity regarding the specific contributions of the proposed method compared to existing methods, such as those mentioned in the paper by Navon et al. (2023).
- The paper could benefit from a more detailed explanation of the theoretical underpinnings, particularly in the appendix, to enhance understanding for readers less familiar with the subject matter.
- The experiments are limited to a few datasets and network architectures, which may not fully demonstrate the generalizability of the proposed method across different scenarios.
- The paper does not discuss potential negative societal impacts of the proposed method, which could be a significant concern for some applications.

**Questions:**
- Can you clarify the specific contributions of the proposed method compared to existing methods, particularly those mentioned in the paper by Navon et al. (2023)?
- How does the proposed method perform when applied to larger, more complex networks, such as those used in large-scale applications like ImageNet?
- Could you provide more details on the training process for the DEEP-ALIGN network, including the duration and complexity of this process?
- How does the proposed method compare to other methods that use neural networks to solve weight alignment problems, such as those mentioned in the paper by Navon et al. (2023)?
- Is there a possibility of extending the proposed method to handle more complex permutation symmetries, such as those that involve multiple layers of permutations?
- How does the method handle the case where the weight matrices are not permutation symmetric, and what are the implications of this for the alignment process?
- Could you discuss the potential negative societal impacts of the proposed method, and how these might be mitigated or addressed in future research?
