 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel graph pooling method named Global-Local Attention Pooling (GLA-Pool), which integrates both global and local properties of graphs. This approach leverages attention mechanisms to identify informative cliques and select significant nodes within these cliques, aiming to capture both global and local topological structures. The methodology involves a two-channel system, with one channel focusing on clique selection and the other on node selection, and is supported by a smart data augmentation technique to enhance performance on small-scale datasets. Extensive experiments demonstrate that GLA-Pool outperforms several state-of-the-art models on six datasets and is competitive on others. However, the paper faces criticism for its computational complexity, potential overfitting on small datasets, and lack of detailed comparative analysis with other recent methods.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel graph pooling method that effectively integrates both global and local properties of graphs, which is a significant advancement in graph neural networks (GNNs).
- The methodology is well-explained, with clear motivation and detailed descriptions, making it easy to understand and replicate.
- Extensive experiments are conducted on eight publicly available graph classification benchmarks, demonstrating the effectiveness of the proposed method in outperforming thirteen state-of-the-art models on six datasets and being competitive on others.
- The paper is well-written, with a clear structure and logical flow, making it accessible and understandable.
- The proposed method shows good performance and outperforms most baselines, demonstrating its effectiveness in graph classification tasks.

**Weaknesses:**
- The computational complexity of the proposed method is high due to the multiple clique and node selection processes, which may limit its applicability to larger graphs.
- The paper lacks a detailed comparison with recent graph pooling methods such as C2N-ABDP and Wit-TopoPool, which could have provided a more comprehensive evaluation of the proposed method.
- The method's performance on some datasets, such as Reddit-binary, is significantly lower than the state-of-the-art, raising concerns about its generalizability and effectiveness.
- The paper does not provide sufficient details on the training process, particularly the selection of hyperparameters and the impact of different training strategies on the performance of the proposed method.
- The method's reliance on clique structures for global topological structure learning may limit its applicability to graphs without such structures, such as those with many isolated nodes.
- The paper does not adequately address the potential for overfitting on small datasets due to the use of data augmentation techniques.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors clarify the computational complexity of the proposed method and discuss potential strategies for reducing it?
- How does the proposed method perform when compared to other recent graph pooling methods like C2N-ABDP and Wit-TopoPool?
- Can the authors provide more details on the training process, including the selection of hyperparameters and the impact of different training strategies on the performance of the proposed method?
- How does the proposed method handle graphs without clique structures, and what are the implications for its performance in such cases?
- Can the authors elaborate on the design choices behind the two-channel approach and discuss the potential for combining different pooling methods to enhance performance?
- How does the proposed method compare in terms of computational efficiency and performance with other state-of-the-art methods, particularly in large-scale graph classification tasks?
