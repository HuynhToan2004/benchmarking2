 **Summary:**
The paper introduces ODEdit, a novel method for blind face restoration using a generative prior from a pre-trained diffusion model, aiming to restore images without assuming specifics about the degradation. The approach leverages Ordinary Differential Equations (ODE) to invert the diffusion process, enhancing the realism of restored images. The methodology is evaluated against various state-of-the-art blind restoration methods, showcasing its effectiveness and versatility across different degradation scenarios. Despite its strengths in image quality and novelty, the paper has been critiqued for its limited evaluation on only one dataset, which might not fully validate its claims.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel approach using Ordinary Differential Equations (ODE) for blind face restoration, which is a significant advancement in the field.
- The methodology is well-explained, with clear figures and a logical structure that aids in understanding the proposed method.
- The paper demonstrates the effectiveness of the proposed method through a series of experiments, showing improvements over existing methods in terms of image quality and restoration accuracy.
- The method's versatility is highlighted by its ability to handle various degradation scenarios, including severe cases, which is a notable strength.
- The paper is well-written, making it accessible and easy to follow, which is crucial for academic papers.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed comparison with existing methods, particularly in terms of computational efficiency and the specifics of how the proposed method outperforms others.
- The evaluation is limited to the CelebA dataset, which might not fully validate the method's effectiveness across different datasets and real-world scenarios.
- The paper does not provide sufficient evidence to support the claims made about the method's superiority over existing methods, particularly in terms of fidelity and realism.
- There are concerns about the method's stability and the potential for instability in inverting higher resolution images, which could affect the accuracy of the restoration.
- The paper could benefit from more comprehensive comparisons with other state-of-the-art methods, including those that use different datasets or different evaluation metrics.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors provide a more detailed comparison with existing methods, including a side-by-side comparison of results and a discussion on the specific advantages of the proposed method?
- How does the proposed method handle different types of degradation, especially those that significantly alter color gradients?
- Can the authors clarify the stability issues observed in inverting higher resolution images and discuss potential solutions or improvements?
- How does the proposed method compare with other methods that use different datasets or different evaluation metrics?
- Could the authors provide more quantitative results to support the claims made about the method's superiority in terms of fidelity and realism?
- Is there a possibility to extend the method to handle different types of degradation beyond those tested in the current experiments?
