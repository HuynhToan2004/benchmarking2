 **Summary:**
The paper introduces MemWalker, a method that allows a language model (LLM) to navigate through a memory tree structured from a long text document, facilitating efficient reading and question answering. The method involves recursively summarizing text into nodes, enabling the LLM to navigate through the tree to find relevant information. This approach is evaluated on several long context question answering tasks, showing superior performance compared to baselines. However, concerns are raised about the novelty and the method's dependence on the quality of the base LLM. The paper also discusses the scalability and efficiency of the memory tree construction and the need for further fine-tuning to enhance the LLM's reasoning capabilities.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel approach to handle long text using a memory tree structure, which is innovative and potentially applicable to various tasks beyond question answering.
- The method is well-explained, with clear and comprehensive explanations of the memory tree construction and navigation processes.
- The paper is well-written, making it easy to understand, and includes detailed examples and visualizations that aid in comprehension.
- The experimental results show that the proposed method outperforms baselines, demonstrating its effectiveness.
- The paper provides a detailed analysis of the method's components, such as reasoning justification, working memory, and stray paths, which helps in understanding the method's strengths and limitations.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper does not sufficiently compare the proposed method with other existing methods for handling long text, such as those using hierarchical summarization or other tree-based approaches.
- The method's dependence on the quality of the base LLM is not adequately addressed, raising concerns about its applicability with weaker or less capable models.
- The method's scalability and efficiency, particularly in constructing the memory tree for extremely long texts, are not thoroughly discussed.
- The paper lacks a detailed discussion on the potential negative societal impacts of the proposed method.
- The method's reliance on the reasoning capabilities of the LLM might limit its applicability to smaller or less capable models.
- The paper does not include a comparison with other recent methods like LlamaIndex, which could provide a more comprehensive evaluation of the proposed method.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors provide a comparison of the proposed method with other tree-based approaches, such as those using hierarchical summarization or other tree structures?
- How does the method perform with weaker or less capable LLMs, and what adjustments might be necessary to ensure its effectiveness?
- Can the authors discuss the scalability and efficiency of the memory tree construction process, particularly in handling extremely long texts?
- How does the method handle the potential negative societal impacts, and what steps are being taken to mitigate these risks?
- Could the authors elaborate on the method's reliance on the reasoning capabilities of the LLM and discuss potential strategies for adapting the method to smaller or less capable models?
