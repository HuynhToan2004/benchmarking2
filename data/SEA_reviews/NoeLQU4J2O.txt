 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel multiplication-free neural network architecture named "Soon Filter," designed to address the gradient bottleneck in the ULEEN architecture. The authors argue that the ULEEN's continuous relaxation of Bloom filters during training hinders learning, and propose a summation operation to alleviate this issue. The Soon Filter, a simplified version of the Bloom Filter, is shown to enhance gradient back-propagation efficiency, leading to improved accuracy and reduced model sizes in various datasets, including MNIST, CIFAR-10, and UCI datasets. The paper also includes theoretical analysis and empirical evidence to support the claims, although it lacks detailed hardware implementation and evaluation.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel and interesting approach to alleviate the gradient bottleneck issue in the ULEEN architecture, proposing a summation operation that allows for more effective gradient back-propagation.
- The proposed method is supported by both theoretical analysis and empirical evidence, showing its effectiveness in improving the performance of ULEEN and other baseline methods.
- The paper is well-written, clear, and easy to follow, making it accessible to readers.
- The methodology is straightforward and the paper provides a detailed analysis of the gradient bottleneck problem, which is significant in the field of neural network architectures.
- The paper demonstrates a significant reduction in model size (up to 4x) compared to baseline methods while maintaining similar accuracy, which is a notable achievement.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks detailed hardware implementation and evaluation, which is crucial for understanding the practical implications and performance of the proposed method.
- The comparison with other multiplication-free architectures, such as DeepShift, is missing, which could have provided a more comprehensive evaluation of the proposed method.
- The paper does not discuss the impact of the proposed method on energy efficiency, which is a critical aspect for edge inference applications.
- The paper does not provide a detailed analysis of the computational overhead introduced by the proposed method, which could affect the practical applicability of the proposed method.
- The paper does not include results on hardware platforms, which is essential for evaluating the practical performance of the proposed method in real-world scenarios.
- The paper does not compare the proposed method with other state-of-the-art methods in terms of accuracy, model size, and energy efficiency, which could have provided a more comprehensive evaluation of the proposed method.
