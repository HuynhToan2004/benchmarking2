 **Summary:**
The paper introduces BioBRIDGE, a novel parameter-efficient learning framework designed to bridge independently trained unimodal biomedical foundation models (FMs) into a multimodal system. This framework leverages knowledge graphs (KGs) to learn transformations between unimodal FMs, enabling multimodal behavior without fine-tuning the underlying unimodal FMs. BioBRIDGE demonstrates superior performance in cross-modal retrieval tasks, outperforming existing KG embedding methods by a significant margin. It also showcases its ability to generalize to unseen modalities or relations, indicating its potential for broader applications in the biomedical domain. The paper provides empirical evidence of BioBRIDGE's effectiveness and out-of-domain generalization capabilities, suggesting its utility as a general-purpose retriever for biomedical multimodal question answering and as a tool for enhancing the guided generation of novel drugs.
