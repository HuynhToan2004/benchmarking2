 **Summary:**
The paper introduces CMP-CV, a novel cross-validation framework designed to benchmark and compare drug response prediction models using different molecular representations. This framework employs automated model training and evaluation using standardized datasets and performance metrics, aiming to provide a comprehensive comparison of various deep learning models in drug response prediction. The study examines the impact of different molecular representations on model performance, highlighting the importance of domain-based model comparisons. The framework is demonstrated to be effective in identifying the strengths and weaknesses of different models across different regions of descriptor space, which is crucial for selecting the most suitable model for specific applications. However, the paper is criticized for its limited scope in terms of the number of models and datasets used, and the lack of novelty in its methodology.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel cross-validation framework, CMP-CV, which facilitates the comparison of different drug response prediction models using various molecular representations.
- The study provides a comprehensive analysis of different molecular representations and their impact on model performance, offering valuable insights into the strengths and weaknesses of different models across different regions of descriptor space.
- The paper is well-structured, clear, and easy to follow, making it accessible to a broad audience.
- The proposed CMP-CV framework is robust and provides a comprehensive comparison of different models, which is crucial for selecting the most suitable model for specific applications.
- The analysis of the impact of molecular representations on the performance of deep learning models is significant, as it helps in understanding the effectiveness of different drug representations in predicting drug responses.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks novelty in its methodology as it primarily benchmarks existing models without introducing significant new methods or techniques.
- The dataset used in the study is relatively small, with only 720 cell lines, which may not be representative enough for a comprehensive analysis.
- The paper does not include a significant number of models for comparison, which limits the scope of the study and the generalizability of the findings.
- The analysis does not provide a comprehensive comparison of different models across all molecular descriptor domains, which could be crucial for understanding the relative strengths and weaknesses of different models.
- The paper does not provide a detailed analysis of the computational complexity of the CMP-CV framework, which is crucial for understanding its scalability and applicability in large-scale drug discovery applications.
- The paper lacks a thorough discussion on the limitations of the proposed framework and the assumptions made in the analysis, which could affect the credibility and applicability of the findings.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors provide a more detailed analysis of the computational complexity of the CMP-CV framework, including the time and resource requirements for training and evaluating different models?
- How does the CMP-CV framework handle the variation in model performance across different molecular descriptor domains, and what insights can it provide into the relative strengths and weaknesses of different models?
- Can the authors discuss the potential impact of the framework on the development of more effective strategies for drug response prediction and optimization?
- Could the authors provide a more detailed analysis of the limitations of the proposed framework, including the assumptions made in the analysis and their potential impact on the findings?
- Can the authors provide a more detailed comparison of the CMP-CV framework with existing benchmarking tools, and discuss the unique contributions of the CMP-CV framework in terms of its methodology and results?
- Could the authors provide a more detailed discussion on the selection of molecular descriptors used in the study, including their relevance and significance in drug discovery and design?
