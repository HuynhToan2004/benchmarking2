 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel approach to addressing the over-squashing problem in message-passing neural networks (MPNNs) by integrating structured state spaces (SSS) from sequence learning. This approach aims to achieve linear sensitivity between nodes, thereby mitigating the exponential sensitivity issues in traditional MPNNs. The methodology involves the use of a graph convolution based on SSS, which is integrated into a Transformer-like architecture. The paper presents a comprehensive theoretical analysis and experimental results demonstrating the effectiveness of the proposed method on both synthetic and real-world datasets. The approach is compared with various baselines and state-of-the-art models, showing promising results in long-range interaction tasks.

**Strengths:**
- The paper presents a novel approach by integrating structured state spaces (SSS) from sequence learning to address the over-squashing problem in message-passing neural networks (MPNNs), which is a significant contribution to the field.
- The authors provide a comprehensive theoretical analysis and experimental results that demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed method on both synthetic and real-world datasets, showcasing its potential for long-range interaction tasks.
- The paper is well-written, with clear explanations and a structured layout that makes it easy to follow, enhancing its accessibility and readability.
- The experimental results are convincing, and the ablation study effectively demonstrates the importance of the S4G-Conv component.
- The paper introduces a novel graph convolution based on the state-space model, which could be a significant contribution to the field if it can be generalized to other types of graphs.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed comparison with other methods that also address the over-squashing problem, such as the GGNN, which could provide a better understanding of the proposed method's advantages and limitations.
- The paper does not adequately address the scalability of the proposed method, particularly in terms of its computational complexity and its applicability to large-scale graphs.
- The paper could benefit from a more detailed discussion on the limitations of the proposed method, including potential negative societal impacts and ethical considerations.
- The paper does not sufficiently discuss the expressivity of the proposed method, which is crucial for understanding its capabilities and potential limitations.
- The paper's reliance on the HiPPO framework may limit its adaptability to different types of graphs, particularly those with complex structures.
- The paper's experimental setup and the selection of datasets could be improved to better reflect real-world scenarios and to include more diverse and complex graphs.
