 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel method for converting neural networks into Gaussian processes (GPs) by utilizing a dual parameterization. This approach enables the representation of neural networks in function space, which is particularly beneficial for scaling to large datasets and incorporating new data without retraining. The method, termed Sparse Function-space Representation (SFR), employs sparse GPs to achieve computational efficiency and leverages the Laplace approximation for dual parameter estimation. The paper provides extensive experimental results across various datasets, demonstrating the method's effectiveness in continual learning, reinforcement learning, and uncertainty quantification. However, the paper's presentation and clarity could be improved, with some reviewers pointing out issues in notation and explanation.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-motivated, clearly written, and provides a comprehensive overview of the background and related work, making it accessible to a broad audience.
- The proposed method is innovative, particularly in its application of dual parameterization to convert neural networks into Gaussian processes, which is a novel approach.
- The paper presents a solid experimental evaluation, demonstrating the effectiveness of the proposed method across various datasets and tasks, including continual learning, reinforcement learning, and uncertainty quantification.
- The methodology is supported by extensive ablation studies, which provide valuable insights into the contributions of different components of the proposed method.
- The paper is well-organized, making it easy to follow, and the use of dual parameterization for sparse GPs is highlighted as a significant contribution.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper suffers from several issues with notation and clarity, particularly in the definitions and explanations of key concepts such as the dual parameters and the role of the Hessian in the Laplace approximation.
- There are inconsistencies and potential errors in the experimental results and methodologies described, such as the discrepancies in the results between different datasets and the lack of clarity in the experimental setups.
- The paper does not adequately address the limitations of the proposed method, which could be crucial for understanding its applicability and potential drawbacks.
- The paper could benefit from a more detailed discussion on the computational complexity and scalability of the proposed method, particularly in large-scale applications.
- The presentation of the paper could be improved, with suggestions for better organization, clearer figures, and more concise explanations of the methodologies and results.

**Questions:**
- Could you clarify the role and calculation of the Hessian in the Laplace approximation, particularly in relation to the dual parameters?
- How does the proposed method compare to other existing methods for converting neural networks into Gaussian processes, and what are the advantages of using dual parameterization?
- Can you provide more details on the experimental setup, such as the specifics of the neural network architectures and the datasets used?
- How does the method perform when using different types of neural networks, such as convolutional neural networks or recurrent neural networks?
- Could you elaborate on the computational complexity and scalability of the proposed method, particularly in large-scale applications?
- How does the method handle the issue of the model becoming outdated when new data is incorporated, and what are the implications for the accuracy and reliability of the predictions?
