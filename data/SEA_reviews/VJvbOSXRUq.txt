 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a benchmarking study aimed at evaluating the performance of perturbation-based explainers in Graph Neural Networks (GNNs). It covers both factual and counterfactual reasoning, employing a range of metrics such as sufficiency, size, stability, and necessity. The study involves a comprehensive evaluation of various GNN explainers using both synthetic and real-world datasets, highlighting the need for more robust explainers that can handle noise and provide stable, feasible explanations. The paper also addresses the issue of counterfactual explainers not adhering to domain constraints, which is a significant limitation in real-world applications. Despite its thorough evaluation and novel insights, the paper is criticized for its limited novelty and the lack of significant advancement over existing methods.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written and easy to follow, with a clear and structured presentation that facilitates understanding.
- The introduction of a benchmarking study on perturbation-based explainers for Graph Neural Networks (GNNs) is novel and valuable, as it systematically evaluates and compares different explainability techniques.
- The paper provides a comprehensive evaluation encompassing counterfactual explainers, which is the first of its kind.
- The findings of the study are insightful and reveal stability to noise and variational factors, as well as the generation of feasible counterfactual recourses, which are critical for practical applications.
- The authors have made significant efforts to ensure the reproducibility of their work by providing a codebase that is publicly accessible.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks significant novelty as it primarily focuses on evaluating existing methods without proposing new explainers or significant advancements in the field.
- The evaluation of the methods is limited to a small set of datasets, which may not fully represent the diversity and complexity of real-world scenarios.
- The paper does not provide sufficient details on the experimental setup, such as the specifics of the datasets used and the conditions under which the experiments were conducted.
- There is a lack of discussion on the limitations of the study and potential negative societal impacts, which are crucial for understanding the broader implications of the research.
- The paper's conclusions are not sufficiently supported by quantitative data, making it difficult to assess the validity of the claims made.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors clarify how the sufficiency metric is calculated and whether it is normalized across different datasets?
- How does the paper address the issue of domain-specific constraints in counterfactual explanations, particularly in scenarios where the explanations need to adhere to specific physical or chemical laws?
- Could the authors provide more detailed explanations on the experimental setup, including the specifics of the datasets used and the conditions under which the experiments were conducted?
- How does the paper ensure the reproducibility of the results, especially in terms of the methodology and the datasets used?
- Could the authors discuss the limitations of their study and potential negative societal impacts, especially in the context of GNN explainers?
