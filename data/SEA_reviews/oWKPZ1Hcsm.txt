 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel approach to offline reinforcement learning (RL) by pre-training both the actor and critic using supervised learning on Monte-Carlo returns. This method aims to improve the efficiency and stability of off-policy RL algorithms by initializing the critic with consistent values, which can be used for subsequent temporal difference learning. The approach is tested across various environments, including MuJoCo and Adroit, demonstrating improvements in performance and stability. The methodology involves a two-step process: first, pre-training the actor and critic using supervised learning, followed by off-policy RL updates. The paper also discusses the challenges of policy collapse and the importance of regularizing both the actor and critic to prevent out-of-distribution (OOD) behavior.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, clear, and easy to follow, making complex concepts accessible to a broad audience.
- The proposed method is simple, intuitive, and effective, showing improvements in performance and stability across various environments.
- The method's simplicity and the use of Monte-Carlo returns as initializations for the critic network are innovative and make sense, enhancing the learning process.
- The paper includes a comprehensive set of experiments, demonstrating the method's effectiveness in both simple and complex environments, and shows significant improvements over baseline methods.
- The paper is well-motivated, with a clear focus on improving the efficiency and stability of off-policy RL algorithms, and provides a thorough literature review, highlighting the novelty of the approach.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed discussion on the potential negative societal impacts of the proposed method, which could be a significant oversight.
- The experimental results are not sufficiently robust, with significant variability in performance across different runs, which could undermine the reliability of the findings.
- The paper does not adequately address the challenges of policy collapse and the importance of regularizing both the actor and critic to prevent OOD behavior, which are critical aspects of offline RL.
- The method's reliance on pre-training for all environments, including those with abundant data, might not be justified, and the paper could benefit from a more nuanced approach that considers the data availability and complexity of different environments.
- The paper does not sufficiently compare its method with existing pre-training methods in offline RL, which could provide a more robust evaluation of the proposed approach.

**Questions:**
- How does the proposed method compare with other pre-training methods in offline RL, such as those mentioned in the related work section?
- Can the authors provide more details on the hyperparameters used in the experiments, particularly how they were chosen and their impact on the results?
- What are the specific contributions of the pre-training phase to the final performance, and how does this compare with the performance achieved without pre-training?
- How does the method handle the potential issue of policy collapse, and what measures are in place to prevent this?
- Could the authors clarify the role of the pre-training phase in addressing the bias-variance tradeoff and optimism-pessimism tradeoff, as mentioned in the paper?
- In the context of the experiments, how does the method perform in environments with different data availability and complexity, and what implications does this have for the generalizability of the findings?
