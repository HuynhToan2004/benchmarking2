 **Summary:**
The paper introduces MATT, a novel defense mechanism against query-based attacks in Machine Learning as a Service (MLaaS) systems. MATT employs a local implicit function to purify images, enhancing the robustness of classifiers without compromising their accuracy. The methodology involves a pool of purification models, each applied to random image patches, which are then combined to maintain a fixed inference cost. Theoretical analysis and extensive experiments on CIFAR-10 and ImageNet demonstrate MATT's effectiveness in defending against various adversarial attacks. Despite its strengths, the paper is criticized for its limited novelty and insufficient comparison with state-of-the-art methods.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel defense mechanism, MATT, which employs a local implicit function to purify images, enhancing the robustness of classifiers without compromising their accuracy.
- The methodology of MATT is theoretically analyzed, and the effectiveness of the proposed purifier-based defense mechanism is validated through extensive experiments on CIFAR-10 and ImageNet.
- The paper is well-written, clear, and easy to follow, making it accessible to a broad audience.
- The authors provide a comprehensive comparison with state-of-the-art methods, highlighting the effectiveness of MATT in defending against various adversarial attacks.
- The use of random patch-wise purification and the pool of purifiers to increase diversity and maintain a moderate inference time is a significant contribution to the field.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed comparison with state-of-the-art methods, particularly in terms of computational efficiency and robustness under different attack scenarios.
- There is a need for more empirical evidence and theoretical analysis to support the claims made about the effectiveness of MATT in defending against query-based attacks.
- The paper could benefit from a more comprehensive literature review and a clearer explanation of the theoretical assumptions and their implications.
- The novelty of the proposed method is limited as it primarily combines existing techniques without significant innovation.
- The paper does not adequately address the scalability and generalizability of MATT across different types of adversarial attacks and datasets.
- There is a lack of discussion on the limitations of MATT, which could affect its practical applicability in real-world scenarios.
