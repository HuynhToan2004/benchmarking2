 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel supervision-free unlearning method called Label-Agnostic Forgetting (LAF), which aims to remove information related to forgotten data at the representation level without requiring labels. This method leverages variational inference and contrastive learning to approximate the distribution of representations for the remaining data and adjust the representation extractor accordingly. The LAF method is tested on various datasets, demonstrating comparable performance to supervised unlearning methods and even outperforming them in some scenarios. However, the method's effectiveness is limited by its reliance on the accuracy of the representation extractor and the similarity between the distributions of remaining and forgotten data.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel approach to unlearning that does not require labels, which is a significant advancement in the field.
- The method is technically sound, with a clear motivation and a well-structured presentation that is easy to follow.
- The experiments are thorough, providing a detailed analysis of the method's performance across various datasets and tasks.
- The paper addresses an important problem in machine learning, particularly relevant in the context of data privacy and unlearning.
- The use of variational inference and contrastive learning to approximate the distribution of representations for the remaining data is innovative and could lead to further research in unlearning at the representation level.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper does not sufficiently address the limitations of the method, particularly its reliance on the accuracy of the representation extractor and the similarity between the distributions of remaining and forgotten data.
- The method's performance is not consistently superior to existing methods, and it sometimes performs worse, particularly in scenarios where the method's assumptions are not met.
- The paper lacks a detailed discussion on the computational complexity and efficiency of the method, which is crucial for practical applications.
- The method's assumptions, such as the similarity between the distributions of remaining and forgotten data, are not thoroughly validated, which could limit the generalizability of the findings.
- There are several typographical errors and unclear descriptions in the paper, which could confuse readers and detract from the paper's credibility.

**Questions:**
- Could you provide more details on the computational complexity and efficiency of the method, particularly in terms of memory and time requirements?
- How does the method perform when the assumptions of similarity between the distributions of remaining and forgotten data are not met?
- Can you clarify the methodology used in the experiments, particularly the selection and handling of the "forgetting" data?
- How does the method ensure that the representation extractor accurately estimates the distribution of representations for the remaining data?
- Can you elaborate on the choice of the VAE architecture and the rationale behind the use of two VAEs for different tasks?
- In the context of the paper's claims about the method's effectiveness, can you provide more detailed evidence or comparisons to substantiate these claims?
