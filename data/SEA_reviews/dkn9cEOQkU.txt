 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel approach to handling fragmentary interaction control problems in reinforcement learning (RL) by utilizing a Multi-step Action Representation (MARS) method. MARS learns a compact and decodable latent space for multi-step action sequences, which is crucial for handling fragmentary interactions caused by network delays or packet losses. This method, which is applicable to various RL algorithms, is evaluated on both simulated and real-world tasks, demonstrating improved performance over existing methods. The paper also discusses the challenges of fragmentary interaction and provides a theoretical framework for understanding these issues.

**Strengths:**
- The paper addresses a significant and relevant problem in the field of reinforcement learning, specifically focusing on fragmentary interaction control problems, which is a critical issue in real-world applications.
- The proposed method, MARS, is innovative and well-motivated, providing a novel approach to handling fragmentary interaction in RL environments.
- The paper is well-written, clear, and easy to follow, making it accessible to a broad audience.
- The experimental results are robust and demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed method, showing that it outperforms existing baselines in both simulated and real-world tasks.
- The paper provides a theoretical framework for understanding the fragmentary interaction problem, which is a significant contribution to the field.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper could benefit from a more detailed discussion on the limitations of the proposed method, particularly in terms of its applicability to different types of RL algorithms and its scalability to more complex environments.
- There is a lack of clarity in some sections of the paper, particularly in the methodology and experimental setup, which could confuse readers and hinder reproducibility.
- The paper could provide more detailed explanations and justifications for certain design choices, such as the use of specific RL algorithms and the methodology for handling fragmentary interactions.
- The paper could benefit from a more comprehensive literature review and comparison with existing methods to better position its contributions within the current research landscape.
- There are some minor typographical and formatting errors that need to be corrected for clarity and professionalism.

**Questions:**

- How does the proposed method perform in environments with more complex interactions or longer intervals between actions?
- Could the authors provide more details on the training procedure for the VAE and the environmental dynamics prediction, particularly in how these components are integrated into the overall method?
- How does the method handle different types of fragmentary interactions, such as those caused by network delays versus packet losses?
- Could the authors elaborate on the role and effectiveness of the action transition scale in the method, particularly in terms of its impact on the performance and generalization capabilities of the model?
- How does the method compare to other existing methods in terms of computational efficiency and scalability, especially in more complex or real-world applications?
