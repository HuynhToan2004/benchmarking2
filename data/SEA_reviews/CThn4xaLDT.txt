 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel equivariant operation, Scalar Interaction, which aims to reduce computational complexity in equivariant graph neural networks (GNNs) by replacing tensor product operations with scalar interactions. This operation, termed REBIF, decomposes equivariant operations into invariant functions, thereby simplifying the computational process. The proposed method is evaluated on the QM9 dataset, showing competitive performance and reduced computational complexity compared to traditional methods. However, concerns are raised about the generalizability of the method beyond the specific datasets used and the scalability of the method to higher-order tensors.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel approach to reducing computational complexity in equivariant graph neural networks (GNNs) by replacing tensor product operations with scalar interactions, which is a significant contribution to the field.
- The proposed method, SINet, shows competitive performance on the QM9 dataset, demonstrating its effectiveness.
- The paper provides a detailed analysis of the expressive power of the proposed method and its limitations, which is crucial for understanding its applicability and potential improvements.
- The method's simplicity and potential for reducing computational complexity are highlighted, making it a potentially impactful contribution to the field.
- The paper is well-written, making the content accessible and understandable, which is essential for disseminating the findings to a broader audience.

**Weaknesses:**
