 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel approach to enhance the scalability of spectral Graph Neural Networks (GNNs) by employing Laplacian sparsification. This method aims to approximate the equivalent propagation matrix of Laplacian filters, enabling the integration of spectral GNNs with existing scalable techniques. Theoretical proofs demonstrate that the proposed model generates Laplacian sparsifiers with high probability and low approximation errors. Extensive experiments across various datasets validate the method's effectiveness, showing comparable or superior performance compared to base models. The approach is particularly beneficial for large-scale datasets and heterophilous graphs, where traditional methods often struggle.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel approach to enhance the scalability of spectral Graph Neural Networks (GNNs) by employing Laplacian sparsification, which is a first in this domain.
- The theoretical proofs provided demonstrate that the proposed model generates Laplacian sparsifiers with high probability and low approximation errors, ensuring the quality and reliability of the generated sparsifiers.
- The methodology is extensively tested across various datasets, showcasing stable improvements compared to corresponding base models, indicating its practical performance.
- The paper is well-written, making it easy to follow, and provides clear mathematical proofs and experimental results that validate the effectiveness of the proposed method.
- The approach is compatible with existing scalable techniques, making it a valuable addition to the field of GNNs.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper does not clearly distinguish between the proposed method and existing methods like SGC, which may lead to confusion about the novelty of the approach.
- There is a lack of discussion on the computational cost and scalability of the proposed method, which is crucial for understanding its practical implications.
- The paper could benefit from a more detailed discussion on the limitations of the proposed method, especially in scenarios where it may not perform optimally or where it may not be applicable.
- The paper does not provide a detailed analysis of the training time and memory usage of the proposed method, which are critical for assessing its practical efficiency.
- There is a need for a more comprehensive discussion on the impact of the proposed method on the performance of GNNs, particularly in terms of the quality of node embeddings and the overall model performance.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors clarify the distinction between the proposed method and existing methods like SGC, particularly in terms of novelty and approach?
- How does the computational cost and scalability of the proposed method compare to other methods, especially in large-scale applications?
- Could the authors provide a detailed analysis of the training time and memory usage of the proposed method, and compare it to other methods to assess its practical efficiency?
- How does the proposed method affect the quality of node embeddings and the overall performance of GNNs?
- Can the authors discuss the limitations of the proposed method and provide scenarios where it may not perform optimally or where it may not be applicable?
- Could the authors provide a detailed discussion on the impact of the proposed method on the scalability of GNNs, particularly in terms of handling large-scale graphs and high-dimensional input features?
