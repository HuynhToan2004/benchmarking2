 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel method, Video Adapter, which leverages a large pretrained video diffusion model as a probabilistic prior to adapt a black-box text-to-video model to specific domains without access to the pretrained model's weights. This approach allows for the generation of high-quality domain-specific videos using a small model that captures task-specific information, thus improving the model's performance in various downstream tasks. The methodology involves a probabilistic approach where the pretrained model's score function acts as a prior, guiding the generation of a task-specific small video model. The paper presents several experiments demonstrating the effectiveness of this approach in generating high-quality videos across different domains, including animation, robotics, and sim-to-real transfer.

**Strengths:**
- The proposed method is novel and effective in adapting a black-box pretrained text-to-video model to a variety of downstream domains without access to the pretrained model's weights.
- The paper is well-written, making it easy to follow, and includes detailed explanations of the methodology and experiments.
- The experiments demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed method in generating high-quality domain-specific videos, showcasing its potential in various applications such as robotics, animation, and sim-to-real transfer.
- The paper introduces a novel probabilistic approach using the score function of a large pretrained video diffusion model as a prior, which guides the generation of a task-specific small video model, enhancing the model's performance in specific domains.
- The methodology is validated through experiments that showcase the generation of high-quality domain-specific videos, demonstrating the effectiveness of the proposed approach.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper does not sufficiently discuss the limitations of the proposed method, particularly the requirement for training a small domain-specific model which might not be completely training-free.
- There is a lack of comparison with other methods such as DreamBooth, which also uses a small model to adapt a pretrained model, and ControlNet, which also uses a probabilistic approach.
- The paper does not provide sufficient details on the computational cost and training time of the proposed method, which could be crucial for practical applications.
- The paper does not discuss how the method could be adapted for different types of videos, such as those with complex content or those requiring specific styles.
- The paper does not provide a detailed analysis of the quality of the generated videos, particularly in terms of temporal consistency and object permanence, which are crucial for practical applications.
- The paper lacks a detailed discussion on the limitations of the proposed method, particularly the requirement for diffusion scores from pretrained black-box models, which might not be readily available for all applications.

**Questions:**
- How does the proposed method compare to other methods like DreamBooth and ControlNet in terms of effectiveness and computational efficiency?
- Can the proposed method be adapted for different types of videos, such as those with complex content or those requiring specific styles?
- How does the proposed method handle the generation of videos with different styles or contents, especially when the training data is limited?
- What are the specific limitations of the proposed method, and how do these affect the practical applicability of the method?
- Could you provide more details on the computational cost and training time of the proposed method, and how these compare to other methods in the field?
- How does the proposed method ensure the quality of the generated videos, particularly in terms of temporal consistency and object permanence?
