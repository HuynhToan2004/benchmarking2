 **Summary:**
The paper introduces an active learning framework for image segmentation, focusing on reducing annotation efforts by posing binary queries to human annotators. This approach allows for the selection of informative image-class pairs, which are then used to train a DeepLabv3+ network. The methodology is evaluated on three datasets, showing that it can reduce annotation time significantly compared to traditional pixel-level annotation. However, the paper is criticized for its limited novelty, as similar approaches have been explored in the past, and for its experimental setup, which is seen as insufficiently robust to support the claims made. The paper's contribution is viewed as marginal, and the method's performance is not convincingly superior to existing methods.

**Strengths:**
- The paper addresses the significant issue of reducing annotation efforts in image segmentation, which is a crucial aspect of active learning.
- The method proposed is straightforward and easy to implement, making it accessible for practical applications.
- The paper is well-organized and easy to follow, with a clear presentation of the methodology and results.
- The paper demonstrates the potential of binary-level annotations to save annotation time, which is a valuable contribution to the field.
- The experimental results show that the proposed method can achieve comparable performance to state-of-the-art methods while reducing annotation time.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a thorough review of related works, particularly in the area of active learning for image segmentation, which limits the understanding of the novelty and contribution of the proposed method.
- The experimental setup is not robust enough to support the claims made, with insufficient comparisons to state-of-the-art methods and a lack of discussion on the impact of hyperparameters.
- The novelty of the proposed method is questionable, as similar approaches have been explored in the past, such as binary-level annotations in image retrieval and segmentation.
- The paper does not adequately address the complexity introduced by the linear programming (LP) formulation, which could affect the practical applicability of the method.
- The method's performance is not convincingly superior to existing methods, with only marginal improvements reported.
- The paper does not sufficiently discuss the limitations of the proposed method, such as its scalability and applicability to different datasets.

**Questions:**
- How does the proposed method compare to state-of-the-art methods in terms of performance and annotation time?
- Can the authors provide a more detailed comparison of the proposed method with existing methods, including a discussion on the impact of hyperparameters?
- How does the method perform on other datasets, such as ADE20k, which are not included in the experiments?
- Could the authors clarify the novelty of the proposed method, especially in relation to existing methods that use binary-level annotations in image segmentation?
- What are the potential applications of the proposed method in real-world scenarios, and how does it address the challenges of active learning in image segmentation?
