 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel multimodal meta-learning framework, Multimodal Iterative Adaptation (MIA), which enhances convergence and performance in gradient-based meta-learning of implicit neural representations (INRs) by leveraging multimodal signals. The framework incorporates State Fusion Transformers (SFTs) to aggregate and fuse state representations from unimodal learners, facilitating cross-modal interactions and improving generalization and memorization. The experiments demonstrate that MIA significantly outperforms unimodal baselines across various multimodal signals, ranging from 1D synthetic functions to real-world vision, climate, and audiovisual data.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel optimization-based meta-learning framework, Multimodal Iterative Adaptation (MIA), which fosters continuous interaction among independent unimodal INR learners, enabling them to capture cross-modal relationships and refine their understanding of signals through iterative optimization steps.
- The paper is well-written and easy to follow, with clear explanations of the proposed methods and their applications.
- The experiments conducted are thorough and demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed method, particularly in multimodal settings.
- The paper presents a novel approach to meta-learning, specifically focusing on multimodal signals, which is an interesting and significant area of research.
- The proposed method shows promising results in improving the modeling capabilities of unimodal meta-learners, achieving significant enhancements in generalization and memorization performances over unimodal baselines.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper could benefit from more detailed descriptions and explanations of the State Fusion Transformers (SFTs) and the specifics of how they are trained and used within the MIA framework.
- The experiments are somewhat limited in scope, focusing primarily on a single dataset and not sufficiently comparing the proposed method with other multimodal meta-learning approaches.
- There is a lack of clarity regarding the definition and role of the State Fusion Transformers (SFTs) in the context of meta-learning.
- The paper does not adequately address the issue of data scarcity, which is a significant challenge in real-world applications.
- The paper could benefit from more detailed discussions on the limitations of the proposed method and potential negative societal impacts.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors provide more detailed explanations of the State Fusion Transformers (SFTs) and their specific training and usage within the MIA framework?
- How does the proposed method perform when applied to other multimodal meta-learning tasks, and what are the specific advantages it offers over other methods in these scenarios?
- How does the paper address the issue of data scarcity, and what are the implications of this limitation on the practical applicability of the proposed method?
- Could the authors elaborate on the potential negative societal impacts of the proposed method and discuss how these might be mitigated or addressed?
- In the context of multimodal meta-learning, how does the proposed method compare with other multimodal meta-learning approaches, particularly in terms of generalization and memorization performances?
