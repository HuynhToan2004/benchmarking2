 **Summary:**
The paper explores a novel multi-agent reinforcement learning (MARL) setting for inventory management, introducing the Whittle Index with Multiple Actions and State constraint (WIMS). This approach extends the Whittle Index to accommodate multiple discrete actions and state constraints, which is critical for managing inventory levels across various stock-keeping units (SKUs). The authors propose a deep MARL algorithm, WIMSN, to address these challenges, demonstrating its effectiveness through extensive experiments involving up to 2307 SKUs. The paper highlights the algorithm's ability to outperform both operation-research-based methods and baseline MARL algorithms, showcasing its practical applicability and theoretical soundness.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel extension of the Whittle Index to a new constrained MARL setting, which is well-explained and supported by a clear motivation.
- The proposed WIMSN algorithm is innovative, combining WIMS with neural networks to solve real inventory management problems efficiently.
- The experiments conducted on a large-scale inventory management problem with 2307 SKUs demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed WIMSN algorithm.
- The paper is well-written, making it accessible and easy to follow, with clear figures and detailed explanations that aid in understanding the methodologies and results.
- The practical application of the proposed method in real-world inventory management scenarios is highlighted, showcasing its potential for scalability and adaptability.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a clear discussion on the limitations of the proposed approach, particularly in terms of its scalability and applicability to different types of inventory management problems.
- The experimental results, while promising, do not include a detailed comparison with other state-of-the-art MARL algorithms, which could have provided a more comprehensive evaluation of the proposed method.
- There is a lack of discussion on the societal impact of the proposed method, which is crucial for understanding the broader implications of the research.
- The paper does not adequately discuss the computational complexity of the proposed method, which is a significant concern for practical applications.
- The paper does not provide a detailed discussion on the scalability of the proposed method, especially in terms of its ability to handle a large number of SKUs.
- There is a need for more comprehensive comparisons with other state-of-the-art MARL algorithms, including a detailed analysis of the performance of WIMSN in different scenarios.

**Questions:**
- How does the proposed method perform in scenarios where the number of SKUs is significantly higher than 2307?
- Can the authors provide a more detailed comparison with other state-of-the-art MARL algorithms, including a discussion on the computational complexity and scalability of WIMSN?
- What are the specific advantages of WIMSN over other MARL algorithms, especially in terms of performance and efficiency?
- How does the proposed method handle scenarios where the inventory constraints are not fixed but rather dynamic?
- Can the authors provide more details on the experimental setup, including the specifics of the inventory management problem and the conditions under which the experiments were conducted?
- What are the potential societal impacts of the proposed method, and how does the paper address these concerns?
