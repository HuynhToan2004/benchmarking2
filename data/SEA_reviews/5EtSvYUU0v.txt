 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a new framework, the Neural Dynamical Kernel (NDK), which unifies the Neural Tangent Kernel (NTK) and Neural Network Gaussian Process (NNGP) kernels. The NDK is derived from a theoretical perspective, integrating elements of the NTK and NNGP, and is shown to describe the dynamics of infinitely wide neural networks during learning. The authors explore how the NDK characterizes the learning process across different time scales, highlighting the role of initialization, regularization, and network depth. The paper also addresses phenomena such as early stopping and representational drift. Despite its theoretical strength, the paper has been critiqued for its presentation clarity and the practical applicability of its findings, particularly in the context of finite-width networks and real-world datasets.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel theoretical framework for understanding the learning dynamics of neural networks in the infinite width limit, which is both original and significant.
- The authors provide a clear and comprehensive review of the literature, highlighting the relevance and importance of their research.
- The paper is well-written, with a clear and logical structure, and the inclusion of supplementary material enhances the comprehensibility of the main text.
- The theoretical results are robust and supported by extensive numerical experiments, which validate the theoretical claims.
- The paper offers a unified perspective on the dynamics of neural networks, integrating elements of the Neural Tangent Kernel (NTK) and Neural Network Gaussian Process (NNGP) kernels, which is a significant advancement in the field.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks clarity in some sections, particularly in the explanation of the derivation of the NDK kernel and the relationship between different kernel functions.
- The practical applicability of the theoretical results is limited, as the paper focuses primarily on infinite-width networks, which may not generalize well to finite-width networks.
- The paper does not sufficiently discuss the implications of its findings for real-world datasets or practical applications, which could limit the impact of the research.
- There are inconsistencies and potential errors in the notation and equations used, which could confuse readers and detract from the paper's clarity.
- The paper could benefit from a more detailed discussion on the practical relevance and implications of the NDK kernel, particularly in terms of its computational complexity and scalability.

**Questions:**
