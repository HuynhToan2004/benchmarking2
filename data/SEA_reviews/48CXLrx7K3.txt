 **Summary:**
The paper investigates the potential for unintentional information leakage from low-dimensional facial portrait representations, specifically through the reconstruction of images from 40- or 32-element feature vectors. It utilizes a pre-trained StyleGAN and a novel loss function that compares perceptual similarity in the FaceNet embedding space to enhance the reconstruction quality. The methodology involves a decoder network that translates feature vectors into generator inputs, which are then used to generate images from the StyleGAN. The paper also discusses the potential risks of privacy breaches and proposes a method to mitigate these risks by ensuring the generated images adhere to specific distributions. However, the paper is criticized for its limited scope, focusing primarily on facial portrait representations, and for not adequately addressing privacy concerns.

**Strengths:**
- The paper addresses an important and timely issue of privacy concerns in machine learning, particularly in the context of facial portrait representations.
- The methodology, including the use of a pre-trained StyleGAN and a novel loss function, is innovative and contributes to the field by improving the quality of the reconstruction.
- The paper is well-written, clearly explaining the methodology and results, and includes a user study to evaluate the effectiveness of the proposed method.
- The proposed method is technically sound and shows potential for improving the quality of the reconstruction, which is crucial for privacy preservation.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper's focus on facial portrait representations limits its applicability to other types of data, which could be a significant drawback in broader privacy applications.
- The paper does not adequately address privacy concerns, particularly the potential for identity leakage through the reconstruction process.
- The experimental results are not convincing, with some figures and tables (e.g., Figure 5 and Table 1) being difficult to interpret or understand.
- The paper lacks a comprehensive evaluation of the method's effectiveness in real-world scenarios, which is crucial for demonstrating its practical utility.
- There are inconsistencies and potential errors in the manuscript, such as incorrect formatting and citation styles, which detract from its professional quality.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors clarify the privacy concerns raised by the reconstruction process, particularly how they address the potential for identity leakage?
- How does the proposed method compare to other existing methods for privacy preservation, especially in terms of its effectiveness and practical applicability?
- Could the authors provide more detailed explanations or examples of how the method could be applied in different scenarios, such as medical diagnosis or stress level assessment, to better demonstrate its utility?
- Given the limitations of the current method to facial portrait representations, are there plans to expand the method to other types of data or to address privacy concerns in a more comprehensive manner?
