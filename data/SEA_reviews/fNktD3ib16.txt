 **Summary:**
The paper investigates the potential negative consequences of knowledge editing in Large Language Models (LLMs), focusing on two key issues: Knowledge Conflict and Knowledge Distortion. It introduces new benchmark datasets and evaluation metrics to quantify these issues, providing a comprehensive analysis of various knowledge editing techniques. The paper also proposes a new method, Multi-Label Edit (MLE), to mitigate the negative effects of knowledge distortion. The experiments conducted on these datasets demonstrate the effectiveness of MLE in addressing these challenges. However, the paper has been critiqued for its limited scope in terms of the models and datasets used, and the novelty of the proposed methods.

**Strengths:**
- The paper addresses a significant and relevant problem in the field of knowledge editing for Large Language Models (LLMs), focusing on the potential risks associated with knowledge editing.
- It introduces new benchmark datasets and evaluation metrics, which are novel and useful for quantifying the quality of knowledge editing.
- The paper is well-written, with clear figures and tables that aid in understanding the content.
- The proposed method, Multi-Label Edit (MLE), is simple yet effective in mitigating the negative effects of knowledge distortion.
- The paper provides a comprehensive analysis of existing knowledge editing techniques, highlighting their strengths and weaknesses.
- The authors conduct experiments on two LLMs, demonstrating the effectiveness of MLE in mitigating knowledge distortion.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper's experimental setup is limited, focusing on only two models (GPT-2XL and GPT-J) and not including more recent or larger models like GPT-3.5 or GPT-4, which could limit the generalizability of the findings.
- The novelty of the proposed method, MLE, is questionable as it appears to be a simple combination of existing techniques without significant innovation.
- The paper does not adequately address the potential negative societal impacts of the research, such as the potential misuse of knowledge editing for malicious purposes.
- The paper's scope is limited to a specific type of knowledge editing, which might not generalize well to other types of knowledge editing.
- The paper lacks a broader evaluation of the proposed method, MLE, and does not include comparisons with other methods, which could provide a more comprehensive understanding of its effectiveness.
- The paper's contribution is seen as limited as it primarily evaluates existing methods without proposing significant new methodologies.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors clarify the novelty of the proposed method, MLE, and explain how it differs from existing techniques?
- How does the paper address the potential negative societal impacts of the research, particularly the misuse of knowledge editing for malicious purposes?
- Can the authors provide more details on the construction of the datasets, particularly how they ensure the accuracy and completeness of the data?
- How does the proposed method, MLE, compare to other methods in terms of effectiveness and efficiency?
- Could the authors provide more examples or case studies to demonstrate the practical applications and benefits of the proposed method?
- In light of the limited scope of the experiments, how do the authors plan to expand the evaluation to include more diverse models and datasets?
