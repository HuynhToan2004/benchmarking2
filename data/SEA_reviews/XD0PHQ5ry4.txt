 **Summary:**
The paper introduces SELF, a novel approach to self-evolution in large language models (LLMs) that leverages self-reflection and feedback mechanisms to enhance model performance. SELF comprises two stages: meta-skill learning, where LLMs learn self-feedback and refinement abilities, and self-evolution, where these skills are applied to generate high-quality training data. The methodology is evaluated across various benchmarks, demonstrating improvements in mathematical and general tasks. However, the paper's experimental setup and the clarity of its contributions are questioned, with concerns about the novelty and the practical applicability of the proposed method.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written and easy to follow, with clear explanations of the proposed method and its components.
- The idea of enabling LLMs to self-evolve is innovative and potentially impactful, especially in the context of mathematical and general tasks.
- The experiments are comprehensive, covering a variety of benchmarks and different configurations of the SELF framework, which helps in understanding the framework's effectiveness.
- The paper is original in its approach to self-evolution, which is a significant advancement in the field of AI and machine learning.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks clarity on the specific contributions of the proposed method, particularly in how it differs from existing methods such as self-consistency.
- The experimental setup is not detailed enough, especially concerning the selection and generation of unlabeled prompts, which is crucial for the success of the self-evolution phase.
- The paper does not provide sufficient comparisons with other methods, such as self-refinement and self-consistency, which could help in understanding the unique contributions of SELF.
- The paper's claims about the model's ability to self-evolve are not convincingly supported by the experimental results, which primarily show improvements over a baseline rather than a significant leap in performance.
- There are issues with the presentation and clarity of some figures and tables, which could hinder the reader's understanding of the results.

**Questions:**
- Could you clarify the specific contributions of SELF compared to existing methods like self-consistency?
- How does the selection and generation of unlabeled prompts impact the success of the self-evolution phase?
- What are the implications of using different starting models in the self-evolution process, and how does this affect the overall performance?
- How does the SELF framework handle the potential for catastrophic forgetting in the self-evolution process?
- Can you provide more details on the experimental setup, particularly the generation of the meta-skill training corpus and the selection of prompts for the self-evolution phase?
- How does the SELF framework compare to other self-refinement methods, and what are the unique advantages of SELF?
