 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel subgraph sampling method, S3, aimed at improving the training of large-scale graphs by minimizing the staleness of historical embeddings of out-of-batch nodes. This method leverages a staleness score-based sampling strategy to ensure that historical embeddings remain relevant, thereby enhancing the accuracy of node embeddings. Theoretical analysis and empirical results demonstrate that S3 can effectively improve the performance of historical embedding-based methods. Despite its innovative approach, the paper is criticized for its limited novelty and the marginal performance improvements over existing methods. The experimental results, while showing some improvements, are not convincingly better than the state-of-the-art methods.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-organized and easy to follow, with a clear motivation and theoretical analysis of the approximation error.
- The proposed method is simple yet effective, and the experimental results demonstrate its effectiveness in improving the performance of historical embedding-based methods.
- The theoretical analysis of the approximation error caused by using historical embeddings for out-of-batch neighbors is insightful and contributes to understanding the impact of historical embeddings on node embeddings.
- The paper provides a good discussion on related works and the optimization objective, which is equivalent to the graph partitioning problem.

**Weaknesses:**
- The novelty of the proposed method is limited as it mainly focuses on improving the staleness of historical embeddings, which has been previously addressed by other methods such as GraphFM and LMC.
- The performance improvements over existing methods like GraphFM and LMC are marginal, and the paper does not convincingly demonstrate superior performance across all datasets.
- The paper lacks a detailed discussion on the limitations of the proposed method, particularly concerning the scalability and generalization to other GNN models and datasets.
- The theoretical analysis and experimental results are not convincingly superior to existing methods, and the paper could benefit from a more comprehensive comparison with state-of-the-art methods.
- The paper does not adequately address the computational complexity and scalability of the proposed method, especially in large-scale graph training scenarios.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors provide more details on how the staleness score-based subgraph sampling method can further benefit other historical embedding-based methods, such as GAS, GraphFM, and LMC?
- How does the proposed method perform on other datasets beyond those tested, and can it be generalized to other types of GNN models?
- What is the computational complexity of the proposed method, and how does it compare to other state-of-the-art methods in terms of training time and memory usage?
- Can the authors provide more experimental results to demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed method on other types of GNN models and datasets?
- How does the proposed method handle the scalability issues in large-scale graph training, and what are the potential limitations in terms of generalization to other types of GNN models?
