 **Summary:**
The paper introduces ATraDiff, a novel method leveraging offline data to generate synthetic trajectories for online reinforcement learning (RL) agents. The method involves training a set of diffusion models to generate trajectories of varying lengths and adapting these models online to mitigate distribution shifts. The approach is evaluated across multiple environments, showing improvements over baseline methods. However, concerns were raised regarding the clarity of the experimental setup, the generalizability of the results, and the novelty of the method compared to existing literature.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, making it easy to follow and understand.
- The proposed method, ATraDiff, is innovative in its application of diffusion models for generating synthetic trajectories, which can be integrated into various online RL algorithms.
- The method's adaptability to handle varying trajectory lengths and mitigate distribution shifts is a significant advantage.
- The paper provides empirical evidence showing that ATraDiff can enhance the performance of online RL methods across a variety of environments.
- The inclusion of an ablation study demonstrates the effectiveness of different components of the proposed method.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed comparison with existing works, particularly those that also utilize diffusion models for trajectory generation.
- The experimental setup is not clearly explained, particularly the selection of environments and the parameters used, which could affect the reproducibility and generalizability of the results.
- The paper does not provide sufficient details on the computational complexity and the training costs associated with the proposed method.
- The method's reliance on image-level generation for better performance raises questions about its applicability in environments where image-level data is not available.
- The paper does not adequately address the scalability and efficiency of the method, especially in environments with high-dimensional observations or where the data distribution significantly varies.
- There are concerns about the novelty of the method, as similar approaches have been previously explored in the literature.

**Questions:**
- Could you clarify how the selection of environments for evaluation was made and whether these environments were chosen based on specific criteria?
- How does the proposed method compare in terms of performance and computational efficiency with other existing methods, particularly those that also utilize diffusion models for trajectory generation?
- Can you provide more details on the training costs and computational complexity of the proposed method, especially in environments with high-dimensional observations?
- How does the method handle the distribution shift between offline and online data, and what measures are taken to ensure that the generated trajectories are effective in mitigating this shift?
- Could you elaborate on the method's scalability and its applicability in environments where image-level data is not available?
- How does the proposed method compare with other methods that use offline data to enhance online RL, particularly in terms of performance and computational efficiency?
