 **Summary:**
The paper introduces G2PTL, a domain-specific pre-trained model designed for encoding geographic knowledge in delivery addresses, focusing on the logistics domain. G2PTL leverages a large-scale heterogeneous graph of delivery addresses to pre-train, integrating both text and graph modeling capabilities. The model is evaluated across four downstream tasks, showing significant improvements over other PTMs in logistics-related tasks. The paper is well-received for its innovative approach, practical application, and strong experimental results, although it is noted for its limited scope to the logistics domain and lack of broader evaluation against non-domain-specific models.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, clear, and easy to follow, with a clear motivation and a well-defined problem addressed.
- The proposed model, G2PTL, is innovative, integrating both text and graph modeling capabilities, which is particularly useful for encoding geographic knowledge in delivery addresses.
- The paper presents strong experimental results, demonstrating the effectiveness of G2PTL in logistics-related tasks, and includes comprehensive ablation studies to understand the impact of different components.
- G2PTL is designed to handle specific knowledge in the delivery address domain, which is crucial for improving the performance of downstream tasks in logistics systems.
- The model has been deployed in production in Cainiao's logistics system, demonstrating its practical applicability and real-world impact.

**Weaknesses:**
- The scope of the paper is limited to the logistics domain, which may limit its applicability to other domains or general scenarios.
- The evaluation is limited to logistics-related tasks, and it would be beneficial to see how G2PTL performs against non-domain-specific models on broader tasks.
- The paper lacks a detailed discussion on the limitations of the proposed model and potential negative societal impacts.
- The model's performance on out-of-distribution (OOD) data is not evaluated, which is crucial for understanding its robustness and generalizability.
- There is a lack of detailed information on the dataset used, including its size, diversity, and how it was constructed.
- The paper does not discuss the generalizability of the model to other domains or scenarios beyond logistics.

**Questions:**
- How does the proposed model perform on out-of-distribution (OOD) data?
- What are the limitations of the proposed model, and how do these limitations impact its practical applicability?
- Could the authors provide more details on the dataset used, including its size, diversity, and construction process?
- How does the model handle noisy or incomplete address data, and what impact does this have on its performance?
- Can the authors discuss the potential negative societal impacts of their work, and how these impacts are mitigated?
- It would be interesting to see how the model performs on non-logistics-related tasks to understand its generalizability. Could the authors provide insights or results on this?
