 **Summary:**
The paper investigates the zero-shot clustering capabilities of pretrained models, specifically focusing on self-supervised learning (SSL) feature encoders. It examines various SSL methods such as contrastive, self-distillation, canonical correlation analysis, masked image modeling, and multi-modal learning. The study compares these methods against supervised learning and different clustering algorithms like K-Means, Agglomerative Clustering, and HDBSCAN. The experiments are conducted on multiple datasets, including ImageNet, to evaluate the performance of these methods in clustering embeddings without fine-tuning. The paper also explores the effectiveness of different SSL methods and their impact on clustering performance, providing insights into the utility of pretrained models in zero-shot clustering scenarios.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-organized, clearly written, and easy to follow, making it accessible to readers.
- It addresses an important problem of zero-shot clustering using pretrained models, which is a significant area of research.
- The paper provides a comprehensive evaluation of various SSL methods and clustering algorithms, which is valuable for understanding the effectiveness of different approaches.
- The experiments are conducted on a diverse set of datasets, including ImageNet, which helps in validating the results across different scenarios.
- The paper includes a detailed discussion on the performance of different SSL methods and clustering algorithms, providing insights into the strengths and weaknesses of each approach.
- The use of Silhouette score as a proxy for evaluating clustering performance is highlighted, which could be useful in scenarios where ground truth labels are not available.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed discussion on the impact of different backbone architectures (ResNet-50 vs ViT-B) on the performance of SSL methods.
- There is no direct comparison with other baseline methods that do not use pretrained models, which could provide a more comprehensive evaluation of the proposed methods.
- The paper does not explore the potential of fine-tuning the clustering algorithms on the test datasets to improve performance, which could be a significant contribution.
- The evaluation metrics used, such as AMI and Silhouette score, may not fully capture the effectiveness of the clustering methods, particularly in scenarios where ground truth labels are not available.
- The paper does not sufficiently address the computational complexity or scalability of the proposed methods, which could limit their practical applicability.
- There is a lack of discussion on the limitations of the proposed methods and potential future research directions, which could enhance the paper's contribution to the field.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors provide a more detailed analysis of the performance differences between ResNet-50 and ViT-B in the context of SSL methods?
- How do the proposed methods perform when fine-tuned on the test datasets?
- What are the potential future directions for research in zero-shot clustering using pretrained models?
- Could the authors discuss the potential of using other evaluation metrics, such as Normalized Mutual Information (NMI), to evaluate the clustering performance?
- How do the proposed methods compare in terms of computational complexity and scalability with other clustering methods that do not use pretrained models?
