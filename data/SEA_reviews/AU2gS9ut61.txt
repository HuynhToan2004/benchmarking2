 **Summary:**
The paper introduces BrainPy, a differentiable brain simulator that integrates brain simulation with brain-inspired computing (BIC) using JAX and XLA. BrainPy offers event-driven operators, sparse and jit connectivity operators, and a multi-scale model building interface to enhance efficiency and scalability in brain simulations. The framework is demonstrated to outperform existing simulators in terms of speed and memory efficiency, with potential applications in large-scale brain simulations and brain-inspired computing. The paper also explores the integration of brain simulation with machine learning, showing the capability of training a biologically plausible spiking model on working memory tasks.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel approach by integrating brain simulation with brain-inspired computing (BIC), which is a significant advancement in the field.
- The use of JAX and XLA for the simulator provides a robust and efficient framework, allowing for the integration of gradient-based optimization algorithms and large-scale brain simulations.
- The paper is well-written, clear, and easy to follow, making it accessible to a broad audience.
- The inclusion of a comprehensive set of benchmarks demonstrates the superior performance of BrainPy over existing simulators, showcasing its potential as a valuable tool for neuroscientists and machine learning researchers.
- The paper effectively bridges the gap between brain simulation and brain-inspired computing, providing a platform for researchers to explore the complexities of the brain and advance interdisciplinary research.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a discussion on the limitations of the proposed framework, which could provide a more comprehensive understanding of its applicability and potential drawbacks.
- The comparison with other simulators could be improved by including more recent and relevant frameworks, such as PyNN and NetPyNE.
- The paper does not adequately address the compatibility of BrainPy with other BIC libraries, which could limit its utility in integrated environments.
- There is a lack of detailed discussion on the implementation of specific features like JIT connectivity and the impact of event-driven computation on memory usage.
- The paper could benefit from a more thorough discussion on the biological plausibility and realism of the models simulated using BrainPy.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors provide a more detailed discussion on the limitations of the proposed framework and potential future directions for improvement?
- How does BrainPy compare in terms of performance and functionality with other recent and relevant brain simulators, such as PyNN and NetPyNE?
- What are the specific advantages of using BrainPy over other existing simulators, particularly in terms of biological realism and scalability?
- Can BrainPy be integrated with other BIC libraries, and if so, what are the implications for model training and optimization?
- How does BrainPy handle the integration of machine learning models with brain simulations, and what are the implications for the biological plausibility and realism of the models?
