 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel approach to Multi-Modal Multi-Task Learning (M3TL) using a sparse mixture of experts (SMoE) and a sparse mixture of attention (SMoA) in a transformer architecture. The methodology aims to address challenges such as modality forgetting, fitting, and learning pace by disentangling network parameters for different modalities and tasks. It employs adaptive expert allocation and learning pace adjustment to optimize model performance. The paper presents comprehensive experiments on various datasets, demonstrating significant performance improvements and reduced computational costs compared to state-of-the-art methods. However, concerns are raised about the limited novelty in the use of SMoE and SMoA, the lack of comparison with other baselines, and the generalization of the proposed method to other datasets and models.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-organized, clearly written, and easy to follow, making it accessible to a broad audience.
- The authors have identified and addressed the challenges of modality forgetting, fitting, and learning pace in multi-modal multi-task learning, proposing a novel solution that disentangles model spaces for different modalities and tasks.
- The proposed method achieves significant performance improvements and reduces computational costs, demonstrating its effectiveness in handling multiple modalities and tasks.
- The paper provides comprehensive experimental results and a detailed ablation study, which supports the validity of the proposed method.
- The use of adaptive expert allocation and learning pace adjustment in the SM4 framework is a novel approach that addresses the challenges of modality fitting and heterogeneous learning pace.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed discussion on how the proposed method disentangles model spaces for different modalities and tasks, which is crucial for understanding the method's effectiveness.
- The use of SMoE and SMoA, while innovative in their application, is not novel in concept, as these techniques have been previously explored in other studies.
- The paper does not compare the proposed method with other baselines, which limits the evaluation of its effectiveness against existing solutions.
- The generalization of the proposed method to other datasets and models is not adequately demonstrated, which raises concerns about its applicability in different scenarios.
- The paper does not discuss the limitations of the proposed method, which is essential for understanding its scope and potential challenges.
- The paper does not include code or a link to the code, which could hinder reproducibility and further research based on the proposed method.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors provide more details on how the proposed method disentangles model spaces for different modalities and tasks?
- How does the proposed method perform when compared to other baselines, especially in terms of computational cost and performance?
- Can the authors discuss the limitations of the proposed method and how these might affect its practical application?
- How does the proposed method perform when applied to other datasets and models?
- Could the authors provide the code or a link to the code to facilitate reproducibility and further research?
