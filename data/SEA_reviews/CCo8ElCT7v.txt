 **Summary:**
The paper presents a comparative analysis between Vision Transformers (ViTs) and Convolutional Neural Networks (CNNs) in face recognition tasks, specifically focusing on face identification and verification. It employs six state-of-the-art models, including EfficientNet, Inception, MobileNet, ResNet, VGG, and Vision Transformers, and utilizes five datasets to evaluate their performance. The study highlights the superiority of ViTs in terms of accuracy, robustness, and inference speed. However, the paper is criticized for its lack of novelty, insufficient comparisons with state-of-the-art models, and unclear experimental results.

**Strengths:**
- The paper provides a comprehensive comparison between Vision Transformers (ViTs) and Convolutional Neural Networks (CNNs) in face recognition tasks, which is valuable for understanding the relative strengths and weaknesses of these architectures.
- The paper is well-structured and easy to follow, making it accessible to a broad audience.
- It evaluates the performance of six state-of-the-art models across five datasets, which helps in drawing meaningful conclusions about the relative strengths of different models in different scenarios.
- The paper includes a thorough examination of the training and evaluation process, as well as the software and hardware configurations used, which enhances the credibility of the results.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks novelty as it does not introduce any new models or methods, and the comparisons are limited to only six models, which may not fully represent the state-of-the-art in face recognition.
- There is a lack of clarity in the experimental results, particularly in the presentation of the ROC curves and the interpretation of the results.
- The paper does not adequately address the challenges specific to face recognition tasks, such as pose variations, facial expressions, and lighting conditions, which are critical for evaluating the robustness of the models.
- The paper does not compare the models with state-of-the-art models, which limits the generalizability of the findings.
- The paper's focus on ViTs and CNNs alone is limiting, as there are other models like R-CNN and Faster R-CNN that could be relevant for face recognition tasks.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors provide a more detailed comparison with state-of-the-art models to enhance the generalizability of the findings?
- How do the authors address the challenges specific to face recognition tasks, such as pose variations, facial expressions, and lighting conditions, in their experimental setup?
- Could the authors clarify the presentation of the ROC curves and the interpretation of the results, particularly in figures where the performance of different models is not clearly distinguishable?
- Why was the decision made to focus solely on ViTs and CNNs, and could the authors consider including other models like R-CNN and Faster R-CNN in future work?
- How do the authors ensure the reproducibility of their results, and what steps have been taken to validate the accuracy and reliability of the experimental setup?
