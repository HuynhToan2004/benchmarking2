 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel variant of the Stochastic Variance Reduced Gradient (SVRG) method, termed alpha-SVRG, which incorporates a linearly decreasing coefficient to optimize the variance reduction term. This modification aims to improve the performance of SVRG, particularly in deep neural networks, by reducing the variance in gradient updates and thereby enhancing convergence. The method is evaluated on various datasets and neural network architectures, showing promising results in terms of reduced gradient variance and improved training loss. However, the theoretical analysis supporting the use of this coefficient is lacking, and the empirical evidence provided is not sufficiently robust to convincingly demonstrate the superiority of alpha-SVRG over existing methods.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, clear, and easy to follow, with a good presentation of the results.
- The proposed method is simple and effective, and the experiments are comprehensive, covering various architectures and datasets.
- The paper provides a novel approach to SVRG by introducing a coefficient to the variance reduction term, which is a significant modification to the original method.
- The authors have demonstrated the effectiveness of their method through various experiments, showing improvements in training loss and gradient variance reduction.
- The use of a coefficient to control the strength of the variance reduction term is a novel and interesting approach, particularly in the context of deep neural networks.

**Weaknesses:**
- The theoretical analysis supporting the use of the coefficient in SVRG is lacking, and the paper does not provide a clear justification for its effectiveness.
- The experimental results are not sufficiently robust to convincingly demonstrate the superiority of the proposed method over existing methods like SGD or AdamW.
- The paper primarily focuses on neural network training and does not explore the application of the proposed method in other areas, such as reinforcement learning or optimization of other types of models.
- The paper does not include a comparison with other variance reduction methods, which could provide a more comprehensive evaluation of the proposed method.
- The experimental setup and the choice of baselines are not clearly justified, and the paper does not sufficiently address the potential impact of the coefficient on the computational efficiency and practical implementation of the method.

**Questions:**
- How does the proposed method perform in terms of generalization accuracy, particularly in comparison to other methods like SGD or AdamW?
- Can the authors provide more details on the experimental setup, such as the choice of hyperparameters and the rationale behind the specific experimental conditions used?
- How does the proposed method compare to other variance reduction methods, particularly in terms of computational efficiency and practical implementation?
- Could the authors clarify the role and impact of the coefficient on the performance of the method, especially in terms of its practical implementation and the computational resources required?
- How does the method perform in different types of neural network architectures, such as recurrent neural networks or transformer models?
