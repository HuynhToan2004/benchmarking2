 **Summary:**
The paper introduces FARMap, a novel method for efficient map-building inspired by the fragmentation of grid cells in the hippocampus. FARMap utilizes a combination of short-term and long-term memory to manage map fragmentation and recall, aiming to reduce memory usage and improve efficiency in exploring complex environments. The method is evaluated against existing baselines in procedurally generated environments and robot simulations, demonstrating improvements in memory usage and exploration speed. However, the paper has been critiqued for its lack of clarity in methodological descriptions, insufficient comparisons with other methods, and potential issues with the biological plausibility of its assumptions.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, making it easy to follow, and the authors have provided a comprehensive review of related work.
- The idea of using grid cell-like map fragmentation is novel and interesting, particularly the use of surprisal to determine fragmentation, which is a new approach.
- The proposed FARMap is simple and effective, with strong experimental results showing improvements in memory usage and efficiency compared to baseline methods.
- The paper provides a clear motivation and a simple and intuitive algorithm that is easy to understand, contributing to its accessibility and applicability.
- The code is provided, which is a significant contribution to the research community.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed description of the method, particularly in Section 3, which could benefit from more detailed explanations and examples.
- The experimental setup is limited to small-scale environments, and it's unclear how the method would perform in more complex, large-scale environments.
- The paper does not sufficiently compare its method with other existing methods, such as Neural SLAM, which could provide a more robust validation of the proposed approach.
- There is a lack of discussion on the limitations and societal impacts of the proposed method, which could be crucial for understanding its broader implications.
- The paper could benefit from more detailed visualizations and explanations of the map fragmentation and recall processes, as well as the use of color coding in figures to aid comprehension.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors provide a more detailed explanation of the method, particularly in Section 3, to help clarify the fragmentation and recall processes?
- How does the method perform in larger, more complex environments, and what are the scalability limits of FARMap?
- Could the authors provide a comparison of FARMap with other methods, such as Neural SLAM, to demonstrate the relative advantages of their approach?
- How does the method handle fragmentation in different environments, and what are the implications of this for the overall performance and efficiency of the mapping process?
- In the context of the experiments, how were the hyperparameters chosen, and what are the implications of these choices for the generalizability of the results?
