 **Summary:**
The paper introduces AutoJoin, a gradient-free adversarial training technique designed for robust maneuvering in image-based perception-to-control tasks. AutoJoin utilizes a denoising autoencoder to enhance the robustness of models against gradient-free perturbations, achieving significant performance improvements over existing methods. The methodology involves jointly learning the denoising autoencoder and the regression model, which allows for simultaneous improvement in both maneuvering and denoising capabilities. Extensive experiments are conducted across multiple datasets, demonstrating significant improvements in model robustness and efficiency compared to existing methods. However, the paper lacks a detailed discussion on the limitations and potential negative societal impacts, and the generalizability of the method to other tasks is not thoroughly explored.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, easy to follow, and provides extensive experimentation, including ablation studies, which support the claims effectively.
- The proposed AutoJoin method achieves the best performance on the maneuvering task while being the most efficient among the compared methods.
- The methodology is innovative, combining a denoising autoencoder with a regression model to enhance model robustness, which is a novel approach in the field.
- The paper demonstrates significant performance improvements over existing methods, showing a substantial reduction in error rates and an increase in model accuracy.
- The methodology is well-explained, with clear illustrations and detailed explanations of the experimental setup and results, which aids in understanding the method's effectiveness.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed discussion on the limitations and potential negative societal impacts of the proposed method, which is crucial for a comprehensive understanding of its applicability and implications.
- The generalizability of the method to other tasks beyond maneuvering is not thoroughly explored, which could limit the understanding of its broader applicability.
- The paper could benefit from a more detailed discussion on the theoretical underpinnings of the method, particularly how the denoising autoencoder contributes to the overall robustness of the model.
- The paper does not include a discussion on the limitations of the method, which could help in understanding its scope and potential challenges in real-world applications.
- The comparison with other methods, such as AugMix, is not sufficiently fair due to differences in the training data and the backbone models used, which could affect the validity of the results.
- The paper could benefit from a more detailed analysis of the computational complexity and resource requirements of the proposed method, especially in comparison to other state-of-the-art methods.

**Questions:**
- Could you provide a detailed discussion on the limitations and potential negative societal impacts of the proposed method?
- How does the AutoJoin method perform in real-world applications, particularly in maneuvering tasks?
- Is there a possibility to extend the AutoJoin method to other tasks beyond maneuvering? If so, what are the potential challenges and limitations?
- Can you clarify the computational complexity and resource requirements of the AutoJoin method compared to other state-of-the-art methods?
- How does the AutoJoin method compare in terms of efficiency and effectiveness with other gradient-based adversarial training methods?
- Could you provide more details on the selection of perturbation types and intensities, and how these affect the performance of the model?
- The paper mentions a potential for the AutoJoin method to be applied to other tasks. Could you provide some examples or scenarios where this might be particularly beneficial?
