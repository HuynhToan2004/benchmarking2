 **Summary:**
The paper addresses the principal-agent problem where the principal commits to a contract across multiple rounds, aiming to learn an optimal contract by observing the outcomes of each round. The authors focus on scenarios where the agent's action space is small, and they propose an algorithm that discovers the optimal contract with high probability in a polynomial number of rounds. The algorithm's novelty lies in its ability to approximate the best-response region of the agent's actions, thereby improving upon existing bounds in online learning settings. The paper also discusses the application of this algorithm in online learning scenarios, providing a no-regret algorithm for the principal. Despite its technical soundness and novel contributions, the paper's presentation and clarity need improvement, particularly in defining key terms and structuring the algorithm descriptions.

**Strengths:**
- The paper addresses an interesting problem in the principal-agent model with hidden actions, focusing on the scenario where the agent's action space is small, which is a novel approach.
- The algorithm proposed is simple, elegant, and theoretically sound, with a novel approach to approximating the best-response region of the agent's actions, which is a significant contribution to the field.
- The paper is well-written, with clear explanations and proofs that are mostly easy to follow, enhancing the understanding of the proposed methods.
- The paper presents a new algorithm that can learn an optimal contract in a polynomial number of rounds, which is a significant improvement over previous results that required exponential time in the size of the action space.
- The application of the algorithm to online learning settings is highlighted, demonstrating its versatility and potential practical applications.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper suffers from several presentation issues, including unclear definitions of key terms and concepts, which could hinder the comprehension of the content.
- The paper lacks a clear discussion on the relationship with related works, particularly in the context of online learning and principal-agent problems, which could have strengthened the paper's position in the literature.
- There are several technical details and notations that are not well-explained or defined, which could confuse readers and detract from the paper's clarity.
- The paper's organization and structure could be improved, particularly in the presentation of algorithms and proofs, to enhance readability and understanding.
- The paper could benefit from more detailed discussions on the practical implications and limitations of the proposed algorithms, especially in terms of their computational complexity and scalability.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors clarify the definition and role of the "clean event" in the context of the algorithm?
- How does the paper handle the assumption of an agent with an action that has non-negative utility, and what are the implications if this assumption does not hold?
- What is the role of the parameter β in the algorithm, and how does it affect the performance of the algorithm?
- Can the authors provide more details on the computational complexity of the algorithm, especially in terms of the size of the action space and the number of outcomes?
- How does the algorithm handle the case where the agent's actions are not known in advance, and how does this affect the learning process?
- Could the authors discuss the relationship between the proposed algorithm and existing algorithms in terms of computational complexity and performance guarantees?
- How does the paper address the practical implications of the algorithm in real-world scenarios, and what are the potential limitations or challenges in implementing the proposed methods?
