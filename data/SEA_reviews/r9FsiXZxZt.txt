 **Summary:**
The paper addresses the challenges of disentangling object properties in multi-object images by leveraging object-centric architectures. It critiques the traditional assumptions of injectivity in generative models and proposes a novel approach that reduces the number of perturbations needed for disentanglement. The methodology integrates slot attention mechanisms with existing disentanglement techniques to enhance the disentanglement of object properties in multi-object scenes. The paper is supported by experiments on synthetic datasets, demonstrating the effectiveness of the proposed method. However, concerns are raised about the limited real-world applicability, the complexity of the method, and the lack of robustness to perturbations.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, clear, and easy to follow, with a clear motivation and a well-defined problem setting.
- The authors provide a novel solution to the problem of disentanglement in multi-object scenes, which is an interesting and relevant topic.
- The proposed method is technically sound, with a reasonable design that makes sense and is supported by experimental results.
- The paper is well-positioned within the current literature, highlighting its relevance and contribution to the field.
- The authors demonstrate the potential of object-centric architectures in achieving disentanglement, which is a significant advancement in the field.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a thorough discussion of related work, particularly missing recent and relevant studies that address similar problems.
- The methodology is complex and requires significant computational resources, which might limit its practical application in real-world scenarios.
- The paper does not adequately address the issue of robustness to perturbations, which is a critical aspect of disentanglement in practical applications.
- The experimental validation is limited to synthetic datasets, which may not fully represent real-world scenarios, raising concerns about the generalizability of the results.
- The paper's claims about the reduction in the number of perturbations needed for disentanglement are not substantiated with clear theoretical proofs or empirical evidence.
- The paper does not sufficiently discuss the limitations of its method, such as its applicability to real-world scenarios and its scalability to larger, more complex datasets.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors provide a more detailed discussion on how their method compares to related works, particularly those that address similar problems in object-centric disentanglement?
- How does the proposed method perform in real-world scenarios, especially in terms of scalability and robustness to perturbations?
- Could the authors clarify the role and contribution of the matching step in the proposed method, especially in scenarios where perturbations are unknown?
- How does the proposed method handle the reduction in the number of perturbations needed for disentanglement, and can this be substantiated with theoretical or empirical evidence?
- Can the authors provide more details on the computational complexity and resource requirements of their method, especially in comparison to other state-of-the-art methods?
