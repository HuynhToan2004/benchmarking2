 **Summary:**
The paper investigates the vulnerability of language model detectors by employing a data-efficient attack that fine-tunes language models to confuse existing detectors, using reinforcement learning to optimize the human-ness score. The authors demonstrate that a 7B parameter Llama-2 model can significantly reduce the effectiveness of detectors like OpenAI RoBERTa-Large, while maintaining a low perplexity. The study also explores the generalization of these attacks across different detectors and the impact of model size and training data. Despite the novel approach and the empirical evidence provided, the paper is critiqued for its lack of theoretical analysis and detailed discussion on the limitations and potential countermeasures against the proposed attacks.

**Strengths:**
- The paper provides a novel approach to evaluating the robustness of language model detectors by fine-tuning a model to evade them, demonstrating that existing detectors can be easily optimized against.
- The experiments are comprehensive and convincing, showing that the proposed method can effectively evade detectors across different settings and model sizes.
- The paper is well-written, making it easy to follow and understand, with clear visualizations that aid in the interpretation of results.
- The methodology is straightforward and practical, which could be of interest to the ICLR community.
- The paper raises significant concerns about the reliability and robustness of current language model detectors, which is crucial for understanding the security and authenticity of text generated by large language models.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a thorough discussion on the limitations of the proposed method and potential countermeasures against the attacks.
- There is a lack of theoretical analysis and detailed discussion on the results, particularly the generalization of attacks across different detectors and the impact of model size and training data.
- The paper does not sufficiently compare its results with existing literature, which could help in understanding the novelty and effectiveness of the proposed method.
- The methodology section could be clearer, especially in terms of the details of the data generation process and the experimental setup.
- The paper's claims about the robustness of detectors are not adequately supported, and the results are not sufficiently discussed in relation to existing literature.
- The paper's conclusions are not well-supported by the data presented, and the generalization of the attacks to other detectors is not convincingly demonstrated.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors provide more details on the data generation process and the experimental setup, particularly how the preference data was generated and how it was used to train the models?
- How does the proposed method compare to other existing methods in terms of its effectiveness and efficiency?
- Can the authors provide more theoretical analysis or insight into why the proposed method works and how it affects the robustness of detectors?
- How generalizable is the proposed method across different detectors and model sizes? What factors influence the generalization of the attacks?
- Could the authors clarify the discrepancies in the results, particularly the low AUROC scores reported in some experiments?
- How do the authors address the concerns about the robustness of detectors and the potential for misuse of the findings?
