 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel approach to node selection in the branch-and-bound algorithm using a graph neural network (GNN) and reinforcement learning (RL). The method models the entire tree state rather than individual nodes, aiming to improve the efficiency of the branch-and-bound algorithm by selecting the most promising nodes for further exploration. The approach is evaluated on various benchmarks, including TSPLIB, UFLP, and MIPLIB, showing improvements in optimality gap reductions and per-node efficiency. However, the paper is criticized for its lack of clarity in methodology and experimental setup, with concerns about the generalizability of the results to real-world scenarios and the fairness of comparisons with other state-of-the-art methods.

**Strengths:**
- The paper addresses a significant challenge in the field of branch-and-bound algorithms by proposing a novel approach using a GNN and RL, which is a novel application of these techniques in this context.
- The methodology is well-presented, with clear explanations of the problem setup, tree representation, and RL for node selection.
- The paper includes a comprehensive set of experiments across various benchmarks, demonstrating the effectiveness of the proposed method in improving the performance of the branch-and-bound algorithm.
- The paper is well-written, making it easy to follow, and the experimental results are robust, showing significant improvements in optimality gap reductions and per-node efficiency.
- The use of a graph neural network (GNN) to model the entire tree state is a novel approach that has shown promise in reducing the optimality gap and improving per-node efficiency.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks clarity in its methodology, particularly in the use of GNNs and RL, and the experimental setup. It is unclear how the GNNs are used to process the tree and how the RL algorithm is integrated into the node selection process.
- The paper does not adequately compare its results with other state-of-the-art methods, such as those mentioned in related works, which could have provided a more robust validation of the proposed method.
- The generalizability of the results is a concern, as the paper primarily focuses on synthetic TSP instances and does not include real-world benchmarks.
- The paper's presentation and clarity could be improved, with better organization and clearer explanations of the methodology and experimental results.
- There are several typographical and grammatical errors throughout the paper that need correction.

**Questions:**
- Can you clarify how the GNNs are used to process the tree and how the RL algorithm is integrated into the node selection process?
- How does the proposed method compare to other state-of-the-art methods in terms of computational efficiency and effectiveness in real-world scenarios?
- How were the hyperparameters for the RL algorithm chosen, and what impact do they have on the performance of the method?
- Could you provide more details on the experimental setup, such as the computational resources used and the time taken for each experiment?
- How does the proposed method perform on more challenging benchmarks, such as those from the MIPLIB or MINLP datasets?
- Can you discuss the potential limitations of the proposed method and how these might affect its applicability in different scenarios?
