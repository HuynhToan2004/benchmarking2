 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel method for optimizing the integration approximation in diffusion models by adjusting the coefficients of the ODE solvers. This approach, termed Improved Integration Approximation (IIA), aims to enhance the sampling quality of diffusion models, particularly in scenarios where the number of forward passes (NFE) is small. The authors demonstrate that IIA can significantly improve the FID scores of various models, including DDIM, DPM-Solver++, and EDM, by optimizing the coefficients through a mean squared error function. The method is supported by extensive experiments that showcase its effectiveness across different models and datasets. However, the paper is criticized for its limited novelty and potential computational overhead, which could be a concern for practical applications.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, clear, and easy to follow, making it accessible to a broad audience.
- The proposed method, Improved Integration Approximation (IIA), is simple, effective, and can be applied to various diffusion models, including DDIM, DPM-Solver++, and EDM.
- The experiments conducted are comprehensive, demonstrating the effectiveness of IIA across different models and datasets, showing significant improvements in FID scores.
- The method's ability to optimize the coefficients of the ODE solvers without changing the pre-trained model is a notable strength, as it allows for flexible application to different models.
- The paper provides a detailed explanation of the methodology, including the derivation of the IIA-EDM, which is a significant contribution to the field.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed analysis of the computational overhead introduced by the IIA method, which is crucial for understanding its practical applicability.
- The method's effectiveness is limited to small NFEs, which may not be practical for most applications.
- The paper does not provide a detailed comparison with other high-order methods like PLMS, which could have provided a more robust evaluation of the proposed method.
- The novelty of the paper is questioned as the approach appears to be a simple application of MMSE optimization, which has been previously used in similar contexts.
- The paper does not sufficiently discuss the potential limitations of the proposed method, such as its applicability to other types of diffusion models or its scalability to larger datasets.
- The paper could benefit from more detailed explanations and justifications for certain methodological choices, such as the use of MMSE for optimizing coefficients.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors provide more details on the computational overhead introduced by the IIA method, including the time and memory requirements?
- How does the IIA method compare in terms of computational efficiency and effectiveness with other high-order methods like PLMS, especially in scenarios where NFE is small?
- Could the authors elaborate on the method's effectiveness in scenarios where NFE is larger, and discuss potential reasons for the observed discrepancies in FID scores?
- Is there a possibility to extend the IIA method to other types of diffusion models, such as those that do not rely on ODE solvers?
- How does the IIA method perform in terms of CLIP scores, and what insights can be derived from these results?
- Could the authors clarify the role and impact of the hyperparameter M in the IIA method, and discuss its sensitivity to different values?
- Can the authors provide a more detailed comparison of the IIA method with other existing methods, including a discussion on its advantages and disadvantages?
