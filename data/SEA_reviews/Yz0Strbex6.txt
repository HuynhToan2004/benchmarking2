 **Summary:**
This paper explores the statistical properties of signature transforms under stochastic integrals, specifically focusing on the consistency of Lasso regression with different signature definitions (Ito and Stratonovich). It examines the correlation structures of signatures for various stochastic processes, such as Brownian motion and Ornstein-Uhlenbeck processes, and their impact on feature selection. The paper provides theoretical insights into the statistical consistency of Lasso regression with different signature definitions and discusses their implications for statistical inference and machine learning applications. The research is supported by both theoretical analysis and numerical simulations, highlighting the importance of appropriate signature definitions and stochastic models in feature selection.
