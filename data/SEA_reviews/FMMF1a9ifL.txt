 **Summary:**
The paper introduces GOLF, a novel framework aimed at optimizing molecular conformations using Neural Network Potentials (NNPs). It addresses the issue of distribution shifts in NNPs by integrating optimization trajectories as additional training data, thereby reducing the need for extensive data collection. The framework is evaluated on a subset of the nablaDFT dataset, showing that it can achieve comparable performance to a DFT-based oracle while using significantly less additional data. The methodology is validated through experiments that demonstrate the effectiveness of GOLF in reducing computational costs and improving the accuracy of NNPs in predicting potential energies.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel framework, GOLF, which effectively utilizes optimization trajectories to enhance the training of Neural Network Potentials (NNPs), addressing the challenge of distribution shifts in NNPs.
- The framework is demonstrated to perform on par with a DFT-based oracle while using significantly less additional data, reducing computational costs and enhancing the efficiency of NNP training.
- The paper provides a clear and logical presentation, effectively communicating the problem, methodology, and results, making it accessible and understandable to readers.
- The authors have conducted thorough experiments and comparisons, demonstrating the effectiveness of the proposed method and providing a detailed analysis of the results.
- The paper is well-written, with clear and concise explanations of the methodology and the results, which are supported by comprehensive evaluations and comparisons with existing methods.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed discussion on the limitations and potential negative societal impacts of the proposed method, which could be crucial for understanding the broader implications of the work.
- There is a need for more clarity and detail in the presentation, particularly in the methodology section, which could benefit from more precise definitions and explanations of terms and processes.
- The paper could benefit from a more comprehensive evaluation, including a broader range of datasets and a comparison with more recent or state-of-the-art methods in the field.

- The paper could be improved by providing more detailed explanations and justifications for the choices made in the methodology, such as the use of specific datasets and the selection of hyperparameters.
