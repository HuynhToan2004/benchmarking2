 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel approach to partial label learning (PLL) by focusing on candidate label set pruning (CLSP), which aims to filter out potentially misleading labels by leveraging the inconsistency between the representation space and the candidate label space. The authors propose a method that identifies and eliminates false candidate labels based on their occurrence in the representation space, which is supported by a theoretical analysis of the pruning error rate. Extensive experiments are conducted across various PLL settings and datasets, demonstrating the effectiveness of the proposed method. Despite its innovative approach, the paper has been critiqued for its lack of clarity in some sections, particularly in the definition of key terms and the presentation of theoretical proofs.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel perspective to the partial label learning (PLL) problem by focusing on candidate label set pruning (CLSP), which is a significant contribution to the field.
- The proposed method is simple, intuitive, and effective, as evidenced by its performance across various PLL settings and datasets.
- The paper is well-written, making it accessible and easy to follow, which is crucial for a broad audience.
- The theoretical analysis provided is comprehensive and helps in understanding the proposed method's effectiveness.
- The empirical results are robust and demonstrate the superiority of the proposed method over existing approaches.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks clarity in some sections, particularly in the definitions and explanations of key terms and concepts such as "label distinguishability" and "down-voting procedure."
- The theoretical proofs and their implications are not clearly explained, which could hinder understanding of the method's foundational contributions.
- There is a lack of discussion on the limitations of the proposed method and potential negative societal impacts, which are crucial for evaluating the method's broader applicability and ethical implications.
- The paper does not adequately compare its method with existing works, which could undermine the perception of its novelty and effectiveness.
- Some sections of the paper, particularly the methodological descriptions and experimental results, are confusing or inadequately explained, which could limit the reader's comprehension and engagement.

**Questions:**
- Can you clarify the definitions and implications of "label distinguishability" and "down-voting procedure" as used in your method?
- How does the proposed method compare to existing approaches in terms of computational efficiency and resource requirements?
- How does the method handle the potential for false negatives in label pruning, and what are the implications of these errors?
- Could you provide more detailed explanations of the theoretical proofs and their implications for the practical application of your method?
- How do you address the potential negative societal impacts of your method, particularly in terms of data privacy and bias in label selection?
- Can you elaborate on the experimental setup and results, particularly in the sections where the results are not clearly explained or where there are discrepancies between different datasets or settings?
