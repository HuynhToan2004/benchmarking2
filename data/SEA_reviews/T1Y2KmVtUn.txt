 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel method for optimizing sensor parameters in an end-to-end manner, focusing on the layout of pixels in imaging sensors. This approach involves learning pixel layouts that can be tailored to specific tasks, such as classification and semantic segmentation. The method is supported by a differentiable sensor simulation framework, which allows for the optimization of sensor parameters alongside those of neural networks. The proposed methodology has been validated through experiments on datasets like MNIST, CelebA, and Cityscapes, demonstrating improvements in task performance. However, concerns were raised about the generalizability of the method to different tasks and its scalability to larger, more complex datasets.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, clearly explaining the methodology and its implementation, including the differentiable sensor simulation framework and the end-to-end optimization of pixel layouts.
- The proposed method is novel, particularly in its approach to jointly optimizing sensor parameters and neural network parameters, which is a significant departure from traditional methods.
- The experiments conducted on datasets like MNIST and CelebA demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed method, showing improvements in task performance.
- The paper provides a detailed explanation of the methodology, including the sensor layout parameterization and its implementation as a drop-in module, which is easily integrable into existing deep learning models.
- The hardware implementation of the learned chip layout for a semantic segmentation task is a notable strength, demonstrating the practical applicability of the proposed method.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a comprehensive comparison with other methods, particularly in terms of performance metrics and the scalability of the proposed method to larger, more complex datasets.
- The generalizability of the method to different tasks is questionable, as the experiments primarily focus on classification and semantic segmentation tasks.
- The paper does not sufficiently discuss the limitations of the proposed method, such as its scalability and the potential impact of different sensor layouts on various tasks.
- The paper could benefit from a more detailed discussion on the hardware implementation of the learned chip layout, including its fabrication and testing.
- The paper's scope is somewhat limited, focusing primarily on semantic segmentation and not exploring other applications or tasks that could benefit from the proposed method.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors provide a more detailed comparison with other methods, including performance metrics and scalability results?
- How does the proposed method perform on other tasks, such as object detection or image classification?
- Can the authors discuss the potential impact of different sensor layouts on various tasks and provide insights into how the method could be adapted to different applications?
- How does the proposed method handle the integration of sensor design parameters and their geometric limits into the modeling of deformations?
- Could the authors provide more details on the hardware implementation of the learned chip layout, including its fabrication and testing?
- How does the proposed method handle the optimization of sensor parameters in real-world scenarios, particularly in terms of energy efficiency and computational complexity?
