 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel test-time training method for large language models (LLMs) using a nearest neighbor (NN) retrieval system. This method involves retrieving a set of NN texts, fine-tuning the LLM on these texts, and then evaluating the LLM on the test text. The approach is evaluated using the Pile dataset and shows that fine-tuning on retrieved texts can significantly improve perplexity metrics. The method is designed to be computationally efficient, with minimal hyperparameter tuning required. Despite its novelty and potential utility, the paper has been critiqued for its limited scope in terms of the tasks and models tested, and the lack of comprehensive evaluation metrics beyond perplexity.
