 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel semi-supervised Bayesian optimization (BO) framework named TSBO, which integrates a teacher-student model to leverage unlabeled data in BO. The teacher model predicts pseudo labels for unlabeled data, which are then used to train a student model. This approach allows for the efficient use of unlabeled data, improving the generalization of the data query model. The methodology includes a bi-level optimization process and two sampling strategies to select unlabeled data, which are evaluated against several baselines in various tasks. Despite the novel approach and promising results, the paper is criticized for its complexity, lack of clarity in presentation, and limited empirical evaluation.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel approach by integrating a teacher-student model into Bayesian optimization (BO), which is a first of its kind.
- The methodology is well-explained, with clear explanations of the teacher-student model and the sampling strategies.
- The paper is well-written and easy to follow, with detailed explanations of the methodology and experiments.
- The proposed method is evaluated on several tasks, demonstrating its effectiveness in improving the generalization of the data query model.
- The use of a teacher-student model and the integration of unlabeled data with pseudo labels are innovative approaches that enhance the utility of unlabeled data in BO.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper is complex and difficult to follow, with numerous equations and notations that require further clarification.
- The empirical evaluation is limited, with only a few datasets used and a lack of comparison with more recent and relevant baselines.
- The computational cost of the proposed method is high, and the paper does not adequately address the scalability of the method.
- The paper lacks a detailed discussion on the limitations of the method, including the computational cost and the potential impact of different sampling strategies.
- The paper could benefit from more comprehensive comparisons with other semi-supervised learning methods and a broader range of datasets to demonstrate the generalizability of the proposed method.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors clarify the computational cost of the proposed method and discuss potential strategies for reducing it?
- How does the proposed method compare to other semi-supervised learning methods, particularly in terms of computational efficiency and generalization capabilities?
- Could the authors provide more details on the selection of the feedback weight (\(\lambda\)) and its impact on the performance of the method?
- What are the specific advantages of using a teacher-student model over other methods in BO, and how does it compare to other methods in terms of computational efficiency and generalization capabilities?
- Can the authors discuss the potential impact of different sampling strategies on the performance of the method and provide a more detailed analysis of the results?
- Could the paper benefit from a more comprehensive comparison with other semi-supervised learning methods, including a broader range of datasets to demonstrate the generalizability of the proposed method?
