 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a comprehensive benchmarking framework for Continuous Glucose Monitoring (CGM) datasets, focusing on predicting blood glucose levels. It presents a set of benchmark models against which new methods can be evaluated, using a curated list of publicly available CGM datasets. The framework aims to address the challenges of reproducibility and accessibility in CGM research by providing a standardized task list and a repository of datasets. The paper also includes an analysis of performance-influencing factors, which could guide future model development. However, concerns were raised about the depth of analysis, the limited novelty of the approach, and the lack of a detailed discussion on the impact of the study.

**Strengths:**
- The paper provides a comprehensive benchmarking framework for Continuous Glucose Monitoring (CGM) datasets, which is a significant contribution to the field.
- It offers a curated list of publicly available CGM datasets, enhancing reproducibility and accessibility in CGM research.
- The standardized task list unifies research objectives, facilitating coordinated efforts and potentially improving the identification of optimal tools for tackling specific challenges.
- The paper includes a set of benchmark models with established baseline performance, enabling the research community to objectively gauge new methods' efficacy.
- The detailed analysis of performance-influencing factors for model development is a valuable resource for future research.
- The paper is well-organized, easy to read, and provides a comprehensive review of existing CGM-based glucose prediction models and datasets.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed discussion on the impact of the study, which could have been expanded to provide a more comprehensive understanding of the significance and implications of the work.
- The novelty of the approach is limited, as the benchmarking framework is not significantly different from existing approaches.
- The depth of analysis is limited, particularly in the areas of uncertainty quantification and the integration of covariates, which could benefit from more detailed exploration.
- The paper does not provide a detailed discussion on the limitations of the study, which could help in understanding the scope and applicability of the findings.
- There is a lack of clarity on the selection criteria for the datasets used, which could affect the generalizability of the results.
- The paper does not discuss the potential societal impact of the study, which is crucial for understanding the broader implications of the work.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors elaborate on the impact of the study and discuss the broader implications of their work in the field of CGM research?
- How do the authors plan to address the limited depth of analysis in future work, particularly in the areas of uncertainty quantification and the integration of covariates?
- Can the authors provide more details on the selection criteria for the datasets used in the study and discuss the implications of these choices for the generalizability of the findings?
- What steps are being taken to ensure the privacy and security of the data used in the study, particularly in light of the sensitive nature of the health records involved?
