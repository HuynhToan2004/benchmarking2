 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel method for handling rolling shutter (RS) images in NeRF-based 3D reconstruction, addressing the challenges posed by the unique distortions in RS images. The method employs a cubic B-Spline to model the camera trajectory, which is optimized along with the NeRF parameters to achieve accurate camera motion and undistorted image results. The paper is well-written and includes comprehensive experimental results, comparing the proposed method with existing RS-aware methods and demonstrating its effectiveness in both synthetic and real-world scenarios. The method's ability to handle complex camera motions and its generalization across different datasets are highlighted. However, the paper could benefit from more detailed comparisons with state-of-the-art methods and a broader evaluation on various real-world datasets.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, clear, and easy to follow, with comprehensive experiments that demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed method.
- The method is simple, intuitive, and effective, with a focus on the physical image formation process of a RS camera, which is crucial for handling rolling shutter effects.
- The paper introduces a novel approach to handling rolling shutter images in NeRF-based 3D reconstruction, which is a significant problem in the field.
- The method's generalization performance is highlighted, showing that it can handle complex camera motions and recover accurate camera motion trajectories simultaneously.
- The paper includes a detailed discussion on the limitations and potential improvements of the method, which is beneficial for future research.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks detailed comparisons with state-of-the-art methods, particularly in terms of the number of control points and the optimization of camera poses.
- The method's evaluation is limited to specific datasets, which may not fully demonstrate its effectiveness across different scenarios.
- The paper could benefit from a broader discussion on the limitations of the method, such as the impact of varying camera motions and the generalization to different datasets.
- The paper does not provide a detailed analysis of the computational cost of the proposed method, which is crucial for real-world applications.
- There is a lack of qualitative results in the main text, which could enhance the understanding of the method's effectiveness.

**Questions:**
- Could you provide a more detailed comparison of the number of control points used in the method compared to other state-of-the-art methods?
- How does the method perform when the camera motion is not smooth or constant?
- Is there a possibility to extend the method to handle multiple camera motions or different types of rolling shutter cameras?
- Can you discuss the potential impact of varying the number of control points on the method's performance?
- Could you provide more details on the computational cost of the proposed method, including the training and inference times?
- How does the method perform when applied to datasets with different characteristics, such as different camera motions or image qualities?
