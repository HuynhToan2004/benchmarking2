 **Summary:**
The paper explores the use of sentence-transformer models for retrieving sentences based on both polarity and semantic similarity. The authors propose a novel approach by fine-tuning sentence-transformer models on augmented supervised data and introduce two new metrics, Polarity Score and Semantic Similarity Score, for evaluation. The paper investigates the impact of different loss functions on model performance and provides empirical evidence on the SST-2 dataset and a sarcastic news dataset. The experiments demonstrate that the proposed method can effectively distinguish between different classes and maintain semantic similarity. However, the paper is criticized for its limited novelty and insufficient theoretical support, as well as the lack of comparisons with more recent state-of-the-art methods.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel approach to retrieving sentences based on polarity and semantic similarity, which is a significant contribution to the field.
- The authors have conducted comprehensive experiments on two datasets, providing a detailed analysis of the effects of different loss functions on model performance.
- The introduction of new metrics, Polarity Score and Semantic Similarity Score, is a valuable contribution to the community, enhancing the evaluation of models in this domain.
- The paper is well-written and easy to follow, making it accessible to a broad audience.
- The experiments are extensive, demonstrating the effectiveness of the proposed method across various scenarios.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks novelty as it primarily focuses on fine-tuning sentence-transformer models on augmented supervised data, which has been extensively explored in previous works.
- The theoretical justification for the proposed metrics and the overall methodology is insufficient, with a lack of discussion on how the metrics are derived and their theoretical underpinnings.
- The experiments are limited to a few datasets, which may not adequately demonstrate the generalizability of the proposed method across different domains.
- There is a significant gap in the comparison of the proposed method with more recent state-of-the-art methods, which could have provided a clearer picture of its advantages or disadvantages.
- The paper does not adequately address the issue of catastrophic forgetting during fine-tuning, which is a critical concern in model adaptation.
- The paper's methodology and experimental setup are not sufficiently detailed, making it difficult to replicate the results or understand the full impact of the proposed method.

**Questions:**
- Can you elaborate on how the Polarity Score and Semantic Similarity Score are derived, and what theoretical foundations support their use?
- How does the proposed method compare to other recent state-of-the-art methods, particularly in terms of performance and efficiency?
- Could you provide more details on the experimental setup, such as the specifics of data augmentation and the hyperparameters used in the experiments?
- How does the proposed method address the issue of catastrophic forgetting during fine-tuning, and what measures have been taken to mitigate this risk?
- Could you clarify the rationale behind the choice of datasets and the impact of using different datasets on the results?
- How does the proposed method perform in scenarios where the datasets have different characteristics, such as varying levels of noise or complexity?
