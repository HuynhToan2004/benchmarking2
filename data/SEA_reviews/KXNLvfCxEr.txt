 **Summary:**
The paper introduces EvIL (Evolutionary Imitation Learning), a novel approach to imitation learning (IL) that integrates evolution strategies with reinforcement learning (RL) to optimize reward functions and dynamics in changing environments. EvIL uses a two-stage process: the outer loop optimizes the reward function and dynamics using evolution strategies, while the inner loop trains a policy using these optimized parameters. This method allows for the recovery of reward functions that induce expert-like behavior across various environments, including tabular and continuous control settings. The paper claims that EvIL outperforms traditional offline approaches like behavioral cloning and inverse reinforcement learning techniques, but its evaluation is limited to simple environments and does not sufficiently compare with more complex baselines.

- The methodology is clearly explained, with detailed algorithms and examples, making it accessible and understandable.
