 **Summary:**
The paper introduces Uni-O4, a novel approach that integrates online and offline reinforcement learning (RL) by employing an on-policy objective for both training phases. This method leverages diverse ensemble policies to address the mismatch between the estimated behavior policy and the offline dataset, ensuring seamless transition between offline and online learning. Uni-O4 is evaluated across various simulated and real-world robot tasks, demonstrating superior performance compared to existing methods. Key contributions include the use of an ensemble of policies for behavior cloning and an offline policy evaluation method for multi-step policy improvement, which are crucial for the algorithm's effectiveness.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, easy to follow, and provides clear explanations of the proposed methods, making it accessible to a broad audience.
- The integration of online and offline RL is a significant advancement, allowing for the seamless transfer of learning between different phases, which is crucial for practical applications.
- The use of diverse ensemble policies for behavior cloning and the offline policy evaluation method for multi-step policy improvement are innovative contributions that enhance the algorithm's performance.
- The paper includes extensive experimental results and ablation studies that support the claims made, demonstrating the effectiveness of the proposed methods.
- The real-world experiments are convincing, showcasing the practical applicability of the proposed methods.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks detailed comparisons with existing methods, particularly in the offline-to-online setting, which could better position the proposed method against current state-of-the-art techniques.
- The use of an ensemble of policies for behavior cloning and the offline policy evaluation method for multi-step policy improvement are novel but not sufficiently differentiated from existing methods, which might lead to questions about the novelty of the approach.
- The paper does not adequately discuss the limitations of the proposed method, which could provide valuable insights into the challenges and potential improvements.
- The computational cost of the ensemble behavior cloning approach is high, which might limit its practical applicability in resource-constrained environments.
- The paper does not discuss the generalizability of the proposed method across different types of tasks or domains, which could be a significant concern for its broader applicability.
- The paper does not provide sufficient details on the experimental setup, such as the number of seeds used for evaluation, which could affect the reliability of the results.

**Questions:**
- Could you clarify the computational cost of the ensemble behavior cloning approach and discuss potential strategies for reducing this cost?
- How does the proposed method handle the generalization across different types of tasks or domains, and what are the implications for its applicability in diverse settings?
- Can you provide more details on the experimental setup, particularly the number of seeds used for evaluation and how the hyperparameters were selected?
- How does the proposed method compare to other state-of-the-art offline-to-online methods, and what are the specific advantages of using Uni-O4 over these methods?
- Could you discuss the potential limitations of the proposed method and suggest areas for future research to address these limitations?
