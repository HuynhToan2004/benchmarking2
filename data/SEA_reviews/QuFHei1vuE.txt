 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel approach to video frame interpolation (VFI) by replacing the traditional time indexing with distance indexing. This method, which can be integrated into existing VFI models, guides the network with an explicit hint about the object's displacement, enhancing the model's capability to predict precise object movements. The paper also proposes an iterative reference-based estimation strategy to mitigate directional ambiguity in long-range motion prediction. The methodology is validated through experiments on the Vimeo90K dataset, showing improved performance over state-of-the-art models. The paper also discusses the potential of the proposed method in various video editing tasks.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel approach to video frame interpolation by replacing the time indexing with distance indexing, which is a significant departure from traditional methods.
- The method is simple yet effective, with clear and detailed explanations that make it easy to follow.
- The paper is well-written, with a clear presentation of ideas and a comprehensive evaluation that demonstrates the effectiveness of the proposed method.
- The methodology is flexible and can be integrated into existing VFI models without requiring extensive modifications, making it a plug-and-play strategy.
- The paper provides a thorough evaluation of the proposed method, showing that it outperforms state-of-the-art models on various datasets.
- The introduction of distance indexing allows for the manipulation of individual objects in a video, enhancing the potential applications of the method in video editing tasks.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed discussion on the limitations of the proposed method, which is crucial for understanding its applicability and potential drawbacks.
- The computational cost of the proposed method, particularly the iterative reference-based estimation, is not discussed, which is important for practical implementation.
- The paper does not provide a detailed comparison with other methods that use similar strategies, such as using optical flow to guide interpolation, which could help contextualize the proposed method's contributions.
- The paper could benefit from a more comprehensive discussion on the physical implications of the distance indexing approach, particularly how it affects the temporal coherence and consistency of the interpolated frames.
- The paper does not discuss the potential negative societal impacts of the proposed method, which is a critical consideration for any technological innovation.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors provide a detailed discussion on the limitations of the proposed method and potential future directions for research?
- How does the computational cost of the proposed method compare to existing methods, and what are the practical implications of using the iterative reference-based estimation strategy?
- Could the authors clarify the discrepancy in the convergence curves shown in Figure 6 and explain why the iterative reference-based estimation strategy does not consistently improve performance across all models?
- How does the proposed method handle scenarios where the object's motion is not linear, and what are the implications of this for the accuracy of the interpolated frames?
- Could the authors discuss the potential negative societal impacts of the proposed method and how these might be mitigated or addressed?
