 **Summary:**
The paper investigates the expressive power of ReLU networks under various convex relaxations, specifically focusing on univariate and multivariate functions. It explores the ability of ReLU networks to encode CPWL functions using different relaxation methods such as IBP, DeepPoly, Triangle, and Multi-Neuron relaxations. The study reveals that while univariate functions can be precisely encoded using more advanced relaxations like Triangle and Multi-Neuron, multivariate functions are fundamentally limited in their expressivity. The paper also discusses the implications of these findings for certified training and proposes that more precise analysis methods like multi-neuron or non-convex relaxations could potentially improve network performance.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, clear, and structured, making it accessible and easy to follow.
- It provides a comprehensive overview of the expressive power of ReLU networks under different convex relaxations, which is a significant contribution to the field.
- The paper presents a novel result on the impossibility of expressing multivariate, convex, monotone CPWL functions precisely with finite ReLU networks, which is a surprising and non-trivial finding.
- The authors provide a detailed analysis of the expressivity of ReLU networks using various convex relaxations, which is both interesting and relevant to the field of certified training.
- The paper is original in its approach and presents a significant contribution to the understanding of the expressive power of ReLU networks under different relaxations.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a conclusion section, which could summarize the findings and discuss their implications.
- The expressivity results presented in the paper are limited to monotone CPWL functions, which may not be the most relevant or practical class of functions for many applications.
- The paper does not adequately address the computational complexity or scalability of the proposed methods, which is a significant limitation given the complexity of the certification methods.
- There is a lack of experimental validation or empirical evidence to support the claims made about the expressive power of ReLU networks under different relaxations.
- The paper does not provide a clear comparison or discussion with other relevant works, such as those that consider different classes of neural networks or different types of relaxations.
- The paper could benefit from a more detailed discussion on the implications of the findings for certified training and the potential for improving network performance using more precise analysis methods.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors clarify the definition of "monotone" in the context of the CPWL functions used in the study?
- How does the expressive power of ReLU networks under different relaxations compare to other types of neural networks, such as those based on different activation functions or using different types of relaxations?
- Can the authors provide more details on the computational complexity and scalability of the certification methods proposed in the paper?
- How do the findings of this study relate to or differ from previous works on the expressive power of neural networks under different relaxations?
- Could the authors discuss the implications of their findings for certified training and suggest potential directions for future research in this area?
