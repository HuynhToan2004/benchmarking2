 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a curriculum-based reinforcement learning quantum architecture search (CRLQAS) method aimed at finding optimal quantum circuits for quantum variational algorithms (VQAs), particularly in noisy intermediate-scale quantum (NISQ) environments. The method employs a tensor-based binary circuit encoding and a random halting mechanism to efficiently search for circuits that minimize energy errors. The CRLQAS method is evaluated against existing QAS algorithms and shows improvements in both noiseless and noisy environments. Despite its strengths in methodology and experimental validation, the paper suffers from several presentation issues and lacks a thorough discussion on the scalability and generalizability of its methods.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel curriculum-based reinforcement learning quantum architecture search (CRLQAS) method, which is a significant contribution to the field of quantum architecture search.
- The methodology is clearly explained, with a detailed description of the CRLQAS method, including the tensor-based binary circuit encoding and random halting mechanism.
- The paper is well-written, making it accessible and understandable to readers.
- The experimental results are comprehensive, comparing the CRLQAS method with existing QAS algorithms and demonstrating its effectiveness in both noiseless and noisy environments.
- The paper addresses the important problem of quantum architecture search in noisy intermediate-scale quantum (NISQ) environments, which is a significant challenge in the field.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a thorough discussion on the scalability of the CRLQAS method, particularly in terms of the number of qubits and the complexity of the molecules it can handle.
- There is a lack of discussion on the generalizability of the method to other types of quantum circuits or quantum algorithms beyond VQAs.
- The paper does not adequately address the computational complexity of the CRLQAS method, which is crucial for understanding its practical applicability.
- The presentation of the paper needs improvement, particularly in the clarity and organization of figures and tables, and the overall structure of the paper.
- The paper does not sufficiently discuss the limitations of the CRLQAS method, which is essential for understanding its applicability and potential challenges.
- The paper does not provide a comprehensive discussion on the noise models used, which are crucial for understanding the performance of the CRLQAS method in different noisy environments.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors provide more details on the scalability of the CRLQAS method, particularly in terms of the number of qubits and the complexity of the molecules it can handle?
- How does the CRLQAS method perform in other types of quantum circuits or quantum algorithms beyond VQAs?
- What are the specific computational complexities of the CRLQAS method, and how do these affect its practical applicability?
- How does the CRLQAS method handle different types and levels of noise, and what are the implications of these on its performance?
- Can the authors provide more details on the noise models used in their experiments, and how do these models affect the performance of the CRLQAS method?
- How does the CRLQAS method compare to other methods in terms of noise resilience and generalizability?
- Can the authors clarify the presentation of figures and tables, particularly in terms of their organization and clarity?
