 **Summary:**
The paper investigates continual reinforcement learning (RL) in the context of MDPs, focusing on catastrophic forgetting and proposing a method to mitigate it by reweighting Bellman targets. The authors introduce a theoretical framework that analyzes the convergence of various RL algorithms, including the Finetune algorithm, and propose a novel method to balance stability and plasticity. This method, which involves reweighting Bellman targets, is shown to perform well in tabular settings, although its applicability in more complex, high-dimensional environments remains untested. The paper also discusses the computational complexity and practical implications of the proposed method, which is compared against existing baselines.

**Strengths:**
- The paper addresses a significant problem in continual reinforcement learning (RL) by proposing a novel theoretical analysis framework for catastrophic forgetting in RL, which is both innovative and relevant to the current research landscape.
- The proposed method of reweighting Bellman targets is theoretically motivated and shows practical utility in the tabular setting, providing a novel approach to addressing the challenges of continual RL.
- The paper is well-written, with clear explanations and a logical flow that aids in understanding the complex theoretical concepts and experimental results.
- The authors provide a thorough review of existing literature and compare their results against relevant baselines, demonstrating the robustness and novelty of their approach.
- The paper includes both theoretical analysis and experimental validation, which supports the claims made about the effectiveness of the proposed method.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper primarily focuses on tabular settings, which limits the generalizability of the findings to more complex, high-dimensional environments.
- There is a lack of clarity and detailed explanation in some sections of the paper, particularly in the definitions and notations used, which could confuse readers and hinder the understanding of the proposed methods.
- The paper could benefit from more extensive experiments to validate the claims made, especially in more complex environments where the proposed method might be less effective.
- The convergence analysis in the paper is somewhat limited, focusing only on the convergence of the algorithm in the tabular setting, which may not fully address the convergence issues in more complex environments.
- The paper does not sufficiently discuss the computational complexity of the proposed method, which could be a significant limitation in practical applications.
- The paper's theoretical contributions, while innovative, may not be entirely novel as similar concepts have been explored in other areas of machine learning, such as neural TSP.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors clarify the notations and definitions used in the paper, particularly in the early sections where these are first introduced?
- How does the proposed method perform in more complex, high-dimensional environments, and what are the potential limitations or challenges in applying the method in such settings?
- Can the authors provide more details on the computational complexity of the proposed method and discuss how it scales with the size of the problem?
- How does the proposed method compare to other existing methods in terms of computational efficiency and practical applicability?
- Could the authors elaborate on the convergence analysis provided in the paper, particularly in relation to the convergence issues in more complex environments?
- What are the potential societal impacts of the proposed method, and how might these be mitigated or addressed?
