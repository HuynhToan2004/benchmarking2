 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel approach to human-agent collaboration in multiplayer online battle arena (MOBA) games, focusing on enhancing the human experience rather than solely optimizing agent performance. The proposed method, Reinforcement Learning from Human Gain (RLHG), involves estimating the human gain from the agent's actions, which is then used to fine-tune the agent's policy. This approach is evaluated in real-world experiments using the MOBA game "Honor of Kings," demonstrating improvements in both objective game performance and subjective human satisfaction. The paper also addresses the challenge of balancing agent autonomy with human enhancement, using a novel method to estimate human gains and adjust agent behavior accordingly.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, clear, and easy to follow, with a clear motivation and well-defined problem.
- The introduction of the human-centered modeling scheme is innovative, focusing on enhancing the human experience in collaborative tasks rather than solely optimizing agent performance.
- The proposed RLHG approach is novel and shows promising results in real-world experiments, demonstrating its potential to improve human-agent collaboration in complex game environments.
- The paper provides a detailed implementation framework and evaluates the effectiveness of the RLHG agent in real-world human-agent tests, showing that it provides participants with a better gaming experience.
- The experiments conducted in a real-world environment with human participants add credibility to the results and demonstrate the practical applicability of the RLHG approach.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a comprehensive comparison with existing baselines, which could have strengthened the argument for the superiority of the proposed method.
- The experiments are limited to a single game environment, which may not fully demonstrate the generalizability of the RLHG approach across different game scenarios.
- The paper does not discuss potential negative societal impacts of the proposed approach, which could be a significant concern in applications where human-agent collaboration is critical.
- The evaluation is limited to a single game, which may not adequately demonstrate the generalizability of the RLHG approach across different game scenarios.
- The paper does not discuss how the proposed approach could be extended to scenarios where multiple agents collaborate with multiple humans, which could limit the applicability of the findings.
- The paper lacks a discussion on the limitations of the proposed approach and potential negative societal impacts, which are crucial for understanding the broader implications of the work.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors provide more details on how the human-centered objective was implemented in the experiments, particularly how the human reward function was defined?
- How does the proposed approach handle scenarios where the human's goals are not aligned with the agent's objectives?
- Could the authors elaborate on the limitations of the proposed approach and discuss potential negative societal impacts, especially in applications where human-agent collaboration is critical?
- How does the proposed approach compare to other methods that aim to improve human-agent collaboration in MOBA games?
- Could the authors provide more details on the experimental setup, particularly how the human policy was trained and how the human gain network was implemented?
- How does the proposed approach handle the credit assignment issue, especially in scenarios where the agent's actions may not directly lead to human gains?
