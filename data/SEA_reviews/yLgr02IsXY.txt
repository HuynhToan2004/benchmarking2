 **Summary:**
The paper introduces AMPipe, a novel system-level optimization technique aimed at accelerating the training of large Mixture-of-Experts (MoE) models, particularly those with long sequences. AMPipe employs intra-block pipelining to optimize the computational and communication costs of the attention and MoE layers, thereby addressing the significant bottlenecks in MoE-based models. The approach is validated through extensive experiments on GPT-based models, demonstrating a 23% improvement in average training throughput and a 41% maximum throughput boost. The paper also discusses the challenges in integrating AMPipe with existing training systems like Megatron-LM and provides insights into the impact of different pipeline degrees on performance.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel intra-block pipelining technique, AMPipe, which addresses the bottlenecks in MoE-based models by optimizing both the attention and MoE layers.
- Extensive experiments were conducted to evaluate the effectiveness of AMPipe, demonstrating an average throughput improvement of 23% and a maximum throughput boost of 41%.
- The paper is well-written, easy to follow, and includes a thorough evaluation of AMPipe's performance under various pipeline degrees, providing insights into the optimal settings for different scenarios.
- The proposed method is simple, efficient, and compatible with most existing training systems, making it applicable to a wide range of MoE-based language and vision models.
- The paper includes a detailed analysis of the impact of different pipeline degrees on performance, which is crucial for practical implementation.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper primarily focuses on GPT-based models, which may limit the generalizability of the results to other MoE-based models.
- The experiments were conducted on relatively small-scale models, raising questions about the scalability of the proposed method to larger models.
- The paper lacks a comprehensive comparison with other existing methods like Tutel and FasterMoE, which could provide a clearer picture of AMPipe's advantages.
- There is a lack of detailed analysis on the computational and memory overheads introduced by AMPipe, which is crucial for understanding its practical applicability.
- The paper does not adequately discuss the potential limitations or drawbacks of AMPipe, which could affect its adoption in real-world scenarios.
- The paper could benefit from a more thorough discussion on the theoretical aspects of AMPipe, particularly how it optimizes the attention and MoE layers.

**Questions:**
- How does AMPipe perform on other MoE-based models beyond GPT, and what are the expected results?
- Can AMPipe be applied to other models that use different types of experts, such as those based on transformers or LSTMs?
- What are the computational and memory overheads introduced by AMPipe, and how do these compare to the baseline models?
- How does AMPipe handle the communication costs between different GPUs, especially in scenarios with high data parallelism?
- Could the authors provide more details on the implementation of AMPipe, particularly how it integrates with existing training systems like Megatron-LM?
- How does AMPipe perform when scaled to larger models, and what are the expected performance improvements?
