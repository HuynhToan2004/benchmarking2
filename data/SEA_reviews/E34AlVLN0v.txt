 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel parallelization algorithm for evaluating and training sequential models, specifically focusing on non-linear differential equations (DEs) and recurrent neural networks (RNNs). The method leverages a fixed-point iteration problem to parallelize the evaluation of sequential models, enhancing training speed without compromising accuracy. This approach, termed DEER, is demonstrated to accelerate training by up to three orders of magnitude compared to traditional sequential methods. The paper also explores the application of DEER in various scenarios such as time series classification and physical systems modeling, showcasing its potential to accelerate research in these areas.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel parallelization algorithm for evaluating and training sequential models, which is a significant contribution to the field.
- The methodology is clearly explained, making it accessible to readers, and the paper is well-structured, enhancing its readability and comprehensibility.
- The paper demonstrates that the proposed method can significantly accelerate training, achieving up to a 10-fold speed increase without compromising accuracy, which is crucial for improving training efficiency.
- The paper includes comprehensive experimental results that validate the effectiveness of the proposed method, and the experiments are designed to showcase the method's impact on various applications.
- The paper provides a theoretical analysis of the algorithm, which adds depth to the understanding of the proposed method.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a discussion on the limitations of the proposed method, which could help readers better understand its applicability and potential drawbacks.
- The paper does not adequately compare the proposed method with other parallelization methods for sequential models, which could provide a more comprehensive evaluation of its effectiveness.

- The paper does not sufficiently discuss the impact of the proposed method on memory usage and computational requirements, which could affect its practical deployment.
