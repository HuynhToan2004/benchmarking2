 **Summary:**
The paper investigates the vulnerability of adversarially trained models to a new type of attack called "two-faced attacks," where slight perturbations in input features are crafted to mislead the model into believing it is more robust than it actually is. The authors demonstrate that these attacks can significantly enhance the perceived robustness of models, potentially leading to unpredictable security issues in real-world applications. The paper provides theoretical insights and empirical evidence, showing that models with higher adversarial robustness are often more susceptible to these attacks. It also proposes a simple countermeasure to mitigate the risk of these attacks, emphasizing the need for caution in model verification phases.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a new type of attack, "two-faced attacks," which is a significant contribution to the field of adversarial machine learning.
- The proposed countermeasure is simple and effective, and the paper provides a clear and detailed explanation of the attacks and their implications.
- The paper is well-written, easy to follow, and includes extensive experiments that support the findings.
- The theoretical analysis of two-faced risk and its relationship with adversarial risk is insightful and adds depth to the understanding of adversarial robustness.
- The paper provides a novel threat model that is relevant in practical scenarios where models are evaluated for robustness before deployment.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a comprehensive discussion on the transferability of two-faced attacks, which is crucial for understanding the practical implications of the attacks.
- There is a lack of discussion on the impact of different adversarial training methods on the vulnerability of models to two-faced attacks.
- The paper could benefit from a more detailed discussion on the countermeasures proposed, including their effectiveness and limitations.
- The scope of the experiments is limited to specific datasets and model architectures, which might not fully capture the generalizability of the findings.
- The paper does not provide a detailed analysis of the computational costs associated with the proposed attacks and countermeasures, which is essential for practical implementation.
- The paper could benefit from a more thorough comparison with existing adversarial training methods and a discussion on how the proposed attacks might affect the performance of these methods.

**Questions:**
- Could you provide a more detailed analysis of the transferability of two-faced attacks?
- How does the choice of adversarial training method affect the vulnerability of models to two-faced attacks?
- What are the computational costs associated with the proposed attacks and countermeasures?
- Can you provide more details on the effectiveness of the countermeasures proposed, including their limitations?
- How do the findings of this paper compare with existing adversarial training methods, and what are the implications for the robustness of these methods against two-faced attacks?
