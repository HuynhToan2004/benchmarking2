 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel re-parameterization method for neural networks, focusing on branch-level substitution and guided activation functions. The proposed method aims to enhance the network's performance by reducing computational complexity and improving non-linear activation functions. The authors claim that this approach can lead to more diverse feature representations and potentially better performance. However, the paper has been critiqued for its lack of clarity in presentation, insufficient experimental validation, and unclear theoretical contributions. Specific concerns include the absence of a detailed comparison with existing methods, unclear definitions of terms, and a lack of comprehensive experimental results.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel branch-level re-parameterization method for neural networks, which allows for the substitution of local paths in the network training process, preserving branch-level connectivity without significant architectural design.
- The proposed guided activation method infuses non-linear activation functions into the re-parameterization process, enhancing the network's performance.
- The paper is well-written, making it easy to follow, and the idea of branch-level re-parameterization is innovative, potentially leading to more efficient and effective neural network architectures.
- The paper provides a comprehensive experimental evaluation, demonstrating the effectiveness of the proposed method through extensive experiments and ablation studies.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks clarity in its presentation, with some sections and figures being difficult to understand, such as Figure 3 and the caption of Figure 4.
- There is a lack of detailed theoretical analysis and comparison with existing methods, particularly in terms of computational complexity and network performance.
- The experimental results are not convincing, with some results showing marginal improvements and others not being statistically significant.
- The paper does not adequately address the computational cost and memory requirements of the proposed method, which are crucial for practical implementation.
- There are several typographical and grammatical errors throughout the paper, which detract from its professional quality.

**Questions:**
- Could you clarify the definition and role of "branch-level connectivity" in your proposed method?
- How does the proposed guided activation function compare to other non-linear activation functions in terms of computational efficiency and effectiveness?
- Can you provide more detailed comparisons of the computational complexity and memory requirements of your method compared to existing methods?
- Why is the proposed method more effective than other methods, and what are the specific advantages in terms of feature diversity and network performance?
- Could you elaborate on the experimental setup and results, particularly the statistical significance of the improvements and the choice of baseline models?
- How does the proposed method handle the computational cost and memory requirements, and what are the implications for practical deployment?
