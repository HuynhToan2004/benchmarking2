 **Summary:**
The paper explores the feature space of face recognition models, proposing a novel method called Intra-class Incoherence (IIC) to enhance the representational capability of features. This method involves decomposing features into orthogonal components, with one component termed "pro-feature" and the other "innovation," which are then combined to create improved features. The authors claim that these innovations can be learned from a pre-trained model using a knowledge distillation approach, which helps in improving the performance of face recognition tasks. The paper presents experimental results showing that the proposed method can outperform existing state-of-the-art models on various benchmarks. However, the novelty of the approach is questioned, as similar concepts have been previously explored, and the method's theoretical underpinnings are not sufficiently detailed.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, easy to follow, and provides clear explanations of the proposed method.
- The introduction of the concept of "innovation" in face recognition is novel and interesting.
- The proposed method is simple yet effective, and the experimental results demonstrate its superiority over existing state-of-the-art methods.
- The motivation behind the method is clear, and the experimental results are convincing, supporting the claims made.
- The use of intra-class incoherence constraints is novel and shows potential for further research in the field.

**Weaknesses:**
- The novelty of the proposed method is limited as it is a straightforward combination of existing techniques, and the theoretical analysis provided is insufficient.
- The paper lacks a detailed discussion on the limitations of the proposed method and its applicability in scenarios with more complex face variations.
- The paper could benefit from more detailed explanations and justifications for certain claims, such as the orthogonal decomposition of features and the impact of different weights on performance.
- The paper does not adequately compare the proposed method with other recent and relevant works, such as "Sphere Confidence Face" and "Data Uncertainty Learning in Face Recognition."
- The paper's experimental setup and results could be more convincing if the experiments were conducted on a larger scale, such as using more datasets or more complex face variations.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors provide more details on the theoretical analysis of the proposed method, particularly how it contributes to the understanding of face recognition?
- How does the proposed method perform when applied to face recognition models trained on more diverse datasets, such as those with significant variations in age, gender, and pose?
- Could the authors elaborate on the choice of weighting factors and their impact on the performance of the model?
- How does the proposed method compare with other recent works that also address the issue of feature incoherence in face recognition?
- Could the authors provide more detailed explanations or justifications for the claims made in the paper, particularly those that are not well-supported by the current evidence?
