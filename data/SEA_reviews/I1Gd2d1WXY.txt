 **Summary:**
The paper introduces the Adaptive Resolution Residual Network (ARRN), a novel architecture that integrates Laplacian residuals and dropout to adaptively adjust network resolution during training and inference. This approach allows the network to be rescaled without retraining, which is beneficial for applications where resolution changes are common. The methodology is supported by theoretical analysis and experimental results, demonstrating improved robustness and computational efficiency. However, the paper lacks comprehensive comparisons with state-of-the-art methods and lacks detailed experimental validation, particularly in terms of the number of parameters and computational complexity.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, clear, and easy to follow, with a logical structure that effectively conveys the key ideas.
- The introduction of Laplacian residuals and dropout as training augmentations is innovative and provides a novel approach to handling resolution changes in deep learning models.
- The theoretical analysis is robust and provides a strong foundation for the proposed methodology.
- The experimental results demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed method, showing improved robustness and computational efficiency compared to existing methods.
- The paper is well-motivated, addressing the challenge of resolution changes in deep learning models, which is a significant and relevant issue.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a comprehensive comparison with state-of-the-art methods, particularly in terms of computational complexity and parameter count.
- The experimental validation is limited to a few datasets and does not include a broader range of real-world applications, which could have provided more robust evidence of the method's effectiveness.
- The paper could benefit from more detailed explanations and examples, especially in sections that require a deeper understanding of the theoretical concepts and methodologies.
- The experimental results, while promising, are not sufficiently convincing due to the lack of comprehensive comparisons and the absence of results on more challenging datasets.
- There are several minor issues with the manuscript, such as unclear figures and missing references, which need to be addressed for clarity and completeness.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors provide a more detailed comparison with state-of-the-art methods, including computational complexity and parameter count?
- How does the proposed method perform on more challenging datasets, such as those used in the ImageNet dataset?
- Can the authors provide more detailed examples and explanations, particularly in sections that require a deeper understanding of the theoretical concepts and methodologies?
- How does the proposed method compare in terms of robustness and computational efficiency with other adaptive resolution methods, such as those based on neural operators?
- Could the authors clarify the experimental setup and results, particularly in figures where the results are not clearly presented or where there are discrepancies between the text and the figures?
