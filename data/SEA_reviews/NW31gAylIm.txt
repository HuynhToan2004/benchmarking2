 **Summary:**
The paper introduces Federated Text-driven Prompt Generation (FedTPG), a novel approach to prompt learning in federated learning (FL) settings. FedTPG leverages a text-driven prompt generator to adapt CLIP models to various image classification tasks, improving generalization performance across both seen and unseen classes. This method contrasts with existing methods like FedCoOp by using a prompt generator that transforms text embeddings into prompt vectors, which are then used to optimize the CLIP model. The paper presents comprehensive experimental evaluations demonstrating the method's superiority over existing federated prompt learning methods, although it shares similarities with recent works like FedMaple. The methodology is supported by a thorough experimental design, including comparisons with baseline methods and ablation studies, but the paper lacks in novelty and theoretical justification.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel approach to prompt learning in federated learning (FL) settings, specifically through the use of a text-driven prompt generator that transforms text embeddings into prompt vectors.
- The proposed method, FedTPG, demonstrates superior generalization performance over existing federated prompt learning methods, as evidenced by comprehensive experimental evaluations across nine diverse image classification datasets.
- The method is well-motivated and supported by a thorough experimental design, including comparisons with baseline methods and ablation studies that demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed approach.
- The paper is well-written, with clear explanations of the proposed method and its experimental setup, making it accessible and understandable.
- The experiments are well-designed, including comprehensive comparisons with baselines and ablation studies, which provide a robust evaluation of the proposed method.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks novelty as it shares similarities with recent works such as FedMaple, particularly in the use of a text-driven prompt generator and the methodology of generating prompt vectors.
- The theoretical justification for the proposed method is weak, with the paper relying heavily on empirical evidence without providing a strong theoretical foundation.
- There is a lack of discussion on the computational overhead and efficiency of the proposed method, which could be a significant concern in practical applications.
- The paper does not adequately address the scalability of the method in terms of the number of classes or clients, which could impact the generalizability of the results.
- The paper could benefit from a more detailed discussion on the limitations of the proposed method and potential directions for future research.

**Questions:**
- How does the proposed method, FedTPG, compare with other recent works such as FedMaple and Bulat et al. in terms of methodology and results?
- Can the authors provide a more detailed discussion on the scalability of the method in terms of the number of classes and clients, and how the results might vary with different sample sizes?
- How does the proposed method address the issue of overfitting to specific tasks, and what measures are in place to prevent this?
- Could the authors provide a more detailed discussion on the computational overhead and efficiency of the proposed method, particularly in comparison to other federated learning methods?
- How does the proposed method handle the issue of non-IID data distribution across clients, and what steps are taken to ensure that the results are robust across different data distributions?
