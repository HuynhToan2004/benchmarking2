 **Summary:**
The paper introduces GeneOH Diffusion, a novel method for denoising hand-object interaction sequences, focusing on refining hand trajectories to remove interaction artifacts. The approach involves a contact-centric HOI representation (GeneOH) and a domain-generalizable denoising scheme that projects noisy data samples from a whitened noise space to a clean data manifold. This method is evaluated on four datasets, demonstrating its effectiveness in handling various noise patterns and generalizing across different scenarios. The paper also explores the use of a diffusion-based denoising model and a canonical denoiser to handle different types of noise. However, concerns were raised about the clarity of the methodology, the lack of detailed experimental validation, and the fairness of the comparisons made with existing methods.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, making it easy to follow and understand the proposed methodologies and results.
- The authors have provided a detailed description of the proposed method, including a novel diffusion-based denoising model that projects noisy data from a whitened noise space to a clean data manifold.
- The method is evaluated on four datasets, demonstrating its effectiveness in handling various noise patterns and generalizing across different scenarios.
- The paper introduces a contact-centric HOI representation named GeneOH, which effectively captures the HOI process and facilitates generalization across diverse HOI scenarios.
- The denoising scheme, consisting of a canonical denoising model and a diffusion-based denoising strategy, is innovative and shows promise for various downstream applications.
- The authors have made their code available, enhancing the reproducibility and accessibility of the research.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed explanation of how the GeneOH representation is designed to facilitate generalization across various HOI scenarios.
- There is insufficient discussion on the choice of parameters and their impact on the results.
- The experimental validation is not detailed enough, with a lack of quantitative metrics and comparisons with other methods.
- The paper does not adequately address the issue of domain generalization, particularly the generalization to unseen interactions and noise patterns.
- The paper does not sufficiently compare the proposed method with existing methods, such as TOCH and ManipNet, which could provide a better understanding of its advantages and limitations.
- There are concerns about the fairness of the comparisons made, particularly the use of different training datasets for different methods.
- The paper could benefit from more detailed explanations and visualizations, such as video demonstrations and detailed visualizations of the denoising process.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors provide a more detailed explanation of how the GeneOH representation is designed to facilitate generalization across various HOI scenarios?
- How does the choice of parameters impact the results, and can the authors provide a sensitivity analysis or ablation studies on these parameters?
- Could the authors clarify the domain generalization capabilities of their method, particularly in handling unseen interactions and noise patterns?
- How does the proposed method compare with existing methods like TOCH and ManipNet, especially in terms of denoising quality and generalization?
- Can the authors provide more detailed quantitative metrics and comparisons with other methods to better validate the effectiveness of their approach?
- How does the proposed method handle different types of noise, and what are the implications for the denoising process?
- Can the authors provide more visualizations or video demonstrations to better illustrate the denoising process and the results?
