 **Summary:**
The paper investigates the relationship between graph generative models and triangle density, focusing on the concept of "overlap" to measure the diversity of generated graphs. It categorizes models into three types based on their edge dependencies: edge independent, node independent, and fully dependent. The paper introduces a new hierarchy of graph generative models and provides theoretical bounds on the number of triangles and other short-length cycles. It also introduces new generative models based on dense subgraph discovery and evaluates them against existing methods. The experiments demonstrate that these models can generate graphs with a high density of triangles while maintaining diversity. However, the paper is criticized for its limited evaluation scope, focusing only on triangle density and not considering other network properties.

**Strengths:**
- The paper provides a clear and well-written introduction to the topic of graph generative models, including a comprehensive overview of related work.
- The proposed hierarchy of graph generative models is well-motivated and theoretically justified, with the introduction of new generative models based on dense subgraph discovery.
- The paper introduces a novel evaluation framework for graph generative models, emphasizing the importance of model-generated graph overlap to ensure both accuracy and edge-diversity.
- The authors provide theoretical bounds on the number of triangles and other short-length cycles, which are crucial for understanding the behavior of graph generative models.
- The paper is well-organized, making it easy to follow, and the proposed models are simple and interpretable, which is beneficial for understanding complex concepts.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper primarily focuses on triangle density, which may not fully capture the complexity and diversity of real-world graph datasets.
- The evaluation is limited to a few datasets, which may not be sufficient to demonstrate the generalizability of the proposed models.
- The paper could benefit from a broader discussion on the role of edge dependency in graph generative models and how it impacts the diversity and accuracy of generated graphs.
- The presentation of the paper could be improved to enhance readability and clarity, particularly in the definition and explanation of key concepts and the structure of proofs.
- There is a lack of detailed discussion on the limitations of the proposed models and how they compare to existing methods in terms of computational efficiency and scalability.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors provide more details on the datasets used in the experiments, including their characteristics and why they were chosen?
- How do the authors address the potential limitations of their models, such as scalability and computational efficiency?
- Could the authors elaborate on the proofs, particularly the proof of Theorem 2 and the use of Shearer's inequality?
- How do the authors define "triangle" in their models, and is it the same as "triangle" in graph theory?
- Could the authors clarify the use of "overlap" in the context of their models and how it is defined and measured?
- How does the paper address the issue of model memorization, and what measures are in place to prevent this?
