 **Summary:**
The paper explores the emergence of temporal references in communication among agents through a referential game setup. It introduces a novel temporal prediction loss to enhance agents' ability to communicate about past observations, which is a significant departure from traditional approaches that rely solely on explicit incentives. The paper demonstrates that the sequential LSTM architecture is crucial for the emergence of temporal references, showing that these references can aid in generalization across different environments. The study also introduces a metric to quantify the use of temporal references and provides a detailed analysis of their emergence and impact on communication efficiency.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, clear, and easy to follow, with a clear presentation of the problem and the proposed solution.
- The introduction of temporal references into emergent communication is a novel and interesting concept, potentially applicable to various domains.
- The paper includes a detailed analysis of the emergence of temporal references, which is a significant contribution to the field.
- The use of a simple yet effective metric to measure the use of temporal references is a valuable addition to the study.
- The authors demonstrate that the sequential LSTM architecture is crucial for the emergence of temporal references, which is a significant finding.
- The paper is well-motivated, with a clear problem statement and a well-defined objective, making it accessible and understandable.

**Weaknesses:**
