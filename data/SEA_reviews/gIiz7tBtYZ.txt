 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel approach to solving the general optimal transport (OT) problem using a neural network-based algorithm, focusing on a class-guided cost functional that preserves class-wise structure. This methodology is demonstrated to outperform existing approaches on image datasets like MNIST and FashionMNIST. The paper provides theoretical error analysis and empirical validation, showing that the proposed method is effective in handling continuous OT problems with general cost functionals. It also discusses the theoretical and practical implications of the proposed method, including its application in transfer learning and domain adaptation.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, clear, and easy to follow, with a clear motivation and a well-structured presentation of the content.
- The proposed method is novel, particularly in its application of a neural network-based algorithm to solve the general OT problem, which is a significant contribution to the field.
- The paper provides a theoretical analysis of the error in the proposed method, which is crucial for understanding the reliability and accuracy of the proposed approach.
- The experimental results are promising, demonstrating the effectiveness of the proposed method in practical applications, such as dataset transfer and image classification.
- The paper addresses an important and relevant problem in the field of optimal transport, which is of significant interest and relevance to the community.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed discussion on the limitations of the proposed method, which could help in understanding its applicability and potential challenges.
- The experimental evaluation is somewhat limited, focusing primarily on MNIST and FashionMNIST datasets, which might not adequately demonstrate the generalizability of the proposed method.
- The paper could benefit from a more comprehensive comparison with existing methods, especially those that also use neural networks for OT problems, to clearly establish the advantages of the proposed method.
- There are some minor typographical and formatting errors that need correction for clarity and professionalism.
- The paper does not sufficiently address the computational efficiency and scalability of the proposed method, which is crucial for practical applications in high-dimensional spaces.

**Questions:**
- Can the proposed method be applied to other types of data beyond images, such as text or audio?
- How does the proposed method compare to other neural network-based approaches for solving OT problems, particularly in terms of computational efficiency and accuracy?
- Could you provide more details on the training and inference times for the proposed method, especially in comparison to other methods?
- Is there a plan to extend the experimental evaluation to include more diverse datasets to better demonstrate the generalizability of the proposed method?
- How does the proposed method handle the class-guided cost functional in more complex scenarios, such as when the classes are not balanced or when the data distributions are not balanced?
- Can the method be adapted to handle other types of regularization terms beyond the one used in the experiments, and how does this affect the performance of the method?
