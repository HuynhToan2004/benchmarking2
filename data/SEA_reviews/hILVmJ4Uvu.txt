 **Summary:**
The paper introduces TWOSOME, a novel framework that leverages Large Language Models (LLMs) in reinforcement learning (RL) to enhance decision-making capabilities in embodied environments. TWOSOME addresses the challenge of LLMs generating invalid actions by utilizing LLMs to compute action probabilities based on textual observations and actions, thus ensuring validity. It also incorporates normalization techniques to balance action probabilities, enhancing the stability and robustness of the policies. The framework is evaluated against baseline methods such as PPO and SayCan in environments like Overcooked and VirtualHome, showing superior sample efficiency and performance. However, concerns were raised about the generalizability of the method to more complex or visually rich environments and the incremental nature of the contribution compared to existing works.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written and easy to follow, with clear descriptions of the proposed method and its components.
- The idea of using LLMs as decision-making agents in embodied environments is innovative and addresses a significant challenge in the field.
- The proposed normalization techniques, such as token normalization and word normalization, are simple yet effective in improving the stability and robustness of the policies.
- The paper conducts extensive experiments to evaluate the performance of the proposed method, demonstrating superior sample efficiency and performance compared to baseline methods.
- The methodology of using LLMs to compute action probabilities based on textual observations and actions is a novel approach that ensures the validity of actions generated by LLMs.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a comprehensive discussion on the limitations of the proposed method, particularly in terms of its generalizability to more complex or visually rich environments.
- The novelty of the proposed method is questioned, as similar approaches using LLMs for decision-making in embodied environments have been explored in previous works.
- The paper does not adequately address the issue of overfitting, which is a significant concern in using LLMs for decision-making tasks.
- The experimental setup is limited to simple environments like Overcooked and VirtualHome, which may not adequately test the method's scalability or robustness in more complex scenarios.
- The paper does not provide a detailed comparison with other LLM-based methods, which could help in understanding the relative strengths and weaknesses of the proposed method.
- The paper does not sufficiently discuss the computational efficiency of the method, which is a critical factor in practical applications.

**Questions:**
- Could you provide a more detailed discussion on the limitations of the proposed method and how these limitations affect the generalizability of the findings?
- How does the proposed method compare to other LLM-based methods in terms of performance, computational efficiency, and scalability?
- Can you elaborate on the choice of environments for testing the method and how these environments were selected?
- How does the method handle the issue of overfitting, especially in complex or visually rich environments?
- Could you provide more details on the computational efficiency of the method, including the training time and the inference speed?
- How does the method perform when the LLM is fine-tuned on the task, and what are the implications of this fine-tuning on the method's effectiveness?
