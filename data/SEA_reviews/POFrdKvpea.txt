 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel NeRF compression framework, ACRF, which leverages 3D data compression techniques to reduce the size of explicit NeRF models. The methodology includes view-independent pruning, importance-guided feature encoding, and importance-prioritized entropy modeling. ACRF and ACRF-F models are presented, each striking a balance between compression performance and encoding time. The framework demonstrates superior performance over existing methods in both synthetic and real-world datasets, showcasing a balance between rate-distortion and encoding time. However, the paper has been critiqued for its limited novelty and lack of comprehensive experimental validation, particularly in terms of the generalizability of the method across different NeRF architectures and its computational efficiency.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-organized and easy to follow, making complex concepts accessible to readers.
- The proposed method is technically sound, combining pruning, feature encoding, and entropy modeling, which are all effective in reducing the size of NeRF models.
- The method achieves high compression rates with negligible performance loss, as demonstrated by experiments on both synthetic and real-world datasets.
- The paper includes a comprehensive analysis of the task of NeRF compression, reformulating it as a 3D data compression problem, which is a novel approach.
- The method is compatible with typical explicit representations, making it adaptable to various NeRF models.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed comparison with other NeRF compression methods, which could provide a more robust evaluation of the proposed method's performance.
- The method's novelty is questioned, as it appears to be a combination of existing techniques without significant innovation.
- The computational efficiency of the method, particularly during encoding, is not thoroughly discussed, which could impact its practical applicability.
- The paper does not adequately address the generalizability of the method across different NeRF architectures, which limits its applicability.
- The method's performance in terms of rendering quality is not convincingly superior to existing methods, and the quantitative results do not clearly demonstrate this.
- The paper lacks a thorough discussion on the limitations and potential negative societal impacts of the proposed method.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors provide a more detailed comparison with other NeRF compression methods, including the performance of ACRF-F on the Synthetic-NeRF dataset?
- How does the method perform when applied to different NeRF architectures, and can it be adapted to handle other types of NeRF representations, such as those based on meshes?
- Can the authors clarify the computational efficiency of the method, particularly during encoding, and discuss potential optimizations to improve this aspect?
- How does the method handle the compression of different types of NeRF models, such as those with varying densities or resolutions?
- Could the authors provide more details on the encoding time and memory requirements of the method, especially in comparison to other state-of-the-art methods?
- How does the method perform when applied to dynamic or real-time NeRF compression, and what are the implications for the quality and speed of the compression process?
