 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel method for meta-learning reinforcement learning (RL) algorithms by incorporating temporal information, specifically the training horizon, into the input features of LPO and LPG. This modification allows for the discovery of RL algorithms that adapt to different training horizons, improving performance over traditional methods. The authors propose using evolutionary strategies for meta-optimization, which they claim can effectively handle long-horizon tasks. The paper provides empirical evidence of the effectiveness of the proposed methods across various tasks and environments, including out-of-distribution evaluations. Despite its innovative approach, the paper is criticized for its limited scope in terms of the number of environments tested and the lack of detailed theoretical analysis.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, clear, and easy to follow, with a clear motivation for the research.
- The proposed method of incorporating temporal information into meta-learning for RL is novel and potentially significant, particularly in the context of long-horizon tasks.
- The empirical results are strong, demonstrating the effectiveness of the proposed method across various tasks and environments.
- The paper provides a detailed analysis of the learned objective functions, which is crucial for understanding the behavior of the proposed methods.
- The use of evolutionary strategies for meta-optimization is highlighted as a promising approach for handling long-horizon tasks.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a comprehensive theoretical analysis, particularly in explaining why the proposed method works and what the theoretical guarantees are.
- The scope of the experiments is limited, with only a few environments used for testing, which may not sufficiently demonstrate the generalizability of the findings.
- The paper does not adequately address the limitations of the method, such as the lack of robustness guarantees and the potential for overfitting.
- The novelty of the method is questioned as it primarily involves adding a new input variable without significant modification to the existing methods.
- The paper could benefit from more detailed comparisons with other meta-learning RL methods and a broader range of environments to better establish the effectiveness of the proposed method.

**Questions:**
- Can you elaborate on how the proposed method adapts to the training horizon and how this adaptation is reflected in the learned objective functions?
- How does the use of evolutionary strategies for meta-optimization specifically benefit the handling of long-horizon tasks?
- In the context of the experiments, how were the training horizons chosen, and were there any ablations conducted to determine the optimal horizon?
- Given the potential for overfitting, how do you ensure that the learned policies generalize well to unseen environments?
- Could you provide more details on the theoretical underpinnings of the proposed method, particularly in terms of the convergence guarantees and the robustness of the learned policies?
