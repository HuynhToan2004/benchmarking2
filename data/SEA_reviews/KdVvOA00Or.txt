 **Summary:**
The paper addresses the target shift problem in regression settings, where the marginal distribution of the target variable changes between the source and target domains, while the conditional distribution of features given the target variable remains constant. The authors propose a nonparametric regularized method called ReTaSA to estimate the importance weight function, which is used to adapt the predictive model for the target domain via weighted empirical risk minimization. The method is theoretically justified, providing statistical consistency and convergence rate guarantees for the importance weight function estimator. The paper includes extensive numerical studies on both synthetic and real-world datasets, demonstrating the effectiveness of the proposed method.

**Strengths:**
- The paper addresses a significant and relevant problem in the field of continuous target shift, which is less explored compared to classification tasks.
- The proposed method is novel and theoretically justified, with a focus on the importance weight function estimator which is shown to be statistically consistent.
- The paper is well-written, making the theoretical analysis accessible and the methodology clear.
- Extensive numerical studies are conducted, demonstrating the effectiveness of the proposed method on both synthetic and real-world datasets.
- The paper provides a theoretical guarantee for the importance weight function estimator, which is a first in this context.
- The method is simple, requiring only the solution of a linear system, and is easy to use.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a clear explanation of the setting and the assumptions, particularly in how the method handles the conditional distribution of features given the target variable remaining constant between domains.
- The paper could benefit from a more detailed discussion on the convergence rate of the importance weight function estimator and its implications for practical applications.
- The paper does not include a comparison with other existing methods for handling the target shift problem in regression settings, which could provide a more comprehensive evaluation of the proposed method.
- The paper does not address the generalizability of the proposed method to different types of regression models or the impact of different types of regression models on the method's performance.
- There is a lack of discussion on the computational complexity and scalability of the proposed method, which could be a significant concern for practical applications.
- The paper does not adequately address the potential limitations or scenarios where the proposed method might not perform optimally, which could help in understanding the method's applicability and robustness.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors provide a clearer explanation of the setting and assumptions, particularly how the method handles the conditional distribution of features given the target variable remaining constant between domains?
- How does the convergence rate of the importance weight function estimator impact the practical application of the method?
- Could the authors provide a more detailed comparison with other existing methods for handling the target shift problem in regression settings?
- How does the proposed method perform in different types of regression models, and are there any specific types of regression models that the method is particularly well-suited for?
- Can the authors discuss the computational complexity and scalability of the proposed method, and how these factors might affect the method's practical applicability?
- Are there any potential limitations or scenarios where the proposed method might not perform optimally, and how might these be addressed or mitigated?
