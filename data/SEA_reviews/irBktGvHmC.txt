 **Summary:**
The paper introduces Syflow, a method designed to identify subgroups within data that exhibit distinct characteristics in the target property distribution. Syflow employs a differentiable objective function to optimize the membership probability of samples in subgroups, which is crucial for understanding and treating diseases. The method leverages neural spline flows to model the subgroup distribution and uses a size-corrected KL divergence to measure the dissimilarity between the subgroup and the overall population distribution. The approach is tested on both synthetic and real-world datasets, demonstrating its scalability and ability to discover subgroups with unique characteristics. However, concerns are raised regarding the clarity and depth of the experimental results, the lack of comparative analysis with existing methods, and the generalizability of the findings.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, clear, and easy to follow, making complex concepts accessible to a broad audience.
- The methodology introduced is innovative, leveraging neural spline flows to model the subgroup distribution, which is a novel approach in the field.
- The paper demonstrates the scalability of the proposed method, particularly in handling large datasets and complex feature spaces, which is a significant advantage over traditional methods.
- The application of the method in real-world scenarios, such as medical diagnosis, is highlighted, showcasing its practical utility.
- The use of a differentiable objective function allows for efficient optimization and the discovery of subgroups with unique characteristics in the target property distribution.

**Weaknesses:**
- The experimental results are not convincingly presented, with figures and tables that lack clarity and depth. The absence of a running time comparison and the lack of detailed results on the size and complexity of the subgroups discovered are significant drawbacks.
- The paper lacks a comprehensive comparison with existing methods, particularly those that use similar approaches or tackle similar problems. This makes it difficult to assess the novelty and effectiveness of the proposed method.
- There is a lack of discussion on the limitations of the method, which is crucial for understanding its applicability and potential drawbacks.
