 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel approach to solving polynomial optimization problems using a neural network architecture that is equivariant to the special linear group SL(2,R). This architecture is designed to handle the non-compact nature of SL(2,R) and is compared against traditional methods like semidefinite programming (SDP) and other neural network architectures. The paper also explores the limitations of this approach, particularly in terms of universality, and proposes an equivariant architecture that approximates the desired output without adhering to the full SL(2,R) symmetry. The methodology is supported by theoretical analysis and empirical results, demonstrating the practical advantages of the proposed approach.
