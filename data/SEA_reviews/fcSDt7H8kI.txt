 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel exploration technique in reinforcement learning (RL) by proposing a method that encourages the agent to take actions that minimize the state-action value function, termed "minimax TD learning." This method is supported by theoretical analysis, particularly in the context of high-dimensional state representation MDPs, and is evaluated against existing baselines such as epsilon-greedy and noisy networks. The experiments are conducted across various arcade games, demonstrating some improvements in sample efficiency. However, the paper's theoretical contributions are questioned, and the empirical results, while showing some improvements, are not convincingly superior to existing methods.

**Strengths:**
- The paper addresses a significant problem in deep reinforcement learning (DRL) by focusing on efficient experience collection, which is crucial for improving sample efficiency.
- The proposed method is simple, intuitive, and supported by theoretical analysis, which enhances its credibility and potential applicability.
- The experiments conducted across various arcade games demonstrate some improvements in sample efficiency, which is a critical aspect of DRL.
- The paper is well-written, making it easy to follow, and the theoretical analysis is well-explained, providing a solid foundation for the proposed method.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed discussion on how the proposed method compares with existing baselines, particularly in terms of computational cost and sample efficiency.
- The theoretical contributions, such as the proofs and definitions used, are not convincingly novel or significantly different from existing methods.
- The empirical results, while showing some improvements, are not consistently superior to existing methods, and the improvements are not statistically significant in some cases.
- The paper does not sufficiently discuss the limitations of the proposed method, which could hinder a full understanding of its applicability and effectiveness.
- The experimental setup and results are not sufficiently robust or comprehensive, with insufficient details provided on the experimental setup, which makes it difficult to replicate or verify the results.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors provide a more detailed comparison of the proposed method with existing baselines, particularly in terms of computational cost and sample efficiency?
- How does the proposed method compare with other exploration methods like UCB, especially in scenarios where it might not perform as well?
- Could the authors clarify the proofs and definitions used in the theoretical analysis, particularly in relation to the assumptions made and their validity in practical scenarios?
- How do the authors address the concerns regarding the statistical significance of the improvements observed in the empirical results?
- Can the authors provide more details on the experimental setup, including the specific parameters used and the methodology employed, to facilitate reproducibility and verification of the results?
