 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel method for text-to-image generation, focusing on a regularization-free approach that addresses the issue of overfitting in text-guided image generation. The proposed method, named Fusion Sampling, modifies the sampling process to enhance the integration of textual and image-derived conditions. This method is evaluated against several existing models, including stable diffusion, textual inversion, DreamBooth, and E4T, demonstrating its effectiveness in preserving fine-grained details and generating high-quality images. The paper also explores the impact of different sampling methods and regularization techniques on the generation process.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel regularization-free approach for text-to-image generation, which is both innovative and effective in preserving fine-grained details.
- The method is well-explained, with clear mathematical formulations and intuitive explanations that aid in understanding the proposed techniques.
- Extensive experiments are conducted, demonstrating the effectiveness of the proposed method across various datasets and conditions.
- The paper is well-written, with clear and detailed explanations of the methodology and experimental setups, making it accessible and easy to follow.
- The proposed method shows potential for real-world applications by allowing for the generation of high-quality images with minimal training time.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed discussion on the limitations of the proposed method, particularly in terms of its generalizability and applicability to different types of images or domains.
- There is a noticeable absence of comparisons with recent state-of-the-art models, such as "Mix-and-Match Diffusion" and "Let's Make a Deal: Text-to-Image Generation with Incomplete User Specifications," which could provide a more comprehensive evaluation of the proposed method.
- The paper does not include quantitative results, which could strengthen the argument for the method's effectiveness.
- The paper's presentation could be improved with better formatting and organization, particularly in the placement of tables and figures.
- The paper does not adequately address the issue of overfitting, especially in relation to the regularization techniques used in the baseline models.
- The paper does not sufficiently discuss the computational efficiency and resource requirements of the proposed method, which could be a significant concern for practical applications.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors clarify how the proposed method handles the issue of overfitting, especially in comparison to the regularization techniques used in the baseline models?
- What is the rationale behind the choice of the regularization parameter λ in the proposed method, and how does it impact the performance of the model?
- How does the proposed method perform when applied to different types of images or domains, and what are the potential limitations or challenges in such applications?
- Could the authors provide more details on the computational efficiency and resource requirements of the proposed method, especially in comparison to other state-of-the-art methods?
- In the context of the ablation studies, could the authors explain the reasons for the observed performance differences between different stages of Fusion Sampling?
