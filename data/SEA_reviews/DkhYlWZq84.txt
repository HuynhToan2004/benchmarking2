 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel task called protein captioning, where the goal is to generate a natural language description of a protein sequence. This is achieved through a method called Protein-to-TextGPT (P2T-GPT), which involves a protein encoder and a causal captioning decoder. The protein encoder uses a combination of a convolutional neural network (CNN) and a transformer to encode the protein sequence, while the decoder generates text descriptions using a cross-attention mechanism. The paper also presents a dataset, ProteinCap, which contains 94,454 protein-text pairs, and demonstrates the effectiveness of P2T-GPT in this task. The methodology is evaluated using various metrics and shown to outperform a self-attention baseline, although the novelty of the approach is questioned due to its similarity to existing methods in protein representation learning.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel task called protein captioning, which is a significant contribution to the field of protein representation learning.
- It proposes a novel method called Protein-to-TextGPT (P2T-GPT), which effectively models the short-range and long-range dependencies of amino acids and translates protein sequences into comprehensive textual descriptions.
- The paper collects a large-scale ProteinCap dataset, which contains more than 94k protein-text pairs, and demonstrates the effectiveness of the proposed approach through extensive experiments.
- The introduction of a new vocabulary for the protein function description is a notable contribution, as it helps in accurately capturing the protein's information.
- The paper is well-written, making it easy to follow, and the methodology is clearly explained, enhancing the reproducibility of the research.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a comprehensive comparison with state-of-the-art models in protein representation learning, which could have provided a clearer understanding of the novelty and effectiveness of the proposed method.
- The dataset used in the study is relatively small (94k protein-text pairs), which might not be sufficient for training large models, and the quality of the dataset is questionable, with potential issues such as inaccurate protein descriptions and protein-text pairing.
- The paper does not adequately address the limitations of the proposed method, which could include the generalizability of the method to different protein types and the potential impact of protein length and sequence complexity on the performance.
- The novelty of the proposed method is questioned due to its similarity to existing methods in protein representation learning, particularly in the use of convolutional neural networks (CNN) and transformers.
- The paper does not sufficiently discuss the limitations of the proposed method, which could include the generalizability of the method to different protein types and the potential impact of protein length and sequence complexity on the performance.

**Questions:**
- How does the proposed method compare to state-of-the-art models in protein representation learning, particularly in terms of performance and novelty?
- What are the specific challenges in applying the proposed method to different types of proteins, and how does the method handle variations in protein length and sequence complexity?
- Can the authors provide more details on the dataset, including its quality, the process of collecting and filtering the data, and the potential impact of inaccurate protein descriptions and protein-text pairing on the results?
- How does the proposed method handle the issue of inaccurate protein descriptions and protein-text pairing, and what steps were taken to ensure the reliability and accuracy of the dataset?
- Can the authors discuss the potential impact of protein length and sequence complexity on the performance of the proposed method, and what modifications or improvements could be made to address these challenges?
