 **Summary:**
The paper addresses the one-to-many mapping problem in point cloud completion networks, proposing a novel consistency loss to ensure consistent completion solutions for incomplete point clouds from the same source. This loss function is designed to mitigate contradictory supervision signals and enhance the generalization capability of point cloud completion networks. The effectiveness of the proposed method is demonstrated through experiments on various benchmarks, showing improvements in completion performance and generalization. Despite its innovative approach, the paper is criticized for its limited technical novelty and the lack of comprehensive experimental validation, particularly in terms of training time and comparisons with more recent methods.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, easy to follow, and the problem addressed is well-motivated.
- The proposed method is simple and effective, and the experimental results are impressive, showing improvements in completion performance and generalization.
- The paper is well-organized, with clear explanations of the proposed method and its implementation.
- The introduction of the consistency loss is a novel approach that enhances the training of point cloud completion networks.
- The paper provides a good discussion on the impact of the one-to-many mapping problem on point cloud completion networks and proposes a simple yet effective solution.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a thorough literature review, particularly in the area of point cloud completion, which could enhance the understanding of the proposed method's contributions.
- The technical novelty of the paper is limited, as the concept of consistency loss is not new, and the experimental results are not sufficiently comprehensive, particularly in terms of training time and comparisons with more recent methods.
- The paper does not adequately address the computational cost of the proposed method, which could be a significant concern for real-world applications.
- The paper does not provide a detailed analysis of the impact of the proposed method on inference time or memory usage, which are critical factors in practical applications.
- The paper could benefit from a more detailed discussion on the limitations of the proposed method and potential future research directions.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors provide a more detailed analysis of the impact of the proposed method on inference time and memory usage?
- How does the proposed method compare in terms of computational efficiency and effectiveness with other recent methods in the field of point cloud completion?
- Could the authors clarify the training time and memory usage of the proposed method compared to other methods in the literature?
- Is there a possibility to extend the proposed method to handle more complex scenarios, such as incomplete point clouds from multiple objects?
- Could the authors discuss the potential of applying the proposed method to other tasks beyond point cloud completion, such as segmentation or registration?
- How does the proposed method handle cases where the incomplete point clouds are not sampled from the same object, and what are the implications for the consistency of the completion solutions?
