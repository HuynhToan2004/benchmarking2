 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel framework for learning abstract state representations in reinforcement learning (RL) environments, focusing on the creation of abstract Markov Decision Processes (MDPs) that can effectively simulate trajectories for goal-based planning. This approach utilizes dynamics-preserving abstractions and a method based on information maximization to ensure that the value of the abstract MDP closely resembles that of the original MDP. The paper also provides theoretical guarantees for the effectiveness of this approach, including the preservation of expected values under the grounding function. Empirical evaluations are conducted in goal-based navigation environments, demonstrating the framework's ability to improve sample efficiency in planning and learning.

**Strengths:**
- The paper presents a novel approach to learning abstract state representations, which is crucial for improving the sample efficiency of planning and learning in reinforcement learning (RL).
- The theoretical analysis is robust, providing a solid foundation for the proposed methodology.
- The paper is well-written, making it accessible and understandable, and the inclusion of code for reproducibility is commendable.
- The methodology is applicable to a variety of domains and tasks, and the empirical results demonstrate its effectiveness.
- The paper provides a theoretical guarantee for the proposed method, which is a significant contribution to the field.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed discussion on the limitations of the proposed method, which is crucial for understanding its applicability and effectiveness in various scenarios.
- The empirical evaluations are limited to only two domains, which may not fully demonstrate the generalizability of the method across different environments.
- The paper could benefit from a more comprehensive comparison with existing methods, especially those that also use abstract MDPs for planning.
- The paper does not address how the proposed method handles the trade-off between computational efficiency and model accuracy, which is a critical aspect of any learning algorithm.
- The clarity of the paper could be improved, particularly in sections where the content is dense and hard to follow.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors clarify how the method handles the trade-off between computational efficiency and model accuracy, especially in scenarios where the model's accuracy is critical?
- How does the method perform when the abstraction is not dynamics-preserving? What are the implications of this on the effectiveness of the planning process?
- Can the authors provide more details on the computational efficiency of the proposed method, particularly in terms of the computational resources required for training and inference?
- How does the method compare to other existing methods that use abstract MDPs for planning? What are the key differences and advantages of the proposed method?
- Could the authors elaborate on the limitations of the proposed method and provide examples or scenarios where the method may not be effective?
