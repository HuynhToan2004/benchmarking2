 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel black-box attack on graph neural networks (GNNs), leveraging the Silhouette Score to measure the difficulty of node classification on graphs. The attack, named SheAttack, aims to minimize the Silhouette Score by adjusting the graph structure and node embeddings, which can be effective in both homophilic and heterophilic settings. The methodology includes a theoretical analysis and empirical validation on various datasets, demonstrating the effectiveness of the proposed approach. However, concerns were raised about the novelty of the attack method and the clarity of some experimental results.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, with a clear introduction and a straightforward methodology.
- The use of Silhouette Score as a loss function for measuring the difficulty of node classification on graphs is innovative and effective.
- The paper provides theoretical analysis and empirical validation, demonstrating the effectiveness of the proposed approach.
- The methodology is adaptable to heterophilic graphs, which is a significant advantage over many existing methods.
- The paper is structured well, making it easy to follow and understand.

**Weaknesses:**
- The novelty of the attack method is questionable as it primarily uses existing concepts like the Silhouette Score and clustering.
- The paper lacks a detailed comparison with state-of-the-art methods, which could undermine the perceived effectiveness of the proposed approach.
- The experimental results are not convincing, particularly in the heterophilic settings, where the performance of the attack is weak.
- The paper does not adequately address the scalability of the proposed method, which is crucial for practical applications.
- The paper does not sufficiently discuss the limitations of the proposed method, which could affect its applicability in real-world scenarios.
- The paper could benefit from a more comprehensive discussion on related works and a more detailed analysis of the experimental results.

**Questions:**
- How does the proposed method compare with other state-of-the-art methods, particularly in terms of effectiveness and efficiency?
- Can the authors provide more details on the experimental setup, such as the choice of hyperparameters and the impact of different clustering methods on the results?
- How does the proposed method perform in different types of graphs, especially those with varying degrees of homophily and heterophily?
- Could the authors elaborate on the theoretical underpinnings of the Silhouette Score and its application in the context of graph attacks?
- What are the practical implications of the proposed method, and how does it compare with existing defense mechanisms against graph attacks?
