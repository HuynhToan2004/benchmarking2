 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel method for grouped kernel pruning (GKP) that allows for dynamic operations within Conv2d(groups), enhancing the efficiency and effectiveness of the pruning process. This approach, named LeanFlex-GKP, employs a flexible group count to simplify the pruning process and reduce the complexity of the GKP procedure. The method is evaluated against various existing methods, showing improvements in performance and efficiency. The authors claim that LeanFlex-GKP is a hassle-free, data-agnostic method that requires minimal hyperparameter tuning, making it user-friendly and efficient. However, the paper is criticized for its limited novelty and the lack of significant improvement over existing methods.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, clear, and easy to follow, with a comprehensive discussion on the background and related works.
- The proposed method is simple, effective, and efficient, with a focus on reducing complexity and dependency requirements.
- The paper provides a comprehensive evaluation of the proposed method, comparing it against a large number of existing methods and demonstrating improvements in performance and efficiency.
- The methodology is straightforward, with clear figures and detailed descriptions, making it accessible and understandable.
- The paper is well-organized and provides a thorough analysis of the proposed method, including a detailed discussion on the method's effectiveness and efficiency.

**Weaknesses:**
- The novelty of the proposed method is limited, as it primarily combines existing techniques such as k-means++ and geometric median.
- The paper lacks a detailed discussion on the limitations and challenges of the proposed method, which could help in understanding its applicability and effectiveness in different scenarios.
- There is a lack of comparative analysis with state-of-the-art pruning methods such as OTOv2, which could provide insights into the effectiveness and efficiency of the proposed method.
- The paper does not provide a detailed analysis of the computational complexity and memory requirements of the proposed method, which could be crucial for understanding its practical applicability.
- The paper does not adequately address the scalability of the proposed method, particularly in terms of its applicability to larger and more complex models.
- The paper lacks a discussion on the potential negative societal impacts of the proposed method, which could be important for understanding the broader implications of the research.

**Questions:**
