 **Summary:**
The paper introduces SpikeCLIP, a novel spiking neural network (SNN) architecture that integrates both image and text encoders, inspired by the CLIP model. The network undergoes a two-stage training process, including alignment pre-training and dual-loss fine-tuning, to enhance its performance in multimodal tasks. SpikeCLIP is evaluated on various image classification datasets, showing competitive results compared to traditional deep neural networks. The paper also discusses the energy efficiency of SpikeCLIP, although the evaluation of its energy consumption is not convincingly demonstrated. The experimental results are supported by extensive ablation studies, which help in understanding the network's behavior under different conditions.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel spiking neural network (SNN) architecture, SpikeCLIP, which integrates both image and text encoders, a significant advancement in the field of SNNs.
- The paper is well-written, clear, and easy to follow, with detailed explanations of the methodology and experimental results.
- The authors provide a comprehensive evaluation of the proposed method, including extensive ablation studies and comparisons with state-of-the-art methods.
- The experimental results show that SpikeCLIP achieves comparable performance to deep neural networks while significantly reducing energy consumption, demonstrating the potential of SNNs in energy-efficient computing.
- The paper includes a thorough evaluation of the proposed method, including extensive ablation studies and comparisons with state-of-the-art methods, which supports the validity and reliability of the results.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed discussion on the limitations and potential negative societal impacts of the proposed method.
- The experimental results do not convincingly demonstrate the claimed energy efficiency of SpikeCLIP, as the energy consumption comparisons are not convincingly presented.
- The paper does not adequately discuss the challenges in training SNNs, which could limit the generalizability of the results.
- The paper does not provide a detailed comparison of SpikeCLIP with existing SNNs and other state-of-the-art methods, which could help in understanding the relative performance of the proposed method.
- The paper does not sufficiently address the computational efficiency of SpikeCLIP, which is crucial for its practical deployment.
- The paper lacks a detailed discussion on the potential negative societal impacts of the proposed method, which could limit its applicability in certain domains.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors provide a more detailed discussion on the challenges in training SNNs and how these were addressed in the current study?
- How does the proposed method compare to other state-of-the-art methods in terms of computational efficiency and energy consumption?
- Could the authors provide a more detailed comparison of SpikeCLIP with existing SNNs and other state-of-the-art methods to help in understanding the relative performance of the proposed method?
- How does the proposed method handle the limitations and potential negative societal impacts discussed in the paper?
- Could the authors clarify the computational efficiency of SpikeCLIP and provide a more detailed comparison with other methods in terms of computational efficiency and energy consumption?
- How does the proposed method perform in terms of generalization to other datasets and tasks beyond those evaluated in the study?
