 **Summary:**
The paper investigates the trade-off between accuracy and fairness in binary classification tasks under various data restrictions. It employs a Bayesian oracle to model the joint distribution of sensitive attributes, class labels, and feature vectors, approximating it using vector quantization. The study explores different fairness definitions and their impact on the trade-off, analyzing scenarios where sensitive attributes are or are not available. The paper proposes a framework to quantify the trade-offs among different fairness notions and their distributional dependencies, supported by experiments on three datasets. Despite its innovative approach, the paper faces criticism for its experimental setup, which does not adequately compare with existing methods and lacks a detailed discussion on the implications of the results.
