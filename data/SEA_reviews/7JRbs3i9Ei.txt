 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a machine learning-based toolkit for predicting the degradation activity of PROTAC molecules using a variety of datasets and models. It curates open-source PROTAC data from PROTAC-Pedia and PROTAC-DB, standardizes it, and develops a Python toolkit with a configurable PyTorch dataset class for processing PROTAC features. The models use different molecular representations such as Morgan fingerprints, MACCS keys, and graph encodings from chemical graphs. The paper evaluates the models using different metrics like accuracy and ROC-AUC and compares them to existing methods like DeepPROTACs. Despite the potential of the toolkit, the paper suffers from several issues including lack of novelty, clarity in methodological descriptions, and limited evaluation metrics.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-organized and easy to follow, making it accessible to a broad audience.
- It introduces a new open-source toolkit for predicting the degradation effectiveness of PROTAC molecules, which is a significant contribution to the field.
- The methodology is clearly described, and the paper provides a detailed description of the data curation process, which is crucial for understanding the dataset's quality.
- The paper proposes a new method for predicting the degradation activity of PROTAC molecules using machine learning, which could potentially accelerate the discovery process.
- The toolkit developed is designed to be flexible and customizable, allowing users to incorporate additional features as needed.
- The paper includes a comprehensive evaluation of the models, comparing their performance against existing methods like DeepPROTACs.

**Weaknesses:**
