 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel method for training generalist agents that can handle complex tasks by integrating a hierarchical diffusion policy with a low-level goal-conditioned policy. This approach, termed Language Control Diffusion (LCD), leverages a diffusion model to generate high-level policies that are conditioned on language and a low-level policy to execute these plans in pixel-based control environments. The method is evaluated on the CALVIN benchmark and demonstrates improved performance over existing methods, achieving a 88.7% success rate across 34 tasks. The paper also discusses the computational efficiency and scalability of the LCD model, showing significant improvements in inference speed and training time.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written and easy to follow, with clear explanations of the proposed method and its components.
- The method introduces a novel approach by integrating a diffusion model with a low-level policy, which is a significant advancement in the field of language-conditioned robotics.
- The empirical results are strong, demonstrating the effectiveness of the method in achieving high success rates and improving inference speed.
- The paper is original in its application of diffusion models to control tasks, showing a creative approach to addressing the challenges of long-horizon planning in complex environments.
- The inclusion of a theoretical analysis in the appendix provides a deeper understanding of the method's theoretical underpinnings.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper could benefit from a more detailed explanation of how the diffusion model and the low-level policy are integrated, particularly in the context of the CALVIN benchmark.
- There is a lack of comparison with other state-of-the-art methods, such as those that use different types of diffusion models or different low-level policies.
- The paper does not adequately address the scalability of the method to longer horizons or more complex tasks, which could be a significant limitation for practical applications.
- The method's reliance on a pre-trained goal-conditioned policy encoder raises questions about the generalizability of the approach to new tasks or environments.
- The paper could benefit from a more thorough discussion of the limitations of the method, including potential issues with generalization and the scalability of the approach.

**Questions:**
- Could you clarify the integration of the diffusion model and the low-level policy in the context of the CALVIN benchmark?
- How does the method handle the scalability to longer horizons or more complex tasks?
- Could you provide more details on the training process for the diffusion model and the low-level policy, including the datasets and training parameters used?
- How does the method handle the potential for hallucination or incorrect predictions by the diffusion model, especially in complex or novel environments?
- Could you discuss the potential for extending the method to include additional levels of hierarchy or to handle different types of tasks or environments?
