 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel framework named 3D Geometry-Guided Conditional Adaption (3D-GeoCA) for solving Partial Differential Equations (PDEs) on arbitrary 3D geometries. This framework leverages a point cloud geometry encoder to extract essential and robust representations of the input geometry, which are then used to guide the adaptation of hidden features in the surrogate model. The methodology is model-agnostic and can be integrated with various surrogate models, including MLP, GNN, and GNO, to reduce L2 errors significantly. The framework is evaluated on the Shape-Net Car computational fluid dynamics dataset, showing improvements over existing methods. However, the evaluation is limited to a single dataset, and the generalization of the method to other datasets or more complex scenarios remains unclear.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel framework, 3D-GeoCA, which is model-agnostic and can be applied to any surrogate model, making it versatile and adaptable.
- The methodology is well-explained, with clear and concise writing that facilitates understanding.
- The paper demonstrates the effectiveness of the proposed method through empirical results, showing a reduction in L2 errors and improvements in convergence rates compared to existing methods.
- The framework is original in its approach to integrating geometry-awareness into neural networks, which is crucial for handling complex 3D geometries in PDEs.
- The experimental results are robust, showing that the proposed method outperforms existing methods on the Shape-Net Car computational fluid dynamics dataset.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed comparison with existing methods, particularly those that also utilize pre-trained models like PointNeXt and Point-BERT.
- The evaluation is limited to a single dataset, which raises concerns about the generalizability of the method to other datasets or more complex scenarios.
- The paper does not provide a detailed analysis of the computational complexity of the proposed method, which is crucial for understanding its scalability and efficiency.
- There is a lack of discussion on the limitations of the proposed method, which is essential for understanding its scope and applicability.
- The paper does not include a discussion on the potential negative societal impacts of the proposed method, which is a significant oversight.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors provide a detailed comparison with existing methods, particularly those that also utilize pre-trained models like PointNeXt and Point-BERT?
- How does the proposed method perform on other datasets or more complex scenarios?
- What is the computational complexity of the proposed method, and how does it scale with the size and complexity of the input data?
- Can the authors provide more details on the limitations of the proposed method and scenarios where it may not be effective?
- How does the method handle the variation in the quality and complexity of the input data, especially when it comes from different sources or is of varying quality?
- Could the authors provide more details on the training process, including the selection of hyperparameters and the impact of different batch sizes on the results?
