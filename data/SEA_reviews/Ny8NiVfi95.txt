 **Summary:**
The paper introduces MAGNeT, a novel non-autoregressive model for text-to-music and text-to-audio generation, which operates on several streams of audio tokens obtained from EnCodec. MAGNeT employs a hybrid architecture that combines autoregressive and non-autoregressive models, utilizing a masked language modeling approach and a rescoring mechanism to improve performance. The model is designed to generate relatively long sequences (30 seconds) using a single model, with significantly faster inference times compared to autoregressive baselines. The paper presents extensive experiments and ablation studies, demonstrating MAGNeT's effectiveness and efficiency. However, concerns are raised about the model's generalization to other datasets and its reliance on a specific audio codec, which may limit its applicability.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, clear, and easy to follow, with comprehensive experimental settings and detailed implementation details.
- The proposed method is novel, focusing on non-autoregressive modeling for audio generation, which is a significant departure from existing methods.
- The model demonstrates high efficiency in terms of inference time and can generate relatively long sequences (30 seconds) using a single model.
- The authors provide extensive ablation studies and analysis, including a detailed analysis of latency, throughput, and generation quality, which helps in understanding the trade-offs between autoregressive and non-autoregressive models.
- The paper includes a comprehensive set of experiments and evaluations, including objective metrics and human studies, which demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed method.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed discussion on the limitations of the proposed method, which could have provided a more balanced view of the model's capabilities and shortcomings.
- There is a significant reliance on the EnCodec model, which may limit the generalization of the proposed method to other datasets or audio codecs.
- The paper does not adequately address the generalization of the proposed method to other datasets or audio codecs, which is a critical limitation given the importance of audio codecs in the field.
- The paper does not provide sufficient details on how the model handles different audio codecs, which is crucial for understanding the model's applicability in real-world scenarios.
- The comparison with other models, such as VampNet, is insufficiently detailed, which could lead to a lack of clarity regarding the model's advantages or disadvantages compared to existing methods.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors provide more details on how the model handles different audio codecs, especially in terms of generalization and performance?
- How does the model perform when trained on different datasets, and what are the implications for its applicability in various scenarios?
- Could the authors discuss the potential of applying the proposed method to other domains beyond audio, such as text-to-image generation?
- In terms of the model's training, how does the use of different datasets or audio codecs affect the model's performance and generalization?
- Could the authors clarify the comparison with VampNet and other models, particularly in terms of the model's performance and the specific advantages or disadvantages of the non-autoregressive approach?
