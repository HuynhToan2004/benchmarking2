 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel method called Mixture of Expert Diffusion Models (MoEDM) aimed at enhancing the efficiency of large-scale diffusion models by selectively activating essential neurons during the sampling process. This approach, which leverages dynamic routing, is designed to optimize runtime performance for specialized tasks while minimizing computational costs. The methodology, tested on various applications such as image generation, shows promising results in terms of both efficiency and robustness, as evidenced by FID and KID scores. However, concerns were raised regarding the novelty of the approach, the clarity of the paper's presentation, and the potential for significant computational overhead due to the need for retraining the model for each task.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written and easy to follow, providing a clear and concise presentation of the proposed methodology.
- The methodology, while simple, is effective in improving the efficiency of large-scale diffusion models, particularly in specialized tasks.
- The paper includes a comprehensive set of experiments that demonstrate the advantages of the proposed method in terms of both efficiency and robustness.
- The paper introduces a novel approach to dynamic routing in diffusion models, which is a significant contribution to the field.
- The paper is well-organized, with a clear structure that facilitates understanding of the proposed method and its implications.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a thorough comparison with existing methods such as MoE and other lightweight diffusion models, which limits the ability to assess the true novelty and effectiveness of the proposed method.
- There is a significant computational overhead associated with the need to train a new MoEDM model for each task, which could be a major drawback for practical deployment.
- The paper does not adequately address the potential for parameter degeneracy in the MoEDM models, which could lead to performance degradation if not managed properly.
- The paper's claims about the generalizability of the MoEDM models across different tasks are not convincingly supported by the experimental results.
- The paper could benefit from a more detailed discussion on the potential limitations and societal impacts of the proposed method.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors provide more details on how the MoEDM models are trained, particularly in terms of the computational resources and time required?
- How do the MoEDM models perform when fine-tuned on different tasks, and what are the implications for their generalizability?
- Could the authors provide more insights into the choice of the gating vector in the MoEDM models, and how it impacts the model's performance?
- What are the specific advantages of using MoEDM over other lightweight diffusion models, and how does it compare in terms of efficiency and effectiveness?
- How does the paper address the potential for parameter degeneracy in the MoEDM models, and what measures are in place to prevent or mitigate this issue?
