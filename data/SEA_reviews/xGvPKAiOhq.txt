 **Summary:**
This paper investigates the impact of over-parameterization on the convergence behavior of gradient descent (GD) in the context of matrix sensing. The study explores two settings: symmetric and asymmetric, demonstrating how over-parameterization can significantly alter the convergence rates of GD. In the symmetric setting, the authors show that GD converges at a rate of O(1/T^2), while in the asymmetric setting, they prove that GD can converge at a linear rate. This linear convergence is dependent on the initialization scale, suggesting that imbalance between initialization factors plays a crucial role in accelerating convergence. The paper also introduces a simple algorithm to remove the initialization scale dependency in the asymmetric setting.

**Strengths:**
- The paper addresses an interesting and relevant problem in the field of matrix sensing, focusing on the impact of over-parameterization on the convergence behavior of gradient descent.
- The results are well-presented, and the paper is generally well-written, with clear explanations and proofs.
- The paper provides a lower bound for symmetric matrix factorization, which is a significant contribution to the field.
- The authors demonstrate a novel phenomenon where asymmetric parameterization can exponentially speed up convergence, which is a surprising and interesting finding.
- The paper includes a simple algorithm to remove the initialization scale dependency in the asymmetric setting, which is a practical application of the theoretical results.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a clear comparison with related works, particularly those that have studied similar problems in the context of deep linear networks.
- The proofs and assumptions made in the paper, such as the RIP condition and the choice of initialization scales, are not well-explained or justified.
- The paper's contributions, particularly the results on linear convergence in the asymmetric setting, are not sufficiently novel or surprising given the existing literature.
- The paper's writing and organization could be improved, particularly in the presentation of proofs and the explanation of technical details.
- The paper's scope is somewhat limited to specific matrix sensing problems, and the generalizability of the findings to other types of problems or settings is not clear.
