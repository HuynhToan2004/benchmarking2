 **Summary:**
The paper introduces T-Rep, a self-supervised learning method for time series representation, focusing on the integration of time-embeddings to enhance the learning of fine-grained temporal dependencies. T-Rep employs two pretext tasks: Time-embedding Divergence Prediction and Time-embedding-conditioned Forecasting, which are designed to capture temporal features effectively. The method is evaluated on various tasks including classification, forecasting, and anomaly detection, showing promising results. Despite its innovative approach, the paper faces criticism for its limited novelty and the need for more rigorous comparisons with existing methods.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written and easy to follow, with clear explanations of the proposed method and its components.
- The introduction of time-embeddings as a pretext task is innovative and effectively captures temporal features, enhancing the learning of fine-grained temporal dependencies.
- The methodology is supported by comprehensive experiments that demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed method across various tasks, showing improvements over existing methods.
- The paper provides a thorough evaluation of the proposed method, including robustness to missing data and comparisons with other self-supervised learning methods.
- The use of time-embeddings in pretext tasks is a novel approach that has been shown to improve performance in time series analysis.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed comparison with other time-embedding methods, which limits the understanding of the advantages of the proposed method.
- The novelty of the method is questioned, as the use of time-embeddings in pretext tasks has been previously explored.
- The paper does not provide sufficient details on the training process, including the number of epochs and the hyperparameters used, which could affect the reproducibility and fairness of the experiments.
- The performance improvements over existing methods are not consistently significant, and the paper does not provide a detailed analysis of the limitations or potential drawbacks of the proposed method.
- The paper could benefit from a more comprehensive discussion on the limitations and potential applications of the proposed method.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors provide a more detailed comparison with other time-embedding methods to clarify the advantages of the proposed method?
- How do the authors justify the novelty of the method, especially considering the existing use of time-embeddings in similar contexts?
- Can the authors provide more details on the training process, including the number of epochs and hyperparameters used, to ensure the fairness and reproducibility of the experiments?
- How do the authors address the issue of the method's performance not being consistently superior to existing methods?
- Could the authors provide a more detailed analysis of the limitations and potential drawbacks of the proposed method, and discuss its potential applications in various domains?
