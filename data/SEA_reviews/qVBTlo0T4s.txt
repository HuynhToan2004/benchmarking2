 **Summary:**
The paper introduces AutoNeRF, a method that utilizes autonomous agents to collect data for training NeRF models in unseen environments. The system employs a modular policy composed of mapping, global, and local policies to guide the agent's exploration. The paper evaluates the method across various tasks including novel view synthesis, metric map estimation, planning, and pose refinement, comparing it with existing methods. The results show that AutoNeRF outperforms baseline methods in several tasks, demonstrating the potential of using autonomous agents for data collection in NeRF training. However, concerns were raised about the generalizability of the method to real-world scenarios and the clarity of the presentation.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, easy to follow, and provides clear explanations of the proposed method and its implementation.
- The integration of a modular policy with NeRF is a novel approach that enhances the training process by ensuring the collected data is optimally utilized.
- The experimental results demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed method in several tasks, showcasing its potential to outperform existing methods.
- The method's ability to use autonomous agents to collect data for training NeRFs in unseen environments is a significant advancement, reducing the need for manual data collection.
- The paper presents a comprehensive evaluation of the method across multiple tasks, demonstrating its versatility and potential applicability in various real-world scenarios.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed discussion on the limitations and potential failure modes of the proposed method, which could be crucial for understanding its practical applicability.
- The comparison with other methods is not extensive enough, particularly in terms of using different NeRF models and varying the number of viewpoints.
- The paper does not sufficiently address the generalizability of the method to real-world scenarios, which could limit its practical utility.
- There are concerns about the clarity of the paper, particularly in the presentation of figures and the explanation of certain methodological aspects.
- The paper does not provide sufficient details on the implementation and training of the NeRF model, which could hinder reproducibility.
- The paper could benefit from a more detailed discussion on the impact of different exploration strategies on the quality of the learned representations.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors provide a detailed discussion on the limitations and potential failure modes of the proposed method?
- How does the performance of the method vary when different NeRF models are used, and what are the implications of using different numbers of viewpoints?
- Could the authors clarify the discrepancies in the evaluation metrics, particularly in the results presented in Tables 2 and 3?
- How does the proposed method perform when using real-world data instead of simulated data?
- Can the authors provide more details on the implementation and training of the NeRF model, including the training time and the computational resources required?
- How does the method handle scenarios where the agent gets stuck or fails to explore the entire scene effectively?
