 **Summary:**
The paper investigates the phenomenon of catastrophic forgetting in neural networks by proposing a novel perspective on how neural networks encode memories during training. It introduces the concept of "memory trace" to explain how neural networks learn to differentiate between different tasks, which is crucial for understanding the occurrence of catastrophic forgetting. The authors propose that the backpropagation process, which is typically viewed as a form of forward propagation, plays a significant role in this encoding process. The paper also discusses the implications of these findings for the design of neural networks that can effectively learn and retain information over time.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel perspective on the neural network's training process, particularly focusing on the memory trace and its role in encoding different tasks, which is a significant contribution to the field.
- The authors provide a clear and comprehensive explanation of the memory trace and its implications for understanding the neural network's behavior, which is well-supported by empirical evidence.
- The paper is well-written, with a clear structure and logical flow, making it accessible and understandable to readers.
- The proposed perspective on neural network training, which emphasizes the importance of memory trace, is innovative and could lead to new insights and methodologies in the field.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a clear explanation of the practical implications of the findings, particularly how the insights into memory trace could be used to improve neural network performance or mitigate catastrophic forgetting.
- There is a significant gap between the theoretical insights and the practical applications, which is not adequately addressed.
- The paper is somewhat disorganized in its presentation, with some sections appearing incomplete or not well-integrated into the overall narrative.
- The experimental setup and results are not convincingly presented, with some figures and results lacking clarity or detail.
- The paper's claims about the necessity of retraining all data to address catastrophic forgetting are not well-supported and may not be feasible in practical settings.
- The paper's focus on catastrophic forgetting is somewhat narrow, and it could benefit from a broader discussion on how the insights into memory trace could be applied to other areas of neural network research.
