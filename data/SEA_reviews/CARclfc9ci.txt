 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel architectural framework called "relational convolutional networks" aimed at learning representations of hierarchical relations between objects. This framework employs a multi-dimensional inner product relation module to model pairwise relations and a relational convolution layer to transform these relations into new objects, capturing higher-order, hierarchical relations. The experiments conducted on relational games and SET datasets demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed architecture in learning relational representations. However, the paper is criticized for its limited evaluation scope, lack of comparison with relevant works, and insufficient discussion on the computational complexity and scalability of the proposed methods.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, clear, and easy to follow, with a clear motivation and a novel approach to modeling relational data.
- The proposed relational convolutional networks (RelConvNet) effectively model hierarchical relations, which is a significant advancement in the field of relational reasoning.
- The experiments conducted on relational games and SET datasets demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed architecture in learning relational representations.
- The use of a multi-dimensional inner product relation module to model pairwise relations and a relational convolution layer to transform these relations into new objects is a novel and interesting approach.
- The paper is well-organized, with clear figures and a clear presentation of the proposed method.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed discussion on the computational complexity and scalability of the proposed methods, which is crucial for understanding the practical applicability of the proposed architecture.
- The evaluation scope is limited, focusing only on relational games and SET datasets, which may not fully demonstrate the generalizability of the proposed architecture to other domains or datasets.
- The paper does not adequately compare the proposed method with existing works, particularly with other relational architectures such as CoRelNet, which could have provided a more robust validation of the proposed method.
- The paper lacks a discussion on the limitations of the proposed architecture and potential negative societal impacts, which are important considerations for the ethical implications of the research.
- The paper does not provide sufficient details on the implementation of the relational convolutional networks, such as the number of filters used and the specifics of the grouping layers, which could affect the reproducibility and comparability of the results.

**Questions:**
- Could you provide more details on the computational complexity and scalability of the proposed methods? How does the computational cost scale with the number of objects and the size of the relation tensor?
- How does the proposed architecture compare to other relational architectures, such as CoRelNet, in terms of performance and scalability?
- Could you provide a more detailed discussion on the limitations of the proposed architecture and potential negative societal impacts?
- What are the specifics of the grouping layers used in the relational convolutional networks? How are these layers implemented, and what are the implications for the computational cost and scalability?
- How does the proposed architecture handle the scalability issues that are common in relational architectures, particularly in terms of the computational cost and the size of the relation tensor?
- Could you provide more details on the implementation of the relational convolutional networks, such as the number of filters used and the specifics of the grouping layers?
- How does the proposed architecture compare to other methods in terms of performance and scalability in more complex and larger-scale datasets?
