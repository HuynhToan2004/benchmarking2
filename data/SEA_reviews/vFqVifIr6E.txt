 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel few-shot learning framework that integrates semantic information from textual encoders with visual features, aiming to enhance the performance of few-shot image classification tasks. The framework utilizes a contrastive learning approach to align visual and textual embeddings, which are then optimized using the MAML algorithm. The methodology is evaluated on various benchmarks and demonstrates state-of-the-art performance. However, concerns were raised about the novelty of the approach, the clarity of the presentation, and the generalization ability of the method across different domains and datasets.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel framework that integrates semantic information from textual encoders with visual features, which is a promising direction for few-shot learning.
- The experiments are comprehensive, covering multiple benchmarks and different domains, demonstrating the effectiveness of the proposed method.
- The paper is well-written, making it easy to follow, and the methodology is clearly explained, contributing to its readability and comprehensibility.
- The proposed method achieves state-of-the-art performance on various benchmarks, indicating its effectiveness.
- The ablation studies provided in the paper are thorough, helping to understand the impact of different components of the proposed method.

**Weaknesses:**
- The novelty of the approach is limited as it primarily involves aligning visual and textual embeddings using contrastive learning, which has been explored in previous works.
- The paper lacks a clear motivation for using semantic information in few-shot learning, and the alignment of visual and textual features seems more suitable for zero-shot learning.
- The generalization ability of the method is questionable, as the performance drops significantly when tested on different domains or with different datasets.
- The paper's presentation could be improved, particularly in terms of clarity and organization, which could enhance its readability and understanding.
- There are several technical details that are unclear or not well-explained, such as the initialization of the metric module and the role of different components in the methodology.
- The paper does not adequately compare its method with recent state-of-the-art methods, which could undermine the perceived effectiveness of the proposed approach.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors clarify the motivation behind using semantic information in few-shot learning and how it enhances the performance of the proposed method?
- How does the proposed method handle the generalization ability across different domains or datasets, especially when the performance drops significantly?
- Could the authors provide more details on the initialization and training of the metric module, particularly how it is initialized and how it affects the performance of the method?
- How does the method perform when tested on larger-scale datasets, such as those used in large-scale visual recognition challenges?
- The paper mentions the use of a masked language model for textual embedding; could the authors explain why this specific model was chosen and how it contributes to the effectiveness of the method?
- How does the proposed method compare with other recent state-of-the-art methods, especially in terms of performance and computational efficiency?
