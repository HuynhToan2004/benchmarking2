 **Summary:**
The paper introduces BO-REP, a novel algorithm designed for bilevel optimization problems where the upper-level function may exhibit unbounded smoothness. BO-REP employs two innovative techniques: initialization refinement and periodic updates, which are crucial for handling the unique challenges posed by unbounded smoothness. Theoretical analysis demonstrates that BO-REP requires a number of iterations proportional to 1/\u03f5, matching the state-of-the-art complexity under bounded smoothness settings. Empirical results on various text classification tasks further validate the algorithm's effectiveness. Despite its theoretical and practical advancements, concerns were raised about the clarity of the experimental setup and the lack of comparisons with certain baseline methods.

**Strengths:**
- The paper addresses a novel and significant problem in bilevel optimization where the upper-level function may be unboundedly smooth, a challenge not commonly addressed in existing literature.
- The proposed BO-REP algorithm introduces innovative techniques such as initialization refinement and periodic updates, which are not commonly used in bilevel optimization algorithms.
- The paper is well-written, clearly explaining the challenges posed by unbounded smoothness in bilevel optimization and the rationale behind the proposed algorithm's design.
- The theoretical analysis is solid, and the paper provides a comprehensive comparison of the proposed algorithm with state-of-the-art methods, highlighting its competitive performance.
- The experimental results are promising, demonstrating the effectiveness of the BO-REP algorithm in practical applications.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks clarity in the experimental setup, particularly in the hyper-representation learning and hyperparameter optimization experiments. There is a need for more detailed descriptions of the datasets, task-specific parameters, and loss functions used.
- The choice of baselines for comparison in the experiments is questionable. Some significant recent works, such as those by Dagreou et al. (2022) and Chen et al. (2023), are missing, which could have provided a more robust comparison.
- The paper's theoretical analysis assumes certain conditions, such as the boundedness of the gradient norm, which might not hold in practical scenarios involving unbounded smoothness.
- There is a lack of discussion on the limitations and potential negative societal impacts of the proposed algorithm.
- The paper could benefit from a more detailed comparison with existing methods that also handle unbounded smoothness, such as those by Zhang et al. (2020b).

**Questions:**
- Can the authors clarify the experimental setup, particularly in the hyper-representation learning and hyperparameter optimization sections, to provide more detailed descriptions of the datasets, task-specific parameters, and loss functions used?
- How does the BO-REP algorithm compare with the recent works by Dagreou et al. (2022) and Chen et al. (2023) in terms of performance and theoretical contributions?
- Could the authors discuss the potential negative societal impacts of their proposed algorithm and how these impacts might be mitigated or addressed?
- In light of the unbounded smoothness assumption, how does the paper address the potential issues arising from the boundedness of the gradient norm, especially in practical scenarios?
