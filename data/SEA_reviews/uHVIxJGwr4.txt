 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel offline reinforcement learning (RL) approach, Ranking-Constrained Actor-Critic (RCAC), aimed at learning branching strategies for Mixed Integer Linear Programs (MILPs) in a data-driven manner. The RCAC algorithm is designed to efficiently learn good branching strategies from sub-optimal or inadequate training signals, addressing the dependency on hand-crafted heuristics and the need for high-quality training data. The approach is evaluated against various benchmarks, demonstrating its effectiveness in both prediction accuracy and computational efficiency compared to previous methods. However, concerns are raised regarding the clarity of the experimental setup and the need for more detailed comparisons with existing methods.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, easy to follow, and clearly articulates the problem and proposed solution.
- The proposed offline RL approach, Ranking-Constrained Actor-Critic (RCAC), is innovative and addresses a significant challenge in MILP solving by learning branching strategies from sub-optimal or inadequate training signals.
- The experiments conducted are thorough, demonstrating the effectiveness of the RCAC algorithm in both prediction accuracy and computational efficiency compared to previous methods.
- The paper includes a detailed ablation study that helps in understanding the contribution of different components of the proposed method.
- The RCAC algorithm is shown to be effective in both exact solving and time-constrained solving, which is crucial for practical applications.

**Weaknesses:**
- The experimental setup and comparisons with existing methods are not clearly described, which makes it difficult to assess the true performance of the RCAC algorithm against state-of-the-art methods.
- The paper lacks a detailed discussion on the computational complexity and scalability of the RCAC algorithm, especially when applied to larger, more complex MILP instances.
- The paper does not adequately address the generalizability of the RCAC algorithm across different types of MILP instances and domains.
- There are several minor typographical and formatting errors that need correction.
- The paper could benefit from a more detailed analysis of the training data and its quality, as well as a discussion on the potential impact of different training datasets on the performance of the RCAC algorithm.

**Questions:**
- Could you clarify the experimental setup and provide more details on how the RCAC algorithm was compared against existing methods?
- How does the RCAC algorithm perform when applied to larger, more complex MILP instances, and what are the computational costs associated with this application?
- Can you provide more insights into the quality and diversity of the training data used to train the RCAC algorithm, and how this data might affect the performance of the algorithm on different types of MILP instances?
- How does the RCAC algorithm handle the generalization to different types of MILP instances and domains, and what steps have been taken to ensure its applicability across a wide range of problems?
- Could you elaborate on the choice of the reward function and how it affects the learning process in the RCAC algorithm?
