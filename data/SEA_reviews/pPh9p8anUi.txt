 **Summary:**
The paper introduces PBADet, a one-stage, anchor-free method for part-body association detection. It leverages a novel approach by incorporating a part-to-body center offset, allowing the method to manage multiple parts-to-body associations without compromising detection accuracy or robustness. The methodology is supported by extensive experiments on various datasets, demonstrating state-of-the-art performance. However, the paper's novelty is questioned due to its similarity to existing methods like ED-Pose, and the lack of a comprehensive comparison with other recent methods is noted. Additionally, concerns about the clarity of the method's motivation and the necessity of the proposed method over existing solutions were raised.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, clear, and easy to follow, with a clear motivation for the proposed method.
- The introduction of a novel one-stage, anchor-free approach for part-body association detection is innovative and well-explained.
- The paper demonstrates state-of-the-art performance on various datasets, showcasing the effectiveness of the proposed method.
- The method's scalability is highlighted, as it can accommodate any number of body parts without increasing the number of center offsets, which is beneficial for practical applications.
- The paper provides a detailed evaluation protocol and comparison with existing methods, which helps in understanding the method's effectiveness and limitations.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed comparison with other recent methods like BPJDet and Hier R-CNN, which could have strengthened the argument for the proposed method's superiority.
- The novelty of the method is questioned due to its similarity to existing methods like ED-Pose, particularly in the use of part-to-body center offsets.
- The paper does not sufficiently differentiate its method from existing solutions, particularly in terms of the role and functionality of the part-to-body center offsets.
- The motivation behind the proposed method is not clearly articulated, making it difficult to understand the necessity and advantages of the method over existing solutions.
- The paper does not provide a detailed analysis of the limitations or potential negative societal impacts of the method, which could be crucial for understanding its applicability and ethical considerations.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors provide a more detailed comparison with BPJDet and Hier R-CNN, particularly in terms of performance and methodological contributions?
- How does the proposed method differentiate from existing methods like ED-Pose, and what specific advantages does it offer over these methods?
- Can the authors clarify the motivation behind the proposed method and how it addresses the limitations of existing solutions in a more convincing manner?
- What are the potential negative societal impacts of the proposed method, and how are these addressed or mitigated?
- Could the authors provide more detailed results and comparisons, especially in terms of the impact of different backbones and the effects of hyper-parameters on the method's performance?
