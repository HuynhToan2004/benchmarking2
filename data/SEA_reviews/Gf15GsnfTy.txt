 **Summary:**
The paper introduces REValueD, a novel approach for solving Factorizable Markov Decision Processes (FMDPs) by decomposing the action space into sub-actions, which are handled independently. The method addresses the issue of increased variance in the target estimation by employing an ensemble of critics and a regularization term to minimize the impact of exploratory sub-actions on the value of optimal sub-actions. Theoretical analysis is provided to justify the variance reduction, and empirical results demonstrate the method's effectiveness compared to existing methods like DecQN and BDQ. The paper also discusses the potential limitations of the approach, such as the need for a large ensemble size and the potential for overestimation bias in the value decomposition.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, easy to follow, and provides clear explanations of the proposed methods.
- The theoretical analysis is sound, and the empirical results demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed method.
- The paper addresses a significant and relevant problem in reinforcement learning, focusing on the challenges of high-dimensional discrete action spaces.
- The introduction of regularization loss to mitigate the effects of exploratory sub-actions is a novel and interesting approach.
- The paper provides a thorough empirical evaluation, including ablations that demonstrate the impact of different components of the proposed method.
- The paper includes theoretical proofs and empirical results, which support the claims made about the method's effectiveness.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper could benefit from a clearer explanation of how the regularization loss works and its impact on the sub-action utility values.
- The experimental results show that the ensemble size has a significant impact on the performance, which might require extensive hyperparameter tuning.
- The paper does not discuss the potential for overestimation bias in the value decomposition, which could be a significant limitation of the proposed method.
- The paper's claims about the variance reduction provided by the ensemble of critics are not convincingly supported by the empirical results, which show that the ensemble size does not always lead to the best performance.
- The paper does not sufficiently differentiate its method from existing methods like DecQN, which might lead to confusion about the novelty and contribution of the proposed method.
- The paper could benefit from a more detailed discussion of the limitations and potential negative societal impacts of the proposed method.

**Questions:**
- Could you clarify how the regularization loss affects the sub-action utility values and how it prevents under or overvaluation of sub-actions?
- How does the proposed method compare to other value decomposition methods like those used in multi-agent reinforcement learning?
- In the context of the stochastic environments discussed in the paper, how does the proposed method perform when the reward or state is stochastic?
- How does the paper address the potential for overestimation bias in the value decomposition, and what steps are taken to mitigate this bias?
- Could you provide more details on the computational efficiency of the proposed method, particularly in terms of the ensemble size and the number of sub-actions?
- How does the paper ensure that the ensemble of critics does not lead to overfitting or other negative effects on the learning process?
