 **Summary:**
The paper introduces TESTAM, a novel mixture-of-experts (MoE) model designed for traffic forecasting. TESTAM integrates three experts for temporal modeling, spatio-temporal modeling with a static graph, and spatio-temporal dependency modeling with a dynamic graph. The model is evaluated on three public traffic datasets, showing significant improvements over existing methods. The authors propose a gating mechanism that selects the most appropriate expert based on traffic conditions, enhancing the model's adaptability. The paper also discusses the limitations of existing models and the need for in-situ spatial modeling, which is crucial for traffic forecasting.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, making it easy to follow and understand, with a clear and concise presentation of the methodology and results.
- The introduction of the TESTAM model, which integrates multiple experts for different aspects of traffic forecasting, is innovative and addresses a significant gap in the current literature.
- The model's ability to adapt to different traffic conditions and its superior performance over existing methods, as demonstrated by the experimental results, highlights its effectiveness.
- The use of a gating mechanism to select the most appropriate expert based on traffic conditions is a novel approach that enhances the model's adaptability and applicability.
- The paper provides a comprehensive review of existing models and discusses the limitations of these models, which helps in understanding the context and the need for the proposed solution.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed discussion on the limitations of the TESTAM model, which could help in understanding its scope and applicability.
- The selection of experts and the gating mechanism are not adequately justified, and the paper could benefit from a more thorough analysis of these components.
- The experimental setup could be improved by including more datasets and varying the prediction windows to test the model's robustness across different scenarios.
- There is a need for more detailed comparisons with existing models, especially in terms of computational efficiency and resource utilization.
- The paper does not adequately address the scalability of the model, particularly in terms of the number of nodes in the graph and the complexity of the model architecture.
- The paper could benefit from a more thorough discussion on the related work, particularly in the context of traffic forecasting and spatio-temporal modeling.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors provide more detailed justification for the selection of experts and the gating mechanism, including a comparison with other methods?
- How does the TESTAM model handle scenarios where the experts disagree on the best course of action?
- What are the specific advantages of the TESTAM model over other models in terms of computational efficiency and resource utilization?
- Could the authors discuss the scalability of the model in more detail, particularly in terms of the number of nodes in the graph and the complexity of the model architecture?
- How does the TESTAM model perform under different traffic conditions, and what are the implications of these conditions on the model's effectiveness?
- Could the authors provide more details on the experimental setup, including the selection of datasets and the prediction windows used?
- What are the potential applications of the TESTAM model beyond traffic forecasting, and how could it be adapted to other domains?
