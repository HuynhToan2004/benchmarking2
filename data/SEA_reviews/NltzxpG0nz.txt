 **Summary:**
The paper introduces Steve-Eye, a novel large multimodal model that integrates a visual encoder with a language model to enhance embodied agents' interaction with open-world environments. The model is designed to process visual-text inputs and generate multimodal feedback, utilizing a semi-automatic strategy to collect extensive instruction pairs. The paper presents three open-world evaluation benchmarks to demonstrate Steve-Eye's capability in strategic action and planning. Despite its innovative approach, the paper faces criticism for its limited technical contribution, insufficient experimental validation, and lack of clarity in methodological descriptions.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel model named Steve-Eye, which effectively integrates a visual encoder with a language model to process visual-text inputs and generate multimodal feedback, enhancing the interaction capabilities of embodied agents in open-world environments.
- The construction of an extensive instruction dataset is a significant contribution, providing a valuable resource for future research in this area.
- The paper is well-written and easy to follow, making it accessible to readers.
- The evaluation benchmarks are well-designed and comprehensive, providing a robust framework for assessing the performance of embodied agents in open-world environments.
- The paper demonstrates the capability of Steve-Eye to perform multimodal perception, foundational knowledge base, and skill prediction and planning, showcasing its versatility and adaptability in various tasks.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed description of the methodology, particularly the training process and the specifics of how the visual encoder and language model are integrated.
- The experimental results do not convincingly demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed method, with some results showing marginal improvements over existing methods.
- The paper's reliance on ChatGPT for data curation raises concerns about the originality and depth of the research.
- The paper's focus on Minecraft as the primary testing environment may limit the generalizability of the findings to other open-world environments.
- The evaluation benchmarks, while well-designed, are limited in scope, and the paper does not compare the proposed method against a broader range of existing methods, which could provide a more comprehensive understanding of its performance.
- The paper's technical contribution is limited, as it primarily combines existing models without significant innovation.

**Questions:**
- Could you provide more details on the training process, specifically how the visual encoder and language model are integrated and how the model is trained to perform multimodal perception and planning?
- How does the model handle scenarios where visual and textual inputs contradict each other?
- What are the specific technical challenges in integrating visual and textual inputs, and how does Steve-Eye address these challenges?
- How does the model ensure the accuracy and reliability of its predictions, particularly in complex or ambiguous environments?
- Could you provide more details on the data curation process, including the selection criteria for the instruction pairs and the validation of the data quality?
- How does the model perform in environments beyond Minecraft, and what adaptations are necessary for it to function effectively in these new environments?
- Can you clarify the role of ChatGPT in the data curation process and how this affects the originality and depth of the research?
