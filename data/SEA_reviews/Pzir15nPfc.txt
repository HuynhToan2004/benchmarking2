 **Summary:**
The paper introduces Contextual Vision Transformers (ContextViT), a method designed to generate robust image representations for datasets experiencing shifts in latent factors across various groups. ContextViT leverages in-context learning to condition vision transformers on group-specific context tokens, which are predicted by a context inference network. This mechanism allows the model to adapt to new testing distributions during inference time, enhancing its ability to generalize across unseen data groups. The paper demonstrates the effectiveness of ContextViT through experiments on benchmarks such as iWildCam, FMoW, JUMP-CP, and Camelyon17-WILDS, showing improvements in out-of-distribution generalization and robustness to batch effects.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel method for generating robust image representations using learned context tokens to capture distribution shifts, which is a significant contribution to the field.
- The integration of a context inference mechanism into ContextViT enhances its ability to adapt and generalize to previously unseen distributions on the fly, which is crucial for real-world applications.
- The paper demonstrates the effectiveness of ContextViT through extensive experiments on benchmarks such as iWildCam, FMoW, JUMP-CP, and Camelyon17-WILDS, showing improvements in out-of-distribution generalization and robustness to batch effects.
- The method is well-motivated and the paper is well-written, making it easy to follow and understand.
- The proposed method is general and can be applied to various vision transformers and datasets, showcasing its versatility.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed comparison with existing methods, particularly in terms of computational cost and performance improvements.
- The paper could benefit from more detailed explanations and examples of the context inference mechanism and the context tokens, as these are critical components of the proposed method.
- The experimental setup could be improved, particularly in terms of using more diverse datasets and benchmarks to demonstrate the robustness and generalizability of the proposed method.
- The paper does not adequately address the scalability of the proposed method, particularly in terms of how it performs with larger or more complex datasets.
- The paper could benefit from more detailed discussions on the limitations of the proposed method, including potential negative societal impacts and ethical considerations.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors provide more detailed comparisons with existing methods in terms of computational cost and performance improvements?
- How does the proposed method perform when applied to other datasets or benchmarks, particularly those not directly related to medical imaging?
- Could the authors provide more detailed explanations and examples of the context inference mechanism and the context tokens?
- How does the proposed method handle different types of contexts, such as those that are not easily identifiable from the input image?
- Could the authors discuss the potential negative societal impacts and ethical considerations of their proposed method?
- How does the proposed method compare in terms of performance and computational efficiency with other state-of-the-art methods in image representation learning?
