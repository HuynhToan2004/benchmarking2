 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel dataset, EQA-MX, designed for Embodied Question Answering (EQA) tasks that incorporate both verbal and nonverbal modalities. This dataset is notable for its large size and diverse set of tasks, including existence prediction, object grounding, perspective-aware object grounding, object counting, object attribute query, object attribute compare, perspective grounding, and relation grounding. The authors propose a method called VQ-Fusion, which integrates vector quantization to align visual and verbal representations, enhancing the performance of EQA tasks. The paper also discusses the limitations of existing datasets and the challenges in handling multimodal expressions, particularly nonverbal gestures.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel dataset, EQA-MX, which is the first to include nonverbal gestures in EQA tasks, addressing a significant gap in current datasets.
- The dataset is large-scale, with over 8 million samples, which is crucial for training and evaluating models effectively.
- The paper proposes a new method, VQ-Fusion, which effectively aligns continuous visual and discrete verbal representations, enhancing the performance of EQA tasks.
- The methodology is well-explained, and the paper provides a detailed analysis of the dataset, including the impact of different components of the dataset on performance.
- The paper addresses an important and understudied problem in the field of EQA, focusing on the integration of multimodal expressions, particularly nonverbal gestures, which are often overlooked in existing datasets.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed discussion on the limitations of the dataset, which is crucial for understanding its applicability and generalizability.
- The paper does not sufficiently differentiate the new dataset from existing ones, particularly in terms of the novelty and impact of the nonverbal gestures.
- The methodology section could benefit from clearer explanations and potentially more detailed diagrams to aid understanding.
- The paper does not adequately address the generalizability of the findings to real-world scenarios, which is a significant concern given the synthetic nature of the dataset.
- There is a lack of discussion on the ethical implications of the dataset and the potential biases that could be introduced by the inclusion of nonverbal gestures.
- The paper does not sufficiently compare the performance of the proposed method with existing state-of-the-art methods, which could undermine the claims of its effectiveness.

**Questions:**
- How does the dataset handle variations in nonverbal gestures, particularly in terms of the range and diversity of gestures used?
- Could the authors provide more details on the dataset's limitations and how these might affect the generalizability of the findings?
- How does the paper ensure the ethical implications of the dataset, particularly in terms of the potential biases introduced by the inclusion of nonverbal gestures?
- Could the authors clarify the methodology section, particularly the explanation of the VQ-Fusion method and the diagrams used to illustrate it?
- How does the proposed method compare with existing state-of-the-art methods in terms of performance and efficiency?
- What are the specific challenges in using the dataset for real-world applications, and how might these be addressed?
