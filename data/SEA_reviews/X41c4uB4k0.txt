 **Summary:**
The paper introduces MuDM, a training-free conditional 3D molecular generation algorithm that leverages off-the-shelf diffusion models and property prediction models to guide molecule generation based on multiple conditions. The method models the loss of property prediction models as energy functions and considers the property relation between multiple conditions as a probabilistic graph, aiming to generate molecules that meet specific conditions without additional training costs. The paper presents experimental results demonstrating the effectiveness of MuDM in handling both single and multiple objectives, and it is compared against several baseline methods. However, the paper is criticized for its lack of novelty, inadequate presentation, and insufficient experimental validation, particularly in terms of the diversity and real-world applicability of the generated molecules.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a training-free conditional 3D molecular generation algorithm, which is a significant contribution to the field.
- The approach is novel and innovative, utilizing diffusion models for molecular generation, which is an interesting direction.
- The method is clearly described, making it easy to follow and understand, and the paper is well-written with clear and detailed explanations.
- The paper provides a comprehensive description of the proposed method, including the use of diffusion models for molecular generation and the handling of multiple conditions.
- The experimental results are convincing, demonstrating the effectiveness of the proposed method.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed discussion on the training and inference costs of the diffusion models used, which are crucial for assessing the practicality of the method.
- The method's reliance on off-the-shelf diffusion models raises concerns about the quality and accuracy of the generated molecules, especially in terms of their diversity and real-world applicability.
- The paper does not adequately address the issue of potential conflicts among given conditions, which is a significant limitation.
- The presentation of the paper needs improvement, particularly in terms of clarity and the organization of figures and equations.
- The paper does not compare its method with recent related works, which could have provided a more robust validation of the proposed approach.
- The experimental validation is insufficient, with limited datasets and properties considered, which may not adequately demonstrate the generalizability and applicability of the proposed method.

**Questions:**
