 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a new layer type, termed the SSC layer, designed to replace convolutional layers in neural networks. This layer operates by shifting and subtracting input channels, reducing computational and memory costs. The SSC layer's effectiveness is demonstrated across various tasks such as image classification, sequence modeling, and single-channel speech separation. Despite its potential in reducing computational and memory requirements, the paper's claims about its novelty and superiority over existing methods are questioned. The reviewers noted that the paper's presentation lacks clarity and depth, with some figures and descriptions being misleading or inadequately explained.

**Strengths:**
- The paper addresses a significant and timely topic with the potential to enhance the efficiency and effectiveness of neural network architectures.
- The proposed SSC layer is described as a potentially faster and more memory-efficient alternative to traditional convolutional layers, which could lead to substantial improvements in computational efficiency.
- The paper is well-written, making it accessible and easy to follow, which is crucial for a broad audience.
- The methodology is clearly explained, and the experimental results are comprehensive, supporting the claims made about the SSC layer's performance.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a thorough discussion on the limitations of the SSC layer, which is crucial for understanding its applicability and effectiveness in various scenarios.
- There is a notable absence of comparative analysis with other existing methods, such as depthwise separable convolutions, which are also known for their efficiency.
- The paper does not provide a detailed analysis of the SSC layer's computational complexity, which is essential for understanding its practical advantages.
- The paper's claims regarding the novelty of the SSC layer are not convincingly supported, as similar methods have been previously explored in the literature.
- The experimental setup and results are not sufficiently robust or comprehensive, with some results showing only marginal improvements over existing methods.
- The presentation of the paper could be improved, with some figures and descriptions being misleading or inadequately explained, which could confuse readers.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors clarify the computational complexity of the SSC layer compared to traditional convolutional layers, and provide a detailed analysis of its efficiency?
- How does the SSC layer perform in scenarios where the input data has varying channel sizes?
- Could the authors provide a more detailed comparison of the SSC layer with other existing methods, particularly depthwise separable convolutions, to establish its relative advantages?
- In light of the claims made about the SSC layer's novelty, could the authors provide a more comprehensive review of the literature to support their claims?
- How does the SSC layer handle upsampling operations, and what are the implications of this limitation on its practical applicability?
- Could the authors explain the discrepancies in the experimental results, particularly the marginal improvements observed in some cases?
