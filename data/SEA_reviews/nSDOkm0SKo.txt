 **Summary:**
The paper investigates the impact of news events on stock prices, proposing a methodology that constructs dependency graphs to model the relationships between companies. This approach integrates news sentiment analysis to predict stock trends using a neural network-based model. The authors claim that considering news from related companies can enhance prediction accuracy, although the methodology lacks rigorous empirical validation and theoretical support. The paper is criticized for its unclear writing, lack of empirical evidence, and inadequate literature review. The methodology's novelty is questioned, as it appears to replicate existing models without significant advancement.

**Strengths:**
- The paper addresses an interesting and relevant topic, focusing on the impact of news events on stock prices, which is crucial for understanding market dynamics.
- The idea of incorporating news from related companies into the analysis is innovative and could potentially enhance prediction accuracy.
- The paper is well-organized, making it easy to follow, and includes a clear diagram that summarizes the methodology.
- The authors demonstrate a commitment to the topic, as evidenced by their detailed implementation and experimental results.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a rigorous methodology and empirical validation. There is no clear definition or explanation of the news sentiment analysis model, and the paper does not provide a detailed algorithm for constructing the dependency graph.
- The literature review is insufficient, and the paper does not adequately compare its approach with existing methods, which limits the assessment of its novelty and effectiveness.
- The writing quality needs improvement; the paper contains numerous grammatical errors, and the language used is sometimes overly dramatic and unprofessional.
- The paper does not provide sufficient empirical evidence to support its claims, such as the accuracy improvement from considering news from related companies.
- The paper does not adequately address the limitations of its approach, nor does it discuss potential negative societal impacts.
- The paper's methodology and results are not clearly explained, making it difficult to understand the contribution of the research.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors provide a clearer explanation of their news sentiment analysis model and the algorithm used to construct the dependency graph?
- How does the paper ensure the reliability and accuracy of the news data used in its analysis?
- Could the authors clarify the methodology and results sections to make the paper more accessible and understandable?
- How does the paper address the potential for biases in news sentiment analysis, especially concerning the subjectivity and objectivity of news sources?
- In light of the limitations and concerns raised, how does the paper plan to address these issues and improve the clarity and rigor of its methodology and results?
