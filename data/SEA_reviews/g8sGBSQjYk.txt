 **Summary:**
The paper explores the application of the Maximum Update Parameterization (MUP) to second-order optimization algorithms, specifically focusing on K-FAC and Shampoo. The authors propose a parameterization scheme that scales learning rates and damping terms based on network width, aiming to enhance feature learning and generalization performance. This approach is supported by empirical results showing improved performance on various datasets, including Word2Vec and ResNets. However, the paper is criticized for its limited scope, focusing on a narrow range of architectures and datasets, and for not adequately addressing the finite-width behavior of the algorithms. The paper also lacks a comprehensive comparison with other second-order methods and fails to discuss the implications of its findings for more complex models like transformers.
