 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel approach to spiking neural networks (SNNs) by focusing on the learnable parameters of dendrite and axon delays, a significant departure from traditional methods that primarily adjust synaptic weights. This method, termed the Exponential Time-Dependent Plasticity (ETDP) algorithm, is designed to optimize the delays based on global and local time differences between presynaptic and postsynaptic spikes, and the forward and backward propagation time of signals. The authors claim that this approach enhances biological plausibility and performance on various benchmark tasks. However, the paper's experimental results are not convincingly superior to existing methods, and the biological plausibility claims are not adequately substantiated.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel approach by focusing on the learnable parameters of dendrite and axon delays in spiking neural networks (SNNs), which is a significant departure from traditional methods that primarily adjust synaptic weights.
- The proposed ETDP algorithm is designed to optimize the delays based on global and local time differences between presynaptic and postsynaptic spikes, and the forward and backward propagation time of signals, which is a biologically plausible approach.
- The paper is well-written, clear, and easy to follow, with a detailed description of the methodology and experimental setup.
- The authors provide a comprehensive literature review, highlighting the relevance and significance of their work within the context of existing research.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a thorough comparison with existing methods, particularly in terms of biological plausibility and performance metrics. The experiments are limited to simple datasets, and the results do not convincingly demonstrate superior performance over existing methods.
- The biological plausibility claims are not adequately substantiated, and the paper does not convincingly argue why the proposed method is more biologically plausible than existing methods.
- There are several issues with the clarity and presentation of the paper, including unclear figures, incorrect references, and inconsistencies in the notation and methodology descriptions.
- The paper does not adequately address the computational complexity of the proposed method, which is crucial for practical implementation.
- The paper does not provide a detailed comparison of the computational complexity of the proposed method with existing methods, which is necessary for evaluating the practicality of the proposed approach.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors clarify the biological plausibility of their method, particularly in relation to existing methods that also incorporate delay learning?
- How does the proposed method compare in terms of computational complexity and practical implementation to existing methods?
- Could the authors provide more detailed experimental results, including comparisons with other state-of-the-art methods and more complex datasets?
- What are the specific advantages of using only dendrite and axon delays as learnable parameters, and how does this approach compare to methods that use both synaptic weights and delays?
- How does the ETDP algorithm compare in terms of biological plausibility and performance to other existing learning algorithms for SNNs?
- Can the authors address the issues with the clarity and presentation of the paper, particularly the unclear figures and incorrect references?
