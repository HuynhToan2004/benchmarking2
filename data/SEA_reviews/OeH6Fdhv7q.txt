 **Summary:**
The paper introduces TapMo, a novel text-driven animation pipeline designed for generating motion in skeleton-free 3D characters. TapMo utilizes a mesh handle predictor to predict skinning weights and mesh vertex handles, enabling adaptive control over mesh deformation. The shape-aware diffusion module generates mesh-specific motions based on text descriptions, ensuring geometric integrity. The methodology is evaluated against existing baselines and shows superior performance in both qualitative and quantitative assessments. However, the paper is criticized for its reliance on existing techniques and the limited novelty of its contributions.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel text-driven animation pipeline for skeleton-free characters, which is a significant advancement in the field of 3D character animation.
- The methodology is well-articulated, with a clear motivation and detailed explanation of the proposed components, such as the Mesh Handle Predictor and Shape-aware Motion Diffusion.
- The paper is well-written, making it easy to follow and understand the methodology and its implementation.
- The results showcase the effectiveness of the proposed method, demonstrating improvements in both qualitative and quantitative evaluations.
- The paper includes a comprehensive set of experiments that validate the effectiveness of the proposed method, including a user study that supports the practical utility of the proposed method.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper primarily relies on existing techniques, such as the Mesh Handle Predictor and the Shape-aware Motion Diffusion, which limits the perceived novelty of the contributions.
- The paper lacks a detailed discussion on the limitations of the proposed method, which could provide a more comprehensive understanding of its applicability and potential drawbacks.
- There is a noticeable absence of visual results in the paper, which could enhance the reader's understanding of the method's capabilities and limitations.
- The paper's reliance on existing datasets and methodologies may limit its perceived innovation, as it does not introduce significant new datasets or methodologies.
- The paper could benefit from a more thorough discussion on the method's scalability and adaptability to various character types and motion descriptions.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors provide more detailed results, such as additional visual examples, to better illustrate the method's capabilities and limitations?
- How does the proposed method handle variations in character shapes and sizes, particularly in scenarios where the character's shape significantly deviates from the training data?
- Can the authors clarify the role and impact of the shape-aware motion diffusion in the overall animation process?
- How does the method handle different motion descriptions, especially those that are not standard or common in the dataset used?
- Could the authors discuss the potential for extending the method to include more complex or diverse motion descriptions, such as those involving contact or complex interactions between characters?
