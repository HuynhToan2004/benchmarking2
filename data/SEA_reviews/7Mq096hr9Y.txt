 **Summary:**
The paper introduces OpenMixup, a comprehensive benchmark for evaluating mixup methods in visual classification tasks. It offers a unified framework for comparing various mixup algorithms across multiple datasets and network architectures, including CNNs and ViTs. The benchmark covers a range of mixup techniques, from static to dynamic, and includes robustness evaluations and transfer learning to object detection tasks. The authors provide extensive experimental results and visualizations to support their findings, highlighting the generalizability and effectiveness of mixup methods. The paper is praised for its thoroughness and the clarity of its presentation, although some concerns were raised about the novelty of the benchmark and the depth of its technical contributions.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written and easy to follow, providing a comprehensive evaluation of mixup methods across various datasets, network architectures, and mixup techniques.
- The authors have conducted extensive experiments, including robustness evaluations and transfer learning to object detection tasks, which support their findings.
- The paper includes visualizations that effectively illustrate the mixup methods and their effects, enhancing the understanding of the results.
- The proposed benchmark, OpenMixup, is a significant contribution to the field, offering a unified framework for evaluating mixup methods and providing valuable insights into their performance and generalizability.
- The paper is praised for its clarity and comprehensiveness, making it a valuable resource for researchers in the field of mixup and data augmentation.

**Weaknesses:**
- The novelty of the benchmark is questioned, as similar benchmarks have been proposed previously, such as MixupBench and Mixup-Benchmark.
- The technical contributions of the paper are limited, as the benchmarking and the evaluation of mixup methods are seen as incremental improvements over existing works.
- The paper could benefit from a more in-depth analysis of the mixup methods, particularly in terms of their performance and the factors influencing their effectiveness.
- The paper lacks a discussion on the limitations of the mixup methods and the potential negative societal impacts of their use.
- The benchmark's focus on mixup methods might limit its applicability to other data augmentation techniques, which could also benefit from similar evaluation frameworks.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors clarify the differences between OpenMixup and existing benchmarks like MixupBench and Mixup-Benchmark?
- How do the authors justify the choice of mixup methods included in the benchmark, and what criteria were used for their selection?
- Could the authors provide more detailed explanations for the observed performance differences between different mixup methods, especially in terms of their impact on training stability and convergence?
- How do the authors plan to address the limitations of the benchmark, such as its focus on mixup methods and its potential to be extended to other data augmentation techniques?
- Could the authors discuss the potential negative societal impacts of mixup methods, particularly in terms of data privacy and security?
