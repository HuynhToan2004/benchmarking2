 **Summary:**
The paper investigates the convergence properties of the top-K sparse softmax gating mixture of experts (MoE) model, specifically focusing on the Gaussian MoE model. It establishes theoretical results regarding the effects of the top-K sparse softmax gating function on both density and parameter estimations. The authors demonstrate that when the true number of experts is known, the convergence rates of density and parameter estimations are both parametric on the sample size. However, when the true number of experts is unknown, the findings suggest that the number of experts selected from the top-K sparse softmax gating function must exceed the total cardinality of a certain number of Voronoi cells associated with the true parameters to ensure convergence of the density estimation. The paper also proposes novel loss functions to capture different behaviors of the input regions and discusses the practical implications of these findings.

**Strengths:**
- The paper provides rigorous statistical guarantees for density estimation and parameter estimation in the top-K sparse softmax gating Gaussian MoE under both exact-specified and over-specified settings, contributing to a better understanding of the gating function in MoE models.
- The paper is well-organized, clearly written, and easy to follow, making it accessible to readers.
- The theoretical results are original and significant, contributing to the field of MoE models.
- The paper addresses a practical problem in the MoE community by providing a theoretical understanding of the gating function.
- The paper establishes convergence rates of density and parameter estimations for Gaussian MoE models with sparse softmax gating, which is of significant interest to the community.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks empirical validation, which is crucial for demonstrating the practical applicability and effectiveness of the proposed theoretical results.
- The assumptions made in the paper, such as the compactness of the parameter space and the boundedness of the input space, are not sufficiently justified or discussed.
- The paper could benefit from more detailed discussions on the practical implications of the theoretical results, particularly in the context of real-world applications.
- There is a lack of comparison with existing works, which could help in positioning the paper within the current literature.
- The paper's scope is limited to Gaussian MoE models, which may not generalize well to other types of MoE models.
- The paper's assumptions, such as the convergence of the MLE and the identifiability of the model, are not adequately justified or discussed.

**Questions:**
- How do the authors justify the assumptions made in the paper, particularly the compactness of the parameter space and the boundedness of the input space?
- Can the authors provide examples or scenarios where the theoretical results can be applied in practical settings?
- How does the paper's theoretical analysis compare with existing works, particularly those by Nguyen et al. (2023a, b)?
- Can the authors provide a comparison between the convergence rates of the proposed model and those of existing models, such as those by Ho et al. (2022)?
- How do the authors address the issue of identifiability in their model, and what are the implications of this assumption on the practical applicability of the model?
- Can the authors provide a discussion on the practical implications of their theoretical results, particularly in terms of their relevance and applicability to real-world problems?
