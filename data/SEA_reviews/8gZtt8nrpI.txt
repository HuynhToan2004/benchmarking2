 **Summary:**
The paper introduces MuLAN, a novel diffusion model that incorporates learned adaptive noise schedules for each pixel in an image, conditioned on a context variable such as a class label or a vector of attributes. This model aims to enhance the likelihood of the data by optimizing the noise schedule, which is typically fixed in existing models. The authors argue that this approach allows for a tighter lower bound on the marginal likelihood of the data, potentially improving performance. The paper presents experimental results on CIFAR10 and ImageNet datasets, showing some improvements in likelihood estimation compared to existing methods. However, the paper's theoretical contributions are questioned, and the experimental results are considered insufficient to fully support the claims.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel approach to learning the diffusion process, which is a significant departure from previous methods that typically fix the noise schedule.
- Theoretical arguments are provided to support the approach, including a discussion on the invariance of the Evidence Lower Bound (ELBO) to the choice of noise schedule.
- The methodology is well-explained, and the paper is generally easy to follow, with clear descriptions of the experimental setup and results.
- The paper presents an interesting perspective on the application of Bayesian inference in diffusion models, which could lead to more effective training strategies.
- The approach of learning a noise schedule that is conditioned on the input data is innovative and could be useful in various applications, such as image generation and denoising.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a thorough discussion on the limitations of the proposed method, particularly concerning the scalability and generalizability of the learned noise schedules across different datasets and conditions.
- The experimental results are not convincing, with the improvements over existing methods being marginal and not statistically significant in some cases.
- There is a lack of clarity on how the context variable, c, is chosen and its impact on the performance of the model.
- The paper does not sufficiently compare the proposed method with other existing methods, such as those that use a noise schedule based on image attributes or those that learn noise schedules for different image regions.
- The theoretical contributions are questioned, particularly regarding the claim that the proposed method allows for a tighter lower bound on the marginal likelihood of the data.
- The paper does not provide sufficient ablation studies to isolate the contributions of different components of the method, such as the auxiliary variable and the noise schedule.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors clarify how the context variable, c, is chosen and its impact on the performance of the model?
- How does the proposed method compare with other existing methods that use a noise schedule based on image attributes or learn noise schedules for different image regions?
- Can the authors provide more details on the training procedure, particularly how the noise schedule is learned and how it affects the training stability?
- How does the proposed method handle the potential instability in the learning objective due to the conditioning on context?
- Could the authors provide more experimental evidence to support the claims made about the effectiveness of the learned noise schedules, such as visualizations of the noise schedules or ablation studies on different datasets?
- How does the proposed method scale to larger datasets and more complex models, and what are the computational costs associated with the additional components of the model?
