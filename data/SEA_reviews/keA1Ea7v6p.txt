 **Summary:**
The paper introduces FedGC, a novel federated learning (FL) framework that leverages generative content to address data heterogeneity. FedGC generates diverse data using a generative model and merges it with real data to enhance model training. The framework is evaluated across various datasets and FL algorithms, demonstrating improvements in data heterogeneity and model performance. Despite its innovative approach, concerns are raised about the practicality of the method due to the computational overhead and the potential for data leakage. The paper also lacks a thorough theoretical analysis and comprehensive empirical validation, particularly in terms of privacy and scalability.

**Strengths:**
- The paper addresses a significant and relevant issue in federated learning (FL) by exploring the use of generative models to mitigate data heterogeneity.
- The methodology is simple and easy to implement, making it accessible for practical applications.
- Extensive experiments are conducted across various datasets, FL algorithms, and data heterogeneity levels, providing a robust validation of the proposed methods.
- The paper is well-written, clear, and easy to follow, with a logical structure that enhances understanding.
- The proposed FedGC framework is flexible and adaptable to various scenarios, showing potential for broad application in FL.
- The paper introduces interesting findings on the effects of data heterogeneity and the role of generated data in enhancing model performance.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a comprehensive theoretical analysis, particularly in the areas of data heterogeneity and the impact of generative content on model performance.
- There is a significant computational overhead due to the generation of additional data, which may not be practical in resource-constrained environments.
- The privacy implications of the method are not adequately addressed, with concerns about potential data leakage and the security of the generative model.
- The paper does not provide a detailed comparison with existing methods, such as FedDecorr, which also addresses data heterogeneity in FL.
- The method's effectiveness is not thoroughly validated under different conditions, such as varying levels of data heterogeneity and different FL algorithms.
- The paper does not discuss the scalability of the method with a large number of clients or the impact of different client participation rates.
- The paper lacks a detailed discussion on the limitations and potential negative societal impacts of the proposed method.

**Questions:**
- Could you provide a more detailed theoretical analysis of the impact of data heterogeneity on model performance and how the proposed method addresses these issues?
- How does the proposed method compare in terms of computational overhead and privacy concerns with existing methods, such as FedDecorr?
- Can you clarify the role of the generative model in data privacy and provide details on how you ensure the security of the model?
- How does the method perform under different levels of data heterogeneity, and what are the optimal conditions for its application?
- Can you discuss the scalability of the method with a large number of clients and the impact of different client participation rates?
- How does the method handle the potential for overfitting due to the inclusion of generated data, and what measures are in place to prevent this?
- Could you provide more details on the experimental setup, such as the number of communication rounds and the specifics of the federated learning algorithms used?
