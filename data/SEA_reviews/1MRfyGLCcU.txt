 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel method for semi-supervised feature selection using graph representation learning (GRL) in tabular data. The approach involves translating unlabeled data into a bipartite graph, where features and samples are represented as nodes, and edges represent the weight of each feature. This graph is utilized to learn low-dimensional embeddings of features and samples, which are then used to select relevant features for classification and regression tasks. The methodology involves a self-supervised edge prediction task and a batch-attention feature weight generation mechanism, which are evaluated on various datasets. Despite the innovative approach, the paper faces criticism for its clarity in explaining the methodology, the novelty of the contributions, and the comparative analysis with existing methods.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written and easy to follow, with a clear structure and logical flow.
- The motivation behind the research is well-articulated, and the methodology is detailed, making it easy to replicate.
- The experiments are thorough, with a variety of baselines used for comparison, and the results are convincing, showing the effectiveness of the proposed method.
- The paper provides a novel approach to feature selection by utilizing graph representation learning, which is a significant advancement in the field.
- The paper includes an ablation study that helps in understanding the impact of different components of the proposed method.
- The paper is well-organized, with clear figures and tables that aid in understanding the experimental results.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a clear explanation of the novelty and contribution of the proposed method compared to existing works, particularly in terms of the use of graph neural networks (GNNs) for feature selection.
- The methodology section could be improved for clarity, especially in explaining the GNN model and the message passing function.
- The experimental setup and results are not sufficiently detailed, with some figures and tables not adequately explained or interpreted.
- There is a lack of discussion on the limitations of the proposed method, which is crucial for understanding its applicability and potential drawbacks.
- The paper does not adequately compare the proposed method with state-of-the-art methods, which could undermine the perceived effectiveness of the proposed method.
- The paper suffers from some grammatical errors and could benefit from better proofreading and editing.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors clarify the novelty and contribution of their method compared to existing works, particularly in terms of the use of GNNs for feature selection?
- How does the proposed method perform when compared to state-of-the-art methods on various datasets, and what are the key differences in performance and effectiveness?
- Could the authors provide a more detailed explanation of the GNN model and the message passing function used in their method?
- How does the proposed method handle the issue of noise in the data, and what measures are in place to ensure the reliability of the selected features?
- Could the authors provide more detailed results and interpretations for the figures and tables used in the paper, particularly those that are not adequately explained in the text?
- How does the proposed method perform when applied to different types of data, such as image or text data, and what are the implications for its effectiveness in these domains?
