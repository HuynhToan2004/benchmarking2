 **Summary:**
The paper explores the challenge of optimizing reward models in language models, focusing on the issue of overoptimization and the correlation between different reward models. The authors propose using constrained reinforcement learning to prevent overoptimization, introducing a novel approach to weighting component reward models dynamically. This method, which involves the use of Lagrange multipliers and gradient-free optimization, aims to optimize towards points where the component reward models are most effective. The paper is supported by empirical results showing that the proposed method can improve evaluation performance by preventing overoptimization. However, the paper is criticized for its lack of theoretical backing, limited scope of testing, and potential over-reliance on heuristics.

**Strengths:**
- The paper addresses an important and relevant problem in the field of language models, focusing on the challenge of overoptimization in reward models.
- The proposed method, which involves using constrained reinforcement learning to prevent overoptimization, is novel and offers a potentially effective solution to the identified problem.
- The paper is well-written, clear, and easy to follow, making it accessible to a broad audience.
- The empirical results demonstrate that the proposed method can improve evaluation performance by preventing overoptimization.
- The paper introduces a new approach to weighting component reward models dynamically, which is a significant contribution to the field.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a thorough theoretical analysis, which could enhance the understanding and validation of the proposed method.
- The experiments are limited in scope, focusing only on dialogue generation with a single dataset, which might not generalize well to other applications or datasets.
- The paper heavily relies on heuristics, which could limit the reproducibility and applicability of the results.
- The paper does not adequately address the issue of overoptimization in general, as it only focuses on the specific case of composite reward functions.
- The paper's reliance on heuristics, such as the use of sigmoid and tanh functions to bound Lagrange multipliers, lacks a clear theoretical justification.
- The paper does not sufficiently address the scalability of the proposed method, particularly in terms of the complexity introduced by the additional reward models and the computational requirements.

**Questions:**
- How does the proposed method perform when the reward models are not correlated?
- Can the method be applied to other types of tasks beyond dialogue generation?
- Is there a way to test the method without access to ground-truth queries?
- Could you provide more details on the computational requirements and scalability of the proposed method?
- How does the method handle the case where the reward models are not directly comparable or have different scales?
- What are the implications of using different types of reward models, such as those based on human evaluations or other types of feedback?
- How does the method handle the issue of overoptimization in general, not just in the context of composite reward functions?
- Can you clarify the role and impact of the KL penalty in your experiments, particularly in relation to the optimization of the proxy point?
