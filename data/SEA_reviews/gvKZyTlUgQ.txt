 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel method for estimating 2D projective transformations using a warped convolutional neural network (WCN). The approach decomposes the projective transformation into six commutative subgroups, each associated with a warp function, allowing for efficient and effective learning of homography through SL(3) algebra. The method is evaluated on various datasets, including MNIST-Proj, S-COCO, and POT, showing improvements over existing methods in homography estimation, planar object tracking, and classification tasks. Despite its innovative approach, the paper faces criticism for its clarity in presentation, the complexity of its methodology, and the limited scope of its experiments.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel approach to estimating projective transformations using a warped convolutional neural network, which is a significant contribution to the field.
- The methodology is well-articulated, with clear explanations of the mathematical underpinnings and the experimental setup.
- The paper demonstrates strong performance in homography estimation and planar object tracking, showing a significant improvement over existing methods.
- The use of SL(3) algebra to represent homography is innovative and could be a useful tool for further research in this area.
- The paper is well-written and easy to follow, with clear figures and detailed explanations that aid in understanding the complex mathematical concepts.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a clear explanation of the advantages and disadvantages of the proposed method compared to existing methods, which could help in understanding the trade-offs involved.
- The experiments are limited to a few datasets and do not include a broader range of tasks, such as image registration or panorama stitching, which could demonstrate the general applicability of the method.
- The paper suffers from poor presentation, with complex equations and figures that are not well-explained, making it difficult for readers to grasp the methodology fully.
- The methodology is complex and involves multiple stages, which could make it difficult to implement and understand fully, especially for readers not familiar with the specific mathematical concepts used.
- The paper could benefit from a more comprehensive discussion of related work and a clearer explanation of the contributions of the proposed method compared to existing methods.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors clarify the advantages and disadvantages of their proposed method compared to existing methods, especially in terms of computational efficiency and accuracy?
- How does the proposed method perform in more complex scenarios, such as image registration or panorama stitching?
- Can the authors provide more detailed explanations of the warp functions and their impact on the performance of the method?
- How does the method handle different types of projective transformations, such as those involving reflection or translation?
- Could the authors simplify the presentation of the paper to make it more accessible to a broader audience, particularly those not familiar with the specific mathematical concepts used?
