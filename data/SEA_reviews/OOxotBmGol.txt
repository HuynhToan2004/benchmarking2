 **Summary:**
The paper introduces LLAMBO, a novel Bayesian optimization (BO) framework that integrates large language models (LLMs) to enhance model-based BO. The framework comprises three primary components: warmstarting, surrogate modeling, and candidate sampling. The authors demonstrate that LLMs can effectively initialize BO through zero-shot prompting, improve surrogate modeling, and enhance candidate sampling. The methodology is tested across various benchmarks, showing promising results in hyperparameter tuning. Despite the innovative approach, concerns about the clarity of the methodological description and the need for more rigorous experimental validation persist.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, with a clear and concise presentation of the research problem, methodology, and results.
- The integration of large language models (LLMs) into Bayesian optimization (BO) is a novel and innovative approach that has the potential to significantly advance the field.
- The methodology is well-motivated, with clear explanations of how LLMs can enhance various components of BO, including warmstarting, surrogate modeling, and candidate sampling.
- The paper is thorough in its investigation of LLM's role in BO, with comprehensive evaluations across different aspects of the methodology.
- The experimental results show significant improvements over baseline methods, indicating the potential of LLMs in BO.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed discussion on the limitations and potential negative societal impacts of the proposed method.
- The clarity of the methodological descriptions, particularly in Section 2.1, could be improved to better understand the specifics of how LLMs are integrated into the BO process.
- The experimental setup could be expanded to include more diverse datasets and a broader range of hyperparameter tuning tasks to better validate the generalizability of the findings.
- The paper could benefit from a more thorough discussion on the computational complexity and efficiency of LLMs in BO, as well as potential strategies for reducing the computational burden.
- There is a need for more rigorous experimental validation, including comparisons with more diverse baseline methods and additional experimental results to support the claims made.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors clarify how the LLM is integrated into the BO process, particularly in terms of the specific natural language queries used and the format of the responses?
- How does the LLM's performance compare to other surrogate modeling methods, such as Gaussian processes?
- Can the authors provide more details on the computational complexity and efficiency of LLMs in BO, including the cost of each step in the optimization process?
- Would it be possible to include more diverse datasets and hyperparameter tuning tasks in future experiments to validate the generalizability of the findings?
- How do the authors address the concerns regarding the clarity of the methodological descriptions and the need for more rigorous experimental validation?
