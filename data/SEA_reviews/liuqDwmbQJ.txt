 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel benchmark, ViLMA, designed to evaluate the temporal reasoning capabilities of video-language models (VidLMs). This benchmark comprises five tests, each with proficiency and main tasks, designed to assess the models' understanding of temporal phenomena in videos. The tests include action counting, situation awareness, change of state, rare actions, and spatial relations. The benchmark is task-agnostic and evaluates models based on their ability to distinguish correct from counterfactual video-text pairs, providing a controlled evaluation suite that reveals the true potential of VidLMs. The paper also includes a proficiency test to assess basic capabilities and demonstrates that current VidLMs perform no better than image-language models (ILMs) in temporal reasoning tasks.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel benchmark, ViLMA, which is the first to test video-language models (VidLMs) on their temporal reasoning capabilities.
- The benchmark is task-agnostic and includes a proficiency test to assess basic capabilities, making it a valuable tool for evaluating VidLMs.
- The paper is well-written, with a clear motivation and a comprehensive evaluation of 12 VidLMs and 2 ILMs, demonstrating that current VidLMs perform no better than ILMs in temporal reasoning tasks.
- The use of counterfactual examples to evaluate VidLMs is innovative and provides a controlled evaluation suite that sheds light on the true potential of these models.
- The paper is well-organized, with a clear structure and detailed descriptions of the data generation methodology and experimental setup.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed explanation of how the foil generation process was conducted, which is crucial for understanding the benchmark's design and the validity of the results.
- The proficiency tests, while designed to assess basic capabilities, do not always align with the main tests in terms of difficulty or relevance, which could affect the evaluation's consistency and validity.
- The benchmark's focus on temporal reasoning is limited, as it only evaluates a subset of VidLMs that have been pre-trained on temporal data. This limits the generalizability of the results to all VidLMs.
- The paper does not include a detailed discussion on how the benchmark's results could be used to guide future research in VidLMs, which could be a significant contribution to the field.
- The use of different models for different tasks and the lack of a unified model for all tasks could lead to inconsistencies in the evaluation results.
- The paper could benefit from more detailed explanations of the foil generation process and the selection criteria for the proficiency tests to enhance the benchmark's validity and reliability.

**Questions:**
- Could you provide more details on how the foil generation process was conducted? What specific criteria were used to ensure the validity and relevance of the foils to the main tests?
- How were the proficiency tests designed to align with the main tests in terms of difficulty and relevance?
- Why were different models used for different tasks, and how does this affect the consistency and reliability of the evaluation results?
- How does the benchmark's focus on temporal reasoning compare to other benchmarks that evaluate VidLMs on temporal understanding?
- Could you elaborate on how the benchmark's results could be used to guide future research in VidLMs? What specific insights or directions for improvement can be derived from the benchmark's findings?
