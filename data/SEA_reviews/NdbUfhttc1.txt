 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel learned optimizer for reinforcement learning (RL) named Optim4RL, which addresses challenges such as the non-iid distribution of gradients, the complexity of agent-gradient distributions, and the stochastic nature of agent-environment interactions. The authors propose a pipeline training approach, gradient processing, and a novel optimizer structure to enhance the stability and effectiveness of RL optimizers. The paper demonstrates the effectiveness of Optim4RL through experiments on both toy tasks and complex RL environments like Brax, showing its potential for generalization to unseen tasks. Despite its innovative approach, the paper is criticized for its limited evaluation scope, focusing primarily on simple RL environments and not comparing against a broader range of existing optimizers.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, clear, and easy to follow, making it accessible to a broad audience.
- It identifies and addresses critical challenges in learning optimizers for RL, such as the non-iid distribution of gradients, the complexity of agent-gradient distributions, and the stochastic nature of agent-environment interactions.
- The proposed method, Optim4RL, is novel and innovative, providing a new approach to learning optimizers for RL.
- The paper includes a comprehensive evaluation, demonstrating the effectiveness of the proposed method across a variety of tasks and environments.
- The authors have conducted a thorough investigation into the properties of the gradient distribution, which is crucial for understanding the behavior of RL optimizers.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a comprehensive evaluation, particularly in terms of the variety of environments used for training and testing. It primarily focuses on simple RL environments and does not include more complex or varied tasks.
- The generalization ability of the learned optimizer is limited, as it is only tested on tasks similar to those used for training.
- There is a lack of comparison with other existing optimizers for RL, which could provide a better understanding of the relative performance of the proposed method.
- The paper does not discuss the potential impact of the learned optimizer on hyperparameter tuning, which is crucial in RL.
- The paper lacks a detailed discussion on the limitations and potential negative societal impacts of the proposed method.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors provide more details on how the learned optimizer might be applied in real-world RL scenarios, especially in terms of its impact on hyperparameter tuning?
- How does the proposed method compare to other existing optimizers for RL, particularly in terms of performance and computational efficiency?
- Can the authors provide more insights into the generalization ability of the learned optimizer, perhaps by testing it on tasks that are significantly different from those used for training?
- Is there a possibility to extend the training of Optim4RL to a larger scale, and what are the expected improvements in performance?
- Could the authors discuss the potential negative societal impacts of their proposed method, particularly in the context of RL applications?
