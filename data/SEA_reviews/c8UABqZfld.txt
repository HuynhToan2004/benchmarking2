 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel loss function, Spatial Matching Loss (SM Loss), designed for mass segmentation in mammography images. The SM Loss integrates higher-level spatial information such as relative distance, sizes, and quantities, which are crucial for mass segmentation. The loss function is evaluated on the INbreast dataset and shows significant improvements over existing methods like AU-Net. The SM Loss is formulated to address the limitations of existing pixel-level losses by incorporating grid-based loss calculation for local comparison and sample-level cell size extraction to enhance local comparison. The paper also includes an ablation study to validate the effectiveness of the proposed loss function.

**Strengths:**
- The proposed SM Loss is a novel approach that integrates higher-level spatial information into loss calculation, which is a significant advancement in mass segmentation for mammography images.
- The paper is well-organized, making it easy to follow, and includes a comprehensive ablation study that demonstrates the effectiveness of the proposed loss function.
- The experimental results are promising, showing that the SM Loss outperforms existing methods on the INbreast dataset, indicating its effectiveness.
- The paper addresses a critical issue in mammography by focusing on the limitations of existing pixel-level losses and proposing a new method that incorporates grid-based loss calculation and sample-level cell size extraction.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed explanation of how the proposed loss function addresses the limitations of existing pixel-level losses, which could be improved by including a more thorough comparison with existing methods.
- The evaluation is limited to a single dataset (INbreast), which may not adequately demonstrate the generalizability of the proposed method across different datasets.
- The paper does not discuss the limitations of the proposed method, which could provide a more balanced view of the research.
- The paper does not include a comparison with other recent methods, such as ASP-Q and Density-ASP, which could provide a more comprehensive evaluation of the proposed method.
- The paper does not include a discussion on the limitations of the proposed method, which could provide a more balanced view of the research.
- The paper could benefit from a more detailed explanation of the algorithm, particularly in the "Extract Grid Size" module and the "Extract False Positive Cells" module, to enhance understanding.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors provide more details on how the proposed loss function addresses the limitations of existing pixel-level losses?
- How does the proposed method perform on other datasets, and what are the implications for its generalizability?
- Could the authors discuss the limitations of the proposed method and how these might affect its practical application?
- In the context of the paper, how does the proposed method compare to other recent methods, such as ASP-Q and Density-ASP?
- Could the authors provide a more detailed explanation of the algorithm, particularly in the "Extract Grid Size" module and the "Extract False Positive Cells" module, to enhance understanding?
