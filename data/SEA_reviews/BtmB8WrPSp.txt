 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel white-box attack method named sparse-PGD for generating adversarial perturbations with an l0 norm constraint. This method decomposes perturbations into a magnitude tensor and a sparsity mask, optimizing them iteratively to ensure the sparsity of perturbations. The method is evaluated against various models and shows improved robustness against sparse perturbations. Additionally, the paper proposes a new ensemble attack, sparse-AutoAttack, which combines sparse-PGD with a black-box attack for comprehensive evaluation. The authors also explore adversarial training using their attack method to enhance model robustness against sparse perturbations. Despite its innovative approach, the paper faces criticisms for its limited experimental scope, lack of comparative analysis with existing methods, and unclear presentation in some sections.

**Strengths:**
- The paper addresses the significant issue of sparse adversarial perturbations, which are less studied compared to other bounded perturbations, and provides a novel method for generating such perturbations.
- The methodology is clearly explained, and the paper is well-written, making it easy to follow.
- The paper introduces a new attack method, sparse-PGD, which decomposes perturbations into a magnitude tensor and a sparsity mask, optimizing them iteratively to ensure the sparsity of perturbations.
- The proposed attack method shows improved robustness against sparse perturbations compared to previous methods, as demonstrated by extensive experiments.
- The paper includes an ensemble attack, sparse-AutoAttack, which combines sparse-PGD with a black-box attack, providing a more comprehensive evaluation against l0 bounded perturbation.
- The adversarial training using the proposed attack method shows improved robustness against sparse perturbations, which is a significant contribution to the field.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a comprehensive evaluation of the proposed method against various types of adversarial perturbations, such as l1-norm and l2-norm bounded perturbations, which could provide a more holistic understanding of the method's effectiveness.
- There is a lack of comparative analysis with existing methods, which could help in understanding the method's advantages and disadvantages more clearly.
- The experimental scope is limited, focusing primarily on CIFAR10, which may not adequately represent the method's performance across different datasets.
- The paper does not adequately address the computational complexity of the proposed method, which could limit its practical application in real-world scenarios.
- The presentation of the paper needs improvement, particularly in the explanation of certain equations and the organization of sections, which could make the paper more accessible to a broader audience.
- The paper does not sufficiently discuss the limitations of the proposed method, which could help in understanding its applicability and potential drawbacks.

**Questions:**
- Could you provide more details on the computational complexity of the proposed method, including the time and memory requirements?
- How does the proposed method compare with existing methods in terms of effectiveness and efficiency?
- In the experiments, how were the hyperparameters, such as the learning rate and number of iterations, chosen, and how do they affect the performance of the method?
- Could you clarify the optimization process for the sparsity mask and the unprojected gradient, especially in the context of the l0 norm optimization?
- How does the proposed method perform on other datasets, such as CIFAR100 or ImageNet, and what are the implications of these results for the generalizability of the method?
- Could you provide more details on the convergence behavior of the proposed method and how it compares with other methods in terms of robustness and efficiency?
