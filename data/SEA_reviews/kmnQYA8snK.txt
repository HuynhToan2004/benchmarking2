 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel method for verifying the correctness of machine learning model inference using ZK-SNARKs, focusing on the MobileNetV2 model on ImageNet. The authors claim that their method is scalable and efficient, with a verification time of 3.23 seconds and a proof size of 5.4KB, which is significantly smaller than previous methods. The paper also explores the application of ZK-SNARKs in various scenarios such as model accuracy verification, model prediction verification, and trustless retrieval, demonstrating the practicality and efficiency of the proposed method. However, the paper is criticized for its lack of novelty, insufficient experimental validation, and unclear presentation, which limits its impact and the understanding of its contributions.

**Strengths:**
- The paper presents a significant improvement in verification time and proof size compared to previous works, which is crucial for practical applications.
- The application of ZK-SNARKs in various scenarios such as model accuracy verification, model prediction verification, and trustless retrieval demonstrates the versatility and potential of the technology.
- The paper is well-written and easy to follow, making the complex concepts accessible to a broader audience.
- The paper provides a detailed analysis of the performance of ZK-SNARKs, including accuracy, setup time, proving time, and verification time, which is crucial for understanding the practical implications of the technology.
- The paper includes a comprehensive evaluation of the proposed method, which is essential for validating the claims made and demonstrating the effectiveness of the proposed solution.

**Weaknesses:**
