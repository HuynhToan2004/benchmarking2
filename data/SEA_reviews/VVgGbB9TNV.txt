 **Summary:**
The paper introduces PromptAttack, a prompt-based adversarial attack designed to evaluate the adversarial robustness of large language models (LLMs) by manipulating the input prompt to generate adversarial samples. The attack consists of three components: original input, attack objective, and attack guidance, which are used to guide the LLM in generating adversarial text. The methodology is evaluated against models like GPT-3.5 and Llama2, showing promising results in terms of attack success rate (ASR) on the GLUE benchmark. However, the paper's novelty is questioned, as it primarily recombines existing methods without significant innovation. The evaluation is also criticized for not adequately comparing with other state-of-the-art attacks and for not providing a comprehensive analysis of the attack's effectiveness across different models and datasets.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, with clear and concise descriptions of the proposed attack and its components.
- The use of a prompt-based adversarial attack to evaluate the robustness of LLMs is innovative and addresses a critical issue in the field.
- The methodology is straightforward and easy to implement, with a focus on practical applications.
- The paper includes a comprehensive set of experiments that demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed attack, particularly against GPT-3.5 and Llama2 models.
- The paper is well-organized and provides a detailed analysis of the attack's effectiveness, including the impact of different perturbation levels and task descriptions.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed comparison with other state-of-the-art attacks, which limits the understanding of its relative performance.
- The evaluation is limited to a few models and datasets, raising concerns about the generalizability of the results.
- The novelty of the proposed attack is questionable, as it primarily combines existing methods without significant innovation.
- The paper does not provide a comprehensive analysis of the attack's effectiveness across different models and datasets, which is crucial for understanding its robustness.
- There is a lack of discussion on the limitations of the proposed method, particularly in terms of its applicability to different types of LLMs and the potential for transferability of the attacks.
- The paper does not adequately address the computational cost and efficiency of the attack, which is a significant concern for practical applications.

**Questions:**
- How does the proposed attack compare in terms of computational cost and efficiency with other state-of-the-art attacks?
- Can the authors provide more detailed comparisons with other attacks, including the AdvGLUE and AdvGLUE++ datasets, to better understand the relative performance of their method?
- How does the proposed attack perform against different types of LLMs, and what are the potential limitations or challenges in applying the attack to these models?
- Can the authors provide more examples of adversarial examples generated by their method, particularly in the appendix, to enhance the reproducibility and transparency of the results?
- How does the proposed attack transfer to other types of LLMs, and what are the implications of this transferability for the robustness of the attacks?
- Can the authors discuss the potential implications of their work for the broader field of LLM security, particularly in terms of developing defensive strategies against adversarial attacks?
