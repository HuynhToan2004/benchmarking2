 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel horizon-free algorithm for adversarial linear mixture MDPs, named HF-O2PS, which utilizes an occupancy measure and high-order moment estimator to achieve a near-optimal regret bound. The algorithm employs variance-uncertainty-aware weighted least square estimator for the transition kernel and an occupancy measure-based technique for the online search of a stochastic policy. Theoretical analysis is provided to demonstrate the algorithm's effectiveness, including a hardness result to justify the near optimality of the algorithm. The paper also addresses the challenges posed by exploration and adversarially chosen reward over episodes, and provides a regret upper bound of O~(d√K+d2).

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, clearly presenting the ideas and results.
- The algorithm design is innovative, using a variance-uncertainty-aware weighted least square estimator for the transition kernel and an occupancy measure-based technique for the online search of a stochastic policy.
- The regret upper bound is derived, which is a significant contribution to the field.
- The paper provides a hardness result to justify the near optimality of the algorithm, enhancing the credibility of the results.
- The paper is technically sound and provides a comprehensive review of related work, which is beneficial for readers unfamiliar with the topic.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed comparison with existing works, particularly in terms of the novelty and improvement over existing methods.
- The regret upper bound is not optimal, as it does not match the lower bound, which raises questions about the tightness of the analysis.
- The computational complexity of the algorithm is not discussed, which is crucial for practical implementation.
- The paper could benefit from a more thorough discussion on the limitations of the proposed algorithm, such as its applicability in different MDP settings.
- The notation used in the paper is sometimes confusing and could be improved for better clarity and understanding.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors provide a more detailed comparison with existing works, particularly in terms of the novelty and improvement over existing methods?
- Why is the regret upper bound not optimal, and how does it compare to the lower bound provided in the paper?
- What is the computational complexity of the proposed algorithm, and how does it compare to other existing methods?
- Is there a specific reason why the algorithm was designed to be applicable only to linear mixture MDPs?
- Can the authors clarify the notation and provide a clearer explanation of the computational steps involved in the algorithm?
