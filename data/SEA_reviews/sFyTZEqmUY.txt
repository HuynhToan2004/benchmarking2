 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel approach to learning a universal simulator (UniSim) of real-world interaction through generative modeling, integrating various datasets including internet text-image pairs, robotics, human activities, panorama scans, and simulated data. UniSim is formulated as an observation prediction model, approximating sampling in a POMDP, and is trained using a video diffusion model. The model's capabilities are demonstrated through its application in training embodied planners, low-level control policies, and video captioning models, showing potential in sim-to-real transfer. However, the paper faces criticism for its limited evaluation, lack of comprehensive comparisons, and unclear presentation, particularly in the methodology and experimental setup.

**Strengths:**
- The paper presents a novel approach to learning a universal simulator (UniSim) of real-world interaction, integrating diverse datasets in a conditional video generation framework.
- The application of UniSim is demonstrated across various domains, including embodied planners, low-level control policies, and video captioning models, showcasing its versatility.
- The paper is well-written, making it easy to follow, and includes a comprehensive literature review.
- The methodology of UniSim is well-explained, with clear illustrations of the training and inference processes.
- The paper includes several examples of UniSim's application, such as training an embodied planner, a low-level control policy, and a video captioning model, demonstrating its effectiveness.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks comprehensive comparisons with other existing methods for learning real-world simulators, which could help in understanding the novelty and effectiveness of UniSim.
- There is a lack of quantitative evaluation, which makes it difficult to assess the performance of UniSim objectively.
- The methodology and experimental setup are not clearly presented, particularly the training details and the generation process of UniSim.
- The paper does not discuss the limitations of the proposed method, which is crucial for understanding its applicability and potential drawbacks.
- The presentation of the paper could be improved, particularly in sections where the methodology and experimental setup are described.
- There is a need for more detailed explanations and examples, especially in the introduction and application sections, to enhance reader comprehension.

**Questions:**

- How does UniSim handle long-horizon repeated interactions, and what are the specific techniques used to ensure consistency across video generations?
- Can you clarify the role of the classifier-free guidance in the generation process and how it influences the output of UniSim?
- How does the method handle the challenge of long-horizon planning, and what are the specific techniques used to ensure that the plan remains effective over extended periods?
- Could you provide more details on the training process, such as the specific datasets used and the parameters of the diffusion model?
- How does the method ensure that the simulated environment remains realistic and consistent with real-world dynamics, especially when dealing with complex tasks like robot manipulation?
- Could you discuss the limitations of the proposed method and how they might affect its applicability in various scenarios?
