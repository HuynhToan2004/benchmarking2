 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel self-supervised framework named JOSENet for violence detection in surveillance videos. It utilizes a two-stream architecture that integrates both RGB frames and optical flows to enhance the detection performance. The proposed method leverages a variance-invariance-covariance regularization (VICReg) approach to optimize the network's performance and reduce computational costs. The framework is evaluated on various datasets, including UCF-Crime, HMDB51, and UCF101, demonstrating its effectiveness in real-time applications. Despite its innovative approach, the paper suffers from a lack of clarity in some sections, including the methodology and experimental results, and the paper's presentation could be improved for better comprehension.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel self-supervised framework for violence detection in surveillance videos, which is an important and challenging task in the field of computer vision.
- The proposed method is efficient in terms of memory usage and computational cost, making it suitable for real-time applications.
- The paper is well-written, with clear and detailed descriptions of the proposed method and its implementation, which aids in understanding and reproducibility.
- The experimental results are comprehensive, including various datasets and evaluation metrics, which demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed method.
- The paper is original in its approach, introducing a new SSL algorithm specifically designed for video streams, which could be of interest to the community.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks clarity in some sections, particularly in the methodology and experimental results, which could confuse readers and hinder the understanding of the proposed method.
- The novelty of the proposed method is questioned as it primarily builds upon existing works such as VICReg and FGN, with limited new contributions.
- The paper does not provide sufficient details on the computational efficiency of the proposed method, which is crucial for real-time applications.
- The paper could benefit from a more thorough discussion on the limitations of the proposed method, including potential biases in unfair prediction and the impact of different datasets.
- There are several grammatical and typographical errors throughout the paper, which detract from its professionalism and readability.

**Questions:**
- Could you clarify the role and effectiveness of the VICReg approach in the proposed method, especially in comparison to other SSL methods like InfoNCE and CoCLR?
- How does the proposed method perform in terms of computational efficiency, particularly in real-time applications?
- Can you provide more details on the preprocessing methods used for the auxiliary task and the impact of different datasets on the performance of the proposed method?
- How does the proposed method handle different types of violence, such as hate crimes, which might be more subtle and require different detection strategies?
- Could you elaborate on the limitations of the proposed method, particularly in terms of fairness and bias in prediction, and how these could be addressed in future work?
