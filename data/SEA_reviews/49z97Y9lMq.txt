 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a new metric for computing optimal transport on the unit circle, named Linear Circular Optimal Transport (LCOT). This metric is derived from the Linear Optimal Transport (LOT) framework and provides a computationally efficient method for comparing circular probability measures. The authors demonstrate the metric's effectiveness through numerical experiments, showing its utility in clustering and classifying probability measures. The paper also discusses the theoretical properties of the LCOT metric and its computational complexity. Despite its novelty and practical benefits, the paper has been critiqued for its lack of extensive theoretical analysis, particularly in the area of generalized linear models, and for not sufficiently distinguishing its contributions from existing literature.
