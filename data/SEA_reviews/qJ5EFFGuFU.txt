 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel method for implicit representation of images, focusing on integrating semantic information into the implicit representation to enhance image inpainting. The approach utilizes a semantic implicit representation (SIR) to capture semantic information and an appearance implicit representation (AIR) to reconstruct image details. This method is evaluated on datasets like CelebAHQ and ADE20K, showing superior performance over existing methods. The paper also includes an ablation study to demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed modules. However, the paper has been critiqued for its lack of clarity in explaining the methodology, particularly the role of the SIR and AIR modules, and the generalization of the method to different datasets and real-world applications.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel approach by integrating semantic information into the implicit representation, which is a significant departure from existing methods that focus solely on appearance.
- The proposed method demonstrates superior performance over state-of-the-art methods in image inpainting tasks, as evidenced by extensive experimental results and ablation studies.
- The paper is well-organized, clearly written, and provides detailed explanations of the proposed method, making it accessible and understandable to readers.
- The method's effectiveness is demonstrated through various experiments, including the use of different image encoders and implicit neural functions, which showcases its versatility and applicability.
- The paper includes a detailed ablation study that provides insights into the contributions of different components of the method, such as the SIR module, which is crucial for understanding the method's effectiveness.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks clarity in explaining the role and function of the SIR and AIR modules, which are central to the proposed method. The relationship between these modules and the overall methodology needs further clarification.
- The generalization of the method to different datasets and real-world applications is not adequately addressed. The method's performance on datasets like CelebAHQ, which primarily consists of human faces, might not translate well to other types of images.
- The paper does not include a discussion on the limitations of the method, which could provide valuable insights into the method's applicability and potential areas for improvement.
- The experimental setup and results are somewhat limited, focusing mainly on the CelebAHQ dataset and not including a broader range of datasets or real-world applications to validate the method's effectiveness.
- There are several minor issues with the presentation of the paper, such as unclear definitions and notations, which could confuse readers and detract from the paper's clarity.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors clarify the role and function of the SIR and AIR modules in the context of the proposed method? How do these modules interact and contribute to the overall methodology?
- How does the proposed method perform on other datasets, particularly those that do not primarily consist of human faces? Can the authors provide experimental results or insights into the method's performance on such datasets?
- Could the authors discuss the limitations of the method and provide suggestions for future research directions?
- How does the method handle the issue of missing semantic information in the input images? Is there a specific mechanism in place to address this issue?
- The paper mentions the use of a semantic implicit representation (SIR) to capture semantic information. Can the authors elaborate on how this representation is learned and how it contributes to the overall methodology?
- The paper includes several references to tables and figures that are not self-explanatory. Can the authors provide more detailed explanations or captions to clarify the content of these references?
