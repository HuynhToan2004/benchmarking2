 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel federated learning algorithm called FedHBM, which leverages a generalized heavy-ball momentum (GHB) method to enhance convergence speed and model quality in non-iid data scenarios. The proposed algorithm addresses the communication efficiency issue by allowing clients to utilize models from previous rounds, thus avoiding the need for additional communication rounds. The paper is supported by extensive experimental evaluations across various datasets, demonstrating the effectiveness of FedHBM in improving model quality and convergence speed. However, concerns were raised about the lack of theoretical analysis, the need for more diverse experimental settings, and the clarity in explaining the technical contributions of the proposed algorithm.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-organized, with clear and detailed explanations of the proposed method and its experimental settings, making it easy to follow.
- The authors provide a comprehensive comparison with other state-of-the-art algorithms, demonstrating the superiority of their method in terms of communication efficiency, model quality, and convergence speed.
- The paper includes a thorough ablation study, which helps in understanding the impact of different components of the proposed algorithm.
- The paper addresses the significant issue of communication efficiency in federated learning, which is crucial for practical applications.
- The proposed algorithm is simple and intuitive, making it potentially easier to implement and understand.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks theoretical analysis, which could strengthen the paper's claims and provide a deeper understanding of the proposed method.
- The experimental settings are somewhat limited, focusing primarily on non-iid data scenarios and not considering other types of heterogeneity such as non-stationary data or heterogeneous local update rules.
- The paper does not adequately address the limitations of the proposed method, such as its applicability to different types of heterogeneity and its scalability to larger datasets or more complex models.
- The paper does not provide sufficient details on the computational complexity of the proposed algorithm, which is crucial for understanding its practical applicability.
- There are several instances of unclear writing and potential errors in the manuscript, such as incorrect references and unclear explanations of technical terms.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors provide a theoretical analysis of the convergence properties of the proposed algorithm?
- How does the proposed algorithm perform in scenarios with non-iid data and heterogeneous local update rules?
- Can the authors clarify the technical contributions of the proposed algorithm compared to existing methods, particularly in terms of communication efficiency?
- What are the specific computational complexities of the proposed algorithm, and how do they compare to existing methods?
- How does the proposed algorithm handle different types of heterogeneity, such as non-stationary data or heterogeneous local update rules?
- Could the authors provide more details on the experimental settings, particularly the specifics of the non-iid data generation and the computational resources used?
