 **Summary:**
The paper introduces AsID, a method aimed at enhancing model-based reinforcement learning by integrating real-world data with simulators. AsID employs a three-stage process: exploration, system identification, and downstream task learning. The exploration phase involves the use of an exploration policy trained in simulation to collect data in the real world, which is then utilized to update the simulator's parameters. This process is designed to improve the accuracy of the simulator, facilitating the training of policies in simulation for real-world deployment. The paper claims that AsID can significantly reduce the amount of real-world data required for successful sim-to-real transfer, thereby enhancing the practical applicability of model-based RL in real-world scenarios.
