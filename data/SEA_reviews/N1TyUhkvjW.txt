 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel method for time series anomaly detection using a combination of reconstruction error and RBF similarity scores. This approach, termed the SimRec score, aims to enhance the detection of subtle anomalies by integrating a layer of RBF neurons within deep learning architectures. The method is evaluated on three benchmark datasets, showing improvements over baseline methods. The authors argue that this method addresses the limitations of reconstruction-based anomaly detection by integrating a non-parametric density estimate using a layer of RBF neurons. However, the paper is criticized for its lack of novelty, insufficient experimental validation, and unclear presentation.

**Strengths:**
- The paper addresses an important problem of time series anomaly detection, which is crucial for various applications.
- The proposed method is simple and easy to understand, making it accessible for practical implementation.
- The use of RBF similarity scores in addition to reconstruction error provides a more robust and comprehensive anomaly detection metric.
- The paper is well-written, making the methodology and results clear and easy to follow.
- The experimental results demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed method, showing improvements over baseline methods in some datasets.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks novelty as the use of RBF similarity scores for anomaly detection in time series data is not a new concept.
- The experimental validation is insufficient, with the paper not comparing the proposed method with a sufficient number of state-of-the-art methods, nor does it provide a comprehensive analysis of the experimental results.
- The paper suffers from poor presentation, with some figures and equations being unclear or inadequately explained.
- The method's performance is not consistently superior across all datasets, and the results are not convincingly superior to existing methods like the Anomaly Transformer.
- The paper lacks a detailed discussion on the limitations of the proposed method and does not include a discussion on the scalability of the proposed method with the size of the dataset.
- There are several typographical and formatting errors that need correction.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors clarify the novelty of their approach compared to existing methods that use RBF similarity scores for anomaly detection in time series data?
- Why was the Anomaly Transformer not included in the comparative analysis, and how does the proposed method compare to it?
- How does the proposed method perform on datasets with a larger number of samples, and what are the implications for its scalability?
- Can the authors provide a more detailed analysis of the experimental results, including the performance of the proposed method on datasets where it did not perform as well as the baseline methods?
- How does the proposed method compare to other state-of-the-art methods in terms of computational efficiency and scalability?
- Could the authors address the typographical and formatting errors in the paper to improve its clarity and professionalism?
