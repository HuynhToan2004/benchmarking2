 **Summary:**
The paper introduces OODRobustBench, a comprehensive benchmark for evaluating out-of-distribution (OOD) adversarial robustness. It assesses 706 models across 23 dataset shifts and 6 threat shifts, revealing that adversarial robustness often suffers from OOD generalization issues. The study also demonstrates a strong linear correlation between in-distribution (ID) and OOD adversarial robustness, suggesting that ID robustness can predict OOD robustness. The benchmark identifies several factors that enhance OOD robustness, including additional data, advanced model architectures, and data augmentation. Despite its strengths, the paper is critiqued for its limited exploration of the underlying causes of OOD robustness and the potential for misleading conclusions due to the correlation between ID and OOD robustness.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a comprehensive benchmark, OODRobustBench, which evaluates 706 models across 23 dataset shifts and 6 threat shifts, providing a robust assessment of OOD adversarial robustness.
- It reveals that adversarial robustness often suffers from severe OOD generalization issues, and the linear correlation between ID and OOD adversarial robustness is strong, enabling the prediction of OOD robustness from ID robustness.
- The benchmark identifies several factors that enhance OOD robustness, including additional data, advanced model architectures, and data augmentation, which are crucial for improving the robustness of models in real-world applications.
- The paper is well-written, easy to follow, and the experimental results are comprehensive, making it a valuable contribution to the field of adversarial robustness.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper primarily focuses on the correlation between ID and OOD adversarial robustness without delving deeper into the underlying causes, which could limit the understanding of the factors influencing OOD robustness.
- The study's scope is somewhat limited as it does not consider different types of models, such as transformers, which might behave differently under OOD shifts.
- The paper's conclusions might be misleading due to the correlation between ID and OOD robustness, which could oversimplify the complexities of OOD robustness.
- The paper does not sufficiently explore the potential impact of various data augmentation methods on OOD robustness, which could provide valuable insights into the effectiveness of different augmentation strategies.
- The paper's title and some of its claims, such as the claim that "simply following the existing methodology may be insufficient to achieve OOD robustness," are overstated and not adequately supported by the evidence presented.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors elaborate on the underlying causes of the observed OOD robustness and how these might influence the correlation between ID and OOD robustness?
- How do different data augmentation methods affect OOD robustness, and could the authors provide more detailed insights into this aspect?
- In light of the correlation between ID and OOD robustness, how can the findings of this study be used to improve OOD robustness in practical applications?
- How do the authors address the potential misleading conclusions that could arise from the correlation between ID and OOD robustness?
- Could the authors clarify the discrepancies and potential misleading claims in the paper, particularly regarding the generalizability of OOD robustness and the effectiveness of different robust training techniques?
