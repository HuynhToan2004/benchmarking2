 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel method called Meta-Learning with Personalized Learning Rates (MLPLR), which dynamically adjusts learning rates based on task performance. The method utilizes the Kullback-Leibler divergence to evaluate task performance and adjusts the learning rate accordingly, with larger rates for well-performing tasks and smaller rates for poorly performing ones. This approach is aimed at improving the performance of meta-learning models by tailoring the learning process to the specific task. Extensive experiments are conducted across various datasets, including CIFAR-FS, miniImagenet, Aircraft, CUB, and Dogs, demonstrating the effectiveness of the proposed method. However, the paper lacks comprehensive theoretical analysis and comparisons with state-of-the-art methods, which limits the depth of its contribution.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, making it easy to follow and understand the proposed method.
- The idea of using KL divergence to adjust the learning rate is novel and intuitive, enhancing the performance of meta-learning models.
- The method does not require additional computational resources, making it practical for real-world applications.
- The paper provides extensive experiments that demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed method across various datasets and settings.
- The simplicity of the method makes it easy to integrate with existing meta-learning algorithms, enhancing its applicability.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a comprehensive theoretical analysis of the proposed method, which could provide a deeper understanding of its effectiveness and limitations.
- The comparison with state-of-the-art methods is insufficient, limiting the evaluation of the proposed method against the latest advancements in the field.
- The experimental results do not show significant improvements over baseline methods, raising questions about the practical benefits of the proposed method.
- The paper could benefit from a more detailed discussion on the challenges and limitations of the proposed method, including its scalability and applicability to different types of meta-learning tasks.
- There are minor typographical and grammatical errors that need to be corrected for clarity and professionalism.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors provide a more detailed theoretical analysis of the proposed method, explaining why it is effective and how it compares to other methods?
- How does the proposed method perform in scenarios where the data distribution across different tasks varies significantly?
- Can the authors provide a comparison of the training time for the proposed method against other methods to assess its computational efficiency?
- Is there a specific reason why the method was not tested on larger datasets like ImageNet, and how does this affect the generalizability of the findings?
- Could the authors discuss the potential challenges and limitations of the proposed method, including its scalability and applicability to different types of meta-learning tasks?
