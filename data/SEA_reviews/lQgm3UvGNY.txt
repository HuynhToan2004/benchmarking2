 **Summary:**
The paper introduces InteR, a novel framework that integrates large language models (LLMs) and retrieval models (RMs) to enhance information retrieval (IR) by refining queries and documents iteratively. This integration allows for the expansion of queries using LLM-generated knowledge collections and the enhancement of prompt formulation using retrieved documents. The framework is evaluated on several benchmarks, showing promising results in zero-shot retrieval performance compared to state-of-the-art methods. However, the paper's novelty is questioned as it primarily combines existing techniques without significant innovation, and the evaluation could be expanded to include more diverse and recent benchmarks.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, clear, and easy to follow, with a clear motivation and a well-defined problem addressed.
- The proposed framework is simple yet effective, and the methodology is straightforward, making it easy to understand and implement.
- The experimental results demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed method, showing improvements over state-of-the-art (SOTA) methods in zero-shot retrieval performance.
- The integration of LLMs and RMs in a synergistic manner is a novel approach that addresses the limitations of both types of models, enhancing the overall retrieval performance.
- The paper includes a thorough evaluation, demonstrating the effectiveness of the proposed method across various benchmarks and tasks.

**Weaknesses:**
- The novelty of the paper is limited as it primarily combines existing techniques without significant innovation.
- The evaluation could be expanded to include more diverse and recent benchmarks to better demonstrate the generalizability of the proposed method.
- The paper does not provide a detailed comparison with existing methods that use LLMs for retrieval, which could have highlighted the advantages of the proposed method more clearly.
- The complexity of the framework and the cost of each iteration are not adequately discussed, which could impact the practical deployment of the method.
- The paper lacks a detailed discussion on the limitations of the proposed method, which is crucial for understanding the scope and applicability of the findings.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors provide a more detailed comparison with existing methods that use LLMs for retrieval to better highlight the advantages of the proposed method?
- How does the proposed method compare in terms of complexity and cost with other state-of-the-art methods, especially in terms of the number of iterations required for optimal performance?
- Can the authors discuss the potential limitations of the proposed method and how these might affect the practical deployment of the framework?
- How does the method perform when using different LLMs, and what are the implications of using different types of LLMs?
- Could the authors provide more detailed results on the performance of the method when using different types of queries, such as factual or opinionated queries?
- What are the specific contributions of the LLM and RM in the final retrieval results, and how do these contributions vary depending on the type of query or document set used?
