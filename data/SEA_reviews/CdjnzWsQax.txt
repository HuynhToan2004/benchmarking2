 **Summary:**
The paper introduces FTS-Diffusion, a novel generative framework designed to model irregular and scale-invariant patterns in financial time series. It comprises three main components: a pattern recognition module, a pattern generation module, and a pattern evolution module. The first module identifies irregular and scale-invariant patterns using a new scale-invariant subsequence clustering (SISC) algorithm. The second module generates segments of patterns using a diffusion-based generative network, while the third models the temporal transition of patterns to aggregate the generated segments. Extensive experiments demonstrate that FTS-Diffusion outperforms existing methods in generating synthetic financial time series that closely resemble real data, improving the accuracy of stock market predictions. However, the paper has been criticized for its lack of clarity in defining the terms "irregular" and "scale-invariant," and for not sufficiently distinguishing its contributions from existing literature.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written and easy to follow, with clear explanations of the proposed methodologies and experiments.
- The introduction of the Scale-Invariant Subsequence Clustering (SISC) algorithm is a significant contribution to the field, particularly in identifying irregular and scale-invariant patterns in financial time series.
- The proposed FTS-Diffusion framework is innovative and effective, demonstrated through extensive experiments that show the model's ability to generate realistic financial time series and improve stock market prediction accuracy.
- The paper addresses a critical issue in financial applications by synthesizing financial time series to augment real-world data, which is crucial for improving the performance of deep learning models in finance.
- The paper provides a detailed discussion on the technical contributions and the limitations of the proposed method, enhancing its credibility and reproducibility.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a clear definition and distinction between the terms "irregular" and "scale-invariant," which are central to the research. These terms are used interchangeably without a formal definition or clear differentiation.
- The paper does not sufficiently discuss the relationship of its work to existing literature, particularly in the context of generative models for financial time series.
- The experimental section could be improved by including more detailed explanations of the experimental setup and results, and by providing more comprehensive comparisons with state-of-the-art methods.
- The paper could benefit from a more rigorous theoretical analysis to support the claims made about the properties of financial time series and the effectiveness of the proposed methods.
- There are issues with the clarity and organization of the paper, particularly in the explanation of the methodology and the experimental results.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors clarify the definitions and distinctions between "irregular" and "scale-invariant" patterns in financial time series?
- How does the proposed method compare to existing generative models for financial time series, particularly in terms of performance and theoretical underpinnings?
- In the experimental section, can the authors provide more detailed explanations of the experimental setup and results, including the statistical significance of the improvements observed?
- How does the proposed method handle the potential for distribution shifts in financial data, and what measures are in place to address these shifts?
- Can the authors provide more detailed comparisons with other state-of-the-art methods, particularly in terms of computational efficiency and scalability?
