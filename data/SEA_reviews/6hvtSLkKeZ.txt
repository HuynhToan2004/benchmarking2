 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel learning-based approach to solve the Class-Constrained Bin Packing Problem (CCBPP), focusing on the manufacturing order consolidation problem (OCP). It employs a graph convolutional network (GCN) to generate a heat-map of probabilities, which is then used to train an encoder-decoder model. The encoder generates the heat-map, and the decoder decodes it into a sequence of items packed together, with a clustering algorithm used to determine the sequence of items. Active search is employed to fine-tune the solution, and the method is evaluated against several heuristic and learning-based methods. The approach is shown to outperform existing methods in terms of solution quality and efficiency, though the paper could benefit from a clearer presentation and more comprehensive evaluation, including comparisons with exact methods and a broader range of datasets.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel learning-based method to solve the Class-Constrained Bin Packing Problem (CCBPP), a problem that has not been extensively studied in existing operational research.
- The methodology is well-explained, with clear figures and descriptions that aid in understanding the proposed approach.
- The paper demonstrates that the proposed method outperforms existing heuristic and learning-based methods, showing promising results in both synthetic and real-world datasets.
- The use of a graph convolutional network (GCN) to generate a heat-map of probabilities and a clustering algorithm to determine the sequence of items is a novel and effective approach for solving the CCBPP.
- The paper provides a comprehensive evaluation, comparing the proposed method with several heuristic and learning-based methods, and includes an ablation study to evaluate the effectiveness of different components of the proposed method.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a clear description of the Cluster Decode algorithm and the training details of the GCN model, making it difficult for readers to fully understand the methodology.
- The paper could benefit from a clearer presentation, particularly in the figures and tables, to improve readability and comprehension.
- The evaluation could be improved by including comparisons with exact methods and a broader range of datasets, including real-world datasets where the ground truth is known.
- The paper does not sufficiently address the limitations of the proposed method, which could affect the generalizability and applicability of the findings.
- There are some minor issues with the presentation, such as missing references and typos, which could be addressed to improve the professional quality of the paper.
