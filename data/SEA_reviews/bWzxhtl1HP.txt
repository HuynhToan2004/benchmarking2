 **Summary:**
The paper explores the use of denoising diffusion probabilistic models (DMs) for unsupervised representation learning, focusing on the connection between time-steps and modular attributes. The authors propose a method that leverages the diffusion process to disentangle attributes, with theoretical insights into the behavior of attributes during the diffusion process. The paper presents an innovative approach to feature learning, showing that attributes are lost in a specific order during the diffusion process, which can be utilized to disentangle attributes. The method is evaluated on various datasets, demonstrating improvements over existing methods in tasks such as attribute prediction and counterfactual generation.

**Strengths:**
- The paper presents a novel approach to disentangled representation learning using a diffusion model, which is both theoretically grounded and empirically validated.
- The theoretical analysis is robust, providing a clear understanding of the behavior of attributes during the diffusion process.
- The methodology is simple yet effective, demonstrated through extensive experiments on multiple datasets.
- The paper is well-written, with clear and concise explanations of the proposed method and its theoretical underpinnings.
- The results are compelling, showing improvements over existing methods in both inference and generation tasks.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a comprehensive comparison with other unsupervised disentangled representation learning methods, particularly those that use contrastive objectives.
- The method's reliance on the diffusion model's attribute loss mechanism may limit its applicability to other models.
- The theoretical analysis and the proof of Theorem 4.1 are not convincing, with some assumptions and conclusions not adequately justified.
- The experimental evaluation is limited to specific datasets and does not include a broader range of datasets or tasks, which could affect the generalizability of the findings.
- There are some issues with the clarity and consistency of the writing, particularly in the explanation of certain methodological details and the use of notation.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors clarify the proof of Theorem 4.1, particularly the assumptions and the implications of the results?
- How does the proposed method compare to other unsupervised disentangled representation learning methods, particularly those that use contrastive objectives?
- Is there a possibility to apply this method to other models beyond the diffusion model, and if so, what are the potential challenges or modifications required?
- Can the authors provide more details on the partition and optimization strategy mentioned in the paper, and how these strategies impact the performance of the model?
- Could the authors elaborate on the experimental setup, particularly the choice of datasets and the evaluation metrics used?
- How does the method perform on other types of data, such as text or audio, and what are the potential challenges or opportunities in these domains?
