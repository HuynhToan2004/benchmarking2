 **Summary:**
The paper introduces FixPO, a novel on-policy reinforcement learning algorithm that combines elements of trust region methods and proximal policy updates. FixPO utilizes a two-phase optimization strategy: a primary phase that adjusts the policy to approximate the trust region constraints, followed by a fixup phase that ensures these constraints are met. This approach allows for the efficient handling of KL divergence, which is crucial for policy updates within the trust region. The algorithm's performance is evaluated across various benchmarks, demonstrating its competitive results against existing methods like TRPO and PPO. However, the paper has been critiqued for its limited novelty, as it heavily relies on prior work, and its empirical evaluation, which is seen as insufficiently robust to establish its advantages convincingly.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel approach to trust region optimization in reinforcement learning (RL) by combining elements of trust region methods and proximal policy updates, which is a significant contribution to the field.
- The methodology is well-explained, making it accessible to readers unfamiliar with the specifics of RL algorithms.
- The experimental setup is robust, with thorough ablation studies and comparisons to other methods, which help to validate the proposed approach.
- The paper is well-written, making it easy to follow, and the authors have provided code for reproducibility, enhancing the transparency and reliability of the research.
- The proposed method shows promise in enforcing trust-region constraints, which is a critical aspect of RL training stability.

**Weaknesses:**
- The novelty of the approach is limited as it heavily relies on existing work, particularly the methodologies introduced in TRPO and PPO.
- The empirical evaluation is insufficient to convincingly demonstrate the advantages of the proposed method over existing approaches.
- The paper does not adequately address the computational efficiency of the proposed method, which is crucial for practical applications.
- There is a lack of clarity in the experimental setup and results, particularly in how the proposed method compares to baseline methods like TRPO and PPO.
- The paper does not sufficiently discuss the limitations of the proposed method, which could affect its applicability in real-world scenarios.
- The paper's writing style could be improved to make it more accessible to a broader audience, including those not familiar with the specifics of RL algorithms.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors provide a more detailed comparison of the computational efficiency of their proposed method against existing approaches?
- How does the proposed method perform in more complex environments or with more complex policies, and what are the implications for training stability and convergence?
- Can the authors clarify the role and impact of the hyperparameters introduced in the proposed method, particularly how they affect the performance and convergence of the algorithm?
- How does the proposed method compare to other methods that also use KL divergence for policy updates, such as those mentioned in the related work section?
- Could the authors provide a more detailed discussion on the limitations of their proposed method and how these might affect its practical applicability?
- How does the proposed method handle the potential issues of outliers in the KL divergence calculation, and what are the implications of this for the overall performance and stability of the training process?
