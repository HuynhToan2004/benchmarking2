 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel approach to constructing new factors from relational graph data in the context of quantitative trading, specifically focusing on capital flow similarity graphs. It proposes six basic graphs from fund-related data and three composite multi-graphs to exploit capital flow similarities. The methodology involves using these graphs to enhance the performance of existing multi-factor models, demonstrating marginal improvements in metrics such as Information Coefficient (IC), TOP group excess returns, long-short returns, and index-enhanced portfolio returns. The paper also explores the integration of these graphs with representation of fund-related graphs, showing that this integration can lead to improved returns in the A-share market.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel approach to constructing new factors from relational graph data, specifically focusing on capital flow similarity graphs, which is a new and interesting research direction in the field of quantitative trading.
- The methodology proposed in the paper is simple yet effective, and the paper provides a clear and detailed description of the methodology used.
- The paper demonstrates the effectiveness of the proposed methodology through experiments, showing marginal improvements in various metrics such as Information Coefficient (IC), TOP group excess returns, long-short returns, and index-enhanced portfolio returns.
- The integration of relational graph data into existing multi-factor models is a significant contribution, potentially leading to more effective and accurate models in the field of quantitative trading.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a thorough discussion on the theoretical underpinnings of the proposed methodology, which could enhance the paper's contribution to the field.
- The paper does not provide a detailed discussion on the limitations of the proposed methodology, which could help in understanding the potential drawbacks and challenges of the approach.
- The paper could benefit from a more rigorous evaluation of the proposed methodology, including a broader range of experiments and comparisons with other existing methods.
- The presentation of the paper could be improved, particularly in terms of clarity and structure, especially in sections where the methodology and results are discussed.
- The paper does not sufficiently discuss the scalability of the proposed methodology, which is a critical aspect for practical applications in the field of quantitative trading.
