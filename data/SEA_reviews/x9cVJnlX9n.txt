 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel Bayesian Optimization (BO) method for model selection that integrates risk-controlling procedures to ensure statistical validity. This method involves a two-stage process: first, BO is used to identify a set of Pareto optimal configurations within a specified region of interest, followed by a statistical test to verify their risk levels. The approach is demonstrated across various tasks, including fairness, robustness, and model efficiency, showing that it outperforms existing baselines in terms of efficiency and effectiveness. The paper also provides theoretical guarantees and empirical evidence to support its claims, though it lacks a detailed discussion on computational complexity and scalability.

**Strengths:**
- The paper presents a novel approach to Bayesian Optimization (BO) by integrating statistical testing with multi-objective optimization, which is a significant advancement in the field.
- The methodology is clearly explained, making it accessible to readers, and the paper provides a comprehensive review of related literature.
- The paper is well-structured and easy to follow, with clear and concise writing that aids in understanding the complex concepts presented.
- The empirical results demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed method, showing improvements over existing baselines in various tasks.
- The theoretical guarantees provided by the authors are solid, enhancing the credibility of the proposed approach.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed discussion on the computational complexity of the proposed method, which is crucial for understanding its scalability and applicability in real-world scenarios.
- There is a noticeable absence of a baseline that uses a single-sided upper bound, which could have provided a more robust comparison to the proposed method.
- The paper does not adequately discuss the limitations of the proposed method, which is essential for understanding its applicability and potential drawbacks.
- The paper does not include a detailed comparison with other related works, such as those that use online conformal prediction for BO, which could provide a more comprehensive evaluation of the proposed method.
- The paper could benefit from a more detailed comparison with other multi-objective Bayesian optimization methods to better establish the novelty and effectiveness of the proposed approach.

**Questions:**
- How does the computational complexity of the proposed method scale with an increase in the number of objectives or constraints?
- Can the authors provide a detailed comparison with other multi-objective Bayesian optimization methods to better establish the novelty and effectiveness of their approach?
- Why was the decision made to not include a baseline that uses a single-sided upper bound, and what were the considerations behind this choice?
- How does the proposed method compare in terms of computational efficiency and effectiveness with other methods that use statistical testing for model selection?
- Could the authors provide a more detailed discussion on the limitations of their proposed method and how these might impact its practical applicability?
