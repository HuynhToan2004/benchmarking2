 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel approach for learning spatiotemporal structure in multivariate time series data using a Group-Aware Transformer (GAFormer). This model incorporates both spatial and temporal group embeddings to enhance the performance of transformers in time series analysis. The authors propose a set of group tokens and an instance-specific group embedding layer to adaptively assign input tokens to these group tokens, thus incorporating structure into learning. The GAFormer is evaluated on various time-series classification and regression tasks, demonstrating state-of-the-art performance. The paper also discusses the interpretability of the learned group structure and provides experimental results that validate the model's effectiveness.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel approach for learning spatiotemporal structure in multivariate time series data, which is a significant contribution to the field.
- The proposed Group-Aware Transformer (GAFormer) incorporates both spatial and temporal group embeddings, which are novel and innovative.
- The paper provides a detailed description of the proposed method and demonstrates its effectiveness through comprehensive experimental results.
- The paper is well-written, clear, and easy to follow, making it accessible to a broad audience.
- The experiments are well-designed and demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed method, showing that it can outperform existing baselines in time series classification and regression tasks.
- The interpretability of the learned group structure is highlighted, providing valuable insights into the model's functionality.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper does not adequately discuss the differences between the proposed method and existing works, such as those by Zhang and Yan (2022) and Zhou et al. (2022), which also use similar approaches.
- The paper lacks a thorough analysis of the time and memory complexity of the proposed method, which is crucial for understanding its practical applicability.
- The interpretability of the learned group structure is not sufficiently explored, and the paper does not provide a detailed explanation of how these groups are learned or how they contribute to the model's performance.
- The paper does not include a comparison with the latest state-of-the-art models, which could have strengthened the validation of the proposed method.
- The paper does not discuss the limitations of the proposed method, which is essential for understanding its applicability and potential improvements.
- The paper could benefit from a more detailed discussion on the grouping structures learned by the model and their implications for the model's performance.
