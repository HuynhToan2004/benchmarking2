 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel approach to multimodal fusion using quaternion networks, termed Prompt Learning with Quaternion Networks (QNet). This method aims to improve the performance of multimodal pre-trained models by fusing features from different modalities into a quaternion space, leveraging the orthogonal properties of quaternions to enhance feature disentanglement. The proposed method, QNet, is evaluated across various datasets and tasks, demonstrating competitive performance against existing methods. Despite its innovative use of quaternions, the paper faces criticism for its lack of clarity in explaining the benefits of using quaternions over simpler methods, and the overall motivation behind the quaternion-based approach remains unclear.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel approach to multimodal fusion using quaternion networks, which is a new and innovative method in the field.
- The proposed method, QNet, is well-motivated and the paper provides a clear explanation of its benefits, such as the ability to disentangle complex feature distributions of different modalities in latent feature space.
- The paper is well-written and easy to follow, with a clear presentation of the methodology and results.
- The experimental results show that QNet outperforms other multimodal models in terms of performance, demonstrating its effectiveness in handling multimodal data.
- The paper is significant in its potential to enhance the performance of multimodal pre-trained models, which is a critical area of research in the field of multimodal learning.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed comparison with simpler methods, such as using different weighting factors for different modalities, which could undermine the necessity of using quaternions for this specific task.
- The paper does not sufficiently explain why quaternions are beneficial for multimodal fusion, and the overall motivation behind using quaternions remains unclear.
- There is a lack of clarity in the presentation of the methodology, particularly in the description of the quaternion-based features and their impact on performance.
- The paper does not include experiments on larger-scale datasets like ImageNet, which could have provided more robust results.
- The paper does not discuss the limitations of the proposed method, which is crucial for understanding the scope and applicability of the findings.
- The paper could benefit from a more thorough discussion on the impact of the k-axis on performance, as it is currently unclear how it contributes to the overall effectiveness of the method.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors provide a more detailed comparison with simpler methods, such as using different weighting factors for different modalities, to clarify the necessity of using quaternions for this specific task?
- Could the authors explain why quaternions are beneficial for multimodal fusion and provide a clearer motivation for their use in this context?
- How does the proposed method, QNet, compare to other existing methods in terms of computational efficiency and scalability, especially when applied to larger datasets?
- Can the authors provide more details on the initialization of quaternion parameters and how they affect the performance of the method?
- In light of the concerns regarding the impact of the k-axis on performance, could the authors clarify its role and significance in the quaternion-based features?
- How does the proposed method handle the potential issues of overfitting, especially in scenarios where the depth of the model increases?
