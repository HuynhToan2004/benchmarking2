 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel framework, Score-Based Adversarial Generation (ScoreAG), which leverages score-based generative models to generate adversarial examples that are semantically-preserving and not limited by traditional Lp norms. The framework is designed to synthesize new adversarial attacks, transform existing images into adversarial examples, and purify images, enhancing the empirical robust accuracy of classifiers. Extensive empirical evaluations demonstrate that ScoreAG matches the performance of existing state-of-the-art attacks and defenses. The paper also explores the limitations and potential future work, including the need for human studies to evaluate the effectiveness of the generated adversarial examples and the applicability of the purifying approach in a preprocessor-blackbox setting.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel approach to generate adversarial examples that are not limited by traditional Lp norms, which is a significant advancement in the field of adversarial machine learning.
- The methodology is well-explained, and the paper is well-written, making it accessible and understandable to readers.
- The proposed framework, Score-Based Adversarial Generation (ScoreAG), is innovative and can be used to synthesize new adversarial attacks, transform existing images into adversarial examples, and purify images, thereby enhancing the empirical robust accuracy of classifiers.
- The paper includes comprehensive experiments and evaluations, demonstrating the effectiveness and applicability of the proposed framework.
- The potential of the proposed framework is significant, as it can be used in various applications such as autonomous driving, medical diagnostics, and financial sector, where robustness against adversarial attacks is crucial.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed comparison with other existing methods, particularly in terms of the effectiveness and efficiency of the proposed framework.
- The experimental results, as shown in Tables 1 and 2, suggest that the proposed framework may not be as effective as other methods in generating adversarial examples.
- The paper does not provide a detailed analysis of the computational complexity of the proposed framework, which could be a significant concern for practical applications.
- The paper could benefit from a more thorough discussion on the limitations and potential future work, particularly in terms of the applicability of the purifying approach in a preprocessor-blackbox setting and the need for human studies to evaluate the effectiveness of the generated adversarial examples.
- The paper does not provide sufficient evidence to support the claim that the generated adversarial examples are semantically-preserving, which is a crucial aspect of the proposed framework.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors provide a more detailed comparison with other existing methods to better understand the effectiveness and efficiency of the proposed framework?
- How does the proposed framework compare in terms of computational complexity with other methods, and what are the implications for practical applications?
- Can the authors provide more evidence to support the claim that the generated adversarial examples are semantically-preserving?
- How does the proposed framework address the limitations discussed in the paper, particularly the applicability of the purifying approach in a preprocessor-blackbox setting?
- Could the authors clarify the role and impact of the hyperparameters used in the framework, especially in relation to the effectiveness of the adversarial attacks and the purification process?
