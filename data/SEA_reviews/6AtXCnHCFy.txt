 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel method for domain generalization in fault detection, specifically focusing on the CWRU bearing dataset and the MNIST dataset. The authors propose a feature shift network (FSN) to align features across domains, which is tested against various domain generalization methods. The methodology involves a feature extractor, a feature shift network, a relation classifier, and a label classifier, all integrated to improve fault detection in different operating conditions. The paper claims that FSN outperforms other methods in domain generalization tasks, though the evaluation is limited to these two datasets, raising concerns about the generalizability of the findings.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-structured and easy to follow, with clear explanations of the proposed method and its applications.
- The introduction of a new domain generalization scenario called Load-Domain domain generalization, along with the feature shift model FSN, is innovative and well-motivated.
- The paper provides a thorough review of related work, effectively positioning the proposed method within the existing literature.
- The proposed method is well-motivated and addresses a significant problem in fault diagnosis, demonstrating a novel approach to domain generalization.
- The paper includes a clear theoretical analysis and provides a detailed explanation of the proposed method, enhancing understanding and reproducibility.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper's evaluation is limited to the CWRU bearing dataset and the MNIST dataset, which may not fully demonstrate the generalizability of the proposed method.
- The paper lacks a comprehensive comparison with other domain generalization methods, particularly those that have been extensively evaluated in similar scenarios.
- The paper's presentation could be improved for clarity and readability, particularly in sections where the content is dense or complex.
- There is a lack of detailed discussion on the limitations of the proposed method and how these might affect its practical application.
- The paper does not provide sufficient empirical evidence to support the claims made about the effectiveness of the proposed method.
- The paper does not adequately address the societal impact of the work, which could be significant in applications such as fault detection in critical infrastructure.

**Questions:**
- How does the proposed method perform on other datasets, particularly those that do not have the same label-to-physical-meaning relationship as the CWRU bearing dataset?
- Can the authors provide more details on the training process for the FSN, including the specifics of how the feature extractor and feature shift network are implemented?
- How does the proposed method compare to other state-of-the-art domain generalization methods, particularly those that have been extensively evaluated in similar scenarios?
- Could the authors provide more empirical evidence to support the claims made about the effectiveness of the proposed method?
- How does the proposed method address the societal impact of fault detection in critical infrastructure, and what measures have been taken to mitigate potential negative consequences?
