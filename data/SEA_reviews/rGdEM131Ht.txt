 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel energy-based model (TF-EBM) for time series forecasting, leveraging a residual dilated convolutional network and a time-frequency feature extracting network. This model aims to address the limitations of existing models by integrating both local and global patterns, improving efficiency and accuracy in long-term forecasting. The model is trained using maximum likelihood estimation with gradient-based Markov Chain Monte Carlo (MCMC) and employs a time-frequency block to construct an unnormalized probability density function over temporal paths. The model's performance is evaluated on several datasets, demonstrating its effectiveness in handling varying prediction lengths and missing data points. However, the paper is criticized for its limited novelty and lack of comparison with state-of-the-art models.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel energy-based model (TF-EBM) for time series forecasting, which employs a time-frequency block to construct an unnormalized probability density function over temporal paths, enhancing the model's efficiency and accuracy in long-term forecasting.
- The TF-EBM model is designed to handle both varying prediction lengths and missing data points with a single pre-trained model, which is a significant advantage in practical applications.
- The paper is well-organized and easy to follow, making it accessible to a broad audience.
- The model's design enables the prediction of long-term time series in a single forward run using Langevin MCMC, which is more efficient than traditional methods.
- The paper includes comprehensive experiments that demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed model in both forecasting and imputation tasks, showcasing its potential as a unified time series forecasting model.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed comparison with state-of-the-art models, which is crucial for establishing the model's effectiveness and advancement over existing methods.
- The novelty of the model is limited, as it primarily combines existing techniques such as dilated convolution and frequency domain analysis without significant innovation.
- The paper does not provide sufficient details on the training process, including the learning rate, number of training epochs, and other hyperparameters, which are essential for reproducibility and understanding the model's performance.
- There is a lack of discussion on the limitations of the proposed model, which could provide insights into its applicability and potential improvements.
- The paper does not adequately address the scalability of the model with increasing sequence lengths, which is crucial for practical applications.
- The paper does not provide sufficient details on the computational complexity and running time of the model, which are essential for understanding its efficiency.

**Questions:**

- How does the proposed model handle the scalability issues with increasing sequence lengths, and what are the potential solutions to address these challenges?
- Can the authors provide a more detailed discussion on the limitations of the proposed model and potential directions for future research?

- How does the proposed model perform in terms of computational complexity and running time, and how does it compare with other models in terms of efficiency?
