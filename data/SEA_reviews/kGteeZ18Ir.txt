 **Summary:**
The paper investigates the impact of persona-assigned LLMs on reasoning tasks, revealing significant disparities in performance across different demographic groups. The study explores the implicit biases in LLMs by assigning them personas and evaluating their reasoning capabilities across 24 datasets. The findings suggest that these biases, while sometimes subtle, can lead to substantial performance drops, particularly in tasks involving math and other quantitative reasoning. The authors propose that these biases are not fully mitigated by prompt-based approaches and suggest that more fundamental changes in the training and deployment of LLMs may be necessary.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, clear, and easy to follow, with a clear structure and logical flow.
- The study is thorough, covering a wide range of datasets and personas, providing a comprehensive analysis of the impact of persona-assigned LLMs on reasoning tasks.
- The findings are significant and provide valuable insights into the performance disparities of LLMs when assigned different personas, highlighting the need for more robust methods to mitigate these biases.
- The paper includes a detailed appendix with additional results and analysis, which enhances the depth of the study.
- The use of ChatGPT as a case study demonstrates the prevalence of the issue across different models, emphasizing the need for broader solutions.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed discussion on the potential reasons for the observed biases, which could include factors such as training data biases, pre-training objectives, and the impact of RLHF.
- The analysis does not consider the impact of prompt length on the results, which could affect the conclusions drawn about the effectiveness of different prompting strategies.
- The study does not sufficiently explore the potential mitigation strategies beyond prompt engineering, such as retraining the LLM or using other techniques like debiasing algorithms.
- There is a lack of discussion on the generalizability of the findings to other LLMs or different types of reasoning tasks, which could limit the applicability of the results.
- The paper could benefit from a more rigorous evaluation of the mitigation strategies proposed, including more comprehensive testing and a comparison with other debiasing methods.

**Questions:**
- How does the model's training data affect the observed biases, and could the bias be mitigated by adjusting the training data or the training objectives?
- Can you provide more details on the selection process for the 24 datasets used in the study, and how these datasets were chosen to ensure a balanced representation across different domains?
- How does the performance of the LLM vary with the length of the prompt, and could this influence the conclusions drawn about the effectiveness of different prompting strategies?
- Have you considered using other debiasing techniques, such as retraining the LLM or using debiasing algorithms, to mitigate the observed biases? If so, what were the results, and how do they compare to the prompt-based strategies used in the study?
- Could you provide more details on the methodology used to select the personas and the datasets, and how these selections were made to ensure a fair and balanced evaluation?
