 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel approach to multivariate time series forecasting using a variant of the Transformer architecture, specifically by inverting the tokenization of time series. Instead of encoding each time step as a token, the model encodes each time series as a variate token, allowing for more effective modeling of multivariate correlations. This approach, termed iTransformer, has been tested on various benchmarks and shows improved performance over existing methods. The paper also discusses the limitations of traditional Transformer architectures in handling multivariate time series and proposes a more efficient training strategy to address computational challenges.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, clear, and easy to follow, with a clear motivation and a novel approach to multivariate time series forecasting.
- The proposed method is simple yet effective, with a focus on improving the attention mechanism for multivariate correlations.
- The results demonstrate significant improvements over previous methods, and the paper provides a thorough analysis of the proposed method's effectiveness.
- The paper is well-organized, with a clear structure that facilitates understanding and reproducibility of the work.
- The proposed method is efficient in terms of training time and memory usage, making it a practical solution for real-world applications.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed discussion on the limitations of the proposed method, particularly in scenarios where it might not perform as well as other methods.
- The novelty of the approach is questioned, as it appears to be a simple modification of existing methods without significant innovation.
- The paper does not provide sufficient comparisons with state-of-the-art methods, which could undermine the claimed improvements.
- There are issues with the clarity and explanation of certain figures and tables, which could confuse readers or hinder understanding.
- The paper could benefit from a more detailed discussion on the computational complexity and efficiency of the proposed method, especially in comparison to other methods.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors provide a more detailed comparison with state-of-the-art methods to better establish the effectiveness of the proposed method?
- How does the proposed method handle scenarios where the number of variates is very large, and what are the computational implications?
- What are the specific benefits of using the proposed method in terms of interpretability or other qualitative measures beyond quantitative performance metrics?
- Could the authors clarify the training strategy and its impact on the performance of the model?
- How does the proposed method handle the permutation-invariant property of the Transformer, and what are the implications for the model's performance?
