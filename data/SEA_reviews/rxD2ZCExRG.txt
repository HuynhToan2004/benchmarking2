 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel framework, HumanTOMATO, for generating whole-body motions from textual descriptions. It utilizes a hierarchical VQ-VAE to model body and hand motions separately, alongside a text-motion-alignment model (TMR) for enhanced text-motion alignment. The framework incorporates a hierarchical GPT for generating discrete codes of body and hand motions in an auto-regressive manner. It also includes a facial conditional VAE for facial expression generation. The paper claims that this approach improves text-motion alignment and generates more diverse and realistic motions compared to existing methods. However, concerns are raised about the novelty of the methodology, the clarity of presentation, and the lack of comprehensive comparisons with state-of-the-art methods.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, making it easy to follow and understand the proposed methodologies.
- The introduction of a text-motion-aligned model for enhanced text-motion alignment is a novel and innovative approach.
- The paper addresses the problem of whole-body motion generation, a significant and challenging task in the field of computer vision.
- The proposed methodology is well-explained, and the paper provides a comprehensive evaluation of the generated motions using both qualitative and quantitative metrics.
- The paper introduces new evaluation metrics for text-motion alignment, which are more accurate and challenging compared to existing metrics.
- The use of hierarchical VQ-VAE and hierarchical GPT for whole-body motion generation is a creative and interesting approach.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a thorough discussion on the limitations and potential negative societal impacts of the proposed method.
- The novelty of the methodology is questionable as it primarily combines existing techniques without significant innovation.
- The paper could benefit from more comprehensive comparisons with state-of-the-art methods, including more recent and relevant works.
- The evaluation section could be improved by including more qualitative results and a broader range of metrics to better assess the performance of the model.
- The paper could provide more detailed explanations and justifications for certain design choices, such as the use of hierarchical VQ-VAE and the motivation behind the proposed metrics.
- The paper suffers from some presentation issues, including inconsistencies in the formatting and clarity of figures and tables.

**Questions:**
- Could you clarify the role and impact of the TMR model in the overall framework, particularly in terms of its contribution to text-motion alignment?
- How does the proposed method handle the alignment between text and motion, especially in scenarios where the text description might not be detailed enough to guide the motion generation?
- In the ablation study, how does the performance of the model vary when using different codebook sizes or different quantization methods?
- Could you provide more qualitative results, such as videos or additional figures, to better demonstrate the capabilities of the proposed method?
- How does the proposed method compare to other recent works in terms of performance and efficiency?
- Can you explain the rationale behind the choice of the hierarchical VQ-VAE and the specific design of the hierarchical GPT?
- How does the paper address the potential limitations and ethical concerns related to the generation of whole-body motions from textual descriptions?
