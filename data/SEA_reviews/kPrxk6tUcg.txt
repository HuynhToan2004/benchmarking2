 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel autoencoder-based matrix completion method, termed AEMC-NE, which incorporates an additional element-wise autoencoder to enhance the reconstruction capabilities of the output layer. This methodology is designed to adaptively learn an activation function, thereby improving the accuracy in predicting missing values in rating matrices. The paper provides theoretical analysis and empirical evidence, including synthetic and real-world datasets, to support the effectiveness of the proposed method. Despite the theoretical insights and some empirical improvements, concerns were raised about the novelty and the incremental nature of the contributions, as well as the clarity and depth of the experimental evaluations.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, with clear and well-structured content that makes it easy to follow.
- The introduction of an adaptive activation function using an element-wise autoencoder is a novel approach, enhancing the predictive capabilities of the model.
- Theoretical analysis is provided, which helps in understanding the theoretical underpinnings of the proposed method.
- The paper includes a comprehensive set of experiments, both synthetic and real-world, which demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed method.
- The methodology is simple and effective, with a clear explanation of the model and its components.

**Weaknesses:**
- The novelty of the proposed method is limited, as it primarily builds on existing works such as Sedhain et al. (2015), with only incremental improvements.
- The paper lacks a detailed comparison with other state-of-the-art methods, which could have provided a clearer picture of the method's advantages or disadvantages.
- The experimental results, while promising, are not convincingly superior to existing methods, especially in terms of statistical significance and practical relevance.
- The theoretical analysis is incomplete and lacks depth, particularly in terms of the assumptions made and the practical implications of these assumptions.
- There is a noticeable absence of discussion on the limitations of the proposed method, which is crucial for understanding its applicability and potential drawbacks.
- The paper could benefit from more detailed explanations and justifications for certain design choices and methodological aspects, such as the choice of activation functions and the impact of different data structures on the model's performance.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors clarify the novelty of the proposed method and how it differs significantly from existing works, particularly Sedhain et al. (2015)?
- How does the proposed method perform when compared to state-of-the-art methods, especially in terms of statistical significance and practical relevance?
- Can the authors provide a more detailed discussion on the limitations of the proposed method and how these might impact its practical applicability?
- How does the choice of activation functions impact the performance of the model, and could different activation functions be explored to potentially improve performance?
- Could the authors elaborate on the theoretical assumptions made in the analysis, particularly the assumptions regarding the nonlinearity of the response function and the impact of these assumptions on the model's performance?
- How does the model handle the cold-start problem, and what are the implications of this for the model's performance in real-world scenarios?
