 **Summary:**
The paper introduces AlignProp, a method for fine-tuning text-to-image diffusion models using end-to-end backpropagation of reward gradients through the denoising process. This approach is designed to improve model performance by optimizing for specific reward functions, such as aesthetic quality and text-image alignment. AlignProp employs techniques like low-rank adapter weight modules and gradient checkpointing to manage memory usage and computational efficiency. The method is evaluated against existing baselines and shows promising results in terms of reward maximization and computational efficiency. However, concerns are raised about the novelty and depth of the experiments, particularly in terms of the variety of reward functions tested and the generalizability of the results.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, easy to follow, and presents a clear motivation for the research.
- The method of fine-tuning diffusion models with reward-based backpropagation is innovative and addresses a significant problem in the field.
- The use of low-rank adapter weight modules and gradient checkpointing to manage memory usage is a practical and efficient solution.
- The paper is evaluated against existing baselines and shows promising results, demonstrating the effectiveness of the proposed method.
- The methodology is simple and straightforward, making it accessible and understandable.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks novelty as similar approaches have been explored in previous works, such as [1] and [2].
- The experiments are limited in scope, focusing primarily on the HPS reward function and not sufficiently exploring a variety of reward functions.
- The paper does not adequately address the generalization of the proposed method to different reward functions or datasets.
- The results presented are not convincing, particularly in the comparison with existing methods like DDPO, and the paper does not sufficiently demonstrate the advantages of AlignProp over these methods.
- The paper lacks a thorough discussion on the limitations of the proposed method, such as its applicability to different types of reward functions and its potential to overfit to specific reward functions.
- The paper does not sufficiently discuss the computational complexity and memory requirements of the proposed method, which are critical for practical deployment.

**Questions:**
- How does the proposed method compare to other existing methods like DDPO, particularly in terms of computational efficiency and reward maximization?
- Can the authors provide more detailed comparisons with other methods, such as those mentioned in the paper, to clarify the advantages of AlignProp?
- How does the method perform when fine-tuned with different reward functions, and what are the implications for the generalizability of the results?
- What are the specific computational and memory requirements of AlignProp, and how do these compare to other methods in terms of practical deployment?
- Can the authors provide more examples of the generated images to better illustrate the capabilities of the proposed method?
- How does the method handle the potential for overfitting to specific reward functions, and what measures are in place to prevent this?
