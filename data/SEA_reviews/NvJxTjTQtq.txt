 **Summary:**
The paper presents a benchmarking study for evaluating the performance of Equivariant Graph Neural Network (EGNN) force fields on various datasets, including two new datasets and three new metrics. It investigates the capabilities and limitations of six EGNN algorithms, focusing on their performance in atomistic simulations. The study highlights the challenges in accurately modeling out-of-distribution data, emphasizing the need for a foundation model to address these issues. The paper provides a comprehensive evaluation of existing models and proposes new benchmarks to advance the field. However, it also faces criticism for not proposing a new model, lacking in the exploration of the reasons behind the model's performance, and not adequately addressing the limitations of the current models.

**Strengths:**
- The paper provides a comprehensive benchmarking study on six existing EGNN force fields, which is beneficial for the community.
- The authors propose two new datasets and four new metrics, which are valuable for evaluating the performance of EGNNs in atomistic simulations.
- The manuscript is well-written, with clear explanations of the models, datasets, and evaluation metrics, making it accessible to a broad audience.
- The study includes a wide range of datasets, covering various atomic systems and scenarios, which enhances the comprehensiveness of the evaluation.
- The paper addresses an important problem in the field of EGNN force fields, specifically focusing on the evaluation of existing models, which is crucial for advancing the technology.
