 **Summary:**
The paper investigates the cost of overfitting in kernel ridge regression (KRR) by analyzing the gap between the test errors of the interpolating ridgeless model and the optimally-tuned model. It introduces an agnostic view that considers overfitting as a function of sample size, rather than focusing on the asymptotic behavior or consistency of a well-specified model. The authors use a Gaussian universality ansatz to derive non-rigorous risk estimates, which are used to characterize different types of overfitting such as benign, tempered, and catastrophic. The paper also provides a refined understanding of overfitting by considering the effective rank of the covariance matrix, which is a key parameter in understanding the behavior of overfitting in different scenarios.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, with clear and concise explanations that are easy to follow, making it accessible to a broad audience.
- It introduces a novel perspective on the cost of overfitting in KRR, focusing on the gap between the test errors of the interpolating ridgeless model and the optimally-tuned model, which is a significant contribution to the field.
- The paper provides a comprehensive characterization of different types of overfitting, including benign, tempered, and catastrophic overfitting, which is a significant advancement in understanding the behavior of overfitting in different scenarios.
- The use of effective rank to characterize the cost of overfitting is a novel and insightful approach that has been well-received by the reviewers.
- The paper is well-positioned within the literature, providing a clear explanation of its contributions and how it builds upon previous work.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks rigorous proofs for some of its claims, which could limit the credibility of the results.
- The assumptions made in the paper, such as the Gaussian design ansatz, are not clearly justified or explained, which could limit the generalizability of the results.
- The paper could benefit from a more detailed discussion on the practical implications of the theoretical results, particularly in terms of how they might be applied in real-world scenarios.
- There are some minor errors and inconsistencies in the paper, such as incorrectly referenced theorems and typos, which could detract from the overall quality of the manuscript.
- The paper does not provide a detailed comparison with existing literature, which could help to contextualize the contributions of the work more effectively.
