 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel method called RENT (Resampling with Noise Transition matrix) for learning with noisy labels, which utilizes a Dirichlet distribution-based per-sample weight sampling (DWS) framework. This method allows for the unified treatment of reweighting and resampling, showing that resampling can be more effective than reweighting in certain scenarios. The RENT method is evaluated on several benchmark datasets and demonstrates consistent improvements over existing methods. However, concerns are raised about the novelty and the depth of theoretical analysis, particularly in the context of existing works that have explored similar ideas.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written and easy to follow, making complex concepts accessible to a broad audience.
- The proposed method, RENT, is novel and innovative, combining reweighting and resampling into a unified framework, which is a significant contribution to the field.
- Extensive experiments are conducted on several benchmark datasets, demonstrating the effectiveness of the proposed method.
- The paper provides a theoretical analysis of the proposed method, which adds depth to the understanding of the proposed approach.
- The utilization of the transition matrix is highlighted as crucial for training a model, which is a novel approach in noisy label learning.

**Weaknesses:**
- The novelty of the paper is questioned as similar ideas have been explored in previous works, particularly in the context of the Dirichlet distribution and its application to noisy label learning.
- The paper lacks a comprehensive comparison with other methods, especially those that utilize the Dirichlet distribution in a similar manner.
- There is a lack of theoretical justification for the effectiveness of the proposed method, particularly in terms of why resampling is superior to reweighting.
- The paper does not adequately discuss the limitations of the proposed method, which is crucial for understanding its applicability and effectiveness in different scenarios.
- The paper's experimental setup and results are not convincingly superior to existing methods, particularly in terms of statistical significance and the depth of analysis provided.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors clarify the novelty of their work in comparison to existing literature, particularly in terms of the application of the Dirichlet distribution to noisy label learning?
- How does the proposed method compare to other methods that utilize the Dirichlet distribution for weighting in noisy label learning?
- Could the authors provide more detailed theoretical justification for why resampling is superior to reweighting, and how this translates into practical benefits for the learning process?
- What are the specific limitations of the proposed method, and under what conditions might it not be effective?
- How does the proposed method perform under different types and levels of noise, and what are the implications of these findings for practical applications?
- Can the authors provide more detailed comparisons with other methods, particularly in terms of statistical significance and the depth of analysis provided?
