 **Summary:**
The paper explores the adversarial robustness of spiking neural networks (SNNs) using rate-encoding, a method inspired by randomized smoothing. It introduces a theoretical framework for provable robustness against adversarial perturbations under the L1 norm, providing a theoretical analysis and empirical validation on various datasets including CIFAR-10, CIFAR-100, SVHN, and ImageNet-100. The authors employ adversarial training algorithms to enhance the robustness of rate-encoded SNNs, comparing them against other adversarial training methods. Despite the novel approach, the paper is criticized for its limited novelty, insufficient empirical robustness, and the need for more comprehensive theoretical and empirical analysis.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel approach to adversarial robustness in spiking neural networks (SNNs) by applying the randomized smoothing framework to rate-encoding, which is a significant advancement in the field.
- Theoretical results are provided to support the observed robustness of rate-encoded SNNs, which are supported by empirical evidence on various datasets.
- The paper is well-written, making it easy to follow, and includes comprehensive experiments that demonstrate the robustness of rate-encoded SNNs against adversarial attacks.
- The authors provide a detailed analysis of the Lipschitz constant of the smooth classifier, which is crucial for understanding the robustness of SNNs.
- The paper includes a thorough literature review, which is beneficial for readers in understanding the current state of research in the field.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a clear motivation for using rate-encoding over other methods, which could undermine the perceived effectiveness of the proposed method.
- The empirical results do not convincingly demonstrate the superiority of the proposed method over existing methods, such as RAT, especially in terms of certified robustness.
- The theoretical analysis, particularly the proofs and theorems, is not well-explained or clearly presented, which could confuse readers and undermine the credibility of the results.
- The paper does not adequately address the computational complexity of the proposed method, which is a significant concern for practical applications.
- The paper does not include a comprehensive discussion on the limitations of the proposed method, which is crucial for understanding the scope and applicability of the findings.
- The comparison with other methods, such as S-IBP and S-CROWN, is not fair or comprehensive, which could mislead readers about the relative effectiveness of the proposed method.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors clarify the motivation behind using rate-encoding over other methods, especially in terms of computational efficiency and robustness?
- How does the proposed method compare in terms of certified robustness with other methods, such as RAT, especially in scenarios where the certified radius is not vacuous?
- Could the authors provide more detailed proofs or explanations for the theoretical results, particularly Theorem 1 and the proofs related to it?
- How does the computational complexity of the proposed method compare with other methods, especially in terms of practical applicability?
- Could the authors discuss the limitations of the proposed method and how these might affect the practical deployment of SNNs in adversarial settings?
- How does the proposed method perform in more complex or diverse adversarial settings, such as those involving different types of adversarial attacks or different noise distributions?
