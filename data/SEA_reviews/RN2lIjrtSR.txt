 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel zero-cost adaptation method for transferring pre-trained image transformers to video recognition tasks, leveraging a dual-head attention mechanism for efficient spatial-temporal modeling. This method, named ZeroI2V, does not require additional parameters or computations during inference, which is a significant advantage over existing methods. The approach involves a linear adaptation strategy using lightweight densely placed linear adapters to facilitate the transfer of image models to video tasks. Extensive experiments across five video recognition benchmarks demonstrate the effectiveness and efficiency of ZeroI2V, showing comparable or better performance than state-of-the-art methods while maintaining parameter and inference efficiency.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel zero-cost adaptation paradigm, which does not require additional parameters or computations during inference, making it a significant advancement in the field.
- The proposed dual-head attention mechanism is innovative and allows for efficient spatial-temporal modeling, which is crucial for video recognition tasks.
- The paper is well-written and easy to follow, with clear explanations of the proposed methods and their implementation.
- The experimental results demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed method across multiple benchmarks, showing significant improvements in performance and efficiency.
- The use of linear adapters for image-to-video adaptation is a novel approach, and the paper provides a comprehensive evaluation of different adapter structures, showing the benefits of full adaptation.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks detailed explanations on how the dual-head attention mechanism is implemented, particularly in terms of the rearrangement of key-value pairs and the impact on the purity of information within each head.
- The comparison with other zero-cost operators is not convincingly superior, and the paper could benefit from a more detailed analysis of the advantages of its method over existing approaches.
- The paper could provide more detailed ablation studies on the impact of different parameters such as the number of temporal heads and the temporal receptive field on the performance.
- The paper could benefit from a more detailed discussion on the limitations of the proposed method, particularly in terms of its applicability to other video tasks beyond action recognition.
- The paper could provide more detailed comparisons with other methods in terms of inference speed and memory usage, which are crucial for practical deployment.

**Questions:**
- Can you provide a more detailed explanation of how the dual-head attention mechanism is implemented, specifically how key-value pairs are rearranged and how this affects the purity of information within each head?
- How does the proposed method compare in terms of inference speed and memory usage with other methods, particularly those that use different architectures or training strategies?
- Is the proposed method limited to action recognition tasks, or can it be applied to other video tasks such as video object detection or video captioning?
- Could you provide more detailed ablation studies on the impact of different parameters such as the number of temporal heads and the temporal receptive field on the performance?
- How does the proposed method handle long-range dependencies in video data, and what are the potential limitations in terms of temporal modeling?
