 **Summary:**
The paper introduces CICD-Coder, a novel framework designed for ICD coding in Chinese Electronic Medical Records (EMRs). This framework addresses the challenges of extracting disease-related information from concise Chinese EMRs and the lack of interpretability in previous methods. It utilizes a multi-axial knowledge approach to retrieve clinical evidence, which is then evaluated using a masked language modeling strategy. The paper presents a comprehensive evaluation module and a large-scale Chinese EMR dataset, demonstrating the effectiveness, reliability, and interpretability of the proposed method. However, concerns were raised regarding the generalizability of the results to other languages or cultures and the lack of detailed comparative analysis with other methods.

**Strengths:**
- The paper addresses a significant and relevant problem in the automatic coding of Chinese Electronic Medical Records (EMRs) using the ICD coding system, which is essential for healthcare systems in China.
- It proposes a novel approach that integrates multi-axial knowledge and clinical evidence, which is not commonly used in previous methods, potentially improving the accuracy and interpretability of ICD coding.
- The paper is well-written and easy to follow, making it accessible to readers.
- The proposed methodology is supported by a large-scale Chinese EMR dataset, which enhances the reliability and applicability of the results.
- The paper provides a detailed evaluation module based on a masked language modeling strategy, which ensures that each knowledge under the axis of the recommended ICD code is supported by reliable evidence.
- The experiments conducted on the Chinese EMRs dataset demonstrate the effectiveness, reliability, and interpretability of the proposed ICD coding method.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed comparison with other methods, which is crucial for establishing the superiority and generalizability of the proposed method.
- The generalizability of the results to other languages or cultures is questionable, as the method is specifically designed for Chinese EMRs.
- There is a lack of discussion on the limitations of the proposed method, which could help in understanding its applicability and potential improvements.
- The paper does not provide sufficient details on the implementation of the Clinical-Simbert model, which is a critical component of the proposed method.
- The paper does not include a detailed comparison with other datasets or languages, which could help in understanding the robustness and applicability of the proposed method.
- The paper does not discuss the impact of different EMR writing styles or cultural differences on the performance of the proposed method.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors provide a more detailed comparison with other methods to establish the superiority and generalizability of the proposed method?
- How does the proposed method handle different EMR writing styles or cultural differences that might affect its performance?
- Could the authors provide more details on the implementation of the Clinical-Simbert model and its impact on the performance of the proposed method?
- How does the proposed method perform when applied to EMRs from different cultures or languages, especially those with significant differences in writing styles or medical terminologies?
- Can the authors discuss the potential limitations of the proposed method and suggest areas for future research to address these limitations?
