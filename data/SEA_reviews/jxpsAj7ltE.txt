 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel approach to Mixture of Experts (MoE) by proposing a soft assignment mechanism that assigns tokens to experts based on weighted averages. This method, termed Soft MoE, aims to address the challenges of sparse MoE, such as training instability, token dropping, and ineffective fine-tuning. The approach is evaluated against ViTs and other MoEs on various vision tasks, showing improved performance and efficiency. The method's effectiveness is demonstrated through extensive experiments, including ablation studies, which show that Soft MoE can achieve competitive performance while reducing training and inference costs. However, concerns about the novelty and the clarity of the method's advantages over existing methods were raised.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, clear, and easy to follow, with a comprehensive evaluation that includes a wide range of experiments and ablation studies.
- The proposed method shows significant improvements in performance and efficiency compared to baseline methods, particularly in the context of vision tasks.
- The paper is technically sound, with a clear explanation of the methodology and the results, which are supported by extensive experimental results.
- The method addresses the challenges of sparse MoE by using a soft assignment mechanism, which is innovative and differentiates it from existing methods.
- The paper is well-organized, making it easy for readers to understand the proposed method and its implications.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed comparison with other existing methods, particularly in terms of the number of parameters and computational efficiency.
- There is a lack of clarity on how the soft assignment mechanism affects the training process, particularly in terms of the impact on the training of token and slot indices.
- The paper does not sufficiently address the limitations of the method, such as its applicability to auto-regressive decoders and the potential for increased memory consumption.
- The method's performance is not convincingly superior to existing methods, particularly in terms of zero-shot performance on downstream tasks.
- The paper could benefit from a more detailed discussion on the method's limitations and potential negative societal impacts.

**Questions:**
- How does the soft assignment mechanism affect the training process, particularly in terms of the training of token and slot indices?
- Could you clarify the advantages of the soft assignment mechanism over other methods, particularly in terms of computational efficiency and performance?
- How does the method compare in terms of zero-shot performance on downstream tasks, especially in contrastive learning?
- Can you provide more details on the method's limitations, particularly in terms of its applicability to auto-regressive decoders and its potential for increased memory consumption?
- How does the method perform when compared to other methods in terms of computational efficiency and performance on different tasks?
