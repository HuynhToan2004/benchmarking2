 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel noise representation, termed \(\int\)-noise, which integrates noise across a pixel area rather than treating it as a discrete value. This approach aims to preserve temporal correlations in noise, enhancing the quality of generated videos by avoiding high-frequency flickering and texture-sticking artifacts. The proposed method involves a noise transport equation that accurately advects noise samples across frames, maintaining noise properties while maximizing temporal correlations. Experimental results demonstrate the effectiveness of the \(\int\)-noise representation in various video editing and generation tasks, showing improvements over traditional noise sampling methods.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, clearly presented, and easy to follow, making it accessible to a broad audience.
- The proposed \(\int\)-noise representation is novel and provides a new perspective on noise handling in diffusion models, potentially enhancing video editing and generation tasks.
- The method is theoretically sound, with a clear explanation of the noise transport equation and its application to video tasks.
- The paper includes comprehensive experimental results that demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed method, showing improvements in temporal coherency and noise preservation.
- The integration of \(\int\)-noise into existing video editing and generation pipelines is straightforward, making it a practical solution for enhancing video quality.
