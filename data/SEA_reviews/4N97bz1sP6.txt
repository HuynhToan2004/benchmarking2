 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel weakly-supervised framework for audio source separation, leveraging a pre-trained CLAP model to generate weak supervision signals from textual descriptions. This framework enables the separation of single-source signals in a target modality (audio) using easily separable corresponding signals in the conditioning modality (language), without requiring single-source samples in the target modality during training. The method is tested on various datasets, showing significant improvements over existing unsupervised and semi-supervised baselines. The authors also explore the application of this framework in semi-supervised learning scenarios, demonstrating its potential to enhance supervised learning performance.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel approach to audio source separation using weak supervision from textual descriptions, which is both innovative and well-executed.
- The proposed framework is well-motivated and effectively leverages a pre-trained CLAP model to generate weak supervision signals, which is a significant advancement in the field.
- The experiments conducted are thorough and demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed method, showing improvements over existing unsupervised and semi-supervised baselines.
- The paper is well-written, making it easy to follow, and the authors provide detailed implementation details, which enhances reproducibility.
- The approach is novel and fills a gap in the literature by exploring the use of weak supervision for audio source separation, which is a significant advancement in the field.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a comprehensive comparison with existing weakly-supervised audio source separation methods, which could have provided a clearer context for the novelty and effectiveness of the proposed method.
- The experiments are limited to synthetic mixtures and do not include real-world mixtures, which could limit the generalizability of the results.
- The paper does not include a detailed analysis of the contributions of each component of the proposed framework, such as the text-based supervision and the mix-and-separate approach.
- The paper could benefit from a more detailed discussion on the potential societal impacts and limitations of the proposed method.
- The method's reliance on the CLAP model for generating weak supervision signals might limit its applicability in scenarios where such models are not available or practical.
- The paper could benefit from a more detailed explanation of the semi-supervised learning approach and the specifics of how the CLAP model is used to generate weak supervision signals.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors provide more details on how the text-based supervision and the mix-and-separate approach contribute to the overall performance of the proposed method?
- How does the proposed method perform when applied to real-world mixtures, and what are the potential challenges or limitations in such scenarios?
- Could the authors elaborate on the potential societal impacts and limitations of the proposed method, and discuss how these might be mitigated or addressed?
- How does the proposed method compare to other weakly-supervised audio source separation methods in terms of performance and practical applicability?
- Could the authors provide more details on the semi-supervised learning approach and the specifics of how the CLAP model is used to generate weak supervision signals?
- How does the proposed method perform when compared to other supervised learning methods, especially in scenarios where both single-source and multi-source data are available?
