 **Summary:**
The paper investigates the zero-shot capabilities of Large Language Models (LLMs) in generating literature reviews, specifically focusing on a plan-based approach where LLMs are prompted to first generate a plan for the review, followed by the actual text generation. The proposed methodology is tested against a new test corpus consisting of recent arXiv papers, demonstrating the effectiveness of the plan-based approach over traditional zero-shot methods. The study also highlights the potential of LLMs in improving literature review quality and discusses the challenges and opportunities in this area.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel plan-based approach to literature review generation, which is a significant advancement in the field.
- The creation of a new test corpus consisting of recent arXiv papers is a valuable contribution to the research community.
- The paper is well-written, easy to follow, and provides a comprehensive empirical evaluation of the proposed method, including a variety of experiments and detailed analysis.
- The introduction of a plan-based strategy for literature review generation is innovative and shows potential for improving the quality of generated literature reviews.
- The paper is well-organized, with clear and well-structured presentation of results and methodology, making it accessible and understandable to readers.
- The authors have demonstrated a strong commitment to research ethics and have provided detailed methodology and results, which are crucial for reproducibility and further research.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed discussion on the limitations of the proposed method, which is crucial for understanding the scope and applicability of the findings.
- The evaluation primarily relies on automatic metrics such as ROUGE, which may not fully capture the quality of the generated text, particularly in terms of factual accuracy and novelty.
- There is a need for more comprehensive human evaluation to better assess the quality of the generated literature reviews, especially in terms of factual accuracy and the ability to identify and cite relevant papers.
- The paper could benefit from a more detailed comparison with other state-of-the-art methods, particularly those that use different evaluation metrics or methodologies.
- The paper could be improved by providing more detailed examples and case studies to illustrate the effectiveness of the proposed method, especially in real-world scenarios.
- There is a need for more clarity in the presentation of results and methodology, particularly in the explanation of how the plan-based approach is implemented and evaluated.

**Questions:**
- Could you provide more details on how the plan-based approach is implemented and evaluated? Specifically, how are the plans generated, and how are they evaluated against the ground truth?
- How does the proposed method perform in terms of factual accuracy and the ability to identify and cite relevant papers? Could you provide more examples or case studies to illustrate these aspects?
- Have you considered using different evaluation metrics beyond ROUGE to assess the quality of the generated literature reviews? If so, what metrics did you consider, and how do they compare to ROUGE in terms of effectiveness?
- Given the limitations of the evaluation approach, how do you plan to address these issues in future research? Are there specific methods or metrics you plan to use to enhance the evaluation of the generated literature reviews?
- Could you discuss the potential implications of the findings for the broader research community, particularly in terms of the practical applications and the broader impact of the research?
