 **Summary:**
The paper investigates the challenges of domain generalization from a single source domain, focusing on the limitations of augmentation-based methods and proposing a novel regularization method, PROF, to enhance domain generalization performance. The authors explore the concept of style-content disentanglement and introduce a new regularization method to address the instability of augmentation-based methods. The paper includes empirical evaluations across various datasets, demonstrating the effectiveness of the proposed method in stabilizing performance and reducing mid-training fluctuations. However, concerns were raised about the novelty of the method, the clarity of its connection to existing works, and the practicality of its implementation, particularly in terms of computational cost and the availability of oracle models.

**Strengths:**
- The paper addresses an important and relevant problem in domain generalization, specifically focusing on the limitations of augmentation-based methods.
- It introduces a novel regularization method, PROF, which is a significant contribution to the field, and provides a detailed analysis of the limitations of existing methods.
- The paper is well-written, with clear and insightful discussions that aid in understanding the proposed methods and their implications.
- The empirical results demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed method in reducing mid-training fluctuations and improving generalization performance.
- The authors provide a comprehensive evaluation across multiple datasets, which strengthens the credibility of their findings.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a clear motivation for the use of a pretrained oracle model, and the benefits of using such a model are not convincingly demonstrated.
- There is a noticeable absence of comparisons with other state-of-the-art methods in domain generalization, which limits the ability to assess the true effectiveness of the proposed method.
- The paper does not adequately address the issue of computational cost, which could be a significant barrier for practical implementation.
- The paper does not provide sufficient theoretical backing for the proposed method, which could enhance its credibility and applicability.
- The connection between the proposed method and existing works, such as those by Von Kugelgen et al. (2021), is not clearly articulated, which may confuse readers about the novelty and distinctiveness of the contributions.
- The paper could benefit from more detailed discussions on the limitations of the proposed method and potential directions for future research.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors provide a more detailed discussion on the computational cost of the proposed method, including the impact of hyperparameters on this cost?
- How does the proposed method compare to other state-of-the-art methods in terms of performance and computational efficiency?
- Can the authors clarify the connection between their work and that of Von Kugelgen et al. (2021), particularly in terms of the theoretical contributions and practical implications?
- How does the proposed method handle the issue of domain generalization when the domain shift is not significant, as observed in the Corrupted CIFAR-10 experiment?
- Could the authors provide more detailed experimental results, such as the impact of different hyperparameters on the performance of the method?
- Is there a possibility to extend the proposed method to other types of data, such as text or tabular data, and how might this affect the performance and applicability of the method?
