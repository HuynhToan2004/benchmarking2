 **Summary:**
The paper introduces DiffTactile, a differentiable tactile simulator that integrates various modules including a FEM-based tactile sensor model, an optical simulation module, and a multi-material simulator. It supports diverse object types and simulates contact dynamics, which is crucial for tactile sensing in robotic manipulation. The simulator is designed to be differentiable, allowing for gradient-based optimization and system identification, potentially reducing the sim-to-real gap. The paper presents several tasks and evaluates the simulator's performance against existing simulators, showing improvements in system identification and tactile feedback. However, the paper lacks detailed comparisons with state-of-the-art methods and does not provide sufficient evidence of real-world application.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a differentiable tactile simulator that supports various tactile-assisted manipulation tasks, which is crucial for simulating soft contact between tactile sensors and objects.
- The system is differentiable, enabling gradient-based policy learning compared to traditional sampling-based algorithms, and supports system identification to close the sim-to-real gap.
- The proposed simulator models tactile sensors using FEM, objects with various materials (rigid, elastic, and plastic) using MLS-MPM, and cable using PBD, providing a comprehensive approach to tactile simulation.
- The paper is well-written and easy to follow, with a clear presentation of the methodology and results.
- The experiments demonstrate the effectiveness of the simulator, showing improvements in system identification and tactile feedback.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed comparison with state-of-the-art methods, particularly in terms of performance and efficiency, which is crucial for validating the contributions of the proposed simulator.
- There is insufficient evidence of the real-world application of the simulator, which is essential for demonstrating the practical utility of the simulator.
- The paper does not provide sufficient details on the computational cost and memory usage of the simulator, which are crucial for evaluating its scalability and applicability in real-world scenarios.
- The optical simulation module's novelty and contribution are questionable, as similar approaches have been previously used in other simulators.
- The paper does not sufficiently address the limitations of the proposed method, which is crucial for understanding the scope and applicability of the simulator.

**Questions:**
- Could you provide a detailed comparison of the proposed simulator with existing simulators in terms of performance, efficiency, and other relevant metrics?
- How does the proposed simulator handle real-world scenarios, especially in terms of sim-to-real transfer?
- Can you provide more details on the computational cost and memory usage of the simulator, particularly how it scales with increasing complexity of the simulation?
- How does the proposed simulator handle different types of tactile sensors, and what are the specific advantages of using FEM for tactile sensor modeling?
- Could you clarify the novelty and contribution of the optical simulation module compared to existing methods?
- What are the specific limitations of the proposed method, and how do these affect the applicability and effectiveness of the simulator?
