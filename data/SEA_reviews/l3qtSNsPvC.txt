 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel sampling theory for graphon signals, focusing on the Paley-Wiener spaces of graphon signals. The authors prove a Poincaré inequality for graphons and show that complements of node subsets satisfying this inequality are unique sampling sets for Paley-Wiener spaces of graphon signals. This theory is used to develop a graphon signal sampling algorithm for large graphs, which is empirically evaluated on graph machine learning tasks, demonstrating its effectiveness. The paper also discusses the consistency of these sampling sets and their application in graph machine learning tasks.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written and the presentation is clear, making it accessible to a broad audience.
- The authors have introduced a novel sampling theory for graphon signals, which is a significant contribution to the field.
- The proof of the Poincaré inequality for graphons is rigorous and well-supported by theoretical analysis.
- The paper includes both theoretical and empirical evaluations, providing a robust validation of the proposed methods.
- The algorithm introduced is efficient and can be applied to various graph machine learning tasks, demonstrating its practical utility.
