 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel approach to constructing equivariant neural networks by leveraging weight-sharing techniques in a message-passing framework. This method is applied to the problem of processing 3D point clouds and is evaluated on several benchmarks, demonstrating its effectiveness in terms of both accuracy and computational efficiency. The authors propose a method of defining invariant attributes for point-pairs that are equivalent under the action of the SE(n) group, which is used to design a convolution kernel that is equivariant to this group. The paper also discusses the theoretical underpinnings of these methods, including the use of homogeneous spaces and the theory of group convolutions. The empirical results show that the proposed method outperforms existing methods in certain scenarios, though it does not consistently outperform all baselines.
