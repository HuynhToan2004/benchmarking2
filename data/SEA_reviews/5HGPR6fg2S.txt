 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel metric called Normalized Space Alignment (NSA) for comparing and aligning representation spaces in neural networks. NSA is a differentiable metric that can be used to compare pairwise distances between two point clouds and serves as a pseudometric, satisfying the properties of a similarity metric. It is computationally efficient, continuous, and differentiable, making it suitable for use as a loss function in neural networks. The paper demonstrates that NSA can effectively approximate the global structural discrepancy during mini-batching and has been applied to various tasks such as dimensionality reduction, link prediction, and adversarial robustness. The authors provide a theoretical analysis of NSA's properties and its application in different scenarios, supported by empirical results on several datasets.

**Strengths:**
