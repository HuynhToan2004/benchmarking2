 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel pruning method for large language models (LLMs) named PGZ, which optimizes model weights to minimize gradient magnitude. This approach is claimed to be effective in reducing model size and improving inference speed, particularly through a one-shot pruning method. The method involves a weight reconstruction strategy that optimizes gradient descent towards zero, which is purported to improve model performance by reducing the number of parameters. The paper presents experiments on various LLMs, including LLaMA, demonstrating competitive performance with existing methods. However, concerns are raised about the novelty and practical efficiency of the proposed method, particularly in terms of its computational cost and the empirical evidence supporting its effectiveness.

**Strengths:**
- The paper addresses a significant and relevant problem in the field of large language models (LLMs) by proposing a novel pruning method that reduces model size and improves inference speed.
- The proposed method is technically sound, with a clear and well-structured presentation that makes it accessible to readers.
- The paper includes comprehensive experiments that demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed method, showing competitive results against existing methods like SparseGPT.
- The methodology is innovative, introducing a novel approach to pruning that is both theoretically interesting and practically applicable.
- The paper is well-written, with a clear and concise structure that facilitates understanding of the proposed method and its implications.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed comparison with state-of-the-art methods, particularly in terms of computational cost and practical efficiency.
- The empirical evidence provided does not convincingly demonstrate the superiority or even the practical efficiency of the proposed method, particularly in terms of its ability to improve model performance or reduce computational cost.
- The paper does not sufficiently address the scalability of the proposed method to larger models or different architectures, which could limit its applicability.
- The paper does not adequately address the potential negative societal impacts of the proposed method, which could be significant given the potential applications of LLMs in various domains.
- The paper's claims regarding the effectiveness of the proposed method are not sufficiently supported by empirical evidence, and the results presented are sometimes inconsistent or inconclusive.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors provide a detailed comparison of the computational cost and practical efficiency of the proposed method with state-of-the-art methods?
- How does the proposed method perform when applied to different model architectures, such as convolutional neural networks (CNNs) or recurrent neural networks (RNNs)?
- Could the authors provide more detailed results on the performance of the pruned models across a broader range of tasks and datasets, particularly those that are more challenging or diverse?
- How does the proposed method address the potential negative societal impacts of LLMs, particularly in terms of bias and fairness?
- Could the authors clarify the specific contributions of the proposed method over existing methods, particularly in terms of its novelty and practical utility?
