 **Summary:**
The paper investigates the internal mechanisms of text-to-image diffusion models, specifically focusing on the Stable Diffusion model. It employs Causal Mediation Analysis to identify causal states within the UNet and text encoder, revealing that knowledge about visual attributes is distributed across various components in the UNet and concentrated in the first self-attention layer of the text encoder. The authors propose a data-free editing method, Diff-QuickFix, which can effectively edit concepts in under a second by updating only a few parameters. This method, although fast, is not compared with other data-free editing methods, and its generalizability to other models is unclear. The paper's findings contribute to a better understanding of how knowledge is stored and manipulated in text-to-image models, offering a novel approach to editing these models without extensive retraining.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, clear, and easy to follow, with a clear motivation and a novel methodology.
- The use of Causal Mediation Analysis (CMA) to study the storage of knowledge in text-to-image models is innovative and provides valuable insights into the mechanisms of these models.
- The proposed data-free editing method, Diff-QuickFix, is simple, fast, and effective, offering a practical solution for editing text-to-image models without extensive retraining.
- The paper presents a thorough analysis of the causal components in text-to-image models, which can be used to design new editing methods.
- The findings on the causal states of different visual attributes in the UNet and text encoder are interesting and contribute to a better understanding of how these models process and store information.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a comprehensive comparison with other data-free editing methods, which limits the evaluation of the proposed method's effectiveness and efficiency.
- The generalizability of the findings to other models, especially those with different architectures or training datasets, is unclear.
- The paper does not discuss the potential societal impacts of the proposed method, which could be significant given the ability to manipulate text-to-image models.
- The analysis and experiments are primarily focused on the Stable Diffusion model, which might limit the applicability of the findings to other models.
- The paper could benefit from a more detailed discussion on the limitations of the proposed method and the assumptions made in the CMA analysis.
- The paper does not sufficiently address the scalability and efficiency of the proposed method in larger or more complex models.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors clarify the role of the CLIP-Score in the CMA analysis and its impact on the results?
- How does the proposed method perform in terms of quantitative metrics like FID score, especially in comparison to other data-free editing methods?
- Is there a possibility to extend the findings to other models, such as Imagen, and what would be the expected outcomes?
- How does the proposed method handle different types of visual attributes, especially those that might not be as clearly defined or distinct as those used in the study?
- Can the authors provide more details on the scalability and efficiency of the Diff-QuickFix method in larger or more complex models?
- How does the method handle the potential for hallucination in the edited images, and what steps are taken to mitigate this issue?
