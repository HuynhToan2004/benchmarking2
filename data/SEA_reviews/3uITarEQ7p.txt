 **Summary:**
The paper explores a novel approach to training small, differentially private language models by selectively pre-training on a subset of public data that closely resembles a private dataset. This method is aimed at improving the performance of models under privacy constraints. The approach involves using a classifier trained with differential privacy to select relevant data for pre-training, followed by fine-tuning on the private dataset. The paper demonstrates that this method can achieve state-of-the-art performance on standard benchmarks, showing that smaller models can match the performance of larger models without access to private data. The methodology is evaluated across various tasks such as language modeling and text classification, showing improvements in performance and efficiency.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel approach to training small, differentially private language models by selectively pre-training on a subset of public data that closely resembles a private dataset, which is a significant advancement in the field of private learning.
- The proposed method shows state-of-the-art performance on standard benchmarks, demonstrating its effectiveness in achieving high performance with smaller models.
- The paper is well-written, clear, and easy to follow, making it accessible to a broad audience.
- The experiments are comprehensive, including a range of model sizes and tasks, which helps in validating the proposed method across different scenarios.
- The paper provides a thorough discussion of the results, including the impact of different data selection methods and the influence of hyperparameters on performance.
- The paper addresses a significant problem in the field of private learning, which is crucial for real-world applications where data privacy is a major concern.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed discussion on the privacy implications of the proposed method, particularly in terms of how the privacy of the private dataset is preserved during the pre-training and fine-tuning stages.
- The paper does not adequately compare the proposed method with other state-of-the-art methods in terms of privacy protection and performance, which could help in understanding the relative advantages and disadvantages of the proposed method.
- There is a lack of theoretical analysis and detailed experimental results, particularly concerning the impact of different data selection methods and the influence of hyperparameters on performance.
- The paper does not discuss the limitations of the proposed method in detail, which could help in understanding the practical constraints and potential improvements for future work.
- The paper does not provide sufficient details on the computational resources and time required for the experiments, which could affect the reproducibility and scalability of the proposed method.
