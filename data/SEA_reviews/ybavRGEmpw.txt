 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel approach to enhancing the adversarial robustness of deep learning models through a method called ARMOR_D. This method leverages a new class of optimal-transport-regularized divergences, constructed via an infimal convolution between an information divergence and an optimal-transport cost, to maximize the expected loss over a neighborhood of distributions. The method is tested on malware detection and image recognition applications, showing significant performance benefits. The ARMOR_D method allows for the simultaneous transport of adversarial samples according to the optimal transport cost and re-weighting according to the information divergence, which is a unique feature that distinguishes it from existing methods. The paper also provides theoretical guarantees for the method's effectiveness and demonstrates its superiority over existing methods in terms of robustified accuracy and false negative rate.
