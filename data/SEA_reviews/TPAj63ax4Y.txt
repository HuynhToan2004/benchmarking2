 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a three-stage pipeline for weakly-supervised referring image segmentation, leveraging CLIP for zero-shot instance segmentation and a constrained greedy matching loss to correct initial segmentation mistakes. This method, which includes stages for instance segmentation, zero-shot selection, and correction, aims to improve upon existing zero-shot and weakly-supervised methods. The authors claim that their approach sets new state-of-the-art performance in both zero-shot and weakly-supervised RIS, with significant improvements over existing methods. However, concerns were raised about the clarity of the paper, the novelty of the approach, and the lack of comprehensive comparisons with state-of-the-art methods.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, easy to follow, and the proposed method is clearly explained.
- The method achieves state-of-the-art performance in both zero-shot and weakly-supervised RIS, outperforming existing methods by significant margins.
- The paper provides a detailed analysis of the proposed method, including ablations on the three-stage pipeline, which demonstrates the effectiveness of each stage.
- The use of CLIP for zero-shot instance segmentation is a novel and interesting approach, which has shown to be effective in the context of referring image segmentation.
- The paper includes a thorough analysis of the proposed method, including ablations on the three-stage pipeline, which demonstrates the effectiveness of each stage.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks clarity in several sections, particularly in the methodology and the description of the datasets used.
- The novelty of the approach is questionable, as it primarily combines existing methods without significant innovation.
- The paper does not provide sufficient experimental results, such as standard deviations or detailed ablation studies, which are necessary for a comprehensive evaluation.
- There is a lack of comparison with the most recent and relevant works, such as those mentioned in the reviews, which limits the evaluation of the proposed method's effectiveness.
- The paper does not discuss the limitations of the proposed method, which is crucial for understanding its applicability and potential drawbacks.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors provide more details on the datasets used, particularly the RefCOCO datasets, and clarify the dataset class projection step?
- How does the proposed method compare to the most recent and relevant works, such as those mentioned in the reviews?
- Could the authors provide more experimental results, such as standard deviations and detailed ablation studies, to support the claims made in the paper?
- How does the proposed method handle scenarios where the zero-shot instance segmentation is incorrect, and what are the implications of this on the overall performance?
- Could the authors discuss the limitations of the proposed method and provide suggestions for future research directions?
