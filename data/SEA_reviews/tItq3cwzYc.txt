 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a lightweight image classification network named SimpleResUNet, which integrates the feature extraction capabilities of U-Net with the efficient feature propagation of ResNet. This network employs an attention mechanism as a classifier to enhance its performance in small-sample image classification tasks. The proposed methodology includes a self-attention classifier, group norm, and adaptive average pooling layer to optimize feature extraction and classification. Experimental results demonstrate the model's superior performance compared to existing methods on various datasets, although the novelty of the approach is questioned due to its similarity to existing models like Res-UNet.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a lightweight network, SimpleResUNet, which is suitable for small-sample image classification tasks and achieves superior performance.
- The network effectively combines the feature extraction capability of U-Net and the efficient feature propagation capability of ResNet, which is novel in its application.
- The attention mechanism is used to capture correlations and dependencies between different features in the feature sequence, enhancing the model's performance.
- The paper is well-written, making it easy to understand, and the proposed method is clearly described with adequate visualizations and explanations.
- The experiments are well-designed, with comparisons against state-of-the-art methods and ablation studies, demonstrating the effectiveness of the proposed method.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a comprehensive comparison with state-of-the-art methods, particularly in terms of computational efficiency and performance on larger datasets.
- The novelty of the proposed method is limited as it is a combination of existing techniques (U-Net, ResNet, and Attention) without significant innovation.
- The paper does not adequately discuss the limitations of the proposed method, which could help in understanding its scope and applicability.
- The paper could benefit from a more detailed discussion on the design choices and the rationale behind using specific components like the attention mechanism and the adaptive average pooling layer.
- The paper does not provide a clear explanation of how the attention mechanism is applied in the proposed method, which is crucial for understanding its effectiveness.

**Questions:**
- How does the proposed method compare in terms of computational efficiency and performance with other state-of-the-art methods, especially on larger datasets?
- Could you provide more details on the design choices, particularly the use of the attention mechanism and the adaptive average pooling layer?
- How does the proposed method handle the issue of long-distance contextual information, which is a known limitation of U-Net?
- Can you clarify the role and operation of the self-attention classifier in the proposed method?
- How does the model handle the imbalance in class distribution in the datasets used, and what strategies were employed to mitigate this issue?
