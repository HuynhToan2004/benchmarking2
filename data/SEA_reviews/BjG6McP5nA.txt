 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel gradient-guided nested sampling algorithm (GGNS) that integrates Hamiltonian slice sampling, parallel updates, and a robust termination criterion. GGNS leverages gradient information to enhance sampling efficiency and scales well with dimensionality, making it suitable for high-dimensional problems. It also explores the application of GGNS with generative flow networks to improve sample quality. The paper presents a theoretical framework and empirical results demonstrating the algorithm's effectiveness in various scenarios, including high-dimensional problems. However, concerns about the novelty and clarity of the presentation, particularly in the theoretical justifications and experimental setups, were raised.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written and easy to follow, with a clear and logical structure.
- The introduction of gradient-guided nested sampling is a significant contribution, providing a novel approach to handling high-dimensional problems.
- The paper provides a comprehensive theoretical framework, including a detailed discussion on the scaling of nested sampling with dimensionality.
- The empirical results demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed method, showing that it can outperform existing methods in certain scenarios.
- The integration of gradient-guided nested sampling with generative flow networks offers a new perspective on improving sample quality in complex models.
- The paper is well-researched, with a thorough review of related works and a clear exposition of the methodology.

**Weaknesses:**
- The novelty of the proposed method is limited, as it primarily combines existing techniques without significant innovation.
- The paper lacks a detailed discussion on the theoretical justifications, particularly concerning the linear scaling of nested sampling with dimensionality.
- The experimental setup and comparisons are not robust, with insufficient details on the experimental conditions and a lack of comprehensive comparisons with other state-of-the-art methods.
- The paper does not adequately address the scalability issues of nested sampling with dimensionality, which is crucial for high-dimensional problems.
- The paper suffers from several typographical and formatting errors, which could detract from its professionalism and readability.
- There is a lack of discussion on the computational complexity and efficiency of the proposed method, which is essential for understanding its practical implications.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors provide more detailed theoretical justifications for the linear scaling of nested sampling with dimensionality?
- How does the proposed method compare in terms of computational complexity and efficiency with other state-of-the-art methods?
- What are the specific advantages of using GGNS over other existing methods, especially in terms of sample quality and computational efficiency?
- Could the authors clarify the experimental setup, particularly the conditions under which the synthetic molecule task was conducted, and provide more detailed comparisons with other methods?
- Is there a possibility to include more comprehensive comparisons with other methods in the experimental section to better establish the effectiveness of the proposed method?
- Can the authors address the concerns regarding the clarity and presentation of the paper, particularly in sections where the theoretical justifications and experimental results are discussed?
