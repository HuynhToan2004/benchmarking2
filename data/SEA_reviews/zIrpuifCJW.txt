 **Summary:**
The paper explores the application of noise in deep learning models, specifically focusing on the impact of noise on task complexity as measured by entropy. It introduces the concept of positive noise, which can enhance model performance by reducing task complexity. The authors demonstrate that noise can be beneficial when strategically injected into the latent space of neural networks, leading to improved performance in tasks such as image classification and domain adaptation. The paper provides theoretical insights and empirical evidence to support these findings, suggesting that noise can be a valuable tool in deep learning, contrary to the common belief that it is detrimental.

**Strengths:**
- The paper addresses a significant and under-explored topic in the field of deep learning, focusing on the use of noise to enhance model performance.
- Theoretical analysis and empirical evidence are provided to support the claims, demonstrating the effectiveness of positive noise in reducing task complexity and improving model performance.
- The paper is well-written, clear, and easy to follow, with a comprehensive literature review that positions the work within the current research landscape.
- The proposed method is simple, effective, and can be applied to any deep learning architecture, showing potential for broader application in various tasks.
- The experiments are extensive, covering different types of noise and models, including ViT and ResNet, and demonstrate significant performance improvements.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed discussion on the specific characteristics of positive noise and how it differs from harmful noise, which could lead to confusion.
- The theoretical analysis is not robust, with several equations and assumptions not well-explained or supported, which could undermine the credibility of the findings.
- The experimental setup and results are not convincingly explained or justified, particularly in terms of the choice of noise parameters and the impact of noise on different model architectures.
- The paper does not adequately address the limitations of the proposed method, such as the potential for overfitting or the applicability to different types of tasks beyond classification.
- There is a lack of comparison with state-of-the-art methods, which could have provided a better context for evaluating the effectiveness of the proposed approach.

**Questions:**
- Could you clarify the specific characteristics of positive noise and how it differs from harmful noise?
- How do you ensure that the noise injected into the model does not lead to overfitting, especially when using a large dataset like ImageNet?
- What is the rationale behind the choice of noise parameters, such as the variance of Gaussian noise and the strength of linear transform noise?
- How does the proposed method compare with other regularization techniques like dropout or weight decay?
- Can you provide more details on the experimental setup, particularly how the noise is injected into the model and the impact on different model architectures?
- How do you address the concerns about the generalizability of the findings beyond classification tasks, especially in domains like NLP where noise is typically considered harmful?
