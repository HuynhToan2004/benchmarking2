 **Summary:**
The paper introduces Im-Tuning, a method aimed at improving the performance of CLIP models in domain generalization tasks by tuning the image encoder. This approach contrasts with traditional methods that focus solely on prompt tuning. Im-Tuning adjusts the scale and bias of image encoder layers to enhance the separability of features, thereby improving generalization capabilities. The method was tested across various benchmarks, showing promising results, although some concerns were raised about the clarity of the method's impact on performance and the novelty of the approach compared to existing methods.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, easy to follow, and includes comprehensive experiments that cover multiple settings and benchmarks.
- The proposed Im-Tuning method is simple and effective, with a straightforward design that enhances the separability of image features.
- The experiments demonstrate that Im-Tuning outperforms baseline methods in various domain generalization tasks, as shown in Table 3.
- The paper provides a clear motivation and a detailed analysis of the limitations of existing methods, such as the inseparability of image features.
- The proposed method is well-motivated, with a clear rationale behind its design, and shows good performance across multiple datasets.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed explanation of the method's impact on performance, particularly how tuning the scale and bias of image encoder layers improves performance.
- The novelty of the method is questioned, as similar approaches have been explored in previous works, such as the tuning of image encoder layers and the use of learnable parameters for image features.
- The paper does not sufficiently compare the proposed method with other state-of-the-art methods, such as CLIP-Adapter, which could provide a more robust evaluation of the method's effectiveness.
- There is a lack of clarity regarding the experimental setup, particularly the specifics of the baseline methods used and the details of the domain generalization benchmarks.
- The paper could benefit from more detailed visualizations and explanations of the method's operation, particularly in how it affects the separability of image features.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors clarify the specific impact of tuning the scale and bias of image encoder layers on performance?
- How does the proposed method compare with other state-of-the-art methods, particularly in terms of performance and computational efficiency?
- Could the authors provide more detailed visualizations or explanations of how the method enhances the separability of image features?
- What are the specifics of the baseline methods used in the experiments, and how do they compare to the proposed method in terms of performance and computational requirements?
- How does the method perform in different scenarios, such as zero-shot learning or few-shot learning, and what are the implications of these results for the general applicability of the method?
