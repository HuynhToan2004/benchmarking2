 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel approach to solving structured POMDPs using a linear representation of the value function, which allows for efficient computation and planning. The authors propose a method that leverages the linear structure of the POMDP to represent the value function in a tractable manner, enabling the use of standard RL algorithms for planning and exploration. This method, termed Multi-step Latent Variable Representation (μLV-Rep), is theoretically analyzed for its sample complexity and is empirically validated against several benchmarks, showing competitive or superior performance compared to existing methods. The paper also discusses the challenges in extending linear structures from MDPs to POMDPs and provides a detailed algorithmic framework.

**Strengths:**
- The paper addresses the significant challenge of solving POMDPs, which are known to be computationally intractable, by proposing a novel approach that extends linear structures from MDPs to POMDPs.
- The authors provide a detailed theoretical analysis of the sample complexity of their proposed method, which is supported by empirical results showing the method's effectiveness in partially observable environments.
- The paper is well-written, making it easy to follow, and includes a comprehensive literature review that situates the work within the broader context of existing research.
- The proposed method is technically sound, with a clear algorithmic framework that is supported by theoretical guarantees and empirical validation.
- The paper provides a clear presentation of the challenges in extending linear structures to POMDPs and proposes a novel approach to address these challenges.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper's empirical evaluation is limited to relatively simple tasks, which may not adequately demonstrate the method's effectiveness in more complex, real-world scenarios.
- The paper's reliance on the L-decodability assumption could limit the generalizability of the findings, as it may not apply to all POMDPs.
- The method's performance in fully observable environments is not compared directly with existing methods, which could undermine the claims of its superiority.
- The paper's presentation could be improved to better distinguish between the proposed method and existing approaches, such as PSR.
- There are some typographical errors and unclear explanations in the paper, which could detract from its clarity and impact.

**Questions:**
- How does the proposed method compare in terms of computational efficiency with existing methods, such as those that rely on L-decodability or other assumptions?
- Can the authors clarify the assumptions made in their theoretical analysis and discuss how these assumptions affect the practical applicability of the method?
- How does the proposed method handle the issue of non-stationarity in POMDPs, and what are the implications of this for the long-term performance of the agent?
- Could the authors provide more details on the experimental setup, particularly the hyperparameters used and the rationale behind the choice of these parameters?
- How does the method perform in fully observable environments, and what are the implications of this for the generalizability of the findings?
