 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel jailbreak attack on vision language models (VLMs) that utilizes adversarial images paired with textual prompts to manipulate the alignment of language models. The attack leverages the joint embedding space of the vision and language encoders to optimize the image to match the embedding of a malicious trigger, thereby bypassing the text-only jailbreak attacks. The methodology is tested across two VLMs, demonstrating a high success rate in jailbreaking. However, concerns are raised about the practicality of the attack due to the high computational cost and the need for access to the vision encoder, which might limit its applicability in real-world scenarios.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, easy to follow, and presents a novel method for jailbreaking vision language models (VLMs) using adversarial images and textual prompts.
- The approach is original, utilizing the joint embedding space of vision and language encoders to optimize the image to match the embedding of a malicious trigger, which is a significant departure from traditional methods.
- The paper demonstrates the effectiveness of the proposed method through empirical results, showing a high success rate in jailbreaking VLMs.
- The authors have provided a detailed evaluation, including human and automatic evaluations, which enhances the credibility of the results.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed explanation of the "modality gap" and its implications on the effectiveness of the attack, which could affect the reliability of the results.
- The practicality of the attack is questionable due to the high computational cost and the need for access to the vision encoder, which might limit its applicability in real-world scenarios.
- The paper does not adequately address the generalization of the attack across different models or the robustness of the attack against various defenses.
- The evaluation section could be improved by including more diverse and realistic scenarios, such as using more diverse datasets and conducting user studies to assess the effectiveness of the attack.
- The paper could benefit from a more comprehensive discussion on the limitations of the proposed method and potential countermeasures to enhance its practicality and applicability.

**Questions:**
- Could you clarify the definition and implications of the "modality gap" on the effectiveness of the attack?
- How does the proposed method generalize to other models, and what are the potential limitations in its applicability?
- What are the computational costs associated with the attack, and how do these costs compare to other methods?
- How does the attack perform against different types of defenses, such as those designed to prevent adversarial attacks?
- Could you provide more details on the "hidden prompt injection" mentioned in the paper, and how it affects the results?
- How do the authors ensure the reliability of the results, particularly in terms of the generalizability and robustness of the attack?
