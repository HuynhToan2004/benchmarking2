 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel branching decoder for set generation tasks, which allows for the generation of multiple sequences in parallel, thereby reducing inference time and improving efficiency. The decoder uses a thresholding strategy to select tokens dynamically at each time-step, branching out new paths for parallel generation. This approach contrasts with traditional sequential decoders that generate all sequences in a single concatenated long sequence. The method is evaluated on keyphrase generation datasets and shows improved performance over existing methods. However, concerns were raised regarding the fairness of the comparison due to the different training methods used, and the paper's contribution to the field is seen as limited by its focus on a specific task.

**Strengths:**
- The proposed branching decoder is a novel approach that effectively addresses the challenges of set generation tasks, particularly in terms of inference speed and efficiency.
- The methodology is well-explained, with clear figures and examples that aid in understanding the proposed approach.
- The paper is well-written, with clear explanations of the proposed method and its implementation.
- The experiments conducted demonstrate the effectiveness of the branching decoder, showing improvements over existing methods.
- The inclusion of code in the supplementary material enhances reproducibility and transparency.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper's contribution is seen as limited due to its focus on a specific task (keyphrase generation) and the lack of broader applicability to other set generation tasks.
- The comparison with other methods is considered unfair due to different training methods used, which may not accurately reflect the performance of the proposed method.
- The paper's experimental evaluation is limited to keyphrase generation datasets, which may not fully demonstrate the generalizability of the proposed method to other set generation tasks.
- The paper does not adequately address the issue of order bias in the generation of sets, which could be a significant limitation in practical applications.
- The paper lacks a thorough discussion on the limitations of the proposed method, particularly in terms of its scalability and applicability to other types of set generation tasks.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors provide a more detailed comparison of the training methods used in the proposed method and those used in other methods to ensure a fair comparison?
- How does the proposed method perform on other set generation tasks beyond keyphrase generation?
- Can the authors provide a more detailed analysis of the limitations of the proposed method, particularly in terms of its scalability and applicability to other types of set generation tasks?
- Could the authors discuss the potential impact of the order bias in the generation of sets and how the proposed method addresses or mitigates this issue?
- How does the proposed method perform in terms of generating sets with different sizes or compositions?
- Could the authors provide more details on the computational resources required for the proposed method, particularly in terms of GPU memory usage and inference time?
