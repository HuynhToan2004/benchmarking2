 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel zero-shot quantization (ZSQ) method called ZeroP, which leverages publicly available data (proxy data) to enhance quantization performance without relying on original data. The method selects proxy datasets based on batch normalization statistics and combines them with synthetic data to fine-tune quantized models. Extensive experiments demonstrate that ZeroP outperforms existing pure-synthetic data methods and achieves comparable results to methods using original data. The paper also explores the impact of proxy data selection on performance and provides a systematic understanding of the role of proxy data in zero-shot quantization.

**Strengths:**
- The paper addresses the critical issue of data-free quantization, which is crucial for practical applications.
- The methodology is well-explained, making it easy to follow and replicate.
- The paper provides extensive experimental results, demonstrating the effectiveness of the proposed method.
- The paper is well-organized, with clear writing and a logical structure that enhances understanding.
- The proposed method is straightforward and does not require significant computational resources, making it accessible for various applications.
- The paper includes a detailed discussion on the limitations of the proposed method, which is a commendable approach to transparency and credibility.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a comprehensive comparison with existing methods that use original data, which could provide a more robust evaluation of the proposed method's effectiveness.
- The selection of proxy datasets and their impact on performance are not thoroughly analyzed, which could limit the understanding of the method's effectiveness.
- The paper does not provide sufficient details on the computational cost and efficiency of the proposed method, which is crucial for practical deployment.
- There is a lack of discussion on how the proposed method could be adapted to different architectures beyond CNNs, which could limit its applicability.
- The paper does not provide a detailed analysis of the performance of the proposed method on larger datasets, which could affect the generalizability of the results.
- The paper does not adequately address the potential privacy concerns associated with using publicly available data.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors provide more detailed comparisons with existing methods that use original data to better evaluate the proposed method's effectiveness?
- How does the selection of proxy datasets affect the performance of the proposed method, and what are the criteria for selecting the most effective datasets?
- Could the authors provide a more detailed analysis of the computational cost and efficiency of the proposed method to better understand its practical deployment?
- How does the proposed method perform on larger datasets, and what are the potential limitations in terms of scalability and performance?
- Could the authors discuss the potential privacy concerns associated with using publicly available data and propose strategies to mitigate these concerns?
- What are the potential improvements in accuracy and efficiency when using a larger number of proxy datasets, and how does this impact the performance of the proposed method?
