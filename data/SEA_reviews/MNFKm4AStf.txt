 **Summary:**
The paper explores the issue of collider bias in learning disentangled representations, proposing a regularization by identification (ReI) method to mitigate this bias. It argues that modern deep learning models for disentanglement are ill-posed due to collider bias, which can lead to dependencies between generating variables. The paper introduces a modular regularization engine designed to align model behavior with causal identification constraints, enhancing the disentanglement of representations. Empirical evidence is provided across various benchmarks and real-world datasets, demonstrating the superiority of ReI in producing disentangled representations that are robust to out-of-distribution examples and align with domain knowledge.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, with clear explanations of the proposed method and its application in causal identification.
- The idea of using a causal identification constraint to enhance the disentanglement of representations is novel and provides a clear motivation for the proposed method.
- The paper introduces a modular regularization engine (ReI) to align the behavior of large-scale models with causal identification constraints, which is a significant contribution to the field.
- The experimental results demonstrate that the proposed method outperforms existing methods in benchmark datasets, indicating its effectiveness.
- The paper provides a comprehensive discussion on the limitations of current methods and the potential limitations of the proposed method, which is commendable for its transparency and honesty.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a clear and concise explanation of the problem it addresses, which could be improved by reorganizing the introduction and the abstract.
- The paper could benefit from more detailed explanations and examples of the collider bias behavior and its impact on learning disentangled representations.
- The experimental results, while promising, are not convincingly superior to existing methods in all scenarios, particularly in more complex or varied datasets.
- The paper does not adequately address the potential limitations of the proposed method, such as its dependency on the accuracy of the causal graph and the potential for overfitting due to the regularization terms.
- There is a need for more detailed comparisons with other methods that also address collider bias, such as the method by Zhang et al. (2021), to establish the superiority of the proposed method.

**Questions:**
- Could you provide more details on the experimental setup, particularly the number of samples used for training and testing, to better understand the robustness of the results?
- How does the proposed method perform when the causal graph is inaccurate or incomplete?
- Can you elaborate on the computational complexity and scalability of the ReI regularization engine, especially in large-scale applications?
- How does the proposed method compare in terms of computational efficiency and resource requirements with other methods that address collider bias?
- Could you provide more examples or case studies that demonstrate the practical applications of the disentangled representations learned by the proposed method?
- How does the method handle scenarios where the causal graph is not known, and how does it compare to other methods that do not rely on a known graph?
