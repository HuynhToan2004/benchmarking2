 **Summary:**
The paper investigates the relationship between rotation prediction and one-class classification (OCC), proposing a two-stage method to enhance OCC performance. The first stage involves learning general representations through contrastive pre-training, followed by the second stage where potentially semantics-preserving samples are selected using density matching. This method aims to improve OCC by accurately estimating the transformation distribution within the dataset, which is crucial for effective representation learning. The paper provides empirical evidence of the strong linear relationship between rotation prediction and OCC performance, suggesting that the effectiveness of rotation as a transformation for OCC is influenced by the transformation bias in the dataset.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, clear, and easy to follow, with clear figures and a well-structured related work section.
- The authors have established a surprising connection between rotation prediction and one-class classification (OCC), revealing a strong linear relationship.
- The proposed two-stage unsupervised framework is novel and shows improved performance on visual one-class classification benchmarks.
- The paper is well-motivated, with clear explanations of the proposed method and its contributions.
- The experiments are well-designed, and the results are convincing, demonstrating the effectiveness of the proposed method.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed explanation of the relation between rotation prediction and OCC, particularly how the accuracy of rotation prediction influences OCC performance.
- The novelty of the proposed method is limited, as it primarily builds upon existing methods like CSI and UniCon, and the improvements in performance are not consistently significant.
- The paper could benefit from more comprehensive experimental evaluations, including a broader range of datasets and transformations.
- The paper does not sufficiently address the issue of noisy labels, which could lead to misleading conclusions about the effectiveness of the proposed method.
- The methodology and results sections could be more detailed, particularly in explaining the experimental setup and the performance metrics used.
- The paper lacks a clear definition and explanation of certain key terms and concepts, such as "RAI" and the "xy-shuffle" strategy.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors provide more detailed explanations on how the accuracy of rotation prediction influences OCC performance?
- How does the proposed method handle the issue of noisy labels, especially in the context of one-class classification?
- Could the authors clarify the role and effectiveness of the linear layer h in the proposed method?
- How does the paper address the potential biases introduced by the use of a reference set in the density matching stage?
- Could the authors elaborate on the experimental setup, particularly how the positive and negative pairs are defined and how the performance metrics are computed?
- How does the proposed method perform on other datasets and transformations beyond those tested, and what are the potential limitations or challenges in applying the method to different datasets?
