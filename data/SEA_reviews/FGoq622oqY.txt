 **Summary:**
The paper introduces BayOTIDE, a Bayesian online multivariate time series imputation method designed to handle missing values in irregularly sampled time series. It employs a functional decomposition of multivariate time series into trend and seasonality components, each modeled by a Gaussian Process with specific kernels. The method is evaluated on both synthetic and real-world datasets, demonstrating its superiority over existing approaches. BayOTIDE offers uncertainty quantification and interpretability, which are crucial for practical applications. However, the paper lacks a thorough discussion on the choice of hyperparameters and the sensitivity of the method to these choices. It also does not sufficiently compare with existing methods that handle irregularly sampled time series, and the experimental setup could be more robust to better demonstrate the method's capabilities.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel Bayesian online multivariate time series imputation method, BayOTIDE, which decomposes time series into trend and seasonality components, each modeled by a Gaussian Process with specific kernels.
- The method is evaluated on both synthetic and real-world datasets, demonstrating its superiority over existing approaches in terms of both accuracy and uncertainty quantification.
- BayOTIDE offers uncertainty quantification and interpretability, which are crucial for practical applications.
- The paper is well-written, easy to follow, and provides a clear explanation of the methodology.
- The methodology is scalable and can handle irregularly sampled time series, which is a significant advantage over many existing methods.
- The paper includes a thorough evaluation, including both synthetic and real-world datasets, which supports the robustness of the proposed method.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper does not adequately discuss the sensitivity of the method to the choice of hyperparameters, which could significantly affect the performance of the model.
- The experimental setup could be more robust, particularly in demonstrating the method's capabilities with irregularly sampled time series.
- The paper lacks a comprehensive comparison with existing methods that handle irregularly sampled time series, which could limit the understanding of BayOTIDE's advantages.
- The method's computational efficiency is not discussed, which could be a concern for practical applications.
- The paper could benefit from a more thorough discussion of the literature, particularly in the area of online multivariate time series imputation.
- The scalability of the method is limited by the number of factors, which could be a significant drawback in scenarios with a large number of channels or a high degree of multivariate correlation.
