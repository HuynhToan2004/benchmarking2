 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a continual learning framework aimed at enhancing the generalization capabilities of deep models in vehicle routing problems (VRPs). This framework employs inter-task and intra-task regularization to retain knowledge from smaller problem sizes and consolidate learning from larger sizes, respectively. It also utilizes an experience replay mechanism to mitigate catastrophic forgetting. The methodology is tested across various VRP sizes, demonstrating improved performance over existing deep models. However, the paper is critiqued for its limited novelty, as similar methods have been explored in other domains, and its applicability to only VRP sizes, which may not fully demonstrate the generalizability of the proposed approach.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, clear, and easy to follow, with a clear motivation and a well-designed methodology.
- The continual learning (CL) approach is novel and interesting, especially in the context of vehicle routing problems (VRPs).
- The proposed method achieves superior performance over existing deep models in both seen and unseen problem sizes.
- The paper includes comprehensive ablation studies that demonstrate the effectiveness of each component of the proposed method.
- The use of experience replay and regularization schemes is innovative and helps in retaining knowledge from smaller problem sizes and mitigating catastrophic forgetting.

**Weaknesses:**
- The novelty of the paper is limited as similar methods have been previously explored in other domains, such as curriculum learning and continual learning.
- The applicability of the proposed method is limited to VRP sizes, which may not fully demonstrate the generalizability of the approach.
- The paper does not sufficiently discuss the limitations of the proposed method, particularly concerning the scalability of the method to larger problem sizes and the computational efficiency of the experience replay mechanism.
- There is a lack of comparison with other continual learning approaches, which could provide a more robust evaluation of the proposed method.
- The paper does not sufficiently address the practical implications of the proposed method, such as its potential for real-world deployment and its impact on the inference time of the models.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors provide more details on how the proposed method performs on larger problem sizes, and discuss the potential scalability limitations?
- How does the computational efficiency of the experience replay mechanism compare to other methods, particularly in terms of inference time?
- Could the authors clarify the role and impact of the experience replay mechanism on the overall performance of the model?
- In terms of practical deployment, how does the proposed method compare to other deep models in terms of inference time and computational resources required?
- How does the proposed method handle the generalization to unseen sizes, and what are the implications of this generalization in real-world applications?
