 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel graph defense method named DRAGON, designed to enhance the robustness of graph neural networks (GNNs) against adversarial attacks. DRAGON employs a denoising module to remove malicious edges associated with attacked nodes, followed by a Mixture-of-Experts (MoE) module that applies differentially private noises to counteract hidden features. The method also avoids the use of heavy adjacency matrix computations, thereby facilitating scalability. The authors claim that DRAGON outperforms existing methods in terms of robustness and scalability, as demonstrated through extensive experiments on various datasets. However, concerns were raised about the novelty of the approach, the clarity of the paper, and the comparative analysis with state-of-the-art methods.

**Strengths:**
- The paper addresses an important and timely issue in the field of graph neural networks (GNNs), focusing on enhancing robustness and scalability against adversarial attacks.
- The proposed DRAGON framework integrates a denoising masked graph auto-encoder and a differential privacy mechanism, providing a novel approach to mitigate robustness degradation.
- The paper is well-organized, clearly written, and includes extensive experiments that demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed method.
- The use of a denoising module and a differential privacy mechanism is innovative, as it allows for the handling of different intensities of attacks and provides a scalable solution.
- The paper is the first to introduce a differential privacy mechanism into GNNs, which is a significant contribution to the field.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a thorough discussion on the limitations and potential negative societal impacts of the proposed method.
- The novelty of the approach is questionable as it appears to be a combination of existing methods without significant innovation.
- The paper suffers from poor writing quality, with numerous grammatical errors and unclear descriptions, which makes it difficult to follow.
- The comparison with state-of-the-art methods is insufficient, and the paper does not adequately discuss the scalability of the proposed method, especially in terms of computational resources and time complexity.
- The paper does not include experiments on large-scale datasets, which is a significant drawback given the claimed scalability of the proposed method.
- There is a lack of detailed theoretical analysis and proof for the claims made about the robustness and scalability of the DRAGON framework.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors provide a more detailed explanation of how the denoising module and the differential privacy mechanism work together to enhance the robustness of GNNs?
- How does the DRAGON framework compare to other state-of-the-art methods in terms of computational resources and time complexity?
- Could the authors clarify the experimental setup, particularly the hyperparameters used in the experiments and the specifics of the adversarial attacks employed?
- How does the DRAGON framework handle different types of adversarial attacks beyond node injection attacks?
- Could the authors provide more details on the scalability of the DRAGON framework, especially in terms of its applicability to large-scale datasets and real-world scenarios?
- Is there a theoretical basis for the claims made about the robustness and scalability of the DRAGON framework?
