 **Summary:**
The paper introduces Ring Attention, a novel memory-efficient approach for training transformer models with extended context lengths. By overlapping the communication of key-value blocks between devices with blockwise computation, Ring Attention enables training sequences that are up to device count times longer than those of prior memory-efficient Transformers. The method distributes long sequences across multiple devices, reducing memory constraints and allowing for the handling of sequences beyond the capabilities of traditional Transformers. The paper demonstrates the effectiveness of Ring Attention through experiments on language modeling tasks, showing improvements in performance and scalability. However, concerns about the novelty of the approach and its comparison with existing methods like blockwise parallel transformers (BPT) are raised.

**Strengths:**
- The paper addresses a significant issue in the field of memory-efficient transformer models, focusing on the challenge of scaling context length while maintaining performance and reducing memory consumption.
- The authors provide a detailed analysis of the memory requirements and propose a novel ring-based blockwise attention mechanism that overlaps the communication of key-value blocks with the computation of blockwise attention, effectively reducing memory constraints.
- The paper is well-organized and easy to follow, with a clear presentation of the problem, the proposed solution, and the results of the experiments.
- The experiments conducted demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed method, showing that it allows for training sequences that are up to device count times longer than those of prior memory-efficient Transformers, and maintains performance while reducing memory usage.
- The paper includes a detailed discussion on the implementation of the ring-based blockwise attention mechanism, which is compatible with existing code for memory efficient transformers, making it a practical solution for real-world applications.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed comparison with existing methods like BPT, particularly in terms of the trade-offs between memory efficiency and performance, which could be crucial for practitioners considering the implementation of these methods.
- There is a lack of discussion on the potential trade-offs in terms of computational complexity and memory usage when scaling up the context length.
- The paper could benefit from a more thorough discussion on the novelty of the ring-based blockwise attention mechanism compared to existing methods like BPT.
- The paper primarily focuses on language modeling tasks and does not explore other potential applications such as reinforcement learning, which could limit the generalizability of the findings.
- The paper does not provide a detailed comparison of the computational costs and the impact of the proposed method on the training time, which could be crucial for understanding the practical implications of the method.

**Questions:**
- How does the ring-based blockwise attention mechanism compare in terms of computational complexity and memory usage with other existing methods like BPT?
- Can the authors provide a detailed comparison of the computational costs and the impact of the proposed method on the training time?
- Are there any potential trade-offs in terms of performance when scaling up the context length, and how do these compare to the memory savings achieved by the proposed method?
- How does the proposed method perform when applied to other tasks beyond language modeling, such as reinforcement learning?
- Can the authors provide more details on the implementation of the ring-based blockwise attention mechanism, including the specifics of the data distribution and communication protocols used?
- How does the proposed method handle the issue of permutation invariance in the attention mechanism, and what are the implications of this for the overall performance of the model?
