 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel framework called RAFA (Reason for Future, Act for Now), which integrates Large Language Models (LLMs) with reinforcement learning (RL) to optimize actions in real-world scenarios. The RAFA framework consists of a "reason for future" component that learns from a memory buffer and generates optimal trajectories, and an "act for now" component that takes initial actions from the planned trajectory and stores feedback for replanning. The theoretical analysis demonstrates that RAFA achieves a $\sqrt{T}$ regret, and empirical results show superior performance compared to existing frameworks. The framework's novelty lies in its ability to leverage LLMs for reasoning and planning in MDPs, providing a theoretical foundation and empirical evidence for its effectiveness.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written and clearly presents the motivation, literature review, and proposed method.
- The proposed framework is novel and provides a theoretical foundation for using LLMs to perform reasoning and planning in MDPs.
- The empirical results show that the proposed method outperforms existing frameworks, demonstrating its effectiveness.
- The theoretical analysis is robust, providing a solid foundation for the proposed method.
- The paper is structured well, with clear explanations of the proposed method and its components.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper does not adequately address the conceptual discrepancies between LLMs and RL, particularly in terms of the numerical system used by RL versus the linguistic system used by LLMs.
- The paper lacks a detailed discussion on how the LLM performs the reasoning and planning tasks, which are crucial for understanding the method's effectiveness.
- The experimental results are not comprehensive, with some results (e.g., ALFWorld) showing significant differences from prior work without adequate explanation.
- The paper does not sufficiently address the limitations of the proposed method, which could impact its practical applicability.
- The paper does not adequately discuss how the proposed method compares to other recent works, such as those using tools or programs with LLMs.

**Questions:**
- How does the proposed method compare to other recent works that use tools or programs with LLMs?
- Can you provide more details on how the LLM performs the reasoning and planning tasks, and how this is different from traditional RL methods?
- How does the proposed method handle the conceptual discrepancies between LLMs and RL, particularly in terms of the numerical system used by RL versus the linguistic system used by LLMs?
- Could you clarify the experimental results, particularly the differences observed in the ALFWorld results compared to prior work?
- How does the proposed method address the limitations discussed in the paper, and what are the potential future directions for research in this area?
