 **Summary:**
The paper introduces Fast LoRA (fLoRA), a novel adaptation of the LoRA method for foundation models, which allows for the computation of different low-rank matrices for each example in a minibatch, enhancing throughput and latency in real-world serving scenarios. This method, fLoRA, is a significant extension of the LoRA method, enabling efficient batching of heterogeneous requests and maintaining the performance merits of LoRA. The paper provides empirical evidence of fLoRA's effectiveness on multilingual code generation and multilingual speech recognition tasks, demonstrating its potential in real-time serving environments. However, concerns were raised about the theoretical justification for the method's efficiency and the scope of its applicability, particularly in high-rank settings.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces fLoRA, a novel method that allows for different low-rank matrices for each example in a minibatch, which is a significant extension of the LoRA method.
- The method is well-explained, with clear and detailed explanations of the computational efficiency and the batching mechanism, making it accessible to readers.
- The paper is well-written, with clear and concise explanations of the methodology and the experimental setup, which facilitates understanding and reproducibility.
- The empirical results demonstrate the effectiveness of fLoRA in enhancing throughput and latency in practical serving scenarios, while maintaining the accuracy of LoRA.
- The paper provides a thorough analysis of the scenarios where fLoRA would be preferred over LoRA in practical applications, which is a valuable contribution to the field.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a thorough theoretical justification for the efficiency of the fLoRA method, particularly in terms of its computational cost and its applicability in high-rank settings.
- The experiments are limited to a few tasks and models, which may not fully demonstrate the generalizability of the fLoRA method across different domains and model sizes.
- There is a lack of discussion on the limitations and potential negative societal impacts of the fLoRA method, which could be crucial for understanding its broader implications.
- The paper does not provide a detailed comparison of fLoRA with other PEFT methods, which could help in understanding its advantages and disadvantages more clearly.
- The presentation of the paper could be improved, particularly in terms of the clarity and organization of the experimental results and the related work section.
