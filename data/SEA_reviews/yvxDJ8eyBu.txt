 **Summary:**
The paper introduces MuseCoco, a two-stage system for generating symbolic music from text descriptions, which is divided into text-to-attribute understanding and attribute-to-music generation stages. This methodology enables the system to operate without paired text-music data, reducing the need for extensive annotations and enhancing versatility and usability. MuseCoco is evaluated against GPT-4 and BART-base, showing superior performance in terms of musicality, controllability, and overall score. However, concerns about the clarity and depth of the evaluation, the complexity of the model, and the generalizability of the results have been raised.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel approach to generating symbolic music from text descriptions, which is a significant advancement in the field.
- The methodology is well-explained, making it easy to follow and understand.
- The system is designed to operate without paired text-music data, which is a novel approach that could potentially reduce the need for extensive annotations.
- The evaluation of MuseCoco shows superior performance compared to GPT-4 and BART-base, indicating the effectiveness of the proposed method.
- The paper is well-written and easy to read, with a clear explanation of the two-stage design and its benefits.
- The use of large-scale models and the integration of musical attributes as a bridge between the text-to-attribute understanding and attribute-to-music generation stages are innovative and effective.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a clear explanation of how the model handles the complexity of musical attributes and how it generalizes across different genres or styles of music.
- The evaluation is limited, focusing only on a few musical attributes and not comparing the results with other methods like MIDI-to-text models.
- The paper does not adequately address the issue of musicality, which is critical for evaluating the quality of generated music.
- There is a lack of detailed analysis and comparison with other state-of-the-art models, which could help in understanding the strengths and limitations of the proposed method.
- The complexity of the model and the training process might limit its accessibility and practicality for some users.
- The paper does not discuss the limitations of the proposed method, which could help in understanding its scope and applicability.
- The generalizability of the results is questionable due to the limited diversity in the evaluation dataset.
