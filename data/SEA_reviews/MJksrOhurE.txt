 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel transformer-based architecture, the Channel Aligned Robust Blend Transformer (CARD), designed for multivariate time series forecasting. CARD addresses the limitations of existing channel-independent (CI) models by incorporating a channel-aligned attention structure that captures both temporal correlations and dynamic dependencies among variables. It also introduces a robust loss function to mitigate overfitting issues. The model is evaluated across seven benchmark datasets, showing superior performance compared to existing methods. However, concerns are raised regarding the clarity and depth of the experimental validation, particularly in demonstrating the advantages of CARD over other models and the generalizability of the results.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel transformer architecture, CARD, which is well-motivated and designed to address the limitations of existing channel-independent models.
- The model is evaluated on seven benchmark datasets, showing superior performance compared to existing methods.
- The paper is well-written, making it easy to follow, and includes a comprehensive experimental section that validates the effectiveness of the proposed method.
- The introduction of a channel-aligned attention structure and a robust loss function are innovative approaches that enhance the model's ability to capture temporal correlations and dynamical dependencies among variables.
- The paper includes an ablation study that shows the effectiveness of each component of the model, contributing to the understanding of its strengths.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed comparison with existing models, particularly in terms of the number of parameters and computational complexity, which are crucial for evaluating the efficiency of the proposed model.
- The paper does not provide sufficient evidence to support the claim that CARD outperforms existing models, particularly in scenarios where the model's performance is not significantly better or even worse than baseline methods.
- The paper does not adequately address the issue of overfitting, which is a critical concern in time series forecasting.
- The experimental section is not convincing, with results showing inconsistencies and a lack of clarity in the presentation of the data.
- The paper does not sufficiently discuss the limitations of the proposed model, which is crucial for understanding its applicability and potential drawbacks.
- There are several minor issues with the presentation of the paper, such as inconsistent formatting and typographical errors, which could detract from the overall quality of the work.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors provide a detailed comparison of the model's performance with other models in terms of computational complexity and parameter count?
- How does the model perform in scenarios where the number of channels is significantly reduced, and what are the implications for the model's effectiveness?
- What are the specific advantages of the CARD model over other existing models, particularly in terms of its ability to capture temporal correlations and dynamical dependencies among variables?
- Could the authors clarify the role and effectiveness of the robust loss function in mitigating overfitting issues?
- How does the model handle the issue of overfitting, particularly in scenarios where the training data is limited?
- Could the authors provide more detailed results and a clearer presentation of the data to support the claims made in the paper?
