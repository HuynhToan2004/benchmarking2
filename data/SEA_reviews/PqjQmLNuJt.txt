 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel framework named Dual Denoising Logical Reasoning (DDLR) for inductive knowledge graph completion, addressing the issue of noise accumulation in relevant entities. DDLR integrates path-based and edge-based sampling to denoise the knowledge graph, employing a path-scoring mechanism to evaluate the importance of paths and single rules to remove irrelevant edges. This dual denoising process enhances logical reasoning by removing irrelevant paths and edges, thus improving the overall performance of the model. The framework was tested on three benchmark datasets and achieved state-of-the-art results. However, concerns were raised about the scalability of the model and the need for more comprehensive comparisons with recent methods.

**Strengths:**
- The paper addresses the significant problem of noise accumulation in knowledge graph completion and proposes a dual denoising logical reasoning (DDLR) framework to tackle this issue.
- The proposed path-scoring mechanism is innovative, effectively assessing the importance of paths and enhancing the selection of relevant paths.
- The paper is well-written, clear, and easy to follow, with a clear motivation for the proposed method.
- The experimental results are promising, demonstrating the effectiveness of the proposed method in inductive knowledge graph completion.
- The paper introduces a novel approach by combining path-based and edge-based sampling, which is a significant contribution to the field.
- The experiments are conducted on three benchmark datasets, and the proposed algorithm surpasses state-of-the-art baselines in terms of prediction accuracy.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed comparison with recent methods such as GraPE, which could provide a clearer understanding of the proposed method's advantages and disadvantages.
- The scalability of the model is a concern, as the number of sampled entities increases with the depth of the graph, potentially leading to high computational costs.
- The paper does not provide sufficient analysis of the limitations of the proposed method, such as the potential for over-filtering important paths or the impact of the number of sampled entities on performance.
- The paper does not include a detailed discussion on the limitations of the proposed method, which could provide insights into its practical applicability and areas for future research.
