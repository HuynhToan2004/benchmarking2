 **Summary:**
The paper introduces CAReNet, a novel architecture that integrates convolutional layers, attention mechanisms, and residual connections to address the challenges of training models from scratch, especially in low-resource scenarios. CAReNet's design aims to balance model size and computational efficiency with performance, as demonstrated through experiments on various datasets including TinyImageNet and STL10. Despite its innovative approach, the paper is criticized for its lack of novelty, insufficient experimental validation, and inadequate comparison with state-of-the-art models. Reviewers noted the need for more rigorous comparisons, including with models like MobileNetV3 and EfficientViT, and a more comprehensive experimental setup to substantiate the claims of model efficiency and effectiveness.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel architecture, CAReNet, which combines convolutional layers, attention mechanisms, and residual connections to address the challenges of training models from scratch.
- The authors provide a detailed description of the proposed architecture and its components, which are well-motivated and explained.
- The paper is well-written, making it easy to follow and understand.
- The experiments conducted demonstrate that CAReNet achieves competitive performance compared to other models like ResNet50, while maintaining a smaller model size.
- The paper is significant in its exploration of training models from scratch, which is a promising approach in the field of deep learning.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks novelty as it primarily combines existing components (convolutional layers, attention mechanisms, and residual connections) without introducing significant new techniques.
- The experimental setup and validation are insufficient. The paper does not include a sufficient number of datasets, and the experiments are limited to simple classification tasks.
- The paper does not adequately compare CAReNet with state-of-the-art models, particularly in terms of model size and computational efficiency.
- There are several grammatical and typographical errors throughout the paper, which detract from its professionalism and clarity.
- The paper's claims of efficiency and effectiveness are not substantiated by rigorous experimental comparisons with other models like MobileNetV3 and EfficientViT.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors provide a more detailed comparison of CAReNet with other models like MobileNetV3 and EfficientViT, particularly in terms of model size and computational efficiency?
- How does CAReNet perform in more complex and diverse tasks beyond simple classification, such as object detection and segmentation?
- Could the authors elaborate on the specific advantages of CAReNet over other models in terms of model size and computational efficiency?
- How were the hyperparameters chosen for CAReNet, and what impact do these choices have on the model's performance and efficiency?
- Could the authors provide more detailed experimental results, including training and inference times, to better support their claims of efficiency and effectiveness?
