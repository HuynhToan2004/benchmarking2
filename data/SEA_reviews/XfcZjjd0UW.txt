 **Summary:**
The paper explores the use of robustness-promoting regularization in Model-Agnostic Meta-Learning (MAML) and its impact on the intrinsic dimension of clean sample features. It proposes increasing the number of training shots to mitigate the loss of intrinsic dimension, thus improving clean accuracy without compromising robustness. The authors conduct extensive experiments across various datasets and settings to demonstrate the effectiveness of their proposed method. Despite its novel approach, the paper's contributions are seen as incremental and its experimental validation is limited to specific settings.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, easy to follow, and presents a clear and concise methodology.
- The proposed method is simple and effective, as demonstrated by extensive experiments across various datasets and settings.
- The paper provides a novel approach to improving the clean accuracy of MAML without compromising robustness, which is a significant advancement in the field.
- The authors have conducted extensive experiments to demonstrate the effectiveness of their method, including comparisons with existing methods and ablation studies.
- The paper introduces a new perspective on the robustness of MAML, which is both interesting and valuable for further research in the field.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper's contribution is seen as incremental and limited, as it primarily focuses on a specific aspect of MAML without addressing broader issues in the field.
- The experimental validation is limited to specific settings, such as using PGD attacks and certain architectures, which may not generalize well to other settings or attacks.
- The paper does not adequately compare its method with existing methods, particularly in terms of computational efficiency and robustness across different attack types.
- There is a lack of discussion on the limitations of the proposed method and its applicability in different scenarios.
- The paper's claims about the intrinsic dimension of clean sample features and its relationship to robustness are not well-supported by existing literature, which may undermine the credibility of the findings.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors provide more details on the computational efficiency of their method compared to existing methods?
- How does the proposed method perform under different types of attacks, such as CW and FGSM?
- Can the authors elaborate on the choice of the number of training shots and its impact on the performance of the model?
- How does the proposed method compare to other robust MAML methods in terms of training efficiency and robustness across different settings?
- Could the authors clarify the relationship between the intrinsic dimension of clean sample features and the robustness of the model, particularly in light of existing literature on this topic?
