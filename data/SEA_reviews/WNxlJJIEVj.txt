 **Summary:**
The paper introduces CDiffuser, a novel method for offline reinforcement learning that leverages diffusion models to generate trajectories with a focus on high-return states. The approach incorporates contrastive learning to bias trajectory generation towards high-reward states, which is a departure from traditional methods that model the dataset distribution without considering reward differences. The method is evaluated on 12 D4RL benchmarks, demonstrating superior performance in most tasks compared to existing methods. The paper also includes ablation studies and visualizations to support the efficacy of the proposed method. Despite its strengths, the paper has been critiqued for its limited evaluation on a small set of environments and its dependence on specific hyperparameters.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, making it easy to follow and understand.
- The proposed method is novel, particularly in applying contrastive learning to bias trajectory generation towards high-reward states, which is a departure from traditional methods.
- The paper includes comprehensive ablation studies and visualizations that support the efficacy of the proposed method.
- The method is evaluated on 12 D4RL benchmarks, demonstrating superior performance in most tasks compared to existing methods.
- The paper is well-motivated and presents a clear and intuitive idea.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed discussion on the limitations of the proposed method.
- The evaluation is limited to a small set of environments (3 locomotion and 3 navigation), which might not sufficiently demonstrate the generalizability of the method.
- The paper does not include a comparison with recent diffusion-based methods, such as Decision Diffuser and Diffuser, which could provide a more comprehensive evaluation.
- The method's performance heavily depends on specific hyperparameters, and the paper does not provide a detailed discussion on how to choose these parameters.
- The paper does not adequately address the limitations of diffusion models in offline RL, particularly the issue of generalization to unseen environments.
- The paper could benefit from a more detailed discussion on the differences between the proposed method and other contrastive learning-based methods in offline RL.

**Questions:**
- How does the proposed method perform in more complex environments, such as Ant or Humanoid?
- Can the authors provide a detailed discussion on how to choose the hyperparameters for the proposed method?
- How does the proposed method compare to Decision Diffuser and Diffuser in terms of performance and computational efficiency?
- Could the authors provide a more detailed comparison with other diffusion-based methods in offline RL, such as Diffuser and Decision Diffuser?
- How does the method handle the generalization to unseen environments, especially in offline RL settings?
- Can the authors provide a more detailed discussion on the limitations of the proposed method and how these might affect its practical application?
