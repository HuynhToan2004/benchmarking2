 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel method for numerical feature representation in deep learning models, specifically addressing the challenges of discretization and embedding. The proposed method, MultiHot Embedding, extends one-hot encoding by allowing multiple activations of neighboring bits, which helps in retaining more information and reducing the training issues associated with narrow bin widths. This approach is evaluated on six numerical feature learning tasks, demonstrating its effectiveness and generalization capabilities. The method is shown to outperform existing state-of-the-art numerical feature representation models, providing a simple yet effective solution for numerical feature representation in deep learning.

**Strengths:**
- The paper addresses the important problem of numerical feature learning, which has been overlooked in deep learning research.
- The proposed MultiHot Embedding is simple, easy to understand, and effective, with a straightforward extension of one-hot encoding.
- The method is evaluated on six numerical feature learning tasks, showing its effectiveness and generalization capabilities.
- The paper is well-written, with clear explanations of the method and its properties, and includes a sensitivity analysis that provides insights into the method's behavior.
- The method is shown to outperform existing state-of-the-art numerical feature representation models, demonstrating its effectiveness.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed comparison with other numerical feature representation methods, such as fuzzy discretization, which could provide a more comprehensive understanding of the proposed method's advantages and limitations.
- The paper does not discuss the limitations of the proposed method, which could help in understanding its applicability and potential drawbacks.
- The paper's experimental setup and results lack sufficient detail and clarity, making it difficult to fully evaluate the proposed method's effectiveness and generalization capabilities.
- The paper could benefit from a more thorough discussion of related work and a broader range of datasets to validate the method's performance across different domains.
- The paper's claims about the method's ability to handle small bin widths and its impact on training efficiency are not convincingly supported by the experimental results.

**Questions:**
- Could you provide more details on the experimental setup, particularly the number of training epochs and the hyperparameters used for the baselines and the proposed method?
- How does the proposed method perform when compared to other numerical feature representation methods, such as fuzzy discretization?
- Can you provide more details on the training efficiency of the MultiHot Embedding compared to one-hot encoding, and how this impacts the overall performance of the model?
- How does the method handle the issue of bin width, and what are the specific settings used for the experiments?
- Could you clarify the discrepancies in the results shown in Table 1 and explain why the proposed method performs better in some cases but not in others?
- How does the method perform when the number of discretization bins is set to a small value, and what are the implications of this setting on the model's performance?
