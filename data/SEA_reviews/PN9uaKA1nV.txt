 **Summary:**
The paper introduces ClinGen, a framework designed to enhance the performance of large language models (LLMs) in clinical natural language processing (NLP) tasks. ClinGen integrates domain-specific knowledge from both knowledge graphs and LLMs to customize prompts for clinical NLP tasks, focusing on generating clinical topics and writing styles. The framework's effectiveness is demonstrated through extensive experiments across 7 clinical NLP tasks and 16 datasets, showing improvements over existing methods. Despite the innovative approach, concerns about the novelty and the generalizability of the findings to other domains are raised.

**Strengths:**
- The paper addresses a significant problem in the field of clinical natural language processing, focusing on the generation of synthetic data for clinical NLP tasks, which is both timely and relevant.
- The authors have conducted extensive experiments, demonstrating the effectiveness of the proposed method through a series of experiments, including an ablation study that shows the effectiveness of the proposed method.
- The paper is well-written and easy to follow, with clear explanations of the methodology and results.
- The methodology integrates knowledge from both knowledge graphs and LLMs to customize prompts, which is a novel approach that enhances the performance of LLMs in clinical NLP tasks.
- The paper provides a comprehensive evaluation of the proposed method across multiple datasets and tasks, demonstrating its effectiveness and potential for real-world applications.

**Weaknesses:**

- The evaluation is limited to a specific domain (clinical NLP), which raises questions about the generalizability of the findings to other domains.
- The paper does not discuss the limitations of the proposed method, which could provide valuable insights into its applicability and potential drawbacks.
