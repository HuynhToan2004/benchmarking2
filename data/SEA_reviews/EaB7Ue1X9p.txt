 **Summary:**
The paper introduces Optimistic Local Latent Safe Optimization (OLLSO), a novel Bayesian optimization (BO) algorithm designed for high-dimensional, safety-critical problems. OLLSO utilizes a distance-preserving autoencoder to map the original high-dimensional space into a low-dimensional latent space, which is then optimized using an optimistic local safe strategy. This strategy employs upper confidence bounds to ensure safety while efficiently optimizing the objective function. The algorithm is validated through experiments on musculoskeletal and spinal cord stimulation tasks, demonstrating its effectiveness in maintaining safety and outperforming baseline methods. However, the paper has been critiqued for its lack of clarity in problem definition, insufficient theoretical analysis, and limited comparisons with state-of-the-art methods.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, with clear explanations of the proposed algorithm and its implementation details, making it easy to follow.
- The proposed method, OLLSO, is novel and addresses a significant problem in high-dimensional constrained optimization, which is often overlooked in existing literature.
- The application of the method to real-world problems, such as the optimization of neuromodulation therapy, demonstrates its practical relevance and impact.
- The paper provides theoretical guarantees and empirical results that show the safety and efficiency of the proposed method, which is validated through experiments on both simulated and real-world tasks.
- The inclusion of clinical results adds a valuable real-world context to the research, highlighting the practical applicability of the proposed method.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a clear definition and motivation for the problem it aims to solve, particularly the distinction between safe exploration and constrained optimization.
- The theoretical analysis provided is insufficient and does not convincingly support the claims made about the safety and efficiency of the proposed method.
- The comparison with state-of-the-art methods is limited, and the paper does not adequately discuss or compare with relevant existing works in the field.
- The experiments conducted are limited in scope, and the results do not convincingly demonstrate the superiority of the proposed method over existing approaches.
- The paper contains several typographical and formatting errors, which could detract from its professional presentation.
- The paper does not sufficiently address the limitations of the proposed method, which could help in understanding its applicability and potential drawbacks.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors clarify the definition and motivation of the problem being addressed, particularly the distinction between safe exploration and constrained optimization?
- How does the proposed method handle the trade-off between safety and efficiency, especially in high-dimensional settings?
- Could the authors provide more detailed theoretical analysis to support the claims made about the safety and efficiency of the proposed method?
- How does the proposed method compare with existing methods in terms of computational efficiency and scalability, especially in high-dimensional settings?
- Could the authors discuss the potential societal impacts of their work, particularly in the context of real-world applications like neuromodulation therapy?
- How does the proposed method handle the issue of non-differentiability in the safety function, and what are the implications of this for the optimization process?
