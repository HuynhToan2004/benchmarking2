 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel methodology for estimating continuous treatment effects using an end-to-end multi-task deep neural network (DNN). This approach is designed to capture the relationship between treatment propensity and treatment effect, which is particularly useful in scenarios where treatment effects vary continuously. The methodology is evaluated using both semi-synthetic and real-world data, demonstrating its effectiveness in estimating treatment effects and optimizing resource allocation. The paper also discusses the challenges of balancing uplift ranking and response estimation performance, providing a theoretical framework and practical applications. Despite its strengths, the paper suffers from issues such as unclear presentation, lack of comprehensive literature review, and insufficient empirical validation.

**Strengths:**
- The paper addresses a significant problem in causal inference by estimating the effect of continuous treatment, which is an important and understudied area.
- The proposed methodology is novel and innovative, integrating a monotonicity network and a Diffloss network to ensure the monotonicity of treatment effects.
- The paper is well-written, making it accessible and easy to follow, which is crucial for a technical paper.
- The methodology is evaluated on both semi-synthetic and real-world data, demonstrating its effectiveness in estimating treatment effects and optimizing resource allocation.
- The paper provides a theoretical framework for the methodology, enhancing its credibility and applicability.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a comprehensive literature review, particularly in the area of deep learning for causal inference, which limits its contribution to the field.
- The presentation of the paper is unclear and confusing, with numerous grammatical errors and inconsistencies in notation and terminology.
- The empirical evaluation is insufficient, with a lack of detailed experimental setup and comparison with state-of-the-art methods.
- The paper does not adequately address the balance between uplift ranking and response estimation performance, which is crucial for practical applications.
- There is a lack of theoretical proof for the performance of the proposed methodology, which could undermine its credibility.
- The paper does not sufficiently discuss the limitations of the proposed methodology, which is essential for understanding its applicability and potential pitfalls.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors clarify the role and significance of the FFT module in the proposed methodology?
- How does the proposed methodology handle the balance between uplift ranking and response estimation performance?
- Can the authors provide a more detailed explanation of the experimental setup and results, particularly in the real-world data section?
- How does the proposed methodology compare with state-of-the-art methods in terms of performance and efficiency?
- Could the authors provide a more detailed theoretical analysis of the methodology, especially regarding the monotonicity of treatment effects?
- How does the proposed methodology handle the potential issues of bias and confounding variables in real-world applications?
