 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel method for generating unlearnable examples by inducing data collapse, which enhances the transferability of these examples across diverse network architectures and adversarial training conditions. This method employs a modified adversarial training process to ensure the robustness and transferability of the unlearnable examples, which are demonstrated to outperform other state-of-the-art techniques in terms of transferability. The paper also addresses the vulnerability of existing unlearnable examples to adversarial training by proposing a refined adversarial training process specifically tailored for the generation of unlearnable examples with robust transferability. Extensive experiments validate the effectiveness of the proposed method, showcasing its potential to protect data from unauthorized use.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, making it easy to follow and understand.
- The proposed method is novel and innovative, particularly the use of data collapse to generate unlearnable examples, which is a first in the literature.
- The paper provides comprehensive experiments that demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed method, showing that it outperforms other state-of-the-art techniques in terms of transferability.
- The methodology is robust, with a refined adversarial training process tailored for the generation of unlearnable examples with robust transferability.
- The paper addresses a significant issue in the field of data protection, specifically the vulnerability of existing unlearnable examples to adversarial training.
- The paper is well-organized and provides clear explanations of the proposed method and its experimental results.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed discussion on the limitations of the proposed method, particularly in terms of scalability and computational cost, which could affect its practical implementation.
- There is a lack of clarity in the presentation of the method, particularly in the description of the algorithm and the experimental setup, which could make it difficult for readers to replicate or understand the results.
- The paper does not provide sufficient details on the training of the score model, which is crucial for the effectiveness of the proposed method.
- The paper could benefit from a more detailed discussion on the related work, especially in comparison to existing methods that also employ data collapse for unlearning.
- The paper does not adequately address the potential societal impacts of the proposed method, particularly in terms of data privacy and security.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors clarify the specific role and impact of the score model in the proposed method, particularly in terms of its training and the data it represents?
- How does the proposed method compare to existing methods that also employ data collapse for unlearning, and what are the key differences in terms of effectiveness and efficiency?
- Can the authors provide more details on the training process of the score model, including the size of the training dataset and the computational resources required?
- How does the proposed method perform when only a portion of the data is protected, and what are the implications for the effectiveness of the unlearning process in such scenarios?
- Could the authors elaborate on the potential societal impacts of the proposed method, particularly in terms of data privacy and security, and how these impacts could be mitigated?
