 **Summary:**
The paper presents a novel approach to multi-objective optimization (MOO) by focusing on the uniform distribution of Pareto objectives on the Pareto front (PF). The authors propose a method using a neural network to adjust preferences, aiming to generate a set of solutions uniformly distributed on the PF. This method is evaluated against existing MOO methods, demonstrating its effectiveness in generating diverse solutions. The paper also introduces a new indicator to measure the uniformity of Pareto objectives and provides theoretical insights into the distribution of Pareto objectives. The approach is validated through experiments on both synthetic and real-world problems, showing improvements over existing methods.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel approach to multi-objective optimization (MOO) by focusing on the uniform distribution of Pareto objectives on the Pareto front (PF), which is a significant and interesting problem.
- The proposed method, using a neural network to adjust preferences, is innovative and shows promising results in generating a set of solutions uniformly distributed on the Pareto front.
- The paper provides theoretical insights into the distribution of Pareto objectives, which is a valuable contribution to the field.
- The experimental results demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed method in generating diverse solutions, which is crucial for practical applications.
- The paper is well-written, with clear explanations of the theoretical concepts and practical applications, making it accessible to a broad audience.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed comparison with existing methods, particularly in terms of computational complexity and scalability, which could affect the practical applicability of the proposed method.
- The paper does not provide a detailed discussion on the limitations of the proposed method, which could be crucial for understanding its applicability and effectiveness in different scenarios.
- The presentation of the paper could be improved, particularly in the clarity of some sections and the inclusion of more detailed explanations and examples to aid understanding.
- There is a lack of discussion on the societal impacts of the proposed method, which is an important consideration for the ethical implications of AI applications.
- The paper does not adequately address the generalizability of the results across different types of problems or the robustness of the method under varying conditions.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors provide more details on the computational complexity and scalability of the proposed method compared to existing methods?
- How does the proposed method perform in scenarios where the Pareto front is not well-defined or when the objectives are conflicting?
- Could the authors elaborate on the limitations of the proposed method and discuss potential future research directions to address these limitations?
- How does the proposed method compare in terms of computational efficiency and effectiveness with other gradient-based methods?
- Could the authors provide a more detailed explanation of the theoretical concepts and assumptions used in the paper, particularly in the appendices?
- How does the paper address the ethical considerations of using AI for MOO, particularly in terms of fairness and transparency in decision-making?
