 **Summary:**
The paper explores the sensitivity of graph kernels to various structural characteristics by interpolating between different graph generative models. It evaluates several kernels against different graph properties, including clustering, degree heterogeneity, triadic closure, and community structure. The authors employ a Spearman correlation measure to assess the sensitivity of each kernel, highlighting the effectiveness of simpler kernels like Shortest Path and Graphlet kernels in capturing structural characteristics. The study provides insights into how different kernels perform under varying graph conditions, contributing to the understanding of kernel selection for graph generative models.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, easy to follow, and provides a comprehensive overview of graph kernels and their applications.
- The introduction of a novel framework for evaluating graph kernels based on their sensitivity to different structural properties is innovative and valuable for practitioners.
- The use of interpolation between graph generative models is an interesting approach that helps in understanding the structural properties captured by different kernels.
- The paper includes a good variety of graph kernels and models, enhancing the comprehensiveness of the study.
- The experimental design is well-executed, with clear explanations of the experimental setup and the results.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a thorough comparison with other graph kernel-based evaluation measures, such as those proposed in O'Bray et al. (2022), which could provide a more robust validation of the proposed framework.
- The paper's reliance on a limited number of graph generative models might limit the generalizability of the findings to more complex or diverse graph structures.
- The sensitivity of graph kernels to various structural characteristics is not clearly demonstrated, especially for the more complex graph properties like complementarity.
- The paper does not discuss the limitations of the proposed framework, which could help in understanding its applicability and potential improvements.
- The experimental setup is somewhat limited, focusing on small-scale graphs which might not adequately represent real-world graph datasets.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors provide more details on the experimental setup, such as the number of graphs sampled and the number of interpolation steps used?
- How do the results of this study compare with those of O'Bray et al. (2022), particularly in terms of the sensitivity of graph kernels to different structural properties?
- Can the authors clarify the relationship between the proposed framework and other existing graph kernel-based evaluation measures, such as those mentioned in Thompson et al. (2022)?
- How do the authors justify the choice of graph kernels and their sensitivity to various structural properties, especially in light of the limited number of models used?
- Could the authors provide additional experimental results on larger-scale graphs to validate the scalability and applicability of the proposed framework?
