 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel diffusion-based purification model, RetPur, aimed at mitigating the effects of adversarial attacks in image retrieval systems. This model leverages a pre-trained diffusion model to purify adversarial samples, enhancing the robustness of retrieval systems against targeted attacks. The methodology is tested across various datasets, demonstrating its effectiveness in both uni-modal and cross-modal retrieval tasks. The paper also discusses the application of adversarial purification tasks to a broader range of attacks, including both generative and iterative approaches. Despite the innovative approach, concerns were raised regarding the novelty of the purification model, the clarity of the presentation, and the depth of experimental validation.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-organized and easy to follow, with a clear and straightforward methodology.
- The proposed purification model is simple yet effective, and the experiments are comprehensive, demonstrating the effectiveness of the model in various scenarios.
- The paper addresses the critical issue of adversarial attacks in retrieval systems, which is an essential area of research.
- The use of a diffusion model for purification is novel and contributes to the field of adversarial purification.
- The paper is well-written, with clear illustrations that aid in understanding the proposed method.

**Weaknesses:**
- The novelty of the purification model is limited, as it primarily adapts existing methods for the specific task of retrieval.
- The paper lacks a detailed analysis of the computational overhead introduced by the proposed method.
- The methodology section is not clearly explained, particularly the role and definition of certain variables and equations.
- The paper does not adequately address the scalability of the proposed method, especially in terms of its applicability to larger datasets or more complex scenarios.
- The experimental validation is insufficient, with a lack of comprehensive ablation studies and comparisons with other defense methods.
- The paper suffers from typographical errors and inconsistencies in the presentation of equations and figures.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors provide a more detailed analysis of the computational overhead introduced by the proposed method?
- How does the proposed method compare in terms of computational efficiency and effectiveness with other defense methods against adversarial attacks in retrieval systems?
- What is the role and definition of the variable "s" in the context of the proposed method?
- How does the proposed method perform in scenarios where the attacker does not have access to the retrieval dataset?
- Could the authors clarify the differences between the proposed method and existing adversarial purification methods, particularly in terms of the novelty and effectiveness?
- How does the proposed method handle different types of adversarial attacks, such as non-targeted attacks, which were not addressed in the experiments?
