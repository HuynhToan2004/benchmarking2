 **Summary:**
The paper introduces two novel methods, Conjugate Integrators and Splitting Integrators, aimed at enhancing the efficiency of sampling from pretrained diffusion models. These methods, designed to be complementary, are evaluated against a wide range of baselines and demonstrate significant improvements in sample quality and generation speed. The authors propose a hybrid method combining these integrators, which achieves state-of-the-art performance in terms of FID score at a reduced number of network function evaluations (NFE). The theoretical frameworks and empirical validations provided support the practical utility and effectiveness of these methods.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces two novel methods, Conjugate Integrators and Splitting Integrators, which are complementary and can be combined to achieve state-of-the-art performance.
- The paper is well-written, with clear explanations of the proposed methods and their theoretical justifications, making it accessible to readers.
- Extensive experiments are conducted, demonstrating the effectiveness of the proposed methods across a range of datasets, including CIFAR-10, CelebA-64, and AFHQv2.
- The paper provides a detailed analysis of the stability of the proposed integrators, enhancing the understanding of their behavior and potential for practical applications.
- The authors have made significant efforts to optimize the sampling process, including the development of a hybrid method that combines the strengths of both integrators, which shows improved performance over existing methods.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper could benefit from a more detailed discussion on the limitations and potential drawbacks of the proposed methods, particularly in terms of their generalizability and applicability to other diffusion models.
- The paper primarily focuses on image generation tasks, which might limit the perceived relevance to the broader ICLR audience.
- The theoretical analysis, while robust, could be better integrated with the practical applications, especially in the context of the proposed integrators and their stability.
- The paper does not include a detailed comparison with some of the most recent and relevant works, which could provide a more comprehensive understanding of the proposed methods' position within the current research landscape.
- The presentation of the paper could be improved, particularly in terms of the clarity and organization of figures and tables, which could enhance the reader's comprehension of the results and findings.

**Questions:**
- How do the proposed methods compare to the latest works, such as those mentioned in the review?
- Can the proposed methods be adapted to other types of diffusion models, such as those used in video generation or audio synthesis?
- How does the choice of Bt affect the performance of the integrators, and could different choices lead to different results?

- How does the proposed hybrid method perform when compared to other state-of-the-art methods, and what are the key factors contributing to its superior performance?
