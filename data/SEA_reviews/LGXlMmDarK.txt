 **Summary:**
The paper explores the integration of stochasticity in Graph Neural Networks (GNNs) to enhance their expressiveness and alleviate issues like over-smoothing and over-squashing. The authors introduce a novel method, Bayesian Rewiring of Node Networks (BRONX), which utilizes a probabilistic framework to model the GNNs' behavior, specifically focusing on the adjacency matrix. The theoretical analysis highlights the benefits of stochasticity in GNNs, showing how it can increase expressiveness and improve robustness against over-smoothing and over-squashing. The paper also proposes a new GNN architecture, BRONX, which incorporates these theoretical insights to design a more robust and expressive GNN. Empirical evaluations are conducted on several datasets, demonstrating the effectiveness of the proposed method.

**Strengths:**
- The paper provides a thorough theoretical analysis of stochastic GNNs, showing how they can enhance expressiveness and alleviate issues like over-smoothing and over-squashing.
- The authors have proposed a novel GNN architecture, BRONX, which incorporates these theoretical insights to design a more robust and expressive GNN.
- The paper is well-written, making the content accessible and easy to follow, with a clear presentation of ideas and theoretical proofs.
- The empirical evaluations demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed method, showing improvements over baseline models.
- The theoretical framework is novel and provides a comprehensive understanding of how stochastic GNNs can be more expressive than the Weisfeiler-Lehman test.
- The paper is original in its approach, combining theoretical analysis with practical implementation, which is both interesting and potentially impactful.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a comprehensive discussion on related works, particularly in the area of stochastic GNNs.
- The empirical results are limited to a few datasets, which might not sufficiently demonstrate the generalizability of the proposed method.
- The theoretical analysis heavily relies on the assumption of a multiplicative noise applied to the original sparse adjacency matrix, which might not hold for all types of stochastic GNNs.
- The paper does not discuss the computational complexity of the proposed method, which could be a significant concern for large-scale applications.
- The paper does not provide a detailed discussion on the limitations of the proposed method, which could help in understanding its practical applicability and potential challenges.
- The paper could benefit from a more detailed comparison with existing stochastic GNNs, especially in terms of performance and computational efficiency.

**Questions:**
- Can you provide more details on how the BRONX architecture performs on larger datasets, such as those used in the OGB benchmark?
- How does the proposed method compare to other stochastic GNNs in terms of performance and computational efficiency?
- Could you elaborate on the choice of the Logit Normal distribution for the stochastic GNNs and its impact on the model's performance?
- How does the proposed method handle the computational complexity, particularly in large-scale applications?
- Is there a plan to release the code for the proposed method to facilitate reproducibility and further research?
- Could you provide more insights into how the proposed method can be adapted to handle different types of stochastic GNNs, such as those using dropout or weight regularization?
