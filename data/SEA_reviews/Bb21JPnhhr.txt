 **Summary:**
The paper introduces AntGPT, a method that leverages large language models (LLMs) to predict long-term actions in videos by integrating both bottom-up and top-down approaches. The bottom-up approach models temporal dynamics using LLMs, while the top-down approach infers goals to plan actions. AntGPT uses a combination of in-context learning and few-shot learning to enhance goal inference, and knowledge distillation to reduce the size of the model while maintaining performance. The method has been tested on various benchmarks and has achieved state-of-the-art results, demonstrating the effectiveness of LLMs in long-term action anticipation.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, clear, and easy to follow, with a comprehensive experimental setup that includes thorough ablations and comparisons to existing methods.
- The integration of LLMs with computer vision algorithms for video understanding is a novel approach, particularly in the context of long-term action anticipation.
- The paper provides a clear motivation and a detailed methodology, including a comprehensive literature review, and the proposed AntGPT achieves state-of-the-art performance on several benchmarks.
- The use of LLMs for long-term action anticipation is innovative and offers a new perspective on the problem.
- The paper includes detailed implementation details and comprehensive ablation studies, which help in understanding the contributions of different components of the proposed method.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed discussion on the limitations of using LLMs for long-term action anticipation, such as the need for large amounts of data and computational resources.
- The paper could benefit from a more detailed discussion on the limitations of the proposed method, including the potential impact of the choice of LLM and the need for extensive training data.
- There is a lack of discussion on the generalizability of the findings to other domains or tasks, which could limit the practical applicability of the proposed method.
- The paper could be improved by providing more detailed explanations of the in-context learning and few-shot learning processes, as well as the specifics of the goal inference process.
- The paper could benefit from a more detailed comparison with other state-of-the-art methods, particularly those that use similar approaches but may not be directly comparable due to different evaluation metrics or methodologies.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors provide more detailed explanations of the in-context learning and few-shot learning processes used in the paper?
- How does the choice of LLM affect the performance of the proposed method, and what are the implications of using different LLMs for different tasks or domains?
- Could the authors discuss the potential impact of the choice of action recognition model on the performance of the proposed method?
- How does the paper address the limitations of using LLMs for long-term action anticipation, particularly in terms of data requirements and computational resources?
- Could the authors provide more details on the goal inference process, including how the goals are defined and how they are used to guide the action anticipation process?
- How does the paper handle the potential ambiguities in the goal inference process, particularly in scenarios where multiple plausible goals could be inferred from the same set of actions?
- Could the authors discuss the potential applications of the proposed method beyond the specific tasks and domains studied in the paper?
