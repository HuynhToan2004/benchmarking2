 **Summary:**
The paper introduces the Representation Jensen-Shannon Divergence (RJSD), a novel method for estimating divergence between probability distributions by embedding them into a Reproducing Kernel Hilbert Space (RKHS). This method avoids the need to estimate probability density functions and provides a lower bound on the classical Jensen-Shannon divergence. The RJSD is used to train generative adversarial networks (GANs) and to detect distribution shifts in two-sample testing problems. Theoretical results are provided to support the method's properties and consistency, and empirical results demonstrate its effectiveness in mode collapse prevention and improved performance in GAN training and two-sample testing.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel method for estimating divergence between probability distributions using the Representation Jensen-Shannon Divergence (RJSD), which is a significant contribution to the field.
- The theoretical results provided in the paper are robust and support the consistency and convergence of the proposed estimator.
- The methodology is well-explained and the paper is well-written, making it accessible and understandable to readers.
- The paper demonstrates the effectiveness of the proposed method in real-world applications, such as GAN training and two-sample testing, showing that it outperforms existing methods.
- The paper establishes a connection between the kernel-based entropy and Shannon's entropy, and the relationship between RJSD with the classical Jensen-Shannon divergence, which is both novel and interesting.
