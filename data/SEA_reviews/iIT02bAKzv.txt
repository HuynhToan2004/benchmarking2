 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel pruning method, Efficient Coarse-to-Fine Layer-Wise Pruning (ECoFLaP), aimed at reducing the computational and memory costs of large Vision-Language Models (VLMs) without compromising their performance. This method employs a two-stage approach, first determining the sparsity ratios of different layers using global importance scores and then performing layer-wise unstructured weight pruning. The approach is validated on various multi-modal and single-modal models and datasets, showing significant performance improvements over existing pruning techniques. However, the paper lacks detailed comparisons with other pruning methods and does not provide a comprehensive evaluation of the computational efficiency and memory usage of the proposed method.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel pruning method, Efficient Coarse-to-Fine Layer-Wise Pruning (ECoFLaP), which effectively reduces computational and memory costs in large Vision-Language Models (VLMs) without compromising performance.
- The method is validated on various multi-modal and single-modal models and datasets, demonstrating significant performance improvements over existing pruning techniques.
- The paper is well-written, easy to follow, and provides a clear explanation of the proposed method and its implementation.
- The use of a coarse-to-fine pruning strategy, combined with zeroth-order gradient computation, offers a more efficient approach to pruning compared to traditional methods.
- The paper includes extensive experimental results and detailed ablation studies, which provide insights into the effectiveness of the proposed method.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed comparison with other pruning methods, particularly in terms of computational efficiency and memory usage.
- The paper does not provide a comprehensive evaluation of the proposed method, especially in terms of its applicability to other types of VLMs and its effectiveness in different scenarios.
- The paper does not sufficiently discuss the limitations of the proposed method, such as its scalability to larger models and its generalizability across different model architectures.
- The paper does not provide a detailed analysis of the computational and memory costs of the proposed method, which is crucial for understanding its practical applicability.
- The paper does not include a discussion on the potential negative societal impacts of the proposed method, which could be a significant concern for some readers.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors provide a more detailed comparison with other pruning methods, particularly in terms of computational efficiency and memory usage?
- How does the proposed method perform when applied to other types of VLMs, and what are the implications for its effectiveness and applicability?
- What are the specific computational and memory costs of the proposed method, and how do these compare to other pruning methods?
- How does the proposed method handle different scenarios, such as varying sparsity ratios across different layers?
- Can the authors provide a more detailed analysis of the limitations of the proposed method, particularly in terms of scalability and generalizability?
- Could the authors include a discussion on the potential negative societal impacts of the proposed method, and how these might be mitigated or addressed?
