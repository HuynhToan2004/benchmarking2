 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel approach to representation learning for regression tasks, termed Supervised Contrastive Learning for Regression with Mixup (SupReMix). This method leverages mixup strategies to generate hard negative and positive pairs at the embedding level, enhancing the contrastive learning process for regression tasks. Theoretical analysis and extensive experiments across multiple datasets demonstrate the superiority of SupReMix over existing methods, showcasing improvements in continuous ordered representations and regression performance. The paper also discusses the challenges of ordinality-awareness and hardness in contrastive learning for regression and proposes a method to address these issues.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel approach to representation learning for regression tasks, specifically addressing the challenges of ordinality-awareness and hardness in contrastive learning for regression.
- The methodology is supported by both theoretical analysis and extensive experiments, demonstrating the effectiveness of the proposed SupReMix in enhancing regression performance.
- The paper is well-structured and clearly written, with a logical flow that aids in understanding the complex concepts presented.
- The empirical results show that SupReMix outperforms other supervised contrastive learning frameworks and vanilla deep regression, highlighting its practical utility.
- The paper addresses the issue of continuous ordered representations for regression, which is a significant contribution to the field.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a comprehensive discussion on the limitations of the proposed method, which could provide a more balanced view of its applicability and effectiveness.
- The paper does not include a direct comparison with other recent methods like SimCSE, which could provide a more robust evaluation of the proposed method.
- There is a lack of clarity in the definitions and roles of "Mix-neg" and "Mix-pos" in the paper, which could confuse readers.
- The paper does not adequately address the generalizability of the proposed method across different types of regression tasks, particularly those involving categorical labels.
- The theoretical analysis is not clearly explained, and the definitions of key terms like "hardness" and "ordinality-awareness" are not well-defined, which could limit the understanding of the proposed method.
- The paper does not sufficiently discuss the limitations of the proposed method, which could provide a more balanced view of its applicability and effectiveness.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors provide more detailed comparisons with other recent methods like SimCSE to better evaluate the effectiveness of SupReMix?
- How does the proposed method perform in scenarios where the regression task involves categorical labels?
- Could the authors clarify the roles and definitions of "Mix-neg" and "Mix-pos" in the context of the paper?
- How does the proposed method handle the generalizability across different types of regression tasks, particularly those involving categorical labels?
- Could the authors provide a more detailed explanation of the theoretical analysis, especially the definitions and roles of key terms like "hardness" and "ordinality-awareness"?
- What are the potential limitations of the proposed method, and how might these affect its practical application?
