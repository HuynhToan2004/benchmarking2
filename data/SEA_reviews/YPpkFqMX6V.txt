 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel approach to hypergraph neural networks by proposing a new message passing homophily measure and a MultiSet framework, which extends the AllSet framework to allow multiple representations for nodes and hyperedges. The authors also introduce a new sampling strategy and a mini-batching method to address scalability issues. The proposed methods are evaluated on various datasets, demonstrating improvements over existing models. The paper also addresses the challenges of defining homophily in hypergraphs, which is crucial for understanding the dynamics of hypernetworks. The experimental results showcase the effectiveness of the proposed methods, although concerns were raised about the clarity of the writing and the need for more rigorous comparisons with existing methods.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel message passing homophily measure and a MultiSet framework, which are significant contributions to the field of hypergraph neural networks.
- The MultiSet framework allows for multiple representations of nodes and hyperedges, which is a novel approach that extends the capabilities of existing methods.
- The paper is well-written, making it accessible and easy to understand, which is crucial for a broad audience.
