 **Summary:**
The paper investigates the critical factors influencing plasticity in visual reinforcement learning (VRL), focusing on data augmentation (DA), the role of different modules, and training stages. The authors identify that data augmentation is crucial for maintaining plasticity, particularly in the critic module, which is most affected by plasticity loss. They propose an adaptive replay ratio (RR) method to manage plasticity loss dynamically, adjusting the RR based on the critic's plasticity level. The paper also introduces a novel metric, Fraction of Active Units (FAU), to measure plasticity and demonstrates that the adaptive RR method improves sample efficiency compared to static RR settings. Despite its novel insights and experimental validations, the paper is criticized for its limited scope, focusing on only one environment and lacking a broader range of experiments.

**Strengths:**
- The paper provides a comprehensive analysis of plasticity in visual reinforcement learning, which is a critical yet under-explored area.
- The authors have conducted a systematic empirical exploration focusing on three primary underexplored facets, deriving insightful conclusions that are valuable for understanding and improving plasticity in VRL.
- The paper is well-written, making it easy to follow and understand, with clear and logical presentation of the findings.
- The proposed adaptive RR method is simple yet effective, addressing the high RR dilemma in VRL.
- The experiments are well-designed, and the results are convincing, showing that the adaptive RR method significantly improves sample efficiency compared to static RR settings.
- The paper is well-organized, with a clear structure and logical flow, making it easy to understand the contributions and insights of the research.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper is limited in its scope, focusing only on one environment (DeepMind Control suite) and not exploring other environments like Atari-57, which could provide a more comprehensive understanding of plasticity in VRL.
- The novelty of the proposed adaptive RR method is questioned as it is a simple combination of existing methods (DA and plasticity injection) without significant innovation.
- The paper lacks a broader range of experiments, particularly in terms of different hyperparameters and environments, which could strengthen the validity of the findings.
- The analysis of plasticity is limited to the critic network, neglecting the actor network, which could provide valuable insights into the role of plasticity in different modules of the VRL.
- The paper does not discuss the limitations of the proposed methods, which is crucial for understanding the scope and applicability of the findings.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors provide more details on the plasticity measurement used in the paper, particularly the FAU metric, and compare it with other metrics like the number of active neurons or the number of non-zero weights?
- How does the proposed adaptive RR method compare with other methods that dynamically adjust the replay ratio, such as those mentioned in the literature?
- Why was the decision made to focus only on the critic network, and what were the reasons for not including the actor network in the analysis?
- Could the authors clarify the methodology used in the experiments, particularly how the adaptive RR method was implemented and how it affects the overall performance of the VRL?
- How does the proposed method perform in more complex environments like Atari-57, and what are the implications for its applicability in real-world scenarios?
