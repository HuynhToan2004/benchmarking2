 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel approach to enhancing the efficiency of evolutionary algorithms for Neural Architecture Search (NAS) by integrating a binary discriminator trained online to predict the relative performance of different architectures. This method, termed PAM-RT, guides the evolutionary process by comparing child architectures against their parents, aiming to improve the speed and accuracy of the search process. The authors demonstrate the effectiveness of this method through experiments on various NAS benchmarks, showing improvements over traditional methods. However, the paper's novelty is questioned due to its similarity to existing works, particularly in its use of binary discriminators and online training.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, clear, and easy to follow, making it accessible to a broad audience.
- The approach of using a binary discriminator to guide the search for better neural architectures is novel and interesting, offering a new perspective on the problem of Neural Architecture Search (NAS).
- The experiments are well-designed, demonstrating the effectiveness of the proposed method in improving the performance of evolutionary algorithms for NAS.
- The paper provides a detailed analysis of the model's accuracy and its impact on evolution, which is crucial for understanding the method's efficacy.
- The methodology is robust, with the use of a binary predictor instead of a regression model, which is more effective in symbolic regression tasks.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a clear motivation for using a binary discriminator, particularly in terms of its advantages over traditional methods like regression models.
- The novelty of the approach is limited, as similar methods have been previously explored, such as the use of binary predictors in NAS.
- The paper does not adequately address the generalization of the binary predictor to unseen architectures, which is a significant challenge in NAS.
- The experimental results do not convincingly demonstrate the superiority of the proposed method over existing approaches, particularly in terms of final performance.
- The paper could benefit from a more detailed discussion on the limitations and potential negative societal impacts of the proposed method.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors clarify the motivation behind using a binary discriminator over a regression model, especially in terms of its advantages in the context of NAS?
- How does the binary predictor generalize to unseen architectures, and what measures are in place to ensure its effectiveness in such scenarios?
- Could the authors provide a more detailed comparison of their method with existing works, particularly in terms of performance and computational efficiency?
- What are the specific challenges and limitations of the proposed method, and how do these impact its practical applicability and effectiveness?
- How does the method perform in terms of robustness to noise and variations in the search space, and what are the implications of these factors on the method's performance?
