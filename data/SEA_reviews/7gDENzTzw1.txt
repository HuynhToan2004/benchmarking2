 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel approach to robust reinforcement learning (RL) by formulating the state perturbation problem as a Stackelberg game and proposing two algorithms, BP-DQN and DP-DQN, to handle different types of state perturbations. The first algorithm, BP-DQN, uses a particle filter to approximate the belief of the true state, while the second, DP-DQN, employs a diffusion model to purify the perturbed state. The paper also explores the concept of pessimistic Q-learning, where the agent maximizes the worst-case Q-value under state perturbations, and presents theoretical results on the performance loss due to pessimism. Experimental results show that these methods outperform existing state-of-the-art methods in various environments, including gridworld and Atari games. However, the paper is critiqued for its limited evaluation scope, lack of comparison with more recent methods, and potential issues with scalability and computational complexity.

**Strengths:**
- The paper presents a novel approach to robust RL by formulating the state perturbation problem as a Stackelberg game, which is both interesting and relevant to the field.
- The paper is well-organized, with clear writing and a good structure that makes the content accessible and understandable.
- The proposed methods are novel and demonstrate good performance in the experiments conducted, showing a clear advantage over existing methods.
- The paper includes a theoretical result on the performance loss due to pessimism, which adds depth to the theoretical understanding of the proposed methods.
- The use of diffusion models to purify perturbed states is a novel approach that has not been explored in prior work.
- The paper addresses a significant issue in the field of RL, namely the robustness of RL agents against state perturbations, which is a critical aspect for practical applications.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a comprehensive evaluation of the proposed methods. It only compares against a limited set of baselines and does not include a broader range of state-of-the-art methods, which could provide a more robust validation of the proposed methods.
- The paper does not include a discussion on the limitations of the proposed methods, which is crucial for understanding the practical applicability and scope of the work.
- The paper could benefit from a more detailed discussion on the scalability of the proposed methods, especially in high-dimensional state spaces.
- There is a lack of clarity in some parts of the paper, particularly in the presentation of algorithms and the explanation of certain methodologies, which could hinder understanding and reproducibility.
- The paper does not adequately address the computational complexity of the proposed methods, which could be a significant concern for practical implementation.
- The paper could benefit from a more comprehensive review of related work, particularly in the area of robust RL and adversarial attacks in RL, to provide a more robust context for the research.
