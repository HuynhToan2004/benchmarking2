 **Summary:**
The paper addresses the challenges of learning from noisy and long-tailed data, focusing on the disparate impacts of sub-populations under such conditions. It introduces a novel fairness regularizer (FR) to mitigate these disparities, aiming to improve the learning performance of both tail and overall sub-populations. The authors conduct extensive experiments on various datasets, including CIFAR and Clothing1M, to validate the effectiveness of the FR. Despite the innovative approach, the paper has been critiqued for its limited scope in terms of dataset diversity and the lack of a comprehensive theoretical analysis.

**Strengths:**
- The paper addresses an important and practical problem of learning from noisy and long-tailed data, which is highly relevant in real-world applications.
- The proposed fairness regularizer is novel and well-motivated, offering a new perspective on addressing disparities in sub-populations.
- The paper is well-written, clear, and easy to follow, making it accessible to a broad audience.
- The experimental results are comprehensive and convincing, demonstrating the effectiveness of the proposed method across various datasets.
- The paper provides a theoretical analysis of the proposed method, enhancing the understanding of its underlying principles.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed discussion on the theoretical foundations of the fairness regularizer, which could limit the understanding of its effectiveness and applicability.
- The dataset used in the experiments is somewhat limited, focusing primarily on image classification tasks. Expanding the dataset to include other types of data could enhance the robustness of the findings.
- There is a lack of comparison with existing methods that address similar issues, such as fairness in long-tailed learning or robustness in noisy label learning.
- The paper does not provide a detailed analysis of the computational complexity or time efficiency of the proposed method, which is crucial for practical implementation.
- The paper's methodology and experimental design could be improved, particularly in the separation of sub-populations and the handling of different noise models.
- The paper lacks a thorough discussion on the limitations and potential negative societal impacts of the proposed method.

**Questions:**
- Could you clarify the relationship between the proposed fairness regularizer and existing methods for fairness in long-tailed learning or robustness in noisy label learning?
- How does the proposed method perform when applied to other types of data, such as text or tabular data?
- Could you provide more details on the computational complexity and time efficiency of the proposed method, especially in comparison to existing methods?
- How does the proposed method handle different noise models, such as the noise transition matrix?
- Can you elaborate on the methodology used for separating sub-populations and the impact of different feature extractors on the results?
- What are the potential negative societal impacts of the proposed method, and how are these addressed or mitigated?
