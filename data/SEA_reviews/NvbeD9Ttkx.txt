 **Summary:**
The paper introduces FOSI, a hybrid optimization method that integrates first-order and second-order information to enhance the performance of base first-order optimizers. FOSI leverages the Lanczos algorithm to estimate extreme eigenvalues and eigenvectors of the Hessian, which are used to split the function into quadratic functions defined on orthogonal subspaces. This approach allows for the simultaneous use of Newton's method and a base optimizer, potentially improving the convergence rate and optimization time of first-order methods. The paper includes theoretical analysis and empirical evaluations that demonstrate FOSI's effectiveness in improving the convergence of first-order methods, although its novelty and computational efficiency are questioned.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, with a clear and comprehensive presentation of the FOSI algorithm and its spectral analysis.
- The empirical evaluation is robust, demonstrating that FOSI significantly improves the convergence of first-order methods in various scenarios, including deep neural networks and quadratic functions.
- The paper provides a detailed description of the FOSI algorithm, which is a novel approach that integrates first-order and second-order information to improve the performance of base first-order optimizers.
- Theoretical analysis is provided, including proofs of convergence under common assumptions and improvements in the condition number of the problem for a large family of base optimizers.
- The paper includes an open implementation, which enhances its reproducibility and accessibility.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a thorough comparison with other existing methods, particularly in terms of computational efficiency and the approximation of Hessian information.
- The novelty of the approach is questioned as similar methods using Lanczos algorithms for Hessian approximation have been previously explored.
- The paper does not sufficiently address the computational complexity and efficiency of FOSI, especially in large-scale optimization problems.
- The paper does not include a comprehensive discussion on the limitations of FOSI, such as its applicability to non-convex functions and its sensitivity to hyperparameters.
- The paper lacks a detailed discussion on the potential negative societal impacts of the proposed method.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors provide a more detailed comparison with other existing methods, particularly in terms of computational efficiency and the approximation of Hessian information?
- How does FOSI compare in terms of computational efficiency and convergence rate with other hybrid optimization methods that leverage second-order information?
- Can the authors discuss the potential negative societal impacts of the proposed method, especially in terms of its application to large-scale optimization problems?
- How does FOSI perform in terms of convergence rate and optimization time when compared to other state-of-the-art optimization methods?
- Can the authors clarify the role of the Lanczos algorithm in the performance of FOSI and discuss how it contributes to the overall effectiveness of the method?
- How does FOSI handle the issue of overfitting, especially in scenarios where the base optimizer is prone to overfitting?
