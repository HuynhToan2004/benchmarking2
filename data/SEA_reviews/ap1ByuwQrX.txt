 **Summary:**
The paper introduces Token Distribution Dynamics (TDD), a method aimed at analyzing the saliency of tokens in LLM outputs by utilizing token distributions rather than traditional attention or gradient-based methods. TDD projects input tokens into the embedding space and estimates their significance based on distribution dynamics over the vocabulary. The method is evaluated across multiple datasets and LLMs, demonstrating its effectiveness in various applications such as toxicity suppression and sentiment steering. Despite its innovative approach, the paper is critiqued for its lack of novelty, insufficient comparison with existing methods, and the need for more extensive human evaluations.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel approach using token distribution dynamics to analyze the saliency of tokens in LLM outputs, which is a departure from traditional methods that heavily rely on attention or gradient.
- The proposed method is simple, efficient, and effective, as demonstrated by its superior performance compared to baseline methods across various datasets and LLMs.
- The paper is well-written, with clear and detailed explanations of the methodology, experiments, and results, making it accessible and understandable to readers.
- The application of the method to prompt manipulation tasks such as toxic language suppression and sentiment steering shows its potential for practical applications.
- The paper includes a comprehensive set of experiments and ablation studies, which provide insights into the effectiveness of the proposed method.

**Weaknesses:**
- The novelty of the method is questioned, as similar ideas have been previously explored in other works. The paper could benefit from a clearer differentiation from existing methods.
- The paper lacks sufficient comparison with state-of-the-art methods, particularly in terms of efficiency and effectiveness. More comprehensive comparisons, including human evaluations, are needed to substantiate the claims made.
- There are inconsistencies and potential errors in the presentation of results, such as the discrepancy between reported values in tables and figures.
- The paper could benefit from a more detailed discussion on the limitations of the method, including its applicability to different LLM architectures and the potential for overfitting due to the use of the same model for both saliency calculation and output generation.
- The paper does not sufficiently address the ethical implications of the proposed method, particularly in terms of its potential for misuse in generating harmful or offensive content.

**Questions:**
- How does the proposed method compare in terms of efficiency and effectiveness with state-of-the-art methods, particularly in large-scale applications?
- Can the authors clarify the discrepancies between reported values in tables and figures, and provide a detailed explanation for these inconsistencies?
- How does the method perform when applied to different LLM architectures, such as encoder-decoder models or models with different tokenization schemes?
- Could the authors provide more details on the ethical implications of the proposed method, particularly in terms of its potential for generating harmful or offensive content?
- How does the method handle the potential for overfitting due to the use of the same model for both saliency calculation and output generation?
- Can the authors provide a more detailed discussion on the limitations of the method and suggest potential avenues for future research to address these limitations?
