 **Summary:**
The paper explores the global convergence of the natural actor-critic (NAC) algorithm using multi-layer neural networks for both the actor and critic parameters. It derives non-asymptotic sample complexity bounds for the NAC algorithm, decomposing the error incurred at each step into errors at the actor and critic steps. The paper also discusses the theoretical underpinnings of NAC algorithms, including the smoothness property of the actor parametrization and the inherent smoothness of the critic parametrization. The authors aim to provide a rigorous analysis of NAC algorithms, although the paper's assumptions and the clarity of its contributions are questioned.

**Strengths:**
- The paper provides a detailed analysis of the NAC algorithm, which is crucial for understanding the theoretical underpinnings of this widely used method in reinforcement learning.
- The paper is well-structured, with clear definitions and a comprehensive proof sketch, making it accessible to readers.
- The authors introduce a novel approach by decomposing the error incurred at each step into errors at the actor and critic steps, which is a significant contribution to the field.
- The paper provides a non-asymptotic sample complexity bound for the NAC algorithm, which is a first in the literature.
- The authors address the sample complexity of NAC algorithms, which is a significant issue in practical applications of these algorithms.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper relies on several assumptions that are not clearly justified or explained, such as the assumption of a smooth activation function for the policy network and the assumptions regarding the compatibility between the actor and critic networks.
- The paper's contribution is not clearly articulated, with the main results (Theorem 1 and Remarks 1 and 2) reiterating existing results without sufficient novelty or depth.
- The paper lacks a detailed discussion on the implications of the sample complexity bound and the practical relevance of the theoretical results.
- There are several typographical errors and unclear sections in the paper, which could confuse readers and detract from the overall quality of the manuscript.
- The paper does not adequately compare its results with existing literature, particularly in terms of the sample complexity bounds provided by other works.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors clarify the assumptions regarding the compatibility between the actor and critic networks, especially how this affects the sample complexity bound?
- How does the paper's analysis compare with existing works, particularly in terms of the assumptions made and the results obtained?
- Can the authors provide a more detailed discussion on the implications of the sample complexity bound and how it might be improved in future work?
- In the proof sketch, could the authors clarify the use of the Moore-Penrose pseudo-inverse and its implications for the proof?
- How does the paper's analysis differ from existing works, particularly in terms of the assumptions made and the results obtained?
