 **Summary:**
The paper explores the integration of unlabeled data into semi-supervised classification problems using a novel framework that combines Distributionally Robust Optimization (DRO) with self-supervised training. This method leverages the robustness of DRO to enhance generalization in scenarios where labeled samples are scarce. Theoretical analysis is provided on the generalization error for a Gaussian mixture model, demonstrating that the use of unlabeled data can significantly improve generalization bounds. Experiments are conducted on both synthetic and real-world datasets, showing that the proposed method outperforms existing techniques. However, the paper is criticized for its limited experimental validation and lack of clarity in some theoretical assumptions and methodological descriptions.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, with clear and concise definitions and proofs.
- The integration of Distributionally Robust Optimization (DRO) with self-supervised training is novel and offers a robust framework for handling out-of-distribution samples.
- The theoretical analysis is solid, particularly in the context of Gaussian mixture models, and the paper provides a comprehensive discussion of the limitations of the proposed method.
- The paper is structured effectively, with a clear introduction and logical presentation of the methodology and results.
- The experimental results are convincing, demonstrating the effectiveness of the proposed method in real-world scenarios.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a comprehensive experimental validation, particularly in scenarios where the unlabeled data distribution is significantly different from the labeled data.
- The assumption of a Gaussian mixture model is strong and may limit the generalizability of the findings.
- The theoretical contributions are somewhat limited, as the proofs rely heavily on existing results and do not offer significant new insights.
- The paper does not adequately address the computational complexity of the proposed method, particularly in large-scale applications.
- There are inconsistencies in the experimental results, with some results showing improvements only under certain conditions, which are not clearly explained.
- The paper lacks a detailed discussion on the societal impact of the proposed method, particularly in terms of potential biases introduced by the use of unlabeled data.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors clarify the role and impact of the parameters λ and γ’ in the optimization problem?
- How does the proposed method handle the computational complexity in large-scale applications, particularly in terms of the optimization problem defined by equation 9?
- In the experimental results, why does the method perform worse when the unlabeled data distribution is significantly different from the labeled data?
- How does the proposed method compare to other semi-supervised learning methods that do not rely on robustness assumptions?
- Could the authors provide a more detailed explanation of the experimental setup, particularly in the "Different distribution" setting, to clarify the methodology and results?
- How does the proposed method ensure that the use of unlabeled data does not introduce significant biases or errors in the classification process?
