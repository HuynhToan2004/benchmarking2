 **Summary:**
The paper introduces SafeDreamer, a novel method that integrates Lagrangian-based methods with the Dreamer framework to enhance safety in reinforcement learning (RL) tasks. SafeDreamer utilizes a world model for planning and balances long-term rewards and costs, achieving nearly zero-cost performance on various tasks, including low-dimensional and vision-only input tasks. The approach is evaluated against other model-based and model-free methods within the Safety-Gymnasium benchmark, demonstrating its effectiveness in balancing performance and safety. However, concerns were raised about the clarity of the paper, the complexity of the algorithm, and the lack of novelty in some aspects.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, easy to follow, and includes a detailed related work section that effectively situates the research within the current literature.
- The integration of the world model with Lagrangian-based methods within the Dreamer framework is a novel approach that has been empirically validated.
- The method achieves nearly zero-cost performance on various tasks, including low-dimensional and vision-only input tasks, within the Safety-Gymnasium benchmark, demonstrating its effectiveness in balancing performance and safety in RL tasks.
- The paper provides a detailed explanation of the methodology, including a clear algorithmic representation that aids in understanding the proposed method.
- The empirical results are convincing, showing that the proposed method outperforms competing model-based and model-free methods within the benchmark.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a clear motivation for the choice of the Lagrangian method, and the connection between the Lagrangian method and the Dreamer framework is not adequately explained.
- The algorithm's complexity and the need for two planning methods (OSRP and OSRP-Lag) are not well justified.
- The paper could benefit from a more detailed comparison with other safe RL algorithms, particularly in terms of performance and computational efficiency.
- There are several typographical and formatting errors throughout the paper that need correction.
- The paper does not adequately address the limitations of the proposed method, such as its applicability to more complex or realistic scenarios.
- The paper's novelty is questioned as it primarily combines existing methods (Dreamer and Lagrangian) without significant innovation.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors provide more details on the motivation behind using the Lagrangian method and its integration with the Dreamer framework?
- How does the proposed method handle the complexity and computational demands of the algorithm, especially in real-world applications?
- Could the authors clarify the role and impact of the Lagrangian multiplier in the training process?
- In terms of safety, how does the method ensure that the learned policy is safe in real-world scenarios, especially when the world model might not perfectly predict the environment's dynamics?
- Could the authors provide a more detailed comparison with other safe RL algorithms, particularly in terms of performance and computational efficiency?
- How does the method perform when applied to more complex or realistic scenarios, such as those involving high-dimensional sensor inputs or dynamic environments?
