 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel approach to the continuous-armed bandit problem, focusing on the reduction of computational complexity by projecting the reward function onto a subspace of a Hilbert space using orthogonal basis functions. This approach allows the transformation of the problem into a linear bandit problem with misspecification, enabling the application of existing algorithms like LinUCB and Phased Elimination. The paper claims that this method can achieve state-of-the-art performance in terms of regret and computational efficiency, although the theoretical guarantees and experimental validations are questioned. The proposed method, while promising, lacks robust empirical evidence and detailed comparative analysis, which are crucial for its acceptance.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel approach to the continuous-armed bandit problem by reducing it to a linear bandit problem with misspecification, which is a significant contribution to the field.
- The use of orthogonal basis functions for feature mapping is innovative and helps in improving computational efficiency, which is crucial in real-world applications.
- The paper is well-written, making it accessible and understandable, which is essential for a broad audience.
- Theoretical results are presented in an informal manner, making them easier to understand and follow, which is beneficial for readers not familiar with the complex mathematical notations used.
- The proposed method is shown to be competitive with existing state-of-the-art algorithms in terms of regret and computational complexity, which is a significant achievement.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks empirical evidence to support the claims made about the computational efficiency of the proposed method. More detailed experiments and comparisons with existing methods are needed to validate these claims.
- There is a significant gap between the theoretical guarantees and practical performance, particularly in terms of regret bounds and computational complexity.
- The paper does not adequately address the limitations of the proposed method, which could lead to a misleading interpretation of its effectiveness.
- The paper's presentation and organization could be improved to make it more accessible to readers unfamiliar with the continuous-armed bandit problem.
- The paper does not provide a detailed comparison with existing methods in terms of computational efficiency and regret bounds, which is crucial for evaluating the practical effectiveness of the proposed method.

**Questions:**
- Could you clarify the computational complexity of the proposed method, particularly in terms of the time complexity of the algorithm?
- How does the proposed method perform in practical scenarios where the reward function is not differentiable or is only approximately differentiable?
- Can you provide more details on the experimental setup and results, including the specific conditions under which the experiments were conducted and the parameters used?
- How does the proposed method compare with existing methods in terms of computational efficiency and regret bounds, especially in scenarios where the reward function is not differentiable?
- Could you explain the choice of orthogonal basis functions and how they affect the performance of the algorithm?
- How does the proposed method handle the misspecification error in practical scenarios, and what are the implications of this error on the overall performance of the algorithm?
