 **Summary:**
The paper explores the capabilities of encoder-decoder models in few-shot learning, focusing on the T5 model and its variants. The authors propose two methods to enhance in-context learning: objective-aligned prompting and a fusion-based approach. They evaluate these methods across a variety of tasks, comparing them to larger decoder-only models and demonstrating that the encoder-decoder models can outperform their larger counterparts in certain scenarios. The paper also discusses the potential of encoder-decoder models to be effective few-shot learners, especially in natural language understanding tasks. However, concerns are raised about the generalizability of the findings to other encoder-decoder models and the fairness of the comparisons made.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written and clearly articulates the experimental setup, making it easy to follow and understand.
- The authors conduct a comprehensive evaluation of encoder-decoder models in few-shot learning, which is a significant contribution to the field.
- The findings of the paper are robust and demonstrate that encoder-decoder models can perform on par with larger decoder-only models, suggesting that the size of the model is not the only determinant of performance.
- The paper introduces a novel fusion-based approach that effectively merges encoder outputs, which is a valuable contribution to the field.
- The experimental results are well-presented and provide a clear demonstration of the effectiveness of the proposed methods.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper primarily focuses on the T5 model and its variants, which limits the generalizability of the findings to other encoder-decoder models.
- The comparison between encoder-decoder models and decoder-only models is not fair, as the encoder-decoder models are trained on different datasets and have fewer parameters, which could affect the results.
- The paper does not adequately address the computational costs of the fusion-based approach, which could be a significant drawback in practical applications.
- The paper lacks a detailed discussion on the limitations of the proposed methods, particularly in the context of generative tasks.
- The paper does not include a comparison with more recent decoder-only models, which could provide a more comprehensive evaluation of the proposed methods.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors provide more details on the computational costs of the fusion-based approach and how it compares to other methods in terms of efficiency?
- How do the proposed methods perform when applied to other encoder-decoder models, particularly those not based on the T5 architecture?
- Can the authors elaborate on the limitations of their methods, particularly in the context of generative tasks?
- Would it be possible to include a comparison with more recent decoder-only models to provide a more comprehensive evaluation of the proposed methods?
- How do the authors justify the choice of the T5 model for their experiments, and could they provide a more detailed discussion on the generalizability of their findings to other encoder-decoder models?
