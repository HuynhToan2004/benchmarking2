 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel method for high-resolution image inpainting using a diffusion model and a ViTSR module. The ViTSR module, based on a vanilla ViT architecture, utilizes position embedding interpolation to facilitate low-resolution training and multi-resolution inference, enhancing computational efficiency. The methodology involves training the diffusion model on low-resolution inputs and employing the ViTSR module for super-resolution reconstruction. The proposed approach shows promising results in terms of computational efficiency and performance, though it faces criticism for its novelty and the clarity of its methodological contributions.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, clear, and easy to follow, with a clear motivation and a good presentation of the proposed method.
- The proposed method achieves state-of-the-art (SOTA) results on image inpainting and super-resolution tasks, showing good performance across various datasets.
- The paper introduces a novel position embedding interpolation method, which allows the model to train at low resolution and perform inference at high resolution, enhancing computational efficiency.
- The authors provide comprehensive experimental results and comparisons, demonstrating the effectiveness of the proposed method.
- The method's simplicity and potential for practical application in real-world scenarios are highlighted, making it accessible and potentially impactful.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a thorough analysis of the limitations of the proposed method, which is crucial for understanding its applicability and potential drawbacks.
- The novelty of the proposed method is questioned, as the use of position embedding interpolation and the ViTSR module is seen as a straightforward extension of existing techniques.
- The paper does not sufficiently compare the proposed method with state-of-the-art models in terms of computational efficiency and performance, which could undermine the claimed advantages.
- The clarity of the paper could be improved, particularly in the explanation of the methodology and the experimental setups, to enhance reader comprehension.
- The paper does not sufficiently address the ethical considerations of the research, which is a significant oversight in the current academic landscape.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors provide more detailed explanations of the methodology, especially in sections that are currently unclear or confusing?
- How does the proposed method compare with other state-of-the-art models in terms of computational efficiency and performance? Could the authors provide a more detailed comparison in this regard?
- What are the specific limitations of the proposed method, and how do these impact the practical applicability of the model?
- How does the use of position embedding interpolation affect the performance of the model, especially in scenarios where the input size changes significantly?
- Could the authors provide more detailed insights into the training and inference processes, particularly how the model handles different input resolutions?
- How does the proposed method address the ethical considerations of the research, and what steps have been taken to ensure the ethical soundness of the work?
