 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a novel generalization method for regression tasks based on the metric learning assumption that the distance between features and labels should be proportional. The method involves minimizing the error of a mapping function that defines the proportion and stabilizing its fluctuations by smoothing out its variations. The paper leverages spectral norm to align the maximum singular values of feature matrices across different domains, which enhances out-of-distribution generalization. The authors conduct experiments on eight benchmark datasets and demonstrate that their method outperforms state-of-the-art approaches in most cases. However, concerns were raised about the clarity of the mathematical formulations, the lack of comparisons with certain baselines, and the need for a more comprehensive theoretical analysis.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written and easy to follow, with clear explanations of the proposed method and its mathematical foundations.
- The method is novel and interesting, with a focus on feature-level generalization in regression tasks, which is a less explored area compared to classification tasks.
- The paper provides a theoretical analysis of the proposed method, which is useful for understanding its underpinnings.
- The experimental results are robust, demonstrating the effectiveness of the proposed method across various datasets.
- The paper is well-organized, with clear presentation of the methodology and results.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed comparison with other state-of-the-art methods, particularly in the domain generalization setting, which could have strengthened the claims of superiority.
- There are some mathematical errors and inconsistencies in the formulations, which could affect the reliability of the theoretical analysis.
- The paper does not sufficiently address the issue of spurious correlations, which could limit the applicability of the proposed method in real-world scenarios.
- The paper could benefit from a more comprehensive discussion on the limitations of the proposed method and potential areas for future research.
- The experimental results are not sufficiently robust, with some results showing marginal improvements over existing methods.
- The paper could benefit from a more detailed discussion on the hyperparameter settings and their impact on the performance of the proposed method.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors clarify the mathematical errors and inconsistencies noted in the formulations, particularly in the proofs and theorems?
- How does the proposed method address the issue of spurious correlations, and what are the implications of this for the generalizability of the results?
- Can the authors provide more detailed comparisons with other state-of-the-art methods, particularly in the domain generalization setting?
- How does the proposed method compare to other methods that use different norms, such as the Frobenius norm, in the spectral alignment of domains?
- Could the authors provide more detailed explanations of the t-SNE visualizations and their implications for the performance of the proposed method?
- How sensitive is the proposed method to the choice of hyperparameters, and what are the optimal settings for different datasets and scenarios?
