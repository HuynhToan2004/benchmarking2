 **Summary:**
The paper discusses the limitations of semantic censorship in large language models (LLMs) and argues that it is an undecidable problem, similar to Rice's theorem. The authors propose that semantic censorship is a misguided approach, and instead, syntactic censorship should be considered. They introduce the concept of "Mosaic Prompts" to demonstrate how malicious users could reconstruct impermissible outputs from a collection of permissible ones. The paper also explores the theoretical and practical implications of these limitations, suggesting that semantic censorship is inherently flawed and that LLMs could be used to bypass it. The authors argue that these findings highlight the need for a shift in perspective from viewing censorship as a machine learning problem to a security problem.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written and provides a clear definition of the problem, which is crucial for understanding the limitations of semantic censorship in LLMs.
- The authors present a novel perspective on the censorship problem, focusing on the limitations of semantic censorship and proposing that syntactic censorship might be a more effective approach.
- The theoretical results, particularly those related to the undecidability of semantic censorship and the impossibility of semantic output censorship, are significant and add depth to the understanding of the challenges in LLM censorship.
- The paper is well-organized and the examples provided are clear and intuitive, making the complex concepts more accessible.
- The authors provide a comprehensive discussion on the limitations of semantic censorship and propose a shift in perspective from an ML problem to a security problem, which is an interesting and potentially impactful contribution to the field.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks empirical results, which could have strengthened the argument by demonstrating the effectiveness of the proposed solutions.
- The theoretical results are not clearly linked to practical scenarios, making it difficult to understand the implications of the findings in real-world applications.
- The paper could benefit from more concrete examples and a clearer explanation of how the theoretical results translate into practical solutions.
- The paper does not sufficiently discuss the limitations of its own approach, particularly the practical challenges in implementing syntactic censorship effectively.
- The paper could be improved by providing more detailed examples and a clearer explanation of the connections between the theoretical results and practical applications.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors provide more concrete examples of how the theoretical results could be applied in practical scenarios to improve the effectiveness of LLM censorship?
- How does the paper address the practical challenges in implementing syntactic censorship, particularly in terms of scalability and computational efficiency?
- Could the authors clarify the connection between the theoretical results and the proposed solutions, especially in relation to the practical implications of the undecidability and impossibility results?
- How does the paper's approach compare to existing methods in terms of effectiveness and computational efficiency?
- Can the authors discuss the potential impact of their findings on the development of LLM censorship systems and suggest areas for future research?
