 **Summary:**
The paper introduces GeoLLM, a method that leverages large language models (LLMs) to extract geospatial knowledge from textual descriptions of locations, particularly utilizing OpenStreetMap data. GeoLLM is demonstrated to outperform traditional satellite-based methods and other LLMs in predicting various geospatial features such as population density and economic indicators. The approach involves fine-tuning LLMs with geospatial prompts and map data, showing significant improvements in performance. The paper also explores the scalability and efficiency of LLMs in geospatial tasks, suggesting potential for broader applications in geospatial prediction and analysis.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a novel approach by leveraging large language models (LLMs) to extract geospatial knowledge from textual descriptions of locations, which is a significant advancement in the field.
- The experiments conducted are comprehensive and demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed method, particularly in improving the performance of LLMs in geospatial tasks.
- The paper is well-written, making it easy to follow and understand the methodology and results.
- The use of OpenStreetMap data in conjunction with LLMs is highlighted as a promising approach, showing potential for broader applications in geospatial prediction and analysis.
- The paper is significant in its exploration of the scalability and efficiency of LLMs in geospatial tasks, suggesting potential for broader applications in the field.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a detailed discussion on the limitations and societal impacts of the proposed method, which is crucial for understanding the broader implications of the research.
- The scope of the experiments is limited to a few models and tasks, which might not adequately demonstrate the generalizability of the findings.
- The paper does not sufficiently discuss the choice of prompts and their impact on the model's performance, which is a critical aspect of the proposed method.
- The novelty of the method is questionable as it primarily involves the fine-tuning of LLMs with geospatial prompts and map data, which might not be seen as a significant advancement over existing methods.
- The paper does not adequately address the potential biases in the data used, which could affect the reliability and generalizability of the results.
- The paper does not sufficiently discuss the computational costs and scalability of the proposed method, which are important considerations for practical applications.
