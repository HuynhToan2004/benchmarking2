 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a new limited-memory variant of the BFGS method, named LG-BFGS, which aims to achieve superlinear convergence rates in non-asymptotic settings. This method is based on a greedy basis selection and displacement aggregation, which maximizes the update progress per iteration and retains a sparse subspace of past curvature information. Theoretical analysis is provided to demonstrate the superlinear convergence rate, and numerical experiments are conducted to validate the theoretical findings. Despite its novelty, the paper has been critiqued for its limited scope in experimental settings and the lack of a comprehensive comparison with other quasi-Newton methods.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces a new limited-memory variant of the BFGS method, LG-BFGS, which achieves superlinear convergence rates in non-asymptotic settings, a first in the literature.
- The proposed method combines greedy basis selection and displacement aggregation, which is a novel approach in limited-memory quasi-Newton methods.
- The paper provides a theoretical analysis of the convergence rate, which is crucial for understanding the behavior of the proposed method.
- The experimental results validate the theoretical findings, demonstrating the effectiveness of the LG-BFGS method.
- The paper is well-written, making it easy to follow, and the presentation is clear, enhancing the understanding of the proposed method.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a comprehensive comparison with other quasi-Newton methods, particularly in terms of computational complexity and memory requirements.
- The experimental settings are limited, focusing only on logistic regression with l2 regularization, which may not adequately represent the performance of the method in more diverse scenarios.
- The paper's theoretical analysis is limited to a specific class of problems, which may not generalize well to other types of problems.
- The paper does not provide a detailed comparison of the computational complexity and memory requirements of the proposed method with other state-of-the-art methods, which is crucial for understanding its practical advantages.
- The paper does not adequately discuss the scalability of the proposed method with the problem size, which is a significant limitation in high-dimensional problems.
- The paper lacks a discussion on the limitations of the proposed method, which is crucial for understanding its applicability and potential drawbacks.

**Questions:**
- Could the authors provide a more detailed comparison of the computational complexity and memory requirements of the proposed method with other state-of-the-art methods?
- How does the proposed method perform in more diverse experimental settings, particularly in scenarios beyond logistic regression with l2 regularization?
- Can the authors discuss the scalability of the proposed method with the problem size, especially in high-dimensional problems?
- How does the proposed method compare in terms of convergence rate and memory requirements with other quasi-Newton methods, particularly those that do not use displacement aggregation?
- Could the authors elaborate on the limitations of the proposed method and discuss potential future research directions to address these limitations?
