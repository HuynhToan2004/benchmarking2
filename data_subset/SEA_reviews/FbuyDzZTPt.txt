 **Summary:**
The paper introduces a rehearsal-free method for class-incremental learning, leveraging a regularization method based on virtual outliers to enhance the decision boundaries of classifiers and prevent inter-task confusion. It proposes a simplified prompt-based method, OnePrompt, which utilizes a single prompt for each task, reducing the complexity and computational cost compared to existing methods. The paper also explores the use of virtual outlier regularization (VOR) to improve the performance of various prompt-based methods. The methodology is evaluated on multiple datasets, showing promising results in terms of accuracy and inference speed. However, concerns about the novelty of the VOR technique and the clarity of its integration with the OnePrompt method are highlighted.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, with clear explanations of the proposed methodologies and their applications.
- The OnePrompt method, which uses a single prompt for each task, is a significant contribution to the field of rehearsal-free class-incremental learning.
- The paper provides a comprehensive evaluation of the proposed methodologies, comparing them to existing state-of-the-art methods and demonstrating their effectiveness.
- The use of virtual outlier regularization (VOR) to enhance the performance of various prompt-based methods is a novel approach that shows promise in improving accuracy and inference speed.
- The paper is original in its approach, combining the strengths of OnePrompt and VOR to address the challenges of rehearsal-free class-incremental learning.

**Weaknesses:**
- The novelty of the VOR technique is questionable, as it appears to be a combination of existing methods without significant new insights or contributions.
- The integration of the VOR technique with the OnePrompt method is not clearly explained, raising concerns about the coherence of the proposed methodologies.
- The paper lacks a detailed analysis of the computational costs and parameters of the proposed methods, which are crucial for understanding their practical implications.
- The experimental results presented in the paper are not sufficiently robust, with some results showing only marginal improvements or even decreases in performance compared to existing methods.
- The paper does not adequately address the limitations of the proposed methods, which could impact the reliability and generalizability of the findings.
- There are inconsistencies and potential errors in the presentation of the paper, such as incorrect references and unclear figures, which could confuse readers.

**Questions:**
- Can the authors clarify the novelty and integration of the VOR technique with the OnePrompt method?
- How does the proposed method compare in terms of computational costs and parameters to other existing methods?
- Could the authors provide more detailed experimental results, including standard deviations and additional metrics, to better assess the robustness and effectiveness of their methods?
- What are the specific computational costs associated with generating virtual outliers and how do these compare to other methods in terms of efficiency?
- How does the proposed method perform on larger datasets or more complex tasks, and what are the potential limitations or challenges in scaling up the approach?
- Can the authors provide more detailed explanations or examples of how the virtual outlier regularization (VOR) technique works and how it impacts the performance of the classifier?
